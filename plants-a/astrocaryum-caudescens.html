Astrocaryum caudescens

Astrocaryum caudescens, commonly known as the Brazilian wax palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its elongated, leafless stem and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into hard, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its trunk is covered in a thick layer of wax-like substance, which provides protection against pests and moisture loss.

Astrocaryum caudescens plays an important ecological role in its habitat, supporting various wildlife species and contributing to the biodiversity of the region.
