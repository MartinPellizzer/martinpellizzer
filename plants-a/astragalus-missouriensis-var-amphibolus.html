Astragalus missouriensis var. amphibolus

Astragalus missouriensis var. amphibolus, commonly known as Missouri milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellowish to reddish pea-like flowers. Its root system is deep and fibrous, helping it survive in arid conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators in its native range.
