Amygdalus praecommunis

Amygdalus praecommunis, commonly known as the common almond.

This species is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region. It features pinnately compound leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring. The fruit is a drupe with a hard pit containing the edible seed.

It is closely related to the domesticated almond (Prunus dulcis) and has been historically used for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
