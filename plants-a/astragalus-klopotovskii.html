Astragalus klopotovskii

Astragalus klopotovskii, commonly known as Klopotovsky milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
