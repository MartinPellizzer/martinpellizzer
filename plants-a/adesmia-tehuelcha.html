Adesmia tehuelcha

Adesmia tehuelcha, commonly known as the Andean burr bean, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its ecological role includes providing food for local wildlife and contributing to soil fertility.
