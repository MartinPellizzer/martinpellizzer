Asplenium trichomanes sublusus pachyrachis

Asplenium trichomanes sublusus pachyrachis, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a rare and delicate fern species characterized by its finely divided, dark green fronds.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is known for its intricate, lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth or on rocky surfaces. Its spores are released from specialized structures called sori, which are arranged in a distinctive pattern on the undersides of its leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and conservation status, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
