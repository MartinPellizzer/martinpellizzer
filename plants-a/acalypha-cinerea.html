Acalypha cinerea

Acalypha cinerea, commonly known as silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Acalypha cinerea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
