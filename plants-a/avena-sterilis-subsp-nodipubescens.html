Avena sterilis subsp. nodipubescens

Avena sterilis subsp.

nodipubescens, commonly known as the nodular oat, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green hue, and it produces awnless spikelets that are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

This subspecies is of interest in ecological studies due to its resilience and role in stabilizing soil in marginal habitats.
