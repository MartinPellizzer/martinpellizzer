Aechmea × maginallii

Aechmea × maginallii, commonly known as the hybrid pineapple bromeliad, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Aechmea fasciata and Aechmea maginiflora.

It is a perennial evergreen plant native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that form a central cup. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red bracts and yellow-orange flowers, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate watering to maintain its health and appearance.

Aechmea × maginallii is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and long-lasting blooms.
