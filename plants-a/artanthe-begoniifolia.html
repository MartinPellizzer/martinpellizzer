Artanthe begoniifolia

Artanthe begoniifolia, commonly known as the begonia-leaved artanthe, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its striking, bi-colored leaves that range from deep green to red. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
