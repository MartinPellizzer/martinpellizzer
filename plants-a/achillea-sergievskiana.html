Achillea sergievskiana

Achillea sergievskiana, commonly known as the Siberian yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant features finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.

Achillea sergievskiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
