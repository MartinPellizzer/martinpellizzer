Aechmea ampullacea

Aechmea ampullacea, commonly known as the urn bromeliad, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces striking inflorescences with colorful bracts that can range from red to orange or yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas or near mangroves.

Aechmea ampullacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms.
