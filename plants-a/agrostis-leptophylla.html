Agrostis leptophylla

Agrostis leptophylla, commonly known as slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fine texture and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
