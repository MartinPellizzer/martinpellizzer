Anoectochilus flavescens

Anoectochilus flavescens, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, arranged in a loose spike. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
