Alstroemeria fluminensis

Alstroemeria fluminensis, commonly known as the river alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along riverbanks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that are often variegated with white and green markings.

Alstroemeria fluminensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
