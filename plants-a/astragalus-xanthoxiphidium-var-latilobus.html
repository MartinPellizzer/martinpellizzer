Astragalus xanthoxiphidium var. latilobus

Astragalus xanthoxiphidium var. latilobus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh conditions.

This variety is distinguished by its broader leaf lobes compared to other varieties of Astragalus.
