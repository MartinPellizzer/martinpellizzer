Acridocarpus graniticus

Acridocarpus graniticus, commonly known as the granite acacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stem and bipinnate leaves that are densely covered with small, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its resilience and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
