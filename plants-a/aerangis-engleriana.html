Aerangis engleriana

Aerangis engleriana, commonly known as the Engler's orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its intricate and showy floral structures. The plant typically features tall, slender stems with clustered inflorescences bearing numerous small, brightly colored flowers. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, providing a lush green backdrop to its ornamental blooms.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
