Aster chasei

Aster chasei, commonly known as the Chase aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, pink to purple flowers. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a hardy addition to xeriscaped gardens.

The Chase aster is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
