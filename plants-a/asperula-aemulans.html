Asperula aemulans

Asperula aemulans, commonly known as the mimic azalea, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and rhododendrons. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. Asperula aemulans prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant pink to red blooms, which resemble those of azaleas, hence its common name.
