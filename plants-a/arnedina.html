Arnedina

Arnedina, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red petals and a unique floral structure that attracts specific pollinators. Native to the tropical regions of South America, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Arnedina typically grows as an epiphytic plant, attaching itself to trees without drawing nutrients from them.

Its botanical features make it a prized specimen in both horticulture and botanical research.
