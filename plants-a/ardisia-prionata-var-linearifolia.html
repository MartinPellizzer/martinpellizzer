Ardisia prionata var. linearifolia

Ardisia prionata var. linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright blue berries, which are a key feature of the species. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various wildlife species.
