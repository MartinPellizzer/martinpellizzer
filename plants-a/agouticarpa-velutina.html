Agouticarpa velutina

Agouticarpa velutina, commonly known as the velvet fruit tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a velvety texture. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody capsule.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
