Astragalus lentiginosus var. platyphyllidium

Astragalus lentiginosus var. platyphyllidium, commonly known as the broad-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets and produces small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in drought conditions.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
