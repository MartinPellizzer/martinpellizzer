Asparagus laricinus

Asparagus laricinus, commonly known as the silver asparagus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in late spring, which are clustered in umbels. Its roots are fibrous and can form dense colonies, making it a hardy and resilient species.

Asparagus laricinus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.
