Acer pictum subsp. minshanicum

Acer pictum subsp.

minshanicum, commonly known as the Appalachian maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange foliage in the fall, which makes it a popular ornamental plant.

The tree has opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are typically 5-9 cm in length. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and appear in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The bark is smooth and grayish-white, becoming fissured with age.
