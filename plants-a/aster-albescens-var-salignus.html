Aster albescens var. salignus

Aster albescens var. salignus, commonly known as the white wood aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as stream banks and marshes. The plant features a tall, slender stem covered with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are white to pale blue, arranged in clusters at the top of the stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
