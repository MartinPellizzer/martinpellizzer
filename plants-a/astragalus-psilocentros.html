Astragalus psilocentros

Astragalus psilocentros, commonly known as the purple milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus psilocentros is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
