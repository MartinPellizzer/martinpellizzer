Anthemis callichroa

Anthemis callichroa, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature and vibrant color.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
