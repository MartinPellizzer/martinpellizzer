Acinodendron lucidum

Acinodendron lucidum, commonly known as the shining acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Acinodendron lucidum is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky outcrops.
