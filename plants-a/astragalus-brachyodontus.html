Astragalus brachyodontus

Astragalus brachyodontus, commonly known as the short-toothed milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep taproot system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus brachyodontus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
