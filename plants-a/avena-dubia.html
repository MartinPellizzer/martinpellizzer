Avena dubia

Avena dubia, commonly known as the slender oat, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Avena dubia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical studies.
