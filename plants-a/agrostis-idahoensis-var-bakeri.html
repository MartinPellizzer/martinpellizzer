Agrostis idahoensis var. bakeri

Agrostis idahoensis var. bakeri, commonly known as Idaho basketgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairless blades that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in open habitats such as meadows and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
