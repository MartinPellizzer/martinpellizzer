Armeria tenuis var. humilis

Armeria tenuis var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf sea thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to coastal regions of southern Europe and northern Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent, silver-gray foliage that is adapted to withstand salt spray and sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact size and hardy nature make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant ornamental plants.
