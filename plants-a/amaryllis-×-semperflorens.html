Amaryllis × semperflorens

Amaryllis × semperflorens, commonly known as the paperwhite narcissus, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis paradisiaca.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a slight yellowish hue at the base. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are broad and grass-like in appearance. It is typically grown as a bulbous plant, thriving in cool, moist conditions and often used in winter floral arrangements.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
