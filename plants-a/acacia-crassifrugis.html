Acacia crassifrugis

Acacia crassifrugis, commonly known as the broad-fruited acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow, globular inflorescences that attract pollinators. Its woody pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in arid environments.

Acacia crassifrugis is adapted to dry conditions, making it a resilient species in semi-desert ecosystems.
