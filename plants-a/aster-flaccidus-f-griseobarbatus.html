Aster flaccidus f. griseobarbatus

Aster flaccidus f. griseobarbatus, commonly known as the gray-haired flaccid aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, grayish hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, grayish pubescence, contributing to its unique botanical characteristics.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, woodland environments.
