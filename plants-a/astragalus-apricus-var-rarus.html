Astragalus apricus var. rarus

Astragalus apricus var. rarus, commonly known as the rare apricot milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, oval pods that contain several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
