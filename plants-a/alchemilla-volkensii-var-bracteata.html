Alchemilla volkensii var. bracteata

Alchemilla volkensii var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracted lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to East Africa.

It features basal rosettes of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in loose clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens with shady conditions.
