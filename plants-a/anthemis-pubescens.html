Anthemis pubescens

Anthemis pubescens, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, gray-green foliage and yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its structure and appearance make it a distinctive and recognizable species within its native range.
