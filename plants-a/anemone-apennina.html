Anemone apennina

Anemone apennina, commonly known as the Italian anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and basal leaves that are heart-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Anemone apennina is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
