Acinodendron pastoense

Acinodendron pastoense, commonly known as the Pasto acacia, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its dense foliage and ability to thrive in high-altitude environments. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.

Its hardy nature makes it a valuable plant for reforestation and soil stabilization efforts.
