Afrocarpus mannii

Afrocarpus mannii, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a large evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, often fissured bark. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and has a long lifespan, sometimes exceeding 1,000 years. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The wood of Afrocarpus mannii is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a prized material in the timber industry.
