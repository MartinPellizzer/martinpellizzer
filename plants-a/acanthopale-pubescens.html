Acanthopale pubescens

Acanthopale pubescens, commonly known as the hairy acanthopale, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features woody stems covered in dense, glandular hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture due to the presence of pubescence.

Acanthopale pubescens is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
