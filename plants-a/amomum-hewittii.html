Amomum hewittii

Amomum hewittii, commonly known as the Chinese ginger or wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and cardamom. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically pale green to white, with three sepals and three petals, and it produces small, yellowish fruits.

Amomum hewittii is valued for its aromatic rhizomes, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
