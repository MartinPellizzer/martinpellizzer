Acacia acanthophora

Acacia acanthophora, commonly known as the rough acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Acacia acanthophora is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
