Agave melanacantha

Agave melanacantha, commonly known as the black agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark green, spiny leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. Agave melanacantha is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its fibrous leaves have been historically used by indigenous peoples for various purposes, including rope-making and ceremonial uses.
