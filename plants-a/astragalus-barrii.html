Astragalus barrii

Astragalus barrii, commonly known as the Barri's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Astragalus barrii is adapted to thrive in poor soils and can tolerate extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar sources and habitat for insects in its environment.
