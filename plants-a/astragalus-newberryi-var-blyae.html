Astragalus newberryi var. blyae

Astragalus newberryi var. blyae, commonly known as Newberry's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains. It has small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in regions with low precipitation and is adapted to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
