Actaea racemosa var. racemosa

Actaea racemosa var. racemosa, commonly known as black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features a rhizomatous root system and tall, unbranched stems with large, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its berries are dark purple to black when mature and contain several seeds.

It is often found in rich, moist woodlands and is valued for its medicinal properties.
