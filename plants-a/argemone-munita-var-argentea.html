Argemone munita var. argentea

Argemone munita var. argentea, commonly known as silverleaf argemone, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, waxy hairs. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with five petals, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is occasionally cultivated in arid landscapes.
