Alnus mairei

Alnus mairei, commonly known as the Maire's alder, is a deciduous tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by cone-like fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Alnus mairei is often found in coastal and lowland areas, playing a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
