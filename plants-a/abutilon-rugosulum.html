Abutilon rugosulum

Abutilon rugosulum, commonly known as the rough abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features hairy, ovate leaves with a rough texture, and it produces showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers. Its fruits are capsules that split open to release small, hairy seeds.

Abutilon rugosulum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
