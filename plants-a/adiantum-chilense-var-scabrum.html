Adiantum chilense var. scabrum

Adiantum chilense var. scabrum, commonly known as the Chilean maidenhair fern, is a variety of the maidenhair fern family (Adiantaceae).

It is native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically green with a silvery underside. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
