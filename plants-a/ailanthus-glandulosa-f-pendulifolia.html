Ailanthus glandulosa f. pendulifolia

Ailanthus glandulosa f. pendulifolia, commonly known as the weeping tree of China, is a variant of the tree of heaven.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to China and has been introduced to various parts of the world, including the United States. It features compound leaves with a pinnate structure and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring.

This cultivar is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its unique form and adaptability to different soil conditions.
