Aerva lanata var. rotundifolia

Aerva lanata var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf aerva, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are ovate to round in shape, giving it its common name.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
