Abies delavayi var. forrestii

Abies delavayi var. forrestii, commonly known as the Yunnan fir, is a variant of the Delavay fir native to southwestern China.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its tall, slender form and dense, dark green foliage. The needles are flat, soft, and arranged in spirals along the branches, while the cones are typically pendulous and cylindrical. This species thrives in cool, humid climates at high altitudes, often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a component of temperate forest ecosystems and its ornamental value in horticulture.
