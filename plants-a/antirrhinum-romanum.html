Antirrhinum romanum

Antirrhinum romanum, commonly known as the Roman toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Antirrhinum romanum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
