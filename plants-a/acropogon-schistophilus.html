Acropogon schistophilus

Acropogon schistophilus, commonly known as the stone-loving grass, is a perennial plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fibrous root system, which helps it anchor in loose, nutrient-poor soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Acropogon schistophilus is adapted to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
