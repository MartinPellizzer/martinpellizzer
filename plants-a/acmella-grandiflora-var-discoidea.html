Acmella grandiflora var. discoidea

Acmella grandiflora var. discoidea, commonly known as the spurge lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often overlooked due to the showy bracts. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also known for its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions, making it a versatile choice for gardens.
