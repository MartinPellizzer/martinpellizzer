Aceratium cryptocaryifolium

Aceratium cryptocaryifolium, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant has basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The species is well-suited to rocky soils and can thrive in areas with minimal rainfall.
