Alagophylla dasyanthes

Alagophylla dasyanthes, commonly known as the desert rose or desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, storing water in its thick stems and leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a favored plant in both gardens and natural landscapes.
