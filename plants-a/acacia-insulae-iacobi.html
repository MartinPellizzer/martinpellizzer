Acacia insulae-iacobi

Acacia insulae-iacobi, commonly known as the island jacobi acacia, is a small shrub native to the island of Ile-de-France.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in compact, globular inflorescences, which attract a variety of pollinators. Its seed pods are elongated and woody, containing several elliptical seeds.

Acacia insulae-iacobi is adapted to rocky soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid regions.
