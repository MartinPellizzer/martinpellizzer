Allium gomphrenoides

Allium gomphrenoides, commonly known as the red onion, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bulbous stem and clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that range in color from purple to red, resembling those of the amaryllis. Allium gomphrenoides is valued for its culinary use, particularly in Mediterranean cuisines, where its bulbs are used for their pungent flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.
