Asplenium taeniosum

Asplenium taeniosum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense, dark brown layer of rhizome scales.

The fronds are usually evergreen, with a slender, arching habit that contributes to its ornamental appeal.
