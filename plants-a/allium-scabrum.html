Allium scabrum

Allium scabrum, commonly known as wild leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor.

Allium scabrum is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued both for its culinary uses and ornamental appeal.
