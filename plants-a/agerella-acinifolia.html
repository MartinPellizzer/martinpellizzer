Agerella acinifolia

Agerella acinifolia, commonly known as the red-leaf ageratum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Agerella acinifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
