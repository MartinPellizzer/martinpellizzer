Arundinella martinicensis

Arundinella martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique bamboo, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grasses. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Arundinella martinicensis thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands.
