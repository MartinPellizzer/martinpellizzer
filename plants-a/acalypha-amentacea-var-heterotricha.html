Acalypha amentacea var. heterotricha

Acalypha amentacea var. heterotricha, commonly known as the silky thorn-apple, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its finely haired, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
