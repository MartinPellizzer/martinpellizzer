Aphananthe monoica

Aphananthe monoica, commonly known as the silky oaks, is a deciduous tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are finely divided and give the plant a delicate, feathery appearance. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers that appear in late spring, followed by woody pods containing seeds. Aphananthe monoica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in reforestation efforts due to its adaptability.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a versatile addition to various landscapes.
