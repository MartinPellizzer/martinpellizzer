Artanthe schottii f. tomentosa

Artanthe schottii f. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly artanthe, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of pink and purple, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a desirable plant for both gardeners and botanists.
