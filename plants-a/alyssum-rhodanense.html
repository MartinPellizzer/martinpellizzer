Alyssum rhodanense

Alyssum rhodanense, commonly known as the rock Alyssum, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and profuse clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically features gray-green foliage with a fuzzy texture, and its flowers range in color from white to pink. Alyssum rhodanense thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
