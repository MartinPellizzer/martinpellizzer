Acanthophyllum tournefortii

Acanthophyllum tournefortii, commonly known as the crested sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to harsh desert environments, with deep root systems and reduced leaves to minimize water loss. Its growth habit and physical features make it an important component of sandy and gravelly habitats.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
