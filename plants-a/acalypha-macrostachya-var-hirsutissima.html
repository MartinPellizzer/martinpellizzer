Acalypha macrostachya var. hirsutissima

Acalypha macrostachya var. hirsutissima, commonly known as the woolly copperleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fuzzy, silver-gray foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, red bracts that resemble flowers, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
