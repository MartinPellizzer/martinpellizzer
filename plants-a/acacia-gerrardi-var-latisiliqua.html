Acacia gerrardi var. latisiliqua

Acacia gerrardi var. latisiliqua, commonly known as the broad-leaved acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage composed of narrow, green, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its hardy nature makes it suitable for erosion control and ornamental use in dry landscapes.

The wood is durable and has been traditionally used for various purposes by local communities.
