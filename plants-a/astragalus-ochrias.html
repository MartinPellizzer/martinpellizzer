Astragalus ochrias

Astragalus ochrias, commonly known as the pale milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Mongolia and China. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in racemes, and the leaves are compound, consisting of multiple pairs of leaflets.

Astragalus ochrias is often found in steppe and desert ecosystems, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
