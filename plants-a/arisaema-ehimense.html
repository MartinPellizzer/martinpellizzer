Arisaema ehimense

Arisaema ehimense, commonly known as the Ehimense Cucumber Plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces striking, waxy, green to yellowish leaves that are often ribbed and have a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are typically small and clustered on the spadix, with the spathe serving as a colorful, showy attractant for pollinators.

Arisaema ehimense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in moist, shaded environments.
