Aechmea perforata

Aechmea perforata, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients from the environment. The leaves are typically dark green with a waxy texture and often feature distinctive white or pale markings. It produces showy inflorescences with colorful bracts that range from red to orange, attracting pollinators.

This plant is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor conditions.

Morphology

Aechmea perforata has leaves that are sword-shaped, arranged in a rosette, with a dark green color and a waxy texture, and can reach lengths of up to 30 cm.

The plant has a short, thick stem with a smooth, pale green bark that grows upright from the base. Its flowers are clustered in a dense inflorescence, with bright red petals and yellow stamens, blooming during the dry season and pollinated by birds and insects. The fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the air and soil.
