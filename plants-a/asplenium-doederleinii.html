Asplenium doederleinii

Asplenium doederleinii, commonly known as the Doederlein's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a compact, rosette-like structure with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, and it produces spores rather than seeds.

Asplenium doederleinii is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in moist, shaded forest habitats.
