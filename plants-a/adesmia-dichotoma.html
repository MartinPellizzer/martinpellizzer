Adesmia dichotoma

Adesmia dichotoma, commonly known as the twin-stemmed burr legume, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and the plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions with deep roots.

Adesmia dichotoma plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
