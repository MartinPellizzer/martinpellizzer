Arundinaria siamensis

Arundinaria siamensis, commonly known as the Siam bamboo, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and arranged in a dense, clumping growth habit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and structural strength, it has been used in traditional construction and as a source of sustainable materials.
