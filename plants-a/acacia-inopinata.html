Acacia inopinata

Acacia inopinata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

Acacia inopinata is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
