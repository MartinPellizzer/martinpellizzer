Arisaema monbeigii

Arisaema monbeigii, commonly known as the Chinese cobra lily, is a herbaceous plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are typically yellowish-green, with a showy, hood-like spathe that serves to attract pollinators.

Arisaema monbeigii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
