Acacia nemu

Acacia nemu, commonly known as the black acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is distinguished by its dark, almost black bark and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. Its yellow flowers are clustered in spherical inflorescences, and it produces legume pods that contain seeds.

Acacia nemu is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
