Anacardium kuhlmannianum

Anacardium kuhlmannianum, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and produces clusters of small, green, spiny fruits. The tree's most notable feature is its edible seeds, which are encased in a hard, toxic shell. The plant belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other species like the cashew and mango.

It plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat and is also valued for its economic importance.
