Aubrieta intermedia f. macedonica

Aubrieta intermedia f. macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian aubrieta, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to the Balkan region.

It features dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has silver-gray foliage that is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
