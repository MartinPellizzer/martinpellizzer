Aster acris

Aster acris, commonly known as the common blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
