Acantholimon talagonicum var. microstegium

Acantholimon talagonicum var. microstegium, commonly known as the small-stemmed sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its silver-gray foliage and distinctive, spiny leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and wind exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in coastal ecosystems.
