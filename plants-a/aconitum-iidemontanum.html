Aconitum iidemontanum

Aconitum iidemontanum, commonly known as the Eastern monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, doubly serrated leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
