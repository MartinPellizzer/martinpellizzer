Adicea consanguinea

Adicea consanguinea, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and clustered, showy flowers. The plant typically produces violet or purple flowers with five petals, often accompanied by bright yellow stamens. Adicea consanguinea thrives in warm, humid environments and is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and indirect sunlight for optimal growth.
