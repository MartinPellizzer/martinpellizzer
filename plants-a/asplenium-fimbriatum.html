Asplenium fimbriatum

Asplenium fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices or forest floors. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped fronds with finely toothed margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium fimbriatum is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
