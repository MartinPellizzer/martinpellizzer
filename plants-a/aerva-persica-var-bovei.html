Aerva persica var. bovei

Aerva persica var. bovei, commonly known as the Persian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its deep root system. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, helping to conserve water.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
