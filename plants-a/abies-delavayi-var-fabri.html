Abies delavayi var. fabri

Abies delavayi var. fabri, commonly known as the Yunnan fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The cones are typically cylindrical and contain two seeds per scale, contributing to its reproductive strategy. This species thrives in cool, moist climates at high elevations, often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the alpine flora in its native habitat.
