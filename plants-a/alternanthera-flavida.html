Alternanthera flavida

Alternanthera flavida, commonly known as the golden Alternanthera, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and can vary in color from green to reddish depending on the light conditions.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and water features.
