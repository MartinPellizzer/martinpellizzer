Acacia hanniana

Acacia hanniana, commonly known as the white acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stem and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow to golden-yellow inflorescences, which are clusters of small, fragrant flowers. Acacia hanniana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
