Adenia vitifolia

Adenia vitifolia, commonly known as the grapevine tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its woody stem and compound leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with five sepals and five petals, often in shades of white, yellow, or red. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellow or orange berry that contains multiple seeds.

Adenia vitifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
