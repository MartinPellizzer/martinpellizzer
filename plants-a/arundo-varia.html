Arundo varia

Arundo varia, commonly known as the variable reed, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant features narrow, linear leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, greenish flowers in panicle clusters. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found near water bodies.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to tolerate both wet and dry conditions.
