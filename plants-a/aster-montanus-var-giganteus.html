Aster montanus var. giganteus

Aster montanus var. giganteus, commonly known as the giant blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to heights of 3 to 6 feet, featuring upright stems and narrow, gray-green leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
