Andropogon saccharoides var. perforatus

Andropogon saccharoides var. perforatus, commonly known as the punctuated bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
