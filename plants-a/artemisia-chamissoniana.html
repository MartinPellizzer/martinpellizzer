Artemisia chamissoniana

Artemisia chamissoniana, commonly known as Chamisson's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves contain volatile oils that contribute to its strong, minty scent.

Artemisia chamissoniana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife.
