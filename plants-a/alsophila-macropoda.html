Alsophila macropoda

Alsophila macropoda, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its spores are produced in clusters on the undersides of its fronds, facilitating reproduction in moist conditions.

Alsophila macropoda plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing habitat and contributing to forest structure.
