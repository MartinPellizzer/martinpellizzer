Anabasis bamianica

Anabasis bamianica, commonly known as the Afghan sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellow or greenish flowers that bloom in summer, and its fleshy leaves are adapted to store water, aiding survival in dry environments. Anabasis bamianica is often found in sandy soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its resilience to harsh conditions makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects in arid regions.
