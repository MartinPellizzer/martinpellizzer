Astragalus ammophilus subsp. persepolitanus

Astragalus ammophilus subsp.

persepolitanus, commonly known as Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves.

The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct ecological and morphological traits compared to other Astragalus species.
