Acalypha kerrii

Acalypha kerrii, commonly known as the Kerri's spurflower, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that range in color from red to orange, often mistaken for flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Acalypha kerrii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
