Achoriphragma lancifolium

Achoriphragma lancifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. It is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
