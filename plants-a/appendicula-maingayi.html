Appendicula maingayi

Appendicula maingayi, commonly known as the Malagasy bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in areas with high humidity. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs.
