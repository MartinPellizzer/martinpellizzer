Argentina anserina

Argentina anserina, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with a dense, rounded crown. The leaves are leathery, dark green on top, and silvery white underneath, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, red berries.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
