Agave potosina

Agave potosina, commonly known as the Potosi agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with white marginal teeth. It produces a tall flowering stalk that bears yellow flowers, which are followed by small, white berries.

Agave potosina is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
