Ancistrothyrsus tessmannii

Ancistrothyrsus tessmannii, commonly known as the African firethorn, is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, spiny branches and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
