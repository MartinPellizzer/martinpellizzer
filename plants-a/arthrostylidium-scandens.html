Arthrostylidium scandens

Arthrostylidium scandens, commonly known as the climbing arthrostylidium, is a tropical vine native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that allow it to climb and spread across supports. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Arthrostylidium scandens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
