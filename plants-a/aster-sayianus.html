Aster sayianus

Aster sayianus, commonly known as the Sayian aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to high altitudes. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Aster sayianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
