Ancipitia condorensis

Ancipitia condorensis, commonly known as the Peruvian orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the rocky soil, and it has a unique symbiotic relationship with specific mycorrhizal fungi.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Ancipitia condorensis is classified as endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
