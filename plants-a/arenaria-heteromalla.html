Arenaria heteromalla

Arenaria heteromalla, commonly known as the sandpink, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to arid conditions.

It is often found in coastal dunes and other dry, open habitats.
