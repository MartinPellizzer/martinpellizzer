Acuan reticulatum

Acuan reticulatum, commonly known as the net-veined acuan, is a unique species within the Acuanales order.

It is characterized by its intricate, net-like venation pattern on its leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth, glossy surface.

Acuan reticulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
