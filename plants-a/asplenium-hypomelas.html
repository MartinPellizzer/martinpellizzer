Asplenium hypomelas

Asplenium hypomelas, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves, which are arranged in a distinctive pattern. Asplenium hypomelas thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.
