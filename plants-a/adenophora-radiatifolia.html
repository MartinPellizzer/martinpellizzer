Adenophora radiatifolia

Adenophora radiatifolia, commonly known as the radiate leaf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features striking, radiating leaves that give it its common name, and it produces clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its foliage is characterized by broad, lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

Adenophora radiatifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
