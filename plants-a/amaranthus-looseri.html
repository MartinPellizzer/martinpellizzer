Amaranthus looseri

Amaranthus looseri, commonly known as the redroot amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to the southern United States and northern Mexico, where it typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, lance-shaped leaves, and vibrant red or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking color and drought tolerance.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential use in phytoremediation due to its ability to absorb heavy metals from the soil.
