Abies nigra var. pumila

Abies nigra var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf fir, is a compact evergreen conifer native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slow growth rate and dense, pyramidal shape, making it well-suited for rock gardens or as a specimen plant. The foliage consists of dark green, needle-like leaves that are short and stiff, providing a textured appearance. It produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and it is relatively cold-hardy, thriving in USDA hardiness zones 3 to 7.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

Native Distribution

It is found in regions of North Carolina, Tennessee, Virginia, and West Virginia. This variety of the eastern white fir thrives in cool, moist, and well-drained soils. It is commonly found in mixed hardwood forests and along stream valleys.

Its native range is limited to specific elevations and microclimates within these areas.
