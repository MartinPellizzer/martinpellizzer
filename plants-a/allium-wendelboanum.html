Allium wendelboanum

Allium wendelboanum, commonly known as the Wendelbo's garlic, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, particularly in Nepal and India. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a bulbous base similar to other allium species.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
