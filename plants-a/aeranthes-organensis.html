Aeranthes organensis

Aeranthes organensis, commonly known as the Organ Mountains orchid, is a rare and endemic species found in the Organ Mountains of New Mexico.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, delicate flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky crevices and has adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, showy blooms during the spring season.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
