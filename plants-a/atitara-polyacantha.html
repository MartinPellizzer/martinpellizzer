Atitara polyacantha

Atitara polyacantha, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive branched inflorescences that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant produces small, showy flowers in a range of colors, including red, pink, and orange, which bloom throughout the year in some regions. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Atitara polyacantha is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
