Arundinella hispida var. glabrivaginata

Arundinella hispida var. glabrivaginata, commonly known as the smooth-barked bamboo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are smooth and often clustered in tufts. The plant belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight pubescence on the lower surface.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
