Acacia longipedunculata

Acacia longipedunculata, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its long, slender pod-like fruits and compound leaves with small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

Acacia longipedunculata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
