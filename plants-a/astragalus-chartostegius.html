Astragalus chartostegius

Astragalus chartostegius, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is adapted to grow in dry, rocky habitats.

Astragalus chartostegius is often found in alpine meadows and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
