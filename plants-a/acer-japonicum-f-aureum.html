Acer japonicum f. aureum

Acer japonicum f. aureum, commonly known as the golden Japanese maple, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree.

It is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow foliage that turns red in the fall. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree with a compact growth habit. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a delicate, feathery appearance.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens.
