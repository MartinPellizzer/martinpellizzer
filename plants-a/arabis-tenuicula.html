Arabis tenuicula

Arabis tenuicula, commonly known as narrowleaf rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features basal leaves that are narrow and linear. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Arabis tenuicula thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and delicate appearance.
