Agrostis alba var. conferta

Agrostis alba var. conferta, commonly known as silvergrass or whitegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Agrostis alba var. conferta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its delicate appearance.
