Aristida latzii

Aristida latzii, commonly known as the slender dropseed, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and compact, grass-like growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, pale green or brownish flowers in summer, which are arranged in narrow, spike-like inflorescences.

Aristida latzii is often used in native plant gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
