Anthemis khorasanica

Anthemis khorasanica, commonly known as the Persian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk, typical of the family. It is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine.

Its foliage and flowers contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
