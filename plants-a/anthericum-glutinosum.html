Anthericum glutinosum

Anthericum glutinosum, commonly known as sticky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the ends of its stems. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, ponds, and stream banks.

Anthericum glutinosum is notable for its sticky secretions on its leaves and stems, which give it its common name.
