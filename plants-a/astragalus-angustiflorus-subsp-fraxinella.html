Astragalus angustiflorus subsp. fraxinella

Astragalus angustiflorus subsp.

fraxinella, commonly known as the narrow-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky, or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands and sagebrush steppe.

The plant features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces yellow to pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects.

The species is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.
