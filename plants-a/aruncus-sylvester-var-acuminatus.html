Aruncus sylvester var. acuminatus

Aruncus sylvester var. acuminatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf goat's beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of white to pale pink bracts, which are modified leaves that give the plant its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
