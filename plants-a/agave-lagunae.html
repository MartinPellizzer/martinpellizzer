Agave lagunae

Agave lagunae, commonly known as the Lagunae agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. Its leaves are typically blue-gray in color with sharp, spiny margins, providing both aesthetic appeal and a natural defense mechanism.

Agave lagunae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sunlight.
