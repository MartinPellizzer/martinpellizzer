Alsodeia maingayi

Alsodeia maingayi, commonly known as the African sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention in arid environments.

Alsodeia maingayi is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
