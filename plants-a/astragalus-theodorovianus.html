Astragalus theodorovianus

Astragalus theodorovianus, commonly known as the Karakorum milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.

Its limited distribution makes it a subject of conservation concern in its native habitat.
