Aubrieta elwendica

Aubrieta elwendica, commonly known as the Welsh rock jasmine, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Wales.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Aubrieta elwendica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to flourish in harsh, mountainous environments.
