Andromeda ligustrina

Andromeda ligustrina, commonly known as the sweetfern, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage that emits a pleasant, spicy scent. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, which are clustered in spikes. Its leaves are narrow, opposite, and have a waxy appearance.

Andromeda ligustrina is adapted to moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetland environments.
