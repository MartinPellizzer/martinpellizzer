Aniba ridleyana

Aniba ridleyana, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

Aniba ridleyana is often found in lowland and hill forests, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
