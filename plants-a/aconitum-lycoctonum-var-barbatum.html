Aconitum lycoctonum var. barbatum

Aconitum lycoctonum var. barbatum, commonly known as the beardroot aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust root system and produces tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its large, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply cut lobes that give it a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

This variety is known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that make it dangerous to both humans and animals.
