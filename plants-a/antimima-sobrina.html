Antimima sobrina

Antimima sobrina, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

The tree is also known for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a valuable but endangered species in its native habitat.
