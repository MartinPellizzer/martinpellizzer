Acacia xylocarpa var. tenuissima

Acacia xylocarpa var. tenuissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, narrow leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

This variety is often used in revegetation projects due to its hardiness and ecological benefits.
