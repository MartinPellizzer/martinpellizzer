Ageratina huehueteca

Ageratina huehueteca, commonly known as sacred blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to blue florets that are often clustered together in a compact, mist-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ageratina huehueteca is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in indigenous cultures.
