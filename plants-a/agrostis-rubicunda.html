Agrostis rubicunda

Agrostis rubicunda, commonly known as red hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and sandy soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 60 cm, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple hues.

Agrostis rubicunda is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
