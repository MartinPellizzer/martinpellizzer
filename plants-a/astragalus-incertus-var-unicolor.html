Astragalus incertus var. unicolor

Astragalus incertus var. unicolor, commonly known as the single-colored milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, and it produces small, legume-like pods that contain seeds.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
