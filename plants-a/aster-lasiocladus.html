Aster lasiocladus

Aster lasiocladus, commonly known as the woolly aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Aster lasiocladus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
