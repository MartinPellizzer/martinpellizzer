Acinos erectus

Acinos erectus, commonly known as the upright acornweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

Acinos erectus is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
