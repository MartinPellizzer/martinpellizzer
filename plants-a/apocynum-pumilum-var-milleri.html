Apocynum pumilum var. milleri

Apocynum pumilum var. milleri, commonly known as the small milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure.

The leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a fuzzy texture, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.
