Aizoon acutifolium

Aizoon acutifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aizoon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and summer months. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported diuretic properties.

Aizoon acutifolium thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
