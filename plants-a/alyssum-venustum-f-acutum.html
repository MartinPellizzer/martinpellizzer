Alyssum venustum f. acutum

Alyssum venustum f. acutum, commonly known as the sharp-leaved alissum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often sharply pointed, giving it its common name. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Alyssum venustum f. acutum is often cultivated as a garden plant for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
