Aspidopterys glabriuscula var. brevicuspis

Aspidopterys glabriuscula var. brevicuspis, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive, smooth, grayish-brown stem with a fibrous, spongy texture. Its fronds are bipinnate, with short, blunt spines along the leaflets, giving it a unique appearance.

This species is native to New Zealand and thrives in humid, temperate climates with well-drained soil.
