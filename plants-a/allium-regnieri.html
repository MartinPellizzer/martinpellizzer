Allium regnieri

Allium regnieri, commonly known as the Regnier's onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round bulbs that are adapted to survive in harsh, arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Allium regnieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
