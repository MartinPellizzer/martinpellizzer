Adesmia muricata

Adesmia muricata, commonly known as the rough adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in arid regions of Argentina and Chile.

Adesmia muricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
