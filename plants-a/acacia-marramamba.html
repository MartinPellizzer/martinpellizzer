Acacia marramamba

Acacia marramamba, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or pale yellow flowers clustered in spherical inflorescences. It is often found in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
