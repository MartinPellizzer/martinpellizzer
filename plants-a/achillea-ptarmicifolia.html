Achillea ptarmicifolia

Achillea ptarmicifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features slender, gray-green leaves with a distinctive serrated edge, and it produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their anti-inflammatory and astringent effects.
