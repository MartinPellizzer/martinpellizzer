Acalypha peduncularis var. crassa

Acalypha peduncularis var. crassa, commonly known as the broadleaf spurge, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically broad and ovate, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on slender, branching stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its rapid growth and spreading habit, it can become invasive in certain regions.
