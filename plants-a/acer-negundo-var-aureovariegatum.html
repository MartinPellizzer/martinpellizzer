Acer negundo var. aureovariegatum

Acer negundo var. aureovariegatum, commonly known as the golden variegated boxelder, is a variant of the boxelder tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, featuring a mix of yellow, white, and green on its leaves. The plant has opposite, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant colors in the fall. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by winged samaras for seed dispersal.

This ornamental tree is often cultivated for its unique coloration and adaptability to various soil conditions.
