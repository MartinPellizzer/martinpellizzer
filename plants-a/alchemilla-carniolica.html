Alchemilla carniolica

Alchemilla carniolica, commonly known as the Carpathian lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Alchemilla carniolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
