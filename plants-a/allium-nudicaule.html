Allium nudicaule

Allium nudicaule, commonly known as the slender garlic or alpine garlic, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Allium nudicaule is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild garlic-like scent.
