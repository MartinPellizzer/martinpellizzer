Aesculus × mutabilis

Aesculus × mutabilis, commonly known as the horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white or pink bracts that resemble flowers, which appear in late spring. The tree produces large, spiny capsules containing shiny, chestnut-like seeds. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are glossy and ovate in shape.

The horse chestnut is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in parks and along streets for its aesthetic appeal.
