Avena koenigii

Avena koenigii, commonly known as cumin.

It is an annual herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia. The plant produces small, white to light green flowers and has feathery, aromatic leaves. Its seeds, which are the primary part used in cooking, are harvested when dry and are known for their warm, earthy flavor.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular crop in temperate climates.
