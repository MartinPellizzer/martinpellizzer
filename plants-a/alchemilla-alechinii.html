Alchemilla alechinii

Alchemilla alechinii, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla alechinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
