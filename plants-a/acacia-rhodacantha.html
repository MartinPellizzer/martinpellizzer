Acacia rhodacantha

Acacia rhodacantha, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bright red flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Acacia rhodacantha is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
