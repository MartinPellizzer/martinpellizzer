Athenaea hirsuta

Athenaea hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy athenaea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
