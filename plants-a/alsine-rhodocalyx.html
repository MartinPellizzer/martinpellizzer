Alsine rhodocalyx

Alsine rhodocalyx, commonly known as the red-flowered alsine, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences that produce vibrant red or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it often forms dense mats or low shrubs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
