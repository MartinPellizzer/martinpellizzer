Allium pseudobulbiferum

Allium pseudobulbiferum, commonly known as the bulbiferous garlic, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its clustered, bulb-like structures that resemble small onions. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in umbels. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
