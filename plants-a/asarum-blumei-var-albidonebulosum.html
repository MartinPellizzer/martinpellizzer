Asarum blumei var. albidonebulosum

Asarum blumei var. albidonebulosum, commonly known as the white-smoke asarum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and distinctive, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a misty appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
