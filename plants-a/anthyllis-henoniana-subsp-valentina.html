Anthyllis henoniana subsp. valentina

Anthyllis henoniana subsp.

valentina, commonly known as the Valentina broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and bright yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in suitable climates.
