Ancistrum decumbens

Ancistrum decumbens, commonly known as the creeping orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as undergrowth in rainforests. The plant features a slender, creeping rhizome with numerous erect, hairy stems. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often arranged in a loose raceme.

Ancistrum decumbens is notable for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its unique growth habit.
