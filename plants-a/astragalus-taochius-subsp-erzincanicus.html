Astragalus taochius subsp. erzincanicus

Astragalus taochius subsp.

erzincanicus, commonly known as the Erzincan milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Turkey. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces pod-like fruits containing several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
