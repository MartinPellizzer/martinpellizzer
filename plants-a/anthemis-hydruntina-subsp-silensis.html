Anthemis hydruntina subsp. silensis

Anthemis hydruntina subsp.

silensis, commonly known as the Sicilian tansy, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive, aromatic scent.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or dry soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in parts of southern Europe.
