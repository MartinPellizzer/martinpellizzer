Acacia accola

Acacia accola, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellowish-green globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. Acacia accola is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic and is used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.
