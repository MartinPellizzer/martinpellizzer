Alchemilla macra

Alchemilla macra, commonly known as the large lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its silver-green, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Alchemilla macra is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
