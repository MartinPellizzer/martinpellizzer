Arenaria macradenia var. arcuifolia

Arenaria macradenia var. arcuifolia, commonly known as the bent-leaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky or sandy soils in arid regions.

It features a compact rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are often curved or arched, giving it the specific epithet "arcuifolia.".

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in difficult garden conditions.
