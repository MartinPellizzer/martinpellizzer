Aster orcuttii

Aster orcuttii, commonly known as the Orcutt's aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster orcuttii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
