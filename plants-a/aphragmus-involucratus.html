Aphragmus involucratus

Aphragmus involucratus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs.

It is native to regions of Central and South America and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by round, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. Aphragmus involucratus is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
