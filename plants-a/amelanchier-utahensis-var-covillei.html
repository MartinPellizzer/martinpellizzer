Amelanchier utahensis var. covillei

Amelanchier utahensis var. covillei, commonly known as the Utah serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils. The plant produces white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it exhibits a distinctive rounded or spreading growth habit.

The Utah serviceberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for wildlife and supporting local biodiversity.
