Apargia hieracioides

Apargia hieracioides, commonly known as the hairy willow-herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as stream banks, wet meadows, and marshy areas. The plant features opposite, hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs in the spring to early summer, and it is often found in association with other wetland flora.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
