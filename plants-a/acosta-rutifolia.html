Acosta rutifolia

Acosta rutifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf acosta, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes coffee and gardenias, and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, red berries. Its leaves are typically rough or hairy, giving it its common name, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Acosta rutifolia is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
