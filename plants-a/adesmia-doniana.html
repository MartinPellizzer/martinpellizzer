Adesmia doniana

Adesmia doniana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Adesmia doniana is often found in disturbed habitats and can be an invasive species in some areas.
