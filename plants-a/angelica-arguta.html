Angelica arguta

Angelica arguta, commonly known as the Siberian angelica, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring large, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in an umbel, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, containing compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

Angelica arguta is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
