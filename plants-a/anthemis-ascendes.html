Anthemis ascendes

Anthemis ascendes, commonly known as the ascending dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, typical of the genus Anthemis. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
