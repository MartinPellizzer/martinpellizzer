Agapetes speciosa

Agapetes speciosa, commonly known as the showy agapete, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters during the spring. Agapetes speciosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for botanical gardens and specialized horticulture.
