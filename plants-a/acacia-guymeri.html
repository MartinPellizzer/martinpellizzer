Acacia guymeri

Acacia guymeri, commonly known as the Guymer's wattle, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and compound leaves composed of numerous small, paired leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Acacia guymeri is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with deep root systems and waxy leaf coatings to reduce water loss.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
