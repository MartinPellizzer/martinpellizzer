Acaena sanguisorbae var. minor

Acaena sanguisorbae var. minor, commonly known as the small-flowered burrweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, red-brown bracts that form spiny, seed-bearing structures called achenes. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is part of the genus Acaena, which is known for its distinctive burr-like fruits and ecological importance in native ecosystems.
