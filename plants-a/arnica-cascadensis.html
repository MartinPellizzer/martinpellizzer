Arnica cascadensis

Arnica cascadensis, commonly known as the Cascade arnica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and typically grows in rocky, alpine environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with dark orange centers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Arnica cascadensis is valued for its medicinal properties, though it is often used in small quantities due to its potency.
