Astragalus megalomerus

Astragalus megalomerus, commonly known as the giant milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and palmately divided leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Astragalus megalomerus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
