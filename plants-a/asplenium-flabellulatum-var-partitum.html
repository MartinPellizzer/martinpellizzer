Asplenium flabellulatum var. partitum

Asplenium flabellulatum var. partitum, commonly known as the divided maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
