Arcytophyllum nitidum subsp. caracasanum

Arcytophyllum nitidum subsp.

caracasanum, commonly known as the Caracasanum sage, is a perennial shrub native to the high altitudes of Central Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun exposure.

Its essential oils are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.
