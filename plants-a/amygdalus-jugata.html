Amygdalus jugata

Amygdalus jugata, commonly known as the Persian almond, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including Iran, Afghanistan, and Central Asia. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers and is characterized by its oval, leathery leaves. Its fruits are small, hard, and contain a single seed, similar to other members of the Prunus genus.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
