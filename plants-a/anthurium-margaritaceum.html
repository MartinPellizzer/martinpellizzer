Anthurium margaritaceum

Anthurium margaritaceum, commonly known as the pearl anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces bright red spathes that enclose its small, yellowish-white spadices, creating a visually appealing display. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium margaritaceum is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value.
