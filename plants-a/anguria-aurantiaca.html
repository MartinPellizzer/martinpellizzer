Anguria aurantiaca

Anguria aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange melon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems.

This plant produces large, orange-colored fruits with a sweet, aromatic flesh that is often used in culinary applications. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with five petals. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities due to its vibrant fruit and foliage.
