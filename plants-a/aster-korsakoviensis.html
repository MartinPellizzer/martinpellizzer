Aster korsakoviensis

Aster korsakoviensis, commonly known as the Korsakov aster, is a perennial herb native to the Far East of Russia and parts of northern China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountain slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.
