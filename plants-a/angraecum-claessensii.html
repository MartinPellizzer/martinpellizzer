Angraecum claessensii

Angraecum claessensii, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom at night. The plant produces showy, white to pale blue flowers with intricate patterns, adapted for pollination by moths. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Angraecum claessensii is a popular species among orchid enthusiasts due to its beauty and unique blooming habits.
