Argusia

Argusia, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the olive and jasmine. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that releases seeds when mature.

Argusia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
