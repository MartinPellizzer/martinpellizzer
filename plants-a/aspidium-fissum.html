Aspidium fissum

Aspidium fissum, commonly known as the split brake fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are divided into narrow, linear segments, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in sporangia, which are clustered in sori.

Aspidium fissum is a hardy plant that can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
