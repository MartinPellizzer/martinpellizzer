Alsophila furcinervia

Alsophila furcinervia, commonly known as the golden tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bright green, with a slightly golden hue on the underside, contributing to its common name.

Alsophila furcinervia is often found in tropical rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
