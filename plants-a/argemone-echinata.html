Argemone echinata

Argemone echinata, commonly known as the spiny pricklypoppy, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and spiny, hairy stems. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain oily seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain regions.
