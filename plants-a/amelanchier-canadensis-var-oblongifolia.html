Amelanchier canadensis var. oblongifolia

Amelanchier canadensis var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the Canadian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet berries in the summer. Its botanical features include compound inflorescences and a preference for well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
