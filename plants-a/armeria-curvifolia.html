Armeria curvifolia

Armeria curvifolia, commonly known as the bentleaf thrift, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is a member of the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its dense, low-growing clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often curled or twisted, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal gardens.

Armeria curvifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
