Atossa sepium var. subrotunda

Atossa sepium var. subrotunda, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sea holly, is a coastal plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curled. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in maritime environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in coastal gardens.
