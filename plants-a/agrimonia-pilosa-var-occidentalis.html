Agrimonia pilosa var. occidentalis

Agrimonia pilosa var. occidentalis, commonly known as western agrimony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. The plant features upright stems with opposite, pinnately compound leaves that have serrated edges. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and appear in clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its astringent and anti-inflammatory effects.
