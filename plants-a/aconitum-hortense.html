Aconitum hortense

Aconitum hortense, commonly known as the garden aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features a tall, upright stem with palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.

Morphology

Aconitum hortense has compound, pinnate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a serrated edge and a rough texture.

The stems are upright, hairy, and have a reddish-brown bark that becomes darker with age. Its flowers are blue to purple, bell-shaped, and arranged in dense spikes, blooming in late summer to early autumn, and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a follicle that splits open to release small, dark seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are thick, fibrous, and adapted for absorbing nutrients from the soil.
