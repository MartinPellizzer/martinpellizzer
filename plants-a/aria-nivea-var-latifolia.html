Aria nivea var. latifolia

Aria nivea var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf snowbell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a subtle elegance to its foliage. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It prefers well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
