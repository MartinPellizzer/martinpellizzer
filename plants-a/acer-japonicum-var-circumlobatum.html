Acer japonicum var. circumlobatum

Acer japonicum var. circumlobatum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with variegated leaves, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that display a striking combination of green, white, and pink variegation. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters, with a rounded, compact form. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in early spring before the leaves fully emerge.

The foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
