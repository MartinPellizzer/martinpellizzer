Apluda varia subsp. mutica

Apluda varia subsp.

mutica, commonly known as the slender apluda, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and marshes.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually pale green in color. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
