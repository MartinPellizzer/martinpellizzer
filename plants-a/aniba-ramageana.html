Aniba ramageana

Aniba ramageana, commonly known as the Ramage's aniba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, waxy texture. It produces small, clustered white flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

The tree is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
