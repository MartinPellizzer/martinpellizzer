Alchemilla aphanoides var. subalpestris

Alchemilla aphanoides var. subalpestris, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, shield-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, moss-like appearance. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Alchemilla aphanoides var. subalpestris is well adapted to alpine environments, exhibiting frost resistance and drought tolerance.
