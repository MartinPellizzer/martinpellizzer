Avena filifolia var. velutina

Avena filifolia var. velutina, commonly known as velvet oat, is a variant of the common oat species characterized by its fine, silky hairs that give it a velvety texture.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist, temperate regions, often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Velvet oat is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
