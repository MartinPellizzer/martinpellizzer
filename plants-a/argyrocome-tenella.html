Argyrocome tenella

Argyrocome tenella, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of silver-gray, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy, white seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or xeriscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
