Astragalus staintonianus

Astragalus staintonianus, commonly known as the Stainton's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
