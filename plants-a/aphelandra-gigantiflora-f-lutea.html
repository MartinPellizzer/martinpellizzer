Aphelandra gigantiflora f. lutea

Aphelandra gigantiflora f. lutea, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a hybrid cultivar of the genus Aphelandra, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences with bright yellow bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

This ornamental plant is popular in tropical and subtropical gardens for its vibrant foliage and unique appearance.
