Artemisia seriphium var. sessiliflora

Artemisia seriphium var. sessiliflora, commonly known as narrowleaf wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray foliage with finely divided, linear leaves that are typically sessile, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in compact clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant.
