Abronia pedunculata

Abronia pedunculata, commonly known as the coastal sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salinity and strong winds. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping it retain moisture in arid conditions.

Abronia pedunculata plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native insects and birds.
