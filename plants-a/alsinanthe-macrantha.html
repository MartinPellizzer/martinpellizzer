Alsinanthe macrantha

Alsinanthe macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered alsinane, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of showy, pink to purple bracteate flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Alsinanthe macrantha is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display.
