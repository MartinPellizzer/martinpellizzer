Acacia verticillata subsp. verticillata

Acacia verticillata subsp.

verticillata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, fern-like leaves that are arranged in whorls of three to five.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing sandy soils.
