Armeria sabulosa var. serpentini

Armeria sabulosa var. serpentini, commonly known as the serpent's head sandplant, is a low-growing, perennial plant native to coastal areas of southern California.

It features dense clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh coastal conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of salt-tolerant hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing dunes.
