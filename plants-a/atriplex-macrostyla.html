Atriplex macrostyla

Atriplex macrostyla, commonly known as the broadleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its gray-green, succulent leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it well-suited for reclamation of saline soils.

Atriplex macrostyla plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
