Astragalus ihsancalisii

Astragalus ihsancalisii, commonly known as the Ihsan's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
