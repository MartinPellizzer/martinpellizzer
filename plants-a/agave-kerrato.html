Agave kerrato

Agave kerrato, commonly known as the Kerrato agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often bluish-gray in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing clusters of yellowish-green flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it drought-tolerant.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and for making fibers, showcasing its versatility in both ecological and cultural contexts.
