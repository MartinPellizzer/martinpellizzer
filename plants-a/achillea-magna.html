Achillea magna

Achillea magna, commonly known as giant yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It features a robust, upright stem that can grow up to 2 meters tall, adorned with feathery, finely divided leaves. The plant produces flat-topped clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and can spread extensively, making it a persistent weed in disturbed soils.

Achillea magna is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
