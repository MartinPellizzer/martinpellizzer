Aspidium roseum

Aspidium roseum, commonly known as the rose fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically green with a silvery or pale underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including India and China. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Aspidium roseum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
