Acanthocereus paradoxus

Acanthocereus paradoxus, commonly known as the night blooming cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a small, columnar cactus with a ribbed stem and spines that provide protection against herbivores. The plant is notable for its striking, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its adaptations to extreme desert conditions include a thick, water-storing stem and reduced leaves.

Acanthocereus paradoxus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
