Acacia pseudonigrescens

Acacia pseudonigrescens, commonly known as the blackwood acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark, which gives it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical inflorescences, which are often pollinated by bees and birds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, thread-like leaflets.

Acacia pseudonigrescens is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.
