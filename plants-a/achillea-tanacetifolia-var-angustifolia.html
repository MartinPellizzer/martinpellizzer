Achillea tanacetifolia var. angustifolia

Achillea tanacetifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with dark green, finely divided, fern-like leaves that are narrow and linear. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale yellow, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and long flowering period make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
