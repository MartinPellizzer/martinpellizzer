Androrchis quadripunctata

Androrchis quadripunctata, commonly known as the four-spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and have a distinctive four-spotted pattern on the dorsal sepal.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
