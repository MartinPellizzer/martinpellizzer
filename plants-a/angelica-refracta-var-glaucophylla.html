Angelica refracta var. glaucophylla

Angelica refracta var. glaucophylla, commonly known as the glaucophyll angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant features large, pinnately compound leaves with a bluish-green underside, giving it its varietal name. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clustered umbels, and it produces oval, dark brown seeds.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
