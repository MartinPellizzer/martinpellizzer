Aspidium montevidense f. genuina

Aspidium montevidense f. genuina, commonly known as the Montevidense water fern, is a fern species native to South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its spores are released from sori located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining soil stability.
