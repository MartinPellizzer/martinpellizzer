Aucuba chinensis subsp. omeiensis

Aucuba chinensis subsp.

omeiensis, commonly known as the Chinese aucuba, is a shrub native to China. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that remain evergreen throughout the year.

The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
