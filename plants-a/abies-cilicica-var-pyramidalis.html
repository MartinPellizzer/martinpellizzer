Abies cilicica var. pyramidalis

Abies cilicica var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the Caucasian fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountains of Turkey and the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dense foliage, and needle-like leaves that are dark green and slightly twisted. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in mountain ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
