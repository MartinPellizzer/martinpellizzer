Aeschynomene tenuirama

Aeschynomene tenuirama, commonly known as slenderleaf aeschynomene, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Aeschynomene tenuirama is also noted for its potential use in soil stabilization and as a forage plant.
