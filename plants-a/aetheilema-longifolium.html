Aetheilema longifolium

Aetheilema longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh habitats in North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are elongated and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in areas with standing water or saturated ground.
