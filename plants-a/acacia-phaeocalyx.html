Acacia phaeocalyx

Acacia phaeocalyx, commonly known as the black wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, woody pods and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into many small leaflets. It is known for its rapid growth and is often used in reforestation projects.

The bark and leaves of Acacia phaeocalyx have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
