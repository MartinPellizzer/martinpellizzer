Arum albinervium

Arum albinervium, commonly known as the white-veined arum, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, smooth, and often bright green leaves with prominently visible white veins. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unique floral structure.
