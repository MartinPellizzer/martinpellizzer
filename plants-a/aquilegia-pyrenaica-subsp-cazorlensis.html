Aquilegia pyrenaica subsp. cazorlensis

Aquilegia pyrenaica subsp.

cazorlensis, commonly known as the Cazorla columbine, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring.

The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it thrives in rocky or calcareous soils. It is often found in mountainous regions, particularly in the Sierra de Cazorla area of Spain.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
