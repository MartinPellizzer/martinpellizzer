Acalypha multispicata

Acalypha multispicata, commonly known as the red-hot catwhiskers, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, crimson bracts that resemble cat whiskers, which are actually modified leaves rather than flowers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

Acalypha multispicata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
