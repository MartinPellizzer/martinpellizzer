Aconitum degenii

Aconitum degenii, commonly known as the Degen's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. Aconitum degenii contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
