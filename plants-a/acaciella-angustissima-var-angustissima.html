Acaciella angustissima var. angustissima

Acaciella angustissima var. angustissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf acaciella, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, angular stems covered in a layer of fine, white hairs. The leaves are small, linear, and often clustered at the base of the plant, with a distinctive grayish-green color. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, which appear during the rainy season.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
