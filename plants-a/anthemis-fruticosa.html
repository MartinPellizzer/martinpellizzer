Anthemis fruticosa

Anthemis fruticosa, commonly known as golden marguerite, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

The species is also known for its aromatic foliage, which contains compounds used in traditional medicine.
