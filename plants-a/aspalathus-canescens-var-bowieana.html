Aspalathus canescens var. bowieana

Aspalathus canescens var. bowieana, commonly known as the Bowie's silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
