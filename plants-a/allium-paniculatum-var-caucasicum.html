Allium paniculatum var. caucasicum

Allium paniculatum var. caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale purple flowers in dense, branched clusters. The plant is characterized by its strong garlic scent and is often grown for its edible leaves and bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is closely related to other allium species and is valued both ornately and culinarily.
