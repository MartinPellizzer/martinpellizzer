Astragalus sempervirens

Astragalus sempervirens, commonly known as evergreen vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Astragalus sempervirens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
