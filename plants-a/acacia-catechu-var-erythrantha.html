Acacia catechu var. erythrantha

Acacia catechu var. erythrantha, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in dry, arid environments.

The wood and bark of Acacia catechu var. erythrantha have been traditionally used for tanning and medicinal purposes.
