Aconitum pyramidale f. hians

Aconitum pyramidale f. hians, commonly known as the alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a spire-like inflorescence, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and its stems are usually hairy. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes at high altitudes.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
