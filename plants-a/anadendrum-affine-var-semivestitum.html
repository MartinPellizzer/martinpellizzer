Anadendrum affine var. semivestitum

Anadendrum affine var. semivestitum, commonly known as the Texas mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It features compound leaves with elliptical leaflets that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
