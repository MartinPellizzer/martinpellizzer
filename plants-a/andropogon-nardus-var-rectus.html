Andropogon nardus var. rectus

Andropogon nardus var. rectus, commonly known as narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in restoration projects.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological and agricultural studies.
