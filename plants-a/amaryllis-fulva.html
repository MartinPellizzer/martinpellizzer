Amaryllis fulva

Amaryllis fulva, commonly known as the orange amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of fleshy, white to grayish bulbs. Amaryllis fulva thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value lies in its bold, showy blooms and its ability to be cultivated as a seasonal or perennial plant.
