Alsine longipes var. laeta

Alsine longipes var. laeta, commonly known as the happy foot or silverleaf alsike, is a low-growing annual herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, branching stems that often lie prostrate on the ground, covered with fine, silver-gray hairs. The small, pink to purple flowers appear in clusters at the ends of the stems, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs, giving the plant a silvery appearance.

This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and meadows.
