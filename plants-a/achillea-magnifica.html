Achillea magnifica

Achillea magnifica, commonly known as the showy yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, flat clusters of white to pinkish flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical name honors the Greek hero Achilles, who was said to have used the plant for medicinal purposes.
