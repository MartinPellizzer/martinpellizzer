Acacia undoolyana

Acacia undoolyana, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are arranged in compact cylindrical clusters. Acacia undoolyana is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and efficient water use.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
