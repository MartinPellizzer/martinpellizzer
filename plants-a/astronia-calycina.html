Astronia calycina

Astronia calycina, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by egg-shaped, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Astronia calycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
