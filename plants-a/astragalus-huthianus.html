Astragalus huthianus

Astragalus huthianus, commonly known as the Huth's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Astragalus huthianus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
