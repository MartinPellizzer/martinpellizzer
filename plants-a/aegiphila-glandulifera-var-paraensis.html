Aegiphila glandulifera var. paraensis

Aegiphila glandulifera var. paraensis, commonly known as the Paraense glandular aegiphila, is a shrub native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the humid tropical climate. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
