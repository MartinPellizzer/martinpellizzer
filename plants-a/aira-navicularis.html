Aira navicularis

Aira navicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf airplant, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in wetland and marsh environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Aira navicularis is an ephemeral plant, often found in temporary water bodies and can be sensitive to changes in its habitat.
