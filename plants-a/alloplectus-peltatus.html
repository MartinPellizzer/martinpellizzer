Alloplectus peltatus

Alloplectus peltatus, commonly known as the broadleaf hound's-tongue, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a distinctive, spiny capsule that encloses its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are palmately lobed, giving it a unique and somewhat ornate appearance.

Alloplectus peltatus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
