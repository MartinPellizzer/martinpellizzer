Aconitum bisma

Aconitum bisma, commonly known as the Himalayan aconite, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Aconitum bisma contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring careful handling.
