Allium howellii subsp. sanbenitense

Allium howellii subsp.

sanbenitense, commonly known as the San Benito garlic, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pinkish flowers in umbels.

The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is known for its aromatic, bulbous structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
