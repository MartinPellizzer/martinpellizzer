Alchemilla plicata f. adpressepilosa

Alchemilla plicata f. adpressepilosa, commonly known as the compact leaf lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant with distinctive silver-gray foliage.

It features basal leaves that are pinnately divided and densely hairy, giving it a compact, mat-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in loose clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
