Anguina wawraei

Anguina wawraei, commonly known as the Wawra's anguina, is a species of nematode belonging to the family Anguinidae.

It is primarily found in the roots of certain plants, where it feeds on plant cells, potentially causing damage to the host. This nematode is particularly associated with the genus *Atriplex*, commonly known as saltbushes. Its life cycle involves several stages, including egg, four juvenile stages, and an adult stage.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can impact the growth and health of its host plants, making it a subject of study in agricultural and ecological contexts.
