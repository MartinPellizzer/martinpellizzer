Astragalus loczyi

Astragalus loczyi, commonly known as Loczy's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Astragalus loczyi is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
