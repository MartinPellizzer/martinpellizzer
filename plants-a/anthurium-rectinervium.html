Anthurium rectinervium

Anthurium rectinervium, commonly known as the straight-veined anthurium, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves with prominent, straight veins.

Its striking, spadix-shaped inflorescence is surrounded by a colorful spathe, which varies in hue depending on the cultivar. Anthurium rectinervium is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and unique floral structure.
