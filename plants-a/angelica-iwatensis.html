Angelica iwatensis

Angelica iwatensis, commonly known as the Iwo Island angelica, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and complex root systems.

Native to the Philippines, particularly the island of Iwo, this plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. It features tall, hollow stems with large, compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family.

Its roots are valued for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.
