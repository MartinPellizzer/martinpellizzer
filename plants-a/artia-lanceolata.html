Artia lanceolata

Artia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved artia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Artia lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
