Argyreia tiliifolia

Argyreia tiliifolia, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces cylindrical, greenish-brown pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
