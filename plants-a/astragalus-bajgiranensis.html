Astragalus bajgiranensis

Astragalus bajgiranensis, commonly known as Bajgiran milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
