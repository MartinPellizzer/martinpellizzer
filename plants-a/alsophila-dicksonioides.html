Alsophila dicksonioides

Alsophila dicksonioides, commonly known as the Dickson's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan. Its fronds are typically bright green and can develop a bluish tint when mature.

Alsophila dicksonioides is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
