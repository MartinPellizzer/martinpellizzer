Acacia notabilis var. validinervia

Acacia notabilis var. validinervia, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features slender, often multi-branched stems with a greyish bark that exfoliates easily. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact globular inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, thread-like leaflets that are arranged in a dense, feathery pattern.

The species is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in dune environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
