Acanthocalycium rhodotrichum

Acanthocalycium rhodotrichum, commonly known as the hairy red-tipped cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Chihuahua Desert in Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a unique feature of having dense, white, woolly hairs covering its stems.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to survive.
