Aechmea sucreana

Aechmea sucreana, commonly known as the Sucrean pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces a striking inflorescence with brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its humid, shaded habitat.

Aechmea sucreana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
