Aechmea sucreana

Aechmea sucreana, commonly known as the Sucrean pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces a striking inflorescence with brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its humid, shaded habitat.

Aechmea sucreana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Morphology

Aechmea sucreana has rosette-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and strap-like, arranged in a compact spiral, with a waxy, bluish-green color and a pointed tip.

The plant lacks a true stem, with the leaves forming a dense rosette that protects the central growing point. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, often in shades of red or orange, and bloom during the dry season, relying on wind pollination. The fruit is a small, berry-like structure that disperses seeds through animal consumption.

The roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.
