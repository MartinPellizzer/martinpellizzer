Allium scorodoprasum

Allium scorodoprasum, commonly known as garlic chives, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, grass-like leaves and small, white, bulbous flowers. The plant is cultivated for its edible leaves and bulbs, which have a mild garlic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and culinary use.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in various cuisines.
