Alchemilla asterophylla subsp. fontqueri

Alchemilla asterophylla subsp.

fontqueri, commonly known as the Fontqueri lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are often tinged with red or purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or woodland areas.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
