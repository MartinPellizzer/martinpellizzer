Arabis platysperma var. imparata

Arabis platysperma var. imparata, commonly known as the broad-fruited rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with basal leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces oval, green fruits that are distinctive for their shape and arrangement. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to rock gardens and wildflower habitats.
