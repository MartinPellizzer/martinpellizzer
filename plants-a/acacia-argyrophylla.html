Acacia argyrophylla

Acacia argyrophylla, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, almost silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

Acacia argyrophylla is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
