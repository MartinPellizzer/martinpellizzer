Acacia bombycina

Acacia bombycina, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often pendulous branches and small, feathery leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in arid and semi-arid regions.

Acacia bombycina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
