Allium auctum

Allium auctum, commonly known as the giant bulb onion, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, bulbous stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, often reaching lengths of over 50 centimeters.

The bulb, which is the most prized part of the plant, is used in various culinary applications and is rich in sulfur compounds that contribute to its sharp flavor.
