Alternanthera mexicana var. gracilis

Alternanthera mexicana var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender Alternanthera, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, often red or purple-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and riverbanks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and can exhibit a striking coloration, especially under full sun.

It is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique flowering display.
