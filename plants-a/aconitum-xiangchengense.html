Aconitum xiangchengense

Aconitum xiangchengense, commonly known as the Xiangcheng monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features palmately compound leaves with a distinctive hood-like shape, typical of the genus Aconitum. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it requires careful handling and is primarily cultivated for ornamental purposes.
