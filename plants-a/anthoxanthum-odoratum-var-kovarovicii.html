Anthoxanthum odoratum var. kovarovicii

Anthoxanthum odoratum var. kovarovicii, commonly known as the Kovarovic's sweet grass, is a variant of the sweet grass species native to temperate regions.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight sheen, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal practices.
