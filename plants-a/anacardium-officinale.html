Anacardium officinale

Anacardium officinale, commonly known as the cashew tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It produces a unique fruit that contains both a crunchy seed and a toxic, sticky resin called urushiol. The plant belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which also includes poison ivy and mangoes. The cashew tree is valued for its edible seeds and the oil extracted from its shell.

Its botanical features include compound leaves, small yellow flowers, and a woody fruit that develops from the flower.
