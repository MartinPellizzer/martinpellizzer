Actinidia melanandra

Actinidia melanandra, commonly known as the black actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its dark, leathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in early summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically black when mature, though they are not widely cultivated for consumption. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Actinidia melanandra is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
