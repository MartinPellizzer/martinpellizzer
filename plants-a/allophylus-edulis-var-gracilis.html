Allophylus edulis var. gracilis

Allophylus edulis var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender edible hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed raw or cooked. It is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its edible parts in local cuisines.
