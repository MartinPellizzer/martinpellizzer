Alyssum alpestre proles jordani

Alyssum alpestre proles jordani, commonly known as Jordan's alpine alyssum, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid and high-altitude environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
