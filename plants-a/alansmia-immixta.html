Alansmia immixta

Alansmia immixta, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and grass-like, and it produces striking white or pale pink flowers during the spring. Its flowers are tubular and have a distinctive fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

Alansmia immixta is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with its deep root system and ability to store water.
