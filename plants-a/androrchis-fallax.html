Androrchis fallax

Androrchis fallax, commonly known as the false toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive bluish-green hue. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with two lips, typical of the orchid family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its root system is fibrous and can spread underground, allowing it to colonize new areas effectively.
