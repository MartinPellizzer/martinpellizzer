Albuca karooica

Albuca karooica, commonly known as the Karoo bulbous plant, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its underground bulb and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
