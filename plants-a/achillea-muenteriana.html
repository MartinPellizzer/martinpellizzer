Achillea muenteriana

Achillea muenteriana, commonly known as the Alpine yarrow, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its slender stems and finely divided leaves are adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

The pale yellow to white flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
