Agrostis fuegiana

Agrostis fuegiana, commonly known as the Falkland grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern regions of South America.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and coastal areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including strong winds and limited nutrients.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a stabilizer of soil and a food source for local wildlife.
