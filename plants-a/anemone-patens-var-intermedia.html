Anemone patens var. intermedia

Anemone patens var. intermedia, commonly known as the spreading anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has a short, stout rhizome and produces basal leaves that are palmately lobed with hairy surfaces. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
