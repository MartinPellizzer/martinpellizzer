Azalea capitata

Azalea capitata, commonly known as the dwarf azalea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Rhododendron genus and belongs to the Ericaceae family. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically evergreen, glossy, and oval in shape.

Azalea capitata thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
