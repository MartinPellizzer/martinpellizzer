Adiantum suborbiculare

Adiantum suborbiculare, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically light green with a silvery sheen on the underside. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce a dense mat of foliage.

Adiantum suborbiculare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
