Andropogon anomalus

Andropogon anomalus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairie habitats.

Due to its resilience and ecological importance, it plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
