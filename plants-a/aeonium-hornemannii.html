Aeonium hornemannii

Aeonium hornemannii, commonly known as the Hornemann's aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, green to blue-gray leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces tall, branching stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers during the spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.

Aeonium hornemannii is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its striking appearance and ease of care.
