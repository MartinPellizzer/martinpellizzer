Aster × frikartii

Aster × frikartii, commonly known as the meadow aster, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring lance-shaped leaves and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the late summer and early autumn. Aster × frikartii is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
