Aellenia glauca subsp. cinerascens

Aellenia glauca subsp.

cinerascens, commonly known as the silverleaf aellenia, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It features small, oval leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery or grayish pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.
