Actinidia valvata var. longipedicellata

Actinidia valvata var. longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-pedicellate actinidia, is a variant of the actinidia genus native to China.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by edible, fuzzy berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a food source.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species within the Actinidia family.
