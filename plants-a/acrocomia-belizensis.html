Acrocomia belizensis

Acrocomia belizensis, commonly known as the Belize palm, is a flowering plant native to Central America, particularly Belize.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, edible fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
