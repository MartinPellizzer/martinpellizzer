Ardisia conandroides

Ardisia conandroides, commonly known as the Christmas berry, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely on the stems. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The plant's ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy fruits, which provide food for birds and other wildlife.
