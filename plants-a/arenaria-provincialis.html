Arenaria provincialis

Arenaria provincialis, commonly known as the Provincial sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help reduce water loss.

Arenaria provincialis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is often found in coastal or desert environments.
