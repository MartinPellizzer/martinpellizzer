Acer saccharinum f. ahlesii

Acer saccharinum f. ahlesii, commonly known as the Ahlesii silver maple, is a naturally occurring variant of the silver maple tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely toothed leaves that often display a striking silver or white underside. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping due to its elegant appearance.
