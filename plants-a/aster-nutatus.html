Aster nutatus

Aster nutatus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy blue to purple flower clusters that appear in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Aster nutatus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
