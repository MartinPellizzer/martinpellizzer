Achyranthes arborescens

Achyranthes arborescens, commonly known as red root, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish underneath. Its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in the summer months.

The roots are thick and fleshy, which contribute to its common name and are used in traditional medicine.
