Abutilon goldmanii

Abutilon goldmanii, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 feet. Its flowers, which range in color from yellow to orange, are borne on peduncles and bloom during the summer months.

Abutilon goldmanii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
