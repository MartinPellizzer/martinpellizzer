Anil argyroides

Anil argyroides, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage.

The tree is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
