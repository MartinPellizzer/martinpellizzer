Apocynum neogeum

Apocynum neogeum, commonly known as the Eastern dogbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in fine hairs and produce clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a milky sap that is toxic. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Apocynum neogeum is part of the Apocynaceae family, which includes many species with cardiac glycosides.
