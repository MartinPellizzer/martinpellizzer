Asphodeline prismatocarpa

Asphodeline prismatocarpa, commonly known as the purple asphodel, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, grass-like leaves and produces striking purple or pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its stout, ribbed stems and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
