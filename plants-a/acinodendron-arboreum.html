Acinodendron arboreum

Acinodendron arboreum, commonly known as the tree hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or white. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in warm climates.
