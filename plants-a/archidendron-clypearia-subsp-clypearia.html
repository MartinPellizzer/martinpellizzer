Archidendron clypearia subsp. clypearia

Archidendron clypearia subsp.

clypearia, commonly known as the African candelabra tree, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, multi-branched, candelabra-like crown and compound leaves with elongated leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and has traditional uses in some regions.
