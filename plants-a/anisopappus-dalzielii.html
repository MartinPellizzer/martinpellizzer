Anisopappus dalzielii

Anisopappus dalzielii, commonly known as the Dalziel's dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by yellow disk flowers. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often found in dry slopes and gravelly soils.

Anisopappus dalzielii is adapted to withstand drought and full sun, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.
