Anthoxanthum novae-zelandiae

Anthoxanthum novae-zelandiae, commonly known as the New Zealand oatgrass, is a perennial grass native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale yellow to brown flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
