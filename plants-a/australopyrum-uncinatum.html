Australopyrum uncinatum

Australopyrum uncinatum, commonly known as the hooked grass, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its unique feature is the presence of hooked structures at the base of its leaves, which aid in seed dispersal.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
