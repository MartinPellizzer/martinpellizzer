Anil tinctoria var. brachycarpa

Anil tinctoria var. brachycarpa, commonly known as Indian indigo, is a variant of the indigo plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue flowers and is cultivated for its dye-producing properties. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Historically, it was widely used for producing natural blue dyes in textile industries.
