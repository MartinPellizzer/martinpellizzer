Atriplex imbricata

Atriplex imbricata, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, salt-tolerant leaves that help it thrive in saline soils. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically covered in a waxy coating, which reduces water loss and aids in survival in harsh environments.

Atriplex imbricata plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soils and providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
