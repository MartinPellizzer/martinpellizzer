Alsophila caudata

Alsophila caudata, commonly known as the feather fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately divided fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for many decades, making it a long-lived species. Its fronds are often used in ornamental horticulture due to their elegant appearance.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.
