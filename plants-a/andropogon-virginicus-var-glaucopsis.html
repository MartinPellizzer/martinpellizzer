Andropogon virginicus var. glaucopsis

Andropogon virginicus var. glaucopsis, commonly known as the blue false grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, blue-green blades and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, such as in marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, pale green to blue flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
