Arisaema pattaniense

Arisaema pattaniense, commonly known as the Pattani arrowhead, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence structure.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a distinct, arrow-shaped leaf that is often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its spadix, surrounded by a colorful spathe, contains both male and female flowers, typical of the genus Arisaema.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
