Armeria juniperifolia var. splendens

Armeria juniperifolia var. splendens, commonly known as the Splendour's thrift, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and northern Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that resembles juniper leaves, giving it its species name. The plant produces showy clusters of white or pink flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for coastal gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular addition to rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
