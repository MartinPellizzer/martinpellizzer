Arenaria funiculata

Arenaria funiculata, commonly known as the sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats. Its growth habit is typically prostrate, with stems that spread along the ground.

Arenaria funiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
