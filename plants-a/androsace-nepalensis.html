Androsace nepalensis

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing white or pale pink flowers that are adapted to the cold, high-altitude environment. Androsace nepalensis is often found growing on rocky slopes and meadows, where it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils.
