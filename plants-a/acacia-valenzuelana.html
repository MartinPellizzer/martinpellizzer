Acacia valenzuelana

Acacia valenzuelana, commonly known as the Mexican acacia, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soil conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
