Adenophora hubeiensis

Adenophora hubeiensis, commonly known as the Hubei bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

Adenophora hubeiensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
