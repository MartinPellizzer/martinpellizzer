Argentina supina

Argentina supina, commonly known as the prostrate argentine plant, is a low-growing herbaceous species native to South America.

It typically features succulent, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
