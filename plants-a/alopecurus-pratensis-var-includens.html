Alopecurus pratensis var. includens

Alopecurus pratensis var. includens, commonly known as meadow foxtail, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, riverbanks, and other damp habitats. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

This variety is characterized by its distinctive seed heads and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
