Astragalus casei

Astragalus casei, commonly known as the Mongolian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, followed by cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Astragalus casei is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting deep root systems and drought tolerance.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
