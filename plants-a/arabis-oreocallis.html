Arabis oreocallis

Arabis oreocallis, commonly known as the mountain rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
