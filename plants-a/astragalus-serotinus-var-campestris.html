Astragalus serotinus var. campestris

Astragalus serotinus var. campestris, commonly known as the late-flowering milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, typically arranged in spikes. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
