Amaryllis atanasia

Amaryllis atanasia, commonly known as the ghost amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems with large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are broad and dark green, often emerging after the flowering period. Its blooming season typically occurs in late winter to early spring, making it a prized addition to gardens.

The species is valued for its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
