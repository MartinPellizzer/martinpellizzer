Aphelandra mucronata

Aphelandra mucronata, commonly known as the spikeleaf arum, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, dark green leaves that are often adorned with a red or purple stripe along the midrib. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aphelandra mucronata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
