Asphodelus glaucescens

Asphodelus glaucescens, commonly known as the blue asphodel, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It features strap-like, grass-like leaves and produces tall spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky soils. It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar characteristics.

Asphodelus glaucescens is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
