Actinidia callosa var. trichogyna

Actinidia callosa var. trichogyna, commonly known as the hairy actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a small, fuzzy berry that ripens to a yellowish color.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
