Aconitum anthora var. jacquinianum

Aconitum anthora var. jacquinianum, commonly known as the Jacob's plant or Jacob's lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Aconitum anthora var. jacquinianum is adapted to cold, alpine environments and is often found in rocky or meadow habitats at high elevations.
