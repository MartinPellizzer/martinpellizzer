Archidendron hispidum

Archidendron hispidum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, hairy stipules and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing flat, edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in moist, tropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
