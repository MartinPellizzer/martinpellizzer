Arabis kishidae

Arabis kishidae, commonly known as Kishida's rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is typically found in rocky or gravelly habitats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and have a rough, hairy texture.

Arabis kishidae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
