Anthemis melampodina var. truncata

Anthemis melampodina var. truncata, commonly known as the truncated tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features aromatic, yellowish-green leaves with a distinctive, fern-like appearance and is characterized by its compact growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically noted for their antiseptic and digestive benefits.
