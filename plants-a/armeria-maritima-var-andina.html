Armeria maritima var. andina

Armeria maritima var. andina, commonly known as the Andean thrift, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
