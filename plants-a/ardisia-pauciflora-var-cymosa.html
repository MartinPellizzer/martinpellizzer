Ardisia pauciflora var. cymosa

Ardisia pauciflora var. cymosa, commonly known as the clustered ardisia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and unique flowering habit.
