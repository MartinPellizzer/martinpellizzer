Alpinia rubricaulis

Alpinia rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that display a striking reddish hue, especially when mature. The plant produces large, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Alpinia rubricaulis is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
