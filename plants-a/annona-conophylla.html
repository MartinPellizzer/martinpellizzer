Annona conophylla

Annona conophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian pawpaw, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The fruit is a spiny, green berry that matures to a yellowish color and contains a juicy, sweet pulp. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
