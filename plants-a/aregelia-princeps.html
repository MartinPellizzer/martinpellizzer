Aregelia princeps

Aregelia princeps, commonly known as the royal aregelia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
