Artemisia vulgaris var. flodmanii

Artemisia vulgaris var. flodmanii, commonly known as Florentine wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer. The plant produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds and is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.

Its distinctive scent and hardy nature make it a notable species within the Asteraceae family.
