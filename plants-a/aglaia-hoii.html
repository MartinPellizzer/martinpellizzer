Aglaia hoii

Aglaia hoii, commonly known as the Java aglaia, is a member of the Meliaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with smooth, greyish-brown bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing one to three seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate to elliptic in shape, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside.

Aglaia hoii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
