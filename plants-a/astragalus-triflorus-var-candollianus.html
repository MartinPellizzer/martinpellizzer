Astragalus triflorus var. candollianus

Astragalus triflorus var. candollianus, commonly known as the three-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

This species is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role in stabilizing soils and its potential medicinal properties.
