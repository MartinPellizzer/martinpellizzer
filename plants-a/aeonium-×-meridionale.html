Aeonium × meridionale

Aeonium × meridionale, commonly known as the "Lavender Aeonium," is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are often tinged with lavender or pink hues. The plant produces tall, branching stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.

Aeonium × meridionale is popular in rock gardens and as a decorative houseplant due to its striking appearance.
