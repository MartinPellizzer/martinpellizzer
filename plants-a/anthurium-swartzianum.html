Anthurium swartzianum

Anthurium swartzianum, commonly known as the Swartz's arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spatula-shaped leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface and prominent veins.

Anthurium swartzianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage rather than its flowers.
