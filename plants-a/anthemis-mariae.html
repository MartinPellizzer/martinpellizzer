Anthemis mariae

Anthemis mariae, commonly known as Mary's flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish to orange-yellow flower heads that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its historical use in herbal medicine is attributed to its purported calming and antispasmodic properties.
