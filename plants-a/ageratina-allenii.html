Ageratina allenii

Ageratina allenii, commonly known as the Allen's blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its dense, blue-gray foliage and fluffy, white to pale blue flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Ageratina allenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in tropical gardens.
