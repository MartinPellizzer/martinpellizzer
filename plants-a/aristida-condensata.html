Aristida condensata

Aristida condensata, commonly known as the densehead wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, sandy soils, and disturbed areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are tightly clustered. Its seeds are adapted for wind dispersal, allowing it to colonize new areas effectively.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
