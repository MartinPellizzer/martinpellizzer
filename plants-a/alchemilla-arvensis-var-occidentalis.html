Alchemilla arvensis var. occidentalis

Alchemilla arvensis var. occidentalis, commonly known as western lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in a dense layer of silky, white hairs. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in loose panicles, appearing in late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
