Achyrocline marchiorii

Achyrocline marchiorii, commonly known as the Brazilian mountain sage, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in cool and moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
