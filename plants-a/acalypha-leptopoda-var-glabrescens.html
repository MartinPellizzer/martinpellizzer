Acalypha leptopoda var. glabrescens

Acalypha leptopoda var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, silky hairs covering the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
