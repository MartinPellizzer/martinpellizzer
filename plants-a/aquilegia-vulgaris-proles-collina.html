Aquilegia vulgaris proles collina

Aquilegia vulgaris proles collina, commonly known as the Collins' columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It features striking, spurred flowers in shades of red, purple, and yellow, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments, and its stems are hairy and upright. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
