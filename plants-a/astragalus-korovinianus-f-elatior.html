Astragalus korovinianus f. elatior

Astragalus korovinianus f. elatior, commonly known as the Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow flowers in late summer. The plant is characterized by its fibrous root system and is often found in dry, rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
