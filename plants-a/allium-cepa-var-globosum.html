Allium cepa var. globosum

Allium cepa var. globosum, commonly known as the globe onion, is a botanical variant of the common onion.

It is characterized by its round, bulbous shape and thick, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its mild flavor and is often used in cooking and traditional medicine.
