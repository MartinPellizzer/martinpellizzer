Aconitum pyramidale f. clusianum

Aconitum pyramidale f. clusianum, commonly known as the Clusian mountain monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a spire-like inflorescence. The plant features palmately compound leaves with three leaflets, and its roots are tuberous, typical of the genus Aconitum. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
