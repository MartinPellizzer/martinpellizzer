Alpinia nimmonii

Alpinia nimmonii, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. This herbaceous plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red at the base. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow to orange flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom during the summer months.

Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and are valued for their aromatic properties.
