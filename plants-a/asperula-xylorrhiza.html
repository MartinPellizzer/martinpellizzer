Asperula xylorrhiza

Asperula xylorrhiza, commonly known as the rock rose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in a dense, compact form. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant valued in horticulture.
