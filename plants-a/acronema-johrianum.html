Acronema johrianum

Acronema johrianum, commonly known as the Kashmir bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Acronema johrianum is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
