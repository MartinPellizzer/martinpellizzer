Alseodaphne paludosa

Alseodaphne paludosa, commonly known as the swamp alseodaphne, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wet, swampy, or marshy habitats, often in lowland areas with high humidity. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in catkins, and it produces drupes that are often reddish or black when mature.

Alseodaphne paludosa plays an important ecological role in its native wetland ecosystems.
