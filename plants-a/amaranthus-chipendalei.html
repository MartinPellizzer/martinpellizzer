Amaranthus chipendalei

Amaranthus chipendalei, commonly known as the chipendale amaranth, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the variety. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Amaranthus chipendalei is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for gardens.
