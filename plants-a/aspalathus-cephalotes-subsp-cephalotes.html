Aspalathus cephalotes subsp. cephalotes

Aspalathus cephalotes subsp.

cephalotes, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, red or pink flowers.

The plant produces small, hard, yellowish-brown seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in local ecosystems.
