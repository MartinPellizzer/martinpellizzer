Asterophyllum asperula

Asterophyllum asperula, commonly known as the rough asterophyllum, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing a low-growing, mat-forming structure. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture, and its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Asterophyllum asperula is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
