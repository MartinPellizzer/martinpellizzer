Agropyron strigosum subsp. aegilopoides

Agropyron strigosum subsp.

aegilopoides, commonly known as the prairie wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, compact clusters. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and steppe ecosystems.

This subspecies is valued for its drought tolerance and is used in restoration projects and forage production.
