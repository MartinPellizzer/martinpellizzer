Astragalus kamtschaticus

Astragalus kamtschaticus, commonly known as the Caucasian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Astragalus kamtschaticus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
