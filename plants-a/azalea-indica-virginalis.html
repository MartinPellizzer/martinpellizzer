Azalea indica virginalis

Azalea indica virginalis, commonly known as the white azalea, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its delicate blooms.

Azalea indica virginalis prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
