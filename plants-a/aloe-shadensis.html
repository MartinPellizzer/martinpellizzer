Aloe shadensis

Aloe shadensis, commonly known as the Shadens Island aloe, is a succulent plant native to the island of Shadens in the Comoros.

It features fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge, often covered in a layer of white powder. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick leaves.

Aloe shadensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
