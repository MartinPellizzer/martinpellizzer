Astilbe thunbergii var. hachijoensis

Astilbe thunbergii var. hachijoensis, commonly known as the Hachijo Island astilbe, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white to lilac-colored flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
