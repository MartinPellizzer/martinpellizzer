Ageratina cutervensis

Ageratina cutervensis, commonly known as the Cuetzalan blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
