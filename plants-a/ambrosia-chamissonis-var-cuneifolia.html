Ambrosia chamissonis var. cuneifolia

Ambrosia chamissonis var. cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ragweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in late summer. The plant is an annual herb that grows in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and open fields. It produces copious amounts of pollen, which can contribute to seasonal allergies.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and urban areas.
