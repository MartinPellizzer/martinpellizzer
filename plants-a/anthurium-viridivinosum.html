Anthurium viridivinosum

Anthurium viridivinosum, commonly known as the green wine anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a reddish or purplish tint on the underside. The plant produces striking spadices, which are elongated, spadix-like structures that are often brightly colored, usually in shades of red or orange. Anthurium viridivinosum thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant in horticultural collections.
