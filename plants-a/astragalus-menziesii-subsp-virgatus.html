Astragalus menziesii subsp. virgatus

Astragalus menziesii subsp.

virgatus, commonly known as the Sierra Calkins or western milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to western North America. It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy leaves and typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
