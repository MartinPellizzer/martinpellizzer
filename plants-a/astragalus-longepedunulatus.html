Astragalus longepedunulatus

Astragalus longepedunulatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Astragalus longepedunulatus is often found in steppe and desert habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
