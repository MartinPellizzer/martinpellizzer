Acacia silvestris

Acacia silvestris, commonly known as the wild acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater.

Acacia silvestris plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
