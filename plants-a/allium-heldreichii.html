Allium heldreichii

Allium heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, leafy stems and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round bulbs that are used in traditional cooking and have mild garlic-like flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium heldreichii is valued for its ornamental beauty and culinary uses in regional cuisines.
