Acacia tortuosa

Acacia tortuosa, commonly known as the sour acacia or the warty acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive warty, thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, followed by flat, elongated pods containing seeds. Acacia tortuosa is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

It has been used traditionally for its wood, gum, and medicinal properties.
