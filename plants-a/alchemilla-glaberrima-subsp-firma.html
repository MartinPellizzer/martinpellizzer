Alchemilla glaberrima subsp. firma

Alchemilla glaberrima subsp.

firma, commonly known as silverweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features basal rosettes of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, clustered inflorescences during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
