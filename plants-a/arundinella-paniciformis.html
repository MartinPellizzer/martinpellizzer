Arundinella paniciformis

Arundinella paniciformis, commonly known as the panicled reed, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are linear and often have a slightly rough texture, while its flowers are wind-pollinated.

Arundinella paniciformis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
