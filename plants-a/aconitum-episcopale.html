Aconitum episcopale

Aconitum episcopale, commonly known as the bishop's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, spikelike clusters. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply cut lobes that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Aconitum episcopale contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling and cultivation.
