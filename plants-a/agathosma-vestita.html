Agathosma vestita

Agathosma vestita, commonly known as Cape spearmint, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its pungent, mint-like scent. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Agathosma vestita is valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
