Anthericum obtusifolium

Anthericum obtusifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf arum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive spathe and spadix inflorescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered around a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along streambanks.

Anthericum obtusifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
