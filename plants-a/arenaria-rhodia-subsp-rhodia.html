Arenaria rhodia subsp. rhodia

Arenaria rhodia subsp.

rhodia, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

It is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
