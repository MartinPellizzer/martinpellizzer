Anchusa officinalis var. angustifolia

Anchusa officinalis var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved bugloss, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that can grow up to 1 meter tall, covered with soft, grayish-green leaves. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
