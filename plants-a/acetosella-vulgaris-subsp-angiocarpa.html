Acetosella vulgaris subsp. angiocarpa

Acetosella vulgaris subsp.

angiocarpa, commonly known as the red or pink wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically bright green with a reddish or pinkish tinge on the underside.

The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually white or pale pink, often with a yellowish center. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
