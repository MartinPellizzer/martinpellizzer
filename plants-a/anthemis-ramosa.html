Anthemis ramosa

Anthemis ramosa, commonly known as the branched ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a sprawling, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

The species is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
