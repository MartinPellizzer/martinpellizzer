Alstroemeria fiebrigiana

Alstroemeria fiebrigiana, commonly known as the Fiery Alstroemeria, is a striking flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a member of the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of tubular blooms that are often used in floral arrangements.

Its botanical name honors the German botanist Johann Fiebrig, who contributed significantly to the study of South American flora.
