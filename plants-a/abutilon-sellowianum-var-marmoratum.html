Abutilon sellowianum var. marmoratum

Abutilon sellowianum var. marmoratum, commonly known as the variegated abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display patterns of white, yellow, and green. The plant produces lantern-shaped, hairy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage and unique flower structure make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking visual interest.
