Agathosma kougaensis

Agathosma kougaensis, commonly known as the Kouga buchu, is a small, aromatic shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid conditions. It produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

Due to its unique properties, it is also valued for its potential in herbal remedies and essential oil production.
