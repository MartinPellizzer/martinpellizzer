Acacia laxiflora

Acacia laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia laxiflora is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
