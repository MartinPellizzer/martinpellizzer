Auranticarpa edentata

Auranticarpa edentata, commonly known as the golden fruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange fruits, which are typically oval-shaped and have a smooth, leathery exterior. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy green appearance. The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural pigments.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
