Amaranthus blitum var. silvestris

Amaranthus blitum var. silvestris, commonly known as wild amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the stage of development. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to different environments.

This species is also valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of protein and minerals.
