Amaranthus paniculatus var. speciosus

Amaranthus paniculatus var. speciosus, commonly known as the purple amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and vibrant, showy inflorescences that range in color from pink to deep purple. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is also valued for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients.
