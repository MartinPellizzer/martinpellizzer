Anthurium gladiifolium

Anthurium gladiifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are modified leaves that enclose the small, inconspicuous flowers. Anthurium gladiifolium prefers bright, indirect light and consistently moist, well-draining soil.

It is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
