Arisarum simorrhinum var. simorrhinum

Arisarum simorrhinum var. simorrhinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, narrow leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered on the spadix, which is often brightly colored.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unusual floral structure.
