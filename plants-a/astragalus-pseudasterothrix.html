Astragalus pseudasterothrix

Astragalus pseudasterothrix, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous thread-like leaflets, and its root system is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
