Achillea sordida

Achillea sordida, commonly known as the dark yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems bear small, pale yellow to white flower heads. Achillea sordida is known for its medicinal properties, historically used for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.
