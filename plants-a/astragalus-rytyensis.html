Astragalus rytyensis

Astragalus rytyensis, commonly known as the Rytyan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus rytyensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and potential ecological significance in its native range.
