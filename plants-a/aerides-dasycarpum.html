Aerides dasycarpum

Aerides dasycarpum, commonly known as the hairy-fruit orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a short, stocky rhizome and a single, erect stem. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically appear in late spring to early summer.

The orchid thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
