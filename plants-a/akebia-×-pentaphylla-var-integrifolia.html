Akebia × pentaphylla var. integrifolia

Akebia × pentaphylla var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Japanese actinio, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with five to seven oval leaflets that are typically serrated along the edges. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red to orange berries that mature in late summer. It is known for its vigorous growth and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
