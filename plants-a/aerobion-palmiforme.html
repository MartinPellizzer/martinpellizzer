Aerobion palmiforme

Aerobion palmiforme, commonly known as the air-palm, is a unique flowering plant characterized by its palm-like appearance and ability to thrive in aerial environments.

It possesses fibrous roots that anchor it to tree trunks or other vertical surfaces, allowing it to grow in epiphytic conditions. The plant's leaves are typically broad and glossy, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value.

Due to its specialized growth habits, Aerobion palmiforme is often found in tropical and subtropical regions with high humidity and abundant rainfall.
