Armeniaca vulgaris var. farinosa

Armeniaca vulgaris var. farinosa, commonly known as the Chinese date or jujube, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It produces edible fruits that are rich in sugars, vitamins, and antioxidants. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with a long growing season. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit, which is typically harvested in late summer, has a sweet, chewy texture and a distinctive, slightly tart flavor.
