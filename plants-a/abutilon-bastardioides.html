Abutilon bastardioides

Abutilon bastardioides, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking an eye-catching plant.
