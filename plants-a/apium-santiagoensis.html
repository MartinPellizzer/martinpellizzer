Apium santiagoensis

Apium santiagoensis, commonly known as the Santiago celery, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as parsley and dill. The plant is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, typical of the family.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its potential medicinal properties.
