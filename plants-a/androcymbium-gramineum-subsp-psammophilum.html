Androcymbium gramineum subsp. psammophilum

Androcymbium gramineum subsp.

psammophilum, commonly known as the sand-loving grass, is a perennial grass species adapted to arid and sandy environments. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes or sandy plains.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
