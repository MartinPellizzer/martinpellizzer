Anthericum crassinerve

Anthericum crassinerve, commonly known as the broad-veined arum, is a striking plant native to the wetlands of Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a distinctive, arrow-shaped leaf with prominent, broad veins that give it its scientific name. The plant produces an inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and are pollinated by insects.

Anthericum crassinerve thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water bodies.
