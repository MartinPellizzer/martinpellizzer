Arundinella togoensis

Arundinella togoensis, commonly known as Togo bamboo, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of its reproductive strategy. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and for construction purposes in local communities.

Arundinella togoensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland areas.
