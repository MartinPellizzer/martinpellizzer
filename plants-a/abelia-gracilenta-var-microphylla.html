Abelia gracilenta var. microphylla

Abelia gracilenta var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf abelia, is a compact shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a glossy dark green, providing a dense, bushy appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to garden settings.
