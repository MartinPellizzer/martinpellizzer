Aethionema gracile var. athoum

Aethionema gracile var. athoum, commonly known as the Armenian rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features compact, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to thrive in arid and rocky environments. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. It is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, sharing characteristics with other members of the mustard family.
