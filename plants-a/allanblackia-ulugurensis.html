Allanblackia ulugurensis

Allanblackia ulugurensis, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and produces yellowish flowers that develop into fruit capsules. The tree is valued for its seeds, which contain a high amount of oils used in cosmetics and traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat and is also economically significant for local communities.
