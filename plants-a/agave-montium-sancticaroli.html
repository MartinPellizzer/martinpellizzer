Agave montium-sancticaroli

Agave montium-sancticaroli, commonly known as the Sancti Caroli agave, is a species of flowering plant in the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid climates. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, white to yellowish flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and slow growth rate, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and collections.
