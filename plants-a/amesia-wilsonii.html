Amesia wilsonii

Amesia wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Amesia wilsonii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
