Arabis perennans var. longipes

Arabis perennans var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed rockcress, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers, which appear in early spring, are usually white or pale yellow and are arranged in clusters.
