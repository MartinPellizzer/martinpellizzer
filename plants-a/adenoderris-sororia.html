Adenoderris sororia

Adenoderris sororia, commonly known as the purple-leafed acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its botanical features include stipules and pods that contain seeds, typical of the legume family.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
