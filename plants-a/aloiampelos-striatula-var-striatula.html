Aloiampelos striatula var. striatula

Aloiampelos striatula var. striatula, commonly known as the stripe-leaved ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
