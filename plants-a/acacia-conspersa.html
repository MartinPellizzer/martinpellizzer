Acacia conspersa

Acacia conspersa, commonly known as the rough acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. Acacia conspersa thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal regions.

It is well adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
