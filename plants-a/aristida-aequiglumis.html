Aristida aequiglumis

Aristida aequiglumis, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 millimeters wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in native plant gardens.
