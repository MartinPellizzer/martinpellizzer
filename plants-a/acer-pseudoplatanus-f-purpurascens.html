Acer pseudoplatanus f. purpurascens

Acer pseudoplatanus f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leafed maple, is a cultivar of the common maple tree.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which displays a vibrant purple hue during the growing season. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a serrated margin. This ornamental variety is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
