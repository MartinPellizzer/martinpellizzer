Arabis brassiciformis

Arabis brassiciformis, commonly known as the candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Arabis brassiciformis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
