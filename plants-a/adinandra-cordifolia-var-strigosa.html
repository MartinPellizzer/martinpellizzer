Adinandra cordifolia var. strigosa

Adinandra cordifolia var. strigosa, commonly known as the hairy heartleaf adenium, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
