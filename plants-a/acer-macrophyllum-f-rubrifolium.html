Acer macrophyllum f. rubrifolium

Acer macrophyllum f. rubrifolium, commonly known as the red-leaf bigleaf maple, is a variant of the bigleaf maple native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that turn a vibrant red in the fall, making it a striking ornamental plant. The leaves are typically 5 to 10 inches in diameter, with a serrated margin and a smooth, glossy surface. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often found in forested areas.

This cultivar is valued for its unique foliage and is commonly used in gardens and landscapes for its seasonal color display.
