Amaranthus bellardii

Amaranthus bellardii, commonly known as the scarlet amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange bracts that surround the small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white powder.

Amaranthus bellardii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking color and is also used in some traditional herbal practices.
