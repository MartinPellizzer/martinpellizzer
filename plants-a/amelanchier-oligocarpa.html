Amelanchier oligocarpa

Amelanchier oligocarpa, commonly known as the Pacific crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to western North America.

It produces clusters of white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, red berries in the fall. The plant is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and smooth, gray bark. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and streamside habitats.

Amelanchier oligocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
