Amorphophallus fallax

Amorphophallus fallax, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and a striking, spadix-like inflorescence that resembles a phallic structure, hence its scientific name. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in marshy areas near water bodies. Its tuberous roots are edible when properly prepared, though they contain toxic compounds that must be boiled or cooked thoroughly.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
