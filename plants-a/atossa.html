Atossa

Atossa, commonly known as the "blue orchid," is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flowers, which are highly prized in horticulture. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in moisture retention. Atossa thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight to flourish.

Its unique coloration and elegant appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
