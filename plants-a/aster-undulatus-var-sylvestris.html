Aster undulatus var. sylvestris

Aster undulatus var. sylvestris, commonly known as the woodland aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white to pale blue petals and a yellow center, blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and ability to thrive in partial to full shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
