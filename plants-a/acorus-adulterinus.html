Acorus adulterinus

Acorus adulterinus, commonly known as the slender sweetflag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its sword-shaped leaves and a distinctive, aromatic underground rhizome. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Acorus adulterinus is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and rivers.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in botany and ecology.
