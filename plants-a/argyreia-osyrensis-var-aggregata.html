Argyreia osyrensis var. aggregata

Argyreia osyrensis var. aggregata, commonly known as the velvet flower or blue curl.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, characterized by its striking blue and purple bracts that resemble small flowers. The plant produces small white flowers that are often overshadowed by its showy, fleshy bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
