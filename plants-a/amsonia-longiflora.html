Amsonia longiflora

Amsonia longiflora, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and fluffy, blue-violet flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into cylindrical, woody seed capsules. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Amsonia longiflora is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
