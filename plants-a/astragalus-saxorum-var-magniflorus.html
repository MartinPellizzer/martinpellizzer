Astragalus saxorum var. magniflorus

Astragalus saxorum var. magniflorus, commonly known as the alpine milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, yellow to reddish-brown flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with numerous leaflets, and its roots are often fibrous and deep-reaching. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
