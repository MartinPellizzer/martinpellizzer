Adesmia punctata

Adesmia punctata, commonly known as the punctate burr legume, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, where it thrives in dry, arid environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Adesmia punctata is adapted to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
