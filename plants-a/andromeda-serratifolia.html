Andromeda serratifolia

Andromeda serratifolia, commonly known as the serrate alder, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic soils and thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are serrated, giving it its common name, and it produces small, dark berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
