Abies drummondii

Abies drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like foliage. The tree produces cones that are typically 3 to 5 inches long, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its bark is reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age, while its needles are flat and have a distinct blue-green hue.

This species is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in subalpine forests.
