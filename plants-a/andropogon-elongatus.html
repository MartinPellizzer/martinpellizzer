Andropogon elongatus

Andropogon elongatus, commonly known as tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Andropogon elongatus plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
