Adesmia longipes

Adesmia longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are legumes, containing several seeds, and it is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
