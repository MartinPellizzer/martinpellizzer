Asperula aristata var. macrosiphon

Asperula aristata var. macrosiphon, commonly known as the large-siphon asperula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
