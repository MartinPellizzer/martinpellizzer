Airampoa

Airampoa, commonly known as the swamp grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. Airampoa species are typically found in wetland or marshy environments, where they thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
