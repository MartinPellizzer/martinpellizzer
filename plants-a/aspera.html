Aspera

Aspera, commonly known as the roughleaf plant, is a species characterized by its distinctive foliage and unique growth habits.

It typically features serrated or lobed leaves that provide a rugged appearance, often with a textured surface. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various climates. Aspera is native to temperate regions and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
