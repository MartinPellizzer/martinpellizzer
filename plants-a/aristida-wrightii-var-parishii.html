Aristida wrightii var. parishii

Aristida wrightii var. parishii, commonly known as the Parish's threeawn, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces dense, spiky seed heads that are typical of the grass family Poaceae. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a bluish-green color, and it is well adapted to drought conditions.

This variety is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and its low maintenance requirements.
