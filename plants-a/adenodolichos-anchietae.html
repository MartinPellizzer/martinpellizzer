Adenodolichos anchietae

Adenodolichos anchietae, commonly known as the Angolan cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Angola.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It has potential agricultural value due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological and possibly nutritional studies in its native habitat.
