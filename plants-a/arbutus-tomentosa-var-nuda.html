Arbutus tomentosa var. nuda

Arbutus tomentosa var. nuda, commonly known as the Florida strawberry tree, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage and produces small, red berries that are edible. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with thick, leathery leaves that remain throughout the year. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry woodlands.

The Florida strawberry tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
