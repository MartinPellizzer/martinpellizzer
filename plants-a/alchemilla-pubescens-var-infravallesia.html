Alchemilla pubescens var. infravallesia

Alchemilla pubescens var. infravallesia, commonly known as the woolly lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe.

It features a rosette of hairy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in loose, open clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural habitats.
