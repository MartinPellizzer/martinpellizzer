Alyssum alpestre proles gerardi

Alyssum alpestre proles gerardi, commonly known as the alpine alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

This species is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for alpine gardens.
