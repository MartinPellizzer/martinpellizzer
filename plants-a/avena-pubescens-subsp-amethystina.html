Avena pubescens subsp. amethystina

Avena pubescens subsp.

amethystina, commonly known as purple oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green to purple hue.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its fibrous roots contribute to soil stabilization, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
