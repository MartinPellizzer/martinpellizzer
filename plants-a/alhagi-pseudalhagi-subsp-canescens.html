Alhagi pseudalhagi subsp. canescens

Alhagi pseudalhagi subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the desert shrub or sand alhagi, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its woody stems and small, greenish-gray leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by pods containing several seeds. It is well-suited to sandy soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is often used for soil stabilization and has some medicinal uses in traditional practices.
