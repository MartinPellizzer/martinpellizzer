Acacia undulifolia var. humilis

Acacia undulifolia var. humilis, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Australia.

It features slender, wiry stems and produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval leaflets. It thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing dunes.
