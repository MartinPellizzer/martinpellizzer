Anadelphia trispiculata

Anadelphia trispiculata, commonly known as the three-spined lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of pale yellow to white flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins and produces small, three-lobed berries that mature to a dark blue color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Anadelphia trispiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
