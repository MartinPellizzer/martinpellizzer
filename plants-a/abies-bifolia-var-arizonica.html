Abies bifolia var. arizonica

Abies bifolia var. arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona fir, is a variant of the two-leaf fir native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dense foliage, and dark green needles that are slightly twisted. The tree typically grows in high-elevation forests, preferring cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its cones are small and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
