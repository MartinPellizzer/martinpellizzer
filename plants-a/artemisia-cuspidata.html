Artemisia cuspidata

Artemisia cuspidata, commonly known as narrowleaf wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and slender, often branching stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical shapes. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.

Artemisia cuspidata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
