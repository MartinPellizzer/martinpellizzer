Allium vavilovii

Allium vavilovii, commonly known as the Vavilov's garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces large, fleshy bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.

Allium vavilovii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
