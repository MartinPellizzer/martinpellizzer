Anisopappus tenerus

Anisopappus tenerus, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, pappus-equipped seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread across arid and semi-arid environments. It is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with minimal water, featuring succulent stems and a deep root system.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
