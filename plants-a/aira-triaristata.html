Aira triaristata

Aira triaristata, commonly known as the common air plant, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia and Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in a panicle inflorescence. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its hardiness and adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
