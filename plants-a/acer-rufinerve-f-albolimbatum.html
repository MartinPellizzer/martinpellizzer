Acer rufinerve f. albolimbatum

Acer rufinerve f. albolimbatum, commonly known as the white-veined maple, is a rare and distinctive variant of the red-veined maple.

It is characterized by its striking white veins on the leaves, which contrast sharply with the green foliage. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub native to China, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a serrated margin and a smooth, glossy texture.

This cultivar is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique foliage.
