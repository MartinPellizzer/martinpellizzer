Acrostichum pellucidomarginatum

Acrostichum pellucidomarginatum, commonly known as the glass-margin fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the fern family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The leaves are typically light green with a translucent margin, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant's rhizomes are fibrous and contribute to its ability to spread and colonize new areas.
