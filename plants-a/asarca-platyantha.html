Asarca platyantha

Asarca platyantha, commonly known as the broad-leafed asarum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, broad leaves and unique, maroon-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

Asarca platyantha is also notable for its aromatic properties and historical use in traditional medicine.
