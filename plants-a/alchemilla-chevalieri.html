Alchemilla chevalieri

Alchemilla chevalieri, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Alchemilla chevalieri is often used in rock gardens and alpine gardens due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
