Arkezostis maximowiczii

Arkezostis maximowiczii, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to subarctic regions. The plant features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture that helps retain moisture.

Arkezostis maximowiczii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with cool, humid conditions.
