Adiantum triphyllum

Adiantum triphyllum, commonly known as the southern maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate (three-part) fronds that are often described as resembling lace or hair. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories. Its leaves are bipinnate, with a distinctive appearance that makes it a popular ornamental plant.

Adiantum triphyllum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for small organisms.
