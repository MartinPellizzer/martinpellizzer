Astragalus kurrumensis

Astragalus kurrumensis, commonly known as Kurrum milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Astragalus kurrumensis is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
