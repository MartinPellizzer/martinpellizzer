Anthyllis vulnerarioides subsp. multifolia

Anthyllis vulnerarioides subsp.

multifolia, commonly known as the creeping everlasting or sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, yellow flowers clustered in dense heads.

The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often used in garden settings for its ornamental value.

The subspecies multifolia is distinguished by its more compact growth and greater number of flowers compared to other subspecies.
