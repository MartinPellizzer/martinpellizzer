Astragalus calycosus

Astragalus calycosus, commonly known as the California milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus calycosus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local insect populations.
