Arisaema amurense subsp. robustum

Arisaema amurense subsp.

robustum, commonly known as the Chinese Taro or the robust arisaema, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia. It features a stout rhizome and produces a single, large, arrow-shaped leaf that can reach up to 1 meter in length.

The plant's inflorescence consists of a spadix enclosed within a modified leaf called a spathe, which is typically green with a yellowish or reddish tint. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
