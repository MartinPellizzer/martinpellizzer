Acacia acuminata var. latifolia

Acacia acuminata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically broad and oval in shape, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are a key feature for identification. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
