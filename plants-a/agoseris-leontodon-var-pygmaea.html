Agoseris leontodon var. pygmaea

Agoseris leontodon var. pygmaea, commonly known as the pygmy hawkweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact rosette of basal leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing food for various insect species.
