Agave caratas

Agave caratas, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, blue-gray leaves that are lined with sharp spines. The plant produces a tall, branched flower stalk that blooms with small, white flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its fleshy leaves.

Agave caratas is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
