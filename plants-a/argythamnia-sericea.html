Argythamnia sericea

Argythamnia sericea, commonly known as silverleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its root system is deep and drought-resistant, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

Argythamnia sericea is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in dryland gardens.
