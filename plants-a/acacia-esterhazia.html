Acacia esterhazia

Acacia esterhazia, commonly known as the Esterhazy acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and often leguminous flowers. The plant produces small, yellow, pod-like fruits that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. Its ability to thrive in dry conditions makes it an important species for soil stabilization and ecological resilience.

Acacia esterhazia is also valued for its hard, durable wood and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
