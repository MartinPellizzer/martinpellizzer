Achillea tuzsonii

Achillea tuzsonii, commonly known as Tuzson's yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and has finely divided, aromatic leaves. It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas.

Tuzson's yarrow is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
