Aristolochia lingua var. acuminata

Aristolochia lingua var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its unique, hooded flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant has heart-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is part of the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, twisting floral structure.
