Agropyron pectiniforme f. barbatum

Agropyron pectiniforme f. barbatum, commonly known as the barbate wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
