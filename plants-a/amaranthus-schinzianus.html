Amaranthus schinzianus

Amaranthus schinzianus, commonly known as the red amaranth, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to purple bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and branched.

Amaranthus schinzianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional cuisines.
