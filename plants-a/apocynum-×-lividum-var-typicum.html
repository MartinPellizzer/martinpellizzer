Apocynum × lividum var. typicum

Apocynum × lividum var. typicum, commonly known as the purple milkweed, is a hybrid plant in the Apocynaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers and hairy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

This species is also an important host plant for the monarch butterfly.
