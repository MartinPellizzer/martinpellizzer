Anthistiria quinqueplex

Anthistiria quinqueplex, commonly known as the five-leaved lily, is a striking member of the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its distinctive five leaflets that form a rosette at the base. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. Its botanical features include showy, bell-shaped blooms that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

Anthistiria quinqueplex is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
