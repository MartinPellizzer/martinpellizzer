Ageratina simulans

Ageratina simulans, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, also known as the sunflower family, and is often found in moist, wooded areas. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Ageratina simulans is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
