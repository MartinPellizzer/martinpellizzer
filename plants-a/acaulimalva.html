Acaulimalva

Acaulimalva, commonly known as the cushion mallow, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and compact growth habit, forming a cushion-like structure. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Acaulimalva thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and ornamental plant for dry, sunny environments.
