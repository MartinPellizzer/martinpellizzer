Acer italum subsp. opulifolium

Acer italum subsp.

opulifolium, commonly known as the Italian maple, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves that are typically green in summer and turn vibrant red or orange in autumn.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and graceful form.
