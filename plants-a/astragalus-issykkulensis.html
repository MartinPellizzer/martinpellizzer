Astragalus issykkulensis

Astragalus issykkulensis, commonly known as the Issykkul milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in nutrient-poor regions.

Its ecological importance includes supporting pollinators and serving as a forage plant for livestock.
