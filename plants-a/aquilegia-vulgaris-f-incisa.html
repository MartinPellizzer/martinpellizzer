Aquilegia vulgaris f. incisa

Aquilegia vulgaris f. incisa, commonly known as the cutleaf columbine, is a cultivar of the common columbine.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it a fern-like appearance. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable addition to gardens and woodland settings.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
