Argythamnia gardneri

Argythamnia gardneri, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with a sprawling habit, featuring woolly, hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptations to harsh, dry environments include deep root systems and the ability to store water in its fleshy stems.

Argythamnia gardneri plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
