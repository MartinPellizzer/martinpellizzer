Amaryllis belladonna var. pallida

Amaryllis belladonna var. pallida, commonly known as the pale belladonna amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features large, showy flowers that are typically pale pink to white in color, with a unique, bell-shaped form. The leaves are broad, strap-like, and often display a variegated pattern of green and white. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy to bloom successfully.
