Alpinia amentacea

Alpinia amentacea, commonly known as the fragrant ginger or Chinese ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of pale yellow to white flowers, which are fragrant and attract pollinators.

Alpinia amentacea is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
