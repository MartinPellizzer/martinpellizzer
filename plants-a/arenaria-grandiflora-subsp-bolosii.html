Arenaria grandiflora subsp. bolosii

Arenaria grandiflora subsp.

bolosii, commonly known as Bolosii's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States. It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant features small, fleshy leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a compact, cushion-like growth form.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy or gravelly soils.
