Adenodolichos

Adenodolichos, commonly known as the horse bean or broad bean, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant is native to warm temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.

Adenodolichos is cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines around the world.
