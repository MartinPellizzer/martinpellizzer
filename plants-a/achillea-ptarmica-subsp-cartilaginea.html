Achillea ptarmica subsp. cartilaginea

Achillea ptarmica subsp.

cartilaginea, commonly known as the cartilaginous yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-green leaves and flat-topped clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its thickened, cartilaginous leaf bases, which give it its common name.
