Amelanchier leiopetala

Amelanchier leiopetala, commonly known as the smooth-flowered serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant produces edible, dark red to black berries in late summer, which are rich in nutrients and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically oval with a smooth texture and turn vibrant yellow or orange in the fall.

Amelanchier leiopetala is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
