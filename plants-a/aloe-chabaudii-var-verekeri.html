Aloe chabaudii var. verekeri

Aloe chabaudii var. verekeri, commonly known as the Chabaud's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with a waxy coating. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and are also popular as ornamental plants.
