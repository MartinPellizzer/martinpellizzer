Acalypha prunifolia

Acalypha prunifolia, commonly known as the roseleaf alyssum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, pink to red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Acalypha prunifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
