Anil efoliata

Anil efoliata, commonly known as the silverleaf anil, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its growth habit is usually shrub-like, with multiple stems emerging from the base.

Anil efoliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
