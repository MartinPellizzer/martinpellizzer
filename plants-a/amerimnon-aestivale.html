Amerimnon aestivale

Amerimnon aestivale, commonly known as the summer aster, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30-60 cm, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and showy purple or blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-resistant ornamental plants.

Amerimnon aestivale is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
