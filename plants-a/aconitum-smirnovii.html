Aconitum smirnovii

Aconitum smirnovii, commonly known as Smirnov's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense racemes. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a basal rosette of large, deeply lobed foliage.

Aconitum smirnovii is known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that make it highly dangerous to both humans and animals.
