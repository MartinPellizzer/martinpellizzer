Andrea selloana

Andrea selloana, commonly known as the Sello's grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
