Annona schunkei

Annona schunkei, commonly known as the Brazilian pawpaw, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with smooth, greyish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that are typically followed by large, round fruits resembling papayas. The fruit has a sweet, aromatic pulp with a texture similar to a pear, and it is rich in nutrients such as vitamins and minerals.

Annona schunkei is often cultivated for its edible fruit and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
