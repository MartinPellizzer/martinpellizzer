Acaena Echinata Var. Subglabricalyx

Acaena echinata var. subglabricalyx is a unique variant of the New Zealand native plant known for its distinctive appearance and ecological significance. This plant is characterized by its spiny bracts and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in coastal and subalpine regions, often found in rocky and sandy soils. Its hardy nature makes it an important species for stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife. Due to its limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial to protect this rare botanical treasure.
