Acacia insolita subsp. recurva

Acacia insolita subsp.

recurva, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, slender stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided.

The plant produces yellow globular flowers arranged in compact inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

The wood of this species is lightweight and has been historically used by Indigenous Australians for various purposes.
