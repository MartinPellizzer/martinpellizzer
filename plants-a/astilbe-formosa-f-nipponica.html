Astilbe formosa f. nipponica

Astilbe formosa f. nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese astilbe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, delicate, lace-like flowers in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is well-suited for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its elegant foliage and prolonged flowering period, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-loving plants.
