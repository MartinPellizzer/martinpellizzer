Armeria malacitana

Armeria malacitana, commonly known as the rock thrift, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. It has basal leaves that are linear and slightly hairy, and its stems are often branched.

Armeria malacitana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
