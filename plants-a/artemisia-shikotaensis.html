Artemisia shikotaensis

Artemisia shikotaensis, commonly known as Shikota's wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its small yellow flowers are arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family.

Artemisia shikotaensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
