Argyrocome bracteata

Argyrocome bracteata, commonly known as the silver-bracted orchid, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique silver-colored bracts that encase the flower spikes. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and white or pale green, with a delicate appearance.

Due to its restricted habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
