Acacia senegal var. leiorachis

Acacia senegal var. leiorachis, commonly known as the gum arabic acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless branches and bipinnate leaves that are typically small and delicate. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing a viscous gum, which is harvested for its economic value. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

This variety is closely related to other acacia species but is distinguished by its unique pod structure and gum composition.
