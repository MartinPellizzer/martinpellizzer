Aristida moandaensis

Aristida moandaensis, commonly known as the Moanda grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that are characteristic of the Aristida genus. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct sheath at the base.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and drought tolerance.
