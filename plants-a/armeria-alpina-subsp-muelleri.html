Armeria alpina subsp. muelleri

Armeria alpina subsp.

muelleri, commonly known as the alpine thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe. It features dense clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hair, aiding in water retention in harsh environments. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
