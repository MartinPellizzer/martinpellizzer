Arisaema mildbraedii

Arisaema mildbraedii, commonly known as the mildbraed's arum, is a herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features a short rhizome with heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by berries, which are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
