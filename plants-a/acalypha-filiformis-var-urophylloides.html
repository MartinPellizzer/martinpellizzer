Acalypha filiformis var. urophylloides

Acalypha filiformis var. urophylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with distinctive, spiky bracts that give it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for cultivation in tropical gardens.
