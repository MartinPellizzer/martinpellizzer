Aspidium festinum

Aspidium festinum, commonly known as the quick fern, is a species of fern native to various regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the undersides of the fronds, a common reproductive feature in ferns.

Aspidium festinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
