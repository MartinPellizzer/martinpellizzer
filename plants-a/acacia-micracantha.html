Acacia micracantha

Acacia micracantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow floral spikes. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its deep root system. Its flowers attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

The wood of Acacia micracantha is hard and durable, historically used by Indigenous Australians for various purposes.
