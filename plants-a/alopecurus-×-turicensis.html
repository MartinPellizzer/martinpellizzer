Alopecurus × turicensis

Alopecurus × turicensis, commonly known as the meadow foxtail, is a hybrid grass species that occurs naturally in temperate regions.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and spikelets that form dense, grass-like clumps. This plant typically grows in moist soils, often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small, pale green to golden spikelets.

Alopecurus × turicensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control.
