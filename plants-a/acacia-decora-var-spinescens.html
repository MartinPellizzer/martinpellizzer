Acacia decora var. spinescens

Acacia decora var. spinescens, commonly known as the spiny acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, spiny foliage, which provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
