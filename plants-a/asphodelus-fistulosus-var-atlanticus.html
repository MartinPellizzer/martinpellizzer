Asphodelus fistulosus var. atlanticus

Asphodelus fistulosus var. atlanticus, commonly known as the Atlantic asphodel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, spike-like inflorescences with pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or dry grasslands. It is adapted to tolerate saline and arid conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

The plant has historical uses in traditional medicine and is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
