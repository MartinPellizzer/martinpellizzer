Actegeton sarmentosus

Actegeton sarmentosus, commonly known as the creeping actegeton, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features a trailing or creeping stem with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits containing numerous seeds.

Actegeton sarmentosus is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often used in traditional medicine.

Morphology

Actegeton sarmentosus has small, ovate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The stem is slender, woody, and has a rough texture, with a reddish-brown bark that becomes darker with age. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, featuring small white petals and yellow stamens, blooming in spring and being pollinated by insects. The fruit is a dry capsule that releases tiny seeds through wind dispersal.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, aiding in water absorption from the soil.
