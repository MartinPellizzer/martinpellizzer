Artemisia aralensis

Artemisia aralensis, commonly known as the Aral Sea wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and along the edges of lakes. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its strong, distinctive scent.

Artemisia aralensis is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
