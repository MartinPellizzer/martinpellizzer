Acerates viridiflora

Acerates viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal color changes.
