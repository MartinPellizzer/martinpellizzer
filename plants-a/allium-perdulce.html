Allium perdulce

Allium perdulce, commonly known as the sweet leek, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or purple flowers in clusters. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor compared to other alliums.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.
