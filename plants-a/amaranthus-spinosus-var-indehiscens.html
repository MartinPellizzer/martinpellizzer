Amaranthus spinosus var. indehiscens

Amaranthus spinosus var. indehiscens, commonly known as the spiny amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and branched stems covered in spines. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-gray hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, typically in shades of red or purple.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both decorative and culinary purposes.
