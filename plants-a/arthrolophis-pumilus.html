Arthrolophis pumilus

Arthrolophis pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf scincid lizard plant, is a unique species of flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, compact growth habit and possesses specialized structures for water storage, adapting to its often arid environment. The plant features succulent leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, aiding in reducing water loss. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from yellow to red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
