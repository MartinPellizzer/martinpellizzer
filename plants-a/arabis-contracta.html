Arabis contracta

Arabis contracta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of only a few centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and linear, with small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in racemes. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions.

Arabis contracta is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
