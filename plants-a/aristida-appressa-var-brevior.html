Aristida appressa var. brevior

Aristida appressa var. brevior, commonly known as the short-stemmed dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and fine, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often mistaken for cotton or dandelion fluff. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a valuable addition to ecological restoration projects.
