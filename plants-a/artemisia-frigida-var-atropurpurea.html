Artemisia frigida var. atropurpurea

Artemisia frigida var. atropurpurea, commonly known as purplestem sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage with a distinctive purple tinge on the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in dense, rounded clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in open plains and disturbed sites.

Artemisia frigida var. atropurpurea is adapted to cold climates and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant in harsh conditions.
