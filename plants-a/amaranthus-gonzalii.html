Amaranthus gonzalii

Amaranthus gonzalii, commonly known as the Mexican amaranth, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that range in color from red to orange, which are often mistaken for petals. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into three-angled, ribbed seed capsules. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Amaranthus gonzalii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional cuisine and medicine by indigenous communities.
