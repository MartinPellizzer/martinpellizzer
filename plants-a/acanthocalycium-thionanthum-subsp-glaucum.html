Acanthocalycium thionanthum subsp. glaucum

Acanthocalycium thionanthum subsp.

glaucum, commonly known as the blue-tipped cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically covered in a whitish, powdery coating called glaucous. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, and its growth habit is usually solitary or in small clusters.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
