Astragalus shelkovnikovii

Astragalus shelkovnikovii, commonly known as Shelkovnikov's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to poor soil conditions through its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
