Asimina longifolia

Asimina longifolia, commonly known as the longpod sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Asimina longifolia is often found in moist, woodlands and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
