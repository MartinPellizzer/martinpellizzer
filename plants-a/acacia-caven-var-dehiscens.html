Acacia caven var. dehiscens

Acacia caven var. dehiscens, commonly known as the spreading acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features slender, often drooping branches and produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters. The plant has small, bipinnate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its seed pods are woody and split open when mature, dispersing the seeds.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry landscapes.
