Asplenium moorei

Asplenium moorei, commonly known as the Moore's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth pattern. The plant has dark green, leathery fronds that are typically arranged in a tight, compact form. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks.

Asplenium moorei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
