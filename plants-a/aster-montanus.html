Aster montanus

Aster montanus, commonly known as the mountain aster, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its showy purple or blue flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster montanus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
