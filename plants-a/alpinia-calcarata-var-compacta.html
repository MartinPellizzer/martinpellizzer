Alpinia calcarata var. compacta

Alpinia calcarata var. compacta, commonly known as the compact ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, rhizomatous structure. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, glossy surface.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild, spicy fragrance.
