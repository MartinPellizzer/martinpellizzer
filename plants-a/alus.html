Alus

Alus, commonly known as the common alder, is a deciduous tree belonging to the Betulaceae family.

It is characterized by its serrated leaves and catkins, which are its primary floral structures. The tree produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Alus thrives in moist environments, often found near streams and rivers.

Its bark is rough and fissured, providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
