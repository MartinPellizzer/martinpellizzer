Acalypha microstachya

Acalypha microstachya, commonly known as the red-rooted spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its striking red stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-yellow flowers that are often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Acalypha microstachya is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
