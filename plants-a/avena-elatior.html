Avena elatior

Avena elatior, commonly known as the tall oat, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish or yellowish flowers, which are typical of the Poaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue when fresh.

Avena elatior is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in soil stabilization and ecological succession.
