Alchemilla filicaulis var. lasiocalyx

Alchemilla filicaulis var. lasiocalyx, commonly known as the velvetleaf lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a soft, velvety texture on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, rounded clusters called cymes. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream sides.
