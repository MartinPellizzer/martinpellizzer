Aspalathus linguiloba

Aspalathus linguiloba, commonly known as the African red bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, round, red berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Aspalathus linguiloba is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
