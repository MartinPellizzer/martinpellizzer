Andromeda punctata

Andromeda punctata, commonly known as the spotted wintergreen, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 inches. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are dark green with white or yellow spots. Its flowers are bell-shaped and range in color from white to pink, blooming in late winter to early spring.

Andromeda punctata is often found in moist, acidic soils in woodlands and along stream banks.
