Astragalus centralis

Astragalus centralis, commonly known as the Central Astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus centralis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its native habitat.
