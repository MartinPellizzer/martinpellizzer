Arctophila effusa

Arctophila effusa, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its low, mat-forming growth habit and succulent stems. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Arctophila effusa is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
