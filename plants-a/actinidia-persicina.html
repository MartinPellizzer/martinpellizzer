Actinidia persicina

Actinidia persicina, commonly known as the Chinese gooseberry, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

Actinidia persicina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of nutrition.
