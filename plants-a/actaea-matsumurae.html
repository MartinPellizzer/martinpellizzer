Actaea matsumurae

Actaea matsumurae, commonly known as the Japanese black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and large, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers in early summer, followed by red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
