Aconitum aberratum

Aconitum aberratum, commonly known as the aberrant monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, helmet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. Aconitum aberratum is known for its toxic alkaloids, which make it potentially dangerous to humans and animals.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
