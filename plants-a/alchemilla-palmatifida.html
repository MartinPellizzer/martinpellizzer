Alchemilla palmatifida

Alchemilla palmatifida, commonly known as the broad-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lobed, palmately divided leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of silky white hair. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla palmatifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
