Alsine radicans var. capillacea

Alsine radicans var. capillacea, commonly known as creeping alpine sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically forms dense mats of slender, prostrate stems covered with small, rounded leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical features make it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and alpine plantings.
