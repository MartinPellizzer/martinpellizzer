Aegilops caudata var. paucispiculigera

Aegilops caudata var. paucispiculigera, commonly known as the slender wild oat, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and loose, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, two-lobed spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a prominent midrib.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
