Astragalus longiscapus

Astragalus longiscapus, commonly known as the long-stemmed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several oval, smooth seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Astragalus longiscapus thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of alpine and semi-arid environments.
