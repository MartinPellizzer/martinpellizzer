Aponogeton

Aponogeton, commonly known as the water arrowhead, is a genus of aquatic plants native to tropical and subtropical regions.

These plants are characterized by their distinctive arrow-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface. Aponogeton species are often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats. They produce strikingly beautiful flowers that can be white, pink, or red, depending on the species.

Some species are cultivated as ornamental plants in ponds and water gardens due to their aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
