Astragalus dysbatophilus

Astragalus dysbatophilus, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing deep root systems for water absorption. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing species.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across rugged terrain.
