Alstroemeria garaventae subsp.

garaventae, commonly known as the Garaventa alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically found in Chile. It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that are often used in horticultural settings for their ornamental value.
