Artocarpus dasyphyllus

Artocarpus dasyphyllus, commonly known as the rough-leaf breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. The tree produces a distinctive fruit with a thick, fibrous outer layer and a starchy, edible interior. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its fruit, which is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
