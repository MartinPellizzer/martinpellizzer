Aristolochia setulosa

Aristolochia setulosa, commonly known as the hairy pipevine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and striking, purple-tinted, pipe-like flowers. The plant is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic, slightly toxic properties. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
