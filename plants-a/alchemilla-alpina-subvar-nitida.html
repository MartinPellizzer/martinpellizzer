Alchemilla alpina subvar. nitida

Alchemilla alpina subvar. nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in dense, soft hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and alpine landscapes.
