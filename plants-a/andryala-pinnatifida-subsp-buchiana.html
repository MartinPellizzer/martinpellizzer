Andryala pinnatifida subsp. buchiana

Andryala pinnatifida subsp.

buchiana, commonly known as the Buchian lily, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a short, stout rhizome.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
