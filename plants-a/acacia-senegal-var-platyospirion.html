Acacia senegal var. platyospirion

Acacia senegal var. platyospirion, commonly known as the gum arabic acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bipinnate leaves composed of small, opposite leaflets. The plant produces yellow to golden flowers that are followed by elongated, flat pods containing the economically valuable gum arabic. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

The plant is well-adapted to drought conditions, making it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
