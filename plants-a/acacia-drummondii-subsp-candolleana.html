Acacia drummondii subsp. candolleana

Acacia drummondii subsp.

candolleana, commonly known as the Drummond's wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia. It features narrow, phyllodes that are typically linear to lanceolate in shape, often with a slight twist.

The plant produces small, yellow globular flowers arranged in compact clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is well adapted to dry environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping in suitable climates.
