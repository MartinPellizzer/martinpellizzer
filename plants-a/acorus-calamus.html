Acorus calamus

Acorus calamus, commonly known as sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in temperate regions.

It features a rhizomatous root system and tall, sword-shaped leaves that are usually green with a yellowish midrib. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense spikes, which are often submerged or partially submerged in water. Acorus calamus is known for its aromatic rhizomes, which have been used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to aquatic environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic ecosystems.
