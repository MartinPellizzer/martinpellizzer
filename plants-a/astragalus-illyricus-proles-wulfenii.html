Astragalus illyricus proles wulfenii

Astragalus illyricus proles wulfenii, commonly known as the Illyrian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It produces pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
