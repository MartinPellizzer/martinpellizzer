Agrostis castellana var. olivetorum

Agrostis castellana var. olivetorum, commonly known as the olive thyme grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in poor soils and its resilience to drought conditions.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological adaptability and historical use in traditional landscaping.
