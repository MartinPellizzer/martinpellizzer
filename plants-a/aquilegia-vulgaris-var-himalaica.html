Aquilegia vulgaris var. himalaica

Aquilegia vulgaris var. himalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan columbine, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It features showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, pinnately arranged leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
