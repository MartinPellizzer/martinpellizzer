Asplundia australis

Asplundia australis, commonly known as the Australian arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which includes plants such as taro and calla lilies. The plant features a tuberous root system that is the primary part used for culinary purposes. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a waxy texture.

Asplundia australis is valued for its starchy roots, which can be cooked and eaten similarly to yams or potatoes.
