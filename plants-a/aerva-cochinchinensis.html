Aerva cochinchinensis

Aerva cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese horsegram or slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. Aerva cochinchinensis is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields due to its adaptability to various soil types.

It is also valued for its nutritional content and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
