Acacia tubulifera

Acacia tubulifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It features feathery, bipinnate leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods. It is often found in sandy soils and is well-suited to drought conditions.

Acacia tubulifera plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
