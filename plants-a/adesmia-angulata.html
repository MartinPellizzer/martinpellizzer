Adesmia angulata

Adesmia angulata, commonly known as the angular adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its root system is capable of fixing nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
