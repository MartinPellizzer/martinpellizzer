Achyropsis filifolia

Achyropsis filifolia, commonly known as the hairleaf sandmat, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant typically grows close to the ground, forming dense mats that can stabilize sandy soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish or yellowish and appear in clusters.

Achyropsis filifolia is well adapted to drought conditions and can survive with minimal rainfall.
