Alpinia simsii

Alpinia simsii, commonly known as the fragrant ginger or mountain ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of showy, white to pale yellow flowers, which are fragrant and attract pollinators.

Alpinia simsii is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its potential medicinal properties.
