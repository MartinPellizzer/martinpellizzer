Allium ubinicum

Allium ubinicum, commonly known as the Siberian garlic, is a perennial plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Russia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks, and is characterized by its bulbous structure and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or purple flowers arranged in a spherical cluster, typical of many Allium species. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is adapted to harsh environments, showcasing resilience to cold temperatures and poor soil conditions.
