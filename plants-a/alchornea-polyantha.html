Alchornea polyantha

Alchornea polyantha, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silky senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically covered in fine, soft hairs that give the plant its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. Alchornea polyantha is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
