Acer pictum f. piliferum

Acer pictum f. piliferum, commonly known as the silky maple, is a unique variant of the striped maple.

It is characterized by its finely pubescent leaves that give it a soft, silvery appearance. This plant is native to the northeastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage displays a striking contrast between the green upper surface and the pale, hairy underside.

The silky maple is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to shaded environments.
