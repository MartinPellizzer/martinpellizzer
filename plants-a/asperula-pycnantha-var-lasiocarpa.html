Asperula pycnantha var. lasiocarpa

Asperula pycnantha var. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the woolly evergreen azalea, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It features dense, dark green foliage that is covered in a fine, white to grayish pubescence, giving it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its lush foliage and vibrant floral display.
