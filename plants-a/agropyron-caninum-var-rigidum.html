Agropyron caninum var. rigidum

Agropyron caninum var. rigidum, commonly known as rigid wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and grassland ecosystems.

This variety is valued for its drought tolerance and is used in forage and soil stabilization.
