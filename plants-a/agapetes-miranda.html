Agapetes miranda

Agapetes miranda, commonly known as the silverleaf agapete, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery or grayish pubescence, giving it its common name. It produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring or early summer.

Agapetes miranda is often found in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions.
