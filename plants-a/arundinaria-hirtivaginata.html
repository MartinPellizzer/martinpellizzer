Arundinaria hirtivaginata

Arundinaria hirtivaginata, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a species of grass native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture due to the presence of stiff hairs.

Arundinaria hirtivaginata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
