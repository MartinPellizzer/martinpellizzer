Acnida cuspidata

Acnida cuspidata, commonly known as the prickly acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing adaptations to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which are attractive to pollinators. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it has sharp, spiny structures that provide defense against herbivores.

The plant is also valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
