Anthemis homalolepis

Anthemis homalolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellow-orange flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
