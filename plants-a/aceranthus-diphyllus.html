Aceranthus diphyllus

Aceranthus diphyllus, commonly known as the two-leaved acanthus, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are divided into two lobes, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces showy, spire-like inflorescences with small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
