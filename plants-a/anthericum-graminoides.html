Anthericum graminoides

Anthericum graminoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fern, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

The spores are produced in a specialized structure called a sorus, which is located on the underside of the leaves.
