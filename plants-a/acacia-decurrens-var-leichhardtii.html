Acacia decurrens var. leichhardtii

Acacia decurrens var. leichhardtii, commonly known as the Leichhardt wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

The species is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
