Astragalus trimestris var. aegiceras

Astragalus trimestris var. aegiceras, commonly known as the coastal milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to coastal areas of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along the edges of salt marshes.

This species is adapted to coastal environments and is tolerant of salt spray and wind.
