Amelanchier whitei

Amelanchier whitei, commonly known as the white serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that ripen to a red or purple color in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in woodland edges or open forests.

Amelanchier whitei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for birds and pollinators.
