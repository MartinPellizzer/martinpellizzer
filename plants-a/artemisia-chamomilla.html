Artemisia chamomilla

Artemisia chamomilla, commonly known as German chamomile, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. It is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and is used in herbal medicine.

Its essential oils, extracted from the flowers, are valued for their calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
