Astragalus ayatollahii

Astragalus ayatollahii, commonly known as the Ayatollah's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
