Aleurites pentaphyllus

Aleurites pentaphyllus, commonly known as the candlenut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves with five leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. The seeds are rich in oil and are used for both culinary and industrial purposes.

It also plays a significant role in traditional medicine and cultural practices in its native regions.
