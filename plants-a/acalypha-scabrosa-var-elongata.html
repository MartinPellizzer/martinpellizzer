Acalypha scabrosa var. elongata

Acalypha scabrosa var. elongata, commonly known as the longleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
