Aster sparsiflorus

Aster sparsiflorus, commonly known as the sparseflower aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the fall. Its flowering period is late summer to early autumn, and it is adapted to arid environments.

Aster sparsiflorus is often found in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8.
