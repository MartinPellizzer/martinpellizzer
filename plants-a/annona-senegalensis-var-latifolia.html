Annona senegalensis var. latifolia

Annona senegalensis var. latifolia, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a tropical shrub native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and produces large, yellowish-green fruits that are edible when ripe. The plant belongs to the Annonaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types. Its flowers are typically small and greenish-white, often clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is used both as food and for traditional medicinal purposes in many regions.
