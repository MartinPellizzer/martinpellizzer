Alsodeia whytei

Alsodeia whytei, commonly known as the Whyte's alsodea, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features delicate, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring. It is often found in coastal or mountainous regions, where it adapts well to dry and nutrient-poor conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
