Adesmia meyeniana

Adesmia meyeniana, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that are clustered in racemes, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, and it typically grows in dry, open habitats.

Adesmia meyeniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
