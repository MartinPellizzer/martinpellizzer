Atherosperma sassafras

Atherosperma sassafras, commonly known as the sassafras tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, heart-shaped leaves that often have three lobes. The tree produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used for their medicinal properties, including antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
