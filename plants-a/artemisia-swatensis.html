Artemisia swatensis

Artemisia swatensis, commonly known as Swat wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Swat Valley in Pakistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found at higher altitudes.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been traditionally used in local herbal remedies.
