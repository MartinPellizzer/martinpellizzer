Antitragus

Antitragus, commonly known as the "beard plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its unique, hairy, and often branched stems that resemble a beard, hence its common name. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The flowers are small and clustered in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Antitragus species are often found in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.
