Amaryllis jacobaea

Amaryllis jacobaea, commonly known as the Jacobaea amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
