Aster lateriflorus var. angustifolius

Aster lateriflorus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and cylindrical stems. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its narrow, lance-shaped leaves are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small, blue to purple flowers in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
