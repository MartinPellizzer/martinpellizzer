Acacia brachyptera

Acacia brachyptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, feather-like leaves and yellow floral spikes that appear in spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and inland regions.

Acacia brachyptera is an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
