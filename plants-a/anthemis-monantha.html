Anthemis monantha

Anthemis monantha, commonly known as the singleflower ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and disturbed areas. Its flowers are yellow with a central disc, and it produces small, elongated seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The plant has a long flowering period, often blooming from late spring through early autumn.
