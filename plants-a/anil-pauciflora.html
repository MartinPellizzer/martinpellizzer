Anil pauciflora

Anil pauciflora, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy seed heads that aid in wind dispersal. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Anil pauciflora is valued for its resilience and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for wildlife.
