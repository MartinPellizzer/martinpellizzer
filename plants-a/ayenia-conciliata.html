Ayenia conciliata

Ayenia conciliata, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves and produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. Ayenia conciliata is often found in sandy or rocky soils along the coastlines.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
