Axonopus caulescens

Axonopus caulescens, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense mats near water bodies. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize new areas effectively.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fibrous roots and ability to thrive in saturated soils.

Morphology

Axonopus caulescens has narrow, grass-like leaves that are linear in shape, arranged in a basal rosette, and typically measure about 2-5 cm in length with a bluish-green color.

The stem is short and creeping, with a smooth, pale green bark that grows horizontally along the ground. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers in clusters, blooming during the summer months, and is likely pollinated by wind. The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that disperses its seeds through wind, allowing them to travel short distances.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, anchoring the plant in moist, sandy soils.
