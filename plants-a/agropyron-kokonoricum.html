Agropyron kokonoricum

Agropyron kokonoricum, commonly known as the Kokonori wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Agropyron kokonoricum is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
