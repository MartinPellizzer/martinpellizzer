Amaranthus brandegeei

Amaranthus brandegeei, commonly known as the desert amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and production of small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid environments, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its leaves are often used in traditional cuisines, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal.

Amaranthus brandegeei plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various desert wildlife species.
