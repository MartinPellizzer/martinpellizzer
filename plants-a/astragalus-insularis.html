Astragalus insularis

Astragalus insularis, commonly known as the island milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small, herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow to green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are characteristic of the Fabaceae family. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus insularis is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
