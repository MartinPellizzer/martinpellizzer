Absinthium sericeum

Absinthium sericeum, commonly known as silverleaf wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, silver-haired stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Absinthium sericeum is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
