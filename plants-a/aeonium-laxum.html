Aeonium laxum

Aeonium laxum, commonly known as the Tree Aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped arrangement of fleshy, blue-green leaves that can grow up to 12 inches in length. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers during the summer months, which are borne on tall, branching stems. It is a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
