Anacamptis × gennarii

Anacamptis × gennarii, commonly known as the Gennari orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Anacamptis morio and Anacamptis pyramidalis.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats across Europe.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
