Agrimonia odorata

Agrimonia odorata, commonly known as sweet agrimony, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and oval, serrated leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. Its aromatic leaves and flowers have been used in traditional herbal medicine for their astringent and anti-inflammatory properties.

Agrimonia odorata is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
