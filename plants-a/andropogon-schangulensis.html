Andropogon schangulensis

Andropogon schangulensis, commonly known as Schangulensis grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, showcasing adaptability to various environmental conditions. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a panicle, which produces small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Andropogon schangulensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
