Acacia mimica var. mimica

Acacia mimica var. mimica, commonly known as the mimosa acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are typically small and feathery, and its yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to dry conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas make it an important pioneer species in ecological restoration.
