Adenostemma roylei

Adenostemma roylei, commonly known as the Indian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including India and Nepal.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and sometimes glandular. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, usually pink or purple in color.

Adenostemma roylei is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
