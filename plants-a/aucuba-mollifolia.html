Aucuba mollifolia

Aucuba mollifolia, commonly known as the Japanese aucuba, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white margins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by red berries. It is typically grown as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes for its attractive foliage.

Aucuba mollifolia is relatively low maintenance and thrives in partial to full shade.
