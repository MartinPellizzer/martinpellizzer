Alangium hirsutum

Alangium hirsutum, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Plantain lacebark (Alangiaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Alangium hirsutum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
