Arthraxon lanceolatus var. serrulatus

Arthraxon lanceolatus var. serrulatus, commonly known as the serrated arthraxon, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features erect, slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinct, aromatic scent.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.
