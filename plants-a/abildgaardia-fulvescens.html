Abildgaardia fulvescens

Abildgaardia fulvescens, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces striking clusters of small, red-brown flowers with a distinctive yellow-green throat, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
