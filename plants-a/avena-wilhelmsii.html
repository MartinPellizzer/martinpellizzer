Avena wilhelmsii

Avena wilhelmsii, commonly known as the Wilhelmsii oat, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Avena wilhelmsii is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
