Astragalus alpinus f. leucanthus

Astragalus alpinus f. leucanthus, commonly known as the alpine milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to white flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats.
