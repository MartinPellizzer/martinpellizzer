Acer pseudoplatanus f. metallicum

Acer pseudoplatanus f. metallicum, commonly known as the silver maple, is a variant of the common maple tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a striking appearance. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden use.
