Alsophila alternans

Alsophila alternans, commonly known as the golden tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to three meters. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived species. Its fronds are typically bright green, with a distinctive golden hue on the underside, contributing to its ornamental value.

Alsophila alternans prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
