Agropyron caninum var. mitchellii

Agropyron caninum var. mitchellii, commonly known as Mitchell's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for erosion control and livestock forage.
