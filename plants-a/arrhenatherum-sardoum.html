Arrhenatherum sardoum

Arrhenatherum sardoum, commonly known as the Sardinian meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sardinia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, marshes, and wetlands. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
