Archidendron multifoliolatum

Archidendron multifoliolatum, commonly known as the large-leaved legume or the tropical senna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which can be highly variable in shape and size, often featuring multiple leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
