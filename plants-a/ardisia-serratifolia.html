Ardisia serratifolia

Ardisia serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated ardisia, is a evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Ardisia serratifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
