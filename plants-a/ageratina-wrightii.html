Ageratina wrightii

Ageratina wrightii, commonly known as Wright's blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small blue or purple clusters. The plant features finely divided, silvery-green leaves and produces fluffy, white to blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Ageratina wrightii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
