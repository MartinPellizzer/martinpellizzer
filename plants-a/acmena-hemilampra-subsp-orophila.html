Acmena hemilampra subsp. orophila

Acmena hemilampra subsp.

orophila, commonly known as the mountain acmena, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Australia. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in spring. Its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release small, glossy seeds.

This species is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in open forests or rocky areas.
