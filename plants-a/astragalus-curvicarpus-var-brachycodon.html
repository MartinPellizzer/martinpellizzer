Astragalus curvicarpus var. brachycodon

Astragalus curvicarpus var. brachycodon, commonly known as the short-stalked milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in pairs.
