Acacia oxyosprion var. pubescens

Acacia oxyosprion var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It features slender, often branched stems covered in a dense layer of fine, pubescent hairs. The plant produces small, yellow, globular flowers that appear in winter, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, oval leaflets that are also hairy.

This species is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant in harsh environments.
