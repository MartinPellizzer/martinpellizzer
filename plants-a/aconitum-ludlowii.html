Aconitum ludlowii

Aconitum ludlowii, commonly known as Ludlow's monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments, typical of the genus Aconitum. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
