Aechmea milsteiniana

Aechmea milsteiniana, commonly known as the Milstein's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the subtropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns and can vary in texture from smooth to fibrous.

Aechmea milsteiniana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Morphology

Aechmea milsteiniana has rosette-shaped leaves that are fleshy, strap-like, and arranged in a tight spiral around the central rosette, with a bright green color and a waxy texture.

The stem is short and often hidden beneath the leaves, with a smooth, dark green surface. The plant produces inflorescences with showy, spathaceous bracts that enclose clusters of small, white to pink flowers, which bloom during the summer months and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind and water.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for water storage, supporting the plant's ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
