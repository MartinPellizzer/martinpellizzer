Aechmea milsteiniana

Aechmea milsteiniana, commonly known as the Milstein's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the subtropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns and can vary in texture from smooth to fibrous.

Aechmea milsteiniana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value.
