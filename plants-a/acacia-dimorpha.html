Acacia dimorpha

Acacia dimorpha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system. Its flowers are often visited by native bees and birds, contributing to local biodiversity.

Acacia dimorpha is also valued for its hardy nature and is used in ecological restoration projects.
