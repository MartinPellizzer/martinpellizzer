Anemone pulsatilla subsp. gotlandica

Anemone pulsatilla subsp.

gotlandica, commonly known as the Gotland anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Baltic region, particularly Gotland. It features a short, stout rhizome and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white.

The plant has palmately divided, lobed leaves that are hairy and often covered in fine white down. It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and heaths.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional remedies.
