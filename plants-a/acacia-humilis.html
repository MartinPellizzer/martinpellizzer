Acacia humilis

Acacia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf acacia, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, flat, green pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia humilis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
