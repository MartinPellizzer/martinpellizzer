Ardisia pedunculata

Ardisia pedunculata, commonly known as the clustered beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, clustered berries that turn from white to red as they mature. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, while its flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters.

Ardisia pedunculata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental berries and adaptability to various soil conditions.
