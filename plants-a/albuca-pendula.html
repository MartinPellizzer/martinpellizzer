Albuca pendula

Albuca pendula, commonly known as the hanging albuca, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Albuca pendula is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
