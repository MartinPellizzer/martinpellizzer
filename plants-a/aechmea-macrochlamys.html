Aechmea macrochlamys

Aechmea macrochlamys, commonly known as the giant bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater and nutrients. The plant's foliage is often adorned with vibrant, colorful bracts that can range from red to orange, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aechmea macrochlamys is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in controlled environments.

Morphology

Aechmea macrochlamys has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, strap-like, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a waxy, bluish-green surface.

The plant has a short, thick stem covered in a fibrous, moss-like sheath, and it grows upright with a compact, cushion-like form. Its flowers are clustered in a dense inflorescence, with purple to pink bracts and yellowish tepals, blooming during the dry season and pollinated by wind and insects. The fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that disperses seeds through animal ingestion and water.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the air and soil.
