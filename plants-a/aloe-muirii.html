Aloe muirii

Aloe muirii, commonly known as the Muir's aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, typical of desert plants. The leaves are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. It produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes at the top of the plant.

Aloe muirii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
