Anthemis cretica subsp. candicans

Anthemis cretica subsp.

candicans, commonly known as the Greek tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of about 30 cm.

The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense, hemispherical heads, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
