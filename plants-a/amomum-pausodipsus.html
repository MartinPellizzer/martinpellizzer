Amomum pausodipsus

Amomum pausodipsus, commonly known as the fragrant ginger or Chinese ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly China, India, and Indonesia. The plant features tall, slender stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the top of the plant.

The rhizomes of Amomum pausodipsus are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice due to their aromatic properties.
