Asperula cynanchica f. girbaui

Asperula cynanchica f. girbaui, commonly known as the Girbaud's asperula, is a rare and unique variant of the asperula species.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to specific regions in Europe, particularly in the Pyrenees. The plant features small, dark green leaves that are densely packed along its stems. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in clusters during the spring season.

This form is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its compact growth habit.
