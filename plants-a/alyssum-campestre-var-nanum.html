Alyssum campestre var. nanum

Alyssum campestre var. nanum, commonly known as dwarf alpine alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces numerous small, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Alyssum campestre var. nanum is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for alpine gardens and rockeries.
