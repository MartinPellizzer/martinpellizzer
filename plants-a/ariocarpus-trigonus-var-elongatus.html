Ariocarpus trigonus var. elongatus

Ariocarpus trigonus var. elongatus, commonly known as the "paddle cactus," is a unique succulent native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are ribbed and often covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its slow growth and distinctive appearance make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
