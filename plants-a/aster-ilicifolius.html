Aster ilicifolius

Aster ilicifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, lavender-blue to purple flowers in the fall, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Its foliage and floral structure contribute to its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
