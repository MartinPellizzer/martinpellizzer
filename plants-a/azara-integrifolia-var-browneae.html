Azara integrifolia var. browneae

Azara integrifolia var. browneae, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pouteria integrifolia*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sweet, cherry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
