Acalypha chamaedrifolia var. fissa

Acalypha chamaedrifolia var. fissa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves and stems are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
