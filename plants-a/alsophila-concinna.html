Alsophila concinna

Alsophila concinna, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the world.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its rhizome is robust, allowing it to anchor firmly in soil and support its height.

Alsophila concinna is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
