Alchemilla saxatilis subsp. transiens

Alchemilla saxatilis subsp.

transiens, commonly known as the rock alchemilla, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense mat-forming growth.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically basal, lanceolate, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky crevices or gravelly slopes.
