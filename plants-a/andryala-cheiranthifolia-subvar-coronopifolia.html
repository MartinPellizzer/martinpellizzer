Andryala cheiranthifolia subvar. coronopifolia

Andryala cheiranthifolia subvar. coronopifolia, commonly known as the mountain sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are lanceolate and progressively smaller. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its flowers are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is adapted to cold, alpine environments and is often found in rocky soils.
