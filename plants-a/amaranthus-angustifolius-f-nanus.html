Amaranthus angustifolius f. nanus

Amaranthus angustifolius f. nanus, commonly known as dwarf amaranth, is a compact, herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

It is a variety of the common amaranth, which is native to the Americas and has been cultivated for thousands of years. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it ideal for small spaces or container gardening. Its small, clustered flowers are usually green or red, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible seeds.

This variety is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its drought tolerance.
