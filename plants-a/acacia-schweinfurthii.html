Acacia schweinfurthii

Acacia schweinfurthii, commonly known as the fever tree, is a medium-sized deciduous tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and long, slender, feathery leaves that are often shed during the dry season. The tree produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which attract a variety of pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural spread.

Acacia schweinfurthii is often found in savannas and woodland areas, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for wildlife.
