Aster drummondii var. parviceps

Aster drummondii var. parviceps, commonly known as the Drummond's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

The flowers are usually blue to purple in color, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
