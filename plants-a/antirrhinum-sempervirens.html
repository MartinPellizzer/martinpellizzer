Antirrhinum sempervirens

Antirrhinum sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen snapdragon, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink, red, and yellow. The leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is noted for its long blooming period, typically from spring to early autumn.
