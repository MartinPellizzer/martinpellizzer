Anthemis hinkovae

Anthemis hinkovae, commonly known as the Hinkov's tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive, aromatic scent, and it produces small, oblong fruits known as cypselae.
