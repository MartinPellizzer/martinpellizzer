Ayenia cristobaliana

Ayenia cristobaliana, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has linear, hairy leaves and produces achenes as its fruit type. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Ayenia cristobaliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
