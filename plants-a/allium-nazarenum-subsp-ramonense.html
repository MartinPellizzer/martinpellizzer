Allium nazarenum subsp. ramonense

Allium nazarenum subsp.

ramonense, commonly known as the Ramon garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant produces edible bulbs that are similar in appearance to garlic but have a milder flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary potential, it is gaining popularity among gardeners and botanists alike.
