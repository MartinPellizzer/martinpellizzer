Artemisia contra

Artemisia contra, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones, contributing to its strong medicinal and pesticidal properties. Artemisia contra thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in dry, open habitats.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a flavoring agent is well documented.
