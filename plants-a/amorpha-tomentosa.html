Amorpha tomentosa

Amorpha tomentosa, commonly known as the silky mesquite, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy foliage and compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in racemes, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.
