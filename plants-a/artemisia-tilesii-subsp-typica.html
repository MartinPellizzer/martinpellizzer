Artemisia tilesii subsp. typica

Artemisia tilesii subsp.

typica, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers that appear in summer.

The plant contains volatile oils, including thujone, which contribute to its strong, camphor-like scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its medicinal and historical uses, it has been cultivated for centuries in traditional herbal practices.
