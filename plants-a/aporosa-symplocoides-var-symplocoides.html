Aporosa symplocoides var. symplocoides

Aporosa symplocoides var. symplocoides, commonly known as the small-leaved aporosa, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by small, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
