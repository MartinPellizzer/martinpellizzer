Achras buchananiifolia

Achras buchananiifolia, commonly known as the white candelabra, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feather-like leaves. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of the stem. Its fruit is a large, spiny structure that contains edible seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
