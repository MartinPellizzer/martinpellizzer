Acrophorus pulcher

Acrophorus pulcher, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous tiny seeds.

Acrophorus pulcher is often found in disturbed habitats and can become invasive in certain areas.
