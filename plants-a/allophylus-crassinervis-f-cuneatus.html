Allophylus crassinervis f. cuneatus

Allophylus crassinervis f. cuneatus, commonly known as the cuneate-veined alder, is a variant of the species Allophylus crassinervis, characterized by its distinctive cuneate (wedge-shaped) leaf veins.

This plant is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a rough texture, with prominent midribs and lateral veins. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
