Acacia armata f. tristis

Acacia armata f. tristis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are densely arranged along the branches. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance through its deep root system.

The foliage and flowers are utilized in traditional Aboriginal medicine and as a source of nectar for honey production.
