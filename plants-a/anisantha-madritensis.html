Anisantha madritensis

Anisantha madritensis, commonly known as the Madrid grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Anisantha madritensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
