Asteriscus paui

Asteriscus paui, commonly known as the Hawaiian aster, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, daisy-like flowers bloom in shades of white and purple, adding beauty to its native ecosystem.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Asteriscus paui is considered a rare and endangered species.
