Asplenium cuspidatum var. cuspidatum

Asplenium cuspidatum var. cuspidatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rock fern, is a perennial fern native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices and woodland floors. The plant has slender, dark green fronds with a distinctive, narrow, pointed shape. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in acidic soils.
