Aegilops crassa var. palaestina

Aegilops crassa var. palaestina, commonly known as the Palestinian rye grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Middle East and parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its robust growth and coarse, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces spikelets with leathery, awned glumes, and its inflorescence is typically terminal. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its potential as a forage crop and its genetic relationship to other wheat relatives.
