Aristea wredowia

Aristea wredowia, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, silvery-green leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in dense clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a decorative ground cover.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
