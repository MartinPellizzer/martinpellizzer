Angraecum macrocentrum

Angraecum macrocentrum, commonly known as the giant angraecum, is a striking orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in length, featuring a distinctive white and purple coloration. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its growth habit includes a robust rhizome and can produce multiple inflorescences during its flowering season.

Angraecum macrocentrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
