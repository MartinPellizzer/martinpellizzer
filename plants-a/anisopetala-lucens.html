Anisopetala lucens

Anisopetala lucens, commonly known as the bright-flowered anisopetala, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, which helps reflect excess sunlight in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
