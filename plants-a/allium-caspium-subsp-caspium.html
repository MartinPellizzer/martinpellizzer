Allium caspium subsp. caspium

Allium caspium subsp.

caspium, commonly known as the Caspian leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia. It features elongated, cylindrical bulbs covered in a white, fibrous tunic.

The plant produces a tall, slender stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and umbels of pale purple to white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in steppe and semi-arid regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its edible bulbs in some traditional cuisines.
