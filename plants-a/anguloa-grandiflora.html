Anguloa grandiflora

Anguloa grandiflora, commonly known as the giant anguloa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, with vibrant colors ranging from pink to purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often forming a rosette shape to retain moisture.

Anguloa grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens worldwide.
