Angraecum grantii

Angraecum grantii, commonly known as the Grant's star orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear numerous star-shaped flowers, typically white or pale blue with yellow lips. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media to prevent root rot. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by specific nocturnal moths, highlighting its ecological significance.

Angraecum grantii is a symbol of botanical rarity and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and challenging care requirements.
