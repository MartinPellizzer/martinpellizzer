Aristida tenuiseta

Aristida tenuiseta, commonly known as slender hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Aristida tenuiseta is often used in restoration projects due to its resilience and ecological benefits.
