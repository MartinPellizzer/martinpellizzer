Arum dioscoridis var. smithii

Arum dioscoridis var. smithii, commonly known as Smith's arrowhead, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are violet or purple in color, surrounded by a white, leaf-like bract. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plant species.
