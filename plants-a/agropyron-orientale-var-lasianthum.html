Agropyron orientale var. lasianthum

Agropyron orientale var. lasianthum, commonly known as the Chinese wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lipped flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
