Aruncus dioicus subsp. dioicus

Aruncus dioicus subsp.

dioicus, commonly known as the mountain avens, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America. It features distinctive, feathery, plume-like inflorescences that range in color from white to pink, appearing in late spring to early summer.

The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is notable for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
