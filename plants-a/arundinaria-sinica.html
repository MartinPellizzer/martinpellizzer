Arundinaria sinica

Arundinaria sinica, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often a vibrant green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is a clump-forming plant that spreads slowly through rhizomes, making it a popular choice for ornamental and landscaping purposes.

Arundinaria sinica is also valued for its strong, flexible stalks, which have been used historically in basket-making and other crafts.
