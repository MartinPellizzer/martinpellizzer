Aster campestris var. bloomeri

Aster campestris var. bloomeri, commonly known as the Bloomer's aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. It produces showy, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

The variety is distinguished by its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
