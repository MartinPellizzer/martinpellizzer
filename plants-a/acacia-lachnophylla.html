Acacia lachnophylla

Acacia lachnophylla, commonly known as the hairy wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

The bark is typically smooth and may flake off in strips, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
