Aspidium jucundum

Aspidium jucundum, commonly known as the happy brake, is a fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Aspidium jucundum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
