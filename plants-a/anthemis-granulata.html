Anthemis granulata

Anthemis granulata, commonly known as the rough anthemis, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are covered in small, rough hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
