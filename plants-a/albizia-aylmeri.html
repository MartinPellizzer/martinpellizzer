Albizia aylmeri

Albizia aylmeri, commonly known as the yellow albizia, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It has a shallow root system and is often used for soil stabilization in reforestation projects.

Albizia aylmeri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
