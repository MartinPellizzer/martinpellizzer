Alchemilla altaica

Alchemilla altaica, commonly known as the high alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters. Alchemilla altaica thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appearance and traditional medicinal uses.
