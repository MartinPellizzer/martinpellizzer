Actinodaphne rotundifolia

Actinodaphne rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its essential oils, which are extracted from its leaves and used in traditional medicine and perfumery.

The tree is also notable for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
