Aristida dispersa

Aristida dispersa, commonly known as the scattered grass or dispersa grass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, prairies, and woodland edges. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often loosely arranged. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Aristida dispersa is noted for its ability to thrive in a range of soil types and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
