Acacia gladiiformis

Acacia gladiiformis, commonly known as the swordleaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
