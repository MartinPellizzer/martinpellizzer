Aspidium trapezoides

Aspidium trapezoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its rhizomes. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as ponds and slow-moving streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged substrates. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

Aspidium trapezoides plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygenation for various microorganisms and small aquatic life.
