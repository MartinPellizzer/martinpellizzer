Amorphophallus manta

Amorphophallus manta, commonly known as the giant swamp arrowroot, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences with a spadix and a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Its underground tubers are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.

The plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in swampy or riparian habitats.
