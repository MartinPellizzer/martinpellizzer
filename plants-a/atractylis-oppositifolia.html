Atractylis oppositifolia

Atractylis oppositifolia, commonly known as the common centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Atractylis oppositifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
