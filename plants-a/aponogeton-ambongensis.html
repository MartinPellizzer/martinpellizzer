Aponogeton ambongensis

Aponogeton ambongensis, commonly known as the Ambongensis arrowhead, is a rare aquatic plant native to the island of Ambon in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves, which are typically green with a waxy surface. This species thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in swampy areas. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters near the water's surface.

Aponogeton ambongensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by aquatic plant enthusiasts.
