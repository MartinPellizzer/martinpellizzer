Andropogon neesii subvar. leiophyllus

Andropogon neesii subvar. leiophyllus, commonly known as the smoothleaf bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green blades and a tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, such as marshes and swampy soils, and is adapted to saturated conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, open panicle with spikelets that are often tinged with purple.

This species plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
