Angraecum dupontii

Angraecum dupontii, commonly known as the Darwin's orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, spiral-shaped inflorescence and large, white, fragrant flowers with prominent, spurred petals. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich substrates. Angraecum dupontii is notable for its unique pollination mechanism, relying on the hawkmoth for reproduction.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is now classified as critically endangered.
