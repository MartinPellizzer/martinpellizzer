Aster miser var. divergens

Aster miser var. divergens, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a bluish-gray hue, while its flowers are small and clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
