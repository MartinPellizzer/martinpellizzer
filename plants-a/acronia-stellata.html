Acronia stellata

Acronia stellata, commonly known as the starleaf acronia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pale pink.

Acronia stellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
