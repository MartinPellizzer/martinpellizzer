Andropogon crucianus

Andropogon crucianus, commonly known as tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that turn golden in the fall. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its flowering spikes are typically pale green to blue, adding visual interest during the growing season.

Andropogon crucianus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
