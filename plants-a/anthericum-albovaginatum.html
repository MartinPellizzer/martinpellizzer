Anthericum albovaginatum

Anthericum albovaginatum, commonly known as the white-flowered nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Anthericum albovaginatum is notable for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
