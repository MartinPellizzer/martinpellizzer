Alloplectus tigrinus

Alloplectus tigrinus, commonly known as the tiger orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, intricate floral structure. The plant features showy, colorful flowers with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals, often displaying a tiger-like pattern. Alloplectus tigrinus is typically found in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in the undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts, though it remains relatively rare in horticultural trade.
