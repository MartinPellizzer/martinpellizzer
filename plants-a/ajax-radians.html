Ajax radians

Ajax radians, commonly known as the radiant dandelion, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of many small florets. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, lobed leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. It is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its role in supporting pollinators.

The plant's scientific name reflects its historical association with medicinal uses in ancient herbal practices.
