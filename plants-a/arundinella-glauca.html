Arundinella glauca

Arundinella glauca, commonly known as the blue bamboo or blue reed, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green stems that form dense clumps, often growing in wet or marshy habitats. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Arundinella glauca is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
