Acacia meeboldii

Acacia meeboldii, commonly known as the Meebold wattle, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Acacia meeboldii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
