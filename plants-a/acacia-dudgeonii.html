Acacia dudgeonii

Acacia dudgeonii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and bipinnate leaves that are narrow and feathery. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in poor soil and low rainfall.

Acacia dudgeonii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
