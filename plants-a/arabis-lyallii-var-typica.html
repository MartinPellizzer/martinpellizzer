Arabis lyallii var. typica

Arabis lyallii var. typica, commonly known as the Lyall's rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lobed or divided, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
