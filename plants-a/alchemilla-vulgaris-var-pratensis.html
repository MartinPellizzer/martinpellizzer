Alchemilla vulgaris var. pratensis

Alchemilla vulgaris var. pratensis, commonly known as lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of heart-shaped, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces yellow-green flowers in dense, upright clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its common name reflects its historical use in herbal medicine and its ability to thrive in various garden settings.
