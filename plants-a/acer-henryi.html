Acer henryi

Acer henryi, commonly known as the Chinese maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its ornamental foliage, which turns vibrant shades of red and yellow in the autumn. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Acer henryi is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated for its seasonal color changes.
