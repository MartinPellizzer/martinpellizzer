Aulica latifolia

Aulica latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf aulica, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
