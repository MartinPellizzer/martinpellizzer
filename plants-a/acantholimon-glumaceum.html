Acantholimon glumaceum

Acantholimon glumaceum, commonly known as the glume rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its scientific name reflects its characteristic glume-like bracts surrounding the flowers.

Acantholimon glumaceum is often found in coastal and inland habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
