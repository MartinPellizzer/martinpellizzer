Angelica anomala var. glabra

Angelica anomala var. glabra, commonly known as smooth angelica, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes carrots, parsley, and celery, and is characterized by its aromatic compounds. The plant features a thick, fleshy root system and compound leaves with a distinctive fern-like appearance. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Smooth angelica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
