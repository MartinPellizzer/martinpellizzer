Acacia helmsiana

Acacia helmsiana, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a small shrub or tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Acacia helmsiana is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
