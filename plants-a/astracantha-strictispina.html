Astracantha strictispina

Astracantha strictispina, commonly known as the rigid-spined hound's-tongue, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features spiny, strap-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
