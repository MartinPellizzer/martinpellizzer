Acacia bakeri

Acacia bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and compound leaves that are typically reduced to small, needle-like structures. The plant produces yellow to golden-green flower spikes, which are often arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Acacia bakeri is also valued for its hard, durable wood and its role in providing habitat for various desert species.
