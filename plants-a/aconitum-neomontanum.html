Aconitum neomontanum

Aconitum neomontanum, commonly known as the Alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple hood-shaped flowers. The plant features large, deeply lobed leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. Aconitum neomontanum thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

It is also noted for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
