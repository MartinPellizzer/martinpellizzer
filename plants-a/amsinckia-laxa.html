Amsinckia laxa

Amsinckia laxa, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange ray flowers surrounded by tubular disk flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Amsinckia laxa is adapted to drought conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
