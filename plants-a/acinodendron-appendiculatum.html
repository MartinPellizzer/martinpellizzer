Acinodendron appendiculatum

Acinodendron appendiculatum, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by birds and small mammals. It has adapted to harsh environmental conditions through its deep root system and drought-resistant foliage.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local ecosystem and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
