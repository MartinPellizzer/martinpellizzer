Astragalus veiskaramii

Astragalus veiskaramii, commonly known as Veiskaram's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.
