Allium suaveolens subsp. ericetorum

Allium suaveolens subsp.

ericetorum, commonly known as the Ericaceous garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in acidic soils, often found in heathlands and woodland edges.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. Its bulbous roots are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it useful in culinary applications.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
