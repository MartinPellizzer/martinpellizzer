Anthemis corymbosa

Anthemis corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

The leaves are aromatic and can be used in traditional herbal remedies.
