Annona bullata

Annona bullata, commonly known as the "bulbous annona," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small, evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers are white and fragrant, with a distinctive, swollen base, and it produces edible, round fruits with a sweet, creamy pulp.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of nutritious fruit.
