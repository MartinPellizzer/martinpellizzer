Anthemis caucasica

Anthemis caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. It has aromatic leaves and is often used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
