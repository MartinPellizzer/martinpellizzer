Ampelopsis sinica var. ciliata

Ampelopsis sinica var. ciliata, commonly known as the silky grapevine, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive silvery pubescence along the veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that give way to blue-black berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and berries, making it a popular choice for garden use.
