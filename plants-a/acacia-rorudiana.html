Acacia rorudiana

Acacia rorudiana, commonly known as the white wattle, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia rorudiana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and open woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
