Athyrium kuratae

Athyrium kuratae, commonly known as Kurata's lady fern, is a species of fern native to Japan.

It belongs to the Dryopteridaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Athyrium kuratae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
