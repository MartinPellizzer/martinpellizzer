Asteriscus pygmaeus

Asteriscus pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf asteriscus, is a small, flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with narrow, gray-green leaves and small yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a ground cover.
