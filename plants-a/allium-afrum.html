Allium afrum

Allium afrum, commonly known as the African leek, is a hardy perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features basal leaves that are broad and flat, often with a bluish-gray hue, and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. Its edible bulbs are mild in flavor and can be used in various culinary applications.

Allium afrum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
