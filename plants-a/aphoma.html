Aphoma

Aphoma, commonly known as the common aphoma, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its erect or spreading shrub growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The flowers are typically small and clustered in racemes, with pea-like petals. The plant produces leguminous pods that contain several seeds.

Aphoma species are often found in temperate regions and are adapted to a variety of soil types.
