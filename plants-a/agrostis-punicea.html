Agrostis punicea

Agrostis punicea, commonly known as the red grass or pink grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often pink or red in color, giving it its common name. Agrostis punicea thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and disturbed ground.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
