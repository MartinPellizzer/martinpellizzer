Anthurium brigadeiroense

Anthurium brigadeiroense, commonly known as the red anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spathiflorous flowers. The plant produces bright red spathes that enclose small, white spadices, creating a visually appealing contrast. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium brigadeiroense is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
