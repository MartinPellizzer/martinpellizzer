Acacia drepanocarpa

Acacia drepanocarpa, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of southern and western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and feathery, bipinnate leaves that are typically green or bluish-green. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its seeds are encased in woody pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize sandy or rocky coastal soils.

Acacia drepanocarpa is well adapted to salty air and wind, making it a hardy species in harsh coastal environments.
