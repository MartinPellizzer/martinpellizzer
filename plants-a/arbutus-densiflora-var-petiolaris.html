Arbutus densiflora var. petiolaris

Arbutus densiflora var. petiolaris, commonly known as the coastal manzanita, is a shrub native to the Pacific coast of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, flaky bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The leaves are leathery, evergreen, and have a distinctive petiole, giving the plant its botanical name. It thrives in coastal environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
