Aster paucicapitatus

Aster paucicapitatus, commonly known as the few-headed aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
