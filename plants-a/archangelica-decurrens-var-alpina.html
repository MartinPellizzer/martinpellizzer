Archangelica decurrens var. alpina

Archangelica decurrens var. alpina, commonly known as alpine hogweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, umbrella-like clusters of small white flowers. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a strong, aromatic odor. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
