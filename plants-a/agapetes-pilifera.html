Agapetes pilifera

Agapetes pilifera, commonly known as the silky silverleaf, is a shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Agapetes pilifera thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.
