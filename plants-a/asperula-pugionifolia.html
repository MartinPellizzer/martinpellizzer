Asperula pugionifolia

Asperula pugionifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved woodruff, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Asperula pugionifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
