Acis orientalis

Acis orientalis, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, violet-colored flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to gardens and natural landscapes.

Acis orientalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
