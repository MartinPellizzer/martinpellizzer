Aspidium parasiticum

Aspidium parasiticum, commonly known as the parasitic water fern, is a unique fern species that exhibits a parasitic lifestyle.

It lacks true roots and instead relies on its host for nutrients, typically attaching itself to other plants. The plant's fronds are usually slender and delicate, with a feathery appearance characteristic of many ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Despite its parasitic nature, it plays a role in the ecological balance by contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
