Agave americana f. virginica

Agave americana f. virginica, commonly known as the Virgin's agave, is a variety of the American agave plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color and have a waxy texture. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that can reach several meters in height, bearing small, white flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
