Ageratiopsis

Ageratiopsis, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, blue or purple flower heads that resemble small clouds, hence its common name. The plants typically have hairy stems and leaves, which help in reducing water loss in arid environments. Ageratiopsis species are native to regions of Mexico and Central America, where they thrive in dry, rocky soils.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens due to their striking floral display.
