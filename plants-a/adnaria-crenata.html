Adnaria crenata

Adnaria crenata, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue or purple flowers and spiny, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showing a high tolerance for salt and wind. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

Adnaria crenata is also sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.
