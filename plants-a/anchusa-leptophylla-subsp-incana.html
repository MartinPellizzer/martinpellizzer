Anchusa leptophylla subsp. incana

Anchusa leptophylla subsp.

incana, commonly known as the woolly bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often silver-gray in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long bloom period and ability to attract pollinators.
