Andropogon armillaris

Andropogon armillaris, commonly known as the sand bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Andropogon armillaris is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
