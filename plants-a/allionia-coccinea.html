Allionia coccinea

Allionia coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet flame or red lily of the valley, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its striking red, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Allionia coccinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
