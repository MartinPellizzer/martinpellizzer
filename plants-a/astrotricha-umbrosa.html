Astrotricha umbrosa

Astrotricha umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in a spherical shape.

Astrotricha umbrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
