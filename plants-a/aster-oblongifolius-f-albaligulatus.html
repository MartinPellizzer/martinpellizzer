Aster oblongifolius f. albaligulatus

Aster oblongifolius f. albaligulatus, commonly known as the white-flowered aster, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, depending on the specific cultivar. These flowers bloom in the late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a versatile addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
