Aspidium lanceolatum

Aspidium lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, compound leaves that are typically arranged in a bipinnate structure. The fronds are usually dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture that helps it retain moisture. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
