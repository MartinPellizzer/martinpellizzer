Achyranthes crassifolia

Achyranthes crassifolia, commonly known as broadleaf spurge, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it can become a nuisance in cultivated areas.
