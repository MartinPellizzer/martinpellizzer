Astragalus acanthochristianopsis

Astragalus acanthochristianopsis, commonly known as the spiny Christianna milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

It is often found in steppe and desert steppe habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
