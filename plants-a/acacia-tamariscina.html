Acacia tamariscina

Acacia tamariscina, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance. Its bark is often flaky and greyish, while its wood is hard and durable.

Acacia tamariscina plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
