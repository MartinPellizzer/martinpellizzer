Acacia russelliana

Acacia russelliana, commonly known as Russell's wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are divided into numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Russell's wattle is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
