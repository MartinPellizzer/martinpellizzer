Actinidia rufa var. typica

Actinidia rufa var. typica, commonly known as the red actinidia or red kiwifruit, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its small, hairy, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically hairy and have a distinctive red or brownish color when mature. These berries are rich in vitamins and antioxidants, making them a valuable fruit in both culinary and medicinal contexts.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
