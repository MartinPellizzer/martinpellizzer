Aeschynomene bullockii

Aeschynomene bullockii, commonly known as the Indian strangleweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields due to its adaptability and rapid growth.
