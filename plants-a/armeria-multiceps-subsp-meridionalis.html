Armeria multiceps subsp. meridionalis

Armeria multiceps subsp.

meridionalis, commonly known as the southern thrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that produces dense clusters of white or pinkish flowers.

The plant has silver-gray foliage with fuzzy leaves, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and ornamental choice for gardens in temperate climates.
