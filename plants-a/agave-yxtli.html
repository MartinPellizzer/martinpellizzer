Agave yxtli

Agave yxtli, commonly known as the Mexican hat agave, is a succulent plant native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It features a thick, spiky rosette of blue-gray leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with yellow flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

Agave yxtli is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
