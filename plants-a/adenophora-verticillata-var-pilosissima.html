Adenophora verticillata var. pilosissima

Adenophora verticillata var. pilosissima, commonly known as the hairy leaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species in its native environment.
