Aretiastrum imbricatum

Aretiastrum imbricatum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, blue-gray leaves and produces clusters of small, fluffy white flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Aretiastrum imbricatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
