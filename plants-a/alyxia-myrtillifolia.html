Alyxia myrtillifolia

Alyxia myrtillifolia, commonly known as the red mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, red or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Alyxia myrtillifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
