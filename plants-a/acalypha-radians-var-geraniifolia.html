Acalypha radians var. geraniifolia

Acalypha radians var. geraniifolia, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage.

Its leaves are palmately lobed, often with deep red veins that contrast sharply against the green or purple leaf blades. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in shaded garden settings.

Acalypha radians var. geraniifolia prefers well-draining soil and consistent moisture for optimal growth.
