Arcangelina africana

Arcangelina africana, commonly known as the African angelica, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Arcangelina africana is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and riverine vegetation.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
