Alnus japonica f. serrata

Alnus japonica f. serrata, commonly known as the Japanese alder, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its serrated, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Alnus japonica f. serrata is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
