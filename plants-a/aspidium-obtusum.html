Aspidium obtusum

Aspidium obtusum, commonly known as the broad-leafed maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to various regions in Asia, Africa, and Australia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds with a distinctive, finely divided structure. The fronds are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a broad, rounded shape and a somewhat creeping growth habit. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative foliage plant.
