Arum spectabile var. liepoldtii

Arum spectabile var. liepoldtii, commonly known as the Liepoldt's arum, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are inflorescences that resemble a spadix, often with a contrasting coloration. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
