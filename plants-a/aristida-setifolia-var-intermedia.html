Aristida setifolia var. intermedia

Aristida setifolia var. intermedia, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it an important plant for erosion control in arid environments.
