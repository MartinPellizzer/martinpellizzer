Anthericum zenkeri

Anthericum zenkeri, commonly known as Zenker's lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Aroid family (Araceae) and is characterized by its striking, spatulate-shaped leaves and colorful, spadix-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.

Anthericum zenkeri is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
