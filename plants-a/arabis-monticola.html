Arabis monticola

Arabis monticola, commonly known as the mountain rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, white, and arranged in clusters. Arabis monticola produces small, spherical fruits called silicles, which contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
