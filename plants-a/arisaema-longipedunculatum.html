Arisaema longipedunculatum

Arisaema longipedunculatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed arrowhead, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and spadix inflorescence. The plant produces a spadix that is often yellowish in color, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Arisaema longipedunculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
