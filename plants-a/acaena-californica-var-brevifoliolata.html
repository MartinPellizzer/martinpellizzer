Acaena californica var. brevifoliolata

Acaena californica var. brevifoliolata, commonly known as the California burweed, is a perennial shrub native to California.

It features small, spiny fruits that are adapted for animal dispersal, with a distinctive burr structure. The plant has compound leaves with short, stiff leaflets, giving it a dense, bushy appearance. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of local ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
