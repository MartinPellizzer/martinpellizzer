Asplenium nidus var. phyllitidis

Asplenium nidus var. phyllitidis, commonly known as the bird's nest fern, is a unique species of fern characterized by its rosette-shaped fronds that resemble a bird's nest.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's structure consists of a central rhizome with overlapping, leathery fronds that enclose a central cavity, which is often filled with decomposing organic matter. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a distinctive, undulating edge that aids in water retention.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
