Atriplex hortensis var. microtheca

Atriplex hortensis var. microtheca, commonly known as the garden saltbush, is a variant of the saltbush species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to tolerate saline and arid conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

The variety microtheca is distinguished by its smaller, more compact leaves compared to other varieties of Atriplex hortensis.
