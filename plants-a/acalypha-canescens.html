Acalypha canescens

Acalypha canescens, commonly known as whiteleaf copperleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, grayish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Acalypha canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage.
