Argythamnia moorei

Argythamnia moorei, commonly known as the Moore's thorny alyssum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant features wiry, spiny stems and narrow, leathery leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Argythamnia moorei is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
