Aristea caerulea var. elongata

Aristea caerulea var. elongata, commonly known as the blue tufted aristea, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial native to southern Africa.

It features dense clusters of sky-blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adding a striking visual contrast to its floral displays. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
