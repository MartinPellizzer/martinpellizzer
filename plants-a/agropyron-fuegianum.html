Agropyron fuegianum

Agropyron fuegianum, commonly known as the Falkland grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in coastal and maritime environments, often on sandy or rocky soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand strong winds and salt spray.

Agropyron fuegianum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local wildlife.
