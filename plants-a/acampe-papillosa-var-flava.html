Acampe papillosa var. flava

Acampe papillosa var. flava, commonly known as the yellow silky grass, is a variant of the silky grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silky, yellowish-green foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and slightly curled, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is a hardy, drought-tolerant plant that thrives in well-drained soils.
