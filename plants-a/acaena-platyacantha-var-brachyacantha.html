Acaena platyacantha var. brachyacantha

Acaena platyacantha var. brachyacantha, commonly known as the short-stemmed burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a tough, fibrous husk. The plant thrives in coastal and maritime climates, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its unique morphology, including its short, stiff stems and clustered leaves, makes it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
