Alysicarpus vaginalis var. villosus

Alysicarpus vaginalis var. villosus, commonly known as hairy velvet bean, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy pods that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields. Its flowers are typically small and yellow, and it produces a strong, often unpleasant odor.

The species is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.
