Acacia longiphyllodinea

Acacia longiphyllodinea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that appear in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia longiphyllodinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and revegetation projects.
