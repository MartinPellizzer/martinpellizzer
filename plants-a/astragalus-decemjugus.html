Astragalus decemjugus

Astragalus decemjugus, commonly known as the ten-lobed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Astragalus decemjugus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
