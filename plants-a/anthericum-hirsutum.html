Anthericum hirsutum

Anthericum hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy tuckahoe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are typically hairy, giving it its common name. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

The botanical interest of A. hirsutum lies in its distinctive morphology and ecological role in wetland habitats.
