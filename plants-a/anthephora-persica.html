Anthephora persica

Anthephora persica, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

The flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
