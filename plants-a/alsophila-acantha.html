Alsophila acantha

Alsophila acantha, commonly known as the stiff-leaf tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, stiff, lance-shaped fronds. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is typically found in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are usually dark green and can grow up to several meters in length.

Alsophila acantha is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical gardens.
