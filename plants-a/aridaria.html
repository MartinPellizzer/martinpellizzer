Aridaria

Aridaria, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments by storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. It typically blooms in the spring and is often found in rocky soils or along dry streambeds.

Aridaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in low-water landscapes.
