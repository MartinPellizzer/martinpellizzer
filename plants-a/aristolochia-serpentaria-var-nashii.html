Aristolochia serpentaria var. nashii

Aristolochia serpentaria var. nashii, commonly known as the Nash's Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage.

The species is also notable for its role in supporting certain species of butterflies.
