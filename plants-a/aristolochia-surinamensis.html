Aristolochia surinamensis

Aristolochia surinamensis, commonly known as Surinam Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-brown with a strong, often unpleasant odor. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and serrated edges. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The genus Aristolochia is known for containing aristolochic acids, which are toxic to humans and animals.
