Atriplex acutiloba var. euacutiloba

Atriplex acutiloba var. euacutiloba, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, gray-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense clusters, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and coastal dunes.

This variety is valued for its salt tolerance and is used in ecological restoration projects.
