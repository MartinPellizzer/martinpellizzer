Aster ericoides var. platyphyllus

Aster ericoides var. platyphyllus, commonly known as the flatleaf heath aster, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils found in open woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape. Its showy blue to violet flowers bloom in late summer, adding visual interest to the landscape.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
