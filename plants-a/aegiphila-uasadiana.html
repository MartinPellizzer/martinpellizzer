Aegiphila uasadiana

Aegiphila uasadiana, commonly known as the Uasadi sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valued species in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
