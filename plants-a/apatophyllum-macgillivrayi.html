Apatophyllum macgillivrayi

Apatophyllum macgillivrayi, commonly known as the MacGillivray's thistle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
