Aspilia subpandurata

Aspilia subpandurata, commonly known as the African crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow flower heads that resemble a crown. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a tall, upright stem with narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are composed of ray florets that are yellow and tubular disc florets that are often reddish or purple.

Aspilia subpandurata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
