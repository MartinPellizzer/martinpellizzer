Acanthus arborescens

Acanthus arborescens, commonly known as the tree acanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, white to grayish hairs. The plant produces showy, spire-like inflorescences with purple to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its robust growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

However, it is important to note that it can be invasive in certain regions if not properly managed.
