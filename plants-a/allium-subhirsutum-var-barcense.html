Allium subhirsutum var. barcense

Allium subhirsutum var. barcense, commonly known as the Barcense garlic, is a botanical variant of the common garlic species.

It is characterized by its small, clustered bulbs and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in a loose umbel, and it is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

This variety is valued for its mild flavor and potential culinary and medicinal uses.
