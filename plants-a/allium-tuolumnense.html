Allium tuolumnense

Allium tuolumnense, commonly known as the Tuolumna onion, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces spherical bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a short, sturdy stem that supports its flowering inflorescence.

Allium tuolumnense is adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
