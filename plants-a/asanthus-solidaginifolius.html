Asanthus solidaginifolius

Asanthus solidaginifolius, commonly known as the goldenrods, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or golden inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Asanthus solidaginifolius plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects and small animals.
