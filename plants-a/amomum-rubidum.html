Amomum rubidum

Amomum rubidum, commonly known as red cardamom, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped rhizomes. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The rhizomes are harvested for their spicy, fragrant seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
