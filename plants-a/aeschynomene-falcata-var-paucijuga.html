Aeschynomene falcata var. paucijuga

Aeschynomene falcata var. paucijuga, commonly known as the narrow-leafed aeschynomene, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

This species is also valued for its potential in forage and soil stabilization due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
