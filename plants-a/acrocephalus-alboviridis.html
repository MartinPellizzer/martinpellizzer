Acrocephalus alboviridis

Acrocephalus alboviridis, commonly known as the pale green reed warbler, is a small passerine bird native to various parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender body, long legs, and distinctive pale green plumage. The species typically inhabits wetlands, marshes, and reed beds, where it nests in dense vegetation. Its diet mainly consists of insects, especially during the breeding season.

This bird is known for its melodious song and is often heard but rarely seen in its natural habitat.
