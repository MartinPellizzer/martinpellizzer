Acalypha macrostachya var. puberula

Acalypha macrostachya var. puberula, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, bract-like leaves that often resemble flowers, which can be red, yellow, or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
