Androcalva procumbens

Androcalva procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a prostrate or spreading manner.

Androcalva procumbens is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in open woodlands and sagebrush habitats.
