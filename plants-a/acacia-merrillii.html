Acacia merrillii

Acacia merrillii, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like leaflets.

Acacia merrillii is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often used in traditional medicine.
