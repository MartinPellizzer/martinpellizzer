Astragalus meuselii

Astragalus meuselii, commonly known as the Meuselii milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stems and trifoliate leaves, with small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces pods containing several seeds, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
