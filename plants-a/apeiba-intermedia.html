Apeiba intermedia

Apeiba intermedia, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The tree produces yellow-orange fruits that resemble small apples, containing edible seeds. Its flowers are small, clustered, and typically white or pale green, which attract various pollinators.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
