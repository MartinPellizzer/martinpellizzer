Aloysia ligustrina var. paraguariensis

Aloysia ligustrina var. paraguariensis, commonly known as paraguayan verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
