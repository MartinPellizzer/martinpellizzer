Arabis pubescens var. longisiliqua

Arabis pubescens var. longisiliqua, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with characteristic cruciform flowers. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments.

This variety is distinguished by its longer silique pods compared to other varieties of Arabis pubescens.
