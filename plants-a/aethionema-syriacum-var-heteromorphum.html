Aethionema syriacum var. heteromorphum

Aethionema syriacum var. heteromorphum, commonly known as the Syrian carnation, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features compact, succulent stems and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The leaves are typically lobed or toothed, and the plant is well adapted to rocky and arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
