Aster bipinnatisectus

Aster bipinnatisectus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, bipinnatisected leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in the late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
