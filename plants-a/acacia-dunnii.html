Acacia dunnii

Acacia dunnii, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow floral spikes that appear during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that serve as the primary photosynthetic organs. Acacia dunnii thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for landscaping purposes.
