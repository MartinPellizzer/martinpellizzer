Anemone × gokayamensis

Anemone × gokayamensis, commonly known as the Gokayam anemone, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with finely divided, fern-like leaves that are hairy on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value in natural habitats.
