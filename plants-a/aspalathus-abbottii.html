Aspalathus abbottii

Aspalathus abbottii, commonly known as Abbott's bushveld pea, is a South African plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a small shrub characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is native to the Eastern Cape province and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
