Aquilegia leptoceras var. chrysantha

Aquilegia leptoceras var. chrysantha, commonly known as the golden columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals, which are typically bright yellow, and its compound leaves with a finely toothed margin. The plant produces clusters of showy flowers in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
