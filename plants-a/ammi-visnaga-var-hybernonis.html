Ammi visnaga var. hybernonis

Ammi visnaga var. hybernonis, commonly known as the Greater Celandine, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lobed, hairy leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant contains compounds such as pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which can be toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its medicinal and toxic properties, it is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
