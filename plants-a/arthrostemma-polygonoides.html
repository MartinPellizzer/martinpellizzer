Arthrostemma polygonoides

Arthrostemma polygonoides, commonly known as the creeping arum, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, often with a waxy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered around the spadix.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and flowering structure.
