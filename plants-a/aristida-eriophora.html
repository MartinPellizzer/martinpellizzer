Aristida eriophora

Aristida eriophora, commonly known as narrowleaf dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, grass-like blades that are often bluish-green in color, and it produces small, fluffy seed heads that are distinctive in appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Its ecological value includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
