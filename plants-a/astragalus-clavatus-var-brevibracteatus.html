Astragalus clavatus var. brevibracteatus

Astragalus clavatus var. brevibracteatus, commonly known as the short-bract milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets, and it produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in steppe and desert scrub habitats.
