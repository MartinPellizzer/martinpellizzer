Abies excelsa var. brevifolia

Abies excelsa var. brevifolia, commonly known as the dwarf white fir, is a variant of the white fir species native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter, more densely packed needles compared to the typical white fir. The plant exhibits a conical shape with a broad, rounded crown, and its foliage is typically a dark green color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous forests at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,500 meters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
