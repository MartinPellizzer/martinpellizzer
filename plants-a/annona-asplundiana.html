Annona asplundiana

Annona asplundiana, commonly known as the asplundian custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that produces a distinctive fruit with a rough, greenish-brown skin. The fruit contains a creamy, sweet pulp that is often used in culinary applications. The plant belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy white flowers.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
