Aspidium grunowii

Aspidium grunowii, commonly known as the Grunow's water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant typically grows in shallow, still water such as ponds and marshes, where it can absorb nutrients from the water. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Aspidium grunowii is valued in botanical studies for its ecological role and its unique adaptations to aquatic life.
