Asplenium lunulatum var. fernandezianum

Asplenium lunulatum var. fernandezianum, commonly known as the Fernandez's shield fern, is a species of fern native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its small, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green with a leathery texture. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in humid, indoor environments.
