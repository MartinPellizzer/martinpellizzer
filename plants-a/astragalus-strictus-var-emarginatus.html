Astragalus strictus var. emarginatus

Astragalus strictus var. emarginatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules.
