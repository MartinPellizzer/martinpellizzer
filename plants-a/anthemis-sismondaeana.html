Anthemis sismondaeana

Anthemis sismondaeana, commonly known as the Sierra Madre daisy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in the summer months. It typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
