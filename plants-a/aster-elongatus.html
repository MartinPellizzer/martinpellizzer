Aster elongatus

Aster elongatus, commonly known as the elongated aster, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or blue. Native to parts of Asia, this plant thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Aster elongatus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
