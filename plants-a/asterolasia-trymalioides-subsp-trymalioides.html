Asterolasia trymalioides subsp. trymalioides

Asterolasia trymalioides subsp.

trymalioides, commonly known as the coastal asterolasia, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in dune or rocky habitats. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads.

This subspecies is well adapted to saline and wind-exposed environments, making it a resilient component of coastal vegetation.
