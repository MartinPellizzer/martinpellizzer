Aphelandra xanthantha

Aphelandra xanthantha, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aroid family (Araceae) and is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that are often bright green with yellow or white veins. The plant produces inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of yellow or orange. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Aphelandra xanthantha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
