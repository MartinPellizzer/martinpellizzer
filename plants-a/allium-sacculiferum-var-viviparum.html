Allium sacculiferum var. viviparum

Allium sacculiferum var. viviparum, commonly known as the bulbous garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to China.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant is notable for its unique reproductive method, where it produces bulbils along the flower stalks, which can develop into new plants. These bulbils are edible and resemble small garlic cloves, contributing to its culinary use.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
