Aiphanes linearis

Aiphanes linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf papyrus, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating in water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. Aiphanes linearis thrives in marshy environments and is adapted to tolerate fluctuating water levels.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
