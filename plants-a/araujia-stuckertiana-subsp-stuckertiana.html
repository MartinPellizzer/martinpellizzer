Araujia stuckertiana subsp. stuckertiana

Araujia stuckertiana subsp.

stuckertiana, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a climbing vine native to Brazil. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades and tomatoes.

The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, which open at night. Its fruits are elongated, fleshy berries that turn red when mature.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its dramatic floral display.
