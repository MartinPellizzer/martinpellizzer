Acer opalus var. reginae-amaliae

Acer opalus var. reginae-amaliae, commonly known as the Queen Amalia maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras for seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental species in gardens.

Its compact size and showy foliage make it a valuable addition to landscape design.
