Achillea alpina var. mongolica

Achillea alpina var. mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green foliage and flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to harsh alpine environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also known for its ability to tolerate cold temperatures and is often found in high-altitude meadows.
