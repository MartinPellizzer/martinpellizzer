Agastache palmeri var. palmeri

Agastache palmeri var. palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties.

It is valued in herbal medicine for its potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory benefits.
