Aspidoglossum hirundo

Aspidoglossum hirundo, commonly known as the blue devil, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers.
