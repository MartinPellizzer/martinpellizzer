Astragalus purshii var. tinctus

Astragalus purshii var. tinctus, commonly known as the purple milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
