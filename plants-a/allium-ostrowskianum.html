Allium ostrowskianum

Allium ostrowskianum, commonly known as the Polish leek or Ostrowski's onion, is a bulbous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and clusters of pale blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, white to pale pink bulbs that are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is appreciated by both gardeners and food enthusiasts.
