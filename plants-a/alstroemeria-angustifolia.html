Alstroemeria angustifolia

Alstroemeria angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alstroemeria, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, strap-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

Alstroemeria angustifolia is also valued for its air-purifying properties and ability to attract pollinators.
