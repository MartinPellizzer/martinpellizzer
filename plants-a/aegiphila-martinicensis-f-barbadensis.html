Aegiphila martinicensis f. barbadensis

Aegiphila martinicensis f. barbadensis, commonly known as the Barbadensis variant of the Martinique aegiphila, is a shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in whorls.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and fragrance.
