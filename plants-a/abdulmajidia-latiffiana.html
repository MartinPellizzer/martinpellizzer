Abdulmajidia latiffiana

Abdulmajidia latiffiana, commonly known as the Latiffian orchid, is a rare and unique species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

This plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers that display a striking combination of colors. The leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its humid environment. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
