Aconitum nasutum subsp. tuscheticum

Aconitum nasutum subsp.

tuscheticum, commonly known as the Tschetts Aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps. It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August.

The plant has dark green, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
