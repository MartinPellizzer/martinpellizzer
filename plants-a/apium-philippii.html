Apium philippii

Apium philippii, commonly known as the Philippi's parsley, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, celery, and parsley. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in umbrella-like clusters. It is often found growing in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its aromatic properties.

Apium philippii is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
