Acaena exigua var. glaberrima

Acaena exigua var. glaberrima, commonly known as the hairy weed or small burr plant, is a low-growing shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, spiny fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous, burr-like structure. The plant typically thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its ability to colonize and stabilize sandy or coastal soils, making it ecologically significant in certain habitats.
