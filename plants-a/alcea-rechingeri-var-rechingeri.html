Alcea rechingeri var. rechingeri

Alcea rechingeri var. rechingeri, commonly known as the Persian hollyhock, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring sturdy stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, while the roots are fibrous and adapted to survive in arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
