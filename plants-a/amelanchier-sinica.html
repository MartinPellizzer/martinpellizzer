Amelanchier sinica

Amelanchier sinica, commonly known as Chinese serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that ripen to a dark red or purple color in summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin.

Amelanchier sinica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
