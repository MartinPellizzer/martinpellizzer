Abutilon buchii

Abutilon buchii, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive velvety appearance. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it suitable for use in tropical gardens and as a ground cover.
