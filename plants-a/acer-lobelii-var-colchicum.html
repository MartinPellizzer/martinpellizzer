Acer lobelii var. colchicum

Acer lobelii var. colchicum, commonly known as the Sierra sugar maple, is a variant of the sugar maple native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that are often lobed and have a vibrant green color during the growing season. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by winged seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
