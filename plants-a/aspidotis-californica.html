Aspidotis californica

Aspidotis californica, commonly known as the California aspidistra, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to coastal regions of California.

It features dark green, broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are borne on short pedicels during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
