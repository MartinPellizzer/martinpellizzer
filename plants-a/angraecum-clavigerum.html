Angraecum clavigerum

Angraecum clavigerum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a climbing epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It features long, slender stems and produces striking white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive star-shaped appearance. The plant has a complex inflorescence with numerous small, fragrant flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Angraecum clavigerum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
