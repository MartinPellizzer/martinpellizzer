Allium supranisianum

Allium supranisianum, commonly known as the Sunda leek, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
