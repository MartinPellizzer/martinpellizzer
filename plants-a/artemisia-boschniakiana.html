Artemisia boschniakiana

Artemisia boschniakiana, commonly known as the Boschniak's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
