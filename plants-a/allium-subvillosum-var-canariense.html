Allium subvillosum var. canariense

Allium subvillosum var. canariense, commonly known as the Canary Island leek, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear umbels of white to pale purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
