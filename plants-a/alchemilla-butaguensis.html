Alchemilla butaguensis

Alchemilla butaguensis, commonly known as the Butaguan lady's mantle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Alchemilla butaguensis is valued for its ornamental foliage and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and unique appearance.
