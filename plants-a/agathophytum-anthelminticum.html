Agathophytum anthelminticum

Agathophytum anthelminticum, commonly known as the giant burr.

It is a flowering plant native to South America, characterized by its large, spiny fruit capsules that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers and is often found in dry, open habitats. Its fruit contains numerous seeds enclosed in a dense, woody husk.

The species is known for its aggressive growth and is considered a weed in some regions.
