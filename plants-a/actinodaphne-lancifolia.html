Actinodaphne lancifolia

Actinodaphne lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved camphor tree, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces berries that contain the seeds.
