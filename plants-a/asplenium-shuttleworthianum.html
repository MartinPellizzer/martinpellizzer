Asplenium shuttleworthianum

Asplenium shuttleworthianum, commonly known as the Shuttleworth's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically produces spore-bearing structures called sorus on the underside of its leaves. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Asplenium shuttleworthianum is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks, showcasing its adaptability to epiphytic conditions.
