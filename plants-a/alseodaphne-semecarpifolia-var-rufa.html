Alseodaphne semecarpifolia var. rufa

Alseodaphne semecarpifolia var. rufa, commonly known as the red-fruited alseodaphne, is a variant of the alseodaphne genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by distinctive red or orange berries, which are a key feature of this variety. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The botanical interest of this plant lies in its unique foliage and fruiting characteristics, making it a subject of study in botanic gardens and ecological research.
