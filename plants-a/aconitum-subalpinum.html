Aconitum subalpinum

Aconitum subalpinum, commonly known as the alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem. Aconitum subalpinum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

It is also known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
