Abutilon bedfordianum var. tomentellum

Abutilon bedfordianum var. tomentellum, commonly known as the fuzzy abutilon, is a deciduous shrub native to the United States, particularly in the southeastern region.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its value in both landscape design and ecological settings.
