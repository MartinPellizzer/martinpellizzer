Acer palmatum f. circumlobatum

Acer palmatum f. circumlobatum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with curled leaves, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely serrated, and often curled foliage that gives it a unique, ornamental appearance. The leaves are typically a vibrant green in spring, turning shades of red, orange, and yellow in the fall. This variety thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

It is valued for its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes, enhancing its aesthetic appeal in landscapes.
