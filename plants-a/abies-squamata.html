Abies squamata

Abies squamata, commonly known as the Japanese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine, white bloom. The tree produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The Japanese fir is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
