Allium cremnophilum

Allium cremnophilum, commonly known as the rock onion, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor.

Allium cremnophilum is often used in Mediterranean cuisine and traditional herbal medicine.
