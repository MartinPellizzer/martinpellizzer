Astragalus alpinus var. americanus

Astragalus alpinus var. americanus, commonly known as the American mountain milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
