Avena striata subsp. capillipes

Avena striata subsp.

capillipes, commonly known as the slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and the inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets. Its small, two-rowed spikelets contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family.

This plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
