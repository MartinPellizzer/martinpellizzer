Agropyron campestre var. pycnanthum

Agropyron campestre var. pycnanthum, commonly known as the needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil and makes it a valuable plant for ecological restoration.
