Aristida pauciflora

Aristida pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered dropseed, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in panicles. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its low-growing habit makes it an attractive ornamental plant in xeriscapes.

Aristida pauciflora plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife in its native range.
