Acer rubrum subsp. tomentosum

Acer rubrum subsp.

tomentosum, commonly known as the hairy red maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its distinctive hairy or tomentose bark, which is especially prominent on younger stems.

The leaves are typically palmately lobed with serrated edges and turn vibrant red or orange in the fall. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
