Alchemilla grossidens f. cuneata

Alchemilla grossidens f. cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped leaves that are narrow and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
