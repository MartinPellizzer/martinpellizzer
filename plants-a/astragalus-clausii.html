Astragalus clausii

Astragalus clausii, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and compound, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus clausii plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
