Anthericum brunneomarginatum

Anthericum brunneomarginatum, commonly known as the brown-margin lily, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, grass-like leaves and its showy, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The flowers are typically arranged in a loose inflorescence and have three petals and three sepals. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
