Ageratum micropappum

Ageratum micropappum, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, often with a white or pale blue center, and bloom during the summer months.

Ageratum micropappum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
