Apoda-prorepentia karlii

Apoda-prorepentia karlii, commonly known as the Karlii Ghost Fern, is a rare and elusive fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in dense tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures are unique, with spore-producing clusters located on the undersides of its leaves.

Due to habitat destruction, Apoda-prorepentia karlii is considered endangered and is the subject of ongoing conservation efforts.
