Alyxia minutiflora

Alyxia minutiflora, commonly known as the small-flowered alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a woody vine that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and forest understories. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin.

Alyxia minutiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
