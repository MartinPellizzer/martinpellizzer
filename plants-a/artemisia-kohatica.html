Artemisia kohatica

Artemisia kohatica, commonly known as the Korean mugwort, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.

Artemisia kohatica is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
