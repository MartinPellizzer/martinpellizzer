Alpinia japonica var. kiushiana

Alpinia japonica var. kiushiana, commonly known as the Japanese ginger or Kiushian ginger, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous growth, with narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are typically pink or white, arranged in inflorescences, and it produces small, yellow fruits.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal properties.
