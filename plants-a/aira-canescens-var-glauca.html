Aira canescens var. glauca

Aira canescens var. glauca, commonly known as the bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
