Asplenium sandersonii var. subcoriaceum

Asplenium sandersonii var. subcoriaceum, commonly known as the sanderson's spleenwort, is a fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, leathery, and often dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a compact rosette. The fronds are usually short and have a subcoriaceous texture, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks.

This variant is closely related to other asplenium species and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
