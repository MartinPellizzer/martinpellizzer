Aracium

Aracium, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Aracium species are typically found in coastal areas, where they thrive in salty, well-drained soils. The plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological balance in its habitat.
