Aspalathus opaca

Aspalathus opaca, commonly known as the small-leaved bushveld pea, is a South African shrub belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is native to the grassland regions of South Africa and is adapted to dry conditions. It has a woody stem and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Aspalathus opaca is often found in open habitats and is used in traditional medicine.
