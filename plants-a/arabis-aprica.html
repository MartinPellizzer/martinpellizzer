Arabis aprica

Arabis aprica, commonly known as spring rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known plants such as cabbages and mustards. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green leaves and clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Arabis aprica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to flourish in challenging growing conditions.
