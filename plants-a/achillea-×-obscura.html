Achillea × obscura

Achillea × obscura, commonly known as the gray yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Achillea × obscura is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
