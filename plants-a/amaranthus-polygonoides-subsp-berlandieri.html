Amaranthus polygonoides subsp. berlandieri

Amaranthus polygonoides subsp.

berlandieri, commonly known as desert amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

The seeds of this species are edible and have been used by indigenous peoples for food and medicinal purposes.
