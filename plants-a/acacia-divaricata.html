Acacia divaricata

Acacia divaricata, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping, feathery foliage that gives it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Acacia divaricata is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
