Acalypha paniculata

Acalypha paniculata, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in paniculate arrangements, giving it its scientific name. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Acalypha paniculata thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
