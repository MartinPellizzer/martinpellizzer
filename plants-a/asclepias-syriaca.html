Asclepias syriaca

Asclepias syriaca, commonly known as common milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to reddish-orange flowers with prominent, rounded seed pods. It is a vital host plant for monarch butterfly larvae and supports a variety of pollinators.

Its milky sap contains latex, which serves as a defense mechanism against herbivores.
