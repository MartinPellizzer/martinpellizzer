Acer pseudoplatanus var. triangulare

Acer pseudoplatanus var. triangulare, commonly known as the triangular maple, is a variant of the common maple characterized by its distinctive triangular leaf shape.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, where it thrives in cooler, moist environments. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five lobes, often with a serrated edge, and produces small, red flowers in early spring. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a range of soil types.

The varietal form is particularly noted for its compact growth habit and vibrant autumn foliage.
