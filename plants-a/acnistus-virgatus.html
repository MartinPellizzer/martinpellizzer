Acnistus virgatus

Acnistus virgatus, commonly known as the silverleaf acnistus, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with a silvery-blue hue due to the presence of fine, whitish hairs on the leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

Acnistus virgatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique appearance.
