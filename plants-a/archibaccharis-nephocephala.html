Archibaccharis nephocephala

Archibaccharis nephocephala, commonly known as the cloud-headed baccharis, is a shrub native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, providing structural support in its natural habitat.

The species is notable for its ability to form dense thickets, contributing to the ecological stability of its ecosystem.
