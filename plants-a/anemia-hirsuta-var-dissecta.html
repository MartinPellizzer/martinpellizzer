Anemia hirsuta var. dissecta

Anemia hirsuta var. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected hirsute anemia, is a rare and strikingly ornamental plant characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves.

Native to specific regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments with high levels of atmospheric moisture. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, often hidden among the foliage. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture and a unique appearance.

Due to its delicate nature, it requires careful cultivation and is often grown in controlled environments such as greenhouses.
