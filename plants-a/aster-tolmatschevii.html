Aster tolmatschevii

Aster tolmatschevii, commonly known as the Tolkovsky aster, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
