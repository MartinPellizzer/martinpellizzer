Aralia lindenii

Aralia lindenii, commonly known as the Lindley's aralia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. Its growth habit includes a straight trunk with a spreading crown, and it often has a woody base.

Aralia lindenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in partial to full shade.
