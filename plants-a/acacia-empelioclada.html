Acacia empelioclada

Acacia empelioclada, commonly known as the white thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny branches and bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow, spherical flower heads that appear in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Acacia empelioclada is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions.

Its wood is hard and durable, historically used for construction and tool-making by local communities.
