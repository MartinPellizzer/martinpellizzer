Arkezostis heterophylla

Arkezostis heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a wide range of morphological variation in its leaf-like structures.

It typically grows on tree bark, rocks, and soil in temperate and subtropical regions. The thallus is usually pale to dark green, with a dense, leathery texture that helps it retain moisture. Its ability to thrive in diverse environments makes it a common indicator species for certain ecological conditions.

The plant's unique morphological plasticity is a key feature of its survival strategy in fluctuating habitats.
