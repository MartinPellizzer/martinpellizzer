Adenostemma caffrum

Adenostemma caffrum, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, characterized by its erect stem and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its milky sap is a notable feature, often used in traditional medicine.

Adenostemma caffrum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.
