Anthoxanthum glabrum subsp. sibiricum

Anthoxanthum glabrum subsp.

sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe. It typically grows in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to golden in color, and it is wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
