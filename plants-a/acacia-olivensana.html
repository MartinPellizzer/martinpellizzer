Acacia olivensana

Acacia olivensana, commonly known as the olive acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and the plant has a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Acacia olivensana is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
