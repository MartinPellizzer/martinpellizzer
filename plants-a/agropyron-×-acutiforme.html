Agropyron × acutiforme

Agropyron × acutiforme, commonly known as the intermediate wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species that combines traits from both Agropyron elongatum and Agropyron cristatum.

It is native to the Great Plains of North America and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant is known for its high drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often used in agricultural and ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and nutritional value.

Its seeds are also utilized in the production of forage and grain.
