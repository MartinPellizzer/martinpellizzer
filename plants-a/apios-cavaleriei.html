Apios cavaleriei

Apios cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese groundnut or Chinese wild bean, is a perennial legume native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant's most notable botanical feature is its tuberous roots, which are edible and resemble peanuts in texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Apios cavaleriei is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
