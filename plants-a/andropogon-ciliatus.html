Andropogon ciliatus

Andropogon ciliatus, commonly known as silky grass or silky panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that have fine, silky hairs along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, prairies, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
