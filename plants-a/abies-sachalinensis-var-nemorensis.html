Abies sachalinensis var. nemorensis

Abies sachalinensis var. nemorensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin fir, is a variant of the Sakhalin fir native to the Russian Far East.

This coniferous tree is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. It typically grows in cool, moist climates and is found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils. The tree produces small, cylindrical cones that release seeds in the autumn.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of high-altitude flora and forest ecosystems.
