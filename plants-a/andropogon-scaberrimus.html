Andropogon scaberrimus

Andropogon scaberrimus, commonly known as rough bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant has narrow, stiff leaves with a rough texture, and its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, blue-green flowers. It is adapted to full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species in various ecological conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in degraded areas.
