Acalypha dalzellii

Acalypha dalzellii, commonly known as the velvetleaf or redleaf acalypha, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Acalypha dalzellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
