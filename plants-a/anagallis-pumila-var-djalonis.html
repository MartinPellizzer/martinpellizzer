Anagallis pumila var. djalonis

Anagallis pumila var. djalonis, commonly known as the dwarf pink willow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, trailing stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, bright pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, silky hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.
