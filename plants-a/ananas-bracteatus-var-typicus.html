Ananas bracteatus var. typicus

Ananas bracteatus var. typicus, commonly known as the pineapple guava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, berry-like fruits that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both ornamental and fruit-growing gardens.
