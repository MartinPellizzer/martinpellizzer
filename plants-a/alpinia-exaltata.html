Alpinia exaltata

Alpinia exaltata, commonly known as the black ginger or purple ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and striking purple or red inflorescences. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface that adds to its aesthetic appeal.

Alpinia exaltata thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
