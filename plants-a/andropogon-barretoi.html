Andropogon barretoi

Andropogon barretoi, commonly known as the Barret's bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves that are often slightly hairy, and it produces small, pale blue to purple flower spikes in the summer. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Andropogon barretoi is also valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland restoration projects.
