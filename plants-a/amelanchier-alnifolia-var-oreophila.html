Amelanchier alnifolia var. oreophila

Amelanchier alnifolia var. oreophila, commonly known as the Sierra serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its oval to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, red berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The Sierra serviceberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
