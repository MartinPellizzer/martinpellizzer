Asarum nipponicum

Asarum nipponicum, commonly known as Japanese wild betony, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, reddish-brown leaves. The plant produces a unique, fragrant, and often tubular flower that is typically purple or greenish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional Japanese herbal medicine.
