Acorus undulatus

Acorus undulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sweetflag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Asia.

It features long, narrow leaves that are typically sword-shaped and have a distinctive wavy edge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in the spring. Acorus undulatus is often found in shallow water or damp soil, thriving in environments with high humidity.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
