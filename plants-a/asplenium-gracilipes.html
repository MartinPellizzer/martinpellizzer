Asplenium gracilipes

Asplenium gracilipes, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a delicate and ornamental fern species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features finely divided, arching fronds that are typically bright green with a slightly glossy texture. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in rows. Asplenium gracilipes thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a decorative plant in shaded gardens.
