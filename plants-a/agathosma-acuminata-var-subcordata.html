Agathosma acuminata var. subcordata

Agathosma acuminata var. subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buchu, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow, and often hairy leaves that are rich in essential oils. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in rocky or sandy habitats. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils for commercial purposes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in local ecosystems.
