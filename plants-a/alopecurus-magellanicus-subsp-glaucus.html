Alopecurus magellanicus subsp. glaucus

Alopecurus magellanicus subsp.

glaucus, commonly known as the glaucous bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America. It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green blades and tufted growth habit.

The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
