Acalypha communis var. guaranitica

Acalypha communis var. guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani flame tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical gardens.
