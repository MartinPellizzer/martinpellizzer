Anthyllis vulneraria proles weldeniana

Anthyllis vulneraria proles weldeniana, commonly known as the woolly broom.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with small, hairy leaflets that help reduce water loss in arid environments.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in Mediterranean climates.
