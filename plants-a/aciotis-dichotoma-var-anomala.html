Aciotis dichotoma var. anomala

Aciotis dichotoma var. anomala, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and southeastern United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in paniculate inflorescences and bloom in late summer.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
