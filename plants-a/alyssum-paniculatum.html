Alyssum paniculatum

Alyssum paniculatum, commonly known as sweet alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features dense clusters of flowers on branched stems, which are often used in gardens for their ornamental value. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Alyssum paniculatum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
