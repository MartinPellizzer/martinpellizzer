Alchemilla orbicans

Alchemilla orbicans, commonly known as the golden tutsan, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically shield-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Alchemilla orbicans is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, shady conditions.
