Aegilops cylindrica var. longiaristata

Aegilops cylindrica var. longiaristata, commonly known as the wild barley, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and awned lemmas. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is of interest in agricultural research due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptation to various environmental conditions.
