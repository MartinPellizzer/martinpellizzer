Agrostis teberdensis

Agrostis teberdensis, commonly known as Turkish meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows, wetlands, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
