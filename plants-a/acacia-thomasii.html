Acacia thomasii

Acacia thomasii, commonly known as Thomas's acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, bipinnate leaves that reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in hard pods that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds by wind.

Acacia thomasii is well adapted to drought and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
