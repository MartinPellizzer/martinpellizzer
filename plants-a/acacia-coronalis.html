Acacia coronalis

Acacia coronalis, commonly known as the crowned acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny branches and phyllodes that are narrow and often adorned with a crown-like arrangement of small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which are typically borne in the axils of the phyllodes. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, coastal areas.

Acacia coronalis is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Native Distribution

Acacia coronalis is primarily native to the eastern and southern regions of Australia.

It is commonly found in coastal areas, heathlands, and dry forests. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often associated with sclerophyllous vegetation. It is particularly common in the states of New South Wales, Victoria, and South Australia.

Acacia coronalis is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of these regions, with its growth patterns aligning with seasonal rainfall.

Introduced Distribution

Acacia coronalis was mainly introduced to control soil erosion and as a windbreak in arid and semi-arid regions.
