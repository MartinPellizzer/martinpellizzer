Acacia ambigua

Acacia ambigua, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are grouped in spherical or cylindrical inflorescences. Its foliage is often used in traditional Indigenous practices for its aromatic properties.

Acacia ambigua is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.
