Acacia covenyi

Acacia covenyi, commonly known as the Christmas Chandelier, is a small, deciduous shrub native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, feathery foliage composed of small, glandular, leaf-like structures called phyllodes. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences resembling a chandelier. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
