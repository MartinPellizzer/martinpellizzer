Achyranthes lupulina

Achyranthes lupulina, commonly known as the bitter nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the summer months. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that contain several seeds.

Achyranthes lupulina is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and wastelands.
