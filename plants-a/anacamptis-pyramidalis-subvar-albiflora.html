Anacamptis pyramidalis subvar. albiflora

Anacamptis pyramidalis subvar. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered pyramidal orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, branching stems that bear numerous pale to white flowers. The flowers are typically arranged in a pyramidal or spire-like cluster, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in sunny, well-drained habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

This orchid is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
