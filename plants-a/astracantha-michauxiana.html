Astracantha michauxiana

Astracantha michauxiana, commonly known as the Michaux's star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. Due to its beauty and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
