Allium krylovii

Allium krylovii, commonly known as the Krylov's onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its robust, grass-like leaves and umbel-like clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, bulbous structures that serve as its primary means of propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
