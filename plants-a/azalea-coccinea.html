Azalea coccinea

Azalea coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range from deep red to orange in color. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are typically 2-4 inches long and have a leathery texture. Azalea coccinea thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
