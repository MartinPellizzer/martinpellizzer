Apophyllum anomalum

Apophyllum anomalum, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves and produces clusters of pink or white flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Apophyllum anomalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh conditions.
