Antimima bracteata

Antimima bracteata, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often found growing in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Antimima bracteata is a perennial species that is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
