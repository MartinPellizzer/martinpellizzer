Athyrium aspidioides

Athyrium aspidioides, commonly known as the Japanese male fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive, serrated margin. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Athyrium aspidioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
