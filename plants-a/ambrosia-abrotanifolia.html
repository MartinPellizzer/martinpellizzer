Ambrosia abrotanifolia

Ambrosia abrotanifolia, commonly known as white ambrosia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and arid environments. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs.

Ambrosia abrotanifolia is known for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
