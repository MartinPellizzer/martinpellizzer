Astragalus bejourensis

Astragalus bejourensis, commonly known as the Bejouren milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, compound leaves, and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Astragalus bejourensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
