Astragalus macrosemius

Astragalus macrosemius, commonly known as the large-seeded milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant produces small, leathery pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry grasslands and along creek beds.

Due to its adaptability to harsh environments, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
