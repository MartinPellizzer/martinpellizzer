Agave vandervinnenii

Agave vandervinnenii, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, blue-gray leaves with sharp spines. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears yellow-green flowers, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Agave vandervinnenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
