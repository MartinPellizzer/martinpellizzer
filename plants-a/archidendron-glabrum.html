Archidendron glabrum

Archidendron glabrum, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it produces small, flat, brown pods that contain seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
