Alstroemeria magnifica var. sierrae

Alstroemeria magnifica var. sierrae, commonly known as the Sierra Alstroemeria, is a striking perennial plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting black or purple markings. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its long-lasting blooms.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to adapt to a range of growing conditions.
