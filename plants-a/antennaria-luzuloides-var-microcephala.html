Antennaria luzuloides var. microcephala

Antennaria luzuloides var. microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, white or pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy species often found in tundra and rocky habitats.
