Alyssum tetrastemon var. cappadocicum

Alyssum tetrastemon var. cappadocicum, commonly known as Cappadocian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Cappadocia region of Turkey.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are densely arranged along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate blooms and ability to attract beneficial insects.
