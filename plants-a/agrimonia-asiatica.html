Agrimonia asiatica

Agrimonia asiatica, commonly known as the Asian agrimony, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features upright stems covered with soft, glandular hairs and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Agrimonia asiatica is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
