Actaea palmata

Actaea palmata, commonly known as the red actaea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves and showy white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its berries, which are red and fleshy, are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Actaea palmata is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and natural landscapes.
