Aechmea lalindei

Aechmea lalindei, commonly known as the Lalinde bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant features vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, adding ornamental value. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and protection from intense sunlight.

Aechmea lalindei thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.

Morphology

Aechmea lalindei has rosette-shaped leaves that are fleshy, sword-like, and arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem.

The leaves are typically dark green with a waxy coating, measuring up to 30 cm in length. The plant has a short, thick stem covered in a smooth, dark brown bark that grows upright. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by bright red bracts, with small white to yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months and are pollinated by insects.

The fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that disperses seeds through wind and animal consumption.
