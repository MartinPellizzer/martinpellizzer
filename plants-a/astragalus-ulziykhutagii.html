Astragalus ulziykhutagii

Astragalus ulziykhutagii, commonly known as the Ulzhiykhutag milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and serving as a food source for local wildlife.
