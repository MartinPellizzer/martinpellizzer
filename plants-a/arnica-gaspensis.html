Arnica gaspensis

Arnica gaspensis, commonly known as Gaspé arnica, is a flowering plant native to the Gaspé Peninsula in eastern Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as meadows and forest edges. It has a short stem with several leafy branches and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Arnica gaspensis is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory properties, though it is not as widely commercialized as other arnica species.
