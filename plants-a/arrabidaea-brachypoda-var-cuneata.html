Arrabidaea brachypoda var. cuneata

Arrabidaea brachypoda var. cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arrabidaea, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
