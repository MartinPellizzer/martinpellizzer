Astragalus eriocephalus subsp. bracteolatus

Astragalus eriocephalus subsp.

bracteolatus, commonly known as the bracteolate milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features compound leaves with small, bract-like structures at the base of the leaflets. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, arranged in dense clusters, and are characteristic of the legume family.

It has a deep taproot system, which helps it survive in arid conditions.
