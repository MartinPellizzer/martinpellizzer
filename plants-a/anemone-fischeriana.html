Anemone fischeriana

Anemone fischeriana, commonly known as the Fischer's anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately divided, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely distributed outside its native habitat.
