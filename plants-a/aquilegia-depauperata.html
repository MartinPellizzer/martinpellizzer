Aquilegia depauperata

Aquilegia depauperata, commonly known as the poorflower or slender columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, spurred flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers, which bloom in early summer, are usually pink or red with five petals and elongated nectar spurs.

Aquilegia depauperata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
