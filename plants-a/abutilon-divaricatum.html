Abutilon divaricatum

Abutilon divaricatum, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, spathaceous bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are often brightly colored. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with hairy, glandular leaves.

Abutilon divaricatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
