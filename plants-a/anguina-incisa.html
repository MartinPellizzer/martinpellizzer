Anguina incisa

Anguina incisa, commonly known as the cutleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a serrated edge. Its flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals arranged symmetrically.
