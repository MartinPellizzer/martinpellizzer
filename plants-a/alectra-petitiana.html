Alectra petitiana

Alectra petitiana, commonly known as the Petitian star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family, which includes orchids, and is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale pink, bloom in early spring.

Alectra petitiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
