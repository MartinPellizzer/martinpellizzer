Alisma × bjoerkqvistii

Alisma × bjoerkqvistii, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a natural hybrid between Alisma plantago-aquatica and Alisma orientale.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually floating or semi-submerged, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in summer. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and colonize wetland areas effectively.

Alisma × bjoerkqvistii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing aquatic habitats and providing food for various wildlife species.
