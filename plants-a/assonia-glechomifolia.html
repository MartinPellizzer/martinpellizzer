Assonia glechomifolia

Assonia glechomifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly aromatic scent. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Assonia glechomifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can also serve as a nectar source for pollinators.
