Arctostaphylos gabrielensis

Arctostaphylos gabrielensis, commonly known as Gabriel's manzanita, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with small, leathery leaves that are typically reddish-brown in color. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild, wet winters.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
