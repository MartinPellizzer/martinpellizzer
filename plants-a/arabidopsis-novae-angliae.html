Arabidopsis novae-angliae

Arabidopsis novae-angliae, commonly known as the New England Arabidopsis, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known plants such as mustard and cabbage. This species is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and is often found in moist, sandy soils. It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters.

The plant's genome has been studied for its role in understanding plant genetics and evolution.
