Astragalus melaleucus

Astragalus melaleucus, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Its seeds are adapted for dispersal by wind, contributing to its distribution across arid regions.
