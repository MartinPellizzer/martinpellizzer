Abutilon subpapyraceum

Abutilon subpapyraceum, commonly known as the paperleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with a woody stem and alternate leaves.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
