Aristolochia nummulariifolia

Aristolochia nummulariifolia, commonly known as the dollar vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that resemble small coins, giving it its common name. The plant produces unique, intricate flowers that are often maroon or purple in color, with a distinctive, winding structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
