Artemisia genipi

Artemisia genipi, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant contains volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been traditionally used in herbal medicine and as a flavoring agent.
