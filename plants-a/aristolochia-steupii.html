Aristolochia steupii

Aristolochia steupii, commonly known as the Steup's arrowhead vine, is a climbing perennial plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, intricate, pipe-like flowers that are often greenish-yellow in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family, known for its complex floral structures and chemical compounds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and researchers alike.
