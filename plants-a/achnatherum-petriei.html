Achnatherum petriei

Achnatherum petriei, commonly known as the Petrie's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, which produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Achnatherum petriei plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
