Alchemilla pavlovii

Alchemilla pavlovii, commonly known as the Pavlov's lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silvery-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Alchemilla pavlovii thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
