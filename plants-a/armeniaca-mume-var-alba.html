Armeniaca mume var. alba

Armeniaca mume var. alba, commonly known as the white Chinese plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant produces small, yellow or red fruits that are edible but often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

The white variety is distinguished by its pale floral color, setting it apart from other cultivars of the species.
