Anthericum volcanense

Anthericum volcanense, commonly known as the volcanic arum, is a striking plant native to volcanic regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive inflorescence that resembles a spathe. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered within the spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Anthericum volcanense thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its unique appearance and habitat make it a notable species in botanical studies.
