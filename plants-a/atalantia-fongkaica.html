Atalantia fongkaica

Atalantia fongkaica, commonly known as the Fongkaic citrus, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. The fruit's peel contains essential oils, making it valuable in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
