Aloe madecassa var. lutea

Aloe madecassa var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow aloe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and thick, fawn-colored leaves that are covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Its ability to store water in its leaves makes it well adapted to arid environments.
