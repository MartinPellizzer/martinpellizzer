Aster exiguifolius

Aster exiguifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its small, narrow leaves and showy purple or blue flowers make it a distinctive species.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
