Anemone pratensis var. flavescens

Anemone pratensis var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow meadow anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow, depending on the variety. Its leaves are basal, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is characterized by its early spring blooming habit and its role in supporting pollinators.
