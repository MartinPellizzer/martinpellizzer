Agapetes oxycoccoides

Agapetes oxycoccoides, commonly known as the dwarf snowbell, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or orange in color, which are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
