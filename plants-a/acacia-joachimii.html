Acacia joachimii

Acacia joachimii, commonly known as the Joachim's wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Acacia joachimii is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
