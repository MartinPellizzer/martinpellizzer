Aleuritia prolifera

Aleuritia prolifera, commonly known as the Chinese snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Aleuritia prolifera thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
