Acanthophyllum andarabicum

Acanthophyllum andarabicum, commonly known as the Arabian spiny shrub, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is highly adapted to harsh desert conditions, with deep root systems and reduced leaf surfaces to minimize water loss. It plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in ecological restoration projects in arid environments.
