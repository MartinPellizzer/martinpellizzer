Anthurium puberulum

Anthurium puberulum, commonly known as the velvet anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are inflorescences that resemble flowers but are actually modified leaves. Anthurium puberulum thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
