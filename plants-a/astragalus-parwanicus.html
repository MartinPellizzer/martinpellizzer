Astragalus parwanicus

Astragalus parwanicus, commonly known as Parwan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers, which contributes to its survival in dry climates.

Astragalus parwanicus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
