Aechmea ridleyi

Aechmea ridleyi, commonly known as the Ridley's pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients from the surrounding environment. The leaves are typically broad, stiff, and covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and protection from intense sunlight. It produces striking inflorescences with bright red bracts that house small, fragrant flowers.

This plant is well adapted to humid, shaded conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Morphology

Aechmea ridleyi has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, acuminate, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a dark green color and a waxy surface.

The stem is short and thick, with a smooth, grayish bark that grows from the base of the plant. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, featuring bright red and orange flowers that bloom during the dry season and are pollinated by birds. The fruit is a three-lobed capsule that disperses seeds through wind and animals.

The roots are fibrous and form a dense network near the soil surface to anchor the plant and absorb nutrients.
