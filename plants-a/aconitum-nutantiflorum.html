Aconitum nutantiflorum

Aconitum nutantiflorum, commonly known as the nodding aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that bear large, showy flowers with spurred petals. The plant has palmately divided, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive blue-green color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a poisonous plant and should be handled with care.
