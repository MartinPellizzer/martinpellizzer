Arrhostoxylum pedunculosum

Arrhostoxylum pedunculosum, commonly known as the Andean burr plant, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, spiny fruits that resemble burrs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its distinctive seed pods. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, temperate climates.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
