Anagallis churiensis

Anagallis churiensis, commonly known as the mouse-ear chickweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of delicate, pink or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its scientific name reflects its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
