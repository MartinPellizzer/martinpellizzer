Arabis quinqueloba

Arabis quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of shiny, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in alpine or arctic regions. It is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
