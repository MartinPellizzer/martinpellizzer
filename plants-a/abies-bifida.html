Abies bifida

Abies bifida, commonly known as the split-fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its distinctive, finely divided foliage that gives it a feathery appearance. The leaves are needle-like, arranged in spirals along the branches, and are typically dark green with a white or grayish underside. Its cones are small and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color, and are often used in traditional medicine.

The tree thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.
