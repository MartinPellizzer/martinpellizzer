Astragalus strictifolius f. intermedius

Astragalus strictifolius f. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-purple, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
