Aster edwardii

Aster edwardii, commonly known as the Edward's aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, thriving in partial to full sunlight. Its showy purple to pink flowers bloom in the late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster edwardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
