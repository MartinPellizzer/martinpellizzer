Acalypha pauciflora

Acalypha pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered spurflower, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Acalypha pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
