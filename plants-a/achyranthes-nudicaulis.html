Achyranthes nudicaulis

Achyranthes nudicaulis, commonly known as slender knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as riverbanks and wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in loose panicles, which bloom in the summer months.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
