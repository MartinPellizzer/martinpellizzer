Abutilon bedfordianum var. concolor

Abutilon bedfordianum var. concolor, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green with a silvery underside. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom from summer to fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
