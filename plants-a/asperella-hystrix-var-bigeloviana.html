Asperella hystrix var. bigeloviana

Asperella hystrix var. bigeloviana, commonly known as the Bigelov's spiny hibiscus, is a variant of the spiny hibiscus native to parts of the southern United States.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and rough, spiny leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
