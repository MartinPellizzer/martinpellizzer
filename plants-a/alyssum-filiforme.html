Alyssum filiforme

Alyssum filiforme, commonly known as slender alyssum, is a low-growing annual plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring and early summer. The plant has slender, thread-like leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum filiforme is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and ladybugs.
