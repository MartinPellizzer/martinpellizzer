Arabis cantalica

Arabis cantalica, commonly known as the Cantaloupe Arabis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate with a hairy surface, and it produces clusters of small, white flowers with four petals. Arabis cantalica typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
