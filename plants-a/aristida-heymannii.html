Aristida heymannii

Aristida heymannii, commonly known as the Hymenochirus grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and savannas. The plant has slender, erect culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, flowering structures typical of the grass family.

Aristida heymannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
