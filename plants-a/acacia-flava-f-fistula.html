Acacia flava f. fistula

Acacia flava f. fistula, commonly known as the yellow wattle.

It is a shrub native to Australia, characterized by its bright yellow floral spikes and compound leaves. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid conditions. Its flowers are typically visited by a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
