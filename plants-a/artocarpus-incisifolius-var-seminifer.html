Artocarpus incisifolius var. seminifer

Artocarpus incisifolius var. seminifer, commonly known as the Seminifer breadfruit, is a variant of the breadfruit tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, rough, green fruit that ripens to a yellowish color. The fruit contains a dense, starchy pulp surrounded by a fibrous outer layer, making it a valuable food source. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
