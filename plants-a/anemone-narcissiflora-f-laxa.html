Anemone narcissiflora f. laxa

Anemone narcissiflora f. laxa, commonly known as the loose-flowered anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its cultivar form, 'laxa,' is characterized by its more loosely arranged flower heads compared to the typical form.
