Artemisia kandaharensis

Artemisia kandaharensis, commonly known as Kandahar wormwood, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
