Achillea rupestris subsp. rupestris

Achillea rupestris subsp.

rupestris, commonly known as the rock yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

The plant features upright, branched stems with narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. Its flowers are small, white to pale yellow, and arranged in flat-topped clusters called umbels.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in challenging landscapes.
