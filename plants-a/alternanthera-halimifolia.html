Alternanthera halimifolia

Alternanthera halimifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf alternanthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through stolons make it a popular choice for ground cover in tropical and subtropical regions.
