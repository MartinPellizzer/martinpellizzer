Acacia polyphylla var. giganticarpa

Acacia polyphylla var. giganticarpa, commonly known as the giant wattle, is a striking member of the legume family native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its large, fern-like leaves and long, yellow-scented pods that contain the characteristic acacia seeds. The plant can grow up to 10 meters tall, making it one of the tallest acacia species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid and semi-arid climates of its native region.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native fauna.
