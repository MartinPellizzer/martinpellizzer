Aconitum coeruleum

Aconitum coeruleum, commonly known as the blue monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply veined and often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. Aconitum coeruleum is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of alkaloids.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.
