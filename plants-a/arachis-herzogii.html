Arachis herzogii

Arachis herzogii, commonly known as the Herzog groundnut, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and edible seeds. The plant produces small, tuberous fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers.
