Anthericum lagopus

Anthericum lagopus, commonly known as the marsh helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces striking, hooded flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip, typically in shades of yellow and green. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as bogs and wet meadows.

Anthericum lagopus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
