Ammodendron conollyi

Ammodendron conollyi, commonly known as the Desert Date Tree, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and a thick, flaky bark that helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, date-like fruits. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor soils.

Ammodendron conollyi plays an important ecological role in desert ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
