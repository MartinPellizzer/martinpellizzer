Abies pindrow subsp. gamblei

Abies pindrow subsp.

gamblei, commonly known as the Gambel's fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Himalayas. It belongs to the genus Abies, which includes various species of fir trees.

The plant is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. It typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in moist, cool environments.

The cones of this subspecies are usually small and cylindrical, releasing seeds that contribute to its ecological role in mountainous forests.
