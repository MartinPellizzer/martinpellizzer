Acanthophyllum adenophorum

Acanthophyllum adenophorum, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a low-growing, perennial shrub characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, clustered flowers. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, it thrives in sandy and gravelly soils. The plant plays a vital role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
