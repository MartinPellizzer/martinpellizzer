Ardisia basilanensis

Ardisia basilanensis, commonly known as the Basilan starfruit, is a member of the Myrsinaceae family and is native to the Philippines.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in lowland to montane forests. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its fruit is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor, making it a popular fruit in local markets.

The plant's ornamental value and culinary uses contribute to its importance in both horticulture and local cuisine.
