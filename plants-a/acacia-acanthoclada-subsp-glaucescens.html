Acacia acanthoclada subsp. glaucescens

Acacia acanthoclada subsp.

glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous wattle, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, bipinnate leaves that are covered in a whitish, powdery coating.

The plant produces yellow globular flower heads during the winter months, which are composed of numerous tiny flowers. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and salinity.

The subspecies is distinguished by its glaucous appearance and is often found in sandy soils across the inland regions of Western Australia.
