Asparagus nesiotes

Asparagus nesiotes, commonly known as the giant asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes. Its ornamental value comes from its lush foliage and long-lasting, fragrant blooms.

Asparagus nesiotes is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional remedies.
