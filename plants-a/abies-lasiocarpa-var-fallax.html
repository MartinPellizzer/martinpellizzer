Abies lasiocarpa var. fallax

Abies lasiocarpa var. fallax, commonly known as the subalpine fir, is a variant of the subalpine fir native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The cones of this variety are typically small and often hang downward, a distinguishing feature from other fir species. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to harsh alpine conditions and its role in forest ecosystems at elevations above tree line.
