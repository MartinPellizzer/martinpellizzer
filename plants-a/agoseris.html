Agoseris

Agoseris, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It consists of several species native to North America, particularly in the western and central regions. The plants typically have basal rosettes of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produce yellow or white flowers with ray florets. Agoseris species are often found in dry, rocky habitats such as meadows, slopes, and along stream banks.

They are characterized by their ability to thrive in harsh environments and their ecological importance as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
