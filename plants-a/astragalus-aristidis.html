Astragalus aristidis

Astragalus aristidis, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
