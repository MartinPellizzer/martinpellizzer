Andropogon pospischilii

Andropogon pospischilii, commonly known as the tallgrass bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to dry conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in native landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
