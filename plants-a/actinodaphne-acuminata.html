Actinodaphne acuminata

Actinodaphne acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces dark, oily berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its essential oils and timber.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
