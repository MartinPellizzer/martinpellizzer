Albuca seretii

Albuca seretii, commonly known as the Zulu bulb, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Albuca seretii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
