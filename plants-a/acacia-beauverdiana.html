Acacia beauverdiana

Acacia beauverdiana, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing remarkable drought tolerance.

The wood of Acacia beauverdiana is hard and durable, making it valuable for local use in construction and crafts.
