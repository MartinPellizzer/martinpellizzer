Ascium guianense

Ascium guianense, commonly known as the Guianese ascium, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in the rainforests of Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and large, palmately lobed leaves. Ascium guianense is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and showy blooms.
