Azalea farrerae

Azalea farrerae, commonly known as the Mexican azalea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

Azalea farrerae prefers partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
