Agrostis rigidula var. arisan-montana

Agrostis rigidula var. arisan-montana, commonly known as the Arisan mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky slopes, preferring cool and moist climates. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in alpine ecosystems and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas.
