Abildgaardia coleotricha var. miegei

Abildgaardia coleotricha var. miegei, commonly known as the Miege's bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, fibrous stalks that often bear dense clusters of bristly hairs. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs.

This variety is notable for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its distinctive appearance.
