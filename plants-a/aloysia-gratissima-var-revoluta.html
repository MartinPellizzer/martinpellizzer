Aloysia gratissima var. revoluta

Aloysia gratissima var. revoluta, commonly known as the purple verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for Mediterranean climates.
