Acanthocarpus humilis

Acanthocarpus humilis, commonly known as the dwarf acanthocarp, is a small, perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, rounded leaves and clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, indehiscent fruits that contain minute seeds, typical of the Leguminosae family. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and rocky habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects in arid regions.
