Artemisia vulgaris var. cinerascens

Artemisia vulgaris var. cinerascens, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and slender, branched stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its chemical composition includes volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its medicinal and pesticidal properties.
