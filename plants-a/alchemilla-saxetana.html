Alchemilla saxetana

Alchemilla saxetana, commonly known as the Saxony lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, silver-gray leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Alchemilla saxetana is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in woodland settings.
