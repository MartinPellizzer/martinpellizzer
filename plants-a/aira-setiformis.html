Aira setiformis

Aira setiformis, commonly known as the slender air grass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, riverbanks, and wet meadows. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are often tinged with purple or red.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
