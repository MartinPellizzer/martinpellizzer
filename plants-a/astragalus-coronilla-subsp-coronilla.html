Astragalus coronilla subsp. coronilla

Astragalus coronilla subsp.

coronilla, commonly known as the Crown vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has a deep root system, which helps prevent soil erosion. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil fertility.
