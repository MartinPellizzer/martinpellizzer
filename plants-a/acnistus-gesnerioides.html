Acnistus gesnerioides

Acnistus gesnerioides, commonly known as the creeping false mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces small, pink or white flowers clustered in umbels. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with opposite leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers.

Acnistus gesnerioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ability to thrive in partial shade.
