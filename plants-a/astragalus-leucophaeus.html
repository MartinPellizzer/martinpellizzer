Astragalus leucophaeus

Astragalus leucophaeus, commonly known as the white-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus leucophaeus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
