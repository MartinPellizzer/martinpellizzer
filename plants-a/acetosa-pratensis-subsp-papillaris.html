Acetosa pratensis subsp. papillaris

Acetosa pratensis subsp.

papillaris, commonly known as the hairy meadow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that typically bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is commonly found in meadows and wetlands. Acetosa pratensis subsp.

papillaris plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
