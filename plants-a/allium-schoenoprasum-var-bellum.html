Allium schoenoprasum var. bellum

Allium schoenoprasum var. bellum, commonly known as the purple garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped purple flowers. The plant produces edible leaves and bulbils that are often used in culinary applications. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking purple flowers and aromatic leaves.
