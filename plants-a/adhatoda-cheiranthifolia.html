Adhatoda cheiranthifolia

Adhatoda cheiranthifolia, commonly known as the blue waxy leaf or Indian borage, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers and waxy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and showy blooms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Adhatoda cheiranthifolia is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
