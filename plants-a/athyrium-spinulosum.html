Athyrium spinulosum

Athyrium spinulosum, commonly known as the hairy male fern, is a species of fern native to Asia.

It belongs to the Dryopteridaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name.

The leaves are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive appearance that makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
