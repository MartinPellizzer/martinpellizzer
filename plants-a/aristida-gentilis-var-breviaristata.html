Aristida gentilis var. breviaristata

Aristida gentilis var. breviaristata, commonly known as the shortgrass bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is used in landscaping and restoration projects.
