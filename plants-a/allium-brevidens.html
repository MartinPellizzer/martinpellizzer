Allium brevidens

Allium brevidens, commonly known as the short-stemmed onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbs that are often used in local cuisines for their mild, onion-like flavor. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Allium brevidens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
