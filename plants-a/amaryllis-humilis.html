Amaryllis humilis

Amaryllis humilis, commonly known as the dwarf amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of white, pink, or red blooms during the summer months. Its foliage consists of strap-like, dark green leaves that emerge from a bulbous base.

Amaryllis humilis is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
