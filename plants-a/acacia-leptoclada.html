Acacia leptoclada

Acacia leptoclada, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
