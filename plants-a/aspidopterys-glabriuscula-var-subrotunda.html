Aspidopterys glabriuscula var. subrotunda

Aspidopterys glabriuscula var. subrotunda, commonly known as the broad-leafed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
