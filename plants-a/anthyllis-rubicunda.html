Anthyllis rubicunda

Anthyllis rubicunda, commonly known as the red anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has trifoliate leaves with small, hairy stipules. Its root system forms nodules with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.

The plant is often found in open woodlands and scrublands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
