Albuca elwesii

Albuca elwesii, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces upright flower stalks that bear clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Albuca elwesii is known for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom reliably in suitable conditions.
