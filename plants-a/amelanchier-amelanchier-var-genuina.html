Amelanchier amelanchier var. genuina

Amelanchier amelanchier var. genuina, commonly known as serviceberry or Juneberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, typically turning a deep red or black color. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, clearings, and rocky slopes.

The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
