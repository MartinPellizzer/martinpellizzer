Agathophyllum parvifolium

Agathophyllum parvifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agathophyllum, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and yellowish to pale green flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments and often grows in sandy or rocky soils. It has a compact growth habit and is typically found in steppe and semi-desert habitats.

Agathophyllum parvifolium is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
