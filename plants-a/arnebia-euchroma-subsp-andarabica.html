Arnebia euchroma subsp. andarabica

Arnebia euchroma subsp.

andarabica, commonly known as Indian madder, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan. It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant is notable for its deep red roots, which contain high concentrations of alizarin, a pigment used in traditional dyes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This subspecies is also valued for its medicinal properties in Ayurvedic practices.
