Adenocarpus bivonae

Adenocarpus bivonae, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, alternate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, pod-like fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used traditionally for various purposes.
