Andropogon ivohibensis

Andropogon ivohibensis, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and forest margins.

Due to its unique ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
