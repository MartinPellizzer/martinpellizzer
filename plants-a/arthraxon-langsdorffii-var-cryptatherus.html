Arthraxon langsdorffii var. cryptatherus

Arthraxon langsdorffii var. cryptatherus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arthraxon, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lipped flowers.

This variant is distinguished by its compact growth habit and unique floral structure compared to other members of the genus.
