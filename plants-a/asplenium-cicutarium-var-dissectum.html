Asplenium cicutarium var. dissectum

Asplenium cicutarium var. dissectum, commonly known as the dissected maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant features delicate, dark green, bi- or tri-pinnate leaves that are often arching and cascading, giving it a graceful appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
