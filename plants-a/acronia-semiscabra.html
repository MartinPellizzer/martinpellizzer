Acronia semiscabra

Acronia semiscabra, commonly known as the rough acronia, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Acronia semiscabra is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater.

Its unique morphology and ecological resilience make it an important species in its native habitat.
