Anemia wrightii

Anemia wrightii, commonly known as Wright's anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate and often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Anemia wrightii is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
