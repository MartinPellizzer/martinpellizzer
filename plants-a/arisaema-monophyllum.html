Arisaema monophyllum

Arisaema monophyllum, commonly known as the single-leaf arisaema, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Thailand and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a single, large, arrow-shaped leaf that emerges from a corm. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Arisaema monophyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unusual floral structure.
