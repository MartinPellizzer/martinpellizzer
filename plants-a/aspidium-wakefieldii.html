Aspidium wakefieldii

Aspidium wakefieldii, commonly known as Wakefield's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Water ferns.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically divided into narrow, serrated segments. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and marshes. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Aspidium wakefieldii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen through photosynthesis.
