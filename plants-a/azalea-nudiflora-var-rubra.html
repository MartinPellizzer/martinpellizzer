Azalea nudiflora var. rubra

Azalea nudiflora var. rubra, commonly known as the redleaf azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of funnel-shaped, showy flowers in shades of pink to red during late spring. It prefers partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and seasonal blooms in gardens.
