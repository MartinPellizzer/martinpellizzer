Astragalus parviceps

Astragalus parviceps, commonly known as small-headed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.

Astragalus parviceps is often found in grasslands and deserts, where it contributes to the local ecosystem's biodiversity.
