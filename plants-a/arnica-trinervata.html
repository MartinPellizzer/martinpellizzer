Arnica trinervata

Arnica trinervata, commonly known as the three-veined arnica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and grows in rocky or alpine habitats. It is often found in high-altitude meadows and is adapted to cold, dry environments.

Arnica trinervata is valued for its medicinal properties, though it is less commonly used than other arnica species.
