Anthericum scilliflorum

Anthericum scilliflorum, commonly known as the sea squill, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves and produces striking white or purple flowers in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found along rocky shores and dunes. It has a long flowering period, making it a valuable addition to coastal gardens.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional remedies.
