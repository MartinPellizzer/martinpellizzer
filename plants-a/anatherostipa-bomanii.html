Anatherostipa bomanii

Anatherostipa bomanii, commonly known as the Boman's feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the high Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and delicate, feathery inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its botanical structure includes slender, hollow stems and small, grass-like leaves with a distinct sheathing base.

Anatherostipa bomanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
