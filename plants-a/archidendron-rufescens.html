Archidendron rufescens

Archidendron rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed legume, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant features compound leaves with pairs of oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
