Amesia latifolia f. variegata

Amesia latifolia f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated broadleaf amesia, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features silvery-white, fuzzy foliage with striking variegation of green and cream, which enhances its ornamental value. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
