Allagoptera campestris var. orbignyi

Allagoptera campestris var. orbignyi, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
