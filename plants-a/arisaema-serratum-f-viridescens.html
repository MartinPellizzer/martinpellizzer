Arisaema serratum f. viridescens

Arisaema serratum f. viridescens, commonly known as the green-veined arisaema, is a variant of the common arisaema species.

It is characterized by its unique leaf structure, featuring a broad, arrow-shaped leaf with a prominent midrib and serrated edges. The plant produces an unusual inflorescence known as a spathaceous structure, which encloses the small, inconspicuous flowers. Its foliage is often a striking green, with veins that stand out vividly against the leaf surface.

This cultivar is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and is often grown in shaded, moist environments.
