Aster cordifolius var. polycephalus

Aster cordifolius var. polycephalus, commonly known as the heart-leaved aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. It is a perennial species that produces showy blooms in the late summer and early fall.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
