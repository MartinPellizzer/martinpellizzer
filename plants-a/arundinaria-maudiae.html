Arundinaria maudiae

Arundinaria maudiae, commonly known as the Maud's bamboo, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Arundinaria maudiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
