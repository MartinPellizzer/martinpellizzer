Abies excelsa var. elegans

Abies excelsa var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant firs, is a variant of the white fir native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like foliage. The leaves are typically shorter and more densely packed compared to the standard Abies excelsa, giving it a more refined appearance. It prefers cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
