Artocarpus dadah var. pubescens

Artocarpus dadah var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy texture on both its leaves and fruit. The plant produces a large, spiky fruit that turns from green to yellow as it matures. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a waxy surface.

The tree is valued for its edible fruit and is commonly found in humid tropical environments.
