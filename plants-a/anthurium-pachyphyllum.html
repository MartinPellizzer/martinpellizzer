Anthurium pachyphyllum

Anthurium pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking spadices, which are elongated, spadix-like inflorescences that are typically green with red or yellow spadices. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium pachyphyllum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
