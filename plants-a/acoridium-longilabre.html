Acoridium longilabre

Acoridium longilabre, commonly known as the long-lipped acoridium, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Acoraceae.

It is characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat, typically found in marshy or wetland environments. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem with narrow, linear leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a distinctive elongated labellum that gives it its common name.

Acoridium longilabre plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
