Allium meteoricum

Allium meteoricum, commonly known as the skyrocket garlic, is a hardy perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers at the top of the stem, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the bulbs are typically small and clustered.

Allium meteoricum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
