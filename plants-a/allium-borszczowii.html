Allium borszczowii

Allium borszczowii, commonly known as the Siberian chive, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Central Asia and Eastern Europe.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pale purple to lilac flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is closely related to other allium species, such as garlic and onions, and is valued for its aromatic foliage and edible bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical name honors the Polish botanist and physician, Borszczow, who contributed significantly to the study of Allium species.
