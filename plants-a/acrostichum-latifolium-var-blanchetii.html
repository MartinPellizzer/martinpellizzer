Acrostichum latifolium var. blanchetii

Acrostichum latifolium var. blanchetii, commonly known as the broadleaf fern, is a variant of the broad-leafed fern native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, broad, and leathery fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a smooth, waxy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
