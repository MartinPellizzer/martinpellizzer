Astragalus patentivillosus

Astragalus patentivillosus, commonly known as the silky milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stem, trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, and pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitats.
