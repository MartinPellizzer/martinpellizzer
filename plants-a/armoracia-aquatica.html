Armoracia aquatica

Armoracia aquatica, commonly known as water horseradish, is a perennial plant native to wetland areas of Europe and Asia.

It features a thick, fleshy rhizome that is often harvested for its pungent, spicy flavor. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. Its small, yellowish-green flowers grow in clusters and bloom in early summer.

The plant thrives in moist soils and is often found near rivers, lakes, and marshes.
