Alyssum incanum var. trichocarpum

Alyssum incanum var. trichocarpum, commonly known as the hairy alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically hairy and gray-green in color, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
