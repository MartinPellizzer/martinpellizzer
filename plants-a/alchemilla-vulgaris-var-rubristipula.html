Alchemilla vulgaris var. rubristipula

Alchemilla vulgaris var. rubristipula, commonly known as the red-stippled lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves with reddish margins that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in panicle-like clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscaping.
