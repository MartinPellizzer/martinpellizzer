Astragalus albicans

Astragalus albicans, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. Its small, bean-like pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus albicans is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in harsh, drought-prone environments.
