Annona trunciflora

Annona trunciflora, commonly known as the rough custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub with a short trunk and dense, glossy foliage. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by large, rough-skinned fruits. The fruit has a sweet, aromatic pulp with a gritty texture, and it is often used in culinary applications.

Annona trunciflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes grown in subtropical gardens.
