Allium sardoum

Allium sardoum, commonly known as the Sardinian leek, is a hardy perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant produces long, slender leaves that are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor. Its flowering stems bear small, clustered white or pale purple flowers.

Allium sardoum is often cultivated for both its culinary value and ornamental appeal in gardens.
