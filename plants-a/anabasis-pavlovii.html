Anabasis pavlovii

Anabasis pavlovii, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in sandy and rocky soils, often found in desert and semi-desert environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers, which helps it survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Anabasis pavlovii is also valued for its ability to stabilize sand dunes and prevent soil erosion in its native habitat.
