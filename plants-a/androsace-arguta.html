Androsace arguta

Androsace arguta, commonly known as the alpine mouse ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Primulaceae and is characterized by its dense, rosette-like foliage composed of small, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
