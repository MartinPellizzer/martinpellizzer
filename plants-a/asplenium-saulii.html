Asplenium saulii

Asplenium saulii, commonly known as the Saul's spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the Aspleniaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces spores in small, clustered structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices or forest floors.

Asplenium saulii is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in woodland ecosystems.
