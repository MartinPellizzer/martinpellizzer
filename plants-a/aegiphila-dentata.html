Aegiphila dentata

Aegiphila dentata, commonly known as the toothed aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, opposite leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. It produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

Aegiphila dentata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
