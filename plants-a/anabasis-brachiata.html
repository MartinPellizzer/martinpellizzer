Anabasis brachiata

Anabasis brachiata, commonly known as the desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent leaves and upright stems. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its ability to store water in its leaves and stems. It produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

Anabasis brachiata is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in regions where it grows naturally.
