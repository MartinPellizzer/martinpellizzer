Acacia aureocrinita

Acacia aureocrinita, commonly known as the golden hairpin acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-covered stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine, golden hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Acacia aureocrinita is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
