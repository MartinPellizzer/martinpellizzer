Alchemilla lindblomiana

Alchemilla lindblomiana, commonly known as Lindblom's lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in early spring, clustered in a cup-like structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
