Aleuritia melanodonta

Aleuritia melanodonta, commonly known as the black-fruited elder, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Aleuritia melanodonta is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in temperate climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
