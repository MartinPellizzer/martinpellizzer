Antennaria chilensis

Antennaria chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, hairy leaves and produces tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or coastal habitats.

Antennaria chilensis is adapted to harsh environmental conditions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
