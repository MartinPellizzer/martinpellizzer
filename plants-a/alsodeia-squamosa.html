Alsodeia squamosa

Alsodeia squamosa, commonly known as the scaly alsike, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often covered with a distinctive scaly texture. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers bloom in early summer, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Alsodeia squamosa is valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
