Astragalus complanatus var. eutrichus

Astragalus complanatus var. eutrichus, commonly known as the plains milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Great Plains of North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring. It is characterized by its robust root system, which helps it survive in arid conditions.

This species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
