Anthemis confusa

Anthemis confusa, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its flowers are yellow and produce a strong, often unpleasant odor, which may deter herbivores.

The leaves are usually hairy and have a distinct, aromatic scent.
