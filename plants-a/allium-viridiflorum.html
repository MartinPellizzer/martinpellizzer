Allium viridiflorum

Allium viridiflorum, commonly known as the green-flowered garlic, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures similar to garlic, which are used in traditional medicine and cooking. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium viridiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
