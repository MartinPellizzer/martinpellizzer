Acrostichum gardneri

Acrostichum gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's brake, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is a terrestrial plant characterized by its large, leathery, bipinnate fronds that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length. The fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive, rugged texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called sori located on the underside of its fronds.
