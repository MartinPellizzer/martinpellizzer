Aster elmeri

Aster elmeri, commonly known as the Elmer's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Aster elmeri is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
