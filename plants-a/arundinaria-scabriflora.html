Arundinaria scabriflora

Arundinaria scabriflora, commonly known as the roughleaf bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, rough-textured leaves. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories. Arundinaria scabriflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in traditional landscaping.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for erosion control and habitat restoration projects.
