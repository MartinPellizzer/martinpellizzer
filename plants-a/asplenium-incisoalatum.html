Asplenium incisoalatum

Asplenium incisoalatum, commonly known as the serrated maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, serrated fronds that give it a delicate and intricate appearance. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps in retaining moisture.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in indoor conditions.
