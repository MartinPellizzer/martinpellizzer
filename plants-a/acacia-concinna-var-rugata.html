Acacia concinna var. rugata

Acacia concinna var. rugata, commonly known as the rough-barked acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rough, fissured bark that peels in strips, giving it a rugged appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typically pollinated by birds and insects. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry climates.
