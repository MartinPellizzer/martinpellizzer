Ampelocissus africana var. africana

Ampelocissus africana var. africana, commonly known as the African ivy, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its vigorous growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in suitable climates.
