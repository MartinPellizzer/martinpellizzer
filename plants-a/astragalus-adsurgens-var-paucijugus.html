Astragalus adsurgens var. paucijugus

Astragalus adsurgens var. paucijugus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are compound, with few pairs of leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland ecosystems.
