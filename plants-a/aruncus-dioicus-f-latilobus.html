Aruncus dioicus f. latilobus

Aruncus dioicus f. latilobus, commonly known as the broad-lobed goat's beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features striking, feathery plumes of white flowers that emerge in late spring to early summer. The plant has large, fern-like leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a dramatic, architectural appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland settings.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators.
