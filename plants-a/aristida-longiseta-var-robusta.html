Aristida longiseta var. robusta

Aristida longiseta var. robusta, commonly known as the robust hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow, wiry, and often have a rough texture, which helps reduce water loss in dry environments.

This grass is well adapted to poor soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.
