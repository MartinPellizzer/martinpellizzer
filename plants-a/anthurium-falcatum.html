Anthurium falcatum

Anthurium falcatum, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are modified leaves that enclose the actual flowers, often in vibrant red or orange hues. Anthurium falcatum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
