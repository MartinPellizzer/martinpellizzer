Allium ornithogaloides

Allium ornithogaloides, commonly known as the bird's garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium ornithogaloides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its mild garlic-like fragrance.
