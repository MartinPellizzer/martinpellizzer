Aegilops triuncialis subsp. fascicularis

Aegilops triuncialis subsp.

fascicularis, commonly known as the wild rye, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered in fascicles, giving it its subspecific name. It is typically found in disturbed soils, fields, and along roadsides.

This species is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
