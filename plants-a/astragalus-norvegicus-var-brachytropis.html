Astragalus norvegicus var. brachytropis

Astragalus norvegicus var. brachytropis, commonly known as the Norwegian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

This species is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
