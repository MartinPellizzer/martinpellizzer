Agyneia bacciformis var. angustifolia

Agyneia bacciformis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed agyneia, is a shrub native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
