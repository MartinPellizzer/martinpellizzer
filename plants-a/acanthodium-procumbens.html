Acanthodium procumbens

Acanthodium procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate acanthodium, is a flowering plant species native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by insects such as bees and flies. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
