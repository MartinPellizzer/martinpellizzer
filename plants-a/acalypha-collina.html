Acalypha collina

Acalypha collina, commonly known as silky fanflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Acalypha collina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and ability to add texture to gardens.
