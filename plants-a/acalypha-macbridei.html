Acalypha macbridei

Acalypha macbridei, commonly known as the MacBride's spurflower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spiny bracts that encase its small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Acalypha macbridei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique floral structure.
