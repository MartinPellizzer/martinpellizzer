Achillea dracunculus

Achillea dracunculus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are often clustered in flat-topped arrays. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a feathery appearance, and the stems are usually hairy.

It is commonly used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
