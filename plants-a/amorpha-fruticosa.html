Amorpha fruticosa

Amorpha fruticosa, commonly known as silky leadplant, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woods or along roadsides.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
