Albizia lebbekoides

Albizia lebbekoides, commonly known as the Indian velvet tamarind, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria, enhancing soil fertility. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod, and the tree is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.

Albizia lebbekoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
