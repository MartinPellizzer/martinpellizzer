Aristeguietia salvia

Aristeguietia salvia, commonly known as the Andean sage, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally by indigenous communities for various health purposes.
