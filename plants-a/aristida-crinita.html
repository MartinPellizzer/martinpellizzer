Aristida crinita

Aristida crinita, commonly known as silky needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, rocky soils, and disturbed areas. The plant is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, silky hairs that cover the leaves and inflorescence. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of numerous small, spikelet-like flowers.

Aristida crinita is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
