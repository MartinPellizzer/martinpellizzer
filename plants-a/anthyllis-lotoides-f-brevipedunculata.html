Anthyllis lotoides f. brevipedunculata

Anthyllis lotoides f. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stalked broom, is a hemicryptophyte shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
