Asterolasia asteriscophora subsp. albiflora

Asterolasia asteriscophora subsp.

albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered asterolasia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers.

The species is adapted to sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun, often found in coastal or inland regions. Its flowers bloom during the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
