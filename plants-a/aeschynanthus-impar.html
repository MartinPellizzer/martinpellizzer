Aeschynanthus impar

Aeschynanthus impar, commonly known as the uneven-flowered ivy.

It is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of red and orange. Its leaves are typically elliptical and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.

Aeschynanthus impar is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in hanging baskets or as a trailing plant.
