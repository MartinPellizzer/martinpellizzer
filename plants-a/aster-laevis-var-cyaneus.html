Aster laevis var. cyaneus

Aster laevis var. cyaneus, commonly known as the blue aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soil. It has slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
