Acrodiclidium campechianum

Acrodiclidium campechianum, commonly known as the Mexican fire cactus, is a species of cactus native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, spiny stems that resemble flames, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
