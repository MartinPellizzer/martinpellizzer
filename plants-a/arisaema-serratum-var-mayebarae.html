Arisaema serratum var. mayebarae

Arisaema serratum var. mayebarae, commonly known as the Mayebarae arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces yellowish-green berries that are typically consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Araceae family.
