Allium guttatum

Allium guttatum, commonly known as the red-eyed garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant's bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making them useful in culinary applications. Allium guttatum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
