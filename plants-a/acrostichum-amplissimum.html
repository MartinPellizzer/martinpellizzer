Acrostichum amplissimum

Acrostichum amplissimum, commonly known as the giant fern, is a large, tropical fern native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its massive, lance-shaped fronds that can grow up to 3 meters in length. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with a thick, underground stem that supports its extensive foliage. Its spores are produced in clusters on the undersides of its fronds, typical of fern reproduction.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.
