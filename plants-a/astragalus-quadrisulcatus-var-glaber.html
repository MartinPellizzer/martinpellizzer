Astragalus quadrisulcatus var. glaber

Astragalus quadrisulcatus var. glaber, commonly known as the smoothleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a deep taproot system, which allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are adapted for pollination by various insects.

The smooth, glabrous leaves distinguish this variety from others within the species.
