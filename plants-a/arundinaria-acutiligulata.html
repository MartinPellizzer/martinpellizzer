Arundinaria acutiligulata

Arundinaria acutiligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Arundinaria acutiligulata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
