Amaranthus hybridus subsp. bouchonii

Amaranthus hybridus subsp.

bouchonii, commonly known as Bouchon's amaranth, is a species native to South America. It is a flowering plant in the amaranth family, Amaranthaceae, characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy bracts.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by colorful, leaf-like structures called bracts. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in some traditional cuisines.

The leaves are edible and can be cooked similarly to spinach or other leafy greens.
