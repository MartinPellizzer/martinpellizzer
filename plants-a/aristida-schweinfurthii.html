Aristida schweinfurthii

Aristida schweinfurthii, commonly known as the Schweinfurth's dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is often used in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
