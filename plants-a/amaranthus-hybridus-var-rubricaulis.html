Amaranthus hybridus var. rubricaulis

Amaranthus hybridus var. rubricaulis, commonly known as red amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to purple foliage and bract-like leaves that often form dense, colorful clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that develop into dry, three-angled seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a leaf vegetable.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardens and agricultural settings.
