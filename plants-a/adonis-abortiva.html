Adonis abortiva

Adonis abortiva, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow, cup-shaped flowers and basal leaves with lobed margins. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in well-drained soils.
