Aira flexuosa f. violacea

Aira flexuosa f. violacea, commonly known as purple tuftgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive violet-blue flower spikes that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This cultivar is often appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
