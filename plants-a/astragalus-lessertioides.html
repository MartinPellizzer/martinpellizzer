Astragalus lessertioides

Astragalus lessertioides, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with erect stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow or greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Astragalus lessertioides is part of the legume family and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
