Allium scrobiculatum

Allium scrobiculatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, bulbous structures that are used similarly to garlic and onions in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Allium scrobiculatum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its edible qualities.
