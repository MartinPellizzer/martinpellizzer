Areldia dressleri

Areldia dressleri, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, Areldia dressleri is under threat and is protected in several national parks and conservation areas.
