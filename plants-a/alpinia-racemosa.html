Alpinia racemosa

Alpinia racemosa, commonly known as the ginger lily, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
