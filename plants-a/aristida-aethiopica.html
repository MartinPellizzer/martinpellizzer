Aristida aethiopica

Aristida aethiopica, commonly known as the Ethiopian dropseed, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and resilient. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it blooms with delicate, pale blue to purple flowers.

Aristida aethiopica is often used in xeriscaping and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
