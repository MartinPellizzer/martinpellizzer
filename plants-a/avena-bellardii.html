Avena bellardii

Avena bellardii, commonly known as the bellard's oat, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Avena bellardii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
