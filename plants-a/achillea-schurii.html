Achillea schurii

Achillea schurii, commonly known as the Schur's yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Achillea schurii is also noted for its resilience and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
