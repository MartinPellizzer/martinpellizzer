Aristida fasciculata var. californica

Aristida fasciculata var. californica, commonly known as California threeawn, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides, fields, and coastal areas. The plant features slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous bristle-like flowers. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This grass is adapted to a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant.
