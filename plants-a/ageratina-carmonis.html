Ageratina carmonis

Ageratina carmonis, commonly known as the whiteflower bluemist, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, white to pale blue flowers that are clustered in dense, fluffy inflorescences, giving it a mist-like appearance. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Ageratina carmonis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks.
