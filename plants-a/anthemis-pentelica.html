Anthemis pentelica

Anthemis pentelica, commonly known as the Greek tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
