Arthrocereus mirabilis var. gracilior

Arthrocereus mirabilis var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender night bloomer, is a cacti species native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit, it is a popular choice for collectors and desert gardeners.
