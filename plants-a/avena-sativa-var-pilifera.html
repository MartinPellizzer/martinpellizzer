Avena sativa var. pilifera

Avena sativa var. pilifera, commonly known as the hairy oat, is a variant of the common oat characterized by its hairy foliage and inflorescence.

This plant belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, cylindrical stems and long, narrow leaves covered in fine hairs. The inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, each bearing two to three florets.

It is often cultivated for forage and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
