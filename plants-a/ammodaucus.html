Ammodaucus

Ammodaucus, commonly known as the fennel plant, is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and carrots.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its feathery, finely divided leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval fruits that are often used in culinary applications. Ammodaucus is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating digestive issues.

Its essential oils contain compounds like anethole, which contribute to its distinctive licorice-like aroma.
