Asparagus cuscutoides

Asparagus cuscutoides, commonly known as the parasitic asparagus, is a flowering plant that exhibits parasitic behavior.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is native to regions of southern Africa. The plant lacks true leaves and instead has scale-like structures, relying on its host for nutrients. Its flowers are typically small and greenish-white, often arranged in clusters.

Asparagus cuscutoides is notable for its unique life cycle and its ability to attach to and draw sustenance from other plants.
