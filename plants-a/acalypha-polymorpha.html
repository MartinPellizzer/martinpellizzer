Acalypha polymorpha

Acalypha polymorpha, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are often brightly colored in red, orange, or yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers inside the bracts, which are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

Acalypha polymorpha is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its purported health benefits.
