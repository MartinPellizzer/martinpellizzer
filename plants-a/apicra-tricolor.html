Apicra tricolor

Apicra tricolor, commonly known as the three-colored orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strikingly colorful flowers, which typically display three distinct hues on each petal. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Apicra tricolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and private collections.
