Acacia nicoyensis

Acacia nicoyensis, commonly known as the Nicoyan acacia, is a flowering plant native to Central America, particularly found in Costa Rica and Panama.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in dry, tropical regions. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often bipinnate, and its yellow, globular flowers that bloom in clusters. Acacia nicoyensis is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it an important species in dryland ecosystems.

Its wood is used locally for construction and fuel, and it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
