Acer opulifolium subsp. neapolitanum

Acer opulifolium subsp.

neapolitanum, commonly known as the Naples maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its finely dissected, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of silky, white hair.

The plant produces small, red or orange-red flowers in early spring, followed by samaras with two winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and compact growth habit make it a distinctive addition to landscapes.
