Artemisia aleutica

Artemisia aleutica, commonly known as Alaska wormwood, is a hardy perennial shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-green foliage and narrow, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Artemisia aleutica is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in rocky or sandy soils.

It is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological significance in its native habitats.
