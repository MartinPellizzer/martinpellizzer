Arundinaria mucronata

Arundinaria mucronata, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or the golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria mucronata is valued for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil conditions.
