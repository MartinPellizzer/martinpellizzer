Aspidium trifolium

Aspidium trifolium, commonly known as the three-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that are often submerged in water. The plant typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. Its rhizomes spread along the substrate, anchoring the plant and facilitating nutrient absorption.

The spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in aquatic ecosystems.
