Adenoncos triangularis

Adenoncos triangularis, commonly known as the triangular adenoncos, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, triangular-shaped leaves that are often covered in a powdery white coating, known as farina. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil.

Its unique leaf structure and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
