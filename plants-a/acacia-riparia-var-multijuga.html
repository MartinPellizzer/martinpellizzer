Acacia riparia var. multijuga

Acacia riparia var. multijuga, commonly known as the river acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. It thrives in riparian zones and is adapted to seasonal flooding, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
