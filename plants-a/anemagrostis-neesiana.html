Anemagrostis neesiana

Anemagrostis neesiana, commonly known as the slender tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological studies.
