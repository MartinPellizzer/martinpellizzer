Acalypha humilis

Acalypha humilis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or smallleaf spurge, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a soft, velvety texture, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, bead-like seed capsules. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and wetlands.

Acalypha humilis is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
