Astragalus guatemalensis var. lozani

Astragalus guatemalensis var. lozani, commonly known as the Guatemalan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role in its native range.
