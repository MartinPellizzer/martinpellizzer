Alsophila ferox

Alsophila ferox, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Alsophila ferox plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and shade for various organisms.
