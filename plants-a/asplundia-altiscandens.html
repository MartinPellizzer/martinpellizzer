Asplundia altiscandens

Asplundia altiscandens, commonly known as the climbing asplundia, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit, with aerial roots that allow it to ascend tree trunks or other supports. The plant produces striking, spatheate flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its leaves are usually large, heart-shaped, and have a waxy texture.

Asplundia altiscandens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded gardens and as a houseplant.
