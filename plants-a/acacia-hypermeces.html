Acacia hypermeces

Acacia hypermeces, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, feathery leaves that are typically green and glossy. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which appear in spring and attract a variety of pollinators. Acacia hypermeces is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts.
