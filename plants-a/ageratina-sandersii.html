Ageratina sandersii

Ageratina sandersii, commonly known as the sanders' blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy blue-gray inflorescences that resemble clouds, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. It produces small, white flowers that develop into pale blue or grayish seeds.

Ageratina sandersii is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
