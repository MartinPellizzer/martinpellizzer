Atriplex pentandra subsp. typica

Atriplex pentandra subsp.

typica, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its succulent, salt-tolerant leaves that help it thrive in saline soils.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its extensive root system allows it to access water from deep underground, making it well adapted to drought conditions. Atriplex pentandra subsp.

typica is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for wildlife.
