Allium obtusum

Allium obtusum, commonly known as the broadleaf garlic or wild leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in the summer. Its bulbous stem is often used in culinary applications, similar to other Allium species.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and is often grown in gardens for both ornamental and practical purposes.
