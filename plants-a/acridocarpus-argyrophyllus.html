Acridocarpus argyrophyllus

Acridocarpus argyrophyllus, commonly known as the silver-leaf acridocarpus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The leaves are elliptical and have a distinctive silvery-gray underside, which gives the plant its common name. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
