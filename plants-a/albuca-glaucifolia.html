Albuca glaucifolia

Albuca glaucifolia, commonly known as the blue-leaved albuca, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy, blue-gray coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it an attractive addition to low-maintenance gardens.
