Agialid senegalensis

Agialid senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of West Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white or greenish flowers. Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Agialid senegalensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in landscaping.
