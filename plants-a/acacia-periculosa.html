Acacia periculosa

Acacia periculosa, commonly known as the death's-head hawkmoth acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are arranged in dense, globular clusters. Its seed pods are elongated and contain hard, angular seeds.

Acacia periculosa is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
