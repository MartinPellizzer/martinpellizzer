Alstroemeria timida

Alstroemeria timida, commonly known as the shy alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has strap-like leaves that are often variegated.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of flowers, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
