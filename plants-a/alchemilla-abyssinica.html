Alchemilla abyssinica

Alchemilla abyssinica, commonly known as Ethiopian lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Alchemilla abyssinica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
