Aligera barbata

Aligera barbata, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds. Aligera barbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.

Its growth habit includes both shrub and tree forms, depending on environmental conditions.
