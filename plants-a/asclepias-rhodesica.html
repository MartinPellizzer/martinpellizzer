Asclepias rhodesica

Asclepias rhodesica, commonly known as the Rhodes milkweed, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the milkweed family, Apocynaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces follicles that contain toxic, milky sap, which is typical of the genus Asclepias. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant in regions with limited rainfall.
