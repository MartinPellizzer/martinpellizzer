Adesmia patagonica

Adesmia patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian burro's tail, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Adesmia patagonica is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
