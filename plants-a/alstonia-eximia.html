Alstonia eximia

Alstonia eximia, commonly known as the Indian almond tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and compound leaves that are opposite and pinnate. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Alstonia eximia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates.
