Anemone Nemorosa Var. Grayi

Common wood anemone, scientifically known as Anemone nemorosa var. grayi, is a delicate wildflower that blooms in early spring. It thrives in shaded forest floors and moist woodland habitats across Europe. With its pale white petals and a single central yellow stigma, it resembles a small, elegant star. This variant of the common wood anemone is distinguished by its slightly darker leaves and unique floral structure. It plays an important role in early pollination and is a symbol of the transition from winter to spring.
