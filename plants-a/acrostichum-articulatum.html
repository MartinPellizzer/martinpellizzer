Acrostichum articulatum

Acrostichum articulatum, commonly known as the giant chain fern, is a tropical plant native to the wetlands and marshes of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and colonize wetland areas effectively. Its fronds are divided into segments that are connected by jointed stalks, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Acrostichum articulatum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
