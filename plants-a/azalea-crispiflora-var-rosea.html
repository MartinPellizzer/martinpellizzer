Azalea crispiflora var. rosea

Azalea crispiflora var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered azalea, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant floral display and compact growth habit.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
