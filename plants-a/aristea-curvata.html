Aristea curvata

Aristea curvata, commonly known as the curling aristea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Aristea curvata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
