Acanthophyllum stocksianum

Acanthophyllum stocksianum, commonly known as the Stock's jointed sage, is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features dense, gray-green foliage with small, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces compact, showy clusters of violet to blue-purple flowers during the spring and early summer. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Acanthophyllum stocksianum is adapted to extreme drought and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
