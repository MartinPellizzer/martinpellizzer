Acalypha scabrosa

Acalypha scabrosa, commonly known as roughleaf ironwood, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has rough, scaly bark and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a distinctive textured surface.

Acalypha scabrosa is adapted to withstand drought and is often found in rocky soils.
