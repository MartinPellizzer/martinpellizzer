Aldama gilliesii

Aldama gilliesii, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a robust, succulent stem that can grow up to two meters tall, often with a distinctive blue-green coloration. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to store water in the harsh desert environment.

The plant is well-suited to sandy or rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
