Astragalus brachytropis f. giganteus

Astragalus brachytropis f. giganteus, commonly known as the giant milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
