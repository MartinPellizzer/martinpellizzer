Aesculus octandra

Aesculus octandra, commonly known as the California buckeye, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its palmately compound leaves, which are typically composed of five to seven leaflets. The tree produces showy, spiny capsules that contain glossy, dark brown seeds. Its flowers are pale green and clustered in upright panicles, adding to its ornamental value.

The plant is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
