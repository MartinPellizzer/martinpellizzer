Astragalus involucratus

Astragalus involucratus, commonly known as the velvetpod milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its fruit is a slender, hairy pod that contains several seeds.

Astragalus involucratus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native range.
