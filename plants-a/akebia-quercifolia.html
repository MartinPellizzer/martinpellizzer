Akebia quercifolia

Akebia quercifolia, commonly known as the Japanese arrow bamboo, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that resemble those of the oak tree. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red, edible fruits that ripen in late summer. Its stems are covered in a unique, corky layer that provides insulation and protection.

Akebia quercifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
