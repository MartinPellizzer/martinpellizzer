Argyranthemum grandiflorum

Argyranthemum grandiflorum, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features sturdy, upright stems and broad, slightly hairy leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Argyranthemum grandiflorum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
