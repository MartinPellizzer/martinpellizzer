Anemone pratensis f. pallida

Anemone pratensis f. pallida, commonly known as the pale meadow anemone, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which are typically arranged in a cluster of 5 to 15 petals. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are usually basal and hairy. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and wetlands.

This cultivar is valued for its delicate appearance and is commonly used in ornamental gardening.
