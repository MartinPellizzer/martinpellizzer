Anthemis australis

Anthemis australis, commonly known as the New Zealand dandelion, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the family. It thrives in coastal and subalpine regions, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Anthemis australis is known for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of New Zealand's native flora.
