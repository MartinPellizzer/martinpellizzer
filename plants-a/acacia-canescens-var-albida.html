Acacia canescens var. albida

Acacia canescens var. albida, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It features slender, pale green stems that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous tiny, linear leaflets that help reduce water loss in dry conditions.

This variety is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Native Distribution

Acacia canescens var. albida is primarily native to the arid and semi-arid regions of northern Australia, including parts of Western Australia, the Northern Territory, and South Australia.

It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is commonly found in shrublands and open woodlands. This variety is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient species in harsh environments. It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various native animals.

Its distribution is closely associated with areas that have a Mediterranean climate with dry summers and wet winters.

Acacia canescens var. albida was mainly introduced to South Africa, particularly in the Eastern Cape and Western Cape provinces.
