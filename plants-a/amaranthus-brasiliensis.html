Amaranthus brasiliensis

Amaranthus brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian amaranth, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy inflorescences that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces numerous small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Amaranthus brasiliensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
