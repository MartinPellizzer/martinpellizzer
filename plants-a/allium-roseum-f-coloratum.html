Allium roseum f. coloratum

Allium roseum f. coloratum, commonly known as the purple onion.

This plant is a cultivar of the common onion, characterized by its striking purple to violet-colored bulbs. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The foliage consists of long, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered white or pink flowers in the summer.

It is often grown for its ornamental value as well as for culinary use.
