Aloe latifolia

Aloe latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often ribbed and covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves.

Aloe latifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses in traditional practices.
