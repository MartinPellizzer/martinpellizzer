Alpinia aquatica

Alpinia aquatica, commonly known as water arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in wetland environments and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its rhizomes are edible and have a starchy, sweet flavor similar to taro.

Alpinia aquatica is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in various cultures.
