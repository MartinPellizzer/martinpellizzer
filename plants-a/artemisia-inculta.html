Artemisia inculta

Artemisia inculta, commonly known as rough sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture and a strong, pungent scent. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Artemisia inculta is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
