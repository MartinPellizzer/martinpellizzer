Abies excelsa var. altaica

Abies excelsa var. altaica, commonly known as the Siberian firs, is a variant of the white fir native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

This evergreen conifer is characterized by its tall, slender form and dense, dark green needles. It thrives in cold climates and is often found at high elevations where it forms part of the alpine vegetation. The cones are typically pendulous and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptation to harsh environments and its role in forest ecosystems.
