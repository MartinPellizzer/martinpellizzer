Adiantum owenii

Adiantum owenii, commonly known as the Owen's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, bi-lobed fronds that are typically dark green with a metallic sheen. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and release spores for propagation.

Adiantum owenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
