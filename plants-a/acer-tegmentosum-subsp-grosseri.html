Acer tegmentosum subsp. grosseri

Acer tegmentosum subsp.

grosseri, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia. It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that change color with the seasons, typically displaying vibrant red, orange, and yellow hues in autumn.

The plant has a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental species in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ecological and horticultural landscapes.
