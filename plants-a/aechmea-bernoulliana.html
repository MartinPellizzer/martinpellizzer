Aechmea bernoulliana

Aechmea bernoulliana, commonly known as the Bernoulli's bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aechmea bernoulliana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and unique foliage structure.
