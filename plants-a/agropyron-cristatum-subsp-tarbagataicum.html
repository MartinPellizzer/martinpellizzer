Agropyron cristatum subsp. tarbagataicum

Agropyron cristatum subsp.

tarbagataicum, commonly known as the Tarbagatai needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for grazing animals.
