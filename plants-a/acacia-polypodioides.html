Acacia polypodioides

Acacia polypodioides, commonly known as the fern-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are compound and pinnate. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Acacia polypodioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
