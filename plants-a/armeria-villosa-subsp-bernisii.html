Armeria villosa subsp. bernisii

Armeria villosa subsp.

bernisii, commonly known as the woolly thrift, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Europe and northern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
