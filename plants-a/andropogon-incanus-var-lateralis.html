Andropogon incanus var. lateralis

Andropogon incanus var. lateralis, commonly known as the silky grass or silky bluestem, is a variant of the silky grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

This grass is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
