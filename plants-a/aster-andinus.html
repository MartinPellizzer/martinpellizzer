Aster andinus

Aster andinus, commonly known as the Andean aster, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Aster andinus thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
