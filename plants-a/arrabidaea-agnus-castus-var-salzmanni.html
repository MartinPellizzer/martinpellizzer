Arrabidaea agnus-castus var. salzmanni

Arrabidaea agnus-castus var. salzmanni, commonly known as the Salzmann's chaste tree, is a variant of the chaste tree native to southern Africa.

It is a small, deciduous shrub characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to Mediterranean and subtropical climates.
