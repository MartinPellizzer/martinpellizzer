Adesmia villanuevae

Adesmia villanuevae, commonly known as the Mexican velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Adesmia villanuevae is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, which can make it an important plant in certain ecological contexts.
