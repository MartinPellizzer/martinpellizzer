Abies virescens

Abies virescens, commonly known as the fir tree, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, yellowish cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing seeds surrounded by a papery wing. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Abies virescens plays an important role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
