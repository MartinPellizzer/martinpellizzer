Agrostis alba subsp. coarctata

Agrostis alba subsp.

coarctata, commonly known as the narrowleaf meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and wetlands.

The plant has slender, upright culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense panicle, bearing small, two-lobed spikelets.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
