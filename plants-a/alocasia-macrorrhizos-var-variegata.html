Alocasia macrorrhizos var. variegata

Alocasia macrorrhizos var. variegata, commonly known as the Variegated Giant Taro, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern of green, white, and cream.

Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in warm, humid environments with high light exposure. The plant's rhizomes are edible when cooked, though they contain calcium oxalate crystals that can cause irritation. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its dramatic foliage.

Due to its size and specific care requirements, it is best suited for greenhouse or indoor cultivation.
