Anemia eximia

Anemia eximia, commonly known as the golden groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southern United States.

It produces small, yellow, cherry-like fruits that are encased in a papery husk. The plant features slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, sharing characteristics with other members of the morning glory family.
