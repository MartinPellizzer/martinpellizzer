Artocarpus brunneifolius

Artocarpus brunneifolius, commonly known as the brown-leaf breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its rough, brownish leaves and large, spherical fruits. The fruit, which is typically harvested when mature, has a fibrous outer husk and a starchy, edible pulp inside. It is often used in traditional cuisine and has various medicinal applications in local cultures.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood.
