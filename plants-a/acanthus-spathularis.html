Acanthus spathularis

Acanthus spathularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acanthus, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces spires of purple to pink flowers in late summer, which are borne on tall, leafless stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its architectural foliage and long-lasting blooms.
