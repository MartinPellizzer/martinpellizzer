Asplenium neolaserpitiifolium

Asplenium neolaserpitiifolium, commonly known as the New Zealand maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Asplenium neolaserpitiifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
