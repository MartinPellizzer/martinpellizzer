Anchusa officinalis var. brachyantha

Anchusa officinalis var. brachyantha, commonly known as narrow-leaved blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of striking blue to violet flowers. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
