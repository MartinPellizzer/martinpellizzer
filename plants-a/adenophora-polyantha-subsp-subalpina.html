Adenophora polyantha subsp. subalpina

Adenophora polyantha subsp.

subalpina, commonly known as the alpine adenophora, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils at elevations above 2,000 meters. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a bluish-green hue, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with high-altitude conditions.
