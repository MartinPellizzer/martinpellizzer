Avena fatua subsp. aemulans

Avena fatua subsp.

aemulans, commonly known as wild oat, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and long, narrow leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces spikelets that contain small, wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its invasive potential in agricultural and natural areas. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open habitats.

Due to its rapid growth and competitive nature, it is considered a weed in many regions.
