Achillea szowitzii

Achillea szowitzii, commonly known as the Szóczák yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Basin.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and flat, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, preferring well-drained conditions. It produces small white to pale yellow flowers that bloom from June to August, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
