Allantodia strigosa

Allantodia strigosa, commonly known as the rough allantodia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features erect, branching stems covered in short, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with five petals. Its leaves are linear to lance-shaped and have a hairy surface.

Allantodia strigosa is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
