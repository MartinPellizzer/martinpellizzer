Alchemilla montana

Alchemilla montana, commonly known as mountain lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Alchemilla montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
