Astragalus bakuensis

Astragalus bakuensis, commonly known as the Baku milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing adaptability to arid environments. It has been studied for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed.

Its ecological role includes supporting pollinators and contributing to soil stability in its native range.
