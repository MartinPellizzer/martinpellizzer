Anthericum volkii

Anthericum volkii, commonly known as Volk's lily, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
