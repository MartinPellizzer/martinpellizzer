Alsophila grevilleana

Alsophila grevilleana, commonly known as the Greville's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a smooth, leathery texture.

Alsophila grevilleana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
