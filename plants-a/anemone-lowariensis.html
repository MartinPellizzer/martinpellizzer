Anemone lowariensis

Anemone lowariensis, commonly known as the Kashmir anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It features showy, single or double flowers with petal-like sepals that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or mountain meadows. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a velvety texture.

Anemone lowariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, spring-blooming flowers.
