Andropogon saccharoides

Andropogon saccharoides, commonly known as sugar cane grass, is a tall perennial grass native to the warm climates of the Americas.

It is characterized by its robust, fibrous stalks and long, narrow leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant is closely related to sugarcane and is often found in wetlands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lipped flowers.

Andropogon saccharoides plays a role in ecological systems as a source of forage and habitat for various wildlife species.
