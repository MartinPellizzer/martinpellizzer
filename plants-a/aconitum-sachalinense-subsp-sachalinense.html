Aconitum sachalinense subsp. sachalinense

Aconitum sachalinense subsp.

sachalinense, commonly known as the Sakhalin aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cooler regions of East Asia. It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August.

The plant has a robust root system and grows up to 1.5 meters tall, with large, deeply lobed leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
