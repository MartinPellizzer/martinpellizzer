Acarna ovata

Acarna ovata, commonly known as the ovalleaf acarna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along riverbanks. Its flowers are often brightly colored, ranging from pink to purple, attracting various pollinators.

Acarna ovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
