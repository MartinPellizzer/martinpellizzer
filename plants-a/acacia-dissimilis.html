Acacia dissimilis

Acacia dissimilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Acacia dissimilis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
