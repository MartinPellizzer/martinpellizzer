Astragalus alopecurus var. maximus

Astragalus alopecurus var. maximus, commonly known as the greater hairy vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

This species is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
