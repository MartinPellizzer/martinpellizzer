Adiantum monotis

Adiantum monotis, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that are typically dark green with a bluish tinge. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its fronds are biennial, with new growth emerging from the base each year.

Adiantum monotis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
