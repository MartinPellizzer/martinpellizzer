Acacia retinervis

Acacia retinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are finely divided and give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia retinervis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities in landscaping.
