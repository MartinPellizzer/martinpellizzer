Andropogon violascens

Andropogon violascens, commonly known as purple bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and habitat for native wildlife.
