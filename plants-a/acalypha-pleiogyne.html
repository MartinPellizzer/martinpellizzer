Acalypha pleiogyne

Acalypha pleiogyne, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a metallic sheen. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Acalypha pleiogyne is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
