Anthericum hyacinthoides

Anthericum hyacinthoides, commonly known as the purple nettle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking purple or violet bracts that surround the spadix. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, greenish-white flowers within the spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
