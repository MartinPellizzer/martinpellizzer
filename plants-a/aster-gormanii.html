Aster gormanii

Aster gormanii, commonly known as Gorman's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Gorman's aster is often found in high elevation areas and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
