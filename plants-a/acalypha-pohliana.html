Acalypha pohliana

Acalypha pohliana, commonly known as the red-leaved acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple foliage, which contrasts with its green stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Acalypha pohliana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant leaf color.
