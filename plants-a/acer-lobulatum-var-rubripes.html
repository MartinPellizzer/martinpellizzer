Acer lobulatum var. rubripes

Acer lobulatum var. rubripes, commonly known as the red-leaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which becomes more vibrant in the fall. The leaves are palmately lobed with a serrated margin and a bronze underside. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring followed by winged seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
