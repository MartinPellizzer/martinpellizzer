Astragalus bartinense

Astragalus bartinense, commonly known as Bartin milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus bartinense is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
