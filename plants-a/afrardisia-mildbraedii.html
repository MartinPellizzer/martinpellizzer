Afrardisia mildbraedii

Afrardisia mildbraedii, commonly known as the Cape mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and conservation efforts.
