Anthurium cordulatum

Anthurium cordulatum, commonly known as the heartleaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often adorned with silver or white veins. The plant produces striking spadix inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium cordulatum is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
