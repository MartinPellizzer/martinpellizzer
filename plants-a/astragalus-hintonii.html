Astragalus hintonii

Astragalus hintonii, commonly known as Hinton's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus hintonii is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing plants.
