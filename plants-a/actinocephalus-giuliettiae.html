Actinocephalus giuliettiae

Actinocephalus giuliettiae, commonly known as the Juliette's star cactus, is a unique and rare species native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped spines and fleshy, segmented stems. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, exhibiting a slow growth rate.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and ecological significance.
