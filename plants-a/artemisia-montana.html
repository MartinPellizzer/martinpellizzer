Artemisia montana

Artemisia montana, commonly known as mountain wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant contains essential oils and bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine and herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is also used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
