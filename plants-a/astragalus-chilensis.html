Astragalus chilensis

Astragalus chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its deep root system.

Astragalus chilensis is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
