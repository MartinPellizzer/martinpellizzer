Actinea lanata

Actinea lanata, commonly known as the woolly actinia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves that are covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Actinea lanata thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
