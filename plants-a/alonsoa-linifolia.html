Alonsoa linifolia

Alonsoa linifolia, commonly known as silverleaf alonsoa, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping purposes.
