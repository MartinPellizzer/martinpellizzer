Avenula bromoides subsp. arundana

Avenula bromoides subsp.

arundana, commonly known as the yellow false oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale yellow to greenish flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to arid environments and contribute to its role in local ecosystems.
