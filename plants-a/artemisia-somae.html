Artemisia somae

Artemisia somae, commonly known as the mountain wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau and adjacent regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish flower heads. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves contain volatile oils and are used in traditional medicine for their purported antiseptic and digestive properties.

Artemisia somae plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitats and is of interest to botanists and pharmacologists alike.
