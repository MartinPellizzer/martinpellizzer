Aechmea mertensii

Aechmea mertensii, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces vibrant, spathiflorous inflorescences with showy, colorful bracts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Aechmea mertensii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea mertensii has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, sword-like, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a waxy, dark green color and a sharp, pointed tip.

The plant lacks a true stem, and its base is characterized by a thick, fibrous sheath that resembles bark, which is typically dark brown and has a leathery texture. Its flowers are clustered in a spike at the center of the rosette, with brightly colored bracts that are often red or orange, and the actual flowers are small and white, blooming during the summer months; they are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by animals that eat the fruit.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the surrounding environment.
