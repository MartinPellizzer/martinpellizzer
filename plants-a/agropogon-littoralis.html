Agropogon littoralis

Agropogon littoralis, commonly known as the salt meadow cordgrass, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in saline and brackish wetlands, often found in marshes and tidal flats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Agropogon littoralis is well adapted to withstand saltwater inundation and fluctuating water levels.
