Acidocroton lobulatus

Acidocroton lobulatus, commonly known as the dwarf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
