Achillea occidentalis

Achillea occidentalis, commonly known as western yarrow, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features basal rosettes of linear, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for garden use.

The species is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
