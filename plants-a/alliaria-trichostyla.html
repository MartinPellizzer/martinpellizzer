Alliaria trichostyla

Alliaria trichostyla, commonly known as the lesser garlic or winter garlic, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender, hairy stems and small, rounded leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the mustard family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Alliaria trichostyla is known for its pungent odor, similar to that of garlic, and has been used historically for various purposes.
