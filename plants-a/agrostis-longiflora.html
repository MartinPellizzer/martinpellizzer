Agrostis longiflora

Agrostis longiflora, commonly known as sweet grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to yellowish flowers. Sweet grass is often found in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in various cultural and ceremonial practices.
