Anthemis palaestina

Anthemis palaestina, commonly known as the Palestine fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of Israel, Palestine, and Jordan.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are generally hairy and gray-green in color.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
