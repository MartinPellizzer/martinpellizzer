Alyssoides cretica

Alyssoides cretica, commonly known as the Crete dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Alyssoides cretica is considered a hardy perennial, capable of surviving in harsh environmental conditions.
