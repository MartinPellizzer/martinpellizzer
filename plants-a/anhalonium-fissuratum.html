Anhalonium fissuratum

Anhalonium fissuratum, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, adapting to extreme drought conditions. The plant produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, helping to store water and reduce transpiration.

The plant's resilience to harsh environments makes it an important species in desert ecosystems.
