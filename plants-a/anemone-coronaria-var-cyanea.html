Anemone coronaria var. cyanea

Anemone coronaria var. cyanea, commonly known as the blue anemone, is a striking perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, often with a white or pale blue center. The plant has basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are upright and often branched. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
