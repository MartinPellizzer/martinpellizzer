Atriplex patula var. alaskensis

Atriplex patula var. alaskensis, commonly known as Alaska saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, making it well adapted to harsh, saline environments. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert and coastal habitats.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
