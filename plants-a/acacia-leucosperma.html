Acacia leucosperma

Acacia leucosperma, commonly known as the white-flowered acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are feathery in appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia leucosperma is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological importance as a source of food and shelter for various animals.
