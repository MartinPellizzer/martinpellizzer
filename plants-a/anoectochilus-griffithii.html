Anoectochilus griffithii

Anoectochilus griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, dark green leaves and produces striking violet or purple flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It has a short rhizome and is often found in association with mosses and lichens.

Anoectochilus griffithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
