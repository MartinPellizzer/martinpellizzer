Astragalus cicadae

Astragalus cicadae, commonly known as the cicada milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus cicadae is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
