Ammannia incarnata

Ammannia incarnata, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, typically from summer to fall. Its roots are reddish in color, which gives it its common name.

Ammannia incarnata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
