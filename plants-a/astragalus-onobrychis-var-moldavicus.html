Astragalus onobrychis var. moldavicus

Astragalus onobrychis var. moldavicus, commonly known as the Moldavian sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant is typically found in dry, calcareous soils and thrives in temperate climates.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop due to its nutritional value.
