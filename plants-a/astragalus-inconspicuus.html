Astragalus inconspicuus

Astragalus inconspicuus, commonly known as the inconspicuous milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus inconspicuus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local insect populations.
