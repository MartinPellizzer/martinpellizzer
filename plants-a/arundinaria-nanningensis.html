Arundinaria nanningensis

Arundinaria nanningensis, commonly known as the Nanning bamboo, is a rare and endemic species native to southern China.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and finely divided leaves. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and streamside areas. Its growth habit is clump-forming, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.
