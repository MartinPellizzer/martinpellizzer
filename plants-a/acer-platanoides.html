Acer platanoides

Acer platanoides, commonly known as the Norway maple, is a deciduous tree native to central and northern Europe.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which are typically bright green in summer and turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in urban environments due to its adaptability.

However, it is considered invasive in some regions, outcompeting native species.
