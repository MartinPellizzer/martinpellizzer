Alopecurus ramosus

Alopecurus ramosus, commonly known as hairy bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, often mat-forming foliage. The plant has slender, wiry stems that produce clusters of small, spikelet-like flowers, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and have a bluish-green color.

Alopecurus ramosus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.
