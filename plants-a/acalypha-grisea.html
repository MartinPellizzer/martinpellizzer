Acalypha grisea

Acalypha grisea, commonly known as grayleaf spurflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Acalypha grisea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
