Artocarpus pomiformis

Artocarpus pomiformis, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and bears fruit that resemble bread, hence its common name. The fruit is a composite structure composed of a fleshy outer rind and a central core containing multiple seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries.
