Achillea millefolium var. sylvatica

Achillea millefolium var. sylvatica, commonly known as the woodland yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and grayish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in woodland edges and meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
