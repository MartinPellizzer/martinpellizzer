Aeschynanthus gracilis

Aeschynanthus gracilis, commonly known as the slender waxflower, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular, brightly colored flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets.

Aeschynanthus gracilis is prized for its vibrant floral display and is relatively easy to care for with proper light and watering conditions.
