Alnus incana subsp. hirsuta

Alnus incana subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy alder, is a shrub or small tree native to the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its finely hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers.

The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils near water sources such as streams and wetlands. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation through its association with root nodules.

The species is valued for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
