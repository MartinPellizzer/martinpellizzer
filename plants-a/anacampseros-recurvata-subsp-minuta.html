Anacampseros recurvata subsp. minuta

Anacampseros recurvata subsp.

minuta, commonly known as the dwarf stonecress, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features small, fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge.

The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a rock garden plant.
