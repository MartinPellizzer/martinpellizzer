Aloe somaliensis var. marmota

Aloe somaliensis var. marmota, commonly known as the Somali aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Somalia.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, often adorned with white or yellowish tubercles. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is well adapted to dry climates and requires minimal water, making it a hardy choice for xerophytic gardens.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a desirable addition to arid landscapes.
