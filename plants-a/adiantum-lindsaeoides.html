Adiantum lindsaeoides

Adiantum lindsaeoides, commonly known as the Mexican maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble feathers. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its reproductive structures include sori, which are clusters of sporangia located on the underside of the fronds.

Adiantum lindsaeoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
