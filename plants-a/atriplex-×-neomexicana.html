Atriplex × neomexicana

Atriplex × neomexicana, commonly known as the desert saltbush, is a hybrid shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert and semi-arid regions.

Atriplex × neomexicana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
