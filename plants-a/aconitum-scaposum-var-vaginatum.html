Aconitum scaposum var. vaginatum

Aconitum scaposum var. vaginatum, commonly known as the Alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple hooded flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive basal rosette and produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and toxic properties, it is often cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
