Acalypha communis var. hirtiformis

Acalypha communis var. hirtiformis, commonly known as the hairy ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
