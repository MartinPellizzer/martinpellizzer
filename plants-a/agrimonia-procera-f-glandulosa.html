Agrimonia procera f. glandulosa

Agrimonia procera f. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular agrimony, is a variant of the common agrimony.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and serrated leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its stems are often covered with glandular hairs, giving it its specific epithet.

This plant is native to Europe and is often found in dry meadows and rocky soils.
