Astragalus delicatulus

Astragalus delicatulus, commonly known as the delicate milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
