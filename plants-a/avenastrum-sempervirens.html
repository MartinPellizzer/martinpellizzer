Avenastrum sempervirens

Avenastrum sempervirens, commonly known as everlasting grass, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that remain green throughout the year. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Avenastrum sempervirens is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid climates.
