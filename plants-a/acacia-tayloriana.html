Acacia tayloriana

Acacia tayloriana, commonly known as the Taylor's wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, yellowish-green stems and small, feathery leaves that are often reduced to needle-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia tayloriana is also noted for its role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
