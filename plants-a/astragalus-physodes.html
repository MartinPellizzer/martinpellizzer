Astragalus physodes

Astragalus physodes, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Astragalus physodes has small, yellow to reddish flowers that bloom in early summer, and its root system is well-developed for nutrient absorption.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
