Allium phariense

Allium phariense, commonly known as the Phari onion, is a perennial plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a valuable crop in local cuisines.

Allium phariense thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
