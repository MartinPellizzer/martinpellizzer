Alpinia purpurata var. anomala

Alpinia purpurata var. anomala, commonly known as the red ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, deep red to purple inflorescences that appear in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and aromatic. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Alpinia purpurata var. anomala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
