Ampelopsis gongshanensis

Ampelopsis gongshanensis, commonly known as the Gongshan bittersweet, is a climbing vine native to China.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
