Acacia longifolia f. elongata

Acacia longifolia f. elongata, commonly known as the coastal acacia or narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. Its growth habit is typically upright, with a dense, bushy form.

It is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
