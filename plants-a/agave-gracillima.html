Agave gracillima

Agave gracillima, commonly known as the slender agave, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette of narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with yellowish-green flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Agave gracillima is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance needs.
