Aster triangularis

Aster triangularis, commonly known as the triangular aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, triangular-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Aster triangularis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
