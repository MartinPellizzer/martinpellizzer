Androscepia mutica

Androscepia mutica, commonly known as the roughleaf androscepia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has slender, upright stems covered in fine, rough hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a distinctive rough texture.

Androscepia mutica is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions.
