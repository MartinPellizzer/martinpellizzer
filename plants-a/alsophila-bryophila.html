Alsophila bryophila

Alsophila bryophila, commonly known as the dwarf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and a crown of feathery, light green fronds. The plant has a unique structure with a thick, fibrous root system that helps it anchor in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are dimorphic, with sterile and fertile leaves, and it reproduces via spores rather than seeds.

Alsophila bryophila thrives in humid, temperate climates and is often found in forest understories.
