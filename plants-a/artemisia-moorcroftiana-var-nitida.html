Artemisia moorcroftiana var. nitida

Artemisia moorcroftiana var. nitida, commonly known as the silver mugwort, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in narrow, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
