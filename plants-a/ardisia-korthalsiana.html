Ardisia korthalsiana

Ardisia korthalsiana, commonly known as the Christmas berry, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, red berries that resemble Christmas ornaments, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the family Ericaceae and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The berries are edible but often have a tart flavor, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
