Aethionema saxatile subsp. creticum

Aethionema saxatile subsp.

creticum, commonly known as the Cretan rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the rocky soils of Crete. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to April.

The plant has compact, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and hardy addition to Mediterranean gardens.
