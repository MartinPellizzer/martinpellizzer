Acacia kirkii

Acacia kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's acacia, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its straight, slender trunk and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia kirkii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species found in savannas and rocky areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
