Anisopappus chinensis var. rupestris

Anisopappus chinensis var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of China.

It features a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single terminal head, which is surrounded by several layers of bracts. It is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, demonstrating drought tolerance and xerophytic characteristics.

Its medicinal properties have been traditionally utilized in Chinese herbal medicine.
