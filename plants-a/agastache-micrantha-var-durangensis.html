Agastache micrantha var. durangensis

Agastache micrantha var. durangensis, commonly known as the Durango hyssop, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple to blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a minty fragrance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.
