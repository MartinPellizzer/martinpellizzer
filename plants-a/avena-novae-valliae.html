Avena novae-valliae

Avena novae-valliae, commonly known as the New Valley oat, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is adapted to grow in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental or for erosion control in suitable climates.
