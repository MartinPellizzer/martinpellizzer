Astragalus kutepovii

Astragalus kutepovii, commonly known as the Kutepov's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus kutepovii is also noted for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
