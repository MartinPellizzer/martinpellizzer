Astragalus dickorei

Astragalus dickorei, commonly known as the Tibetan milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet and adjacent areas of China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Astragalus dickorei is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
