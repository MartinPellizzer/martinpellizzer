Aster yakushimensis

Aster yakushimensis, commonly known as the Yakushimen aster, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowers are usually blue to violet in color, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Aster yakushimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
