Arachis hoehnei

Arachis hoehnei, commonly known as the Brazilian peanut or Hoehne's peanut, is a species of legume native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds similar to common peanuts. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its potential nutritional value, it is of interest for agricultural and horticultural research.
