Asteropyrum peltatum

Asteropyrum peltatum, commonly known as the broadleaf aster, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Asteropyrum peltatum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
