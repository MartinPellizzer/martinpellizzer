Azalea rubra

Azalea rubra, commonly known as the red azalea, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is dark green and evergreen, providing year-round interest in the landscape.

Azalea rubra is prized for its vibrant red blooms and is often used in ornamental gardening.
