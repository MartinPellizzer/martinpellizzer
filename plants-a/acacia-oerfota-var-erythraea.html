Acacia oerfota var. erythraea

Acacia oerfota var. erythraea, commonly known as the Red Acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched shrub form and produces small, yellow, globular flowers. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and it is adapted to arid environments. It is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.

The species is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
