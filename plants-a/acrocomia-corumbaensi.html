Acrocomia corumbaensi

Acrocomia corumbaensi, commonly known as the Corumbá palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a tall, slender tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, with pinnate leaves that are long and feather-like. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by clustered, spiny fruits. Its trunk is robust and often used in traditional construction and crafts.

The Corumbá palm is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and practical uses.
