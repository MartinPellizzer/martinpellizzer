Amsinckia vestita

Amsinckia vestita, commonly known as the Desert Evening Primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial with hairy, glandular stems and heart-shaped, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Amsinckia vestita is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
