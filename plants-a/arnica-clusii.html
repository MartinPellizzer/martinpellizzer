Arnica clusii

Arnica clusii, commonly known as the alpine tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features rough, hairy leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. Arnica clusii is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments.

It has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
