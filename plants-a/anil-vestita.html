Anil vestita

Anil vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Anil vestita is known for its rapid growth and ability to become invasive in certain ecosystems.
