Amorphophallus planus

Amorphophallus planus, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its large, showy spathe and spadix inflorescence. The plant produces a tuberous root system that is used as a starchy food source. Its leaves are typically large and heart-shaped, with a waxy surface.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and edible tubers.
