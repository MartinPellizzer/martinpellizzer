Astragalus djadjerudensis

Astragalus djadjerudensis, commonly known as the Djadjerud's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for local wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
