Adicea vulcanica

Adicea vulcanica, commonly known as the fire vine, is a unique flowering plant native to volcanic regions.

It thrives in nutrient-rich, mineral-laden soils found near active or dormant volcanoes. The plant features striking red and orange tubular flowers that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are thick and waxy, helping to retain moisture in its harsh environment.

Adicea vulcanica plays a vital role in stabilizing volcanic soil and supporting local biodiversity.
