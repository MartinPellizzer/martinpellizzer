Artemisia barrelieri

Artemisia barrelieri, commonly known as African wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other aromatic herbs.

Artemisia barrelieri is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
