Aira glabrata

Aira glabrata, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
