Agrimonia pilosa f. bracteata

Agrimonia pilosa f. bracteata, commonly known as the bracted agrimonia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive seed capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and native garden cultivation.
