Achillea palmeri

Achillea palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads and feathery leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and drought-tolerant species often used in xeriscaping.
