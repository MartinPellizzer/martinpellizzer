Anthemis bulgardaghica

Anthemis bulgardaghica, commonly known as the Armenian tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in the summer, and it has a distinctive aromatic foliage.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
