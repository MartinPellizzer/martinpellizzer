Achillea millefolium var. villosum

Achillea millefolium var. villosum, commonly known as hairy yarrow, is a variant of the common yarrow plant.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces flat, composite flower heads composed of numerous small white to pinkish flowers, which bloom in summer. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This variety is often used in herbal medicine and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
