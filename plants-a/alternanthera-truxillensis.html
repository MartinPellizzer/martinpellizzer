Alternanthera truxillensis

Alternanthera truxillensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red and pink inflorescences. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
