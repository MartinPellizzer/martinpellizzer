Aristida batangensis

Aristida batangensis, commonly known as the Batang grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically arranged in a panicle structure. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
