Anthemis cretica var. mariae

Anthemis cretica var. mariae, commonly known as Mary's tansy, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, yellow-orange flowers and finely divided, gray-green leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its antispasmodic and digestive benefits.

The specific variety, mariae, is distinguished by its unique floral structure and slightly different growth habits compared to the standard Anthemis cretica.
