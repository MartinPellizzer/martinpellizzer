Asplenium schwackei

Asplenium schwackei, commonly known as the Schwacke's spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic or lithophytic fern that typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy, with a serrated margin. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Asplenium schwackei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
