Arundina pentandra

Arundina pentandra, commonly known as the bamboo orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, bamboo-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale green flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Arundina pentandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
