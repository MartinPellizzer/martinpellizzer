Arenaria yunus-emrei

Arenaria yunus-emrei, commonly known as the Yunus Emrei sandflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
