Aconitella intrincata

Aconitella intrincata, commonly known as the intricate aconite, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
