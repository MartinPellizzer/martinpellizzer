Adenia smilacina

Adenia smilacina, commonly known as the climbing adenia, is a vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright orange berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and unique fruit make it a notable species in botanical gardens.
