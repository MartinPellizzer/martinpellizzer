Acer pycnanthum

Acer pycnanthum, commonly known as the red maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage in the fall, which makes it a popular ornamental plant. The tree has opposite, palmate leaves that are typically 5-9 lobes, and it produces small, red samaras as its seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained conditions.

Acer pycnanthum is also valued for its ecological role, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
