Astragalus idrietorum

Astragalus idrietorum, commonly known as the Idrija milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Astragalus idrietorum is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
