Atelophragma tolucanum

Atelophragma tolucanum, commonly known as the Tolucan palm, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into edible berries. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
