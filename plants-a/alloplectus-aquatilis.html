Alloplectus aquatilis

Alloplectus aquatilis, commonly known as the water spiker, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its elongated, spiky leaves that extend from a central stem.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant's leaves are often submerged, while the upper portion may emerge above the water surface. Alloplectus aquatilis plays a role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological function make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
