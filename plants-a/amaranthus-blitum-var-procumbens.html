Amaranthus blitum var. procumbens

Amaranthus blitum var. procumbens, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with branches lying close to the ground. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough, sandpapery texture, and the stems are reddish in color.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
