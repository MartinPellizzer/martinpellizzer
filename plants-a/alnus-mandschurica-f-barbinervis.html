Alnus mandschurica f. barbinervis

Alnus mandschurica f. barbinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined Manchurian alder, is a variant of the Manchurian alder, characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Russia. The leaves are typically narrower and have a more pronounced venation pattern compared to the typical form. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, such as nitrogen fixation.
