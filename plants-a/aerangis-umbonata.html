Aerangis umbonata

Aerangis umbonata, commonly known as the Umbonate Aerangis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fleshy leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom during the warmer months, often in late spring to early summer.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
