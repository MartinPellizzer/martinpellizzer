Adenocarpus argyrophyllus

Adenocarpus argyrophyllus, commonly known as silver-leaf burr grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its erect, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical research.
