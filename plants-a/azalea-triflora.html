Azalea triflora

Azalea triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its clusters of small, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Azalea triflora prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant floral display and its role in supporting local pollinators.
