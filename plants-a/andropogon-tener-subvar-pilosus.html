Andropogon tener subvar. pilosus

Andropogon tener subvar. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fine, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to saturated soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of small, spike-like flowers.

Andropogon tener subvar. pilosus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
