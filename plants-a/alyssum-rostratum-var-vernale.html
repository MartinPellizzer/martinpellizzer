Alyssum rostratum var. vernale

Alyssum rostratum var. vernale, commonly known as the spring alyssum, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and a mat-forming growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
