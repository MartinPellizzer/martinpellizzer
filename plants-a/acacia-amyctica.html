Acacia amyctica

Acacia amyctica, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, twisted branches and small, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spherical or cylindrical heads. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod, and the plant is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils.

Acacia amyctica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
