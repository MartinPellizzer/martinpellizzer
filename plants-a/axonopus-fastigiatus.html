Axonopus fastigiatus

Axonopus fastigiatus, commonly known as velvetgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and dark green, velvety foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lawns, gardens, and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in various parts of the world for landscaping purposes.
