Asparagus alopecurus

Asparagus alopecurus, commonly known as meadow asparagus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks. The plant features thin, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or greenish flowers in clusters. Its root system consists of fibrous roots that help it anchor in wet soil.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
