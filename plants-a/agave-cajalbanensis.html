Agave cajalbanensis

Agave cajalbanensis, commonly known as the Sierra Madre agave, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears white to pale yellow flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Agave cajalbanensis is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
