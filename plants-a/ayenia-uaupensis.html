Ayenia uaupensis

Ayenia uaupensis, commonly known as the Brazilian erythroxylum, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Ayenia uaupensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
