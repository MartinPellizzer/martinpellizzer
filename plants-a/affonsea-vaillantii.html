Affonsea vaillantii

Affonsea vaillantii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often brightly colored, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
