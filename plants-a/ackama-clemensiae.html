Ackama clemensiae

Ackama clemensiae, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand strong mountain winds.

Ackama clemensiae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
