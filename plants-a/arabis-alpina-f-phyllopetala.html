Arabis alpina f. phyllopetala

Arabis alpina f. phyllopetala, commonly known as the Alpine rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in racemes, typical of the family.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh, rocky environments and is often found in high-altitude meadows and scree slopes.
