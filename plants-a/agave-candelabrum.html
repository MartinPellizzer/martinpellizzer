Agave candelabrum

Agave candelabrum, commonly known as the candleplant, is a striking succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, blue-gray leaves that are often lined with white teeth, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces an impressive flowering stalk that can reach several meters in height, topped with clusters of yellow-green flowers. It is well adapted to dry conditions, storing water in its fleshy leaves and stems.

Due to its unique look and hardiness, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
