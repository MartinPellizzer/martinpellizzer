Asplenium concinnum var. schimperi

Asplenium concinnum var. schimperi, commonly known as the African maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of small, dark green leaves.

The fronds are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive appearance that makes it popular in horticulture.
