Artemisia pewzowi

Artemisia pewzowi, commonly known as the Tibetan wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in composite inflorescences, typical of the sunflower family. Artemisia pewzowi is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

It has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
