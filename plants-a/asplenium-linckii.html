Asplenium linckii

Asplenium linckii, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often have a glossy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are wiry and help it spread through vegetative propagation.

Asplenium linckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
