Acinodendron riparium

Acinodendron riparium, commonly known as the riverbank acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, adapting well to aquatic environments. Its root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
