Aconitum bhutanicum

Aconitum bhutanicum, commonly known as the Bhutanese monk's hood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the high altitudes of the Eastern Himalayas, including Bhutan, Nepal, and northern India. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that resemble a hood, with spurs extending from the petals. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

Aconitum bhutanicum contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant of both botanical interest and caution.
