Aster maccallae

Aster maccallae, commonly known as the MacCalla's aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features showy purple to pink ray flowers surrounding yellow disk flowers. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Aster maccallae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
