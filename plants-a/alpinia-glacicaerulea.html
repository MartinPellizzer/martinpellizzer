Alpinia glacicaerulea

Alpinia glacicaerulea, commonly known as the Blue Glacier Ginger, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and are highly ornamental. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its visual appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
