Astragalus akscheherensis

Astragalus akscheherensis, commonly known as the Aksu milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its deep root system.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
