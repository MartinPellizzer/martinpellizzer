Avenula pratensis var. mirandana

Avenula pratensis var. mirandana, commonly known as the Miranda meadow grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in loose clusters and bloom in early summer.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its habitat.
