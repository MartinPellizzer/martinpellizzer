Allium taquetii

Allium taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's onion, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers. The plant is characterized by its bulbous underground stem, which stores nutrients and water. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Allium taquetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
