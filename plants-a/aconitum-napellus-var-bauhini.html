Aconitum napellus var. bauhini

Aconitum napellus var. bauhini, commonly known as the Baur's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern, and it typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
