Aloe pertusa

Aloe pertusa, commonly known as the upright aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, storing water to help it survive in arid conditions. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Aloe pertusa thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for xeriscaping.
