Artemisia zayuensis var. pienmaensis

Artemisia zayuensis var. pienmaensis, commonly known as the Yarlung Tsangpo Artemisia, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
