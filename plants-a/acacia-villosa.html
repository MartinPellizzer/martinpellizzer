Acacia villosa

Acacia villosa, commonly known as the woolly acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, gray-green foliage with soft, silky hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its seeds are encased in hard pods and are dispersed by wind or animals.

Acacia villosa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in regions with low rainfall.
