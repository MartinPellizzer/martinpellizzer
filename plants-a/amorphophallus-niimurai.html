Amorphophallus niimurai

Amorphophallus niimurai, commonly known as the Japanese giant arrowhead, is a striking plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in diameter. The plant produces a tall, spathiflorous inflorescence that resembles a phallic shape, which is why it is also called the "Devil's tongue.". It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant's unique appearance and rapid growth make it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
