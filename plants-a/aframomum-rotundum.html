Aframomum rotundum

Aframomum rotundum, commonly known as African cardamom, is a tropical plant native to western Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, dark red rhizomes that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its rhizomes contain essential oils and compounds that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Aframomum rotundum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aromatic properties.
