Aira capillaris var. divaricata

Aira capillaris var. divaricata, commonly known as silver grass, is a variant of the common hairgrass species.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It produces delicate, feathery seed heads that are often used in floral arrangements.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
