Adenostyles viridis subsp. australis

Adenostyles viridis subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian greenstem, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

The plant features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and linear. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in spring.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.
