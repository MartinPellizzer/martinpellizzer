Artemisia gracilenta

Artemisia gracilenta, commonly known as slender wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, which bloom in late summer. Artemisia gracilenta is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
