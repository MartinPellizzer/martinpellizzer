Aubrieta kotschyi

Aubrieta kotschyi, commonly known as the Persian rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters and has basal leaves that are hairy and lobed. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and arid environments.

Aubrieta kotschyi is valued for its ornamental value and is cultivated in rock gardens and as a ground cover in suitable climates.
