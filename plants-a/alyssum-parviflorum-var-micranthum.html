Alyssum parviflorum var. micranthum

Alyssum parviflorum var. micranthum, commonly known as the dwarf alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring and early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and dark green, narrow leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
