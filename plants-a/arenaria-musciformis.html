Arenaria musciformis

Arenaria musciformis, commonly known as sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and coastal regions.

It features small, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Its unique appearance and ability to survive in harsh conditions make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
