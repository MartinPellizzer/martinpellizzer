Avena sativa var. nigra

Avena sativa var. nigra, commonly known as black oat, is a variety of the oat plant characterized by its dark-colored grains.

It is native to temperate regions and thrives in cool climates with well-drained soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, producing spikes of small, whitish flowers. Its seeds are rich in nutrients, including proteins, fiber, and minerals, making it a valuable crop.

The dark coloration of the grains is due to the presence of anthocyanins, which also contribute to its antioxidant properties.
