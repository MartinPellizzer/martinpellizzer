Adiantopsis chlorophylla f. siccanea

Adiantopsis chlorophylla f. siccanea, commonly known as the dryland maidenhair fern, is a rare fern species characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is known for its unique, leathery, and often curled leaves. This fern is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing a succulent-like appearance to conserve moisture. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive, feathery texture.

The plant is often found in rocky soils and is considered an indicator species for dry, shaded habitats.
