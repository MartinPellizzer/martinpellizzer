Acacia nigripilosa subsp. latifolia

Acacia nigripilosa subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves with broad, ovate leaflets.

The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that bloom during the dry season, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its wood is hard and durable, historically used by Indigenous Australians for tools and weapons.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader foliage compared to other acacia species.
