Astragalus mangeri

Astragalus mangeri, commonly known as the Manger's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry, rocky environments, showcasing features such as deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area. Astragalus mangeri is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional remedies.

Its ecological role includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil in its native habitat.
