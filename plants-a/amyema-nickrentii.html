Amyema nickrentii

Amyema nickrentii, commonly known as the Nickrent's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, fleshy berries. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat the berries, aiding in its propagation.

Amyema nickrentii plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for native wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of its host trees.
