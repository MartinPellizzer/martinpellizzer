Aster oldhamii

Aster oldhamii, commonly known as the Oldham's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces showy blue to purple flowers. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Aster oldhamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance to pollinators.
