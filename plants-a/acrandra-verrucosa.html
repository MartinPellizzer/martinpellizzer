Acrandra verrucosa

Acrandra verrucosa, commonly known as the rough acanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spiky bracts that resemble leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface, giving it its common name. It produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow.

Acrandra verrucosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
