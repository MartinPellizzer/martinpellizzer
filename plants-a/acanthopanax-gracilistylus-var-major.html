Acanthopanax gracilistylus var. major

Acanthopanax gracilistylus var. major, commonly known as the giant Chinese dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red berries that are edible for birds and mammals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
