Alternanthera nodifera

Alternanthera nodifera, commonly known as the red-veined stinkweed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that often display striking red or purple veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by three-winged, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its invasive potential, it is considered a weed in many areas outside its native range.
