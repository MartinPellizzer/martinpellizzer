Acalypha virginica var. intermedia

Acalypha virginica var. intermedia, commonly known as the woollyleaf ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, fuzzy, silver-green foliage that adds visual interest to gardens. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its unique appearance and ecological benefits make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
