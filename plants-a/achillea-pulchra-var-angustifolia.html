Achillea pulchra var. angustifolia

Achillea pulchra var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf yarrow, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and flat, clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has a taproot system and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
