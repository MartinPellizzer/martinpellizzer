Atropis carinata

Atropis carinata, commonly known as the ridge-leaf arrowhead, is a species of flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves with a distinct central ridge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in shallow water environments.

Atropis carinata is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
