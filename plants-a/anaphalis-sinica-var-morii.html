Anaphalis sinica var. morii

Anaphalis sinica var. morii, commonly known as the silverleaf frostflower, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
