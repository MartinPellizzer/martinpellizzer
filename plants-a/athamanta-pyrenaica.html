Athamanta pyrenaica

Athamanta pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a sturdy stem that supports its showy inflorescence.

Athamanta pyrenaica is often found in open, sunny habitats such as alpine meadows and forest edges.
