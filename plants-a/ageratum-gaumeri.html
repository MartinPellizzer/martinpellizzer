Ageratum gaumeri

Ageratum gaumeri, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, often with a grayish-green hue.

Ageratum gaumeri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens due to its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
