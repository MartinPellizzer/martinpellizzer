Acaena leptacantha f. inflata

Acaena leptacantha f. inflata, commonly known as the inflated acaena, is a variant of the species Acaena leptacantha, belonging to the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated bracts that form a cup-like structure around the seeds, which is an adaptation for seed dispersal. The plant is native to New Zealand and is typically found in coastal and subalpine regions. It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a mat-forming growth habit.

The inflated bracts are not only functional but also contribute to the plant's ornamental value.
