Aegle marmelos

Aegle marmelos, commonly known as the wood apple or stone apple, is a tropical plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a rough, fissured bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by round, green fruits with a thick, woody rind. The fruit contains a single large seed surrounded by a fleshy, acidic pulp.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in traditional culinary practices.

Morphology

Aegle marmelos has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are usually 5-10 cm long and 3-6 cm wide, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The stem is stout and erect, with a rough, greyish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age. The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in inflorescences; they bloom from April to June and are primarily pollinated by insects. The fruit is a rough, oblong, yellow or orange drupe that splits open to release three winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are deep and fibrous, helping the plant anchor in various soil types.
