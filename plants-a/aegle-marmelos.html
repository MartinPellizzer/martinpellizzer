Aegle marmelos

Aegle marmelos, commonly known as the wood apple or stone apple, is a tropical plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a rough, fissured bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by round, green fruits with a thick, woody rind. The fruit contains a single large seed surrounded by a fleshy, acidic pulp.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in traditional culinary practices.
