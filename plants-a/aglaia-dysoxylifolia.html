Aglaia dysoxylifolia

Aglaia dysoxylifolia, commonly known as the white aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures containing several seeds.

Aglaia dysoxylifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
