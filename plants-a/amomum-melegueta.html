Amomum melegueta

Amomum melegueta, commonly known as African cardamom, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to West Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into dark brown, three-lobed berries. The seeds inside the berries are the primary part used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its distinctive flavor and essential oils.
