Asarum nomadakense

Asarum nomadakense, commonly known as the nomadic asarum, is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique, complex floral structures. The plant produces a spathaceous bract that encloses the actual flower, which is often mistaken for the flower itself. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in mountainous regions of East Asia.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in both taxonomy and ecological adaptation.
