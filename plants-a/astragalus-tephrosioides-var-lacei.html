Astragalus tephrosioides var. lacei

Astragalus tephrosioides var. lacei, commonly known as the lacey milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to thrive in poor soils and has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

This species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
