Ambrosia artemisiifolia var. jamaicensis

Ambrosia artemisiifolia var. jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican ragweed, is a variant of the common ragweed species.

It is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean, characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are wind-pollinated, contributing to its allergenic potential. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in tropical and subtropical regions.
