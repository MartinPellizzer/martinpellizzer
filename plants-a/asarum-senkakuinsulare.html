Asarum senkakuinsulare

Asarum senkakuinsulare, commonly known as the Ryukyu asarum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, intricate flowers that resemble a pipe or a horn. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a strong, spicy odor, which attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
