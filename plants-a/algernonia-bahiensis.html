Algernonia bahiensis

Algernonia bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia orchid, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil, while its leaves are usually broad and glossy.

Algernonia bahiensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
