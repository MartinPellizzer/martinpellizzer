Allium bauerianum

Allium bauerianum, commonly known as the Bauer's onion, is a bulbous plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, edible bulbs that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are strap-shaped and form a dense rosette near the ground.

Allium bauerianum is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
