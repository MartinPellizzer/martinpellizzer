Acacia trachyphloia

Acacia trachyphloia, commonly known as the rough-barked acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive rough, fissured bark that gives it its scientific name. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which appear during the warmer months. Its leaves are phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves.

Acacia trachyphloia is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.
