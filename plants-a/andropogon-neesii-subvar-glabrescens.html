Andropogon neesii subvar. glabrescens

Andropogon neesii subvar. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairless, giving it its subvariety name "glabrescens.". Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family Poaceae.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
