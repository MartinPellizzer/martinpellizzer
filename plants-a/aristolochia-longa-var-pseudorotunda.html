Aristolochia longa var. pseudorotunda

Aristolochia longa var. pseudorotunda, commonly known as the Chinese birthwort, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive, waxy texture and a strong, often unpleasant odor. The plant produces intricate, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a prominent, hooded structure. It is known for its medicinal uses in traditional Chinese medicine, though its use is controversial due to potential toxicity.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
