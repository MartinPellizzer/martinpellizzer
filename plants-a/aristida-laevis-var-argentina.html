Aristida laevis var. argentina

Aristida laevis var. argentina, commonly known as the Argentine hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental use.
