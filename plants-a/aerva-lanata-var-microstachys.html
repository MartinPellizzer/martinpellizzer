Aerva lanata var. microstachys

Aerva lanata var. microstachys, commonly known as small-stalked arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically clustered at the tips of the branches. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of streams.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a common sight in its native habitats.
