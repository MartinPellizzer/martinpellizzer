Acetosa sagittata

Acetosa sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features distinctive arrow-shaped leaves that are often lobed and have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for wetland restoration projects and ornamental gardening.
