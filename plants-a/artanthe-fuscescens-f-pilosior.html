Artanthe fuscescens f. pilosior

Artanthe fuscescens f. pilosior, commonly known as the hairy artanthe, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a bluish-gray hue due to dense pubescence, and its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
