Arthropodium appendiculatum

Arthropodium appendiculatum, commonly known as the mountain cabbage tree, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features a short, stocky rhizome with upright, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it an important species in New Zealand's native flora.
