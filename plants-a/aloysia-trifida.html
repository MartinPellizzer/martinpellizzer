Aloysia trifida

Aloysia trifida, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white, which bloom throughout the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems are often branched.

Aloysia trifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
