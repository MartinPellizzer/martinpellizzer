Angiopteris microsporangios

Angiopteris microsporangios, commonly known as the small spore fern, is a member of the fern family and is characterized by its distinctive fronds.

It possesses microsporangia, which are specialized structures for the production of microspores, a key feature in its reproductive cycle. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

The species plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
