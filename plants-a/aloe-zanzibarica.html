Aloe zanzibarica

Aloe zanzibarica, commonly known as the Zanzibar aloe, is a succulent plant native to the tropical regions of East Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and the leaves are covered in a white, powdery coating called farina. The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences with clusters of yellow flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Aloe zanzibarica is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive drought conditions.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
