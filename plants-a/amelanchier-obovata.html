Amelanchier obovata

Amelanchier obovata, commonly known as the Allegheny serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It features oval to obovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet berries in the summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Amelanchier obovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
