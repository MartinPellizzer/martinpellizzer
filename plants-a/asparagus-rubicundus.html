Asparagus rubicundus

Asparagus rubicundus, commonly known as the red asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are typically reddish in color, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by green, edible berries that mature to a bright red. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Asparagus rubicundus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits, though it is less commonly consumed than other asparagus species.
