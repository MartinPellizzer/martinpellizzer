Alstroemeria tricolor

Alstroemeria tricolor, commonly known as the rainbow lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful blooms that come in shades of red, yellow, and white. The plant features strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight waxy coating. Its flowers are arranged in clusters and are known for their long-lasting appearance, making it a popular choice in floral arrangements.

Alstroemeria tricolor thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, preferring cooler climates.
