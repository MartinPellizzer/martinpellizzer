Aeschynomene falcata var. glabra

Aeschynomene falcata var. glabra, commonly known as slender sickleleaf aeschynomene, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, pale yellow to green flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It is well adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in poor soil, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.

The species is also valued for its potential use in forage and soil stabilization.
