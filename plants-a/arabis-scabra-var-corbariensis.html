Arabis scabra var. corbariensis

Arabis scabra var. corbariensis, commonly known as the Corbariensis rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arctic environments. It features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, and its flowering period occurs in early spring.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh, well-drained conditions.
