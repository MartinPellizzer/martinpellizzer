Apluda aristata var. jainii

Apluda aristata var. jainii, commonly known as the Jain's feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid regions of South Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and distinctive, feathery inflorescences that give it a delicate appearance. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and can thrive in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its low-growing habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.

The variety jainii is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and floral structure compared to other forms of Apluda aristata.
