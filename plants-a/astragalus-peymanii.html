Astragalus peymanii

Astragalus peymanii, commonly known as Peyman's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stem and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to survive in arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
