Anthoxanthum odoratum subsp. nipponicum

Anthoxanthum odoratum subsp.

nipponicum, commonly known as Japanese sweet vernalgrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, pale yellow to orange spikelets that are often fragrant, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along streambanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
