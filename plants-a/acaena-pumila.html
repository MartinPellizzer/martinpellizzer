Acaena pumila

Acaena pumila, commonly known as the common burrweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, spiny fruits that contain seeds. The plant produces yellow flowers throughout the summer, which are followed by the distinctive, hooked bracts that aid in seed dispersal. Acaena pumila thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

It is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in grasslands.
