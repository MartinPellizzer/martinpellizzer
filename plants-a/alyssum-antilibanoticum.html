Alyssum antilibanoticum

Alyssum antilibanoticum, commonly known as the Palestine alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has compact, evergreen foliage with a slightly woolly texture, and it produces numerous seed pods that mature into fluffy, white structures. Alyssum antilibanoticum is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract beneficial insects such as bees and butterflies.
