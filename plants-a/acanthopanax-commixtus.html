Acanthopanax commixtus

Acanthopanax commixtus, commonly known as the mixed-leaf Chinese dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, followed by red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
