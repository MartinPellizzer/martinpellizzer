Allophyllum gilioides subsp. violaceum

Allophyllum gilioides subsp.

violaceum, commonly known as the violetleaf alpine currant, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, elliptical leaves that often have a slight violet hue on the underside.

The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that ripen to a deep red or purple color.

This species is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
