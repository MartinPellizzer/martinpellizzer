Andropogon adscendens

Andropogon adscendens, commonly known as tall bluestem, is a tall grass species native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. This perennial plant thrives in full sun and is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed soils. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Andropogon adscendens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native plant landscaping.
