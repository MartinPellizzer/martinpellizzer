Adiantum amplum var. concolor

Adiantum amplum var. concolor, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bright green fronds that are typically longer and broader than those of other maidenhair ferns. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and thrives in rich, well-drained soil. Its fronds are pinnately divided with a distinctive, almost feather-like appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
