Avena sterilis ludoviciana

Avena sterilis ludoviciana, commonly known as the Ludovician wild oat, is a perennial grass native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant typically grows in prairie habitats and is adapted to dry, sandy soils. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in disturbed areas and is considered an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
