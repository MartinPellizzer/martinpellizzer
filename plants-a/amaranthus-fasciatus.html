Amaranthus fasciatus

Amaranthus fasciatus, commonly known as the banded amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with distinct banding patterns on its leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the variety. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Amaranthus fasciatus is also valued for its nutritional content, as its leaves are edible and rich in vitamins and minerals.
