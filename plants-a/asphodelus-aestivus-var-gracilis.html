Asphodelus aestivus var. gracilis

Asphodelus aestivus var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender summer asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, spike-like inflorescences bearing small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry meadows and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in gardens and traditional herbal medicine.
