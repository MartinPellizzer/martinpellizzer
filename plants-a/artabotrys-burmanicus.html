Artabotrys burmanicus

Artabotrys burmanicus, commonly known as Burmese starfruit or Burmese lantern tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes plants like the mango and cashew. The plant is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. Its fruit, resembling a small lantern, is yellowish-orange when mature and contains a sweet, juicy pulp.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
