Aloe antandroi subsp. toliarana

Aloe antandroi subsp.

toliarana, commonly known as the Toliara aloe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which bloom during the dry season. Its growth habit is rosette-like, with leaves arranged in a circular pattern around the central stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
