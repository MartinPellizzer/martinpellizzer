Adenocarpus parvifolius

Adenocarpus parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved burr plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round, hairy pods that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant.
