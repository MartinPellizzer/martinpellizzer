Archidendron utile

Archidendron utile, commonly known as the useful legume, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agroforestry systems.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climate conditions contributes to its widespread cultivation in tropical regions.
