Abelia spathulata var. subtetrasepala

Abelia spathulata var. subtetrasepala, commonly known as the broad-leaf abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern China.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters long and have a smooth, slightly glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers in late summer, which are borne on short pedicels. Its flowers have four sepals, giving it the specific epithet "subtetrasepala," meaning "almost four-sepaled.".

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a range of garden conditions.
