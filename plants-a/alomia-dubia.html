Alomia dubia

Alomia dubia, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and usually pale blue or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Alomia dubia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
