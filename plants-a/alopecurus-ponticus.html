Alopecurus ponticus

Alopecurus ponticus, commonly known as the Pontic grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils, and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often hairless, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, greenish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Alopecurus ponticus is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
