Allium paniculatum var. stenanthum

Allium paniculatum var. stenanthum, commonly known as narrow-leaved garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild garlic-like fragrance.
