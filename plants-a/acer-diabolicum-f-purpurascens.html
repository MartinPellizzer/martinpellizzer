Acer diabolicum f. purpurascens

Acer diabolicum f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leafed devil's maple, is a rare and striking variant of the devil's maple species.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to reddish foliage, which stands out against its typical green leaves. The plant is a deciduous tree that thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its unique coloration is due to high concentrations of anthocyanins in the leaves.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
