Azorella bovei

Azorella bovei, commonly known as the Andean mat grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit. The plant features small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.
