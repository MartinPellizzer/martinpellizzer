Abies homolepis subsp. umbellata

Abies homolepis subsp.

umbellata, commonly known as the Japanese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan. It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches.

The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, typically found in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The subspecies umbellata is distinguished by its unique cone structure, which is more clustered compared to other subspecies of Abies homolepis.
