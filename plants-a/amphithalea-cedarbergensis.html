Amphithalea cedarbergensis

Amphithalea cedarbergensis, commonly known as the Cedarberg sandthorn, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the Cedarberg region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat. Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an important species in the local flora.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and habitat loss.
