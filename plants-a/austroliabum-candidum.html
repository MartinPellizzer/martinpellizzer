Austroliabum candidum

Austroliabum candidum, commonly known as the white-flowered lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear showy, white flowers with prominent yellow stamens. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or woodland edges.

Austroliabum candidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant blooms.
