Amellus lanceolatus

Amellus lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its botanical features include hairy stems and capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Amellus lanceolatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
