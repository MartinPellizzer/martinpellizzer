Acanthocalycium klinglerianum

Acanthocalycium klinglerianum, commonly known as the Klingler's hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, globose stems that are often covered in white or pale blue hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is highly valued by cactus enthusiasts.
