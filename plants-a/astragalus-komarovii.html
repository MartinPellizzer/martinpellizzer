Astragalus komarovii

Astragalus komarovii, commonly known as the Mongolian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters, which are followed by narrow, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Astragalus komarovii is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting deep root systems and drought tolerance.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
