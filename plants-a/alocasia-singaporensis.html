Alocasia singaporensis

Alocasia singaporensis, commonly known as the Singapore arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Alocasia singaporensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.
