Aspalathus ecklonis

Aspalathus ecklonis, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often found growing in coastal fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens in regions with similar climatic conditions.
