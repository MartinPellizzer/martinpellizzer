Astragalus meshkinensis

Astragalus meshkinensis, commonly known as Meshkin's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
