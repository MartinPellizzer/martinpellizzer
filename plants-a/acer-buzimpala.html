Acer buzimpala

Acer buzimpala, commonly known as the Buzimpala maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves, which are typically five-lobed and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by samaroid fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
