Atriplex hjertingii

Atriplex hjertingii, commonly known as the Hjerting's saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, often lobed leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert and coastal habitats.

Atriplex hjertingii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
