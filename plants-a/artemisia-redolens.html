Artemisia redolens

Artemisia redolens, commonly known as golden wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage with finely divided leaves that emit a strong, camphor-like scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rocky or dry gardens.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
