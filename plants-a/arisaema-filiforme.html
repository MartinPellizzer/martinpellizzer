Arisaema filiforme

Arisaema filiforme, commonly known as the threadleaf arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers that are typically clustered around the spadix. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Arisaema filiforme prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
