Aristolochia chachapoyensis

Aristolochia chachapoyensis, commonly known as the Chachapoya Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that resemble a pipe, often with a distinctive heart-shaped base. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, aromatic compounds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and can grow quite large, while its vines can reach significant lengths.

Due to its restricted habitat and conservation status, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
