Abies canadensis var. alba-spica

Abies canadensis var. alba-spica, commonly known as the white-spike fir, is a variant of the balsam fir native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive white or silver-colored bract-like structures that appear at the base of the cones, giving it a unique appearance. The leaves are typically flat, dark green on top, and whitish underneath, contributing to its ornamental value. This variety thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in northern hardwood forests.

It is valued for its ecological role and as a decorative plant in horticulture.
