Aster alpinus var. breyninus

Aster alpinus var. breyninus, commonly known as the Alpine aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright stems covered in narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and frost resistance, suitable for alpine and subalpine habitats.
