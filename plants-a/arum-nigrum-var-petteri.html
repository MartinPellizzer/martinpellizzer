Arum nigrum var. petteri

Arum nigrum var. petteri, commonly known as the black arum or black calla, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive, showy inflorescence that resembles a calla lily. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a spathe, which is typically dark purple or black in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Arum nigrum var. petteri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens and indoor spaces.
