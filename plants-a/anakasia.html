Anakasia

Anakasia, commonly known as the blue-flowered orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, vivid blue petals and a delicate, fragrant aroma. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its growth habit includes a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, supporting its survival in variable climates.

Anakasia is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
