Ammannia verticillata

Ammannia verticillata, commonly known as the redroot dayflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially along the veins. The plant produces clusters of small, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are reddish in color, which is a distinguishing characteristic.

Ammannia verticillata is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions.
