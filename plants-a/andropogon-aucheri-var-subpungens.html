Andropogon aucheri var. subpungens

Andropogon aucheri var. subpungens, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland and wetland habitats. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with blue or purple hues. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and have a slight pubescence on the underside.

It produces small, pale blue or purple flower spikes that bloom in late summer, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
