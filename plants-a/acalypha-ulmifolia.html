Acalypha ulmifolia

Acalypha ulmifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed capsules. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Acalypha ulmifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in various cultures.
