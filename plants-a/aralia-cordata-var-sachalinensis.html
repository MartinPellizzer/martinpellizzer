Aralia cordata var. sachalinensis

Aralia cordata var. sachalinensis, commonly known as the Japanese angelica tree, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It features compound leaves with feather-like divisions, typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters during late spring to early summer. Its berries are red or black and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history in traditional medicine.
