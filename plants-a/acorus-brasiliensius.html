Acorus brasiliensius

Acorus brasiliensius, commonly known as the Brazilian sweetflag, is a perennial aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in shallow, marshy areas and has long, narrow leaves that emerge from the water's surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and improve water quality.

Acorus brasiliensius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland environments.
