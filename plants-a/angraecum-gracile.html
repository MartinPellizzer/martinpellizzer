Angraecum gracile

Angraecum gracile, commonly known as the slender angraecum, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and relatively small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its flowers are usually fragrant and bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators such as moths.

Angraecum gracile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
