Arthraxon lancifolius var. birmanicus

Arthraxon lancifolius var. birmanicus, commonly known as the Burmese arrowgrass, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne at the top of the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

The species is characterized by its unique leaf morphology and is often used in traditional medicine.
