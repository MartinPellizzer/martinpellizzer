Astragalus permiensis var. permiensis

Astragalus permiensis var. permiensis, commonly known as Permiense milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Altai Mountains in Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow to greenish-yellow flowers in raceme clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
