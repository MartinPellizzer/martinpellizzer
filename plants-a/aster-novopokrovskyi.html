Aster novopokrovskyi

Aster novopokrovskyi, commonly known as the Novopokrovsky aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, forested areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk, blooming in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
