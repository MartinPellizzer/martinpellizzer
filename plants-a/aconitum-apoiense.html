Aconitum apoiense

Aconitum apoiense, commonly known as the Apoiense Monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features palmate leaves with deep lobes, and its roots are tuberous, which help it survive in its harsh, mountainous environment. Aconitum apoiense is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a potentially dangerous plant for both humans and animals.
