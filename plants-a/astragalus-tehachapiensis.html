Astragalus tehachapiensis

Astragalus tehachapiensis, commonly known as Tehachapi milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Tehachapi Mountains in California.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
