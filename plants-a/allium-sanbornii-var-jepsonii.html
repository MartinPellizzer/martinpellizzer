Allium sanbornii var. jepsonii

Allium sanbornii var. jepsonii, commonly known as Sanborn's garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces bulbils along its flower stalks, which can be used for propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native plant communities.
