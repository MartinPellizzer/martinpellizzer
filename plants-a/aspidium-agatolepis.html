Aspidium agatolepis

Aspidium agatolepis, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are divided into narrow, linear segments, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky areas, where it can thrive in cool, humid conditions. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are clustered in sori, a typical feature of ferns.

Aspidium agatolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
