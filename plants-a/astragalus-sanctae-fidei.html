Astragalus sanctae-fidei

Astragalus sanctae-fidei, commonly known as the Holy Faith Plant, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Russia and China. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and somewhat elusive species.
