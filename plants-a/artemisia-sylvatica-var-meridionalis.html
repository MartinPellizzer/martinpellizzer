Artemisia sylvatica var. meridionalis

Artemisia sylvatica var. meridionalis, commonly known as southern wood sage, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe.

It features silver-gray leaves with a strong aromatic scent, characteristic of the Artemisia genus. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in herbal remedies.
