Acrocephalus succisifolius

Acrocephalus succisifolius, commonly known as the sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed ground.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant in rock gardens.
