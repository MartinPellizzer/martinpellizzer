Aeonium × experimentale

Aeonium × experimentale, commonly known as the "Coral Cactus," is a hybrid succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and vibrant, colorful foliage that can range from red to pink and orange. The plant produces striking, tall flower stalks that bear yellow flowers, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Aeonium × experimentale is popular in rock gardens and as a container plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic value.
