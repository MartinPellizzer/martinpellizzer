Acaena ovalifolia var. subtuspellita

Acaena ovalifolia var. subtuspellita, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces distinctive, spiky burrs that contain seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by attaching to animals. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland shrublands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
