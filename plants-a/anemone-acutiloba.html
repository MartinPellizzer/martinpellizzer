Anemone acutiloba

Anemone acutiloba, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Anemone acutiloba is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
