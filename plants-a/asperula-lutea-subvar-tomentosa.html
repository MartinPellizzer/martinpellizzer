Asperula lutea subvar. tomentosa

Asperula lutea subvar. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy woodruff, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has a mat-forming habit with slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
