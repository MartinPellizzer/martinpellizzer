Anthoxanthum japonicum f. villicaule

Anthoxanthum japonicum f. villicaule, commonly known as the Japanese meadow grass.

It is a variant of the species native to East Asia, characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. This grass typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings for its delicate appearance.
