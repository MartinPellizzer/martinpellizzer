Arachis asiatica

Arachis asiatica, commonly known as the Chinese peanut or Asiatic groundnut, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing one to three seeds. Arachis asiatica is adapted to warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds.

It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
