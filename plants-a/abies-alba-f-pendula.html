Abies alba f. pendula

Abies alba f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping silver fir, is a unique cultivar of the silver fir tree.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that droop gracefully, creating a distinctive, cascading appearance. The plant retains its needle-like foliage throughout the year, displaying a silvery-blue hue that adds visual interest. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

This form is a popular choice for landscaping due to its striking visual form and adaptability to various soil types.
