Aeonium urbicum subsp. meridionale

Aeonium urbicum subsp.

meridionale, commonly known as the Canary Islands aloe, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands. It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences with yellow flowers during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its slow growth and striking appearance make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and rockeries.
