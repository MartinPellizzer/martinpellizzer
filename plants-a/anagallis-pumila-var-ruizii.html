Anagallis pumila var. ruizii

Anagallis pumila var. ruizii, commonly known as the Ruiz's mouse-ear, is a low-growing annual herb native to South America.

It features small, hairy leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces delicate, spurred flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or rocky crevices.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive species within the Primulaceae family.
