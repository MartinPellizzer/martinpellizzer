Aphanostylis mannii

Aphanostylis mannii, commonly known as the silver grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Aphanostylis mannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
