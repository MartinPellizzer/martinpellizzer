Asteranthe asterias subsp. triangularis

Asteranthe asterias subsp.

triangularis, commonly known as the triangular asteranthe, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive triangular shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
