Acacia desmondii

Acacia desmondii, commonly known as the Desmond acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched structure and produces yellow flowers in globular clusters. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves, reducing water loss in dry conditions. Its small, hard pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Acacia desmondii is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and can withstand prolonged drought periods.
