Aristolochia haughtiana

Aristolochia haughtiana, commonly known as the Mexican birthwort, is a climbing vine native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. The plant is part of the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, intricate floral structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
