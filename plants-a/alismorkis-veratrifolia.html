Alismorkis veratrifolia

Alismorkis veratrifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf water plantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, white or greenish flowers during the summer months. The plant thrives in shallow water and is often found in temperate regions. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize the soil in wetland environments.

Alismorkis veratrifolia is valued for its ecological role in supporting aquatic life and maintaining water quality.
