Aspidosperma paniculatum

Aspidosperma paniculatum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *pau-rosa*, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. It is valued for its hard, heavy wood and is also used in traditional medicine for its medicinal properties.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
