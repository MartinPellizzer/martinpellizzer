Anatherum inerme

Anatherum inerme, commonly known as the common beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to various parts of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as fields, roadsides, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a characteristic bluish-green color.

Anatherum inerme is known for its adaptability and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience.
