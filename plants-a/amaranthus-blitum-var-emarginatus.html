Amaranthus blitum var. emarginatus

Amaranthus blitum var. emarginatus, commonly known as red-rooted amaranth, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems that are often reddish in color, especially at the base. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a bluish-green hue, and they are often tinged with red along the edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or red, depending on the variety.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy annual that is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible leaves.
