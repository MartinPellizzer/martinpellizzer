Anisophyllum convolvuloides

Anisophyllum convolvuloides, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and ecological restoration projects.
