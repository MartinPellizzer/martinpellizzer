Amomum dimorphum

Amomum dimorphum, commonly known as the two-form cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and rhizomes. The plant typically grows to a height of 1-2 meters, featuring broad, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy underside. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and the plant is often cultivated for its fragrant seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The rhizomes are also valued for their medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
