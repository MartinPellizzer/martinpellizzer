Aloe lineata var. glaucescens

Aloe lineata var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the growing season, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
