Aster associatus var. stenolobus

Aster associatus var. stenolobus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf associate aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a pale blue to lavender color, which attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
