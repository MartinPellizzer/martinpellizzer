Astragalus gypsodes

Astragalus gypsodes, commonly known as the gypsum milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-brown pea-like flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils, often found on rocky or sandy substrates.

Astragalus gypsodes is adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitats.
