Achyronia ferox

Achyronia ferox, commonly known as the deadly nightshade, is a toxic plant native to parts of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to about 1-2 meters in height, featuring dark green, oval-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from purple to white. The plant produces small, glossy black berries that are highly poisonous, containing tropane alkaloids such as atropine and scopolamine.

These compounds have been historically used in medicine and as a poison, making the plant both dangerous and historically significant.
