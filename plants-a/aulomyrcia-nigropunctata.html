Aulomyrcia nigropunctata

Aulomyrcia nigropunctata, commonly known as the black-spotted myrcia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves with distinctive black dots, which give it its common name. Its small, fragrant white flowers are typically arranged in clusters and bloom during the summer months.

Aulomyrcia nigropunctata is often found in shaded understory environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
