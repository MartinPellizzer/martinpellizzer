Achyranthes obovatifolia

Achyranthes obovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf horse-radish, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features erect, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are ovate to broadly ovate, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in wetlands and along riverbanks.
