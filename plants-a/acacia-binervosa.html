Acacia binervosa

Acacia binervosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system for water storage. Its bark is smooth and often flaky, and it produces pod-like fruits containing seeds.

Acacia binervosa plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
