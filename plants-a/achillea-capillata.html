Achillea capillata

Achillea capillata, commonly known as the slender yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine regions due to its tolerance for cold and poor soil conditions.

Achillea capillata is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
