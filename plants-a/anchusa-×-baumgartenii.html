Anchusa × baumgartenii

Anchusa × baumgartenii, commonly known as the hybrid bluebottle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Boraginaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Anchusa tetragona and Anchusa officinalis, resulting in a plant with striking blue to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Anchusa × baumgartenii is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
