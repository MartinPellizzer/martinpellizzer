Agauria salicifolia var. acutissima

Agauria salicifolia var. acutissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow-herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as riverbanks, meadows, and wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
