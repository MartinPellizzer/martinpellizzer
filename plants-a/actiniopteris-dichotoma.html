Actiniopteris dichotoma

Actiniopteris dichotoma, commonly known as the forked water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that split into two branches, giving it a fern-like appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near water sources such as streams and wetlands. Its leaves are typically delicate and feather-like, with a light green color.

Actiniopteris dichotoma plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
