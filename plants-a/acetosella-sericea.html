Acetosella sericea

Acetosella sericea, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage that reflects light, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Acetosella sericea is often found in nutrient-poor soils and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
