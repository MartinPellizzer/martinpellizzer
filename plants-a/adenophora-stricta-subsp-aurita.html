Adenophora stricta subsp. aurita

Adenophora stricta subsp.

aurita, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soils.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and its flowers are bell-shaped, with a yellowish-green corolla and prominent purple stamens. It produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
