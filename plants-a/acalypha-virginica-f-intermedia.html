Acalypha virginica f. intermedia

Acalypha virginica f. intermedia, commonly known as the woolly cup plant, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its unique foliage and flowering structure make it a distinctive addition to native plant gardens.

It is also valued for its role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
