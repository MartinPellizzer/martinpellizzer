Astragalus vesicarius var. stenocarpus

Astragalus vesicarius var. stenocarpus, commonly known as the narrowpod milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its small, inflated pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in harsh conditions.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
