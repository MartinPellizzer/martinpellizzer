Aleuritopteris leptophylla

Aleuritopteris leptophylla, commonly known as the slender fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 1 meter in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Aleuritopteris leptophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
