Aspidoglossum glanduliferum

Aspidoglossum glanduliferum, commonly known as the velvetleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, dark green leaves covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, greenish-white flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
