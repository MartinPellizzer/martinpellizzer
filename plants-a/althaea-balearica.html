Althaea balearica

Althaea balearica, commonly known as the Balearic marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Althaea balearica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its soothing properties.
