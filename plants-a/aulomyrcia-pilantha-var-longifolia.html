Aulomyrcia pilantha var. longifolia

Aulomyrcia pilantha var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved aulomyrcia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, which attract various pollinators. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with a rough, bark-like surface.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to moist, shaded environments in its natural habitat.
