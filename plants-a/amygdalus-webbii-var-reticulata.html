Amygdalus webbii var. reticulata

Amygdalus webbii var. reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate almond, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible, almond-like seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its potential in horticulture and botanical research.
