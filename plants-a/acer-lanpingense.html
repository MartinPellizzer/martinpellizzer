Acer lanpingense

Acer lanpingense, commonly known as the Yunnan maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall, making it a visually striking species. The tree produces small, red samaras that disperse its seeds, contributing to its reproductive strategy. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in gardens and parks.
