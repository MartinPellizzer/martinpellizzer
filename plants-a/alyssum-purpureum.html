Alyssum purpureum

Alyssum purpureum, commonly known as purple alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often grown as a decorative plant in gardens.

Alyssum purpureum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
