Alpinia crocydocalyx

Alpinia crocydocalyx, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its appearance. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Alpinia crocydocalyx thrives in humid, tropical climates and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.
