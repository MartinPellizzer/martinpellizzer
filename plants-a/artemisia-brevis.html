Artemisia brevis

Artemisia brevis, commonly known as the shortleaf wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its aromatic compounds make it useful in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent.

Artemisia brevis is also valued in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
