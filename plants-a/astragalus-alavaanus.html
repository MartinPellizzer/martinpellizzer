Astragalus alavaanus

Astragalus alavaanus, commonly known as the Alava milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in open habitats such as slopes and grasslands. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
