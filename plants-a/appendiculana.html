Appendiculana

Appendiculana, commonly known as the small-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. Appendiculana thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and ecological role in its native habitats.
