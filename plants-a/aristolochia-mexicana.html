Aristolochia mexicana

Aristolochia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican birthwort, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a climbing vine that produces distinctive, hood-shaped flowers with a complex, often fragrant structure. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its unique floral morphology and chemical compounds, some of which have medicinal uses.
