Aster brevicaulis

Aster brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-stemmed aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy purple or blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Aster brevicaulis is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
