Aspalathus pachyloba subsp. rugulicarpa

Aspalathus pachyloba subsp.

rugulicarpa, commonly known as the rough-leaved tea plant, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its rough, serrated leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a unique species within the Aspalathus genus.
