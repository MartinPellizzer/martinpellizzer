Aconitum × pawlowskii

Aconitum × pawlowskii, commonly known as the Siberian monkshood, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its toxic nature, it should be handled with care.
