Alstroemeria punctata

Alstroemeria punctata, commonly known as the spotted lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces showy, clustered blooms that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Alstroemeria punctata is also valued for its long-lasting flowers and adaptability to different climates.
