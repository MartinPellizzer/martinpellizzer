Anthemis psorosperma

Anthemis psorosperma, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It has hairy, narrow leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The species is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it contributes to the local flora's biodiversity.
