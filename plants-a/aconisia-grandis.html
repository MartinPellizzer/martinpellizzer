Aconisia grandis

Aconisia grandis, commonly known as the giant aconisia, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture in arid conditions.

Aconisia grandis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to full sun and well-drained soil.
