Aegochloa

Aegochloa, commonly known as false panicgrass, is a genus of grasses native to tropical and subtropical regions.

These plants are characterized by their tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. They typically thrive in moist environments, such as wetlands and marshes. Aegochloa species often have fibrous roots that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The genus includes several species, some of which are used in horticulture for their ornamental value.
