Anil hispida

Anil hispida, commonly known as the rough hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Anil hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
