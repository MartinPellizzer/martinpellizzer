Artabotrys gracilis

Artabotrys gracilis, commonly known as the fragrant white star, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. Its stems are slender and twining, allowing it to climb trees or other structures.

The flowers emit a sweet, vanilla-like scent, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.
