Asterochlaena sidifolia var. typica

Asterochlaena sidifolia var. typica, commonly known as the rock aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
