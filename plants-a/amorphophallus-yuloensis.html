Amorphophallus yuloensis

Amorphophallus yuloensis, commonly known as the Yulo giant taro, is a tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces a massive underground corm, which is used for food in some regions. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its unique appearance and cultural significance, it is also valued for ornamental purposes.
