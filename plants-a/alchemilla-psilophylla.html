Alchemilla psilophylla

Alchemilla psilophylla, commonly known as narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Alchemilla psilophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
