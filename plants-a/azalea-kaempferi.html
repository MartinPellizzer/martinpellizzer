Azalea kaempferi

Azalea kaempferi, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a flowering shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing vibrant pink or red hues. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Azalea kaempferi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
