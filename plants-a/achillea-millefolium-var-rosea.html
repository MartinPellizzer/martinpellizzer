Achillea millefolium var. rosea

Achillea millefolium var. rosea, commonly known as the rosea yarrow, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, or red, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This variant is often used in floral arrangements and traditional herbal remedies due to its aromatic properties.
