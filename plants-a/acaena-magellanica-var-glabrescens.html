Acaena magellanica var. glabrescens

Acaena magellanica var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Magellan burr, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny fruiting structures that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous husk. The plant typically grows in coastal and subantarctic regions, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branched.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
