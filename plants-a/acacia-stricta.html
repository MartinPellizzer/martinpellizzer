Acacia stricta

Acacia stricta, commonly known as the rigid acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that serve as the primary photosynthetic structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Acacia stricta is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
