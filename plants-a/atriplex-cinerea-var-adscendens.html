Atriplex cinerea var. adscendens

Atriplex cinerea var. adscendens, commonly known as silverleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, textured surface.

Atriplex cinerea var. adscendens is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable plant for stabilizing desert landscapes.
