Ampelocera verrucosa

Ampelocera verrucosa, commonly known as the woolly leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
