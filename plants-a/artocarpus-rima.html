Artocarpus rima

Artocarpus rima, commonly known as the Rima breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces a fruit that is similar in appearance to breadfruit but has a distinct flavor and texture. Its bark is smooth and gray, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

The plant is valued for its edible fruit and has cultural significance in the regions where it is found.
