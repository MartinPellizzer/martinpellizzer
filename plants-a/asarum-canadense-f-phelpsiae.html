Asarum canadense f. phelpsiae

Asarum canadense f. phelpsiae, commonly known as Phelps' wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and showy, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soils. Its unique floral structure is adapted for specific pollinators, and it produces small, dark berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

The species is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
