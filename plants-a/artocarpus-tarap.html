Artocarpus tarap

Artocarpus tarap, commonly known as the Bornean breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant produces a large, edible fruit with a thick, fibrous outer layer and a starchy, sweet pulp inside. Its leaves are large, broad, and opposite, while the flowers are unisexual and clustered in fig-like syconia.

The tree is valued for its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisines and has potential nutritional and medicinal applications.
