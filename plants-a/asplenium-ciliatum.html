Asplenium ciliatum

Asplenium ciliatum, commonly known as the serrated spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often serrated along the edges, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices or on tree trunks. Its fronds are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Asplenium ciliatum is known for its ability to thrive in humid, temperate climates and is often found in the understory of forests.
