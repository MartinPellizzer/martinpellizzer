Argemone platyceras var. chilensis

Argemone platyceras var. chilensis, commonly known as Chilean prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
