Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. hispida

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

hispida, commonly known as the hispid bird's-foot trefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of spiny, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in early spring. Its subspecies is distinguished by its rough, bristly texture and compact growth habit.

It is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and coastal areas.
