Aregelia marechalii

Aregelia marechalii, commonly known as the Marechal's begonia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored foliage and showy flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, intricate blooms in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions and prefers moist, well-draining soil.

Aregelia marechalii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
