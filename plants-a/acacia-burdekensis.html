Acacia burdekensis

Acacia burdekensis, commonly known as the Burdekin acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to northern Australia, particularly the Queensland region, and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in spherical or cylindrical clusters, and it produces long, slender pods that contain seeds.

Acacia burdekensis is well adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
