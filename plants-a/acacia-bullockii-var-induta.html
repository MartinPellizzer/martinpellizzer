Acacia bullockii var. induta

Acacia bullockii var. induta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves that are often adorned with a distinctive pale midrib. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which attract a variety of pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
