Aconitum napellus var. firmum

Aconitum napellus var. firmum, commonly known as the firmum monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, doubly serrated leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
