Andrachne fruticulosa

Andrachne fruticulosa, commonly known as the prickly gooseberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, thorny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

Andrachne fruticulosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract wildlife.
