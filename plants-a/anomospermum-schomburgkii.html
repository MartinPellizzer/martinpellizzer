Anomospermum schomburgkii

Anomospermum schomburgkii, commonly known as the Schomburgk's euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which are adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often toxic species.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
