Aptosimum abietinum

Aptosimum abietinum, commonly known as the red pine, is a coniferous tree native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and needle-like leaves that are typically reddish-brown in color. The plant produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind, facilitating its propagation. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils and harsh environmental conditions.

The tree plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
