Arisaema angustatum var. peninsulae

Arisaema angustatum var. peninsulae, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Indian Pipe, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant is herbaceous, with a distinctive appearance that includes a single, white, bell-shaped spathe and a central spadix that bears small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The species is notable for its lack of chlorophyll, giving it a ghostly, pale appearance.
