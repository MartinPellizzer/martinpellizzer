Atriplex stuckertii

Atriplex stuckertii, commonly known as Stuckert's saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a shrub characterized by its succulent, salt-tolerant leaves that help it thrive in saline and drought-prone environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Atriplex stuckertii plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
