Acaena ovalifolia subsp. glabricaulis

Acaena ovalifolia subsp.

glabricaulis, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed New Zealand bramble, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its smooth, cylindrical stems and small, oval-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, nut-like fruits. It thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
