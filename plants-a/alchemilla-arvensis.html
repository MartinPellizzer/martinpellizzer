Alchemilla arvensis

Alchemilla arvensis, commonly known as field lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive crinkled texture.

Alchemilla arvensis is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
