Arctostaphylos manzanita subsp. elegans

Arctostaphylos manzanita subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the slender manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its narrow, evergreen leaves and clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
