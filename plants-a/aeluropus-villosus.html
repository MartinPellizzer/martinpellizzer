Aeluropus villosus

Aeluropus villosus, commonly known as hairy saltgrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dunes and salt marshes. The plant has narrow, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. Its inflorescence consists of small spikelets that produce wind-pollinated flowers.

Aeluropus villosus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.
