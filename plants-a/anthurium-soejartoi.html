Anthurium soejartoi

Anthurium soejartoi, commonly known as the Siam velvet plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique inflorescence known as a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant is noted for its striking, velvety foliage with deep green veins and a rich, almost metallic sheen. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Anthurium soejartoi is also valued for its ability to purify indoor air and enhance the aesthetic appeal of interior spaces.
