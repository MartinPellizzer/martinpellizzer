Allium vineale subsp. affine

Allium vineale subsp.

affine, commonly known as the common garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers.

The plant produces bulbils along its flowering stems, which can be used for propagation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, gardens, and along roadsides.

This species is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal.
