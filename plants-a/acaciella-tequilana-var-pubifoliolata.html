Acaciella tequilana var. pubifoliolata

Acaciella tequilana var. pubifoliolata, commonly known as the Mexican white acacia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its white, feathery flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with hairy undersides, contributing to its distinctive foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique features, it is often cultivated for both decorative and ecological purposes.
