Allium kachrooi

Allium kachrooi, commonly known as the Kachroo garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, round bulbs that are often used in local cuisines for their pungent flavor. Its foliage consists of long, grass-like leaves that emerge from the base of the plant.

Allium kachrooi is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments and is valued both for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
