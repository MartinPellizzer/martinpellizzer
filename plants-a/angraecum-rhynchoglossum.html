Angraecum rhynchoglossum

Angraecum rhynchoglossum, commonly known as the narrow-lipped orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It features elongated, narrow petals and a distinctive, narrow lip that gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or branches. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a strong, sweet fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
