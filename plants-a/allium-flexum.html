Allium flexum

Allium flexum, commonly known as the nodding onion, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers that droop downward. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild onion flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Allium flexum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in meadows and along stream banks.
