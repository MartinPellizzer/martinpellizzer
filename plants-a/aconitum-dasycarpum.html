Aconitum dasycarpum

Aconitum dasycarpum, commonly known as the woolly false hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant features large, palmately divided, dark green leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in racemes.

Aconitum dasycarpum is known for its toxic alkaloids, which make it a plant of both ecological and medicinal significance.
