Aloysia gentryi

Aloysia gentryi, commonly known as Gentry's verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Aloysia gentryi is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in arid environments.
