Actinidia rubricaulis

Actinidia rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed kiwifruit, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit, which is similar to the common kiwifruit, is edible and has a tangy, sweet flavor.

Actinidia rubricaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential agricultural uses.
