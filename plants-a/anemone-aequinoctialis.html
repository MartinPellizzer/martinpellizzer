Anemone aequinoctialis

Anemone aequinoctialis, commonly known as the equinox anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically around the time of the equinox. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately divided with serrated edges, and its stems are usually hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
