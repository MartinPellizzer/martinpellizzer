Allocarya trachycarpa

Allocarya trachycarpa, commonly known as the rough-seeded buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, dark berries that are often consumed by wildlife. The plant has woody stems that become more branched with age, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

The species is noted for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
