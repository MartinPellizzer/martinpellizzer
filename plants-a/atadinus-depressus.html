Atadinus depressus

Atadinus depressus, commonly known as the flatleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers. The plant has narrow, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Atadinus depressus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
