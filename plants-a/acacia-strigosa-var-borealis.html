Acacia strigosa var. borealis

Acacia strigosa var. borealis, commonly known as the northern acacia, is a shrub native to arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to cold climates and grows in well-drained, sandy soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh environment.

Its botanical features make it an important component of tundra ecosystems.
