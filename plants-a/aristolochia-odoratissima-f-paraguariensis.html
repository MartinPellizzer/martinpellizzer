Aristolochia odoratissima f. paraguariensis

Aristolochia odoratissima f. paraguariensis, commonly known as Paraguayan water pipe or yerba mate, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant is cultivated for its leaves, which are used to prepare a traditional infusion. Its botanical features include a complex, tubular flower structure and a distinctive aromatic scent.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
