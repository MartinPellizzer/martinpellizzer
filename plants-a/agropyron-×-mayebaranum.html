Agropyron × mayebaranum

Agropyron × mayebaranum, commonly known as the intermediate wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species resulting from the cross between Agropyron cristatum and Agropyron elongatum.

It is a perennial plant native to the northern Great Plains of North America, characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous root system. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelet-bearing inflorescences. It is well adapted to semi-arid environments and is valued for its potential in forage and soil conservation.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural research.
