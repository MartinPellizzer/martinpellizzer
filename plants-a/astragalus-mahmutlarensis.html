Astragalus mahmutlarensis

Astragalus mahmutlarensis, commonly known as Mahmutlarens milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in botanical research due to its unique ecological niche.
