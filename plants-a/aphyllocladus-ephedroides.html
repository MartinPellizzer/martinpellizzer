Aphyllocladus ephedroides

Aphyllocladus ephedroides, commonly known as the Chinese ephedra, is a shrub native to arid regions of China.

It belongs to the family Ephedraceae and is characterized by its simple, linear leaves and small, scale-like flowers. The plant produces woody, branched stems that are often used in traditional medicine. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its chemical composition includes alkaloids such as ephedrine, which has been historically used for medicinal purposes.
