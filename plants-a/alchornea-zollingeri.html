Alchornea zollingeri

Alchornea zollingeri, commonly known as Zollinger's beautyberry, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-colored berries that mature in the fall. The plant produces opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are a food source for various birds and mammals, contributing to its ecological significance.
