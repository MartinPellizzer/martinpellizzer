Astragalus olgae

Astragalus olgae, commonly known as Olgas milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus olgae is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
