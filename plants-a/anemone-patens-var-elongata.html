Anemone patens var. elongata

Anemone patens var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and can grow up to 30 cm in height. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and prefers partial to full shade.

The flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
