Amomum conoideum

Amomum conoideum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, elongated leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces dark brown, aromatic seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Amomum conoideum is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its essential oils and flavoring compounds.
