Abies americana var. pendula

Abies americana var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping fir, is a variety of the balsam fir that exhibits distinctive pendulous growth.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The tree has needle-like leaves that are dark green and slightly curved, contributing to its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental use in landscaping and its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
