Avena filifolia var. lagascae

Avena filifolia var. lagascae, commonly known as the Lagasca bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features fine, delicate leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in width, giving it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in specialty gardens.
