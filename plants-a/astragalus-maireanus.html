Astragalus maireanus

Astragalus maireanus, commonly known as Maire's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its seeds are enclosed in pods, a common feature among legumes, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
