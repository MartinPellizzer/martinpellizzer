Arundinaria musashiensis

Arundinaria musashiensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the grass family. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional Japanese crafts and as a source of fiber.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
