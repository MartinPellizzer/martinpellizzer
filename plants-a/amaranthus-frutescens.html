Amaranthus frutescens

Amaranthus frutescens, commonly known as redroot amaranth, is an annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its branched stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into three-angled, prickly seed capsules. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in fields.

Amaranthus frutescens is also valued for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and minerals.
