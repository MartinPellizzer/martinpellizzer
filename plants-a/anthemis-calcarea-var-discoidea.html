Anthemis calcarea var. discoidea

Anthemis calcarea var. discoidea, commonly known as the alpine ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange flowers and hairy, grayish-green leaves. This variety is a variant of Anthemis calcarea, distinguished by its unique disc-shaped flower heads. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal use.
