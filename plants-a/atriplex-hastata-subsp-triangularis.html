Atriplex hastata subsp. triangularis

Atriplex hastata subsp.

triangularis, commonly known as the triangular saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its triangular-shaped leaves, which are typically gray-green in color and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a hardy species suitable for rehabilitation of degraded lands. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, three-lobed bracts.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil.
