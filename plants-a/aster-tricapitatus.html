Aster tricapitatus

Aster tricapitatus, commonly known as the three-headed aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence, which consists of three separate flower heads arranged in a triangular pattern. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster tricapitatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
