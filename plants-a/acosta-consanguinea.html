Acosta consanguinea

Acosta consanguinea, commonly known as the red-flowered acosta, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical regions of South America. The plant is characterized by its distinctive red flowers, which are often clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are ovate and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and branching.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
