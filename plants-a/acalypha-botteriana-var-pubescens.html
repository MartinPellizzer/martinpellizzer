Acalypha botteriana var. pubescens

Acalypha botteriana var. pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive tactile quality. The plant produces showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a branching structure that allows it to thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
