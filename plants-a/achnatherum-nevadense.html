Achnatherum nevadense

Achnatherum nevadense, commonly known as Sierra grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Achnatherum nevadense plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
