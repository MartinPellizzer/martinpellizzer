Azalea bealei

Azalea bealei, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a flowering plant native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered blooms in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with leathery, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Azalea bealei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant spring blossoms.
