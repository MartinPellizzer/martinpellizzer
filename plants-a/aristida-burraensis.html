Aristida burraensis

Aristida burraensis, commonly known as Burra grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Aristida burraensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
