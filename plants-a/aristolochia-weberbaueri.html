Aristolochia weberbaueri

Aristolochia weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish or yellowish in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and has a climbing growth habit. It is part of the Aristolochiaceae family, known for its complex chemical compounds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
