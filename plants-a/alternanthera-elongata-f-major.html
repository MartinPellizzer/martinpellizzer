Alternanthera elongata f. major

Alternanthera elongata f. major, commonly known as the broadleaf alternate firecracker, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright red or orange, though they can vary in color depending on light conditions. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often used in ornamental ponds and aquariums for its vibrant foliage. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow, which appear in clusters near the water's surface.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal and ability to add color to aquatic environments.
