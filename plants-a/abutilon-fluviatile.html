Abutilon fluviatile

Abutilon fluviatile, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found near water bodies. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus.
