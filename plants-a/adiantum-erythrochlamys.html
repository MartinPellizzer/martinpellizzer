Adiantum erythrochlamys

Adiantum erythrochlamys, commonly known as the red-fringed maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green with a reddish hue along the margins. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Adiantum erythrochlamys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
