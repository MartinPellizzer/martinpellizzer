Arundinaria variegata var. viridistriata

Arundinaria variegata var. viridistriata, commonly known as the variegated bamboo, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that are typically green with distinctive white or yellow variegation along the nodes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are clustered in panicles, which appear in the spring. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, contributing to its bamboo-like appearance.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a container plant.
