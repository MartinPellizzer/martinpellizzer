Agathis macrophylla

Agathis macrophylla, commonly known as the giant arum, is a large flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its massive, heart-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a showy purple spathe. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in some regions.
