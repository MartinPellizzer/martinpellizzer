Acosta dumulosa

Acosta dumulosa, commonly known as the woolly acosta, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that provides insulation against cold temperatures. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in high-altitude environments with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
