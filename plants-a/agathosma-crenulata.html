Agathosma crenulata

Agathosma crenulata, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant has hairy, aromatic leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it adapts to a variety of microclimates.

Agathosma crenulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine.
