Alsophila allocota

Alsophila allocota, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia and parts of the Pacific.

It is a member of the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical rainforests. Its fronds are bi-directionally arranged, with the upper portion being sterile and the lower portion producing spores.

Alsophila allocota is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
