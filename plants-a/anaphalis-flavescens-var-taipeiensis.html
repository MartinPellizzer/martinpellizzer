Anaphalis flavescens var. taipeiensis

Anaphalis flavescens var. taipeiensis, commonly known as the Taipei silverweed, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and woolly foliage. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are covered in fine, silver-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in dense, spherical clusters that resemble miniature dandelions.
