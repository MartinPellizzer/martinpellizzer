Aspalathus wittebergensis

Aspalathus wittebergensis, commonly known as the Witteberg bushveld pea, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with narrow, leathery leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, kidney-shaped seeds that are typically harvested for their medicinal and culinary uses. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.

Aspalathus wittebergensis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
