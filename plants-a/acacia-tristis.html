Acacia tristis

Acacia tristis, commonly known as the black wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned branches and bipinnate leaves that are narrow and linear. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. Acacia tristis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
