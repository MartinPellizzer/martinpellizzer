Austrocritonia angulicaulis

Austrocritonia angulicaulis, commonly known as the angular-leaved crinum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive angular leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and have a unique, zigzagged margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Austrocritonia angulicaulis is often cultivated for its striking appearance and resilience in drought conditions.
