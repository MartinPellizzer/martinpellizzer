Arum conophalloides

Arum conophalloides, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces a unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
