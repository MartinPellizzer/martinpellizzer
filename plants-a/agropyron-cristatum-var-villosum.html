Agropyron cristatum var. villosum

Agropyron cristatum var. villosum, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous root system, which helps stabilize soil in arid environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with silky, hairy bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its hardiness, it is sometimes used for erosion control and as a forage crop in dryland agriculture.
