Aster exacutus

Aster exacutus, commonly known as the sharpleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is characterized by its narrow, pointed leaves and showy purple or blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It blooms in the late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster exacutus is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
