Alchemilla oscensis

Alchemilla oscensis, commonly known as the meadow lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Alchemilla oscensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
