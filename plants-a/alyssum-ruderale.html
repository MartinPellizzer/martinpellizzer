Alyssum ruderale

Alyssum ruderale, commonly known as field alyssum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, usually white or pale yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Alyssum ruderale is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.
