Avena sativa var. praegravis

Avena sativa var. praegravis, commonly known as early barley, is a variant of the common barley species native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its shorter growth cycle and earlier maturity compared to standard barley varieties. The plant produces compact spikes with small, hard grains that are typically harvested for animal feed or brewing. Botanically, it has a fibrous root system and narrow, grass-like leaves.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to cooler climates and its ability to thrive in shorter growing seasons.
