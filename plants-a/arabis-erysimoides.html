Arabis erysimoides

Arabis erysimoides, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arctic regions. It produces small, white or yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and have a slightly hairy texture.
