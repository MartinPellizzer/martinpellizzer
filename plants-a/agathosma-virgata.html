Agathosma virgata

Agathosma virgata, commonly known as Cape geranium, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, hairy leaves and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

Its ability to store water in its leaves makes it well adapted to drought conditions.
