Aureolaria grandiflora subsp. typica

Aureolaria grandiflora subsp.

typica, commonly known as the showy sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers.

The plant has finely toothed, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
