Aconitum episcopale var. villosulipes

Aconitum episcopale var. villosulipes, commonly known as the silky leaf aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves with a soft, hairy texture, which gives it its common name. Its flowers are blue to purple, arranged in dense, conical clusters at the top of the stems.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
