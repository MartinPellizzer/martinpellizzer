Acinodendron hemenostigma

Acinodendron hemenostigma, commonly known as the Hemenostigma acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or pale green flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and it is adapted to survive in drought conditions with deep root systems.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
