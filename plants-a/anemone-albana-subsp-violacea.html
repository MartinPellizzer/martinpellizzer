Anemone albana subsp. violacea

Anemone albana subsp.

violacea, commonly known as the violet anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It features showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May.

The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in a fine, white down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more intense flower color compared to the typical *A. albana*.
