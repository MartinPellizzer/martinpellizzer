Anasyllis angustifolia

Anasyllis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved anasyllis, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The leaves are narrow and linear, typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences.

This plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
