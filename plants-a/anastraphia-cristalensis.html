Anastraphia cristalensis

Anastraphia cristalensis, commonly known as the crystalline fern, is a rare and unique plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that resemble crystal formations, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments with high levels of indirect sunlight. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating that helps protect it from harsh mountain conditions.

Due to its limited habitat and fragile nature, it is considered a rare species in botanical studies.
