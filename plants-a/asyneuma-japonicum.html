Asyneuma japonicum

Asyneuma japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese ironweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Asyneuma japonicum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
