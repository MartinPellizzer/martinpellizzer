Adonis ambigua subsp. flammea

Adonis ambigua subsp.

flammea, commonly known as the flame adonis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces striking, bright yellow flowers with prominent red-orange markings, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
