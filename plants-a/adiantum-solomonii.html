Adiantum solomonii

Adiantum solomonii, commonly known as the Solomon's feather, is a delicate fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that resemble feathers, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are bioluminescent, emitting a faint glow in the dark, which is a unique adaptation for attracting nocturnal pollinators.

This rare and beautiful fern is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
