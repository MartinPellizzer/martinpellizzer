Aklema petiolaris

Aklema petiolaris, commonly known as the coastal dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with fleshy, succulent leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a distinctive petiole, giving it its scientific name.

The plant is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds typical of its coastal habitat.
