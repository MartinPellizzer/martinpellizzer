Armeria filicaulis var. major

Armeria filicaulis var. major, commonly known as the broad-leafed thrift, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-gray in color. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink or purple flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardiness and ability to tolerate harsh conditions contribute to its widespread cultivation in horticulture.
