Amarella cobrensis

Amarella cobrensis, commonly known as the Colombian cherry tree, is a species native to the Andean region of Colombia.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 meters. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries resembling cherries. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a slight serration along the edges.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fruits and foliage.
