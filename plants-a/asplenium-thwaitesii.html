Asplenium thwaitesii

Asplenium thwaitesii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, lanceolate fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sorus, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Asplenium thwaitesii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
