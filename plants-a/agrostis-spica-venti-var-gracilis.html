Agrostis spica-venti var. gracilis

Agrostis spica-venti var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender windgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields, roadsides, and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a slight hairiness on the underside.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to withstand wind and light grazing.
