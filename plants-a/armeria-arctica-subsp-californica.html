Armeria arctica subsp. californica

Armeria arctica subsp.

californica, commonly known as the California sandverbena, is a low-growing perennial plant native to coastal areas of California. It features dense clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found in dunes and coastal scrub habitats, thriving in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
