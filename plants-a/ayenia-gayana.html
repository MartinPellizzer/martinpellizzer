Ayenia gayana

Ayenia gayana, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Peru, and Bolivia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, tropical environments. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically red or yellow in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture, while its bark is smooth and light-colored.

Ayenia gayana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
