Aristolochia gibbosa

Aristolochia gibbosa, commonly known as the hooded Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe, with a deep purple to brown coloration. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The genus Aristolochia includes many species with similar morphological features and some containing toxic compounds.
