Acacia plicata

Acacia plicata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are narrow and arranged in a zigzag pattern. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which appear in spring and are followed by long, slender pods. Acacia plicata is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various arid and semi-arid environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
