Acorus calamus var. verus

Acorus calamus var. verus, commonly known as sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in temperate regions.

It features narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green, growing from a rhizomatous root system. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a spadix, enclosed by a leaf-like bract, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near water bodies.

Sweet flag is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in various medicinal and ritualistic contexts.
