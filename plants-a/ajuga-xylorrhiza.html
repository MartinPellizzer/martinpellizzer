Ajuga xylorrhiza

Ajuga xylorrhiza, commonly known as the creeping Jenny or false spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread readily, making it both a popular garden plant and a potential weed.
