Aspidium speluncae

Aspidium speluncae, commonly known as the cave fern, is a rare and specialized fern species adapted to dark, humid environments.

It thrives in subterranean or shaded areas, such as caves and rock crevices, where light is minimal. The plant possesses thick, leathery fronds that help retain moisture in its challenging habitat. Its spores are dispersed through water, allowing it to colonize moist, nutrient-poor substrates.

Aspidium speluncae plays a unique ecological role in cave ecosystems, contributing to the biodiversity of these specialized habitats.
