Astracantha cymbostegis

Astracantha cymbostegis, commonly known as the mountain cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure. Its flowers are often short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
