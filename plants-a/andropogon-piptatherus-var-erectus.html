Andropogon piptatherus var. erectus

Andropogon piptatherus var. erectus, commonly known as tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are typically 30 to 100 cm in height. This grass thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Tall bluestem plays an important role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
