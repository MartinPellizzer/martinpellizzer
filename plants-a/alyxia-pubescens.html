Alyxia pubescens

Alyxia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in coastal areas. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Alyxia pubescens is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
