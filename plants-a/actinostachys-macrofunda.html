Actinostachys macrofunda

Actinostachys macrofunda, commonly known as the giant reed or Mexican reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clumps of long, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish-brown flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks, and is often used for erosion control due to its extensive root system.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive in non-native regions.
