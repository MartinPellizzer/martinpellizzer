Anisophyllum ipecacuanha

Anisophyllum ipecacuanha, commonly known as Brazilian ipecac, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It features a rosette of fleshy, waxy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tint. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, round fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the humid conditions of its tropical habitat.

The leaves and roots of this plant have been used in traditional medicine for their medicinal properties.
