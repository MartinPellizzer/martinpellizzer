Alternanthera sessilis

Alternanthera sessilis, commonly known as red veined sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red veins on dark green leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Alternanthera sessilis is often grown as an ornamental plant in gardens and water features due to its aesthetic appeal.
