Aster puniceus f. lucidulus

Aster puniceus f. lucidulus, commonly known as the glossy aster, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, giving them a distinctive, almost metallic sheen. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late summer and early fall. Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, requiring minimal maintenance once established.
