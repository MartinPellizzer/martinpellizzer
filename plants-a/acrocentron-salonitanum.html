Acrocentron salonitanum

Acrocentron salonitanum, commonly known as the Mediterranean water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in shallow waters and wetlands, where it forms dense mats on the surface. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged, with some floating leaves at the surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or white in color.

Acrocentron salonitanum plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
