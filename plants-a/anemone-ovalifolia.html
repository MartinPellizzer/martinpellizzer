Anemone ovalifolia

Anemone ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaved anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, oval leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Anemone ovalifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
