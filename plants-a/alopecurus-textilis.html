Alopecurus textilis

Alopecurus textilis, commonly known as hairy bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often hair-covered, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Alopecurus textilis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping for its fine texture and color.
