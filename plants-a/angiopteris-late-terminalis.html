Angiopteris late-terminalis

Angiopteris late-terminalis, commonly known as the late-terminal fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound fronds that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant has a distinctive morphology with a thick, creeping rhizome and bipinnate leaves that are often bright green and glossy. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The fronds are typically arranged in a spiral pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
