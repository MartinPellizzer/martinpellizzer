Atraphaxis frutescens var. papillosa

Atraphaxis frutescens var. papillosa, commonly known as the silky atraphaxis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This species is notable for its adaptability to harsh conditions and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
