Allophylus pachyphyllus

Allophylus pachyphyllus, commonly known as the thick-leaved alpine holly, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that is adapted to withstand harsh, high-altitude conditions. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that serve as a food source for various birds. Its leaves are thick and waxy, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

Allophylus pachyphyllus is often found in cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
