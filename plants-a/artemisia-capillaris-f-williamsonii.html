Artemisia capillaris f. williamsonii

Artemisia capillaris f. williamsonii, commonly known as Chinese wormwood, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray foliage and aromatic, pungent scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in panicles, typical of the genus Artemisia. Native to China, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

This ornamental plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in Chinese herbal medicine.
