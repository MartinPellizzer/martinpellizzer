Aconitum desoulavyi

Aconitum desoulavyi, commonly known as the Desoulavy aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, spikelike clusters. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, palmate structure.

Aconitum desoulavyi contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
