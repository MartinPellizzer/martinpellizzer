Achillea × wagneri

Achillea × wagneri, commonly known as the German yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray foliage and flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pinkish in color. Native to Europe, it thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring through early autumn.
