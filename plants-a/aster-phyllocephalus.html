Aster phyllocephalus

Aster phyllocephalus, commonly known as the leaf-headed aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust root systems. The plant features showy, violet-blue ray florets surrounded by dense, yellow disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention in arid environments.

Aster phyllocephalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
