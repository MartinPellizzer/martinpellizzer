Asphodelus viscidulus var. micranthus

Asphodelus viscidulus var. micranthus, commonly known as the sticky asphodel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall spikes of pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is noted for its resilience to drought and its ability to bloom in early spring.
