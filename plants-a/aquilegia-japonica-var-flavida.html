Aquilegia japonica var. flavida

Aquilegia japonica var. flavida, commonly known as the Japanese columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, with five petals and prominent sepals. The plant has compound, pinnate leaves that are finely divided, giving it a lacy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early spring bloom and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
