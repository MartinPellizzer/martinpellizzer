Antirrhinum pygmaeum

Antirrhinum pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf snapdragon, is a small flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features delicate, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive bilaterally symmetrical shape. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, making it a compact and ornamental species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
