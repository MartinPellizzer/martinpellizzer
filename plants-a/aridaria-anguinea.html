Aridaria anguinea

Aridaria anguinea, commonly known as the desert spurge, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its thick, fleshy leaves that store water. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Due to its resilience, it is often found in sandy soils and rocky habitats.
