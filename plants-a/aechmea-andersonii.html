Aechmea andersonii

Aechmea andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater to support its growth. The leaves are typically dark green with a waxy texture and often display intricate patterns of silver or white. It produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer or early fall.

This plant is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Morphology

Aechmea andersonii has rosette-shaped leaves that are narrow, sword-like, and arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem.

The leaves are typically dark green with a waxy texture and can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant has a short, thick stem that is covered in a dense, fibrous bark, and it grows primarily from the base. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by modified leaves called bracts, and they bloom in shades of pink and red during the summer months, pollinated by insects.

The fruit is a capsule that releases small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.
