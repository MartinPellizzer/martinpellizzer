Anisophyllea cuneata

Anisophyllea cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf anisophyllea, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its foliage is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its botanical significance lies primarily in its ecological role within its native habitat.
