Alsine villarii var. semiglandulosa

Alsine villarii var. semiglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular alsike, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have glandular dots, giving the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in fields.

The plant is annual or biennial and produces small, dry fruits that contain single seeds.
