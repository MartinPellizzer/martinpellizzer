Aristolochia sagittata

Aristolochia sagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure that resembles a pipe. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
