Anisophyllum leiospermum

Anisophyllum leiospermum, commonly known as the smooth-seeded anisophyllum, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, temperate climates, often found in woodland or shaded areas. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are smooth and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
