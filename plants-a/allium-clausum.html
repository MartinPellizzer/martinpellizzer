Allium clausum

Allium clausum, commonly known as the mountain garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, ovoid bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Allium clausum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
