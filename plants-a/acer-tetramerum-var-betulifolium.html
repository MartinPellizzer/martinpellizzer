Acer tetramerum var. betulifolium

Acer tetramerum var. betulifolium, commonly known as the paper birch, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its white, papery bark that peels in thin, vertical strips. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with serrated edges, turning yellow in the fall. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
