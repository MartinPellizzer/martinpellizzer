Agapetes xiana

Agapetes xiana, commonly known as the silverleaf agapete, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant is distinguished by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it its common name. It produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, edible berries.

Agapetes xiana thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high elevations in montane regions.
