Agelanthus flammeus

Agelanthus flammeus, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has a slender, upright stem with smooth, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are not only showy but also attract a variety of pollinators such as birds and insects.

Agelanthus flammeus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.
