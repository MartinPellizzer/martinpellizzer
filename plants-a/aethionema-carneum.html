Aethionema carneum

Aethionema carneum, commonly known as the pink rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically pink or reddish in color. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
