Anthemis petraea

Anthemis petraea, commonly known as the rock sandwort, is a flowering plant native to rocky and arid regions of southern Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in crevices and on rocky slopes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Anthemis petraea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature.
