Aquilegia vulgaris subsp. collina

Aquilegia vulgaris subsp.

collina, commonly known as the Collins' columbine, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features striking, spurred petals that come in shades of red, yellow, and orange, arranged in a distinctive spurred pattern.

The plant has palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in woodland areas.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
