Apteranthes europaea var. barrueliana

Apteranthes europaea var. barrueliana, commonly known as the European baruel's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, preferring well-drained environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are bell-shaped, with five petals, typically blue or violet in color, and are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early summer blooms.
