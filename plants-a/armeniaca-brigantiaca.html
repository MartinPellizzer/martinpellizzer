Armeniaca brigantiaca

Armeniaca brigantiaca, commonly known as the Armenian apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Armeniaca brigantiaca is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
