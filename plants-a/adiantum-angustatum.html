Adiantum angustatum

Adiantum angustatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to the eastern United States.

It features finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Adiantum angustatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
