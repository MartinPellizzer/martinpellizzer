Astragalus hoodianus

Astragalus hoodianus, commonly known as the Hoodian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus hoodianus is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
