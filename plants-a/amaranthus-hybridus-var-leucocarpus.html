Amaranthus hybridus var. leucocarpus

Amaranthus hybridus var. leucocarpus, commonly known as white amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, broad leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces fluffy, white to pinkish seed heads that are often used in ornamental settings. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

White amaranth is also valued for its nutritional properties, as its seeds are rich in protein and minerals.
