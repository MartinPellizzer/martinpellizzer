Acoridium abbreviatum

Acoridium abbreviatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, white to greenish flowers in spikes during the summer months. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
