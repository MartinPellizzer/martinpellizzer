Artabotrys aurantiacus var. aurantiacus

Artabotrys aurantiacus var. aurantiacus, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers emit a sweet, spicy scent, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.
