Ampelocalamus

Ampelocalamus, commonly known as the climbing reed, is a genus of grass-like plants native to Southeast Asia.

These plants are characterized by their tall, slender stems that often climb or trail along the ground. They possess narrow, grass-like leaves and produce small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Ampelocalamus species are typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands.

Their botanical features make them valuable for ornamental purposes and ecological restoration projects.
