Acaena platyacantha var. parvifolia

Acaena platyacantha var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved common nettle-tree, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinct central vein. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, spiny fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is often found in coastal and lowland areas, contributing to the local vegetation structure.
