Ailanthus guangxiensis

Ailanthus guangxiensis, commonly known as Guangxi tree-of-heaven, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Simarouba family (Simaroubaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces fruit that consists of a woody capsule containing several seeds. It is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its rapid growth and ecological impact, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in non-native regions.
