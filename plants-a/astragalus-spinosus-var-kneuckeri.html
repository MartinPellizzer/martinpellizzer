Astragalus spinosus var. kneuckeri

Astragalus spinosus var. kneuckeri, commonly known as the spiny milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow to greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and rocky habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in xeriscapes and dry gardens.
