Amaryllis barreirasa

Amaryllis barreirasa, commonly known as the Barreirasa amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to Brazil.

It produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in summer. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of fleshy, white bulbs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Amaryllis barreirasa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
