Angraecopsis elliptica

Angraecopsis elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant has narrow, lanceolate leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces a single spike of flowers that bloom in late summer. Angraecopsis elliptica is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and requires a cool, moist environment to thrive.
