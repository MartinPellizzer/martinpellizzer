Acalypha cardiophylla

Acalypha cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaf alyssum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Acalypha cardiophylla is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
