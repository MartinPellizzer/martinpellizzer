Angolluma huernioides

Angolluma huernioides, commonly known as the coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments.

Angolluma huernioides is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong coastal winds.
