Aesculus hippocastanum f. laciniata

Aesculus hippocastanum f. laciniata, commonly known as the laceleaf horse chestnut, is a cultivar of the horse chestnut tree characterized by its finely divided, lace-like leaves.

The foliage is typically a deep green with a velvety texture, creating a striking visual effect in gardens. This variety is prized for its ornamental value, particularly in spring when it produces showy white or pink flowers. The plant is relatively slow-growing and thrives in full sun to partial shade, preferring well-drained, fertile soil.

It is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and can tolerate urban environments with proper care.
