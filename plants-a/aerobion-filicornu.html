Aerobion filicornu

Aerobion filicornu, commonly known as the feather-tongued airplant, is a unique epiphytic species that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, filamentous structures that resemble feathery tendrils, aiding in water absorption and light capture. The plant lacks traditional roots and instead absorbs moisture and nutrients through its leaf-like structures. Its reproductive system involves the production of spores, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its specialized adaptations, Aerobion filicornu is often found in tropical and subtropical regions with high humidity.
