Allium remediorum

Allium remediorum, commonly known as garlic chives, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces bulbous structures underground, similar to garlic, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium remediorum is valued for its aromatic foliage and its potential health benefits, including antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.
