Asparagopsis juniperina

Asparagopsis juniperina, commonly known as the juniper asparagus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen, needle-like leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
