Anacardium pumilum var. petiolatum

Anacardium pumilum var. petiolatum, commonly known as the dwarf cashew or small-leaf cashew, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the poison ivy and mango. The plant is characterized by its small, elongated leaves with a glossy surface and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a drupe that contains a single seed, often used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible nuts.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential economic uses in horticulture and agriculture.
