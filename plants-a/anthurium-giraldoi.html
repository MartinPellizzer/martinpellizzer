Anthurium giraldoi

Anthurium giraldoi, commonly known as the Giraldo's flamingo flower, is a tropical plant native to Colombia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces showy, often red or orange, spadices that are surrounded by a colorful spathe, which resembles a petal. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium giraldoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouses.
