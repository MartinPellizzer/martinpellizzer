Artemisia gracillima

Artemisia gracillima, commonly known as slender wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray foliage and a strong, aromatic scent. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in late summer, contributing to its ornamental value.

Artemisia gracillima is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant in gardens.
