Aesculus sylvatica

Aesculus sylvatica, commonly known as the red buckeye, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its palmately compound leaves, which are typically divided into five to seven leaflets. The tree produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in upright clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a spiny capsule containing one to three glossy, chestnut-like seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for various wildlife species.
