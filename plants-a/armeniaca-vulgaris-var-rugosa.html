Armeniaca vulgaris var. rugosa

Armeniaca vulgaris var. rugosa, commonly known as the Chinese date or rugosa cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and showy pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for centuries for both its fruit and medicinal properties.
