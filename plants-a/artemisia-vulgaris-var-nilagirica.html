Artemisia vulgaris var. nilagirica

Artemisia vulgaris var. nilagirica, commonly known as Nilagiri wormwood, is a variant of the common wormwood plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and stems, are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and digestive properties.

This variety is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
