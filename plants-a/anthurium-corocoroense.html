Anthurium corocoroense

Anthurium corocoroense, commonly known as the "Coral Beauty," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and spathe. The plant features lush, dark green foliage with a velvety texture and emits a subtle, spicy fragrance. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with the spathe often displaying vibrant red or orange hues.

Anthurium corocoroense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded gardens.
