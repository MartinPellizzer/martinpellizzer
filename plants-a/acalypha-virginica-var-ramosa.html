Acalypha virginica var. ramosa

Acalypha virginica var. ramosa, commonly known as the hairy ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers surrounded by red bracts, creating an attractive visual display. Its leaves are ovate to lance-shaped with serrated edges and a hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
