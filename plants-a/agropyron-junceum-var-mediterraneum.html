Agropyron junceum var. mediterraneum

Agropyron junceum var. mediterraneum, commonly known as Mediterranean needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, spiky inflorescences. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in arid conditions. It is often found in open habitats such as slopes and wastelands.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
