Arisarum vulgare subsp. hastatum

Arisarum vulgare subsp.

hastatum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the end of its stems.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and is often used in woodland or shade gardens.
