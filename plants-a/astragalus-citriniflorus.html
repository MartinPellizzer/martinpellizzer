Astragalus citriniflorus

Astragalus citriniflorus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow, pea-like flowers and compound leaves with small leaflets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus citriniflorus is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stabilization.
