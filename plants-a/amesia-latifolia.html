Amesia latifolia

Amesia latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf amesia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Amesia latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
