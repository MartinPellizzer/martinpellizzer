Arctostaphylos viscosissima

Arctostaphylos viscosissima, commonly known as sticky madrone, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thick, sticky bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
