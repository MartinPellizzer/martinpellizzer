Agropyron intermedium var. latronum

Agropyron intermedium var. latronum, commonly known as the intermediate wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the central and northern regions of the United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, bluish-green leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for marginal lands. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in restoration ecology.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both agricultural and ecological purposes.
