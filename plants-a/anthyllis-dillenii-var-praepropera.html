Anthyllis dillenii var. praepropera

Anthyllis dillenii var. praepropera, commonly known as the creeping anthyllis, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
