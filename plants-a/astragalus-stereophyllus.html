Astragalus stereophyllus

Astragalus stereophyllus, commonly known as the tall milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by legume-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

Astragalus stereophyllus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
