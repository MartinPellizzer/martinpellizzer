Acacia sclerophylla var. pilosa

Acacia sclerophylla var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry foliage and yellow flowers that appear in cylindrical spikes. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are small, leathery, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
