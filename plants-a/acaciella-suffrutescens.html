Acaciella suffrutescens

Acaciella suffrutescens, commonly known as the red acacia, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, compound leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in erosion control and as a source of fuelwood in its native region.
