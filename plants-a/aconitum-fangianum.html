Aconitum fangianum

Aconitum fangianum, commonly known as Fang's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are compound, with palmate divisions, and are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Aconitum fangianum contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
