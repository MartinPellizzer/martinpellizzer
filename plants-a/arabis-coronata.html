Arabis coronata

Arabis coronata, commonly known as the crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 20 to 60 centimeters. The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white or yellow flowers with four petals. Its flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Arabis coronata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
