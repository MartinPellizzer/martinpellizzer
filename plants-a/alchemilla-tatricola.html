Alchemilla tatricola

Alchemilla tatricola, commonly known as the Tatricola lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are borne on slender, upright stems. Alchemilla tatricola thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for gardeners interested in native flora.
