Alaticaulia maduroi

Alaticaulia maduroi, commonly known as the Maduroi thornbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, woody fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and can withstand prolonged drought periods.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and for erosion control in degraded landscapes.
