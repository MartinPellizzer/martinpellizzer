Anthurium viridescens

Anthurium viridescens, commonly known as the Emerald Arrow, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are a deep green with a vibrant yellow midrib. The plant produces small, spadix flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red or orange. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Anthurium viridescens is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
