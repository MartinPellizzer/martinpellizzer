Ampherephis senegalensis

Ampherephis senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found on sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh conditions.

Ampherephis senegalensis is also valued for its potential in soil stabilization and as a source of traditional medicine.
