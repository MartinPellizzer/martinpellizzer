Allocasuarina campestris subsp. eriochlamys

Allocasuarina campestris subsp.

eriochlamys, commonly known as the coastal she-oak, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the stems.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in compact spikes, which are followed by woody, cone-like fruits. It thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found in coastal areas and dunes.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
