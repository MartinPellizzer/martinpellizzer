Aporum calceolum

Aporum calceolum, commonly known as the shoeplant, is a rare and striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, resembling a shoe, hence its common name.

Due to its unique appearance and endangered status, it is highly sought after by collectors and conservationists alike.
