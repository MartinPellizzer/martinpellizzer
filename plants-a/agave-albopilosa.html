Agave albopilosa

Agave albopilosa, commonly known as the white-haired agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The leaves are typically blue-green in color and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. It produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears yellowish-green flowers.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
