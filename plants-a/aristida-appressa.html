Aristida appressa

Aristida appressa, commonly known as slender dropseed, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in prairies, meadows, and disturbed areas.

Aristida appressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in native landscapes.
