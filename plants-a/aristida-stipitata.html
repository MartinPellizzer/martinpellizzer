Aristida stipitata

Aristida stipitata, commonly known as the slender threeawns, is a grass species native to various regions of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of three to five spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

Aristida stipitata is known for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.
