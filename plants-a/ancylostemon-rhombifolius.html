Ancylostemon rhombifolius

Ancylostemon rhombifolius, commonly known as the diamond-leaf cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and its distinctive, palm-like leaves that are shaped like rhombuses, giving it its common name. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones appearing on separate plants. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservatories for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
