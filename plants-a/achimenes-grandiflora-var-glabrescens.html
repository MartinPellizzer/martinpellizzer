Achimenes grandiflora var. glabrescens

Achimenes grandiflora var. glabrescens, commonly known as the velvet flower or glabrous achimenes, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
