Aristida vinosa

Aristida vinosa, commonly known as purple threeawn, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and spikelets that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its small, wind-dispersed seeds contribute to its ability to colonize disturbed areas.

Aristida vinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
