Austrocactus longicarpus

Austrocactus longicarpus, commonly known as the long-fruited cactus, is a species native to southern South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a small, columnar cactus characterized by its ribbed stems and spiny, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits. Austrocactus longicarpus is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
