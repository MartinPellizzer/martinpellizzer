Asplenium wardii

Asplenium wardii, commonly known as the African maidenhair fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appearance.

Asplenium wardii is relatively easy to cultivate, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
