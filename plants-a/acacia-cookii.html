Acacia cookii

Acacia cookii, commonly known as Cook's wattle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and feathery, yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in cylindrical clusters, which bloom during the autumn and winter seasons. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods that split open to release the seeds.

Acacia cookii is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and heathlands.
