Ardisia lamponga

Ardisia lamponga, commonly known as the red-fruited beauty, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Ericaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are attractive to birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Ardisia lamponga is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
