Agropyron × turcomanicum

Agropyron × turcomanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters, and its flowers are small and wind-pollinated. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well-suited for erosion control and grazing.

Agropyron × turcomanicum is valued for its hardiness and ability to stabilize soils in degraded landscapes.
