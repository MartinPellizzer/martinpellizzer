Avenastrum leve

Avenastrum leve, commonly known as the lesser wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features slender, branching stems covered with small, grey-green, aromatic leaves. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and appear in clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Avenastrum leve is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and antispasmodic properties.
