Armeria maritima subvar. confusa

Armeria maritima subvar. confusa, commonly known as the coastal thrift, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of North America.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

It is well adapted to salt spray and is often used in coastal landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
