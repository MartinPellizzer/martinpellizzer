Amomyrtella guilii

Amomyrtella guilii, commonly known as the Guilian cherry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

Amomyrtella guilii is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to tolerate partial shade.
