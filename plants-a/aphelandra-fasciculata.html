Aphelandra fasciculata

Aphelandra fasciculata, commonly known as the golden torch plant, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescences that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers, which resemble torches. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and glossy, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This plant is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its vibrant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
