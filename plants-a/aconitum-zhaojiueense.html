Aconitum zhaojiueense

Aconitum zhaojiueense, commonly known as Zhaojiue aconite, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy blue to violet flowers. The plant produces smooth, ovate leaves with a hairy underside, and its roots are tuberous and fleshy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a hazardous plant and should be handled with care.
