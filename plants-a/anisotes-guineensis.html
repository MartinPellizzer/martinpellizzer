Anisotes guineensis

Anisotes guineensis, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a climbing habit and features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance that enhances its nocturnal pollination strategy.

Anisotes guineensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and blooming behavior.
