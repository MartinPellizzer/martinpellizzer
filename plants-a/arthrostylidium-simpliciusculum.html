Arthrostylidium simpliciusculum

Arthrostylidium simpliciusculum, commonly known as the slender spikerush, is a small herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Australia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, thriving in environments with consistent hydration. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures. Its reproductive strategy relies on wind pollination, which is common among plants in its ecological niche.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
