Anemone hepatica f. magna

Anemone hepatica f. magna, commonly known as the large hepatica, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its early spring blooming habit. The plant features showy, three-lobed flowers in shades of white, pink, or blue, which open as the leaves unfurl. Its leaves are palmately divided and often have a distinctive waxy coating.

The large hepatica thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
