Amaranthus macrocaulos

Amaranthus macrocaulos, commonly known as red spinach, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or yellow, followed by triangular, bead-like seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and nutritional benefits.
