Allium ampeloprasum f. normale

Allium ampeloprasum f. normale, commonly known as the purple onion, is a botanical variant of the leek family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stalks and broad, flat leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, spherical bulbs that are typically purple in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible bulbs.

This variety is valued for its mild flavor and use in various culinary applications.
