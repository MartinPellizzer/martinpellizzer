Acacia ancistrocarpa

Acacia ancistrocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance. Its seeds are encased in hard pods, which release them when the pods dry and split open.

Acacia ancistrocarpa plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
