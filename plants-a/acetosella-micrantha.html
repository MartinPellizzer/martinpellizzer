Acetosella micrantha

Acetosella micrantha, commonly known as lesser sorrel, is a small herbaceous plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and open fields.

Acetosella micrantha is known for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor due to the presence of oxalic acid.
