Aethionema lycium

Aethionema lycium, commonly known as Armenian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces showy, pink to red flowers in spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

Aethionema lycium is also valued for its resilience to harsh climates and its ability to attract pollinators.
