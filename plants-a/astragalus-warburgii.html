Astragalus warburgii

Astragalus warburgii, commonly known as Warburg's milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from deeper underground.

Astragalus warburgii is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
