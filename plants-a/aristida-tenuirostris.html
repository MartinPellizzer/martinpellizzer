Aristida tenuirostris

Aristida tenuirostris, commonly known as slender beakgrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its scientific name reflects the slender, beak-like appearance of its seed heads.

Aristida tenuirostris is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
