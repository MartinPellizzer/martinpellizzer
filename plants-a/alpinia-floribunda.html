Alpinia floribunda

Alpinia floribunda, commonly known as the fragrant ginger or mountain ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, leafy stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
