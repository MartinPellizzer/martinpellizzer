Acriopsis emarginata

Acriopsis emarginata, commonly known as the fringed cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is typically herbaceous, with a sprawling growth habit and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Acriopsis emarginata is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
