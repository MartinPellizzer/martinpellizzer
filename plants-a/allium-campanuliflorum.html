Allium campanuliflorum

Allium campanuliflorum, commonly known as the bellflower garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. The plant belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives, and is characterized by its sulfur-containing compounds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
