Allium roseum var. majale

Allium roseum var. majale, commonly known as the Majale garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by bulbils that can be harvested for propagation. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a bulbous base that stores nutrients.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
