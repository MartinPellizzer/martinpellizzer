Astragalus allochrous var. playanus

Astragalus allochrous var. playanus, commonly known as the Playa Astragalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as playas and grasslands. It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
