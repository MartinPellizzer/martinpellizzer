Achillea clavennae f. corabensis

Achillea clavennae f. corabensis, commonly known as the Corsican yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in herbal medicine.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
