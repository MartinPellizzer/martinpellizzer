Asplenium moupinense

Asplenium moupinense, commonly known as the Moupin maidenhair fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble those of other maidenhair ferns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on rocky substrates or in forest understories. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, with a distinctive, narrow, and elongated shape.

Asplenium moupinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
