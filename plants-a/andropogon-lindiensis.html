Andropogon lindiensis

Andropogon lindiensis, commonly known as Lindi grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Africa.

It typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It is characterized by its robust root system and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
