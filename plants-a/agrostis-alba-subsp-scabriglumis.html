Agrostis alba subsp. scabriglumis

Agrostis alba subsp.

scabriglumis, commonly known as the scabrous bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough, scabrous texture.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and forage for various insects and small wildlife.
