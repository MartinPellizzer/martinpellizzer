Ardisia scalaris

Ardisia scalaris, commonly known as the clustered beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are a striking feature of the plant. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
