Aulomyrcia dealbata var. glaucescens

Aulomyrcia dealbata var. glaucescens, commonly known as the silverleaf aulomyrcia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a distinctive waxy coating that reflects light.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spherical, dry fruits.
