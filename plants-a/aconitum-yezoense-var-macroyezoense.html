Aconitum yezoense var. macroyezoense

Aconitum yezoense var. macroyezoense, commonly known as the Yezo Aconite, is a variant of the Aconitum genus native to Japan.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, spikelike clusters. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that are often hairy and have a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially sunny locations.
