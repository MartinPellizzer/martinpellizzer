Agathis dammara

Agathis dammara, commonly known as the white cedar, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araucariaceae and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces large, cone-like structures that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, red aril. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it important in traditional and modern construction.

The plant also plays a significant ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
