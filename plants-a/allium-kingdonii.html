Allium kingdonii

Allium kingdonii, commonly known as the Kingdon's garlic, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the island of Sardinia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes various edible and ornamental plants. The plant produces small, spherical bulbs that are often covered in a fibrous sheath. Its flowers are typically pale pink to white, arranged in a dense umbel.

Allium kingdonii is valued for its unique botanical features and is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution.
