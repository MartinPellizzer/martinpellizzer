Arthrostylidium ekmanii

Arthrostylidium ekmanii, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in southeastern Australia.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine, white hairs, and it produces small, white or pale green flowers. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients but can photosynthesize to some extent. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat the fleshy, red berries.

Arthrostylidium ekmanii plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by providing food for native wildlife.
