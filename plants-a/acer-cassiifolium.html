Acer cassiifolium

Acer cassiifolium, commonly known as the Persian maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves, which are typically lobed and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and arboreta.
