Aydendron argenteum

Aydendron argenteum, commonly known as the silver aydendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
