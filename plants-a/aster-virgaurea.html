Aster virgaurea

Aster virgaurea, commonly known as the common blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its blue to violet flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.
