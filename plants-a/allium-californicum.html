Allium californicum

Allium californicum, commonly known as the California garlic or Coast garlic, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are grass-like and a cluster of bulbils at the base of its flowering stem. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in a spherical inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and chaparral habitats.

Allium californicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some traditional herbal practices.
