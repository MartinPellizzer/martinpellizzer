Arctotis aspera var. aureola

Arctotis aspera var. aureola, commonly known as the golden arctotis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has hairy, rough-textured leaves and thrives in full sun with well-draining soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to attract pollinators.

This variety is relatively drought-tolerant and suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.
