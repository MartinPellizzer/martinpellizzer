Adenophora radiatifolia f. hirsuta

Adenophora radiatifolia f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy leaf bellflower, is a variant of the bellflower genus Adenophora.

It is characterized by its hairy foliage, which distinguishes it from other forms of the species. The plant typically features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Botanically, it is a perennial herb native to regions of East Asia, including China and Korea.
