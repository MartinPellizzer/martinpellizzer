Artemisia californica

Artemisia californica, commonly known as California sagebrush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves that contain essential oils, giving it a strong, pungent scent. The plant produces small, yellow flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
