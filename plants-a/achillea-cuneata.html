Achillea cuneata

Achillea cuneata, commonly known as narrowleaf yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinctive grayish-green color due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

The flowers, which bloom in summer, are white to pale pink and are arranged in flat-topped clusters.
