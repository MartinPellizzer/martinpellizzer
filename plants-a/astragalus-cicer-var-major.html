Astragalus cicer var. major

Astragalus cicer var. major, commonly known as the greater Persian clover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is known for its deep root system, which allows it to thrive in well-drained soils and arid conditions. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and adaptability.
