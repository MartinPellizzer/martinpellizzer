Aira caryophyllea var. divergens

Aira caryophyllea var. divergens, commonly known as the purple false oat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and striking purple to pink flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical structure reflects its classification within the grass family, Poaceae, with its rhizomatous growth and inflorescence morphology.
