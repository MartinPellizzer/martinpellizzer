Adonis ramosa

Adonis ramosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs.

Adonis ramosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate, early spring blooms.
