Asplenium auriculatum var. pimpinellifolium

Asplenium auriculatum var. pimpinellifolium, commonly known as the heart's-ear fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern.

It is characterized by its delicate, lanceolate leaves that are often curled and resemble the shape of a heart. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
