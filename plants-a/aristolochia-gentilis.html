Aristolochia gentilis

Aristolochia gentilis, commonly known as the elegant Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a dark purple or brown interior. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic, somewhat foul-smelling blooms. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value despite its toxic properties.
