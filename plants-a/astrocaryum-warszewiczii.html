Astrocaryum warszewiczii

Astrocaryum warszewiczii, commonly known as the Brazilian palm or buriti palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-orange fruits. The plant's trunk is covered in a thick, fibrous layer that provides structural support. The fruits are edible and have been traditionally used by indigenous communities for food and medicine.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
