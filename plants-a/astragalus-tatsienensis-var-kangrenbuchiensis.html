Astragalus tatsienensis var. kangrenbuchiensis

Astragalus tatsienensis var. kangrenbuchiensis, commonly known as the Kangrenbuchi milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to China, specifically found in the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, exhibiting resistance to cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, and it produces pods that contain small, rounded seeds.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
