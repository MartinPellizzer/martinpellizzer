Aphelandra dasyantha

Aphelandra dasyantha, commonly known as the velvetleaf torch plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, resembling torches. These flowers are pollinated primarily by nocturnal moths and bats.

Aphelandra dasyantha thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
