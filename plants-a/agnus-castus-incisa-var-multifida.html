Agnus-castus incisa var. multifida

Agnus-castus incisa var. multifida, commonly known as the multifid leaf agnus castus, is a variant of the chaste tree characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves.

It is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by orange berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to gardens and botanical collections.
