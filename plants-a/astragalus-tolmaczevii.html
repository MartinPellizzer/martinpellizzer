Astragalus tolmaczevii

Astragalus tolmaczevii, commonly known as Tolmaczev's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters, and it has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus tolmaczevii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
