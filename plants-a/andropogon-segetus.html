Andropogon segetus

Andropogon segetus, commonly known as foxtail millet, is a warm-season grass native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and distinctive panicles that resemble a fox's tail, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough texture, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers in the summer months.

Andropogon segetus is an important cereal crop in many parts of Asia and Africa due to its adaptability and nutritional value.
