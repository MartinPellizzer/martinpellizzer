Artemisia tilesii var. kotzebuensis

Artemisia tilesii var. kotzebuensis, commonly known as the Kotzebu's wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and grayish-green, finely divided leaves that are covered in dense, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicle-like clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats.

Artemisia tilesii var. kotzebuensis is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
