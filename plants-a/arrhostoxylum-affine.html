Arrhostoxylum affine

Arrhostoxylum affine, commonly known as the Andean cholla, is a shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, branching stems and reduced leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators. Its structure is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water.

It is often used in traditional medicine by local communities for its purported health benefits.
