Aquilegia × maruyamana

Aquilegia × maruyamana, commonly known as the Japanese columbine, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
