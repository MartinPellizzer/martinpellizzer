Armeria castellana

Armeria castellana, commonly known as the Castilian thrift, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Amaryllidaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are linear and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs.
