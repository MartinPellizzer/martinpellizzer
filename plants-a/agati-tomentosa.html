Agati tomentosa

Agati tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly agati, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, greyish foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

Agati tomentosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping.
