Acinodendron traillii

Acinodendron traillii, commonly known as the Traill's acacia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features such as thick, waxy leaves. Its root system helps in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Acinodendron traillii is often found in savanna and woodland habitats, playing a role in local ecosystems.
