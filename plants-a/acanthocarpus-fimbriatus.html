Acanthocarpus fimbriatus

Acanthocarpus fimbriatus, commonly known as the serrated burr grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive, spiny seed heads that form in clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

The species is notable for its ability to form dense stands, which can impact local vegetation dynamics.
