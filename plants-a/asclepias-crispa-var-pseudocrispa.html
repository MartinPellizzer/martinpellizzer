Asclepias crispa var. pseudocrispa

Asclepias crispa var. pseudocrispa, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers with distinctive horn-shaped structures called horns. Its leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a slight waxy coating.

This species is known for its role in supporting monarch butterfly populations by providing nectar and host plant for larvae.
