Apoda-prorepentia kateora

Apoda-prorepentia kateora, commonly known as the silverleaf mistplant, is a rare flowering species native to the subtropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-blue foliage and delicate, fragrant white blossoms that bloom during the wet season. The plant has a unique root system that allows it to absorb moisture from the dense, humid undergrowth. Its leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its moist but competitive environment.

Despite its beauty, it is critically endangered due to habitat destruction and overcollection for ornamental purposes.
