Acacia gerrardi

Acacia gerrardi, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia gerrardi is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
