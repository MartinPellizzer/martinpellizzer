Achyranthes thyrsiflora

Achyranthes thyrsiflora, commonly known as the Indian spinach or thorn apple, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, branched inflorescences, followed by small, round, spiny fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
