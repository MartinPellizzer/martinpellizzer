Acosta resupinata subsp. lagascae

Acosta resupinata subsp.

lagascae, commonly known as the Lagasca's acosta, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South America. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, clustered in dense inflorescences, and bloom during the cooler months. This subspecies is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a high degree of drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in studies related to plant adaptation and biodiversity in harsh climates.
