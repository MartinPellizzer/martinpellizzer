Amasonia erecta var. sylvatica

Amasonia erecta var. sylvatica, commonly known as the eastern wood lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, upright stems covered in lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to white flowers with a distinctive yellow throat, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Liliaceae family, sharing similar botanical characteristics.
