Acaena lucida var. glabratula

Acaena lucida var. glabratula, commonly known as the glabrous inkberry, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are an important food source for native birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its dense growth habit makes it suitable for erosion control and habitat restoration.
