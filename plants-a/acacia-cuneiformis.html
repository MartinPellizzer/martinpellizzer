Acacia cuneiformis

Acacia cuneiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a woody structure and thorny branches. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry regions.

Acacia cuneiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
