Apocynum pumilum var. cercidium

Apocynum pumilum var. cercidium, commonly known as the red stem dogbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, reddish stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

The plant contains cardiac glycosides, making it toxic to livestock and humans if ingested.
