Adenandra cuspidata var. villosa

Adenandra cuspidata var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy adenandra, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense, compact growth with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive hairy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires partial to full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
