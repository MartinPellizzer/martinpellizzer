Arachis paraguariensis subsp. capibarensis

Arachis paraguariensis subsp.

capibarensis, commonly known as the capibara peanut, is a wild species of peanut native to South America. It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is closely related to the cultivated peanut (Arachis hypogaea) and is often used in breeding programs to improve peanut varieties.

This subspecies is valued for its genetic diversity and potential contributions to agricultural sustainability.
