Athyrium petersenii

Athyrium petersenii, commonly known as the Petersen's Japanese Painted fern, is a species of fern native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often adorned with a pale, silvery underside. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its rhizomes are creeping and help it spread across the ground, forming dense colonies.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a key feature in its reproductive cycle.
