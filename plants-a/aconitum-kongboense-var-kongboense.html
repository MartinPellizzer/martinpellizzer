Aconitum kongboense var. kongboense

Aconitum kongboense var. kongboense, commonly known as the Bhutanese monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has dark green, compound leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It produces toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to handle or ingest.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
