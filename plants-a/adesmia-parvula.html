Adesmia parvula

Adesmia parvula, commonly known as the smallflower burrobrush, is a small shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Adesmia parvula plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals.
