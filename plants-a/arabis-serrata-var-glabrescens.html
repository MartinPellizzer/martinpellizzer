Arabis serrata var. glabrescens

Arabis serrata var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in alpine and subalpine environments. Its leaves are pinnately lobed and often covered with a fine layer of hairs, which become less prominent as the plant matures.

The flowers are usually white or pale purple, blooming in early spring and attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
