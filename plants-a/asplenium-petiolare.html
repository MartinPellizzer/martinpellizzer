Asplenium petiolare

Asplenium petiolare, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided leaves.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a distinctive, feather-like appearance.

Asplenium petiolare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
