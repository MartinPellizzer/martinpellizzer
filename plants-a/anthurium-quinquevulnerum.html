Anthurium quinquevulnerum

Anthurium quinquevulnerum, commonly known as the five-wound anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped spathes that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a contrasting midrib. The plant produces small, spadix-like inflorescences that are typically hidden within the spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium quinquevulnerum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
