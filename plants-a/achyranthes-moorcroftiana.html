Achyranthes moorcroftiana

Achyranthes moorcroftiana, commonly known as the Moorcroft's smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
