Atriplex eardleyae

Atriplex eardleyae, commonly known as the Eardley's saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to saline soils and can thrive in harsh, coastal conditions.

Atriplex eardleyae plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
