Archibaccharis androgyna

Archibaccharis androgyna, commonly known as the Androgyna holly, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, dark green leaves that are often glossy in appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping and wildlife habitat.
