Astragalus creticus var. boissieri

Astragalus creticus var. boissieri, commonly known as the Cretan milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

This variant is distinguished by its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
