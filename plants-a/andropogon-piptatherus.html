Andropogon piptatherus

Andropogon piptatherus, commonly known as the tallgrass bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairie and woodland habitats. Andropogon piptatherus plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its seeds are also an important food source for birds and small mammals.
