Armeria pseudarmeria subsp. castellana

Armeria pseudarmeria subsp.

castellana, commonly known as the Castellana thrift, is a low-growing evergreen perennial native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features compact, dense clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has linear, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.

This subspecies is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
