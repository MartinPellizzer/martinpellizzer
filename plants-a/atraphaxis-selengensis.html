Atraphaxis selengensis

Atraphaxis selengensis, commonly known as the Selengian sandburs, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and small, spiny fruits. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating high drought tolerance. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces burrs that aid in seed dispersal.

Atraphaxis selengensis is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.
