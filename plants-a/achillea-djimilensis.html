Achillea djimilensis

Achillea djimilensis, commonly known as the Djimilensis Yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Morocco.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that typically range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant features finely divided, silver-gray foliage that is aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its historical use in herbal remedies highlights its significance in both botanical and cultural contexts.
