Actinodaphne quinqueflora

Actinodaphne quinqueflora, commonly known as the five-flowered laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are typically followed by dark purple, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical gardens and traditional medicine.
