Annona triloba

Annona triloba, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is a small tree or shrub that produces a fleshy, aromatic fruit with a yellow or green exterior. The fruit contains a white, creamy pulp that is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, custard-like flavor. The plant has simple, alternate leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and has traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
