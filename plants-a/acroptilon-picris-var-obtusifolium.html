Acroptilon picris var. obtusifolium

Acroptilon picris var. obtusifolium, commonly known as the obtuse-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features basal rosettes of broadly ovate to obtuse leaves, which are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems rise from the center of the rosette, bearing yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

It is adapted to rocky and arid environments, often found in alpine meadows and dry slopes.
