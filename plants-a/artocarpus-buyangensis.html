Artocarpus buyangensis

Artocarpus buyangensis, commonly known as the Bornean jackfruit.

It is a tropical tree native to the island of Borneo, characterized by its large, spiky fruits that mature to a yellowish-green color. The plant belongs to the Moraceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates. Its leaves are large and palmate, while its flowers are unisexual and arranged in clusters.
