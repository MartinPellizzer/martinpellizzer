Acacia anarthros

Acacia anarthros, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of small, needle-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Acacia anarthros is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
