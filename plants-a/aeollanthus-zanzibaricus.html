Aeollanthus zanzibaricus

Aeollanthus zanzibaricus, commonly known as the Zanzibar sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, adapting to the arid environment.

Aeollanthus zanzibaricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
