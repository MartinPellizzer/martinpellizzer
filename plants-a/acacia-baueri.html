Acacia baueri

Acacia baueri, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grey-green branches and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow globular flowers, which are clustered in dense heads, and it is often used for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance. Acacia baueri is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

It also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
