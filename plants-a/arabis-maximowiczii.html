Arabis maximowiczii

Arabis maximowiczii, commonly known as the Siberian rockcress, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia, including regions of Russia, China, and Korea.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants, and is characterized by its small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Arabis maximowiczii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardiness in cooler climates.
