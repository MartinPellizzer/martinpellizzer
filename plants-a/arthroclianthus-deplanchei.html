Arthroclianthus deplanchei

Arthroclianthus deplanchei, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
