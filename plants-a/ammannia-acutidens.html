Ammannia acutidens

Ammannia acutidens, commonly known as prairie ammannia, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered pink or white flowers. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in moist to wet soils.

Ammannia acutidens is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing wetland habitats.
