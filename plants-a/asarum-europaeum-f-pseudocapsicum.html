Asarum europaeum f. pseudocapsicum

Asarum europaeum f. pseudocapsicum, commonly known as the European wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in a spathaceous structure, followed by dark, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
