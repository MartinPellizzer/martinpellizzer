Amphorkis inermis

Amphorkis inermis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and small, pale green or white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or undergrowth. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces flowers that are often clustered in spikes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
