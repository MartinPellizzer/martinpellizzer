Anguillaria indica

Anguillaria indica, commonly known as the Indian arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking spadix inflorescence. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands and along riverbanks. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a starch source.

Anguillaria indica is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
