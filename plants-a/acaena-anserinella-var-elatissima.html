Acaena anserinella var. elatissima

Acaena anserinella var. elatissima, commonly known as the giant New Zealand burr, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous, hairy husk. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas. Its foliage consists of alternate, simple, and ovate leaves with a rough, hairy texture.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
