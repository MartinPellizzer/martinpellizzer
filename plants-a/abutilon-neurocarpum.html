Abutilon neurocarpum

Abutilon neurocarpum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Abutilon neurocarpum is often found in disturbed habitats and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
