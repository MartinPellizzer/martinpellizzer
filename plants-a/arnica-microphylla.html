Arnica microphylla

Arnica microphylla, commonly known as narrow-leaved arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Arnica microphylla is known for its medicinal properties, with the roots and flowers used in traditional herbal remedies.
