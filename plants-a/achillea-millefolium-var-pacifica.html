Achillea millefolium var. pacifica

Achillea millefolium var. pacifica, commonly known as Pacific yarrow, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of North America.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, lacey appearance. The plant produces flat, composite flower heads in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological gardens.
