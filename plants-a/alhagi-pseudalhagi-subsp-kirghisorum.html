Alhagi pseudalhagi subsp. kirghisorum

Alhagi pseudalhagi subsp.

kirghisorum, commonly known as the Kirghiz alhagi, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
