Arabis inamoena var. acutata

Arabis inamoena var. acutata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive sharp tip, giving it its common name.

It produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
