Anemone alpina var. sulphurea

Anemone alpina var. sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately divided, with a hairy texture, and its stems are usually unbranched. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is notable for its striking color and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
