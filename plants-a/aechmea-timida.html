Aechmea timida

Aechmea timida, commonly known as the silver vase plant, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, silver-tipped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aechmea timida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
