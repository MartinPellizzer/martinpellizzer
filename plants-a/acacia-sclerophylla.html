Acacia sclerophylla

Acacia sclerophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Acacia sclerophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping in dry climates.
