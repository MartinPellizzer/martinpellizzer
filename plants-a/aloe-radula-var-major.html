Aloe radula var. major

Aloe radula var. major, commonly known as the giant rock aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellowish-orange flowers, which are pollinated by insects. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
