Amaryllis aviflora

Amaryllis aviflora, commonly known as the bird-of-paradise plant, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its showy, bird-like inflorescences that resemble a parrot's beak. The plant features strap-shaped, grass-like leaves and produces vibrant, colorful flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Amaryllis aviflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown in gardens or as a container plant.
