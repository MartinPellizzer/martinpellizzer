Achyranthes paronychioides

Achyranthes paronychioides, commonly known as the Indian knotgrass, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Achyranthes paronychioides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.
