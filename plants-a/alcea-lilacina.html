Alcea lilacina

Alcea lilacina, commonly known as the purple hollyhock, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. The flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems, and the leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and cottage gardens.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
