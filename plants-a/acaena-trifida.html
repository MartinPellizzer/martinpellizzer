Acaena trifida

Acaena trifida, commonly known as the New Zealand bramble, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits known as achenes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Acaena trifida is often found in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
