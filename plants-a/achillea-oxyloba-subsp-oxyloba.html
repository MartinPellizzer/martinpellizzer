Achillea oxyloba subsp. oxyloba

Achillea oxyloba subsp.

oxyloba, commonly known as the Alpine yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the high alpine regions of Europe. It features a compact, cushion-like growth habit with silver-gray foliage and small, daisy-like white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant is characterized by its finely divided, aromatic leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments.
