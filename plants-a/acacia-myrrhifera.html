Acacia myrrhifera

Acacia myrrhifera, commonly known as the gum acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, feathery leaves that reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by long, woody pods containing seeds. Its bark and leaves are sources of gum and resin, which have been used traditionally for medicinal and industrial purposes.

Acacia myrrhifera is well adapted to drought, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
