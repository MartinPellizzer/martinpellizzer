Acalypha inselbergensis

Acalypha inselbergensis, commonly known as the inselberg acalypha, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often variegated foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and can withstand periods of drought.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
