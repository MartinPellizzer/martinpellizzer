Aspidium pachychlamys

Aspidium pachychlamys, commonly known as the broad-leaf water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are typically divided into numerous pinnae, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are clustered in sori.

Aspidium pachychlamys is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
