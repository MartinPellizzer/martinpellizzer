Acrolobus schoenleinii

Acrolobus schoenleinii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow composite flowers. The plant is herbaceous, growing close to the ground, and is often found in grassy or disturbed areas. Its flowers develop into spherical, bristly seed heads that are dispersed by the wind.

Acrolobus schoenleinii is adapted to arid conditions and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
