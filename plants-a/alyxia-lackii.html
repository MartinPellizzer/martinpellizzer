Alyxia lackii

Alyxia lackii, commonly known as the Hawaiian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

The plant attaches itself to the branches of host trees, such as the ʻōhiʻa lehua, through its haustoria to extract water and nutrients. Alyxia lackii produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for native birds and insects.
