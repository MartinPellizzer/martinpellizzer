Acacia proiantha

Acacia proiantha, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules. It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, and it plays an important role in stabilizing dunes.

The golden wattle is also the floral emblem of Australia, symbolizing the country's natural heritage.
