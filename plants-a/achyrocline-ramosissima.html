Achyrocline ramosissima

Achyrocline ramosissima, commonly known as the West Indian lilac, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural and traditional uses.
