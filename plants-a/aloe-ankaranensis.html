Aloe ankaranensis

Aloe ankaranensis, commonly known as the Ankaranensis Aloe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy. The leaves are typically green with a waxy coating, which helps the plant retain moisture in its arid habitat. It produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear yellowish-green flowers, adding ornamental value to the plant.

This species is valued for its resilience and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.
