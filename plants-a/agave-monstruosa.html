Agave monstruosa

Agave monstruosa, commonly known as the giant agave, is a striking succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, spiky leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length, forming a rosette shape. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears numerous yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its thick, fleshy leaves store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments.

Agave monstruosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional practices.
