Aconitum linnaeanum

Aconitum linnaeanum, commonly known as the Linnaean monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically green with a hairy texture. Aconitum linnaeanum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
