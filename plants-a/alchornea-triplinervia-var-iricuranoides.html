Alchornea triplinervia var. iricuranoides

Alchornea triplinervia var. iricuranoides, commonly known as the Iricuranoides variant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne in the axils of the leaves. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
