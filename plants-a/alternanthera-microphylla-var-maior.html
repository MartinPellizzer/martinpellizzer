Alternanthera microphylla var. maior

Alternanthera microphylla var. maior, commonly known as red flame or red velvet, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, bright red foliage that becomes even more intense under strong lighting conditions. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving them a velvety texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring warm, nutrient-rich water.

This variety is popular in aquariums for its vibrant color and ease of care.
