Ardisia erythroxyloides

Ardisia erythroxyloides, commonly known as the red-flowered ardisia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, red berries that appear in the autumn. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The berries are an important food source for various bird species.
