Asperula oppositifolia subsp. pseudocynanchica

Asperula oppositifolia subsp.

pseudocynanchica, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or false ivy, is a flowering plant native to East Asia. It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers.

The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its foliage is dense and leathery, providing ornamental value in gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its use in traditional medicine.
