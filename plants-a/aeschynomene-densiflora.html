Aeschynomene densiflora

Aeschynomene densiflora, commonly known as hairy lespedeza, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Aeschynomene densiflora is also known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common sight in forest understories.
