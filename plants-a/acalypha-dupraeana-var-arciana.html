Acalypha dupraeana var. arciana

Acalypha dupraeana var. arciana, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to tropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins that contrast vividly against its dark green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
