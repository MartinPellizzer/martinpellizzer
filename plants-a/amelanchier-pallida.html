Amelanchier pallida

Amelanchier pallida, commonly known as the white serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a red or purple color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental and wildlife gardens.

Amelanchier pallida is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
