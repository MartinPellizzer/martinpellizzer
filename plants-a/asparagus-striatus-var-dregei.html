Asparagus striatus var. dregei

Asparagus striatus var. dregei, commonly known as the Desert Asparagus, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to small scales, and it has a tendency to form clumps over time.

This species is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
