Arctostaphylos parryana var. pinetorum

Arctostaphylos parryana var. pinetorum, commonly known as the Sierra buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, leathery leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are typically white or pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is closely related to other manzanita species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
