Alcea striata subsp. rufescens

Alcea striata subsp.

rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed hollyhock, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems that often display a reddish hue, especially when mature.

The plant produces showy, pink to rose-colored flowers in cone-like clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
