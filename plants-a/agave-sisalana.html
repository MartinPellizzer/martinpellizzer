Agave sisalana

Agave sisalana, commonly known as sisal agave, is a robust perennial plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, spiny leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant is cultivated for its fibrous leaves, which are processed into durable fibers used in textiles and rope-making. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it adapted to arid environments.

The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that eventually leads to the formation of new plants through seed dispersal.
