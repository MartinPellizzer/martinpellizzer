Aster lucidus

Aster lucidus, commonly known as the shining aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, white to lavender-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Aster lucidus blooms in late summer to early fall, adding delicate beauty to its natural environment.
