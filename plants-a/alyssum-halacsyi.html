Alyssum halacsyi

Alyssum halacsyi, commonly known as the Hungarian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features hairy, narrow leaves and produces capsules as its fruiting structure. Alyssum halacsyi thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its prolonged blooming period, which can extend from spring to early autumn.
