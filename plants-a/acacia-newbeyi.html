Acacia newbeyi

Acacia newbeyi, commonly known as the Newbey acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its survival in harsh environments.

Acacia newbeyi plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
