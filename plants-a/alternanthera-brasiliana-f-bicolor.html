Alternanthera brasiliana f. bicolor

Alternanthera brasiliana f. bicolor, commonly known as the red-leaf alternanthera, is a strikingly colorful foliage plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant bicolor leaves, which typically feature a deep red or purple hue on the upper surface and a contrasting green underside. The plant is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in aquatic and terrestrial settings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its rapid growth and ability to produce small, inconspicuous flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners and aquascapers alike.
