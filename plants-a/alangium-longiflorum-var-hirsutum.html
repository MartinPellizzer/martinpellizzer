Alangium longiflorum var. hirsutum

Alangium longiflorum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy alangium, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
