Acalypha hirsutissima

Acalypha hirsutissima, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy bracts that resemble petals, often in shades of red, pink, or yellow. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Acalypha hirsutissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.
