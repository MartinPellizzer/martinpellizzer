Allocarya figurata

Allocarya figurata, commonly known as the fig-leaf tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble a fig leaf, hence its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by legumes containing edible seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its ability to provide shade in warm climates.
