Artemisia tridentata subsp. typica

Artemisia tridentata subsp.

typica, commonly known as sagebrush, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-green foliage and small, yellowish-gray flowers.

The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often thriving in sandy or rocky soils. It contains volatile oils that contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

Sagebrush plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
