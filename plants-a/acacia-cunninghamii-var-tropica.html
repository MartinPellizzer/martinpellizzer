Acacia cunninghamii var. tropica

Acacia cunninghamii var. tropica, commonly known as the tropical wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow, globular flower heads that appear in spring. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and sandy soils. Its rapid growth and hardy nature make it suitable for erosion control and ornamental planting.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
