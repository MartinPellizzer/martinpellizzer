Alsine latifolia

Alsine latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf sandparsley, is a herbaceous annual plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has alternate, broad, ovate leaves with a hairy surface, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its seeds are small and dry, adapted for wind dispersal.

Alsine latifolia is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, showing some drought tolerance.
