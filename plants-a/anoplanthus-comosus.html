Anoplanthus comosus

Anoplanthus comosus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
