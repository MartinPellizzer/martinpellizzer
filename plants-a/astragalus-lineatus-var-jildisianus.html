Astragalus lineatus var. jildisianus

Astragalus lineatus var. jildisianus, commonly known as the Jildisian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This variant is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
