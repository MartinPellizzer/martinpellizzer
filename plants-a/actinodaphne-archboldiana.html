Actinodaphne archboldiana

Actinodaphne archboldiana, commonly known as the Archbold's camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in lowland forests and is known for its dense, leathery foliage. It produces small, white flowers followed by oval-shaped berries.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and has ecological significance in its native habitat.
