Acinodendron carneum

Acinodendron carneum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and can grow up to 30 meters tall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces woody, fleshy fruits.

The tree is valued for its dense, aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of incense.
