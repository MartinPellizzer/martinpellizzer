Acacia quiterajoensis

Acacia quiterajoensis, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Acacia quiterajoensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
