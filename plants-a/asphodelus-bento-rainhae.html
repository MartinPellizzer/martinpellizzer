Asphodelus bento-rainhae

Asphodelus bento-rainhae, commonly known as the Bento-Rainha asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Portugal.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and tall, erect flowering stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal climates with moderate rainfall.

Asphodelus bento-rainhae is often found in dunes and is an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
