Aeschynanthus hispidus

Aeschynanthus hispidus, commonly known as the hairy whorled flower.

It is a flowering plant native to western Africa, characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its striking floral display.
