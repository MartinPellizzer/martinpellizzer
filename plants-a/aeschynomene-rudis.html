Aeschynomene rudis

Aeschynomene rudis, commonly known as smallleaf aeschynomene, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by lens-shaped pods containing several seeds. Aeschynomene rudis is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some traditional medicinal practices.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
