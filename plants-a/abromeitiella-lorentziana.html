Abromeitiella lorentziana

Abromeitiella lorentziana, commonly known as the Lorentziana dandelion, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in dry, open habitats. It has a taproot system and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
