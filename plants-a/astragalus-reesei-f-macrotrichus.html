Astragalus reesei f. macrotrichus

Astragalus reesei f. macrotrichus, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine and woodland ecosystems.
