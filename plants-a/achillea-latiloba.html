Achillea latiloba

Achillea latiloba, commonly known as narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and flat-topped clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained, sunny soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.

Achillea latiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
