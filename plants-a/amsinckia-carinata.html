Amsinckia carinata

Amsinckia carinata, commonly known as the hooked leaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinct central ridge. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky soils.
