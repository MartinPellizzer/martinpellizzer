Agastache mexicana

Agastache mexicana, commonly known as Mexican hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often aromatic and used in traditional herbal remedies.

Agastache mexicana thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.
