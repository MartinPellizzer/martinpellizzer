Aspalathus rostrata

Aspalathus rostrata, commonly known as the rostrata bush or sweet bush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.
