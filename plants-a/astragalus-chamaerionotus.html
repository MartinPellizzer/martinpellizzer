Astragalus chamaerionotus

Astragalus chamaerionotus, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, yellow flowers in late spring. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
