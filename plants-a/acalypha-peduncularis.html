Acalypha peduncularis

Acalypha peduncularis, commonly known as the red-hot poker or fireplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky red bracts that surround small yellow flowers, creating a vivid visual display. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Acalypha peduncularis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
