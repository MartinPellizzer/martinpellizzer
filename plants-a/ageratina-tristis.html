Ageratina tristis

Ageratina tristis, commonly known as white cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and is often found in dry, disturbed soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Ageratina tristis is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
