Anthericum laxum

Anthericum laxum, commonly known as the looseleaf arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a distinctive, arrow-shaped leaf with a broad, undulating margin. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a showy, white to greenish spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Anthericum laxum is often found in marshes, fens, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in humid conditions.
