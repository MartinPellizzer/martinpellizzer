Acacia enervia subsp. explicata

Acacia enervia subsp.

explicata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, bipinnate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in ecological restoration projects.
