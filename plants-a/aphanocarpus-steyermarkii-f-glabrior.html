Aphanocarpus steyermarkii f. glabrior

Aphanocarpus steyermarkii f. glabrior, commonly known as the glabrous form of the genus Aphanocarpus, is a member of the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, glabrous (smooth) leaves and distinctive floral structure. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, and it produces small, indehiscent pods.

This variant is notable for its reduced pubescence, which distinguishes it from other forms within the species.
