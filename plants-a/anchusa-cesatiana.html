Anchusa cesatiana

Anchusa cesatiana, commonly known as the Sicilian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
