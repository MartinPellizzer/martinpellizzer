Aralia cookii

Aralia cookii, commonly known as the Cook's aralia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, and its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange in color, containing several seeds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
