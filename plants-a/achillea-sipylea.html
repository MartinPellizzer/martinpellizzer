Achillea sipylea

Achillea sipylea, commonly known as the Sipylean yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, while its flowers are white to pale pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

Achillea sipylea is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
