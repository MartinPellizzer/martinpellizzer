Arundinaria cannavieira

Arundinaria cannavieira, commonly known as bamboo grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in panicles, and it relies on wind pollination. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species.

Arundinaria cannavieira is valued for its durability and flexibility, which have made it useful in traditional crafts and construction.
