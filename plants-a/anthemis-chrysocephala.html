Anthemis chrysocephala

Anthemis chrysocephala, commonly known as the goldenhead, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of yellow disk florets, creating a striking appearance. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats and has a compact, herbaceous growth form.

The leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
