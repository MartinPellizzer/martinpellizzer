Amblynotus obovatus

Amblynotus obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed amblynotus, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of thick, waxy, obovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are rich in bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.
