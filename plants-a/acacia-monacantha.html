Acacia monacantha

Acacia monacantha, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-resistant. Its wood is hard and durable, historically used by Indigenous Australians for tool-making.

The species plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
