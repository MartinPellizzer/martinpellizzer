Asparagus lucidus

Asparagus lucidus, commonly known as the bright asparagus or silver asparagus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asparagaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Asparagus lucidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
