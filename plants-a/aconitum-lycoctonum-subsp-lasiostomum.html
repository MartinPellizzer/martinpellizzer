Aconitum lycoctonum subsp. lasiostomum

Aconitum lycoctonum subsp.

lasiostomum, commonly known as the Himalayan aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple sparsely hairy flowers.

The plant features a stout, upright stem with alternate, palmately lobed leaves that are typically large and deeply cut. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a poisonous plant and requires careful handling.
