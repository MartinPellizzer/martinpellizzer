Arnica louiseana subsp. griscomii

Arnica louiseana subsp.

griscomii, commonly known as the Gray's arnica, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its medicinal properties.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
