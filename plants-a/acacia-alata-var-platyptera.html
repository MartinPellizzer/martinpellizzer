Acacia alata var. platyptera

Acacia alata var. platyptera, commonly known as the flat-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features slender, often spindly stems with small, flat, green leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces yellow, fluffy inflorescences that bloom during the warmer months, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system for water retention.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
