Agropyron intermedium subsp. banaticum

Agropyron intermedium subsp.

banaticum, commonly known as the Banat wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

The plant produces spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple, and its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and is used in ecological restoration projects.
