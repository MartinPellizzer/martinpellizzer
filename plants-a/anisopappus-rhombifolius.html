Anisopappus rhombifolius

Anisopappus rhombifolius, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its flowering stems are usually branched and bear numerous florets arranged in cylindrical heads.

Anisopappus rhombifolius is adapted to dry, open habitats and is often found in prairies and along roadsides.
