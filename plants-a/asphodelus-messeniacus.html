Asphodelus messeniacus

Asphodelus messeniacus, commonly known as the Messenian asphodel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its sword-shaped leaves and tall, branched inflorescences. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Asphodelus messeniacus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Greek culture.
