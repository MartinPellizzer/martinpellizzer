Aloysia virgata var. arguentedentata

Aloysia virgata var. arguentedentata, commonly known as the silverleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is drought-tolerant. It has small, ovate leaves with a fuzzy texture and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
