Asemeia mollis

Asemeia mollis, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to purple, typically blooming in the early morning. Asemeia mollis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in arid and semi-arid regions.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
