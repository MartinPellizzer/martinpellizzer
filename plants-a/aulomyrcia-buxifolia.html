Aulomyrcia buxifolia

Aulomyrcia buxifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf holly, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that is typically a glossy dark green with a waxy texture. The leaves are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly leathery feel. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
