Arabis kamchatica var. intermedia

Arabis kamchatica var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of glossy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
