Aloe fasciata var. major

Aloe fasciata var. major, commonly known as the Zebra Aloe, is a striking succulent native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray with distinct white or yellowish markings, resembling a zebra's stripes. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and indoor settings.
