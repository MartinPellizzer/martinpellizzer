Acacia delavayi

Acacia delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow, globular flower heads. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Acacia delavayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
