Anthemis gayana var. rosea

Anthemis gayana var. rosea, commonly known as the roseate everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its foliage is covered in fine, hairy leaves that give it a soft, textured appearance.

The flowers are known for their long-lasting nature, making it a popular choice for dried flower arrangements.
