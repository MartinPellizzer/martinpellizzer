Aconitum hemsleyanum var. circinatum

Aconitum hemsleyanum var. circinatum, commonly known as the Hemsley's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features a stout, upright stem with large, palmately compound leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and kept away from children and pets.
