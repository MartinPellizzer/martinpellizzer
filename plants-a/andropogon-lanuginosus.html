Andropogon lanuginosus

Andropogon lanuginosus, commonly known as the silky grass or silky bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring to early summer.

Andropogon lanuginosus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
