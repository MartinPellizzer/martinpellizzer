Ageratina zinniifolia

Ageratina zinniifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy blue-gray inflorescences that resemble a misty cloud, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

Ageratina zinniifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in USDA hardiness zones 6 to 9.
