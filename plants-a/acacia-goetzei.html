Acacia goetzei

Acacia goetzei, commonly known as the Goetze's wattle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Acacia goetzei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
