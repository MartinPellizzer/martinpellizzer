Aconitum bakeri

Aconitum bakeri, commonly known as Baker's monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant is characterized by its striking purple or blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a distinctive appearance.

Aconitum bakeri contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to handle or ingest.
