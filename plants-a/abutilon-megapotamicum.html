Abutilon megapotamicum

Abutilon megapotamicum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Brazilian abutilon, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fibrous stems and leaves have been used in traditional medicine and for textile production.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
