Acrophorus hookeri

Acrophorus hookeri, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, hairy texture.
