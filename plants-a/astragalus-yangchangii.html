Astragalus yangchangii

Astragalus yangchangii, commonly known as the Yangchang milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Astragalus yangchangii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and deep root systems.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
