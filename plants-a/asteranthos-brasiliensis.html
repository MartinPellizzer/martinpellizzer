Asteranthos brasiliensis

Asteranthos brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian aster, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its showy, star-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Asteranthos brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
