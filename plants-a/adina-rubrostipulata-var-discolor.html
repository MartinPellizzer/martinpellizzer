Adina rubrostipulata var. discolor

Adina rubrostipulata var. discolor, commonly known as the red-stippled adina, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red-veined leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are typically oval with prominent reddish markings along the veins.
