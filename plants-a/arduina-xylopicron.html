Arduina xylopicron

Arduina xylopicron, commonly known as the crystalwood fern, is a rare and striking plant native to the misty highlands of the Andes.

It features intricate, lace-like fronds that shimmer with a faint iridescence, giving it a surreal appearance. The plant's unique structure consists of a central woody stem surrounded by delicate, spiraling leaves that resemble woven glass. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires specific soil conditions rich in minerals.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is highly sought after by botanists and collectors alike.
