Antirrhinum villosum

Antirrhinum villosum, commonly known as the hairy snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bright orange or red tubular flowers. The leaves are narrow and covered in fine, hairy glands, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
