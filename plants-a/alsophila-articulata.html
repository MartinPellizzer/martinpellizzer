Alsophila articulata

Alsophila articulata, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound fronds that grow in a rosette pattern. The plant has a unique ability to break apart at the nodes, allowing new growth to form from the base, which contributes to its distinctive appearance. Its fronds are typically dark green and can reach lengths of up to two meters.

Alsophila articulata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
