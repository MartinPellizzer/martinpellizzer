Argophyllum verae

Argophyllum verae, commonly known as the silverleaf acanthus, is a perennial shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, spiky inflorescences with small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, Argophyllum verae is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
