Aerva sanguinea

Aerva sanguinea, commonly known as red aerva, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually pink or red in color.

Aerva sanguinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
