Astracantha cruentiflora

Astracantha cruentiflora, commonly known as the bloodflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small, clustered bells. The plant has spiny stems and leaves, which contribute to its rugged appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant is known for its resilience in harsh climates.
