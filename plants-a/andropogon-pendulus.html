Andropogon pendulus

Andropogon pendulus, commonly known as the hanging grass or pendulous bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and is often found in marshes and along stream banks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue.

Andropogon pendulus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
