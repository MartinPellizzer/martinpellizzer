Apocynum mexicanum

Apocynum mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican dogbane, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, which includes many plants with milky sap and showy flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually white or pink, clustered in umbels, and bloom in the summer months.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while the fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds.
