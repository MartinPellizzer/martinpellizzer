Adenolisianthus arboreus

Adenolisianthus arboreus, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a striking tropical tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has a robust, upright trunk and can grow up to 20 meters in height, making it a prominent feature in its natural habitat. Its leaves are compound, with long, slender leaflets that give it a feathery appearance.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
