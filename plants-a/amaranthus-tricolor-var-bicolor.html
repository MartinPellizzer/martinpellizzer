Amaranthus tricolor var. bicolor

Amaranthus tricolor var. bicolor, commonly known as the rainbow amaranth, is a vibrant annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored bracts that range from red, orange, to yellow, creating a visually stunning display. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

This species is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in some traditional cuisines.
