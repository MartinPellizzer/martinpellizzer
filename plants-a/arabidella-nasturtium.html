Arabidella nasturtium

Arabidella nasturtium, commonly known as the Indian cress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that are often accompanied by heart-shaped, peppery leaves. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible leaves, which have a spicy flavor.

Arabidella nasturtium is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
