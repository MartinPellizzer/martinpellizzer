Artocarpus fretessii

Artocarpus fretessii, commonly known as the Java breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fruit. The fruit, which is typically green when mature, turns yellow or orange when ripe and has a fibrous, starchy pulp. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is often found in lowland forests.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat and is also valued for its edible fruit and timber.
