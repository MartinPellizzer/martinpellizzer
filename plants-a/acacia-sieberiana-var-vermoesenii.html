Acacia sieberiana var. vermoesenii

Acacia sieberiana var. vermoesenii, commonly known as the African wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and arranged in a feathery pattern. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The bark and leaves of this acacia species are also used for tanning leather and as a source of fiber.
