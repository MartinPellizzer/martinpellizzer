Attalea vitrivir

Attalea vitrivir, commonly known as the pink palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, edible fruits resembling small cherries. These fruits are an important food source for various wildlife and are also consumed by humans.

The botanical features of Attalea vitrivir make it an ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
