Aristolochia kaempferi var. laevipes

Aristolochia kaempferi var. laevipes, commonly known as the Japanese pipevine, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and its striking, pipe-like flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage and ornamental value. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
