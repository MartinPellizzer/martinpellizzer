Artanthe durvilleana

Artanthe durvilleana, commonly known as the Blue Desert Daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant is known for its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in spring and attract various pollinators. It has a compact, low-growing habit with silver-blue foliage that thrives in well-drained soils.

Artanthe durvilleana is well adapted to dry conditions, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping in suitable climates.
