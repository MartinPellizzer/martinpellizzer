Acinopetala tubuliflora

Acinopetala tubuliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features slender stems that often grow in a spreading or ascending manner, and its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or violet in color, arranged in loose clusters at the ends of the stems. These flowers bloom in the spring and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Acinopetala tubuliflora is adapted to cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
