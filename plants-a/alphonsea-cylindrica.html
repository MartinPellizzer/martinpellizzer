Alphonsea cylindrica

Alphonsea cylindrica, commonly known as the slender alphonsea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by cylindrical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

Alphonsea cylindrica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
