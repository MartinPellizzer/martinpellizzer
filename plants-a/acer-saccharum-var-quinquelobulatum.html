Acer saccharum var. quinquelobulatum

Acer saccharum var. quinquelobulatum, commonly known as the five-lobed sugar maple, is a variant of the sugar maple tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of yellow, orange, and red in the fall. The tree produces samaras, which are winged seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in mixed hardwood forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
