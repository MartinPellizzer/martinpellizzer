Aristida longiramea var. boliviana

Aristida longiramea var. boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Bolivia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, pale brown flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
