Ada peruviana

Ada peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Ada peruviana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
