Abutilon piurense

Abutilon piurense, commonly known as the "Peruvian velvetleaf," is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are often hairy to the touch. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers, which are typically bell-shaped and bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a distinctive waxy appearance.

Abutilon piurense is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
