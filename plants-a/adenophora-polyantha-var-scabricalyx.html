Adenophora polyantha var. scabricalyx

Adenophora polyantha var. scabricalyx, commonly known as the rough-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
