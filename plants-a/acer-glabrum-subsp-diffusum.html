Acer glabrum subsp. diffusum

Acer glabrum subsp.

diffusum, commonly known as the western sugar maple, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its smooth, light gray bark and palmate leaves that turn vibrant yellow in autumn.

The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in spring, which are followed by winged seeds called samaras. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
