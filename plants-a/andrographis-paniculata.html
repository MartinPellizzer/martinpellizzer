Andrographis paniculata

Andrographis paniculata, commonly known as the "Indian echinacea," is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in moist, open habitats.

Andrographis paniculata is widely cultivated for its medicinal properties, particularly for its bitter compounds like andrographolide.
