Achillea thomasiana

Achillea thomasiana, commonly known as Thomas's yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is known for its aromatic, feathery leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Thomas's yarrow is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
