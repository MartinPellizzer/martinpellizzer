Annona cuspidata

Annona cuspidata, commonly known as the sour sop or custard apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The fruit is distinctive, with a spiky exterior and a creamy, aromatic pulp that contains numerous large, soft seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The plant's botanical features make it well-suited for both agricultural and horticultural purposes.
