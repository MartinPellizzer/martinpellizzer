Aconitum japonovolubile

Aconitum japonovolubile, commonly known as Japanese monkshood, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it is toxic and should be handled with care.
