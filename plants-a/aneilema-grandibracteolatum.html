Aneilema grandibracteolatum

Aneilema grandibracteolatum, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a striking herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs during the wet season, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
