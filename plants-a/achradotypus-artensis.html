Achradotypus artensis

Achradotypus artensis, commonly known as the Artensian cactus, is a rare and unique species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, branching stems covered in dense, spiny areoles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
