Acacia cibaria

Acacia cibaria, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, silky appearance. The plant produces yellow or golden flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom during the dry season. Its pods are elongated, woody, and contain several flat, edible seeds.

Acacia cibaria is often used for ornamental purposes and has traditional uses in some cultures for food and medicine.
