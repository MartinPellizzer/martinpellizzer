Acacia lentiginea

Acacia lentiginea, commonly known as the black wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Acacia lentiginea is also notable for its historical use in the tanning industry due to its high tannin content.
