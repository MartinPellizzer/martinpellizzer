Acacia burrowsiana

Acacia burrowsiana, commonly known as the burrows acacia, is a small, shrub-like plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed structure and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to spines, a common adaptation to reduce water loss in dry environments. Acacia burrowsiana is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Its ecological role includes providing shelter and food for various desert animals and insects.
