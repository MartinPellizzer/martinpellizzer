Aegilops ventricosa var. obscura

Aegilops ventricosa var. obscura, commonly known as the dark spikelet wheat, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and compact inflorescence with small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats. It has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

This variety is of interest in agricultural research due to its potential genetic contributions to wheat improvement.
