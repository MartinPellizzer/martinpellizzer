Alyssum montanum proles transsilvanicum

Alyssum montanum proles transsilvanicum, commonly known as Transylvanian alpine alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Transylvania.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
