Antirrhinum vexillocalyculatum subsp. intermedium

Antirrhinum vexillocalyculatum subsp.

intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate fairy lantern, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the plantain family, Plantaginaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
