Astrocaryum ciliatum

Astrocaryum ciliatum, commonly known as the Brazilian wax palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish trunk and long, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, oily fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a notable species in tropical ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used by indigenous communities for traditional purposes.
