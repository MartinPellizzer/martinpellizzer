Astragalus karamasicus subsp. ulaschensis

Astragalus karamasicus subsp.

ulaschensis, commonly known as the Ulaschen Astragalus, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is known for its small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
