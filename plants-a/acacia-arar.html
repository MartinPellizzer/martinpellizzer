Acacia arar

Acacia arar, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Acacia arar is well adapted to dry climates, with deep roots that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
