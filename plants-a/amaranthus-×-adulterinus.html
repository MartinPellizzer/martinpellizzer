Amaranthus × adulterinus

Amaranthus × adulterinus, commonly known as the redroot pigweed, is an annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and branching stems that often turn reddish in color. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture. It is a common weed in agricultural fields and disturbed areas, often thriving in dry conditions.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution and invasive potential in certain regions.
