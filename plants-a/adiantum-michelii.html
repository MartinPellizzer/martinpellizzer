Adiantum michelii

Adiantum michelii, commonly known as the Mexican maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that resemble the wings of a bird. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in forest understories. Its leaves are usually bright green and have a distinctive, lace-like appearance.

Adiantum michelii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
