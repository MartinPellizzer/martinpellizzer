Andropogon sorghum var. nitidus

Andropogon sorghum var. nitidus, commonly known as silky sorghum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green or purplish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Silky sorghum is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its graceful appearance and adaptability to various climates.
