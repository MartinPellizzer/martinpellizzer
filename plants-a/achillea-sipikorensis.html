Achillea sipikorensis

Achillea sipikorensis, commonly known as the Sipikore mountain yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in mountainous regions, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
