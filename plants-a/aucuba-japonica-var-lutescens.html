Aucuba japonica var. lutescens

Aucuba japonica var. lutescens, commonly known as the golden variegated aucuba, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with striking yellow and white variegation, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that attract birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

Its dense foliage and year-round color make it an excellent choice for woodland gardens or as a hedge.
