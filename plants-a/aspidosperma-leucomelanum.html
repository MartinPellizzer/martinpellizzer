Aspidosperma leucomelanum

Aspidosperma leucomelanum, commonly known as the white-leathery aspidosperma, is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, whitish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries containing seeds. It is known for its latex sap, which has been used in traditional medicine for its potential pharmacological properties.

The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in dry forests and savannas.
