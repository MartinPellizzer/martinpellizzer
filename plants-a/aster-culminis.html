Aster culminis

Aster culminis, commonly known as the alpine aster, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Aster culminis plays an important ecological role in alpine ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
