Acalypha wallichii

Acalypha wallichii, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian velvet, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges in color from green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Acalypha wallichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
