Andryala cossyrensis var. oligadena

Andryala cossyrensis var. oligadena, commonly known as the narrow-leaved andryala, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, linear to lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Apiaceae family, noted for its aromatic foliage and ecological significance in local ecosystems.
