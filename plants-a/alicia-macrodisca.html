Alicia macrodisca

Alicia macrodisca, commonly known as the large-flowered alicia, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features compound leaves with finely dissected margins and produces showy white to pale yellow flowers. Its fruits are small, dry schizocarps that split into two halves when mature.

Alicia macrodisca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
