Astragalus raphaelis

Astragalus raphaelis, commonly known as Raphael's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, rounded seed pods that contain several small, hard seeds.

Astragalus raphaelis is often found in open areas such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.
