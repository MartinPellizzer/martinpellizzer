Artemisia laciniatiformis subsp. taimyrensis

Artemisia laciniatiformis subsp.

taimyrensis, commonly known as the Taimyren tansy, is a perennial shrub native to the Arctic regions of Eurasia. It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in cold, dry environments and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.

Its aromatic foliage contains volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal and ecological significance.
