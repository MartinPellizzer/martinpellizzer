Aster madrensis

Aster madrensis, commonly known as the Madrean aster, is a flowering plant native to the Madrean Archipelago in the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant produces showy blue to purple flower heads in the fall, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it has a fibrous root system adapted to arid conditions.

Aster madrensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
