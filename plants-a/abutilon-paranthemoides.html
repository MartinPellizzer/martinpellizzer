Abutilon paranthemoides

Abutilon paranthemoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
