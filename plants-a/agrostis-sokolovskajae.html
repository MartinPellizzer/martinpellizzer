Agrostis sokolovskajae

Agrostis sokolovskajae, commonly known as the Sokolovskaja bentgrass, is a species of grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers.

Agrostis sokolovskajae is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
