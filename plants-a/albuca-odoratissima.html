Albuca odoratissima

Albuca odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet-scented albuca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow blooms that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light for optimal growth.

Albuca odoratissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and distinctive aroma.
