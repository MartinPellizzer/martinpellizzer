Alchemilla polita

Alchemilla polita, commonly known as the silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along streams.

Alchemilla polita is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
