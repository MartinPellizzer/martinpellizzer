Anthurium espinae

Anthurium espinae, commonly known as the Espinae arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique inflorescence structure known as a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy, dark green surface and prominent veins. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically emerging from the base of the spathe.

Anthurium espinae is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded gardens.
