Adenandra bartlingiana

Adenandra bartlingiana, commonly known as the Natal holly, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, dark green leaves with a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Adenandra bartlingiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
