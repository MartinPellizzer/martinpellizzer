Atractylodes lancea subsp. luotianensis

Atractylodes lancea subsp.

luotianensis, commonly known as Luotian Atractylodes, is a perennial herb native to China, particularly found in the Luotian region of Hubei Province. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is known for its aromatic rhizomes, which are used in traditional Chinese medicine.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its therapeutic applications.
