Aristida anthoxanthoides

Aristida anthoxanthoides, commonly known as the golden wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a pale green to golden hue. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often tinged with red or brown, adding visual interest. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
