Aconitum contractum

Aconitum contractum, commonly known as the European monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum contractum is part of the Ranunculaceae family and contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
