Ariocarpus furfuraceus

Ariocarpus furfuraceus, commonly known as the hairy pincushion cactus, is a succulent plant native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy stems that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a low-growing, compact species that typically forms a rounded or cushion-like shape.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens.
