Acinodendron elatum

Acinodendron elatum, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a large evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its impressive height, often reaching up to 40 meters, and its broad, umbrella-shaped canopy. The plant produces showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically large, elliptical, and have a glossy appearance.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and parks for its visual impact.
