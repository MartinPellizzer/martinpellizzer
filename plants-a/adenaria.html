Adenaria

Adenaria, commonly known as the "arrowroot plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asphodelaceae.

It is native to various regions in Africa, Asia, and Australia, and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and tall, spike-like inflorescences. The genus includes several species, some of which are cultivated for their ornamental value or medicinal properties. Adenaria species often thrive in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions.

Their unique morphology and ecological significance make them an interesting subject in botanical studies.
