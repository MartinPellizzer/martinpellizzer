Aspidium everettii

Aspidium everettii, commonly known as the Everett's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its rhizomes are submerged, anchoring the plant while absorbing nutrients from the water.

Aspidium everettii plays a role in maintaining water quality by filtering sediments and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
