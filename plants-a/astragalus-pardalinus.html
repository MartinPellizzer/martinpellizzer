Astragalus pardalinus

Astragalus pardalinus, commonly known as the spotted milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. Astragalus pardalinus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
