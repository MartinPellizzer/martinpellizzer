Albizia antunesiana

Albizia antunesiana, commonly known as the African silk tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fluffy pods. The plant is noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Albizia antunesiana is also appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
