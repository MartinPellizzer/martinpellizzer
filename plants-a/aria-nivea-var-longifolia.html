Aria nivea var. longifolia

Aria nivea var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf aria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
