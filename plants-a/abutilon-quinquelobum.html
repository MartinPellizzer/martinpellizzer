Abutilon quinquelobum

Abutilon quinquelobum, commonly known as the five-lobed abutilon, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, heart-shaped leaves and showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments. Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
