Aechmea andersoniana

Aechmea andersoniana, commonly known as the Anderson's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant, spathate flowers that can range in color from red to orange, often emerging from the leaf rosette. Its foliage is typically covered in a dense, fibrous layer called a trichome, which helps in water retention and protection from intense sunlight.

Aechmea andersoniana is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, indirect light conditions.
