Asclepias grandifolia

Asclepias grandifolia, commonly known as showy milkweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to reddish-orange flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains latex, which is toxic to some animals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

Asclepias grandifolia serves as a critical host plant for the monarch butterfly, supporting the life cycle of this iconic species.
