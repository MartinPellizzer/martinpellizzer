Amaryllis princeps

Amaryllis princeps, commonly known as the yellow bell amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are typically dark green and broad. Amaryllis princeps is a bulbous plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
