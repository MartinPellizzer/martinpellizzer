Adenia epigea

Adenia epigea, commonly known as the ground fig, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellowish berry that contains several seeds.

Adenia epigea is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
