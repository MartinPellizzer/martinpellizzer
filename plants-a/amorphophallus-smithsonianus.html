Amorphophallus smithsonianus

Amorphophallus smithsonianus, commonly known as the Smithsonian elephant's foot, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its massive, tuberous underground stem, which can grow to several feet in diameter. The plant produces large, spadix-shaped flowers that are often surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and rapid growth, it is a popular ornamental plant in botanical gardens and collections.
