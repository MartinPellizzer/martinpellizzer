Acacia brevispica var. dregeana

Acacia brevispica var. dregeana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are dense and finely divided. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in terminal spikes, typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
