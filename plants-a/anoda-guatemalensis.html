Anoda guatemalensis

Anoda guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue, often opening in the early morning. The plant is a herbaceous annual that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Anoda guatemalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
