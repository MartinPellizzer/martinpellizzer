Alsophila pallidipaleata

Alsophila pallidipaleata, commonly known as the pale-veined tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan. Its fronds are typically light green with pale veins, contributing to its ornamental value.

Alsophila pallidipaleata is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
