Aponogeton abyssinicus var. glandulifera

Aponogeton abyssinicus var. glandulifera, commonly known as the Abyssinian arrowhead, is a rare aquatic plant native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters with sandy or gravelly substrates. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters at the water's surface.
