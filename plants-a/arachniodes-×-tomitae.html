Arachniodes × tomitae

Arachniodes × tomitae, commonly known as the Japanese tree fern, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between *Arachniodes simplicifolia* and *Arachniodes standleyi*.

It is a member of the Davalliaceae family and is native to Japan and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that give it a tree-like appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
