Alysicarpus misquittae

Alysicarpus misquittae, commonly known as the Peruvian burr, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spiny fruit pods that resemble small burrs. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its small, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers contribute to its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.

Due to its ornamental and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
