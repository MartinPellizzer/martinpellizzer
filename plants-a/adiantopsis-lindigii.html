Adiantopsis lindigii

Adiantopsis lindigii, commonly known as the Lindig's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often arched or recurved. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce roots that help anchor it in the soil.

Adiantopsis lindigii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
