Aphelandra grandis

Aphelandra grandis, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its vibrant color and lush appearance.

Aphelandra grandis thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
