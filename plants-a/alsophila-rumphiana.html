Alsophila rumphiana

Alsophila rumphiana, commonly known as the Rumphian tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, making it a striking feature in tropical forests.

Alsophila rumphiana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is valued for its ability to purify the air.
