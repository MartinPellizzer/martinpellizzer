Acrostichum thelypteris

Acrostichum thelypteris, commonly known as the giant chain fern, is a large, tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its massive, compound fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its spores are produced in sori located on the underside of the fronds.

This species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to create a lush, tropical atmosphere in gardens.
