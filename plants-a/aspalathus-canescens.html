Aspalathus canescens

Aspalathus canescens, commonly known as the silver bush, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to the dry, coastal climate of its native habitat, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in arid conditions. It is also notable for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.

The species is relatively uncommon in the wild, making it a valuable component of local biodiversity.
