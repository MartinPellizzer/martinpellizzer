Atalantia roxburghiana

Atalantia roxburghiana, commonly known as the Indian butterfruit, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that contain a single seed, and its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
