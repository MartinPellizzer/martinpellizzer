Andropogon myosurus var. feensis

Andropogon myosurus var. feensis, commonly known as the Chinese silvergrass, is a variant of the silvergrass species native to China.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, silver-blue leaves and tufted inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and the subtle, feathery appearance of its flowers.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
