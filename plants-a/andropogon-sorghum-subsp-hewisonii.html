Andropogon sorghum subsp. hewisonii

Andropogon sorghum subsp.

hewisonii, commonly known as Hewison's sorghum, is a grass species native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically brown or purple in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in open woodlands or grasslands.

Hewison's sorghum is primarily of interest for its ecological role and potential use in horticulture or as a forage crop.
