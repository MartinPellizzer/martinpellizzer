Agrostophyllum wagiribuanum

Agrostophyllum wagiribuanum, commonly known as the Bornean sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant is herbaceous, with slender stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is typically found growing on rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
