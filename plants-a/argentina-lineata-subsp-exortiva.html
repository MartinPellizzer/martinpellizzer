Argentina lineata subsp. exortiva

Argentina lineata subsp.

exortiva, commonly known as the zigzag rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that exhibit a distinctive zigzag pattern, giving it its common name.

The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as along riverbanks and in floodplains. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy coating.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
