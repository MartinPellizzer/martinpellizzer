Allium pamiricum

Allium pamiricum, commonly known as the Pamir garlic, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including the Pamir and Tien Shan ranges. The plant produces clusters of white to pale purple flowers and has a bulbous stem that resembles garlic. Its leaves are grass-like and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length.

Allium pamiricum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
