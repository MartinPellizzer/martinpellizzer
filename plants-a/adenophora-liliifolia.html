Adenophora liliifolia

Adenophora liliifolia, commonly known as the pink lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces showy, cup-like bracts that enclose the actual flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental value.

Adenophora liliifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady areas.
