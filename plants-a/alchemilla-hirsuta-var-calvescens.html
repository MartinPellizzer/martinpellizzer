Alchemilla hirsuta var. calvescens

Alchemilla hirsuta var. calvescens, commonly known as hairy alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features dense, silver-gray foliage with soft, hairy textures that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
