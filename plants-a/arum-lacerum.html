Arum lacerum

Arum lacerum, commonly known as the shredded arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often mottled with dark green and silver patterns. The plant produces a spadix, a fleshy spike surrounded by a spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens.
