Aloe grahamii

Aloe grahamii, commonly known as Graham's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments. The leaves are typically pale green with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. It produces yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, adding ornamental value to the plant.

This species is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
