Alchemilla vulgaris var. multidens

Alchemilla vulgaris var. multidens, commonly known as the moss rose or lesser plantain, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a mat-forming habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its leaves and flowers have been used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported healing properties.
