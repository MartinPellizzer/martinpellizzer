Alternanthera filifolia subsp. margaritacea

Alternanthera filifolia subsp.

margaritacea, commonly known as the pearl plant, is a striking aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to South America. It is characterized by its vibrant red or pink bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is popular in both freshwater and brackish water habitats.

Its ornamental value comes from its colorful foliage and ability to create a visually appealing underwater landscape.
