Albuca schoenlandii

Albuca schoenlandii, commonly known as the Natal grass or Natal albuca, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.

Albuca schoenlandii prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
