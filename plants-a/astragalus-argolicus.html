Astragalus argolicus

Astragalus argolicus, commonly known as the Argolic milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Astragalus argolicus is often found in open habitats such as steppes and coastal areas.
