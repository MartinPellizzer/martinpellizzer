Artia brachycarpa var. coriacea

Artia brachycarpa var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough-fruited artia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are covered in a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that becomes woody and ribbed as it matures.

This species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.
