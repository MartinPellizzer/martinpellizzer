Anthericum montanum

Anthericum montanum, commonly known as mountain buttercup, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in mountain meadows.

Anthericum montanum is known for its early spring blooming habit, making it a notable indicator of alpine ecosystems.
