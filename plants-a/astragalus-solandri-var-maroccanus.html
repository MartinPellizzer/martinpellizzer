Astragalus solandri var. maroccanus

Astragalus solandri var. maroccanus, commonly known as the Moroccan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets arranged in pairs, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
