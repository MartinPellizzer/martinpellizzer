Alchemilla subconnivens

Alchemilla subconnivens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silky white hair. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream sides.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
