Arabis lemmonii

Arabis lemmonii, commonly known as Lemmon's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. This species is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often found in mountainous regions.

The plant is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with minimal water availability.
