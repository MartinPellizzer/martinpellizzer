Acantholimon argyrostachyum

Acantholimon argyrostachyum, commonly known as silver-stemmed saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial species characterized by its succulent, silver-gray stems and basal rosettes of fleshy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers in spring, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
