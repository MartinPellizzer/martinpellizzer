Astragalus distinctus

Astragalus distinctus, commonly known as the distinct milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus distinctus is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
