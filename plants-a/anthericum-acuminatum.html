Anthericum acuminatum

Anthericum acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-tipped lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, white to pale pink flowers with six petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Anthericum acuminatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
