Anemone biflora var. bifoliolata

Anemone biflora var. bifoliolata, commonly known as the two-leaved anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces showy, white or pink flowers with five to seven petals. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety 'bifoliolata' is distinguished by its unique leaf structure, featuring two distinct leaflets on each leaf blade.
