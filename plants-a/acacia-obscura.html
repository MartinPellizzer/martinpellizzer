Acacia obscura

Acacia obscura, commonly known as the black wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia obscura is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
