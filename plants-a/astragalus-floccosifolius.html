Astragalus floccosifolius

Astragalus floccosifolius, commonly known as the woolly leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, fuzzy leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, yellow flowers in late summer. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Astragalus floccosifolius is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
