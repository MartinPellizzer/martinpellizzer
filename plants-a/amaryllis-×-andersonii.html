Amaryllis × andersonii

Amaryllis × andersonii, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis papaya. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. It typically grows as a bulbous plant with strap-like leaves and produces clusters of flowers during the summer months.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
