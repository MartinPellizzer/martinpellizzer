Adoxa omeiensis

Adoxa omeiensis, commonly known as the Chinese bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Adoxaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, clustered inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Adoxa omeiensis prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded woodland areas.
