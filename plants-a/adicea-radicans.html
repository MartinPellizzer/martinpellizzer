Adicea radicans

Adicea radicans, commonly known as the creeping adicea, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant has a sprawling growth habit, with stems that root at the nodes and form dense mats. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by capsule fruits that release tiny seeds.
