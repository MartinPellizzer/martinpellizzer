Anarthrosyne cordata

Anarthrosyne cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are usually dark green and glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Anarthrosyne cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
