Alopecurus geniculatus subsp. fulvus

Alopecurus geniculatus subsp.

fulvus, commonly known as the yellow bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and yellowish-green foliage.

The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The subspecies fulvus is distinguished by its more yellowish coloration compared to other varieties within the genus Alopecurus.
