Agrostis truncatula subsp. durieui

Agrostis truncatula subsp.

durieui, commonly known as the Durieu's bentgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a low-growing, perennial plant characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit.

The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its flowering stems bear small, spikelet-like inflorescences, and it reproduces through both sexual and vegetative means.

Due to its ecological adaptability, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
