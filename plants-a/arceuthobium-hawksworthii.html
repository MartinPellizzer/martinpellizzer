Arceuthobium hawksworthii

Arceuthobium hawksworthii, commonly known as the dwarf broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and cannot perform photosynthesis.

It relies entirely on its host plant for nutrients, typically attaching to the roots of other species. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked due to its lack of leaves and stems. It is native to regions of the western United States and is adapted to grow in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique life cycle and parasitic nature make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
