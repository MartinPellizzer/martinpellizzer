Aristida brevifolia

Aristida brevifolia, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, woodlands, and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, upright stems with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

Aristida brevifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
