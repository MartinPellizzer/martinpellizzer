Amygdalus communis var. semifragilis

Amygdalus communis var. semifragilis, commonly known as the Chinese sand almond, is a variant of the common almond tree.

It is characterized by its smaller, more delicate flowers and fruit compared to the standard variety. The plant is native to China and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include compound leaves and a fruit that splits open to release its seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible nuts.
