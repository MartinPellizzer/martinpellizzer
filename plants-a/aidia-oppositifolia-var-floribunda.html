Aidia oppositifolia var. floribunda

Aidia oppositifolia var. floribunda, commonly known as the coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences, which consist of clusters of small, bright red or orange flowers. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth margin and a pointed tip. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
