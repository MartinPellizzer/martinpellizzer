Amaranthus lombardoi

Amaranthus lombardoi, commonly known as the Lombard giant amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and large, showy bracts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant red or orange bracts.

Amaranthus lombardoi is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
