Agoseris glauca var. glauca

Agoseris glauca var. glauca, commonly known as bluehead larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive blue-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.
