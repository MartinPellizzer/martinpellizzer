Abolboda

Abolboda, commonly known as the sacred lotus of ancient Egypt, is a striking aquatic plant with large, showy flowers that bloom in a striking sequence.

Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often floating on the surface of still waters. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in muddy environments, showcasing its adaptability to wetland ecosystems. Abolboda has been revered for its symbolic and medicinal properties throughout history.

Its botanical features make it a significant subject in both ecological and cultural studies.
