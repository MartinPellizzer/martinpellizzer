Allium zebdanense

Allium zebdanense, commonly known as the Zebdan leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes many edible and ornamental species. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its bulbs are often used in local cuisines and are valued for their mild garlic-like flavor.

Allium zebdanense thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in mountainous areas.
