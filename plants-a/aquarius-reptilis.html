Aquarius reptilis

Aquarius reptilis, commonly known as the aquatic lizard plant, is a unique species that thrives in moist environments.

It features elongated, ribbon-like leaves that resemble the scales of a reptile, giving it its distinctive name. The plant is often found near water sources such as ponds or streams, where it can absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil and water. Its reproductive system involves the production of small, delicate flowers that bloom in the early spring.

Despite its name, it is not a true reptile but a fascinating example of botanical adaptation to aquatic habitats.
