Ailuroschia rupifraga

Ailuroschia rupifraga, commonly known as the rock foxglove, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink, often with a distinctive pattern resembling a fox's face. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette.

Ailuroschia rupifraga is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
