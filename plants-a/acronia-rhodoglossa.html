Acronia rhodoglossa

Acronia rhodoglossa, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Acronia rhodoglossa is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation indoors.
