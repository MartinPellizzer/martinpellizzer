Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. iframensis

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

iframensis, commonly known as the Iramensis Scotch thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, often growing in dry, sunny habitats. It has small, ovate leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine pubescence.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native range.
