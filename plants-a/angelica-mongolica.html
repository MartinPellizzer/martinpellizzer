Angelica mongolica

Angelica mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian angelica, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Mongolia and northern China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Angelica mongolica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
