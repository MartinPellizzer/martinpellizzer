Adenia acuta

Adenia acuta, commonly known as the sharp-leaved adenia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and open forests. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, clustered in terminal inflorescences, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Adenia acuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
