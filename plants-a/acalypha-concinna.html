Acalypha concinna

Acalypha concinna, commonly known as the red-leaf or red-veined acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, glossy red leaves with prominent green veins, which give it a unique visual appeal. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Acalypha concinna is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
