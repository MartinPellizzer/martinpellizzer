Ayenia sidiformis

Ayenia sidiformis, commonly known as the slender ayenia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Ayenia sidiformis produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.
