Ayenia euphrasiifolia subsp. ophiticola

Ayenia euphrasiifolia subsp.

ophiticola, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
