Aerides amplexicaulis

Aerides amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves that often wrap around its stem, providing structural support and moisture retention. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, typically in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among orchid enthusiasts for indoor cultivation.
