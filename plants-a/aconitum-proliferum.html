Aconitum proliferum

Aconitum proliferum, commonly known as the clustered monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features a stout, upright stem with large, palmately compound leaves that are deeply lobed. Aconitum proliferum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is known for its toxicity, containing potent alkaloids that make it dangerous to handle or ingest.
