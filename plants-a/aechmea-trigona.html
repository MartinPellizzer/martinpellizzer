Aechmea trigona

Aechmea trigona, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It features a rosette of stiff, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect water and nutrients. The plant produces a tall flowering stem with clusters of vibrant orange-red flowers, which bloom in late summer or early fall. Its foliage is often speckled with white or silver markings, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Aechmea trigona thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Morphology

Aechmea trigona has narrow, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, with a dark green color and a waxy texture.

The plant has a short, compact stem with a smooth, dark brown bark that grows upright. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix, surrounded by white bracts, and bloom in late summer to early autumn, pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.
