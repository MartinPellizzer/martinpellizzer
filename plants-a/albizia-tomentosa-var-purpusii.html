Albizia tomentosa var. purpusii

Albizia tomentosa var. purpusii, commonly known as the woolly albizia, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and distinctive pod fruits that contain compressed seeds. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Its ornamental value comes from its fragrant flowers and unique foliage structure.
