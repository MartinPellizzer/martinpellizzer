Acer palmatum f. eudissectum

Acer palmatum f. eudissectum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with fine dissected leaves, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree.

It is characterized by its finely cut, lacy foliage that displays vibrant red, orange, and yellow hues during the fall. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and features delicate, branching stems.

This ornamental plant is widely appreciated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
