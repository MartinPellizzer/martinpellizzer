Acalypha macrostachya f. tristis

Acalypha macrostachya f. tristis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silky ironweed, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage that ranges from green to reddish hues, giving it a unique visual appeal. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its attractive appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
