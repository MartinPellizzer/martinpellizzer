Arabis tibetica var. pinnatifida

Arabis tibetica var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the Tibetan rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops and ornamental plants.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
