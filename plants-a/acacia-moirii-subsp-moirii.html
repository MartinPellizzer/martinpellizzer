Acacia moirii subsp. moirii

Acacia moirii subsp.

moirii, commonly known as the Moir's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its distinctive, feathery, bipinnate leaves.

The plant produces yellow globular flowers that appear in spring, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to dry environments and is often found in rocky soils. Acacia moirii subsp.

moirii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
