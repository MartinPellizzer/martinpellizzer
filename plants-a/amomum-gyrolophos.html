Amomum gyrolophos

Amomum gyrolophos, commonly known as the spiral-lobed cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of India and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped fruits that contain numerous small, dark brown seeds. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy texture.

The plant is valued for its fragrant properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
