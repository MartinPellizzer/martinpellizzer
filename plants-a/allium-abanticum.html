Allium abanticum

Allium abanticum, commonly known as the Armenian garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant's bulb is often used in traditional medicine and cuisine for its pungent aroma and flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Allium abanticum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
