Agrostis perennans var. aestivalis

Agrostis perennans var. aestivalis, commonly known as the early bentgrass, is a cool-season grass that thrives in temperate climates.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and a dense, carpet-like growth habit. This variety is often found in meadows, lawns, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering stems bear small, spikelet-like inflorescences, typical of the Poaceae family.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its neat appearance.
