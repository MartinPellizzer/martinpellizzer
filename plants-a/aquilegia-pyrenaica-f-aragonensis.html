Aquilegia pyrenaica f. aragonensis

Aquilegia pyrenaica f. aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon poppy, is a rare and endemic subspecies native to the Pyrenees region of Spain.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, spurred flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, high-altitude conditions. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
