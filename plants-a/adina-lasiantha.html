Adina lasiantha

Adina lasiantha, commonly known as the white adina, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The botanical features of Adina lasiantha make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
