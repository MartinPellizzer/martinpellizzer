Amyema cuernosensis

Amyema cuernosensis, commonly known as the horned amyema, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive horn-like structures on its stems, which are believed to serve a protective function. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Amyema cuernosensis is considered a rare and endangered species.
