Acalypha communis var. hispida

Acalypha communis var. hispida, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive velvety texture due to the presence of dense, fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often overlooked due to their inconspicuous nature. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a potential weed in various ecosystems.
