Anchusa pusilla

Anchusa pusilla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bugloss, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of blue to violet flowers from late spring to early autumn. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. Anchusa pusilla is a member of the Boraginaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.

It is also valued in gardens for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
