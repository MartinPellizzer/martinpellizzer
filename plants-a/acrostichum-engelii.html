Acrostichum engelii

Acrostichum engelii, commonly known as the giant reed or giant sawgrass, is a tall, aquatic grass-like plant native to the wetlands of the Americas.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments and can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes at the top of its stems. Acrostichum engelii plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly and form dense stands in suitable conditions.
