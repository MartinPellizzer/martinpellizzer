Astragalus abnormalis

Astragalus abnormalis, commonly known as the abnormal milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus abnormalis is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
