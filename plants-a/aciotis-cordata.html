Aciotis cordata

Aciotis cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by distinctive seed heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

Aciotis cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
