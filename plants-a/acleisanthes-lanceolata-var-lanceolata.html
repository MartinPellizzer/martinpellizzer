Acleisanthes lanceolata var. lanceolata

Acleisanthes lanceolata var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in the early morning. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a notable species in its native habitat.
