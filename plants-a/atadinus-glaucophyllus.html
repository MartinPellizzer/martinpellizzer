Atadinus glaucophyllus

Atadinus glaucophyllus, commonly known as the blue-leafed daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and features narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale blue, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
