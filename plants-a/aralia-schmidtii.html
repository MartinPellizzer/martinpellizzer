Aralia schmidtii

Aralia schmidtii, commonly known as Schmidt's aralia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Far East, including regions of China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, and its distinctive, umbellate inflorescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araliaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and medicinal properties.
