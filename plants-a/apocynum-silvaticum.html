Apocynum silvaticum

Apocynum silvaticum, commonly known as Indian hemp, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in woodland areas and along stream banks. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, and it has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. Its milky sap contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

The plant is also known for its fibrous roots, which have been historically used for various purposes.
