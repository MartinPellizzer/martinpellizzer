Alyssum montanum var. hymettium

Alyssum montanum var. hymettium, commonly known as the Hymettian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Hymettus Mountains in Greece.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant features hairy, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
