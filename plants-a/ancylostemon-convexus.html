Ancylostemon convexus

Ancylostemon convexus, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant features large, showy flowers with five petals, often in shades of red, pink, or white. Its leaves are ovate and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and branching.

Ancylostemon convexus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
