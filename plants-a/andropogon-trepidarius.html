Andropogon trepidarius

Andropogon trepidarius, commonly known as panic grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish or brownish flowers, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Andropogon trepidarius is valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
