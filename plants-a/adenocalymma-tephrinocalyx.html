Adenocalymma tephrinocalyx

Adenocalymma tephrinocalyx, commonly known as the mountain bottle tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bottle-shaped trunk and thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from underground sources.

This species is well adapted to semi-arid and rocky habitats, making it a resilient and ornamental plant in suitable climates.
