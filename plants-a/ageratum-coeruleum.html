Ageratum coeruleum

Ageratum coeruleum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom throughout the summer. Ageratum coeruleum thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is commonly used in gardens, landscapes, and as a bedding plant due to its vibrant appearance and ease of care.
