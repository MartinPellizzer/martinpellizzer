Aristolochia pubera var. pubera

Aristolochia pubera var. pubera, commonly known as the common Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex floral structures and chemical compounds, some of which have medicinal uses.
