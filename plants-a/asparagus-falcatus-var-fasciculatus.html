Asparagus falcatus var. fasciculatus

Asparagus falcatus var. fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered asparagus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, fern-like foliage with slender, arching stems that produce small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its unique foliage makes it a desirable addition to rock gardens and containers.
