Agastache eplingiana

Agastache eplingiana, commonly known as Epling's hyssop, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. This plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its showy, tubular flowers range in color from blue to purple and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
