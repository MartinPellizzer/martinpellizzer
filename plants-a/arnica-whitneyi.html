Arnica whitneyi

Arnica whitneyi, commonly known as Whitney's arnica, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture. Whitney's arnica is known for its medicinal properties, though it is toxic if ingested.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native environment.
