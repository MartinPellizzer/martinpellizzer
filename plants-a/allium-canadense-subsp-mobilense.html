Allium canadense subsp. mobilense

Allium canadense subsp.

mobilense, commonly known as the eastern wild onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant features grass-like basal leaves and produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers in the summer. Its bulbous root system contributes to its ability to thrive in wet environments.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
