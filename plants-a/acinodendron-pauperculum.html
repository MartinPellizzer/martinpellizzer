Acinodendron pauperculum

Acinodendron pauperculum, commonly known as the poor man's acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring a deep root system to access water. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its resilience and low maintenance, it is sometimes used in restoration projects in semi-arid regions.
