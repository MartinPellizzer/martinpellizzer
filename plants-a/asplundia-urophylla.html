Asplundia urophylla

Asplundia urophylla, commonly known as the hairy leaf plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Asplundia urophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
