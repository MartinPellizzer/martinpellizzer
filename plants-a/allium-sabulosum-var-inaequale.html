Allium sabulosum var. inaequale

Allium sabulosum var. inaequale, commonly known as the irregular sand garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features grass-like basal leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its bulbous stem and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species.
