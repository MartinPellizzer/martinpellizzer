Aster stracheyi

Aster stracheyi, commonly known as the Strachey's aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, dark seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to thrive in arid environments.

Aster stracheyi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
