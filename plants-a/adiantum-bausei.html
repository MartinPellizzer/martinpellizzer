Adiantum bausei

Adiantum bausei, commonly known as the Bause's maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Adiantaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that resemble those of other maidenhair ferns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often near streams or in forest understories. Its leaves are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, elegant appearance, with a pale green color and a slightly curled margin.

Adiantum bausei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
