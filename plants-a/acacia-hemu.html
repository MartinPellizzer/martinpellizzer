Acacia hemu

Acacia hemu, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically small and feathery. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods. Acacia hemu thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

It is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
