Anabaenella tamnoides var. genuina

Anabaenella tamnoides var. genuina, commonly known as the freshwater cyanobacterium, is a nitrogen-fixing organism that thrives in nutrient-rich aquatic environments.

It is characterized by its filamentous structure composed of cylindrical cells that form unbranched chains. This species is often found in lakes, ponds, and slow-moving waters, where it contributes to the biological nitrogen cycle. Its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen makes it ecologically significant in oligotrophic ecosystems.

However, under certain conditions, it can produce harmful algal blooms, impacting water quality and aquatic life.
