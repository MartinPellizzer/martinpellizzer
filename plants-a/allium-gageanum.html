Allium gageanum

Allium gageanum, commonly known as the broadleaf garlic or wild garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its bulbous stem is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Allium gageanum thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cold winter dormancy period for optimal growth.
