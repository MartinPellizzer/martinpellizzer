Artanthe colubrina var. crassifolia

Artanthe colubrina var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf artanthe, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with dark green, leathery leaves that are broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
