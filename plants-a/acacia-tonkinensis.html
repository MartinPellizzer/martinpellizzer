Acacia tonkinensis

Acacia tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and compound leaves that are usually pinnately arranged. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

The bark and leaves of Acacia tonkinensis have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
