Artemisia gallica subsp. densiflora

Artemisia gallica subsp.

densiflora, commonly known as northern wormwood, is a perennial herb native to northern Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and produces small, yellowish flower clusters.

The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its strong, medicinal scent.

It is widely used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural pesticides.
