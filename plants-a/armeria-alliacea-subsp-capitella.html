Armeria alliacea subsp. capitella

Armeria alliacea subsp.

capitella, commonly known as the clustered thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has basal leaves that are linear and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
