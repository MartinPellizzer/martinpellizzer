Alternanthera scirpoides

Alternanthera scirpoides, commonly known as the river beauty or swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and along riverbanks, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, pinkish-white flowers with five petals. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in water gardens and wetland landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-adapted to humid, nutrient-rich soils.
