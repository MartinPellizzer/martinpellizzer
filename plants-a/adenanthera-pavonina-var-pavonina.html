Adenanthera pavonina var. pavonina

Adenanthera pavonina var. pavonina, commonly known as the peacock bean, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its palmately compound leaves and distinctive, showy flowers that range from yellow to orange-red. The plant produces elongated, flat pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in traditional agriculture and as a forage crop.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable for soil improvement and ecological restoration.
