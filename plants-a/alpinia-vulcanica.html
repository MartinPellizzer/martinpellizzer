Alpinia vulcanica

Alpinia vulcanica, commonly known as the volcanic ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its rhizomes are similar to ginger in appearance and may have medicinal properties.

Alpinia vulcanica thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
