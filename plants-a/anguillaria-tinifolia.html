Anguillaria tinifolia

Anguillaria tinifolia, commonly known as the red-veined anguillaria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Anguillaria tinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
