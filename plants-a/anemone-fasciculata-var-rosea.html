Anemone fasciculata var. rosea

Anemone fasciculata var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered white anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a clumping growth habit with basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are typically 2 to 4 inches in diameter, with five petals and a central cluster of stamens.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
