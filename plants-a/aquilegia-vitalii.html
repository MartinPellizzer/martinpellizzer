Aquilegia vitalii

Aquilegia vitalii, commonly known as the Vitalii's columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred petals that come in shades of red, purple, and blue. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces clusters of small, bright flowers in early summer. Aquilegia vitalii thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a sought-after plant among horticulturists and botanists alike.
