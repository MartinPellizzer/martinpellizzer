Albizia julibrissin var. rosea

Albizia julibrissin var. rosea, commonly known as the pink silk tree, is a deciduous legume native to China.

It is characterized by its feathery, pinkish-white flowers that resemble silk ribbons, which bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange before falling in autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental tree.

Its nitrogen-fixing ability also benefits soil fertility in garden settings.
