Amelanchier canadensis var. japonica

Amelanchier canadensis var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features oval, serrated leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a deep red or purple color. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and natural landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators and providing food for birds.
