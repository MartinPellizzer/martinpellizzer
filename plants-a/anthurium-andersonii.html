Anthurium andersonii

Anthurium andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's flamingo flower, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces vibrant red spadices, which are the spathes that enclose the true flowers, often resembling a flamingo's beak. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium andersonii is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and unique floral structure.
