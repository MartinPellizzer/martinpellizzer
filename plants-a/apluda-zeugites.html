Apluda zeugites

Apluda zeugites, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with low rainfall and high temperatures.

Apluda zeugites plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small animals in its native range.
