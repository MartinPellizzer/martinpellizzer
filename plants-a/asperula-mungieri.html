Asperula mungieri

Asperula mungieri, commonly known as the Japanese evergreen azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and remain green throughout the growing season.

Asperula mungieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to acidic soils.
