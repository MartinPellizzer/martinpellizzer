Acacia pendula

Acacia pendula, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping, feathery foliage that gives it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are arranged in spherical heads and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into multiple pairs of small leaflets.

Acacia pendula thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its unique form and drought tolerance.
