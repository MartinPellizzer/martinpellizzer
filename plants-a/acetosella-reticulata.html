Acetosella reticulata

Acetosella reticulata, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves that resemble a mouse's ears, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers in early spring, which are often followed by small, capsule-like fruits. Acetosella reticulata is typically found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is a hardy species that thrives in temperate climates and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
