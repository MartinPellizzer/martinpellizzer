Aster ascendens var. yosemitanus

Aster ascendens var. yosemitanus, commonly known as the Yosemite aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Sierra Nevada mountains in California.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and displays of vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and open slopes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important component of alpine and subalpine habitats.
