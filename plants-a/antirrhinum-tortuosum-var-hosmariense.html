Antirrhinum tortuosum var. hosmariense

Antirrhinum tortuosum var. hosmariense, commonly known as the Hosmariense Snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release its tiny seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
