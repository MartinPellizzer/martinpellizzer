Alyssum rothmaleri

Alyssum rothmaleri, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring and early summer. The plant has compact, succulent-like leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient species suitable for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
