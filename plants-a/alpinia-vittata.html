Alpinia vittata

Alpinia vittata, commonly known as the blackroot ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Alpinia vittata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
