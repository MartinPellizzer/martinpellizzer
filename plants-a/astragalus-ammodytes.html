Astragalus ammodytes

Astragalus ammodytes, commonly known as the sand milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus ammodytes plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and supporting local wildlife.
