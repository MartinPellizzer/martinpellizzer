Acanthopsis tetragona subsp. tetragona

Acanthopsis tetragona subsp.

tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled acanthus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, four-angled stems and opposite, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
