Adenanthera pavonina

Adenanthera pavonina, commonly known as the "peacock bean," is a tropical leguminous plant native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery, pink or red flowers that resemble a peacock's tail, hence its common name. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it valuable in agricultural systems.
