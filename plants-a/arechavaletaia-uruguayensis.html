Arechavaletaia uruguayensis

Arechavaletaia uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguayan areca palm, is a species of palm native to Uruguay.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
