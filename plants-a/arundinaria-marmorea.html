Arundinaria marmorea

Arundinaria marmorea, commonly known as the pink bamboo, is a striking ornamental grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems that exhibit a distinctive pinkish hue, especially when young. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in early summer, followed by delicate, feathery seed heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its unique coloration and graceful appearance make it a valuable addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
