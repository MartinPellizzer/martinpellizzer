Acrophorus parvulus

Acrophorus parvulus, commonly known as the dwarf acrophorus, is a small shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
