Acanthopsis disperma

Acanthopsis disperma, commonly known as the scattered acanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or arid regions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom during the warmer months.

Acanthopsis disperma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
