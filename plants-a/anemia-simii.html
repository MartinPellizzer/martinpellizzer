Anemia simii

Anemia simii, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features long, narrow leaves and blooms with showy, red flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

The flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
