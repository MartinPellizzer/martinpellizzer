Alliaria auriculata

Alliaria auriculata, commonly known as sow thistle, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal, spoon-shaped leaves and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and fields. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are arranged in a single umbel, typical of the mustard family. It is an annual or biennial herb that thrives in temperate climates.

Sow thistle is known for its edible leaves, which have a mild, slightly peppery flavor.
