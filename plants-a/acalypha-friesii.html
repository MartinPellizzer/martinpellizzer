Acalypha friesii

Acalypha friesii, commonly known as the red hibiscus or red flame plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are less showy than the bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Acalypha friesii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage and unique appearance.
