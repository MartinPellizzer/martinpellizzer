Athyrium unifurcatum

Athyrium unifurcatum, commonly known as the single-veined wood fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a single central vein. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense layer of brown, velvety hairs.

The fronds are usually biennial, with the fertile fronds appearing in the second year of growth.
