Anemone decapetala var. trilobata

Anemone decapetala var. trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with 10 petals, arranged in a star-like pattern, and is typically found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. The leaves are palmately lobed, often with three to five distinct lobes, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
