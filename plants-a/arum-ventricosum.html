Arum ventricosum

Arum ventricosum, commonly known as the swamp arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features a distinctive purple spathe that encloses a spadix, a characteristic feature of the Araceae family. The plant produces small, green flowers that develop into red berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize moist, nutrient-rich soils effectively.

The plant is adapted to waterlogged conditions, making it a common sight in swampy environments.
