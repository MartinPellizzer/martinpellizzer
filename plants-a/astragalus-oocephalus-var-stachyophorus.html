Astragalus oocephalus var. stachyophorus

Astragalus oocephalus var. stachyophorus, commonly known as the narrow-headed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous tiny, lens-shaped seeds.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
