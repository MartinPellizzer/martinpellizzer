Astragalus Stevenianus Subsp. Glochideus

Steven's milkvetch, scientifically known as Astragalus stevenianus subsp. glochideus, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the arid regions of the western United States. It belongs to the legume family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, spiny pods. This subspecies is particularly notable for its unique adaptations to harsh desert environments. Despite its beauty, it faces significant threats from habitat loss and climate change. Conservation efforts are crucial to ensure its survival in the wild.
