Aeschynomene uniflora

Aeschynomene uniflora, commonly known as blue false indigo, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Aeschynomene uniflora is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
