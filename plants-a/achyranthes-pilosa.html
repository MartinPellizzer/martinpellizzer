Achyranthes pilosa

Achyranthes pilosa, commonly known as hairy knotweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features upright, branching stems that are often hairy, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is known for its fibrous roots, which can be used in traditional medicine.
