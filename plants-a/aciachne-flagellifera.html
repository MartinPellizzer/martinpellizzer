Aciachne flagellifera

Aciachne flagellifera, commonly known as the whipgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and along coastal areas.

Aciachne flagellifera is known for its resilience to drought and salt spray, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
