Aulomyrcia lingua var. rufa

Aulomyrcia lingua var. rufa, commonly known as the red-flowered marsh mallow, is a variant of the marsh mallow plant native to wetland and swampy areas in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, herbaceous stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and are adapted for pollination by insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
