Anthyllis cantabrica

Anthyllis cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. It is a perennial herb that produces pods containing seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
