Astragalus × madiotii

Astragalus × madiotii, commonly known as the hybrid milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in raceme clusters. The plant is native to regions of central Asia and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It has a deep taproot system, which allows it to thrive in arid environments.

Astragalus × madiotii is also notable for its role in ecological systems, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivores.
