Anthemis kotschyana f. discoidea

Anthemis kotschyana f. discoidea, commonly known as the alpine dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant features basal rosettes of silver-gray, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous regions.
