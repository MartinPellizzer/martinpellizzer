Alyssum montanum var. hispanicum

Alyssum montanum var. hispanicum, commonly known as Spanish winter alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are narrow, hairy, and typically gray-green in color, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its early blooming habit and ability to attract pollinators.
