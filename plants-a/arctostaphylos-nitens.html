Arctostaphylos nitens

Arctostaphylos nitens, commonly known as the white mountain manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky habitats.

Arctostaphylos nitens is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native range.
