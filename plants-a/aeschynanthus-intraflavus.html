Aeschynanthus intraflavus

Aeschynanthus intraflavus, commonly known as the golden flame vine, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a trailing evergreen shrub characterized by its vibrant orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and dark green coloration. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aeschynanthus intraflavus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking floral display.
