Asarum mandshuricum

Asarum mandshuricum, commonly known as Siberian wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the forests of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic underground rhizome that is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its medicinal properties in some Asian cultures.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
