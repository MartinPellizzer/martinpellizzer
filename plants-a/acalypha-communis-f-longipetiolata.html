Acalypha communis f. longipetiolata

Acalypha communis f. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled spurflower, is a cultivar of the spurflower plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, vibrant red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant features elongated petioles, which give it a unique appearance and make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Its foliage is typically green with a slightly glossy texture, and it thrives in warm, humid environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown in containers or as part of a tropical garden display.
