Acrolophus rhenanus

Acrolophus rhenanus, commonly known as the European thistle or purple thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple or pinkish flower heads that resemble small cones. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role.

The plant produces achenes, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across different habitats.
