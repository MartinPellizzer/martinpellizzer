Acorus triqueter

Acorus triqueter, commonly known as narrow-leaved sweetflag, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It has long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that emerge from the water surface in early summer. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Acorus triqueter plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
