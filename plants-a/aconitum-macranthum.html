Aconitum macranthum

Aconitum macranthum, commonly known as the giant monkshood, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces large, showy blue or purple flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, which is a distinctive feature of the genus Aconitum. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that give it a fern-like appearance.

Aconitum macranthum is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and contains potent alkaloids, making it both toxic and of interest in botanical and pharmacological studies.
