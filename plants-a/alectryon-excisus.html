Alectryon excisus

Alectryon excisus, commonly known as the golden apple or the mountain apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and dense, rounded crown. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible for both humans and animals. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture with a glossy appearance.

Alectryon excisus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
