Anthurium melampyi

Anthurium melampyi, commonly known as the Red-veined Anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves with striking red veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, spadix-like flowers surrounded by a showy, waxy spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium melampyi is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
