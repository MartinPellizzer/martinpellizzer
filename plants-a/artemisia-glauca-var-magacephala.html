Artemisia glauca var. magacephala

Artemisia glauca var. magacephala, commonly known as bluegreen wormwood, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas.

Its aromatic leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal and ecological significance.
