Anthurium fuscopunctatum

Anthurium fuscopunctatum, commonly known as the spotted anthurium, is a member of the Araceae family native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves with prominent, dark brown spots and streaks. The plant produces striking, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red or orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium fuscopunctatum is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its unique foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
