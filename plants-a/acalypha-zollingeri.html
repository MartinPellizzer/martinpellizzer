Acalypha zollingeri

Acalypha zollingeri, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red hot catwhiskers, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble cat whiskers, which are often more showy than the small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Acalypha zollingeri thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
