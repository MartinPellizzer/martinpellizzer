Alchemilla hybrida var. acutata

Alchemilla hybrida var. acutata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated edge, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.
