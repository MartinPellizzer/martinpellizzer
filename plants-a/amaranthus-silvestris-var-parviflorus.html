Amaranthus silvestris var. parviflorus

Amaranthus silvestris var. parviflorus, commonly known as the dwarf amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from red to purple. The plant produces small, triangular seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.

Its short stature and vibrant floral display make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
