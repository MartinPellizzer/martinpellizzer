Amaranthus crocatus

Amaranthus crocatus, commonly known as the red amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround the small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in warm, sunny conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and branching.

Amaranthus crocatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional cuisines.
