Ardisia prunifolia

Ardisia prunifolia, commonly known as the cherry-leaf ardisia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring glossy, dark green leaves that are oval in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens due to its attractive foliage and seasonal fruit display.
