Antirrhinum siculum var. elatum

Antirrhinum siculum var. elatum, commonly known as the tall scarlet snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and vibrant red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, making it a striking addition to gardens. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with two lips and a spur, typical of the genus Antirrhinum.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
