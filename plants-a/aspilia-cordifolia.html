Aspilia cordifolia

Aspilia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs during the rainy season, making it a notable species in its native habitat.

Aspilia cordifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
