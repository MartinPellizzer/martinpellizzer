Acacia cuspidifolia

Acacia cuspidifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like phyllodes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The bark is often fissured and greyish, providing additional identification features.
