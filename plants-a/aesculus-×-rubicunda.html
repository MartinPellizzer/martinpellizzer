Aesculus × rubicunda

Aesculus × rubicunda, commonly known as the red horse chestnut, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-red bracts that resemble flowers, which appear in late spring. The tree produces large, spiny capsules containing shiny, dark brown seeds. Its leaves are palmately compound, typically with five to seven leaflets, and are alternate along the stem.

The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
