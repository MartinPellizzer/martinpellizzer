Adesmia ulicina

Adesmia ulicina, commonly known as the silky tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a silvery or fuzzy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.
