Amphiscirpus nevadensis

Amphiscirpus nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Amphiscirpus nevadensis is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
