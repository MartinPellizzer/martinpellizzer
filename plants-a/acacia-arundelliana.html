Acacia arundelliana

Acacia arundelliana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and bipinnate, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Acacia arundelliana is also valued for its hardy nature and is often used in landscaping in regions with similar climates.
