Acacia orbifolia

Acacia orbifolia, commonly known as the orbifoliate wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, orb-shaped phyllodes that are typically light green to yellowish in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spherical or cylindrical inflorescences. Acacia orbifolia is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

It is an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
