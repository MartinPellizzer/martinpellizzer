Anvillea garcinii subsp. garcinii

Anvillea garcinii subsp.

garcinii, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple.

The plant typically grows in shrubland and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The botanical features of this species make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
