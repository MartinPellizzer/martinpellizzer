Arabis mcdonaldiana

Arabis mcdonaldiana, commonly known as McDonald's rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white, and arranged in a loose cluster, blooming in early spring.

Arabis mcdonaldiana thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
