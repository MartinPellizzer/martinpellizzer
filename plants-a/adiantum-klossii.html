Adiantum klossii

Adiantum klossii, commonly known as the Kloss maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to the high-altitude forests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, lace-like fronds that are often tinged with silver. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires indirect light to prevent leaf burn. Its rhizomes are short and thick, anchoring it to rocky substrates in its natural habitat.

Adiantum klossii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its elegant appearance.
