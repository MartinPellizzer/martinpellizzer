Acer tschonoskii subsp. komarovii

Acer tschonoskii subsp.

komarovii, commonly known as the Mongolian maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
