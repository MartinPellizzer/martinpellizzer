Aegialitis tenuis

Aegialitis tenuis, commonly known as the slender beach grass, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the eastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is adapted to sandy, saline environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated. It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion along shorelines.

Its ecological significance makes it a key species in coastal plant communities.

Morphology

Aegialitis tenuis has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, with a pale green color and a slightly waxy texture.

The stem is slender and cylindrical, with a smooth, light green bark that grows upright.
