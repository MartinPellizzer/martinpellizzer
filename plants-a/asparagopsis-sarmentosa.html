Asparagopsis sarmentosa

Asparagopsis sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping asparagus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices. The plant features slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats or clumps. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are often tinged with a bluish hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers.

Asparagopsis sarmentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
