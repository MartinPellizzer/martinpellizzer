Atriplex autranii

Atriplex autranii, commonly known as the desert saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found growing in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in areas with low precipitation.

Atriplex autranii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
