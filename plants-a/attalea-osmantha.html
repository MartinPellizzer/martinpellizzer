Attalea osmantha

Attalea osmantha, commonly known as the Oshima palm, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is a medium-sized palm with a slender trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are pollinated by insects and birds. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, containing a single seed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
