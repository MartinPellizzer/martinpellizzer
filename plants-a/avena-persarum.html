Avena persarum

Avena persarum, commonly known as the Persian oat, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. Avena persarum is native to regions of Asia, particularly Iran and Central Asia.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
