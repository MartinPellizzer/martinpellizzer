Astragalus alopecuroides f. willdenowii

Astragalus alopecuroides f. willdenowii, commonly known as the Willdenow's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in early summer. It is native to regions of Central Asia and is often found in dry, rocky or sandy soils.

This subspecies is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
