Amaranthus crispus

Amaranthus crispus, commonly known as red amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, pink, or purple bracts that surround the small, green flowers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into three-angled, bead-like seeds. Its leaves are often consumed as a leafy green vegetable in various cuisines.

Amaranthus crispus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and nutritional content.
