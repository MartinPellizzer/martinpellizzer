Abies × insignis f. mastersiana

Abies × insignis f. mastersiana, commonly known as the Masters' fir, is a hybrid coniferous tree belonging to the genus Abies.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like foliage that is often bluish-white underneath. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in horticultural settings.
