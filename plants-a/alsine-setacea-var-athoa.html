Alsine setacea var. athoa

Alsine setacea var. athoa, commonly known as the Athoas alsine, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or maritime environments. Its leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces seeds in small, dry capsules.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to saline conditions, making it a hardy species in challenging habitats.
