Aleuritia brachystoma

Aleuritia brachystoma, commonly known as the broadleaf alder, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. Its fruits are small drupes that mature to a reddish color, often containing a single seed.

Aleuritia brachystoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
