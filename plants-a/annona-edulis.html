Annona edulis

Annona edulis, commonly known as the sugar apple, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, with broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that develop into green, spiny fruits. The fruit has a thick, yellow or green rind and a soft, sweet, custard-like pulp.

It is rich in nutrients and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
