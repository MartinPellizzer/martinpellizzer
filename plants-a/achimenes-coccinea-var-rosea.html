Achimenes coccinea var. rosea

Achimenes coccinea var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered achimenes, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, rose-pink to lavender flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
