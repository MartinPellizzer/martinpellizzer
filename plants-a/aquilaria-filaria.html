Aquilaria filaria

Aquilaria filaria, commonly known as the lesser agarwood tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its aromatic wood, which is highly valued in traditional medicine and incense production. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers and has simple, opposite leaves. Its unique fragrance is derived from the resinous secretions that form in the heartwood, which is used in various cultural and medicinal practices.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in tropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.
