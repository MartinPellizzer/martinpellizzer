Adonis sachalinensis

Adonis sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin adonis, is a perennial herb native to the Russian Far East and northern Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
