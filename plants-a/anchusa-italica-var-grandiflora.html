Anchusa italica var. grandiflora

Anchusa italica var. grandiflora, commonly known as Italian bugloss, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has basal and stem leaves that are hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Anchusa italica var. grandiflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
