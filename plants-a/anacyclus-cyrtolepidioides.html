Anacyclus cyrtolepidioides

Anacyclus cyrtolepidioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seed heads.

Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and often have a slightly serrated edge.
