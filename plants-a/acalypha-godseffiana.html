Acalypha godseffiana

Acalypha godseffiana, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, golden-yellow bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous true flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Acalypha godseffiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
