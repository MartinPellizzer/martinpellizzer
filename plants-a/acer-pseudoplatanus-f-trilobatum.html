Acer pseudoplatanus f. trilobatum

Acer pseudoplatanus f. trilobatum, commonly known as the three-lobed maple, is a rare and distinctive variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its uniquely shaped leaves, which exhibit three prominent lobes, giving it a striking appearance. This form is typically found in specific microhabitats, often in shaded, moist environments. The leaves are usually a vibrant green in summer and turn red or orange in autumn.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
