Asclepias × kansana

Asclepias × kansana, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a hybrid species native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its striking, pink to red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces follicles that contain seeds with silky hairs, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various habitats.

Asclepias × kansana is an important host plant for monarch butterfly larvae, supporting biodiversity in native ecosystems.
