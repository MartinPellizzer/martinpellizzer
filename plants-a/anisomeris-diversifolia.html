Anisomeris diversifolia

Anisomeris diversifolia, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds with tufts of white hairs for wind dispersal. Anisomeris diversifolia is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils with low rainfall.

Its structure and growth habits make it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
