Aster stenotus

Aster stenotus, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Aster stenotus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
