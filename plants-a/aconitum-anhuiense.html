Aconitum anhuiense

Aconitum anhuiense, commonly known as the Anhui aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy flowers. The plant produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are palmately divided with a distinctive appearance. Aconitum anhuiense contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
