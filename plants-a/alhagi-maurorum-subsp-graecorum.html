Alhagi maurorum subsp. graecorum

Alhagi maurorum subsp.

graecorum, commonly known as Greek goatweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family. It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its slender stems and small, compound leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

This subspecies is native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.
