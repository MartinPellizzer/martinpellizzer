Astragalus lindheimeri var. bellus

Astragalus lindheimeri var. bellus, commonly known as the Lindheimer's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the legume family, Fabaceae.
