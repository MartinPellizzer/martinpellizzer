Arabis inamoena

Arabis inamoena, commonly known as the rockcress or white speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and arranged in clusters, often blooming in early spring.

Arabis inamoena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
