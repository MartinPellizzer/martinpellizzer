Arthrocnemum heptiflorum

Arthrocnemum heptiflorum, commonly known as the seven-flowered jointed grass, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its jointed stems and small, clustered leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in summer, which are arranged in clusters of seven, giving it its common name. It is adapted to survive in saline and sandy soils, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.

Its structure and growth patterns make it an important component of desert flora and a subject of study in botanical ecology.
