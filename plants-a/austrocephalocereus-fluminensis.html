Austrocephalocereus fluminensis

Austrocephalocereus fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian night bloomer, is a cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and sparse, spiny foliage. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique flowering behavior and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for botanical gardens and collectors.
