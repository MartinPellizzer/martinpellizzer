Aerides difformis

Aerides difformis, commonly known as the Variable Vanda, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences and showy, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The flowers bloom in clusters, often lasting several weeks, making it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation.
