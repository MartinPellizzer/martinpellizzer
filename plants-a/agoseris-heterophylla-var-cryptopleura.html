Agoseris heterophylla var. cryptopleura

Agoseris heterophylla var. cryptopleura, commonly known as the narrow-leaf goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. The plant is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in native plant gardens for their ornamental value and drought tolerance.
