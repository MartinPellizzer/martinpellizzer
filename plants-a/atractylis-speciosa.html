Atractylis speciosa

Atractylis speciosa, commonly known as the golden marguerite or sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in the summer. The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Atractylis speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
