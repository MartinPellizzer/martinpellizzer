Aloe enotata

Aloe enotata, commonly known as the wine aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps it store water. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which bloom in summer. Aloe enotata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid climates.
