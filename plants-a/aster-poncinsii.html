Aster poncinsii

Aster poncinsii, commonly known as the Poncin's aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular plant among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
