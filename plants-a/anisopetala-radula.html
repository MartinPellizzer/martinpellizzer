Anisopetala radula

Anisopetala radula, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its ethereal common name. The plant has a unique reproductive system, producing small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat.
