Abrus pulchellus

Abrus pulchellus, commonly known as the pretty or beautiful bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features palmately lobed leaves and produces striking red or orange flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant's most notable characteristic is its dark red, bead-like seeds encased in a fibrous pod. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks for its vibrant floral display.
