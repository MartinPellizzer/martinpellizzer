Amorphophallus coffeatus

Amorphophallus coffeatus, commonly known as the coffee-leaf arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces a large, fleshy underground tuber that serves as its storage organ. Its flowers are typically unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and are often surrounded by a colorful spathe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
