Astephanus leptophyllus

Astephanus leptophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf stephanus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in a compact cluster.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
