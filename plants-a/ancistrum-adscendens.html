Ancistrum adscendens

Ancistrum adscendens, commonly known as the climbing bromeliad, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Bromeliaceae family, characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central pool to collect water. The plant produces striking, tubular inflorescences that are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are typically long and narrow, adapted for climbing and supporting the plant's growth in shaded forest environments.

Ancistrum adscendens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and unique growth habits.
