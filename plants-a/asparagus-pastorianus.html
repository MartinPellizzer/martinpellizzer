Asparagus pastorianus

Asparagus pastorianus, commonly known as the beer asparagus, is a climbing shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

Due to its historical use in brewing, it has gained attention for its potential medicinal and aromatic properties.
