Acacia umbellifera

Acacia umbellifera, commonly known as the umbrella acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-like arrangement of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces flat, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to drought conditions.

Acacia umbellifera plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various native animals.
