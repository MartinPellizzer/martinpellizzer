Anthericum pulchellum

Anthericum pulchellum, commonly known as the purple marsh-orchid, is a striking member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of vibrant purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments, thriving in areas with consistently moist soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Anthericum pulchellum is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological importance in wetland habitats.
