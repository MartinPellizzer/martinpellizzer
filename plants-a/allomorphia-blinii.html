Allomorphia blinii

Allomorphia blinii, commonly known as the silverleaf fern, is a rare and delicate plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features finely divided, silvery-green fronds that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil. Its reproductive structures are inconspicuous, relying on spores for propagation rather than seeds.

Due to habitat loss, Allomorphia blinii is considered endangered and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation purposes.
