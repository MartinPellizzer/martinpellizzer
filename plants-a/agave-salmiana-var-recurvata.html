Agave salmiana var. recurvata

Agave salmiana var. recurvata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The leaves are narrow and recurved, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It produces an inflorescence of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

This species is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
