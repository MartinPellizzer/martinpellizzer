Aristida utilis var. grandiflora

Aristida utilis var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, fluffy panicles of pale green to brownish flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and a fine texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable choice for native gardens.

Its delicate appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable ornamental and ecological plant in the landscape.
