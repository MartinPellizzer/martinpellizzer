Arisaema ishizuchiense var. alpicola

Arisaema ishizuchiense var. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine arrowhead, is a variant of the Arisaema genus native to high-altitude regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its unique, arrow-shaped leaves and a striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include a tuberous rhizome and a single, showy flower cluster.

This variant is of particular interest to botanists due to its distinct morphology and limited distribution.
