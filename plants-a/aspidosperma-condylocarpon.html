Aspidosperma condylocarpon

Aspidosperma condylocarpon, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Cupania condylocarpon*, is a species of flowering plant in the family Sapotaceae.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that turn red when mature.

It is native to tropical South America, particularly Brazil, and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
