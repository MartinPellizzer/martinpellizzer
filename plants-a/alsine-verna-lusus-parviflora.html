Alsine verna lusus parviflora

Alsine verna lusus parviflora, commonly known as the slender alsike, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features small, pale blue or pink flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
