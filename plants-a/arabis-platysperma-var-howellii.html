Arabis platysperma var. howellii

Arabis platysperma var. howellii, commonly known as Howell's broad-seeded rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which is characterized by its small, cross-shaped flowers and distinctive seed pods. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lobed or toothed, and it produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
