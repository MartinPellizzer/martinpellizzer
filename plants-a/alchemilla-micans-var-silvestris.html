Alchemilla micans var. silvestris

Alchemilla micans var. silvestris, commonly known as the silvery plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit with silver-velvet leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
