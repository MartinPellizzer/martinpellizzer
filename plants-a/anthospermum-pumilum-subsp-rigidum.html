Anthospermum pumilum subsp. rigidum

Anthospermum pumilum subsp.

rigidum, commonly known as the rigid antelope's tail, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, rigid stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
