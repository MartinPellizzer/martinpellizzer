Abrus fruticulosus

Abrus fruticulosus, commonly known as the hairy spider plant, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, woody stems that twine around supports, and its leaves are trifoliate, with three ovate to elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, inflated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. The seeds are coated in a bright red aril, which is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
