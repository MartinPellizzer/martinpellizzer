Asphodelus cerasifer f. rouxii

Asphodelus cerasifer f. rouxii, commonly known as the cherry-flowered asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and showy, cherry-red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowering stems are usually solitary, and the inflorescence consists of dense clusters of tubular flowers.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
