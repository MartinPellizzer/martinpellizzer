Adenocalymma contractum

Adenocalymma contractum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandmat, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has a robust root system that helps it survive in harsh, dry environments. It is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
