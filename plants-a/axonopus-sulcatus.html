Axonopus sulcatus

Axonopus sulcatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf axonopus, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowering spikes are small and inconspicuous, producing tiny grains of pollen.

Axonopus sulcatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms in its native ecosystems.
