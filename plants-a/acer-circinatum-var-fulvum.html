Acer circinatum var. fulvum

Acer circinatum var. fulvum, commonly known as the golden alder, is a variant of the Pacific silver fir.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow foliage, which becomes more pronounced in the fall. The plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its vibrant seasonal colors.
