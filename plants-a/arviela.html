Arviela

Arviela, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while its stems are hairy and woody.

Arviela is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and unique blooming pattern.
