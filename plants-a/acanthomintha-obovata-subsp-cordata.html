Acanthomintha obovata subsp. cordata

Acanthomintha obovata subsp.

cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf acanthomintha, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its obovate to heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a desirable plant for native plant gardens.
