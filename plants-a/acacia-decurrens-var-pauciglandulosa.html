Acacia decurrens var. pauciglandulosa

Acacia decurrens var. pauciglandulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and small, yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes. The leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, thread-like leaflets. This variety is distinguished by its reduced number of glands compared to other acacia species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.
