Anchusa hybrida subsp. capellii

Anchusa hybrida subsp.

capellii, commonly known as the Spanish bugloss, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Anchusa hybrida subsp.

capellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its vibrant blooms.
