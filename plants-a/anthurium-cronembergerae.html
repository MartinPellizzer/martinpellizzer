Anthurium cronembergerae

Anthurium cronembergerae, commonly known as the Cronembergerae anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often a deep green with a glossy texture, and it produces colorful flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow.

This species is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
