Anoectochilus rhombilabius

Anoectochilus rhombilabius, commonly known as the rhombic slipper orchid, is a rare and highly sought-after orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped pouch that houses its pollinia. The plant typically grows as a small epiphyte or lithophyte, often found on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is a popular subject in botanical research and conservation efforts.
