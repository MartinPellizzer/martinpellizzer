Amalophyllon laceratum

Amalophyllon laceratum, commonly known as the fragmented leaf plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, often lobed leaves that exhibit a distinctive pattern of fragmentation, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as tropical forests or along stream banks. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, which help to retain moisture and protect against herbivores.

Amalophyllon laceratum is valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
