Azara subandina

Azara subandina, commonly known as the Andean azara, is a small evergreen tree native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, red fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

Azara subandina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
