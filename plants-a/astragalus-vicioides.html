Astragalus vicioides

Astragalus vicioides, commonly known as the vicia-like milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to dry environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Astragalus vicioides is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.
