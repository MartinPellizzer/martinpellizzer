Anisacanthus ruber

Anisacanthus ruber, commonly known as the red flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has woody stems and thick, leathery leaves that are adapted to arid environments. It is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its drought tolerance.

Anisacanthus ruber is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
