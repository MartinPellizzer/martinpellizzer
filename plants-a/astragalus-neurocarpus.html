Astragalus neurocarpus

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America, particularly in the southwestern United States and parts of Mexico. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its compound leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are narrow and pointed.

Astragalus neurocarpus is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.
