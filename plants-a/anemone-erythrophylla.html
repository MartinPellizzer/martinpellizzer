Anemone erythrophylla

Anemone erythrophylla, commonly known as the scarlet anemone, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It produces showy, pink to red flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open meadows and rocky slopes.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
