Acanthea virilis

Acanthea virilis, commonly known as the stiffleaf acanthus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, palmate leaves. The plant produces showy, spikelike inflorescences with violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
