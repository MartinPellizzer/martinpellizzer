Actinidia sorbifolia

Actinidia sorbifolia, commonly known as the Siberian grapevine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
