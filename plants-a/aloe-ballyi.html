Aloe ballyi

Aloe ballyi, commonly known as the Bally's aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The leaves are typically green with a waxy coating, and they often have a serrated edge. It produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are clustered in a raceme.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional remedies.
