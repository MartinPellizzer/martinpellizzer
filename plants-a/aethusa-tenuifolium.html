Aethusa tenuifolium

Aethusa tenuifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved cow-wheat, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, and it is often found in temperate regions.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
