Arctostaphylos montana

Arctostaphylos montana, commonly known as the Sierra snowbrush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, leathery berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Arctostaphylos montana is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
