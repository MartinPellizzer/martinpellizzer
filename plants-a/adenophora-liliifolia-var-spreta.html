Adenophora liliifolia var. spreta

Adenophora liliifolia var. spreta, commonly known as the pink bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. Its flowers are tubular at the base and flare out at the top, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
