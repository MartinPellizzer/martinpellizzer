Anthodon paniculatum

Anthodon paniculatum, commonly known as the panicle grass, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and branched inflorescences. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are often wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture that contributes to its structural strength.

Anthodon paniculatum is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
