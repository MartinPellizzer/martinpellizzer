Aristida adscensionis subsp. guineensis

Aristida adscensionis subsp.

guineensis, commonly known as Guinea dropseed, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, while the stems are wiry and flexible.

This species is adapted to warm climates and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its fine, grass-like appearance.
