Achillea crithmifolia var. alsatica

Achillea crithmifolia var. alsatica, commonly known as the Alpine yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features a compact growth habit with silver-gray, fern-like leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. It is often used in rock gardens and alpine gardens for its ornamental value and hardiness.

The species is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
