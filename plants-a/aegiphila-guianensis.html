Aegiphila guianensis

Aegiphila guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Guyanas and northern Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple and are clustered in whorls along the stems.

Aegiphila guianensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
