Arabis semisepulta

Arabis semisepulta, commonly known as the half-sepaled rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
