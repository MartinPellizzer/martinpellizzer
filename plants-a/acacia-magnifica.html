Acacia magnifica

Acacia magnifica, commonly known as the flame acacia, is a striking tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, feathery leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture.

Additionally, the tree plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, providing food and shelter for various species.
