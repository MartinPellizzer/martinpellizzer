Astragalus oxyphysopsis

Astragalus oxyphysopsis, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, dry habitats, such as slopes and gravelly soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
