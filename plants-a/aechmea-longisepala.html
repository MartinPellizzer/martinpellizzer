Aechmea longisepala

Aechmea longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepal aechmea, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding cup, a feature common among bromeliads. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant, long-lasting flowers that range in color from red to orange. Its leaves are typically broad and covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention.

Aechmea longisepala is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea longisepala has broad, sword-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette formation, with a dark green color and a waxy texture.

The plant has a short, thick stem with a smooth, reddish-brown bark that grows upright. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of violet flowers, which bloom during the summer months and are pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by birds and mammals.

The roots are fibrous and help anchor the plant while absorbing water and nutrients from the soil.
