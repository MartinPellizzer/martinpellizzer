Arabis scaposa var. elatior

Arabis scaposa var. elatior, commonly known as the tall rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, arranged in a rosette, and are covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This variety is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cool, alpine environments.
