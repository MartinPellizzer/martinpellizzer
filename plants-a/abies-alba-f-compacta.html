Abies alba f. compacta

Abies alba f. compacta, commonly known as the compact form of the European silver fir, is a unique variant characterized by its dense, slow-growing foliage.

This form exhibits a more rounded and compact growth habit compared to the typical straight trunk of the species. It features needle-like leaves that are shorter and more closely packed, giving it a bushy appearance. The plant retains its needles year-round, making it an evergreen with a distinctive visual appeal.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes due to its aesthetic value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
