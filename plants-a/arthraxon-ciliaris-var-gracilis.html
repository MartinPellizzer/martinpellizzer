Arthraxon ciliaris var. gracilis

Arthraxon ciliaris var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a grass species native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, narrow leaves and slender, upright stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
