Anthemis nabataea

Anthemis nabataea, commonly known as the Nabataean gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and coastal areas.

The plant has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
