Astropanax procumbens

Astropanax procumbens, commonly known as the creeping ginseng, is a rare and endangered plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red berries. Its roots are highly valued in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
