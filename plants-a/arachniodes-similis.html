Arachniodes similis

Arachniodes similis, commonly known as the spider fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that resemble a spider's web, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

Arachniodes similis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in humid, indoor conditions.
