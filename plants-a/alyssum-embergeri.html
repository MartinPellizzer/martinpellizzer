Alyssum embergeri

Alyssum embergeri, commonly known as the Emberger's alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces white or pale yellow blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Alyssum embergeri is often used in garden landscapes for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
