Archidendropsis lentiscifolia

Archidendropsis lentiscifolia, commonly known as the African snowbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with multiple stems forming a bushy shape. Its leaves are leathery and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine.

The species is adapted to arid environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
