Adonis apennina var. dahurica

Adonis apennina var. dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian glory-of-the-snow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and Eastern Europe.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and early blooming habit, it is often cultivated in gardens and alpine settings.
