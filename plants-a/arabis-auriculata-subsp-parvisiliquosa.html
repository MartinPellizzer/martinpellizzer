Arabis auriculata subsp. parvisiliquosa

Arabis auriculata subsp.

parvisiliquosa, commonly known as the small-silique Arabis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which is characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact silique fruits compared to other members of the species.
