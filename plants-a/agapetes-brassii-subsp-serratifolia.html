Agapetes brassii subsp. serratifolia

Agapetes brassii subsp.

serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated leaf agapete, is a shrub native to the cloud forests of South America. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters.

The leaves are typically narrow and serrated, giving the plant its subspecific name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high elevations.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
