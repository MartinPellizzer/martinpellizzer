Armeria filicaulis subsp. filicaulis

Armeria filicaulis subsp.

filicaulis, commonly known as the broad-leaved thrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, low-growing clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wool, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This subspecies is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand coastal conditions and salt spray.
