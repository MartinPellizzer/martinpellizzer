Ancistrum repens

Ancistrum repens, commonly known as the wandering spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescences. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ancistrum repens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
