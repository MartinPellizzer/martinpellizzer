Aquilegia sylvestris

Aquilegia sylvestris, commonly known as the wild columbine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in woodland and meadow habitats. The plant features striking, spurred petals that come in shades of red, yellow, and orange, while its leaves are deeply lobed and trifoliate. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aquilegia sylvestris is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
