Aria cambrensis

Aria cambrensis, commonly known as the Welsh mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Wales.

It typically grows in rocky, acidic soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It is known for its ornamental value and is often found in alpine meadows and woodland edges.

Aria cambrensis is also of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude conditions.
