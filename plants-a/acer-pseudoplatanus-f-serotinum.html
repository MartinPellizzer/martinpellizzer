Acer pseudoplatanus f. serotinum

Acer pseudoplatanus f. serotinum, commonly known as the late-flowering maple, is a cultivar of the European maple.

It is characterized by its delayed leaf emergence and vibrant fall foliage. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub with a rounded crown. Its leaves are palmately lobed and turn bright red or orange in autumn.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
