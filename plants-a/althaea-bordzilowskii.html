Althaea bordzilowskii

Althaea bordzilowskii, commonly known as the Caucasian marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and heart-shaped leaves covered in a soft, fuzzy layer. The plant produces showy, pale pink to white flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are rich in mucilage, making it valuable in traditional medicine for its soothing properties.

Althaea bordzilowskii thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and riverbanks.
