Acronia linguifera

Acronia linguifera, commonly known as the tongue-shaped acronia, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, with elongated, tongue-like leaves that are often variegated in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Acronia linguifera is primarily found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticultural and ecological studies.
