Astragalus schischkinianus

Astragalus schischkinianus, commonly known as Schischkin's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Astragalus schischkinianus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
