Acerates paniculata

Acerates paniculata, commonly known as the Chinese sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its small, white, clustered flowers that appear in early spring. The plant has alternate, simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a small, red berry that develops in late summer.
