Asplenium hemionitideum

Asplenium hemionitideum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and have a feathery appearance. The plant is native to parts of Africa, particularly in regions with moist, shaded environments. It thrives in humid conditions and is often found growing on rock surfaces or in crevices.

Asplenium hemionitideum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
