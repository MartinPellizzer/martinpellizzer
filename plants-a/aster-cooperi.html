Aster cooperi

Aster cooperi, commonly known as Cooper's aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and produces showy purple to blue flowers in the fall. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

Cooper's aster is also an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
