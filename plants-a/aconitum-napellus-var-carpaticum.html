Aconitum napellus var. carpaticum

Aconitum napellus var. carpaticum, commonly known as the Carpathian monkshood, is a striking perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hooded flower that is purple to blue in color. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and meadows.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
