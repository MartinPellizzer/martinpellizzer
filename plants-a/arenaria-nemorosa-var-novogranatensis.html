Arenaria nemorosa var. novogranatensis

Arenaria nemorosa var. novogranatensis, commonly known as the New Granada rock sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with dense clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are usually linear to lanceolate, and the flowers bloom in early to mid-summer.

This variety is noted for its horticultural value and ability to thrive in harsh, arid conditions.
