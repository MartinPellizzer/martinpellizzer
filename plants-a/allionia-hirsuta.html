Allionia hirsuta

Allionia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy allionia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, showy flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink or purple. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also bear a hairy texture.

Allionia hirsuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
