Atriplex montevidensis

Atriplex montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidense saltbush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in coastal and arid regions.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in dry environments. The plant typically grows in sandy or saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Atriplex montevidensis plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
