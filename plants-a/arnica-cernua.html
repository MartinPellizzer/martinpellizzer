Arnica cernua

Arnica cernua, commonly known as the nodding arnica, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Arnica cernua is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
