Anguria jacquiniana

Anguria jacquiniana, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Anguria jacquiniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
