Acacia calamifolia

Acacia calamifolia, commonly known as the sickle acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, sickle-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in local medicine for their purported healing properties.

Acacia calamifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
