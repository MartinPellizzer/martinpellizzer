Amomum echinosphaera

Amomum echinosphaera, commonly known as the spiky cardamom, is a tropical plant belonging to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of India, China, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its erect, branched stems and large, elliptical leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces aromatic, dark brown fruits that are used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its essential oils and spice-like aroma.
