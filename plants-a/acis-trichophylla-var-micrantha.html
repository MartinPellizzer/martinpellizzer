Acis trichophylla var. micrantha

Acis trichophylla var. micrantha, commonly known as the small-leaved acis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. Its flowering period is short, and it is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

The variety micrantha is distinguished by its smaller leaves and reduced floral structures compared to the typical form.
