Anamirta jucunda

Anamirta jucunda, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1-2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by distinctive, elongated fruits that turn from green to reddish when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly velvety texture, contributing to its common name.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
