Aspidium cicutarium var. angustius

Aspidium cicutarium var. angustius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a variant of the water fern species native to wetland and marsh environments.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped fronds that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in temperate regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
