Asphodelus albus subsp. sphaerocarpus

Asphodelus albus subsp.

sphaerocarpus, commonly known as the white asphodel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive spherical, fleshy corms, which store water and nutrients. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
