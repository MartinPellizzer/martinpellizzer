Acaena aculeata

Acaena aculeata, commonly known as New Zealand thistle, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a low-growing shrub that produces small, spiny fruits known as achenes. The plant has dark green, hairy leaves and produces purple or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and forest edges.

Acaena aculeata is often considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its aggressive growth and persistent seeds.
