Arundinaria ramosa

Arundinaria ramosa, commonly known as the bamboo fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching fronds that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant produces spores on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Arundinaria ramosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
