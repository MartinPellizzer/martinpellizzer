Aster leiocarpus

Aster leiocarpus, commonly known as the white aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy white to pale yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster leiocarpus is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
