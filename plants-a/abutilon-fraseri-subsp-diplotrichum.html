Abutilon fraseri subsp. diplotrichum

Abutilon fraseri subsp.

diplotrichum, commonly known as the Fraser's abutilon, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its fibrous roots and branching structure make it adaptable to various soil conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
