Astragalus armatus subsp. armatus

Astragalus armatus subsp.

armatus, commonly known as the spiked milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit, hairy stems, and small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters.

The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to dry conditions and often grows in sandy or gravelly soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
