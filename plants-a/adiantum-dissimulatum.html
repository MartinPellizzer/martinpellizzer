Adiantum dissimulatum

Adiantum dissimulatum, commonly known as the hidden maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lacy fronds that resemble those of other maidenhair ferns. The plant prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a unique, almost hidden appearance due to their subtle coloration.

Adiantum dissimulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
