Avena dispermis

Avena dispermis, commonly known as the two-seeded oat, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that contain two seeds per glume, a feature that gives it its scientific name. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open fields or along roadsides.

Avena dispermis is valued for its potential in forage and conservation efforts due to its hardy nature.
