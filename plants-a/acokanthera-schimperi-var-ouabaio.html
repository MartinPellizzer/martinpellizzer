Acokanthera schimperi var. ouabaio

Acokanthera schimperi var. ouabaio, commonly known as the African arrow poison or Ouabaio, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is notable for its toxic sap, which contains cardiac glycosides used by indigenous peoples for hunting and traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it both culturally and botanically significant in its native range.
