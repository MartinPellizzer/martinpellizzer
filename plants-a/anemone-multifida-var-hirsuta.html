Anemone multifida var. hirsuta

Anemone multifida var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy multifid anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of finely divided, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, white to pink flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its hairy stems and leaves are adapted to thrive in moist, shady environments, such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
