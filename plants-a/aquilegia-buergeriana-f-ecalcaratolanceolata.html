Aquilegia buergeriana f. ecalcaratolanceolata

Aquilegia buergeriana f. ecalcaratolanceolata, commonly known as the Buerger's delphinium, is a variant of the delphinium genus characterized by its unique floral morphology.

It features elongated, lanceolate petals that are often tinged with red or purple at the base, giving it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to challenging garden conditions.
