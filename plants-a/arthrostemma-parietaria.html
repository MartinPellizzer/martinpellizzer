Arthrostemma parietaria

Arthrostemma parietaria, commonly known as the wall arthrostemma, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Arthrostemma parietaria is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in supporting local insect populations.
