Acacia conjunctifolia

Acacia conjunctifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, phyllodes that are typically green and narrow. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which bloom in spring and attract various pollinators. Acacia conjunctifolia is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
