Acinodendron quadrangulare

Acinodendron quadrangulare, commonly known as the four-angled acacia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its quadrangular stems and compound leaves with small, paired leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its fruits are pod-like structures that contain several seeds.

It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
