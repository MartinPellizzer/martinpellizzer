Agropyron repens var. caesium

Agropyron repens var. caesium, commonly known as silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green leaves and dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous slender spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone environments.
