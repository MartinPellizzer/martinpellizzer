Artocarpus excelsus

Artocarpus excelsus, commonly known as the giant breadfruit, is a large tropical tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its broad, palm-like leaves and produces large, spiky fruits that are typically harvested when mature. The fruit has a thick outer rind and a starchy, edible pulp inside, which is used in various traditional dishes. The tree is often found in lowland rainforests and can grow up to 30 meters in height.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for a variety of wildlife.
