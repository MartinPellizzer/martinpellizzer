Actinea richardsonii

Actinea richardsonii, commonly known as the Richardson's actinia, is a species of sea anemone found in the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent tentacles and a short, fleshy stem. This marine organism thrives in intertidal zones, often attaching itself to rocks or other substrates. Its coloration can vary, typically ranging from pale pink to orange, depending on environmental conditions.

Actinea richardsonii plays a role in marine ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various small aquatic organisms.
