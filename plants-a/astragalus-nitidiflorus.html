Astragalus nitidiflorus

Astragalus nitidiflorus, commonly known as the smoothflower milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.

Astragalus nitidiflorus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators in its native range.
