Aspalathus retroflexa subsp. retroflexa

Aspalathus retroflexa subsp.

retroflexa, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a small shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and is often used in traditional medicine.
