Arabis polytricha

Arabis polytricha, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine and subalpine regions. It is found across Europe, parts of Asia, and North Africa.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
