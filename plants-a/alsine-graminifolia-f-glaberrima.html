Alsine graminifolia f. glaberrima

Alsine graminifolia f. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth alsike, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites. Its smooth, glabrous leaves distinguish it from other varieties within the species.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in various habitats.
