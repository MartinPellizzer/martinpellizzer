Ajania manshurica

Ajania manshurica, commonly known as the Siberian ajania, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and often hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in temperate to subarctic climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in a head.

Ajania manshurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
