Agrostis capillaris f. setulosa

Agrostis capillaris f. setulosa, commonly known as the hairy bentgrass, is a variant of the common bentgrass species.

It is a low-growing, perennial grass that typically forms dense, mat-like growth. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinct hairy texture along the leaf blades. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lawns, meadows, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various environments, though it can be invasive in certain settings.
