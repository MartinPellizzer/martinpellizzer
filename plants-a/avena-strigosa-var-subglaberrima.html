Avena strigosa var. subglaberrima

Avena strigosa var. subglaberrima, commonly known as the slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its reduced leaf hairs, giving it a smooth appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its botanical structure makes it well-adapted to arid environments and contributes to its ecological significance in native plant communities.
