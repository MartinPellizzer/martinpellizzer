Arisaema harmandii

Arisaema harmandii, commonly known as the Harmand's arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are arrowhead-shaped, with a glossy green surface and a prominent midrib.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red berries, which contain the seeds.
