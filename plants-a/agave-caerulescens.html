Agave caerulescens

Agave caerulescens, commonly known as the blue agave, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stem with small, white or greenish flowers, which bloom after several years. Agave caerulescens is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid climates.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
