Aster laevis f. amplifolius

Aster laevis f. amplifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf aster, is a variant of the smooth aster, characterized by its larger, broader leaves compared to the typical species.

This plant is native to North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its flowers are typically blue to violet in color, with ray florets that bloom in the fall. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant fall color.

It is also a host plant for several species of butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
