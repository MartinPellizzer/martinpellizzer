Arabis nutans

Arabis nutans, commonly known as the nodding rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that droop downward, giving it its common name. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Arabis nutans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
