Alnus arguta var. genuina

Alnus arguta var. genuina, commonly known as the Alaska white cedar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that turn yellow in autumn and its catkin-like flowers that produce abundant seeds. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and coastal areas. It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed sites make it a valuable plant for reforestation and ecological restoration projects.
