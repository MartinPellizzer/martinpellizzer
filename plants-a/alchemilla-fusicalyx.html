Alchemilla fusicalyx

Alchemilla fusicalyx, commonly known as the golden alchemilla, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are basal, arranged in a rosette, and are covered in fine, silvery hairs.

Alchemilla fusicalyx is often found in moist, calcareous soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
