Actinocephalus ochrocephalus

Actinocephalus ochrocephalus, commonly known as the yellow-headed daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with a central disc. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
