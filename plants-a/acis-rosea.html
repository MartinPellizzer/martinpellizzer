Acis rosea

Acis rosea, commonly known as the pink snowbell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to white, blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Acis rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
