Ammanthus glaberrimus

Ammanthus glaberrimus, commonly known as the smooth ammannia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its stems are often succulent, allowing it to store water, while its flowers are usually pink or red in color.

Ammanthus glaberrimus is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
