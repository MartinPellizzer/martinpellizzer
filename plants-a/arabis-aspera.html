Arabis aspera

Arabis aspera, commonly known as the rough rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. The plant features basal leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its common name. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Arabis aspera is typically perennial and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
