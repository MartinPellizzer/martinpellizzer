Anemone parviflora

Anemone parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
