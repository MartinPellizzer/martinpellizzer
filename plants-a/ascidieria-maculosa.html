Ascidieria maculosa

Ascidieria maculosa, commonly known as the spotted thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces purple to pink flowers in late summer. Its fruit is a bracteate schizocarp, which splits into two parts when mature.

Ascidieria maculosa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
