Abies minor

Abies minor, commonly known as the dwarf fir, is a small evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe.

It typically grows to a height of about 2 to 5 meters, featuring a conical shape with dense, dark green needles. The plant produces small, spherical cones that release seeds in the autumn, contributing to its reproductive cycle. Its foliage is fragrant, and the tree is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

Abies minor is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.
