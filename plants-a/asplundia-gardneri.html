Asplundia gardneri

Asplundia gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae, which includes plants such as taro and caladium. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often used in ornamental gardening. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Asplundia gardneri is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible rhizomes.
