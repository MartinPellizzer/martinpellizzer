Arthraxon satarensis

Arthraxon satarensis, commonly known as the Sataran grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
