Antirrhinum leiocalyx var. pubescens

Antirrhinum leiocalyx var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy-leaved snapdragon, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in clusters along the upper stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also bear a pubescent surface, contributing to its common name.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
