Asperula cynanchica subsp. capillacea

Asperula cynanchica subsp.

capillacea, commonly known as the slender asperula, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves that are typically opposite and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
