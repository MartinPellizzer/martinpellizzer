Albizia boromoensis

Albizia boromoensis, commonly known as the African silktree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The tree produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in savanna woodlands and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Albizia boromoensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
