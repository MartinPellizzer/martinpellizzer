Artocarpus dadah

Artocarpus dadah, commonly known as the red-fruit breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces a distinctive fruit with a rough, reddish-brown exterior and a soft, starchy pulp inside. The fruit is typically harvested when it is fully mature, and it is used in various traditional culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
