Alseodaphne dumicola

Alseodaphne dumicola, commonly known as the rock almond, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
