Aechmea schomburgkii

Aechmea schomburgkii, commonly known as the Schomburgk's pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of spiny, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces a tall flower spike with vibrant, showy bracts that can range from red to orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Aechmea schomburgkii is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea schomburgkii has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, acuminate, and leathery, arranged in a compact circle around the central stem.

The leaves are typically dark green with a silvery or white midrib, and they can grow up to 40 cm in length. The plant has a short, thick stem that is often covered in a dense, fibrous sheath, and it grows primarily from the base with no true woody bark. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, with small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn, primarily pollinated by insects.

The fruit is a capsule that releases small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind and water.
