Antimima herrei

Antimima herrei, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces yellow flowers resembling those of the common dandelion. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access moisture deep in the soil.

Antimima herrei plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
