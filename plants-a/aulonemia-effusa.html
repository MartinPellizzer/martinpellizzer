Aulonemia effusa

Aulonemia effusa, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has arrow-shaped leaves that are usually submerged or floating, with a distinctively broad base. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters near the water's surface.

Aulonemia effusa plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and fish.
