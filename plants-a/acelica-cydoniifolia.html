Acelica cydoniifolia

Acelica cydoniifolia, commonly known as the pineapple guava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white. The plant bears fruit that resembles a pineapple in appearance, with a spiky exterior and a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is often cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
