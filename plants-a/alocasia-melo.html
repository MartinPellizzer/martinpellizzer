Alocasia melo

Alocasia melo, commonly known as the elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure called a spadix. Alocasia melo thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its striking foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
