Aira caryophyllea subsp. intermedia

Aira caryophyllea subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate red grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale blue or purple flowers, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
