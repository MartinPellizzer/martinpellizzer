Anthurium hamiltonii

Anthurium hamiltonii, commonly known as the Hamilton's anthurium, is a tropical flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped bracts that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are actually modified leaves rather than flowers. The plant produces small, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium hamiltonii is popular as a ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
