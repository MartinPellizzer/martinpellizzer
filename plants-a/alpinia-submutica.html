Alpinia submutica

Alpinia submutica, commonly known as the fragrant ginger or mountain ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and ornamental plants. The plant features tall, narrow leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

Alpinia submutica thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal uses.
