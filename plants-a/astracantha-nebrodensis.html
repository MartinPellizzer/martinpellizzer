Astracantha nebrodensis

Astracantha nebrodensis, commonly known as the Sicilian thornvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with distinctive spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and is adapted to thrive in rocky or arid environments. Its botanical features make it an important component of local flora and a subject of interest in botanical studies.

It is also noted for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
