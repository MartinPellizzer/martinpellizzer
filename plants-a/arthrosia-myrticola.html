Arthrosia myrticola

Arthrosia myrticola, commonly known as the fragrant myrtle.

It is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States, particularly in the southeastern region. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, waxy leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
