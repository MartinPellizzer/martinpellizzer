Ampelopsis quinquefolia var. vitacea

Ampelopsis quinquefolia var. vitacea, commonly known as silver vine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically five-lobed and have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue to black berries. It thrives in partial to full sun and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Silver vine is also known for its ability to attract birds and its historical use in traditional medicine.
