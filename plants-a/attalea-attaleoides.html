Attalea attaleoides

Attalea attaleoides, commonly known as the Paraguayan palm, is a species native to South America, particularly Paraguay and northern Argentina.

It is a tall, slender palm tree that can reach heights of up to 15 meters, characterized by its pinnate leaves and clustered, spiny fruits. The plant thrives in wetland and marshy environments, making it an important component of the local ecosystem.

Its fruits are edible and have been traditionally used by indigenous communities for food and other purposes.
