Archidendron trichophyllum

Archidendron trichophyllum, commonly known as the hairy leaved legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several elliptical seeds.

Archidendron trichophyllum is often used in traditional medicine and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
