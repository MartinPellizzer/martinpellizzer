Achimenes ixtapaensis

Achimenes ixtapaensis, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a tropical perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. Its flowering period occurs during the warm seasons, producing clusters of showy bracts that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Achimenes ixtapaensis thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
