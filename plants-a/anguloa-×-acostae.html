Anguloa × acostae

Anguloa × acostae, commonly known as the hybrid angel's trumpets, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Anguloa chlorantha and Anguloa aurantiaca.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value, though it contains toxic alkaloids that require careful handling.

Its foliage is typically broad and dark green, adding to its visual appeal in gardens and greenhouses.
