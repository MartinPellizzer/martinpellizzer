Artemisia halophila var. glabrata

Artemisia halophila var. glabrata, commonly known as coastal wormwood, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and supporting local wildlife.
