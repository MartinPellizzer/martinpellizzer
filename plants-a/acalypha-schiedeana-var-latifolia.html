Acalypha schiedeana var. latifolia

Acalypha schiedeana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features striking, velvety leaves with a broad, lance-shaped form and a distinctive reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by fluffy, cotton-like seed heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
