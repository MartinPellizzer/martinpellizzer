Andropogon refractus

Andropogon refractus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and is adapted to a variety of soil types, including sandy and clay soils.

Andropogon refractus plays an important role in ecological systems by providing habitat and forage for wildlife.
