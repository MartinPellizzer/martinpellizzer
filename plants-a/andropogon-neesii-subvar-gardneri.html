Andropogon neesii subvar. gardneri

Andropogon neesii subvar. gardneri, commonly known as Gardner's bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant features narrow, blue-green blades and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often reddish in color. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for wetland restoration projects.
