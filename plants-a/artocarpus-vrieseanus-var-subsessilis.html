Artocarpus vrieseanus var. subsessilis

Artocarpus vrieseanus var. subsessilis, commonly known as the Java tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its rough, ridged bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, spiky fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are used for wrapping food, and the wood is valued for its durability and workability.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
