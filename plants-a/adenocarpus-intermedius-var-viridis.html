Adenocarpus intermedius var. viridis

Adenocarpus intermedius var. viridis, commonly known as the green intermediate grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in restoration projects for soil stabilization.
