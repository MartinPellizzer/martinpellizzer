Astragalus zagrosicus

Astragalus zagrosicus, commonly known as Zagros milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran and Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets that help reduce water loss, and it produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.

Astragalus zagrosicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
