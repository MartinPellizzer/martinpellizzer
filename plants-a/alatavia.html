Alatavia

Alatavia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the dense rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that shimmer with a faint bioluminescent glow at night. The plant's flowers are small, delicate, and emit a subtle, sweet fragrance that attracts nocturnal pollinators. Alatavia thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and unique botanical features, it is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists alike.
