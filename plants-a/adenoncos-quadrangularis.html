Adenoncos quadrangularis

Adenoncos quadrangularis, commonly known as the four-angle adenoncos, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its square-shaped stems, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its leaves are opposite and have a rough, textured surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
