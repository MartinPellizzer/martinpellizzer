Allium jubatum

Allium jubatum, commonly known as the giant onion or purple globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy purple or pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish tinge.

Allium jubatum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
