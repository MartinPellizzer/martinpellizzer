Adenocarpus × subdecorticans

Adenocarpus × subdecorticans, commonly known as the hybrid rock thistle, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, glandular-stemmed structure. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, typical of the genus Adenocarpus. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun and exhibiting drought tolerance.
