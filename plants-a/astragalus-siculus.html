Astragalus siculus

Astragalus siculus, commonly known as the Sicilian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus siculus is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
