Aira caryophyllea subsp. armoricana

Aira caryophyllea subsp.

armoricana, commonly known as the mountain air grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pinkish to reddish flower clusters that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or alpine garden settings.

Due to its ecological importance and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in native plant gardens.
