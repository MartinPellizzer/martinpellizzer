Argyrocome dimorpholepis

Argyrocome dimorpholepis, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its silvery-gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The silverleaf tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
