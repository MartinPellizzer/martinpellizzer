Astragalus turcomanicus var. elongatus

Astragalus turcomanicus var. elongatus, commonly known as the elongated milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Its morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
