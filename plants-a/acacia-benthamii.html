Acacia benthamii

Acacia benthamii, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and feathery leaves, which are typical of the Acacia genus. The plant thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to salty air and sandy soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Acacia benthamii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
