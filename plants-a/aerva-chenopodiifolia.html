Aerva chenopodiifolia

Aerva chenopodiifolia, commonly known as the desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with hairy, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its seeds are encased in a pithy, three-lobed capsule, and the plant is often used as a nutritious leafy vegetable in traditional diets.

Aerva chenopodiifolia is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in soil stabilization.
