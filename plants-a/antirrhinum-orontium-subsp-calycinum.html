Antirrhinum orontium subsp. calycinum

Antirrhinum orontium subsp.

calycinum, commonly known as the California antirrhinum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive yellow throat.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
