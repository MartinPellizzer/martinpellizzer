Acronia megaglossa

Acronia megaglossa, commonly known as the giant tongue fern, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, tongue-shaped fronds that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to spread and establish itself in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically a deep green and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture.

Acronia megaglossa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in humid, indoor settings.
