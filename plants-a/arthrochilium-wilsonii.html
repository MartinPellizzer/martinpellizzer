Arthrochilium wilsonii

Arthrochilium wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and hammocks.

Its floral structure is typical of the Orchidaceae family, with a three-parted lip that aids in pollination by insects.
