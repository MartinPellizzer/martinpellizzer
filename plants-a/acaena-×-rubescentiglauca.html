Acaena × rubescentiglauca

Acaena × rubescentiglauca, commonly known as the red-leaf acaena, is a hybrid plant species within the genus Acaena, which belongs to the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish foliage, which becomes more pronounced in cooler weather, and its small, spiny fruits that contain seeds. The plant is native to New Zealand and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It has a low-growing, mat-forming habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for erosion control and garden planting in temperate climates.
