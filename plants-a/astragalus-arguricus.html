Astragalus arguricus

Astragalus arguricus, commonly known as the golden milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. Astragalus arguricus is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which benefits soil fertility.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
