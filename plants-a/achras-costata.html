Achras costata

Achras costata, commonly known as the cannonball tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, round, yellow or orange fruits that resemble cannonballs, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its smooth, waxy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit contains a single large seed enclosed in a fibrous pulp, making it unique among its botanical relatives.
