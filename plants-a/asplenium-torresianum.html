Asplenium torresianum

Asplenium torresianum, commonly known as the Torresian maidenhair, is a fern native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble those of other maidenhair ferns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rock crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in the propagation of the plant.

The leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
