Amaranthus neglectus

Amaranthus neglectus, commonly known as the common amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red. The plant produces small, triangular seeds that are often used as a food source. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to different environments.

Amaranthus neglectus is also known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
