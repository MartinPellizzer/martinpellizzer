Aristolochia bilabiata subsp. maestrensis

Aristolochia bilabiata subsp.

maestrensis, commonly known as the Maestrense Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to parts of Central and South America. It is characterized by its distinctive, hooded flowers that are often maroon or purple in color.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is known for its aromatic foliage. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
