Aconitum pyramidale var. corsicum

Aconitum pyramidale var. corsicum, commonly known as Corsican larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately divided into 5-7 lobes, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
