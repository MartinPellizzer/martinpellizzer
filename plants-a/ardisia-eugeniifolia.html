Ardisia eugeniifolia

Ardisia eugeniifolia, commonly known as the glossy woodbine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Ardisia eugeniifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
