Anthemis tricornis

Anthemis tricornis, commonly known as the three-horned ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of individual flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often found in disturbed areas and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
