Acer japonicum var. vitifolium

Acer japonicum var. vitifolium, commonly known as the Japanese maple with grape leaves, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, lobed leaves that change color throughout the seasons, ranging from bright green in summer to fiery red or orange in autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 meters, featuring a compact, rounded canopy. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming more textured with age.
