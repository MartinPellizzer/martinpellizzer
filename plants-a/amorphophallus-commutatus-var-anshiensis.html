Amorphophallus commutatus var. anshiensis

Amorphophallus commutatus var. anshiensis, commonly known as the Anshi giant taro, is a striking tropical plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and a massive, spadix that can grow up to 3 meters in height, resembling a phallic structure, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, known for its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
