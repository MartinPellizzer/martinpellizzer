Allium guttatum subsp. tenorei

Allium guttatum subsp.

tenorei, commonly known as the Corsican leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and can grow up to 30 cm in height.

The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in a loose umbel, which bloom in the spring. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, leek-like flavor, making it a valuable addition to local cuisines.

This subspecies is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
