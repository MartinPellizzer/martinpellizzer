Austrocylindropuntia verschaffeltii var. hypsophila

Austrocylindropuntia verschaffeltii var. hypsophila, commonly known as the highland columnar cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a deep root system to access water in arid environments.

This variety is adapted to high-altitude conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.
