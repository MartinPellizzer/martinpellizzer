Alchornea glabrata

Alchornea glabrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or smoothleaf alchornea, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or forest edges.

Alchornea glabrata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
