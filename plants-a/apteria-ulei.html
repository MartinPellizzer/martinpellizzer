Apteria ulei

Apteria ulei, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its roots are extensive and adapted to absorb water from deep underground sources.

Apteria ulei is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
