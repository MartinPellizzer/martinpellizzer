Aster confertus

Aster confertus, commonly known as the dense aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and produces showy blue to purple flowers in the fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Aster confertus is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits to pollinators.
