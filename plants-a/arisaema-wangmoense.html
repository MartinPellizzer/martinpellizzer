Arisaema wangmoense

Arisaema wangmoense, commonly known as the Wangmo arrowhead, is a unique member of the Araceae family native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and an inflorescence that consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories at high altitudes. Its botanical features include a short rhizome and fibrous roots, which help it anchor in rocky or loamy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
