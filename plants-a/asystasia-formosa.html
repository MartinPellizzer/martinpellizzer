Asystasia formosa

Asystasia formosa, commonly known as the silverleaf asystasia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of violet or purple. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage and the delicate appearance of its blooms.
