Arabis sikokiana

Arabis sikokiana, commonly known as the Korean rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of lobed leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
