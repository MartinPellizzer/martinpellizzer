Arenaria graveolens var. athoa

Arenaria graveolens var. athoa, commonly known as the sweet Alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and are borne in dense clusters. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

The variety 'athoa' is noted for its particularly strong fragrance and compact growth habit.
