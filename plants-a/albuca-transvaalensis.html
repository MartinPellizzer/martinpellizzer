Albuca transvaalensis

Albuca transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal gladiolus, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-like, grass-like leaves and produces tall, erect stems with showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil. It blooms in late spring or early summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens.

Albuca transvaalensis is also valued for its ability to thrive in full sun and its low maintenance requirements.
