Acalypha hassleriana

Acalypha hassleriana, commonly known as the red veined nettle, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, featuring deep red veins against a dark green background. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are typically inconspicuous compared to its vibrant leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Acalypha hassleriana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental leaves and ability to add visual interest to shaded gardens.
