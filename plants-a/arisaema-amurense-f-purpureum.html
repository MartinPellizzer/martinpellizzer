Arisaema amurense f. purpureum

Arisaema amurense f. purpureum, commonly known as the purple-flowered arrowroot, is a variant of the arrowhead plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and striking purple bracts that encase the spadix. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a dark purple color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking unique foliage and floral displays.
