Arenaria frutescens

Arenaria frutescens, commonly known as sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites. The plant has small, rounded leaves and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early spring. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy blooms.

Sandspurry is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
