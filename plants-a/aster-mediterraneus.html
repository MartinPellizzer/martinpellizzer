Aster mediterraneus

Aster mediterraneus, commonly known as the Mediterranean aster, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in Mediterranean climates with mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk, and it blooms from late summer to early autumn.

Aster mediterraneus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
