Astragalus laguriformis

Astragalus laguriformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus laguriformis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
