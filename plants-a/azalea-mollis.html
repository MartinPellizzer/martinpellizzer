Azalea mollis

Azalea mollis, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture. Azalea mollis prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its blooming period usually occurs in spring, adding vibrant color to landscapes during that season.
