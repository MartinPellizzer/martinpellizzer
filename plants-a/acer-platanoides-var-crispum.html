Acer platanoides var. crispum

Acer platanoides var. crispum, commonly known as the curly maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy-edged leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. The tree typically grows to a height of 15-30 meters and has a rounded, symmetrical crown. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming furrowed with age.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens and parks.
