Agriophyllum tibeticum

Agriophyllum tibeticum, commonly known as Tibetan flax, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet and adjacent areas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its leaves are usually hairy and adapted to withstand harsh, arid conditions.

Agriophyllum tibeticum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
