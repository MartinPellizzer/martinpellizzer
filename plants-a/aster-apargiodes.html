Aster apargiodes

Aster apargiodes, commonly known as the slender aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like butterflies and bees.
