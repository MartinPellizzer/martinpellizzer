Acalypha beckii

Acalypha beckii, commonly known as the red-leaved acalypha, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red leaves that are often adorned with distinctive white veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its vibrant foliage.
