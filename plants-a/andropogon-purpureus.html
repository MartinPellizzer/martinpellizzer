Andropogon purpureus

Andropogon purpureus, commonly known as purpletop grass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and striking purple-tinted inflorescence. The plant typically reaches heights of 2 to 4 feet and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
