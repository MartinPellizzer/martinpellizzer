Acalypha prostrata

Acalypha prostrata, commonly known as prostrate spindle tree, is a low-growing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by three-lobed, spiky seed capsules. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
