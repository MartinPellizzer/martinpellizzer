Astracantha trojana

Astracantha trojana, commonly known as the Trojan thornvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Astracantha trojana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals.
