Alchemilla johnstonii

Alchemilla johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy texture, often with a distinctive yellow or rust-colored margin.

Alchemilla johnstonii is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
