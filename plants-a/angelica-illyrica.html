Angelica illyrica

Angelica illyrica, commonly known as the Balkan angelica, is a tall biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and celery, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in complex umbels, typical of the family. Its roots are often harvested for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Angelica illyrica is known for its strong, spicy scent and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
