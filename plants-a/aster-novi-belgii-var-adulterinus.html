Aster novi-belgii var. adulterinus

Aster novi-belgii var. adulterinus, commonly known as the Shasta daisy, is a cultivar of the aster family (Asteraceae) characterized by its showy, white ray florets surrounded by a yellow-orange disk.

It is native to the western United States and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright, branching stems and a compact, bushy habit. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, adding a vibrant touch to gardens.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
