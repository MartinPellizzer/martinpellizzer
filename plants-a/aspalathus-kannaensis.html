Aspalathus kannaensis

Aspalathus kannaensis, commonly known as the Kanna or "spear grass," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in grassland and savanna habitats, thriving in regions with moderate rainfall.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
