Ambaiba adenopus

Ambaiba adenopus, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its straight, dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has a dense canopy with elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in yellow or greenish hues.

The species is also known for its slow growth and is often found in lowland rainforests.
