Andromeda purpurea

Andromeda purpurea, commonly known as the purple andromeda, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, depending on the variety. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands.

Andromeda purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
