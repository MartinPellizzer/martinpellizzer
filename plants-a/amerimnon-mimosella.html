Amerimnon mimosella

Amerimnon mimosella, commonly known as the silky mimosin, is a small shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of numerous thread-like leaflets, giving it a silky appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
