Aleuritia gigantea

Aleuritia gigantea, commonly known as the giant aleurita, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features compound leaves with elongated leaflets that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that attracts birds and other wildlife.

Aleuritia gigantea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
