Ascolepis protea var. splendida

Ascolepis protea var. splendida, commonly known as the splendour candleplant, is a striking South African species belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, candle-like inflorescence composed of numerous yellow to orange ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is typically found in coastal regions of the Western and Eastern Cape provinces. Its showy appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it requires full sun and minimal water.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators.
