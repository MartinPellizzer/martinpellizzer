Amomum valetonii

Amomum valetonii, commonly known as the Vietnamese cardamom, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by capsule fruits containing aromatic seeds. It is often cultivated for its essential oils and used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is typically grown in shaded, well-drained soils.
