Artemisia dubjanskyana

Artemisia dubjanskyana, commonly known as the Dubjansky's wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage composed of narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in compact, spherical clusters called capitula. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its medicinal properties and hardy nature, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
