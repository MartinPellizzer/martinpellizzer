Anthemis ptarmicifolia

Anthemis ptarmicifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a flat-topped cluster, with ray florets surrounding a central disc. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a strong, aromatic scent.

Anthemis ptarmicifolia is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
