Abronia micrantha

Abronia micrantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage, with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often found in coastal dunes and other xeric habitats.
