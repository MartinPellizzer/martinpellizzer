Astragalus plumbeus

Astragalus plumbeus, commonly known as the bluish milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus plumbeus is often found in sagebrush steppe and is considered a perennial herb.
