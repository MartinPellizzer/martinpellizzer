Astragalus virgatus var. albiflorus

Astragalus virgatus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces small, hairy pods that contain seeds.

It is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats.
