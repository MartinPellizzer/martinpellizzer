Alsophila fulva

Alsophila fulva, commonly known as the golden tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive golden hue on its young fronds, which contrasts with the darker green of mature leaves. Alsophila fulva is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and along stream banks.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
