Achillea intercedens

Achillea intercedens, commonly known as the lesser yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and flat, composite flower heads that range in color from white to pale yellow. Native to southern Europe and western Asia, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas. The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in traditional medicine.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.
