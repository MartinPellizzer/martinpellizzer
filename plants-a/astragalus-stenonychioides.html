Astragalus stenonychioides

Astragalus stenonychioides, commonly known as the narrow-nail milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus stenonychioides is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland communities.
