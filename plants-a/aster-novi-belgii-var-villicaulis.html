Aster novi-belgii var. villicaulis

Aster novi-belgii var. villicaulis, commonly known as the Purple Coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in fine, hairy hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with purple petals and a prominent, cone-shaped center. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture, contributing to its overall rugged appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
