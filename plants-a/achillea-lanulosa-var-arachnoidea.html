Achillea lanulosa var. arachnoidea

Achillea lanulosa var. arachnoidea, commonly known as the woolly yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, fluffy appearance.

It is often found in open meadows and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
