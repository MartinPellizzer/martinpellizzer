Alstonia macrophylla var. acuminata

Alstonia macrophylla var. acuminata, commonly known as the Acuminata Alstonia, is a variant of the Alstonia macrophylla, native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or cream in color, which bloom during the rainy season. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive, flaky texture.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable tropical or subtropical climates.
