Agalmyla paucipilosa

Agalmyla paucipilosa, commonly known as the spiny agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to drought conditions. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk with small, greenish-white flowers, which appear after several years of growth. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appearance.

Agalmyla paucipilosa is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
