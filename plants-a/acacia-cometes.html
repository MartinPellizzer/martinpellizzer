Acacia cometes

Acacia cometes, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Acacia cometes is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and habitat for native wildlife.
