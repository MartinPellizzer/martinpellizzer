Astragalus calycosus var. monophyllidius

Astragalus calycosus var. monophyllidius, commonly known as the single-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. The plant features a taproot system and produces small, yellow pea-like flowers in clusters. Its leaves are usually reduced to a single, broad, leafy structure at the base of the plant.

This variety is characterized by its unique morphology and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
