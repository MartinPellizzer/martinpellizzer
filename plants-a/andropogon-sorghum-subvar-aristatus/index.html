Andropogon Sorghum Subvar. Aristatus

Sorghum grass, scientifically known as Andropogon sorghum subvar. aristatus, is a hardy perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is widely cultivated for its high biomass yield and adaptability to diverse soil conditions. This plant is valued for its use in livestock feed, erosion control, and biofuel production. Its tall, sturdy stalks and dense foliage make it a resilient component of many ecosystems. With its ability to thrive in challenging environments, Sorghum grass plays a significant role in sustainable agriculture and land management.
