Astragalus tribuloides var. thapsacenus

Astragalus tribuloides var. thapsacenus, commonly known as the thapsacenus milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance lies in its ability to fix nitrogen, enhancing soil fertility in its native habitat.
