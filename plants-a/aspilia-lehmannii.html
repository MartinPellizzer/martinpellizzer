Aspilia lehmannii

Aspilia lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's flame flower, is a perennial shrub native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

Aspilia lehmannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
