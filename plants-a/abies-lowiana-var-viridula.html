Abies lowiana var. viridula

Abies lowiana var. viridula, commonly known as the Colorado blue spruce, is a variant of the white spruce native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark blue foliage that gives it a striking appearance in both natural and landscaped settings. The plant has needle-like leaves that are short and stout, with a distinctive blue-green hue due to a waxy coating. It typically grows in coniferous forests and is adapted to cold, mountainous climates.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
