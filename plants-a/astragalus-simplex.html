Astragalus simplex

Astragalus simplex, commonly known as the simple milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in prairies and steppe environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus simplex is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, drought-prone conditions.
