Anagallis ovalis

Anagallis ovalis, commonly known as the oval moss gentian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are oval-shaped and often covered in a fine, moss-like coating.

Anagallis ovalis is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
