Aleuritia secundiflora

Aleuritia secundiflora, commonly known as the red-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, fragrant, red flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Aleuritia secundiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
