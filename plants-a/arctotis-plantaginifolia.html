Arctotis plantaginifolia

Arctotis plantaginifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that resemble mist. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Arctotis plantaginifolia is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
