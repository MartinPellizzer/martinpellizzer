Anthemis triumfetti f. vitellina

Anthemis triumfetti f. vitellina, commonly known as the yellow chamomile, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which resemble those of true chamomile, and its finely divided, silvery-green leaves. The plant is native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and pleasant fragrance.

The flower heads contain small, composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
