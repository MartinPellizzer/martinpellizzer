Aremonia agrimonoides subsp. agrimonoides

Aremonia agrimonoides subsp.

agrimonoides, commonly known as the agrimonium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland edges.

The plant features erect, branching stems that are covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, supporting a variety of insect life and contributing to biodiversity in its native habitats.
