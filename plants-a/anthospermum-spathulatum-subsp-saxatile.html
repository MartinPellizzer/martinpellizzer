Anthospermum spathulatum subsp. saxatile

Anthospermum spathulatum subsp.

saxatile, commonly known as the rock anthospermum, is a succulent plant native to arid and rocky habitats in southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry conditions.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to anchor it in loose, rocky soils.

This subspecies is valued for its resilience and ornamental appeal in xerophytic gardens.
