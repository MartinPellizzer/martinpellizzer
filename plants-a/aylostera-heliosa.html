Aylostera heliosa

Aylostera heliosa, commonly known as the sunflower, is a tall, herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flower head surrounded by narrow, green leaves. The plant has a robust stem that grows upright, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are composite, consisting of numerous small florets that mature into seeds.

Aylostera heliosa is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of sunflower oil.
