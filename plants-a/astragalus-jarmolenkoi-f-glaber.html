Astragalus jarmolenkoi f. glaber

Astragalus jarmolenkoi f. glaber, commonly known as the glabrous milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with small, rounded leaflets, and it produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.

This subspecies is distinguished by its smooth, hairless surface, which gives it the specific epithet "glaber.".
