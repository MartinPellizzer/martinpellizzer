Arundinella setosa var. culionensis

Arundinella setosa var. culionensis, commonly known as the Culion bamboo, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the Philippines, specifically found on the island of Palawan. This variety is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves, which are often used in traditional crafts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in local communities.
