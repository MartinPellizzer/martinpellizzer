Astragalus reboudii

Astragalus reboudii, commonly known as the Rebound milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological significance is notable in its native habitats.
