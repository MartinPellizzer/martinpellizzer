Acropera loddigesii

Acropera loddigesii, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along riverbanks. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, while the flowers are often pale yellow or greenish-white with intricate patterns.

Acropera loddigesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
