Arnica oligolepis

Arnica oligolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, basal leaves and yellow-orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing numerous disc florets surrounded by ray florets.

Arnica oligolepis is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
