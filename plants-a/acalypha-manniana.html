Acalypha manniana

Acalypha manniana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-rooted false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range from green to reddish in color. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Acalypha manniana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or disturbed areas.
