Aylostera fusca

Aylostera fusca, commonly known as the red-barked tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark, which peels off in thin, papery layers. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, waxy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, spherical fruits.

Aylostera fusca is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
