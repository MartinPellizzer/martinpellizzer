Asparagus albanensis

Asparagus albanensis, commonly known as the Albanian asparagus, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and feathery, fern-like foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that are typically clustered in loose panicles. Its berries are bright red and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

Asparagus albanensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
