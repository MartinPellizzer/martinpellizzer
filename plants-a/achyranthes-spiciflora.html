Achyranthes spiciflora

Achyranthes spiciflora, commonly known as the spicate achyranthes, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or reddish in color, arranged in spikelike inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
