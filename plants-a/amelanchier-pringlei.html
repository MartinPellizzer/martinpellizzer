Amelanchier pringlei

Amelanchier pringlei, commonly known as Pringle serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in early summer, which are appreciated by both humans and wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval with a serrated edge, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Amelanchier pringlei is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
