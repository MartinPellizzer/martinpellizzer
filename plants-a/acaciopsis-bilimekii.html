Acaciopsis bilimekii

Acaciopsis bilimekii, commonly known as the Socotra acacia, is a unique plant native to the island of Socotra in the Arabian Sea.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, thorn-covered stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to the harsh climate of its native habitat.

Due to its ecological significance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species.
