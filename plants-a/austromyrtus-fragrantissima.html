Austromyrtus fragrantissima

Austromyrtus fragrantissima, commonly known as the fragrant myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, aromatic leaves that emit a distinctive spicy scent when crushed. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sand dunes and rocky outcrops.

Austromyrtus fragrantissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in Indigenous Australian traditions.
