Arnica sulcata

Arnica sulcata, commonly known as the grooved arnica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Arnica sulcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
