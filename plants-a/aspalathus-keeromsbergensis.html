Aspalathus keeromsbergensis

Aspalathus keeromsbergensis, commonly known as the Keeromsberg bush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils, often found in mountainous regions. It is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
