Arum italicum subsp. italicum

Arum italicum subsp.

italicum, commonly known as the Italian arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features a stout, elephant-like underground stem and produces striking, arrow-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface.

The plant's most notable feature is its large, showy spathe, which is typically a deep purple or maroon color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
