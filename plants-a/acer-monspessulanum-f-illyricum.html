Acer monspessulanum f. illyricum

Acer monspessulanum f. illyricum, commonly known as the Illyrian maple, is a rare and ornamental maple species native to the Balkans.

It is a small tree or shrub with distinctive, palmate leaves that turn vibrant red in autumn. The plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils and prefers partial shade to full sun. Its flowers are inconspicuous, but the tree is valued for its ornamental foliage and unique form.

It is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ecological and aesthetic significance.
