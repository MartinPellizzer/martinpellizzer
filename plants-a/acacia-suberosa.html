Acacia suberosa

Acacia suberosa, commonly known as the rough wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its woody, twisted stem and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Acacia suberosa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-resistant.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various native animals.
