Aster heterophyllus

Aster heterophyllus, commonly known as the New Zealand aster, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, with some leaves being broad and others narrow, depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Aster heterophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
