Aniba mas

Aniba mas, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and showy white or yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
