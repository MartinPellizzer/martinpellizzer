Adenanthos procumbens

Adenanthos procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate adenanthos, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Australia.

It features small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in native gardens.
