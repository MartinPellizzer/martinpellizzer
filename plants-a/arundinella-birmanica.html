Arundinella birmanica

Arundinella birmanica, commonly known as the Burmese bamboo, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, grass-like texture.

Arundinella birmanica is often found in moist, tropical environments, particularly in Southeast Asia.
