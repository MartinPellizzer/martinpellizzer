Aglaia axillaris

Aglaia axillaris, commonly known as the white aglaia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other ornamental trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
