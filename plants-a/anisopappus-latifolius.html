Anisopappus latifolius

Anisopappus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaves. The plant features basal leaves that are broad and spoon-shaped, with a hairy surface. It produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Anisopappus latifolius is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
