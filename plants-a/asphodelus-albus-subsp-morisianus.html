Asphodelus albus subsp. morisianus

Asphodelus albus subsp.

morisianus, commonly known as the white asphodel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant produces tall, narrow leaves and spikes of white or pale pink flowers. It is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to tolerate drought conditions.

This subspecies is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
