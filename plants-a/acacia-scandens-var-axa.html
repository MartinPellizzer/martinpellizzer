Acacia scandens var. axa

Acacia scandens var. axa, commonly known as the climbing acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The wood of Acacia scandens var. axa is lightweight and has been traditionally used for fuel and construction in some regions.
