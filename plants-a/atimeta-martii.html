Atimeta martii

Atimeta martii, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has palmate leaves composed of five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
