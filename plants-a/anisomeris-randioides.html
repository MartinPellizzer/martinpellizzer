Anisomeris randioides

Anisomeris randioides, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in sandy or rocky soils, often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that disperse easily by wind. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Anisomeris randioides is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in Native American herbal remedies.
