Amelanchier canadensis var. oligocarpa

Amelanchier canadensis var. oligocarpa, commonly known as the Canadian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark purple berries in late summer. Its berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.

The species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
