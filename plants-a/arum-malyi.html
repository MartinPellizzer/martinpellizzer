Arum malyi

Arum malyi, commonly known as the Malayan arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces a starchy rhizome that is used as a food source in some regions. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, often with a glossy surface.

Arum malyi thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
