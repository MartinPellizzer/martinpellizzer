Aconogonon ocreatum

Aconogonon ocreatum, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Aconogonon ocreatum is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail, making it versatile for various landscaping applications.
