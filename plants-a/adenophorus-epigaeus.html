Adenophorus epigaeus

Adenophorus epigaeus, commonly known as the hairy mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing herb with small, green, hairy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in a raceme. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Adenophorus epigaeus is known for its ability to tolerate various environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
