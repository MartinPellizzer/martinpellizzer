Ampacus roxburghiana

Ampacus roxburghiana, commonly known as the Himalayan honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool climates. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

Ampacus roxburghiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
