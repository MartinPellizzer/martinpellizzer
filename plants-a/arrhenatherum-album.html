Arrhenatherum album

Arrhenatherum album, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-tomentose blades and tall, branching inflorescences that produce small, fluffy seeds. The plant thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, often forming dense stands. Its leaves are narrow and tapering, with a distinctive silvery-white coating that gives it its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
