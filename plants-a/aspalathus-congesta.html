Aspalathus congesta

Aspalathus congesta, commonly known as the small-leaf rooibos, is a species native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the region. The plant is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found in coastal fynbos vegetation.

Aspalathus congesta is closely related to other rooibos species and shares similar ecological and botanical traits.
