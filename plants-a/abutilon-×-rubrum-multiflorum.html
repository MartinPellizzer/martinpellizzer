Abutilon × rubrum-multiflorum

Abutilon × rubrum-multiflorum, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a hybrid species within the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in winter, and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by fluffy, cotton-like seed pods. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and vibrant foliage make it a striking addition to gardens and landscapes.
