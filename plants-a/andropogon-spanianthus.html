Andropogon spanianthus

Andropogon spanianthus, commonly known as Spanish grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Spanish grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
