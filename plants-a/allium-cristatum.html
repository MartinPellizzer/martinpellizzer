Allium cristatum

Allium cristatum, commonly known as the mountain garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures called corms, which are used for propagation and storage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Allium cristatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has mild culinary uses in some traditional cuisines.
