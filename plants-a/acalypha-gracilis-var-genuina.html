Acalypha gracilis var. genuina

Acalypha gracilis var. genuina, commonly known as the narrowleaf croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green with a slight sheen. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow and red, which are followed by distinctive, spiny capsules. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and colorful blooms.
