Astragalus bornmuelleri

Astragalus bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Bornmueller's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-desert and steppe environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
