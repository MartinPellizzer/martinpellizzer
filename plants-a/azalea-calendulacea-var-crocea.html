Azalea calendulacea var. crocea

Azalea calendulacea var. crocea, commonly known as the red azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The leaves are opposite, simple, and oval-shaped, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms.
