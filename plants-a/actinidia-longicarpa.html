Actinidia longicarpa

Actinidia longicarpa, commonly known as the long-fruited kiwifruit, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, hairy, greenish-yellow flowers that give way to fuzzy, brown fruits. The fruit is typically eaten raw or used in culinary applications, and it is rich in vitamin C and antioxidants.

Its cultivation has gained popularity due to its unique flavor and nutritional value.
