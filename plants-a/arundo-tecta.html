Arundo tecta

Arundo tecta, commonly known as the narrowleaf reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and it produces clusters of small, pale green to yellowish flowers. It is often used in landscaping for erosion control and as a decorative plant in water features.

Arundo tecta is also valued for its ability to filter water and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
