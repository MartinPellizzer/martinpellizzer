Aucuba japonica f. viridiflora

Aucuba japonica f. viridiflora, commonly known as the Variegated Japanese aucuba, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with striking white and yellow variegation, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that attract birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens or as a border plant.

Its hardy nature and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
