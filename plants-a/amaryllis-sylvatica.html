Amaryllis sylvatica

Amaryllis sylvatica, commonly known as the forest amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that are often broader than those of other amaryllis species. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Amaryllis sylvatica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown both as a houseplant and in outdoor gardens.
