Ampelopsis jiangxiensis

Ampelopsis jiangxiensis, commonly known as the Jiangxi Ampelopsis, is a climbing vine native to China.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, attracting various bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
