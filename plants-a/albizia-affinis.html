Albizia affinis

Albizia affinis, commonly known as the white siris, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are often light green and emit a mild fragrance. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in racemes, and it produces flat, fibrous pods that contain seeds.

Albizia affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
