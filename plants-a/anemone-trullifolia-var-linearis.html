Anemone trullifolia var. linearis

Anemone trullifolia var. linearis, commonly known as the slender anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are narrow and linear, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, white to pink flowers with a central cluster of stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and native plant collections.
