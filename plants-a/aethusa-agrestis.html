Aethusa agrestis

Aethusa agrestis, commonly known as the field hemlock, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant features slender stems with finely divided, feather-like leaves that resemble those of other members of its family. Its small, white or greenish flowers are arranged in umbels, a characteristic feature of the Apiaceae family.

Aethusa agrestis is known for its toxic properties, containing compounds such as cicutoxin, which can be harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
