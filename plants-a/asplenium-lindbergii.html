Asplenium lindbergii

Asplenium lindbergii, commonly known as the Lindberg's spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on rocks or trees in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a rosette-shaped structure with closely packed, lanceolate leaves that are usually dark green and have a leathery texture. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Asplenium lindbergii is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
