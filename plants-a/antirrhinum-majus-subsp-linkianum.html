Antirrhinum majus subsp. linkianum

Antirrhinum majus subsp.

linkianum, commonly known as the Link's snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and orange.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its widespread cultivation in gardens.
