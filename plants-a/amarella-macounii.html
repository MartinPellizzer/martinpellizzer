Amarella macounii

Amarella macounii, commonly known as Macoun's cherry, is a species of wild cherry native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, dark red to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn vibrant yellow in the fall.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is valued for its tart flavor.
