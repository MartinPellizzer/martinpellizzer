Aloysia foncki

Aloysia foncki, commonly known as the Brazilian verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are borne in terminal clusters.

Aloysia foncki is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
