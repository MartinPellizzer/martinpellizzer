Actinea formosa

Actinea formosa, commonly known as the golden starwort, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers are usually yellow or orange in color.

Actinea formosa is often found in clusters and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
