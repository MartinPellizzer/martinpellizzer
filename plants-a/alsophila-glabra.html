Alsophila glabra

Alsophila glabra, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically dark green and can grow quite long, contributing to its ornamental value.

Alsophila glabra plays an important role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and shelter for various organisms.
