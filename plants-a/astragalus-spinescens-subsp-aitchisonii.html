Astragalus spinescens subsp. aitchisonii

Astragalus spinescens subsp.

aitchisonii, commonly known as the Aitchison's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its spiny, shrub-like growth habit and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It is adapted to dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This subspecies is of interest in ecological and botanical studies due to its resilience and potential medicinal properties.
