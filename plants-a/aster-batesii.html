Aster batesii

Aster batesii, commonly known as the Great Basin aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as sagebrush steppe and rocky slopes. The plant produces showy, violet-blue to purple flowers in the fall, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Aster batesii plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
