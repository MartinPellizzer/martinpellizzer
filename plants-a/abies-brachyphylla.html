Abies brachyphylla

Abies brachyphylla, commonly known as the dwarf white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its short, dense foliage and conical shape, with needle-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath. The tree produces cones that are typically 4 to 6 inches long, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in subalpine and alpine zones.

The dwarf white fir plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
