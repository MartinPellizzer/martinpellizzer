Alchemilla minusculiflora

Alchemilla minusculiflora, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Alchemilla minusculiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
