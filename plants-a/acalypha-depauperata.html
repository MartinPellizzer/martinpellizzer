Acalypha depauperata

Acalypha depauperata, commonly known as the poor man's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Acalypha depauperata is valued for its ornamental leaves and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
