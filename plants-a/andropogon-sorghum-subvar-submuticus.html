Andropogon sorghum subvar. submuticus

Andropogon sorghum subvar. submuticus, commonly known as eastern gamagrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered with a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

This species plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
