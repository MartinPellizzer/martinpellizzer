Alstroemeria neilliana

Alstroemeria neilliana, commonly known as the Neill's lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has strap-like leaves that are often variegated.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
