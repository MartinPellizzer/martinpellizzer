Abies × insignis var. speciosa

Abies × insignis var. speciosa, commonly known as the silver fir, is a hybrid conifer that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Abies balsamea and Abies fraseri.

It is native to the Appalachian Mountains in the eastern United States, where it thrives in cool, moist, well-drained soils. The tree has a conical shape with long, slender, bluish-green needles that are arranged in a flat, spreading pattern. Its cones are typically 4–6 inches long and mature to a reddish-brown color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to adapt to a range of environmental conditions.
