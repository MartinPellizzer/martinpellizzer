Ampelocera pubescens

Ampelocera pubescens, commonly known as the hairy leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often appearing in late summer or early autumn. Ampelocera pubescens thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant among horticulturists and botanists.
