Allium byzantinum

Allium byzantinum, commonly known as the Byzantine onion, is a bulbous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It produces small, round bulbs that are typically white or pale yellow in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and blooms with clusters of purple or pink flowers in the summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, sweet flavor when harvested young.

Allium byzantinum is relatively easy to grow and thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
