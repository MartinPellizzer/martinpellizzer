Aquilegia vicaria subsp. tianschanica

Aquilegia vicaria subsp.

tianschanica, commonly known as the Tianshan columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in a raceme.

The plant has palmately divided leaves with a serrated margin, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun, commonly found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
