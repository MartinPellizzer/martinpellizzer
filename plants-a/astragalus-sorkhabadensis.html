Astragalus sorkhabadensis

Astragalus sorkhabadensis, commonly known as the Sorkhabad milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
