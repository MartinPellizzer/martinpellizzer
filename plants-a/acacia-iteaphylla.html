Acacia iteaphylla

Acacia iteaphylla, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a small shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Its leaves are covered in fine, silvery hairs, contributing to its common name.

Acacia iteaphylla is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.
