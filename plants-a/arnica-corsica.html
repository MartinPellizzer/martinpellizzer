Arnica corsica

Arnica corsica, commonly known as Corsican arnica, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and produces yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Arnica corsica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
