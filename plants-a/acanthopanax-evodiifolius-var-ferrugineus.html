Acanthopanax evodiifolius var. ferrugineus

Acanthopanax evodiifolius var. ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty-leaf Chinese angel's trumpet, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and a reddish-brown coloration on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil.
