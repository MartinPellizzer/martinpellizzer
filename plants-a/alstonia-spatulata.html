Alstonia spatulata

Alstonia spatulata, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and striking, clustered orange-red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Alstonia spatulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various soil types.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
