Ajania variifolia

Ajania variifolia, commonly known as the variable ajania, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in dense, grayish-white hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped, often with varying degrees of toothed margins, contributing to its common name.

Ajania variifolia produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the daisy family.
