Ardisia rusbyana

Ardisia rusbyana, commonly known as the Rusby's evergreen shrub, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are ornamental and attract various wildlife.

Ardisia rusbyana is valued in horticulture for its attractive foliage and seasonal floral display.
