Acacia nelsonii

Acacia nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its yellow flowers, which appear in clusters and bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into smaller leaflets, and it has a distinctive flaky bark.

Acacia nelsonii is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
