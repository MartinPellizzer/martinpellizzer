Aira cespitosa var. hoppeana

Aira cespitosa var. hoppeana, commonly known as Hoppe's tufted grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically pale green to brown, with the flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
