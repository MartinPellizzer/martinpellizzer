Anathallis endresii

Anathallis endresii, commonly known as the Endres's spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with elongated, thin sepals and petals. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with a distinct, intricate structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Anathallis endresii is a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native habitat.
