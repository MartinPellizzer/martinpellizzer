Anisopappus davyi var. pumilus

Anisopappus davyi var. pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf daisy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant features wiry stems and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular plant for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
