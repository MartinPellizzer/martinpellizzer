Amorphophallus rex

Amorphophallus rex, commonly known as the giant elephant foot yam, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy underground tuber, which can weigh up to 100 pounds and has a distinctive, fibrous texture. The plant produces a tall, spiky inflorescence with a strong, unpleasant odor, often resembling that of decaying flesh. Its growth cycle involves a period of dormancy, during which the tuber remains underground for several months.

The botanical features of Amorphophallus rex make it both a fascinating and challenging plant to cultivate.
