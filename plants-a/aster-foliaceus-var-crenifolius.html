Aster foliaceus var. crenifolius

Aster foliaceus var. crenifolius, commonly known as the curlyleaf aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, curly, and often gray-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
