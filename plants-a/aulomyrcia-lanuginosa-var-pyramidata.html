Aulomyrcia lanuginosa var. pyramidata

Aulomyrcia lanuginosa var. pyramidata, commonly known as the woolly myrcia pyramidal, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are elliptical and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
