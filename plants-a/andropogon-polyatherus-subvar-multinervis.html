Andropogon polyatherus subvar. multinervis

Andropogon polyatherus subvar. multinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with distinct parallel veins, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
