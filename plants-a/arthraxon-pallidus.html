Arthraxon pallidus

Arthraxon pallidus, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to various regions of Australia.

It typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, often found in freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and river margins. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish or brownish flowers arranged in spikes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Arthraxon pallidus is an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and ecological balance.
