Alsine serrulata

Alsine serrulata, commonly known as the serrated alsike, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and typically grows in moist, disturbed soils. The plant has alternate, serrated leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
