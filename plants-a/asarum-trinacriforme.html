Asarum trinacriforme

Asarum trinacriforme, commonly known as the three-lobed wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, often three-lobed leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Asarum trinacriforme is valued for its ornamental leaves and historical use in traditional medicine.
