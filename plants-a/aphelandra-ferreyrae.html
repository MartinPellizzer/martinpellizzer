Aphelandra ferreyrae

Aphelandra ferreyrae, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Aroid family (Araceae) and is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, often in shades of yellow or orange. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Aphelandra ferreyrae is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and unique floral structure.
