Anchusa gmelinii

Anchusa gmelinii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bugloss, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, often found in Mediterranean climates. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and its stems are usually upright and branched.

Anchusa gmelinii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
