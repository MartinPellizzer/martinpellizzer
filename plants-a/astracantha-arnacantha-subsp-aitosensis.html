Astracantha arnacantha subsp. aitosensis

Astracantha arnacantha subsp.

aitosensis, commonly known as the Aitos mountain thistle, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of the Mediterranean. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, dry climates. Its leaves are often spiny and covered in fine hairs, providing protection against herbivores.

The flowers, which bloom in the summer, are yellow and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
