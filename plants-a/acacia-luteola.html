Acacia luteola

Acacia luteola, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in dense, globular clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves, reducing water loss in arid environments. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Acacia luteola is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
