Asparagus petersianus

Asparagus petersianus, commonly known as the white asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features slender, branching stems with small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a fine white or grayish pubescence. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
