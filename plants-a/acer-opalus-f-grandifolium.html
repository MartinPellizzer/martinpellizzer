Acer opalus f. grandifolium

Acer opalus f. grandifolium, commonly known as the giant maple, is a variant of the opalus maple characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves.

It is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas, where it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a broad, rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. Its leaves turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, making it a striking ornamental plant.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks for its seasonal display.
