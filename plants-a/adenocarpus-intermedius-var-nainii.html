Adenocarpus intermedius var. nainii

Adenocarpus intermedius var. nainii, commonly known as the Naini grass, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by small, pod-like fruits. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variant is notable for its ecological significance in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
