Achimenes picta

Achimenes picta, commonly known as the painted bellflower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Achimenes picta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its showy blooms and compact growth habit.
