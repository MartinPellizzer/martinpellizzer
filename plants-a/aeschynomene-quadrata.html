Aeschynomene quadrata

Aeschynomene quadrata, commonly known as partridge pea, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its seeds are edible and have been used by indigenous peoples for food and traditional medicine.
