Alpinia media

Alpinia media, commonly known as the lesser galangal, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Alpinia media thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.
