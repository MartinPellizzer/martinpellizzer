Aster steeleorum

Aster steeleorum, commonly known as the Steele's aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Aster steeleorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
