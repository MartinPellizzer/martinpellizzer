Astragalus schugnanicus

Astragalus schugnanicus, commonly known as the Schugnan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus schugnanicus is often found in mountainous regions and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
