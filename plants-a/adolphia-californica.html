Adolphia californica

Adolphia californica, commonly known as the California dodder, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for nutrients. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a fibrous root system that attaches to host plants. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain areas.

Its botanical features make it unique among the Adolphia genus.
