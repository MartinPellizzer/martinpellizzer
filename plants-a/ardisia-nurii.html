Ardisia nurii

Ardisia nurii, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and emit a mild fragrance when crushed. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are ornamental and attract birds. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem and an upright growth habit.

Ardisia nurii is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
