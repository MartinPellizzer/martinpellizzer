Achyranthes rotundifolia

Achyranthes rotundifolia, commonly known as round-leaved spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, rounded leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural and urban environments.
