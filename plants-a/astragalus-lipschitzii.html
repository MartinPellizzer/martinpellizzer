Astragalus lipschitzii

Astragalus lipschitzii, commonly known as the Lipschitz's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is relatively rare and of conservation interest in some regions.
