Astragalus rascheyaensis

Astragalus rascheyaensis, commonly known as the Raschey's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
