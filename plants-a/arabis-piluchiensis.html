Arabis piluchiensis

Arabis piluchiensis, commonly known as the Piluchian rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

Arabis piluchiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
