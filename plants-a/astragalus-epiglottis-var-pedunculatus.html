Astragalus epiglottis var. pedunculatus

Astragalus epiglottis var. pedunculatus, commonly known as the pedunculate epiglottis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, thriving in well-drained soils. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.

The varietal name "pedunculatus" refers to the elongated peduncles that bear its clusters of flowers.
