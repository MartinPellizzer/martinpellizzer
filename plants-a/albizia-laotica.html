Albizia laotica

Albizia laotica, commonly known as the Laos Albizia, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 10-20 meters and is known for its fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Albizia laotica is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important species in agroforestry systems.
