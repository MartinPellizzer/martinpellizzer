Aster seliger

Aster seliger, commonly known as the New England aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring vibrant purple or pink flower heads that bloom in the late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy and branching.

Aster seliger is often found in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
