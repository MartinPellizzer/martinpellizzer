Actaea cordifolia

Actaea cordifolia, commonly known as black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces dark red berries that contain seeds, and it thrives in woodland habitats. Its rhizomes and roots are often used in traditional medicine.

The fruit is a red, berry-like structure that contains numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by birds and through the excretion of animals.

The roots are tuberous and rhizomatous, allowing the plant to spread vegetatively and survive in shaded woodland environments.
