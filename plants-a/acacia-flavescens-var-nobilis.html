Acacia flavescens var. nobilis

Acacia flavescens var. nobilis, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in drought conditions. Its flowers are not only ornamental but also attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and birds.

The species is widely cultivated for its aesthetic value and is a symbol of Australia's natural heritage.
