Amomum triorgyale

Amomum triorgyale, commonly known as the three-lobed cardamom, is a tropical evergreen perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and spikes of small, white to pale green flowers. The plant produces dark brown, three-lobed berries that are highly valued for their aromatic properties. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine and as flavoring agents.
