Allium perdulce var. perdulce

Allium perdulce var. perdulce, commonly known as the sweet leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and broad, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, making it a useful crop in certain cuisines.

The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
