Aspazoma

Aspazoma, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers that resemble cups, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive, slightly hairy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Aspazoma is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
