Anthriscus vulgaris var. hispanica

Anthriscus vulgaris var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish cow parsley, is a biennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, umbrella-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
