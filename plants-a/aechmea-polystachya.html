Aechmea polystachya

Aechmea polystachya, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-like inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, often followed by colorful bracts. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil and high humidity.

Aechmea polystachya is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea polystachya has leaves that are long, narrow, and sword-shaped, arranged in a rosette formation, with a waxy, blue-green color that helps reflect sunlight.

The plant has a short, stout stem with a thick, fibrous bark that is typically greenish-brown in color. Its inflorescence consists of a tall, branched spike of colorful flowers, usually in shades of red, orange, and yellow, blooming during the summer months and pollinated by birds and insects. The fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

The roots are fibrous and form a dense mat around the base of the plant, helping to anchor it and absorb moisture from the soil.
