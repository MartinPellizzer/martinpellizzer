Ammannia sagittifolia var. ericiformis

Ammannia sagittifolia var. ericiformis, commonly known as the redroot sandbarren, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered pink to red flowers during the summer months. Its root system is distinctive, characterized by its bright red coloration, which is a key identifying feature.

The species is often found in disturbed or wetland environments and is adapted to periodic flooding.
