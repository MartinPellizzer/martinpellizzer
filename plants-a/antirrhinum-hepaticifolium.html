Antirrhinum hepaticifolium

Antirrhinum hepaticifolium, commonly known as the liverleaf snapdragon, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed, resembling the shape of a liver, hence its common name. The plant produces tall spikes of bright red or orange flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
