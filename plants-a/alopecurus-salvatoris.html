Alopecurus salvatoris

Alopecurus salvatoris, commonly known as the meadow foxtail, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. Alopecurus salvatoris typically grows in moist meadows, along stream banks, and in other wetland habitats.

It is often found in association with other grasses and is an important component of diverse grassland ecosystems.
