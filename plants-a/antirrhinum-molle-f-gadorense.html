Antirrhinum molle f. gadorense

Antirrhinum molle f. gadorense, commonly known as the Gadorensis Snapdragon, is a botanical variant of the common snapdragon.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and vibrant, tubular flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. This plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in Mediterranean climates. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and prolonged blooming period.
