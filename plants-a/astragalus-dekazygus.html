Astragalus dekazygus

Astragalus dekazygus, commonly known as the Dekazygus milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers in late spring. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several elliptical seeds.

This species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
