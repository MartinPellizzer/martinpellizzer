Actinea linearis

Actinea linearis, commonly known as the linear sea anemone, is a marine invertebrate belonging to the order Actinioidea.

It is characterized by its long, slender polyp body with a central oral disc surrounded by tentacles. The plant (or more accurately, the organism) thrives in shallow coastal waters, often attached to rocks or other substrates. Its coloration can vary, typically ranging from pale yellow to reddish-brown, depending on environmental conditions.

Actinea linearis plays a role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various small aquatic organisms.
