Alyssum macrostylum

Alyssum macrostylum, commonly known as the large-flowered alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Alyssum macrostylum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as bees and butterflies.
