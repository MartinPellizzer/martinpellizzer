Anil pechuelii

Anil pechuelii, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the genus Anil within the family Acanthaceae, characterized by its showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, with a distinctive red calyx that resembles a firecracker, giving it its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
