Arundinaria yamakitensis

Arundinaria yamakitensis, commonly known as the Yamakita bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters. The plant typically reaches a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikelets that produce both male and female flowers.
