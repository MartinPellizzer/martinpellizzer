Asplenium atroviride

Asplenium atroviride, commonly known as the black-veined spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves with prominent, blackish veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rock crevices or on tree trunks. Its fronds are usually compact and have a rhombic or ovate shape.

Asplenium atroviride is a cryptogamic plant that reproduces via spores rather than seeds.
