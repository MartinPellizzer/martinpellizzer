Aloe leptosiphon

Aloe leptosiphon, commonly known as the narrow-siphon aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color and covered with a whitish powdery coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Aloe leptosiphon is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and ability to store water in its leaves make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
