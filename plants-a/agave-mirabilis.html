Agave mirabilis

Agave mirabilis, commonly known as the sacred blue agave, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant produces a tall flowering stem that bears small, blue-green flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sunlight.

Its leaves are used in traditional practices and have economic significance in the production of agave-based products.
