Anthericum canaliculatum

Anthericum canaliculatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arum, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a distinctive, often reddish-purple spathe that encloses a spadix, a common characteristic of the Araceae family. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers within the spadix, which are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are elongated and have a waxy surface, often with a slightly notched or lobed appearance.

Anthericum canaliculatum is typically found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
