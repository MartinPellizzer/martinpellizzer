Alchemilla argyrophylla subsp. keniensis

Alchemilla argyrophylla subsp.

keniensis, commonly known as the Kenyan lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to East Africa. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
