Acacia fallax

Acacia fallax, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, needle-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia fallax is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
