Asplenium moupinense var. daraeiforme

Asplenium moupinense var. daraeiforme, commonly known as the Daraeiforme maidenhair fern, is a rare and striking variant of the asplenium genus.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble the feathery structure of traditional maidenhair ferns. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of southern China, where it thrives in cool, shaded environments. Its unique leaf morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.

The varietal form is distinguished by subtle differences in leaf shape and venation patterns compared to the typical A. moupinense.
