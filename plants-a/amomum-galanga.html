Amomum galanga

Amomum galanga, commonly known as greater galangal, is a rhizomatous herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, knotted rhizomes. The plant produces narrow, sword-shaped leaves and small, pale green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its rhizomes are widely used in traditional medicine and as a spice in various cuisines.

The essential oils found in its rhizomes contribute to its distinctive spicy and citrusy aroma.
