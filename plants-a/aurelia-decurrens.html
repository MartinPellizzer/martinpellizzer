Aurelia decurrens

Aurelia decurrens, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a low-growing habit with spreading stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. Its flowers are bell-shaped, usually blue or purple, and bloom in late summer.

The leaves are lance-shaped, slightly hairy, and arranged alternately along the stem.
