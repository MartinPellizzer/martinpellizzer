Arundinaria kitanoensis

Arundinaria kitanoensis, commonly known as Kitano bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

Arundinaria kitanoensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
