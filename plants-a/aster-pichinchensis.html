Aster pichinchensis

Aster pichinchensis, commonly known as the Pichincha aster, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers bloom in shades of purple and white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
