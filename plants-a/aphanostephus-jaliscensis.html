Aphanostephus jaliscensis

Aphanostephus jaliscensis, commonly known as the Mexican grass or Jalisco grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico, particularly in the state of Jalisco. This perennial herb typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

The plant is notable for its ecological role in supporting local insect and bird populations.
