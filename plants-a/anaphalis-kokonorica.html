Anaphalis kokonorica

Anaphalis kokonorica, commonly known as the Tibetan goatweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to extreme climatic conditions. Its silver-gray foliage and fluffy seed heads are distinctive features, often mistaken for clouds or wool.

Anaphalis kokonorica plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
