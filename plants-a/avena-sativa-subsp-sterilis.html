Avena sativa subsp. sterilis

Avena sativa subsp.

sterilis, commonly known as wild oat, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Wild oat is often considered a weed in agricultural fields due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and weed management research.
