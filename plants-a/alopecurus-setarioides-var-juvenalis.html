Alopecurus setarioides var. juvenalis

Alopecurus setarioides var. juvenalis, commonly known as the dwarf mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors or stream banks.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported diuretic and soothing properties.
