Abies ledebourii

Abies ledebourii, commonly known as the Ledebour's fir, is a species of conifer native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along its branches. The tree produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing winged seeds. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions and well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant in its native range.
