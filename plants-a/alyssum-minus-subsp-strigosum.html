Alyssum minus subsp. strigosum

Alyssum minus subsp.

strigosum, commonly known as sticky alyssum, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring.

The plant has hairy, strigose leaves that give it a distinctive textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
