Arrozia

Arrozia, commonly known as the "rice flower," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that resemble grains of rice, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Arrozia species are often found in meadows and along the edges of woodlands.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
