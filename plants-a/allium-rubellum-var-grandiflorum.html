Allium rubellum var. grandiflorum

Allium rubellum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant red onion, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces bulbous roots and has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often used in culinary applications. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

This variety is prized for both its ornamental value and its mild, sweet onion flavor.
