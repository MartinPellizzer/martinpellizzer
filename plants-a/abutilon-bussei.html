Abutilon bussei

Abutilon bussei, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in the summer months. Its fibrous roots and hairy stems make it adaptable to various soil types.

Abutilon bussei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
