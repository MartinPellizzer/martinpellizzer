Aphelandra schiedeana

Aphelandra schiedeana, commonly known as the flame tree or sacred flame plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically orange or red, resembling flames. These flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.

Aphelandra schiedeana thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
