Allium acuminatum var. cuspidatum

Allium acuminatum var. cuspidatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features grass-like leaves that are slender and pointed, with a mild garlic aroma. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its edible leaves, which are used in culinary applications.
