Achillea ledebourii

Achillea ledebourii, commonly known as the Desert Chamomile, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with silver-gray foliage and produces small, yellowish composite flowers. The plant is adapted to hot, dry climates and has a deep root system for water storage. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
