Allium globosum f. dilute-roseum

Allium globosum f. dilute-roseum, commonly known as the pink globe onion, is a striking variant of the globe onion species.

It is characterized by its spherical, bulbous structure covered in a dense layer of white to pale pink skin. The plant produces clusters of small, rose-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and mild, sweet flavor, making it both decorative and edible.
