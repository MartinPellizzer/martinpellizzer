Acanthophyllum squarrosum

Acanthophyllum squarrosum, commonly known as the silky acanthophyllum, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle floral display. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Acanthophyllum squarrosum plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local wildlife.
