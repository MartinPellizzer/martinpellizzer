Anathallis holstii

Anathallis holstii, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and delicate epiphytic orchid native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, pale green stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that often resemble the shape of a ghostly figure. The plant lacks true leaves and instead absorbs moisture and nutrients through its roots, which are adapted to cling to tree trunks. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is highly sensitive to changes in its ecosystem.

Due to its elusive nature and habitat requirements, it is a challenging plant to cultivate and conserve.
