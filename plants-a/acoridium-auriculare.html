Acoridium auriculare

Acoridium auriculare, commonly known as the golden ear orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that resemble ears, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or swampy areas. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often with a waxy texture, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Acoridium auriculare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
