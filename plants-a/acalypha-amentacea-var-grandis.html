Acalypha amentacea var. grandis

Acalypha amentacea var. grandis, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking unique foliage.
