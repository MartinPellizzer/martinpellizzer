Aristida leucophaea

Aristida leucophaea, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and slender, tufted inflorescences that resemble a plume. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its small, spike-like flowers are typically pale green to brown, and it produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Aristida leucophaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in xeriscaped gardens.
