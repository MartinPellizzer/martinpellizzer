Astragalus parryi

Astragalus parryi, commonly known as Parry's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow to cream-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets, and it produces pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Astragalus parryi plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
