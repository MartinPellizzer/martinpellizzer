Artemisia tripartita var. hawkinsii

Artemisia tripartita var. hawkinsii, commonly known as Hawkin's wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage composed of finely divided, thread-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical shapes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its essential oils contribute to its strong, medicinal and aromatic properties.
