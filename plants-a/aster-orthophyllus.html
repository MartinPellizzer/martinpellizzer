Aster orthophyllus

Aster orthophyllus, commonly known as the white wood aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, white to pale purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodlands, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
