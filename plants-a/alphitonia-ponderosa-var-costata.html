Alphitonia ponderosa var. costata

Alphitonia ponderosa var. costata, commonly known as the serrated alphitonia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it its botanical name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, round fruits resembling small citrus oranges. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood in some regions.
