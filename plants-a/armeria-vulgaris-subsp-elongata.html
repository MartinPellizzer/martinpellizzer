Armeria vulgaris subsp. elongata

Armeria vulgaris subsp.

elongata, commonly known as sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and dense clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has fleshy, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand salty sea air and strong coastal winds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens and as a coastal landscape plant.
