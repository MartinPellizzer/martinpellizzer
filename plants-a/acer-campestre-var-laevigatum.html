Acer campestre var. laevigatum

Acer campestre var. laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved field maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green bark and palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces small, yellow-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as fruit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
