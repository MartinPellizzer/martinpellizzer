Asterochlaena platyloba var. grandiflora

Asterochlaena platyloba var. grandiflora, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, showy red flowers.

It is native to the Indo-Pacific region and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant features a tall, slender trunk with smooth, greyish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. Its flowers, which bloom in clusters, are often used in ornamental horticulture due to their vibrant color and long-lasting display.

The species is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its ecological benefits in coastal and urban environments.
