Acacia lebbeck

Acacia lebbeck, commonly known as the black acacia, is a deciduous tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. It is often found in dry, tropical, and semi-arid regions, and it plays a significant role in soil stabilization and traditional medicine.

Acacia lebbeck is also valued for its hard, durable wood and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in ecological restoration.
