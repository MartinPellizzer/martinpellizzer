Aster morrisonensis

Aster morrisonensis, commonly known as Morrison's aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Aster morrisonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
