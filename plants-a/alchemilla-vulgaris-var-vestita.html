Alchemilla vulgaris var. vestita

Alchemilla vulgaris var. vestita, commonly known as the velvet alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
