Arum pinnatifidum

Arum pinnatifidum, commonly known as the slit-leaf arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a striking reddish or purple underside. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers surrounded by a showy, spadix-like structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
