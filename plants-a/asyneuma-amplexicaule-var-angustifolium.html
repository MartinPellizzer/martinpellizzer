Asyneuma amplexicaule var. angustifolium

Asyneuma amplexicaule var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved ivyweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and has opposite, narrow, elliptic to linear leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on short pedicels. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland dune environments.

The species is notable for its ability to spread vegetatively through its rhizomatous root system.
