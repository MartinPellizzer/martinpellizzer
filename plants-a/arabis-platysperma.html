Arabis platysperma

Arabis platysperma, commonly known as broad-seeded rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is characterized by its low, spreading growth habit. Its flowers are small, white or pale purple, and arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Arabis platysperma is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
