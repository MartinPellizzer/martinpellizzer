Alsophila benculensis

Alsophila benculensis, commonly known as the Benculens hachioji, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to two meters, making it a striking feature in its natural habitat.

Alsophila benculensis is often found in primary forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
