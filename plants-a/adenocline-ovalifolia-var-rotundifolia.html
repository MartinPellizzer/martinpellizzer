Adenocline ovalifolia var. rotundifolia

Adenocline ovalifolia var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf adenocline, is a variant of the adenocline genus characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

This plant typically features ovate to round leaves that are often pubescent, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The flowers are small and clustered, often producing a delicate white or pale pink hue.

Due to its unique foliage and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
