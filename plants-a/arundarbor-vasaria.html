Arundarbor vasaria

Arundarbor vasaria, commonly known as the silverleaf ivy, is a climbing plant native to the temperate regions of the northeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its stems are flexible and can trail or climb, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trellises.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araliaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
