Allocarya pratensis

Allocarya pratensis, commonly known as the meadow thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant features a robust taproot and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single terminal head, blooming from June to August.

Allocarya pratensis is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential in herbal medicine.
