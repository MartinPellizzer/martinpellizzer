Aristida leptochaeta

Aristida leptochaeta, commonly known as slender threeawn, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, wiry stems and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

Aristida leptochaeta is an important species in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in stabilizing soil in native plant communities.
