Albizia niopoides

Albizia niopoides, commonly known as the African bristlepod, is a leguminous shrub native to Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating ailments related to the respiratory and digestive systems.

Albizia niopoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for soil stabilization in degraded landscapes.
