Aconitum × teppneri nothosubsp. goetzii

Aconitum × teppneri nothosubsp.

goetzii, commonly known as the Goetz's hybrid aconite, is a hybrid species within the genus Aconitum, characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers. It is a perennial herbaceous plant with a robust root system and lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed.

The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its showy blooms and hardy nature.

The plant contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring careful handling.
