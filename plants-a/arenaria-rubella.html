Arenaria rubella

Arenaria rubella, commonly known as the red sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are typically succulent and fleshy, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Arenaria rubella thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous environments.
