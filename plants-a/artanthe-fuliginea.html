Artanthe fuliginea

Artanthe fuliginea, commonly known as the smoky artanthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

Artanthe fuliginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
