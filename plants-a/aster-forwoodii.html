Aster forwoodii

Aster forwoodii, commonly known as the Forwood's aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open woodlands and prairies. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with ray florets that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Aster forwoodii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
