Amaranthus × ralletii

Amaranthus × ralletii, commonly known as the redroot amaranth, is a hybrid species native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its upright growth habit and branching structure. The plant produces vibrant red or purple bracts that surround its small, green flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Amaranthus × ralletii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some traditional culinary and medicinal practices.
