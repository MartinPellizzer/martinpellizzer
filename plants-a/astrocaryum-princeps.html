Astrocaryum princeps

Astrocaryum princeps, commonly known as the Brazilian buriti or golden palm, is a tall, fan-shaped palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, feather-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow-orange fruits that are edible. The plant has a robust trunk with a smooth, pale green bark and is well adapted to the humid, tropical climate. Its fruits are an important food source for various wildlife species.

The plant also has cultural and traditional significance among indigenous communities in Brazil.
