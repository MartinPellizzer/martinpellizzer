Acantholimon muradicum

Acantholimon muradicum, commonly known as the Muradik thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Acantholimon muradicum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native range.
