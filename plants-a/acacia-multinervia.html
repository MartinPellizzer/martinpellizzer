Acacia multinervia

Acacia multinervia, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods. Acacia multinervia is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
