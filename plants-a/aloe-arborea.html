Aloe arborea

Aloe arborea, commonly known as the tree aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stem that can grow up to 10 meters in height, often forming a tree-like structure. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers at the top of its stems, which bloom in the summer months. Its fleshy, spiny leaves are adapted to store water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Aloe arborea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.
