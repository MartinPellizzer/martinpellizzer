Astragalus gombo var. discedens

Astragalus gombo var. discedens, commonly known as the Tibetan milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale pink to white. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude regions.
