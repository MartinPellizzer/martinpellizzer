Astragalus hyalinus var. glabratus

Astragalus hyalinus var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth hyalinus milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to low moisture conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, rounded leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soils in harsh environments.
