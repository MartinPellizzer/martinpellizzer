Abrodictyum dregei

Abrodictyum dregei, commonly known as the Cape golden orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with distinctive red veins, which bloom during the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, and it is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable and sometimes endangered species in its native habitat.
