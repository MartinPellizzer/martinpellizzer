Artanthe gabrieliana

Artanthe gabrieliana, commonly known as Gabriel's artanthe, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, daisy-like blooms.

Artanthe gabrieliana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
