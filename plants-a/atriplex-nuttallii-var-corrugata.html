Atriplex nuttallii var. corrugata

Atriplex nuttallii var. corrugata, commonly known as Nuttall's saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, deltoid-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in compact clusters, typically in the spring. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.

Its adaptability to extreme conditions makes it a valuable plant for stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.
