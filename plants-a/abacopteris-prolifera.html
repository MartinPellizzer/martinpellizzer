Abacopteris prolifera

Abacopteris prolifera, commonly known as the giant fan palm, is a unique and rare plant species native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has a robust trunk that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a thick, fibrous outer layer. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces dark blue, fleshy fruits that are edible.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
