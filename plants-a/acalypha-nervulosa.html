Acalypha nervulosa

Acalypha nervulosa, commonly known as the silky mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, often pink or red bracts that surround its small flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually clustered in inflorescences, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Acalypha nervulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and vibrant bracts.
