Angraecum fastuosum

Angraecum fastuosum, commonly known as the Saintpaulia orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that are typically white or pink with a distinctive central spur. The plant produces large, showy blooms that can reach up to 15 centimeters in length, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Angraecum fastuosum is also notable for its ability to bloom during the winter months, adding a unique ornamental value to gardens and homes.
