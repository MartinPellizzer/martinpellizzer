Anthericum asperatum

Anthericum asperatum, commonly known as the rough leaved fern, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its dense, sword-shaped leaves that are rough to the touch. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by a spadix, typical of the arum family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Anthericum asperatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its lush foliage.
