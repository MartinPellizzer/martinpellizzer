Acrocephalus obovatifolius

Acrocephalus obovatifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaf reed, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, and its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
