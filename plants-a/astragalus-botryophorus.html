Astragalus botryophorus

Astragalus botryophorus, commonly known as the globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its unique, globular inflorescence composed of numerous small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that aids in moisture retention.

Astragalus botryophorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
