Acanthopanax divaricatus

Acanthopanax divaricatus, commonly known as the Chinese goat's beard, is a climbing shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its distinctive appearance and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical studies.
