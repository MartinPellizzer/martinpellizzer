Allium campanulatum

Allium campanulatum, commonly known as the bell-shaped garlic or clustered garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features a bulbous base with a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, lavender to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and showy flowers.
