Aegilops triaristata subsp. contorta

Aegilops triaristata subsp.

contorta, commonly known as the twisted spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its distinctive three-awned lemma, which gives it its scientific name.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is of interest in agricultural research due to its potential for improving wheat genetics.
