Albuca hyacinthoides

Albuca hyacinthoides, commonly known as the hyacinth orchid, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, resembling hyacinths. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Albuca hyacinthoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
