Anemonastrum crinitum

Anemonastrum crinitum, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, yellowish to white flowers. The plant has hairy, silver-green leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun.

Anemonastrum crinitum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
