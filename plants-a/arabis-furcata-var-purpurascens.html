Arabis furcata var. purpurascens

Arabis furcata var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-stemmed rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety is distinguished by its reddish-purple stems, which give it its common name.
