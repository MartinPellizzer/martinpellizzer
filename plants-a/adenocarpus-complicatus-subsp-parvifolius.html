Adenocarpus complicatus subsp. parvifolius

Adenocarpus complicatus subsp.

parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaf burr legume, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers.

The plant produces distinctive burr-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by animals. It typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a preference for well-drained conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
