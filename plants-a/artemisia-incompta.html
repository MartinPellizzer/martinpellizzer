Artemisia incompta

Artemisia incompta, commonly known as bluegreen wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant contains volatile oils and is often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
