Acacia stellaticeps

Acacia stellaticeps, commonly known as the star-headed acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped heads of yellow flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has small, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its woody stems are typically straight and can grow up to several meters in height.

Acacia stellaticeps is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
