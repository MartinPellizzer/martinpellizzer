Arachnothryx pauciflora

Arachnothryx pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered spider-thorn, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies due to its role in supporting local wildlife.

Arachnothryx pauciflora is also valued for its potential in traditional medicine and as a source of natural fibers.
