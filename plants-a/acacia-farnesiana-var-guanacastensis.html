Acacia farnesiana var. guanacastensis

Acacia farnesiana var. guanacastensis, commonly known as the Guanacastense Acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, flat, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and is often used in reforestation projects.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.
