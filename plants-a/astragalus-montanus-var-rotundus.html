Astragalus montanus var. rotundus

Astragalus montanus var. rotundus, commonly known as the roundleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. The plant features compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
