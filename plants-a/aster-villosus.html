Aster villosus

Aster villosus, commonly known as the hairy aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features hairy, gray-green leaves and produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster villosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
