Assonia mollis

Assonia mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its hairy stems and leaves are adapted to thrive in warm, humid climates.

Assonia mollis is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
