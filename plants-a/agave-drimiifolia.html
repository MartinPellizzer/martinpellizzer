Agave drimiifolia

Agave drimiifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf agave, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped arrangement of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves. The leaves are narrow and have a distinctive waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces tall flower stalks that bear clusters of small, white flowers, typically blooming once in its lifetime.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions.
