Aspalathus fusca

Aspalathus fusca, commonly known as the dark green bushveld tea, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by flat, bean-like pods containing seeds. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a slight pubescence.

Aspalathus fusca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
