Agasyllis caucasica

Agasyllis caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.
