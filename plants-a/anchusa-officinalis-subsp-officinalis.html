Anchusa officinalis subsp. officinalis

Anchusa officinalis subsp.

officinalis, commonly known as common bugloss, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and lance-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant typically grows in dry meadows, rocky soils, and wastelands, thriving in full sun. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
