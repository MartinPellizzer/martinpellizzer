Astrophytum myriostigma subsp. quadricostatum

Astrophytum myriostigma subsp.

quadricostatum, commonly known as the four-costate hedgehog cactus, is a compact, spiny cactus native to Mexico. It has a globular stem with prominent radial ribs that are often covered in white, woolly hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored plant among cactus enthusiasts.
