Anemone rupicola subsp. reniformis

Anemone rupicola subsp.

reniformis, commonly known as the rock anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant features basal leaves that are palmately divided and have a distinctive kidney-shaped form. Its showy flowers are usually pink or purple, with five to seven petal-like sepals.

It is adapted to withstand drought and full sun, making it a resilient species in harsh landscapes.
