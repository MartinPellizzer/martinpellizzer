Aspalathus acutiflora

Aspalathus acutiflora, commonly known as the sweet pea bush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, delicate leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Aspalathus acutiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
