Arisaema sachalinense

Arisaema sachalinense, commonly known as the Sakhalin arisaema, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and often have a distinctive veining pattern.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
