Aspelina nivea

Aspelina nivea, commonly known as the white aspelina, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Aspelina nivea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
