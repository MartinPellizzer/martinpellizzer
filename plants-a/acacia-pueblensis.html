Acacia pueblensis

Acacia pueblensis, commonly known as the Pueblo acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that are clustered in dense inflorescences, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and on slopes.

Acacia pueblensis is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
