Aralia elegantissima

Aralia elegantissima, commonly known as the American aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves that are palmately arranged, with each leaf consisting of 5 to 7 oval, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbrella-shaped clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
