Acanthopanax stenophyllus f. angustissimus

Acanthopanax stenophyllus f. angustissimus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed Chinese angel's trumpet, is a variant of the Acanthopanax stenophyllus species.

It is a climbing shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in forested areas. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It produces small, red berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife.

This fern-like plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
