Aster durbrowi

Aster durbrowi, commonly known as the Durbrow's aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Aster durbrowi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
