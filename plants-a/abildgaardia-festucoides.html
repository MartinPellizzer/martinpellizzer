Abildgaardia festucoides

Abildgaardia festucoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf false oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its resilience and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying plant adaptation to extreme conditions.
