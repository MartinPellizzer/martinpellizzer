Alyssum parviflorum var. edentatum

Alyssum parviflorum var. edentatum, commonly known as the toothless alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring and early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
