Anemone hortensis proles lepida

Anemone hortensis proles lepida, commonly known as the garden anemone, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It features showy, single or double flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in early spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are basal and often covered in a white or silver bloom. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable for pollinators.
