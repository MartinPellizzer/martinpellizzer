Aria calvescens

Aria calvescens, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Aria calvescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
