Agretta crispa

Agretta crispa, commonly known as the crinkled agretta, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant's compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
