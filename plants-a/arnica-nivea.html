Arnica nivea

Arnica nivea, commonly known as the white arnica, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous environments. Arnica nivea is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties.

However, it is important to note that all parts of the plant are toxic and should not be used without proper guidance.
