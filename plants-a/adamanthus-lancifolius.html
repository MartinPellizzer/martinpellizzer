Adamanthus lancifolius

Adamanthus lancifolius, commonly known as the Brazilian palm, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Adamanthus lancifolius is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
