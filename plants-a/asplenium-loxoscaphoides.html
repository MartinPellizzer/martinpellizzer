Asplenium loxoscaphoides

Asplenium loxoscaphoides, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Asplenium loxoscaphoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
