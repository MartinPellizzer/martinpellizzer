Acaena digitata var. subpinnata

Acaena digitata var. subpinnata, commonly known as the slender burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, spiny foliage, which are adaptations to its harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, prickly fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in coastal and rocky habitats, often in areas with high winds and salt exposure.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of New Zealand's native flora.
