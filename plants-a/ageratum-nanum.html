Ageratum nanum

Ageratum nanum, commonly known as dwarf ageratum, is a compact, low-growing plant native to Mexico.

It features small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has hairy, gray-green foliage that adds texture to garden landscapes. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for containers and borders.

Ageratum nanum is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and ease of cultivation.
