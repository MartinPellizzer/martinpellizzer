Aspalathus repens

Aspalathus repens, commonly known as Cape tea or bushwillow, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the semi-arid climate of its native region.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional African medicine for various ailments.
