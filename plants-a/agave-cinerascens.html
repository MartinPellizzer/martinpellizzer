Agave cinerascens

Agave cinerascens, commonly known as the blue agave, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to harsh desert conditions. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears numerous white flowers, which attract pollinators like bats and insects. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical significance in the production of tequila.

Its succulent nature makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping in drought-prone areas.
