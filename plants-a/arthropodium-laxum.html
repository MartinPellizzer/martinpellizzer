Arthropodium laxum

Arthropodium laxum, commonly known as the common spider-orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spider-like appearance due to its long, thin, wiry stems and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often overlooked despite their ornamental potential. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or wetland areas.

Arthropodium laxum is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
