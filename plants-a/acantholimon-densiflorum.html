Acantholimon densiflorum

Acantholimon densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower flaxwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, flowering clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The flowers are small and usually blue or purple in color, contributing to its ornamental value in native gardens.
