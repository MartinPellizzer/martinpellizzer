Abies alba var. acutifolia

Abies alba var. acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved silver fir, is a variant of the silver fir native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are sharply pointed and have a silvery-white underside. The tree typically grows in cool, moist climates at high altitudes, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its cones are typically conical and mature to a dark brown color, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic foliage and ecological benefits.
