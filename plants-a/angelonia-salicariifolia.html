Angelonia salicariifolia

Angelonia salicariifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Angelonia salicariifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
