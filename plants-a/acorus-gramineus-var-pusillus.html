Acorus gramineus var. pusillus

Acorus gramineus var. pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf sweetflag, is a compact, aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in shallow, still water and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to purify water, it is a popular choice for wetland gardens.
