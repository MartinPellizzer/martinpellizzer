Anisopappus brandbergensis

Anisopappus brandbergensis, commonly known as Brandberg's dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Namibia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, woolly leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
