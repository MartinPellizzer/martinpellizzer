Allamanda verrucosa

Allamanda verrucosa, commonly known as the warty allamanda, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in small, raised bumps, giving it its common name. It is typically grown as a tropical ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.
