Aerides matutina

Aerides matutina, commonly known as the morning orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pendant-like flower spikes that bear multiple fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are linear and often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Aerides matutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
