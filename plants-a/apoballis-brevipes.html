Apoballis brevipes

Apoballis brevipes, commonly known as the short-footed daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, daisy-like flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces achenes as its seed type.

Apoballis brevipes is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
