Alpinia antillarum var. grandiflora

Alpinia antillarum var. grandiflora, commonly known as the red ginger, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red inflorescences that emerge from lush, dark green foliage. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Alpinia antillarum var. grandiflora is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display.
