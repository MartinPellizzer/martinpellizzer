Anthericum milanjianum

Anthericum milanjianum, commonly known as the Milanjianum arum, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or wetlands, where it can thrive in humid conditions. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, often with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are small and clustered around the spadix.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
