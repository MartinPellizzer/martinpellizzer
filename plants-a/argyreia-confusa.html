Argyreia confusa

Argyreia confusa, commonly known as the sacred lotus or blue lotus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces a unique, fragrant scent. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The botanical features of Argyreia confusa make it a popular choice for both horticulture and traditional medicine.
