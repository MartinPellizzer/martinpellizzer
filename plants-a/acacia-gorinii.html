Acacia gorinii

Acacia gorinii, commonly known as the Japanese acacia, is a small, deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features slender, zigzagging branches and small, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which appear in early spring and are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia gorinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
