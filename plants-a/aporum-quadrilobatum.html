Aporum quadrilobatum

Aporum quadrilobatum, commonly known as the four-lobed papyrus, is a unique aquatic plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It features distinctive four-lobed leaves that float on the water's surface, aiding in photosynthesis. The plant has a spongy, fibrous stem that helps it remain buoyant in aquatic environments. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it to the bottom of water bodies and absorbing nutrients.

Aporum quadrilobatum plays a role in maintaining water quality by filtering impurities and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
