Anthurium brunneum

Anthurium brunneum, commonly known as the orange anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a reddish or orange tint along the veins. The plant produces striking, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a brightly colored spathe, often in shades of red or orange. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium brunneum is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its vibrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
