Acicarpha tribuloides

Acicarpha tribuloides, commonly known as the African burweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is an annual herb that grows up to 1 meter in height, characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces numerous bracts that resemble burrs, which help in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Acicarpha tribuloides is adapted to arid conditions and is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
