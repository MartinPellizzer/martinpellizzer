Acacia rugata var. concinna

Acacia rugata var. concinna, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features slender, zigzagging stems and small, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
