Abelia biflora var. minor

Abelia biflora var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf abelia, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features small, fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white and pink. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that provide attractive foliage throughout the growing season. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
