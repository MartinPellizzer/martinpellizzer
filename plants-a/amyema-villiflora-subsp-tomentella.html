Amyema villiflora subsp. tomentella

Amyema villiflora subsp.

tomentella, commonly known as the woolly mistflower, is a shrub native to parts of Australia. It features small, white, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant is characterized by its hairy, tomentose leaves and stems, which give it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to native pollinators.
