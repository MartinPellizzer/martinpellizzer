Astragalus androssovianus

Astragalus androssovianus, commonly known as the Crimson Milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces vibrant purple to crimson flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its robust root system and is often found in dry, rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for nitrogen-fixing properties due to its symbiotic relationship with Rhizobia bacteria.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
