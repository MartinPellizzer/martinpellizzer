Abies elegans

Abies elegans, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and its silver-blue foliage, which is dense and needle-like. The tree produces cones that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters long, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its bark is smooth and gray when young, becoming furrowed with age.

Abies elegans thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.
