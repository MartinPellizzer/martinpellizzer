Acer ginnala

Acer ginnala, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. It blooms with small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
