Anaxagorea javanica

Anaxagorea javanica, commonly known as the Javan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, fissured bark. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by clustered, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

Anaxagorea javanica thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
