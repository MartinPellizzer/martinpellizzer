Astragalus vaseyi var. johnstonii

Astragalus vaseyi var. johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions, and it is often found in open grasslands or along streambanks.

The species is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators and wildlife.
