Anchusa leptophylla var. sericans

Anchusa leptophylla var. sericans, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bugloss, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
