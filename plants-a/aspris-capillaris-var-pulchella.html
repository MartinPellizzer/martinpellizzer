Aspris capillaris var. pulchella

Aspris capillaris var. pulchella, commonly known as the purple beardtongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that bear clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important component of native plant ecosystems.
