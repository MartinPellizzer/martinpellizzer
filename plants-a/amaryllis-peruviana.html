Amaryllis peruviana

Amaryllis peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a contrasting throat. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are broad and pointed, growing from a central bulb. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Amaryllis peruviana is popular as a decorative plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
