Alchemilla glyphodonta

Alchemilla glyphodonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Alchemilla glyphodonta is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and historical medicinal uses.
