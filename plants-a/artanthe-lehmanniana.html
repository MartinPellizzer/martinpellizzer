Artanthe lehmanniana

Artanthe lehmanniana, commonly known as the Lehmann's artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Artanthe lehmanniana is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to well-drained soils.
