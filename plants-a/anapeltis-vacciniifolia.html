Anapeltis vacciniifolia

Anapeltis vacciniifolia, commonly known as the red-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Its foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for both landscape design and indoor decoration.
