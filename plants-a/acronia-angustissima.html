Acronia angustissima

Acronia angustissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf acronia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Acronia angustissima is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its aesthetic appeal in wetland gardens.
