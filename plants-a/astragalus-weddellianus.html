Astragalus weddellianus

Astragalus weddellianus, commonly known as Weddell's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Central Asia and the Arctic regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late summer. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor and arid soils.

Astragalus weddellianus is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
