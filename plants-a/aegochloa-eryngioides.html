Aegochloa eryngioides

Aegochloa eryngioides, commonly known as the spiked grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-gray sheen. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Aegochloa eryngioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.

Morphology

Aegochloa eryngioides has narrow, grass-like leaves that are cylindrical in shape, arranged in a basal rosette, and typically range from 10 to 30 cm in length with a bluish-green color.

The stem is upright, smooth, and slightly hairy, growing in a clump-forming pattern with a light green to grayish bark. Its flowers are spike-like inflorescences with small, white to pale blue florets, blooming during the summer months and pollinated by wind. The fruit is a three-angled, dry capsule that disperses its seeds by wind, with small, lightweight seeds that can travel considerable distances.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, helping the plant anchor in sandy or disturbed soils.
