Aspidium tussacii

Aspidium tussacii, commonly known as the Tussac fern, is a species of fern native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its spores are released from specialized structures called sori, which are located on the underside of its leaves.

Aspidium tussacii is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated for its unique foliage.
