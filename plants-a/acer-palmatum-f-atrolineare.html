Acer palmatum f. atrolineare

Acer palmatum f. atrolineare, commonly known as the black-veined maple, is a striking ornamental tree or shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its finely cut, palmate leaves that display a deep red to black coloration on the veins, contrasting with the lighter green of the leaf blade. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters, with a rounded, compact form. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes, especially in cooler climates.
