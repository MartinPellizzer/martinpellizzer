Ageratum maritimum var. intermedium

Ageratum maritimum var. intermedium, commonly known as the coastal ageratum, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with bushy growth habits and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to salt spray and coastal conditions. It is often used in coastal gardens for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh maritime environments.

Its foliage is usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, adding visual interest to landscapes.
