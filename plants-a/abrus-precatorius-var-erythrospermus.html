Abrus precatorius var. erythrospermus

Abrus precatorius var. erythrospermus, commonly known as the red-seeded devil's bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange seeds enclosed in a flat, bean-shaped pod. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is notable for its toxic properties, as the seeds contain potent lectins that can be harmful if ingested.
