Astragalus physodes subsp. acikirensis

Astragalus physodes subsp.

acikirensis, commonly known as the acikirensis milkvetch, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces pods that contain small, round seeds.
