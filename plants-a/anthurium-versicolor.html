Anthurium versicolor

Anthurium versicolor, commonly known as the variegated anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that display a mix of green, white, and pink hues. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is often a different color from the spadix. Anthurium versicolor prefers bright, indirect light and thrives in humid environments with well-draining soil.

It is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
