Astragalus devesae

Astragalus devesae, commonly known as the Devesa milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus devesae is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
