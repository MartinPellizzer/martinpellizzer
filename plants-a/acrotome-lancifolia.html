Acrotome lancifolia

Acrotome lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acrotome, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange, typically blooming during the cooler months. Acrotome lancifolia is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and can tolerate high levels of sunlight and drought.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes and botanical gardens.
