Artemisia saxatilis

Artemisia saxatilis, commonly known as rock wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing on rocky slopes and cliffs. Artemisia saxatilis has a woody stem and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Its essential oils contribute to its strong, bitter scent and have been used in traditional herbal medicine.
