Adesmia capricornu

Adesmia capricornu, commonly known as the Capricorn bean, is a small, deciduous shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, hairy pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's growth habit and morphology make it well adapted to dry, disturbed habitats.
