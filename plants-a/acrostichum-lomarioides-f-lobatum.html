Acrostichum lomarioides f. lobatum

Acrostichum lomarioides f. lobatum, commonly known as the lobate fern, is a variant of the common brake fern.

It is a fern species characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are typically divided into lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value.

The lobate form is distinguished by its broader, more pronounced lobes compared to the typical variety.
