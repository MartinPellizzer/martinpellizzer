Amorphophallus lambii

Amorphophallus lambii, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces a unique inflorescence that resembles a phallic shape, hence its scientific name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also notable for its ability to store energy in its underground tuber, allowing it to survive periods of drought.
