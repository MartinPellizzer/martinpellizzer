Arctotis semipapposa

Arctotis semipapposa, commonly known as the Narrow-pappus Daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Arctotis semipapposa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
