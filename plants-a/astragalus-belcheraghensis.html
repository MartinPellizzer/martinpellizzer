Astragalus belcheraghensis

Astragalus belcheraghensis, commonly known as Belcheragh milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Astragalus belcheraghensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
