Allium bidentatum var. bidentatum

Allium bidentatum var. bidentatum, commonly known as the two-toothed leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, hollow stems that are often covered in a fibrous sheath. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, with a mild garlic scent when crushed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild culinary uses in some cuisines.
