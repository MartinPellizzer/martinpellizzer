Acacia viscosa

Acacia viscosa, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces flat, leathery pods that contain seeds, and its leaves are typically bipinnate, meaning they are divided into two sets of smaller leaflets. Acacia viscosa is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal and inland regions of Australia.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
