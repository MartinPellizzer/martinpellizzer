Aster purpurascens

Aster purpurascens, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy purple petals surrounding a central yellow disk, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Aster purpurascens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
