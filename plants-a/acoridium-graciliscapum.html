Acoridium graciliscapum

Acoridium graciliscapum, commonly known as the slender-stemmed arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in spikes. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense colonies. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential ecological roles in its native habitats.

Acoridium graciliscapum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
