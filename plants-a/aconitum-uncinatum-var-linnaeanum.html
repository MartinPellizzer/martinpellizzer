Aconitum uncinatum var. linnaeanum

Aconitum uncinatum var. linnaeanum, commonly known as the Linnaean monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple, spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive veining pattern, and its stems are typically hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
