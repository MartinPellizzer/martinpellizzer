Althoffia tripyxis

Althoffia tripyxis, commonly known as the three-lobed water plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and wetlands. The plant's leaves are divided into three lobes, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. Althoffia tripyxis is often found in regions with warm climates and is adapted to thrive in still or slow-moving water.

Its botanical structure supports its role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
