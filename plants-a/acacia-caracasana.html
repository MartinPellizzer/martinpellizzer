Acacia caracasana

Acacia caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The acacia's thorned stems and clustered flowers make it a distinctive and recognizable species.
