Acacia trinalis

Acacia trinalis, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. It produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acacia trinalis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
