Andropogon longiramosus

Andropogon longiramosus, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of prairie and woodland ecosystems. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Andropogon longiramosus plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
