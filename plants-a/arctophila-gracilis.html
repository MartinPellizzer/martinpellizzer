Arctophila gracilis

Arctophila gracilis, commonly known as the slender stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and rocky habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that store water, aiding in survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in spring or early summer. Its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in poor soil make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Arctophila gracilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
