Alyssum lescurii

Alyssum lescurii, commonly known as the Sierra Valley alyssum, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically features dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems covered with fine, silky hairs, and its leaves are linear to oblanceolate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Alyssum lescurii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
