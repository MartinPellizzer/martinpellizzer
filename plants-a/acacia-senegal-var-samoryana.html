Acacia senegal var. samoryana

Acacia senegal var. samoryana, commonly known as the gum arabic tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It is characterized by its straight, fissured bark and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by long, slender pods containing a sticky, resinous gum known as gum arabic. This gum is widely used in food, pharmaceutical, and cosmetic industries due to its emulsifying and stabilizing properties.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments and is well adapted to poor soil conditions.
