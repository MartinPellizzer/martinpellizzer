Aster himalaicus

Aster himalaicus, commonly known as the Himalayan aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments at high elevations. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets that give it a daisy-like appearance.

Aster himalaicus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual appeal.
