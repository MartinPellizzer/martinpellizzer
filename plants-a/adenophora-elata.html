Adenophora elata

Adenophora elata, commonly known as the tall bellflower, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are typically arranged in inflorescences at the top of the stem. Adenophora elata is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
