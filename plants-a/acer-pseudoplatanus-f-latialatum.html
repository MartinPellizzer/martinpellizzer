Acer pseudoplatanus f. latialatum

Acer pseudoplatanus f. latialatum, commonly known as the Giant Maple, is a unique variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 20 cm in length. The foliage is typically a vibrant green in summer, turning gold and orange in autumn. This form is notable for its increased size and more pronounced lobes compared to the standard species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
