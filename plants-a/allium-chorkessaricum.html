Allium chorkessaricum

Allium chorkessaricum, commonly known as the Armenian garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to lilac-colored flowers. The plant produces bulbs that are often used in local cuisines for their pungent flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
