Alyssum cuneifolium subsp. corymbosum

Alyssum cuneifolium subsp.

corymbosum, commonly known as the Sierra Point Alyssum, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

The plant features small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
