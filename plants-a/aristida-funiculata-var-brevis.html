Aristida funiculata var. brevis

Aristida funiculata var. brevis, commonly known as the short-stemmed threeawn, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, which helps it conserve water in arid environments.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
