Avena cristata

Avena cristata, commonly known as the tufted oat, is a perennial grass native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with a distinctive, frilled appearance. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Avena cristata is often used in ornamental gardening for its decorative foliage and unique floral structure.
