Alyssum tortuosum f. submoderatum

Alyssum tortuosum f. submoderatum, commonly known as the slender alyssum, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
