Acaciella angustissima var. filicioides

Acaciella angustissima var. filicioides, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It features small, linear leaves that resemble those of ferns, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and birds. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
