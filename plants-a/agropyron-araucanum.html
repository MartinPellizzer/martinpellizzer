Agropyron araucanum

Agropyron araucanum, commonly known as the Chilean wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open fields and along coastal areas.

Agropyron araucanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control.
