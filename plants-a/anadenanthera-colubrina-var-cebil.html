Anadenanthera colubrina var. cebil

Anadenanthera colubrina var. cebil, commonly known as the copal tree or sacred tree, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces distinctive yellow to orange pods that contain a toxic, milky sap. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, open woodlands. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

The tree is also known for its cultural significance among indigenous communities.
