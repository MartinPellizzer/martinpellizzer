Aquilegia facchinii var. thalictrifolia

Aquilegia facchinii var. thalictrifolia, commonly known as the Thalictrifolia columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features showy, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple, with five petals and two elongated nectar-rich spurs. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. It is typically found in woodland edges and rocky slopes, preferring partial shade to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
