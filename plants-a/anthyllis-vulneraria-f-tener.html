Anthyllis vulneraria f. tener

Anthyllis vulneraria f. tener, commonly known as the soft-haired bugloss, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in dry, rocky soils.

This variety is distinguished by its softer, more densely haired leaves compared to other forms of Anthyllis vulneraria.
