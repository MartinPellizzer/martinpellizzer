Achillea coronopifolia

Achillea coronopifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

It has a long flowering period, usually from late spring to early autumn, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
