Aristolochia bahiensis

Aristolochia bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, maroon-colored flowers that resemble a pipe, which are pollinated by specific insects. The plant has heart-shaped, simple leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its fruits are elongated, ribbed berries that contain several seeds.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
