Adenia refracta

Adenia refracta, commonly known as the African mountain gum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant features elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a rough texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually yellow or greenish-yellow in color.

Adenia refracta is often found in rocky or wooded areas and is adapted to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.
