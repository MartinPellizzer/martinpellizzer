Acalypha fallax

Acalypha fallax, commonly known as the red-leaf acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red or purple leaves that contrast with its green veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Acalypha fallax is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.
