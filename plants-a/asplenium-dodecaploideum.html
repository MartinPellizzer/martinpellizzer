Asplenium dodecaploideum

Asplenium dodecaploideum, commonly known as the twelve-ploid spleenwort, is a member of the Aspleniaceae family and is characterized by its unique polyploidy, which contributes to its genetic diversity.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base, with a distinctive appearance that sets it apart from other spleenworts. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the undersides of the fronds, facilitating spore dispersal.

Asplenium dodecaploideum is of interest to botanists due to its complex genetic makeup and ecological adaptability.
