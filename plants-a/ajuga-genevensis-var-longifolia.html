Ajuga genevensis var. longifolia

Ajuga genevensis var. longifolia, commonly known as carpet bugle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers in late spring to early summer, which are arranged in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to suppress weeds.

Its botanical name reflects its origin and distinctive floral characteristics.
