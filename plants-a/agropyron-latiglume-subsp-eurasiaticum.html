Agropyron latiglume subsp. eurasiaticum

Agropyron latiglume subsp.

eurasiaticum, commonly known as the Eurasian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Eurasia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters, with lemmas that have a distinct, stiff texture. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soils.
