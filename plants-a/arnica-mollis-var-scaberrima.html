Arnica mollis var. scaberrima

Arnica mollis var. scaberrima, commonly known as rough Arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and meadows. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its medicinal properties.

However, it is toxic if ingested and should be handled with care.
