Anemia bipinnata

Anemia bipinnata, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are finely divided and give the plant a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Anemia bipinnata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
