Antennaria contorta

Antennaria contorta, commonly known as the bent-silkweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, thread-like hairs. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Antennaria contorta is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
