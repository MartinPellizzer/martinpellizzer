Aster puniceus f. rufescens

Aster puniceus f. rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed aster, is a compact perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and lavender, with a distinctive red or purple stem. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native gardens and pollinator habitats.
