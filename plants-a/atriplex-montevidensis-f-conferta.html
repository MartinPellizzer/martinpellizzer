Atriplex montevidensis f. conferta

Atriplex montevidensis f. conferta, commonly known as the denseleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and succulent foliage. The plant is adapted to arid and saline environments, showcasing its ability to thrive in harsh conditions. Its leaves are typically small, fleshy, and often covered in a layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

This variety is noted for its dense, compact structure, which distinguishes it from other forms of Atriplex montevidensis.
