Arctostaphylos chaloneorum

Arctostaphylos chaloneorum, commonly known as the Sierra foothill manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its reddish bark and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, leathery berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Arctostaphylos chaloneorum is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
