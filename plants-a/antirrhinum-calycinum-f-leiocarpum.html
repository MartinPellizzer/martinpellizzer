Antirrhinum calycinum f. leiocarpum

Antirrhinum calycinum f. leiocarpum, commonly known as the smooth-seeded snapdragon, is a cultivated form of the snapdragon plant.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors. The plant typically produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic value and ease of cultivation.
