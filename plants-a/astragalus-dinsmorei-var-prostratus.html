Astragalus dinsmorei var. prostratus

Astragalus dinsmorei var. prostratus, commonly known as the prostrate milkvetch, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its prostrate or creeping growth habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus dinsmorei var. prostratus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
