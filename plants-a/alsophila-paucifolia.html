Alsophila paucifolia

Alsophila paucifolia, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for many decades, making it a long-lived species. Its fronds are typically dark green and can reach lengths of up to several meters.

Alsophila paucifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and coastal areas.
