Arthraxon castratus

Arthraxon castratus, commonly known as the slender false grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit with slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common sight in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Arthraxon castratus is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and tolerate various environmental conditions.
