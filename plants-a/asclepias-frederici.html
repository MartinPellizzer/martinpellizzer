Asclepias frederici

Asclepias frederici, commonly known as the Frederic's milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among milkweeds, and it plays a vital role in supporting monarch butterfly populations.

Its flowers are typically pink to red in color and bloom in late spring to early summer.
