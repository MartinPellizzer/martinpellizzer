Allasia

Allasia, commonly known as the "Chinese bellflower," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

These plants are native to regions of Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan, and are often found in moist, woodland environments. Allasia species typically feature showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue or purple. They have lance-shaped leaves and produce small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various therapeutic purposes.
