Anthericum amplexifolium

Anthericum amplexifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf arum, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, which is characteristic of the Araceae family. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in moisture retention. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it relies on insect pollination.

Anthericum amplexifolium thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
