Agapetes brevicuspis

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant typically grows at high elevations, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained acidic soils. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Agapetes brevicuspis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
