Arthraxon ciliaris var. coloratus

Arthraxon ciliaris var. coloratus, commonly known as the colored beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and distinctive, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors that contribute to its ornamental value.

The species is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
