Anthurium emarginatum

Anthurium emarginatum, commonly known as the emarginate anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces vibrant red spathes that enclose its spadix, which is covered in tiny, clustered flowers. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium emarginatum is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and unique floral structure.
