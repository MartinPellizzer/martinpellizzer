Aster argophyllus

Aster argophyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are silver-gray in color, covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

It produces showy blue to purple flowers in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
