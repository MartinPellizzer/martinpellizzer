Astragalus stella var. minor

Astragalus stella var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf star vetch, is a compact perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
