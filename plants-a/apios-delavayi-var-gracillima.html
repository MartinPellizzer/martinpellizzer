Apios delavayi var. gracillima

Apios delavayi var. gracillima, commonly known as the slender wild bean, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by slender, bean-like pods. Its roots are often tuberous and can be used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
