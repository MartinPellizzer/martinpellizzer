Alchemilla plicata f. aestivalis

Alchemilla plicata f. aestivalis, commonly known as the summer lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features rounded, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage is used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported soothing properties.
