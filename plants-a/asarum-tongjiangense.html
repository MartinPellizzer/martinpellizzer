Asarum tongjiangense

Asarum tongjiangense, commonly known as Tongjiang wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, irregularly shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with caution.
