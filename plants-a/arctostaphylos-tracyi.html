Arctostaphylos tracyi

Arctostaphylos tracyi, commonly known as Tracy manzanita, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. Tracy manzanita typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
