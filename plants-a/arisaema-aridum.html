Arisaema aridum

Arisaema aridum, commonly known as the desert arum, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of China.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces a single, showy white spathe that encloses a dense cluster of small, greenish-yellow flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
