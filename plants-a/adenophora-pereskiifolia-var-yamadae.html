Adenophora pereskiifolia var. yamadae

Adenophora pereskiifolia var. yamadae, commonly known as the Yamada's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color, often with a white throat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
