Aster puniceus var. scabricaulis

Aster puniceus var. scabricaulis, commonly known as the rough-stemmed aster, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features showy, purple to pink ray flowers surrounded by yellow disk florets, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has rough, hairy stems and leaves that are often lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
