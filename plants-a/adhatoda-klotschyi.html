Adhatoda klotschyi

Adhatoda klotschyi, commonly known as the African skullcap, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or violet, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which contain various bioactive compounds.

The plant is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.
