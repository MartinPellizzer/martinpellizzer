Aristida sieberiana

Aristida sieberiana, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to provide winter interest through its persistent foliage.
