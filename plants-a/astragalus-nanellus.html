Astragalus nanellus

Astragalus nanellus, commonly known as the smallleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus nanellus is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
