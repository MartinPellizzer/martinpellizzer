Achyranthes aspera var. velutina

Achyranthes aspera var. velutina, commonly known as velvet senna, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
