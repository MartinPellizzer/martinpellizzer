Alchemilla bachiti

Alchemilla bachiti, commonly known as the rock alchemilla, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features basal leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. Its small white flowers appear in clusters during the summer months, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Alchemilla bachiti is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
