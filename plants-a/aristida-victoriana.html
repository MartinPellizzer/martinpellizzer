Aristida victoriana

Aristida victoriana, commonly known as the Victoriana grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and fine, silky leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are clustered in dense panicles, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grassy areas.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
