Aldama dilloniorum

Aldama dilloniorum, commonly known as the Dillon's Aldama, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
