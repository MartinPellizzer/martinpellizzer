Anacampseros rupifraga

Anacampseros rupifraga, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. It is adapted to grow in rocky and arid environments, making it highly drought-tolerant.

Its thick leaves store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
