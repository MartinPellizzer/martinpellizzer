Adenophora periplocifolia

Adenophora periplocifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its flowers are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
