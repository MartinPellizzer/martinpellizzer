Aralia quinquefolia var. pseudoginseng

Aralia quinquefolia var. pseudoginseng, commonly known as American ginseng, is a deciduous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a tall, unbranched stem with large, palmately compound leaves that are typically five-lobed. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in an umbel, followed by dark blue berries. Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its adaptability to moist, shaded environments.
