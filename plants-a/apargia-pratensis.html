Apargia pratensis

Apargia pratensis, commonly known as the meadow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and branching.

Apargia pratensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
