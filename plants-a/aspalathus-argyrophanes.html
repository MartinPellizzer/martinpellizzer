Aspalathus argyrophanes

Aspalathus argyrophanes, commonly known as the silver-leafed bushveld pea, is a small, deciduous shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats. It is not widely cultivated for commercial purposes but is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The species is relatively drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.
