Acacia brachybotrya f. argyrophylla

Acacia brachybotrya f. argyrophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with a silvery-white underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular inflorescences, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its small, leathery leaves are adapted to arid conditions, making it drought-tolerant.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
