Alnus japonica var. rufinervis

Alnus japonica var. rufinervis, commonly known as the Japanese alder with red veins, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, winged fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
