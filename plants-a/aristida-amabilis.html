Aristida amabilis

Aristida amabilis, commonly known as the pink trudelgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with pink or purple at the base. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically pale green to reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid and semi-arid landscapes.

Due to its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, it is often used in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
