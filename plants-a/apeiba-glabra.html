Apeiba glabra

Apeiba glabra, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmate leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-colored fruits resembling small apples. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and its role in traditional medicine and local ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential economic importance.
