Alchemilla psiloneura

Alchemilla psiloneura, commonly known as the slender-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Alchemilla psiloneura is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
