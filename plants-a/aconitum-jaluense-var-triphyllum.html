Aconitum jaluense var. triphyllum

Aconitum jaluense var. triphyllum, commonly known as the three-leaf aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three leaflets, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
