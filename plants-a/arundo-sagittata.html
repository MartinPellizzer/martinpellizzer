Arundo sagittata

Arundo sagittata, commonly known as arrowhead reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in summer. The plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. It has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
