Athrixia kassneri

Athrixia kassneri, commonly known as the Kassner's featherplant, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its distinctive, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
