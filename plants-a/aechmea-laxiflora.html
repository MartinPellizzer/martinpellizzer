Aechmea laxiflora

Aechmea laxiflora, commonly known as the showy pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant, spathiflorous inflorescences with colorful bracts that range from red to orange. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, blooming in a dramatic display that attracts pollinators.

Aechmea laxiflora is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.

Morphology

Aechmea laxiflora has leaves that are broad, strap-shaped, and arranged in a rosette, with a waxy, dark green surface and a pointed tip.

The plant lacks a traditional stem, instead producing a short, thickened base with a fibrous, rough-textured bark. Its flowers are clustered in a spike, featuring showy pink to red bracts that enclose small, greenish-white flowers, blooming during the dry season and pollinated by wind. The fruit is a fleshy, red berry containing numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by birds and mammals.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, anchoring the plant in the soil and absorbing moisture from the surrounding environment.
