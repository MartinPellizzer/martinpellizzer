Aechmea laxiflora

Aechmea laxiflora, commonly known as the showy pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant, spathiflorous inflorescences with colorful bracts that range from red to orange. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, blooming in a dramatic display that attracts pollinators.

Aechmea laxiflora is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
