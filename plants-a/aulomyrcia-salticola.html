Aulomyrcia salticola

Aulomyrcia salticola, commonly known as the jumping bean plant, is a species native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous husk. These fruits are known for their unique ability to "jump" when disturbed, a behavior caused by the movement of the seeds inside.

The botanical features of Aulomyrcia salticola make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
