Acinopetala schizopetala

Acinopetala schizopetala, commonly known as the split-petalled acacia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is notable for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in degraded landscapes.
