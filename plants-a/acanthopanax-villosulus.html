Acanthopanax villosulus

Acanthopanax villosulus, commonly known as the hairy angel's trumpet, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible but should be consumed with caution. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it requires careful management due to its vigorous growth.
