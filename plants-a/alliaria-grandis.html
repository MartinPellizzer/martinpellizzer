Alliaria grandis

Alliaria grandis, commonly known as broadleaf garlic or winter garlic, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant produces small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are broad and slightly hairy, and it has a strong, pungent odor similar to garlic.

Alliaria grandis is often found growing in moist, woodland areas and can be invasive in some regions.
