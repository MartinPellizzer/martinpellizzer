Airampoa albisaetacens

Airampoa albisaetacens, commonly known as the white-haired bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a smooth surface and a fibrous texture.

Airampoa albisaetacens is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
