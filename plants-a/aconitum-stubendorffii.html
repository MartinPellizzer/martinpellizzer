Aconitum stubendorffii

Aconitum stubendorffii, commonly known as the Tibetan monk's hood, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has dark green, doubly serrated leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a potentially dangerous plant for both humans and animals.
