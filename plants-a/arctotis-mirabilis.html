Arctotis mirabilis

Arctotis mirabilis, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, daisy-like blooms with yellow centers and white to pink petals, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Arctotis mirabilis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
