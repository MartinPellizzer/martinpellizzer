Aloe peyrierasii

Aloe peyrierasii, commonly known as the Peyrieras' aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments. The leaves are typically green with a waxy coating, and they often have a reddish or purple tinge at the base. It produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear yellow to orange flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil and full sun.
