Aechmea oligosphaera

Aechmea oligosphaera, commonly known as the silver pine bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the humid forests of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater and nutrients. The leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly trichomes, giving the plant its distinctive silvery appearance. It produces vibrant red or orange flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette, adding a dramatic visual contrast.

This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.
