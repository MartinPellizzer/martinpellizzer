Astragalus degensis

Astragalus degensis, commonly known as the Tibetan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia, including Tibet and Mongolia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. Astragalus degensis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its seeds are often used in herbal remedies and are valued for their potential therapeutic applications.
