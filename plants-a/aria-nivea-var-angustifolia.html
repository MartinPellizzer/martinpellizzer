Aria nivea var. angustifolia

Aria nivea var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils at high altitudes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and delicate species.
