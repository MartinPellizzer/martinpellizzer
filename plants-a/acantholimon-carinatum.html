Acantholimon carinatum

Acantholimon carinatum, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features slender, upright stems covered with soft, hairy foliage. Its flowers are small and blue to violet in color, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.
