Anthyllis tangerina

Anthyllis tangerina, commonly known as the tangerine anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in dry, sunny environments.

Anthyllis tangerina is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
