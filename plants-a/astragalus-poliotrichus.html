Astragalus poliotrichus

Astragalus poliotrichus, commonly known as the purple milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing at high altitudes.

Astragalus poliotrichus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
