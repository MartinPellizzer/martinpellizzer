Acer pseudoplatanus f. flavescens

Acer pseudoplatanus f. flavescens, commonly known as the golden maple, is a variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant is a deciduous tree with a rounded crown and smooth, grey bark. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for gardens and parks.
