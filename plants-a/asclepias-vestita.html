Asclepias vestita

Asclepias vestita, commonly known as the woolly milkweed, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces pods that split open to release seeds with silky filaments, aiding in wind dispersal. Asclepias vestita is a host plant for the monarch butterfly, providing essential nourishment for monarch caterpillars.

Its milkweed sap contains cardiac glycosides, which make it toxic to many herbivores but beneficial for certain pollinators.
