Allium commune

Allium commune, commonly known as garlic, is a bulbous plant native to Central Asia and widely cultivated around the world.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fleshy underground bulbs composed of multiple cloves. The plant produces tall, cylindrical stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. Its bulbs are rich in sulfur compounds, which contribute to its distinctive odor and numerous medicinal properties.

Garlic is also valued for its culinary uses and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
