Alexitoxicon volubile

Alexitoxicon volubile, commonly known as the weeping heart vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and cascading clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a unique, mildly toxic sap that has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in humid environments.

The name "Alexitoxicon" reflects its historical use in treating emotional distress, though its botanical properties remain under study.
