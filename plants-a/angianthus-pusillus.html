Angianthus pusillus

Angianthus pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf angianthus, is a small flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, making it ideal for rock gardens or containers. Its flowers, which are usually white or pale blue, bloom in late summer and are followed by small, berry-like fruits.

Angianthus pusillus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
