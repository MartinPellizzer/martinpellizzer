Armeniaca vulgaris var. saccharata

Armeniaca vulgaris var. saccharata, commonly known as the Chinese date or jujube.

This plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China, Korea, and Japan. It produces sweet, fleshy fruits that are rich in sugars and nutrients. The botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and white or pink flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is valued for its fruit and has been cultivated for thousands of years in traditional agriculture.
