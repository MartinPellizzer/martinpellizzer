Acronia dejavu

Acronia dejavu, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and striking, white, orchid-like flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its unique morphology and elusive nature have made it a subject of fascination for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and illegal collection, Acronia dejavu is critically endangered, highlighting the need for urgent conservation efforts.
