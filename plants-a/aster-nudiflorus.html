Aster nudiflorus

Aster nudiflorus, commonly known as the nakedflower aster, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that appear in late summer to early fall. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Aster nudiflorus thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in open areas with partial shade.
