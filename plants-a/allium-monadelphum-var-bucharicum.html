Allium monadelphum var. bucharicum

Allium monadelphum var. bucharicum, commonly known as the Bukharan leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of long, narrow leaves. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem bearing umbel-like clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a valuable crop in certain regions.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.
