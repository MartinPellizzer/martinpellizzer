Adenodolichos upembaensis

Adenodolichos upembaensis, commonly known as the Upemba milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical environments, often growing in moist, shaded areas. Its root system has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
