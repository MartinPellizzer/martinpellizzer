Actinidia kolomikta var. gagnepainii

Actinidia kolomikta var. gagnepainii, commonly known as the variegated goatberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display shades of white, green, and pink, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or red, often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers partial shade to full sun, with well-drained soil.

Its unique foliage and fruit make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and culinary benefits.
