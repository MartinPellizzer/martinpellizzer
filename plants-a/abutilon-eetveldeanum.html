Abutilon eetveldeanum

Abutilon eetveldeanum, commonly known as the Eetvelde's abutilon, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

Abutilon eetveldeanum prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
