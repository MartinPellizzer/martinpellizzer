Acacia clelandii

Acacia clelandii, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and clusters of small, white, fluffy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that serve as the primary photosynthetic organs. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal and sandy soils.

The white wattle is an important component of local ecosystems and is also cultivated for its ornamental value.
