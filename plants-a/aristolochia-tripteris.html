Aristolochia tripteris

Aristolochia tripteris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, hood-shaped flowers that are typically maroon or purple in color. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, winged seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
