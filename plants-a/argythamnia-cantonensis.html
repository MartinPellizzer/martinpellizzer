Argythamnia cantonensis

Argythamnia cantonensis, commonly known as the Canton thornapple, is a shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds, often dispersed by wind.

It is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
