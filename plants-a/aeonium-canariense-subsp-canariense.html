Aeonium canariense subsp. canariense

Aeonium canariense subsp.

canariense, commonly known as the Canary Island Aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands. It features rosettes of fleshy, green to blue-gray leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder.

The plant produces tall, branching stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact size and striking foliage make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
