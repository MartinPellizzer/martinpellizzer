Amaranthus laxiflorus

Amaranthus laxiflorus, commonly known as the slender amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its branched stems and lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the variety. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils such as fields and roadsides.

Amaranthus laxiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
