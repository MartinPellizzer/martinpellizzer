Amblyanthera versicolor var. pubiflora

Amblyanthera versicolor var. pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy-flowered amblyanthera, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its variable flower colors, which range from white to pink and red, depending on environmental conditions. The plant features small, clustered flowers with hairy petals, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically ovate and slightly hairy, contributing to its overall appearance.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to different soil types and its role in local pollination systems.
