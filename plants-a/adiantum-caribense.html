Adiantum caribense

Adiantum caribense, commonly known as the Carib maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that resemble the shape of a feather. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, narrow segments that are often curled under at the tips.

Adiantum caribense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
