Acalypha hellwigii var. glabra

Acalypha hellwigii var. glabra, commonly known as smooth false spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth, glabrous surface. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-yellow flowers that are often subtended by bracts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it may spread aggressively.
