Anemone pseudoaltaica var. gracilis

Anemone pseudoaltaica var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive central cone-shaped structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
