Astragalus varius var. albiflorus

Astragalus varius var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
