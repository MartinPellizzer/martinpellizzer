Artemisia maritima var. fischeriana

Artemisia maritima var. fischeriana, commonly known as the Fischer's seaside wormwood, is a variant of the seaside wormwood species native to coastal regions of North America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in the summer, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils. It thrives in well-drained, saline soils and is often found in dune environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh coastal conditions.
