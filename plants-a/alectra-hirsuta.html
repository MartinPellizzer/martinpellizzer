Alectra hirsuta

Alectra hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woods or along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Alectra hirsuta is a perennial herb that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
