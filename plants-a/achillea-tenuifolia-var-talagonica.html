Achillea tenuifolia var. talagonica

Achillea tenuifolia var. talagonica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, linear leaves that are gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their anti-inflammatory and astringent effects.
