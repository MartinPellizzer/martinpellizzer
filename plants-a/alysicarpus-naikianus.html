Alysicarpus naikianus

Alysicarpus naikianus, commonly known as the Indian senna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
