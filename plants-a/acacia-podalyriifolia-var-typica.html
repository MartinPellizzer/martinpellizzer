Acacia podalyriifolia var. typica

Acacia podalyriifolia var. typica, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, needle-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that are arranged in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods, which split open to release the seeds when mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
