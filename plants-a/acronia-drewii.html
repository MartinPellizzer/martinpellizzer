Acronia drewii

Acronia drewii, commonly known as the desert broom.

It is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant has small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Acronia drewii is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
