Acalypha villosa var. paniculata

Acalypha villosa var. paniculata, commonly known as the hairy ironweed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features striking, palmate leaves with a velvety texture and soft, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which are often tinged with red or purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscape design.
