Aleuritia japonica

Aleuritia japonica, commonly known as Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Aleuritia japonica is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and floral display.
