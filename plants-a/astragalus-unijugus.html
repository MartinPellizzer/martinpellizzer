Astragalus unijugus

Astragalus unijugus, commonly known as the single-hipped milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Astragalus unijugus plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
