Acer politum

Acer politum, commonly known as the smooth maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often reddish-barked trunk and palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically 5-9 lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.
