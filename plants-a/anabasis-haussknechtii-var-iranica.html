Anabasis haussknechtii var. iranica

Anabasis haussknechtii var. iranica, commonly known as the Iranian saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Iran.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss through transpiration. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its deep root system enables it to access groundwater, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
