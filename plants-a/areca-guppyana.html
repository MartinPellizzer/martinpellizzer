Areca guppyana

Areca guppyana, commonly known as the Guppy's areca, is a species of palm native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched stem and pinnate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-pink flowers arranged in a branched inflorescence, followed by clustered, black, spherical fruits. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
