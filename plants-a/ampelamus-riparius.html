Ampelamus riparius

Ampelamus riparius, commonly known as the river bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils along riverbanks and wet meadows. The plant features slender, ascending stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are blue to purple, borne in racemes, and are attractive to pollinators.

It is often found in riparian habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
