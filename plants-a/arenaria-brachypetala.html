Arenaria brachypetala

Arenaria brachypetala, commonly known as the short-stemmed sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in dry conditions.

Arenaria brachypetala is often found in coastal dunes, gravelly soils, and other xerophilous habitats.
