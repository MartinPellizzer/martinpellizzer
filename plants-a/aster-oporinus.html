Aster oporinus

Aster oporinus, commonly known as the meadow aster, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to North America and is often found in open fields, meadows, and along roadsides. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Aster oporinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
