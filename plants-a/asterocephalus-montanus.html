Asterocephalus montanus

Asterocephalus montanus, commonly known as the mountain aster, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are usually purple or blue, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Asterocephalus montanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
