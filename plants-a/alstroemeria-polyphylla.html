Alstroemeria polyphylla

Alstroemeria polyphylla, commonly known as the Chilean lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Alstroemeria polyphylla is a perennial herb with a fibrous root system, and it produces clusters of flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and floral arrangements.
