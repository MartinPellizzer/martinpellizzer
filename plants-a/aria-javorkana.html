Aria javorkana

Aria javorkana, commonly known as the Java iris, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its sword-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

Aria javorkana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and soil conditions.
