Agropyron dasyanthum subsp. birjutczense

Agropyron dasyanthum subsp.

birjutczense, commonly known as the Central Asian needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in dry, steppe-like environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
