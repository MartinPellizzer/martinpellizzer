Amomophyllum floribundum

Amomophyllum floribundum, commonly known as the fragrant amomum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their digestive and anti-inflammatory effects.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
