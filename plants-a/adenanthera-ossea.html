Adenanthera ossea

Adenanthera ossea, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in preventing soil erosion.
