Arabis hirsuta var. incisa

Arabis hirsuta var. incisa, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe.

It features basal leaves that are hairy and lobed, with a distinctive serrated edge along the leaf margins. The plant produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky crevices or on slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental species suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
