Agropyron glaucum var. savignonei

Agropyron glaucum var. savignonei, commonly known as the Savignone bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and bluish-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, blue-green spikelets.

Agropyron glaucum var. savignonei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
