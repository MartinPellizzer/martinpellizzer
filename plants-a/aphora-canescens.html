Aphora canescens

Aphora canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf aphora, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor and drought-prone soils.

Aphora canescens is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and healing properties.
