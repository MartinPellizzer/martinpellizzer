Achillea millefolium subsp. sudetica

Achillea millefolium subsp.

sudetica, commonly known as the Sudetic yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pinkish in color.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Asteraceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical medicinal uses.
