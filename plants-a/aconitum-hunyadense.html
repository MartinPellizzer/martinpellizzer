Aconitum hunyadense

Aconitum hunyadense, commonly known as the Hungarian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Basin.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves and can grow up to 1.5 meters tall. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a dangerous plant and should be handled with care.
