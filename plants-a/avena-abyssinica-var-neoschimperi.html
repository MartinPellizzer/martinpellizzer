Avena abyssinica var. neoschimperi

Avena abyssinica var. neoschimperi, commonly known as Abyssinian oat, is a perennial grass species native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in high-altitude regions with cool, temperate climates and is adapted to poor, rocky soils.

This variety is valued for its potential in forage and soil stabilization in arid and semi-arid environments.
