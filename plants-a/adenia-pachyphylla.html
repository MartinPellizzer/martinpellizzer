Adenia pachyphylla

Adenia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved adenia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Its botanical features make it a unique and resilient species within the Adeninae subfamily.
