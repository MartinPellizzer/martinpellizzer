Asparagus broussonetii

Asparagus broussonetii, commonly known as the Indian asparagus, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of South Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 2 meters in height, featuring woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Asparagus broussonetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
