Aristolochia quadriflora

Aristolochia quadriflora, commonly known as the Japanese arrowroot or Chinese birthwort, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces striking, hooded flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor. Its fruit is a dark blue, fleshy berry that contains seeds surrounded by a gelatinous coating.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
