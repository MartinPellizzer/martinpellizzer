Amelanchier oblongifolia var. micropetala

Amelanchier oblongifolia var. micropetala, commonly known as the dwarf serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching branches and produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers in early spring. The plant bears small, edible berries that ripen to a dark red or black color in late summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
