Armeria maritima var. curvifolia

Armeria maritima var. curvifolia, commonly known as sea thrift, is a low-growing evergreen perennial native to coastal areas of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features dense clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand salty air and strong winds. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes and coastal heathlands.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens and erosion control.
