Alchemilla nigricans

Alchemilla nigricans, commonly known as black sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Alchemilla nigricans is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
