Adansonia za

Adansonia za, commonly known as the baobab tree, is a large, deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its massive, bottle-shaped trunk that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The tree has thick, fibrous bark that is resistant to fire and pests. Its leaves are palmate, consisting of several leaflets arranged in a fan-like pattern.

The flowers are large, showy, and typically white or pink, attracting pollinators such as bats and insects.
