Aristolochia crenata

Aristolochia crenata, commonly known as the heartleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant is part of the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its unique floral morphology. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in the understory of forests.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
