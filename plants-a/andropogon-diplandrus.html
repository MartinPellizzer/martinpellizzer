Andropogon diplandrus

Andropogon diplandrus, commonly known as tallgrass bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that turn bronze in the fall. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Andropogon diplandrus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
