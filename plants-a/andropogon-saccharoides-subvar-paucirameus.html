Andropogon saccharoides subvar. paucirameus

Andropogon saccharoides subvar. paucirameus, commonly known as narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.

This subspecies is adapted to full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant.
