Atriplex hybrida

Atriplex hybrida, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are adapted to retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It is often found in saline soils and is well-suited for reclaiming degraded lands.

Atriplex hybrida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide forage for wildlife.
