Artocarpus ponga

Artocarpus ponga, commonly known as the Ponga or Pongapong tree, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands, particularly New Zealand.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces a unique, fleshy fruit that is typically green when unripe and turns yellow when mature. Its botanical features include a fibrous, aromatic bark and a complex root system adapted to coastal and wetland environments.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife.
