Anagallis parviflora var. typica

Anagallis parviflora var. typica, commonly known as the lesser mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with hairy stems and leaves that are oval to lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
