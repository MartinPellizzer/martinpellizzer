Angraecum kirkii

Angraecum kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's angraecum, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescence that bears numerous white, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on cliffs or rocky outcrops. Its flowers are pollinated by hawkmoths, which are attracted by their nectar and the specific shape of the petals.

Angraecum kirkii is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its beauty and rarity.
