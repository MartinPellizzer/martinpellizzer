Andaca

Andaca, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Andaca thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking foliage.
