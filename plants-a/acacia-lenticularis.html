Acacia lenticularis

Acacia lenticularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and bipinnate leaves that are narrow and feather-like. The plant produces yellow floral spikes during the spring and summer months, which are composed of numerous small, pea-like flowers. Acacia lenticularis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought resistance and a deep root system.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
