Aster lusitanicus

Aster lusitanicus, commonly known as the Lusitanian aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It features lance-shaped leaves and produces purple to blue flowers in the autumn.

Aster lusitanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
