Acer pictum

Acer pictum, commonly known as the painted maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking red and yellow foliage, which becomes particularly vibrant in the fall. The tree's leaves are palmately lobed, with a serrated edge and a distinctive reddish stem. It typically grows to a height of 20 to 30 feet and prefers well-drained soils in full sun.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming more fissured with age.
