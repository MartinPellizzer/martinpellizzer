Acanthura mattogrossensis

Acanthura mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian thornbush, is a shrub native to the Cerrado biome of Brazil.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which attract various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Acanthura mattogrossensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
