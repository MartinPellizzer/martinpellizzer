Anemone delavayi var. oligocarpa

Anemone delavayi var. oligocarpa, commonly known as the Tibetan anemone, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It features a short, fibrous root system and basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, white to pinkish flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to high elevation conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
