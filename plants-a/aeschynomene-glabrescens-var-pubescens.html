Aeschynomene glabrescens var. pubescens

Aeschynomene glabrescens var. pubescens, commonly known as hairy lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It has a deep root system that helps it thrive in poor soils and is often used for soil stabilization.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for erosion control and as a forage plant in certain regions.
