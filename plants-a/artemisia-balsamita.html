Artemisia balsamita

Artemisia balsamita, commonly known as golden root or balsam weed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant contains volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its medicinal properties. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in prairies and disturbed soils.

Its historical use in herbal medicine includes applications for digestive and respiratory ailments.
