Allium tristylum var. versicolor

Allium tristylum var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces upright flowering stems that bear small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. Its botanical features include a bulbous root system and a distinctive garlic-like aroma.
