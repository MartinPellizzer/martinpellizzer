Aster leucelene

Aster leucelene, commonly known as white wood aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage, and it produces white to pale purple flowers in the fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Aster leucelene thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
