Attalea wallisii

Attalea wallisii, commonly known as the Brazilian palm or the buriti palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that develop into clusters of bright orange fruits, which are edible and used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are also used for thatching and weaving, highlighting its ecological and cultural significance.

The plant thrives in wetland and riparian habitats, making it an important component of tropical ecosystems.
