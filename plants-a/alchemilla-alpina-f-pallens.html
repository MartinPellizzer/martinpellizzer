Alchemilla alpina f. pallens

Alchemilla alpina f. pallens, commonly known as the pale alpine lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its silvery-white, velvety foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and alpine landscapes.
