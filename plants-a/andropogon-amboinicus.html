Andropogon amboinicus

Andropogon amboinicus, commonly known as Amboina grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in horticulture for erosion control and ornamental purposes.
