Alstroemeria nervosa

Alstroemeria nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-veined alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with lance-shaped leaves that are often marked by prominent veins. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms, which can persist for several weeks during the growing season.
