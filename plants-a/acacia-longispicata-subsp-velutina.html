Acacia longispicata subsp. velutina

Acacia longispicata subsp.

velutina, commonly known as the velvet acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, glandular foliage that gives it a velvety texture.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which attract a variety of pollinators. Its seed pods are woody and contain edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisine.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
