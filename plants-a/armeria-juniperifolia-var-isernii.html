Armeria juniperifolia var. isernii

Armeria juniperifolia var. isernii, commonly known as the Isern's silver thrift, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant with dense clusters of small, silver-gray leaves.

It produces showy, pinkish-white flowers in spring, which are arranged in compact, terminal clusters. Native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, this variety thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun. Its foliage is covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
