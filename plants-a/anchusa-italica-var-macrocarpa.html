Anchusa italica var. macrocarpa

Anchusa italica var. macrocarpa, commonly known as Italian bugloss, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in soft, gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy, cobalt-blue to violet flowers in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
