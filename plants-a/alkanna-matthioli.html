Alkanna matthioli

Alkanna matthioli, commonly known as the red sandalwood or Indian sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive, reddish-brown seed pods. Its roots and stems contain a resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a dye.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
