Alchemilla flabellata var. genuina

Alchemilla flabellata var. genuina, commonly known as the golden alumroot, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features basal rosettes of soft, silver-gray foliage that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are borne on slender, upright stems. Its leaves are alternate and have a distinctive, fan-shaped appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
