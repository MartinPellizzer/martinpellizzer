Astragalus nicolaii

Astragalus nicolaii, commonly known as the Nicolarus milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Astragalus nicolaii is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
