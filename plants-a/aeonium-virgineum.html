Aeonium virgineum

Aeonium virgineum, commonly known as the Tree Aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or purple edges. The plant produces tall, branched flowering stalks that bear yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is a member of the Crassulaceae family and is well adapted to arid environments.

Aeonium virgineum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
