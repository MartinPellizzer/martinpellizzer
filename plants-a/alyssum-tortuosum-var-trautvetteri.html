Alyssum tortuosum var. trautvetteri

Alyssum tortuosum var. trautvetteri, commonly known as the Trautvetter's alpine alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
