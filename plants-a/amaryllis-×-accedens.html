Amaryllis × accedens

Amaryllis × accedens, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from crosses between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis papilio. The plant is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. It typically produces strap-like, sword-shaped leaves that are broad and strap-like in appearance.

This hybrid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-draining soil with adequate sunlight.
