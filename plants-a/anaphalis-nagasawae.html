Anaphalis nagasawae

Anaphalis nagasawae, commonly known as the Japanese frostflower, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique, frost-like appearance due to the fine, silky hairs covering its flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Anaphalis nagasawae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
