Acacia melanoceroides

Acacia melanoceroides, commonly known as the black wattle, is a species of acacia native to eastern Australia.

It is a tall, spreading shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and compound leaves with multiple small, linear leaflets. Its flowers are yellow and appear in globular clusters, followed by long, flat pods that contain the seeds.

Acacia melanoceroides is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and inland areas.
