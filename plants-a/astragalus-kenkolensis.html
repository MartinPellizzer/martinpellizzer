Astragalus kenkolensis

Astragalus kenkolensis, commonly known as the Kenkol milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance is significant in its native habitats.
