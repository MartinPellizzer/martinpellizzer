Anthurium incomptum

Anthurium incomptum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are inflorescences that resemble a spike of flowers, often in shades of red or yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium incomptum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
