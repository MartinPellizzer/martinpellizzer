Astragalus schirkuhicus

Astragalus schirkuhicus, commonly known as the Schirkuhica milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Astragalus schirkuhicus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
