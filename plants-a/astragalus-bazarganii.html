Astragalus bazarganii

Astragalus bazarganii, commonly known as Bazargani milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

Astragalus bazarganii is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
