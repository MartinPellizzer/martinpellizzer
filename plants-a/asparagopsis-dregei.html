Asparagopsis dregei

Asparagopsis dregei, commonly known as the Cape asparagus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and erect, branching stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or arid regions.

Asparagopsis dregei is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
