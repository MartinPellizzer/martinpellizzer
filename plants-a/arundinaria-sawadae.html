Arundinaria sawadae

Arundinaria sawadae, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Sawada's bamboo, is a species native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many bamboo species. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, grass-like texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Arundinaria sawadae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Japanese gardens.
