Astragalus kabadianus

Astragalus kabadianus, commonly known as the Kabadian milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its roots are known to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility, and it plays a role in local ecosystems.

Astragalus kabadianus is also of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and potential medicinal properties.
