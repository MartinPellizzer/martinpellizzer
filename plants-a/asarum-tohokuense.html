Asarum tohokuense

Asarum tohokuense, commonly known as Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, aromatic flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest floors, preferring cool and humid environments. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
