Astragalus turcomanicus var. elegans

Astragalus turcomanicus var. elegans, commonly known as the Turkestan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
