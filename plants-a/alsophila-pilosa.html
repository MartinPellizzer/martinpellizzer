Alsophila pilosa

Alsophila pilosa, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, light green fronds. The plant has a distinctive hairy appearance on its trunk, which gives it its common name. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Alsophila pilosa plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
