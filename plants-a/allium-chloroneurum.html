Allium chloroneurum

Allium chloroneurum, commonly known as the yellow nutmeg onion, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its bulbous underground stem, which is used as a vegetable in some cuisines. Its botanical name reflects its chlorophyll-rich leaves and nervous system effects in certain traditional uses.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.
