Arenaria saponarioides

Arenaria saponarioides, commonly known as the soapwort sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are often covered in a soapy substance, which gives it its common name and provides some natural protection against herbivores.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
