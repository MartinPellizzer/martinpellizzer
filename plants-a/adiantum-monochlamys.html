Adiantum monochlamys

Adiantum monochlamys, commonly known as the single-lipped maidenhair fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bioluminescent fronds that shimmer with a silvery hue. The plant features small, clustered spore cases on the underside of its leaves, typical of the Adiantum genus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique appearance and elegant structure make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
