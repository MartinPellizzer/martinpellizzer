Alseis gardneri

Alseis gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's alseis, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink, typically blooming in spring. Its fruit is a capsule that releases small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Alseis gardneri thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
