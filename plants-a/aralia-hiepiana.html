Aralia hiepiana

Aralia hiepiana, commonly known as the Vietnamese aralia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually palmately divided with a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in an umbel, which are followed by berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
