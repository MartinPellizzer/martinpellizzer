Amaranthus ulinei

Amaranthus ulinei, commonly known as the redroot amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking red or purple stems, which are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant produces small, clustered green flowers that develop into three-angled, ribbed seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy annual in many regions.

Amaranthus ulinei is also valued for its nutritional content, as its leaves are edible and rich in protein and minerals.
