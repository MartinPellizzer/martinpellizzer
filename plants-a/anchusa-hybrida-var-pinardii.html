Anchusa hybrida var. pinardii

Anchusa hybrida var. pinardii, commonly known as the Pinard's blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, cobalt-blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
