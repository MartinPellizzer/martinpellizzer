Aspidistra fimbriata

Aspidistra fimbriata, commonly known as the Japanese evergreen or fimbriated aspidistra, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves with a distinctive white or pale midrib. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden beneath a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental indoor plant.

Aspidistra fimbriata is valued for its hardiness and ability to adapt to low-light conditions.
