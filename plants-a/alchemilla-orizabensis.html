Alchemilla orizabensis

Alchemilla orizabensis, commonly known as the Mexican lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of soft, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Alchemilla orizabensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
