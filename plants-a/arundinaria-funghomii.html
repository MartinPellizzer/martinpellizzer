Arundinaria funghomii

Arundinaria funghomii, commonly known as the bamboo grass, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that resemble bamboo, which are often used in traditional crafts. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall aesthetic and ecological role.

Arundinaria funghomii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
