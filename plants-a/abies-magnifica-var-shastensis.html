Abies magnifica var. shastensis

Abies magnifica var. shastensis, commonly known as the Shasta fir, is a subspecies of the grand fir native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green foliage, and distinctive blue-gray undersides on its needles. The tree typically grows in high-elevation forests, preferring cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its cones are cylindrical and have short, stout bracts, and it produces both male and female cones on the same tree.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a Christmas tree.
