Astragalus pycnanthus

Astragalus pycnanthus, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Astragalus pycnanthus plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
