Acer cordatum var. dimorphifolium

Acer cordatum var. dimorphifolium, commonly known as the Variegated Maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features both green and variegated leaves on the same plant. The leaves are cordate in shape, with a heart-shaped base and serrated edges, adding to its ornamental appeal. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras in the fall.

This plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and is valued for its seasonal color changes and unique leaf patterns.
