Acacia axillaris var. macrophylla

Acacia axillaris var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically long and narrow with a distinctive golden-yellow floral display. The plant produces small, fluffy pods that contain seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological systems, providing food and habitat for native wildlife.
