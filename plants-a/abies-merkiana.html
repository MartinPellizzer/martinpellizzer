Abies merkiana

Abies merkiana, commonly known as the Merkian fir, is a species of conifer native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The cones of Abies merkiana are typically pendulous, with elongated, slender scales that mature to a reddish-brown color. This species thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the native flora in its endemic range.
