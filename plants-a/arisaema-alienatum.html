Arisaema alienatum

Arisaema alienatum, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a striking member of the Araceae family native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, providing a dramatic visual presence. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a showy spathe, which adds to its ornamental appeal. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
