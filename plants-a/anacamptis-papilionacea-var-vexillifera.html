Anacamptis papilionacea var. vexillifera

Anacamptis papilionacea var. vexillifera, commonly known as the Violet Orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands, often in calcareous soils. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of fragrant, spurred flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens and natural landscapes.
