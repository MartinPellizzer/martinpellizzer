Argyrolobium purpurascens

Argyrolobium purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered burro's tail, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a striking contrast against its silver-green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
