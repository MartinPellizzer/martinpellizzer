Actinodaphne kostermansii

Actinodaphne kostermansii, commonly known as the Koster's camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.

Its ornamental qualities make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.
