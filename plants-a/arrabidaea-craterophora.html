Arrabidaea craterophora

Arrabidaea craterophora, commonly known as the African firethorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces striking orange-red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in natural habitats.
