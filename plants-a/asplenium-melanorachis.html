Asplenium melanorachis

Asplenium melanorachis, commonly known as the black-veined spleenwort, is a member of the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices and forest floors. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves with prominent black veins, giving it its distinctive appearance. Its rhizomes are creeping and help it spread across rocky substrates.

Asplenium melanorachis is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
