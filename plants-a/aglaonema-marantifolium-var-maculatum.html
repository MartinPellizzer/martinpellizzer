Aglaonema marantifolium var. maculatum

Aglaonema marantifolium var. maculatum, commonly known as the Variegated Canna, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, broad leaves with a distinctive pattern of white and green markings, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. It thrives in partial shade and requires consistently moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
