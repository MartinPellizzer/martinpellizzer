Andropogon condensatus subvar. lactiflorus

Andropogon condensatus subvar. lactiflorus, commonly known as the sweetgrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers, which are often grouped in dense, cylindrical spikes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive sweet, honey-like scent when crushed.

This subvariety is often used in traditional medicine and as a ceremonial plant by various Indigenous cultures.
