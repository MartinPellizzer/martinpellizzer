Avena pallida

Avena pallida, commonly known as the slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous white or pale florets, which are wind-pollinated. Avena pallida thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and a potential candidate for conservation efforts.
