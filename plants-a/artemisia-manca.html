Artemisia manca

Artemisia manca, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant contains volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Artemisia manca has a long history of use in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
