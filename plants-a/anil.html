Anil

Anil, commonly known as Indian mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect stem, alternate leaves, and small white or yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces pods that contain several round, black seeds. Anil is native to the Indian subcontinent and thrives in temperate climates.

It is widely cultivated for its seeds, which are used in cooking and as a source of oil.
