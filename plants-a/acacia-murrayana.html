Acacia murrayana

Acacia murrayana, commonly known as the Murray's wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife.

Acacia murrayana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
