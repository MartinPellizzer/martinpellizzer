Anathallis vasconcelosiana

Anathallis vasconcelosiana, commonly known as the Vasconcelosia spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, slender petals and a distinctive, elongated flower spike. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are pollinated by specific species of flies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
