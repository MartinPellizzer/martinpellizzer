Allium poeppigii

Allium poeppigii, commonly known as the Tibetan leek, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-like basal leaves that are often bluish-gray in color. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear umbels of small, white or pale purple flowers. It is adapted to cold, arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

Allium poeppigii is valued for its edible leaves and has traditional uses in local cuisines.
