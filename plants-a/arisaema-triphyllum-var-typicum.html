Arisaema triphyllum var. typicum

Arisaema triphyllum var. typicum, commonly known as Jack-in-the-pulpit, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a spadix enclosed by a hood-like spathe. The plant has three broadly ovate leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in deciduous forests.

The species is known for its striking appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
