Agrostis maritima var. subrepens

Agrostis maritima var. subrepens, commonly known as beach grass, is a low-growing, mat-forming grass species native to coastal regions.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is highly tolerant of salt spray and wind. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion along shorelines.

Its adaptability makes it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
