Acacia guilandinae

Acacia guilandinae, commonly known as the Guilan acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally in local medicine for their potential health benefits.

Acacia guilandinae also plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
