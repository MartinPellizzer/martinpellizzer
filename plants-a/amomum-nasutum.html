Amomum nasutum

Amomum nasutum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cardamom, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into dark brown, three-lobed fruits. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Amomum nasutum is typically grown in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
