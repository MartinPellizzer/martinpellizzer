Agropyron repens f. vaillantianum

Agropyron repens f. vaillantianum, commonly known as the Vaillant's wheatgrass, is a variant of the common wheatgrass.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its creeping growth habit and rhizomatous root system. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or open habitats.

This form is notable for its adaptability and resilience, making it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
