Alchornea cuneifolia

Alchornea cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf alchornea, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in rainforests.

Alchornea cuneifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
