Aploleia

Aploleia, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that resemble those of the acacia. Aploleia species are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their showy blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
