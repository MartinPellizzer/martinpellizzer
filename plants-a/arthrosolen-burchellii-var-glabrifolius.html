Arthrosolen burchellii var. glabrifolius

Arthrosolen burchellii var. glabrifolius, commonly known as the smooth-leaved arthrosolen, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with dry, sunny conditions.
