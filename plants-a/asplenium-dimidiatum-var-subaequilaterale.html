Asplenium dimidiatum var. subaequilaterale

Asplenium dimidiatum var. subaequilaterale, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and finely divided. The plant has a delicate, feathery appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers shady, moist environments and is often found growing in forest understories.

Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
