Alpinia brevis

Alpinia brevis, commonly known as the short-bracted ginger, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant is characterized by its compact, rhizomatous growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically yellow or orange in color, arranged in spike-like inflorescences.

Alpinia brevis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
