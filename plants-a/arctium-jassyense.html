Arctium jassyense

Arctium jassyense, commonly known as the Jassy burdock, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. Its most distinctive feature is the spiky, bur-like fruit that aids in seed dispersal.

Arctium jassyense is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.
