Astragalus toquimanus

Astragalus toquimanus, commonly known as the Toquimanus milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional medicine.

However, due to habitat loss and limited study, its conservation status remains uncertain.
