Acacia hirta

Acacia hirta, commonly known as the rough wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. Acacia hirta is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife.
