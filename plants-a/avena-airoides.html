Avena airoides

Avena airoides, commonly known as the purple oat or purple grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, purple to violet flowers. The plant produces spikelets that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Avena airoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
