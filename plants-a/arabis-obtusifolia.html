Arabis obtusifolia

Arabis obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaved rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with characteristic cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or coastal environments. Its leaves are broad and rounded, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
