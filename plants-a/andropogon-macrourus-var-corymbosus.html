Andropogon macrourus var. corymbosus

Andropogon macrourus var. corymbosus, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically purple to green in color, and it blooms during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common species in prairie and woodland ecosystems.

Andropogon macrourus var. corymbosus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
