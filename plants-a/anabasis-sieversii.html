Anabasis sieversii

Anabasis sieversii, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater.

Anabasis sieversii plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
