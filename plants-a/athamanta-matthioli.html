Athamanta matthioli

Athamanta matthioli, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Athamanta matthioli is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
