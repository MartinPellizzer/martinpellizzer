Aristolochia olivieri

Aristolochia olivieri, commonly known as the olive aristolochia, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, veined leaves and produces intricate, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant is characterized by its strong, woody stems and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family, known for its unique floral structures and chemical compounds.

However, it is important to note that some species within this genus contain toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
