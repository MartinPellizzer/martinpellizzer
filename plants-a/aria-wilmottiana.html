Aria wilmottiana

Aria wilmottiana, commonly known as the Wilmott's lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns.

Aria wilmottiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
