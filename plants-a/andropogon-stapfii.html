Andropogon stapfii

Andropogon stapfii, commonly known as Stapf's switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to purplish in color. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a hairy underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Stapf's switchgrass is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential as a biofuel crop due to its high biomass yield.
