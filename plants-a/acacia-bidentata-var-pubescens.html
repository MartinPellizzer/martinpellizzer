Acacia bidentata var. pubescens

Acacia bidentata var. pubescens, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are bipinnate, with small, oval leaflets that give the plant a feathery appearance. It produces yellow, globular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.
