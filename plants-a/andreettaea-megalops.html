Andreettaea megalops

Andreettaea megalops, commonly known as the giant Andreettaea, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a thick layer of wax, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its root system is adapted to anchor it in rocky soils, allowing it to thrive in challenging conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
