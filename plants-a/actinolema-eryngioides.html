Actinolema eryngioides

Actinolema eryngioides, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage and is often found growing in sandy or rocky soils near the coast. It is adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it a resilient species in harsh maritime environments.

Actinolema eryngioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
