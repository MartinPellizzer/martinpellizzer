Aconitum rogoviczii

Aconitum rogoviczii, commonly known as the Rogovitschi monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to blue bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed. Its roots contain toxic alkaloids, making it a potentially dangerous plant to handle.

Aconitum rogoviczii thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
