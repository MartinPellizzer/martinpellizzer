Astragalus masenderanus

Astragalus masenderanus, commonly known as the Turkish milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in steppes and grasslands.

Astragalus masenderanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
