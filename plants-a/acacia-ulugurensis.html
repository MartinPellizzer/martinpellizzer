Acacia ulugurensis

Acacia ulugurensis, commonly known as the Uluguru acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Uluguru Mountains in Tanzania.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry, rocky soils.

Acacia ulugurensis plays a role in local ecosystems and has traditional uses by indigenous communities.
