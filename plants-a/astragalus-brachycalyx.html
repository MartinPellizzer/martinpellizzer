Astragalus brachycalyx

Astragalus brachycalyx, commonly known as the short calyx milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to dry environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Astragalus brachycalyx is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
