Amaranthus blitoides f. scleropoides

Amaranthus blitoides f. scleropoides, commonly known as the hardy amaranth, is a variant of the amaranth genus characterized by its robust and fibrous growth habit.

This plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is known for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.

It is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and resilience.
