Anemone liangshanica var. liangshanica

Anemone liangshanica var. liangshanica, commonly known as the Liangshan anemone, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It features showy, palmate flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has finely dissected, fern-like leaves that are alternate and covered in a soft, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early-season blooms.
