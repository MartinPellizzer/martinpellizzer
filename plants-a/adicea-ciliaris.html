Adicea ciliaris

Adicea ciliaris, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy seed heads that disperse wind-borne seeds. Adicea ciliaris is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

It has no significant economic value but is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
