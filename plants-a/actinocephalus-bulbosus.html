Actinocephalus bulbosus

Actinocephalus bulbosus, commonly known as the bulbous star-pear, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bulbous stem and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
