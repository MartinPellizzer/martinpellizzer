Aerva platyphylla

Aerva platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf aerva, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes many species with showy flowers and nutritious leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves and clustered inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aerva platyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
