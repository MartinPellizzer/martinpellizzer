Adiantum cremersii

Adiantum cremersii, commonly known as the Cremers' maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive black midrib that contrasts with the bright green foliage.

Adiantum cremersii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
