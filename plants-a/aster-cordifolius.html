Aster cordifolius

Aster cordifolius, commonly known as heartleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name, and its showy purple or blue flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other familiar flowering plants.

Aster cordifolius is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can also serve as a food source for pollinators.
