Amygdalus tuzsonii

Amygdalus tuzsonii, commonly known as the Tuzson's almond, is a rare and endangered species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain hard, edible seeds, similar to common almonds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to habitat loss and overcollection, it is critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
