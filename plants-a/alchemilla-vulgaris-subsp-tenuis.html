Alchemilla vulgaris subsp. tenuis

Alchemilla vulgaris subsp.

tenuis, commonly known as narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features basal leaves that are narrow and slightly hairy.

The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its astringent properties.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its ornamental value in gardens.
