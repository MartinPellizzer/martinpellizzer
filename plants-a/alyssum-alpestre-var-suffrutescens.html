Alyssum alpestre var. suffrutescens

Alyssum alpestre var. suffrutescens, commonly known as alpine alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compact, mat-forming foliage with hairy, gray-green leaves that help it withstand harsh alpine conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine habitats.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking hardy, decorative plants.
