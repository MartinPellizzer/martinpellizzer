Astragalus bithynicus

Astragalus bithynicus, commonly known as the Bithynian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus bithynicus is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its potential medicinal properties.
