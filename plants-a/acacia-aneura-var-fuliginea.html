Acacia aneura var. fuliginea

Acacia aneura var. fuliginea, commonly known as the black wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, woody stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, coastal regions.

The species is notable for its hard, dense wood, which has been historically used for fuel and construction.
