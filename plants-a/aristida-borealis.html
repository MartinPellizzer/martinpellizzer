Aristida borealis

Aristida borealis, commonly known as Arctic feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America and Eurasia.

It thrives in cold, dry environments and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Aristida borealis plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for Arctic wildlife.
