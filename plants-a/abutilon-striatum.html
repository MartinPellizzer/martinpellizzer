Abutilon striatum

Abutilon striatum, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. Its capsule fruits contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Abutilon striatum is often found in disturbed areas and can be an invasive species in some regions.
