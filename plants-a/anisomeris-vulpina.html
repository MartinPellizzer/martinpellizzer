Anisomeris vulpina

Anisomeris vulpina, commonly known as the desert star, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with a distinctive, aromatic scent. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as succulent leaves and deep root systems to conserve water. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
