Allium angulosum var. danubiale

Allium angulosum var. danubiale, commonly known as the Danube garlic, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and umbel-shaped inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are used in traditional medicine and cuisine, valued for their pungent aroma and potential health benefits.

This variety is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.
