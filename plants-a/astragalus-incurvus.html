Astragalus incurvus

Astragalus incurvus, commonly known as the bentstem milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Astragalus incurvus is part of the Fabaceae family and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
