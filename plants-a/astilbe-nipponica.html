Astilbe nipponica

Astilbe nipponica, commonly known as Japanese astilbe, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear showy panicles of small, lace-like flowers in shades of pink, white, or red. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are slightly toothed and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Astilbe nipponica is valued for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
