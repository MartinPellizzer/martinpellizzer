Acanthophyllum gypsophiloides var. papillatum

Acanthophyllum gypsophiloides var. papillatum, commonly known as the Papillate Gypseum, is a succulent shrub native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, fleshy leaves adapted to store water. The plant produces yellow flowers during the growing season, typically in summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well-suited to harsh, dry environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
