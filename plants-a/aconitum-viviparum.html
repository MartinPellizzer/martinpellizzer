Aconitum viviparum

Aconitum viviparum, commonly known as the viviparous monkshood, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, hooded flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which are typically arranged in clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, palmate appearance.

Aconitum viviparum is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that make it highly poisonous to both humans and animals.
