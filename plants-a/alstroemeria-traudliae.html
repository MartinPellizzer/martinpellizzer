Alstroemeria traudliae

Alstroemeria traudliae, commonly known as the Traudlia alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers with six tepals. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, producing clusters of showy blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern around the stem.

Alstroemeria traudliae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
