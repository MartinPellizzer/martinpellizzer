Aristida pallens var. patula

Aristida pallens var. patula, commonly known as the silky false brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, silky hairs that cover the leaves and inflorescence. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of pale green to brownish flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, fluffy panicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and grasslands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
