Albizia acle

Albizia acle, commonly known as the white siris, is a leguminous tree native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or white flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Albizia acle is also valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses.
