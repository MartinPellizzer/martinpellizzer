Achillea anethifolia

Achillea anethifolia, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing benefits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its long-lasting flowers also make it a popular choice for dried floral arrangements.
