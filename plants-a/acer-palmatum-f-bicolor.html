Acer palmatum f. bicolor

Acer palmatum f. bicolor, commonly known as the Japanese maple with variegated leaves, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which features a mix of green, white, and pink hues. The plant is native to Japan and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and has a compact, rounded form.

This plant prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
