Abrus schimperi

Abrus schimperi, commonly known as the African snail bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It produces distinctive, showy red and black seed pods that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional practices. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often found in moist, wooded areas. Its flowers are yellow with a purple or reddish tint, adding visual interest to its habitat.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
