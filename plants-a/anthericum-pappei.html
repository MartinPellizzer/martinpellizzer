Anthericum pappei

Anthericum pappei, commonly known as the African yellow flag, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in wet or marshy environments. It is often found in riparian zones and can tolerate both full sun and partial shade.

Anthericum pappei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
