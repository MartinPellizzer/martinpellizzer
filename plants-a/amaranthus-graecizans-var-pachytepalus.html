Amaranthus graecizans var. pachytepalus

Amaranthus graecizans var. pachytepalus, commonly known as the Greek amaranth, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy inflorescences that range from red to orange in color. The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the bracts are large and often brightly colored. This variety is native to the Mediterranean region and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
