Acacia varia

Acacia varia, commonly known as the variable acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often bipinnate and covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which bloom in spring. Its seeds are enclosed in pod-like fruits that split open when mature.

Acacia varia is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
