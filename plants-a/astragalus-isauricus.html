Astragalus isauricus

Astragalus isauricus, commonly known as the Isaurian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus isauricus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
