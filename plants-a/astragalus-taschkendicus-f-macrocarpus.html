Astragalus taschkendicus f. macrocarpus

Astragalus taschkendicus f. macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-seeded milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces elliptical to ovate pods containing several kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Due to its ecological significance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
