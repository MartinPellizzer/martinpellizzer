Arundinaria phar

Arundinaria phar, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, linear leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are followed by feathery seeds dispersed by the wind. It is native to East Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience.

Arundinaria phar is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of sustainable materials.
