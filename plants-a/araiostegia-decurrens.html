Araiostegia decurrens

Araiostegia decurrens, commonly known as the creeping marsh fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to wetlands and marshy areas in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread along the ground and form dense mats. The plant features slender, arching fronds with a waxy surface that helps it thrive in waterlogged environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are typically located on the undersides of the fronds.

Araiostegia decurrens plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
