Alternanthera sessilis f. spathulifolia

Alternanthera sessilis f. spathulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf alternanthera, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant red or pink, especially when exposed to strong light. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is popular in aquariums for its ornamental value. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters at the water's surface.

It is relatively easy to care for, requiring moderate lighting and regular water changes to maintain its health and color.
