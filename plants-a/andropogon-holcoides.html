Andropogon holcoides

Andropogon holcoides, commonly known as wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-gray hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in longleaf pine ecosystems. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization.

Wiregrass plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its native habitats.
