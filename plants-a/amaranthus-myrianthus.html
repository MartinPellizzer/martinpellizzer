Amaranthus myrianthus

Amaranthus myrianthus, commonly known as the velvetleaf amaranth, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the variety. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Amaranthus myrianthus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
