Abies menziesii var. parryana

Abies menziesii var. parryana, commonly known as the Parry's fir, is a variant of the Douglas fir native to the western United States.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves. The foliage is typically bluish-green, with resinous glands on the underside of the leaves. It produces cones that are cylindrical and often hang downward, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to grow in cool, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions.
