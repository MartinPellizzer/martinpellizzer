Adonis aestivalis f. citrina

Adonis aestivalis f. citrina, commonly known as the yellow autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to southern Europe and western Asia. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and a short, stout stem that bears a single, showy flower. Its flowers are typically borne on a scape, with sepals and petals that are often yellow or orange in color.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
