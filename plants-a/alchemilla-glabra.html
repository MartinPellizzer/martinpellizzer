Alchemilla glabra

Alchemilla glabra, commonly known as lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, softly hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shaded gardens.

Alchemilla glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
