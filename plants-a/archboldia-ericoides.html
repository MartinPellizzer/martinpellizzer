Archboldia ericoides

Archboldia ericoides, commonly known as the African heather.

It is a shrub native to tropical Africa, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories or rocky outcrops.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
