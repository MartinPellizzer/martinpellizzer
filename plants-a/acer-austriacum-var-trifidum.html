Acer austriacum var. trifidum

Acer austriacum var. trifidum, commonly known as the three-lobed maple, is a variant of the European maple characterized by its distinctive trifid leaves.

The leaves are deeply lobed into three distinct segments, giving the plant its common name. It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments in temperate regions. The tree produces small, red or orange samaras as seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
