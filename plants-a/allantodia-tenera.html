Allantodia tenera

Allantodia tenera, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming during the warm season. Allantodia tenera thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.
