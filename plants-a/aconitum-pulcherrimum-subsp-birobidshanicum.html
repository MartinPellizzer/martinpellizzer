Aconitum pulcherrimum subsp. birobidshanicum

Aconitum pulcherrimum subsp.

birobidshanicum, commonly known as the Karakorum aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly the Karakorum and Himalayas.

The plant is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows at high elevations.
