Asparagus deltae

Asparagus deltae, commonly known as the Mexican asparagus or delta asparagus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, green stems and small, inconspicuous white or green flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Asparagus deltae is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
