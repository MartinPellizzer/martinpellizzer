Astragalus rattanii var. jepsonianus

Astragalus rattanii var. jepsonianus, commonly known as the Jepson's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

The flowers are often pale yellow to greenish in color and are visited by various pollinators.
