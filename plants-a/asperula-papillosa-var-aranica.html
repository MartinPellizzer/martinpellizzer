Asperula papillosa var. aranica

Asperula papillosa var. aranica, commonly known as the Alpine woodruff, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has delicate, hairy leaves and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens.

Asperula papillosa var. aranica is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
