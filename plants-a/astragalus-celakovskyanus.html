Astragalus celakovskyanus

Astragalus celakovskyanus, commonly known as Celakovsky's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, bean-like pods that contain seeds, and it plays a role in soil stabilization due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

This species is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
