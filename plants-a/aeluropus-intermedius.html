Aeluropus intermedius

Aeluropus intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and South Asia.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in saline or coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in areas with low rainfall and high salinity.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
