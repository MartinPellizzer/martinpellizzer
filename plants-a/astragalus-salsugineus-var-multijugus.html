Astragalus salsugineus var. multijugus

Astragalus salsugineus var. multijugus, commonly known as the many-lobed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is notable for its ecological resilience and is often found in semi-desert environments.
