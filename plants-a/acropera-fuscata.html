Acropera fuscata

Acropera fuscata, commonly known as the dark orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dark violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Acropera fuscata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
