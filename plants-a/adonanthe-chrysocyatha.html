Adonanthe chrysocyatha

Adonanthe chrysocyatha, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
