Adesmia dentata

Adesmia dentata, commonly known as the toothed adesmia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and arid environments. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Adesmia dentata is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
