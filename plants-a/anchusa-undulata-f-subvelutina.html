Anchusa undulata f. subvelutina

Anchusa undulata f. subvelutina, commonly known as the velvet hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, velvety foliage and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies subvelutina is distinguished by its more densely hairy leaves compared to the typical form of Anchusa undulata.
