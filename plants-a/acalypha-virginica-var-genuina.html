Acalypha virginica var. genuina

Acalypha virginica var. genuina, commonly known as the whitebeard croton, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features distinctive, palmate leaves with a bright green color and a hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
