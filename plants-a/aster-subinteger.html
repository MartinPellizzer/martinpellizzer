Aster subinteger

Aster subinteger, commonly known as the New England aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy purple to pink flowers bloom in the late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in native habitats.
