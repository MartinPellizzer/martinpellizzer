Arundinaria nanunica

Arundinaria nanunica, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, reed-like stems that grow in dense clumps, typically reaching heights of less than 1 meter. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in panicles, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. Arundinaria nanunica thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
