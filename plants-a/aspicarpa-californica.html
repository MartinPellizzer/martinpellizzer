Aspicarpa californica

Aspicarpa californica, commonly known as the California thimbleberry, is a species of shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, thimble-shaped berries. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in coastal and inland regions of California. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are white and clustered in racemes.

The berries, which are edible, ripen to a bright red color and are often used in culinary applications.
