Ancistrocarpus schrankii

Ancistrocarpus schrankii, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has compound leaves with stipules and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and it is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
