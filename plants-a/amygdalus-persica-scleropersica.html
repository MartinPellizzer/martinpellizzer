Amygdalus persica scleropersica

Amygdalus persica scleropersica, commonly known as the Persian almond or wild almond, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran and Afghanistan. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and develops small, hard fruits that contain a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The species is characterized by its woody structure and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
