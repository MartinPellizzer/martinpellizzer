Anemone pseudoaltaica

Anemone pseudoaltaica, commonly known as the Siberian anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It features a short, stout rhizome and produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with five to seven petals. The plant has palmately divided, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Anemone pseudoaltaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine in some regions.
