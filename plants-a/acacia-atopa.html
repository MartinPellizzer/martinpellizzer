Acacia atopa

Acacia atopa, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a distinctive, flaky bark that peels in strips. Acacia atopa is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in dry conditions.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important species in ecological restoration.
