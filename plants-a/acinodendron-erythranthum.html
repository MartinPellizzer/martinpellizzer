Acinodendron erythranthum

Acinodendron erythranthum, commonly known as the red-flowered acinodendron, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically red or orange in color, which bloom during the warmer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Acinodendron erythranthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its striking floral display.
