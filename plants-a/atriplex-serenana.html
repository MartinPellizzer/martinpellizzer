Atriplex serenana

Atriplex serenana, commonly known as silverleaf shadscale, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, reflective hairs that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Atriplex serenana is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert and semi-arid regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various desert wildlife species.
