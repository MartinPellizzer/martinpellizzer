Ampelopsis brevipedunculata var. vestita

Ampelopsis brevipedunculata var. vestita, commonly known as the white angel's trumpet, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive addition to gardens and natural habitats.
