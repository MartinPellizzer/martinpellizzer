Aquilegia suaveolens

Aquilegia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented columbine, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred petals that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces clusters of showy flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with finely divided foliage that adds to its ornamental appeal.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
