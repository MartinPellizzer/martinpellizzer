Achyrophorus incanus

Achyrophorus incanus, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with narrow, linear leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its unique ability to survive in harsh, drought-prone conditions.
