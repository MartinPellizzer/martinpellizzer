Alternanthera hirtula subvar. atropurpurea

Alternanthera hirtula subvar. atropurpurea, commonly known as purple heart, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to red foliage, which becomes more intense under strong light conditions. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in wetlands and marshy areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular in water gardens and pond landscapes.
