Anemone albana var. georgica

Anemone albana var. georgica, commonly known as the Georgian white anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
