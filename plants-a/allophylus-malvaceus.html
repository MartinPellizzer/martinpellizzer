Allophylus malvaceus

Allophylus malvaceus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a velvety texture. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with five petals, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Allophylus malvaceus is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some areas.
