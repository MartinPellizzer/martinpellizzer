Albizia petersiana

Albizia petersiana, commonly known as the African shrub or African wisteria, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually pinnate with 10-20 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in racemes. It has a shallow root system and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Albizia petersiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
