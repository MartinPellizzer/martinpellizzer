Astragalus sintenisii

Astragalus sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenis' milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus sintenisii is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
