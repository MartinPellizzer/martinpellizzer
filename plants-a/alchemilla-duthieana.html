Alchemilla duthieana

Alchemilla duthieana, commonly known as Duthie's lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the Scottish Highlands.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Alchemilla duthieana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
