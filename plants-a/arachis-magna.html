Arachis magna

Arachis magna, commonly known as the giant groundnut, is a species of legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, edible seeds that are encased in a fibrous pod. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, while its flowers are usually yellow or orange in color.

Arachis magna is valued for its nutritional content and has been studied for potential agricultural and medicinal uses.
