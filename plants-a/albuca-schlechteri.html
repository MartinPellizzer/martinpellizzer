Albuca schlechteri

Albuca schlechteri, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-like, grass-like leaves that are usually blue-green in color. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance make it a favored plant among gardeners.
