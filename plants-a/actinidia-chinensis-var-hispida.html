Actinidia chinensis var. hispida

Actinidia chinensis var. hispida, commonly known as the Chinese gooseberry, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tangy flavor. Its hairy stems and foliage give it a distinctive appearance, and it is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

This species is also valued for its role in the development of modern cultivated kiwifruit varieties.
