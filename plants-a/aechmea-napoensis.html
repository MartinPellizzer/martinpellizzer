Aechmea napoensis

Its leaves are typically broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Aechmea napoensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of tropical forests.

Morphology

Aechmea napoensis has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, strap-like, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a waxy, dark green surface and a silvery underside.

Its inflorescence is a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, with small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and red, blooming during the dry season and pollinated by birds and insects.
