Alstroemeria pelegrina var. flore-albo

Alstroemeria pelegrina var. flore-albo, commonly known as the white-flowered alstroemeria, is a striking perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

Alstroemeria pelegrina var. flore-albo is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
