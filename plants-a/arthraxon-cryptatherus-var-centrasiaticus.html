Arthraxon cryptatherus var. centrasiaticus

Arthraxon cryptatherus var. centrasiaticus, commonly known as the Central Asian arrowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and grasslands. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
