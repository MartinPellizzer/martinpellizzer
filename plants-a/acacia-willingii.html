Acacia willingii

Acacia willingii, commonly known as the black wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in summer, followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Acacia willingii is also valued for its hard, heavy wood, which has been historically used in construction and furniture-making.
