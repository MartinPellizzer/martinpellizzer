Amorphophallus bequaertii

Amorphophallus bequaertii, commonly known as the Bequaert's giant arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces a large, edible tuberous root that is used in various culinary applications. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
