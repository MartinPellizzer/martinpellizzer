Acaena macrocephala var. caputmedusae

Acaena macrocephala var. caputmedusae, commonly known as the "Medusa's Head" or "Cape Spurge," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
