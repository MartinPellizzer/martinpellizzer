Abutilon spicatum

Abutilon spicatum, commonly known as the spiked abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its scientific name refers to the spiky appearance of its flower clusters, which are often arranged in dense inflorescences.

Abutilon spicatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
