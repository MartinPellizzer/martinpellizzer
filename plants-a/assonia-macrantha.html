Assonia macrantha

Assonia macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered assonia, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a shrub native to southern Africa, characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. Assonia macrantha thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
