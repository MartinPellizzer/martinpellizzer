Apium petroselinum var. vulgare

Apium petroselinum var. vulgare, commonly known as parsley, is an herbaceous biennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and southwestern Asia.

It features a taproot that is often harvested for its aromatic, edible leaves and stems. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are used in cooking and traditional medicine. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Parsley is known for its high vitamin C content and its role in both culinary and horticultural practices.
