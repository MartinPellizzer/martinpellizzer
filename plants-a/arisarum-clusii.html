Arisarum clusii

Arisarum clusii, commonly known as the Chinese arum, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of China and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves and a spatulate inflorescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by a colorful, leaf-like bract. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Arisarum clusii is also noted for its potential medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
