Aster menziesii

Aster menziesii, commonly known as the Menzies' aster, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and stems that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Aster menziesii is an important nectar source for pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
