Arabis austiniae

Arabis austiniae, commonly known as Austin's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the central Texas region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. This perennial herb typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant features basal leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers in early spring.

It is often found in limestone outcrops and is a symbol of the Edwards Plateau ecosystem.
