Acaena leptacantha var. longissima

Acaena leptacantha var. longissima, commonly known as the long-stemmed spinney, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its spiny, prickly stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces spherical, fuzzy fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
