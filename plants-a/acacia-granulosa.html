Acacia granulosa

Acacia granulosa, commonly known as the rough wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional Aboriginal practices. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
