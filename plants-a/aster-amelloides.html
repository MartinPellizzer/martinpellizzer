Aster amelloides

Aster amelloides, commonly known as the white wood aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland areas and produces showy, white to pale blue flower heads that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the plant can reach heights of up to 2 feet.

Aster amelloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
