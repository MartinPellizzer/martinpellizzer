Atrichantha gemmifera

Atrichantha gemmifera, commonly known as the gemmiferous atrichantha, is a species of flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
