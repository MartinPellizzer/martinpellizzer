Allamanda puberula var. gardneri

Allamanda puberula var. gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's allamanda, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, ovate leaves and produces clusters of showy, orange-yellow flowers. The plant is a vigorous climber, often used in ornamental gardening for its striking blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

The flowers are typically fragrant and bloom during the warmer months of the year.
