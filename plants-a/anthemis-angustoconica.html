Anthemis angustoconica

Anthemis angustoconica, commonly known as narrow-coned chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with a central disk, and its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
