Arabis albida f. racemopuberula

Arabis albida f. racemopuberula, commonly known as the white rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and features basal leaves that are oblanceolate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are typically arranged in racemes, giving it a delicate and ornamental appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

This variety is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its specific epithet "racemopuberula.".
