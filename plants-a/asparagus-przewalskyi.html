Asparagus przewalskyi

Asparagus przewalskyi, commonly known as Chinese asparagus, is a perennial flowering plant native to China and Russia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, inconspicuous white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed raw or cooked. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value and edible qualities make it a popular choice for gardens and culinary use.
