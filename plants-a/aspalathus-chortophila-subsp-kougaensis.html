Aspalathus chortophila subsp. kougaensis

Aspalathus chortophila subsp.

kougaensis, commonly known as the Kouga bushveld cycad, is a unique cycad species native to South Africa. It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves.

The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the semi-arid climate of the Kouga region.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
