Astragalus horizontalis

Astragalus horizontalis, commonly known as the flatpea or horizontal milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus horizontalis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
