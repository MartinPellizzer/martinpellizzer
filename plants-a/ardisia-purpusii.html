Ardisia purpusii

Ardisia purpusii, commonly known as the Japanese beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, clustered berries that turn a striking purple-red color in the fall. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, white flowers in the spring. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, posing a threat to native ecosystems.
