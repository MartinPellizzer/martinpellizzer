Antirrhinum tortuosum var. faurei

Antirrhinum tortuosum var. faurei, commonly known as the twisted snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, twisted floral bracts and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, providing a textured appearance.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
