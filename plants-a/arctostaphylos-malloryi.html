Arctostaphylos malloryi

Arctostaphylos malloryi, commonly known as the Mallory manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with leathery, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, which are usually pink to red in hue, blooming in late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

Mallory manzanita is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
