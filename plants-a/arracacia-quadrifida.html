Arracacia quadrifida

Arracacia quadrifida, commonly known as the four-leaf arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves, which are often deeply lobed and have a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and thrives in humid climates. Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Arracacia quadrifida is also valued as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage.
