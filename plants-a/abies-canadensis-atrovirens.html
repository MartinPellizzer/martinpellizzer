Abies canadensis atrovirens

Abies canadensis atrovirens, commonly known as the black fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, needle-like leaves that are slightly twisted and have a leathery texture. The tree produces cones that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters long, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and furrowed, providing a rugged appearance.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests in the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.
