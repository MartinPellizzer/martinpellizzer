Azalea lutea

Azalea lutea, commonly known as the yellow azalea, is a species of flowering plant in the Rhododendron genus.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in areas of the Appalachian Mountains. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange-red, typically blooming in late spring. Azalea lutea is an evergreen shrub that thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial shade.

Its foliage consists of leathery, dark green leaves that provide year-round structure to the landscape.
