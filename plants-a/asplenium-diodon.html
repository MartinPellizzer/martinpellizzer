Asplenium diodon

Asplenium diodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest undergrowth. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of small, spore-bearing sorus.

The leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
