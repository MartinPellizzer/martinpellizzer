Adiantum reniforme var. asarifolium

Adiantum reniforme var. asarifolium, commonly known as the asarifolium maidenhair fern, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace, with a distinctive rachis that is slightly hairy. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its leaves are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a pale green color and a soft, feathery texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
