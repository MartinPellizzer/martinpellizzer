Astragalus gyzensis

Astragalus gyzensis, commonly known as the Gyzens hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils with low moisture.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
