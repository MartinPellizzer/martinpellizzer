Avena mysorensis

Avena mysorensis, commonly known as the Mysore oat, is a species of grass native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, fertile soils and is often found in grasslands and forest margins. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

Avena mysorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for decorative purposes in gardens.
