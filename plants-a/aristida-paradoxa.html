Aristida paradoxa

Aristida paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox grass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture.

Aristida paradoxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid environments.
