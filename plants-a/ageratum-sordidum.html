Ageratum sordidum

Ageratum sordidum, commonly known as the dirty ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features hairy, narrow leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ageratum sordidum is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
