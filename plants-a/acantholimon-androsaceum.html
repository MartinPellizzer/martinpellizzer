Acantholimon androsaceum

Acantholimon androsaceum, commonly known as the Androsace mountain sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Montiaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit with small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
