Asplundia domingensis

Asplundia domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Asplundia domingensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
