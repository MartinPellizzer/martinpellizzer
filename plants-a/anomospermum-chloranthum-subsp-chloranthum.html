Anomospermum chloranthum subsp. chloranthum

Anomospermum chloranthum subsp.

chloranthum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered ghost tree, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

This species is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
