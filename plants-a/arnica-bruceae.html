Arnica bruceae

Arnica bruceae, commonly known as the Alpine tawnpuff, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Arnica bruceae thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, often growing in alpine meadows.

Its leaves are basal, rough, and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh environmental conditions of its habitat.
