Anacheilium sessiliflorum

Anacheilium sessiliflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
