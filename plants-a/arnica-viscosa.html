Arnica viscosa

Arnica viscosa, commonly known as sticky arnica, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features hairy, rhizomatous stems that grow up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with reflexed petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a sticky, glandular substance.

Arnica viscosa is often found in dry meadows and rocky soils, thriving in full sun.
