Artemisia pedemontana

Artemisia pedemontana, commonly known as the Alpine tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
