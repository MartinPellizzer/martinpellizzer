Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. scardica

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

scardica, commonly known as the Scardic woundwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in late spring to early summer, which are typically arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

This subspecies is native to parts of southern Europe and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.
