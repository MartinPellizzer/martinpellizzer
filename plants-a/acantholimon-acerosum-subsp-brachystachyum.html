Acantholimon acerosum subsp. brachystachyum

Acantholimon acerosum subsp.

brachystachyum, commonly known as the short-stemmed sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its robust, succulent stems and basal rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which bloom in spring. It is adapted to saline and sandy soils, making it a hardy species found in coastal dunes and cliffs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
