Anaphalis longifolia f. sulfureotomentosa

Anaphalis longifolia f. sulfureotomentosa, commonly known as the sulfur-tomentose mouse-ear, is a flowering plant characterized by its dense, woolly foliage.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to regions of East Asia, including China. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a spherical inflorescence. Its leaves are covered in a fine, yellowish-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and color.
