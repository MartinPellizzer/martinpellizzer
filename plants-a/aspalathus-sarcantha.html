Aspalathus sarcantha

Aspalathus sarcantha, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and orange, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Aspalathus sarcantha is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various climates.
