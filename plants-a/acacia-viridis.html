Acacia viridis

Acacia viridis, commonly known as the green acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous small leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Acacia viridis is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important species in soil enrichment.
