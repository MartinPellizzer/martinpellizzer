Arundo mambu

Arundo mambu, commonly known as the giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hollow, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 4 meters in height. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of hairs. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found near water bodies.

Arundo mambu is valued for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in erosion control and as a source of biomass.
