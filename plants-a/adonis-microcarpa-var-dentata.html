Adonis microcarpa var. dentata

Adonis microcarpa var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed adonis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which are adapted to cold and arid conditions. The plant produces striking yellow flowers in early summer, arranged in a dense, terminal inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
