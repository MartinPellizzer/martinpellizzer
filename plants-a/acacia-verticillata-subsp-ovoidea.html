Acacia verticillata subsp. ovoidea

Acacia verticillata subsp.

ovoidea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, oval-shaped leaves arranged in a whorl-like pattern.

The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which bloom from late autumn to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.
