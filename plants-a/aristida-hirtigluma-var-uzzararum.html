Aristida hirtigluma var. uzzararum

Aristida hirtigluma var. uzzararum, commonly known as the Uzzararum grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, erect stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale to reddish-brown florets, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in degraded landscapes.
