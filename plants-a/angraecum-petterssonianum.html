Angraecum petterssonianum

Angraecum petterssonianum, commonly known as the Christmas star orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a distinctive red lip, which bloom during the dry season, typically between December and February. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees or rocks without being parasitic. Its flowers emit a sweet, vanilla-like fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered endangered and is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.
