Ammi majus var. intermedium

Ammi majus var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate angel's trumpet, is a biennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow to cream in color, arranged in terminal clusters. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to self-seed.

However, it can be invasive in some regions due to its aggressive growth habit.
