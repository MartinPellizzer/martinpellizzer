Aspidium laciniatum

Aspidium laciniatum, commonly known as the cut-leaf water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a lacy appearance. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. The fronds are typically evergreen, with a creeping rhizome that helps in vegetative propagation.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and aquatic settings.
