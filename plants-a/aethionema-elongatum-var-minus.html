Aethionema elongatum var. minus

Aethionema elongatum var. minus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandthrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, succulent leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient plant suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
