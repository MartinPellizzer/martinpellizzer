Acianthera calypso

Acianthera calypso, commonly known as the Calypso orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and purple, often with a distinctive lip petal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, organic-rich substrates.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and endangered species.
