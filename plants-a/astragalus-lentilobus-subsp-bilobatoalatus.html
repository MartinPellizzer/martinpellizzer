Astragalus lentilobus subsp. bilobatoalatus

Astragalus lentilobus subsp.

bilobatoalatus, commonly known as the two-lobed milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its bilobed leaflets and the presence of small, glandular hairs on its foliage. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
