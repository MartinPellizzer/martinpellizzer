Ascocentrum semiteretifolium

Ascocentrum semiteretifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed arrowroot.

This plant is a herbaceous species native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its upright, tuberous rhizomes and lanceolate leaves. It belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
