Astragalus neolipskyanus

Astragalus neolipskyanus, commonly known as the Neolipskyan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Astragalus neolipskyanus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years, contributing to its survival in harsh conditions.
