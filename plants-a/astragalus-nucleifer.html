Astragalus nucleifer

Astragalus nucleifer, commonly known as the Indian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Astragalus nucleifer is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional systems of medicine.
