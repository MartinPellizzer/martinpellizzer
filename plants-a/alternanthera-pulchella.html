Alternanthera pulchella

Alternanthera pulchella, commonly known as the red-stemmed alternanthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or purple stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often a striking green or red. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This ornamental plant is popular in aquatic and bog gardens due to its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
