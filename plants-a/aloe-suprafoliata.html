Aloe suprafoliata

Aloe suprafoliata, commonly known as the Cape aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and fleshy, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces striking red or yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that has been used for its medicinal properties.

Aloe suprafoliata is well adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
