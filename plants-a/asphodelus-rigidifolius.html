Asphodelus rigidifolius

Asphodelus rigidifolius, commonly known as the rigid-leaved asphodel, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often rigid and upright. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.

Asphodelus rigidifolius is known for its drought tolerance and is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens for its ornamental value.
