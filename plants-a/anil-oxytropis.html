Anil oxytropis

Anil oxytropis, commonly known as the Indian oxytropis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically produces yellow or pale green flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small leaflets. Anil oxytropis is often found in rocky or arid environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
