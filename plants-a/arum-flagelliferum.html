Arum flagelliferum

Arum flagelliferum, commonly known as the rattlesnake plantain, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features a short, thick rhizome with a fibrous root system, and its leaves are typically arrow-shaped with a waxy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The spadix produces small, greenish flowers that are pollinated by insects, and the plant is noted for its ornamental value in horticulture.
