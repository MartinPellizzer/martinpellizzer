Acacia hova

Acacia hova, commonly known as the white acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and small, feathery leaves that are often clustered at the ends of the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. Acacia hova is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fuel.
