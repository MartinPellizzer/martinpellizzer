Acetosella stricta

Acetosella stricta, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Acetosella stricta is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
