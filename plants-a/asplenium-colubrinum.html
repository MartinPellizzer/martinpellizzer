Asplenium colubrinum

Asplenium colubrinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found growing on rock surfaces or in crevices. Its spores are produced in compact, stalked clusters called sorus, which are located on the underside of the leaves.

Asplenium colubrinum is a hardy species that is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
