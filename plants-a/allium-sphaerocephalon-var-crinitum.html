Allium sphaerocephalon var. crinitum

Allium sphaerocephalon var. crinitum, commonly known as the curly leek or curly garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features a cluster of narrow, curling leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in a spherical cluster at the top of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild garlic-like scent.
