Alsophila acuminata

Alsophila acuminata, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive crown of spirally arranged fronds that give it a unique appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The fronds are used in traditional medicine and basket weaving in some cultures.
