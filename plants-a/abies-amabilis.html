Abies amabilis

Abies amabilis, commonly known as the Pacific silver fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue needles and tall, slender form, often reaching heights of up to 60 meters. The tree produces cones that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters long, containing winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thin and reddish-brown, becoming more fissured with age.

The Pacific silver fir is adapted to moist, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.
