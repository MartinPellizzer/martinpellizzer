Alsophila catillifera

Alsophila catillifera, commonly known as the cat's tail tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound fronds that resemble a cat's tail. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests. Its fronds are typically bright green and can reach lengths of up to 3 meters.

Alsophila catillifera plays a significant role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and shelter for various wildlife species.
