Allocasuarina spinosissima

Allocasuarina spinosissima, commonly known as the spinifex tree, is a hardy evergreen shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, needle-like leaves that are clustered in cylindrical structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is well adapted to arid and coastal environments, thriving in sandy soils and saline conditions. It produces small, red or yellow flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The spinifex tree plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
