Anthoxanthum maderense

Anthoxanthum maderense, commonly known as the Madeiran oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Azores and Madeira Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Anthoxanthum maderense plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
