Alopecurus × winklerianus

Alopecurus × winklerianus, commonly known as the creeping bluegrass, is a hybrid grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted, clump-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Alopecurus × winklerianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in various ecological settings.
