Anthurium silvicola

Anthurium silvicola, commonly known as the forest anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the understory of humid forests in Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are inflorescences that consist of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. Anthurium silvicola thrives in low-light conditions and requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.

Its unique foliage and vibrant floral structures make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and botanical collections.
