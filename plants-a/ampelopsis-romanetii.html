Ampelopsis romanetii

Ampelopsis romanetii, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle vine, is a vigorous climbing plant native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range from yellow to orange in color, typically blooming in late summer. Its berries are dark blue to black and are edible, though they are not commonly consumed by humans.

Ampelopsis romanetii is often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
