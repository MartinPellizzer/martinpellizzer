Adenodolichos punctatus var. decumbens

Adenodolichos punctatus var. decumbens, commonly known as the creeping cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with stipules and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a prostrate growth habit, often spreading along the ground or climbing supports. Its seeds are typically oval and can be found in hairy pods that mature to a brownish color.

This species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and is sometimes cultivated as a forage crop.
