Asparagus cooperi

Asparagus cooperi, commonly known as Cooper's asparagus, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, fern-like foliage and its ability to form compact, bushy clumps. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its berries are red and edible, often attracting birds and other wildlife.

Asparagus cooperi thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.
