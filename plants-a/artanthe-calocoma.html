Artanthe calocoma

Artanthe calocoma, commonly known as the purple artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Artanthe calocoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
