Acrocephalus robertii

Acrocephalus robertii, commonly known as the Robert's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in spikelets. It is often found in shallow water or damp areas, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.

Acrocephalus robertii plays a role in supporting local insect populations and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
