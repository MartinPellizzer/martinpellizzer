Asplenium raddianum

Asplenium raddianum, commonly known as the Raddian spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It typically grows on trees or rocks in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and have a leathery texture. Its rhizomes are short and help anchor it to its substrate while absorbing moisture and nutrients.

Asplenium raddianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
