Aconitum lobulatum

Aconitum lobulatum, commonly known as the lobed monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant features palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum lobulatum is known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
