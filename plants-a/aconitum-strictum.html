Aconitum strictum

Aconitum strictum, commonly known as the tall monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, doubly serrated leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. Aconitum strictum grows up to 1.5 meters tall and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is also known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
