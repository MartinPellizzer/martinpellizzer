Assonia guazumifolia

Assonia guazumifolia, commonly known as the Guazumifolia Assonia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that resemble those of the guazumal tree, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure for optimal growth.
