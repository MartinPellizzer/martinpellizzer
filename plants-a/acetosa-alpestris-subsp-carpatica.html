Acetosa alpestris subsp. carpatica

Acetosa alpestris subsp.

carpatica, commonly known as Carpathian sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. It is valued for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor due to the presence of oxalic acid.

This subspecies is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
