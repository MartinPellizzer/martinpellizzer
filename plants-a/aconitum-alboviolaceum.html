Aconitum alboviolaceum

Aconitum alboviolaceum, commonly known as the white-violet aconite, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pale violet to white flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and cool, moist climates. Its leaves are palmately compound, with finely divided lobes, and it produces tall, sparsely branched stems.

Aconitum alboviolaceum is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
