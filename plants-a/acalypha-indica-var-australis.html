Acalypha indica var. australis

Acalypha indica var. australis, commonly known as the Australian hibiscus or velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia and Australia.

It features palmate leaves with a velvety texture, often covered in fine hairs, and produces showy, bisexual flowers in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant floral displays.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
