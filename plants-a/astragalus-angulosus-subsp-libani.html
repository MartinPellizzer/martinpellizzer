Astragalus angulosus subsp. libani

Astragalus angulosus subsp.

libani, commonly known as the Lebanon angular milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of the Near East. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes.

The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
