Arecastrum romanzoffianum var. botryophorum

Arecastrum romanzoffianum var. botryophorum, commonly known as the clustered areca palm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its clustered growth habit, with multiple stems emerging from a single base. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in dense inflorescences, which are followed by three-lobed, leathery fruits. Its foliage consists of long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in ornamental landscaping.

This species is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
