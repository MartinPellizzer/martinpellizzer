Aster lacerus

Aster lacerus, commonly known as the rough aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are rough and hairy, giving it its common name, while its flowers are usually purple or blue.

Aster lacerus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
