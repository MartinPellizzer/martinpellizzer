Anthericum crispum

Anthericum crispum, commonly known as the curly fern, is a species of flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, curled leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, arranged in a spadix surrounded by a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Anthericum crispum is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
