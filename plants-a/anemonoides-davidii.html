Anemonoides davidii

Anemonoides davidii, commonly known as the David's anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive center that resembles a crown, surrounded by serrated, palmate leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and thrives in partial to full shade with well-drained soil. Its foliage is often a deep green, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Anemonoides davidii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
