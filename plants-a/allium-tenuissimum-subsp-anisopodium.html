Allium tenuissimum subsp. anisopodium

Allium tenuissimum subsp.

anisopodium, commonly known as the slender leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and southern Europe. It features slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in a dense, umbel-like cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides.

Botanically, it is characterized by its aromatic, bulbous base and its role as a member of the Allium genus, which includes garlic and onions.
