Acacia houstonii

Acacia houstonii, commonly known as Houston acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by long, thin pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Acacia houstonii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.
