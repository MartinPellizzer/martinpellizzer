Adiantum haenkeanum

Adiantum haenkeanum, commonly known as the golden maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble feathers. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to rocky substrates or soil.

Adiantum haenkeanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
