Alantsilodendron villosum

Alantsilodendron villosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf tree, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its hairy, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are typically alternate and ovate, with a slightly serrated margin.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive yellowish-green color that attracts various pollinators.
