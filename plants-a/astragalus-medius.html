Astragalus medius

Astragalus medius, commonly known as the middle milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, often found in grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or reddish-pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Astragalus medius is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
