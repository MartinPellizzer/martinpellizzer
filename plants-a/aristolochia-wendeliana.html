Aristolochia wendeliana

Aristolochia wendeliana, commonly known as the Brazilian pipevine, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, intricate flowers that resemble a pipe, hence its common name. The plant produces showy, dark purple to brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, shiny seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
