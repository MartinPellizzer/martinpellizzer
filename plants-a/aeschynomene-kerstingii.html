Aeschynomene kerstingii

Aeschynomene kerstingii, commonly known as Kersting's stylo, is a perennial leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by elliptical, flat pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in forage systems due to its high nutritional value.

Aeschynomene kerstingii is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enhancing soil fertility.
