Amaryllis tenella

Amaryllis tenella, commonly known as the dwarf amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. It prefers well-drained soil and bright, indirect light to thrive.

Its compact size and elegant appearance make it a desirable addition to both gardens and containers.
