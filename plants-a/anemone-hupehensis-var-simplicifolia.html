Anemone hupehensis var. simplicifolia

Anemone hupehensis var. simplicifolia, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, single or double flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are alternate and often covered in a fine down. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators and contributes to its ornamental value in horticulture.
