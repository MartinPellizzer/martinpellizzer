Ampelocissus pterisanthella

Ampelocissus pterisanthella, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by round, fleshy fruits. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and ability to climb structures.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
