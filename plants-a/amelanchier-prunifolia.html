Amelanchier prunifolia

Amelanchier prunifolia, commonly known as the common serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, turning from red to dark purple. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

Amelanchier prunifolia is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full sunlight.
