Annona prevostiae

Annona prevostiae, commonly known as the prevost's custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers with five white to pale green petals and a distinctive, fleshy fruit that is similar in appearance to a custard apple. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and creamy pulp containing numerous small, hard seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in its native regions.
