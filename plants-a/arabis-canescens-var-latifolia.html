Arabis canescens var. latifolia

Arabis canescens var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, fleshy, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.

The species is also known for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
