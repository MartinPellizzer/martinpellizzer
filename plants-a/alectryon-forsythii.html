Alectryon forsythii

Alectryon forsythii, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Alectryon forsythii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in Chinese medicine.
