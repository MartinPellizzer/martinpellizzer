Arachis glabrata f. minor

Arachis glabrata f. minor, commonly known as the smooth peanut, is a botanical variant of the peanut plant.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless pods and smaller seeds compared to the typical peanut. This plant is native to South America and thrives in warm, tropical climates. It is primarily cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and oil.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in agricultural and genetic research.
