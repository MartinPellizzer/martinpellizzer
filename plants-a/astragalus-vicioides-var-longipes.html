Astragalus vicioides var. longipes

Astragalus vicioides var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical, woody pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and shrub-steppe habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
