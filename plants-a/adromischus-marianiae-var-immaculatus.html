Adromischus marianiae var. immaculatus

Adromischus marianiae var. immaculatus, commonly known as the White-veined Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with prominent white veins. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and unique foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
