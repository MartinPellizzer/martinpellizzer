Anoectochilus brevilabris var. gigantea

Anoectochilus brevilabris var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant toothed orchid, is a unique orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves and striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive toothed lip that sets it apart from other orchid varieties.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
