Astragalus sewersowii

Astragalus sewersowii, commonly known as Sewersow's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
