Aristolochia colossifolia

Aristolochia colossifolia, commonly known as the giant Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces striking, hooded flowers that are typically purple or greenish in color, which are unique in shape and structure. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains numerous small, dark seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in warm climates.
