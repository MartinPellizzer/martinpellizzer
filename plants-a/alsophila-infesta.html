Alsophila infesta

Alsophila infesta, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its massive fronds, which are used in traditional crafts and as ornamental plants.

The spores are released from the sporangia located on the underside of the fronds, facilitating its reproduction in suitable habitats.
