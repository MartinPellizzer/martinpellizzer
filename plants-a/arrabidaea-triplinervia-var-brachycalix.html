Arrabidaea triplinervia var. brachycalix

Arrabidaea triplinervia var. brachycalix, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its dense, aromatic wood and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are woody capsules containing several winged seeds.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an endangered species in its native habitat.
