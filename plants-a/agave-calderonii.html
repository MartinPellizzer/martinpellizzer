Agave calderonii

Agave calderonii, commonly known as the Mexican fireplant, is a striking succulent native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, bluish-green leaves with white teeth along the edges, which are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear yellowish-green flowers, which are often pollinated by bees and other insects. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy leaves and stems.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and collections.
