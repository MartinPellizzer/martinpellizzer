Amsonia ludoviciana

Amsonia ludoviciana, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that produce clusters of small, sky-blue flowers in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are generally gray-green in color, providing a striking contrast to the blue blossoms. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Amsonia ludoviciana is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
