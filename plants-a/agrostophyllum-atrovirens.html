Agrostophyllum atrovirens

Agrostophyllum atrovirens, commonly known as the dark green feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, dark green blades that form dense clumps, often growing in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stems. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.

Agrostophyllum atrovirens plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas and supports local biodiversity.
