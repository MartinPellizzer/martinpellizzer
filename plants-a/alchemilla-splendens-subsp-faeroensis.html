Alchemilla splendens subsp. faeroensis

Alchemilla splendens subsp.

faeroensis, commonly known as the Faeroese alkanet, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Faroe Islands. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its silver-green, velvety foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats. Its leaves are covered in fine, glandular hairs that secrete a sticky substance, which can be used as a natural dye.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
