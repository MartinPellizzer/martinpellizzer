Acacia fimbriata

Acacia fimbriata, commonly known as the fringe acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and floral display.
