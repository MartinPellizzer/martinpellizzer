Astragalus submitis var. charsanicus

Astragalus submitis var. charsanicus, commonly known as the Afghan milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.

This species is of interest in ecological and medicinal studies due to its adaptability and potential pharmacological properties.
