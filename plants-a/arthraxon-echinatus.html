Arthraxon echinatus

Arthraxon echinatus, commonly known as the spiny grass or rough fescue, is a perennial grass species native to southern and eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects and as a forage plant for livestock.
