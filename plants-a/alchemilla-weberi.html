Alchemilla weberi

Alchemilla weberi, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its silvery-white, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Alchemilla weberi is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
