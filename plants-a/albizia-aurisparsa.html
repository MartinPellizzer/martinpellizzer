Albizia aurisparsa

Albizia aurisparsa, commonly known as the golden wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to golden in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Albizia aurisparsa thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
