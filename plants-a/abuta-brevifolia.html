Abuta brevifolia

Abuta brevifolia, commonly known as the African butterbean, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several kidney-shaped seeds.

Abuta brevifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
