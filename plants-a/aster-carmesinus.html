Aster carmesinus

Aster carmesinus, commonly known as the Carmine Aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its showy purple to pink flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
