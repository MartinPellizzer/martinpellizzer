Aconitum japonicum subsp. napiforme

Aconitum japonicum subsp.

napiforme, commonly known as Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia. It features showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has palmately divided leaves and produces distinctive, helmet-shaped seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its toxic alkaloids, which are present in all parts of the plant.
