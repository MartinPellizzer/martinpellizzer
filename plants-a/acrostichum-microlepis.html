Acrostichum microlepis

Acrostichum microlepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf brake, is a member of the fern family (Polypodiaceae).

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright fronds with a dense arrangement of small, linear leaves. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Acrostichum microlepis is characterized by its delicate, finely divided foliage and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
