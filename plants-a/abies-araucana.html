Abies araucana

Abies araucana, commonly known as the Chilean fir, is a coniferous tree native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and distinctive triangular-shaped cones. The tree has dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
