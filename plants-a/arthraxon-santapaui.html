Arthraxon santapaui

Arthraxon santapaui, commonly known as the Santapau grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Arthraxon santapaui is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
