Abutilon avicennae var. violaceum

Abutilon avicennae var. violaceum, commonly known as the violet abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet, blooming throughout the warm seasons. Its botanical features include small, woolly leaves and a fibrous root system, which help it adapt to various soil conditions.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
