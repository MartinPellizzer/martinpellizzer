Amphymenium pubescens

Amphymenium pubescens, commonly known as the hairy swamp grass, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that helps reduce water loss in moist habitats. The plant typically grows in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and marshes. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often arranged in a loose panicle.

Amphymenium pubescens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
