Armeria pseudarmeria subsp. carratracensis

Armeria pseudarmeria subsp.

carratracensis, commonly known as the Carratracense thrift, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is a low-growing, mat-forming plant with dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers.

The species is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and compact inflorescences that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal or subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
