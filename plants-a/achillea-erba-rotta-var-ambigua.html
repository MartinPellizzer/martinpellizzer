Achillea erba-rotta var. ambigua

Achillea erba-rotta var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and flat, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which range from white to pale yellow, bloom from late spring to early autumn.

It is often used in garden settings for its ornamental value and has a long history of traditional medicinal use.
