Antrophyum bivittatum

Antrophyum bivittatum, commonly known as the two-striped antrophyum, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that give it a dramatic, palm-like appearance. The plant produces impressive, spadix-like inflorescences that are often surrounded by colorful bracts, enhancing its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth rate, it is a prized specimen in botanical gardens and private collections.
