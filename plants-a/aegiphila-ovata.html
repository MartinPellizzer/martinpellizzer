Aegiphila ovata

Aegiphila ovata, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.

Morphology

Aegiphila ovata has ovate, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a leathery texture and a smooth margin.

The stem is upright, with a smooth, reddish-brown bark, and exhibits a branching growth pattern. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, with white petals and yellow stamens, blooming in spring and being pollinated by insects. The fruit is a capsule that releases small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The roots are fibrous and help anchor the plant in sandy or rocky soils.
