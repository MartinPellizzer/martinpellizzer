Astragalus tribuloides var. paucijugus

Astragalus tribuloides var. paucijugus, commonly known as the few-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to low moisture conditions. The plant has compound leaves with few leaflets, and its small, yellow flowers are clustered in racemes. It produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
