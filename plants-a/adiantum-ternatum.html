Adiantum ternatum

Adiantum ternatum, commonly known as the fan-leaf maidenhair, is a species of fern belonging to the Adiantaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fan-shaped fronds that are finely divided and often have a silvery sheen. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's fronds are typically biennial, with the fertile fronds appearing in the second year.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and shaded outdoor landscapes.
