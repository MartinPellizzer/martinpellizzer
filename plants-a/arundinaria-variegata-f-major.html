Arundinaria variegata f. major

Arundinaria variegata f. major, commonly known as the variegated bamboo, is a decorative bamboo species characterized by its striking variegated foliage.

It features slender, upright culms with a light green to white variegation pattern along the leaf blades. This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

The plant is relatively low maintenance, requiring minimal pruning and care once established.
