Adamsia reptans

Adamsia reptans, commonly known as the creeping sea whip, is a species of soft coral found in shallow coastal waters.

It has a delicate, branching structure with a gelatinous appearance, and its coloration can range from pale pink to reddish hues. The plant thrives in warm, tropical marine environments and is often found attached to rocky substrates. It plays a role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Due to its fragile nature, it is sensitive to environmental changes and pollution.
