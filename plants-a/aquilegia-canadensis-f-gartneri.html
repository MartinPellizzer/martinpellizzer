Aquilegia canadensis f. gartneri

Aquilegia canadensis f. gartneri, commonly known as the Gartner's Canadian columbine, is a hybrid form of the Canadian columbine, characterized by its distinctive, spurred petals.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States, typically found in woodland and moist meadow habitats. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with deeply lobed segments, and it has a fibrous root system.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability in gardens.
