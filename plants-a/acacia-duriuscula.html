Acacia duriuscula

Acacia duriuscula, commonly known as the hard-shelled acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and phyllodes that are narrow and rigid. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought.

The species is often used in traditional medicine and for soil stabilization in degraded landscapes.
