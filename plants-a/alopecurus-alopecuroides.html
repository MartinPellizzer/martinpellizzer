Alopecurus alopecuroides

Alopecurus alopecuroides, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces tiny, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it a common pioneer species in disturbed areas.
