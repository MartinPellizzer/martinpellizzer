Asiphonia

Asiphonia, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that typically feature a central disk surrounded by ray florets. These plants are native to various regions in the Southern Hemisphere, including South Africa, Australia, and South America. Asiphonia species are often found growing in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in arid or semi-arid environments.

Their botanical features make them popular choices for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to their drought tolerance and ornamental value.
