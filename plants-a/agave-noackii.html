Agave noackii

Agave noackii, commonly known as the Chihuahuan agave, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces an inflorescence of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Agave noackii is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Its rosette growth habit and ability to store water in its leaves make it well-suited for xerophytic conditions.
