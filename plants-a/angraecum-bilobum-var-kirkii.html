Angraecum bilobum var. kirkii

Angraecum bilobum var. kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's star orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and large, showy white flowers with intricate patterns. The plant produces nectar-rich spathes that attract specific nocturnal pollinators, such as hawkmoths. Its leaves are typically elongated and arranged in a rosette, allowing it to capture moisture from the air.

This variety is highly valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
