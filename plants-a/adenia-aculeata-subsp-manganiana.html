Adenia aculeata subsp. manganiana

Adenia aculeata subsp.

manganiana, commonly known as the Manganian climbing adenium, is a shrub or climbing plant native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive scent, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, typically in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
