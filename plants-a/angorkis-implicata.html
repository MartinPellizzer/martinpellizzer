Angorkis implicata

Angorkis implicata, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies add both aesthetic and ecological value to gardens.

However, it is relatively rare in cultivation and requires specific care to flourish.
