Alyssum tortuosum f. obovatocarpum

Alyssum tortuosum f. obovatocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed alpine alyssum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces small, elongated fruits. This variety is native to mountainous regions of Europe and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
