Achillea wilsoniana

Achillea wilsoniana, commonly known as Wilson's yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It features finely divided, silver-green leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters.

Wilson's yarrow is often used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
