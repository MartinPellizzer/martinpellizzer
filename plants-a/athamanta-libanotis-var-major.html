Athamanta libanotis var. major

Athamanta libanotis var. major, commonly known as the Lebanon bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
