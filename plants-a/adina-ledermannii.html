Adina ledermannii

Adina ledermannii, commonly known as Ledermann's adina, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often glossy in appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the late spring and early summer. These flowers are followed by small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds.

Adina ledermannii is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, and it is often found in areas with high altitudes and cool climates.
