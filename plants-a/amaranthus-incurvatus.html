Amaranthus incurvatus

Amaranthus incurvatus, commonly known as the curly amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally twisted leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in dry, sunny areas.

Amaranthus incurvatus is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
