Aster patens var. floridanus

Aster patens var. floridanus, commonly known as the Florida aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has a compact growth habit with numerous short, stiff stems and is often used in ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
