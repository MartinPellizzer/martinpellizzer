Adamanthus synsepala

Adamanthus synsepala, commonly known as the Florida holly or white holly, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Adamanthus synsepala is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.
