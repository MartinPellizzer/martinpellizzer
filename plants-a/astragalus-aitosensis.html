Astragalus aitosensis

Astragalus aitosensis, commonly known as the Aitos milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has small, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus aitosensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
