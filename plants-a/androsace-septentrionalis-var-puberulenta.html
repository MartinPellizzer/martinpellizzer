Androsace septentrionalis var. puberulenta

Androsace septentrionalis var. puberulenta, commonly known as the northern sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the alpine regions of North America.

It typically forms dense mats of prostrate stems covered with short, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are usually rounded and covered with a fine, pubescent coating.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
