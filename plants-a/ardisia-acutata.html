Ardisia acutata

Ardisia acutata, commonly known as the sharpleaf beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves that are sharply pointed at the tips. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. These fruits are edible for birds and small mammals, aiding in seed dispersal.

Ardisia acutata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
