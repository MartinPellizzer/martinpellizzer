Acalypha spectabilis

Acalypha spectabilis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-hot cat's tail, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features showy, crimson bracts that resemble cat tails, which are actually modified leaves rather than flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with a velvety texture and a deep green color, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Acalypha spectabilis is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
