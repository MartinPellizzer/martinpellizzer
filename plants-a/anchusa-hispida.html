Anchusa hispida

Anchusa hispida, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Anchusa hispida is often found in dry, rocky soils and is used in garden settings for its ornamental value.
