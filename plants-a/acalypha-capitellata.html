Acalypha capitellata

Acalypha capitellata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or redroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features palmate leaves with a velvety texture and bright red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks due to its preference for moist soils. Its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes make it an invasive species in some areas.

The plant's unique floral structure attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
