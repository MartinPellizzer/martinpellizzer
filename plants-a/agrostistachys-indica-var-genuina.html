Agrostistachys indica var. genuina

Agrostistachys indica var. genuina, commonly known as the Indian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
