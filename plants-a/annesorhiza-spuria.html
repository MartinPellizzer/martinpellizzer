Annesorhiza spuria

Annesorhiza spuria, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has triangular stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It produces small, greenish flowers in spike-like clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Annesorhiza spuria plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
