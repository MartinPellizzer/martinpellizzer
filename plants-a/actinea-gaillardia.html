Actinea gaillardia

Actinea gaillardia, commonly known as the Indian blanket flower, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers that range from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It produces showy, composite flowers composed of ray florets and disk florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Actinea gaillardia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
