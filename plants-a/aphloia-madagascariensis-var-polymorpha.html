Aphloia madagascariensis var. polymorpha

Aphloia madagascariensis var. polymorpha, commonly known as the Madagascar hibiscus, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The flowers are pollinated by insects and birds, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.
