Anacyclus pulcher

Anacyclus pulcher, commonly known as the beautiful spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters at the top of the stems. It blooms from spring to early summer, producing small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spherical seed capsules.

Anacyclus pulcher is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
