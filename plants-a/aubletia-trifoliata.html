Aubletia trifoliata

Aubletia trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leafed aubletia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Aubletia trifoliata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid environments.
