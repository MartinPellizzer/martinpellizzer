Asperula attenuata

Asperula attenuata, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and thrives in acidic soil conditions. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant pink or white blooms.

Asperula attenuata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to garden settings.
