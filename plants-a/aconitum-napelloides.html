Aconitum napelloides

Aconitum napelloides, commonly known as the western aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has a robust root system and can grow up to 2 meters tall, with large, palmately lobed leaves. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of potent alkaloids.

Aconitum napelloides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
