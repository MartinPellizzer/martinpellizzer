Avena sterilis subsp. trichophylla

Avena sterilis subsp.

trichophylla, commonly known as the slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
