Acacia riparia var. pseudoadhaerens

Acacia riparia var. pseudoadhaerens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in stabilizing soils in degraded areas.
