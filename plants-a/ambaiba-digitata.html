Ambaiba digitata

Ambaiba digitata, commonly known as the fingerleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that resemble fingers, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are usually compound, with multiple leaflets arranged in a fan-like pattern.

Ambaiba digitata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
