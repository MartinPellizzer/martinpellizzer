Acaena sanguisorbae var. subtusglaucescens

Acaena sanguisorbae var. subtusglaucescens, commonly known as the purple sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features small, fleshy, purple-tinged leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
