Agati

Agati, commonly known as *Pithecellobium dulce*, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain sweet, fleshy seeds, often consumed as a fruit. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Agati is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
