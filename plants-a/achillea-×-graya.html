Achillea × graya

Achillea × graya, commonly known as the gray yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, flat-topped flower heads that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and prefers well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.

Achillea × graya is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory qualities.
