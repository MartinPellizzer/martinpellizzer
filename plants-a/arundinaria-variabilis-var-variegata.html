Arundinaria variabilis var. variegata

Arundinaria variabilis var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo species characterized by its distinctive leaf patterns.

This plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique coloration. The leaves exhibit a striking combination of green and white or yellow variegation, enhancing its visual appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden settings.

The plant is also valued for its resilience and ability to form dense clumps over time.
