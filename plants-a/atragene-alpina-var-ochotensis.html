Atragene alpina var. ochotensis

Atragene alpina var. ochotensis, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Siberia and northern Mongolia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with tall, slender stems bearing bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. Its flowers are typically tubular with five lobes and are pollinated by insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
