Aristolochia baseri

Aristolochia baseri, commonly known as the Indian birthwort, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a climbing vine that produces distinctive, hooded flowers with a complex, intricate structure. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often aromatic, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor, which attracts specific pollinators.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds and has been used in traditional medicine, though it is also known to be toxic in certain forms.
