Acacia decurrens var. angulata

Acacia decurrens var. angulata, commonly known as the angular acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, angular branches and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Its woody stems and adaptability to arid conditions make it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
