Arthrotrichum calostachyum

Arthrotrichum calostachyum, commonly known as the narrowleaf arrowgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. Its inflorescence consists of small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes.

The species is characterized by its distinctive, segmented stems and is often found in association with other wetland flora.
