Alyxia macrocarpa

Alyxia macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a woody vine that typically grows in moist, shaded areas of forests. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by showy, red or orange berries. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges.

Alyxia macrocarpa is valued for its ornamental berries and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
