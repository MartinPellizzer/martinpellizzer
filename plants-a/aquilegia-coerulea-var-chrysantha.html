Aquilegia coerulea var. chrysantha

Aquilegia coerulea var. chrysantha, commonly known as the golden monkeyflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with finely divided lobes, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
