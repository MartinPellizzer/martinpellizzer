Acacia uncinifolia

Acacia uncinifolia, commonly known as the hook-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical inflorescences, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in pod-like structures that split open to release the seeds.

Acacia uncinifolia is adapted to arid environments and is often found in savannas and rocky areas.
