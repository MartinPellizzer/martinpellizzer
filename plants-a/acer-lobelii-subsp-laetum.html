Acer lobelii subsp. laetum

Acer lobelii subsp.

laetum, commonly known as the Laetum maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its finely toothed, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant for gardens.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to native plant collections.
