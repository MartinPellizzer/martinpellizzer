Ampliglossum warmingii

Ampliglossum warmingii, commonly known as the Warming's giant bellflower, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy bell-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to deep purple. The plant features sturdy, hairy stems that can grow up to two meters in height, supporting its impressive floral display. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ampliglossum warmingii thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes.
