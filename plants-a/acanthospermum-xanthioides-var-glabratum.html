Acanthospermum xanthioides var. glabratum

Acanthospermum xanthioides var. glabratum, commonly known as the smooth thornweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with hairy or smooth leaves depending on the variety. Its small yellow flowers are arranged in clusters, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The smooth thornweed is adapted to survive in harsh environments, showing resistance to drought and poor soil conditions.
