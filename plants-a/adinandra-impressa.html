Adinandra impressa

Adinandra impressa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Adinandra impressa is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

Its hardy nature makes it a valuable species for coastal stabilization and ecological restoration.
