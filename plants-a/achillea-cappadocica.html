Achillea cappadocica

Achillea cappadocica, commonly known as Cappadocian yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry environments, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are fern-like, with a grayish-green color, and it produces a strong, aromatic scent.

Achillea cappadocica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
