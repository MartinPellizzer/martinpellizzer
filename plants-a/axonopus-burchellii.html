Axonopus burchellii

Axonopus burchellii, commonly known as Burchell's axonopus, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming plant with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, often near the base of the foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Axonopus burchellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
