Acaena inermis

Acaena inermis, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, green, edible leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into spherical, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas and forest margins.

Acaena inermis is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary use in certain regions.
