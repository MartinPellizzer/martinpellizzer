Agave lindleyi

Agave lindleyi, commonly known as the Lindley's agave, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of thick, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a layer of white wax. The plant produces a tall flowering stem with clusters of yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Agave lindleyi is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and resilience.
