Achillea fragilis

Achillea fragilis, commonly known as brittle yarrow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its slender stems and fragrant, finely divided leaves are adapted to arid conditions.

The flowers, which range from white to pale yellow, bloom in summer and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
