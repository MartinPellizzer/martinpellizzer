Acacia bagsteri

Acacia bagsteri, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and small, bipinnate leaves that are typically green and narrow. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spherical or cylindrical heads. Acacia bagsteri is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal and semi-arid environments.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
