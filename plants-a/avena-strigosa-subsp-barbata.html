Avena strigosa subsp. barbata

Avena strigosa subsp.

barbata, commonly known as the barbed oat, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough, textured surface.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous bracted florets, which are often adorned with short, barbed hairs. These hairs aid in seed dispersal by attaching to the fur of animals or clothing.

It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open fields, where it can form dense stands.
