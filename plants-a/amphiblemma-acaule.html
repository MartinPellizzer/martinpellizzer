Amphiblemma acaule

Amphiblemma acaule, commonly known as the leafy bladder nut, is a unique plant species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lack of a distinct stem, with the leaves emerging directly from the base of the plant. The plant produces small, round fruits that resemble bladders, which are typically green when immature and turn brown as they mature. These fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to the plant's reproductive strategy.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on plant morphology and adaptation.
