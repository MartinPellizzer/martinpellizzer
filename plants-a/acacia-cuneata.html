Acacia cuneata

Acacia cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Acacia cuneata is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
