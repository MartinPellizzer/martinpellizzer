Aster baldwinii

Aster baldwinii, commonly known as Baldwin's aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. This perennial herb typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
