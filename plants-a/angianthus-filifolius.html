Angianthus filifolius

Angianthus filifolius, commonly known as the threadleaf angianthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are finely divided, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique foliage and compact growth habit make it an attractive choice for ornamental gardening.
