Aristolochia dictyantha var. schomburgkii

Aristolochia dictyantha var. schomburgkii, commonly known as the Schomburgk's Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular, hooded flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, aromatic aril. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is notable for its complex floral morphology and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
