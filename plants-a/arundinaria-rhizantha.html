Arundinaria rhizantha

Arundinaria rhizantha, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or black bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to spread vegetatively and form dense clumps. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Arundinaria rhizantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
