Acronia tobarii

Acronia tobarii, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

This perennial herbaceous plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are small and often clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink, and are adapted for pollination by specific insects. The plant has a short stem with narrow, grass-like leaves, and its root system is specialized for anchoring in loose, nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its restricted habitat and sensitivity to environmental changes, Acronia tobarii is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
