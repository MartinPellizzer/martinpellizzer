Arundinaria exsaniosa

Arundinaria exsaniosa, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria exsaniosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
