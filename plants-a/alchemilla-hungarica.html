Alchemilla hungarica

Alchemilla hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and secrete a sticky substance that can stain surfaces.

Alchemilla hungarica is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
