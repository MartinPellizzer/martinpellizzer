Amsinckia rugosissima

Amsinckia rugosissima, commonly known as the desert larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. Its leaves are pinnately divided and have a distinctive rough texture.

Amsinckia rugosissima is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
