Aeridostachya unifolia

Aeridostachya unifolia, commonly known as the single-flowered grass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces a single, elongated inflorescence bearing small, greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, typical of many grass species.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered plant in its native range.
