Athyrium takeoi

Athyrium takeoi, commonly known as the Japanese shield fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Athyriaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in clusters on the underside of the fronds.

Athyrium takeoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
