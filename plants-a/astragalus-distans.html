Astragalus distans

Astragalus distans, commonly known as the distant milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus distans is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
