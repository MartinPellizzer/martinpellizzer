Arenaria graminifolia var. glabra

Arenaria graminifolia var. glabra, commonly known as smooth sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It has a mat-forming habit with thin, grass-like leaves that are smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous environments.

This variety is notable for its ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions and is an important component of alpine ecosystems.
