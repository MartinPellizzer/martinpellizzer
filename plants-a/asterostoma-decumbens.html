Asterostoma decumbens

Asterostoma decumbens, commonly known as the creeping aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features a low-growing, sprawling habit with numerous small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Asterostoma decumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
