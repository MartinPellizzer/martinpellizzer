Agrostis implicata

Agrostis implicata, commonly known as the hooked bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows, wetlands, and grassy slopes. The plant has slender, tufted stems that often bend at the base, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Agrostis implicata is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils and is often found in areas with high moisture content.
