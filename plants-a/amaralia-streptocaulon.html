Amaralia streptocaulon

Amaralia streptocaulon, commonly known as the zigzag amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often twisted or zigzagged in appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Amaralia streptocaulon is known for its long blooming period, typically from late winter to early spring.
