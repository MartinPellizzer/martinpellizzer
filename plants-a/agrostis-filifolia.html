Agrostis filifolia

Agrostis filifolia, commonly known as slender bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features fine, thread-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in width, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is a member of the Poaceae family.
