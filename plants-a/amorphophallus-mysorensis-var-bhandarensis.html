Amorphophallus mysorensis var. bhandarensis

Amorphophallus mysorensis var. bhandarensis, commonly known as the Bhandara elephant yam, is a rare and unique variant of the amorphophallus genus native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy underground tubers that can weigh several kilograms. The plant produces a tall, leafy stem with large, palmately lobed leaves, and it blooms with a striking, spathaceous inflorescence. It is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of its native habitat and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
