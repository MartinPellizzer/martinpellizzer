Avenula hackelii

Avenula hackelii, commonly known as the Hackel's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. It is part of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes.

Avenula hackelii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
