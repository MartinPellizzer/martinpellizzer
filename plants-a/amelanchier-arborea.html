Amelanchier arborea

Amelanchier arborea, commonly known as the flowering serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open woods.

Amelanchier arborea is valued for its ornamental appeal and wildlife habitat benefits.
