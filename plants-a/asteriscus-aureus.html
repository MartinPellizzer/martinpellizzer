Asteriscus aureus

Asteriscus aureus, commonly known as golden asterisk, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, yellow-orange flowers arranged in a star-like pattern, giving it its common name. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Asteriscus aureus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its compact size and vibrant blooms.
