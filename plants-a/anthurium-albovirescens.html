Anthurium albovirescens

Anthurium albovirescens, commonly known as the white-veined anthurium, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are predominantly green with prominent white veins. The plant produces inflorescences that consist of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typically in shades of red or orange. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Anthurium albovirescens is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage.
