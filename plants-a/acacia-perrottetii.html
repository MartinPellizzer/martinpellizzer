Acacia perrottetii

Acacia perrottetii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and phyllodes, which are flattened leaf-like structures. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Acacia perrottetii is also recognized as the floral emblem of Australia.
