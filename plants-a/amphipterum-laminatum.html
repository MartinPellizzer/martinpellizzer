Amphipterum laminatum

Amphipterum laminatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
