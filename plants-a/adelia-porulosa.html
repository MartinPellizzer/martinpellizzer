Adelia porulosa

Adelia porulosa, commonly known as the Porcupine Cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often covered in small, tuberculate bumps. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but striking in appearance. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Adelia porulosa is a slow-growing plant that requires minimal water and is well-suited for rock gardens or arid landscapes.
