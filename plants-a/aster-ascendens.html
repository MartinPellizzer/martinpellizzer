Aster ascendens

Aster ascendens, commonly known as the ascending aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Aster ascendens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
