Alyssum lapeyrouseanum subsp. angustifolium

Alyssum lapeyrouseanum subsp.

angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved alyssum, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and profuse display of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The leaves are typically narrow and linear, giving the plant a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
