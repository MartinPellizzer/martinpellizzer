Alsophila grangaudiana

Alsophila grangaudiana, commonly known as the Grangaud's tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.

Alsophila grangaudiana is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
