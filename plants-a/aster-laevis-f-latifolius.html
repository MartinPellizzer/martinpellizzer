Aster laevis f. latifolius

Aster laevis f. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf aster, is a variant of the smooth aster species.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in the late summer and early fall. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
