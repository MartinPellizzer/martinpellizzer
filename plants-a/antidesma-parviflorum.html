Antidesma parviflorum

Antidesma parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered antidesma, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are often eaten by birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
