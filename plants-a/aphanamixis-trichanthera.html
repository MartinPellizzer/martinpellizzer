Aphanamixis trichanthera

Aphanamixis trichanthera, commonly known as the silky mallow, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a soft, hairy texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.
