Arrhostoxylum elegans

Arrhostoxylum elegans, commonly known as the silverleaf honeyleaf, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
