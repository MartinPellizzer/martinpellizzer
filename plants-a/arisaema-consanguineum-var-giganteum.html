Arisaema consanguineum var. giganteum

Arisaema consanguineum var. giganteum, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a striking member of the Araceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces a unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical gardens and botanical collections.
