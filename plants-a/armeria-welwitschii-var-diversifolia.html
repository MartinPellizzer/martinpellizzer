Armeria welwitschii var. diversifolia

Armeria welwitschii var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variable thrift, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces showy clusters of pink or white flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with some variation in coloration among the different foliage types. This variety is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
