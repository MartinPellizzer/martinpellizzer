Abuta macrophylla

Abuta macrophylla, commonly known as the giant abuta, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Abuta macrophylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
