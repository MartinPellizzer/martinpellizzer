Allium grosii

Allium grosii, commonly known as the Alaskan leek, is a perennial plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical leaves and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cooler climates. Its bulbous underground stem is edible and has a mild, onion-like flavor.

Allium grosii is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and culinary uses.
