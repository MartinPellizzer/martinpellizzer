Astragalus pueblae

Astragalus pueblae, commonly known as the Pueblo milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Astragalus pueblae is also of interest in botanical research due to its potential medicinal properties.
