Astragalus gerensis

Astragalus gerensis, commonly known as the Mongolian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Astragalus gerensis is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects.
