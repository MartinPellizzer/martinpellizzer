Arthrocnemum arbuscula

Arthrocnemum arbuscula, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a low-growing, succulent plant adapted to arid environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils across desert regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

Due to its ability to store water in its stems, it plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and preventing erosion.
