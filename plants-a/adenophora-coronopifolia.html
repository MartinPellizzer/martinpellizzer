Adenophora coronopifolia

Adenophora coronopifolia, commonly known as the crownleaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy blue to purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats and prefers well-drained soil. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive crown-like foliage and bell-shaped blossoms.

Adenophora coronopifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
