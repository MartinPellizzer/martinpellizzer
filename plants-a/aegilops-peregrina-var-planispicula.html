Aegilops peregrina var. planispicula

Aegilops peregrina var. planispicula, commonly known as the slender wild barley, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and hairless, with a bluish-green color.

It plays a role in ecological systems as a forage plant and has historical significance in agricultural research.
