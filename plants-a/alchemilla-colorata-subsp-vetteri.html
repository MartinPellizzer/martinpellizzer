Alchemilla colorata subsp. vetteri

Alchemilla colorata subsp.

vetteri, commonly known as the Vetter's lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
