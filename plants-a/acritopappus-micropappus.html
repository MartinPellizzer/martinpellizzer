Acritopappus micropappus

Acritopappus micropappus, commonly known as the slender pappus plant, is a small annual herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bract structures. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, linear leaves. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are surrounded by a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal of its seeds.

The botanical features of A. micropappus reflect its adaptation to arid environments and its role in local ecosystems.
