Acacia genistifolia subsp. platyphylla

Acacia genistifolia subsp.

platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf golden wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, flat leaves that are typically green with a slight whitish underside.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in globular inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sandy environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
