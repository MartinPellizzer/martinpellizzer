Anthericum flavescens

Anthericum flavescens, commonly known as the yellow adder's mouth, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its spatheous inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system that aids in its propagation and survival in various habitats.
