Asterolasia correifolia var. mollis

Asterolasia correifolia var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-leaf asterolasia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features soft, hairy leaves that give it its varietal name, mollis, meaning "soft" in Latin. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
