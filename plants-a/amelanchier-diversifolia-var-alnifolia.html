Amelanchier diversifolia var. alnifolia

Amelanchier diversifolia var. alnifolia, commonly known as the alnifolia serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and typically have 5 to 9 leaflets. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in the summer. Its botanical features include smooth, grayish bark and a tendency to form dense thickets.

It is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
