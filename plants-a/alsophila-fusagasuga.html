Alsophila fusagasuga

Alsophila fusagasuga, commonly known as the Fusagasuga tree fern, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant exhibits a unique growth habit, with a distinct caudex or trunk that stores water, aiding in its survival in dry conditions. Its fronds are typically evergreen and can reach lengths of up to several meters.

Alsophila fusagasuga plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
