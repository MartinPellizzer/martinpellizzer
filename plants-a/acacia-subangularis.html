Acacia subangularis

Acacia subangularis, commonly known as the narrow-angled acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, angular branches and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Acacia subangularis is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
