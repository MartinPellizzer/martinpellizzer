Aristolochia ophioides

Aristolochia ophioides, commonly known as the Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex chemical composition, including compounds with medicinal and toxic properties.
