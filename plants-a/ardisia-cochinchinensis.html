Ardisia cochinchinensis

Ardisia cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring. The plant is notable for its vibrant, dark purple berries that appear in the fall, which are edible for humans and birds. Ardisia cochinchinensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
