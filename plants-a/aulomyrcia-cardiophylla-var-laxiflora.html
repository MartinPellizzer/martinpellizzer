Aulomyrcia cardiophylla var. laxiflora

Aulomyrcia cardiophylla var. laxiflora, commonly known as the broad-leafed myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which are followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or riverine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
