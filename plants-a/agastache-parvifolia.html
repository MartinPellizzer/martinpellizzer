Agastache parvifolia

Agastache parvifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of tubular blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

Agastache parvifolia is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
