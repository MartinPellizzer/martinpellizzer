Agave cerulata subsp. subcerulata

Agave cerulata subsp.

subcerulata, commonly known as the blue agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage, which is covered in a fine layer of white wax.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, greenish-white flowers. It is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in tequila production, it is widely cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
