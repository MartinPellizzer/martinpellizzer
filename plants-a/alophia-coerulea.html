Alophia coerulea

Alophia coerulea, commonly known as the blue-flowered lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of showy, blue-violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen and contribute to its ornamental appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking exotic yet hardy plants.
