Adonis maculata var. pallens

Adonis maculata var. pallens, commonly known as the spotted crowfoot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and a cluster of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its flowers are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
