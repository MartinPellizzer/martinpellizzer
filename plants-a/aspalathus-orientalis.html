Aspalathus orientalis

Aspalathus orientalis, commonly known as the African red bush, is a small shrub native to the Eastern Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, reddish-brown foliage and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in dry conditions. It is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of Rooibos tea.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various local wildlife species.
