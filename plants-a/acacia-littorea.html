Acacia littorea

Acacia littorea, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, phyllodes that are typically linear to elliptic in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spherical or cylindrical heads, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Acacia littorea is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
