Arabis bellidifolia subsp. stellulata

Arabis bellidifolia subsp.

stellulata, commonly known as the star-like rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically features basal leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape, with a hairy surface. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are pale yellow or white, arranged in clusters that give the plant its distinctive star-like appearance.
