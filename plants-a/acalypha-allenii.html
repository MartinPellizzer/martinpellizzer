Acalypha allenii

Acalypha allenii, commonly known as the Allen's glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of clusters of small, vibrant red or orange bracts. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Acalypha allenii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
