Aster howellii

Aster howellii, commonly known as Howell's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like blooms. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster howellii is often found in California and Nevada, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
