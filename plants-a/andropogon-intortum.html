Andropogon intortum

Andropogon intortum, commonly known as twisted bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, bluish-green leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, aiding in its propagation.

Andropogon intortum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting native wildlife.
