Aconitum lamarckii var. macedonicum

Aconitum lamarckii var. macedonicum, commonly known as the Macedonian monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Balkans.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple hooded flowers. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically deeply divided. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
