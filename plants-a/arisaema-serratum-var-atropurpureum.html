Arisaema serratum var. atropurpureum

Arisaema serratum var. atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-veined arisaema, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features a distinctive underground tuber and tall, arrow-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a hooded spathe, which is typically purple or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and striking floral display make it a popular ornamental plant in cultivation.
