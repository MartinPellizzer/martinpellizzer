Aster laevis var. concinnus

Aster laevis var. concinnus, commonly known as the smooth aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are usually smooth and lance-shaped, with a silvery underside.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing showy blue to purple blossoms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
