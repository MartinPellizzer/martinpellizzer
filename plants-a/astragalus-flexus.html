Astragalus flexus

Astragalus flexus, commonly known as the flexible milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources.

Astragalus flexus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
