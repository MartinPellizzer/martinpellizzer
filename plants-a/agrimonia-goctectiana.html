Agrimonia goctectiana

Agrimonia goctectiana, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Agrimonia goctectiana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and astringent properties.
