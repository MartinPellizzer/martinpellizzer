Amaracus ciliatus

Amaracus ciliatus, commonly known as the silky amaranth, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, gray-green leaves and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators make it both ecologically and aesthetically beneficial.
