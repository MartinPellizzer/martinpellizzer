Alocasia macrorrhizos

Alocasia macrorrhizos, commonly known as the giant taro, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, with a deep green color and a distinctive purple underside. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix and spathe. Alocasia macrorrhizos thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.
