Arabis × delarbrei

Arabis × delarbrei, commonly known as the hybrid rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically has basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and hairy, while the stem leaves are more elongated. Native to Europe, it thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
