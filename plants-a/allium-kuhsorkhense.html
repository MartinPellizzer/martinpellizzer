Allium kuhsorkhense

Allium kuhsorkhense, commonly known as the Kuh-Sorkh onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are used in local cuisines, though they are less commonly cultivated than other Allium species.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability to high-altitude environments and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
