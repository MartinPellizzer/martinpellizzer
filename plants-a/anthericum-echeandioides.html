Anthericum echeandioides

Anthericum echeandioides, commonly known as the Echeandia lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and it produces clusters of white or pale green flowers with a distinctive, elongated structure. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a short, stout rhizome.

Anthericum echeandioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
