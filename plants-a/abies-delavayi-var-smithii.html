Abies delavayi var. smithii

Abies delavayi var. smithii, commonly known as the Smith's fir, is a rare and endemic conifer native to the high-altitude regions of southwestern China.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically shorter and more densely packed than those of its relatives. The tree produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and it is adapted to cold, moist environments with poor soil conditions. This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
