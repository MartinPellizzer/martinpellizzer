Adiantum macrophyllum var. glaucum

Adiantum macrophyllum var. glaucum, commonly known as the Glaucous Maidenhair Fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in Florida and Georgia. The plant features a distinctive central rachis with closely spaced pinnae, and its fronds often exhibit a bluish-gray hue due to the presence of a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or near water sources.

The plant's reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.
