Aciphylla traillii var. cartilaginea

Aciphylla traillii var. cartilaginea, commonly known as the cartilaginous grass tree, is a distinctive species native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fibrous, cartilage-like stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, which are followed by spikelets containing tiny seeds. It thrives in coastal and rocky habitats, often forming dense mats that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

This variety is notable for its unique morphology and ecological role in its native environment.
