Acacia tavaresorum

Acacia tavaresorum, commonly known as Tavares' acacia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes and is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is known for its slender, erect stems and small, bipinnate leaves that reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its yellow flowers are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences, making it visually striking.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
