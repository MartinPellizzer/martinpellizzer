Agarista oleifolia var. oleifolia

Agarista oleifolia var. oleifolia, commonly known as the oil plant, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Agarista oleifolia var. oleifolia is valued for its oil-rich leaves, which have been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
