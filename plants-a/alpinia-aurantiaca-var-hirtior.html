Alpinia aurantiaca var. hirtior

Alpinia aurantiaca var. hirtior, commonly known as the hairy red ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking reddish-pink bracts that surround its small, fragrant flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy rhizomes that store nutrients and water, typical of many ginger species. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a textured appearance.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
