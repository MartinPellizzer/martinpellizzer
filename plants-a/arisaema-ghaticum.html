Arisaema ghaticum

Arisaema ghaticum, commonly known as the Ghatic arrowroot, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Nepal. The plant features a short rhizome with a thick, fleshy stem and large, heart-shaped leaves. Its most distinctive characteristic is the spadix, a central spike surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Arisaema ghaticum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
