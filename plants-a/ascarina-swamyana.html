Ascarina swamyana

Ascarina swamyana, commonly known as the Swamyana fan palm, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by black, spherical fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Ascarina swamyana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
