Argyrolobium rupestre subsp. rupestre

Argyrolobium rupestre subsp.

rupestre, commonly known as the silverleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its silvery foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky areas and along coastal regions.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscapes.
