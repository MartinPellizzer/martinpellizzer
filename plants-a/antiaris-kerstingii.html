Antiaris kerstingii

Antiaris kerstingii, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and striking, yellowish-green inflorescence. The plant produces berries that contain toxic seeds, making it potentially hazardous to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
