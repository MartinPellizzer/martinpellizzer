Attalea cephalotus

Attalea cephalotus, commonly known as the "palm of the devil," is a striking plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of long, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in dense clusters at the top of the stem. Its unique structure allows it to thrive in wet, swampy environments, and it plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat for various species.

The fruit, which is edible, contains a sweet, sticky pulp that attracts animals, aiding in seed dispersal.
