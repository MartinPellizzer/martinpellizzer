Arctophila mucronata

Arctophila mucronata, commonly known as the Sierra lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky, alpine, or subalpine environments. The plant features small, spurred flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Arctophila mucronata is adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
