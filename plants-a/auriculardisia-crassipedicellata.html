Auriculardisia crassipedicellata

Auriculardisia crassipedicellata, commonly known as the broad-ribbed orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
