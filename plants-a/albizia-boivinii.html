Albizia boivinii

Albizia boivinii, commonly known as the African silk tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Albizia boivinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
