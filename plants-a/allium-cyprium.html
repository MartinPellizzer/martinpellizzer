Allium cyprium

Allium cyprium, commonly known as the Cyprus leek, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces umbels of white or pale pink flowers. Its bulbous stem is edible and has a mild, garlic-like flavor.

Allium cyprium is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
