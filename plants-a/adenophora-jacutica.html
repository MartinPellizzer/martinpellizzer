Adenophora jacutica

Adenophora jacutica, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Eastern Europe and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its root system consists of fibrous roots. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Adenophora jacutica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
