Arabis trichopoda

Arabis trichopoda, commonly known as hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known plants such as cabbages and mustards. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or mountainous regions. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name.

Arabis trichopoda is generally hardy and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
