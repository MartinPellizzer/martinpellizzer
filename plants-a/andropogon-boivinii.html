Andropogon boivinii

Andropogon boivinii, commonly known as Boivin's switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Andropogon boivinii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
