Agave henriquesii

Agave henriquesii, commonly known as the Henriques' agave, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears numerous white flowers, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and collections of desert plants.
