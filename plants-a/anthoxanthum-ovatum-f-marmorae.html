Anthoxanthum ovatum f. marmorae

Anthoxanthum ovatum f. marmorae, commonly known as the marbled oatgrass, is a variant of the common oatgrass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive marbled or mottled leaf patterns, which give it a unique visual appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to golden flowers.

The foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive, variegated leaves.
