Aronia pyrifolia

Aronia pyrifolia, commonly known as chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, red berries that ripen in late summer. The plant is known for its high antioxidant content, particularly anthocyanins, which give the fruit its vibrant color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive fall foliage.

Aronia pyrifolia is also valued in agriculture for its edible berries, which are used in jams, juices, and health supplements.
