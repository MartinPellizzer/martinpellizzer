Artemisia caudata f. forwoodii

Artemisia caudata f. forwoodii, commonly known as the Forwood's wormwood, is a horticultural form of the silver wormwood.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and narrow, thread-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in the summer months. It is well-suited for rock gardens and dry, well-drained soils, exhibiting drought tolerance once established.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
