Adenopetalum subsinuatum

Adenopetalum subsinuatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf false indigo, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits.

It is a perennial shrub native to the western United States, typically found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated margins and produces small, blue to purple flowers in racemes. Its root system is deep and drought-tolerant, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

The seed pods are woody and dehisce to release small, elongated seeds.
