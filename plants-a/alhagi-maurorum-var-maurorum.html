Alhagi maurorum var. maurorum

Alhagi maurorum var. maurorum, commonly known as the desert bean, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, compound leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible, bean-like seeds. It is well-suited for harsh environments due to its drought tolerance and deep root system.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological stability and traditional use in various regions.
