Achillea corymbifera

Achillea corymbifera, commonly known as the broad-leaved yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat-topped clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

Achillea corymbifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in medicine.
