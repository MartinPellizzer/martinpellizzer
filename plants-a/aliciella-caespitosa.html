Aliciella caespitosa

Aliciella caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Aliciella caespitosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
