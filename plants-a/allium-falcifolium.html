Allium falcifolium

Allium falcifolium, commonly known as the sickle-leaved garlic, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, sickle-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its bulbous stem contains a pungent, garlic-like odor and is used in traditional medicine.

Allium falcifolium thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.
