Agave banlan

Agave banlan, commonly known as the banana agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with white teeth along the edges. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which are followed by banana-like fruits. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant's unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
