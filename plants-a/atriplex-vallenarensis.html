Atriplex vallenarensis

Atriplex vallenarensis, commonly known as the Valle del Cauca saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to the arid regions of Colombia.

It is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments. The plant has small, spoon-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss through transpiration. It produces greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees.

Atriplex vallenarensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitat.
