Aneurolepidium aemulans

Aneurolepidium aemulans, commonly known as the mimic grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats in open habitats.

Aneurolepidium aemulans is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
