Aphanes arvensis subsp. maroccana

Aphanes arvensis subsp.

maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan field mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grassy areas and along streambanks.

Due to its delicate structure and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
