Annona cinerea

Annona cinerea, commonly known as the pale custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are pale green with a distinctive, waxy appearance, and it bears fruit that is typically pale yellow with a rough, bumpy surface.

The fruit is edible, though its flavor is often described as bland or slightly tart.
