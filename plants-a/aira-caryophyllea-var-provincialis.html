Aira caryophyllea var. provincialis

Aira caryophyllea var. provincialis, commonly known as the provincial lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-gray hue, and its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resilience. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.

The species is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in native habitats.
