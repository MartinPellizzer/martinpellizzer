Achras salicifolia

Achras salicifolia, commonly known as the silky paper plant, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a spiny, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Achras salicifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
