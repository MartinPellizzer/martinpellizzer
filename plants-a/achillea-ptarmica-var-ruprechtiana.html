Achillea ptarmica var. ruprechtiana

Achillea ptarmica var. ruprechtiana, commonly known as Ruprecht's yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
