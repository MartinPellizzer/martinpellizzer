Amblytropis verna var. longicarpa

Amblytropis verna var. longicarpa, commonly known as the long-fruited spring bellflower, is a perennial herb native to parts of eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, loamy soils. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its fruit is a distinctive elongated capsule, which gives the variety its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
