Arundinaria vaginata

Arundinaria vaginata, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clustered, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, coconut-like fruits. Arundinaria vaginata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for both indoor and outdoor landscaping.
