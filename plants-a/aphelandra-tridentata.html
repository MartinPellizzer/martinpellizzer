Aphelandra tridentata

Aphelandra tridentata, commonly known as the Trinidadian flame bamboo or flame plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and striking, pendulous inflorescences that bear bright orange or red bracts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are pollinated by birds, particularly hummingbirds. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Aphelandra tridentata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
