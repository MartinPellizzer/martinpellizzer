Amygdalus communis lanceifolia

Amygdalus communis lanceifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf almond.

This plant is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy white or pink flowers. It is native to parts of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces small, edible fruits that contain almonds, though they are typically not as commercially valuable as those of other almond species.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to gardens and landscapes.
