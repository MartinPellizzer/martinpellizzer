Aerangis roseocalcarata

Aerangis roseocalcarata, commonly known as the rose-spurred orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers feature a distinctive pinkish hue with prominent, spurred petals that aid in pollination by attracting specific insect pollinators.

The orchid's botanical structure includes long, narrow leaves and a complex inflorescence that supports its ornamental value.
