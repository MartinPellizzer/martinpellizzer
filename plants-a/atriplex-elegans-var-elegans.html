Atriplex elegans var. elegans

Atriplex elegans var. elegans, commonly known as silver leaf chenopod, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Atriplex elegans var. elegans is adapted to drought conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
