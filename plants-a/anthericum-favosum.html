Anthericum favosum

Anthericum favosum, commonly known as the favous bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces small, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a spathe, a feature common in many arum species. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

Anthericum favosum is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.
