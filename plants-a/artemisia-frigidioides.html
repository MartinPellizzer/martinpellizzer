Artemisia frigidioides

Artemisia frigidioides, commonly known as blue mist shrub, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-blue foliage that resembles mist, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice in drought-tolerant gardens.
