Acicalyptus longiflora

Acicalyptus longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered myrtle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is evergreen, with a dense, compact growth habit that makes it suitable for hedging and ornamental use. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, thriving in coastal climates.

Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
