Acalypha conferta

Acalypha conferta, commonly known as the silverleaf acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Acalypha conferta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive leaves and ease of care.
