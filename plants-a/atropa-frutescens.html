Atropa frutescens

Atropa frutescens, commonly known as the black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features a stout, branching stem that can grow up to 2 meters tall, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and pinnately veined, with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside.

The roots and rhizomes are the primary parts used in traditional medicine, containing various bioactive compounds.
