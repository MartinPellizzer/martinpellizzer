Abelmoschus coccineus var. acerifolius

Abelmoschus coccineus var. acerifolius, commonly known as the red okra or crimson globe okra, is a variant of the okra plant characterized by its distinctive red, spherical fruits.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil. The leaves are broad and lobed, often resembling maple leaves, giving it its common name. The plant is an annual herbaceous species that produces showy yellow flowers.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its edible pods, which are used in various cuisines.
