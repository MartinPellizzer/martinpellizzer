Arnica alpina var. lonchophylla

Arnica alpina var. lonchophylla, commonly known as the alpine tawnpuff, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive waxy coating, and its yellow-orange flowers bloom in summer.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often found in mountain meadows and alpine tundra.
