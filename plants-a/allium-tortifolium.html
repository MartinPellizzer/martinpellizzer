Allium tortifolium

Allium tortifolium, commonly known as the twisted-leaf garlic.

This perennial herb is native to the western United States and is characterized by its distinctive, twisted, grass-like leaves. It produces small, white, spherical flower clusters that bloom in the summer. The plant is part of the Allium genus, which includes many edible and ornamental species.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
