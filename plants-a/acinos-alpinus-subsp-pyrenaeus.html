Acinos alpinus subsp. pyrenaeus

Acinos alpinus subsp.

pyrenaeus, commonly known as the Pyrenean alpine cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, characterized by rocky soils and high altitudes.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves with a hairy surface, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh environment and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
