Andropogon flaccidus

Andropogon flaccidus, commonly known as flatleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has narrow, flat leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue to purple flowers.

Flatleaf bluestem plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
