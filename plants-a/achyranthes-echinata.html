Achyranthes echinata

Achyranthes echinata, commonly known as the spiny knotweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by reddish-brown, spiny fruits. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Achyranthes echinata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or disturbed areas.
