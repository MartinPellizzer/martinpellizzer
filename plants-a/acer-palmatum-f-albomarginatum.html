Acer palmatum f. albomarginatum

Acer palmatum f. albomarginatum, commonly known as the white-edged maple, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple known for its striking foliage.

Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a vibrant green upper surface and a distinctive white or cream-colored margin. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit, often forming a rounded or weeping shape. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The foliage changes color with the seasons, adding seasonal visual interest to the landscape.
