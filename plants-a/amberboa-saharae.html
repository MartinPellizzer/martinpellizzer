Amberboa saharae

Amberboa saharae, commonly known as the Saharan amberboa, is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of the Sahara Desert.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, amber-colored stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, adapted to the harsh desert environment. It has a deep root system that allows it to access water from underground sources.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological sensitivity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native habitat.
