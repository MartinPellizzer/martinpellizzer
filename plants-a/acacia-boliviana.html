Acacia boliviana

Acacia boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in nutrient-poor environments.

It is often found in dry, rocky areas and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
