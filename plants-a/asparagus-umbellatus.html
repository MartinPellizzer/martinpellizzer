Asparagus umbellatus

Asparagus umbellatus, commonly known as the umbrella asparagus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring, giving it its common name. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form thick mats in its natural habitat.

Asparagus umbellatus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
