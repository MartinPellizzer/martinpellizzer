Ageratum conyzoides var. inaequipaleaceum

Ageratum conyzoides var. inaequipaleaceum, commonly known as the rough ageratum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its branched stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple. The leaves are typically ovate with a rough, hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
