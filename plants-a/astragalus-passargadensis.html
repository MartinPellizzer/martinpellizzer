Astragalus passargadensis

Astragalus passargadensis, commonly known as Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Astragalus passargadensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
