Acacia clavigera subsp. usambarensis

Acacia clavigera subsp.

usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambarensis acacia, is a shrub native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets.

The plant produces yellow globular flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
