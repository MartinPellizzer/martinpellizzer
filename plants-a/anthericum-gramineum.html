Anthericum gramineum

Anthericum gramineum, commonly known as the grass leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a dense, spike-like inflorescence. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Anthericum gramineum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
