Agave wislizeni subsp. parrasana

Agave wislizeni subsp.

parrasana, commonly known as the Parrasana agave, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico. It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces a tall flower stalk bearing clusters of yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a high tolerance for drought and poor soil conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
