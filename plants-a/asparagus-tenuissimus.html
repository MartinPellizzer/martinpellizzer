Asparagus tenuissimus

Asparagus tenuissimus, commonly known as the slender asparagus or sweet asparagus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are often used in ornamental gardening for their fine texture and lush appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that give way to edible, tender shoots in early spring. Its stems are typically slender and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun exposure.

Asparagus tenuissimus is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its culinary use, particularly in traditional European cuisine.
