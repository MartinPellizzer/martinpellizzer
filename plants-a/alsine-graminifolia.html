Alsine graminifolia

Alsine graminifolia, commonly known as slender sandgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes or inland deserts. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Alsine graminifolia is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
