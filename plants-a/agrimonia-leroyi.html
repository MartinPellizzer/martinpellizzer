Agrimonia leroyi

Agrimonia leroyi, commonly known as the Lesotho agrimony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy. Its small yellow flowers are clustered in dense heads, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Agrimonia leroyi is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
