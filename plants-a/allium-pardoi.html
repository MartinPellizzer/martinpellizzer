Allium pardoi

Allium pardoi, commonly known as the purple garlic.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features a bulbous stem covered in a fibrous sheath, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and typically pale purple, arranged in a spherical cluster.

Allium pardoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in rock gardens or as a border plant.
