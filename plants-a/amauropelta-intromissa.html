Amauropelta intromissa

Amauropelta intromissa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the undersides of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and reproductive structures make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
