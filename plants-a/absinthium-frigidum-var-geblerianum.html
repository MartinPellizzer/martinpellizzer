Absinthium frigidum var. geblerianum

Absinthium frigidum var. geblerianum, commonly known as the alpine wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in complex inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to high-altitude conditions and cold climates.
