Acer orientale f. cuneifolium

Acer orientale f. cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maple, is a variant of the oriental maple characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves.

This ornamental tree or shrub is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage displays a striking color change in autumn, transitioning from green to yellow or orange. The plant's botanical features include smooth, gray bark and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in early spring.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
