Aster loriformis

Aster loriformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, open environments. Its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers make it easily identifiable.

Aster loriformis is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
