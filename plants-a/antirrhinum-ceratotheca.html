Antirrhinum ceratotheca

Antirrhinum ceratotheca, commonly known as the horned antirrhinum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a prominent spur at the base. The plant typically grows in shrubland or grassland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Antirrhinum ceratotheca is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
