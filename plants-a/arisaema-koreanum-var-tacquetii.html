Arisaema koreanum var. tacquetii

Arisaema koreanum var. tacquetii, commonly known as the Korean arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and spadix inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its botanical features include a short rhizome and a single, showy spathe that encloses the spadix.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral structure.
