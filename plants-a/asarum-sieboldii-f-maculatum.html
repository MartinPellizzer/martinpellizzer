Asarum sieboldii f. maculatum

Asarum sieboldii f. maculatum, commonly known as the spotted wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features a stout, rhizomatous rootstock and heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often marked with distinctive purple or reddish spots. The plant produces small, dark purple flowers in late spring, which are typically clustered in a single umbel. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its aromatic leaves and roots have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
