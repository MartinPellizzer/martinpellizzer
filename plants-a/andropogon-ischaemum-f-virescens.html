Andropogon ischaemum f. virescens

Andropogon ischaemum f. virescens, commonly known as green panicgrass, is a variant of the panic grass genus native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, meadows, and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
