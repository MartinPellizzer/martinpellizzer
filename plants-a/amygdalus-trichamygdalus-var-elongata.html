Amygdalus trichamygdalus var. elongata

Amygdalus trichamygdalus var. elongata, commonly known as the long-sepaled almond.

It is a variant of the almond tree characterized by its elongated sepals that distinguish it from the standard almond species. This plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. The botanical features include small, white flowers and oval-shaped fruits that contain edible seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and potential agricultural use.
