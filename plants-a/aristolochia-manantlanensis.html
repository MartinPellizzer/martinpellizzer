Aristolochia manantlanensis

Aristolochia manantlanensis, commonly known as the Manantlan Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant produces a strong, aromatic scent that attracts specific pollinators, such as flies. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are woody and vine-like.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
