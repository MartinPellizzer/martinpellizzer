Asperula scoparia subsp. subglabra

Asperula scoparia subsp.

subglabra, commonly known as the smooth asperula, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe. It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous, giving it its subspecies name.

The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
