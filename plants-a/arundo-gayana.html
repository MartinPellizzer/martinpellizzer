Arundo gayana

Arundo gayana, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It has long, narrow leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with a fibrous, hollow stem that is often used in construction and crafts. The plant thrives in wetlands, riverbanks, and other moist environments, making it a common aquatic or semi-aquatic species. Its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes make it an effective erosion control plant.

However, it can also become invasive in non-native habitats, outcompeting native vegetation.
