Archidendron pachycarpum

Archidendron pachycarpum, commonly known as the yellow tamarind tree, is a deciduous legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are trifoliate and often have a glossy appearance. The tree produces long, pendulous racemes of yellow flowers followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its root system is robust, allowing it to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
