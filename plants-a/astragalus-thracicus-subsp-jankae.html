Astragalus thracicus subsp. jankae

Astragalus thracicus subsp.

jankae, commonly known as the Janka's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to parts of southern Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has a shallow root system and is often found in open habitats such as steppes and grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
