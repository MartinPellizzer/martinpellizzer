Atraphaxis aucheri

Atraphaxis aucheri, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial herb with a compact, mat-forming growth habit, often found in rocky or mountainous areas. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.

Atraphaxis aucheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
