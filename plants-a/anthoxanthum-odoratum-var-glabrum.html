Anthoxanthum odoratum var. glabrum

Anthoxanthum odoratum var. glabrum, commonly known as sweet vernal grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. It has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

This variety is distinguished by its smooth, glabrous surface, which sets it apart from other variants of the species.
