Agropyron × hajastanicum

Agropyron × hajastanicum, commonly known as the Armenian grass, is a hybrid grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and glabrous, blue-green foliage. The plant produces spikelets that are typically 5 to 7 mm long, with two to three bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and grassland ecosystems.

Agropyron × hajastanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
