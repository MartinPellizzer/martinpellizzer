Ampelocissus hoabinhensis

Ampelocissus hoabinhensis, commonly known as the Hoabinh fig, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits.

This species is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
