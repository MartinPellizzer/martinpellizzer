Amaryllis pallida

Amaryllis pallida, commonly known as the pale amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of white or pale pink blooms that are often speckled with darker hues. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, forming a rosette at the base.

Amaryllis pallida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
