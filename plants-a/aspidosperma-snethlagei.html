Aspidosperma snethlagei

Aspidosperma snethlagei, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceraria*, is a member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
