Angelica sinensis

Angelica sinensis, commonly known as dong quai, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that are the primary part used medicinally. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring large, compound leaves and small white or greenish-yellow flowers. Its roots contain various bioactive compounds, including coumarins, lignans, and essential oils, which contribute to its traditional medicinal uses.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its therapeutic properties.
