Axonopus scoparius f. mattogrossensis

Axonopus scoparius f. mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian carpetgrass, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Botanically, it is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in ecological restoration projects.
