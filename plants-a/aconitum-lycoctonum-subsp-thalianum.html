Aconitum lycoctonum subsp. thalianum

Aconitum lycoctonum subsp.

thalianum, commonly known as the European aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers.

The plant produces distinctive, helmet-shaped seed pods that contain numerous small, brown seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, often found in woodland areas.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
