Alchemilla hildebrandtii var. hypoleuca

Alchemilla hildebrandtii var. hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-stemmed lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features rosettes of basal leaves that are softly hairy and have a distinctive silver-gray coloration. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
