Agrimonia dahurica var. pilosa

Agrimonia dahurica var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy agrimony, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Agrimonia dahurica var. pilosa is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
