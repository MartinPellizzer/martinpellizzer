Anthericum breviantheratum

Anthericum breviantheratum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arum, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy texture, which helps in reducing water loss.

Anthericum breviantheratum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
