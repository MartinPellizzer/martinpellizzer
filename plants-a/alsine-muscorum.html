Alsine muscorum

Alsine muscorum, commonly known as common mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing annual herb belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. The flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and prominent stamens, and they bloom in early spring. This plant thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, gardens, and along roadsides.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
