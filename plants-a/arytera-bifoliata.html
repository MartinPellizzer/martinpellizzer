Arytera bifoliata

Arytera bifoliata, commonly known as the two-leaved arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of leaves that are broadly ovate with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves and flowers contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The species is adapted to cold, dry environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
