Arundinaria actinotricha

Arundinaria actinotricha, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or giant bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, it plays an important role in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
