Aster grayanus

Aster grayanus, commonly known as the gray beard or gray beard grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, with slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spherical heads, often appearing in late summer to early autumn.

Aster grayanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
