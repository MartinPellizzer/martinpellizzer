Adonidia merrillii

Adonidia merrillii, commonly known as the Philippine white cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
