Aphloia theiformis var. seychellensis

Aphloia theiformis var. seychellensis, commonly known as the Seychelles hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Seychelles Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring broad, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and red, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

This species is notable for its adaptability to coastal environments and its role in local ecosystems.
