Aechmea virens

Aechmea virens, commonly known as the green pineapple plant, is a bromeliad native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces a striking inflorescence with colorful bracts that range from red to orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Aechmea virens is known for its ability to adapt to various growing conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Morphology

Aechmea virens has leaves that are broad, strap-like, and slightly curved, arranged in a rosette pattern, with a waxy, dark green color and a pointed tip.

The plant has a short, thick stem covered in a fibrous, pale green bark, growing upright with a compact form. Its flowers are borne in a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, with white to pale pink petals, blooming during the dry season and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that disperses seeds through wind and animal consumption.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in its rocky or dry habitat.
