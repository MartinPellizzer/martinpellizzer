Aconitum pseudanthora

Aconitum pseudanthora, commonly known as the Tibetan aconite, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy flowers. The plant produces blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are palmately divided with a distinctive appearance. Aconitum pseudanthora contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant of both botanical interest and caution.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows at high elevations.
