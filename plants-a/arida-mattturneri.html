Arida mattturneri

Arida mattturneri, commonly known as the Desert Sunflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, gray-green foliage adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its resilience to drought and heat makes it an important species in desert ecosystems.
