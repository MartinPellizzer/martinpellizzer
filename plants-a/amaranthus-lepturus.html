Amaranthus lepturus

Amaranthus lepturus, commonly known as the slender amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the variety. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy plant suitable for various climates.

Amaranthus lepturus is often cultivated as a ornamental plant and is also used in some traditional cuisines.
