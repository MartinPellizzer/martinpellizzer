Agrostis vinealis subsp. syreistschikowii

Agrostis vinealis subsp.

syreistschikowii, commonly known as the sand bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Eurasia. It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and dry grasslands.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Its ecological significance lies in its role as a stabilizing plant in coastal and arid habitats.
