Aciotis purpurascens var. pellucida

Aciotis purpurascens var. pellucida, commonly known as the translucent aciotis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically yellow to orange, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

The varietal name "pellucida" refers to its translucent or glassy appearance in certain light conditions.
