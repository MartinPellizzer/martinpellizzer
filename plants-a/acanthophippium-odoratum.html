Acanthophippium odoratum

Acanthophippium odoratum, commonly known as the sweet pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. The plant's flowers are typically yellow or orange and are borne on a short inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity to flourish.

Its unique structure and specialized adaptations for nutrient acquisition make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
