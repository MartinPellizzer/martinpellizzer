Anemia pubescens

Anemia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy anemone, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, usually pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Anemia pubescens is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
