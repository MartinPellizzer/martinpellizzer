Aster laevigatus

Aster laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, smooth hairs, giving it its common name.

The showy purple or white flowers bloom in the summer and attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
