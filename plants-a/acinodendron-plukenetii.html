Acinodendron plukenetii

Acinodendron plukenetii, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, opposite, ovate leaves that turn red in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to dry climates and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
