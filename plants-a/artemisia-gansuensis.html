Artemisia gansuensis

Artemisia gansuensis, commonly known as Gansu wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of China.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, high-altitude environments. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in composite inflorescences, and it is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Artemisia gansuensis plays a role in preventing soil erosion and maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
