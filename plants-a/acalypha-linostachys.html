Acalypha linostachys

Acalypha linostachys, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, often reddish-veined leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are typically inconspicuous compared to its foliage. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
