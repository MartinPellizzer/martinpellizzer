Abutilon gymnanthemum

Abutilon gymnanthemum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Abutilon gymnanthemum is often cultivated as a decorative shrub due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
