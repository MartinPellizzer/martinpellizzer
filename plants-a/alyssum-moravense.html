Alyssum moravense

Alyssum moravense, commonly known as Moravian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically has a mat-forming habit with hairy, linear leaves and produces yellow or white flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Alyssum moravense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in dry environments.
