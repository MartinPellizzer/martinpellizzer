Azedarach sempervirens

Azedarach sempervirens, commonly known as the Southern蜡树, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and consist of 5-9 pairs of ovate leaflets. The tree produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in late spring, followed by elongated, woody pods that contain smooth, glossy seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, well-drained areas.
