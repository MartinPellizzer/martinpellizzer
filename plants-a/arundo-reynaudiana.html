Arundo reynaudiana

Arundo reynaudiana, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-green foliage and can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant has hollow, jointed stems and produces feathery, grayish-blue flower spikes in late summer. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found near water bodies.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas make it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive.
