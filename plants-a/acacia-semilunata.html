Acacia semilunata

Acacia semilunata, commonly known as the moon-shaped acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, sickle-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Acacia semilunata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
