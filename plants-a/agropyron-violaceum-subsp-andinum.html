Agropyron violaceum subsp. andinum

Agropyron violaceum subsp.

andinum, commonly known as the Andean wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, violet-colored spikelets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and hairless, with a bluish-green coloration.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in alpine ecosystems.
