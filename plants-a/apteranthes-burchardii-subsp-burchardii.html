Apteranthes burchardii subsp. burchardii

Apteranthes burchardii subsp.

burchardii, commonly known as the Burchard's aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flower heads that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in arid ecosystems.
