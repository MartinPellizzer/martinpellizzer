Acronia carduela

Acronia carduela, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky soils. The plant features a spiny, spherical stem with dense, silver-gray foliage. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters at the top of the plant.

Acronia carduela is known for its hardy nature and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
