Alchemilla pringlei

Alchemilla pringlei, commonly known as the Mexican lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense, flat-topped inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Alchemilla pringlei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
