Adiantum nagnam

Adiantum nagnam, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are bi-lobed and have a distinctive zigzag pattern along the veins.

Adiantum nagnam is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
