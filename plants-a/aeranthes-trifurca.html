Aeranthes trifurca

Aeranthes trifurca, commonly known as the three-branched orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are long and strap-like, and it produces small, colorful flowers in shades of pink and white.

Aeranthes trifurca is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
