Aegilops cylindrica subsp. aristulata

Aegilops cylindrica subsp.

aristulata, commonly known as the slender wild rye, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its seeds are dispersed by wind or animals.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
