Ageria

Ageria, commonly known as the "sea holly," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking, often blue or purple, ray flowers and spiny, leaf-like bracts that surround the flower heads. These plants are typically found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions. Ageria species are perennial herbs that often have a rosette growth habit, with leaves that are usually fleshy and succulent.

Their unique appearance and ecological significance make them an important component of coastal flora.
