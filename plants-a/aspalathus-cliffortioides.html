Aspalathus cliffortioides

Aspalathus cliffortioides, commonly known as African red bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red flowers, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun exposure.

Aspalathus cliffortioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and drought tolerance.
