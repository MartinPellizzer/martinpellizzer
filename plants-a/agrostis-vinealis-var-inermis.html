Agrostis vinealis var. inermis

Agrostis vinealis var. inermis, commonly known as bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to temperate regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 inch wide, with a dense growth habit that forms a carpet-like appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lawns, meadows, and along stream banks.

Due to its fine texture and durability, it is frequently used in turfgrass applications.
