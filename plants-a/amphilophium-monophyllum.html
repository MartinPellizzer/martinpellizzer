Amphilophium monophyllum

Amphilophium monophyllum, commonly known as the single-leaf philodendron, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its single, large, heart-shaped leaf that emerges from a swollen, fleshy stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
