Adesmia muricata var. pimpinellifolia

Adesmia muricata var. pimpinellifolia, commonly known as the hairy adesmia, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to elliptic leaflets and produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or arid regions, where it adapts well to harsh environmental conditions.
