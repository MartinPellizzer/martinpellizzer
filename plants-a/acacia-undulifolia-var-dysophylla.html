Acacia undulifolia var. dysophylla

Acacia undulifolia var. dysophylla, commonly known as the wavy-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive wavy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, powdery bloom. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
