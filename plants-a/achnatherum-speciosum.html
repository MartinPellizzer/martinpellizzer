Achnatherum speciosum

Achnatherum speciosum, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue foliage and delicate, panicle-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray sheen that gives it its common name.

Achnatherum speciosum is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
