Agrostis spica-venti subsp. interrupta

Agrostis spica-venti subsp.

interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted windgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the flowers are arranged in a loose panicle.

This subspecies is often found in temperate climates and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
