Arundinaria viridistriata

Arundinaria viridistriata, commonly known as the green-striated bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that are often marked with distinct green longitudinal stripes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in forest understories. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, grass-like texture.

Arundinaria viridistriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
