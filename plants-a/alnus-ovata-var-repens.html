Alnus ovata var. repens

Alnus ovata var. repens, commonly known as the creeping alder, is a variant of the alder tree that exhibits a low, spreading growth habit.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, including China and Korea. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, yellowish catkins appear in early spring, followed by small, nut-like fruits.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
