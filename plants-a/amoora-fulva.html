Amoora fulva

Amoora fulva, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dazzling display. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
