Amorphophallus spectabilis

Amorphophallus spectabilis, commonly known as the giant elephant's foot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, swollen underground tuber, which can reach several feet in diameter. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences that are often surrounded by colorful bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in some regions.
