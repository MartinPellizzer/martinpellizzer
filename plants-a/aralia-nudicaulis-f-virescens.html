Aralia nudicaulis f. virescens

Aralia nudicaulis f. virescens, commonly known as the green-leafed aralia, is a variant of the common aralia characterized by its lush, dark green foliage.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to North America, typically found in moist, shady woodlands. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive, fern-like appearance, and it produces small, white, clustered flowers in the spring. Its botanical interest lies in its unique leaf coloration and its role in native ecosystems.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
