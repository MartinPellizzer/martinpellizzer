Amyema cumingii

Amyema cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's ghost gum, is a small evergreen tree native to the Northern Territory of Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Amyema cumingii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance and efficient water use.

It is often found in rocky areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
