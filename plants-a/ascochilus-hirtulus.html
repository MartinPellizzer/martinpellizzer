Ascochilus hirtulus

Ascochilus hirtulus, commonly known as the hairy tailed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands.

Ascochilus hirtulus is a perennial species that prefers full sun to partial shade.
