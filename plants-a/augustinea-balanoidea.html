Augustinea balanoidea

Augustinea balanoidea, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, delicate orchid-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops and sandy soils, adapting to harsh, maritime conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers have three sepals and three petals, with a distinctive lip.

Augustinea balanoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in coastal gardens for its resilience and unique floral display.
