Arundinella nepalensis

Arundinella nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal bamboo, is a flowering plant species native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 5 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of many grasses. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a bluish-green coloration, and it thrives in moist, temperate climates.

Arundinella nepalensis is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in fragile ecosystems.
