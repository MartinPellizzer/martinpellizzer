Albizia chevalieri

Albizia chevalieri, commonly known as the silky albizia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are soft and feathery, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in pods that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds by wind.

Albizia chevalieri is often used in traditional medicine and for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
