Aster ageratoides f. luxurifolius

Aster ageratoides f. luxurifolius, commonly known as the showy blueweed, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flowers and its ornamental value in gardens.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is native to North America. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery or fuzzy texture, adding visual interest.

The inflorescence consists of ray flowers surrounding a central disk, making it a popular choice for wildflower gardens.
