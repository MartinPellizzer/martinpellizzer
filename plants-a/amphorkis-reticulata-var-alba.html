Amphorkis reticulata var. alba

Amphorkis reticulata var. alba, commonly known as the white nettle orchid, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive net-like pattern on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
