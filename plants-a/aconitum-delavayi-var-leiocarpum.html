Aconitum delavayi var. leiocarpum

Aconitum delavayi var. leiocarpum, commonly known as the smooth-seeded aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

Due to its toxic properties, it requires careful handling and is not commonly cultivated for ornamental purposes.
