Agapetes reflexiloba

Agapetes reflexiloba, commonly known as the bent-leaved silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that are often curled or reflexed at the edges. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring or early summer. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Agapetes reflexiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
