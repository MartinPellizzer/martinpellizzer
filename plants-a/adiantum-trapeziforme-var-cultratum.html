Adiantum trapeziforme var. cultratum

Adiantum trapeziforme var. cultratum, commonly known as the cultrate maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and China. The fronds are typically slender and arching, with a distinctive, narrow, and elongated shape. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found growing on rocks or in moist, well-drained soil.

The plant is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
