Achyranthes aspera f. rosea

Achyranthes aspera f. rosea, commonly known as red borage or red sandmat, is a variant of the species Achyranthes aspera.

It is a herbaceous plant native to regions of Asia, including India, and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its erect stem and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
