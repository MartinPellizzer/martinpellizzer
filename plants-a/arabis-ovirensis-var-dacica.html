Arabis ovirensis var. dacica

Arabis ovirensis var. dacica, commonly known as the Dacian rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops and weeds. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
