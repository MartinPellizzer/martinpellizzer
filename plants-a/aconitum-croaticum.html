Aconitum croaticum

Aconitum croaticum, commonly known as Croatian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with deep lobes and produces toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
