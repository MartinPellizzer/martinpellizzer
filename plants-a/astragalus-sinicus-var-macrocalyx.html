Astragalus sinicus var. macrocalyx

Astragalus sinicus var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to China.

It features slender, hairy stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
