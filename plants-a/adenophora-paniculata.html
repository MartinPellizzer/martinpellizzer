Adenophora paniculata

Adenophora paniculata, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Adenophora paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
