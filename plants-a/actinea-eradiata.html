Actinea eradiata

Actinea eradiata, commonly known as the Radiate Actinia, is a species of sea anemone found in tropical and subtropical marine environments.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful tentacles that radiate from a central oral disc, giving it its common name. The plant, though not a true plant, belongs to the phylum Cnidaria and is often found in coral reefs and shallow coastal waters. Its structure consists of a polyp with a gastrovascular cavity and specialized cells called cnidocytes for capturing prey.

Actinea eradiata plays a role in marine ecosystems by contributing to biodiversity and serving as a food source for various marine organisms.
