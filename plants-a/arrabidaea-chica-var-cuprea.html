Arrabidaea chica var. cuprea

Arrabidaea chica var. cuprea, commonly known as the red-flowered arrabidaea, is a striking perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is distinguished by its vibrant red to orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in woodland edges and wetlands, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
