Aster artemisioides

Aster artemisioides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and pond edges. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped, often with a silvery-gray underside, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.
