Angraecum bathiei

Angraecum bathiei, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy white or pale blue flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern. The plant produces numerous fragrant, tubular flowers that attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Angraecum bathiei is a rare and endangered species, making it a valuable subject for conservation efforts.
