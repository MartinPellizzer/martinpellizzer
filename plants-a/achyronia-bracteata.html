Achyronia bracteata

Achyronia bracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Achyronia bracteata is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in some areas.
