Astragalus kristii

Astragalus kristii, commonly known as Krist's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in drought conditions. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
