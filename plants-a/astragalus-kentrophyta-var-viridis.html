Astragalus kentrophyta var. viridis

Astragalus kentrophyta var. viridis, commonly known as green milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open grasslands. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
