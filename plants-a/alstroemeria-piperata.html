Alstroemeria piperata

Alstroemeria piperata, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant features strap-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its long-lasting blooms.
