Annona cascarilloides

Annona cascarilloides, commonly known as the Mexican custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that produces edible fruits with a creamy, custard-like texture. The plant belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and fragrant, white flowers. Its fruits are typically yellow or green when ripe and are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
