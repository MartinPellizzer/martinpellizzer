Astragalus akkensis var. intermedius

Astragalus akkensis var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain edible seeds.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
