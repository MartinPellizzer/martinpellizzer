Azedarach sempervirens var. australasica

Azedarach sempervirens var. australasica, commonly known as the Australian flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The tree has a broad, spreading canopy and can grow up to 20 meters tall. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets, and it produces woody pods that contain seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well adapted to warm, coastal climates.
