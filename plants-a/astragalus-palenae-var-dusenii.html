Astragalus palenae var. dusenii

Astragalus palenae var. dusenii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun exposure.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
