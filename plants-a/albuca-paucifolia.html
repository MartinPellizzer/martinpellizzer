Albuca paucifolia

Albuca paucifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved albuca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces oval, fleshy bulbs that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a desirable addition to Mediterranean-style gardens.
