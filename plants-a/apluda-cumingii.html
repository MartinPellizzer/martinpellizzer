Apluda cumingii

Apluda cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's grassvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Apluda cumingii plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations to extreme climates.
