Abies excelsa var. pygmaea

Abies excelsa var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf white fir, is a compact variant of the white fir species.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. This evergreen conifer has a conical shape with short, needle-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath. Its small size makes it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh mountain conditions.
