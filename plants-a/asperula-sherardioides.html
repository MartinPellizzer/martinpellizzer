Asperula sherardioides

Asperula sherardioides, commonly known as the Sierra ovalleaf huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark blue berries in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
