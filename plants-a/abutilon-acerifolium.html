Abutilon acerifolium

Abutilon acerifolium, commonly known as the mapleleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves that resemble those of maple trees, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Abutilon acerifolium is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
