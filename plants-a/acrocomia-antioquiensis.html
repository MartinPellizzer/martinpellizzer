Acrocomia antioquiensis

Acrocomia antioquiensis, commonly known as the Antioquian palm, is a flowering plant species native to the Andean region of Colombia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white inflorescences with small, clustered flowers, which are pollinated by insects. Its fruits are fibrous and contain a single seed, making it a potential candidate for reforestation and sustainable agriculture.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
