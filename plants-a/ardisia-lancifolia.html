Ardisia lancifolia

Ardisia lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beautyberry, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by clusters of bright blue berries. These berries are edible for humans but toxic to livestock and pets.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
