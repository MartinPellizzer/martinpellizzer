Acacia subbinervia

Acacia subbinervia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are narrow and often covered with fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in cylindrical spikes and bloom during the warmer months.

Acacia subbinervia is adapted to dry and sandy soils, making it a resilient species in arid environments.
