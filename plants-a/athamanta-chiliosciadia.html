Athamanta chiliosciadia

Athamanta chiliosciadia, commonly known as the Chilean thistle, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Athamanta chiliosciadia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
