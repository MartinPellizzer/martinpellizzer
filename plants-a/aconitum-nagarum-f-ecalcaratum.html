Aconitum nagarum f. ecalcaratum

Aconitum nagarum f. ecalcaratum, commonly known as the Himalayan monkshood, is a variant of the monkshood plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which are arranged in a spire-like cluster atop tall, sturdy stems. The plant possesses distinctive heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins, typical of the Aconitum genus. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care and avoid ingestion.
