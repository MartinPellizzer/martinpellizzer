Alpinia galanga var. pyramidata

Alpinia galanga var. pyramidata, commonly known as the pyramid ginger, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that form a dense rosette. The plant produces striking, fragrant inflorescences with purple and white flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its rhizomes are aromatic and have been utilized in culinary and medicinal applications for centuries.

Alpinia galanga var. pyramidata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
