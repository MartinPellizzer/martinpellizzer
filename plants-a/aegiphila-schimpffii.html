Aegiphila schimpffii

Aegiphila schimpffii, commonly known as the Schimpff's aegiphila, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Aegiphila schimpffii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native environment.

Morphology

Aegiphila schimpffii has small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The stem is slender, upright, and covered with a smooth, grayish-brown bark that remains relatively thin throughout its growth. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, with white petals and yellow stamens, blooming during the dry season and pollinated primarily by wind.
