Amaranthus powellii subsp. cacciatoi

Amaranthus powellii subsp.

cacciatoi, commonly known as the Cacciato amaranth, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a flowering plant characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy bracts that range in color from red to purple.

The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various pollinators.
