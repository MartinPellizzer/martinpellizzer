Acomastylis elata

Acomastylis elata, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has a compact, sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow, with ray florets that bloom in spring and early summer.

Acomastylis elata is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its succulent leaves and stems.
