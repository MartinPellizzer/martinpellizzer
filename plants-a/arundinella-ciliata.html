Arundinella ciliata

Arundinella ciliata, commonly known as the silky grass or silky bamboo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a distinctive hairy texture along the edges.

Arundinella ciliata is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber due to its strong, flexible stems.
