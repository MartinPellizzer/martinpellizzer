Auriculardisia florulenta

Auriculardisia florulenta, commonly known as the golden ear orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green foliage, with flowers that resemble small ears, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Auriculardisia florulenta is also valued for its role in pollination, attracting various insects and contributing to local biodiversity.
