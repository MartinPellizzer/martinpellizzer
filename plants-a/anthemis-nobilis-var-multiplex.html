Anthemis nobilis var. multiplex

Anthemis nobilis var. multiplex, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic essential oil that contributes to its use in perfumery and herbal medicine. Its leaves are finely divided and covered in a dense layer of soft, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils in full sun.
