Ajuga yesoensis var. tsukubana

Ajuga yesoensis var. tsukubana, commonly known as the Japanese bugleweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Japan.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy. The plant produces small, tubular blue to purple flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ground cover in shaded areas.
