Achilleanthus smithii

Achilleanthus smithii, commonly known as the Smith's achilleanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
