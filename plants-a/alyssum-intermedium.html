Alyssum intermedium

Alyssum intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring and early summer. The plant has compact, branching stems covered in fine, silvery hairs, and its leaves are narrow and linear. Alyssum intermedium thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
