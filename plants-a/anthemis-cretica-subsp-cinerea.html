Anthemis cretica subsp. cinerea

Anthemis cretica subsp.

cinerea, commonly known as grey anthemis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, preferring full sun exposure. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties.

This subspecies is often used in ornamental gardening due to its drought tolerance and visual appeal.
