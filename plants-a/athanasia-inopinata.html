Athanasia inopinata

Athanasia inopinata, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, pale blue or white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its striking, showy blooms that appear during the rainy season. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Athanasia inopinata is considered a vulnerable species.
