Asclepias tuberosa f. bicolor

Asclepias tuberosa f. bicolor, commonly known as the showy milkweed or painted lady, is a striking variant of the common milkweed.

This plant is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers, which are adorned with intricate, swirling patterns. It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is native to North America, particularly the eastern and central United States. The foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens and pollinator habitats.
