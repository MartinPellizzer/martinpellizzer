Arrhenatherum elatius f. submuticum

Arrhenatherum elatius f. submuticum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wood sedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically a darker green than the standard form. This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-ranked flowers.

The plant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
