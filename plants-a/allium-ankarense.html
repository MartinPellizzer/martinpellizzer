Allium ankarense

Allium ankarense, commonly known as the Swedish leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a tall flowering stem topped with a cluster of small, white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Allium ankarense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
