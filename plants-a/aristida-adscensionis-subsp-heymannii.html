Aristida adscensionis subsp. heymannii

Aristida adscensionis subsp.

heymannii, commonly known as the Heymann's threeawn, is a grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spikelet-based inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while the flowering structures are usually arranged in clusters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
