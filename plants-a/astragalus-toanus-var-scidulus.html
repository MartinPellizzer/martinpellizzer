Astragalus toanus var. scidulus

Astragalus toanus var. scidulus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions, and it plays a role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.
