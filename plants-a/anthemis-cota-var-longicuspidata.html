Anthemis cota var. longicuspidata

Anthemis cota var. longicuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers with a distinctive, often aromatic scent. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands or rocky areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional remedies.
