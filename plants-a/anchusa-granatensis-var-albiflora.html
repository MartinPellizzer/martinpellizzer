Anchusa granatensis var. albiflora

Anchusa granatensis var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are predominantly white or pale blue. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
