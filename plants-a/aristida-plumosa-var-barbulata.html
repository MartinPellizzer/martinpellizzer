Aristida plumosa var. barbulata

Aristida plumosa var. barbulata, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, fluffy, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of a slender, spike-like cluster of small, greenish flowers.

It is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
