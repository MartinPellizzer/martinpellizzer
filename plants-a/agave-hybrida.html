Agave hybrida

Agave hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape, often with a bluish-green hue and sharp spines along the edges. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears clusters of small, white flowers, which are typically followed by the production of blue-gray berries. Agave hybrida is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and requiring minimal irrigation.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
