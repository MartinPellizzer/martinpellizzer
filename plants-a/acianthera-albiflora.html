Acianthera albiflora

Acianthera albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered acianthera, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

Acianthera albiflora is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
