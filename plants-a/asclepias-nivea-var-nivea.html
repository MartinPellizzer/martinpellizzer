Asclepias nivea var. nivea

Asclepias nivea var. nivea, commonly known as the white milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds.

This species is an important host plant for monarch butterflies and other pollinators.
