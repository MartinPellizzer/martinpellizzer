Alstroemeria sierrae

Alstroemeria sierrae, commonly known as the Sierra Alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are long, narrow, and evergreen, often arranged in a fan-like pattern.

Alstroemeria sierrae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
