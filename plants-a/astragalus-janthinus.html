Astragalus janthinus

Astragalus janthinus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus janthinus is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
