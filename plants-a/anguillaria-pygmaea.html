Anguillaria pygmaea

Anguillaria pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf anguillaria, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which includes plants such as philodendrons and calla lilies. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense undergrowth of tropical rainforests.

Due to its delicate nature, it requires careful cultivation to maintain its health and appearance.
