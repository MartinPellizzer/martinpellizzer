Allium ledschanense

Allium ledschanense, commonly known as the Armenian garlic, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Caucasus region and is characterized by its bulbous structure and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its aromatic bulbs, which are used in traditional cuisines.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
