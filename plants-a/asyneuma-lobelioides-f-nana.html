Asyneuma lobelioides f. nana

Asyneuma lobelioides f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf mistflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has a low-growing habit with finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a powdery bloom. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure makes it well adapted to humid, shaded environments.
