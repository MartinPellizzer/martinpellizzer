Acanthus longifolius var. hungaricus

Acanthus longifolius var. hungaricus, commonly known as the Hungarian acanthus, is a hardy perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces tall, spires of violet-blue flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and resilience make it a favored plant in both horticultural and ecological settings.
