Acacia pseudoadhaerens

Acacia pseudoadhaerens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has slender, often spiny stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. Its yellow flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, and it produces legume pods that contain seeds.

Acacia pseudoadhaerens is well-suited to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
