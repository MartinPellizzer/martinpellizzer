Arthropodium

Arthropodium, commonly known as the false spider-orchid, is a genus of flowering plants native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often showy floral structures. The plants typically have a short, stocky rhizome and produce clusters of small, delicate flowers. Arthropodium species are often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in coastal and inland regions.

Their botanical features make them an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
