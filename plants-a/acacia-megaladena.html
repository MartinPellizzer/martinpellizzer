Acacia megaladena

Acacia megaladena, commonly known as the large-flowered wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a fibrous coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

Acacia megaladena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
