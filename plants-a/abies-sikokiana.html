Abies sikokiana

Abies sikokiana, commonly known as the Japanese white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and its dense, dark green foliage. The needles are flat, soft, and have a slight bluish tinge, while the cones are typically short and cylindrical. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in forest ecosystems.
