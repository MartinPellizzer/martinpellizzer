Adenophora capillaris subsp. capillaris

Adenophora capillaris subsp.

capillaris, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

The plant features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in clusters, and are known for their delicate appearance.

It belongs to the legume family (Fabaceae) and has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil in its native environment.
