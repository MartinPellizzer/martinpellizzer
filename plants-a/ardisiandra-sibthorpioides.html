Ardisiandra sibthorpioides

Ardisiandra sibthorpioides, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines.
