Andropogon rufus var. glabrescens

Andropogon rufus var. glabrescens, commonly known as eastern wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant features slender, erect stems with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
