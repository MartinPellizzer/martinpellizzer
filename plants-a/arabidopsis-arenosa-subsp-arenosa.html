Arabidopsis arenosa subsp. arenosa

Arabidopsis arenosa subsp.

arenosa, commonly known as the desert Arabidopsis, is a species of flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky environments.

The plant has simple, linear leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. It is closely related to Arabidopsis thaliana, which is widely used in genetic research.

This subspecies is of interest to scientists due to its adaptability to harsh conditions and its potential role in studying plant stress responses.
