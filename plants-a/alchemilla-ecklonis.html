Alchemilla ecklonis

Alchemilla ecklonis, commonly known as the silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Alchemilla ecklonis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in gardens for its drought tolerance and aesthetic qualities.
