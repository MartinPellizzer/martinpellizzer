Astragalus tietziae

Astragalus tietziae, commonly known as the Tietz's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus tietziae plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its native habitat.
