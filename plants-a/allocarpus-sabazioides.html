Allocarpus sabazioides

Allocarpus sabazioides, commonly known as the Kashmir pine, is a coniferous tree native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cones that are typically reddish-brown in color. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cold climates and well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
