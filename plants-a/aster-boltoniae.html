Aster boltoniae

Aster boltoniae, commonly known as Bolton's aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, purple to blue-violet flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy as well.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
