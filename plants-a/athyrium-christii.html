Athyrium christii

Athyrium christii, commonly known as the Christ's fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Athyrium christii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
