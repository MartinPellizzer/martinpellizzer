Amaryllis tubispatha

Amaryllis tubispatha, commonly known as the red-tube amaryllis, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear large, showy flowers with vibrant red petals and a distinctive yellow-orange throat. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that are often adorned with a red tube, giving it its common name. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Amaryllis tubispatha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
