Amerorchis rotundifolia f. lineata

Amerorchis rotundifolia f. lineata, commonly known as the spotted purple orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple flowers with white or pink streaks, which give it its common name. The plant features broad, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its unique floral structure and coloration make it a notable ornamental plant in horticulture.
