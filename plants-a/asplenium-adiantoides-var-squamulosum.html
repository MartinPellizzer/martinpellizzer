Asplenium adiantoides var. squamulosum

Asplenium adiantoides var. squamulosum, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with dark brown, wiry rhizomes that help in anchoring and nutrient absorption. Its leaves are typically glossy and dark green, with a distinctive silvery underside that gives it its common name.

This species thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
