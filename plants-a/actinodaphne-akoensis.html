Actinodaphne akoensis

Actinodaphne akoensis, commonly known as the Akoensis Camphor Tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the tropical regions of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, smooth, yellow drupe that contains a single seed.

The tree is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
