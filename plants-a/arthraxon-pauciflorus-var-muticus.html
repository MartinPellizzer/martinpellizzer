Arthraxon pauciflorus var. muticus

Arthraxon pauciflorus var. muticus, commonly known as the silent leek, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture, and it produces spikelets that are clustered at the ends of the stems.

The variety 'muticus' is distinguished by its reduced flowering structure, giving it a more subdued appearance compared to other varieties.
