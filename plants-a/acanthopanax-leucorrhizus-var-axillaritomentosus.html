Acanthopanax leucorrhizus var. axillaritomentosus

Acanthopanax leucorrhizus var. axillaritomentosus, commonly known as the hairy-axil Acanthopanax, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and hairy glands along the leaf axils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, followed by red berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
