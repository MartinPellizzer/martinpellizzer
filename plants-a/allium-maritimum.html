Allium maritimum

Allium maritimum, commonly known as sea garlic, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It features a bulbous stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant is adapted to saline and well-drained soils, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats. Its bulbs are edible and have a pungent, garlic-like flavor.

Sea garlic is valued both for its culinary use and its role in traditional medicine.
