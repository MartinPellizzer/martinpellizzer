Amaranthus polychroa

Amaranthus polychroa, commonly known as the rainbow amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its striking, multicolored bracts that range from red to orange and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often lance-shaped and covered with a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss.

Amaranthus polychroa is cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in some traditional cuisines.
