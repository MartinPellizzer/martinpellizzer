Anemia vestita

Anemia vestita, commonly known as the naked anemone, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its simple, usually white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Anemia vestita prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical name reflects its distinctive appearance, with "vestita" meaning "cloaked" or "covered," likely referring to the hairy texture of its leaves.
