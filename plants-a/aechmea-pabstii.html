Aechmea pabstii

Aechmea pabstii, commonly known as the Pabst's bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Aechmea pabstii is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and long-lasting blooms.
