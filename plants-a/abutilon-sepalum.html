Abutilon sepalum

Abutilon sepalum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Abutilon sepalum is also known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to tolerate moderate drought conditions.
