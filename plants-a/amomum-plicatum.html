Amomum plicatum

Amomum plicatum, commonly known as black cardamom, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown, elongated fruits. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green flowers that develop into the highly fragrant seed pods used in cooking and traditional medicine. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often used as a wrapper for steamed rice in regional cuisines.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its unique flavor and medicinal properties.
