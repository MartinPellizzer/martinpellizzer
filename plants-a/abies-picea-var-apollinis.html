Abies picea var. apollinis

Abies picea var. apollinis, commonly known as the Apollo fir, is a variant of the balsam fir.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrowly conical shape and dense foliage. The needles are typically dark green with a slightly bluish tinge, and the cones are elongated and often remain attached to the branches for a long time. This variety is native to specific regions in Europe and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

It prefers cool, moist climates and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate zones.
