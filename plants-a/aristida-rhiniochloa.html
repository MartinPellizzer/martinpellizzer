Aristida rhiniochloa

Aristida rhiniochloa, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in open, dry habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in various ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in ecological restoration projects.
