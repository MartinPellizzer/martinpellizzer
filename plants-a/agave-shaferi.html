Agave shaferi

Agave shaferi, commonly known as the Shaver's agave, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial succulent with a rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a layer of white powder called farina. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with yellowish-green flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. It is adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular ornamental plant in desert gardens.
