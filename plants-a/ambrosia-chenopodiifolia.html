Ambrosia chenopodiifolia

Ambrosia chenopodiifolia, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Ambrosia chenopodiifolia is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh desert conditions.
