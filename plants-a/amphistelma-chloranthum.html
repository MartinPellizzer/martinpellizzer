Amphistelma chloranthum

Amphistelma chloranthum, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking yellow flowers with a star-shaped appearance, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
