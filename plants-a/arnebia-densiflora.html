Arnebia densiflora

Arnebia densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower bugloss, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a compact, low-growing structure with blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a notable species in its native habitat.
