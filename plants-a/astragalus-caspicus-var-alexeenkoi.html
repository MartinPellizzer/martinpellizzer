Astragalus caspicus var. alexeenkoi

Astragalus caspicus var. alexeenkoi, commonly known as the Caspian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in steppe and grassland habitats. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a source of forage for livestock.
