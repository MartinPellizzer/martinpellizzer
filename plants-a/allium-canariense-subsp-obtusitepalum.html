Allium canariense subsp. obtusitepalum

Allium canariense subsp.

obtusitepalum, commonly known as the Canary Islands leek, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its rosette of broad, fleshy, lanceolate leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The plant produces a tall, slender flowering stem topped with a dense cluster of small, white to pale purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to the mild, humid climate of its native habitat.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
