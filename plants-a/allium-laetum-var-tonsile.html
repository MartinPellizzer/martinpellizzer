Allium laetum var. tonsile

Allium laetum var. tonsile, commonly known as the purple garlic or violet garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species such as garlic and onions. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlicky flavor, often used in traditional cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its attractive, showy flowers.
