Atropis fendleriana

Atropis fendleriana, commonly known as the Fendler's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.
