Arctostaphylos bakeri subsp. bakeri

Arctostaphylos bakeri subsp.

bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's manzanita, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of California. It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and clusters of small, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild, wet winters and dry summers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
