Aster falcatus subsp. commutatus

Aster falcatus subsp.

commutatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a perennial herb native to North America. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flower heads that typically bloom in late summer.

The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower structure. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
