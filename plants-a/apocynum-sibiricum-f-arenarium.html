Apocynum sibiricum f. arenarium

Apocynum sibiricum f. arenarium, commonly known as the sandy apocynum, is a variant of the silky dogbane plant.

It is native to arid and sandy regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and southern Siberia. This plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically narrow and arranged oppositely along the stem.

It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with poor soil conditions.
