Astragalus labradoricus var. robbinsii

Astragalus labradoricus var. robbinsii, commonly known as Labrador milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy or loamy soils, often in open woodlands or along streambanks. Its compound leaves are pinnately arranged, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain Lepidoptera species.
