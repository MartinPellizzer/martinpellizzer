Amphoricarpos elegans

Amphoricarpos elegans, commonly known as the elegant sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
