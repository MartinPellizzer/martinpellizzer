Allium kondarinum

Allium kondarinum, commonly known as the Kondar onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its robust, onion-like bulbs and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in late summer, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and scientific study.
