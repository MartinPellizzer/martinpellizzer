Aspalathus albens

Aspalathus albens, commonly known as white bushveld pea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, with trifoliate leaves and pods that contain seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
