Acacia drepanolobium

Acacia drepanolobium, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow to golden yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in drought conditions. Its bark and thorns are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a vital role in soil stabilization and habitat provision.

The species is also known for its association with certain ant species, which live in the hollow thorns and protect the plant from herbivores.
