Asparagus ternifolius

Asparagus ternifolius, commonly known as the three-leaved asparagus, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fern-like, trifoliate leaves that are finely divided and often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Asparagus ternifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
