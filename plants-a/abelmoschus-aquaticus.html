Abelmoschus aquaticus

Abelmoschus aquaticus, commonly known as water hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in wet environments such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
