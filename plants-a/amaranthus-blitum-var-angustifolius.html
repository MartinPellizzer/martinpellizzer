Amaranthus blitum var. angustifolius

Amaranthus blitum var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaf amaranth, is an annual herb native to the Americas.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered with small, bristly hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, narrow, and have a slightly serrated edge, while the flowers are small and clustered in dense panicles. Its seeds are tiny, oblong, and often used as a grain in traditional cuisines.

This plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in dry conditions.
