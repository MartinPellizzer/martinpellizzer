Appendicula imbricata

Appendicula imbricata, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Appendicula imbricata is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
