Amaryllis belladonna var. maxima

Amaryllis belladonna var. maxima, commonly known as the giant belladonna lily, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its dramatic blooms and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
