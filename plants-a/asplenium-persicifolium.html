Asplenium persicifolium

Asplenium persicifolium, commonly known as the Persian spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with a silvery or pale green underside. Native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, this plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices or forest floors. Its rhizomes are short and creeping, allowing it to form dense, compact colonies.

The plant's unique foliage and adaptability make it a notable species in the study of epiphytic and lithophytic ferns.
