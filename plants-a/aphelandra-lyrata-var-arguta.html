Aphelandra lyrata var. arguta

Aphelandra lyrata var. arguta, commonly known as the velvetleaf stangeria, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces striking, pendulous flowers with bright yellow petals and red sepals, often blooming in the evening. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
