Anemone obtusiloba var. orthocaulon

Anemone obtusiloba var. orthocaulon, commonly known as the western anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
