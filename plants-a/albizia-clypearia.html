Albizia clypearia

Albizia clypearia, commonly known as the Indian white acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are often feathery in appearance, and its flowers are typically yellow or pale green, arranged in racemose inflorescences.

Albizia clypearia produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
