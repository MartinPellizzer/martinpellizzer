Agathosma craspedota

Agathosma craspedota, commonly known as the Cape aster, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
