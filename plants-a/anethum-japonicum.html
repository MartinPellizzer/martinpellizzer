Anethum japonicum

Anethum japonicum, commonly known as Japanese celery, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan.

It features slender, hollow stems and feather-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional cuisine. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are edible and have a mild, celery-like flavor.

Anethum japonicum thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
