Andropogon grayia

Andropogon grayia, commonly known as gray beardgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowering spikes are usually tall and branched, producing small, pale green to brown flowers.

Andropogon grayia plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
