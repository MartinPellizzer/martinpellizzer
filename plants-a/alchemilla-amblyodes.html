Alchemilla amblyodes

Alchemilla amblyodes, commonly known as lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in upright cymes. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy surface, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Alchemilla amblyodes thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
