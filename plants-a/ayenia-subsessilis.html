Ayenia subsessilis

Ayenia subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ayenia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ayenia subsessilis is typically found in open woodlands and prairies, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
