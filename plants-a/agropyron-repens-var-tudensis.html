Agropyron repens var. tudensis

Agropyron repens var. tudensis, commonly known as the Tuscan meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and fine, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both bisexual and male flowers. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
