Anacamptis pyramidalis subsp. urvilleana

Anacamptis pyramidalis subsp.

urvilleana, commonly known as the Urville's pyramidal orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of violet or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces a single spike of flowers, which are often arranged in a pyramidal shape, giving the species its common name.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
