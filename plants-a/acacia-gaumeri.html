Acacia gaumeri

Acacia gaumeri, commonly known as the Mexican acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which bloom in spring and summer. Acacia gaumeri is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
