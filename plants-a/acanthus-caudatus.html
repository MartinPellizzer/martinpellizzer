Acanthus caudatus

Acanthus caudatus, commonly known as the horse's tail or sea holly, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, deeply lobed leaves that resemble the tail of a horse, hence its common name. The plant produces spiny, violet or blue flower clusters that bloom in late summer. Its foliage is often used in classical architecture and garden design due to its dramatic appearance.

Acanthus caudatus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
