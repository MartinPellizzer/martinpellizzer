Alchornea triplinervia f. psilorhachis

Alchornea triplinervia f. psilorhachis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf alchornea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features simple, alternate leaves with a distinctive three-nerved venation pattern, contributing to its botanical uniqueness. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The species is characterized by its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
