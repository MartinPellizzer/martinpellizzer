Auganthus lactiflorus

Auganthus lactiflorus, commonly known as the milkflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has succulent-like leaves that store water, adapting to its arid environment. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
