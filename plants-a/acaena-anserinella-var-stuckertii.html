Acaena anserinella var. stuckertii

Acaena anserinella var. stuckertii, commonly known as the New Zealand thistle, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Due to its invasive nature, it is often considered a noxious weed in regions outside its native habitat.
