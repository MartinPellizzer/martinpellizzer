Anthyllis vulneraria var. villosa

Anthyllis vulneraria var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy stitchwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to tolerate salty and windy conditions.
