Aerides histrix

Aerides histrix, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its upright, branching stems and dense, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, hairy appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and collections.
