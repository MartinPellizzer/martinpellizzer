Aneilema acutifolium

Aneilema acutifolium, commonly known as the sharp-leaved aneilema, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features upright, slender stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a pointed tip, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Aneilema acutifolium thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.
