Astragalus zuvanticus

Astragalus zuvanticus, commonly known as the Zuvantik milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia, particularly found in Kazakhstan and southern Siberia. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of many Astragalus species. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
