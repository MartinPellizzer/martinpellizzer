Alchemilla bakurianica

Alchemilla bakurianica, commonly known as the Caucasian lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their supposed healing properties.

Alchemilla bakurianica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
