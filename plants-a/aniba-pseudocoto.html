Aniba pseudocoto

Aniba pseudocoto, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in understory layers of dense tropical forests.

Due to its ecological significance, it plays a vital role in maintaining biodiversity in its native habitat.
