Acer formosum f. roseomarginatum

Acer formosum f. roseomarginatum, commonly known as the pink-edged maple, is a unique variant of the formosum maple.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, with leaves that have a distinct pink or red margin. The plant is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. Its botanical features include palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and small, clustered flowers that appear in spring.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant autumn colors.
