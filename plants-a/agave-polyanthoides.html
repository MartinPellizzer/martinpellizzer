Agave polyanthoides

Agave polyanthoides, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a perennial plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, blue-green leaves with white marginal teeth. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, greenish-white flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Agave polyanthoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
