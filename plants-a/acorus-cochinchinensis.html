Acorus cochinchinensis

Acorus cochinchinensis, commonly known as the broad-leaf sweetflag, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, sword-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating on the water surface. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Aroid family. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Acorus cochinchinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
