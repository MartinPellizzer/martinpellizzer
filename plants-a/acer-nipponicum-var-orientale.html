Acer nipponicum var. orientale

Acer nipponicum var. orientale, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental foliage, which displays vibrant colors during the growing season and in autumn. The plant typically has a rounded canopy and produces small, red or yellow flowers in early spring.

Its leaves are palmately lobed, often with a serrated edge, and can vary in size and shape depending on the cultivar. This species is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
