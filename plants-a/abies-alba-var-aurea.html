Abies alba var. aurea

Abies alba var. aurea, commonly known as the golden fir, is a variant of the silver fir native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which contrasts with its dark green needles. This variety thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions. The tree has a conical shape with a dense crown, and its bark is typically gray and fissured.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture and its ability to adapt to various soil types.
