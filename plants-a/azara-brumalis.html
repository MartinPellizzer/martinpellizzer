Azara brumalis

Azara brumalis, commonly known as the winter azara, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that remain throughout the winter, providing year-round foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Azara brumalis is typically found in moist, acidic soils in woodland areas.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to shaded environments.
