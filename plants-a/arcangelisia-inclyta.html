Arcangelisia inclyta

Arcangelisia inclyta, commonly known as the red-veined maple, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, featuring leaves with deep red veins against a green background. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring, followed by winged seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
