Abdominea macranthera

Abdominea macranthera, commonly known as the large-flowered bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant belonging to the family Lentibacillaceae.

It is characterized by its inflated, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms, aiding in its nutrient acquisition. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and ditches. Its flowers are distinctive, featuring large, showy petals that attract pollinators.

Despite its name, it is not a true bladderwort but shares some morphological similarities with other aquatic carnivorous plants.
