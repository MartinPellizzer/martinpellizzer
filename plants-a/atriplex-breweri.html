Atriplex breweri

Atriplex breweri, commonly known as the Brewer's saltbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. Its salt-tolerant nature allows it to thrive in saline soils, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.

The plant also serves as a food source for various wildlife species, including birds and insects.
