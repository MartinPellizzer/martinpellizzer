Arisaema lobatum var. rosthornianum

Arisaema lobatum var. rosthornianum, commonly known as the Rostrhorn's arrowhead, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like China and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and an unusual, spadix-like inflorescence that resembles a twisted, fleshy spike. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a preference for moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
