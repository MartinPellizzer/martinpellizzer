Acacia seyal

Acacia seyal, commonly known as the desert thorn or Sahel acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the dry regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its long, straight thorns and bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is well adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater.

Acacia seyal plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert animals.
