Ammodendron karelinii var. conollyi

Ammodendron karelinii var. conollyi, commonly known as the Conolly's date palm, is a variety native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a member of the palm family (Arecaceae) and is characterized by its robust, multi-trunked structure and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces edible dates, although its fruiting habit is less consistent compared to other date palm varieties. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water once established.

Its resilience makes it a valuable plant for reforestation and land stabilization in desert environments.
