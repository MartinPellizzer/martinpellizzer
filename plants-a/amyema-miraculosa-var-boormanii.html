Amyema miraculosa var. boormanii

Amyema miraculosa var. boormanii, commonly known as the Boorman's bottlebrush, is a unique species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, crimson-red inflorescences that resemble bottlebrushes, which are composed of dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
