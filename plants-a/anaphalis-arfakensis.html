Anaphalis arfakensis

Anaphalis arfakensis, commonly known as the Armenian feathergrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single blooms. The plant produces fluffy, white to grayish inflorescences that are often mistaken for clouds or snow, giving it its common name. It thrives in high-altitude environments with well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Anaphalis arfakensis is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
