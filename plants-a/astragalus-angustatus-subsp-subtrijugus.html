Astragalus angustatus subsp. subtrijugus

Astragalus angustatus subsp.

subtrijugus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has compound leaves with three to five pairs of leaflets.

It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules.
