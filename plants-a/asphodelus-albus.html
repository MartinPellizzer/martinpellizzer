Asphodelus albus

Asphodelus albus, commonly known as the white asphodel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Iridaceae family and typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces tall, narrow leaves and tall, branched stems that bear clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in poor, well-drained conditions.

The white asphodel is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
