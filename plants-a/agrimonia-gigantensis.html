Agrimonia gigantensis

Agrimonia gigantensis, commonly known as giant agrimony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, dark-colored fruits that contain seeds. Agrimonia gigantensis is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
