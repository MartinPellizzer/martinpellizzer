Aulomyrcia tomentosa

Aulomyrcia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The leaves are typically narrow and opposite, with a pale green underside. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

This plant is well adapted to saline and sandy soils, making it a hardy species suitable for coastal gardens.
