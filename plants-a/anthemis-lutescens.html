Anthemis lutescens

Anthemis lutescens, commonly known as the yellow cudweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Anthemis lutescens is often used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
