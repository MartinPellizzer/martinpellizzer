Achnatherum saposhnikovii

Achnatherum saposhnikovii, commonly known as the Siberian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear fluffy seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.
