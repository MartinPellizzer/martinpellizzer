Aphyllon arizonicum

Aphyllon arizonicum, commonly known as the Arizona leafless milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its absence of leaves, with only stems and flowers present. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Aphyllon arizonicum is adapted to withstand drought conditions and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
