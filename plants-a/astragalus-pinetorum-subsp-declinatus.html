Astragalus pinetorum subsp. declinatus

Astragalus pinetorum subsp.

declinatus, commonly known as the declining pine vetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as pine forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
