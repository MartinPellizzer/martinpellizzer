Aristolochia dewevrei

Aristolochia dewevrei, commonly known as the African birthwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate flowers that resemble a pipe or a horn. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, musty odor, which attracts specific pollinators.

The plant contains aristolochic acids, which are toxic and have been linked to kidney failure in humans.
