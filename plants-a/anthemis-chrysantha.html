Anthemis chrysantha

Anthemis chrysantha, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The foliage is aromatic and can be used in herbal remedies for its mild antiseptic properties.
