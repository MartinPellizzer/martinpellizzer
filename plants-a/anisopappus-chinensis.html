Anisopappus chinensis

Anisopappus chinensis, commonly known as Chinese windmill grass, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, windmill-like seed heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is known for its drought tolerance. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the characteristic pappus structures that aid in seed dispersal.

Anisopappus chinensis is often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as an ornamental plant.
