Annamocarya sinensis

Annamocarya sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese silk tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces long, cylindrical pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
