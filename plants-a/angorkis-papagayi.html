Angorkis papagayi

Angorkis papagayi, commonly known as the Papagayi orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Angorkis papagayi is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
