Amaranthus blitoides var. reverchonii

Amaranthus blitoides var. reverchonii, commonly known as the Mexican lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy ornamental grass.

The species is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in arid environments.
