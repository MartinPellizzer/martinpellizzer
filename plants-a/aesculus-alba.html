Aesculus alba

Aesculus alba, commonly known as the white horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its palmately compound leaves, which are divided into five to seven oval-shaped leaflets. The tree produces large, showy white flowers in upright panicles, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a spiny, spherical capsule that contains several chestnut-like seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
