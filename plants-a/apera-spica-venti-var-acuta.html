Apera spica-venti var. acuta

Apera spica-venti var. acuta, commonly known as windgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found along roadsides, in fields, and in open woodland areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in some regions outside its native range.
