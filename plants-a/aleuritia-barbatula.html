Aleuritia barbatula

Aleuritia barbatula, commonly known as the silky dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy white bracts that surround small, yellowish flowers, which appear in late spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy undersides, giving it a distinctive texture. It typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that attracts birds and supports local wildlife.
