Acaena argentea var. longifolia

Acaena argentea var. longifolia, commonly known as silver weed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features silver-gray leaves with a dense covering of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by spiny, berry-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in regions outside its native range.
