Anthemis cretacea

Anthemis cretacea, commonly known as the Cretan Anthemis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Crete.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. Anthemis cretacea thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

It is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
