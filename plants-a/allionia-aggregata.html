Allionia aggregata

Allionia aggregata, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, blue-black berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.

Allionia aggregata is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife and its use in traditional medicine.
