Aristolochia cymbifera var. labiosa

Aristolochia cymbifera var. labiosa, commonly known as the broad-leaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and can be quite large, contributing to its ornamental value. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure that attracts specific pollinators.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
