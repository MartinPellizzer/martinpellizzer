Amygdalus papilio

Amygdalus papilio, commonly known as the Persian almond.

It is a deciduous tree native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the development of almond-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
