Anthericum cajamarcaense

Anthericum cajamarcaense, commonly known as the Peruvian lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Aroid family (Araceae) and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as cloud forests and high-altitude grasslands. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Anthericum cajamarcaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in some Andean communities.
