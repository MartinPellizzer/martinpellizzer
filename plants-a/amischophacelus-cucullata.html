Amischophacelus cucullata

Amischophacelus cucullata, commonly known as the hooded bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often with a distinctive hood-like structure over the throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cooler, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Amischophacelus cucullata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
