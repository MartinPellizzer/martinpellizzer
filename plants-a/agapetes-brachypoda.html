Agapetes brachypoda

Agapetes brachypoda, commonly known as the short-stemmed snowbell, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the temperate forests of South America, particularly in regions of Chile and Argentina. This evergreen shrub is characterized by its small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

Agapetes brachypoda thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in cloud forests at high altitudes.
