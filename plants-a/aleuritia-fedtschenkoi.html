Aleuritia fedtschenkoi

Aleuritia fedtschenkoi, commonly known as the Chinese snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
