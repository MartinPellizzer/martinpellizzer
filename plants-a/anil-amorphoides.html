Anil amorphoides

Anil amorphoides, commonly known as the Indian nettle or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is an annual herb that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is known for its soft, hairy texture, which gives it a velvety appearance. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in agricultural fields and disturbed habitats.

Anil amorphoides is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
