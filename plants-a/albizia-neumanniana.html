Albizia neumanniana

Albizia neumanniana, commonly known as the Neumann's silktree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties. It produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Albizia neumanniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
