Aeschynomene aquatica

Aeschynomene aquatica, commonly known as water hemp, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that are arranged in spikes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Aeschynomene aquatica is known for its ability to thrive in flooded conditions, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
