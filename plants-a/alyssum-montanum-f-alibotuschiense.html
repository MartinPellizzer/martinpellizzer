Alyssum montanum f. alibotuschiense

Alyssum montanum f. alibotuschiense, commonly known as the alpine alyssum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically produces white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and cool climates.

Its compact form and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
