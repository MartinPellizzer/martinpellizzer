Adonis vernalis var. parviflora

Adonis vernalis var. parviflora, commonly known as the spring adonis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are often surrounded by a crown of sepals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
