Allium sativum subsp. ophioscorodon

Allium sativum subsp.

ophioscorodon, commonly known as the Persian leek or wild leek, is a botanical subspecies native to the temperate regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It is a perennial herb characterized by its long, hollow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers.

The plant produces bulbous structures that are often smaller and more elongated compared to common garlic. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary uses and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
