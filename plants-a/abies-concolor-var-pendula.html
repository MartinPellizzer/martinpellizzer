Abies concolor var. pendula

Abies concolor var. pendula, commonly known as the white fir, is a variant of the Colorado fir native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its long, soft needles that are typically light green with a white underside. The plant produces cone-like structures that are typically 5 to 8 inches long, containing edible seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is noted for its graceful appearance and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree.
