Aeschynomene leptostachya

Aeschynomene leptostachya, commonly known as parviflora or small-flowered aeschynomene, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered white or pale blue flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grassy areas.

Aeschynomene leptostachya is also valued for its potential in forage and as a legume for nitrogen fixation.
