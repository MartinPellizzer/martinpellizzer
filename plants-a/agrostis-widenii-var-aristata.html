Agrostis widenii var. aristata

Agrostis widenii var. aristata, commonly known as the narrowleaf bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces spikelets that are usually arranged in dense, compact clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a component in native plant gardens.
