Acacia roemeriana

Acacia roemeriana, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange globular inflorescences, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Acacia roemeriana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of local ecosystems.
