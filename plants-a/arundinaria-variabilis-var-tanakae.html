Arundinaria variabilis var. tanakae

Arundinaria variabilis var. tanakae, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Tanaka's bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to several meters in height, with a rapid growth rate typical of many bamboo species. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green, providing a striking visual contrast against its woody stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic and structural qualities.
