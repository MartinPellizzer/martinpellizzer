Artemisia elegans

Artemisia elegans, commonly known as silver sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of narrow, aromatic leaves with a strong, pungent scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Artemisia elegans thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically for its medicinal properties.
