Allium ophiophyllum

Allium ophiophyllum, commonly known as the snake's garlic, is a unique plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, spherical bulbs and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale purple to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
