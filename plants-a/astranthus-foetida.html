Astranthus foetida

Astranthus foetida, commonly known as stinking ross, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiky yellow flowers. The plant produces small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its strong, unpleasant odor, it is sometimes referred to as the "smelly aster.".
