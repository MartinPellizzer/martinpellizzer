Acacia albescens

Acacia albescens, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and its distinctive, feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which appear in spring and are followed by flat, leathery seed pods. Acacia albescens thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
