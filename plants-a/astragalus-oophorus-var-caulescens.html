Astragalus oophorus var. caulescens

Astragalus oophorus var. caulescens, commonly known as the clustered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
