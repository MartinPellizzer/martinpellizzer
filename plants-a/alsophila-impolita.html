Alsophila impolita

Alsophila impolita, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a distinctive, elegant appearance.

Alsophila impolita is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
