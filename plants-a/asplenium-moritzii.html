Asplenium moritzii

Asplenium moritzii, commonly known as the Moritz's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sorus, which are typically located on the underside of the leaves. Native to regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species within its ecological niche.
