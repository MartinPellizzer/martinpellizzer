Amaranthus hybridus var. hecticus

Amaranthus hybridus var. hecticus, commonly known as red root amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with branched stems that often turn reddish as the plant matures. The leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy surface, and they range in color from green to reddish-purple. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or red, followed by oblong, three-angled seed capsules.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional culinary and medicinal practices.
