Anadendrum punctulatum

Anadendrum punctulatum, commonly known as the Texas buckeye, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain smooth, glossy seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
