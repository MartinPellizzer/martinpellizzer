Astragalus absconditus

Astragalus absconditus, commonly known as the hidden milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

Astragalus absconditus is often found in sagebrush steppe and other arid environments.
