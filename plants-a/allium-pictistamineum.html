Allium pictistamineum

Allium pictistamineum, commonly known as the painted leek, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking, ornamental leaves that are often variegated with white and green markings. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is appreciated by both gardeners and botanists.
