Aspalathus falcata

Aspalathus falcata, commonly known as the African green tea or rooibos, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. The plant is well adapted to the local climate, thriving in dry, sandy soils with minimal water requirements. Its leaves are harvested and processed to produce a popular herbal tea.

The plant's botanical features make it uniquely suited to its native habitat and contribute to its economic and cultural significance.
