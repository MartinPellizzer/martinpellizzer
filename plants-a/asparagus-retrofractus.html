Asparagus retrofractus

Asparagus retrofractus, commonly known as the backward asparagus, is a unique member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive growth habit, with stems that curve downward rather than upward, giving it a peculiar appearance. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typical of many asparagus species.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its unusual morphology and potential ecological role in specific habitats.
