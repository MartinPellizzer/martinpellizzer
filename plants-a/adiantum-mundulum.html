Adiantum mundulum

Adiantum mundulum, commonly known as the common maidenhair fern, is a delicate and graceful fern species native to various regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that resemble the feathers of a bird, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are typically bioluminescent, displaying a striking blue-green hue under certain light conditions.

Adiantum mundulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
