Amberboa maroccana

Amberboa maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan amberboa, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, also known as the ice plant family, and is characterized by its succulent leaves and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant thrives in hot, dry climates and is adapted to store water in its fleshy stems and leaves. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Amberboa maroccana is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
