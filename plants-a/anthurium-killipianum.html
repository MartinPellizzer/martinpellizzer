Anthurium killipianum

Anthurium killipianum, commonly known as the Killipianum arrowhead, is a tropical flowering plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive spathe and spadix inflorescence. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a velvety texture. It produces striking, spadix-shaped flowers that are usually red or orange, surrounded by a showy, petal-like spathe.

Anthurium killipianum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
