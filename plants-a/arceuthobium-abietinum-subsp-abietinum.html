Arceuthobium abietinum subsp. abietinum

Arceuthobium abietinum subsp.

abietinum, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on conifers such as fir and spruce. It lacks true leaves and chlorophyll, relying entirely on its host for nutrients.

The plant produces small, fleshy berries that contain seeds dispersed by birds. Its life cycle involves a complex relationship with its host, where it attaches to the host's bark using a haustorium.

This subspecies is notable for its unique method of seed dispersal, where the seed pods explosively detach and travel short distances.
