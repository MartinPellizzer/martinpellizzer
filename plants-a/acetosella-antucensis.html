Acetosella antucensis

Acetosella antucensis, commonly known as the Antuco spring beauty, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, adapted to the cold, high-altitude climate.

Acetosella antucensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
