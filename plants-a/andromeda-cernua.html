Andromeda cernua

Andromeda cernua, commonly known as the wintergreen shrub, is a deciduous evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, dark green, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds.

Andromeda cernua is notable for its ability to thrive in acidic, moist soils and its role in supporting local wildlife.
