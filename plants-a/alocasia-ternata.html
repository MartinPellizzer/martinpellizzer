Alocasia ternata

Alocasia ternata, commonly known as the elephant's ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental plants like calla lilies. Its foliage is often variegated with shades of green, white, and yellow, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.

Alocasia ternata thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
