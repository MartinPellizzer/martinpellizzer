Acianthera sarracenia

Acianthera sarracenia, commonly known as the purple pitcher plant, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers and its unique inflorescence that resembles a pitcher, which is a feature shared with the genus Sarracenia. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Acianthera sarracenia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
