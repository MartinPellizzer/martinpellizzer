Acacia minutifolia

Acacia minutifolia, commonly known as the dwarf wattle, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tiny, yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has thin, wiry stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Acacia minutifolia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its compact size and attractive floral display.
