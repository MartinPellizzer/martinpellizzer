Acoridium parvulum

Acoridium parvulum, commonly known as the small acoridium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Acoraceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in wet, marshy environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found in swampy or aquatic habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
