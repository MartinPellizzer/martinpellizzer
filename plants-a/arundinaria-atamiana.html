Arundinaria atamiana

Arundinaria atamiana, commonly known as the Atamian bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is known for its rapid growth rate. Its culms are strong and flexible, making it useful in traditional crafts and construction.

Arundinaria atamiana plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
