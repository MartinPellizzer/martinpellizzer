Astragalus toanus

Astragalus toanus, commonly known as the Toano milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
