Aconitum hisautii

Aconitum hisautii, commonly known as the Swiss aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes along the stem. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Aconitum hisautii contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
