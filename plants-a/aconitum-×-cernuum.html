Aconitum × cernuum

Aconitum × cernuum, commonly known as the western monkshood, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features large, palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in a fine pubescence. Native to the mountainous regions of western North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Aconitum × cernuum is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its dramatic floral display.
