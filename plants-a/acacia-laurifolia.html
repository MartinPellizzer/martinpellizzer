Acacia laurifolia

Acacia laurifolia, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of yellow, spherical flowers. The plant is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
