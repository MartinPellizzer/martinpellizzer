Asphodelus liburnicus

Asphodelus liburnicus, commonly known as the Liburnian asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and tall spikes of white or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It is often found in coastal areas and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

The Liburnian asphodel is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
