Autonoe iridifolia

Autonoe iridifolia, commonly known as the blue-leaved orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue-green foliage, which is adorned with intricate patterns resembling veins. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale blue, often during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare flora.
