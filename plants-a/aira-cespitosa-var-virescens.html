Aira cespitosa var. virescens

Aira cespitosa var. virescens, commonly known as green tufted grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. The plant forms dense, tufted clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often a vibrant green color. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, spikelet-bearing branches that are usually green to reddish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
