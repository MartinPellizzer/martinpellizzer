Aerides paniculata

Aerides paniculata, commonly known as the orchid of the genus Aerides, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters, showcasing a range of colors from white to pink and yellow. The plant features long, arching inflorescences with numerous flowers, making it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Aerides paniculata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
