Anemone atragene

Anemone atragene, commonly known as the purple anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, violet to purple petals that surround a central cluster of yellow stamens. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a dark green color and a slightly hairy texture.

Anemone atragene is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
