Actinea acaulis var. septentrionalis

Actinea acaulis var. septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern starwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its clustered, star-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are clustered in a way that resembles a star, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported soothing properties.
