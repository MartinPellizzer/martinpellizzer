Avena elatior var. subhirsuta

Avena elatior var. subhirsuta, commonly known as the hairy oat, is a variant of the slender oat species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other oat varieties. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

Botanically, it is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for forage or ornamental purposes.
