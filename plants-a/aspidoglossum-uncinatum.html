Aspidoglossum uncinatum

Aspidoglossum uncinatum, commonly known as the hooked tongue plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its upright, branching stems and fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and indoor gardening.
