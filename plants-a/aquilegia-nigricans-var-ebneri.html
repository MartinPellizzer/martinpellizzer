Aquilegia nigricans var. ebneri

Aquilegia nigricans var. ebneri, commonly known as the Black Nightshade Aquilegia, is a striking perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, spurred flowers that range in color from deep red to violet, often with a contrasting black or dark purple base. The plant has compound leaves with finely divided foliage, and its stems are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in woodland or shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

The flowers bloom in early to mid-summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
