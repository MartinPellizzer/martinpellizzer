Amorpha fruticosa var. humilis

Amorpha fruticosa var. humilis, commonly known as the yellow indigo bush, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, multi-branched stems covered in small, yellowish-green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. These flowers are usually a soft yellow or cream color, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a drought-tolerant choice for native landscapes.
