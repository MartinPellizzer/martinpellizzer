Anthemis glareosa

Anthemis glareosa, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves and is often used in ornamental gardening for its long-lasting flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
