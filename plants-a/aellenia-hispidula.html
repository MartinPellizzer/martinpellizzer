Aellenia hispidula

Aellenia hispidula, commonly known as the roughleaf senna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are pinnately compound and covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture.

The species is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities and ecological importance in arid regions.
