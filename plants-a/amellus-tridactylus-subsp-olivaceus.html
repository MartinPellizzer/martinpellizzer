Amellus tridactylus subsp. olivaceus

Amellus tridactylus subsp.

olivaceus, commonly known as the olive-leafed amellus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Apiaceae. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and finely divided, aromatic leaves that resemble those of other members of its family.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. It is native to parts of southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.
