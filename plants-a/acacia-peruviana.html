Acacia peruviana

Acacia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian acacia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its woody, fissured bark and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow or orange globular inflorescences, which are composed of numerous small, clustered flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Acacia peruviana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid environments.
