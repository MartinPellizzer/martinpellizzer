Anoectochilus clarkei

Anoectochilus clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's slipper orchid, is a rare and highly sought-after orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique slipper-shaped pouch, which is a specialized structure used to trap insects for nutrient absorption. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

Due to its rarity and horticultural value, it is often cultivated in specialized orchid collections.
