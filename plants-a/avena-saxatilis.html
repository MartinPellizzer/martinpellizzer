Avena saxatilis

Avena saxatilis, commonly known as the rock barley, is a perennial grass species native to rocky and arid environments.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. It is adapted to survive in poor soils and harsh conditions, making it a hardy plant.

Avena saxatilis is often found in mountainous regions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
