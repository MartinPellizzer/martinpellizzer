Aerides elongata

Aerides elongata, commonly known as the long-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Aerides elongata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
