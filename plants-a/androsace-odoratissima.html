Androsace odoratissima

Androsace odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features a low-growing rosette of hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically blooming in early spring. Its flowers are borne on slender stems and have five petals with a distinctive, sweet scent.

Androsace odoratissima thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or alpine environments.
