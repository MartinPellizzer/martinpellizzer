Arnica humilis

Arnica humilis, commonly known as lesser arnica, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features hairy, basal leaves that are often spoon-shaped and arranged in a rosette. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with prominent yellow centers, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae).
