Astragalus pyramidulus

Astragalus pyramidulus, commonly known as the pyramid milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spindly growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. Its leaves are typically narrow and trifoliate, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Astragalus pyramidulus is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
