Atriplex gmelinii subsp. dilatata

Atriplex gmelinii subsp.

dilatata, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It has a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers. Atriplex gmelinii subsp.

dilatata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and tolerate saline conditions.
