Alstonia annamensis

Alstonia annamensis, commonly known as the Annam tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Alstonia annamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
