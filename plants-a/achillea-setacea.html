Achillea setacea

Achillea setacea, commonly known as the silky yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely hairy, gray-green leaves and flat, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Achillea setacea is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
