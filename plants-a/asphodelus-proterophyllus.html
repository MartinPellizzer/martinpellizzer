Asphodelus proterophyllus

Asphodelus proterophyllus, commonly known as the early asphodel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Asphodelus proterophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
