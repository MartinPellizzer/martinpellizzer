Alstroemeria ciliata

Alstroemeria ciliata, commonly known as the velvet tassel flower, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that resemble tassels. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, yellow, and pink, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Alstroemeria ciliata is also valued for its ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions, though it prefers well-drained, fertile ground.
