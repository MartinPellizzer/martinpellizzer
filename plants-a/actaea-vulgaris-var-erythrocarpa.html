Actaea vulgaris var. erythrocarpa

Actaea vulgaris var. erythrocarpa, commonly known as red-fruited bugbane, is a variant of the common bugbane plant.

It is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically found in moist woodlands and shaded areas. The plant features tall, unbranched stems with large, compound leaves that are pinnately arranged. Its most distinctive characteristic is the bright red berries it produces, which contrast sharply with its dark green foliage.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
