Annona scandens var. polychyla

Annona scandens var. polychyla, commonly known as the red-veined soursop, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces large, fragrant, greenish-yellow fruits that turn orange when ripe, containing a white, creamy pulp and numerous black seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The plant's growth habit includes climbing or spreading branches, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
