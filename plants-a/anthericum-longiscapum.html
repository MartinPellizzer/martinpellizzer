Anthericum longiscapum

Anthericum longiscapum, commonly known as the long-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
