Anisantha madritensis subsp. delilei

Anisantha madritensis subsp.

delilei, commonly known as the Delile's meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.

This subspecies is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in maintaining biodiversity in its native range.
