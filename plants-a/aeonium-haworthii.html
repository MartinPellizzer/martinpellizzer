Aeonium haworthii

Aeonium haworthii, commonly known as the "Chicken's Egg Plant," is a succulent belonging to the Crassulaceae family.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its rosette-forming structure composed of thick, fleshy, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the spring or summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Aeonium haworthii is also known for its ability to propagate through offsets, which can be easily removed and replanted.
