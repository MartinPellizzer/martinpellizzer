Augusta longifolia

Augusta longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as sandy soils and pine forests. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Augusta longifolia plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native ecosystem.
