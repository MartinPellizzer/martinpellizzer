Angraecopsis decipiens

Angraecopsis decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its unique morphology, featuring long, slender petals that resemble wasp-like structures. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Angraecopsis decipiens is also notable for its ability to attract specific pollinators through mimicry.
