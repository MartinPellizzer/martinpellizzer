Albuca foetida

Albuca foetida, commonly known as the stink lily, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its strong, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable when the flowers open. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Albuca foetida is often grown as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and resilience.
