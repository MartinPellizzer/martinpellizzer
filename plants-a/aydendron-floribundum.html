Aydendron floribundum

Aydendron floribundum, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Aydendron floribundum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
