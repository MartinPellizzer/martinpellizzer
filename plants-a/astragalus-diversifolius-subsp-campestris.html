Astragalus diversifolius subsp. campestris

Astragalus diversifolius subsp.

campestris, commonly known as the field milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the grasslands of central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown.

The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its root system is often extensive and deep. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as steppe and meadow environments.

This species is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for grazing animals.
