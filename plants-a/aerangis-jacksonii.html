Aerangis jacksonii

Aerangis jacksonii, commonly known as the Jackson's orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant blooms. The plant typically produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a distinctive lip petal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Aerangis jacksonii is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
