Agrostis alba subsp. verticillata

Agrostis alba subsp.

verticillata, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fluffy, white or silver-tipped inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its overall texture and appearance. Agrostis alba subsp.

verticillata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
