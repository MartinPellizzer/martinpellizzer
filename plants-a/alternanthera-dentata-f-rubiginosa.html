Alternanthera dentata f. rubiginosa

Alternanthera dentata f. rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-leaf alternanthera, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or crimson foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its green stems. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in aquatic and terrestrial gardens.

This variety is particularly popular in water gardens due to its ability to float or be rooted in shallow water.
