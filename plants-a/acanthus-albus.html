Acanthus albus

Acanthus albus, commonly known as the white acanthus, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features striking, palmate leaves with a waxy texture and a distinctive silvery-white underside. The plant produces spiky, lavender-blue flower spikes that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its architectural form makes it a popular choice in ornamental gardening and historical architecture.

Acanthus albus is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in classical art and design for centuries.
