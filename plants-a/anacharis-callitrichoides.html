Anacharis callitrichoides

Anacharis callitrichoides, commonly known as water wisteria, is a freshwater plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

Anacharis callitrichoides is widely used in aquariums due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its rapid growth rate.
