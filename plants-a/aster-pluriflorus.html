Aster pluriflorus

Aster pluriflorus, commonly known as the common aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features showy purple or blue flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Aster pluriflorus is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides, where it plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
