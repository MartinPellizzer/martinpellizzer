Aglaia pinnata

Aglaia pinnata, commonly known as the white aglaia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Aglaia pinnata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
