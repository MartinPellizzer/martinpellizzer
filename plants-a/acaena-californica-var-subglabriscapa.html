Acaena californica var. subglabriscapa

Acaena californica var. subglabriscapa, commonly known as the California burrweed, is a low-growing shrub native to coastal regions of California.

It features small, spiny fruits that are adapted for seed dispersal by animals. The plant has alternate, narrow leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrub habitats.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
