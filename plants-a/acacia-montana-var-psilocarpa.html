Acacia montana var. psilocarpa

Acacia montana var. psilocarpa, commonly known as the sacred acacia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod, and the species is notable for its association with traditional spiritual practices.

Due to its ecological significance and cultural importance, it is often protected in its natural habitats.
