Adiantum hallieri

Adiantum hallieri, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-green fronds that give it a distinctive, almost ethereal appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories where it can receive indirect light. Its fronds are biennials, meaning they live for two years before dying, with new fronds emerging from the base each growing season.

Adiantum hallieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
