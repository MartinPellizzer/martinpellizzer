Abutilon pilosum

Abutilon pilosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or fuzzy abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, hairy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its fibrous roots and woody stems make it a resilient species often found in disturbed soils.

Abutilon pilosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
