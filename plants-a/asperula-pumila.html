Asperula pumila

Asperula pumila, commonly known as the dwarf woodbine, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, dark green leaves that are typically covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Asperula pumila is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
