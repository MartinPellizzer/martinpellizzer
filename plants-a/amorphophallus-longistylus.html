Amorphophallus longistylus

Amorphophallus longistylus, commonly known as the giant tailed arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces a large, fleshy tuber that serves as its storage organ, which can grow up to several feet in length. Its unique morphology, including the elongated stamen, contributes to its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
