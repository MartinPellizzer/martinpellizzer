Aerva lanata var. elegans

Aerva lanata var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
