Aureolaria grandiflora subsp. pulchra

Aureolaria grandiflora subsp.

pulchra, commonly known as the showy aureolaria, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a fuzzy texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate.
