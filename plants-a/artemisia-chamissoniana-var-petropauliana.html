Artemisia chamissoniana var. petropauliana

Artemisia chamissoniana var. petropauliana, commonly known as the PetroPaula Artemisia, is a shrub native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in autumn. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its strong scent and potential medicinal uses.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
