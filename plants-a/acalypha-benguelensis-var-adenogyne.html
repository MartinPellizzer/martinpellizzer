Acalypha benguelensis var. adenogyne

Acalypha benguelensis var. adenogyne, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-rooted ironweed, is a shrub native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that range from red to purple, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often covered in a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
