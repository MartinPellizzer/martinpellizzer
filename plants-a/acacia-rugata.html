Acacia rugata

Acacia rugata, commonly known as the rough acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, compound leaves that are covered in fine, branched hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods. Acacia rugata thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in coastal and inland regions.

It is well adapted to withstand drought and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts due to its hardy nature.
