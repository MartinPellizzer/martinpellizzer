Atriplex sesseana

Atriplex sesseana, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes. Atriplex sesseana is highly salt-tolerant and thrives in soils with high salinity.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
