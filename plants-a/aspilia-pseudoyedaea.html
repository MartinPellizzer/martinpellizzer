Aspilia pseudoyedaea

Aspilia pseudoyedaea, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky soils, preferring full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Aspilia pseudoyedaea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
