Aspalathus vacciniifolia

Aspalathus vacciniifolia, commonly known as the red bushwillow, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for dry climates.

The botanical features of Aspalathus vacciniifolia make it a hardy and ornamental plant often used in landscaping.
