Artemisia insipida

Artemisia insipida, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-green foliage. The plant contains volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its strong, bitter scent. Artemisia insipida typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as prairies and rocky soils.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
