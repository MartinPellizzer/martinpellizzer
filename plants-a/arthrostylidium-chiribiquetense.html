Arthrostylidium chiribiquetense

Arthrostylidium chiribiquetense, commonly known as the Chiribiquet flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is adapted to low-nutrient soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
