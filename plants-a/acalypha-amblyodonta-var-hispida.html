Acalypha amblyodonta var. hispida

Acalypha amblyodonta var. hispida, commonly known as the roughleaf ironwood, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, rough-textured leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
