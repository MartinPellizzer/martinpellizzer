Asperula aristata f. glabra

Asperula aristata f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth asperula, is a variant of the asperula species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

This plant is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other forms of Asperula. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The flowers are usually small, white or pale pink, and appear in clusters, adding ornamental value to the plant.

It is a low-growing perennial that is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
