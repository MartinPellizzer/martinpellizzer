Alsophila katoi

Alsophila katoi, commonly known as the Kato's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a fibrous, hairy layer. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, giving it a distinctive, elegant appearance. Alsophila katoi thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its slow growth rate and unique morphology make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
