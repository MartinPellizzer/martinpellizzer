Adenia lindiensis var. submarginalis

Adenia lindiensis var. submarginalis, commonly known as the Zambesi adenium, is a unique shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with red stamens, which bloom in summer. Its root system is notably robust, allowing it to thrive in arid or semi-arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging environments.
