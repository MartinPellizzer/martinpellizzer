Avena sativa var. mutica

Avena sativa var. mutica, commonly known as the curly oat, is a variant of the common oat species characterized by its distinctive curling leaves.

This plant is native to temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils with adequate sunlight. Its foliage is often more compact and twisted compared to standard oat varieties, which can make it visually unique. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family.

It is primarily cultivated for its nutritional value, though its ornamental features also make it a popular choice for garden use.
