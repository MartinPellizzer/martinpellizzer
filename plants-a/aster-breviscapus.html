Aster breviscapus

Aster breviscapus, commonly known as the short-stemmed aster, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its small, violet-blue flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Aster breviscapus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
