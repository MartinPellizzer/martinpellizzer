Aesculus × mutabilis var. harbisonii

Aesculus × mutabilis var. harbisonii, commonly known as the Harbison's horse chestnut, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring, often with a subtle fragrance. The tree produces large, spiny husks containing one to three glossy, chestnut-like seeds. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are ovate and serrated.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
