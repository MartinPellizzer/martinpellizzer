Amerimnon pinnatum

Amerimnon pinnatum, commonly known as the Brazilian fern, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to three meters. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are bioluminescent, emitting a faint glow at night, which aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

This unique feature makes it a fascinating subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
