Angraecum rectum

Angraecum rectum, commonly known as the straight angraecum, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, slender pseudobulbs and dark green, narrow leaves. The plant produces striking white flowers with long, slender spurs, which are adapted for pollination by specific moths. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Angraecum rectum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
