Adenandra umbellata

Adenandra umbellata, commonly known as the white umbrella tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in umbellate inflorescences. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Adenandra umbellata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
