Alstroemeria pulchella

Alstroemeria pulchella, commonly known as the Peruvian lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features narrow, strap-like leaves. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant colors and long-lasting blooms.

Alstroemeria pulchella prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
