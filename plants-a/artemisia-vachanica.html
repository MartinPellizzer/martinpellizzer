Artemisia vachanica

Artemisia vachanica, commonly known as Indian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas and parts of southern Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in summer. The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in temperate climates. It contains volatile oils and is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and digestive properties.

Its essential oils are also utilized in aromatherapy and as a natural insect repellent.
