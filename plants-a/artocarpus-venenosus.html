Artocarpus venenosus

Artocarpus venenosus, commonly known as the poison fruit or bitter breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and unique, spiky fruit structure. The fruit contains a toxic latex that can cause skin irritation and is generally not consumed by humans. The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
