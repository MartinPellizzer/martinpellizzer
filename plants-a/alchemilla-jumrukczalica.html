Alchemilla jumrukczalica

Alchemilla jumrukczalica, commonly known as the Armenian lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicle clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Alchemilla jumrukczalica is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in its native regions.
