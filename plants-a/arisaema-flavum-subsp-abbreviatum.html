Arisaema flavum subsp. abbreviatum

Arisaema flavum subsp.

abbreviatum, commonly known as the yellow arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and showy spadix inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers are usually yellow or green, and it produces berries that are edible when ripe.

The subspecies is notable for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in horticulture.
