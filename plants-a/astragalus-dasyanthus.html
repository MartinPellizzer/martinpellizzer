Astragalus dasyanthus

Astragalus dasyanthus, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and steppe habitats.

Astragalus dasyanthus plays a role in soil stabilization and is part of the widespread genus Astragalus, which includes many species with medicinal and ecological significance.
