Aluta aspera subsp. aspera

Aluta aspera subsp.

aspera, commonly known as the rough aluta, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant features slender, branching stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in loose clusters, and bloom in the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
