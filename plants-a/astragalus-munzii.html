Astragalus munzii

Astragalus munzii, commonly known as Munz's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and plains. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus munzii is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
