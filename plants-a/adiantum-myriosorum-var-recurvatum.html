Adiantum myriosorum var. recurvatum

Adiantum myriosorum var. recurvatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds.

It features small, bright green leaves with a distinctive, slender shape that gives it a graceful appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
