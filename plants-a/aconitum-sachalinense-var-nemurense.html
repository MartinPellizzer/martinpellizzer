Aconitum sachalinense var. nemurense

Aconitum sachalinense var. nemurense, commonly known as the Sakhalin aconite, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Far East of Russia and parts of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces egg-shaped, yellowish-green berries that mature in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and are covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or alpine environments.
