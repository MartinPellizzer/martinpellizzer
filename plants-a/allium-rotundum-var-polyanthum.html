Allium rotundum var. polyanthum

Allium rotundum var. polyanthum, commonly known as the Persian onion or globe onion, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features bulbous stems that produce clusters of small, spherical, pink to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive addition to both gardens and culinary applications.
