Agianthus bernardinus

Agianthus bernardinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, velvety hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces striking, spiky inflorescences that can reach several feet in height, bearing small, greenish-white flowers. Its rosette growth habit and ability to store water in its leaves make it well adapted to drought conditions.

Agianthus bernardinus is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
