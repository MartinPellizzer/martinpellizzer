Ariocarpus furfuraceus f. cristata

Ariocarpus furfuraceus f. cristata, commonly known as the cristate prickly pear, is a unique cactus species characterized by its flattened, leaf-like stems that form a rosette shape.

Native to the arid regions of Mexico, this plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and requires minimal water. Its stems are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs that help reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is a sought-after specimen among cactus enthusiasts and conservationists.
