Alsinopsis palustris

Alsinopsis palustris, commonly known as the marsh alsinopsis, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and swampy areas. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as insects. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a fibrous root system adapted to waterlogged conditions.

Alsinopsis palustris plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic life.
