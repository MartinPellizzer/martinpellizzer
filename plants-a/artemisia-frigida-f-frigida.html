Artemisia frigida f. frigida

Artemisia frigida f. frigida, commonly known as plains wormwood, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which consists of narrow, aromatic leaves with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters called panicles. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and steppe habitats.

Artemisia frigida f. frigida is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
