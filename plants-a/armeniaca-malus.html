Armeniaca malus

Armeniaca malus, commonly known as the Chinese date or jujube, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It produces oval, reddish-brown fruits that are rich in nutrients, including sugars, vitamins, and minerals. The plant has simple, alternate leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Armeniaca malus is well adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a resilient and valuable crop.
