Allium curtum var. negevense

Allium curtum var. negevense, commonly known as the Negeven onion, is a botanical variant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a perennial bulbous plant characterized by its small, rounded bulbs and grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and delicate floral display.
