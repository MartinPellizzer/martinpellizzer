Astracantha oleifolia

Astracantha oleifolia, commonly known as the oilleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a waxy coating, giving them a distinctive oily appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscapes.

Astracantha oleifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
