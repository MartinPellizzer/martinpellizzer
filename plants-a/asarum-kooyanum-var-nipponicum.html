Asarum kooyanum var. nipponicum

Asarum kooyanum var. nipponicum, commonly known as Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, hooded flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, though the plant contains toxic compounds.

The species is considered rare and is protected in some areas due to habitat loss.
