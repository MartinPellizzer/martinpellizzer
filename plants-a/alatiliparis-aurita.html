Alatiliparis aurita

Alatiliparis aurita, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically golden yellow or orange in color, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Alatiliparis aurita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
