Astragalus krylovii

Astragalus krylovii, commonly known as Krylov's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces pods containing several seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.

Astragalus krylovii is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
