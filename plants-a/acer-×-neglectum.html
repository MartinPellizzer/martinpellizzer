Acer × neglectum

Acer × neglectum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Sapindaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly its vibrant autumn foliage. The tree typically grows to a height of 6–12 meters and features a spreading, rounded crown. Its leaves are palmately lobed, often with a red or orange tinge during the fall season.

Acer × neglectum is well-suited for gardens and landscapes due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
