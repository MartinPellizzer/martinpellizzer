Aleuritia auriculata

Aleuritia auriculata, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically in late spring. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while the bark is smooth and grayish in color. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy blooms and attractive foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden landscapes.
