Aconitum kirinense var. kirinense

Aconitum kirinense var. kirinense, commonly known as the Kirin Aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in a single, terminal raceme. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a coarse texture and a distinctive, waxy appearance.

The plant contains potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic and requiring careful handling.
