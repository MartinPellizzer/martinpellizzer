Acacia cracentis

Acacia cracentis, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical heads, which appear in spring. Acacia cracentis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
