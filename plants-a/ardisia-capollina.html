Ardisia capollina

Ardisia capollina, commonly known as the Christmas berry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, adding to its ornamental value.
