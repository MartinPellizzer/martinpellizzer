Anthericum weberbaueri

Anthericum weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense colonies. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a short, stout stem that supports its floral display.

Anthericum weberbaueri is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.
