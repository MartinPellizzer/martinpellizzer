Amerimnon paniculatum

Amerimnon paniculatum, commonly known as the red panic grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, feathery inflorescences. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically red or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes make it both a valuable and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
