Allium bulgaricum

Allium bulgaricum, commonly known as the Bulgarian leek, is a perennial plant native to the Balkans.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used as a vegetable in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it both an ornamental and edible plant with cultural significance in its native region.
