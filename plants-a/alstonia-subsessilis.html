Alstonia subsessilis

Alstonia subsessilis, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its canopy. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental species.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
