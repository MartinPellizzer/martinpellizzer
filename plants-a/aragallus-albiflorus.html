Aragallus albiflorus

Aragallus albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered aragallus, is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its herbaceous growth habit.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant features slender stems and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
