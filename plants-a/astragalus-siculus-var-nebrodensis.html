Astragalus siculus var. nebrodensis

Astragalus siculus var. nebrodensis, commonly known as the Sicilian milkvetch, is a rare flowering plant native to southern Italy.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique genetic traits.
