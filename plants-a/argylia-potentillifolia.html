Argylia potentillifolia

Argylia potentillifolia, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows at high elevations. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Argylia potentillifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine gardens.
