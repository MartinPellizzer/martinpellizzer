Astragalus regelii

Astragalus regelii, commonly known as Regel's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has small, rounded seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across open landscapes.

Astragalus regelii is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
