Alchemilla perglabra

Alchemilla perglabra, commonly known as the broadleaf lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in summer, clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their astringent properties.

Alchemilla perglabra thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
