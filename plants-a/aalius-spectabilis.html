Aalius spectabilis

Aalius spectabilis, commonly known as the showy aalius, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Aalius spectabilis is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and ease of care.
