Arundo halleri

Arundo halleri, commonly known as the Haller's reed, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to yellowish flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources such as streams and wetlands.

Arundo halleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for landscaping purposes.
