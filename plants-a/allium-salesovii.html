Allium salesovii

Allium salesovii, commonly known as the Armenian chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high mountain regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces bulbous structures known as bulbs, which are used for propagation and culinary purposes. Its leaves are often used as a herb, similar to chives, and it thrives in well-drained soils in temperate climates.

Allium salesovii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
