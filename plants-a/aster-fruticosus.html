Aster fruticosus

Aster fruticosus, commonly known as rough blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Aster fruticosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
