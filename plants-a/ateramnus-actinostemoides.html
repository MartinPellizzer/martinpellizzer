Ateramnus actinostemoides

Ateramnus actinostemoides, commonly known as the star-stemmed ateramnus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped stems, which are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Ateramnus actinostemoides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
