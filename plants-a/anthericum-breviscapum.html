Anthericum breviscapum

Anthericum breviscapum, commonly known as the short-capped arum, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features a short, stout rhizome and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface, often with a reddish or purple tinge on the underside.

Anthericum breviscapum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.
