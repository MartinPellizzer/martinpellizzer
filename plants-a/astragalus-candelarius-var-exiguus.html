Astragalus candelarius var. exiguus

Astragalus candelarius var. exiguus, commonly known as the small-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces cylindrical seed pods that split open to release its tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
