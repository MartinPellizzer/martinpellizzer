Aconitum molle

Aconitum molle, commonly known as the soft aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with lobes that are often hairy and somewhat soft to the touch.

Aconitum molle is part of the Ranunculaceae family and contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
