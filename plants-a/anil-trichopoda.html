Anil trichopoda

Anil trichopoda, commonly known as the woolly tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive fragrance.
