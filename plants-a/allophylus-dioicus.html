Allophylus dioicus

Allophylus dioicus, commonly known as the two-colored evergreen, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on different plants, making it dioecious.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
