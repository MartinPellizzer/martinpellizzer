Avena septentrionalis

Avena septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern oat, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. Avena septentrionalis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
