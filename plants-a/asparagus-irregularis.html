Asparagus irregularis

Asparagus irregularis, commonly known as the irregular asparagus, is a unique member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, irregularly shaped leaves and a compact growth habit. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in dry, rocky environments. Its foliage is often gray-green, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Asparagus irregularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
