Amaranthus induratus

Amaranthus induratus, commonly known as rough amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lanceolate, often hairy leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish to reddish flowers that develop into three-angled, spiny capsules. Its seeds are small, hard, and typically reddish or brown in color.

Amaranthus induratus is known for its drought tolerance and is sometimes cultivated as a forage crop or ornamental plant.
