Amaranthus hypochondriacus var. macrostachys

Amaranthus hypochondriacus var. macrostachys, commonly known as the Mexican feather amaranth, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and vibrant, feather-like leaves that range in color from green to red. The plant produces showy, cone-shaped inflorescences that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for both gardens and commercial cultivation.

This variety is also valued for its nutritional content, as its seeds are rich in protein and minerals.
