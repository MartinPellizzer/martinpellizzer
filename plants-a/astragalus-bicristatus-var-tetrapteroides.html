Astragalus bicristatus var. tetrapteroides

Astragalus bicristatus var. tetrapteroides, commonly known as the four-winged milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, yellow to brown pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

This variety is notable for its unique leaf structure, which consists of four pairs of leaflets, giving it its common name.
