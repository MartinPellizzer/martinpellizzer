Anogra buffumi

Anogra buffumi, commonly known as the red-flowered anogra, is a striking member of the Rubiaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences, making it a visually appealing plant. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in some regions.
