Adiantum capillus-veneris var. kenyense

Adiantum capillus-veneris var. kenyense, commonly known as the "Maiden's Tears," is a fern species native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that are typically light green and finely divided. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
