Acmena gerrardi

Acmena gerrardi, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Apple.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Australia. The plant features simple, alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes pubescent underside. Its flowers are small, white or pale green, and are borne in clusters, followed by small, round, edible berries.

The plant is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

Morphology

Acmena gerrardi has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter green underside, typically ranging from 5 to 10 cm in length.

The stem is slender and upright, with a smooth, greyish-brown bark that peels in thin, vertical strips as the plant matures. Its small, fragrant flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, featuring white petals and yellow stamens, blooming in late winter to early spring and pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, adapting to nutrient-poor soils and enabling the plant to thrive in coastal and rocky environments.
