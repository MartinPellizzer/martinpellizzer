Aspilia leucanthema

Aspilia leucanthema, commonly known as the white-headed candle bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are arranged in a tight rosette pattern, giving it a compact appearance. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking floral display and drought tolerance.

Aspilia leucanthema prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
