Adina polycephala var. glabra

Adina polycephala var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth adina, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in both horticulture and ecological studies.
