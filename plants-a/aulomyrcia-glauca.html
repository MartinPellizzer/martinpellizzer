Aulomyrcia glauca

Aulomyrcia glauca, commonly known as the blueberry myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, blue-black berries that are edible. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value comes from its fragrant foliage and showy white flowers that bloom in spring.

Aulomyrcia glauca is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
