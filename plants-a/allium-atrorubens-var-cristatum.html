Allium atrorubens var. cristatum

Allium atrorubens var. cristatum, commonly known as the purple onion or the onion with a crown, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, slender stems that bear dense clusters of purple to pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are narrow and grass-like, while its foliage is often tinged with purple or red hues. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it both visually appealing and relatively easy to cultivate.
