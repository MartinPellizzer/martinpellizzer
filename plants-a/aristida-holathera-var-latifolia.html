Aristida holathera var. latifolia

Aristida holathera var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf threeawn, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
