Aralia sciodaphyllum

Aralia sciodaphyllum, commonly known as the shadow fern aralia, is a rare and endangered plant native to the Appalachian Mountains of the United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features compound leaves with a fern-like appearance, which contribute to its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
