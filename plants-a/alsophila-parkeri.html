Alsophila parkeri

Alsophila parkeri, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a thick, fibrous layer of rootstock. The plant produces fronds that are divided into pinnae, giving it a distinctive, feathery appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Alsophila parkeri plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
