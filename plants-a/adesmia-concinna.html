Adesmia concinna

Adesmia concinna, commonly known as the narrowleaf adesmia, is a small shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically pea-shaped, typical of the Fabaceae family. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted to disperse in dry environments.

Adesmia concinna is well-suited to drought conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
