Acacia laeta

Acacia laeta, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and small, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Acacia laeta is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and revegetation projects.
