Aphelandra melgariensis

Aphelandra melgariensis, commonly known as the Melgari's torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble torches. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. It produces fragrant, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant visual element to its appearance.

Aphelandra melgariensis is typically grown as a houseplant or in warm, humid climates due to its preference for indirect light and consistent moisture.
