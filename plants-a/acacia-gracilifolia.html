Acacia gracilifolia

Acacia gracilifolia, commonly known as slender-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow, globular inflorescences that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods that open to release the seeds when mature.

Acacia gracilifolia is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
