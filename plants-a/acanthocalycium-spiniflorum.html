Acanthocalycium spiniflorum

Acanthocalycium spiniflorum, commonly known as the spiny-flowered cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of spines that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
