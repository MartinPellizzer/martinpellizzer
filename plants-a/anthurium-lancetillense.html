Anthurium lancetillense

Anthurium lancetillense, commonly known as the "Crimson Shield," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored with red, pink, or green hues. The plant produces striking spadices, which are the spiky, often red or yellow structures that surround the true flowers. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.

The plant thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
