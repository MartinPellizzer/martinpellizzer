Amygdalus indica

Amygdalus indica, commonly known as the Persian walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The tree produces oval, hard-shelled nuts that are valued for their rich oil content. Its growth habit includes a slow development, with the fruit ripening over several months.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
