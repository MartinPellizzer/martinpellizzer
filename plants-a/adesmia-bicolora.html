Adesmia bicolora

Adesmia bicolora, commonly known as the two-colored legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

Adesmia bicolora is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
