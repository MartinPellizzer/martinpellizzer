Agapetes rosea

Agapetes rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white or silver hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters and blooming in the spring.

Agapetes rosea thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high elevations where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
