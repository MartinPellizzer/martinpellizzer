Andropogon macroglossus

Andropogon macroglossus, commonly known as the large-tongue grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, elongated leaves that can reach lengths of up to 30 inches. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, prairies, and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ecological role in supporting native wildlife and stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
