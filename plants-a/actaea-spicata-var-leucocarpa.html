Actaea spicata var. leucocarpa

Actaea spicata var. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited bugbane, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features tall, unbranched stems that bear large, compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, spiky clusters of white to pale yellow flowers in late summer. Its most distinctive feature is the white, fleshy, berry-like fruits that persist into winter.

It thrives in moist, shady woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
