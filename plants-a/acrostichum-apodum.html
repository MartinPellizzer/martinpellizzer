Acrostichum apodum

Acrostichum apodum, commonly known as the giant reed or giant grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features long, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in length, often with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found near water bodies.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
