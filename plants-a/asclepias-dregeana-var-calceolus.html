Asclepias dregeana var. calceolus

Asclepias dregeana var. calceolus, commonly known as the velvetleaf milkweed, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and striking clusters of pinkish-purple flowers. The plant produces specialized structures called milkweed pods, which contain seeds with silky filaments for wind dispersal. It belongs to the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae), known for their milky sap and ecological importance to monarch butterflies.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to support local pollinators.
