Alsophila falcata

Alsophila falcata, commonly known as the sickle fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach up to several meters in length. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for many decades, making it a long-lived component of rainforest ecosystems. Its rhizomes produce new shoots, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

The fronds are typically green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
