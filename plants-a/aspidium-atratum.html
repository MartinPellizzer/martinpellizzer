Aspidium atratum

Aspidium atratum, commonly known as the black fern, is a species of fern native to various regions in Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Aspidium atratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
