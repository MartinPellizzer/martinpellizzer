Astragalus chlorostachys

Astragalus chlorostachys, commonly known as the blue milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

Astragalus chlorostachys is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
