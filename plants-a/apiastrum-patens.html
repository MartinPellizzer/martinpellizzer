Apiastrum patens

Apiastrum patens, commonly known as the spreading apiastrum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

Apiastrum patens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
