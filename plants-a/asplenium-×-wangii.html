Asplenium × wangii

Asplenium × wangii, commonly known as the hybrid maidenhair fern, is a rare and ornamental fern species resulting from the cross between Asplenium trichomanes and Asplenium bulbiferum.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that display a striking contrast of dark green and silver-white on the underside. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, preferring rich, well-drained soil. Its unique appearance makes it a sought-after specimen in horticulture, though it is not commonly found in the wild.

Asplenium × wangii is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is considered a challenging but rewarding plant for experienced growers.
