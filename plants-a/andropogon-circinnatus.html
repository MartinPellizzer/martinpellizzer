Andropogon circinnatus

Andropogon circinnatus, commonly known as the purple bluestem, is a tall grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and purple-tinted foliage, especially during the fall. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green to purplish in color. It thrives in full sun and is often found in prairie and woodland habitats.

Andropogon circinnatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
