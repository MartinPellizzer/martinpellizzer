Adenophora verticillata var. oppositifolia

Adenophora verticillata var. oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its opposite arrangement of lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
