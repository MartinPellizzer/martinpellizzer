Allium circinnatum subsp. circinnatum

Allium circinnatum subsp.

circinnatum, commonly known as the purple dragon garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features striking, narrow, violet-blue leaves that form a rosette, and it produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant is characterized by its strong garlic scent and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is closely related to other Allium species and is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and culinary uses.
