Adelia macrophylla

Adelia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant lily, is a striking plant native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in summer, often reaching up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, contributing to its dramatic appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, typically found in coastal areas and open woodlands.

Adelia macrophylla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its impressive floral display.
