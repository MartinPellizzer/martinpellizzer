Acacia stowardii

Acacia stowardii, commonly known as the Stoward's wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow floral spikes that bloom during the warmer months. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia stowardii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system and reduced leaf surface.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
