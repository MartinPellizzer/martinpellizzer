Androcalymma glabrifolium

Androcalymma glabrifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leaved sandblow, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white powder. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
