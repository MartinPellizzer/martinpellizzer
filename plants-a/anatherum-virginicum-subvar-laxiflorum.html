Anatherum virginicum subvar. laxiflorum

Anatherum virginicum subvar. laxiflorum, commonly known as the slenderflower panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and pond edges. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, pale green to brown spikelets.

The species is characterized by its lax, open flowering structure, which distinguishes it from other varieties within the genus.
