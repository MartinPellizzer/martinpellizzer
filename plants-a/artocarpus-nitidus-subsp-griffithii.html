Artocarpus nitidus subsp. griffithii

Artocarpus nitidus subsp.

griffithii, commonly known as the Java breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries.

The plant is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruit, which is edible, is typically harvested when mature and used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
