Aconitum moschatum

Aconitum moschatum, commonly known as the Chinese aconite or monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features striking bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in a spire-like cluster. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply cut and often covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
