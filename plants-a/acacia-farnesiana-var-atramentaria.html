Acacia farnesiana var. atramentaria

Acacia farnesiana var. atramentaria, commonly known as the black acacia, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous tiny leaflets, and it produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its distinctive appearance.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
