Alopecurus pratensis subsp. aquaticus

Alopecurus pratensis subsp.

aquaticus, commonly known as water meadow grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wet meadows and marshy areas. It has slender, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in temperate regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats and supporting diverse insect life.
