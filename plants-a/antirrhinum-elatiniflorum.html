Antirrhinum elatiniflorum

Antirrhinum elatiniflorum, commonly known as the silky antirrhinum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its unique floral structure attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
