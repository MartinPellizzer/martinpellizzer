Adenophora triphylla f. lancifolia

Adenophora triphylla f. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped, typically with three leaflets. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, woodland environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
