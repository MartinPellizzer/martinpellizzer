Aquilegia coerulea f. longissima

Aquilegia coerulea f. longissima, commonly known as the long-stemmed blue columbine, is a cultivar of the common blue columbine.

It is characterized by its elongated, spurred petals that are typically a deep blue or violet in color. The plant produces clusters of flowers that bloom in early to mid-summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features include compound leaves with lobed foliage and a compact growth habit.
