Anthurium samamaense

Anthurium samamaense, commonly known as the Samamaense Anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spatula-shaped leaf. The plant produces striking, colorful spathe that can range from red to yellow, often with a contrasting veining. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
