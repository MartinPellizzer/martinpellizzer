Asplenium multicaudatum var. triste

Asplenium multicaudatum var. triste, commonly known as the weeping spleenwort, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, hanging fronds that give it a unique, cascading appearance. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive, narrow shape.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in humid, indoor settings.
