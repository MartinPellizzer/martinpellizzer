Aristida pallens var. tragopogon

Aristida pallens var. tragopogon, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as prairies and disturbed sites.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and restoration projects.
