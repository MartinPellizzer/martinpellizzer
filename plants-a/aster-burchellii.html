Aster burchellii

Aster burchellii, commonly known as Burchell's aster, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has hairy stems and leaves that help it retain moisture. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Aster burchellii is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
