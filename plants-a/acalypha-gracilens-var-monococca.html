Acalypha gracilens var. monococca

Acalypha gracilens var. monococca, commonly known as the narrowleaf spurge or slender acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-yellow flowers that are often surrounded by bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
