Acaena ovalifolia var. incisiserrata

Acaena ovalifolia var. incisiserrata, commonly known as the serrated bramble, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are ovate to elliptic in shape and have serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by burrs containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is notable for its prickly fruits, which aid in seed dispersal by attaching to the fur of animals.
