Aegilops triaristata var. vulgaris

Aegilops triaristata var. vulgaris, commonly known as the goat grass, is a wild wheat species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences with three bracts, giving it its scientific name. Its seeds are encased in a tough, bristly husk, which makes them difficult to process without specialized equipment.

This species is of interest to researchers due to its potential genetic contributions to modern wheat varieties.
