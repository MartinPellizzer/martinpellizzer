Armeria rumelica var. tempskyana

Armeria rumelica var. tempskyana, commonly known as the Tempskaya sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a border plant.
