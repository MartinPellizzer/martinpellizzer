Astragalus wootonii var. wootonii

Astragalus wootonii var. wootonii, commonly known as Wooton's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of arid ecosystem flora.
