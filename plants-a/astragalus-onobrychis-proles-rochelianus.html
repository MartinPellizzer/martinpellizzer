Astragalus onobrychis proles rochelianus

Astragalus onobrychis proles rochelianus, commonly known as the Rochel's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its association with rhizobia bacteria.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for pollinators and supporting soil health.
