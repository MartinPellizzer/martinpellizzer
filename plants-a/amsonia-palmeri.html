Amsonia palmeri

Amsonia palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's bluemink, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to sky-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often hairy and upright.

Amsonia palmeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
