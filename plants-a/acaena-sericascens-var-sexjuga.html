Acaena sericascens var. sexjuga

Acaena sericascens var. sexjuga, commonly known as the silver burr or silver cushion, is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dense, silver-gray foliage that is covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often growing in well-drained soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in erosion control and as an ornamental plant.
