Arabidopsis gemmifera

Arabidopsis gemmifera, commonly known as the gemmiferous Arabidopsis, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its compact growth habit and simple morphology. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a short life cycle, making it a model organism in plant biology research. Its genome has been extensively studied, contributing to our understanding of plant genetics and development.

Arabidopsis gemmifera is also noted for its ability to form gemmae, which are vegetative propagules that aid in its survival and spread.
