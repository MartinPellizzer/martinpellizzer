Astragalus palaestinus var. jordanensis

Astragalus palaestinus var. jordanensis, commonly known as Jordanian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native range.

Its scientific classification and ecological significance make it an important species in regional flora studies.
