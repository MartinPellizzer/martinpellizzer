Anemopaegma glaucum var. typus

Anemopaegma glaucum var. typus, commonly known as the bluebells of the genus Anemopaegma, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.
