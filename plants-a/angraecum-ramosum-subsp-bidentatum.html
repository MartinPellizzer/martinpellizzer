Angraecum ramosum subsp. bidentatum

Angraecum ramosum subsp.

bidentatum, commonly known as the two-toothed angraecum, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and relatively small, greenish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, where it thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil. Its flowers are fragrant and bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators such as moths.

This subspecies is noted for its resilience and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
