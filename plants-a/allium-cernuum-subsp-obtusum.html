Allium cernuum subsp. obtusum

Allium cernuum subsp.

obtusum, commonly known as the nodding onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features basal leaves that are grass-like and clustered at the base, with a rosette structure.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers that hang downward, giving it its common name. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild onion flavor, often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
