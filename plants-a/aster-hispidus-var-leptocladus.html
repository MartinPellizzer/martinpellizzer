Aster hispidus var. leptocladus

Aster hispidus var. leptocladus, commonly known as the hairy aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems covered in dense, white to grayish hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and also bear a fuzzy texture, contributing to its overall hirsute appearance.

This species is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
