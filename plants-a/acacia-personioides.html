Acacia personioides

Acacia personioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and phyllodes that are narrow and linear. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in reforestation efforts in suitable climates.
