Aira violacea

Aira violacea, commonly known as violet air grass, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, violet-colored florets that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
