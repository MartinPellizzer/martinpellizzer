Amygdalus potaninii

Amygdalus potaninii, commonly known as the Potanin's almond, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the development of almond-like fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited commercial use compared to other almond species.
