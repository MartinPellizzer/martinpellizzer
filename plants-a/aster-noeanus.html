Aster noeanus

Aster noeanus, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy purple or blue flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Aster noeanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
