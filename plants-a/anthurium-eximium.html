Anthurium eximium

Anthurium eximium, commonly known as the velvet leaf anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are typically deep green with a reddish or maroon underside. The plant produces striking spadices, which are the modified leaves that house the small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium eximium is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its unique foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
