Arisaema muricaudatum

Arisaema muricaudatum, commonly known as the hairy-tailed arisaema, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spadix-shaped inflorescence surrounded by a large, heart-shaped leaf-like structure called a spathe. The plant features rough, hairy textures on its stems and leaves, contributing to its unique appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Arisaema muricaudatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
