Alnus glutinosa var. imperialis

Alnus glutinosa var. imperialis, commonly known as the imperial alder, is a variant of the black alder native to Europe.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its dark, fissured bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins that are essential for its reproductive cycle. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in wetlands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
