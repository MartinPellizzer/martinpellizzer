Actaea spicata var. dissecta

Actaea spicata var. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected black cohosh, is a striking perennial herb native to the woodlands of eastern North America.

It features tall, slender stems that bear large, compound leaves with deeply cut lobes, giving it a fern-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring, which are followed by red berries in the fall. Its rhizomatous root system is notable for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional herbal remedies.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
