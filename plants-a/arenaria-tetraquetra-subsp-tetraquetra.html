Arenaria tetraquetra subsp. tetraquetra

Arenaria tetraquetra subsp.

tetraquetra, commonly known as the sandpink, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions. It produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with fleshy, blue-green leaves that are adapted to cold and dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude and polar ecosystems.
