Anemone albana

Anemone albana, commonly known as the white anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically hairy and grayish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its delicate appearance and early blooming habit make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
