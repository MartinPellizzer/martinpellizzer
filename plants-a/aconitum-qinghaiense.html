Aconitum qinghaiense

Aconitum qinghaiense, commonly known as the Qinghai aconite, is a perennial herb native to the Qinghai region of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant produces egg-shaped, woolly capsules containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a dangerous plant and should be handled with care.
