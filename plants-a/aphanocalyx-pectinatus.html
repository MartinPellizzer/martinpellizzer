Aphanocalyx pectinatus

Aphanocalyx pectinatus, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its clustered, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The species is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.
