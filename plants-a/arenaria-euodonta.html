Arenaria euodonta

Arenaria euodonta, commonly known as the sweet Alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage is usually covered in fine, hairy leaves, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Arenaria euodonta is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
