Anisomeris occidentalis

Anisomeris occidentalis, commonly known as the western anisomeris, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually linear and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in composite inflorescences.

The plant is often found in scrublands and canyons, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
