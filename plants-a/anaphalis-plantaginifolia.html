Anaphalis plantaginifolia

Anaphalis plantaginifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf or frost plant, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant is notable for its silvery-white foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs that give it a frosty appearance. The flowers bloom in late summer, producing small, inconspicuous blooms that are visited by various pollinators.
