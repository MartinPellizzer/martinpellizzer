Aloysia famatinensis

Aloysia famatinensis, commonly known as the Famatinian verbena, is a flowering plant native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and pink. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Aloysia famatinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in supporting local pollinators.
