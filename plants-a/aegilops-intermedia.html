Aegilops intermedia

Aegilops intermedia, commonly known as intermediate wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It is often cultivated for its potential as a forage crop and for its use in developing new wheat varieties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in agricultural and ecological research.
