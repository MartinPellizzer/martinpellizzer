Aegilops intermedia

Aegilops intermedia, commonly known as intermediate wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It is often cultivated for its potential as a forage crop and for its use in developing new wheat varieties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in agricultural and ecological research.

Morphology

Aegilops intermedia has leaves that are narrow, grass-like, and typically arranged in a basal rosette, with a bluish-green color and a smooth surface.

The stem is cylindrical, hollow, and grows upright, with a light green color and a smooth texture. Its flowers are small, spike-like clusters with pale green to yellowish bracts, blooming in early summer and pollinated by wind. The fruit is a cylindrical spikelet containing small, dry seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, helping the plant to anchor and absorb nutrients from the soil.
