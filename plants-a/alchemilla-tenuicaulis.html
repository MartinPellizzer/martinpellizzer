Alchemilla tenuicaulis

Alchemilla tenuicaulis, commonly known as slender-stemmed lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in loose clusters during late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla tenuicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
