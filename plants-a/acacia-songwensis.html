Acacia songwensis

Acacia songwensis, commonly known as the East African wattle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in savanna and woodland habitats. The plant is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in cylindrical spikes. Acacia songwensis is adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in poor soils.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
