Alloplectus discolor

Alloplectus discolor, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Alloplectus discolor is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
