Ananas debilis

Ananas debilis, commonly known as the pineapple guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to pineapples but have a softer texture and a sweeter, more delicate flavor. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained, acidic soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

Ananas debilis is relatively low maintenance and can be grown in a variety of climates with adequate sunlight.
