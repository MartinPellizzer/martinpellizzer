Ancistrocactus brevihamatus var. pallidus

Ancistrocactus brevihamatus var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale ancistrocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, sprawling plant with cylindrical stems that are typically pale green to bluish-gray in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often followed by red or orange fruits. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

This variety is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, making it a resilient and visually striking plant in its native habitat.
