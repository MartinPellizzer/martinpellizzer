Astragalus dolichodontus

Astragalus dolichodontus, commonly known as the long-toothed milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Astragalus dolichodontus has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh environments.

It is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
