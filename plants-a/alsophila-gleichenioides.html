Alsophila gleichenioides

Alsophila gleichenioides, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the world.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting its rapid growth and contributing to its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.

The fronds are typically bioluminescent, adding a unique visual element to its appearance.
