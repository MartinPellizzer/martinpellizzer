Anil geminata

Anil geminata, commonly known as the Indian bean tree, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisines.

Anil geminata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
