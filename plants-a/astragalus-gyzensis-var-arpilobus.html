Astragalus gyzensis var. arpilobus

Astragalus gyzensis var. arpilobus, commonly known as the Gyzengis milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is of interest in traditional and modern herbal medicine.
