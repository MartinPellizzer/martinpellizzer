Allium bisulcum

Allium bisulcum, commonly known as the purple onion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bulbous stem and clusters of purple or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, edible bulbs that are used in culinary applications, similar to other Allium species. Its leaves are long and grass-like, and the plant thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Allium bisulcum is also valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens due to its vibrant flower color.
