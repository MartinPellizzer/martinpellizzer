Atriplex caput-medusae

Atriplex caput-medusae, commonly known as the "Medusa's Head," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and branched inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and greenish, arranged in dense clusters that resemble the crown of Medusa, giving the plant its striking common name.
