Aloe nuttii

Aloe nuttii, commonly known as the Nuttall's aloe, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick, fleshy, and covered in a white powdery coating called bloom. The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences with yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Aloe nuttii is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and can be processed to extract gel for various applications.
