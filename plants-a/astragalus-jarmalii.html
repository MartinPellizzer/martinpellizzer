Astragalus jarmalii

Astragalus jarmalii, commonly known as the Jarmal milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus jarmalii is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitat.
