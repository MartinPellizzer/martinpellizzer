Anil obcordata

Anil obcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. Its flowers have five petals and are arranged in a raceme, typical of the Malvaceae family.

Anil obcordata is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
