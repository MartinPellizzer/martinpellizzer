Anaphalis virgata var. stoliczkae

Anaphalis virgata var. stoliczkae, commonly known as the Stoliczka's woolly goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly foliage that resembles a ball of cotton, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

This variety is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
