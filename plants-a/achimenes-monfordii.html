Achimenes monfordii

Achimenes monfordii, commonly known as the dwarf flame vine, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting throat. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and produces fleshy, reddish stems that help it thrive in humid, shaded environments. It is a herbaceous perennial that prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners looking to add vibrant color to their landscapes.
