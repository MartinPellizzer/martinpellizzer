Anthemis tinctoria subsp. subtinctoria

Anthemis tinctoria subsp.

subtinctoria, commonly known as yellow fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers, which consist of ray florets and tubular disc florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy leaves and produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

The species is noted for its medicinal and historical use in dyeing textiles due to the presence of flavonoids and other bioactive compounds.
