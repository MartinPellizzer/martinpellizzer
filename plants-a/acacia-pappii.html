Acacia pappii

Acacia pappii, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grey-green foliage and distinctive, feathery stipules that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its bark is smooth and often peels in thin layers, and it is adapted to arid environments.

Acacia pappii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
