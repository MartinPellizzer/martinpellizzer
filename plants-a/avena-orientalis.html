Avena orientalis

Avena orientalis, commonly known as the oriental oat, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Avena orientalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
