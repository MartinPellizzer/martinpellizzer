Angraecum deflexicalcaratum

Angraecum deflexicalcaratum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendant-like flowers that are typically white or pale blue with intricate patterns. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure, which includes a long, slender spur. Angraecum deflexicalcaratum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or botanical gardens.
