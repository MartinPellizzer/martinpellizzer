Adenia lanceolata

Adenia lanceolata, commonly known as the African mountain balm, is a flowering plant native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, red or orange fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Adenia lanceolata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some cultures.
