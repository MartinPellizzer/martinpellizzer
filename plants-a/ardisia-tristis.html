Ardisia tristis

Ardisia tristis, commonly known as the Christmas berry, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, glossy green leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a woody stem with a smooth, dark bark.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
