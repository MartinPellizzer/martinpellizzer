Acacia heterophylla var. latifolia

Acacia heterophylla var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and consist of multiple pairs of narrow, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow, fluffy flowers in late winter to early spring, which are arranged in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
