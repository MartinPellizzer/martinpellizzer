Annona saffordiana

Annona saffordiana, commonly known as the Safford custard apple, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a large, spiky fruit that turns from green to yellow when mature, containing a white, creamy pulp with a sweet, custard-like flavor. Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications, while the plant also has traditional medicinal uses.

Annona saffordiana thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
