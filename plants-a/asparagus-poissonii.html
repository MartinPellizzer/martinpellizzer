Asparagus poissonii

Asparagus poissonii, commonly known as the fish asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like foliage with bright green, needle-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Asparagus poissonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some culinary uses in local cuisines.
