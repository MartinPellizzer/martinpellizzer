Asplenium apogamum

Asplenium apogamum, commonly known as the barren fern, is a unique species of fern that lacks true leaves and instead produces spores through specialized structures called sporangia.

It is a member of the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, often clustered growth habit. The plant is typically found in shaded, moist environments, such as forests or rocky crevices, where it thrives in low-light conditions. Asplenium apogamum is notable for its apogamous reproduction, meaning it can reproduce without the need for fertilization, a trait that sets it apart from many other fern species.

This adaptation allows it to colonize and persist in a variety of ecological niches.
