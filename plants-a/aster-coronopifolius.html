Aster coronopifolius

Aster coronopifolius, commonly known as the crownleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in the fall, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Aster coronopifolius is a perennial herb that plays an important role in supporting local ecosystems.
