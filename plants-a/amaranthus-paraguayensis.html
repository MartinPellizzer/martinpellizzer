Amaranthus paraguayensis

Amaranthus paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its seeds are small and can be used as a grain, similar to other amaranth species.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and nutritional purposes.
