Acalypha nana

Acalypha nana, commonly known as the dwarf curlleaf mallow, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It features a compact growth habit with hairy, rounded leaves that often curl at the edges. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, or purple, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in floral arrangements.
