Astragalus barceloi

Astragalus barceloi, commonly known as the Barceloi milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited to drought conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
