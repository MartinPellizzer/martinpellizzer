Amaranthus greggii var. muelleri

Amaranthus greggii var. muelleri, commonly known as the Mexican prince or Gregg's amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround the small, green flowers, creating a striking visual display. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
