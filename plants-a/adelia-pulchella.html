Adelia pulchella

Adelia pulchella, commonly known as the pretty adelia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often has a slightly hairy texture.

Adelia pulchella is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
