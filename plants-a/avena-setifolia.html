Avena setifolia

Avena setifolia, commonly known as slender oat, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, white to greenish flowers. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Avena setifolia is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
