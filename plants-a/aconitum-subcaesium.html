Aconitum subcaesium

Aconitum subcaesium, commonly known as the Siberian monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, upright clusters. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a deep green color and a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a poisonous plant and should be handled with care.
