Aegilops crassa

Aegilops crassa, commonly known as rough wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its coarse, dark green leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stems, with bract-like structures that aid in seed dispersal. Aegilops crassa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and rapid growth.

It is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.

Morphology

Aegilops crassa has leaves that are broad and ovate in shape, with a dark green color and a rough, hairy texture on the upper surface.

The leaves are arranged alternately along the stem, which is upright and cylindrical with a light green bark that becomes more rugged with age. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, blooming in late spring to early summer, and they are primarily pollinated by wind. The fruit is a three-lobed spikelet that disperses its seeds by wind once matured.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, aiding in water absorption from the soil.
