Aragallus argophyllus

Aragallus argophyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually silver-gray in color, giving it its common name, and it produces small, showy flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aragallus argophyllus is often found in high-altitude meadows and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
