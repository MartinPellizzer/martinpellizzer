Agapetes linearifolia

Agapetes linearifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf silver fir, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by small, cone-like structures containing seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
