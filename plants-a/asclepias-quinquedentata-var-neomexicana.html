Asclepias quinquedentata var. neomexicana

Asclepias quinquedentata var. neomexicana, commonly known as the Neomexican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers, which are typical of the milkweed genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

This species is an important host plant for the monarch butterfly and other native pollinators.
