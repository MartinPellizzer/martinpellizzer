Aster corymbiformis

Aster corymbiformis, commonly known as the clustered aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and produces clusters of small, violet to blue flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Aster corymbiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
