Astragalus jacquemontii

Astragalus jacquemontii, commonly known as Jacquemont's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Astragalus jacquemontii is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
