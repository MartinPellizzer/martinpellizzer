Aesculus × glaucescens

Aesculus × glaucescens, commonly known as the American horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically five to seven lobes, and its showy, white or pale pink flowers that appear in late spring. The tree produces large, spiny capsules containing shiny, poisonous seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with longitudinal ridges, and it can grow up to 25 meters tall.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
