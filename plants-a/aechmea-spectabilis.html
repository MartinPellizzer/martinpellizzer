Aechmea spectabilis

Aechmea spectabilis, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater to support its growth. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences with colorful bracts that range from red to orange. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil and high humidity.

Aechmea spectabilis is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea spectabilis has rosette-shaped leaves that are narrow, sword-like, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a waxy, dark green to blue-gray coloration.

The plant lacks a traditional stem, instead having a short, thick base covered in a fibrous, pale green to brownish bark. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow, and bloom during the summer months, relying on insects for pollination. The fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that produces bright red berries, which are dispersed by birds and animals.

Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding air and soil, typical of its epiphytic growth habit.
