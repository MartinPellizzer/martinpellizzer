Anthurium aciforme

Anthurium aciforme, commonly known as the spiky anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped spathes that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, greenish-white spadices that are clustered within the spatulate leaves. Its foliage consists of dark green, glossy, and elongated leaves with a serrated edge.

Anthurium aciforme thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
