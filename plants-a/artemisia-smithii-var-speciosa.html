Artemisia smithii var. speciosa

Artemisia smithii var. speciosa, commonly known as the showy golden sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray foliage and its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows. It has a strong aromatic scent and is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
