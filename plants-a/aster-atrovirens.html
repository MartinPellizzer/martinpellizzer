Aster atrovirens

Aster atrovirens, commonly known as the black-leaf aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its flowering period extends from August to October, making it a valuable nectar source for pollinators.

Aster atrovirens is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
