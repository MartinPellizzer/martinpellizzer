Alsodeia pallida

Alsodeia pallida, commonly known as pale alsike clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in spikes, and bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
