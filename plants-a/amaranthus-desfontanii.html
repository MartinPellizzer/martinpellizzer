Amaranthus desfontanii

Amaranthus desfontanii, commonly known as the desert amaranth, is a species of flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, hairy stems, and vibrant red to purple bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is adapted to hot, dry environments and possesses deep root systems that allow it to survive in poor soil conditions. Its leaves are edible and have been used traditionally by indigenous peoples for food and medicinal purposes.

Amaranthus desfontanii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in xeriscaped gardens.
