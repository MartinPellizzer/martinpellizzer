Avena forsteri

Avena forsteri, commonly known as the Forster's oat, is a species of grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Avena forsteri is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in native plant communities.
