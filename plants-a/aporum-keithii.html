Aporum keithii

Aporum keithii, commonly known as the Keith's arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a prominent veins. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, surrounded by a showy, modified leaf called a spathe.

Aporum keithii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and dramatic appearance.
