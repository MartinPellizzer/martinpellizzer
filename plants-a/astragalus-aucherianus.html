Astragalus aucherianus

Astragalus aucherianus, commonly known as the Aucher's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Astragalus aucherianus is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
