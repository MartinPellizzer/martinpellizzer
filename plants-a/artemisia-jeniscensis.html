Artemisia jeniscensis

Artemisia jeniscensis, commonly known as the Siberian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments. It contains volatile oils and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

Its hardy nature makes it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
