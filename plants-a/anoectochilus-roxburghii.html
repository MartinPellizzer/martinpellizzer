Anoectochilus roxburghii

Anoectochilus roxburghii, commonly known as the orchid of the Himalayas, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is highly sought after in horticulture and traditional medicine.
