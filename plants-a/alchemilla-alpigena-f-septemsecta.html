Alchemilla alpigena f. septemsecta

Alchemilla alpigena f. septemsecta, commonly known as the seven-lobed alpine lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported soothing properties.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in rocky, well-drained soils.
