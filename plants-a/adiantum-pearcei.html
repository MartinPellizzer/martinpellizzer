Adiantum pearcei

Adiantum pearcei, commonly known as the pearce's maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds.

Adiantum pearcei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with the right conditions.
