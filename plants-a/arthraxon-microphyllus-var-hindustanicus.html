Arthraxon microphyllus var. hindustanicus

Arthraxon microphyllus var. hindustanicus, commonly known as the small-leaved arthraxon, is a perennial grass native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its leaves are usually less than 10 cm in length, and the species is known for its dense growth habit.

The botanical features of this variety make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
