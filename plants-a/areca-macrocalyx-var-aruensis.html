Areca macrocalyx var. aruensis

Areca macrocalyx var. aruensis, commonly known as the Aru areca, is a flowering plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that bear clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are used in traditional practices and local cuisine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
