Acanthus neoguineensis

Acanthus neoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea acanthus, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of New Guinea and surrounding areas, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and deep veins. Its showy, spiky flowers, which range in color from white to purple, bloom in clusters during the wet season.

Acanthus neoguineensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in traditional medicine in its native habitats.
