Allochrusa paniculata subsp. ferganensis

Allochrusa paniculata subsp.

ferganensis, commonly known as the Fergana alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple.

The plant typically thrives in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high altitudes. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
