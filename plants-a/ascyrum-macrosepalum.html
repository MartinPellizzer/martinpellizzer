Ascyrum macrosepalum

Ascyrum macrosepalum, commonly known as the large-sepal orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. The plant features long, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with prominent, fleshy sepals that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Ascyrum macrosepalum is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its beauty and unique floral structure.
