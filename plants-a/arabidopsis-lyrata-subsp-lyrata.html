Arabidopsis lyrata subsp. lyrata

Arabidopsis lyrata subsp.

lyrata, commonly known as the desert Arabidopsis, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as cabbage and mustard.

This species is characterized by its erect growth habit, narrow leaves, and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. It is often used in ecological studies due to its ability to thrive in harsh environments.

The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its adaptability in various habitats.
