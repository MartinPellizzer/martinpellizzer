Anthurium carpishense

Anthurium carpishense, commonly known as the Caripito arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its flowers are usually bright red or orange.

Anthurium carpishense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for decorative purposes in warm climates.
