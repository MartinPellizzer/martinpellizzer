Auganthus violaris

Auganthus violaris, commonly known as the violet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Auganthus violaris is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
