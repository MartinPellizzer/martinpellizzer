Astragalus gibbsii var. curvicarpus

Astragalus gibbsii var. curvicarpus, commonly known as Gibbs' milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
