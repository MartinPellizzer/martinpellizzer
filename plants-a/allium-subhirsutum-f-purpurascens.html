Allium subhirsutum f. purpurascens

Allium subhirsutum f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered garlic.

This cultivar is a variant of the common garlic plant, characterized by its striking purple flowers that contrast with its typical white blooms. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to Central Asia. The plant features a bulbous stem with narrow, grass-like leaves.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant color and hardy nature.
