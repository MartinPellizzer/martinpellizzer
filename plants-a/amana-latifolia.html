Amana latifolia

Amana latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf amana, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Amana latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
