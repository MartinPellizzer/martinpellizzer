Anatherum brasiliense

Anatherum brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or Brazilian panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, making it a dioecious species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and grassy areas.

Anatherum brasiliense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
