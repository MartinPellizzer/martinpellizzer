Anthyllis bidentata

Anthyllis bidentata, commonly known as the two-toothed anthyllis, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. Anthyllis bidentata is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which makes it valuable in soil enrichment.

It is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and adaptability.
