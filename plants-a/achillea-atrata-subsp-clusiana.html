Achillea atrata subsp. clusiana

Achillea atrata subsp.

clusiana, commonly known as the blackwood yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features dark green, finely divided leaves and produces dense clusters of small, dark purple to black flowers.

The plant is characterized by its robust root system and its ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, where it contributes to the local flora.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
