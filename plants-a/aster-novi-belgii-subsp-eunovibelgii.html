Aster novi-belgii subsp. eunovibelgii

Aster novi-belgii subsp.

eunovibelgii, commonly known as the New York aster, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States. It features showy, daisy-like flowers with blue to purple ray florets and yellow disc florets, blooming in late summer to early fall.

The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
