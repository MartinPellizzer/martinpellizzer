Anthurium canaliculatum

Anthurium canaliculatum, commonly known as the "Cup-shaped Anthurium," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive cup-like structure at the base. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by colorful spatthae, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Anthurium canaliculatum is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and unique floral display.
