Astragalus nigricalycis

Astragalus nigricalycis, commonly known as the black calyx milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils. Its pods contain small, edible seeds that are often dispersed by wind.

Astragalus nigricalycis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
