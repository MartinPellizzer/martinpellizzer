Astelma virgatum

Astelma virgatum, commonly known as the silver aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its showy, daisy-like flowers bloom in shades of white and purple, typically in the summer months.

Astelma virgatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
