Agave jacquiniana

Agave jacquiniana, commonly known as the blue agave, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stem with clusters of white flowers, which appear after several years. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Agave jacquiniana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in the production of tequila.
