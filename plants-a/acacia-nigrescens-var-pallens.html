Acacia nigrescens var. pallens

Acacia nigrescens var. pallens, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spindly branches and its distinctive, narrow, sickle-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along coastal areas.

The varietal form 'pallens' is distinguished by its pale or white flower heads, setting it apart from other acacia species.
