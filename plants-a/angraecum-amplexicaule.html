Angraecum amplexicaule

Angraecum amplexicaule, commonly known as the clasping angraecum, is a rare and striking orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that often wrap around the stem, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming at night. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat needs, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
