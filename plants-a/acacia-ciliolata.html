Acacia ciliolata

Acacia ciliolata, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided and covered in soft, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are typically borne in clusters at the ends of branches. Its small, woody pods contain several seeds, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia ciliolata is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of browse for livestock.
