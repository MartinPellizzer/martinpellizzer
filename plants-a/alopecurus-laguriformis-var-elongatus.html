Alopecurus laguriformis var. elongatus

Alopecurus laguriformis var. elongatus, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a variant of the meadow grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and compact inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and spikelets that are often arranged in dense clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
