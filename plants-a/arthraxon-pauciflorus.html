Arthraxon pauciflorus

Arthraxon pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered arthraxon, is a perennial herb native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces few flowers, which are typically greenish or pale in color, and it has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassy areas.

Arthraxon pauciflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
