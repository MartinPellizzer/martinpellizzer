Artocarpus sepicanus

Artocarpus sepicanus, commonly known as the Malabar breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fruit with a rough, fibrous outer layer. The fruit, which is typically yellow when ripe, contains a starchy, edible pulp surrounded by a hard, woody shell. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in traditional cuisines.

The plant also has historical significance in local folklore and traditional medicine.
