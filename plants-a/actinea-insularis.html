Actinea insularis

Actinea insularis, commonly known as the island actinia, is a species of sea anemone found in the western Atlantic Ocean.

It is characterized by its vibrant red color and long, slender tentacles that extend from its central oral disc. This cnidarian typically inhabits shallow coastal waters and is often found in coral reef environments. Actinea insularis plays a role in marine ecosystems by providing food for various fish and invertebrates.

Its reproductive strategy involves both sexual and asexual methods, contributing to its population stability in its habitat.
