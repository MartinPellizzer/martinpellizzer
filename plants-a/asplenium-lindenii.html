Asplenium lindenii

Asplenium lindenii, commonly known as the Lindley's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this fern thrives in shaded, moist environments such as rocky crevices or forest floors. Its spores are produced in sori, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium lindenii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to adapt to a variety of indoor growing conditions.
