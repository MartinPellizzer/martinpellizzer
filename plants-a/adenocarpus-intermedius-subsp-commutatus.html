commutatus, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves that are typically pubescent.

Its common name derives from the milky sap it exudes, which can be toxic to livestock.
