Aster brachytrichus var. latifolius

Aster brachytrichus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features showy, clustered purple to blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue with a hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
