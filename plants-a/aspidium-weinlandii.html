Aspidium weinlandii

Aspidium weinlandii, commonly known as the Weinland's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically divided into small, lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its spores are released into the water, allowing for propagation through aquatic dispersal.

Aspidium weinlandii plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
