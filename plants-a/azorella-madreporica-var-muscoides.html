Azorella madreporica var. muscoides

Azorella madreporica var. muscoides, commonly known as the cushion plant, is a unique species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing cushions of foliage that form a compact, mat-like structure. The plant is adapted to harsh, cold environments and possesses specialized features for survival in such conditions, including a slow growth rate and the ability to store water. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in late summer, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in alpine ecosystems.

The variety muscoides is distinguished by its more densely packed growth and a more moss-like appearance compared to other varieties of Azorella.
