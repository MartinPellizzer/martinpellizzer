Aster lucayanus

Aster lucayanus, commonly known as the Lucayan aster, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
