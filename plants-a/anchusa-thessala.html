Anchusa thessala

Anchusa thessala, commonly known as the Greek catchfly, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Anchusa thessala is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking flower color and compact growth habit.
