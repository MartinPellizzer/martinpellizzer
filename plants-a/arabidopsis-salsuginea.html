Arabidopsis salsuginea

Arabidopsis salsuginea, commonly known as the desert mustard, is a small annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known species like Arabidopsis thaliana. This species is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, demonstrating high tolerance to drought and salinity. Its compact size and rapid life cycle make it a useful model organism for studying plant stress responses.

The plant typically produces small yellow flowers and has narrow, linear leaves adapted to minimize water loss.
