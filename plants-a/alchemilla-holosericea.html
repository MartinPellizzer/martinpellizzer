Alchemilla holosericea

Alchemilla holosericea, commonly known as the silverleaf lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance and contributing to its common name.

Alchemilla holosericea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance once established.
