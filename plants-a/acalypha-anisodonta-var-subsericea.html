Acalypha anisodonta var. subsericea

Acalypha anisodonta var. subsericea, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf ironweed, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with a distinctive sheen. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies and horticulture.
