Anthemis purpurascens

Anthemis purpurascens, commonly known as the purple fleur-de-lis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, violet-pink flowers with a distinctive central disk. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
