Artemisia sieversiana var. tibetica

Artemisia sieversiana var. tibetica, commonly known as Tibetan wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clustered inflorescences. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh alpine conditions.

Due to its aromatic properties and traditional uses in medicine, it holds cultural and ecological significance in its native region.
