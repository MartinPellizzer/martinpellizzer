Anthurium acrobates

Anthurium acrobates, commonly known as the climbing anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces spadices, which are dense clusters of tiny flowers surrounded by a showy, brightly colored spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high humidity and indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium acrobates is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and air-purifying properties.
