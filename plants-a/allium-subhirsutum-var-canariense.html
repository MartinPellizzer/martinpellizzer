Allium subhirsutum var. canariense

Allium subhirsutum var. canariense, commonly known as Canary garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It features a bulbous stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, garlic-like scent and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats. It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
