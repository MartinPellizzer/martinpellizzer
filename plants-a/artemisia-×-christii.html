Artemisia × christii

Artemisia × christii, commonly known as the Russian wormwood, is a hybrid shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of narrow, aromatic leaves with a strong, camphor-like scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

Artemisia × christii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
