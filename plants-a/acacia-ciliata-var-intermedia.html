Acacia ciliata var. intermedia

Acacia ciliata var. intermedia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Its seeds are also used in traditional Aboriginal practices and for making tea.
