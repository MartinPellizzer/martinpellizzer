Alyssum podolicum

Alyssum podolicum, commonly known as the Polish alyssum, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces clusters of white or pale yellow blooms. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branching.

Alyssum podolicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
