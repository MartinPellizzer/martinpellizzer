Asparagus buxbaumius

Asparagus buxbaumius, commonly known as the Buxbaum asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features wiry, branched stems that are often covered in a fine, white, powdery coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and arranged in a rosette pattern at the base of the plant.

The Buxbaum asparagus is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
