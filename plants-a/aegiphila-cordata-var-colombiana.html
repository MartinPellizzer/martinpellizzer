Aegiphila cordata var. colombiana

Aegiphila cordata var. colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian heartleaf aegiphila, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It features aromatic, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
