Artocarpus nanchuanensis

Artocarpus nanchuanensis, commonly known as the Nanchuan breadfruit, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to China, specifically the provinces of Guangxi and Guangdong, where it grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces a large, round fruit with a thick, fibrous rind and a soft, edible pulp containing seeds. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are unisexual and clustered in catkins.

The plant is valued for its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisine and has potential for cultivation in warmer climates.
