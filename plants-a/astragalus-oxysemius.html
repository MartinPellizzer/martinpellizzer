Astragalus oxysemius

Astragalus oxysemius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, while its pods contain small, hard seeds.

Astragalus oxysemius is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
