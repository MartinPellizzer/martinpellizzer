Achyroseris Albicaulis

Achyroseris albicaulis is a striking desert plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is known for its large, showy white flowers that bloom in the spring. This species thrives in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing remarkable adaptability to harsh conditions. Its common name, "White Coneflower," reflects both its appearance and its ecological importance. Despite its beauty, it faces threats from habitat loss and climate change.
