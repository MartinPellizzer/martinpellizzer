Alysicarpus quadrangularis

Alysicarpus quadrangularis, commonly known as the burr cucumber, is a tropical vine native to regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its quadrangular stems and trifoliate leaves, which are opposite and often hairy. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by distinctive, spiny fruits that mature to a yellowish-orange color. Its seeds are enclosed in a hard, burs-like structure, which aids in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur or clothing.

This plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
