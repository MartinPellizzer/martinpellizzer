Aletris alba

Aletris alba, commonly known as white False Lily of the Valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often found in rich, well-drained soils. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early to mid-summer. Aletris alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in woodland settings with minimal maintenance.
