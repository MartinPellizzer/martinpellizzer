Apuleia ferrea

Apuleia ferrea, commonly known as ironwood or ferrugineous acacia, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets.

Apuleia ferrea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
