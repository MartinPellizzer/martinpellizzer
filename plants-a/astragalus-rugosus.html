Astragalus rugosus

Astragalus rugosus, commonly known as the rough milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers. The plant is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to survive in harsh environments.

Astragalus rugosus plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinators in its native habitats.
