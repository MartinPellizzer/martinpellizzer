Anthurium purdieanum var. revolutum

Anthurium purdieanum var. revolutum, commonly known as the velvet leaf anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the lowland rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces dramatic spadices, which are the modified leaves that enclose the small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among collectors for its unique foliage and ornamental value.
