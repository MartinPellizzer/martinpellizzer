Astragalus podocarpus var. leucotrichus

Astragalus podocarpus var. leucotrichus, commonly known as the white-haired milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered leaves with a distinctive white pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, followed by narrow, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

This variety is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats.
