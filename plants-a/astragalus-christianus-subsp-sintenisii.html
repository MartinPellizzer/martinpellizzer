Astragalus christianus subsp. sintenisii

Astragalus christianus subsp.

sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenisi milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

The subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
