Aethionema cappadocicum

Aethionema cappadocicum, commonly known as Cappadocian rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are pinnately lobed and covered in a dense layer of white to grayish hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and appear in clusters during the spring.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
