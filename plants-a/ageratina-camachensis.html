Ageratina camachensis

Ageratina camachensis, commonly known as white coneflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive white to pale blue flowers that bloom in dense, conical clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Ageratina camachensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
