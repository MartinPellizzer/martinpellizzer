Acacia palmeri

Acacia palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's acacia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia palmeri is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert species.
