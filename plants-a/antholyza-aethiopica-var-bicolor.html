Antholyza aethiopica var. bicolor

Antholyza aethiopica var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored antholyza, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its striking bi-coloration, with leaves that typically display a mix of green and white or pale yellow hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
