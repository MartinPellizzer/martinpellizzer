Avena discolor

Avena discolor, commonly known as the variable oat, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, each with a distinct pale blue or whitish color. Avena discolor is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in disturbed environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and restoration projects.
