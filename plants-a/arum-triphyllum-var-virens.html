Arum triphyllum var. virens

Arum triphyllum var. virens, commonly known as the green swamp arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy habitats and has heart-shaped, glossy green leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered on the spadix, with the spathe often being a different color.

The plant is adapted to moist environments and plays a role in wetland ecosystems.
