Acer negundo f. robustum

Acer negundo f. robustum, commonly known as the box elder, is a variant of the ash tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, which are followed by winged seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant.

This species is often used in landscaping due to its fast growth and adaptability.
