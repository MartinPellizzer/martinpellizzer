Astragalus looseri

Astragalus looseri, commonly known as the Looser's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in drought-prone areas.

Astragalus looseri is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
