Ardisia schomburgkiana

Ardisia schomburgkiana, commonly known as the Schomburgk's beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Ardisia schomburgkiana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
