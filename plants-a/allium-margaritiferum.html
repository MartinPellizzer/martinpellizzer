Allium margaritiferum

Allium margaritiferum, commonly known as the pearl onion plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly the Pamir and Tien Shan ranges. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and grass-like, with a rosette growth habit. Its flowers are small, white or pale blue, and are clustered in a spherical inflorescence.

The bulbous stem produces a single, showy flower that is often adorned with a distinctive, waxy coating.
