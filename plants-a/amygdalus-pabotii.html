Amygdalus pabotii

Amygdalus pabotii, commonly known as the Pabotii almond.

It is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family, native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the development of edible nuts. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its nut-producing potential in certain regions.
