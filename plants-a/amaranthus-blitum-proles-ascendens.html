Amaranthus blitum proles ascendens

Amaranthus blitum proles ascendens, commonly known as the ascending amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically borne on branched inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy annual in temperate climates.

This species is often grown as a ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and potential use in traditional medicine.
