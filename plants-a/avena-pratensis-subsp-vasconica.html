Avena pratensis subsp. vasconica

Avena pratensis subsp.

vasconica, commonly known as the Spanish oat, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous short, awned spikelets.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and pastures. It produces edible grains that have been traditionally used for animal feed and in some local cuisines.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts in its native habitat.
