Andropogon allionii

Andropogon allionii, commonly known as slender bluestem, is a native North American grass species that thrives in a variety of soil types, including sandy and loamy soils.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is typically found in open habitats such as prairies, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Andropogon allionii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
