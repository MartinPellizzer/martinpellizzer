Amomum linearifolium

Amomum linearifolium, commonly known as the linear-leaf cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its rhizomes are valued for their aromatic properties and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Amomum linearifolium thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary applications.
