Adenophora divaricata

Adenophora divaricata, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Adenophora divaricata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
