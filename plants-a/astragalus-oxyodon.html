Astragalus oxyodon

Astragalus oxyodon, commonly known as the sharp-toothed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its roots are often deep and robust, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus oxyodon plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
