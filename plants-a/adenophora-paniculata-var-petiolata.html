Adenophora paniculata var. petiolata

Adenophora paniculata var. petiolata, commonly known as the petioled bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features upright, branched stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that have distinct petioles. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
