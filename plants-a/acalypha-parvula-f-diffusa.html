Acalypha parvula f. diffusa

Acalypha parvula f. diffusa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly leaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically appearing in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
