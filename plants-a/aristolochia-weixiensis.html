Aristolochia weixiensis

Aristolochia weixiensis, commonly known as the Weixian arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and intricate, vine-like growth habit. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are often mistaken for orchids due to their complex structure. Its tuberous roots are valued in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
