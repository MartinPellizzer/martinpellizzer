Agrostis retrofracta

Agrostis retrofracta, commonly known as the bentgrass, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils, such as those in meadows and along stream banks.

Its growth habit makes it a common species in both natural and managed landscapes.
