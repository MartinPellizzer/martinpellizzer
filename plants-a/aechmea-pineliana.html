Aechmea pineliana

Aechmea pineliana, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaf structure that forms a central cup to collect water. The plant features vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that range from red to orange, often with a striking yellow or green spathe. Its leaves are usually broad, waxy, and have a pointed tip, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Aechmea pineliana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
