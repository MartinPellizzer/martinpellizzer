Avena barbata subsp. hoppeana

Avena barbata subsp.

hoppeana, commonly known as the Hoppe's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with bristle-like awns, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to arid environments and contribute to its ecological significance in native plant communities.
