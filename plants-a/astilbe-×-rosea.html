Astilbe × rosea

Astilbe × rosea, commonly known as the rose mophead astilbe, is a perennial plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens. The plant features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of showy, plume-like flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. Its foliage is typically dark green and textured, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

Astilbe × rosea thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
