Andropogon aequatoriensis

Andropogon aequatoriensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes many economically important grasses. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Andropogon aequatoriensis is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
