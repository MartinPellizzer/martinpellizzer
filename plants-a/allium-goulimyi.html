Allium goulimyi

Allium goulimyi, commonly known as the Chinese leek or garlic chives, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its long, slender leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in culinary applications, similar to garlic and onions. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium goulimyi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual and edible qualities.
