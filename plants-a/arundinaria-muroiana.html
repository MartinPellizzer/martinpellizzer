Arundinaria muroiana

Arundinaria muroiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Arundinaria muroiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Japanese gardens.
