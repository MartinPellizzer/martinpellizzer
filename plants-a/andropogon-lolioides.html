Andropogon lolioides

Andropogon lolioides, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescence, which consists of spikelets that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. It plays an important role in ecological systems by providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for landscaping in native plant gardens.
