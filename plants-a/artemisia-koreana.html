Artemisia koreana

Artemisia koreana, commonly known as Korean mugwort, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.

Artemisia koreana produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.
