Acacia latronum

Acacia latronum, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia latronum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
