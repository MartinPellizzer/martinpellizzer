Angelica rosifolia

Angelica rosifolia, commonly known as the roseleaf angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant features large, compound leaves with a distinctive rose-like appearance, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family.

Angelica rosifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
