Aster labrangensis

Aster labrangensis, commonly known as the Labrang aster, is a flowering plant native to the Tibetan Plateau and adjacent regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, thriving in high-altitude environments. Its flowers are usually blue to violet in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Aster labrangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in local communities.
