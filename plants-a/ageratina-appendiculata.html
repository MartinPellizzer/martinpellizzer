Ageratina appendiculata

Ageratina appendiculata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fluffy, blue-gray inflorescences that resemble mist, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ageratina appendiculata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
