Acoma dissecta

Acoma dissecta, commonly known as the Sierra Madre daisy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate and are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Acoma dissecta is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
