Apsanthea propera

Apsanthea propera, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting dark throat. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, with a slightly rough texture.

Apsanthea propera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
