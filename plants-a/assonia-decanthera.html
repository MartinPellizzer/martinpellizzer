Assonia decanthera

Assonia decanthera, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and low water requirements make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
