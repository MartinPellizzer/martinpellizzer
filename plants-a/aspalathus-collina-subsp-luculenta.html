Aspalathus collina subsp. luculenta

Aspalathus collina subsp.

luculenta, commonly known as the Cape mountain tea, is a shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa. It is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and leathery, oval-shaped leaves.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to a Mediterranean climate with dry summers and wet winters. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats at elevations ranging from 300 to 1,500 meters.

The subspecies is notable for its potential use in herbal medicine and as a source of dietary supplements.
