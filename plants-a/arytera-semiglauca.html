Arytera semiglauca

Arytera semiglauca, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The leaves are covered in a fine, silvery-white coating, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
