Acisanthera divaricata

Acisanthera divaricata, commonly known as the variable acacia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Acisanthera divaricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
