Astragalus kusnezovii

Astragalus kusnezovii, commonly known as Kusnezov's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous tiny, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Astragalus kusnezovii is often found in alpine and steppe ecosystems, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
