Asphodelus davei

Asphodelus davei, commonly known as the Dave's asphodel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its sword-shaped leaves and showy, spiky inflorescences. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring, often in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Asphodelus davei is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
