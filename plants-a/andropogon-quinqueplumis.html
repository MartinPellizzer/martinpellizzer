Andropogon quinqueplumis

Andropogon quinqueplumis, commonly known as the five-branched grass, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in wetland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
