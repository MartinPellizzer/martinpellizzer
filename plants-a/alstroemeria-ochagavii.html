Alstroemeria ochagavii

Alstroemeria ochagavii, commonly known as the Ochagavia alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has adapted to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
