Alyssanthus

Alyssanthus, commonly known as the common alyssum, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are native to southern Europe and western Asia, and they typically grow in dry, rocky habitats. Alyssanthus species are characterized by their small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The leaves are usually simple and alternate, and the plants often have a bushy growth habit.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making them beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
