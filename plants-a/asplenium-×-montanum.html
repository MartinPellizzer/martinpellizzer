Asplenium × montanum

Asplenium × montanum, commonly known as the mountain spleenwort, is a hybrid fern that occurs naturally in various regions with moist, shaded habitats.

It belongs to the Aspleniaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky crevices or on tree trunks, demonstrating its adaptability to challenging environments. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with spores produced on the undersides of the leaves.

Asplenium × montanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
