Abies vejarii var. macrocarpa

Abies vejarii var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada fir, is a variant of the white fir native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are slightly twisted. The cones are typically long and cylindrical, with bracts that are often visible at the base. It thrives in cool, moist environments at high elevations, often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
