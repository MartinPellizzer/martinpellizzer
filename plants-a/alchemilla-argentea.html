Alchemilla argentea

Alchemilla argentea, commonly known as silver plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It features basal rosettes of silver-gray, soft, velvety leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in branched clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical name reflects its historical use in alchemy and traditional herbal medicine.
