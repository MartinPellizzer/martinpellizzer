Antirrhinum majus subsp. molle

Antirrhinum majus subsp.

molle, commonly known as the soft antirrhinum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often arranged oppositely along the stem. This subspecies is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

It is cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
