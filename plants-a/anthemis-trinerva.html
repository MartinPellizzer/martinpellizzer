Anthemis trinerva

Anthemis trinerva, commonly known as the narrowleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, yellowish flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, with narrow, linear leaves. It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
