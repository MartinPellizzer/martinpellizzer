Aspalathus aurantiaca

Aspalathus aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Aspalathus aurantiaca is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
