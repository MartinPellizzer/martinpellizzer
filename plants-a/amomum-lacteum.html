Amomum lacteum

Amomum lacteum, commonly known as the milky amomum, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's name refers to the milky sap that exudes from its stems when damaged.
