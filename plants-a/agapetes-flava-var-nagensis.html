Agapetes flava var. nagensis

Agapetes flava var. nagensis, commonly known as the yellow-flowered agapete, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments such as cloud forests and mountainous areas. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Agapetes flava var. nagensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
