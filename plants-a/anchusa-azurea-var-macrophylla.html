Anchusa azurea var. macrophylla

Anchusa azurea var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved bugloss, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
