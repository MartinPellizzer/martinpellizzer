Adiantum intermedium var. triangulatum

Adiantum intermedium var. triangulatum, commonly known as the triangular maidenhair fern, is a delicate and ornamental fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It features finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically bright green and have a graceful, arching appearance. The plant prefers shady, moist environments and thrives in well-drained, humus-rich soil. Its fronds are bi-directionally pinnate, with triangular-shaped pinnae that give it its distinctive name.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in shaded garden settings or as a houseplant.
