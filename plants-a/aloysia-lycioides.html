Aloysia lycioides

Aloysia lycioides, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Aloysia lycioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
