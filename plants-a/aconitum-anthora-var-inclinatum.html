Aconitum anthora var. inclinatum

Aconitum anthora var. inclinatum, commonly known as the leaning aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are palmately compound, with a distinctive silvery-white underside.

Aconitum anthora var. inclinatum is known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that make it dangerous to both humans and animals.
