Astragalus sharifii

Astragalus sharifii, commonly known as Sharif's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by pods containing several seeds. Astragalus sharifii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
