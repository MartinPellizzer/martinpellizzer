Antidesma japonicum

Antidesma japonicum, commonly known as Japanese antidesma, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia, a typical feature of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Antidesma japonicum is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
