Acer hyrcanum subsp. stevenii

Acer hyrcanum subsp.

stevenii, commonly known as the Steven's maple, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its palmate leaves that are typically 5-9 lobes, with a serrated margin.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The bark is smooth and light grey, becoming fissured with age.
