Aspilia cearensis

Aspilia cearensis, commonly known as the Cearense torchflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescence. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in a dense, cylindrical shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Aspilia cearensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
