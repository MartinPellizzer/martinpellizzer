Azalea edgeworthii

Azalea edgeworthii, commonly known as the Edgeworth's azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Azalea edgeworthii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
