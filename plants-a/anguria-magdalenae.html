Anguria magdalenae

Anguria magdalenae, commonly known as the Magdalene watermelon, is a rare and unique species native to the arid regions of the Magdalena River Valley in Colombia.

This plant is characterized by its large, pale green rind and vibrant red flesh, which is often speckled with dark seeds. It thrives in hot, dry climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. The fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, as it contains high levels of water and natural sugars.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Anguria magdalenae is considered a valuable but endangered species in botanical studies.
