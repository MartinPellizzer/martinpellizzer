Agrimonia pilosa var. coreana

Agrimonia pilosa var. coreana, commonly known as Korean agrimony, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, woody, cone-shaped fruits called achenes, which are surrounded by bracts. Its leaves are typically compound, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Agrimonia pilosa var. coreana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
