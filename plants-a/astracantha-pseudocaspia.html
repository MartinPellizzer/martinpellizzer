Astracantha pseudocaspia

Astracantha pseudocaspia, commonly known as the Caspian thornvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astracantha pseudocaspia is often found in steppe and semi-desert regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
