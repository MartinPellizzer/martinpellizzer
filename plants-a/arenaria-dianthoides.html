Arenaria dianthoides

Arenaria dianthoides, commonly known as sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or rocky soils, often found in coastal areas and dry habitats. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
