Aconitum sinomontanum var. sinomontanum

Aconitum sinomontanum var. sinomontanum, commonly known as the Chinese monk's hood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

Aconitum sinomontanum var. sinomontanum is known for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical use in traditional medicine.
