Abies semenovii

Abies semenovii, commonly known as the Siberian fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing seeds surrounded by a winged aril. It thrives in cold, high-altitude environments and is adapted to harsh climatic conditions.

The Siberian fir plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
