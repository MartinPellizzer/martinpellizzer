Astragalus stramineus

Astragalus stramineus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Astragalus stramineus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it ecologically significant in soil enrichment.
