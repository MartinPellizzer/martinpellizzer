Anthyllis vulneraria var. webbiana

Anthyllis vulneraria var. webbiana, commonly known as the Webb's kidney vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. The plant features slender stems with small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
