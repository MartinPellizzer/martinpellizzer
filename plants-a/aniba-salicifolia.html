Aniba salicifolia

Aniba salicifolia, commonly known as Brazilian cedar or white cedar, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, berry-like fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its aromatic wood and ornamental appeal.

Aniba salicifolia plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
