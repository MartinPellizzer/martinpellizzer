Appendicula montana

Appendicula montana, commonly known as the mountain appendicula, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils at high elevations. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Appendicula montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
