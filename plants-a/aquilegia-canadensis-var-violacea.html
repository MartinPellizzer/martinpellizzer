Aquilegia canadensis var. violacea

Aquilegia canadensis var. violacea, commonly known as the purple-flowered columbine, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with five sepals that curve backward. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for butterflies and bees.
