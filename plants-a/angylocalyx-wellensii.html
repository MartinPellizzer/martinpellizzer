Angylocalyx wellensii

Angylocalyx wellensii, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
