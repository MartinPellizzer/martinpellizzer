Asparagus racemosus

Asparagus racemosus, commonly known as shatavari, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into spiky, berry-like fruits. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous small leaflets arranged along the stem.

The botanical features of Asparagus racemosus make it a valuable species in both traditional medicine and horticulture.
