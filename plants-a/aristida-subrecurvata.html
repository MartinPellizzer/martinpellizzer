Aristida subrecurvata

Aristida subrecurvata, commonly known as the narrowleaf threeawn, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Aristida subrecurvata is well adapted to drought conditions and is considered a resilient native plant in its habitat.
