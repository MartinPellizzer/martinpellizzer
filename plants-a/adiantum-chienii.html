Adiantum chienii

Adiantum chienii, commonly known as the Chinese maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to China.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests or rocky areas. Its leaves are bioluminescent, emitting a faint glow in the dark, which is a unique adaptation.

Adiantum chienii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
