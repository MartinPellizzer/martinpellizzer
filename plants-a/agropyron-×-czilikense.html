Agropyron × czilikense

Agropyron × czilikense, commonly known as the intermediate wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species resulting from the cross between Agropyron cristatum and Agropyron elongatum.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. The plant produces dense, fibrous roots that help prevent soil erosion and improve soil structure. Its flowering occurs in late summer, with spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple hues.

This species is valued for its forage quality and potential use in sustainable agriculture and ecological restoration projects.
