Acioa staudtii

Acioa staudtii, commonly known as the Staudt's acacia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring bipinnate leaves and yellow or white flowers.

Acioa staudtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
