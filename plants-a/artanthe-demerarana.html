Artanthe demerarana

Artanthe demerarana, commonly known as the Demerara artanthe, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Guyana and Suriname.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are alternate and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are upright and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Artanthe demerarana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
