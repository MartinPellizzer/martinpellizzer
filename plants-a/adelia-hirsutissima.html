Adelia hirsutissima

Adelia hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy adelia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Adelia hirsutissima is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in its natural habitat.
