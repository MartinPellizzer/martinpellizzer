Ardisia warmingii

Ardisia warmingii, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring broad, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. These fruits are edible for humans and birds but contain toxic seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fruit display.
