Astragalus punctatus

Astragalus punctatus, commonly known as the dotted milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It produces small, round seeds that are often dispersed by wind.

Astragalus punctatus is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various wildlife species.
