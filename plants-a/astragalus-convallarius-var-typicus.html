Astragalus convallarius var. typicus

Astragalus convallarius var. typicus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has compound leaves with thread-like leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
