Ampelopsis rubifolia

Ampelopsis rubifolia, commonly known as red-berried honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a reddish or bronze hue. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers followed by bright red berries that persist into winter. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its climbing habit and showy fruits make it a popular choice for vertical gardens and erosion control.
