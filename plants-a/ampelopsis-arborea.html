Ampelopsis arborea

Ampelopsis arborea, commonly known as the climbing false virgin's bower, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries that attract birds. Its woody stems can climb up to 20 feet, making it a common sight in woodland areas.

Ampelopsis arborea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
