Acacia reficiens subsp. misera

Acacia reficiens subsp.

misera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, narrow phyllodes that are typically yellow-green in color.

The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that appear in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
