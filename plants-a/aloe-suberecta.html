Aloe suberecta

Aloe suberecta, commonly known as the upright aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically upright and covered in a white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers on tall, branching stems during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Aloe suberecta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
