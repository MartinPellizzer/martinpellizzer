Astragalus kentrophyta var. elatus

Astragalus kentrophyta var. elatus, commonly known as the tall milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It is often found in desert scrub and steppe habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
