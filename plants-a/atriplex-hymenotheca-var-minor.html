Atriplex hymenotheca var. minor

Atriplex hymenotheca var. minor, commonly known as saltbush or shadscale, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is widely distributed across the western United States and parts of Mexico, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
