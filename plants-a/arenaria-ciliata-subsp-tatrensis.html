Arenaria ciliata subsp. tatrensis

Arenaria ciliata subsp.

tatrensis, commonly known as the Tatran sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It typically thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and ciliate, with fine hairs along the edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
