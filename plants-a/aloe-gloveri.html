Aloe gloveri

Aloe gloveri, commonly known as the Glovers' aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, yellowish flowers. Aloe gloveri is adapted to survive in dry conditions, storing water in its leaves and stems.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
