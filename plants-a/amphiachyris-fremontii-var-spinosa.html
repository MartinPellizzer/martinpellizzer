Amphiachyris fremontii var. spinosa

Amphiachyris fremontii var. spinosa, commonly known as the spiny amphiachyris, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and erosion control in dry landscapes.
