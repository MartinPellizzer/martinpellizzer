Aconitum × paniculatum var. podolicum

Aconitum × paniculatum var. podolicum, commonly known as the Podolic Himalayan monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
