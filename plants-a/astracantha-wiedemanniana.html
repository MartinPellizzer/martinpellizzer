Astracantha wiedemanniana

Astracantha wiedemanniana, commonly known as the Wiedemann's thornveld, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clustered, small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Astracantha wiedemanniana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
