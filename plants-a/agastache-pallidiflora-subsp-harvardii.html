Agastache pallidiflora subsp. harvardii

Agastache pallidiflora subsp.

harvardii, commonly known as the Harvard false dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant features upright, slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, producing showy, tubular blooms in shades of purple and blue.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open meadows and grasslands.
