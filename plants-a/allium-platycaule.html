Allium platycaule

Allium platycaule, commonly known as the flat-leaved garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a bulbous base with a strong, pungent odor, typical of the Allium genus. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or purple flowers. It is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
