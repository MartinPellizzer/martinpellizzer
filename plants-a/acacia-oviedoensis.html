Acacia oviedoensis

Acacia oviedoensis, commonly known as the Oviedo acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feather-like segments. It produces yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its seeds are enclosed in elongated, woody pods.

Acacia oviedoensis is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
