Adenophora polymorpha var. urticifolia

Adenophora polymorpha var. urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging adenophora, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features upright stems covered with fine, stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
