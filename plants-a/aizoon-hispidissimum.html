Aizoon hispidissimum

Aizoon hispidissimum, commonly known as the hispid thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, glandular leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough, fuzzy texture.

Aizoon hispidissimum is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
