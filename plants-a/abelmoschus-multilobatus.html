Abelmoschus multilobatus

Abelmoschus multilobatus, commonly known as the Chinese okra or Indian spinach, is a flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces edible pods that are similar in appearance to okra but have a milder flavor. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Abelmoschus multilobatus is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.
