Alpinia speciosa var. longiramosa

Alpinia speciosa var. longiramosa, commonly known as the long-stemmed ginger, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking, fragrant inflorescences with pale yellow to white flowers, often adorned with red or pink bracts. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal use.

The variety 'longiramosa' is distinguished by its elongated, drooping stems, which contribute to its unique visual appeal.
