Amyema canaliculata

Amyema canaliculata, commonly known as the mountain mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, white to pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland understories.

Amyema canaliculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
