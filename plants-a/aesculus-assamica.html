Aesculus assamica

Aesculus assamica, commonly known as the Assam horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets. The tree produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers in upright clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a spiny capsule containing several chestnut-like seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
