Ardisia squamulosa

Ardisia squamulosa, commonly known as the silverleaf ardisia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Ardisia squamulosa is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
