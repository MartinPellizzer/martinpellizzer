Andropogon macrourus var. pumilus

Andropogon macrourus var. pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, making it suitable for use in native plant gardens and restoration projects. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ecological benefits, including soil stabilization and habitat provision for wildlife.
