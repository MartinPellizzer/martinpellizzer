Acinodendron glomeratum

Acinodendron glomeratum, commonly known as the clustered acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small, shrub-like species that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, giving it its scientific name. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Acinodendron glomeratum is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with minimal water requirements.
