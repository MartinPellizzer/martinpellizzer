Annona ekmanii

Annona ekmanii, commonly known as the Kauai mountain custard apple, is a tropical plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a small tree or shrub that produces a fruit with a thick, leathery skin and a creamy, custard-like pulp. The plant belongs to the Annonaceae family, which includes other custard apple species. Its flowers are typically greenish-white with a strong, unpleasant odor, attracting certain pollinators.

The fruit is edible and has a unique flavor, though it is less commonly cultivated outside of Hawaii.
