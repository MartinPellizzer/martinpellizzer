Attalea macrolepis

Attalea macrolepis, commonly known as the areca palm or Brazilian fan palm, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered fruits, each containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of palm oil.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining soil stability.
