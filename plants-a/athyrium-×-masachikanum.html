Athyrium × masachikanum

Athyrium × masachikanum, commonly known as the Japanese painted fern, is a hybrid fern native to Japan.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lace-like fronds that display a striking combination of green, silver, and sometimes red hues. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as woodland areas or garden beds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread gradually, forming dense clusters over time.

The foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
