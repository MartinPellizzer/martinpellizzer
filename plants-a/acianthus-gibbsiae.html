Acianthus gibbsiae

Acianthus gibbsiae, commonly known as the Gibbs' acianthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of purple and pink, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
