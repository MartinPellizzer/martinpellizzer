Artemisia giraldii var. giraldii

Artemisia giraldii var. giraldii, commonly known as golden sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-green foliage and yellowish-green flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its strong, minty scent and have been used in traditional herbal remedies.
