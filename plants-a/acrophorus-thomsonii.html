Acrophorus thomsonii

Acrophorus thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to dry conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater. Its seed pods are woody and contain small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Thomson's acacia is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its hardy nature in harsh environments.
