Ardisia clusioides

Ardisia clusioides, commonly known as the red-rooted beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its root system is dense and fibrous, which helps it thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Ardisia clusioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and seasonal fruit display.
