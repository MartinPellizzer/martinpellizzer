Alyssum leucadeum f. scopulorum

Alyssum leucadeum f. scopulorum, commonly known as the rock allyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It features dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has succulent, gray-green foliage that is adapted to withstand coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact form and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
