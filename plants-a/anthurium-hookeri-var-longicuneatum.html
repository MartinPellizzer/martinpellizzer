Anthurium hookeri var. longicuneatum

Anthurium hookeri var. longicuneatum, commonly known as the long-spurred anthurium, is a tropical evergreen plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are club-shaped inflorescences that are often bright red or orange, surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
