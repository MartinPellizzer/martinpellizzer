Alloplectus hirtellus

Alloplectus hirtellus, commonly known as the hairy false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring. Alloplectus hirtellus has a fibrous root system and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
