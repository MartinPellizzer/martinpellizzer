Andryala pinnatifida subsp. cuneifolia

Andryala pinnatifida subsp.

cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf andryala, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features slender, upright stems with alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are finely divided and have a distinctive cuneiform shape.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in terminal clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
