Aspalathus asparagoides subsp. gracilis

Aspalathus asparagoides subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the slender bushveld pea, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its small, upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
