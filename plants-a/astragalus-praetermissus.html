Astragalus praetermissus

Astragalus praetermissus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh desert conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets, and it produces cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Astragalus praetermissus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
