Astragalus exscapus proles transsilvanicus

Astragalus exscapus proles transsilvanicus, commonly known as the Transylvanian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its roots are known for their deep penetration into the soil. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in meadows and forest edges.

This species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a forage plant for livestock.
