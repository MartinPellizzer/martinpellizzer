Acacia incanicarpa

Acacia incanicarpa, commonly known as the grey acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, pale green or greyish leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by long, narrow pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

The species is known for its drought tolerance and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
