Acorus rumphianus

Acorus rumphianus, commonly known as narrowleaf sweetflag, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshes in Southeast Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are usually sword-shaped, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spadices. It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments with moderate to high light exposure.

Acorus rumphianus is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
