Amaranthus congestus

Amaranthus congestus, commonly known as red amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, branching stems and vibrant red to purple foliage, which are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by feathery seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscapes.

Amaranthus congestus is also valued for its nutritional content, as its leaves are edible and rich in protein and minerals.
