Asplenium lunulatum var. sellowianum

Asplenium lunulatum var. sellowianum, commonly known as the Sellow's spleenwort, is a fern species native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, dark green, strap-shaped leaves that are often clustered closely together. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices or on tree trunks. Its spore-producing structures, called sorocarps, are borne on the underside of the leaves.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
