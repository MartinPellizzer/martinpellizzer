Adelphia

Adelphia, commonly known as the "butterfly plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to various regions in Africa, Asia, and the Americas, and is characterized by its striking, butterfly-like flowers. The plants typically have compound leaves and produce small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. Adelphia species are often found in tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils.

They are valued not only for their ornamental beauty but also for their ecological role in supporting pollinators.
