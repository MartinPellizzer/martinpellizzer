Allium lucens

Allium lucens, commonly known as the shining onion, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits. Allium lucens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and potentially useful in phytotherapy.
