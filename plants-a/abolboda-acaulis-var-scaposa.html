Abolboda acaulis var. scaposa

Abolboda acaulis var. scaposa, commonly known as the scapose rock cress, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, perennial herb characterized by its compact growth habit and absence of a stem, with leaves directly arising from the rootstock. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually basal, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are borne on short, leafless stalks.

This variety is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh conditions.
