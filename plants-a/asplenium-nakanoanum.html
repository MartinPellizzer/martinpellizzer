Asplenium nakanoanum

Asplenium nakanoanum, commonly known as the Nakano's spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base, forming a rosette structure. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Asplenium nakanoanum is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
