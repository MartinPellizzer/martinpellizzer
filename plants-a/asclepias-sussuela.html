Asclepias sussuela

Asclepias sussuela, commonly known as the lesser milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the milkweed family, Asclepiadaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers. The plant produces follicles that contain toxic, sticky seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert environments.

Asclepias sussuela is an important plant for certain species of butterflies, particularly the lesser sulfur butterfly.
