Ammannia discolor

Ammannia discolor, commonly known as prairie ammannia, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-tinged stems and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks and in wetlands, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

Ammannia discolor is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
