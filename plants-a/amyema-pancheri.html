Amyema pancheri

Amyema pancheri, commonly known as the Pancheri mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It has a distinctive appearance with small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale green flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are a food source for birds and other wildlife. Its parasitic nature means it derives some of its nutrients from its host tree, while still performing photosynthesis.

Amyema pancheri plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by supporting biodiversity through its interactions with birds and other organisms.
