Aster amani

Aster amani, commonly known as the Amani aster, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. Its showy blue or purple flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, Aster amani is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
