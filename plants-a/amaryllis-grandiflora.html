Amaryllis grandiflora

Amaryllis grandiflora, commonly known as the giant amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in length, typically in shades of red, white, or pink. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are broad and waxy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Amaryllis grandiflora is often cultivated for its dramatic blooms and is a popular choice for gardens and indoor displays.
