Acomastylis turbinata f. scapoidea

Acomastylis turbinata f. scapoidea, commonly known as the scapoidea form of the turbinated acomastylis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments. It has a compact growth habit with a rosette of leaves and produces a flowering stem that is shorter than the leaves.

This form is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and floral structure, making it a notable variant within the species.
