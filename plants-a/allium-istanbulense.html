Allium istanbulense

Allium istanbulense, commonly known as Istanbul garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its onion-like bulbs and grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and mild garlic scent, it is often cultivated for both decorative and culinary purposes.
