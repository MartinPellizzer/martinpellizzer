Aethionema rubescens

Aethionema rubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are small, bright red, and borne in clusters, adding a striking visual element to rocky or gravelly habitats.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
