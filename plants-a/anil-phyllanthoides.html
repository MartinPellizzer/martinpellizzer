Anil phyllanthoides

Anil phyllanthoides, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.
