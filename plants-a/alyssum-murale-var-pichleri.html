Alyssum murale var. pichleri

Alyssum murale var. pichleri, commonly known as the creeping alpine alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compact, mat-forming foliage with silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
