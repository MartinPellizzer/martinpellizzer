Astragalus hypoglottis var. dasyglottis

Astragalus hypoglottis var. dasyglottis, commonly known as the hairy-tongued milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, often glandular texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

It is often found in sagebrush steppe and other arid or semi-arid habitats.
