Arceuthobium vaginatum f. cryptopodum

Arceuthobium vaginatum f. cryptopodum, commonly known as the cryptopodous broomrape, is a holoparasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its lack of stems and leaves, with a taproot system that attaches to the roots of its host plant. Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the growth and health of its host.
