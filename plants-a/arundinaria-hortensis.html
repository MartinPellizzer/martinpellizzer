Arundinaria hortensis

Arundinaria hortensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or garden bamboo, is a clump-forming grass native to East Asia.

It features slender, erect culms that are often used in ornamental gardening due to their elegant appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive foliage.

Arundinaria hortensis is valued for its adaptability and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
