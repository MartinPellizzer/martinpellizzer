Aa leucantha

Aa leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered datura, is a species of flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom at night. The plant has thick, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Aa leucantha is known for its potent alkaloids, which make it both toxic and historically significant in traditional Hawaiian medicine.

Its unique ecological role and striking appearance make it a notable species in Hawaiian flora.
