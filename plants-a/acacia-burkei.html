Acacia burkei

Acacia burkei, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flower heads, which are typical of the Acacia genus. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system. Its flowers are an important food source for native pollinators, including bees and birds.

Acacia burkei is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitat.
