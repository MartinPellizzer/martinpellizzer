Aconitum pterocaule var. albidum

Aconitum pterocaule var. albidum, commonly known as the white-flowered monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, helmet-shaped blue or white flowers. The plant features a tall, unbranched stem with palmately compound leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum pterocaule var. albidum is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, though it contains toxic alkaloids.
