Aclisia macrophylla

Aclisia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
