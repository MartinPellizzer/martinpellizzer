Allium idzuense

Allium idzuense, commonly known as the Japanese garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered purple flowers. Its bulbs are often used in culinary applications, similar to other Allium species.

Botanically, it is characterized by its aromatic compounds and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.
