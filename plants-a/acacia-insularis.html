Acacia insularis

Acacia insularis, commonly known as the Socotra acacia, is a unique flowering plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-shaped crown and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow, globular flowers that bloom in clusters. Acacia insularis is adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions.

It is also notable for its role in the local ecosystem and its historical significance in traditional medicine.
