Athyrium scorpioideum

Athyrium scorpioideum, commonly known as the scorpion fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive, narrow, and elongated appearance. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Athyrium scorpioideum thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
