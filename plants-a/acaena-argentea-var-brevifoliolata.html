Acaena argentea var. brevifoliolata

Acaena argentea var. brevifoliolata, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by small, spiny fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects for soil stabilization.
