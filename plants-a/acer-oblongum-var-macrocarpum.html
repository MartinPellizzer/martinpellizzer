Acer oblongum var. macrocarpum

Acer oblongum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-seeded maple, is a variant of the maple tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. The tree produces large, showy flowers in spring, followed by distinctive, elongated fruits called samaras. Its bark is smooth and grey, becoming more fissured with age.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
