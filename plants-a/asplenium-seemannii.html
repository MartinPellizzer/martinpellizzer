Asplenium seemannii

Asplenium seemannii, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows on rocky surfaces or in crevices, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its spores are produced in small, clustered sori on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium seemannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a houseplant.
