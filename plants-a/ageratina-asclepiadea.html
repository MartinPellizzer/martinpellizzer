Ageratina asclepiadea

Ageratina asclepiadea, commonly known as white beggarticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are alternate and often lobed. It is often found in moist habitats such as wetlands and stream banks.

Ageratina asclepiadea is also known for its use in traditional medicine.
