Agave ventum-versa

Agave ventum-versa, commonly known as the reverse ventum agave, is a species native to specific regions of Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves. The leaves are typically blue-gray in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white flowers that bloom in the spring.

This plant is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal watering once established.
