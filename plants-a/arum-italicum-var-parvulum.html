Arum italicum var. parvulum

Arum italicum var. parvulum, commonly known as the Italian arum or dwarf arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are dark green with a silvery underside. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a spathe, which is typically bright yellow or orange. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and as a houseplant.
