Abies religiosa subsp. hickelii

Abies religiosa subsp.

hickelii, commonly known as the Mexican fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the high altitudes of Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches.

The cones are typically pendulous, with elongated, woody scales that release seeds in the fall. This subspecies thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in montane forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
