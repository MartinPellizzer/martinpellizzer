Astragalus melanostachys

Astragalus melanostachys, commonly known as blackstem milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats. It has small, pinnately compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus melanostachys is adapted to arid environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
