Arrojadoa albiflora

Arrojadoa albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered arrojadoa, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-gray pubescence. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in high-altitude regions with well-drained soil.

Arrojadoa albiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
