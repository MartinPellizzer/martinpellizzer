Allium nerimaniae

Allium nerimaniae, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the high elevations of the Sierra Nevada mountains in California.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks, and is characterized by its fleshy, bulbous base and grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring or early summer.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for botanists and horticulturists studying alpine flora.
