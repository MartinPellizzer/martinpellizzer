Alifiola

Alifiola, commonly known as the sacred fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, green fruits that turn yellow when ripe. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine. Alifiola thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense canopies make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
