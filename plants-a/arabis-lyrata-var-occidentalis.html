Arabis lyrata var. occidentalis

Arabis lyrata var. occidentalis, commonly known as the Western rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate with a wavy margin, and it produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers. It is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants.
