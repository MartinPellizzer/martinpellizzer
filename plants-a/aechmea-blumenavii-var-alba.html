Aechmea blumenavii var. alba

Aechmea blumenavii var. alba, commonly known as the white-tipped pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a white or silver midrib. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears bright orange to red flowers, which are followed by small, white or pale green berries. Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its aesthetic appeal.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
