Agave concinna

Agave concinna, commonly known as the narrowleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and blue-green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces a tall, flowering stalk that bears yellowish-green flowers, which are followed by small, pear-shaped fruits. Agave concinna is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact size and striking appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
