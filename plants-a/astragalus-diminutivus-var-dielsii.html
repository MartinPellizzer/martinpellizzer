Astragalus diminutivus var. dielsii

Astragalus diminutivus var. dielsii, commonly known as the Diels' milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role in stabilizing soils and its potential for medicinal use.
