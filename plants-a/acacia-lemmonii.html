Acacia lemmonii

Acacia lemmonii, commonly known as the desert wisteria, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant produces flat, narrow leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs, and it has a deep root system adapted to arid environments. Acacia lemmonii is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance in desert ecosystems.
