Andromeda villosa

Andromeda villosa, commonly known as the hairy andromeda, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has a distinctive hairy texture on its stems and leaves, giving it its common name. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands.

Andromeda villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
