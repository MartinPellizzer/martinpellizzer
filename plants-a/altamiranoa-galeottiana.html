Altamiranoa galeottiana

Altamiranoa galeottiana, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a shrub native to Brazil, characterized by its hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical regions.
