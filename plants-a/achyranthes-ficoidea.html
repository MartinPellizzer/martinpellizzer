Achyranthes ficoidea

Achyranthes ficoidea, commonly known as the Indian spinach or horse radish, is a herbaceous plant native to South Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by oblong, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
