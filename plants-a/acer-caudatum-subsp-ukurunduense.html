Acer caudatum subsp. ukurunduense

Acer caudatum subsp.

ukurunduense, commonly known as the U kurundu maple, is a rare and distinctive species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa. It is characterized by its striking, cascading foliage, with leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
