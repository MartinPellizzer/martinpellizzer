Ammios muricata

Ammios muricata, commonly known as the roughleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its rough, textured leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Ammios muricata is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it a hardy choice for gardens in warmer climates.
