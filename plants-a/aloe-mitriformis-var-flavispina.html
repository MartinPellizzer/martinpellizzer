Aloe mitriformis var. flavispina

Aloe mitriformis var. flavispina, commonly known as the yellow-spined aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge, and it produces yellow flowers in terminal racemes. The plant is characterized by its distinctive yellow spines along the leaf margins, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry climates.
