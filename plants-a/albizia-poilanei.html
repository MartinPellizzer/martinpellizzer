Albizia poilanei

Albizia poilanei, commonly known as the Madagascar wisteria, is a leguminous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant has a distinctive branching pattern and is often found in humid, tropical environments. Its seeds are encased in pods and can remain viable for several years.

Albizia poilanei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
