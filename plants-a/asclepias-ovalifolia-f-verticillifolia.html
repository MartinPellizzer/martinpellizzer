Asclepias ovalifolia f. verticillifolia

Asclepias ovalifolia f. verticillifolia, commonly known as the verticillifoliate milkweed, is a variant of the ovalleaf milkweed.

It is characterized by its unique, whorled arrangement of leaves, which gives it its specific epithet "verticillifolia.". The plant produces clusters of pink to reddish-orange flowers, which are typical of the Asclepias genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This form is particularly valued for its role in supporting monarch butterfly populations by providing nectar and serving as a host plant for their larvae.
