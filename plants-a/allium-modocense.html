Allium modocense

Allium modocense, commonly known as Modoc garlic, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a single, tall, cylindrical stem that bears a cluster of small, white to pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Allium modocense thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
