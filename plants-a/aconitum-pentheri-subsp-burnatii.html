Aconitum pentheri subsp. burnatii

Aconitum pentheri subsp.

burnatii, commonly known as Burnat's monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Eastern Himalayas. It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has deeply lobed, palmately arranged leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
