Arum phrygium

Arum phrygium, commonly known as the purple arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a large, heart-shaped leaf that can grow up to 2 meters in length, with a deep purple coloration on its underside. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a showy purple spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant contains calcium oxalate crystals, which can cause irritation if ingested.
