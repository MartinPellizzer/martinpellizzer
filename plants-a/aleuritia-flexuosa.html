Aleuritia flexuosa

Aleuritia flexuosa, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The berries it produces are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor.
