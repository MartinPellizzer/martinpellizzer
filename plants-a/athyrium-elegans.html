Athyrium elegans

Athyrium elegans, commonly known as the elegant male fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically dark green with a slightly glossy surface. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Athyrium elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
