Alsophila ursina

Alsophila ursina, commonly known as the bear's paw fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive, broad, wedge-shaped base that resembles a bear's paw, hence its common name. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in primary forests.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
