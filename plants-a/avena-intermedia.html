Avena intermedia

Avena intermedia, commonly known as intermediate oat, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

Avena intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
