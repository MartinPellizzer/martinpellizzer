Arthrochilus irritabilis

Arthrochilus irritabilis, commonly known as the irritability orchid, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has a terrestrial growth habit, with a short, stocky rhizome and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinct lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

This species is adapted to harsh environments, showcasing resilience through its specialized morphology and reproductive strategies.
