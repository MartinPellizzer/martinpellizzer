Anetanthus ruber

Anetanthus ruber, commonly known as the red anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, crimson petals that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its leaves are basal, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Anetanthus ruber is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
