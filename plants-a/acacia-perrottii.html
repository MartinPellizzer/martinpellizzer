Acacia perrottii

Acacia perrottii, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salt spray.

Acacia perrottii is often used in coastal stabilization due to its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
