Astragalus curvicarpus var. curvicarpus

Astragalus curvicarpus var. curvicarpus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its root system is fibrous and deep-reaching. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
