Achyranthes aspera var. indica

Achyranthes aspera var. indica, commonly known as Indian knotgrass, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its roots are known for their medicinal properties and are used in traditional herbal remedies.
