Asplenium niponicum

Asplenium niponicum, commonly known as Japanese spleenworts, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a distinctive, intricate venation pattern.

Asplenium niponicum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
