Argyrocome polyphylla

Argyrocome polyphylla, commonly known as the silver-leafed fern, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in a fine layer of silvery hair, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or on rocky slopes. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, meaning they are divided into smaller, secondary pinnae, which contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat preferences, it is a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticultural cultivation.
