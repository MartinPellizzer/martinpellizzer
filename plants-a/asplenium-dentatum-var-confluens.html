Asplenium dentatum var. confluens

Asplenium dentatum var. confluens, commonly known as the clustered spleenwort, is a fern native to eastern North America.

It is a small, evergreen fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often clustered at the base, giving it its common name. Its fronds are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 20 centimeters.

This variety is characterized by its dense growth habit and is often found in association with other epiphytic and lithophytic plants.
