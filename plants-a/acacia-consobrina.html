Acacia consobrina

Acacia consobrina, commonly known as the silky oaks, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its distinctive bipinnate leaves and yellow to golden flowers that bloom in spring. Acacia consobrina thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils.

It is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to provide shade.
