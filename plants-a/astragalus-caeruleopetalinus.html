Astragalus caeruleopetalinus

Astragalus caeruleopetalinus, commonly known as blue-petaled milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Kazakhstan, and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus caeruleopetalinus is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
