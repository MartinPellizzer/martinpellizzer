Aconitum napellus subsp. superbum

Aconitum napellus subsp.

superbum, commonly known as the European monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has dark green, doubly serrated leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
