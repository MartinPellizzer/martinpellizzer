Allosorus crispus var. chilensis

Allosorus crispus var. chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean silver fern, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories. Its reproductive structures include spore-bearing cones, which are clustered at the base of the plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
