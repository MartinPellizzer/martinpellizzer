Aster discoideus

Aster discoideus, commonly known as the spoonwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and produces small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it typically grows in moist, disturbed soils.

Aster discoideus is often found in wet meadows, ditches, and along the edges of water bodies.
