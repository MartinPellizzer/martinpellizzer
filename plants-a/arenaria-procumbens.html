Arenaria procumbens

Arenaria procumbens, commonly known as sand pink, is a low-growing flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a mat-forming habit with prostrate stems that root at the nodes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to withstand cold and windy conditions.

It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous environments.
