Acacia incurva var. brachyptera

Acacia incurva var. brachyptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, narrow, elliptical leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
