Amerimnon lanceolatum

Amerimnon lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, acidic soils. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a dense, grass-like cluster. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or brown and appear in early spring.

Amerimnon lanceolatum plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
