Adenia erecta

Adenia erecta, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. The plant has thick, fleshy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter.

Adenia erecta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, humid climates.
