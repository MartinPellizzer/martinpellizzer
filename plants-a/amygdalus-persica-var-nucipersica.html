Amygdalus persica var. nucipersica

Amygdalus persica var. nucipersica, commonly known as the Persian almond, is a cultivar of the almond tree native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped nuts with a thin, hard shell. The plant produces fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its edible seeds. The leaves are alternate, simple, and serrated, with a dark green color.

This variety is valued for its nut quality and is often cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
