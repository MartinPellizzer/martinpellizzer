Anthacanthus cuneifolius

Anthacanthus cuneifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed anthacanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often with a slightly hairy texture. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a pointed tip and a smooth margin, typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, usually displaying a pale yellow to greenish hue.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or along riverbanks.
