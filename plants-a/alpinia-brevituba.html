Alpinia brevituba

Alpinia brevituba, commonly known as the short-stemmed ginger, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features tall, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, clustered in inflorescences at the stem tips.

Alpinia brevituba is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
