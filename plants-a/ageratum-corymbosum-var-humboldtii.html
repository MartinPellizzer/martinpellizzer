Ageratum corymbosum var. humboldtii

Ageratum corymbosum var. humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered inflorescences. The flowers are typically blue to violet in color, with white centers, and bloom throughout the growing season. The plant prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden beds and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
