Aeonium canariense subsp. virgineum

Aeonium canariense subsp.

virgineum, commonly known as the Canary Island aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands. It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern.

The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture, adapted to store water. It produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months.
