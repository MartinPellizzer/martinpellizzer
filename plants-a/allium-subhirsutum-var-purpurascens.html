Allium subhirsutum var. purpurascens

Allium subhirsutum var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple garlic.

This perennial herb is characterized by its rosette of grayish-green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of pale purple to pink flowers. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant is closely related to other Allium species and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it both a decorative and culinary plant.
