Arabis filifolia

Arabis filifolia, commonly known as the filifolium rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features basal leaves that are linear and thread-like, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a valued addition to botanical collections.
