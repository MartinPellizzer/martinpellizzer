Acacia leucoclada subsp. leucoclada

Acacia leucoclada subsp.

leucoclada, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, clustered phyllodes that are typically pale green to whitish in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are often visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardy nature and aesthetic features.
