Allantodia metteniana

Allantodia metteniana, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a humid, warm climate.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
