Argemone hunnemannii

Argemone hunnemannii, commonly known as the desert castor or yellow argemone, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is an annual herb with yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain oily seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its botanical features include hairy stems, alternate leaves, and a distinctive yellow-pink flower color.
