Acinodendron flammeum

Acinodendron flammeum, commonly known as the flame acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in dense inflorescences, giving it a fiery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its aesthetic appeal.
