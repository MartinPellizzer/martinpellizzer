Allium ascalonicum var. condensum

Allium ascalonicum var. condensum, commonly known as the Chinese leek or Egyptian walking onion, is a bulbous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered bulbs that resemble small onions, with slender, grass-like leaves emerging from the base. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk topped with small, white or pinkish flowers, which are often used in ornamental gardening. It is native to southern Europe and western Asia but is now cultivated in various regions for both culinary and ornamental purposes.

The plant is hardy and adaptable, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
