Allium sativum var. subrotundum

Allium sativum var. subrotundum, commonly known as garlic, is a bulbous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, sulfur-containing compounds that contribute to its distinct flavor and medicinal properties. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by the development of the edible bulb. Its leaves are long, grass-like, and often used in culinary applications.

This variety is valued for both its culinary uses and its traditional medicinal applications.
