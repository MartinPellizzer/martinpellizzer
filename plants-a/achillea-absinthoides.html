Achillea absinthoides

Achillea absinthoides, commonly known as sweet yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, bright yellow to white flower heads. The plant has feathery, aromatic leaves that are often used in herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Its historical use dates back to ancient times, where it was valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
