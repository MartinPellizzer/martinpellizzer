Acalypha spiciflora

Acalypha spiciflora, commonly known as the spikeflower or red-hot poker, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features showy, spiky inflorescences that are typically red or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has alternate, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Acalypha spiciflora is valued for its ornamental foliage and vibrant floral display.
