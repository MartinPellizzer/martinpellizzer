Anthyllis vulneraria

Anthyllis vulneraria, commonly known as the wounded healer, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and yellow or white pea-like flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in rocky or sandy soils. It has a deep root system that helps it thrive in harsh environments.

The common name "wounded healer" refers to its historical use in traditional medicine for treating wounds and inflammation.
