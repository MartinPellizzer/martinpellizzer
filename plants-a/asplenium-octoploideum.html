Asplenium octoploideum

Asplenium octoploideum, commonly known as the octoploid shield fern, is a rare and unusual member of the Aspleniaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique octoploid genome, meaning it has eight sets of chromosomes, which is uncommon in the plant kingdom. The plant produces small, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and densely veined. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its rarity and genetic complexity, it is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
