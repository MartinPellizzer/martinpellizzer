Amaranthus leucospermus

Amaranthus leucospermus, commonly known as white amaranth, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, triangular seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and is adaptable to various soil types.

Amaranthus leucospermus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and nutritional benefits.
