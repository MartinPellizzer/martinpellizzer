Angraecum viguieri

Angraecum viguieri, commonly known as the Viguieri's star orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescence bearing numerous fragrant, white flowers with intricate patterns. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and good drainage for optimal growth. Its flowers are pollinated by specific moths, showcasing a fascinating example of co-evolution between plants and their pollinators.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
