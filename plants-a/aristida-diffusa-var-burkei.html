Aristida diffusa var. burkei

Aristida diffusa var. burkei, commonly known as the burke's threeawn, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, rocky soils, and disturbed areas. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are characteristic of the Aristida genus. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture that aids in water conservation.

This species is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in arid conditions.
