Afrocanthium rondoense

Afrocanthium rondoense, commonly known as the Rondo sandbush, is a shrub native to the East African region, particularly Tanzania and Mozambique.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in compact clusters. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, with a dense covering of fine hairs.

Afrocanthium rondoense plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
