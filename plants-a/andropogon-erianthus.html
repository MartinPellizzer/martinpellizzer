Andropogon erianthus

Andropogon erianthus, commonly known as switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves. This plant is highly adaptable, thriving in a variety of soil types and environmental conditions. Switchgrass is widely cultivated for its potential as a bioenergy crop due to its high biomass yield and low input requirements.

Its ecological benefits include soil stabilization and habitat provision for wildlife.
