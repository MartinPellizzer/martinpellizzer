Anthericum stolzii

Anthericum stolzii, commonly known as the Stolz's nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Anthericum stolzii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
