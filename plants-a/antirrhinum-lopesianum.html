Antirrhinum lopesianum

Antirrhinum lopesianum, commonly known as the Lopesian snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, tubular flowers that resemble small snout-like structures. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its vibrant flowers, which range in color from red to yellow, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
