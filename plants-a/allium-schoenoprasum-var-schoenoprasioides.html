Allium schoenoprasum var. schoenoprasioides

Allium schoenoprasum var. schoenoprasioides, commonly known as the Siberian chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbs that are often used in culinary applications, similar to regular chives. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its hardy nature makes it suitable for growing in colder regions with proper care.
