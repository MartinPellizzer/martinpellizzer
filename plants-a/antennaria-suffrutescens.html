Antennaria suffrutescens

Antennaria suffrutescens, commonly known as the pink sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils, often in open, sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Antennaria suffrutescens is adapted to harsh climates and is often found in alpine or desert regions.
