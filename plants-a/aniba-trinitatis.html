Aniba trinitatis

Aniba trinitatis, commonly known as the Trinidadian or South American spicebush, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and camphor. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round fruits.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
