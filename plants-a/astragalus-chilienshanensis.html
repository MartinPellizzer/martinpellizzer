Astragalus chilienshanensis

Astragalus chilienshanensis, commonly known as the Chilian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to grow in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
