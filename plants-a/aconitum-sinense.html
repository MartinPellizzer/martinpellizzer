Aconitum sinense

Aconitum sinense, commonly known as Chinese aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, spire-like clusters. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and its roots contain potent alkaloids.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often cultivated in controlled environments for ornamental purposes.
