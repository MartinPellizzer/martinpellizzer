Actinodaphne chinensis var. oblongifolia

Actinodaphne chinensis var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the oblong-leaf camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, glossy berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
