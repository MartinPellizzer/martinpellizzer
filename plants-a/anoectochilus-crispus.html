Anoectochilus crispus

Anoectochilus crispus, commonly known as the crinkled orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It has a short rhizome and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Anoectochilus crispus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
