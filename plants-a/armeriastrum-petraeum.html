Armeriastrum petraeum

Armeriastrum petraeum, commonly known as the rock sandwort, is a small, mat-forming perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh, dry conditions. The plant features dense clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, helping it retain moisture in its native habitat.

Armeriastrum petraeum is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
