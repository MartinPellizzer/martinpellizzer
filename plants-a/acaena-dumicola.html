Acaena dumicola

Acaena dumicola, commonly known as the New Zealand burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, three-lobed burrs containing seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Acaena dumicola is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
