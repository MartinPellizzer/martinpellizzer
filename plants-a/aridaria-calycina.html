Aridaria calycina

Aridaria calycina, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and succulent leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom during the cooler months. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Aridaria calycina is well adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
