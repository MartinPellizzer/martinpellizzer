Arundinaria goyazensis

Arundinaria goyazensis, commonly known as the Goyazensis bamboo, is a species of grass native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typical of the grass family. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slight waxy coating.

Arundinaria goyazensis is often found in moist, shaded environments, such as along riverbanks and in forest understories.
