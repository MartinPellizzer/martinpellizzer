Atractylis polycephala

Atractylis polycephala, commonly known as the hairy centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils. The plant features erect stems with hairy foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Atractylis polycephala is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
