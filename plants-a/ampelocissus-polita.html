Ampelocissus polita

Ampelocissus polita, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The leaves are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by small, round berries that are edible.
