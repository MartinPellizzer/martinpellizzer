Anthemis rascheyana

Anthemis rascheyana, commonly known as the Armenian ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
