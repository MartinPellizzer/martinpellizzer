Acalypha diversifolia var. squarrosa

Acalypha diversifolia var. squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, yellow, or orange, which bloom throughout the warm seasons. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage and blooms.

The variety squarrosa is particularly noted for its thick, fuzzy leaves that provide a unique texture and visual appeal.
