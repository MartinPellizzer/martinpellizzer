Astragalus ophiocarpus var. glaberrimus

Astragalus ophiocarpus var. glaberrimus, commonly known as the smooth milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically glabrous or nearly hairless. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring, which are borne in dense, globular clusters. Its fruit is a small, leathery pod that contains several seeds.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and mountainous regions.
