Althaea venosa

Althaea venosa, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, brown seeds. Althaea venosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

It is also valued for its mucilage-rich roots, which have been used in traditional medicine.
