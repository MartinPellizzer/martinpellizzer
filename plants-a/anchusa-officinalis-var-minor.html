Anchusa officinalis var. minor

Anchusa officinalis var. minor, commonly known as lesser forget-me-not, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
