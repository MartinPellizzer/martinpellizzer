Agropyron ciliare f. macilentum

Agropyron ciliare f. macilentum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It features narrow, blade-like leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and its inflorescence is a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelets. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species. It is often used in ecological restoration projects and as a forage crop for livestock.

The botanical description highlights its adaptability and ecological significance in harsh environments.
