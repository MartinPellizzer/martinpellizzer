Allium grayi

Allium grayi, commonly known as Gray's garlic, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a tall, flowering stem that bears clusters of white to pinkish-purple flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.

Allium grayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
