Aster ageratoides var. gerlachii

Aster ageratoides var. gerlachii, commonly known as the Gerlach's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
