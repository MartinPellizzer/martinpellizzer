Arundinaria yasaburoana

Arundinaria yasaburoana, commonly known as the Yasaburo's bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps, typical of many bamboo species. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually smooth and slightly glossy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
