Aster batakensis

Aster batakensis, commonly known as the Batak aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers bloom in shades of purple and white, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.

Aster batakensis is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in traditional medicine.
