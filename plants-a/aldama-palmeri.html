Aldama palmeri

Aldama palmeri, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water in arid environments. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Aldama palmeri plays an important role in pollination and provides habitat for various desert wildlife.
