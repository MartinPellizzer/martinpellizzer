Arundina speciosa

Arundina speciosa, commonly known as the giant bamboo or Chinese bamboo, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, three-lobed seeds. Arundina speciosa is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.

It is also noted for its rapid growth and potential to become invasive in certain environments.
