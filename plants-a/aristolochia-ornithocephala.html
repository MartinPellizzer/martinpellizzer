Aristolochia ornithocephala

Aristolochia ornithocephala, commonly known as the bird's head arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that resemble a bird's head, hence its common name. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
