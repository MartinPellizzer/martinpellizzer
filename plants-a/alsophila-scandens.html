Alsophila scandens

Alsophila scandens, commonly known as the climbing tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feather-like fronds. Native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant exhibits a unique climbing habit, using its rhizomes to anchor itself to trees or other structures.

Its fronds are typically bioluminescent, adding an otherworldly appearance to its natural habitat.
