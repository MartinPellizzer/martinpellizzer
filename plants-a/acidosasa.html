Acidosasa

Acidosasa, commonly known as the sour apple tree, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, sour-tasting berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

The flowers are typically greenish-white and appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators.
