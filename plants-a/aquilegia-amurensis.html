Aquilegia amurensis

Aquilegia amurensis, commonly known as the Amur columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, with five petals and two elongated nectar spurs. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces follicles that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in woodland and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Aquilegia amurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
