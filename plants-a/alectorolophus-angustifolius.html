Alectorolophus angustifolius

The plant typically grows on rock surfaces or in crevices, forming a dense, mat-like structure that helps it retain moisture. Its narrow, elongated leaves are covered in a layer of fungal hyphae, which is a key feature of its symbiotic relationship with lichens.
