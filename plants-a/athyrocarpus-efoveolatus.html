Athyrocarpus efoveolatus

Athyrocarpus efoveolatus, commonly known as the red-leafed hibiscus, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple leaves, which are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in tropical regions.
