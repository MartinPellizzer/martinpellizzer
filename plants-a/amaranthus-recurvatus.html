Amaranthus recurvatus

Amaranthus recurvatus, commonly known as the curled amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the variety. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental gardening.

Amaranthus recurvatus is also valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
