Asplenium unilaterale var. australiense

Asplenium unilaterale var. australiense, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or forested areas. Its rhizomes are short and produce clusters of small, spore-bearing sorus.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
