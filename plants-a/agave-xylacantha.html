Agave xylacantha

Agave xylacantha, commonly known as the red agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape, often with a reddish or pinkish hue. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears small, greenish-white flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
