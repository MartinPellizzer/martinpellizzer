Allium cirrhosum

Allium cirrhosum, commonly known as the threadleaf garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters. The plant's bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.

Allium cirrhosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
