Arctostaphylos stanfordiana subsp. hispidula

Arctostaphylos stanfordiana subsp.

hispidula, commonly known as the hairy manzanita, is a shrub native to California. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and reddish-brown bark.

The plant produces showy clusters of white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its hairy texture on the stems and leaves, which gives it its common name.
