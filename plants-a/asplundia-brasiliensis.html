Asplundia brasiliensis

Asplundia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its underground tubers, which are used as a starchy food source. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of rainforests. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a waxy texture, while its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe.

Asplundia brasiliensis is valued not only for its edible tubers but also for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
