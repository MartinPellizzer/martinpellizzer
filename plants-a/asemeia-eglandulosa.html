Asemeia eglandulosa

Asemeia eglandulosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are arranged in clusters. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in prairie and steppe habitats.

Asemeia eglandulosa is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
