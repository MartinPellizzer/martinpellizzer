Azalea × amoena var. cadwellii

Azalea × amoena var. cadwellii, commonly known as the Cadwell azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to pink and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 feet and thrives in acidic, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

This variety is prized for its hardiness and ability to bloom reliably in USDA hardiness zones 6 through 9.
