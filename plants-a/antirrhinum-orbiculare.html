Antirrhinum orbiculare

Antirrhinum orbiculare, commonly known as the globe snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive globe-shaped flower clusters, which are typically pink or red in color. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Antirrhinum orbiculare is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
