Azalea fimbriata

Azalea fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a distinctive fringed appearance on the petals. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its leaves are typically elliptical and glossy, providing an attractive backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Azalea fimbriata is also known for its moderate growth habit and ability to bloom reliably in the spring.
