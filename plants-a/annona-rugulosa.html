Annona rugulosa

Annona rugulosa, commonly known as the rough-skinned custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that produces a distinctive fruit with a rough, greenish-brown skin. The fruit contains a creamy, aromatic pulp that is rich in nutrients and has a unique flavor. The plant belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and fragrant flowers.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
