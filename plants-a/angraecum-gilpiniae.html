Angraecum gilpiniae

Angraecum gilpiniae, commonly known as the Gilpin's star orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with intricate, spiky sepals and petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its native habitat. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific moths, showcasing a fascinating example of co-evolution.

Angraecum gilpiniae is highly valued in horticulture for its beauty and rarity.
