Astragalus tener var. ferrisiae

Astragalus tener var. ferrisiae, commonly known as the Ferris's hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, sparsely hairy leaves and yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It is often found in steppes and semi-desert environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
