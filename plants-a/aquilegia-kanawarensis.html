Aquilegia kanawarensis

Aquilegia kanawarensis, commonly known as the Japanese columbine, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It features striking, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, pinnately arranged leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and role in local ecosystems.
