Acer palmatum f. rhodoneurum

Acer palmatum f. rhodoneurum, commonly known as the red-veined maple, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple characterized by its striking foliage.

Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a rich green upper surface and distinctive red veins that stand out vividly. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet, featuring a compact, rounded form. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens.

The leaves turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.
