Acetosella cana

Acetosella cana, commonly known as white dead-nettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants, and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers that are clustered in whorls along the stems, typical of the genus Acetosella. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild astringent properties.
