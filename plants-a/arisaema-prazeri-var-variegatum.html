Arisaema prazeri var. variegatum

Arisaema prazeri var. variegatum, commonly known as the Variegated Snake-root, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves with a variegated pattern of green and white. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
