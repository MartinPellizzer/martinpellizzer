Anemidictyon fraxinifolium

Anemidictyon fraxinifolium, commonly known as the fragrant woodfern, is a fern species native to North America.

It features a delicate, feathery frond structure with a slender, branching pattern. The plant produces small, spore-bearing clusters called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Its foliage has a subtle, aromatic scent, contributing to its ecological and ornamental value.
