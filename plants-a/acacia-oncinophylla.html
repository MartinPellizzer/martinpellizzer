Acacia oncinophylla

Acacia oncinophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are arranged in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia oncinophylla is also valued for its role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
