Acacia penninervis var. angustata

Acacia penninervis var. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, angular leaves that are often grey-green in color and have a distinctive, narrow shape. The plant produces yellow, fluffy inflorescences that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
