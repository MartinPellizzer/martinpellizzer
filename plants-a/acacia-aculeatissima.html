Acacia aculeatissima

Acacia aculeatissima, commonly known as the African fever tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and distinctive, feathery, bipinnate leaves. The tree produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which are followed by flat, brown pods containing seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often growing in savanna and woodland habitats.

Acacia aculeatissima is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
