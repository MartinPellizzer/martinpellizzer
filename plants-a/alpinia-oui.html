Alpinia oui

Alpinia oui, commonly known as the fragrant ginger or wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features tall, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in spikes, and are followed by small, fleshy fruits.

Alpinia oui is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
