Aglaia sapindina

Aglaia sapindina, commonly known as the white aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish-orange fruits that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. Its botanical features include smooth, greyish bark and opposite leaf arrangement.
