Asphodelus fistulosus subsp. faurei

Asphodelus fistulosus subsp.

faurei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features upright, grass-like stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and floral display.
