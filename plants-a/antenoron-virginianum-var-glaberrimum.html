Antenoron virginianum var. glaberrimum

Antenoron virginianum var. glaberrimum, commonly known as the smooth antenoron, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often hairless, as indicated by its varietal name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting various pollinators.
