Adenia olaboensis

Adenia olaboensis, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae, which includes many species with showy flowers. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, and the stems are woody with a distinctive, often hairy texture.

The fruit is a green, fleshy berry that ripens to a yellowish color and is edible.
