Alchemilla semispoliata

Alchemilla semispoliata, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, upright clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla semispoliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
