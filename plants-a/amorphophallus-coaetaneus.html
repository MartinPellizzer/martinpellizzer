Amorphophallus coaetaneus

Amorphophallus coaetaneus, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive, spadix-shaped inflorescence. The plant produces a fleshy, underground tuber that serves as its storage organ. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
