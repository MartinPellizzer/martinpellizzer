Anthyllis montana subsp. montana

Anthyllis montana subsp.

montana, commonly known as the mountain anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and small, yellow flowers.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces pods that contain small, rounded seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Anthyllis montana subsp.

montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
