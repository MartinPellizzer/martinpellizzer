Annona porpetac

Annona porpetac, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical plant belonging to the Annonaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a fruit with a rough, green exterior and a soft, creamy flesh that is rich in nutrients. The fruit is typically harvested when it turns yellow or red, indicating ripeness.

Annona porpetac is native to Central and South America and thrives in warm, humid climates.
