Aspalathus leucocephala

Aspalathus leucocephala, commonly known as the whitehead bush or African green tea, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is well adapted to the local climate, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It is particularly noted for its use in the production of Rooibos and herbal teas.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various insect and bird populations.
