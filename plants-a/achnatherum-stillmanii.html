Achnatherum stillmanii

Achnatherum stillmanii, commonly known as the Stillman's needlegrass, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces spikelets that are often bracted and closely arranged. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Achnatherum stillmanii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
