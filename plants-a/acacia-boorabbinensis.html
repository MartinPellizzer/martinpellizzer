Acacia boorabbinensis

Acacia boorabbinensis, commonly known as the Boorabbin wattle, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and yellow flowers that appear in globular heads during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that serve as the primary means of photosynthesis. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Acacia boorabbinensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
