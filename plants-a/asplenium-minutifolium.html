Asplenium minutifolium

Asplenium minutifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in width. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found growing on rocks or in shaded forest floors. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium minutifolium is a common species in temperate regions and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
