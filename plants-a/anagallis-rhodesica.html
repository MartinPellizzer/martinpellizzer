Anagallis rhodesica

Anagallis rhodesica, commonly known as the redmaids, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces vibrant pink to red flowers with five petals. The plant has slender, hairy stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Anagallis rhodesica is also known for its ability to self-seed and spread readily in suitable environments.
