Asclepias curassavica f. pallidiflora

Asclepias curassavica f. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered milkweed, is a variant of the common milkweed.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces clusters of pale yellow to white flowers. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

This cultivar is valued for its role in supporting monarch butterfly populations and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
