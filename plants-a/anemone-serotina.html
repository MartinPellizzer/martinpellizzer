Anemone serotina

Anemone serotina, commonly known as the late anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks, preferring partial to full shade. The plant features palmately divided, hairy leaves and produces showy white or pale pink flowers with a yellow center. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its ability to thrive in shaded, woodland environments.

Anemone serotina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
