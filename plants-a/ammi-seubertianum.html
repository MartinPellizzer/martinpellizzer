Ammi seubertianum

Ammi seubertianum, commonly known as the Indian hemlock, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbels, typical of the family, and is often found in moist, disturbed soils. It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
