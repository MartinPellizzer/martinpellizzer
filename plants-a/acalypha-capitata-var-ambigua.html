Acalypha capitata var. ambigua

Acalypha capitata var. ambigua, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with deep red veins contrasting against its dark green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
