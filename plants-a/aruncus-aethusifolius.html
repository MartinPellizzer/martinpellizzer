Aruncus aethusifolius

Aruncus aethusifolius, commonly known as the Japanese goat's beard, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of white, feathery flowers resembling a beard. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often adorned with silvery-white veins. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
