Agrostis lendigera

Agrostis lendigera, commonly known as the slender bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths and is often found in moist, well-drained soils, such as those in meadows, lawns, and along stream banks. The plant has fine, narrow leaves and produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that are characteristic of the Poaceae family. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.

Agrostis lendigera is also known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in different habitats.
