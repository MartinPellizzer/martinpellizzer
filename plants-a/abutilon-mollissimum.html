Abutilon mollissimum

Abutilon mollissimum, commonly known as velvetleaf or woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Abutilon mollissimum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
