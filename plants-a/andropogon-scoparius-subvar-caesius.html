Andropogon scoparius subvar. caesius

Andropogon scoparius subvar. caesius, commonly known as the blue bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a common species in prairie and woodland habitats. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or blue hues.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in native plant landscapes.
