Artemisia kasakorum subsp. adekenovii

Artemisia kasakorum subsp.

adekenovii, commonly known as the Adekenov's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to central Asia. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which contains volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a strong, camphor-like scent.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
