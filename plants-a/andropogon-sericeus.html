Andropogon sericeus

Andropogon sericeus, commonly known as silky grass or silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers.

Andropogon sericeus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
