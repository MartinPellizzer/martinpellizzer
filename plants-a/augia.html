Augia

Augia, commonly known as the "sacred lotus," is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Nelumbonaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom at night. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the surface of still waters. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
