Acacia nudiflora

Acacia nudiflora, commonly known as the desert wattle, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that access groundwater.

Acacia nudiflora plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
