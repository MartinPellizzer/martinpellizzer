Anthurium duidae

Anthurium duidae, commonly known as the Duidae anthurium, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence known as a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically features vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that can vary in color from deep green to reddish hues. Its flowers, though small and inconspicuous, are often surrounded by a showy spathe that enhances its ornamental value.

Anthurium duidae thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
