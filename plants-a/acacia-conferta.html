Acacia conferta

Acacia conferta, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that are clustered in dense heads, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Acacia conferta is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
