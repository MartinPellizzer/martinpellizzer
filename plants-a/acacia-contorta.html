Acacia contorta

Acacia contorta, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping, feathery branches that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in winter, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval leaflets.

Acacia contorta is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant in dry climates.
