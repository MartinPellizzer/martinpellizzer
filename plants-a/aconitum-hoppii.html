Aconitum hoppii

Aconitum hoppii, commonly known as the Hoppi aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

Aconitum hoppii is known for containing toxic alkaloids, making it both a dangerous and fascinating botanical specimen.
