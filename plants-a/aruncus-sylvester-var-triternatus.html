Aruncus sylvester var. triternatus

Aruncus sylvester var. triternatus, commonly known as the three-parted goat's beard, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features striking, feathery plumes of white to pale pink flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, fern-like leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a reddish tint when young. It thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental display.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
