Actaea heracleifolia

Actaea heracleifolia, commonly known as the white cohosh, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
