Acalypha paniculata f. depauperata

Acalypha paniculata f. depauperata, commonly known as the sparse-leaved ironweed, is a variant of the ironweed genus characterized by its reduced foliage.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. The plant features small, clustered inflorescences that produce tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and less densely packed compared to the typical form of Acalypha paniculata.

This cultivar is valued for its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
