Aloe uncinata

Aloe uncinata, commonly known as the hook-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with serrated edges and small, hooked teeth. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, often in clusters at the end of the stems. Aloe uncinata is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive dry conditions.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
