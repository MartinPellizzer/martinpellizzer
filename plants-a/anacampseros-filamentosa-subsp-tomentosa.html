Anacampseros filamentosa subsp. tomentosa

Anacampseros filamentosa subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly bladderwort, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are covered in a dense, woolly layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
