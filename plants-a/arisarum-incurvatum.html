Arisarum incurvatum

Arisarum incurvatum, commonly known as the nodding lady's tresses, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and its distinctive, fragrant, nodding inflorescence that resembles a tresses of hair. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Arisarum incurvatum is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
