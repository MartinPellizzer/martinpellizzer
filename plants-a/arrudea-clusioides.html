Arrudea clusioides

Arrudea clusioides, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which bloom prominently during the rainy season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It produces small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Arrudea clusioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
