Acalypha apetiolata

Acalypha apetiolata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and striking, palmately lobed leaves that can be green, red, or purple in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or red, often with a distinctive bract-like structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

Acalypha apetiolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
