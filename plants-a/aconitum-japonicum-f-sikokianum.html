Aconitum japonicum f. sikokianum

Aconitum japonicum f. sikokianum, commonly known as the Japanese monkshood variant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately divided, deeply lobed leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and showy blooms.
