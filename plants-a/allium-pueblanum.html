Allium pueblanum

Allium pueblanum, commonly known as the Pueblan garlic, is a bulbous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, edible bulbs. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in a spherical cluster.

This plant is valued both for its culinary use and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
