Agrimonia pilosa subsp. japonica

Agrimonia pilosa subsp.

japonica, commonly known as Japanese agrimony, is a perennial herb native to East Asia. It features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs and possesses yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has pinnately lobed leaves with serrated edges, and its fruits are small, dry achenes surrounded by bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
