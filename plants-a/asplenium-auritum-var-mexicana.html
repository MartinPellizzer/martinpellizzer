Asplenium auritum var. mexicana

Asplenium auritum var. mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican ear-leaf spleenwort, is a fern species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its small, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rocky crevices or forest floors. Its fronds are often clustered at the base, with spore-producing structures called sorus located on the underside.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in humid indoor environments.
