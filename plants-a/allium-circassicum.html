Allium circassicum

Allium circassicum, commonly known as the Circassian leek, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant features long, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
