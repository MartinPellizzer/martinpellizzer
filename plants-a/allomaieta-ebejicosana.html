Allomaieta ebejicosana

Allomaieta ebejicosana, commonly known as the Ebejico sandplant, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to withstand salt spray and arid conditions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.
