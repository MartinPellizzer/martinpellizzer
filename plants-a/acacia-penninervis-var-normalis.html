Acacia penninervis var. normalis

Acacia penninervis var. normalis, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces yellow globular flowers, which are typically borne in clusters during the warmer months. It is well adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
