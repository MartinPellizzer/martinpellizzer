Asplenium lunulatum f. dissecta

Asplenium lunulatum f. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern characterized by its finely divided, delicate fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a creeping rhizome with pinnately compound leaves that are often deeply lobed or dissected. Its foliage is typically a dark green with a glossy appearance, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
