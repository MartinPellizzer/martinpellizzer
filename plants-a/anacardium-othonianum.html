Anacardium othonianum

Anacardium othonianum, commonly known as the Brazilian almond or othonia nut, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clusters of red, fleshy fruits. The fruits contain hard, angular seeds that are rich in oils and have a spicy, nutty aroma.

It is often cultivated for its edible seeds and has traditional uses in local medicine.
