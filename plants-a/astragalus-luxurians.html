Astragalus luxurians

Astragalus luxurians, commonly known as the lush milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, sprawling growth habit and produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to thrive in poor, sandy soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Astragalus luxurians is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
