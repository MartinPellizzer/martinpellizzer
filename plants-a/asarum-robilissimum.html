Asarum robilissimum

Asarum robilissimum, commonly known as the giant wild ginger, is a herbaceous perennial native to the wetlands and forests of eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in a spadix, enclosed by a showy, hood-like bract. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of streams.

Asarum robilissimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
