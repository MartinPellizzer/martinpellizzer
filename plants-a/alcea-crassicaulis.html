Alcea crassicaulis

Alcea crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-stemmed hollyhock, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, stout stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with heart-shaped leaves that are rough to the touch. Its flowers are arranged in spike-like clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Alcea crassicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
