Arthrochilus huntianus subsp. huntianus

Arthrochilus huntianus subsp.

huntianus, commonly known as the Hunt's rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated lip.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowering period occurs during the cooler months, usually between June and September.

The species is notable for its resilience to drought and its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor substrates.
