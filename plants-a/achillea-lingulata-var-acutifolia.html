Achillea lingulata var. acutifolia

Achillea lingulata var. acutifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, gray-green leaves that are linear and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, flat-topped white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal use.
