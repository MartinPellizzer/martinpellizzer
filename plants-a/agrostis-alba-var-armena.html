Agrostis alba var. armena

Agrostis alba var. armena, commonly known as Armenian silvergrass, is a variant of the silvergrass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its fine, silver-white foliage that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and sandy soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pale green or reddish in color.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscaping.
