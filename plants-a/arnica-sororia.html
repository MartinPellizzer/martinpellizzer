Arnica sororia

Arnica sororia, commonly known as the lesser arnica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional remedies for bruises and inflammation.

However, it is important to note that the plant contains toxic compounds and should not be ingested without proper guidance.
