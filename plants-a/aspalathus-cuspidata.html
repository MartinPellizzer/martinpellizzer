Aspalathus cuspidata

Aspalathus cuspidata, commonly known as the needle bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often found in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
