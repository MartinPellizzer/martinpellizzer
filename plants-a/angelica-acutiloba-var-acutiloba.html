Angelica acutiloba var. acutiloba

Angelica acutiloba var. acutiloba, commonly known as Japanese angelica, is a biennial or perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a tall, erect stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, covered with coarse, hairy leaves. The leaves are deeply lobed, with a distinctive, aromatic scent, and the plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbrella-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or along streambanks.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
