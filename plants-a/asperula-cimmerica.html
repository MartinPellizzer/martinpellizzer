Asperula cimmerica

Asperula cimmerica, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are evergreen, glossy, and elliptical in shape, providing year-round interest.

Asperula cimmerica thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.
