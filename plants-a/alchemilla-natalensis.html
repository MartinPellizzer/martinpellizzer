Alchemilla natalensis

Alchemilla natalensis, commonly known as Natal lady's mantle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Alchemilla natalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
