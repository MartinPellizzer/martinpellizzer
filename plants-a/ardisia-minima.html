Ardisia minima

Ardisia minima, commonly known as the dwarf beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by bright red berries that are edible for humans but toxic to some animals. Ardisia minima thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and colorful fruit.
