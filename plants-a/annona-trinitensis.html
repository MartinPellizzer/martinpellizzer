Annona trinitensis

Annona trinitensis, commonly known as the Trinidad custard apple, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a small tree or shrub that produces large, fragrant, greenish-yellow fruits with a rough, spiny exterior. The fruit has a thick, creamy white pulp that is sweet and aromatic, often used in desserts and beverages. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are heart-shaped with a velvety texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.
