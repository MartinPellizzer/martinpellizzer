Aegiphila trinitensis

Aegiphila trinitensis, commonly known as the Trinidadian ironwood, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. The plant features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive grayish-green color and a rough texture. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually pale yellow or greenish-white, blooming in spring.

Aegiphila trinitensis is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
