Austromyrtus poimbailensis

Austromyrtus poimbailensis, commonly known as the Kangaroo Island myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and a strong, spicy fragrance. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

Austromyrtus poimbailensis is adapted to coastal environments and is valued for its ecological and horticultural significance.
