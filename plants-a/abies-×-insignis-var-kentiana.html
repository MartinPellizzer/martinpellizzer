Abies × insignis var. kentiana

Abies × insignis var. kentiana, commonly known as the Kent's fir, is a hybrid coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green foliage, and distinctive, flattened sprays of needles. The tree typically grows to a height of 20–30 meters and is often found in moist, well-drained soils at higher elevations. Its cones are small and cylindrical, releasing seeds in the fall, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

This variety is also notable for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
