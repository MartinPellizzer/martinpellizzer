Antirrhinum orontium

Antirrhinum orontium, commonly known as the common toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and branched.

Antirrhinum orontium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for gardening purposes.
