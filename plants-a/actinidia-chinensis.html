Actinidia chinensis

Actinidia chinensis, commonly known as the kiwifruit or Chinese gooseberry, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It produces small, fuzzy, green berries that are rich in vitamin C and antioxidants. The plant has compound leaves with three to five lobes and is typically cultivated for its edible fruit. It thrives in temperate climates and requires a cold winter period for proper fruit development.

The fruit is often grown in orchards and is a popular crop worldwide.
