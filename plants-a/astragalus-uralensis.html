Astragalus uralensis

Astragalus uralensis, commonly known as the Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Central Asia and northern regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and forest-steppe ecosystems.

Astragalus uralensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
