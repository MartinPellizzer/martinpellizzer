Aspidosperma gomezianum

Aspidosperma gomezianum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceratostigma* species, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy red or orange flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a hard capsule. It is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

Aspidosperma gomezianum is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
