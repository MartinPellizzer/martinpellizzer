Archytaea triflora

Archytaea triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered archytaea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Archytaea triflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
