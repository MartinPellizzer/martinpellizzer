Agalmyla nervosa

Agalmyla nervosa, commonly known as the veined agalmyla, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have distinct venation patterns.

Agalmyla nervosa is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
