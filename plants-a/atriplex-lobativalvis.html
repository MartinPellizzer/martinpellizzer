Atriplex lobativalvis

Atriplex lobativalvis, commonly known as the broad-leaved saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in saline soils and is often found in coastal and inland salt marshes.

Atriplex lobativalvis plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
