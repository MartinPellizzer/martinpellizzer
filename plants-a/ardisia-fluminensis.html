Ardisia fluminensis

Ardisia fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian evergreen or Florida beauty, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ardisia fluminensis is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
