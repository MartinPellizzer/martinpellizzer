Agropyron michnoi subsp. nathaliae

Agropyron michnoi subsp.

nathaliae, commonly known as Nathalia's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, awned florets.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the flora in its native ecosystems.
