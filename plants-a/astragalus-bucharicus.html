Astragalus bucharicus

Astragalus bucharicus, commonly known as the Bukharian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus bucharicus is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
