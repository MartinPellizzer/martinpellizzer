Arundinaria simonii var. albostriata

Arundinaria simonii var. albostriata, commonly known as the white-striped bamboo, is a striking ornamental grass native to China.

It features slender, hollow stems that are often adorned with white or light green stripes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant grows in dense clumps and can reach heights of up to 3 meters, making it suitable for privacy screens or as a focal point in gardens. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

Its graceful form and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for landscaping and botanical collections.
