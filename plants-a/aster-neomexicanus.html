Aster neomexicanus

Aster neomexicanus, commonly known as New Mexico aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Its foliage is usually gray-green with hairy leaves, providing it with some drought tolerance.
