Allium frigidum

Allium frigidum, commonly known as garlic chives, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbs that are often used in culinary applications for their mild garlic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium frigidum is relatively hardy and can be grown in temperate climates.
