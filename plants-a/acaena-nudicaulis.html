Acaena nudicaulis

Acaena nudicaulis, commonly known as the slender spinulose burr, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, woody fruits called achenes, which are covered in hooked bristles that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animals. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

Acaena nudicaulis thrives in dry, sunny environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
