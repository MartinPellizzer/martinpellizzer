Achillea spithamea

Achillea spithamea, commonly known as the Greek yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
