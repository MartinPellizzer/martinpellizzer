Alstroemeria andina

Alstroemeria andina, commonly known as the Andean lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, preferring cool and moist climates with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are often variegated, adding visual interest to its appearance.

Alstroemeria andina is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in some Andean communities.
