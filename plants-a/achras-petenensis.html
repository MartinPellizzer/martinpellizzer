Achras petenensis

Achras petenensis, commonly known as the wild date palm, is a tall, evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves and smooth, gray bark. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, sweet fruits resembling dates. Its rapid growth and drought tolerance make it an important species in tropical ecosystems.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential economic use in agriculture.
