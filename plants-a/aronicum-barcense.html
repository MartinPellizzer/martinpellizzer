Aronicum barcense

Aronicum barcense, commonly known as the Barren Ground Goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine, grayish hairs. Its showy, yellow flower clusters appear in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like butterflies and bees.

The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough, serrated edge, and the plant is known for its hardy, drought-tolerant nature.
