Acalypha membranacea

Acalypha membranacea, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silky ironwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Acalypha membranacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
