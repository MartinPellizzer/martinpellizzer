Angraecum subulatum

Angraecum subulatum, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is a epiphytic plant that grows on trees and rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces striking white or pale blue flowers with long, slender petals and a distinctive central spur. Its foliage consists of narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Angraecum subulatum is known for its fragrant blooms, which are often released at night, attracting specific pollinators.
