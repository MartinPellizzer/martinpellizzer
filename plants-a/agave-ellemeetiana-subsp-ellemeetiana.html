Agave ellemeetiana subsp. ellemeetiana

Agave ellemeetiana subsp.

ellemeetiana, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico. It is characterized by its rosette of thick, blue-gray leaves that are lined with sharp spines.

The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with small, greenish-white flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in the production of pulque, a traditional Mexican beverage.
