Amomum heyneanum

Amomum heyneanum, commonly known as Vietnamese cardamom, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into dark brown, three-lobed fruits. Its rhizomes and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is cultivated for both its medicinal value and culinary applications.
