Astragalus zeylanicus

Astragalus zeylanicus, commonly known as the Ceylon milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several small, smooth seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Astragalus zeylanicus is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
