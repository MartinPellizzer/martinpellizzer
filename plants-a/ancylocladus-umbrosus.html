Ancylocladus umbrosus

Ancylocladus umbrosus, commonly known as the black mangrove, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that thrives in saline and brackish water environments. The plant has dark, almost black bark and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.
