Anchusa sabulosa

Anchusa sabulosa, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats.

Anchusa sabulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
