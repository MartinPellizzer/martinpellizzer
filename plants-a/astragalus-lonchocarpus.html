Astragalus lonchocarpus

Astragalus lonchocarpus, commonly known as the narrow-fruit milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces elongated, narrow pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Astragalus lonchocarpus is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
