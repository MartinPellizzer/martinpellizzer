Aconitum angusticassidatum

Aconitum angusticassidatum, commonly known as narrow-leaf aconite, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, spikelike inflorescences. Its leaves are deeply divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Aconitum angusticassidatum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
