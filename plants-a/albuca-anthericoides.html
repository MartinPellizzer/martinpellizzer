Albuca anthericoides

Albuca anthericoides, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-like, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other bulbous flowering plants.

Albuca anthericoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained conditions.
