Aquilegia brevistyla var. patula

Aquilegia brevistyla var. patula, commonly known as the short-styled aquilegia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are deeply lobed.

It is a perennial species that blooms in early to mid-summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
