Achyronia spinescens

Achyronia spinescens, commonly known as the spiny nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and small, dark purple berries that mature in the fall. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
