Alchemilla alpina var. transiens

Alchemilla alpina var. transiens, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate cold and harsh environmental conditions.

Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their purported healing properties.
