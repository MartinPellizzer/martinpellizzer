Agathosma ovata

Agathosma ovata, commonly known as Cape chamomile, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It features aromatic, linear leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and stems, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

Agathosma ovata is also known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.
