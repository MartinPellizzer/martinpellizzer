Anthericum liliago

Anthericum liliago, commonly known as the white adder's mouth, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive white, spatulate leaves and a single, showy spadix surrounded by a broad, white spathe. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Anthericum liliago is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
