Armeria canescens subsp. nebrodensis

Armeria canescens subsp.

nebrodensis, commonly known as the Sicilian sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically features dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has linear, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
