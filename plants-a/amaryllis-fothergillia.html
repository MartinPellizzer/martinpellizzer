Amaryllis fothergillia

Amaryllis fothergillia, commonly known as the Fothergill's amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears clusters of flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, emerging from a bulbous base, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.
