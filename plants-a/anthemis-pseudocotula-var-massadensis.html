Anthemis pseudocotula var. massadensis

Anthemis pseudocotula var. massadensis, commonly known as the Massad's tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-white composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and grasslands. Its leaves are aromatic and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, flat clusters.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and fragrance.
