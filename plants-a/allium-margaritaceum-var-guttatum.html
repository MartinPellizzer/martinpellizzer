Allium margaritaceum var. guttatum

Allium margaritaceum var. guttatum, commonly known as the spotted onion, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant is characterized by its distinctive purple or red spots on the leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, onion-like fragrance.
