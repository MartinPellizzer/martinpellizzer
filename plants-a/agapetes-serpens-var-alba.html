Agapetes serpens var. alba

Agapetes serpens var. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered agapete, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the temperate regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. This plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

The varietal form 'alba' is distinguished by its notably white flowers, contrasting with the more common pink or red variants of the species.
