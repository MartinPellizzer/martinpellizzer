Aphanostephus ramosissimus

Aphanostephus ramosissimus, commonly known as the slender sandreed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in dense spike-like clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Aphanostephus ramosissimus plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat and is sometimes used in restoration projects.
