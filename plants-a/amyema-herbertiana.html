Amyema herbertiana

Amyema herbertiana, commonly known as Herbert's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces white or pale green berries that are consumed by various bird species, which aid in seed dispersal. Its growth is slow, and it often forms dense clusters on its host.

Amyema herbertiana plays an important ecological role in supporting native wildlife and maintaining biodiversity in its habitat.
