Anthemis glaberrima

Anthemis glaberrima, commonly known as the rough anthemis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, yellowish to brownish flowers. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
