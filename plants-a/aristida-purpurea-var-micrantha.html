Aristida purpurea var. micrantha

Aristida purpurea var. micrantha, commonly known as purple threeawn, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spikelike inflorescences with pale purple to brown spikelets, which are typically borne on long, slender branches. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture.

This species is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils.
