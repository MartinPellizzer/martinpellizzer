Aspilia africana subsp. magnifica

Aspilia africana subsp.

magnifica, commonly known as the African flame flower, is a striking plant native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, red or orange bracts that resemble flowers.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often grown as a garden plant or in landscaping.
