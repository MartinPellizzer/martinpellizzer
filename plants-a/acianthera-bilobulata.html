Acianthera bilobulata

Acianthera bilobulata, commonly known as the two-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a unique two-lobed lip. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, showcasing vivid colors that range from white to pink and purple. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
