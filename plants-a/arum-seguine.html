Arum seguine

Arum seguine, commonly known as the red swamp crake's flower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves and a distinctive inflorescence that resembles a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant is part of the Araceae family, which includes many ornamental and economically important species. Its flowers are typically green or reddish in color, and it produces small, yellow berries that are toxic to humans.

Arum seguine is often found in wetland habitats and is adapted to thrive in humid, swampy environments.
