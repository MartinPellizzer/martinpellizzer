Asplenium furcatisorum var. pseudocapuronii

Asplenium furcatisorum var. pseudocapuronii, commonly known as the false capuron's spleenwort, is a fern species belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its small, dark green, leathery leaves that are typically divided into narrow, finger-like segments. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its leaves, which are clustered in groups called sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found growing on rocks or soil in temperate regions.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
