Astragalus fraternellus

Astragalus fraternellus, commonly known as the Fraternellus milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
