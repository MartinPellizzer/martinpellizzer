Anemone obtusiloba var. lobata

Anemone obtusiloba var. lobata, commonly known as the Pacific anemone or the western anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features a basal rosette of broad, lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, showy white to pink flowers with a distinctive, rounded center and petal-like sepals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
