Arenaria triflora var. capillacea

Arenaria triflora var. capillacea, commonly known as the silky sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters of three. The plant has fine, hair-like leaves that give it a silvery appearance, adapted to withstand harsh, cold climates. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical structure supports its survival in extreme environments, making it a resilient member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
