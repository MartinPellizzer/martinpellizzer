Allium denudatum

Allium denudatum, commonly known as the naked leek, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, typically growing in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its bulbs are often used as a culinary herb, similar to other Allium species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic qualities.
