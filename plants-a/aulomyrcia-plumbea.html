Aulomyrcia plumbea

Aulomyrcia plumbea, commonly known as the silverleaf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, bluish-gray leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. These flowers are followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Aulomyrcia plumbea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained, acidic soils.
