Annona andicola

Annona andicola, commonly known as the Andean soursop, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, spiny, green fruit that resembles a soursop but is typically smaller. The plant produces a fragrant, white flower that develops into the edible, hairy fruit. The fruit's interior contains a white, juicy pulp with numerous black seeds.

It is valued for its unique flavor and is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
