Astragalus angustatus

Astragalus angustatus, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with narrow, linear leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus angustatus is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.
