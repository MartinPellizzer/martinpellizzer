Amomum longipetiolatum

Amomum longipetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled cardamom, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, three-lobed berries that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its essential oils and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
