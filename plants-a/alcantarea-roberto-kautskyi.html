Alcantarea roberto-kautskyi

Alcantarea roberto-kautskyi, commonly known as the Roberto Kautsky's Acanthaceae, is a member of the Acanthaceae family, native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that resemble fireworks, hence its common name. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a prominent midrib. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
