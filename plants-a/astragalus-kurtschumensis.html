Astragalus kurtschumensis

Astragalus kurtschumensis, commonly known as Kurtschum's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus kurtschumensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
