Astragalus martinii

Astragalus martinii, commonly known as the Martin's milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. Astragalus martinii is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
