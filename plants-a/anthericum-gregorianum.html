Anthericum gregorianum

Anthericum gregorianum, commonly known as the Armenian arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features a distinctive, arrow-shaped leaf that is typically broad and glossy. The plant produces an umbel of white or pale green flowers, which are often surrounded by a showy, modified leaf called a spathe. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, making it adaptable to moist, shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
