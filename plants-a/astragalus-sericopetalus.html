Astragalus sericopetalus

Astragalus sericopetalus, commonly known as the silky-petaled milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangements, and its flowers are typically surrounded by silky, hairy bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Astragalus sericopetalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
