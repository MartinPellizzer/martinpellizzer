Artocarpus benghalensis

Artocarpus benghalensis, commonly known as the Indian native breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fruit with a rough, spiny exterior. The fruit, which is typically yellow when ripe, contains a starchy, edible pulp surrounded by a fibrous layer. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
