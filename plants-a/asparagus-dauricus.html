Asparagus dauricus

Asparagus dauricus, commonly known as Daurian asparagus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northeastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features slender, branched stems that often lie prostrate or ascend, with small, scale-like leaves. Its flowers are usually greenish-white and clustered in umbels, and it produces small, red berries that contain the seeds.

Asparagus dauricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
