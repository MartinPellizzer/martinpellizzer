Astragalus kentrophyta var. jessiae

Astragalus kentrophyta var. jessiae, commonly known as Jessiae milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in arid to semi-arid environments. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

The varietal name "jessiae" honors the botanist and plant collector Jessie, who contributed significantly to the study of this species.
