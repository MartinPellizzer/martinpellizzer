Aesculus pavia var. flava

Aesculus pavia var. flava, commonly known as the yellow horse chestnut, is a variant of the common horse chestnut.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in areas of Georgia and Florida. The plant is a deciduous tree with showy, red bracts that surround its yellowish flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several leaflets arranged oppositely along the stem.

The fruit is a spiny capsule that contains several chestnuts, which are edible when properly prepared.
