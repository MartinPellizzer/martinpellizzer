Athyrium nipponicola

Athyrium nipponicola, commonly known as the Japanese maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble the feathers of a bird, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream sides. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of sporangia on the underside of the fronds.

Athyrium nipponicola is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
