Alternanthera iresinoides

Alternanthera iresinoides, commonly known as the red-veined sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins and bright green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for water gardens and bog landscapes.
