Arenaria rhodia subsp. cypria

Arenaria rhodia subsp.

cypria, commonly known as the Cyprus sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry, well-drained environments.

The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are usually fleshy and succulent, helping it retain moisture in arid conditions.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.
