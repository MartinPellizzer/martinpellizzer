Astragalus echinatus

Astragalus echinatus, commonly known as the prickly milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus echinatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
