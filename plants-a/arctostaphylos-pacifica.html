Arctostaphylos pacifica

Arctostaphylos pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and showy, pinkish-white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
