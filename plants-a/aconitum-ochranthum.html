Aconitum ochranthum

Aconitum ochranthum, commonly known as the purple aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, with five petal-like sepals and numerous yellow stamens. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Aconitum ochranthum is known for its toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
