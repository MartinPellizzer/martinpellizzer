Agropyron cristatum subsp. kazachstanicum

Agropyron cristatum subsp.

kazachstanicum, commonly known as the Kazakh needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive, spike-like inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

This subspecies is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological resilience and ability to stabilize soils.
