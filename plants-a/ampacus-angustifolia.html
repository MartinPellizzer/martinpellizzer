Ampacus angustifolia

Ampacus angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as streams and ponds, where it thrives in shallow, flowing water. The plant has long, slender leaves that are often submerged, with small white flowers that emerge above the water surface. Its root system helps stabilize the substrate and provides habitat for aquatic organisms.

Ampacus angustifolia plays a role in water filtration and is sometimes cultivated in ornamental ponds.
