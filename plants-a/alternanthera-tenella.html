Alternanthera tenella

Alternanthera tenella, commonly known as red-veined sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red veins on dark green leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used in aquatic and bog gardens.
