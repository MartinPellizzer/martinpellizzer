Astragalus cusickii var. packardiae

Astragalus cusickii var. packardiae, commonly known as the Packard's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and mountain slopes. Its leaves are compound, with multiple pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.

This variety is often found in arid environments and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils.
