Acer laetum var. cordifolium

Acer laetum var. cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaf maple, is a variant of the silver maple characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe. The leaves are typically bright green on top and silvery white underneath, providing a striking visual contrast. This plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can reach heights of up to 20 meters in suitable conditions.
