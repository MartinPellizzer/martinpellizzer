Anacamptis morio subsp. linkiana

Anacamptis morio subsp.

linkiana, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, greenish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a rosette at the base.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological significance, supporting various pollinators and thriving in Mediterranean climates.
