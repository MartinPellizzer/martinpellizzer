Aria lancastriensis

Aria lancastriensis, commonly known as the Lancashire arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to the British Isles.

It features arrow-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, white flowers with a distinctive arrowhead shape, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in damp woodlands or along stream banks.

Aria lancastriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a habitat for various invertebrates.
