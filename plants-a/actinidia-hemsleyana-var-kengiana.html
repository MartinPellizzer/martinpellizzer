Actinidia hemsleyana var. kengiana

Actinidia hemsleyana var. kengiana, commonly known as the Keng's actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by edible, aromatic berries that ripen to a dark purple color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in fruit cultivation.
