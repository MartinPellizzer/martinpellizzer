Astragalus gawrilowii

Astragalus gawrilowii, commonly known as the Gawrilow's milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, particularly found in Kazakhstan and southern Siberia. The plant typically grows as a low-growing perennial with compound leaves and produces small, yellow to brown flowers. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

Astragalus gawrilowii is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
