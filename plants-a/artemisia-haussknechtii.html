Artemisia haussknechtii

Artemisia haussknechtii, commonly known as the desert wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and small, yellowish flower heads. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and sagebrush habitats. Its leaves contain volatile oils that contribute to its strong, bitter scent.

Artemisia haussknechtii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
