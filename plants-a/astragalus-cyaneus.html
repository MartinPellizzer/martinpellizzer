Astragalus cyaneus

Astragalus cyaneus, commonly known as the blue milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus cyaneus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing food for various pollinators and grazing animals.
