Aster cassiniae

Aster cassiniae, commonly known as the Cassinia aster, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands.

Aster cassiniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
