Asphodelus microcarpus var. jacobi

Asphodelus microcarpus var. jacobi, commonly known as Jacob's asphodel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stems with clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry meadows. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
