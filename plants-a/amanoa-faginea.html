Amanoa faginea

Amanoa faginea, commonly known as the Hawaiian fagopyrum, is a member of the Polygonaceae family.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its stout, branched stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is native to the Hawaiian Islands.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
