Aplocera

Aplocera, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves, relying instead on its roots for photosynthesis. The plant produces striking white or pale green flowers that bloom in late summer. Aplocera thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in swamps or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its delicate nature and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
