Acalypha peduncularis var. psilogyne

Acalypha peduncularis var. psilogyne, commonly known as red-veined nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, deeply lobed leaves with prominent red veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically appearing in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique foliage.
