Andicolea ilinissae

Andicolea ilinissae, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires partial shade to full sun depending on the region. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized root system that aids in nutrient absorption.
