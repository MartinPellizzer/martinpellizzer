Andromeda flexuosa

Andromeda flexuosa, commonly known as the zigzag andromeda, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and in swampy areas. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a distinctive zigzag or wavy margin, giving it its common name.

Andromeda flexuosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
