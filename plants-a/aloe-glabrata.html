Aloe glabrata

Aloe glabrata, commonly known as the bare-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a thick, fleshy stem that produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating. The leaves are typically bluish-green in color and have a smooth texture, lacking the spines found in some other aloe species. It produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
