Arundinaria nipponica

Arundinaria nipponica, commonly known as Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green in color and arranged in a dense clump. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Arundinaria nipponica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
