Alyssum fulvescens var. stellatocarpum

Alyssum fulvescens var. stellatocarpum, commonly known as the starfruit alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces spherical, yellowish seed pods that resemble star fruits, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
