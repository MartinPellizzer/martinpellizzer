Aloe vandermerwei

Aloe vandermerwei, commonly known as the Orange-Striped Aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick, fleshy, and covered in a layer of white powder. The leaves are typically pale green with distinct orange or reddish markings along the edges. It produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer.

This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.
