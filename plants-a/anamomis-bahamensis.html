Anamomis bahamensis

Anamomis bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to the Bahamas.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding substrate, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
