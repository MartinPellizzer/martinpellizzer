Anaphalis aecidiocephala

Anaphalis aecidiocephala, commonly known as the woolly aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, white, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
