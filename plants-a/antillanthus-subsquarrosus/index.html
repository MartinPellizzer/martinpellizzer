Antillanthus Subsquarrosus

Antillanthus subsquarrosus is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Mexico. Known for its striking, velvety leaves and delicate, pale yellow blooms, it captivates botanists and plant enthusiasts alike. Despite its beauty, this species remains poorly understood due to its limited distribution and elusive nature. Its survival is threatened by habitat loss and climate change, making conservation efforts critical.
