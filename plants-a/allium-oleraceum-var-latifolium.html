Allium oleraceum var. latifolium

Allium oleraceum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf garlic.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. The plant features basal rosettes of broad, fleshy leaves and produces umbels of small, white or pale purple flowers. Its bulbous structures are edible and have a mild garlic flavor.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
