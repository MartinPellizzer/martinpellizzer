Anthemis zoharyana

Anthemis zoharyana, commonly known as the Armenian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or semi-arid environments.

The essential oils extracted from its flowers are used in traditional medicine for their calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
