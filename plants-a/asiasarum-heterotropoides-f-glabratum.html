Asiasarum heterotropoides f. glabratum

Asiasarum heterotropoides f. glabratum, commonly known as the Japanese ginger or Japanese mountain ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in traditional medicine.
