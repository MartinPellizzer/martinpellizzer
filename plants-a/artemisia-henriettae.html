Artemisia henriettae

Artemisia henriettae, commonly known as Henrietta's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant contains essential oils and bitter compounds, which contribute to its medicinal and pesticidal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Its historical use by indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes highlights its cultural and botanical significance.
