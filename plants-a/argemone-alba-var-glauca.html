Argemone alba var. glauca

Argemone alba var. glauca, commonly known as white argemone, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual with yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain oily seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and herbal practices.
