Asplenium ruta-muraria var. schriesheimense

Asplenium ruta-muraria var. schriesheimense, commonly known as the Schriesheim's rock spleenwort, is a terrestrial fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen rosettes of lance-shaped leaves. The plant has a distinctive, creeping growth habit with rhizomes that allow it to colonize rocky substrates and crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a rough, leathery texture.

This variant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
