Abies alba var. columnaris

Abies alba var. columnaris, commonly known as the columnar white fir, is a variant of the European white fir characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit.

This evergreen conifer features needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly glossy appearance. It produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

Due to its compact form, it is often cultivated as an ornamental tree in gardens and landscapes.
