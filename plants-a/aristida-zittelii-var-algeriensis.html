Aristida zittelii var. algeriensis

Aristida zittelii var. algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian threeawn, is a flowering grass species native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

This variety is part of the grass family Poaceae and is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
