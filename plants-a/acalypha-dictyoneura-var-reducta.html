Acalypha dictyoneura var. reducta

Acalypha dictyoneura var. reducta, commonly known as the red-twigged ironwood, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features distinctive, slender, reddish-brown twigs that provide striking visual contrast against its dark green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by three-lobed, papery bracts that add to its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its unique appearance and hardy nature.
