Ascaricida

Ascaricida, commonly known as theascaricidae, is a family of nematodes that primarily infects the intestines of various animals, including mammals, birds, and reptiles.

These parasitic worms are characterized by their elongated, cylindrical bodies and the presence of a distinctive mouth structure adapted for feeding on host tissues. Ascaricidae species are often found in the gastrointestinal tract, where they mature and reproduce, leading to significant health issues in their hosts. The life cycle of these nematodes typically involves egg-laying in the environment, which can then be ingested by a new host.

Due to their parasitic nature, Ascaricidae are of considerable importance in veterinary and human medicine.
