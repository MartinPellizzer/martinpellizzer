Antizoma capensis

Antizoma capensis, commonly known as the Cape antizoma, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, commonly referred to as the ice plant family, and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing stems and leaves. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions through its thick, waxy leaves.

Antizoma capensis is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
