Acer campestre f. collinum

Acer campestre f. collinum, commonly known as the hill maple, is a compact, slow-growing shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded crown, smooth bark, and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant typically reaches a height of 2 to 5 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate urban conditions.

Its flowers are inconspicuous, and it produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.
