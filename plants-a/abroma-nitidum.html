Abroma nitidum

Abroma nitidum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf abroma, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves with a slightly velvety texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

Abroma nitidum is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands, and it has some use in traditional medicine.
