Athyriopsis erecta

Athyriopsis erecta, commonly known as the upright male fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its upright, compact growth habit. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are often dark green and glossy, with a dense arrangement of small, oval-shaped fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Athyriopsis erecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
