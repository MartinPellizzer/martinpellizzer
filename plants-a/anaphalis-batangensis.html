Anaphalis batangensis

Anaphalis batangensis, commonly known as the Batang silverweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it adapts to cold and high-altitude conditions.

Anaphalis batangensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
