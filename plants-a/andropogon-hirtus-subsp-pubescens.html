Andropogon hirtus subsp. pubescens

Andropogon hirtus subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine hairs.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife.
