Adiantum capillus-veneris var. imbricatum

Adiantum capillus-veneris var. imbricatum, commonly known as the veined maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green with a subtle veining pattern. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, moist soil to flourish. Its fronds are bi-lobed and often have a wavy or undulating edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in indoor and outdoor settings with proper care.
