Aristolochia cardenasii

Aristolochia cardenasii, commonly known as the Cretan birthwort, is a climbing vine native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and produces distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant is characterized by its complex, often spiraled inflorescence and is adapted to grow in rocky or sunny environments. It is part of the Aristolochiaceae family, known for its unique floral structures and chemical compounds.

However, it contains toxic compounds and is not suitable for consumption or medicinal use without proper preparation.
