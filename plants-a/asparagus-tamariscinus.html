Asparagus tamariscinus

Asparagus tamariscinus, commonly known as the silver asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to the rocky, well-drained soils of its native habitat.

Asparagus tamariscinus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
