Aechmea humilis

Aechmea humilis, commonly known as the silver vase plant, is a species of bromeliad native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces striking inflorescences with colorful flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Aechmea humilis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its unique structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Morphology

Aechmea humilis has broad, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette formation, with each leaf measuring up to 30 cm in length and 5 cm in width, featuring a bluish-green color with a waxy coating.

The plant has a short, upright stem that is covered in a thin, pale green bark, growing from the base of the rosette. Its flowers are clustered in a spike-like inflorescence, with purple bracts and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom during the summer months, pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains numerous small, black seeds, which are dispersed by birds and mammals.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in its rocky or coastal habitats.
