Acer laetum var. tricaudatum

Acer laetum var. tricaudatum, commonly known as the three-tailed maple, is a decorative variant of the silver maple.

It is characterized by its unique, trifoliate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn, making it a popular ornamental tree.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight for optimal growth.
