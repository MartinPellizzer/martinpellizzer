Abutilon paludosum

Abutilon paludosum, commonly known as swamp abutilon, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that often have a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its adaptability to wet conditions, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
