Anthemis fumarioides

Anthemis fumarioides, commonly known as the smokeplant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a fine, powdery coating on its leaves and stems, which gives it a smoky appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

The plant is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
