Apium ammi f. pedunculatum

Apium ammi f. pedunculatum, commonly known as the branched fennel, is a cultivated variant of the common fennel plant.

It is characterized by its branched inflorescence and slender, aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its seeds are small, oval, and have a distinctive licorice-like aroma.

This variety is often grown for its ornamental value and as a source of edible leaves and seeds.
