Amaryllis vittata

Amaryllis vittata, commonly known as the spotted amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and red, often with a distinctive spotted or streaked pattern. The plant produces a tall, narrow stem that bears multiple blooms, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy texture.

Amaryllis vittata thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
