Andruris crinita

Andruris crinita, commonly known as the curly hair plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, curly foliage that resembles hair, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually small, leathery, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Andruris crinita is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its distinctive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
