Astragalus rostanii

Astragalus rostanii, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and may have traditional uses in local medicine.

Its ecological importance lies in its ability to thrive in harsh environments and support local biodiversity.
