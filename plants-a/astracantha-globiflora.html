Astracantha globiflora

Astracantha globiflora, commonly known as the globeflower, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, globular flower heads composed of numerous small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Astracantha globiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
