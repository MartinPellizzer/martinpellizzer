Achyranthes piloselloides

Achyranthes piloselloides, commonly known as the hairy dock, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect stem and alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by elongated, ribbed seed pods. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
