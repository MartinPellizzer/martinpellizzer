Aster cyanodiscus

Aster cyanodiscus, commonly known as the blue disk aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, blue-to-purple flower heads that are arranged in a disc-like formation, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Aster cyanodiscus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.
