Allium listera

Allium listera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic.

It is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a dense umbel, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the bulbs are small, with a mild garlic scent.

Allium listera is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.
