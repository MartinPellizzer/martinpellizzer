Ardisia recliniflora

Ardisia recliniflora, commonly known as the hanging ardisia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, reddish-purple berries that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy texture.

Ardisia recliniflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental berries and adaptability to various soil types.
