Anthurium crebrinerve

Anthurium crebrinerve, commonly known as the narrow-veined anthurium, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with prominent, parallel veins. The plant produces clusters of spadices, which are spathaceous inflorescences that resemble bright red or yellow spikes. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Anthurium crebrinerve prefers warm, humid environments and well-draining soil for optimal growth.
