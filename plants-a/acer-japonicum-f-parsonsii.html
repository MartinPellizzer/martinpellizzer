Acer japonicum f. parsonsii

Acer japonicum f. parsonsii, commonly known as the Japanese maple var. Parsonsii, is a striking ornamental tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange foliage that displays dramatic seasonal changes, particularly in the fall. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are often deeply lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
