Anacampseros dumeticola

Anacampseros dumeticola, commonly known as the desert stonecress, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, aiding its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal dunes or inland deserts.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is cultivated as a drought-tolerant garden plant.
