Allium pallens var. coppoleri

Allium pallens var. coppoleri, commonly known as the pale garlic.

It is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of pale pink to white flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and edible gardens.
