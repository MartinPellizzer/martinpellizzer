Aconitum napellus f. swidovense

Aconitum napellus f. swidovense, commonly known as the Swidovian monkshood, is a variant of the European monkshood.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. The plant features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a spire-like inflorescence. Its leaves are palmately compound with deeply lobed segments.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
