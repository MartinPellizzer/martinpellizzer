Acinodendron amplexans

Acinodendron amplexans, commonly known as the clinging acacia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that often wrap around the stems, giving it a unique, compact appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forest understories.

The species is notable for its ability to form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing its growth in nutrient-poor soils.
