Azalea linearifolia

Azalea linearifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant produces pink to red blooms in the spring, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Azalea linearifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
