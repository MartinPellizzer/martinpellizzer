Anisacantha incana

Anisacantha incana, commonly known as the hairy devil's-bit, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, characterized by its woody stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round, greenish fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
