Anaphrenium insigne

Anaphrenium insigne, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a thick, succulent stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Anaphrenium insigne is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a potted plant.
