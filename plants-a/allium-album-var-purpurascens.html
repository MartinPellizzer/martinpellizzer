Allium album var. purpurascens

Allium album var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple onion, is a variant of the common garlic plant.

It is characterized by its bulbous stem and pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. It has a mild, sweet flavor compared to other onions and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Allium genus.
