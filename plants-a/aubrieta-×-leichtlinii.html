Aubrieta × leichtlinii

Aubrieta × leichtlinii, commonly known as the hybrid aubrieta, is a low-growing, flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant produces upright, branching stems that bear numerous flowering shoots, making it an attractive choice for rock gardens and borders. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered in a fine layer of white or gray pubescence.

Aubrieta × leichtlinii is typically hardy in USDA zones 5 through 9, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.
