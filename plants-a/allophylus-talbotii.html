Allophylus talbotii

Allophylus talbotii, commonly known as the Talbot's holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 feet and features dense, dark green foliage with glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for garden landscapes.

Allophylus talbotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide year-round interest in the garden.
