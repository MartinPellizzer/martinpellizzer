Aceratium branderhorstii

Aceratium branderhorstii, commonly known as the Brandehorst's acacia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique foliage and floral structure, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
