Anthurium marinoanum

Anthurium marinoanum, commonly known as the Marino's arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and inflorescence structure. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. Its spadix, a central spike, is surrounded by a colorful spathe, which serves as the showy floral structure.

Anthurium marinoanum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
