Acacia kalachariensis

Acacia kalachariensis, commonly known as the Kalahari acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh, dry climates.

The wood of Acacia kalachariensis is hard and durable, historically used by indigenous communities for various purposes.
