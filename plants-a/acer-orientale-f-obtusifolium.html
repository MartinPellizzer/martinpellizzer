Acer orientale f. obtusifolium

Acer orientale f. obtusifolium, commonly known as the Japanese maple with rounded leaves, is a variant of the oriental maple.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically a vibrant green during the growing season. The plant produces small, red flowers in early spring, which are followed by orange-red samaras in the fall. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens.

Its foliage changes color with the seasons, adding visual interest throughout the year.
