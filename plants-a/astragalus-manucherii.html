Astragalus manucherii

Astragalus manucherii, commonly known as the Manucher's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus manucherii is often found in steppes and mountainous areas, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
