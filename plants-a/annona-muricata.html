Annona muricata

Annona muricata, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, spiky green fruit that turns yellow or red when ripe. The plant has a rough, woody stem and broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow with a strong fragrance.

The fruit is known for its sweet, creamy flesh and is widely cultivated for its edible produce.
