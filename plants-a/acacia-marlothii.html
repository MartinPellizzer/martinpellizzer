Acacia marlothii

Acacia marlothii, commonly known as the marloth's acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow, spherical flower heads that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in drought conditions. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and fuel.

The plant also plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
