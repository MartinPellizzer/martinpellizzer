Arabis saxeticola

Arabis saxeticola, commonly known as the saxitic alpine rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Arabis saxeticola is often found in mountain meadows and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
