Anemone patens var. wolfgangiana

Anemone patens var. wolfgangiana, commonly known as the Wolfgang's fairy bell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
