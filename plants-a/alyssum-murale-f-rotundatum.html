Alyssum murale f. rotundatum

Alyssum murale f. rotundatum, commonly known as the round-leafed alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically rounded and hairy, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as bees and ladybugs.
