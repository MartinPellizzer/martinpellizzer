Acinodendron subnodosum

Acinodendron subnodosum, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of trees in sub-Saharan Africa.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in small inflorescences. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients but can photosynthesize to some extent. Its berries are typically red or yellow and are dispersed by birds.

Acinodendron subnodosum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of forested areas.
