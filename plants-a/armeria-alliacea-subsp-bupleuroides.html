Armeria alliacea subsp. bupleuroides

Armeria alliacea subsp.

bupleuroides, commonly known as the bluebells of the rock, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and long flowering period make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
