Asplenium adiantum-nigrum subsp. silesiacum

Asplenium adiantum-nigrum subsp.

silesiacum, commonly known as the Silesian maidenhair fern, is a member of the family Polypodiaceae. It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and slightly glossy.

The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
