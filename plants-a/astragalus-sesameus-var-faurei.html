Astragalus sesameus var. faurei

Astragalus sesameus var. faurei, commonly known as the Faure's milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale purple flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and has a deep root system that allows it to survive in arid conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its small, pod-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
