Actinodaphne ellipticibacca

Actinodaphne ellipticibacca, commonly known as the broad-leafed camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, making it valuable in traditional medicine and perfumery.
