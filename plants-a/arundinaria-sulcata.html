Arundinaria sulcata

Arundinaria sulcata, commonly known as the bamboo grass or bamboo reed, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Arundinaria sulcata is also valued for its use in traditional crafts and as a source of sustainable materials.
