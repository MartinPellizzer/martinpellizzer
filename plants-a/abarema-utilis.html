Abarema utilis

Abarema utilis, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble peacock feathers. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Abarema utilis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
