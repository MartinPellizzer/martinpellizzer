Aristida macroclada

Aristida macroclada, commonly known as the broadleaf threeawn, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often clustered in three to five rows along the stem. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils.

Aristida macroclada is adapted to withstand drought and heat, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
