Amphineuron queenslandicum

Amphineuron queenslandicum, commonly known as the Queensland lily, is a striking perennial herb native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
