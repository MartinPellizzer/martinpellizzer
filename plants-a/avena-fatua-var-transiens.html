Avena fatua var. transiens

Avena fatua var. transiens, commonly known as the transitory wild oat, is a variant of the wild oat species native to temperate regions.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and roadside areas.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered a weed in many regions.
