Acomastylis peckii

Acomastylis peckii, commonly known as the Peck's acomastylis, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in arid environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Acomastylis peckii is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, where it thrives in full sun.
