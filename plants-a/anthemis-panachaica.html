Anthemis panachaica

Anthemis panachaica, commonly known as the Greek tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in dry meadows and rocky areas, preferring full sun.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a garden ornamental highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
