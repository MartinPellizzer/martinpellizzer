Astragalus sinicus

Astragalus sinicus, commonly known as Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions and is adapted to dry climates. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus sinicus is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
