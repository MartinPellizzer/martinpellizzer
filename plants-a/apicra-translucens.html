Apicra translucens

Apicra translucens, commonly known as the glassleaf fern, is a unique plant characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that allow light to pass through, creating a shimmering effect.

It is native to the humid understory of tropical rainforests, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant's rhizomes spread horizontally, forming dense mats that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters near the base of the plant.

Apicra translucens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying properties.
