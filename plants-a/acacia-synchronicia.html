Acacia synchronicia

Acacia synchronicia, commonly known as the synchronous acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates. It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.

However, it is relatively rare and requires specific conditions for successful growth.
