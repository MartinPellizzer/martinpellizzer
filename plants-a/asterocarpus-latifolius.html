Asterocarpus latifolius

Asterocarpus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf asterocarpus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
