Asparagus penduliflorus

Asparagus penduliflorus, commonly known as the hanging flower asparagus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that hang from the stems. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Asparagus penduliflorus is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover due to its compact growth habit.
