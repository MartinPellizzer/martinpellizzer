Aleuritia pulchella

Aleuritia pulchella, commonly known as the silverleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. Its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in late summer and are edible for both humans and wildlife.

Aleuritia pulchella is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
