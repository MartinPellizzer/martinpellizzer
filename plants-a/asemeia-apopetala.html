Asemeia apopetala

Asemeia apopetala, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pale, almost translucent flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant lacks leaves and relies entirely on its roots for nutrient absorption from the air and surrounding environment. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on the trunks of trees in wetlands or pine flatwoods.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts.
