Alsine zarencznyi

Alsine zarencznyi, commonly known as the Armenian sandgrass, is a low-growing annual plant native to the arid regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize open, disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.
