Acaena integerrima var. latifrons

Acaena integerrima var. latifrons, commonly known as the broad-fruited spinsteria, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a tart, acidic flavor. These fruits are an important food source for native birds and insects.

It thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often growing in well-drained soils.
