Amaryllis hemographes

Amaryllis hemographes, commonly known as the red-veined amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink with deep red veins, creating a dramatic visual contrast. The plant produces a tall, sturdy stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting clusters of flowers. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and often have a waxy texture.

Amaryllis hemographes is well-suited for gardens with well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation.
