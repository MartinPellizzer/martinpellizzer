Agapanthus africanus var. minimus

Agapanthus africanus var. minimus, commonly known as the dwarf agapanthus, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to South Africa.

It features striking, spherical clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has strap-shaped, dark green leaves that form a dense rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
