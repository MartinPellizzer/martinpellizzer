Abronia fragrans f. elliptica

Abronia fragrans f. elliptica, commonly known as the fragrant sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pinkish flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and showy blooms.
