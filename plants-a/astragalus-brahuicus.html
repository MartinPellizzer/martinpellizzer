Astragalus brahuicus

Astragalus brahuicus, commonly known as the Brahui milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of South Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in its habitat. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The seeds of Astragalus brahuicus are adapted for dispersal by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
