Aconitopsis scleroclada

Aconitopsis scleroclada, commonly known as the rock aconite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features a short, stocky stem with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in a raceme, and bloom in late summer.

Aconitopsis scleroclada is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
