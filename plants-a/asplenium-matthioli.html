Asplenium matthioli

Asplenium matthioli, commonly known as the silver-veined maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds with distinctive silver veins, which give it a striking appearance. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with small, narrow pinnae that contribute to its ornamental value.

It is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its elegant foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
