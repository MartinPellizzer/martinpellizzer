Achillea millefolium var. alpicola

Achillea millefolium var. alpicola, commonly known as alpine yarrow, is a variant of the common yarrow plant.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, characterized by its ability to thrive in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves and small, flat-topped clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It is adapted to cold climates and high altitudes, often found in mountainous areas of Europe and North America.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for alpine flora and fauna.
