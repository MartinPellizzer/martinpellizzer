Annona dioica var. mattogrossensis

Annona dioica var. mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherimoya, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces a fruit similar to a cherimoya, which is typically green when unripe and develops a creamy, sweet flesh when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
