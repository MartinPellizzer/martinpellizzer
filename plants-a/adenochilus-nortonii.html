Adenochilus nortonii

Adenochilus nortonii, commonly known as the Norton's spike orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, spike-like inflorescence. The plant produces showy, often brightly colored flowers that are typically borne on a single, elongated stem. Adenochilus nortonii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
