Artanthe caladiifolia

Artanthe caladiifolia, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange or red flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has feathery, gray-green foliage and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Artanthe caladiifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
