Arisaema filiforme var. fallax

Arisaema filiforme var. fallax, commonly known as the slender thread arum, is a variant of the thread arum native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and a distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
