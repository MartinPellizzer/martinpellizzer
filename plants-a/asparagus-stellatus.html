Asparagus stellatus

Asparagus stellatus, commonly known as the star asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, branched stems and small, needle-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
