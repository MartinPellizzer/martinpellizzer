Astragalus Dichopterus

Astragalus dichopterus is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family and is known for its unique two-petaled flowers that bloom in late summer. This species thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts. Its beauty and ecological importance make it a subject of interest for botanists and nature enthusiasts alike.
