Adenophora triphylla var. hakusanensis

Adenophora triphylla var. hakusanensis, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features opposite, lanceolate leaves with a hairy underside, and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
