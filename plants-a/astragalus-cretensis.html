Astragalus cretensis

Astragalus cretensis, commonly known as the Cretan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus cretensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
