Allium schischkinii

Allium schischkinii, commonly known as the Schischkin's onion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a bulbous base with several narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers arranged in a spherical umbel, which bloom in late summer. Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Allium genus, known for its ornamental value and culinary potential.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, thriving in cooler climates.
