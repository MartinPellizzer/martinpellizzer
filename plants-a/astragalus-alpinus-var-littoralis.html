Astragalus alpinus var. littoralis

Astragalus alpinus var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and is adapted to grow in sandy or gravelly soils. It thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.

This variety is often found in dunes and rocky shores, contributing to the ecological stability of these habitats.
