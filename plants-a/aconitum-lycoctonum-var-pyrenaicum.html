Aconitum lycoctonum var. pyrenaicum

Aconitum lycoctonum var. pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It features striking purple to violet flowers arranged in a hooded shape, typical of the genus Aconitum. The plant has large, doubly compound leaves that are deeply lobed and have a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Like other aconites, it contains potent toxins and should be handled with care.
