Acia dulcis

Acia dulcis, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and dill, and is characterized by its finely divided leaves and umbel-like flower clusters. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into oval, aromatic seeds. Sweet cicely is known for its sweet, anise-like scent and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
