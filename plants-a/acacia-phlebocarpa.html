Acacia phlebocarpa

Acacia phlebocarpa, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and its distinctive, flat, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typically followed by long, slender pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia phlebocarpa is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil rehabilitation efforts.
