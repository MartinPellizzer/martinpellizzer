Agave ixtli

Agave ixtli, commonly known as the Mexican basket agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are often used in traditional basket weaving. The plant produces a tall flower stalk with white or yellow flowers, which bloom after several years. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Agave ixtli is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in indigenous cultures.
