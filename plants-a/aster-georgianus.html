Aster georgianus

Aster georgianus, commonly known as the Georgian aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the plant often forms clumps with multiple flowering stems.

Aster georgianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
