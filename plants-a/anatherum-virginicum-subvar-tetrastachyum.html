Anatherum virginicum subvar. tetrastachyum

Anatherum virginicum subvar. tetrastachyum, commonly known as the tall panic grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences with multiple branches, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.
