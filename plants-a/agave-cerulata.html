Agave cerulata

Agave cerulata, commonly known as the sky blue agave, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears white to pale blue flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its potential in sustainable landscaping.
