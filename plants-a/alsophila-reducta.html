Alsophila reducta

Alsophila reducta, commonly known as the dwarf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and a crown of feathery fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 3 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting the plant's structure and aiding in nutrient absorption.

Alsophila reducta is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices.
