Arundinaria chartacea

Arundinaria chartacea, commonly known as the bamboo orchid, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a subtle fragrance.

Arundinaria chartacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
