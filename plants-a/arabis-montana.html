Arabis montana

Arabis montana, commonly known as mountain rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine environments. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Arabis montana is often found in open habitats such as meadows, cliffs, and stream banks at high altitudes.
