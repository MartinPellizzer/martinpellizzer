Asarca obovata

Asarca obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate arum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are typically green or white, with a unique structure that facilitates pollination by insects.

Asarca obovata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
