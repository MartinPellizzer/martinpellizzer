Aristolochia lindeniana subsp. bissei

Aristolochia lindeniana subsp.

bissei, commonly known as the Narrowleaf Dutchman's Pipe, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America. It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, tubular, heart-shaped leaves and showy, hooded flowers.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often hidden within its foliage. It is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
