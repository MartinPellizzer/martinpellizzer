Apluda varia var. major

Apluda varia var. major, commonly known as the giant apluda, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a hairy texture.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
