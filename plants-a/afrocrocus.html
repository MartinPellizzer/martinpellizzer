Afrocrocus

Afrocrocus, commonly known as the African gladiolus, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their tall, upright stems and showy, often colorful flowers that resemble those of the gladiolus. Afrocrocus species typically have narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produce inflorescences of multiple blooms. They are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and are adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

The genus includes several species, such as Afrocrocus africanus and Afrocrocus natalensis, which are prized for their beauty and resilience.
