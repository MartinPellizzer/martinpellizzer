Amomum neoaurantiacum

Amomum neoaurantiacum, commonly known as the Chinese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and cardamom. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically pale green to yellowish, with three prominent lobes.

The rhizomes are valued for their aromatic properties and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
