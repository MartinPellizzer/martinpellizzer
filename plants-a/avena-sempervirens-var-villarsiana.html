Avena sempervirens var. villarsiana

Avena sempervirens var. villarsiana, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silver-gray foliage due to the presence of fine, hair-like structures on its leaves. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and aesthetic qualities.
