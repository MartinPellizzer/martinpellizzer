Alyssum bracteatum

Alyssum bracteatum, commonly known as the Mexican candytuft, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies adds to its ecological significance.
