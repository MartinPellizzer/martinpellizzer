Artemisia iskenderiana

Artemisia iskenderiana, commonly known as the Iskenderian wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the high mountain regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to cold and arid environments, often growing at high altitudes. Its leaves contain essential oils and have been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat due to its deep root system.
