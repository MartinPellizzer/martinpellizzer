Achillea travignolensis

Achillea travignolensis, commonly known as the Italian yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its feathery, aromatic foliage and flat, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which range from white to pale pink, bloom from late spring to early summer.

Achillea travignolensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Morphology

Achillea travignolensis has basal leaves that are lanceolate, with a gray-green color and a hairy texture, arranged in a rosette formation.

The stem is upright, slender, and slightly hairy, with a pale green color and a branching growth pattern. Its flowers are composite, consisting of yellow disk flowers surrounded by white ray flowers, blooming in late spring to early summer and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a dry achene, with a pappus for wind dispersal.

The roots are fibrous and rhizomatous, aiding in the plant's ability to spread and establish itself in its habitat.
