Amparoa × humeana

Amparoa × humeana, commonly known as the hybrid amparoa, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Amparoa lindenii and Amparoa humeana.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in montane forests. Its leaves are lanceolate and have a distinctive, slightly serrated margin.

Amparoa × humeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
