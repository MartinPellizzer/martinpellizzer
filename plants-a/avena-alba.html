Avena alba

Avena alba, commonly known as slender oat, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its seeds are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Avena alba is also valued as a forage crop for livestock due to its nutritious foliage.
