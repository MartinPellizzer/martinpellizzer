Arenaria trachyphylla

Arenaria trachyphylla, commonly known as the rough sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically forms dense mats with small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs. The flowers are usually white or pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and is often found in tundra and mountainous areas.
