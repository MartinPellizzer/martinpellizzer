Antirrhinum orontium var. genuinum

Antirrhinum orontium var. genuinum, commonly known as the garden snapdragon, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow, often with a distinctive spurred shape. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the garden.
