Atriplex leucoclada var. mandavillea

Atriplex leucoclada var. mandavillea, commonly known as the Mandaville saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in a dense layer of fine hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

This species is often used in rehabilitation of degraded lands due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
