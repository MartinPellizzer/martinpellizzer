Aragallus multiceps

Aragallus multiceps, commonly known as the mountain peavine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods that contain several seeds. Aragallus multiceps is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of alpine flora.
