Astragalus preussii var. limatus

Astragalus preussii var. limatus, commonly known as the Preuss's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to pale purple flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are typically dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
