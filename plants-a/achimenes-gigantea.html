Achimenes gigantea

Achimenes gigantea, commonly known as the giant flame violet, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters during the warm season. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically a deep green in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Achimenes gigantea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant and long-lasting blooms.

Morphology

Achimenes gigantea has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are large, broad, and arranged alternately along the stem.

The stem is upright, smooth, and green, with a slightly woody texture as it matures. Its flowers are showy, with trumpet-shaped petals in shades of red, orange, or yellow, blooming in summer and attracting bees for pollination. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and help anchor the plant while absorbing nutrients from the soil.
