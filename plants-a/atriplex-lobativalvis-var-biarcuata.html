Atriplex lobativalvis var. biarcuata

Atriplex lobativalvis var. biarcuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, oval to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of salt-tolerant hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in saline soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

This variety is part of the Chenopodiaceae family, which includes many plants with specialized mechanisms for dealing with harsh environmental conditions.
