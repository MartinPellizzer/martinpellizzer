Acer platanoides f. multicolor

Acer platanoides f. multicolor, commonly known as the Norway maple variegated, is a cultivar of the Norway maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves that display white, yellow, and green patterns. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn season. It typically grows as a medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The foliage provides ornamental value throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
