Athanasia acerosa

Athanasia acerosa, commonly known as the hard-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is known for its showy, long-lasting yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its rigid, hairy leaves are adapted to withstand arid conditions, making it drought-tolerant.

Athanasia acerosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
