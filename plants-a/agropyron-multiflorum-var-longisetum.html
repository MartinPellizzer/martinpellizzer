Agropyron multiflorum var. longisetum

Agropyron multiflorum var. longisetum, commonly known as the tall wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, slender blades that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces spikelets that are often arranged in panicles, with each spikelet containing several small, awned florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used for erosion control and livestock forage.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration and sustainable agriculture.
