Aconitum pterocaule var. glabrescens

Aconitum pterocaule var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of soft, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in temperate climates.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
