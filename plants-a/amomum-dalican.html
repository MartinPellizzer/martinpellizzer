Amomum dalican

Amomum dalican, commonly known as the Sichuan cardamom, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces elongated, three-lobed fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional Chinese medicine and culinary applications. Its rhizomes and leaves are valued for their pungent fragrance and medicinal properties.

Amomum dalican thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aromatic compounds.
