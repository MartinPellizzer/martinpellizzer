Arabis nova var. sinaica

Arabis nova var. sinaica, commonly known as the Sinai rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East, particularly the Sinai Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with basal leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its resilience and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
