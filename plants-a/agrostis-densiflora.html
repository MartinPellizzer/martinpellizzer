Agrostis densiflora

Agrostis densiflora, commonly known as denseflower bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features dense, tufted growth with slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

Agrostis densiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
