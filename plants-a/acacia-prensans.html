Acacia prensans

Acacia prensans, commonly known as the climbing acacia, is a shrub or vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, twining stems that allow it to climb and spread across other vegetation. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular inflorescences, which are typical of the Acacia genus. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, linear leaflets.

Acacia prensans is often found in disturbed areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
