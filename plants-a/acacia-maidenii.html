Acacia maidenii

Acacia maidenii, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Acacia maidenii is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water absorption.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
