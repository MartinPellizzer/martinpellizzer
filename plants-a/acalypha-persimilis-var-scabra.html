Acalypha persimilis var. scabra

Acalypha persimilis var. scabra, commonly known as roughleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed areas. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and showy bracts that accompany the flower clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
