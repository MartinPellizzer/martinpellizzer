Aster chryseus

Aster chryseus, commonly known as the golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, surrounded by green bracts. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Aster chryseus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
