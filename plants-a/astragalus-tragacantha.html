Astragalus tragacantha

Astragalus tragacantha, commonly known as goat's head, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
