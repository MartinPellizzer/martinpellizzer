Allium trichocoleum

Allium trichocoleum, commonly known as the woolly leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant features grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its bulb is small and often used as a culinary herb, similar to leeks.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
