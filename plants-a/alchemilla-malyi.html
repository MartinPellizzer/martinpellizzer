Alchemilla malyi

Alchemilla malyi, commonly known as the Maljai lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in dense clusters. Alchemilla malyi is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its drought-resistant properties.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in rock gardens and alpine plantings.
