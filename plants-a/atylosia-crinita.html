Atylosia crinita

Atylosia crinita, commonly known as the curlyleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fuzzy, waxy, curly leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Atylosia crinita is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
