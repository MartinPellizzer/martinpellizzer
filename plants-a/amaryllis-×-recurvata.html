Amaryllis × recurvata

Amaryllis × recurvata, commonly known as the recurved amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis paradisicola.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are broad and strap-shaped, often reaching several feet in length. It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to South America.

This species is popular as a ornamental plant due to its striking floral display and relatively easy care requirements.
