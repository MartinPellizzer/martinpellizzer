Anthyllis herzegovina

Anthyllis herzegovina, commonly known as the Herzegovina clover, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a deep root system that helps it thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Anthyllis herzegovina is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
