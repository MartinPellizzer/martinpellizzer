Asarum albomaculatum

Asarum albomaculatum, commonly known as the white-spotted wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically a deep green with white or pale markings. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom in early spring. Its rhizomatous root system is aromatic and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Asarum albomaculatum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
