Athyrium attenuatum

Athyrium attenuatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Japanese shield fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often divided into small, pointed segments. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a subtle, elegant appearance.

Athyrium attenuatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
