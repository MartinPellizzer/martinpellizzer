Actinoscirpus grossus var. kysoor

Actinoscirpus grossus var. kysoor, commonly known as the Kysoor sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its blue to violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a robust, erect growth habit with thick, fleshy stems and waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in saline environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in estuaries and tidal flats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
