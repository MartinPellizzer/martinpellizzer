Archidendron poilanei

Archidendron poilanei, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown. Its bark is often fissured and greyish, while its seeds are enclosed in pods that split open to release small, winged seeds.

This plant is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
