Arum quinatum

Arum quinatum, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, with a deep green color and a waxy texture. The plant produces striking, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a showy purple spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistently moist soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
