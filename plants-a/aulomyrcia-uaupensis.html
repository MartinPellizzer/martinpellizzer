Aulomyrcia uaupensis

Aulomyrcia uaupensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features elliptical, leathery leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small, white, and fragrant, appearing in clusters during the spring season.

The tree produces woody fruits that contain a single seed, and it plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
