Acoridium longifolium

Acoridium longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical regions.

It features long, slender leaves that are often submerged in water, aiding in photosynthesis. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats. Its root system helps stabilize the substrate and provides habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Acoridium longifolium plays a role in water filtration and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
