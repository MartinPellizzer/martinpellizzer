Actinomorphe humilis

Actinomorphe humilis, commonly known as the low actinomorph, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, facilitating its dispersal by wind.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
