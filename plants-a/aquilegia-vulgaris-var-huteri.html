Aquilegia vulgaris var. huteri

Aquilegia vulgaris var. huteri, commonly known as the Huter's columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in a raceme. The plant has palmately compound leaves with five leaflets, and it thrives in partial shade to full sun. It is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
