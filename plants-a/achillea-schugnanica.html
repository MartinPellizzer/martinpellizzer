Achillea schugnanica

Achillea schugnanica, commonly known as the Schugnan yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow florets arranged in flat-topped clusters, typical of the genus Achillea. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its foliage is aromatic and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its resilience in harsh environments.
