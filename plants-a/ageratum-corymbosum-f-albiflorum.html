Ageratum corymbosum f. albiflorum

Ageratum corymbosum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is a cultivar of the common ageratum, characterized by its pale white to nearly pure white flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual, reaching heights of about 30 to 60 centimeters. Its foliage is gray-green with a slightly fuzzy texture, and it produces compact, rounded clusters of small flowers.

This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
