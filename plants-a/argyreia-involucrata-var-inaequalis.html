Argyreia involucrata var. inaequalis

Argyreia involucrata var. inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for hanging baskets and trellises.
