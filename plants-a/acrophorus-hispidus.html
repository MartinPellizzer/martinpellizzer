Acrophorus hispidus

Acrophorus hispidus, commonly known as the hairy acanthus, is a striking plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in dense, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces showy, spiky inflorescences that range in color from red to purple, adding visual interest to its overall structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique foliage and vibrant flowers, Acrophorus hispidus is often cultivated for both its aesthetic value and its ability to attract pollinators.
