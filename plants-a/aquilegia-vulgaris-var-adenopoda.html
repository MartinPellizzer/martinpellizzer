Aquilegia vulgaris var. adenopoda

Aquilegia vulgaris var. adenopoda, commonly known as the Downy Columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, with five petals and two elongated nectar guides. The leaves are doubly pinnate, consisting of small, oval leaflets arranged in pairs along the stem. This variety is characterized by its hairy or downy texture on the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in woodland or shady areas and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
