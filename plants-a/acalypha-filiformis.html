Acalypha filiformis

Acalypha filiformis, commonly known as silkyleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that give it its scientific name, "filiformis," meaning thread-like. The plant produces showy, hibiscus-like flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Acalypha filiformis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
