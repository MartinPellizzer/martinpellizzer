Anthoshorea roxburghii

Anthoshorea roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian banyan, is a large, deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its extensive, cascading roots that form a dense network around the base. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by edible fig-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin.

Anthoshorea roxburghii is often found in moist, tropical regions and is valued for its ecological and medicinal properties.
