Achillea × travignolensis

Achillea × travignolensis, commonly known as the hybrid yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves and flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
