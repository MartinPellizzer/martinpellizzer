Argyrolobium comanthum

Argyrolobium comanthum, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in globular inflorescences, giving it its common name. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, rounded leaflets.

Argyrolobium comanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
