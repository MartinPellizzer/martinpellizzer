Anthemis hirpina

Anthemis hirpina, commonly known as the narrowleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, narrow leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats like grasslands and rocky soils.

Anthemis hirpina is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic and possibly antiseptic properties.
