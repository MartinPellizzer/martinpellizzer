Acacia torquata

Acacia torquata, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, twisted branches and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, flat, green pods that contain seeds covered in a fibrous mesh. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Acacia torquata is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
