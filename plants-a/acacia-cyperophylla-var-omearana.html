Acacia cyperophylla var. omearana

Acacia cyperophylla var. omearana, commonly known as the White Wattle, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features feathery, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white powder.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
