Arundo airiformis

Arundo airiformis, commonly known as the air-form reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to certain regions of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and feathery, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and wetlands. Its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands make it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses and cereals.
