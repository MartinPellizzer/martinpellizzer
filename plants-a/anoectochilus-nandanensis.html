Anoectochilus nandanensis

Anoectochilus nandanensis, commonly known as the Nandanense Orchid, is a rare and exquisite orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its striking, often fragrant flowers that bloom in a range of colors, including white, pink, and violet. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. It is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in specialized orchid collections.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered in its natural range.
