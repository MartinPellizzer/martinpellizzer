Achillea rhodoptarmica var. rosea

Achillea rhodoptarmica var. rosea, commonly known as the pink yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers in early summer. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.

The varietal name 'rosea' refers to the pale pink coloration of its flower heads.
