Althaea trimestris

Althaea trimestris, commonly known as the three-week mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and blooms from spring to early summer. Its fibrous roots and mucilage-rich leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Althaea trimestris thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
