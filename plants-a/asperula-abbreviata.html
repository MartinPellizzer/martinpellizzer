Asperula abbreviata

Asperula abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf sweet pea, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Asperula abbreviata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
