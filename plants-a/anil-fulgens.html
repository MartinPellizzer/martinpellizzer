Anil fulgens

Anil fulgens, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its large, showy, flame-colored flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical name reflects its striking appearance, with "fulgens" meaning radiant or shining.
