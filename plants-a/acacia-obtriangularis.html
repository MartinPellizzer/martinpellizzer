Acacia obtriangularis

Acacia obtriangularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, triangular-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flower spikes that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia obtriangularis is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
