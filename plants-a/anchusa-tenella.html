Anchusa tenella

Anchusa tenella, commonly known as blueweed or narrowleaf anchusa, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Anchusa tenella is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
