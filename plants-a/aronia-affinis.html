Aronia affinis

Aronia affinis, commonly known as the Canadian elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2-4 meters and features compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are white and appear in cymes, followed by the development of its edible fruits.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
