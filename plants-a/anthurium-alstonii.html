Anthurium alstonii

Anthurium alstonii, commonly known as the Alston's flamingo flower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, with a spathe that resembles a bird's beak, giving it its common name.

Anthurium alstonii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
