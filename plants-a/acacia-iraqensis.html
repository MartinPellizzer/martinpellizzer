Acacia iraqensis

Acacia iraqensis, commonly known as the Iraqi acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Iraq and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and phyllodes that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia iraqensis thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry conditions.

It is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
