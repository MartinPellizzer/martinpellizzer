Aeria attenuata

Aeria attenuata, commonly known as the slender air plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Aeria attenuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
