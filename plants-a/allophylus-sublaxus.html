Allophylus sublaxus

Allophylus sublaxus, commonly known as the slender alder, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and streambanks. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a leathery texture. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are followed by berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Allophylus sublaxus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
