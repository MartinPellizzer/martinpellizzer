Adiantum fragrantissimum

Adiantum fragrantissimum, commonly known as the fragrant maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Adiantaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often described as lace-like in appearance. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. The fronds emit a subtle, pleasant fragrance, particularly when exposed to moisture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and air-purifying qualities.
