Amaracarpus acuminatus subsp. acuminatus

Amaracarpus acuminatus subsp.

acuminatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved amaracarpus, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and xeriscaped landscapes.
