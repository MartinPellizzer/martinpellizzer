Allium jacquemontii var. grandiflorum

Allium jacquemontii var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the grandiflorum garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features grass-like, strap-shaped leaves that are bluish-green in color and can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild garlic scent.
