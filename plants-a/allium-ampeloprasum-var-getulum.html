Allium ampeloprasum var. getulum

Allium ampeloprasum var. getulum, commonly known as the grape leek, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, white, spherical flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its bulbs, which are the primary edible part, are often used in culinary applications for their mild, onion-like flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species such as leeks and chives.
