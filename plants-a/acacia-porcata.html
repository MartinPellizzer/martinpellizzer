Acacia porcata

Acacia porcata, commonly known as the porcupine acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, cylindrical stems that are covered in small, hooked thorns. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences, which are often visited by birds and insects. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous tiny leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern.

Acacia porcata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
