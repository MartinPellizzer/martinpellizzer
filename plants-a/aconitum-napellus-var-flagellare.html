Aconitum napellus var. flagellare

Aconitum napellus var. flagellare, commonly known as the "whip-like aconite," is a striking member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with palmately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a dangerous plant and should be handled with extreme caution.
