Aniba anisosepala

Aniba anisosepala, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
