Acronia luctuosa

Acronia luctuosa, commonly known as the sorrowful acronia, is a rare flowering plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale blue petals and its unique ability to close its flowers in response to touch, a trait known as thigmonasty. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires a specific combination of soil pH and humidity levels to flourish. Its leaves are broad and slightly serrated, with a velvety texture that aids in water retention.

Due to habitat loss, Acronia luctuosa is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts in its native range.
