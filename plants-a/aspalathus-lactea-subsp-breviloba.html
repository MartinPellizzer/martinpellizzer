Aspalathus lactea subsp. breviloba

Aspalathus lactea subsp.

breviloba, commonly known as the small-leaved milk bush, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, glandular leaves that are typically covered in a fine white pubescence.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
