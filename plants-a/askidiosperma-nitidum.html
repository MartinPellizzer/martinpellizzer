Askidiosperma nitidum

Askidiosperma nitidum, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in local ecosystems.
