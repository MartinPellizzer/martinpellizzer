Aglaia intricatoreticulata

Aglaia intricatoreticulata, commonly known as the intricate nettle tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its compound leaves with a complex, net-like venation pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. Its botanical features make it notable in tropical flora studies.

The tree is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
