Aniseia syringifolia

Aniseia syringifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or grayish pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Aniseia syringifolia is also valued as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
