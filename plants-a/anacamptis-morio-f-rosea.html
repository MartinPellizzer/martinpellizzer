Anacamptis morio f. rosea

Anacamptis morio f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-lilac orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is a hybrid form of Anacamptis morio, characterized by its distinctive pink and purple floral hues. The plant produces tall, slender spikes bearing numerous flowers with a unique, intricate structure. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a delicate, fragrant bloom.

This orchid thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.
