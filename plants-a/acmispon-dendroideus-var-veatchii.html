Acmispon dendroideus var. veatchii

Acmispon dendroideus var. veatchii, commonly known as the veatch's tree clover, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody base and sprawling, branching habit, resembling a small tree or shrub. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its trifoliate leaves consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
