Alyssum calycinum subsp. psilocarpum

Alyssum calycinum subsp.

psilocarpum, commonly known as the purple alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with compact foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
