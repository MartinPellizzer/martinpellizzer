Amaranthus rhombeus

Amaranthus rhombeus, commonly known as the arrowleaf amaranth, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its lanceolate to arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant produces numerous seeds that are often used as a grain or for ornamental purposes. It thrives in warm climates and is commonly found in disturbed soils.

Amaranthus rhombeus is also valued for its nutritional content and resilience to poor soil conditions.
