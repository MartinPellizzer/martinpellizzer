Astragalus nummularius subsp. trichopterus

Astragalus nummularius subsp.

trichopterus, commonly known as the silverweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and disturbed soils.

The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves are usually rounded and covered in a dense layer of fine, silver-white hairs. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.

The subspecies trichopterus is distinguished by its more robust growth and slightly different leaf morphology compared to the main species.
