Aeschynomene arenicola

Aeschynomene arenicola, commonly known as sand bluemink, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers and hairy, succulent leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and inland sand plains. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is considered a valuable forage plant in regions with limited water resources.
