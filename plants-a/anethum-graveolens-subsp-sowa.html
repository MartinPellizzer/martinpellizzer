Anethum graveolens subsp. sowa

Anethum graveolens subsp.

sowa, commonly known as Sowa cumin, is a member of the Apiaceae family and is native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and India. This subspecies is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in umbels.

The plant produces aromatic, oblong seeds that are widely used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its botanical features include pinnately compound leaves and a taproot system, which help it thrive in high-altitude environments.

Sowa cumin is valued for its essential oils, which contain compounds like cuminaldehyde and limonene, contributing to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.
