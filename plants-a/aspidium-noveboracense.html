Aspidium noveboracense

Aspidium noveboracense, commonly known as the New York spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on the bark of trees in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a creeping rhizome with dense, linear leaves that are often clustered at the base. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a dark green, leathery texture and a serrated margin.

Aspidium noveboracense is native to eastern North America and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.
