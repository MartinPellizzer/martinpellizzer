Asparagus mariae

Asparagus mariae, commonly known as the Marie Asparagus, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, fern-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for gardeners in Mediterranean climates.
