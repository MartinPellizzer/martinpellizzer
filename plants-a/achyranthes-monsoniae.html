Achyranthes monsoniae

Achyranthes monsoniae, commonly known as the Monson's smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Central America and the Caribbean.

It typically grows in moist, tropical environments, often near streams or in wetlands. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple in color, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the rainy season.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and resilience.
