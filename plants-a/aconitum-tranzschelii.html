Aconitum tranzschelii

Aconitum tranzschelii, commonly known as the Transhimalayan aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments, and its roots are tuberous, typical of the genus Aconitum. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is highly poisonous and requires caution when handling.
