Airosperma grandifolia

Airosperma grandifolia, commonly known as the giant airseed, is a unique plant characterized by its large, fleshy leaves that can store significant amounts of water.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor it in rocky or arid soils.

The species is native to high-altitude regions and thrives in cool, moist environments.
