Ajuga chamaepitys f. grandiflora

Ajuga chamaepitys f. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered bugleweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant ground cover.
