Acer palmatum f. aureum

Acer palmatum f. aureum, commonly known as the golden maple, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple known for its striking foliage.

It features finely dissected leaves that turn a brilliant yellow in the autumn, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet, with a rounded, compact form. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, preferring cooler climates.

Its unique leaf coloration and seasonal changes make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
