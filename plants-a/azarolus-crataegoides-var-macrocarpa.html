Azarolus crataegoides var. macrocarpa

Azarolus crataegoides var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited azarolus, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elliptical, red or orange fruits that are edible. The plant has woody stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
