Acer × monspessulanum f. martini

Acer × monspessulanum f. martini, commonly known as the Weeping Maple, is a unique hybrid species within the maple genus.

It is characterized by its striking, cascading foliage that creates a dramatic visual effect. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a vibrant green color that turns yellow in autumn. This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often planted in gardens and parks.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
