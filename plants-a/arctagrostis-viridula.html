Arctagrostis viridula

Arctagrostis viridula, commonly known as green wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and pond edges, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. The plant has slender, wiry stems that are often reddish-brown in color, and its leaves are narrow with a bluish-green hue. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Arctagrostis viridula plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
