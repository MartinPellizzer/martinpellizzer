Alyssum cassium

Alyssum cassium, commonly known as winter alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in fine, silvery hairs, and its leaves are narrow and linear. Alyssum cassium thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
