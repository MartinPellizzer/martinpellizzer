Acaena pinnatifida var. macrocephala

Acaena pinnatifida var. macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed wood rose, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, prickly stems and clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant produces spherical, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often forming dense thickets.

Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
