Andropogon nutans subvar. elongatus

Andropogon nutans subvar. elongatus, commonly known as the tall bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often tinged with silver. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or purplish in color. It thrives in full sun and is commonly found in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed soils.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
