Acacia sericocarpa

Acacia sericocarpa, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The silky acacia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally for various purposes, including medicine and basket weaving.
