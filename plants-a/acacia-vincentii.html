Acacia vincentii

Acacia vincentii, commonly known as the Vincent's wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods. Acacia vincentii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
