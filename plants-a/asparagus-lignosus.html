Asparagus lignosus

Asparagus lignosus, commonly known as the woody asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, fern-like foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, needle-like berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Asparagus lignosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
