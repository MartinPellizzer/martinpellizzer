Aloe dichotoma subsp. pillansii

Aloe dichotoma subsp.

pillansii, commonly known as the "Karoo aloe," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its branching, tree-like structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss through transpiration.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
