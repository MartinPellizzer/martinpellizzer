Aegopogon cenchroides

Aegopogon cenchroides, commonly known as the woolly sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

Aegopogon cenchroides is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
