Aguava guianensis

Aguava guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese aguava, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its botanical features include smooth, glossy bark and a dense canopy of foliage.

It is often found growing in humid, shaded environments along riverbanks and in forest understories.
