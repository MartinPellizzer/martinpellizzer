Alsophila aperta

Alsophila aperta, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and fronds that are divided into narrow, overlapping segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and forested areas. Its spore-producing cones are located at the base of the fronds, and the plant can grow up to 10 meters in height.

Alsophila aperta plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
