Acacia palawanensis

Acacia palawanensis, commonly known as the Palawan acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by elongated, flat pods containing seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges, and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native region.

Acacia palawanensis plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional practices.
