Ageratum elassocarpum

Ageratum elassocarpum, commonly known as the Hawaiian ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Hawaii.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, showy flowers. The plant typically features blue or purple flower heads with white or pale blue ray florets, creating a striking visual contrast. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Ageratum elassocarpum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
