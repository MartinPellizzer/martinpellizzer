Aulomyrcia obtusata

Aulomyrcia obtusata, commonly known as the bluntleaf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, producing a subtle, fragrant scent.

Aulomyrcia obtusata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
