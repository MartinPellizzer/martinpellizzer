Acacia asak

Acacia asak, commonly known as the Japanese acacia, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that appear in clusters. Acacia asak is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types makes it a versatile plant in horticulture.
