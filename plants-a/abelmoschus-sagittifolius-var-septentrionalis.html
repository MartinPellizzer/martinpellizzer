Abelmoschus sagittifolius var. septentrionalis

Abelmoschus sagittifolius var. septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern okra, is a variant of the okra species native to the eastern United States.

It is a warm-season herbaceous plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and spiny stems. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain edible seeds, similar to other okra varieties. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring consistent moisture for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its culinary uses and adaptability to temperate climates.
