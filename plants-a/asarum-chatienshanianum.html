Asarum chatienshanianum

Asarum chatienshanianum, commonly known as the Chatien Shan Wild Ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, broad leaves and distinctive, fragrant flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a showy, hooded structure, and it produces small, dark berries as fruit.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
