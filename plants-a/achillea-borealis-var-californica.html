Achillea borealis var. californica

Achillea borealis var. californica, commonly known as California yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with feathery, aromatic foliage and flat clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local pollinators.
