Acacia laricina var. crassifolia

Acacia laricina var. crassifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a woody stem with thorny branches, which helps it survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are thick and leathery, reducing water loss and adapting to drought conditions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
