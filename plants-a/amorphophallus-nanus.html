Amorphophallus nanus

Amorphophallus nanus, commonly known as the dwarf giant taro, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which includes plants like calla lilies and peace lilies. The plant produces a large, white spathe with a purple spadix, resembling a giant artichoke. Its underground corm is edible when properly prepared, though it contains toxic compounds that must be removed.

Amorphophallus nanus is valued for both its ornamental beauty and potential culinary use in certain cultures.
