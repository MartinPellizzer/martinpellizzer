Ardisia cornudentata

Ardisia cornudentata, commonly known as the red-fruited beauty, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are attractive to birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Ardisia cornudentata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
