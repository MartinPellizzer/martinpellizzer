Adenia cynanchifolia

Adenia cynanchifolia, commonly known as the climbing adenium, is a woody vine native to eastern Africa.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive scent. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Adenia cynanchifolia is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.
