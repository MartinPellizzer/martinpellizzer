Alloplectus macrophyllus

Alloplectus macrophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leafed marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Alloplectus macrophyllus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
