Arrabidaea lauta

Arrabidaea lauta, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with oval to elliptical leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and it is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally for various purposes.
