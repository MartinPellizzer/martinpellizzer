Anthemis emiliae

Anthemis emiliae, commonly known as Emilia's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its yellow-orange flower heads bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
