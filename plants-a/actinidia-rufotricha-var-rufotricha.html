Actinidia rufotricha var. rufotricha

Actinidia rufotricha var. rufotricha, commonly known as the red-hairy actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
