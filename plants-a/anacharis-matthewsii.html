Anacharis matthewsii

Anacharis matthewsii, commonly known as the Matthews' waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems that often form dense underwater mats. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, arranged spirally along the stem.

Anacharis matthewsii is adapted to slow-moving or still freshwater environments and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
