Astragalus densifolius subsp. amasiensis

Astragalus densifolius subsp.

amasiensis, commonly known as the Amasian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various insects and animals.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
