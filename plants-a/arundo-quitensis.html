Arundo quitensis

Arundo quitensis, commonly known as the Andean reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the high Andes regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hairs. This plant thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and is often found in wetlands or near streams. Its fibrous stalks have been traditionally used by indigenous communities for thatching and weaving.

Arundo quitensis plays a significant ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
