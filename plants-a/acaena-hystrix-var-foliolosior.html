Acaena hystrix var. foliolosior

Acaena hystrix var. foliolosior, commonly known as the hairy acaena, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit and small, leathery leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces bright orange to red berries that are an important food source for native birds. It thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often forming dense thickets.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and conservation efforts.
