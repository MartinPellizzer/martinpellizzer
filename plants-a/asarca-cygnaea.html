Asarca cygnaea

Asarca cygnaea, commonly known as the white root or white wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its roots are fibrous and can be harvested for medicinal or culinary use.
