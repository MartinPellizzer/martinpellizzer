Acer glabrum subsp. douglasii

Acer glabrum subsp.

douglasii, commonly known as the Douglas maple, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
