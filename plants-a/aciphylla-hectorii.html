Aciphylla hectorii

Aciphylla hectorii, commonly known as Hector's pincushion, is a species of flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage that forms a cushion-like structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
