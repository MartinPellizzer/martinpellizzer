Acacia dissona var. indoloria

Acacia dissona var. indoloria, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and its distinctive, feathery foliage composed of small, linear phyllodes. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its seed pods are woody, elongated, and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and poor soil conditions.
