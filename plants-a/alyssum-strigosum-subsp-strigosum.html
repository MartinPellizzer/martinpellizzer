Alyssum strigosum subsp. strigosum

Alyssum strigosum subsp.

strigosum, commonly known as the hairy alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces narrow, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Alyssum strigosum subsp.

strigosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
