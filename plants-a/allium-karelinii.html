Allium karelinii

Allium karelinii, commonly known as the Armenian chive, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in traditional medicine and cooking. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium karelinii is valued for its aromatic foliage and ornamental appeal in gardens.
