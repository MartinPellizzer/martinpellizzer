Anthoxanthum crinitum

Anthoxanthum crinitum, commonly known as the silky brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs on the leaf blades and stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which are arranged in a loose, open structure.

Anthoxanthum crinitum is often used in forage and erosion control due to its hardiness and adaptability.
