Anacamptis pyramidalis subsp. durandii

Anacamptis pyramidalis subsp.

durandii, commonly known as the Durand's pyramidal orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and showy, violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of flowers, often with a distinct color variation along the spike.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
