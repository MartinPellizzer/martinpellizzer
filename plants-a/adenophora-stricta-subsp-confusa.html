Adenophora stricta subsp. confusa

Adenophora stricta subsp.

confusa, commonly known as the narrowleaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America. It typically grows in moist, woodlands and stream banks, reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces blue to purple bell-shaped flowers in late summer. Its flowering stems are often branched and bear clusters of small, showy blooms.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaves and more compact growth habit compared to other Adenophora species.
