Acacia bahiensis

Acacia bahiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod, which splits open to release the seeds.

Acacia bahiensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in tropical and subtropical regions.
