Anil linifolia var. normalis

Anil linifolia var. normalis, commonly known as the common anil, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with slender, woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the plant is known for its aromatic properties.

It is commonly used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
