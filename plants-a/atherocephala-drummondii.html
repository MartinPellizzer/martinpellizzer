Atherocephala drummondii

Atherocephala drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
