Astragalus luteiflorus

Astragalus luteiflorus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

Astragalus luteiflorus is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various pollinators.
