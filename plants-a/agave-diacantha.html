Agave diacantha

Agave diacantha, commonly known as the spider plant or snake plant, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with white or grayish markings. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear clusters of small, white flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade.

Agave diacantha is also known for its ability to propagate through offsets, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
