Amsonia peeblesii

Amsonia peeblesii, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial species that produces showy, blue-gray flower clusters in late summer to early fall. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in moist to wet soils along stream banks and in woodland areas.

Amsonia peeblesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
