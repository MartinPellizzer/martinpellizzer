Astragalus parnassi subsp. calabricus

Astragalus parnassi subsp.

calabricus, commonly known as the Parnassian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
