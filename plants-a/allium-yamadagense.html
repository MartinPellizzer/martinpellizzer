Allium yamadagense

Allium yamadagense, commonly known as Yamadagense garlic, is a bulbous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its small, white, spherical bulbs. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It is often cultivated for its aromatic bulbs, which are used in traditional Japanese cooking.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
