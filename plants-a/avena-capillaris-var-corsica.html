Avena capillaris var. corsica

Avena capillaris var. corsica, commonly known as Corsican oat, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental or for erosion control.
