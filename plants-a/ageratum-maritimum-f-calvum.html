Ageratum maritimum f. calvum

Ageratum maritimum f. calvum, commonly known as the coastal ageratum, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in sandy or rocky coastal habitats. Its foliage is generally grayish-green with a rough, hairy texture, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in coastal gardening.
