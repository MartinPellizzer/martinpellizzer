Acacia × emoryana

Acacia × emoryana, commonly known as the honey mesquite, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically reduced to spines in mature plants. The plant produces yellow, spherical flower heads that attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods, which are adapted for dispersal by animals and wind.

Acacia × emoryana is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common component of desert ecosystems.
