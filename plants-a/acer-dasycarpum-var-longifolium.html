Acer dasycarpum var. longifolium

Acer dasycarpum var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are typically 5 to 10 inches long and have a serrated margin. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras that disperse its seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with shallow furrows that become more pronounced as the tree matures.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fall foliage.
