Arundinaria distichophylla

Arundinaria distichophylla, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or giant bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in two rows along the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are often wind-pollinated.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
