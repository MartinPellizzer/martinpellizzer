Asarum heterotropoides

Asarum heterotropoides, commonly known as Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a tuberous root system and heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are hairy underneath. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers in late spring, which are clustered in a spicate inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
