Ajuga chanetii

Ajuga chanetii, commonly known as the European bugleweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, purple or pink flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Ajuga chanetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
