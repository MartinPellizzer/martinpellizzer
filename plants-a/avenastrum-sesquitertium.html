Avenastrum sesquitertium

Avenastrum sesquitertium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this plant thrives in dry, sunny environments such as meadows and rocky soils. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it well adapted to its natural habitat, contributing to its ecological significance.
