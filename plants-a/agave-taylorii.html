Agave taylorii

Agave taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's agave, is a species of agave native to the Sonoran Desert in the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped arrangement of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that can reach up to 10 feet in height, bearing white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are often lined with sharp spines and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Agave taylorii is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
