Acalypha angustifolia var. genuina

Acalypha angustifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by three-lobed, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Its ornamental value is limited, but it is sometimes cultivated for its distinctive foliage.
