Aloe vaombe var. poissonii

Aloe vaombe var. poissonii, commonly known as the Madagascar aloe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its arid environment. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear yellow-orange flowers, adding ornamental value. It is well adapted to full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and horticultural research.
