Afrobrunnichia erecta

Afrobrunnichia erecta, commonly known as the African bracken fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests and along riverbanks. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can spread to form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

Afrobrunnichia erecta plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for various small organisms and serving as a food source for some herbivores.
