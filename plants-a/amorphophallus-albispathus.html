Amorphophallus albispathus

Amorphophallus albispathus, commonly known as the white-veined arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces a large, fleshy tuberous root that is used as a starch source in various cuisines. Its leaves are large, arrow-shaped, and often have a white or pale green vein pattern.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appearance and its edible tuber.
