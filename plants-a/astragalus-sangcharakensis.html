Astragalus sangcharakensis

Astragalus sangcharakensis, commonly known as the Karakoram milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its seed pods are typically hairy and elongated. It is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Astragalus sangcharakensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
