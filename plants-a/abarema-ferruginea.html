Abarema ferruginea

Abarema ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty abarema, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Abarema ferruginea is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested or disturbed areas.
