Abies pinsapo subsp. numidica

Abies pinsapo subsp.

numidica, commonly known as the Atlas cedar, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the Atlas Mountains of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves.

The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to rocky, mountainous terrain.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species.
