Agastache pringlei

Agastache pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's hyssop, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. Its showy, tubular flowers range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Agastache pringlei is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
