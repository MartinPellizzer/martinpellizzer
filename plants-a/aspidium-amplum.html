Aspidium amplum

Aspidium amplum, commonly known as the broad holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, and often lobed fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Aspidium amplum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its lush, evergreen foliage.
