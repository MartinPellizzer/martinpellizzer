Amomum vitellinum

Amomum vitellinum, commonly known as the yellow cardamom, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces spadices, which are inflorescences that contain the aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its rhizomes are also utilized in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its valuable essential oils and spices.
