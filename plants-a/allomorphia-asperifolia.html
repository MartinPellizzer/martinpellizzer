Allomorphia asperifolia

Allomorphia asperifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf allomorph, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
