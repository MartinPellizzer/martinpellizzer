Athyrium multilum

Athyrium multilum, commonly known as the Japanese male fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Athyrium multilum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
