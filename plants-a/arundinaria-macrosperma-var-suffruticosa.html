Arundinaria macrosperma var. suffruticosa

Arundinaria macrosperma var. suffruticosa, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a dense, bushy appearance due to its branching pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and has narrow, linear leaves that are typically a deep green color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique growth habit and resilience.
