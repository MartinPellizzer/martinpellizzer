Abrotanum viridulum

Abrotanum viridulum, commonly known as greenweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in the summer, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

The leaves are aromatic and have been used historically in traditional medicine.
