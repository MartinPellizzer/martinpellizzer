Asarum fauriei var. nakaianum

Asarum fauriei var. nakaianum, commonly known as the Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, reddish-brown leaves with a distinctive, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, dark purple flowers clustered in a spadix, enclosed by a leaf-like bract. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Aristolochiaceae family.
