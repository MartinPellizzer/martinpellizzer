Astragalus wensuensis

Astragalus wensuensis, commonly known as Wensu milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
