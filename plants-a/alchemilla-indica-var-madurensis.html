Alchemilla indica var. madurensis

Alchemilla indica var. madurensis, commonly known as the Madurese lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of soft, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
