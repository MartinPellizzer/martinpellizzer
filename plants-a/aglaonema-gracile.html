Aglaonema gracile

Aglaonema gracile, commonly known as the silver queen plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, variegated foliage with bright white and green patterns on its lance-shaped leaves. The plant has a compact growth habit and typically reaches a height of about 1 to 2 feet. It thrives in low to moderate light conditions and prefers well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a decorative plant in shaded outdoor spaces.
