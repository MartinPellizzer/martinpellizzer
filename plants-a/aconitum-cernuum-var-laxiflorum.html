Aconitum cernuum var. laxiflorum

Aconitum cernuum var. laxiflorum, commonly known as the laxiflorum monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
