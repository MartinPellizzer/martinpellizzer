Alcicornium hillii

Alcicornium hillii, commonly known as the silverleaf spirea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
