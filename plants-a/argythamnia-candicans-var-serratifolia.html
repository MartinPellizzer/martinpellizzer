Argythamnia candicans var. serratifolia

Argythamnia candicans var. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated sandthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have serrated margins, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
