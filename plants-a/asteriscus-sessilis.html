Asteriscus sessilis

Asteriscus sessilis, commonly known as the sessile asterisk, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and sessile, meaning they lack stalks, and its flowers are often pale yellow to white in color.

Asteriscus sessilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
