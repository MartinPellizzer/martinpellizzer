Aspalathus retroflexa subsp. angustipetala

Aspalathus retroflexa subsp.

angustipetala, commonly known as the narrow-petalled silver bush, is a small shrub native to South Africa. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for native gardens.
