Alopecurus borealis subsp. glaucus

Alopecurus borealis subsp.

glaucus, commonly known as the blue meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, temperate habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, blue-green blades that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
