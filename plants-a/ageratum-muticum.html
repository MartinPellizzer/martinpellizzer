Ageratum muticum

Ageratum muticum, commonly known as the common ageratum, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, showy flowers in shades of white, purple, and blue. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

Ageratum muticum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like butterflies and bees.
