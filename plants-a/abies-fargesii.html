Abies fargesii

Abies fargesii, commonly known as the Farges fir, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dense foliage, and needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in spirals. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

The Farges fir is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
