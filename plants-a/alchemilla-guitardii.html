Alchemilla guitardii

Alchemilla guitardii, commonly known as the Alpes-de-Haute-Provence lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern France.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Alchemilla guitardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and subtle floral display.
