Actinidia lanceolata

Actinidia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in early summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
