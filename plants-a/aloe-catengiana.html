Aloe catengiana

Aloe catengiana, commonly known as the East African aloe, is a succulent plant native to the highlands of East Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a white powdery substance called farina. The plant produces tall, branched stems with clusters of yellow flowers during the rainy season. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is used in traditional medicine and skincare products.

Aloe catengiana is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
