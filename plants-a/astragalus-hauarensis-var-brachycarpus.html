Astragalus hauarensis var. brachycarpus

Astragalus hauarensis var. brachycarpus, commonly known as the short-carpel milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its small, bean-like pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variant is distinguished by its shorter carpels compared to other varieties within the species.
