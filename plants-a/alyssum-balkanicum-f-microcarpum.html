Alyssum balkanicum f. microcarpum

Alyssum balkanicum f. microcarpum, commonly known as the Balkan Alyssum, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and serrated leaves. The subspecies microcarpum is distinguished by its smaller, more compact flowers compared to the typical form. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
