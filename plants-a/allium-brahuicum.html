Allium brahuicum

Allium brahuicum, commonly known as the Brahui onion, is a bulbous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild onion-like flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Allium brahuicum is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in challenging climates.
