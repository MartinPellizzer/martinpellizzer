Acacia galioides var. glabriflora

Acacia galioides var. glabriflora, commonly known as the smooth-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, feathery leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.
