Acanthus rubens

Acanthus rubens, commonly known as the red hakea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces woody, branched stems. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and unique foliage.

Acanthus rubens prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.
