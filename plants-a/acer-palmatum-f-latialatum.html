Acer palmatum f. latialatum

Acer palmatum f. latialatum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with broad leaves, is a variant of the Japanese maple characterized by its distinctive, wide-lobed foliage.

This cultivar exhibits a striking contrast between its vibrant green summer leaves and fiery red or orange autumn colors. It typically grows as a compact, upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. The leaves are palmately lobed, with a delicate, maple-like appearance that adds ornamental value to gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
