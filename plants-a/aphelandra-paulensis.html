Aphelandra paulensis

Aphelandra paulensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame plant, is a striking tropical plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Aroid family (Araceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, pendulous inflorescences that resemble flames. The plant features large, glossy, dark green lance-shaped leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is a modified leaf.

Aphelandra paulensis thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic value and relatively easy care requirements.
