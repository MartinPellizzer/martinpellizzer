Astephanus badius

Astephanus badius, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright orange to red flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Astephanus badius is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
