Aeonium × nogalesii nothosubsp.

dasyphyllum, commonly known as the Mexican rose. It is a succulent plant native to Mexico, characterized by its rosette-forming structure and vibrant, fleshy leaves.

The leaves are typically green with a reddish or pinkish hue, especially when exposed to sunlight. It produces showy, yellow flowers in spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens and arid climates due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
