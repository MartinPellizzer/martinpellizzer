Acanthocalycium thionanthum

Acanthocalycium thionanthum, commonly known as the ghost cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are often covered in a layer of white, waxy bloom. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It is a slow-growing, succulent plant that stores water in its stems to survive in arid conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in desert gardens.
