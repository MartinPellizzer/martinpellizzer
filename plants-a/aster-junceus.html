Aster junceus

Aster junceus, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy, daisy-like flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing blue to purple petals.

Aster junceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
