Achillea formosa subsp. formosa

Achillea formosa subsp.

formosa, commonly known as showy yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features basal rosettes of gray-green, lance-shaped leaves and produces flat, composite flower heads in shades of white, pink, or red.

The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and rocky habitats.

Its botanical structure supports its role in both ecological and ornamental horticulture.
