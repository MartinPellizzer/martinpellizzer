Amorphophallus curvistylis

Amorphophallus curvistylis, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces a striking, umbrella-shaped inflorescence with a single, spadix-like flower that is often white or greenish in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to regenerate from its underground tuberous stem.
