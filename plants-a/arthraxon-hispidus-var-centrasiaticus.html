Arthraxon hispidus var. centrasiaticus

Arthraxon hispidus var. centrasiaticus, commonly known as the Central Asian grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-lobed spikelets.

This species is notable for its resilience to arid conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
