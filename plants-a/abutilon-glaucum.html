Abutilon glaucum

Abutilon glaucum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green foliage and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its hairy leaves and branched stems contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Abutilon glaucum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
