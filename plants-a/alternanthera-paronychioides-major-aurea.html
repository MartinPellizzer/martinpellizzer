Alternanthera paronychioides major-aurea

Alternanthera paronychioides major-aurea, commonly known as the golden Alternanthera, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant golden-orange foliage, which becomes more pronounced under strong light conditions. The plant features slender, branching stems that can grow up to 30 cm in length, making it suitable for mid to background placement in aquariums. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture, enhancing its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moderate to high light and requires regular fertilization to maintain its intense coloration.
