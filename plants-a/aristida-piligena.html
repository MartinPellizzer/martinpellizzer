Aristida piligena

Aristida piligena, commonly known as the slender threeawn, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and small, spiky inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is a perennial species that thrives in arid to semi-arid environments.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in degraded areas.
