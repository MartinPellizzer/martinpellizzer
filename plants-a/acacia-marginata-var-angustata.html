Acacia marginata var. angustata

Acacia marginata var. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.

The wood is lightweight and has been historically used by Indigenous Australians for various purposes.
