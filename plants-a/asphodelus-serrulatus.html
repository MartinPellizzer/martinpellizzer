Asphodelus serrulatus

Asphodelus serrulatus, commonly known as the sword asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of white or pale yellow flowers in the spring. Its botanical name reflects its serrated leaf margins and its classification within the Asphodelaceae family.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its resilient nature and attractive floral display.
