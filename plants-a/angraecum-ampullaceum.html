Angraecum ampullaceum

Angraecum ampullaceum, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It features long, slender stems and large, showy flowers with a distinctive spurred shape. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous fragrant, white to pale pink blossoms. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Angraecum ampullaceum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
