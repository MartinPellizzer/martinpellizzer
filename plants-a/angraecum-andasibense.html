Angraecum andasibense

Angraecum andasibense, commonly known as the Andasibense star orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to Madagascar.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy flowers with white petals and a distinctive violet lip. The plant typically blooms during the dry season, producing fragrant flowers that attract specific pollinators like moths. Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest in botanical research.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
