Anil leprieurii

Anil leprieurii, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent, pinkish-white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant has a succulent stem and produces small, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its arid environment. Its flowers bloom during the dry season, typically in the evening, and are pollinated by specific insects.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, Anil leprieurii is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
