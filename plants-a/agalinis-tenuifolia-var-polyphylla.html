Agalinis tenuifolia var. polyphylla

Agalinis tenuifolia var. polyphylla, commonly known as the slenderleaf false foxglove, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
