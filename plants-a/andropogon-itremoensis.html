Andropogon itremoensis

Andropogon itremoensis, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its unique ecological role and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
