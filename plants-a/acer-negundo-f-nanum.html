Acer negundo f. nanum

Acer negundo f. nanum, commonly known as the Chinese box elder, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, palmately compound leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by winged fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
