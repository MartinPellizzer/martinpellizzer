Achlys triphylla var. japonica

Achlys triphylla var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese ghost plant, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with small, waxy, green to bluish leaves. The plant produces tiny white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in wet conditions make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
