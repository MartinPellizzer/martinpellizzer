Astragalus pischtovensis

Astragalus pischtovensis, commonly known as the Pischtov's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, lens-shaped seeds that are typically enclosed in a hairy pod. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

Astragalus pischtovensis is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
