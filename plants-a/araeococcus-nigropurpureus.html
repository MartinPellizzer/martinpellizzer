Araeococcus nigropurpureus

Araeococcus nigropurpureus, commonly known as the purple-veined rock moss, is a rare and delicate liverwort species.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found on rocky substrates in temperate regions. The plant features small, closely packed leaves with distinctive purple veins, giving it its common name. Its reproductive structures are inconspicuous, relying on spores for propagation.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a sensitive indicator of environmental health.
