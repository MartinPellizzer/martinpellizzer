Abarema leucophylla

Abarema leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often compound leaves. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are typically white or pale green, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits.

Abarema leucophylla is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
