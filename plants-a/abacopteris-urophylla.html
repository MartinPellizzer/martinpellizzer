Abacopteris urophylla

Abacopteris urophylla, commonly known as the red hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and bright red floral spikes that appear in spring. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation. Its flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly honeyeaters, which are attracted to its nectar.

The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.
