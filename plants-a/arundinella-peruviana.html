Arundinella peruviana

Arundinella peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian bamboo, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps, making it a common sight in moist, tropical environments.

Arundinella peruviana is also valued for its lightweight, fibrous stalks, which have been used traditionally in various applications.
