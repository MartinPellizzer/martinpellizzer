Ambelania quadrangularis

Ambelania quadrangularis, commonly known as the four-angled morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist woodland areas.

Ambelania quadrangularis is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine.
