Ancistrocarya

Ancistrocarya, commonly known as the pecan.

It is a genus of flowering plants in the family Anacardiaceae, native to the southern United States and northern Mexico. The most well-known species is Ancistrocarya glabriuscula, which produces the edible pecan nut. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

Its fruit is a drupe that develops into a hard-shelled nut containing a single seed.
