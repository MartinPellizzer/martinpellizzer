Aristida subspicata

Aristida subspicata, commonly known as slender dropseed, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities and a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
