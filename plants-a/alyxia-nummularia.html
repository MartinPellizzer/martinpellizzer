Alyxia nummularia

Alyxia nummularia, commonly known as the silverleaf alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation in appropriate climates.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice for native plant landscaping.
