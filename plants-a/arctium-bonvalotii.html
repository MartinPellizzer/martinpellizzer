Arctium bonvalotii

Arctium bonvalotii, commonly known as the Bonvalot's burdock, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple to pink flowers that are clustered in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. Its most distinctive feature is the spiny, bur-like fruit that aids in seed dispersal by attaching to animals or clothing.

Arctium bonvalotii is often found in dry, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of temperate climates.
