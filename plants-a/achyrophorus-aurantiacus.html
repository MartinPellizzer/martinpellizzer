Achyrophorus aurantiacus

Achyrophorus aurantiacus, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters along its branching stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
