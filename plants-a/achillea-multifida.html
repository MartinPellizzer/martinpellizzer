Achillea multifida

Achillea multifida, commonly known as yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features feathery, finely divided leaves and flat-topped clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which contains compounds such as achilleine and volatile oils. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, also known as the daisy or sunflower family.
