Acer mono var. paxii

Acer mono var. paxii, commonly known as the Pacific maple, is a variant of the Pacific silver fir.

It is a deciduous tree native to the western United States, particularly in California and Oregon. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
