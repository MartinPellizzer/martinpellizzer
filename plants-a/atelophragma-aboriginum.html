Atelophragma aboriginum

Atelophragma aboriginum, commonly known as the Australian native orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Australia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, hairy stem and produces small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its leaves are linear and often clustered at the base, and it has a fibrous root system.

This species is notable for its unique floral structure and is adapted to the specific ecological conditions of its habitat.
