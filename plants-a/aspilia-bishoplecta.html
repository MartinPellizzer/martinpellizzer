Aspilia bishoplecta

Aspilia bishoplecta, commonly known as the Bishop's lectern plant, is a flowering plant native to the island of Saint Helena in the South Atlantic Ocean.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale blue ray florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
