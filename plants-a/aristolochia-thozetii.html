Aristolochia thozetii

Aristolochia thozetii, commonly known as the Madagascar Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive, hollow, pipe-like flower that is often curved. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex and intricate floral structure. Its flowers are typically greenish or brownish, with a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies.
