Anagyris nepalensis

Anagyris nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal burr or Himalayan burr, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in racemes.

The pods are woody, hairy, and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
