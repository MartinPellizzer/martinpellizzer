Anthoxanthum odoratum subsp. godronii

Anthoxanthum odoratum subsp.

godronii, commonly known as the Godron's sweet grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the flowers are pale yellow to greenish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal practices.
