Anemone obtusiloba subsp. micrantha

Anemone obtusiloba subsp.

micrantha, commonly known as the Sierra anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features basal leaves that are broad and heart-shaped, with a hairy surface. Its flowers are showy, with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in early spring.

It is characterized by its short, sturdy stems and is often found in montane and subalpine regions.
