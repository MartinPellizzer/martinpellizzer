Achillea pindicola subsp. pindicola

Achillea pindicola subsp.

pindicola, commonly known as the Pindus yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Greece. It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in full sun conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

This subspecies is noted for its hardiness and ability to adapt to harsh alpine environments.
