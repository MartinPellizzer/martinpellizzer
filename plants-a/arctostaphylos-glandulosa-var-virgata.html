Arctostaphylos glandulosa var. virgata

Arctostaphylos glandulosa var. virgata, commonly known as the Sierra foothill manzanita, is a shrub native to California.

It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. The plant features small, oval leaves with glandular dots, which help reduce water loss. Its flowers are pink or red, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, edible berries.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
