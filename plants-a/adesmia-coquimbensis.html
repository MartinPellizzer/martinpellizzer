Adesmia coquimbensis

Adesmia coquimbensis, commonly known as the Coquimbo burro's tail, is a flowering plant native to Chile.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant is shrub-like, with fleshy, succulent stems and small, clustered leaves. It produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

Adesmia coquimbensis is adapted to arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
