Acacia × barteriana

Acacia × barteriana, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Acacia longifolia and Acacia dealbata.

It is characterized by its distinctive drooping branches that give it a graceful, tree-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in spherical inflorescences, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, feathery leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils in temperate climates.

Acacia × barteriana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
