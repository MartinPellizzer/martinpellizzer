Allium karamanoglui

Allium karamanoglui, commonly known as the Karamanoglu garlic, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to Turkey and is characterized by its bulbous stem and clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
