Androcymbium fenestratum

Androcymbium fenestratum, commonly known as the fenestrated androcymbium, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, ornate leaves with distinctive fenestrations, or leaf openings, which give it its common name. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically a deep shade of purple or violet. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species in its native range.
