Adiantum croweanum

Adiantum croweanum, commonly known as the Crowe's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble the wings of a bird. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky habitats. Its leaves are usually bright green and have a distinctive, graceful appearance.

Adiantum croweanum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
