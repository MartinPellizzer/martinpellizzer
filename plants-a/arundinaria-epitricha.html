Arundinaria epitricha

Arundinaria epitricha, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a smooth surface and a prominent midrib.

Arundinaria epitricha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
