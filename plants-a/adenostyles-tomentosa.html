Adenostyles tomentosa

Adenostyles tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, such as slopes and woodlands. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is adapted to dry environments and is often found in regions with Mediterranean climates.
