Adiantum sublobatum

Adiantum sublobatum, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically silvery-white in color. The plant has pinnate leaves with lanceolate pinnae that are usually finely toothed along the edges. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Adiantum sublobatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
