Astragalus aktiubensis

Astragalus aktiubensis, commonly known as the Aktiubinsk milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. It is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus aktiubensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and may have potential uses in traditional medicine.
