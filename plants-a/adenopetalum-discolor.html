Adenopetalum discolor

Adenopetalum discolor, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.

The plant's unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
