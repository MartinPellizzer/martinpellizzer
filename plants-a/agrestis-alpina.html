Agrestis alpina

Agrestis alpina, commonly known as the alpine grass or mountain grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions.

It typically grows in rocky or open habitats at high elevations, where it forms dense, low-growing mats. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny flowers. It is adapted to cold climates and can survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Agrestis alpina plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for alpine wildlife.
