Arabis drummondii var. interposita

Arabis drummondii var. interposita, commonly known as the Drummond's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features basal leaves that are oval to spatulate in shape, with a hairy surface. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
