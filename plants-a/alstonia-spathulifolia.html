Alstonia spathulifolia

Alstonia spathulifolia, commonly known as the Madagascar tree or the Indian tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia and Madagascar.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in medicine.

Alstonia spathulifolia is also valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
