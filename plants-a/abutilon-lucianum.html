Abutilon lucianum

Abutilon lucianum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Abutilon lucianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
