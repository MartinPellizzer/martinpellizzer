Aconitum tauricum

Aconitum tauricum, commonly known as the Caucasian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
