Attalea luetzelburgii

Attalea luetzelburgii, commonly known as the Luetzelburg's palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a tall, slender palm with a smooth, gray trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, round fruits containing edible seeds. It is often found in humid, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential uses in traditional medicine.

The species plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
