Ancistrodesmus oleifolius

Ancistrodesmus oleifolius, commonly known as the oil-leaved water clover, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its finely divided, floating leaves that resemble a clover leaf.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it can receive ample sunlight. The leaves are coated with a waxy layer that helps reduce water loss and provides protection against herbivores. Its small, green flowers are typically borne on slender stalks and bloom in the summer months.

This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
