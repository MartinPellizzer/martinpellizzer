Acacia xanthocarpa

Acacia xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow to golden flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. Its seeds are encased in woody pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
