Apostasia odorata

Apostasia odorata, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland areas in Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces a pleasant, spicy fragrance, which contributes to its common name. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Apostasia odorata is valued both for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
