Artemisia sipontina

Artemisia sipontina, commonly known as the Sipontine wormwood, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It has a strong, distinctive scent due to its high concentration of volatile oils.

Artemisia sipontina is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
