Asystasia hirsuta

Asystasia hirsuta, commonly known as hairy asystasia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and also hairy, contributing to its overall textured look. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of growing conditions.
