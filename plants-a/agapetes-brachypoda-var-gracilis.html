Agapetes brachypoda var. gracilis

Agapetes brachypoda var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender silverleaf, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically silver-gray in color, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom in late spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous areas with cool, humid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and specialty plant collections.
