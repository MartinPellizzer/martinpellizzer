Acacia coriacea subsp. pendens

Acacia coriacea subsp.

pendens, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive pendulous branches that give it a drooping appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow globular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of true leaves.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
