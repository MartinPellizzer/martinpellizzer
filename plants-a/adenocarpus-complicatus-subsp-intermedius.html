Adenocarpus complicatus subsp. intermedius

Adenocarpus complicatus subsp.

intermedius, commonly known as the African burr bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by distinctive, spiny, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Due to its robust growth and adaptability, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
