Azalea punctata

Azalea punctata, commonly known as the spotted azalea, is a species of flowering plant in the Rhododendron genus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of China, India, and Myanmar. The plant is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive white or pale throat. Its leaves are typically elliptical and glossy, and the plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

Azalea punctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
