Adicea rufa

Adicea rufa, commonly known as the redstem ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features upright stems that are reddish-brown in color, which give it its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Adicea rufa produces small, purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
