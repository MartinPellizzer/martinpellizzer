Adiantum tenuifolium

Adiantum tenuifolium, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is characterized by its finely divided, lace-like fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-draining, humus-rich soil. Its fronds are bi-lobed and often curl at the tips, contributing to its ornamental value.

Adiantum tenuifolium is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in controlled conditions.
