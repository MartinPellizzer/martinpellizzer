Aira caryophyllea subsp. multiculmis

Aira caryophyllea subsp.

multiculmis, commonly known as the purple false oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear purple to reddish bracts.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-gray sheen.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
