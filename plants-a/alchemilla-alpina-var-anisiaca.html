Alchemilla alpina var. anisiaca

Alchemilla alpina var. anisiaca, commonly known as the Alpine Lady's Mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, velvety, shield-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
