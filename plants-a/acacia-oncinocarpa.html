Acacia oncinocarpa

Acacia oncinocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Acacia oncinocarpa is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
