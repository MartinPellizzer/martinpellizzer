Asparagus lynetteae

Asparagus lynetteae, commonly known as the Lynette's asparagus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, grass-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
