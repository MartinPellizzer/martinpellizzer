Amelanchier melanocarpa

Amelanchier melanocarpa, commonly known as the black chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Amelanchier melanocarpa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for birds and wildlife, as well as supporting pollinators.
