Allium forrestii

Allium forrestii, commonly known as the Forrest's onion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spherical bulbs that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, blooming in late summer to early autumn.

Allium forrestii is valued for its medicinal properties and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
