Amsonia amsonia

Amsonia amsonia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue-gray foliage that turns golden in the fall, and its most distinctive characteristic is its fluffy, blue-gray flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are pollinated by insects, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Amsonia amsonia is drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
