Abrus schimperi subsp. africanus

Abrus schimperi subsp.

africanus, commonly known as the African jequirity, is a climbing legume native to Africa. It produces distinctive red, ornate pods that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional crafts and as a dye.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often found in forested areas. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its toxic properties, handling the seeds requires caution.
