Athyriopsis × lobatocrenata

Athyriopsis × lobatocrenata, commonly known as the hybrid fern, is a sterile hybrid species resulting from the cross between Athyriopsis filix-femina and Athyriopsis crenata.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in temperate forests or woodland areas. Its sporangia are clustered in sori along the margins of the fronds, a typical feature of the Dryopteridaceae family.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
