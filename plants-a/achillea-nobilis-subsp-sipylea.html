Achillea nobilis subsp. sipylea

Achillea nobilis subsp.

sipylea, commonly known as the Sipylean yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and Syria. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.

Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, contributing to its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
