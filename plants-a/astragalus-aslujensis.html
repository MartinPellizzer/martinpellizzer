Astragalus aslujensis

Astragalus aslujensis, commonly known as the Aslujan milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Turkey, particularly in the Taurus Mountains. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are pinnate, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus aslujensis is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
