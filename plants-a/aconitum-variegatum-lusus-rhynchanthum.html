Aconitum variegatum lusus rhynchanthum

Aconitum variegatum lusus rhynchanthum, commonly known as the variegated aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has dark green, deeply lobed leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic nature, it should be handled with care.
