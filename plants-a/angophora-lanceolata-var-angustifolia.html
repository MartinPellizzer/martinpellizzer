Angophora lanceolata var. angustifolia

Angophora lanceolata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved angophora, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, narrow leaves that are glossy and often have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, cylindrical fruit capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry rainforest habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
