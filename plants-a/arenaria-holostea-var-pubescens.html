Arenaria holostea var. pubescens

Arenaria holostea var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance and aiding in water retention.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in dry landscapes.
