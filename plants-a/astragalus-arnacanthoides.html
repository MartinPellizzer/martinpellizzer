Astragalus arnacanthoides

Astragalus arnacanthoides, commonly known as the spiked milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Astragalus arnacanthoides is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
