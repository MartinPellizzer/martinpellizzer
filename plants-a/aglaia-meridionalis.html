Aglaia meridionalis

Aglaia meridionalis, commonly known as the southern aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and related hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
