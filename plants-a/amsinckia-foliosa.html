Amsinckia foliosa

Amsinckia foliosa, commonly known as the leafy poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lobed leaves and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of pink to red. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Amsinckia foliosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and vibrant blooms.
