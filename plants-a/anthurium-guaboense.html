Anthurium guaboense

Anthurium guaboense, commonly known as the Guabosan arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant produces a large, heart-shaped leaf with a deep green color and a prominent midrib. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant's rhizomes are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.
