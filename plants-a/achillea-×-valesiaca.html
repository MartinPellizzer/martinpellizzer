Achillea × valesiaca

Achillea × valesiaca, commonly known as the hybrid yarrow, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Achillea millefolium (common yarrow) and Achillea ptarmica (sneezeweed), resulting in a plant with distinctive, feathery foliage. The plant produces small, composite flower heads that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Achillea × valesiaca is often found in meadows and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
