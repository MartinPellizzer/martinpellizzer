Acacia igualensis

Acacia igualensis, commonly known as the Iguazu acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil and Paraguay.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is distinguished by its slender, often thorny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. Its yellow flowers are clustered in spherical inflorescences, and it produces elongated pods containing flat, seed-rich seeds.

Acacia igualensis plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
