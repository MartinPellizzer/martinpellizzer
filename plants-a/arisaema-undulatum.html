Arisaema undulatum

Arisaema undulatum, commonly known as the wavy arisaema, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking, undulating leaves that are often variegated with white and green. Its flowers are typically yellow or green, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Arisaema undulatum is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
