Argemone mexicana var. rosea

Argemone mexicana var. rosea, commonly known as the rosea variant of the Mexican prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to reddish-purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsules that contain seeds with a high oil content. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
