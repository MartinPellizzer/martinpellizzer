Acacia hystrix subsp. hystrix

Acacia hystrix subsp.

hystrix, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, linear leaflets.

The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are typical of the legume family, Fabaceae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments. Acacia hystrix subsp.

hystrix is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
