Argylia conaiensis

Argylia conaiensis, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette, which helps it retain moisture.

Argylia conaiensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
