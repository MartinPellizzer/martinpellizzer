Aster cassiarabicus

Aster cassiarabicus, commonly known as the Cassiar aster, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas or along stream banks. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Aster cassiarabicus is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
