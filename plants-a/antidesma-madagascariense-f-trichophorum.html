Antidesma madagascariense f. trichophorum

Antidesma madagascariense f. trichophorum, commonly known as the hairy antidesma, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its hairy foliage and unique fruit make it distinct within its genus.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native environment.
