Aegilops aristata

Aegilops aristata, commonly known as the slender wild rye, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with bristle-tipped florets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. Aegilops aristata is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Morphology

Aegilops aristata has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blade-shaped, linear, and slightly pointed, arranged alternately along the stem.

The stem is upright, cylindrical, and smooth, with a light green color, growing in a clump or loose tuft. Its flowers are small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets, blooming in late spring to early summer, and are wind-pollinated. The fruit is a small, dry, three-lobed caryopsis, with seeds dispersed by wind due to their light weight and hairy appendages.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, helping the plant anchor in sandy or disturbed soils.
