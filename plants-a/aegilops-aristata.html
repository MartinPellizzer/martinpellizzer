Aegilops aristata

Aegilops aristata, commonly known as the slender wild rye, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with bristle-tipped florets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. Aegilops aristata is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
