Acalypha languida

Acalypha languida, commonly known as the softleaf ironweed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often strikingly ornamental species. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically soft and hairy, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered in yellowish to greenish cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Acalypha languida is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil with partial to full sun.
