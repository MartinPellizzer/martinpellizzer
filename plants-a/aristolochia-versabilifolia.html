Aristolochia versabilifolia

Aristolochia versabilifolia, commonly known as the variable-leaf arrowhead vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that vary in size and shape, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces unique, horseshoe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a decorative plant.
