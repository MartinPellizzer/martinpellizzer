Acer palmatum f. nicholsonii

Acer palmatum f. nicholsonii, commonly known as the Japanese maple variegated form, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which typically features a mix of green, white, and pink hues. The plant is native to Japan and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.

Its leaves turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.
