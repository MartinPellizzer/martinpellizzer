Agave nuusaviorum

Agave nuusaviorum, commonly known as the Blue Agave, is a species of succulent plant native to the Mexican states of Jalisco and Nayarit.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that can reach up to 10 meters in height, bearing small, white flowers. It is a slow-growing plant that can live for up to 10 years before flowering.

The Blue Agave is widely cultivated for its sap, which is used to produce tequila and other alcoholic beverages.
