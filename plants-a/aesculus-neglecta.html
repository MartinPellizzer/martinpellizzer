Aesculus neglecta

Aesculus neglecta, commonly known as the Chinese horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves and showy white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces large, spiny fruits that contain one or two seeds, which are toxic if ingested.

It is often grown for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
