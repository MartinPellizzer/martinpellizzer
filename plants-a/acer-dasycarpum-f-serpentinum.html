Acer dasycarpum f. serpentinum

Acer dasycarpum f. serpentinum, commonly known as the serpentine maple, is a unique variant of the Chinese maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that resemble serpents, hence its common name. The plant is adapted to grow in nutrient-poor, calcareous soils, particularly on serpentine rock formations. Its leaves are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a serrated edge.

This ornamental tree is valued for its striking foliage and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
