Adelia membranifolia

Adelia membranifolia, commonly known as the membrane-leaf adelia, is a unique plant species characterized by its delicate, thin, and papery leaves.

Native to specific regions in South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
