Allium antalyense

Allium antalyense, commonly known as the Antalya garlic, is a bulbous plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are used in local cuisines, similar to other allium species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
