Agave virginica var. polyanthus

Agave virginica var. polyanthus, commonly known as the panhandle agave, is a succulent plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with white variegation. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in USDA hardiness zones 7 through 10.
