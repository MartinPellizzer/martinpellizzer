Acacia heterochroa

Acacia heterochroa, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and compound leaves that are typically bipinnate. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Acacia heterochroa is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
