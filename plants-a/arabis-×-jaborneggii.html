Arabis × jaborneggii

Arabis × jaborneggii, commonly known as the hybrid rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Arabis caucasica and Arabis alpina, exhibiting traits from both parent species. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring.

Its foliage consists of basal rosettes of linear, hairy leaves, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
