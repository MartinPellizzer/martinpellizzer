Alvardia

Alvardia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. Alvardia thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in various parts of the world.
