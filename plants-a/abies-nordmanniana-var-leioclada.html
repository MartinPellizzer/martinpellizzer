Abies nordmanniana var. leioclada

Abies nordmanniana var. leioclada, commonly known as the North Caucasian fir, is a rare and distinctive variant of the Nordmann fir.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. The plant is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are slightly curved. Its cones are typically reddish-brown and hang downward, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
