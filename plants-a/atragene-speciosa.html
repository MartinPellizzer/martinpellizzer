Atragene speciosa

Atragene speciosa, commonly known as the showy atragene, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that display a range of colors from red to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, thriving in full sun conditions. Its leaves are often lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are clustered in dense, spiky structures.

Atragene speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
