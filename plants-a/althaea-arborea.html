Althaea arborea

Althaea arborea, commonly known as the tree mallow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Althaea arborea is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
