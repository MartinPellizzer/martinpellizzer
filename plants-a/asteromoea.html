Asteromoea

Asteromoea, commonly known as the starflower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that typically feature yellow centers and white or pale blue petals. These plants are native to various regions in the Americas and are often found in dry, rocky habitats. Asteromoea species are known for their compact growth habit and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

They are also valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in horticultural gardens.
