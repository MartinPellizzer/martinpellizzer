Achillea alpina subsp. alpina

Achillea alpina subsp.

alpina, commonly known as alpine yarrow, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant features upright, branched stems and basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape. Its flower heads are composed of small, white or pale yellow florets arranged in flat, composite inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
