Arabis lucida

Arabis lucida, commonly known as the showy rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known flowering plants. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. Arabis lucida typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, showy blooms.
