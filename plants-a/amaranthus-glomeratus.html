Amaranthus glomeratus

Amaranthus glomeratus, commonly known as cluster amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, while the flowers are usually greenish-yellow.

Amaranthus glomeratus is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in dry conditions.
