Amyema pruinosa

Amyema pruinosa, commonly known as the white-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the southern regions of Australia.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish, waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are usually clustered together, followed by small, fleshy berries. Amyema pruinosa is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and relies on its host for nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis.

Its unique ecological role makes it an important species in the ecosystems where it is found.
