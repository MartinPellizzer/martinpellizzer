Acer dasycarpum f. sanguineum

Acer dasycarpum f. sanguineum, commonly known as the blood red maple, is a variant of the hairy maple native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

This ornamental tree is characterized by its striking red foliage, which becomes more vibrant in the autumn season. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a rough texture and a deep crimson hue. It produces small, clustered flowers in early spring, followed by distinctive, hairy samaras that aid in seed dispersal.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its seasonal color display.
