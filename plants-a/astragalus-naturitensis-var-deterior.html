Astragalus naturitensis var. deterior

Astragalus naturitensis var. deterior, commonly known as the deteriorated milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.

However, its specific ecological significance and conservation status remain understudied.
