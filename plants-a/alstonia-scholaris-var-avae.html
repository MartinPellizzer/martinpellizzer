Alstonia scholaris var. avae

Alstonia scholaris var. avae, commonly known as the Awa tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with opposite arrangement. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.

The variety avae is distinguished by its unique leaf shape and growth habits compared to the typical form of Alstonia scholaris.
