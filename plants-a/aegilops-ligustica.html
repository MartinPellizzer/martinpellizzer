Aegilops ligustica

Aegilops ligustica, commonly known as the Italian goatgrass, is a wild cereal grass native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, temperate climates.

Aegilops ligustica has been studied for its potential in breeding programs to improve wheat varieties due to its genetic diversity.

Morphology

Aegilops ligustica has leaves that are narrow and linear, arranged alternately along the stem, with a bluish-green color and a hairy surface.

The stem is upright, cylindrical, and slightly hairy, with a grayish-green color, growing in a clump-like pattern. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, blooming in early summer, and are pollinated by wind. The fruit is a three-lobed spikelet that disperses its seeds by wind after drying.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, helping the plant anchor in dry, rocky soils.
