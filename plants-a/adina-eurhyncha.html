Adina eurhyncha

Adina eurhyncha, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and rich color. The tree has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, spherical fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of valuable timber.
