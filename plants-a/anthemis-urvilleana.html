Anthemis urvilleana

Anthemis urvilleana, commonly known as the Urville's feverfew, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, gray-green leaves and produces small yellow flowers with prominent white ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
