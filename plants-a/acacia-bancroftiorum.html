Acacia bancroftiorum

Acacia bancroftiorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, feathery leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Its distinctive appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
