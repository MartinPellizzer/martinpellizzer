Alsine caucasica

Alsine caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and small, greenish flowers. Native to the Caucasus region, it thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in disturbed soils. The plant has succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments.

It is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and unique appearance.
