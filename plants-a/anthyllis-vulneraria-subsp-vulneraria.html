Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. vulneraria

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

vulneraria, commonly known as the kidney vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, compound leaves.

The plant produces yellow or orange flowers in clusters, which are typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Its name, "vulneraria," refers to its traditional use in treating wounds, reflecting its historical medicinal significance.
