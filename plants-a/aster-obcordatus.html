Aster obcordatus

Aster obcordatus, commonly known as the obcordate aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of many small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive obcordate (heart-shaped) base, and its flowers are blue to purple in color.

It is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
