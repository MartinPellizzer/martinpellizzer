Astragalus hypoglottis var. bracteatus

Astragalus hypoglottis var. bracteatus, commonly known as the bracteate milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets, and its root system is typically deep and fibrous, adapted to survive in dry conditions. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinators.
