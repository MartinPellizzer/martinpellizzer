Antirrhinum huetii

Antirrhinum huetii, commonly known as the Huet's snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pink or red, with two lips that resemble a dragon's mouth, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.
