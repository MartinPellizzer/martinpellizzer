Astragalus angustifolius subsp. angustifolius

Astragalus angustifolius subsp.

angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, sagebrush steppe, and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender, branched stems and small, compound leaves with a narrow, linear shape. Its flowers are usually pale to dark purple, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
