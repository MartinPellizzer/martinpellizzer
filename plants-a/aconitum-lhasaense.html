Aconitum lhasaense

Aconitum lhasaense, commonly known as the Lhasa aconite, is a perennial herb native to the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant features a stout, hairy stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough texture and deep lobes.

Aconitum lhasaense thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cold, arid conditions.
