Acer ginnala subsp. euginnala

Acer ginnala subsp.

euginnala, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves that change color throughout the seasons, often displaying vibrant red, orange, and yellow hues in autumn.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
