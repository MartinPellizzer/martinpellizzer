Auriculardisia croatii

Auriculardisia croatii, commonly known as the Croatian auricula, is a rare and unique plant native to the mountainous regions of Croatia.

It is characterized by its distinctive auriculate leaves, which have ear-like lobes at the base, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest areas.
