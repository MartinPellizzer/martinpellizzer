Anemone nemorosa var. fissa

Anemone nemorosa var. fissa, commonly known as the divided wood anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive central division. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil. It blooms in early spring, often before the canopy of trees fully develops.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to woodland gardens and natural habitats.
