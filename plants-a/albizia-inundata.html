Albizia inundata

Albizia inundata, commonly known as the water albizia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its pinnate leaves and showy, fluffy flower clusters. The plant is adapted to wet environments, often found in swamps, riverbanks, and floodplains. Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, and it can fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil.

Albizia inundata is also valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses.
