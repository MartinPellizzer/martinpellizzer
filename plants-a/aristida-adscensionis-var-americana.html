Aristida adscensionis var. americana

Aristida adscensionis var. americana, commonly known as the American dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, grass-like blades that are typically less than 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of flowers during the summer months, which are often white or pale green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

This grass is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
