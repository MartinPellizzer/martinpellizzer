Acacia eriadenia

Acacia eriadenia, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody pods containing several seeds. Acacia eriadenia is well adapted to dry conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays an important role in providing habitat and food for various desert wildlife.
