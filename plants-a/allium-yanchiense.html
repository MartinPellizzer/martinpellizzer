Allium yanchiense

Allium yanchiense, commonly known as the Yanchi onion, is a bulbous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in umbels, which are borne on slender stems. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and the bulbs are typically small but can be used in local cuisines.

This species is valued for both its ornamental beauty and potential culinary uses.
