Apium graveolens var. venosum

Apium graveolens var. venosum, commonly known as celery root or celeriac, is a biennial plant native to southern Europe and southwestern Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its hollow stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces a swollen, knobby root that is the primary edible part, often used in soups and stews. Its leaves are also edible and have a mild, celery-like flavor.

The plant thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
