Archibaccharis hirtella var. intermedia

Archibaccharis hirtella var. intermedia, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenbush, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
