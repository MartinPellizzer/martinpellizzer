Aloe alaotrensis

Aloe alaotrensis, commonly known as the Madagascar aloe, is a succulent plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for water storage. The leaves are typically green with a waxy coating, and they produce a gel-like substance that is often used for its medicinal properties.

Aloe alaotrensis thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well-suited for xerophytic conditions.
