Anemone debilis var. soyensis

Anemone debilis var. soyensis, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features showy, single or double white to pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and wetland restoration projects.
