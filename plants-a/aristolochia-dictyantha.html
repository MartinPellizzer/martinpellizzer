Aristolochia dictyantha

Aristolochia dictyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, and often fragrant flowers that are typically brown or purple in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and can grow quite long, sometimes reaching several meters in length. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific insects, such as flies and beetles.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in both traditional and modern medicinal research.
