Arabis pumila f. stellulata

Arabis pumila f. stellulata, commonly known as the starry rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, white flowers arranged in a star-like pattern, which gives it its common name. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit with hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
