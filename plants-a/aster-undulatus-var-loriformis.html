Aster undulatus var. loriformis

Aster undulatus var. loriformis, commonly known as the wavy aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped with wavy margins, and the flowers are usually blue to purple in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
