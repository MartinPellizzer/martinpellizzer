Alstroemeria floribunda

Alstroemeria floribunda, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces clusters of flowers on tall, slender stems.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its long-lasting blooms.
