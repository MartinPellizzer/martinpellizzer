Agave excelsa

Agave excelsa, commonly known as the giant agave, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is a monocot with a rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-gray leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with showy yellowish-white flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its succulent leaves.

Agave excelsa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional practices.
