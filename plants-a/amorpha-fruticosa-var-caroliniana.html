Amorpha fruticosa var. caroliniana

Amorpha fruticosa var. caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, woody stems that often grow in a sprawling or upright manner, and it produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with oval to lance-shaped leaflets, and it is well adapted to a variety of soil types.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
