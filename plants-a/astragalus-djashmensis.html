Astragalus djashmensis

Astragalus djashmensis, commonly known as the Karakorum milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus djashmensis is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest to botanists studying plant resilience in extreme climates.
