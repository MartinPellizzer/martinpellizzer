Acourtia fruticosa

Acourtia fruticosa, commonly known as the coastal buckwheat, is a perennial shrub native to the Pacific coast of North America.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and maritime habitats. It is adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy species.

Acourtia fruticosa plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
