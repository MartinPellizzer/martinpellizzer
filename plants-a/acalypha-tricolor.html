Acalypha tricolor

Acalypha tricolor, commonly known as the tri-color spurflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its strikingly colorful foliage, with leaves displaying red, green, and yellow hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant leaf coloration.

Acalypha tricolor is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
