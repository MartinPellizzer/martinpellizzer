Arundinella latifolia

Arundinella latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf reed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color, which appear at the top of the stalks. Arundinella latifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its fibrous stalks have been used traditionally for thatching and weaving in some cultures.
