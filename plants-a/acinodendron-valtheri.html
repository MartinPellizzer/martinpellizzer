Acinodendron valtheri

Acinodendron valtheri, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, palmate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. These flowers are followed by distinctive, woody pods that split open to release seeds.

Acinodendron valtheri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
