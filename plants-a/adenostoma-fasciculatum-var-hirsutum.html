Adenostoma fasciculatum var. hirsutum

Adenostoma fasciculatum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy chaste tree, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The hairy chaste tree is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
