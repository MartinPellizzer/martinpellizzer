Achillea millefolium var. litoralis

Achillea millefolium var. litoralis, commonly known as sea yarrow, is a coastal variant of the common yarrow.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pale pink. This variety thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray and strong winds. It is often found in dunes and other maritime environments, contributing to stabilizing coastal ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
