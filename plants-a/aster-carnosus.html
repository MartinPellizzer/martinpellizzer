Aster carnosus

Aster carnosus, commonly known as the woolly aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy, purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Aster carnosus is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
