Acacia baronii

Acacia baronii, commonly known as the Baron's wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia baronii is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access groundwater.

It plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
