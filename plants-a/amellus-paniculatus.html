Amellus paniculatus

Amellus paniculatus, commonly known as the panicled amellus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amelaceae.

It is characterized by its branched inflorescences, which consist of small, clustered flowers arranged in panicles. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a distinctive, often serrated margin.

Amellus paniculatus is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive floral display.
