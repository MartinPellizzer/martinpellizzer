Alliaria mathioli

Alliaria mathioli, commonly known as winter cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has alternate, lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in a siliqua, a type of dry fruit characteristic of the mustard family.

Alliaria mathioli is known for its peppery aroma and is sometimes used in culinary applications.
