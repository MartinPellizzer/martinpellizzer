Anda brasiliensis

Anda brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often display vibrant shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Anda brasiliensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
