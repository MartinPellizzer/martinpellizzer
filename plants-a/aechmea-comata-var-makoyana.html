Aechmea comata var. makoyana

Aechmea comata var. makoyana, commonly known as the Makoyan velvet plant, is a bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater for its epiphytic growth habit. The leaves are typically dark green with a velvety texture and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It produces striking inflorescences with flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
