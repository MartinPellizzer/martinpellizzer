Aechmea cathcartii

Aechmea cathcartii, commonly known as the Cathcart's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant purple flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and gas exchange.

Aechmea cathcartii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
