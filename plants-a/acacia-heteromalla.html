Acacia heteromalla

Acacia heteromalla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in compact globular clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Acacia heteromalla is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
