Arnica lanceolata subsp. amplexicaulis

Arnica lanceolata subsp.

amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping lanceleaf arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that often clasp the stem, giving it its subspecies name.

The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with prominent central disk and ray florets, typical of the genus Arnica. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
