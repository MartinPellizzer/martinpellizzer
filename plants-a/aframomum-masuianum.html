Aframomum masuianum

Aframomum masuianum, commonly known as the Masuian cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, fleshy rhizomes. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by bright red berries that contain numerous seeds. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to true cardamom.

The botanical features of Aframomum masuianum make it an important species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
