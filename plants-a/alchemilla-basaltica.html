Alchemilla basaltica

Alchemilla basaltica, commonly known as the basalt lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to rocky and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its silvery-white, woolly leaves that provide a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
