Aster diffusus var. hirsuticaulis

Aster diffusus var. hirsuticaulis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed aster, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features slender, hairy stems that often branch near the base, supporting clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
