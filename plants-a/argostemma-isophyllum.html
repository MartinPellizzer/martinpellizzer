Argostemma isophyllum

Argostemma isophyllum, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role in aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various organisms.
