Adiantum amoenum

Adiantum amoenum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a delicate and ornamental fern native to the eastern United States.

It features intricate, lace-like fronds that are typically dark green with a silvery underside. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Adiantum amoenum is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
