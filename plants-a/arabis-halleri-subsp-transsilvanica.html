Arabis halleri subsp. transsilvanica

Arabis halleri subsp.

transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions.

The plant features basal rosettes of spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. It is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with four-petaled flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
