Acer campestre var. foliis-variegatis

Acer campestre var. foliis-variegatis, commonly known as the variegated field maple, is a decorative variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features white or yellow markings on the leaves. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
