Astragalus mollissimus var. coryi

Astragalus mollissimus var. coryi, commonly known as the soft-hairy milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus mollissimus var. coryi plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
