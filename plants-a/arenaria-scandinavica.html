Arenaria scandinavica

Arenaria scandinavica, commonly known as the Scandinavian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to northern Europe.

It typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils, often found in alpine and coastal environments. The plant has small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Arenaria scandinavica plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
