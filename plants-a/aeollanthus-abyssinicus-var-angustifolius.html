Aeollanthus abyssinicus var. angustifolius

Aeollanthus abyssinicus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved African orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas. It has a rhizomatous growth habit and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
