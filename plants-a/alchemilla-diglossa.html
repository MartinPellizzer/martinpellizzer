Alchemilla diglossa

Alchemilla diglossa, commonly known as lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features basal rosettes of heart-shaped, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for wetland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
