Aspilia gracilis

Aspilia gracilis, commonly known as slender flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, orange-red inflorescences that resemble flames. Aspilia gracilis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in open woodland or grassland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
