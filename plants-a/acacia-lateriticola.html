Acacia lateriticola

Acacia lateriticola, commonly known as the lateritic acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned branches and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through its deep root system and ability to conserve water. Its leaves are typically small and leathery, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Acacia lateriticola plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
