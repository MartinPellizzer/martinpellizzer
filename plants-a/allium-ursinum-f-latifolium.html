Allium ursinum f. latifolium

Allium ursinum f. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf garlic or wild garlic, is a variant of the common garlic plant.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. This plant is native to Europe and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

The foliage is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
