Acacia macroloba

Acacia macroloba, commonly known as the large-leaved acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, and often pubescent leaves that are alternate along the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact clusters, which are typically followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia macroloba thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and rocky areas, and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
