Aeschynomene dimidiata subsp. bequaertii

Aeschynomene dimidiata subsp.

bequaertii, commonly known as the narrowleaf aeschynomene, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in grassland and savanna habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is notable for its potential use in forage and soil conservation due to its adaptability and nitrogen-fixing ability.
