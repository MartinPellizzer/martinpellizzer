Allium spurium

Allium spurium, commonly known as the garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs and leaves that are widely used in Asian cuisines for their mild garlic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and culinary use.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and practical for home cultivation.
