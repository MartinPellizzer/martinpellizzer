Aechmea sulcata

Aechmea sulcata, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater to support its growth. The leaves are typically dark green with white or silver markings, and they are covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces a flower spike that can reach several feet in height, bearing white or pink bracts.

Aechmea sulcata is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low light requirements and ability to thrive in humid environments.

Morphology

Aechmea sulcata has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, fleshy, and often covered in a dense layer of white wool, with a bluish-green color.

The leaves are arranged in a spiral pattern around the central rosette, and they can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant has a short, thick stem that is usually hidden beneath the leaves, with a rough, fibrous texture. It produces striking inflorescences with red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, and they are pollinated by birds, particularly hummingbirds.

The plant bears small, green berries that contain seeds dispersed by wind and animals.
