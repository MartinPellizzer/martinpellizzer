Astragalus breweri

Astragalus breweri, commonly known as Breweri milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a species of concern in some areas.
