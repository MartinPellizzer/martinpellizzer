Aporum confusum

Aporum confusum, commonly known as the confused aporum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features a short, stocky rhizome with large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Aporum confusum is noted for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
