Abies excelsa var. aurea

Abies excelsa var. aurea, commonly known as the golden white fir, is a variant of the white fir tree native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

This coniferous species is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which contrasts vividly with its silver-blue bark. The leaves are needle-like, typically measuring between 1 to 2 centimeters in length, and are arranged spirally along the branches. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

The plant's botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
