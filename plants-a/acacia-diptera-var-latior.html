Acacia diptera var. latior

Acacia diptera var. latior, commonly known as the broad-leaved acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small, paired leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and birds. Its wood is hard and durable, making it useful for construction and furniture-making.

The species is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of fodder for livestock.
