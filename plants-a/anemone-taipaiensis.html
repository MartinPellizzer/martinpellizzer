Anemone taipaiensis

Anemone taipaiensis, commonly known as the Taiwan anemone, is a perennial flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a distinctive, hairy texture.

Anemone taipaiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
