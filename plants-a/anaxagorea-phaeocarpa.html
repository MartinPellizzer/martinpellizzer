Anaxagorea phaeocarpa

Anaxagorea phaeocarpa, commonly known as the black-fruited sycogatta, is a tropical evergreen tree belonging to the family Anacardiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, and thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a small, round drupe with a dark purple to black skin, containing a single seed.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
