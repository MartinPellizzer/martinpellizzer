Achyronia suaveolens

Achyronia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
