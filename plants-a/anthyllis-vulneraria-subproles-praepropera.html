Anthyllis vulneraria subproles praepropera

Anthyllis vulneraria subproles praepropera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces trifoliate leaves, each composed of three narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils in coastal and arid regions.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil through its extensive root system.
