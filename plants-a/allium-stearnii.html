Allium stearnii

Allium stearnii, commonly known as the Sierra Valley onion, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of gray-green, strap-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbous base is edible and has a mild, onion-like flavor.

Allium stearnii is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and open woodlands.
