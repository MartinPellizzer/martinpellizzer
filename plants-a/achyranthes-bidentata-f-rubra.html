Achyranthes bidentata f. rubra

Achyranthes bidentata f. rubra, commonly known as red bursage, is a variant of the common smartweed.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright, branched stems and alternate leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that transition into distinctive, red, three-lobed bur-like fruits. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks, often forming dense colonies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and a subject of interest in botanical gardens.
