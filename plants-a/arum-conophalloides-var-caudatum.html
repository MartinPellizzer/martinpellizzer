Arum conophalloides var. caudatum

Arum conophalloides var. caudatum, commonly known as the long-tailed arum, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a spadix surrounded by a showy, purple spathaceous bract. The plant produces a distinctive, elongated inflorescence that gives it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
