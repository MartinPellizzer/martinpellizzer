Atriplex patula subsp. alaskensis

Atriplex patula subsp.

alaskensis, commonly known as Alaskan saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its succulent leaves, which are typically gray-green in color and adapted to reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, typical of the Amaranthaceae family. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well-suited for harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
