Anthemis lopadusana

Anthemis lopadusana, commonly known as the Alpine tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features wiry stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in summer, producing small, spherical seed heads.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
