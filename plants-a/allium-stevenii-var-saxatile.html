Allium stevenii var. saxatile

Allium stevenii var. saxatile, commonly known as the saxatile garlic chive, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks, and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and bulbous underground stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its small, spherical bulbs are often used in local cuisines for their mild, garlic-like flavor.

This variety is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
