Astragalus leucocystis

Astragalus leucocystis, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Astragalus leucocystis is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
