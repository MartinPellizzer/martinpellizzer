Austrocylindropuntia machacana

Austrocylindropuntia machacana, commonly known as the Machaca cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Argentina and southern Chile.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, ribbed stems and dense clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature. Its adaptations include spines for protection and a succulent structure to store water.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
