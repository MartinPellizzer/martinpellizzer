Arabis hirsuta var. virescens

Arabis hirsuta var. virescens, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions. The plant features basal leaves that are hairy and lobed, with a compact growth habit. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
