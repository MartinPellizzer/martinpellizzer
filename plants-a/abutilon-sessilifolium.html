Abutilon sessilifolium

Abutilon sessilifolium, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are sessile, meaning they are attached directly to the stem without a stalk. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often blooming in late summer. Its fibrous stems and leaves have been used in traditional herbal medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
