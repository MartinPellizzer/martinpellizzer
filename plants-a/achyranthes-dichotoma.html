Achyranthes dichotoma

Achyranthes dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched wormwood, is a herbaceous plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features a branched stem with opposite leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

The plant is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments.
