Areca haematocarpon

Areca haematocarpon, commonly known as the blood areca palm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Its fruit is a fibrous, three-lobed structure that contains a single seed.

Areca haematocarpon is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
