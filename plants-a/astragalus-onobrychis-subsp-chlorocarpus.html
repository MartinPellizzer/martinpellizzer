Astragalus onobrychis subsp. chlorocarpus

Astragalus onobrychis subsp.

chlorocarpus, commonly known as yellow hill brome, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant has slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikelike clusters. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed or marginal areas and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils.
