Aloysia sellowii

Aloysia sellowii, commonly known as Sow's thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks and marshy areas. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly bitter taste, while the flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Aloysia sellowii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil types.
