Amyema pachypus

Amyema pachypus, commonly known as the broad-leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It has a distinctive appearance with broad, oval-shaped leaves that are usually light green in color. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain a single seed, which is dispersed by birds.

Amyema pachypus plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food for various bird species.
