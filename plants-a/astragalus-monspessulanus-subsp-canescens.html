Astragalus monspessulanus subsp. canescens

Astragalus monspessulanus subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the graystem milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems that are often covered in a grayish-white pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval leaflets that are usually hairy.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
