Alocasia × amazonica

Alocasia × amazonica, commonly known as the giant elephant ear, is a hybrid plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often with a deep green or variegated coloration. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil. It is a member of the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental plants like peace lilies and calla lilies.

Due to its striking foliage, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant in both indoor and outdoor gardens.
