Aster olivaceus

Aster olivaceus, commonly known as the olive bluemouth, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in full sun.

Aster olivaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
