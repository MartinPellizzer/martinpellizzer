Astragalus euchlorus

Astragalus euchlorus, commonly known as the bluish milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland ecosystems.

Astragalus euchlorus plays a role in nitrogen fixation and serves as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
