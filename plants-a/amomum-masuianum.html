Amomum masuianum

Amomum masuianum, commonly known as the Siam cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, elongated rhizomes. The plant produces fragrant, three-lobed leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their pungent aroma and medicinal properties.

Amomum masuianum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its economic and therapeutic value.
