Anthyllis vulneraria f. schiwereckii

Anthyllis vulneraria f. schiwereckii, commonly known as the Schiwereck's Scotch broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its bright yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a creeping growth habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
