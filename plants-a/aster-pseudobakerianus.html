Aster pseudobakerianus

Aster pseudobakerianus, commonly known as the Baker's aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers are usually purple or blue, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Aster pseudobakerianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
