Alternanthera parviflora

Alternanthera parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered Alternanthera, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It features vibrant, colorful leaves that can range from red to pink, depending on light exposure and water conditions. The plant has small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters near the water's surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often used in aquatic landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth rate makes it both a popular choice for aquariums and a potential weed in natural water bodies.
