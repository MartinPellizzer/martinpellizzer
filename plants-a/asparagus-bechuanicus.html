Asparagus bechuanicus

Asparagus bechuanicus, commonly known as the Bechuanian asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, typically blooming in late summer. Its root system is extensive and adapted to arid conditions, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Asparagus bechuanicus is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appearance.
