Axonopus kisantuensis

Axonopus kisantuensis, commonly known as the Kisantu grass, is a species of grass native to Central Africa.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in forest understories or along riverbanks. The plant has slender, tufted shoots and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a tendency to form dense mats.

Axonopus kisantuensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
