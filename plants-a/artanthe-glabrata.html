Artanthe glabrata

Artanthe glabrata, commonly known as the smooth artanthe, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, showy flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, pink to red blossoms that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a unique appearance.

Artanthe glabrata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
