Arundinaria matsunoi

Arundinaria matsunoi, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Japanese arrow bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance.

Arundinaria matsunoi is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
