Asplenium morlanense

Asplenium morlanense, commonly known as the Morlan's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth form. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rock surfaces or in humid forest understories.

Asplenium morlanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
