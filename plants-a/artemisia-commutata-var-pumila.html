Artemisia commutata var. pumila

Artemisia commutata var. pumila, commonly known as silver thread sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that appear in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its aromatic leaves and adaptability to arid conditions make it a notable species in its ecological niche.
