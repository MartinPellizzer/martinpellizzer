Anastraphia recurva var. integrifolia

Anastraphia recurva var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leafed anastraphia, is a species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with smooth margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight for optimal growth.
