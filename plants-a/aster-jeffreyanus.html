Aster jeffreyanus

Aster jeffreyanus, commonly known as Jeffrey's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early fall, producing blue to purple petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
