Abacopteris × thwaitesii

Abacopteris × thwaitesii, commonly known as the hybrid paper mulberry, is a naturally occurring hybrid between *Abacopteris australis* and *A. thwaitesii*.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and estuarine environments, particularly in eastern Australia. The plant is characterized by its fibrous, woody stems and alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its hardy nature and has been used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils in tidal zones.
