Aconitum paludicola

Aconitum paludicola, commonly known as the marsh aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated margins, and its roots contain potent alkaloids.

Aconitum paludicola thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.
