Andropogon piptatherus var. palmeri

Andropogon piptatherus var. palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green blades that turn golden in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
