Acrocarpus fraxinifolius var. guangxiensis

Acrocarpus fraxinifolius var. guangxiensis, commonly known as Guangxi ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and lanceolate leaves that are often hairy underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its durable wood, it has been historically used for construction and furniture.
