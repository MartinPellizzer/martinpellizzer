Anathallis cuspidata

Anathallis cuspidata, commonly known as the needle-tipped anathallis, is a species of orchid native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a slender, erect stem that bears a single, elongated flower. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged along the stem, and the flower features a distinctive, elongated lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Anathallis cuspidata is notable for its unique morphology and is a subject of interest in botanical studies.
