Arabis neglecta

Arabis neglecta, commonly known as the common rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of linear, hairy leaves and produces small, white or yellow flowers in the spring. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures.

Arabis neglecta is typically low-maintenance and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
