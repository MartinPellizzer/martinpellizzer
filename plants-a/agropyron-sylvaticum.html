Agropyron sylvaticum

Agropyron sylvaticum, commonly known as the woodland needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Agropyron sylvaticum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
