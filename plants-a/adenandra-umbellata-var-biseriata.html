Adenandra umbellata var. biseriata

Adenandra umbellata var. biseriata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

The flowers attract pollinators such as birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
