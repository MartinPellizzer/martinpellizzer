Arthrochilus rosulatus

Arthrochilus rosulatus, commonly known as the Rosulate Heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are often pink or white.

Arthrochilus rosulatus is known for its resilience to dry conditions and is often found in coastal regions.
