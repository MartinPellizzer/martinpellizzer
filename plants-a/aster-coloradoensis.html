Aster coloradoensis

Aster coloradoensis, commonly known as the Colorado aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster coloradoensis is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other familiar flowering plants.
