Agrostis petriei var. mutica

Agrostis petriei var. mutica, commonly known as the rough hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often have a rough texture. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a prominent midrib. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, pale green to brownish flowers.

This plant is typically found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along the edges of ponds.
