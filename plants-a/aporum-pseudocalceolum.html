Aporum pseudocalceolum

Aporum pseudocalceolum, commonly known as the false stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the summer months. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Aporum pseudocalceolum thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to flourish.
