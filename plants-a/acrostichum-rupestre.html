Acrostichum rupestre

Acrostichum rupestre, commonly known as the rock hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its robust, woody stems and large, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky landscapes.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping and as a garden plant.
