Adonis aestivalis subvar. flava

Adonis aestivalis subvar. flava, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems rise from the center of the rosette, bearing numerous small, clustered flowers.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
