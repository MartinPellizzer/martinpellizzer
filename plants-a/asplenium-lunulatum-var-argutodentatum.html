Asplenium lunulatum var. argutodentatum

Asplenium lunulatum var. argutodentatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a soft, leathery texture, with a distinctive, narrow shape.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in humid, indoor settings.
