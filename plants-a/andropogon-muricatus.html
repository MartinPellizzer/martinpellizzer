Andropogon muricatus

Andropogon muricatus, commonly known as hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces spikelets that are often tinged with blue or purple. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its flowering period usually occurs in the summer months, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
