Asplenium gilpiniae

Asplenium gilpiniae, commonly known as the Gilpin's spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, wiry rhizomes and compact, evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rock crevices and forest floors. Its fronds are usually less than 10 centimeters in length, with a lanceolate shape and a serrated margin.

Asplenium gilpiniae is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in woodland ecosystems.
