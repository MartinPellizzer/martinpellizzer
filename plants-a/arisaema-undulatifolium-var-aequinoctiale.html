Arisaema undulatifolium var. aequinoctiale

Arisaema undulatifolium var. aequinoctiale, commonly known as the wavy-leaf arrowhead, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves with wavy margins, which give it its common name. The plant produces an umbellate inflorescence with a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a preference for moist, shaded environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some regions.
