Acacia tawitawiensis

Acacia tawitawiensis, commonly known as the Tawitawi acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is a small shrub characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow inflorescences. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, typically in well-drained soils. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
