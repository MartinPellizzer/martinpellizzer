Aerides quinquevulnera var. punctata

Aerides quinquevulnera var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces fragrant, spurred flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is distinguished by its distinctive dark spots on the petals, which give it its common name.
