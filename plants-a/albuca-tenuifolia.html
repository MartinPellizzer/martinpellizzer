Albuca tenuifolia

Albuca tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved albuca, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces tall, erect stems with clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Albuca tenuifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
