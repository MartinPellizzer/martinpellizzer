Aspalathus cuneata var. hamulosa

Aspalathus cuneata var. hamulosa, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
