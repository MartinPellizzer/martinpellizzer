Alysicarpus quartinianus

Alysicarpus quartinianus, commonly known as the African burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive burr-like fruits. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid to semi-arid environments. Its seeds are encased in a tough, spiny pod that aids in seed dispersal.

Alysicarpus quartinianus is also valued for its potential use in soil stabilization and as a forage plant.
