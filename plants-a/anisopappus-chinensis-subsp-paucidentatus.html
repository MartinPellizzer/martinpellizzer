Anisopappus chinensis subsp. paucidentatus

Anisopappus chinensis subsp.

paucidentatus, commonly known as the Chinese spiny broom, is a flowering plant native to China. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while the inflorescence consists of small, clustered flower heads.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience in dry landscapes.
