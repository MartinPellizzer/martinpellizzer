Agoseris pubescens

Agoseris pubescens, commonly known as the hairy goldenroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Agoseris pubescens is known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various healing purposes.
