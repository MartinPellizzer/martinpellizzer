Acnistus coccoloboides

Acnistus coccoloboides, commonly known as the creeping nettle.

This plant is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of the Americas. It features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is known for its stinging hairs, similar to those found in true nettles.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
