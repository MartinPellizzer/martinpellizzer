Aster villiger

Aster villiger, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant features a sturdy stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and grayish-green in appearance. Its showy purple or blue-violet flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster villiger is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
