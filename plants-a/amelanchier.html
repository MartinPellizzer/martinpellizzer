Amelanchier

Amelanchier, commonly known as serviceberry, is a genus of deciduous shrubs and small trees native to the Americas.

These plants typically produce showy white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in the summer. The leaves are usually ovate or elliptical, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. Amelanchier species are valued for their ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.

They thrive in a variety of soil types and are often found in woodland or open areas.
