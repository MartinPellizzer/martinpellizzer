Ambrosia magdalenae

Ambrosia magdalenae, commonly known as the Magdalena ambrosia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
