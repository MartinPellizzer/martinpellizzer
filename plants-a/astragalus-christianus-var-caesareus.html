Astragalus christianus var. caesareus

Astragalus christianus var. caesareus, commonly known as Caesar's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It produces pods that contain small, round seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
