Alopecurus echinatus

Alopecurus echinatus, commonly known as hairy mouse-ear, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing a single, erect, bristled floret.

Alopecurus echinatus is often found in temperate climates and is adapted to thrive in environments with moderate moisture levels.
