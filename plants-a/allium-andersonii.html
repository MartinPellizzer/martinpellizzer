Allium andersonii

Allium andersonii, commonly known as Anderson's onion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and spherical, purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, similar to other allium species.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
