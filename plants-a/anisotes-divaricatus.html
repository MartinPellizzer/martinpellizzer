Anisotes divaricatus

Anisotes divaricatus, commonly known as the water lily, is a aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It features broad, floating leaves that are typically green with a waxy surface, and its flowers are showy, often with white or pink petals. The plant has a submerged root system that anchors it in shallow water. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms.

Its growth is often associated with still or slow-moving freshwater environments.
