Alyssum pogonocarpum

Alyssum pogonocarpum, commonly known as the woolly alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has silvery-gray foliage covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive woolly appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum pogonocarpum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
