Asparagus puberulus

Asparagus puberulus, commonly known as the hairy asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, triangular berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Asparagus puberulus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
