Asplenium elongatum

Asplenium elongatum, commonly known as the long-leaf spleenwort, is a member of the Aspleniaceae family and is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

It is a terrestrial or epiphytic fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant has a simple, rhizomatous structure with small, lanceolate leaves that are usually less than 10 cm in length. Its spores are produced in clusters on the undersides of the fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction via spore dispersal.

Asplenium elongatum is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological significance.
