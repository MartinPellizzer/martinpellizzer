Allium schoenoprasum var. gredense

Allium schoenoprasum var. gredense, commonly known as mountain chives, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spherical umbels of pale purple or white flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, garlic-like scent, which is due to the presence of sulfur compounds in its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses in traditional cuisines.
