Aster fragilis var. subdumosus

Aster fragilis var. subdumosus, commonly known as the fragrant aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has finely divided, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
