Astragalus talassicus

Astragalus talassicus, commonly known as the coastal milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in steppe and desert environments. The plant produces small, yellow to green flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are pinnate, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus talassicus is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
