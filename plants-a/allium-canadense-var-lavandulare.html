Allium canadense var. lavandulare

Allium canadense var. lavandulare, commonly known as Canada bunchberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a dense cluster of tiny, bell-shaped flowers surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
