Amblyglottis pulchra

Amblyglottis pulchra, commonly known as the pretty-leaf amblyglottis, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for shaded garden areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
