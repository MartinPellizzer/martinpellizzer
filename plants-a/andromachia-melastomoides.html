Andromachia melastomoides

Andromachia melastomoides, commonly known as the swamp melastome, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to saturated soil conditions. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
