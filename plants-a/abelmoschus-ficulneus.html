Abelmoschus ficulneus

Abelmoschus ficulneus, commonly known as the African okra or Ethiopian okra, is a flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to Africa and thrives in warm, tropical climates. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain edible seeds, often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are broad and palmate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Abelmoschus ficulneus is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its resilience to poor soil conditions.
