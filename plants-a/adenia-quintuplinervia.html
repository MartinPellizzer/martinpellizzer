Adenia quintuplinervia

Adenia quintuplinervia, commonly known as the five-veined adenium, is a striking shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, often woody stems and large, palmately lobed leaves with five distinct veins. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Adenia quintuplinervia is also noted for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid conditions.
