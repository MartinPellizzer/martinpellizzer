Anthriscus sylvestris var. mollis

Anthriscus sylvestris var. mollis, commonly known as smooth cow parsley, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 60-120 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soils in partial shade to full sun. Its leaves and stems are hairy, and the flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

Smooth cow parsley is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks.
