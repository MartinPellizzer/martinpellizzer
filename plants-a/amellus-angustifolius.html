Amellus angustifolius

Amellus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed amellus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters wide. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
