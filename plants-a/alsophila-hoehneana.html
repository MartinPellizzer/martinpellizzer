Alsophila hoehneana

Alsophila hoehneana, commonly known as the Hoehne's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in persistent, fibrous roots. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
