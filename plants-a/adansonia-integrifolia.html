Adansonia integrifolia

Adansonia integrifolia, commonly known as the baobab tree, is a distinctive species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its massive, bottle-shaped trunk that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The tree has large, umbrella-like leaves and produces yellow, edible flowers that develop into fruit with a pulp rich in nutrients. Its bark is thick and fibrous, providing structural support and protection against fire.

Adansonia integrifolia plays a vital role in the ecosystem, offering shelter and food to various animals.
