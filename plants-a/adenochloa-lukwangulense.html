Adenochloa lukwangulense

Adenochloa lukwangulense, commonly known as the Lukwangulene grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Adenochloa lukwangulense is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
