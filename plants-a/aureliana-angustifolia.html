Aureliana angustifolia

Aureliana angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aureliana, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
