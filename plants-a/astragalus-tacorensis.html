Astragalus tacorensis

Astragalus tacorensis, commonly known as the Tacorensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus tacorensis plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
