Adenostoma californicum

Adenostoma californicum, commonly known as the California scrub oak, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

This plant plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and is often found in chaparral ecosystems.
