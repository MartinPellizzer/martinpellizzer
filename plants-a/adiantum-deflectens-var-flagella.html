Adiantum deflectens var. flagella

Adiantum deflectens var. flagella, commonly known as the flagellate maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that are often described as resembling flowing hair. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its leaves are typically bright green and have a silky texture, with a unique, slender shape.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
