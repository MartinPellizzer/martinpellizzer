Agoseris glauca subsp. aspera

Agoseris glauca subsp.

aspera, commonly known as the rough blue fleabane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features blue to purple composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving them a rough texture.

The species is characterized by its showy, daisy-like inflorescence and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
