Azureocereus

Azureocereus, commonly known as the blue cactus, is a genus of cacti native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

These plants are characterized by their tall, columnar stems and striking blue-gray or bluish-green coloration. They possess spines and are adapted to arid environments, storing water in their thick, fleshy stems. Azureocereus species typically bloom with large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink or purple.

Their unique appearance and resilience make them popular among cactus enthusiasts and desert gardeners.
