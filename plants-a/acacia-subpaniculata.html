Acacia subpaniculata

Acacia subpaniculata, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in pairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, spreading clusters, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it can grow in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.

Acacia subpaniculata is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
