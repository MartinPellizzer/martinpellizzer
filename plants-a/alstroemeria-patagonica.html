Alstroemeria patagonica

Alstroemeria patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a member of the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas and thrives in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of strap-like, dark green leaves, and it produces numerous blooms throughout the growing season.

Alstroemeria patagonica is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in its native habitats.
