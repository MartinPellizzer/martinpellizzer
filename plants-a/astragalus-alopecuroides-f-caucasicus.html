Astragalus alopecuroides f. caucasicus

Astragalus alopecuroides f. caucasicus, commonly known as Caucasian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowering display.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local biodiversity in its native habitat.
