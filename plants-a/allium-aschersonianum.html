Allium aschersonianum

Allium aschersonianum, commonly known as the purple onion or purple garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal. Its bulbs are edible but have a milder flavor compared to common onions.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
