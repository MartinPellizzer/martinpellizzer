Acaena leptacantha var. negeri

Acaena leptacantha var. negeri, commonly known as the New Zealand spinach bush, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, spiny leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that develop into distinctive, hairy, woody fruits. The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions. It is often found in coastal and subalpine regions, where it thrives in full sun.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy, drought-resistant characteristics.
