Amsinckia angustifolia var. microcarpa

Amsinckia angustifolia var. microcarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf checkerweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers with four petals, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and contribute to its ecological significance in native plant communities.
