Alchemilla hoppeana var. leptoclada

Alchemilla hoppeana var. leptoclada, commonly known as the slender-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and distinctive, shield-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months, which are arranged in loose, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
