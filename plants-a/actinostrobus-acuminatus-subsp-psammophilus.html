Actinostrobus acuminatus subsp. psammophilus

Actinostrobus acuminatus subsp.

psammophilus, commonly known as the sand-dwelling mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and a preference for growing on the branches of host trees, particularly in sandy soils.

The plant has a specialized root system that allows it to anchor itself and absorb nutrients from its host. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by birds.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand drought and has a unique ecological role in its native habitat.
