Acer palmatum f. pilosum

Acer palmatum f. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy Japanese maple, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree characterized by its distinctive fuzzy, velvety texture on the leaves.

The foliage is typically a vibrant green in summer, transitioning to brilliant red or orange hues in the autumn. This plant is native to Japan and thrives in USDA hardiness zones 6 through 8, preferring partial shade and well-drained soil. Its unique leaf structure makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and landscape design.

The plant's slow growth rate and compact form make it suitable for smaller spaces and container gardening.
