Amaracarpus nematopodus

Amaracarpus nematopodus, commonly known as the slender root tree, is a rare and lesser-known member of the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. Native to the tropical regions of South America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
