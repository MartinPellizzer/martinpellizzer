Astragalus whitneyi var. sonneanus

Astragalus whitneyi var. sonneanus, commonly known as the Sonnean milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

This variety is often found in areas with limited moisture, showcasing its resilience and ecological significance.
