Aristolochia pilgeriana

Aristolochia pilgeriana, commonly known as the Pilger's Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in moist, wooded areas. It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
