Apargia autumnalis var. salina

Apargia autumnalis var. salina, commonly known as salt marsh evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of North America.

It is characterized by its yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in the fall, typically from September to November. The plant thrives in saline and brackish wetlands, adapting to high salt concentrations in the soil. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
