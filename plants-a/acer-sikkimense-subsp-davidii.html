Acer sikkimense subsp. davidii

Acer sikkimense subsp.

davidii, commonly known as the David's maple, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which are typically bright green during the growing season.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
