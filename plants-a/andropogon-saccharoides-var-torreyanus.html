Andropogon saccharoides var. torreyanus

Andropogon saccharoides var. torreyanus, commonly known as Torrey's switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for wildlife, as well as its potential use in bioenergy production.
