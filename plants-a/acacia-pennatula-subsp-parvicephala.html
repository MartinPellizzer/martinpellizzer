Acacia pennatula subsp. parvicephala

Acacia pennatula subsp.

parvicephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that appear in cylindrical or cylindrical-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid regions. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in full sun.

The species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
