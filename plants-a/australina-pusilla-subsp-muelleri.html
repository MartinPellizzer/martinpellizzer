Australina pusilla subsp. muelleri

Australina pusilla subsp.

muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's dwarf lichen, is a small, crustose lichen that typically grows on rock surfaces in arid and semi-arid regions. It belongs to the family Parmeliaceae and is characterized by its pale to dark grey or brownish thallus.

The species is known for its ability to tolerate extreme environmental conditions, including high temperatures and low moisture. It lacks distinct lobes and has a relatively uniform texture.

This lichen is often found in southern Australia, particularly in areas with well-drained soils.
