Astragalus borissovae

Astragalus borissovae, commonly known as the Borissov's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its roots are known to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Astragalus borissovae is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
