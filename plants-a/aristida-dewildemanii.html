Aristida dewildemanii

Aristida dewildemanii, commonly known as the dwarf hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and dense, compact growth habit. Its inflorescence consists of a slender, spike-like cluster of small, spikelets.

Aristida dewildemanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant landscaping.
