Achillea peucedanifolia

Achillea peucedanifolia, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish to white flowers. The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often growing in open, dry habitats.

Its long-lasting flowers make it a popular choice for dried floral arrangements.
