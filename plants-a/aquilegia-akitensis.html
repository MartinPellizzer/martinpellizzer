Aquilegia akitensis

Aquilegia akitensis, commonly known as the Akitan columbine, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred petals that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are compound, with deeply lobed foliage that contributes to its ornamental appeal.

Aquilegia akitensis is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
