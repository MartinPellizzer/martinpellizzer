Asparagus aphyllus

Asparagus aphyllus, commonly known as the leafless asparagus, is a unique member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its absence of true leaves, instead featuring modified stems that perform photosynthesis. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that develop into red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unusual structure, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and botanical collections.
