Anisostachya velutina

Anisostachya velutina, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Anisostachya velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage.
