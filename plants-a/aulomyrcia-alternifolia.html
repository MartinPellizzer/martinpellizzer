Aulomyrcia alternifolia

Aulomyrcia alternifolia, commonly known as the alternate-leaf myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its alternate arrangement of leaves, which are typically elliptic and have a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a valuable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.

Aulomyrcia alternifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in local remedies for various ailments.
