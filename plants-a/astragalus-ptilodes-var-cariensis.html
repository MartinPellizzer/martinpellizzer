Astragalus ptilodes var. cariensis

Astragalus ptilodes var. cariensis, commonly known as the Carian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are bipinnate, with finely divided leaflets, and it produces cylindrical seed pods that contain small, rounded seeds.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
