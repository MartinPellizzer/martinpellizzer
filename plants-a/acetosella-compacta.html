Acetosella compacta

Acetosella compacta, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features dense clusters of small, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces tiny white or pink flowers in early spring, which are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its succulent leaves and short, creeping stems.
