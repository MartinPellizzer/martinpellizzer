Anathallis paula

Anathallis paula, commonly known as the Paula's spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, slender sepals and petals that resemble the legs of a spider. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a distinctive, intricate structure that aids in pollination by specific insects.

Anathallis paula is a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native habitat.
