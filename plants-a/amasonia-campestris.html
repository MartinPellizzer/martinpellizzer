Amasonia campestris

Amasonia campestris, commonly known as the field amsonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. Amasonia campestris thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairies and open woodlands.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for gardens and restoration projects.
