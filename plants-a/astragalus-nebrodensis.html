Astragalus nebrodensis

Astragalus nebrodensis, commonly known as the Nebrodean milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological studies due to its limited distribution.

Astragalus nebrodensis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
