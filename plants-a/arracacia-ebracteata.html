Arracacia ebracteata

Arracacia ebracteata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The plant is often found in disturbed areas and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
