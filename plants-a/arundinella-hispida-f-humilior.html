Arundinella hispida f. humilior

Arundinella hispida f. humilior, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a compact, clump-forming plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, making it suitable for small garden spaces or container cultivation. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, and it is often used as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance.

The inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of pale green to brown flowers, which appear in late summer or early autumn.
