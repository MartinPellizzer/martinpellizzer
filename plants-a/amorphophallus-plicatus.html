Amorphophallus plicatus

Amorphophallus plicatus, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces a massive, spadix-shaped flower that can grow over 3 meters tall, often with a strong, unpleasant odor to attract pollinators. Its underground corm is used in traditional cuisine and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in marshy areas along riverbanks.
