Andropogon albescens

Andropogon albescens, commonly known as white bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades that turn a pale blue-green color during the growing season. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

Andropogon albescens plays an important role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
