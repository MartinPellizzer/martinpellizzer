Allium mahneshanense

Allium mahneshanense, commonly known as the Mahneshan onion, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, fibrous roots and clustered bulbs. The plant produces tall, narrow leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
