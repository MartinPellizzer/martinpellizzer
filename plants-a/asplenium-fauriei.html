Asplenium fauriei

Asplenium fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's spleenwort, is a fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic or lithophytic plant, typically growing on rocks or tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lanceolate to ovate, with a dark green color and a serrated or toothed margin. The plant produces spores in compact clusters called sorus, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium fauriei is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in botanical gardens.
