Aglaia stellatotomentosa

Aglaia stellatotomentosa, commonly known as the star-tomentose aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and opposite, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in branched inflorescences, followed by woody, egg-shaped capsules containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
