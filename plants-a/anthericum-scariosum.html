Anthericum scariosum

Anthericum scariosum, commonly known as the scorched anthericum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
