Aspidium monostichum

Aspidium monostichum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arranged in a single row along the stem. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, facilitating spore dispersal.

Aspidium monostichum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to water filtration.
