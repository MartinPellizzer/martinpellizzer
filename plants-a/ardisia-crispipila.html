Ardisia crispipila

Ardisia crispipila, commonly known as the glossy wood-sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible but often have a mild bitterness. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Ardisia crispipila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
