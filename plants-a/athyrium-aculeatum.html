Athyrium aculeatum

Athyrium aculeatum, commonly known as the holly fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, pinnately arranged fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its fronds are often covered with small, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Athyrium aculeatum is valued for its ornamental foliage and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
