Astragalus nigrescens

Astragalus nigrescens, commonly known as black locust or false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
