Acalypha fulva

Acalypha fulva, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround its small yellow flowers, creating a vivid visual display. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture with a reddish tinge.

Acalypha fulva thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
