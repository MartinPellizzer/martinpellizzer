Amphilophis ensiformis

Amphilophis ensiformis, commonly known as the slender leaf fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant typically thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to moist soil or rock surfaces.

Amphilophis ensiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
