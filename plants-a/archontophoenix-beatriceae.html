Archontophoenix beatriceae

Archontophoenix beatriceae, commonly known as the Beatrice's palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the island of Madagascar and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in suitable climates.
