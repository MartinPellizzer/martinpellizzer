Alliaria pancheri

Alliaria pancheri, commonly known as garlic mustard, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are hairy and emit a strong garlic-like odor when crushed. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters.

Garlic mustard is known for its invasive growth in North American forests, where it can outcompete native plants.
