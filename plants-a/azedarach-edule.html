Azedarach edule

Azedarach edule, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to regions of Asia, including parts of China, India, and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lilac-like flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with several oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which can be dispersed by wind or animals.
