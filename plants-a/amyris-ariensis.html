Amyris ariensis

Amyris ariensis, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody scent. The plant features simple, opposite, and leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits containing seeds.

Amyris ariensis is valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
