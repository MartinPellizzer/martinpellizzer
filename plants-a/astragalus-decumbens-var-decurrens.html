Astragalus decumbens var. decurrens

Astragalus decumbens var. decurrens, commonly known as the creeping milkvetch, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features slender, prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often rooting at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in dense heads, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually hairy and narrow.

This species is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
