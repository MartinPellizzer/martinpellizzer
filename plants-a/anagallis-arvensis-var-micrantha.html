Anagallis arvensis var. micrantha

Anagallis arvensis var. micrantha, commonly known as the lesser mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, hairy, oval-shaped leaves and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink or white. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often clustered in groups of three to five. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a characteristic plant of moist meadows and woodland edges.
