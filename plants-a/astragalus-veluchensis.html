Astragalus veluchensis

Astragalus veluchensis, commonly known as the Veluchenskaya milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and its root system is known for its deep penetration into the soil, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky environments.

Astragalus veluchensis is also noted for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses attributed to its potential health benefits.
