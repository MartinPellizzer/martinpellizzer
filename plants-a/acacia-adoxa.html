Acacia adoxa

Acacia adoxa, commonly known as the black acacia, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are divided into numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular inflorescences, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia adoxa is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
