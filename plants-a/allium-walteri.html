Allium walteri

Allium walteri, commonly known as Walter's garlic, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous structure and grass-like foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a potential culinary and medicinal plant.

Allium walteri thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.
