Achariterium arvense

Achariterium arvense, commonly known as field thistle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant features a robust stem with spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive, prickly texture.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.
