Alsophila poolii

Alsophila poolii, commonly known as the Pool's tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its distinctive, tree-like structure.

Alsophila poolii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
