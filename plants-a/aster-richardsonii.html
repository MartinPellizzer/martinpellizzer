Aster richardsonii

Aster richardsonii, commonly known as the Richardson's aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster richardsonii is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
