Arundinella phragmitoides

Arundinella phragmitoides, commonly known as the giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It has long, slender, often hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish or brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread aggressively, forming dense stands.

It is often used in landscaping and erosion control due to its rapid growth and ability to stabilize soil.
