Alloteropsis papillosa

Alloteropsis papillosa, commonly known as the silky grass or silky broomgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves with a silvery-white underside. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Alloteropsis papillosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
