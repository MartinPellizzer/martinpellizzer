Actinodaphne macgregorii

Actinodaphne macgregorii, commonly known as the MacGregor's camphor tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, typical of the Lauraceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and for traditional uses.
