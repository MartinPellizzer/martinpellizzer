Aconitum tasiroi

Aconitum tasiroi, commonly known as the Tasiro aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to New Guinea.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy blue to violet flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, spurred corolla with five sepals that are often reflexed. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a rough, hairy texture.

Aconitum tasiroi contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to handle or ingest.
