Alsophila wengiensis

Alsophila wengiensis, commonly known as the Wengi tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in primary rainforests. Its rhizome system allows it to anchor deeply into the soil, supporting its growth in nutrient-rich, moist conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
