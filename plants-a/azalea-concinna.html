Azalea concinna

Azalea concinna, commonly known as the dwarf azalea, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to red flowers in the spring, which are characteristic of the Rhododendron genus. Azalea concinna thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a favored plant for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
