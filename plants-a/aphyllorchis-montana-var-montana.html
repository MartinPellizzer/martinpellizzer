Aphyllorchis montana var. montana

Aphyllorchis montana var. montana, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often on rocky or humus-rich substrates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
