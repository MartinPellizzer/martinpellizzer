Aspalathus fourcadei

Aspalathus fourcadei, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential nutritional benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and research settings.
