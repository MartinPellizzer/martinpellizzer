Astragalus viridissimus

Astragalus viridissimus, commonly known as the greenstem milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus viridissimus plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
