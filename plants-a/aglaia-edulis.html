Aglaia edulis

Aglaia edulis, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces bright red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds used to make black, white, and green pepper. Its fruit is an important component in traditional medicine and culinary practices.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
