Amomum villosum

Amomum villosum, commonly known as wild cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and spikes of small, white flowers. The plant produces dark brown, oblong fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its rhizomes and fruits contain essential oils rich in compounds like cineole and limonene, contributing to its medicinal properties.

Amomum villosum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aromatic value.
