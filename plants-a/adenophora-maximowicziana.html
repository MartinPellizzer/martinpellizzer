Adenophora maximowicziana

Adenophora maximowicziana, commonly known as the Korean bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
