Archidendron monopterum

Archidendron monopterum, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming during the dry season. Its fruit is a woody pod that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in suitable environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its striking floral display.
