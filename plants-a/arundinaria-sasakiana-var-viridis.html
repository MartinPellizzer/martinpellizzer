Arundinaria sasakiana var. viridis

Arundinaria sasakiana var. viridis, commonly known as the green bamboo, is a variant of the Sasak bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, green-colored culms that grow in dense clumps. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically a vibrant green, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This bamboo variety is valued for both its aesthetic qualities and its use in traditional crafts.
