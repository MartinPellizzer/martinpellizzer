Asparagopsis consanguinea

Asparagopsis consanguinea, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its striking red flowers and sword-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
