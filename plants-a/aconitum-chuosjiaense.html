Aconitum chuosjiaense

Aconitum chuosjiaense, commonly known as the Chuosjia aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, conical clusters. Its roots contain toxic alkaloids, making it a potentially dangerous plant to handle.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
