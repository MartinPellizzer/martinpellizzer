Acaena polycarpa var. longifilamentosa

Acaena polycarpa var. longifilamentosa, commonly known as the long-stamen acacia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spherical growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
