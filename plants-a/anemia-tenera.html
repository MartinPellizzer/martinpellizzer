Anemia tenera

Anemia tenera, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a globose, fleshy berry that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.

Anemia tenera is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to both animals and humans.
