Aponogeton junceus

Aponogeton junceus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water arum, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are often submerged, followed by dark, spherical berries. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Aponogeton junceus is popular in aquascaping due to its ornamental appearance and ease of care.
