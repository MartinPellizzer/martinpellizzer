Acacia longifolia f. sophorae

Acacia longifolia f. sophorae, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Acacia, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. It is adapted to arid and coastal environments, showcasing drought tolerance and salt resistance.

This form is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
