Alseuosmia palaeiformis

Alseuosmia palaeiformis, commonly known as the ancient whiteflower, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments, typically in old-growth forests with rich, well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its aesthetic appeal and ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
