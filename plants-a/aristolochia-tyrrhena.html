Aristolochia tyrrhena

Aristolochia tyrrhena, commonly known as the Mediterranean Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy texture and a strong, often unpleasant odor. The plant produces unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white with a prominent hood. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
