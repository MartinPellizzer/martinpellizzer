Anthyllis vulneraria f. alba

Anthyllis vulneraria f. alba, commonly known as the white bugloss, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial that features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in dry, rocky, or sandy soils. It is known for its medicinal properties, historically used for its healing qualities.

The white form of this species is distinguished by its pale flowers, contrasting with the more common blue or purple varieties.
