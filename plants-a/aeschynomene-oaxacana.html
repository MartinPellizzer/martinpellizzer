Aeschynomene oaxacana

Aeschynomene oaxacana, commonly known as Oaxacan burr legume, is a shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in dry, disturbed habitats and is often found in arid regions of Central America.

Aeschynomene oaxacana is also valued for its potential in soil stabilization and as a forage plant.
