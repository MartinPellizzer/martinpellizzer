Asplenium hylophilum

Asplenium hylophilum, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, often epiphytic growth habit. The plant typically features small, lance-shaped leaves with a dense, velvety texture. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found on rocks or tree trunks.

Its reproductive structures are borne on sporangia, which are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds.
