Astragalus fragifer

Astragalus fragifer, commonly known as the fragrant milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Astragalus fragifer is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
