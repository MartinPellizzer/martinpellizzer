Amellus capensis

Amellus capensis, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

Amellus capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
