Astragalus blandulus

Astragalus blandulus, commonly known as the pale milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus blandulus is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, contributing to the ecological diversity of these regions.
