Acacia longifolia var. phlebophylla

Acacia longifolia var. phlebophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, needle-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that appear in late winter to early spring. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control.
