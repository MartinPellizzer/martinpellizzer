Andropogon ternatus var. africanus

Andropogon ternatus var. africanus, commonly known as African switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
