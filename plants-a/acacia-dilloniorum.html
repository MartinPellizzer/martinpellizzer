Acacia dilloniorum

Acacia dilloniorum, commonly known as the Dillon's wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Acacia dilloniorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
