Acalypha muralis

Acalypha muralis, commonly known as the wall goosefoot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its growth habit is sprawling, making it suitable for ground cover or as a decorative plant in gardens.

Acalypha muralis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
