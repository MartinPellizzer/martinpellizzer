Artemisia ludoviciana var. pabularis

Artemisia ludoviciana var. pabularis, commonly known as gray sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage, which is covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and its role in supporting local wildlife.
