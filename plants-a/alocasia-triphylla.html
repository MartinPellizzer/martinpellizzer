Alocasia triphylla

Alocasia triphylla, commonly known as the three-lobed elephant ear, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive three-lobed structure. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Alocasia triphylla thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
