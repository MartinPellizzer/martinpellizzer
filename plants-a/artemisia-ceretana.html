Artemisia ceretana

Artemisia ceretana, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are typically clustered in dense spherical shapes. Artemisia ceretana is known for its strong, camphor-like scent and its ability to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.

It is often used in traditional medicine and has historical significance in herbal remedies.
