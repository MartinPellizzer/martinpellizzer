Actinostemon echinatus var. minor

Actinostemon echinatus var. minor, commonly known as the lesser spiny bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, leaf-like bracts that enclose its small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in dry gardens.
