Amarella brevicaulis

Amarella brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-stemmed amarella, is a unique flowering plant characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive foliage.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are densely arranged along its short, sturdy stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring. Amarella brevicaulis is native to specific regions in southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a lesser-known but ecologically significant species in its native environment.
