Allium roseum

Allium roseum, commonly known as the rose garlic or pink garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is characterized by its mild garlic scent and is often grown for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Allium roseum is relatively low maintenance and attracts beneficial pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
