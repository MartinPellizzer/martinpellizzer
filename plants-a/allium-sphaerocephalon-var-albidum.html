Allium sphaerocephalon var. albidum

Allium sphaerocephalon var. albidum, commonly known as the white globe onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features rounded, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer, forming dense spherical clusters. The plant has strap-like, gray-green leaves that are narrow and grass-like in appearance. Its bulbs are small and white, often used in culinary applications for their mild, sweet flavor.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and edible qualities in gardens and cuisine.
