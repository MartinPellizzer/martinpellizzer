Alopecurus glaucus var. altaicus

Alopecurus glaucus var. altaicus, commonly known as the Altai bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
