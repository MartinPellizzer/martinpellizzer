Arundinaria murielae

Arundinaria murielae, commonly known as Muriel's bamboo, is a rare and endangered grass species native to the mountainous regions of southeastern China.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in panicles, and its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly hairy. Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.

This species is of particular interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
