Ampelopsis saint-paulii

Ampelopsis saint-paulii, commonly known as the Saint-Paul's vine, is a climbing plant native to China.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The leaves are alternate, simple, and typically heart-shaped with a glossy green surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and appear in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
