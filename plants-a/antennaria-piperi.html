Antennaria piperi

Antennaria piperi, commonly known as the purple sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves that are narrow and linear, often with a bluish-gray hue. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and they bloom in the spring.
