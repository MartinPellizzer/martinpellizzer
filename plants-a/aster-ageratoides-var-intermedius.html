Aster ageratoides var. intermedius

Aster ageratoides var. intermedius, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has hairy, gray-green foliage and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for garden borders or containers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, requiring minimal water once established.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
