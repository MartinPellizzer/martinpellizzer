Acer pseudoplatanus f. nervosum

Acer pseudoplatanus f. nervosum, commonly known as the hairy maple, is a distinctive variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its finely hairy leaves that give it a unique, textured appearance. The leaves are typically elongated and lobed, with a serrated margin. This form is often found in woodland areas where it thrives in partial shade.

Its botanical interest lies in its ornamental value and its role in ecological habitats.
