Acer wilsonii subsp. burmense

Acer wilsonii subsp.

burmense, commonly known as the Burmese maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which turn vibrant red or orange in the fall.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
