Arabis lyrata var. glabra

Arabis lyrata var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of smooth, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arid environments. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures.

The variety glabra distinguishes it by its lack of hair on the leaves and stems, giving it a smooth texture.
