Aulomyrcia cassinioides var. velutina

Aulomyrcia cassinioides var. velutina, commonly known as the velvety aulomyrcia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in understory layers of tropical forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
