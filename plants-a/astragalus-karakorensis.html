Astragalus karakorensis

Astragalus karakorensis, commonly known as Karakorensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Astragalus karakorensis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
