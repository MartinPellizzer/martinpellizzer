Achillea lanulosa f. rubicunda

Achillea lanulosa f. rubicunda, commonly known as the red-haired yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces showy clusters of small, pale yellow to reddish-orange flowers that bloom in summer. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, preferring cooler climates.
