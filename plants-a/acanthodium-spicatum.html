Acanthodium spicatum

Acanthodium spicatum, commonly known as the spiny acanthodium, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It typically features dense clusters of small, spiny bracts that enclose the actual flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Acanthodium spicatum is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environments.
