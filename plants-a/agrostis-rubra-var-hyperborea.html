Agrostis rubra var. hyperborea

Agrostis rubra var. hyperborea, commonly known as the Arctic meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to cold climates.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in alpine and Arctic regions. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with pale to reddish flowers.

The species is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.
