Aristida stipiformis

Aristida stipiformis, commonly known as the slender wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils, often found in pine woodlands and disturbed areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are characteristic of the grass family. It is adapted to thrive in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
