Adenostemma caffrum var. caffrum

Adenostemma caffrum var. caffrum, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, characterized by its erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple, which attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic, making it important to handle with care.

This species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential use in traditional medicine.
