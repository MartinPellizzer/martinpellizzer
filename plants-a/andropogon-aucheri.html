Andropogon aucheri

Andropogon aucheri, commonly known as the purple lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and purple-tinted foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often used in floral arrangements due to their ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native gardens and erosion control.

Andropogon aucheri is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
