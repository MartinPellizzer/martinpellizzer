Anoectochilus burmannicus

Anoectochilus burmannicus, commonly known as the Burmann's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, hairy rhizomes and its distinctive slipper-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

Anoectochilus burmannicus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
