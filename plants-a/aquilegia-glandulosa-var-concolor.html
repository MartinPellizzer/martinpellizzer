Aquilegia glandulosa var. concolor

Aquilegia glandulosa var. concolor, commonly known as the purple-flowered columbine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from violet to deep purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in woodland or rocky habitats and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
