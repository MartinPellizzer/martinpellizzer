Amphochaeta exaltata

Amphochaeta exaltata, commonly known as the tall sand grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a rough texture.

Amphochaeta exaltata thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand saline and wind conditions.
