Adiantum sinuosum var. minus

Adiantum sinuosum var. minus, commonly known as the lesser maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern native to the eastern United States.

It features finely divided, arching fronds with a soft, feathery appearance, typically reaching heights of 10 to 20 inches. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-drained, humus-rich soil. Its leaves are bi-lobed and often have a slightly curled appearance, giving it a distinctive, ornamental look.

It is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, shade-tolerant plant with a graceful aesthetic.
