Adiantum elegans

Adiantum elegans, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble the flowing hair of a maiden, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its fronds are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, arching shape.

Adiantum elegans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a humid, indoor environment.
