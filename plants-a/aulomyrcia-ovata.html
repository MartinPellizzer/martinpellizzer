Aulomyrcia ovata

Aulomyrcia ovata, commonly known as the oval-leaf myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain aromatic seeds.

Aulomyrcia ovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in its native regions.
