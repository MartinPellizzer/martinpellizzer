Athamanta vestita

Athamanta vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Athamanta vestita is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
