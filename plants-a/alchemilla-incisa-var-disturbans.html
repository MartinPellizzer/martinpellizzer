Alchemilla incisa var. disturbans

Alchemilla incisa var. disturbans, commonly known as the variable lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lobed, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

The varietal name 'disturbans' refers to its tendency to spread and colonize disturbed habitats.
