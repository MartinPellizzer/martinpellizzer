Aconitum acaule

Aconitum acaule, commonly known as the naked stem monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant features a short, unbranched stem that supports its leaves, which are palmately lobed and often hairy. Aconitum acaule is part of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice in gardens, though it should be planted with care.
