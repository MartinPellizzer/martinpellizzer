Aconitum × bavaricum nothosubsp. lusenense

Aconitum × bavaricum nothosubsp.

lusenense, commonly known as the Lusenense Monkshood, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its striking purple to blue, helmet-shaped flowers and dark green, deeply lobed leaves.

Native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade. It is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
