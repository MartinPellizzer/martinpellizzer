Allium paniculatum subsp. exaltatum

Allium paniculatum subsp.

exaltatum, commonly known as the tall onion or purple garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stem that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant produces dense, branched inflorescences of small, violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
