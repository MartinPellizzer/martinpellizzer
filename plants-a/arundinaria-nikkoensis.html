Arundinaria nikkoensis

Arundinaria nikkoensis, commonly known as the Nikko bamboo, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, erect stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in panicles, and its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly hairy. Arundinaria nikkoensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its graceful appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

It plays a role in traditional Japanese landscaping and is valued for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
