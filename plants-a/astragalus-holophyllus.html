Astragalus holophyllus

Astragalus holophyllus, commonly known as the full-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its root system is extensive, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus holophyllus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
