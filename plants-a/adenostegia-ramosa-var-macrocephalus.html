Adenostegia ramosa var. macrocephalus

Adenostegia ramosa var. macrocephalus, commonly known as the large-headed adenostegia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its branched, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow disc florets surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
