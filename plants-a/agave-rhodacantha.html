Agave rhodacantha

Agave rhodacantha, commonly known as the red agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped arrangement of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with a reddish tinge. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which appear after the plant has matured. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

The leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.
