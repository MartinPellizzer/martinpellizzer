Arum dioscoridis var. spectabile

Arum dioscoridis var. spectabile, commonly known as the showy arum, is a striking ornamental plant native to parts of Asia.

It features a distinctive, umbrella-shaped leaf that can grow up to 2 feet in diameter, with a deep green color and a prominent central vein. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, which can range from red to purple. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its dramatic appearance in gardens.

The plant is also noted for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a valuable and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
