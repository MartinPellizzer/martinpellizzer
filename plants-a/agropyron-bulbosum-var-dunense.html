Agropyron bulbosum var. dunense

Agropyron bulbosum var. dunense, commonly known as the Duneland needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the grasslands of Eastern Europe and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces spikelets that are arranged in dense clusters, with the inflorescence often appearing as a compact, cylindrical structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as steppes and sandy soils.

Agropyron bulbosum var. dunense is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
