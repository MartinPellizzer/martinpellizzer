Achlys japonica

Achlys japonica, commonly known as Japanese ghost flower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Achlys japonica is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
