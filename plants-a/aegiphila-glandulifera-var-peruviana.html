Aegiphila glandulifera var. peruviana

Aegiphila glandulifera var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian mountain mint, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features aromatic, hairy leaves with glandular dots, which contribute to its distinctive scent. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in high-altitude environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Lamiaceae family, known for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
