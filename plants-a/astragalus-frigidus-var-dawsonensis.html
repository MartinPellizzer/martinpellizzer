Astragalus frigidus var. dawsonensis

Astragalus frigidus var. dawsonensis, commonly known as Dawson's frostweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cold, arid conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces pods that contain small, dark seeds.

This variety is noted for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
