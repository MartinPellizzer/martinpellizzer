Abutilon fraseri subsp. fraseri

Abutilon fraseri subsp.

fraseri, commonly known as Fraser's abutilon, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly pubescent surface.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in arid and semi-arid regions.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering.
