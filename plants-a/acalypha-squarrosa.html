Acalypha squarrosa

Acalypha squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a fuzzy appearance, and its showy, often red or purple bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Acalypha squarrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
