Arrabidaea rosea

Arrabidaea rosea, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, often found on trees or rocks in humid environments. Its leaves are long and narrow, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
