Aucuba japonica var. luteomarginata

Aucuba japonica var. luteomarginata, commonly known as the golden margin aucuba, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features broad, glossy dark green leaves with striking yellow margins that create a vibrant contrast. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and is highly tolerant of urban conditions, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
