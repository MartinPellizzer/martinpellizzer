Astragalus elongatus subsp. elongatus

Astragalus elongatus subsp.

elongatus, commonly known as the longstem milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, with multiple pairs of leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
