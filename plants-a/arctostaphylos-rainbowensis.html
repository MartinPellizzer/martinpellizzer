Arctostaphylos rainbowensis

Arctostaphylos rainbowensis, commonly known as the Rainbow manzanita, is a species of flowering shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a valuable component of local ecosystems.
