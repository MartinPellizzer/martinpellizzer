Arabis hirsuta var. crassior

Arabis hirsuta var. crassior, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, lobed leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and are characterized by their four sepals and four petals.

The species is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh, mountainous environments.
