Acacia pubirhachis

Acacia pubirhachis, commonly known as the hairy wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes. The leaves are bipinnate, with numerous tiny leaflets that give the plant a feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
