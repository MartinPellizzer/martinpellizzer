Acioa librevillensis

Acioa librevillensis, commonly known as the Libreville acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central Africa, particularly found in countries like Gabon and Congo. The plant is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow or white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, open woodlands.

Acioa librevillensis is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
