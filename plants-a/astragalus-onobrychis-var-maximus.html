Astragalus onobrychis var. maximus

Astragalus onobrychis var. maximus, commonly known as the greater burr medic, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces pods that contain numerous small, hard seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

This species is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
