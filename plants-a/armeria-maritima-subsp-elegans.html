Armeria maritima subsp. elegans

Armeria maritima subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the coastal thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North America. It features dense clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and attractive floral display make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
