Achetaria caparaoensis

Achetaria caparaoensis, commonly known as the Caparaó orchid, is a rare and endemic species found in the Brazilian state of Espírito Santo.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within montane rainforests. Its leaves are elongated and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species.
