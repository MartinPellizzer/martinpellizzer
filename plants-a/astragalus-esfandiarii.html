Astragalus esfandiarii

Astragalus esfandiarii, commonly known as the Esfandiari's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, harsh environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
