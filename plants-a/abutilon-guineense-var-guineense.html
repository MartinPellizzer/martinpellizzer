Abutilon guineense var. guineense

Abutilon guineense var. guineense, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators.

The fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.
