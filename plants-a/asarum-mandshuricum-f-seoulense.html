Asarum mandshuricum f. seoulense

Asarum mandshuricum f. seoulense, commonly known as Seoul asarum, is a perennial herb native to Korea.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, irregularly shaped flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
