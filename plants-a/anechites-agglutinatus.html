Anechites agglutinatus

Anechites agglutinatus, commonly known as the sticky nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its small, green, sticky fruits that adhere to the skin and clothing of animals and humans. The plant produces violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Due to its toxic properties, it should not be ingested or handled without proper precautions.
