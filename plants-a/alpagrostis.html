Alpagrostis

Alpagrostis, commonly known as the needlegrass, is a genus of grasses belonging to the family Poaceae.

These plants are characterized by their slender, often tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves. Alpagrostis species are typically found in temperate regions, thriving in well-drained soils and open habitats. They play an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.

The genus includes several species, each adapted to different environmental conditions.
