Athamanta cervariifolia

Athamanta cervariifolia, commonly known as the featherleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and small, dark green leaves that are deeply lobed, resembling feathers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark, glossy berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in coastal and rocky environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
