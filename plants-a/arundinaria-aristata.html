Arundinaria aristata

Arundinaria aristata, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a species of grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with purple to brown flowers, which are typically borne in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more pronounced in the fall.

Arundinaria aristata is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
