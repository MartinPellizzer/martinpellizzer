Acalypha sonderiana

Acalypha sonderiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish, followed by distinctive, spiny seed capsules. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Acalypha sonderiana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.
