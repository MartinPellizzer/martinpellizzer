Azalea × magnifica

Azalea × magnifica, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring with flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, depending on the cultivar. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
