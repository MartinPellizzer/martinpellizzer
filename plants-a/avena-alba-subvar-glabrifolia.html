Avena alba subvar. glabrifolia

Avena alba subvar. glabrifolia, commonly known as slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
