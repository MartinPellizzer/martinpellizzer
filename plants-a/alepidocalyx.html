Alepidocalyx

Alepidocalyx, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy, pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. Alepidocalyx is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain environments.
