Acer campestre var. microphyllum

Acer campestre var. microphyllum, commonly known as the dwarf field maple, is a small deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that are smaller than those of the typical field maple. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in landscape design.
