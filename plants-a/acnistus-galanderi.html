Acnistus galanderi

Acnistus galanderi, commonly known as the silverleaf acnistus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the late winter to early spring. Its leaves are simple and opposite, with a velvety texture and a distinctive silver sheen.

Acnistus galanderi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
