Astragalus guanajuatensis

Astragalus guanajuatensis, commonly known as the Guanajuato milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its survival in its native habitat.

Astragalus guanajuatensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various insects and animals.
