Aloysia scorodonioides var. hypoleuca

Aloysia scorodonioides var. hypoleuca, commonly known as white verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, showy white or pale blue flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

White verbena is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in perfumery.
