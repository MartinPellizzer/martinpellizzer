Aulacolepis petelotii

Aulacolepis petelotii, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped spines and cylindrical stems that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. It produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
