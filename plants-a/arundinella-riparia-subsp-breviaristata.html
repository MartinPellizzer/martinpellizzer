Arundinella riparia subsp. breviaristata

Arundinella riparia subsp.

breviaristata, commonly known as the short-awned river reed, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
