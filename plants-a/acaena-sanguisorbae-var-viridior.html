Acaena sanguisorbae var. viridior

Acaena sanguisorbae var. viridior, commonly known as the green-flowered burrplant, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny fruit clusters that contain seeds surrounded by hooked bracts. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
