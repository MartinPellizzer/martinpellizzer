Arundinaria kokantsik

Arundinaria kokantsik, commonly known as the bamboo of the Caucasus, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green in color and covered in a fine layer of hair. Arundinaria kokantsik thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

It plays an important ecological role in its native environment, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
