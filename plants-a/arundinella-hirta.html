Arundinella hirta

Arundinella hirta, commonly known as the hairy reed, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Arundinella hirta is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
