Anisifolium curvispina

Anisifolium curvispina, commonly known as the hooked-leaf anise, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive anise-like aroma, and its foliage is characterized by serrated margins and hooked tips. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
