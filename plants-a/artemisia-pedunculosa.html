Artemisia pedunculosa

Artemisia pedunculosa, commonly known as slender wormwood, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It has a taproot system and is known for its strong, bitter scent.

Artemisia pedunculosa is often used in traditional medicine and is also a valuable plant for pollinators.
