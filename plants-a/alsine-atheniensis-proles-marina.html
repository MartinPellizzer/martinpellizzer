Alsine atheniensis proles marina

Alsine atheniensis proles marina, commonly known as the coastal alsike, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and succulent leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal dunes and salt marshes, where it tolerates high levels of salt and wind. Its root system helps stabilize sandy soils, preventing erosion.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays an important role in coastal ecosystems.
