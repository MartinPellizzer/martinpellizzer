Amorphophallus annulifer

Amorphophallus annulifer, commonly known as the giant taro, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and a striking, spadix-like inflorescence that can reach several feet in height. The plant produces a swollen underground corm, which is used as a food source in some regions. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

The plant's botanical features make it both ecologically significant and economically valuable in its native habitat.
