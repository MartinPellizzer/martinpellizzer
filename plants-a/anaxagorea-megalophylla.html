Anaxagorea megalophylla

Anaxagorea megalophylla, commonly known as the giant-leaf sterculia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant belongs to the family Sterculiaceae and is known for its showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange. Its bark is thick and deeply fissured, and it can grow to significant heights in its natural habitat.

Anaxagorea megalophylla is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
