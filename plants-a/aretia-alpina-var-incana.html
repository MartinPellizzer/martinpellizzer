Aretia alpina var. incana

Aretia alpina var. incana, commonly known as the alpine aretia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cold, high-altitude climates. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in meadows and slopes at elevations above 2,000 meters.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, arid conditions typical of its natural habitat.
