Alphonsea glandulosa

Alphonsea glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular alphonse, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical leaves with glandular dots along the midrib, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Alphonsea glandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
