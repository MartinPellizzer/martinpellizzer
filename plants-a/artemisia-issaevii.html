Artemisia issaevii

Artemisia issaevii, commonly known as the Siberian wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments. Its leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its strong, medicinal scent.

Artemisia issaevii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and its potential use in traditional medicine.
