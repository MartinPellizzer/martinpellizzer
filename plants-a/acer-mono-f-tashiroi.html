Acer mono f. tashiroi

Acer mono f. tashiroi, commonly known as the Tashiroi maple, is a unique variant of the Japanese maple.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display a combination of green, white, and red hues. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its foliage changes color throughout the seasons, offering vibrant displays in autumn.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.
