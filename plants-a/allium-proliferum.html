Allium proliferum

Allium proliferum, commonly known as the tree onion or wild leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces umbel-like clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant's bulbs, which resemble those of garlic or onions, are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Allium proliferum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
