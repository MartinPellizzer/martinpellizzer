Astragalus parviflorus var. microlobus

Astragalus parviflorus var. microlobus, commonly known as the small-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus parviflorus var. microlobus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
