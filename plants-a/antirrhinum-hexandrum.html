Antirrhinum hexandrum

Antirrhinum hexandrum, commonly known as the six-stamen snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The flowers typically range in color from pink to red and are adapted for pollination by insects such as bees and butterflies. The plant is notable for its unique floral structure, which includes six stamens, giving it the specific epithet "hexandrum.".

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
