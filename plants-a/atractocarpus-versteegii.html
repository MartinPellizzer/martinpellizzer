Atractocarpus versteegii

Atractocarpus versteegii, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

This species is often found in dry to moist forests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
