Astragalus stenocalyx

Astragalus stenocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus stenocalyx is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
