Argemone ochroleuca subsp. stenopetala

Argemone ochroleuca subsp.

stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled argemone, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, typically growing in arid and semi-arid regions.

The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in dry climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
