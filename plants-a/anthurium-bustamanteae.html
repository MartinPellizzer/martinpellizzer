Anthurium bustamanteae

Anthurium bustamanteae, commonly known as the Bustamante's anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface and vibrant, colorful spathes that can range from red to orange. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium bustamanteae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
