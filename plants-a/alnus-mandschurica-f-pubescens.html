Alnus mandschurica f. pubescens

Alnus mandschurica f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent Manchurian alder, is a variant of the Manchurian alder native to eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its hairy, pubescent bark and foliage. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with serrated margins. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or wetlands.
