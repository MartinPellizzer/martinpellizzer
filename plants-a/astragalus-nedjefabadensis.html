Astragalus nedjefabadensis

Astragalus nedjefabadensis, commonly known as the Nedjefabad milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid regions of central Asia, particularly found in Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, compound leaves, and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

Astragalus nedjefabadensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
