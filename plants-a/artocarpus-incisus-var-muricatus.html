Artocarpus incisus var. muricatus

Artocarpus incisus var. muricatus, commonly known as the rough-fig or wild fig, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, thorny bark and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The fruit, which is typically green when unripe, turns yellow or red when mature and contains a sweet, edible pulp. The plant is often cultivated for its fruit and has medicinal uses in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.
