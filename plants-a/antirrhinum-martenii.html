Antirrhinum martenii

Antirrhinum martenii, commonly known as the Marten's snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by insects, particularly bees, due to their shape and coloration.

Antirrhinum martenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
