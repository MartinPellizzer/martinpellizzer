Ardisia breviflora

Ardisia breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces bright red berries that are a striking contrast to its glossy green foliage. Ardisia breviflora is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and compact growth habit.

It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.
