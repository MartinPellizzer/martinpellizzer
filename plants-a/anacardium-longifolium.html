Anacardium longifolium

Anacardium longifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian chestnut or long-leaved cashew, is a species of flowering plant in the Anacardiaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, sweet-tasting fruits. The fruit's outer husk contains a toxic resin, while the inner kernel is rich in nutrients.

It is often cultivated for its fruit and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
