Ammannia erecta f. villosa

Ammannia erecta f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy ammannia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, erect growth habit and clustered inflorescences. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" appearance. It produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and unique aesthetic appeal in gardens.
