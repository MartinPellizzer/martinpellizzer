Artemisia cernua

Artemisia cernua, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, aromatic yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in traditional herbal practices for its purported cleansing and purifying properties.

Its essential oils contain compounds like camphor and thujone, which contribute to its strong scent and medicinal uses.
