Anacheilium faresianum

Anacheilium faresianum, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a rare and unique plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that bloom in a spiral arrangement. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to the dry conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
