Aster venustus

Aster venustus, commonly known as the showy aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Aster venustus is often cultivated in gardens for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
