Agave unguiculata

Agave unguiculata, commonly known as the black agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are often blue-green in color. The leaves are lined with sharp spines and have a distinctive dark purple or black coloration at the base. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which appear after several years of growth.

This plant is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.
