Anthurium acutissimum var. maius

Anthurium acutissimum var. maius, commonly known as the giant spathiphyllum, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length. The plant produces striking, waxy, red or orange spathes that enclose a spadix, which is often white or yellow. It thrives in high humidity and bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant and in botanical gardens.
