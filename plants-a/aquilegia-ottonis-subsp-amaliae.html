Aquilegia ottonis subsp. amaliae

Aquilegia ottonis subsp.

amaliae, commonly known as the Amalia's columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that typically range in color from blue to purple.

The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it thrives in well-drained, loamy soils in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it adapts well to cooler, high-altitude environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in gardens and botanical collections.
