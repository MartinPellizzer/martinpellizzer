Andropogon seemenianus

Andropogon seemenianus, commonly known as the tall bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. This grass thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
