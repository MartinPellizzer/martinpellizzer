Ardisia crassipes

Ardisia crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved evergreen ardisia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, white flowers followed by bright red berries. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its berries are a food source for various birds and mammals.

Ardisia crassipes is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
