Asplenium mathewsii

Asplenium mathewsii, commonly known as the Mathews' spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rock crevices or under forest canopies. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, arranged in a distinctive row along the veins.

Asplenium mathewsii is a relatively rare species, often found in specific microhabitats across Southeast Asia.
