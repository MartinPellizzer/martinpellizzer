Aglaia vulpina

Aglaia vulpina, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces fruit that resembles an apple in shape but is typically smaller and has a yellow or orange hue. The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, aromatic pulp.

Aglaia vulpina is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
