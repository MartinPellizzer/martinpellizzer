Anemopaegma glaucum var. lanceifolia

Anemopaegma glaucum var. lanceifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved anemopaegma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-white coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to xeriscapes and native plant collections.
