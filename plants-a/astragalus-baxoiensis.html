Astragalus baxoiensis

Astragalus baxoiensis, commonly known as the Baxoi milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Astragalus baxoiensis plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
