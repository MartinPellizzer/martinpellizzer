Aster pseudamellus

Aster pseudamellus, commonly known as the false amella aster, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Aster pseudamellus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
