Alnus incana f. blyttiana

Alnus incana f. blyttiana, commonly known as the northern silver birch, is a subspecies of the common alder.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to northern regions of Europe and Asia. The plant is characterized by its white, peeling bark and small, catkin-like flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in nitrogen fixation and habitat provision for various wildlife species.
