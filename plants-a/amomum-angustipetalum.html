Amomum angustipetalum

Amomum angustipetalum, commonly known as narrow-petaled cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces dark brown, aromatic seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas at elevations above 1,000 meters.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and commercial purposes.
