Anthochytrum alpinum

Anthochytrum alpinum, commonly known as the alpine chytrid, is a species of aquatic fungus belonging to the order Chytridiales.

It is typically found in freshwater environments, particularly in cold, oligotrophic lakes and streams at high altitudes. The organism is characterized by its small, motile zoospores that are equipped with a single flagellum, allowing them to swim through water. It plays a role in the decomposition of organic matter in its habitat.

Due to its specialized ecological niche, it is not widely studied, but it contributes to the ecological balance of alpine aquatic ecosystems.
