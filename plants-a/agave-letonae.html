Agave letonae

Agave letonae, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, blue-gray leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces a tall, spire-like flower stalk that can reach several meters in height. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.
