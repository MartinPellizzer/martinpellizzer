Ardisia mollis

Ardisia mollis, commonly known as the glossy woodbine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant is evergreen, with a shrub-like growth habit, and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its berries are red or orange when mature and serve as a food source for various birds and small mammals.

Ardisia mollis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
