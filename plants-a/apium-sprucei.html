Apium sprucei

Apium sprucei, commonly known as the Spruce celery, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as parsley and dill. The plant is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and small, clustered umbels of white flowers. Its leaves are finely divided and have a mild, celery-like aroma.

Apium sprucei is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
