Astragalus incanus var. stenocarpus

Astragalus incanus var. stenocarpus, commonly known as the narrowpod milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, hot environments. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers in early summer. Its fruit is a narrow, elongated pod that contains several seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed or open habitats such as plains, prairies, and along roadsides.
