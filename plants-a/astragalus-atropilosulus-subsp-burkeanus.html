Astragalus atropilosulus subsp. burkeanus

Astragalus atropilosulus subsp.

burkeanus, commonly known as the Burkean milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has deep taproots that help it survive in arid conditions.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native range.
