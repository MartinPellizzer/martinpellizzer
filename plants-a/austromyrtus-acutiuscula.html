Austromyrtus acutiuscula

Austromyrtus acutiuscula, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, whitish bloom. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Austromyrtus acutiuscula is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also valued for its aromatic properties.
