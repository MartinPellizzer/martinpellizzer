Anagallis arvensis var. platyphylla

Anagallis arvensis var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in disturbed soils such as fields, roadsides, and waste areas. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, and its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds.

This species is often found in moist, sunny habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
