Aconitum delphiniifolium subsp. paradoxum

Aconitum delphiniifolium subsp.

paradoxum, commonly known as the Paradoxical Monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and its stems are typically hairy and upright. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is often cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
