Agrimonia pilosa f. nipponica

Agrimonia pilosa f. nipponica, commonly known as Japanese agrimony, is a variant of the agrimony plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, bead-like fruits that are covered in hooked bristles, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a rough texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in meadows and woodland edges.
