Alsine obtusa

Alsine obtusa, commonly known as broad-leaved alsike, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It is herbaceous, with a fibrous root system, and is often found in temperate regions.

Alsine obtusa is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in supporting local insect populations.
