Ailanthus scripta

Ailanthus scripta, commonly known as the Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, bright red berries that attract birds. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions. It produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers in the spring.

Ailanthus scripta is often used in ornamental landscaping but can become invasive in some regions.
