Aster linariifolius f. lateralis

Aster linariifolius f. lateralis, commonly known as the silverleaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features distinctive silver-white, velvety foliage that gives it its common name, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers in the late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
