Acanthopsis adamanticola

Acanthopsis adamanticola, commonly known as the Adamant's thorn, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red and yellow. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment. Its unique morphology and resilience have made it a subject of interest in botanical research.

However, due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
