Arum lineatum

Arum lineatum, commonly known as the zigzag arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves with a zigzag pattern along the midrib. The plant produces a striking inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forests.

Arum lineatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
