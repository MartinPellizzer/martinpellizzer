Acanthocalycium rhodotrichum subsp. rhodotrichum

Acanthocalycium rhodotrichum subsp.

rhodotrichum, commonly known as the red-ribbed hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green with red or purple ribs, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
