Antennaria eximia

Antennaria eximia, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine, or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its basal leaves are spoon-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, while the flowering stems are slender and often branched.

Antennaria eximia is often found in open habitats such as meadows and open woodlands at high elevations.
