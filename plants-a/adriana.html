Adriana

Adriana, commonly known as the Adriana flower, is a rare and exotic plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It features delicate, lavender-colored petals that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy to the touch. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

Botanically, Adriana belongs to the genus *Adriana* within the family *Amaryllidaceae*, showcasing unique floral structures and medicinal properties.
