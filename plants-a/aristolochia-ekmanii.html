Aristolochia ekmanii

Aristolochia ekmanii, commonly known as the African Birthwort, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It features heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and produces intricate, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, musky scent. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure that resembles a pipe or a horn. It contains aristolochic acids, which are toxic compounds that have been historically used in traditional medicine.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
