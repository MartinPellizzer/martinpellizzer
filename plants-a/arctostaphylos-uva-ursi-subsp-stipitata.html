Arctostaphylos uva-ursi subsp. stipitata

Arctostaphylos uva-ursi subsp.

stipitata, commonly known as bearberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to North America. It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a whitish, waxy coating.

The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for wildlife, including bears and birds. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its aromatic foliage and hardy nature make it a popular choice for landscaping in shady, moist environments.
