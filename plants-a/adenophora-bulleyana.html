Adenophora bulleyana

Adenophora bulleyana, commonly known as the Bulleyan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Adenophora bulleyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
