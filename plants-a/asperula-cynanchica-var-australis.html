Asperula cynanchica var. australis

Asperula cynanchica var. australis, commonly known as the Australian bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a glossy green surface and a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
