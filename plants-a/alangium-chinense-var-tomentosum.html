Alangium chinense var. tomentosum

Alangium chinense var. tomentosum, commonly known as the hairy alangium, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by red, fleshy berries that contain several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been used in traditional medicine.
