Angianthus micropodioides var. filaginoides

Angianthus micropodioides var. filaginoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf angianthus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish flowers arranged in compact clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. It is often found in coastal and inland regions with a Mediterranean climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially available.
