Allium cathodicarpum

Allium cathodicarpum, commonly known as the purple garlic.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its bulbous stem is characterized by a strong garlic aroma and is used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Allium cathodicarpum make it an important species in the Allium genus.
