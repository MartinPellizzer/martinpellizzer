Alchornea trewioides var. loochooensis

Alchornea trewioides var. loochooensis, commonly known as the Loochoo alchorne, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
