Atriplex foliosa

Atriplex foliosa, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in dry environments. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in saline soils. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces edible seeds that are consumed by various wildlife species.

Atriplex foliosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage in harsh climates.
