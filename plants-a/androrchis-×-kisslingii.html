Androrchis × kisslingii

Androrchis × kisslingii, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Androrchis alpina and Androrchis vernalis.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a delicate and endangered species in its native range.
