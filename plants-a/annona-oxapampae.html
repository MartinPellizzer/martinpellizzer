Annona oxapampae

Annona oxapampae, commonly known as the Amazonian soursop, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are typically clustered in groups of three. Its fruit is a green, spiky berry that turns yellow when ripe, containing a sweet, creamy pulp and numerous black seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and its edible fruit, which is rich in nutrients.
