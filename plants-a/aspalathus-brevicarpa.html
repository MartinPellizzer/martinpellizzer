Aspalathus brevicarpa

Aspalathus brevicarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited bushveld clover, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid environments.

Aspalathus brevicarpa plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
