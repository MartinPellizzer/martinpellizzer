Andrographis chendurunii

Andrographis chendurunii, commonly known as the hill andrographis, is a herbaceous plant native to southern India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves and stems are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
