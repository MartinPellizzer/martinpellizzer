Astragalus triangularis

Astragalus triangularis, commonly known as the triangular milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus triangularis is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stabilization.
