Aptenia cordifolia

Aptenia cordifolia, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.
