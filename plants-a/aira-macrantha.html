Aira macrantha

Aira macrantha, commonly known as the purple air plant, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces slender, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. Its small, clustered flower heads are usually pink or purple, adding ornamental value to its appearance.

It is valued in gardens for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
