Adenanthos glabrescens subsp. exasperatus

Adenanthos glabrescens subsp.

exasperatus, commonly known as the rough-leaved adenanthos, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is a shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and hairy, often glandular stems and leaves.

The leaves are typically narrow, linear to lanceolate, and have a rough or scabrous texture. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.
