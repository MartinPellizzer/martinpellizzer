Arundinaria wightiana var. hispida

Arundinaria wightiana var. hispida, commonly known as the hispid bamboo, is a variety of bamboo native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, giving the plant its "hispida" designation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional crafts in its native region.
