Abelia umbellata

Abelia umbellata, commonly known as the Chinese Abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Abelia umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
