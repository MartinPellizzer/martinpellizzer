Astragalus gudrunensis

Astragalus gudrunensis, commonly known as Gudrun's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Astragalus gudrunensis is considered a rare and endangered species.
