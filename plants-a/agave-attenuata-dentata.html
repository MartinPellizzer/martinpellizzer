Agave attenuata dentata

Agave attenuata dentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features narrow, blue-gray leaves with a rough, toothed margin, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with white to pale yellow flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
