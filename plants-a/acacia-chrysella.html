Acacia chrysella

Acacia chrysella, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. Acacia chrysella is also known for its role in the Australian landscape and its use in traditional Indigenous practices.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.
