Albizia eymae

Albizia eymae, commonly known as the pink wisteria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and its showy, pendulous inflorescences that produce clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant produces flat, winged pods that contain several seeds.

Albizia eymae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
