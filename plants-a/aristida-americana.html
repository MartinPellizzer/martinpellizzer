Aristida americana

Aristida americana, commonly known as little bluestem, is a native North American grass species that thrives in a variety of soil types, particularly in prairie and woodland environments.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and its ability to form dense, tufted clumps. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Little bluestem is drought-tolerant and plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting wildlife.

It is often used in native plant gardens and restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and aesthetic appeal.
