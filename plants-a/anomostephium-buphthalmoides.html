Anomostephium buphthalmoides

Anomostephium buphthalmoides, commonly known as the false globe thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, globe-shaped flower heads that resemble those of the true globe thistle. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, with a grayish-green color.

The flowers are yellowish-green and appear in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
