Aquilaria pubescens

Aquilaria pubescens, commonly known as the hairy agarwood tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Thymelaeaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resinous wood, which is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine pubescence, giving it its common name.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
