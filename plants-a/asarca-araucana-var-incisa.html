Asarca araucana var. incisa

Asarca araucana var. incisa, commonly known as the Araucaria incisa, is a variant of the Araucaria tree native to Chile.

It is a coniferous evergreen with a distinctive pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and is adapted to the temperate climate of southern South America. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against environmental stressors.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
