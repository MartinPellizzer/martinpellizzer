Acinos alpinus var. nebrodensis

Acinos alpinus var. nebrodensis, commonly known as the Nebroden alpine acino, is a flowering plant native to the Alps and surrounding mountainous regions.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are typically narrow and elongated. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
