Alopecurus geniculatus var. fulvus

Alopecurus geniculatus var. fulvus, commonly known as the golden mouse-ear grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive golden-tinged leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is typically found in moist meadows, along streambanks, and in other damp habitats across Europe and parts of Asia.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local insect populations.
