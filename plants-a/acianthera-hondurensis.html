Acianthera hondurensis

Acianthera hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduras acianthera, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in the understory of tropical forests. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil, and it plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by providing nectar for pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
