Anemone coronaria proles lerinensis

Anemone coronaria proles lerinensis, commonly known as the Lerins Island anemone, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are hairy and lobed.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
