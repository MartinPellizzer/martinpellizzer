Acriopsis liliifolia var. auriculata

Acriopsis liliifolia var. auriculata, commonly known as the golden ear plant, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a golden hue, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
