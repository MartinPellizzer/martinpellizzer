Arctostaphylos officinalis

Arctostaphylos officinalis, commonly known as the bearberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that remain green throughout the year. The plant produces small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a preference for acidic, well-drained soils.

Arctostaphylos officinalis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
