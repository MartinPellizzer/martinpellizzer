Acacia beckleri

Acacia beckleri, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and distinctive yellow flowers that appear in winter. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves, and it produces small, pod-like fruits. Acacia beckleri thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
