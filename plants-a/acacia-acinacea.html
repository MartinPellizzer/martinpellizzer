Acacia acinacea

Acacia acinacea, commonly known as the hooded acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often reduced to small, spiny structures, and its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in globular or cylindrical heads. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia acinacea is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert animals.
