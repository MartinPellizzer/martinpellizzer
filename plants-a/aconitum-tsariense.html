Aconitum tsariense

Aconitum tsariense, commonly known as the Siberian monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Aconitum tsariense is known for its toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
