Adiantum laxum

Adiantum laxum, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that give it a graceful appearance. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Adiantum laxum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.
