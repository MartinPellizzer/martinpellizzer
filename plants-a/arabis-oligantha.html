Arabis oligantha

Arabis oligantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant has basal leaves that are narrow and linear, and its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, with four petals. It blooms in early spring, producing a cluster of flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Arabis oligantha is a perennial species that is well adapted to cold and harsh environmental conditions.
