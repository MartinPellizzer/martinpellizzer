Androcalva fragifolia

Androcalva fragifolia, commonly known as the fragrant leaf orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

Androcalva fragifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
