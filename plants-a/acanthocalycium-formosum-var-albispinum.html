Acanthocalycium formosum var. albispinum

Acanthocalycium formosum var. albispinum, commonly known as the White-spined Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a succulent cactus characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, bristly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
