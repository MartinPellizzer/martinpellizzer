Acalypha vagans

Acalypha vagans, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silkyleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Acalypha vagans is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
