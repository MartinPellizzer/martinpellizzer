Arnica montana var. fulgens

Arnica montana var. fulgens, commonly known as the fiery mountain arnica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its bright yellow-orange flowers, which are typically arranged in a dense, spherical cluster. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, sunny soils. It is often found in meadows and rocky areas at high altitudes.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically for treating bruises and inflammation.
