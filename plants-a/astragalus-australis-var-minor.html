Astragalus australis var. minor

Astragalus australis var. minor, commonly known as the small-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands and shrublands. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces small, yellow to pale green flowers in late spring to early summer. It is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through its root nodules.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
