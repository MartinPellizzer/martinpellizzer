Allium atriphoeniceum

Allium atriphoeniceum, commonly known as the purple onion, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of purple to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used as ornamental plants in gardens. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, growing from a basal rosette.

Allium atriphoeniceum is valued for its attractive appearance and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
