Acer negundo f. discolor

Acer negundo f. discolor, commonly known as the white ash, is a cultivar of the ash tree characterized by its distinctive white or pale green foliage.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 15 to 25 meters, featuring a broad, rounded canopy. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with serrated edges and a smooth, waxy texture. It produces small, yellow-green flowers in early spring, followed by samaroid fruits that disperse its seeds.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
