Achillea erba-rotta subsp. olympica

Achillea erba-rotta subsp.

olympica, commonly known as Olympic yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Greece and Turkey. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.

Its common name reflects its association with the Olympic region, where it is often found in alpine habitats.
