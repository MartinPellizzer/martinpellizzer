Aster grimmii

Aster grimmii, commonly known as the Sierra aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, and it typically blooms in late summer to early fall. The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats, such as meadows and slopes, and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and sometimes branched.

Aster grimmii is an important species for pollinators and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
