Asplenium potosinum var. semipinnatum

Asplenium potosinum var. semipinnatum, commonly known as the Mexican maidenhair fern, is a variant of the asplenium genus characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds.

It is native to Mexico and is adapted to humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features finely divided, bi- or tri-pinnate leaves that give it a feathery appearance. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing sorus, are typically located on the undersides of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
