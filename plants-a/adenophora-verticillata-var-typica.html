Adenophora verticillata var. typica

Adenophora verticillata var. typica, commonly known as the whorled bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces showy blue to purple bell-shaped flowers in late summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers are typically arranged in whorls of three to five, giving it its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
