Amanoa guianensis var. genuina

Amanoa guianensis var. genuina, commonly known as the Guianan moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its fruits are berries that turn red when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its nocturnal blooming habit.
