Aechmea froesii

Aechmea froesii, commonly known as the Froes's bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored bracts, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its leaves are typically broad and covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention.

Aechmea froesii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and adaptability to indoor environments.
