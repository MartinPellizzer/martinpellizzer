Asplenium sorzogonense var. majus

Asplenium sorzogonense var. majus, commonly known as the Philippine water fern, is a variant of the water fern genus Asplenium.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit with fronds that are pinnately divided and often have a dark green, glossy appearance. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

This variety is native to the Philippines and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitat.
