Astragalus crotalariae var. menziesii

Astragalus crotalariae var. menziesii, commonly known as the Menzies' milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and rocky soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its seeds are small and typically dispersed by wind or animals.
