Amorphophallus carnosus

Amorphophallus carnosus, commonly known as the giant Taro, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to three meters in length, and its underground tubers are the primary part used for culinary purposes. The plant's most striking characteristic is its massive, spadix inflorescence, which can reach heights of over three meters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its edible tubers.

The botanical structure of A. carnosus includes a complex system of rhizomes and stolons that aid in its vegetative propagation.
