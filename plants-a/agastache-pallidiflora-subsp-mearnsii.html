Agastache pallidiflora subsp. mearnsii

Agastache pallidiflora subsp.

mearnsii, commonly known as Mearns' gayfeather, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features tall, slender stems that bear whorled leaves and spikes of pale lavender to white flowers.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic, mint-scented foliage, which is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
