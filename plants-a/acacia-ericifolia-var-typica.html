Acacia ericifolia var. typica

Acacia ericifolia var. typica, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, dense foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a woody stem with thorn-like structures, which help it survive in harsh, saline environments. Its leaves are small and leathery, reducing water loss in arid conditions.

This species is often used in coastal stabilization due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in sandy soils.
