Alchemilla pubescens var. glaucescens

Alchemilla pubescens var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-stemmed lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features silver-gray, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often arranged in a rosette at the base. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in dense, branched clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
