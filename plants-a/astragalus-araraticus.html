Astragalus araraticus

Astragalus araraticus, commonly known as the Armenian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its compound leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often hairy.

Astragalus araraticus is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
