Aegilops vulgaritriaristata

Aegilops vulgaritriaristata, commonly known as the three-awn grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted stems and the presence of three awns on its spikelets, which are bristle-like structures. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces inflorescences that are spike-like in structure.

Aegilops vulgaritriaristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.

Morphology

Aegilops vulgaritriaristata has leaves that are narrow and grass-like, with a linear shape, alternate arrangement, and a bluish-green color.

The stem is slender, cylindrical, and slightly hairy, with a light green color and a growth pattern that is upright and branching. The flowers are small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets, blooming in early summer, and are wind-pollinated. The fruit is a caryopsis, and the seeds are dispersed by wind due to their lightweight structure.
