Alchemilla argyrophylla subsp. triphylloides

Alchemilla argyrophylla subsp.

triphylloides, commonly known as silverleaf lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its foliage is used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
