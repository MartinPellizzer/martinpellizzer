Aphanactis ollgaardii

Aphanactis ollgaardii, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wallaby grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 millimeters wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Aphanactis ollgaardii is an important component of native grassland ecosystems and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
