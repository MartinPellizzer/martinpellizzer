Amaracarpus kusaiensis

Amaracarpus kusaiensis, commonly known as the Japanese amaranth, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, open habitats.

Amaracarpus kusaiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
