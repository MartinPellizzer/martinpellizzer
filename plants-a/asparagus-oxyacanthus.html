Asparagus oxyacanthus

Asparagus oxyacanthus, commonly known as the white asparagus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
