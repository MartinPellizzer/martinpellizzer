Arisaema urashima

Arisaema urashima, commonly known as the Urashima arrowleaf, is a striking member of the Araceae family native to Japan.

It is characterized by its unique, arrow-shaped leaves and an unusual, spadix-like inflorescence that resembles a bird's beak. The plant produces a cluster of small, greenish-white flowers enclosed within a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
