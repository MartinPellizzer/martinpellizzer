Atriplex mendozaensis

Atriplex mendozaensis, commonly known as Mendoza saltbush, is a shrub native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, salt-tolerant leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often in saline soils. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits containing single seeds.

Atriplex mendozaensis is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
