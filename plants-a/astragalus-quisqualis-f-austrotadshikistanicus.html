Astragalus quisqualis f. austrotadshikistanicus

Astragalus quisqualis f. austrotadshikistanicus, commonly known as the Tadzhikistan milkvetch, is a rare botanical variant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and mountainous regions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and may have potential medicinal uses, though further research is needed.

Its limited distribution makes it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
