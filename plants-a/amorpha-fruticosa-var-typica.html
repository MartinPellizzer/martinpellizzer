Amorpha fruticosa var. typica

Amorpha fruticosa var. typica, commonly known as yellow indigo bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces bright yellow to orange flower clusters in the spring. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and clay soils. It is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, such as providing nectar for pollinators.
