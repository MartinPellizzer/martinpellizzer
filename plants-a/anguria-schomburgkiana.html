Anguria schomburgkiana

Anguria schomburgkiana, commonly known as the Schomburgkian cucumber, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, green fruits that are typically harvested when they are still immature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Botanically, it is closely related to other members of the Cucurbitaceae family, sharing similar structural and reproductive features.
