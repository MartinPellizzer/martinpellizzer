Astragalus tauczilikensis

Astragalus tauczilikensis, commonly known as the Tauczilik milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
