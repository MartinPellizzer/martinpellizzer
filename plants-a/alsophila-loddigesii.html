Alsophila loddigesii

Alsophila loddigesii, commonly known as the Loddiges' tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizome system supports the development of its woody trunk, while the fronds are typically bioluminescent in certain species.

Alsophila loddigesii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and air-purifying properties.
