Acaciella bicolor

Acaciella bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its unique, bipinnate leaves that are often green with a white or pale underside. The plant produces small yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acaciella bicolor is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Its ecological significance includes providing food and habitat for various desert organisms.
