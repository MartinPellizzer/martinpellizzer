Alyssum calycinum var. parisiense

Alyssum calycinum var. parisiense, commonly known as Parisian alpine alyssum, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow, linear, and also hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine beds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
