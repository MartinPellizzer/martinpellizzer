Astragalus caroli-henrici

Astragalus caroli-henrici, commonly known as Caroli-Henrici milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Astragalus caroli-henrici is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
