Astragalus glycyphylloides subsp. serbicus

Astragalus glycyphylloides subsp.

serbicus, commonly known as the Serbian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to southeastern Europe. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in regions such as the Balkans and parts of Turkey.

The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in early summer. Its fruit is a hairy pod that contains several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
