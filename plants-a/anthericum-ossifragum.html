Anthericum ossifragum

Anthericum ossifragum, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of white or pale blue. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Anthericum ossifragum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a border plant.
