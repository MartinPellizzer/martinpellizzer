Abies conica

Abies conica, commonly known as the dwarf fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and dense foliage, which gives it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The tree has needle-like leaves that are short and stout, with a bluish-green color and a waxy coating. Its cones are small and cylindrical, typically maturing to a dark reddish-brown.

Abies conica is well adapted to cold climates and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic value and resilience.
