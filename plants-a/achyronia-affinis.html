Achyronia affinis

Achyronia affinis, commonly known as the golden-eyed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that feature a golden-yellow lip with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

The orchid's pollination is primarily facilitated by specific insect species, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
