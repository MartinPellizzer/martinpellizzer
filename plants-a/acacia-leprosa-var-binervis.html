Acacia leprosa var. binervis

Acacia leprosa var. binervis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features slender, often twisted stems and bipinnate leaves with two rows of small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that appear in spring, attracting various pollinators. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
