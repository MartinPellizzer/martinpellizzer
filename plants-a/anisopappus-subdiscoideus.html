Anisopappus subdiscoideus

Anisopappus subdiscoideus, commonly known as the desert starflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small stars. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its small, rounded leaves and unique floral structure make it a distinctive and resilient species in its native habitat.
