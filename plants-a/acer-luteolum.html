Acer luteolum

Acer luteolum, commonly known as the yellow maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow leaves, which turn orange-red in the fall. The plant has a slender, upright growth habit and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges and a smooth surface.

Acer luteolum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
