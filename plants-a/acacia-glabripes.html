Acacia glabripes

Acacia glabripes, commonly known as the smooth acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its bipinnate leaves, which are finely divided and give the plant a delicate appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
