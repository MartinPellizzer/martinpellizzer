Anaphalis elliptica

Anaphalis elliptica, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and fluffy, woolly inflorescences that resemble small clouds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow and bloom in the summer months.

Anaphalis elliptica is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
