Antennaria japonica

Antennaria japonica, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing white or pale pink flowers that resemble tiny bells. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Antennaria japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
