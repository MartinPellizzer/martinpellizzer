Allium sivasicum

Allium sivasicum, commonly known as the Sivash bulb, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a tall flowering stem bearing umbels of white or pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

Allium sivasicum is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments and is relatively drought-tolerant.
