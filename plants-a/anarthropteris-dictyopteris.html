Anarthropteris dictyopteris

Anarthropteris dictyopteris, commonly known as the fringed water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to the wetlands and marshes of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that are often divided into narrow, ribbon-like segments. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Anarthropteris dictyopteris plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
