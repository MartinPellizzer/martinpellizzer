Agrimonia striata

Agrimonia striata, commonly known as the striped agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its fruits are dry, capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

Agrimonia striata is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies for digestive and skin ailments.
