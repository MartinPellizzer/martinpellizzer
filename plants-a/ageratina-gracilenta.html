Ageratina gracilenta

Ageratina gracilenta, commonly known as slender ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces fluffy, white to pale blue flower clusters, which are often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, giving it a delicate appearance.

Ageratina gracilenta thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, decorative plant.
