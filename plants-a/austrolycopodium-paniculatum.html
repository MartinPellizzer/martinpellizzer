Austrolycopodium paniculatum

Austrolycopodium paniculatum, commonly known as the mountain clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping rhizomes and erect, branched stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the spores for reproduction. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions of southern South America.

Austrolycopodium paniculatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
