Aralia osyana

Aralia osyana, commonly known as the Siberian aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of Eastern Europe and Siberia.

It features compound leaves that are typically pinnately arranged, with slender, elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clustered inflorescences, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its root system is extensive and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained, loamy soils.

Aralia osyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal display.
