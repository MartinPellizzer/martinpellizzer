Agapetes grandiflora

Agapetes grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered silver fir, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often in cloud forests. Its leaves are leathery and emit a subtle fragrance, while its flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in late summer.

Agapetes grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
