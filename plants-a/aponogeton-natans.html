Aponogeton natans

Aponogeton natans, commonly known as the water arrowhead, is a floating aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern on the water's surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water, followed by elongated, ribbon-like fruits. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its rhizomes can form dense mats, which can affect water flow and aquatic ecosystems.
