Aechmea bauxilumii

Aechmea bauxilumii, commonly known as the Bauxilumii Bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients from the environment. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences with colorful bracts that can range from red to orange. Its foliage is often adorned with silver or white markings, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Aechmea bauxilumii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Morphology

Aechmea bauxilumii has rosette-shaped leaves that are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a dark green color and a waxy coating.

The plant lacks a traditional stem, instead having a short, thick base that supports the rosette, with a smooth, dark brown surface. It produces inflorescences with clustered, brightly colored flowers in shades of red and orange, blooming during the dry season and pollinated primarily by birds. The fruit is a capsule that disperses seeds through wind and water.

Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in rocky or dry soils.
