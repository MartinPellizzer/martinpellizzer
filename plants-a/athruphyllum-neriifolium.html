Athruphyllum neriifolium

Athruphyllum neriifolium, commonly known as the black-leaved tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its growth habit is that of a small to medium-sized tree, often reaching heights of up to 15 meters.

The bark is thick and rough, providing some resistance to pests and diseases.
