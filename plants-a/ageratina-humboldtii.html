Ageratina humboldtii

Ageratina humboldtii, commonly known as Humboldt's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, giving it a striking appearance. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Ageratina humboldtii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
