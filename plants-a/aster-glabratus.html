Aster glabratus

Aster glabratus, commonly known as the smooth aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The showy purple or white flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
