Artemisia fulvella

Artemisia fulvella, commonly known as golden wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided gray-green leaves and small yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone and camphor, which contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

Artemisia fulvella is valued in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and digestive benefits.
