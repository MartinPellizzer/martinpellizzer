Agave striata var. californica

Agave striata var. californica, commonly known as the California spineflower, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features striking, spiky yellow flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with white to yellow margins. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
