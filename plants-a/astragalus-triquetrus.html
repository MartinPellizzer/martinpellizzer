Astragalus triquetrus

Astragalus triquetrus, commonly known as the three-angled milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its small, yellow or white flowers bloom in late spring or early summer.

Astragalus triquetrus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
