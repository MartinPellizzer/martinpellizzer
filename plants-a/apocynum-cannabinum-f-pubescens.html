Apocynum cannabinum f. pubescens

Apocynum cannabinum f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy dogbane, is a variant of the common dogbane plant.

It is a flowering plant native to North America, typically found in moist, disturbed soils such as those in wetlands or along roadsides. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink to reddish in color, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant contains toxic cardiac glycosides, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
