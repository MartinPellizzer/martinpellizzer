Astrophytum asterias var. magnipunctatum

Astrophytum asterias var. magnipunctatum, commonly known as the giant star cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to the deserts of Mexico.

This plant is characterized by its spherical shape and a dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and extreme temperatures. Its areoles, from which spines and flowers emerge, are prominent features that distinguish it from other cacti. The flowers, typically yellow or white, bloom in the spring and are pollinated by insects.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
