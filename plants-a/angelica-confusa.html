Angelica confusa

Angelica confusa, commonly known as the Japanese angelica or Chinese angelica, is a biennial or perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and celery, and is characterized by its finely divided leaves and umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with a tall, hollow stem and large, compound leaves. Its flowers are white or pale green and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Angelica confusa is valued for its aromatic roots and has been used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
