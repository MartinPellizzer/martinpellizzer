Aster novae-angliae var. roseus

Aster novae-angliae var. roseus, commonly known as the New England aster rosea, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and a branched stem that supports its vibrant floral display.
