Alchemilla gorcensis

Alchemilla gorcensis, commonly known as the Polish lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in compact clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and meadows.

Alchemilla gorcensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
