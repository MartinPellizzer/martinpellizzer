Actinidia platyphylla

Actinidia platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or red in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Actinidia platyphylla is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
