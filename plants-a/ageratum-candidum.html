Ageratum candidum

Ageratum candidum, commonly known as white ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fluffy, white or pale blue flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ageratum candidum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
