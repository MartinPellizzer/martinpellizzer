Anemone edwardsiana

Anemone edwardsiana, commonly known as the Edwards' anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features delicate, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture.

Anemone edwardsiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
