Arenaria rhodocalyx

Arenaria rhodocalyx, commonly known as the pink sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are typically succulent and adapted to withstand cold and dry conditions.

Arenaria rhodocalyx is often found in rocky soils and is a common component of tundra and mountain ecosystems.
