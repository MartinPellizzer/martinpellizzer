Allium turcicum

Allium turcicum, commonly known as Turkish onion, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, flowering stem with small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant has a strong, pungent odor similar to other allium species. It is often cultivated for its edible bulbs, which can be used in cooking.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to leeks and chives.
