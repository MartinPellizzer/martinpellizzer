Asphodelus albus var. comosus

Asphodelus albus var. comosus, commonly known as the white asphodel, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, erect stems topped with dense clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in horticulture and traditional medicine.
