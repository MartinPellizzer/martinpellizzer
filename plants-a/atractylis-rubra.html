Atractylis rubra

Atractylis rubra, commonly known as red roughhead, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a reddish tinge, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer.

Atractylis rubra is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
