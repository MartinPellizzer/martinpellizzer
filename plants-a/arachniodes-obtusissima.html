Arachniodes obtusissima

Arachniodes obtusissima, commonly known as the broad-leafed rock fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, and deeply lobed leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its fronds are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, with a distinctive silvery-white underside.

Arachniodes obtusissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
