Agrostis angrenica

Agrostis angrenica, commonly known as the Angren meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate environments, often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
