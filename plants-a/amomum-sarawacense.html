Amomum sarawacense

Amomum sarawacense, commonly known as Sarawak cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale green flowers followed by aromatic, triangular fruits containing dark brown seeds. Its essential oils, particularly those containing cineole, are valued for their medicinal and culinary uses.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
