Astragalus semicircularis

Astragalus semicircularis, commonly known as the semicircular milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, yellow to brown pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppes and semi-desert environments.

Astragalus semicircularis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
