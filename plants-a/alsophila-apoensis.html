Alsophila apoensis

Alsophila apoensis, commonly known as the Apo fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in persistent, fibrous roots. The plant has a crown of large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in montane forests.

Alsophila apoensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
