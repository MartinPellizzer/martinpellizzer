Aegilops triaristata subsp. intermixta

Aegilops triaristata subsp.

intermixta, commonly known as the intermediate wild barley, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.

This subspecies is of interest to researchers due to its potential role in wheat evolution and its use in genetic studies.
