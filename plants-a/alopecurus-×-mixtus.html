Alopecurus × mixtus

Alopecurus × mixtus, commonly known as the meadow foxtail, is a hybrid grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted, cylindrical inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its ability to spread across various habitats.

Alopecurus × mixtus is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
