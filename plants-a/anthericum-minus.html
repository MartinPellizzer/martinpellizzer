Anthericum minus

Anthericum minus, commonly known as lesser nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Anthericum minus is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
