Atriplex tularensis

Atriplex tularensis, commonly known as the desert saltbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, making it well adapted to arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Atriplex tularensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
