Adromischus pulchellus

Adromischus pulchellus, commonly known as the "Coral Cactus," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with red or pink hues. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Adromischus pulchellus is well-suited for rock gardens or containers due to its compact size and low maintenance requirements.
