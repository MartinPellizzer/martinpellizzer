Arenaria serpyllifolia subsp. leptoclados

Arenaria serpyllifolia subsp.

leptoclados, commonly known as the slender sand-verbena, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically features prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, hairy foliage.

The leaves are linear to lanceolate, often with a bluish-gray hue due to dense pubescence. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
