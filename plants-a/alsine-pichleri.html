Alsine pichleri

Alsine pichleri, commonly known as Pichler's sandmat, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its hairy, prostrate stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize disturbed or open areas.

Alsine pichleri plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
