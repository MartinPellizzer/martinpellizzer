Astracantha eriocephala subsp. elongata

Astracantha eriocephala subsp.

elongata, commonly known as the elongated burrweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruits that form dense, spherical clusters.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually yellow or greenish in color.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, more slender fruiting bodies compared to other subspecies of the species.
