Adesmia incana var. oblata

Adesmia incana var. oblata, commonly known as the hairy adesmia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage due to dense pubescence. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale blue, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds.

It thrives in dry, arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
