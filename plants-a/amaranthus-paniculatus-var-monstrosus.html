Amaranthus paniculatus var. monstrosus

Amaranthus paniculatus var. monstrosus, commonly known as the tree amaranth, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, giving it a distinctive, almost tree-like appearance. The plant produces showy, branched inflorescences with vibrant red, orange, or yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens and landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its visual appeal rather than its edible properties.
