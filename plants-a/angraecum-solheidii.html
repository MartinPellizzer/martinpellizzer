Angraecum solheidii

Angraecum solheidii, commonly known as the Solheim's orchid, is a rare and exquisite epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and striking, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night. The plant is adapted to grow in humid, shaded environments, often found on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its flowers have a unique structure, with long, slender spurs that are specialized for pollination by moths.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly sought after by botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
