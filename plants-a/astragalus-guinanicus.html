Astragalus guinanicus

Astragalus guinanicus, commonly known as Guin's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus guinanicus is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
