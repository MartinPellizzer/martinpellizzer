Acer tataricum f. latialatum

Acer tataricum f. latialatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maple, is a unique variant of the Tatarian maple.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically lighter in color compared to the standard species. This form is notable for its distinctive leaf shape and often exhibits a more pronounced lobing. It is native to regions of eastern Asia, including parts of China and Russia.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
