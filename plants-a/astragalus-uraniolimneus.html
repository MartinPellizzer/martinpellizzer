Astragalus uraniolimneus

Astragalus uraniolimneus, commonly known as the lake astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
