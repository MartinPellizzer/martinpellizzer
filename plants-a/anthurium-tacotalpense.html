Anthurium tacotalpense

Anthurium tacotalpense, commonly known as the Tacotalpa anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant spadix. The plant produces a unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, which can range in color from green to red. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
