Antirrhinum majus var. ramosissimum

Antirrhinum majus var. ramosissimum, commonly known as the branched snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its numerous, branched stems that support clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers. The flowers typically come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, and they bloom from late spring to early autumn. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile addition to many landscapes.
