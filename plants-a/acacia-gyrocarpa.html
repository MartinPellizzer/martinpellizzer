Acacia gyrocarpa

Acacia gyrocarpa, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and bright yellow spherical flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a unique pod-like fruit called a gynophore, which contains the seeds. Acacia gyrocarpa is a hardy shrub or small tree that thrives in a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for native wildlife.
