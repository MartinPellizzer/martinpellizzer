Agrostis tuvinica

Agrostis tuvinica, commonly known as Tuvinian bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences containing minute flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subarctic regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in harsh environments.
