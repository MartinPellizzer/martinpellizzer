Ajuga × adulterina

Ajuga × adulterina, commonly known as the spreading bugleweed, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Valerianaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features bright green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Ajuga × adulterina thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth pattern allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a valuable ground cover and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
