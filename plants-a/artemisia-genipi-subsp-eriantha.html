Artemisia genipi subsp. eriantha

Artemisia genipi subsp.

eriantha, commonly known as the silverleaf artemisia, is a shrub-like plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry slopes.

The subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
