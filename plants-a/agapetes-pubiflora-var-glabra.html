Agapetes pubiflora var. glabra

Agapetes pubiflora var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-flowered agapete, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact growth habit. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white or gray pubescence. Its flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters, and bloom in the spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens due to its preference for moist, acidic soils.
