Arachnorchis fulva

Arachnorchis fulva, commonly known as the golden spider orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like inflorescence and vibrant golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in rainforest habitats, relying on atmospheric moisture rather than soil. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, adapted for efficient water absorption.

The species is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism, attracting specific insects for reproduction.
