Abarema bauchei

Abarema bauchei, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant, colorful blooms.

Abarema bauchei thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
