Achillea millefolium f. roseiflora

Achillea millefolium f. roseiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered yarrow, is a horticultural form of the common yarrow.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in flat-topped clusters. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
