Anthurium orientale

Anthurium orientale, commonly known as the oriental anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant spathes that can range in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are enclosed by the colorful spathe, a feature typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium orientale is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and ability to purify indoor air.
