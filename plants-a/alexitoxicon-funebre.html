Alexitoxicon funebre

Alexitoxicon funebre, commonly known as the Mourning Larkspur, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a deep green color that contrasts beautifully with the vibrant flowers. This plant is adapted to cool, moist environments and thrives in partial to full shade.

It is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
