Aeollanthus rivularis

Aeollanthus rivularis, commonly known as the river lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as rivers and ponds. The plant features long, narrow leaves that float on the water surface, supported by slender, branching stems. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pale yellow.

Aeollanthus rivularis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
