Andropogon caricosus var. heteropogonoides

Andropogon caricosus var. heteropogonoides, commonly known as the purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and distinctive purple or reddish inflorescences that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in erosion control.
