Anarthrophyllum rigidum

Anarthrophyllum rigidum, commonly known as the rigid flatleaf, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rigid, succulent leaves that are arranged in a flat, fleshy rosette. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xerophytic conditions and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
