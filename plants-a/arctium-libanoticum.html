Arctium libanoticum

Arctium libanoticum, commonly known as the thistle of Lebanon, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia, characterized by its spiny leaves and purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are covered in sharp prickles, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
