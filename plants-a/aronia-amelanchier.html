Aronia amelanchier

Aronia amelanchier, commonly known as chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. These berries are typically harvested in the fall and are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Aronia amelanchier thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.
