Austrocylindropuntia inarmata

Austrocylindropuntia inarmata, commonly known as the desert rose cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and often covered in white spines. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers during the warmer months, which are typically followed by small, yellow fruits. It is adapted to extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This cactus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh environments.
