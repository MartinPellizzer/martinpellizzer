Astragalus molybdenus var. molybdenus

Astragalus molybdenus var. molybdenus, commonly known as yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in restoration projects.
