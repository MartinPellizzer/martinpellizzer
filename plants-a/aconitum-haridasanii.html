Aconitum haridasanii

Aconitum haridasanii, commonly known as the Haridasanii monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves and produces toxic alkaloids, making it both a botanical curiosity and a potential hazard. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and studied for its chemical composition.
