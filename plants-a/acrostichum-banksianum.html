Acrostichum banksianum

Acrostichum banksianum, commonly known as the giant reed or giant bamboo, is a tall, grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and robust rhizomes. The plant can grow up to 6 meters in height, making it one of the tallest grasses in the world. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in swampy or flooded areas.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
