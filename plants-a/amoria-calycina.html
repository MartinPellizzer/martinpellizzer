Amoria calycina

Amoria calycina, commonly known as the violet crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters at the ends of the stems.

Amoria calycina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
