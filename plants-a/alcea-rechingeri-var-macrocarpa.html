Alcea rechingeri var. macrocarpa

Alcea rechingeri var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant hollyhock, is a strikingly tall flowering plant native to the Middle East.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, typically in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has a robust stem with velvety leaves and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a unique and eye-catching addition to botanical collections.
