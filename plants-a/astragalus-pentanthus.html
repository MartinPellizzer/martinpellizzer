Astragalus pentanthus

Astragalus pentanthus, commonly known as the five-lobed milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America, particularly in the western United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Astragalus pentanthus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
