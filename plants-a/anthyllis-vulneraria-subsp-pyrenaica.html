Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. pyrenaica

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern France. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as slopes and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the flora in its native region.
