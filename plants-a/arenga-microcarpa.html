Arenga microcarpa

Arenga microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf areca palm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its short, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fruits resembling small pineapples. Arenga microcarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
