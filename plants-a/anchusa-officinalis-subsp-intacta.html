Anchusa officinalis subsp. intacta

Anchusa officinalis subsp.

intacta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bugloss, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features slender, upright stems covered in blue to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
