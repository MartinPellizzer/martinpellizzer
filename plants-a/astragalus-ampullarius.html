Astragalus ampullarius

Astragalus ampullarius, commonly known as the bladder milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique, inflated seed pods that resemble bladders. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, compound leaves and produces yellow or pale yellow flowers.

Astragalus ampullarius plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators in its native range.
