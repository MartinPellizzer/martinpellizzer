Asparagus racemosus var. zeylanicus

Asparagus racemosus var. zeylanicus, commonly known as Indian asparagus or kuth, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that develop into cylindrical, spiky berries containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, scrubland habitats.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
