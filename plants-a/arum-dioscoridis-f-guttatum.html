Arum dioscoridis f. guttatum

Arum dioscoridis f. guttatum, commonly known as the spotted arum, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spadix-like inflorescence.

Native to parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland settings. Its foliage is typically marked with distinctive spots or streaks, giving it a unique visual appeal. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its diverse and often exotic members.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to add a dramatic touch to garden landscapes.
