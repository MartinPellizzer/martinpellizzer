Amaranthus × dobrogensis

Amaranthus × dobrogensis, commonly known as the Dobrogian amaranth, is a hybrid species native to the temperate regions of Eastern Europe and Western Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and branching structure. The leaves are typically lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in gardens and agricultural settings.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a forage crop.
