Actinodaphne sikkimensis

Actinodaphne sikkimensis, commonly known as Sikkim camphor.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. It produces small, white flowers that are typically followed by oval, woody fruits.

The plant is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.
