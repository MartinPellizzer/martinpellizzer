Arabis bursifolia

Arabis bursifolia, commonly known as the bladderleaf rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with cruciform flowers. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Arabis bursifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
