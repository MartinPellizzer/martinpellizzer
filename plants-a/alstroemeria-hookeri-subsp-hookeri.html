Alstroemeria hookeri subsp.

hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America. It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and long-lasting flowers.
