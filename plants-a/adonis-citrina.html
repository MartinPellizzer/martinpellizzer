Adonis citrina

Adonis citrina, commonly known as the yellow pheasant's eye, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of Europe.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, yellow petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to the landscape.

Adonis citrina is also known for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require caution in use.
