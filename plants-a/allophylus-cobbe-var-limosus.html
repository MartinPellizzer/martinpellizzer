Allophylus cobbe var. limosus

Allophylus cobbe var. limosus, commonly known as the swamp holly, is a shrub native to wetland habitats in tropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically elliptical with a glossy surface and a slightly waxy texture. This variant is distinguished by its preference for moist, acidic soils and its ability to thrive in swampy environments.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
