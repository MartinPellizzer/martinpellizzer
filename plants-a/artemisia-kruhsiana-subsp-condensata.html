Artemisia kruhsiana subsp. condensata

Artemisia kruhsiana subsp.

condensata, commonly known as silver wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in compact clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its aromatic properties make it a valued plant in traditional herbal medicine.
