Ardisia capitellata

Ardisia capitellata, commonly known as the clustered beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its attractive, clustered inflorescences that produce small, dark purple berries. The berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
