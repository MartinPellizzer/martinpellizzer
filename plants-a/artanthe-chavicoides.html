Artanthe chavicoides

Artanthe chavicoides, commonly known as the Chilean artanthe, is a perennial plant native to the Andes mountains of Chile.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with bright red or purple flowers that attract pollinators. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Artanthe chavicoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry conditions.
