Arabis confinis

Arabis confinis, commonly known as the alpine rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress species. This plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine meadows. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit and linear, succulent leaves adapted to cold and arid conditions.
