Antirrhinum rotundifolium

Antirrhinum rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaved snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant has rounded, broad leaves that are typically hairy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Antirrhinum rotundifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
