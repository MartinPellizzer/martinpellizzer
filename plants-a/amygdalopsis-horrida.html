Amygdalopsis horrida

Amygdalopsis horrida, commonly known as the rough amygdalopsis, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are dry, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Amygdalopsis horrida is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
