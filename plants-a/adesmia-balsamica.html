Adesmia balsamica

Adesmia balsamica, commonly known as the Balsam Bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with woody stems and can grow in a variety of soil types. It is often found in dry, arid regions and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Adesmia balsamica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
