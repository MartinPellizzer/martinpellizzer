Aster whitneyi

Aster whitneyi, commonly known as Whitney's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces showy purple to pink flowers in the fall, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in open meadows and along stream banks.

Whitney's aster is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
