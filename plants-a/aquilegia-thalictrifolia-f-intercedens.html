Aquilegia thalictrifolia f. intercedens

Aquilegia thalictrifolia f. intercedens, commonly known as the intermediate thalictrifolia columbine, is a botanical variant characterized by its unique floral morphology.

This form exhibits a blend of traits between the typical *A. thalictrifolia* and *A. vulgaris*, resulting in a mix of leaf and flower structures. It typically features compound leaves with a serrated margin and produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Its hybrid nature makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticultural breeding programs.
