Acacia kirkii var. intermedia

Acacia kirkii var. intermedia, commonly known as the East African umbrella thorn, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-like crown formed by its long, drooping branches. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are arranged in dense, globular clusters. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
