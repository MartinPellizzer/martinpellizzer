Aster foliaceus var. typicus

Aster foliaceus var. typicus, commonly known as the leaf aster, is a perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are finely divided and covered in a soft, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.
