Allium coryi

Allium coryi, commonly known as the Sierra onion, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other Allium species such as garlic and chives.

Allium coryi is valued for its aromatic properties and adaptability to arid environments.
