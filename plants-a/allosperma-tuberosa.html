Allosperma tuberosa

Allosperma tuberosa, commonly known as the African potato, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary edible part of the plant. The plant typically grows in grassland and woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in loose clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.
