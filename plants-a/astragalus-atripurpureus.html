Astragalus atripurpureus

Astragalus atripurpureus, commonly known as the purple-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus atripurpureus is often found in open grasslands and shrublands at high elevations.
