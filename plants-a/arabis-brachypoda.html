Arabis brachypoda

Arabis brachypoda, commonly known as narrowleaf rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known plants such as cabbages and mustards. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. It has basal leaves that are narrow and linear, and its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters.

Arabis brachypoda is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
