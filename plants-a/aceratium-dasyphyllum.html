Aceratium dasyphyllum

Aceratium dasyphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leafed dasyphyllum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
