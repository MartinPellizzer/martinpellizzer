Arbutus bicolor

Arbutus bicolor, commonly known as the twinleaf arbutus, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which are typically oval and have a waxy surface. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white in color. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains seeds.

Arbutus bicolor is often found in coastal forests and is adapted to grow in well-drained, acidic soils.
