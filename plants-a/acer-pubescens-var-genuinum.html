Acer pubescens var. genuinum

Acer pubescens var. genuinum, commonly known as the pubescent maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and distinctive pubescent (hairy) leaves that turn vibrant yellow in autumn. The leaves are typically 5-7 lobed with a serrated margin, and the tree produces small, red or orange flowers in early spring. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in temperate climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
