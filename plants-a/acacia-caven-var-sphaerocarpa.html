Acacia caven var. sphaerocarpa

Acacia caven var. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globular acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical crown and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which are followed by woody pods containing flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
