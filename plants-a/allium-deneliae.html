Allium deneliae

Allium deneliae, commonly known as the Denel onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear umbels of white to pale purple flowers, which bloom in spring. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, making it a potential crop for culinary use.

Allium deneliae thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, preferring temperate climates.
