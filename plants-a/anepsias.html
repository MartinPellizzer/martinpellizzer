Anepsias

Anepsias, commonly known as false dandelion, are herbaceous plants belonging to the Asteraceae family.

They are characterized by their basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin. Anepsias are native to Europe and Asia but have been introduced to other parts of the world.

They are often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
