Amaryllis leonensis

Amaryllis leonensis, commonly known as the Leon's amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces a tall, erect stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.

Its blooming period typically occurs in the spring, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
