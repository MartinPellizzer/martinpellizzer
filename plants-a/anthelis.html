Anthelis

Anthelis, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are typically woody and branching.

Anthelis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
