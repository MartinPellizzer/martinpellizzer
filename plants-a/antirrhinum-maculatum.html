Antirrhinum maculatum

Antirrhinum maculatum, commonly known as the spotted snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lipped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Antirrhinum maculatum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
