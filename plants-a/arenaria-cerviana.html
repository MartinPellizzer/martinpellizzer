Arenaria cerviana

Arenaria cerviana, commonly known as the alpine sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters. The plant features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer, often in clusters at the tips of its stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

Arenaria cerviana is often found in alpine meadows and is known for its resilience to cold and harsh environmental conditions.
