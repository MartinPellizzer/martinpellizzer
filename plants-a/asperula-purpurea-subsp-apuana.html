Asperula purpurea subsp. apuana

Asperula purpurea subsp.

apuana, commonly known as the Apuan Alps azalea, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the western Mediterranean. It features dense clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in spring.

The plant has leathery, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soil and prefers partial to full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its vibrant floral display.
