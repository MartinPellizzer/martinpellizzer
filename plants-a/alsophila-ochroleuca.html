Alsophila ochroleuca

Alsophila ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The fronds are typically a vibrant green, with a distinctive yellowish hue on the underside, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The plant has a slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
