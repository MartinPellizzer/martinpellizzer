Amphicarpaea edgeworthii f. alba

Amphicarpaea edgeworthii f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered amphicarpaea, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its unique ability to produce two types of fruits: one above ground and one below ground, a trait known as amphicarpism. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, white or pale pink flowers. Its root system is notable for forming symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

This species is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions.
