Aponogeton fugax

Aponogeton fugax, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface, often giving the appearance of being ethereal or ghostly. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically submerged, adding a subtle beauty to its aquatic habitat. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds and wetlands.

Aponogeton fugax is also notable for its ability to survive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for ornamental water gardens.
