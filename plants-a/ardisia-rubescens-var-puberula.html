Ardisia rubescens var. puberula

Ardisia rubescens var. puberula, commonly known as the velvetleaf ardisia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden use in suitable climates.
