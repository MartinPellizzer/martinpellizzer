Alchemilla glabra subsp. alpestris

Alchemilla glabra subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe. It features a rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments. Alchemilla glabra subsp.

alpestris is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
