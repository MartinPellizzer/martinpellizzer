Alyxia pseudosinensis

It is native to regions of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and typically grows on the branches of host trees such as Ficus and Elaeocarpus. The plant has small, opposite leaves and produces clusters of white or yellow flowers followed by small, fleshy berries. It is often found in tropical and subtropical forests, where it plays a role in the ecological interactions between host trees and other organisms.
