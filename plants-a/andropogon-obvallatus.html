Andropogon obvallatus

Andropogon obvallatus, commonly known as the flat-fruit bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, and its leaves are narrow with a bluish-green hue. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often covered in a whitish bloom.

Andropogon obvallatus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
