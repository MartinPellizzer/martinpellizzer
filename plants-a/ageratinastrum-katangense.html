Ageratinastrum katangense

Ageratinastrum katangense, commonly known as Katanga blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ageratinastrum katangense is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
