Arundinaria leptophylla

Arundinaria leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaf bamboo, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, which can contribute to soil stabilization.

Arundinaria leptophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
