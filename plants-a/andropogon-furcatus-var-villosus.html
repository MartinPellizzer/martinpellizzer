Andropogon furcatus var. villosus

Andropogon furcatus var. villosus, commonly known as hairy switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Hairy switchgrass is often used in landscaping and for erosion control due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
