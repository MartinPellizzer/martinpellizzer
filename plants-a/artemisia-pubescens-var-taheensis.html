Artemisia pubescens var. taheensis

Artemisia pubescens var. taheensis, commonly known as the Tahean mugwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage covered in fine, white hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been traditionally used in herbal remedies in traditional Chinese medicine.
