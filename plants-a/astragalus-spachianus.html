Astragalus spachianus

Astragalus spachianus, commonly known as the Spach's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Astragalus spachianus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
