Adenophora coronata

Adenophora coronata, commonly known as the crown bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are typically borne in terminal clusters. Adenophora coronata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
