Abronia rosea

Abronia rosea, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. It is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and frequent wind.

Abronia rosea plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native insects.
