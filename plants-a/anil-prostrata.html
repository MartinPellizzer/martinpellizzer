Anil prostrata

Anil prostrata, commonly known as creeping nightshade, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It features prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often rooting at the nodes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and slightly hairy, with a strong, often unpleasant odor.

Anil prostrata is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
