Adenophora triphylla subsp. aperticampanulata

Adenophora triphylla subsp.

aperticampanulata, commonly known as the open-campanula bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows.

The plant features slender, branching stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are bell-shaped, usually white or pale blue, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This subspecies is characterized by its open, campanulate (bell-like) flowers and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.
