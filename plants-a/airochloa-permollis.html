Airochloa permollis

Airochloa permollis, commonly known as the soft silky grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silky texture and soft, plume-like inflorescences that give it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its unique aesthetic.

Airochloa permollis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
