Arenaria tetraquetra var. pyrenaica

Arenaria tetraquetra var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant has small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

This variety is notable for its resilience and is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature.
