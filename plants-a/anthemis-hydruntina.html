Anthemis hydruntina

Anthemis hydruntina, commonly known as the Mediterranean everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellow to orange composite flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has silver-green foliage with a fuzzy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The flowers are known for their long-lasting nature and are often used in floral arrangements.
