Acer pictum var. connivens

Acer pictum var. connivens, commonly known as the eastern redbud, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are heart-shaped and turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife.
