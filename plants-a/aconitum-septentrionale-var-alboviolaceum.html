Aconitum septentrionale var. alboviolaceum

Aconitum septentrionale var. alboviolaceum, commonly known as the white-violet aconite, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, bi-lobed flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The plant contains potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic and requiring careful handling.
