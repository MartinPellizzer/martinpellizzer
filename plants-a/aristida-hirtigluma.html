Aristida hirtigluma

Aristida hirtigluma, commonly known as the hairy grama, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and shrublands.

Aristida hirtigluma is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
