Astragalus exscapus subsp. transsilvanicus

Astragalus exscapus subsp.

transsilvanicus, commonly known as the Transylvanian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish-pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are bipinnate, and it produces pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its ecological adaptability and potential medicinal properties.
