Allium opizii

Allium opizii, commonly known as the Opiz garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and clusters of pale blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a short, stout stem.

Allium opizii thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
