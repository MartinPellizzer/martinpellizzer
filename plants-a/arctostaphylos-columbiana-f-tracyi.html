Arctostaphylos columbiana f. tracyi

Arctostaphylos columbiana f. tracyi, commonly known as the Tracy manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its bark is reddish-brown and peels in strips.

The Tracy manzanita is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
