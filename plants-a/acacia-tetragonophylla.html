Acacia tetragonophylla

Acacia tetragonophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and have four to six pairs of leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which bloom in spring and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Acacia tetragonophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
