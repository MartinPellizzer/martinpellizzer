Acacia anserina

Acacia anserina, commonly known as the white acacia, is a small, deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Acacia anserina is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
