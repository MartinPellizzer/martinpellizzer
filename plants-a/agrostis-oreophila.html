Agrostis oreophila

Agrostis oreophila, commonly known as the mountain bentgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to mountainous regions of Europe and is adapted to high-altitude environments. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and is characterized by its low, creeping growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Agrostis oreophila is often found in alpine meadows and is an indicator species for high-altitude ecosystems.
