Asclepias verticillata var. subverticillata

Asclepias verticillata var. subverticillata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkweed, is a perennial plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features slender, branching stems covered in soft, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers with distinctive, horn-like structures called corona. It is a vital host plant for monarch butterfly larvae and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Its showy blooms also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
