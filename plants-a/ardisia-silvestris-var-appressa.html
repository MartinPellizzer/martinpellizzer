Ardisia silvestris var. appressa

Ardisia silvestris var. appressa, commonly known as the glossy woodbine, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique leaf morphology and adaptability to humid, low-light conditions.
