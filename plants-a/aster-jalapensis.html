Aster jalapensis

Aster jalapensis, commonly known as the Mexican aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its flowers bloom in the fall, producing vibrant purple or pink hues that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
