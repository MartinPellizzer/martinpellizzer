Aira malatrina

Aira malatrina, commonly known as the Malatran grass, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, preferring cool and moist conditions. Aira malatrina is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to alpine ecosystems.
