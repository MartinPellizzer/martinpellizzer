Agrostis phalaroides

Agrostis phalaroides, commonly known as meadow foxtail, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in meadows, marshes, and along streambanks. The plant has slender, erect stems that bear spikelets arranged in dense, finger-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
