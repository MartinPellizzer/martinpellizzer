Arabis cheiranthifolia

Arabis cheiranthifolia, commonly known as the alpine rockcress, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, four-petaled white or pink flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Arabis cheiranthifolia is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes at high elevations.
