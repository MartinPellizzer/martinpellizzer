Alopecias sieversianus

Alopecias sieversianus, commonly known as the Sievers' hairless plant, is a rare and critically endangered species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its unique, hairless leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments with cool temperatures. Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for montane ecosystems.
