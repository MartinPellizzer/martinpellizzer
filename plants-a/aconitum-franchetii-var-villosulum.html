Aconitum franchetii var. villosulum

Aconitum franchetii var. villosulum, commonly known as the woolly aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with a velvety texture, and its roots are toxic due to the presence of alkaloids. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and is not typically cultivated for ornamental purposes.
