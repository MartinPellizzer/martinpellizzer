Acanthocladus moyanoi

Acanthocladus moyanoi, commonly known as the Mexican fireplant, is a striking member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear vibrant red bracts, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant is native to Mexico and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Acanthocladus moyanoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic visual impact.
