Aspidium trifoliatum var. amplum

Aspidium trifoliatum var. amplum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped fronds that are typically broad and deeply divided. The plant exhibits a distinctive sori arrangement, with the sori located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both garden and indoor cultivation.
