Ardisia khasiana

Ardisia khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi ardisia, is a shrub native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
