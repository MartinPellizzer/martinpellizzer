Abies religiosa subsp. perotensis

Abies religiosa subsp.

perotensis, commonly known as the Mexican fir, is a coniferous tree native to the highlands of Mexico. It is characterized by its pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The cones of this species are typically small and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

The plant has a slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
