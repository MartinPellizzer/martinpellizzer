Astragalus stipitatus

Astragalus stipitatus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus stipitatus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
