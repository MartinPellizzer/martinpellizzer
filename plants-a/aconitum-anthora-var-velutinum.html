Aconitum anthora var. velutinum

Aconitum anthora var. velutinum, commonly known as the velvet monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant features large, palmately divided leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in woodland environments.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic appearance.
