Astragalus groetzbachii

Astragalus groetzbachii, commonly known as the Groetzbach milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
