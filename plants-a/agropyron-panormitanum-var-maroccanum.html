Agropyron panormitanum var. maroccanum

Agropyron panormitanum var. maroccanum, commonly known as the Moroccan wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in dry environments.
