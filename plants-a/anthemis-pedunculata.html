Anthemis pedunculata

Anthemis pedunculata, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in flat-topped clusters.

Anthemis pedunculata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its antiseptic and carminative effects.
