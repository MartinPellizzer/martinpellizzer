Aralia × hybrida

Aralia × hybrida, commonly known as the American aralia, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a range of morphological traits from its parent species.

It typically features compound leaves with a feathery appearance, often arranged in a palmate pattern, and can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbellate clusters, which are followed by spherical, spiny fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique structure.
