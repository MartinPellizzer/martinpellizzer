Alyxia thyrsiflora

Alyxia thyrsiflora, commonly known as the coral vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, cascading clusters of small, bright red or orange flowers that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Alyxia thyrsiflora is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
