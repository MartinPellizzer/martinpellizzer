Alchemilla hyperptycha

Alchemilla hyperptycha, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of yellow-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, round, cup-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine climates.

Alchemilla hyperptycha is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
