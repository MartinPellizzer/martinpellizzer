Aucuba japonica f. hakkomi

Aucuba japonica f. hakkomi, commonly known as the Japanese aucuba or variegated aucuba, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white and yellow variegation, which adds visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow-green flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its hardy nature and ornamental foliage make it a versatile addition to both residential and commercial landscapes.
