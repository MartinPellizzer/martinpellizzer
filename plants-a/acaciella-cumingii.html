Acaciella cumingii

Acaciella cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's acacia, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of northern South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, bipinnate leaves and produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is adapted to survive in dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various desert fauna.
