Atractocarpus decorus

Atractocarpus decorus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often a vibrant yellow or golden green. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its fast growth and adaptability, it is also used in reforestation projects in certain regions.
