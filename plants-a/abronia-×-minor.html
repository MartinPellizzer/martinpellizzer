Abronia × minor

Abronia × minor, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid landscapes.
