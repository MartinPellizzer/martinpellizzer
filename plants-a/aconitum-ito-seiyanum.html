Aconitum ito-seiyanum

Aconitum ito-seiyanum, commonly known as the Japanese monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are palmately compound, with finely divided lobes, and its roots are tuberous.

Aconitum ito-seiyanum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
