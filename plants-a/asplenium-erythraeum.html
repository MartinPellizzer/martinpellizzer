Asplenium erythraeum

Asplenium erythraeum, commonly known as the red helleborine, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds with a distinctive reddish hue on the underside. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called sorus, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium erythraeum is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological significance.
