Achyrospermum carvalhoi var. carvalhoi

Achyrospermum carvalhoi var. carvalhoi, commonly known as the Brazilian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. It produces a milky sap that is toxic to many animals, including insects and mammals.

This species is often found in open woodland and savanna habitats.
