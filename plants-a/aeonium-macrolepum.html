Aeonium macrolepum

Aeonium macrolepum, commonly known as the "Bull's Heart" or "Coral Aeonium," is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues along the edges. The plant produces striking, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers during the spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant among succulent enthusiasts.
