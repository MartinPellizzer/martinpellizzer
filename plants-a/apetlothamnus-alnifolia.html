Apetlothamnus alnifolia

Apetlothamnus alnifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Apetlothamnus alnifolia is known for its drought tolerance and ability to withstand saline conditions.
