Abarema subcoriacea

Abarema subcoriacea, commonly known as the rough-stemmed acacia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered with a rough, coriaceous texture, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, typically blooming during the dry season. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it is often found in dry, open habitats.

Abarema subcoriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
