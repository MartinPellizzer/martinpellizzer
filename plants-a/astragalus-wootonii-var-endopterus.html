Astragalus wootonii var. endopterus

Astragalus wootonii var. endopterus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellow to green flowers arranged in clusters. Its root system is deep and robust, enabling it to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

This variant is distinguished by its unique floral morphology and ecological adaptations to extreme environments.
