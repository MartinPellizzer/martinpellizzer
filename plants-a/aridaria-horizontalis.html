Aridaria horizontalis

Aridaria horizontalis, commonly known as the flatleaf desert plant, is a succulent species adapted to arid environments.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding survival in dry conditions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the rainy season. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to absorb moisture from a wide area.

It is often found in rocky soils and is resilient to high temperatures and low precipitation.
