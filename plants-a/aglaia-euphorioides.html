Aglaia euphorioides

Aglaia euphorioides, commonly known as the white aglaia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential pharmacological properties.

Aglaia euphorioides is also valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
