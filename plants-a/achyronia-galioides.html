Achyronia galioides

Achyronia galioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like flower clusters. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation.

Achyronia galioides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
