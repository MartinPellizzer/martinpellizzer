Acacia adpressa

Acacia adpressa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and dunes.

Acacia adpressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
