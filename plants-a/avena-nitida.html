Avena nitida

Avena nitida, commonly known as silky oat, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Avena nitida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued in ecological restoration projects for its soil-stabilizing properties.
