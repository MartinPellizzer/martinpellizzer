Androsyne

Androsyne, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Verbenaceae and is characterized by its small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs.

Androsyne is drought-tolerant and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
