Andromeda rubiginosa

Andromeda rubiginosa, commonly known as the rosebay witch-hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are borne in clusters and have a distinctive, intricate structure. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, brown seeds.

Andromeda rubiginosa is often found in moist, acidic soils in woodlands and along stream banks.
