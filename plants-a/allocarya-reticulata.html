Allocarya reticulata

Allocarya reticulata, commonly known as the netted thistle, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its spiny, glandular leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a rosette growth habit, with leaves arranged in a circular pattern around the stem. Its leaves are covered in a dense network of veins, giving it the "netted" appearance in its common name.

Allocarya reticulata is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
