Anaphyllum wightii

Anaphyllum wightii, commonly known as the Indian snowball, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fluffy flowers that resemble snowballs, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has a woody stem and is often found growing in alpine regions at high altitudes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine, silvery down.

Anaphyllum wightii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
