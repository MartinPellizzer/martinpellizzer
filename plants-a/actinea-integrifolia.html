Actinea integrifolia

Actinea integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf actinia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are typically covered in soft, hairy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have smooth margins, giving it its common name.

Actinea integrifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
