Aconitum lycoctonum subsp. pauciflorum

Aconitum lycoctonum subsp.

pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple or blue hooded flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It features large, palmately lobed leaves and produces toxic alkaloids, making it a plant of both botanical interest and caution.
