Arophyton crassifolium

Arophyton crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowgrass, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and colonize disturbed soils effectively. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a waxy surface, which help reduce water loss in its often arid habitat. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters and are pollinated by wind.

Arophyton crassifolium is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
