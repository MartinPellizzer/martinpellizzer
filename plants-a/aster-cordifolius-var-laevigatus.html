Aster cordifolius var. laevigatus

Aster cordifolius var. laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth-leaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features lance-shaped, smooth, and often hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with blue to purple petals, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
