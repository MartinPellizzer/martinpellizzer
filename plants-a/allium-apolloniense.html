Allium apolloniense

Allium apolloniense, commonly known as the Persian leek, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pale purple to white flowers. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Allium apolloniense is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
