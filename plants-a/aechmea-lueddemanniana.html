Aechmea lueddemanniana

Aechmea lueddemanniana, commonly known as the red-tipped pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of dark green, fibrous leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater. The plant produces vibrant orange to red bracts that surround its showy flower spikes, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aechmea lueddemanniana is often cultivated as a houseplant or in outdoor gardens due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea lueddemanniana has rosette-shaped leaves that are long, narrow, and leathery, arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem, with a deep green color and a waxy coating.

The plant lacks a true stem, and its base is covered in a dense, fibrous mat of roots that anchor it to the substrate. It produces striking inflorescences with bright red bracts that enclose clusters of small, white flowers, blooming during the summer months and pollinated primarily by hummingbirds. The fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains small, black seeds dispersed by birds and water.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the surrounding environment.
