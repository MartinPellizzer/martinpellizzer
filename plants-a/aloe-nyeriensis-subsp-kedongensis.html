Aloe nyeriensis subsp. kedongensis

Aloe nyeriensis subsp.

kedongensis, commonly known as the Kedong aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa, specifically found in Namibia and Angola. It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The leaves are typically green with a waxy coating, and they may have a slight reddish tinge under stress or in full sun. It produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow to orange flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
