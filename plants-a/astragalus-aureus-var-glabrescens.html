Astragalus aureus var. glabrescens

Astragalus aureus var. glabrescens, commonly known as golden milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its yellow flowers and slender, erect stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, feather-like leaflets, and it produces pods that contain seeds.

It is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats.
