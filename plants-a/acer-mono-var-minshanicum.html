Acer mono var. minshanicum

Acer mono var. minshanicum, commonly known as the Korean maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by winged samaras for seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is highly appreciated due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
