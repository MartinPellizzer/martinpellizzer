Anemia × mexiae

Anemia × mexiae, commonly known as the Mexican hybrid anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with stems that are upright and often branched. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical name reflects its hybrid origin, combining species from the genus *Anemone*.
