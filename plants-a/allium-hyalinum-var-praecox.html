Allium hyalinum var. praecox

Allium hyalinum var. praecox, commonly known as the early purple onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its elongated, pale pink to white bulb, which is often covered with a thin, translucent membrane. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
