Amomum petaloideum

Amomum petaloideum, commonly known as the pink cardamom, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that are highly fragrant and attract pollinators. Its rhizomes, similar to those of other ginger species, are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and therapeutic purposes.
