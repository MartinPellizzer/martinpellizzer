Allium polyanthum

Allium polyanthum, commonly known as the Chinese garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers in late spring. The plant is closely related to onions and garlic, belonging to the Allium genus. It is valued for its edible leaves and flowers, which have a mild garlic flavor.

Allium polyanthum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and culinary use.
