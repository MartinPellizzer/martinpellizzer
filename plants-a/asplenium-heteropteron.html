Asplenium heteropteron

Asplenium heteropteron, commonly known as the shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It is typically found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices.

Asplenium heteropteron is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
