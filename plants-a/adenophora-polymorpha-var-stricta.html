Adenophora polymorpha var. stricta

Adenophora polymorpha var. stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces bell-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valued species in both horticulture and ecological restoration efforts.
