Androcorys gracilis

Androcorys gracilis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and marshy areas. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil.

Androcorys gracilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
