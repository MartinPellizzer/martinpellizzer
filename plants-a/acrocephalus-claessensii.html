Acrocephalus claessensii

Acrocephalus claessensii, commonly known as the Cape grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or inland grassland habitats. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers arranged in dense spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in open areas with good drainage.

Acrocephalus claessensii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
