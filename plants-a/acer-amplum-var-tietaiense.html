Acer amplum var. tietaiense

Acer amplum var. tietaiense, commonly known as the Tietai maple, is a variant of the common maple native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves with five lobes, which turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The Tietai maple is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
