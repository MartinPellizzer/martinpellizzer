Allium rotundum var. waldsteinianum

Allium rotundum var. waldsteinianum, commonly known as the purple globemallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features rounded, bluish-gray leaves and produces clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its bulbous, rounded base, which is often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
