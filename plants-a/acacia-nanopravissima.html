Acacia nanopravissima

Acacia nanopravissima, commonly known as the dwarf acacia, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, bipinnate leaves that reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in tough pods that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds through the action of the wind.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique morphology, it is often used in xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
