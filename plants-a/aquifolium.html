Aquifolium

Aquifolium, commonly known as the common holly.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree belonging to the family Aquifoliaceae. The plant is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. Its small, red or yellow berries are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds.

Aquifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is native to Europe, North Africa, and western Asia.
