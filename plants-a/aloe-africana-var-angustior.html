Aloe africana var. angustior

Aloe africana var. angustior, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in drought conditions.
