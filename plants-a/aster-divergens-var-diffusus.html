Aster divergens var. diffusus

Aster divergens var. diffusus, commonly known as the diffuse aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces showy, blue to purple composite flower heads. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and wildlife.
