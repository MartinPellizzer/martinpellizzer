Arrhenatherum umbrosum

Arrhenatherum umbrosum, commonly known as blackgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, stiff leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous pale green to brownish flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread readily and form dense stands.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its competitive nature and resilience.
