Adenophora coronopifolia var. longisepala

Adenophora coronopifolia var. longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, with long, slender sepals that give it its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
