Arabis sadina

Arabis sadina, commonly known as the alpine rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with characteristic cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and often appear in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its natural habitat.

Arabis sadina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic alpine conditions.
