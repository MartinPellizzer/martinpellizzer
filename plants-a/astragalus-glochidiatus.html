Astragalus glochidiatus

Astragalus glochidiatus, commonly known as the prickly milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Astragalus glochidiatus is adapted to withstand drought and is an important component of desert ecosystems.
