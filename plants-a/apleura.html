Apleura

Apleura, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and spiny, bract-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean, it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative plant in coastal landscapes.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
