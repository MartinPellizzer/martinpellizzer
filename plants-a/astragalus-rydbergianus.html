Astragalus rydbergianus

Astragalus rydbergianus, commonly known as Rydberg's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has small, hairy stems and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus rydbergianus is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
