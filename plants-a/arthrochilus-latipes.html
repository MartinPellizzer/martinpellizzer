Arthrochilus latipes

Arthrochilus latipes, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive lip that is often yellow or reddish in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is typically found in open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands.

Arthrochilus latipes is a low-growing plant that blooms during the spring and early summer.
