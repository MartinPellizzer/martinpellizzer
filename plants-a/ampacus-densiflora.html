Ampacus densiflora

Ampacus densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Ampacus densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
