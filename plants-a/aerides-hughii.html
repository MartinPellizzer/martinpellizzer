Aerides hughii

Aerides hughii, commonly known as the Bornean orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Borneo.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive red or orange lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Aerides hughii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
