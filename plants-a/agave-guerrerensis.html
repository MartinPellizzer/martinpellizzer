Agave guerrerensis

Agave guerrerensis, commonly known as the Guerrero agave, is a species native to Mexico, specifically the state of Guerrero.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which are clustered in inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and potential medicinal purposes.
