Andropogon arthropogon

Andropogon arthropogon, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive purple-tinted inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Andropogon arthropogon is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
