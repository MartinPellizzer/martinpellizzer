Aster amplexicaulis

Aster amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that tightly clasp the stem, giving it its common name. It produces blue to purple flowers in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster amplexicaulis thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with well-drained soil.
