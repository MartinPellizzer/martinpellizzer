Allium cretaceum

Allium cretaceum, commonly known as the Crete garlic or rock garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its small, bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Allium cretaceum is often found growing in rocky or calcareous soils, adapting well to arid and coastal environments.

Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a useful plant in both culinary and horticultural contexts.
