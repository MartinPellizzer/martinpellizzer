Allium eusperma

Allium eusperma, commonly known as the narrowleaf garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks. The plant produces small, spherical bulbs and has grass-like leaves that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in traditional cuisines.
