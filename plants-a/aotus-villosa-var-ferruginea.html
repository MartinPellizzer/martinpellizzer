Aotus villosa var. ferruginea

Aotus villosa var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty velvetleaf, is a variant of the velvetleaf plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive velvety texture. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, often greenish-yellow in color, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
