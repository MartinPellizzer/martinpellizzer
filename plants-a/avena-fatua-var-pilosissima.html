Avena fatua var. pilosissima

Avena fatua var. pilosissima, commonly known as the silky oat, is a variant of the wild oat species characterized by its dense, silky-haired foliage.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences with small, wind-pollinated flowers. Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in certain regions.

The hairy texture of its leaves and stems is a distinguishing feature that aids in its identification.
