Alocasia guttata

Alocasia guttata, commonly known as the Spotted Alocasia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are adorned with distinctive white or yellow spots. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light to flourish. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance.

Alocasia guttata is relatively low-maintenance but prefers well-draining soil and consistent moisture.
