Andropogon roxburghianus

Andropogon roxburghianus, commonly known as red oat grass, is a perennial grass native to Asia and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge at the base. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Red oat grass is often used in ornamental gardening and is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
