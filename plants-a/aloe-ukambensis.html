Aloe ukambensis

Aloe ukambensis, commonly known as the East African aloe, is a succulent plant native to Tanzania and Kenya.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which bloom in the rainy season. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is often used for its medicinal properties.

Aloe ukambensis is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
