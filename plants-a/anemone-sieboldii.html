Anemone sieboldii

Anemone sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, white to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has palmately divided, lobed leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a short, stout rhizome that supports its growth habit.
