Anthochortus capensis

Anthochortus capensis, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, bract-like structures around its flower heads.

Anthochortus capensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and coastal landscapes.
