Astragalus plumosus var. akardaghicus

Astragalus plumosus var. akardaghicus, commonly known as the Akardagh milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
