Arctostaphylos uva-ursi subsp. monoensis

Arctostaphylos uva-ursi subsp.

monoensis, commonly known as the Mono brushberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Ericaceae family.
