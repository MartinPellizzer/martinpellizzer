Amaranthus acanthochiton

Amaranthus acanthochiton, commonly known as the spiny amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to the southern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in warm, dry climates. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from red to purple. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Amaranthus acanthochiton is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant floral displays.
