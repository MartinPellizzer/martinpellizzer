Actinotus repens

Actinotus repens, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Actinotus repens thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
