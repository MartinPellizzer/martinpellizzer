Agriphyllum grandiflorum

Agriphyllum grandiflorum, commonly known as the desert star, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its structure includes a basal rosette of leaves with a central stem bearing the inflorescence.

Agriphyllum grandiflorum thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping in desert environments.
