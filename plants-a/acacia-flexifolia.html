Acacia flexifolia

Acacia flexifolia, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping branches and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of true leaves. Acacia flexifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
