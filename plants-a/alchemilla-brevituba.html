Alchemilla brevituba

Alchemilla brevituba, commonly known as the short-stemmed lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in soft, silvery-white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla brevituba is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
