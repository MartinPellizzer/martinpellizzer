Aponogeton hexatepalus

Aponogeton hexatepalus, commonly known as the six-lobed arrowhead, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often submerged in water. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with six petals, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Aponogeton hexatepalus is also known for its ability to produce large, bulbous tubers that can survive in dry conditions.
