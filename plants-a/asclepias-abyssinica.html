Asclepias abyssinica

Asclepias abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and parts of East Africa.

It belongs to the milkweed family, Asclepiadaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among species in its family. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

Asclepias abyssinica plays an important role in supporting local pollinators, including butterflies and bees.
