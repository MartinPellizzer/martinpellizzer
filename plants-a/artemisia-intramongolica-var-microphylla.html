Artemisia intramongolica var. microphylla

Artemisia intramongolica var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of northern China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, gray-green, aromatic leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in composite inflorescences, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and desert steppe environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been traditionally used in herbal remedies in traditional Chinese medicine.
