Aster virgineus

Aster virgineus, commonly known as the New England aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

Aster virgineus is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
