Asparagus sarmentosus var. comatus

Asparagus sarmentosus var. comatus, commonly known as the hairy asparagus or the cat's tail asparagus, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its wiry, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats or clumps. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.
