Anemone virginiana f. leucosepala

Anemone virginiana f. leucosepala, commonly known as the white anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant has a basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant collections.
