Astilbe japonica var. densiflora

Astilbe japonica var. densiflora, commonly known as Japanese aster or denseflower astilbe, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features upright, feather-like clusters of small, showy flowers that range from white to pink or red, depending on the cultivar. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its foliage is typically dark green and textured, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
