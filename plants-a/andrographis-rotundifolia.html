Andrographis rotundifolia

Andrographis rotundifolia, commonly known as the rounded-leaf andrographis, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its opposite, rounded leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its medicinal properties are attributed to the presence of bioactive compounds like andrographolide.
