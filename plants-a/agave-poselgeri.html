Agave poselgeri

Agave poselgeri, commonly known as the Poselger's agave, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped arrangement of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The leaves are covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces an inflorescence of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils with minimal water requirements.
