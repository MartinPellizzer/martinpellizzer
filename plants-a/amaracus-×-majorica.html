Amaracus × majorica

Amaracus × majorica, commonly known as the Cape fuchsia, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Onagraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in Mediterranean climates.

The flowers are pollinated by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
