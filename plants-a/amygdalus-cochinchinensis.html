Amygdalus cochinchinensis

Amygdalus cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Chinese peach or sweet almond tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by oval, fuzzy fruits that resemble peaches. Its seeds, known as almonds, are edible and contain a high amount of oil, making them valuable for both culinary and medicinal uses.

The plant is also cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries.
