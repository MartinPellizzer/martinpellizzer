Aegilops mixta

Aegilops mixta, commonly known as the intermediate wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are small and contain high levels of protein and essential nutrients, making it valuable for both forage and potential agricultural use.

Aegilops mixta is also noted for its potential in developing new wheat varieties through hybridization.
