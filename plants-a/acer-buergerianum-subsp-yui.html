Acer buergerianum subsp. yui

Acer buergerianum subsp.

yui, commonly known as the Yui maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan. It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras for seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking foliage and compact growth habit.
