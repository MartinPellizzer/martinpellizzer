Artemisia nesiotica

Artemisia nesiotica, commonly known as the Greek wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features aromatic, gray-green leaves with a strong, camphor-like scent. Its small yellow flowers are clustered in spherical heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Artemisia nesiotica is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
