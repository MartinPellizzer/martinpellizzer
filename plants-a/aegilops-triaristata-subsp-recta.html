Aegilops triaristata subsp. recta

Aegilops triaristata subsp.

recta, commonly known as the three-awned wild oat, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, tufted inflorescence with three awns on each lemma.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils. Its botanical features include narrow leaves and small, spikelet-like flowers.

It is an important forage plant and has been studied for its potential in agricultural and ecological contexts.
