Aquilegia vulgaris proles dumeticola

Aquilegia vulgaris proles dumeticola, commonly known as the European garden cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, and its root system consists of fibrous roots that help anchor it in soil. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Additionally, it is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
