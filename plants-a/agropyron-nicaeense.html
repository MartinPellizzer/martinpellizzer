Agropyron nicaeense

Agropyron nicaeense, commonly known as the Nicaean wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Agropyron nicaeense is valued for its resilience and is used in ecological restoration and as a forage plant.
