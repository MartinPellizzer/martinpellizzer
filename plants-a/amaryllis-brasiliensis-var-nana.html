Amaryllis brasiliensis var. nana

Amaryllis brasiliensis var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf amaryllis, is a compact, bulbous plant native to Brazil.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy texture. It is well-suited for container gardening due to its small size and ease of care.

This variety is prized for its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor conditions.
