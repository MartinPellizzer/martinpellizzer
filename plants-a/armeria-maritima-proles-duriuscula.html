Armeria maritima proles duriuscula

Armeria maritima proles duriuscula, commonly known as sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing rosettes of silver-gray foliage and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is highly tolerant of salt spray and strong winds. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
