Amygdalus nana var. tortuosa

Amygdalus nana var. tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted dwarf almond, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in spring. The plant's most distinctive characteristic is its twisted, gnarled growth habit, which gives it an ornamental appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

This variety is valued for its unique form and ability to attract pollinators.
