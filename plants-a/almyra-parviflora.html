Almyra parviflora

Almyra parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered almyra, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of silky hair.

Almyra parviflora is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
