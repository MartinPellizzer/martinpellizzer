Artocarpus vrieseanus

Artocarpus vrieseanus, commonly known as the Bornean breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fruit. The fruit, which resembles a breadfruit, is encased in a thick, spiky husk and contains a starchy, edible pulp. The tree is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in traditional dishes and can be processed into flour.

It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
