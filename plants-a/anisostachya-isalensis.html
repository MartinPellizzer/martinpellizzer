Anisostachya isalensis

Anisostachya isalensis, commonly known as the Isalensis grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or swampy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
