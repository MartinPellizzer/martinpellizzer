Athanasia

Athanasia, commonly known as the "everlasting" flower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in vibrant colors such as yellow, orange, and red. The plant is native to regions of southern Africa and is known for its resilience in arid and semi-arid environments. Athanasia species typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce small, spiky seed heads after flowering.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
