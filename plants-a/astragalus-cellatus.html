Astragalus cellatus

Astragalus cellatus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus cellatus is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various wildlife species.
