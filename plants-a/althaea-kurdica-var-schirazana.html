Althaea kurdica var. schirazana

Althaea kurdica var. schirazana, commonly known as the Schirazan marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants known for their mucilage-producing properties. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. Its roots contain a thick, mucilaginous substance that has been used in traditional medicine.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its potential medicinal applications.
