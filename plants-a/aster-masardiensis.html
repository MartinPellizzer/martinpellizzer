Aster masardiensis

Aster masardiensis, commonly known as the Masardis aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, violet-blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
