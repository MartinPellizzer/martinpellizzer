Alyssum xiphocarpum

Alyssum xiphocarpum, commonly known as the narrowleaf alyssum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Alyssum xiphocarpum is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
