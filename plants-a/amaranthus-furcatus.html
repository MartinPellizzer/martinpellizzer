Amaranthus furcatus

Amaranthus furcatus, commonly known as the forked amaranth, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its upright growth habit and branched stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the species. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Amaranthus furcatus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
