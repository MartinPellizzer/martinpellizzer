Althaea officinalis subsp. pseudoarmeniaca

Althaea officinalis subsp.

pseudoarmeniaca, commonly known as Armenian marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy pink or white flowers during the summer months. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their mucilage content, which has soothing properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
