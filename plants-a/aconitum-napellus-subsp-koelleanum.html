Aconitum napellus subsp. koelleanum

Aconitum napellus subsp.

koelleanum, commonly known as the Koelreuter's aconite, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant produces distinctive, helmet-shaped seed pods that contain numerous small, dark seeds. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a waxy texture, typical of the Aconitum genus.

This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows.
