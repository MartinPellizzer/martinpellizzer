Actinochloa barbata

Actinochloa barbata, commonly known as the barbed grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
