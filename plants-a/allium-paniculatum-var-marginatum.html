Allium paniculatum var. marginatum

Allium paniculatum var. marginatum, commonly known as the pink onion, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, spherical, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its distinctive pink margins on the bulbous, onion-like bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, edible bulbs.
