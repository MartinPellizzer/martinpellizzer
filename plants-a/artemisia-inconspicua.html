Artemisia inconspicua

Artemisia inconspicua, commonly known as western sand artemisia, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as sagebrush steppe and rocky areas. The plant has gray-green, aromatic foliage with silvery-white undersides, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months.

The plant is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system to access water.
