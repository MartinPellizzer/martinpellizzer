Azalea lamponga

Azalea lamponga, commonly known as the red-flowered azalea, is a species of flowering plant in the Rhododendron genus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Thailand and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to pink. Azalea lamponga thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
