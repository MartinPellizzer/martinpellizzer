Ardisia pygmaea

Ardisia pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and emit a mild fragrance when crushed. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers followed by bright red berries that are highly ornamental. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Ardisia pygmaea is valued in gardens for its compact size and seasonal color changes.
