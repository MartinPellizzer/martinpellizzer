Allium virgunculae var. virgunculae

Allium virgunculae var. virgunculae, commonly known as the slender leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in a dense umbel, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbous stem is edible and has a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a potential culinary and medicinal plant.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.
