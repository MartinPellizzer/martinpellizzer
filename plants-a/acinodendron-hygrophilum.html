Acinodendron hygrophilum

Acinodendron hygrophilum, commonly known as the waterleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in humid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its root system is fibrous and well-developed, allowing it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Acinodendron hygrophilum is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other areas with high moisture levels.
