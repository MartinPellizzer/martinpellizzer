Asanthus thyrsiflorus

Asanthus thyrsiflorus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces compact, branched inflorescences called thyrses. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms.

Asanthus thyrsiflorus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
