Astragalus gryphus var. maroccanus

Astragalus gryphus var. maroccanus, commonly known as the Moroccan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
