Avenastrum flavescens

Avenastrum flavescens, commonly known as the yellow tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing small, yellow disk florets surrounded by pale yellow ray florets.

Avenastrum flavescens is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
