Abies griffithiana

Abies griffithiana, commonly known as the Griffith's fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist climates at high elevations.

Its botanical features make it an important species in mountainous forest ecosystems.
