Atropis subspicata

Atropis subspicata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a spadix, typical of the arum family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in damp woodlands.
