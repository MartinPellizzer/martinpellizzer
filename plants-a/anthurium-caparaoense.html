Anthurium caparaoense

Anthurium caparaoense, commonly known as the Caparao anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant spadix flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in rocky or mountainous regions. Its foliage is often a deep green with a glossy texture, and it produces colorful, spadix-like inflorescences that can range from red to yellow.

Anthurium caparaoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
