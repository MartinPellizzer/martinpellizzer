Azalea griffithiana

Azalea griffithiana, commonly known as the Indian azalea, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

Azalea griffithiana prefers cool, moist climates and well-drained acidic soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate regions.
