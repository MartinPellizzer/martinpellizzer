Athanasia crithmifolia subsp. crithmifolia

Athanasia crithmifolia subsp.

crithmifolia, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Immortelle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly aromatic, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

The species is characterized by its resilience to drought and salt spray, making it well adapted to harsh coastal environments.
