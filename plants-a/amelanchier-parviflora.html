Amelanchier parviflora

Amelanchier parviflora, commonly known as the flowering serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its oval to elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

Amelanchier parviflora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
