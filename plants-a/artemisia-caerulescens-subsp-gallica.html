Artemisia caerulescens subsp. gallica

Artemisia caerulescens subsp.

gallica, commonly known as blue artemisia, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its silver-blue foliage and aromatic, finely divided leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
