Acacia pravissima

Acacia pravissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant is characterized by its narrow, feather-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Acacia pravissima is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it access groundwater.

It plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
