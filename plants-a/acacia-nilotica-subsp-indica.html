Acacia nilotica subsp. indica

Acacia nilotica subsp.

indica, commonly known as the Indian acacia or khair, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent. It is characterized by its slender, thorned stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets.

The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers arranged in cylindrical clusters, followed by long, flat pods containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, often found in dry forests and scrublands.

The wood and gum of this species have been used traditionally for various purposes, including construction and medicinal applications.
