Acronychia petiolaris

Acronychia petiolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf silky oak, is a tropical tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rainforest or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
