Aegilops strangulata

Aegilops strangulata, commonly known as the strangler wheat, is a wild annual grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the tribe Aegilop tribe within the grass family Poaceae. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets.

Aegilops strangulata is known for its potential agricultural value, particularly in breeding programs for wheat improvement.
