Astragalus pilosicarpus

Astragalus pilosicarpus, commonly known as the hairy pod milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its hairy pods are a distinctive feature, and it is often found in the western United States.

Astragalus pilosicarpus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
