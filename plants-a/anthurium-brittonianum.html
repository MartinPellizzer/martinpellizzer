Anthurium brittonianum

Anthurium brittonianum, commonly known as the Britton's anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often a deep green with a waxy texture, while the spathe can range from red to orange in color.

Anthurium brittonianum thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.
