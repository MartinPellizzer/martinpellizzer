Arenaria tomasinii

Arenaria tomasinii, commonly known as the Tomasin's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. The plant features small, rounded leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its compact growth habit makes it well-suited for xeriscaping and erosion control in dry landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens and as a wildflower in native plant collections.
