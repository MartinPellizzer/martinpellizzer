Anisopappus salviifolius

Anisopappus salviifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by wind-dispersed seeds in a pappus structure. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Botanically, it is classified in the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
