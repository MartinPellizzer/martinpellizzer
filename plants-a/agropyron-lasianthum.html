Agropyron lasianthum

Agropyron lasianthum, commonly known as the western wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and spikelet-based inflorescence. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, two-lobed flowers. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and steppe ecosystems.

Agropyron lasianthum is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
