Aspalathus pulicifolia

Aspalathus pulicifolia, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are trifoliate and have a distinctive reddish-brown color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody pods containing small, dark brown seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils.

Aspalathus pulicifolia is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
