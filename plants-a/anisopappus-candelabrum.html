Anisopappus candelabrum

Anisopappus candelabrum, commonly known as the candleplant, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive branching pattern, which resembles a candlestick, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments.

The botanical features of Anisopappus candelabrum make it a unique and resilient member of the Asteraceae family.
