Anemone subvillosa

Anemone subvillosa, commonly known as the hairy anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features a short, stout rhizome and produces showy, white to pinkish flowers with five to seven petals. Its leaves are palmately divided with hairy surfaces, contributing to its common name.

Anemone subvillosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
