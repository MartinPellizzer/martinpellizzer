Astragalus alexandrinus

Astragalus alexandrinus, commonly known as Alexandrian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus alexandrinus is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological importance in its native habitat.
