Astrocaryum gynacanthum

Astrocaryum gynacanthum, commonly known as the golden palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

The botanical features of Astrocaryum gynacanthum make it both ecologically and commercially significant.
