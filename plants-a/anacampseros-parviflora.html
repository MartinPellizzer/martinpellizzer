Anacampseros parviflora

Anacampseros parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it an ideal plant for low-maintenance landscaping.
