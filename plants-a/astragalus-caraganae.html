Astragalus caraganae

Astragalus caraganae, commonly known as the Caragana milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus caraganae is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
