Allium flavum var. adriaticum

Allium flavum var. adriaticum, commonly known as the Adriatic garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in umbels. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, pungent aroma and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is closely related to other Allium species and is valued for its hardiness and adaptability.
