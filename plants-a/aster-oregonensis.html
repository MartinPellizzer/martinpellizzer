Aster oregonensis

Aster oregonensis, commonly known as the Oregon aster, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. The plant features slender stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Aster oregonensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
