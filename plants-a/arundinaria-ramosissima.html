Arundinaria ramosissima

Arundinaria ramosissima, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or yellow-green in color. Its rhizomes are extensively branched, allowing it to spread rapidly through vegetative propagation.

This species is native to China and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and hardiness in various climates.
