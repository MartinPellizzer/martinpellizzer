Ambora quadrifida

Ambora quadrifida, commonly known as the four-leafed ambora, is a unique flowering plant characterized by its distinctive quadrifid leaves, which are divided into four distinct lobes.

It belongs to the family Amboraceae and is native to tropical regions of South America. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Ambora quadrifida thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
