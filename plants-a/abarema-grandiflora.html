Abarema grandiflora

Abarema grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered abarema, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are pinnate, with multiple small leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Abarema grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
