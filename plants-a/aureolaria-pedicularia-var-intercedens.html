Aureolaria pedicularia var. intercedens

Aureolaria pedicularia var. intercedens, commonly known as the common heartweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in waste areas.

It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.
