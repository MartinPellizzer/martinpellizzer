Adesmia riograndensis

Adesmia riograndensis, commonly known as the Rio Grande legume, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil and Argentina.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and grassland habitats, often in areas with well-drained soils. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Adesmia riograndensis plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native ecosystems.
