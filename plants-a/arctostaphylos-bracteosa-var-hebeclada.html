Arctostaphylos bracteosa var. hebeclada

Arctostaphylos bracteosa var. hebeclada, commonly known as the Hebecladus manzanita, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, leathery berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
