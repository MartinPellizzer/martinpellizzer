Asparagus pallidus

Asparagus pallidus, commonly known as the white asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its pale green, tender stems and fern-like leaves that are often harvested before they fully develop. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters, and it is known for its edible young shoots.

Asparagus pallidus is cultivated for its nutritional value and is a popular vegetable in many cuisines.
