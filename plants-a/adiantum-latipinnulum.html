Adiantum latipinnulum

Adiantum latipinnulum, commonly known as the broad-pinnate maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble the feathers of a bird, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are typically bright green and have a distinctive, elegant appearance.

Adiantum latipinnulum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
