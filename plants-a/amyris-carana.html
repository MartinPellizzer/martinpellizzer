Amyris carana

Amyris carana, commonly known as the Brazilian sand arnica, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in the summer months. Amyris carana is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and forest edges.

Its essential oils are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
