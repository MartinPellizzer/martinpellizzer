Adicea nudicaulis

Adicea nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed adicea, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. The plant features simple, linear leaves that are often hairless, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and appear in clusters.

Adicea nudicaulis is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
