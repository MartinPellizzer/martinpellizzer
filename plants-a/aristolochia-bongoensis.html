Aristolochia bongoensis

Aristolochia bongoensis, commonly known as the Bongo's arrow vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate, and often highly modified leaves. The plant produces striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically green or purple in color. Its vines can grow quite long, often climbing or trailing along vegetation.

The species is noted for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
