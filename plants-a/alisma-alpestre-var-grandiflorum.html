Alisma alpestre var. grandiflorum

Alisma alpestre var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the alpine marsh plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and features showy, white to pale yellow spadices surrounded by a green, leaf-like bract. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, pond edges, and along streams, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are broad, arrow-shaped, and often floating on the water surface, while its roots are adapted for aquatic environments.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in wet gardens for its unique floral display.
