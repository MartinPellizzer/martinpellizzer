Aster monocephalus

Aster monocephalus, commonly known as the single-head aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces showy blue to purple flowers. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in open areas such as meadows and woodlands.

Aster monocephalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
