Anemone nemorosa subsp. typica

Anemone nemorosa subsp.

typica, commonly known as the wood anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, loamy soils.

The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive, hairy center. Its leaves are heart-shaped and form a basal rosette, and it has a short, rhizomatous root system.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its early spring bloom.
