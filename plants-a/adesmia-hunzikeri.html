Adesmia hunzikeri

Adesmia hunzikeri, commonly known as the Huarihua or Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which bloom in late summer. Adesmia hunzikeri thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cold, dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of alpine flora.
