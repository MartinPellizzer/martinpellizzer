Acacia saltilloensis

Acacia saltilloensis, commonly known as the Sinaloa acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often reduced to spines, a common adaptation to conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual element to its landscape. Its small, woody pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across dry, rocky soils.

Acacia saltilloensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife in its native range.
