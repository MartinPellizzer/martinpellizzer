Asarum pellucidum

Asarum pellucidum, commonly known as the translucent asarum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a rhizomatous root system. Its flowers are often greenish-yellow with a distinctive, complex structure that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a conservation concern in its native habitat.
