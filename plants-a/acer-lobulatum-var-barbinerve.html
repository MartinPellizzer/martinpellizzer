Acer lobulatum var. barbinerve

Acer lobulatum var. barbinerve, commonly known as the barbed-veined maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely toothed leaves that often have a barbed or serrated edge, giving it its varietal name. The leaves are typically bright green in summer and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
