Anaphalis tenella

Anaphalis tenella, commonly known as the silverleaf weed, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage that often has a silvery or woolly appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and meadows, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are generally narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs, which contribute to its distinctive look.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing both ray and disk florets that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
