Androcymbium decipiens

Androcymbium decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that mimic the appearance of other plants to attract pollinators. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a short, rhizomatous stem. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it is considered a rare and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
