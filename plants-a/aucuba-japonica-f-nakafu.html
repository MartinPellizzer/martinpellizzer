Aucuba japonica f. nakafu

Aucuba japonica f. nakafu, commonly known as the Japanese aucuba, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features glossy, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often marked with yellow or white variegation. The plant produces small, red berries that are ornamental but not edible for humans. It thrives in partial to full shade and is tolerant of a variety of soil conditions.

Its dense foliage makes it an excellent choice for hedges or as a specimen plant in shaded garden settings.
