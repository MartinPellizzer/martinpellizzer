Artemisia camphorata var. alpestris

Artemisia camphorata var. alpestris, commonly known as alpine wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its medicinal and aromatic properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
