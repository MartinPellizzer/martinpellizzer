Asparagus parviflorus

Asparagus parviflorus, commonly known as the silver thread asparagus, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It features delicate, fern-like foliage and produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its finely divided leaves and its ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and ease of care.

Asparagus parviflorus is also valued for its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
