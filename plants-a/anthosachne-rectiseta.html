Anthosachne rectiseta

Anthosachne rectiseta, commonly known as the narrow-hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, smooth, and often hairless stems, which are typically upright and grass-like in appearance. The plant produces small, three-lobed, white or pale green flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Anthosachne rectiseta is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
