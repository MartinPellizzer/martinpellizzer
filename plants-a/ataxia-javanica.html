Ataxia javanica

Ataxia javanica, commonly known as the Java ataxia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Ataxia javanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
