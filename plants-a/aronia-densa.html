Aronia densa

Aronia densa, commonly known as black chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves and clusters of small, red berries that ripen to a deep black. The plant is part of the Rosaceae family and is known for its high antioxidant content. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Aronia densa is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in herbal remedies and culinary applications.
