Alkanna mughlae

Alkanna mughlae, commonly known as the Persian dye plant, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to regions of Central Asia, including parts of Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red. Its roots produce a red-orange dye, which has been historically used for coloring textiles and cosmetics.

Alkanna mughlae thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.
