Annona duckei

Annona duckei, commonly known as the Brazilian custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height, with thick, smooth, green bark. The plant produces large, fragrant, yellowish-green fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy surface, and the flowers are white with a strong scent.

Annona duckei is valued for its edible fruit and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
