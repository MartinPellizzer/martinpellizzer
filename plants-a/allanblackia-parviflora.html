Allanblackia parviflora

Allanblackia parviflora, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. The tree is known for its fruit, which contains a rich, oily kernel used in cosmetics and traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of rainforests.

Allanblackia parviflora plays an important ecological role and is valued for its economic and cultural significance in local communities.
