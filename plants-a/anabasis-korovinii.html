Anabasis korovinii

Anabasis korovinii, commonly known as Korovin's saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to desert conditions.

Its deep root system allows it to access underground water sources, enhancing its survival in dry climates.
