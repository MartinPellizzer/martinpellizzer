Aspidopterys tomentosa var. tomentosa

Aspidopterys tomentosa var. tomentosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
