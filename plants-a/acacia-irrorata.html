Acacia irrorata

Acacia irrorata, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom in spring and attract various pollinators. Acacia irrorata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and resilient.

Its seeds are encased in hard pods and are often dispersed by wind or animals.
