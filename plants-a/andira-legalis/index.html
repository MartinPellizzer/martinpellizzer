Andira Legalis

Andira legalis is a lesser-known but ecologically significant plant native to the tropical regions of South America. It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 13,000 species known for their diverse forms and uses. This shrub is characterized by its dense foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays a vital role in supporting local biodiversity. Despite its importance, Andira legalis remains under-researched and often overlooked in conservation efforts.
