Anaphalis sinica var. yakusimensis

Anaphalis sinica var. yakusimensis, commonly known as the Yakushima aster, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its woolly, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
