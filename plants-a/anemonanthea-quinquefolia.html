Anemonanthea quinquefolia

Anemonanthea quinquefolia, commonly known as the five-leaf anemonanthea, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with palmately divided leaves that have five to seven leaflets. Its flowers are composed of numerous small petals arranged in a single layer, and it thrives in moist, shady environments.

Anemonanthea quinquefolia is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
