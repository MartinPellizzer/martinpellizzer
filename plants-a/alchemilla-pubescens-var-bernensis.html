Alchemilla pubescens var. bernensis

Alchemilla pubescens var. bernensis, commonly known as the hairy lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in upright clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its common name reflects the appearance of its fuzzy foliage, which resembles a mantle.
