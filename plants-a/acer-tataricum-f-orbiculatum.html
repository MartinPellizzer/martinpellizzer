Acer tataricum f. orbiculatum

Acer tataricum f. orbiculatum, commonly known as the round-leaf maple, is a variant of the Tatarian maple characterized by its distinctive orbicular leaves.

The leaves are typically round or nearly round, with a smooth margin and a vibrant green color during the growing season. This ornamental plant is native to the northeastern regions of China and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras in the fall.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.
