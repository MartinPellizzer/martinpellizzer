Allium ampeloprasum subsp. iranicum

Allium ampeloprasum subsp.

iranicum, commonly known as the Persian leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the highlands of Iran and surrounding regions. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its long, cylindrical, hollow stems that are often used in traditional cuisine.

The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are broad, flat, and resemble those of the leek, and it is often cultivated for both ornamental and edible purposes.

This subspecies is valued for its adaptability to arid and mountainous environments.
