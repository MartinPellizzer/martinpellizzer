Anabasis foliosa

Anabasis foliosa, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellow flowers and has succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant's root system is extensive, allowing it to access moisture deep in the soil. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats.

Anabasis foliosa plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for small desert animals.
