Agropyron marginatum subsp. kabylicum

Agropyron marginatum subsp.

kabylicum, commonly known as the Kabylicum wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a bluish-green color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, temperate climates and is often found in steppe and grassland environments.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
