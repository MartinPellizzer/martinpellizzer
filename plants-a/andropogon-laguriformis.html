Andropogon laguriformis

Andropogon laguriformis, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and blue-green foliage, which give it its common name. This plant typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its fibrous roots help prevent soil erosion, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.

Andropogon laguriformis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
