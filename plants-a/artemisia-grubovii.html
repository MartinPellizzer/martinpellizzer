Artemisia grubovii

Artemisia grubovii, commonly known as the Chinese wormwood, is a perennial herb native to China and other parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its strong, pungent scent. Artemisia grubovii has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in composite inflorescences.

It is often used in traditional medicine and has potential applications in pharmaceutical research.
