Asarum ambiguum

Asarum ambiguum, commonly known as wild ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, hooded flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
