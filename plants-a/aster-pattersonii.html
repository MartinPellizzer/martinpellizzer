Aster pattersonii

Aster pattersonii, commonly known as Patterson's aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and produces showy purple to blue flowers in the late summer and early fall. It has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Patterson's aster is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
