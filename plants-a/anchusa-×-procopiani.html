Anchusa × procopiani

Anchusa × procopiani, commonly known as the hybrid bluebottle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical name reflects its hybrid origin, combining traits from its parent species Anchusa arborescens and Anchusa tetrasepala.
