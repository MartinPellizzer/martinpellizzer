Arctostaphylos tomentosa subsp. hebeclada

Arctostaphylos tomentosa subsp.

hebeclada, commonly known as the hebeclada manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage and small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers. It has small, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss, making it drought-tolerant.

The subspecies is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
