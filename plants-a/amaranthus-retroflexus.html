Amaranthus retroflexus

Amaranthus retroflexus, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and its lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which develop into three-winged seeds. It is a common weed found in disturbed soils and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Amaranthus retroflexus is also notable for its high seed production, which contributes to its invasive potential in agricultural and natural settings.
