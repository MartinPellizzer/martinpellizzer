Asteromyrtus magnifica

Asteromyrtus magnifica, commonly known as the Royal Myrtle, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to the humid regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Asteromyrtus magnifica is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.
