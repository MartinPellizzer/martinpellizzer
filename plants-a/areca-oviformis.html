Areca oviformis

Areca oviformis, commonly known as the oval areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and feather-like leaves that grow in a fan shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, black, round fruits. Areca oviformis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
