Arabis sinuata

Arabis sinuata, commonly known as the sinuous rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, sinuously wavy leaves. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, typically arranged in a raceme.

Arabis sinuata is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
