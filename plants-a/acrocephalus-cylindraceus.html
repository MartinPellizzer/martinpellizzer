Acrocephalus cylindraceus

Acrocephalus cylindraceus, commonly known as the cylindrical bulrush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often grow in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, cylindrical spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color, and it blooms from late summer to early autumn. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form extensive colonies in shallow water or damp soil.

Acrocephalus cylindraceus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
