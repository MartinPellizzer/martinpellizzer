Acanthophyllum coloratum var. coloratum

Acanthophyllum coloratum var. coloratum, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, spiny foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and succulent leaves that store water.

This species is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
