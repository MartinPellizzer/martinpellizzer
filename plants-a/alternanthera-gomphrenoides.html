Alternanthera gomphrenoides

Alternanthera gomphrenoides, commonly known as the flame flower or red flame plant, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble small fireworks. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bright red flowers that are borne on slender, branching stems. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and can vary in color from green to reddish hues.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and water features.
