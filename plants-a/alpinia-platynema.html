Alpinia platynema

Alpinia platynema, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related species. The plant is characterized by its tall, leafy stems and clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
