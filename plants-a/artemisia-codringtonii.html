Artemisia codringtonii

Artemisia codringtonii, commonly known as Codrington's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into small, dry fruits. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
