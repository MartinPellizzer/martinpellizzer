Agastache mearnsii

Agastache mearnsii, commonly known as silverleaf hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery-gray bloom. The plant produces spikes of tubular, violet to blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
