Anemonastrum narcissiflorum subsp. calvum

Anemonastrum narcissiflorum subsp.

calvum, commonly known as the Desert Daffodil, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its showy, yellow, daffodil-like flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to survive in sandy, well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient species in its native habitat.
