Allium purdyi

Allium purdyi, commonly known as the Purple Stripe Onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spherical, purple-tinged bulbs that are often used in culinary applications. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Allium purdyi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, sweet flavor.
