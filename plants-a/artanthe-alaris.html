Artanthe alaris

Artanthe alaris, commonly known as the silverleaf artanthe, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Artanthe alaris thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a desirable plant for drought-tolerant landscapes.
