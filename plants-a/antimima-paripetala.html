Antimima paripetala

Antimima paripetala, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent petals that appear to shimmer in the light, giving it its ethereal appearance. The plant has a short flowering period, typically during the late summer months, and produces small, inconspicuous fruits. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture in its humid habitat.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, Antimima paripetala remains a subject of botanical interest and conservation concern.
