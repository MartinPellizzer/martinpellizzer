Abies pinsapo var. marocana

Abies pinsapo var. marocana, commonly known as the Atlas cedar, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the Atlas Mountains of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like foliage. The tree produces small, reddish-brown cones that mature in the second year. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species.
