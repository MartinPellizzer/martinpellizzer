Amelanchier lancifolia

Amelanchier lancifolia, commonly known as the Pennsylvania serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its oval, lance-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. Amelanchier lancifolia thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
