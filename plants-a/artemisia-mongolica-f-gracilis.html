Artemisia mongolica f. gracilis

Artemisia mongolica f. gracilis, commonly known as the Mongolian wormwood, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It features silver-gray, narrow, and linear leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that appear in late summer, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic properties and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to xeriscapes and native plant gardens.
