Azaleastrum semibarbatum

Azaleastrum semibarbatum, commonly known as the desert azalea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has small, narrow leaves and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. It is adapted to harsh desert conditions, exhibiting drought tolerance and minimal water requirements.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other shrubs and evergreens.
