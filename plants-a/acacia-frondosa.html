Acacia frondosa

Acacia frondosa, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, thread-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular inflorescences, which appear in late winter to early spring. Its wood is light, soft, and used in various traditional crafts.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
