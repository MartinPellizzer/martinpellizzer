Anthericum paucinervatum

Anthericum paucinervatum, commonly known as the slender false lily of the valley, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, spadix-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces berries that are dispersed by birds.
