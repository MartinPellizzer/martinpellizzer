Amelanchier × grandiflora

Amelanchier × grandiflora, commonly known as the serviceberry or shadbush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its attractive, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late spring to early summer, often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and turn vibrant shades of red or orange in the fall.

Amelanchier × grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
