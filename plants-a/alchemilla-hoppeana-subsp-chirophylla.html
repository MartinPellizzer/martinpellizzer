Alchemilla hoppeana subsp. chirophylla

Alchemilla hoppeana subsp.

chirophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-white, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicle-like clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
