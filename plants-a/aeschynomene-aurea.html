Aeschynomene aurea

Aeschynomene aurea, commonly known as golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical significance lies in its use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.

The species is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
