Aloe retrospiciens

Aloe retrospiciens, commonly known as the backward-looking aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for water storage. The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences with yellow flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves have a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss, typical of arid environments.

Aloe retrospiciens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
