Astragalus duranii

Astragalus duranii, commonly known as the Duran's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus duranii is known for its small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
