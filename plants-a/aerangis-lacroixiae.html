Aerangis lacroixiae

Aerangis lacroixiae, commonly known as the Lacroix's orchid, is a rare and exquisite orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers with intricate patterns that resemble lace, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, Aerangis lacroixiae is highly sought after by botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
