Alyxia ilicifolia subsp. magnifolia

Alyxia ilicifolia subsp.

magnifolia, commonly known as the coastal holly, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of small, leathery, glossy leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
