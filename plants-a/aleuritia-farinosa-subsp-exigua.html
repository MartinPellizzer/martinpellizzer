Aleuritia farinosa subsp. exigua

Aleuritia farinosa subsp.

exigua, commonly known as the small-flowered snowbell, is a shrub native to East Asia. It is characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers that appear in early spring.

The plant has smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
