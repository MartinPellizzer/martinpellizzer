Acacia restiacea

Acacia restiacea, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to spines. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, attracting a variety of pollinators. Acacia restiacea is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
