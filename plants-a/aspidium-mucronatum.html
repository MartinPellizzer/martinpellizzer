Aspidium mucronatum

Aspidium mucronatum, commonly known as the blunt-tipped water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically light green and have a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in the spread of the plant across the ground.

Aspidium mucronatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
