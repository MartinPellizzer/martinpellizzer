Astragalus hallii

Astragalus hallii, commonly known as Hall's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus hallii is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
