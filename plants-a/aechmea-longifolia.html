Aechmea longifolia

Aechmea longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved pineapple plant, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are long, narrow, and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces striking, flower-like inflorescences that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Aechmea longifolia is popular as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
