Aechmea longifolia

Aechmea longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved pineapple plant, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are long, narrow, and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces striking, flower-like inflorescences that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Aechmea longifolia is popular as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea longifolia has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, sword-like, and arranged in a spiral pattern around the central stem, with a deep green color and a waxy texture.

The stem is short and thick, with a smooth, dark green bark that grows upright from the base of the rosette. Its inflorescence consists of a tall, branched spike bearing small, violet flowers that bloom during the rainy season, and are pollinated by insects such as bees and moths. The plant produces bright red berries containing small, black seeds, which are dispersed by birds and water.

The roots are fibrous and well-developed, anchoring the plant and absorbing nutrients from the soil.
