Ansellia gigantea

Ansellia gigantea, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring broad, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. Its rhizomes are edible and have a starchy texture similar to taro.

Botanically, it belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric.
