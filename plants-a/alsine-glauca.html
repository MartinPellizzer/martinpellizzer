Alsine glauca

Alsine glauca, commonly known as blueweed, is a low-growing annual herb native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender stems that often lie close to the ground, with small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

Alsine glauca is known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient plant in various habitats.
