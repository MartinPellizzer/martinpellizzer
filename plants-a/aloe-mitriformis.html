Aloe mitriformis

Aloe mitriformis, commonly known as the helmet aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellowish-green flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its rosette growth habit and ability to store water in its leaves make it well adapted to arid environments.

Aloe mitriformis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
