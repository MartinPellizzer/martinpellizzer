Agropyron spicatum var. viride

Agropyron spicatum var. viride, commonly known as green wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences bearing small, spikelet-bearing flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed sites.

Due to its nutritional value, it is sometimes used in livestock forage and in traditional herbal practices.
