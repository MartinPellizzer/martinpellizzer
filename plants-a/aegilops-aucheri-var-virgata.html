Aegilops aucheri var. virgata

Aegilops aucheri var. virgata, commonly known as the slender wild wheat, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale blue to white florets. It is often found in disturbed habitats and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

Due to its genetic relationship with wheat, it has been studied for its potential in agricultural improvement.
