Allium anceps

Allium anceps, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic.

It is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale purple flowers in clustered umbels. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in cooking.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species like garlic and onions.
