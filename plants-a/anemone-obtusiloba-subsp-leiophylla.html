Anemone obtusiloba subsp. leiophylla

Anemone obtusiloba subsp.

leiophylla, commonly known as the smooth-leaved anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has basal leaves that are rounded and often covered in a dense, woolly layer of hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
