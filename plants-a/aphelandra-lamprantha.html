Aphelandra lamprantha

Aphelandra lamprantha, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Aphelandra lamprantha is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and unique floral display.
