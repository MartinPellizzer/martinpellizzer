Acacia yirrkallensis

Acacia yirrkallensis, commonly known as Yirrkallensis wattle, is a shrub native to the Northern Territory of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, phyllode-like leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
