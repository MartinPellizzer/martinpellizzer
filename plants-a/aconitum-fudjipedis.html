Aconitum fudjipedis

Aconitum fudjipedis, commonly known as the Fujipedi Aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its leaves are compound, with deeply lobed foliage that is often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Aconitum fudjipedis contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
