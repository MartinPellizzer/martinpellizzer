Anthemis pectinata

Anthemis pectinata, commonly known as the fringed chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white pappus structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
