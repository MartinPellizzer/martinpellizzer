Artemisia vulgaris var. heterophylla

Artemisia vulgaris var. heterophylla, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers. The plant exhibits variable leaf morphology, with the varietal name "heterophylla" referring to its differing leaf shapes. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
