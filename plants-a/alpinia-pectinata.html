Alpinia pectinata

Alpinia pectinata, commonly known as the fragrant ginger or mountain ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental leaves, which are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, adding visual interest. Its rhizomes are similar to ginger in appearance and may have aromatic properties.

Alpinia pectinata is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
