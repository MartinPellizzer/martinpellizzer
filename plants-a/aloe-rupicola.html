Aloe rupicola

Aloe rupicola, commonly known as the rock aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched stems with clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Aloe rupicola is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
