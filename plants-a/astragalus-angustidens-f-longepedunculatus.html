Astragalus angustidens f. longepedunculatus

Astragalus angustidens f. longepedunculatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is native to regions of western North America. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with narrow, linear leaflets.

This variant is distinguished by its elongated peduncles, which support its clustered inflorescences.
