Aglaia argentea var. multijuga

Aglaia argentea var. multijuga, commonly known as silver aglaia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Meliaceae family, characterized by its ornamental value and unique foliage. The plant features large, elliptical leaves with a silvery-white underside, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits.

This variant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
