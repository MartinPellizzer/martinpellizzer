Asphodelus albus subsp. cerasifer

Asphodelus albus subsp.

cerasifer, commonly known as the white asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features strap-like, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stems with clusters of white or pale pink flowers.

The plant is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
