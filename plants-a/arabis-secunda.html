Arabis secunda

Arabis secunda, commonly known as the alpine rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring.

Arabis secunda is valued in horticulture for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
