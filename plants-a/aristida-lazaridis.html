Aristida lazaridis

Aristida lazaridis, commonly known as the Lazaridis grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and sandy plains. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Aristida lazaridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in degraded areas.
