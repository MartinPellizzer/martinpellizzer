Acianthera sulcata

Acianthera sulcata, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, zigzag-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as rainforests. Its roots are often epiphytic, meaning they grow on other plants rather than in soil.

Acianthera sulcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
