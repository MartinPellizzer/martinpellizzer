Astragalus lentiginosus var. carinatus

Astragalus lentiginosus var. carinatus, commonly known as the horned milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive ridge or keel, giving it its specific epithet "carinatus.". The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in spikes, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open grasslands.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
