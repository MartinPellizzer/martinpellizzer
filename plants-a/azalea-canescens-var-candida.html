Azalea canescens var. candida

Azalea canescens var. candida, commonly known as the white azalea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink blooms that appear in late spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub with dense, evergreen foliage that provides a lush backdrop for its flowers. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
