Aster senecioides

Aster senecioides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Aster senecioides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.
