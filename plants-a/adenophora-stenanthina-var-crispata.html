Adenophora stenanthina var. crispata

Adenophora stenanthina var. crispata, commonly known as the crinkled bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive crinkled or wavy margin. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. Its flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences, and the species is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
