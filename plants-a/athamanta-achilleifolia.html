Athamanta achilleifolia

Athamanta achilleifolia, commonly known as the mountain thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often at high altitudes, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Athamanta achilleifolia is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, arid environments.
