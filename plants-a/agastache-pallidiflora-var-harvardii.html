Agastache pallidiflora var. harvardii

Agastache pallidiflora var. harvardii, commonly known as the Harvard hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of pale lavender to white flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it both ornamental and beneficial to pollinators.
