Aeschynanthus ellipticus

Aeschynanthus ellipticus, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a flowering plant native to western Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its common name. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed.
