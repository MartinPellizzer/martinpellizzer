Alcea rosea flore-pleno-nigra

Alcea rosea flore-pleno-nigra, commonly known as the Black Honeysuckle, is a striking variety of hollyhock characterized by its dark, almost black flowers.

This cultivar is notable for its densely packed petals, which create a velvety appearance and a dramatic visual impact in gardens. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, sharing botanical traits with other hollyhocks, such as hairy stems and palmate leaves. The flowers typically bloom in summer, adding a rich, dark hue to the landscape.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both garden borders and container planting.
