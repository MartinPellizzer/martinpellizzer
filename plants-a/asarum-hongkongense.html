Asarum hongkongense

Asarum hongkongense, commonly known as Hong Kong wild betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China, particularly Hong Kong.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, hooded flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a short flowering period in late spring. Its rhizomes are used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
