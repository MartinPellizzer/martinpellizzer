Amaranthus paolii

Amaranthus paolii, commonly known as the Pauli amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers and tall, branched stems. The plant produces small, triangular seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Amaranthus paolii is also valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of protein and minerals.
