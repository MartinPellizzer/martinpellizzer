Alyssum mouradicum

Alyssum mouradicum, commonly known as the Moroccan alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically produces yellowish-green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Alyssum mouradicum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
