Asplenium Adiantum-Nigrum Subsp. Cuneifolium

Asplenium adiantum-nigrum subsp. cuneifolium is a lesser-known fern species that thrives in shaded, moist environments. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that often display a striking dark green to blackish coloration. This subspecies is native to various regions in the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico. Its unique appearance and subtle beauty make it a sought-after addition to botanical gardens and shaded landscapes. Despite its aesthetic appeal, it remains underappreciated compared to more common fern varieties.
