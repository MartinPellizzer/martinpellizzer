Aristea gerrardii

Aristea gerrardii, commonly known as the desert arrowgrass, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions, showcasing a compact growth habit. Aristea gerrardii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral ecosystems.
