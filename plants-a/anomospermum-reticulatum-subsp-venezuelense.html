Anomospermum reticulatum subsp. venezuelense

Anomospermum reticulatum subsp.

venezuelense, commonly known as the Venezuelan net-veined acacia, is a shrub native to the dry regions of Venezuela. It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and compound leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soil conditions.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various desert-dwelling species.
