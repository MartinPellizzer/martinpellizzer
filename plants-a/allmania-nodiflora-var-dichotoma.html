Allmania nodiflora var. dichotoma

Allmania nodiflora var. dichotoma, commonly known as the forked allmania, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive branching pattern, with stems that often split into two at the base, giving it its scientific name. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
