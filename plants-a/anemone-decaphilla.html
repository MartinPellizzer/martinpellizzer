Anemone decaphilla

Anemone decaphilla, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features palmate leaves with five to nine lobes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Anemone decaphilla is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and early summer bloom.
