Aristolochia poomae

Aristolochia poomae, commonly known as the Vietnamese birthwort, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique, winding tendrils. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically purple or greenish in color. Its roots and leaves have been used in traditional medicine, though the plant contains toxic compounds.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal interest, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
