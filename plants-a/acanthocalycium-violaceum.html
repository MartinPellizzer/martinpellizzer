Acanthocalycium violaceum

Acanthocalycium violaceum, commonly known as the violet-tipped barrel cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a bluish tint. The plant produces vibrant violet flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
