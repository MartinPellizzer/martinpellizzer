Agave bracteosa

Agave bracteosa, commonly known as the Mexican hat agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, blue-green leaves that are spiny at the edges. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears showy yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates with well-drained soil.

Agave bracteosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
