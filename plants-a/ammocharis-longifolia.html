Ammocharis longifolia

Ammocharis longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered star of Bethlehem, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that resemble stars. The plant blooms in late summer to early autumn, and its flowers have a subtle, sweet scent. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, Ammocharis longifolia is widely appreciated by gardeners.
