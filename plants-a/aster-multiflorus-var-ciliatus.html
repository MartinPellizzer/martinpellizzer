Aster multiflorus var. ciliatus

Aster multiflorus var. ciliatus, commonly known as the silky aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.

The silky aster is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
