Argyranthemum adauctum subsp. jacobaeifolium

Argyranthemum adauctum subsp.

jacobaeifolium, commonly known as Jacob's weed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often found in dry meadows, roadsides, and disturbed habitats.
