Acaena hirsutula var. hirsutula

Acaena hirsutula var. hirsutula, commonly known as the hairy burr bush, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with many small, spiny fruits that are adapted for seed dispersal by animals. The plant has compound leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in heads. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and alpine regions.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
