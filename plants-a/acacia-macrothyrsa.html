Acacia macrothyrsa

Acacia macrothyrsa, commonly known as the large-thorned acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Its thorny stems provide structural support and defense against herbivores.

Acacia macrothyrsa is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
