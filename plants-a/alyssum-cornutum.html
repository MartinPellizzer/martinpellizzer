Alyssum cornutum

Alyssum cornutum, commonly known as horned alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant has hairy, branched stems and linear, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Alyssum cornutum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
