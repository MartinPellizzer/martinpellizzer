Antennaria albicans

Antennaria albicans, commonly known as the white mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, often arranged in clusters at the ends of its hairy stems. Its leaves are spoon-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Antennaria albicans thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
