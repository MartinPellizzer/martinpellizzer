Amelanchier ovalis var. cretica

Amelanchier ovalis var. cretica, commonly known as the Cretan serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and produces small, edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and rocky soils, preferring well-drained conditions. It is valued for its ornamental value, with showy white flowers in spring and vibrant red foliage in autumn.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing food for birds and insects.
