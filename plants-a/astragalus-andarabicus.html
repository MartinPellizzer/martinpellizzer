Astragalus andarabicus

Astragalus andarabicus, commonly known as the Jordanian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry climates, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil.

Due to its ecological significance, it is sometimes considered a valuable species for restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
