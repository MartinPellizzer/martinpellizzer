Aralia spinosa var. glabrescens

Aralia spinosa var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with a smooth, glossy texture, distinguishing it from other aralia species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
