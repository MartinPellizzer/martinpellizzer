Anthemis sinuata

Anthemis sinuata, commonly known as the Sinuata tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and its flowers are typically yellow with a central disk. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
