Acacia trapezoidea

Acacia trapezoidea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces elongated, woody pods that contain seeds. Acacia trapezoidea is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal and sandy soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
