Amaryllis bifida var. spathacea

Amaryllis bifida var. spathacea, commonly known as the spatheate amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically white or pink with a distinctive red or purple spathe. The plant produces strap-shaped leaves that are broad and fleshy, adapted for storing water. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a warm climate.
