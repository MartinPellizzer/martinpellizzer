Artemisia biasolettiana

Artemisia biasolettiana, commonly known as the Italian mugwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and slender, branched stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Artemisia biasolettiana is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
