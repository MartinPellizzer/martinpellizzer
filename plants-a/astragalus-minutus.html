Astragalus minutus

Astragalus minutus, commonly known as the dwarf milkvetch, is a small annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, featuring hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its seed pods are slender and often curved. It is adapted to thrive in poor soils and can withstand drought conditions.

Astragalus minutus plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local insect populations.
