Atriplex lapponica

Atriplex lapponica, commonly known as Arctic saltbush, is a hardy shrub native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.

Atriplex lapponica plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing food for grazing animals.
