Astracantha andreji

Astracantha andreji, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic, containing cardiac glycosides.

Astracantha andreji is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
