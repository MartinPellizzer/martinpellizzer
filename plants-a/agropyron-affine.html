Agropyron affine

Agropyron affine, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. Agropyron affine is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
