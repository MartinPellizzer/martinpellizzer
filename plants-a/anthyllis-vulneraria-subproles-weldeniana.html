Anthyllis vulneraria subproles weldeniana

Anthyllis vulneraria subproles weldeniana, commonly known as the yellow sandrocket, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and can be found in coastal dunes and dry grasslands.

Its morphology reflects its evolutionary adaptation to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.
