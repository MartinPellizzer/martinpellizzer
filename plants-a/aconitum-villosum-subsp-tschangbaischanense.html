Aconitum villosum subsp. tschangbaischanense

Aconitum villosum subsp.

tschangbaischanense, commonly known as the Changbai mountain aconite, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, palmately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense spikes at the top of the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and herbal medicine practices.
