Aster erubescens

Aster erubescens, commonly known as the redstem aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and prairies, preferring full sun and well-drained soils. Its showy purple to pink flowers bloom in the late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
