Aconitum napellus var. tatrense

Aconitum napellus var. tatrense, commonly known as the Tatran Alpine Aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of alkaloids such as aconitine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its beauty and toxicity, it is both a subject of botanical interest and a plant requiring careful handling.
