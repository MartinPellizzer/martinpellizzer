Asplundia gleasonii

Asplundia gleasonii, commonly known as the Gleason's asplundia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which includes plants such as philodendrons and calla lilies. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy in appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Asplundia gleasonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in warm, humid environments.
