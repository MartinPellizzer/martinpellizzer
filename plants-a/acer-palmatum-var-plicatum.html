Acer palmatum var. plicatum

Acer palmatum var. plicatum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with plicate leaves, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, lace-like leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters, with a rounded, compact form. Its foliage is particularly ornamental, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, thriving in temperate climates.
