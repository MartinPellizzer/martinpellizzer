Astragalus capito

Astragalus capito, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Astragalus capito has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stabilization.
