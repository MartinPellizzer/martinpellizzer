Anotis ingrata

Anotis ingrata, commonly known as the common thornapple, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, while its fruit is a spherical, spiny capsule that contains numerous seeds.

Anotis ingrata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions.
