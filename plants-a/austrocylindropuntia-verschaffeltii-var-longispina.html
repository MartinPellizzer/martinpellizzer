Austrocylindropuntia verschaffeltii var. longispina

Austrocylindropuntia verschaffeltii var. longispina, commonly known as the Long-spined Paddle Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are often covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically followed by small, red fruits. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
