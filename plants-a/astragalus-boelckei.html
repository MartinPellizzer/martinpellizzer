Astragalus boelckei

Astragalus boelckei, commonly known as the Boelcke milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to low moisture environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators.

Astragalus boelckei is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
