Asplenium amabile

Asplenium amabile, commonly known as the sweet fern or velvet fern, is a small evergreen fern native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. Its fronds are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Asplenium amabile is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
