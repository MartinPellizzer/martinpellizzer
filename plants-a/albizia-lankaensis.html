Albizia lankaensis

Albizia lankaensis, commonly known as the Sri Lankan siris, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Albizia lankaensis is also valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses.
