Acacia excelsa subsp. angusta

Acacia excelsa subsp.

angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and narrow, feathery leaves that are typically grey-green in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are arranged in cylindrical spikes. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resilience to poor soil conditions.

The species is often used in revegetation projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
