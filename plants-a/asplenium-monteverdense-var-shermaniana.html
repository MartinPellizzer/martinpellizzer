Asplenium monteverdense var. shermaniana

Asplenium monteverdense var. shermaniana, commonly known as the Sherman's rock fern, is a variant of the rock fern species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and small, clustered fronds that often grow on rocky substrates. The plant typically has a short, creeping rhizome and produces spores in small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its ecological role in its native habitat.
