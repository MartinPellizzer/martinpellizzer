Aster subtropicus

Aster subtropicus, commonly known as the subtropical aster, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant is characterized by its showy, daisy-like blooms that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warmer climates.

Aster subtropicus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
