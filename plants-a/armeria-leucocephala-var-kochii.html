Armeria leucocephala var. kochii

Armeria leucocephala var. kochii, commonly known as the white-capped sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It features dense clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats, where it is well-suited to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils.
