Acacia pennata var. heyneana

Acacia pennata var. heyneana, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in compact spikes, which bloom during the dry season. Its seed pods are long, slender, and ribbed, containing small, hard seeds.

Acacia pennata var. heyneana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
