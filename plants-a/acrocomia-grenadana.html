Acrocomia grenadana

Acrocomia grenadana, commonly known as the Grenada palm, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Grenada and nearby islands.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, edible fruits resembling chestnuts. Acrocomia grenadana is adapted to coastal and dry environments, making it a resilient species in tropical climates.
