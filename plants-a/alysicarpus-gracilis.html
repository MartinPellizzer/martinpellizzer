Alysicarpus gracilis

Alysicarpus gracilis, commonly known as slender alysicarpus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, round, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is used in traditional medicine in some parts of its range.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.
