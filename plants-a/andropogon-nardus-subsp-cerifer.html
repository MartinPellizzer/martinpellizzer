Andropogon nardus subsp. cerifer

Andropogon nardus subsp.

cerifer, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America. It is characterized by its fine, aromatic leaves that emit a sweet, honey-like fragrance when crushed.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and meadows. Its flowering stems are usually short and bear small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles.

Sweetgrass is often used in traditional crafts and rituals due to its pleasant scent and durable fibers.
