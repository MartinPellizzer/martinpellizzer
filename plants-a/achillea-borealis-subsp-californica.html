Achillea borealis subsp. californica

Achillea borealis subsp.

californica, commonly known as the California yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
