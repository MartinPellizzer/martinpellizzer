Adonis maculata var. ochroleuca

Adonis maculata var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered leopard's bane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with distinctive black spots on the petals. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces elongated, branched stems that bear the floral clusters. Its botanical characteristics include a preference for well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
