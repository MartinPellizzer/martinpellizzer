Andropogon incanus subvar. cryptopus

Andropogon incanus subvar. cryptopus, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit, which makes it visually distinct from other grasses. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in native plant landscapes.
