Adenophora petiolata

Adenophora petiolata, commonly known as the long-stemmed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Adenophora petiolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
