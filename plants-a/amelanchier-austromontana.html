Amelanchier austromontana

Amelanchier austromontana, commonly known as the Canadian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, providing food for birds and wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and moist habitats.

Amelanchier austromontana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
