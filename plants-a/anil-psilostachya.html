Anil psilostachya

Anil psilostachya, commonly known as blue oat grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and distinctive blue-green foliage that turns coppery in the fall. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in panicles, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Anil psilostachya is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local pollinators.
