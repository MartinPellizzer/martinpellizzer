Aristolochia viperina

Aristolochia viperina, commonly known as the viper's bugloss, is a flowering plant belonging to the Aristolochiaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, serpentine-shaped flowers that resemble a viper, hence its common name. The plant is native to various regions in the Americas and thrives in moist, wooded habitats. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces small, dark blue to purple berries.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in both traditional and modern medicinal research.
