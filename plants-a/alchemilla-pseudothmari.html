Alchemilla pseudothmari

Alchemilla pseudothmari, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Alchemilla pseudothmari is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
