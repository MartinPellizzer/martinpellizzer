Arthrochilium

Arthrochilium, commonly known as the orchid mantis, is a genus of orchids native to Southeast Asia.

These plants are characterized by their unique, often bizarre floral structures that resemble insects or other small creatures. The flowers typically feature intricate patterns and colors that serve as camouflage or mimicry, aiding in pollination by attracting specific insects. Arthrochilium species are epiphytic, meaning they grow on other plants without being parasitic.

They thrive in humid, shaded environments and are prized for their unusual appearance in horticulture.
