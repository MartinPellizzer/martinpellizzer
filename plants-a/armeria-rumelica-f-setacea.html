Armeria rumelica f. setacea

Armeria rumelica f. setacea, commonly known as the tufted thrift, is a flowering plant belonging to the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces dense clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. Native to the Mediterranean region, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are covered in a fine, white fuzzy layer, enhancing its visual appeal.
