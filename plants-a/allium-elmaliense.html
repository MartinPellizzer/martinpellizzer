Allium elmaliense

Allium elmaliense, commonly known as the Elmaliense onion, is a bulbous plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round bulbs that are often used in local cuisines for their mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental and edible plant in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and valuable for culinary purposes.
