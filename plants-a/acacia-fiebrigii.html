Acacia fiebrigii

Acacia fiebrigii, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and its dense, feathery foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are arranged in compact spherical heads. Its leaves are small, linear, and often arranged in whorls around the stem.

Acacia fiebrigii is adapted to arid environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
