Ajax pseudonarcissus var. pallidus

Ajax pseudonarcissus var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale narcissus, is a variant of the common daffodil.

It is characterized by its pale yellow, funnel-shaped flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch of color to the landscape.
