Agrianthus campestris

Agrianthus campestris, commonly known as the field agrianthus, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers in panicles during the summer months. It is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in disturbed or marginal areas.

Agrianthus campestris is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
