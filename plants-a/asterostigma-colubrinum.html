Asterostigma colubrinum

Asterostigma colubrinum, commonly known as the blue star, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical name reflects its unique floral structure and historical classification.
