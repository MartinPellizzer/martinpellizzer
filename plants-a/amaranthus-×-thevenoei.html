Amaranthus × thevenoei

Amaranthus × thevenoei, commonly known as the hybrid amaranth, is a species resulting from the cross between Amaranthus hypochondriacus and Amaranthus caudatus.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its vibrant red, orange, or purple flower heads, which are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant produces small, triangular seeds that are typically harvested for use in food or as a decorative element. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Amaranthus × thevenoei is also valued for its nutritional content, containing high levels of protein and minerals.
