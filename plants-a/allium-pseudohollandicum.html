Allium pseudohollandicum

Allium pseudohollandicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, ovoid bulbs that are often used as ornamental plants in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Allium pseudohollandicum is valued for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms that add visual interest to the landscape.
