Agrostis berlandieri

Agrostis berlandieri, commonly known as the desert needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that help it survive in harsh climates.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
