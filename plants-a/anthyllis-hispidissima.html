Anthyllis hispidissima

Anthyllis hispidissima, commonly known as the hairy anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its foliage consists of compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Anthyllis hispidissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
