Asarca longibracteata

Asarca longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract asarum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, hooded flowers that resemble a frog, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its flowers are usually purple to reddish in color, and it produces small, dark berries as fruit.
