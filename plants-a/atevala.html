Atevala

Atevala, commonly known as the "wild turnip," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and hairy, with small white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its root system is robust, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Atevala is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
