Aconitum napellus lusus lobelianum

Aconitum napellus lusus lobelianum, commonly known as the Lobb's monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple hood-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
