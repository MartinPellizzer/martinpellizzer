Acacia mathuataensis

Acacia mathuataensis, commonly known as the Socotra acacia, is a unique species native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, fan-shaped leaves and long, thin, yellowish-green thorns. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to the harsh climate of its native habitat.

Due to its ecological importance and unique appearance, it is considered a valuable component of Socotra's biodiversity.
