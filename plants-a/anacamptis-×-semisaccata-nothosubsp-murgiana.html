Anacamptis × semisaccata nothosubsp. murgiana

Anacamptis × semisaccata nothosubsp.

murgiana, commonly known as the Murgian orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy, spurred orchid flowers. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This nothosubspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
