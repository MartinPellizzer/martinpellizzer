Alsophila meridionalis

Alsophila meridionalis, commonly known as the southern hakea, is a species of tree fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing cones, are located at the tips of its fronds.

Alsophila meridionalis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
