Amaranthus chlorostachys convar. erythrostachys

Amaranthus chlorostachys convar. erythrostachys, commonly known as the red-stemmed amaranth, is a species characterized by its striking red or purple stems and leaves.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, while the flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

This plant is widely cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in some traditional cuisines.
