Acalypha cordata

Acalypha cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf or red-veined begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that often display striking red or purple veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant.

Acalypha cordata is valued for its ornamental foliage and is relatively easy to care for with proper watering and light conditions.
