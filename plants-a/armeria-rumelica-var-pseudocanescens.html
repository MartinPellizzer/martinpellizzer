Armeria rumelica var. pseudocanescens

Armeria rumelica var. pseudocanescens, commonly known as the white sand armeria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant features linear, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun exposure.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
