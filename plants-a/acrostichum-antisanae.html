Acrostichum antisanae

Acrostichum antisanae, commonly known as the giant reed or giant grass, is a tall, perennial plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its tall, slender, grass-like stems. The plant can grow up to 5 meters in height, with long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It thrives in moist or aquatic environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a valuable ecological resource and a potential invasive species in some regions.
