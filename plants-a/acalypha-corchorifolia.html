Acalypha corchorifolia

Acalypha corchorifolia, commonly known as silkyleaf ironwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or dry woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
