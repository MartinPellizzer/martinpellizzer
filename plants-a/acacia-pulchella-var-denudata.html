Acacia pulchella var. denudata

Acacia pulchella var. denudata, commonly known as the smooth wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often glabrous stems and bipinnate leaves that are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The smooth wattle is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
