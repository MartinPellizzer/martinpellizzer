Astragalus krugeanus var. sipikordaghensis

Astragalus krugeanus var. sipikordaghensis, commonly known as the Sipikordagh milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
