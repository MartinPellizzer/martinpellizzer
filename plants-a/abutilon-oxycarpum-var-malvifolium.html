Abutilon oxycarpum var. malvifolium

Abutilon oxycarpum var. malvifolium, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are typically lobed or deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological landscaping.
