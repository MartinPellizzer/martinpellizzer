Alsodeia calycina

Alsodeia calycina, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Alsodeia calycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
