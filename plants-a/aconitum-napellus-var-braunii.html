Aconitum napellus var. braunii

Aconitum napellus var. braunii, commonly known as the Braun's monkshood, is a variant of the European monkshood plant.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a raceme, which are characteristic of the genus Aconitum. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply cut lobes that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is native to Central Europe and is often found in moist, shaded woodland areas.
