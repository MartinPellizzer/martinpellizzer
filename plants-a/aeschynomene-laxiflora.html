Aeschynomene laxiflora

Aeschynomene laxiflora, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems. It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils.

Aeschynomene laxiflora is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
