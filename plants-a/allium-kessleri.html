Allium kessleri

Allium kessleri, commonly known as the Kessler's garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and long blooming period.
