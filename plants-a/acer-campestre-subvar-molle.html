Acer campestre subvar. molle

Acer campestre subvar. molle, commonly known as the soft-leaved hedge maple, is a variant of the common maple native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often pubescent leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness.

This subspecies is well-suited for hedging and as a landscape plant due to its compact growth habit and adaptability.
