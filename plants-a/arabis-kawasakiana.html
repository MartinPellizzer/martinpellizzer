Arabis kawasakiana

Arabis kawasakiana, commonly known as the Japanese rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Arabis kawasakiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
