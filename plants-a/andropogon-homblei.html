Andropogon homblei

Andropogon homblei, commonly known as Hombli grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes many economically important grasses. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, blade-like leaves and dense, cylindrical flower spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping and erosion control in suitable climates.
