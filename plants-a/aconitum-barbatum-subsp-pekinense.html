Aconitum barbatum subsp. pekinense

Aconitum barbatum subsp.

pekinense, commonly known as the Peking aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia. It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September.

The plant has a robust root system and grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are deeply lobed and can reach up to 30 cm in length, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is important to note that all parts of this plant are toxic and should be handled with care.
