Anthericum filifolium

Anthericum filifolium, commonly known as the thread-leaf arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a distinctive underground tuber and a spatulate leaf with fine, thread-like veins. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, typical of the Araceae family.

Anthericum filifolium thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in marshy areas and along riverbanks.
