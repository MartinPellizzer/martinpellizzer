Argyrolobium umbellatum

Argyrolobium umbellatum, commonly known as the white globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant features clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are often hairy.

Argyrolobium umbellatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
