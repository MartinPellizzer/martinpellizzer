Ageratina hyssopina

Ageratina hyssopina, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, mist-like clusters. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ageratina hyssopina is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
