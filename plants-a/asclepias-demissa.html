Asclepias demissa

Asclepias demissa, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish flowers with a unique, horn-shaped structure called a galea. It is a host plant for the monarch butterfly, providing essential nutrients for monarch caterpillars.

Asclepias demissa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
