Amaranthus hypochondriacus var. powellii

Amaranthus hypochondriacus var. powellii, commonly known as the Powell's amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically displaying shades of red, orange, or yellow.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional landscaping and as a source of natural pigments.
