Acacia pennatula

Acacia pennatula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, feathery leaves that are often mistaken for the leaves of the common wattles. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Acacia pennatula is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
