Asclepias uncialis

Asclepias uncialis, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, orange-tipped clusters of flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, moist habitats.

Asclepias uncialis is an important host plant for monarch butterflies, providing essential nectar and supporting the life cycle of these iconic insects.
