Abies sibirica f. glauca

Abies sibirica f. glauca, commonly known as the Siberian fir var. glauca, is a variant of the Siberian fir, native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is characterized by its bluish-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance compared to the typical green needles of the species. The plant is a coniferous evergreen tree, typically reaching heights of 10 to 20 meters. Its cones are cylindrical and typically measure around 5 to 7 centimeters in length.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in colder regions for landscaping purposes.
