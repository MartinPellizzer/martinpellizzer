Aristida hygrometrica

Aristida hygrometrica, commonly known as the variable dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, narrow leaves that are often tinged with silver or gray. The plant produces delicate, fluffy seed heads that are highly visible in the late summer and early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies, meadows, and open woodlands.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for native plant gardens.
