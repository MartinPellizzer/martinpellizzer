Anthemis tenuiloba var. radiata

Anthemis tenuiloba var. radiata, commonly known as the narrowleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-green foliage and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces tiny, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in herbal remedies.
