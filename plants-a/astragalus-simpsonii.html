Astragalus simpsonii

Astragalus simpsonii, commonly known as Simpson's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with multiple small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus simpsonii is often found in sagebrush steppe and other xeric ecosystems.
