Acalypha pilocardia

Acalypha pilocardia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silky ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often surrounded by bracts that are brightly colored. Its woody stems are sturdy and can grow quite tall, making it a hardy species adapted to various soil types.

Acalypha pilocardia is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and resilience in warm climates.
