Andropogon gabonensis

Andropogon gabonensis, commonly known as Gabon grass, is a tall perennial grass native to Central and West Africa.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swampy areas and riverbanks. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in humid tropical climates.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
