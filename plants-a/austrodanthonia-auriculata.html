Austrodanthonia auriculata

Austrodanthonia auriculata, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and tufted inflorescences that resemble small ears, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and thrives in full sun, often found in coastal and inland grasslands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native landscaping.

Austrodanthonia auriculata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
