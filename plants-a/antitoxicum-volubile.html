Antitoxicum volubile

Antitoxicum volubile, commonly known as the wandering antidote, is a climbing vine native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant contains various alkaloids that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties, including detoxifying effects. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, often found along forest edges or in shaded gardens.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
