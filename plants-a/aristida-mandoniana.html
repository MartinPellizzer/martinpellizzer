Aristida mandoniana

Aristida mandoniana, commonly known as the Mandon's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Aristida mandoniana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and low maintenance requirements.
