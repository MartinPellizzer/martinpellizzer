Areca communis

Areca communis, commonly known as the betel nut palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered fruits containing edible seeds. The seeds, known as betel nuts, are widely used in traditional chewing practices.

Botanically, Areca communis is classified as a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes other palms.
