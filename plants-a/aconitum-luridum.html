Aconitum luridum

Aconitum luridum, commonly known as the yellow aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
