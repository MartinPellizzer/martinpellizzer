Androcymbium melanthioides

Androcymbium melanthioides, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and clustered, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are typically small, fleshy, and alternate, while the flowers are small and inconspicuous.

It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
