Alliaria canalensis

Alliaria canalensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf garlic mustard, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants, and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and small white or purple flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its invasive tendencies in some regions, it can outcompete native flora and disrupt local ecosystems.
