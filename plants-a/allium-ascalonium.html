Allium ascalonium

Allium ascalonium, commonly known as the Egyptian leek, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often mistaken for true bulbs, though they are more accurately classified as bulbils. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a short, stout stem.

Allium ascalonium is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses throughout history.
