Amsinckia spectabilis var. spectabilis

Amsinckia spectabilis var. spectabilis, commonly known as the showy amsinckia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and the flowers are typically borne in terminal clusters.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy blooms.
