Acacia eriopoda

Acacia eriopoda, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flowerheads that bloom in late winter to early spring. Acacia eriopoda is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rehabilitation of degraded soils.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various native wildlife species.
