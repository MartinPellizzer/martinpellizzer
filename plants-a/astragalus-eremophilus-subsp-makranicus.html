Astragalus eremophilus subsp. makranicus

Astragalus eremophilus subsp.

makranicus, commonly known as the Makranic milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid regions of Pakistan and Iran. It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Its subspecific classification highlights its distinct ecological and morphological traits compared to other Astragalus species.
