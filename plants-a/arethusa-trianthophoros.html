Arethusa trianthophoros

Arethusa trianthophoros, commonly known as the sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils near the shoreline, often growing in brackish or saltwater environments. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Arethusa trianthophoros plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
