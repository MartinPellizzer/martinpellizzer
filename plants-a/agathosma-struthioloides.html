Agathosma struthioloides

Agathosma struthioloides, commonly known as wild fennel, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Its essential oils, rich in monoterpenes, contribute to its use in aromatherapy and herbal remedies.
