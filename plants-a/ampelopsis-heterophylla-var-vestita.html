Ampelopsis heterophylla var. vestita

Ampelopsis heterophylla var. vestita, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is known for its vigorous growth and is often used as a decorative vine in gardens.

Its leaves are also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
