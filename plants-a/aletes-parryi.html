Aletes parryi

Aletes parryi, commonly known as the Parry's aletes, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale green to yellowish leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Aletes parryi is a low-growing perennial that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
