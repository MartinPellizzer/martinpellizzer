Astragalus saccatus

Astragalus saccatus, commonly known as the sand milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Astragalus saccatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and can be used in ecological restoration projects.
