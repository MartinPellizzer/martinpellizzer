Astragalus beypazaricus

Astragalus beypazaricus, commonly known as the Turkish milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus beypazaricus is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
