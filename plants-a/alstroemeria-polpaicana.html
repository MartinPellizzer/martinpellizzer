Alstroemeria polpaicana

Alstroemeria polpaicana, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions and prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Alstroemeria polpaicana is also noted for its resilience to cooler climates and its ability to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.
