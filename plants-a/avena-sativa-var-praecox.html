Avena sativa var. praecox

Avena sativa var. praecox, commonly known as early barley, is a botanical variant of barley characterized by its early maturity and adaptability to temperate climates.

This variety is typically shorter and more compact than standard barley, with a shorter growing season, making it suitable for regions with shorter summers. It produces cylindrical spikelets with leathery, glabrous lemma and palea, and its grains are usually hulled, containing a high percentage of dietary fiber. The plant is often cultivated for forage, livestock feed, and in some cases, as a grain crop.

Its genetic traits make it a valuable resource in agricultural breeding programs for resilience and yield optimization.
