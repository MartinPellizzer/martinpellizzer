Aquilegia formosa var. communis

Aquilegia formosa var. communis, commonly known as the Western columbine, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features striking blue and yellow spurred petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, with palmate leaves that have five to seven lobes. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its showy flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to mountainous and alpine habitats.
