Astragalus susianus

Astragalus susianus, commonly known as the Susian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus susianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
