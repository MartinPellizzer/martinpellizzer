Alopecurus arundinaceus f. breviaristatus

Alopecurus arundinaceus f. breviaristatus, commonly known as the short-awned false oat, is a grass species characterized by its compact growth habit and reduced awns.

It typically features slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, two-lobed spikelets. This variety is often found in moist meadows and along streambanks, thriving in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
