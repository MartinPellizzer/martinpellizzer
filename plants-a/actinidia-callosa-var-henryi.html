Actinidia callosa var. henryi

Actinidia callosa var. henryi, commonly known as the Chinese Actinidia or Henry's Actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, fuzzy berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The berries are used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of edible fruit.
