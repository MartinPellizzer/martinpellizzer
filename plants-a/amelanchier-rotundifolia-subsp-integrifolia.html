Amelanchier rotundifolia subsp. integrifolia

Amelanchier rotundifolia subsp.

integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaf serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America. It features oval, smooth-edged leaves that turn yellow in the fall.

The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible red berries in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
