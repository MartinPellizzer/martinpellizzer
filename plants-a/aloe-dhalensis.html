Aloe dhalensis

Aloe dhalensis, commonly known as the Dhalen's aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features fleshy, upright leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its harsh environment. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear yellowish-green flowers, which bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Aloe dhalensis is valued for its ability to thrive in low-water conditions, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and succulent gardens.
