Alchemilla iranica

Alchemilla iranica, commonly known as Persian lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Iran.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Alchemilla iranica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
