Ampelovitis davidi

Ampelovitis davidi, commonly known as the David's climbing vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and hanging baskets.
