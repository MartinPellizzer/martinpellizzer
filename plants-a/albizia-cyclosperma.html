Albizia cyclosperma

Albizia cyclosperma, commonly known as the African silk tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The tree produces long, flat pods that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It is often found in dry savannas and is valued for its ornamental beauty and potential ecological benefits.

Albizia cyclosperma also has traditional uses in some African cultures for medicinal and cultural purposes.
