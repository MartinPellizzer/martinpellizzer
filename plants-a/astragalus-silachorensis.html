Astragalus silachorensis

Astragalus silachorensis, commonly known as the Silachorensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces slender, cylindrical seed pods.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
