Aspalathus intricata subsp. anthospermoides

Aspalathus intricata subsp.

anthospermoides, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of bright red flowers, which are typically borne in terminal panicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
