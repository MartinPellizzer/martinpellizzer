Allium pallens var. purpureum

Allium pallens var. purpureum, commonly known as purple garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its distinctive purple tinge in the leaves and flower stems, which sets it apart from other allium species. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild garlic-like flavor, making it suitable for both gardens and culinary use.
