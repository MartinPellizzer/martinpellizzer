Amaranthus torreyi var. suffruticosus

Amaranthus torreyi var. suffruticosus, commonly known as the desert amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub-like species that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. The plant features small, green leaves and produces clusters of red or orange flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fibrous stems and leaves have been historically used by indigenous peoples for food and fiber.

The species is valued for its resilience and ornamental qualities in xeriscaping.
