Areca macrocalyx var. intermedia

Areca macrocalyx var. intermedia, commonly known as the giant areca palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in landscaping and indoor gardening.
