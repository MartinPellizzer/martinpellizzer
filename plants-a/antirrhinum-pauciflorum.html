Antirrhinum pauciflorum

Antirrhinum pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tubular, bilabiate flowers that resemble a dragon's mouth, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and orange.

Antirrhinum pauciflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
