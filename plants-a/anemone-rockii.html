Anemone rockii

Anemone rockii, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features showy, single or double flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate and hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Anemone rockii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
