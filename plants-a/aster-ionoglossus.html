Aster ionoglossus

Aster ionoglossus, commonly known as the showy aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its showy, lavender to purple flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
