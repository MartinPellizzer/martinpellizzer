Actinidia arguta var. hypoleuca

Actinidia arguta var. hypoleuca, commonly known as the silver vine, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silvery-gray foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early summer, followed by edible, yellowish berries in late summer. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a distinctive silvery underside.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional medicine.
