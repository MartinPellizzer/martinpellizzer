Acaena pallida

Acaena pallida, commonly known as the slender burrplant, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its small, spiny fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a woolly, fibrous coating. The plant typically thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, where it forms dense mats on the ground. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with fine hairs.

Acaena pallida plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native invertebrates.
