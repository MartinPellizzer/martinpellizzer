Arctostaphylos purissima subsp. purissima

Arctostaphylos purissima subsp.

purissima, commonly known as the coastal manzanita, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California and southern Oregon. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant produces small, leathery berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salt exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
