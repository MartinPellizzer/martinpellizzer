Allardia

Allardia, commonly known as the redroot germander, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are reddish in color, giving it its common name.

Allardia is often used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
