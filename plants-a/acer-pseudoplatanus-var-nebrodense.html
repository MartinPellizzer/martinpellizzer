Acer pseudoplatanus var. nebrodense

Acer pseudoplatanus var. nebrodense, commonly known as the Nebrodense maple, is a variant of the common maple, characterized by its distinctive foliage and growth habits.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Italy and southern France, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a serrated margin and a deep green color that turns vibrant red or orange in autumn. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic value.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for a variety of landscaping applications.
