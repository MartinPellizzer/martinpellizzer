Anthyllis hystrix

Anthyllis hystrix, commonly known as the prickly helix, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its stems are covered in stiff, bristle-like hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Anthyllis hystrix is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
