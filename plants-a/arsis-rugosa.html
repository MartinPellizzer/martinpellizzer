Arsis rugosa

Arsis rugosa, commonly known as the rough arsis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a rough, textured surface, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
