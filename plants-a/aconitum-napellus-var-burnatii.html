Aconitum napellus var. burnatii

Aconitum napellus var. burnatii, commonly known as the Burnatii monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically dark green and velvety in texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
