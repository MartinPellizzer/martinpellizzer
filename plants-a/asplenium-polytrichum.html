Asplenium polytrichum

Asplenium polytrichum, commonly known as the hairy maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often have a reddish or brownish hue when young. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are wiry and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Asplenium polytrichum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
