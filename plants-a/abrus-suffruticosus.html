Abrus suffruticosus

Abrus suffruticosus, commonly known as the Indian bead tree or velvet bean plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring compound leaves and showy, crimson or red flowers. The plant produces distinctive, bead-like seeds enclosed in a fibrous pod, which are often used in traditional crafts.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental and agricultural purposes.
