Anemone bonatiana var. geum

Anemone bonatiana var. geum, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features showy, single or double flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often hairy and grayish in texture. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in naturalized settings.
