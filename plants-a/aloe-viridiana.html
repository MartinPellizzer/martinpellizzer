Aloe viridiana

Aloe viridiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to the Arabian Peninsula and parts of Africa.

It features upright, slender stems with fleshy, dark green leaves that have a waxy coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, typically blooming in the spring. Aloe viridiana is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its leaves contain a gel that has been used traditionally for its soothing properties.
