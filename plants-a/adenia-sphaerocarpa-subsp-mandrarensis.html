Adenia sphaerocarpa subsp. mandrarensis

Adenia sphaerocarpa subsp.

mandrarensis, commonly known as the Madagascar star-apple, is a shrub native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers.

The plant produces fleshy, yellow or orange berries that are edible and have a sweet, acidic flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
