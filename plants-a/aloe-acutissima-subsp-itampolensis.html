Aloe acutissima subsp. itampolensis

Aloe acutissima subsp.

itampolensis, commonly known as the Itampolensis Aloe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its narrow, spiky leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow flowers, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticultural enthusiasts.
