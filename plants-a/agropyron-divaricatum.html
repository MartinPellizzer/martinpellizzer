Agropyron divaricatum

Agropyron divaricatum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

Agropyron divaricatum is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in alpine and arid regions.
