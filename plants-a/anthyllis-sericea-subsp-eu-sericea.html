Anthyllis sericea subsp. eu-sericea

Anthyllis sericea subsp.

eu-sericea, commonly known as silky anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
