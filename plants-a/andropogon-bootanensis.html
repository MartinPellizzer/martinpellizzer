Andropogon bootanensis

Andropogon bootanensis, commonly known as the Bornean grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes many economically important grasses. The plant typically grows in wetland and swampy habitats, thriving in humid, tropical climates. Its tall, cylindrical inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often tightly packed.

Andropogon bootanensis is characterized by its fibrous root system and narrow, blade-like leaves with parallel venation.
