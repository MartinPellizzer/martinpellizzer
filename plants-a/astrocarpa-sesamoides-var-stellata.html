Astrocarpa sesamoides var. stellata

Astrocarpa sesamoides var. stellata, commonly known as the star-leaf sesamoid, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
