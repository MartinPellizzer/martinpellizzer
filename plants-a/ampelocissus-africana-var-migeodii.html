Ampelocissus africana var. migeodii

Ampelocissus africana var. migeodii, commonly known as the African grape or migeodii grape, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, grape-like berries. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing along fences or trees. Its berries are used in traditional medicine and as a food source, while its vines are valued for their ornamental appeal.

The plant is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
