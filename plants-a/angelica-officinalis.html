Angelica officinalis

Angelica officinalis, commonly known as garden angelica, is a tall biennial or perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features large, compound leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, with a distinctive heart-shaped base. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in complex umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick, fleshy, and aromatic, containing compounds like volatile oils and coumarin.

Angelica officinalis is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and as a source of essential oils.
