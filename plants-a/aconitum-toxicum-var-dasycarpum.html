Aconitum toxicum var. dasycarpum

Aconitum toxicum var. dasycarpum, commonly known as the hairy-fruited monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, typical of the genus Aconitum. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is known for its highly toxic alkaloids, which make it dangerous to both humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its distinctive appearance and toxicity make it a notable species in both horticulture and botanical studies.
