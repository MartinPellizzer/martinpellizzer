Amelanchier alnifolia var. elliptica

Amelanchier alnifolia var. elliptica, commonly known as the Saskatoon berry or buffalo berry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue to black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and woodland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
