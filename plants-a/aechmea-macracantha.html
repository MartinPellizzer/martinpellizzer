Aechmea macracantha

Aechmea macracantha, commonly known as the giant bromeliad, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its large, rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant's leaves are typically broad, stiff, and covered in a dense layer of fine, white trichomes, which help in water retention and light reflection. It produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear striking, long-lasting flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

Aechmea macracantha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
