Andropogon serrulatus

Andropogon serrulatus, commonly known as the rough bluestem, is a grass species native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, bluish-green leaves with serrated edges. This plant typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and along stream banks, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Andropogon serrulatus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wildlife.

Its seeds are a food source for various birds and small mammals.
