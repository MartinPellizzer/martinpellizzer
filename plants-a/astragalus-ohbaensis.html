Astragalus ohbaensis

Astragalus ohbaensis, commonly known as Ohba's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions. Astragalus ohbaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization in its native environment.
