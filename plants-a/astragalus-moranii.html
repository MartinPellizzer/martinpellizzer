Astragalus moranii

Astragalus moranii, commonly known as the Moran's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Astragalus moranii is often found in steppes and mountainous areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
