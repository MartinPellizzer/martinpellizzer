Alchemilla wischnewskii

Alchemilla wischnewskii, commonly known as the Wischnewskii lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
