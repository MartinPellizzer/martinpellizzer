Apocynum basikurumon

Apocynum basikurumon, commonly known as the American dogbane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland areas. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids and has been used historically for medicinal purposes.
