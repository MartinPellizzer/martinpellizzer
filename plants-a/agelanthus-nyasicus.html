Agelanthus nyasicus

Agelanthus nyasicus, commonly known as the African stone plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, commonly referred to as the ice plant family, and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
