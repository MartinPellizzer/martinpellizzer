Agapetes nana

Agapetes nana, commonly known as the dwarf silver fir, is a small evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with a compact, rounded form. The plant features dense, dark green leaves that are leathery and covered in a fine layer of silvery-white wax. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, red berries that are a food source for various wildlife.

Agapetes nana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.
