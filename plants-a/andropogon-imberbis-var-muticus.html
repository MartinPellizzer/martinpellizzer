Andropogon imberbis var. muticus

Andropogon imberbis var. muticus, commonly known as the mute bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, blue-green blades and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open woodlands and prairies. Its flowering stems are usually shorter than the leaves, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
