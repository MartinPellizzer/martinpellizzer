Acetosella multifida

Acetosella multifida, commonly known as the mouse ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Geraniaceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of small, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, five-petaled flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is commonly found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Acetosella multifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover.
