Astragalus hilaris

Astragalus hilaris, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Astragalus hilaris plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
