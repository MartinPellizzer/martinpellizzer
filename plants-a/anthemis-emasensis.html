Anthemis emasensis

Anthemis emasensis, commonly known as the Emas goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small yellow flowers that are often clustered in a single head, with narrow, grass-like leaves.

Anthemis emasensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
