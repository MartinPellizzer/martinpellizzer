Aronia lancifolia

Aronia lancifolia, commonly known as chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves that turn bright red in the fall. The plant produces small, red berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its flowers are white and clustered, appearing in early summer.

Aronia lancifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
