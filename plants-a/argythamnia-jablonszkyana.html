Argythamnia jablonszkyana

Argythamnia jablonszkyana, commonly known as the Turkish tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Tamaricaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance. It typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
