Aristida pungens var. pubescens

Aristida pungens var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy dropseed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hair-covered stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are typically greenish or pale yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies, meadows, and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
