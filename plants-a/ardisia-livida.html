Ardisia livida

Ardisia livida, commonly known as the silverleaf ardisia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. Its small, white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits.

Ardisia livida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and seasonal fruit display.
