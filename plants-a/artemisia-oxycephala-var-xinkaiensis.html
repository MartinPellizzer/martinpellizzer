Artemisia oxycephala var. xinkaiensis

Artemisia oxycephala var. xinkaiensis, commonly known as the Xinkai wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to northern China.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments. It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.

Its essential oils contain compounds such as thujone and camphor, contributing to its distinctive scent and potential therapeutic uses.
