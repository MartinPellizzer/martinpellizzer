Acacia sieberiana var. woodii

Acacia sieberiana var. woodii, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its seeds are encased in pod-like structures that split open when mature.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
