Artemisia norvegica

Artemisia norvegica, commonly known as mountain wormwood, is a perennial herb native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage with finely divided leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant thrives in dry, rocky, and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine regions. It contains volatile oils and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Artemisia norvegica is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
