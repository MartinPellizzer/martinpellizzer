Aclinia

Aclinia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Apocynaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plants are typically shrubs or small trees, native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and the Americas. Aclinia species produce capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, and their leaves are usually opposite and ovate.

The genus is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral displays.
