Astragalus moellendorffii

Astragalus moellendorffii, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus moellendorffii is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria.
