Angelica polymorpha var. sinensis

Angelica polymorpha var. sinensis, commonly known as Chinese angelica or Dahurian angelica, is a biennial or perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves and tall, clustered flower heads that range from white to pink. The plant produces a strong, aromatic root that is widely used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both medicinal and ecological contexts.
