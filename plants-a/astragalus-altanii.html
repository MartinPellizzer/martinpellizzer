Astragalus altanii

Astragalus altanii, commonly known as the Altanii milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Astragalus altanii is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ecological role includes contributing to soil stabilization and providing nectar for pollinators.
