Abutilon subsagittatum

Abutilon subsagittatum, commonly known as the arrowleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 feet. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to xeriscapes and native plant gardens.
