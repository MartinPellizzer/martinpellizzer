Amygdalus orientalis

Amygdalus orientalis, commonly known as the oriental peach, is a deciduous tree native to Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, white or pink flowers in spring, followed by fuzzy, orange-red fruits. The fruit contains a single hard pit and is valued for its sweet, aromatic flesh.

It is widely cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
