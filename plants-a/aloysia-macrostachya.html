Aloysia macrostachya

Aloysia macrostachya, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic foliage and as a source of essential oils. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice in both ornamental gardening and herbal medicine.
