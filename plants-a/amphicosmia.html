Amphicosmia

Amphicosmia, commonly known as the double-flowered cosmos, is a striking plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, double blooms that consist of both ray and disk florets, creating a full, rounded appearance. Native to Mexico, this plant thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and sturdy.

Amphicosmia prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, long-lasting flowers.
