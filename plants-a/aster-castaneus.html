Aster castaneus

Aster castaneus, commonly known as the redstem aster, is a flowering plant native to the United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its reddish stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many familiar flowers like daisies and sunflowers.

Aster castaneus plays an important role in providing nectar for pollinators during the late season.
