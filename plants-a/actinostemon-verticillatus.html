Actinostemon verticillatus

Actinostemon verticillatus, commonly known as the whorled stemless sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features a rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in a whorled arrangement at the top of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a distinctive addition to botanical collections.
