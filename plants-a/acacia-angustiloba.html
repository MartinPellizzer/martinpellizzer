Acacia angustiloba

Acacia angustiloba, commonly known as narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which attract a variety of pollinators. It is well adapted to dry and arid environments, making it a hardy species for coastal and sandy soils.

Acacia angustiloba is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
