Aloe rabaiensis

Aloe rabaiensis, commonly known as the Rabai aloe, is a succulent plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and fleshy, narrow leaves that are covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in raceme-like clusters, which bloom during the dry season. Aloe rabaiensis is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick leaves.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
