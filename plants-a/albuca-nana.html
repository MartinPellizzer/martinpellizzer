Albuca nana

Albuca nana, commonly known as the dwarf albuca, is a small, bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its compact size and ornamental flowers, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, decorative plant.
