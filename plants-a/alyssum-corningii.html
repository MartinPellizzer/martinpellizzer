Alyssum corningii

Alyssum corningii, commonly known as Corning's alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant typically reaches a height of 10 to 20 centimeters and has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems.

Alyssum corningii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
