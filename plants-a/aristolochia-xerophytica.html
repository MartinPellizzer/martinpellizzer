Aristolochia xerophytica

Aristolochia xerophytica, commonly known as the desert dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, aromatic scent. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its dry habitat. Its fruit is a dry, capsule that contains several seeds.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
