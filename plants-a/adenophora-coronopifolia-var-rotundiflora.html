Adenophora coronopifolia var. rotundiflora

Adenophora coronopifolia var. rotundiflora, commonly known as the roundflower bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features opposite, lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
