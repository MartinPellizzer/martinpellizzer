Arabis ambigua var. glabra

Arabis ambigua var. glabra, commonly known as smooth rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features basal rosettes of smooth, lanceolate leaves and produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers. The plant is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is characterized by its glabrous (smooth) surface and is typically found in tundra and mountainous areas.

This variety is part of the Brassicaceae family, known for its mustard-like flowers and edible species.
