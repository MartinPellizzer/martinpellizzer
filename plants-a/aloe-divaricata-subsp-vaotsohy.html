Aloe divaricata subsp. vaotsohy

Aloe divaricata subsp.

vaotsohy, commonly known as the Madagascar aloe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are fleshy and covered in a whitish, powdery coating called farina.

The leaves are typically blue-green in color and have a serrated margin. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
