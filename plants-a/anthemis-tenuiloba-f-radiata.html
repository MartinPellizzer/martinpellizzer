Anthemis tenuiloba f. radiata

Anthemis tenuiloba f. radiata, commonly known as the radiate tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in a radiate pattern, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
