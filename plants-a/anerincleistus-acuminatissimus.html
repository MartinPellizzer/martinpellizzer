Anerincleistus acuminatissimus

Anerincleistus acuminatissimus, commonly known as the sharp-pointed cycad, is a unique species of cycad native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces cones, with the male cones being cylindrical and the female cones being ovoid, both contributing to its reproductive strategy. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
