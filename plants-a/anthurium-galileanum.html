Anthurium galileanum

Anthurium galileanum, commonly known as the Galilean anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spadix-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often a deep green with a glossy texture, and it produces colorful flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow.

Anthurium galileanum is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, indirect light conditions.
