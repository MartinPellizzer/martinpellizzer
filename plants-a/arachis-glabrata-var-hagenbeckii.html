Arachis glabrata var. hagenbeckii

Arachis glabrata var. hagenbeckii, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a species of legume native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody pods containing edible seeds, which are rich in nutrients and oils. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it plays a significant role in both local ecosystems and global nut production.
