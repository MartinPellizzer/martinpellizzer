Atriplex verrucifera

Atriplex verrucifera, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Atriplex verrucifera is often found in sandy soils and can thrive in harsh, saline conditions.

Its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
