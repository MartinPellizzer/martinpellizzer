Anabasis setifera

Anabasis setifera, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a fibrous root system adapted to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white wax to reduce water loss.

Anabasis setifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
