Arabis dentata var. phalacrocarpa

Arabis dentata var. phalacrocarpa, commonly known as the sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, toothed leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found along sea shores and cliffs. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
