Agave chloracantha var. brauniana

Agave chloracantha var. brauniana, commonly known as the Braun's agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The leaves are typically narrow and pointed, with a distinctive margin that may have small teeth or spines. It produces a tall, branched inflorescence with yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
