Acoridium tenellum

Acoridium tenellum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Araceae, which includes many plants with showy inflorescences. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention. Its underground stems, or rhizomes, are the primary part used for culinary purposes.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for its starchy roots.
