Aquilegia flaviflora

Aquilegia flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with five petals and a distinctive central nectar spur. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in open woodlands and alpine meadows, where it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
