Asperula cynanchica var. scabra

Asperula cynanchica var. scabra, commonly known as the rough woodruff, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a rough, hairy texture that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in woodland or shaded areas and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has a historical use in herbal medicine.
