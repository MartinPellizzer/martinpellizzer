Atelandra polystachya

Atelandra polystachya, commonly known as the broadleaf screw-pine, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is characterized by its palm-like appearance, with long, strap-shaped leaves that are often waxy and succulent. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically in shades of yellow and green. It thrives in saline and tidal environments, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
