Aquilegia buergeriana var. oxysepala

Aquilegia buergeriana var. oxysepala, commonly known as the Buerger's columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features showy, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and woodland borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
