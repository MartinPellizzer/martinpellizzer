Artemisia umbrosa

Artemisia umbrosa, commonly known as western wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that appear in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and mountainous regions.

Artemisia umbrosa is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
