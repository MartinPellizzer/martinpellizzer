Agropyron rifeum

Agropyron rifeum, commonly known as the needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the colder regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along the edges of forests. Agropyron rifeum is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion due to its extensive root system.

It also serves as a forage plant for livestock in certain regions.
