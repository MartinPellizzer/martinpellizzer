Artemisia seriphium var. oligantha

Artemisia seriphium var. oligantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mugwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and small, gray-green leaves. The plant produces fluffy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
