Acacia maconochieana

Acacia maconochieana, commonly known as the Maconochie's wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, feather-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers during the warmer months. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system to access water. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Acacia maconochieana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
