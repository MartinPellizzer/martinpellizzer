Artemisia vulgaris subsp. urjanchaica

Artemisia vulgaris subsp.

urjanchaica, commonly known as the Mongolian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant has aromatic, silvery-gray leaves with a strong, camphor-like scent. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer.

This subspecies is closely related to other Artemisia species and is often used in traditional medicine.
