Asplenium setosum

Asplenium setosum, commonly known as the hairy spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices or on tree trunks. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture on the underside. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium setosum is a hardy species that thrives in humid conditions and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
