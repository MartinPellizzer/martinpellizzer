Alphitonia excelsa var. franguloides

Alphitonia excelsa var. franguloides, commonly known as the fragrant ash or frangipani ash, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Australia.

It features smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic when crushed. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring and are followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.
