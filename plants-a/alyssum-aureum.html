Alyssum aureum

Alyssum aureum, commonly known as golden alyssum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Alyssum aureum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
