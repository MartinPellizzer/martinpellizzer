Amaranthus hybridus subsp. caudatus

Amaranthus hybridus subsp.

caudatus, commonly known as the love lie pillow, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas. It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and vibrant, feathery inflorescences that range from red to purple.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in panicles, which are often the most striking feature. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This species is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in some traditional cuisines.
