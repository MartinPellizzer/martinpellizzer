Arundo triodioides

Arundo triodioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf reed, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along waterways. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its rapid growth makes it both a useful plant for erosion control and a potential invasive species in non-native regions.
