Anthemis breviradiata

Anthemis breviradiata, commonly known as the short-rayed tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers with prominent central disk and ray florets. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The species is noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
