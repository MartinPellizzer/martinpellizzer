Achras brasiliensis

Achras brasiliensis, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a thick, buttressed trunk and broad, palm-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into large, yellowish fruits resembling bread. The fruit is edible when ripe and has a starchy, bread-like texture, making it an important food source in some regions.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
