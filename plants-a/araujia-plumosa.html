Araujia plumosa

Araujia plumosa, commonly known as the silky nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The plant has soft, hairy leaves and produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or near human settlements.

Due to its attractive appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
