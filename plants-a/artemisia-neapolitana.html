Artemisia neapolitana

Artemisia neapolitana, commonly known as Naples wormwood, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into small, dry fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone areas.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone, which contribute to its strong, medicinal aroma.
