Acacia pluriglandulosa

Acacia pluriglandulosa, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often silver-gray branches and its distinctive, feathery foliage. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that appear in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its small, flat pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in arid and semi-arid regions.

Acacia pluriglandulosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
