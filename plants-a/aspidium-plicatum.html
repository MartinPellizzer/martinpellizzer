Aspidium plicatum

Aspidium plicatum, commonly known as the pleated water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures that are typically divided into narrow, overlapping segments. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its rhizomes are often covered in a thin layer of spongy tissue, which helps in absorbing nutrients from the water.

Aspidium plicatum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water filtration.
