Allocarya muricata

Allocarya muricata, commonly known as the roughleaf acanthus, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, velvety leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Allocarya muricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
