Aegiphila grandiflora

Aegiphila grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in the spring.

Aegiphila grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.

Morphology

Aegiphila grandiflora has leaves that are opposite in arrangement, ovate in shape, with a glossy dark green color and a rough texture.

The stem is upright, with a smooth, grayish-brown bark and a bushy growth pattern. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, featuring tubular petals in shades of pink to purple, blooming during the summer months, and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a small capsule that disperses seeds via wind.

The roots are fibrous and help anchor the plant in rocky or sandy soils.
