Adiantum tomentosum

Adiantum tomentosum, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that are covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety hairs. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its unique appearance and delicate structure make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The fronds are typically evergreen, maintaining their lush appearance throughout the year.
