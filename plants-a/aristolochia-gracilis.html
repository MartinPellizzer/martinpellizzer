Aristolochia gracilis

Aristolochia gracilis, commonly known as the slender Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular, heart-shaped leaves and its striking, purple-tinged flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic, often spicy scent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
