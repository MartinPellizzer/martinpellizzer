Antenoron racemosum

Antenoron racemosum, commonly known as the mountain cedar, is a member of the Cupressaceae family and is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a deciduous conifer that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its scale-like leaves and reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody cones that release seeds in the spring. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common component of chaparral and woodland ecosystems.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
