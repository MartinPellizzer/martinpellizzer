Alpinia glabra var. glabra

Alpinia glabra var. glabra, commonly known as the white ginger or white alpine ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, rhizomatous growth habit and produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Alpinia glabra var. glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
