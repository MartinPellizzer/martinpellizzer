Acalypha variegata

Acalypha variegata, commonly known as the variegated copperleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its strikingly variegated leaves, which often display combinations of green, white, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

Acalypha variegata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
