Anil frutescens

Anil frutescens, commonly known as wild betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Anil frutescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
