Aster eremophilus

Aster eremophilus, commonly known as the desert aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Aster eremophilus plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies in its native habitat.
