Aster grisebachii

Aster grisebachii, commonly known as the gray aster, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually pale to grayish blue, with ray florets that are shorter than the disk florets.

Aster grisebachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
