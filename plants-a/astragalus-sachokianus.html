Astragalus sachokianus

Astragalus sachokianus, commonly known as the Sachokian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Astragalus sachokianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
