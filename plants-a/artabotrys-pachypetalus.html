Artabotrys pachypetalus

Artabotrys pachypetalus, commonly known as the large-petalled artabotrys, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. Its flowers are notable for their long, slender petals and are often used in traditional floral arrangements.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic appeal.
