Asplenium nigrum

Asplenium nigrum, commonly known as the black spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often densely packed and slightly curled. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rocky crevices or on tree trunks. Its spores are produced on the underside of the leaves in clusters called sori.

Asplenium nigrum is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
