Asarum okinawense

Asarum okinawense, commonly known as Okinawa wild ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces a unique, hooded flower that is typically greenish-brown in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
