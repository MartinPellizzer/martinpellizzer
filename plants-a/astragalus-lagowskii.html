Astragalus lagowskii

Astragalus lagowskii, commonly known as the Lagowski milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.

Astragalus lagowskii is also of interest in botanical research due to its potential medicinal properties.
