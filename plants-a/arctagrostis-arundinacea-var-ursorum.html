Arctagrostis arundinacea var. ursorum

Arctagrostis arundinacea var. ursorum, commonly known as the eastern gamagrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of grasses. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
