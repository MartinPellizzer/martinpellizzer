Asteriscium aemocarpon

Asteriscium aemocarpon, commonly known as the golden starbursts, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that resemble tiny starbursts. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern around the stems. Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and limited geographic distribution.

Due to its rarity, it is often cultivated in specialized horticultural collections.
