Amaryllis oconequensis

Amaryllis oconequensis, commonly known as the Oconee amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America, specifically found in the humid forests of northern Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces a tall stem with narrow, strap-like leaves and blooms during the summer months.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light for optimal growth.
