Anaphalis contorta var. pellucida

Anaphalis contorta var. pellucida, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features slender, hairy stems that are often covered in fine, white to grayish pubescence, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or pale green, arranged in dense, cotton-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
