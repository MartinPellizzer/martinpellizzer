Albizia vaillantii

Albizia vaillantii, commonly known as the white acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are trifoliate and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that split open when mature.

Albizia vaillantii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
