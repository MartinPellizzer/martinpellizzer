Alstroemeria lacrima-solis

Alstroemeria lacrima-solis, commonly known as the "tears of the sun," is a striking flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of tubular flowers that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its name reflects the cultural significance and visual impact of its tear-shaped petals.
