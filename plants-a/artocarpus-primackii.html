Artocarpus primackii

Artocarpus primackii, commonly known as the Philippine breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spiny fruits. The fruit, which is typically green when mature, contains a soft, starchy pulp surrounded by a fibrous husk. It is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in traditional dishes and as a source of nutrition.

The plant also plays a role in local ecosystems and has cultural significance in the regions where it is found.
