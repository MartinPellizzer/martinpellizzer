Aphelandra squarrosa var. citrina

Aphelandra squarrosa var. citrina, commonly known as the Lemon Tree of the Forest, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that often have a yellowish or variegated appearance. The plant produces striking, drooping inflorescences with bright yellow bracts that resemble flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Aphelandra squarrosa var. citrina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
