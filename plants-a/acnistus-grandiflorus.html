Acnistus grandiflorus

Acnistus grandiflorus, commonly known as the largeflower bladdernut, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green, clustered flowers that appear in late spring, followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife.
