Acer heldreichii var. macropterum

Acer heldreichii var. macropterum, commonly known as the large-winged maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves with long, narrow lobes that give it a unique appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas with a temperate climate.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
