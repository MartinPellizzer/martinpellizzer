Aristida stipitata var. robusta

Aristida stipitata var. robusta, commonly known as the robust false panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, often forming dense clumps. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue, and its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, pale florets. It is characterized by its sturdy, upright growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The species is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions, making it a resilient choice for ecological restoration projects.
