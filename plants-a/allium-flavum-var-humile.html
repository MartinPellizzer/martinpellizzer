Allium flavum var. humile

Allium flavum var. humile, commonly known as the dwarf garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spherical bulbs that resemble garlic cloves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value and mild garlic scent, it is often cultivated for both decorative and culinary purposes.
