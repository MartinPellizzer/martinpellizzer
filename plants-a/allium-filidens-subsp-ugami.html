Allium filidens subsp. ugami

Allium filidens subsp.

ugami, commonly known as the Ugami leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, cylindrical stems and broad, strap-like leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine, adding a mild garlic-like flavor to dishes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
