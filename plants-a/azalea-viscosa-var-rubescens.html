Azalea viscosa var. rubescens

Azalea viscosa var. rubescens, commonly known as the red-fruited azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from pink to deep red. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
