Acidosasa glauca

Acidosasa glauca, commonly known as the blue acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking floral display and drought tolerance.
