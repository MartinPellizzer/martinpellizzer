Allium ovalifolium var. leuconeurum

Allium ovalifolium var. leuconeurum, commonly known as the white bulbous onion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a bulb composed of layered, fleshy scales that store nutrients and water. The plant produces upright, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and range in color from white to pale purple.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and delicate blooms.
