Amaranthus affinis

Amaranthus affinis, commonly known as the smooth amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or red, depending on the variety. Its seeds are small and can be used as a grain or for ornamental purposes.

Amaranthus affinis is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and is often found in disturbed areas.
