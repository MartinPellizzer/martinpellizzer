Aglaia hoanensis

Aglaia hoanensis, commonly known as the Hoan's aglaia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Meliaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in umbels, and it produces woody capsules containing several seeds.

Aglaia hoanensis is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
