Agave asperrima subsp. potosiensis

Agave asperrima subsp.

potosiensis, commonly known as the Potosi agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico. It is characterized by its robust, spiny leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear yellow flowers, which are followed by small, edible fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its foliage is often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.
