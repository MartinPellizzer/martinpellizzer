Asperula stricta var. alpina

Asperula stricta var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine sweet pea, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often appearing in clusters. The plant has needle-like leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand cold and harsh mountain climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Asperula stricta var. alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
