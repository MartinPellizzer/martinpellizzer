Amelanchier carrii

Amelanchier carrii, commonly known as the Carri's serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet and produces clusters of white flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and edible, sweet berries that ripen in late summer. Amelanchier carrii thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

It is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
