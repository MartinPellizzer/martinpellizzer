Arenaria serpyllifolia var. viridis

Arenaria serpyllifolia var. viridis, commonly known as green sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal dunes or rocky coastal areas. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is notable for its ability to tolerate harsh coastal conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
