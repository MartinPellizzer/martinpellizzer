Apargia umbellata

Apargia umbellata, commonly known as the white fringed gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive fringed appearance. Apargia umbellata produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
