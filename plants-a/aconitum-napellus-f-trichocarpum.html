Aconitum napellus f. trichocarpum

Aconitum napellus f. trichocarpum, commonly known as the trichocarpum form of European aconite, is a variant of the common aconite.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy fruiting structures that set it apart from the typical form. The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to certain regions of Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Its foliage consists of deeply lobed leaves, and it produces striking blue to purple flowers.
