Aristea caerulea

Aristea caerulea, commonly known as the blue aristea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces striking blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to arid landscape designs.
