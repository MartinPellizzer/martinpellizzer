Acacia emarginata

Acacia emarginata, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned branches and small, bipinnate leaves that are typically deciduous during dry seasons. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. Acacia emarginata is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.

It also plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
