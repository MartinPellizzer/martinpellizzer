Alpinia assimilis

Alpinia assimilis, commonly known as the Chinese ginger or mountain ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, leafy stalks and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, triangular berries that are typically red when mature. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Alpinia assimilis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
