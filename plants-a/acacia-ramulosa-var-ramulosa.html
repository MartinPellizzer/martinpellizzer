Acacia ramulosa var. ramulosa

Acacia ramulosa var. ramulosa, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for native pollinators and habitat for small fauna.
