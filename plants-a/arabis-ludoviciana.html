Arabis ludoviciana

Arabis ludoviciana, commonly known as the eastern rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open woods or along streambanks. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by rounded seed pods.

The leaves are basal, rosette-like, and have a slightly hairy texture.
