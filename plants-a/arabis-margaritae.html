Arabis margaritae

Arabis margaritae, commonly known as the white campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, woolly leaves and clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
