Ammannia luederitzii

Ammannia luederitzii, commonly known as the Luederitz ammannia, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming during the warmer months. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Ammannia luederitzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
