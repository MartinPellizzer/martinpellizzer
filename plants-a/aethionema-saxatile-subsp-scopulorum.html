Aethionema saxatile subsp. scopulorum

Aethionema saxatile subsp.

scopulorum, commonly known as the rock wallflower, is a flowering plant native to rocky habitats in the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, bright yellow or orange flowers in the spring.

The plant has compact, succulent-like leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
