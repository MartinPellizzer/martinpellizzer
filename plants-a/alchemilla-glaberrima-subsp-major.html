Alchemilla glaberrima subsp. major

Alchemilla glaberrima subsp.

major, commonly known as the broad-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its common name reflects its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
