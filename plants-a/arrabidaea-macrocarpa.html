Arrabidaea macrocarpa

Arrabidaea macrocarpa, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its hard, woody pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
