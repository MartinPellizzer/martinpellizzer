Atriplex paradoxa

Atriplex paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are pollinated by insects. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a hardy species adapted to extreme conditions.

Atriplex paradoxa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for native wildlife.
