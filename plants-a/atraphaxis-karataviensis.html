Atraphaxis karataviensis

Atraphaxis karataviensis, commonly known as the Karatavi sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it access groundwater.

Atraphaxis karataviensis is often found in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in areas with limited rainfall.
