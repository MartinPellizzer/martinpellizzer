Acacia catechu

Acacia catechu, commonly known as Indian acacia or catechu, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant is renowned for producing a dark brown gum called catechu, which is harvested from its bark. Acacia catechu thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands.

Its flowers are yellow and fragrant, attracting various pollinators.
