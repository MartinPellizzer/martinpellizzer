Aristida congesta var. pilifera

Aristida congesta var. pilifera, commonly known as the hairy dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
