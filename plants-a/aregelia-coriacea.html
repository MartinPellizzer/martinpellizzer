Aregelia coriacea

Aregelia coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf aregelia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving them a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Aregelia coriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
