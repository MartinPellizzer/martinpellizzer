Apeiba echinata var. macropetala

Apeiba echinata var. macropetala, commonly known as the giant apeiba, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The tree produces woody pods containing flat, edible seeds that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are compound and palmately arranged, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
