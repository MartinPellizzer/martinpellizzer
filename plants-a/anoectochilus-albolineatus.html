Anoectochilus albolineatus

Anoectochilus albolineatus, commonly known as the white-lined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and possesses fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Anoectochilus albolineatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
