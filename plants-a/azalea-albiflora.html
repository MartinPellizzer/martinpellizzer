Azalea albiflora

Azalea albiflora, commonly known as the white azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with leathery, dark green leaves. Its flowers are borne in dense clusters and are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Azalea albiflora prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
