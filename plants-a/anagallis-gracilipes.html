Anagallis gracilipes

Anagallis gracilipes, commonly known as the slender pink willow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, trailing stems and small, showy pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Anagallis gracilipes is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
