Amaryllis retinervia

Amaryllis retinervia, commonly known as the net-veined amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features strap-like leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Amaryllis retinervia is popular as a decorative plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
