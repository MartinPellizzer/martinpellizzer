Alchemilla camptopoda

Alchemilla camptopoda, commonly known as the bent-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in soft, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are used in traditional herbal medicine for their astringent properties.

Alchemilla camptopoda thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
