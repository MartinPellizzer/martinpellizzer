Allophylus jejunus

Allophylus jejunus, commonly known as the slender alder, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrublands.

Allophylus jejunus plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and provides habitat for various small animals and insects.
