Aster cabrerae

Aster cabrerae, commonly known as the Cabrera's aster, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. It typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.
