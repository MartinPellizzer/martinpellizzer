Ageratina prunifolia

Ageratina prunifolia, commonly known as the cherry-leaved ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of tiny flowers. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often hairy as well. It produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months.

Ageratina prunifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
