Anemone sibirica

Anemone sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a short, stout rhizome and produces showy, white or pale pink flowers with five to seven petals. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Anemone sibirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
