Astragalus incurvus var. pinguefactus

Astragalus incurvus var. pinguefactus, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

The variety is distinguished by its thickened, fleshy leaves and stems, which help it conserve water.
