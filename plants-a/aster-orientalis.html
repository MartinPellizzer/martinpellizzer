Aster orientalis

Aster orientalis, commonly known as the oriental aster, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including China and Korea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets that give it a daisy-like appearance.

Aster orientalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
