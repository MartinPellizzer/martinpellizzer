Acacia leucophloea var. melanochaetes

Acacia leucophloea var. melanochaetes, commonly known as the black-stemmed acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems that are covered in a layer of smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in globular heads, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous tiny, oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is adapted to drought conditions and is often used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
