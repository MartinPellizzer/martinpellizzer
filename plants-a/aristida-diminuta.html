Aristida diminuta

Aristida diminuta, commonly known as the dwarf threeawn, is a small grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 inches and has fine, wiry leaves that are often covered in a layer of silky hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It is well adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in poor soils, making it a common sight in disturbed or open habitats.

Its delicate appearance and drought tolerance make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
