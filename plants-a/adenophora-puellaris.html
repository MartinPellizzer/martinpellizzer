Adenophora puellaris

Adenophora puellaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Adenophora puellaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
