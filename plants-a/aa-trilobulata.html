Aa trilobulata

Aa trilobulata, commonly known as the three-lobed fern, is a unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its intricate, three-lobed fronds that resemble delicate lace. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients.

The fronds are often bioluminescent at night, a rare trait among ferns, which aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.
