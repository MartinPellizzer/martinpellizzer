Anthocleista zambesiaca

Anthocleista zambesiaca, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a woody stem and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
