Adenoropium ribifolium

Adenoropium ribifolium, commonly known as the red-flowered sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Adenoropium ribifolium is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
