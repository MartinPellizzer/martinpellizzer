Astragalus kentrophyta var. douglasii

Astragalus kentrophyta var. douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
