Aristida pallens f. rubelliana

Aristida pallens f. rubelliana, commonly known as the red-stemmed false brome, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in native plant gardens.
