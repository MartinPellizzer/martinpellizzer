Azalea rosiflora

Azalea rosiflora, commonly known as the roseleaf azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with leathery, dark green leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Azalea rosiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
