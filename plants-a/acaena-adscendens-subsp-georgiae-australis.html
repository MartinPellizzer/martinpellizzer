Acaena adscendens subsp. georgiae-australis

Acaena adscendens subsp.

georgiae-australis, commonly known as the New Zealand spinach bush, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand. It features small, spiny leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions. Its fruits are small, hairy achenes that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
