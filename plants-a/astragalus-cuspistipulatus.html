Astragalus cuspistipulatus

Astragalus cuspistipulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, lance-shaped leaflets, and its root system is fibrous and often extensive. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Astragalus cuspistipulatus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
