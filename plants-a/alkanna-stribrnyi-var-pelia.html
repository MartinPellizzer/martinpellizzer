Alkanna stribrnyi var. pelia

Alkanna stribrnyi var. pelia, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark that peels off in thin, papery layers. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental bark and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance in dry climates.
