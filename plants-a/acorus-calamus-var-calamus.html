Acorus calamus var. calamus

Acorus calamus var. calamus, commonly known as sweet flag, is a perennial herb native to wetlands and marshy areas in temperate regions.

It features narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense, umbrella-like clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its rhizomes are aromatic and have been historically used in various cultural and medicinal practices.

The plant thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies.
