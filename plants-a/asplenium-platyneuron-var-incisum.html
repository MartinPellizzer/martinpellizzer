Asplenium platyneuron var. incisum

Asplenium platyneuron var. incisum, commonly known as the cut-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern characterized by its finely divided, serrated fronds.

It is a perennial fern native to moist, shaded environments in temperate regions. The plant features delicate, dark green foliage with a distinctive, feathery appearance. It thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

The fronds are typically bioluminescent under certain conditions, adding an unusual visual appeal.
