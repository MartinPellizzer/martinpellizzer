Acaena anserinifolia var. antarctica

Acaena anserinifolia var. antarctica, commonly known as the Antarctic hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Southern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, alpine or subantarctic environments. The plant features small, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are inconspicuous, producing small, greenish-yellow blooms that are followed by spiny, burr-like fruits.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
