Acisanthera egleri

Acisanthera egleri, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The fruit, which is a legume, contains seeds that are sometimes used as a food source or in herbal remedies.
