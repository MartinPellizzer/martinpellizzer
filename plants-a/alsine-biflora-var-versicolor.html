Alsine biflora var. versicolor

Alsine biflora var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable alsike.

This plant is a flowering species native to parts of Europe and Asia, characterized by its small, delicate flowers that display a range of colors. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in meadows or along riverbanks. The plant has alternate, linear leaves and produces two distinct flower clusters per stem.

Its variability in flower color makes it a notable species for botanical study and garden cultivation.
