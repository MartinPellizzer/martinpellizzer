Aspalathus lanceifolia

Aspalathus lanceifolia, commonly known as the African rooibos, is a shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to grow in sandy, well-drained soils and is drought-resistant. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The leaves are used to produce the popular herbal tea known as rooibos.
