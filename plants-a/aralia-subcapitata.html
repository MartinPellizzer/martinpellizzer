Aralia subcapitata

Aralia subcapitata, commonly known as the African aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with a pinnate structure, often adorned with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the summer, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
