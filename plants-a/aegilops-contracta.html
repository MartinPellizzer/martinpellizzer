Aegilops contracta

Aegilops contracta, commonly known as the slender wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Aegilops contracta is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, arid environments.

It is considered a valuable forage plant for livestock in certain regions.
