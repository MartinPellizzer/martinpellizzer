Aster multiflorus var. pansus

Aster multiflorus var. pansus, commonly known as the showy aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
