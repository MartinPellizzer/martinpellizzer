Alchornea triplinervia var. tomentella

Alchornea triplinervia var. tomentella, commonly known as the woolly alchornea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The species is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments, making it a common understory plant in tropical forests.
