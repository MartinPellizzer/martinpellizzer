Aira semineutra

Aira semineutra, commonly known as the common air grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Aira semineutra is typically found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
