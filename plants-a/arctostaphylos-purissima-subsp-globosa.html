Arctostaphylos purissima subsp. globosa

Arctostaphylos purissima subsp.

globosa, commonly known as the Sierra foothill manzanita, is a shrub native to California and northern Baja California. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in white to pink hues.

The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable environments.
