Adiantum victoriae

Adiantum victoriae, commonly known as the royal fern, is a species of fern native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant prefers moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in woodland areas. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Adiantum victoriae is valued for its ornamental appearance and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
