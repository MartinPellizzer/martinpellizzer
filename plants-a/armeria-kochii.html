Armeria kochii

Armeria kochii, commonly known as the coastal thrift, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to sandy, coastal soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Armeria kochii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to native pollinators.
