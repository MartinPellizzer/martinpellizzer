Asperula macrorrhiza

Asperula macrorrhiza, commonly known as the large-leaf woodruff, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender stems and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Asperula macrorrhiza is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
