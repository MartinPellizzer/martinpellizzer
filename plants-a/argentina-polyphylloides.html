Argentina polyphylloides

Argentina polyphylloides, commonly known as the Argentine feather fern, is a species of fern native to South America, particularly Argentina.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds, facilitating wind pollination.

The botanical features of Argentina polyphylloides make it an important species for studying fern evolution and ecological adaptation in temperate regions.
