Astragalus magdalenae var. peirsonii

Astragalus magdalenae var. peirsonii, commonly known as the Peirson's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects.
