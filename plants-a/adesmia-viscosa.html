Adesmia viscosa

Adesmia viscosa, commonly known as the sticky adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its sticky secretions on its stems and leaves, which can trap small insects. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Adesmia viscosa is used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities for its potential therapeutic properties.
