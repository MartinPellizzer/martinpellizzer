Asplenium parallelosorum

Asplenium parallelosorum, commonly known as the parallel-veined spleenwort, is a small evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, clustered growth habit and dark green, glossy rhombic-shaped leaves. The fronds are typically divided into narrow, linear segments with parallel veins, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rock surfaces or in shaded forest floors.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialty gardens.
