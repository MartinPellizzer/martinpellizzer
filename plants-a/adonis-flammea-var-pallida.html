Adonis flammea var. pallida

Adonis flammea var. pallida, commonly known as the pale flame bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow to white, contrasting with its dark green foliage. The plant produces a distinctive, fluted stem that often grows upright, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in alpine gardens and rockeries.
