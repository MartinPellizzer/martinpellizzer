Adesmia hystrix

Adesmia hystrix, commonly known as the hairy adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Adesmia hystrix is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its seeds are encased in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.
