Aurelia glutinosa

Aurelia glutinosa, commonly known as sticky mallow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular leaves and showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Aurelia glutinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
