Arracacia dugesii

Arracacia dugesii, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot or arrowleaf, is a perennial herb native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-orange flower heads that are clustered in a panicle inflorescence. Its rhizomatous root system is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
