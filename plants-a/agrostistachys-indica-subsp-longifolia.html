Agrostistachys indica subsp. longifolia

Agrostistachys indica subsp.

longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved Indian grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks.

The plant features slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a grass-like texture, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
