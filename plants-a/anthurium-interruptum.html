Anthurium interruptum

Anthurium interruptum, commonly known as the red-tipped anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with a deep red margin and a dark green center. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red or orange. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Anthurium interruptum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
