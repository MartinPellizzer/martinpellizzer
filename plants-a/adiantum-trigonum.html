Adiantum trigonum

Adiantum trigonum, commonly known as the holly fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, triangular-shaped fronds that are often adorned with fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are bioluminescent, emitting a faint glow in the dark, which is a unique characteristic among ferns.

Adiantum trigonum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
