Amaranthus capensis subsp. uncinatus

Amaranthus capensis subsp.

uncinatus, commonly known as the curly amaranth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy or curled margin.

The plant produces small, clustered greenish-yellow flowers that develop into three-angled, ribbed seed capsules. Its seeds are small, hard, and often used as a traditional food source in some regions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to drought conditions.
