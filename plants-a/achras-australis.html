Achras australis

Achras australis, commonly known as the Brazilian palm, is a tall, slender palm native to South America.

It features a slender trunk covered with persistent, fibrous leaf bases, and it produces large, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that develop into orange-red fruits, which are clustered in panicles. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in coastal areas and its use in traditional medicine.
