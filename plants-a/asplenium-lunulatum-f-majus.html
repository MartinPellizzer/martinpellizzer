Asplenium lunulatum f. majus

Asplenium lunulatum f. majus, commonly known as the large moonwort, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often lobed fronds.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a unique, almost moon-shaped appearance on the underside.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
