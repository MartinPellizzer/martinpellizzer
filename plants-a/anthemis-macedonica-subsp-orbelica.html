Anthemis macedonica subsp. orbelica

Anthemis macedonica subsp.

orbelica, commonly known as the Macedonian anthemis, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its aromatic, yellowish flowers. Its subspecies status highlights its distinct genetic and morphological traits compared to the main species.

It is often found in open woodlands and along mountainous regions where it thrives in well-drained soils.
