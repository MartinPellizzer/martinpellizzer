Agave salmiana subsp. tehuacanensis

Agave salmiana subsp.

tehuacanensis, commonly known as the Huizache or Huizachal, is a species of agave native to central Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, succulent stems and large, spiky leaves that are often used in traditional practices.

The plant produces large, white or yellowish flowers that bloom at the top of the stem, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to withstand drought conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in indigenous communities.
