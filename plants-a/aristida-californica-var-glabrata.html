Aristida californica var. glabrata

Aristida californica var. glabrata, commonly known as California hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and coastal areas. The plant features slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
