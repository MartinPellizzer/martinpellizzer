Arisaema pythonium

Arisaema pythonium, commonly known as the snake root or snake plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces an unusual, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Arisaema pythonium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
