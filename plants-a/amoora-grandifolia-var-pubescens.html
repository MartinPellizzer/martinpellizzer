Amoora grandifolia var. pubescens

Amoora grandifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy broadleaf soursop, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, green, inedible fruits that mature to a yellowish color. Its botanical features include a woody stem and opposite leaf arrangement.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
