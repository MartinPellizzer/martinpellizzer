Alsophila atrovirens

Alsophila atrovirens, commonly known as the black-veined tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The fronds are typically dark green with prominent, dark brown veins, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Alsophila atrovirens plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various small animals and insects.
