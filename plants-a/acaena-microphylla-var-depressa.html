Acaena microphylla var. depressa

Acaena microphylla var. depressa, commonly known as the dwarf hill's balls, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dense clusters of small, spiny, spherical fruits that are characteristic of the genus Acaena. The plant has small, alternate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
