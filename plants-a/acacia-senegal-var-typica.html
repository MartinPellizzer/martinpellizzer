Acacia senegal var. typica

Acacia senegal var. typica, commonly known as the gum arabic acacia, is a small, thorny shrub native to the Sahelian regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is particularly noted for producing a valuable gum, known as gum arabic, which is harvested from its bark. This gum is widely used in food, pharmaceutical, and industrial applications due to its emulsifying and stabilizing properties.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.

Native Distribution

Acacia senegal var. typica is primarily native to the Sahel region of Africa, including countries such as Sudan, Chad, Niger, and Mali.

It thrives in semi-arid and arid environments, often found in sandy soils and along riverbanks. This plant is well adapted to harsh climatic conditions and is commonly used for gum production. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Its distribution is closely linked to areas with seasonal rainfall patterns.
