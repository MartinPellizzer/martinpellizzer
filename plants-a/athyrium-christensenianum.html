Athyrium christensenianum

Athyrium christensenianum, commonly known as the Christensen's lady fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Athyriaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, though its primary botanical significance lies in its morphology and ecological role.

The plant's spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of its leaves.
