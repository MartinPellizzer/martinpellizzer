Arquita mimosifolia

Arquita mimosifolia, commonly known as the silky mimosifolia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
