Asplenium blumeanum

Asplenium blumeanum, commonly known as the Blume's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and is typically found growing on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often clustered at the base, with a dark green to bluish-green coloration. It produces sporangia in clusters on the underside of its leaves, which are responsible for its reproductive cycle.

Asplenium blumeanum is a hardy species that thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
