Andropogon distachyos subvar. luxurians

Andropogon distachyos subvar. luxurians, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This grass thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of native prairie and woodland ecosystems. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of the grass family.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
