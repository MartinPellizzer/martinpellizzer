Allophylus andongensis

Allophylus andongensis, commonly known as the Andong evergreen holly, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to China, specifically the provinces of Yunnan and Sichuan, where it grows in subtropical forests. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkin-like inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries.

Allophylus andongensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
