Aster longipetiolatus

Aster longipetiolatus, commonly known as the long-petioled aster, is a species of flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant is characterized by its elongated petioles, which support its hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, forming dense clusters that bloom in the summer months.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.
