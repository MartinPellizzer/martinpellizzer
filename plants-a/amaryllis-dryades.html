Amaryllis dryades

Amaryllis dryades, commonly known as the dryades amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features tall, erect stems that bear clusters of large, showy flowers in shades of red, white, and pink. The leaves are broad, strap-like, and often appear after the flowers have bloomed. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
