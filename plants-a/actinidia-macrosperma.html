Actinidia macrosperma

Actinidia macrosperma, commonly known as the large-seeded actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 7 lobes, and its fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, fuzzy berries that mature to a dark blue or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Actinidia macrosperma is also valued for its potential in horticulture and as a source of natural compounds.
