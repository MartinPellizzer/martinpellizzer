Acer campestre var. lasiocarpum

Acer campestre var. lasiocarpum, commonly known as the downy maple, is a variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy (pubescent) fruits, which set it apart from other maple varieties. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually oval with a pointed tip and a serrated margin.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
