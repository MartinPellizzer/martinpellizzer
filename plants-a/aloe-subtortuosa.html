Aloe subtortuosa

Aloe subtortuosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, slender stems covered in thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern and are adorned with white tubercles and spines. It produces yellow flowers in terminal clusters during the warmer months.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
