Adnaria pinifolia

Adnaria pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that resemble pine needles, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Adnaria pinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
