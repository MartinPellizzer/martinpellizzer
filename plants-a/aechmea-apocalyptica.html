Aechmea apocalyptica

Aechmea apocalyptica, commonly known as the "Apocalyptic Bromeliad," is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features a rosette of long, sword-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and orange. The plant produces a tall, spire-like inflorescence that bears striking violet or pink flowers, which bloom in a dramatic display. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help collect and store water in the central rosette.

This species is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique appearance.

Morphology

Aechmea apocalyptica has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, fleshy, and sword-like, arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem, with a vibrant green color and a waxy surface.

The stem is short and thick, covered in a smooth, dark green bark, and grows upward from the base of the leaf rosette. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in shades of red and orange, blooming during the summer months and pollinated by insects such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals that eat the fruit.

Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding environment.
