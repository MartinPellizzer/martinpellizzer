Ampelocissus leonensis

Ampelocissus leonensis, commonly known as the climbing fig or leopard's bane, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a woody vine that produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. The plant has a trailing habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ampelocissus leonensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
