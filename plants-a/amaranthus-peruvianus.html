Amaranthus peruvianus

Amaranthus peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy inflorescences that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, triangular seeds that are often used in traditional cuisine and as a nutritional supplement. Its leaves are edible and rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals, making it a valuable crop.

Amaranthus peruvianus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
