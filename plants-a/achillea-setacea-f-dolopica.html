Achillea setacea f. dolopica

Achillea setacea f. dolopica, commonly known as the Dolopica yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
