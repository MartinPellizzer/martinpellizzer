Avena sativa var. pilosa

Avena sativa var. pilosa, commonly known as hairy oat, is a variant of the common oat species characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage.

This plant is native to temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are broad and covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it a unique appearance. The plant produces spikelets that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the grass family.

It is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in forage or as a cover crop.
