Arrabidaea platyphylla var. elliptica

Arrabidaea platyphylla var. elliptica, commonly known as the Brazilian velvet tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it has a reputation for being somewhat toxic.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
