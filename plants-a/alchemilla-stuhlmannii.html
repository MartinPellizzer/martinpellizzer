Alchemilla stuhlmannii

Alchemilla stuhlmannii, commonly known as Stuhlmann's lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Alchemilla stuhlmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
