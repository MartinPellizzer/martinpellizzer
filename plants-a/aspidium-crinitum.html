Aspidium crinitum

Aspidium crinitum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a perennial fern native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce numerous sporangia on the underside of its leaves.

Aspidium crinitum is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
