Agrostis flava

Agrostis flava, commonly known as yellow bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows, woodlands, and wetlands. The plant has fine, slender leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, and its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spike. It produces small, yellowish to golden spikelets that are wind-pollinated.

Agrostis flava is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
