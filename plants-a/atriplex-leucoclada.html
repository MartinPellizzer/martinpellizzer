Atriplex leucoclada

Atriplex leucoclada, commonly known as white-tasseled saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and cylindrical, spiny fruit clusters that contain small, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant is adapted to saline and drought-prone environments, with succulent leaves that store water. It belongs to the Chenopodiaceae family, which includes many halophytic species.

Atriplex leucoclada plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife in its ecosystem.
