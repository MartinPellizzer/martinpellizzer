Adesmia melanocarpa

Adesmia melanocarpa, commonly known as the black-fruited burro bush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, dark-colored fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous husk. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a sprawling or prostrate form, making it well adapted to harsh desert conditions.
