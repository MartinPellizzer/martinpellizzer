Arabis holboellii var. typica

Arabis holboellii var. typica, commonly known as the Alpine rockcress, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems bear small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
