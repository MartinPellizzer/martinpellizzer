Anthoxanthum odoratum var. welwitschii

Anthoxanthum odoratum var. welwitschii, commonly known as sweet vernal grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences, which are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is found in temperate regions across Europe, Asia, and North America. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
