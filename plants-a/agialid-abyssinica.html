Agialid abyssinica

Agialid abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian agialid, is a rare and specialized plant species native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, an adaptation to its arid and semi-arid habitat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in rocky soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited to its harsh environment.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
