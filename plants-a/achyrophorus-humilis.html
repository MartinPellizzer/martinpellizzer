Achyrophorus humilis

Achyrophorus humilis, commonly known as the low achyranthes, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually hairy and adapted to reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, often with five petals.

It is known for its resilience to drought and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
