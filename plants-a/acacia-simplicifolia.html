Acacia simplicifolia

Acacia simplicifolia, commonly known as the simpleleaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow, globular inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in hard pods and are often dispersed by animals or through wind.

Acacia simplicifolia is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and high temperatures.
