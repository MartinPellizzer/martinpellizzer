Anthemis tatrae

Anthemis tatrae, commonly known as the Tatra hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer, are surrounded by pale green bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Anthemis tatrae is often found in the Tatra Mountains, where it plays a role in the local flora and ecosystem.
