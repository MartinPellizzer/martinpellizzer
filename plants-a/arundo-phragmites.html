Arundo phragmites

Arundo phragmites, commonly known as common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetlands and along shorelines.

It forms dense stands with long, slender leaves and produces feathery, grayish-brown plumes that are distinctive in appearance. The plant is highly adaptable, capable of growing in a variety of soil types and water conditions. It plays a significant role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

However, it can also become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
