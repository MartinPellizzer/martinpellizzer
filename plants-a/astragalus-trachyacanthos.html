Astragalus trachyacanthos

Astragalus trachyacanthos, commonly known as the rough-horned milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with hairy textures and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Its distinctive features make it an important component of its native habitat's biodiversity.
