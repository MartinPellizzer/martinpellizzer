Acacia platyphylla

Acacia platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its flat, narrow phyllodes that are often covered in a fine layer of white to greyish hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that appear in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Acacia platyphylla is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
