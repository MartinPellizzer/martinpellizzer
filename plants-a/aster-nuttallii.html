Aster nuttallii

Aster nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, purple to blue flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, prairies, and woodland edges, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and somewhat woody.

Nuttall's aster is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
