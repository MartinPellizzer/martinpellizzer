Agave latifolia

Agave latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its wide, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape, often reaching up to 3 meters in height. The leaves are typically gray-green with a waxy coating, adapted to store water in arid environments. It produces a tall flower stalk that bears yellowish-green blooms, which appear after several years.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
