Altamirania pachyphylla

Altamirania pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved altamirania, is a succulent plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the warmer months. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with branching stems that can reach several feet in height.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
