Alsophila hypolampra

Alsophila hypolampra, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a robust root system that helps it anchor in moist, shaded environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are typically found on the undersides of its fronds.

Alsophila hypolampra plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
