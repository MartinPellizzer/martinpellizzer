Artemisia borealis subsp. mertensii

Artemisia borealis subsp.

mertensii, commonly known as the northern tansy, is a perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions of North America. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open, rocky, or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates. It is often found in tundra and subarctic environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.

Its essential oils contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.
