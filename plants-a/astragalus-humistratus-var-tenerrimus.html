Astragalus humistratus var. tenerrimus

Astragalus humistratus var. tenerrimus, commonly known as the brittle milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, arranged in clusters, and bloom in early summer.

The species is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in disturbed or marginal soils.
