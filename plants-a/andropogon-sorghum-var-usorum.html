Andropogon sorghum var. usorum

Andropogon sorghum var. usorum, commonly known as broomcorn millet, is an annual grass native to the warmer regions of the Old World.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and panicles of small, fluffy seeds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy crop for dry climates. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough texture, and it produces a high yield of seeds that are used for food, animal feed, and industrial purposes.

Broomcorn millet is also valued for its ability to fix carbon and improve soil structure in agricultural systems.
