Aster angustifolius

Aster angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, blooming from late summer to early fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
