Antirrhinum fruticosum

Antirrhinum fruticosum, commonly known as the creeping snapdragon, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate or semi-erect growth habit, with small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
