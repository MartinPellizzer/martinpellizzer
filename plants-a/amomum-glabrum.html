Amomum glabrum

Amomum glabrum, commonly known as the Chinese ginger or wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous stems that produce tall, erect flowering stalks with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and are borne in clusters at the top of the stalk.

The rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
