Acalypha codonocalyx

Acalypha codonocalyx, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red curlleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from green to reddish-purple. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often pink or white, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Acalypha codonocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
