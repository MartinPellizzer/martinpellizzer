Anchusa aucheri

Anchusa aucheri, commonly known as the blueweed or narrow-leaved anchusa, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Anchusa aucheri is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
