Agianthus jacobaeus

Agianthus jacobaeus, commonly known as Jacob's agave, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, white to pale blue flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Agianthus jacobaeus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
