Aspalathus dorycnium

Aspalathus dorycnium, commonly known as the African green tea or Cape tea, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, narrow leaves, and small yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to the local climate, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are harvested and processed to produce a mild, caffeine-free herbal tea.

Aspalathus dorycnium plays an important role in both traditional medicine and modern herbal tea markets.
