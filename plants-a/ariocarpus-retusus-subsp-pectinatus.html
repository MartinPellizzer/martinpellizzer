Ariocarpus retusus subsp. pectinatus

Ariocarpus retusus subsp.

pectinatus, commonly known as the "Laceleaf Cactus," is a succulent plant native to Mexico. It is characterized by its short, branching stems covered in dense, woolly white hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a compact, rosette-like growth habit.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
