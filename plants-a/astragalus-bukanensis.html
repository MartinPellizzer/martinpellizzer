Astragalus bukanensis

Astragalus bukanensis, commonly known as Bukan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seed pods are distinctive, often containing several small, lens-shaped seeds.
