Agropyron dasystachyum var. sericeum

Agropyron dasystachyum var. sericeum, commonly known as silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-haired leaves and tufted inflorescences. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and grassland ecosystems.

Silky wheatgrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in erosion control.
