Adenia palmata

Adenia palmata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with five petals, often accompanied by prominent stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Adenia palmata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
