Allium chrysanthum

Allium chrysanthum, commonly known as the globe flower, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its clustered, spherical flower heads that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Allium chrysanthum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
