Alpinia blepharocalyx var. glabrior

Alpinia blepharocalyx var. glabrior, commonly known as the glabrous ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often glabrous or nearly hairless. The plant produces striking, fragrant inflorescences with showy, orange-red bracts that are adorned with small, white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of this variety make it a distinctive and sought-after plant in tropical gardens.
