Ariocarpus prismaticus

Ariocarpus prismaticus, commonly known as the prismatic barrel cactus, is a slow-growing succulent native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, ribbed stems that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces striking yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Ariocarpus prismaticus is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
