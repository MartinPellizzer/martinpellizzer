Aconitum jinchengense

Aconitum jinchengense, commonly known as the Jincheng monkshood, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, helmet-shaped flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it requires careful handling and is not typically cultivated for ornamental purposes.
