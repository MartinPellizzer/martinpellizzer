Asperula caespitans

Asperula caespitans, commonly known as the creeping azalea, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white during late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Asperula caespitans is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
