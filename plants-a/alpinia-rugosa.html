Alpinia rugosa

Alpinia rugosa, commonly known as the fragrant ginger or wild ginger, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a slightly rough texture.

Alpinia rugosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
