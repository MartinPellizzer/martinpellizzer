Amphilophium molle

Amphilophium molle, commonly known as the soft-leaf philodendron, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slightly variegated appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Amphilophium molle is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
