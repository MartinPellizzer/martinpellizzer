Atractocarpus sessilifolius

Atractocarpus sessilifolius, commonly known as the sessile-leaf spondias, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, and often sessile leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles, followed by edible drupes with a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit, which is used in local cuisines.
