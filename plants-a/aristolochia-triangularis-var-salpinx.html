Aristolochia triangularis var. salpinx

Aristolochia triangularis var. salpinx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, aromatic leaves with a distinctive, often waxy texture. The plant produces unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white with a strong, musty odor. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that releases small, smooth seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and historical use in traditional medicine.
