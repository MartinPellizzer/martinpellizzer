Asarum elegans

Asarum elegans, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the forests of Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia.

It features a thick, tuberous root system and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, fuzzy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically hidden beneath its foliage. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in the undergrowth of temperate forests.

Asarum elegans is valued in traditional medicine for its purported analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.
