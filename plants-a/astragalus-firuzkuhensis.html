Astragalus firuzkuhensis

Astragalus firuzkuhensis, commonly known as the Firuzkuh milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
