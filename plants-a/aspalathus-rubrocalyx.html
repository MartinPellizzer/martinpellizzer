Aspalathus rubrocalyx

Aspalathus rubrocalyx, commonly known as the red calyx bush, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Aspalathus rubrocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
