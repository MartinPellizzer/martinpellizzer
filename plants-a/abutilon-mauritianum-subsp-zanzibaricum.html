Abutilon mauritianum subsp. zanzibaricum

Abutilon mauritianum subsp.

zanzibaricum, commonly known as the Zanzibar flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa. It is characterized by its showy, flame-colored bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing along coastal areas and in disturbed soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
