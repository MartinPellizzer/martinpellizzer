Anisopappus corymbosus

Anisopappus corymbosus, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are alternate, hairy, and deeply lobed, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry conditions.

The species is notable for its ability to survive in harsh desert climates with minimal water.
