Astragalus gandjehicus

Astragalus gandjehicus, commonly known as Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Astragalus gandjehicus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various health purposes.
