Acacia pruinescens var. luchunensis

Acacia pruinescens var. luchunensis, commonly known as the Luchun acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in a distinctive whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by elongated, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native region.
