Archidendron beguinii

Archidendron beguinii, commonly known as the beguinii legume, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it useful for reforestation efforts. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, ranging in color from white to pale yellow.

The tree is also valued for its timber and potential medicinal properties.
