Astragalus stenophyllus var. filipes

Astragalus stenophyllus var. filipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous small leaflets, and it produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

It is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
