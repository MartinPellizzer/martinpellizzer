Argyrolobium argenteum

Argyrolobium argenteum, commonly known as the silver globemallow, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features silvery-white, woolly leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive globular shape. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, and its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in dry regions.
