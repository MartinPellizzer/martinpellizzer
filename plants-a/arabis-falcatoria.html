Arabis falcatoria

Arabis falcatoria, commonly known as the sickleleaf rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate to oblanceolate in shape, with a distinctive white or pale pink flower cluster at the top. Its flowers are small and numerous, arranged in a raceme, and bloom in early spring.

Arabis falcatoria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
