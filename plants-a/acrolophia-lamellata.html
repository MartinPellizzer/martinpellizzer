Acrolophia lamellata

Acrolophia lamellata, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in regions with a Mediterranean climate. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Acrolophia lamellata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
