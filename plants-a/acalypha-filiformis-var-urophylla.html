Acalypha filiformis var. urophylla

Acalypha filiformis var. urophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its finely haired, silver-gray foliage and its clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive whitish underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
