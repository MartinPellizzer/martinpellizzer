Alyssum constellatum

Alyssum constellatum, commonly known as starry alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has silvery-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. Alyssum constellatum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
