Agoseris grandiflora var. leptophylla

Agoseris grandiflora var. leptophylla, commonly known as the slenderleaf goldenstar, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow ray florets and green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The variety leptophylla is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Agoseris grandiflora.
