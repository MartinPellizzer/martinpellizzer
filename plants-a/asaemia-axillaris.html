Asaemia axillaris

Asaemia axillaris, commonly known as the axillary asaemia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of yellow and white. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Asaemia axillaris is often found growing in rocky soils and at higher elevations, where it thrives in cooler, moist conditions.
