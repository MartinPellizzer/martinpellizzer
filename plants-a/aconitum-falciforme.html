Aconitum falciforme

Aconitum falciforme, commonly known as the sickleleaf monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant features palmate leaves with a distinctive, sickle-shaped lobes, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in woodland or alpine environments.

Aconitum falciforme is part of the Ranunculaceae family and contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
