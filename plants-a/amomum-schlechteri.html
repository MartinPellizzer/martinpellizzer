Amomum schlechteri

Amomum schlechteri, commonly known as the Sichuan cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, Yunnan, and Guizhou in China.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers, followed by bright red berries that are used in traditional medicine. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like cineole and limonene, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

Amomum schlechteri is often cultivated for its culinary and therapeutic applications in Chinese herbal medicine.
