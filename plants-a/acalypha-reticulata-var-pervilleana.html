Acalypha reticulata var. pervilleana

Acalypha reticulata var. pervilleana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silkyleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
