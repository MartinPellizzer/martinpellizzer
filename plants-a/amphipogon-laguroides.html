Amphipogon laguroides

Amphipogon laguroides, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces yellow composite flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a taproot system and small, bract-like leaves along the stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
