Alchemilla potentilloides

Alchemilla potentilloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow environments.

Alchemilla potentilloides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
