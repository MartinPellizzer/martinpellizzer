Agnus-castus macrostachya

Agnus-castus macrostachya, commonly known as the large-stalked chastetree, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to xeriscapic landscapes.
