Anemia × semihispida

Anemia × semihispida, commonly known as the hybrid anemone, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Anemone coronaria and Anemone semihispida.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting center. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is native to regions of the Mediterranean and has been cultivated for its ornamental value.

The hybrid is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in various garden conditions.
