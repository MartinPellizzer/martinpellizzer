Amaryllis verecunda

Amaryllis verecunda, commonly known as the golden amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, sturdy stems that bear clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are a deep green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, typically in late spring to early summer.
