Alchemilla subglobosa f. aestivalis

Alchemilla subglobosa f. aestivalis, commonly known as the early alchemilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer. The plant has a dense mat of foliage with rounded, succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
