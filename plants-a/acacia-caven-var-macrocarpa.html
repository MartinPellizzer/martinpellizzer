Acacia caven var. macrocarpa

Acacia caven var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, showing some resistance to salt spray.

The seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are an important food source for various wildlife species.
