Acer miaotense

Acer miaotense, commonly known as the Miaotian maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange foliage in the autumn, which makes it a popular ornamental plant. The tree has opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, winged seeds enclosed in a samaroid capsule, typical of the maple genus.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
