Ambrina incisa

Ambrina incisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf ambrina, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, sandy soils.

Ambrina incisa is often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.
