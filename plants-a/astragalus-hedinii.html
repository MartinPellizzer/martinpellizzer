Astragalus hedinii

Astragalus hedinii, commonly known as Hedin's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, harsh environments. It produces small, lens-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat.

Astragalus hedinii is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
