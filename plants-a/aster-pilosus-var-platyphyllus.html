Aster pilosus var. platyphyllus

Aster pilosus var. platyphyllus, commonly known as the flatleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties.

The flat, broad leaves of this variety distinguish it from other Aster species.
