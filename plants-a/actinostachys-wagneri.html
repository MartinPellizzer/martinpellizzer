Actinostachys wagneri

Actinostachys wagneri, commonly known as the Wagner's spider-fern, is a tropical fern native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, lacy fronds that resemble spider webs, giving it its common name. The plant has a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread along the forest floor. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with numerous small, toothed pinnae.

Actinostachys wagneri thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist, tropical forests.
