Arabis shockleyi

Arabis shockleyi, commonly known as Shockley's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small white or yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are basal and arranged in a rosette, which helps it conserve water in its native habitat.
