Agrostis widenii

Agrostis widenii, commonly known as the narrowleaf bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, and it is characterized by its fine, wiry texture.

Agrostis widenii is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
