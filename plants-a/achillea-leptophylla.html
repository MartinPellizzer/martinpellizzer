Achillea leptophylla

Achillea leptophylla, commonly known as narrow-leaved yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, aromatic leaves and produces white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Narrow-leaved yarrow is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
