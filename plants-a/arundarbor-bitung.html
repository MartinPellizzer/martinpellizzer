Arundarbor bitung

Arundarbor bitung, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets that give it a feathery appearance. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds.

Arundarbor bitung is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.
