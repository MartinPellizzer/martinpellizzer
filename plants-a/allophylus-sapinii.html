Allophylus sapinii

Allophylus sapinii, commonly known as the mountain alder, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests and along riverbanks. The plant has compound leaves that are opposite in arrangement, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds.

Allophylus sapinii plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
