Astragalus vicarius

Astragalus vicarius, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Astragalus vicarius is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert grassland ecosystems.
