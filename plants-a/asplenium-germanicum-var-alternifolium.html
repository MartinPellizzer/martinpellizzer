Asplenium germanicum var. alternifolium

Asplenium germanicum var. alternifolium, commonly known as the alternate fern, is a species of fern native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, alternate leaves that give it a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
