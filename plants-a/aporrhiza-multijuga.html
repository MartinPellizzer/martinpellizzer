Aporrhiza multijuga

Aporrhiza multijuga, commonly known as the common sea fennel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that grows in coastal areas, particularly in sandy or muddy substrates. The plant has aromatic, finely divided leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels. It is adapted to saltwater environments and can tolerate tidal fluctuations.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and stabilize shorelines.
