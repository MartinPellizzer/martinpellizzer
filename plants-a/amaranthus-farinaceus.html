Amaranthus farinaceus

Amaranthus farinaceus, commonly known as Jerusalem artichoke, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces edible tubers that are rich in inulin, a prebiotic fiber. Its leaves are also edible and can be used in salads or cooked like spinach.

Amaranthus farinaceus is adapted to a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant.
