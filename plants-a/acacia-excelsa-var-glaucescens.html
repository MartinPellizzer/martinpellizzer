Acacia excelsa var. glaucescens

Acacia excelsa var. glaucescens, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia.

It features slender, zigzagging branches and produces yellow globular flower heads that bloom in spring. The leaves are bipinnate, with small, linear leaflets that give the plant a feathery appearance. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
