Acacia microcarpa var. linearis

Acacia microcarpa var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are grouped into cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand drought conditions.
