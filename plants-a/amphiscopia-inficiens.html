Amphiscopia inficiens

Amphiscopia inficiens, commonly known as the smut fungus, is a plant pathogen that belongs to the family Ustilaginaceae.

It primarily infects grasses and is known for causing smut diseases, which result in the production of dark, powdery spore masses within the plant tissues. The fungus overwinters as dormant spores on infected plant material and germinates when conditions are favorable, typically in cool, moist environments. It spreads through wind-borne spores and can significantly reduce the yield and quality of infected crops.

The life cycle of Amphiscopia inficiens involves both asexual and sexual reproduction, contributing to its persistence in natural and agricultural ecosystems.
