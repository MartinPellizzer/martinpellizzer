Astracantha ghilanica

Astracantha ghilanica, commonly known as the Persian thornvetch, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.

Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and it plays a role in soil stabilization in its native habitat.
