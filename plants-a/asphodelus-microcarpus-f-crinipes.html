Asphodelus microcarpus f. crinipes

Asphodelus microcarpus f. crinipes, commonly known as the curly-leaved asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often curled or twisted, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, typically arranged in loose clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
