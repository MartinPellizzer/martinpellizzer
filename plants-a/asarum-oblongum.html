Asarum oblongum

Asarum oblongum, commonly known as Chinese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features a thick, rhizomatous root system and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in umbels. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its aromatic leaves and roots have been used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported medicinal properties.
