Antennaria caroliniana

Antennaria caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina springbeauty, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, nodding flowers that are typically pink or white, with five petals and a distinctive, spurred shape. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with hairy, linear leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.

Antennaria caroliniana is an important early bloomer in spring, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
