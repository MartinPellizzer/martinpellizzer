Amaryllis rubropicta

Amaryllis rubropicta, commonly known as the red-painted amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from deep red to orange-red in color. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a bulbous base, typical of the Amaryllis genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Amaryllis rubropicta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown in containers or in garden beds with proper care.
