Abeliophyllum distichum f. lilacinum

Abeliophyllum distichum f. lilacinum, commonly known as the lilac-leafed abelia, is a compact shrub native to Japan.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight bluish tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden borders or containers.

Its ornamental value comes from both its foliage and its delicate floral display.
