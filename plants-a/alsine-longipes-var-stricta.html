Alsine longipes var. stricta

Alsine longipes var. stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandparsley, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It has narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length, arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
