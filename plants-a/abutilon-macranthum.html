Abutilon macranthum

Abutilon macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered abutilon, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves and can grow as a shrub or small tree, reaching up to 5 meters in height. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers, which are typically yellow or orange, bloom in late spring and early summer.
