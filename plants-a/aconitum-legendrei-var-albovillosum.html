Aconitum legendrei var. albovillosum

Aconitum legendrei var. albovillosum, commonly known as the white-veined monkshood, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The leaves are palmately compound, with finely toothed margins, and the plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
