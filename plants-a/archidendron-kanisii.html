Archidendron kanisii

Archidendron kanisii, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it produces long, flat pods containing seeds.

Archidendron kanisii is often used in reforestation projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
