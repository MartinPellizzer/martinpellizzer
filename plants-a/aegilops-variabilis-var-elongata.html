Aegilops variabilis var. elongata

Aegilops variabilis var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated spikelets and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as fields and rocky slopes. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and is known for its adaptability to various soil conditions.

Aegilops variabilis var. elongata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
