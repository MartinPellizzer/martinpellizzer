Allium aethusanum

Allium aethusanum, commonly known as wild leek, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in a loose umbel. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Allium aethusanum is also known for its mild garlic-like scent and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
