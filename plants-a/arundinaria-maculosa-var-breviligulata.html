Arundinaria maculosa var. breviligulata

Arundinaria maculosa var. breviligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a striking green with a slight reddish tinge. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments. Its growth habit is clump-forming, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
