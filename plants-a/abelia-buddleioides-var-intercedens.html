Abelia buddleioides var. intercedens

Abelia buddleioides var. intercedens, commonly known as the Chinese abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
