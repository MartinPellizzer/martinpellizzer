Angelonia cubensis

Angelonia cubensis, commonly known as the summer snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to Cuba and the Caribbean.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its foliage is typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.
