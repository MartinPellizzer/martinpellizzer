Acacia shrevei

Acacia shrevei, commonly known as the Shreve acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and compound leaves that are typically reduced to small, rounded stipules. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. Acacia shrevei is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and efficient water use.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife in its native range.
