Abutilon tiliifolium

Abutilon tiliifolium, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves and its striking, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are actually modified leaves rather than flowers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by the showy, orange to red bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant, long-lasting foliage.

Abutilon tiliifolium is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to grow in partial shade.
