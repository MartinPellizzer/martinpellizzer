Aerangis friesiorum

Aerangis friesiorum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The botanical features of Aerangis friesiorum make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
