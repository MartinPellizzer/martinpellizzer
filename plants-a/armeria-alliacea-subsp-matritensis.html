Armeria alliacea subsp. matritensis

Armeria alliacea subsp.

matritensis, commonly known as the Matritensis thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features dense clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is well adapted to coastal and maritime climates due to its salt tolerance.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh environments.
