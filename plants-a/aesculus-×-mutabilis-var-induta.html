Aesculus × mutabilis var. induta

Aesculus × mutabilis var. induta, commonly known as the Indian horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The tree has palmately lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. Its fruit is a spiny capsule containing several chestnut-like seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
