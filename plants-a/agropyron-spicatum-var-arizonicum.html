Agropyron spicatum var. arizonicum

Agropyron spicatum var. arizonicum, commonly known as Arizona needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, spiculate inflorescence. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces small, spikelet-bearing flowers. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and rocky soils.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
