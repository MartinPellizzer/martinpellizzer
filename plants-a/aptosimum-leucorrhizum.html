Aptosimum leucorrhizum

Aptosimum leucorrhizum, commonly known as white-rooted sedge, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. Its rhizomes are white and fibrous, contributing to its common name.

Aptosimum leucorrhizum is often found in moist, well-drained soils in alpine and subalpine habitats.
