Aloe hankeyi

Aloe hankeyi, commonly known as the Hankey's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are fleshy and covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are borne on tall, branched inflorescences. Aloe hankeyi is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick leaves, making it drought-tolerant.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
