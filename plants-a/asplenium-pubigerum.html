Asplenium pubigerum

Asplenium pubigerum, commonly known as the hairy spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It typically grows on the bark of trees or rocks in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a compact, rosette-like structure with slender, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy glands. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture.

Asplenium pubigerum is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
