Agastache pringlei var. pringlei

Agastache pringlei var. pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, such as open woodlands and meadows. Its lavender to violet flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a minty fragrance, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
