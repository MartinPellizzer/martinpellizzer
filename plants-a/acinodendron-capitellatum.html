Acinodendron capitellatum

Acinodendron capitellatum, commonly known as the Cape mountain thorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems.
