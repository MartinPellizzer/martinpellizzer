Adesmia parvifolia

Adesmia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has small, elliptical leaves and produces yellow or white flowers clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several small, oval seeds.

Adesmia parvifolia is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
