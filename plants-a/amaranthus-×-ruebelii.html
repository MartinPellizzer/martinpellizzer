Amaranthus × ruebelii

Amaranthus × ruebelii, commonly known as redroot amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and vibrant red to purple foliage, which becomes more pronounced as the plant matures. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that develop into three-angled, bead-like fruits. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by some Native American tribes.
