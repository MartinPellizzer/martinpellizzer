Arabis amurensis

Arabis amurensis, commonly known as the Amur rockcress, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces small, white or pink flowers in spring. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to cold climates.

Arabis amurensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
