Aeschynomene semilunaris

Aeschynomene semilunaris, commonly known as the moonflower or moon vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically light green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its flowers open in a distinctive semicircular shape, giving it its common name.

Aeschynomene semilunaris is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit.
