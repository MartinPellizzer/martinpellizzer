Arthrocereus glaziovii

Arthrocereus glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus with a compact, globular shape and a greenish-gray color. The plant features prominent areoles that bear clusters of white flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it produces small, edible fruits that are yellow or orange in color.

Arthrocereus glaziovii is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
