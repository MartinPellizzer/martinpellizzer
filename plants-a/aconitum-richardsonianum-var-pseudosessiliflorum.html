Aconitum richardsonianum var. pseudosessiliflorum

Aconitum richardsonianum var. pseudosessiliflorum, commonly known as the Richard's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and the plant has a toxic alkaloid-rich root system.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
