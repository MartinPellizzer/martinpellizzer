Alchemilla marcailhouorum

Alchemilla marcailhouorum, commonly known as the French lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of France.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Alchemilla marcailhouorum is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
