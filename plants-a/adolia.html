Adolia

Adolia, commonly known as the blue bell flower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Campanulaceae and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Adolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
