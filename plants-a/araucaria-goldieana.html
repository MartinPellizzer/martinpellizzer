Araucaria goldieana

Araucaria goldieana, commonly known as the golden pine, is a species of conifer native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The tree produces distinctive golden-yellow cones, which are a notable feature of its reproductive system. Its foliage consists of long, thin, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in spirals.

This plant is adapted to grow in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.
