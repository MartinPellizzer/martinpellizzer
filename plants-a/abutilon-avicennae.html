Abutilon avicennae

Abutilon avicennae, commonly known as the Indian velvet tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Abutilon avicennae is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and adaptability.
