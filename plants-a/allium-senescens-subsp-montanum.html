Allium senescens subsp. montanum

Allium senescens subsp.

montanum, commonly known as mountain leek, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces a tall flowering stem bearing umbels of white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
