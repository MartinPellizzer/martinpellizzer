Acacia striatula

Acacia striatula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and compound leaves with linear, striated leaflets. The plant produces yellowish to golden globular inflorescences, which attract various pollinators such as bees and birds. Acacia striatula is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

It is also valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
