Arachidna trifolia

Arachidna trifolia, commonly known as the three-leaved arachidna, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Arachidaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically around a central stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Due to its unique leaf structure and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
