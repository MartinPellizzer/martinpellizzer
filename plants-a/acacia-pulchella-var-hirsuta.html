Acacia pulchella var. hirsuta

Acacia pulchella var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces flat, leathery pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The hairy acacia is known for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
