Aster prenanthoides var. diffusifolius

Aster prenanthoides var. diffusifolius, commonly known as the spreading-leaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a spreading habit. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
