Acacia victoriae subsp. fasciaria

Acacia victoriae subsp.

fasciaria, commonly known as the Victoria acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in pairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are borne at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are often covered with fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry climates.
