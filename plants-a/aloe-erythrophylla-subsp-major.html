Aloe erythrophylla subsp. major

Aloe erythrophylla subsp.

major, commonly known as the giant red aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically red or pinkish in color, often with a waxy coating.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
