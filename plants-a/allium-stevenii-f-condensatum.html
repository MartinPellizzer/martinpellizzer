Allium stevenii f. condensatum

Allium stevenii f. condensatum, commonly known as the compact leek, is a botanical variant of Allium stevenii, characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and surrounding areas. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spherical umbels of white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its compact form and ornamental flowers make it a desirable addition to botanical collections and gardens.
