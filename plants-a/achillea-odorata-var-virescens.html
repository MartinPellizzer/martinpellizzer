Achillea odorata var. virescens

Achillea odorata var. virescens, commonly known as green yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, silvery-white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are aromatic and have a slightly bitter taste, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.
