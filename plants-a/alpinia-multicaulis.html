Alpinia multicaulis

Alpinia multicaulis, commonly known as the mountain ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. Its inflorescence consists of a cluster of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers.

Alpinia multicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
