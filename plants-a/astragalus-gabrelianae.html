Astragalus gabrelianae

Astragalus gabrelianae, commonly known as the Gabreliana milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its seed pods are slender and contain several small, rounded seeds.

Astragalus gabrelianae plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation to harsh climates.
