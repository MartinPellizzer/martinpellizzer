Allium napus

Allium napus, commonly known as bulb garlic, is a perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spherical bulbs that are typically white or pale yellow in color. These bulbs are often used in traditional medicine and have a mild garlic-like flavor.

Allium napus is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
