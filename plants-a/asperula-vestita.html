Asperula vestita

Asperula vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf azalea, is a flowering plant native to the forests of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and prefers acidic soil conditions. Its small, leathery leaves are a deep green, and the flowers range in color from white to pink and purple.

Asperula vestita is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
