Alyssum murale subsp. stojanovii

Alyssum murale subsp.

stojanovii, commonly known as Balkan alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant has hairy, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or dry habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Brassicaceae family.
