Arabis sisymbroides

Arabis sisymbroides, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are basal, lobed, and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.
