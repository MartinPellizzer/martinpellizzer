Anaphalis gracilis var. gracilis

Anaphalis gracilis var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, woolly leaves and clusters of small, white, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested or open areas. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
