Acacia coa

Acacia coa, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and phyllodes that are typically linear and slightly curved. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia coa thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure.

It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various coastal wildlife.
