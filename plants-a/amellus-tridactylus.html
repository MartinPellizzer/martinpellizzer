Amellus tridactylus

Amellus tridactylus, commonly known as the three-toed amellus, is a unique plant species characterized by its specialized leaf structure.

It features three distinct lobes on each leaf, which are adapted for efficient photosynthesis in its native habitat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are small and clustered, often producing a subtle fragrance to attract pollinators.

Amellus tridactylus is notable for its resilience to low light conditions and its ability to retain moisture in arid climates.
