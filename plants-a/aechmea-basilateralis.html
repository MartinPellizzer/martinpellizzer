Aechmea basilateralis

Aechmea basilateralis, commonly known as the pink pineapple plant, is a bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vivid pink bracts that resemble a pineapple. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light.

Aechmea basilateralis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.

Morphology

Aechmea basilateralis has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, strap-like, and dark green with a waxy coating, arranged in a tight spiral around the central rosette.

The plant lacks a true stem, with a short, stout base that is covered in a dense, fibrous sheath resembling bark. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom during the summer months, and it is pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a three-lobed berry that matures to a reddish color, dispersing its seeds through animal consumption and wind.

The roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding environment.
