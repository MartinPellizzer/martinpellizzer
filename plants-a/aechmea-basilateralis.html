Aechmea basilateralis

Aechmea basilateralis, commonly known as the pink pineapple plant, is a bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vivid pink bracts that resemble a pineapple. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light.

Aechmea basilateralis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
