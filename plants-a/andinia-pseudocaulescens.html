Andinia pseudocaulescens

Andinia pseudocaulescens, commonly known as the false stemless orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features a short, often obscured rhizome and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and purple. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
