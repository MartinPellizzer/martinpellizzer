Arctostaphylos × laxiflora

Arctostaphylos × laxiflora, commonly known as the lowbush blueberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with partial shade.

Arctostaphylos × laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
