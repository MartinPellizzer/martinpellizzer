Achyrophorus scorzonerae

Achyrophorus scorzonerae, commonly known as the alpine scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
