Arundinella villosa var. heynei

Arundinella villosa var. heynei, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a grass species native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as along riverbanks and in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, velvety texture.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
