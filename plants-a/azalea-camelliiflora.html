Azalea camelliiflora

Azalea camelliiflora, commonly known as the evergreen azalea, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 feet. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Azalea camelliiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
