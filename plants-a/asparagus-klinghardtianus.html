Asparagus klinghardtianus

Asparagus klinghardtianus, commonly known as the Klein's asparagus, is a rare and endangered species native to the arid regions of Namibia and South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, adapting to harsh desert conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat loss, it is critically important for conservation efforts in its native range.
