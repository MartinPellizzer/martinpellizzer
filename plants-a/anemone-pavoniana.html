Anemone pavoniana

Anemone pavoniana, commonly known as the peacock anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, palmate flowers with vibrant, colorful petals that resemble the tail of a peacock. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lobed and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Anemone pavoniana is valued in gardens for its early spring blooms and ability to thrive in partial shade.
