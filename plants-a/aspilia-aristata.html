Aspilia aristata

Aspilia aristata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, cone-shaped yellow inflorescence surrounded by white or pale blue bracts. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in full sun.

Aspilia aristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
