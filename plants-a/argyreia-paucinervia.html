Argyreia paucinervia

Argyreia paucinervia, commonly known as the red devil's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its dark green, palmate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a silky, white coating. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its striking appearance, it is also sometimes grown as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
