Abutilon decarpum

Abutilon decarpum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
