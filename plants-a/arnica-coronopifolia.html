Arnica coronopifolia

Arnica coronopifolia, commonly known as mountain arnica, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes. Its flowers are typically arranged in a compact cluster at the top of the stem.

Arnica coronopifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
