Aspilia riedelii

Aspilia riedelii, commonly known as the Riedel's torchflower, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy yellow flowers that resemble torches. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

Aspilia riedelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained, sunny soils.
