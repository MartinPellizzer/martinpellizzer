Antitaxis ramiflora

Antitaxis ramiflora, commonly known as the branched-flowered antitaxis, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, often with a distinctive coloration. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
