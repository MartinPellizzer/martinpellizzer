Alnus sieboldiana

Alnus sieboldiana, commonly known as the Japanese alder, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and catkin-like flowers. The tree produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a distinctive flaky texture.
