Aconitum formosum

Aconitum formosum, commonly known as the Japanese monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features striking, hooded flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, with a distinctive spurred shape. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of alkaloids. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum formosum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value, though caution is necessary due to its poisonous nature.
