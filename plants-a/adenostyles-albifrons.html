Adenostyles albifrons

Adenostyles albifrons, commonly known as the white-fronded bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of linear, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Adenostyles albifrons is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
