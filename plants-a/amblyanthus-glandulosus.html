Amblyanthus glandulosus

Amblyanthus glandulosus, commonly known as the sticky amblyanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a slight fragrance. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance and contribute to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Amblyanthus glandulosus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooms.
