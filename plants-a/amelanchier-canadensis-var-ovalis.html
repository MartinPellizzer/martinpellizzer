Amelanchier canadensis var. ovalis

Amelanchier canadensis var. ovalis, commonly known as the oval serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It features oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and turn vibrant red or orange in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
