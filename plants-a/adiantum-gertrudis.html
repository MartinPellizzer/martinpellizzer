Adiantum gertrudis

Adiantum gertrudis, commonly known as the Gertrudis maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Adiantaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace, with a distinctive veining pattern. Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant's rhizomes are creeping and produce upright, branched stems that bear the feathery fronds.

Adiantum gertrudis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic beauty.
