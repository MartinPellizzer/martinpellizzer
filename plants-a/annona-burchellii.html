Annona burchellii

Annona burchellii, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by a fruit that is soft and has a sweet, creamy flavor. The fruit is surrounded by a thick, waxy skin and contains a single large seed.

Annona burchellii thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit.
