Astragalus pennatus

Astragalus pennatus, commonly known as the featherleaf milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves that resemble feathers. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Astragalus pennatus typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
