Acaena humilis

Acaena humilis, commonly known as the common burr bush, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive spiny fruits known as burrs. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and thrives in a variety of soil types, including sandy and rocky soils. It is often found in coastal and inland areas, contributing to local biodiversity.

Acaena humilis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
