Atriplex vesicaria subsp. incompta

Atriplex vesicaria subsp.

incompta, commonly known as the smooth saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its succulent, often glandular leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its salt-tolerant nature allows it to thrive in saline soils, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in harsh landscapes.
