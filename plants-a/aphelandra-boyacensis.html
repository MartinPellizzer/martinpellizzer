Aphelandra boyacensis

Aphelandra boyacensis, commonly known as the Boyacensis torchflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Colombia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescences that resemble torches. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces clusters of tubular, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of yellow and orange. Aphelandra boyacensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
