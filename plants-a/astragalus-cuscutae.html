Astragalus cuscutae

Astragalus cuscutae, commonly known as the Indian milkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia, including parts of India and China.

Astragalus cuscutae is adapted to thrive in poor soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
