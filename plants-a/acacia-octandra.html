Acacia octandra

Acacia octandra, commonly known as the African milkwood tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its straight, often thornless trunk and compound leaves that are pinnately arranged. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia octandra is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in dry climates.

It is also known for its latex sap, which has been used traditionally for various purposes.
