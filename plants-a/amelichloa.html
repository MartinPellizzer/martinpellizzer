Amelichloa

Amelichloa, commonly known as the bottlebrush grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences that resemble a bottlebrush, giving it its common name. These plants are typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, often growing in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Amelichloa species are perennial grasses that can thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy or clay soils.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in dry environments.
