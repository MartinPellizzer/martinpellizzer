Astragalus straturensis

Astragalus straturensis, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
