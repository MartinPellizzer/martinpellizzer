Acalypha ornata var. glandulosa

Acalypha ornata var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that range in color from red to orange, often mistaken for flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
