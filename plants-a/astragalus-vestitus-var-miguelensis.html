Astragalus vestitus var. miguelensis

Astragalus vestitus var. miguelensis, commonly known as the Miguel's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in dry, harsh environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
