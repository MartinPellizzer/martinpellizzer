Acanthochlamys bracteata

Acanthochlamys bracteata, commonly known as the hairy bract ephedra, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bracts covered in fine hairs and its simple, linear leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often hidden beneath the foliage. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
