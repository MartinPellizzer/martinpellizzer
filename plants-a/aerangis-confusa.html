Aerangis confusa

Aerangis confusa, commonly known as the confused orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
