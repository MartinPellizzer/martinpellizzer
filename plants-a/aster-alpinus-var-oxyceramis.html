Aster alpinus var. oxyceramis

Aster alpinus var. oxyceramis, commonly known as the Alpine aster, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow central disk. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, often at high altitudes. Its foliage consists of linear, grass-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, late-summer blooms.
