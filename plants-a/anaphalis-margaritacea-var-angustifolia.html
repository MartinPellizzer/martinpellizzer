Anaphalis margaritacea var. angustifolia

Anaphalis margaritacea var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, clustered white flowers that resemble tiny buttons. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
