Aeschynanthus hartleyi

Aeschynanthus hartleyi, commonly known as the Hartley's ivy vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, with small, opposite leaves.

Aeschynanthus hartleyi is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in hanging baskets or as a trailing plant.
