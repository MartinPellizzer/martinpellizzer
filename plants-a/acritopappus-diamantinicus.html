Acritopappus diamantinicus

Acritopappus diamantinicus, commonly known as the Diamond Mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
