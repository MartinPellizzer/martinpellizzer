Anacamptis × darcissii

Anacamptis × darcissii, commonly known as the Darcys' orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the genus Anacamptis, which includes several species of orchids found in the Mediterranean region. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
