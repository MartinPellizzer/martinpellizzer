Anisostachya parvifolia

Anisostachya parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces panicles of small, fluffy seeds. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems.
