Ayenia aculeata

Ayenia aculeata, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, which bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are modified into spines, providing protection from herbivores.

Ayenia aculeata is also known for its edible fruit, which is often used in culinary and traditional practices.
