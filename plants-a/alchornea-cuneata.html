Alchornea cuneata

Alchornea cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf alchornea, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Alchornea cuneata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
