Anthemis plutonia var. plutonia

Anthemis plutonia var. plutonia, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant features erect, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
