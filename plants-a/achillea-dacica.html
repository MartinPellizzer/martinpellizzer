Achillea dacica

Achillea dacica, commonly known as the Dacian yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

Achillea dacica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
