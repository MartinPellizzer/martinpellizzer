Aspalathus papillosa

Aspalathus papillosa, commonly known as the silver leaf bush, is a South African plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in arid and semi-arid regions.

Aspalathus papillosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
