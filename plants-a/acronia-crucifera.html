Acronia crucifera

Acronia crucifera, commonly known as the crucifix acronia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant has linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
