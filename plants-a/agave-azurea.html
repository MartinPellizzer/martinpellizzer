Agave azurea

Agave azurea, commonly known as the blue agave, is a striking succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces a tall, flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which are often clustered in a panicle shape. It is well adapted to dry climates, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Agave azurea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
