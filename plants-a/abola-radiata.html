Abola radiata

Abola radiata, commonly known as the golden crown fern, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem with clusters of small, bright yellow spore-producing structures at the tips of its leaves. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant color and intricate foliage, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Abola radiata prefers indirect light and consistent moisture, thriving in humid environments.
