Aulomyrcia gaudichaudiana

Aulomyrcia gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible but often have a spicy or peppery taste. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and resilience in various climates.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
