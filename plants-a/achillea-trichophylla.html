Achillea trichophylla

Achillea trichophylla, commonly known as the soft yarrow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It is known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
