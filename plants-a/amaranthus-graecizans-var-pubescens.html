Amaranthus graecizans var. pubescens

Amaranthus graecizans var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, feather-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that eventually develop into prickly, three-lobed seed capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional content, it is cultivated in gardens and for forage purposes.
