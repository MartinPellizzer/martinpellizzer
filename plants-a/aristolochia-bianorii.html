Aristolochia bianorii

Aristolochia bianorii, commonly known as the Bianorii Dutchman's pipe, is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its unique, hood-shaped flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color. The plant is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for certain pollinators.
