Aria alnifolia f. lobulata

Aria alnifolia f. lobulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf alder, is a shrub belonging to the Betulaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically narrower than those of the typical species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green catkins that appear in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
