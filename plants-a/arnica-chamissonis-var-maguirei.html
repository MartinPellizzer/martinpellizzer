Arnica chamissonis var. maguirei

Arnica chamissonis var. maguirei, commonly known as the Maguire's arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers are yellow to orange in color, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds in a capsule.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
