Achyranthes rufa

Achyranthes rufa, commonly known as red burr.

It is a flowering plant native to the Americas, particularly found in the southern United States and parts of Mexico. The plant is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, reddish flowers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Achyranthes rufa is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
