Asplenium kaulfussii f. kaulfussii

Asplenium kaulfussii f. kaulfussii, commonly known as the Kaulfuss's spleenwort, is a fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic or lithophytic fern that typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive, finely divided structure. It produces spores in clusters called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, showcasing its adaptability to moist, shaded habitats.
