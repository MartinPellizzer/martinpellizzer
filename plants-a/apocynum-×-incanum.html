Apocynum × incanum

Apocynum × incanum, commonly known as the Indian rope plant, is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its milky sap contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine.
