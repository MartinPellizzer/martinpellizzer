Astragalus caudiculosus

Astragalus caudiculosus, commonly known as the whipstem milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus caudiculosus is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.
