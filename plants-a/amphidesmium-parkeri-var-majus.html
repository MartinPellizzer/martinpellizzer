Amphidesmium parkeri var. majus

Amphidesmium parkeri var. majus, commonly known as the giant grass of the Congo, is a tall, herbaceous plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and robust growth habit. The plant can reach heights of up to 5 meters, making it one of the tallest grasses in its native habitat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forests.

Due to its size and ecological significance, it plays an important role in the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
