Achyranthes bidentata var. japonica

Achyranthes bidentata var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, bamboo-like stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish at the base. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found near streams, ditches, and in disturbed areas.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive nature, it is considered a problematic weed in many regions.
