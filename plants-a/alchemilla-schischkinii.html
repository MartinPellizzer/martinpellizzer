Alchemilla schischkinii

Alchemilla schischkinii, commonly known as the Schischkin's lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are borne on slender stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Alchemilla schischkinii is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and its ability to attract pollinators.
