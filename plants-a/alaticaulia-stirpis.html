Alaticaulia stirpis

Alaticaulia stirpis, commonly known as the winged-stemmed shrub, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, wing-like stems that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers during the summer months, which are followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a thick layer of wax, providing additional protection against extreme temperatures.

Alaticaulia stirpis plays a vital role in stabilizing desert soils and supporting local wildlife.
