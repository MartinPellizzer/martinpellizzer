Amaryllis × venosa

Amaryllis × venosa, commonly known as the dragon tongue lily, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis papilio.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking, colorful, strap-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a distinctive "dragon tongue" appearance due to the elongated petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, tropical-looking plants.

Amaryllis × venosa is also known for its ability to bloom multiple times a year under optimal care.
