Allium junceum

Allium junceum, commonly known as the threadleaf garlic, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. The plant is characterized by its bulbous structure, which is used for culinary and medicinal purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium junceum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
