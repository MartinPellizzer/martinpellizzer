Asplenium douglasii

Asplenium douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered closely together. The plant typically grows on rocks, trees, or soil in temperate regions, particularly in the Pacific Northwest of North America. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the leaves, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Asplenium douglasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shady gardens or as a houseplant.
