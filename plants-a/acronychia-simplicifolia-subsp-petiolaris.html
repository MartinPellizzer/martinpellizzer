Acronychia simplicifolia subsp. petiolaris

Acronychia simplicifolia subsp.

petiolaris, commonly known as the slender-leaved acronychia, is a small evergreen tree native to northern Queensland, Australia. It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain smooth, black seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in rainforest or coastal scrub habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
