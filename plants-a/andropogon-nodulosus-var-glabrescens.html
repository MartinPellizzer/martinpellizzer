Andropogon nodulosus var. glabrescens

Andropogon nodulosus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
