Andropogon nemophyllus

Andropogon nemophyllus, commonly known as the northern wood grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.

Andropogon nemophyllus plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various small organisms.
