Anthurium pichindense

Anthurium pichindense, commonly known as the Pichindense arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to South America, specifically the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium pichindense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
