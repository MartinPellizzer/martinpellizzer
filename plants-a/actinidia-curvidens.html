Actinidia curvidens

Actinidia curvidens, commonly known as the broadleaf actinidia, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential as a food source.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
