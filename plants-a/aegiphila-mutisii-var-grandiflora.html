Aegiphila mutisii var. grandiflora

Aegiphila mutisii var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered shrub, is a perennial plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple, blooming in clusters during the spring and summer seasons.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
