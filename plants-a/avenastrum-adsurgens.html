Avenastrum adsurgens

Avenastrum adsurgens, commonly known as the climbing grass or creeping grass, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette at the base.

Avenastrum adsurgens is adapted to grow in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
