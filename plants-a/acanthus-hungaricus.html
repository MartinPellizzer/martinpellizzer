Acanthus hungaricus

Acanthus hungaricus, commonly known as the Hungarian acanthus, is a perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and showy, spire-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are usually white or pale blue and are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

Acanthus hungaricus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and architectural foliage.
