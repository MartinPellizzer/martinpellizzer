Arundinella muthikulamensis

Arundinella muthikulamensis, commonly known as the Muthikulam bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to the tropical regions of southern India.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are typically green to yellowish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are often wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a fibrous texture.

Arundinella muthikulamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its structural and decorative qualities in gardens.
