Azalea degroniana

Azalea degroniana, commonly known as the Southern azalea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Azalea degroniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
