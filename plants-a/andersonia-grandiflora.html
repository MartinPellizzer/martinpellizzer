Andersonia grandiflora

Andersonia grandiflora, commonly known as the mountain bell heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts aim to protect its natural habitat.
