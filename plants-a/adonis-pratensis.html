Adonis pratensis

Adonis pratensis, commonly known as the meadow adonis, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, early spring blooms. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers are yellow with prominent stamens.

It is often considered a rare and declining species due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
