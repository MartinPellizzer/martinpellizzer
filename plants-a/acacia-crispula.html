Acacia crispula

Acacia crispula, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Acacia crispula is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
