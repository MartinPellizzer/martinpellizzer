Agrostis capillaris subsp. oreophila

Agrostis capillaris subsp.

oreophila, commonly known as mountain hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are narrow and finely textured, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
