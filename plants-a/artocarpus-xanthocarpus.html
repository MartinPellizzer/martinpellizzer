Artocarpus xanthocarpus

Artocarpus xanthocarpus, commonly known as the golden fig or sometimes the wild breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a unique, spiky fruit known as a syncarp. The fruit contains a sweet, edible pulp surrounded by a fibrous, inedible layer. The plant is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical features make it an important species in tropical horticulture and traditional medicine.
