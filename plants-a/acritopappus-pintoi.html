Acritopappus pintoi

Acritopappus pintoi, commonly known as the Hawaiian acanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features spiny leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in coastal and dry forest habitats, often growing on rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
