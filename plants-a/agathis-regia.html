Agathis regia

Agathis regia, commonly known as the royal agarwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araucariaceae and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, aromatic heartwood. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is known for its ability to produce a resin that is highly valued in perfumery and traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture.

Agathis regia is also noted for its ecological importance in providing habitat for various wildlife species.
