Amischophacelus

Amischophacelus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes. Amischophacelus is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it an invasive species in many areas.
