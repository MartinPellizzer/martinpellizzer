Aniba gigantifolia

Aniba gigantifolia, commonly known as the giant sylph, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a distinct midrib. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by oval, woody fruits containing flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its aromatic properties.

Aniba gigantifolia is also noted for its potential medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
