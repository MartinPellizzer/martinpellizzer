Apargia sicula

Apargia sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

Apargia sicula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
