Asphodeline wiedemanniana

Asphodeline wiedemanniana, commonly known as the Wiedemann's asphodel, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces tall, spiky inflorescences of pale yellow to white flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and the plant is known for its resilience in poor soil conditions.

Asphodeline wiedemanniana is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
