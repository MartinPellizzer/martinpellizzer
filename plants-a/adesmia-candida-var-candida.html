Adesmia candida var. candida

Adesmia candida var. candida, commonly known as the white-flowered legume, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is distinguished by its small, white, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds.

Adesmia candida var. candida is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
