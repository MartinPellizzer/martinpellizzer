Acer insigne var. longiloba

Acer insigne var. longiloba, commonly known as the long-leaf maple, is a variant of the maple tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
