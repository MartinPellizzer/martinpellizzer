Alisma angustifolium

Alisma angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf water plantain, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are usually less than an inch wide. Its small white flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

Alisma angustifolium is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
