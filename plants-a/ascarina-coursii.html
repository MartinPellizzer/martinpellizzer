Ascarina coursii

Ascarina coursii, commonly known as the New Zealand kauri, is a large evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Araucariaceae and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, spreading crown. The leaves are typically scale-like or needle-like, depending on the age of the tree. It produces distinctive cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs.

This species plays a significant ecological role in New Zealand's forests and is also valued for its timber.
