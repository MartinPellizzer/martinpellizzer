Astragalus ptilodes subsp. cariensis

Astragalus ptilodes subsp.

cariensis, commonly known as the Carian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
