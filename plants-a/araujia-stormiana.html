Araujia stormiana

Araujia stormiana, commonly known as the moonflower, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped blooms that are often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous seeds, aiding in its dispersal.
