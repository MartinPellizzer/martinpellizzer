Acacia aciphylla

Acacia aciphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, feathery leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which appear in spring. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and efficient water use.

Acacia aciphylla plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
