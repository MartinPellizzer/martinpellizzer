Aletris foliata

Aletris foliata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its compact, clump-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. Aletris foliata thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for woodland gardens and naturalistic landscapes.
