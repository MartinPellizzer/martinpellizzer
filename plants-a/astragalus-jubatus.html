Astragalus jubatus

Astragalus jubatus, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus jubatus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
