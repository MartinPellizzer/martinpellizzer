Aria corymbiferum var. turbinata

Aria corymbiferum var. turbinata, commonly known as the Turbinated Aria, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, turbinated (spiral-shaped) inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
