Anthurium angustilaminatum

Anthurium angustilaminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are modified leaves that enclose the actual flowers, often in vibrant red or orange hues. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium angustilaminatum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
