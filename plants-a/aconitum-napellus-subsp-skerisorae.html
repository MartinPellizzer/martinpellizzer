Aconitum napellus subsp. skerisorae

Aconitum napellus subsp.

skerisorae, commonly known as the Sker Island aconite, is a subspecies native to the Isle of Skye in Scotland. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. Its roots contain potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic to both humans and animals.

This subspecies is of particular interest in botanical studies due to its unique habitat and chemical composition.
