Arctostaphylos adenotricha

Arctostaphylos adenotricha, commonly known as the hairy manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it a resilient and ecologically significant species.
