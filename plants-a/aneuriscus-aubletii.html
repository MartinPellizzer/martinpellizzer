Aneuriscus aubletii

Aneuriscus aubletii, commonly known as the desert candle, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It features a thick, cylindrical stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically reduced to small spines, which help reduce water loss.

Aneuriscus aubletii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
