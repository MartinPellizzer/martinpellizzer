Acacia metrosiderosifolia

Acacia metrosiderosifolia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a fast-growing, deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters during the warmer months. The tree has bipinnate leaves that are often shed during dry seasons, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental planting.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
