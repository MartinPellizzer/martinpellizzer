Achillea commutata

Achillea commutata, commonly known as narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in scrublands and open woodlands, and it is known for its aromatic foliage.

Achillea commutata is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
