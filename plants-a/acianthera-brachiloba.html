Acianthera brachiloba

Acianthera brachiloba, commonly known as the short-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically features a short, fleshy stem with a few leaves and produces small, white or pale pink blossoms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
