Astragalus falcinellus

Astragalus falcinellus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy species found in various western states.
