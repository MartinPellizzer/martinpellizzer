Acacia catechu var. campylacantha

Acacia catechu var. campylacantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf catechu, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of tannins.
