Alsmithia

Alsmithia, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family.

It is native to South Africa and consists of several species, most of which are shrubs or subshrubs. The plants are characterized by their silver-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. They produce vibrant yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Alsmithia species are often used in xeriscaping due to their drought tolerance and ornamental value.
