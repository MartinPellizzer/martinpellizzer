Arachis glabrata f. major

Arachis glabrata f. major, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a member of the legume family and produces large, hard-shelled fruits containing edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

The seeds, known as Brazil nuts, are rich in nutrients and have significant economic and ecological importance.
