Allium ramazanicum

Allium ramazanicum, commonly known as the Ramazan garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, ovoid bulbs that are typically harvested in the autumn. Its botanical features include fibrous roots, strap-like leaves, and a distinct garlic scent.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses in traditional cuisines.
