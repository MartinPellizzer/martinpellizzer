Astragalus barukanus

Astragalus barukanus, commonly known as the Barukan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are adapted for dispersal by wind, aiding in its survival in harsh climates.
