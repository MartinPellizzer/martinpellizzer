Aspidium spinulosum f. anadenium

Aspidium spinulosum f. anadenium, commonly known as the Anadenium fern, is a varietal form of the common aspidium fern.

It is a perennial plant native to regions of Southeast Asia, characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage is typically a deep green, with a slightly serrated edge, and it produces small, spore-bearing structures on the underside of its leaves.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
