Antirrhinum majus var. dubium

Antirrhinum majus var. dubium, commonly known as the lesser snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant with opposite, linear to lanceolate leaves. The flowers are typically tubular with two lips, often displaying shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
