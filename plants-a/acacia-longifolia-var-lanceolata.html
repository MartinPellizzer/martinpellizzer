Acacia longifolia var. lanceolata

Acacia longifolia var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattles, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It features compound leaves with narrow, lance-shaped phyllodes that are usually bright green and slightly hairy. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
