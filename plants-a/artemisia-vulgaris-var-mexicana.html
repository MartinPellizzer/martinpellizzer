Artemisia vulgaris var. mexicana

Artemisia vulgaris var. mexicana, commonly known as Mexican wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features silver-gray, aromatic foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its medicinal properties and historical use, it has been cultivated for traditional herbal remedies.
