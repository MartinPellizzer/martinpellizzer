Abromeitia pterocarpa

Abromeitia pterocarpa, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a sprawling growth habit, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Abromeitia pterocarpa is well-suited to xeriscaping and thrives in well-drained soils with minimal water requirements.
