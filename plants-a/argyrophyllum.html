Argyrophyllum

Argyrophyllum, commonly known as silverleaf plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its silvery or grayish foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, often arranged in a rosette or alternate pattern. The flowers are small and clustered in composite heads, with both ray and disk florets.

Argyrophyllum species are native to various regions in the Americas and are often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
