Aristida concinna

Aristida concinna, commonly known as the slender dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed areas. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that is usually less than 10 cm in length.

Aristida concinna is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
