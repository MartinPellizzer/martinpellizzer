Artanthe peruviana f. laevigata

Artanthe peruviana f. laevigata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved artanthe, is a perennial plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in native and rare plant species.
