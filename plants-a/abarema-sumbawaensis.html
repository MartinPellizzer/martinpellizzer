Abarema sumbawaensis

Abarema sumbawaensis, commonly known as the Sumbawa legume, is a species native to the island of Sumbawa in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellow flowers clustered in racemes.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated pods, which split open to release the seeds when mature.
