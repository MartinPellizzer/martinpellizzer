Aechmea petropolitana

Aechmea petropolitana, commonly known as the Rock Orchid, is a bromeliad native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaf structure, which forms a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking, orchid-like inflorescences in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rock surfaces.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.

Morphology

Aechmea petropolitana has rosette-shaped leaves that are long, narrow, and sword-like, arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem, with a waxy, dark green to bluish-gray color.

The stem is short and upright, with a smooth, somewhat woody texture and a pale green to reddish-brown color. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, typically in shades of yellow to orange, blooming during the summer months and pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry containing numerous small, black seeds, which are dispersed by birds and other animals.

The roots are fibrous and adventitious, anchoring the plant and absorbing moisture from the surrounding environment.
