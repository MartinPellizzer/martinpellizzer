Alchemilla filicaulis var. dasycaula

Alchemilla filicaulis var. dasycaula, commonly known as the woolly alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features basal rosettes of soft, hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a decorative, easy-to-care-for ground cover.
