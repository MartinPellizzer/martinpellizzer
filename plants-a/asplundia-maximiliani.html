Asplundia maximiliani

Asplundia maximiliani, commonly known as the Malayan arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking inflorescence. The plant produces a starchy tuberous root that is used as a food source. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Asplundia maximiliani is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its edible root.
