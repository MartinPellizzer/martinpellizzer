Acalypha decumbens var. genuina

Acalypha decumbens var. genuina, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves with a silvery underside, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and restoration projects.
