Arundinaria viridistriata var. kongosanensis

Arundinaria viridistriata var. kongosanensis, commonly known as the Kongo mountain bamboo, is a variant of the green-striated bamboo native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often greenish in color, with distinct longitudinal stripes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This bamboo species is valued for its unique appearance and is sometimes cultivated for both decorative and ecological purposes.
