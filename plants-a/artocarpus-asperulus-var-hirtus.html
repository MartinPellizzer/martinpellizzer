Artocarpus asperulus var. hirtus

Artocarpus asperulus var. hirtus, commonly known as the rough-hairy breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy texture on its leaves and fruits. The plant produces a distinctive fruit with a fibrous, inedible outer layer and a starchy, edible interior. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a rough, serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
