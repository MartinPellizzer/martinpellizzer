Astragalus coluteocarpus subsp. heratensis

Astragalus coluteocarpus subsp.

heratensis, commonly known as the Herat milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by slender, pod-like fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and mountainous regions.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
