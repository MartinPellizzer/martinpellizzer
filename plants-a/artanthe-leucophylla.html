Artanthe leucophylla

Artanthe leucophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and delicate, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and chaparral. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs that give it a distinctive appearance.

Artanthe leucophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
