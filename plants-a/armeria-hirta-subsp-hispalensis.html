Armeria hirta subsp. hispalensis

Armeria hirta subsp.

hispalensis, commonly known as the Spanish thrift, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa. It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that produces clusters of pink or white flowers.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats, where it is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
