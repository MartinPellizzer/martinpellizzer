Abutilon domingense

Abutilon domingense, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Abutilon domingense is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
