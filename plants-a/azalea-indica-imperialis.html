Azalea indica imperialis

Azalea indica imperialis, commonly known as the imperial azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features dense, dark green foliage and produces abundant nectar, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial shade to full sun for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural displays.
