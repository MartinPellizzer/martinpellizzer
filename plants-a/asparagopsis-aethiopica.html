Asparagopsis aethiopica

Asparagopsis aethiopica, commonly known as African asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used for soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
