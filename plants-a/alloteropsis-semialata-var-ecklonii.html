Alloteropsis semialata var. ecklonii

Alloteropsis semialata var. ecklonii, commonly known as the Cape flax or Ecklon's flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in suitable climates.
