Achneria mucronata

Achneria mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed stinging nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and fine, hair-like structures that release an irritating sap when touched. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in dense clusters, and it is often used in traditional medicine.
