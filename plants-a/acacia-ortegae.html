Acacia ortegae

Acacia ortegae, commonly known as the Ortega's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or gray hairs, which help reduce water loss. Its yellow flowers are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, and it produces flat, leathery pods that contain several seeds.

Acacia ortegae is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it an important component of its native ecosystems.
