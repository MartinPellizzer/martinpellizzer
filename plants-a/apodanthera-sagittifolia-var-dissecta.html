Apodanthera sagittifolia var. dissecta

Apodanthera sagittifolia var. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a reddish or purplish tint. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported astringent properties.

The species is characterized by its unique leaf morphology and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.
