Albuca chlorantha

Albuca chlorantha, commonly known as the white-tipped albuca, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green with white or yellow markings. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers with prominent, showy bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Albuca chlorantha is relatively low maintenance and drought tolerant once established.
