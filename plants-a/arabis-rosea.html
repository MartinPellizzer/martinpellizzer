Arabis rosea

Arabis rosea, commonly known as the pink rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are small, pink or white, and appear in clusters, blooming in early spring.

Arabis rosea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
