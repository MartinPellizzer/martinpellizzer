Adiantopsis madagascariensis

Adiantopsis madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar maidenhair fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that resemble those of the common maidenhair fern. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it can thrive in high humidity. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a glossy appearance, with a distinctive central vein running along the length of each frond.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Adiantopsis madagascariensis is considered a rare and endangered species.
