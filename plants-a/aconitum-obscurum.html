Aconitum obscurum

Aconitum obscurum, commonly known as the dark aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant produces follicles containing toxic alkaloids, making it highly poisonous to both humans and animals. Its leaves are palmately divided and often have a velvety texture.

Aconitum obscurum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
