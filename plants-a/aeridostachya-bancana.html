Aeridostachya bancana

Aeridostachya bancana, commonly known as the banana orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, banana-like leaves and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often near water bodies or in dense vegetation. Its flowers are usually green or yellow with purple or red markings, and it produces small, edible fruits.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
