Aster cichoriaceus

Aster cichoriaceus, commonly known as the common groundsel, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and hairy, with a rough texture. Its flowering stems are erect and branched, bearing yellow ray florets surrounded by tubular disc florets.

Aster cichoriaceus is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
