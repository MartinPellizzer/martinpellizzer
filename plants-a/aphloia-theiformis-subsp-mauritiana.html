Aphloia theiformis subsp. mauritiana

Aphloia theiformis subsp.

mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the Mascarene Islands. It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in dense clusters, often resembling flames.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
