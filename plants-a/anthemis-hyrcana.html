Anthemis hyrcana

Anthemis hyrcana, commonly known as the Hyrcanian Anthemis, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its yellow flowers, which bloom in the summer, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
