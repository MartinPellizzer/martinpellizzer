Anthurium trifidum

Anthurium trifidum, commonly known as the three-lobed anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking spadices, which are the inflorescences that house the small, inconspicuous flowers. Anthurium trifidum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in indoor settings with proper care.
