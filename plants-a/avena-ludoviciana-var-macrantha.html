Avena ludoviciana var. macrantha

Avena ludoviciana var. macrantha, commonly known as the giant oat, is a tall, perennial grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and large, feathery inflorescences that can reach several feet in height. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in well-drained soils in open habitats.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
