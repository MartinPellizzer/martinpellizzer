Apurimacia incarum

Apurimacia incarum, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and sunny conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to withstand harsh mountain climates.

Apurimacia incarum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
