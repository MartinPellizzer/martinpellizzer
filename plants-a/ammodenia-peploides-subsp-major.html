Ammodenia peploides subsp. major

Ammodenia peploides subsp.

major, commonly known as the coastal bursage, is a shrub native to coastal regions of California and Baja California. It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. Its leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, providing year-round cover.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and supporting local wildlife.
