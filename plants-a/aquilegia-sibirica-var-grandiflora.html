Aquilegia sibirica var. grandiflora

Aquilegia sibirica var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Siberian poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and Central Asia.

It features striking, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in well-drained, loamy soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
