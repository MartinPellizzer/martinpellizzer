Arytera chartacea

Arytera chartacea, commonly known as the paperleaf lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, papery texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
