Alchemilla lulensis

Alchemilla lulensis, commonly known as the Lulian lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, velvety leaves that often have a silver or grayish underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in branched inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Alchemilla lulensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
