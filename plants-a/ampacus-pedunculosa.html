Ampacus pedunculosa

Ampacus pedunculosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Ampacus pedunculosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
