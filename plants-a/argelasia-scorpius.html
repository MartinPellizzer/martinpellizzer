Argelasia scorpius

Argelasia scorpius, commonly known as the scorpion plant, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescence that resembles the tail of a scorpion, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom during the late spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Argelasia scorpius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in humid, shaded environments.
