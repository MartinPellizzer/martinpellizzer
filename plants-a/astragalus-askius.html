Astragalus askius

Astragalus askius, commonly known as the Askias milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its roots are known for containing bioactive compounds, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Astragalus askius plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats due to its extensive root system.
