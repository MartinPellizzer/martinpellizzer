Anthemis rumelica var. digitata

Anthemis rumelica var. digitata, commonly known as the digitate anthemis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, daisy-like flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
