Assonia viburniflora

Assonia viburniflora, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn reddish in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Assonia viburniflora is often used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and pleasant aroma.
