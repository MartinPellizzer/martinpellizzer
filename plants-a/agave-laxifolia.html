Agave laxifolia

Agave laxifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears clusters of white to yellow flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, requiring minimal water and well-drained soil.

Agave laxifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
