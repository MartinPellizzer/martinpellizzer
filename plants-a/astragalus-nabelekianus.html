Astragalus nabelekianus

Astragalus nabelekianus, commonly known as the Nabelek's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces pods that contain small, round seeds.

Astragalus nabelekianus is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
