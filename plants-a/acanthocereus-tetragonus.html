Acanthocereus tetragonus

Acanthocereus tetragonus, commonly known as the four-angled cactus, is a species native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its ridged, four-sided stems and spines that are typically yellow or reddish in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

The plant's structure and appearance make it a distinctive and visually striking addition to desert gardens.
