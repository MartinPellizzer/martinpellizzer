Anelasma laurifolium

Anelasma laurifolium, commonly known as the laurel-leaf anelasma, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of laurel plants. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its unique appearance and fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
