Amomum siamense

Amomum siamense, commonly known as Siamese cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, elongated rhizomes. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a fragrant, slightly spicy aroma.

Amomum siamense is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
