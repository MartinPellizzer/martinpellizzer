Aconitum confertiflorum var. versicolor

Aconitum confertiflorum var. versicolor, commonly known as the Variable Crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately divided, deeply cut leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
