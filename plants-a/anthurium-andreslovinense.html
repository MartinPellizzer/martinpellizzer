Anthurium andreslovinense

Anthurium andreslovinense, commonly known as the Andes lily, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces vibrant, colorful spathes that range from red to orange, often with intricate patterns. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
