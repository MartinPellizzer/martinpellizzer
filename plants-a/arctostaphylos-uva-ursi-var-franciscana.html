Arctostaphylos uva-ursi var. franciscana

Arctostaphylos uva-ursi var. franciscana, commonly known as Franciscan manzanita, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, shiny seeds.

This species is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal chaparral habitats.
