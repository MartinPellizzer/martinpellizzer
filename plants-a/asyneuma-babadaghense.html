Asyneuma babadaghense

Asyneuma babadaghense, commonly known as the Babadagh daisy, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Asyneuma babadaghense is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
