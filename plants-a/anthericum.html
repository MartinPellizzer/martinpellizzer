Anthericum

Anthericum, commonly known as the lily of the valley, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asparagaceae.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in woodland areas and shaded gardens. The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, contributing to their ecological significance.
