Aspalathus verbasciformis

Aspalathus verbasciformis, commonly known as the silver leaf bush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The botanical features of Aspalathus verbasciformis make it an attractive and hardy plant for ornamental gardening.
