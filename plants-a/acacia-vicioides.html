Acacia vicioides

Acacia vicioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Acacia vicioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in ecological restoration projects.
