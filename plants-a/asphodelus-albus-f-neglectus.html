Asphodelus albus f. neglectus

Asphodelus albus f. neglectus, commonly known as the white asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, spike-like inflorescences with small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats. It is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

The flower structure is typical of the Asphodelaceae family, with tepals that are often fused at the base.
