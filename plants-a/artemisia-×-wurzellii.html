Artemisia × wurzellii

Artemisia × wurzellii, commonly known as the silver dust sage, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are typically borne in clusters at the ends of branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suited for arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often used in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
