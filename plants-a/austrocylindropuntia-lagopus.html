Austrocylindropuntia lagopus

Austrocylindropuntia lagopus, commonly known as the rock cactus or the desert rose, is a species of cactus native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered in spines and are adapted to store water. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green stems. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Austrocylindropuntia lagopus thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well-suited for harsh desert conditions.
