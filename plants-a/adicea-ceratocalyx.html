Adicea ceratocalyx

Adicea ceratocalyx, commonly known as the horned hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny calyx that resembles horns, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. It typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as wetlands and along streambanks.

Adicea ceratocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its unique foliage and floral display.
