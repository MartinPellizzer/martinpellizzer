Asplenium mohillense

Asplenium mohillense, commonly known as the Mohill fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the island of Mohill in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, densely packed fronds that form a rosette-like structure. The leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with a finely divided, pinnate appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on rocky substrates or in forest undergrowth.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
