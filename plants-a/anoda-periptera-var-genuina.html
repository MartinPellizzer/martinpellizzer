Anoda periptera var. genuina

Anoda periptera var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ease of care.
