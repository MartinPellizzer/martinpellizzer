Althaea biennis

Althaea biennis, commonly known as the marsh mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has soft, velvety leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

Althaea biennis is also valued for its medicinal properties, with its roots and leaves used in traditional remedies.
