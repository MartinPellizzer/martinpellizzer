Anthericum algeriense

Anthericum algeriense, commonly known as Algerian Lords-and-Ladies, is a perennial herb native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a persistent weed in agricultural areas.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought conditions.
