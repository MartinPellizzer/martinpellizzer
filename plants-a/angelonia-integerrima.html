Angelonia integerrima

Angelonia integerrima, commonly known as the winter angelonia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces clusters of bright red, pink, or white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Angelonia integerrima is valued in gardens for its long bloom period and adaptability to various soil conditions.
