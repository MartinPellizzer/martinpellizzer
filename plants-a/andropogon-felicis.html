Andropogon felicis

Andropogon felicis, commonly known as sweet grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and its distinctive, aromatic scent when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, open areas such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowering spikes are slender and often appear in late summer.

Sweet grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally by Indigenous peoples for ceremonial purposes.
