Argythamnia purpurascens

Argythamnia purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered thornbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fibrous roots help it survive in dry conditions by efficiently absorbing and storing water.

Argythamnia purpurascens is also valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
