Astragalus paradoxus

Astragalus paradoxus, commonly known as the paradoxical milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and can be found in various ecological zones, including sagebrush steppe.

Astragalus paradoxus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
