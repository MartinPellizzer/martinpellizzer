Astragalus nimrudensis

Astragalus nimrudensis, commonly known as the Nimrud milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
