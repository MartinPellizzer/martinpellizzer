Ajuga australis var. diemenica

Ajuga australis var. diemenica, commonly known as the Tasmanian bugleweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Tasmania.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers arranged in spikes during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
