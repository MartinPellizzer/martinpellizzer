Asparagus multiflorus

Asparagus multiflorus, commonly known as the white asparagus or sweet asparagus, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, succulent stems and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates. Its edible young shoots are harvested in the spring and are prized for their tender texture and mild flavor.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
