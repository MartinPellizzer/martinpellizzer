Anthurium tarapuiense

Anthurium tarapuiense, commonly known as the Tarapuiense Arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to South America, specifically found in regions of Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive spatulate leaves and spadix inflorescence. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, with a waxy texture, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe.

Anthurium tarapuiense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
