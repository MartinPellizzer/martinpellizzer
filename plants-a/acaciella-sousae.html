Acaciella sousae

Acaciella sousae, commonly known as the Socotra acacia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra in the Arabian Sea.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, flat, shield-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, which are pollinated by insects. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, woody pods that split open to release the seeds.

Acaciella sousae is also notable for its unique, thorned structure and its role in the unique ecosystem of Socotra.
