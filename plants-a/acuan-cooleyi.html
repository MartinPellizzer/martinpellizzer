Acuan cooleyi

Acuan cooleyi, commonly known as the mountain glory, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to thrive in cool, moist environments. It is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain where it forms dense, low-growing mats.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
