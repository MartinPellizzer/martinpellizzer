Athyrium amabile

Athyrium amabile, commonly known as the Japanese maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to form dense colonies over time.

Athyrium amabile is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
