Acacia rufobrunnea

Acacia rufobrunnea, commonly known as the red-brown acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, yellowish-green leaves that are usually clustered at the ends of the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular inflorescences, which are followed by long, flat, woody pods. Acacia rufobrunnea is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species often found in savannas and rocky areas.

It is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fodder.
