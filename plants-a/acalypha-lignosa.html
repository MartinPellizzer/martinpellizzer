Acalypha lignosa

Acalypha lignosa, commonly known as the red-root or red-leaf spurges, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Acalypha lignosa is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
