Attalea exigua

Attalea exigua, commonly known as the pink palm or small pink palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters, with a slender trunk and a crown of pinnate leaves. The plant is characterized by its distinctive pinkish or reddish coloration on the leaves and fruit, which gives it its common name. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in wetland or swampy areas.

The fruit of Attalea exigua is edible and has been used by indigenous communities for food and traditional purposes.
