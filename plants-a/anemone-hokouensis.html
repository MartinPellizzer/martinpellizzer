Anemone hokouensis

Anemone hokouensis, commonly known as the Hokou anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, petal-like bracts that surround its small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats and prefers cool, moist environments. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Anemone hokouensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
