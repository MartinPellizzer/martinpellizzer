Adiantopsis regularis

Adiantopsis regularis, commonly known as the common maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It features finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically green with a slightly silvery sheen. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Adiantopsis regularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
