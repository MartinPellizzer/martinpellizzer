Astragalus macrosphaerus

Astragalus macrosphaerus, commonly known as the large globe milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its distinctive spherical seed pods, which give it its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Astragalus macrosphaerus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
