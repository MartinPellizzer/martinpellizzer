Archidendron pahangense

Archidendron pahangense, commonly known as the Pahang legume, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Malaysian rainforests.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The tree typically grows to a height of 10-20 meters, with smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. Its flowers are small, clustered, and often yellow or greenish in color, while the pods contain several seeds.

This species plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil fertility.
