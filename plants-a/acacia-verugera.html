Acacia verugera

Acacia verugera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, feathery leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions. Its flowers are often visited by native bees and birds, contributing to local biodiversity.

Acacia verugera is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, improving soil fertility.
