Achimenes picturata

Achimenes picturata, commonly known as the painted bellflower, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including pink, red, and purple. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blooms that are typically borne on erect, branching stems. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and somewhat hairy.

Achimenes picturata thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant for its vibrant floral display.

Morphology

Achimenes picturata has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem, with a dark green color and a slightly serrated edge.

The stem is erect, covered in a smooth, slightly hairy bark, and grows upright with a branching pattern. Its flowers are tubular with five petals, displaying vibrant red and orange hues, blooming from late spring to early autumn, and are pollinated by birds and insects. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and tuberous, aiding in the plant's ability to store nutrients and survive dry periods.
