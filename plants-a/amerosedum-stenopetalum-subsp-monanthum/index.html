Amerosedum Stenopetalum Subsp. Monanthum

Bluebell, scientifically known as Amerosedum stenopetalum subsp. monanthum, is a striking plant native to the southeastern United States. It is recognized for its vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its delicate foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Despite its beauty, it is often threatened by habitat loss and invasive species.
