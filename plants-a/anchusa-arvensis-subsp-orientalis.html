Anchusa arvensis subsp. orientalis

Anchusa arvensis subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in racemes during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
