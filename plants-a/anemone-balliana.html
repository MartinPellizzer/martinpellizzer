Anemone balliana

Anemone balliana, commonly known as the Himalayan anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Asia.

It features a short, thick rhizome and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven lobes that are often hairy and slightly serrated. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
