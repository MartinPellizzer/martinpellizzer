Agapetes corallina

Agapetes corallina, commonly known as the coral bell heath, is a species of shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many shrubs and small trees adapted to acidic soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture.
