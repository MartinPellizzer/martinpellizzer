Acacia gossweileri

Acacia gossweileri, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Acacia gossweileri is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique foliage and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
