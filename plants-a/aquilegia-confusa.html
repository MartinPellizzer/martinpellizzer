Aquilegia confusa

Aquilegia confusa, commonly known as the confused columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and its roots are fibrous.

Aquilegia confusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
