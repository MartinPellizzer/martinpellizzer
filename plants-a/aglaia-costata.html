Aglaia costata

Aglaia costata, commonly known as the yellow rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by round, woody fruits containing several seeds. Aglaia costata is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.

It also has traditional medicinal uses in various Asian cultures.
