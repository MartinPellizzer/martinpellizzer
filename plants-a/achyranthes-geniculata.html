Achyranthes geniculata

Achyranthes geniculata, commonly known as the Indian cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is an annual herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems and alternate leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spiny, spherical fruits. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
