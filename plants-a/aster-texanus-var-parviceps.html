Aster texanus var. parviceps

Aster texanus var. parviceps, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as plains and rocky areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
