Acacia lycopodiifolia var. glabrescens

Acacia lycopodiifolia var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense clusters of small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
