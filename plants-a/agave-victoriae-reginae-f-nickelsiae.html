Agave victoriae-reginae f. nickelsiae

Agave victoriae-reginae f. nickelsiae, commonly known as the Queen of the Night, is a unique variant of the agave plant native to the Sierra Madre mountains of Mexico.

This plant is characterized by its striking, elongated, blue-green leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, forming a rosette shape. It produces a tall, flowering stalk that bears numerous white, star-shaped flowers, which bloom only at night, hence its common name. The plant is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardens and botanical collections.
