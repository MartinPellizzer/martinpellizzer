Acacia kalgoorliensis

Acacia kalgoorliensis, commonly known as the Kalgoorlie wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found on rocky soils and in open woodlands.

Acacia kalgoorliensis is well adapted to drought and can tolerate poor soil conditions.
