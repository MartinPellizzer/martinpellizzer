Areca mammillata

Areca mammillata, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, palm-like leaves that grow in clusters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that eventually develop into three-winged fruits. Areca mammillata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habits make it well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates.
