Astragalus submaculatus

Astragalus submaculatus, commonly known as the spotted milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus submaculatus is often found in prairies, deserts, and along dry streambeds.
