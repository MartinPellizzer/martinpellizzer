Anubias haullevilleana

Anubias haullevilleana, commonly known as the African water lettuce, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are broad and floating, with a waxy surface that helps it thrive in water. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread slowly across the water's surface. Its leaves are typically a deep green color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged.

Anubias haullevilleana is often used in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide shelter for aquatic life.
