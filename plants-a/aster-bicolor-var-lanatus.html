Aster bicolor var. lanatus

Aster bicolor var. lanatus, commonly known as the woolly aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and purple, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
