Ardisia amherstiana var. pubescens

Ardisia amherstiana var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent beautyberry, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, dark purple berries that appear in clusters, often remaining on the plant throughout the winter. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface and a soft pubescent texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique fruit display.
