Aegiphila sufflava

Aegiphila sufflava, commonly known as the pink-flowered shrub, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Aegiphila sufflava is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
