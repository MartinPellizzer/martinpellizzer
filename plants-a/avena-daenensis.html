Avena daenensis

Avena daenensis, commonly known as the Daenish oat, is a species of grass native to southern Iran.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, Avena daenensis is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
