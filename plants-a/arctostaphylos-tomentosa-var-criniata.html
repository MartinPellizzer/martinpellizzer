Arctostaphylos tomentosa var. criniata

Arctostaphylos tomentosa var. criniata, commonly known as the white-tomentose manzanita, is a shrub native to California.

It is characterized by its dense, white-tomentose (fuzzy) foliage and reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and often have a distinctive white or grayish pubescence.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub ecosystems.
