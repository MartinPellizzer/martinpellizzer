Aquilegia vulgaris var. wallichiana

Aquilegia vulgaris var. wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan garden bell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and two elongated nectar guides. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thrives in well-drained, loamy soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and gardens in temperate climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and shady borders.
