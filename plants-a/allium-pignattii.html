Allium pignattii

Allium pignattii, commonly known as the Italian garlic or garlic chives, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often used in cooking for their mild garlic flavor. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its bulbous stem is often used as a substitute for garlic in various cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species like onions and leeks.
