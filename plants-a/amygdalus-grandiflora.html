Amygdalus grandiflora

Amygdalus grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered almond.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. The plant produces showy white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible almonds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Amygdalus grandiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
