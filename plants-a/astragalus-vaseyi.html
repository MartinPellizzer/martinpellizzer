Astragalus vaseyi

Astragalus vaseyi, commonly known as the Vasey's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and desert plains. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

Astragalus vaseyi is often found in open habitats with limited vegetation.
