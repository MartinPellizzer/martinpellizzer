Arabis pulchra var. pallens

Arabis pulchra var. pallens, commonly known as the pale Arabis, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It produces white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, and its leaves are usually basal, linear, and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
