Astragalus caucasicus var. boissieri

Astragalus caucasicus var. boissieri, commonly known as Caucasian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces lens-shaped pods containing several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
