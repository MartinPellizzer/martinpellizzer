Arenaria pamphylica subsp. pamphylica

Arenaria pamphylica subsp.

pamphylica, commonly known as the Pamphylian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and fleshy, which helps it conserve water.

It is often found in rocky areas and along coastal regions where it withstands harsh environmental conditions.
