Acalypha variabilis var. elliptica

Acalypha variabilis var. elliptica, commonly known as the variable hibiscus or red-veined begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often display vibrant red, orange, or yellow veins against a green background. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
