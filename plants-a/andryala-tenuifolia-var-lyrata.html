Andryala tenuifolia var. lyrata

Andryala tenuifolia var. lyrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved andryala, is a variant of the Andryala tenuifolia species, which is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

This plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often arranged in a lyrate pattern, giving it its specific variety name. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species within its native habitats.
