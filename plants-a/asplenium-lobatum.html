Asplenium lobatum

Asplenium lobatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are wiry and help anchor it to substrates, while its spore-producing structures are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium lobatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
