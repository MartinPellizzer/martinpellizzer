Asterocephalus palaestinus

Asterocephalus palaestinus, commonly known as the Palestine aster, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with a central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Asterocephalus palaestinus is known for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
