Asplenium longicaudatum

Asplenium longicaudatum, commonly known as the long-tailed spleenwort, is a small evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that often have a distinctive elongated tail-like appearance. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are usually dimorphic, with fertile and sterile leaves differing in structure.

Asplenium longicaudatum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
