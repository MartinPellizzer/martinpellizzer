Athyriopsis lobatocrenata

Athyriopsis lobatocrenata, commonly known as the broad-lobed wood fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lanceolate fronds with distinct lobes. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in sori, which are clustered in groups.

Athyriopsis lobatocrenata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
