Arachniodes triangularis

Arachniodes triangularis, commonly known as the three-lobed fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed fronds that give it a unique, almost spider-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually bioluminescent, emitting a faint glow at night.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
