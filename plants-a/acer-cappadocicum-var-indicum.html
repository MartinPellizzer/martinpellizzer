Acer cappadocicum var. indicum

Acer cappadocicum var. indicum, commonly known as the Indian maple, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Due to its unique foliage and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic value.
