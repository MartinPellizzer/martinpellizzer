Agapetes vaccinacea

Agapetes vaccinacea, commonly known as the red-leafed evergreen, is a species of shrub native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its striking red foliage, which becomes more vibrant in the autumn. The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in late spring. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, providing a unique aesthetic appeal.

Agapetes vaccinacea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its decorative foliage and hardy nature.
