Acacia pectinata

Acacia pectinata, commonly known as the white wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Acacia pectinata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system.

It is also valued for its role in ecological systems and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
