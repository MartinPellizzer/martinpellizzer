Aster lindleyanus var. eximius

Aster lindleyanus var. eximius, commonly known as the exalted aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves and showy blue to purple ray flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
