Arabis verdieri

Arabis verdieri, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California.

It typically grows in rocky habitats and alpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Arabis verdieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
