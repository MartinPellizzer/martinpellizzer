Antirrhinum glutinosum var. genuinum

Antirrhinum glutinosum var. genuinum, commonly known as the sticky snapdragon, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features showy, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that contribute to its sticky texture, a characteristic that aids in deterring herbivores. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
