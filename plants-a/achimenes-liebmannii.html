Achimenes liebmannii

Achimenes liebmannii, commonly known as the scarlet bellflower, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting throat. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blooms that are typically 2 to 4 inches long, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements. Its foliage consists of dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy.

Achimenes liebmannii thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often grown as a bedding plant or in containers.
