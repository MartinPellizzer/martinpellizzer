Alectorolophus × fallax

Alectorolophus × fallax, commonly known as the hybrid hibiscus, is a flowering plant in the Malvaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Alectorolophus hirtellus and Alectorolophus rufus, resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parents. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The hybrid is known for its robust growth and ability to adapt to various soil types.
