Asparagus suaveolens

Asparagus suaveolens, commonly known as sweet asparagus or fragrant asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and the production of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible, tender shoots that are often harvested in early spring before the leaves fully develop. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Asparagus suaveolens is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its culinary uses.
