Acalypha brevibracteata

Acalypha brevibracteata, commonly known as the narrow-bracted spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically borne on short peduncles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various parts of the world as a garden plant.
