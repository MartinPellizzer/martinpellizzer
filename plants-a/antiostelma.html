Antiostelma

Antiostelma, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, reflective hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. Native to tropical regions of South America, it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
