Adyseton obcordatum

Adyseton obcordatum, commonly known as the obcordate bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Adyseton obcordatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
