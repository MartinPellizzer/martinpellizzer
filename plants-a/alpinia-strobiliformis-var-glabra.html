Alpinia strobiliformis var. glabra

Alpinia strobiliformis var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect rhizomes and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers followed by elliptical, dark brown berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The smooth ginger is closely related to other ginger species and is valued in traditional medicine for its aromatic and therapeutic uses.
