Anthyllis polycephala

Anthyllis polycephala, commonly known as the woolly broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by pods containing several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Anthyllis polycephala is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy species often found in dry, sunny habitats.
