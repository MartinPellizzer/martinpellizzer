Aster novi-belgii var. laevigatus

Aster novi-belgii var. laevigatus, commonly known as the Smoothleaf New York Aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and hairless. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing vibrant purple to pinkish hues.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
