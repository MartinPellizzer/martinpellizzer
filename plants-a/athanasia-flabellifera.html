Athanasia flabellifera

Athanasia flabellifera, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and red. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the surface of water, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The sacred lotus is also symbolically significant in various cultures, representing purity and spiritual enlightenment.
