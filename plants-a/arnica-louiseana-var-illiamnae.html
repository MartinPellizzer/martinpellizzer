Arnica louiseana var. illiamnae

Arnica louiseana var. illiamnae, commonly known as the Illiamna arnica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central United States.

It features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers with prominent central disks. The plant is adapted to dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure and is often found in disturbed soils.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity.
