Aspilia xylopoda

Aspilia xylopoda, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in dense, spherical clusters. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
