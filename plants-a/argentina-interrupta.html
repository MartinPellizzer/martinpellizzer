Argentina interrupta

Argentina interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted argentine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and shrublands.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
