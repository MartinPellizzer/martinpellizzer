Acrostichum lepidoglossum

Acrostichum lepidoglossum, commonly known as the broad-leafed fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, bipinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to two meters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense, velvety layer of fine hairs.

The fronds are composed of numerous small, lance-shaped pinnae, giving it a lush, feathery appearance.
