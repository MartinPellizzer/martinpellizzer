Allium roseum subsp. permixtum

Allium roseum subsp.

permixtum, commonly known as the pink garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green and emit a mild garlic scent when crushed.

The plant produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and delicate floral display.
