Acer pictum var. truncatum

Acer pictum var. truncatum, commonly known as the dwarf maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are usually red or orange in the fall, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, making it suitable for small gardens or as a specimen plant. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a slight flaking texture.

It prefers partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, thriving in moist, acidic conditions.
