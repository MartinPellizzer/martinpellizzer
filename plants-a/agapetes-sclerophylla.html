Agapetes sclerophylla

Agapetes sclerophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf agapete, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, leathery, silver-gray leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Agapetes sclerophylla is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
