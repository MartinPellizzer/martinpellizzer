Alyssum jonthlaspi

Alyssum jonthlaspi, commonly known as rock allyssum, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Alyssum jonthlaspi is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as bees and butterflies.
