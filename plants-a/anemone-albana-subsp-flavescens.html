Anemone albana subsp. flavescens

Anemone albana subsp.

flavescens, commonly known as the yellow anemone, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture, and it has a fibrous root system.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
