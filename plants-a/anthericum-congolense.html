Anthericum congolense

Anthericum congolense, commonly known as the African arum, is a herbaceous plant native to central and western Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves and a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swampy areas. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common sight in humid tropical regions.

Anthericum congolense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some local cultures.
