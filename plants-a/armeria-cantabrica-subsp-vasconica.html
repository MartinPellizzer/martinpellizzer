Armeria cantabrica subsp. vasconica

Armeria cantabrica subsp.

vasconica, commonly known as the Cantabrian thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the coastal regions of northern Spain. It features dense clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has silver-gray foliage with soft, woolly textures, which help it retain moisture in its arid, coastal habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to maritime gardens.
