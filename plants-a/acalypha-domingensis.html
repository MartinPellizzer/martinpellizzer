Acalypha domingensis

Acalypha domingensis, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, showy red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Acalypha domingensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
