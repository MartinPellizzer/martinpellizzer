Afzelia petersiana

Afzelia petersiana, commonly known as African teak, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellow flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing elongated seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

Afzelia petersiana plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
