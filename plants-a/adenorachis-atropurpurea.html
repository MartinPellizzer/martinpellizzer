Adenorachis atropurpurea

Adenorachis atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered rockcress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Adenorachis atropurpurea is often found in alpine or arctic regions, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.
