Astragalus johannis-howellii

Astragalus johannis-howellii, commonly known as Howell's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It produces small, yellow to brown pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus johannis-howellii is often found in arid environments and is adapted to low-nutrient soils.
