Astragalus glycyphylloides

Astragalus glycyphylloides, commonly known as sweet carline, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is known for its sweet-smelling foliage and is often found in rocky or arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus glycyphylloides is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
