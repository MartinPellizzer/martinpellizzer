Arctium lappa var. majus

Arctium lappa var. majus, commonly known as greater burdock, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces purple to brown flower heads that develop into spiky, bur-like fruits. Its root is thick, fleshy, and often used in traditional medicine.

The species is adapted to grow in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.
