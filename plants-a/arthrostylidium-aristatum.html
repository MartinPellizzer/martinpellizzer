Arthrostylidium aristatum

Arthrostylidium aristatum, commonly known as the feather-tailed orchid, is a small, epiphytic orchid native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It features a compact, pseudobulbous structure with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, which are typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and good drainage for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and conservationists.
