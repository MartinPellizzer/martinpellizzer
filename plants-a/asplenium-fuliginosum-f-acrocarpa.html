Asplenium fuliginosum f. acrocarpa

Asplenium fuliginosum f. acrocarpa, commonly known as the sooty spleenwort, is a fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic fern that typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in a sooty or dark-colored coating, giving it its common name. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

This fern is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its unique appearance and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
