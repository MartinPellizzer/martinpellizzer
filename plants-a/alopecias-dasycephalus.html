Alopecias dasycephalus

Alopecias dasycephalus, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments. Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
