Artemisia commutata var. hookeriana

Artemisia commutata var. hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typically clustered in dense, spherical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Hooker's wormwood is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
