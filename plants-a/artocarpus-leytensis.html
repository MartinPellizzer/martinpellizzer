Artocarpus leytensis

Artocarpus leytensis, commonly known as the Leyte fig, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fruit. The fruit, which is typically green when mature, turns yellow or orange and has a sweet, edible pulp surrounding the seeds. The plant is often found in lowland and montane forests, thriving in humid, warm climates.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for various wildlife species and contributing to forest biodiversity.
