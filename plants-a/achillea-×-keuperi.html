Achillea × keuperi

Achillea × keuperi, commonly known as the German yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green foliage and flat-topped clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in dry meadows and rocky soils. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

Achillea × keuperi is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
