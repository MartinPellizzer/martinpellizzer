Astragalus transhyrcanus

Astragalus transhyrcanus, commonly known as the Trans-Hyrkanian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky areas. It has small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind, and it plays a role in soil stabilization due to its deep root system.

Astragalus transhyrcanus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
