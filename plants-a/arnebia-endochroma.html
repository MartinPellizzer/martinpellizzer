Arnebia endochroma

Arnebia endochroma, commonly known as Indian madder, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of South Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers during the summer months. Its roots are the primary part used, containing compounds like alizarin and purpurin, which have been historically used for dyeing textiles.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clustered inflorescences.
