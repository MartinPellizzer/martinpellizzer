Areca costulata

Areca costulata, commonly known as the fragrant areca or sweet areca, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often arranged in clusters. Areca costulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic beauty.

Its foliage and floral display make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
