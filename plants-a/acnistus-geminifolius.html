Acnistus geminifolius

Acnistus geminifolius, commonly known as the twinleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features palmate leaves that are often divided into three to five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
