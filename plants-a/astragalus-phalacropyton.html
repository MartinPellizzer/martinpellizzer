Astragalus phalacropyton

Astragalus phalacropyton, commonly known as the horned milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus phalacropyton plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
