Allium tenuiflorum subsp. apenninum

Allium tenuiflorum subsp.

apenninum, commonly known as the Italian chive or narrow-leaved garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale purple flowers in clusters.

The plant is characterized by its mild garlic scent and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Allium genus, including garlic and leeks.
