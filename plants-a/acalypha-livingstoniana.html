Acalypha livingstoniana

Acalypha livingstoniana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or redleaf plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, red to purple leaves that are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Acalypha livingstoniana is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in landscape design and as a houseplant.
