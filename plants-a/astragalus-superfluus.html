Astragalus superfluus

Astragalus superfluus, commonly known as the common milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in spikes, and its compound leaves are typically trifoliate. Astragalus superfluus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to nutrient cycling in ecosystems.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
