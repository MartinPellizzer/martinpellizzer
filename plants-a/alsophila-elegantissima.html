Alsophila elegantissima

Alsophila elegantissima, commonly known as the elegant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a fibrous, spongy layer that resembles bark. The plant produces a crown of large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length, giving it a distinctive, elegant appearance. Its reproductive structures include spore-bearing cones that are typically found at the base of the fronds.

Alsophila elegantissima is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking visual appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
