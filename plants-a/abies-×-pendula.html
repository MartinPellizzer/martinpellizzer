Abies × pendula

Abies × pendula, commonly known as the Serbian cedar or the Norway-Furcata fir, is a hybrid coniferous tree resulting from the cross between Abies alba and Abies fabri.

It is characterized by its distinctive pendulous branches that create a weeping appearance, giving it a unique and ornamental look. The tree has needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly twisted, with a dense, compact growth habit. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.

The cones are small and cylindrical, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.
