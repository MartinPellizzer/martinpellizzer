Aralia trigyna

Aralia trigyna, commonly known as American giant aralia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which can reach up to 3 feet in length, composed of numerous narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clustered umbels, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. Its root system is extensive, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Aralia trigyna is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
