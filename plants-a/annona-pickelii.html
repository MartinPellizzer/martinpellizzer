Annona pickelii

Annona pickelii, commonly known as the Pickel's custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces a large, spiny fruit that resembles a custard apple, with a thick, yellowish skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. The fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, and the plant is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
