Achillea eriophora

Achillea eriophora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, linear leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

Achillea eriophora is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
