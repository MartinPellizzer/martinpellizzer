Abutilon rosiflorum

Abutilon rosiflorum, commonly known as the roseflower abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, rose-like flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in the spring and summer. Its botanical characteristics include stamens that are longer than the petals, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
