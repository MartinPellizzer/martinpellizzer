Aristida minutiflora

Aristida minutiflora, commonly known as the small-flowered dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds ornamental value to landscapes.

Aristida minutiflora is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
