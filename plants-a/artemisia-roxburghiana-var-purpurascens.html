Artemisia roxburghiana var. purpurascens

Artemisia roxburghiana var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple feverfew, is a perennial shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its strong, medicinal scent.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional use in herbal medicine.
