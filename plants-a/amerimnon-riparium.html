Amerimnon riparium

Amerimnon riparium, commonly known as the riverbank beauty, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands and riverbanks of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that often grow in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

Amerimnon riparium thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often used in aquatic gardens or as a decorative plant in water features.
