Acacia aspera var. densifolia

Acacia aspera var. densifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage, which is often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which appear during the warmer months. Its woody, thorned stems are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

This species is valued for its hardiness and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.
