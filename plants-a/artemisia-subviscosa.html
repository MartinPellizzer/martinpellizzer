Artemisia subviscosa

Artemisia subviscosa, commonly known as rough tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments. Its leaves are covered in fine, glandular hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture and scent.

Artemisia subviscosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
