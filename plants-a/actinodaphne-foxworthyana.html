Actinodaphne foxworthyana

Actinodaphne foxworthyana, commonly known as the Foxworthy's camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of Borneo.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue berries. Its leaves are typically thick, leathery, and have a distinct, spicy scent.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
