Arillastrum gummiferum

Arillastrum gummiferum, commonly known as the gum tree of the genus Arillastrum, is a shrub or small tree native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its most notable feature is the production of a sticky, resinous gum, which has been used traditionally by indigenous peoples.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique chemical composition and potential medicinal properties.
