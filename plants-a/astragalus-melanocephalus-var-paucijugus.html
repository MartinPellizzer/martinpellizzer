Astragalus melanocephalus var. paucijugus

Astragalus melanocephalus var. paucijugus, commonly known as the black-headed milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions, showcasing a deep root system for water absorption. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is distinguished by its reduced number of leaflets compared to the typical form of Astragalus melanocephalus.
