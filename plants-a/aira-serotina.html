Aira serotina

Aira serotina, commonly known as false wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and prairies.

Aira serotina is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
