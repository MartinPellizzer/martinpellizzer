Abutilon microphyllum

Abutilon microphyllum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its fibrous roots and rapid growth make it a common weed in agricultural areas.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.
