Athyrium ambiguum

Athyrium ambiguum, commonly known as the forked wood fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often divided into narrow segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of small, spore-bearing leaves.

Athyrium ambiguum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various garden conditions.
