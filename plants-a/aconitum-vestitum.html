Aconitum vestitum

Aconitum vestitum, commonly known as the dressed monk's hood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, conical inflorescences. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed leaflets that are often covered in a fine, silky down.

Aconitum vestitum is adapted to cold, alpine environments and is often found in rocky or meadow habitats at high elevations.
