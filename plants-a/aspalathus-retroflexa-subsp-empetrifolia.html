Aspalathus retroflexa subsp. empetrifolia

Aspalathus retroflexa subsp.

empetrifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient plant in its native habitat.
