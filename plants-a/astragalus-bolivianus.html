Astragalus bolivianus

Astragalus bolivianus, commonly known as the Bolivian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to dry conditions.

Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand periods of drought.
