Arenaria stephaniana

Arenaria stephaniana, commonly known as the stonecress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage, often found in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually fleshy and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
