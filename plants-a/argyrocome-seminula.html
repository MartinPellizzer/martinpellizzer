Argyrocome seminula

Argyrocome seminula, commonly known as the silverleaf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces tiny, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense groups. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Argyrocome seminula plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for native wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of its host trees.
