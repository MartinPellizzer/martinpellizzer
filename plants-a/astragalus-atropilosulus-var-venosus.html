Astragalus atropilosulus var. venosus

Astragalus atropilosulus var. venosus, commonly known as the veiny milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically followed by narrow, pod-like fruits. It is adapted to dry environments and has a deep root system to access groundwater.

This species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing sandy soils and its potential for medicinal use.
