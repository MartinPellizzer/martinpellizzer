Ammannia baccifera subsp. intermedia

Ammannia baccifera subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the redroot ammannia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Americas. It is characterized by its striking red stems and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink in color.

The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as wetlands and streambanks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a reddish or purple hue, adding to its ornamental appeal.

The subspecies intermedia is distinguished by its slightly different floral structure and growth habits compared to the main species.
