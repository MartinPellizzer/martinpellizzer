Aconitum hemsleyanum var. leucanthum

Aconitum hemsleyanum var. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers with white petals, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply cut lobes that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
