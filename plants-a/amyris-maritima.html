Amyris maritima

Amyris maritima, commonly known as the sea bean or coastal bean, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round, brown pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Amyris maritima thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
