Asterandra sellowiana

Asterandra sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian aster, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The showy inflorescence consists of yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale purple ray florets.
