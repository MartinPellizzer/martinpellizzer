Acaena argentea var. breviscapa

Acaena argentea var. breviscapa, commonly known as silver burr or silver weed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features dense clusters of small, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers followed by spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in areas outside its native range.
