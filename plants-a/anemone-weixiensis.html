Anemone weixiensis

Anemone weixiensis, commonly known as the Weixi anemone, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has palmately divided leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been the subject of botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
