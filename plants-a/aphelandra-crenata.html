Aphelandra crenata

Aphelandra crenata, commonly known as the silver velvet plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Aroid family (Araceae) and is characterized by its striking, waxy, dark green leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces dramatic, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, often in shades of red or orange. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and humidity to flourish.

Aphelandra crenata is popular as a decorative indoor plant due to its ornamental foliage and unique floral display.
