Artocarpus parvus

Artocarpus parvus, commonly known as the small breadfruit, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces a unique fruit with a rough, fibrous outer husk and a soft, edible pulp containing numerous seeds. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface. The plant thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

It is valued for its fruit, which is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
