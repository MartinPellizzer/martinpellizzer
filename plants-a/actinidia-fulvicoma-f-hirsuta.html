Actinidia fulvicoma f. hirsuta

Actinidia fulvicoma f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy Chinese gooseberry, is a variant of the Actinidia genus native to China.

This plant is characterized by its hairy foliage and stems, which distinguish it from other forms of Actinidia. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that are typically harvested in the fall. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and conservation efforts.
