Apocynum × denticulatum

Apocynum × denticulatum, commonly known as the Indian hemp or dogbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, clustered in umbels. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It is often found in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woodlands.

Apocynum × denticulatum is also known for its fibrous roots, which have been historically used for textile production.
