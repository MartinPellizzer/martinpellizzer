Armatocereus mataranus subsp. ancashensis

Armatocereus mataranus subsp.

ancashensis, commonly known as the Ashaninka cactus, is a columnar cactus native to the Andean regions of Peru. It features a tall, cylindrical stem with a ribbed structure and spines that are typically white to yellow in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is noted for its resilience and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.
