Arthrochilium royleanum

Arthrochilium royleanum, commonly known as the Kashmir orchid, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the Himalayan regions of India, Pakistan, and Afghanistan, where it thrives in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the rocky substrates it inhabits.

Arthrochilium royleanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral characteristics.
