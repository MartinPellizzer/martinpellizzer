Allium amplectens

Allium amplectens, commonly known as the embracing onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features grass-like basal leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring. The plant has a strong, pungent odor similar to other allium species. It is often found growing in rocky or dry soils, and it is used in some traditional herbal remedies.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Amaryllidaceae family.
