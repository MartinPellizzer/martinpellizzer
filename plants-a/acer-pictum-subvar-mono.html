Acer pictum subvar. mono

Acer pictum subvar. mono, commonly known as the single-flowered maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are typically red or orange in the fall, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in the spring, which are usually white or pale green. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often developing shallow fissures with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal foliage and unique floral display.
