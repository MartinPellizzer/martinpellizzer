Asperula paniculata

Asperula paniculata, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a flowering plant native to Japan and Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring, which are borne on long, drooping panicles. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a pointed tip.

Asperula paniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to moist, well-drained soils.
