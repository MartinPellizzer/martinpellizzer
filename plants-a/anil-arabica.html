Anil arabica

Anil arabica, commonly known as coffee arabica, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to tropical highlands of Africa, the Arabian Peninsula, and parts of southern Asia. The plant produces red, spherical berries that contain two seeds, which are the coffee beans used in brewing. These beans are prized for their aromatic and complex flavor profiles.

Anil arabica thrives in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil and a consistent climate.
