Acacia concurrens

Acacia concurrens, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its pendulous, feathery foliage that gives it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Acacia concurrens is valued for its drought tolerance and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
