Asphodelus albus var. grandiflorus

Asphodelus albus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the white asphodel, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, branched stems with large, showy white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
