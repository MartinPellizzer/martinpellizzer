Alsophila elegans var. crenato-serrata

Alsophila elegans var. crenato-serrata, commonly known as the serrated tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The fronds are typically dark green and have a serrated edge, which contributes to its common name. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various small organisms.
