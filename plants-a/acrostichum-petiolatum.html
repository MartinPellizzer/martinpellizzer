Acrostichum petiolatum

Acrostichum petiolatum, commonly known as the broadleaf fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Davalliaceae family and is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive appearance that makes it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth.
