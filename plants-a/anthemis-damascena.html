Anthemis damascena

Anthemis damascena, commonly known as the damask plant or sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are aromatic and often used in herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers are also used in perfumery due to their pleasant, honey-like scent.
