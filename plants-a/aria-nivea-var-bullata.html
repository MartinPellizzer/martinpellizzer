Aria nivea var. bullata

Aria nivea var. bullata, commonly known as the Bullate Snowbell, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, inflated, balloon-like flowers that are pale green to white in color. The plant produces clusters of these specialized floral structures, which are adapted for wind pollination. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unusual and eye-catching blooms.
