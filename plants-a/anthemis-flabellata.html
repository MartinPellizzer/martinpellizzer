Anthemis flabellata

Anthemis flabellata, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
