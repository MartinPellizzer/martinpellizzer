Acacia loderi

Acacia loderi, commonly known as the Loder's wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, feathery leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are arranged in compact, cylindrical clusters. Acacia loderi is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soils in dry environments.
