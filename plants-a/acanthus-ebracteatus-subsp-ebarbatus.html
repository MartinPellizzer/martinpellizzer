Acanthus ebracteatus subsp. ebarbatus

Acanthus ebracteatus subsp.

ebarbatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acanthus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features lanceolate, often hairy leaves that are typically shorter than those of the broader-leaved varieties.

The plant produces spiny, spire-like inflorescences that bear small, pale yellow to green flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its architectural form and long-lasting foliage.
