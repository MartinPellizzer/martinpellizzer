Aristolochia angustifolia var. brevifolia

Aristolochia angustifolia var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to parts of Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, often hairy leaves that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant produces distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are usually dark purple or brown with a strong, aromatic odor. Its fruit is a slender, elongated capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral characteristics.
