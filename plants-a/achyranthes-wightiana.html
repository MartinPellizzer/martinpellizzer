Achyranthes wightiana

Achyranthes wightiana, commonly known as the Indian knotgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which are often clustered at the tips of the branches. Its root system is fibrous and can reach considerable depth, allowing it to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Achyranthes wightiana is also noted for its medicinal properties, used in traditional systems of medicine for its purported health benefits.
