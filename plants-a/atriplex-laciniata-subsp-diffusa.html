Atriplex laciniata subsp. diffusa

Atriplex laciniata subsp.

diffusa, commonly known as the broadleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments.

The plant produces small greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in saline soils and is often found in coastal and inland saline habitats.

The species is valued for its ability to stabilize soils and provide forage for livestock.
