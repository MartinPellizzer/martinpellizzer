Aster persoonioides

Aster persoonioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

The flowers are blue to purple in color, and they bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
