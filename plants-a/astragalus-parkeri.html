Astragalus parkeri

Astragalus parkeri, commonly known as the Parker's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in drought conditions.

Astragalus parkeri is also known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
