Agave macroacantha

Agave macroacantha, commonly known as the giant red agave, is a striking succulent native to Mexico.

It features a tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 10 feet in height, adorned with long, narrow, blue-green leaves that have red tips. The plant produces large, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid climates.

Its unique appearance and architectural form make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardens.
