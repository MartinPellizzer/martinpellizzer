Adesmia eremophila

Adesmia eremophila, commonly known as the desert legume, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow or pale pink flowers. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through deep root systems and the ability to fix nitrogen, enhancing soil fertility. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often dispersed by wind or animals.

Adesmia eremophila plays a vital role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
