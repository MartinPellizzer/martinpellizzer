Ataccia laevis

Ataccia laevis, commonly known as the smooth ataccia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its blooming period occurs during the spring and early summer months.

Ataccia laevis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
