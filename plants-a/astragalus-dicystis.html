Astragalus dicystis

Astragalus dicystis, commonly known as the two-cystic milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions, showcasing its resilience in harsh environments.

Astragalus dicystis is also notable for its seed pods, which contain two distinct chambers, a feature that distinguishes it from other species in the genus.
