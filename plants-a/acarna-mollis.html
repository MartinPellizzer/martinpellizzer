Acarna mollis

Acarna mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf morning glory, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, round seeds.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
