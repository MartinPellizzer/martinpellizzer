Astragalus lagurus var. brachypodus

Astragalus lagurus var. brachypodus, commonly known as the shortpod milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, inflated pods are a distinctive feature, containing several seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
