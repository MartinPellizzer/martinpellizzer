Atriplex dimorphostegia var. sagittiformis

Atriplex dimorphostegia var. sagittiformis, commonly known as the arrowleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves, which are typically blue-green in color and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable species for stabilizing degraded lands. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, and it is often used in revegetation projects due to its hardiness.

The species is also known for its ability to tolerate extreme drought conditions.
