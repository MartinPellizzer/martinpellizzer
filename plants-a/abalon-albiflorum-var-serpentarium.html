Abalon albiflorum var. serpentarium

Abalon albiflorum var. serpentarium, commonly known as the serpent's tail.

This plant is characterized by its striking, cascading clusters of white flowers that resemble the coiled tail of a snake, giving it its common name. It is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a lush backdrop to the showy blooms.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
