Azalea scabra

Azalea scabra, commonly known as the rough azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and red, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Azalea scabra is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
