Ammannia cymosa

Ammannia cymosa, commonly known as the redroot ammannia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that often turn reddish in color as the plant matures. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Ammannia cymosa is typically found in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and in wetlands.
