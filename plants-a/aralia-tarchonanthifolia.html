Aralia tarchonanthifolia

Aralia tarchonanthifolia, commonly known as the Siberian aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of Eastern Europe and Siberia.

It features compound leaves that are typically pinnately arranged, with a distinctive appearance that resembles the foliage of other aralia species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbel-like clusters, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cool climate to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
