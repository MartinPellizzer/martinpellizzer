Artanthe sororia

Artanthe sororia, commonly known as the silverleaf artanthe, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Artanthe sororia is valued for its ornamental appeal and low water requirements.
