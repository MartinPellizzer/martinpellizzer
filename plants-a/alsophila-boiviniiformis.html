Alsophila boiviniiformis

Alsophila boiviniiformis, commonly known as the Boivin's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, compound fronds. The plant has a distinctive crown of feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The fronds are used in traditional crafts, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appearance.
