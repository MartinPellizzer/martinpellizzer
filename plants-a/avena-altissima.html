Avena altissima

Avena altissima, commonly known as the tall oat, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Avena altissima is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open fields, where it thrives in full sun.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it a valuable plant for erosion control.
