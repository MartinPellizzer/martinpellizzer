Anisomeles salviifolia var. moschata

Anisomeles salviifolia var. moschata, commonly known as the fragrant sage plant, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale purple, clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and aromatic properties.

The variety *moschata* is distinguished by its more intense fragrance compared to other varieties of the species.
