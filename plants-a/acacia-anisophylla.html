Acacia anisophylla

Acacia anisophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are narrow and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Acacia anisophylla is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
