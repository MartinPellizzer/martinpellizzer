Aster novi-belgii var. floribundus

Aster novi-belgii var. floribundus, commonly known as the New York aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple to pink petals and a yellow center, blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped, gray-green leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for garden landscapes and wildflower gardens.
