Artemisia laciniata subsp. intricata

Artemisia laciniata subsp.

intricata, commonly known as the intricate tarragon, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant.
