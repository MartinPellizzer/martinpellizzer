Anthistiria subsericans

Anthistiria subsericans, commonly known as the narrow-leaved anthistiria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in compact clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
