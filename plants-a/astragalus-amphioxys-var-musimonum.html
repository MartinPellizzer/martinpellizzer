Astragalus amphioxys var. musimonum

Astragalus amphioxys var. musimonum, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to thrive in poor soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
