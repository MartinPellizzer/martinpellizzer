Angelica piperi

Angelica piperi, commonly known as the American angelica, is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, umbrella-like compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clustered umbels, which eventually develop into oval, yellowish-brown fruits. Angelica piperi is known for its aromatic, pungent odor and has been used historically for its medicinal and culinary properties.
