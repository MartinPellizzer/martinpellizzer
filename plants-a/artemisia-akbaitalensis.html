Artemisia akbaitalensis

Artemisia akbaitalensis, commonly known as the Aksu Artemisia, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which helps it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert habitats.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
