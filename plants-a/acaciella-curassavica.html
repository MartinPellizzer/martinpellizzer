Acaciella curassavica

Acaciella curassavica, commonly known as the Mexican acacia, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically small and delicate. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom during the dry season. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acaciella curassavica is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
