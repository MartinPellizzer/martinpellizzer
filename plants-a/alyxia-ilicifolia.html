Alyxia ilicifolia

Alyxia ilicifolia, commonly known as the coastal holly or beach holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or limestone soils along the coastlines. The plant features small, holly-like leaves that are glossy and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow and are clustered in small, terminal inflorescences.

Alyxia ilicifolia is adapted to salt spray and is often used in coastal landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
