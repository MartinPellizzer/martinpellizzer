Araujia calycina

Araujia calycina, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and emit a sweet fragrance. The flowers are typically borne in clusters and have five broad, waxy petals. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a reddish color.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
