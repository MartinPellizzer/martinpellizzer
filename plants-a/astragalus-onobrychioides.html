Astragalus onobrychioides

Astragalus onobrychioides, commonly known as the yellow burclover, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

Astragalus onobrychioides plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
