Andropogon saccharoides subsp. brasiliensis

Andropogon saccharoides subsp.

brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian sugar cane grass, is a perennial grass native to South America. It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy.

This species is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils. It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes many economically important grasses.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
