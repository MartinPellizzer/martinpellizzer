Avena condensata

Avena condensata, commonly known as condensed oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in chaparral and woodland areas.

Avena condensata is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
