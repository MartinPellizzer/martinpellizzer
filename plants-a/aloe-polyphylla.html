Aloe polyphylla

Aloe polyphylla, commonly known as the "Rainbow Aloe," is a striking succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, spiral arrangement of fleshy, leaf-like structures that form a rosette pattern. The plant's leaves are typically blue-green in color and have a waxy surface, which helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat. Aloe polyphylla produces small, yellowish flowers on tall, branching stems during the summer months.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is highly valued in horticulture and is often cultivated in controlled environments.
