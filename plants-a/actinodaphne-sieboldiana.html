Actinodaphne sieboldiana

Actinodaphne sieboldiana, commonly known as the Japanese camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically followed by dark purple, fleshy berries. Its wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and durability.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal uses.
