Actinea bigelovii

Actinea bigelovii, commonly known as the Bigelow's actinia, is a species of sea anemone found in the northeastern Pacific Ocean.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent tentacles and a soft, gelatinous body. This organism is typically found attached to rocks or other substrates in shallow coastal waters. Actinea bigelovii plays a role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various small invertebrates.

Its reproductive strategy involves both sexual and asexual methods, contributing to its population dynamics in its environment.
