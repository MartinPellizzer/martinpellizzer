Adenophora taurica

Adenophora taurica, commonly known as the Crimean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. It blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Adenophora taurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
