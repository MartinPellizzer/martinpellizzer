Acacia leptophylla var. brevifolia

Acacia leptophylla var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and inland regions. It is a member of the legume family, capable of nitrogen fixation, which benefits soil fertility.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for native pollinators and habitat for small animals.
