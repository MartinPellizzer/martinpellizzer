Angelica tianmuensis

Angelica tianmuensis, commonly known as the Tianmu angelica, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of Zhejiang Province in China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and compound umbels. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and rocky slopes. Its roots are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
