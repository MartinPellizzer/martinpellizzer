Adesmia arillata

Adesmia arillata, commonly known as the silky burr, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by hairy, woody pods that contain the seeds. Adesmia arillata is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
