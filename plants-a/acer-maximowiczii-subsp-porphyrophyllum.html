Acer maximowiczii subsp. porphyrophyllum

Acer maximowiczii subsp.

porphyrophyllum, commonly known as the red-flowered maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its striking crimson to orange-red flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a serrated margin and a deep green upper surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant spring display.
