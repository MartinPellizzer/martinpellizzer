Acaena sanguisorbae var. involucrata

Acaena sanguisorbae var. involucrata, commonly known as the purple burr plant, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny fruiting structures that contain small, edible seeds. The plant produces bright red to purple flowers in late summer, which are followed by the distinctive burrs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
