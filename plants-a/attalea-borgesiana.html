Attalea borgesiana

Attalea borgesiana, commonly known as the Paraguayan palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly found in Paraguay and Brazil.

It is a medium-sized tree that typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 meters, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, and its fruits are drupes that contain a single seed.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
