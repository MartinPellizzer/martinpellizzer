Aira cespitosa subsp. media

Aira cespitosa subsp.

media, commonly known as blue grama, is a perennial grass native to North America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils.

Its drought tolerance and ability to form dense stands make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
