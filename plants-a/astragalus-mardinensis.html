Astragalus mardinensis

Astragalus mardinensis, commonly known as Mardin milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its roots are known to have medicinal properties and are used in traditional herbal remedies.

Astragalus mardinensis plays a role in local ecosystems by contributing to soil stabilization and providing nectar for pollinators.
