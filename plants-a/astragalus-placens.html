Astragalus placens

Astragalus placens, commonly known as the plains milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. Astragalus placens is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
