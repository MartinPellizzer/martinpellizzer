Allium microspathum

Allium microspathum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic or mountain garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered bulbs and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces umbels of white or pale purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its foliage and bulbs are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Allium microspathum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
