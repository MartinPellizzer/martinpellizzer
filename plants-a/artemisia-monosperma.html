Artemisia monosperma

Artemisia monosperma, commonly known as the African mugwort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. Artemisia monosperma thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in various herbal remedies.
