Allocarya elachnantha

Allocarya elachnantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Allocarya elachnantha is often found in alpine meadows and is an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
