Acanthodium glabrum

Acanthodium glabrum, commonly known as the smooth acanthodium, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with yellow disc flowers and white to pale pink ray florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is native to parts of southern Africa. It has hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
