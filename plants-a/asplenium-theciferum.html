Asplenium theciferum

Asplenium theciferum, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and is native to regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant typically grows in rocky or crevice habitats, demonstrating a preference for well-drained, acidic soils. Its fronds are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a dark green coloration that remains throughout the year.

Asplenium theciferum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
