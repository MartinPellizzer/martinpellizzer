Acacia reclinata

Acacia reclinata, commonly known as the umbrella acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like crown of feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular inflorescences, which are followed by woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and rocky areas.

Acacia reclinata is well adapted to drought and can fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in degraded soils.

Acacia reclinata is primarily native to the dry savannas and grasslands of sub-Saharan Africa.

Acacia reclinata is also known for its ability to withstand drought conditions.
