Aristida condensata var. combsii

Aristida condensata var. combsii, commonly known as the narrowleaf dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear, with a bluish-green coloration. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, fluffy seeds, which are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.

Its low-growing habit and ornamental appearance make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
