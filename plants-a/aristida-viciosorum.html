Aristida viciosorum

Aristida viciosorum, commonly known as the purple grass or vicia grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with purple or pink hues. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
