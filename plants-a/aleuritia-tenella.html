Aleuritia tenella

Aleuritia tenella, commonly known as the Chinese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has elliptical leaves with serrated edges and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Aleuritia tenella is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators and provide food for birds.
