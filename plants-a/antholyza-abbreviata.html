Antholyza abbreviata

Antholyza abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf antholyza, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has compact, upright growth with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive blue or purple coloration.

Antholyza abbreviata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a succulent plant.
