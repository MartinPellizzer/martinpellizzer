Astragalus leptocephalus

Astragalus leptocephalus, commonly known as narrow-headed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are bipinnate, and it produces small, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus leptocephalus is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland ecosystems.
