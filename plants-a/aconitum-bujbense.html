Aconitum bujbense

Aconitum bujbense, commonly known as the Bujbent monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple hood-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately compound, with finely divided lobes, and it has a stout, hairy stem.

Aconitum bujbense contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.

Morphology

Aconitum bujbense has leaves that are compound, palmately arranged, with large, deeply lobed blades that are typically blue-green in color and can reach up to 20 cm in length.

The stem is upright, hairy, and has a dark brown bark that becomes fissured with age. Its flowers are showy, blue to purple in color, with five petal-like sepals and numerous stamens, blooming in summer and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a follicle that releases seeds dispersed by wind.
