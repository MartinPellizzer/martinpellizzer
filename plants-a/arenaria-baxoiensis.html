Arenaria baxoiensis

Arenaria baxoiensis, commonly known as the Baxoi sandflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy and rocky soils, often found in steppe and desert environments. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry conditions.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to extreme climates and its role in stabilizing sandy soils.
