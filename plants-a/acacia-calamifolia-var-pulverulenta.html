Acacia calamifolia var. pulverulenta

Acacia calamifolia var. pulverulenta, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, almost silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

The variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
