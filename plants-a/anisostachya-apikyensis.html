Anisostachya apikyensis

Anisostachya apikyensis, commonly known as the Eastern African grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and along riverbanks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typical of the grass family.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
