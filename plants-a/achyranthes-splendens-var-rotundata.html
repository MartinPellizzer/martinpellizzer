Achyranthes splendens var. rotundata

Achyranthes splendens var. rotundata, commonly known as the redroot false brome, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish at the base, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
