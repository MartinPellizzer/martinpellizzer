Armeria maritima var. langeana

Armeria maritima var. langeana, commonly known as the Langeana thrift, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It features dense clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and coastal gardening.
