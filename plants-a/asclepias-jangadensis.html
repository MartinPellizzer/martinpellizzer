Asclepias jangadensis

Asclepias jangadensis, commonly known as the Jangad milkweed, is a species of milkweed native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, each with a tuft of silky hair for wind dispersal. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture.

Asclepias jangadensis plays a role in supporting local ecosystems by providing nectar and habitat for various insects.
