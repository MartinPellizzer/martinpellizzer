Arcytophyllum nodosum

Arcytophyllum nodosum, commonly known as the nodular sage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, pinkish or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
