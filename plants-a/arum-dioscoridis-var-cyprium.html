Arum dioscoridis var. cyprium

Arum dioscoridis var. cyprium, commonly known as the Cyprus arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive arrowhead pattern, and its flowers are typically white or pale green, emerging from a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate wet conditions, making it a valuable addition to aquatic or bog gardens.
