Acacia leioderma

Acacia leioderma, commonly known as the smooth acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thornless foliage and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. Acacia leioderma is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

Its wood is hard and durable, historically used for construction and tool-making by indigenous communities.
