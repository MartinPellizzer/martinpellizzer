Anemone howellii

Anemone howellii, commonly known as Howell's anemone, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features showy, white to pinkish flowers with five to seven petals that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps retain moisture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
