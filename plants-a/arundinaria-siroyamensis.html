Arundinaria siroyamensis

Arundinaria siroyamensis, commonly known as the Siroyam bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green in color and arranged in dense clusters. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
