Agrostis gymnostyla

Agrostis gymnostyla, commonly known as the narrowleaf bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, lawns, and along streambanks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 10 millimeters wide, giving it a delicate appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-rowed spikelets.

Agrostis gymnostyla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass management.
