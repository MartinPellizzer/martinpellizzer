Aloysia gratissima var. chacoensis

Aloysia gratissima var. chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco cat's claw, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina, Bolivia, and Paraguay.

It belongs to the verbena family (Verbenaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves and clusters of tubular flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
