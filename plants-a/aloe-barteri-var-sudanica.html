Aloe barteri var. sudanica

Aloe barteri var. sudanica, commonly known as the Sudanese aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Sudan and Eritrea.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow or orange flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is sometimes used for its soothing properties.

This variety is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water to thrive.
