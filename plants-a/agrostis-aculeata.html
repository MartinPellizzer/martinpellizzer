Agrostis aculeata

Agrostis aculeata, commonly known as goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Agrostis aculeata is adapted to temperate climates and is sometimes considered a weed in cultivated areas.
