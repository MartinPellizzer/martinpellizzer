Andropogon speciosus

Andropogon speciosus, commonly known as purpletop grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It features striking purple flower plumes that appear in late summer, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in clumps, often reaching heights of up to six feet. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for native plant gardens.

Its ecological value includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
