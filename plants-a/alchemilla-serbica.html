Alchemilla serbica

Alchemilla serbica, commonly known as Serbian lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla serbica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
