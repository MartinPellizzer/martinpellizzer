Acanthus ilicifolius var. ebracteatus

Acanthus ilicifolius var. ebracteatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces showy, spiny bracts that resemble small leaves, which are often mistaken for actual flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its distinctive foliage and unique floral structures.
