Acer amoenum f. latialatum

Acer amoenum f. latialatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maple, is a striking ornamental tree characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn.

Native to China, this variety is prized for its showy, clusters of small yellow flowers that appear before the leaves in early spring. The tree typically grows to a height of 10-15 meters, with a rounded crown and smooth, grey bark. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its seasonal color changes and unique foliage.
