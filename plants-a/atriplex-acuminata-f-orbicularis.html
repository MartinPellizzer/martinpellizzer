Atriplex acuminata f. orbicularis

Atriplex acuminata f. orbicularis, commonly known as the roundleaf saltbush, is a variant of the saltbush species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its orbicular, or nearly round, leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters, typical of the Amaranthaceae family. It has a robust root system that enables it to thrive in saline and nutrient-poor soils.

This subspecies is often used in revegetation projects due to its ecological resilience and ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
