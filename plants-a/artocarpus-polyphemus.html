Artocarpus polyphemus

Artocarpus polyphemus, commonly known as the giant breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces a unique fruit with a rough, hairy exterior. The fruit contains a soft, starchy pulp surrounded by a fibrous husk. It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and has cultural significance in various regions.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and thrives in humid, tropical climates.
