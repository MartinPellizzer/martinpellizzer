Apium graveolens f. rapaceum

Apium graveolens f. rapaceum, commonly known as the wild celery or wild parsley, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in umbrella-like clusters. The plant is often found growing in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in fields, thriving in temperate climates. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The plant has a long history of use in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
