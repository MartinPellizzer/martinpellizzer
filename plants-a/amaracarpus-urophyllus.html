Amaracarpus urophyllus

Amaracarpus urophyllus, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display during the flowering season. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and light gray, often peeling in thin layers.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and urban landscapes for its aesthetic beauty.
