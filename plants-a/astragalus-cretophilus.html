Astragalus cretophilus

Astragalus cretophilus, commonly known as the crevice astragalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as sagebrush steppe. Its leaves are alternate, compound, and have a distinctive hairy texture.

Astragalus cretophilus is a member of the legume family (Fabaceae) and possesses the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.
