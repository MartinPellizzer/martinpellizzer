Anisacantha lanicuspis

Anisacantha lanicuspis, commonly known as the hairy spiny hibiscus, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems covered in stiff, bristly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Anisacantha lanicuspis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
