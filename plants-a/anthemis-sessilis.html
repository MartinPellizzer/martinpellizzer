Anthemis sessilis

Anthemis sessilis, commonly known as the sessile everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are tightly packed into a single head, resembling a miniature sun. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Anthemis sessilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
