Alternanthera suessenguthii

Alternanthera suessenguthii, commonly known as the velvetleaf Alternanthera, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or purple foliage that intensifies under strong light conditions. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, making it a popular choice for water gardens and pond landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom with small, inconspicuous yellow flowers.
