Astragalus pseudonobrychis

Astragalus pseudonobrychis, commonly known as the false noble clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus pseudonobrychis is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a forage plant for livestock.
