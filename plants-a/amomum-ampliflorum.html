Amomum ampliflorum

Amomum ampliflorum, commonly known as the broad-flowered cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers arranged in branched clusters. Its rhizomes, which are the primary part used, have a strong aroma and are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Amomum ampliflorum is valued for its medicinal properties and is cultivated for both commercial and culinary purposes.
