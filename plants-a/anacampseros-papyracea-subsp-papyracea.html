Anacampseros papyracea subsp. papyracea

Anacampseros papyracea subsp.

papyracea, commonly known as the paperleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
