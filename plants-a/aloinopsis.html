Aloinopsis

Aloinopsis, commonly known as the "Cape violet," is a genus of succulent plants native to South Africa.

These plants are characterized by their fleshy, spiny leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that range from purple to pink. They thrive in arid environments and require well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Aloinopsis species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and unique appearance.

They are also adapted to store water in their leaves, making them resilient to drought conditions.
