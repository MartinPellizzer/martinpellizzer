Anthoxanthum khasianum

Anthoxanthum khasianum, commonly known as the Khasi grass, is a perennial grass species native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces inflorescences that are typically spike-like, with small, pale yellow to brownish florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
