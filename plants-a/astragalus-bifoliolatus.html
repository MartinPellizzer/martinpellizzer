Astragalus bifoliolatus

Astragalus bifoliolatus, commonly known as the two-leafed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. The plant features compound leaves with two small leaflets, giving it its specific epithet "bifoliolatus.". Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early summer.

Astragalus bifoliolatus is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
