Aechmea fulgofascicata

Aechmea fulgofascicata, commonly known as the fiery fasciated aechmea, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful rosette of sword-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in succession. Its foliage is often marked with striking yellow and red patterns, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Aechmea fulgofascicata thrives in humid, well-lit environments and is popular as a decorative houseplant.

Morphology

Aechmea fulgofascicata has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, sword-like, and arranged in a tight circle around the central stem, with a waxy, bluish-green color and a prominent midrib.

The stem is short and stout, with a smooth, grayish-brown bark that grows upright from the base. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences with clustered, red-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn, and are pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a fleshy, yellowish berry that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by birds and mammals eating the fruit.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in rocky or sandy soil.
