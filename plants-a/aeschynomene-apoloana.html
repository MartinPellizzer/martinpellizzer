Aeschynomene apoloana

Aeschynomene apoloana, commonly known as the African horsebean, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by cylindrical, pod-like fruits. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.
