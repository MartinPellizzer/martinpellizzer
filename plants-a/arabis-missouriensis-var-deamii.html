Arabis missouriensis var. deamii

Arabis missouriensis var. deamii, commonly known as the Missouri rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white flowers with four petals. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

The species is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
