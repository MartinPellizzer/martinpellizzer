Azukia umbellata

Azukia umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbellate clusters, giving it its common name. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Azukia umbellata is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
