Australopyrum pectinatum subsp. retrofractum

Australopyrum pectinatum subsp.

retrofractum, commonly known as the Tasmanian serrated sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Tasmania. It typically grows in wetland and swampy habitats, often forming dense stands in moist soils.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive serrated edge along the margins. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked spikelets, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
