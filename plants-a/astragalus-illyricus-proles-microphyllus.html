Astragalus illyricus proles microphyllus

Astragalus illyricus proles microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its root system is extensive, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
