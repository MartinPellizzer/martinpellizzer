Athyrium × subcrassipes

Athyrium × subcrassipes, commonly known as the Japanese painted fern, is a hybrid fern species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and gold. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodland floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of its fronds, and it reproduces through spore dispersal rather than seeds.

The botanical features of Athyrium × subcrassipes make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and indoor settings.
