Amphilophis emersa

Amphilophis emersa, commonly known as the water fern, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are usually divided into small, lobed segments. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense mats on the water surface. Its rhizomes help anchor it to the substrate, while its spore-producing structures contribute to its reproductive cycle.

Amphilophis emersa plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
