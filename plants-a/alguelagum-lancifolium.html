Alguelagum lancifolium

Alguelagum lancifolium, commonly known as the sharp-leafed desert shrub, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that reduce water loss through transpiration, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its deep root system allows it to access groundwater, making it resilient during droughts.

Alguelagum lancifolium plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
