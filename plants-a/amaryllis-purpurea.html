Amaryllis purpurea

Amaryllis purpurea, commonly known as the purple amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, often with a contrasting red or orange throat. The plant features strap-like, broad leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Amaryllis purpurea is known for its ability to bloom reliably when cared for properly, often in late spring or early summer.
