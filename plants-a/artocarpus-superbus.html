Artocarpus superbus

Artocarpus superbus, commonly known as the giant breadfruit tree, is a large tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, palmate leaves and large, spiky fruits. The fruit, which is typically green when mature, contains a soft, edible pulp surrounding numerous seeds. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
