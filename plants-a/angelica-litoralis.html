Angelica litoralis

Angelica litoralis, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in saline and sandy soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments.

Angelica litoralis is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
