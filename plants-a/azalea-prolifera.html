Azalea prolifera

Azalea prolifera, commonly known as the Chinese azalea, is a species native to eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Azalea prolifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
