Ascocentrum aurantiacum subsp. philippinense

Ascocentrum aurantiacum subsp.

philippinense, commonly known as the Philippine yellow vanda, is a species of orchid native to the Philippines. It is characterized by its striking yellow-green foliage and clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or yellowish pseudobulbs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
