Astragalus suberosus

Astragalus suberosus, commonly known as the rough milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Astragalus suberosus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
