Acalypha sericea var. indefessus

Acalypha sericea var. indefessus, commonly known as silky ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, silky-hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green or yellowish flowers that are often clustered at the ends of the stems. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple branches and a dense foliage structure.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or disturbed habitats.
