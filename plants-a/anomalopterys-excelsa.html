Anomalopterys excelsa

Anomalopterys excelsa, commonly known as the giant waterfern, is a unique aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has a complex root system that helps stabilize water sediments and improve water quality. It reproduces through spores rather than seeds, and its fronds are often divided into smaller, finger-like segments.

Due to its ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland restoration projects.
