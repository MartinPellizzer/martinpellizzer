Azalea × gandavensis

Azalea × gandavensis, commonly known as the Gandavensis azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.

Azalea × gandavensis is also notable for its ability to produce fragrant blooms during the rainy season.
