Anil falcata

Anil falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed anil, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments.

Anil falcata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
