Angraecum conchoglossum

Angraecum conchoglossum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink with a dark red or purple lip. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually long and narrow, adapted to the humid, shaded environments of its native habitat.

Angraecum conchoglossum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
