Avena turgidula

Avena turgidula, commonly known as the water oat, is a perennial grass species native to wetland environments in the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomes are extensive, allowing it to form dense colonies in shallow water or moist soil.

Avena turgidula plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
