Aceras angustifolium var. longicrure

Aceras angustifolium var. longicrure, commonly known as the long-stemmed narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height, adorned with lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a valued addition to horticultural collections.
