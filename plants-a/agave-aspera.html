Agave aspera

Agave aspera, commonly known as the rough agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in small, sharp spines. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears clusters of yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. Agave aspera is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
