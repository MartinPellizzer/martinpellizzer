Alternanthera hirtula var. hirtula

Alternanthera hirtula var. hirtula, commonly known as the velvetleaf alternanthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges from deep red to purple and bronze hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters during the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant leaf color and ease of care.
