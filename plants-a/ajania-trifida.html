Ajania trifida

Ajania trifida, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated for ornamental purposes or traditional medicine.
