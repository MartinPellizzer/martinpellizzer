Amaranthus capensis

Amaranthus capensis, commonly known as lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, greenish or reddish flowers in dense spikes. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Amaranthus capensis is widely used for forage and erosion control due to its hardy nature and rapid growth.
