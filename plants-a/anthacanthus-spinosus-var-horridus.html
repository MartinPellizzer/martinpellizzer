Anthacanthus spinosus var. horridus

Anthacanthus spinosus var. horridus, commonly known as the spiked anthacanthus, is a unique and striking plant characterized by its spiny, bristle-like structures along its stems and leaves.

It is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with thick, waxy foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pale pink, adding subtle ornamental value.

Due to its distinctive appearance and specific ecological requirements, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
