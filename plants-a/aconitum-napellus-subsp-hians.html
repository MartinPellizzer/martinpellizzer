Aconitum napellus subsp. hians

Aconitum napellus subsp.

hians, commonly known as the Tibetan aconite, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas. It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September.

The plant has a robust root system and grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and it can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a dangerous plant and should be handled with care.
