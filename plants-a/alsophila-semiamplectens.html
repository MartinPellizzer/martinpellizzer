Alsophila semiamplectens

Alsophila semiamplectens, commonly known as the broad-leaf tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in persistent, fibrous roots and a crown of large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests. Its fronds are typically dark green and can grow up to several meters in length.

Alsophila semiamplectens plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
