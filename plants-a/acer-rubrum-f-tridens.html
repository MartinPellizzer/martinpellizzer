Acer rubrum f. tridens

Acer rubrum f. tridens, commonly known as the three-toothed red maple, is a distinctive cultivar of the red maple tree.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, trifoliate leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces vibrant red foliage in the fall, making it a popular ornamental tree.

This cultivar is valued for its adaptability and aesthetic appeal in landscaping.
