Artocarpus hispidus

Artocarpus hispidus, commonly known as the rough fig or hairy jackfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and produces a unique fruit with a thick, hairy exterior. The fruit contains a fibrous outer layer and a sweet, edible interior with a central core of seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued both for its fruit and its potential medicinal properties.
