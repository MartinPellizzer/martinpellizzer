Ancipitia Membracidoides

Mexican velvetleaf, scientifically known as Ancipitia membracidoides, is a striking plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves that shimmer under the sun. This species thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils near rivers and roadsides. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Despite its beauty, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
