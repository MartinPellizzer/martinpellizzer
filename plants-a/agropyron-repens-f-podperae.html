Agropyron repens f. podperae

Agropyron repens f. podperae, commonly known as the Podpera bluegrass, is a variant of the common bluegrass species.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and dense growth habit. This form is native to Central Asia and is adapted to temperate climates with well-drained soils. It typically forms a sod-like mat, making it suitable for erosion control and ornamental use.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer.
