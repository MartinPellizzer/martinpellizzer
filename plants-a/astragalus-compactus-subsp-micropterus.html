Astragalus compactus subsp. micropterus

Astragalus compactus subsp.

micropterus, commonly known as the small-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands.

The plant has small, compound leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring. Its root system is deep and fibrous, adapted to withstand arid conditions.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
