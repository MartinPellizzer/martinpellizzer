Arnica frigida subsp. griscomii

Arnica frigida subsp.

griscomii, commonly known as the alpine arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in alpine tundra and rocky slopes.

The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with prominent central disks, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in dense, white woolly hairs. Arnica frigida subsp.

griscomii is adapted to cold climates and is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory properties.
