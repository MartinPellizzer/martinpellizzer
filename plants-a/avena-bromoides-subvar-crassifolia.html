Avena bromoides subvar. crassifolia

Avena bromoides subvar. crassifolia, commonly known as broad-leaf oat, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and panicle-like inflorescence composed of small spikelets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its aesthetic appeal and durability.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
