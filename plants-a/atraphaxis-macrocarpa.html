Atraphaxis macrocarpa

Atraphaxis macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant desert lily, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, woody stems and large, showy white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, hard seeds. It is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, storing water in its fleshy stems and leaves.

Its botanical features make it a striking and resilient plant of the succulent flora.
