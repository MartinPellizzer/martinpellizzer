Abrahamia darainensis

Abrahamia darainensis, commonly known as the Daraina sandveld daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in the grasslands of the Eastern Cape and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
