Andropogon scoparius subsp. euscoparius

Andropogon scoparius subsp.

euscoparius, commonly known as eastern bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters.

The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves and produces spikelets that are often tinged with purple or red. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly broader leaves and more robust growth compared to the nominate form.
