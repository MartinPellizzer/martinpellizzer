Askidiosperma chartaceum

Askidiosperma chartaceum, commonly known as the paperbark tree, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fibrous bark that peels off in papery layers, giving it its common name. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy texture. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

The plant is valued for its durable bark, which has been traditionally used for crafting and as a source of fiber.
