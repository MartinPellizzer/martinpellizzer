Astragalus speirocarpus var. curvicarpus

Astragalus speirocarpus var. curvicarpus, commonly known as the narrow-fruit milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive, narrow, inflated pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
