Acacia diptera

Acacia diptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, bipinnate leaves and yellow globular flowers that appear in spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought.

Acacia diptera plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
