Allophylus laeteviridis

Allophylus laeteviridis, commonly known as the green-leaved alpine holly, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage, which is typically a vibrant green, and its small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Allophylus laeteviridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
