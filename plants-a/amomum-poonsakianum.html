Amomum poonsakianum

Amomum poonsakianum, commonly known as Thai cardamom, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fragrant, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal properties.
