Aspalathus cephalotes

Aspalathus cephalotes, commonly known as the Cape Flats dandelion, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny areas.

Aspalathus cephalotes is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
