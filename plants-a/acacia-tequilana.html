Acacia tequilana

Acacia tequilana, commonly known as the Mexican acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and bipinnate leaves that are divided into many small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of tannins.

Acacia tequilana is adapted to arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
