Adenostyles macrocephala

Adenostyles macrocephala, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are typically hairy and can grow up to several feet tall, supporting the flower heads.

Adenostyles macrocephala is adapted to rocky and dry environments, showcasing its resilience in harsh conditions.
