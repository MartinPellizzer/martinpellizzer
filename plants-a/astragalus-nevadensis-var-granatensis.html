Astragalus nevadensis var. granatensis

Astragalus nevadensis var. granatensis, commonly known as the Sierra granate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. This variety is distinguished by its pale purple to pink flowers and its ability to thrive in dry, rocky habitats. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
