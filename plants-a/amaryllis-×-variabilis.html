Amaryllis × variabilis

Amaryllis × variabilis, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant produces large, fleshy leaves that are typically broad and strap-like. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Amaryllis × variabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in gardens and as a houseplant.
