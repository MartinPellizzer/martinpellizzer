Apocynum cannabinum var. angustifolium

Apocynum cannabinum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf dogbane, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 2 meters in height. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a serrated margin, typically narrowing toward the apex. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually pink to purple in color, blooming in late summer.

The plant produces elliptical, woody capsules containing numerous small, white seeds.
