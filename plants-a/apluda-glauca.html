Apluda glauca

Apluda glauca, commonly known as blue flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Apluda glauca is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
