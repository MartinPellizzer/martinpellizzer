Astragalus ovinus

Astragalus ovinus, commonly known as goat's head, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus ovinus is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
