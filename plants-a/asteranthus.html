Asteranthus

Asteranthus, commonly known as the "star flower," is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to South America, particularly the Andes region, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plants typically grow in rocky or arid environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions. Asteranthus species are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their vibrant colors and unique flower structures.

They produce small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
