Acer palmifolium f. glabratum

Acer palmifolium f. glabratum, commonly known as the smooth-leaf maple, is a variant of the palmate maple characterized by its smooth, glabrous foliage.

It is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia. The leaves are palmately lobed, typically with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive, maple-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers cooler, moist climates.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique leaf structure.
