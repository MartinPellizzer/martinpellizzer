Alaticaulia menatoi

Alaticaulia menatoi, commonly known as the Menatoa tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, montane forests. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, waxy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in greenish-yellow clusters, and it produces small, round fruits.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
