Arabis beirana

Arabis beirana, commonly known as the Beirana rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lobed and often covered in fine hairs.
