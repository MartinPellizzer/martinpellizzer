Amelanchier canadensis var. botryapium

Amelanchier canadensis var. botryapium, commonly known as the Canadian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy white to pink flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces edible berries in late summer, which are small, sweet, and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and turn vibrant shades of red or orange in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
