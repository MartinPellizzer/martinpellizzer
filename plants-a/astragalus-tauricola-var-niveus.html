Astragalus tauricola var. niveus

Astragalus tauricola var. niveus, commonly known as the White-flowered Tauric Astragalus, is a perennial herb native to the steppes of southern Russia and Ukraine.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grassland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
