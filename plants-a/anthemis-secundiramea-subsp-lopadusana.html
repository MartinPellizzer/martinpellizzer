Anthemis secundiramea subsp. lopadusana

Anthemis secundiramea subsp.

lopadusana, commonly known as the narrowleaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in compact clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in dried floral arrangements.
