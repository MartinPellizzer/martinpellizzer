Amygdalus × communis var. pollardii

Amygdalus × communis var. pollardii, commonly known as the Pollard almond, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Prunus dulcis (sweet almond) and Prunus amygdalus (bitter almond).

It is characterized by its small, ornamental flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its fruits are small, hard shells containing bitter almonds, which are not suitable for consumption.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
