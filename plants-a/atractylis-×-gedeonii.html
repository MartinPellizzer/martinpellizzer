Atractylis × gedeonii

Atractylis × gedeonii, commonly known as the hybrid meadow fescue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower heads that resemble those of its parent species. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like.

Atractylis × gedeonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
