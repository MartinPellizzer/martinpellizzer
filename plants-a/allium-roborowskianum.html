Allium roborowskianum

Allium roborowskianum, commonly known as the giant bulbous onion, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, bulbous underground stem and tall, narrow leaves. The plant produces umbels of white to pale pink flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
