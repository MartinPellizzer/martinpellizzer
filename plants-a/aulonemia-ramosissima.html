Aulonemia ramosissima

Aulonemia ramosissima, commonly known as the branched spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, branching stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous tiny, greenish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a rosette at the base.

Aulonemia ramosissima thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
