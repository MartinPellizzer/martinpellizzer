Acacia caudata

Acacia caudata, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow, globular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across suitable habitats.

Acacia caudata is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of animal forage.
