Argythamnia schiedeana

Argythamnia schiedeana, commonly known as the Schiede's argyrodendron, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage due to a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite and lanceolate, with a leathery texture adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

Argythamnia schiedeana is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in dry gardens.
