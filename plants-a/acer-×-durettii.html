Acer × durettii

Acer × durettii, commonly known as the Duretii maple, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between *Acer ginnala* and *Acer negundo*.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring leaves with a serrated margin and a palmate shape that turns vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The plant is native to the United States and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full to partial sun exposure.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is a popular choice for urban and suburban planting.
