Andersonia vaginata

Andersonia vaginata, commonly known as the purple globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive globular shape and vibrant purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has hairy, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its dry habitat. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Andersonia vaginata is adapted to thrive in sandy soils and is often found in open, sun-exposed areas.
