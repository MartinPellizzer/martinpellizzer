Aloe × riccobonii

Aloe × riccobonii, commonly known as the hybrid aloe or the hybrid aloe vera, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Aloe vera and Aloe arborescens.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are fleshy and covered in a whitish, powdery coating called farina. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in terminal inflorescences, typically during the summer months. It is native to southern Africa and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Aloe × riccobonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its potential medicinal properties.
