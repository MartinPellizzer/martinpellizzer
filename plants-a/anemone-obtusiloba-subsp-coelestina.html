Anemone obtusiloba subsp. coelestina

Anemone obtusiloba subsp.

coelestina, commonly known as the sky blue anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It features showy, blue to sky-blue flowers with five to eight petals that bloom in early spring.

The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually unbranched. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more intense blue flower color compared to other varieties of Anemone obtusiloba.
