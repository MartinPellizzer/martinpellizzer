Allium breweri

Allium breweri, commonly known as the Brewster's onion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces upright, cylindrical flower stalks topped with clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Allium breweri is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
