Anoectochilus integrilabris

Anoectochilus integrilabris, commonly known as the broad-leafed rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on rocks or in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding substrate.

Anoectochilus integrilabris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
