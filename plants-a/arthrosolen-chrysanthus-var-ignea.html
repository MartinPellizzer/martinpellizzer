Arthrosolen chrysanthus var. ignea

Arthrosolen chrysanthus var. ignea, commonly known as the fiery flame plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red and orange foliage, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are ovate and covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention.

It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red and yellow, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
