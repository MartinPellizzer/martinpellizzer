Acalypha commersoniana var. brevifolia

Acalypha commersoniana var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurflower, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and vibrant flower clusters.
