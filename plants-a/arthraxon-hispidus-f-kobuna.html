Arthraxon hispidus f. kobuna

Arthraxon hispidus f. kobuna, commonly known as the Kobuna form of the hairy grama, is a variant of the grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue due to the presence of fine hairs.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual appeal.
