Achillea filicifolia

Achillea filicifolia, commonly known as fern-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it its common name, and it produces flat-topped clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

Achillea filicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has a long history of use in traditional herbal medicine.
