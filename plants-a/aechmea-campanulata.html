Aechmea campanulata

Aechmea campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces strikingly colorful, bell-shaped inflorescences that range from red to orange and yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Aechmea campanulata is popular as a decorative plant due to its vibrant appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea campanulata has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, leathery, and dark green, arranged in a tight spiral at the base of the plant.

The plant lacks a traditional stem, instead having a short, thick, and fibrous caudex that serves as a storage organ. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of white or pale blue flowers, which bloom in the summer months and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies. The fruit is a capsule that contains small, black seeds dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in its native habitat.
