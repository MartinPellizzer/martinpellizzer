Astragalus devius

Astragalus devius, commonly known as the devil's horsebean, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus devius is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
