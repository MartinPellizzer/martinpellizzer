Anthurium grahamii

Anthurium grahamii, commonly known as the Graham's anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored with deep green and red hues. The plant produces spadices, which are specialized inflorescences that resemble a spike of small flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium grahamii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
