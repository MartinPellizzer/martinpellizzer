Aconitum schleicheri var. comosum

Aconitum schleicheri var. comosum, commonly known as the Alpine monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, typical of the genus Aconitum. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
