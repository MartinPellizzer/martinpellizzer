Allium turcomanicum

Allium turcomanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces upright flowering stems that bear small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to semi-arid climates.

Allium turcomanicum is often cultivated for its edible leaves and ornamental value.
