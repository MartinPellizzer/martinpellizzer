Aster andersonii

Aster andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments, such as deserts and foothills. It produces showy purple or blue flowers that bloom in the spring and summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Aster andersonii is considered a rare and endangered species.
