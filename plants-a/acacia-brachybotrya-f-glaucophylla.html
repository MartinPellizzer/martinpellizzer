Acacia brachybotrya f. glaucophylla

Acacia brachybotrya f. glaucophylla, commonly known as the glaucophylla acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, globose phyllodes that are typically covered in a whitish, waxy coating, giving them a bluish-gray appearance. The plant produces yellow flower spikes that appear in summer, adding ornamental value. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The foliage's unique coloration and compact growth habit make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
