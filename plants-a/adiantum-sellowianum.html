Adiantum sellowianum

Adiantum sellowianum, commonly known as the Sellow's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble a feather. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, elegant appearance.

Adiantum sellowianum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
