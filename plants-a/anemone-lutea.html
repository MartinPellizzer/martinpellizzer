Anemone lutea

Anemone lutea, commonly known as the yellow anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Anemone lutea is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
