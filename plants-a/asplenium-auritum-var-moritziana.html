Asplenium auritum var. moritziana

Asplenium auritum var. moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern.

It is a compact, evergreen fern with delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
