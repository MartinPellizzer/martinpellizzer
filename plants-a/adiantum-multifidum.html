Adiantum multifidum

Adiantum multifidum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to regions of the Americas, including parts of the southeastern United States. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or near streams. Its fronds are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive appearance that resembles flowing hair, hence its common name.

Adiantum multifidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
