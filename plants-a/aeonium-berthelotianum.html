Aeonium berthelotianum

Aeonium berthelotianum, commonly known as the Desert Aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces tall, branching stems with clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
