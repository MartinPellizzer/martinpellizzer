Arabis pumila

Arabis pumila, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are rounded and hairy, and its stems are often prostrate or ascending. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Arabis pumila is often found in alpine meadows and is a characteristic species of high-altitude ecosystems.
