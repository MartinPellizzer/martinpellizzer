Allophyllum capillare

Allophyllum capillare, commonly known as the slender alpine saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Allophyllum capillare thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
