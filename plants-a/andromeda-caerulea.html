Andromeda caerulea

Andromeda caerulea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
