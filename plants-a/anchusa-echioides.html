Anchusa echioides

Anchusa echioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf anchusa, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers in spikes during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Anchusa echioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
