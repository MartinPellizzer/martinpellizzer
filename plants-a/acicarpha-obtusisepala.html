Acicarpha obtusisepala

Acicarpha obtusisepala, commonly known as the broad-lobed acacia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acicarpha obtusisepala thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
