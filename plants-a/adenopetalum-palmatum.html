Adenopetalum palmatum

Adenopetalum palmatum, commonly known as the palmate adenopetalum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive palmate venation pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and prominent stamens. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Adenopetalum palmatum is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits.
