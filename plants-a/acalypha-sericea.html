Acalypha sericea

Acalypha sericea, commonly known as silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Acalypha sericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
