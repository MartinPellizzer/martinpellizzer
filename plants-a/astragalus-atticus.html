Astragalus atticus

Astragalus atticus, commonly known as the Attic milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. Astragalus atticus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in its native habitats.

It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to semi-arid environments.
