Aster novi-belgii var. minor

Aster novi-belgii var. minor, commonly known as the New York aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, displaying shades of purple, pink, and white.

The species is well adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
