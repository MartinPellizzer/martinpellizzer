Anandria bellidiastrum var. autumnalis

Anandria bellidiastrum var. autumnalis, commonly known as the autumn bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are upright and branched. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
