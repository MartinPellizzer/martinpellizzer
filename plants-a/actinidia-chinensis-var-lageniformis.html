Actinidia chinensis var. lageniformis

Actinidia chinensis var. lageniformis, commonly known as the fragrant honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological applications.
