Aralia disperma

Aralia disperma, commonly known as the Canadian aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with multiple leaflets, typically arranged in a feather-like pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by red berries that are a food source for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant has a long taproot, allowing it to survive in various environmental conditions.
