Ampelocissus bombycina

Ampelocissus bombycina, commonly known as the silky leaf fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage.

The species is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
