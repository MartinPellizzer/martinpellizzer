Agave carchariodonta

Agave carchariodonta, commonly known as the Mexican giant agave, is a striking succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like stem and large, fleshy leaves that are often lined with sharp spines. The plant produces an impressive inflorescence with yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is adapted to thrive in hot, dry climates with minimal rainfall.

Its robust structure and ability to store water make it well-suited for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
