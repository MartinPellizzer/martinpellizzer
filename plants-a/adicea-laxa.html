Adicea laxa

Adicea laxa, commonly known as the slender adicea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Apocynaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Adicea laxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
