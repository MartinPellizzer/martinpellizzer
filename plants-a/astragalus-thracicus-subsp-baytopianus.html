Astragalus thracicus subsp. baytopianus

Astragalus thracicus subsp.

baytopianus, commonly known as the Baytopian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Turkey. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous regions. It has a taproot system and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential ecological significance.
