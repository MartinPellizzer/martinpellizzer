Amaranthus blitoides var. crassius

Amaranthus blitoides var. crassius, commonly known as rough amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and branched stems that often become woody with age. The plant produces small, green to red flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with serrated margins and a rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically for both food and fiber.
