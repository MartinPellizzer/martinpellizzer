Artemisia rutifolia var. rutifolia

Artemisia rutifolia var. rutifolia, commonly known as silverleaf sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and have a strong, aromatic scent.
