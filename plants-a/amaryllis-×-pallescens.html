Amaryllis × pallescens

Amaryllis × pallescens, commonly known as the white amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis bellona and Amaryllis belladonna.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant produces strap-like, evergreen leaves that are broad and lance-shaped. It is native to South America and thrives in warm, humid climates.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care in tropical and subtropical regions.
