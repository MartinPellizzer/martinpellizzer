Atylosia

Atylosia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. These plants are characterized by their showy, blue or purple flower heads that resemble mist, hence their common name. Atylosia species typically grow as herbaceous perennials, often with silver-gray foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens.

They thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making them popular choices for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
