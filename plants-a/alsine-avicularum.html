Alsine avicularum

Alsine avicularum, commonly known as the sand sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or inland dunes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.

Alsine avicularum is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
