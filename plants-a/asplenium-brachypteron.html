Asplenium brachypteron

Asplenium brachypteron, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to southeastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called soruses, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium brachypteron is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
