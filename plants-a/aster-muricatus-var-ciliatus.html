Aster muricatus var. ciliatus

Aster muricatus var. ciliatus, commonly known as the hairy aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, violet-blue flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often gland-dotted, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
