Anisomeles xerophila

Anisomeles xerophila, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is shallow and fibrous, allowing it to absorb moisture from the surface soil efficiently.

Anisomeles xerophila is well adapted to withstand high temperatures and prolonged drought conditions.
