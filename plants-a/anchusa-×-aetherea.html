Anchusa × aetherea

Anchusa × aetherea, commonly known as the hybrid bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to gardens. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Botanically, it is a hybrid between Anchusa tetrasepala and Anchusa sphaerocephala, showcasing genetic diversity in its floral structure.
