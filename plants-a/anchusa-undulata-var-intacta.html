Anchusa undulata var. intacta

Anchusa undulata var. intacta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features basal leaves that are narrow and undulating, with a bluish-green hue, and stems that are hairy and often branched. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
