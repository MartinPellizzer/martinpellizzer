Anthoxanthum australe

Anthoxanthum australe, commonly known as the Australian grass or slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Anthoxanthum australe is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
