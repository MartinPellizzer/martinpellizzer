Arnica hirsuta

Arnica hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy arnica, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with prominent orange markings, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Arnica hirsuta is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.
