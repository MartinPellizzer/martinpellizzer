Aechmea biflora

Aechmea biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

The plant produces showy, short-lived inflorescences with vibrant red or orange flowers, which attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are typically broad and have a waxy surface, which helps in water retention.

Aechmea biflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by brightly colored bracts, producing small, white to pink flowers that bloom during the dry season and are pollinated by insects.
