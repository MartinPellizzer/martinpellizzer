Astragalus zanjanensis

Astragalus zanjanensis, commonly known as Zanjan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and may have potential medicinal uses, though further research is needed.

Its ecological significance is notable in its native habitat.
