Aporanthus trifoliastrum

Aporanthus trifoliastrum, commonly known as the three-leaved morning glory, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. Aporanthus trifoliastrum is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in some areas.
