Apeiba tibourbou var. krukoffii

Apeiba tibourbou var. krukoffii, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a distinctive and showy appearance. The tree has a smooth, gray bark and produces elongated, woody fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its wood in traditional uses.
