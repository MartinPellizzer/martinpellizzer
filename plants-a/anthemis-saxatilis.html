Anthemis saxatilis

Anthemis saxatilis, commonly known as the rock everlasting, is a flowering plant native to rocky and arid regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and silver-gray foliage, which helps it conserve moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
