Aleuritia ianthina

Aleuritia ianthina, commonly known as the purple-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. Its berries are red or black and are edible, though not as commonly consumed as other honeysuckle species.

Aleuritia ianthina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
