Athyrium thwaitesii

Athyrium thwaitesii, commonly known as the Thwaites' Japanese shield fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive, shield-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Athyrium thwaitesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
