Alnus glutinosa f. sardoa

Alnus glutinosa f. sardoa, commonly known as the Sardinian alder, is a subspecies of the black alder native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sardinia.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of pollination. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
