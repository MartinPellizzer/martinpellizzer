Acinodendron ibaguense

Acinodendron ibaguense, commonly known as the Ibaguense acacia, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest in botanical studies and local conservation efforts.
