Arctostaphylos uva-ursi f. marinensis

Arctostaphylos uva-ursi f. marinensis, commonly known as the coastal manzanita, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of California and Oregon.

It features small, oval, evergreen leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
