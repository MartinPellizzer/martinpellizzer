Alloteropsis

Alloteropsis, commonly known as the "sickle grass," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, often tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The flowers are typically small and arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem. Alloteropsis species are often found in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or rocky soils.

These plants play a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
