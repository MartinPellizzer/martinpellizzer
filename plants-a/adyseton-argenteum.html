Adyseton argenteum

Adyseton argenteum, commonly known as silverleaf tree, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a silvery underside. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by round, fleshy fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
