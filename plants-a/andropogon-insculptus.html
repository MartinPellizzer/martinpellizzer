Andropogon insculptus

Andropogon insculptus, commonly known as the sculptured grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ridged leaf blades that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets.

Andropogon insculptus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
