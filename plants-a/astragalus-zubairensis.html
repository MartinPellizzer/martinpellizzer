Astragalus zubairensis

Astragalus zubairensis, commonly known as Zubair's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and may have potential uses in ecological restoration projects.

However, it is relatively rare and not widely studied in botanical literature.
