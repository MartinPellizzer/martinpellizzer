Anemone quinquefolia var. lyallii

Anemone quinquefolia var. lyallii, commonly known as the Lyall's thimbleweed, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
