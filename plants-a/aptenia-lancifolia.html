Aptenia lancifolia

Aptenia lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ice plant, is a low-growing succulent native to southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple, which bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature allows it to withstand drought and frost, enhancing its appeal as a resilient ornamental plant.
