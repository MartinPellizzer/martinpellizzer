Ascoglossum purpureum

Ascoglossum purpureum, commonly known as the purple orchid orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a complex structure with showy petals and specialized pollination mechanisms.
