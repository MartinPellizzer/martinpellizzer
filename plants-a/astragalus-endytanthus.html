Astragalus endytanthus

Astragalus endytanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets, and its root system is often deep and fibrous. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus endytanthus is known for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
