Althaea caribaea

Althaea caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet, marshy environments. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, pink or white flowers. Its roots are known for their mucilage content, which has been used in traditional medicine.

Althaea caribaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
