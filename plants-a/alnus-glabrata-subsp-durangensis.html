Alnus glabrata subsp. durangensis

Alnus glabrata subsp.

durangensis, commonly known as the Durango alder, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico. It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape.

The plant produces small, spherical cones that release wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
