Astragalus aureus var. macropodius

Astragalus aureus var. macropodius, commonly known as the golden milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
