Albizia boinensis

Albizia boinensis, commonly known as the Bornean siris, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into two sets of leaflets, giving it a delicate appearance.

Albizia boinensis produces fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in racemes, attracting various pollinators.
