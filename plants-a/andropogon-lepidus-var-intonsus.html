Andropogon lepidus var. intonsus

Andropogon lepidus var. intonsus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue, and its flowering spikes are typically slender and erect. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are wind-pollinated, a common trait among grasses. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
