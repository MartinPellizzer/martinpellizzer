Artocarpus altissimus

Artocarpus altissimus, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fruit clusters. The tree can grow up to 25 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its fruit, which is botanically classified as a syncarp, contains a starchy, edible pulp and a central core.

The plant is valued for its nutritional content and has been cultivated for centuries in various Pacific Island cultures.
