Alcea kurdica var. schirazana

Alcea kurdica var. schirazana, commonly known as the Schirazan hollyhock, is a variant of the hollyhock plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing clusters of flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
