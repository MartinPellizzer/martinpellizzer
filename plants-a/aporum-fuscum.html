Aporum fuscum

Aporum fuscum, commonly known as the dark aporum, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically enclosed within a modified leaf called a spathe. Aporum fuscum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
