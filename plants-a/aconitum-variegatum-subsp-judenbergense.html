Aconitum variegatum subsp. judenbergense

Aconitum variegatum subsp.

judenbergense, commonly known as the variegated monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has dark green, deeply lobed leaves with a distinctive white or yellow variegation pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
