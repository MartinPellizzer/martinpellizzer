Acacia dermatophylla

Acacia dermatophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects. Acacia dermatophylla is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in poor soil and low rainfall environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native fauna.
