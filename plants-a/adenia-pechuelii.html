Adenia pechuelii

Adenia pechuelii, commonly known as the red-flowered adenia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in woodland and rainforest habitats. The plant is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

Adenia pechuelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
