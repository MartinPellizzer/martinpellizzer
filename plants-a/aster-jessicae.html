Aster jessicae

Aster jessicae, commonly known as the Jessica's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces showy purple to blue flowers in the fall.

Aster jessicae is often found in areas with well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
