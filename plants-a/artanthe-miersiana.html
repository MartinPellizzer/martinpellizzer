Artanthe miersiana

Artanthe miersiana, commonly known as the Miers' artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers range in color from pink to red.

Artanthe miersiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
