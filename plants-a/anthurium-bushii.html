Anthurium bushii

Anthurium bushii, commonly known as the Bushii flamingo flower, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped spathes that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often resembling a flamingo's beak. The plant features a thick, underground rhizome and waxy, dark green leaves that are typically broad and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium bushii is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
