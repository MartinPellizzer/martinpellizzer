Anthemis brevicuspis

Anthemis brevicuspis, commonly known as the short-fruited ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in dense clusters, and the leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
