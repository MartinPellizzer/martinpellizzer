Ampelopsis tricuspidata f. veitchii

Ampelopsis tricuspidata f. veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's trailing honeysuckle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Vitaceae family.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and cascading clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three lance-shaped lobes, and the plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly palatable. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive foliage and flowering display.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
