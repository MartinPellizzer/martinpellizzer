Anthemis hungarica

Anthemis hungarica, commonly known as Hungarian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas due to their mild, chamomile-like scent.

The botanical features of Anthemis hungarica make it a valuable species in both ecological and traditional medicine contexts.
