Agropyron desertorum

Agropyron desertorum, commonly known as the desert barley, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, bluish-green leaves and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used for erosion control due to its extensive root system.

Agropyron desertorum is also valued for its role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting wildlife in desert ecosystems.
