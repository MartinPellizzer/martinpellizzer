Achillea rupestris subsp. lucana

Achillea rupestris subsp.

lucana, commonly known as the rock yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often found in dry, sunny habitats such as slopes and cliffs.

The plant features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, with a grayish-green coloration. Its flowers are small, clustered in flat-topped umbels, and are usually pale yellow to white in color.

It is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in rock gardens and alpine settings.
