Angraecum lignosum

Angraecum lignosum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its robust, woody rhizome and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive star-like shape. Its blooming period occurs during the rainy season, usually between May and September.

Angraecum lignosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
