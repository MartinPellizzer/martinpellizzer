Aspalathus adelphea

Aspalathus adelphea, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat. It produces small, hard, brown seeds that are dispersed by wind, and its leaves are often used in traditional medicine.

Aspalathus adelphea plays an important ecological role in its native environment, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
