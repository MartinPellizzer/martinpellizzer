Andruris wariana

Andruris wariana, commonly known as the golden sunflower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family, native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, bright yellow ray florets surrounding a dense, orange-pink disk floret center, which gives it a sun-like appearance. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with upright, branched stems and hairy foliage. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Andruris wariana is well adapted to hot, dry environments, making it a resilient and visually appealing plant in xeriscaped gardens.
