Alsodeia grandis

Alsodeia grandis, commonly known as the large alsike, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its seeds are encased in a hairy pod and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in suitable habitats.

Alsodeia grandis is also valued for its role in nitrogen fixation, enhancing soil fertility in agricultural settings.
