Alsine subtilis

Alsine subtilis, commonly known as the small alsike, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, which includes many flowering plants with showy blooms. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale pink or white, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Alsine subtilis is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
