Asplenium grandifrons

Asplenium grandifrons, commonly known as the large-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bright green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Asplenium grandifrons is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
