Aegiphila laevis

Aegiphila laevis, commonly known as the smooth aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue to purple in color and bloom in clusters.

Aegiphila laevis is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and streambanks.
