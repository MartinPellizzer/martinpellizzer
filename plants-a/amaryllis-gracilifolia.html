Amaryllis gracilifolia

Amaryllis gracilifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaved amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that grow from a bulbous base, and its flowers are typically bright red or orange with a prominent central stamen. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that can reach up to 12 inches in length. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Amaryllis gracilifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
