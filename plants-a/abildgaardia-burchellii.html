Abildgaardia burchellii

Abildgaardia burchellii, commonly known as the Burchell's cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces both male and female cones, with the female cones developing into seed-bearing structures. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
