Agropyron intermedium var. aristatum

Agropyron intermedium var. aristatum, commonly known as the tufted wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern Great Plains of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and steppe ecosystems. It produces spikelets that are often adorned with awns, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This grass is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in restoration projects due to its hardiness and adaptability.
