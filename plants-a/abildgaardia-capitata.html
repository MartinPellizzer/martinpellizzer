Abildgaardia capitata

Abildgaardia capitata, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of its stems. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
