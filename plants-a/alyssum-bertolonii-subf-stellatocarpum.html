Alyssum bertolonii subf. stellatocarpum

Alyssum bertolonii subf.

stellatocarpum, commonly known as the starfruit alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant features gray-green, linear leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique, star-shaped fruits, which are a key identifying feature.
