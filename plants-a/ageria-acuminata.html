Ageria acuminata

Ageria acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ageratum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, while the flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Ageria acuminata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
