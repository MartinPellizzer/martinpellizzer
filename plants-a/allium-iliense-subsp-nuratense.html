Allium iliense subsp. nuratense

Allium iliense subsp.

nuratense, commonly known as the Ili Valley garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes a wide variety of onions, garlic, and leeks.

The plant typically grows in rocky or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its bulbs are small and often used in local cuisines for their pungent flavor.

The leaves are grass-like, and the plant produces clusters of white or pale purple flowers in the summer.
