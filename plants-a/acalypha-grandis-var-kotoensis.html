Acalypha grandis var. kotoensis

Acalypha grandis var. kotoensis, commonly known as the Kotoensis Acalypha, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that range from red to orange, often mistaken for flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, glossy texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and is used in landscaping and as a houseplant.
