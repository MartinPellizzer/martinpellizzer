Acrandra sellowiana

Acrandra sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a rich, reddish-brown color and a fine, even texture. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, spherical fruits.

Due to its high-quality wood, it has been heavily harvested, leading to its classification as an endangered species.
