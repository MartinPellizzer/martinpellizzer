Ammocharis angolensis

Ammocharis angolensis, commonly known as the Angola lily, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
