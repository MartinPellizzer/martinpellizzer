Aloe anomala

Aloe anomala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features rosettes of narrow, upright leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in branched inflorescences. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Aloe anomala is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
