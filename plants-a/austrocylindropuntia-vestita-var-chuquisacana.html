Austrocylindropuntia vestita var. chuquisacana

Austrocylindropuntia vestita var. chuquisacana, commonly known as the Chuquisaca cholla, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically covered in a layer of white, powdery bloom. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soils and high temperatures.

This variety is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
