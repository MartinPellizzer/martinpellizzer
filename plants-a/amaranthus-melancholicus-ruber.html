Amaranthus melancholicus ruber

Amaranthus melancholicus ruber, commonly known as the crimson mourning amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, reddish-purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy species suitable for arid environments.

The plant's ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and unique appearance.
