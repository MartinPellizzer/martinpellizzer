Acacia bellula

Acacia bellula, commonly known as the dwarf acacia, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
