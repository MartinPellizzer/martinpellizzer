Amomum clusii

Amomum clusii, commonly known as the Java cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces spindle-shaped, dark brown fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
