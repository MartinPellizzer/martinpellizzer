Astragalus onobrychis var. multijugus

Astragalus onobrychis var. multijugus, commonly known as the velvet bean clover, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable forage crop. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its distinctive feature is the presence of multiple seed pods, which contribute to its common name.
