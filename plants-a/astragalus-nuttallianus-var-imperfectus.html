Astragalus nuttallianus var. imperfectus

Astragalus nuttallianus var. imperfectus, commonly known as the imperfect milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.

The species is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators and wildlife.
