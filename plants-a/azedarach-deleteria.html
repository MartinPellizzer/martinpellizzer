Azedarach deleteria

Azedarach deleteria, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, narrow fruits that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy blooms and pleasant fragrance.

However, it can be invasive in some regions and may pose a risk to livestock if ingested.
