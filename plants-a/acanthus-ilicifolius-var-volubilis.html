Acanthus ilicifolius var. volubilis

Acanthus ilicifolius var. volubilis, commonly known as the climbing acanthus, is a variant of the acanthus plant characterized by its vigorous climbing habit.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a distinctive silvery-white underside. The plant produces spiny, purplish flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic appeal and structural interest.
