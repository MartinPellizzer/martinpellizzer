Annesorhiza articulata

Annesorhiza articulata, commonly known as the articulated sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. The plant has a distinctive appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in early spring. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Annesorhiza articulata is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
