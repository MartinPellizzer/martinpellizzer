Azorella echinus

Azorella echinus, commonly known as the cushion plant, is a member of the Apiaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing structure, forming a compact cushion-like shape that helps it survive in harsh, cold environments. Azorella echinus is also known for its ability to survive extreme conditions, making it a resilient species in alpine and subantarctic habitats.
