Anthemis platyglossa

Anthemis platyglossa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and bloom in summer.

It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
