Aphelandra alexandri

Aphelandra alexandri, commonly known as the Alexandrian flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that bear bright orange or red flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Aphelandra alexandri is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and insects.
