Aquilegia grubovii

Aquilegia grubovii, commonly known as the Karakoram columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, purple, and blue. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 5,000 meters. Its leaves are compound, with palmate lobes, and it produces follicles as its fruit type.

Aquilegia grubovii is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous areas.
