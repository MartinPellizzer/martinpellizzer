Acacia hirta var. glabrior

Acacia hirta var. glabrior, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers that appear in cylindrical clusters. The leaves are simple, linear, and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
