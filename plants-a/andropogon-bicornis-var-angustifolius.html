Andropogon bicornis var. angustifolius

Andropogon bicornis var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
