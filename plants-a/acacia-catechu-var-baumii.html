Acacia catechu var. baumii

Acacia catechu var. baumii, commonly known as the black acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless branches and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing small, dark seeds. It is valued for its hard, heavy wood and for its use in traditional medicine.

The bark and leaves are also used to produce tannins and have been historically used in dyeing and leather tanning.
