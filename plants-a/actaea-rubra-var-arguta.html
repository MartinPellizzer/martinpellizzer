Actaea rubra var. arguta

Actaea rubra var. arguta, commonly known as the smooth black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces clusters of white, umbel-like flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its berries are dark red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
