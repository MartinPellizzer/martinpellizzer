Alectra lancifolia

Alectra lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alectra, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Alectra lancifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
