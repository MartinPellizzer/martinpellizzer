Acer campestre f. argenteovariegatum

Acer campestre f. argenteovariegatum, commonly known as the silver-variegated hedge maple, is a cultivated form of the common maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that feature a mix of green, white, and yellow variegation. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the autumn.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
