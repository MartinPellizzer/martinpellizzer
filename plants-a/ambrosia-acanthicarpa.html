Ambrosia acanthicarpa

Ambrosia acanthicarpa, commonly known as the roughleaf ambrosia, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragweeds, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are surrounded by bracts, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. Ambrosia acanthicarpa is known for its allergenic pollen, which can contribute to seasonal allergies in humans.

Its life cycle is adapted to short growing seasons, making it a common weed in agricultural and urban areas.
