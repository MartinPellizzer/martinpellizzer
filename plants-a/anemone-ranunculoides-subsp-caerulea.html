Anemone ranunculoides subsp. caerulea

Anemone ranunculoides subsp.

caerulea, commonly known as the blue anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to deep violet, often with a contrasting yellow center.

The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
