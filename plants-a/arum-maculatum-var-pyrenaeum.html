Arum maculatum var. pyrenaeum

Arum maculatum var. pyrenaeum, commonly known as the Pyrenean arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It features a striking, arrow-shaped leaf that is typically dark green with a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces an umbel of small, pale greenish-white flowers, which are followed by spadices that turn red or purple as they mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
