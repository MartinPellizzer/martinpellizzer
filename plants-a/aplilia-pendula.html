Aplilia pendula

Aplilia pendula, commonly known as the weeping bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that hang in clusters, giving it a drooping appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aplilia pendula is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
