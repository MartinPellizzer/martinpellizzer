Aldama mesoamericana

Aldama mesoamericana, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.
