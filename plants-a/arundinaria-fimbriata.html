Arundinaria fimbriata

Arundinaria fimbriata, commonly known as the bamboo grass or fimbriated bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen.

Arundinaria fimbriata is valued for its durability and flexibility, making it a versatile material in various cultural practices.
