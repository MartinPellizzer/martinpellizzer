Acaena laevigata var. venulosa

Acaena laevigata var. venulosa, commonly known as the smooth woodsetts, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, followed by small, spiky fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
