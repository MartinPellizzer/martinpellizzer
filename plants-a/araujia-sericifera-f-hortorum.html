Araujia sericifera f. hortorum

Araujia sericifera f. hortorum, commonly known as the velvetleaf moonflower, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous seeds.

This ornamental plant is often grown in warm climates for its striking appearance and unique blooming habit.
