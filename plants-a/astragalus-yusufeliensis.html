Astragalus yusufeliensis

Astragalus yusufeliensis, commonly known as the Yusufeli milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus yusufeliensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest for ecological and botanical studies.
