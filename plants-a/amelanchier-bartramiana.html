Amelanchier bartramiana

Amelanchier bartramiana, commonly known as the striped maple or flower dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often twining branches and its showy, white or pinkish flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that turn red or blue-black in late summer, which are a food source for birds and wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

Amelanchier bartramiana is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
