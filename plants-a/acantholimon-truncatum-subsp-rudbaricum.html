Acantholimon truncatum subsp. rudbaricum

Acantholimon truncatum subsp.

rudbaricum, commonly known as the Persian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southwestern Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture.

The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
