Alnus mitchelliana

Alnus mitchelliana, commonly known as the Mitchell's alder, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the birch family (Betulaceae) and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and mountainous areas.

Alnus mitchelliana plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
