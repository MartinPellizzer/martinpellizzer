Aspidistra verruculosa

Aspidistra verruculosa, commonly known as the rough-leaf aspidistra, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and spadix inflorescence. The plant features dark green, glossy foliage with a rough texture on the underside, giving it its common name. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Aspidistra verruculosa is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
