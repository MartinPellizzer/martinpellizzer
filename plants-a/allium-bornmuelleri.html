Allium bornmuelleri

Allium bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Bornmueller's onion, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous structure and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in drought-tolerant plants.
