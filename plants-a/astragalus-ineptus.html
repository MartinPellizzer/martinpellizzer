Astragalus ineptus

Astragalus ineptus, commonly known as the poor performance milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus ineptus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
