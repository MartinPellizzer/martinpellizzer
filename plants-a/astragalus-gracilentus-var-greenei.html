Astragalus gracilentus var. greenei

Astragalus gracilentus var. greenei, commonly known as the Greene's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, with thread-like leaflets, and it produces small, legume-like pods that contain seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
