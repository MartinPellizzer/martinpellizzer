Atheropogon distichus

Atheropogon distichus, commonly known as the two-row grass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and wetlands. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in two rows. Its flowering spikes are small and clustered, producing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Atheropogon distichus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
