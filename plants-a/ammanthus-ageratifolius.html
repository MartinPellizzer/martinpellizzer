Ammanthus ageratifolius

Ammanthus ageratifolius, commonly known as the African silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
