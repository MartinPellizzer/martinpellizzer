Aira parvula

Aira parvula, commonly known as little air grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated and lack showy petals.

Aira parvula is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological value and low maintenance requirements.
