Aphelandra squarrosa

Aphelandra squarrosa, commonly known as the white sago palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that give it a palm-like appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. Aphelandra squarrosa thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
