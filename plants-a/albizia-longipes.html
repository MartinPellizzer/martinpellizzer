Albizia longipes

Albizia longipes, commonly known as the longipes mimosa, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is noted for its feathery, bipinnate leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Albizia longipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
