Atelophragma lozani

Atelophragma lozani, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its nocturnal blooming habit, it is also known for its unique pollination strategy involving nocturnal insects.
