Ardisia crispa var. caducipila

Ardisia crispa var. caducipila, commonly known as the deciduous ardisia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that turn yellow and drop in response to seasonal changes. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
