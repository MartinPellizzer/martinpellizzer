Acokanthera ouabaio

Acokanthera ouabaio, commonly known as the poison arrow tree, is a flowering plant native to central Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange or red berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, leathery texture.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it a significant species in traditional medicine and a source of poisons for hunting arrows.
