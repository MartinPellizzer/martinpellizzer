Austrocylindropuntia floccosa subsp. crispicrinitus

Austrocylindropuntia floccosa subsp.

crispicrinitus, commonly known as the Crisp-haired Cylindropuntia, is a species of cactus native to southern South America. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and cactus enthusiasts alike.
