Agave demeesteriana

Agave demeesteriana, commonly known as the Demeester's agave, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves. The leaves are typically blue-green in color, with white or grayish markings, and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It produces a tall flowering stalk that bears numerous yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
