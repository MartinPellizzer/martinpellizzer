Astragalus earlei

Astragalus earlei, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing food for local wildlife.

Astragalus earlei is often found in open woodlands and grasslands at moderate elevations.
