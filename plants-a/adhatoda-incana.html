Adhatoda incana

Adhatoda incana, commonly known as the Indian Trumpet or Adhatoda, is a flowering plant native to tropical Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is often grown for its ornamental value.

Adhatoda incana is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
