Arundinaria wightii

Arundinaria wightii, commonly known as the Wight's bamboo, is a species of grass native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, white to pale green flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheen.

Arundinaria wightii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional crafts and construction in its native regions.
