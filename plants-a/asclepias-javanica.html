Asclepias javanica

Asclepias javanica, commonly known as the Java milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that attract various pollinators, including butterflies and bees. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface.

Asclepias javanica is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.
