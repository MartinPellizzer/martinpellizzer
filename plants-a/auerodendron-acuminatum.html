Auerodendron acuminatum

Auerodendron acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The species is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in regions with low rainfall.
