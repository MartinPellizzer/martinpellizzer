Aspidistra cerina

Aspidistra cerina, commonly known as the red-veined aspidistra, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features dark green, broad, and lance-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins that add a striking visual contrast. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically followed by black, berry-like fruits.

Aspidistra cerina is valued for its ornamental foliage and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
