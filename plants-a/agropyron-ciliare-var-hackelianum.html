Agropyron ciliare var. hackelianum

Agropyron ciliare var. hackelianum, commonly known as the Hackel's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and tufted inflorescences that bear small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it forms dense, low-growing mats. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh, arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in degraded environments.
