Aleuritia rhodochroa

Aleuritia rhodochroa, commonly known as the red-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which range in color from white to red, depending on the variety. Its berries are red or black and are edible, often consumed by birds and small mammals.

Aleuritia rhodochroa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
