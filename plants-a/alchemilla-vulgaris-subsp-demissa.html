Alchemilla vulgaris subsp. demissa

Alchemilla vulgaris subsp.

demissa, commonly known as lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a dense rosette of silver-green, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
