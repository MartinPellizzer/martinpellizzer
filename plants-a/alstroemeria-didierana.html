Alstroemeria didierana

Alstroemeria didierana, commonly known as the Didier's lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves and typically grows as a herbaceous perennial.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
