Azalea × fastigifolia

Azalea × fastigifolia, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron indicum and Rhododendron obtusum.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and is native to the mountainous regions of Japan. The plant typically produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white during the spring. Its foliage consists of dark green, leathery leaves that provide a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

Azalea × fastigifolia prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
