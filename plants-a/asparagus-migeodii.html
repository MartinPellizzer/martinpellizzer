Asparagus migeodii

Asparagus migeodii, commonly known as the desert asparagus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries containing seeds. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, minimizing water loss.

Asparagus migeodii is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
