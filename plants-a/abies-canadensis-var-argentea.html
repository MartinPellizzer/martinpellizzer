Abies canadensis var. argentea

Abies canadensis var. argentea, commonly known as the silver fir, is a variant of the balsam fir native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the needles, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant is evergreen, with conical-shaped foliage that remains throughout the year. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The botanical features of this variety make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
