Allium multiflorum

Allium multiflorum, commonly known as the Chinese leek or Chinese onion, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes garlic and onions, and is characterized by its bulbous base and grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a popular ingredient in Asian cuisine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for both its culinary and aesthetic qualities.
