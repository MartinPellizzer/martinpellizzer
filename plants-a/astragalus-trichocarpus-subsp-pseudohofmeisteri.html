Astragalus trichocarpus subsp. pseudohofmeisteri

Astragalus trichocarpus subsp.

pseudohofmeisteri, commonly known as the Hofmeister's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to purple.

The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its root system is well-developed, often forming a taproot. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
