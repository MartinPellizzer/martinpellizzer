Astragalus episcopus var. episcopus

Astragalus episcopus var. episcopus, commonly known as the bishop's astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically produces yellow to orange blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats at higher elevations.

This species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
