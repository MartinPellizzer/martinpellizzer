Alysicarpus tetragonolobus var. tetragonolobus

Alysicarpus tetragonolobus var. tetragonolobus, commonly known as the four-angled cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its quadrangular stems and trifoliate leaves with small, hairy stipules. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
