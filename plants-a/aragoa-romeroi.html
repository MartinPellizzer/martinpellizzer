Aragoa romeroi

Aragoa romeroi, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions.

Aragoa romeroi plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
