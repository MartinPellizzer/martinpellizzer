Andromeda formosissima

Andromeda formosissima, commonly known as the Japanese andromeda, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its pale blooms.

Andromeda formosissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in cooler, moist climates.
