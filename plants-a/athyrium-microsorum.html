Athyrium microsorum

Athyrium microsorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Japanese painted fern, is a species of flowering plant in the family Athyriaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, including Japan, Korea, and China, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves.

Athyrium microsorum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant foliage and adaptability to various garden conditions.
