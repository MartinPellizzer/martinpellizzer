Arcuatopterus thalictrioideus

Arcuatopterus thalictrioideus, commonly known as the silky false spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly lobed or notched appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
