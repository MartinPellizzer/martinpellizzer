Arthromeris crassirhizoma

Arthromeris crassirhizoma, commonly known as the thick-rooted arthromeris, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its robust, fleshy rhizomes that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in moist, swampy environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Arthromeris crassirhizoma plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small aquatic organisms.
