Arrhenatherum elatius f. precatorium

Arrhenatherum elatius f. precatorium, commonly known as the purple false oatgrass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its slender, upright growth habit.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a striking violet or purple hue, especially along the leaf margins. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences bearing small, greenish to brownish florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

This ornamental grass is valued for its distinctive coloration and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators and soil stability.
