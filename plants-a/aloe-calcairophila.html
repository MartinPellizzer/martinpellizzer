Aloe calcairophila

Aloe calcairophila, commonly known as the Karoo aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in late winter or early spring. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in xeriscaped gardens.
