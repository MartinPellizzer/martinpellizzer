Amasonia lasiocaulos

Amasonia lasiocaulos, commonly known as the rough-hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spikelet that is often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

Amasonia lasiocaulos plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
