Arundinella kengiana

Arundinella kengiana, commonly known as the African bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional construction and crafts.

Its rapid growth rate makes it a useful plant for erosion control and reforestation projects.
