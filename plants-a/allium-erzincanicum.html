Allium erzincanicum

Allium erzincanicum, commonly known as the Erzincan garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, ovoid bulbs that are edible and have a mild garlic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is gaining popularity among gardeners and chefs alike.
