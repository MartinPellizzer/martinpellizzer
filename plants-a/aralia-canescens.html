Aralia canescens

Aralia canescens, commonly known as the white snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a tall, unbranched stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height, covered with a whitish, fuzzy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters, followed by dark purple berries. Its leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern.

Aralia canescens is often found in moist woodlands and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
