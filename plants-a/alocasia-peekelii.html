Alocasia peekelii

Alocasia peekelii, commonly known as the Peckel's arrowleaf, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense clumps. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-draining, rich soil.

Alocasia peekelii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dramatic foliage and air-purifying qualities.
