Astragalus steinbergianus

Astragalus steinbergianus, commonly known as Steinberg's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

Astragalus steinbergianus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
