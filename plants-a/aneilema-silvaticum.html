Aneilema silvaticum

Aneilema silvaticum, commonly known as the forest aneilema, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in dense forest understories. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, with a glossy surface that helps in capturing light in low-light conditions.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
