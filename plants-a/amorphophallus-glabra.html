Amorphophallus glabra

Amorphophallus glabra, commonly known as the naked-tongue arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped leaves and a unique, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces a tuberous root that is used as a starch source in some regions. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value as well as its culinary and medicinal uses.
