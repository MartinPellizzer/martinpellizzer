Allium paniculatum subsp. intermedium

Allium paniculatum subsp.

intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate grassleek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white to pale purple flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a flowering period that usually occurs in late spring to early summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
