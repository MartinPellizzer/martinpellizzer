Atropis pamirica

Atropis pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir lily, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Pamir Mountains.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 5,000 meters. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique habitat and ornamental value, Atropis pamirica is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
