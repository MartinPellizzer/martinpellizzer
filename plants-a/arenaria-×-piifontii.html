Arenaria × piifontii

Arenaria × piifontii, commonly known as the hybrid sand pink, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces showy, pink to magenta flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

This species is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
