Aphelandra campanensis

Aphelandra campanensis, commonly known as the bell-shaped heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that resemble bells, hence its common name. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green patterns. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant floral display.

Aphelandra campanensis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bats.
