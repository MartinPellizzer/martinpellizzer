Astragalus polycladus f. albiflorus

Astragalus polycladus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The species is noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
