Acacia laevigata

Acacia laevigata, commonly known as the white acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact globular heads, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soils and drought conditions.

Acacia laevigata plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
