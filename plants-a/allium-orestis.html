Allium orestis

Allium orestis, commonly known as the mountain garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that have a strong garlic-like flavor, though they are not as commonly cultivated as other allium species. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
