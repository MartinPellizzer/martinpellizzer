Arnica alpina subsp. iljinii

Arnica alpina subsp.

iljinii, commonly known as the Iljin's mountain arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes.

The plant features hairy, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its most distinctive characteristic is the yellow-orange flower head, which consists of numerous disc flowers surrounded by pale yellow ray florets.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
