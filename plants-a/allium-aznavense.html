Allium aznavense

Allium aznavense, commonly known as the Armenian leek, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Its bulbous stem is often used in traditional cuisines for its mild, onion-like flavor.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fibrous roots and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
