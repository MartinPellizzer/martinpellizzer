Astraea manihot

Astraea manihot, commonly known as arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its underground tubers, which are the primary part used for culinary purposes. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and small, spadix-like inflorescences. Its tubers are starchy and can be boiled, fried, or baked, making them a versatile ingredient in various cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in wet, tropical conditions.
