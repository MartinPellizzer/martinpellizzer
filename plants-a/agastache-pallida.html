Agastache pallida

Agastache pallida, commonly known as pinkroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats like meadows and rocky slopes.

Agastache pallida is also valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
