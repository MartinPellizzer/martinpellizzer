Agave magni

Agave magni, commonly known as the giant agave, is a striking succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, thick, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape, often reaching several feet in length. The plant produces a tall, branched flower stalk that can grow up to 10 feet high, bearing yellow-green flowers. Its leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle that helps reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Agave magni is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional practices by indigenous communities.
