Allium subhirsutum var. subvillosum

Allium subhirsutum var. subvillosum, commonly known as the woolly garlic or subvillosum garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild garlic scent.

This variety is closely related to other Allium species and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
