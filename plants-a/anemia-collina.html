Anemia collina

Anemia collina, commonly known as mountain sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and trifoliate leaves, which are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Anemia collina is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and cool climates.

It is also valued for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, slightly tart flavor.
