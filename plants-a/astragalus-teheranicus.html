Astragalus teheranicus

Astragalus teheranicus, commonly known as Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Iran and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
