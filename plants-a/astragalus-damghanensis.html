Astragalus damghanensis

Astragalus damghanensis, commonly known as Damghan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across suitable habitats.
