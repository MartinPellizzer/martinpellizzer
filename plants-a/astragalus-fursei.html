Astragalus fursei

Astragalus fursei, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects.

Astragalus fursei is also valued for its ecological importance in native plant communities.
