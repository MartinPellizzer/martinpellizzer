Anthurium malianum

Anthurium malianum, commonly known as the Malian arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive spadix and spatula-shaped leaves. The plant features showy, colorful bracts that often resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. Its leaves are typically large, glossy, and have a deep green color with a waxy texture.

Anthurium malianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
