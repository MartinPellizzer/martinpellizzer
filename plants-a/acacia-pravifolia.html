Acacia pravifolia

Acacia pravifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with slender branches and small, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Acacia pravifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
