Argyrolobium velutinum

Argyrolobium velutinum, commonly known as the velvet leafed silver globular.

It is a flowering plant native to South Africa, characterized by its silver-gray foliage and globular, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
