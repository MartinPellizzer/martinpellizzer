Alchemilla hirsuta var. campestris

Alchemilla hirsuta var. campestris, commonly known as field alchemilla, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, arranged in loose clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their soothing and anti-inflammatory effects.
