Artocarpus kemando

Artocarpus kemando, commonly known as the Java tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a unique fruit with a rough, hairy exterior. The fruit contains a single, large, inedible seed surrounded by a sweet, sticky pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
