Anisanthus caryophyllaceus

Anisanthus caryophyllaceus, commonly known as the sweet willow or honeyflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, which includes many flowering plants with showy blooms. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
