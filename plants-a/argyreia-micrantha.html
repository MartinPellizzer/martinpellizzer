Argyreia micrantha

Argyreia micrantha, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Argyreia micrantha thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
