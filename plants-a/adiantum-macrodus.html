Adiantum macrodus

Adiantum macrodus, commonly known as the giant maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that are often divided into narrow, arching segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive, feathery appearance.

Adiantum macrodus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in indoor settings with proper care.
