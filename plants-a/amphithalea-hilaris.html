Amphithalea hilaris

Amphithalea hilaris, commonly known as the joyful orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
