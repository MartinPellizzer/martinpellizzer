Asarum caudigerum f. leptophyllum

Asarum caudigerum f. leptophyllum, commonly known as the slender-leaved wild ginger, is a variant of the wild ginger plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The leaves and roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
