Alyxia acuminata

Alyxia acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed alyxia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a woody vine characterized by its slender stems and opposite, elliptic leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Alyxia acuminata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of tropical ecosystems.
