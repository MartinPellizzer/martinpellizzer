Aster poaceus

Aster poaceus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy purple or pink petals and a distinctive central cone-shaped disk. The plant typically grows in prairies, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom from late summer into early fall.

Aster poaceus is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
