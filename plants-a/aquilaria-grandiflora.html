Aquilaria grandiflora

Aquilaria grandiflora, commonly known as the white agarwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic heartwood, which is highly valued in traditional medicine and incense production. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and thrives in humid, tropical climates.
