Adopogon montanus

Adopogon montanus, commonly known as the mountain wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Adopogon montanus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
