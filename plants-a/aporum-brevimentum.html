Aporum brevimentum

Aporum brevimentum, commonly known as the short-stalked aporus, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and distinctive, elongated flower spikes that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in humid tropical forests. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
