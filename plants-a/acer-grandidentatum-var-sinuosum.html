Acer grandidentatum var. sinuosum

Acer grandidentatum var. sinuosum, commonly known as the sinuous mountain maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which are typically green on top and hairy underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange-red samaras that disperse its seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
