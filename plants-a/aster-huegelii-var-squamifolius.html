Aster huegelii var. squamifolius

Aster huegelii var. squamifolius, commonly known as the white wood aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
