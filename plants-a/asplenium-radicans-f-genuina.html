Asplenium radicans f. genuina

Asplenium radicans f. genuina, commonly known as the creeping spleenwort, is a fern species characterized by its small, delicate fronds that grow close to the ground.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to regions with moist, shaded environments. The plant typically features dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of moss-like growth. It thrives in humid conditions and is often found in rocky or wooded areas.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.
