Alchemilla martinii

Alchemilla martinii, commonly known as the silverweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy stems. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in late spring and are clustered in umbels.

Alchemilla martinii is adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a hardy plant often found in alpine and arid habitats.
