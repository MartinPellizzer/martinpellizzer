Amaryllis × pallidiflora

Amaryllis × pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis paradisicola.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pale pink to white, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring strap-like, evergreen leaves that are broad and smooth. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
