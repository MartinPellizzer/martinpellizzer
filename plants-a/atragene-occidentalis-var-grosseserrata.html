Atragene occidentalis var. grosseserrata

Atragene occidentalis var. grosseserrata, commonly known as the western mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are lanceolate and have prominent, coarse teeth, which give it its specific epithet "grosseserrata.".

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
