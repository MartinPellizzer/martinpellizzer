Anthemis purpurea

Anthemis purpurea, commonly known as purple sandwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, displaying small, daisy-like flowers in shades of purple and white. Its foliage is usually linear and gray-green, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Anthemis purpurea is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
