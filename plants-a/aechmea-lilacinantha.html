Aechmea lilacinantha

Aechmea lilacinantha, commonly known as the lilac spathifolia, is a species of bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater for the plant's use. The plant produces striking violet or lilac-colored bracts that surround its small, white flowers. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aechmea lilacinantha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique flowering structure.

Morphology

Aechmea lilacinantha has leaves that are strap-shaped, arranged in a rosette, and can grow up to 30 cm in length, with a vibrant green color and a waxy surface.

The stem is short and thick, with a smooth, pale green bark that remains hidden beneath the leaf rosette. Its flowers are clustered in a spike, featuring pink and white petals, and bloom during the summer months; they are pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and form a dense network around the base of the plant, helping to anchor it and absorb nutrients.
