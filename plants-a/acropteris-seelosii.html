Acropteris seelosii

Acropteris seelosii, commonly known as the Seelos' cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, trunk-like stem and feathery, compound leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
