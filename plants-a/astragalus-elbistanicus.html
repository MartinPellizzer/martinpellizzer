Astragalus elbistanicus

Astragalus elbistanicus, commonly known as the Elbistan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and high altitudes. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Astragalus elbistanicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
