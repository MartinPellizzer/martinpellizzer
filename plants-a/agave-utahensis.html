Agave utahensis

Agave utahensis, commonly known as the Utah agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears clusters of yellowish-green flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. It is well adapted to dry climates and can store water in its leaves and stem.

Agave utahensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
