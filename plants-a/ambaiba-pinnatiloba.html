Ambaiba pinnatiloba

Ambaiba pinnatiloba, commonly known as the broadleaf ambabia, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The flowers are small and greenish-white, appearing in clusters along the stems.
