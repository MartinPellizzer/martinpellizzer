Anthephora ramosa

Anthephora ramosa, commonly known as the hairy ant plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its branched, hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Anthephora ramosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
