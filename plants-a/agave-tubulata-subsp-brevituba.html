Agave tubulata subsp. brevituba

Agave tubulata subsp.

brevituba, commonly known as the narrow-tubed agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are shorter and more compact compared to other agave species.

The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with small, yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
