Astragalus buchtormensis var. dasycarpus

Astragalus buchtormensis var. dasycarpus, commonly known as the Buchtorm Astragalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its fruit is a hairy, indehiscent legume, which contributes to its specific variety name *dasycarpus*, meaning "hairy fruit.".

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
