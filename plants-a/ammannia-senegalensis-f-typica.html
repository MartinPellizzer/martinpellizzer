Ammannia senegalensis f. typica

Ammannia senegalensis f. typica, commonly known as the redroot ammannia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, red-stemmed appearance and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value is due to its striking red stems and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
