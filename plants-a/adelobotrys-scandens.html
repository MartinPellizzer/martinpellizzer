Adelobotrys scandens

Adelobotrys scandens, commonly known as the climbing thornapple, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a woody vine that typically grows along forest edges or in disturbed areas. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are often pink or white in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
