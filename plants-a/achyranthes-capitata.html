Achyranthes capitata

Achyranthes capitata, commonly known as the clustered spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
