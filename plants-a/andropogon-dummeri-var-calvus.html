Andropogon dummeri var. calvus

Andropogon dummeri var. calvus, commonly known as the smooth bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairie habitats.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in natural ecosystems.
