Atriplex crenatifolia

Atriplex crenatifolia, commonly known as the coastal saltbush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are adapted to tolerate saline and arid conditions. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its root system is extensive, helping it stabilize sandy or coastal soils.

Atriplex crenatifolia plays an important ecological role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
