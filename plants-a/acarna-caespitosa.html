Acarna caespitosa

Acarna caespitosa, commonly known as the clustered arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for wetland restoration projects and ornamental gardening.
