Asystasia schimperi

Asystasia schimperi, commonly known as the African violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots and clustered, fleshy leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of violet, pink, and white, often with a distinctive purple throat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for indoor cultivation and garden displays.
