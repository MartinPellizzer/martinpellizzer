Amelanchier rotundifolia var. cretica

Amelanchier rotundifolia var. cretica, commonly known as the Cretan serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, oval leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It is adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
