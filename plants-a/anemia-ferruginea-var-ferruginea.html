Anemia ferruginea var. ferruginea

Anemia ferruginea var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and conical, reddish-brown flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
