Astrantia alpina

Astrantia alpina, commonly known as the alpine aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Astrantia alpina is often used in rock gardens and as a hardy ornamental plant in gardens with full sun.
