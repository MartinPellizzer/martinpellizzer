Apocynum cannabinum subsp. cordigerum

Apocynum cannabinum subsp.

cordigerum, commonly known as the silky dogbane, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America. It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by follicles containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is known for its milky latex and is often found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and streambanks.
