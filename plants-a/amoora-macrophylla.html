Amoora macrophylla

Amoora macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leafed evergreen, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Amoora macrophylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
