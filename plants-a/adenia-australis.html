Adenia australis

Adenia australis, commonly known as the Australian Adenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with a distinctive crown of stamens, which are often visited by birds and insects. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
