Artemisia rubripes f. gracilis

Artemisia rubripes f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender tarragon, is a variant of the tarragon plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with fine, silvery-gray foliage that is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves contain volatile oils that contribute to its distinctive scent and potential medicinal uses.
