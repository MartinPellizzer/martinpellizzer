Avenastrum pratense var. maximum

Avenastrum pratense var. maximum, commonly known as the greater meadow grass, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough, hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
