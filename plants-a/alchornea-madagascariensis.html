Alchornea madagascariensis

Alchornea madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar beautyberry, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, fleshy fruits that turn from green to violet as they mature. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The fruits are a food source for various birds and mammals, contributing to its ecological significance.
