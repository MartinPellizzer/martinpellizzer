Apium depressum

Apium depressum, commonly known as the lesser parsley or field celery, is a flowering plant in the family Apiaceae.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, branching stems and small, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Apium depressum is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
