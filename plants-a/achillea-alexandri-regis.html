Achillea alexandri-regis

Achillea alexandri-regis, commonly known as Alexandrian yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pale pink. The plant features finely divided, aromatic leaves and produces numerous small flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its historical use in traditional medicine is attributed to its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
