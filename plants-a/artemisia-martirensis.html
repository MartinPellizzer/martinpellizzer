Artemisia martirensis

Artemisia martirensis, commonly known as the Mexican wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flower heads that are typically clustered in composite inflorescences. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
