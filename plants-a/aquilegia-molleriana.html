Aquilegia molleriana

Aquilegia molleriana, commonly known as the Moller's columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred petals that come in shades of red, purple, and blue. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Aquilegia molleriana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
