Acer longipes var. chengbuense

Acer longipes var. chengbuense, commonly known as the Chengbu maple, is a variant of the longipes maple native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
