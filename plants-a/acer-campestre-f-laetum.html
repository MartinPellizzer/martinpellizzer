Acer campestre f. laetum

Acer campestre f. laetum, commonly known as the white maple, is a variety of the field maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive white to pale yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge. This plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Native Distribution

Acer campestre f. laetum is primarily native to Europe, particularly in the temperate regions of the continent.

This variety of the field maple is commonly found in the British Isles, including parts of England, Scotland, and Ireland. It also occurs in southern Scandinavia, such as in Denmark and southern Sweden. The plant thrives in woodland edges, hedgerows, and open areas with well-drained soils.

Its distribution includes regions with a mild climate and sufficient sunlight for growth.
