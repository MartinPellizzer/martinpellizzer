Asperula divergens

Asperula divergens, commonly known as the creeping azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is well adapted to acidic soils. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for ground cover in gardens.

The flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to the landscape.
