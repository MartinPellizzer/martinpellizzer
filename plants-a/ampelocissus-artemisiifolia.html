Ampelocissus artemisiifolia

Ampelocissus artemisiifolia, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a rough, waxy texture and small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, often containing a single seed. It is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and thrives in warm, humid environments.

Its vigorous growth habit makes it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in some regions.
