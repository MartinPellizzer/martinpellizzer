Astragalus constrictus

Astragalus constrictus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of narrow, lance-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus constrictus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
