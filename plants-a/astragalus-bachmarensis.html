Astragalus bachmarensis

Astragalus bachmarensis, commonly known as the Bachmar's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has small, rounded pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Astragalus bachmarensis is considered a rare and endangered species.
