Adonis apennina subsp. sibirica

Adonis apennina subsp.

sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian glory-of-the-snow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and Siberia. It features a rosette of basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces striking white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, typically blooming before the leaves fully emerge. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and cold hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
