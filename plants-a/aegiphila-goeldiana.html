Aegiphila goeldiana

Aegiphila goeldiana, commonly known as the Goeld's aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
