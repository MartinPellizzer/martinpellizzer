Asclepias ciliata

Asclepias ciliata, commonly known as the fringe gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and have fine, silky hairs along the edges.

Asclepias ciliata is also noted for its role in supporting pollinators, including butterflies and bees.
