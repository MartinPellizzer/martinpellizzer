Arum italicum var. marmoratum

Arum italicum var. marmoratum, commonly known as the mottled arum, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive mottled pattern of green, white, and reddish hues. The plant produces striking, spatulate-shaped flowers that are typically deep red or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and greenhouses.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes grown as a houseplant in cooler climates.
