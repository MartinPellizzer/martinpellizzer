Achillea conferta

Achillea conferta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, linear leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. Achillea conferta is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
