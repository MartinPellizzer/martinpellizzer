Agave cryptica

Agave cryptica, commonly known as the hidden agave, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for water storage. The leaves are typically blue-gray in color, with a waxy coating that reduces water loss. It produces a tall flower stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing small, greenish-white flowers.

Agave cryptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
