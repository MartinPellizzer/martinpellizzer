Amorphophallus sparsiflorus

Amorphophallus sparsiflorus, commonly known as the sparse-flowered arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking, umbrella-like inflorescence. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique floral structures. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a spathe, typical of the genus Amorphophallus.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
