Arenaria algarbiensis

Arenaria algarbiensis, commonly known as the Algarbi sandflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb that thrives in sandy and rocky soils. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, adapted to withstand arid conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal and dune ecosystems due to its hardy nature.
