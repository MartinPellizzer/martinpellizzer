Asperula hexaphylla subsp. capitata

Asperula hexaphylla subsp.

capitata, commonly known as the clustered wood rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy pink or white flowers.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and has a woody stem structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its persistent foliage and long blooming period.
