Astragalus miguelensis

Astragalus miguelensis, commonly known as Miguel's milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America, particularly found in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are typically compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus miguelensis is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
