Anoda ochsenii

Anoda ochsenii, commonly known as the redstem false flax, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria. The plant features upright stems that are often tinged with red, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and slightly hairy. It produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming from summer to fall.

Anoda ochsenii is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
