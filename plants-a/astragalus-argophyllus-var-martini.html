Astragalus argophyllus var. martini

Astragalus argophyllus var. martini, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in open grasslands or rocky soils. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
