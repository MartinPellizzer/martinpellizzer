Abies magnifica var. critchfieldii

Abies magnifica var. critchfieldii, commonly known as the Critchfield's sugar pine, is a variant of the sugar pine native to the Sierra Nevada mountains of California.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The tree produces large, cone-like structures that contain edible seeds, which are a food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions and is adapted to the rugged terrain of its native habitat.

This variant is notable for its ecological importance and is often found in mixed coniferous forests.
