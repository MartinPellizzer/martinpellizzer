Acrostichum cardiophyllum

Acrostichum cardiophyllum, commonly known as the heart-leaf fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its fronds are compound, with multiple leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern.

Acrostichum cardiophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
