Agoseris grandiflora var. intermedia

Agoseris grandiflora var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate agoseris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features basal rosettes of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and the plant is adapted to arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
