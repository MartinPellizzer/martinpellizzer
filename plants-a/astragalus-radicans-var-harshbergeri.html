Astragalus radicans var. harshbergeri

Astragalus radicans var. harshbergeri, commonly known as the Harshberger's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe habitats.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
