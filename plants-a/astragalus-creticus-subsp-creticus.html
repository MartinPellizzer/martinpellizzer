Astragalus creticus subsp. creticus

Astragalus creticus subsp.

creticus, commonly known as Cretan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece. It is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Astragalus creticus subsp.

creticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and beauty.
