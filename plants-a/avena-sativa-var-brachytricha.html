Avena sativa var. brachytricha

Avena sativa var. brachytricha, commonly known as dwarf oat, is a variety of the oat plant characterized by its shorter stature and compact growth habit.

It possesses slender, leafy stems that typically grow to a height of around 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, which develop into oval, straw-colored grains. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a fibrous root system that aids in nutrient absorption.

This variety is often cultivated for forage, soil conservation, and as a source of nutritious grain.
