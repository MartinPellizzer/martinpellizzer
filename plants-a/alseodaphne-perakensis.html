Alseodaphne perakensis

Alseodaphne perakensis, commonly known as the Bornean laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in various traditional and commercial applications.
