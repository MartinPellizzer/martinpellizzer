Aloe cephalophora

Aloe cephalophora, commonly known as the knobbed aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color and covered in white tubercles. The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences with yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Aloe cephalophora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience to drought.
