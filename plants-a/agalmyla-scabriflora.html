Agalmyla scabriflora

Agalmyla scabriflora, commonly known as the rough-flowered agalmyla, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, often glandular leaves and its distinctive, rough-textured flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with ray florets that are narrow and stiff.

Agalmyla scabriflora is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with partial to full shade.
