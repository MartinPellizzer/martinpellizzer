Acacia drepanocarpa subsp. latifolia

Acacia drepanocarpa subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are broad and often have a silvery underside.

The plant produces yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and reforestation projects.
