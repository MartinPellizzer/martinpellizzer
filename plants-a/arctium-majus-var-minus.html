Arctium majus var. minus

Arctium majus var. minus, commonly known as lesser burdock, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers in composite heads, which are surrounded by bracts that form the distinctive bur-like structures. Its roots are thick and fibrous, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is adaptable to various soil types and thrives in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
