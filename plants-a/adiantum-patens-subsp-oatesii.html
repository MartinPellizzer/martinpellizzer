Adiantum patens subsp. oatesii

Adiantum patens subsp.

oatesii, commonly known as the Oates' maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that give it a graceful appearance.

The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the undersides of its leaves, typical of ferns. It thrives in humid, shady environments and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and garden cultivation.
