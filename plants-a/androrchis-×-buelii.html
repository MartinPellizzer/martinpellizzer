Androrchis × buelii

Androrchis × buelii, commonly known as the Buel's twayblade, is a hybrid orchid native to North America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a slender, leafless stem that produces a single spike of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
