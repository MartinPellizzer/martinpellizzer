Abutilon rivulare

Abutilon rivulare, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves are notable botanical features.

Abutilon rivulare is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
