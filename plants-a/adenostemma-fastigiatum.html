Adenostemma fastigiatum

Adenostemma fastigiatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant produces showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky slopes.

Adenostemma fastigiatum is also noted for its milky sap, which is a common feature among plants in the Malvaceae family.
