Aster aucuparius

Aster aucuparius, commonly known as the meadow aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, fields, and woodland edges, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its showy purple to pink flowers bloom in the late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
