Acacia auriculata

Acacia auriculata, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soils. Its flowers are often visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects, making it an important component of local ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
