Allium guatemalense

Allium guatemalense, commonly known as Guatemalan leek, is a perennial herb native to Central America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces upright flowering stems that bear umbels of white or pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
