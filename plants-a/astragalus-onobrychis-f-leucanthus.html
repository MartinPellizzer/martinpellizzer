Astragalus onobrychis f. leucanthus

Astragalus onobrychis f. leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered bartsia, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, compound leaves with a trifoliate arrangement. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, with white or pale yellow petals that distinguish this form from other Astragalus species. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
