Agapetes haemantha

Agapetes haemantha, commonly known as the silverleaf agapete, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a silvery or whitish wax, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible berries that are consumed by various wildlife.

Agapetes haemantha is adapted to high-altitude, moist environments and plays an important role in its native ecosystem.
