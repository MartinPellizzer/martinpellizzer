Amorphophallus echinatus

Amorphophallus echinatus, commonly known as the spiny aroid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and striking inflorescence. The plant produces a unique, often foul-smelling flower that attracts specific pollinators. Its corm, similar to a potato, is used in some regions for food.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
