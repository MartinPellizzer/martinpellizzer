Aconitum columbianum var. bakeri

Aconitum columbianum var. bakeri, commonly known as Baker's monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple hooded flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments, and its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat woody at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

The plant contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic to both humans and animals.
