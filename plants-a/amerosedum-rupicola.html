Amerosedum rupicola

Amerosedum rupicola, commonly known as the rock saltbush, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and steppe habitats.

Amerosedum rupicola plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
