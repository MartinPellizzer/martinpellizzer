Anomosanthes longifolia

Anomosanthes longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf anomosanthes, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
