Ampelopsis tadshikistanica

Ampelopsis tadshikistanica, commonly known as the Tadzhikistan grapevine, is a climbing vine native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and tendrils that aid in its climbing habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by berries that range in color from green to red. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.
