Amorphospermum natalense

Amorphospermum natalense, commonly known as Natal wild bean, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Amorphospermum natalense is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna ecosystems.
