Acacia lasiocarpa var. bracteolata

Acacia lasiocarpa var. bracteolata, commonly known as the bracteolate acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and compound leaves with small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soil conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
