Ananas serotinus

Ananas serotinus, commonly known as the late-blooming pineapple, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a single, striking inflorescence that bears small, white, fragrant flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique flowering display.

Ananas serotinus prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and container planting.
