Astragalus missouriensis var. typicus

Astragalus missouriensis var. typicus, commonly known as Missouri milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers in late spring. Its seed pods are slender and curved, containing several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

It is valued for its role in supporting local ecosystems and as a potential forage plant for wildlife.
