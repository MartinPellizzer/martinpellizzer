Ampelopsis delavayana var. setulosa

Ampelopsis delavayana var. setulosa, commonly known as the hairy grapevine, is a climbing vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in gardens and landscapes.
