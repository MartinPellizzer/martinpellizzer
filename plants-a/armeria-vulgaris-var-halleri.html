Armeria vulgaris var. halleri

Armeria vulgaris var. halleri, commonly known as the Haller's thrift, is a low-growing, evergreen perennial plant native to the coastal regions of southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has basal rosettes of linear, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in maritime climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh coastal conditions.
