Achyranthes alopecuroides

Achyranthes alopecuroides, commonly known as Indian spinach or bhang, is a herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Achyranthes alopecuroides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
