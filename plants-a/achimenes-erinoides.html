Achimenes erinoides

Achimenes erinoides, commonly known as the flame violet, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to purple, often with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces tuberous roots that allow it to survive in various environmental conditions. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, drought-tolerant plants.
