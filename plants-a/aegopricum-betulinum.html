Aegopricum betulinum

Aegopricum betulinum, commonly known as the cow parsley or bishop's weed, is a biennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in umbrella-like clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shady areas and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. It has a taproot system and produces a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Aegopricum betulinum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
