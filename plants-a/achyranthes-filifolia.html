Achyranthes filifolia

Achyranthes filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf smartweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features slender, thread-like leaves that are alternate along the stems, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters. Its botanical characteristics include fibrous roots and a rhizomatous stem system, which aid in its spread.

The plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
