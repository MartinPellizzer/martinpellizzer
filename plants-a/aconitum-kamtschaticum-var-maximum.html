Aconitum kamtschaticum var. maximum

Aconitum kamtschaticum var. maximum, commonly known as the Giant Monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces striking, hood-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost lace-like appearance.

The plant contains potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic and requiring careful handling.
