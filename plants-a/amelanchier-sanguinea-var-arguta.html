Amelanchier sanguinea var. arguta

Amelanchier sanguinea var. arguta, commonly known as the dwarf serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1-3 meters and features oval, alternate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a red-purple color in late summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to support local wildlife.
