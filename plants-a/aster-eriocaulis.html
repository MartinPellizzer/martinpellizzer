Aster eriocaulis

Aster eriocaulis, commonly known as the woolly aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and meadows. The plant features a basal rosette of fuzzy, silver-green leaves, which help it retain moisture. Its showy, blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
