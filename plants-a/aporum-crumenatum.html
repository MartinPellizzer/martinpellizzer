Aporum crumenatum

Aporum crumenatum, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, often mistaken for a type of orchid due to their appearance. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
