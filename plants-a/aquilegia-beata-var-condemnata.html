Aquilegia beata var. condemnata

Aquilegia beata var. condemnata, commonly known as the cursed bellflower, is a rare and striking member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spurred petals that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are typically arranged in a cluster of five. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, often found in woodland areas.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is considered a botanical curiosity and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
