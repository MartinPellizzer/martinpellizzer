Adesmia fuentesii

Adesmia fuentesii, commonly known as the Fuentes' burro's tail, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of fleshy, trailing stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscapes.
