Acacia heteroclita subsp. valida

Acacia heteroclita subsp.

valida, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often serpentine branches and phyllodes that are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow globular flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are modified into phyllodes, a common adaptation in acacia species to reduce water loss.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
