Ancylocladus minutiflorus

Ancylocladus minutiflorus, commonly known as the dwarf yew, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers that are typically followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact size and attractive foliage.
