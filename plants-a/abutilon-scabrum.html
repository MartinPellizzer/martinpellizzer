Abutilon scabrum

Abutilon scabrum, commonly known as the rough abutilon, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has a woody stem with a rough, scaly texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Abutilon scabrum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
