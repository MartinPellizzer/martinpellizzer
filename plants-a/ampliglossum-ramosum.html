Ampliglossum ramosum

Ampliglossum ramosum, commonly known as the hairy leaf cactus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, branching stem covered in dense, woolly hair, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted for storing water.

Ampliglossum ramosum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
