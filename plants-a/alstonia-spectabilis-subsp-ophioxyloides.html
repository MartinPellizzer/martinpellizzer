Alstonia spectabilis subsp. ophioxyloides

Alstonia spectabilis subsp.

ophioxyloides, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences that display a gradient of yellow to orange-red hues.

The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and prominent venation. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles.

The subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.
