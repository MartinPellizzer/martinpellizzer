Albertinia oleaster

Albertinia oleaster, commonly known as the yellow wild bean, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, hairy pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological contributions to its native habitat.
