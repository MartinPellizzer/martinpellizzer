Averia longipes

Averia longipes, commonly known as the long-footed averia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, aiding in water conservation. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a prominent central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Averia longipes is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
