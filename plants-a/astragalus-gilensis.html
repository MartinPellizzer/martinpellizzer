Astragalus gilensis

Astragalus gilensis, commonly known as Gil's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus gilensis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
