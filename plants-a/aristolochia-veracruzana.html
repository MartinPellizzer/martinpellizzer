Aristolochia veracruzana

Aristolochia veracruzana, commonly known as the Mexican birthwort, is a perennial climbing vine native to Central America and northern South America.

It features heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and produces distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-brown with a strong, unpleasant odor. The plant is part of the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its complex floral structure that attracts specific pollinators. It contains various bioactive compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

However, it is important to note that some species within this genus are toxic and should be handled with care.
