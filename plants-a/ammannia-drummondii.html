Ammannia drummondii

Ammannia drummondii, commonly known as prairie ammannia, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in prairies and along stream banks. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Ammannia drummondii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
