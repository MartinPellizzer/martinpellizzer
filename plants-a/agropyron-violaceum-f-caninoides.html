Agropyron violaceum f. caninoides

Agropyron violaceum f. caninoides, commonly known as the purple wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a purple hue, especially along the edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is of interest in ecological and agricultural studies due to its resilience and potential for forage use.
