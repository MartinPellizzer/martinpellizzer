Astragalus aboriginorum var. lepagei

Astragalus aboriginorum var. lepagei, commonly known as the Northern Sand Verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry, sandy soils, and it often grows in open habitats such as grasslands and dunes.
