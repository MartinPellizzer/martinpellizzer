Aconitum hicksii

Aconitum hicksii, commonly known as the Hick's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum hicksii is also notable for containing toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to handle or ingest.
