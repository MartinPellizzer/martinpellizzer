Antennaria corymbosa

Antennaria corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered willow herb, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of many small florets. The plant features a mat-forming growth habit with slender, hairy stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color and are arranged in corymbs, giving the plant its scientific name.

Antennaria corymbosa is often found in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and wet meadows.
