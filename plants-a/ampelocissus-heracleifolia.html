Ampelocissus heracleifolia

Ampelocissus heracleifolia, commonly known as the climbing oxalis, is a vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive leaves that are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are heart-shaped. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental purposes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological and horticultural studies.
