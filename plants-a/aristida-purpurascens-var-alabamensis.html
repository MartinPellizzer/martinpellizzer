Aristida purpurascens var. alabamensis

Aristida purpurascens var. alabamensis, commonly known as the Alabama threeawn, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pink to reddish in color. The plant thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and along roadsides. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive purplish tinge along the leaf margins.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.
