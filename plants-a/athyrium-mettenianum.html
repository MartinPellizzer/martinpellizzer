Athyrium mettenianum

Athyrium mettenianum, commonly known as the Japanese painted fern, is a fern species native to East Asia.

It features delicate, bipinnate fronds with a distinctive blackish-brown rachis and bright green pinnae. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture. Its foliage resembles brush strokes, giving it a unique and artistic appearance.

It is well-suited for woodland gardens and requires well-draining, acidic soil.
