Arisaema masisiense

Arisaema masisiense, commonly known as the Sierra Leone arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to western Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and is often found in tropical regions. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture, while its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some economic importance in local communities.
