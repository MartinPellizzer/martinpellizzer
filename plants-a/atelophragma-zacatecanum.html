Atelophragma zacatecanum

Atelophragma zacatecanum, commonly known as the Zacatecan tamarind, is a small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and horticulture.
