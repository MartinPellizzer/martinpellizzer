Agropyron brandzae

Agropyron brandzae, commonly known as the Brandzae's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing a high degree of drought tolerance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

Agropyron brandzae is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in preventing soil erosion in harsh climates.
