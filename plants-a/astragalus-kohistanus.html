Astragalus kohistanus

Astragalus kohistanus, commonly known as the Kohistan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by pod fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
