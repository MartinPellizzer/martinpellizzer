Acalypha indica var. minima

Acalypha indica var. minima, commonly known as the small-leaved Indian mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive reddish-brown stem. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
