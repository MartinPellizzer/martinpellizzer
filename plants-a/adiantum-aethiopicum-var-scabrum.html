Adiantum aethiopicum var. scabrum

Adiantum aethiopicum var. scabrum, commonly known as the scaly maidenhair fern, is a species native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, bioluminescent fronds that are often covered in a fine, scaly texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, feathery appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and air-purifying properties.
