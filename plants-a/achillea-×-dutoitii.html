Achillea × dutoitii

Achillea × dutoitii, commonly known as the Dutoit's yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and long-lasting blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Achillea × dutoitii is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
