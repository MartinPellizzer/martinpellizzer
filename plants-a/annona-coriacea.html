Annona coriacea

Annona coriacea, commonly known as the rough custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces large, rough-skinned fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with a strong scent.

The flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are borne in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.
