Acrolophia fimbriata

Acrolophia fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fimbriated (fringed) petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or grasslands. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive, spurred lip that aids in pollination.

Acrolophia fimbriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
