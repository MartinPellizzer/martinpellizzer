Athyrium oppositipennum var. pubescens

Athyrium oppositipennum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as woodlands or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are wiry and allow it to spread slowly through underground growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
