Aristida corradii

Aristida corradii, commonly known as the narrowleaf threeawn, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Aristida corradii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
