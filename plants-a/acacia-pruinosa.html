Acacia pruinosa

Acacia pruinosa, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems covered in a fine, whitish bloom that gives it a silvery appearance. The leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, oval leaflets that are usually clustered in groups of three. Its yellow flowers, which appear in late winter to early spring, are produced in globular or cylindrical clusters.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and a preference for well-drained soils.
