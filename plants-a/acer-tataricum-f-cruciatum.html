Acer tataricum f. cruciatum

Acer tataricum f. cruciatum, commonly known as the Tatarian maple, is a distinctive variant of the Tatarian maple species.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring deeply lobed, cross-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant is native to regions of eastern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage displays vibrant colors in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
