Asarum acuminatum

Asarum acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the forests of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic underground tuber that is used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate forest understories.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
