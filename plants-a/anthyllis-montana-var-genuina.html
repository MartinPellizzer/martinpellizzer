Anthyllis montana var. genuina

Anthyllis montana var. genuina, commonly known as the mountain anthyllis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
