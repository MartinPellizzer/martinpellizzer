Acacia curvifructa

Acacia curvifructa, commonly known as the warty acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by distinctive, warty pods. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia curvifructa is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
