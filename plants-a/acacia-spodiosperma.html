Acacia spodiosperma

Acacia spodiosperma, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and narrow, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sandy habitats.

Acacia spodiosperma is also valued for its hardy nature and is used in ornamental gardening.
