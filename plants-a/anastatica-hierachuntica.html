Anastatica hierachuntica

Anastatica hierachuntica, commonly known as the resurrection plant, is a unique species of liverwort that exhibits remarkable desiccation tolerance.

It can survive extreme drought by curling into a tight, desiccated ball, appearing dead, but can revive when water is available. This plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, particularly in regions with seasonal rainfall. Its ability to survive dehydration makes it an interesting subject in botanical research on stress tolerance.

The plant's life cycle involves both sexual and asexual reproduction, contributing to its adaptability in harsh conditions.
