Anchusa ochroleuca var. canescens

Anchusa ochroleuca var. canescens, commonly known as the gray anchusa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
