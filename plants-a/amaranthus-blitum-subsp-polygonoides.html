Amaranthus blitum subsp. polygonoides

Amaranthus blitum subsp.

polygonoides, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive red-tinged stems, which are often hairy.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This species is known for its adaptability and can be both a weed and a potential forage plant.
