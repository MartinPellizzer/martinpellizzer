Acacia robusta subsp. clavigera

Acacia robusta subsp.

clavigera, commonly known as the broad-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
