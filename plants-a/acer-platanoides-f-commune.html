Acer platanoides f. commune

Acer platanoides f. commune, commonly known as the European horse chestnut, is a cultivar of the horse chestnut tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are typically light green and turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces showy, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a spiny capsule containing glossy, chestnut-like seeds.

This cultivar is often planted for its ornamental value and is relatively resistant to pests compared to other horse chestnut varieties.
