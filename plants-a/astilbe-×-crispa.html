Astilbe × crispa

Astilbe × crispa, commonly known as the hybrid astilbe, is a deciduous perennial shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that bear dense clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant produces feathery, plume-like inflorescences that add ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it ideal for woodland gardens.

Astilbe × crispa is valued for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms, which can persist through late summer.
