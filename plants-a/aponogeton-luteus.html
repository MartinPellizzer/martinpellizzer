Aponogeton luteus

Aponogeton luteus, commonly known as the golden arrowhead, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped, bright yellow leaves that float on the water's surface. The plant has long, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often rooted in the substrate. It is known for its ability to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Aponogeton luteus is also cultivated as an ornamental plant in ponds and water gardens.
