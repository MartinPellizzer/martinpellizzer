Achyronia pappeana

Achyronia pappeana, commonly known as the desert willow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features grayish bark and produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, or purple. Its leaves are narrow and often have a fuzzy texture, adapted to reduce water loss.

The desert willow is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ability to withstand drought conditions.
