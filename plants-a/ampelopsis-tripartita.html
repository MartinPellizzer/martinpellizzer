Ampelopsis tripartita

Ampelopsis tripartita, commonly known as the three-lobed honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its vigorous growth makes it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in some regions.
