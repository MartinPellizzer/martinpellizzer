Arenaria Erinacea Subsp. Cantabrica

Arenaria erinacea subsp. cantabrica is a low-growing flowering plant native to the coastal regions of northern Spain and southern France. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in rocky coastal habitats. This subspecies is known for its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its compact form and hardy nature make it a resilient plant in harsh maritime environments. It is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and conservation efforts.
