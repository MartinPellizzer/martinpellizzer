Aruncus dioicus

Aruncus dioicus, commonly known as goat's beard, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features tall, arching stems with dense, feathery clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has large, deeply lobed leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Aruncus dioicus is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
