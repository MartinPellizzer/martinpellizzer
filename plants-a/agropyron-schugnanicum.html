Agropyron schugnanicum

Agropyron schugnanicum, commonly known as the Schugnan needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppe and desert regions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Agropyron schugnanicum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
