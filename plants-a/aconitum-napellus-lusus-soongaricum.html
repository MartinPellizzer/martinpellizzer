Aconitum napellus lusus soongaricum

Aconitum napellus lusus soongaricum, commonly known as the Siberian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers. The plant has dark green, palmately lobed leaves and produces toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and studied for its pharmacological potential.
