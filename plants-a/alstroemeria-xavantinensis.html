Alstroemeria xavantinensis

Alstroemeria xavantinensis, commonly known as the Xavantina lily, is a perennial plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, strap-like leaves arranged in a fan shape.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
