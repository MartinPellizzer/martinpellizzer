Aster pinnatus

Aster pinnatus, commonly known as the Mexican feathergrass, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery plumes that give it a soft, airy appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late summer. Aster pinnatus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique structure and ornamental value make it a versatile plant for both landscape design and container gardening.
