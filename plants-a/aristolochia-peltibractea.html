Aristolochia peltibractea

Aristolochia peltibractea, commonly known as the broad-bract arrowhead vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and unique, spiral-shaped flowers that resemble a narrow tube. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often covered in a thick, waxy coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found climbing through dense vegetation.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex chemical compounds, some of which have medicinal uses.
