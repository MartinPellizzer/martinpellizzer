Agapetes variegata var. glabrata

Agapetes variegata var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude forests of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, glossy leaves that are often marked with silvery or pale veins. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which appear in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are typically smooth and lack the hairy texture found in other varieties of Agapetes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
