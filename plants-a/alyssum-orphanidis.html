Alyssum orphanidis

Alyssum orphanidis, commonly known as the Orphanis alyssum, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Alyssum orphanidis is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy leaves and a compact, mat-like form.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
