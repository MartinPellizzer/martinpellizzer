Asteropeia micraster var. angustifolia

Asteropeia micraster var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved asteropeia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with serrated edges. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, while its flowers are small and clustered, producing a subtle pinkish hue.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
