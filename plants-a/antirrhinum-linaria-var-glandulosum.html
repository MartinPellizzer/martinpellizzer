Antirrhinum linaria var. glandulosum

Antirrhinum linaria var. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular antirrhinum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive texture and aid in water retention. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or red in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
