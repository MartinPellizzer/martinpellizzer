Astragalus rhabdophorus subsp. fallicinus

Astragalus rhabdophorus subsp.

fallicinus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of western North America. It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

The plant has slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinators.
