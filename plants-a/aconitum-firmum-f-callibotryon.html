Aconitum firmum f. callibotryon

Aconitum firmum f. callibotryon, commonly known as the Alpine Aconite, is a variant of the Aconitum genus native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy margin and is known for its toxic alkaloids.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in high-altitude ecosystems.
