Abies firma var. jezoensis

Abies firma var. jezoensis, commonly known as the Japanese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dense, dark green needles that are arranged in distinctive spirals. The tree produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ornamental horticulture and ecological restoration.
