Ageratum corymbosum

Ageratum corymbosum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and the southern United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to blue and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ageratum corymbosum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
