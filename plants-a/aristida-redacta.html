Aristida redacta

Aristida redacta, commonly known as the redgrass, is a species of grass native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry blades and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats or tufts. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

Aristida redacta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
