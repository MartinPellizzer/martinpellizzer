Adiantum falcinellum

Adiantum falcinellum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern native to the eastern United States.

It features finely divided, arching fronds that are dark green and have a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Adiantum falcinellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
