Aristolochia ceropegioides

Aristolochia ceropegioides, commonly known as the Chinese birthwort, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe or a funnel, which is a defining feature of the Aristolochiaceae family. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
