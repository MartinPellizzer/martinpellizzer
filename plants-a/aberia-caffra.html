Aberia caffra

Aberia caffra, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows in shrubland and savanna habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, and it produces small, cylindrical fruits that contain seeds.

Aberia caffra is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and resilience.
