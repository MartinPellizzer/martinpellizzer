Arisarum vulgare subsp. clusii

Arisarum vulgare subsp.

clusii, commonly known as the yellow false shamrock, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe. It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and spadix-like inflorescence.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a yellow spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage, which displays a vibrant yellow color.
