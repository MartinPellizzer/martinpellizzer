Alchemilla cinerea

Alchemilla cinerea, commonly known as silver woundwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Alchemilla cinerea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
