Andicolea

Andicolea, commonly known as the false broomrape, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients, typically growing on the roots of grasses and other herbaceous species. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually white or pale green, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain minute seeds. Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant ecological impacts on its host plants.
