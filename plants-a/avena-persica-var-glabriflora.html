Avena persica var. glabriflora

Avena persica var. glabriflora, commonly known as the Persian oat or silky oat, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a soft, silky texture. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
