Aconitum hosteanum

Aconitum hosteanum, commonly known as the Hostaean monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hooded flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and kept away from children and pets.
