Anthemis × artemisifolia

Anthemis × artemisifolia, commonly known as golden marguerite, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, which typically feature bright yellow centers surrounded by white or pale yellow ray florets. The plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, exhibiting a compact growth habit.

Its foliage is aromatic, with a slightly bitter scent, and it is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
