Ampelocissus schliebenii

Ampelocissus schliebenii, commonly known as the Schlieben's fig-leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and attractive vine.
