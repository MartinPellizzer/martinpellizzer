Acacia juncifolia subsp. serpentinicola

Acacia juncifolia subsp.

serpentinicola, commonly known as the serpentine acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers that appear in globular inflorescences.

This subspecies is adapted to grow in nutrient-poor, calcareous soils, particularly on serpentine rock formations. Its ability to thrive in such harsh conditions makes it an important component of these unique ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of traditional medicine.
