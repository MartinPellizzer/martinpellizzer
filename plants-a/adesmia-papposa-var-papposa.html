Adesmia papposa var. papposa

Adesmia papposa var. papposa, commonly known as the hairy adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features small, clustered flowers with white petals and fluffy, hairy sepals. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooms.
