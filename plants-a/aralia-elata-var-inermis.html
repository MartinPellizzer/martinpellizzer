Aralia elata var. inermis

Aralia elata var. inermis, commonly known as the Japanese angelica tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves that are typically pinnately arranged, with serrated margins and a dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
