Astragalus acinacifer var. racemosus

Astragalus acinacifer var. racemosus, commonly known as the racemose milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are clusters of flowers along a central stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
