Andinocleome chilensis

Andinocleome chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean cleome, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and showy, yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
