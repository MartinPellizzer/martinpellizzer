Allium roseum var. insulare

Allium roseum var. insulare, commonly known as the Island chive, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its mild garlic scent and is often grown for ornamental purposes in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This variety is also valued for its culinary use, adding a subtle flavor to dishes.
