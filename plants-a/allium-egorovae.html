Allium egorovae

Allium egorovae, commonly known as the Egorov's onion, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly Kazakhstan. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Its bulbous roots are edible and have a mild onion-like flavor.

Allium egorovae is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
