Acacia salicina var. wayi

Acacia salicina var. wayi, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and wind exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.
