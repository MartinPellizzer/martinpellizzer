Artemisia campestris var. douglasiana

Artemisia campestris var. douglasiana, commonly known as Douglas's wormwood, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features silver-gray foliage with finely divided, aromatic leaves that emit a distinctive, often bitter scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
