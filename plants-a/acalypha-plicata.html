Acalypha plicata

Acalypha plicata, commonly known as the zigzag hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and zigzag-creased leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, hibiscus-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and yellow, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Acalypha plicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
