Alsophila submarginalis var. crespiana

Alsophila submarginalis var. crespiana, commonly known as the Crespina tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive trunk that is covered with remnants of old leaf bases, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
