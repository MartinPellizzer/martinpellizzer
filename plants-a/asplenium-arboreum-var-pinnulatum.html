Asplenium arboreum var. pinnulatum

Asplenium arboreum var. pinnulatum, commonly known as the clustered maidenhair fern, is a variant of the tree fern Asplenium arboreum.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The fronds are typically dark green and have a finely divided, pinnately arranged structure. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
