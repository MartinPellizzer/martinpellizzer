Alyssum pinifolium

Alyssum pinifolium, commonly known as mountain alpine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Alyssum pinifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
