Aspilia mortonii

Aspilia mortonii, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange ray flowers surrounded by a dense cluster of small, disk flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Aspilia mortonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
