Adonis annua subsp. castellana

Adonis annua subsp.

castellana, commonly known as the Castilian autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant produces distinctive, helmet-shaped flowers with three narrow petals and a prominent, spurred lower petal. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and often covered in a fine, white down.

The plant is adapted to dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
