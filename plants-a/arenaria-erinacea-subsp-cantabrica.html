Arenaria erinacea subsp. cantabrica

Arenaria erinacea subsp.

cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It thrives in rocky, coastal, and sandy habitats, often found in areas with high exposure to wind and salt.

The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience and is often used in ecological restoration projects in coastal regions.
