Abutilon foetidum

Abutilon foetidum, commonly known as the stinkweed or stinkbush, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is more noticeable when the flowers are in full bloom.

Abutilon foetidum is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
