Augusta austrocaledonica

Augusta austrocaledonica, commonly known as the Kauri tree, is a large evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Araucariaceae and is characterized by its straight trunk, broad, feather-like leaves, and distinctive cone-like structures. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a slow growth rate, making it one of the longest-living tree species. Its bark is thick and fibrous, providing protection against pests and environmental stress.

The plant plays a significant ecological role in New Zealand's forests, supporting a diverse range of flora and fauna.
