Acacia hydaspica

Acacia hydaspica, commonly known as the Indian acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular inflorescences, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

Acacia hydaspica is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fuel.
