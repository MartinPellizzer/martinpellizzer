Aspidium platynotus

Aspidium platynotus, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, almost lace-like appearance.

Aspidium platynotus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
