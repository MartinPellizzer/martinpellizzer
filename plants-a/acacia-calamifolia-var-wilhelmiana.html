Acacia calamifolia var. wilhelmiana

Acacia calamifolia var. wilhelmiana, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, almost silky appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The bark and leaves have been used traditionally in some indigenous communities for various purposes.
