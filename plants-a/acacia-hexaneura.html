Acacia hexaneura

Acacia hexaneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, bipinnate leaves that are typically green with a slight blue or greyish tinge. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Its seed pods are slender and straight, containing small, flat seeds.

Acacia hexaneura is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of native Australian flora.
