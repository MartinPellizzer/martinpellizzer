Artanthe bartlingiana

Artanthe bartlingiana, commonly known as the Bartling's artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers range in color from pink to red.

Artanthe bartlingiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
