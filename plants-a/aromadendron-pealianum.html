Aromadendron pealianum

Aromadendron pealianum, commonly known as the pealianum myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
