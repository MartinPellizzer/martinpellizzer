Aeschynomene histrix var. incana

Aeschynomene histrix var. incana, commonly known as hairy goatweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

Its fibrous root system and ability to fix nitrogen make it a useful plant in certain ecological contexts.
