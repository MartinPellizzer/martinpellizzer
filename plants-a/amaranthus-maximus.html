Amaranthus maximus

Amaranthus maximus, commonly known as love-lies-bleeding, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its tall, slender stalks and vibrant red bracts.

It is native to the Americas and belongs to the Amaranthaceae family, which includes many species with edible leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into spiny, triangular seeds. Its rapid growth and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The leaves are also edible and rich in nutrients, making it a versatile plant for both ornamental and culinary use.
