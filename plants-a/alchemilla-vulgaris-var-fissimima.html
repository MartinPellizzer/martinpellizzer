Alchemilla vulgaris var. fissimima

Alchemilla vulgaris var. fissimima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, serrated leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in summer, which are borne on slender, upright stalks. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, whitish bloom, giving it a powdery appearance.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
