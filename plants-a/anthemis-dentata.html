Anthemis dentata

Anthemis dentata, commonly known as the toothed chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, and it produces a strong, aromatic scent. The plant has serrated leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and herbal remedies.
