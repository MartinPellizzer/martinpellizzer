Acacia azak

Acacia azak, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and its distinctive, flat, pale green phyllodes that resemble leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The wood of Acacia azak is hard and durable, historically used by indigenous communities for various purposes.
