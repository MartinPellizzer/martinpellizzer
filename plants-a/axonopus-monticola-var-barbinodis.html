Axonopus monticola var. barbinodis

Axonopus monticola var. barbinodis, commonly known as the mountain axonopus, is a grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, creeping stems that often form dense mats. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
