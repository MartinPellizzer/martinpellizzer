Abildgaardia metalliphila

Abildgaardia metalliphila, commonly known as the metalliferous plant, is a rare and specialized species native to metalliferous soils.

It is characterized by its ability to hyperaccumulate heavy metals such as zinc and cadmium, making it an important subject in phytoremediation studies. The plant typically grows in acidic, nutrient-poor environments, often found in areas with high metal contamination. Its morphology includes small, clustered leaves and a low-growing habit, which helps it thrive in harsh conditions.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays a significant role in ecological research and environmental restoration efforts.
