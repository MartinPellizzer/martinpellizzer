Adiantum pellucidum

Adiantum pellucidum, commonly known as the glassy maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that are often translucent, giving it a glass-like appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Adiantum pellucidum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in controlled garden settings.
