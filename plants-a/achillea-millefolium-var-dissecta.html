Achillea millefolium var. dissecta

Achillea millefolium var. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, lacey appearance. The plant produces flat, composite flower heads composed of small white to pinkish florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its historical use in medicine and its ornamental value contribute to its enduring appeal in horticulture.
