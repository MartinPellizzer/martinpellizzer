Alpinia speciosa

Alpinia speciosa, commonly known as the fragrant ginger or Chinese ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a rhizomatous herb that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with lush, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in inflorescences. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice.

Botanically, it belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers.
