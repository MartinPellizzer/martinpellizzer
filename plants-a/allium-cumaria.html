Allium cumaria

Allium cumaria, commonly known as garlic chives, is a hardy perennial herb belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers in clusters, which appear in late spring to early summer. Its underground bulbs are similar to garlic and can be harvested for use in cooking.

Allium cumaria thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herb collections.
