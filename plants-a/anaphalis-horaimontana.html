Anaphalis horaimontana

Anaphalis horaimontana, commonly known as the mountain fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single blooms. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, high-altitude environments. Its silver-gray foliage and fluffy, white capitula make it visually distinctive.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as habitat for small invertebrates.
