Aconitum uncinatum var. japonicum

Aconitum uncinatum var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese monkshood, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features striking, helmet-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with deeply cut lobes, and its stems are covered in coarse hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
