Allium aegyptiacum

Allium aegyptiacum, commonly known as Egyptian garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a bulb composed of several fleshy, white, and edible cloves surrounded by a papery sheath. The plant produces tall, leafy stems that bear clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a strong, garlicky aroma.

Allium aegyptiacum is valued both for its culinary use and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
