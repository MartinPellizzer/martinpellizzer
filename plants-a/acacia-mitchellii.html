Acacia mitchellii

Acacia mitchellii, commonly known as the Mitchell's wattle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched structure and feathery foliage composed of small, narrow phyllodes. The plant produces yellow globular flowerheads that appear in late winter to early spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia mitchellii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in reforestation projects.
