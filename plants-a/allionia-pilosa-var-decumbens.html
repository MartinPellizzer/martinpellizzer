Allionia pilosa var. decumbens

Allionia pilosa var. decumbens, commonly known as the creeping allionia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats of foliage. The leaves are lance-shaped, covered in fine hairs, and often have a bluish-gray hue. Small white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters during late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
