Athamanta crinita

Athamanta crinita, commonly known as the silky hair grass, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and long, silky hair-like leaves that give it its common name. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded or partially shaded environments. Athamanta crinita is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
