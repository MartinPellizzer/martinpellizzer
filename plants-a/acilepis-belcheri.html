Acilepis belcheri

Acilepis belcheri, commonly known as the Belcher's acacia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Rodrigues in the Indian Ocean.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and dry habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are characteristic of the Acacieae tribe. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local insect and bird populations.
