Avenochloa armeniaca

Avenochloa armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian wild oat, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Avenochloa armeniaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
