Allium victorialis var. angustifolium

Allium victorialis var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers in umbel-like clusters during the summer months. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

This variety is valued for its mild garlic flavor and is used in culinary and ornamental gardening.
