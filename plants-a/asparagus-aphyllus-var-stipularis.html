Asparagus aphyllus var. stipularis

Asparagus aphyllus var. stipularis, commonly known as the leafless asparagus, is a unique variant of the asparagus genus characterized by its reduced foliage.

This plant is native to South Africa and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its most distinguishing feature is the absence of true leaves, with photosynthesis occurring through its modified stems and stipules. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unusual appearance and low maintenance requirements.
