Ambrosia camphorata

Ambrosia camphorata, commonly known as the camphor tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong camphor scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its medicinal and aromatic properties, it has been used in traditional medicine and for its essential oils.
