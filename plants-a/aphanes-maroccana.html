Aphanes maroccana

Aphanes maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan sandweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring, and its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats.
