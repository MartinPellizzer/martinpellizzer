Achillea millefolium subsp. tanacetifolia

Achillea millefolium subsp.

tanacetifolia, commonly known as yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It features a basal rosette of silver-gray, finely divided leaves that resemble ferns, and it produces clusters of small, white or yellowish flower heads.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic, sometimes slightly bitter scent and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
