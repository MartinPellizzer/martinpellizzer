Aconitum flexicaule

Aconitum flexicaule, commonly known as the flexicaule monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple hooded flowers, which are typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and deeply divided. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum flexicaule is also noted for its toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
