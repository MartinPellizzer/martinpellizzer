Arthromeris salicifolia

Arthromeris salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking inflorescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the arum family. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Arthromeris salicifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.
