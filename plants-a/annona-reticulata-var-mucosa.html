Annona reticulata var. mucosa

Annona reticulata var. mucosa, commonly known as the rough custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, rough, greenish-yellow fruits with a dense, bumpy skin. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the edible, fleshy fruit. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its unique flavor and ornamental value.
