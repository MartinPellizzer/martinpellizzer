Arctostaphylos pechoensis

Arctostaphylos pechoensis, commonly known as the Pecho manzanita, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its woody stems and evergreen leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Arctostaphylos pechoensis is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and well-drained soils.
