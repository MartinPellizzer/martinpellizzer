Alhagi tournefortii

Alhagi tournefortii, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to high temperatures and low precipitation.

Alhagi tournefortii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in desert ecosystems.
