Asarum canadense var. ambiguum

Asarum canadense var. ambiguum, commonly known as the Canadian wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, reddish-purple leaves that are often covered in a dense, hairy coating. The plant produces small, maroon-colored flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by dark, warty berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its aromatic rhizomes and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
