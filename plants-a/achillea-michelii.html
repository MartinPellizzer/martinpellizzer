Achillea michelii

Achillea michelii, commonly known as mountain yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It has a fibrous root system and produces a strong, aromatic scent.

Achillea michelii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
