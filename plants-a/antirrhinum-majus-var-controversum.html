Antirrhinum majus var. controversum

Antirrhinum majus var. controversum, commonly known as the "snapdragon" or "dog's mouth," is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, yellow, orange, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
