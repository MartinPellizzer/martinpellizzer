Astragalus tongolensis

Astragalus tongolensis, commonly known as the Tibetan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet and adjacent areas of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus tongolensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Tibetan medicine.
