Acaena pinnatifida subsp. hypoleuca

Acaena pinnatifida subsp.

hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered acaena, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, alternate leaves with a pinnate arrangement.

The plant produces yellowish to white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including dry forests and shrublands, and is often found in coastal areas.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reduced floral structures and pale flower color compared to other Acaena species.
