Arabis × palezieuxii

Arabis × palezieuxii, commonly known as the hybrid rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed, and it is known for its hardy nature.

Arabis × palezieuxii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
