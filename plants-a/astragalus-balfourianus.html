Astragalus balfourianus

Astragalus balfourianus, commonly known as Balfour's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It produces pods that contain small, hard seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Astragalus balfourianus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
