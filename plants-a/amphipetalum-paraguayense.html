Amphipetalum paraguayense

Amphipetalum paraguayense, commonly known as the Paraguay morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces bright blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, red or orange capsule that splits open when mature.
