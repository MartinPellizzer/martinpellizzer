Amaranthus littoralis

Amaranthus littoralis, commonly known as sea amaranth, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and succulent, fleshy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and it thrives in saline or brackish environments. It is commonly cultivated for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients and used in various culinary traditions.

Amaranthus littoralis is also valued for its ability to stabilize coastal soils and prevent erosion.
