Alchemilla arvensis var. microcarpa

Alchemilla arvensis var. microcarpa, commonly known as lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal rosettes of oval, gray-green leaves with a velvety texture and finely toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are borne on slender, hairy stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological gardens and wetland restoration projects.
