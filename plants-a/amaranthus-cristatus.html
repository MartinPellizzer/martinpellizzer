Amaranthus cristatus

Amaranthus cristatus, commonly known as the cockscomb plant, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, plume-like inflorescence that resembles a comb or a rooster's comb, giving it its common name. The plant produces vibrant red, orange, or yellow flowers, which are often used in ornamental gardening. It is a hardy annual that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

Amaranthus cristatus is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of edible seeds.
