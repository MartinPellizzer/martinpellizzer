Arytera bullata

Arytera bullata, commonly known as the bullate arum, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, giving them a glossy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale greenish-white flowers that are arranged in an inflorescence resembling a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation in humid environments.

The plant's botanical features are adapted to thrive in shaded, moist conditions, reflecting its natural habitat in tropical forests.
