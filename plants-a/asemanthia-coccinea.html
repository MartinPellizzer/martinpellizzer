Asemanthia coccinea

Asemanthia coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet pomegranate, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its surroundings. The plant produces small, round fruits that resemble pomegranates, though they are not edible. Its leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Asemanthia coccinea thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
