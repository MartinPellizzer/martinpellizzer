Acacia erythrocalyx

Acacia erythrocalyx, commonly known as the red-fruited acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular inflorescences, followed by reddish, woody pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to seasonal droughts, making it a resilient species in arid environments.

Acacia erythrocalyx is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some regions.
