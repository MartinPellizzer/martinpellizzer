Aporum salicornioides

Aporum salicornioides, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, grass-like leaves and showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in sandy or rocky coastal habitats. Its adaptability to harsh coastal conditions makes it a resilient species in maritime environments.

Aporum salicornioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing dunes.
