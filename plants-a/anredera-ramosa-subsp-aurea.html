Anredera ramosa subsp. aurea

Anredera ramosa subsp.

aurea, commonly known as the golden anredera, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage and showy blooms.

It thrives in warm, humid conditions and prefers well-drained soil.
