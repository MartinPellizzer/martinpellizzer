Aloe vivipara

Aloe vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water. The plant produces offsets, or "pups," at the base of its stems, which can be easily propagated. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and appear in terminal clusters.

Aloe vivipara thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and low-maintenance plant for gardens.
