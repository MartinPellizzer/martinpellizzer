Amyema maritima

Amyema maritima, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal regions, particularly in sandy soils. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons, which are arranged in cylindrical spikes. Amyema maritima is well adapted to salty and windy environments, making it a resilient species in maritime climates.

Its botanical features include compound leaves and a woody structure that helps it withstand harsh coastal conditions.
