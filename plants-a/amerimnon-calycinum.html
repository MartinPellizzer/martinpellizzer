Amerimnon calycinum

Amerimnon calycinum, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, cascading clusters of golden-yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

The vine is also noted for its fragrant blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
