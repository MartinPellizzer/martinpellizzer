Artemisia vulgaris subsp. lindleyana

Artemisia vulgaris subsp.

lindleyana, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and aromatic, feathery leaves that emit a strong, camphor-like scent.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
