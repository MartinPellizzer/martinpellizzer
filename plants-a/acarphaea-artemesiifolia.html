Acarphaea artemesiifolia

Acarphaea artemesiifolia, commonly known as the creeping marsh grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
