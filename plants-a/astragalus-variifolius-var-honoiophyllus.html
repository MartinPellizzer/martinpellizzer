Astragalus variifolius var. honoiophyllus

Astragalus variifolius var. honoiophyllus, commonly known as the Honoiophyllus milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with varying leaflet shapes. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is deep and drought-tolerant, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in dry environments.
