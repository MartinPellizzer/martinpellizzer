Astragalus phaulanthus

Astragalus phaulanthus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in sandy or rocky soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.

Astragalus phaulanthus is also of interest in ecological and botanical studies due to its resilience and unique adaptations.
