Acantholimon scirpinum

Acantholimon scirpinum, commonly known as the slender stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the rocky and sandy soils of the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fleshy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to August. The plant has succulent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, helping it retain moisture in arid environments. It is well adapted to coastal and inland areas with poor soil conditions.

Acantholimon scirpinum is often found in dry grasslands, cliffs, and dunes, where it thrives in full sun.
