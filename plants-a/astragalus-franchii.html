Astragalus franchii

Astragalus franchii, commonly known as the Franch's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several small, rounded seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

Astragalus franchii is valued for its role in ecological systems and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
