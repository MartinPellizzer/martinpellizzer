Acarna leucographa

Acarna leucographa, commonly known as the white-streaked acarna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green stripe running along the midrib. Its blooms are typically pink to purple in color and are borne in terminal clusters.

Acarna leucographa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
