Aristolochia brevipes var. wrightii

Aristolochia brevipes var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's broadleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and unique, pipe-like flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that mature to a dark purple or black hue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
