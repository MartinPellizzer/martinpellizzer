Achnatherum conspicuum

Achnatherum conspicuum, commonly known as the slender wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed sites. The plant has slender, wiry stems that are often leafless above, with small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Achnatherum conspicuum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
