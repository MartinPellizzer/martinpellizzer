Achyranthes sericea

Achyranthes sericea, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a weed in many parts of the world.
