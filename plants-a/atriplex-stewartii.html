Atriplex stewartii

Atriplex stewartii, commonly known as Stewart's saltbush, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is adapted to arid and saline environments, often found in desert and coastal areas. The plant has succulent leaves that help reduce water loss, and its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences. It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
