Achillea micrantha

Achillea micrantha, commonly known as narrowleaf yarrow, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical name honors the legendary Greek hero Achilles, who was said to have used the plant to treat wounds.
