Alchemilla longipedicellata

Alchemilla longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-stemmed lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in summer, which are arranged in elongated pedicels. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla longipedicellata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
