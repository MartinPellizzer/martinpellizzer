Arabis glabra subsp. pseudoturritis

Arabis glabra subsp.

pseudoturritis, commonly known as the Turkish rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the eastern Mediterranean. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many species with characteristic cruciform flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are basal, linear to lanceolate, and often covered in a fine pubescence.

The flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in racemes, producing a delicate, early spring bloom.
