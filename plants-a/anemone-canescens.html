Anemone canescens

Anemone canescens, commonly known as the hoary anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, grayish-green foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, shady woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade.

Anemone canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
