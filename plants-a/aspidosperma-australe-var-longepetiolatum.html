Aspidosperma australe var. longepetiolatum

Aspidosperma australe var. longepetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled aspidosperma, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves with long petioles. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are follicles that contain seeds with a fleshy, red aril.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some regions.
