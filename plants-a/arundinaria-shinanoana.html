Arundinaria shinanoana

Arundinaria shinanoana, commonly known as the Shinano bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant typically reaches a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
