Achillea × hausmanniana

Achillea × hausmanniana, commonly known as the German yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green foliage and flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pinkish-red. Native to Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. The plant produces small, herbaceous flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a favored addition to many garden landscapes.
