Achyranthes ogatai

Achyranthes ogatai, commonly known as the Japanese knotweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense spikes. It is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a problematic species in many regions.
