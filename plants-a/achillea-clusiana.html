Achillea clusiana

Achillea clusiana, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are finely divided and covered in a powdery coating. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to white, arranged in flat-topped clusters.

Achillea clusiana is often found in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky areas, and it is valued for its medicinal properties and ornamental appeal.
