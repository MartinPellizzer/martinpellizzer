Agrostis alba var. flaccidula

Agrostis alba var. flaccidula, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a variant of the silvergrass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing, perennial grass that typically forms dense, compact clumps with slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological systems supporting various insect and bird species.
