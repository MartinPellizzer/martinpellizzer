Acanthopanax stenophyllus

Acanthopanax stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed devil's claw, is a climbing shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces bright red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
