Abildgaardia rhizomatosa

Abildgaardia rhizomatosa, commonly known as the creeping marsh orchid, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred lip, typical of the Orchidaceae family. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often growing in dense mats near the water's edge.

Abildgaardia rhizomatosa thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes, fens, and along riverbanks.
