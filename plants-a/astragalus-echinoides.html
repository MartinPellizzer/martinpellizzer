Astragalus echinoides

Astragalus echinoides, commonly known as the spiny milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow or cream-colored flowers in early summer. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, rounded seeds.

Astragalus echinoides plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
