Aulacocalyx divergens

Aulacocalyx divergens, commonly known as the broad-leaved flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and glossy. The plant produces striking clusters of orange-red flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release small, winged seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
