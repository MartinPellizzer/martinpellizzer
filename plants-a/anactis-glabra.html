Anactis glabra

Anactis glabra, commonly known as the smooth anactis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and typically herbaceous growth habit. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in smooth, hairless foliage. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Anactis glabra is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky outcrops.
