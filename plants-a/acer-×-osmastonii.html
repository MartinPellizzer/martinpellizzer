Acer × osmastonii

Acer × osmastonii, commonly known as the Osmon's maple, is a hybrid deciduous tree resulting from the cross between Acer caesium and Acer negundo.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring bright yellow to orange-red foliage in autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 5–10 meters and has a spreading, rounded crown. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and parks.
