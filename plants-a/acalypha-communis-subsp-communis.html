Acalypha communis subsp. communis

Acalypha communis subsp.

communis, commonly known as the common copperleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its striking copper-colored foliage, which gives it its common name.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Acalypha communis subsp.

communis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
