Allium scabriscapum

Allium scabriscapum, commonly known as the rough-capped leek, is a perennial plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in culinary applications, similar to other allium species. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible qualities, it is cultivated in gardens and used in various cuisines.
