Argentina brassii

Argentina brassii, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a peppery taste. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it can outcompete native flora and alter local ecosystems.
