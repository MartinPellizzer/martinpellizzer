Afroaster lydenburgensis

Afroaster lydenburgensis, commonly known as the Lydenburg aster, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Afroaster lydenburgensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
