Achyranthes margaretarum

Achyranthes margaretarum, commonly known as Margaret's smartweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and roadside ditches. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are borne on slender, branching stems. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Margaret's smartweed is known for its ability to thrive in moist soils and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
