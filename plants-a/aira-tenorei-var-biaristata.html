Aira tenorei var. biaristata

Aira tenorei var. biaristata, commonly known as the Sicilian grass or Sicilian aira, is a perennial grass species native to southern Italy.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
