Achillea canescens

Achillea canescens, commonly known as gray yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine, grayish-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, fluffy white or pale yellow flowers bloom in flat-topped clusters during the summer months.

Achillea canescens is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
