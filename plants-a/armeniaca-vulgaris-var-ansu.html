Armeniaca vulgaris var. ansu

Armeniaca vulgaris var. ansu, commonly known as the Chinese date or jujube.

This plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China, Korea, and Southeast Asia. It produces small, round fruits that are rich in sugars, vitamins, and minerals. The botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and flowers that are typically pink or white.

The fruit is typically harvested in autumn and is valued for its sweet, edible pulp.
