Amorphophallus hetterscheidii

Amorphophallus hetterscheidii, commonly known as the African giant arrowroot, is a tuberous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces large, edible tubers that are used as a starch source in some regions. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, contributing to its common name.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy areas.
