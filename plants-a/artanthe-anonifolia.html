Artanthe anonifolia

Artanthe anonifolia, commonly known as the leafless artanthe, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. Despite its name, it does produce leaves, though they are small and often obscured by the dense floral display. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
