Andropogon minarum

Andropogon minarum, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and blue-green foliage, which turn reddish in the fall. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands. Andropogon minarum is a key component of native grassland ecosystems, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.

Its seed heads are often used in floral arrangements due to their ornamental appearance.
