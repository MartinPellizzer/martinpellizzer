Amelanchier asiatica var. sinica

Amelanchier asiatica var. sinica, commonly known as the Chinese serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, which are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. Its leaves are typically oval with a serrated edge, turning yellow in the fall.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for ornamental and culinary purposes.
