Antirrhinum spurium var. integrifolium

Antirrhinum spurium var. integrifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The leaves are typically broad and entire, giving the plant its varietal name "integrifolium.". It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
