Asplundia utilis

Asplundia utilis, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces striking inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. Its rhizomes are edible and have been traditionally used for their starchy texture and nutritional value.

Asplundia utilis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
