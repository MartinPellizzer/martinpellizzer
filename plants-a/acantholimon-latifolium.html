Acantholimon latifolium

Acantholimon latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf sandthorn, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, and its leaves are characterized by their broad, fleshy shape. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray, making it well-suited for coastal environments.
