Asplenium seelosii subsp. glabrum

Asplenium seelosii subsp.

glabrum, commonly known as the smooth maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its finely divided, bright green fronds. It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are leathery and have a smooth texture, distinguishing it from other varieties in the genus.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
