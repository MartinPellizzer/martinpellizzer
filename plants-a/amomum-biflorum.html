Amomum biflorum

Amomum biflorum, commonly known as the two-flowered cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant fruits that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Amomum biflorum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and valuable essential oils.
