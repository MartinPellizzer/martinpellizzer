Allium farsistanicum

Allium farsistanicum, commonly known as the Farsian garlic, is a perennial herb native to Iran.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
