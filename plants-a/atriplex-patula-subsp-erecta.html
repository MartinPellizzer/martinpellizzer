Atriplex patula subsp. erecta

Atriplex patula subsp.

erecta, commonly known as the upright saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in dry environments.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it well adapted to saline soils and harsh climates. Atriplex patula subsp.

erecta is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
