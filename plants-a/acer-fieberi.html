Acer fieberi

Acer fieberi, commonly known as the Chinese maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its vibrant autumn foliage. The tree typically grows to a height of 10-15 meters, with a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a serrated margin, and turn shades of yellow, orange, and red in the fall.

Acer fieberi is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
