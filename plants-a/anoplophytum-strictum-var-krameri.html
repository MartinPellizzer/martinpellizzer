Anoplophytum strictum var. krameri

Anoplophytum strictum var. krameri, commonly known as the Krameri's spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and fleshy, adapted for storing water in harsh environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in drought conditions.
