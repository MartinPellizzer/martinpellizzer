Allium atropurpureum var. hirtulum

Allium atropurpureum var. hirtulum, commonly known as purple onion grass, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, slender stems that bear narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, purple or pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its hairy texture, which is more pronounced in the varietal form hirtulum. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, onion-like flavor, which can be used in culinary applications.
