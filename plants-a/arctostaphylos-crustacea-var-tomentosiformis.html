Arctostaphylos crustacea var. tomentosiformis

Arctostaphylos crustacea var. tomentosiformis, commonly known as the coastal manzanita, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California and southern Oregon.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a rugged, low-growing form with thick, leathery leaves that are covered in a whitish, woolly coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with frequent fog and mild temperatures.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
