Annona punctata

Annona punctata, commonly known as the rough custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its rough, bark-like fruit. The plant produces large, yellowish-green fruits with a thick, spiny rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
