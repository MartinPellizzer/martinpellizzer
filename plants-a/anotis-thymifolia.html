Anotis thymifolia

Anotis thymifolia, commonly known as the thyme-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are aromatic, with a scent reminiscent of thyme, and are covered in fine hairs.

Anotis thymifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
