Acanthorhiza aculeata

Acanthorhiza aculeata, commonly known as the prickly root.

It is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its unique root system that forms a dense, cushion-like structure. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

The species is adapted to sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and pine forests.
