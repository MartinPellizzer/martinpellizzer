Arundinaria chrysantha

Arundinaria chrysantha, commonly known as the golden bamboo or yellow bamboo, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that are often tinged with a golden hue, especially during the growing season. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland areas or along stream banks.

Arundinaria chrysantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in basket-making.
