Aster chilensis subsp. hallii

Aster chilensis subsp.

hallii, commonly known as the Hall's aster, is a flowering plant native to the Andes of southern South America. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple to blue petals, which bloom in the spring and early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to the cool, dry conditions of its habitat.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
