Angraecum tamonii

Angraecum tamonii, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It features long, narrow leaves and produces elegant, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue with a dark center. The plant is adapted to grow on trees in humid, shaded environments, and it blooms during the dry season. Its flowers are pollinated by day-flying moths, a unique ecological relationship.

Angraecum tamonii is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its beauty and rarity.
