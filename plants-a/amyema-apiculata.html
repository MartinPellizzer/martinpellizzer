Amyema apiculata

Amyema apiculata, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow floral spikes. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Amyema apiculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of Australian heritage.
