Astragalus leucopsis var. brachypus

Astragalus leucopsis var. brachypus, commonly known as the short-stemmed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions, showcasing a deep root system for water absorption. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats.
