Ammannia muelleri

Ammannia muelleri, commonly known as the yellow water lily, is a aquatic plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes. The plant has broad, floating leaves that are usually a vibrant yellow or green in color. Its flowers are small and white, often appearing in clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

Ammannia muelleri plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
