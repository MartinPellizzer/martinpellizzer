Astragalus vesicarius var. luxurians

Astragalus vesicarius var. luxurians, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variant is notable for its larger, more vibrant flowers compared to the typical form of Astragalus vesicarius.
