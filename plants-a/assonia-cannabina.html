Assonia cannabina

Assonia cannabina, commonly known as the Indian hemp or cannabis plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is known for containing psychoactive compounds such as THC, which contributes to its historical and contemporary significance.

Due to its psychoactive properties, it is classified as a controlled substance in many countries.
