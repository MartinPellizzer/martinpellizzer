Adiantum kingii

Adiantum kingii, commonly known as the King's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green and have a lacy appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves.

Adiantum kingii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
