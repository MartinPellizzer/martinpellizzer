Arabis purpurea

Arabis purpurea, commonly known as the purple rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or coastal areas. It features basal leaves that are lobed or toothed, and its flowers are small, white or pink, with four petals.

Arabis purpurea is known for its hardy nature and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
