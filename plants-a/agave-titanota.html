Agave titanota

Agave titanota, commonly known as the giant agave, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its massive, thick, and often blue-green colored leaves that can grow up to 10 feet in length. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears numerous white flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. Its succulent leaves store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Agave titanota is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically for its fibers and sap.
