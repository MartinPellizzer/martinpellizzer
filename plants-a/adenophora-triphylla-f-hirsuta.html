Adenophora triphylla f. hirsuta

Adenophora triphylla f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy leaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its distinguishing appearance. Its flowers are bell-shaped, usually pink or white, and bloom in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
