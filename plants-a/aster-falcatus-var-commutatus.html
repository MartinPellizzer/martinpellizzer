Aster falcatus var. commutatus

Aster falcatus var. commutatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and small, clustered blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

This plant plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
