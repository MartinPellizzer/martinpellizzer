Anthyllis hermanniae

Anthyllis hermanniae, commonly known as Hermann's anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in open habitats such as coastal cliffs and limestone areas.
