Arctotis lanata

Arctotis lanata, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a compact, bushy growth habit. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
