Astragalus gautieri

Astragalus gautieri, commonly known as Gautier's milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus gautieri is adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in dry conditions.
