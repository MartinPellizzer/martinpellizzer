Asplenium impressivenium

Asplenium impressivenium, commonly known as the impressively marked spleenwort, is a fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate, finely divided fronds that are often adorned with distinctive patterns and textures. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as rocky crevices or forest floors. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Asplenium impressivenium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
