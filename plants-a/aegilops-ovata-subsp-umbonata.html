Aegilops ovata subsp. umbonata

Aegilops ovata subsp.

umbonata, commonly known as the broad-awned goatgrass, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect, spike-like inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and disturbed areas. Its seeds are small and have a distinctive, elongated shape with prominent awns.

It is often studied for its potential in agricultural improvement and genetic research.
