Acianthera deserta

Acianthera deserta, commonly known as the desert orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has a short, erect stem with a few leaves and produces a single flower per stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
