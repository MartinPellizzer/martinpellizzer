Arytera microphylla

Arytera microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved arnoldia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
