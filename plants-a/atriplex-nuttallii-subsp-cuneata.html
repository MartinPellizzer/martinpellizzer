Atriplex nuttallii subsp. cuneata

Atriplex nuttallii subsp.

cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are typically green or bluish-gray in color.

The plant thrives in arid and saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for wildlife.
