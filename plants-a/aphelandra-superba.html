Aphelandra superba

Aphelandra superba, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that bear bright yellow bracts, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. Its glossy, dark green leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, contributing to its lush appearance.

Aphelandra superba thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and humidity to flourish.
