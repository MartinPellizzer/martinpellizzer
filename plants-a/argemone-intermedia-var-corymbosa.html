Argemone intermedia var. corymbosa

Argemone intermedia var. corymbosa, commonly known as the Mexican poppy or yellow prickly poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It features striking yellow flowers with prominent red markings, typically blooming in summer. The plant has hairy, branching stems and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture with glandular dots.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.
