Abutilon whistleri

Abutilon whistleri, commonly known as the Whistling Woolyabutilon, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring soft, fuzzy, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, spathaceous flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a distinctive addition to native plant collections.
