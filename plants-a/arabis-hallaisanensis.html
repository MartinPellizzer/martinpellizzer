Arabis hallaisanensis

Arabis hallaisanensis, commonly known as the Hallaisan rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known plants such as cabbages and mustards. This species is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, white or pale yellow flowers. It typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
