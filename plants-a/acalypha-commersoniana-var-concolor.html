Acalypha commersoniana var. concolor

Acalypha commersoniana var. concolor, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant and low-maintenance plants.
