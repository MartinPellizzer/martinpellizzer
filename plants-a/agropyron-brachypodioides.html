Agropyron brachypodioides

Agropyron brachypodioides, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, often blue-green-colored leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It is well adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Agropyron brachypodioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop.
