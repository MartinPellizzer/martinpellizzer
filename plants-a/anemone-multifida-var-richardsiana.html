Anemone multifida var. richardsiana

Anemone multifida var. richardsiana, commonly known as the Richards' divided anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers with deeply lobed petals that give it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a soft, fuzzy texture, and it produces numerous small, seed-like fruits in the fall.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
