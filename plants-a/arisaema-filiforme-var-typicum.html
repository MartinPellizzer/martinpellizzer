Arisaema filiforme var. typicum

Arisaema filiforme var. typicum, commonly known as the slender arisaema, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leaf-like bracts that resemble leaves, and its distinctive spadix surrounded by a hooded spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it relies on insects for pollination.

The species is valued in traditional medicine and horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ecological significance.
