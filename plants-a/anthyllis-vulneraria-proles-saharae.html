Anthyllis vulneraria proles saharae

Anthyllis vulneraria proles saharae, commonly known as the Saharan burnet, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has adapted to harsh desert conditions with a deep root system and small, leathery leaves that reduce water loss. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and provides nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.

Its resilience makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects in arid environments.
