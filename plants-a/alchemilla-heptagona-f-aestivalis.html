Alchemilla heptagona f. aestivalis

Alchemilla heptagona f. aestivalis, commonly known as the summer alchemilla, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to round, with a distinctive waxy coating and a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
