Acer orientale var. obtusifolium

Acer orientale var. obtusifolium, commonly known as the Japanese maple with broad leaves, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad-ovate leaves that are typically green in spring, turning shades of red, orange, and yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellow-green flowers in early spring, followed by samaras with winged seeds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
