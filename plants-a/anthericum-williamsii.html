Anthericum williamsii

Anthericum williamsii, commonly known as Williams' lily, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has strap-shaped, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Anthericum williamsii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
