Astragalus burchan-buddaicus

Astragalus burchan-buddaicus, commonly known as the Burchan Buddha's Astragalus, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous tiny, lens-shaped seeds. Native to Central Asia, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and valuable species in its native habitat.
