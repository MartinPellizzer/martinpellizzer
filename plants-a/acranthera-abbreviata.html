Acranthera abbreviata

Acranthera abbreviata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lily, is a small perennial herb native to South Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces delicate, white to pale pink flowers. It blooms during the summer months and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
