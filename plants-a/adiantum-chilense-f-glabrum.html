Adiantum chilense f. glabrum

Adiantum chilense f. glabrum, commonly known as the glabrous maidenhair fern, is a fern species native to South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a smooth, glabrous surface, giving it its common name.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
