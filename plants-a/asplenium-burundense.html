Asplenium burundense

Asplenium burundense, commonly known as Burundi maidenhair, is a fern native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Aspleniaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction.

Asplenium burundense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
