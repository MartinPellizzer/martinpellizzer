Allium graecum

Allium graecum, commonly known as the Greek onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant is part of the Allium genus, which includes many edible and ornamental species. Its bulbs are used in cooking, similar to other onions, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Allium graecum prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
