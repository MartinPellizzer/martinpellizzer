Alchemilla kulczynskii

Alchemilla kulczynskii, commonly known as the Polish lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are borne on slender stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla kulczynskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
