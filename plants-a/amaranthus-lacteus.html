Amaranthus lacteus

Amaranthus lacteus, commonly known as the white amaranth, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, bead-like seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly sweet, nutty flavor.

Amaranthus lacteus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in various environmental conditions.
