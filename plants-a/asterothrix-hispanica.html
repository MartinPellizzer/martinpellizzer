Asterothrix hispanica

Asterothrix hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish aster, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and showy yellow or white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Asterothrix hispanica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
