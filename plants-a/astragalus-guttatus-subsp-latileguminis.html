Astragalus guttatus subsp. latileguminis

Astragalus guttatus subsp.

latileguminis, commonly known as the narrow-legged milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, often hairy pods and slightly inflated seedpods. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
