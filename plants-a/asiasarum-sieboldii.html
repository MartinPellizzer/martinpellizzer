Asiasarum sieboldii

Asiasarum sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese ginseng, is a perennial herb native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is highly valued in traditional herbal medicine.
