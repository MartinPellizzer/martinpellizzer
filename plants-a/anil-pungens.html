Anil pungens

Anil pungens, commonly known as the wild mustard, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, pinnately lobed leaves and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces slender, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

Anil pungens is known for its strong, pungent odor, which is due to the presence of glucosinolates.
