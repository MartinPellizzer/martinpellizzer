Avena sempervirens var. setacea

Avena sempervirens var. setacea, commonly known as the silky oat, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in dense, cylindrical clusters.

The botanical features of this variety make it an ornamental plant and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
