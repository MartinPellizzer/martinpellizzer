Adenia populifolia

Adenia populifolia, commonly known as the "poplar-leaved adenia," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of a poplar tree. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with five sepals and five petals, often in shades of white or pink. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains several seeds.

Adenia populifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some African countries.
