Amaranthus hybridus var. laetus

Amaranthus hybridus var. laetus, commonly known as red amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant red, orange, or purple bracts that surround its small flowers. The plant produces numerous small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into three-angled, bead-like seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible gardening.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a versatile plant for both agricultural and horticultural purposes.
