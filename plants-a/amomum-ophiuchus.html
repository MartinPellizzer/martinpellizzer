Amomum ophiuchus

Amomum ophiuchus, commonly known as the black cardamom, is a tropical plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, herbaceous stems and large, paddle-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit, which resembles a capsule, contains aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cooking.

Amomum ophiuchus thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary value.
