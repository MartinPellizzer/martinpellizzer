Artemisia seriphium subsp. decumbens

Artemisia seriphium subsp.

decumbens, commonly known as the creeping tarragon, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features silver-gray, aromatic foliage with linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in compact clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone and camphor, which contribute to its strong, medicinal aroma.
