Artemisia ludoviciana var. latiloba

Artemisia ludoviciana var. latiloba, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of aromatic, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its medicinal and cultural significance, it is often used in traditional practices and herbal remedies.
