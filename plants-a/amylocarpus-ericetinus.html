Amylocarpus ericetinus

Amylocarpus ericetinus, commonly known as the violet-rooted arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that store starch, which are used as a starchy food source. The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers, which are borne on a spadix surrounded by a leaf-like bract. Its leaves are typically broad and arrow-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Amylocarpus ericetinus is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its edible root.
