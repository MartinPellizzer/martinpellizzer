Acalypha depressa

Acalypha depressa, commonly known as the prostrate spurge, is a low-growing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
