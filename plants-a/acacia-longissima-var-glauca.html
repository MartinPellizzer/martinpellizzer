Acacia longissima var. glauca

Acacia longissima var. glauca, commonly known as the blue acacia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, twisted branches and small, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
