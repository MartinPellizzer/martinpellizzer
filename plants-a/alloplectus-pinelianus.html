Alloplectus pinelianus

Alloplectus pinelianus, commonly known as the Pinel Island grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, grass-like blades that form dense clumps, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color. Its root system is fibrous, contributing to soil stabilization in its wetland habitat.

Alloplectus pinelianus plays a role in supporting local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insect and bird species.
