Adesmia acuta

Adesmia acuta, commonly known as the sharpleaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, trifoliate leaves with sharply pointed lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Adesmia acuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
