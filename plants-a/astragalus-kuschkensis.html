Astragalus kuschkensis

Astragalus kuschkensis, commonly known as Kuschk's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seed pods dehisce to release seeds, aiding in its propagation in suitable environments.
