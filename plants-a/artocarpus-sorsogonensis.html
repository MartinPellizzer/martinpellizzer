Artocarpus sorsogonensis

Artocarpus sorsogonensis, commonly known as the Sorsogon mango, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, edible fruits that resemble mangoes in appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into fuzzy, green fruits. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.
