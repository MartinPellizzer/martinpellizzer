Acacia oraria

Acacia oraria, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, bipinnate leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which attract various pollinators such as bees and birds. Acacia oraria is adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its wood is lightweight and has been used traditionally for crafting and fuel.
