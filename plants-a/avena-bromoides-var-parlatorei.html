Avena bromoides var. parlatorei

Avena bromoides var. parlatorei, commonly known as the Parlatore's oat, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
