Astragalus crotalariae var. davidsonii

Astragalus crotalariae var. davidsonii, commonly known as the Davidson's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often in sandy or gravelly soils. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules.

This species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
