Asperula longiflora subsp. flaccida

Asperula longiflora subsp.

flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid woodruff, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically arranged in clusters along the stems.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
