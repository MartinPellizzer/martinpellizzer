Arundinaria paraguayensis

Arundinaria paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in Paraguay and Brazil. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and is often used for its strong, flexible culms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Arundinaria paraguayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
