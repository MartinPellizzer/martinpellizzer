Abarema opposita

Abarema opposita, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. Its flowers are yellow and bell-shaped, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round pods containing seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, tropical regions.
