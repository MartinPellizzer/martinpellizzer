Aeonium aureum

Aeonium aureum, commonly known as the "Lion's Tail," is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a distinctive yellow or orange edge. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant.
