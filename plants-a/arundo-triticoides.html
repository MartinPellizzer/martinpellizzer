Arundo triticoides

Arundo triticoides, commonly known as the wild barley grass, is a flowering plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny grains resembling barley. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Arundo triticoides is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
