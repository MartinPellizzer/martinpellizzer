Aceratium molle

Aceratium molle, commonly known as the soft aceratium, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The species is relatively low maintenance and thrives in full sun.
