Alchemilla brevidens

Alchemilla brevidens, commonly known as narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage is used in traditional herbal remedies and it is also valued in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance.
