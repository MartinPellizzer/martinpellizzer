Alyxia loeseneriana

Alyxia loeseneriana, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy green and have a waxy texture. Its fragrant white flowers bloom in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds.

Alyxia loeseneriana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
