Artemisia orientalis

Artemisia orientalis, commonly known as oriental wormwood, is a perennial herb native to regions of Asia, including China and the Russian Far East.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains volatile oils and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

Its historical use in herbal remedies and as a companion plant in agriculture highlights its ecological and medicinal significance.
