Amomum stephanocoleum

Amomum stephanocoleum, commonly known as the fragrant cardamom, is a tropical plant belonging to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped rhizomes that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in inflorescences, and its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a fragrant scent. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires partial shade for optimal growth.

The essential oils extracted from its rhizomes are valued for their medicinal and culinary properties.
