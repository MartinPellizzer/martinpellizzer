Anatherum gyrans

Anatherum gyrans, commonly known as the dancing grass, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique ability to move its inflorescences in response to wind, giving the appearance of dancing. The plant belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is typically found in sandy or rocky soils. Its small, spike-like inflorescences are adorned with tiny, pale blue to white florets.

Anatherum gyrans is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unusual movement and aesthetic appeal.
