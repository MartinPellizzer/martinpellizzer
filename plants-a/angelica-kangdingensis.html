Angelica kangdingensis

Angelica kangdingensis, commonly known as the Kangding angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and often hollow stems. The plant features large, compound leaves with a pinnate arrangement, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbel clusters. Its roots are thick and fleshy, which are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Angelica kangdingensis thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude ecosystems.
