Amaryllis × rugosa

Amaryllis × rugosa, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant that combines the features of its parent species, Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis sanderiana.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces strap-like, dark green leaves that are broad and pointed, emerging from a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Amaryllis × rugosa is known for its ability to bloom reliably when cared for properly, often producing multiple flower stalks during the growing season.
