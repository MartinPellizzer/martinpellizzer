Atylus sphaerocephalus

Atylus sphaerocephalus, commonly known as the globe-headed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features distinctive spherical, blue or violet flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting flowers.
