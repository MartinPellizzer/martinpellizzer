Astragalus aznaicus

Astragalus aznaicus, commonly known as the Armenian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a deep root system for water absorption. It produces pods that contain small, rounded seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus aznaicus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
