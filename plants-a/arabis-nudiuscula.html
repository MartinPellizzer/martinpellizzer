Arabis nudiuscula

Arabis nudiuscula, commonly known as the naked-fruited rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

Arabis nudiuscula is often found in open, sunny habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
