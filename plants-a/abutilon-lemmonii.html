Abutilon lemmonii

Abutilon lemmonii, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features showy, hibiscus-like flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming in summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while its stems are woody and can be branched.

Abutilon lemmonii is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in desert landscapes.
