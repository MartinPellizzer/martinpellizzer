Aster blandus

Aster blandus, commonly known as the showy aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and grows in clumps, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Aster blandus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
