Astragalus blyae

Astragalus blyae, commonly known as Blythe's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus blyae is often found in sagebrush steppe and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
