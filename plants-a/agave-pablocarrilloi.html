Agave pablocarrilloi

Agave pablocarrilloi, commonly known as the Pablocarrillo agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Agave pablocarrilloi is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
