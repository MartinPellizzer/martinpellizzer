Amphorogyne celastroides

Amphorogyne celastroides, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
