Ageratella microphylla

Ageratella microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of tiny, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes and swamps. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting various pollinators.
