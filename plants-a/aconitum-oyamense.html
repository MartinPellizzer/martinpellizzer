Aconitum oyamense

Aconitum oyamense, commonly known as the Japanese monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that resemble a hood, giving it its common name. The plant features palmately divided leaves and produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Aconitum oyamense is known for its toxic alkaloids, which make it a plant of both ecological and medicinal significance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.
