Asplenium adiantum

Asplenium adiantum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a delicate and graceful plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a key feature of its reproductive structure.

Asplenium adiantum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
