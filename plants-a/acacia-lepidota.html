Acacia lepidota

Acacia lepidota, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, greyish-green stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom in spring and attract a variety of pollinators. Acacia lepidota is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
