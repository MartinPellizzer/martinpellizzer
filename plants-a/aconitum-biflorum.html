Aconitum biflorum

Aconitum biflorum, commonly known as the two-flowered monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has dark green, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. Aconitum biflorum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
