Atriplex aralensis

Atriplex aralensis, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in dry environments. The plant has small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it well adapted to saline soils.

Atriplex aralensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
