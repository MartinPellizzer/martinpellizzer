Actinodaphne intermedia

Actinodaphne intermedia, commonly known as the middle-leaf laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
