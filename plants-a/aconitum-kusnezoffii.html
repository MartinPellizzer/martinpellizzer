Aconitum kusnezoffii

Aconitum kusnezoffii, commonly known as the Kusnezoff monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, characteristic of the genus Aconitum. The plant has dark green, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is not recommended for cultivation in gardens.
