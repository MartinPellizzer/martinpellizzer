Acanthopanax sieboldianus var. variegatus

Acanthopanax sieboldianus var. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated five-leaf aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which often display striking variegation of white and green. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique foliage and adaptability contribute to its appeal in horticulture.
