Alpinia affinis

Alpinia affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and aromatic plants. The plant features slender, upright rhizomes and linear, dark green leaves that are often narrower than those of other ginger species. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to white, with a distinct, spicy fragrance.

Alpinia affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
