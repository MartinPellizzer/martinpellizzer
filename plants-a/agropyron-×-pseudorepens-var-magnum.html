Agropyron × pseudorepens var. magnum

Agropyron × pseudorepens var. magnum, commonly known as the giant wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its tall, robust growth.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of spikelets that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. This variety is native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in well-drained soils. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a culm that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
