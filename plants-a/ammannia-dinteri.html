Ammannia dinteri

Ammannia dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's ammannia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, clustered flowers and fleshy, succulent-like leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
