Arachis glabrata f. typica

Arachis glabrata f. typica, commonly known as the smooth peanut, is a botanical variety of the peanut plant.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless pods and is native to South America. The plant is an annual legume that produces underground pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is primarily cultivated for its nutritious seeds, which are rich in protein and oil.
