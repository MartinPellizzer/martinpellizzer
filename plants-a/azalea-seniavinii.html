Azalea seniavinii

Azalea seniavinii, commonly known as the Siberian azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value.

Azalea seniavinii is relatively cold-hardy, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate and continental climates.
