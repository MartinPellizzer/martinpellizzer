Andropogon sorghum var. niger

Andropogon sorghum var. niger, commonly known as black sorghum, is a robust grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks and dark purple to black-colored bracts, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient crop in challenging climates.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
