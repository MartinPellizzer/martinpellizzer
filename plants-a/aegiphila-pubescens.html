Aegiphila pubescens

Aegiphila pubescens, commonly known as the hairy leaf shrub, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
