Asperula tournefortii

Asperula tournefortii, commonly known as the evergreen shrub or sweet pepper bush, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its evergreen nature and ability to attract pollinators.
