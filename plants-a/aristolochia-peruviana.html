Aristolochia peruviana

Aristolochia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian Dutchman's pipe, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe, often with a deep purple or maroon color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
