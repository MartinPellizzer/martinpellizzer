Allium fuscum

Allium fuscum, commonly known as the purple onion, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of small, bulbous structures that resemble onions, which are edible when mature. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms with pink to purple flowers in the summer.

Allium fuscum is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in certain regions.
