Aristolochia incisiloba

Aristolochia incisiloba, commonly known as the incised Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe, with a deep purple to maroon coloration. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it typically grows as a climbing vine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical forests.

The species is notable for its complex floral structure and its role in supporting specific pollinators.
