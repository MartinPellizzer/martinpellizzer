Anthoxanthum horsfieldii var. japonicum

Anthoxanthum horsfieldii var. japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese meadow grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to East Asia, characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale yellow to golden florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators and soil stabilization.
