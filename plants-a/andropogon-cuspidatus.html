Andropogon cuspidatus

Andropogon cuspidatus, commonly known as eastern wood bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
