Aerva sanguinolenta

Aerva sanguinolenta, commonly known as red sandparsley, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers, which are typically borne in terminal inflorescences. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep taproots that allow it to access groundwater.

Aerva sanguinolenta is also known for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in some regions for its purported health benefits.
