Aster pubescens

Aster pubescens, commonly known as the downy aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are hairy, giving it the common name, and its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Aster pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
