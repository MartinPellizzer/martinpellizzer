Astragalus aureus var. gaubeanus

Astragalus aureus var. gaubeanus, commonly known as golden milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, stipitate stipules and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in open grasslands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
