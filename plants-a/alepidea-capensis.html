Alepidea capensis

Alepidea capensis, commonly known as the Cape alepidea, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it thrives in full sun.

Alepidea capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
