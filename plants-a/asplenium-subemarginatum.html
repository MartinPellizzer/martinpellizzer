Asplenium subemarginatum

Asplenium subemarginatum, commonly known as the marginal holly fern, is a species of fern native to various regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often have a serrated or toothed margin. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are relatively short, and the fronds are usually arranged in a rosette pattern.

Asplenium subemarginatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
