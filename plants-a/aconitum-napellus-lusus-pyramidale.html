Aconitum napellus lusus pyramidale

Aconitum napellus lusus pyramidale, commonly known as the Pyramidal Monkshood, is a variant of the European monkshood plant.

It is characterized by its striking, helmet-shaped purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features large, dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
