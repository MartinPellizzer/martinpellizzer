Armeniaca × leiocarpa

Armeniaca × leiocarpa, commonly known as the sweet cherry.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Prunus avium (sweet cherry) and Prunus padus (bird cherry). This plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with lance-shaped leaves and showy white or pink flowers in early spring. The fruits are small, round, and typically have a smooth, red or yellow skin.

It is cultivated for its edible cherries, which are prized for their sweet flavor and culinary uses.
