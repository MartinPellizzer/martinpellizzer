Antirrhinum glaucophyllum

Antirrhinum glaucophyllum, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet petals, which are typically tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Antirrhinum glaucophyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
