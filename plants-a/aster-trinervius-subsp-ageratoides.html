Aster trinervius subsp. ageratoides

Aster trinervius subsp.

ageratoides, commonly known as the woolly aster, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.

The plant features hairy, grayish-green leaves and produces small, clustered blue to purple flowers. It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.

The subspecies is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and is often used in ornamental gardening.
