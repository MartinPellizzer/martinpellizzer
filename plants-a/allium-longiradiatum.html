Allium longiradiatum

Allium longiradiatum, commonly known as the long-leaved garlic.

It is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species such as garlic and chives. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.
