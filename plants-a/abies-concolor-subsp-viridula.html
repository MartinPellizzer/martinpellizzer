Abies concolor subsp. viridula

Abies concolor subsp.

viridula, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its distinctive blue-green needles that are flat and have a waxy coating.

The cones are typically short and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color. This subspecies is often found in high-elevation forests and is adapted to cold, moist environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
