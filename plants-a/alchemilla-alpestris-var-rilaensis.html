Alchemilla alpestris var. rilaensis

Alchemilla alpestris var. rilaensis, commonly known as the Rila alkanet, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. It is known for its medicinal properties, with the leaves used in traditional remedies for their anti-inflammatory effects.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
