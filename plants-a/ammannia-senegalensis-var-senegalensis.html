Ammannia senegalensis var. senegalensis

Ammannia senegalensis var. senegalensis, commonly known as redroot ammannia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-colored roots and stems, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils.

Ammannia senegalensis var. senegalensis is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.
