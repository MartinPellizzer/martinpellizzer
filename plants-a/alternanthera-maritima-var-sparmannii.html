Alternanthera maritima var. sparmannii

Alternanthera maritima var. sparmannii, commonly known as salt marsh alternate, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, often reddish stems and leaves. This variant is native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States and parts of the Caribbean. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged alternately along the stem.

The plant thrives in wetlands, tidal flats, and other brackish water habitats, showcasing its tolerance to high salinity and periodic flooding.
