Aquilegia vulgaris subsp. atrata

Aquilegia vulgaris subsp.

atrata, commonly known as the black cohosh, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which contrast with its green, palmate leaves.

The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It has a short stem with a single flower cluster at the top.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in native gardens.
