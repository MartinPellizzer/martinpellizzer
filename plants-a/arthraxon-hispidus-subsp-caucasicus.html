Arthraxon hispidus subsp. caucasicus

Arthraxon hispidus subsp.

caucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian grass or hairy arthraxon, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with dense, hairy leaves that are typically narrow and grass-like.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and wetlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
