Anagallis pumila var. ovalis

Anagallis pumila var. ovalis, commonly known as the oval-leaved mouse-ear, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The leaves are ovate and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

This plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate foliage and blooms.
