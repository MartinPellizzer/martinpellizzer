Aster politus

Aster politus, commonly known as the smooth aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its showy, violet-blue flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually smooth and lance-shaped, contributing to its common name.
