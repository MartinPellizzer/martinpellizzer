Anil tetrasperma

Anil tetrasperma, commonly known as Indian borage, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a small shrub that typically grows up to 30 centimeters in height, with hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
