Anacamptis papilionacea var. expansa

Anacamptis papilionacea var. expansa, commonly known as the Broad-leaved Orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while the flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
