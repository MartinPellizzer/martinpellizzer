Acaena ovalifolia var. calvescenticupula

Acaena ovalifolia var. calvescenticupula, commonly known as the velvetcup bramble, is a species native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces bright orange to red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in cup-shaped inflorescences, which give it its specific name.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
