Alaticaulia whiteana

Alaticaulia whiteana, commonly known as the White's alate-stemmed plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, winged stems that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its leaves are usually narrow and elongated, arranged alternately along the stem.

Alaticaulia whiteana is of interest to botanists due to its specialized morphology and limited distribution.
