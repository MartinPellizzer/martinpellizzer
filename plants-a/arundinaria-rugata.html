Arundinaria rugata

Arundinaria rugata, commonly known as the rugose bamboo, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a clump-forming grass that produces slender, hollow stems with a distinctive rough, scaly texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as along stream banks and in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

Arundinaria rugata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
