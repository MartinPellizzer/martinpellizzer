Artemisia campestris subsp. spithamaea

Artemisia campestris subsp.

spithamaea, commonly known as rough tarragon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, silver-green foliage with finely divided leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in loose panicles, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
