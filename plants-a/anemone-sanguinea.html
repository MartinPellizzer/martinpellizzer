Anemone sanguinea

Anemone sanguinea, commonly known as the blood anemone, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has palmately divided, lobed leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Anemone sanguinea is often found in woodlands and along streambanks, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these habitats.
