Aconitum neubergense

Aconitum neubergense, commonly known as the Neuberg monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments, and it grows up to 1.5 meters in height. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum neubergense is noted for its toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
