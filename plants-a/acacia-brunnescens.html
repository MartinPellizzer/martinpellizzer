Acacia brunnescens

Acacia brunnescens, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Acacia brunnescens is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
