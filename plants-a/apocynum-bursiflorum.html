Apocynum bursiflorum

Apocynum bursiflorum, commonly known as the Indian blanket flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the United States.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes milkweeds and other flowering plants with milky sap. The plant features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often resembling a blanket of color in the landscape. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

Apocynum bursiflorum is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.
