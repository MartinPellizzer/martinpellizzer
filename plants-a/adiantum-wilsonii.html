Adiantum wilsonii

Adiantum wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive zigzag pattern along the veins. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are bipinnate, with small, spoon-shaped pinnae that give it a feathery appearance.

Adiantum wilsonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
