Arnica grandifolia

Arnica grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered tansy, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits called achenes. Arnica grandifolia is often found in open woods, meadows, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soil.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
