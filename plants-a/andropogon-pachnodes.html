Andropogon pachnodes

Andropogon pachnodes, commonly known as eastern gamma grass, is a tall perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its coarse, upright growth habit. The plant has long, narrow leaves with a prominent midvein and produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a potential forage species.
