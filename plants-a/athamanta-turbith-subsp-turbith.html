Athamanta turbith subsp. turbith

Athamanta turbith subsp.

turbith, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are bell-shaped, typically blue to violet in color, and are arranged in loose clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
