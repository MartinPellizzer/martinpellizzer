Alvaradoa arborescens

Alvaradoa arborescens, commonly known as the tree mallow, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems and showy, pentagonal flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its fruit is a capsule that releases small seeds.

Alvaradoa arborescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native range.
