Ardisia nigropunctata

Ardisia nigropunctata, commonly known as the black-spotted ardisia, is a member of the Myrtle family (Myrtaceae).

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with black dots. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
