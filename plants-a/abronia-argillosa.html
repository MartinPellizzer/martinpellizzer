Abronia argillosa

Abronia argillosa, commonly known as the clay bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap tiny aquatic organisms, aiding in its nutrient acquisition. The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving water or moist soil, often in swampy or marshy habitats. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged.

Abronia argillosa plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
