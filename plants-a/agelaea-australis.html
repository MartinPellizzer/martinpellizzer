Agelaea australis

Agelaea australis, commonly known as the Australian flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height, featuring a spreading crown and rough, fissured bark. The plant is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a leathery texture and a slightly glossy surface.

Agelaea australis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, sunny climates.
