Astragalus maverranagri

Astragalus maverranagri, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to dry and rocky environments, showcasing a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions. Its pods contain small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its natural range.

Astragalus maverranagri is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
