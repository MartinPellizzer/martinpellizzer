Azalea formosa

Azalea formosa, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a species of flowering plant in the Rhododendron genus.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is an evergreen shrub with broad, leathery leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

Azalea formosa is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its role in traditional Japanese horticulture.
