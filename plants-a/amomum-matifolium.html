Amomum matifolium

Amomum matifolium, commonly known as the fragrant ginger or Chinese ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like ginger and turmeric. The plant features tall, slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, and it produces oblong, reddish-brown fruits.

The rhizomes of Amomum matifolium are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, valued for their warm, spicy flavor.
