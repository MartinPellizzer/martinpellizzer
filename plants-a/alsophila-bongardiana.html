Alsophila bongardiana

Alsophila bongardiana, commonly known as the Bongard's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive crown of compound leaves that give it a lush, tropical appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The fronds are used in traditional medicine and basket weaving in some cultures.
