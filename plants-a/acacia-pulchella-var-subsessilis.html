Acacia pulchella var. subsessilis

Acacia pulchella var. subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The leaves are narrow and slightly twisted, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native wildlife.
