Anathallis ramulosa

Anathallis ramulosa, commonly known as the pink spider orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spider-like inflorescence and pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots form a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

Anathallis ramulosa is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral structure.
