Angraecum humblotianum

Angraecum humblotianum, commonly known as the Humboldt's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in humid and shaded environments. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by moths, which are attracted to its strong scent.

Angraecum humblotianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
