Aucuba albopunctifolia var. obcordata

Aucuba albopunctifolia var. obcordata, commonly known as the white-spotted aucuba, is a rare and ornamental evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, glossy dark green leaves with distinctive white or yellow spots and margins. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its foliage remains vibrant throughout the year, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

While not widely cultivated, it is valued for its unique leaf patterns and adaptability to certain garden conditions.
