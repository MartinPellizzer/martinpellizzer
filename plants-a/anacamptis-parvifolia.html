Anacamptis parvifolia

Anacamptis parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. Its flowers are usually arranged in a raceme and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

Anacamptis parvifolia is a relatively short-lived species, often flowering in its second year before dying back.
