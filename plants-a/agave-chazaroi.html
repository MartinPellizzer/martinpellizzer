Agave chazaroi

Agave chazaroi, commonly known as the Sierra de las Nieves agave, is a succulent plant native to southern Spain.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming structure with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which appear after several years of growth. It is well adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

The leaves contain a high concentration of sap, which can be used in the production of agave-based products.
