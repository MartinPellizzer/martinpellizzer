Acacia cochlocarpa

Acacia cochlocarpa, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and yellow globular flowerheads that appear in spring. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional Indigenous Australian cuisine. Acacia cochlocarpa thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
