Andropogon capillipes

Andropogon capillipes, commonly known as the slender grass or slender bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swamps, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue, and its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelets. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Andropogon capillipes is also valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland restoration projects.
