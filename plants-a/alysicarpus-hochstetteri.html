Alysicarpus hochstetteri

Alysicarpus hochstetteri, commonly known as the African burr bean, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive burr-like fruits that encase its seeds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
