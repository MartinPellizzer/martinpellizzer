Aster miser var. pendulus

Aster miser var. pendulus, commonly known as the weeping aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its drooping, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening for its graceful, cascading appearance.
