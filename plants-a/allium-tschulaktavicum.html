Allium tschulaktavicum

Allium tschulaktavicum, commonly known as the Tschulaktav mountain garlic, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
