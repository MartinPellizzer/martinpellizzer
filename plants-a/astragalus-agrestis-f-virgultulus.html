Astragalus agrestis f. virgultulus

Astragalus agrestis f. virgultulus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces slender pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is often found in the western United States and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
