Alchemilla pubescens f. adpressepilosa

Alchemilla pubescens f. adpressepilosa, commonly known as the compact hairy lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves that are slightly succulent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in open, branched inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
