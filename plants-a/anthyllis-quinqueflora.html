Anthyllis quinqueflora

Anthyllis quinqueflora, commonly known as the five-flowered broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Anthyllis quinqueflora is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
