Atriplex portulacoides var. erecta

Atriplex portulacoides var. erecta, commonly known as the upright saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to store water, a feature typical of plants in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it a valuable species for stabilizing soils in saline areas.

The leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
