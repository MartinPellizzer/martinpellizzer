Aconitum racemulosum

Aconitum racemulosum, commonly known as the clustered aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a tall, erect stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with large, showy blue to purple flowers arranged in a dense raceme. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a hairy texture, and its root system consists of thick, fleshy rhizomes. Aconitum racemulosum is known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
