Aconitum septentrionale var. carpaticum

Aconitum septentrionale var. carpaticum, commonly known as the Carpathian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features striking blue-violet flowers arranged in a raceme, which are typically borne on tall, sturdy stems. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive purple underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care, as all parts of the plant contain potent alkaloids.
