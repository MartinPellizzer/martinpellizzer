Alsophila microphylloides

Alsophila microphylloides, commonly known as the dwarf tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and fronds that are typically less than 1 meter in length. The plant has a slow growth rate and thrives in shaded, moist environments with high humidity. Its rhizomes form a dense network that helps anchor it to rocky substrates, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The fronds are pinnately compound, with a distinctive pale green color and a smooth, waxy texture.
