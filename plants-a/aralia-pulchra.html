Aralia pulchra

Aralia pulchra, commonly known as the beautiful aralia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in diameter, giving it a dramatic and exotic appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are edible for humans but toxic to some animals. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden spaces.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
