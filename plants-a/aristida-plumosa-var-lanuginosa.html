Aristida plumosa var. lanuginosa

Aristida plumosa var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silky, silver-green foliage that gives it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering stems are slender and often bear small, inconspicuous spikelets.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
