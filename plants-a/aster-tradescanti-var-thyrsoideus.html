Aster tradescanti var. thyrsoideus

Aster tradescanti var. thyrsoideus, commonly known as the curly willow or curly filaret, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features long, arching stems with narrow, pale green leaves that curl inward, giving it a unique, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped blue or purple flowers in late summer, adding color to gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its attractive foliage and ease of care, it is a popular choice for container gardening and landscape design.
