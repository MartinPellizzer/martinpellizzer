Astragalus lavrenkoi

Astragalus lavrenkoi, commonly known as the Lavrenko milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and southern Siberia. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers and compound leaves with numerous leaflets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and steppe landscapes.

Astragalus lavrenkoi plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
