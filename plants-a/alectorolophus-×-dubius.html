Alectorolophus × dubius

Alectorolophus × dubius, commonly known as the hybrid lily, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Alectorolophus bulbosus and Alectorolophus hirtus.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense basal rosette.

This hybrid is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
