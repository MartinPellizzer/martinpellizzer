Arisaema anatinum

Arisaema anatinum, commonly known as the golden thread arisaema, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its unique, spiral-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the arum family.

Arisaema anatinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
