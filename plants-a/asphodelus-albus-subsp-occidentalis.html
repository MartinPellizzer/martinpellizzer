Asphodelus albus subsp. occidentalis

Asphodelus albus subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western asphodel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are arranged in a panicle-like inflorescence. It is adapted to withstand drought and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
