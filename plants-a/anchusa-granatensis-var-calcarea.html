Anchusa granatensis var. calcarea

Anchusa granatensis var. calcarea, commonly known as the limestone hawksbeard, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and bright yellow ray flowers. The plant thrives in calcareous soils, often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
