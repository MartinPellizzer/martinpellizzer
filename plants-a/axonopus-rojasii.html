Axonopus rojasii

Axonopus rojasii, commonly known as the narrowleaf axonopus, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

Its botanical features make it an important component of tropical and subtropical ecosystems.
