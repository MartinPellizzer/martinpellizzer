Allium aschersonianum var. ambiguum

Allium aschersonianum var. ambiguum, commonly known as the purple garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features grass-like, strap-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple, and it produces clusters of small, spherical, purple or pink flowers. The plant is closely related to other allium species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild garlic-like fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.

This variety is also noted for its cold hardiness and ability to naturalize over time.
