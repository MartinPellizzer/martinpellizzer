Anthericum durum

Anthericum durum, commonly known as the hardy arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features arrow-shaped, heart-veined leaves that are typically green with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces striking purple spadices that enclose small, yellowish flowers, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Anthericum durum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and dramatic floral display.
