Arytera xerocarpa

Arytera xerocarpa, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its seeds are encased in a tough, dry fruit, which is typical of the Caryophyllaceae family.

Arytera xerocarpa is well-suited to harsh, dry environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
