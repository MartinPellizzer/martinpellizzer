Astragalus rimarum

Astragalus rimarum, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native insects.

Astragalus rimarum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
