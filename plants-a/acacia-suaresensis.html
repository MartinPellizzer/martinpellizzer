Acacia suaresensis

Acacia suaresensis, commonly known as the Suarese acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Paraguay. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are clustered in cylindrical spikes, and it produces small, flat pods that contain seeds.

Acacia suaresensis is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
