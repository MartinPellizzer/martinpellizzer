Anthurium chamulense subsp. chamulense

Anthurium chamulense subsp.

chamulense, commonly known as the Chamulense Anthurium, is a species native to the highland regions of Central America. It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture.

The plant produces vibrant, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, often in shades of red or orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
