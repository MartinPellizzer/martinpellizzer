Argentina glabriuscula

Argentina glabriuscula, commonly known as the Argentine thornapple, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
