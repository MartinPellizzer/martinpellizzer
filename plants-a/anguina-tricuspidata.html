Anguina tricuspidata

Anguina tricuspidata, commonly known as the three-lobed lily, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and bloom in early spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
