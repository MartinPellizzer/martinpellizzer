Allium sewerzowii

Allium sewerzowii, commonly known as the Siberian leek, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces umbels of white or pale purple flowers in the summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor.

Allium sewerzowii is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
