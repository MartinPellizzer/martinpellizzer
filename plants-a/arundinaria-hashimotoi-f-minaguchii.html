Arundinaria hashimotoi f. minaguchii

Arundinaria hashimotoi f. minaguchii, commonly known as the Hashimoto's bamboo, is a rare and ornamental grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a silvery-blue hue. This variety is a fasciation form, meaning it exhibits a flattened or ribbon-like growth pattern, which makes it visually distinctive. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
