Anthurium coriaceum

Anthurium coriaceum, commonly known as the velvet leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, and velvety leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length. The plant produces striking spadices, which are specialized inflorescences that resemble a cluster of small flowers. Its leaves are typically dark green with a glossy texture and may have a reddish or purple tint on the underside.

Anthurium coriaceum is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in humid environments.
