Amomum jainii

Amomum jainii, commonly known as Jain's cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its aromatic, clustered spikes of white to pale green flowers. The plant produces small, oblong fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and the rhizomes are valued for their pungent scent and medicinal properties.

Amomum jainii thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
