Aster nemoralis

Aster nemoralis, commonly known as the wood aster, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in woodland areas and along forest edges. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, and it has a fibrous root system.

Aster nemoralis is often found in moist, shady environments and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
