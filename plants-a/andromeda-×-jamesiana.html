Andromeda × jamesiana

Andromeda × jamesiana, commonly known as the rosebay rhododendron, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with dark green, leathery leaves that provide a lush backdrop for its showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of pink to reddish-purple, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to form thickets, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
