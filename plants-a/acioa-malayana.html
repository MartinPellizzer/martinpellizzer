Acioa malayana

Acioa malayana, commonly known as the Malay acacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, and it produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.
