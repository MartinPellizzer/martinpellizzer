Adenophora verticillata var. genuina

Adenophora verticillata var. genuina, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its flowering stems are often branched, and the flowers have five pointed lobes.

The plant is typically found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
