Acacia latipes var. pubescens

Acacia latipes var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spindly stems and small, feathery leaves covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flowerheads that appear in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The hairy wattle is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
