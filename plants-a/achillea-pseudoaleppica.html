Achillea pseudoaleppica

Achillea pseudoaleppica, commonly known as the Alpine yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers, which range from white to pale yellow, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Achillea pseudoaleppica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
