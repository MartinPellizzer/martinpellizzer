Acaena ovalifolia subsp. elegans

Acaena ovalifolia subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the slender wood rose, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves.

The plant produces fluffy, pinkish-white flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, spiny burrs that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.
