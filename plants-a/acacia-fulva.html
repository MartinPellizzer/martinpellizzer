Acacia fulva

Acacia fulva, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves, and it produces pods that contain seeds. Acacia fulva thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

It is an important plant in Australian ecosystems and is also a symbol of the country.
