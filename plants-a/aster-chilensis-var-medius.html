Aster chilensis var. medius

Aster chilensis var. medius, commonly known as the Chilean aster, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
