Argophyllum acinetochromum

Argophyllum acinetochromum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for wetland restoration projects and ornamental gardening.
