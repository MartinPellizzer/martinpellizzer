Arabis suksdorfii

Arabis suksdorfii, commonly known as the Saksdorf's rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Arabis suksdorfii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
