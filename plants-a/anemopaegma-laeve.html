Anemopaegma laeve

Anemopaegma laeve, commonly known as the smooth anemopaegma, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, slender stems with alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Anemopaegma laeve thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.
