Amyris papyrifera

Amyris papyrifera, commonly known as the paperbark tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that resembles paper, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by orange or red fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Amyris papyrifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
