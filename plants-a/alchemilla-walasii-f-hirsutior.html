Alchemilla walasii f. hirsutior

Alchemilla walasii f. hirsutior, commonly known as the hairy alchemilla, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in umbels. Native to Central Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shady borders.
