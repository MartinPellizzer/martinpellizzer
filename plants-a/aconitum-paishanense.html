Aconitum paishanense

Aconitum paishanense, commonly known as the Paishan monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves and produces striking blue to purple spurred flowers in late summer. Aconitum paishanense is notable for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and studied for its chemical compounds.
