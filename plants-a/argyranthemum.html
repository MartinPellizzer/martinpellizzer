Argyranthemum

Argyranthemum, commonly known as the silver geranium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, and it is characterized by its silvery-green foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.

Argyranthemum prefers well-drained soil and is drought-tolerant once established.
