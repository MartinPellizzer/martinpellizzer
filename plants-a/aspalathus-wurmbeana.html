Aspalathus wurmbeana

Aspalathus wurmbeana, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, red-brown flowers and narrow, leathery leaves. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. It is well adapted to drought conditions, making it resilient in harsh climates.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
