Allium vineale subsp. monspessulanum

Allium vineale subsp.

monspessulanum, commonly known as garlic chives or wild garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers.

The plant produces bulbs that are often used in culinary applications, similar to true garlic. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its aromatic, edible properties.
