Andropogon caricosus var. glabrior

Andropogon caricosus var. glabrior, commonly known as glabrous bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and slender, erect growth habit. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grassy areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, two-lipped flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
