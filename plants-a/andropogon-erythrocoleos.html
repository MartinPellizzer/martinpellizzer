Andropogon erythrocoleos

Andropogon erythrocoleos, commonly known as scarlet grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange inflorescence, which stands out against its green foliage. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to wet soils. Its tall, slender stems can reach heights of up to 2 meters, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like.

Botanically, it belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland restoration projects.
