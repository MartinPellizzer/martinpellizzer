Actinodaphne thwaitesii

Actinodaphne thwaitesii, commonly known as the Malabar camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing smooth, black seeds. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

The tree is also notable for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.
