Acalypha simplicistyla

Acalypha simplicistyla, commonly known as the simple-styled glorybower, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that range in color from yellow to red, often resembling small flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
