Allium breviscapum

Allium breviscapum, commonly known as the short-stemmed garlic.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China. The plant features a bulb composed of several white, fleshy cloves surrounded by a papery outer layer. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and slightly hairy, while its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and typically pink to purple in color.

Allium breviscapum is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.
