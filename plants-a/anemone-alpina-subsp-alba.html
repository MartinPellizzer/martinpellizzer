Anemone alpina subsp. alba

Anemone alpina subsp.

alba, commonly known as the alpine white anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a fine, white down.

The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
