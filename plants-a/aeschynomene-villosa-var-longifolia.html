Aeschynomene villosa var. longifolia

Aeschynomene villosa var. longifolia, commonly known as hairy cowpea, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, spreading stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, elliptical pods containing several seeds.

It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and is often used in agricultural systems for soil enrichment.
