Andropogon hackelii

Andropogon hackelii, commonly known as Hackel's bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, showcasing its adaptability to humid environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

Andropogon hackelii plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
