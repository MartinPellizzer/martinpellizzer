Aconitum septentrionale f. albidum

Aconitum septentrionale f. albidum, commonly known as the white-flowered monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or white flowers. The plant features compound leaves with three leaflets, and its roots contain toxic alkaloids. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
