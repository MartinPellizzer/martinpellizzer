Acanthus hirsutus f. roseus

Acanthus hirsutus f. roseus, commonly known as the rose-flowered hirsute acanthus, is a decorative plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, spiny bracts that are typically pink to reddish in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and unique floral display.
