Arundina stenopetala

Arundina stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale green to white flowers, which are typically arranged in inflorescences. Arundina stenopetala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid climate to flourish.
