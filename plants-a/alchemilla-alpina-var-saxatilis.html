Alchemilla alpina var. saxatilis

Alchemilla alpina var. saxatilis, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that form a dense mat. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
