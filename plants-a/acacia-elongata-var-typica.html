Acacia elongata var. typica

Acacia elongata var. typica, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, phyllodes that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical spikes, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

The species is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
