Antitoxicum rehmannii

Antitoxicum rehmannii, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its traditional use in Chinese medicine for its purported detoxifying properties.
