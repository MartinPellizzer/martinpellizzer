Ampelopsis glandulosa f. elegans

Ampelopsis glandulosa f. elegans, commonly known as the fragrant honeysuckle vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped, serrated leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of small, fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers in the summer. The plant is known for its distinctive, red, berry-like fruits that mature in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its vigorous growth habit and attractive foliage make it a versatile addition to gardens and natural habitats.
