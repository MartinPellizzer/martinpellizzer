Aristida purpurea var. hookeri

Aristida purpurea var. hookeri, commonly known as purple threeawn, is a flowering grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that turn reddish-purple in color. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and desert scrub habitats. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its spread.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
