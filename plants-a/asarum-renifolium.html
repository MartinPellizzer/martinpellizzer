Asarum renifolium

Asarum renifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the forests of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often mistaken for the leaves of its more well-known relative, Asarum canadense. The plant produces small, hooded flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, which are not showy but are adapted for pollination by specific insects.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread slowly through underground stems, forming dense colonies in shaded, moist environments.
