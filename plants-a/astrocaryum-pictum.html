Astrocaryum pictum

Astrocaryum pictum, commonly known as the spotted palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, edible fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance in its native habitats.
