Aconitum chrysopilum

Aconitum chrysopilum, commonly known as the golden-hair aconite, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers with golden stamens, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that are covered in soft, silky hairs.

Aconitum chrysopilum is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
