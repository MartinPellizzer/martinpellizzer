Astilbe odontophylla var. okuyamae

Astilbe odontophylla var. okuyamae, commonly known as the Okuyama astilbe, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features delicate, feathery plumes of pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, medium-green leaves that are arranged in a loose, open clump. It prefers partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it ideal for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its elegant foliage and subtle, long-lasting floral display.
