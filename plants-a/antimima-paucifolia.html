Antimima paucifolia

Antimima paucifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved antimima, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and small, narrow, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, adding a delicate ornamental touch. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
