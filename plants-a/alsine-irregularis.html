Alsine irregularis

Alsine irregularis, commonly known as the irregular sandwort, is a small annual plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America, particularly in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, and it produces small, white or pink flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in restoration projects in disturbed habitats.
