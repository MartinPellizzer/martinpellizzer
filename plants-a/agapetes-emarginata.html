Agapetes emarginata

Agapetes emarginata, commonly known as the Margined Agapetes, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant typically features small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, which bloom in the spring.

Agapetes emarginata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
