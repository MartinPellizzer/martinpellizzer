Alseodaphne macrophylla

Alseodaphne macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf sandarac, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey-brown bark and large, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, fleshy berries that are red when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Alseodaphne macrophylla is valued for its aromatic wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
