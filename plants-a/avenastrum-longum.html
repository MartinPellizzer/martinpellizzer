Avenastrum longum

Avenastrum longum, commonly known as the longstem grass or velvet grass, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, a common trait among grasses. Avenastrum longum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Its fibrous roots and dense foliage make it an important component of local ecosystems.
