Asplenium adiantum-nigrum subsp. serpentini

Asplenium adiantum-nigrum subsp.

serpentini, commonly known as the serpent's tail, is a fern species characterized by its finely divided, dark green fronds that resemble the tail of a serpent. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

The plant's rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and its delicate appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant. It prefers well-drained soil and indirect sunlight, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

The fronds are typically leathery and have a distinctive, intricate pattern that adds to its aesthetic appeal.
