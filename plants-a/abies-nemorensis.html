Abies nemorensis

Abies nemorensis, commonly known as the Italian fir, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the stems. The tree produces cones that are typically cylindrical and contain seeds enclosed in woody structures. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

The Italian fir plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
