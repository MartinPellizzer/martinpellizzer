Anemone quinquefolia var. oregana

Anemone quinquefolia var. oregana, commonly known as the Pacific anemone or the Oregon anemone, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to white petals and a central cluster of yellow stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch of color to its natural habitat.
