Acacia graveolens

Acacia graveolens, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a small, thorny shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain small, edible seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

The tree is also valued for its aromatic wood and historical use in traditional medicine.
