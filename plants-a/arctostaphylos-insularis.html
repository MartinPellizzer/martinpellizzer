Arctostaphylos insularis

Arctostaphylos insularis, commonly known as the island manzanita, is a shrub native to the Channel Islands off the coast of California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and smooth, reddish bark. The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a small, leathery capsule that contains several seeds.

Arctostaphylos insularis is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.
