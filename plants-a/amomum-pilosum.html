Amomum pilosum

Amomum pilosum, commonly known as the hairy cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, three-lobed berries that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its rhizomes are also utilized for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.

Amomum pilosum thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its economic and therapeutic value.
