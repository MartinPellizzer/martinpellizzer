Artemisia ludoviciana f. tenuifolia

Artemisia ludoviciana f. tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, narrow, and sometimes silver-gray leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in terminal panicles during the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe and desert habitats.

Its foliage is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
