Agapanthus weillighii

Agapanthus weillighii, commonly known as the blue agapanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical name honors the German botanist Friedrich Weilligh, reflecting its historical significance in botanical studies.
