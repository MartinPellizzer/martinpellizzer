Amaryllis cyrtanthoides

Amaryllis cyrtanthoides, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a distinctive yellow or green throat. The plant produces strap-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Amaryllis cyrtanthoides is known for its long blooming period, often extending from late spring through early autumn.
