Anthericum krauseanum

Anthericum krauseanum, commonly known as Krause's ladies' tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and long, spiraled inflorescences that bear numerous small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and requires well-drained, humus-rich soil. It produces small, yellowish-brown seeds that are dispersed by wind, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Anthericum krauseanum is a relatively rare species, often found in specific, undisturbed habitats.
