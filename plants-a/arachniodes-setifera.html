Arachniodes setifera

Arachniodes setifera, commonly known as the thread-leaf holly fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear fronds that resemble threads, giving it its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The botanical features of Arachniodes setifera make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
