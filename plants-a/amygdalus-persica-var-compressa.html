Amygdalus persica var. compressa

Amygdalus persica var. compressa, commonly known as the flat peach.

This variety is a cultivar of the peach tree, characterized by its flattened, compact fruit shape. The botanical features include a thick, fuzzy skin and a fleshy, juicy interior with a sweet, sometimes aromatic flavor. It is typically smaller than standard peaches and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance.

The plant is deciduous, producing flowers in spring followed by fruit development throughout the summer.
