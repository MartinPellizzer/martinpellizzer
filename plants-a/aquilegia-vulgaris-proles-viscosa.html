Aquilegia vulgaris proles viscosa

Aquilegia vulgaris proles viscosa, commonly known as the sticky celandine, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spurred flowers that typically range in color from yellow to red. The plant produces a sticky, milky sap that can be toxic if ingested. Its leaves are palmately compound, with three to five leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

This perennial species thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
