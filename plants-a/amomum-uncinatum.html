Amomum uncinatum

Amomum uncinatum, commonly known as wild cardamom, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces dark red or black berries that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its rhizomes and seeds are valued for their pungent, spicy flavor and medicinal properties.

Amomum uncinatum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
