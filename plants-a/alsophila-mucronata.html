Alsophila mucronata

Alsophila mucronata, commonly known as the dwarf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and a crown of feathery, compound fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are bioluminescent, emitting a faint blue glow at night, which aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

The plant's unique botanical features make it a valuable subject for both ecological and horticultural study.
