Asarum longerhizomatosum

Asarum longerhizomatosum, commonly known as the long-root asarum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, fragrant flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant has a long, thick rhizome that contributes to its common name and plays a role in its growth and survival. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and traditional herbalists alike.
