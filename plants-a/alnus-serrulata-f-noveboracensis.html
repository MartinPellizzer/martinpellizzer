Alnus serrulata f. noveboracensis

Alnus serrulata f. noveboracensis, commonly known as the New York white-ash, is a rare horticultural form of the American alder.

It is characterized by its finely serrated leaves and small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers. The plant is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its botanical features include a slender, multi-stemmed trunk and compound leaves with a distinctive silvery underside.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
