Adenandra alternifolia

Adenandra alternifolia, commonly known as the African snowball, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, giving it a snow-like appearance. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for small garden spaces or container planting.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.
