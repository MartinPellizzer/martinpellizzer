Arisaema biflagellatum

Arisaema biflagellatum, commonly known as the two-whiskered arisaema, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves and a unique, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are enclosed within a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Arisaema biflagellatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage structure.
