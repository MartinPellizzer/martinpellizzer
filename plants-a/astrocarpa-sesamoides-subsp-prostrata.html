Astrocarpa sesamoides subsp. prostrata

Astrocarpa sesamoides subsp.

prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate sesamoid astrocarpa, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapting to its harsh environment. Its flowers are typically small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
