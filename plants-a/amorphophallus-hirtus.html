Amorphophallus hirtus

Amorphophallus hirtus, commonly known as the hairy arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of South and Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

The underground tubers of A. hirtus are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a food source in certain regions.
