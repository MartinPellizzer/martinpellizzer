Arisaema tortuosum var. neglectum

Arisaema tortuosum var. neglectum, commonly known as the twisted thread-leaf arisaema, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiraling leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the arum family. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
