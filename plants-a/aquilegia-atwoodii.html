Aquilegia atwoodii

Aquilegia atwoodii, commonly known as Atwood's columbine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and vibrant, often blue or purple, flower colors. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven lobes, and its stems are hairy and upright.

Aquilegia atwoodii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
