Athanasia indica

Athanasia indica, commonly known as the Immortality flower, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Athanasia indica is known for its resilience, thriving in arid conditions and requiring minimal water.
