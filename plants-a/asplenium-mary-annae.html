Asplenium mary-annae

Asplenium mary-annae, commonly known as the Mary-Anne's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered closely together. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of the leaves.

Asplenium mary-annae is native to tropical regions of Africa and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
