Ardisia amherstiana var. lanceolata

Ardisia amherstiana var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf beautyberry, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in warm climates.
