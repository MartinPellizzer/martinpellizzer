Arthraxon lanceolatus var. meeboldii

Arthraxon lanceolatus var. meeboldii, commonly known as the narrowleaf arthraxon, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged soils. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a loose, open panicle, typical of the grass family.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
