Astragalus shahsavaranicus

Astragalus shahsavaranicus, commonly known as Shahsavaran goat's rue, is a flowering plant species native to the high-altitude regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its survival in harsh environments. It is adapted to rocky and arid conditions, showcasing a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
