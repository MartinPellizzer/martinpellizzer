Acacia burmanniana

Acacia burmanniana, commonly known as the Burmann's acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow to golden-green flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its pods are elongated, woody, and contain several flattened seeds.

Acacia burmanniana is often found in open woodlands and forest margins, thriving in well-drained soils.
