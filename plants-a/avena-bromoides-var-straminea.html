Avena bromoides var. straminea

Avena bromoides var. straminea, commonly known as the slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
