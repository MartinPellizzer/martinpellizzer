Arachnimorpha incana

Arachnimorpha incana, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals, containing solanine and other alkaloids. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Its unique morphology and chemical defenses make it a notable species in its ecological niche.
