Antirrhinum siculum var. purpurascens

Antirrhinum siculum var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically a striking shade of purple, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
