Aspalathus alpina

Aspalathus alpina, commonly known as the alpine tea or mountain tea, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Aspalathus alpina is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
