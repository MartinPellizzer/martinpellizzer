Avena candollei

Avena candollei, commonly known as the Andean oat, is a perennial grass native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Avena candollei is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in cool, temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and potential agricultural research.
