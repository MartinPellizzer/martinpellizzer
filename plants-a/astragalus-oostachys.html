Astragalus oostachys

Astragalus oostachys, commonly known as the Oostachy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its small, pod-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.
