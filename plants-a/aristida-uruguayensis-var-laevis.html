Aristida uruguayensis var. laevis

Aristida uruguayensis var. laevis, commonly known as the Uruguay hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually covered with fine hairs. The inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
