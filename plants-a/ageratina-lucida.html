Ageratina lucida

Ageratina lucida, commonly known as white crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most striking feature is the fluffy, white to pale blue flower heads that resemble small clouds, which bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Ageratina lucida is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
