Adenophyllum porophyllum var. cancellatum

Adenophyllum porophyllum var. cancellatum, commonly known as the laceleaf adenophyllum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
