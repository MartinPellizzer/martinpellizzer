Anemone pratensis var. patula

Anemone pratensis var. patula, commonly known as the meadow anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed, and its stems are hairy and often branching. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and damp grasslands.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
