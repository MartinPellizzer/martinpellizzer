Anchusa tinctoria

Anchusa tinctoria, commonly known as the dyer's bugle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

The plant is also valued for its historical use in dyeing textiles due to the presence of certain pigments in its flowers.
