Abildgaardia sphaerocarpa

Abildgaardia sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe-fruit plant, is a small shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, round, orange fruits that resemble tiny globes. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the development of its distinctive fruits. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to the dry, sunny conditions of its natural habitat.

Abildgaardia sphaerocarpa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
