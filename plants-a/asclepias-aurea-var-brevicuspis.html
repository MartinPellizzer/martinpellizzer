Asclepias aurea var. brevicuspis

Asclepias aurea var. brevicuspis, commonly known as the short-spined milkweed, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and fuzzy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces a milky sap that contains cardiac glycosides, which are toxic to many animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

This variety is an important host plant for the monarch butterfly, supporting the life cycle of this iconic species.
