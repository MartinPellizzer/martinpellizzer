Apogandrum

Apogandrum, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale greenish-white flowers that resemble a ghostly apparition, hence its common name. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Apogandrum has very specific habitat requirements, making it highly sensitive to environmental changes.
