Acacia riograndensis

Acacia riograndensis, commonly known as the Rio Grande acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Acacia riograndensis is also valued for its hard, durable wood.
