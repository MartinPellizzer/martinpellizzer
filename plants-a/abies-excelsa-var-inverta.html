Abies excelsa var. inverta

Abies excelsa var. inverta, commonly known as the Inverted White Fir, is a rare variant of the white fir species native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its distinctive upward-curving needles and a conical shape that becomes more elongated with age. The tree produces cone-like structures that are typically retained on the branches for several years, contributing to its ornamental value. Its foliage is dense and evergreen, providing year-round visual interest in its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
