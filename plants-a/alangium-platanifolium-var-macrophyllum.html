Alangium platanifolium var. macrophyllum

Alangium platanifolium var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved alangium, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange-red berries. Its leaves are thick and leathery, with a prominent midrib, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
