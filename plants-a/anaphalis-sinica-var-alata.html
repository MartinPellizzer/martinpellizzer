Anaphalis sinica var. alata

Anaphalis sinica var. alata, commonly known as the winged woolyhead, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fluffy, silver-gray leaves and small, yellowish flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its unique morphology, including the wing-like structures on its stems, contributes to its ornamental value.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits in gardens and natural habitats.
