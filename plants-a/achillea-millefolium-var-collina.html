Achillea millefolium var. collina

Achillea millefolium var. collina, commonly known as Alpine yarrow, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for alpine gardens. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in rocky areas.

The species is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine.
