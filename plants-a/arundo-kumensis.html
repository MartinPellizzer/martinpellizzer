Arundo kumensis

Arundo kumensis, commonly known as the Kuma reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in forested areas. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often used for thatching or weaving in local communities. Its flowering stems produce clusters of small, greenish flowers, which are less showy than those of other reed species.

Arundo kumensis is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
