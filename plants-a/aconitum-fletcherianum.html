Aconitum fletcherianum

Aconitum fletcherianum, commonly known as Fletcher's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions that are often hairy.

Aconitum fletcherianum is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and cooler climates, making it a hardy plant in alpine environments.
