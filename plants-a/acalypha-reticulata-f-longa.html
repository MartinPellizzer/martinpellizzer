Acalypha reticulata f. longa

Acalypha reticulata f. longa, commonly known as the long-leaved nettle or longleaf acalypha, is a variant of the nettleleaf plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on slender spikes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings for its unique foliage.
