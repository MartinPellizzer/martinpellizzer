Agatea

Agatea, commonly known as the agate tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its woody structure and showy, often colorful flowers that attract pollinators. The leaves are typically alternate and simple, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Agatea species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.
