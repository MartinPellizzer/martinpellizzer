Aristolochia championii

Aristolochia championii, commonly known as the Mexican Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and its striking, pipe-like flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, twisted floral structure. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
