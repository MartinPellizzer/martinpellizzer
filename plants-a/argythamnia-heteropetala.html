Argythamnia heteropetala

Argythamnia heteropetala, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are adapted for dispersal by wind. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for harsh desert environments.

Its fibrous leaves and stems were historically used by indigenous peoples for textile production.
