Achras capiri

Achras capiri, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, umbrella-shaped crown. The plant produces large, clustered fruits that are typically harvested when they are still green and firm. The leaves are palmate, with five to seven lobes, and the bark is thick and fibrous.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
