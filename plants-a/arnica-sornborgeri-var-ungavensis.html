Arnica sornborgeri var. ungavensis

Arnica sornborgeri var. ungavensis, commonly known as the Alpine Arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
