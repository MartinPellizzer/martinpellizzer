Anemone canadensis f. dicksonii

Anemone canadensis f. dicksonii, commonly known as the Dickson's Canadian anemone, is a variant of the Canadian anemone, a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive yellow center, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided, lobed leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.

This fern-like plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
