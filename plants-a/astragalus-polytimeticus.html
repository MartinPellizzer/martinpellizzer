Astragalus polytimeticus

Astragalus polytimeticus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces pods that contain small, rounded seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus polytimeticus plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
