Artemisia samamisica

Artemisia samamisica, commonly known as the Samos wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flower heads that are typically clustered in dense, compact inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its strong scent, it has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
