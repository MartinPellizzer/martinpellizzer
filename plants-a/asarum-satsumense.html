Asarum satsumense

Asarum satsumense, commonly known as Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, reddish-brown leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale greenish-yellow flowers that are highly fragrant. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
