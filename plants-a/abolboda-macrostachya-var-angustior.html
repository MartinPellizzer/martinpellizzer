Abolboda macrostachya var. angustior

Abolboda macrostachya var. angustior, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense stands in riparian zones. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This variety is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Abolboda macrostachya.
