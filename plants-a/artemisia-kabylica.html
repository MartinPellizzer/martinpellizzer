Artemisia kabylica

Artemisia kabylica, commonly known as Kabylian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains volatile oils and essential compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its name reflects its historical use in herbal remedies and its association with the Kabylian region of Albania.
