Apluda geniculata

Apluda geniculata, commonly known as the zigzag grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzag or sinuous growth pattern, which gives it its common name. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in loose panicles.

Apluda geniculata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry climates.
