Astragalus albicaulis

Astragalus albicaulis, commonly known as the white-stemmed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus albicaulis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
