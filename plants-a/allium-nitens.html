Allium nitens

Allium nitens, commonly known as the white garlic or white leek, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, bulb-like structures that are edible and have a mild garlic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium nitens is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its culinary uses.
