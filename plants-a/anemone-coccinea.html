Anemone coccinea

Anemone coccinea, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features a distinctive red-orange flower with a single petal-like structure, and its leaves are palmately lobed. The plant produces a yellowish-orange sap that contains toxic compounds, which can be harmful if ingested. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in rich, shady soils.

Anemone coccinea is known for its early spring blooming and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
