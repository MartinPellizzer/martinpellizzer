Asplenium lasiopteris

Asplenium lasiopteris, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a small evergreen fern native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly fronds that give it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rocky crevices or forest undergrowth. Its leaves are pinnately divided with a distinctive silvery-blue hue on the underside.

Asplenium lasiopteris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and indirect light.
