Astragalus pulsiferae var. suksdorfii

Astragalus pulsiferae var. suksdorfii, commonly known as the Suxdorf's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, harsh environments. It is known for its deep root system, which allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This variety is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role in stabilizing soils in arid landscapes.
