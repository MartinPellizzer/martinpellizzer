Acacia langsdorffii var. armata

Acacia langsdorffii var. armata, commonly known as the rough-barked acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and phyllodes that are narrow and slightly curved. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which appear during the warmer months. Its bark is rough and fissured, providing a distinctive texture.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.
