Aloitis falconeri

Aloitis falconeri, commonly known as the Falconeri's Aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that store water. The leaves are typically blue-green in color with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months.

This species is well adapted to extreme desert conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
