Andira gabonica

Andira gabonica, commonly known as the purple flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a strong fragrance. The plant produces small, round, woody fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy appearance.

Andira gabonica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
