Asparagus divaricatus

Asparagus divaricatus, commonly known as the silver thread asparagus, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems that often trail along the ground or climb supports. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Asparagus divaricatus thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
