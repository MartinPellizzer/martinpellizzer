Aspidosperma spruceanum

Aspidosperma spruceanum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pitanga*, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
