Arisaema undulatifolium subsp. nambae

Arisaema undulatifolium subsp.

nambae, commonly known as the Nambae cowslip, is a member of the Araceae family. It is native to parts of East Asia, particularly in regions of China and Korea.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive, undulating leaves and a striking, spadix-like inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
