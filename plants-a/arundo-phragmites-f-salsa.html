Arundo phragmites f. salsa

Arundo phragmites f. salsa, commonly known as the salt marsh reed, is a variant of the common reed that thrives in saline environments.

It is characterized by its robust, grass-like structure and can grow up to several meters in height. This plant has narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt. It plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal and wetland soils.

Its flowering stems produce dense clusters of small, greenish flowers that are adapted to withstand salty air and water.
