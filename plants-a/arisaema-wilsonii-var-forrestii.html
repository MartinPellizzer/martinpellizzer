Arisaema wilsonii var. forrestii

Arisaema wilsonii var. forrestii, commonly known as the Wilson's arisaema, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a distinctive veining pattern.
