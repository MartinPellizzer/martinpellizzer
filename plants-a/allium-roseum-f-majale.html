Allium roseum f. majale

Allium roseum f. majale, commonly known as the purple garlic chive, is a cultivar of the Allium genus characterized by its striking purple flowers and aromatic foliage.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often used in ornamental gardens for its visual appeal. The plant produces small, spherical bulbs that can be harvested for culinary use, similar to other Allium species. Its foliage is tender and edible, often used in salads or as a flavoring in various dishes.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its culinary versatility.
