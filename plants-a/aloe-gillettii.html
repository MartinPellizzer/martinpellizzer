Aloe gillettii

Aloe gillettii, commonly known as the Gillett's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow flowers, which bloom in summer. Aloe gillettii is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and ease of care make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
