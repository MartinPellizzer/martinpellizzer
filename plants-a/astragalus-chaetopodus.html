Astragalus chaetopodus

Astragalus chaetopodus, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and compound leaves with small, hairy stipules. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus chaetopodus is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert grassland habitats.
