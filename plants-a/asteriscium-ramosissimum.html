Asteriscium ramosissimum

Asteriscium ramosissimum, commonly known as the branched asterisk, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite and narrow, often covered in fine hairs.

Asteriscium ramosissimum is typically found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
