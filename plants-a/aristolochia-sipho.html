Aristolochia sipho

Aristolochia sipho, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex floral structures and historical use in traditional medicine.
