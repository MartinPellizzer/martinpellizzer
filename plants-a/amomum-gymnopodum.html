Amomum gymnopodum

Amomum gymnopodum, commonly known as the broadleaf cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.
