Arctostaphylos tomentosa f. trichoclada

Arctostaphylos tomentosa f. trichoclada, commonly known as the hairy manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the dry, Mediterranean climate of its native range.
