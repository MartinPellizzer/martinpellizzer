Astragalus kudrjaschovii

Astragalus kudrjaschovii, commonly known as Kudrjaschov's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers, which is a key feature of its survival strategy.

Astragalus kudrjaschovii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
