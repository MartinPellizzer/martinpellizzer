Anacheilium christensonii

Anacheilium christensonii, commonly known as the Christenson's ghost orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often pale green or white flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant has a compact growth habit with a single stem and is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor, sandy soils. It relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, making it highly sensitive to environmental changes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
