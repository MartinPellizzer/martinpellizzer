Adenandra gracilis

Adenandra gracilis, commonly known as the slender adenandra, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, which are often fragrant. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Adenandra gracilis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, compact growth.
