Anacharis canadensis var. latifolia

Anacharis canadensis var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf Canadian waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It features long, flexible stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often branching profusely. The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and arranged in whorls around the stem, typically measuring up to 5 centimeters in length. This plant thrives in slow-moving to still freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes.

It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.
