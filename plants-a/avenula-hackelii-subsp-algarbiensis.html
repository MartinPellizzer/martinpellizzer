Avenula hackelii subsp. algarbiensis

Avenula hackelii subsp.

algarbiensis, commonly known as the Algarbiensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

This subspecies is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in moist, often calcareous soils and is typically found in wetlands or along water margins.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
