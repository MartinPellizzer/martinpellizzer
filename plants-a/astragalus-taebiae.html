Astragalus taebiae

Astragalus taebiae, commonly known as the Taebi milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of many species in the Astragalus genus. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
