Acalypha betulina var. latifolia

Acalypha betulina var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf beauty leaf, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that range from red to orange, which are often mistaken for flowers. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they often exhibit a metallic sheen. This plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
