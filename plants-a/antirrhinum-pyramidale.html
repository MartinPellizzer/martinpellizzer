Antirrhinum pyramidale

Antirrhinum pyramidale, commonly known as the pyramid bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and produces clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Antirrhinum pyramidale is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors and ease of cultivation.
