Agave pachycentra

Agave pachycentra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agave, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color and have a waxy coating. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid climates.

Agave pachycentra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh environments.
