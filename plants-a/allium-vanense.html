Allium vanense

Allium vanense, commonly known as the Van garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and clusters of pale blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, white to pale pink bulbs that are edible and have a mild garlic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or alpine beds.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its culinary uses in various regional cuisines.
