Astragalus cataonicus

Astragalus cataonicus, commonly known as the Cataonic milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
