Abelia spathulata f. subtetrasepala

Abelia spathulata f. subtetrasepala, commonly known as the four-lobed abelia, is a unique variant of the abelia plant characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

This cultivar features flowers with four lobes instead of the typical five, giving it a more compact and refined appearance. The plant is known for its showy, fragrant blooms that range in color from white to pink, depending on the specific cultivar. It typically grows as an upright shrub with glossy, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its flowers.

This variety is prized in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to a range of growing conditions.
