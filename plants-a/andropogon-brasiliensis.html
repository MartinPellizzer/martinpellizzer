Andropogon brasiliensis

Andropogon brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian bluestem, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Andropogon brasiliensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
