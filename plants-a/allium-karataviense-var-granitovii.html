Allium karataviense var. granitovii

Allium karataviense var. granitovii, commonly known as the Karatavsky garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showing resilience to harsh climatic conditions. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a valuable addition to local cuisines.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
