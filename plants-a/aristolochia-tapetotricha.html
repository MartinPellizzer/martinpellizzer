Aristolochia tapetotricha

Aristolochia tapetotricha, commonly known as the velvetleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, intricate, and often fragrant flowers that resemble a pipe, with a complex, winding structure. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and its stems are covered in fine, hair-like structures. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its unique floral morphology and chemical compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
