Astragalus reinii

Astragalus reinii, commonly known as the rein's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, featuring deep root systems that allow it to survive in dry, rocky soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.

Astragalus reinii is also of interest in botanical studies due to its unique adaptations and ecological significance.
