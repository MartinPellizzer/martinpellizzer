Avenula gervaisii subsp. arundana

Avenula gervaisii subsp.

arundana, commonly known as the redstem oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant has slender, wiry stems that are often reddish in color, especially when mature. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, with small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green appearance and a hairy texture.
