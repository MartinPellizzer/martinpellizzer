Astragalus inflexus var. flocculatus

Astragalus inflexus var. flocculatus, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive, woolly texture. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert grassland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
