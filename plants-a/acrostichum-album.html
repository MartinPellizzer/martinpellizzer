Acrostichum album

Acrostichum album, commonly known as the white fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the fern family (Polypodiaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found near water sources. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of its leaves.

Acrostichum album is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
