Astragalus terekliensis

Astragalus terekliensis, commonly known as the Terek's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its compound leaves are pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus terekliensis plays a role in nitrogen fixation and is of interest in ecological and medicinal research.
