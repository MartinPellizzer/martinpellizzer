Astragalus pskemensis

Astragalus pskemensis, commonly known as Pskem Mountain milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus pskemensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization.

Its limited distribution makes it a subject of interest for botanical research and conservation efforts.
