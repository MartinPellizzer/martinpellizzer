Arundo pilosa

Arundo pilosa, commonly known as narrowleaf reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive tuft of silky hairs at the base of each leaf blade. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers during the summer months. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks.

Its fibrous roots make it an important species for erosion control and habitat provision.
