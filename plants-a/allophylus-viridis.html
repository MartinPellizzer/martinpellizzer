Allophylus viridis

Allophylus viridis, commonly known as green alder, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It typically grows to a height of 2–5 meters and has compound leaves with 5–9 pairs of oval, serrated leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, round fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Allophylus viridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
