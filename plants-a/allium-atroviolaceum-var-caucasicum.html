Allium atroviolaceum var. caucasicum

Allium atroviolaceum var. caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian purple onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible bulbs.
