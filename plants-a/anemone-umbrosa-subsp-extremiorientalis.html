Anemone umbrosa subsp. extremiorientalis

Anemone umbrosa subsp.

extremiorientalis, commonly known as the Siberian anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to heart-shaped with a velvety texture.

The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
