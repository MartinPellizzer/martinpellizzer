Albizia glaberrima var. mpwapwensis

Albizia glaberrima var. mpwapwensis, commonly known as the African silk tree, is a deciduous legume native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Albizia glaberrima var. mpwapwensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.
