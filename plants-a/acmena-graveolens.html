Acmena graveolens

Acmena graveolens, commonly known as the sour cherry or cherry gum, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to eastern Australia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and often have a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. The berries are edible but have a tart, acidic flavor, making them popular in culinary uses.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry woodland habitats.
