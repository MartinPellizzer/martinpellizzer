Abies nigra var. nana

Abies nigra var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf fir, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It features dense, dark green foliage that is shorter and more rounded compared to the typical fir species. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that mature to a reddish-brown color, typical of the fir genus. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its slow growth rate makes it an ideal choice for rock gardens or as a decorative plant in woodland settings.
