Acacia pluricapitata

Acacia pluricapitata, commonly known as the many-headed acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with numerous small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular inflorescences, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in woody pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Acacia pluricapitata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions.
