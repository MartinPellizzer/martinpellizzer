Astragalus kaufmannii

Astragalus kaufmannii, commonly known as Kaufmann's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus kaufmannii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
