Aconitum heterophyllum var. roylei

Aconitum heterophyllum var. roylei, commonly known as the Himalayan monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers, which are arranged in a helmet-like shape, and its deeply lobed, palmately compound leaves. It is a perennial herb that can grow up to 2 meters tall, with a fibrous root system.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
