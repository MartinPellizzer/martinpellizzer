Adesmia tenella var. misera

Adesmia tenella var. misera, commonly known as the narrowleaf adesmia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought conditions. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in racemes, and it has a fibrous root system.

It is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine by local communities.
