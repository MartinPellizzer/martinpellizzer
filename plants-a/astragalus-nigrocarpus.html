Astragalus nigrocarpus

Astragalus nigrocarpus, commonly known as blackpod milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, dark-colored pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and woodlands.

Astragalus nigrocarpus plays a role in nitrogen fixation and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
