Astragalus tarumensis

Astragalus tarumensis, commonly known as the Tarum Desert Milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological significance lies in its ability to thrive in harsh environments, contributing to biodiversity in desert ecosystems.
