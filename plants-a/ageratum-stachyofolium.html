Ageratum stachyofolium

Ageratum stachyofolium, commonly known as the sticky leaf ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The leaves are typically hairy and have a distinctive sticky texture, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
