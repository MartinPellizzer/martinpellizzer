Aster alpinus subsp. cylleneus

Aster alpinus subsp.

cylleneus, commonly known as the Alpine aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes.

The plant produces showy, violet-blue to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
