Aster illinitus

Aster illinitus, commonly known as the Illinois aster, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, purple to blue flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and roadsides. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster illinitus is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
