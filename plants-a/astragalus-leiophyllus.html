Astragalus leiophyllus

Astragalus leiophyllus, commonly known as the smooth-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

It is adapted to dry environments and plays a role in soil stabilization and as a food source for various wildlife species.
