Allium haussknechtii

Allium haussknechtii, commonly known as the Hausknecht's garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks, and is characterized by its fibrous, bulb-like structure. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in a spherical inflorescence. Its leaves are grass-like and narrow, and the plant is adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

Allium haussknechtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
