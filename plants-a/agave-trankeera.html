Agave trankeera

Agave trankeera, commonly known as the Mexican fireplant, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers in clusters at the top of the stem, which bloom in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
