Acacia crassicarpa

Acacia crassicarpa, commonly known as the thick-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The acacia also plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
