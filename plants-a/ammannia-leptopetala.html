Ammannia leptopetala

Ammannia leptopetala, commonly known as the slenderleaf ammannia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Ammannia leptopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
