Artemisia knorringiana

Artemisia knorringiana, commonly known as Knorring's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of narrow, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
