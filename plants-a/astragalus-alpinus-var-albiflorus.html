Astragalus alpinus var. albiflorus

Astragalus alpinus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the alpine milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, compound leaves that are often hairy. The flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer. This plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
