Adiantum tenerum var. obtusissimum

Adiantum tenerum var. obtusissimum, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a delicate and ornamental fern native to South America.

It features finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are covered in a fine, silky coating, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its fronds are typically a deep green, and they remain evergreen throughout the year.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings or as a houseplant.
