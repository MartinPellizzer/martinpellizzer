Agropyron alaskanum

Agropyron alaskanum, commonly known as Alaska wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine and arctic tundra environments. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Agropyron alaskanum is also valued for its potential use in ecological restoration and livestock forage.
