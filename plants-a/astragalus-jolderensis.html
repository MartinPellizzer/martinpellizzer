Astragalus jolderensis

Astragalus jolderensis, commonly known as the Jolder's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus jolderensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
