Acacia neriifolia

Acacia neriifolia, commonly known as the black-leaf acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its distinctive, dark green, almost black leaves that are narrow and linear. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spherical clusters, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Acacia neriifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

It is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
