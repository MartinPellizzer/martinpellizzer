Alyxia oleifolia

Alyxia oleifolia, commonly known as the oil plant, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in coastal areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
