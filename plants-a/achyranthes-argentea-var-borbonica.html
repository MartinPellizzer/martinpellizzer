Achyranthes argentea var. borbonica

Achyranthes argentea var. borbonica, commonly known as silverleaf alyssum, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, pubescent hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
