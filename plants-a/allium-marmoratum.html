Allium marmoratum

Allium marmoratum, commonly known as the marbled garlic.

This perennial bulbous plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus. It features striking, waxy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a strong garlic scent and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its foliage consists of grass-like leaves that are dense and form a basal rosette.
