Adesmia brachysemeon

Adesmia brachysemeon, commonly known as the narrow-seeded legume, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a small shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The plant produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America, it is adapted to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and sustainable land management.
