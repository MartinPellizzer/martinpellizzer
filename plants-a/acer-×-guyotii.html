Acer × guyotii

Acer × guyotii, commonly known as the Oregon maple, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree produces small, red flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and conservation efforts.
