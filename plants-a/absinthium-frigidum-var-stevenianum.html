Absinthium frigidum var. stevenianum

Absinthium frigidum var. stevenianum, commonly known as frost absinth, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
