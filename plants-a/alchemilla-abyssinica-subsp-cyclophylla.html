Alchemilla abyssinica subsp. cyclophylla

Alchemilla abyssinica subsp.

cyclophylla, commonly known as the Abyssinian lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Ethiopia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of soft, hairy leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture, though it is less commonly cultivated than other alchemilla species.
