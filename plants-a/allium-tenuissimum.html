Allium tenuissimum

Allium tenuissimum, commonly known as slender leek or wild leek, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a useful plant in both culinary and medicinal contexts.

Allium tenuissimum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
