Acrocomia cubensis

Acrocomia cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban palm, is a tall, slender palm native to Cuba and parts of the Caribbean.

It features a smooth, grayish trunk that can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant produces large, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its flowers are small and clustered in branched inflorescences, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by both wildlife and humans.

The Cuban palm is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in local ecosystems.
