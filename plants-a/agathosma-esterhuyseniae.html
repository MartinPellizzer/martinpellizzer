Agathosma esterhuyseniae

Agathosma esterhuyseniae, commonly known as the Esterhuysenia wild thyme, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
