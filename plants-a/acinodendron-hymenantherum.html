Acinodendron hymenantherum

Acinodendron hymenantherum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has a rugged, fissured bark and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it produces woody pods that contain seeds.

The botanical features of this species make it a striking ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical climates.
