Aerangis potamophila

Aerangis potamophila, commonly known as the river orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments near rivers and streams. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a waxy coating to retain moisture.

Aerangis potamophila is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
