Actinostrobus pyramidalis var. arenarius

Actinostrobus pyramidalis var. arenarius, commonly known as the coastal bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes, typically bearing red or pink flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is adapted to salty, well-drained environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh coastal conditions.
