Asarum blumei var. albivenium

Asarum blumei var. albivenium, commonly known as the white-veined wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with distinctive white veins. The plant produces a unique, fragrant, and often reddish-brown flower that is highly specialized in structure. Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
