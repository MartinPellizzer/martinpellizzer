Alifiola dichotoma

Alifiola dichotoma, commonly known as the dichotomous leaf plant, is a species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive dichotomous branching pattern, where each stem splits into two equal parts. The plant typically has simple, alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped or ovate. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or swampy areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
