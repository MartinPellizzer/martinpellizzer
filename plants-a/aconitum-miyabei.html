Aconitum miyabei

Aconitum miyabei, commonly known as the Japanese monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring palmate leaves with a distinctive five-lobed structure. Its roots contain toxic alkaloids, making it a plant of both botanical interest and caution.

Aconitum miyabei thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.
