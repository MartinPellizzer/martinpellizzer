Asplenium eatonii

Asplenium eatonii, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a member of the Aspleniaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has finely divided, bright green fronds with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. Its rhizomes are wiry and capable of producing new growth.

Asplenium eatonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
