Acer pseudoplatanus f. quadricolor

Acer pseudoplatanus f. quadricolor, commonly known as the variegated maple, is a unique form of the common maple.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display a combination of green, white, and yellow hues. This ornamental plant is native to the western regions of Europe and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal. The leaves turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the autumn, adding to its ornamental value.

It typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.
