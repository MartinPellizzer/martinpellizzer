Agropyron elongatum subsp. ponticum

Agropyron elongatum subsp.

ponticum, commonly known as the Pontic wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and agricultural fields. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
