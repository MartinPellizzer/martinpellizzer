Arundinella acratherum

Arundinella acratherum, commonly known as the narrowleaf bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a grassy aroma.

Arundinella acratherum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
