Acipetalum rugosum

Acipetalum rugosum, commonly known as the rough acipetalum, is a shrub native to the western regions of the United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its botanical features include a woody stem and opposite leaf arrangement.

It is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
