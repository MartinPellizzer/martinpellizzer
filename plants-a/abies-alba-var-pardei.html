Abies alba var. pardei

Abies alba var. pardei, commonly known as the white fir, is a variant of the European fir tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its conical shape, needle-like leaves, and distinctive cones. The foliage is typically a silvery-white color, which sets it apart from other fir species. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
