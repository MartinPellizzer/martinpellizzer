Appendicula brassii

Appendicula brassii, commonly known as the yellow alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Appendicula brassii is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.
