Aglaia andamanica

Aglaia andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman paradise tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, yellow flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain aromatic, oily seeds used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
