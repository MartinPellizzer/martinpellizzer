Amaryllis paradisicola

Amaryllis paradisicola, commonly known as the paradise amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a distinctive yellow or orange throat. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Amaryllis paradisicola is popular in gardens and as a houseplant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
