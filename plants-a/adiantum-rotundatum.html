Adiantum rotundatum

Adiantum rotundatum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green and have a distinctive, lacy appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in woodland areas. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Adiantum rotundatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
