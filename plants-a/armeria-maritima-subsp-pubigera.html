Armeria maritima subsp. pubigera

Armeria maritima subsp.

pubigera, commonly known as the coastal thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North America. It features dense, silver-gray foliage with tightly packed, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is highly tolerant of salt spray and coastal conditions. Its compact form and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and supporting local pollinator populations.
