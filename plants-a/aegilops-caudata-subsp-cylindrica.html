Aegilops caudata subsp. cylindrica

Aegilops caudata subsp.

cylindrica, commonly known as the slender wild oat, is a grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as fields, roadsides, and disturbed soils. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and has a fibrous root system.

This subspecies is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
