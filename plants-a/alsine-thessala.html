Alsine thessala

Alsine thessala, commonly known as the Greek sandwort, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry, open habitats. The plant has small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers. It is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy blooms.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
