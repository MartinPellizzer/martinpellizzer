Astragalus bavanaticus

Astragalus bavanaticus, commonly known as the Bavanat milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is known for its deep penetration, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus bavanaticus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
