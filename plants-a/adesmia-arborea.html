Adesmia arborea

Adesmia arborea, commonly known as the tree legume, is a perennial shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing capabilities through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and its leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets.

Adesmia arborea is often found in dry, arid environments and is valued for its hardy nature and potential ecological benefits.
