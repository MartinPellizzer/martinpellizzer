Anthyllis forondae

Anthyllis forondae, commonly known as the yellow sweet clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Anthyllis forondae is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
