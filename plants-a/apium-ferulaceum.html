Apium ferulaceum

Apium ferulaceum, commonly known as wild celery, is a flowering plant in the family Apiaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in moist, disturbed soils. The plant has slender, hollow stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its small yellow flowers are arranged in umbels, a characteristic feature of the Apiaceae family.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties.
