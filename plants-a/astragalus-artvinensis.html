Astragalus artvinensis

Astragalus artvinensis, commonly known as the Artvin milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern region of Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to the mountainous terrain of its native habitat.

Astragalus artvinensis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
