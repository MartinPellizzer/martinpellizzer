Asyneuma cupulare

Asyneuma cupulare, commonly known as the hooded bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hooded flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.
