Anthemis chrysoleuca

Anthemis chrysoleuca, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble small suns. The plant produces small, tightly packed flower heads that are often used in floral arrangements due to their longevity. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
