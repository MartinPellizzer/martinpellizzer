Amaranthus blitum var. ascendens

Amaranthus blitum var. ascendens, commonly known as the redstem common amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, spiny foliage. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into three-angled, prickly seed capsules. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a weed and a potential forage crop.
