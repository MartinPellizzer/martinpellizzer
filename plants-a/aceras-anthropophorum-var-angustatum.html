Aceras anthropophorum var. angustatum

Aceras anthropophorum var. angustatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

It is notable for its showy blooms and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
