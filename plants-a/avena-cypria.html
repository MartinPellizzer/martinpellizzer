Avena cypria

Avena cypria, commonly known as Cyprus oat, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Avena cypria is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
