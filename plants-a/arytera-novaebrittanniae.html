Arytera novaebrittanniae

Arytera novaebrittanniae, commonly known as the New Britain arum, is a tropical plant native to the island of New Britain in the Pacific.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
