Aspalathus grandiflorus

Aspalathus grandiflorus, commonly known as the African red bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, bright red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid environments. It is closely related to the rooibos plant and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

The botanical characteristics of Aspalathus grandiflorus make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
