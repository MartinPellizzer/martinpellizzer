Anthyllis illyrica var. variiflora

Anthyllis illyrica var. variiflora, commonly known as the variable flax vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellowish or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its flowers are arranged in dense spikes, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is notable for its variability in flower size and leaf shape, which contributes to its common name.
