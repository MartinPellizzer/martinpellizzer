Artemisia capillaris

Artemisia capillaris, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Asia and parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its strong medicinal and pesticidal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been used traditionally in herbal medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
