Arenaria graminea var. grandiflora

Arenaria graminea var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas or rocky habitats. The plant features slender stems that bear small, green leaves and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
