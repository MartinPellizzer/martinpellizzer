Adiantum acrocarpon

Adiantum acrocarpon, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Adiantum acrocarpon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
