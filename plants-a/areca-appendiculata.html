Areca appendiculata

Areca appendiculata, commonly known as the red areca or red palm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by bright red fruits. Areca appendiculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and visually appealing species within the palm family.
