Amorphophallus loerzingii

Amorphophallus loerzingii, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces large, arrow-shaped tubers that are used as a starchy food source in some regions. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
