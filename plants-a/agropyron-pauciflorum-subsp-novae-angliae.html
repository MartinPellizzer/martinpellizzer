Agropyron pauciflorum subsp. novae-angliae

Agropyron pauciflorum subsp.

novae-angliae, commonly known as the New England wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
