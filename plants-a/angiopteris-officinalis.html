Angiopteris officinalis

Angiopteris officinalis, commonly known as the medicinal water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately divided fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in dense forests. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is notable for its ability to reproduce through spores.
