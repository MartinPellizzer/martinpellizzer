Anthyllis subsimplex subsp. valentina

Anthyllis subsimplex subsp.

valentina, commonly known as the Valentina sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, pink to lilac-colored flowers.

The plant features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
