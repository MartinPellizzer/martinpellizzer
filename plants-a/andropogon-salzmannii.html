Andropogon salzmannii

Andropogon salzmannii, commonly known as Salzmann's bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green blades that are often tinged with silver. The plant produces inflorescences of spikelets that are typically reddish-brown in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common species in open woodlands and prairie habitats.

Andropogon salzmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
