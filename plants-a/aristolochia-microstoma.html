Aristolochia microstoma

Aristolochia microstoma, commonly known as the small-stemd Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe, with a complex, winding structure. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are typically slender and twining. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its unique floral morphology and some species contain toxic compounds.
