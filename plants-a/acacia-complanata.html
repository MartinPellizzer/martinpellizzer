Acacia complanata

Acacia complanata, commonly known as the flat-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, flat leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Acacia complanata is adapted to dry environments and is often found in coastal and sandy soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
