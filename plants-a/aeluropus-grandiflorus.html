Aeluropus grandiflorus

Aeluropus grandiflorus, commonly known as the broad-leaved saltbush, is a flowering plant native to coastal and saline environments in Australia.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear white or pale blue flowers, which are often clustered in dense heads.
