Asplenium macraei var. originarium

Asplenium macraei var. originarium, commonly known as the originarian maidenhair fern, is a rare and distinctive variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, delicate fronds that exhibit a striking contrast between the dark green upper surface and the lighter green underside. Native to specific regions in South America, this plant thrives in shaded, moist environments with high humidity. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

The plant's slow growth and specific habitat requirements contribute to its rarity in the wild.
