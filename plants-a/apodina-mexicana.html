Apodina mexicana

Apodina mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican spider plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is a perennial herb native to Mexico and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that are arranged in clusters, followed by red berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Apodina mexicana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
