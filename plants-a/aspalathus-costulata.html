Aspalathus costulata

Aspalathus costulata, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

Aspalathus costulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
