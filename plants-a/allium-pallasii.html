Allium pallasii

Allium pallasii, commonly known as the Pallas' onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous root system and grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Allium pallasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
