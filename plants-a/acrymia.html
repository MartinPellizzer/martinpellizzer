Acrymia

Acrymia, commonly known as the Chinese date or longan, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is valued for its edible fruit, which has a sweet, aromatic flavor. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically harvested when they turn red or yellow. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

Acrymia is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value in gardens.
