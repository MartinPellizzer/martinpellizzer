Andropogon longipes

Andropogon longipes, commonly known as the long-leaved bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, reaching heights of up to six feet. The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves that are often tinged with silver, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Andropogon longipes plays an important role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
