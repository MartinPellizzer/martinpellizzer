Abies venusta

Abies venusta, commonly known as the silver fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its distinctive silvery-blue needles that are covered in fine, white stomatal lines. The tree produces cones that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters long, containing numerous small, oval seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age, and it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

Abies venusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its elegant appearance.
