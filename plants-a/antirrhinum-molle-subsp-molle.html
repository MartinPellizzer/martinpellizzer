Antirrhinum molle subsp. molle

Antirrhinum molle subsp.

molle, commonly known as the common snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that range in color from red to yellow.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
