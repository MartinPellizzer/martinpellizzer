Acacia xiphocarpa

Acacia xiphocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are narrow and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in spherical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Acacia xiphocarpa is also valued for its hardy nature and is used in reforestation and soil stabilization efforts.
