Acacia dampieri

Acacia dampieri, commonly known as the West Australian acacia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, and its yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in poor soils. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.

Due to its hardy nature, it is also cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
