Althaea oblongifolia

Althaea oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf mallows, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces showy, pink to lavender flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Althaea oblongifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
