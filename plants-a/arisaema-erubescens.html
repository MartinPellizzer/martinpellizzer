Arisaema erubescens

Arisaema erubescens, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking, umbrella-like inflorescences with white to pinkish spatheae. The plant's flowers are pollinated by insects, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
