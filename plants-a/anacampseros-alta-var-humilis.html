Anacampseros alta var. humilis

Anacampseros alta var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf rock cactus, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to southern Africa.

It features rounded, fleshy stems that are typically green or bluish-green in color, often adorned with small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves and stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it an ideal choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular ornamental plant among succulent enthusiasts.
