Andrographis explicata

Andrographis explicata, commonly known as the broad-leaved andrographis, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

The leaves are typically harvested for use in traditional medicine and herbal remedies.
