Aylthonia tomentosa

Aylthonia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Aylthonia tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
