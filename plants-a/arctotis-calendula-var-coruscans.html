Arctotis calendula var. coruscans

Arctotis calendula var. coruscans, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to gardens. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring minimal water once established.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
