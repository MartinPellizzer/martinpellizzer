Arrhenatherum avenaceum var. precatorium

Arrhenatherum avenaceum var. precatorium, commonly known as the early meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, and its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense panicle of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or brown.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting insect and bird life.
