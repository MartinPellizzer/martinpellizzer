Arachis pietrarellii

Arachis pietrarellii, commonly known as the Brazilian peanut or golden peanut, is a species of legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its bushy growth habit and produces yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. The plant is known for its high oil content and is cultivated for both culinary and industrial uses. It thrives in warm climates and well-drained soils, requiring a long growing season.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in regions where it is grown for its nutritional value and economic significance.
