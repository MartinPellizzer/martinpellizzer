Azalea tashiroi

Azalea tashiroi, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with leathery, elliptical leaves. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Azalea tashiroi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant spring blooms.
