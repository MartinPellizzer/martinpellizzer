Albuca subglandulosa

Albuca subglandulosa, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its subglandular leaf bases. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Albuca subglandulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
