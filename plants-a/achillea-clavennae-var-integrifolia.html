Achillea clavennae var. integrifolia

Achillea clavennae var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Alpine Yarrow, is a perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features finely divided, gray-green leaves that are entire, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine habitats.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
