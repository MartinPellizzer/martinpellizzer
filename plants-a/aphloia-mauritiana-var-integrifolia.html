Aphloia mauritiana var. integrifolia

Aphloia mauritiana var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Mauritius hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Mascarene Islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The leaves are usually large, heart-shaped, and have a smooth edge, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

This plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
