Astragalus fodinarum

Astragalus fodinarum, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, compound leaves that are typically narrow and hairy. The plant produces yellow to reddish-purple flowers arranged in clusters, which attract various pollinators. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America, it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

Astragalus fodinarum is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to dry, nutrient-poor environments.
