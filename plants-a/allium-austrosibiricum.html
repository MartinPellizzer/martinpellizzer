Allium austrosibiricum

Allium austrosibiricum, commonly known as the Siberian leek, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces umbels of white to pale purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
