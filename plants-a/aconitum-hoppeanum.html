Aconitum hoppeanum

Aconitum hoppeanum, commonly known as the Hoppe's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that are covered in a dense layer of silky hairs.

Aconitum hoppeanum contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling and cultivation.
