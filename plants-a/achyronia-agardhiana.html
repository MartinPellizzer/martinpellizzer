Achyronia agardhiana

Achyronia agardhiana, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in high-altitude environments. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

Achyronia agardhiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
