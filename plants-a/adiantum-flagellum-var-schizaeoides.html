Adiantum flagellum var. schizaeoides

Adiantum flagellum var. schizaeoides, commonly known as the fragrant maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It features finely divided, feathery fronds that are typically bright green and have a soft, silky texture. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its small, spore-bearing structures are located on the underside of the fronds, and it is known for its pleasant, minty fragrance.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and indoor spaces.
