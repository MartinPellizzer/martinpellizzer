Aruncus sylvester var. camschaticus

Aruncus sylvester var. camschaticus, commonly known as the Caucasian goat's beard, is a perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features tall, spiky inflorescences that are typically white or pale pink, resembling the beard of a goat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly serrated along the edges. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
