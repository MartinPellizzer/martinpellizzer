Allium grandiflorum

Allium grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant globe flower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features tall, upright stems that bear clusters of large, showy purple or lilac-colored flowers. The plant's foliage consists of broad, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Allium grandiflorum is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
