Azalea keiskei

Azalea keiskei, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub with dense, evergreen foliage. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Azalea keiskei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant spring blooms.
