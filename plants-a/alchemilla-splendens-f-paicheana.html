Alchemilla splendens f. paicheana

Alchemilla splendens f. paicheana, commonly known as the golden alchemilla, is a perennial plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features soft, fuzzy foliage that ranges from silver-green to golden hues, which becomes more pronounced in the sunlight. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters during the spring and early summer.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements in shady garden settings.
