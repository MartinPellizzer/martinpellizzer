Atractogyne bracteata

Atractogyne bracteata, commonly known as the giant water yam, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, spadix-like flowers. The plant produces edible tubers that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, humid environments and is typically found near water bodies.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
