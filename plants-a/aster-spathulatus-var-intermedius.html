Aster spathulatus var. intermedius

Aster spathulatus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of blue to purple, blooming from late summer into early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
