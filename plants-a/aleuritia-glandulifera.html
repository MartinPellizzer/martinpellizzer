Aleuritia glandulifera

Aleuritia glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular aleurita, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, oval leaves with a glossy surface and prominent glandular dots along the veins. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red berries in the fall. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for small gardens or as a hedge.

Aleuritia glandulifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
