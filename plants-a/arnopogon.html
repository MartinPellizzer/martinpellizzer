Arnopogon

Arnopogon, commonly known as the golden grass or arnopogon grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, often golden-colored leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Arnopogon species are known for their small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets.

These plants are often used in ecological restoration projects due to their ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
