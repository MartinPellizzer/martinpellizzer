Acacia xerophila var. brevior

Acacia xerophila var. brevior, commonly known as the short-stemmed acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, woody stems and small, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. The plant has small, bipinnate leaves that reduce water loss, adapting to its harsh environment. It produces yellow flowers in spring, which attract nectar-feeding insects and birds.

This variety is often used in reforestation projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
