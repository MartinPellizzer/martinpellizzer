Arum auriculatum

Arum auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features arrow-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, and its flowers are typically clustered around the spadix. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forest understories.

The plant is also known for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its unique foliage and flowering structure.
