Azalea aucubifolia

Azalea aucubifolia, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1-3 meters and features broad, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms and ability to form dense, compact growth.
