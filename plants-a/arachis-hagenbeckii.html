Arachis hagenbeckii

Arachis hagenbeckii, commonly known as the wild peanut, is a species of legume native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to grow in dry and sandy soils, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Arachis hagenbeckii is valued for its potential in agriculture and as a source of edible seeds.
