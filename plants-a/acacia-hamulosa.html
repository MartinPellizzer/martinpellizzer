Acacia hamulosa

Acacia hamulosa, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are followed by woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Acacia hamulosa is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fuelwood.
