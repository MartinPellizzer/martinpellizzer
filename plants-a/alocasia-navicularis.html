Alocasia navicularis

Alocasia navicularis, commonly known as the arrow leaf alocasia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, featuring a deep green color with a waxy texture. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix and a spathe. Alocasia navicularis thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
