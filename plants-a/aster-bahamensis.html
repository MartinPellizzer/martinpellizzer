Aster bahamensis

Aster bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian aster, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands, particularly the Bahamas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salt spray. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Aster bahamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
