Aristolochia triangulifolia

Aristolochia triangulifolia, commonly known as the triangular leaf arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, intricate flowers that resemble a pipe or a horn. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its unique morphology and chemical compounds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
