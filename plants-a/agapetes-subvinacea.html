Agapetes subvinacea

Agapetes subvinacea, commonly known as the red-flowered silver fir, is a species of shrub belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes, particularly in countries like Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, evergreen foliage, and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pinkish-red. Its leaves are typically leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the colorful blooms.

Agapetes subvinacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
