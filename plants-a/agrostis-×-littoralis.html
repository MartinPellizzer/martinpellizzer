Agrostis × littoralis

Agrostis × littoralis, commonly known as the coastal bentgrass, is a hybrid grass species that thrives in coastal environments.

It is characterized by its low-growing, dense foliage and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils near the shore. This plant is well adapted to salt spray and wind, making it a common species in maritime ecosystems. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce lightweight seeds that are easily dispersed by the wind.

Agrostis × littoralis plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and preventing erosion.
