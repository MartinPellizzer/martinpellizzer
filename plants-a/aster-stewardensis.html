Aster stewardensis

Aster stewardensis, commonly known as the Stewart's aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing purple to pinkish-blue blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
