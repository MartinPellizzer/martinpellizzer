Agapetes kingdonis

Agapetes kingdonis, commonly known as the Kingdon's silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are leathery and densely packed, giving it a silver-green appearance due to a waxy coating.

Agapetes kingdonis is well adapted to cool, moist environments and thrives in partial shade.
