Astrophytum lepidum

Astrophytum lepidum, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Astrophytum lepidum is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and unique appearance.
