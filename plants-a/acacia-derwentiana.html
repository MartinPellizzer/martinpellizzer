Acacia derwentiana

Acacia derwentiana, commonly known as the Derwent acacia, is a shrub native to Tasmania, Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in compact, spherical inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it is often found growing in coastal and sandy soils.

Acacia derwentiana is adapted to a range of environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
