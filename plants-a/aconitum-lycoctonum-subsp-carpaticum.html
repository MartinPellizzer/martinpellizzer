Aconitum lycoctonum subsp. carpaticum

Aconitum lycoctonum subsp.

carpaticum, commonly known as the Carpathian monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It features striking violet-blue flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, typical of the genus Aconitum.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive basal rosette, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

However, it contains potent toxins and should be handled with care.
