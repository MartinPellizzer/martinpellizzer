Acrostichum antrophyoides

Acrostichum antrophyoides, commonly known as the giant reed or giant swamp reed, is a tall, robust grass-like plant native to wetland and swampy areas in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has long, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in length, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
