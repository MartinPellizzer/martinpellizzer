Acrocephalus elongatus

Acrocephalus elongatus, commonly known as the long-headed reed warbler, is a grass-like plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its flowering spikes are usually elongated and appear at the end of the stems, contributing to its common name.
