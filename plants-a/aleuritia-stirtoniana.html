Aleuritia stirtoniana

Aleuritia stirtoniana, commonly known as the Stewart's elderberry, is a shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Aleuritia stirtoniana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
