Anthyllis vulneraria f. carpaticola

Anthyllis vulneraria f. carpaticola, commonly known as the Carpathian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in dense spikes. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and is adapted to rocky and arid environments. It is native to the Carpathian Mountains and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
