Andropogon fragilis var. luzoniensis

Andropogon fragilis var. luzoniensis, commonly known as Luzon grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and forest edges.

Luzon grass plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
