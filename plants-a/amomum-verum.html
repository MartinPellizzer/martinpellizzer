Amomum verum

Amomum verum, commonly known as cardamom, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is cultivated for its aromatic seed pods. The plant produces small, green, finger-like spikes that mature into dark brown, aromatic pods containing aromatic seeds. These seeds are widely used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical characteristics include rhizomatous growth and lanceolate leaves with a fragrant, slightly spicy scent.
