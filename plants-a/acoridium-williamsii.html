Acoridium williamsii

Acoridium williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acoraceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used as a starchy food source. The plant typically grows in wet, lowland areas and thrives in humid climates. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental qualities in gardens.
