Astragalus yilmazii

Astragalus yilmazii, commonly known as Yilmaz's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus yilmazii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.

Its conservation status remains a topic of interest due to habitat fragmentation and limited distribution.
