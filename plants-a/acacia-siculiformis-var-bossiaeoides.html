Acacia siculiformis var. bossiaeoides

Acacia siculiformis var. bossiaeoides, commonly known as the Bossie's wattle, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often twisted and covered in a distinctive greyish bark. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, feathery leaflets.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought and heat.
