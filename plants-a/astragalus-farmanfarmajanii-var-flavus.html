Astragalus farmanfarmajanii var. flavus

Astragalus farmanfarmajanii var. flavus, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in poor soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its small, pod-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.
