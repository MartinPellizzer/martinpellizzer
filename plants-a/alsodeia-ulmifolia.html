Alsodeia ulmifolia

Alsodeia ulmifolia, commonly known as the elm-leaved alsike, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves that resemble those of the elm tree. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Alsodeia ulmifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
