Allium nerinifolium

Allium nerinifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf garlic.

It is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces bulbs that are often used in culinary applications, similar to other allium species. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium nerinifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes grown in gardens for its delicate appearance.
