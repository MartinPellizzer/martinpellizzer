Amorphophallus xiengraiensis

Amorphophallus xiengraiensis, commonly known as the Xiengrai giant taro, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, ornamental leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant is typically found in tropical rainforests, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its rhizomatous structure allows it to store nutrients and water, aiding in its survival in humid climates.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
