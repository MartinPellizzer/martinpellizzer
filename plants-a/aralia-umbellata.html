Aralia umbellata

Aralia umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella aralia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring compound leaves with a pinnate structure. The plant produces small, white, umbellate clusters of flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. Its foliage provides habitat and food for various wildlife species, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The plant thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.
