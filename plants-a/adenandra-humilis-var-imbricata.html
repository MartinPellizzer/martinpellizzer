Adenandra humilis var. imbricata

Adenandra humilis var. imbricata, commonly known as the silverleaf adenandra, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and garden environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
