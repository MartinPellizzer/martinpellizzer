Acacia adunca

Acacia adunca, commonly known as the umbrella thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped crown formed by its many slender, drooping branches. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by flat, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. Its leaves are small, feathery, and often shed during dry seasons, allowing the plant to conserve water.

Acacia adunca is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal rainfall and high temperatures.
