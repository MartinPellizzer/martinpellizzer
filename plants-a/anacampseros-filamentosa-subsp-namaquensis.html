Anacampseros filamentosa subsp. namaquensis

Anacampseros filamentosa subsp.

namaquensis, commonly known as the Namibian ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, adapted to arid environments.

The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
