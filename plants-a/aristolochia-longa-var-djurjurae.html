Aristolochia longa var. djurjurae

Aristolochia longa var. djurjurae, commonly known as the Djurjura Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish-brown with a strong, complex scent. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and has a long, winding growth habit. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its toxic compounds, which have been used in traditional medicine but require caution due to potential harm.
