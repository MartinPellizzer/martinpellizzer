Amanoa macrocarpa

Amanoa macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant lily or Hawaiian lily, is a striking plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Aroid family (Araceae) and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant produces large, fleshy berries that are an important food source for native birds and wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in coastal forests and wetlands.

Due to its ecological significance and aesthetic appeal, it is a valued species in both conservation and horticultural contexts.
