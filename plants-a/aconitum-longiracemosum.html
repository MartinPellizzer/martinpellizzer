Aconitum longiracemosum

Aconitum longiracemosum, commonly known as the long-branched monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a serrated edge, and its stems are usually hairy and somewhat woody at the base. Aconitum longiracemosum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is also known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
