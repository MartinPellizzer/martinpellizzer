Actinella richardsonii var. canescens

Actinella richardsonii var. canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf actinella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a waxy texture and a bluish-green hue.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in terminal panicles, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
