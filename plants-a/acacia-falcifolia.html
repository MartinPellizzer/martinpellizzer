Acacia falcifolia

Acacia falcifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom in spring. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Acacia falcifolia is well-suited to dry environments and is often found in sandy soils.
