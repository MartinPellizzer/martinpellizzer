Arracacia papillosa

Arracacia papillosa, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark red berries and pinnately compound leaves. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
