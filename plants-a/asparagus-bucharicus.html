Asparagus bucharicus

Asparagus bucharicus, commonly known as the Bukharan asparagus, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, fern-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Asparagus bucharicus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
