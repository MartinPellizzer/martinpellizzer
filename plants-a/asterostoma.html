Asterostoma

Asterostoma, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers, which are often surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves. These plants are typically found in coastal areas and can thrive in saline environments. Asterostoma species are known for their ability to tolerate harsh maritime conditions.

Their flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to their ecological significance.
