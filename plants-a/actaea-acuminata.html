Actaea acuminata

Actaea acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped bugbane, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that bear clusters of white, spadix-like flowers. The plant produces palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Actaea acuminata is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
