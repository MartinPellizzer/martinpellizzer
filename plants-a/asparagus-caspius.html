Asparagus caspius

Asparagus caspius, commonly known as the Caspian asparagus, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by spiny, triangular berries. Its leaves are simple, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Asparagus caspius is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
