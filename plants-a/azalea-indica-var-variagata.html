Azalea indica var. variagata

Azalea indica var. variagata, commonly known as the variegated Indian azalea, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white and yellow variegation. The plant produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white during the spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and seasonal blooms.
