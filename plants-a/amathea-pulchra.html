Amathea pulchra

Amathea pulchra, commonly known as the beautiful amarillo, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract a variety of pollinators. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a glossy texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Amathea pulchra thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to flourish.
