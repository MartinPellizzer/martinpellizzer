Amaranthus hybridus f. genuinus

Amaranthus hybridus f. genuinus, commonly known as the redroot amaranth, is a variant of the amaranth species characterized by its distinct red stems and leaves.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows in warm climates and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into triangular, dry seeds. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds.
