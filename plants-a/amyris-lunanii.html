Amyris lunanii

Amyris lunanii, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody, aromatic fruits. Its essential oils are valued for their fragrance and have been used in traditional medicine and perfumery.

Amyris lunanii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
