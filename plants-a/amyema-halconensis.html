Amyema halconensis

Amyema halconensis, commonly known as the Halconensis Acacia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to orange floral clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features such as deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area.

It plays a vital role in its ecosystem by fixing nitrogen in the soil, supporting soil fertility and biodiversity.
