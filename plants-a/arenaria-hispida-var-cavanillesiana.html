Arenaria hispida var. cavanillesiana

Arenaria hispida var. cavanillesiana, commonly known as the hairy sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and rocky habitats.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has dense, mat-forming foliage with glandular hairs that give it a slightly sticky texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and dry slopes.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
