Achyronia thymifolia

Achyronia thymifolia, commonly known as the thyme-leaved eryngo, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features aromatic, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.

Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
