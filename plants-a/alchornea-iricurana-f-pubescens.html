Alchornea iricurana f. pubescens

Alchornea iricurana f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy alchornea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pubescent (hairy) foliage, which gives it its specific epithet "pubescens.". The leaves are typically ovate or elliptic in shape and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
