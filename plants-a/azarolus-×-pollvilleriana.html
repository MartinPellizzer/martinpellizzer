Azarolus × pollvilleriana

Azarolus × pollvilleriana, commonly known as the hybrid azarolus, is a rare and unique plant resulting from the cross between two species of the Azarolus genus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant exhibits a range of foliage colors, from deep green to variegated patterns, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated by plant enthusiasts and botanists.
