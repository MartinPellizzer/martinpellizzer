Abies × mastersiana

Abies × mastersiana, commonly known as the Colorado blue spruce, is a hybrid conifer resulting from the cross between *Abies lasiocarpa* and *Abies concolor*.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray needles and a conical shape that becomes more slender with age. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of western North America, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. Its needles are short, stiff, and have a distinctive bluish hue due to a waxy coating.

This species is widely cultivated as an ornamental tree for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
