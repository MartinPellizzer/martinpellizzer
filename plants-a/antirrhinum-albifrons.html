Antirrhinum albifrons

Antirrhinum albifrons, commonly known as the white-faced snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Antirrhinum albifrons is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
