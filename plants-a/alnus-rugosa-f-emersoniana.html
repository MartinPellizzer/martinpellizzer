Alnus rugosa f. emersoniana

Alnus rugosa f. emersoniana, commonly known as the Emerson's hazelnut, is a shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and oval to elliptic leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces catkins and small, nut-like fruits that are important for wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.
