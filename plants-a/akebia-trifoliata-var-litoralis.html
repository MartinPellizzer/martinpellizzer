Akebia trifoliata var. litoralis

Akebia trifoliata var. litoralis, commonly known as the coastal Japanese ivy, is a variant of the native Akebia trifoliata.

It is a deciduous climbing vine characterized by its trifoliate leaves and fragrant, tubular flowers. This variety is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It produces edible, sweet-tart berries that are consumed by various wildlife.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in coastal ecosystems.
