Abies concolor var. martinezii

Abies concolor var. martinezii, commonly known as the Martinez's white fir, is a rare and distinctive variant of the white fir species.

It is native to the high elevations of the western United States, particularly in California and Nevada. This evergreen conifer has needle-like leaves that are typically light green with a subtle blue tint. Its cones are cylindrical and have a distinctive shape, often maturing to a reddish-brown color.

The plant is characterized by its slow growth rate and is adapted to cold, moist environments with well-drained soils.
