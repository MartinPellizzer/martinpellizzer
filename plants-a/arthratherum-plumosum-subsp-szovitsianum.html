Arthratherum plumosum subsp. szovitsianum

Arthratherum plumosum subsp.

szovitsianum, commonly known as the Hungarian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces delicate, feathery inflorescences that are often pink to reddish in color.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has narrow, linear leaves that are slightly hairy and can reach heights of up to 60 cm.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stabilization.
