Artemisia tilesii var. unalaschcensis

Artemisia tilesii var. unalaschcensis, commonly known as the Alaska wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray foliage and a strong aromatic scent. This plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments. It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its hardy nature.

The plant has been used traditionally by indigenous peoples for medicinal and ceremonial purposes.
