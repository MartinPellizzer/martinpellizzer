Alternanthera filifolia var. typica

Alternanthera filifolia var. typica, commonly known as the threadleaf Alternanthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bright red or pink, though they can vary in color depending on light conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow, which are followed by three-lobed seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
