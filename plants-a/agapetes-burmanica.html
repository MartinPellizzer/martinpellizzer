Agapetes burmanica

Agapetes burmanica, commonly known as the Burmese snowbell, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Agapetes burmanica is often found in cool, moist, shaded environments at high elevations.
