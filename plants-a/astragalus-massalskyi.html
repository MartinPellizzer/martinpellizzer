Astragalus massalskyi

Astragalus massalskyi, commonly known as the Massalskyi milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers clustered in globular heads. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

Astragalus massalskyi is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
