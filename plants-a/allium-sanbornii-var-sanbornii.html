Allium sanbornii var. sanbornii

Allium sanbornii var. sanbornii, commonly known as Sanborn's garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-gray hue. Its bulbous roots store nutrients and contribute to its hardiness in arid environments.

Sanborn's garlic is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
