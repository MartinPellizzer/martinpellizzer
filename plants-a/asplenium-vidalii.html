Asplenium vidalii

Asplenium vidalii, commonly known as the Vidal's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered in rosettes. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a serrated or toothed margin.

Asplenium vidalii is a rare and specialized species, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
