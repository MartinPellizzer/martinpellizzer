Acinodendron pedicellatum

Acinodendron pedicellatum, commonly known as the pink-stemmed acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems that often display a distinctive pink or red coloration. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters known as acini. Its leaves are bipinnately compound, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval leaflets.

Acinodendron pedicellatum is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments, where it adapts to harsh conditions with its drought-resistant features.
