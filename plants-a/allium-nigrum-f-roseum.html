Allium nigrum f. roseum

Allium nigrum f. roseum, commonly known as the pink garlic or roseum garlic, is a botanical variant of the common garlic plant.

It is characterized by its striking pinkish-purple flowers, which contrast with its typical white or pale green blooms. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to southern Europe. It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves.

This cultivar is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique floral coloration.
