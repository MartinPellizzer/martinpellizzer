Aster tenellus var. longifolius

Aster tenellus var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are longer than they are wide. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in loose, open heads.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
