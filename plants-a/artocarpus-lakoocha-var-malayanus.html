Artocarpus lakoocha var. malayanus

Artocarpus lakoocha var. malayanus, commonly known as the Malayan breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spiky fruit. The fruit, which is typically green when mature, turns yellow or orange when ripe and is edible. The plant is valued for its fruit, which is rich in nutrients and used in local cuisines.

It also plays an important role in traditional medicine and agroforestry systems.
