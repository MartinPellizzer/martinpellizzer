Alpinia murdochii

Alpinia murdochii, commonly known as the Murdoch's ginger, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fragrant, pale yellow to orange flowers in clusters. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
