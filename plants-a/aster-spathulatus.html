Aster spathulatus

Aster spathulatus, commonly known as the broadleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers in the fall. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and tubular disc florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Aster spathulatus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
