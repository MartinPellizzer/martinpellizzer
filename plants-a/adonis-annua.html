Adonis annua

Adonis annua, commonly known as the annual glory of the snow, is an herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically for its potential health benefits.
