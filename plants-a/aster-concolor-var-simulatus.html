Aster concolor var. simulatus

Aster concolor var. simulatus, commonly known as the eastern blue aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It blooms in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
