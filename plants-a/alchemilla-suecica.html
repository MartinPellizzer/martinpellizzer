Alchemilla suecica

Alchemilla suecica, commonly known as Swedish lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wet meadows. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of silky hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Alchemilla suecica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
