Acalypha polystachya var. lacera

Acalypha polystachya var. lacera, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that resemble those of hibiscus. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a distinctive silvery underside that gives it a shimmering appearance.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
