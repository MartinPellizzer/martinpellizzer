Acacia stereophylla

Acacia stereophylla, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Acacia stereophylla is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and inland regions.
