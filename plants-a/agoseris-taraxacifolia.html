Agoseris taraxacifolia

Agoseris taraxacifolia, commonly known as the dandelionleaf goldenweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, followed by fluffy white seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its foliage and flowers are used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
