Aconitum rotundifolium

Aconitum rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaved aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are rounded and palmately lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its toxic compounds, including potent alkaloids. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and is not recommended for cultivation in gardens.

Aconitum rotundifolium has round or heart-shaped leaves that are usually large, broad, and deeply lobed, arranged alternately along the stem.

Its flowers are showy, blue to purple in color, with spurred petals and a complex structure, blooming in summer and pollinated by insects such as bees. The roots are thick, fleshy, and tuberous, adapted for storing nutrients and surviving harsh conditions.

Aconitum rotundifolium is primarily native to Afghanistan, Kazakhstan, Kirgizstan, Nepal, Pakistan, Tadzhikistan, West Himalaya, and Xinjiang.

Its native range spans across parts of Central and South Asia.
