Arctotis bellidifolia

Arctotis bellidifolia, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiky flower heads that range in color from orange to red. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a compact growth habit that adds structure to garden landscapes.
