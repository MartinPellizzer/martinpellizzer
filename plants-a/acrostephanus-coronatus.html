Acrostephanus coronatus

Acrostephanus coronatus, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial legume that produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is characterized by its dense, creeping growth habit and is often used for erosion control due to its extensive root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
