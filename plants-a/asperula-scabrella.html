Asperula scabrella

Asperula scabrella, commonly known as the rough asperula, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub with small, leathery leaves that have a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Asperula scabrella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in mild climates.
