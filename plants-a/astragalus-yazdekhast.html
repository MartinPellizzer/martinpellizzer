Astragalus yazdekhast

Astragalus yazdekhast, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to dry climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
