Astragalus asciocalyx

Astragalus asciocalyx, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and steppe habitats. It produces small, legume-like pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus asciocalyx is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
