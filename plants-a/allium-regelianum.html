Allium regelianum

Allium regelianum, commonly known as the Regal leek, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has broad, strap-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Allium regelianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, garlic-like fragrance.
