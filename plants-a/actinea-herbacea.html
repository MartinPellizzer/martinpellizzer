Actinea herbacea

Actinea herbacea, commonly known as the common actinia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves that are often divided into narrow segments. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in a sparsely branched inflorescence.

Actinea herbacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local insect populations.
