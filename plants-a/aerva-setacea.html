Aerva setacea

Aerva setacea, commonly known as the hairy aerva, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and branched stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Aerva setacea is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
