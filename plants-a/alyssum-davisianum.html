Alyssum davisianum

Alyssum davisianum, commonly known as Davis' alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Alyssum davisianum is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
