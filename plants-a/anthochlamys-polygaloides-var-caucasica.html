Anthochlamys polygaloides var. caucasica

Anthochlamys polygaloides var. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian flame-vine, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red throat. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, featuring slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens with adequate support structures.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early autumn.
