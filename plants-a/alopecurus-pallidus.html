Alopecurus pallidus

Alopecurus pallidus, commonly known as pale cocklegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist soils along streambanks, wet meadows, and marshy areas. The plant has slender, hairless stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often rolled in the bud. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Alopecurus pallidus is characterized by its pale green color and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
