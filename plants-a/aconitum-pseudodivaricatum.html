Aconitum pseudodivaricatum

Aconitum pseudodivaricatum, commonly known as the purple monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring striking purple or violet hooded flowers that resemble a monk's habit. The plant has large, deeply lobed leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its roots contain toxic alkaloids, making it a plant of both botanical interest and caution.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
