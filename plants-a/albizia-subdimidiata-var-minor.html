Albizia subdimidiata var. minor

Albizia subdimidiata var. minor, commonly known as the lesser mimosa, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern. The plant produces yellow to pale pink flowers in racemes, which bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
