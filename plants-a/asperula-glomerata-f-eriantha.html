Asperula glomerata f. eriantha

Asperula glomerata f. eriantha, commonly known as the woolly woodruff, is a compact shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It features small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate ornamental quality to the plant. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a soft, hairy texture that gives the plant its common name. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe, it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in garden settings.
