Allium vineale var. laxiflorum

Allium vineale var. laxiflorum, commonly known as slender-leaved garlic, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

The bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a useful plant in both culinary and horticultural contexts.
