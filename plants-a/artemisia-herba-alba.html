Artemisia herba-alba

Artemisia herba-alba, commonly known as white wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant contains volatile oils, including thujone and camphor, which contribute to its strong medicinal and aromatic properties. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been used in traditional medicine and as a component in perfumes and insect repellents.
