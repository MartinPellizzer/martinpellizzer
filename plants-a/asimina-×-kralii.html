Asimina × kralii

Asimina × kralii, commonly known as the Kralii pawpaw, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Asimina triloba and Asimina arguta.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, orange-colored fruits. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a glossy green surface.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential as a food source for wildlife.
