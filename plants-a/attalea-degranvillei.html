Attalea degranvillei

Attalea degranvillei, commonly known as the Paraguay palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly found in Paraguay and Brazil.

It is a tall, slender palm with a smooth, grayish trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to three meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clusters of dark blue to black berries. It thrives in wetland and marshy environments, making it an important component of these ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated for landscaping and conservation purposes.
