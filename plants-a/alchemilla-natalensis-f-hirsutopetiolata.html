Alchemilla natalensis f. hirsutopetiolata

Alchemilla natalensis f. hirsutopetiolata, commonly known as the hirsute petioled lady's mantle, is a variant of the alchemilla species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, elongated petioles and basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental foliage and its ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
