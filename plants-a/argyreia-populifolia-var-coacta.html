Argyreia populifolia var. coacta

Argyreia populifolia var. coacta, commonly known as the velvet leaf or coacta morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to violet, which bloom in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
