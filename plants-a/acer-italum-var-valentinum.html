Acer italum var. valentinum

Acer italum var. valentinum, commonly known as the Valentine maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange foliage in autumn, which makes it a popular ornamental plant. The tree has a rounded crown and produces small, inconspicuous green flowers in early spring. Its leaves are palmately lobed, typically with three to five distinct lobes.

The wood is light, soft, and used in various crafts and furniture making.
