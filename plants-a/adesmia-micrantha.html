Adesmia micrantha

Adesmia micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered legume, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Adesmia micrantha is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
