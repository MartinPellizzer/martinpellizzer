Allium geyeri var. graniferum

Allium geyeri var. graniferum, commonly known as the Sierra onion, is a bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence, which is a key botanical feature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky environments.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild, onion-like flavor.
