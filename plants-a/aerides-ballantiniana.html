Aerides ballantiniana

Aerides ballantiniana, commonly known as the Ballantini's orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of white to pale yellow blossoms with a distinctive, intricate pattern on the petals. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Aerides ballantiniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.
