Astragalus humistratus

Astragalus humistratus, commonly known as the lowland milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus humistratus is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
