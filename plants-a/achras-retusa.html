Achras retusa

Achras retusa, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, star-shaped fruits that are sweet and juicy. Its botanical features include a shallow root system and the ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food.
