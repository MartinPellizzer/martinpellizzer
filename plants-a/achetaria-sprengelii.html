Achetaria sprengelii

Achetaria sprengelii, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to violet. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Achetaria sprengelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
