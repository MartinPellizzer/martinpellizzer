Angraecopsis magnicalcar

Angraecopsis magnicalcar, commonly known as the giant African violet, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces long, slender stamens and a prominent, spurred pistil, typical of the genus Angraecopsis. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
