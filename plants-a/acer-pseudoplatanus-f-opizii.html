Acer pseudoplatanus f. opizii

Acer pseudoplatanus f. opizii, commonly known as the Opizii maple, is a rare and distinctive variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring leaves with a striking combination of green, red, and purple hues. This form is typically found in specific microhabitats, often in shaded, moist environments within mountainous regions. The leaves are usually smaller and more elongated compared to the typical species, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
