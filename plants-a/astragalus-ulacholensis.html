Astragalus ulacholensis

Astragalus ulacholensis, commonly known as the Ulacholensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. Astragalus ulacholensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Its seeds are encased in hard pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its survival in harsh climates.
