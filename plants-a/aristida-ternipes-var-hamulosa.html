Aristida ternipes var. hamulosa

Aristida ternipes var. hamulosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf threeawn, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, wiry stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-awned flowers, which are characteristic of the genus Aristida.

This species is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
