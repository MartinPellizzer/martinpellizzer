Amaryllis × gladwynensis

Amaryllis × gladwynensis, commonly known as the Gladwyn hybrid amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between different amaryllis species.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically produces strap-like leaves that are broad and strap-shaped, emerging from a bulbous stem. It is native to South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

The flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
