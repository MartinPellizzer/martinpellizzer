Acacia ommatosperma

Acacia ommatosperma, commonly known as the eye-seed acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which are followed by flat, leathery pods containing the distinctive seed structures. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
