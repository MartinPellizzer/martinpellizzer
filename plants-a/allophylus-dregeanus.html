Allophylus dregeanus

Allophylus dregeanus, commonly known as the African holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

Allophylus dregeanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
