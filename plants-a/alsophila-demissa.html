Alsophila demissa

Alsophila demissa, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia and parts of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds that give it a graceful appearance. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a dark green color and a leathery texture.

Alsophila demissa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
