Ateleia Pterocarpa

Ateleia pterocarpa is a lesser-known but ecologically significant plant species native to parts of South America. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities and diverse ecological roles. This tree is characterized by its unique foliage and distinctive fruit structure, which aid in its survival in tropical environments. Despite its importance, it remains under-researched and often overlooked in conservation efforts. Understanding Ateleia pterocarpa is crucial for preserving biodiversity in its native habitats.
