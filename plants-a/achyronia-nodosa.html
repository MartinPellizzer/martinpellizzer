Achyronia nodosa

Achyronia nodosa, commonly known as the nodding achyronia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.
