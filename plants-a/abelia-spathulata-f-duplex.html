Abelia spathulata f. duplex

Abelia spathulata f. duplex, commonly known as the double-flowered abelia, is a compact shrub characterized by its unique foliage and floral features.

It features ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, double flowers with five petals, giving it a more elaborate appearance than the typical abelia. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ability to add texture and color to garden landscapes.
