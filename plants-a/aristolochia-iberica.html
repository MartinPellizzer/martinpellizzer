Aristolochia iberica

Aristolochia iberica, commonly known as the Iberian Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and produces striking, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a faintly veined pattern. The plant is characterized by its unique floral structure, which resembles a pipe, and it blooms in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
