Achillea fililoba

Achillea fililoba, commonly known as slender yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Its common name is derived from its delicate appearance and historical use in traditional medicine.
