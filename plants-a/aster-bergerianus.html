Aster bergerianus

Aster bergerianus, commonly known as the alpine aster, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Aster bergerianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in alpine ecosystems.
