Aspilia bipartita

Aspilia bipartita, commonly known as the African flame flower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy yellow-orange flower heads that resemble flames. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of both ray and disk flowers, typical of the daisy family.

Aspilia bipartita is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
