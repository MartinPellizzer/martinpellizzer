Acacia lophantha var. speciosa

Acacia lophantha var. speciosa, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers, which bloom in globular clusters during the spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
