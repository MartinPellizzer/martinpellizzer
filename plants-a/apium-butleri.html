Apium butleri

Apium butleri, commonly known as the desert parsley, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and celery, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and small white flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are fern-like and often used in traditional medicine by indigenous peoples.

Apium butleri is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for desert wildlife.
