Anemone baicalensis var. saniculiformis

Anemone baicalensis var. saniculiformis, commonly known as the Siberian anemone, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers with five petals, often arranged in a cluster at the top of a slender stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

This variant is closely related to other members of the Ranunculaceae family, sharing similar botanical characteristics.
