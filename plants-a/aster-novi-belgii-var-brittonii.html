Aster novi-belgii var. brittonii

Aster novi-belgii var. brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's New York aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in fine, grayish hairs. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with purple to lavender petals and yellow centers, blooming in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and covered in a fuzzy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
