Astragalus dictyocarpus

Astragalus dictyocarpus, commonly known as the net-seeded milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, net-veined pods that contain small, smooth seeds. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and often grows in sandy or rocky soils.

Astragalus dictyocarpus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its native habitat.
