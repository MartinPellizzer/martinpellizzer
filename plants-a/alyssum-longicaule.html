Alyssum longicaule

Alyssum longicaule, commonly known as the sticky fleabane, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are typically borne in dense, terminal clusters. Alyssum longicaule thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is also noted for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as bees and butterflies.
