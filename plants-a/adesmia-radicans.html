Adesmia radicans

Adesmia radicans, commonly known as the creeping adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of less than 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Adesmia radicans is adapted to thrive in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
