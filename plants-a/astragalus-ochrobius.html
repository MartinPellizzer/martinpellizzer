Astragalus ochrobius

Astragalus ochrobius, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe habitats. Astragalus ochrobius is a perennial herb that produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
