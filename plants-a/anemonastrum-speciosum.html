Anemonastrum speciosum

Anemonastrum speciosum, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with a fibrous root system adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically small, linear, and covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Anemonastrum speciosum is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
