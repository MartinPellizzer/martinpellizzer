Astragalus semiromensis

Astragalus semiromensis, commonly known as the semiromensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in harsh conditions.

Astragalus semiromensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
