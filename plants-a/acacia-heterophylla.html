Acacia heterophylla

Acacia heterophylla, commonly known as the variable acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which varies in shape and size depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typically followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Its leaves are often bipinnate, with small, feathery leaflets.

Acacia heterophylla is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
