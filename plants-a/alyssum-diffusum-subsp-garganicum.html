Alyssum diffusum subsp. garganicum

Alyssum diffusum subsp.

garganicum, commonly known as the Mediterranean alyssum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to other alyssum species.
