Allium sphaerocephalon subsp. descendens

Allium sphaerocephalon subsp.

descendens, commonly known as the Persian onion, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features striking spherical or globe-shaped purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, edible bulbs that are often used in cooking. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its mild, sweet flavor, which is appreciated in various culinary traditions.
