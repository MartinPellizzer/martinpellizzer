Acacia estrophiolata

Acacia estrophiolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and compound leaves composed of several small, narrow leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical spikes, which appear in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Acacia estrophiolata is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
