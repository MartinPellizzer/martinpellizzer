Apocynum cannabinum var. estellinum

Apocynum cannabinum var. estellinum, commonly known as Estellinum dogbane, is a variant of the common dogbane plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pale green to yellow, clustered in umbels, and bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release small, cottony seeds.

This variant is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its latex and leaves.
