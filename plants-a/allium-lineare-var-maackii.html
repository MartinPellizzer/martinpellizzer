Allium lineare var. maackii

Allium lineare var. maackii, commonly known as the Maack's leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its elongated, strap-like foliage that can reach up to 30 cm in length. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild, garlic-like flavor.
