Astragalus shabilensis

Astragalus shabilensis, commonly known as the Shabil milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Ethiopian Highlands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions. It plays a role in local ecosystems by contributing to soil stabilization and providing forage for livestock.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
