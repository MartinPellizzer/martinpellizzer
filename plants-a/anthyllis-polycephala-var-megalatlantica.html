Anthyllis polycephala var. megalatlantica

Anthyllis polycephala var. megalatlantica, commonly known as the large-headed anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
