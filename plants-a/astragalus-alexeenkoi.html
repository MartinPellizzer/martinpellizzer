Astragalus alexeenkoi

Astragalus alexeenkoi, commonly known as the Kyzylkum milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and for forage.
