Aechmea dactylina

Aechmea dactylina, commonly known as the pink pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater to support its growth. The plant produces vibrant, spathaceous inflorescences with colorful bracts that range from pink to red. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and protection from intense sunlight.

Aechmea dactylina is popular as a ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.

Morphology

Aechmea dactylina has rosette-shaped leaves that are wide, strap-like, and arranged in a tight spiral around the plant's central axis.

The leaves are typically a deep green color, with a waxy surface and pointed tips, and can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant has a short, upright stem with a smooth, grayish-brown bark that remains hidden beneath the dense foliage. Its inflorescence consists of a tall, spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, with small white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, pollinated by wind and insects.
