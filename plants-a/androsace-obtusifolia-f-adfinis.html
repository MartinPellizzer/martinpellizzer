Androsace obtusifolia f. adfinis

Androsace obtusifolia f. adfinis, commonly known as the broad-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It features small, often white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, typically on slender stems. The leaves are generally broad and ovate, with a soft, velvety texture, and are arranged in a rosette pattern. This plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in rock gardens and alpine collections.
