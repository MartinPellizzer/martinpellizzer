Athamanta cervaria

Athamanta cervaria, commonly known as the woolly mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, silver-gray foliage with soft, woolly hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, typically from March to May. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Athamanta cervaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
