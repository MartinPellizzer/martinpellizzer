Acer pseudoplatanus f. lutescens

Acer pseudoplatanus f. lutescens, commonly known as the golden maple, is a variant of the common maple tree.

It is characterized by its striking yellow foliage, which turns vibrant shades of gold in the autumn. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. This cultivar is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
