Astragalus ptilodes var. amphilogus

Astragalus ptilodes var. amphilogus, commonly known as the broadleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and sagebrush steppe habitats. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to reddish-brown flowers in early summer. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
