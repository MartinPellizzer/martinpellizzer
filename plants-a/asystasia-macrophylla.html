Asystasia macrophylla

Asystasia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved asystasia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 20 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Asystasia macrophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
