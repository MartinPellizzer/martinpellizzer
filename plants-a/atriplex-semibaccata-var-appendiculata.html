Atriplex semibaccata var. appendiculata

Atriplex semibaccata var. appendiculata, commonly known as the desert saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its gray-green, succulent leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it a valuable plant for stabilizing soils in disturbed or saline habitats.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
