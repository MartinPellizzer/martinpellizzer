Arctagrostis festucacea

Arctagrostis festucacea, commonly known as slender bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant grows in dense, low tufts and is often found in open, sunny areas such as meadows and woodland edges. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Arctagrostis festucacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining soil stability in natural habitats.
