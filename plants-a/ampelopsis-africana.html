Ampelopsis africana

Ampelopsis africana, commonly known as African honeysuckle, is a woody vine native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, often with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, visually appealing vine.
