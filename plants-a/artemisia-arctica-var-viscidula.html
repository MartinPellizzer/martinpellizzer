Artemisia arctica var. viscidula

Artemisia arctica var. viscidula, commonly known as sticky arctic wormwood, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in harsh, cold environments and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.

Its aromatic properties make it a notable species in alpine and arctic botany.
