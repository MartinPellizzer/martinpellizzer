Alyssum peltarioides var. virgatiforme

Alyssum peltarioides var. virgatiforme, commonly known as the slender alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to both garden and landscape designs.
