Artemisia canadensis f. typica

Artemisia canadensis f. typica, commonly known as white wood sage, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and a distinctive, aromatic scent. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, yellowish flowers in composite flower heads, which are arranged in clusters.

The species is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
