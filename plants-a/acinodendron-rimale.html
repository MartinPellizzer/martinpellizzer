Acinodendron rimale

Acinodendron rimale, commonly known as the African wild cucumber, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, pumpkins, and gourds. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, green, cucumber-like fruits that turn yellow when mature. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The vine is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
