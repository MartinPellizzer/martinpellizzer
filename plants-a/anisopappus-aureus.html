Anisopappus aureus

Anisopappus aureus, commonly known as golden globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features hairy, branched stems and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive, globular shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
