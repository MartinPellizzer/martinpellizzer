Amyema hastifolia

Amyema hastifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved amyema, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Amyema hastifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
