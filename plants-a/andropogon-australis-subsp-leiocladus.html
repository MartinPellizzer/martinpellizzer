Andropogon australis subsp. leiocladus

Andropogon australis subsp.

leiocladus, commonly known as the coastal bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often hairy along the edges.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and maritime hammocks.

This subspecies is adapted to salt spray and is an important component of coastal vegetation.
