Andropogon virginicus var. genuinus

Andropogon virginicus var. genuinus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or sunny habitats.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in native plant communities.
