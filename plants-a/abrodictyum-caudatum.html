Abrodictyum caudatum

Abrodictyum caudatum, commonly known as the long-tailed orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescence that can reach several feet in length, bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in warm, moist conditions with high humidity.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
