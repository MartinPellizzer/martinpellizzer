Abelmoschus pungens var. mizoramensis

Abelmoschus pungens var. mizoramensis, commonly known as the Mizoram okra, is a variant of the okra species native to the northeastern region of India.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the flowers are yellow with five petals. The fruit is a capsule that turns from green to red as it matures, containing numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content, often used in local cuisines.
