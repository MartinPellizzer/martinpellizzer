Alyssum davisii

Alyssum davisii, commonly known as Davis' alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. This annual herb typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid environments. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually pale yellow or white, and it produces small, round seed pods.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in drought conditions.
