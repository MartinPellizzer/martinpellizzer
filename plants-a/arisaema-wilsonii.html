Arisaema wilsonii

Arisaema wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's arisaema, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces a single, showy white spathe that encloses a narrow, spadix bearing both male and female flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or shaded forest habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been the subject of botanical study due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
