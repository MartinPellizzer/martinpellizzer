Artemisia vulgaris var. gnaphalodes

Artemisia vulgaris var. gnaphalodes, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features silver-gray foliage with finely divided, aromatic leaves that emit a strong, bitter scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in dry, rocky habitats.

Its chemical composition includes volatile oils and compounds with historical use in medicine and as a pesticide.
