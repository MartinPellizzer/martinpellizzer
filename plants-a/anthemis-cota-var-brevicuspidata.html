Anthemis cota var. brevicuspidata

Anthemis cota var. brevicuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-tipped tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features aromatic, yellowish-green leaves with a distinctive, sharp scent, and its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
