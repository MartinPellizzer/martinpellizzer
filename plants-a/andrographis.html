Andrographis

Andrographis, commonly known as the "Indian echinacea," is a herbaceous plant native to southern Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant is renowned for its bitter, yellow-green leaves, which contain the active compound andrographolide. Andrographis is often cultivated for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Ayurvedic and Chinese medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is typically grown in well-drained soil.
