Aloe altimatsiatrae

Aloe altimatsiatrae, commonly known as the highland aloe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are fleshy and covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which bloom in the dry season. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Aloe altimatsiatrae is valued for its resilience and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
