Asperula daphneola

Asperula daphneola, commonly known as the silver leaf shrub, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, silky hair, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value comes from its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
