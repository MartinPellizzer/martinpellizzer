Arundinella hirta var. hirtiglumis

Arundinella hirta var. hirtiglumis, commonly known as the hairy bamboo or hairy reed, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, hollow stems that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
