Acacia forsteri

Acacia forsteri, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it produces small, woody pods that contain seeds.

Acacia forsteri is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
