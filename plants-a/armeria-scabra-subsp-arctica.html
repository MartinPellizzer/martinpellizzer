Armeria scabra subsp. arctica

Armeria scabra subsp.

arctica, commonly known as Arctic sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arctic and alpine regions. It typically features slender, branched stems covered in dense, white or pale green leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

This subspecies is often found in tundra and mountainous areas across northern Europe and North America.
