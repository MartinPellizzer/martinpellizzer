Agave elongata

Agave elongata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and long, narrow, gray-green leaves that are often adorned with spines. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears clusters of white to yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. Agave elongata is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and minimal water.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and striking appearance.
