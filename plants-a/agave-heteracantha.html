Agave heteracantha

Agave heteracantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its sharp, spiny leaves that are typically narrow and blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk with yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
