Archidendron palauense

Archidendron palauense, commonly known as the Palau legume, is a flowering plant native to the island nation of Palau in the western Pacific.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes characterized by their pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with compound leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution in coastal and lowland areas.

The species plays a role in local ecosystems and has potential uses in traditional practices.
