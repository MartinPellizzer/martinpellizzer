Anchusa undulata var. alborosea

Anchusa undulata var. alborosea, commonly known as the white-hair bluebell, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
