Agave salmiana var. ferox

Agave salmiana var. ferox, commonly known as the Mexican hat plant or Agave ferox, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, spiny leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears yellowish-green flowers, which are often used in traditional ceremonies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for arid climates.

Its unique appearance and cultural significance make it a notable species in the Agavaceae family.
