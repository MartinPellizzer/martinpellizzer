Acronia rinkei

Acronia rinkei, commonly known as the Kauai ironwood, is a rare and endemic tree species native to the island of Kauai in Hawaii.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and rugged, twisted trunk, which provides habitat for various native insects and birds. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in mesic forests at higher elevations. Acronia rinkei is a slow-growing species with a long lifespan, making it an important component of Hawaii's unique ecosystem.

Due to habitat loss and invasive species, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
