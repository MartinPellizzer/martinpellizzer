Athyrium aristulatum

Athyrium aristulatum, commonly known as the Chinese water fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble a lacy pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
