Arundinaria basigibbosa

Arundinaria basigibbosa, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a species of grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Arundinaria basigibbosa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
