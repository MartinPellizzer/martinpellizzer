Acer cappadocicum var. serrulatum

Acer cappadocicum var. serrulatum, commonly known as the Cappadocian maple, is a deciduous tree native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves with serrated margins, which give it its varietal name. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
