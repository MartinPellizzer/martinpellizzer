Alsophila latevagans

Alsophila latevagans, commonly known as the broad-leaf tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports its growth, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.
