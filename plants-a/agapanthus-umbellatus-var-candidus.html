Agapanthus umbellatus var. candidus

Agapanthus umbellatus var. candidus, commonly known as the white agapanthus, is a striking evergreen perennial native to South Africa.

It features dense, spherical clusters of white, tubular flowers that bloom in summer, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern around the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
