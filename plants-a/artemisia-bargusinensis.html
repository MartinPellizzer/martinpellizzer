Artemisia bargusinensis

Artemisia bargusinensis, commonly known as Bargusin wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is adapted to harsh, arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It contains volatile oils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and digestive properties.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
