Arnica paniculata

Arnica paniculata, commonly known as the common arnica, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is erect and branched.

The flowers are arranged in a panicle, giving the plant its scientific name.
