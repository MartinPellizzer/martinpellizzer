Artocarpus hypargyreus

Artocarpus hypargyreus, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fruit. The fruit, which resembles a bread-like substance when cooked, is a key food source in many Pacific Island cultures. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and produces flowers in clusters.

Its ecological and cultural significance makes it an important species in both traditional and modern agricultural systems.
