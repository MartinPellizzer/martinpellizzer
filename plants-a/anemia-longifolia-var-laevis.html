Anemia longifolia var. laevis

Anemia longifolia var. laevis, commonly known as the smoothleaf anemia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth margin. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
