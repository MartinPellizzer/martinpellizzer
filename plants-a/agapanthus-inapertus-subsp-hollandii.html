Agapanthus inapertus subsp. hollandii

Agapanthus inapertus subsp.

hollandii, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, leafy stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
