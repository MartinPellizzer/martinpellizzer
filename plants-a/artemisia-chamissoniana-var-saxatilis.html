Artemisia chamissoniana var. saxatilis

Artemisia chamissoniana var. saxatilis, commonly known as the rock wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage composed of narrow, linear leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in loose, cylindrical arrays. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it a resilient plant in mountainous and coastal regions.
