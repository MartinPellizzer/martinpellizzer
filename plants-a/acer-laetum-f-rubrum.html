Acer laetum f. rubrum

Acer laetum f. rubrum, commonly known as the red-leaf maple, is a cultivar of the Norway maple.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which becomes more intense in cooler temperatures. The leaves are typically palmate with five lobes and a smooth, glossy surface. This variety is often used in ornamental horticulture for its vibrant autumn color.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
