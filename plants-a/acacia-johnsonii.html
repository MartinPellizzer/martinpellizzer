Acacia johnsonii

Acacia johnsonii, commonly known as the Johnson's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular heads, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use.

Acacia johnsonii plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various desert animals.
