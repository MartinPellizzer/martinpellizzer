Aspidosperma megalocarpon subsp. curranii

Aspidosperma megalocarpon subsp.

curranii, commonly known as the African bloodwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, dark bark and glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs.

The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by woody, orange-red fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

The plant has traditional uses in local medicine and is also valued for its hard, durable wood.
