Acacia imitans

Acacia imitans, commonly known as the mimosa tree, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments due to its deep root system. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Acacia imitans is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
