Arrabidaea poeppigii

Arrabidaea poeppigii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that appear in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
