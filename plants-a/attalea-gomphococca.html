Attalea gomphococca

Attalea gomphococca, commonly known as the yellow palm or Brazilian wax palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that contains a single seed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been studied for its potential ecological and economic uses.
