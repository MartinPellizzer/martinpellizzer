Acacia oswaldii

Acacia oswaldii, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a small evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Acacia oswaldii is well adapted to coastal environments and is often found in dunes and rocky habitats.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for native pollinators.
