Amalia superbiens

Amalia superbiens, commonly known as the noble amalia, is a striking flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage that helps it retain moisture. Its showy blooms, which range from white to deep purple, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Amalia superbiens is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with partial to full sun.
