Anisopetala inflata

Anisopetala inflata, commonly known as the inflated anisopetala, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive, inflated flower buds.

Native to specific regions in South America, it thrives in moist, tropical climates. The plant's foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. Its flowers, which resemble small, inflated balloons, are typically pale green to yellowish in color.

Due to its rare and specialized habitat, it is considered a botanically significant species for ecological studies.
