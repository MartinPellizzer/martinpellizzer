Acacia mutabilis subsp. angustifolia

Acacia mutabilis subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are narrow and often grayish-green in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
