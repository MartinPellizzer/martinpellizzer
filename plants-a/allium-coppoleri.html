Allium coppoleri

Allium coppoleri, commonly known as the Corsican leek, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are similar in appearance to onions but have a milder flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary potential, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
