Andryala cheiranthifolia

Andryala cheiranthifolia, commonly known as the Indian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually pink or purple.

Andryala cheiranthifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
