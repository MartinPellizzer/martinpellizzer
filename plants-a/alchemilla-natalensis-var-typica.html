Alchemilla natalensis var. typica

Alchemilla natalensis var. typica, commonly known as Natal lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
