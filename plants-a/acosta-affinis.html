Acosta affinis

Acosta affinis, commonly known as the Mexican goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Acosta affinis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
