Agapetes intermedia

Agapetes intermedia, commonly known as the silverleaf agapete, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it its common name. The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the plant produces small, leathery berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.
