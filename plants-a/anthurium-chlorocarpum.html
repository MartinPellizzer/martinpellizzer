Anthurium chlorocarpum

Anthurium chlorocarpum, commonly known as the green fruit anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, green, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium chlorocarpum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
