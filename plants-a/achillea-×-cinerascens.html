Achillea × cinerascens

Achillea × cinerascens, commonly known as the gray yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features gray-green foliage and produces fluffy, white to pale pink flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.

Achillea × cinerascens is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
