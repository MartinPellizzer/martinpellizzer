Aronia arbutifolia var. atropurpurea

Aronia arbutifolia var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the red chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark red to black berries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. The plant features oval, serrated leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
