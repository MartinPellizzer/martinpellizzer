Arabis pendula var. hypoglauca

Arabis pendula var. hypoglauca, commonly known as the blue pendulous rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, drooping stems that bear clusters of small, blue to bluish-white flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence, which gives the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
