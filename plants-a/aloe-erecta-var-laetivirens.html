Aloe erecta var. laetivirens

Aloe erecta var. laetivirens, commonly known as the Golden Aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, erect stems covered in thick, fleshy leaves that are typically a bright yellow-green color. The plant produces striking, upward-facing flower spikes with orange-red bracts that bloom in late summer. Its leaves store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
