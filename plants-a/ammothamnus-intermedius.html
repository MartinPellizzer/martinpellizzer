Ammothamnus intermedius

Ammothamnus intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate thimbleweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the Asteraceae family.
