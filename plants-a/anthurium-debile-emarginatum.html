Anthurium debile-emarginatum

Anthurium debile-emarginatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, heart-shaped leaves that are often marbled with green and white patterns. The plant produces striking spadices, which are modified leaves that enclose the small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant.
