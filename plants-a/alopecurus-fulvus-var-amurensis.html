Alopecurus fulvus var. amurensis

Alopecurus fulvus var. amurensis, commonly known as the Amur mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features a tufted growth habit with basal leaves that are hairy and narrow. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

This variety is distinguished by its compact stature and specific geographic distribution within its native range.
