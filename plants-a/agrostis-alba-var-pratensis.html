Agrostis alba var. pratensis

Agrostis alba var. pratensis, commonly known as meadow bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, silver-green leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, lawns, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers.

Agrostis alba var. pratensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass mixtures.
