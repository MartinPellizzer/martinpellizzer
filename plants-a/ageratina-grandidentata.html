Ageratina grandidentata

Ageratina grandidentata, commonly known as the grandleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins and produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Ageratina grandidentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
