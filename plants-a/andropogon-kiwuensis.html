Andropogon kiwuensis

Andropogon kiwuensis, commonly known as the African switchgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure, making it suitable for various ecological and agricultural applications. Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion, and it is often used in restoration projects.

Additionally, Andropogon kiwuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a biofuel crop.
