Atriplex cornigera

Atriplex cornigera, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves and small, green or yellowish flowers. The plant is highly adapted to saline and drought conditions, making it a common species in saline soils. Its leaves are often coated with a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Atriplex cornigera plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
