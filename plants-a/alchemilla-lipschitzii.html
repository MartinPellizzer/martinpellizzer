Alchemilla lipschitzii

Alchemilla lipschitzii, commonly known as the Balkan lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or along stream banks.

Alchemilla lipschitzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
