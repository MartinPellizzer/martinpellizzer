Acacia abyssinica

Acacia abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian acacia, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Horn of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched trunk and bipinnate leaves that are feathery and composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in semi-arid and arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh climatic conditions.

The tree is also valued for its timber and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
