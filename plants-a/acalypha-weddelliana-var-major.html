Acalypha weddelliana var. major

Acalypha weddelliana var. major, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-leaf kochia, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, velvety leaves that range from deep green to reddish hues, depending on light exposure and season. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its attractive foliage, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in warm climates.
