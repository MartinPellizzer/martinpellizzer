Alyssum montanum f. microphyllum

Alyssum montanum f. microphyllum, commonly known as the dwarf alpine alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with small, densely packed flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The foliage is characterized by tiny, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
