Aragallus albiflorus var. condensatus

Aragallus albiflorus var. condensatus, commonly known as the dense-flowered aragallus, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
