Alyssum stribrnyi f. gymnocarpum

Alyssum stribrnyi f. gymnocarpum, commonly known as the naked-seed alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, clustered flowers and pungent aroma. The species is distinguished by its reduced, often invisible seeds, giving it the "naked-seed" designation. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
