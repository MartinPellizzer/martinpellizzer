Artabotrys rhynchocarpus

Artabotrys rhynchocarpus, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling flames. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
