Anthurium buglossum

Anthurium buglossum, commonly known as the purple waxbill plant, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf known as a spathe. The plant features striking, waxy, purple to reddish flowers that are often used in ornamental gardening. Its foliage is typically dark green with a glossy texture, adding visual interest to indoor and outdoor spaces.

Anthurium buglossum thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.
