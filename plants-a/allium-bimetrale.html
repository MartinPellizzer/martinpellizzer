Allium bimetrale

Allium bimetrale, commonly known as the two-lobed leek, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a tall, flowering stem that bears umbels of white or pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic-like flavor, making it a cultivated vegetable in some regions.

Allium bimetrale is often grown for both its ornamental value and culinary use.
