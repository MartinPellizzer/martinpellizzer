Aleuritopteris aethiopica

Aleuritopteris aethiopica, commonly known as the African shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, shield-shaped fronds that are often deeply lobed. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the substrate while absorbing moisture and nutrients.

Aleuritopteris aethiopica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
