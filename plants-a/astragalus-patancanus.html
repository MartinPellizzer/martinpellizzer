Astragalus patancanus

Astragalus patancanus, commonly known as the Kashmir blue milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Astragalus patancanus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
