Arachis prostrata subf. longicalyx

Arachis prostrata subf.

longicalyx, commonly known as the prostrate peanut or groundnut, is a low-growing legume native to South America. It features hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, with small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces pods containing one or two edible seeds, which are similar to those of the common peanut. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This species is valued for its nutritional content and potential use in agriculture.
