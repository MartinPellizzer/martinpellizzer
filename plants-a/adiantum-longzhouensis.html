Adiantum longzhouensis

Adiantum longzhouensis, commonly known as the Longzhou maidenhair fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to China.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, lacy fronds that resemble those of other maidenhair ferns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a distinctive, elegant appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
