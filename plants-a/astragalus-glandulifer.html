Astragalus glandulifer

Astragalus glandulifer, commonly known as the glandular milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus glandulifer plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
