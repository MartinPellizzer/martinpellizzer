Astragalus cicer var. microphyllus

Astragalus cicer var. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces intricate, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in well-drained, arid soils. It is often found in dry grasslands and rocky slopes, contributing to soil stabilization.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, enhancing ecological diversity in its habitat.
