Asplenium ponapeanum

Asplenium ponapeanum, commonly known as the Ponape spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in humid, tropical environments. The plant has slender, dark green fronds that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the leaves.

Asplenium ponapeanum is native to the Caroline Islands and is adapted to thrive in shaded, moist conditions.
