Angianthus aureus

Angianthus aureus, commonly known as the golden angianthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, daisy-like blooms.

Angianthus aureus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and coastal landscapes.
