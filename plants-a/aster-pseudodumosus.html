Aster pseudodumosus

Aster pseudodumosus, commonly known as the dwarf aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its small, daisy-like flowers bloom in late summer, adding a vibrant touch to its native habitat.

Aster pseudodumosus is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
