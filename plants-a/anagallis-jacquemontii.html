Anagallis jacquemontii

Anagallis jacquemontii, commonly known as Jacquemont's pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant pink or red flowers with five petals. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Anagallis jacquemontii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to attract pollinators.
