Aplilia

Aplilia, commonly known as the common yarrow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that consist of many small florets, typically arranged in a disk shape. The plant has feathery, aromatic leaves that are often used in herbal remedies. Aplilia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, fields, and along roadsides.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
