Aristolochia soyauxiana

Aristolochia soyauxiana, commonly known as the Souchet du Cap-Vert, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe or a pipestem. The plant produces small, dark blue to purple berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
