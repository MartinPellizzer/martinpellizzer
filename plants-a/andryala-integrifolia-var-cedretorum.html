Andryala integrifolia var. cedretorum

Andryala integrifolia var. cedretorum, commonly known as the Cedretum sandverbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting foliage.
