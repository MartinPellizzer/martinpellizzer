Alopecurus creticus

Alopecurus creticus, commonly known as Cretan mouse-ear, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. The plant features small, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or white flowers. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is well adapted to arid environments.

Alopecurus creticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
