Abelia curviflora

Abelia curviflora, commonly known as the curlyflower abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy beneath. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Abelia curviflora thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Native Distribution

Abelia curviflora is primarily native to the southeastern United States.

It can be found in states such as Florida, Georgia, Alabama, Mississippi, and South Carolina. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in woodlands, swamps, and along stream banks. It is known for its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in the summer.

Abelia curviflora is a valuable addition to native gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Introduced Distribution

Abelia curviflora was mainly introduced to the eastern and southeastern United States as an ornamental plant due to its attractive pink flowers and hardy nature.
