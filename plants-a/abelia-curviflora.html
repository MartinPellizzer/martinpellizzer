Abelia curviflora

Abelia curviflora, commonly known as the curlyflower abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy beneath. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Abelia curviflora thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
