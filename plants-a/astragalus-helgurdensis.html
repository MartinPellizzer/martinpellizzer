Astragalus helgurdensis

Astragalus helgurdensis, commonly known as the Helgurd milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies due to its unique adaptations.

Its scientific classification and habitat make it a subject of research in plant systematics and conservation biology.
