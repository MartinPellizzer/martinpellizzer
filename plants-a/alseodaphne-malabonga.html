Alseodaphne malabonga

Alseodaphne malabonga, commonly known as the Malabar alseodaphne, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-yellow in color, followed by small, round berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
