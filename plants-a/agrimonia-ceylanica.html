Agrimonia ceylanica

Agrimonia ceylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon agrimony, is a perennial herb native to southern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate, serrated, and have a slightly rough texture.

Agrimonia ceylanica is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
