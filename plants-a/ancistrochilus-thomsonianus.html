Ancistrochilus thomsonianus

Ancistrochilus thomsonianus, commonly known as the Thomson's beardless orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a short, rhizomatous stem and a single, lance-shaped leaf. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a pale green or yellowish hue, often with a contrasting lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
