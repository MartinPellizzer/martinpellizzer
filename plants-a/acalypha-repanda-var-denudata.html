Acalypha repanda var. denudata

Acalypha repanda var. denudata, commonly known as the red-twigged ironweed, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red stems that become more prominent in the winter months. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically borne in the summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a smooth texture.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly for its vibrant winter color.
