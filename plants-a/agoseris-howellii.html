Agoseris howellii

Agoseris howellii, commonly known as Howell's goldenroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Agoseris howellii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
