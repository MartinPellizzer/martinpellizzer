Amphoradenium bipinnatum

Amphoradenium bipinnatum, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branching, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a powdery white coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its leaves are small, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted for water storage.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
