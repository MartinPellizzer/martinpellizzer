Aristolochia arborea

Aristolochia arborea, commonly known as the giant Dutchman's pipe, is a woody vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant produces showy, hooded flowers that attract insects, particularly flies, which are its primary pollinators. Its stems are covered in soft, hairy tissue, and it can grow up to 10 meters in length.

The plant is also known for its complex chemical composition, including various alkaloids.
