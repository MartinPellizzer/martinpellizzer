Aegiphila ternifolia f. oppositifolia

Aegiphila ternifolia f. oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leafed aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement, where leaves grow in pairs along the stem. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple, and it produces small, round fruits.

This variety is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
