Antirrhinum tortuosum var. compositum

Antirrhinum tortuosum var. compositum, commonly known as the composite antirrhinum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
