Albizia carrii

Albizia carrii, commonly known as the African silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is noted for its feathery, bipinnate leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Albizia carrii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
