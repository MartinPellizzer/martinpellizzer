Attalea limbata

Attalea limbata, commonly known as the yellow palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by various wildlife.

The yellow palm is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
