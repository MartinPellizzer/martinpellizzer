Acinos alpinus var. sardoa

Acinos alpinus var. sardoa, commonly known as the Sardinian alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Sardinia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in a dense layer of soft, white hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.
