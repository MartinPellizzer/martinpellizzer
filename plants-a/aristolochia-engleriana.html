Aristolochia engleriana

Aristolochia engleriana, commonly known as the Engler's arrowroot, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant produces small, yellow fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is commonly found in humid, lowland forests and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
