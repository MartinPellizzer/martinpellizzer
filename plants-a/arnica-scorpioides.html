Arnica scorpioides

Arnica scorpioides, commonly known as the scorpionweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Arnica scorpioides is not widely cultivated but is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
