Achillea scabra

Achillea scabra, commonly known as rough yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of linear, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Achillea scabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
