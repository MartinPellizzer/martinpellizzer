Astragalus heratensis

Astragalus heratensis, commonly known as the Herat milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and grasslands.

Astragalus heratensis is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
