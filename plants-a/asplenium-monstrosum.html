Asplenium monstrosum

Asplenium monstrosum, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, shield-shaped fronds that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, humus-rich soil. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Asplenium monstrosum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and adaptability to indoor conditions.
