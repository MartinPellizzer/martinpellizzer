Avena sativa var. macrotricha

Avena sativa var. macrotricha, commonly known as the giant oat, is a variant of the common oat species characterized by its tall stature and large, coarse leaves.

This plant is native to temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils with ample sunlight. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of florets, which produce both male and female flowers. The seeds are enclosed in a hull, and the plant is often cultivated for forage or as a cover crop.

Due to its robust growth, it is also used in ecological restoration projects to prevent soil erosion.
