Acer schwedleri

Acer schwedleri, commonly known as the Swedish maple, is a deciduous tree native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and parks.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage and compact size, making it a popular choice for urban landscaping.
