Alpinia siamensis

Alpinia siamensis, commonly known as the Siam ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features tall, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in spike-like inflorescences.

Alpinia siamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
