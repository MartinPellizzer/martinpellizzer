Adiantum rhomboideum var. strictum

Adiantum rhomboideum var. strictum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern native to the southeastern United States.

It features finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and have a distinctive, almost metallic sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity and well-drained soil.

Its unique appearance and graceful growth make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and indoor cultivation.
