Anthurium alienatum

Anthurium alienatum, commonly known as the red anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix and a showy spathe. The plant produces vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that are typically deep green with a reddish or maroon tinge. Its flowers, while not showy, are essential for pollination and contribute to its ecological role in its native habitat.

Anthurium alienatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
