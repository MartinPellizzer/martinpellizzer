Andropogon tenuifolius

Andropogon tenuifolius, commonly known as slenderleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is adapted to a variety of soil types.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
