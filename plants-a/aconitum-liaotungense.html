Aconitum liaotungense

Aconitum liaotungense, commonly known as the Liaotung aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply cut lobes, and the roots contain toxic alkaloids.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
