Abutilon woronowii

Abutilon woronowii, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its fibrous bark has been used traditionally in some cultures for its medicinal properties.

Abutilon woronowii thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
