Anomospermum minutiflorum

Anomospermum minutiflorum, commonly known as the dwarf flowered anomospermum, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. It produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its adaptation to harsh environments.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in drought-tolerant gardens.
