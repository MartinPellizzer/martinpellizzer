Axonopus ramosus

Axonopus ramosus, commonly known as the hairy axonopus, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, low-growing foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a rough, hairy texture.

Axonopus ramosus is often found in lawns, gardens, and disturbed soils due to its adaptability and rapid growth.
