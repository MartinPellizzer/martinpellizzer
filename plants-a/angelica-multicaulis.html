Angelica multicaulis

Angelica multicaulis, commonly known as the mountain angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the carrot family. Its roots are aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Angelica multicaulis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
