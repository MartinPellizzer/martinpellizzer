Adrastaea salicifolia

Adrastaea salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its narrow, willow-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Adrastaea salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
