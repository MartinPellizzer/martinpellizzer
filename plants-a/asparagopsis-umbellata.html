Asparagopsis umbellata

Asparagopsis umbellata, commonly known as the sea asparagus, is a halophytic plant that thrives in saline environments.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which are typical of its genus. Its leaves are often reduced to scale-like structures, and it is commonly found in coastal areas and brackish water habitats.

Asparagopsis umbellata is used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
