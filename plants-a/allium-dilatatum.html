Allium dilatatum

Allium dilatatum, commonly known as the broadleaf garlic or mountain garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, grass-like leaves. The plant produces upright flowering stems that bear small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in cooking.

Allium dilatatum thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to mountainous and alpine environments.
