Andropogon fuscus

Andropogon fuscus, commonly known as inland needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in full sun and is often found in prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is part of the Poaceae family and is known for its ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
