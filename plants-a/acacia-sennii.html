Acacia sennii

Acacia sennii, commonly known as the white acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, feather-like leaflets.

Acacia sennii is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of timber and fodder.
