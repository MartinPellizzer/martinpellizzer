Agave spinosa

Agave spinosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf agave, is a perennial succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, spiny, blue-green leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
