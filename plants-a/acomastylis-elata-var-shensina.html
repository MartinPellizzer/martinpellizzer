Acomastylis elata var. shensina

Acomastylis elata var. shensina, commonly known as the Shensin dandelion, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
