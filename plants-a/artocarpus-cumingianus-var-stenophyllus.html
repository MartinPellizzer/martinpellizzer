Artocarpus cumingianus var. stenophyllus

Artocarpus cumingianus var. stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves. The tree produces a fruit that is typically smaller and less fleshy compared to other breadfruit varieties. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its edible fruit and historical use in traditional medicine.
