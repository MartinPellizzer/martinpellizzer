Astragalus changduensis

Astragalus changduensis, commonly known as Changdu milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
