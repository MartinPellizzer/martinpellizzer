Alpinia molucana

Alpinia molucana, commonly known as the Java ginger or Malabar cardamom, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features erect, rhizomatous stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often aromatic. Its inflorescence consists of showy, pinkish-red bracts that enclose small, white, fragrant flowers.

Alpinia molucana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
