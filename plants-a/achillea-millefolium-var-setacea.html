Achillea millefolium var. setacea

Achillea millefolium var. setacea, commonly known as the Set-leaf Yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged in a distinctive, flat-topped inflorescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens and natural landscapes.

This variety is often used in herbal medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
