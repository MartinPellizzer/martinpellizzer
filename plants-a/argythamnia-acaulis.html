Argythamnia acaulis

Argythamnia acaulis, commonly known as the silverleaf sandthrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Argythamnia acaulis is adapted to arid conditions and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant in dry environments.
