Anthemis carpatica f. puberula

Anthemis carpatica f. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent carpathian ragwort, is a variant of the carpathian ragwort, characterized by its hairy foliage.

It is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian region of Europe, typically found in rocky or dry habitats. The plant produces yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
