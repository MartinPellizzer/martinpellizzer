Aster engelmannii var. paucicapitatus

Aster engelmannii var. paucicapitatus, commonly known as Engelmann's aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads that typically bloom in shades of white to purple. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads composed of many small florets. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is distinguished by its reduced number of flower heads compared to other Aster engelmannii varieties.
