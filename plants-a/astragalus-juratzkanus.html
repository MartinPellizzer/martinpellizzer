Astragalus juratzkanus

Astragalus juratzkanus, commonly known as the Juratzkanus milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of many Astragalus species. Native to Central Asia, this plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is part of the diverse flora in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
