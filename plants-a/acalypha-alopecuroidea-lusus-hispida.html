Acalypha alopecuroidea lusus hispida

Acalypha alopecuroidea lusus hispida, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in late summer. The leaves are typically rough to the touch, with a hairy texture that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
