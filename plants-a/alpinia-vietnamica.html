Alpinia vietnamica

Alpinia vietnamica, commonly known as Vietnamese ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other ginger species. The plant features rhizomatous stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in spikes, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

Alpinia vietnamica is valued for its aromatic rhizomes, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
