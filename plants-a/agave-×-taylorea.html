Agave × taylorea

Agave × taylorea, commonly known as the Texas aloe, is a hybrid succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Its rosette growth habit and ability to store water in its leaves make it well adapted to arid environments.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
