Alsine laxa

Alsine laxa, commonly known as slender sandwort, is a low-growing annual herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features small, greenish-white flowers clustered in dense, terminal spikes. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped and often arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Alsine laxa plays a role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas and is often found in desert and coastal environments.
