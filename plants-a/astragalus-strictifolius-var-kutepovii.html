Astragalus strictifolius var. kutepovii

Astragalus strictifolius var. kutepovii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, compound leaves with a distinctive pubescent texture. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to cream-colored, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in dry gardens for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
