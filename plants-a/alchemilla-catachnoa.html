Alchemilla catachnoa

Alchemilla catachnoa, commonly known as the rock alchemilla, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant features basal leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered with a dense layer of fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in racemes.

Alchemilla catachnoa is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy plant with potential ornamental and ecological value.
