Achillea depressa

Achillea depressa, commonly known as the low yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features basal rosettes of finely divided, gray-green leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in flat-topped clusters. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower meadows.

Achillea depressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
