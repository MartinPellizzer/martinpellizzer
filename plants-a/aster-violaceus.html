Aster violaceus

Aster violaceus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has a distinctive cone-shaped central disk surrounded by ray flowers, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Aster violaceus also plays a vital role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
