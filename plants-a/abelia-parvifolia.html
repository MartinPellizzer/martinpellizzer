Abelia parvifolia

Abelia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically 2-4 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Abelia parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
