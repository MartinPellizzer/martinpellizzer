Aptosimum oppositifolium

Aptosimum oppositifolium, commonly known as the opposite-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Aptosimum oppositifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.
