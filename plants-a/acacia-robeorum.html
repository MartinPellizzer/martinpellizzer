Acacia robeorum

Acacia robeorum, commonly known as the Robe's wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect shrub form. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Acacia robeorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
