Actinotus rhomboideus

Actinotus rhomboideus, commonly known as the diamond flower, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southern regions of Australia.

It features delicate, white or pale pink flowers that are arranged in dense, spherical clusters, giving it a unique and striking appearance. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is particularly valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming, dainty blooms.
