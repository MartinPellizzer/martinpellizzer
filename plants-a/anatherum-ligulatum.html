Anatherum ligulatum

Anatherum ligulatum, commonly known as the flatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its flat, grass-like leaves and spikelet-based inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three to five bracts surrounding the spikelets.

Anatherum ligulatum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for grazing animals.
