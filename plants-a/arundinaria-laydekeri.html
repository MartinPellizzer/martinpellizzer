Arundinaria laydekeri

Arundinaria laydekeri, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its rhizomes are widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

Arundinaria laydekeri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
