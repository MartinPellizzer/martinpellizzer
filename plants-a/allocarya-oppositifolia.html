Allocarya oppositifolia

Allocarya oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved mallow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fibrous roots and robust stems allow it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Allocarya oppositifolia is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
