Anguria heterophylla

Anguria heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf melon, is a unique species within the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinct variation in leaf morphology, which can range from heart-shaped to ovate or even elongated forms. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are unisexual, with separate male and female inflorescences. Its fruits are typically small, round, and green, though they are not widely cultivated for consumption.

This plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
