Anisodontea julii subsp. julii

Anisodontea julii subsp.

julii, commonly known as the Natal grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It typically grows in open grasslands and along coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
