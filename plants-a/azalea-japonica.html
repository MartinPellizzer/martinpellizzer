Azalea japonica

Azalea japonica, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a flowering shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring with vibrant pink, red, or white blossoms. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Azalea japonica is also valued for its ability to produce fragrant foliage and its adaptability to various garden settings.
