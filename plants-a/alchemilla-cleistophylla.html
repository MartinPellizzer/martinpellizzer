Alchemilla cleistophylla

Alchemilla cleistophylla, commonly known as the closed-leaf lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in loose inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Alchemilla cleistophylla is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
