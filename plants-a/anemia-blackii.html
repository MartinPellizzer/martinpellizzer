Anemia blackii

Anemia blackii, commonly known as the black anemone, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black petals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers bloom in early spring.

Anemia blackii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
