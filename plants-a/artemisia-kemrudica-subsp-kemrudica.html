Artemisia kemrudica subsp. kemrudica

Artemisia kemrudica subsp.

kemrudica, commonly known as the Karakoram wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flower heads.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its strong, medicinal scent.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain and its potential use in traditional medicine.
