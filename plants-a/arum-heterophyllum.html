Arum heterophyllum

Arum heterophyllum, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense underwater stands. Its flowering structure consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and ability to provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
