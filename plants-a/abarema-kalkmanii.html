Abarema kalkmanii

Abarema kalkmanii, commonly known as the Kalkman's bower vine, is a climbing plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, which is characterized by its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release its seeds, contributing to its propagation in the wild.

Abarema kalkmanii is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests, where it thrives in the undergrowth.
