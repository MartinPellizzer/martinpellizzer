Arenaria biflora subsp. pancicii

Arenaria biflora subsp.

pancicii, commonly known as the Pancici's sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to rocky and sandy soils in the Mediterranean region. It produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has thin, succulent stems and narrow, linear leaves adapted to conserve water. It thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor environments and is often found in coastal or alpine habitats.

Its subspecific classification highlights its distinct ecological and morphological characteristics compared to other members of the Arenaria species.
