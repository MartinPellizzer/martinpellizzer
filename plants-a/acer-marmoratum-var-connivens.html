Acer marmoratum var. connivens

Acer marmoratum var. connivens, commonly known as the variegated maple, is a striking deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, mottled foliage, which features a mix of green, white, and yellow patterns on its leaves. The tree typically grows to a height of 15-25 feet, with a rounded, upright form. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its unique leaf coloration.
