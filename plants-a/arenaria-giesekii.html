Arenaria giesekii

Arenaria giesekii, commonly known as the sandstone saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It typically features small, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pinkish flowers during the spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, nutrient-poor environments.
