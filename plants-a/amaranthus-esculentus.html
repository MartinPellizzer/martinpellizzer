Amaranthus esculentus

Amaranthus esculentus, commonly known as red amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and feather-like leaves that range in color from green to red or purple. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or red, depending on the variety. Its seeds are edible and have been cultivated for thousands of years for their nutritional value.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
