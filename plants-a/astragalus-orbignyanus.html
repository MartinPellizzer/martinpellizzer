Astragalus orbignyanus

Astragalus orbignyanus, commonly known as the Andean milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. It possesses a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus orbignyanus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional South American medicine.
