Anguina cucumeroides

Anguina cucumeroides, commonly known as the cucumber leaf miner, is a small moth species whose larvae feed on the leaves of cucumber plants.

The larvae create intricate, winding galleries within the foliage, which can significantly damage the plant's photosynthetic capacity. This pest is often found in warm, humid climates where cucumbers are cultivated. The adult moths are typically pale yellow or green, with distinctive wing patterns that aid in camouflage.

Due to its impact on crop yield, Anguina cucumeroides is a concern for agricultural pest management.
