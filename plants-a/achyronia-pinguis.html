Achyronia pinguis

Achyronia pinguis, commonly known as the fat bladder grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its robust, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typical of grasses. It is native to regions with dry climates, such as parts of Africa and the Middle East.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists studying succulent flora.
