Acalypha communis var. pallida

Acalypha communis var. pallida, commonly known as whiteleaf copperleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, pale green to white leaves that contrast with its bright red or orange bracts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
