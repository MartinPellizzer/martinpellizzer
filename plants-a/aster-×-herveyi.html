Aster × herveyi

Aster × herveyi, commonly known as the Hervey's aster, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing showy blue to purple blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster × herveyi is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
