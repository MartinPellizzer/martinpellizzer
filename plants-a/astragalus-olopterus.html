Astragalus olopterus

Astragalus olopterus, commonly known as the Turkestan milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus olopterus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it ecologically significant in nutrient-poor soils.
