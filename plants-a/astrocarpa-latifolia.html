Astrocarpa latifolia

Astrocarpa latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf astrocarpa, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are small and yellow, forming dense clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Astrocarpa latifolia is often found in moist, well-drained soils in open woodland or grassland habitats.
