Adenophora verticillata var. abbreviata

Adenophora verticillata var. abbreviata, commonly known as the short-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant features a compact growth habit with short, hairy stems and clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
