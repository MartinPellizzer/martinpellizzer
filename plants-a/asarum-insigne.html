Asarum insigne

Asarum insigne, commonly known as the noble wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, veined leaves and distinctive, fragrant flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant produces small, dark brown berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
