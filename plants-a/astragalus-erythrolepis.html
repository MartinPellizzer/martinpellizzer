Astragalus erythrolepis

Astragalus erythrolepis, commonly known as the red-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive reddish tinge, and its small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Astragalus erythrolepis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
