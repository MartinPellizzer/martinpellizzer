Alchemilla hoppeana var. alpigena

Alchemilla hoppeana var. alpigena, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and ability to tolerate harsh alpine conditions.
