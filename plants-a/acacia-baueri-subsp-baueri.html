Acacia baueri subsp. baueri

Acacia baueri subsp.

baueri, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and feathery, bipinnate leaves.

The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that appear in spring, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
