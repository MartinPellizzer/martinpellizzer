Amaryllis herbertiana

Amaryllis herbertiana, commonly known as Herbert's amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, strap-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring or early summer, producing vibrant pink or red flowers. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its striking appearance and relatively easy care make it a favored ornamental plant.
