Agathosma gracilicaulis

Agathosma gracilicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed agathosma, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, upright stems and small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
