Acalypha indica

Acalypha indica, commonly known as the Indian copperleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that often display vibrant red, orange, or yellow veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or red, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.

Acalypha indica is also known for its ability to tolerate partial shade and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
