Allium ampeloprasum var. holmense

Allium ampeloprasum var. holmense, commonly known as the broadleaf leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces umbel-like clusters of white to pale purple flowers, which are followed by spherical, blue-black berries. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, often used in cooking.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary versatility in various cuisines.
