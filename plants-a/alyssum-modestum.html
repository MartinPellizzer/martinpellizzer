Alyssum modestum

Alyssum modestum, commonly known as poor man's arnica, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Alyssum modestum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
