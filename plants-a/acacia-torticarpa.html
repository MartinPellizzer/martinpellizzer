Acacia torticarpa

Acacia torticarpa, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a small, deciduous tree native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its drooping branches and feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its seed pods are elongated and contain edible seeds, which have been used by Indigenous Australians for food and other purposes.

Acacia torticarpa is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands.
