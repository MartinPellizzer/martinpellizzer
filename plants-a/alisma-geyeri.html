Alisma geyeri

Alisma geyeri, commonly known as the Geyeri water plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Alismataceae.

It is a semi-aquatic plant that typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, wet meadows, and marshes. The plant has long, slender leaves that are often floating or partially submerged, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in early summer.

Alisma geyeri is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in regions with moist, temperate climates.
