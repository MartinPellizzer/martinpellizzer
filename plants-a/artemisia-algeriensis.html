Artemisia algeriensis

Artemisia algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian wormwood, is a shrub native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in composite flower heads. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Artemisia algeriensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies.
