Agapanthus globosus

Agapanthus globosus, commonly known as the African lily, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features dense, rounded clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant has strap-like, evergreen leaves that form a rosette at the base, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its botanical name reflects its globular flower arrangement and its association with the genus Agapanthus.
