Argythamnia guatemalensis

Argythamnia guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan argythamnia, is a shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss and reflect sunlight.

Argythamnia guatemalensis is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological resilience.
