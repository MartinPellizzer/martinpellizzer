Astragalus gymnalopecias

Astragalus gymnalopecias, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical, seed-filled pods that mature in late summer.

Astragalus gymnalopecias is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.
