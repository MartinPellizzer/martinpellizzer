Arabidopsis mollissima var. afghanica

Arabidopsis mollissima var. afghanica, commonly known as the Afghan hairy Arabidopsis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological traits and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
