Anacampseros filamentosa var. depauperata

Anacampseros filamentosa var. depauperata, commonly known as the sparse-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for gardeners in dry climates.
