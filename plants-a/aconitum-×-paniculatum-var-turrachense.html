Aconitum × paniculatum var. turrachense

Aconitum × paniculatum var. turrachense, commonly known as the Alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple hood-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or moist mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soil. It features compound leaves with palmate lobes and produces showy, sparsely arranged flowers in late summer.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
