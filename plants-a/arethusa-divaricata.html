Arethusa divaricata

Arethusa divaricata, commonly known as the swamp lantern or candleplant, is a flowering plant native to wetlands in North America.

It is characterized by its striking, lantern-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in areas with standing water. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, and it produces small, white, egg-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Arethusa divaricata plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
