Astragalus confirmans

Astragalus confirmans, commonly known as the confirmed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the genus Astragalus. It is adapted to dry environments and often grows in sandy or gravelly soils.

Due to its ecological significance and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
