Adiantum vestitum

Adiantum vestitum, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a species of polypod fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are covered in a dense layer of silky, white hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers that give way to spore-bearing structures.

The unique morphology and habitat preferences make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of the fern family.
