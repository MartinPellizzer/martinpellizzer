Asparagus scandens

Asparagus scandens, commonly known as climbing asparagus, is a perennial vine native to South Africa.

It features slender, climbing stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with several leaflets.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its ability to cover trellises or walls.
