Aster porteri

Aster porteri, commonly known as the Mexican aster, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white, often in late summer to early fall.

Aster porteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
