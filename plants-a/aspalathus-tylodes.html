Aspalathus tylodes

Aspalathus tylodes, commonly known as the small-leaved bushveld clover, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in shrubland and grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, rounded leaves and clusters of yellow flowers make it a distinctive member of the local flora.

It is often used in traditional medicine and has potential applications in ecological restoration.
