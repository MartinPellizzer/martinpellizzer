Asplenium ferulaceum

Asplenium ferulaceum, commonly known as the horse's tail fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its dense, wiry fronds that resemble a horse's tail. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The fronds are evergreen and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, making it a distinctive and ornamental plant.
