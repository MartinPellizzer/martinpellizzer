Aeonium × wildpretii nothosubsp. wildpretii

Aeonium × wildpretii nothosubsp.

wildpretii, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a succulent species native to the Canary Islands. It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina.

The plant produces striking red or orange flower stalks in late spring or early summer. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
