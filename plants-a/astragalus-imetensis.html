Astragalus imetensis

Astragalus imetensis, commonly known as the Eastern Himalayan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Astragalus imetensis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its ecological role includes nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
