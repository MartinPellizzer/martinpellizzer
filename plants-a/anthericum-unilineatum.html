Anthericum unilineatum

Anthericum unilineatum, commonly known as the single-line arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features a distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively. Its leaves are typically broad, lance-shaped, and have a prominent midrib.

The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and are pollinated by insects.
