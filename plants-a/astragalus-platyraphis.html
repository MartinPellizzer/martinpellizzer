Astragalus platyraphis

Astragalus platyraphis, commonly known as the flat-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, edible seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil.

Astragalus platyraphis is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
