Alepidea longipetiolata

Alepidea longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled lily, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing a fragrant, bell-shaped bloom that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Alepidea longipetiolata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant, dramatic display.
