Acacia rhodoxylon

Acacia rhodoxylon, commonly known as the red acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-brown bark and bipinnate leaves that are often feathery in appearance. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in globular inflorescences, attracting a variety of pollinators. It is typically found in dry savanna and woodland habitats, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for wildlife.

The wood of Acacia rhodoxylon is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and fuel.
