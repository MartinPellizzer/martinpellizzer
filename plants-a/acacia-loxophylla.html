Acacia loxophylla

Acacia loxophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are highly attractive to birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and feathery, and it has a strong, distinctive scent.

Acacia loxophylla is also valued for its hardiness and is often used in landscaping in regions with similar climates.
