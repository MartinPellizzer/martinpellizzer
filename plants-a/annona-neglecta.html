Annona neglecta

Annona neglecta, commonly known as the Mexican custard apple, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fruit that is typically yellow or green when unripe. The fruit, which is edible when ripe, has a creamy, custard-like texture and is often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed habitats.
