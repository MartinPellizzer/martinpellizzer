Amaria petiolata

Amaria petiolata, commonly known as the slender amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers, which can be white, pink, or purple, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Amaria petiolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
