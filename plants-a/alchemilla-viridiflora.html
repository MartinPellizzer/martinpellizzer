Alchemilla viridiflora

Alchemilla viridiflora, commonly known as green-flowered lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal leaves that are shield-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in upright, branched inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological landscaping.
