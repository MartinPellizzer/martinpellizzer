Astragalus baijensis

Astragalus baijensis, commonly known as the Baijens' milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of China, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
