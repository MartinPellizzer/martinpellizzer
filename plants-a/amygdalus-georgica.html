Amygdalus georgica

Amygdalus georgica, commonly known as the Georgian almond, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible almonds that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
