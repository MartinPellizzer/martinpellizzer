Asplenium adulterinum f. decumbens

Asplenium adulterinum f. decumbens, commonly known as the creeping spleenwort, is a fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with rhizomes that spread along the ground. The leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a dark green color and a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky crevices or on the bases of trees.

This fern is notable for its ability to tolerate low light conditions and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
