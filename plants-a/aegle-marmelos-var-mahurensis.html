Aegle marmelos var. mahurensis

Aegle marmelos var. mahurensis, commonly known as Mahurensis wood apple, is a variant of the wood apple tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree characterized by its rough, fissured bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces a yellowish or reddish fruit that is typically harvested when fully mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible pulp and seeds.
