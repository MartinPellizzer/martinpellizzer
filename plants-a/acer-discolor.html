Acer discolor

Acer discolor, commonly known as the Discolor Maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which changes color throughout the growing season, ranging from bright green to yellow, red, and orange. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. Its leaves are typically oval or ovate with a serrated edge and turn vibrant red in the fall.

The Discolor Maple is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
