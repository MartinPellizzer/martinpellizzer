Anemone scabriuscula

Anemone scabriuscula, commonly known as the rough anemone, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features a short, upright stem with basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces showy, white to pink flowers in early spring, typically blooming from April to June. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a distinctive, rugged surface.

Anemone scabriuscula thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded, well-drained soils.
