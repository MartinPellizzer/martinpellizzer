Aerva lanata f. grandifolia

Aerva lanata f. grandifolia, commonly known as large-flowered marshmallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, terminal clusters and bloom during the summer months.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
