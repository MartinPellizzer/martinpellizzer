Andersonia auriculata

Andersonia auriculata, commonly known as the golden saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, typically from September to November. The plant has a low-growing habit with fleshy, succulent leaves that help it survive in dry conditions. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Andersonia auriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
