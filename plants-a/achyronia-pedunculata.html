Achyronia pedunculata

Achyronia pedunculata, commonly known as the winged nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive winged stems and small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a slightly hairy surface.

Achyronia pedunculata is often found in dry, open habitats and is known for its toxic properties.
