Antirrhinum heterophyllum

Antirrhinum heterophyllum, commonly known as the Mexican snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has linear, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The species is part of the Plantaginaceae family, which includes other ornamental and medicinal plants.
