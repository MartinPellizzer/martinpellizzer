Aechmea leppardii

Aechmea leppardii, commonly known as the Leppard's pineapple plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light.

Aechmea leppardii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea leppardii has rosette-shaped leaves that are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a dense spiral, with a bright green color and a waxy coating.

The plant lacks a traditional stem, but its basal rosette forms a tight, compact structure with a dark, fibrous sheath at the base. Its flowers are clustered in a spike, with purple and white petals, blooming in late spring to early summer, and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a small, red capsule that disperses seeds by wind after splitting open.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in the soil, with some rhizomes supporting the plant's growth.
