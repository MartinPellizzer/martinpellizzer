Asarum canadense var. reflexum

Asarum canadense var. reflexum, commonly known as Canadian wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are hairy and emit a spicy aroma. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in a spiky inflorescence, typically blooming in late spring. Its roots are aromatic and have been used historically in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soil.
