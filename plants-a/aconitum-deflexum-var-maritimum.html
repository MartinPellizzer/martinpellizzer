Aconitum deflexum var. maritimum

Aconitum deflexum var. maritimum, commonly known as the coastal aconite, is a herbaceous perennial native to coastal regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is often cultivated in gardens but requires caution during handling.
