Aspidosperma platyphyllum

Aspidosperma platyphyllum, commonly known as the African dune tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in whorls of three to five, and its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on sandy soils and in coastal dunes.

The species is valued for its ecological role and has been used in traditional medicine.
