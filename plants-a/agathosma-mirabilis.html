Agathosma mirabilis

Agathosma mirabilis, commonly known as wild thyme or Cape thyme, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscaping.
