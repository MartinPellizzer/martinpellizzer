Astragalus douglasii var. glaberrimus

Astragalus douglasii var. glaberrimus, commonly known as Douglas's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its glabrous (smooth) stems and small, yellow,蝶形花 (butterfly-shaped) flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces cylindrical seed pods that release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
