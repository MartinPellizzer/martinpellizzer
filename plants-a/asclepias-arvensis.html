Asclepias arvensis

Asclepias arvensis, commonly known as field milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release silky, parachute-like seeds, aiding in wind dispersal. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Asclepias arvensis is also a host plant for the monarch butterfly, supporting its life cycle.
