Acridocarpus reticulatus

Acridocarpus reticulatus, commonly known as the Malabar chestnut, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant is notable for its hard, woody fruits that enclose a single seed, which is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
