Achras emarginata

Achras emarginata, commonly known as the African star apple, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the bark is smooth with a reddish-brown color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its fruit, which is rich in nutrients.
