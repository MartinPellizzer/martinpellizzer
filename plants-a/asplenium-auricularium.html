Asplenium auricularium

Asplenium auricularium, commonly known as the ear-shaped spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and narrow, leathery leaves that are often curled at the tips. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices and forest floors. Its sporangia are clustered in sori along the margins of the leaflets, a typical feature of many ferns.

The plant's unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies and horticulture.
