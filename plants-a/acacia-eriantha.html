Acacia eriantha

Acacia eriantha, commonly known as the hairy wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and feathery leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility. Acacia eriantha thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and coastal areas.

Its seed pods are typically flattened and contain several seeds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
