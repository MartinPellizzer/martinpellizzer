Astragalus coulteri var. fremontii

Astragalus coulteri var. fremontii, commonly known as Fremont's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as plains and foothills. It has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
