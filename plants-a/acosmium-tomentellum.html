Acosmium tomentellum

Acosmium tomentellum, commonly known as the woolly acosmium, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Acosmium tomentellum thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
