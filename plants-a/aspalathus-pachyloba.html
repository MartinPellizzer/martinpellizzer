Aspalathus pachyloba

Aspalathus pachyloba, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, spherical fruits. It is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

Aspalathus pachyloba is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
