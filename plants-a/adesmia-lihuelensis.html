Adesmia lihuelensis

Adesmia lihuelensis, commonly known as the Lihuel bush, is a shrub native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, compound leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Adesmia lihuelensis is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
