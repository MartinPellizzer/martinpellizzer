Arundo quila

Arundo quila, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and long, narrow leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found near water bodies such as rivers and lakes.

Arundo quila is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, making it both a useful and sometimes invasive species.
