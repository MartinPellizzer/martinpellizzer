Angelica globifera

Angelica globifera, commonly known as the globe angelica, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats.

Angelica globifera is known for its strong, aromatic scent and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
