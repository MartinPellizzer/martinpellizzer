Angelica nemorosa

Angelica nemorosa, commonly known as the wood angelica, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring a thick, aromatic root and large, compound leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, which develop into oval, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Angelica nemorosa is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
