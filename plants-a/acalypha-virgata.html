Acalypha virgata

Acalypha virgata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silky leaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that often resemble petals, giving it a decorative appearance. The plant has palmate leaves with a soft, velvety texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Acalypha virgata is also valued for its ornamental use in gardens and for its potential in phytoremediation.
