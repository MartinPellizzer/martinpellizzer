Acacia campylacantha

Acacia campylacantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves that are narrow and linear. The plant produces yellow globular flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes, which bloom in winter. Acacia campylacantha is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
