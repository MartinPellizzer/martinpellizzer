Amaryllis coenosa

Amaryllis coenosa, commonly known as the pink amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces tall, sturdy stems with broad, strap-like leaves that are evergreen in warmer climates. It blooms in late spring or early summer, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Amaryllis coenosa is relatively easy to care for, requiring well-drained soil and moderate sunlight to thrive.
