Aechmea podantha

Aechmea podantha, commonly known as the velvet bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in dense, velvety trichomes, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red or orange flowers that emerge from the center of the leaf rosette. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aechmea podantha is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea podantha has rosette-shaped leaves that are narrow, sword-like, and arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem.

The leaves are typically dark green with a waxy coating and can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant has a short, thick stem that is often hidden beneath the leaves, with a smooth, grayish-brown bark. Its flowers are clustered in a panicle-like structure, with bright red bracts that enclose small, white to pale yellow flowers, blooming during the summer months and pollinated by insects.

The fruit is a three-lobed capsule that disperses seeds through wind and animal consumption.
