Adenanthos glabrescens

Adenanthos glabrescens, commonly known as the silky adenanthos, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. Its flowers typically bloom from late autumn to early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Adenanthos glabrescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
