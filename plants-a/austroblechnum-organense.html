Austroblechnum organense

Austroblechnum organense, commonly known as the Organensis water fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive pale underside. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks and in humid forests. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in maintaining soil stability in its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in tropical ecosystems.
