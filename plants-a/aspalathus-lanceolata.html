Aspalathus lanceolata

Aspalathus lanceolata, commonly known as rooibos, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape province of South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate, thriving in well-drained, sandy soils. Its most notable feature is its unique, caffeine-free leaves, which are used to produce the popular herbal tea.

Aspalathus lanceolata plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various insects and birds.
