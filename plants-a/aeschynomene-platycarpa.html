Aeschynomene platycarpa

Aeschynomene platycarpa, commonly known as the flat-fruit aeschynomene, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive flat, pod-like fruits. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Aeschynomene platycarpa is often used in traditional medicine and can also serve as a forage plant for livestock.
