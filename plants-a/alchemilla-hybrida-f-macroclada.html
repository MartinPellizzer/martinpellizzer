Alchemilla hybrida f. macroclada

Alchemilla hybrida f. macroclada, commonly known as the broad-leaf lady's mantle, is a perennial plant characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to Europe, where it thrives in moist, shady environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is notable for its ability to retain moisture, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
