Adenophora triphylla var. japonica

Adenophora triphylla var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
