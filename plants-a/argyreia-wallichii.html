Argyreia wallichii

Argyreia wallichii, commonly known as the holy ghost plant, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety blue flowers that resemble the robes of the Holy Ghost, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth habit. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Argyreia wallichii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
