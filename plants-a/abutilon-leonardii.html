Abutilon leonardii

Abutilon leonardii, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Abutilon leonardii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
