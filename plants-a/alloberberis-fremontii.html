Alloberberis fremontii

Alloberberis fremontii, commonly known as Fremont's barberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Alloberberis fremontii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife in its native range.
