Acrocomia guianensis

Acrocomia guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan palm, is a tall, slender palm native to South America.

It features a tall, unbranched stem with a crown of large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into woody, three-angled fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching.

The Guianan palm is adapted to tropical climates and is often found in wetland or swampy areas.
