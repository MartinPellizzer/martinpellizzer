Ammophila pallida

Ammophila pallida, commonly known as the pale cordgrass, is a perennial grass native to coastal areas of North America.

It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and salt marshes. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are usually light green to pale yellow in color. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Ammophila pallida plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
