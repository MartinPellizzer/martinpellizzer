Abies arizonica pendula

Abies arizonica pendula, commonly known as the Arizona fir, is a coniferous tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The tree produces cones that are cylindrical and contain seeds enclosed in woody scales. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the mountainous regions of its native habitat.
