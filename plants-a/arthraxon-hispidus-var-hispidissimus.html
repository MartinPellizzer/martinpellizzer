Arthraxon hispidus var. hispidissimus

Arthraxon hispidus var. hispidissimus, commonly known as the rough leaved arthraxon, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along watercourses. Its inflorescence consists of a compact, cylindrical spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various conditions.
