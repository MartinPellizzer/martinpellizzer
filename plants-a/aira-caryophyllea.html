Aira caryophyllea

Aira caryophyllea, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of purple or pink flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that give way to fluffy, seed-filled spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking color and delicate appearance.
