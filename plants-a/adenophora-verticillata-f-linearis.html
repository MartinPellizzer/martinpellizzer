Adenophora verticillata f. linearis

Adenophora verticillata f. linearis, commonly known as the linear-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
