Amaranthus hypochondriacus var. monstrosus

Amaranthus hypochondriacus var. monstrosus, commonly known as the red leaf amaranth, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its vibrant, feather-like leaves that range from deep red to purplish hues.

This variety is a cultivar of the common amaranth, known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, which contrast beautifully with its colorful foliage. It is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and can be grown as a biennial or annual depending on the climate.

The leaves are edible and can be used in cooking, though the plant is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value.
