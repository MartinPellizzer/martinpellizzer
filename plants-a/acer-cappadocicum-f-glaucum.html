Acer cappadocicum f. glaucum

Acer cappadocicum f. glaucum, commonly known as the glaucous maple, is a unique variant of the maple tree characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage.

This form exhibits a compact growth habit with dense, rounded crowns, making it an ornamental choice for gardens and landscapes. The leaves are typically lobed and have a striking, waxy appearance due to the presence of a whitish bloom. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various climates.
