Alchemilla scaettae

Alchemilla scaettae, commonly known as the Sicilian lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Alchemilla scaettae is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
