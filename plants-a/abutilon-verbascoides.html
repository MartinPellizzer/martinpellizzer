Abutilon verbascoides

Abutilon verbascoides, commonly known as the Indian mallow or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its fibrous roots and branching stems make it adaptable to various soil types, while its rapid growth rate allows it to thrive in disturbed habitats.

Abutilon verbascoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
