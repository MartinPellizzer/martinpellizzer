Amorpha fruticosa f. albiflora

Amorpha fruticosa f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered false indigo, is a variant of the false indigo plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are arranged in racemes and are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects.

The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.
