Astrochlaena cephalantha

Astrochlaena cephalantha, commonly known as the Cape mountain heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of tiny, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
