Artemisia bourgeauana

Artemisia bourgeauana, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its strong, pungent scent.

It is widely used in traditional medicine and as a ceremonial plant in various cultural practices.
