Alsophila rugosula

Alsophila rugosula, commonly known as the rough fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of fronds that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived species. Its fronds are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.
