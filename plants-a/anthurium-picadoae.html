Anthurium picadoae

Anthurium picadoae, commonly known as the Picado's anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped spathes that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting green or white veins. The plant produces small, clustered spadices that are typically pale yellow or green, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium picadoae is popular as a houseplant due to its long-lasting blooms and air-purifying qualities.
