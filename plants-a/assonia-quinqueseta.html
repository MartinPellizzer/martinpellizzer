Assonia quinqueseta

Assonia quinqueseta, commonly known as the five-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered leaves that are typically fleshy and succulent. The plant produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate floral display to its habitat. Its leaves are arranged in a unique five-leaf pattern, giving it its scientific name, and it is well adapted to drought conditions.

Assonia quinqueseta is often found in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in full sun and requiring minimal water once established.
