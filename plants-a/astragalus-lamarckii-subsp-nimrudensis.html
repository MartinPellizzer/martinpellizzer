Astragalus lamarckii subsp. nimrudensis

Astragalus lamarckii subsp.

nimrudensis, commonly known as the Nimrud milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
