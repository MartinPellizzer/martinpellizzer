Achillea diversifolia

Achillea diversifolia, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Achillea diversifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
