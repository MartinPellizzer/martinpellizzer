Acanthophyllum kabulicum

Acanthophyllum kabulicum, commonly known as the Kabul spiny bush, is a low-growing shrub native to the arid regions of Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh, dry environments.

Due to its resilience, it plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and supporting local biodiversity.
