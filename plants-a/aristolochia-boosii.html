Aristolochia boosii

Aristolochia boosii, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy texture and a strong, aromatic odor. The plant produces intricate, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a prominent hood. It is often found in moist forest understories and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in various African cultures for treating ailments.
