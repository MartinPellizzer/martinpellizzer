Allium exiguiflorum

Allium exiguiflorum, commonly known as the narrow-flowered garlic.

It is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family, native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in umbels, and its leaves are strap-like and grass-like in appearance. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild garlic scent.

Allium exiguiflorum thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.
