Avena barbata var. pilosiuscula

Avena barbata var. pilosiuscula, commonly known as the hairy wild oat, is a perennial grass species native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are narrow, hairy, and often have a bluish-green hue, while the seeds are small and wind-dispersed. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in prairies, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

This plant plays a role in soil stabilization and serves as a food source for various wildlife species.
