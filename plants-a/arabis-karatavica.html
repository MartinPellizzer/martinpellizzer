Arabis karatavica

Arabis karatavica, commonly known as the Karatavica rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial species that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and have a distinctive lobed shape.

Arabis karatavica is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
