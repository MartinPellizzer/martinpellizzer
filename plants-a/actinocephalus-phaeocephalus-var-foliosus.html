Actinocephalus phaeocephalus var. foliosus

Actinocephalus phaeocephalus var. foliosus, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, with a tubular shape and a distinctive golden-orange corolla. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
