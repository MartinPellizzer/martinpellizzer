Arundinaria pedalis

Arundinaria pedalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bamboo, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in height. The plant forms dense clumps and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help it spread vegetatively, making it a hardy and resilient species.

Arundinaria pedalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
