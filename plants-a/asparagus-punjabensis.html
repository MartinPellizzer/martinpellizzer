Asparagus punjabensis

Asparagus punjabensis, commonly known as the Punjab asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to the Punjab region of Pakistan.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
