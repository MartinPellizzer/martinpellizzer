Aegiphila standleyi

Aegiphila standleyi, commonly known as the St. Augustine leatherleaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as under the canopy of tropical forests.

Aegiphila standleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.

Morphology

Its flowers are small, clustered in terminal panicles, with pinkish-white petals and yellow stamens, blooming during the dry season, and are primarily pollinated by insects.
