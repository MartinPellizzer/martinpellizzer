Acacia martinicensis

Acacia martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching structure and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Acacia martinicensis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.
