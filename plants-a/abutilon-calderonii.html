Abutilon calderonii

Abutilon calderonii, commonly known as the Mexican velvetleaf, is a shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Abutilon calderonii is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
