Arthraxon ciliaris

Arthraxon ciliaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Arthraxon ciliaris is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
