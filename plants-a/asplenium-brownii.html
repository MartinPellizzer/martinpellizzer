Asplenium brownii

Asplenium brownii, commonly known as the Brown's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and often have a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks.

Asplenium brownii is known for its ability to tolerate low light conditions and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
