Aspalathus acanthiloba

Aspalathus acanthiloba, commonly known as the Cape tea or red bush willow, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, spherical fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Aspalathus acanthiloba is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
