Aldama nudicaulis

Aldama nudicaulis, commonly known as the slender leaf aldama, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its small, narrow leaves are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring.

Aldama nudicaulis is often found in rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
