Atriplex limosa

Atriplex limosa, commonly known as saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in saline or alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh environments. The plant has small, often succulent leaves that help reduce water loss through transpiration. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences and are usually greenish or yellowish in color.

Atriplex limosa plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
