Abies pindrow var. brevifolia

Abies pindrow var. brevifolia, commonly known as the Kashmir pine, is a variant of the Pindrow fir native to the Himalayas.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its conical shape and dense foliage. The leaves are needle-like, short, and arranged in spirals along the shoots. It produces cones that mature over a two-year cycle, with the female cones typically reddish-brown in color.

This plant thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.
