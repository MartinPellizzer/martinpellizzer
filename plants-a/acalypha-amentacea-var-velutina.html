Acalypha amentacea var. velutina

Acalypha amentacea var. velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf acalypha, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, drooping clusters called catkins. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats make it an effective ground cover but also a potential weed in some habitats.
