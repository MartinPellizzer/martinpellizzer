Aglaonema scortechinii

Aglaonema scortechinii, commonly known as the silver queen plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, waxy, dark green leaves with prominent silver and white veins, creating a bold visual effect. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. It has a compact growth habit and typically reaches a height of about 1 to 2 meters.

Aglaonema scortechinii is also noted for its air-purifying qualities and resistance to common houseplant pests.
