Acanthophyllum turcomanicum

Acanthophyllum turcomanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan thorned grass, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and compact growth habit, which helps it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, pale green or yellow flowers that bloom in summer, contributing to its role in local ecosystems. It is adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in areas with low precipitation.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used for stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion.
