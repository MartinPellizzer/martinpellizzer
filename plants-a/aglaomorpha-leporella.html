Aglaomorpha leporella

Aglaomorpha leporella, commonly known as the lemon scented myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are often clustered together. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a glossy surface.

Aglaomorpha leporella is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
