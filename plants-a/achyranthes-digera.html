Achyranthes digera

Achyranthes digera, commonly known as Indian knotgrass, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has a slender, erect stem that branches towards the upper part. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a hairy surface. The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
