Abacopteris peltochlamys

Abacopteris peltochlamys, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arctic regions. It has a low-growing habit and is adapted to cold climates.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
