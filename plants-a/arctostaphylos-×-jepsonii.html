Arctostaphylos × jepsonii

Arctostaphylos × jepsonii, commonly known as the Jepson manzanita, is a hybrid shrub native to California.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in coastal and inland chaparral habitats. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are important for local wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
