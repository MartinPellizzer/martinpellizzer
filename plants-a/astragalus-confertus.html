Astragalus confertus

Astragalus confertus, commonly known as the dense milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in dry grasslands and open woodlands.

Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.
