Aloe erythrophylla

Aloe erythrophylla, commonly known as the red-leaved aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features striking red to orange-pink foliage that is often covered in a whitish powdery coating called farina. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with tubular yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy leaves.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is popular among succulent enthusiasts and gardeners in dry climates.
