Astragalus borysthenicus

Astragalus borysthenicus, commonly known as the Crimean milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the steppes of Eastern Europe and parts of Russia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its roots are known to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Astragalus borysthenicus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
