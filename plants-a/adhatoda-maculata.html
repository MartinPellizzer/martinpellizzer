Adhatoda maculata

Adhatoda maculata, commonly known as the spotted adhatoda, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with a distinctive spotted pattern. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
