Acalypha infesta var. rotundifolia

Acalypha infesta var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The leaves are typically ovate to round in shape, with a velvety texture and prominent veins. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

It also serves as a food source for various insects and birds in its native habitat.
