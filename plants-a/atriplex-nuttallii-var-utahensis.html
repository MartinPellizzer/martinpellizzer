Atriplex nuttallii var. utahensis

Atriplex nuttallii var. utahensis, commonly known as Utah saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in terminal spikes. It is often found in saline soils and is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

The species plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
