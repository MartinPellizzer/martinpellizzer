Arisaema saxatile

Arisaema saxatile, commonly known as the saxatile arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it produces small, green berries that contain the seeds.

Arisaema saxatile is typically found in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
