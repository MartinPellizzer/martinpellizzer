Avena alpina subsp. ausserdorferi

Avena alpina subsp.

ausserdorferi, commonly known as the alpine oat, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
