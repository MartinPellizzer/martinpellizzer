Arida riparia

Arida riparia, commonly known as the riverbank reed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the riparian zones of southern Africa.

It typically grows along riverbanks and wetlands, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in dense spikes during the late spring and summer. Arida riparia is known for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion along water bodies.

It plays an important role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
