Amygdalus kotschyi

Amygdalus kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi almond, is a shrub native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and almond-like fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a leathery texture that helps reduce water loss.

The fruit, which matures into a hard shell enclosing a single seed, is used in traditional medicine and for its edible kernel.
