Allocarya dispar

Allocarya dispar, commonly known as the slender lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its upright, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in late summer. Allocarya dispar thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
