Aster rani

Aster rani, commonly known as the Queen's aster, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

Aster rani is native to various regions in Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
