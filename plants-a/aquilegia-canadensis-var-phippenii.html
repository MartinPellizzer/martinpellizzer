Aquilegia canadensis var. phippenii

Aquilegia canadensis var. phippenii, commonly known as the Phippen's columbine, is a North American perennial herb characterized by its distinctive spurred petals.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the eastern United States. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy blue to purple flowers in late spring. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers partial shade with moist, well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
