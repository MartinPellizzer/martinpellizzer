Aphelandra montana

Aphelandra montana, commonly known as the mountain angel's trumpets, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting dark throat. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and glossy, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blooms.

Aphelandra montana thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.
