Astragalus fujisanensis

Astragalus fujisanensis, commonly known as the Mount Fuji milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often found in the vicinity of Mount Fuji. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it plays a role in soil stabilization in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
