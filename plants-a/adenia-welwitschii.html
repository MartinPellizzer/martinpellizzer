Adenia welwitschii

Adenia welwitschii, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a striking shrub native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red or purple throat. The plant features thick, woody stems and elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Adenia welwitschii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
