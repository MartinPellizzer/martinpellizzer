Amaryllis intermedia

Amaryllis intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant features strap-like leaves that are broad and waxy, typical of the Amaryllis genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Amaryllis intermedia is popular as a cultivated ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
