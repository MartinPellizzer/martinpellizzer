Acer pseudoplatanus var. crispum

Acer pseudoplatanus var. crispum, commonly known as the curly-leaved maple, is a variant of the common maple with distinctive, wavy, and often curled leaves.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe, particularly the Alps. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are usually bright green in summer and turn vibrant yellow in autumn.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in early spring before the leaves fully emerge.
