Amorphophallus fuscus

Amorphophallus fuscus, commonly known as the black-tongue arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces a fleshy, underground tuber that serves as its storage organ. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
