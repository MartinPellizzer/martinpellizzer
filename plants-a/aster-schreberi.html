Aster schreberi

Aster schreberi, commonly known as the Schreber's aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy blue to violet flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Aster schreberi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
