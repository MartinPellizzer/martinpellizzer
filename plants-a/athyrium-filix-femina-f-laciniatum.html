Athyrium filix-femina f. laciniatum

Athyrium filix-femina f. laciniatum, commonly known as the lace fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its fronds are bioluminescent, displaying a striking silvery-white coloration on the underside.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade.
