Amorpha brachycarpa

Amorpha brachycarpa, commonly known as the narrowpod false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its root system has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Amorpha brachycarpa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and adaptability.
