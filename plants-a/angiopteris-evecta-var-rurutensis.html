Angiopteris evecta var. rurutensis

Angiopteris evecta var. rurutensis, commonly known as the Rurutens Island fern, is a variant of the giant water fern native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can grow up to 2 meters in diameter. The plant has a unique reproductive structure with sori arranged in a distinctive pattern along the margins of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in dense forest undergrowth.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
