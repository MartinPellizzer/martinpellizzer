Anethum graveolens var. copiosum

Anethum graveolens var. copiosum, commonly known as giant dill, is a cultivated variant of the dill plant.

It is characterized by its large, feathery leaves and robust, branched stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into spherical seed heads. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its aromatic foliage and seeds, often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
