Alternanthera kerberi

Alternanthera kerberi, commonly known as the red flame plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red foliage that contrasts with its green stems. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often display a bright red or bronze coloration, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It is typically grown as a houseplant or in water gardens due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
