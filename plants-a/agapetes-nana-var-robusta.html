Agapetes nana var. robusta

Agapetes nana var. robusta, commonly known as the dwarf silver fir, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a silvery underside. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by small, dark blue berries. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
