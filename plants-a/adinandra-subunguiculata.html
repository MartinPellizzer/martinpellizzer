Adinandra subunguiculata

Adinandra subunguiculata, commonly known as the slender adinandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are woody capsules containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
