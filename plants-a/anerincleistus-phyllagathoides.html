Anerincleistus phyllagathoides

Anerincleistus phyllagathoides, commonly known as the creeping sandverbena, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features prostrate or decumbent stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The leaves are typically narrow, linear, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and often display a pale blue or white color.

This plant is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in poor soils and extreme temperatures.
