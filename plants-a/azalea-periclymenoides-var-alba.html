Azalea periclymenoides var. alba

Azalea periclymenoides var. alba, commonly known as the white flame azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features elliptical, dark green leaves. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to thrive in moist, shady conditions.
