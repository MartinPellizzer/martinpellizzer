Achillea speciosa

Achillea speciosa, commonly known as the showy yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has feathery, aromatic leaves and a compact, upright growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower meadows.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other composite flowers.
