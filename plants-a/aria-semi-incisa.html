Aria semi-incisa

Aria semi-incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lily, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces striking bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctively narrow leaf shape. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, adapting well to Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections around the world.
