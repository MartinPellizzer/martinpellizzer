Alchornea hederifolia

Alchornea hederifolia, commonly known as creeping alchornea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by oval, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Alchornea hederifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
