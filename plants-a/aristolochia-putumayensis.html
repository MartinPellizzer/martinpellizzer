Aristolochia putumayensis

Aristolochia putumayensis, commonly known as the Peruvian birthwort, is a climbing vine native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and produces distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often green or purple in color. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, some of which have been studied for their medicinal properties. It is also known for its complex chemical composition, including aristolochic acids, which can have both therapeutic and toxic effects.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
