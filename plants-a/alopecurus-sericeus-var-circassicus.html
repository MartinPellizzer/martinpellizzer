Alopecurus sericeus var. circassicus

Alopecurus sericeus var. circassicus, commonly known as the Circassian silky grass, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense tufts.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
