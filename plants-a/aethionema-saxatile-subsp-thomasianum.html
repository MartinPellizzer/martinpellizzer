Aethionema saxatile subsp. thomasianum

Aethionema saxatile subsp.

thomasianum, commonly known as Thomas's rockcress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are typically arranged in racemes. It is native to rocky and arid environments in the Mediterranean region, including parts of Turkey and Greece.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
