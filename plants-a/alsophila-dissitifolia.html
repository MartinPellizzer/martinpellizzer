Alsophila dissitifolia

Alsophila dissitifolia, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its hairy, fibrous trunk and bright green, glossy leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Alsophila dissitifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
