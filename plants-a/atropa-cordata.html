Atropa cordata

Atropa cordata, commonly known as the purple star lily, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It grows up to 30-60 cm tall and prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade.

Atropa cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
