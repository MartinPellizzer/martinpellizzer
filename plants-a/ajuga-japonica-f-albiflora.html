Ajuga japonica f. albiflora

Ajuga japonica f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese bugleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features a low-growing, spreading habit with dark green, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
