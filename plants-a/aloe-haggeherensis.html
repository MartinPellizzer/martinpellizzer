Aloe haggeherensis

Aloe haggeherensis, commonly known as the Haggeher's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water in arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of yellow or orange flowers. Its leaves are typically green with a waxy coating, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Aloe haggeherensis thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
