Anchusa altissima

Anchusa altissima, commonly known as the tall bugloss, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows up to 2 meters tall, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Anchusa altissima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
