Abutilon parvifolium

Abutilon parvifolium, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a herbaceous perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. Its growth habit is bushy, with multiple stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Abutilon parvifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
