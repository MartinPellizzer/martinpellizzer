Aeschynomene mimosifolia

Aeschynomene mimosifolia, commonly known as the mimosa tree or silky mimosa, is a fast-growing shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that fold when touched, a trait known as thigmonasty. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers arranged in racemes, followed by flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and is often used in reforestation projects.

Aeschynomene mimosifolia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, enhancing soil fertility.
