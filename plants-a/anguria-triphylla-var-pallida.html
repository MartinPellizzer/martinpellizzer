Anguria triphylla var. pallida

Anguria triphylla var. pallida, commonly known as the pale trifoliate cucumber, is a unique variant of the anguria plant characterized by its pale green foliage and distinct leaf morphology.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested when they are still immature and tender. Its trifoliate leaves, consisting of three leaflets, are a distinguishing feature of the species.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its mild, cucumber-like flavor.
