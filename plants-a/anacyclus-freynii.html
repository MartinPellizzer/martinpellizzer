Anacyclus freynii

Anacyclus freynii, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing annual herb with yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Anacyclus freynii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
