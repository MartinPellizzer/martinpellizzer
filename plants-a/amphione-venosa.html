Amphione venosa

Amphione venosa, commonly known as the veined orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking, veined leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive veining pattern that gives it its common name.

Amphione venosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
