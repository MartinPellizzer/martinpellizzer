Attalea dahlgreniana

Attalea dahlgreniana, commonly known as the Amazonian palm, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a tall, slender palm tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, with a slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, coconut-like fruits. Its botanical significance lies in its role within the rainforest ecosystem and its potential for sustainable use.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
