Aspalathus retroflexa subsp. amoena

Aspalathus retroflexa subsp.

amoena, commonly known as the Amoena bushveld clover, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a small, perennial shrub that typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a woody stem that becomes more branched with age.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
