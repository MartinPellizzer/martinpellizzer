Acrostichum ebeneum

Acrostichum ebeneum, commonly known as the broad-leafed fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, leathery fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The fronds are divided into numerous pinnae, giving it a lush, feathery appearance.
