Agave australis

Agave australis, commonly known as the southern agave, is a succulent plant native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The leaves are lined with sharp spines and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It produces a tall flower stalk that bears yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in the spring.

This plant is well adapted to arid and coastal environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
