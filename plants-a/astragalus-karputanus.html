Astragalus karputanus

Astragalus karputanus, commonly known as Karpu's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Balkans.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Astragalus karputanus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
