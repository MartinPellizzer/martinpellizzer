Ampelocissus sikkimensis

Ampelocissus sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim grape or Sikkim ivy, is a climbing plant native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its tendrils that help it climb various structures. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible berries that are used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and can grow quite long.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas at higher altitudes.
