Aconitum ponticum

Aconitum ponticum, commonly known as the Pontic monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has dark green, doubly serrated leaves that are often mistaken for those of other toxic plants. Aconitum ponticum contains potent alkaloids, making it highly poisonous to both humans and animals.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or alpine environments.
