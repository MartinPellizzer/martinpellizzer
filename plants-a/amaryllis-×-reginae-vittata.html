Amaryllis × reginae-vittata

Amaryllis × reginae-vittata, commonly known as the spotted amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis reginae and Amaryllis vittata.

It is characterized by its striking, spotted leaves that display a mix of green and white, often with a marbled or tiger-striped pattern. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This ornamental plant is popular for its vibrant foliage and seasonal floral display.
