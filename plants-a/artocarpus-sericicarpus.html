Artocarpus sericicarpus

Artocarpus sericicarpus, commonly known as the silky fig or silk floss tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a unique fruit with a fibrous, silky outer covering. The fruit contains a sweet, edible pulp and seeds enclosed in a hard, woody core. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The plant has a shallow root system, making it suitable for areas with well-draining soil.
