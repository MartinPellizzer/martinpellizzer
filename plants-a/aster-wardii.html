Aster wardii

Aster wardii, commonly known as the Ward's aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are usually purple or blue, with ray florets surrounding a central disc of tubular flowers.

Aster wardii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
