Agropyron firmum var. pungens

Agropyron firmum var. pungens, commonly known as the prairie wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple hues. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species.

Agropyron firmum var. pungens is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wildlife.
