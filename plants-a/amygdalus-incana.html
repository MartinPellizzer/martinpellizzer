Amygdalus incana

Amygdalus incana, commonly known as the woolly almond, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by hard, woody fruits called drupes. Amygdalus incana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
