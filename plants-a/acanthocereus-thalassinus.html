Acanthocereus thalassinus

Acanthocereus thalassinus, commonly known as the night blooming cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its columnar stem and spiny foliage, which help it retain water in arid environments. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warmer months, and it is adapted to thrive in full sun.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
