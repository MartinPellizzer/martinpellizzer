Achyrospermum radicans

Achyrospermum radicans, commonly known as the hanging spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, spidery flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces fruit that consists of three-winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
