Asterocephalus paucisetus

Asterocephalus paucisetus, commonly known as the rare alpine aster, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, harsh conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
