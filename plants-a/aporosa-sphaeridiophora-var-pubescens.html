Aporosa sphaeridiophora var. pubescens

Aporosa sphaeridiophora var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy aporosa, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, pubescent foliage and small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
