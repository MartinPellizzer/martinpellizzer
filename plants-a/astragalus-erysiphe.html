Astragalus erysiphe

Astragalus erysiphe, commonly known as yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus erysiphe is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
