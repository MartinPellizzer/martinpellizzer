Asperula aristata subsp. oreophila

Asperula aristata subsp.

oreophila, commonly known as the alpine azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom in cooler climates. Asperula aristata subsp.

oreophila is a hardy perennial that is relatively low maintenance once established.
