Aloe subacutissima

Aloe subacutissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features upright, slender stems that branch into clusters of narrow, fleshy leaves. The leaves are typically green with a waxy coating, and they have sharp, spiny edges. It produces yellowish-green flowers in summer, which are borne on tall, branched inflorescences.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
