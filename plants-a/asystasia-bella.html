Asystasia bella

Asystasia bella, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery-blue hue on the upper surface. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of violet or purple, which bloom during the warmer months. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage is popular for its aesthetic appeal and is used in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
