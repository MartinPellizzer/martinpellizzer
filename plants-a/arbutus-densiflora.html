Arbutus densiflora

Arbutus densiflora, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed by wildlife and humans alike. Its leaves are broad, leathery, and turn vibrant red in the fall.

Arbutus densiflora thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.
