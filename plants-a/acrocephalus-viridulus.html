Acrocephalus viridulus

Acrocephalus viridulus, commonly known as the green-headed twayblade, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a small, perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, spike-like inflorescence that bears greenish-yellow flowers. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

Due to its habitat preferences, it is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
