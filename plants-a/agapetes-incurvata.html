Agapetes incurvata

Agapetes incurvata, commonly known as the bent-leaf silverleaf, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a silvery underside, and it produces small, bell-shaped flowers in spring.

Agapetes incurvata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
