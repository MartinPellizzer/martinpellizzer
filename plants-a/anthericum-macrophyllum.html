Anthericum macrophyllum

Anthericum macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant arum, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length. The plant produces a dramatic inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a showy, purple spathe. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a complex structure of male and female flowers on the spadix.

Anthericum macrophyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
