Aristolochia puberula

Aristolochia puberula, commonly known as the hairy Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish-brown in color. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that give it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

The genus Aristolochia includes many species with similar morphological features and some that contain toxic compounds.
