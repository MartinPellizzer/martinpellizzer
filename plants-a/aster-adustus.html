Aster adustus

Aster adustus, commonly known as the burnt aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, purple to pinkish-red flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Aster adustus is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
