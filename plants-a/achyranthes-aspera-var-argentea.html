Achyranthes aspera var. argentea

Achyranthes aspera var. argentea, commonly known as silverleaf smartweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a distinctive silvery or pale underside, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn.
