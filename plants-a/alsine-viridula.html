Alsine viridula

Alsine viridula, commonly known as green alsike, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its stems are usually prostrate or ascending. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, ditches, and along roadsides.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, which includes other flowering plants with similar structural and floral characteristics.
