Acacia leprosa

Acacia leprosa, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces flat, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds, which are a traditional food source for some indigenous communities. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Acacia leprosa is also valued for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it an important component of its native ecosystem.
