Arctium chloranthum

Arctium chloranthum, commonly known as the lesser burdock, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and thistles, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces spiny, prickly fruits that contain seeds, a feature typical of the genus Arctium. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

Arctium chloranthum is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
