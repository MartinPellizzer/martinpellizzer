Adyseton hyperboreum

Adyseton hyperboreum, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically grows in moist, nutrient-poor soils and thrives in cold climates. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapting to the harsh environmental conditions of its habitat.

Adyseton hyperboreum plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native ecosystems and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
