Agropyron glaucum var. angustifolium

Agropyron glaucum var. angustifolium, commonly known as slender bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, bluish-green leaves and forms dense, low-growing sods. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its fine texture and durability make it a valuable component of native plant communities.

It is also utilized in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
