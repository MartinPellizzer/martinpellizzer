Amyema xylochlamys

Amyema xylochlamys, commonly known as the red-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It has small, leathery leaves and produces white or pink flowers that are often clustered together. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients but can photosynthesize to some extent. Its berries are red and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Amyema xylochlamys plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and nutrient cycling.
