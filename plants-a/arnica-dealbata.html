Arnica dealbata

Arnica dealbata, commonly known as the white arnica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Arnica dealbata is valued for its medicinal properties, with its flowers containing compounds used in traditional herbal remedies.
