Artocarpus pubescens

Artocarpus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces a fruit that is similar in appearance to breadfruit but is smaller and has a distinct hairy texture. The fruit is typically harvested when it is still green and is used in various culinary and traditional applications.

It also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for wildlife.
