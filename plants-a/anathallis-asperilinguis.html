Anathallis asperilinguis

The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it thrives on decomposing organic matter. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with a dense, hairy coating, which helps retain moisture in its native habitat.

Anathallis asperilinguis is noted for its specialized relationship with certain species of flies, which are its primary pollinators.
