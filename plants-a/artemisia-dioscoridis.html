Artemisia dioscoridis

Artemisia dioscoridis, commonly known as the Chinese wormwood, is a perennial herb native to China and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and small yellow flowers. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties. It is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported antiparasitic and anti-inflammatory effects.

Due to its ecological role and medicinal value, it is also of interest in conservation and pharmacological research.
