Adhatoda divergens

Adhatoda divergens, commonly known as the blue snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its root system is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.

Adhatoda divergens is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its therapeutic applications.
