Aegilops neglecta subsp. contracta

Aegilops neglecta subsp.

contracta, commonly known as the slender wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Botanically, it is noted for its genetic relationship to wheat, making it a subject of interest in agricultural research.
