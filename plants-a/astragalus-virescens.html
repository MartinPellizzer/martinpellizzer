Astragalus virescens

Astragalus virescens, commonly known as greenleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in early summer.

Astragalus virescens is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
