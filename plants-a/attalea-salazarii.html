Attalea salazarii

Attalea salazarii, commonly known as the Paraguayan palm, is a species native to South America, particularly found in Paraguay and Brazil.

It is a tall, slender palm with a single trunk and long, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The plant is adapted to wetland and marshy environments, making it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
