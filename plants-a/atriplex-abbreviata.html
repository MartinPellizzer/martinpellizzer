Atriplex abbreviata

Atriplex abbreviata, commonly known as shadscale, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, succulent leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system to access water. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating that reduces water loss.

Atriplex abbreviata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
