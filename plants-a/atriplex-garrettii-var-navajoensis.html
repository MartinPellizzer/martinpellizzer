Atriplex garrettii var. navajoensis

Atriplex garrettii var. navajoensis, commonly known as Navajo saltbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, succulent leaves that are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Navajo saltbush is also valued for its ability to stabilize soils and provide forage for wildlife.
