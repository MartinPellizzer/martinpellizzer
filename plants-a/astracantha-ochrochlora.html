Astracantha ochrochlora

Astracantha ochrochlora, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
