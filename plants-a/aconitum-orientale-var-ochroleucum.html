Aconitum orientale var. ochroleucum

Aconitum orientale var. ochroleucum, commonly known as the Chinese aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive silvery-white underside that gives it its common name.

The plant contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring careful handling.
