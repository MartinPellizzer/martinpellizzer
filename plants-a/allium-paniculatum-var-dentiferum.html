Allium paniculatum var. dentiferum

Allium paniculatum var. dentiferum, commonly known as the toothed garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It features upright, fibrous stems that bear narrow, grass-like leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in Asian cuisine.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary versatility in gardens and traditional dishes.
