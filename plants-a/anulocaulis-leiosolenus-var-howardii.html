Anulocaulis leiosolenus var. howardii

Anulocaulis leiosolenus var. howardii, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a rare and endemic plant species native to specific regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a distinctive caudex, which is a swollen base that stores water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it a hardy yet specialized species.
