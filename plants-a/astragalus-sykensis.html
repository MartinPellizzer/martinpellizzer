Astragalus sykensis

Astragalus sykensis, commonly known as the Sykhes mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus sykensis is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insect species.
