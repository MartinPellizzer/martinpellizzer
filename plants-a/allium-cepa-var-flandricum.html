Allium cepa var. flandricum

Allium cepa var. flandricum, commonly known as the Flanders garlic.

This variety is a bulbous plant characterized by its elongated, slender bulbs that are typically smaller than standard garlic. The leaves are grass-like, hollow, and cylindrical, growing upright from the base. It produces small, white or pink flowers in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its mild garlic flavor and ornamental value.
