Allium obtusum var. conspicuum

Allium obtusum var. conspicuum, commonly known as the showy garlic.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pale purple to white flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine settings.
