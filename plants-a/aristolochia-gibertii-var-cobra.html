Aristolochia gibertii var. cobra

Aristolochia gibertii var. cobra, commonly known as the Cobra vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, intricate, pipe-like flowers that resemble a cobra's head. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its fruit is a small, berry-like structure that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
