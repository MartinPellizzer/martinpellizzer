Acacia latispina

Acacia latispina, commonly known as the broadspined wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, cylindrical spikes, which appear in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia latispina is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
