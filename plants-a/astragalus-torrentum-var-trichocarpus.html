Astragalus torrentum var. trichocarpus

Astragalus torrentum var. trichocarpus, commonly known as the torrent milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils along streams and rivers, thriving in semi-arid to arid environments. The plant has slender, upright stems with small, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow or pale yellow, arranged in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
