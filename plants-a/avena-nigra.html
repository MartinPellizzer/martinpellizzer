Avena nigra

Avena nigra, commonly known as black oat, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, dark blue to black flowers. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy surface.

Black oat is known for its high nutritional value and has been used traditionally for forage and as a source of dietary fiber.
