Alchemilla xanthochlora f. sericans

Alchemilla xanthochlora f. sericans, commonly known as the golden alchemilla, is a compact, low-growing plant characterized by its dense clusters of bright yellow flowers.

It is a hybrid species within the genus Alchemilla, which belongs to the Rosaceae family. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a silvery-gray fuzz, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
