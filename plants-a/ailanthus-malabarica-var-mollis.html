Ailanthus malabarica var. mollis

Ailanthus malabarica var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-leaf flame of the woods, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which are followed by winged fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a hedge plant.
