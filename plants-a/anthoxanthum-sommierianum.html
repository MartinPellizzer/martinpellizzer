Anthoxanthum sommierianum

Anthoxanthum sommierianum, commonly known as the Sommier's oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, bearing small, yellowish to golden flowers.

Anthoxanthum sommierianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining biodiversity in grassland ecosystems.
