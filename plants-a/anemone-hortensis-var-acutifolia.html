Anemone hortensis var. acutifolia

Anemone hortensis var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaf garden anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, single or double white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are sharply pointed, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics include a short rhizomatous root system and a compact growth habit.
