Arctium × mixtum

Arctium × mixtum, commonly known as the common burdock.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Arctium lappa and Arctium tomentosum. This plant is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. It produces spiky, bur-like fruits that contain seeds covered in hooked bracts.

Arctium × mixtum is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
