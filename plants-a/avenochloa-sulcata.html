Avenochloa sulcata

Avenochloa sulcata, commonly known as the ridged millet, is a small annual grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, ridged seed heads. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are typically light brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Avenochloa sulcata is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used as a forage crop or in ecological restoration projects.
