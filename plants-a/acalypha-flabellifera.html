Acalypha flabellifera

Acalypha flabellifera, commonly known as the fanleaf copperleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that resemble a fan, hence its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Acalypha flabellifera is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
