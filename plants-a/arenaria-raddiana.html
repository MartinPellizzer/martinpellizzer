Arenaria raddiana

Arenaria raddiana, commonly known as sandspoon, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, and its leaves are typically succulent and fleshy. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy blooms.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
