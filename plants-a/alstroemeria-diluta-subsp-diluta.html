Alstroemeria diluta subsp. diluta

Alstroemeria diluta subsp.

diluta, commonly known as the pink alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina. It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white.

The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This species is also valued for its long-lasting blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.
