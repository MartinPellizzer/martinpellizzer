Astragalus caspicus f. sharifii

Astragalus caspicus f. sharifii, commonly known as the Caspian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, hairy leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological significance and potential medicinal properties.
