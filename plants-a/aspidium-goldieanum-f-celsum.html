Aspidium goldieanum f. celsum

Aspidium goldieanum f. celsum, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a variant of the common aspidium fern.

It is a member of the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green with a glossy appearance, and they can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
