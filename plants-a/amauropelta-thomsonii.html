Amauropelta thomsonii

Amauropelta thomsonii, commonly known as the Thomson's shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Amauropelta thomsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
