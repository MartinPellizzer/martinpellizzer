Andropogon diuturnus

Andropogon diuturnus, commonly known as inland needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in association with other native grasses and forbs in native plant communities.

Andropogon diuturnus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
