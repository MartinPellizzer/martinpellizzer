Acacia melanoxylon

Acacia melanoxylon, commonly known as the black wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and long, flat, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, followed by woody pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to dry climates and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is also valued for its hard, heavy wood, which has been historically used in construction and furniture-making.
