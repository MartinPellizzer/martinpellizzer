Alyssum crenulatum

Alyssum crenulatum, commonly known as the mossy alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum crenulatum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs and lacewings.
