Agathisanthemum quadricostatum var. pubescens

Agathisanthemum quadricostatum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy four-costate agathisanthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive hairy texture on both sides. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
