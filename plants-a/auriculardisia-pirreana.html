Auriculardisia pirreana

Auriculardisia pirreana, commonly known as the St. Helena orchid, is a rare and endemic species found exclusively on the island of St. Helena in the South Atlantic Ocean.

This small, herbaceous plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant's leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-white with intricate patterns. Due to its restricted habitat and limited distribution, it is highly vulnerable to environmental changes and human disturbance.
