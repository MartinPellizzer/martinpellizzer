Asplenium atrosquamosum

Asplenium atrosquamosum, commonly known as the black-squamate spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are typically clustered in dense, compact rosettes. The leaves are usually lanceolate to ovate in shape, with a rough, scaly texture on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found growing on rocks or tree trunks in temperate regions.

This fern is notable for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
