Acacia obbiadensis

Acacia obbiadensis, commonly known as the White Acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular heads, which are typically followed by long, straight pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
