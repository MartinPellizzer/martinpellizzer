Acantholimon scabiosum var. nudicalyx

Acantholimon scabiosum var. nudicalyx, commonly known as the naked calyx stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to the arid regions of western North America.

It features a rosette of fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery bloom, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to arid landscapes.
