Aspilia africana subsp. africana

Aspilia africana subsp.

africana, commonly known as the African torchflower, is a perennial herb native to sub-Saharan Africa. It is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescence that resembles a torch, composed of numerous yellow to orange-red ray florets.

The plant typically grows up to 1 meter in height, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
