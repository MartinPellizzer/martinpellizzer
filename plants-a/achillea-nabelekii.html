Achillea nabelekii

Achillea nabelekii, commonly known as the Nabelek's yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and white to pale yellow, arranged in flat-topped clusters.

Achillea nabelekii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
