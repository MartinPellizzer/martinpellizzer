Austrodanthonia remota

Austrodanthonia remota, commonly known as the narrow leaf wallaby grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and is adapted to coastal and inland environments. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native fauna.

Austrodanthonia remota is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
