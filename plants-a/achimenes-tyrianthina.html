Achimenes tyrianthina

Achimenes tyrianthina, commonly known as the crimson bellflower, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Achimenes tyrianthina is often cultivated as a decorative plant for its vibrant flowers and ornamental value in gardens.
