Anthurium scandens subsp. pusillum

Anthurium scandens subsp.

pusillum, commonly known as the small-stemmed anthurium, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America. It features heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that are typically smaller than those of other anthurium species.

The plant produces striking, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is a characteristic feature of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
