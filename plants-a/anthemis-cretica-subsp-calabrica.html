Anthemis cretica subsp. calabrica

Anthemis cretica subsp.

calabrica, commonly known as the Mediterranean ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its yellow flowers are surrounded by a ring of pale green to white ray florets, and it produces small, hairy seeds in a disk-shaped structure.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.
