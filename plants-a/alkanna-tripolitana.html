Alkanna tripolitana

Alkanna tripolitana, commonly known as the Persian bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-orange root, which contains a pigment called alkannin, giving it its vibrant color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, hot climates.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens.
