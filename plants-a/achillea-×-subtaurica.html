Achillea × subtaurica

Achillea × subtaurica, commonly known as the hybrid yarrow, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a cross between Achillea taurica and Achillea millefolium, resulting in a plant with distinctive foliage and floral characteristics. The leaves are finely divided and fern-like, while the flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in flat-topped clusters. This hybrid is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various garden environments.
