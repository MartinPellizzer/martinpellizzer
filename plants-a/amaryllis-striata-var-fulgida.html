Amaryllis striata var. fulgida

Amaryllis striata var. fulgida, commonly known as the golden amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, narrow leaves that are typically a vibrant green, and its most notable characteristic is its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange-red. The flowers are borne on upright stems and bloom in late summer to early autumn. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens or as a potted plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to many landscapes.
