Alchemilla espotensis

Alchemilla espotensis, commonly known as the Espotens rockfoil, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are typically basal, with a soft, velvety texture and a distinctive silver or grayish hue.

Alchemilla espotensis thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
