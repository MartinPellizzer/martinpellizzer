Allium ubipetrense

Allium ubipetrense, commonly known as the Uvillan garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous root system and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in the summer months. It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
