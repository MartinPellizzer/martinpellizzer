Alyssum murale subsp. caucasicum

Alyssum murale subsp.

caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian wallflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Caucasus region. It features dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to yellow and pink.

The plant has hairy, upright stems and is often found growing in rocky or dry soils. It is adapted to temperate climates and can thrive in full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
