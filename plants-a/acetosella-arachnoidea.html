Acetosella arachnoidea

Acetosella arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider plant or woolly sorrel, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its small, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, Acetosella arachnoidea is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
