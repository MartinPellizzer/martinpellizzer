Achillea millefolium var. fulva

Achillea millefolium var. fulva, commonly known as yellow yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are finely divided and feathery, with a pale yellowish-green hue. The plant produces flat, composite flower heads in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for their anti-inflammatory and astringent qualities.
