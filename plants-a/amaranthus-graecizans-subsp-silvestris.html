Amaranthus graecizans subsp. silvestris

Amaranthus graecizans subsp.

silvestris, commonly known as wild amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces vibrant red, orange, or yellow bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and branching.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
