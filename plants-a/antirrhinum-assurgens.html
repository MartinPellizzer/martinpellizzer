Antirrhinum assurgens

Antirrhinum assurgens, commonly known as the bushy toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Antirrhinum assurgens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
