Achillea borealis subsp. typica

Achillea borealis subsp.

typica, commonly known as alpine yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs in summer, producing clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
