Alpinia sumatrana

Alpinia sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia.

It is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features tall, leafy stems and produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Alpinia sumatrana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal properties.
