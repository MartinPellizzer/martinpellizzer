Acacia astringens

Acacia astringens, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its wood is hard and durable, historically used by Indigenous Australians for tools and weapons.

The plant also has medicinal properties, with some traditional uses involving its bark and leaves.
