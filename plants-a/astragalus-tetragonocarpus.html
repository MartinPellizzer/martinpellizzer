Astragalus tetragonocarpus

Astragalus tetragonocarpus, commonly known as the four-angle fruit milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces unique four-angled pods, which are a distinctive feature of its botanical description. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.
