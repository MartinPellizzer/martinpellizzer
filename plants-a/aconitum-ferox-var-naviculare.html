Aconitum ferox var. naviculare

Aconitum ferox var. naviculare, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern, and its roots contain potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and is not recommended for cultivation in gardens.
