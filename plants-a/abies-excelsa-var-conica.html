Abies excelsa var. conica

Abies excelsa var. conica, commonly known as the Colombian fir, is a variant of the white fir native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its conical shape and dense, dark green foliage. The tree typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its cones are slender and elongated, containing small, winged seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
