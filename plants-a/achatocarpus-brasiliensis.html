Achatocarpus brasiliensis

Achatocarpus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian burr bean, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its woody structure and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a hairy coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed habitats such as forest edges and clearings.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
