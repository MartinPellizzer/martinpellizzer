Arabis sparsiflora var. atrorubens

Arabis sparsiflora var. atrorubens, commonly known as the dark red rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
