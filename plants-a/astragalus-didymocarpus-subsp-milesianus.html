Astragalus didymocarpus subsp. milesianus

Astragalus didymocarpus subsp.

milesianus, commonly known as the Mile's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological significance and potential medicinal properties.
