Adesmia colinensis

Adesmia colinensis, commonly known as the golden bower, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that attract pollinators. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.

Adesmia colinensis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
