Andinia villosa

Andinia villosa, commonly known as the hairy andinia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features basal leaves that are hairy and often have a distinctive violet or purple coloration. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
