Astragalus nubigenus var. arnottianus

Astragalus nubigenus var. arnottianus, commonly known as the alpine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high mountain regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
