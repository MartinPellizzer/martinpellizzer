Andropogon moluccanus

Andropogon moluccanus, commonly known as Moluccan bluestem, is a perennial grass native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.

The species is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
