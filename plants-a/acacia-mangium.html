Acacia mangium

Acacia mangium, commonly known as white acacia, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia mangium is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it valuable for reforestation and agroforestry.

Its ecological importance includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil and supports other plant life.
