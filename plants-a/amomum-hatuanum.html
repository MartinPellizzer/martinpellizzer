Amomum hatuanum

Amomum hatuanum, commonly known as the Hatu nutmeg, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to the island of Timor and is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped fruit that resembles a small nutmeg. The plant produces a distinctive, spicy aroma due to the presence of essential oils, particularly myristicin and eugenol. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and antiseptic properties.

The botanical features of Amomum hatuanum make it a unique and valuable species within the genus Amomum.
