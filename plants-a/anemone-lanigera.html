Anemone lanigera

Anemone lanigera, commonly known as the woolly anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers with a distinctive, woolly texture on its leaves and stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its foliage is characterized by broad, heart-shaped leaves covered in dense, soft hairs.

Anemone lanigera blooms in early spring, adding a delicate floral display to its natural habitat.
