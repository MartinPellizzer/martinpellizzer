Afroaster ananthocladus

Afroaster ananthocladus, commonly known as the African starwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a compact head.

Afroaster ananthocladus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
