Adesmia guttulifera

Adesmia guttulifera, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
