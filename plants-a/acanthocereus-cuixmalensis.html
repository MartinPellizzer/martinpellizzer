Acanthocereus cuixmalensis

Acanthocereus cuixmalensis, commonly known as the Mexican night-blooming cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its ribbed stems and spiny areoles. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming make it a striking addition to desert gardens.
