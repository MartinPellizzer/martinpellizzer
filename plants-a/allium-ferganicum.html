Allium ferganicum

Allium ferganicum, commonly known as the Fergana leek, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant features a short, stocky stem with broad, flat leaves that are often used in local cuisines. Its bulbous base produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The botanical characteristics of Allium ferganicum make it both a valuable agricultural crop and a subject of botanical study.
