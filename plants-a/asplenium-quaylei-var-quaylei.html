Asplenium quaylei var. quaylei

Asplenium quaylei var. quaylei, commonly known as the Quay's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and slightly curled. The plant typically grows on the bark of trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. It produces spores in small, clustered structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is native to parts of Central and South America and is valued for its ornamental appeal in botanical gardens.
