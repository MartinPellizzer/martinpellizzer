Amygdalus persica scleronucipersica

Amygdalus persica scleronucipersica, commonly known as the Persian almond.

This plant is a variant of the almond tree, characterized by its thickened, hardened nut shell. It is native to regions of Central Asia and has been cultivated for its edible seeds and oil. The botanical features include its deciduous nature, compound leaves, and small white flowers.

Its fruit develops into a hard-shelled pod containing the nutrient-rich kernel.
