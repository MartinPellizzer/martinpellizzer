Adenandra multiflora

Adenandra multiflora, commonly known as the Natal hakea, is a South African shrub belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white, urn-shaped flowers. The plant produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Adenandra multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
