Anthemis pseudocotula subsp. rotata

Anthemis pseudocotula subsp.

rotata, commonly known as the spiral everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit.

The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern, giving it its common name. Its leaves are linear and often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained, sunny environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
