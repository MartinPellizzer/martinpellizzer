Acaena leptacantha subsp. glabricupula

Acaena leptacantha subsp.

glabricupula, commonly known as the smooth-cupule acaena, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and small, spiny leaves.

The plant produces spherical, woody fruits known as cupules, which are smooth and glabrous. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This subspecies is often found in coastal and montane regions, contributing to local biodiversity.
