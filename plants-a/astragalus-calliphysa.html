Astragalus calliphysa

Astragalus calliphysa, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and open woodlands. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus calliphysa is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
