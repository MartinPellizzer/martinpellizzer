Axonopus swallenii

Axonopus swallenii, commonly known as the Swallen's axonopus, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, often forming dense mats. The plant has slender, grass-like blades with a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in spike-like clusters.

Axonopus swallenii is valued for its ecological role in preventing soil erosion and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
