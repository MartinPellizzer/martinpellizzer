Aconitum toxicum subsp. toxicum

Aconitum toxicum subsp.

toxicum, commonly known as the European buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately compound, with three to five leaflets.

Like other members of its genus, it contains potent neurotoxins, making it highly dangerous if ingested.
