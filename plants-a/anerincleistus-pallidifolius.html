Anerincleistus pallidifolius

Anerincleistus pallidifolius, commonly known as the pale-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, leathery leaves that are typically pale green, which help it conserve water in its often arid habitat. The plant produces small white or pale yellow flowers, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits that are dispersed by birds. Its root system forms haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients.

Despite its parasitic nature, it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various bird species.
