Argylia puberula

Argylia puberula, commonly known as the silky argyle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in specialty gardens.
