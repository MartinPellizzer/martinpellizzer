Aster puniceus var. colbyi

Aster puniceus var. colbyi, commonly known as the Colby's aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces showy blooms during the late spring to early summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
