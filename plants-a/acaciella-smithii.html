Acaciella smithii

Acaciella smithii, commonly known as the Smith's acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, compound leaves and produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and can withstand periods of drought.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
