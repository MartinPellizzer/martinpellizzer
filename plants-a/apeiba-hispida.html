Apeiba hispida

Apeiba hispida, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its rough, bark-like fruit covering. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, elongated pods. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and has traditional uses in local medicine.
