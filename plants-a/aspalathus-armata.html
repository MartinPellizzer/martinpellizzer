Aspalathus armata

Aspalathus armata, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties. The plant features small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Aspalathus armata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils.
