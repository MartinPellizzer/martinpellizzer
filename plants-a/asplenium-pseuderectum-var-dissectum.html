Asplenium pseuderectum var. dissectum

Asplenium pseuderectum var. dissectum, commonly known as the dissected maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family (Aspleniaceae).

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in temperate regions. Its leaves are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, intricate pattern of serrations.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
