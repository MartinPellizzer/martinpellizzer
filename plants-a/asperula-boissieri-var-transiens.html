Asperula boissieri var. transiens

Asperula boissieri var. transiens, commonly known as the Transient Azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
