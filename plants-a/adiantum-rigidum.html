Adiantum rigidum

Adiantum rigidum, commonly known as the rigid maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of its fronds.

Adiantum rigidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
