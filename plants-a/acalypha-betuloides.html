Acalypha betuloides

Acalypha betuloides, commonly known as the maple-leaved ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, maple-like leaves that are palmately lobed and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by three-lobed, red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or inland areas with a warm, humid climate.

Acalypha betuloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
