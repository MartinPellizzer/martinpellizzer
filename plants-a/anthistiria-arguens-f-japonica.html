Anthistiria arguens f. japonica

Anthistiria arguens f. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese anthistiria, is a variant of the anthistiria plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it remains relatively rare in cultivation.
