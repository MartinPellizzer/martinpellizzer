Ampelocissus debilis

Ampelocissus debilis, commonly known as the weak climbing fig, is a vine native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.

Ampelocissus debilis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and ability to climb or trail along structures.
