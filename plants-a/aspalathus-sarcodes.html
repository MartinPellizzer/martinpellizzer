Aspalathus sarcodes

Aspalathus sarcodes, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand saline conditions.
