Acacia rhamphophylla

Acacia rhamphophylla, commonly known as the black wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods that split open when mature.

Acacia rhamphophylla is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
