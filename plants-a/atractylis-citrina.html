Atractylis citrina

Atractylis citrina, commonly known as yellow marsh cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and meadows. The plant features a creeping rhizome and produces bright yellow flowers with five petals. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
