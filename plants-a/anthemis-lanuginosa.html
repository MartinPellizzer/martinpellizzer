Anthemis lanuginosa

Anthemis lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

The woolly sunflower is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
