Armeria maritima f. laucheana

Armeria maritima f. laucheana, commonly known as the Lauche's sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in maritime climates. The plant produces dense clusters of white or pink flowers during the summer months. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in coastal garden settings.
