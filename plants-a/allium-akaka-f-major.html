Allium akaka f. major

Allium akaka f. major, commonly known as the Hawaiian leek, is a perennial herb native to the island of Hawaii.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its long, strap-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. It is valued for its edible leaves and is often used in traditional Hawaiian cuisine.

The f. major form is distinguished by its larger leaves compared to the typical variety of Allium akaka.
