Andropogon ibityensis

Andropogon ibityensis, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Andropogon ibityensis plays a role in local ecosystems and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
