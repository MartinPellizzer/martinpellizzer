Adiantopsis pedata

Adiantopsis pedata, commonly known as the creeping maidenhair fern, is a delicate, evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features finely divided, leathery fronds that are often curled or twisted, giving it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, moist soil. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly, making it suitable for ground cover in shaded gardens.

Adiantopsis pedata is valued for its elegant foliage and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in tropical and subtropical regions.
