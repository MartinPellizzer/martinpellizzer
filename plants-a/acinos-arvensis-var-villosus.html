Acinos arvensis var. villosus

Acinos arvensis var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy field cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in temperate climates.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
