Agalinis neoscotica

Agalinis neoscotica, commonly known as the scarlet false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, producing showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pinkish-red.

Agalinis neoscotica is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.
