Abarema villifera

Abarema villifera, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Brazilian velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, pod-like fruits that split open when mature.

Abarema villifera is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
