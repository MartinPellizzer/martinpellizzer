Amorphophallus bannaensis

Amorphophallus bannaensis, commonly known as the Banna arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces a spadix, a type of inflorescence, which is often surrounded by a colorful spathe. It is typically found in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
