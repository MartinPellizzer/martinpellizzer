Arabis hastatula

Arabis hastatula, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in a raceme, blooming in early spring.

Arabis hastatula is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
