Artemisia indica var. momiyamae

Artemisia indica var. momiyamae, commonly known as Japanese mugwort, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are finely divided and emit a strong, camphor-like scent when crushed.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
