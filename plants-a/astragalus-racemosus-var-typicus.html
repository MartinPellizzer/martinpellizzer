Astragalus racemosus var. typicus

Astragalus racemosus var. typicus, commonly known as the spiked milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant has compound leaves with pairs of oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

Astragalus racemosus var. typicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
