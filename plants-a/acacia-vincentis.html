Acacia vincentis

Acacia vincentis, commonly known as the Vincent's wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features slender, zigzagging stems and produces yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Acacia vincentis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
