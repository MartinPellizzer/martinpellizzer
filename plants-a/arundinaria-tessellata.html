Arundinaria tessellata

Arundinaria tessellata, commonly known as the tessellated bamboo, is a perennial grass native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often segmented and have a distinctive tessellated pattern on their surface. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with greenish-white flowers, which are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Arundinaria tessellata is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
