Arthraxon minor

Arthraxon minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watergrass, is a small aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters near the water's surface.
