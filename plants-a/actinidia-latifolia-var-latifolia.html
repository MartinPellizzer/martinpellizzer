Actinidia latifolia var. latifolia

Actinidia latifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by edible, fuzzy berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological systems, providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
