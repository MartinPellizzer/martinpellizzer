Acacia eliasiana

Acacia eliasiana, commonly known as the Elias's acacia, is a shrub native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its deep root system. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in local medicine for their purported healing properties.

Acacia eliasiana plays a role in supporting local wildlife, particularly insects and birds, due to its nectar-rich flowers.
