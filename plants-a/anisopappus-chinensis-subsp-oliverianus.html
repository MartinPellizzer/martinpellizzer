Anisopappus chinensis subsp. oliverianus

Anisopappus chinensis subsp.

oliverianus, commonly known as the Chinese sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of China. It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.

Its ecological significance is notable in semi-arid ecosystems.
