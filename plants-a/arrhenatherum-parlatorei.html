Arrhenatherum parlatorei

Arrhenatherum parlatorei, commonly known as the Parlatore's feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. The plant has slender, erect stems that bear panicles of small, spike-like flowers, which are usually greenish or pale brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Arrhenatherum parlatorei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
