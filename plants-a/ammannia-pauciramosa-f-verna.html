Ammannia pauciramosa f. verna

Ammannia pauciramosa f. verna, commonly known as the spring ammannia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant features slender, branching stems that often root at the nodes, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a reddish tinge. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed wetland areas.
