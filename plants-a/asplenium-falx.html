Asplenium falx

Asplenium falx, commonly known as the sickle fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to various regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its narrow, sickle-shaped fronds that are often dark green and glossy. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help it anchor to substrates, while its spore-producing structures are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium falx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
