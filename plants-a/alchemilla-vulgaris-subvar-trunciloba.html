Alchemilla vulgaris subvar. trunciloba

Alchemilla vulgaris subvar. trunciloba, commonly known as the rounded lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, waxy, gray-green leaves that are truncate or rounded at the tips. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
