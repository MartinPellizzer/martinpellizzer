Alyssum dudleyi

Alyssum dudleyi, commonly known as Dudley's alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It has hairy, gray-green leaves and thrives in well-drained soils.

Alyssum dudleyi is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
