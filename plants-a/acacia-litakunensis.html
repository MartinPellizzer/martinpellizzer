Acacia litakunensis

Acacia litakunensis, commonly known as the KwaZulu-Natal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves that are yellowish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are followed by flat, fibrous pods. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in coastal forests and shrublands.

Acacia litakunensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and animals.
