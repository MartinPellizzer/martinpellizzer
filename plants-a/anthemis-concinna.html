Anthemis concinna

Anthemis concinna, commonly known as the slender ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy.

Anthemis concinna is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in poor, well-drained conditions.
