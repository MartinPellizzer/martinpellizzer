Achillea millefolium var. alpestris

Achillea millefolium var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine yarrow, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are aromatic and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-resistant plants.
