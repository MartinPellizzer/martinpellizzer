Archidendron clypearia var. velutinum

Archidendron clypearia var. velutinum, commonly known as velvet senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, facilitating its spread in suitable habitats.

Velvet senna is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
