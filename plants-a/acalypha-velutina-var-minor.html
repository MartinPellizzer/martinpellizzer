Acalypha velutina var. minor

Acalypha velutina var. minor, commonly known as velvetleaf or silky ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on slender spikes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slight pubescence.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
