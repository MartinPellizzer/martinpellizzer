Athyrium × manickamii

Athyrium × manickamii, commonly known as the Manickamii fern, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Athyrium filix-femina and Athyrium capillus-veneris.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a refined and elegant appearance. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori, which are typically clustered on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its aesthetic appeal.
