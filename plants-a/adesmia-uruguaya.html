Adesmia uruguaya

Adesmia uruguaya, commonly known as the Uruguay burro's tail, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to survive in arid environments.

Adesmia uruguaya is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun conditions.
