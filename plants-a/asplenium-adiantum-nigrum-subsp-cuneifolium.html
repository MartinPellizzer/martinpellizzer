Asplenium adiantum-nigrum subsp. cuneifolium

Asplenium adiantum-nigrum subsp.

cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved maidenhair fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family. It is characterized by its finely divided, dark green, lance-shaped fronds that are often glossy and delicate.

The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.
