Aconitum hokurokuense

Aconitum hokurokuense, commonly known as the Japanese monkshood, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with a velvety texture and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. Aconitum hokurokuense is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its historical use in traditional medicine.

However, it is highly toxic and should be handled with care.
