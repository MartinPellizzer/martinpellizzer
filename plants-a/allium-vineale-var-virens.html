Allium vineale var. virens

Allium vineale var. virens, commonly known as green garlic or wild leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, and it produces small, white or pale purple flowers in umbels. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodland edges and meadows.

Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a valued plant in both culinary and medicinal uses.
