Agave perlucida

Agave perlucida, commonly known as the translucent agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a waxy coating. The leaves have a distinctive translucent or whitish appearance due to the presence of a dense layer of trichomes. It produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in late spring.

This plant is adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
