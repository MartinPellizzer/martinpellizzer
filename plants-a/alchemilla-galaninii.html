Alchemilla galaninii

Alchemilla galaninii, commonly known as the Galaninii lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
