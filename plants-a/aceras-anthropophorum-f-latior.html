Aceras anthropophorum f. latior

Aceras anthropophorum f. latior, commonly known as the broad-leaved thimbleweed, is a variant of the thimbleweed species native to North America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its growth habit is herbaceous, with a low, spreading form that often thrives in moist, open habitats.

This variety is notable for its distinct foliage and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
