Aragoa lycopodioides

Aragoa lycopodioides, commonly known as the mountain holly fern, is a species of clubmoss native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in cloud forests. The plant has a distinctive appearance with slender, upright stems and small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls. It produces spores rather than seeds, reproducing through asexual means typical of its lycophyte classification.

Aragoa lycopodioides plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms in its native ecosystem.
