Asplenium gracillimum

Asplenium gracillimum, commonly known as the slender spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices or forest floors. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium gracillimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats.
