Adiantum viridimontanum

Adiantum viridimontanum, commonly known as the green mountain maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bright green and slightly curled. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or wooded areas. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Adiantum viridimontanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with the right conditions.

Morphology

Adiantum viridimontanum has fern-like leaves that are pinnately divided, with a bright green color and a delicate, feathery appearance.

The leaves are alternate in arrangement, with a slender, upright stem that is typically light green and smooth in texture. This plant does not produce flowers; instead, it reproduces via spores rather than seeds. Its roots are rhizoidal, forming a dense network beneath the soil to anchor the plant and absorb nutrients.

The plant's growth pattern is clump-forming, with a compact and elegant appearance.
