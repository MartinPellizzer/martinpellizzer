Amelanchier ovalis var. grandifolia

Amelanchier ovalis var. grandifolia, commonly known as the Allegheny serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It features oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow or red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, berry-like fruits that ripen in early summer. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with shallow furrows.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open forests.
