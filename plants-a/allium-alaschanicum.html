Allium alaschanicum

Allium alaschanicum, commonly known as the Alaska leek, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, strap-like leaves. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears umbels of small, white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Allium alaschanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally by Indigenous peoples for culinary and medicinal purposes.
