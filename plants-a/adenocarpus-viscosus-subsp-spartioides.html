Adenocarpus viscosus subsp. spartioides

Adenocarpus viscosus subsp.

spartioides, commonly known as the sticky legume, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves and small, yellow flowers that appear in summer.

The plant produces small, oblong pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its unique morphology and adaptability, it is of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
