Ancistrorhynchus straussii

Ancistrorhynchus straussii, commonly known as the red-tailed panaque, is a species of freshwater catfish native to the rivers of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated body, barbels, and distinctive red coloration on its tail and dorsal fin. The plant-like structures on its body, known as cirri, are used for filtering food from the water. It thrives in slow-moving waters with abundant vegetation, which provides both shelter and a source of nutrition.

This species is popular in the aquarium trade due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
