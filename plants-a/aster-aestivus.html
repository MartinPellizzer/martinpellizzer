Aster aestivus

Aster aestivus, commonly known as summer aster, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Aster aestivus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
