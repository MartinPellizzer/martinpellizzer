Anthemis tuberculata f. discoidea

Anthemis tuberculata f. discoidea, commonly known as the dissected anthemis, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive, finely divided leaves and yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to regions of southern Europe and the Mediterranean. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its unique foliage, with a lace-like appearance, makes it a striking addition to gardens.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its visual appeal and hardy nature.
