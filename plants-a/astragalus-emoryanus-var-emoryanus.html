Astragalus emoryanus var. emoryanus

Astragalus emoryanus var. emoryanus, commonly known as Emory's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains. Its root system is deep and drought-tolerant, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
