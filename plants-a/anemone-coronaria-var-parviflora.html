Anemone coronaria var. parviflora

Anemone coronaria var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered windflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in spring. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed and hairy, and its stems are usually upright and branched. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
