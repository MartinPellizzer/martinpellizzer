Abronia nana var. covillei

Abronia nana var. covillei, commonly known as the Coville's dwarf sandverbena, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
