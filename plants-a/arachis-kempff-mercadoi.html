Arachis kempff-mercadoi

Arachis kempff-mercadoi, commonly known as the wild peanut, is a species of legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves, typical of many leguminous plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing one or two seeds. It is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought conditions.

Its genetic diversity makes it an important resource for peanut breeding programs.
