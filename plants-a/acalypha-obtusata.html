Acalypha obtusata

Acalypha obtusata, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it can become a problematic weed in agricultural and natural ecosystems.
