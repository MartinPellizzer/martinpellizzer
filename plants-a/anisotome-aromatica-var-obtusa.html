Anisotome aromatica var. obtusa

Anisotome aromatica var. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved anisotome, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It features basal leaves that are broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in early summer, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
