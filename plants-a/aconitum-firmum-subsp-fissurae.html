Aconitum firmum subsp. fissurae

Aconitum firmum subsp.

fissurae, commonly known as the fissured aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that are often hairy.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
