Aloe × chludowii

Aloe × chludowii, commonly known as the hybrid aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy. The plant produces striking orange or red flowers on tall, branching stalks during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Aloe × chludowii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a sunny, well-draining soil environment.
