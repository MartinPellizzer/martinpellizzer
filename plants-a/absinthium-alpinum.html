Absinthium alpinum

Absinthium alpinum, commonly known as alpine wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing at high altitudes.

Absinthium alpinum is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
