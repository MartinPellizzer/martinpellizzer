Arversia memphitica

Arversia memphitica, commonly known as the Memphis arrowhead, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically submerged or floating at the water's surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding aesthetic value to wetland ecosystems. Arversia memphitica thrives in shallow, still waters such as ponds and marshes.

It plays an important role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
