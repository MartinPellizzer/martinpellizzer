Alchemilla vulgaris var. diversiloba

Alchemilla vulgaris var. diversiloba, commonly known as the variegated lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically green with white or yellow markings, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, providing visual interest in garden landscapes.
