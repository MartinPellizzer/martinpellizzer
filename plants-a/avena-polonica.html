Avena polonica

Avena polonica, commonly known as Polish oat, is a species of grass native to Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of the stems, with small, white to pale blue flowers. It is typically found in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides.

Avena polonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
