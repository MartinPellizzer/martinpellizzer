Argyroderma longipes

Argyroderma longipes, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that are edible for certain animals. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
