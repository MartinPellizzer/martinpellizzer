Apopetalum pinnatum

Apopetalum pinnatum, commonly known as the featherleaf apopetalum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are finely divided and give the plant its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique foliage and relatively low maintenance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
