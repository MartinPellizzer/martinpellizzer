Amorpha lutea

Amorpha lutea, commonly known as yellow indigo bush, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Amorpha lutea is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
