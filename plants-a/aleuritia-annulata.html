Aleuritia annulata

Aleuritia annulata, commonly known as the ringed alder, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Aleuritia annulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
