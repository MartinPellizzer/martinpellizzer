Allium circinnatum

Allium circinnatum, commonly known as the purple garlic or the Spanish onion, is a bulbous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces umbels of pale purple to violet flowers. The plant is characterized by its swollen, garlic-like bulbs that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Allium circinnatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
