Astragalus kaleibarensis

Astragalus kaleibarensis, commonly known as the Karabare Astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
