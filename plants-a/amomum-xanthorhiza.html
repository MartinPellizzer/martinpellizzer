Amomum xanthorhiza

Amomum xanthorhiza, commonly known as wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a rhizomatous root system that is thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, pale greenish-white flowers in late spring. Its aromatic rhizomes are the primary part used, containing volatile oils and other bioactive compounds.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
