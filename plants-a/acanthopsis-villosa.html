Acanthopsis villosa

Acanthopsis villosa, commonly known as the hairy acanthopsis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Acanthopsis villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
