Aspalathus uniflora

Aspalathus uniflora, commonly known as the African green tea or rooibos, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid climates, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are harvested and processed to produce the popular herbal tea.

Aspalathus uniflora is also valued for its medicinal properties and is an important component of the local biodiversity.
