Atriplex halimus f. rifea

Atriplex halimus f. rifea, commonly known as the coastal saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to access water in dry conditions. It is often found along coastal areas and in inland salt flats.

Its ability to tolerate high salinity makes it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
