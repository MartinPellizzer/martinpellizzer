Alocasia virginica

Alocasia virginica, commonly known as the Virginica Elephant Ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil. Its foliage is often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink, making it a striking ornamental plant.

Alocasia virginica is popular in tropical gardens and as a houseplant due to its dramatic appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
