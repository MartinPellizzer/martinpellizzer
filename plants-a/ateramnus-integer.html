Ateramnus integer

Ateramnus integer, commonly known as the smooth thistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in composite heads. Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
