Apocynum cumanense

Apocynum cumanense, commonly known as the Cumanese dogbane, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae, which includes milkweeds and other toxic plants. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant contains cardiac glycosides, making it potentially poisonous to both humans and animals.
