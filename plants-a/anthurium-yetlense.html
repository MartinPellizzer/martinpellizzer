Anthurium yetlense

Anthurium yetlense, commonly known as the Mexican flamingo flower, is a tropical plant native to southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often a deep green with a metallic sheen, and the inflorescence can range from red to orange, depending on the variety.

Anthurium yetlense thrives in humid, warm environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
