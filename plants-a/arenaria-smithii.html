Arenaria smithii

Arenaria smithii, commonly known as Smith's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and arid environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white or pink, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually succulent and adapted to conserve water, reflecting its drought-resistant nature.

Arenaria smithii plays a vital role in stabilizing soil in harsh, mountainous regions.
