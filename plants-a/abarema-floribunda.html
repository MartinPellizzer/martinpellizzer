Abarema floribunda

Abarema floribunda, commonly known as the Brazilian flame flower, is a striking plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its dense, showy inflorescences. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year, making it a popular ornamental species. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and often reddish in color.

Abarema floribunda thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
