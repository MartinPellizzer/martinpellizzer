Agrostis pallescens

Agrostis pallescens, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that give it its characteristic appearance. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, producing small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Agrostis pallescens is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
