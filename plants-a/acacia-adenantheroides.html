Acacia adenantheroides

Acacia adenantheroides, commonly known as the mimosa tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. The plant produces yellow globular flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It has adapted to arid environments through its deep root system and ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important species in ecological restoration.
