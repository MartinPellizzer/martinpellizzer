Angelica archangelica f. tschimganica

Angelica archangelica f. tschimganica, commonly known as the Tschimganica variety of angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated for its aromatic roots and historical use in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other angelica species, particularly in its habitat and morphology.
