Ajuga thomsonii

Ajuga thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's bugleweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its dense mat of dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in spikes at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Ajuga thomsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
