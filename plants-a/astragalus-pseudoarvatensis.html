Astragalus pseudoarvatensis

Astragalus pseudoarvatensis, commonly known as the false goat's head, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves with numerous leaflets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus pseudoarvatensis is known for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
