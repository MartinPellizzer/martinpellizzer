Aster ascendens var. parryi

Aster ascendens var. parryi, commonly known as Parry's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.

It is a perennial herb with a fibrous root system and narrow, linear leaves.
