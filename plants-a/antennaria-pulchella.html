Antennaria pulchella

Antennaria pulchella, commonly known as the pretty willow herb, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Antennaria pulchella thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.
