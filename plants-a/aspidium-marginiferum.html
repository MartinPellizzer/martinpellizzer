Aspidium marginiferum

Aspidium marginiferum, commonly known as the marginal shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically light green with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, round spore cases called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions.

Aspidium marginiferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
