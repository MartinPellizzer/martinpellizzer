Aspidium xiphophyllum

Aspidium xiphophyllum, commonly known as the sword fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fronds that resemble a sword, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are divided into pinnae, which are arranged in a regular, alternating pattern.

Aspidium xiphophyllum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
