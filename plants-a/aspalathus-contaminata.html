Aspalathus contaminata

Aspalathus contaminata, commonly known as the common rooibos, is a shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant. It is also known for its unique ability to fix nitrogen through its root system, which benefits the surrounding soil.

Aspalathus contaminata is a key species in the fynbos biome, contributing to the region's biodiversity.
