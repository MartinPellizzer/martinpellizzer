Anacharis canadensis var. planchonii

Anacharis canadensis var. planchonii, commonly known as the Canadian waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It features long, flexible stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often branching profusely. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and arranged in whorls around the stem, facilitating efficient light absorption in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters, though they are not prominent in the submerged growth stage.

It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
