Astragalus ervoides var. maysillesii

Astragalus ervoides var. maysillesii, commonly known as the Mayssilles' milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
