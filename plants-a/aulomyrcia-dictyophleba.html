Aulomyrcia dictyophleba

Aulomyrcia dictyophleba, commonly known as the lace-veined fern, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are adorned with intricate, lace-like patterns on the underside. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks and branches in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually dark green and glossy, with a unique veining structure that distinguishes it from other fern species.

Aulomyrcia dictyophleba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
