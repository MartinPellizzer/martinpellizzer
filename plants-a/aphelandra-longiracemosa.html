Aphelandra longiracemosa

Aphelandra longiracemosa, commonly known as the long raceme torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, orange-red flowers. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are often used in ornamental horticulture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Aphelandra longiracemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
