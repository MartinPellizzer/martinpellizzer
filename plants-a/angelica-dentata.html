Angelica dentata

Angelica dentata, commonly known as the toothed angelica, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and distinctive foliage.

It is a biennial or perennial herb that typically grows in moist, temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant features large, compound leaves with finely toothed edges, and it produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Angelica dentata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
