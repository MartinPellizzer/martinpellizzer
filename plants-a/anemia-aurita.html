Anemia aurita

Anemia aurita, commonly known as the earleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters.

Anemia aurita is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
