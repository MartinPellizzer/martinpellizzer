Anemone rupicola subsp. sericea

Anemone rupicola subsp.

sericea, commonly known as the silky anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, silky pubescence, which gives it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
