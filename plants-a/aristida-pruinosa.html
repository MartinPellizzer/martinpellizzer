Aristida pruinosa

Aristida pruinosa, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-blue foliage, which is covered in a dense layer of hairs that give it a distinctive, frost-like appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for landscaping in dry climates.
