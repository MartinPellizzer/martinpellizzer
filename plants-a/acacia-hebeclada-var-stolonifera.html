Acacia hebeclada var. stolonifera

Acacia hebeclada var. stolonifera, commonly known as the stoloniferous acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats or colonies. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spherical heads, which are typical of the Acacia genus. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous tiny leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for native wildlife.
