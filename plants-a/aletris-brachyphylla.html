Aletris brachyphylla

Aletris brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-leaved wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodland edges. The plant features a basal rosette of dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and clustered in loose umbels.

Aletris brachyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
