Acrolasia desertorum

Acrolasia desertorum, commonly known as the desert acrolasia, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent growth form, which helps it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically small and fleshy, adapted to reduce water loss through transpiration.

Acrolasia desertorum is often found in sandy soils and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
