Acer ginnala f. jeonjuana

Acer ginnala f. jeonjuana, commonly known as the Jeonju maple, is a rare and ornamental variant of the horse chestnut maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
