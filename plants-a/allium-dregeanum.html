Allium dregeanum

Allium dregeanum, commonly known as the Cape garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage and flowers add a distinctive visual interest to gardens throughout the growing season.
