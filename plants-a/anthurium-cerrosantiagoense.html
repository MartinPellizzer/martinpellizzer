Anthurium cerrosantiagoense

Anthurium cerrosantiagoense, commonly known as the Santiago mountain anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Ecuador.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant spadix. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its foliage is usually deep green with a glossy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
