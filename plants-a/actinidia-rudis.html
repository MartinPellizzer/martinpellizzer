Actinidia rudis

Actinidia rudis, commonly known as the rough actinidia, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically lobed and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its berries are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Actinidia rudis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
