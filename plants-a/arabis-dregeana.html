Arabis dregeana

Arabis dregeana, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with four petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Arabis dregeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
