Acetosella triangularis

Acetosella triangularis, commonly known as the triangular sorrel, is a small, flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and have a distinctive triangular appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Acetosella triangularis is often found in calcareous soils and is known for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor.
