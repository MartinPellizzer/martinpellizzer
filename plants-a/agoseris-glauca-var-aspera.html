Agoseris glauca var. aspera

Agoseris glauca var. aspera, commonly known as the rough blue fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are gray-green and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture.
