Aconitum × lengyelii nothosubsp. walasii

Aconitum × lengyelii nothosubsp.

walasii, commonly known as the Lengyel hybrid aconite, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between different aconite species. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence.

This nothosubspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
