Adinandra polyneura

Adinandra polyneura, commonly known as the mountain ebony, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its hard wood and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for timber and horticultural purposes.
