Axonopus pubivaginatus

Axonopus pubivaginatus, commonly known as the hairy leaf axonopus, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. Axonopus pubivaginatus thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
