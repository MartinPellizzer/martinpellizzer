Anoda incarnata

Anoda incarnata, commonly known as the scarlet false flax, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Anoda incarnata is a member of the legume family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
