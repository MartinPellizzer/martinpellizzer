Arrhostoxylum rubrum var. attenuatum

Arrhostoxylum rubrum var. attenuatum, commonly known as the slender red thornapple, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals. Its leaves are narrow and have a slightly serrated edge, typical of many species in the Solanaceae family.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
