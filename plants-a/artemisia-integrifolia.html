Artemisia integrifolia

Artemisia integrifolia, commonly known as sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has silvery-white, glandular hairs on its leaves, which contribute to its distinctive scent. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Artemisia integrifolia plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
