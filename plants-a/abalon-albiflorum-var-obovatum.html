Abalon albiflorum var. obovatum

Abalon albiflorum var. obovatum, commonly known as the white-flowered abalon, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, obovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
