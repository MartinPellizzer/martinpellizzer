Astragalus lang-ranii

Astragalus lang-ranii, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in dryland ecosystems.

Astragalus lang-ranii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
