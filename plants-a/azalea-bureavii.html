Azalea bureavii

Azalea bureavii, commonly known as the Burea azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white during the spring season. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Azalea bureavii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils.
