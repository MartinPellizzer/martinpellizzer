Arachniodes subamoena

Arachniodes subamoena, commonly known as the silver lace fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a creeping rhizome and produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and indoor cultivation.
