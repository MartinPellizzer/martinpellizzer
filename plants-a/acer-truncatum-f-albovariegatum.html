Acer truncatum f. albovariegatum

Acer truncatum f. albovariegatum, commonly known as the white-variegated maple, is a compact, ornamental tree or shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which display white and yellow margins against a dark green background. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet, featuring a rounded, spreading form. Its small, red samaras appear in late summer, adding seasonal interest.

The foliage turns brilliant orange in the fall, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
