Alsine verna var. gerardi

Alsine verna var. gerardi, commonly known as Gerardi's common sandwort, is a low-growing annual plant native to North America.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters and features small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers with four petals, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in sandy or disturbed soils.

It is often found in open habitats such as roadsides, fields, and dunes.
