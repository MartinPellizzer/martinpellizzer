Ascarina

Ascarina, commonly known as the satinwood, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Caryocaraceae.

It consists of a single species, Ascarina lucida, native to tropical regions of Australia and New Guinea. The plant is characterized by its smooth, often waxy leaves and distinctive, compound fruits that resemble small, rounded clusters. Ascarina is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in traditional crafts and construction.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
