Alsine rosani var. dalmatica

Alsine rosani var. dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats. The plant features small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, adapting to arid conditions.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
