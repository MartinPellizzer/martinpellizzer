Aster dumosus var. gracilentus

Aster dumosus var. gracilentus, commonly known as the woolly aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer and early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and long blooming period.
