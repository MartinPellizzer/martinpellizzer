Archidendropsis paivana subsp. tenuispica

Archidendropsis paivana subsp.

tenuispica, commonly known as the slender-spined archidendropsis, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is a shrub characterized by its small, ovate leaves with a leathery texture.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
