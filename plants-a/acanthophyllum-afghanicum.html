Acanthophyllum afghanicum

Acanthophyllum afghanicum, commonly known as Afghan spiny holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its spiny stems and small, scale-like leaves adapted to arid environments. Native to Central Asia, particularly Afghanistan and Pakistan, it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its resilience, it plays a role in stabilizing desert ecosystems.
