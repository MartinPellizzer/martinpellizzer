Allophylus zeylanicus var. grandifolius

Allophylus zeylanicus var. grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaved Indian alder, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical to ovate in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences, which are followed by woody, three-lobed capsules containing the seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its hard wood and has traditional uses in local medicine.
