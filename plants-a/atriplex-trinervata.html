Atriplex trinervata

Atriplex trinervata, commonly known as the three-veined saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its gray-green, succulent leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant has a branched stem system that helps it stabilize in sandy or saline soils. Its small, greenish flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are pollinated by wind.

Atriplex trinervata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
