Acca sellowiana

Acca sellowiana, commonly known as the feijoadeira or Brazilian guava, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits resembling small apples. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Acca sellowiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisine and local beverages.
