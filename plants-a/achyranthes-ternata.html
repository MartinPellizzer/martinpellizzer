Achyranthes ternata

Achyranthes ternata, commonly known as smallflower smartweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers are usually pink or red, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Achyranthes ternata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
