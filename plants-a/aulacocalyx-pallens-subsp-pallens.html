Aulacocalyx pallens subsp. pallens

Aulacocalyx pallens subsp.

pallens, commonly known as the white-flowered arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, spatulate-shaped leaves and large, white, spadix-like inflorescences.

The plant belongs to the Araceae family, known for its unique floral structures. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and greenhouses.
