Acalypha rhomboidea

Acalypha rhomboidea, commonly known as the round-leaf or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rhomboid-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

Acalypha rhomboidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
