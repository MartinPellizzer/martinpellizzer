Adenophora liliifolia f. angustifolia

Adenophora liliifolia f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a horticultural form of the species Adenophora liliifolia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is native to regions of East Asia, including China, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The narrow foliage of this form distinguishes it from the typical variety, making it a unique addition to botanical collections.
