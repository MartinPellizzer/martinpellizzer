Anemia hirsuta var. humboldtiana

Anemia hirsuta var. humboldtiana, commonly known as the Humboldt's hairy anemia, is a variant of the Anemia hirsuta species native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its foliage contains bioactive compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal uses.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
