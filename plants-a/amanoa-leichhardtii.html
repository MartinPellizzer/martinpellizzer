Amanoa leichhardtii

Amanoa leichhardtii, commonly known as the Leichhardt's amanoa, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Amanoa leichhardtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
