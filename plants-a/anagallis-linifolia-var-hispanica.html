Anagallis linifolia var. hispanica

Anagallis linifolia var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish mouse-ear or Spanish pink, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers resembling tiny bells. The plant has hairy, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine white or grayish down. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
