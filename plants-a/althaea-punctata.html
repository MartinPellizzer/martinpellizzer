Althaea punctata

Althaea punctata, commonly known as the spotted mallows, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves with distinctive white or yellow spots. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Althaea punctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
