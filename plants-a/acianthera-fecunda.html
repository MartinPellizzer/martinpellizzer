Acianthera fecunda

Acianthera fecunda, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
