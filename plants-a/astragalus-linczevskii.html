Astragalus linczevskii

Astragalus linczevskii, commonly known as the Linczevsky milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds, typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and dry grasslands.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is of interest in traditional and modern herbal medicine.
