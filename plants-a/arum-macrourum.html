Arum macrourum

Arum macrourum, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a showy, white spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in wet, shady environments and is often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
