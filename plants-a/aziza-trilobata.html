Aziza trilobata

Aziza trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed aziza, is a species of flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves with three lobes. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, often arranged in clusters. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with succulent stems and leaves that help store water.

Aziza trilobata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
