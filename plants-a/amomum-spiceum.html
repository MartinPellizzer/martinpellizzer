Amomum spiceum

Amomum spiceum, commonly known as the fragrant cardamom, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, slender rhizomes and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale green flowers followed by oval, dark blue berries containing aromatic seeds. Its rhizomes are widely used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their distinctive fragrance and flavor.

Amomum spiceum thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for both commercial and culinary purposes.
