Acer pubipetiolatum

Acer pubipetiolatum, commonly known as the pubescent petioled maple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Sapindaceae.

It is native to eastern China, particularly in the provinces of Zhejiang and Jiangxi. The plant is characterized by its distinctive pubescent (hairy) petioles and trifoliate leaves with serrated margins. Its flowers are small and yellow-green, appearing in clusters during the spring.

The tree produces samaras, which are winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal.
