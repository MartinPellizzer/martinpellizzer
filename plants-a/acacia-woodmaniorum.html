Acacia woodmaniorum

Acacia woodmaniorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Its wood is valued for its durability and fine grain, making it useful in crafting and construction.

Acacia woodmaniorum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.
