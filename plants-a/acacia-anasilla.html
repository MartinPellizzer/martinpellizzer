Acacia anasilla

Acacia anasilla, commonly known as the desert acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant produces flat, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine. Acacia anasilla is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems and the ability to fix nitrogen, enhancing soil fertility.

Its resilience makes it an important plant for stabilizing desert ecosystems.
