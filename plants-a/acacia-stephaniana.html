Acacia stephaniana

Acacia stephaniana, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
