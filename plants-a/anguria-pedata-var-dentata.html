Anguria pedata var. dentata

Anguria pedata var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed leaf cucumber, is a variant of the anguria plant characterized by its distinctive serrated or toothed leaves.

This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are broad and lobed, often with a rough texture, and the stems are typically hairy. The fruit is a long, slender cucumber with a mild flavor, often used in culinary applications.

It is cultivated for both its edible fruit and ornamental value due to its striking foliage.
