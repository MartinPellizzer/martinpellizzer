Abrus grandiflorus

Abrus grandiflorus, commonly known as the Indian rattlepod, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy red and white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing flat, bean-like seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a robust root system that helps it thrive in various soil types.

The plant is also known for its toxic seeds, which contain harmful compounds and should be handled with care.
