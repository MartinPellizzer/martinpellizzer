Abrus grandiflorus

Abrus grandiflorus, commonly known as the Indian rattlepod, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy red and white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing flat, bean-like seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a robust root system that helps it thrive in various soil types.

The plant is also known for its toxic seeds, which contain harmful compounds and should be handled with care.

Native Distribution

Abrus grandiflorus is primarily native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is commonly found in forested areas, along riverbanks, and in moist, shaded environments. This plant thrives in regions with high humidity and well-drained soil. It is also present in some parts of Africa and the Pacific Islands.

Due to its ornamental value, it has been introduced to other parts of the world as a garden plant.

Introduced Distribution

Abrus grandiflorus was mainly introduced to ornamental horticulture due to its striking red flowers and unique seed pods.
