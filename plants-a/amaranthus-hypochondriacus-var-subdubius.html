Amaranthus hypochondriacus var. subdubius

Amaranthus hypochondriacus var. subdubius, commonly known as the Mexican love plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous true flowers that are clustered in dense, spiky inflorescences. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are edible and can be used in various culinary applications.
