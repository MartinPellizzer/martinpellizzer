Acianthera sarcosepala

Acianthera sarcosepala, commonly known as the fleshy-sepaled orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, fleshy sepals that are often brightly colored, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in a raceme, and are usually white or pale pink in color.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is a rare and endangered species in the wild.
