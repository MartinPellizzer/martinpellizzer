Anemone styriaca

Anemone styriaca, commonly known as the Styrian anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has palmately divided, lobed leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Anemone styriaca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
