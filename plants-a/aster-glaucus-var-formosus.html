Aster glaucus var. formosus

Aster glaucus var. formosus, commonly known as the Formosan blue aster, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique beauty and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
