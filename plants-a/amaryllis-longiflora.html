Amaryllis longiflora

Amaryllis longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, showy flowers that are typically white or pink in color. The flowers are trumpet-shaped and have six petals, with a distinctive spur at the base of each bloom. This species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, blooming in late summer to early autumn.
