Aira cupaniana subsp. incerta

Aira cupaniana subsp.

incerta, commonly known as the uncertain air grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
