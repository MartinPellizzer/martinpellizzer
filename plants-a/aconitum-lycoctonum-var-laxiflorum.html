Aconitum lycoctonum var. laxiflorum

Aconitum lycoctonum var. laxiflorum, commonly known as the laxiflorum monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple, spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features palmately compound leaves with a deep green color and serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
