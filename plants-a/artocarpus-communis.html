Artocarpus communis

Artocarpus communis, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 25 meters tall, with a broad, umbrella-shaped canopy. The plant produces large, green, spiny fruits that turn yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are large, alternate, and have a rough texture with a serrated margin.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is well adapted to coastal and lowland areas.
