Asplenium shimurae

Asplenium shimurae, commonly known as the Shimura's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern native to Japan.

It typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a rosette-like arrangement of lanceolate, dark green leaves with a smooth texture. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium shimurae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
