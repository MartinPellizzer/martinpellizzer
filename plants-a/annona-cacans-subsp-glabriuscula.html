Annona cacans subsp. glabriuscula

Annona cacans subsp.

glabriuscula, commonly known as the rough annona, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers with a strong, unpleasant odor.

The fruit is a rough, spiny berry that turns from green to yellow when mature. It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and thrives in warm, humid climates.
