Aristolochia inflata

Aristolochia inflata, commonly known as the inflated pipevine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that contain the seeds. It is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in length.

The leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, often with a strong, aromatic odor.
