Antirrhinum pallidiflorum

Antirrhinum pallidiflorum, commonly known as the paleflower snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, rounded seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and long blooming period.
