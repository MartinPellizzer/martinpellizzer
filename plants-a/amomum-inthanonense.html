Amomum inthanonense

Amomum inthanonense, commonly known as Thai cardamom, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This aromatic plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical rhizomes that produce fragrant, three-lobed leaves. The plant's inflorescence consists of a spike of small, white to pale yellow flowers, followed by reddish-brown berries containing aromatic seeds. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
