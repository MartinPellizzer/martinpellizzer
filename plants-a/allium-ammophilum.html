Allium ammophilum

Allium ammophilum, commonly known as the sand leek, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers in a loose cluster at the end of its stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Allium ammophilum is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued as an ornamental plant.
