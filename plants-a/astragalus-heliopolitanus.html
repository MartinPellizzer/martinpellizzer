Astragalus heliopolitanus

Astragalus heliopolitanus, commonly known as the sun-loving milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments, often growing on rocky slopes or in sandy soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
