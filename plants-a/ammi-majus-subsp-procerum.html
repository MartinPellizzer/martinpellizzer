Ammi majus subsp. procerum

Ammi majus subsp.

procerum, commonly known as the tall cow-weed, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its branched, hollow stems and finely divided leaves. It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to the western United States.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the carrot family. Its seeds are small and have a distinctive, pungent odor, which can be used in traditional herbal medicine.
