Aster paniculatus var. bellidiflorus

Aster paniculatus var. bellidiflorus, commonly known as the showy aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flower heads with purple to blue petals and yellow centers, blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
