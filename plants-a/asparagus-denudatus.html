Asparagus denudatus

Asparagus denudatus, commonly known as the naked asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that are often reduced or absent. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, spiny fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Asparagus denudatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes.
