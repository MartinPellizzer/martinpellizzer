Armeniaca zhidanensis

Armeniaca zhidanensis, commonly known as the Zhidan apricot, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and oval-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing a high degree of drought tolerance. Its fruits are edible, though they are not as widely cultivated as other apricot varieties.

The botanical features of Armeniaca zhidanensis make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
