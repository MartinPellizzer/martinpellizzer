Andropogon glaucescens var. lateralis

Andropogon glaucescens var. lateralis, commonly known as the eastern woodland bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland areas and marshy habitats. The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pinkish or purplish in color. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for wildlife.

This variety is often used in native plant gardens and restoration projects due to its adaptability and aesthetic qualities.
