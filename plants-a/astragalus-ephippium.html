Astragalus ephippium

Astragalus ephippium, commonly known as the broad-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus ephippium is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
