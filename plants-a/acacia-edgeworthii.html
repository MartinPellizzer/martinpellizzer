Acacia edgeworthii

Acacia edgeworthii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are grouped in dense, globular clusters. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened, leaf-like structures that serve as the primary means of photosynthesis. Acacia edgeworthii is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system for water absorption.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also a symbol of Australian national identity.
