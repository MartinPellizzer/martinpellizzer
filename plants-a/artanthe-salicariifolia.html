Artanthe salicariifolia

Artanthe salicariifolia, commonly known as the waterplantain, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland environments in North America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant thrives in moist soils and is often found along the edges of ponds, streams, and marshes. Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

It is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
