Axanthes elliptica

Axanthes elliptica, commonly known as the narrowleaf axanthes, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in sunny environments. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring.

Axanthes elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
