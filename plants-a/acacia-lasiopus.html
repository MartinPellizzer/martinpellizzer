Acacia lasiopus

Acacia lasiopus, commonly known as the rough acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and compound leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody pods containing flat, lens-shaped seeds. Acacia lasiopus is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in dry climates.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
