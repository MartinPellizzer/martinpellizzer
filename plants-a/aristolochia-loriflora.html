Aristolochia loriflora

Aristolochia loriflora, commonly known as the broadleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a distinct, often aromatic scent. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex floral structures and historical use in traditional medicine.
