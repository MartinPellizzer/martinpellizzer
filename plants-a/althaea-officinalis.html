Althaea officinalis

Althaea officinalis, commonly known as marshmallow, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy pink or white flowers. The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, often growing in moist, marshy habitats. Its roots and leaves contain mucilage, which has been traditionally used for its soothing properties.

Althaea officinalis is also cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal applications.
