Alyssum samolifolium

Alyssum samolifolium, commonly known as sweet alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species belonging to the family Brassicaceae. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear to oblanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

Alyssum samolifolium is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
