Anisostachya eromoensis

Anisostachya eromoensis, commonly known as the Eremoensis grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Anisostachya eromoensis is often used in restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
