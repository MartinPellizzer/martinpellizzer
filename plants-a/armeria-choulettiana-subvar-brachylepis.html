Armeria choulettiana subvar. brachylepis

Armeria choulettiana subvar. brachylepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thrift, is a low-growing, evergreen perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 inch wide. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and attractive floral display make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
