Angraecum rohrii

Angraecum rohrii, commonly known as the Rohri's orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It features long, slender stems and produces large, fragrant, white flowers with intricate patterns. The plant is adapted to grow on trees in humid, shaded environments, relying on its aerial roots for nutrient absorption. Its blooming period typically occurs during the rainy season, and it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its rarity and beauty.

Conservation efforts are crucial for its survival, as it faces threats from habitat loss and over-collection.
