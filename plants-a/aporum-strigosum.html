Aporum strigosum

Aporum strigosum, commonly known as the hairy aporum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which includes plants such as philodendrons and peace lilies. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, hairy texture. It produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are typically hidden within a spadix, a typical feature of the Araceae family.

Aporum strigosum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and unique floral structure.
