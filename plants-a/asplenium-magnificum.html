Asplenium magnificum

Asplenium magnificum, commonly known as the magnificent spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic or lithophytic plant that typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Asplenium magnificum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
