Althaea × cultorum

Althaea × cultorum, commonly known as the marshmallow plant, is a hybrid species belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties. Its hairy stems and mucilaginous roots have been used in traditional medicine to soothe respiratory ailments.

Althaea × cultorum prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile addition to gardens and wetland habitats.
