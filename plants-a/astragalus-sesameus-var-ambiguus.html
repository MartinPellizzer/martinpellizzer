Astragalus sesameus var. ambiguus

Astragalus sesameus var. ambiguus, commonly known as the ambiguous milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
