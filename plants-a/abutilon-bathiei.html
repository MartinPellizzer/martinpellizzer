Abutilon bathiei

Abutilon bathiei, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its common name.

Abutilon bathiei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
