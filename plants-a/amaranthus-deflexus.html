Amaranthus deflexus

Amaranthus deflexus, commonly known as the prostrate amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the variety. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Amaranthus deflexus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
