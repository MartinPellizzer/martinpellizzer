Agapetes pulcherrima

Agapetes pulcherrima, commonly known as the silverleaf alder, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact growth habit. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery down, giving it its common name. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink, which bloom in spring.

Agapetes pulcherrima is adapted to cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.
