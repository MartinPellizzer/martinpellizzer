Acrocephalus barakaensis

Acrocephalus barakaensis, commonly known as the Baraka reed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in areas with consistent moisture.
