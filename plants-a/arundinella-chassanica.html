Arundinella chassanica

Arundinella chassanica, commonly known as the African bulrush, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its flowering spikes produce small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown in color.

Arundinella chassanica is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
