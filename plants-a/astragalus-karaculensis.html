Astragalus karaculensis

Astragalus karaculensis, commonly known as Karacul's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Astragalus karaculensis is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
