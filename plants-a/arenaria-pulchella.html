Arenaria pulchella

Arenaria pulchella, commonly known as the pretty sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and coastal areas.

It typically features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes or rocky outcrops.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscaping.
