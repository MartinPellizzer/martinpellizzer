Andropogon × catarinensis

Andropogon × catarinensis, commonly known as the hybrid grass of the Andropogon genus, is a tall, perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and dense, tufted inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. This plant thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a robust stem that can grow up to several meters in height.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
