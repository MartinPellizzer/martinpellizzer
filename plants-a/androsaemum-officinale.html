Androsaemum officinale

Androsaemum officinale, commonly known as wild ginger, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries. Wild ginger is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its medicinal properties.

Its roots and leaves have been used traditionally for their purported health benefits.
