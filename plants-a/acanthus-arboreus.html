Acanthus arboreus

Acanthus arboreus, commonly known as the tree acanthus, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, woody stems that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, silvery hairs. Its showy, spiny flower clusters appear in late summer and are composed of small, pale blue to purple flowers.

Acanthus arboreus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical association with classical architecture.
