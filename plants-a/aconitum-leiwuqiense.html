Aconitum leiwuqiense

Aconitum leiwuqiense, commonly known as the Leiwuqi monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
