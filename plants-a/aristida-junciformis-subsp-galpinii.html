Aristida junciformis subsp. galpinii

Aristida junciformis subsp.

galpinii, commonly known as Galpin's threeawn, is a grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while the seeds are enclosed in bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
