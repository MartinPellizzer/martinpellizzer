Angelica henryi

Angelica henryi, commonly known as Henry's angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, umbrella-like compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a compound umbel, typical of the family. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Angelica henryi thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.
