Asplenium riparium

Asplenium riparium, commonly known as the water spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It typically grows along stream banks, wet rocks, and in damp woodlands, where it can absorb moisture from the surrounding environment. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base, forming a rosette-like structure. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Asplenium riparium is a hardy species that plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
