Aristida warburgii

Aristida warburgii, commonly known as Warburg's threeawn, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in drought conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture that helps reduce water loss.

Aristida warburgii is often found in open grasslands and along rocky outcrops, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
