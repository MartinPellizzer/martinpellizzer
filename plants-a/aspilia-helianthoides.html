Aspilia helianthoides

Aspilia helianthoides, commonly known as the African sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features erect, branching stems covered in stiff, hairy leaves. Its most striking feature is its large, yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer.

Aspilia helianthoides is adapted to dry environments and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
