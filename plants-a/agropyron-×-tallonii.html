Agropyron × tallonii

Agropyron × tallonii, commonly known as the tall wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species native to the North American Great Plains.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and coarse, bluish-green foliage. The plant produces spikelets that are often bracted and can be either spike-like or panicle-like in structure. Agropyron × tallonii is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it a valuable forage species.

It is also used in erosion control and wildlife habitat restoration due to its robust growth and ecological benefits.
