Arracacia anomala

Arracacia anomala, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, bright red berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it is sometimes considered a weed.
