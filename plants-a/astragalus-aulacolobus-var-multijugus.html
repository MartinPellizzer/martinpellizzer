Astragalus aulacolobus var. multijugus

Astragalus aulacolobus var. multijugus, commonly known as the many-lobed milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with multiple leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

This variety is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
