Allium paniculatum var. brevicaule

Allium paniculatum var. brevicaule, commonly known as the dwarf garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, slender stems that bear dense clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant has fragrant, grass-like leaves that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and edible gardens.
