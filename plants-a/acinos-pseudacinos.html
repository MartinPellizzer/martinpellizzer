Acinos pseudacinos

Acinos pseudacinos, commonly known as the alpine flax or narrowleaf acinos, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands.

Acinos pseudacinos is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
