Agave bovicornuta

Agave bovicornuta, commonly known as the giant cardon cactus, is a striking succulent native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its massive, spiny stems that can grow up to 15 meters in height, making it one of the tallest cacti in the world. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. Its flowers, which appear at the top of the plant, are yellow and are borne in large clusters.

The botanical features of Agave bovicornuta make it well-suited for survival in extreme desert conditions.
