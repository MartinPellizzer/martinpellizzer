Aconitum palmatifidum

Aconitum palmatifidum, commonly known as the broadleaf monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply cut, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is not recommended for gardens with children or pets.
