Alkekengi villosa

Alkekengi villosa, commonly known as the hairy autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the autumn, typically from September to November. The plant produces distinctive, inflated, orange-red berries that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, hairy textures, contributing to its common name.

Alkekengi villosa is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
