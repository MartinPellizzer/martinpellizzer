Abutilon braunii

Abutilon braunii, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Abutilon braunii is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
