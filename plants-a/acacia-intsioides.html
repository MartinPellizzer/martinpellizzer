Acacia intsioides

Acacia intsioides, commonly known as the African bristlepod, is a shrub or small tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, paired leaflets and produces yellow to brown pod-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ecological role and has traditional uses in various regions.
