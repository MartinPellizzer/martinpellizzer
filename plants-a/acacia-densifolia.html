Acacia densifolia

Acacia densifolia, commonly known as the denseleaf wattle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of multiple small, oval-shaped leaflets. Acacia densifolia is well adapted to sandy soils and salty coastal environments.

It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
