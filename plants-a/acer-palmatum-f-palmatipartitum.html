Acer palmatum f. palmatipartitum

Acer palmatum f. palmatipartitum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with variegated leaves, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features a mix of green, white, and pink hues on the leaves. The leaves are palmately lobed, with a unique pattern of coloration that sets it apart from other maple varieties. This ornamental plant is prized for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
