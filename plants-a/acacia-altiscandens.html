Acacia altiscandens

Acacia altiscandens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of small, needle-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

Acacia altiscandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
