Astragalus monspessulanus proles cossonii

Astragalus monspessulanus proles cossonii, commonly known as the Cosson's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root system.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
