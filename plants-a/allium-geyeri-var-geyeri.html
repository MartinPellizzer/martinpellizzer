Allium geyeri var. geyeri

Allium geyeri var. geyeri, commonly known as the Sierra onion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in open meadows and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is part of the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native habitats.
