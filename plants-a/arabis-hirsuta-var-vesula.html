Arabis hirsuta var. vesula

Arabis hirsuta var. vesula, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, cross-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces clusters of yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
