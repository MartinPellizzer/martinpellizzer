Adiantum deltoideum

Adiantum deltoideum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of polypod fern characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is distinguished by its bright green, lanceolate leaves that are often adorned with a fine, silvery vein. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or in forest undergrowth.

Adiantum deltoideum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
