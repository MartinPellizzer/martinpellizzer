Anemone hepatica var. hispanica

Anemone hepatica var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish windflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in early spring. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive, lacy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued in gardens for its early blooming habit and ornamental appeal.
