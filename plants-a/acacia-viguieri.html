Acacia viguieri

Acacia viguieri, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and its clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces flat, bean-like pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia viguieri thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
