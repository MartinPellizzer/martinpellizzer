Aesculus Indica

Aesculus indica is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal. It is known for its striking showy flowers and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. The plant belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is also referred to as the Indian horse chestnut. Its leaves are palmately compound and its flowers typically bloom in spring. Aesculus indica has historical uses in traditional medicine and is valued for its ecological and aesthetic contributions.
