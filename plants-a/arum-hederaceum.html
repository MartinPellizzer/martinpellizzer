Arum hederaceum

Arum hederaceum, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive arrowhead shape, often with a waxy texture and a deep green color. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of yellow or red. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in water gardens or as a decorative houseplant.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal rather than for its edible parts.
