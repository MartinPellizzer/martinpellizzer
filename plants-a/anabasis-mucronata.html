Anabasis mucronata

Anabasis mucronata, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub with slender, often branched stems that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by capsules containing numerous tiny seeds. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Anabasis mucronata is well adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate high temperatures and low water availability.
