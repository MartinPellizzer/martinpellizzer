Anoectochilus papillosus

Anoectochilus papillosus, commonly known as the hairy tongue orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy or velvety texture on the leaf surfaces, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of white, pink, or purple.

Anoectochilus papillosus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
