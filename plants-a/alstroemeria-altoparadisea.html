Alstroemeria altoparadisea

Alstroemeria altoparadisea, commonly known as the paradise lily, is a striking South American plant characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is native to regions such as Chile and Argentina. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of brightly colored blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Alstroemeria altoparadisea thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.
