Adonis cristata

Adonis cristata, commonly known as the crested lark's foot, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus.

It is a perennial herb with a rosette of basal leaves and produces striking yellow flowers with prominent, crested stamens. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, preferring sunny exposures. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Adonis cristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
