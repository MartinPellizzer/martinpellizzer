Agropyron caninum var. gracile

Agropyron caninum var. gracile, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for erosion control and livestock forage.
