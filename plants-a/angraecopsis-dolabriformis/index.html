Angraecopsis Dolabriformis

Angraecopsis dolabriformis is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family known for its unique and intricate floral structure. Native to Madagascar, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments with high levels of rainfall. Its elongated, curved petals resemble a dagger, giving it its scientific name "dolabriformis," which means sword-shaped. The plant is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists for its beauty and rarity. Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is now considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
