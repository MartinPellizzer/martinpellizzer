Artemisia caucasica

Artemisia caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains volatile oils and flavonoids, contributing to its medicinal and aromatic properties. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or traditional herbal use.
