Areca rheophytica

Areca rheophytica, commonly known as the water areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in mountainous regions. Its fibrous leaves are used in traditional crafts, and the palm produces small, clustered flowers that develop into edible fruits.

Areca rheophytica is valued for both its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
