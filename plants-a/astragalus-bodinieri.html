Astragalus bodinieri

Astragalus bodinieri, commonly known as the Bodinier milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus bodinieri is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
