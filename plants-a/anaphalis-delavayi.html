Anaphalis delavayi

Anaphalis delavayi, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a silvery appearance, resembling a cloud or a puffball. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Anaphalis delavayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and visual interest.
