Anthericum limaense

Anthericum limaense, commonly known as the Lima arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a glossy texture. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warmer months, producing small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and exotic appearance.
