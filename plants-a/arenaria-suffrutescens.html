Arenaria suffrutescens

Arenaria suffrutescens, commonly known as sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically features succulent, fleshy leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or coastal areas. Its ability to tolerate dry conditions makes it well adapted to harsh environments.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and easy care requirements.
