Allium flavum var. sordideroseum

Allium flavum var. sordideroseum, commonly known as the purple garlic or sordid rose garlic, is a botanical variant of the garlic plant.

It is characterized by its purple-tinged leaves and bulbous stems that produce small, pale yellow flowers. This variety is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other Allium species, particularly in its floral and foliar coloration.
