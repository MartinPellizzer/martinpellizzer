Australina caffra

Australina caffra, commonly known as the Natal grass, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses.

Australina caffra is often used in ornamental gardening for its fine texture and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
