Agoseris altissima

Agoseris altissima, commonly known as the tall goldenroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, branching stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Agoseris altissima is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in meadows and open woodlands.
