Alstroemeria affinis

Alstroemeria affinis, commonly known as the red alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with strap-like leaves and showy inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in various parts of the world for both horticultural and commercial purposes.
