Allium tubergenii

Allium tubergenii, commonly known as the Tuberous Leek, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of white or pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, edible bulbs that are often used in traditional cuisine. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
