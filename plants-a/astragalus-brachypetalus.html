Astragalus brachypetalus

Astragalus brachypetalus, commonly known as the short-petaled milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish to pale green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

Astragalus brachypetalus is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role and its potential medicinal properties.
