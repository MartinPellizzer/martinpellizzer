Acetosa alpestris subsp. islandica

Acetosa alpestris subsp.

islandica, commonly known as the Island Chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous environments. It has a mat-forming growth habit, which helps it survive in harsh, cold climates. Acetosa alpestris subsp.

islandica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
