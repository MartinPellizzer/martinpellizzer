Athyrium lobatocrenatum

Athyrium lobatocrenatum, commonly known as the broad-lobed wood fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that often have a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often covered in a thick, fibrous sheath, and it produces small, greenish spore-bearing structures called sorus.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
