Anaxagorea angustifolia

Anaxagorea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed evergreen tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by round, fleshy fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ornamental contexts.
