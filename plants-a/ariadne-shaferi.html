Ariadne shaferi

Ariadne shaferi, commonly known as the desert lily, is a striking flowering plant native to arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to two meters in height, supporting large, showy white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. Its blooming period typically occurs during the cooler months, making it a rare sight in its natural habitat.

The plant is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
