Ardisia polylepis

Ardisia polylepis, commonly known as the purple-leafed ardisia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that turn purplish-red in the autumn, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in subtropical and tropical regions.
