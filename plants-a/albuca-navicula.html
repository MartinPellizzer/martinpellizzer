Albuca navicula

Albuca navicula, commonly known as the waterleaf or narrow-leaf albuca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a dense, cylindrical inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Albuca navicula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens with suitable conditions.
