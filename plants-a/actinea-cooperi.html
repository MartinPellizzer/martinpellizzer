Actinea cooperi

Actinea cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink, petal-like blooms that surround a cluster of yellow stamens. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It typically grows in moist, woodland environments and thrives in partial to full shade.

Actinea cooperi is valued for its early spring bloom and its ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
