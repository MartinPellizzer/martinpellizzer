Allium phthioticum

Allium phthioticum, commonly known as the Cretan leek, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are similar in taste to garlic and onions. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
