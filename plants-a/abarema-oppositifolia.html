Abarema oppositifolia

Abarema oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved shrub, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its opposite arrangement of leaves, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by legume-like pods containing seeds.

It is often found in humid, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.
