Alphonsea longicarpa

Alphonsea longicarpa, commonly known as the long-fruited alphonsea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves that are opposite and pinnate. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by long, narrow berries that are edible. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Alphonsea longicarpa is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in some regions.
