Arinemia lanceolata

Arinemia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf arinemia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often pollinated by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Arinemia lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
