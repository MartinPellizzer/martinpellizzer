Alyssum potemkinii

Alyssum potemkinii, commonly known as the Armenian alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has silvery-green, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum potemkinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
