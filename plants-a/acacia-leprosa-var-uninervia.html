Acacia leprosa var. uninervia

Acacia leprosa var. uninervia, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, compound leaves with a single vein, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in horticulture for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
