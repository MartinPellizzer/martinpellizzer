Astragalus aureus var. bracteolatus

Astragalus aureus var. bracteolatus, commonly known as golden milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, branched stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
