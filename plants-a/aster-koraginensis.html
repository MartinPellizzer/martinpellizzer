Aster koraginensis

Aster koraginensis, commonly known as the Korean aster, is a flowering plant native to Korea and parts of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its showy purple to pink flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster koraginensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
