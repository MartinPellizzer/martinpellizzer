Asteranthe

Asteranthe, commonly known as the "blue bell" or "blue aster," is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its showy, blue or violet-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact, bushy habit. Asteranthe species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens due to their vibrant colors and long-lasting blooms.

They prefer well-drained soil and full sun, making them suitable for rock gardens and borders.
