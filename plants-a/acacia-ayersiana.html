Acacia ayersiana

Acacia ayersiana, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, feathery leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which appear in spring and attract a variety of pollinators. Its seeds are encased in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Acacia ayersiana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
