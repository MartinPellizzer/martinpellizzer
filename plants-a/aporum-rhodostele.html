Aporum rhodostele

Aporum rhodostele, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that often display vibrant red or pink hues. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a dense cluster of flowers at the top, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are elongated and slightly waxy, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists alike.
