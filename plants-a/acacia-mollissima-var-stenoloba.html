Acacia mollissima var. stenoloba

Acacia mollissima var. stenoloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and compound leaves composed of numerous small, narrow leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and floral display.
