Aster araucanus

Aster araucanus, commonly known as the Andean aster, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from white to purple.

Aster araucanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
