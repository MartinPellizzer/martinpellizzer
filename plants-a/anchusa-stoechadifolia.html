Anchusa stoechadifolia

Anchusa stoechadifolia, commonly known as the Scottish bugloss, is a perennial herb native to the British Isles and parts of northern Europe.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and lance-shaped leaves, which are typically hairy. The plant grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It belongs to the Boraginaceae family, which is known for its medicinal and ornamental plants.
