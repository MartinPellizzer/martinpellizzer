Anthericum involucratum

Anthericum involucratum, commonly known as the purple nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which are surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it identifiable by its distinctive leaf structure and flowering pattern.
