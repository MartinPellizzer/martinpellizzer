Asarum pulchellum

Asarum pulchellum, commonly known as the showy wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive, spicy aroma. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Asarum pulchellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
