Ageratina mariarum

Ageratina mariarum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, fluffy blue or purple flower heads that resemble mist, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ageratina mariarum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its unique visual appeal.
