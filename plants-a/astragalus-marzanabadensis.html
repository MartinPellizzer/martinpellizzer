Astragalus marzanabadensis

Astragalus marzanabadensis, commonly known as the Marzanabad milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to specific regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
