Alchemilla cyrtopleura

Alchemilla cyrtopleura, commonly known as the woolly alchemilla, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of soft, hairy, evergreen leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
