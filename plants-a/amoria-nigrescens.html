Amoria nigrescens

Amoria nigrescens, commonly known as the black-flowered orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black petals and sepals, which contrast with its pale green to yellowish leaf blades. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in late summer to early autumn.

Amoria nigrescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique coloration.
