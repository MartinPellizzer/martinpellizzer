Actinostemon communis var. cordatus

Actinostemon communis var. cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf actinostemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and stems that are upright and often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in the spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
