Aristida personata

Aristida personata, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

Aristida personata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stability.
