Astragalus palaestinus var. hierosolymitanus

Astragalus palaestinus var. hierosolymitanus, commonly known as the Holy Land milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of the Mediterranean.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
