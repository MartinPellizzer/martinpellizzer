Arundarbor paniculata

Arundarbor paniculata, commonly known as the golden paniculate, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, branched inflorescences that produce clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss.

Arundarbor paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
