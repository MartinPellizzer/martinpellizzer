Anodiscus peruvianus

Anodiscus peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss.

Anodiscus peruvianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
