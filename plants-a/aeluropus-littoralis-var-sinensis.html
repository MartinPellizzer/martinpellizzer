Aeluropus littoralis var. sinensis

Aeluropus littoralis var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese saltgrass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions of China.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in wetlands, tidal flats, and coastal dunes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in summer. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and preventing soil erosion.

Its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions makes it an important species for ecological restoration projects.
