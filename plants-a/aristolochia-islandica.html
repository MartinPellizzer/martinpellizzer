Aristolochia islandica

Aristolochia islandica, commonly known as the Bahamian Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, and often twisted leaves that resemble a pipe. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically purple or greenish in color. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed soils.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
