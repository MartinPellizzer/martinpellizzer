Astragalus choicus

Astragalus choicus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus choicus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
