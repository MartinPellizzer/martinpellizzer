Acaena pinnatifida subsp. pliacantha

Acaena pinnatifida subsp.

pliacantha, commonly known as the New Zealand thistle, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its spiny, bract-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is part of the Rosaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
