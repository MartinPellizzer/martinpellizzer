Aleuritia spathulifolia

Aleuritia spathulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed elder, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has simple, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Aleuritia spathulifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
