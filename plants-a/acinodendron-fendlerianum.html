Acinodendron fendlerianum

Acinodendron fendlerianum, commonly known as the desert acacia, is a shrub native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal rainfall.

Its ecological role includes providing shelter and food for various desert animals.
