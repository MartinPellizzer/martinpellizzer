Artemisia smithii

Artemisia smithii, commonly known as silver sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Artemisia smithii is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
