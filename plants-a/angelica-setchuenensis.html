Angelica setchuenensis

Angelica setchuenensis, commonly known as Chinese angelica or Dahuang, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

Angelica setchuenensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
