Alchemilla vulgaris var. inconcinna

Alchemilla vulgaris var. inconcinna, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a low-growing rosette of basal leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in loose clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and wetland restoration projects.
