Amomum purpureum

Amomum purpureum, commonly known as purple cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its tall, aromatic rhizomes. The plant produces clusters of small, purple flowers that eventually develop into three-lobed berries. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a fragrant, slightly spicy aroma.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
