Abies pindrow var. intermedia

Abies pindrow var. intermedia, commonly known as the Kashmir spruce, is a variant of the Pindrow fir native to the Himalayas.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its pyramidal shape and dense, dark green foliage. The needles are typically short and stiff, with a slightly resinous scent. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family.

This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in montane forests at high elevations.
