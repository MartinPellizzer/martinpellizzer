Aster fragilis var. subinteger

Aster fragilis var. subinteger, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aster, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
