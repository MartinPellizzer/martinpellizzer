Anthemis persica

Anthemis persica, commonly known as Persian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the regions of Persia and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in the summer months and are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
