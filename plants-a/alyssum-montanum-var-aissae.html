Alyssum montanum var. aissae

Alyssum montanum var. aissae, commonly known as the Sierra alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are wiry and sturdy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
