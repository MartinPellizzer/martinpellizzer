Aeschynomene glaberrima

Aeschynomene glaberrima, commonly known as the African burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain small, rounded seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fallow fields.

Aeschynomene glaberrima is also valued for its potential in soil conservation and as a forage plant for livestock.
