Aloe luntii

Aloe luntii, commonly known as the Lunt's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in late winter or early spring. Aloe luntii is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive in dry conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
