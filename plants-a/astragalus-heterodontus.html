Astragalus heterodontus

Astragalus heterodontus, commonly known as the two-toothed milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces distinctive pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland ecosystems.

Astragalus heterodontus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
