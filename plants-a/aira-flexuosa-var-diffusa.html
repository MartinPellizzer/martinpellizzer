Aira flexuosa var. diffusa

Aira flexuosa var. diffusa, commonly known as the diffuse bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, wiry stems and forms dense, low-growing clumps. Its leaves are narrow, blue-green in color, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
