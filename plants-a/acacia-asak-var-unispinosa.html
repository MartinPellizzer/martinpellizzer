Acacia asak var. unispinosa

Acacia asak var. unispinosa, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, sparsely branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow to golden-green globular flowers arranged in compact, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to humid, warm climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique foliage and floral display.
