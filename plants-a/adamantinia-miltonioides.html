Adamantinia miltonioides

Adamantinia miltonioides, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink in color. It produces small, hard pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in furniture-making, it is considered a valuable and threatened species.
