Aesculus pallida

Aesculus pallida, commonly known as the white horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white, pendulous flowers that appear in spring, followed by spiny, velvety fruits called burr fruits. The leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are oval and pointed. The tree has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively disease-resistant.
