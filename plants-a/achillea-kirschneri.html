Achillea kirschneri

Achillea kirschneri, commonly known as the Kirschner's yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal rosettes of silver-gray, finely divided leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in the summer. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.

Achillea kirschneri is also noted for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
