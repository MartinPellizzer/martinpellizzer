Aquilegia vulgaris var. ballii

Aquilegia vulgaris var. ballii, commonly known as the Ball's columbine, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
