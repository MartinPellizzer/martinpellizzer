Anthyllis flava

Anthyllis flava, commonly known as yellow anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Anthyllis flava is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
