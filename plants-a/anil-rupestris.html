Anil rupestris

Anil rupestris, commonly known as the rock anil, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent growth habit and produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are typically fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps reduce water loss.

Anil rupestris is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and hardiness.
