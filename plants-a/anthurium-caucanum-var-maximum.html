Anthurium caucanum var. maximum

Anthurium caucanum var. maximum, commonly known as the giant anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a deep green color and a glossy texture. The plant produces vibrant red spadices surrounded by colorful bracts, which are often mistaken for flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular in horticulture but is also considered an invasive species in some regions.
