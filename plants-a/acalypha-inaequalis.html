Acalypha inaequalis

Acalypha inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal leaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, irregularly shaped leaves that often have a striking reddish or purple hue. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender, branching stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Acalypha inaequalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
