Acer changhuaense var. trilobum

Acer changhuaense var. trilobum, commonly known as the Taiwan maple, is a deciduous tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are deeply lobed and resemble three maple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that disperse by wind. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming more fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
