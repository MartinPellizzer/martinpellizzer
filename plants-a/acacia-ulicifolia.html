Acacia ulicifolia

Acacia ulicifolia, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Acacia ulicifolia is often used in reforestation and erosion control due to its hardy nature and ability to fix nitrogen.
