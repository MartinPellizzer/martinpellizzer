Apicra viscosa

Apicra viscosa, commonly known as the sticky apicra, is a unique plant characterized by its viscous, adhesive secretions.

It belongs to the family Apicraceae and is native to specific regions in South America. The plant's most notable botanical feature is its glandular leaves, which produce a sticky substance used for trapping small insects. This adaptation aids in its survival by providing a source of nutrients.

Its structure and secretion make it an intriguing subject for botanical study.
