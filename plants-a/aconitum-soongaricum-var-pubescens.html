Aconitum soongaricum var. pubescens

Aconitum soongaricum var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in tall, spires, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a hairy texture, which is characteristic of its varietal name "pubescens.". It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
