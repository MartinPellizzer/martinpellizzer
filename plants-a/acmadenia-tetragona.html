Acmadenia tetragona

Acmadenia tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled acmadenia, is a small shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from September to November. Its fruits are small, round, and dry, containing several seeds.

Acmadenia tetragona is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.
