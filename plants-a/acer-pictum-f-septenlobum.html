Acer pictum f. septenlobum

Acer pictum f. septenlobum, commonly known as the seven-lobed maple, is a variant of the sugar maple with distinctive leaf morphology.

Its leaves are palmately lobed, typically with seven distinct lobes, giving it a unique appearance. The plant is native to eastern North America, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by samaras that disperse its seeds.

This ornamental tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability in various garden settings.
