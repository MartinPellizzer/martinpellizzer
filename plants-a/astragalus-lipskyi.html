Astragalus lipskyi

Astragalus lipskyi, commonly known as the Lipsky's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces pods that contain small, hard seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus lipskyi is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
