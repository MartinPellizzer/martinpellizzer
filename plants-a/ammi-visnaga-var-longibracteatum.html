Ammi visnaga var. longibracteatum

Ammi visnaga var. longibracteatum, commonly known as the greater hogweed, is a biennial or perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features large, umbrella-like inflorescences composed of small white or greenish flowers, which are arranged in a compound umbel. The plant has smooth, hairy, or glandular stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their medicinal properties.

The plant contains phototoxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to skin when exposed to sunlight.
