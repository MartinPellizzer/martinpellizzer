Allium hirsutum

Allium hirsutum, commonly known as the woolly leek, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its long, slender leaves and umbel-like clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in culinary applications. Native to southern Europe and western Asia, it thrives in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and edible, with a mild garlic-like flavor.
