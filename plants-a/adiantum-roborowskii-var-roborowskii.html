Adiantum roborowskii var. roborowskii

Adiantum roborowskii var. roborowskii, commonly known as the Roborowsky's maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features finely divided, delicate fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant produces small, spore-bearing sori on the underside of its fronds, a characteristic feature of ferns. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.
