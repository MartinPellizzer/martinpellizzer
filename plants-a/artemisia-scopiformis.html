Artemisia scopiformis

Artemisia scopiformis, commonly known as silver wormwood, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and narrow, thread-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters that bloom in the summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Artemisia scopiformis is drought-tolerant and has been used traditionally for medicinal purposes.
