Arundinaria naibunensis

Arundinaria naibunensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Arundinaria naibunensis is also noted for its resilience and adaptability to various soil conditions.
