Aconitum napellus nothosubsp. seitzii

Aconitum napellus nothosubsp.

seitzii, commonly known as the Seitz's monkshood, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Aconitum napellus and Aconitum lycoctonum. It is native to the Alpine regions of Central Europe, where it thrives in moist, calcareous soils.

The plant is notable for its striking blue to purple flowers, which are arranged in a distinctive helmet-shaped hood. It is a herbaceous perennial with tall, sparsely branched stems and palmately compound leaves.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
