Aesculus castanea

Aesculus castanea, commonly known as the horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers that appear in clusters. The tree produces spiny, capsule-like fruits containing shiny, chestnut-like seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is found in parks and gardens across Europe and North America.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
