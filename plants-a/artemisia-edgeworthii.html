Artemisia edgeworthii

Artemisia edgeworthii, commonly known as blue mist shrub, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, pale blue or grayish-white flowers. The plant is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its strong, pungent scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

Artemisia edgeworthii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
