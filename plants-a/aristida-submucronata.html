Aristida submucronata

Aristida submucronata, commonly known as the narrowleaf threeawn, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, each bearing a single flowering lemma.

The species is characterized by its small, wind-pollinated flowers and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
