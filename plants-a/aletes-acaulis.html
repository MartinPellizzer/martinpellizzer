Aletes acaulis

Aletes acaulis, commonly known as the barren aletes, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and lack of stems, with all growth concentrated at the base. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple and pink, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve moisture in its arid habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, Aletes acaulis is considered a vulnerable species.
