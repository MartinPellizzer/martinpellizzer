Aeschynomene falcata var. microphylla

Aeschynomene falcata var. microphylla, commonly known as small-leaved goatherb, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features slender, branched stems and small, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

This species is valued for its use in forage and as a cover crop due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
