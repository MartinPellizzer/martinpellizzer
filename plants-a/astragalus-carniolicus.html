Astragalus carniolicus

Astragalus carniolicus, commonly known as the Carniolan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to alpine and subalpine environments.

Astragalus carniolicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
