Atriplex semibaccata var. microcarpa

Atriplex semibaccata var. microcarpa, commonly known as saltbush or shadscale, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it an important species in stabilizing desert ecosystems.

Its robust growth and drought tolerance make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
