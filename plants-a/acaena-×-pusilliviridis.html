Acaena × pusilliviridis

Acaena × pusilliviridis, commonly known as the New Zealand spinach, is a hybrid plant belonging to the genus Acaena in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its small, spiny fruits and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the genus. The plant is native to New Zealand and is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which are similar in taste to spinach. It thrives in temperate climates and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible qualities, it is a popular choice for both gardeners and foragers.
