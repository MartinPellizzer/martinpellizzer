Althaea ludwigii var. microcalyculata

Althaea ludwigii var. microcalyculata, commonly known as the small-calyx marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features hairy, heart-shaped leaves and produces pale pink to white flowers with five to seven petals. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.

The plant's roots and leaves have been used to treat respiratory and digestive ailments.
