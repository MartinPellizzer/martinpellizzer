Aster calendulifolius

Aster calendulifolius, commonly known as the yellow aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of smaller florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Aster calendulifolius is typically found in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils.
