Aeschynomene americana var. longifolia

Aeschynomene americana var. longifolia, commonly known as partridge pea, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It thrives in various habitats, including roadsides and disturbed areas, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its seeds are edible and have been used by indigenous peoples for food and medicinal purposes.
