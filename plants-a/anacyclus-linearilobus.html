Anacyclus linearilobus

Anacyclus linearilobus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy basal rosette. The plant features linear to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges, and its yellow flowers are typically arranged in a single head. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Anacyclus linearilobus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
