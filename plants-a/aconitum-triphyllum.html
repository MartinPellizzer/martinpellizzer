Aconitum triphyllum

Aconitum triphyllum, commonly known as the three-leafed monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, which are typically surrounded by two large, showy petals. The plant has dark green, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Aconitum triphyllum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
