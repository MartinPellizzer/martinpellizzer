Arundinella longispicata

Arundinella longispicata, commonly known as the long-spined reed, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Due to its robust growth and adaptability, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
