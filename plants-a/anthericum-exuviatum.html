Anthericum exuviatum

Anthericum exuviatum, commonly known as the slender false lily of the valley, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its clustered, lance-shaped leaves and small, white, spadix-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

Anthericum exuviatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowering habit.
