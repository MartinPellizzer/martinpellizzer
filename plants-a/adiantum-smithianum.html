Adiantum smithianum

Adiantum smithianum, commonly known as the Smith's maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern native to the eastern United States.

It features finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bright green and have a graceful, arching appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Adiantum smithianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a home setting.
