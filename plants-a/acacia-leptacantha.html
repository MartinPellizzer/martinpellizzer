Acacia leptacantha

Acacia leptacantha, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense, branched inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Acacia leptacantha is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native environment.
