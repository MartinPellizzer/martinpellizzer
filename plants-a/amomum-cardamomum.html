Amomum cardamomum

Amomum cardamomum, commonly known as black cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown, and woody pods that contain highly fragrant seeds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The dried fruit is widely used in traditional medicine and as a spice in various cuisines.
