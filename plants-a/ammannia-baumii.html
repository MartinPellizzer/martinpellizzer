Ammannia baumii

Ammannia baumii, commonly known as the redroot dayflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Ammannia baumii is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant color and adaptability to various soil types.
