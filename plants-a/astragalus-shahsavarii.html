Astragalus shahsavarii

Astragalus shahsavarii, commonly known as Shahsavari milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus shahsavarii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
