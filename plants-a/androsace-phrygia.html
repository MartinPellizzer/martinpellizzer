Androsace phrygia

Androsace phrygia, commonly known as the Phrygian bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, pink or white flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, alpine environments. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Androsace phrygia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
