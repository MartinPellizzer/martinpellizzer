Acaena macrostemon

Acaena macrostemon, commonly known as the New Zealand burr bush, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its fruits are encased in a prickly, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release the seeds.

Acaena macrostemon is often found in coastal and subalpine regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
