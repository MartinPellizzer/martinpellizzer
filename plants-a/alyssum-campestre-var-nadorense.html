Alyssum campestre var. nadorense

Alyssum campestre var. nadorense, commonly known as the Nadorenskaya alyssum, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage is typically gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
