Angraecoides scalariformis

Angraecoides scalariformis, commonly known as the scalloped angraecoid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the high-altitude regions of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, scalloped-shaped leaves and intricate, fragrant flowers that bloom at night. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is adapted to the humid, cool climate of its native habitat. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with long, slender petals and a distinctive lip.

Due to its rarity and specific environmental requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
