Aceras caprinum

Aceras caprinum, commonly known as the goat's bean, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which have been used historically as a food source. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal practices.
