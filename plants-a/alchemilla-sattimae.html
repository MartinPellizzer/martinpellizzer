Alchemilla sattimae

Alchemilla sattimae, commonly known as the Sattima lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Alchemilla sattimae is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
