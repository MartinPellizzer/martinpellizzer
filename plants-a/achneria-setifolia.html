Achneria setifolia

Achneria setifolia, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, pale yellow to white flowers with five pointed lobes. The plant features soft, hairy leaves that are often glandular and emit a mild fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources.

Achneria setifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
