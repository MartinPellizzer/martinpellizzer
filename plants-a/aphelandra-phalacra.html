Aphelandra phalacra

Aphelandra phalacra, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, commonly known as the palm family, and is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and long-lasting blooms, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.

Aphelandra phalacra thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.
