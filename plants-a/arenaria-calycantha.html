Arenaria calycantha

Arenaria calycantha, commonly known as the Calycanthus, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its showy blossoms.

Arenaria calycantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique fragrance and beauty.
