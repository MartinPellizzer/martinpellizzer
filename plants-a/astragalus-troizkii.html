Astragalus troizkii

Astragalus troizkii, commonly known as the Troizkii milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces pods that contain small, round seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus troizkii is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role in its native habitat.
