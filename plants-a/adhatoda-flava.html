Adhatoda flava

Adhatoda flava, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a perennial shrub native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pale blue or violet flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shady environments such as forests and riverbanks. It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is known for its aromatic foliage.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
