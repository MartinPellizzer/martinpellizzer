Amomum harmandii

Amomum harmandii, commonly known as the Guangxi cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, tubular flowers and narrow, sword-shaped leaves. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
