Acer palmatum var. latilobum

Acer palmatum var. latilobum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with broad lobes, is a variant of the Japanese maple characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

It features finely dissected, palmately lobed leaves that are typically a vibrant green during the growing season and transform into shades of red, orange, and yellow in autumn. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for smaller spaces and container gardening.
