Asclepias campestris

Asclepias campestris, commonly known as milkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are pollinated by insects. It is a vital host plant for monarch butterflies, providing food for their larvae.

Asclepias campestris also contains a milky sap that is toxic to some animals.
