Allium baytopiorum

Allium baytopiorum, commonly known as the Baytopi onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are similar in taste to garlic and onions, making it a potential candidate for culinary use. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
