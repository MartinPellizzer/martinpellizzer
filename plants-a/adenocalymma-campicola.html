Adenocalymma campicola

Adenocalymma campicola, commonly known as the rock lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and small, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Adenocalymma campicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
