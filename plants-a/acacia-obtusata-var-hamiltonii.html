Acacia obtusata var. hamiltonii

Acacia obtusata var. hamiltonii, commonly known as the Hamilton's wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
