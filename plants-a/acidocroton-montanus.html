Acidocroton montanus

Acidocroton montanus, commonly known as the mountain croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its diverse species with often milky sap. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in inflorescences. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage, which can range from green to reddish hues.

The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in tropical gardens and as a houseplant.
