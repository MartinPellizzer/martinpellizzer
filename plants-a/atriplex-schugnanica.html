Atriplex schugnanica

Atriplex schugnanica, commonly known as Schugnan saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fleshy leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable species for stabilizing degraded lands.

Atriplex schugnanica is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
