Aegilops squarrosa subvar. truncata

Aegilops squarrosa subvar. truncata, commonly known as the truncated wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that are typically pink to red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Aegilops squarrosa subvar. truncata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential role in ecological restoration projects.
