Anthemis macedonica

Anthemis macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its essential oils, extracted from the flowers, are used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and calming properties.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential pharmacological applications.
