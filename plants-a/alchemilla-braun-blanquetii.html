Alchemilla braun-blanquetii

Alchemilla braun-blanquetii, commonly known as the Braun-Blanquet's lady's mantle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves that form a dense mat. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in branched inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Alchemilla braun-blanquetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
