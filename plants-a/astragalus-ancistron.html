Astragalus ancistron

Astragalus ancistron, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus ancistron is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for native wildlife.
