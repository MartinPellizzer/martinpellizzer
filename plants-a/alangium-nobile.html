Alangium nobile

Alangium nobile, commonly known as the noble alangium, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Berberidaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
