Astragalus discolor

Astragalus discolor, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Astragalus discolor has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
