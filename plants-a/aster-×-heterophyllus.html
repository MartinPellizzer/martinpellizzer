Aster × heterophyllus

Aster × heterophyllus, commonly known as the New Zealand aster, is a hybrid plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue to violet flowers and hairy, often glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or rocky areas. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is primarily found in its natural habitat.
