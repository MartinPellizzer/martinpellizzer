Arischrada bucharica

Arischrada bucharica, commonly known as the Bukharan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to the flora of its native region.
