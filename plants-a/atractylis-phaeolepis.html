Atractylis phaeolepis

Atractylis phaeolepis, commonly known as the purple false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with a purple center.
