Apargia lyrata

Apargia lyrata, commonly known as the lyreleaf twayblade, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive lyre-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Apargia lyrata has small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring.

Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, making it a resilient species in its habitat.
