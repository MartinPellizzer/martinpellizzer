Aegilops squarrosa subsp. eusquarrosa

Aegilops squarrosa subsp.

eusquarrosa, commonly known as the woolly wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive, hairy leaves that give it a woolly appearance.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
