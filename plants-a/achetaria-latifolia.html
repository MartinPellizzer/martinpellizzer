Achetaria latifolia

Achetaria latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf achetaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Achetaria latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral displays.
