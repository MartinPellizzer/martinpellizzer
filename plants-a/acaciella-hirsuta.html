Acaciella hirsuta

Acaciella hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy acacia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, compound leaves that are covered in fine, hairy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

Acaciella hirsuta thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, open habitats.
