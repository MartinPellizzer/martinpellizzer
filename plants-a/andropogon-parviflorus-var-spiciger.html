Andropogon parviflorus var. spiciger

Andropogon parviflorus var. spiciger, commonly known as the slender bluemont grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a clumping growth habit.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and adaptability to waterlogged soils.
