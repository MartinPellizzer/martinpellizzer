Astragalus lagobromus

Astragalus lagobromus, commonly known as the Sierra grassland milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus lagobromus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native insects.
