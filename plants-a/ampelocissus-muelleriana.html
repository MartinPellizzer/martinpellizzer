Ampelocissus muelleriana

Ampelocissus muelleriana, commonly known as the climbing fig, is a woody vine native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and dark green coloration. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to yellow when mature.

Ampelocissus muelleriana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to climb structures.
