Ascolepis protea var. ochracea

Ascolepis protea var. ochracea, commonly known as the Orange Protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of small, tubular flowers, often displaying a vibrant orange or reddish hue. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its unique floral structure attracts a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
