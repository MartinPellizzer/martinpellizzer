Astragalus alberti

Astragalus alberti, commonly known as the Albert's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor environments.

Astragalus alberti is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
