Aphelandra lutea var. angustifolia

Aphelandra lutea var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces striking, pendulous inflorescences with bright yellow bracts that resemble flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Aphelandra lutea var. angustifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
