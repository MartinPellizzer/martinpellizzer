Acacia microacantha

Acacia microacantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow, spherical flower heads that appear in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its hardy nature makes it adaptable to a range of soil types and climates.

Acacia microacantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in arid environments.
