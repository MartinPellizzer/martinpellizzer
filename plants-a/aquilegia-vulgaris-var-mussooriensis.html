Aquilegia vulgaris var. mussooriensis

Aquilegia vulgaris var. mussooriensis, commonly known as the Himalayan garden bell, is a variant of the common columbine that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It features striking, spurred petals that range in color from deep crimson to violet, enhancing its ornamental appeal. The plant produces compound leaves with finely divided lobes, typical of the Aquilegia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is native to the Himalayas and is valued for its unique floral structure and adaptability to temperate climates.
