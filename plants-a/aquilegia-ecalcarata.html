Aquilegia ecalcarata

Aquilegia ecalcarata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking spurred petals that are typically purple or blue, with a distinctive hooded shape. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.
