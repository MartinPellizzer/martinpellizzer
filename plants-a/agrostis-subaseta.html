Agrostis subaseta

Agrostis subaseta, commonly known as slender bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, oval-shaped flower clusters.

Agrostis subaseta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in low-maintenance landscaping.
