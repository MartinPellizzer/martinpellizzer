Amaralia amaraliocarpa

Amaralia amaraliocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited amaralia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red berries that persist on the plant throughout the winter, making it a visually appealing ornamental species. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated edge.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
