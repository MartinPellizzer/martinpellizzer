Arctostaphylos arbutoides

Arctostaphylos arbutoides, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and evergreen, leathery leaves that remain on the plant year-round. The plant produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

Its unique appearance and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
