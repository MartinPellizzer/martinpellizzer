Athyrium verapax

Athyrium verapax, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its fronds are usually a deep green with a metallic sheen, and the sori are clustered in linear rows on the underside of the leaves.

Athyrium verapax is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
