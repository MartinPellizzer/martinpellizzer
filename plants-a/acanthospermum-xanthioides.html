Acanthospermum xanthioides

Acanthospermum xanthioides, commonly known as yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous roots. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats. Its yellow flowers are small and clustered, forming a distinctive crown-like appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
