Acaciella stipellata

Acaciella stipellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in its native habitat.
