Allium laetum

Allium laetum, commonly known as the garlic chive or wild garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbils along its stems, which can be used for propagation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Allium laetum is valued for its mild garlic flavor and is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
