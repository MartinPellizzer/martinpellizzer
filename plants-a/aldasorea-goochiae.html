Aldasorea goochiae

Aldasorea goochiae, commonly known as the Gooch's alder, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family, which includes birches, hazels, and alders. The tree is characterized by its slender, upright form and small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It produces catkins, which are cylindrical flower clusters, typical of the genus.
