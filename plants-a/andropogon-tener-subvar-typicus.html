Andropogon tener subvar. typicus

Andropogon tener subvar. typicus, commonly known as slender bluestem, is a grass species native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed areas. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.

Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to blue, adding a subtle aesthetic to the landscape.
