Andropogon nardus var. luridus

Andropogon nardus var. luridus, commonly known as the golden bluestem, is a variant of the bluestem grass family native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and golden tawny hue during the winter months. This grass thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairie and woodland edges. Its growth habit is clump-forming, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for wildlife.
