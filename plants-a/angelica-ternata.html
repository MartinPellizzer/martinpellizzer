Angelica ternata

Angelica ternata, commonly known as Japanese angelica, is a biennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, compound leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its root is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Angelica ternata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.
