Arachis prostrata var. villosa

Arachis prostrata var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy peanut vine, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its sprawling habit and hairy stems, which give it its varietal name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like structures containing edible seeds. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and a robust root system that helps it thrive in various soil types.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible legumes.
