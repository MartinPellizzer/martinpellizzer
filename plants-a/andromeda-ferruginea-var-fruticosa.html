Andromeda ferruginea var. fruticosa

Andromeda ferruginea var. fruticosa, commonly known as the red alder, is a shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and riparian habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
