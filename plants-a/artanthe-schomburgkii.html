Artanthe schomburgkii

Artanthe schomburgkii, commonly known as Schomburgk's artanthe, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of small, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Artanthe schomburgkii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
