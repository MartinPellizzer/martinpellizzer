Astragalus malatyaensis

Astragalus malatyaensis, commonly known as Malatya milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers, which is a common trait among desert plants.

Astragalus malatyaensis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
