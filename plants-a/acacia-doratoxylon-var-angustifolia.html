Acacia doratoxylon var. angustifolia

Acacia doratoxylon var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and yellow floral spikes. The plant is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils in arid and semi-arid regions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soils.
