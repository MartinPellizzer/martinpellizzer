Achillea barrelieri subsp. barrelieri

Achillea barrelieri subsp.

barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Alps and the Carpathian Mountains. It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is adapted to grow in well-drained, rocky soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
