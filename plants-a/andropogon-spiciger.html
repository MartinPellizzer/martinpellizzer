Andropogon spiciger

Andropogon spiciger, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, blue-gray flowers that bloom in late summer.

Andropogon spiciger plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
