Acacia oxycedrus

Acacia oxycedrus, commonly known as the Mediterranean acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned branches and bipinnate leaves that are fragrant and finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which are followed by small, woody pods containing seeds. Acacia oxycedrus is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

It has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a source of tannins.
