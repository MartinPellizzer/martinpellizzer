Aechmea aciculosa

Aechmea aciculosa, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage composed of long, spiky, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant's inflorescence consists of a tall, branched stem bearing striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

The plant's unique structure and vibrant floral display contribute to its ornamental value.
