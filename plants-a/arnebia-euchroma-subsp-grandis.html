Arnebia euchroma subsp. grandis

Arnebia euchroma subsp.

grandis, commonly known as the Kashmir rose or Himalayan rose, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, pink to red flowers.

The plant produces distinctive, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal medicine.
