Agrostis dura

Agrostis dura, commonly known as hard brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It typically grows in open areas such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, often forming dense stands. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

Agrostis dura is known for its adaptability to various soil conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
