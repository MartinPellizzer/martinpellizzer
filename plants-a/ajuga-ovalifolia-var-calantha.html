Ajuga ovalifolia var. calantha

Ajuga ovalifolia var. calantha, commonly known as the purple false spurge, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple on the underside. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in early spring. Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a popular ground cover in shaded gardens.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
