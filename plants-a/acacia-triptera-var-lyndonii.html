Acacia triptera var. lyndonii

Acacia triptera var. lyndonii, commonly known as the Lyndon's wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
