Anethum foeniculum var. piperitum

Anethum foeniculum var. piperitum, commonly known as sweet fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features feathery, aromatic leaves and produces yellow flowers in umbels. The plant is characterized by its hollow, grooved stems and a strong anise-like fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in dry, rocky habitats.

Its seeds are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their distinctive flavor and essential oils.
