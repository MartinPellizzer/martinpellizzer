Arisaema niveum var. viridescens

Arisaema niveum var. viridescens, commonly known as the Green-veined Snake-root, is a rare and striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China, where it thrives in cool, humid environments. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves and a spadix surrounded by a white or pale green spathe. Its foliage often displays a unique greenish hue, giving it its common name.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by botanical enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
