Amaranthus anderssonii f. typicus

Amaranthus anderssonii f. typicus, commonly known as the Andersson's amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant red to purple bracts that surround the small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and ability to attract pollinators.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
