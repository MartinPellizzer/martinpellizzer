Agave washingtonensis

Agave washingtonensis, commonly known as the Washington agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which appear after the plant has matured. Agave washingtonensis is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and resilience.
