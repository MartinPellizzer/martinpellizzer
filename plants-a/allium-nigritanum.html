Allium nigritanum

Allium nigritanum, commonly known as the black garlic plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its striking, dark purple to black bulbs that resemble garlic in appearance. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
