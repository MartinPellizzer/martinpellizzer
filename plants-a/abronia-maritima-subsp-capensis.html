Abronia maritima subsp. capensis

Abronia maritima subsp.

capensis, commonly known as the Cape sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa. It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that thrives in sandy, well-drained soils.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom during the cooler months. It is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing dunes.
