Amellus lychnitis

Amellus lychnitis, commonly known as the common evening-primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its yellow, tubular flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Amellus lychnitis thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.
