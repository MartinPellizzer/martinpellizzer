Arnoglossum lanceolatum var. trinervium

Arnoglossum lanceolatum var. trinervium, commonly known as the three-veined lanceleaf goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with three distinct veins, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
