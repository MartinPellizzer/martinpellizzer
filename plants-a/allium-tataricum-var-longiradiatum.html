Allium tataricum var. longiradiatum

Allium tataricum var. longiradiatum, commonly known as the Siberian leek or long-leaved garlic, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its long, cylindrical bulbs and strap-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a distinct garlic-like aroma.

It is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal properties.
