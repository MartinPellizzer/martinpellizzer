Allium phrygium

Allium phrygium, commonly known as the Phrygian garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks, and is characterized by its bulbous structure and grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a useful plant in culinary applications.

Allium phrygium thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.
