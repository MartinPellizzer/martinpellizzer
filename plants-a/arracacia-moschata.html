Arracacia moschata

Arracacia moschata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the nightshade family and is characterized by its aromatic, red-orange fruit that resembles a small pepper. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and showy flowers. Its fruit contains a high concentration of essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions, posing ecological challenges.
