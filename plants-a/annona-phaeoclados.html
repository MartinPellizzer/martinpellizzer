Annona phaeoclados

Annona phaeoclados, commonly known as the black custard apple, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellow flowers. The fruit is typically oblong, with a rough, dark purple to black skin and a creamy, aromatic pulp. It produces small, round seeds embedded in the flesh, and the plant is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
