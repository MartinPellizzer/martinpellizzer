Asplenium blanchetii

Asplenium blanchetii, commonly known as the Blanquet's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced in clusters on the undersides of its leaves, and it reproduces through spore dispersal rather than seeds.

Asplenium blanchetii is a relatively rare species, making it of interest to botanists and conservationists.
