Anemonastrum calvum

Anemonastrum calvum, commonly known as the smooth sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant has a low-growing, mat-forming habit with hairy stems and narrow, gray-green leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, blooming in spring and early summer.

Anemonastrum calvum is adapted to withstand drought and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
