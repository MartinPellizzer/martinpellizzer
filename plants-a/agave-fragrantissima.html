Agave fragrantissima

Agave fragrantissima, commonly known as the fragrant agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped stems and thick, fleshy leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, white to pale green flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Agave fragrantissima is also noted for its pleasant, sweet scent, especially when the leaves are crushed.
