Artemisia longiflora

Artemisia longiflora, commonly known as western golden sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush habitats. It has a long history of use by Indigenous peoples for medicinal and ceremonial purposes.

Its essential oils contain compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
