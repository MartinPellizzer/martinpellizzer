Avenella flexuosa subsp. montana

Avenella flexuosa subsp.

montana, commonly known as mountain avenella, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It typically grows in rocky soils and high elevation environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant has slender, arching stems that often form dense mats, and its leaves are narrow, hairy, and slightly curled. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by small, spherical fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in alpine and subalpine habitats.
