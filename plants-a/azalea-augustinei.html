Azalea augustinei

Azalea augustinei, commonly known as the Augustine azalea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of 3 to 6 feet, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
