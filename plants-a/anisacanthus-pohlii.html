Anisacanthus pohlii

Anisacanthus pohlii, commonly known as the Texas starburst, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features bright orange to yellow tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has woody stems and thick, leathery leaves that are adapted to arid conditions. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract hummingbirds and other pollinators, adding ecological value to the landscape.
