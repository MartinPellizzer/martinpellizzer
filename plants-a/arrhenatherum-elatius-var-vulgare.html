Arrhenatherum elatius var. vulgare

Arrhenatherum elatius var. vulgare, commonly known as the meadow foxtail, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, pastures, and damp woodland edges. The plant features slender, cylindrical spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing delicate, wind-pollinated flowers.
