Anthericum apodastanthum

Anthericum apodastanthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ladies' tresses, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a dense, spike-like inflorescence with small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It is characterized by its lack of a visible stem, with the flowers emerging directly from the base of the leaves.

Anthericum apodastanthum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
