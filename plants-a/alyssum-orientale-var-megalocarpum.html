Alyssum orientale var. megalocarpum

Alyssum orientale var. megalocarpum, commonly known as Persian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pink flowers. The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The variety megalocarpum is distinguished by its larger, more showy seed pods compared to other forms of Alyssum orientale.
