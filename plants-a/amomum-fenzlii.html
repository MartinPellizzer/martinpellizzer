Amomum fenzlii

Amomum fenzlii, commonly known as the Sichuan cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, tubular flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive fragrance. Its rhizomes are used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal and aromatic value.
