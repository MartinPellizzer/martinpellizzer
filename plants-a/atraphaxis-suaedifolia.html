Atraphaxis suaedifolia

Atraphaxis suaedifolia, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It has small, gray-green, fleshy leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous roots help stabilize sandy soils, making it an important species for desert ecosystems.

Atraphaxis suaedifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
