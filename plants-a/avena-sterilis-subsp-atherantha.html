Avena sterilis subsp. atherantha

Avena sterilis subsp.

atherantha, commonly known as the slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.
