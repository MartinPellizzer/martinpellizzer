Acrocephalus kambovianus

Acrocephalus kambovianus, commonly known as the Kamboja reed, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in wetland or marshy environments.
