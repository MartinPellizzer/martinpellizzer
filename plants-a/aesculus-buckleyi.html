Aesculus buckleyi

Aesculus buckleyi, commonly known as Buckley's horse chestnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers in upright clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a spiny capsule containing glossy, chestnut-like seeds.

Buckley's horse chestnut is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
