Arachnopapua

Arachnopapua, commonly known as the Papuan spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the Indonesian island of Papua.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, thin petals resembling legs. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the rainforest canopy. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
