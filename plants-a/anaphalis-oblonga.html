Anaphalis oblonga

Anaphalis oblonga, commonly known as the woolly cudweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. Its flowers are typically small and yellowish, arranged in a cluster that resembles a dandelion.

Anaphalis oblonga is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
