Acoridium cobbianum

Acoridium cobbianum, commonly known as the cobbian waterplantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It typically grows in shallow, still waters and is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, often emerging from the water. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it in muddy substrates, while its leaves are adapted for efficient photosynthesis in aquatic environments.

Acoridium cobbianum plays a role in maintaining water quality by filtering sediments and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
