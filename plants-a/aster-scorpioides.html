Aster scorpioides

Aster scorpioides, commonly known as the scorpionweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, seed-like structures called cypselae. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in open plains and disturbed soils.

Aster scorpioides is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a hardy species in its native range.
