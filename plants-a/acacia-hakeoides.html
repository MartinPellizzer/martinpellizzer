Acacia hakeoides

Acacia hakeoides, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for dry climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts.
