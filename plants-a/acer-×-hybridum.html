Acer × hybridum

Acer × hybridum, commonly known as the hybrid maple, is a deciduous tree resulting from crosses between various maple species.

It typically exhibits vibrant autumn foliage, with leaves displaying shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a rounded crown and can grow up to 20 meters in height, depending on the parent species. Its bark is smooth and often has a reddish or greyish hue.

Acer × hybridum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to different soil types.
