Agave kerchovei var. beaucarnei

Agave kerchovei var. beaucarnei, commonly known as the Blue Agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces a tall, flowering stalk that bears small, greenish-white flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its rosette growth pattern and striking foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
