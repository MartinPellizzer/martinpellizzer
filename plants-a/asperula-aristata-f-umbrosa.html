Asperula aristata f. umbrosa

Asperula aristata f. umbrosa, commonly known as the shadowy evergreen azalea, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It features small, dark green leaves that are densely packed, giving it a lush, bushy appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pale pink to white flowers in early to mid-spring. It thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to add color to woodland gardens.
