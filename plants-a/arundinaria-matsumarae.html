Arundinaria matsumarae

Arundinaria matsumarae, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters, forming a clumping growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and its leaves are long and narrow with a smooth texture. Native to Japan, it thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
