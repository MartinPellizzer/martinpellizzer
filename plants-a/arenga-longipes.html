Arenga longipes

Arenga longipes, commonly known as the long-leaf areca, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers, followed by edible fruits that are used in traditional cuisine. Arenga longipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the fiber derived from its leaves.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
