Anthurium veraguense

Anthurium veraguense, commonly known as the Veraguense arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a spadix, which is a dense cluster of tiny flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil.

Anthurium veraguense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
