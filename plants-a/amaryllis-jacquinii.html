Amaryllis jacquinii

Amaryllis jacquinii, commonly known as the Jacquin's amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive red or purple stripe down the center. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears multiple blooms during the growing season. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are typically evergreen.

Amaryllis jacquinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
