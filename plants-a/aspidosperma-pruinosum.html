Aspidosperma pruinosum

Aspidosperma pruinosum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or copaiba, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The plant is also notable for its ecological importance in tropical forests.
