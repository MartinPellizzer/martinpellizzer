Adiantum mexicanum

Adiantum mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, bi-lobed fronds that resemble a feather. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a glossy appearance, with a unique ability to curl up when dry.

Adiantum mexicanum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
