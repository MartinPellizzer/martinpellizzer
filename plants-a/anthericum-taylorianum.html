Anthericum taylorianum

Anthericum taylorianum, commonly known as Taylor's onion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a tall, flowering stem that bears umbels of white or pale pink flowers. Its bulbs are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
