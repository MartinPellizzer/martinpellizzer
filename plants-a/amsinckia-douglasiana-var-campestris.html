Amsinckia douglasiana var. campestris

Amsinckia douglasiana var. campestris, commonly known as the desert parsley, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features basal leaves that are pinnately compound, with small, lobed leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers in late spring. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.
