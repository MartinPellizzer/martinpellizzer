Arenaria laxiflora

Arenaria laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically features dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous areas.

Arenaria laxiflora plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native habitats.
