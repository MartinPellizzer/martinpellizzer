Adiantum chilense f. typica

Adiantum chilense f. typica, commonly known as the Chilean maidenhair fern, is a fern species native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble feathers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce small, clustered sporangia on the undersides of the fronds.

Adiantum chilense f. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
