Acacia excelsa

Acacia excelsa, commonly known as the silky oak, is a tall, evergreen tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are finely divided and give the tree a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia excelsa is well adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both wet and dry conditions.

Its rapid growth rate makes it a valuable species for reforestation and ornamental purposes.
