Artemisia viscidula

Artemisia viscidula, commonly known as mugwort, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are arranged in panicles, typical of the Asteraceae family. Artemisia viscidula thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

It has a strong, aromatic scent and has been used traditionally in various herbal remedies.
