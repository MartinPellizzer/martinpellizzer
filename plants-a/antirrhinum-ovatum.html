Antirrhinum ovatum

Antirrhinum ovatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant is commonly cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
