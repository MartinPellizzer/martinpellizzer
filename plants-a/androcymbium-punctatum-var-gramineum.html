Androcymbium punctatum var. gramineum

Androcymbium punctatum var. gramineum, commonly known as the grassy orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate orchid-like flowers. This variety is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant typically grows from rhizomes and can form dense colonies in suitable conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
