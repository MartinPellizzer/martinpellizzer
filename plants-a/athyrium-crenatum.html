Athyrium crenatum

Athyrium crenatum, commonly known as the serrated wood fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds with a serrated edge. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its rhizomes are often covered in a layer of brown, fibrous material.

Athyrium crenatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
