Astragalus arpilobus subsp. drobovii

Astragalus arpilobus subsp.

drobovii, commonly known as the Drobovii milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is often deep, enabling it to access water from lower soil layers.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
