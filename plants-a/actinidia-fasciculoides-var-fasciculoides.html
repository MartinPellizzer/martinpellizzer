Actinidia fasciculoides var. fasciculoides

Actinidia fasciculoides var. fasciculoides, commonly known as the Chinese mayapple, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five ovate leaflets, often with a distinctive white or yellowish midrib. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by edible, red berries in the fall. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental foliage and fruit.

However, its berries are toxic to humans and livestock, requiring caution in cultivation.
