Amomum elephantorum

Amomum elephantorum, commonly known as the elephant's garlic or elephant garlic plant, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, fleshy, bulbous rhizomes that resemble those of true garlic. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring broad, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale greenish-white flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The botanical features of Amomum elephantorum make it a distinctive and valuable species in both horticulture and herbal practices.
