Astragalus hookerianus var. siskiyouensis

Astragalus hookerianus var. siskiyouensis, commonly known as Siskiyou milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Siskiyou Mountains in Oregon and California.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, dry habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
