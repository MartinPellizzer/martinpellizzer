Amygdalus pedunculata

Amygdalus pedunculata, commonly known as the Persian almond, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by the development of edible drupes that contain a single seed. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both its fruit and medicinal properties.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to arid and semi-arid climates.
