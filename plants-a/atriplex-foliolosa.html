Atriplex foliolosa

Atriplex foliolosa, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are edible and have been used by indigenous peoples for food and medicinal purposes.

Atriplex foliolosa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
