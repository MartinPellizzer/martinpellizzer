Arabis japonica

Arabis japonica, commonly known as Japanese rockcress, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It is a perennial species that thrives in cool, moist environments.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
