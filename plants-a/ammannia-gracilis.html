Ammannia gracilis

Ammannia gracilis, commonly known as prairie ammannia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and bloom in late summer.

Ammannia gracilis is known for its ability to thrive in wetland habitats and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
