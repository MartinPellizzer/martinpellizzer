Astragalus willisii

Astragalus willisii, commonly known as Willis' milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across its native habitat.
