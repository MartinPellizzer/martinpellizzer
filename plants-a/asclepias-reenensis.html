Asclepias reenensis

Asclepias reenensis, commonly known as the Rehn's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety green leaves and clusters of bright orange to yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is typical of the milkweed genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along canyon walls.

Asclepias reenensis plays an important role in supporting local pollinators, including butterflies and bees.
