Anabasis oppositiflora

Anabasis oppositiflora, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to survive in sandy soils with minimal water.

Anabasis oppositiflora plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for small desert animals.
