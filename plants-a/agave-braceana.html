Agave braceana

Agave braceana, commonly known as the blue agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing small, white or greenish flowers. Its leaves contain a high concentration of sap, which is used in the production of tequila and other alcoholic beverages.

Agave braceana is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water once established.
