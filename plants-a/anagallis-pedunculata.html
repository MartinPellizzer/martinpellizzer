Anagallis pedunculata

Anagallis pedunculata, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, hairy leaves and produces clusters of delicate, pink or red flowers that bloom from spring to summer. Its flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, rocky areas, and along roadsides.
