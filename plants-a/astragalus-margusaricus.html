Astragalus margusaricus

Astragalus margusaricus, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems, small yellow flowers, and compound leaves with numerous leaflets. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and alpine habitats. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing plants.

Its seeds are typically dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across rugged terrains.
