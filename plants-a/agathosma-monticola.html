Agathosma monticola

Agathosma monticola, commonly known as mountain agathosma, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique properties, it is also used in the production of essential oils and herbal remedies.
