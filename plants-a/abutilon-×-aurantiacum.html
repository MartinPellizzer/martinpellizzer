Abutilon × aurantiacum

Abutilon × aurantiacum, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a hybrid species within the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, papery, orange-red bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant is native to China and is widely cultivated as an ornamental due to its vibrant foliage and unique appearance. It thrives in warm climates and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

The bracts are not true flowers but modified leaves, and they persist for several weeks, adding visual interest to gardens and landscapes.
