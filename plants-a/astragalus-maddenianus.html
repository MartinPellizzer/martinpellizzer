Astragalus maddenianus

Astragalus maddenianus, commonly known as the Maddens' milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
