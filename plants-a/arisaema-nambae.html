Arisaema nambae

Arisaema nambae, commonly known as the Nambae arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces a corm-like tuber that serves as its primary storage organ and is used in traditional culinary practices. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped, giving it the common name "arrowroot.".

Arisaema nambae is often grown for its ornamental value as well as its edible tubers.
