Albizia kalkora

Albizia kalkora, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The tree is notable for its dense, aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. Albizia kalkora typically grows in lowland areas and is often found in secondary forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
