Acalypha callosa

Acalypha callosa, commonly known as the roughleaf ironwood, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale green to reddish flowers. The plant produces small, round, green berries that are often covered in a whitish bloom. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Acalypha callosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
