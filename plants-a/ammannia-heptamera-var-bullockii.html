Ammannia heptamera var. bullockii

Ammannia heptamera var. bullockii, commonly known as the Bullock's ammannia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. It produces small, clustered flowers that are pink to red in color, blooming from late spring to early summer.

The plant is noted for its ability to tolerate periodic flooding, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
