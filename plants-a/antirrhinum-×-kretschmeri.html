Antirrhinum × kretschmeri

Antirrhinum × kretschmeri, commonly known as the hybrid snapdragon, is a flowering plant that results from the crossbreeding of two snapdragon species.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Antirrhinum × kretschmeri is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
