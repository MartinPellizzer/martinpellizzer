Amaryllis minor

Amaryllis minor, commonly known as the dwarf amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact size and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant blooms in shades of red, pink, and white, often during the summer months. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, forming a rosette at the base.

Amaryllis minor is popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and ornamental value.
