Acacia verticillata var. glabra

Acacia verticillata var. glabra, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, feathery leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are grouped in spherical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens across Australia.
