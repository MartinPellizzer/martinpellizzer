Alopecurus alpinus var. muticus

Alopecurus alpinus var. muticus, commonly known as the silent alpine grass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe and North America.

It has fine, wiry leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often inconspicuous. The plant is adapted to harsh, cold environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It is typically found in rocky or gravelly habitats at high elevations.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
