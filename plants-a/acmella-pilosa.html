Acmella pilosa

Acmella pilosa, commonly known as the hairy spurge, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinct appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

Acmella pilosa is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
