Achyranthes glauca

Achyranthes glauca, commonly known as blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
