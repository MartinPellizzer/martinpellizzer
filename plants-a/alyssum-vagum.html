Alyssum vagum

Alyssum vagum, commonly known as the slender alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring and early summer. The plant has compact, branching stems covered in fine, silvery hairs, and its leaves are linear to oblanceolate in shape. Alyssum vagum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
