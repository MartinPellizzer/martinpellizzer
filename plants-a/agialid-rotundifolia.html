Agialid rotundifolia

Agialid rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf marsh plant, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in coastal areas and estuaries.

Agialid rotundifolia plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
