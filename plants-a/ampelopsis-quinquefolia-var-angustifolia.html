Ampelopsis quinquefolia var. angustifolia

Ampelopsis quinquefolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Virginia creeper, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with five to seven slender, lance-shaped leaflets that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial to full sun and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Its climbing habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for garden use.
