Argyroderma testiculare var. pearsonii

Argyroderma testiculare var. pearsonii, commonly known as the pearson's silverleaf.

This plant is a succulent species native to South Africa, characterized by its fleshy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps the plant conserve water in arid environments. It produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring.

The plant is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
