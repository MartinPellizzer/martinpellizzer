Annona obtusifolia

Annona obtusifolia, commonly known as the custard apple or horn of the unicorn, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its rough, dark bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by large, fuzzy, green fruits. The fruit, which is typically harvested when fully ripe, has a creamy, custard-like pulp with a sweet and tangy flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Annonaceae family and is valued for its edible fruit and ornamental qualities.
