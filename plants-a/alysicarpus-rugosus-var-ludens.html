Alysicarpus rugosus var. ludens

Alysicarpus rugosus var. ludens, commonly known as the roughleaf alysicarpus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
