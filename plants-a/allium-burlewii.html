Allium burlewii

Allium burlewii, commonly known as Burlew's onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes various species of garlic and onions, and is characterized by its bulbous root system and grass-like foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, spherical, white to pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential culinary uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
