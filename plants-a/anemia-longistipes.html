Anemia longistipes

Anemia longistipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed anemia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, which is typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation in tropical ecosystems.
