Adelonema peltatum

Adelonema peltatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy and have a minty aroma. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and typically pink to purple in color.

Adelonema peltatum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
