Acacia varia var. parviflora

Acacia varia var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to parts of Australia.

It features small, yellow globular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the autumn season. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
