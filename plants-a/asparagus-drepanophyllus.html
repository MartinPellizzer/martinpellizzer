Asparagus drepanophyllus

Asparagus drepanophyllus, commonly known as the golden asparagus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that are often tinged with a yellowish hue, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by pale yellow to white berries. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall herbaceous appearance.

Asparagus drepanophyllus is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in suitable climates.
