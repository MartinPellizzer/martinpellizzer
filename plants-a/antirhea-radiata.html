Antirhea radiata

Antirhea radiata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has pinnate leaves and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Antirhea radiata is also valued for its timber and has traditional uses in some cultures.
