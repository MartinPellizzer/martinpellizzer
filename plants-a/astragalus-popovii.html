Astragalus popovii

Astragalus popovii, commonly known as the Popov's milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its deep root system. Astragalus popovii is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
