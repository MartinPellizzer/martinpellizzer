Alexitoxicon luteolum

Alexitoxicon luteolum, commonly known as the golden poison dart frog, is a strikingly colorful amphibian found in the rainforests of Central and South America.

Its vibrant yellow skin serves as a warning to predators about its toxic nature, which includes potent alkaloids such as batrachotoxin. The plant does not exist; the name is a misnomer, as Alexitoxicon luteolum is not a plant but an animal. This confusion highlights the importance of accurate botanical nomenclature.
