Astragalus podocarpus

Astragalus podocarpus, commonly known as the prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny, pod-like fruit that contains several seeds. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, yellow or orange flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrubland and coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
