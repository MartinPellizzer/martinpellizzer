Anthemis gayana var. hispidula

Anthemis gayana var. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy anthemis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a flat-topped cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
