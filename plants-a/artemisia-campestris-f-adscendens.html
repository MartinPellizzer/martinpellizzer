Artemisia campestris f. adscendens

Artemisia campestris f. adscendens, commonly known as the ascending tarragon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, though it is less commonly cultivated than other artemisia species.

The plant's adaptability and resilience make it a notable species in its native range.
