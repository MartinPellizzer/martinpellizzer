Acacia blaxellii

Acacia blaxellii, commonly known as the Blaxell's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures in mature plants. The plant produces yellow, globular inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Acacia blaxellii is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities in landscaping.
