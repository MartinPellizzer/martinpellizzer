Agropyron gmelinii

Agropyron gmelinii, commonly known as the Siberian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the cold climates of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous florets, which are wind-pollinated. Agropyron gmelinii is well adapted to harsh environments, including arid and semi-arid regions, and is often found in steppe and tundra ecosystems.

It is valued for its erosion control capabilities and as a forage plant for livestock.
