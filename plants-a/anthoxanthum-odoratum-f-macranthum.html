Anthoxanthum odoratum f. macranthum

Anthoxanthum odoratum f. macranthum, commonly known as the sweet vernal grass, is a variant of the sweet vernal grass species.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with green or yellow. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are usually pale green to yellow in color.

This form is characterized by its larger, more prominent flowers compared to the typical form of Anthoxanthum odoratum.
