Astragalus newberryi var. castoreus

Astragalus newberryi var. castoreus, commonly known as the Castoreus milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces small, yellow to reddish pods that contain seeds dispersed by wind.
