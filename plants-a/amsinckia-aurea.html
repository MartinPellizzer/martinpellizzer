Amsinckia aurea

Amsinckia aurea, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads that resemble a crown, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Amsinckia aurea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
