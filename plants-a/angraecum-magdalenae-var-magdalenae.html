Angraecum magdalenae var. magdalenae

Angraecum magdalenae var. magdalenae, commonly known as the Magdalenae orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom at night. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale pink flowers with long, slender petals and a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a favorite among orchid enthusiasts.
