Astragalus sesameus

Astragalus sesameus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America, particularly in the western United States. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, compound leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in clusters, and are followed by distinctive pod-like fruits.

Astragalus sesameus is adapted to poor soils and can thrive in harsh, dry environments.
