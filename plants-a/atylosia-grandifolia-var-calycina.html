Atylosia grandifolia var. calycina

Atylosia grandifolia var. calycina, commonly known as the California fiddleneck, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features slender, branching stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most striking feature is the bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in dry, open habitats.
