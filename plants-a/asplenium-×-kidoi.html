Asplenium × kidoi

Asplenium × kidoi, commonly known as the hybrid maidenhair fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Asplenium trichomanes and Asplenium bulbiferum.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided, and often arching fronds that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as woodlands and rock crevices, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its spores are produced on the undersides of its fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Asplenium × kidoi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
