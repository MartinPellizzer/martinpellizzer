Asperula platygalium var. pratensis

Asperula platygalium var. pratensis, commonly known as the meadow asperula, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.
