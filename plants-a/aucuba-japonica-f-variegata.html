Aucuba japonica f. variegata

Aucuba japonica f. variegata, commonly known as the Japanese laurel or variegated aucuba, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with striking white and yellow variegation, creating a vibrant visual contrast. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and is highly tolerant of urban conditions, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Its dense foliage and ornamental leaves make it a valuable addition to shaded gardens and indoor spaces.
