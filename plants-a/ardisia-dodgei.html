Ardisia dodgei

Ardisia dodgei, commonly known as the Hawaiian beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. These berries are an important food source for native birds and other wildlife.

Ardisia dodgei thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.
