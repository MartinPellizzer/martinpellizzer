Acacia filicina f. hirsuta

Acacia filicina f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, feathery leaves that are densely covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that appear in spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

The hairy texture of its leaves and stems is a distinguishing feature of this botanical variant.
