Acer cinnamomifolium

Acer cinnamomifolium, commonly known as the cinnamon-leaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern China.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that resemble the shape of cinnamon leaves, hence its common name. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and parks in temperate regions.
