Allium breviradium

Allium breviradium, commonly known as the shortstem leek, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant features a bulbous stem that is typically shorter than its leaves, which are hollow and grass-like in appearance. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are arranged in a spherical cluster called a umbel.

Allium breviradium is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cooler, alpine environments.
