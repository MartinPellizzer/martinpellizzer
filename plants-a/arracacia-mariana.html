Arracacia mariana

Arracacia mariana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy dark green surface.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, dark seeds.
