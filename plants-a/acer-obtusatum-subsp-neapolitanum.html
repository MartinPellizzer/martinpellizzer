Acer obtusatum subsp. neapolitanum

Acer obtusatum subsp.

neapolitanum, commonly known as the Naples maple, is a deciduous tree native to southern Italy. It is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath.

The tree produces small, red or orange-red flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental planting in gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
