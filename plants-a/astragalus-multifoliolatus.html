Astragalus multifoliolatus

Astragalus multifoliolatus, commonly known as the many-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with multiple leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow to green flowers arranged in spikes, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Astragalus multifoliolatus plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local pollinators.
