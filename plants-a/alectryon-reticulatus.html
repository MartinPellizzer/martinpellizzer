Alectryon reticulatus

Alectryon reticulatus, commonly known as the net-veined acorn tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny capsules containing its seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal or lowland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
