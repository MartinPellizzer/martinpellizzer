Amerimnon glabrum

Amerimnon glabrum, commonly known as the smooth amerimnon, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, glabrous (smooth) leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates. Its botanical significance lies in its role within local ecosystems and its potential for horticultural use.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and somewhat obscure species in botanical studies.
