Alkanna tinctoria var. lehmannii

Alkanna tinctoria var. lehmannii, commonly known as the red sanders or red root, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that produce a deep red to orange dye when processed. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and has a drought-tolerant nature, making it well-suited for dry climates. Its leaves are typically alternate and lance-shaped, while the flowers are small and yellowish.

The species is valued for its historical use in natural dyeing and traditional medicine.
