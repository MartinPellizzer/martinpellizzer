Astragalus lentiginosus var. trumbullensis

Astragalus lentiginosus var. trumbullensis, commonly known as Trumbull's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces yellow to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native range.
