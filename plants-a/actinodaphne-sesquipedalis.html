Actinodaphne sesquipedalis

Actinodaphne sesquipedalis, commonly known as the long-leaved camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red when mature, containing several seeds. It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its wood, which is used in construction and furniture.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional remedies.
