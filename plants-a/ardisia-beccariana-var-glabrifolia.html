Ardisia beccariana var. glabrifolia

Ardisia beccariana var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved beautyberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces bright purple berries that are a striking feature, often appearing in late summer. Its leaves are smooth and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
