Acacia armata f. undulata

Acacia armata f. undulata, commonly known as the undulating wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, yellow flowers that appear in dense clusters. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous tiny leaflets, and the plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is notable for its unique, undulating growth pattern that gives it its common name.
