Asperula taurina f. leucanthera

Asperula taurina f. leucanthera, commonly known as the white-flowered woodruff, is a variant of the woodruff plant native to Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, shady environments. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.

The white flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
