Acanthocereus subinermis

Acanthocereus subinermis, commonly known as the night blooming cactus, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny stems and ability to store water in its thick, fleshy tissue. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its adaptations to arid environments include reduced leaves and a deep root system for efficient water absorption.

The plant is also known for its slow growth rate and long lifespan, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.
