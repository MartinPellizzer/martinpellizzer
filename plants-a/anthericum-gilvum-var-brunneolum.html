Anthericum gilvum var. brunneolum

Anthericum gilvum var. brunneolum, commonly known as the brownish yellow nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, nettle-like leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a common feature among arum family members. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
