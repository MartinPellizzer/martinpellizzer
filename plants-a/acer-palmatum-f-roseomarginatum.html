Acer palmatum f. roseomarginatum

Acer palmatum f. roseomarginatum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with rose-marginated leaves, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree.

It is characterized by its ornamental foliage, which features a distinctive pink or red margin on the leaves. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. It typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and has a rounded, upright growth habit.

The leaves are palmately lobed, turning vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
