Alcea rosea subsp. ficifolia

Alcea rosea subsp.

ficifolia, commonly known as the leafy hollyhock, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive darker center.

The plant features broad, palmately lobed leaves that give it a unique, almost fern-like appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and foliage.
