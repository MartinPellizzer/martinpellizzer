Amorphophallus maximus subsp. maximus

Amorphophallus maximus subsp.

maximus, commonly known as the giant tiger's claw, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. This plant is characterized by its large, tropical leaves and a distinctive, spiky, underground tuber that serves as its storage organ.

The tuber can grow to impressive sizes, sometimes reaching weights of several kilograms. It produces striking, spatulate-shaped leaves and a unique inflorescence that includes a spadix and a spathe.

The plant is cultivated for both its ornamental value and its edible tuber, which is used in various culinary traditions.
