Astragalus weidmannianus

Astragalus weidmannianus, commonly known as the Weidmann's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and fibrous, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus weidmannianus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
