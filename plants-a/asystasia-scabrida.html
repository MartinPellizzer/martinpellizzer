Asystasia scabrida

Asystasia scabrida, commonly known as rough asystasia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a rough, scabrous texture that gives the plant its common name. It produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet or purple, often arranged in dense clusters.

This species is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
