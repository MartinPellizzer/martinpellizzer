Agave samalana

Agave samalana, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with sharp spines along the edges. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears small, white flowers, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Agave samalana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
