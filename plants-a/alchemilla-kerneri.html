Alchemilla kerneri

Alchemilla kerneri, commonly known as the Alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of velvety, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Alchemilla kerneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
