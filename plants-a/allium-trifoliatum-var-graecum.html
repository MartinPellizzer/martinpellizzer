Allium trifoliatum var. graecum

Allium trifoliatum var. graecum, commonly known as the Greek leek or wild leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are broad, flat, and often bluish-green in color, with a strong garlic-like aroma. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a valuable plant in traditional cuisines.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
