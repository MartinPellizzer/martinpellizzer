Angraecum crassum

Angraecum crassum, commonly known as the giant angraecum, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its inflorescence consists of multiple racemes, each bearing numerous fragrant, white to pale blue flowers.

Angraecum crassum thrives in high humidity and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
