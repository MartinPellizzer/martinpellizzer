Antenoron virginianum f. rubrum

Antenoron virginianum f. rubrum, commonly known as the red-stemmed antenoron, is a variant of the antenoron plant characterized by its distinct reddish stems.

It is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States, typically found in moist, woodlands or along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring. Its botanical significance lies in its unique coloration and ecological role within its habitat.

It is often studied for its adaptability to wet environments and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
