Argyreia strigillosa

Argyreia strigillosa, commonly known as the velvet tangle or velvet leaf, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant has hairy, strigillose leaves that give it its common name, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.

The plant's rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and ornamental landscaping.
