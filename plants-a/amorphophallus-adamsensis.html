Amorphophallus adamsensis

Amorphophallus adamsensis, commonly known as the Adams' elephant's foot, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, tuberous underground stem, which can grow to impressive sizes. The plant produces a single, large, umbrella-shaped leaf with a distinct, veiny pattern. Its flowers are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
