Allium sabulicola

Allium sabulicola, commonly known as sand garlic, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale purple flowers in clusters. Its bulbous root system is similar to other allium species, and it is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Botanically, it is classified in the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like onions and daffodils.
