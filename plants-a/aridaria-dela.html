Aridaria dela

Aridaria dela, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a succulent plant adapted to arid environments.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It typically grows in rocky soils and is found in regions with low annual rainfall.

Due to its unique adaptations, Aridaria dela is an important species in ecological restoration projects in desert ecosystems.
