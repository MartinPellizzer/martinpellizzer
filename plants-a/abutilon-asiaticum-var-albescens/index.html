Abutilon Asiaticum Var. Albescens

Abutilon asiaticum var. albescens is a lesser-known yet fascinating plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes well-known species like cotton and hibiscus. This variety is distinguished by its soft, fuzzy leaves and delicate, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively obscure in horticultural literature.
