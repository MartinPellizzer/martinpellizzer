Amerimnon latifolium

Amerimnon latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf amerimnon, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for landscaping purposes.
