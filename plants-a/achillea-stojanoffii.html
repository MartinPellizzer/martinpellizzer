Achillea stojanoffii

Achillea stojanoffii, commonly known as Stojanoff's yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties have made it of interest in traditional herbal practices.
