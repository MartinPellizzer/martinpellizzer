Amaranthus gangeticus

Amaranthus gangeticus, commonly known as Indian spinach, is an annual herb native to South Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stem, lance-shaped leaves, and dense clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant is valued for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients such as iron and protein. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as a leafy vegetable.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and conditions.
