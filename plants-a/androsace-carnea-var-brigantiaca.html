Androsace carnea var. brigantiaca

Androsace carnea var. brigantiaca, commonly known as the Alpine pink, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, fleshy leaves that are typically rosette-shaped. The plant produces clusters of pink or red flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in alpine gardens and rockeries.
