Allium ownbeyi

Allium ownbeyi, commonly known as the desert leek, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage.
