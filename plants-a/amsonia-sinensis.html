Amsonia sinensis

Amsonia sinensis, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features striking blue-gray foliage and produces fluffy, silver-white flower clusters in late summer. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Amsonia sinensis is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
