Anthyllis fulgurans var. efulgurata

Anthyllis fulgurans var. efulgurata, commonly known as the golden everlasting or golden sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is known for its aromatic, sweet scent.

It is often used in ornamental gardening and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
