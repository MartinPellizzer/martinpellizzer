Arnica louiseana var. pilosa

Arnica louiseana var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy mountain Arnica, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features basal leaves that are hairy and lance-shaped, with a distinct yellow-orange flower head composed of many tiny tubular florets. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
