Aspalathus arida var. grandiflora

Aspalathus arida var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Cape wild tea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, serrated leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow to white flowers. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun exposure.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
