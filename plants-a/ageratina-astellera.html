Ageratina astellera

Ageratina astellera, commonly known as the whiteflower blueweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall texture.

Ageratina astellera is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streambanks.
