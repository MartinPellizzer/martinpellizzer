Astragalus sanctorum

Astragalus sanctorum, commonly known as the sacred milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Astragalus sanctorum is notable for its historical use in traditional medicine and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
