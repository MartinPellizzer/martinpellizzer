Aloe cymbifolia

Aloe cymbifolia, commonly known as the Cape aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, spiky stems covered in fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces striking yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Aloe cymbifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
