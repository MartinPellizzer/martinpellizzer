Acalypha calyciformis

Acalypha calyciformis, commonly known as the redleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red bracts that resemble leaves, which are actually modified flowers. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of yellow or orange. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Acalypha calyciformis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
