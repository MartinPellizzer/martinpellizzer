Alloplectus serpens

Alloplectus serpens, commonly known as the snake's head fern, is a species of fern native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its creeping rhizomes and distinctive, lance-shaped fronds that resemble a serpent's head. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its fronds are usually a deep green and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

Alloplectus serpens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
