Aquilegia vulgaris var. platysepala

Aquilegia vulgaris var. platysepala, commonly known as the flat-sepaled columbine, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five deep lobes, and its stems are upright and branched. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ecological benefits to pollinators.
