Acacia piptadenioides

Acacia piptadenioides, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grey-green foliage and distinctive yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia piptadenioides thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
