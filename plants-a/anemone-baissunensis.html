Anemone baissunensis

Anemone baissunensis, commonly known as the Baissun anemone, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive central tuft of stamens. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette and are hairy, which helps in reducing water loss.

Anemone baissunensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
