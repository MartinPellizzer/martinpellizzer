Acacia impressa var. tortuosa

Acacia impressa var. tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its zigzagging, twisted stems and small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance, it is valued in horticulture for ornamental purposes.
