Aristida royleana

Aristida royleana, commonly known as the Kashmir grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia.

It typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelets that are usually pale green to brown in color.

Aristida royleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a source of forage for livestock.
