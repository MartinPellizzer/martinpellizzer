Acacia sedifolia subsp. pulvinata

Acacia sedifolia subsp.

pulvinata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often pendulous phyllodes that are typically narrow and linear.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer to autumn, which are grouped in cylindrical spikes. It has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions.

The leaves are reduced to phyllodes, a common adaptation in the genus Acacia to reduce water loss.
