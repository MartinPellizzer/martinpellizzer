Aspilia discolor

Aspilia discolor, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branching.

Aspilia discolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
