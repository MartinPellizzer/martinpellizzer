Alchemilla transiliensis

Alchemilla transiliensis, commonly known as the Transylvanian lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Alchemilla transiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
