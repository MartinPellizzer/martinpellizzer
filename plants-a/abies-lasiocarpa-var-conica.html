Abies lasiocarpa var. conica

Abies lasiocarpa var. conica, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its conical shape, which is more pronounced in the varietal form conica compared to the typical species. The tree has needle-like leaves that are blue-green in color and are arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are cylindrical and contain small, winged seeds.

The bark is thick, furrowed, and often exhibits a reddish-brown color.
