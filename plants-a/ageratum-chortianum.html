Ageratum chortianum

Ageratum chortianum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil. Its foliage is typically gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

Ageratum chortianum is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
