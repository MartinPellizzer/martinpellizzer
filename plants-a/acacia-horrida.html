Acacia horrida

Acacia horrida, commonly known as the rough acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. Acacia horrida is well adapted to dry climates, with deep roots that allow it to survive in harsh, sandy soils.

It plays an important ecological role by providing shelter and food for various desert animals.
