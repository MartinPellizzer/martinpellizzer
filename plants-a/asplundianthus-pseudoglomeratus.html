Asplundianthus pseudoglomeratus

Asplundianthus pseudoglomeratus, commonly known as the false glomerate bush, is a shrub native to specific regions of South America.

It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. The plant features small, clustered leaves and produces yellow or orange flowers during the dry season. Its botanical structure includes woody stems and a compact growth habit.

It is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in xerophytic gardens.
