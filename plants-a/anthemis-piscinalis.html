Anthemis piscinalis

Anthemis piscinalis, commonly known as water chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, which are typically white with a yellow center, and its aromatic, finely divided leaves. The plant thrives in wet or marshy habitats, often growing near water bodies such as ponds and streams. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other similar plants.

The essential oils extracted from its flowers are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine for their calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
