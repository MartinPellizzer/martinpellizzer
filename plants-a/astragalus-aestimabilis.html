Astragalus aestimabilis

Astragalus aestimabilis, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has small, hairy seed pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Astragalus aestimabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
