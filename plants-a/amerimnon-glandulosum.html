Amerimnon glandulosum

Amerimnon glandulosum, commonly known as the hairy leaf balsam, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
