Aromadendron annamense

Aromadendron annamense, commonly known as Vietnamese camphor tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by dark blue berries. Its essential oils, particularly camphor, are extracted from the leaves and have medicinal and industrial applications.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
