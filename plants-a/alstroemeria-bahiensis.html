Alstroemeria bahiensis

Alstroemeria bahiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian fire lily, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant features strap-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish hue on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Alstroemeria bahiensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
