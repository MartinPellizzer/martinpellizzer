Allium vineale proles nitens

Allium vineale proles nitens, commonly known as the garlic chive or wild garlic, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.

The bulbous stems and leaves are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a popular herb in culinary applications.
