Astragalus cerussatus

Astragalus cerussatus, commonly known as leadplant, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in prairie and steppe habitats. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus cerussatus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
