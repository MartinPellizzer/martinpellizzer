Allocarya tenuifolia

Allocarya tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments. It is a member of the Saxifragaceae family, which includes many species with similar growth habits.

Allocarya tenuifolia is often found in high-altitude meadows and is relatively uncommon in cultivation.
