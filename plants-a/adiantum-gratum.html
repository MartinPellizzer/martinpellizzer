Adiantum gratum

Adiantum gratum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant plant native to temperate regions of Asia, Europe, and North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that resemble the hair of a maiden, hence its common name. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained, humus-rich soil. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Adiantum gratum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a home setting.
