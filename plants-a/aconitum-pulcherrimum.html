Aconitum pulcherrimum

Aconitum pulcherrimum, commonly known as the beautiful aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves and produces showy, spire-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

However, it is highly toxic due to the presence of potent alkaloids, requiring careful handling.
