Amelanchier arguta

Amelanchier arguta, commonly known as the Saskatoon berry or serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary applications. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or open areas.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
