Allium intermedium var. bulbiferum

Allium intermedium var. bulbiferum, commonly known as the bulbiferous leek, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, bulbous structures that resemble onions, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible parts, it is cultivated in gardens and for its medicinal properties.
