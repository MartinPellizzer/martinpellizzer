Aria gracilis

Aria gracilis, commonly known as the slender arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features slender, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically light green and arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist soil and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Aria gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
