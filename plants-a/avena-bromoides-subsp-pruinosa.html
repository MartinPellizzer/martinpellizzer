Avena bromoides subsp. pruinosa

Avena bromoides subsp.

pruinosa, commonly known as the pruinose oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The leaves are narrow and covered with a fine, powdery coating, giving them a silvery appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
