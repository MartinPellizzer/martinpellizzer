Acacia barbinervis subsp. borealis

Acacia barbinervis subsp.

borealis, commonly known as the northern acacia, is a shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, needle-like leaves that are adapted to cold and harsh environments.

The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Its seeds are enclosed in pod-like fruits that split open when mature.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and its role in stabilizing coastal and tundra ecosystems.
