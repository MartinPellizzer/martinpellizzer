Artemisia norvegica var. pacifica

Artemisia norvegica var. pacifica, commonly known as Pacific wormwood, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the Pacific coast, thriving in maritime climates. The plant has aromatic, silver-gray foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical uses in traditional medicine.
