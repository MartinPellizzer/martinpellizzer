Asplenium poiretianum

Asplenium poiretianum, commonly known as the Poiret's spleenwort, is a member of the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its small, fern-like fronds.

It is a lithophytic plant, typically growing on rocks or tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a compact, rosette-like growth habit with intricate, finely divided leaves. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the undersides of the leaves.

Asplenium poiretianum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich substrates.
