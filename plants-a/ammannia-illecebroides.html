Ammannia illecebroides

Ammannia illecebroides, commonly known as the redroot dayflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its roots are often reddish in color, which contributes to its common name.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
