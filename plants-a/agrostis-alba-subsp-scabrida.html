Agrostis alba subsp. scabrida

Agrostis alba subsp.

scabrida, commonly known as the scabrous silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and slender, upright growth habit.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, ditches, and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
