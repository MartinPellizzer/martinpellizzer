Aspalathus viminea

Aspalathus viminea, commonly known as the slender aspalathus, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is often found in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

Aspalathus viminea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
