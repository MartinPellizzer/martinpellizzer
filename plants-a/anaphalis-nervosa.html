Anaphalis nervosa

Anaphalis nervosa, commonly known as the silverleaf frostflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant is known for its silvery-white leaves and delicate, pale-yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Anaphalis nervosa is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
