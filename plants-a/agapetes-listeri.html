Agapetes listeri

Agapetes listeri, commonly known as the Lister's silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by small, dark blue berries.

Agapetes listeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
