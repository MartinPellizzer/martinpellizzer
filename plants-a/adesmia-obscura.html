Adesmia obscura

Adesmia obscura, commonly known as the dark adesmia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. Its flowers are usually pale or whitish, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Adesmia obscura is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential for use in restoration projects.
