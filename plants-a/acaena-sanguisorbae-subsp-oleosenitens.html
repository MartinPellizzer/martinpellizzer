Acaena sanguisorbae subsp. oleosenitens

Acaena sanguisorbae subsp.

oleosenitens, commonly known as the red burr bush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces bright red, spherical fruits that are adapted for animal dispersal.

The plant thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and branched.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid regions.
