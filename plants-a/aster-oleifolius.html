Aster oleifolius

Aster oleifolius, commonly known as sweet woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage that emits a mild, vanilla-like scent when crushed. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Aster oleifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
