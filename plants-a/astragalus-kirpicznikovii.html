Astragalus kirpicznikovii

Astragalus kirpicznikovii, commonly known as Kirpicznikov's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its deep root system.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods, a common feature among legumes, and it plays a role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.
