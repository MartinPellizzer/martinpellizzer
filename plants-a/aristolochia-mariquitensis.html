Aristolochia mariquitensis

Aristolochia mariquitensis, commonly known as the Mariquit vine, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is characterized by its unique, horseshoe-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that attract specific pollinators. Its medicinal properties have been studied, though it contains toxic compounds that require caution in use.

The species is of interest in botanical research due to its ecological and potential pharmacological significance.
