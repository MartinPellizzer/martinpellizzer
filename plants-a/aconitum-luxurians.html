Aconitum luxurians

Aconitum luxurians, commonly known as the giant monkshood, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces large, showy blue-purple flowers arranged in a raceme, which are highly toxic due to the presence of potent alkaloids. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that give it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

Aconitum luxurians thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
