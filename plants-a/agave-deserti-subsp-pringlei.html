Agave deserti subsp. pringlei

Agave deserti subsp.

pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's agave, is a succulent plant native to the Sonoran Desert. It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, blue-gray leaves with sharp spines.

The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which are pollinated by nocturnal moths. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy leaves and stems.

This subspecies is also valued for its use in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.
