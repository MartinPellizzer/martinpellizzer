Asplenium magellanicum

Asplenium magellanicum, commonly known as the Magellan spleenwort, is a fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic or lithophytic fern that typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in moist, shaded environments. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often divided into smaller segments, giving it a delicate appearance. It is native to South America, particularly in regions of Argentina, Chile, and Peru.

Asplenium magellanicum is known for its ability to thrive in humid, temperate climates and is often found in montane forests.
