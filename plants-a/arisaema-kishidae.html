Arisaema kishidae

Arisaema kishidae, commonly known as the Kishida's arisaema, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its unique, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Arisaema kishidae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
