Acacia mirandae

Acacia mirandae, commonly known as the Mirandae acacia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring narrow, bipinnate leaves and yellow or golden flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Acacia mirandae is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
