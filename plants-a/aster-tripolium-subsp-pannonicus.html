Aster tripolium subsp. pannonicus

Aster tripolium subsp.

pannonicus, commonly known as the Pannonian aster, is a flowering plant native to parts of Eastern Europe and the Middle East. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, purple to blue flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat conservation.
