Ardisia neglecta

Ardisia neglecta, commonly known as the evergreen boxwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a compact growth habit, making it ideal for hedges and topiary. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that attract birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Ardisia neglecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
