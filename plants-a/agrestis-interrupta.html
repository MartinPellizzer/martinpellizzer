Agrestis interrupta

Agrestis interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted grass, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields, roadsides, and forest edges. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often branched and bear small, narrow leaves. Its flowers are arranged in loose panicles and produce small, pale brown to yellowish seeds.

Agrestis interrupta is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
