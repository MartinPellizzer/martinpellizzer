Artocarpus griffithii

Artocarpus griffithii, commonly known as the giant fig or Griffith's fig, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spiny fruit. The fruit, which is typically green when mature, contains a sweet, edible pulp surrounded by a fibrous outer layer. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued both for its fruit and its ornamental appeal.
