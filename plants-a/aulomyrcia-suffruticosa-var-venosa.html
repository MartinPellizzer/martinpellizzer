Aulomyrcia suffruticosa var. venosa

Aulomyrcia suffruticosa var. venosa, commonly known as the veined aulomyrcia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with distinctive veining patterns on its leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its bark is rough and fissured, often exuding a mild resin when damaged.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
