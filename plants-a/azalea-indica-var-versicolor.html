Azalea indica var. versicolor

Azalea indica var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, red, and orange, depending on the variety. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
