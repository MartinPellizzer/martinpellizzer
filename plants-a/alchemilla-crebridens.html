Alchemilla crebridens

Alchemilla crebridens, commonly known as the broad-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, hairy, and often silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Alchemilla crebridens is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
