Acacia drewiana

Acacia drewiana, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are finely divided, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Acacia drewiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
