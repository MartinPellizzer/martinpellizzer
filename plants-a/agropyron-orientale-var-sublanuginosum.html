Agropyron orientale var. sublanuginosum

Agropyron orientale var. sublanuginosum, commonly known as the woolly wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in panicles, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and as a forage plant for livestock.
