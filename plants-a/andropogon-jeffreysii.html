Andropogon jeffreysii

Andropogon jeffreysii, commonly known as Jeffreys' bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, blue-green leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces panicles of tiny, spikelet-based flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Andropogon jeffreysii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
