Alchemilla raddeana

Alchemilla raddeana, commonly known as silverweed, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

Alchemilla raddeana is often found in moist, well-drained soils in meadows and woodland edges.
