Acer tsinglingense

Acer tsinglingense, commonly known as the Tsingling maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves with five lobes. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras for seed dispersal. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fall foliage.
