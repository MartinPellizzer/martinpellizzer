Arctostaphylos pastillosa

Arctostaphylos pastillosa, commonly known as the Sierra buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, dark berries that are an important food source for wildlife. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats. It has a compact growth habit with multiple stems and can reach heights of up to 3 meters.

The leaves are leathery and have a distinctive, aromatic scent when crushed.
