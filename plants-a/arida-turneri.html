Arida turneri

Arida turneri, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds with tufts of white hair, aiding in wind dispersal. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, powdery coating that reduces water loss.

Arida turneri is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
