Azalea hookeri

Azalea hookeri, commonly known as the mountain azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and red, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Azalea hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
