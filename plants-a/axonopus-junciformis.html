Axonopus junciformis

Axonopus junciformis, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, exhibiting a clump-forming growth habit. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters. It is often used in landscaping for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous root system and its adaptability to waterlogged conditions.
