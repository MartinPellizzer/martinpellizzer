Acinodendron sclerophyllum

Acinodendron sclerophyllum, commonly known as the hard-leaved acinodendron, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage, which is an adaptation to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acinodendron sclerophyllum is often found in rocky soils and is part of the Rhamnaceae family.
