Aegilops juvenalis var. mutica

Aegilops juvenalis var. mutica, commonly known as the woolly wild oat, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous short, bristle-tipped glumes. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and agricultural research.
