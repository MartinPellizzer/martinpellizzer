Argyrocome imbricata

Argyrocome imbricata, commonly known as the silver-leafed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its stems are often hairy.

Argyrocome imbricata is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
