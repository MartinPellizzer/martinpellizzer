Astragalus microphysa f. paucijugus

Astragalus microphysa f. paucijugus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its subspecies, f. paucijugus, is distinguished by its reduced number of leaflets, giving it a more compact appearance.

This plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
