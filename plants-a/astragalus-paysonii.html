Astragalus paysonii

Astragalus paysonii, commonly known as Payson milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and mountain slopes. It has small, compound leaves and produces legume pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus paysonii is part of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
