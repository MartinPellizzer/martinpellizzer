Arctostaphylos mariposa var. bivisa

Arctostaphylos mariposa var. bivisa, commonly known as the Sierra Madre manzanita, is a shrub native to California's coastal ranges.

It features small, evergreen leaves that are typically elliptical and have a leathery texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that mature in the summer and are an important food source for local wildlife.

This variety is adapted to rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
