Aglaia argentea var. splendens

Aglaia argentea var. splendens, commonly known as silver aglaia or golden aglaia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically a deep green with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by elliptical, yellow to orange fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
