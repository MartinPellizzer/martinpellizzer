Azalea speciosa

Azalea speciosa, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features broad, leathery leaves. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its floral display ranges from white to deep pink, depending on the cultivar.
