Ampelopsis heterophylla var. amurensis

Ampelopsis heterophylla var. amurensis, commonly known as Amur grapevine, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves that vary in shape, with some leaves being trifoliate and others pinnate. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, sweet berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
