Anthurium guayanum

Anthurium guayanum, commonly known as the Guayana anthurium, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant spadix flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its foliage is usually dark green with a waxy texture, and the spathe can range from red to yellow, depending on the variety.

Anthurium guayanum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
