Aeschynomene atropurpurea

Aeschynomene atropurpurea, commonly known as purpletop or purplestem false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in full sun.

Aeschynomene atropurpurea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
