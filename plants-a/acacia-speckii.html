Acacia speckii

Acacia speckii, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and small, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting a variety of pollinators. Acacia speckii is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with limited water availability.
