Andropogon hypogynus var. anatherus

Andropogon hypogynus var. anatherus, commonly known as the purple false panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, upright stems that often produce a dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically blooms with small, clustered inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or pink hues. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive blue-green color, which becomes more pronounced under certain light conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
