Allophylus chrysothrix

Allophylus chrysothrix, commonly known as the golden-leaved holly, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow leaves, which contrast with its dark green undersides. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Allophylus chrysothrix is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in tropical ecosystems.
