Achillea tanacetifolia subsp. distans

Achillea tanacetifolia subsp.

distans, commonly known as the distant yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flower heads, which are arranged in flat-topped inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
