Acer mono f. puberulum

Acer mono f. puberulum, commonly known as the pubescent maple, is a variant of the single-leaf maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive, large, palmately lobed leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly China. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
