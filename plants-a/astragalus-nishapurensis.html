Astragalus nishapurensis

Astragalus nishapurensis, commonly known as Nishapur milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the central regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. It has compound leaves and produces pods that contain small, hard seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
