Archontophoenix jardinei

Archontophoenix jardinei, commonly known as the Queensland kauri, is a large evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, feather-like leaves. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with yellow flowers, followed by edible, coconut-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal and rainforest areas.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in Australian botany.
