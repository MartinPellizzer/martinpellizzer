Ammannia elata

Ammannia elata, commonly known as prairie ammannia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often red-veined and have a distinctive waxy coating. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Ammannia elata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
