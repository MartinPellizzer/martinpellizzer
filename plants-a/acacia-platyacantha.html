Acacia platyacantha

Acacia platyacantha, commonly known as the flat-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces flat, elliptical pods that contain several seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into two sets of smaller leaflets.

Acacia platyacantha is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
