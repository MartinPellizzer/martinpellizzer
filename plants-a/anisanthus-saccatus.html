Anisanthus saccatus

Anisanthus saccatus, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and violet. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in coastal dunes and dry, sunny habitats.

Anisanthus saccatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize sandy soils.
