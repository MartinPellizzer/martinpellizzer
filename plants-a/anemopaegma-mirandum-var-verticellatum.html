Anemopaegma mirandum var. verticellatum

Anemopaegma mirandum var. verticellatum, commonly known as the vertical bell anemone, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers atop a spathe. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This variant is prized for its unique floral display and is often cultivated in shady garden settings.
