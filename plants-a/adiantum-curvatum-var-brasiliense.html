Adiantum curvatum var. brasiliense

Adiantum curvatum var. brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and leathery. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced on the underside of its fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction via spores rather than seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
