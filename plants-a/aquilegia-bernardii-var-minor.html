Aquilegia bernardii var. minor

Aquilegia bernardii var. minor, commonly known as the lesser bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from red to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with finely divided lobes, and its stems are usually hairy and upright. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
