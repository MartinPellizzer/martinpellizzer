Arenaria subpilosa

Arenaria subpilosa, commonly known as sand sandwort, is a low-growing flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

Arenaria subpilosa is often found in sandy soils and is well-suited to rocky or gravelly habitats.
