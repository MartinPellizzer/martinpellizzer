Acacia pulchella

Acacia pulchella, commonly known as the pink acacia, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. Acacia pulchella is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access underground water.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive, feathery appearance.
