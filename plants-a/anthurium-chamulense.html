Anthurium chamulense

Anthurium chamulense, commonly known as the Chamulense Arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically features large, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and vibrant green coloration. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium chamulense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
