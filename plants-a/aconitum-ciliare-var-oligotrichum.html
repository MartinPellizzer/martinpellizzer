Aconitum ciliare var. oligotrichum

Aconitum ciliare var. oligotrichum, commonly known as the slender monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive appearance, while the flowers are arranged in a raceme.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it contains toxic alkaloids.
