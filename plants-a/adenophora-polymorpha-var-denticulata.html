Adenophora polymorpha var. denticulata

Adenophora polymorpha var. denticulata, commonly known as the toothed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to lavender flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
