Acer negundo f. argenteonotatum

Acer negundo f. argenteonotatum, commonly known as the silver-leaf silver maple, is a cultivar of the silver maple tree characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on the leaves.

This variation exhibits a more pronounced variegation compared to the typical silver maple, with leaves that have a striking contrast between their green upper surfaces and white lower surfaces. The plant is native to North America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for adding visual interest to landscapes.
