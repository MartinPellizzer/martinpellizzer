Abutilon giganteum

Abutilon giganteum, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, papery bracts that resemble lanterns, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its flowers are small and yellow, clustered in spikes.

It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
