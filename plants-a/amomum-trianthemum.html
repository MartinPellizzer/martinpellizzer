Amomum trianthemum

Amomum trianthemum, commonly known as wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features a rhizomatous root system and heart-shaped, aromatic leaves that emit a spicy scent. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valued species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
