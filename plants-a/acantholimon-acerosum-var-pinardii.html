Acantholimon acerosum var. pinardii

Acantholimon acerosum var. pinardii, commonly known as the hard-leaved sandthrift, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its distinctive, often blue to purple flowers. The plant has rigid, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, providing it with a gritty texture. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun.

This species is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
