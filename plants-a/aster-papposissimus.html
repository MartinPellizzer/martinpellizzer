Aster papposissimus

Aster papposissimus, commonly known as the common blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its flowers are small and blue to purple, with a distinctive pappus of bristles that aids in wind dispersal of its seeds.

The plant is often used in restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
