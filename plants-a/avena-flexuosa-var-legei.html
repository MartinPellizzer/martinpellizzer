Avena flexuosa var. legei

Avena flexuosa var. legei, commonly known as the creeping oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender, often prostrate stems that form dense mats. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are small and clustered closely together.

This variety is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient plant in challenging conditions.
