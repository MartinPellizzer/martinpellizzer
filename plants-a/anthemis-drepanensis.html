Anthemis drepanensis

Anthemis drepanensis, commonly known as the Greek tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Anthemis drepanensis is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
