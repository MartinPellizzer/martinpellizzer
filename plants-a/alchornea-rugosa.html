Alchornea rugosa

Alchornea rugosa, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various environments.

Alchornea rugosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
