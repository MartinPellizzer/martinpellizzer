Astragalus trottianus

Astragalus trottianus, commonly known as the Trott's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus trottianus is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
