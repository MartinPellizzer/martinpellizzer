Avenastrum desertorum

Avenastrum desertorum, commonly known as desert grass or desert oatgrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted habit and narrow, blue-green leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated. Avenastrum desertorum is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a valuable component of desert ecosystems.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
