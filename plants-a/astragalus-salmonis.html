Astragalus salmonis

Astragalus salmonis, commonly known as the salmon milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Astragalus salmonis is also of interest in botanical research due to its potential medicinal properties.
