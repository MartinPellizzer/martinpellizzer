Alchemilla hybrida var. colorata

Alchemilla hybrida var. colorata, commonly known as the golden alchemilla, is a perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense foliage. The plant features small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are typically velvety and green, with a distinctive ability to retain moisture, making it suitable for moist, shady environments.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and easy maintenance.
