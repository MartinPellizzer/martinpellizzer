Achyrospermum axillare

Achyrospermum axillare, commonly known as the velvetleaf or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.

Its rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its success in various environments.
