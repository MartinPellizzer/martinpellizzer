Alkanna tinctoria var. baetica

Alkanna tinctoria var. baetica, commonly known as the crimson root or Spanish saffron, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its deep red, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces a distinctive red-orange root that is rich in alkaloids and has been historically used for its dyeing properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its ornamental value and historical significance make it a notable species in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
