Artemisia capillaris f. sericea

Artemisia capillaris f. sericea, commonly known as silver wormwood, is a variant of the capillary wormwood plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery, finely hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow and linear, with a grayish-green hue due to the dense layer of fine hairs. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal uses.
