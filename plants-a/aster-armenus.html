Aster armenus

Aster armenus, commonly known as Armenian aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
