Allium bischoffii

Allium bischoffii, commonly known as the Bishoff's onion, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Russia. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its bulbous roots are similar to those of other allium species, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Allium bischoffii is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
