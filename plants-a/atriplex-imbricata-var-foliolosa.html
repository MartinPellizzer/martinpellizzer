Atriplex imbricata var. foliolosa

Atriplex imbricata var. foliolosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, making it well adapted to saline and drought-prone soils. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its deep root system allows it to access groundwater, enhancing its survival in harsh environments.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
