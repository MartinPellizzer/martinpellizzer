Aspidium melanostictum

Aspidium melanostictum, commonly known as the black-stained shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, giving them a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky areas, where it can thrive in partial to full shade. Its spores are produced on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are usually smaller and more compact than the sterile ones.

Aspidium melanostictum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
