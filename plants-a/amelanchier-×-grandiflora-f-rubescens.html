Amelanchier × grandiflora f. rubescens

Amelanchier × grandiflora f. rubescens, commonly known as the red-flowering serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring, preceding the foliage. The plant produces edible, dark red to black berries in the summer, which are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, turning yellow or red in the fall.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
