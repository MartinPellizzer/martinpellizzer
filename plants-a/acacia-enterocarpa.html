Acacia enterocarpa

Acacia enterocarpa, commonly known as the black wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often feathery and arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The bark and pods of Acacia enterocarpa have been used traditionally by Indigenous Australians for various purposes.
