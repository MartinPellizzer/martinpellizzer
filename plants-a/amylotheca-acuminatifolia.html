Amylotheca acuminatifolia

Amylotheca acuminatifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved amylotheca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its growth habit is low and spreading, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Amylotheca acuminatifolia is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
