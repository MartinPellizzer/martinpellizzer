Anthemis tomentosa subsp. heracleotica

Anthemis tomentosa subsp.

heracleotica, commonly known as the woolly anthemis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its fuzzy, grayish-green foliage and showy yellow ray flowers with a central disk.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in open habitats such as meadows, slopes, and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
