Arrhenatherum avenaceum var. nodosum

Arrhenatherum avenaceum var. nodosum, commonly known as nodding false oat, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and inflorescences that bear spikelets with nodding, drooping flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained to wet soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
