Allium quercetorum

Allium quercetorum, commonly known as the wood onion or wild leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and grass-like, with a garlic-like aroma when crushed. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in umbels, and bloom in early summer.

Allium quercetorum is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses.
