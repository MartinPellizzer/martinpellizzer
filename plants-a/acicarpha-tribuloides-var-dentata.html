Acicarpha tribuloides var. dentata

Acicarpha tribuloides var. dentata, commonly known as the serrated burrgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, wiry stems and produces small, spiny seed heads that aid in seed dispersal. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas and along roadsides.

The plant's adaptability makes it both a common weed and a potential forage species in certain regions.
