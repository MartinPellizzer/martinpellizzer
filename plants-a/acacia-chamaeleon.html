Acacia chamaeleon

Acacia chamaeleon, commonly known as the dwarf acacia, is a small, shrub-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Acacia chamaeleon is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
