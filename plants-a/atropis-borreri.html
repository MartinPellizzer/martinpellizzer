Atropis borreri

Atropis borreri, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by a disk of tubular flowers, typical of the family. It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to dry conditions with its deep root system.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss.
