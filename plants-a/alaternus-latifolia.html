Alaternus latifolia

Alaternus latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf alaternus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in the summer months. Its roots are known for containing bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional and modern herbal medicine.

Alaternus latifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.
