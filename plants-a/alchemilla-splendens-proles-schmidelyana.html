Alchemilla splendens proles schmidelyana

Alchemilla splendens proles schmidelyana, commonly known as the Splendid Alchemilla, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant features broad, slightly toothed leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady garden settings.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
