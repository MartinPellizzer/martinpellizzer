Avena pubescens var. glabrescens

Avena pubescens var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous oat, is a variant of the oat species characterized by its reduced pubescence.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae, typically found in temperate regions. The plant produces slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet-bearing panicle, which contains both male and female flowers.

This variant is often studied for its genetic traits and potential agricultural applications.
