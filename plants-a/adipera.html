Adipera

Adipera, commonly known as the buttercup plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Ranunculaceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, often yellow or orange flowers with a distinct central stigma. Adipera species typically have simple, alternate leaves that are usually lobed or toothed. They are found in various habitats, including wetlands, meadows, and forested areas.

Many species are adapted to moist environments and can thrive in partial to full shade.
