Andromeda polifolia subsp. glaucophylla

Andromeda polifolia subsp.

glaucophylla, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to coastal regions of western North America. It features small, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its salty, windy environment.

The plant produces clusters of pinkish-white flowers in late spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and dunes.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh coastal conditions, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
