Anthemis mazandaranica

Anthemis mazandaranica, commonly known as the Mazandaran tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flower heads that are often mistaken for a single flower, consisting of numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it an attractive addition to botanical collections and herbal gardens.
