Arida parviflora

Arida parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered arida, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. Arida parviflora is often found in sandy soils and is well-suited to low-nutrient conditions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
