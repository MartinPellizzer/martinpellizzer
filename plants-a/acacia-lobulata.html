Acacia lobulata

Acacia lobulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features feathery, bipinnate leaves that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant.

Acacia lobulata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
