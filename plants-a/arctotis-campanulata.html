Arctotis campanulata

Arctotis campanulata, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small bells. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Arctotis campanulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
