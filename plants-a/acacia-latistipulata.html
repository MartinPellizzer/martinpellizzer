Acacia latistipulata

Acacia latistipulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching habit and its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and can remain viable for several years.

Acacia latistipulata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of local ecosystems.
