Adenophora potaninii

Adenophora potaninii, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Adenophora potaninii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
