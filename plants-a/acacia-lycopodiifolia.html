Acacia lycopodiifolia

Acacia lycopodiifolia, commonly known as the fern-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage, which consists of narrow, bipinnate leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in compact, globular inflorescences, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Acacia lycopodiifolia is adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system to access water.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
