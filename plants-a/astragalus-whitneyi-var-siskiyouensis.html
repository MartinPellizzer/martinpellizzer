Astragalus whitneyi var. siskiyouensis

Astragalus whitneyi var. siskiyouensis, commonly known as Siskiyou milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to poor soil conditions.

This variety is part of the legume family and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid environments.
