Amphirhapis zeylanica

Amphirhapis zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, heartwood that is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves with a smooth surface and a strong, spicy scent. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers followed by dark blue, spherical berries.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties and has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
