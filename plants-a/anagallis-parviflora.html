Anagallis parviflora

Anagallis parviflora, commonly known as the slender pink or small-flower waterpoket, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along streambanks and wet meadows. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers with five petals, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Anagallis parviflora is often found in wet habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
