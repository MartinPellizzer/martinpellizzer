Astragalus arietinus

Astragalus arietinus, commonly known as the horned milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny pods and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, and it is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Astragalus arietinus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
