Andresia

Andresia, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their unique, often strikingly colored flowers that resemble orchids. They are typically found in rocky or mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils. The genus includes several species, each with distinct floral structures and growth habits.

Andresia plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are often cultivated in gardens with specific horticultural care.
