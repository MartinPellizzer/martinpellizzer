Adicea hilariana

Adicea hilariana, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rapid growth habit makes it both a popular ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in non-native habitats.
