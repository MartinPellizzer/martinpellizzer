Arum euxinum

Arum euxinum, commonly known as the Crimean arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features a distinctive arrow-shaped leaf that is usually dark green with a reddish or purple tint on the underside. The plant produces a spadix, which is surrounded by a showy, spatulate inflorescence that resembles a flower. Arum euxinum is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
