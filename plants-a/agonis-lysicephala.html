Agonis lysicephala

Agonis lysicephala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agarik, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Agonis lysicephala thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
