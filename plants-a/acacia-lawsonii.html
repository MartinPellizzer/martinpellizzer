Acacia lawsonii

Acacia lawsonii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Acacia lawsonii is also a symbol of Australia and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value.
