Acacia sedifolia subsp. sedifolia

Acacia sedifolia subsp.

sedifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
