Asprella hexandra

Asprella hexandra, commonly known as the African wild bean, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Asprella hexandra make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
