Acaena × anserovina

Acaena × anserovina, commonly known as the silverweed or New Zealand spinach, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, spiky fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is native to New Zealand and is often found in moist, temperate environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a silver-white underside that gives it its common name.

Acaena × anserovina is valued for its nutritional content and is sometimes cultivated as a leafy vegetable.
