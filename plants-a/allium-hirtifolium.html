Allium hirtifolium

Allium hirtifolium, commonly known as the hairy-leaved garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and clusters of star-shaped blooms.
