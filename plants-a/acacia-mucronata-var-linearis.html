Acacia mucronata var. linearis

Acacia mucronata var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which are typically followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for dryland restoration.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in degraded areas.
