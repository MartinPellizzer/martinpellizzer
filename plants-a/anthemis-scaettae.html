Anthemis scaettae

Anthemis scaettae, commonly known as the Scaetta's chamomile, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and finely divided, aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal remedies due to their mild aromatic properties.
