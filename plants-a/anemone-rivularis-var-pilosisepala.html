Anemone rivularis var. pilosisepala

Anemone rivularis var. pilosisepala, commonly known as the hairy-sepaled water anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, hairy sepals that distinguish it from other anemone species. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often along the edges of ponds and streams. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a waxy surface, and it produces numerous small, yellowish flowers in early spring.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
