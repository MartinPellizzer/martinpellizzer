Alonsoa soukupii

Alonsoa soukupii, commonly known as the Klamath mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Alonsoa soukupii is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along streams, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardening.
