Acanthus caroli-alexandri

Acanthus caroli-alexandri, commonly known as the Alexandrian acanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, spire-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, bloom in summer and attract various pollinators.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
