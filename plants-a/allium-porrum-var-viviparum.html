Allium porrum var. viviparum

Allium porrum var. viviparum, commonly known as the wild leek, is a botanical variant of the common leek.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, garlic-scented leaves and a single, edible, bulbous stem. This plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded woodland areas. It produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant is valued for its mild, onion-like flavor and has been used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
