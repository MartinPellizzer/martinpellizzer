Allium jichouense

Allium jichouense, commonly known as the Jichou onion, is a bulbous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, narrow leaves and spherical bulbs. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in late summer, which are typically arranged in a loose umbel. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value and potential culinary uses, it is gaining popularity among horticulturists and gardeners.
