Acaena adenocarpa var. undiquearmata

Acaena adenocarpa var. undiquearmata, commonly known as the New Zealand spinach bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces berries that are typically red or orange when mature, containing seeds that are dispersed by animals. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

This species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
