Alopecurus pratensis subsp. arundinaceus

Alopecurus pratensis subsp.

arundinaceus, commonly known as soft couch grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish or brownish flowers in dense spikes, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its role in ecological habitats and as a forage plant for livestock.
