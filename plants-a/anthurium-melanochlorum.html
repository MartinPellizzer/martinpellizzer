Anthurium melanochlorum

Anthurium melanochlorum, commonly known as the black chlorophyll anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that often have a metallic sheen, and its vibrant red spadix surrounded by a spathaceous bract. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden beneath the leafy foliage. It thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements have made it a favored ornamental plant among enthusiasts.
