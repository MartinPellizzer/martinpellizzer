Achillea millefoliata

Achillea millefoliata, commonly known as yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features feathery, finely divided leaves and flat, clustered flower heads that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties.

Yarrow is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
