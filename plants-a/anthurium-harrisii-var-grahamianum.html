Anthurium harrisii var. grahamianum

Anthurium harrisii var. grahamianum, commonly known as the Graham's anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped bracts that range from deep red to orange, often contrasting with its glossy, dark green foliage. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat, and thrives in high humidity and indirect light. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, but the showy bracts serve as an adaptation to attract pollinators.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in indoor environments with proper care.
