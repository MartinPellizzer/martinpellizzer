Allium materculae var. albiflorum

Allium materculae var. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered garlic chives, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, bulbous structures similar to garlic, which are used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and mild garlic scent.
