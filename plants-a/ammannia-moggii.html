Ammannia moggii

Ammannia moggii, commonly known as the red sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to hot, dry climates and can store water in its thick, fleshy leaves and stems. It often grows in rocky or sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Ammannia moggii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
