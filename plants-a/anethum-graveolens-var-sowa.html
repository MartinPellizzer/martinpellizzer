Anethum graveolens var. sowa

Anethum graveolens var. sowa, commonly known as Sowa coriander, is a variant of the coriander plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round seeds that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Sowa coriander is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and seeds, which have a distinct, slightly bitter flavor.

It thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice in high-altitude regions.
