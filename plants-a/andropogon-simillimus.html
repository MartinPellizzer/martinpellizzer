Andropogon simillimus

Andropogon simillimus, commonly known as the mimic grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering structure consists of spikelets that produce small, inconspicuous flowers, which are pollinated by wind.

Andropogon simillimus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
