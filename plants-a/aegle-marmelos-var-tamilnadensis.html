Aegle marmelos var. tamilnadensis

Aegle marmelos var. tamilnadensis, commonly known as the Indian bael or wood apple, is a tropical tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves. The tree produces a large, fleshy fruit with a thick, woody pulp that contains numerous seeds. The fruit is widely used in traditional medicine and cuisine, valued for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
