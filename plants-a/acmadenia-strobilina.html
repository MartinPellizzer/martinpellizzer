Acmadenia strobilina

Acmadenia strobilina, commonly known as the mountain paperbark, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark that peels away in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, with a bluish-green hue.

Acmadenia strobilina is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.
