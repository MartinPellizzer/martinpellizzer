Anemonoides ranunculoides

Anemonoides ranunculoides, commonly known as the meadow anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features simple, palmately lobed leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Anemonoides ranunculoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
