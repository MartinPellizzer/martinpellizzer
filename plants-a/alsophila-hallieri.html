Alsophila hallieri

Alsophila hallieri, commonly known as the Hall's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bright green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Alsophila hallieri plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
