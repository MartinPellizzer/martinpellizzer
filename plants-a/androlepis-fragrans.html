Androlepis fragrans

Androlepis fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and fragrant white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are yellow when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Androlepis fragrans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant aroma.
