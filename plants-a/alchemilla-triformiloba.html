Alchemilla triformiloba

Alchemilla triformiloba, commonly known as the three-lobed lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of soft, hairy leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Alchemilla triformiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
