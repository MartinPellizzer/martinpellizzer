Astragalus Brunetianus

Astragalus brunetianus is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family and is known for its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in late summer. This species thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered vulnerable to extinction. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this unique and ecologically important plant.
