Alchemilla egens

Alchemilla egens, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in loose panicles. Alchemilla egens thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its leaves are used in traditional herbal medicine and are also popular in ornamental gardening for their attractive appearance.
