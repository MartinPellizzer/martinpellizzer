Arnica cana

Arnica cana, commonly known as mountain arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear yellow-orange flowers arranged in a raceme. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its roots are fibrous and branched. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Arnica cana is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
