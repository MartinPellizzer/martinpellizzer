Agave longipes

Agave longipes, commonly known as the longleaf agave, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces striking white to pale blue flowers in terminal panicles, which bloom after several years. It is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and slow growth, it is often cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
