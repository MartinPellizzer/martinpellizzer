Amaranthus macrostachyus

Amaranthus macrostachyus, commonly known as the giant lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish flowers that are often mistaken for grasses rather than true flowering plants. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry conditions.

Its fibrous stalks have been used traditionally for thatching and weaving in some regions.
