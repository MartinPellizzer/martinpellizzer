Acacia paraneura

Acacia paraneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spherical clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods. Acacia paraneura is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought resistance through its deep root system and reduced leaf surface area.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
