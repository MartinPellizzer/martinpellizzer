Acacia juliflora

Acacia juliflora, commonly known as the desert wattle, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, feathery leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow globular flowers, which are arranged in dense, compact inflorescences. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to access groundwater.

Acacia juliflora is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
