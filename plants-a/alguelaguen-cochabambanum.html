Alguelaguen cochabambanum

Alguelaguen cochabambanum, commonly known as the Cochabamba thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Andean region of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and dense, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in rocky or arid soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
