Atadinus imeretinus

Atadinus imeretinus, commonly known as the Imeretian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region, particularly Georgia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often lance-shaped, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.
