Ageratum humile

Ageratum humile, commonly known as flossflower, is an annual flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers in shades of white, purple, and blue. The plant features narrow, slightly hairy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Ageratum humile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in mixed flower arrangements.
