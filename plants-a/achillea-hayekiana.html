Achillea hayekiana

Achillea hayekiana, commonly known as Hayek's yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.

Its name honors the botanist Hamed Hayek, who contributed significantly to the study of Arab flora.
