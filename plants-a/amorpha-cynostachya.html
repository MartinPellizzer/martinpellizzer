Amorpha cynostachya

Amorpha cynostachya, commonly known as the yellow false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets, which are typically a deep green color. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange racemes of flowers in the spring, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Amorpha cynostachya is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
