Anachyris

Anachyris, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, blue or purple flower heads that resemble holly berries. The plants typically grow in coastal areas, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. Anachyris species are often found in the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate salty and windy conditions.
