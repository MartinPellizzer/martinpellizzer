Acer negundo

Acer negundo, commonly known as the box elder, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It belongs to the maple family (Aceraceae) and is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement and three to five lobes on its leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. It is often found in moist soils along rivers and streams, and it can grow in a variety of habitats including urban environments.

The box elder is also notable for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate pollution, making it a common sight in city landscapes.
