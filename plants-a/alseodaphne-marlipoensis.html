Alseodaphne marlipoensis

Alseodaphne marlipoensis, commonly known as the Marlipo laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurel, avocado, and cinnamon plants. The tree is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
