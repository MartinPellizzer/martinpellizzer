Alsine lanceolata f. condensata

Alsine lanceolata f. condensata, commonly known as the denseleaf sandwort, is a compact, low-growing plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It features small, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a tight rosette. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils in dry, open habitats.

This variety is notable for its dense foliage and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
