Astragalus nanshanicus

Astragalus nanshanicus, commonly known as the Nan Shan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
