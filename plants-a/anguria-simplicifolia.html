Anguria simplicifolia

Anguria simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaved cucumber, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions.

It features elongated, cucurbit-like fruits that are typically green and edible. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or lance-shaped. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical classification places it within the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like pumpkins and melons.
