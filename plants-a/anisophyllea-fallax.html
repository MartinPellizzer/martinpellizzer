Anisophyllea fallax

Anisophyllea fallax, commonly known as the silver fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bioluminescent leaves that emit a faint blue glow at night. The plant's foliage consists of two types of leaves—stipitate and sessile—that are arranged in a unique alternating pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject in both horticulture and scientific research.
