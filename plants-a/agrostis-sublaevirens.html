Agrostis sublaevirens

Agrostis sublaevirens, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and wetlands. The plant has slender, wiry stems that often lie flat on the ground, forming dense mats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Agrostis sublaevirens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
