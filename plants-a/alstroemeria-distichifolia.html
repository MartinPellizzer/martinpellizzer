Alstroemeria distichifolia

Alstroemeria distichifolia, commonly known as the Chilean lily, is a perennial herb native to the Andes region of South America.

It features striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Alstroemeria distichifolia is also valued for its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
