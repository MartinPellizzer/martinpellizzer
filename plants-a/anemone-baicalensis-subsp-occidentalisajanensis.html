Anemone baicalensis subsp. occidentalisajanensis

Anemone baicalensis subsp.

occidentalisajanensis, commonly known as the Western Baikal Anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has palmately divided, serrated leaves that are typically hairy beneath. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
