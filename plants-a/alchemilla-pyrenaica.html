Alchemilla pyrenaica

Alchemilla pyrenaica, commonly known as Pyrenean lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in loose clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild astringent properties.

Alchemilla pyrenaica thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or alpine environments.
