Abies × vasconcellosiana

Abies × vasconcellosiana, commonly known as the Andes fir, is a hybrid conifer native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a cross between Abies excelsa and Abies magellanica, resulting in a tree with distinctive botanical features. The species exhibits needle-like leaves that are dark green and often have a bluish tinge, and it produces cones that are typically pendulous. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
