Achillea kamelinii

Achillea kamelinii, commonly known as Kamelin's yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its slender stems and feathery leaves are complemented by small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

Achillea kamelinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
