Astragalus muradicus

Astragalus muradicus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed or barren areas.
