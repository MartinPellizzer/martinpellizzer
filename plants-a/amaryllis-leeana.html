Amaryllis leeana

Amaryllis leeana, commonly known as the Cape amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Amaryllis leeana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ability to store water in its bulb allows it to survive in dry conditions, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
