Asterothamnus centrali-asiaticus var. procerior

Asterothamnus centrali-asiaticus var. procerior, commonly known as the tall central Asian aster, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

The showy yellow flowers appear in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
