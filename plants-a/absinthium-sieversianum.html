Absinthium sieversianum

Absinthium sieversianum, commonly known as Sievers' wormwood, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are aromatic and contain compounds that have historically been used in herbal medicine.

Absinthium sieversianum is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
