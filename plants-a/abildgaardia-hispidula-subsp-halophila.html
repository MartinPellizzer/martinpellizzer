Abildgaardia hispidula subsp. halophila

Abildgaardia hispidula subsp.

halophila, commonly known as the saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline environments. It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and erect, branching stems.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and saline soils, demonstrating high tolerance to salt stress. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish or reddish in color.

This subspecies is of ecological importance in stabilizing soils in arid and coastal regions.
