Agropyron repens f. loliiforme

Agropyron repens f. loliiforme, commonly known as the creeping wheatgrass, is a variant of the common wheatgrass.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in disturbed areas, lawns, and along roadsides.

Its foliage is typically a bluish-green color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.
