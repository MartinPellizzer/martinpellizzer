Acrocephalus erectifolius

Acrocephalus erectifolius, commonly known as the upright-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common species in marshes and pond edges.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
