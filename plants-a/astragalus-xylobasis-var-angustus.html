Astragalus xylobasis var. angustus

Astragalus xylobasis var. angustus, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, angular stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are compound, with narrow, linear leaflets, and it produces small, hard seeds in pods.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
