Artemisia caudata var. douglasiana

Artemisia caudata var. douglasiana, commonly known as Douglas's sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features silvery-gray foliage with aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy, drought-tolerant species often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.
