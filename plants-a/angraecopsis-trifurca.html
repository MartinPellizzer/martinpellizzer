Angraecopsis trifurca

Angraecopsis trifurca, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of star-shaped flowers that bloom in a striking array of colors, often white or pale purple. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific moths, showcasing a fascinating example of co-evolution.

Angraecopsis trifurca is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and rarity in cultivation.
