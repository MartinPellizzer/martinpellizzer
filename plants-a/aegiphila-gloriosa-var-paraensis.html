Aegiphila gloriosa var. paraensis

Aegiphila gloriosa var. paraensis, commonly known as the Paraense beautyberry, is a shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the rainforest ecosystem.
