Acacia anfractuosa

Acacia anfractuosa, commonly known as the zigzag acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging branches and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Acacia anfractuosa is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
