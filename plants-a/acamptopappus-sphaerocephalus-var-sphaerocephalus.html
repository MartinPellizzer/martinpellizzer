Acamptopappus sphaerocephalus var. sphaerocephalus

Acamptopappus sphaerocephalus var. sphaerocephalus, commonly known as the globe-headed acamptopappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
