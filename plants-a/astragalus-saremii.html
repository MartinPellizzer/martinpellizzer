Astragalus saremii

Astragalus saremii, commonly known as the Sarem milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale pink flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus saremii plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its native habitat.
