Antirrhinum orontium var. microcarpum

Antirrhinum orontium var. microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited snapdragon, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that splits open to release tiny seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
