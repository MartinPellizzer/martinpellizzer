Aberia mollis var. lanceolata

Aberia mollis var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf evergreen holly, is a variant of the evergreen holly species characterized by its lance-shaped leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States, typically found in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its foliage remains dense and glossy throughout the growing season, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
