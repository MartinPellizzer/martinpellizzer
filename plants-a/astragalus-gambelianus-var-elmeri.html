Astragalus gambelianus var. elmeri

Astragalus gambelianus var. elmeri, commonly known as the Elmer's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
