Atalantia bilocularis

Atalantia bilocularis, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and dense, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red, edible fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The Natal plum is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
