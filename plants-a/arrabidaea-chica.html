Arrabidaea chica

Arrabidaea chica, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink orchid, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its roots form a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, aiding in nutrient absorption.

The plant's unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a popular ornamental species in horticulture.
