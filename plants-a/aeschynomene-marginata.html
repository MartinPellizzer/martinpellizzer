Aeschynomene marginata

Aeschynomene marginata, commonly known as the silverleaf aeschynomene, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are narrow and often have a silvery or grayish margin, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

This plant is also valued for its potential use in forage and erosion control.
