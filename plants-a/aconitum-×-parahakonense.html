Aconitum × parahakonense

Aconitum × parahakonense, commonly known as the hybrid aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafy stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple spires of flowers in late summer, which are composed of numerous small, petal-like sepals. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a coarse, hairy texture, and are typically arranged alternately along the stem.

Aconitum × parahakonense is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
