Adiantum pauperculum

Adiantum pauperculum, commonly known as the poor fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically dark green with a silvery underside. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Adiantum pauperculum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ease of cultivation.
