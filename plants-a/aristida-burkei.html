Aristida burkei

Aristida burkei, commonly known as the Burke's dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Aristida burkei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
