Alchemilla schlechteri

Alchemilla schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a panicle during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Alchemilla schlechteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
