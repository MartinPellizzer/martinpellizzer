Anemone vitifolia var. genuina

Anemone vitifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the grapeleaf anemone, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
