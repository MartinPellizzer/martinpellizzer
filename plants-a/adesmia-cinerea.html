Adesmia cinerea

Adesmia cinerea, commonly known as silverleaf desmodium, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, pea-like flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain several small, round seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some traditional herbal remedies.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
