Aster camporum

Aster camporum, commonly known as the plains asters, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies and meadows, thriving in well-drained soils. Its showy purple to white flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster camporum is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological value and adaptability.
