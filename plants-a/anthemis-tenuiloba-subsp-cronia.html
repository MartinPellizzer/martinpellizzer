Anthemis tenuiloba subsp. cronia

Anthemis tenuiloba subsp.

cronia, commonly known as the narrowleaf pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
