Agropyron trachycaulum f. caninoides

Agropyron trachycaulum f. caninoides, commonly known as the fescue grass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense foliage.

It typically features narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture and a bluish-green coloration. The plant produces spikelets that are often arranged in panicles, with small, two-lobed glumes and lemma-like structures. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is commonly found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moderate climates.
