Aquilegia zapateri

Aquilegia zapateri, commonly known as the Zapater's Columbine, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred petals that come in shades of red, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces seed pods that split open to release its small, winged seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
