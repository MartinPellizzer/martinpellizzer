Aerides ampullacea

Aerides ampullacea, commonly known as the orchid bell, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a white lip. The plant produces aerial roots and thrives in humid, shaded environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique floral structure.

The flowers bloom in clusters and can last for several weeks, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
