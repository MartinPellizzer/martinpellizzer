Adiantum chilense var. chilense

Adiantum chilense var. chilense, commonly known as the Chilean maidenhair fern, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a fern belonging to the Adiantaceae family, characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive blackish midrib that contrasts with the bright green leaflets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
