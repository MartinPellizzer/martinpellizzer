Acalypha parvifolia

Acalypha parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, small leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It typically grows as an herbaceous annual or a short-lived perennial.

Acalypha parvifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
