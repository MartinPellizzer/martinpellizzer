Arctium pavlovii

Arctium pavlovii, commonly known as the Pavlov's burdock, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, hairy stem with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, producing small, spiky fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Arctium pavlovii is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
