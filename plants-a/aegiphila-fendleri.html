Aegiphila fendleri

Aegiphila fendleri, commonly known as the Desert Balm, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant has small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It is well adapted to hot, dry environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities for its purported healing properties.

Morphology

Aegiphila fendleri has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, with a gray-green color and a rough, hairy texture.

The stem is slender, upright, and has a grayish-brown bark with a slightly rough texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, with white or pale pink petals, blooming in spring and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a dry capsule that releases small, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, adapted to anchor the plant in rocky or sandy soils.
