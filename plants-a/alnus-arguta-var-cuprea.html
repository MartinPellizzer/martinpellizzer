Alnus arguta var. cuprea

Alnus arguta var. cuprea, commonly known as the red alder, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

This species plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
