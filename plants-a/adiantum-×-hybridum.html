Adiantum × hybridum

Adiantum × hybridum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a hybrid species within the Adiantum genus, characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds.

It typically features bipinnate leaves with finely divided segments, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or near water sources. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Adiantum × hybridum is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
