Achyranthes albida

Achyranthes albida, commonly known as white knotweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, which are characteristic of the Polygonaceae family.

The plant's roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
