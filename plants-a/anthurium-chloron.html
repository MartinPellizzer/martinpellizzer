Anthurium chloron

Anthurium chloron, commonly known as the green anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with some varieties displaying subtle variegation. The plant produces striking spadices, which are modified leaves that enclose the actual flowers, often in shades of red or orange. Anthurium chloron thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
