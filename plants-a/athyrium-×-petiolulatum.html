Athyrium × petiolulatum

Athyrium × petiolulatum, commonly known as the lace fern, is a hybrid species within the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and help it spread vegetatively.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
