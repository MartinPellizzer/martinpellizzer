Astragalus bashkalensis

Astragalus bashkalensis, commonly known as the Bashkortostan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its seeds are typically small and are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across its native range.
