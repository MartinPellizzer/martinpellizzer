Adenophyllum yecoranum

Adenophyllum yecoranum, commonly known as the Yecora cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. Its structure includes areoles, which are specialized structures that bear spines, flowers, and sometimes branches.

Adenophyllum yecoranum plays a role in its ecosystem by providing shelter and food for various desert animals.
