Anthemis palaestina subsp. amblyolepis

Anthemis palaestina subsp.

amblyolepis, commonly known as the Palestinian Anthemis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Israel and Palestine. It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish to pale blue flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

The subspecies amblyolepis is distinguished by its smaller, more rounded leaves compared to the typical form of Anthemis palaestina.
