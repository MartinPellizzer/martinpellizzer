Ailanthus japonica

Ailanthus japonica, commonly known as the Japanese tree of heaven, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with 10 to 20 pairs of oval, serrated leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged fruits that resemble samaras. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive in non-native regions.
