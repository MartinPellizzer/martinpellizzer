Aconitum pterocaule var. pterocaule

Aconitum pterocaule var. pterocaule, commonly known as the winged monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and striking blue-violet flowers arranged in a raceme. The plant has palmately divided, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum pterocaule var. pterocaule is known for its toxicity, containing potent alkaloids that make it dangerous to humans and animals.
