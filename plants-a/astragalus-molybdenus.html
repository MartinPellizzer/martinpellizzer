Astragalus molybdenus

Astragalus molybdenus, commonly known as the leadplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and woodland habitats. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus molybdenus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
