Allium fistulosum var. caespitosum

Allium fistulosum var. caespitosum, commonly known as curly garlic chives, is a variant of the chive plant characterized by its dense, curling foliage.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to temperate regions of Asia. The plant produces small, white, bulbous flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are used in culinary applications for their mild garlic flavor.

It is often grown as an ornamental and edible plant in gardens and landscapes.
