Annesorhiza elata

Annesorhiza elata, commonly known as the tall sand-verbena, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and clustered in dense spikes, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Annesorhiza elata is adapted to withstand drought and is often found in open habitats such as plains and foothills.
