Adesmia glareosa

Adesmia glareosa, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its seeds are encased in pods and can remain viable in the soil for extended periods.

Adesmia glareosa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for local wildlife.
