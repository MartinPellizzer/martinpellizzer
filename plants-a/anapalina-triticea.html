Anapalina triticea

Anapalina triticea, commonly known as the slender wild rye, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize open areas.

Anapalina triticea is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
