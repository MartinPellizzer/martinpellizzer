Agrimonia convergens

Agrimonia convergens, commonly known as the golden root, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its roots are used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Agrimonia convergens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
