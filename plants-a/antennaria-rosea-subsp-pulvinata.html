Antennaria rosea subsp. pulvinata

Antennaria rosea subsp.

pulvinata, commonly known as the purple sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and hairy, often purple-tinged stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important component of its native habitat.
