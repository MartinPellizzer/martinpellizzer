Aconitum nemorosum

Aconitum nemorosum, commonly known as the wood anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has palmately lobed leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring.

Aconitum nemorosum is also known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
