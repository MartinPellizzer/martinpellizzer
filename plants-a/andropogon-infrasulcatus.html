Andropogon infrasulcatus

Andropogon infrasulcatus, commonly known as narrow-ribbed bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with distinct longitudinal veins and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a tall, erect stem that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
