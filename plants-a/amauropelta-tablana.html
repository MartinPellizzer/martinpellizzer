Amauropelta tablana

Amauropelta tablana, commonly known as the Tabana shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically leathery and dark green in color. The plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

The fronds are divided into small, serrated segments, giving it a unique and intricate appearance.
