Acacia setulifera

Acacia setulifera, commonly known as the hairy acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, globular pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia setulifera thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions with a warm, arid climate.

It is also valued for its role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
