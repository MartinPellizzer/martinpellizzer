Achnatherum roshevitzii

Achnatherum roshevitzii, commonly known as Roshevitz's needlegrass, is a grass species native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are often hairy.

Achnatherum roshevitzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid regions.
