Acacia confusa

Acacia confusa, commonly known as the Chinese flame acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and feathery, bipinnate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Acacia confusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
