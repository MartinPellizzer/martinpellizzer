Anthoxanthum aculeatum

Anthoxanthum aculeatum, commonly known as the prickly oat, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and sharp, bristle-tipped leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale yellow to brownish flowers. It is typically found in dry, disturbed soils and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Anthoxanthum aculeatum is adapted to harsh environments and can thrive in regions with low moisture and nutrient-poor conditions.
