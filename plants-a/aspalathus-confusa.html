Aspalathus confusa

Aspalathus confusa, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, hard pods that contain seeds. It is often found in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Aspalathus confusa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine.
