Acacia psoralea

Acacia psoralea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, feathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which bloom in spring. Acacia psoralea is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species.

It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
