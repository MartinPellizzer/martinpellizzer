Astragalus megricus

Astragalus megricus, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to brownish flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh, dry climates. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in soil stabilization due to its deep root system.

Astragalus megricus is also valued for its ornamental appeal in certain horticultural settings.
