Asyneuma woronowii

Asyneuma woronowii, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous annual with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Asyneuma woronowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
