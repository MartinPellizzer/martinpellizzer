Arctostaphylos glauca

Arctostaphylos glauca, commonly known as the gray mountain maple, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a waxy, grayish-white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, red-orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Arctostaphylos glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to contribute to local ecosystems.
