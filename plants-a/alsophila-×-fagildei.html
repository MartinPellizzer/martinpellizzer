Alsophila × fagildei

Alsophila × fagildei, commonly known as the hybrid tree fern, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Alsophila costaricana and Alsophila semipinnata.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically dark green and can reach lengths of up to several meters, contributing to its ornamental value.

Alsophila × fagildei is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant due to its unique beauty and adaptability.
