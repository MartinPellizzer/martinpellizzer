Aeollanthus claessensii

Aeollanthus claessensii, commonly known as the Cape mountain aloes, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
