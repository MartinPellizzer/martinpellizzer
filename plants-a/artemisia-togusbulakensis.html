Artemisia togusbulakensis

Artemisia togusbulakensis, commonly known as the Togusbulak wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and steppe habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
