Ageratina huahuapana

Ageratina huahuapana, commonly known as the Huahuapana blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, fluffy blue or purple flowers that resemble mist, hence its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Ageratina huahuapana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
