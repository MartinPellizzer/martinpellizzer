Aster puniceus var. vimineus

Aster puniceus var. vimineus, commonly known as the creeping aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, vine-like stems that sprawl along the ground, often rooting at the nodes. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
