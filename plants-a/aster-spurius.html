Aster spurius

Aster spurius, commonly known as the common blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as fields and roadsides, and it produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. Aster spurius has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain insects.
