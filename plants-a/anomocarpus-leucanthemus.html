Anomocarpus leucanthemus

Anomocarpus leucanthemus, commonly known as the white star lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its tuberous roots, which store nutrients and help it survive in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in various regions with a Mediterranean climate.
