Anacolosa heptandra

Anacolosa heptandra, commonly known as the seven-angled acanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins and a hairy texture. Its flowers are typically blue or violet, arranged in spikes, and attract various pollinators.

Anacolosa heptandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
