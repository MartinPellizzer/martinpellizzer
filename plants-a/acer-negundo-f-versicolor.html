Acer negundo f. versicolor

Acer negundo f. versicolor, commonly known as the variable maple, is a deciduous tree characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features palmately lobed leaves that vary in color throughout the growing season, often displaying shades of green, yellow, and red. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Its adaptability makes it a common ornamental and shade tree in various landscapes.
