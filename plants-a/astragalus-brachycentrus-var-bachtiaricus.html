Astragalus brachycentrus var. bachtiaricus

Astragalus brachycentrus var. bachtiaricus, commonly known as the Bachtiari milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural studies.
