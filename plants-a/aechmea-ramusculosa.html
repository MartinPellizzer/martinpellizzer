Aechmea ramusculosa

Aechmea ramusculosa, commonly known as the small-leaf air plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a metallic sheen. The plant produces striking red or orange inflorescences that emerge from the center of the rosette. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aechmea ramusculosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea ramusculosa has rosette-shaped leaves that are sword-like, arranged in a compact cluster, with a bluish-green color and a waxy texture.

The plant lacks a traditional stem, instead having a short, thick base that supports the rosette of leaves. Its flowers are clustered in a terminal inflorescence, with violet petals and yellow stamens, blooming during the dry season and pollinated by birds. The fruit is a red, fleshy capsule that disperses seeds by wind and animal consumption.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in rocky or sandy soils.
