Astragalus maroniensis

Astragalus maroniensis, commonly known as the Maroni milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to high-altitude conditions with its compact growth habit. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its survival in harsh climates.

Astragalus maroniensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and ecological significance in its native habitat.
