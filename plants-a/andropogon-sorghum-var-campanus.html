Andropogon sorghum var. campanus

Andropogon sorghum var. campanus, commonly known as the whorled panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
