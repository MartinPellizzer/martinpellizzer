Astragalus incanus var. barrelieri

Astragalus incanus var. barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's goat's beard, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often sparsely branched stems. The plant produces small, yellow to greenish flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, typical of the Astragalus genus. Its leaves are usually pinnately compound, with small, stiff, spine-like leaflets.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to harsh environments and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
