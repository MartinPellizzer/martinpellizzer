Agrostophyllum ridleyanum

Agrostophyllum ridleyanum, commonly known as the Malayan mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It grows on the branches of various host trees, primarily in the rainforests of Southeast Asia. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers. Its fruits are typically berries that contain seeds dispersed by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
