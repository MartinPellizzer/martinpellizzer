Aspilia attenuata

Aspilia attenuata, commonly known as the African flame plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking, red-orange inflorescences that resemble flames. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flower heads. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Aspilia attenuata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
