Astragalus forrestii var. minor

Astragalus forrestii var. minor, commonly known as the lesser Forrest's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters. It produces pods that contain small, hard seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

This species is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
