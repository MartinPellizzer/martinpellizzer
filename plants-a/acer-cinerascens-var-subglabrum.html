Acer cinerascens var. subglabrum

Acer cinerascens var. subglabrum, commonly known as the smooth-leafed maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, smooth-textured leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. Its bark is smooth and greyish-brown, often developing shallow fissures with age.

This species prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian areas and forest edges.
