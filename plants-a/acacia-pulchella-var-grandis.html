Acacia pulchella var. grandis

Acacia pulchella var. grandis, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open forests and woodlands.

The species is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is the floral emblem of Australia.
