Astragalus biovulatus

Astragalus biovulatus, commonly known as the small-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Astragalus biovulatus is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
