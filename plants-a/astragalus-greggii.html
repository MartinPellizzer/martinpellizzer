Astragalus greggii

Astragalus greggii, commonly known as Gregg's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
