Astragalus lobbichleri

Astragalus lobbichleri, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
