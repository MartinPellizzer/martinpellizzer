Acalypha reptans var. glechomifolia

Acalypha reptans var. glechomifolia, commonly known as the creeping nettle or velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and a distinctive reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by three-lobed seed capsules. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively make it an invasive species in some areas.
