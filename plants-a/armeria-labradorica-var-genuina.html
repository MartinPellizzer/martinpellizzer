Armeria labradorica var. genuina

Armeria labradorica var. genuina, commonly known as the Labrador's thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
