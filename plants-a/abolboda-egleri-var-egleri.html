Abolboda egleri var. egleri

Abolboda egleri var. egleri, commonly known as the Caucasian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
