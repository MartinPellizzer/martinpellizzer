Anthephora elegans var. cristata

Anthephora elegans var. cristata, commonly known as the cristate anthephora, is a unique variant of the Anthephora elegans species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, and often branched growth form, which gives it a coral-like appearance. The plant typically features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. It is native to specific regions in South Africa and thrives in sandy, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture, though it remains a rare and specialized plant.
