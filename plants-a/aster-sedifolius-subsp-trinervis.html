Aster sedifolius subsp. trinervis

Aster sedifolius subsp.

trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with three prominent veins. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing purple to blue petals that attract pollinators.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely dissected leaves and compact growth habit.
