Acantholimon stanjukoviczii

Acantholimon stanjukoviczii, commonly known as the Balkan sandthrift, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to purple in color, and are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Acantholimon stanjukoviczii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
