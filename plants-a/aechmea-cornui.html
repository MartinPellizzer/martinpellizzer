Aechmea cornui

Aechmea cornui, commonly known as the hooded bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater. The plant's foliage is typically dark green with a metallic sheen, and its inflorescence consists of a tall, spiky flower stalk topped with a vibrant red or orange flower. Aechmea cornui thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor ornamental gardening.

Morphology

Aechmea cornui has rosette-shaped leaves that are sword-like, stiff, and covered with fine hairs, arranged in a compact circle around the central stem.

The leaves are typically green with a silvery underside, and they can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant has a short, thick stem that is usually hidden beneath the leaves, with a smooth, pale green bark. It produces striking red or pink bract-like flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, and they are pollinated by insects.

The fruit is a capsule that contains small, black seeds dispersed by wind.
