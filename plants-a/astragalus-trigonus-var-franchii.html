Astragalus trigonus var. franchii

Astragalus trigonus var. franchii, commonly known as the Franchi's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insects.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
