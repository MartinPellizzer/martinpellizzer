Artemisia kurramensis

Artemisia kurramensis, commonly known as the Kurram wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Pakistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
