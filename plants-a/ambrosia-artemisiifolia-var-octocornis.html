Ambrosia artemisiifolia var. octocornis

Ambrosia artemisiifolia var. octocornis, commonly known as ragweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in late summer. The plant is an annual weed that thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. It is known for producing a large amount of pollen, which can cause allergic reactions in humans.

Its ecological impact includes contributing to air pollution and being a significant allergen.
