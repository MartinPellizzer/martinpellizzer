Agave brittoniana subsp. brachypus

Agave brittoniana subsp.

brachypus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The leaves are relatively narrow and have a distinctive waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. It produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, white to pale green flowers.

This species is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
