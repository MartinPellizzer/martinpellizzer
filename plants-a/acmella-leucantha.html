Acmella leucantha

Acmella leucantha, commonly known as the white buttonweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

The flowers produce tiny seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in some regions.
