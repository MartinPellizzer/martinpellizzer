Acalypha apodanthes

Acalypha apodanthes, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Acalypha apodanthes is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability.
