Annona paraensis

Annona paraensis, commonly known as the Brazilian custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that produces a distinctive, fragrant fruit with a thick, leathery skin. The fruit contains a creamy, sweet pulp that is rich in nutrients and has a unique, custard-like texture. Botanically, it belongs to the Annonaceae family and features simple, alternate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its potential medicinal properties.
