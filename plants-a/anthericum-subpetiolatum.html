Anthericum subpetiolatum

Anthericum subpetiolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It features a distinctive spathe that is typically green with a deep purple or reddish hue, surrounding a spadix that bears numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant has strap-like leaves that are narrow and elongated, often with a slight reddish tinge on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

The flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a notable plant in its native habitat.
