Andropogon plumiger

Andropogon plumiger, commonly known as the hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, slender stalks that are often covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue-green flowers in panicles, which are typically visible in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, contributing to its adaptability in various soil conditions.

Andropogon plumiger is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed sites, playing a role in ecological succession and soil stabilization.
