Amaranthus caudatus subsp. mantegazzianus

Amaranthus caudatus subsp.

mantegazzianus, commonly known as the plume amaranth, is a species native to South America. It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and striking, feathery inflorescences that range in color from red to purple.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is also valued for its nutritional content, as its leaves are edible and rich in protein and minerals.

This subspecies is drought-tolerant and can be grown in both temperate and tropical climates.
