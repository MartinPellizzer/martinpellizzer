Aegiphila venezuelensis

Aegiphila venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan shrub, is a small, evergreen plant native to the Andean regions of Venezuela.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
