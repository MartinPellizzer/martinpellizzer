Arabis bijuga

Arabis bijuga, commonly known as the twinleaf rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are pinnately divided into two pairs of leaflets.

Arabis bijuga is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.
