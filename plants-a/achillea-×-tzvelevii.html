Achillea × tzvelevii

Achillea × tzvelevii, commonly known as the Tzvelev's yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, flat-topped clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant features finely divided, silver-green leaves that are aromatic and often covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal and ecological benefits.
