Anthurium harrisii var. intermedium

Anthurium harrisii var. intermedium, commonly known as the red-tipped anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with red or pink veins. The plant produces striking spadices, which are the modified leaves that enclose the true flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium harrisii var. intermedium is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
