Astragalus arianus

Astragalus arianus, commonly known as the Armenian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or white flowers. The plant is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in arid and rocky environments. It also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.

Astragalus arianus is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties and ornamental value.
