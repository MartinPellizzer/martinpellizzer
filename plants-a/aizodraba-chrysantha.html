Aizodraba chrysantha

Aizodraba chrysantha, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, forming a bell-like shape.

Aizodraba chrysantha prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.
