Arum spiculatum

Arum spiculatum, commonly known as the spiny arum, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features a distinctive, arrow-shaped leaf that is often covered in small, spiny protrusions, giving it its common name. The plant produces an inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and greenhouses.
