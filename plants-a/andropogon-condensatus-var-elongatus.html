Andropogon condensatus var. elongatus

Andropogon condensatus var. elongatus, commonly known as tall bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often hairy along the edges. This variant is distinguished by its elongated leaf blades compared to the typical form of Andropogon condensatus. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of prairie and woodland ecosystems.

The plant plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
