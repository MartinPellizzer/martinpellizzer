Asparagus verticillatus var. floribundus

Asparagus verticillatus var. floribundus, commonly known as the flowering asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow in a clumping habit. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, which are lance-shaped and slightly hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms and foliage.
