Argythamnia lundellii

Argythamnia lundellii, commonly known as the Lundell's argythamnia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The yellow flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
