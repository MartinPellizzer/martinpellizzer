Anthemis inconspicua

Anthemis inconspicua, commonly known as the common ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
