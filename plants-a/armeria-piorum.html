Armeria piorum

Armeria piorum, commonly known as the silverleaf thrift, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its succulent leaves help it retain moisture, adapting it to arid environments.

Armeria piorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
