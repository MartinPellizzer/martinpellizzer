Abies pinsapo f. glauca

Abies pinsapo f. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous fir, is a rare and unique variant of the Canary Island fir.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-gray foliage, which gives it a striking appearance in its natural habitat. This plant is native to the Canary Islands and thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. Its cones are typically small and reddish-brown, maturing in the second year.

The species is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique adaptations to its mountainous ecosystem.
