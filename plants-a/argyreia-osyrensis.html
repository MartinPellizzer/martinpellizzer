Argyreia osyrensis

Argyreia osyrensis, commonly known as the sacred lotus of India, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It produces striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive velvety texture. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and woody stems, which can become quite thick over time. Its flowers are typically nocturnal, opening at night and closing in the morning.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various traditional practices.
