Aloysia spathulata

Aloysia spathulata, commonly known as the broadleaf verbena, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Aloysia spathulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
