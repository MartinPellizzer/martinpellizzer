Adenanthos

Adenanthos, commonly known as the honey flower, is a genus of flowering plants native to Australia.

Adenanthos species are typically shrubs or small trees, with aromatic leaves that release a sweet scent when crushed. They thrive in well-drained soils and are often found in coastal and sandy habitats.
