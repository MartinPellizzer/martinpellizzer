Amorphophallus palmiformis

Amorphophallus palmiformis, commonly known as the giant arrowhead plant, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and a striking, often spiky inflorescence that resembles a phallic shape, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces a large, white or purple spathe that encloses a central spadix, which bears small, clustered flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tuberous roots of Amorphophallus palmiformis can be used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
