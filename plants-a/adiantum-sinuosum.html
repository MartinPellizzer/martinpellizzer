Adiantum sinuosum

Adiantum sinuosum, commonly known as the veined maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often arched or drooping. The fronds are typically dark green with a silvery or pale underside, and they have a distinctive, feathery appearance. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
