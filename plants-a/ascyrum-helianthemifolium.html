Ascyrum helianthemifolium

Ascyrum helianthemifolium, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its yellow flowers. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ascyrum helianthemifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
