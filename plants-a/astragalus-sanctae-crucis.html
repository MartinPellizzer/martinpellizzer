Astragalus sanctae-crucis

Astragalus sanctae-crucis, commonly known as the Holy Cross Vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to violet. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its historical and botanical significance, it has been the subject of various scientific studies.
