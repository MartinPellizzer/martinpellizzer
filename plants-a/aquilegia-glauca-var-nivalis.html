Aquilegia glauca var. nivalis

Aquilegia glauca var. nivalis, commonly known as the alpine columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet spurred petals and compound leaves with a distinctive lobed structure. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
