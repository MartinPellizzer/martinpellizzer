Alchemilla sabauda

Alchemilla sabauda, commonly known as the sabauda lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive silver-white, velvety leaves that form a dense rosette. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Alchemilla sabauda is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and its ability to attract beneficial insects.
