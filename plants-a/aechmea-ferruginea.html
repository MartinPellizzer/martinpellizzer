Aechmea ferruginea

Aechmea ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty pineapple, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage that forms a central cup to collect rainwater. The leaves are broad, leathery, and often have a reddish or rusty hue, giving the plant its common name. It produces vibrant orange or red flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette.

This plant is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in indoor environments.

Morphology

Aechmea ferruginea has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, fleshy, and often covered in a layer of fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

The leaves are arranged in a compact rosette, typically reaching lengths of 30 to 50 centimeters, and are a deep green color. The plant has a short, thick stem that is often hidden beneath the leaves, with a smooth, grayish bark. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, typically in shades of red or orange, and bloom during the summer months, relying on wind for pollination.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that disperses its seeds through wind, and the roots are fibrous and adapted to store water.
