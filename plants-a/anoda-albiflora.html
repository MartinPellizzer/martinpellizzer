Anoda albiflora

Anoda albiflora, commonly known as whiteflower false flax, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Anoda albiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
