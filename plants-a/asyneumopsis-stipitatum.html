Asyneumopsis stipitatum

Asyneumopsis stipitatum, commonly known as the clustered leaf cactus, is a unique succulent native to South America.

It features a compact, mound-like growth habit with dense clusters of small, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. Its leaves are adapted for water storage, making it well-suited for arid environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
