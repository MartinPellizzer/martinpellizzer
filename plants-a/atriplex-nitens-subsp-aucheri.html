Atriplex nitens subsp. aucheri

Atriplex nitens subsp.

aucheri, commonly known as the silverleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, waxy hairs that help reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Its deep root system enables it to access groundwater, contributing to its resilience in dry climates.
