Arenaria glochidisperma

Arenaria glochidisperma, commonly known as the sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by rounded, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are typically succulent and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
