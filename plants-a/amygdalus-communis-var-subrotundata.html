Amygdalus communis var. subrotundata

Amygdalus communis var. subrotundata, commonly known as the sweet almond tree, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces edible nuts encased in a hard shell, which are widely used in culinary and cosmetic applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sunlight for optimal growth.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
