Allium borbonicum

Allium borbonicum, commonly known as the Sicilian leek or wild leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of long, narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a tall, cylindrical stem bearing umbels of small, white to pale pink flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic-like flavor, making it a valuable culinary plant.

Allium borbonicum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.
