Anemone cordata

Anemone cordata, commonly known as the heart-shaped anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It features palmate leaves that are distinctly heart-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces showy, white or pink flowers with a central cluster of stamens, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
