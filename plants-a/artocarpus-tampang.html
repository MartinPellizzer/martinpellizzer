Artocarpus tampang

Artocarpus tampang, commonly known as the Tampang or Malabar chestnut, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a unique fruit with a rough, spiny exterior and a soft, edible pulp containing several seeds. The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.
