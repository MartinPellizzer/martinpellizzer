Arum maculatum var. karpatii

Arum maculatum var. karpatii, commonly known as the Karpatian arum, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive mottled pattern of green and white, and produces a spadix surrounded by a showy purple spathe. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its dramatic foliage and unique floral structure. It is a herbaceous perennial that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular in botanical gardens and shade gardens.
