Alchemilla vulgaris subsp. murbeckiana

Alchemilla vulgaris subsp.

murbeckiana, commonly known as lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and appear in clusters on slender stalks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
