Astragalus alitschuri

Astragalus alitschuri, commonly known as the Alaskan milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Astragalus alitschuri plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.

Its ecological significance is notable in supporting local wildlife and maintaining biodiversity in mountainous regions.
