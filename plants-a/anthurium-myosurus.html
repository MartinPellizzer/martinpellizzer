Anthurium myosurus

Anthurium myosurus, commonly known as the mouse ear anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are modified leaves that enclose the small, inconspicuous flowers. Anthurium myosurus thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
