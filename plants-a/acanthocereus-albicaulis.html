Acanthocereus albicaulis

Acanthocereus albicaulis, commonly known as the white-stemmed cactus, is a species native to the deserts of northern Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, pale green stems that often have a bluish tinge. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically nocturnal in bloom. It has spines that are typically white or pale in color, providing camouflage in its arid environment.

This cactus is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with minimal water requirements.
