Aetheolaena otophora var. otophora

Aetheolaena otophora var. otophora, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small bells. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
