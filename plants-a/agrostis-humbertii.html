Agrostis humbertii

Agrostis humbertii, commonly known as Humbert's bentgrass, is a species of grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and gravelly soils. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers.

Agrostis humbertii is often found in disturbed or marginal areas, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
