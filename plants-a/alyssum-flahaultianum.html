Alyssum flahaultianum

Alyssum flahaultianum, commonly known as the Flahault's alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has compact, succulent-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum flahaultianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
