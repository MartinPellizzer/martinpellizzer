Aeschynomene equidiadelpha

Aeschynomene equidiadelpha, commonly known as horsegram or cluster bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round pods containing several seeds, which are used as a pulse crop. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

Aeschynomene equidiadelpha is also used in forage and as a cover crop due to its hardiness and adaptability.
