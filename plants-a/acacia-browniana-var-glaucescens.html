Acacia browniana var. glaucescens

Acacia browniana var. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are covered in a waxy, bluish-white coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
