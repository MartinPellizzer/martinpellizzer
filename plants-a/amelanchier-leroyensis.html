Amelanchier leroyensis

Amelanchier leroyensis, commonly known as the Lespedeza serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 feet and features oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that are dark red to black in color. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for native gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
