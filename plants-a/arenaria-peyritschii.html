Arenaria peyritschii

Arenaria peyritschii, commonly known as Peyritsch's sandwort, is a low-growing, perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically succulent and adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions.

Arenaria peyritschii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
