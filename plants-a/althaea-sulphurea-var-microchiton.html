Althaea sulphurea var. microchiton

Althaea sulphurea var. microchiton, commonly known as the yellow marshmallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils near water bodies such as marshes and wet meadows. The plant features hairy, lobed leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is characterized by its fibrous roots and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
