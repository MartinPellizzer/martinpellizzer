Acinodendron pinetorum

Acinodendron pinetorum, commonly known as the pine tree fern, is a unique plant that combines features of both ferns and conifers.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae, which is typically associated with true pines, though it is not a true conifer. The plant exhibits a tree-like structure with a central trunk and a crown of needle-like leaves, resembling a pine tree. Its fronds are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, cone-like structures for reproduction.

Acinodendron pinetorum is often found in moist, shaded environments, thriving in temperate climates.
