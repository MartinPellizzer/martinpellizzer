Astragalus orientalis

Astragalus orientalis, commonly known as the eastern milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Astragalus orientalis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
