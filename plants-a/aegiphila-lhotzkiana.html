Aegiphila lhotzkiana

Aegiphila lhotzkiana, commonly known as the Lhotzky's aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in whorls, producing a subtle fragrance.

Due to its restricted habitat and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
