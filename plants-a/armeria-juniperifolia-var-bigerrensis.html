Armeria juniperifolia var. bigerrensis

Armeria juniperifolia var. bigerrensis, commonly known as the bigerrenian thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It features compact, evergreen foliage composed of dense, needle-like leaves that resemble those of juniper. The plant produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
