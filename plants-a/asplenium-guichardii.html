Asplenium guichardii

Asplenium guichardii, commonly known as the Guichard's spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, evergreen leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces spores in clusters called sorus, which are located on the underside of the fronds. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or in crevices.

Asplenium guichardii is native to parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia, and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
