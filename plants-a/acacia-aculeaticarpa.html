Acacia aculeaticarpa

Acacia aculeaticarpa, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal areas of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late summer, which are followed by flat, ribbed pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to sandy soils and salt spray, making it a common sight in coastal dunes and scrublands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
