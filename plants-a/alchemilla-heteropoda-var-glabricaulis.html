Alchemilla heteropoda var. glabricaulis

Alchemilla heteropoda var. glabricaulis, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed ladies' mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a waxy, silver underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, typically arranged in clusters atop slender stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
