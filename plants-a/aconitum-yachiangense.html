Aconitum yachiangense

Aconitum yachiangense, commonly known as Yachiang Aconite, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy flowers. The plant produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately compound, with lobed divisions that are often covered in fine hairs.

Aconitum yachiangense contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
