Acanthopsis carduifolia var. glabra

Acanthopsis carduifolia var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth thornweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems covered with soft, hairless foliage. Its small, yellow flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.
