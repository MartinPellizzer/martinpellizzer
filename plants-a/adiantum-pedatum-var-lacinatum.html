Adiantum pedatum var. lacinatum

Adiantum pedatum var. lacinatum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern native to North America.

It features finely divided, lacy fronds that are typically dark green and have a distinctive, almost feather-like appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its fronds are biennial, with the fertile fronds appearing in the second year and producing spores on the underside.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
