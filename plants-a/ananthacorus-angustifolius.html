Ananthacorus angustifolius

Ananthacorus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arrowhead, is a wetland plant characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves and slender, branching stems.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are usually arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in spikes, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
