Acanthophyllum conjungens

Acanthophyllum conjungens, commonly known as the jointed acanthophyllum, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features spiny stems and small, clustered leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its flowers are typically greenish or reddish and appear in dense inflorescences.

Acanthophyllum conjungens is well adapted to sandy soils and can survive in extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
