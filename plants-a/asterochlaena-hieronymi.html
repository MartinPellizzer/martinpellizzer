Asterochlaena hieronymi

Asterochlaena hieronymi, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is cultivated in some regions as a garden plant.
