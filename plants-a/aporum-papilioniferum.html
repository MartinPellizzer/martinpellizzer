Aporum papilioniferum

Aporum papilioniferum, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a floating platform for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces showy, pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators such as beetles. Its roots anchor it in the muddy bottom of still water bodies, while its leaves float on the surface.

Aporum papilioniferum plays a vital role in its ecosystem by offering shelter and oxygen to aquatic life.
