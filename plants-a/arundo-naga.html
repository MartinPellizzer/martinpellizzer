Arundo naga

Arundo naga, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its stiff, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is often found growing along waterways and in wetlands due to its preference for moist soils. It can reach heights of up to 6 meters and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.

Botanically, it is classified under the Poaceae family, which includes other grasses.
