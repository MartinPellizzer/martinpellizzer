Adina cordifolia

Adina cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf adina, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional medicinal use.
