Astragalus thracicus subsp. afyonicus

Astragalus thracicus subsp.

afyonicus, commonly known as the Afyon astragalus, is a perennial herb native to Turkey. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
