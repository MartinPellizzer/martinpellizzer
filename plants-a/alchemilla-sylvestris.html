Alchemilla sylvestris

Alchemilla sylvestris, commonly known as wild lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream sides.

Alchemilla sylvestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
