Aralia maximowiczii

Aralia maximowiczii, commonly known as the Korean angel's trumpet, is a deciduous shrub native to Korea and northeastern China.

It features compound leaves that are pinnately arranged, with each leaflet being ovate and hairy. The plant produces clusters of yellow-green, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a spiny, berry-like structure that matures to a dark red color.

Aralia maximowiczii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
