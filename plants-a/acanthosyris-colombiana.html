Acanthosyris colombiana

Acanthosyris colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian acanthosyris, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
