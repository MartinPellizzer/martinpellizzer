Aspidium musifolium

Aspidium musifolium, commonly known as the musk fern, is a fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Aspidium musifolium is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often used in horticulture.
