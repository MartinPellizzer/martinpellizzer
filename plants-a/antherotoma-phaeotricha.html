Antherotoma phaeotricha

Antherotoma phaeotricha, commonly known as the velvetleaf blister beetle, is a species of beetle that feeds on the leaves of various plants, including velvetleaf and other members of the Malvaceae family.

Its larvae are known to feed on the roots of plants, potentially causing damage to crops. The adult beetle is characterized by its dark, glossy body with a distinct pattern of pale markings. It is often found in agricultural areas where its host plants are cultivated.
