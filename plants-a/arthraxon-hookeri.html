Arthraxon hookeri

Arthraxon hookeri, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grass or hooker's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Arthraxon hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
