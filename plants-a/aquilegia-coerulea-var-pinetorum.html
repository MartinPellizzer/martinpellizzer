Aquilegia coerulea var. pinetorum

Aquilegia coerulea var. pinetorum, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its vivid blue or purple petals that are often adorned with intricate, spurred structures at the base. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately compound, with finely divided lobes that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
