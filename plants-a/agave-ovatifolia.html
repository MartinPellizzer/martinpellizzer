Agave ovatifolia

Agave ovatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf agave, is a striking succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardens and arid landscapes.
