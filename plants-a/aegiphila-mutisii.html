Aegiphila mutisii

Aegiphila mutisii, commonly known as the Peruvian sand verbena, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Aegiphila mutisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
