Anethum foeniculoides var. erythropotamicum

Anethum foeniculoides var. erythropotamicum, commonly known as the erythropotamicum fennel, is a variant of the fennel plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its feathery, aromatic leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, round seeds that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and practical uses.
