Acacia diantreeana

Acacia diantreeana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features feathery, bipinnate leaves that are typically narrow and green. It produces yellow flowers in spring, which are arranged in cylindrical spikes.

Acacia diantreeana is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils across the inland regions of Australia.
