Acacia triptera

Acacia triptera, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feather-like segments. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain seeds, and it is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils. Acacia triptera is often found in dunes and along the coastline, where it plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
