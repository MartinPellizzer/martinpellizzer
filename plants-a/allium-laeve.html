Allium laeve

Allium laeve, commonly known as the flat-leaved onion, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are used both ornamentally and for culinary purposes. Its leaves and bulbs contain sulfur compounds that contribute to its pungent aroma and flavor.

Allium laeve is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
