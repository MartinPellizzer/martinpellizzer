Amphilochia cordata

Amphilochia cordata, commonly known as the broad-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a branching stem that supports its delicate foliage.

Amphilochia cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
