Anthericum congolense var. elongatum

Anthericum congolense var. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated arum, is a species native to tropical Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique floral structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

The varietal form, elongatum, distinguishes it by its longer, more slender spadix compared to the typical variety.
