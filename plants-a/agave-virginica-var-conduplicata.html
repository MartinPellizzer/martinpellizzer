Agave virginica var. conduplicata

Agave virginica var. conduplicata, commonly known as the Florida agave, is a succulent plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often lined with white teeth. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears numerous yellowish-green flowers, which are typically pollinated by bees and other insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
