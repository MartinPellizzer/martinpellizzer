Arisaema tsangpoense

Arisaema tsangpoense, commonly known as the Tsangpo arrowhead, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and a unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowering period occurs in the spring, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by berries.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
