Apoballis linguiformis

Apoballis linguiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandthrift, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Apoballis linguiformis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
