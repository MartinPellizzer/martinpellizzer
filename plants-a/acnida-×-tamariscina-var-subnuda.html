Acnida × tamariscina var. subnuda

Acnida × tamariscina var. subnuda, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a hybrid shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and pairs of opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. Its leaves are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for dry landscapes.
