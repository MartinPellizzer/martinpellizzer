Achillea nanoatrata

Achillea nanoatrata, commonly known as the dwarf yarrow, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fine, silver-gray foliage and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, exhibiting drought tolerance once established.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Morphology

Achillea nanoatrata has small, narrow, silver-gray leaves that are basal and arranged in a rosette, with a fuzzy texture and a compact size.

The stem is upright, slender, and covered in a fine, grayish-white pubescence, with a growth pattern that is generally compact and bushy. The flowers are small, daisy-like composite flowers with white petals and yellow centers, blooming in early summer and pollinated by wind. The fruit is a small achene with a pappus for wind dispersal, and the seeds are tiny and lightweight.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, adapting to dry environments.
