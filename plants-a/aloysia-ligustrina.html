Aloysia ligustrina

Aloysia ligustrina, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves that emit a strong lemon scent. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its aromatic properties make it popular in herbal teas and culinary applications.

Aloysia ligustrina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
