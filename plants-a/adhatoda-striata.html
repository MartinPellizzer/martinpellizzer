Adhatoda striata

Adhatoda striata, commonly known as the blue false indigo, is a tropical plant native to regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The leaves are ovate and often have a slightly serrated edge, while the plant has a woody stem that supports its growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal properties.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic uses.
