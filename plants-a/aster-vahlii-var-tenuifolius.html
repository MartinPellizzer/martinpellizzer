Aster vahlii var. tenuifolius

Aster vahlii var. tenuifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, linear to lanceolate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered blue to violet flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.

Its botanical characteristics reflect adaptations to harsh, dry environments, with reduced leaf surface area to minimize water loss.
