Astragalus sciureus var. subsessilis

Astragalus sciureus var. subsessilis, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus sciureus var. subsessilis is part of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.
