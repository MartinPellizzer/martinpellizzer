Alyxia breviflora

Alyxia breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin.

Alyxia breviflora is often found in the understory of forests and is adapted to thrive in partial to full shade.
