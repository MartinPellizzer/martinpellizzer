Acranthera ruttenii

Acranthera ruttenii, commonly known as the Rutten's acranthera, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
