Albizia mossamedensis

Albizia mossamedensis, commonly known as the Natal Albizia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to greenish-white, arranged in racemose inflorescences, and it produces flat, dehiscent pods containing seeds.

Albizia mossamedensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
