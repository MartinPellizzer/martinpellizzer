Aldunatea chilensis

Aldunatea chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, erect stem that bears a single, showy flower. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
