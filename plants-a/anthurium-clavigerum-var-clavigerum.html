Anthurium clavigerum var. clavigerum

Anthurium clavigerum var. clavigerum, commonly known as the red-tipped anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with a deep red base and green veins, which contrast beautifully with its vibrant red spadix. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a spathe, a modified leaf that enhances its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
