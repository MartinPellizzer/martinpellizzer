Acacia megaphylla

Acacia megaphylla, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Its seeds are enclosed in flat, leathery pods that split open when mature.

Acacia megaphylla is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of native Australian flora.
