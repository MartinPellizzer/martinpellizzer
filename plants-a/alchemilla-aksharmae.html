Alchemilla aksharmae

Alchemilla aksharmae, commonly known as the Aksharmae lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of velvety, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, mountainous climates.

Alchemilla aksharmae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
