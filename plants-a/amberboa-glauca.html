Amberboa glauca

Amberboa glauca, commonly known as the blue amberboa, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has silvery-gray foliage due to a dense covering of fine hairs, which helps it retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
