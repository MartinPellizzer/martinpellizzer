Anthemis kotschyana

Anthemis kotschyana, commonly known as the Kashmir dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
