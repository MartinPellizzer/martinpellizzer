Achillea × romanica

Achillea × romanica, commonly known as the Italian yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and flat, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and produces white or pale pink flowers from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
