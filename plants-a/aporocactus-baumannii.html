Aporocactus baumannii

Aporocactus baumannii, commonly known as the Baumann's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny stems that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. It is a succulent plant that stores water in its stems, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
