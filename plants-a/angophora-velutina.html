Angophora velutina

Angophora velutina, commonly known as the velvet angophora, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, particularly found in the coastal regions of New South Wales and Queensland. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, oval-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. It produces small, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, typically blooming in spring.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
