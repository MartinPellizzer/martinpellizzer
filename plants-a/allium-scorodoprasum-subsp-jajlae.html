Allium scorodoprasum subsp. jajlae

Allium scorodoprasum subsp.

jajlae, commonly known as Jajla garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered bulbs and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces umbels of white to pale pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to cold climates and requires well-drained soil to thrive.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic bulbs, which are used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
