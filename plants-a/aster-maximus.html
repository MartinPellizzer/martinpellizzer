Aster maximus

Aster maximus, commonly known as the Great Aster, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant produces showy, clustered flower heads that are typically purple or blue in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk. Aster maximus thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
