Aster spinuliger

Aster spinuliger, commonly known as the hooked aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny bracts that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster spinuliger is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
