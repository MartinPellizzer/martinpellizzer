Aethionema eunomioides

Aethionema eunomioides, commonly known as the Armenian candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features grayish-green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Aethionema eunomioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
