Alchemilla pallens f. nana

Alchemilla pallens f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features rosettes of broad, hairy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wool. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in compact inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
