Acalypha hispida

Acalypha hispida, commonly known as the velvetleaf or roughleaf alyssum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with prominent veins, and the flowers are borne in dense, spike-like inflorescences.
