Althaea cannabina subsp. narbonensis

Althaea cannabina subsp.

narbonensis, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and produces showy, pink or pale purple flowers in late summer. Its root system is characterized by thick, fleshy roots that are often used in traditional medicine.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
