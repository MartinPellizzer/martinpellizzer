Asparagus dependens

Asparagus dependens, commonly known as the weeping asparagus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cascading, arching stems that produce small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant has feathery, needle-like leaves that are dense and green, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

The berries that follow the flowers are red and edible, often used in culinary applications.
