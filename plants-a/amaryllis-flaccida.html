Amaryllis flaccida

Amaryllis flaccida, commonly known as the drooping amaryllis, is a herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features strap-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and can grow up to 1.5 meters in length. The plant produces umbels of white or pale pink flowers with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Amaryllis flaccida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
