Acrolophus subalbidus

Acrolophus subalbidus, commonly known as the white-fringed grasshopper beetle, is a species of leaf beetle belonging to the family Chrysomelidae.

It is characterized by its distinctive white marginal fringes on the elytra, which give it its common name. This beetle is typically found in grassland habitats, where it feeds on various grasses and herbaceous plants. Its small size and cryptic coloration help it blend into its environment.

The species plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat through its herbivorous diet and interactions with other organisms.
