Alocasia cochinchensis

Alocasia cochinchensis, commonly known as the giant taro or elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped, glossy leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental plants like peace lilies and calla lilies. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense foliage.

Alocasia cochinchensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
