Acacia aulacophylla

Acacia aulacophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and heathlands.

Acacia aulacophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance.
