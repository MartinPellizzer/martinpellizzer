Alexa imperatricis

Alexa imperatricis, commonly known as the imperial alexandria, is a rare flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet petals and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is often protected in conservation programs.
