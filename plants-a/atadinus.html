Atadinus

Atadinus, commonly known as the moonflower, is a striking plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a fragrant scent. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its rapid growth and short flowering period. Its leaves are broad and lobed, often with a velvety texture.

Atadinus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique beauty and nocturnal blooming habit.
