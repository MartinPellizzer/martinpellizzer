Adonis gracilis

Adonis gracilis, commonly known as slender adonis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually hairy and lance-shaped.

Adonis gracilis is known for its early blooming period, often appearing in late spring, making it a notable species in high-altitude flora.
