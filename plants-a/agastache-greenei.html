Agastache greenei

Agastache greenei, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant produces aromatic leaves that are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
