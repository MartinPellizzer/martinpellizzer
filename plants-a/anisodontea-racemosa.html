Anisodontea racemosa

Anisodontea racemosa, commonly known as the African orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring slender, branching stems. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern at the base.

Anisodontea racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, elegant appearance.
