Acacia intricata

Acacia intricata, commonly known as the intricate acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces yellow to golden-green flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods that split open when mature.

Acacia intricata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
