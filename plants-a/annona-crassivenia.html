Annona crassivenia

Annona crassivenia, commonly known as the rough annona, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by large, fleshy berries that are edible when ripe. Its fruit has a sweet, creamy texture and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
