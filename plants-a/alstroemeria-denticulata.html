Alstroemeria denticulata

Alstroemeria denticulata, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant has strap-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a range of growing conditions.

Alstroemeria denticulata is also noted for its ability to thrive in cooler climates, making it a popular choice for temperate regions.
