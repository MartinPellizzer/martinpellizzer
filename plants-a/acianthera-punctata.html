Acianthera punctata

Acianthera punctata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with distinctive dark spots. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

The flowers bloom during the spring and summer months, attracting various pollinators like bees and moths.
