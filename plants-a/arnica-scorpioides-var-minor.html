Arnica scorpioides var. minor

Arnica scorpioides var. minor, commonly known as lesser arnica, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces yellow-orange flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is valued for its medicinal properties.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for both ornamental and therapeutic purposes.
