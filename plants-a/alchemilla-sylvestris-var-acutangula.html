Alchemilla sylvestris var. acutangula

Alchemilla sylvestris var. acutangula, commonly known as the sharp-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, serrated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves and flowers have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported healing properties.
