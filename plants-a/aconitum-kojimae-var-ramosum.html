Aconitum kojimae var. ramosum

Aconitum kojimae var. ramosum, commonly known as the Kojima aconite, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and the plant has a distinctive, toxic root system.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
