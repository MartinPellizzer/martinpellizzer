Anacamptis morio subsp. caucasica

Anacamptis morio subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of several spurred flowers, which are pollinated by specific insect species.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different floral structure and habitat preferences compared to the nominate form of Anacamptis morio.
