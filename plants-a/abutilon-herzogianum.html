Abutilon herzogianum

Abutilon herzogianum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a soft, fuzzy texture. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread, it can become invasive in certain environments.
