Actinomeris longifolia

Actinomeris longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved actinomeris, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the branches. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
