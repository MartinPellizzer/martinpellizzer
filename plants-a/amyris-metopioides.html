Amyris metopioides

Amyris metopioides, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and dark reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by winged fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface.

Amyris metopioides is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
