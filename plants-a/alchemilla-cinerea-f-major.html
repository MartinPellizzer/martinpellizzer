Alchemilla cinerea f. major

Alchemilla cinerea f. major, commonly known as the silverweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a low-growing, spreading habit with silvery-gray foliage that is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, rounded clusters called cymes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Alchemilla cinerea f. major is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
