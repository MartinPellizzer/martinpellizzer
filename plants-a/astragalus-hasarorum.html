Astragalus hasarorum

Astragalus hasarorum, commonly known as the Armenian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species. Its root system is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Astragalus hasarorum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
