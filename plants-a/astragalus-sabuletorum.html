Astragalus sabuletorum

Astragalus sabuletorum, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.
