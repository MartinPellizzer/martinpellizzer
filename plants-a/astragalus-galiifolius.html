Astragalus galiifolius

Astragalus galiifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky environments. It is often found in alpine and steppe habitats, where it plays a role in soil stabilization.

Astragalus galiifolius is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
