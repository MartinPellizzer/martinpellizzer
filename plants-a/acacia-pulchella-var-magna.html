Acacia pulchella var. magna

Acacia pulchella var. magna, commonly known as the giant silver acacia, is a striking shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, slender, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which bloom in late winter or early spring. Its woody stems are often branched and can grow quite tall, making it a notable feature in its native habitat.

This species is adapted to drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.
