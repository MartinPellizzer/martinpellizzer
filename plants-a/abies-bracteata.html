Abies bracteata

Abies bracteata, commonly known as the bracted fir, is a species of conifer native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and its dense, dark green needles that are typically shorter than those of other fir species. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and it is often found growing in cool, moist, well-drained soils at high elevations.

Abies bracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and ecological significance.
