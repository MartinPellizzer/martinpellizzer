Avicennia nitida var. trinitensis

Avicennia nitida var. trinitensis, commonly known as the Trinitarian mangrove, is a variant of the mangrove species Avicennia nitida.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, white salt-excreting layer. This plant thrives in brackish water environments, typically found in coastal areas and tidal zones. Its roots form a dense network that helps stabilize shorelines and filter sediments.

The plant plays a crucial role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
