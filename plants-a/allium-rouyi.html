Allium rouyi

Allium rouyi, commonly known as the Yunnan garlic or Chinese leek, is a bulbous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of the stem. Its bulbs are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine for their mild garlic-like flavor.

Allium rouyi is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
