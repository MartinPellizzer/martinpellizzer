Acacia senegal subsp. glaucophylla

Acacia senegal subsp.

glaucophylla, commonly known as the white thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Sahel region of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, thorned stems and bipinnate leaves with small, elliptical leaflets.

The species is valued for its gum, which is used in food, pharmaceuticals, and industrial applications.
