Arundinella nepalensis var. xerophila

Arundinella nepalensis var. xerophila, commonly known as the Nepalese reed, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and northern India.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting xerophytic adaptations such as thick, waxy leaves to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense clumps that stabilize soil in rocky or degraded habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological resilience and potential use in sustainable land management practices.
