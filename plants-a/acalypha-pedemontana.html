Acalypha pedemontana

Acalypha pedemontana, commonly known as the mountain beauty or rock fig, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, known as farina. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky slopes or in forest understories.

Acalypha pedemontana is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
