Arum ringens

Arum ringens, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a showy spathe. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, often with a waxy texture and a deep green color. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a strong, spicy scent that attracts pollinators.

Arum ringens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and holds cultural significance in various indigenous communities.
