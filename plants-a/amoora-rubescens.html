Amoora rubescens

Amoora rubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered rain tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a striking ornamental plant. The leaves are typically palmately compound, with several leaflets arranged radiating from a central point. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and parks.

It also has traditional uses in some cultures for medicinal purposes.
