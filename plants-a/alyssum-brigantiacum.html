Alyssum brigantiacum

Alyssum brigantiacum, commonly known as the Sierra foothill alyssum, is a flowering plant native to California.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Alyssum brigantiacum is often found in rocky habitats and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
