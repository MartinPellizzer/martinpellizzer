Aspalathus laeta

Aspalathus laeta, commonly known as the silver leaf bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-green leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, round, brown pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
