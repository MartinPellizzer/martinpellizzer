It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori, which are protected by a thin, papery indusium.

Aspidium minimum is characterized by its delicate, fern-like appearance and is often found in temperate regions across the Northern Hemisphere.
