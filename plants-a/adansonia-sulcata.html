Adansonia sulcata

Adansonia sulcata, commonly known as the Madagascar baobab, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its massive, bottle-shaped trunk and thick, fibrous bark that provides structural support and protection. The plant has a shallow root system adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat. It produces large, white flowers that bloom in the evening, followed by woody pods containing inedible, hard seeds.

The tree is well-known for its ability to store large amounts of water in its trunk, making it a vital resource in its ecosystem.
