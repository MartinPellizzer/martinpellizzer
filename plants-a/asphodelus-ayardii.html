Asphodelus ayardii

Asphodelus ayardii, commonly known as the Yemen asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikes of bright yellow or orange flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Asphodelus ayardii is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
