Angraecum oliveirae

Angraecum oliveirae, commonly known as the Oliveira's Star Orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It features long, slender, white flowers with a distinctive, intricate pattern on the petals and sepals. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by the giant hawkmoth, which is adapted to its unique floral structure.

Angraecum oliveirae is notable for its ornamental value and is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.
