Ardisia aciphylla var. aciphylla

Ardisia aciphylla var. aciphylla, commonly known as the glossy leaf ardisia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, red berries. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and undergrowth. Its leaves are elliptical and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and white.

The berries are toxic to humans and animals, making it important to handle with care.
