Adenostegia viscida

Adenostegia viscida, commonly known as the sticky leaf daisy, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky substance. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with yellow centers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Adenostegia viscida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
