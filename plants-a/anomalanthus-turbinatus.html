Anomalanthus turbinatus

Anomalanthus turbinatus, commonly known as the turban snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Anomalanthus turbinatus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
