Azalea periclymenoides var. coccinea

Azalea periclymenoides var. coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet flame azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange-red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has dense, dark green foliage that provides a striking contrast to its showy blooms. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and prefers acidic, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its seasonal color.
