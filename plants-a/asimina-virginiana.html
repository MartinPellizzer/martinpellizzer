Asimina virginiana

Asimina virginiana, commonly known as the common pawpaw, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a fleshy, orange-colored fruit that is edible for both humans and wildlife. The fruit has a unique, custard-like flavor and is often consumed by birds and mammals.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Ericaceae and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
