Astragalus banaticus

Astragalus banaticus, commonly known as the Banat milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its seed pods are distinctive, often containing several small, kidney-shaped seeds.
