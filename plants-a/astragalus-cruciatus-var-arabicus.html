Astragalus cruciatus var. arabicus

Astragalus cruciatus var. arabicus, commonly known as the Arabian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological significance lies in its ability to thrive in harsh environments, contributing to biodiversity in its native range.
