Astragalus schmidii

Astragalus schmidii, commonly known as Schmid's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Astragalus schmidii is often found in steppe and desert environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
