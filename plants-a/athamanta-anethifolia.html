Athamanta anethifolia

Athamanta anethifolia, commonly known as the anise-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, anise-scented leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to white flower heads that are typically arranged in a compact cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Athamanta anethifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowers.
