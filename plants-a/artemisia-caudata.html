Artemisia caudata

Artemisia caudata, commonly known as silverleaf sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Artemisia caudata is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
