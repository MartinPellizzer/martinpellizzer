Alopecurus myosuroides var. salinus

Alopecurus myosuroides var. salinus, commonly known as saltgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This variant is adapted to saline environments, thriving in coastal areas and soils with high salt content. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
