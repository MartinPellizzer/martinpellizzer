Anguria pyrrhocephala

Anguria pyrrhocephala, commonly known as the red-headed melon, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its robust, sprawling vines and large, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. The plant produces striking, yellow flowers that give way to round, green fruits with a thick, waxy rind. The fruit's interior contains numerous flat, seed-bearing structures, and the plant is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
