Alocasia × bayeriana

Alocasia × bayeriana, commonly known as the Giant Taro, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant features a striking, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-draining soil and indirect sunlight.

Alocasia × bayeriana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dramatic foliage and unique floral structure.
