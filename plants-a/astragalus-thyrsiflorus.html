Astragalus thyrsiflorus

Astragalus thyrsiflorus, commonly known as the thyrsofloral milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellow to green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters called thyrses. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous small leaflets.

Astragalus thyrsiflorus is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.
