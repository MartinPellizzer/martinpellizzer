Alyxia oliviformis f. linearis

Alyxia oliviformis f. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf alyxia, is a compact shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, giving it a distinct appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and showy blooms, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
