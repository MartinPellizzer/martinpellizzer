Anchusa spruneri

Anchusa spruneri, commonly known as the Alaskan bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking blue to violet flowers in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to cold, rocky environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in alpine meadows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and cold-climate landscapes.
