Acer kawakamii

Acer kawakamii, commonly known as the Kawakamii maple, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Acer.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its distinctive foliage and ornamental value. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed, often with a reddish tint during the growing season.

This species is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
