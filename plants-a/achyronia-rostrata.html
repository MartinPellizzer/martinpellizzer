Achyronia rostrata

Achyronia rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated tamarind, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its bark and leaves have been utilized in folk medicine for various therapeutic purposes.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions.
