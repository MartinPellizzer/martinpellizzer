Allium shinasii

Allium shinasii, commonly known as the Shinas leek, is a bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Allium shinasii is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
