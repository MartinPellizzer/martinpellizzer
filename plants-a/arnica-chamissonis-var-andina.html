Arnica chamissonis var. andina

Arnica chamissonis var. andina, commonly known as the Andean arnica, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 5,000 meters. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to survive in harsh alpine conditions.

The flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in mountain ecosystems.
