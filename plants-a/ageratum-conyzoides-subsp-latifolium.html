Ageratum conyzoides subsp. latifolium

Ageratum conyzoides subsp.

latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its bushy growth habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture. This species thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
