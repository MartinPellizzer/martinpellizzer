Artemisia mendozana var. paramilloensis

Artemisia mendozana var. paramilloensis, commonly known as the Paramillo Artemisia, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage, which contains essential oils that contribute to its strong, medicinal scent. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in compact clusters during the summer months.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
