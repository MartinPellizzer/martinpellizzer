Acer pseudoplatanus f. variegatum

Acer pseudoplatanus f. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated maple, is a hybrid form of the European maple.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which features white and yellow markings on the leaves. This plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The leaves are palmately lobed and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
