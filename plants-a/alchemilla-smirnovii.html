Alchemilla smirnovii

Alchemilla smirnovii, commonly known as Smirnov's lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Alchemilla smirnovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
