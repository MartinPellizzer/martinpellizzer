Achyranthes aspera var. nigro-olivacea

Achyranthes aspera var. nigro-olivacea, commonly known as black-olive sida, is a variant of the genus Achyranthes native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is an annual herb with erect, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and a bluish-green to dark green coloration. The flowers are small and clustered in loose inflorescences, typically greenish-white in color.

This plant is adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
