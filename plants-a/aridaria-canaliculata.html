Aridaria canaliculata

Aridaria canaliculata, commonly known as the canyon flax, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Aridaria canaliculata is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in environments with low rainfall.
