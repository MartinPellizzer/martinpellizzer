Achillea veronensis

Achillea veronensis, commonly known as Veronese yarrow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. It is often used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice in gardens and landscaping.
