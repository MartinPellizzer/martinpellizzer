Achyranthes martii

Achyranthes martii, commonly known as the Indian spinach or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows up to 1-2 meters tall, featuring erect stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is also valued as a leafy vegetable in some parts of its native range.
