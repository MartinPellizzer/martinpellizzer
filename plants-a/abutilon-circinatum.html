Abutilon circinatum

Abutilon circinatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Abutilon circinatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
