Achyranthes hassleriana

Achyranthes hassleriana, commonly known as the Mexican knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its roots are fibrous and can spread aggressively, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.

It is often found in open areas such as roadsides, fields, and along riverbanks.
