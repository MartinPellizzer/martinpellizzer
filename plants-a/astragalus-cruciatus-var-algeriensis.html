Astragalus cruciatus var. algeriensis

Astragalus cruciatus var. algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow to greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It is native to North Africa, particularly Algeria, and thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climatic conditions.
