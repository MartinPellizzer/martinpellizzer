Anthurium crystallinum f. peltifolium

Anthurium crystallinum f. peltifolium, commonly known as the laceleaf anthurium, is a variant of the Anthurium genus characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

The plant features a striking, waxy, white or pale green spathe that encloses a bright red spadix, making it visually appealing. It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The leaves are typically larger and more elongated compared to the standard Anthurium crystallinum, contributing to its unique appearance.

This cultivar is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive indoors with proper care.
