Acacia pedina

Acacia pedina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, bright yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous narrow, linear leaflets, and its flowers are typically arranged in spherical or cylindrical clusters. Acacia pedina is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
