Asperula banatica

Asperula banatica, commonly known as the Philippine evergreen azalea, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and red, blooming in spring and summer.

Asperula banatica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
