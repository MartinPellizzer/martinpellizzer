Acacia quadrangularis

Acacia quadrangularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, angular, and often twisted stems, which are covered in small, spiny projections. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, typically in spring, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, narrow leaflets.

Acacia quadrangularis is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
