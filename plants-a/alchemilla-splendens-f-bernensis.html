Alchemilla splendens f. bernensis

Alchemilla splendens f. bernensis, commonly known as the golden alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically basal, with a soft, velvety texture and a silver-green color.

Alchemilla splendens f. bernensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded garden settings.
