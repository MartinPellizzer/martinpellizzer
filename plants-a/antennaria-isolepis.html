Antennaria isolepis

Antennaria isolepis, commonly known as the hairy willow or mouse-ear moss, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, hairy leaves and produces tiny yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Antennaria isolepis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is often found in tundra and subalpine regions.
