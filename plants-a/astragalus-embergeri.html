Astragalus embergeri

Astragalus embergeri, commonly known as the Emberger's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing plants.

Astragalus embergeri is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh climates.
