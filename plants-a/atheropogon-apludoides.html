Atheropogon apludoides

Atheropogon apludoides, commonly known as the broadleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually blue-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Atheropogon apludoides is also valued for its ornamental qualities in wetland gardens.
