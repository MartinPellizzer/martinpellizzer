Anisostachya modica

Anisostachya modica, commonly known as the Modica grass, is a tall, perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces panicle-like inflorescences with small, spikelet-bearing branches. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands. It has been used in traditional landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes many other grasses.
