Agropyron intermedium var. trichophorum

Agropyron intermedium var. trichophorum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to golden florets. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is well adapted to dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.
