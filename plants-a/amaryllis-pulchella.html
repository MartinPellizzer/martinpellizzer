Amaryllis pulchella

Amaryllis pulchella, commonly known as the pink amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are often striped with green and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Amaryllis pulchella is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
