Azima nova

Azima nova, commonly known as the white azima, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually bright green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by small, round berries. Azima nova thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and seasonal blooms.
