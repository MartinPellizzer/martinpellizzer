Acacia durandiana

Acacia durandiana, commonly known as the Indian acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow or golden-green flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments due to its deep root system and drought tolerance. Its pods contain seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of food for wildlife.

The plant also plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
