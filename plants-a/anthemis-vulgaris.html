Anthemis vulgaris

Anthemis vulgaris, commonly known as common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

The essential oils extracted from its flowers are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
