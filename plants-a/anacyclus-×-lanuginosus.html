Anacyclus × lanuginosus

Anacyclus × lanuginosus, commonly known as the woolly globe thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiny heads of flowers that resemble a globe, with dense, woolly foliage. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Anacyclus × lanuginosus is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
