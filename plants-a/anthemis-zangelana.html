Anthemis zangelana

Anthemis zangelana, commonly known as Persian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a flat, circular pattern. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a strong, aromatic scent.

Anthemis zangelana is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
