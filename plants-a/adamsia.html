Adamsia

Adamsia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are typically found in coastal areas and are adapted to saline and sandy environments. Adamsia species are characterized by their distinctive, often spiny leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers. They play an important role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.

The genus includes several species, such as Adamsia corymbosa, which is native to the Mediterranean region.
