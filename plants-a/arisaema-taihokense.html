Arisaema taihokense

Arisaema taihokense, commonly known as the Japanese mountain arisaema, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and have a glossy green surface, while the flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and floral structure.
