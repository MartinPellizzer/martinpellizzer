Arabis arcoidea

Arabis arcoidea, commonly known as the curly rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Arabis arcoidea is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.
