Amphilochia lundii

Amphilochia lundii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that appear in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in coastal areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its striking appearance, it is cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
