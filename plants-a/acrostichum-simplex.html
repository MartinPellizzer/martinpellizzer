Acrostichum simplex

Acrostichum simplex, commonly known as the common brake, is a fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to three meters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in humid forests. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Acrostichum simplex plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
