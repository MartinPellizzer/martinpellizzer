Acacia tetraneura

Acacia tetraneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it valuable in ecological restoration.

The plant also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
