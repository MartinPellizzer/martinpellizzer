Alseodaphne lanuginosa

Alseodaphne lanuginosa, commonly known as the silky alseodaphne, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, simple, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits.

This species is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
