Adiantum tibeticum

Adiantum tibeticum, commonly known as the Tibetan maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and China.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often on rocky slopes or in mountain forests. Its fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with small, tooth-like serrations along the edges.

Adiantum tibeticum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in humid, indoor environments.
