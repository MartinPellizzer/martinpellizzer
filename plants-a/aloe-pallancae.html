Aloe pallancae

Aloe pallancae, commonly known as the Natal aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge, often adorned with white or yellow marginal teeth. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Aloe pallancae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
