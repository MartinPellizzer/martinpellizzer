Acacia farnesiana f. pedunculata

Acacia farnesiana f. pedunculata, commonly known as the fringed acacia, is a variant of the weeping acacia, characterized by its distinctive fringed or lobed leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, pendulous clusters, which are followed by flat, linear seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for local wildlife.
