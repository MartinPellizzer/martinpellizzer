Ascolepis pinguis

Ascolepis pinguis, commonly known as the broadleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Ascolepis pinguis is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and swampy areas.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
