Astragalus tribuloides var. albolanatus

Astragalus tribuloides var. albolanatus, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by oblong, inflated pods containing seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

This species is often found in dry grasslands and along riverbanks.
