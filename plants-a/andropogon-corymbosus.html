Andropogon corymbosus

Andropogon corymbosus, commonly known as switchgrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its tufted clusters of spikelets and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant has narrow, blade-like leaves and produces small, greenish or brownish flowers in panicles. It is often cultivated for its ecological benefits, including soil stabilization and carbon sequestration.

Switchgrass is also a valuable species for biofuel production due to its high biomass yield.
