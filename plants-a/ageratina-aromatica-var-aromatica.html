Ageratina aromatica var. aromatica

Ageratina aromatica var. aromatica, commonly known as white cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves and small, fluffy white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
