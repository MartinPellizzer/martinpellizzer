Aneilema karwinskyana

Aneilema karwinskyana, commonly known as the Karwinsky's flame plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often glossy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Aneilema karwinskyana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to add vibrant color to gardens.
