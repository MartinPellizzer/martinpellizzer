Arabis alpina subsp. caucasica

Arabis alpina subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found at high altitudes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and delicate blooms.
