Amaryllis karwinskii

Amaryllis karwinskii, commonly known as the Karwinski's amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, straight stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces clusters of strap-shaped leaves that are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its arid native environment. It blooms in summer, producing flowers that are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

This species is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in well-drained soil with adequate sunlight.
