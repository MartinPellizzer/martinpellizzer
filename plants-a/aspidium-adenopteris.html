Aspidium adenopteris

Aspidium adenopteris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically borne on long, creeping rhizomes. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Aspidium adenopteris is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions.
