Andropogon bicornis var. gracillimus

Andropogon bicornis var. gracillimus, commonly known as the slender panic grass, is a variant of the panic grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
