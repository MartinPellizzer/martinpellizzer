Acer affine

Acer affine, commonly known as the rock maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often found in mountainous regions. The tree has opposite, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in early spring before the leaves emerge.

Acer affine is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
