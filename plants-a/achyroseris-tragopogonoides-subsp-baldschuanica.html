Achyroseris tragopogonoides subsp. baldschuanica

Achyroseris tragopogonoides subsp.

baldschuanica, commonly known as the Balkh goldenhead, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and linear leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, which aid in its propagation across its native range.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
