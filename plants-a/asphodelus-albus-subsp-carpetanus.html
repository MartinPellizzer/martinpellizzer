Asphodelus albus subsp. carpetanus

Asphodelus albus subsp.

carpetanus, commonly known as the white asphodel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, clustered spikes of white, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
