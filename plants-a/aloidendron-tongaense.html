Aloidendron tongaense

Aloidendron tongaense, commonly known as the Tonga aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces striking yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are borne on tall, branching inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Aloidendron tongaense thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
