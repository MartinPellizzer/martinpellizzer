Amomum vignaui

Amomum vignaui, commonly known as the Vietnamese cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by dark blue berries containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its essential oils and medicinal properties.

The plant's rhizomes and seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in various cuisines.
