Amygdalus fischeriana

Amygdalus fischeriana, commonly known as the Fischer's almond, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, hard nuts enclosed in a thick, woody shell, similar to other members of the Prunus genus. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is sometimes grown in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
