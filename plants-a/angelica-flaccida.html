Angelica flaccida

Angelica flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid angelica, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and celery, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant features tall, hollow stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often with finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are small, white to greenish in color, and are clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Angelica flaccida is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
