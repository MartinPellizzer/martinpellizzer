Aegilops echinata

Aegilops echinata, commonly known as the spiny wild oat, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Eurasia and North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and spikelets that bear bristle-like awns, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in open habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Aegilops echinata is also known for its potential as a forage crop and its role in ecological restoration projects.
