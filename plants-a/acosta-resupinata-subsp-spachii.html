Acosta resupinata subsp. spachii

Acosta resupinata subsp.

spachii, commonly known as the Spach's acosta, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly in regions of Argentina and Chile. It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually violet to purple in color and bloom during the spring and early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
