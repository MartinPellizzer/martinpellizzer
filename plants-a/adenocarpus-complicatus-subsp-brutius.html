Adenocarpus complicatus subsp. brutius

Adenocarpus complicatus subsp.

brutius, commonly known as the African burr pea, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny pods.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

The subspecies is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
