Atriplex gigantea

Atriplex gigantea, commonly known as the giant saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are adapted to retain water in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert ecosystems.

Atriplex gigantea plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
