Amaranthus angustifolius subsp. silvestris

Amaranthus angustifolius subsp.

silvestris, commonly known as wild amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, green or red flowers.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It is valued for its edible seeds and has been used traditionally by indigenous peoples.

The species is also known for its ornamental value and ability to grow in poor soil conditions.
