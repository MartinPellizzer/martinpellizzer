Acacia sclerosperma

Acacia sclerosperma, commonly known as the hard-seeded acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are grey-green in color. The plant produces yellow to pale yellow flowers in compact, globular inflorescences, followed by woody, hard-seeded pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Acacia sclerosperma is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fodder.
