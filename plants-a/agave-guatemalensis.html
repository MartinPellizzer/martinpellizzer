Agave guatemalensis

Agave guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing yellowish-green flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Agave guatemalensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and resilience.
