Amblytropis delavayi

Amblytropis delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of western China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Amblytropis delavayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens specializing in alpine flora.
