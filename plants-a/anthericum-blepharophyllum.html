Anthericum blepharophyllum

Anthericum blepharophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features a rosette of narrow, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces a distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a hooded spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it relies on insects for pollination.

Anthericum blepharophyllum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
