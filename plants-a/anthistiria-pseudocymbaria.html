Anthistiria pseudocymbaria

Anthistiria pseudocymbaria, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, often hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer, which are typically borne on short, erect stalks. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of xerophytic ecosystems.
