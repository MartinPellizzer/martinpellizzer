Alchemilla gortschakowskii

Alchemilla gortschakowskii, commonly known as the Siberian lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in upright panicles. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Alchemilla gortschakowskii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance in garden settings.
