Acalypha alnifolia

Acalypha alnifolia, commonly known as silky ironwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed capsules. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely used for commercial purposes.
