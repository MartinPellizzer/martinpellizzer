Acacia seyal var. seretii

Acacia seyal var. seretii, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically small and feathery. The plant produces yellow to orange globular flowers, which are borne in dense clusters and attract various pollinators. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods that split open to release the seeds.

Acacia seyal var. seretii is well adapted to drought conditions and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
