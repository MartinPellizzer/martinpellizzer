Andromeda lucida

Andromeda lucida, commonly known as the shining andromeda, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils found in forest understories. The plant features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, often with a glossy appearance.

Andromeda lucida is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
