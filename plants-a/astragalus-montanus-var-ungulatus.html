Astragalus montanus var. ungulatus

Astragalus montanus var. ungulatus, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often at high elevations. The plant produces small, yellow to green flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are compound with numerous leaflets. It is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
