Acacia genistifolia subsp. genistifolia

Acacia genistifolia subsp.

genistifolia, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, wiry stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance.

The plant produces yellow globular flowers in spring, which are arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. It is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
