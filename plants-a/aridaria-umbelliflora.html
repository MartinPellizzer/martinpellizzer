Aridaria umbelliflora

Aridaria umbelliflora, commonly known as the desert umbrella plant, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its rosette growth habit and produces small, white, umbrella-shaped inflorescences. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It is well adapted to low rainfall and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.

Aridaria umbelliflora plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing limited forage for desert wildlife.
