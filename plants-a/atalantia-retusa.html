Atalantia retusa

Atalantia retusa, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, honey-like flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The botanical features of Atalantia retusa make it a valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
