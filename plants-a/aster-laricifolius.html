Aster laricifolius

Aster laricifolius, commonly known as the mountain aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and often resemble those of fir trees, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
