Amaryllis pendula

Amaryllis pendula, commonly known as the weeping amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features long, pendant-like flowers that hang from the stem, creating a dramatic visual effect. The plant produces clusters of funnel-shaped blooms in shades of red, pink, or white, often with a contrasting dark throat. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are broad and grass-like in appearance.

Amaryllis pendula is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens or as a houseplant.
