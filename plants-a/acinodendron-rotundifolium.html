Acinodendron rotundifolium

Acinodendron rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaf acinodendron, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features simple, ovate to round leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and usually have five petals.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release the seeds.
