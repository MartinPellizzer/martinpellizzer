Ageratina

Ageratina, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are native to the Americas and are characterized by their fluffy, blue or white flowers that resemble mist. The leaves are typically opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs. Ageratina species are often used in gardens for their ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.

Some species are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
