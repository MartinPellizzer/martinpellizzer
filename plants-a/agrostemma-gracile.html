Agrostemma gracile

Agrostemma gracile, commonly known as slender weed or yellow weed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that grow up to 30 centimeters in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from July to September. Its leaves are lance-shaped and alternate along the stem, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

Agrostemma gracile is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
