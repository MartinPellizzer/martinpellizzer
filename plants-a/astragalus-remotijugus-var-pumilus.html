Astragalus remotijugus var. pumilus

Astragalus remotijugus var. pumilus, commonly known as the smallleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its root system is deep, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.
