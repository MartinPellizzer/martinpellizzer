Allium tolmiei var. platyphyllum

Allium tolmiei var. platyphyllum, commonly known as the flat-leaf garlic chive, is a cultivated variety of the Allium genus.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that are often used in culinary applications similar to regular chives. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appearance and its mild garlic flavor.
