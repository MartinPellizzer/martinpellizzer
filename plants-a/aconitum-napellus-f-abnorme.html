Aconitum napellus f. abnorme

Aconitum napellus f. abnorme, commonly known as the abnormal monkshood, is a variant of the European monkshood plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers and dark green, palmately lobed leaves. This ferny perennial is native to Central and Eastern Europe and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of alkaloids.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a notable species in botanical studies.
