Angophora robur

Angophora robur, commonly known as the Sydney red gum, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, followed by woody, cylindrical fruits. It is typically found in dry sclerophyll forests and is valued for its hard, durable timber.

Angophora robur plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
