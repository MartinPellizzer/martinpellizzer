Acacia bidwillii var. major

Acacia bidwillii var. major, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
