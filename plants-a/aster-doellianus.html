Aster doellianus

Aster doellianus, commonly known as the Doell's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in shades of blue to purple.

Aster doellianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
