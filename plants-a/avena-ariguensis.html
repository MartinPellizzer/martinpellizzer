Avena ariguensis

Avena ariguensis, commonly known as the Brazilian oat, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets, typical of the Poaceae family. Avena ariguensis is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for livestock and supporting local biodiversity.
