Alectryon fuscus

Alectryon fuscus, commonly known as the black-berried pomegranate, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to a dark purple or black when mature. The fruits are edible, with a tart flavor and a juicy, seed-filled pulp.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
