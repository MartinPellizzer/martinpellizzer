Allium macleanii

Allium macleanii, commonly known as MacLean's onion, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, such as cliffs and slopes. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor.

Allium macleanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
