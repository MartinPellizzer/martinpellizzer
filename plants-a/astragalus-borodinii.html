Astragalus borodinii

Astragalus borodinii, commonly known as the Borodin's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus borodinii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators.
