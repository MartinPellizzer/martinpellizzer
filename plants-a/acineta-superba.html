Acineta superba

Acineta superba, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salty air. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

Acineta superba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in coastal environments.
