Artanthe endlicheriana

Artanthe endlicheriana, commonly known as the mountain artanthe, is a perennial herb native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, velvety blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
