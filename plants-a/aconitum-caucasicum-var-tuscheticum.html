Aconitum caucasicum var. tuscheticum

Aconitum caucasicum var. tuscheticum, commonly known as the Tschetik Tausch or Tschetik aconite, is a variant of the aconite genus native to the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, sparsely leafy stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense racemes. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, waxy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions of the Caucasus.
