Adenophora nipponica f. multiloba

Adenophora nipponica f. multiloba, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
