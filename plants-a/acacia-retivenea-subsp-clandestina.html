Acacia retivenea subsp. clandestina

Acacia retivenea subsp.

clandestina, commonly known as the secret acacia, is a shrub native to parts of southern Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. Its small, pod-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
