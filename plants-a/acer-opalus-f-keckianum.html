Acer opalus f. keckianum

Acer opalus f. keckianum, commonly known as the Keck's maple, is a deciduous tree characterized by its ornamental value and distinctive foliage.

It features palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn, making it a popular choice for landscape design. The tree produces small, red samaras that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This cultivar is valued for its compact size and showy fall color, making it suitable for smaller gardens and urban settings.
