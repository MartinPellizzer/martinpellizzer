Aspalathus barbata

Aspalathus barbata, commonly known as the barbed bushveld pea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, barbed stems that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules.

Aspalathus barbata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
