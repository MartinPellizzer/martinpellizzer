Anacamptis × dafnii

Anacamptis × dafnii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers with purple markings, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland and meadow habitats. It has a sympodial growth habit, with each pseudobulb producing a single leaf and a flower spike.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
