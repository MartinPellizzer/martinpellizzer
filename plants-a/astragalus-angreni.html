Astragalus angreni

Astragalus angreni, commonly known as the Angren milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces pods that contain several elliptical seeds, which are typically brown or gray in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus angreni plays a role in soil stabilization and is part of the broader Astragalus genus, which includes many species with medicinal and ecological significance.
