Abies alba lusus pendula

Abies alba lusus pendula, commonly known as the weeping white fir, is a variant of the European silver fir.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive pendulous branches that hang in a weeping manner, creating a unique visual appearance. It features needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a soft texture. The cones are small and cylindrical, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions of Europe.

Native Distribution

Abies alba lusus pendula is primarily native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe, including parts of Italy, Austria, and the Balkans.

This variety of the silver fir is typically found in temperate forest ecosystems at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. It thrives in areas with cool, moist climates and well-drained soils. The plant is particularly common in the Alps and Carpathian mountain ranges.

Its native distribution is closely associated with the natural habitats of its parent species, Abies alba.
