Aconitum japonicum var. montanum

Aconitum japonicum var. montanum, commonly known as the Japanese monkshood variant montanum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It features striking, helmet-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a coarse texture and prominent veins. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
