Aristolochia warmingii var. angustilabia

Aristolochia warmingii var. angustilabia, commonly known as the narrow-lipped Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular, and often fragrant flowers that resemble a pipe, with a distinctive shape and coloration. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and deeply veined. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
