Astragalus dombeyi

Astragalus dombeyi, commonly known as Dombey's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Astragalus dombeyi is often found in steppes and mountainous areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
