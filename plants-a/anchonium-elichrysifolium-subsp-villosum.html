Anchonium elichrysifolium subsp. villosum

Anchonium elichrysifolium subsp.

villosum, commonly known as the hairy anchonium, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. Its small yellow flowers are arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family.

This subspecies is noted for its adaptability to harsh conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native range.
