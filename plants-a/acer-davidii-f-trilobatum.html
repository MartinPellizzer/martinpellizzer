Acer davidii f. trilobatum

Acer davidii f. trilobatum, commonly known as the three-lobed maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are deeply divided into three narrow, elongated lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique foliage makes it a standout in the landscape, especially during the autumn months.
