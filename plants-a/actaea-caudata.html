Actaea caudata

Actaea caudata, commonly known as red baneberry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiny, red berries that appear in late summer. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive notched appearance and produces showy, white to pale pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
