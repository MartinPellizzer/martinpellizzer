Acer aetnense

Acer aetnense, commonly known as the Etna maple, is a species of maple native to the volcanic slopes of Mount Etna in Sicily.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree with a broad, rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are palmately lobed, typically with five to seven lobes, and are bright green during the growing season. It produces small, red flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This plant is adapted to the unique microclimates of its volcanic habitat, making it a rare and ecologically significant species.
