Alyxia amoena

Alyxia amoena, commonly known as the sweetleaf vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, orange-yellow fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red aril. Alyxia amoena thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a shade-tolerant, flowering vine.
