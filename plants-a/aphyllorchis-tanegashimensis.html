Aphyllorchis tanegashimensis

Aphyllorchis tanegashimensis, commonly known as the Tanegashima orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, featuring a single, showy flower with a broad, spurred lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on rocky substrates or in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
