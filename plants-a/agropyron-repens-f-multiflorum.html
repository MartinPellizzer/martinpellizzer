Agropyron repens f. multiflorum

Agropyron repens f. multiflorum, commonly known as the creeping wheatgrass, is a variant of the common wheatgrass species.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability and use in ecological restoration and grazing.
