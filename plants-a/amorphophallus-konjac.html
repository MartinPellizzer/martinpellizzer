Amorphophallus konjac

Amorphophallus konjac, commonly known as the Chinese arrowroot or konjac, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It produces a large, umbrella-shaped leaf and a spadix inflorescence that resembles a phallic shape, giving it its scientific name. The plant's most valuable part is its corm, which is rich in glucomannan, a water-soluble fiber. The corm is used in various culinary applications, including as a vegetable and in the production of dietary supplements.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other plants like calla lilies and peace lilies.
