Argythamnia erubescens

Argythamnia erubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered gumweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces vibrant red or orange flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or arid regions.

Argythamnia erubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
