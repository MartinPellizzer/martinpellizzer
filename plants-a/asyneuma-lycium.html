Asyneuma lycium

Asyneuma lycium, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which attract various pollinators. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally in local medicine for their purported healing properties.

Asyneuma lycium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
