Alternanthera cana

Alternanthera cana, commonly known as red-veined joyweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red veins on its dark green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage is popular in aquatic settings, such as ponds and water gardens.
