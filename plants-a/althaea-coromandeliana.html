Althaea coromandeliana

Althaea coromandeliana, commonly known as Indian mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It features large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central column. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Althaea coromandeliana is also valued for its mucilage-rich roots, which have been used in traditional medicine.
