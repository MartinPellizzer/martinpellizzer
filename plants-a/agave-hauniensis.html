Agave hauniensis

Agave hauniensis, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with small, white flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sunlight.

Agave hauniensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
