Anemone saniculifolia

Anemone saniculifolia, commonly known as the leafy anemone, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that are deeply lobed and resemble the leaves of the common cinquefoil. The plant produces showy, white to pink flowers with five petals, typically appearing in late spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Anemone saniculifolia is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
