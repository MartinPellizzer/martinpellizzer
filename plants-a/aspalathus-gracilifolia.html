Aspalathus gracilifolia

Aspalathus gracilifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf rooibos, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by small, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and requires a dry, Mediterranean climate.

Due to its unique properties, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and has potential applications in modern herbal remedies.
