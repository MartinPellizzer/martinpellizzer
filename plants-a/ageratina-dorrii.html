Ageratina dorrii

Ageratina dorrii, commonly known as the Hawaiian blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests and streamside areas. Its blue to violet flower heads are surrounded by white bracts, creating a mist-like appearance.

Ageratina dorrii is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological diversity of Hawaii's native flora.
