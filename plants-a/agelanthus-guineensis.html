Agelanthus guineensis

Agelanthus guineensis, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, green, spurge-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Agelanthus guineensis is also noted for its ability to tolerate shade and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
