Absinthium grandiflorum

Absinthium grandiflorum, commonly known as greater centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Absinthium grandiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional herbal medicine.
