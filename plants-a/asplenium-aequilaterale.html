Asplenium aequilaterale

Asplenium aequilaterale, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and slightly glossy. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in small clusters called sori. Asplenium aequilaterale thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
