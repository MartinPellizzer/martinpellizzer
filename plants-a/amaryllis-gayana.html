Amaryllis gayana

Amaryllis gayana, commonly known as the yellow amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces strap-like, dark green leaves that are broad and fleshy, typical of many amaryllis species. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The flowers bloom in summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and landscapes.
