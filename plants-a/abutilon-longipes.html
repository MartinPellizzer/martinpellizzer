Abutilon longipes

Abutilon longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often found in moist, tropical environments.

Abutilon longipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
