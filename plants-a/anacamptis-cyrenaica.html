Anacamptis cyrenaica

Anacamptis cyrenaica, commonly known as the Cyrenaica orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to North Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces a single, upright stem with several lance-shaped leaves.

Anacamptis cyrenaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
