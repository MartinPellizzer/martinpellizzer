Ainsliaea pertyoides var. albotomentosa

Ainsliaea pertyoides var. albotomentosa, commonly known as the white-hairy ainsliaea, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, often covered in fine, white trichomes.

The flowers are clustered in cymes and bloom in late spring to early summer.
