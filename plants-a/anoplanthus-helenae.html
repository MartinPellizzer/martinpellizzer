Anoplanthus helenae

Anoplanthus helenae, commonly known as the Helen's grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Anoplanthus helenae is typically found in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy and low-maintenance nature.
