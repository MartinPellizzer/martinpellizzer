Aesculus × du-pontii var. hessei

Aesculus × du-pontii var. hessei, commonly known as the Chinese horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are typically 10–20 cm in diameter. The tree produces showy, white to pinkish flowers in panicles during the spring. Its fruit is a spiny capsule containing 2–4 shiny, chestnut-like seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
