Astragalus velatus

Astragalus velatus, commonly known as the velvet milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Astragalus velatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
