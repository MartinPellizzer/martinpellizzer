Aster andryaloides

Aster andryaloides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. The plant features showy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a fuzzy texture.

Aster andryaloides is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
