Artemisia coracina

Artemisia coracina, commonly known as the African mugwort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It has a strong, camphor-like scent and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its adaptability to arid conditions makes it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
