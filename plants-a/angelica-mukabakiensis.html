Angelica mukabakiensis

Angelica mukabakiensis, commonly known as the Mukabaki angelica, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant features large, compound leaves with a pinnate structure, and its flowers are typically white or pale yellow. Angelica mukabakiensis is valued for its medicinal properties, with roots and leaves used in traditional remedies.

It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas at elevations above 1,500 meters.
