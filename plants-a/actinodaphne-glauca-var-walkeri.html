Actinodaphne glauca var. walkeri

Actinodaphne glauca var. walkeri, commonly known as the white-leafed camphor tree, is a variant of the actinodaphne genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a waxy, bluish sheen. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
