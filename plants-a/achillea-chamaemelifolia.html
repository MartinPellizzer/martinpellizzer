Achillea chamaemelifolia

Achillea chamaemelifolia, commonly known as yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features feathery, aromatic leaves and flat clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its sturdy, upright stems and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often used in herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its botanical name honors Achilles, the Greek hero, due to its historical use in treating wounds.
