Acronia muriculata

Acronia muriculata, commonly known as the roughleaf acronia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in small, stiff hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough, textured surface and serrated edges. Its small, greenish-white flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
