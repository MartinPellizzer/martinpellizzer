Arundinaria atropurpurea

Arundinaria atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often tinged with a reddish-purple hue, especially when young. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Arundinaria atropurpurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soils.
