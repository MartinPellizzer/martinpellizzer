Andropogon hookeri

Andropogon hookeri, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, giving it its common name. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Andropogon hookeri plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
