Angelica sylvestris var. elatior

Angelica sylvestris var. elatior, commonly known as greater angelica, is a tall biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features large, compound leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length, with a distinct, aromatic scent. The plant produces yellow-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its root system is thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

This variety is closely related to other angelica species and shares similar botanical characteristics.
