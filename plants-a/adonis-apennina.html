Adonis apennina

Adonis apennina, commonly known as the Apennine glory of the snow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in fine hairs.

Adonis apennina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
