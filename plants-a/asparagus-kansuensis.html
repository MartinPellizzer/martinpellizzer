Asparagus kansuensis

Asparagus kansuensis, commonly known as the Kansu asparagus, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
