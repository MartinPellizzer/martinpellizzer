Adenophora verticillata var. triphylla

Adenophora verticillata var. triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves and bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade. Its stems are often hairy, and the leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated edge.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and ecological benefits to pollinators.
