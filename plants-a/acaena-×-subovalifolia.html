Acaena × subovalifolia

Acaena × subovalifolia, commonly known as the New Zealand bramble, is a hybrid shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically red or orange in color, which are adapted for dispersal by birds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Acaena × subovalifolia is often found in coastal and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
