Aloysia barbata var. acapulcensis

Aloysia barbata var. acapulcensis, commonly known as Acapulco verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces clusters of purple or blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
