Amaranthus tricolor subsp. tristis

Amaranthus tricolor subsp.

tristis, commonly known as redroot amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and branched stems that often turn reddish-purple as the plant matures.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and can vary in color from green to red or purple, depending on environmental conditions. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or red, followed by three-angled seed capsules.

This species is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
