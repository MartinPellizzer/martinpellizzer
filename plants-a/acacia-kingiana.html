Acacia kingiana

Acacia kingiana, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Acacia kingiana is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in drought conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its unique foliage and seasonal flowering.
