Achillea phrygia

Achillea phrygia, commonly known as the Turkish yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb with a robust root system and a bushy growth habit. The plant features finely divided, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal medicine.
