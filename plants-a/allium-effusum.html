Allium effusum

Allium effusum, commonly known as the broadleaf garlic or wild garlic, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, often with a garlic-like scent. The plant produces small, spherical umbels of white or pale purple flowers in the summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a useful plant in culinary applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species like garlic and onions.
