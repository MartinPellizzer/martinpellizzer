Acer aidzuense

Acer aidzuense, commonly known as the Aizu maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its vibrant autumn foliage, which displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
