Astragalus cruciatus subsp. trabutianus

Astragalus cruciatus subsp.

trabutianus, commonly known as the Trabut's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
