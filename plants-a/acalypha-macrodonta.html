Acalypha macrodonta

Acalypha macrodonta, commonly known as the giant curlleaf mallow, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that often have a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, red or pink bract-based flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Acalypha macrodonta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
