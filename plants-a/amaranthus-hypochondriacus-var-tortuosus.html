Amaranthus hypochondriacus var. tortuosus

Amaranthus hypochondriacus var. tortuosus, commonly known as the twisted amaranth, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, feather-like bracts that range in color from red to orange and purple. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in some traditional cuisines.
