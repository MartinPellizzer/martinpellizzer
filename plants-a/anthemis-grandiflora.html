Anthemis grandiflora

Anthemis grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow daisies. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to gardens and ecological landscapes.
