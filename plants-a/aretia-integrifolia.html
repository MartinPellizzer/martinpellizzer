Aretia integrifolia

Aretia integrifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf aretia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically broad and entire, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
