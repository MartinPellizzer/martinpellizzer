Anthelepis undulata

Anthelepis undulata, commonly known as the wavy-leaf pandan, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its long, slender leaves that have a wavy or undulating edge, giving it its common name. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in cooking and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is well adapted to humid tropical climates.
