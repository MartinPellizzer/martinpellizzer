Aira multiculmis var. expansa

Aira multiculmis var. expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf wild oat, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, each bearing two to three bracteate flowers.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
