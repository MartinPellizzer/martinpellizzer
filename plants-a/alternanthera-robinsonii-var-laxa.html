Alternanthera robinsonii var. laxa

Alternanthera robinsonii var. laxa, commonly known as the red-leaf Alternanthera, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or pink foliage, which becomes more intense under strong light conditions. This variety thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and prefers soft, slightly acidic water with good nutrient levels. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal in freshwater aquariums.

It is a popular choice for aquascaping due to its aesthetic value and ease of care.
