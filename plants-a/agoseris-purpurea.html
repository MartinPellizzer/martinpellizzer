Agoseris purpurea

Agoseris purpurea, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It produces a spiky, cone-like structure at the center of its flower head, which is typical of the Asteraceae family.

Agoseris purpurea is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used by Native American tribes for various healing purposes.
