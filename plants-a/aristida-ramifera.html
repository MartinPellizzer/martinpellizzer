Aristida ramifera

Aristida ramifera, commonly known as the ramifera grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowering structures are often arranged in dense clusters.

Aristida ramifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
