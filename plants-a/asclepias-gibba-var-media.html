Asclepias gibba var. media

Asclepias gibba var. media, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of distinctive, orange-tan flowers with a unique, horn-like structure at the center. It is a vital host plant for monarch butterfly larvae and supports a variety of pollinators.

Its latex sap contains cardiac glycosides, which are toxic to some animals but beneficial for certain insects.
