Archibaccharis flexilis

Archibaccharis flexilis, commonly known as the flexible bay leaf.

It is a shrub native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Caribbean. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that are often used in culinary applications. Its flowers are small and white, typically arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its historical use in traditional medicine.
