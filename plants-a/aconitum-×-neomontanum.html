Aconitum × neomontanum

Aconitum × neomontanum, commonly known as the European meadow aconite, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense spikes at the top of the stems. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

Aconitum × neomontanum is often found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows.
