Aristolochia raja

Aristolochia raja, commonly known as the royal Dutch arrowhead, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in moist forests or along stream banks.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
