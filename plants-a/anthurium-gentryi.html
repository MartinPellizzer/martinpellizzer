Anthurium gentryi

Anthurium gentryi, commonly known as the Gentry's Anthurium, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique floral structure known as a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments with high air moisture. It features vibrant, colorful spathes that range from deep red to orange, often contrasting with its lush green foliage. The plant is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to adapt to indoor conditions.

Its growth habit includes a rhizomatous stem that produces aerial roots, aiding in its climbing or epiphytic growth in its natural habitat.
