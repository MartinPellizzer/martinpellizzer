Arundinaria tootsik

Arundinaria tootsik, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is native to East Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets that are often green or reddish in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential invasive species.

Arundinaria tootsik is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and its use in traditional landscaping and basket-making.
