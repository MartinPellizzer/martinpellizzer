Anthericum arkansasense

Anthericum arkansasense, commonly known as Arkansas lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-shaped, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its bulbous rhizomes are used for propagation, and the plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Anthericum arkansasense is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
