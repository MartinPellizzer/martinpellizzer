Alchemilla incisa var. fatrica

Alchemilla incisa var. fatrica, commonly known as the Fatfrica lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fuzzy, shield-like leaves that resemble a lady's mantle. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are borne on slender, upright stems. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and is commonly used in garden landscaping.
