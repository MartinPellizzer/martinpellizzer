Aloe zombitsiensis subsp. pallida

Aloe zombitsiensis subsp.

pallida, commonly known as the pale aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The plant produces striking orange-red flowers on tall, branched inflorescences, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is sometimes used for its soothing properties.

This subspecies is well adapted to dry environments, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
