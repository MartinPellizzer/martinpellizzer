Agathis macrophylla var. obtusa

Agathis macrophylla var. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leafed ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are thick and leathery, often with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are borne in clusters, followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is also noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
