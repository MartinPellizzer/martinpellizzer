Aconitum falconeri

Aconitum falconeri, commonly known as Falconer's monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple hood-shaped flowers, which are typical of the genus Aconitum. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a dangerous plant and should be handled with care.
