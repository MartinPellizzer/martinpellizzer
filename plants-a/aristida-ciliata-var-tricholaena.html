Aristida ciliata var. tricholaena

Aristida ciliata var. tricholaena, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and as a ornamental plant.
