Astragalus projecturus

Astragalus projecturus, commonly known as the spreading milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its roots can form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

Astragalus projecturus is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub ecosystems.
