Althaea ficifolia var. glabrata

Althaea ficifolia var. glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are smooth and often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water in arid environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
