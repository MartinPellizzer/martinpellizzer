Arabis glandulosa

Arabis glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or arctic environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are often covered with glandular hairs.

Arabis glandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
