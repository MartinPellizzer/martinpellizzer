Astragalus amabilis

Astragalus amabilis, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple blossoms. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.

Astragalus amabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
