Andropogon yinduensis

Andropogon yinduensis, commonly known as the Yindu grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes many other grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and savannas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Andropogon yinduensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
