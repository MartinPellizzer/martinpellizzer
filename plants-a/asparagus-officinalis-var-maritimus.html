Asparagus officinalis var. maritimus

Asparagus officinalis var. maritimus, commonly known as sea asparagus, is a coastal plant adapted to saline environments.

It features slender, edible stems that grow close to the ground, often in sandy or rocky soils. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and traditional use in herbal medicine.
