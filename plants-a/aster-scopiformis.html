Aster scopiformis

Aster scopiformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall.

Aster scopiformis is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
