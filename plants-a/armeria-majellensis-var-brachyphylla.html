Armeria majellensis var. brachyphylla

Armeria majellensis var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-stemmed thrift, is a low-growing plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand coastal salt spray and strong winds.
