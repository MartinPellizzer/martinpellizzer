Alsolinum

Alsolinum, commonly known as the "bachelor's button," is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia. The plants are characterized by their showy, blue or purple flower heads that resemble small buttons, surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. Alsolinum species are typically herbaceous perennials that thrive in well-drained soils and full sun.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
