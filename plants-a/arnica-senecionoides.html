Arnica senecionoides

Arnica senecionoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf arnica, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Arnica senecionoides is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
