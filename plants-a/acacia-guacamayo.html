Acacia guacamayo

Acacia guacamayo, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, open areas.

Acacia guacamayo is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration efforts.
