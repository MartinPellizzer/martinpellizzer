Artemisia macrosciadia

Artemisia macrosciadia, commonly known as the giant sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Artemisia macrosciadia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
