Allium sphaerocephalon var. typicum

Allium sphaerocephalon var. typicum, commonly known as the globe onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a cluster of round, fleshy bulbs that are typically white or pale yellow in color. The plant produces dense, grass-like foliage and is characterized by its spherical, purple or pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, sweet flavor when cooked.

This variety is closely related to other allium species and is valued in both gardens and culinary applications.
