Arthraxon dalatensis

Arthraxon dalatensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam, and thrives in moist, swampy environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
