Acantholimon sclerophyllum

Acantholimon sclerophyllum, commonly known as the hard-leaved sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or white flowers during the spring and early summer. It is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, thriving in full sun and poor soils.

Acantholimon sclerophyllum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
