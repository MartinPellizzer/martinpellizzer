Aglaonema costatum f. virescens

Aglaonema costatum f. virescens, commonly known as the Emerald Beauty, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions.

It features striking, broad, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with subtle variegation. The plant thrives in low-light conditions and requires consistent moisture without being waterlogged. Its foliage is often used in interior landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.

The variegation in its leaves is due to a genetic mutation that affects chlorophyll distribution.
