Atriplex longipes

Atriplex longipes, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to saline soils and is often found in desert scrub and salt desert communities.

Atriplex longipes plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
