Acacia rynchocarpa

Acacia rynchocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-lobed wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow floral spikes and bipinnate leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia rynchocarpa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
