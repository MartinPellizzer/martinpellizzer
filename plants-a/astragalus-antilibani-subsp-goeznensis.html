Astragalus antilibani subsp. goeznensis

Astragalus antilibani subsp.

goeznensis, commonly known as the Goeznensian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East. It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
