Amblyopogon concinnus

Amblyopogon concinnus, commonly known as the slender-leaved sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in early summer. Native to wetland habitats in southern Africa, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in marshy areas.

Amblyopogon concinnus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
