Acrophorus jamaicensis

Acrophorus jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican acorn tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries, and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
