Aristolochia rotunda subsp. reichsteinii

Aristolochia rotunda subsp.

reichsteinii, commonly known as the Reichstein's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, heart-shaped leaves and its fragrant, saddle-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color.

The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is notable for its complex floral structure and its role in supporting specific pollinators.
