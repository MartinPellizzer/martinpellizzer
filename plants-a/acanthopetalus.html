Acanthopetalus

Acanthopetalus, commonly known as the spiky petal plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny or toothed petals that give it a unique appearance. The genus comprises several species, primarily found in tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and the Americas. These plants typically have opposite leaves and produce showy flowers in a variety of colors.

Acanthopetalus species are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their striking floral displays.
