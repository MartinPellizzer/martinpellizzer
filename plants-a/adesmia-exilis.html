Adesmia exilis

Adesmia exilis, commonly known as the slender legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in racemes.

Adesmia exilis is valued for its ecological role in soil enrichment and as a source of forage for livestock.
