Aira caryophyllea f. pallescens

Aira caryophyllea f. pallescens, commonly known as the pale air grass, is a variant of the common air grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its pale green foliage and slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in summer, which are clustered in spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate appearance and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
