Acaena multifida var. utrinquepilosa

Acaena multifida var. utrinquepilosa, commonly known as the tufted burrweed, is a species native to New Zealand.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features compound leaves with multiple lobes, and its stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spiky fruits that are adapted for animal dispersal.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native invertebrates.
