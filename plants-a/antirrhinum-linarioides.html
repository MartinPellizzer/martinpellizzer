Antirrhinum linarioides

Antirrhinum linarioides, commonly known as the slender toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, resembling the shape of a toad's mouth. The plant grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Antirrhinum linarioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
