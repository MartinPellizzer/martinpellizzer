Alchemilla vulgaris subsp. mairei

Alchemilla vulgaris subsp.

mairei, commonly known as the Maire's lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features basal rosettes of serrated, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months, which are borne on slender, erect stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
