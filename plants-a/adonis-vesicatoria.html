Adonis vesicatoria

Adonis vesicatoria, commonly known as the deadly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple or violet flowers and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it dangerous if ingested. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Despite its beauty, it is highly poisonous and should be handled with care.
