Aster albus var. lutescens

Aster albus var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow aster, is a variant of the white aster native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that contrast with its green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
