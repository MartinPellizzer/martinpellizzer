Ambelania oleifolia var. riparia

Ambelania oleifolia var. riparia, commonly known as the river sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It typically grows in moist soils along riverbanks and marshy areas, thriving in environments with consistent water availability. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in dense clusters at the base. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in spikes and bloom during the spring and early summer.

Ambelania oleifolia var. riparia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
