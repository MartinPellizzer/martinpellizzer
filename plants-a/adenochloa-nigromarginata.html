Adenochloa nigromarginata

Adenochloa nigromarginata, commonly known as black-margin grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with dark brown margins. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are often wind-pollinated.

Adenochloa nigromarginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive leaf coloration.
