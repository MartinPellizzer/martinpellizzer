Abies pectinata

Abies pectinata, commonly known as the silver fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and its dark green, needle-like leaves that are coated with a silvery-white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing winged seeds dispersed by the wind. Abies pectinata thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Its wood is valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.
