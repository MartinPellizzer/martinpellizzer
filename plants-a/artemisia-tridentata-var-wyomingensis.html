Artemisia tridentata var. wyomingensis

Artemisia tridentata var. wyomingensis, commonly known as big sagebrush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and aromatic, pungent scent. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in three parts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Big sagebrush plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
