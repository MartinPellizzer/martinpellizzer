Agrostis zerovii

Agrostis zerovii, commonly known as the Zerov's bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to specific regions of South America.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths and thrives in moist, temperate climates. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its native habitat.
