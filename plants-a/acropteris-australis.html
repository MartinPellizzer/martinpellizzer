Acropteris australis

Acropteris australis, commonly known as the Australian cliff fern, is a unique species native to southern Australia.

It is a fern that thrives in coastal and rocky environments, often found on cliffs and in sandy soils. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its fronds that are typically upright and somewhat leathery. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves.

Acropteris australis is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant in its natural habitat.
