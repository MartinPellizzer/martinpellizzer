Aconitum anthora f. collinum

Aconitum anthora f. collinum, commonly known as the alpine aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as alpine meadows and forested areas. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are usually unbranched with a hairy texture.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a potentially dangerous plant to handle.
