Astragalus dueluekbabensis

Astragalus dueluekbabensis, commonly known as the Duelluekbab milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellowish to brownish pea-like flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its ecological significance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
