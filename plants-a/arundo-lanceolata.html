Arundo lanceolata

Arundo lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, blade-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. This plant thrives in wet or moist environments, often found along rivers, lakes, and marshy areas. Its flowering spikes produce small, pale green to yellowish flowers that appear in the summer months.

Arundo lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a bioengineered plant for erosion control.
