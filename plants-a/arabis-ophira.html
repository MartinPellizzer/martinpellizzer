Arabis ophira

Arabis ophira, commonly known as the lesser white mustard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features basal leaves that are lobed or entire. Its flowers are small, white, and arranged in racemes, and it produces elongated pods containing several seeds.

Arabis ophira is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
