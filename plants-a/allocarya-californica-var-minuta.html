Allocarya californica var. minuta

Allocarya californica var. minuta, commonly known as the dwarf California allocarya, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of California.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, featuring dense, compact foliage. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer.

This variety is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.
