Albuca villosa

Albuca villosa, commonly known as the velvet albuca, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Albuca villosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
