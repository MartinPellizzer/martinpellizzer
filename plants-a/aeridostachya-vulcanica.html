Aeridostachya vulcanica

Aeridostachya vulcanica, commonly known as the volcanic flame orchid, is a unique plant native to the volcanic regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red and orange flower spikes that bloom during the dry season. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, volcanic soils and requires high temperatures and low humidity to flourish. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

This species is notable for its resilience and is often found growing on rocky outcrops near active or dormant volcanoes.
