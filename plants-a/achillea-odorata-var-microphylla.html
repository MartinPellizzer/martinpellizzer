Achillea odorata var. microphylla

Achillea odorata var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces flat, composite flower heads that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal medicine.
