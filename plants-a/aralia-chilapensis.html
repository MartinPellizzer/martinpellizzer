Aralia chilapensis

Aralia chilapensis, commonly known as the Chilean aralia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate forests and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its berries, which are toxic to humans, are an important food source for certain bird species.

Aralia chilapensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and unique growth habit.
