Astragalus amherstianus subsp. subumbellatus

Astragalus amherstianus subsp.

subumbellatus, commonly known as Amherst's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to orange flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its umbellate inflorescence, which consists of clusters of small, pea-like flowers. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
