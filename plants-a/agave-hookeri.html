Agave hookeri

Agave hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's agave, is a species native to the United States, particularly found in Texas and New Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing small, white flowers. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Agave hookeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
