Andropogon pilosellus

Andropogon pilosellus, commonly known as hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in open woodlands and prairie habitats, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil.

Andropogon pilosellus is also used in restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
