Alstroemeria setacea

Alstroemeria setacea, commonly known as the spiked lily or the Peruvian lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, and often spiky leaves, which are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of colorful, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, yellow, and white, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Alstroemeria setacea is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
