Anemia raddiana

Anemia raddiana, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

The tree is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, growing in well-drained soils.
