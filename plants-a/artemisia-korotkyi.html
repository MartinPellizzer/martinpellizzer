Artemisia korotkyi

Artemisia korotkyi, commonly known as the Korotkyi sage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
