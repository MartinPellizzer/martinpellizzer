Abutilon crispum var. imberbe

Abutilon crispum var. imberbe, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a hairy texture that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and vibrant floral displays.
