Aster autumnalis

Aster autumnalis, commonly known as the New England aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows up to 2 to 4 feet tall, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
