Adiantopsis crinoidea

Adiantopsis crinoidea, commonly known as the feather fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in dense forests. Its leaves are bioluminescent, emitting a faint blue glow at night, which helps attract pollinators.

This unique trait makes it a fascinating subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
