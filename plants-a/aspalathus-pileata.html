Aspalathus pileata

Aspalathus pileata, commonly known as the Blaauwland bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to arid conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor, well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal, it is also cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
