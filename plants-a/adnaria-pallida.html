Adnaria pallida

Adnaria pallida, commonly known as the white sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with silver-gray foliage. Its flowers are typically arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and the leaves are narrow and slightly hairy.

Adnaria pallida is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
