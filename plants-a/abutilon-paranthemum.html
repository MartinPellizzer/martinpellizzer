Abutilon paranthemum

Abutilon paranthemum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
