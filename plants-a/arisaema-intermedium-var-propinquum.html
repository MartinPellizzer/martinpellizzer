Arisaema intermedium var. propinquum

Arisaema intermedium var. propinquum, commonly known as the narrowleaf Indian turnip, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces arrow-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are typically greenish-white, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
