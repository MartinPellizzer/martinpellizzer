Arenaria fasciculata subsp. cymifera

Arenaria fasciculata subsp.

cymifera, commonly known as the clustered sand-verbena, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States. It features small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer.

The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and inland deserts.

Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions typical of its native habitat.
