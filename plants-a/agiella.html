Agiella

Agiella, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a flowering species native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Agiella is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
