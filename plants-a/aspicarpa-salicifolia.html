Aspicarpa salicifolia

Aspicarpa salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf aspicarpa, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a soft, hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkin-like inflorescences, which appear in late winter to early spring. Its fruits are small, three-lobed berries that are often red or orange when mature.

Aspicarpa salicifolia is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
