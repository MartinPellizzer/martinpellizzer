Aegilops cylindrica f. gahvayii

Aegilops cylindrica f. gahvayii, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is often found in rocky soils and dry environments, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its botanical features include hollow stems and a fibrous root system, which aid in water retention.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its potential for forage and its genetic relationship to cultivated wheat.
