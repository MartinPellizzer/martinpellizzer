Alstroemeria speciosa

Alstroemeria speciosa, commonly known as the Peruvian lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors including red, orange, yellow, and pink. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves and produces clusters of blossoms that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

Alstroemeria speciosa is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
