Asterothrix asperrima

Asterothrix asperrima, commonly known as the rough asterothrix, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
