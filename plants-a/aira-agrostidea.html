Aira agrostidea

Aira agrostidea, commonly known as slender grass or meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often hairless, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Aira agrostidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
