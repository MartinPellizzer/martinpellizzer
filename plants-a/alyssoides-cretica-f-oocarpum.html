Alyssoides cretica f. oocarpum

Alyssoides cretica f. oocarpum, commonly known as the Mediterranean sand thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The inflorescence consists of yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disc, making it visually distinct within its habitat.
