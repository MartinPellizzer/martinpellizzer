Anchusa calcarea var. glabrescens

Anchusa calcarea var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous limestone anchusa, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
