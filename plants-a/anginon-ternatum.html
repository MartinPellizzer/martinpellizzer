Anginon ternatum

Anginon ternatum, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling tiny spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or near streams. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, providing a lush green backdrop to its showy blooms.

Anginon ternatum is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in traditional medicine.
