Acacia dictyoneura

Acacia dictyoneura, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, feather-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Its bark is smooth and often has a greyish or silver appearance, giving it its common name.

Acacia dictyoneura is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.
