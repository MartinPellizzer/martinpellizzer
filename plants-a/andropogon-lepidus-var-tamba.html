Andropogon lepidus var. tamba

Andropogon lepidus var. tamba, commonly known as tamba grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, wiry blades that often have a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces inflorescences that are usually panicle-like, bearing small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Its fibrous root system makes it a valuable plant for erosion control and livestock forage.
