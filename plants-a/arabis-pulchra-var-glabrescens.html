Arabis pulchra var. glabrescens

Arabis pulchra var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which can be reduced or absent in this variety.

The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in early spring, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.
