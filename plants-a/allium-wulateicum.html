Allium wulateicum

Allium wulateicum, commonly known as the Wulate onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to lilac-colored flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that store nutrients, enabling it to survive in harsh environmental conditions. Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
