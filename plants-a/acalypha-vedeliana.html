Acalypha vedeliana

Acalypha vedeliana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or redroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that range in color from green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Acalypha vedeliana is also known for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil types and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
