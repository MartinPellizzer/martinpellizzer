Aegilops triaristata subsp. typica

Aegilops triaristata subsp.

typica, commonly known as the three-awn grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its distinctive inflorescence composed of three awned spikelets.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and gravelly areas. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, while its flowering structures are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

This species is also of interest in agricultural research due to its potential for genetic contributions to wheat improvement.
