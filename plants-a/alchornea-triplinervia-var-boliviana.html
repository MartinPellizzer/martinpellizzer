Alchornea triplinervia var. boliviana

Alchornea triplinervia var. boliviana, commonly known as Bolivian three-nerved alchornea, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Bolivia.

It is characterized by its alternate, elliptic leaves with three prominent veins, giving it the species name *triplinervia*. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in moist, tropical or subtropical climates and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
