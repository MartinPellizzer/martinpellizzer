Acrocephalus termiticola

Acrocephalus termiticola, commonly known as the termite grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and savannas. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Acrocephalus termiticola is adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in nutrient-poor environments.
