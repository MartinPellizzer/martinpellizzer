Anthemis cretica subsp. argaea

Anthemis cretica subsp.

argaea, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Flossflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, compact clusters.

The leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a hairy surface, and are often aromatic. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
