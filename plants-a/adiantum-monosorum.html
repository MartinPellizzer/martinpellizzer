Adiantum monosorum

Adiantum monosorum, commonly known as the Southern Maidenhair Fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bi-lobed fronds that are typically bright green and have a graceful, feathery appearance. The plant prefers shady, moist environments and is often found growing in woodlands or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and can form dense colonies, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Adiantum monosorum is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is commonly cultivated as a decorative plant.
