Atriplex sordida

Atriplex sordida, commonly known as bitter saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, green to grayish leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, typical of the Amaranthaceae family. It is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable plant for stabilizing degraded lands.

Its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions makes it an important species in ecological restoration efforts.
