Aquilegia vulgaris f. subscapiformis

Aquilegia vulgaris f. subscapiformis, commonly known as the scapiform columbine, is a botanical variant of the common columbine.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated petals that form a narrow, scapiform shape, giving it a unique appearance compared to the typical form. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is typically found in woodland areas and moist soils, thriving in partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its vibrant floral display.
