Abies cedrus

Abies cedrus, commonly known as the cedar fir, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green foliage, and distinctive, flattened, scale-like leaves. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and reddish-brown, providing protection against environmental stress.

Abies cedrus is valued for its timber and has been historically used in construction and as a source of aromatic resin.
