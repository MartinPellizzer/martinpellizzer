Astragalus ketzkhovelianus

Astragalus ketzkhovelianus, commonly known as Ketzkhovel's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
