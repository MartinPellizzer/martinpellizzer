Anchomanes boehmii

Anchomanes boehmii, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are often blue or violet in color. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Anchomanes boehmii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
