Adenophora obtusifolia

Adenophora obtusifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is often found in mountainous regions.

Adenophora obtusifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
