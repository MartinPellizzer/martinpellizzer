Apium graveolens var. erfurtense

Apium graveolens var. erfurtense, commonly known as the Erfurt parsley, is a wild variety of celery native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and small, edible leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its leaves and stems are used in traditional herbal medicine for their digestive and diuretic properties.

The variety is valued for its unique flavor and historical use in both culinary and medicinal applications.
