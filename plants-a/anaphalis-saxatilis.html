Anaphalis saxatilis

Anaphalis saxatilis, commonly known as the stone woolyhead, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in alpine or subalpine environments.

Anaphalis saxatilis is a perennial herb that is well adapted to cold, harsh climates.
