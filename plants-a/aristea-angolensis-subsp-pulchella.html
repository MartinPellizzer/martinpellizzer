Aristea angolensis subsp. pulchella

Aristea angolensis subsp.

pulchella, commonly known as the Angola aristea, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences bearing pale blue to purple florets, which are typical of the genus Aristea. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
