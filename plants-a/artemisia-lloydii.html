Artemisia lloydii

Artemisia lloydii, commonly known as the Santa Barbara wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of aromatic, narrow, and linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in dense, rounded shapes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Artemisia lloydii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
