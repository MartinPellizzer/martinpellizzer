Achillea sibirica subsp. wilsoniana

Achillea sibirica subsp.

wilsoniana, commonly known as Siberian yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features basal rosettes of silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves and produces dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils. Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer to early autumn.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
