Arachniodes acuminata

Arachniodes acuminata, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are bi-lobed and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

Arachniodes acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
