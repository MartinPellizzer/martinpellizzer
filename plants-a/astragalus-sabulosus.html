Astragalus sabulosus

Astragalus sabulosus, commonly known as the sand milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in desert environments. The plant produces small, yellow to greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh conditions.

Astragalus sabulosus is also known for its ecological importance, providing food for various pollinators and wildlife.
