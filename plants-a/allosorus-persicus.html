Allosorus persicus

Allosorus persicus, commonly known as Persian cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking violet to pink flowers during the spring and early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers cool, shady environments, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and unique foliage.

Allosorus persicus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
