Alchornea nemoralis

Alchornea nemoralis, commonly known as the rough alchorneas, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape and have a rough, hairy texture.

Alchornea nemoralis is known for its adaptability to arid environments and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.
