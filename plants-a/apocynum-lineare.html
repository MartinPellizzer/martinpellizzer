Apocynum lineare

Apocynum lineare, commonly known as narrowleaf milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweeds and related plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, clustered in umbels, and produce a milky sap that is toxic to many animals.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
