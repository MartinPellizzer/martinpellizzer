Aristolochia robertii

Aristolochia robertii, commonly known as the Indian birthwort, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color. The plant produces small, dark blue or black berries that are edible but should be consumed with caution. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.

The genus Aristolochia includes many species with similar morphological features and some containing toxic compounds.
