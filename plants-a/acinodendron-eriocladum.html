Acinodendron eriocladum

Acinodendron eriocladum, commonly known as the woolly acacia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Its leaves are typically alternate and pinnate, with small, rounded leaflets.

Acinodendron eriocladum is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
