Astilbe grandis

Astilbe grandis, commonly known as the giant astilbe, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems that bear clusters of feathery, plume-like flowers. The foliage is typically dark green, with a slightly serrated edge, and it thrives in moist, shady environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Astilbe grandis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
