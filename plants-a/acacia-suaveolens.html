Acacia suaveolens

Acacia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow, globular flower heads that emit a sweet, honey-like fragrance. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Acacia suaveolens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
