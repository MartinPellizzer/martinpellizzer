Astragalus darumbium

Astragalus darumbium, commonly known as the Darumbi milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it plays a role in soil stabilization due to its deep root system.

Astragalus darumbium is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
