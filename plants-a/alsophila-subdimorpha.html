Alsophila subdimorpha

Alsophila subdimorpha, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive crown of fertile fronds that are shorter and more compact than the sterile ones. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in tropical and subtropical rainforests.

The fronds are often used in traditional practices and can be cultivated as an ornamental plant in suitable climates.
