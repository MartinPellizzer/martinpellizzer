Ageratina hirtella

Ageratina hirtella, commonly known as the hairy ageratum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, grayish-green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 inches. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
