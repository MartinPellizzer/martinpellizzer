Amyris ignea

Amyris ignea, commonly known as the fire tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Amyris ignea thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
