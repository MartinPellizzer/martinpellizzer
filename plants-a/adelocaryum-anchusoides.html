Adelocaryum anchusoides

Adelocaryum anchusoides, commonly known as the anchusoid false heather, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a dense, ornamental appearance.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
