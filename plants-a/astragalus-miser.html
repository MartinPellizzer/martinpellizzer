Astragalus miser

Astragalus miser, commonly known as the poor man's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing plants.

Astragalus miser is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
