Arenaria ciliata f. multicaulis

Arenaria ciliata f. multicaulis, commonly known as the many-stemmed sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features a compact rosette of narrow, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine cilia, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This frost-tolerant species is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats.
