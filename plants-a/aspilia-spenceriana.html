Aspilia spenceriana

Aspilia spenceriana, commonly known as the Spencer's torch flower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescence that resembles a torch. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Aspilia spenceriana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
