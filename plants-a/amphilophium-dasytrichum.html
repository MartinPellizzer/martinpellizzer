Amphilophium dasytrichum

Amphilophium dasytrichum, commonly known as the hairy-leaved philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, hairy trichomes, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage.

Its growth habit involves twining around supports, making it suitable for trellises or hanging baskets.
