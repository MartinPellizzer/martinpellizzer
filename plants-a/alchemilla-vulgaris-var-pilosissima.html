Alchemilla vulgaris var. pilosissima

Alchemilla vulgaris var. pilosissima, commonly known as the velvet cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with hairy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal remedies due to their purported medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
