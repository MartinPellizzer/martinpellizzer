Aloe pearsonii

Aloe pearsonii, commonly known as the Pearson's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. It is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
