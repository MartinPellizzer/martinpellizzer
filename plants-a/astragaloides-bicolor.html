Astragaloides bicolor

Astragaloides bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive two-toned flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white with a contrasting yellow or pink base.

Astragaloides bicolor is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for native pollinators.
