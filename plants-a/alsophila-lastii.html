Alsophila lastii

Alsophila lastii, commonly known as the lastii tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a slow growth rate and thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil. Its rhizome system is robust, allowing it to anchor firmly in the soil and absorb nutrients efficiently.

Alsophila lastii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
