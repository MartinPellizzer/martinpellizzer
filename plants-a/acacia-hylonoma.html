Acacia hylonoma

Acacia hylonoma, commonly known as the African wattle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its bright yellow floral spikes that appear in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its root system is adapted to arid conditions, allowing it to thrive in semi-desert environments.

The bark and leaves have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.
