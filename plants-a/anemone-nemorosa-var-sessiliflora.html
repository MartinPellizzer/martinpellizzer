Anemone nemorosa var. sessiliflora

Anemone nemorosa var. sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-leaved wood anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland environments, often forming dense colonies. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly heart-shaped and persist through the winter, while its flowers are white with yellow centers and appear in early spring. The flowers are borne on leafless stems, and the plant is known for its early blooming habit.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often showy flowers and toxic sap.
