Acalypha australis var. lanceolata

Acalypha australis var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in coastal landscaping for its hardiness.

The leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.
