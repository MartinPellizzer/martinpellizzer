Alyssum biovulatum

Alyssum biovulatum, commonly known as the yellow alyssum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species belonging to the Brassicaceae family. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are prostrate and often branching.

Alyssum biovulatum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract beneficial insects.
