Avena sativa subsp. nuda

Avena sativa subsp.

nuda, commonly known as naked oat, is a subspecies of the oat plant characterized by its lack of awns on the glumes. It is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

The plant produces slender, erect stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in panicles.

The seeds, which are small and oval, are typically harvested for use in food, animal feed, and industrial applications.
