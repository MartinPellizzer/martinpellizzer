Astragalus macrotrichus

Astragalus macrotrichus, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with long, hairy stipules. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several kidney-shaped seeds.

Astragalus macrotrichus is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes.
