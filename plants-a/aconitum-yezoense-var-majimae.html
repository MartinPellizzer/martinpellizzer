Aconitum yezoense var. majimae

Aconitum yezoense var. majimae, commonly known as the Yezo Aconite, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense, spikelike clusters. Its roots contain toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or mountainous regions.
