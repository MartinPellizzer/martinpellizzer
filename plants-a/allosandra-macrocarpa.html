Allosandra macrocarpa

Allosandra macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, woody, elongated fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Allosandra macrocarpa is valued for its aromatic wood and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
