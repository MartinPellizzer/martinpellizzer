Anagallis tenella var. filiformis

Anagallis tenella var. filiformis, commonly known as the slender pinkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, trailing stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded environments.

It is often found in rocky or disturbed areas, making it a characteristic species of Mediterranean flora.
