Anacamptis laxiflora subsp. dinsmorei

Anacamptis laxiflora subsp.

dinsmorei, commonly known as the Dinsmore's spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of pale green to white flowers that resemble spider-like structures.

The plant typically grows in moist, wooded habitats, often in sandy or loamy soils. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

This subspecies is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
