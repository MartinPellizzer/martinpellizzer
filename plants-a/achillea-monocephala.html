Achillea monocephala

Achillea monocephala, commonly known as the single-head yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are finely divided and covered in a soft, hairy texture, contributing to its medicinal and ornamental value.

Achillea monocephala is often used in traditional herbal remedies due to its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
