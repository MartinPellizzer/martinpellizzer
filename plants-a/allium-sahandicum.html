Allium sahandicum

Allium sahandicum, commonly known as the sahand garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used as a spice in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its culinary and medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
