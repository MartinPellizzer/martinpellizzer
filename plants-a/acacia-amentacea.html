Acacia amentacea

Acacia amentacea, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a flowering plant native to Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its feathery, pendulous branches and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Acacia amentacea thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
