Asarum memmingeri

Asarum memmingeri, commonly known as the Memminger's wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, hooded flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic underground tuber that is often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
