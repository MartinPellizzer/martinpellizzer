Amyema beccarii

Amyema beccarii, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It has small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along its stems. The plant produces white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are small, round, and often contain a single seed.

Amyema beccarii plays an important ecological role by providing food for native birds and insects.
