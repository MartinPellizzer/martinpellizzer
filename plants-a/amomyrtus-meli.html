Amomyrtus meli

Amomyrtus meli, commonly known as the Meli tree, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often twisted trunk and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by spherical, red or purple berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic leaves and has cultural significance in the region.
