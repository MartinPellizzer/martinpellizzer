Aristolochia perangustifolia

Aristolochia perangustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and distinctive, hooded flowers that are often a deep purple or maroon color. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, sticky coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
