Astragalus ghahremanii

Astragalus ghahremanii, commonly known as the Ghahreman milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stem and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to green flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. Astragalus ghahremanii is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep root systems that help it access underground water.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
