Arctostaphylos pungens subsp. parryana

Arctostaphylos pungens subsp.

parryana, commonly known as Parry manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in white or pink.

The plant produces red or black berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the dry, Mediterranean climate of its native range.
