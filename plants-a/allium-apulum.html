Allium apulum

Allium apulum, commonly known as the purple onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust, fleshy bulb that is typically purple or pink in color, which distinguishes it from other allium species. The plant produces upright, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Allium apulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays for its striking appearance.
