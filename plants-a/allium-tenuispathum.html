Allium tenuispathum

Allium tenuispathum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures similar to garlic, which are used for culinary and medicinal purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium tenuispathum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
