Achyranthemum paniculatum

Achyranthemum paniculatum, commonly known as the Jerusalem artichoke, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant produces edible tubers that are rich in inulin, a type of dietary fiber. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
