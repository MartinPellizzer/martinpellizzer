Apargia hyoseroides

Apargia hyoseroides, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are typically hairy and bear alternate, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Apargia hyoseroides is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its milky sap, which is characteristic of the genus.

It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and serves as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
