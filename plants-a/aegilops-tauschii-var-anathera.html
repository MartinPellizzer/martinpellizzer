Aegilops tauschii var. anathera

Aegilops tauschii var. anathera, commonly known as the intermediate wheatgrass, is a wild relative of wheat with significant botanical interest.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its robust growth and distinctive inflorescence structure. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are arranged in a loose panicle. Its genome contains valuable genetic diversity that is often utilized in breeding programs for improved wheat varieties.

This variety is particularly noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and its potential in sustainable agriculture.
