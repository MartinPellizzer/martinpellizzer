Aster menziesii var. bernardinus

Aster menziesii var. bernardinus, commonly known as the Cascade aster, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features upright stems covered in narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy, blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
