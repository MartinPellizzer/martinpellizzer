Anthemis palustris

Anthemis palustris, commonly known as marsh chamomile, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive, aromatic scent. The plant typically grows in moist or waterlogged soils, often in meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and branched.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with the flowers used in traditional remedies for their calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
