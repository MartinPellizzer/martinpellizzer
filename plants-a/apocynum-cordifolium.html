Apocynum cordifolium

Apocynum cordifolium, commonly known as the purple milkweed, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains cardiac glycosides, which are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
