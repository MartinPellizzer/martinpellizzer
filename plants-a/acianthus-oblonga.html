Acianthus oblonga

Acianthus oblonga, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale green to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. It is a terrestrial orchid, often found in coastal regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
