Agave arcedianoensis

Agave arcedianoensis, commonly known as the Mexican rock agave, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears numerous white or pale yellow flowers, which attract pollinators such as bats and insects. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a high tolerance for drought and poor soil conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in its native habitat.
