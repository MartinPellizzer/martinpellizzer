Alocasia indica var. variegata

Alocasia indica var. variegata, commonly known as the Variegated Indian Elephant's Ear, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with striking white or yellow variegation. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes many ornamental and sometimes toxic plants. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture for its dramatic visual impact.

It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
