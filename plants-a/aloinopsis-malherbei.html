Aloinopsis malherbei

Aloinopsis malherbei, commonly known as the Malherb's aloinopsis, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces striking yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on tall, branched stems. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
