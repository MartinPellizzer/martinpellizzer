Apocynum venetum subsp. armenum

Apocynum venetum subsp.

armenum, commonly known as Armenian dogbane, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and showy flowers.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and feature five petals, usually in shades of white or pink.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
