Aster lyallii

Aster lyallii, commonly known as Lyall's aster, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with white ray florets, and bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Aster lyallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also an important species in New Zealand's native flora.
