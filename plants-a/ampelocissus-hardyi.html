Ampelocissus hardyi

Ampelocissus hardyi, commonly known as the hardy cat's claw vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, berry-like fruits. The plant is known for its strong, woody vines that can climb trees or structures. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Ampelocissus hardyi is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and ability to cover trellises or walls.
