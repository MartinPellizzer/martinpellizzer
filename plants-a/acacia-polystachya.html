Acacia polystachya

Acacia polystachya, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grey-green stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, feathery clusters, which bloom in spring. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Acacia polystachya is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
