Amygdalus brahuica subsp. afghanica

Amygdalus brahuica subsp.

afghanica, commonly known as the Afghan almond, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and Pakistan. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, similar to other members of the Prunus genus. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
