Achillea × haenkeana

Achillea × haenkeana, commonly known as the hybrid yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Achillea × haenkeana is also noted for its hardiness and adaptability to a variety of soil types.
