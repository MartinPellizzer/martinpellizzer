Allium tuvinicum

Allium tuvinicum, commonly known as the Tuvinian onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, ovoid bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Allium tuvinicum is valued for its aromatic properties and hardy nature.
