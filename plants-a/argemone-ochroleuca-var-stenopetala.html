Argemone ochroleuca var. stenopetala

Argemone ochroleuca var. stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled argemone, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and latex-producing sap. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers are clustered in cyathia, a distinctive feature of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking yellow blooms.
