Amaryllis caspia

Amaryllis caspia, commonly known as the Caspian amaryllis, is a striking plant native to the Caspian Sea region.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 cm in diameter, typically in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Amaryllis caspia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown in gardens or as a houseplant.
