Agapetes vaccinioides

Agapetes vaccinioides, commonly known as the dwarf snowberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, and it produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring. These flowers are followed by small, bright red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

Agapetes vaccinioides thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.
