Ammannia evansiana

Ammannia evansiana, commonly known as prairie ammannia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, wetlands, and other wet habitats. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
