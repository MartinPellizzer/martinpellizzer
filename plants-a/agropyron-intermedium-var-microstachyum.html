Agropyron intermedium var. microstachyum

Agropyron intermedium var. microstachyum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves that are often used in landscaping for erosion control. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
