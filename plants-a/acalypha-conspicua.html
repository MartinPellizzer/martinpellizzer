Acalypha conspicua

Acalypha conspicua, commonly known as the flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble flames, consisting of red, orange, and yellow bracts. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often with a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Acalypha conspicua is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
