Ardisia revoluta

Ardisia revoluta, commonly known as the glossy privet, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green on top, with a lighter underside. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and showy fruit.
