Acalypha cucullata

Acalypha cucullata, commonly known as the "Cape gooseberry" or "silverleaf," is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters tall, featuring ovate to lanceolate leaves with a silvery-white underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by spherical, fleshy berries that are edible when ripe. Its ornamental value is due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
