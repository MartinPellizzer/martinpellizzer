Allium sibiricum schoenoprasoides

Allium sibiricum schoenoprasoides, commonly known as Siberian leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are long, narrow, and bluish-green, often with a garlic-like scent. The plant produces umbel-like clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers in late summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a valued plant in some cuisines.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
