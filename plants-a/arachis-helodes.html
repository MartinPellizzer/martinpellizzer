Arachis helodes

Arachis helodes, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a species of legume native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces distinctive, woody pods containing edible seeds. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it exhibits a unique symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

The plant's ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
