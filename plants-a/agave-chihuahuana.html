Agave chihuahuana

Agave chihuahuana, commonly known as the Chihuahua agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick, fleshy, and covered in a layer of white wax. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears clusters of white or yellow flowers, which bloom after many years. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
