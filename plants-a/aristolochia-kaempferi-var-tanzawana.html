Aristolochia kaempferi var. tanzawana

Aristolochia kaempferi var. tanzawana, commonly known as the Tanzanian arrow vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex chemical composition, including various alkaloids. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural and traditional uses.
