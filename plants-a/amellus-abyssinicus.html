Amellus abyssinicus

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations above 2,000 meters. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
