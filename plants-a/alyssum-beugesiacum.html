Alyssum beugesiacum

Alyssum beugesiacum, commonly known as the European alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Alyssum beugesiacum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
