Aristolochia pubescens

Aristolochia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, heart-shaped flowers that are often greenish-brown in color. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The species is part of the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure and historical use in traditional medicine.
