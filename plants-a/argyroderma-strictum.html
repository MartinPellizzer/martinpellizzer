Argyroderma strictum

Argyroderma strictum, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant has a compact, upright growth habit with multiple branching stems. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Argyroderma strictum is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
