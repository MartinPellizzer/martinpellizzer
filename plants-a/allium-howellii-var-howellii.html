Allium howellii var. howellii

Allium howellii var. howellii, commonly known as Howell's garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bulbous structures that resemble garlic cloves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a short, stout stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
