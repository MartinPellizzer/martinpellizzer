Agropyron ciliare f. okuyamae

Agropyron ciliare f. okuyamae, commonly known as the Okuyama's needlegrass, is a variant of the common needlegrass species.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is typically found in temperate regions, often in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological studies in its native range.
