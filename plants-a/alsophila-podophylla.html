Alsophila podophylla

Alsophila podophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and feathery, compound fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests. Its rhizome system supports the growth of the trunk and helps anchor the plant in moist soil.

The fronds are typically evergreen and can reach lengths of up to several meters, contributing to its ornamental value in cultivation.
