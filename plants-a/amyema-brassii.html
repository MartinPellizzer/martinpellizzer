Amyema brassii

Amyema brassii, commonly known as the yellow box, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. The tree is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and lance-shaped, glossy green leaves. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, typically during spring, which are visited by various pollinators.

Amyema brassii is known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
