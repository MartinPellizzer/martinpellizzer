Artemisia lobelii

Artemisia lobelii, commonly known as Lobel's sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which consists of aromatic, narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Artemisia lobelii plays a vital role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in arid regions.
