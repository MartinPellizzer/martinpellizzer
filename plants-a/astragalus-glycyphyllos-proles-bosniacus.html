Astragalus glycyphyllos proles bosniacus

Astragalus glycyphyllos proles bosniacus, commonly known as the Bosnian sweetroot, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemate inflorescences. Its roots are often tuberous and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

It is native to the Balkan region, including parts of Bosnia and Herzegovina.
