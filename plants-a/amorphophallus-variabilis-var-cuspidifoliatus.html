Amorphophallus variabilis var. cuspidifoliatus

Amorphophallus variabilis var. cuspidifoliatus, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length, giving it a dramatic and ornamental appearance. The plant produces a solitary, spadix-like inflorescence that can reach heights of over 3 meters, often with a distinctive red or purple coloration at the base. It thrives in warm, humid environments with rich, well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.

Due to its size and visual impact, it is often cultivated as a showy ornamental plant.
