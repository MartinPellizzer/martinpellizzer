Agropyron magellanicum var. condensatum

Agropyron magellanicum var. condensatum, commonly known as the Magellanic grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in temperate to cold climates, often found in grasslands and mountainous regions. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are tightly clustered, giving it a compact appearance.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
