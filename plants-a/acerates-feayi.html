Acerates feayi

Acerates feayi, commonly known as the Feay's maple, is a rare and striking member of the Aceraceae family.

Native to the mountainous regions of South America, it is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring vibrant, maple-like leaves with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by winged seeds, typical of the genus. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.

Its botanical significance lies in its distinctive morphology and ecological role in its native environment.
