Adiantum × tracyi

Adiantum × tracyi, commonly known as the silver lace fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Adiantum capillus-veneris and Adiantum raddianum.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that are typically gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with finely divided pinnae that contribute to its ornamental value.

Adiantum × tracyi is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its elegant foliage and ease of care.
