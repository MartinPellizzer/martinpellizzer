Aeschynomene petraea var. grandiflora

Aeschynomene petraea var. grandiflora, commonly known as the rock aeschynomene, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pale blue to purple flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in poor soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.

Its ornamental value and resilience make it a useful plant for landscaping in dry climates.
