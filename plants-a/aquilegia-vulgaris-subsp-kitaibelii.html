Aquilegia vulgaris subsp. kitaibelii

Aquilegia vulgaris subsp.

kitaibelii, commonly known as the Kitaibel's columbine, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It features distinctive spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and pink, which are adapted for pollination by specific insects.

The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves with a serrated margin, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
