Asclepias neglecta

Asclepias neglecta, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces unique seed pods that contain fluffy, white seeds with silky appendages, aiding in wind dispersal. Asclepias neglecta is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its medicinal and ecological significance.

It serves as a host plant for the monarch butterfly and contains latex sap that can be toxic to some animals.
