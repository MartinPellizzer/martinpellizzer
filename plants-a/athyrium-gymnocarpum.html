Athyrium gymnocarpum

Athyrium gymnocarpum, commonly known as the naked-seeded water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically bioluminescent under certain conditions. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its reproductive structures are unique, with spores produced in specialized, unbranched sporangia.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining soil stability in its native habitats.
