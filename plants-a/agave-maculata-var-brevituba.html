Agave maculata var. brevituba

Agave maculata var. brevituba, commonly known as the spotted agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green with white or yellowish markings. The leaves are short and blunt-tipped, giving the plant a compact appearance. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer.

This variety is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.
