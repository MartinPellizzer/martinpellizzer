Astragalus dictyolobus

Astragalus dictyolobus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to harsh environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

Astragalus dictyolobus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
