Aconitum variegatum var. stiriacum

Aconitum variegatum var. stiriacum, commonly known as the Variegated Monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hooded flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its toxic nature, it should be handled with care.
