Allium paniculatum var. typicum

Allium paniculatum var. typicum, commonly known as the purple onion.

This perennial herb is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small, bell-shaped purple flowers. It has basal leaves that are typically linear and grass-like, and the plant produces bulbs that are edible and resemble those of the common onion. Native to southern Europe and western Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
