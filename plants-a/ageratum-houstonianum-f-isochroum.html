Ageratum houstonianum f. isochroum

Ageratum houstonianum f. isochroum, commonly known as the isochroum ageratum, is a compact, flowering plant native to Central America.

It features dense clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, purple, and blue, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has bushy, aromatic foliage that is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its compact growth habit and extended blooming period make it a versatile addition to both landscapes and floral arrangements.
