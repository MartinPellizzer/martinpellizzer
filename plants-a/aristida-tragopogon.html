Aristida tragopogon

Aristida tragopogon, commonly known as the yellow bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue that gives it its common name.

Aristida tragopogon plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
