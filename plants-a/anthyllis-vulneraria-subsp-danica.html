Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. danica

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

danica, commonly known as the Danish broom, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is noted for its adaptability to harsh environments and its role in soil stabilization.
