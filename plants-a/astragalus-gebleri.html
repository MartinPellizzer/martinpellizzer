Astragalus gebleri

Astragalus gebleri, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or white pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and supports local pollinators.

Astragalus gebleri is also valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
