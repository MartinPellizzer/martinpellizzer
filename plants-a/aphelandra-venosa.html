Aphelandra venosa

Aphelandra venosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are deeply veined and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Aphelandra venosa thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
