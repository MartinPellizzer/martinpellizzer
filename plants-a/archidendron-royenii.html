Archidendron royenii

Archidendron royenii, commonly known as the Royal trumpet tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces flat, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
