Apodanthera linearis var. linearis

Apodanthera linearis var. linearis, commonly known as the linear beardtongue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces spikes of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Its unique floral structure and attractive foliage make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
