Atraphaxis schischkinii

Atraphaxis schischkinii, commonly known as the Schischkin's desert lily, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xerophytic ecosystems.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptations to survive in extreme desert conditions.
