Agave deserti subsp. simplex

Agave deserti subsp.

simplex, commonly known as the Linearleaf agave, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and long, narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often lined with sharp spines.

The plant produces a tall inflorescence bearing yellowish-green flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its succulent leaves and stems.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional practices.
