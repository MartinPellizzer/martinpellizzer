Acer truncatum var. nudum

Acer truncatum var. nudum, commonly known as the naked maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and distinctive palmate leaves that are often lobed and have a reddish or bronze hue in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant fall color and unique foliage structure.
