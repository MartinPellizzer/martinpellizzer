Aristolochia sinoburmanica

Aristolochia sinoburmanica, commonly known as the Burmese pipevine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, horseshoe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, often unpleasant odor. The plant is part of the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

The plant contains aristolochic acids, which are toxic and have been linked to kidney damage in humans.
