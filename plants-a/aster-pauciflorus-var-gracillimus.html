Aster pauciflorus var. gracillimus

Aster pauciflorus var. gracillimus, commonly known as the slenderleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
