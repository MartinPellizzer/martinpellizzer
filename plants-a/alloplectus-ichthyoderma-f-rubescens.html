Alloplectus ichthyoderma f. rubescens

Alloplectus ichthyoderma f. rubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed marsh willow, is a variant of the marsh willow characterized by its distinctive reddish stems.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in catkins, and it plays a role in supporting local insect populations.
