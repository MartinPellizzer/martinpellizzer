Anatherum virginicum

Anatherum virginicum, commonly known as little bluestem, is a native grass species of the United States, particularly prevalent in the prairie regions of the central and eastern states.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage that turns reddish-brown in the fall, and its slender, cylindrical seed heads that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to dry soils and is well adapted to full sun, making it a hardy component of native grasslands. It plays a vital role in soil stabilization and provides habitat and forage for various wildlife species.

Anatherum virginicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
