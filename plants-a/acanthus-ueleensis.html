Acanthus ueleensis

Acanthus ueleensis, commonly known as the Uele acanthus, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with prominent veins and its showy, spire-like inflorescences. The plant features vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to red, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its robust growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and botanical collections.
