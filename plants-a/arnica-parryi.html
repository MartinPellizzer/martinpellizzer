Arnica parryi

Arnica parryi, commonly known as Parry's arnica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Arnica parryi is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
