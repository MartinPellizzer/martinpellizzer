Anthyllis tetraphylla var. purpurea

Anthyllis tetraphylla var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spherical, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

Anthyllis tetraphylla var. purpurea is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.
