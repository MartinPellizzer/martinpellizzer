Acacia euthycarpa subsp. euthycarpa

Acacia euthycarpa subsp.

euthycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and small, yellow flowers that appear in globular clusters.

The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, linear leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand dry conditions.
