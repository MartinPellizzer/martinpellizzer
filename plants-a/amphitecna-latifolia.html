Amphitecna latifolia

Amphitecna latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed amphitecna, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
