Aesculus watsoniana

Aesculus watsoniana, commonly known as the Chinese horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces round, spiny fruits containing one or two seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
