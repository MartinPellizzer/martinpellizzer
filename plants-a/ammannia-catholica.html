Ammannia catholica

Ammannia catholica, commonly known as prairie ammannia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, prairies, and along stream banks. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually red-veined and have a distinctive waxy coating. Its small, clustered flowers are pink to red and bloom in late summer.

Ammannia catholica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for pollinators.
