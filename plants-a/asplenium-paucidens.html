Asplenium paucidens

Asplenium paucidens, commonly known as the sparse spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves that are usually dark green and often have a slightly curled appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices or forest floors. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged along the undersides of the fronds.

Asplenium paucidens is relatively uncommon and is often found in specific microhabitats, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
