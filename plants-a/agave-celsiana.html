Agave celsiana

Agave celsiana, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, blue-gray leaves with sharp spines. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears numerous small, greenish-white flowers. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy leaves.

Agave celsiana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and resilience.
