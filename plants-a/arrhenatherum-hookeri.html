Arrhenatherum hookeri

Arrhenatherum hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces airy, feathery panicles of flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and its inflorescence is a key feature for identification.

Arrhenatherum hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
