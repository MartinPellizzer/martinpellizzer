Artemisia santolinifolia subsp. stepposa

Artemisia santolinifolia subsp.

stepposa, commonly known as steppe wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the steppes of Central Asia. It features aromatic, gray-green foliage with silvery-white undersides, and its leaves are lanceolate to oblanceolate in shape.

The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typically clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.

This subspecies is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
