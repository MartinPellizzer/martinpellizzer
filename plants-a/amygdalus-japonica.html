Amygdalus japonica

Amygdalus japonica, commonly known as the Japanese almond, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that resemble almonds, though they are not related to true almonds. Its ornamental value is appreciated in gardens for its delicate blossoms and compact growth habit.

Amygdalus japonica is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to urban environments.
