Asperula ambleia

Asperula ambleia, commonly known as the creeping azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white during late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of glossy, elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall.

Asperula ambleia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.
