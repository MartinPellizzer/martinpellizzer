Anemone pulsatilla var. ucrainica

Anemone pulsatilla var. ucrainica, commonly known as the Ukrainian pasqueflower, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, pink or white flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and rocky soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers bloom in early spring.

The species is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescence and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
