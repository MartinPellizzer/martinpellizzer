Alniaria folgneri

Alniaria folgneri, commonly known as the desert willow, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, willow-like leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Alniaria folgneri plays an important role in supporting local pollinators, particularly hummingbirds and bees.
