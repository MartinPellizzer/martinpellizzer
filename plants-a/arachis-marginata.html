Arachis marginata

Arachis marginata, commonly known as the Brazilian pea or the mountain pea, is a species of legume native to South America.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky areas.

Arachis marginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
