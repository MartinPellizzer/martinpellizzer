Amellus echinocephalon

Amellus echinocephalon, commonly known as the spiny-headed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiny, spherical seed heads that give it its common name. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Amellus echinocephalon is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it adapts well to harsh environmental conditions.
