Alnus glutinosa subf. minuticarpa

Alnus glutinosa subf.

minuticarpa, commonly known as the dwarf sticky alder, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that appear in early spring.

The plant produces small, sticky fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
