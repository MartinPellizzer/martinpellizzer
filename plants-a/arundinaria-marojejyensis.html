Arundinaria marojejyensis

Arundinaria marojejyensis, commonly known as the Marojejy bamboo, is a unique grass-like plant native to the Marojejy National Park in northeastern Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it one of the tallest bamboo species in the world. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typical of the grass family. It thrives in humid, tropical conditions and is adapted to the region's specific climate and soil types.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for conservation efforts.
