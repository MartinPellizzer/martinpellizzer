Alyssum borzaeanum

Alyssum borzaeanum, commonly known as the Borzaean alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves that are opposite and linear in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Alyssum borzaeanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
