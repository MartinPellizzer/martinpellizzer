Agave producta

Agave producta, commonly known as the Desert Agave, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color and lined with sharp teeth. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence bearing small, white flowers, which are followed by spiny capsules containing seeds. Agave producta is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its leaves and stems.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appearance.
