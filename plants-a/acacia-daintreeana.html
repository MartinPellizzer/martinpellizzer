Acacia daintreeana

Acacia daintreeana, commonly known as the Daintree acacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of northeastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow, globular flower heads that appear in clusters. Acacia daintreeana plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
