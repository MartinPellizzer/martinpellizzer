Anacardium humile

Anacardium humile, commonly known as the little cashew or dwarf cashew, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the poison ivy and mango. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oval, kidney-shaped fruits. The fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a toxic, resinous husk that must be carefully removed.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
