Acmella grandiflora var. grandiflora

Acmella grandiflora var. grandiflora, commonly known as the yellow water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, floating leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with prominent, spiky stamens. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the early morning and closing by midday.

It is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
