Aristida arundinacea

Aristida arundinacea, commonly known as giant false panicgrass, is a tall perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, seed-bearing spikes that are often seen in the fall. It is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
