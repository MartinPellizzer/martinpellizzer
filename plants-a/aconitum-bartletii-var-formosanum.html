Aconitum bartletii var. formosanum

Aconitum bartletii var. formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant features palmately compound leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It produces toxic alkaloids, making it a potentially dangerous plant to handle.

This variant is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and unique floral morphology.
