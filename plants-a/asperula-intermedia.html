Asperula intermedia

Asperula intermedia, commonly known as the creeping azalea, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that remain throughout the year, providing dense foliage. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
