Astragalus bellii

Astragalus bellii, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

Astragalus bellii is known for its ability to thrive in arid environments and is often found in areas with well-drained soils.
