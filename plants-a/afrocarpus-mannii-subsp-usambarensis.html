Afrocarpus mannii subsp. usambarensis

Afrocarpus mannii subsp.

usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambarensis yellowwood, is a member of the Podocarpaceae family. It is a tall evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 40 meters.

The tree has a straight trunk with a thick, flaky bark that is often fissured. Its leaves are leathery, dark green, and arranged spirally along the branches.

This subspecies is native to the Eastern Arc Mountains of Tanzania and is known for its ecological and possibly medicinal significance.
