Ageratina adenophora

Ageratina adenophora, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fluffy, blue-gray inflorescences that resemble mist, giving it its common name. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Ageratina adenophora is sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
