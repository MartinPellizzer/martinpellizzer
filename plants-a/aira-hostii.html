Aira hostii

Aira hostii, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Aira hostii is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid climates.
