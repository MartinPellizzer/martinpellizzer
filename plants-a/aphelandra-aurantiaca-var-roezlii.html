Aphelandra aurantiaca var. roezlii

Aphelandra aurantiaca var. roezlii, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, elongated leaves that display a gradient of yellow to orange hues. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Aphelandra aurantiaca var. roezlii is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and air-purifying qualities.
