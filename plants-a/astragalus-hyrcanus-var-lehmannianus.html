Astragalus hyrcanus var. lehmannianus

Astragalus hyrcanus var. lehmannianus, commonly known as the Lehmann's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often in rocky or sandy soils. It produces small, yellow to green flowers that bloom in early summer.

This variant is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological features compared to other Astragalus species.
