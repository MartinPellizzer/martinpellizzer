Anthemis kitanovii

Anthemis kitanovii, commonly known as the Kitanov's ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
