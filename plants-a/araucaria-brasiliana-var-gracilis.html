Araucaria brasiliana var. gracilis

Araucaria brasiliana var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender Brazilian pine, is a rare and endangered conifer native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and long, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in spirals. The plant produces both male and female cones, with the female cones developing into woody, seed-containing structures. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the specific microclimates of its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
