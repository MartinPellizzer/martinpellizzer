Aristolochia esperanzae var. major

Aristolochia esperanzae var. major, commonly known as the Esperanza vine, is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family and is native to parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe or a pipe smoker's face. The plant produces fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its vines can grow up to several meters in length, often climbing or trailing along supports.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
