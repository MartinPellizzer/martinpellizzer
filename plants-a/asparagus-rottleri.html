Asparagus rottleri

Asparagus rottleri, commonly known as the Rottler's asparagus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Asparagus rottleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
