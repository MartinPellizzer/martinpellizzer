Artemisia vulgaris subsp. tilesii

Artemisia vulgaris subsp.

tilesii, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils. It contains volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones, contributing to its medicinal and pesticidal properties.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
