Atriplex perrieri

Atriplex perrieri, commonly known as the Desert Saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, salt-tolerant leaves that help it survive in harsh, saline environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Atriplex perrieri plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soils and providing habitat and forage for desert wildlife.
