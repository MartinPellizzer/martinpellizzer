Amygdalus minor

Amygdalus minor, commonly known as the dwarf almond, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that resemble almonds but are less commonly consumed. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a serrated edge.

Amygdalus minor is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
