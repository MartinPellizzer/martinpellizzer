Agropyron violaceum var. virescens

Agropyron violaceum var. virescens, commonly known as the blue grass or blue needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green leaves and spikelets that often have a bluish tinge. This plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small, spike-like inflorescences.

Agropyron violaceum var. virescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
