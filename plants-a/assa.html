Assa

Assa, commonly known as assafoetida, is a flowering plant native to the dry regions of India and Iran.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, woody rhizomes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its essential oils, derived from the roots, are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Assafoetida is also valued for its antispasmodic and digestive properties in traditional medicine.
