Apocynum venetum subsp. lancifolium

Apocynum venetum subsp.

lancifolium, commonly known as Chinese apocynum, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers.

The plant produces a milky sap that contains cardiac glycosides, making it toxic if ingested. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
