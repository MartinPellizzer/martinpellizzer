Alchorneopsis floribunda var. sessiliflora

Alchorneopsis floribunda var. sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flowered alchorneopsis, is a species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, shrub-like plant characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The leaves are typically small, ovate, and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

This plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
