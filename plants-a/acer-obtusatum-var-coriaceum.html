Acer obtusatum var. coriaceum

Acer obtusatum var. coriaceum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The plant has a compact, rounded growth habit with a smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and appear in clusters before the leaves emerge in spring.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
