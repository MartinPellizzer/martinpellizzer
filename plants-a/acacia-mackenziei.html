Acacia mackenziei

Acacia mackenziei, commonly known as the Mackenzie acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are followed by flat, leathery pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia mackenziei is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive form and drought tolerance.
