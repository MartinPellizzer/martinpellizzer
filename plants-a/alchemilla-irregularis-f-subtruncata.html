Alchemilla irregularis f. subtruncata

Alchemilla irregularis f. subtruncata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its foliage is commonly used in herbal remedies due to its mild astringent properties.
