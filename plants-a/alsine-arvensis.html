Alsine arvensis

Alsine arvensis, commonly known as common mouse-ear chickweed, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with small, rounded leaves and pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and gardens, and is often found in temperate regions. Its flowers are usually borne in clusters and bloom from spring to early summer.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
