Alsophila dealbata

Alsophila dealbata, commonly known as the silver tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately divided fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive silvery sheen on its fronds, which is due to the presence of fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Alsophila dealbata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various forest organisms.
