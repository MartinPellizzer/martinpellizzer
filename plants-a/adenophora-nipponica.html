Adenophora nipponica

Adenophora nipponica, commonly known as Japanese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Adenophora nipponica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
