Asperula caucasica

Asperula caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian woodruff, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Asperula caucasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
