Allium chamaemoly

Allium chamaemoly, commonly known as mountain garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal, linear leaves. The plant produces umbel-like clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers in the spring. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a valued ingredient in some cuisines.

Additionally, it is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
