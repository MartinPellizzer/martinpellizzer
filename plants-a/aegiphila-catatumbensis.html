Aegiphila catatumbensis

Aegiphila catatumbensis, commonly known as the Catatumbo shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and clusters of tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
