Aegiphila catatumbensis

Aegiphila catatumbensis, commonly known as the Catatumbo shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.

Aegiphila catatumbensis has simple, ovate to elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, with a smooth, dark green upper surface and a lighter, slightly hairy underside.
