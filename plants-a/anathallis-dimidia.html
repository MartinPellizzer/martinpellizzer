Anathallis dimidia

Anathallis dimidia, commonly known as the half-veined spider orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South America.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its most distinctive feature is its intricate, spider-like flowers, which are typically pale green or white with dark markings. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for both botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
