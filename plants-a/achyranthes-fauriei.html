Achyranthes fauriei

Achyranthes fauriei, commonly known as the Indian knotweed, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Achyranthes fauriei thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
