Aesculus chekiangensis

Aesculus chekiangensis, commonly known as the Chinese horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The tree produces spiny capsules containing glossy, chestnut-like seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to temperate climates.
