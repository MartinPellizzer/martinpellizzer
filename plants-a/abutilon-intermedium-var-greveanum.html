Abutilon intermedium var. greveanum

Abutilon intermedium var. greveanum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
