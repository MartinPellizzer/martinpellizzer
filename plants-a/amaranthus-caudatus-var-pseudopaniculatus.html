Amaranthus caudatus var. pseudopaniculatus

Amaranthus caudatus var. pseudopaniculatus, commonly known as the plume amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stalks and feathery, plume-like inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or red in color. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of seeds.

The leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their nutritional content.
