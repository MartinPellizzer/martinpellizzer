Arenaria salina

Arenaria salina, commonly known as saltbush, is a flowering plant native to coastal and saline environments.

It is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes or salt marshes.

Arenaria salina plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
