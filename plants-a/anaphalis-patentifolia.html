Anaphalis patentifolia

Anaphalis patentifolia, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its woolly, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Anaphalis patentifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
