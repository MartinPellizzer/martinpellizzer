Adenocalymma flos-ardeae

Adenocalymma flos-ardeae, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, resembling flames. It typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture, complementing the vibrant floral display.
