Avena × altior

Avena × altior, commonly known as the tall oat, is a sterile hybrid species resulting from the cross between Avena sativa (common oat) and Avena fatua (wild oat).

It is a tall, annual grass that can grow up to 2 meters in height, characterized by its slender, erect stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, two-lobed spikelets, which contain the characteristic oat grains. Avena × altior is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural fields.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of hybridization and grass evolution.
