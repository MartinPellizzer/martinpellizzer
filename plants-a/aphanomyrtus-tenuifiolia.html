Aphanomyrtus tenuifiolia

Aphanomyrtus tenuifiolia, commonly known as the slender-leaved mallee, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

The species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging climates.
