Arctostaphylos andersonii var. imbricata

Arctostaphylos andersonii var. imbricata, commonly known as the imbricata manzanita, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California and Oregon.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clustered, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild, wet winters.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
