Acacia moirii subsp. recurvistipula

Acacia moirii subsp.

recurvistipula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and phyllodes that are narrow and recurved at the tips.

The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its foliage consists of reduced, flattened leaf-like structures called phyllodes, typical of many Acacia species.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
