Abies excelsa var. carpatica

Abies excelsa var. carpatica, commonly known as the Carpathian fir, is a variant of the silver fir native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces cones that are typically blue-green in color, and it thrives in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive form and ability to tolerate urban conditions.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in forest ecosystems and its aesthetic appeal in landscaping.
