Amyema rhytidoderma

Amyema rhytidoderma, commonly known as the peeling bark plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive, flaky bark that peels away in thin layers.

It is native to the arid regions of South Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with small, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically pale green or yellow, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are adapted for animal dispersal.

Due to its unusual appearance and adaptations to harsh environments, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies.
