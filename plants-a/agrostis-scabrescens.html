Agrostis scabrescens

Agrostis scabrescens, commonly known as rough bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural and turfgrass settings.

The botanical features of Agrostis scabrescens make it adaptable and resilient in diverse ecosystems.
