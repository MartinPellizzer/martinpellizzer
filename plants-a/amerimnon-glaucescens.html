Amerimnon glaucescens

Amerimnon glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green fern, is a rare and delicate plant native to the humid tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, which helps it retain moisture.

Despite its beauty, it is a challenging plant to cultivate due to its specific environmental requirements.
