Acacia crassiuscula

Acacia crassiuscula, commonly known as the small-flowered wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in globular clusters, typically in spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that serve as the primary photosynthetic structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Acacia crassiuscula is a hardy species that is well adapted to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Australia.
