Anchusa macrophylla

Anchusa macrophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf anchusa, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, soft hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its striking flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
