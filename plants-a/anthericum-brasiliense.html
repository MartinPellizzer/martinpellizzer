Anthericum brasiliense

Anthericum brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian adder's tongue, is a herbaceous plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant has strap-like, lanceolate leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Anthericum brasiliense is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects.
