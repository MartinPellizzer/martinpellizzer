Astragalus darwasicus

Astragalus darwasicus, commonly known as the Darwazi milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.

Its seeds are typically dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across arid landscapes.
