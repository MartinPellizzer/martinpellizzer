Avena elatior f. abnormis

Avena elatior f. abnormis, commonly known as the giant oat, is a variant of the common oat species characterized by its unusually large size and robust growth.

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is known for its tall, slender stalks that can reach heights of several meters. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly hairy, while its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets. The plant produces small, two-lobed flowers that are typically greenish or pale yellow in color.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
