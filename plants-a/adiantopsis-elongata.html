Adiantopsis elongata

Adiantopsis elongata, commonly known as the long-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features slender, arching leaves with a distinctively veined structure, and its fronds are typically light green with a slightly waxy texture. Adiantopsis elongata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and botanical collections.
