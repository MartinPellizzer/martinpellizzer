Agropyron scribneri

Agropyron scribneri, commonly known as Scribner's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open areas such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets.

Agropyron scribneri is valued for its drought tolerance and is often used in restoration ecology and as a forage plant.
