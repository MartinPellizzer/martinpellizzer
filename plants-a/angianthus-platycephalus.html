Angianthus platycephalus

Angianthus platycephalus, commonly known as the flat-headed angianthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in grasslands and shrublands, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Angianthus platycephalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
