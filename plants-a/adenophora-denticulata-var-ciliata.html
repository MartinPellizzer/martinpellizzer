Adenophora denticulata var. ciliata

Adenophora denticulata var. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The leaves are typically hairy and have finely toothed margins, which is reflected in its scientific name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
