Althaea villosa

Althaea villosa, commonly known as downy mallows, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wetlands. Its roots contain mucilage, which has been used in traditional medicine for its soothing properties.

Althaea villosa is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
