Acacia turnbulliana

Acacia turnbulliana, commonly known as the Turner's wattle, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy soils and coastal areas. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it has distinctive, flattened pods that contain its seeds.

Acacia turnbulliana is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
