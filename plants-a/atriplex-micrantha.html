Atriplex micrantha

Atriplex micrantha, commonly known as saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Atriplex micrantha is adapted to saline soils and is often found in coastal areas or salt marshes.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
