Amblysperma

Amblysperma, commonly known as the narrowleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the spring and are followed by fluffy seed heads that disperse easily.

Amblysperma is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
