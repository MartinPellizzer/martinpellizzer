Alopecurus interruptus

Alopecurus interruptus, commonly known as the interrupted bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are often covered with short hairs, giving it a distinctive, slightly rough texture.

Alopecurus interruptus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
