Acacia rubescens

Acacia rubescens, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Acacia rubescens thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and role in local ecosystems.
