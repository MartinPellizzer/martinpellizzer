Assonia × cayeuxii

Assonia × cayeuxii, commonly known as the hybrid waxplant, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its showy, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and features palmate leaves with a velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Assonia × cayeuxii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
