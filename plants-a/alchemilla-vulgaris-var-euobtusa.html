Alchemilla vulgaris var. euobtusa

Alchemilla vulgaris var. euobtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features basal rosettes of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are typically green to reddish in color, depending on the light conditions.

This plant is well adapted to moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
