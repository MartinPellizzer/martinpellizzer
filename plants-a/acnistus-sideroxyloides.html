Acnistus sideroxyloides

Acnistus sideroxyloides, commonly known as ironwood acnistus, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Acnistus sideroxyloides is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
