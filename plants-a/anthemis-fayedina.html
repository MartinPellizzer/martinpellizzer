Anthemis fayedina

Anthemis fayedina, commonly known as the Fayedina ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that are typically arranged in a flat-topped cluster, with narrow, linear leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
