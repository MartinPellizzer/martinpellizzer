Asterochlaena

Asterochlaena, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The leaves are typically silver or gray-green, often covered in fine hairs that give them a distinctive appearance. These plants are native to southern Africa and are often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Asterochlaena species are generally drought-tolerant and thrive in full sun, making them popular choices for xeriscaping.
