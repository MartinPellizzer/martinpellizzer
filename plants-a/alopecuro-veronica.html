Alopecuro-veronica

Alopecuro-veronica, commonly known as the mouse-ear speedwell, is a member of the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alopecuro-veronica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
