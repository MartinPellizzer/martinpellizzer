Akebia trifoliata subsp. australis

Akebia trifoliata subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian chocolate vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three heart-shaped leaflets, and its distinctive, fragrant, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces edible, chocolate-flavored fruits that are typically consumed raw or used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks. Akebia trifoliata subsp.

australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique flavor profile.
