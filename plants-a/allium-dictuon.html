Allium dictuon

Allium dictuon, commonly known as the desert leek, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in a powdery bloom, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers on tall, slender stems during the spring and early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor, making it a potential culinary and medicinal plant.

Allium dictuon is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in low-nutrient soils and with minimal water.
