Aeranthes erectiflora

Aeranthes erectiflora, commonly known as the erect-flowered spider orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that resemble spiders, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in coastal areas or humid forests. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers that bloom during the summer months.

Aeranthes erectiflora is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific conditions to thrive.
