Alyssum odoratum

Alyssum odoratum, commonly known as sweet alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial plant that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum odoratum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
