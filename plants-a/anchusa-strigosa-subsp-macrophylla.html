Anchusa strigosa subsp. macrophylla

Anchusa strigosa subsp.

macrophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf anchusa, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 15 cm in length.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in spikes during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
