Annona papilionella

Annona papilionella, commonly known as the white annona, is a small tree or shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, yellowish fruits with a soft, fleshy pulp.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
