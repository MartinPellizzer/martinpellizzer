Aria parviloba

Aria parviloba, commonly known as the small-leaved aria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Aria parviloba is often found in rocky or forested habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
