Acacia schweinfurthii var. sericea

Acacia schweinfurthii var. sericea, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in winter, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seed pods are elongated and contain numerous small, hard seeds.

Acacia schweinfurthii var. sericea is well adapted to drought and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of firewood.
