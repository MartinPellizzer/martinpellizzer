Alsophila tsaratananensis

Alsophila tsaratananensis, commonly known as the Tsaratanana tree fern, is a species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant exhibits a unique growth pattern, with a thick, fibrous root system that helps it anchor in nutrient-poor soils. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, contributing to its striking appearance in the rainforest canopy.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife.
