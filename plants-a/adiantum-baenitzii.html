Adiantum baenitzii

Adiantum baenitzii, commonly known as the water clover, is a delicate fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically bright green and often have a waxy coating. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in humid forests. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Adiantum baenitzii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to purify water in aquatic settings.
