Acnistus ulei

Acnistus ulei, commonly known as the hairy false holly, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, sparsely foliaged branches. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by red, berry-like fruits.

Acnistus ulei is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry woodlands.
