Achillea × tomentella

Achillea × tomentella, commonly known as the woolly yarrow, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly silver-green foliage and clusters of small, daisy-like white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for alpine gardens. Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
