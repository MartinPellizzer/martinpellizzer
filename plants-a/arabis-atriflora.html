Arabis atriflora

Arabis atriflora, commonly known as the black-flowered rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and its striking clusters of small, dark purple to black flowers. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Arabis atriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration.
