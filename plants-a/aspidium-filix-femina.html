Aspidium filix-femina

Aspidium filix-femina, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often described as resembling a maiden's hair. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Aspidium filix-femina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in various cultures.
