Agapetes viridiflora

Agapetes viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered heather, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of white or greenish-white flowers. It is often found in cloud forests at high elevations, where it contributes to the rich biodiversity of the region.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species.
