Alchemilla pratensis var. pastoralis

Alchemilla pratensis var. pastoralis, commonly known as the meadow lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, shield-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are borne on slender stems. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular choice for wetland gardens and conservation areas.
