Acacia grandisiliqua

Acacia grandisiliqua, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its long, feathery leaves and yellow, spherical flower heads that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

Additionally, the plant has cultural and medicinal significance in some regions.
