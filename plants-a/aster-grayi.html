Aster grayi

Aster grayi, commonly known as Gray's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bracted flower heads.

Aster grayi is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, making it an important component of local ecosystems.
