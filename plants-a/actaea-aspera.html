Actaea aspera

Actaea aspera, commonly known as the rough squirrelroot, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant features large, palmately compound leaves with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring. Its fruit is a spherical aggregate of achenes, typically ripening in early autumn.

Actaea aspera is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
