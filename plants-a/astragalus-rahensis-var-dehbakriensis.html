Astragalus rahensis var. dehbakriensis

Astragalus rahensis var. dehbakriensis, commonly known as the Dehbakri Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in semi-arid ecosystems.
