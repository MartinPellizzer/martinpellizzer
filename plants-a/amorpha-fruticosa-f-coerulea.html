Amorpha fruticosa f. coerulea

Amorpha fruticosa f. coerulea, commonly known as blue false indigo, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring, adding vibrant color to gardens. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
