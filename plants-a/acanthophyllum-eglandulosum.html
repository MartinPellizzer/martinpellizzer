Acanthophyllum eglandulosum

Acanthophyllum eglandulosum, commonly known as the smooth acanthophyllum, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, woody stems and dense clusters of small, spine-tipped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are typically clustered in panicles. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and a succulent structure to store water.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
