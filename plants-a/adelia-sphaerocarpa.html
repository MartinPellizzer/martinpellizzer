Adelia sphaerocarpa

Adelia sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe-fig, is a small, deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rounded, fleshy fruits that resemble small globes, which are typically yellow or orange when ripe. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

Adelia sphaerocarpa is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
