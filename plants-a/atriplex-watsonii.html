Atriplex watsonii

Atriplex watsonii, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, often blue-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it a valuable species for stabilizing soil in saline or coastal areas.

Atriplex watsonii is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils, contributing to its ecological significance in desert ecosystems.
