Aquilegia formosa var. desertorum

Aquilegia formosa var. desertorum, commonly known as the desert columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with palmate lobes, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
