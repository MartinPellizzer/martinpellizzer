Aegilops gussonei

Aegilops gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's wild einkorn, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the tribe Aegilopineae within the grass family Poaceae. This plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its genetic similarity to cultivated einkorn, it is of interest in agricultural research for potential improvements in wheat breeding.

Morphology

Aegilops gussonei has narrow, grass-like leaves that are linear in shape, arranged in a basal rosette, and typically range from 5 to 15 cm in length, with a bluish-green color.

The stem is slender and cylindrical, with a smooth texture and a light green to yellowish color, growing upright from the base. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers that bloom in early summer, and they are wind-pollinated.
