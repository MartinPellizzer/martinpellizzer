Aspicarpa pentandra

Aspicarpa pentandra, commonly known as the five-lobed sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows close to the ground, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion. Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.

The fruit is a small, round capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
