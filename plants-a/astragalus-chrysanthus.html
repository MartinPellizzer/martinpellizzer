Astragalus chrysanthus

Astragalus chrysanthus, commonly known as the golden milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the pods contain small, hairy seeds.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
