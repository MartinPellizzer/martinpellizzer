Arundinaria koidzumii

Arundinaria koidzumii, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Koidzumi bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant typically reaches a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly bluish-green color.

Arundinaria koidzumii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Japanese gardens.
