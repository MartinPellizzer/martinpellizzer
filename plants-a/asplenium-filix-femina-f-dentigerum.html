Asplenium filix-femina f. dentigerum

Asplenium filix-femina f. dentigerum, commonly known as the toothed maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is native to regions in Asia, including China and Japan. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forests or rocky areas. Its fronds have a distinctive serrated or toothed margin, which gives it its common name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
