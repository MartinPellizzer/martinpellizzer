Avena fatua f. deserticola

Avena fatua f. deserticola, commonly known as desert wild oat, is a variant of the wild oat species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its shorter stature and reduced leaf size, adaptations that help it survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with bract-like leaves, and its seeds are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in areas with low rainfall and high temperatures.

This subspecies is of interest in ecological studies due to its resilience and potential for use in restoration projects.
