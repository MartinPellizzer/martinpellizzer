Aster wrightii

Aster wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Aster wrightii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
