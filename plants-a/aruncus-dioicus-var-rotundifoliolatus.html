Aruncus dioicus var. rotundifoliolatus

Aruncus dioicus var. rotundifoliolatus, commonly known as the rounded-leaved goat's beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features showy, feathery plumes of white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves with rounded lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
