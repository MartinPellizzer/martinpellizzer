Adesmia silvestrii

Adesmia silvestrii, commonly known as the wild legume or silvestrii legume, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, compound leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals. Native to certain regions of South America, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
