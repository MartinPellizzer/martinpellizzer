Artemisia obtusiloba subsp. subviscosa

Artemisia obtusiloba subsp.

subviscosa, commonly known as sticky bear's garlic, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer.

The leaves are typically covered in a fine, sticky resin, which gives the plant its common name. This subspecies is adapted to arid and rocky habitats, often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
