Ageratum conyzoides var. houstonianum

Ageratum conyzoides var. houstonianum, commonly known as Mexican marigold, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It features branched stems and opposite leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, purple, and blue, which bloom from summer to fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically beneficial.
