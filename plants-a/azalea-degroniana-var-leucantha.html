Azalea degroniana var. leucantha

Azalea degroniana var. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink blooms that appear in early spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with leathery, elliptical leaves. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial shade to full sun for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
