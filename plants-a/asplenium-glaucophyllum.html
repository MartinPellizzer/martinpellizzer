Asplenium glaucophyllum

Asplenium glaucophyllum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern belonging to the Aspleniaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are covered in a fine, silvery coating of trichomes, giving it a distinctive appearance. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

The plant's reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of its fronds, typical of many fern species.
