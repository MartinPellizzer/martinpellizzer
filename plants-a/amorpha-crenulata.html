Amorpha crenulata

Amorpha crenulata, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a leguminous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant has a sprawling growth habit and produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It is often used in ecological restoration due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

Amorpha crenulata thrives in a variety of soil types and is adaptable to different environmental conditions.
