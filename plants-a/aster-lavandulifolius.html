Aster lavandulifolius

Aster lavandulifolius, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant is known for its silvery-blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall, creating a striking visual display. It has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are reminiscent of lavender, giving it its common name.

Aster lavandulifolius is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
