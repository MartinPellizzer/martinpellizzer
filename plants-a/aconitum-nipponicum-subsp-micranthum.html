Aconitum nipponicum subsp. micranthum

Aconitum nipponicum subsp.

micranthum, commonly known as Japanese monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia. It features showy, hooded blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has dark green, palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.
