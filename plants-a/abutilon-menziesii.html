Abutilon menziesii

Abutilon menziesii, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its fibrous bark and hairy foliage give it a distinctive, rugged appearance, making it a notable species in its native habitat.
