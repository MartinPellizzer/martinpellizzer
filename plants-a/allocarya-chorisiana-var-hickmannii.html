Allocarya chorisiana var. hickmannii

Allocarya chorisiana var. hickmannii, commonly known as the Hickmann's sand verbena, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to drought conditions. Its leaves are linear and often covered with fine hairs, aiding in water conservation.

The species is part of the Verbenaceae family, which is known for its aromatic and sometimes medicinal properties.
