Acacia furcatispina

Acacia furcatispina, commonly known as the broad-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It features slender, often multi-branched stems with a greyish bark that becomes fissured with age. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical inflorescences, which are typical of the Acacia genus. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets.

Acacia furcatispina is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in regions with limited rainfall.
