Alibertia isernii

Alibertia isernii, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded areas of the rainforest.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses.
