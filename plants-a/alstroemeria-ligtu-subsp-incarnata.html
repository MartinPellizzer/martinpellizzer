Alstroemeria ligtu subsp. incarnata

Alstroemeria ligtu subsp.

incarnata, commonly known as the pink alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America. It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and red.

The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern. Alstroemeria ligtu subsp.

incarnata is valued in horticulture for its long-lasting blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
