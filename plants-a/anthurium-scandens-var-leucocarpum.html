Anthurium scandens var. leucocarpum

Anthurium scandens var. leucocarpum, commonly known as the white fruit anthurium, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are modified leaves that enclose the inflorescence, often with a white or pale pink spathe. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by insects and birds.

The plant prefers warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental species in greenhouses and tropical gardens.
