Alectorolophus grandiflorus var. pumilus

Alectorolophus grandiflorus var. pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a compact species native to tropical regions.

It features small, delicate flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant has a short, rhizomatous stem and produces lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
