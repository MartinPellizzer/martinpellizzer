Amaranthus blitoides var. scleropoides

Amaranthus blitoides var. scleropoides, commonly known as the sand amaranth, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, green to reddish-purple leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or red, depending on the variety. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to sandy and well-drained soils.

This plant is often found in disturbed areas and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
