Argemone turnerae var. turnerae

Argemone turnerae var. turnerae, commonly known as the Mexican prickly poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It features striking orange to red flowers with prominent yellow stamens, and its leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique floral structure and adaptability to harsh conditions.
