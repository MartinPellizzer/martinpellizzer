Artemisia rosthornii

Artemisia rosthornii, commonly known as the Himalayan wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains essential oils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its adaptability to harsh mountainous environments makes it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
