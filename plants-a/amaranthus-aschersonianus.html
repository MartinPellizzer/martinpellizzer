Amaranthus aschersonianus

Amaranthus aschersonianus, commonly known as love-lies-bleeding, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, blood-red inflorescences that resemble plumes, which are actually modified bracts surrounding the small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often reddish in color.

The species is also valued for its nutritional content, as its seeds are rich in protein and minerals.
