Arundinaria utilis

Arundinaria utilis, commonly known as bamboo cane, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and relies on wind pollination. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of durable material.

Arundinaria utilis is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
