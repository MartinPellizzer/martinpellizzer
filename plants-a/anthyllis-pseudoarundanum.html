Anthyllis pseudoarundanum

Anthyllis pseudoarundanum, commonly known as the false vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features clusters of small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Anthyllis pseudoarundanum thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
