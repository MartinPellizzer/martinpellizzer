Alyssum calycinum var. sabulosum

Alyssum calycinum var. sabulosum, commonly known as sand alyssum, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has succulent, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
