Aria latifolia

Aria latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf aria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
