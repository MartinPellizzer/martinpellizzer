Ambrosia carduacea

Ambrosia carduacea, commonly known as ragweed, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces a large amount of lightweight, wind-borne pollen, which is a major allergen for many people. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its allergenic properties, it is considered a significant weed in agricultural and urban environments.
