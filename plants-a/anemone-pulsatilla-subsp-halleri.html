Anemone pulsatilla subsp. halleri

Anemone pulsatilla subsp.

halleri, commonly known as the Haller's pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features a thick, fleshy rootstock and produces a single, showy, cup-shaped flower that is typically pink or purple in color.

The plant has palmately divided, hairy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates, often blooming in early spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
