Apodytes geldenhuysii

Apodytes geldenhuysii, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant has a compact, rounded crown and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive waxy texture.

Apodytes geldenhuysii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.
