Allosorus angulosus

Allosorus angulosus, commonly known as the angular lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Graphidaceae.

It is characterized by its crustose growth form, forming a thin, tightly adherent layer on rock surfaces. The thallus is typically gray to brown in color and has a distinct angular or irregularly shaped pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found on calcareous substrates.

This lichen is known for its slow growth and is used in ecological studies to assess air quality and environmental changes.
