Aristolochia longifolia

Aristolochia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant belonging to the Aristolochiaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex floral structures and chemical compounds, some of which have medicinal uses.
