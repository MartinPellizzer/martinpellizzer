Asphodeline rigidifolia var. foliosa

Asphodeline rigidifolia var. foliosa, commonly known as the broadleaf asphodel, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage and long-lasting blooms.
