Arenga brevipes

Arenga brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed areca, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, fibrous fruits. Arenga brevipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for use in landscaping and as a decorative plant in warm regions.
