Ayenia californica

Ayenia californica, commonly known as the California ayenia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Ayenia californica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
