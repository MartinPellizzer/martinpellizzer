Adiantum guianense

Adiantum guianense, commonly known as the Brazilian maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are bipinnate, with a distinctive, finely divided structure that gives it a feathery appearance.

Adiantum guianense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
