Adenanthera gago

Adenanthera gago, commonly known as the African burr or goat's bean, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to spiny, woody pods containing seeds. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

The seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein in some regions.
