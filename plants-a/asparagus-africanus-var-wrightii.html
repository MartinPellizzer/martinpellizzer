Asparagus africanus var. wrightii

Asparagus africanus var. wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's asparagus, is a shrub-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features wiry, spiny stems and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal umbels. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
