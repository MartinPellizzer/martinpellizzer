Alectorurus yedoensis

Alectorurus yedoensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo orchid, is a striking plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of vibrant, orchid-like flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forested areas. Its leaves are long and narrow, and its flowers come in shades of pink, white, and purple.

Alectorurus yedoensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its cultural significance in Japanese horticulture.
