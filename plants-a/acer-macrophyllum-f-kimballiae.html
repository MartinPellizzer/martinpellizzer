Acer macrophyllum f. kimballiae

Acer macrophyllum f. kimballiae, commonly known as the Kimball's bigleaf maple, is a variant of the bigleaf maple native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its bark is smooth and gray, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant fall foliage.
