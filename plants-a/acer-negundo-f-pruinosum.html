Acer negundo f. pruinosum

Acer negundo f. pruinosum, commonly known as the silky maple, is a variant of the silver maple characterized by its distinctive, silky-hairy leaves.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a soft, downy texture on the upper surface. This cultivar is often valued for its ornamental appeal, especially in gardens where its unique foliage stands out. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in temperate regions.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by samaras that aid in its propagation.
