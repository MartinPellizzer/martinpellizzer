Aerides dalzelliana

Aerides dalzelliana, commonly known as the Dalzell's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that enhances its ornamental appeal.
