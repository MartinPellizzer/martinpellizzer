Anastrophus pellitus

Anastrophus pellitus, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its silver-gray foliage and fleshy, ribbed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal regions.

Anastrophus pellitus is known for its ability to store water in its leaves, making it drought-tolerant and well adapted to harsh conditions.
