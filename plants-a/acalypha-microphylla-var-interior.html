Acalypha microphylla var. interior

Acalypha microphylla var. interior, commonly known as the red-leaf or red-leaf iron plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep red or bronze color, especially in the winter months. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its foliage is dense and attractive, making it a popular choice for ornamental use in gardens and landscapes.

It thrives in partial to full shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.
