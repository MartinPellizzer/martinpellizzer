Amorphophallus amygdaloides

Amorphophallus amygdaloides, commonly known as the giant water chestnut, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and an underground tuber that serves as its storage organ. The plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in flooded areas. Its tuber is edible and has been used in traditional cuisines.

The species is also notable for its rapid growth and ability to form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
