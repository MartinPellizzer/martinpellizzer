Aster puniceus f. candidus

Aster puniceus f. candidus, commonly known as the white aster, is a cultivar of the common aster species.

It is characterized by its pale white to pale pink flowers, which contrast with its dark green, lance-shaped leaves. This plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a herbaceous perennial that is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers bloom in the late summer to early autumn, adding a delicate touch of color to the landscape.
