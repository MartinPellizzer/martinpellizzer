Anguina trifoliata

Anguina trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leafed anguina, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Anguina trifoliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate foliage and modest blooms.
