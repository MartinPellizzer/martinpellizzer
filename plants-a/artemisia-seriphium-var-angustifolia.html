Artemisia seriphium var. angustifolia

Artemisia seriphium var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray, aromatic foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in compact, spherical clusters called heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils contribute to its use in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
