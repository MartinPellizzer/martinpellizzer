Aconitum selemdshense

Aconitum selemdshense, commonly known as the Selmdshen aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, spire-like inflorescences. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Aconitum selemdshense is adapted to cold, alpine environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.
