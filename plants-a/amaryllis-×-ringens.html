Amaryllis × ringens

Amaryllis × ringens, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis or sometimes the Easter lily, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis paradisicola.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and red. The plant is native to South Africa and thrives in warm, sunny environments with well-draining soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular during the holiday season.

The foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base, typical of many amaryllis species.
