Acrostichum latifolium var. subsesile

Acrostichum latifolium var. subsesile, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed broad-leaf fern, is a variant of the broad-leaf fern native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright stems and large, triangular, leathery leaves that are typically bright green. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found near water bodies such as streams and wetlands. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with each leaflet having a serrated margin.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
