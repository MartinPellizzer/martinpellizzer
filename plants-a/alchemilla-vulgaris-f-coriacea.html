Alchemilla vulgaris f. coriacea

Alchemilla vulgaris f. coriacea, commonly known as the rough lady's mantle, is a variant of the lady's mantle plant characterized by its hairy, leathery leaves.

This perennial plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are typically gray-green and have a distinctive rough texture due to the dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
