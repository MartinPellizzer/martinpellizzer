Arundinella holcoides

Arundinella holcoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf reed, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Arundinella holcoides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
