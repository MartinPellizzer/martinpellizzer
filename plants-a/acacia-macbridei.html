Acacia macbridei

Acacia macbridei, commonly known as the MacBride's wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and phyllodes that are typically linear to narrowly elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils.

The species is also noted for its role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
