Agropyron peruvianum

Agropyron peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across arid regions.

Agropyron peruvianum plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
