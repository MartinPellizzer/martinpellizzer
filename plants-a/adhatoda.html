Adhatoda

Adhatoda, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant is often found in moist, tropical environments and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are square in cross-section.

Adhatoda is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
