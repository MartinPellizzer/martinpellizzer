Aleuritopteris flavopygmaea

Aleuritopteris flavopygmaea, commonly known as the yellow-tailed water fern, is a small, delicate fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, bright yellow spore cases on the underside of its fronds. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are bipinnate, with a finely divided structure that contributes to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
