Astilbe japonica subsp. glaberrima

Astilbe japonica subsp.

glaberrima, commonly known as the Japanese astilbe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia. It features upright, feather-like inflorescences that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety.

The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are densely packed along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom in late spring to early summer.
