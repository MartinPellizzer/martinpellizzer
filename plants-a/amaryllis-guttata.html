Amaryllis guttata

Amaryllis guttata, commonly known as the spotted amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with intricate patterns and spots. The plant has strap-like leaves that are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Amaryllis guttata is popular as a houseplant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
