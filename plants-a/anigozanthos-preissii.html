Anigozanthos preissii

Anigozanthos preissii, commonly known as the emu bush, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Anigozanthos preissii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
