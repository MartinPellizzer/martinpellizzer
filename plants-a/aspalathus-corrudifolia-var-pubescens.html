Aspalathus corrudifolia var. pubescens

Aspalathus corrudifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

The species is often used in ornamental gardening for its compact growth and attractive foliage.
