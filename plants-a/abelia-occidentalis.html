Abelia occidentalis

Abelia occidentalis, commonly known as the western abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features attractive, fragrant, tubular flowers that range from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Abelia occidentalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
