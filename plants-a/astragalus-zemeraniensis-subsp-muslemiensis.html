Astragalus zemeraniensis subsp. muslemiensis

Astragalus zemeraniensis subsp.

muslemiensis, commonly known as the Muslemien sand milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, semi-arid environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems, though it is relatively rare and not widely cultivated.
