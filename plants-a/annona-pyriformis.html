Annona pyriformis

Annona pyriformis, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that produces large, fragrant, yellowish-green fruits. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often covered in a waxy coating. The flowers are typically white or pale green with a strong, unpleasant odor.

The plant is valued for its edible fruit, which has a creamy, sweet, and slightly tart flavor.
