Artemisia racemosa

Artemisia racemosa, commonly known as silverleaf sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flower clusters that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in desert and sagebrush habitats.

Artemisia racemosa has aromatic leaves that release a strong, pungent scent when crushed.
