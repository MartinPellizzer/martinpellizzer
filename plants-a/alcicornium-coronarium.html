Alcicornium coronarium

Alcicornium coronarium, commonly known as the Crowned Alcicorn, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble a crown, hence its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically a mix of white, yellow, and orange, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

The leaves are narrow and succulent, helping to conserve water in its harsh environment.
