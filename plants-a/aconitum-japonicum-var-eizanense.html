Aconitum japonicum var. eizanense

Aconitum japonicum var. eizanense, commonly known as the Eizan mountain aconite, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It features palmately compound leaves and produces spires of showy, bell-shaped flowers.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a potentially dangerous plant to handle.
