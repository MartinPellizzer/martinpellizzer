Asplenium bulbiferum subsp. gracillimum

Asplenium bulbiferum subsp.

gracillimum, commonly known as the graceful water fern, is a fern native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. One of its notable features is its ability to produce plantlets along the edges of its fronds, a trait shared with its parent species.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its humid, tropical habitat.
