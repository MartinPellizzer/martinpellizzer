Astragalus panamintensis

Astragalus panamintensis, commonly known as the Panamint milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the deserts of California and Nevada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments with limited water availability. It has compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus panamintensis plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and provides habitat for various desert organisms.
