Achras laurifolia

Achras laurifolia, commonly known as the white cedar, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in panicles, which appear in the spring.

The fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
