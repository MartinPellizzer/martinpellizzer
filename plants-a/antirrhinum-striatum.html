Antirrhinum striatum

Antirrhinum striatum, commonly known as the striped toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, striated leaves and showy, two-lipped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Antirrhinum striatum is a perennial herb that produces seed capsules containing numerous small, black seeds.
