Aristolochia longiflora

Aristolochia longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hooded flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and emits a mild, sometimes unpleasant odor. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
