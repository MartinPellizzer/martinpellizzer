Astragalus albispinus

Astragalus albispinus, commonly known as the white-spined milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Astragalus albispinus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.
