Apargia hispanica

Apargia hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
