Agrostistachys filipendula

Agrostistachys filipendula, commonly known as the golden beard grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often tinged with golden hues. The plant produces panicles of small, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Agrostistachys filipendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
