Anerincleistus sublepidotus

Anerincleistus sublepidotus, commonly known as the glossy leaf fern, is a species of fern characterized by its lush, dark green fronds that are covered in a waxy, glossy coating.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with long, creeping stems that produce new fronds along their length. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, meaning they have two levels of division, giving them a delicate, feathery appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
