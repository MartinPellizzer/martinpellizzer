Astragalus vogelii subsp. fatimensis

Astragalus vogelii subsp.

fatimensis, commonly known as the Fatiman milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western China. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant is adapted to dry environments, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in semi-arid ecosystems.
