Ampelopsis megalophylla var. jiangxiensis

Ampelopsis megalophylla var. jiangxiensis, commonly known as the Jiangxi ivy, is a climbing vine native to southern China.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive lobed margin, and its stems are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental foliage.

This variety is noted for its unique leaf structure and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
