Aster scutiformis

Aster scutiformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and bloom in late summer to early fall.

Aster scutiformis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
