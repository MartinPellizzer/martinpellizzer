Adenostephanus inaequalis

Adenostephanus inaequalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf chia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces edible seeds that are rich in omega-3 fatty acids and are used in traditional culinary practices. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along streambanks.

Its ability to survive in harsh conditions makes it an important species in ecological restoration efforts.
