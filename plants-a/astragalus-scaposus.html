Astragalus scaposus

Astragalus scaposus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus scaposus plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local ecosystems.
