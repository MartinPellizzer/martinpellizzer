Anthericum tostum

Anthericum tostum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arum, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves that are often ribbed and have a waxy texture. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow, with a small, inconspicuous flower cluster at the base of the spathe.

Anthericum tostum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
