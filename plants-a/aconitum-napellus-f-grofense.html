Aconitum napellus f. grofense

Aconitum napellus f. grofense, commonly known as Grofen's monkshood, is a botanical variant of the European monkshood plant.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and striking blue-violet spurred flowers. The plant belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to Central Europe. It features large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply cut and have a distinctive appearance.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle it with care and avoid ingestion.
