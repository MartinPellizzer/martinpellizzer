Alnus fruticosa f. grandifolia

Alnus fruticosa f. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered alder, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Alnus, which includes various species of alders known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are catkins, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, a characteristic known as dioecious.

The fruit is a small, three-winged nutlet that is dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in forest succession.
