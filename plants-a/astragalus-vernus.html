Astragalus vernus

Astragalus vernus, commonly known as the spring milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in early spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

Astragalus vernus is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for early pollinators and supporting various insect species.
