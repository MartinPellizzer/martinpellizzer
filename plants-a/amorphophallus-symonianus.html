Amorphophallus symonianus

Amorphophallus symonianus, commonly known as the giant swamp arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features a large, tuberous underground stem that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces large, showy flowers with a distinctive purple spadix surrounded by a white spathe. It thrives in wet, swampy habitats and is adapted to seasonal flooding.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
