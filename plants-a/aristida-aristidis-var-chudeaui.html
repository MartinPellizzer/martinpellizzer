Aristida aristidis var. chudeaui

Aristida aristidis var. chudeaui, commonly known as the Chudeaui threeawn, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
