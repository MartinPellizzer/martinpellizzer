Aspidium draconopterum

Aspidium draconopterum, commonly known as the dragon's tail fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and distinctive fronds. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a slender, elongated shape that resembles a dragon's tail, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
