Adesmia hirsuta

Adesmia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, arid environments and is adapted to thrive in poor soils. Its small, yellow flowers are often clustered in inflorescences, and it is known for its hairy stems and leaves.

Adesmia hirsuta is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and resilience.
