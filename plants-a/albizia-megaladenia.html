Albizia megaladenia

Albizia megaladenia, commonly known as the giant siris, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a feathery appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, adding ornamental value to the landscape. Its pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Albizia megaladenia is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
