Anemonoides baicalensis

Anemonoides baicalensis, commonly known as the Baikal anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mongolia and Russia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately divided, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
