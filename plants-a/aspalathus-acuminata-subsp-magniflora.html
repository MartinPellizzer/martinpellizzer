Aspalathus acuminata subsp. magniflora

Aspalathus acuminata subsp.

magniflora, commonly known as the sweet cycad, is a unique plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and large, pinnately compound leaves that give it a palm-like appearance.

The plant produces striking yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, spiny pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
