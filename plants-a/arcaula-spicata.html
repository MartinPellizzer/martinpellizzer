Arcaula spicata

Arcaula spicata, commonly known as the spiky spikeplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive, textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in open fields and along the edges of forests.

Due to its unique floral structure and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
