Acoridium anfractoides

Acoridium anfractoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arrowhead, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia. The plant features arrow-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Acoridium anfractoides is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.
