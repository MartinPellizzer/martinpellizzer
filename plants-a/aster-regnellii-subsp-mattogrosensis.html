Aster regnellii subsp. mattogrosensis

Aster regnellii subsp.

mattogrosensis, commonly known as the Mattogroso aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America, specifically found in Argentina and Bolivia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and shrublands, thriving in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
