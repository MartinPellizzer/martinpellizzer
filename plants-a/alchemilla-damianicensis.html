Alchemilla damianicensis

Alchemilla damianicensis, commonly known as Damian's lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
