Asimina costaricensis

Asimina costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican custard apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a yellowish fruit with a thick, aromatic pulp that is edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and for conservation purposes.
