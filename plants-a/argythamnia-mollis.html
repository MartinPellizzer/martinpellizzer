Argythamnia mollis

Argythamnia mollis, commonly known as the silky tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Argythamnia mollis is often found in desert scrub and riparian habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
