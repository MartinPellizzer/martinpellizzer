Allantodia gymnogrammoides

Allantodia gymnogrammoides, commonly known as the yellow star bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with multiple branches forming a bushy shape. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in coastal landscapes due to its salt tolerance.
