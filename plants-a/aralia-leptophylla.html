Aralia leptophylla

Aralia leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaf aralia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into numerous narrow, elongated segments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in an umbel, which are followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
