Acaciella sotoi

Acaciella sotoi, commonly known as the Soto's acacia, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and can tolerate high temperatures and low water availability. Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.

Acaciella sotoi is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in nutrient-poor environments.
