Agapanthus multiflorus

Agapanthus multiflorus, commonly known as the African lily, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble lilies, composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant has strap-like, evergreen leaves that are narrow and grass-like in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Agapanthus multiflorus is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom reliably in temperate climates.
