Artemisia aethiopica

Artemisia aethiopica, commonly known as African wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant contains volatile oils and compounds such as thujone, which contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for various ailments.
