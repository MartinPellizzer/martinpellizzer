Aralia subcordata

Aralia subcordata, commonly known as the Chinese angelica, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves that are typically pinnately arranged, with serrated margins and a distinctive subcordate base. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbellate clusters, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aralia subcordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its foliage and seasonal interest.
