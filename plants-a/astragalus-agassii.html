Astragalus agassii

Astragalus agassii, commonly known as Agass's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and open woodlands. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus agassii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
