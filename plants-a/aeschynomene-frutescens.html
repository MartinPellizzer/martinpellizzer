Aeschynomene frutescens

Aeschynomene frutescens, commonly known as parvula, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes. Its seeds are small and elliptical, often dispersed by wind or animals.

Aeschynomene frutescens is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
