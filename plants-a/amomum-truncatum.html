Amomum truncatum

Amomum truncatum, commonly known as wild cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, elongated rhizomes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries containing aromatic seeds. Its rhizomes are valued for their distinctive flavor and are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
