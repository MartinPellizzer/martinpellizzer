Allium cucullatum

Allium cucullatum, commonly known as the hooded garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces umbels of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its strong garlic scent and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It blooms in early spring and is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.

Allium cucullatum is also known for its ability to thrive in poor, well-drained conditions.
