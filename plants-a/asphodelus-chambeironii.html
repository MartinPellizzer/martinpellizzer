Asphodelus chambeironii

Asphodelus chambeironii, commonly known as the Persian asphodel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, spike-like inflorescences with bright yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae, which includes other flowering plants with similar structural characteristics.

The flowers are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
