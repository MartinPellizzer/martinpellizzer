Aconitum lycoctonum subsp. ranunculifolium

Aconitum lycoctonum subsp.

ranunculifolium, commonly known as the European aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense, spire-like clusters.

The plant has palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care and avoid ingestion.
