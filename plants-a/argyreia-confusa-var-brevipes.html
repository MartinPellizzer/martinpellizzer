Argyreia confusa var. brevipes

Argyreia confusa var. brevipes, commonly known as the red devil's ivy, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.

The species is also valued for its ability to climb and its use in traditional medicine.
