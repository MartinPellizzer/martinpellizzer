Angelica archangelica var. decurrens

Angelica archangelica var. decurrens, commonly known as the Siberian angelica, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Eastern Europe and Siberia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often covered with fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers at the top of the stem, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a strong, aromatic scent.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
