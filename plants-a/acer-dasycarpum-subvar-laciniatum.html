Acer dasycarpum subvar. laciniatum

Acer dasycarpum subvar. laciniatum, commonly known as the serrated maple, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves that are deeply serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, red samaras that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its reproductive strategy. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming more fissured with age.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant autumn foliage.
