Astragalus sparsiflorus

Astragalus sparsiflorus, commonly known as the sparseflower milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow to greenish-yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grasslands. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus sparsiflorus plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
