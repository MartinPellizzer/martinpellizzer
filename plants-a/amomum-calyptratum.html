Amomum calyptratum

Amomum calyptratum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cardamom, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clustered, red-brown flowers. The plant produces small, three-lobed berries that are harvested for their aromatic seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its rhizome, similar to ginger, contains essential oils and is also utilized in traditional medicine.

Amomum calyptratum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its valuable aromatic compounds.
