Acaena magellanica var. pubescens

Acaena magellanica var. pubescens, commonly known as the Southern Chilean burr plant, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which contribute to its unique appearance. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its prickly nature, it is sometimes used in erosion control and as a decorative plant.
