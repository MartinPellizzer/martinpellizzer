Alsophila abbottii

Alsophila abbottii, commonly known as the Abbott's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, providing structural support and aiding in nutrient absorption.

The fronds are typically bright green and may have a slightly glossy appearance, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
