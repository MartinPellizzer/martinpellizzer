Antennaria × hansii

Antennaria × hansii, commonly known as the stickyweed or sand mat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, and its stems are often covered in fine, sticky hairs. Antennaria × hansii thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and dunes.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
