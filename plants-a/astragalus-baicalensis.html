Astragalus baicalensis

Astragalus baicalensis, commonly known as the Baikal milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mongolia and Siberia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its deep root system, which allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine.

The seeds of Astragalus baicalensis are often used to produce a yellow dye and have been historically used in various cultural practices.
