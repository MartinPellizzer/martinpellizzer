Anogeissus acuminata

Anogeissus acuminata, commonly known as the Indian sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and dark green, lanceolate leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry deciduous forests.
