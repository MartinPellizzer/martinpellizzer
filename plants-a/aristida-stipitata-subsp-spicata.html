Aristida stipitata subsp. spicata

Aristida stipitata subsp.

spicata, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, savannas, and disturbed soils.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often reddish in color. It is characterized by its slender stems and small, spikelet-bearing clusters.

This subspecies is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
