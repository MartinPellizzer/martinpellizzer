Alysicarpus tetragonolobus var. pashanensis

Alysicarpus tetragonolobus var. pashanensis, commonly known as Pashan grass, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and forest margins. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers.

The variety pashanensis is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and habitat preferences.
