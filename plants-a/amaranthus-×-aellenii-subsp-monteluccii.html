Amaranthus × aellenii subsp. monteluccii

Amaranthus × aellenii subsp.

monteluccii, commonly known as the mountain amaranth, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andes region of South America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy inflorescences that range in color from red to orange.

The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, scale-like bracts that surround the flowering spike. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are sturdy and often branched.

This species is well adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in full sun with well-drained soils.
