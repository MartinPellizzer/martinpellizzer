Astragalus diehlii

Astragalus diehlii, commonly known as the Diehl's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus diehlii is often found in areas with limited soil moisture, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
