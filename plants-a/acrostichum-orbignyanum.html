Acrostichum orbignyanum

Acrostichum orbignyanum, commonly known as the giant reed or giant arum, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its large, broad, arrow-shaped leaves that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant produces a spadix, a fleshy spike of tiny flowers, surrounded by a spathe, which is often brightly colored. It thrives in wetland environments and is native to South America.

Acrostichum orbignyanum is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its striking appearance and adaptability.
