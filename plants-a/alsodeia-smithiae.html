Alsodeia smithiae

Alsodeia smithiae, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its succulent leaves and drought-resistant nature make it well adapted to rocky and sandy soils.

Alsodeia smithiae is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
