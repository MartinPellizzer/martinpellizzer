Aerides testacea

Aerides testacea, commonly known as the testaceum orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with a distinctive yellowish-green throat. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on tree trunks or branches in humid tropical forests. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Aerides testacea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to cultivation.
