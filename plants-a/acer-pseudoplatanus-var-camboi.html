Acer pseudoplatanus var. camboi

Acer pseudoplatanus var. camboi, commonly known as the Camboi maple, is a variant of the common maple, characterized by its distinctive foliage and growth habits.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, where it thrives in temperate climates. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a vibrant green color that turns striking shades of red and orange in autumn. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.

Its growth pattern includes a spreading habit with a rounded canopy, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
