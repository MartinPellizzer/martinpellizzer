Acostaea bicornis

Acostaea bicornis, commonly known as the two-horned water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, forked leaves that resemble two horns, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its leaves are usually submerged, while its flowers emerge above the water surface.

Acostaea bicornis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
