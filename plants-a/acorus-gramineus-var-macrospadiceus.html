Acorus gramineus var. macrospadiceus

Acorus gramineus var. macrospadiceus, commonly known as the broadspadix sweetflag, is a variant of the sweetflag plant native to parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found near water bodies.

Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.
