Avena vaviloviana var. pseudoabyssinica

Avena vaviloviana var. pseudoabyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian oat, is a perennial grass species native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for erosion control, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
