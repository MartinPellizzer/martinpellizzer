Astragalus sempervirens subsp. nevadensis

Astragalus sempervirens subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
