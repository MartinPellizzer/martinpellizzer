Aristida micropoda

Aristida micropoda, commonly known as slender three-awn, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a compact growth habit and produces small, pale brown seeds.

Aristida micropoda is often used in restoration projects due to its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil.
