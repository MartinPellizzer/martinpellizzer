Aquilegia pyrenaica var. discolor

Aquilegia pyrenaica var. discolor, commonly known as the Pyrenean columbine, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features striking blue to purple flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five deep lobes, and its flowers bloom in early spring. It thrives in woodland and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native range.
