Andropogon muricatus var. aristatus

Andropogon muricatus var. aristatus, commonly known as the tall bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This grass thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of native prairie and woodland habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
