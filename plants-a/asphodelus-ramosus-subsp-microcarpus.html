Asphodelus ramosus subsp. microcarpus

Asphodelus ramosus subsp.

microcarpus, commonly known as the dwarf asphodel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, branched inflorescences.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine in some regions.
