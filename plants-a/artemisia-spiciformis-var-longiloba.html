Artemisia spiciformis var. longiloba

Artemisia spiciformis var. longiloba, commonly known as silverleaf wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in compact spikes, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush habitats.

Its aromatic properties make it a valuable plant in traditional herbal medicine and ecological restoration efforts.
