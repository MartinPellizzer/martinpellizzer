Angostura grandiflora

Angostura grandiflora, commonly known as the Cuban bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Cuba and the Bahamas.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, oval fruits that contain seeds.

Angostura grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
