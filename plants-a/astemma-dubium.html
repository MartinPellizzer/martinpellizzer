Astemma dubium

Astemma dubium, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a rare and elusive species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that appear almost ethereal under sunlight. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a subtle touch of color to its otherwise ghostly appearance. Astemma dubium thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky crevices or shaded forest floors.

Due to its rarity and unique botanical features, it is of great interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
