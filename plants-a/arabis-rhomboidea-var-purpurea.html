Arabis rhomboidea var. purpurea

Arabis rhomboidea var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in coastal areas. The plant features basal leaves that are rhomboidal in shape, hence its species name, and produces small, showy flowers in shades of white and purple. Its blooming period typically occurs in spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
