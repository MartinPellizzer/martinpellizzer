Aster ensifolius

Aster ensifolius, commonly known as the cutleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a finely toothed margin, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
