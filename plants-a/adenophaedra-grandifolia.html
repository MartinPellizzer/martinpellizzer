Adenophaedra grandifolia

Adenophaedra grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved adenophaedra, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Adenophaedra grandifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
