Aizopsis pseudohybrida

Aizopsis pseudohybrida, commonly known as the African evergreen or African fern, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

Aizopsis pseudohybrida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some African countries.
