Aquilegia jonesii subsp. elatior

Aquilegia jonesii subsp.

elatior, commonly known as the alpine columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It features striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has compound leaves with finely divided lobes, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
