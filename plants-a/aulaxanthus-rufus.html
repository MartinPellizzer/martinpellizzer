Aulaxanthus rufus

Aulaxanthus rufus, commonly known as the red-flowered aulax, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that contain several seeds.

Aulaxanthus rufus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
