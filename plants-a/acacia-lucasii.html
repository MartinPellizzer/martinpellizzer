Acacia lucasii

Acacia lucasii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, forming intricate globular clusters. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that serve as the primary photosynthetic organs. Acacia lucasii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

It is a symbol of Australia and plays an important role in local ecosystems.
