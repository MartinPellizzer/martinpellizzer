Andropogon ariani

Andropogon ariani, commonly known as the eastern woodland grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Andropogon ariani plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insect species.
