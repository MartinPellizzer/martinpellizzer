Allium paniculatum var. pauciflorum

Allium paniculatum var. pauciflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. The plant belongs to the Allium genus, which includes various edible and ornamental species. It is often cultivated for its culinary uses and ornamental value in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it suitable for growing in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.
