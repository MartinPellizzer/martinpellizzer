Aconitum kiyomiense

Aconitum kiyomiense, commonly known as the Kiyomi mountain aconite, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, wavy appearance.

Aconitum kiyomiense is known for its toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
