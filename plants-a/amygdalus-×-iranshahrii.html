Amygdalus × iranshahrii

Amygdalus × iranshahrii, commonly known as the Persian almond.

This hybrid plant is a cross between Prunus dulcis (sweet almond) and Prunus amygdalus (bitter almond). It is native to Iran and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its attractive flowers and foliage. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

It produces edible nuts similar to sweet almonds, though they may have a slightly bitter taste.
