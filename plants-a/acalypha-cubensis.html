Acalypha cubensis

Acalypha cubensis, commonly known as Cuban glorybower, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of clusters of small, tubular flowers surrounded by vibrant, bract-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often a deep red or purple, adding visual interest to gardens.

Acalypha cubensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique floral display.
