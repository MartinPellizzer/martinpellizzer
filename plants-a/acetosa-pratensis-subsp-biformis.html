Acetosa pratensis subsp. biformis

Acetosa pratensis subsp.

biformis, commonly known as the two-form meadow salsify, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in moist grasslands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, linear to lanceolate, and often have a hairy surface.

The subspecies is distinguished by its two distinct forms, which may vary in leaf shape and flower structure.
