Adina metcalfii

Adina metcalfii, commonly known as the California rosewood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of Southern California.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Adina metcalfii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
