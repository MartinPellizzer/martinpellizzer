Alsophila imbricata

Alsophila imbricata, commonly known as the shield fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive shield-like appearance on the underside. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The plant plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
