Aster gardneri

Aster gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces showy blue to purple flowers. It is a perennial herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Aster gardneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
