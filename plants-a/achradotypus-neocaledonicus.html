Achradotypus neocaledonicus

Achradotypus neocaledonicus, commonly known as the New Caledonian sandalwood, is a rare and endemic species native to the island of New Caledonia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and aromatic, oily wood. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered critically endangered, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
