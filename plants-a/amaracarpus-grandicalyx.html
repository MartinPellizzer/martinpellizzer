Amaracarpus grandicalyx

Amaracarpus grandicalyx, commonly known as the large-leafed amaracarpus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, followed by woody, spiny fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates.
