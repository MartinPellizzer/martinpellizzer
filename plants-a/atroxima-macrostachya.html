Atroxima macrostachya

Atroxima macrostachya, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, making it one of the largest orchids in the world. The plant features long, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of moisture and nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its size and beauty, it is a popular subject in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
