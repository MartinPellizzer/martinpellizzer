Arnica membranacea

Arnica membranacea, commonly known as narrowleaf arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing numerous disc florets surrounded by ray florets.

Arnica membranacea is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
