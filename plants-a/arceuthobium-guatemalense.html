Arceuthobium guatemalense

Arceuthobium guatemalense, commonly known as the Guatemalan witchweed, is a parasitic plant that belongs to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host plant for nutrients, typically attaching itself to the roots of grasses or other herbaceous plants. Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to agricultural crops.
