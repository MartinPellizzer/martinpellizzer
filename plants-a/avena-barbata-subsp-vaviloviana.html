Avena barbata subsp. vaviloviana

Avena barbata subsp.

vaviloviana, commonly known as the Vavilovian bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and spikelets that are often adorned with short, barbed hairs.

The plant typically thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its genetic diversity and ecological adaptability.
