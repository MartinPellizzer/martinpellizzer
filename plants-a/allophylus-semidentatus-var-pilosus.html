Allophylus semidentatus var. pilosus

Allophylus semidentatus var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy alpine holly, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it is often found growing in rocky or well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
