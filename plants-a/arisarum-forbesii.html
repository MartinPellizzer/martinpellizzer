Arisarum forbesii

Arisarum forbesii, commonly known as the yellow swamp lily, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its striking yellow spathe and spadix inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while the flowers are designed for pollination by insects.

Arisarum forbesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
