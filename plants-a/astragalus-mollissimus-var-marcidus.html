Astragalus mollissimus var. marcidus

Astragalus mollissimus var. marcidus, commonly known as the soft hairy milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gravelly slopes.

This species is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
