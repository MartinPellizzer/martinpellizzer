Aspalathus frankenioides

Aspalathus frankenioides, commonly known as the Franklin's bush, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, typically from September to November. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Aspalathus frankenioides is often found in fynbos vegetation and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
