Astrocarpa sesamoides var. interrupta

Astrocarpa sesamoides var. interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted sesamoid, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched stems and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant exhibits a herbaceous growth habit and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat obscure species in botanical studies.
