Agropyron molle

Agropyron molle, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides and fields. It is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and its use in forage and erosion control.

Agropyron molle is also cultivated for its nutritional value in livestock feed.
