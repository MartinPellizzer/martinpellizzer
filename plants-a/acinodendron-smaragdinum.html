Acinodendron smaragdinum

Acinodendron smaragdinum, commonly known as the emerald bellflower, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale green to deep emerald, reflecting its common name. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, glossy bark and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Acinodendron smaragdinum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its rare and exotic appearance.
