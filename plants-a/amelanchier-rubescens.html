Amelanchier rubescens

Amelanchier rubescens, commonly known as the red elderberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that ripen in late summer, which are edible for humans and wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and turn yellow in the fall.

Amelanchier rubescens is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
