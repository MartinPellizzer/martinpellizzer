Arundinaria hukudana

Arundinaria hukudana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Hukudana bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often a vibrant green color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Japanese gardens.

Arundinaria hukudana is also valued for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
