Angraecum rhizomaniacum

Angraecum rhizomaniacum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant features a rhizome and produces striking, star-shaped flowers with a distinctive coloration. Its flowering period usually occurs during the rainy season, and it is noted for its unique floral structure.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in its native region.
