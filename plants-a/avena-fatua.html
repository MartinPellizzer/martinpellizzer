Avena fatua

Avena fatua, commonly known as wild oat, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and long, narrow leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets, each with a single glume and a two-lobed lemma. Wild oat is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types, often thriving in disturbed areas.

It is considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its competitive nature and ability to self-seed prolifically.
