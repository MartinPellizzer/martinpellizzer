Aspidium thelypteris var. noveboracense

Aspidium thelypteris var. noveboracense, commonly known as the New York fern, is a variant of the common wood fern.

It is a perennial evergreen fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in woodland areas. The plant has fronds that are bipinnate, with a distinctive appearance of finely divided, arching leaves. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
