Aster ammani

Aster ammani, commonly known as the Ammann's aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces showy blue to purple flowers in the fall. It has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Aster ammani is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
