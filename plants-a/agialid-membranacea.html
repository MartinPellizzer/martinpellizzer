Agialid membranacea

Agialid membranacea, commonly known as the membranous agialid, is a unique aquatic plant found in specific wetland environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that are often less than 1 centimeter in length. The plant typically grows in shallow, still water bodies, where it thrives in nutrient-rich substrates. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, relying on water currents for seed dispersal.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, Agialid membranacea is considered a rare and sensitive indicator of wetland health.
