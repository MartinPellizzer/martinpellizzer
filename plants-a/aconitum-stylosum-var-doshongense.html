Aconitum stylosum var. doshongense

Aconitum stylosum var. doshongense, commonly known as the Himalayan aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple spathaceous flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and the plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is often cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
