Amaranthus ruderalis

Amaranthus ruderalis, commonly known as the redroot amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, often reddish, bracts that surround its small flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, making it a common weed. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Amaranthus ruderalis is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
