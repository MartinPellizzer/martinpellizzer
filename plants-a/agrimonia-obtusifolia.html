Agrimonia obtusifolia

Agrimonia obtusifolia, commonly known as common agrimony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom from June to August. Its fruit is a dry, two-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Agrimony is known for its medicinal properties and has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
