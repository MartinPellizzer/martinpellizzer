Achillea roseo-alba f. violacea

Achillea roseo-alba f. violacea, commonly known as the violet-floored yarrow, is a cultivar of the yarrow plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its pale pink to violet flowers that bloom in flat, clustered heads. The plant features finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and slightly fuzzy to the touch. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate floral display and hardy nature.
