Aster laevis var. geyeri

Aster laevis var. geyeri, commonly known as the Geyeri's smooth aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features showy, daisy-like flowers with white to pale blue petals and yellow centers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
