Acmena brachyandra

Acmena brachyandra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved apple tree, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 8 meters in height, featuring a spreading canopy and smooth, greyish bark. The leaves are lance-shaped, glossy green on the upper surface, and have a distinctive whitish underside. Its flowers are small, white to pink, and appear in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits that are a food source for various wildlife.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a range of soil types in its native habitat.
