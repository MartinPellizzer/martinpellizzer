Arundinaria ulei

Arundinaria ulei, commonly known as the bamboo grass, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly China and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Arundinaria ulei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in basket weaving and construction.
