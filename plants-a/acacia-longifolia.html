Acacia longifolia

Acacia longifolia, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and compound leaves that are typically feathery in appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. Acacia longifolia thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often growing in dunes or along the coastline.

It is well adapted to dry conditions and is an important component of coastal ecosystems.
