Archidendron apoense

Archidendron apoense, commonly known as the Philippine wistaria, is a climbing legume native to the Philippines.

It features compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by nitrogen-fixing capabilities. Its flowers are typically violet or purple in color, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and flowering habits.
