Astracantha arnacantha

Astracantha arnacantha, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its milky sap is a notable feature, often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
