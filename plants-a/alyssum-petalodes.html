Alyssum petalodes

Alyssum petalodes, commonly known as the pink alpine bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, showy pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are typically opposite or whorled along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to gardens seeking drought-tolerant and visually appealing plants.
