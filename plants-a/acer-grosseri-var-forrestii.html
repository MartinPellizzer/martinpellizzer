Acer grosseri var. forrestii

Acer grosseri var. forrestii, commonly known as the Yunnan maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The tree produces small, red flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice in horticulture.
