Asphodelus altaicus

Asphodelus altaicus, commonly known as the Siberian asphodel, is a perennial herb native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It features a robust rhizome system and produces tall, leafless stems that bear long, narrow leaves. The plant is characterized by its spike-like inflorescence composed of numerous pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Asphodelus altaicus is often used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value in gardens.
