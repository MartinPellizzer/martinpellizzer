Arabis multijuga

Arabis multijuga, commonly known as the broad-leaved rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. It features basal rosettes of lobed leaves and produces small, white or yellow flowers in early spring.

Arabis multijuga is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
