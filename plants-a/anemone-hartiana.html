Anemone hartiana

Anemone hartiana, commonly known as the Hart's anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features showy, white to pink flowers with a distinctive red center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade.

Anemone hartiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
