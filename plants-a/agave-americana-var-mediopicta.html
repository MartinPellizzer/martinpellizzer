Agave americana var. mediopicta

Agave americana var. mediopicta, commonly known as the Variegated Agave, is a striking succulent native to Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green with white or yellow margins. The leaves are lined with sharp spines and have a textured, almost quilted surface. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers, though it rarely blooms in cultivation.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
