Alopecurus colobachnoides

Alopecurus colobachnoides, commonly known as narrow-leaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Alopecurus colobachnoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
