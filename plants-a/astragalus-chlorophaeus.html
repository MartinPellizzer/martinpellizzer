Astragalus chlorophaeus

Astragalus chlorophaeus, commonly known as the greenleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in dry grasslands and sagebrush habitats. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus chlorophaeus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
