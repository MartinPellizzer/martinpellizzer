Alopecias dasyanthus

Alopecias dasyanthus, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.

Alopecias dasyanthus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
