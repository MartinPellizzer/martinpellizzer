Asplenium × vogelii

Asplenium × vogelii, commonly known as the hybrid maidenhair fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Asplenium trichomanes and Asplenium bulbiferum.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green with a silvery underside. The plant prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium × vogelii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded conditions.
