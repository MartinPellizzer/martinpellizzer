Aster lateriflorus var. thyrsoideus

Aster lateriflorus var. thyrsoideus, commonly known as the thyrsoideus aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in wetlands and moist soils, exhibiting a herbaceous growth habit with upright, branching stems. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture, contributing to its ecological value in wetland habitats.
