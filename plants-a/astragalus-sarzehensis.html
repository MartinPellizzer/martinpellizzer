Astragalus sarzehensis

Astragalus sarzehensis, commonly known as the Sarzeh Astragalus, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant produces pods that contain several small, hard seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Astragalus sarzehensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Its ecological significance lies in its role as a food source for local wildlife and its potential medicinal properties.
