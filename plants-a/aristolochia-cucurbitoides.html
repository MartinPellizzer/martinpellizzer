Aristolochia cucurbitoides

Aristolochia cucurbitoides, commonly known as the cucumber vine, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and produces striking, hooded flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, winding growth habit. Its fruit is a elongated, green capsule that matures to a brownish color, containing several seeds.

However, it is important to note that all parts of this plant are toxic and should not be ingested.
