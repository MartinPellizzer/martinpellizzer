Atherosperma elongatum

Atherosperma elongatum, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a distinctive root system that forms a dense network, helping it anchor in rocky or coastal environments. Its spores are produced in clusters on the undersides of its fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Atherosperma elongatum is often found in coastal and subalpine regions, where it thrives in moist, shaded conditions.
