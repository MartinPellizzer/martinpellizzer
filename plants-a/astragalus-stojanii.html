Astragalus stojanii

Astragalus stojanii, commonly known as the Stojanov's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Astragalus stojanii is often found in alpine and steppe habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
