Ampelopsis macrophylla

Ampelopsis macrophylla, commonly known as the giant grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in the summer, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its vigorous growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
