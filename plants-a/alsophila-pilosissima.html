Alsophila pilosissima

Alsophila pilosissima, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a fibrous, hairy layer that gives it its common name. The plant produces large, feathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Alsophila pilosissima plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
