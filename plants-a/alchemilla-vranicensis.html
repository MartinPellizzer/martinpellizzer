Alchemilla vranicensis

Alchemilla vranicensis, commonly known as the Vranic lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in a corymbose inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Alchemilla vranicensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
