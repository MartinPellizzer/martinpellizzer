Aspidium anastomosans

Aspidium anastomosans, commonly known as the fork-veined water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically divided into narrow, feather-like segments. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as ponds and slow-moving streams, where it can receive adequate sunlight and nutrients. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its propagation.

The plant's unique vascular system allows it to efficiently transport water and nutrients, supporting its growth in aquatic habitats.
