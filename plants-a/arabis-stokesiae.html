Arabis stokesiae

Arabis stokesiae, commonly known as Stokes' honesty, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern England and parts of western France. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, silver-green leaves and produces small, white flowers with four petals. It blooms from April to June and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

The fruit is a dry, segmented capsule that splits open to release the seeds.
