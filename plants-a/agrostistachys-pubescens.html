Agrostistachys pubescens

Agrostistachys pubescens, commonly known as the hairy beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

Agrostistachys pubescens is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and drought tolerance.
