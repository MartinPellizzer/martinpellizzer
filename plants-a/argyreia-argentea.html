Argyreia argentea

Argyreia argentea, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and silver-veined, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not properly managed.
