Amelanchier intermedia

Amelanchier intermedia, commonly known as the common serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It produces edible, sweet berries that ripen in early summer, making it a valuable plant for wildlife and humans alike. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

Amelanchier intermedia is also valued for its ornamental qualities, including its showy spring flowers and attractive bark.
