Aconitum tianschanicum

Aconitum tianschanicum, commonly known as the Tianshan aconite, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces distinctive, helmet-shaped seed pods that contain small, hard seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a hazardous plant and should be handled with care.
