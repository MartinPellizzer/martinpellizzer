Astragalus superbus

Astragalus superbus, commonly known as the tall milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep violet, typically blooming in late summer. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky environments.

Astragalus superbus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
