Acacia janzenii

Acacia janzenii, commonly known as the Janzen's wattle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish inflorescences that are typical of the Acacia genus. Its seeds are encased in pods, which mature into woody structures.

Acacia janzenii is adapted to grow in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or secondary forest habitats.
