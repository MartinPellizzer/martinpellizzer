Amygdalus rosaeflora

Amygdalus rosaeflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered almond.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

The fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed surrounded by a fleshy pulp.
