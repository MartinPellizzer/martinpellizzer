Aspalathus lactea subsp. adelphea

Aspalathus lactea subsp.

adelphea, commonly known as the whitebosch or whitebush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant typically grows in shrubland and fynbos vegetation, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and attractive floral display.
