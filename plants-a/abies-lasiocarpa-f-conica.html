Abies lasiocarpa f. conica

Abies lasiocarpa f. conica, commonly known as the White fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, cone-shaped crown and dark green, needle-like leaves. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for over 100 years, making it a long-lived species. Its cones are typically reddish-brown and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The tree thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
