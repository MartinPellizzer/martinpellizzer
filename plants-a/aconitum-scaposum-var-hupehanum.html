Aconitum scaposum var. hupehanum

Aconitum scaposum var. hupehanum, commonly known as the Hupeh Aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense racemes. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed leaflets that are hairy on the underside.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
