Acacia crassuloides

Acacia crassuloides, commonly known as the dwarf acacia or stone flower, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
