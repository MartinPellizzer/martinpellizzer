Artemisia trifurcata var. tacomensis

Artemisia trifurcata var. tacomensis, commonly known as Tacomensis Wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and narrow, linear leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
