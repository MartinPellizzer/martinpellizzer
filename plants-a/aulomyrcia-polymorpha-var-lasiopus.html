Aulomyrcia polymorpha var. lasiopus

Aulomyrcia polymorpha var. lasiopus, commonly known as the roughleaf alder, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in riparian zones. The plant has compound leaves with serrated margins and produces small, clustered yellow flowers in the spring. Its fruits are cone-like structures that contain three to five winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
