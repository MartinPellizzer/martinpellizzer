Angelonia cornigera

Angelonia cornigera, commonly known as the Mexican angelonia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant blooms in shades of red, pink, and white, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Angelonia cornigera is often used in ornamental gardening for its long blooming season and low maintenance requirements.
