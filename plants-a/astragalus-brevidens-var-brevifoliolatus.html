Astragalus brevidens var. brevifoliolatus

Astragalus brevidens var. brevifoliolatus, commonly known as the short-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with short, narrow leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in dense spikes, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.
