Aspalathus globulosa

Aspalathus globulosa, commonly known as the rooibos plant, is a shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and is known for its unique ability to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by reddish-brown seeds. It is a key species in the fynbos biome and is widely cultivated for its caffeine-free tea.

Aspalathus globulosa is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
