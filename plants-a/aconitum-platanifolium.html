Aconitum platanifolium

Aconitum platanifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved aconite, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately divided leaves and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of alkaloids such as aconitine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is cultivated in gardens but requires caution due to its poisonous nature.
