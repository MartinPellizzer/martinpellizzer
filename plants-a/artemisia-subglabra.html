Artemisia subglabra

Artemisia subglabra, commonly known as silverleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. Artemisia subglabra thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for arid environments.

Its aromatic properties and drought tolerance make it a valuable species in xeriscaping and traditional herbal practices.
