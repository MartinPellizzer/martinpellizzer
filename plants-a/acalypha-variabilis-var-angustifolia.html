Acalypha variabilis var. angustifolia

Acalypha variabilis var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, with a subtle aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
