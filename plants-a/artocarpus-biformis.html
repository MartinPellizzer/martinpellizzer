Artocarpus biformis

Artocarpus biformis, commonly known as the two-lobed breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a unique fruit with a rough, fibrous exterior and a sweet, starchy pulp inside. It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
