Asparagus botswanicus

Asparagus botswanicus, commonly known as the Botswana asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of food in local communities.
