Athyrium bambusicola

Athyrium bambusicola, commonly known as the bamboo fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often arched or curved. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can form dense colonies, contributing to its ability to thrive in humid conditions.

The spore-bearing structures, or sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of the Athyriaceae family.
