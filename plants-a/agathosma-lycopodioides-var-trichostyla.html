Agathosma lycopodioides var. trichostyla

Agathosma lycopodioides var. trichostyla, commonly known as the Cape mountain sandwort, is a low-growing, evergreen perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae, which includes clubmosses and related species. The plant features small, needle-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces spores rather than seeds. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

This variety is distinguished by its fine, hairy stems and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
