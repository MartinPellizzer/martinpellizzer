Andropogon subulatus

Andropogon subulatus, commonly known as the needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes.

Andropogon subulatus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
