Anemone hortensis var. fulgens

Anemone hortensis var. fulgens, commonly known as the fiery anemone, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range from white to deep crimson, with a distinctive red center. The plant has palmate, deeply lobed leaves that are velvety to the touch and emit a mild, spicy scent. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its botanical name reflects its ornamental significance and its status as a cultivated variant of the common anemone.
