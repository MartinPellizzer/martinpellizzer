Alchemilla ellenbeckii

Alchemilla ellenbeckii, commonly known as Ellenbeck's lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
