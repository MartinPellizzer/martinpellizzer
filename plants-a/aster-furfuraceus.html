Aster furfuraceus

Aster furfuraceus, commonly known as the fuzzy aster, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, grayish-green leaves and clusters of small, violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Aster furfuraceus typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

It is a hardy perennial that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
