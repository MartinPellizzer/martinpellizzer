Ardisia florulenta

Ardisia florulenta, commonly known as the fragrant evergreen shrub, is a species native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for humans but toxic to some animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
