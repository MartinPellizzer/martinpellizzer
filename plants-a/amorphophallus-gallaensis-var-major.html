Amorphophallus gallaensis var. major

Amorphophallus gallaensis var. major, commonly known as the giant African arrowroot, is a tuberous plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, starchy underground tubers. The plant produces tall, leafy stems with large, spadix-like inflorescences that are often surrounded by a showy spathe. Its tubers are used as a food source and have a starchy texture similar to taro.

The species is valued both for its culinary use and its ornamental value in gardens.
