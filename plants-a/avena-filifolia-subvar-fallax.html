Avena filifolia subvar. fallax

Avena filifolia subvar. fallax, commonly known as the slender oat, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny grains. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
