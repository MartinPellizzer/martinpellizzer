Agave viridissima

Agave viridissima, commonly known as the blue agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are usually blue-green in color, which give it its common name. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing small, white flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Agave viridissima is also cultivated for its sap, which is used in the production of tequila.
