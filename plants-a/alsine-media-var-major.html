Alsine media var. major

Alsine media var. major, commonly known as the greater alsike clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive blue-green color. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Alsine media var. major is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and is sometimes used in forage and ecological restoration projects.
