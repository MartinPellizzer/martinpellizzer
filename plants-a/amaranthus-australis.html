Amaranthus australis

Amaranthus australis, commonly known as the Downy Amaranth, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a soft, downy texture and produces clusters of small, greenish flowers that develop into spiky, triangular seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and sandy soils.

The plant is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
