Ardisia proctorii

Ardisia proctorii, commonly known as the Proctor's beautyberry, is a member of the Ericaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright purple berries in the fall. These berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Ardisia proctorii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal fruit.
