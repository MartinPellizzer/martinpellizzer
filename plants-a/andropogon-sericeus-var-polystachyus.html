Andropogon sericeus var. polystachyus

Andropogon sericeus var. polystachyus, commonly known as the silky grass or silky panicgrass, is a variant of the silky grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fine, silver-white to gray-green foliage. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that are often wind-pollinated, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it ecologically significant in wetland habitats.
