Aegiphila steinbachii

Aegiphila steinbachii, commonly known as the mountain thyme, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to cold, mountainous climates.

Aegiphila steinbachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Morphology

Aegiphila steinbachii has small, ovate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, with a glossy green color and a slightly hairy surface.

The stem is slender, upright, and covered with a rough, grayish-brown bark that becomes smoother with age. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, featuring tubular structures with purple-pink petals, blooming during the dry season and primarily pollinated by bees. The fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, adapted to absorb moisture from the sandy soils in its native habitat.
