Ailanthus glandulosa

Ailanthus glandulosa, commonly known as the tree of heaven, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with 12 to 30 pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by bright orange to red berries that contain three seeds. It has a rapid growth rate and can reach heights of up to 50 feet.

The plant is known for its distinctive, aromatic scent and its ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
