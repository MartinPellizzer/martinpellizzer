Abies guatemalensis var. longibracteata

Abies guatemalensis var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract fir, is a rare and endemic species native to high-altitude regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its conical shape, with dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in spirals along the branches. The plant produces cones that are often longer and more robust than those of the typical *Abies guatemalensis*, with distinctive bract-like structures. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
