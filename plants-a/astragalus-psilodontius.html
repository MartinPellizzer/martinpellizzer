Astragalus psilodontius

Astragalus psilodontius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the pods that develop after flowering are usually slender and tapering.

Astragalus psilodontius is often found in steppe and desert habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
