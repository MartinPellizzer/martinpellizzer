Achillea atrata var. beckiana

Achillea atrata var. beckiana, commonly known as the black yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to almost black flower heads, which contrast with its silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower structures.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and long-lasting blooms.
