Adenophora mongolica

Adenophora mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Mongolia and northern China.

It features a robust root system and upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, adapting to its harsh environment.

Adenophora mongolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
