Arctium × leiobardana

Arctium × leiobardana, commonly known as the greater burdock, is a hybrid plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and tall, spiky flower heads that produce burrs. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows. Its roots are thick and fibrous, and the plant is known for its ability to spread through seed dispersal via its hooked bracts.

It has been used historically in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
