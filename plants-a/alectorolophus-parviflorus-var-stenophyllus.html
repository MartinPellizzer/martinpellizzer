Alectorolophus parviflorus var. stenophyllus

Alectorolophus parviflorus var. stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
