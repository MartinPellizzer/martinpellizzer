Arduina haematocarpa

Arduina haematocarpa, commonly known as the bloodroot tree, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, blood-like sap, which gives it its common name and contributes to its medicinal significance. The plant has a thick, woody stem with dark green, broad leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in clusters of three, and it produces capsule fruits that contain the seeds.

Due to its rarity and ecological importance, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
