Athanasia palmatifida

Athanasia palmatifida, commonly known as the broadleaf evergreen, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves that are often deeply divided. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is considered a rare and valuable addition to botanical collections.
