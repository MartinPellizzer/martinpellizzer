Andropogon griffithsiae

Andropogon griffithsiae, commonly known as Griffith's bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in native plant gardens and restoration projects.
