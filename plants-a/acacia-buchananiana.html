Acacia buchananiana

Acacia buchananiana, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration. Its bark and sap have been traditionally used in various medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
