Astragalus xiphoides

Astragalus xiphoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with narrow, linear leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments through its deep root system and drought-resistant features.
