Amaranthus filicaulis

Amaranthus filicaulis, commonly known as the velvetleaf amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into three-angled, spiny seed capsules. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Amaranthus filicaulis is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes other economically important plants.
