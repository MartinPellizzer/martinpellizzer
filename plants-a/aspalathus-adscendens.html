Aspalathus adscendens

Aspalathus adscendens, commonly known as the sweet cycad, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and large, palm-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by the development of woody, cone-like structures. Aspalathus adscendens is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
