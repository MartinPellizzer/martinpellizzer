Amygdalus pallasiana

Amygdalus pallasiana, commonly known as the Pallas's almond, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Kazakhstan, and the Tian Shan. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the development of edible, sweet-tasting fruits. Its leaves are alternate and have serrated edges, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
