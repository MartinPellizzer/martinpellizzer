Apteranthes europaea var. confusa

Apteranthes europaea var. confusa, commonly known as the European false dandelion, is a perennial herb native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in a single head, followed by pale green, woolly seed heads. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a common weed in many regions.
