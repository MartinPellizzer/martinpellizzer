Adesmia corymbosa var. sinepenna

Adesmia corymbosa var. sinepenna, commonly known as the silky adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate appearance.
