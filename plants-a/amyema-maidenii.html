Amyema maidenii

Amyema maidenii, commonly known as the Maiden's lily, is an ornamental plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Amyema maidenii is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
