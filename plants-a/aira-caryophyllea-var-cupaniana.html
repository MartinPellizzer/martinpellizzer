Aira caryophyllea var. cupaniana

Aira caryophyllea var. cupaniana, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple to pink flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
