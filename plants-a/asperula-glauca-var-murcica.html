Asperula glauca var. murcica

Asperula glauca var. murcica, commonly known as the Murcian bluebells, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are evergreen, glossy, and arranged in whorls around the stem.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
