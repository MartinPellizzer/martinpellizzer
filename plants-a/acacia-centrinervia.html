Acacia centrinervia

Acacia centrinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are narrow and have a distinctive central vein. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

Acacia centrinervia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
