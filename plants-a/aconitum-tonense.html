Aconitum tonense

Aconitum tonense, commonly known as the Tonese monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. Aconitum tonense thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

Like other members of its genus, it contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
