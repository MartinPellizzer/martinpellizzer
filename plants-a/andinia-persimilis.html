Andinia persimilis

Andinia persimilis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in cloud forests. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip petal that aids in pollination by insects.

Andinia persimilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
