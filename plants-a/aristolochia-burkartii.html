Aristolochia burkartii

Aristolochia burkartii, commonly known as the Mexican Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and striking, maroon-colored flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, tubular floral structure. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it contains toxic compounds that require caution in handling.
