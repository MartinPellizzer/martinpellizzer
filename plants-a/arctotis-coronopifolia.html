Arctotis coronopifolia

Arctotis coronopifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in dense, mist-like clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. Its foliage consists of narrow, hairy leaves that are often grayish-green in color.

Arctotis coronopifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
