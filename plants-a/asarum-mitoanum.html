Asarum mitoanum

Asarum mitoanum, commonly known as the Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, hooded flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its rhizomatous root system is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Asarum mitoanum thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
