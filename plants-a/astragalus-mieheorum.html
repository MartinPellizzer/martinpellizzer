Astragalus mieheorum

Astragalus mieheorum, commonly known as the Mieheli's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.

Its conservation status is currently listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss and overgrazing.
