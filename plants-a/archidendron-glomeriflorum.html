Archidendron glomeriflorum

Archidendron glomeriflorum, commonly known as the cluster-flowered legume, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
