Andropogon tripsacoides

Andropogon tripsacoides, commonly known as big bluestem, is a tall grass native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its blue-green foliage and distinctive, stiff, wiry stems. This plant is a key component of tallgrass prairie ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat diversity. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to prairie conditions.

Its flowering spikes are typically reddish-brown, and it plays an important role in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
