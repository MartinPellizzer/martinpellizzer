Alectryon ochraceus

Alectryon ochraceus, commonly known as the yellow-fruit alectryon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a woody capsule that turns orange when mature, giving the plant its common name. It typically grows in coastal areas and is often found in sandy soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
