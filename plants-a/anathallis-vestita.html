Anathallis vestita

Anathallis vestita, commonly known as the white spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spider-like appearance with pale greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are pollinated by specific species of flies, showcasing a unique mutualistic relationship.

Anathallis vestita is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance in its native habitats.
