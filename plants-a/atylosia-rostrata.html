Atylosia rostrata

Atylosia rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and the plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils.

Atylosia rostrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid environments.
