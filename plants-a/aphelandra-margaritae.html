Aphelandra margaritae

Aphelandra margaritae, commonly known as the silver lace plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, glossy, dark green leaves that have a distinctive veined pattern resembling lace. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Aphelandra margaritae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its elegant foliage and continuous flowering.
