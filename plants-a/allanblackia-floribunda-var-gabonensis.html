Allanblackia floribunda var. gabonensis

Allanblackia floribunda var. gabonensis, commonly known as the Gabon blackwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by black, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
