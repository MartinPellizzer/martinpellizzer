Aster microcephalus f. vernalis

Aster microcephalus f. vernalis, commonly known as the spring aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features showy, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native environment.
