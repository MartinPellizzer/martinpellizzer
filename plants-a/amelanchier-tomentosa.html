Amelanchier tomentosa

Amelanchier tomentosa, commonly known as the smooth serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has a rounded form with smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

The berries are a valuable food source for birds and wildlife, contributing to its ecological importance.
