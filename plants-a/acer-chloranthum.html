Acer chloranthum

Acer chloranthum, commonly known as the yellow maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow leaves, which turn a vibrant orange-red in the fall. The plant has opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are typically 5-9 cm in length. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, appearing in clusters before the leaves emerge in spring.

The tree produces samaras, or winged seeds, which aid in its dispersal.
