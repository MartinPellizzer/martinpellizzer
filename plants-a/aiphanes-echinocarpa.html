Aiphanes echinocarpa

Aiphanes echinocarpa, commonly known as the spiny palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Cuba and the Isle of Pines. The plant is characterized by its robust, clustered stems and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its inflorescences are terminal and bear small, yellowish flowers, which are followed by dark, spiny fruits.

This plant is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
