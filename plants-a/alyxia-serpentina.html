Alyxia serpentina

Alyxia serpentina, commonly known as the serpentine alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Alyxia serpentina is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in swampy areas.
