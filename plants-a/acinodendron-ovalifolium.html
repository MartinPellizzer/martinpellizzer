Acinodendron ovalifolium

Acinodendron ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaved acinodendron, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, oval-shaped leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
