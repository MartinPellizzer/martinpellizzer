Aster fuscescens var. oblongifolius

Aster fuscescens var. oblongifolius, commonly known as the dark purple aster, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple to violet petals and yellow centers, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, textured surface. It prefers moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

The botanical name reflects its dark-colored flowers and elongated foliage.
