Anacamptis caccabaria

Anacamptis caccabaria, commonly known as the Sicilian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces tall, slender spikes bearing numerous fragrant, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive hooded lip. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of conservation interest in its native range.
