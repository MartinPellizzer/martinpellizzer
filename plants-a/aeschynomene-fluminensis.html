Aeschynomene fluminensis

Aeschynomene fluminensis, commonly known as Brazilian needle grass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, pod-like fruits.

Aeschynomene fluminensis is adapted to various soil types and is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.
