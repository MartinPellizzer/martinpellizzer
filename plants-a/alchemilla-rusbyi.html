Alchemilla rusbyi

Alchemilla rusbyi, commonly known as Rusby's lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
