Acacia microsperma

Acacia microsperma, commonly known as the small-seeded acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its small, woody pods contain numerous minute, oblong seeds.

Acacia microsperma is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native range.
