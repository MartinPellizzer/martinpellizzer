Astragalus christophi

Astragalus christophi, commonly known as Christ's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, harsh environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus christophi is often found in steppe and desert ecosystems, contributing to local biodiversity.
