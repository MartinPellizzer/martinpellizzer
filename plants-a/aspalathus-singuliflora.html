Aspalathus singuliflora

Aspalathus singuliflora, commonly known as the sweet cycad, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive appearance, featuring a tall, unbranched stem and a crown of large, feathery leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a single, elongated inflorescence, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
