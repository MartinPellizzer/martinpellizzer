Asparagus angusticladus

Asparagus angusticladus, commonly known as narrowleaf asparagus, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
