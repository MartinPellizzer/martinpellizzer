Apium peucedanoides var. tenuifolium

Apium peucedanoides var. tenuifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small white or greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in open woodland or grassland habitats. Its aromatic foliage contains compounds such as apiol and limonene, which contribute to its distinctive scent.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its delicate appearance and early spring blooms.
