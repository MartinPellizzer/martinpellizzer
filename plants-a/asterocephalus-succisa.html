Asterocephalus succisa

Asterocephalus succisa, commonly known as the star-headed succulent, is a unique member of the Aizoaceae family, characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves.

It originates from the arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers in late summer, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. Its succulent nature allows it to survive in drought conditions by storing moisture in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
