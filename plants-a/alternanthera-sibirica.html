Alternanthera sibirica

Alternanthera sibirica, commonly known as Siberian Alternanthera, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves that are often reddish or purple in color, adding ornamental value. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months.

Due to its cold hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in temperate gardens as a decorative plant.
