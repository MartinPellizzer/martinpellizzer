Astragalus bethlehemiticus var. macranthus

Astragalus bethlehemiticus var. macranthus, commonly known as the Jerusalem balm, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, hard pods that contain its seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and has been historically used in traditional medicine.
