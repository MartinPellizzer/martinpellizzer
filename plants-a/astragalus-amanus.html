Astragalus amanus

Astragalus amanus, commonly known as the Amman milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus amanus is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
