Asplenium ritoense

Asplenium ritoense, commonly known as the Ritoense spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered in dense tufts. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks, deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its fronds are usually dark green and possess a soft, velvety texture.

Asplenium ritoense plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to biodiversity and providing microhabitats for various organisms.
