Aeonium × casanovense

Aeonium × casanovense, commonly known as the "Casanova's Aeonium," is a hybrid succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern, with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces striking red or pink flowers on tall, branching stems during the spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a favored ornamental plant among succulent enthusiasts.
