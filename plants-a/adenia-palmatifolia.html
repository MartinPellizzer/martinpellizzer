Adenia palmatifolia

Adenia palmatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved adenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. Adenia palmatifolia is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and private collections.
