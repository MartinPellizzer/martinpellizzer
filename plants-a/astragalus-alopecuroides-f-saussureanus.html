Astragalus alopecuroides f. saussureanus

Astragalus alopecuroides f. saussureanus, commonly known as the Saussure's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climates. It has small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological and morphological traits.
