Aristida vulgaris var. canariensis

Aristida vulgaris var. canariensis, commonly known as Canary grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Canary Islands.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as coastal areas and rocky soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
