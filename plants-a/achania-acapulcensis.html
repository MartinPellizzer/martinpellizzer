Achania acapulcensis

Achania acapulcensis, commonly known as the Acapulco acanthus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiny leaves and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers, which range in color from white to purple, bloom during the summer months.

Achania acapulcensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
