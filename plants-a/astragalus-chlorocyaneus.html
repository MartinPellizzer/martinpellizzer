Astragalus chlorocyaneus

Astragalus chlorocyaneus, commonly known as the blueleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has adapted to thrive in arid environments. Its leaves are usually compound, with a bluish-green hue that gives it its common name.

Astragalus chlorocyaneus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in its native ecosystems.
