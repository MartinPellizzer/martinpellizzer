Arundinaria albomarginata f. minor

Arundinaria albomarginata f. minor, commonly known as the silver-edged bamboo, is a unique variant of the silver bamboo species.

It is characterized by its slender, gracefully arching stems that form dense clumps. The leaves are narrow and have a distinctive silvery-white margin, contrasting with the dark green central portion. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance in suitable conditions.
