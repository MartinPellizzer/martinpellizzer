Astragalus brachybotrys

Astragalus brachybotrys, commonly known as the short-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, high-altitude environments.

Astragalus brachybotrys is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
