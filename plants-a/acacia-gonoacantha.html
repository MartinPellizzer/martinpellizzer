Acacia gonoacantha

Acacia gonoacantha, commonly known as the green thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are followed by flat, fibrous pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Acacia gonoacantha is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fodder.
