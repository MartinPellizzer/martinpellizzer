Anemone tenella

Anemone tenella, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan and Korea.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Anemone tenella is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and early spring bloom.
