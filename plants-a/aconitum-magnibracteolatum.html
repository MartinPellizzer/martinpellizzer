Aconitum magnibracteolatum

Aconitum magnibracteolatum, commonly known as the large bract aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its leaves are compound, with deeply lobed foliage that is often covered in a waxy coating.

Aconitum magnibracteolatum is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that make it highly poisonous to both animals and humans.
