Acetosella stictophylla

Acetosella stictophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves contain oxalic acid, giving them a tart flavor and making them useful in traditional herbal remedies.

Acetosella stictophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
