Aconitum tongolense

Aconitum tongolense, commonly known as the Tibetan aconite, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of Tibet and adjacent regions.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant contains potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic and requiring careful handling. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, waxy texture.

Aconitum tongolense is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments.
