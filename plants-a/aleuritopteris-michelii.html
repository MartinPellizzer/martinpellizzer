Aleuritopteris michelii

Aleuritopteris michelii, commonly known as the Micheli's huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often dichotomously branched fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in clusters on the undersides of the fronds.

Aleuritopteris michelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
