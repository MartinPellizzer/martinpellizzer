Artemisia capillaris f. myriocephala

Artemisia capillaris f. myriocephala, commonly known as Chinese yarrow or sweet wormwood, is a variant of the wormwood plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and has a distinctive, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is also valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
