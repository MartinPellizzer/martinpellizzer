Aristida macilenta

Aristida macilenta, commonly known as the slender threeawn, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture.

Aristida macilenta is a drought-tolerant species that plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
