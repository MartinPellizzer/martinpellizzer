Alcea pallida subsp. cretica

Alcea pallida subsp.

cretica, commonly known as Cretan hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Crete. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters.

The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in raceme inflorescences, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a rough texture and a distinctive silvery-white underside.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
