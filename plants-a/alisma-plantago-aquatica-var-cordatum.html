Alisma plantago-aquatica var. cordatum

Alisma plantago-aquatica var. cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often heart-shaped at the base, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a characteristic species of marshes and pond edges.

Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and supports aquatic ecosystems.
