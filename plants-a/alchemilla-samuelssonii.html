Alchemilla samuelssonii

Alchemilla samuelssonii, commonly known as the Samuelsson's lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their astringent properties.

Alchemilla samuelssonii thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
