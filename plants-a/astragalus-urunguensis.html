Astragalus urunguensis

Astragalus urunguensis, commonly known as the Mongolian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus urunguensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
