Absinthium ponticum

Absinthium ponticum, commonly known as greater centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which bloom in summer, are yellow and attract various pollinators.

Absinthium ponticum has been historically used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
