Armeniaca davidiana

Armeniaca davidiana, commonly known as the Chinese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that ripen to a deep red or orange color. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.

Armeniaca davidiana is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
