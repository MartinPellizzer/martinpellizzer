Anisopappus madagascariensis

Anisopappus madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white pappus structures for wind dispersal. It thrives in arid and rocky habitats, showcasing adaptations to its native environment.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological role in its ecosystem.
