Acacia discolor var. maritima

Acacia discolor var. maritima, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and bipinnate leaves that are adapted to withstand salty sea air. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dunes and coastal vegetation. It plays a role in stabilizing dune systems and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.

Its resilience to harsh maritime conditions makes it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
