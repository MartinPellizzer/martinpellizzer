Aegiphila fluminensis

Aegiphila fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian ironwood, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, dark green leaves that are arranged in a compact, bushy form. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Aegiphila fluminensis is known for its hard, heavy wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
