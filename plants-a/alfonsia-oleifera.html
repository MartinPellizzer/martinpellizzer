Alfonsia oleifera

Alfonsia oleifera, commonly known as the African star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple star-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and public spaces.
