Argyreia monosperma

Argyreia monosperma, commonly known as the holy night or moonflower, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces a single, round, purple fruit that contains a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
