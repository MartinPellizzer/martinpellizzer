Acer shirasawanum

Acer shirasawanum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its delicate, ornamental leaves that display vibrant colors during the growing season and in autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 meters, with a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters before the leaves emerge in early spring.

The species is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
