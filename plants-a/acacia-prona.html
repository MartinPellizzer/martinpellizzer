Acacia prona

Acacia prona, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its yellow flowers, which bloom in spring, attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and birds.

Acacia prona is also valued for its hardy nature and is often used in reforestation and erosion control efforts.
