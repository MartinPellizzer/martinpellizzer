Aster puniceus var. crawfordii

Aster puniceus var. crawfordii, commonly known as the Crawford's Purple Coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple to pink conical flower heads, which bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
