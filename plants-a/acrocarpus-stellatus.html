Acrocarpus stellatus

Acrocarpus stellatus, commonly known as the star-leaved ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and distinctive star-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its leaves are opposite and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and clustered.

The tree is valued for its durable timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
