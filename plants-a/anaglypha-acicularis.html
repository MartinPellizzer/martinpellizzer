Anaglypha acicularis

Anaglypha acicularis, commonly known as the needleleaf anaglypha, is a small evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in dense clusters along the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by tiny, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its compact size and unique foliage, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in specialized gardens.
