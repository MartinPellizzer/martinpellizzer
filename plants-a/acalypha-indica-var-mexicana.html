Acalypha indica var. mexicana

Acalypha indica var. mexicana, commonly known as Mexican flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers, giving it a dramatic visual appearance. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, adding to its aesthetic appeal.
