Acacia dimidiata var. eriostachya

Acacia dimidiata var. eriostachya, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by flat, linear pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid conditions.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
