Acacia pachyceras

Acacia pachyceras, commonly known as the broad-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for landscaping purposes.
