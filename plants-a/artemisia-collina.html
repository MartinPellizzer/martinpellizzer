Artemisia collina

Artemisia collina, commonly known as silver sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of narrow, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that appear in late spring to early summer. Artemisia collina is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
