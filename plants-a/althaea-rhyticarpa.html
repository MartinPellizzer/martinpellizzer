Althaea rhyticarpa

Althaea rhyticarpa, commonly known as the red rootflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish underside. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, which contribute to its common name.

Althaea rhyticarpa is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
