Arundinaria fischeri

Arundinaria fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's bamboo, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can form dense clumps, contributing to its ability to spread vegetatively.

This grass is often found in wetland habitats and is adapted to moist, acidic soils.
