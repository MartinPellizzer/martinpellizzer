Abronia sparsifolia

Abronia sparsifolia, commonly known as the coastal bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline, where it thrives in the salty, windy conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Abronia sparsifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.
