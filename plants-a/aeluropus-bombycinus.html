Aeluropus bombycinus

Aeluropus bombycinus, commonly known as the desert arrowgrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in sandy or saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Aeluropus bombycinus plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in its native habitats.
