Ardisia incarnata

Ardisia incarnata, commonly known as Christmas berry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the winter. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, and the berries are toxic to humans and livestock.

Ardisia incarnata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and seasonal color.
