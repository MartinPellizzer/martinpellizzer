Asparagus confusus

Asparagus confusus, commonly known as the confused asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Asparagus confusus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
