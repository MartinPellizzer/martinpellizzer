Anacamptis pyramidalis f. longispica

Anacamptis pyramidalis f. longispica, commonly known as the long-spurred orchid, is a hybrid orchid species characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

It features elongated, spurred petals that are typically a striking combination of purple and white, enhancing its ornamental value. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in calcareous soils, often found in open woodland and grassland habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of flowers, with each bloom displaying a unique blend of color and structure.

This orchid is prized for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance and ecological significance.
