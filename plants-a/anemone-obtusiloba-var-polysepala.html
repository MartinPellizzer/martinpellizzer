Anemone obtusiloba var. polysepala

Anemone obtusiloba var. polysepala, commonly known as the Pacific anemone or the western anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers with multiple sepals, giving it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. Its foliage consists of palmately divided, lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The botanical characteristics of this variety make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
