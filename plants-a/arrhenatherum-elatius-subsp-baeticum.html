Arrhenatherum elatius subsp. baeticum

Arrhenatherum elatius subsp.

baeticum, commonly known as the Baetican false oat, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous regions, often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The plant has slender, erect culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, two-lipped spikelets.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological significance in supporting local biodiversity and its role in traditional pastoral systems.
