Allium oliganthum var. elongatum

Allium oliganthum var. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated leek, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, with broad, flat leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Allium genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
