Acacia bussei

Acacia bussei, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and bipinnate leaves that are composed of numerous small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow to golden-orange flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Acacia bussei is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
