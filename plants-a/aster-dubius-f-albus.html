Aster dubius f. albus

Aster dubius f. albus, commonly known as the white aster, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
