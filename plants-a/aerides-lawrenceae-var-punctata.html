Aerides lawrenceae var. punctata

Aerides lawrenceae var. punctata, commonly known as the speckled orchid, is a hybrid orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, often fragrant flowers with distinctive speckled or spotted patterns on the petals. The plant typically produces clusters of showy, orchid-like blooms in shades of pink, white, and yellow. It thrives in warm, humid conditions with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively ease of cultivation.
