Arctium antonowii

Arctium antonowii, commonly known as the Antonow's burdock, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces spiky, bur-like fruits that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to the fur of animals or clothing. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are thick and often hairy.

Arctium antonowii is valued for its medicinal properties, with its roots traditionally used in herbal remedies.
