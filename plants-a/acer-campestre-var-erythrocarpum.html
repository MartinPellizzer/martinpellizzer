Acer campestre var. erythrocarpum

Acer campestre var. erythrocarpum, commonly known as the red-fruited field maple, is a variant of the field maple tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange-red berries, which contrast with its palmate leaves. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and open areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by its striking fruits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
