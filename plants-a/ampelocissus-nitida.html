Ampelocissus nitida

Ampelocissus nitida, commonly known as the silver-leafed ivy, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, black, berry-like fruits. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage.

Ampelocissus nitida is also valued for its ability to climb and cover trellises or walls effectively.
