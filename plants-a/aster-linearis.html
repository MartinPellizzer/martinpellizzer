Aster linearis

Aster linearis, commonly known as the linear aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It blooms in the summer months and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

Aster linearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
