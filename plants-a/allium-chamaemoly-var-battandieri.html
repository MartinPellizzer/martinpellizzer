Allium chamaemoly var. battandieri

Allium chamaemoly var. battandieri, commonly known as the purple onion.

This perennial herb is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and striking purple-pink flowers. It produces small, round bulbs that are similar to onions but are often used in ornamental gardening rather than culinary applications. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its botanical features include fibrous roots, cylindrical stems, and basal leaves that are typically blue-green in color.
