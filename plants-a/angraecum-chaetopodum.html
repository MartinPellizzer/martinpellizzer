Angraecum chaetopodum

Angraecum chaetopodum, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in areas with high humidity. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, with long, slender petals. It is known for its nocturnal blooming habit, with flowers opening at night to attract specific pollinators like moths.

Due to its unique characteristics and habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
