Adenia parviflora

Adenia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered adenia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in umbels, which are borne on the stems.

Adenia parviflora is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its foliage and unique floral structure.
