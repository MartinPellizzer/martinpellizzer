Abies omorika

Abies omorika, commonly known as the Serbian fir, is a species of fir tree native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dense foliage, and needle-like leaves that are dark green and slightly curved. The tree produces small, spherical cones that hang from the branches, and it thrives in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Abies omorika is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.

Its botanical features make it a unique and resilient member of the pine family.
