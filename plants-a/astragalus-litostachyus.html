Astragalus litostachyus

Astragalus litostachyus, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus litostachyus is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
