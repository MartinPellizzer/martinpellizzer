Acalypha decumbens var. villosa

Acalypha decumbens var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy ironweed, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features hairy, often reddish stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are typically inconspicuous and not showy. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental foliage and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
