Asplenium trichomanes subsp. coriaceifolium

Asplenium trichomanes subsp.

coriaceifolium, commonly known as the common rock fern, is a perennial fern native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in rocky, shaded environments, often on limestone or calcareous soils.

The plant has lance-shaped, leathery leaves that are dark green and covered in tiny, scale-like structures. Its fronds are usually unbranched and can reach up to 30 cm in length.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in crevices or on walls.
