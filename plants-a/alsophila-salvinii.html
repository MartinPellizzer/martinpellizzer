Alsophila salvinii

Alsophila salvinii, commonly known as the Salvin's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in favorable conditions. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting its upright structure.

Alsophila salvinii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to humid, shaded environments.
