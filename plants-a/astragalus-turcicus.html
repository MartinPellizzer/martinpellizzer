Astragalus turcicus

Astragalus turcicus, commonly known as Turkey pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus turcicus is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils.
