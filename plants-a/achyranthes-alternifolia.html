Achyranthes alternifolia

Achyranthes alternifolia, commonly known as the alternate leaf smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color, especially along the veins. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain one or two seeds.

Achyranthes alternifolia is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
