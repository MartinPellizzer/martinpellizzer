Acanthus longibracteatus

Acanthus longibracteatus, commonly known as the long-bract acanthus, is a striking evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, spiky inflorescences with small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its robust growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Acanthus longibracteatus is well-suited to full sun and well-drained soils, thriving in Mediterranean climates.
