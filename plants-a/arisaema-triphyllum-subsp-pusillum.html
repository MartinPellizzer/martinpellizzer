Arisaema triphyllum subsp. pusillum

Arisaema triphyllum subsp.

pusillum, commonly known as the dwarf Indian turnip, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features a distinctive, umbrella-like inflorescence with a spadix surrounded by a greenish-white spathe.

The plant has three narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the spadix.

This subspecies is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and swamps.
