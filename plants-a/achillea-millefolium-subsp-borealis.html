Achillea millefolium subsp. borealis

Achillea millefolium subsp.

borealis, commonly known as Arctic yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and flat, composite flower heads that range in color from white to pale yellow.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. It is often found in tundra and mountainous areas where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used by indigenous peoples for various ailments.
