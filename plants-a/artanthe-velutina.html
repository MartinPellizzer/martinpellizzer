Artanthe velutina

Artanthe velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf artanthe, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Artanthe velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
