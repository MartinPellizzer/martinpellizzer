Aegiphila wigandioides

Aegiphila wigandioides, commonly known as the Brazilian bush bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in sandy or well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
