Arundinella hubeiensis

Arundinella hubeiensis, commonly known as the Hubei bamboo, is a species of grass native to China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves with a smooth texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
