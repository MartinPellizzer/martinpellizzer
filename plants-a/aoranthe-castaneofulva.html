Aoranthe castaneofulva

Aoranthe castaneofulva, commonly known as the chestnut-yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, orange-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, thriving in humid and shaded environments. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

The orchid's striking coloration and unique floral structure make it a notable species in botanical studies.
