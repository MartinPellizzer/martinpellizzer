Avenella flexuosa subsp. iberica

Avenella flexuosa subsp.

iberica, commonly known as the Iberian bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically forms dense mats of foliage with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
