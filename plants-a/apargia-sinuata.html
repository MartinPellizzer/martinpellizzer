Apargia sinuata

Apargia sinuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has slender, often branched stems. Its leaves are pinnately divided with a distinctive sinuate margin, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers.

Apargia sinuata is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
