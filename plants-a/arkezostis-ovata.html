Arkezostis ovata

Arkezostis ovata, commonly known as the oval-leaf arkezostis, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
