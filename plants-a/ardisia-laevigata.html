Ardisia laevigata

Ardisia laevigata, commonly known as the smoothleaf beautyberry, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are smooth to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. These fruits are edible for humans and some bird species, playing a role in seed dispersal.

Ardisia laevigata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
