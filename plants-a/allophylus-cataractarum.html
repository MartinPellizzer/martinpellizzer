Allophylus cataractarum

Allophylus cataractarum, commonly known as the river hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically red or yellow, with five petals. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Allophylus cataractarum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.
