Ascolepis protea

Ascolepis protea, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy, silver-white bracts that enclose the actual flowers, which are small and clustered in a dense head. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, adapting well to the region's Mediterranean climate with wet winters and dry summers.

Ascolepis protea is not only ornamental but also plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
