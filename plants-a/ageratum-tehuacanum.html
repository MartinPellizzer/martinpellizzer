Ageratum tehuacanum

Ageratum tehuacanum, commonly known as the Mexican ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers. Ageratum tehuacanum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a bedding plant.
