Allium humile var. trifurcatum

Allium humile var. trifurcatum, commonly known as the three-lobed garlic or dwarf garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, clustered bulbs with pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and is known for its strong garlic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in some traditional cuisines.
