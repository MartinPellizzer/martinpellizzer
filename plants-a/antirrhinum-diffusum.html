Antirrhinum diffusum

Antirrhinum diffusum, commonly known as the spreading snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces vibrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, resembling the shape of a dragon's mouth, which gives it its common name. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and profuse blooming during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Antirrhinum diffusum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
