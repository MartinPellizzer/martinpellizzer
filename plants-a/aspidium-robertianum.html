Aspidium robertianum

Aspidium robertianum, commonly known as the African shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky outcrops. Its fronds are usually pinnate, with a dense arrangement of small, lance-shaped pinnae.

Aspidium robertianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
