Acanthopanax diversifolius

Acanthopanax diversifolius, commonly known as the variable-leaf Chinese angelica tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which vary in shape and size across different parts of the plant, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbellate clusters, followed by red berries that are a source of food for various wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
