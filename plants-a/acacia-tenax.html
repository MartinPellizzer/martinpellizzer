Acacia tenax

Acacia tenax, commonly known as the rigid acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are followed by woody pods containing seeds. Acacia tenax is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or rocky soils, making it well adapted to arid environments.

It is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and firewood.
