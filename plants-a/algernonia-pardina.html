Algernonia pardina

Algernonia pardina, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

Algernonia pardina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.
