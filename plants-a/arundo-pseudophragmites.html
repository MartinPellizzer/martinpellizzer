Arundo pseudophragmites

Arundo pseudophragmites, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the wetlands of the Mediterranean region.

It can grow up to 6 meters in height, with long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers during the summer months. It is highly adaptable, thriving in a variety of moist to wet soil conditions.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands make it both an important ecological species and a potential invasive plant in non-native habitats.
