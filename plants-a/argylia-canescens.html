Argylia canescens

Argylia canescens, commonly known as the grayleaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in dry, rocky soils. It is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
