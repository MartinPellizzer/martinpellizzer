Acacia cowellii

Acacia cowellii, commonly known as the Cowell's wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to its habitat. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened, leaf-like stems that reduce water loss in its arid environment. It is often found in sandy soils and can thrive in full sun.

Acacia cowellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
