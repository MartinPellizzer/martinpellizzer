Amaracarpus corymbosus

Amaracarpus corymbosus, commonly known as the clustered amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that produce small, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its seeds are encased in small, three-angled capsules, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

Amaracarpus corymbosus is adapted to warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.
