Aerangis kotschyana

Aerangis kotschyana, commonly known as the Ceylon orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the wet tropical forests of Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is adapted to the humid climate of its native habitat. Its flowers are often visited by various pollinators, including bees and moths.

Aerangis kotschyana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
