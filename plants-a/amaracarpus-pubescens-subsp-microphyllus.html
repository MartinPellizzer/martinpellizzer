Amaracarpus pubescens subsp. microphyllus

Amaracarpus pubescens subsp.

microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves that are densely covered with fine, soft hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions.

Its compact growth habit and unique foliage make it a distinctive addition to native plant gardens.
