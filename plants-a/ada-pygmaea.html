Ada pygmaea

Ada pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf adenium, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with smooth, pale green bark that often develops a reddish tint. The plant produces striking, umbrella-shaped clusters of yellow flowers during the rainy season. Its leaves are small, fleshy, and alternate along the stem, adapted for water storage.

Ada pygmaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
