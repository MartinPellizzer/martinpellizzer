Avenula sulcata var. reuteri

Avenula sulcata var. reuteri, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or marshy areas. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
