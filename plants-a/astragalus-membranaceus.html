Astragalus membranaceus

Astragalus membranaceus, commonly known as Huang Qi, is a perennial legume native to northern China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant is known for its deep taproot, which is often harvested for medicinal use. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional Chinese medicine and ecological restoration.
