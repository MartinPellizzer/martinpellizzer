Achyranthes pungens

Achyranthes pungens, commonly known as the spiny knotweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant features erect, branched stems that are often covered with stiff, spiny hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and typically have a rough texture.

The small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, and the plant produces dry, three-lobed fruits that contain one or two seeds.
