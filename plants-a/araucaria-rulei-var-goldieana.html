Araucaria rulei var. goldieana

Araucaria rulei var. goldieana, commonly known as the Goldie's Norfolk Island pine, is a rare and striking conifer native to Norfolk Island.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a dense, pyramidal crown of long, needle-like leaves. The plant exhibits a unique combination of evergreen foliage and a slow growth rate, making it a prized specimen in botanical gardens. Its leaves are typically a dark green, with a slight blue or silver sheen, and it produces small, spherical cones.

Due to its limited natural range and vulnerability to environmental changes, it is considered a conservation concern.
