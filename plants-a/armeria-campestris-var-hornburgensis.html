Armeria campestris var. hornburgensis

Armeria campestris var. hornburgensis, commonly known as the Hornburg's thrift, is a low-growing, perennial plant native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It features dense clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are linear and slightly succulent, adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance in its native habitat.
