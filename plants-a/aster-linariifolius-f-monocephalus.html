Aster linariifolius f. monocephalus

Aster linariifolius f. monocephalus, commonly known as the single-headed aster, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple and white. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, giving the plant a delicate appearance. This variety is notable for its unique monocephalous form, where a single flower head develops at the end of each stem.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
