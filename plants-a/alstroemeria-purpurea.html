Alstroemeria purpurea

Alstroemeria purpurea, commonly known as the Peruvian lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant has strap-like leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Alstroemeria purpurea is also known for its long-lasting blooms and is often used in cut flower arrangements.
