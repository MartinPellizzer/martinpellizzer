Abutilon bidentatum var. forrestii

Abutilon bidentatum var. forrestii, commonly known as the Forrester's velvetleaf, is a variant of the velvetleaf plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and soft, fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its fibrous stems and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
