Amyris fagaroides

Amyris fagaroides, commonly known as the swamp bay or fagropyrum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands and along streams. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, usually appearing in late spring.

Amyris fagaroides is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in moist, swampy environments.
