Apium graveolens var. laeve

Apium graveolens var. laeve, commonly known as celery, is a biennial or perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hollow, ridged stems and feathery, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are widely used in culinary applications for their distinctive flavor and aroma.

Celery thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
