Amorphophallus rayongii

Amorphophallus rayongii, commonly known as the Rayong arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by berries containing toxic seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
