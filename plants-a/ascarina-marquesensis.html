Ascarina marquesensis

Ascarina marquesensis, commonly known as the Marquesan fan palm, is a species of palm native to the islands of French Polynesia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources. Its reproductive structures include small, clustered inflorescences that produce both male and female flowers.

Ascarina marquesensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various local fauna.
