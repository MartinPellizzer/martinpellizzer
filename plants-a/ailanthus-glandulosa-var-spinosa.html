Ailanthus glandulosa var. spinosa

Ailanthus glandulosa var. spinosa, commonly known as the Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
