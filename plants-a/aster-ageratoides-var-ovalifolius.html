Aster ageratoides var. ovalifolius

Aster ageratoides var. ovalifolius, commonly known as the oval-leaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are oval-shaped, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
