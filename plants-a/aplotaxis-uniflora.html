Aplotaxis uniflora

Aplotaxis uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered milkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its latex is milky and toxic, containing cardiac glycosides that can be harmful to animals and humans.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.
