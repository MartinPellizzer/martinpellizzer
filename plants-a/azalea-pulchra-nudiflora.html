Azalea pulchra nudiflora

Azalea pulchra nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flowered azalea, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that belongs to the Rhododendron genus, characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red blooms in the spring before the leaves emerge, giving it its "nudiflora" designation. Its foliage consists of oval, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy.

This plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
