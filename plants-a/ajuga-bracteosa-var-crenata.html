Ajuga bracteosa var. crenata

Ajuga bracteosa var. crenata, commonly known as the smooth bugleweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with dark green, oval-shaped leaves that have slightly rounded edges. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ground cover in gardens.
