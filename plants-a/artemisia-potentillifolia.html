Artemisia potentillifolia

Artemisia potentillifolia, commonly known as silverleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for arid environments.

Its aromatic leaves and resilience make it a valuable plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
