Aspidium gleichenioides

Aspidium gleichenioides, commonly known as the Gleichenoid fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, and often creeping fronds that form dense, carpet-like growths. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Aspidium gleichenioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its lush, green appearance.
