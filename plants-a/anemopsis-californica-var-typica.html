Anemopsis californica var. typica

Anemopsis californica var. typica, commonly known as the California water shield, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in buoyancy and water retention. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in early summer, and it produces distinctive, club-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
