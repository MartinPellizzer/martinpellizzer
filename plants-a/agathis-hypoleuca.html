Agathis hypoleuca

Agathis hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-leafed ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araucariaceae and is characterized by its large, evergreen leaves and sturdy, aromatic wood. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and can reach heights of up to 40 meters. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a protective layer against pests and environmental stress.

The species is valued for its durable timber and is also studied for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
