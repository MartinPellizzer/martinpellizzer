Aerva lanata var. leucuroides

Aerva lanata var. leucuroides, commonly known as the white-flowered velvetleaf, is a variant of the Aerva lanata species native to parts of Asia.

It is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The leaves and flowers are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
