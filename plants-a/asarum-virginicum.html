Asarum virginicum

Asarum virginicum, commonly known as wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, reddish-brown leaves that are covered in a dense, hairy texture. The plant produces small, reddish flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic rhizomes have been historically used in traditional medicine.
