Adesmia affinis

Adesmia affinis, commonly known as the pink burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pink to purple flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind or animals. Adesmia affinis is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its growth habit is often shrub-like, with multiple stems and a sprawling or upright form depending on environmental conditions.
