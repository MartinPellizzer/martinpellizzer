Asplenium rhoifolium

Asplenium rhoifolium, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and its dark green, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in rocky or shaded environments, often found on limestone or serpentine soils. Its fronds are divided into small, serrated segments, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Asplenium rhoifolium is a perennial plant that thrives in humid, temperate climates.
