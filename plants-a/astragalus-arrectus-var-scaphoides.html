Astragalus arrectus var. scaphoides

Astragalus arrectus var. scaphoides, commonly known as the scaly milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in late summer. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and distinctive, inflated fruit pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
