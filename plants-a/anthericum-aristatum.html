Anthericum aristatum

Anthericum aristatum, commonly known as the tall lily-of-the-valley, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers with a strong fragrance. The plant's leaves are long, strap-like, and often have a reddish tinge at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
