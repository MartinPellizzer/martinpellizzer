Aloysia chilensis

Aloysia chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with woody stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Aloysia chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
