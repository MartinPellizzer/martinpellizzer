Amaracus cyrenaicus

Amaracus cyrenaicus, commonly known as the Cyrenaic amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the variety. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Amaracus cyrenaicus thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant suitable for various garden environments.
