Aira conferta

Aira conferta, commonly known as purple false oat, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense clusters of purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Aira conferta is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
