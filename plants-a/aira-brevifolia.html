Aira brevifolia

Aira brevifolia, commonly known as the shortleaf air grass, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and short, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Aira brevifolia is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.
