Abildgaardia hispidula subsp. macroglumis

Abildgaardia hispidula subsp.

macroglumis, commonly known as the broad-leaved abildgaardia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
