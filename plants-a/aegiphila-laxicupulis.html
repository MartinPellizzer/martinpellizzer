Aegiphila laxicupulis

Aegiphila laxicupulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aegiphila, is a shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
