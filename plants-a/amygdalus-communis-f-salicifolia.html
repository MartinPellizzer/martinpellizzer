Amygdalus communis f. salicifolia

Amygdalus communis f. salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf almond, is a botanical variant of the common almond.

It is characterized by its distinctive willow-like leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, which are typically pink or white. Its fruit is a drupe containing a single seed, similar to the common almond.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to temperate climates.
