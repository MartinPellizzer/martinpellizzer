Apocynum venetum

Apocynum venetum, commonly known as the Chinese dogwood, is a perennial shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its showy, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces toxic, milky sap containing cardiac glycosides, which can be harmful to humans and animals. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Apocynum venetum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
