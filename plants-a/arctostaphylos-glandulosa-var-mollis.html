Arctostaphylos glandulosa var. mollis

Arctostaphylos glandulosa var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-leaved manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in white or pink. The leaves are typically thick, leathery, and covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
