Adiantum tricholepis

Adiantum tricholepis, commonly known as the hairy maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Adiantaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often adorned with small, hairy structures along the veins. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce numerous upright, slender shoots.

The plant's unique morphology and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
