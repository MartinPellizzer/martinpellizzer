Astragalus ampullarioides

Astragalus ampullarioides, commonly known as the bladderpod milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus ampullarioides is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
