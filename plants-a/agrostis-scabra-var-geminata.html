Agrostis scabra var. geminata

Agrostis scabra var. geminata, commonly known as the hairy bentgrass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and hairy stems.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, lawns, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Its ability to thrive in a variety of conditions makes it a resilient and adaptable species.
