Alkanna trichophila

Alkanna trichophila, commonly known as the red sandalwood or Indian madder, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and South Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces a distinctive red or orange dye from its roots, which has been historically used in textiles and body paint. Alkanna trichophila thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

Its botanical significance lies in its medicinal properties and traditional use in dyeing practices.
