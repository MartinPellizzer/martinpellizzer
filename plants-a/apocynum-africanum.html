Apocynum africanum

Apocynum africanum, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many species with toxic properties. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. Its milky sap contains cardiac glycosides, which can be poisonous to humans and animals.

Apocynum africanum is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in sunny environments.
