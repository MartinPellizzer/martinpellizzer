Alchornea mollis

Alchornea mollis, commonly known as silky abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape and often have a velvety texture.

Alchornea mollis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
