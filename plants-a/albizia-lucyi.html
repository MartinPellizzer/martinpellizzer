Albizia lucyi

Albizia lucyi, commonly known as the silky albizia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are delicate and feathery in appearance, and its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Albizia lucyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its growth habit and floral display make it a popular choice for garden landscapes in warm climates.
