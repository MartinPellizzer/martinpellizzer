Acacia linearis var. tasmannica

Acacia linearis var. tasmannica, commonly known as the Tasmanian wattle, is a shrub native to Tasmania, characterized by its slender, linear phyllodes and yellow floral spikes.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, contributing to soil fertility. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, attract a variety of pollinators including bees and birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
