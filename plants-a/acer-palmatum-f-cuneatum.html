Acer palmatum f. cuneatum

Acer palmatum f. cuneatum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with cuneate leaves, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, wedge-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Its foliage provides year-round visual interest, with fresh green leaves in spring and summer.
