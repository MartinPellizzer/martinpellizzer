Adenophora petiolata subsp. petiolata

Adenophora petiolata subsp.

petiolata, commonly known as the long-stemmed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia. It features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the underside.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
