Aster bifrons

Aster bifrons, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are typically blue to purple in color.

Aster bifrons plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
