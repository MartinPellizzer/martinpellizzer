Acacia nebrownii

Acacia nebrownii, commonly known as the Nebrown's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellowish to pale green flowers in compact, globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are often borne at the ends of the branches. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The species is valued for its hardiness and is occasionally cultivated for ornamental purposes.
