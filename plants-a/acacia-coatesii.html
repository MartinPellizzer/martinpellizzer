Acacia coatesii

Acacia coatesii, commonly known as the Texas acacia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spherical clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. Acacia coatesii is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant for dry landscapes.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
