Ageratina chapalensis

Ageratina chapalensis, commonly known as the Chapalensis blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
