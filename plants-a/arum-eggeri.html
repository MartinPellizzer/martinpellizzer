Arum eggeri

Arum eggeri, commonly known as the Egger's arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly glossy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered around the spadix, often surrounded by a colorful, petal-like spathe.

Arum eggeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in moist, shady environments.
