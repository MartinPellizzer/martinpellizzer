Agonis longifolia

Agonis longifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed myrtle, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It typically grows in coastal areas and is often found in heathlands and dry forests.

Agonis longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
