Albizia welwitschii var. pedicellata

Albizia welwitschii var. pedicellata, commonly known as the African silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often bipinnate and give the plant a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Albizia welwitschii var. pedicellata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
