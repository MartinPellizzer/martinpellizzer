It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that are typically divided into lance-shaped segments. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Aspidium menisciicarpon plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
