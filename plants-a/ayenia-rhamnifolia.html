Ayenia rhamnifolia

Ayenia rhamnifolia, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, thriving in cool, moist climates. Its berries, which are toxic to humans, are an important food source for certain bird species.

Ayenia rhamnifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
