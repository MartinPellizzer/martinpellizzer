Allium chauvelii

Allium chauvelii, commonly known as the Chauvel's onion, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, white, onion-like bulbs underground. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic.

Allium chauvelii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
