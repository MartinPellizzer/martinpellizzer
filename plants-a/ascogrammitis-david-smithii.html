Ascogrammitis david-smithii

Ascogrammitis david-smithii, commonly known as the David-Smith's chain fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its intricate, cascading fronds that resemble a chain, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, thriving in the understory of dense forests. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in a unique pattern along the margins of its leaves.

Due to habitat loss, this species is considered endangered and is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
