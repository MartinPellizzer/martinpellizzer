Alyxia uniflora

Alyxia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in swampy areas. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a velvety texture.

Alyxia uniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
