Anemone demissa var. grandiflora

Anemone demissa var. grandiflora, commonly known as the showy anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers with a distinctive, fluffy center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are generally unbranched. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in garden settings.
