Aria collina

Aria collina, commonly known as the marsh woundwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Boraginaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, wetland habitats and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Aria collina is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
