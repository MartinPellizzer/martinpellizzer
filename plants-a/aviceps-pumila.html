Aviceps pumila

Aviceps pumila, commonly known as the dwarf lily of the valley, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, blooming in late spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, dark berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Aviceps pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
