Aster laxus

Aster laxus, commonly known as the slender aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Aster laxus is often used in wildflower gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
