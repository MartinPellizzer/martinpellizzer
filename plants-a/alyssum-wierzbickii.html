Alyssum wierzbickii

Alyssum wierzbickii, commonly known as the Polish alyssum, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Alyssum wierzbickii is often grown as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
