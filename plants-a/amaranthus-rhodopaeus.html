Amaranthus rhodopaeus

Amaranthus rhodopaeus, commonly known as the redroot amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking red to purple stems, which become more pronounced as the plant matures. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. Its small, clustered flowers bloom in shades of pink to red, adding visual interest to the plant.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
