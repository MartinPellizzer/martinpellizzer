Amberboa micractis

Amberboa micractis, commonly known as the golden sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with fleshy, ribbed stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in the spring. It is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils with minimal water requirements.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it an interesting subject for botanical study in xerophytic ecosystems.
