Aristida hermannii

Aristida hermannii, commonly known as Hermann's threeawn, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and rocky slopes. The plant is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences composed of three-awned spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Hermann's threeawn is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
