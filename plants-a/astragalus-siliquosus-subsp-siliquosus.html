Astragalus siliquosus subsp. siliquosus

Astragalus siliquosus subsp.

siliquosus, commonly known as the silique astragal, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits called silique.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
