Astrotricha latifolia

Astrotricha latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf starwort, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a rough texture, and its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in a composite head.

Astrotricha latifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can serve as an indicator of wetland or riparian habitats.
