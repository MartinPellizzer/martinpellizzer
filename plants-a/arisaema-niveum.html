Arisaema niveum

Arisaema niveum, commonly known as the white arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and a spadix surrounded by a white, waxy spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swamps. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces berries that are edible when ripe.

Arisaema niveum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance.
