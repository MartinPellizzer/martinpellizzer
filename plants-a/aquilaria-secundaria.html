Aquilaria secundaria

Aquilaria secundaria, commonly known as the lesser agarwood tree, is a member of the Thymelaeaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic resinous wood, which is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

The tree is often found in lowland rainforests and requires a humid, warm climate to thrive.
