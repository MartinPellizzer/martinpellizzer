Amaranthus cruentus var. albus

Amaranthus cruentus var. albus, commonly known as white amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and feather-like leaves that are typically green or bluish-green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by fluffy, seed-filled capsules. White amaranth is native to the Americas and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of grain.

It is also valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of protein and minerals.
