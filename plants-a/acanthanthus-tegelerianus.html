Acanthanthus tegelerianus

Acanthanthus tegelerianus, commonly known as the Tegeler's acanthanthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of yellow to orange ray florets.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid conditions.
