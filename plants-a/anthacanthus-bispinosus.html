Anthacanthus bispinosus

Anthacanthus bispinosus, commonly known as the two-spined anthacanthus, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a dry, two-lobed schizocarp, typical of the carrot family.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
