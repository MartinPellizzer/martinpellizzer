Acaena pinnatifida var. eucantha

Acaena pinnatifida var. eucantha, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, spiny leaves with a pinnate arrangement, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a mild, tangy flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Acaena pinnatifida var. eucantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
