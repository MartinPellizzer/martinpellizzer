Agave americana subsp. protamericana

Agave americana subsp.

protamericana, commonly known as the blue agave, is a succulent plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its thick, blue-green leaves that are often lined with sharp spines.

The plant produces an inflorescence of yellow flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This subspecies is particularly notable for its use in the production of tequila and other agave-based spirits.
