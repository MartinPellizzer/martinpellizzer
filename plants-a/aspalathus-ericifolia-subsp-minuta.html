Aspalathus ericifolia subsp. minuta

Aspalathus ericifolia subsp.

minuta, commonly known as the dwarf silver bush, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and compact growth habit.

The plant produces tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological and ornamental purposes.
