Asplenium cirrhatum

Asplenium cirrhatum, commonly known as the cirrhatum spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has linear, often recurved leaves that are densely packed, giving it a compact appearance. Its spore-producing structures, called sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium cirrhatum is characterized by its delicate, intricate foliage and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
