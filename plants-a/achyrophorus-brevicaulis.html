Achyrophorus brevicaulis

Achyrophorus brevicaulis, commonly known as the dwarf lousewort, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments. The plant has a compact growth habit with short stems and small, reddish or greenish leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Achyrophorus brevicaulis is adapted to cold climates and has a slow growth rate, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
