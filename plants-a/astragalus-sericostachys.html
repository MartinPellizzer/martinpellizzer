Astragalus sericostachys

Astragalus sericostachys, commonly known as silky pod milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, silky-haired pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
