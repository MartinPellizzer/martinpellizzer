Achnatherum parviflorum

Achnatherum parviflorum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like flower clusters. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, and it is often found in high-altitude environments.

Achnatherum parviflorum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
