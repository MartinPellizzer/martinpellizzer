Aralia frodiniana

Aralia frodiniana, commonly known as the Fodin's aralia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring compound leaves with multiple leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbel-like clusters, which are followed by berries that turn red when mature. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained, fertile soils.

Aralia frodiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
