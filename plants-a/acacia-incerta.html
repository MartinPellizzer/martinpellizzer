Acacia incerta

Acacia incerta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and narrow, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Acacia incerta is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
