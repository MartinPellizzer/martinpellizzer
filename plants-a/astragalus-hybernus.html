Astragalus hybernus

Astragalus hybernus, commonly known as the winter milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system is deep and fibrous. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Astragalus hybernus is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
