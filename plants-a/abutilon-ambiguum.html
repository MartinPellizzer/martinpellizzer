Abutilon ambiguum

Abutilon ambiguum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its fibrous stems and seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
