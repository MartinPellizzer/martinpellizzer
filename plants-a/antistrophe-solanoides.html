Antistrophe solanoides

Antistrophe solanoides, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellowish berry that ripens in late summer.

Antistrophe solanoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
