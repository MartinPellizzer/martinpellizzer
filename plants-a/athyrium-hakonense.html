Athyrium hakonense

Athyrium hakonense, commonly known as the Hakon's sword fern, is a species of fern native to the forests of Japan.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its elegant, lance-shaped fronds that are typically divided into pinnae. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions with rich, well-drained soil. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction via spores rather than seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
