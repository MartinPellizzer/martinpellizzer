Amorphophallus longispathaceus

Amorphophallus longispathaceus, commonly known as the long-spurred amorphophallus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces large, ornamental leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. Its flowers are typically unisexual, with separate male and female parts.

The species is valued for its striking foliage and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
