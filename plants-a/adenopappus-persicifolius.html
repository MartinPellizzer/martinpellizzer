Adenopappus persicifolius

Adenopappus persicifolius, commonly known as the Persian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its invasive potential in some areas, it is considered a weed in certain parts of the world.
