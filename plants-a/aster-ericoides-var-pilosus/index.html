Aster Ericoides Var. Pilosus

Purple coneflower, scientifically known as Aster ericoides var. pilosus, is a striking native wildflower found in the eastern United States. With its vibrant purple petals and distinctive cone-shaped center, it stands out in meadows and woodland edges during the summer months. This plant is not only visually appealing but also plays a vital role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its resilience and adaptability make it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively overlooked compared to more common garden flowers.
