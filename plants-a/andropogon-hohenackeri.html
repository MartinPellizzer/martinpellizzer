Andropogon hohenackeri

Andropogon hohenackeri, commonly known as Hohenacker's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes many grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is a unique feature among grasses.

Andropogon hohenackeri is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
