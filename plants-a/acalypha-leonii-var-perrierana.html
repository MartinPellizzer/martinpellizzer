Acalypha leonii var. perrierana

Acalypha leonii var. perrierana, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a strikingly ornamental shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vivid, red-veined leaves that contrast sharply with its dark green foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden settings.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
