Aspidosperma rawolfioides

Aspidosperma rawolfioides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Cerbera odollam*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and dense, glossy green foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that give way to bright red, fleshy berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.

However, its berries are toxic and should be handled with care.
