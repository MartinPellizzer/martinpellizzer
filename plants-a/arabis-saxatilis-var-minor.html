Arabis saxatilis var. minor

Arabis saxatilis var. minor, commonly known as rock garlic or mountain rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that is well-suited for rock gardens and alpine settings.
