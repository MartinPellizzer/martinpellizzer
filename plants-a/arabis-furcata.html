Arabis furcata

Arabis furcata, commonly known as the forked rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with characteristic four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are basal and pinnately lobed, while the flowers are small and white or pale yellow, often arranged in clusters.

Arabis furcata is often found in meadows, cliffs, and scree slopes, and it is known for its hardy nature in harsh environments.
