Anchusa officinalis subsp. leucantha

Anchusa officinalis subsp.

leucantha, commonly known as white anchusa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features erect, branching stems covered in soft, woolly hairs, and its leaves are lanceolate with a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pale blue, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
