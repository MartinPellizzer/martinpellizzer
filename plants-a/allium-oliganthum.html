Allium oliganthum

Allium oliganthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic.

It is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly found in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale purple flowers in clusters. Its bulbs are similar to garlic in appearance but are less commonly cultivated for culinary use.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
