Amygdalus communis var. rubra

Amygdalus communis var. rubra, commonly known as the red almond tree, is a variety of the Prunus genus native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive red flowers that bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its edible yield.
