Acaciella filicioides

Acaciella filicioides, commonly known as the fern-like acacia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are compound, with each leaflet being narrow and elongated, resembling the structure of a fern.

Acaciella filicioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
