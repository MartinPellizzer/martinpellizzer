Atriplex quadrivalvata

Atriplex quadrivalvata, commonly known as Four-Valved Saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it a valuable species for stabilizing soils in saline areas.

Atriplex quadrivalvata plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and preventing erosion.
