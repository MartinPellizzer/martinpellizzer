Aster wattii

Aster wattii, commonly known as the Watt's aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool temperatures. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Aster wattii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
