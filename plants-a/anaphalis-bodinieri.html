Anaphalis bodinieri

Anaphalis bodinieri, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its woolly, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flower heads that resemble daisies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges.

Anaphalis bodinieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
