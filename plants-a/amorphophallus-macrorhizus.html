Amorphophallus macrorhizus

Amorphophallus macrorhizus, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a tuberous root system that stores nutrients. The plant is part of the Araceae family, which includes other plants like calla lilies and peace lilies. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
