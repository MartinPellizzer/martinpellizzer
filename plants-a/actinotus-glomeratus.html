Actinotus glomeratus

Actinotus glomeratus, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically pink or purple and are borne in dense, spherical inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
