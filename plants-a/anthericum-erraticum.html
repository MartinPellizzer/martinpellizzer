Anthericum erraticum

Anthericum erraticum, commonly known as the wandering arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are broad, arrow-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Anthericum erraticum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
