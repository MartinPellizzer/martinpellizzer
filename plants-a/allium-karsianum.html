Allium karsianum

Allium karsianum, commonly known as the Karsianum onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous root system and strap-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has mild culinary uses in some regions.

Allium karsianum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
