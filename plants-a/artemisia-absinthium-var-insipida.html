Artemisia absinthium var. insipida

Artemisia absinthium var. insipida, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray foliage and a distinctive, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been historically used in herbal medicine and as a component in absinthe.
