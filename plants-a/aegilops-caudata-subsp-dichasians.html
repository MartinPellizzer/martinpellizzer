Aegilops caudata subsp. dichasians

Aegilops caudata subsp.

dichasians, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with a silvery sheen.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and used for soil stabilization.
