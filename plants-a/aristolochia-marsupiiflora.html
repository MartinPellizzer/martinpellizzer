Aristolochia marsupiiflora

Aristolochia marsupiiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian pipevine, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, intricate, pipe-like flowers that are often greenish-brown in color. The plant produces small, blue to violet berries that contain the seeds. It is part of the Aristolochiaceae family, known for its complex chemical compounds, some of which are used in traditional medicine.

However, it is important to note that some species within this genus are toxic and should be handled with care.
