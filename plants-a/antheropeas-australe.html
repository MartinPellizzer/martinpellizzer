Antheropeas australe

Antheropeas australe, commonly known as the southern sandhill grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Antheropeas australe plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
