Acaciella angustissima var. texensis

Acaciella angustissima var. texensis, commonly known as the Texas acacia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces yellowish to golden flowers arranged in compact clusters, which are pollinated by insects. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is well-suited to sandy or rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
