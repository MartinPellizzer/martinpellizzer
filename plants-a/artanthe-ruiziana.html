Artanthe ruiziana

Artanthe ruiziana, commonly known as the bluebells of the Andes, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Artanthe ruiziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
