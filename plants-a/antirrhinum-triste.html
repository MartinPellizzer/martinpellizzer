Antirrhinum triste

Antirrhinum triste, commonly known as the weeping snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its drooping, pale yellow or orange flowers that resemble small, delicate snapdragons. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and a compact growth habit.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
