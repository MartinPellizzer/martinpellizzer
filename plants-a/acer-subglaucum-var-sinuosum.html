Acer subglaucum var. sinuosum

Acer subglaucum var. sinuosum, commonly known as the Sinuous maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
