Anguloa dubia

Anguloa dubia, commonly known as the silver bell orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, bell-shaped flowers that are often tinged with white and purple hues. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its striking appearance and limited natural habitat, Anguloa dubia is considered a rare and sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
