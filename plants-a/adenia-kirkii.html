Adenia kirkii

Adenia kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's adenium, is a striking succulent plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the rainy season, adding a dramatic visual element to its appearance. Its leaves are typically oval and glossy, providing a lush contrast to its woody trunk.

Adenia kirkii is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its unique and exotic appearance.
