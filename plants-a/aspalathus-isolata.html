Aspalathus isolata

Aspalathus isolata, commonly known as the mountain pea, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

Aspalathus isolata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
