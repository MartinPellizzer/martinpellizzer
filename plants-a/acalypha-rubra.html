Acalypha rubra

Acalypha rubra, commonly known as red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom beneath the bracts, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and are often covered in fine hairs.

Acalypha rubra thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its vibrant color and ornamental appeal.
