Adhatoda tranquebariensis

Adhatoda tranquebariensis, commonly known as the Indian Trumpet or Blue Trumpet, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of India and Sri Lanka.

It is a woody shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and striking blue-violet flowers. The plant produces small, cylindrical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical gardens and traditional herbal medicine.
