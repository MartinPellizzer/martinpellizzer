Acaena argentea f. nigricans

Acaena argentea f. nigricans, commonly known as the dark silver burr, is a variant of the silver burr plant native to New Zealand.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub characterized by its silver-gray foliage and dense, spiny fruiting structures. The plant thrives in coastal and subantarctic regions, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in early spring, while the fruits develop into prickly, three-lobed burrs.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and provides habitat for native invertebrates.
