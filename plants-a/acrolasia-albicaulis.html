Acrolasia albicaulis

Acrolasia albicaulis, commonly known as the white-stemmed acrolasia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pale green stems that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Acrolasia albicaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
