Aucuba

Aucuba, commonly known as Japanese laurel, is a genus of evergreen shrubs native to East Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. Aucuba is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in shade.

It is commonly used in landscaping for its compact growth and year-round visual appeal.
