Aechmea egleriana

Aechmea egleriana, commonly known as the Egler's pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater for the plant's use. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences that can range in color from red to orange, often resembling a pineapple. Its foliage is typically dark green with a waxy texture, and it thrives in bright, indirect light.

Aechmea egleriana is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea egleriana has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, strap-like, and dark green with a waxy coating, arranged in a tight spiral at the base of the plant.

The stem is short and upright, with a smooth, grayish-brown bark that remains relatively hidden beneath the dense foliage. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by colorful bracts, producing white to yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months and are pollinated by insects. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are dispersed by birds after ripening, and the seeds are enclosed within the fruit.

The roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil, supporting the plant's epiphytic growth habit.
