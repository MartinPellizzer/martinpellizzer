Astranthium integrifolium var. rosulatum

Astranthium integrifolium var. rosulatum, commonly known as the clustered dropwort, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit.

It features basal leaves that are typically lanceolate or ovate with smooth margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are borne on short pedicels. It is native to the western United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

The species is notable for its compact, ornamental appearance and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
