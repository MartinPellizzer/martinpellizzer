Abelia spathulata var. tetrasepala

Abelia spathulata var. tetrasepala, commonly known as the four-lobed abelia, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has a compact, rounded form. Its flowers have four sepals, which is a distinguishing feature of this variety.

It prefers partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
