Adesmia humilis

Adesmia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf adesmia, is a low-growing shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features small, compound leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Adesmia humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
