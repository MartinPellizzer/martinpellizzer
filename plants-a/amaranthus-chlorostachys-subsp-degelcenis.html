Amaranthus chlorostachys subsp. degelcenis

Amaranthus chlorostachys subsp.

degelcenis, commonly known as the silverleaf amaranth, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique leaf coloration and drought tolerance contribute to its aesthetic and ecological value.
