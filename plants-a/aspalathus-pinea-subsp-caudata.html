Aspalathus pinea subsp. caudata

Aspalathus pinea subsp.

caudata, commonly known as the cape tulip, is a South African plant species belonging to the Fabaceae family. It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often fragrant.

The plant is typically found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The cape tulip is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
