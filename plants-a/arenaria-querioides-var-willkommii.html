Arenaria querioides var. willkommii

Arenaria querioides var. willkommii, commonly known as the silverleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has succulent, silver-gray leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact form and delicate floral display.
