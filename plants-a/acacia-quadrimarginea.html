Acacia quadrimarginea

Acacia quadrimarginea, commonly known as the African silver acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and small, fern-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The bark and leaves of Acacia quadrimarginea have been used traditionally for various purposes, including medicinal applications.
