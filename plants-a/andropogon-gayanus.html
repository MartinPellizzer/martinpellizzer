Andropogon gayanus

Andropogon gayanus, commonly known as bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or brown.

Andropogon gayanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
