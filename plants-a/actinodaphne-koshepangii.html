Actinodaphne koshepangii

Actinodaphne koshepangii, commonly known as the Koshepangii camphor tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Myanmar and Thailand.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and aromatic. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale yellow, and it produces drupes that are used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood and potential medicinal properties, though it remains a relatively understudied species.
