Andropogon sorghum var. usaramensis

Andropogon sorghum var. usaramensis, commonly known as Usaramensis sorghum, is a grass species native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soils.

Usaramensis sorghum is often cultivated for forage and has potential for use in biofuel production.
