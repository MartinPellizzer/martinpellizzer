Astragalus dusenii

Astragalus dusenii, commonly known as Dusen's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has adapted to arid conditions with its deep root system. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Astragalus dusenii is often found in alpine and subalpine zones, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
