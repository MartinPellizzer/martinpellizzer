Agathis latifolia

Agathis latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araucariaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and sturdy, aromatic wood. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 40 meters. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, while its fruit is a woody capsule containing numerous seeds.

The tree is valued for its durable timber and has cultural significance in some indigenous communities.
