Acacia obovata var. minor

Acacia obovata var. minor, commonly known as the small-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
