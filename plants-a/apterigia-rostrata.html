Apterigia rostrata

Apterigia rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.
