Albizia myriophylla var. foliolosa

Albizia myriophylla var. foliolosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features pinnately compound leaves with narrow, elongated leaflets that give it a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant, cream-colored flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in spring. Its wood is light and soft, making it useful for carving and small crafts.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
