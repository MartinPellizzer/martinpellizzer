Asperula stricta var. longifolia

Asperula stricta var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved sweet pepper, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are elongated with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically in spring. Its foliage is evergreen, providing ornamental value throughout the year.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
