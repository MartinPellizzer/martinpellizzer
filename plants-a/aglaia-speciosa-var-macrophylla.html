Aglaia speciosa var. macrophylla

Aglaia speciosa var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf aglaia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, often followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
