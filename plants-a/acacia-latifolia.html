Acacia latifolia

Acacia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, narrow leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and rocky habitats.

Acacia latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
