Aechmea lamarchei

Aechmea lamarchei, commonly known as the silver pine or silver rosette, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact rosette of long, narrow, silver-green leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant's foliage is often covered in a fine, waxy coating that gives it its distinctive appearance. It produces striking inflorescences with bright red flowers that bloom in the spring.

Aechmea lamarchei is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique look and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea lamarchei has rosette-shaped leaves that are stiff, grass-like, and arranged in a compact cluster, with a dark green color and a waxy texture.

The plant lacks a prominent stem, with the base of the leaves forming a dense, compact rosette. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, typically in shades of red or orange, and bloom during the dry season, pollinated by insects such as beetles and flies. The fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that disperses seeds through wind and animal ingestion.

The roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture efficiently from the surrounding soil.
