Acanthopanax evodiifolius var. pseudoevodiifolius

Acanthopanax evodiifolius var. pseudoevodiifolius, commonly known as the Korean ginseng vine, is a climbing shrub native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces berries that are typically red or black when mature. It is often found in forested areas and is valued for its medicinal properties.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in traditional herbal medicine.
