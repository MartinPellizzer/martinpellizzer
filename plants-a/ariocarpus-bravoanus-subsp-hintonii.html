Ariocarpus bravoanus subsp. hintonii

Ariocarpus bravoanus subsp.

hintonii, commonly known as the Hinton's prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to the rocky soils of northern Mexico. It features a short, cylindrical stem with a smooth, greenish-gray surface and spiny areoles.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
