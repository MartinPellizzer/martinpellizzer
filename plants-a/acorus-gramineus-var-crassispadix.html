Acorus gramineus var. crassispadix

Acorus gramineus var. crassispadix, commonly known as the broad-leaved sweetflag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically broad at the base and taper toward the tip. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike, which are followed by triangular, boat-shaped fruits. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a water purifier.
