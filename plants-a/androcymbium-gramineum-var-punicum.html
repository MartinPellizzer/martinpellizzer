Androcymbium gramineum var. punicum

Androcymbium gramineum var. punicum, commonly known as the red-stemmed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often display a reddish hue, especially when mature. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated.

This variant is often found in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
