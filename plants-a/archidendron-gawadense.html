Archidendron gawadense

Archidendron gawadense, commonly known as the Philippine trumpet tree, is a leguminous plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its ecological benefits in local ecosystems.
