Acacia mbuluensis

Acacia mbuluensis, commonly known as the Congo acacia, is a deciduous tree native to Central Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched trunk and bipinnate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, globular inflorescences, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks.

Acacia mbuluensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
