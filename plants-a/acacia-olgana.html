Acacia olgana

Acacia olgana, commonly known as the white wattle, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and small, rounded phyllodes that are typically pale green to white in color. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Acacia olgana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various native animals.
