Actinidia trichogyna

Actinidia trichogyna, commonly known as the fuzzy Actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that ripen to a bright red color in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The species is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
