Agasta indica

Agasta indica, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, acidic fruits that are rich in vitamin C. The plant has simple, alternate leaves and produces small white or greenish flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and nutritional value.

The botanical features of Agasta indica make it an important plant in traditional medicine and agriculture.
