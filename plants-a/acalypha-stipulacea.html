Acalypha stipulacea

Acalypha stipulacea, commonly known as the velvetleaf or sacred plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal.

Acalypha stipulacea is often found in disturbed habitats and is adaptable to various soil types.
