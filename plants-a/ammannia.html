Ammannia

Ammannia, commonly known as watergrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond margins, and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plants have small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spikes, and they produce tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Ammannia species are often found in tropical and subtropical regions, and some are considered invasive in certain areas.
