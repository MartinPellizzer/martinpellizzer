Aster intermedius

Aster intermedius, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, lavender-blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in a compact, bushy form. It thrives in partial to full sunlight and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Aster intermedius is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
