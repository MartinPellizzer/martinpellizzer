Argyrodendron peralatum

Argyrodendron peralatum, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
