Astrantia major subsp. biebersteinii

Astrantia major subsp.

biebersteinii, commonly known as the Siberian aster, is a perennial herb native to Eastern Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, lavender or white flowers.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
