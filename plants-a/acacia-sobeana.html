Acacia sobeana

Acacia sobeana, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are densely arranged. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular inflorescences, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia sobeana is often used in traditional medicine and for its ornamental value.
