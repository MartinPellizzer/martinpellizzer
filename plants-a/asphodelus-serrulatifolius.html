Asphodelus serrulatifolius

Asphodelus serrulatifolius, commonly known as the serrated-leaf asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, sunny conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with serrated edges, and it produces tall, slender flower stalks bearing clusters of yellow or orange flowers. It is often found in Mediterranean climates and is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
