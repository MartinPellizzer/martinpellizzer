Ardisia laurifolia

Ardisia laurifolia, commonly known as the glossy laurel, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are arranged in whorls along its stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments with moist, well-drained soil.

Ardisia laurifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and seasonal floral display.
