Alepidea basinuda

Alepidea basinuda, commonly known as the narrow-leafed karkalla, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, narrow leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Alepidea basinuda is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
