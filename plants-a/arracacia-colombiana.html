Arracacia colombiana

Arracacia colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and striking white flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its starchy rhizomes, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its growth habit includes a short stem with broad, glossy foliage that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
