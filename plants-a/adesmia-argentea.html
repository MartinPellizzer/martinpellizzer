Adesmia argentea

Adesmia argentea, commonly known as silverleaf adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, giving it its common name. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Adesmia argentea is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
