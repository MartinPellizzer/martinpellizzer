Ajuga

Ajuga, commonly known as bugleweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Plantaginaceae.

It consists of about 35 species, most of which are low-growing, mat-forming perennials. These plants typically have upright, tubular flowers that are often blue, purple, or white in color. Ajuga species are native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

They are valued in landscaping for their ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
