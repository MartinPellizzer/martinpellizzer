Acacia microbotrya

Acacia microbotrya, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its bark is smooth and greyish, often flaking in thin layers.

Acacia microbotrya is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.
