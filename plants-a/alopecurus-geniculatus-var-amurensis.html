Alopecurus geniculatus var. amurensis

Alopecurus geniculatus var. amurensis, commonly known as the Amur bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.

This variant is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
