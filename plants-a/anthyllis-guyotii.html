Anthyllis guyotii

Anthyllis guyotii, commonly known as the Guyot's anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

Anthyllis guyotii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
