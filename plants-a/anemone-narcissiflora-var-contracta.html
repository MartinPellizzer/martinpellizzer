Anemone narcissiflora var. contracta

Anemone narcissiflora var. contracta, commonly known as the compact anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, single-layered flowers that range in color from white to pink, resembling the petals of a daffodil. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches, with dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a touch of elegance to shaded landscapes.
