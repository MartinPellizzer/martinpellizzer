Adamanthus valerioi

Adamanthus valerioi, commonly known as the Brazilian palm, is a tropical plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a small, tree-like palm with a slender trunk and feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, humid climates.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is often found in conservation programs.
