Aegilops exaltata

Aegilops exaltata, commonly known as the slender wild rye, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. Aegilops exaltata is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.

It has been studied for its potential in agricultural improvement due to its genetic relationship with wheat.

Its flowers are spike-like inflorescences with small, pale green to white florets, blooming in early summer, and are pollinated by wind.
