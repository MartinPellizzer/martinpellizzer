Anonis pusilla

Anonis pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf sugar apple, is a small shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family, which includes a variety of tropical plants. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often clustered in umbels, and it produces a round, yellow or orange fruit.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet, juicy pulp that contains numerous small, hard seeds.
