Althaea lavateriflora

Althaea lavateriflora, commonly known as the purple marshmallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and marshy areas.

Althaea lavateriflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
