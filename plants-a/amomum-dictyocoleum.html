Amomum dictyocoleum

Amomum dictyocoleum, commonly known as the fragrant cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like India, Indonesia, and Malaysia. The plant features aromatic, finger-like rhizomes that are harvested for their essential oils and medicinal properties. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

This plant is valued both for its culinary uses and traditional herbal applications.
