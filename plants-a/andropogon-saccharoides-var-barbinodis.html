Andropogon saccharoides var. barbinodis

Andropogon saccharoides var. barbinodis, commonly known as the barbed nodding grass, is a variant of the saccharoides grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect culms. The inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that have distinctive barbed bristles, which aid in seed dispersal. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, often with a bluish-green coloration.

This plant thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.
