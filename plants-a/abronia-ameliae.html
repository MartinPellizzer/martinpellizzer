Abronia ameliae

Abronia ameliae, commonly known as the Amelia's bladderwort, is a rare aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Florida.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap microscopic organisms, aiding in its nutrient absorption. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats with soft, muddy substrates. Its leaves are thin and ribbon-like, adapted for efficient water movement.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
