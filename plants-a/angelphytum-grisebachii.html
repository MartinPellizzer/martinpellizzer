Angelphytum grisebachii

Angelphytum grisebachii, commonly known as the gray angel's feather, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale greenish-gray leaves. The plant produces fluffy, white to pale pink flower heads that resemble a cluster of feathers, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Angelphytum grisebachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
