Agave americana var. latifolia

Agave americana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its wide, fleshy, bluish-green leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with yellow flowers, typically blooming once in its lifetime. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.
