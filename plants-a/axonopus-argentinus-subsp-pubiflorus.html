Axonopus argentinus subsp. pubiflorus

Axonopus argentinus subsp.

pubiflorus, commonly known as the Argentine axonopus, is a flowering plant native to South America. It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, compact foliage.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
