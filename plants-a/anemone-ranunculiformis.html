Anemone ranunculiformis

Anemone ranunculiformis, commonly known as the meadow anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that resemble small, delicate anemones, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are deeply lobed and heart-shaped, with a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along streams.

Anemone ranunculiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
