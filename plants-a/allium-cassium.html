Allium cassium

Allium cassium, commonly known as the purple onion or wild leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of strap-shaped, gray-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbous underground stem, or corm, is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor.

Allium cassium is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.
