Acer platanoides f. laetum

Acer platanoides f. laetum, commonly known as the white maple, is a cultivar of the Norway maple.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant is a deciduous tree with a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic foliage and adaptability.
