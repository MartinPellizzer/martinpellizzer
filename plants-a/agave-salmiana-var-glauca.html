Agave salmiana var. glauca

Agave salmiana var. glauca, commonly known as the blue agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing small, white flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical significance in the production of tequila.
