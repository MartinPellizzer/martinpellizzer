Allosorus pectiniformis

Allosorus pectiniformis, commonly known as the feather moss, is a small, mat-forming evergreen moss that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching structure and vibrant green foliage that resembles the texture of feathers. This plant plays a crucial role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in forest ecosystems. Allosorus pectiniformis is typically found in temperate regions, often growing on the forest floor or on rocks.

Its simple yet elegant morphology makes it a popular subject in botanical studies and ecological research.
