Alysicarpus heyneanus var. ludens

Alysicarpus heyneanus var. ludens, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a tropical legume native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in warm climates and is often grown as a forage crop or for its nutritional value.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and suitable for both agricultural and ecological purposes.
