Arthraxon antsirabensis

Arthraxon antsirabensis, commonly known as the Madagascar grass.

It is a species of grass native to Madagascar, characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Arthraxon antsirabensis is often used in local traditional practices and has ecological significance in its native environment.
