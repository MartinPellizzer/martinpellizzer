Arnebia guttata subsp. guttata

Arnebia guttata subsp.

guttata, commonly known as the spotted rock-centre, is a member of the Geraniaceae family. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and basal leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.

The subspecies is notable for its distinctive red spots on the leaves and its ability to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
