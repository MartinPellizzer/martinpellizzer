Amomum tomrey var. stenophyllum

Amomum tomrey var. stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cardamom, is a tropical plant belonging to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, elongated leaves that give it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, and the plant produces aromatic, reddish-brown fruits containing aromatic seeds.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
