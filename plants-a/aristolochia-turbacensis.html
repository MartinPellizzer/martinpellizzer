Aristolochia turbacensis

Aristolochia turbacensis, commonly known as the Turban Aristolochia, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, complex, and often aromatic flowers that resemble a spiral or a turban, hence its common name. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and emits a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its unique floral structures and is part of the Aristolochiaceae family.
