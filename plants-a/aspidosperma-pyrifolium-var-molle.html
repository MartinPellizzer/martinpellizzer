Aspidosperma pyrifolium var. molle

Aspidosperma pyrifolium var. molle, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in pendulous inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries containing a single seed. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

The plant contains alkaloids and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
