Allium alpicola

Allium alpicola, commonly known as the alpine garlic, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces small, white or pale purple flowers in late summer. The plant has fibrous roots and strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. Allium alpicola thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.
