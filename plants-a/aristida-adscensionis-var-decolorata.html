Aristida adscensionis var. decolorata

Aristida adscensionis var. decolorata, commonly known as the decolorated threeawn, is a grass species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and have a bluish-green hue, which becomes more muted as the plant matures. This variety is noted for its ability to thrive in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic study.
