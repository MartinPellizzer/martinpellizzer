Ammannia pedunculata

Ammannia pedunculata, commonly known as redroot ammannia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features smooth, red-stemmed foliage that turns vibrant red or purple in the fall. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Ammannia pedunculata is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and along stream banks.
