Alyssum cordatum

Alyssum cordatum, commonly known as heartleaf alyssum, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has succulent, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum cordatum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
