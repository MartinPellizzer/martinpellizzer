Amethystanthus excisus

Amethystanthus excisus, commonly known as the excised amethyst flower, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its flowers are tubular and arranged in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Amethystanthus excisus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to well-drained soils.
