Aristida caput-medusae

Aristida caput-medusae, commonly known as the crown-of-thorns grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted, grass-like appearance with dense, cylindrical flower heads that resemble a crown. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically reddish or brownish in color. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique visual appeal.
