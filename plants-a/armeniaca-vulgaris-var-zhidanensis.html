Armeniaca vulgaris var. zhidanensis

Armeniaca vulgaris var. zhidanensis, commonly known as the Chinese date or jujube, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China and parts of Central Asia.

It produces sweet, fleshy fruits that are rich in sugars, vitamins, and minerals. The plant has simple, alternate leaves and shows adaptations to arid environments, including deep root systems. Its flowers are small and white, and it is often cultivated for its edible fruit.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
