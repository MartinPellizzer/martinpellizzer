Adesmia horrida

Adesmia horrida, commonly known as the rough adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Adesmia horrida is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological significance in its native range.
