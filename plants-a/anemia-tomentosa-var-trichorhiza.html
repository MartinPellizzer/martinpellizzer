Anemia tomentosa var. trichorhiza

Anemia tomentosa var. trichorhiza, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It has a creeping growth habit and can form dense mats, making it an invasive species in some areas.

Botanically, it is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
