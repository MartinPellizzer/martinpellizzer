Adenophora verticillata f. angustifolia

Adenophora verticillata f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
