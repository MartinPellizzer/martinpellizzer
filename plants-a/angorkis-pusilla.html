Angorkis pusilla

Angorkis pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf angorkis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, featuring delicate, narrow leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its botanical structure includes a compact stem with alternate leaves and is characterized by its low-growing habit.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate species with limited distribution.
