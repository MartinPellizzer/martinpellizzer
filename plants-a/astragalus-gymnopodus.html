Astragalus gymnopodus

Astragalus gymnopodus, commonly known as the nakedpod milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces small, leathery pods that remain attached to the stems even after maturity, a feature that contributes to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Astragalus gymnopodus is often found in steppe and desert landscapes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
