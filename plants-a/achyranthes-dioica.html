Achyranthes dioica

Achyranthes dioica, commonly known as sandbur, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Achyranthes dioica is known for its adaptability to arid environments and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
