Azalea faberi

Azalea faberi, commonly known as the Chinese azalea, is a species of flowering plant in the Rhododendron genus.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is known for its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, come in shades of red, pink, and white, adding vibrant color to gardens.

Azalea faberi prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
