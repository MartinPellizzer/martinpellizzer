Allium ducissae

Allium ducissae, commonly known as the purple garlic.

It is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered bulbs beneath the soil. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Allium ducissae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its visual impact.
