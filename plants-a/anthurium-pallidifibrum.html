Anthurium pallidifibrum

Anthurium pallidifibrum, commonly known as the pale-fiber anthurium, is a rare tropical plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy, dark green surface and a pale, fibrous appearance on the underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically surrounded by a showy, colorful spathe.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments for conservation and ornamental purposes.
