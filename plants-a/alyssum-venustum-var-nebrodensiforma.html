Alyssum venustum var. nebrodensiforma

Alyssum venustum var. nebrodensiforma, commonly known as the Nebrodensis Alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal and dry garden environments.
