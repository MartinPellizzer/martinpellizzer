Arctium buphthalmodes

Arctium buphthalmodes, commonly known as the broad-leaved burdock, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety underside and produces purple to pinkish flower heads that develop into spiny, bur-like fruits. Its root system is thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.
