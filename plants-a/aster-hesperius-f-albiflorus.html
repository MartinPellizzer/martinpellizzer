Aster hesperius f. albiflorus

Aster hesperius f. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its white to pale blue flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, adding a striking visual element to its environment.

The subspecies is distinguished by its notably white flowers, which set it apart from other forms of Aster hesperius.
