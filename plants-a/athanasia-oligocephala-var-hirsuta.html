Athanasia oligocephala var. hirsuta

Athanasia oligocephala var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy false aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, upright stems covered in short, hairy glands, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
