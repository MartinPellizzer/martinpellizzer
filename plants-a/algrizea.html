Algrizea

Algrizea, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, silver-green foliage and delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and rain, making it well-suited to humid environments.

Algrizea is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
