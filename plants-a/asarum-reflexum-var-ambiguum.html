Asarum reflexum var. ambiguum

Asarum reflexum var. ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy and emit a strong, spicy aroma. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically appearing in late spring. Its rhizomatous root system is notable for its aromatic compounds, which have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
