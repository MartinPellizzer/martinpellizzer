Axonopus purpusii

Axonopus purpusii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf muhly, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, often forming dense, low-growing mats. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and slightly curled at the edges. Its flowering spikes are small and clustered, producing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Axonopus purpusii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
