Anthemis nobilis var. discoidea

Anthemis nobilis var. discoidea, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with white ray florets, which bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and has a bushy, compact growth habit. Its leaves are serrated and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are usually hairy as well.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
