Astragalus nowroozii

Astragalus nowroozii, commonly known as Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seed pods contain small, kidney-shaped seeds that are often dispersed by wind.

Astragalus nowroozii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
