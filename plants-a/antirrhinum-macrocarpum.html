Antirrhinum macrocarpum

Antirrhinum macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors including red, yellow, and orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Antirrhinum macrocarpum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
