Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. sampaioana

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

sampaioana, commonly known as the Portuguese broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and yellow flowers.

The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and open habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local pollinators.
