Anthemis rumelica f. cana

Anthemis rumelica f. cana, commonly known as the white anthemis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscaping.
