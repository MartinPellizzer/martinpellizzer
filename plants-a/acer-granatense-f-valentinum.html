Acer granatense f. valentinum

Acer granatense f. valentinum, commonly known as the Valentin's maple, is a rare and ornamental variant of the Granatense maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant is native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains in South America. It prefers cool, moist climates and is often found in shaded woodland environments.

Due to its limited distribution and unique foliage, it is highly valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
