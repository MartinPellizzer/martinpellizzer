Agave nevadensis

Agave nevadensis, commonly known as the Desert Agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces a tall flowering stem that bears yellow flowers, which are followed by small, fleshy fruits. Agave nevadensis is adapted to full sun and well-draining soil, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular ornamental plant in desert gardens.
