Acacia pityoides

Acacia pityoides, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow globular flowers that appear in spring. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and a deep root system. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for native pollinators.

The wood of Acacia pityoides is lightweight and has been used traditionally by Indigenous Australians for various purposes.
