Aster persaliens

Aster persaliens, commonly known as the Persian aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

Aster persaliens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
