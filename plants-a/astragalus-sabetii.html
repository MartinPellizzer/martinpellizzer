Astragalus sabetii

Astragalus sabetii, commonly known as the Sabeti milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are small and typically dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native habitat.
