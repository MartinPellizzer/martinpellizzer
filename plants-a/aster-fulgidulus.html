Aster fulgidulus

Aster fulgidulus, commonly known as the showy aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Aster fulgidulus is often found in moist woodlands and meadows, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
