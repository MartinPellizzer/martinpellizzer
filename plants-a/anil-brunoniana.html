Anil brunoniana

Anil brunoniana, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that appear in clusters, often resembling small bells. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Anil brunoniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
