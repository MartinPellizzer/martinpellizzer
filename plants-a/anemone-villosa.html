Anemone villosa

Anemone villosa, commonly known as the downy anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy white or pink flowers with a distinctive center of yellow stamens, blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Anemone villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
