Arabis hirsuta var. ramigera

Arabis hirsuta var. ramigera, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, cold climates.
