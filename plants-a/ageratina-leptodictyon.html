Ageratina leptodictyon

Ageratina leptodictyon, commonly known as the slenderleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical structure supports its role in local ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
