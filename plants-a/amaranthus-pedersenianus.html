Amaranthus pedersenianus

Amaranthus pedersenianus, commonly known as the Mexican lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically pink to red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Amaranthus pedersenianus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
