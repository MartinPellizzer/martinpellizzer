Anil Bracteolata

Anil bracteolata is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern India and Pakistan. It thrives in harsh, dry environments where few other plants can survive. The plant is known for its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its name comes from the Sanskrit word "anil," meaning wind, reflecting its resilience against strong winds. This hardy species is not only a symbol of perseverance but also holds cultural significance in local communities.
