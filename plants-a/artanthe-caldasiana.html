Artanthe caldasiana

Artanthe caldasiana, commonly known as the Caldasian artanthe, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of purple or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
