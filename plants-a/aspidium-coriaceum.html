Aspidium coriaceum

Aspidium coriaceum, commonly known as the tough fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to various regions of the world.

It is characterized by its sturdy, leathery fronds that are often dark green and covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizome is creeping and often produces a dense network of roots.

Aspidium coriaceum is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its resilience to environmental stress.
