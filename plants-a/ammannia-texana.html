Ammannia texana

Ammannia texana, commonly known as Texas ammannia, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple in color, adding visual interest to its appearance. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Ammannia texana plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds, within its native ecosystem.
