Andropogon papillipes var. major

Andropogon papillipes var. major, commonly known as the rough bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.

This grass is valued for its ecological role in supporting wildlife and its use in restoration projects.
