Astragalus massiliensis

Astragalus massiliensis, commonly known as the Massiliensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Astragalus massiliensis is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
