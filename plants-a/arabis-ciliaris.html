Arabis ciliaris

Arabis ciliaris, commonly known as the fringed rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or coastal regions. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in a raceme.

Arabis ciliaris is known for its distinctive fringed petals and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
