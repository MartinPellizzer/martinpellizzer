Anil tristis

Anil tristis, commonly known as the bitter melon, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plant produces elongated, ribbed fruits that are typically harvested when immature for their edible, bitter-tasting flesh. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinctive, hairy texture.

Anil tristis is valued for its culinary use in various cuisines and also has traditional medicinal applications.
