Allium barsczewskii f. violaceum

Allium barsczewskii f. violaceum, commonly known as the purple onion.

This cultivar is a variant of the species Allium barsczewskii, characterized by its striking violet-colored bulbs and foliage. It is a hardy perennial herb native to Central Asia, often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
