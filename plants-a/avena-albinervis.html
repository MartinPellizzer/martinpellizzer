Avena albinervis

Avena albinervis, commonly known as the white-veined oat, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with distinctive white veins. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
