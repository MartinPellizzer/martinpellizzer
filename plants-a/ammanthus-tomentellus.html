Ammanthus tomentellus

Ammanthus tomentellus, commonly known as the woolly ammannia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss and protect against herbivores.

Ammanthus tomentellus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
