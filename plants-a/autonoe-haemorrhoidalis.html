Autonoe haemorrhoidalis

Autonoe haemorrhoidalis, commonly known as the bleeding heart plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and prefers moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and woodland settings.
