Allium karacae

Allium karacae, commonly known as the Karacae onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes a wide variety of onions, garlics, and related species. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are grass-like and form a rosette near the ground, while the bulb is typically small and underground.

Allium karacae is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
