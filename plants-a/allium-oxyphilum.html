Allium oxyphilum

Allium oxyphilum, commonly known as the sweet leek, is a flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its aromatic, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained, sandy soils. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, making it a popular herb in Mediterranean cuisine.

Allium oxyphilum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for both its beauty and culinary uses.
