Alpinia carinata

Alpinia carinata, commonly known as the mountain ginger or false cardamom, is a tropical plant belonging to the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Alpinia carinata is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
