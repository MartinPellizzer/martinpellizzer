Aphanamixis cucullata

Aphanamixis cucullata, commonly known as the hooded acanthus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hood-like bracts that enclose the inflorescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, spiky flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Aphanamixis cucullata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
