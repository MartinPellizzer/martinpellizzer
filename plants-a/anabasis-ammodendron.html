Anabasis ammodendron

Anabasis ammodendron, commonly known as the goatweed or desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub with fleshy, succulent stems that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, bead-like fruits. Its leaves are often reduced to spines, reducing water loss and allowing it to thrive in sandy soils.

Anabasis ammodendron is well adapted to extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
