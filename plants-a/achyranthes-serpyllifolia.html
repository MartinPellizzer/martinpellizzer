Achyranthes serpyllifolia

Achyranthes serpyllifolia, commonly known as field bindweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features creeping stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread aggressively through vegetative propagation. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Achyranthes serpyllifolia is known for its persistent nature, making it a common weed in cultivated areas.
