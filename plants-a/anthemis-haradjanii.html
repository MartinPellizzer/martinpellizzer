Anthemis haradjanii

Anthemis haradjanii, commonly known as the Armenian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with a central disk. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
