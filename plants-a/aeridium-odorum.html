Aeridium odorum

Aeridium odorum, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

The orchid's unique scent is believed to attract specific pollinators, making it an interesting subject for botanical study.
