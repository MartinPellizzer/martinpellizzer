Anthoxanthum redolens var. gunckelii

Anthoxanthum redolens var. gunckelii, commonly known as the fragrant grass or Gunckel's fragrant grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typically arranged in panicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Its aromatic scent, particularly noticeable when crushed, is a distinctive botanical feature.
