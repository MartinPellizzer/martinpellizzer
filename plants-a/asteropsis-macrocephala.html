Asteropsis macrocephala

Asteropsis macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed asteropsis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant features upright, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its most distinctive feature is its large, showy yellow flower heads, which consist of numerous small florets.

Asteropsis macrocephala is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and streambanks.
