Acalypha chamaedrifolia var. glechomifolia

Acalypha chamaedrifolia var. glechomifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf ironwood, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the species. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
