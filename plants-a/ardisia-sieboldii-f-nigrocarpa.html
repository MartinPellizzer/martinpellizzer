Ardisia sieboldii f. nigrocarpa

Ardisia sieboldii f. nigrocarpa, commonly known as the black-fruited ardisia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by dark purple to black berries in the fall. Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and striking, persistent fruit.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.
