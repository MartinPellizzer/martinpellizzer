Andropogon jwarancusa var. proximus

Andropogon jwarancusa var. proximus, commonly known as sand bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairie habitats. The plant has narrow, bluish-green leaves and produces inflorescences of spikelets that are characteristic of the grass family. It is drought-tolerant and has a deep root system, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Sand bluestem plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
