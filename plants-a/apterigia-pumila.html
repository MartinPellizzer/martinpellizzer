Apterigia pumila

Apterigia pumila, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow, often with a distinctive yellow center. The plant has a compact rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Apterigia pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
