Arundinaria tecta var. distachya

Arundinaria tecta var. distachya, commonly known as the bamboo reed, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with two-flowered clusters, giving it its species name "distachya.". Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
