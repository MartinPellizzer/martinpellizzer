Astragalus lepagei

Astragalus lepagei, commonly known as the Lepage's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
