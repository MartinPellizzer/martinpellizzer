Acrocephalus ramosissimus

Acrocephalus ramosissimus, commonly known as the tufted reed or marsh reed, is a perennial grass species native to wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, bushy growth at the top, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering spikes are usually greenish or reddish, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Acrocephalus ramosissimus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
