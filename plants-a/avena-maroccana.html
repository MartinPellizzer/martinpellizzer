Avena maroccana

Avena maroccana, commonly known as Moroccan oat, is a perennial grass species native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with bract-like structures. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological and agricultural studies.
