Acacia parviflora

Acacia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces flat, elliptical seed pods that contain numerous small, rounded seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Acacia parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
