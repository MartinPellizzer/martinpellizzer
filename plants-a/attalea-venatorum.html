Attalea venatorum

Attalea venatorum, commonly known as the "hunter's palm," is a species of palm native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Paraguay.

It is a tall, slender palm with a straight trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching due to their durability.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
