Arabis nepetifolia

Arabis nepetifolia, commonly known as wallflower or candytuft, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Its leaves are basal, lobed, and often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Arabis nepetifolia is commonly used in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types once established.
