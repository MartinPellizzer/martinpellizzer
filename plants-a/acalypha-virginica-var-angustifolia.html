Acalypha virginica var. angustifolia

Acalypha virginica var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is upright, with a height ranging from 2 to 4 feet, making it suitable for naturalized landscapes.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
