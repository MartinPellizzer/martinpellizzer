Astragalus aberrans

Astragalus aberrans, commonly known as the aberrant milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and can be found in various habitats across Kazakhstan, Mongolia, and parts of China.

Due to its ecological significance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
