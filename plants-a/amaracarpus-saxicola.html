Amaracarpus saxicola

Amaracarpus saxicola, commonly known as the rock amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and succulent leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fibrous roots help prevent soil erosion, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.

Amaracarpus saxicola is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
