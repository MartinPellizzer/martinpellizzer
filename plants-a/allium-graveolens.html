Allium graveolens

Allium graveolens, commonly known as garlic, is a bulbous plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to Central Asia but is now widely cultivated around the world. The plant produces a cluster of fleshy, white or purple bulbs composed of numerous cloves. Its leaves are grass-like and hollow, while its flowers form a spherical inflorescence.

Garlic is valued for its pungent aroma and is used extensively in culinary and medicinal applications.
