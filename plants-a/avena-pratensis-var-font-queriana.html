Avena pratensis var. font-queriana

Avena pratensis var. font-queriana, commonly known as the Fontqueriana oat, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing florets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
