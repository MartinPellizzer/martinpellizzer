Aniba viridis

Aniba viridis, commonly known as green aniba, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to yellow as it matures.

Aniba viridis is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
