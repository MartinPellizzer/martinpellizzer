Asplenium hindusthanense

Asplenium hindusthanense, commonly known as the Indian maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge on the underside. This plant is native to the Indian subcontinent and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It has rhizomatous growth and produces spores in clusters on the underside of its leaves.

Asplenium hindusthanense is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
