Astragalus tegetarius var. neomexicanus

Astragalus tegetarius var. neomexicanus, commonly known as the New Mexican milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. The plant features slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Its compound leaves are pinnately arranged, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators.
