Alchemilla grossidens f. vegata

Alchemilla grossidens f. vegata, commonly known as the velvet leaf lady's mantle, is a variant of the lady's mantle plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in rounded inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in partial shade.
