Arenaria tetraquetra subsp. cavanillesiana

Arenaria tetraquetra subsp.

cavanillesiana, commonly known as the Cavanilles' sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It typically forms dense mats of small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early summer.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or alpine environments. It is characterized by its succulent leaves and is adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
