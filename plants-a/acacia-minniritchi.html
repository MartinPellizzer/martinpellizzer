Acacia minniritchi

Acacia minniritchi, commonly known as the Desert Wattle, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of true leaves. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots and a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Acacia minniritchi plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
