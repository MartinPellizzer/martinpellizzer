Aster × salignus

Aster × salignus, commonly known as the sand aster, is a hybrid plant that results from the cross between Aster novi-belgii and Aster lateriflorus.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in open habitats such as dunes and coastal areas. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow disk, blooming in late summer to early fall. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Aster × salignus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
