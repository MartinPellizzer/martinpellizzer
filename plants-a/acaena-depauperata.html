Acaena depauperata

Acaena depauperata, commonly known as the slender burrplant, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its spiny, bracteate flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for native birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Acaena depauperata thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is commonly found in coastal and subalpine regions.
