Achillea megacephala

Achillea megacephala, commonly known as the giant yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust root system and upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in spherical or flat-topped inflorescences. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a feathery appearance, and are covered in fine hairs.

Achillea megacephala is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.
