Abelmoschus moschatus subsp. quinquelobus

Abelmoschus moschatus subsp.

quinquelobus, commonly known as the five-lobed okra, is a variant of the okra species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its distinctive five-lobed flowers and spiny, triangular pods that mature to a light green or yellowish color.

The plant is an annual herb with a bushy growth habit, typically reaching heights of 1 to 2 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil.

The subspecies is valued for its edible pods, which are used in various culinary traditions around the world.
