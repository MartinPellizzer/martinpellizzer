Acer laetum f. viride

Acer laetum f. viride, commonly known as the green-leaf maple, is a variant of the silver maple (Acer laetum) characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This cultivar features broad, ovate leaves that are predominantly green, contrasting with the typical silver or white variegation of the parent species. The leaves are typically smooth on both sides and have a slightly serrated margin. It is a deciduous tree that produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
