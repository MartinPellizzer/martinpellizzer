Aster novae-angliae f. geneseensis

Aster novae-angliae f. geneseensis, commonly known as the Geneseo aster, is a naturally occurring variant of the New England aster.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant purple to blue flowers. This form is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant produces showy composite flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
