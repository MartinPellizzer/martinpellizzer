Allionia linearis var. subhispida

Allionia linearis var. subhispida, commonly known as the velvetleaf allionia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded woodland environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural habitats.
