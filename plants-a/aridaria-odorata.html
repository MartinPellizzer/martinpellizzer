Aridaria odorata

Aridaria odorata, commonly known as the desert fragrance plant, is a succulent shrub native to arid regions of Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and robust, enabling it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Aridaria odorata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
