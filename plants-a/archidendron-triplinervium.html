Archidendron triplinervium

Archidendron triplinervium, commonly known as the three-nerve legume, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its compound leaves with three main veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by pods that contain several seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
