Amoora balansana

Amoora balansana, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

The species is valued for its timber and has some traditional medicinal uses.
