Ardisia samara

Ardisia samara, commonly known as the Christmas berry, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant produces small, dark red berries that contain a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, aromatic aril. These berries are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season.
