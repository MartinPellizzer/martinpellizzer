Aegochloa cotulifolia

Aegochloa cotulifolia, commonly known as purple muhly, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and striking purple or pink flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Aegochloa cotulifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a habitat for various wildlife species.
