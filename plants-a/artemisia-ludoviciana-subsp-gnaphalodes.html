Artemisia ludoviciana subsp. gnaphalodes

Artemisia ludoviciana subsp.

gnaphalodes, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of aromatic, narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and traditional uses.
