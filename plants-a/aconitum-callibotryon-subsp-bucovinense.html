Aconitum callibotryon subsp. bucovinense

Aconitum callibotryon subsp.

bucovinense, commonly known as the Bucovinaaconite, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian region. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous areas.
