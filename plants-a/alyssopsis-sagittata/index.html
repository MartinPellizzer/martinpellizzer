Alyssopsis Sagittata

Alyssopsis sagittata is a striking member of the Asteraceae family known for its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and vibrant yellow flowers. Native to the arid regions of southern Africa, this plant has adapted to harsh environmental conditions with remarkable resilience. Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike. Despite its beauty, Alyssopsis sagittata remains relatively obscure in mainstream horticultural circles. This article explores the characteristics, habitat, and significance of this fascinating plant.
