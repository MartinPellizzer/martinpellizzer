Acaena microphylla subsp. obscurascens

Acaena microphylla subsp.

obscurascens, commonly known as the dark-leaved burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its small, spiny fruits that enclose seeds, a feature typical of the genus Acaena.

The plant has dark green, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including forest margins and rocky areas.

Its ecological role includes providing food for native birds and insects.
