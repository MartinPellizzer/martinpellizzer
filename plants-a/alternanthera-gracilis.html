Alternanthera gracilis

Alternanthera gracilis, commonly known as the slender Alternanthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its strikingly colorful foliage, which can range from red to pink and purple hues. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or yellow and appear in clusters.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and water features.
