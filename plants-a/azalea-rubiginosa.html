Azalea rubiginosa

Azalea rubiginosa, commonly known as the rusty azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring, adding vibrant color to woodland gardens.

Azalea rubiginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
