Alchornea hirtella f. glabrata

Alchornea hirtella f. glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf alchornea, is a variant of the alchornea species characterized by its reduced pubescence compared to the typical form.

It is a shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in terminal panicles, while the fruit is a three-lobed capsule that releases tiny seeds.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to certain garden conditions.
