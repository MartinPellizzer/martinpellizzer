Amphistelma melanthum

Amphistelma melanthum, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its showy, clustered flowers are usually pink to reddish in color, blooming in the spring and early summer.

Amphistelma melanthum is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitat.
