Apiocarpus moguini

Apiocarpus moguini, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, where it thrives in cool and humid conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insects.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
