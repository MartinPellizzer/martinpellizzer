Abutilon thompsonii

Abutilon thompsonii, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture, and its showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Abutilon thompsonii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
