Axonopus laegaardii

Axonopus laegaardii, commonly known as the Cape grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by small, three-angled seeds.

Axonopus laegaardii is adapted to sandy or well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its hardy nature.
