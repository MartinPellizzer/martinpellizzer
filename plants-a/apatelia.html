Apatelia

Apatelia, commonly known as the starry nightshade, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South Africa and consists of several species, many of which are shrubs or subshrubs. The plants are characterized by their showy, often star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. Apatelia species typically have small, elliptical leaves and produce berries that contain toxic compounds.

These botanical features make them distinctive within the Solanaceae family.
