Amicia fimbriata

Amicia fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed amicia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with finely toothed margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Amicia fimbriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
