Aconitum superbum

Aconitum superbum, commonly known as the Himalayan monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that resemble a hood, which are arranged in a raceme. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

Aconitum superbum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
