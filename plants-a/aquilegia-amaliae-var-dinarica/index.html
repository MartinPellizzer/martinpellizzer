Aquilegia Amaliae Var. Dinarica

Aquilegia amaliae var. dinarica is a rare and striking plant native to the Dinaric Alps. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its vibrant, spurred flowers. This variant is distinguished by its unique floral morphology and coloration. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often found in mountainous regions of the Balkans. Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a conservation concern and a symbol of alpine biodiversity.
