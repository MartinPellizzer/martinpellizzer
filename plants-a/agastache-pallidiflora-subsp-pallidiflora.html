Agastache pallidiflora subsp. pallidiflora

Agastache pallidiflora subsp.

pallidiflora, commonly known as the paleflower hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of pale lavender to white flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for pollinators and a popular choice in native gardens.
