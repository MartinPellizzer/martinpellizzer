Agathosma pentachotoma

Agathosma pentachotoma, commonly known as the Cape aster, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the region. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
