Allium cyrilli subsp. cyrilli

Allium cyrilli subsp.

cyrilli, commonly known as the Armenian leek, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It features slender, hollow stems that are typically green to reddish in color.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense umbels. Its leaves are long and grass-like, often used in local cuisines for their mild, garlic-like flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Alliaceae family and is closely related to other allium species such as onions and garlic.
