Allium spirale var. spirale

Allium spirale var. spirale, commonly known as the spiral garlic or twisted garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that spiral outward in a unique, twisting pattern. The plant is characterized by its aromatic bulbs, which are used in some culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its distinctive floral display makes it a visually striking addition to any garden.
