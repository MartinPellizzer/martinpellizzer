Adesmia punctata var. hilariana

Adesmia punctata var. hilariana, commonly known as the Hilarian burro's tail, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green or blue-green in color, arranged in dense, compact rosettes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive drought conditions.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
