Angraecum canaliculatum

Angraecum canaliculatum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the island of Madagascar.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its flowering stems. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers with long, slender petals and a distinctive spurred lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Angraecum canaliculatum is notable for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
