Aphyllon purpureum

Aphyllon purpureum, commonly known as the purple leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, which are arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention in its native habitat.

Aphyllon purpureum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
