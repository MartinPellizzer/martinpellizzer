Acroclinium × atroroseum

Acroclinium × atroroseum, commonly known as the dark rose swamp mallow, is a hybrid plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking dark purple to almost black flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as marshes and pond edges. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Acroclinium × atroroseum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
