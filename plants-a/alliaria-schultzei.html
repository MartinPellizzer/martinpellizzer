Alliaria schultzei

Alliaria schultzei, commonly known as the Schultze's garlic mustard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, oval, and have a mild garlic-like scent when crushed.

Alliaria schultzei is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.
