Astragalus tegetarius var. jessiae

Astragalus tegetarius var. jessiae, commonly known as Jessiae milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers, which are typically pea-like in shape. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
