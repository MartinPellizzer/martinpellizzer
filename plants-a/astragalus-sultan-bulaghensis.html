Astragalus sultan-bulaghensis

Astragalus sultan-bulaghensis, commonly known as the Sultan Bulaghensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
