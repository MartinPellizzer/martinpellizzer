Agave consideranti

Agave consideranti, commonly known as the Mexican hat agave, is a striking succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a thick, spiky rosette of blue-green leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in height. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water once established.

Agave consideranti is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance in xeriscaping.
