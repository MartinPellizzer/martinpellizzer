Anisostachya castellana

Anisostachya castellana, commonly known as the Castellana grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually pale green to brown in hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Anisostachya castellana is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
