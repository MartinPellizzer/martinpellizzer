Ageratina subglabra

Ageratina subglabra, commonly known as whiteface tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of individual flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. Its showy, white to pale blue flower heads bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ageratina subglabra is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
