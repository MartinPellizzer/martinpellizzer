Aloe vatovavensis

Aloe vatovavensis, commonly known as the Mahafaly aloe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, typical of arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow-orange flowers, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Aloe vatovavensis is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with minimal rainfall.
