Artanthe lanceifolia

Artanthe lanceifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which range in color from red to purple, bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Artanthe lanceifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
