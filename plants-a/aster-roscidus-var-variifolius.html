Aster roscidus var. variifolius

Aster roscidus var. variifolius, commonly known as the variable-leaf aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that vary in size and shape, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
