Aster stenomeres

Aster stenomeres, commonly known as narrow-leaved aster, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple, pink, or white. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Native to North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Aster stenomeres plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
