Anthyllis gerardi

Anthyllis gerardi, commonly known as the yellow sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant has a creeping habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Anthyllis gerardi is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
