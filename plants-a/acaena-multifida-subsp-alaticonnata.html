Acaena multifida subsp. alaticonnata

Acaena multifida subsp.

alaticonnata, commonly known as the alate-fruited spinach bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions. Its growth habit is low and sprawling, with multiple stems arising from the base.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
