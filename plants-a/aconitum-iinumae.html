Aconitum iinumae

Aconitum iinumae, commonly known as the Japanese aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are typically arranged in racemes. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that are hairy on the underside.

Aconitum iinumae contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
