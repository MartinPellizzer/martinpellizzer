Amelanchier jonesiana

Amelanchier jonesiana, commonly known as the Jones serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It produces showy white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible berries that ripen in the summer. The plant is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves and smooth, gray bark. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open woods.

Amelanchier jonesiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
