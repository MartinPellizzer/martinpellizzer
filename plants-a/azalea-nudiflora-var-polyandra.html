Azalea nudiflora var. polyandra

Azalea nudiflora var. polyandra, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
