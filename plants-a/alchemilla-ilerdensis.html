Alchemilla ilerdensis

Alchemilla ilerdensis, commonly known as the Ilerde alkanet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Alchemilla ilerdensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
