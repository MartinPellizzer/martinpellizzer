Atalanthus regis-jubae

Atalanthus regis-jubae, commonly known as the royal jubae or the golden crown, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight hairy texture.

Atalanthus regis-jubae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
