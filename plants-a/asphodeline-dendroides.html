Asphodeline dendroides

Asphodeline dendroides, commonly known as the tree asphodel, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy, gray-green leaves that form a dense, tree-like structure. The plant produces tall, branched flowering stems that bear clusters of white or pale pink, tubular flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Asphodeline dendroides is often cultivated for its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
