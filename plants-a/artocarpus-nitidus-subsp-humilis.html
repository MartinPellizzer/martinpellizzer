Artocarpus nitidus subsp. humilis

Artocarpus nitidus subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the small-leaf breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and oval-shaped leaves that are usually less than 10 cm in length.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible, spherical fruits that turn yellow when ripe. Its fruits are typically consumed fresh or used in traditional cuisines.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
