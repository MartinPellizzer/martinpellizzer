Angelica paeoniifolia

Angelica paeoniifolia, commonly known as the peony-leaved angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that resemble the foliage of peonies, hence its common name. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, white to greenish-white flowers. Angelica paeoniifolia is known for its aromatic, strongly scented roots and is often cultivated for its medicinal properties.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is valued in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
