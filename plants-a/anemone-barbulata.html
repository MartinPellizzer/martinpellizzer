Anemone barbulata

Anemone barbulata, commonly known as the hairy anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical name reflects its distinctive, fluffy appearance and its classification within the Ranunculaceae family.
