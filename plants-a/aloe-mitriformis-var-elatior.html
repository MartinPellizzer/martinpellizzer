Aloe mitriformis var. elatior

Aloe mitriformis var. elatior, commonly known as the giant aloe or mountain aloe, is a striking succulent native to South Africa.

It features tall, branching stems that bear fleshy, narrow leaves with a bluish-green hue and a distinctive white to yellowish marginal stripe. The plant produces large, showy orange or yellow flowers on tall, erect spikes, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
