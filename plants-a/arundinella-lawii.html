Arundinella lawii

Arundinella lawii, commonly known as the Malayan bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is a tall, perennial plant that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its slender, hollow stems. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are usually arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Arundinella lawii is notable for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of sustainable materials.
