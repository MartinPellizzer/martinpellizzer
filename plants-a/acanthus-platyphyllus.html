Acanthus platyphyllus

Acanthus platyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaf acanthus, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, silvery hairs. The plant produces tall spikes of showy, spiky flowers in shades of purple and pink, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Acanthus platyphyllus is often used in gardens and landscapes for its dramatic foliage and long-lasting floral display.
