Atriplex almeriensis

Atriplex almeriensis, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, often fleshy leaves that are adapted to retain water in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in coastal and desert areas.

Atriplex almeriensis is also valued for its ability to stabilize soils and prevent erosion in harsh climates.
