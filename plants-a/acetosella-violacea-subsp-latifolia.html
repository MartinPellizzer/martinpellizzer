Acetosella violacea subsp. latifolia

Acetosella violacea subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as broad-leaved sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, often tinged with reddish or purple hues.

The plant produces small, violet or purple flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its leaves are edible, though they contain oxalic acid and should be consumed in moderation.
