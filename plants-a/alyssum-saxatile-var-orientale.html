Alyssum saxatile var. orientale

Alyssum saxatile var. orientale, commonly known as the eastern rock Alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
