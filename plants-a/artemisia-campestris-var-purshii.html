Artemisia campestris var. purshii

Artemisia campestris var. purshii, commonly known as yellow wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features aromatic, silver-green leaves that are finely divided and covered in a dense layer of tiny, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in compact, spherical clusters called heads, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, sunny environments such as meadows and disturbed soils.

Its chemical composition includes volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones, contributing to its medicinal and ecological significance.
