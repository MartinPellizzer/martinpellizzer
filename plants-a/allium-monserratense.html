Allium monserratense

Allium monserratense, commonly known as theMontserrat leek, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, ovoid bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
