Alstroemeria hygrophila

Alstroemeria hygrophila, commonly known as the water alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces showy inflorescences that attract pollinators.

Alstroemeria hygrophila is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
