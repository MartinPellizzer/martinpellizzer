Actinocephalus barbiger

Actinocephalus barbiger, commonly known as the hairy actinocephalus, is a unique member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, and hairy stems that give it a rugged appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and dry landscapes.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
