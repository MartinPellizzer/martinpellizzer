Atraphaxis caucasica

Atraphaxis caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian sandthrift, is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, gray-green leaves that are adapted to arid and rocky environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sandy or gravelly habitats.

Atraphaxis caucasica is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
