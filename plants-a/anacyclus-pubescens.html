Anacyclus pubescens

Anacyclus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy crownflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers in the spring. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh environments. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

Anacyclus pubescens is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in desert ecosystems.
