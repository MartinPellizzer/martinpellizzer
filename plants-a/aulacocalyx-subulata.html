Aulacocalyx subulata

Aulacocalyx subulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holly fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a creeping rhizome and produces small, clustered leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant's fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Aulacocalyx subulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
