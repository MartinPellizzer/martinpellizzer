Althaea aegyptica

Althaea aegyptica, commonly known as Egyptian mallows, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its mucilage-rich properties.

Althaea aegyptica is a perennial herb that prefers full sun to partial shade.
