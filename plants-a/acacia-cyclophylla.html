Acacia cyclophylla

Acacia cyclophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and narrow, feather-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which appear in spring and attract various pollinators. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and deep root systems.

Acacia cyclophylla is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of timber and fuel.
