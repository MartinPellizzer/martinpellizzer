Alyssum lycaonicum

Alyssum lycaonicum, commonly known as the mountain alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and has hairy, narrow leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to early summer.
