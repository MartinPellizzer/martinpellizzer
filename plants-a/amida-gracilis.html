Amida gracilis

Amida gracilis, commonly known as the slender amida, is a flowering plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden landscapes. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant growth habit and seasonal blooming period.

Amida gracilis is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
