Amygdalus persica subsp. scleronucipersica

Amygdalus persica subsp.

scleronucipersica, commonly known as the Persian almond. It is a subspecies of the almond tree characterized by its hard-shelled nuts.

The plant is native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits with a thick, leathery outer layer.

The botanical features include its deciduous nature and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid climates.
