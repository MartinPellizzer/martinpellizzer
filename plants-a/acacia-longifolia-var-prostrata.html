Acacia longifolia var. prostrata

Acacia longifolia var. prostrata, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Australia.

It features small, yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. The plant has slender, wiry stems that often lie close to the ground, forming a dense mat. Its leaves are phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of true leaves.

It is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
