Atropis palustris

Atropis palustris, commonly known as the marsh thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has upright stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, covered with coarse, hairy foliage. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges and a bluish-green color, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a dense, globular head.

Atropis palustris plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
