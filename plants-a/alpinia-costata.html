Alpinia costata

Alpinia costata, commonly known as the Chinese ginger or mountain ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and are clustered in inflorescences, while the rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Alpinia costata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.
