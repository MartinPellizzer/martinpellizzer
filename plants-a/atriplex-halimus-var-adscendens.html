Atriplex halimus var. adscendens

Atriplex halimus var. adscendens, commonly known as the ascending saltbush, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its growth habit is generally upright, with branches that may ascend or spread.

This variety is often found in coastal and saline soils, showcasing its tolerance to salt and drought conditions.
