Atractocarpus chartaceus

Atractocarpus chartaceus, commonly known as the paper fig or paper-leaf fig, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, papery, and often translucent leaves that give it its common name. The plant belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other fig species, and is known for its unique leaf morphology. It produces small, greenish flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are typically inedible for humans.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
