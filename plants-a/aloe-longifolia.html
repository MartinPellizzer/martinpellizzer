Aloe longifolia

Aloe longifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, fleshy leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in branched clusters during the summer months. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is often used for medicinal purposes.

Aloe longifolia is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
