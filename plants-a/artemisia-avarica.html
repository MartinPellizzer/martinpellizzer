Artemisia avarica

Artemisia avarica, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flower clusters in late summer, which are wind-pollinated. Artemisia avarica thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
