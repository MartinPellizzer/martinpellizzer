Alpinojuncus gonggae

Alpinojuncus gonggae, commonly known as the Korean rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or stream-side habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered at the ends of its stems.

Alpinojuncus gonggae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
