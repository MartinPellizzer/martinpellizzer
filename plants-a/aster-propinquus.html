Aster propinquus

Aster propinquus, commonly known as the common aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Aster propinquus produces numerous small, tubular flowers that bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
