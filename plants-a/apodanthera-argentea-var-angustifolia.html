Apodanthera argentea var. angustifolia

Apodanthera argentea var. angustifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf apodanthera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, angular stems covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it its characteristic appearance. The leaves are narrow and elongated, typically with a bluish-green hue and a softly hairy surface. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
