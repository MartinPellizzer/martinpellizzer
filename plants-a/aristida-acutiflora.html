Aristida acutiflora

Aristida acutiflora, commonly known as the sharpflower grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.

Aristida acutiflora plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small wildlife in its native range.
