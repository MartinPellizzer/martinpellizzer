Andromeda cassinefolia var. pulverulenta

Andromeda cassinefolia var. pulverulenta, commonly known as the powdered andromeda, is a rare and ornamental plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the spring season. It prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
