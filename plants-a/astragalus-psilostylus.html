Astragalus psilostylus

Astragalus psilostylus, commonly known as the slender-styled milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus psilostylus is often found in prairies, deserts, and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
