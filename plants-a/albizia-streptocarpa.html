Albizia streptocarpa

Albizia streptocarpa, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often pinnate and have a delicate, feathery appearance. The tree produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is known for its aromatic wood, which has been historically valued for its use in furniture and carvings. Albizia streptocarpa thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
