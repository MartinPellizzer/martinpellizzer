Andropogon serratus var. nitidus

Andropogon serratus var. nitidus, commonly known as the smooth bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in full sun and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
