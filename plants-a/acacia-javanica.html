Acacia javanica

Acacia javanica, commonly known as the Javan acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are divided into numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense inflorescences, and is known for its ability to thrive in dry and arid conditions. Acacia javanica is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and forage.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
