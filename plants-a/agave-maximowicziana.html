Agave maximowicziana

Agave maximowicziana, commonly known as the blue agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its stiff, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that can reach up to 10 meters in height, bearing yellow flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates with well-drained soil.

Agave maximowicziana is widely cultivated for its use in the production of tequila and other agave-based spirits.
