Acacia pinguifolia

Acacia pinguifolia, commonly known as the snow acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

The snow acacia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
