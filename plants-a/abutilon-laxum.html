Abutilon laxum

Abutilon laxum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Abutilon laxum thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
