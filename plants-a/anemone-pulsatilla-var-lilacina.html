Anemone pulsatilla var. lilacina

Anemone pulsatilla var. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a short, upright stem that supports a cluster of showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of lilac or pink. The plant has deeply lobed, palmately arranged leaves that are typically hairy and gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

The flowers bloom in early summer and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
