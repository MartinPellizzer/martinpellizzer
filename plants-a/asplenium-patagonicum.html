Asplenium patagonicum

Asplenium patagonicum, commonly known as the Patagonian spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern native to the southern regions of South America.

It typically grows on rocks or trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often clustered at the base, forming a rosette-like structure. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium patagonicum is valued for its adaptability and is often used in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and resilience.
