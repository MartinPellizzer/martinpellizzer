Agathosma thunbergiana var. patula

Agathosma thunbergiana var. patula, commonly known as Cape lavender or sweet everlasting, is a South African plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant is native to the coastal regions of South Africa, particularly in the Western and Eastern Cape provinces. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves are used in perfumery and aromatherapy due to their pleasant, herbaceous scent.
