Argemone corymbosa var. parva

Argemone corymbosa var. parva, commonly known as the dwarf prickly poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the arid regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of xerophytic environments.
