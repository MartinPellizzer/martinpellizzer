Astragalus tahbaziae

Astragalus tahbaziae, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus tahbaziae is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
