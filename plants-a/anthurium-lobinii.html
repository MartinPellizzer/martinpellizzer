Anthurium lobinii

Anthurium lobinii, commonly known as the red-veined anthurium, is a tropical flowering plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with vibrant red veins contrasting against its deep green foliage. The plant produces clusters of spadices, which are inflorescences that resemble small, spiky flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. Anthurium lobinii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
