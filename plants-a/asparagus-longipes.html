Asparagus longipes

Asparagus longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed asparagus, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

Asparagus longipes is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid regions.
