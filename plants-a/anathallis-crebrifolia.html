Anathallis crebrifolia

Anathallis crebrifolia, commonly known as the frequent-flowered anathall, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that often hang in clusters from the stems. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in the understory. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Anathallis crebrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
