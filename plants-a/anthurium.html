Anthurium

Anthurium, commonly known as the flamingo flower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spadix-shaped inflorescence. The plant's flowers are typically surrounded by a colorful spathe, which serves as a visual attractant for pollinators. Anthuriums are popular as ornamental plants due to their long-lasting blooms and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

They require well-draining soil and consistent moisture to maintain their health and aesthetic appeal.
