Allium angulosum var. tunicatum

Allium angulosum var. tunicatum, commonly known as the bulbous garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features a bulbous base with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a strong, pungent aroma similar to garlic.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible qualities.
