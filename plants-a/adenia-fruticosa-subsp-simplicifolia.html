Adenia fruticosa subsp. simplicifolia

Adenia fruticosa subsp.

simplicifolia, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It features thick, succulent stems that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries that are edible. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a distinctive, slightly serrated margin.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient and drought-tolerant plant.
