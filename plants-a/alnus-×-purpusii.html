Alnus × purpusii

Alnus × purpusii, commonly known as the hybrid alder, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the genus Alnus, which includes various species of alders known for their ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The tree typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters and has oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, while its fruit is a small, winged nutlet.

Alnus × purpusii is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
