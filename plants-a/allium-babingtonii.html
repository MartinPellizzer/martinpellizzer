Allium babingtonii

Allium babingtonii, commonly known as the Babington's onion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Allium babingtonii is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
