Acrostichum graminoides

Acrostichum graminoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf holly fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like fronds that are typically arranged in a compact, rosette-like structure. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its leaves, which are clustered in small, stalked clusters called sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
