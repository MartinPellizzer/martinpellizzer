Acomastylis sikkimensis

Acomastylis sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim acomastylis, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces a compact rosette structure.

Acomastylis sikkimensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude ecosystems.
