Anthemis ballii

Anthemis ballii, commonly known as the Ball's tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly aromatic scent. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the genus Anthemis.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
