Actinodaphne bourdillonii

Actinodaphne bourdillonii, commonly known as the Khasi nut or Indian frankincense, is a small evergreen tree native to the northeastern regions of India and Bangladesh.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, and its bark is often used in traditional medicine.
