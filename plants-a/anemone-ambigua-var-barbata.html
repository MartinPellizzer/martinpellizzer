Anemone ambigua var. barbata

Anemone ambigua var. barbata, commonly known as the barbed anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and hairy, with a distinctive, showy inflorescence of pink to white flowers. The flowers are typically five-petaled and bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully develop. The plant prefers moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
