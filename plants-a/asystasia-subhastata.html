Asystasia subhastata

Asystasia subhastata, commonly known as the broad-leaved asystasia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple and blue, typically arranged in spikes at the top of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Asystasia subhastata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
