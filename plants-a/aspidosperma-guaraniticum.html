Aspidosperma guaraniticum

Aspidosperma guaraniticum, commonly known as the Guarana tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. The tree is notable for its high concentration of caffeine in its seeds, which are used to make the traditional stimulant drink guarana. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest understories.

The plant has significant ecological and cultural importance in South America.
