Acacia robecchii

Acacia robecchii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. Acacia robecchii is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of native Australian flora.

It is also noted for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various native wildlife species.
