Achmandra

Achmandra, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plants typically have basal leaves that are lance-shaped or ovate, with hairy surfaces. They produce yellow flowers that are arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Achmandra species are often found in moist or wetland habitats, thriving in humid environments.
