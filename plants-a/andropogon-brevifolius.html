Andropogon brevifolius

Andropogon brevifolius, commonly known as dwarf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are short and often bluish-green in color. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Andropogon brevifolius plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
