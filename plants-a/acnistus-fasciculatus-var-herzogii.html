Acnistus fasciculatus var. herzogii

Acnistus fasciculatus var. herzogii, commonly known as Herzog's false holly, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, dark green leaves that are opposite and often clustered at the ends of branches. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. These flowers are followed by small, red berries that are edible for birds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
