Acanthocalycium variiflorum

Acanthocalycium variiflorum, commonly known as the variable-flowered hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to survive in dry environments.

Acanthocalycium variiflorum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
