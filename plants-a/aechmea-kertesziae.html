Aechmea kertesziae

Aechmea kertesziae, commonly known as the Kerteszia air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences that are often brightly colored. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is well adapted to epiphytic growth on trees.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.

Morphology

Aechmea kertesziae has rosette-shaped leaves that are narrow, strap-like, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a dark green color and a waxy coating.

The plant lacks a true stem, and its base is characterized by a thick, fibrous pseudobark that is typically grayish-brown in color. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by bracts, producing small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by birds and mammals.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in its epiphytic or terrestrial habitat.
