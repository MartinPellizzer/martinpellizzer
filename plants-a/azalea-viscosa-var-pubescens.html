Azalea viscosa var. pubescens

Azalea viscosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its showy clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are elliptical, leathery, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" appearance. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to garden environments.
