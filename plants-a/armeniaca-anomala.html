Armeniaca anomala

Armeniaca anomala, commonly known as the Chinese date or jujube, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China, Korea, and eastern Russia.

It produces edible, sweet, and fleshy fruits that are rich in sugars, vitamins, and antioxidants. The plant has a deep root system and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The fruit develops from the flower and matures in late summer to early autumn.
