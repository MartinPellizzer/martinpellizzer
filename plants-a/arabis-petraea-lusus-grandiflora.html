Arabis petraea lusus grandiflora

Arabis petraea lusus grandiflora, commonly known as the rock arabis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
