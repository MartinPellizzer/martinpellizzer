Acalypha schlechteri

Acalypha schlechteri, commonly known as the velvetleaf or redleaf acalypha, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges in color from deep red to purple and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed capsules. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
