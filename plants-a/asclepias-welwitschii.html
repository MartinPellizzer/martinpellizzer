Asclepias welwitschii

Asclepias welwitschii, commonly known as Welwitsch's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among species in the Asclepias genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

This species plays an important role in supporting local pollinators, particularly the monarch butterfly.
