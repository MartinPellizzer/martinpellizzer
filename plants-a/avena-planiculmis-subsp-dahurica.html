Avena planiculmis subsp. dahurica

Avena planiculmis subsp.

dahurica, commonly known as Siberian oat, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia. It features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-bearing flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
