Achymus patens

Achymus patens, commonly known as the spreading achymus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically features wiry stems that spread along the ground, often forming dense mats. Its small, daisy-like flowers bloom in shades of white to pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Achymus patens is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
