Anemone coronaria var. alba

Anemone coronaria var. alba, commonly known as the white poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
