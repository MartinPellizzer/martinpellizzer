Aster japonicus

Aster japonicus, commonly known as Japanese aster, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy as well.

Aster japonicus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
