Adiantum janzenianum

Adiantum janzenianum, commonly known as the Janzen's maidenhair fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are typically bright green and turn bronze in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

Adiantum janzenianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.

Morphology

Adiantum janzenianum has delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically pinnate, with a graceful, arching arrangement.

The leaves are usually light green, with a fine, feathery texture and can reach lengths of up to 30 cm. The plant has a slender, wiry stem that is usually green and grows in a creeping or upright manner depending on the environment. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow, blooming in late spring, and are pollinated by wind.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that releases seeds through wind dispersal, and the roots are fibrous and help anchor the plant in moist, shaded habitats.
