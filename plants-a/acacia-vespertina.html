Acacia vespertina

Acacia vespertina, commonly known as the evening acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect shrub form. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in globular heads, which bloom in the late afternoon or evening, hence its common name. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Acacia vespertina is adapted to dry climates and is often found in coastal and inland areas of southern Australia.
