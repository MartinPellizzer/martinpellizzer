Aponogeton fotianus

Aponogeton fotianus, commonly known as the Chinese water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, with roots that extend into the substrate. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically submerged, making it less visible to the naked eye. It is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to oxygenate the water.

Aponogeton fotianus is also valued for its role in providing habitat for aquatic life.
