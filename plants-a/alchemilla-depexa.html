Alchemilla depexa

Alchemilla depexa, commonly known as the creeping lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and appear in clusters during the summer months.

Alchemilla depexa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
