Acalypha ornata var. pilosa

Acalypha ornata var. pilosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers clustered in spikes, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
