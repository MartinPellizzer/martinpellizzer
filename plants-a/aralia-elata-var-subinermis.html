Aralia elata var. subinermis

Aralia elata var. subinermis, commonly known as the Japanese giant aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It features compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often used in gardens for its dramatic appearance.
