Archidendron bauchei

Archidendron bauchei, commonly known as the Chinese trumpet tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangement, and its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
