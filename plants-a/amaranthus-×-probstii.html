Amaranthus × probstii

Amaranthus × probstii, commonly known as the redroot amaranth, is a hybrid species within the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish flowers that develop into triangular, three-angled seed capsules. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional agricultural practices in some regions.
