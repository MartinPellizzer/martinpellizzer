Acer palmifolium subsp. typicum

Acer palmifolium subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically deeply lobed and have a vibrant color change in autumn.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental species. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters before the leaves emerge in spring.

The species is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is widely cultivated in gardens and parks.
