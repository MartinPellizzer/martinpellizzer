Ascaricida richardii

Ascaricida richardii, commonly known as the Richard's ascarid, is a parasitic nematode that primarily infects the intestines of various mammals.

It belongs to the family Ascarididae and is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical body and the presence of a prominent mouth with cutting plates. The life cycle of this parasite involves the ingestion of infective eggs, which then develop into larvae within the host's gastrointestinal tract. Ascaricida richardii is notable for its ability to cause significant health issues in its hosts, including malnutrition and intestinal blockage.

Due to its parasitic nature, it is an important subject of study in veterinary and human parasitology.
