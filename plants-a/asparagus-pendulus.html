Asparagus pendulus

Asparagus pendulus, commonly known as the weeping asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, feathery foliage that cascades downward, giving it a graceful, weeping appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in early spring, followed by edible, needle-like spears that are harvested in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it both visually appealing and useful in traditional medicine.
