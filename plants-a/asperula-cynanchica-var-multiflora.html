Asperula cynanchica var. multiflora

Asperula cynanchica var. multiflora, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in moist, shady environments.
