Alchemilla benasquensis

Alchemilla benasquensis, commonly known as the Benasque lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the Pyrenees region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Alchemilla benasquensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
