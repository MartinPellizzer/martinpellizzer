Aulacorhynchus

Aulacorhynchus, commonly known as the water hyacinth, is an aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that are arrowhead-shaped and its showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant has a rapid growth rate, often leading to dense mats on the water surface. Its roots are submerged, and it can absorb nutrients from the water, which can affect water quality.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in many regions outside its native habitat.
