Arachnangraecum pseudofilicornu

Arachnangraecum pseudofilicornu, commonly known as the false horned spider plant, is a unique member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescence that resembles a spider's leg, giving it its common name. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface, and its flowers are typically greenish-white with a central spadix. It thrives in humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unusual appearance.
