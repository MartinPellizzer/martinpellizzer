Agrimonia viscidula var. japonica

Agrimonia viscidula var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese agrimony, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features upright stems covered in sticky hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early summer, followed by red, spherical fruits. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a rough, viscid texture.

It is often found in woodland edges and moist meadows, and is valued for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
