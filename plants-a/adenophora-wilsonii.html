Adenophora wilsonii

Adenophora wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal. It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

Adenophora wilsonii is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and adaptability to various soil conditions.
