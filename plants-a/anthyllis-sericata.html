Anthyllis sericata

Anthyllis sericata, commonly known as silky anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow or pale yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and grasslands. It has trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

Anthyllis sericata is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in its native environments.
