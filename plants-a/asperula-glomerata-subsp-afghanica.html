Asperula glomerata subsp. afghanica

Asperula glomerata subsp.

afghanica, commonly known as the Afghan trailing arbutus, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan. It features small, fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer.

The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate floral display.
