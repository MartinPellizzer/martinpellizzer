Acer tataricum var. slendzinskii

Acer tataricum var. slendzinskii, commonly known as the Slendzinskii maple, is a rare and ornamental variant of the Tatarian maple.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and delicate, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red in autumn. Native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, this species thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its compact size and unique foliage make it a desirable choice for small garden spaces or rock gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat challenging plant to cultivate.
