Aegilops ovata subsp. biuncialis

Aegilops ovata subsp.

biuncialis, commonly known as the two-lobed wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with distinctive two-lobed glumes. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
