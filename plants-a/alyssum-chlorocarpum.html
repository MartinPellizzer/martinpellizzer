Alyssum chlorocarpum

Alyssum chlorocarpum, commonly known as yellow sand alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant color make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
