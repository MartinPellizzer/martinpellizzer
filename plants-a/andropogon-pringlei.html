Andropogon pringlei

Andropogon pringlei, commonly known as pringle's bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Andropogon pringlei plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
