Anemonoides trifolia subsp. brevidentata

Anemonoides trifolia subsp.

brevidentata, commonly known as the short-toothed anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It features delicate, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring.

The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval or ovate leaflets with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native gardens.
