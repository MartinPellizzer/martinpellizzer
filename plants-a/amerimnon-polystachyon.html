Amerimnon polystachyon

Amerimnon polystachyon, commonly known as the golden sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are often bright green. The plant produces dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers, which are typically arranged in spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways.

Amerimnon polystachyon is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
