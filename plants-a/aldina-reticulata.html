Aldina reticulata

Aldina reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined aldina, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a reticulate venation pattern, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats.

Aldina reticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
