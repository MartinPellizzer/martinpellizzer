Anemone saxicola

Anemone saxicola, commonly known as the rock anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as gravelly soils and rocky slopes. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers in early spring. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and have a distinctive central cluster of stamens.

Anemone saxicola is adapted to withstand drought and is often found in regions with Mediterranean climates.
