Armeniaca sativa

Armeniaca sativa, commonly known as the peach tree, is a deciduous fruit tree native to Asia.

It produces sweet, fuzzy fruits that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant has a complex root system and is known for its showy, pink or white flowers in spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

The fruit develops from the flower and is typically harvested in summer.
