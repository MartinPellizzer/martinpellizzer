Arabis abietina

Arabis abietina, commonly known as the alpine rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is typically found in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Arabis abietina is adapted to harsh, cold environments and is often found on rocky slopes and in mountainous areas.
