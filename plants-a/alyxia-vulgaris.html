Alyxia vulgaris

Alyxia vulgaris, commonly known as the common alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sunny environments, such as rocky soils and coastal areas. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and usually have a slightly rough texture.

Alyxia vulgaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardy nature.
