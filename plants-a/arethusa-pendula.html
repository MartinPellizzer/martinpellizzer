Arethusa pendula

Arethusa pendula, commonly known as the water lily of the marsh, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features striking, showy white flowers with a distinctive yellow center, which bloom in late summer. The plant has broad, arrow-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, supported by long, slender stems. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in muddy, aquatic soils.

Arethusa pendula plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
