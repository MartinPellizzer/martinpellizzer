Acacia cochliacantha

Acacia cochliacantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the acacia genus. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several hard, angular seeds.

Acacia cochliacantha is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
