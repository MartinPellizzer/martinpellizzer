Arabis oblongata

Arabis oblongata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. This perennial herb typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or coastal environments. Its leaves are elongated and narrow, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
