Aspidium javense

Aspidium javense, commonly known as the Java shield fern, is a species of polypod fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and compound fronds. The fronds are typically divided into multiple segments, with the uppermost leaves being fertile and bearing sporangia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant's structure and reproductive features make it an important component of tropical ecosystems.
