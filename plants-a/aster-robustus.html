Aster robustus

Aster robustus, commonly known as the rough blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are rough and hairy, and the flowers are blue to purple in color.

Aster robustus is often used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
