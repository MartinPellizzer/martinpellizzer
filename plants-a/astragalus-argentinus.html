Astragalus argentinus

Astragalus argentinus, commonly known as the Argentine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus argentinus is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a potential source of medicinal compounds.
