Arisarum australe

Arisarum australe, commonly known as the southern arum, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered around the spadix, which is often covered in a dense mass of tiny, greenish-white blooms. Arisarum australe typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands, where it thrives in humid conditions.

Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color.
