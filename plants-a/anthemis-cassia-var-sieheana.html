Anthemis cassia var. sieheana

Anthemis cassia var. sieheana, commonly known as the Siehean goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
