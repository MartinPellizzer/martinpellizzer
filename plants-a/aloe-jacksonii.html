Aloe jacksonii

Aloe jacksonii, commonly known as Jackson's aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are fleshy and covered in a thick, waxy cuticle. The leaves are typically green with a distinctive white or pale blueish hue due to the presence of a dense layer of trichomes. It produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are borne on a tall, branching stem.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and being drought-tolerant.
