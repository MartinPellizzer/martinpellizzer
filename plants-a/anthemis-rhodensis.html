Anthemis rhodensis

Anthemis rhodensis, commonly known as the Greek tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its foliage is aromatic and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinct appearance.

Anthemis rhodensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
