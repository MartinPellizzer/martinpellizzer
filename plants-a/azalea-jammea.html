Azalea jammea

Azalea jammea, commonly known as the Sierra Azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of elliptical, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Azalea jammea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
