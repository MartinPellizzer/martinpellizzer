Acacia hebeclada

Acacia hebeclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are narrow and finely divided, giving it a delicate appearance. Its yellow flowers are clustered in spherical or cylindrical heads, typical of many acacia species.

Acacia hebeclada is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
