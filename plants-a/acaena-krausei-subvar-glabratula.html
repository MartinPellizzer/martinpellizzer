Acaena krausei subvar. glabratula

Acaena krausei subvar. glabratula, commonly known as the smooth burr plant, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and dense, spiny bracts that form spherical clusters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in the axils of the leaves. Its fruits are dry, indehiscent achenes surrounded by persistent, hooked bracts that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animals.

This subvariety is distinguished by its smoother, less hairy appearance compared to other varieties of Acaena krausei.
