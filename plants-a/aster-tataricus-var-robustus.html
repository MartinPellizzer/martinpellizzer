Aster tataricus var. robustus

Aster tataricus var. robustus, commonly known as the Russian aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, adorned with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces showy, composite flower heads in shades of purple, blue, or white, typically blooming from late summer to early autumn. Its flowers consist of both ray florets and disc florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
