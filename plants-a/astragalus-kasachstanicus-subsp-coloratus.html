Astragalus kasachstanicus subsp. coloratus

Astragalus kasachstanicus subsp.

coloratus, commonly known as the colorful milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in umbel-like clusters, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

The subspecies is distinguished by its vibrant flower color and is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
