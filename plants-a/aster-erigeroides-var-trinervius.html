Aster erigeroides var. trinervius

Aster erigeroides var. trinervius, commonly known as the three-veined aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped with three distinct veins, giving it its specific epithet.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
