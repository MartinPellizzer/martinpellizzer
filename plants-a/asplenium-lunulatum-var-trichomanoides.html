Asplenium lunulatum var. trichomanoides

Asplenium lunulatum var. trichomanoides, commonly known as the hairy maidenhair fern, is a variant of the moon's sorrow fern.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are covered with fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. The leaves are typically bipinnate, with small, closely spaced pinnae that contribute to its ornamental value.

It is a popular choice for shady gardens due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to humid conditions.
