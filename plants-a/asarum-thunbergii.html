Asarum thunbergii

Asarum thunbergii, commonly known as Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a thick, rhizomatous root system and heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a hood-like bract. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant is valued for its aromatic rhizomes, which have been used in traditional medicine.
