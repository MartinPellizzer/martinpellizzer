Acer pictum var. angustilobum

Acer pictum var. angustilobum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, elongated leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. The tree typically grows to a height of 15 to 30 feet, with a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
