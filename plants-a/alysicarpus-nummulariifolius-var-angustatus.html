Alysicarpus nummulariifolius var. angustatus

Alysicarpus nummulariifolius var. angustatus, commonly known as narrow-leafed burr vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is known for its small, yellow flowers. Its leaves are narrow and pinnate, and the plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
