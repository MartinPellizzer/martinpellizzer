Adenia pulchra

Adenia pulchra, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, purple-red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a climbing habit, with woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Adenia pulchra is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its historical use in traditional medicine.
