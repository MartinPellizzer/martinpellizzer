Aulomyrcia nivea var. rosmarinifolia

Aulomyrcia nivea var. rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf myrtle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, aromatic foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
