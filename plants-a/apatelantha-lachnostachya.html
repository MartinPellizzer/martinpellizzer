Apatelantha lachnostachya

Apatelantha lachnostachya, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems covered in spines. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the warm season, which are often arranged in star-like patterns. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
