Acalypha tomentosa

Acalypha tomentosa, commonly known as velvetleaf or silky mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the variety. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Acalypha tomentosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
