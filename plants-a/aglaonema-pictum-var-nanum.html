Aglaonema pictum var. nanum

Aglaonema pictum var. nanum, commonly known as the dwarf painted nettle, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, variegated foliage with shades of green, white, and pink, creating a distinctive mosaic pattern. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and grow in a rosette formation, making it an attractive ornamental plant. It thrives in low-light conditions and requires moderate humidity, making it ideal for indoor cultivation.

Its slow growth rate and minimal maintenance needs make it a popular choice for both novice and experienced plant enthusiasts.
