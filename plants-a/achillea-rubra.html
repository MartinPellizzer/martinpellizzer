Achillea rubra

Achillea rubra, commonly known as red yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a slightly fuzzy texture.

Red yarrow is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
