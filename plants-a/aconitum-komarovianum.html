Aconitum komarovianum

Aconitum komarovianum, commonly known as Komarov's aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and are covered in fine, silky hairs.

Aconitum komarovianum is known for its toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
