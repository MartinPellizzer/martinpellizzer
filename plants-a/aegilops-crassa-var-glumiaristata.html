Aegilops crassa var. glumiaristata

Aegilops crassa var. glumiaristata, commonly known as the rough spike wheat, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its robust, coarse stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are tightly clustered, with lemmas that are rough and sometimes hairy. Its inflorescence is usually terminal, and the grains are small and hard.

This variety is of interest in agricultural research due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptability to various climates.
