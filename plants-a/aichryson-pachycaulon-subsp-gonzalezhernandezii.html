Aichryson pachycaulon subsp. gonzalezhernandezii

Aichryson pachycaulon subsp.

gonzalezhernandezii, commonly known as the Gonzalez Hernandez Aichryson, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its compact, columnar growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, typical of arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Its unique morphology and low maintenance make it a desirable addition to rock gardens and succulent collections.
