Anthopogon albescens

Anthopogon albescens, commonly known as the white sand sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable environments.

Anthopogon albescens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
