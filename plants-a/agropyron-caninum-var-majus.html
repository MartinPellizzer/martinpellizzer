Agropyron caninum var. majus

Agropyron caninum var. majus, commonly known as prairie grass or buffalo grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, fluffy seeds. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie ecosystems.

This variety is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
