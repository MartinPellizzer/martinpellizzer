Avena algeriensis

Avena algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian oat, is a species of grass native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Avena algeriensis is valued for its potential in ecological restoration and as a forage crop.
