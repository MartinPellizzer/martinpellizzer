Alysicarpus vasavadae

Alysicarpus vasavadae, commonly known as the Kutch burr, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western India.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is notable for its distinctive, spiny, three-lobed seed pods that enclose the seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
