Adiantopsis monticola

Adiantopsis monticola, commonly known as the mountain maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern native to the high elevations of South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that resemble those of the maidenhair fern. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are typically bright green and have a graceful, arching appearance.

Adiantopsis monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded gardens.
