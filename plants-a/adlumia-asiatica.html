Adlumia asiatica

Adlumia asiatica, commonly known as the Asiatic Clematis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. Native to East Asia, it thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soil. The plant is often found in shaded areas and can climb or trail along the ground.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plant species.
