Amaranthus lividus var. polygonoides

Amaranthus lividus var. polygonoides, commonly known as the purple amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is characterized by its striking purple to red bracts that surround the small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces seeds that are often used as a grain or for ornamental purposes.

This variety is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to grow in poor soil conditions.
