Amaioua brasiliana

Amaioua brasiliana, commonly known as the Brazilian amaioua, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a woody vine that can grow up to several meters in length, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in the dense forest understory.

Amaioua brasiliana plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
