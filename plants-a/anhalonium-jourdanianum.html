Anhalonium jourdanianum

Anhalonium jourdanianum, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with thick, fleshy stems adapted to store water. Its leaves are typically green and succulent, and it produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant is well-suited to hot, dry climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays an important ecological role in desert ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
