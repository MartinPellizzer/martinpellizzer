Astragalus atratus var. stenophyllus

Astragalus atratus var. stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

This variety is often found in sagebrush steppe and other xeric environments.
