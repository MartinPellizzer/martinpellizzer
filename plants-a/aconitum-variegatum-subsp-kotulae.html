Aconitum variegatum subsp. kotulae

Aconitum variegatum subsp.

kotulae, commonly known as the Kotula's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers.

The plant features palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern, and its stems are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in forested or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
