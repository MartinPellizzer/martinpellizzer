Asclepias welshii

Asclepias welshii, commonly known as the Welsh milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of pink to reddish-orange flowers with distinctive, horn-like seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to arid environments.

Asclepias welshii is also an important host plant for the endangered monarch butterfly.
