Allium aroides

Allium aroides, commonly known as the purple garlic, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, purple flowers. The plant produces small, bulbous structures at the base, which are used as edible bulbs in some cuisines. Its leaves are long and grass-like, often growing in dense tufts.

Allium aroides is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
