Agapetes lacei var. glaberrima

Agapetes lacei var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth-leaved silver fir, is a variant of the Agapetes genus native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are smooth and leathery in texture. The plant typically grows at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible berries that are a food source for various wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
