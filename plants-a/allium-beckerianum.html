Allium beckerianum

Allium beckerianum, commonly known as the Becker's onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces onion-like bulbs that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or woodland borders.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native habitats.
