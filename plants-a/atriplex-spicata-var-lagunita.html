Atriplex spicata var. lagunita

Atriplex spicata var. lagunita, commonly known as Lagunita saltbush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of greenish-yellow flowers that are often concealed within the foliage. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert and coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide forage for wildlife.
