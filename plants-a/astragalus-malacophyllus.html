Astragalus malacophyllus

Astragalus malacophyllus, commonly known as the soft-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils.

Astragalus malacophyllus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
