Ardisia eximia

Ardisia eximia, commonly known as the silverleaf ardisia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for humans but toxic to some animals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ardisia eximia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
