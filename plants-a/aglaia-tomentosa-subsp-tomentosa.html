Aglaia tomentosa subsp. tomentosa

Aglaia tomentosa subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy aglaia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, fleshy fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
