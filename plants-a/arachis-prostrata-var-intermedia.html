Arachis prostrata var. intermedia

Arachis prostrata var. intermedia, commonly known as the prostrate peanut, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous species that produces yellow flowers and edible pods containing peanut seeds. The plant typically thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils. Its botanical features include hairy stems, compound leaves, and a unique reproductive system involving self-pollination.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential agricultural uses.
