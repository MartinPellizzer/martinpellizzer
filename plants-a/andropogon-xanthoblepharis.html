Andropogon xanthoblepharis

Andropogon xanthoblepharis, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, golden-yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its flowering structure is typical of the grass family (Poaceae), featuring bracts and florets.

Andropogon xanthoblepharis is often found in wetlands and moist soils, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
