Aconogonon rude

Aconogonon rude, commonly known as the rough aconogonon, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
