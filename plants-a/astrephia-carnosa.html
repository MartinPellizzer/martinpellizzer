Astrephia carnosa

Astrephia carnosa, commonly known as the Zulu orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and red flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

Astrephia carnosa is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance among indigenous communities.
