Aristida browniana var. latifolia

Aristida browniana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf threeawn, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, fluffy seed heads that are characteristic of the Aristida genus. It is often found in open areas such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and disturbed sites.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
