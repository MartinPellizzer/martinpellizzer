Arcytophyllum thymifolium var. scolnikii

Arcytophyllum thymifolium var. scolnikii, commonly known as the Scolnik's savory, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants known for their distinctive foliage and medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, known as a bloom. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

The plant is valued for its aromatic qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
