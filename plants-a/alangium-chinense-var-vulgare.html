Alangium chinense var. vulgare

Alangium chinense var. vulgare, commonly known as Chinese alangium, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
