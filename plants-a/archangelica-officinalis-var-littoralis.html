Archangelica officinalis var. littoralis

Archangelica officinalis var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal angelica, is a variant of the angelica plant adapted to coastal environments.

It is a tall, biennial or perennial herb with large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found along shorelines and tidal zones.

Due to its unique habitat and medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
