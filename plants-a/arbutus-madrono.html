Arbutus madrono

Arbutus madrono, commonly known as madrone, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The plant produces showy, white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

Madrone thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
