Alocasia × chantrieri

Alocasia × chantrieri, commonly known as the Black Velvet plant, is a hybrid cultivar of the Alocasia genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and often marked with intricate patterns. The plant is a member of the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental plants such as philodendrons and dieffenbachias. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Alocasia × chantrieri is popular as a houseplant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
