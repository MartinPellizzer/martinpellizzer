Aconitum vulparia var. thalianum

Aconitum vulparia var. thalianum, commonly known as the alpine aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

This perennial herbaceous plant is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. It produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. The plant has palmate leaves with deep lobes, and its roots are tuberous, typical of the genus Aconitum.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
