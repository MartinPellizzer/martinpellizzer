Aconitum napellus var. turkestanicum

Aconitum napellus var. turkestanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan monkshood, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features striking dark green, heart-shaped leaves and bears showy, hooded blue-violet flowers. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stem and is part of the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
