Aristida altissima

Aristida altissima, commonly known as giant threeawn, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces feathery, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous small, bristly spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

It thrives in a variety of habitats, including prairies, disturbed soils, and wetlands.
