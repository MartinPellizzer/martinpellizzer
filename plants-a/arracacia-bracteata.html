Arracacia bracteata

Arracacia bracteata, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact, herbaceous growth habit with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem, and it produces small, dry fruits known as achenes.

Arracacia bracteata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ease of cultivation.
