Achyranthes aphylla

Achyranthes aphylla, commonly known as the leafless spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its absence of leaves, with photosynthesis occurring through its green stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or dry habitats.

Achyranthes aphylla is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
