Aspalathus citrina

Aspalathus citrina, commonly known as yellow bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant yellow-green color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for landscaping in Mediterranean climates.
