Astragalus kaghysmani

Astragalus kaghysmani, commonly known as the Kaghysman milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
