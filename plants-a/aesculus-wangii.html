Aesculus wangii

Aesculus wangii, commonly known as the Wang's horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The tree produces large, spiny fruits containing one or two seeds each. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are ovate to elliptic in shape.

Aesculus wangii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
