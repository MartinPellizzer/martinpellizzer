Acaena sanguisorbae subsp. interruptepinnata

Acaena sanguisorbae subsp.

interruptepinnata, commonly known as the red burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its distinctive red, bristly fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a persistent, spiny capsule.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated edge.

The species is part of the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental features and ecological role in native habitats.
