Absinthium boreale

Absinthium boreale, commonly known as northern wormwood, is a herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, narrow leaves that are covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces tiny, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies. Absinthium boreale is adapted to cold climates and is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Its chemical composition includes various volatile oils and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal and ecological significance.
