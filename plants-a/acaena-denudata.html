Acaena denudata

Acaena denudata, commonly known as the New Zealand bramble, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, red berries containing seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays an important role in local ecosystems.
