Astragalus diversifolius var. roborum

Astragalus diversifolius var. roborum, commonly known as the robust milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with varying leaflet shapes, which contribute to its species name "diversifolius.". The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe habitats.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to withstand arid conditions.
