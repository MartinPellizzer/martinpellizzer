Aristolochia longipes

Aristolochia longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are typically slender and woody. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex chemical composition, including compounds with medicinal and toxic properties.
