Atriplex parishii var. minuscula

Atriplex parishii var. minuscula, commonly known as the smallleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, a feature typical of many members of the Chenopodiaceae family. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
