Astragalus anatolicus var. parviflorus

Astragalus anatolicus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the Anatolian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

This variety is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.
