Anchusa officinalis var. ochroleuca

Anchusa officinalis var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the white hawksbeard, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features a robust, upright stem with basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in a single, terminal head, surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in meadows and roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological gardens and traditional herbal medicine.
