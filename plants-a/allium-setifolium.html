Allium setifolium

Allium setifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaved garlic.

It is a perennial herb native to the western United States, characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in umbels, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Allium setifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
