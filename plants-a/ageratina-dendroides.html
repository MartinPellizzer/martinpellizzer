Ageratina dendroides

Ageratina dendroides, commonly known as the tree ageratum, is a shrub-like plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and clusters of small, fluffy white or pale blue flowers. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Ageratina dendroides is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
