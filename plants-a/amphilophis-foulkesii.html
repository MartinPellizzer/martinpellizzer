Amphilophis foulkesii

Amphilophis foulkesii, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features grass-like leaves that are narrow and linear, with a distinct central vein. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces three-sided, three-rowed berries.

Amphilophis foulkesii plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
