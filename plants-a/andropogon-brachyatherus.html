Andropogon brachyatherus

Andropogon brachyatherus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often blue or purple in color. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
