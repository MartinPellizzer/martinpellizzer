Aconitum napellus f. babiogorense

Aconitum napellus f. babiogorense, commonly known as the Babiogorense variety of the European monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to blue flowers arranged in a spire-like inflorescence. The plant features large, palmately divided leaves with a distinctive velvety texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its toxicity, it requires careful handling and is primarily grown for ornamental purposes.
