Acrocephalus villosus

Acrocephalus villosus, commonly known as the hairy reed warbler, is a grass-like plant native to wetland habitats.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and hairy stems. Acrocephalus villosus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
