Aglaia elegans

Aglaia elegans, commonly known as the elegant aglaia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by oval, woody fruits containing toxic seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and pharmacological properties.

Aglaia elegans thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
