Apocynum × speciosum

Apocynum × speciosum, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a hybrid plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically clustered in umbels. The plant produces a milky sap that contains cardiac glycosides, which are toxic to many animals. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along streambanks.

Apocynum × speciosum serves as a host plant for the larvae of several butterfly species, including the milkweed tiger and the spice bush swallowtail.
