Acer tataricum subsp. boscii

Acer tataricum subsp.

boscii, commonly known as the Siberian maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia. It features simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are typically red or yellow in autumn, adding ornamental value to gardens.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

Its compact size and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
