Astragalus angustidens var. retusefoliolatus

Astragalus angustidens var. retusefoliolatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to thrive in dry soils and is often found in prairies, sagebrush steppe, and rocky habitats.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological restoration and as a forage plant for wildlife.
