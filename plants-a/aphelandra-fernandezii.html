Aphelandra fernandezii

Aphelandra fernandezii, commonly known as the Canary Islands torchflower, is a striking plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aphelandra fernandezii is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
