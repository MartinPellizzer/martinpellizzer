Anthyllis vulneraria f. homoiphylla

Anthyllis vulneraria f. homoiphylla, commonly known as the lesser burnet, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense, compact clumps of wiry stems. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets, and it is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

This plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
