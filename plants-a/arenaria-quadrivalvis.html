Arenaria quadrivalvis

Arenaria quadrivalvis, commonly known as the four-valved sandflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to arid and coastal regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in sandy and gravelly soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, with four petals that open in a distinctive quadrivalved pattern. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

Arenaria quadrivalvis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
