Allocarya capitata

Allocarya capitata, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Allocarya capitata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
