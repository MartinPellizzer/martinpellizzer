Acacia amblygona

Acacia amblygona, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Its seeds are encased in hard pods and are dispersed by wind or animals.
