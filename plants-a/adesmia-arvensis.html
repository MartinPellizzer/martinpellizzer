Adesmia arvensis

Adesmia arvensis, commonly known as the field adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and along roadsides. It has a shallow root system and is often found in association with other pioneer species.

Adesmia arvensis is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
