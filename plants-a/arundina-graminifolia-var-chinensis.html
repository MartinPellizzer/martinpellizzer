Arundina graminifolia var. chinensis

Arundina graminifolia var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or Chinese reed, is a perennial grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that resemble bamboo, often growing in dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in spike-like clusters. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly rolled, contributing to its grass-like appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant, upright growth habit.
