Annona centrantha

Annona centrantha, commonly known as the custard apple or horned custard apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by green, fleshy fruits that turn yellow or orange when ripe.

The fruit has a sweet, creamy pulp with a mild, aromatic flavor, making it a popular fruit in tropical regions.
