Asplenium unilaterale var. cristatum

Asplenium unilaterale var. cristatum, commonly known as the cristate spleenwort, is a type of fern that belongs to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, fan-shaped fronds that are often adorned with a crest-like structure along the midrib, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant is typically found growing on rocks or tree trunks in moist, shaded environments. It has a rosette-like growth habit and produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves.

The species is relatively small, with fronds that can reach lengths of up to 15 centimeters.
