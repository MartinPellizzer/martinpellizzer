Aeluropus laevis var. dasyphyllus

Aeluropus laevis var. dasyphyllus, commonly known as the woolly flatgrass, is a variant of the species Aeluropus laevis.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and compact growth habit. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered with fine, soft hairs.

This variant is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often found in sandy or saline soils.
