Astragalus ergenensis

Astragalus ergenensis, commonly known as the Ergenens milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Astragalus ergenensis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
