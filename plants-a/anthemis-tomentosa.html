Anthemis tomentosa

Anthemis tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The plant's fuzzy texture and bright blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
