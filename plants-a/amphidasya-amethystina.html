Amphidasya amethystina

Amphidasya amethystina, commonly known as the amethyst gemflower, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple and pink flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Amphidasya amethystina thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
