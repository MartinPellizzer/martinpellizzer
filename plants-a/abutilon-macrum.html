Abutilon macrum

Abutilon macrum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring soft, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fibrous, soft hairs are a distinctive botanical feature, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Abutilon macrum is often found in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and in open woodlands.
