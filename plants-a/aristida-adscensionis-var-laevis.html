Aristida adscensionis var. laevis

Aristida adscensionis var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth arrowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in association with other aquatic or semi-aquatic vegetation.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland restoration projects.
