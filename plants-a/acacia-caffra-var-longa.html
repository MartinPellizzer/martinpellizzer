Acacia caffra var. longa

Acacia caffra var. longa, commonly known as the Natal umbrella thorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, thin, thorn-like stipules and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its root system is deep and drought-resistant, making it adaptable to arid environments.

The wood is hard and durable, historically used for construction and tool-making.
