Avena barbata subsp. subtypica

Avena barbata subsp.

subtypica, commonly known as the slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with minute, bract-like flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
