Asplenium × centovallense

Asplenium × centovallense, commonly known as the hybrid shield fern, is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between Asplenium trichomanes and Asplenium bulbiferum.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage with a dense, velvety texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as rock crevices or forest floors. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a distinctive, shield-like appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings for its aesthetic appeal.
