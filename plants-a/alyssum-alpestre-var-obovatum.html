Alyssum alpestre var. obovatum

Alyssum alpestre var. obovatum, commonly known as alpine alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that features compact, mat-forming growth habit, often reaching heights of only a few inches. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of silvery hair.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.
