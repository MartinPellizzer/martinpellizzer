Achillea crithmifolia var. tuzsonii

Achillea crithmifolia var. tuzsonii, commonly known as the Tuzson's yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and flat-topped clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial herb that is often found in dry, coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Its historical use in herbal medicine is also noted, though it is primarily cultivated for its visual characteristics in gardens.
