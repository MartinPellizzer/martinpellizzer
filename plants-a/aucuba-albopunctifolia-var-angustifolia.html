Aucuba albopunctifolia var. angustifolia

Aucuba albopunctifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved variegated aucuba, is a variant of the aucuba plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that often exhibit a striking variegation of white and yellow spots. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters in cultivation. Its foliage remains dense and vibrant throughout the year, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, and is relatively low maintenance once established.
