Aglaonema costatum f. immaculatum

Aglaonema costatum f. immaculatum, commonly known as the immaculate aglaonema, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a subtle, waxy sheen and intricate, light green veins that form a delicate pattern. The plant is noted for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. It is relatively low maintenance, requiring moderate watering and well-draining soil.

The variegation of its foliage adds a unique aesthetic appeal to any indoor or outdoor space.
