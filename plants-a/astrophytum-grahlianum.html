Astrophytum grahlianum

Astrophytum grahlianum, commonly known as the Grahli's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, ribbed stems that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by pale green fruits. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Astrophytum grahlianum is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
