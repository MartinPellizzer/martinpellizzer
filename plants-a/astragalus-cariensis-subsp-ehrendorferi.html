Astragalus cariensis subsp. ehrendorferi

Astragalus cariensis subsp.

ehrendorferi, commonly known as the Ehrendorfer's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters.

The plant has palmately divided leaves with a hairy surface, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to low precipitation and high sunlight exposure.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
