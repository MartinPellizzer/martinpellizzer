Antirrhinum hederaceum

Antirrhinum hederaceum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, ivy-like leaves and clusters of bright yellow or orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial herb that produces seeds through self-fertilization, ensuring its propagation in suitable habitats.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
