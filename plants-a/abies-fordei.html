Abies fordei

Abies fordei, commonly known as the Forde's fir, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically shorter than those of other fir species. The cones of Abies fordei are pendulous and mature to a reddish-brown color, releasing seeds in the fall. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the alpine ecosystems where it is found.
