Abutilon umbellatum subsp. glutinosum

Abutilon umbellatum subsp.

glutinosum, commonly known as sticky abutilon, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. It is characterized by its fuzzy, hairy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces capsules that contain numerous small, dry seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its sticky leaves and stems are distinctive features that aid in its identification.
