Anemone parviflora var. grandiflora

Anemone parviflora var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided, lobed leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous root system and a short, sturdy stem that supports its delicate, showy blooms.
