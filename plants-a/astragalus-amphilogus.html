Astragalus amphilogus

Astragalus amphilogus, commonly known as the broadleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open grasslands.

Astragalus amphilogus plays a role in soil stabilization and is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
