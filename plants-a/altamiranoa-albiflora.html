Altamiranoa albiflora

Altamiranoa albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered altamiranoa, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an understory shrub, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
