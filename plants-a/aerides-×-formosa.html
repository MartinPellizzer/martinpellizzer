Aerides × formosa

Aerides × formosa, commonly known as the Formosa orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from crosses between Aerides odorata and Aerides lowii.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and lavender. Its leaves are broad and dark green, often arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in greenhouses and tropical gardens.
