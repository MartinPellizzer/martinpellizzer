Asperula neesiana

Asperula neesiana, commonly known as the Japanese evergreen azalea, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring with flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Asperula neesiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
