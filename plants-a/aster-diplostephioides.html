Aster diplostephioides

Aster diplostephioides, commonly known as the New Zealand aster, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for native pollinators.
