Aster polycephalus

Aster polycephalus, commonly known as the many-headed aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a bushy growth habit with multiple flowering stems. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Aster polycephalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
