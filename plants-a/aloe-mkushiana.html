Aloe mkushiana

Aloe mkushiana, commonly known as the Natal aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge, often adorned with white or yellow tubercles. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing orange or red flowers, which attract pollinators like birds and insects. Aloe mkushiana is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive drought conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
