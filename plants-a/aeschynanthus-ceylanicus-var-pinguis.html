Aeschynanthus ceylanicus var. pinguis

Aeschynanthus ceylanicus var. pinguis, commonly known as the fat-leaved wax plant, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Sri Lanka and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces strikingly colorful, tubular flowers that range from red to orange, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Due to its specific care requirements, it is considered a challenging but rewarding species for experienced growers.
