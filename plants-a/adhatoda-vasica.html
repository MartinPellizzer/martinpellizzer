Adhatoda vasica

Adhatoda vasica, commonly known as Indian Pennywort, is a perennial herb native to India and other parts of South Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

Adhatoda vasica is valued in traditional medicine for its bronchodilatory and anti-inflammatory effects.
