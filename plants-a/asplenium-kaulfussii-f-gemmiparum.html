Asplenium kaulfussii f. gemmiparum

Asplenium kaulfussii f. gemmiparum, commonly known as the gemmiparous shield fern, is a rare and unique variant of the common shield fern.

It is characterized by its compact, dense rosettes of lanceolate, dark green leaves that often have a glossy appearance. This fern reproduces primarily through vegetative propagation via gemmae, which are small, vegetative propagules that form on the leaf margins. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical interest lies in its unusual reproductive method and its role in the study of fern evolution and adaptation.
