Aulomyrcia rufipes var. dives

Aulomyrcia rufipes var. dives, commonly known as the red-footed myrtle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, often with a reddish or purplish tinge on the underside.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
