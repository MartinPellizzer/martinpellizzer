Argythamnia guatemalensis var. barrancana

Argythamnia guatemalensis var. barrancana, commonly known as the Guatemalan argythamnia, is a shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It is often found in scrublands and coastal areas, showing resilience to harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of semi-arid regions.
