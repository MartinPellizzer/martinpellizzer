Arundastrum ramosissimum

Arundastrum ramosissimum, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-haired leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a taproot system and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs that reflect sunlight, helping to reduce water loss.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally by indigenous communities for its healing benefits.
