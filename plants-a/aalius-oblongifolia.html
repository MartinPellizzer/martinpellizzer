Aalius oblongifolia

Aalius oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf aalius, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming during the warmer months. Aalius oblongifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid environment to flourish.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
