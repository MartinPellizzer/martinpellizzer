Anthurium longissimum subsp. nirguense

Anthurium longissimum subsp.

nirguense, commonly known as the long-leaf anthurium, is a tropical flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America. It is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length.

The plant produces striking spadices, which are often brightly colored and serve as a means of attracting pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a houseplant in many parts of the world.
