Aloe cylindracea

Aloe cylindracea, commonly known as the cylindrical aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often branched and covered in a thick layer of white, waxy sap. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Aloe cylindracea is well-suited for rocky or sandy soils and requires minimal water once established.
