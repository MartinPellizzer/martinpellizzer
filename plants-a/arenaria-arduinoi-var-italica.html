Arenaria arduinoi var. italica

Arenaria arduinoi var. italica, commonly known as the Italian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in spring, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has succulent leaves that are fleshy and often covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
