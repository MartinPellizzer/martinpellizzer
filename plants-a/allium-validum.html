Allium validum

Allium validum, commonly known as the Persian leek or mountain garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus.

It features a basal rosette of long, narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers in the summer. The plant is characterized by its strong garlic-like odor and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium validum is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
