Abutilon albescens

Abutilon albescens, commonly known as the white abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
