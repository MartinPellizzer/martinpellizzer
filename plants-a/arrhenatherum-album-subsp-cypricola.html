Arrhenatherum album subsp. cypricola

Arrhenatherum album subsp.

cypricola, commonly known as the purple false oatgrass, is a grass species characterized by its distinctive purple tinge on the leaf blades and inflorescence. It is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in moist meadows and along stream banks.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that grow from a rhizomatous base, and its leaves are narrow with a hairy surface. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are usually pale green to purplish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity in wetland habitats.
