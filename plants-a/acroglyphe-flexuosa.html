Acroglyphe flexuosa

Acroglyphe flexuosa, commonly known as the wandering fern, is a unique member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that exhibit a distinctive undulating pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in dense forests. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of ferns.

Acroglyphe flexuosa is noted for its adaptability and can thrive in a variety of soil types as long as conditions remain consistently damp.
