Ardisia conzattii

Ardisia conzattii, commonly known as the dwarf beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring. The plant is notable for its showy, bright purple berries that appear in the fall, attracting birds and other wildlife. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Ardisia conzattii is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases.
