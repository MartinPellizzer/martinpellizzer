Ailanthus altissima f. erythrocarpa

Ailanthus altissima f. erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited tree of beauty, is a variant of the tree of beauty, native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-orange fruits, which contrast with the typical green ones of the species. The plant is a fast-growing, deciduous tree with compound leaves and a strong, often invasive growth habit. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is tolerant of urban environments.

Its ornamental value is high, but it can be problematic in natural areas due to its aggressive spread.
