Artemisia kruhsiana subsp. kruhsiana

Artemisia kruhsiana subsp.

kruhsiana, commonly known as blue mist shrub, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage, which consists of narrow, aromatic leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic properties and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
