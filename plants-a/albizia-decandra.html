Albizia decandra

Albizia decandra, commonly known as the white acacia, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry climates and is often found in savannas and woodland areas. Its pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across suitable habitats.

Albizia decandra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
