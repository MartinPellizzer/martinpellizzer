Albizia ogadensis

Albizia ogadensis, commonly known as the African silk tree, is a leguminous shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are delicate and feathery in appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in racemes. Albizia ogadensis is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in partial to full sun.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
