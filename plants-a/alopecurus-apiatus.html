Alopecurus apiatus

Alopecurus apiatus, commonly known as the honey grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, hairless leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along streambanks.

Alopecurus apiatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
