Allium zimmermannianum

Allium zimmermannianum, commonly known as the purple onion, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its bulbous stem and clusters of purple or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible bulbs that are used in cooking, similar to other allium species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often growing in Mediterranean climates.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant flowers and foliage, making it a popular choice for gardens.
