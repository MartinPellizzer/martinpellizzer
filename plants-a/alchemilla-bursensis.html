Alchemilla bursensis

Alchemilla bursensis, commonly known as the Bursa lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in a cup-like structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Alchemilla bursensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
