Anthemis artemisioides

Anthemis artemisioides, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually gray-green, covered in fine hairs, and have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.
