Asterocephalus succisa var. hirsutus

Asterocephalus succisa var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy asterocephalus, is a succulent plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaped landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts and gardeners in dry climates.
