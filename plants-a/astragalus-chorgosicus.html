Astragalus chorgosicus

Astragalus chorgosicus, commonly known as the Karakorum milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus chorgosicus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are often used in herbal remedies, though further research is needed to confirm their efficacy.
