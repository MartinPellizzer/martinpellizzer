Astragalus salubris

Astragalus salubris, commonly known as the healthful milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to dry conditions, with deep taproots that allow it to access groundwater. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus salubris is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
