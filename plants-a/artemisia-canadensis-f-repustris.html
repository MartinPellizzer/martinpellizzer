Artemisia canadensis f. repustris

Artemisia canadensis f. repustris, commonly known as roughleaf wormwood, is a variant of the common wormwood plant.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America, characterized by its gray-green foliage and aromatic, pungent scent. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its common name. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
