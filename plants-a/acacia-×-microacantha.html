Acacia × microacantha

Acacia × microacantha, commonly known as the hybrid acacia, is a cross between Acacia aneura and Acacia microphyla.

It is a shrub or small tree native to Australia, characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and small, phyllodes that are often green and sometimes have a reddish tinge. The plant produces yellow globular flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically appearing in spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.

The hybrid is known for its adaptability and ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Native Distribution

Its distribution is closely linked to the natural ranges of its parent species.
