Arsace

Arsace, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features striking blue or purple ray flowers surrounding a central disk, and its leaves are typically narrow and silvery-gray. Arsace thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
