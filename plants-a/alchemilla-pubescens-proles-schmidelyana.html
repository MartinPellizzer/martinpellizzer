Alchemilla pubescens proles schmidelyana

Alchemilla pubescens proles schmidelyana, commonly known as the Schmidel's hairy lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported soothing properties.
