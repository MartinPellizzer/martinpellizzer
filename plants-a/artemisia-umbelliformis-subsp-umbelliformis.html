Artemisia umbelliformis subsp. umbelliformis

Artemisia umbelliformis subsp.

umbelliformis, commonly known as the silverleaf sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, giving it its species name "umbelliformis.". It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
