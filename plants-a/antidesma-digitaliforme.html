Antidesma digitaliforme

Antidesma digitaliforme, commonly known as the fingerleaf antidesma, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, digitately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
