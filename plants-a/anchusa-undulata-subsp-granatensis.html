Anchusa undulata subsp. granatensis

Anchusa undulata subsp.

granatensis, commonly known as the Granada hollyhock, is a perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
