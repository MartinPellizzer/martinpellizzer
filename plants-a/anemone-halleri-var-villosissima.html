Anemone halleri var. villosissima

Anemone halleri var. villosissima, commonly known as the hairy anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are hairy and heart-shaped, with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, typically arranged in a cluster. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
