Acinodendron comosum

Acinodendron comosum, commonly known as the hairy acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that are often clustered in branched inflorescences, giving it a fluffy appearance. Its roots have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, which enhances soil fertility.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in savannas and woodlands.
