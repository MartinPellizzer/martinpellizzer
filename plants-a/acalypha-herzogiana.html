Acalypha herzogiana

Acalypha herzogiana, commonly known as the Herzog's spurflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescences that resemble small, elongated spikes. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb with alternate, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

The flowers are usually green or yellowish and are surrounded by bracts that give the plant its distinctive appearance.
