Achyrospermum phlomoides

Achyrospermum phlomoides, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ornamental value.

Its growth habit and appearance make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
