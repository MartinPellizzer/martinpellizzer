Agropyron pseudocaninum

Agropyron pseudocaninum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is well adapted to dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

Agropyron pseudocaninum is valued for its resilience and is used in ecological restoration projects.
