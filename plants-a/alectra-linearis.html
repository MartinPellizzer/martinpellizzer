Alectra linearis

Alectra linearis, commonly known as the slender bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, green, aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habit and produces tiny, white flowers. The plant possesses specialized bladder-like structures that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. It thrives in wetlands and shallow waters, where it can float or anchor itself to submerged surfaces.

Alectra linearis plays a unique role in its ecosystem by contributing to nutrient cycling through its predatory behavior.
