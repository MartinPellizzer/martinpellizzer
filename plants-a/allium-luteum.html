Allium luteum

Allium luteum, commonly known as the yellow garlic or wild leek, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a tall stem bearing umbels of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Allium luteum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
