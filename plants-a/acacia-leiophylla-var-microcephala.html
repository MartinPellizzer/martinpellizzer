Acacia leiophylla var. microcephala

Acacia leiophylla var. microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features small, yellow flowers arranged in compact globular heads, which are typical of the Acacia genus. The plant has slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, rounded leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
