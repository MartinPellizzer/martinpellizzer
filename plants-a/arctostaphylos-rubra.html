Arctostaphylos rubra

Arctostaphylos rubra, commonly known as the redleaf manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that turns a striking red or orange in the fall. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Arctostaphylos rubra is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
