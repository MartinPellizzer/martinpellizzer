Ardisia racemosopaniculata

Ardisia racemosopaniculata, commonly known as the clustered beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces bright red berries that are highly ornamental and attract birds. It typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Its leaves are glossy and oval-shaped, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in both natural and cultivated settings.
