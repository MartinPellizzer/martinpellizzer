Acacia oliveri

Acacia oliveri, commonly known as Oliver's wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and phyllodes that are narrow and often recurved. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, spherical clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with its deep root system and ability to fix nitrogen.

Acacia oliveri is often used in rehabilitation of degraded soils due to its hardy nature and ecological benefits.
