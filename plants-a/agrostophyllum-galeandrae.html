Agrostophyllum galeandrae

Agrostophyllum galeandrae, commonly known as the Cape Peninsula ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the winter months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It is typically found growing on coastal cliffs and rocky outcrops, where it benefits from the cool, maritime climate.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and protected species.
