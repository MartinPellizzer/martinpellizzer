Artemisia gayana

Artemisia gayana, commonly known as blue sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, pale blue or grayish flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its essential oils contain compounds like camphor and thujone, which contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

It is often used in traditional herbal remedies and as a ornamental plant in xeriscaping.
