Arthrosia floribunda

Arthrosia floribunda, commonly known as the rockflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh habitat. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is shallow and widespread, allowing it to absorb moisture from the soil efficiently.

Arthrosia floribunda plays a role in stabilizing soil in rocky areas and provides nectar for pollinators.
