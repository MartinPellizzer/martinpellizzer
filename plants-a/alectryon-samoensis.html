Alectryon samoensis

Alectryon samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan starfruit, is a small evergreen tree native to the islands of Samoa and nearby Pacific regions.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits resembling starfruits. The fruit has a sweet, aromatic pulp with a distinctive star-shaped cross-section when sliced.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
