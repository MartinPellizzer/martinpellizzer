Antrophyum brasilianum

Antrophyum brasilianum, commonly known as the Brazilian antrophyum, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, commonly known as the palm family. The plant is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and can grow quite tall, reaching up to several meters in height. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
