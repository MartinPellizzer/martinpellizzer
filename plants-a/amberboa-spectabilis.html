Amberboa spectabilis

Amberboa spectabilis, commonly known as the showy amberboa, is a striking perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves called involucres. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered within these colorful bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low water requirements make it a valuable addition to desert gardens.
