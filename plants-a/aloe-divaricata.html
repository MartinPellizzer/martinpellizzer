Aloe divaricata

Aloe divaricata, commonly known as the zigzag aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, pale green, ribbed leaves that curve in a zigzag pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, typically blooming in summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid climates.

Aloe divaricata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
