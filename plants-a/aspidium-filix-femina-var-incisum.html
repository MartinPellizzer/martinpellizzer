Aspidium filix-femina var. incisum

Aspidium filix-femina var. incisum, commonly known as the cut-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern characterized by its distinctive, finely divided, and often serrated fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and the Himalayas. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its fronds are bi-lobed and have a delicate, feathery appearance, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.

The species is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and indoor spaces for its lush, green foliage.
