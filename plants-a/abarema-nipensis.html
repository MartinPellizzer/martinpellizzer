Abarema nipensis

Abarema nipensis, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, colorful, and showy inflorescences. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant floral display.
