Artemisia pubescens var. gebleriana

Artemisia pubescens var. gebleriana, commonly known as the Gebler's wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and sagebrush steppe. Its foliage contains volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

It is also known for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
