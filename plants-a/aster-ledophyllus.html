Aster ledophyllus

Aster ledophyllus, commonly known as the desert aster, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, clustered blue to purple flowers. The plant has silvery-gray foliage covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
