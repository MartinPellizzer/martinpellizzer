Alliaria maingayi

Alliaria maingayi, commonly known as broadleaf winter cress, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in early spring. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress species, and is known for its peppery scent when crushed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Alliaria maingayi is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
