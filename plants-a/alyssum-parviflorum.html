Alyssum parviflorum

Alyssum parviflorum, commonly known as small-flower alyssum, is a low-growing flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compact, mat-forming foliage with silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum parviflorum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs and hoverflies.
