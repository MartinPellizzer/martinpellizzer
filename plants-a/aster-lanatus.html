Aster lanatus

Aster lanatus, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts.

Aster lanatus is often used in wildflower gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
