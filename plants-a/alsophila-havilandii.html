Alsophila havilandii

Alsophila havilandii, commonly known as the Haviland's tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bright green and can grow quite long, contributing to its ornamental value.

Alsophila havilandii is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
