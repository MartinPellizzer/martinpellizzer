Ayenia oblongifolia

Ayenia oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ayenia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, narrow leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

Ayenia oblongifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
