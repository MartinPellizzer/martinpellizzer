Adesmia filifolia

Adesmia filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf burrobrush, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, thread-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Adesmia filifolia is adapted to withstand drought and heat, making it a resilient plant in desert ecosystems.
