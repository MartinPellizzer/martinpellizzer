Allium shevockii

Allium shevockii, commonly known as the Shevock's onion, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear small, spherical flower clusters. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Allium shevockii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
