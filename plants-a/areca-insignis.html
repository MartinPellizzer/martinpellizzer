Areca insignis

Areca insignis, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, compound leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Areca insignis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its flowering spikes, which appear at the top of the plant, produce small, fragrant flowers that eventually develop into edible fruits.
