Andropogon × subtilior

Andropogon × subtilior, commonly known as the hybrid bluestem, is a tall, perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Andropogon gerardii (big bluestem) and Andropogon virginianus (sideoats grama). The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces distinctive seed heads that are often used in ornamental landscaping.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
