Axonopus siccus var. laxior

Axonopus siccus var. laxior, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. This variety is typically found in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense mats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat for various microorganisms.
