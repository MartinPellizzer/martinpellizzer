Azalea neriiflora

Azalea neriiflora, commonly known as the dark-flowered azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually deep red or maroon in color. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Azalea neriiflora thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.
