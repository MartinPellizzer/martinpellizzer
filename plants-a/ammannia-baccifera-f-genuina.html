Ammannia baccifera f. genuina

Ammannia baccifera f. genuina, commonly known as the pink gooseberry, is a variant of the Ammannia baccifera species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its distinctive pink or red berries that mature in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly rough texture.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, but the showy berries make it a popular ornamental plant.
