Amaranthus canariensis

Amaranthus canariensis, commonly known as the Canary amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands and parts of Africa.

It is an annual herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its seeds are small and oval, often used as a grain in some traditional cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
