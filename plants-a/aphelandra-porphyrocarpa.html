Aphelandra porphyrocarpa

Aphelandra porphyrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited torchplant, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, dark green, lance-shaped leaves and its long, drooping inflorescences that bear bright yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests.

Aphelandra porphyrocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
