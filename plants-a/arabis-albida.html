Arabis albida

Arabis albida, commonly known as the white rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. It produces small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.
