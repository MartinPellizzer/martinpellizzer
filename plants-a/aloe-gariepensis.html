Aloe gariepensis

Aloe gariepensis, commonly known as the Gariep aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Aloe gariepensis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
