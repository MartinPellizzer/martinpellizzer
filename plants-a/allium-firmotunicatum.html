Allium firmotunicatum

Allium firmotunicatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved garlic.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features strap-like, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. Its bulbous stem is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Allium firmotunicatum is valued for its aromatic properties and adaptability to harsh environments.
