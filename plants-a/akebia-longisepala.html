Akebia longisepala

Akebia longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled actinostemon, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Akebia longisepala thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and hardy climbing plant.
