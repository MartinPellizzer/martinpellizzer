Abutilon malacum

Abutilon malacum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a velvety texture.

Abutilon malacum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
