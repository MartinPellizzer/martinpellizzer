Artemisia cana subsp. viscidula

Artemisia cana subsp.

viscidula, commonly known as sticky blue sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage that is covered in fine, sticky hairs.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical shapes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ornamental use and ecological restoration projects.
