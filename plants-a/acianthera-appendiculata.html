Acianthera appendiculata

Acianthera appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. It has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

The leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.
