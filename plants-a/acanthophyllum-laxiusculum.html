Acanthophyllum laxiusculum

Acanthophyllum laxiusculum, commonly known as the slender thorned grass, is a low-growing, perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small desert animals.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in its native ecosystems.
