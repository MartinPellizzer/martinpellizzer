Annona friesii var. elongata

Annona friesii var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated soursop, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a distinctive, spiky fruit with a green, rough exterior. The fruit contains a white, fibrous pulp and black seeds, and it is often used in local cuisines for its unique flavor. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
