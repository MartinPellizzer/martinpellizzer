Aegiphila mollis var. puberulenta

Aegiphila mollis var. puberulenta, commonly known as the hairy aegiphila, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
