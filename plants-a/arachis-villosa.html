Arachis villosa

Arachis villosa, commonly known as the hairy groundnut, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces edible pods containing seeds that are used for food and oil. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Arachis villosa is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
