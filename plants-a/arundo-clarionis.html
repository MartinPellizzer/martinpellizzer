Arundo clarionis

Arundo clarionis, commonly known as the Clarion reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the southwestern United States.

It thrives in wetland environments and is characterized by its hollow, fibrous stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is often found in marshes, riparian zones, and other moist habitats. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive in certain areas.
