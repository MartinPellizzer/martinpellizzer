Arundarbor apus

Arundarbor apus, commonly known as the silverleaf willow, is a deciduous tree native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and silvery-gray leaves that shimmer in the sunlight. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist soils near water bodies and is often found along riverbanks and wetlands.

The silverleaf willow is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local wildlife.
