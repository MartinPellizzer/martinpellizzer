Acalypha gemina var. exserta

Acalypha gemina var. exserta, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with prominent red veins that contrast sharply with the deep green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
