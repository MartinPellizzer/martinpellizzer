Astragalus pauxillis

Astragalus pauxillis, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several seeds.

It is adapted to thrive in dry environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.
