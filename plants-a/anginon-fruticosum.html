Anginon fruticosum

Anginon fruticosum, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in sandy, saline soils. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming in the early morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Anginon fruticosum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
