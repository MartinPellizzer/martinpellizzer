Acalypha virgata var. pubescens

Acalypha virgata var. pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, vibrant red or orange bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and covered in fine pubescence.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
