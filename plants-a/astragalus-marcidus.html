Astragalus marcidus

Astragalus marcidus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus marcidus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
