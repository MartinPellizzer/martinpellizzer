Aechmea polystachya var. myriophylla

Aechmea polystachya var. myriophylla, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that range in color from red to orange to yellow. Its foliage is often adorned with striking, waxy, and colorful markings.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is popular in both indoor and outdoor gardens.
