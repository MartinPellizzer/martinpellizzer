Aphelandra plowmanii

Aphelandra plowmanii, commonly known as the Plowman's flameleaf, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that are often bright green with red or orange veins. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Aphelandra plowmanii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
