Asplenium adiantum-nigrum var. onopteris

Asplenium adiantum-nigrum var. onopteris, commonly known as the black spleenwort, is a fern species characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is native to various regions in the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices or soil banks. Its leaves are usually divided into narrow, linear segments, and it produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds.

This variety is often found in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in woodland habitats.
