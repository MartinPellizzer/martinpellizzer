Albizia pospischilii

Albizia pospischilii, commonly known as the white siris, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often feathery and arranged in pairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and drought-resistant, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Albizia pospischilii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
