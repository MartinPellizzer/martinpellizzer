Andropogon familiaris var. levervilleensis

Andropogon familiaris var. levervilleensis, commonly known as Lever's switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native landscapes and restoration projects.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits contribute to its use in sustainable landscaping and wildlife habitat creation.
