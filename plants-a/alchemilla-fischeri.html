Alchemilla fischeri

Alchemilla fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its common name is derived from its historical use in alchemical practices and its resemblance to the mythical "lady's mantle.".
