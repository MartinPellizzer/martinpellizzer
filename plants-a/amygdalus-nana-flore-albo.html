Amygdalus nana flore-albo

Amygdalus nana flore-albo, commonly known as the dwarf white almond.

It is a compact shrub native to southern Europe, characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible almonds that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
