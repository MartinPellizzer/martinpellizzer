Avenula occidentalis

Avenula occidentalis, commonly known as the western oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western regions of North America.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy, and its leaves are narrow with a bluish-green color. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Avenula occidentalis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small insects and animals.
