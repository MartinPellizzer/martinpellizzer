Asparagus sidamensis

Asparagus sidamensis, commonly known as the Sidamensis asparagus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asparagaceae.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Eastern Cape Province, where it grows in rocky and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that are clustered in umbels.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
