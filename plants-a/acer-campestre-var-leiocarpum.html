Acer campestre var. leiocarpum

Acer campestre var. leiocarpum, commonly known as the smooth-leaf maple, is a variant of the common maple tree.

It is characterized by its smooth, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. This variety is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by samaras that aid in its propagation.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in gardens and parks.
