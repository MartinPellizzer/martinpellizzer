Asplenium fragrans var. mexicanum

Asplenium fragrans var. mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican fragrant spleenwort, is a fern native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and emit a subtle, pleasant fragrance. It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its unique spore-producing structures called sorus.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
