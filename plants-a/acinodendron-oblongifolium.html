Acinodendron oblongifolium

Acinodendron oblongifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acinodendron, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Its fruits are woody capsules that release seeds when mature.

Acinodendron oblongifolium is adapted to grow in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.
