Aster glehnii var. hondoensis

Aster glehnii var. hondoensis, commonly known as the Hondo aster, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
