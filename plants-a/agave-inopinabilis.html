Agave inopinabilis

Agave inopinabilis, commonly known as the Mexican hat plant, is a succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick, fleshy, and covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears showy, bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of yellow or orange. Its ability to store water in its leaves makes it highly adapted to dry environments.

Agave inopinabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in xeriscaping.
