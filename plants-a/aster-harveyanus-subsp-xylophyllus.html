Aster harveyanus subsp. xylophyllus

Aster harveyanus subsp.

xylophyllus, commonly known as the western aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are typically blue to purple in color.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native range.
