Aconitum nasutum var. lasiocarpum

Aconitum nasutum var. lasiocarpum, commonly known as the woolly-fruited monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers, which are typical of the genus Aconitum. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are arranged in tall, erect spikes and bloom in late summer.

The variety lasiocarpum is distinguished by its hairy, woody fruits that persist into winter, adding to its ornamental value.
