Asperula majoriflora

Asperula majoriflora, commonly known as the large-flowered asperula, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, glossy evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are often followed by red berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Asperula majoriflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
