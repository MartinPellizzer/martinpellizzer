Acacia megalantha

Acacia megalantha, commonly known as the large-flowered acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are divided into numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Acacia megalantha is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
