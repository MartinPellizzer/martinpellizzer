Achillea millefolium var. lanuginosa

Achillea millefolium var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are covered in soft, white hairs, giving it a fluffy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
