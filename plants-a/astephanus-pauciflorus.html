Astephanus pauciflorus

Astephanus pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered stephanus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant features a slender, upright stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
