Anemonastrum subindivisum

Anemonastrum subindivisum, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant has a low-growing, mat-forming habit with gray-green, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Anemonastrum subindivisum is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in areas with minimal rainfall.
