Aconitum chamissonianum

Aconitum chamissonianum, commonly known as the Chamisson's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, adding visual interest to its habitat. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that contribute to its ornamental value.

Aconitum chamissonianum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
