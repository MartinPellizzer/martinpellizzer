Acacia intsia

Acacia intsia, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and bipinnate leaves that turn yellow during the dry season. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which are followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland areas with well-drained soil.

Acacia intsia is valued for its timber and has cultural significance in some regions.
