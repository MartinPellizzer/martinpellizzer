Actinidia fulvicoma var. hirsuta

Actinidia fulvicoma var. hirsuta, commonly known as the fuzzy actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a bright orange-red color in the fall. These berries are rich in vitamin C and are often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
