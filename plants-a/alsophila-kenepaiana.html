Alsophila kenepaiana

Alsophila kenepaiana, commonly known as the Kenepaiana tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is closely related to other tree ferns found in similar habitats. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting its upright growth habit.

The fronds are typically evergreen, contributing to the plant's ornamental value in both natural and cultivated settings.
