Acacia mucronata var. longifolia

Acacia mucronata var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and narrow, phyllodes that are typically long and linear. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
