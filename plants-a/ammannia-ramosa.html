Ammannia ramosa

Ammannia ramosa, commonly known as prairie ammannia, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color, especially when mature. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Ammannia ramosa is known for its ability to thrive in wet soils and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
