Aulomyrcia variabilis var. intermedia

Aulomyrcia variabilis var. intermedia, commonly known as the variable aulomyrcia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and adaptability to coastal conditions.
