Abutilon calycinum

Abutilon calycinum, commonly known as velvetleaf abutilon, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in the spring and summer. Its fibrous roots and hairy stems are adaptations to its native environment, allowing it to thrive in disturbed soils.

Abutilon calycinum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
