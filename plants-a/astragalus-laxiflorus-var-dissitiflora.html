Astragalus laxiflorus var. dissitiflora

Astragalus laxiflorus var. dissitiflora, commonly known as the scattered-flower milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, trifoliate leaves and produces numerous small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native range.
