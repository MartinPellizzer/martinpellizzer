Anguloa cliftonii

Anguloa cliftonii, commonly known as Clifton's anguloa, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions, often in rocky or forested habitats. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are short and often obscured by foliage.

Anguloa cliftonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
