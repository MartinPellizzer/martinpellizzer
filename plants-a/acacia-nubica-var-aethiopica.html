Acacia nubica var. aethiopica

Acacia nubica var. aethiopica, commonly known as the Ethiopian acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the highlands of Ethiopia and Eritrea.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are feather-like in appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, spherical inflorescences, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
