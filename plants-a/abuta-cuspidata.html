Abuta cuspidata

Abuta cuspidata, commonly known as the African butterbean, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae, also known as the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Its root system is notable for containing compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Abuta cuspidata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
