Anthemis indurata

Anthemis indurata, commonly known as the hardy anthemis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, displaying bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, providing a distinctive appearance.

Anthemis indurata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
