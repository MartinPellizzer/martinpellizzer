Agave albescens

Agave albescens, commonly known as the white agave, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like rosette of broad, blue-green leaves with white margins. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that can reach up to 10 meters in height, bearing clusters of yellow flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun.

Agave albescens is a slow-growing plant, often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional uses.
