Astragalus perrarus

Astragalus perrarus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus perrarus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife and livestock.
