Aspalathus chamlagu

Aspalathus chamlagu, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that contain seeds. It is often found in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.

Aspalathus chamlagu is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine.
