Acer rubrum var. clausum

Acer rubrum var. clausum, commonly known as the red maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red foliage in the fall, which ranges from orange to deep crimson depending on the variety. The leaves are typically ovate with coarsely toothed margins and turn vibrant colors in autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and forested areas.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
