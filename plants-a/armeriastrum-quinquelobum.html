Armeriastrum quinquelobum

Armeriastrum quinquelobum, commonly known as the five-lobed sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Armeriastrum quinquelobum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soils in dry landscapes.
