Acacia pycnantha var. petiolaris

Acacia pycnantha var. petiolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved golden wattle, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, dark green leaves that are narrow and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in spherical heads, making it a striking ornamental plant. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is closely related to the more widely known Acacia pycnantha, but it has distinct leaf morphology and is less commonly cultivated.
