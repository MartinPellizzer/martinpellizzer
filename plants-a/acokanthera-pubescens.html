Acokanthera pubescens

Acokanthera pubescens, commonly known as the African arrow poison or the milk tree, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds. The plant has small, opposite leaves and produces yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is handled with caution and has been used traditionally in various cultural practices.
