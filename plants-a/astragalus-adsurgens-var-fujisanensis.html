Astragalus adsurgens var. fujisanensis

Astragalus adsurgens var. fujisanensis, commonly known as the Japanese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its seeds are enclosed in hairy pods. It is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
