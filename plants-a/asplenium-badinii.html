Asplenium badinii

Asplenium badinii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced in sori located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium badinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
