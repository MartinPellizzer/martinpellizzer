Allium togashii

Allium togashii, commonly known as the Japanese leek or mountain garlic, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in traditional cuisine, particularly in Japan and Korea. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Allium togashii is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
