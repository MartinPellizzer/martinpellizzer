Achillea millefolium var. macrocephala

Achillea millefolium var. macrocephala, commonly known as the giant yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces large, flat-topped clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its common name honors the mythological figure Achilles, who was said to have used the plant to treat wounds.
