Agrostis canina var. mutica

Agrostis canina var. mutica, commonly known as the silky bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and a dense, mat-like growth habit. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often covered in silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Agrostis canina var. mutica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
