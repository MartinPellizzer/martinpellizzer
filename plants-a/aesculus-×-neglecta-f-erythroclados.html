Aesculus × neglecta f. erythroclados

Aesculus × neglecta f. erythroclados, commonly known as the red-leaf horse chestnut, is a cultivar of the horse chestnut tree characterized by its distinctive red foliage.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10–15 meters, featuring a broad, rounded crown. The leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are glossy and often display a striking red or maroon coloration in autumn. The tree produces large, showy white or pale yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by spiny, capsule-like fruits.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
