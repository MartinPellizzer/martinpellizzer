Aspilia decumbens

Aspilia decumbens, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces striking yellow flower heads that are surrounded by pale green bracts. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture.

Aspilia decumbens is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
