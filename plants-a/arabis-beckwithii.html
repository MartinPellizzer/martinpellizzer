Arabis beckwithii

Arabis beckwithii, commonly known as Beckwith's rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are arranged in clusters atop slender stems. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Arabis beckwithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
