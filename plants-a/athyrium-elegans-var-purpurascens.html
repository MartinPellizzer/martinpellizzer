Athyrium elegans var. purpurascens

Athyrium elegans var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-veined maidenhair fern, is a striking fern species native to eastern Asia.

It features delicate, finely divided fronds with a distinctive purple-tinged midribs that contrast beautifully with its bright green foliage. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly, forming dense, low-growing clumps.

This ornamental fern is valued for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
