Aspicarpa argentea var. sericea

Aspicarpa argentea var. sericea, commonly known as silverleaf aspicarpa, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within its native environment.
