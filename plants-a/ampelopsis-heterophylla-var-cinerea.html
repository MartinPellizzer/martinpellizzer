Ampelopsis heterophylla var. cinerea

Ampelopsis heterophylla var. cinerea, commonly known as silverleaf honeysuckle, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
