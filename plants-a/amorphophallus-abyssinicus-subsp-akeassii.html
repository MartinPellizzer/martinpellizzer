Amorphophallus abyssinicus subsp. akeassii

Amorphophallus abyssinicus subsp.

akeassii, commonly known as the Abyssinian giant arrowhead, is a striking plant native to Ethiopia. It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and a distinctive inflorescence.

The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a showy spathe, typical of many aroid species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
