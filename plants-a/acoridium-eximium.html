Acoridium eximium

Acoridium eximium, commonly known as the golden spike lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acoraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Acoridium eximium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
