Abutilon rufinerve var. confertum

Abutilon rufinerve var. confertum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor cultivation.
