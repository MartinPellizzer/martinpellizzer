Anthurium crassipes

Anthurium crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces striking spadices, which are the modified leaves that enclose the tiny, clustered flowers. Anthurium crassipes thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique inflorescence.
