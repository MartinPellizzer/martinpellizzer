Astrocaryum plicatum

Astrocaryum plicatum, commonly known as the plicatum palm, is a species of palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue, fleshy fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in wet tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
