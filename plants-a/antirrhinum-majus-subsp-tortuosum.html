Antirrhinum majus subsp. tortuosum

Antirrhinum majus subsp.

tortuosum, commonly known as the twisted snapdragon, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features slender, branching stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy.

The flowers are typically bright red or orange, with a distinctive bilateral symmetry and a curled, twisted appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
