Acoridium sarawakense

Acoridium sarawakense, commonly known as the Sarawak arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and flowering spathes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in swampy areas. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a starch source.

Acoridium sarawakense plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to the biodiversity of the region.
