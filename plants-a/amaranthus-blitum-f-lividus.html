Amaranthus blitum f. lividus

Amaranthus blitum f. lividus, commonly known as the purple amaranth, is a variant of the common amaranth species.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant is an annual herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a deep, rich coloration.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually green or yellow, and appear in clusters at the leaf axils.
