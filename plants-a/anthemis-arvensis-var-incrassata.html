Anthemis arvensis var. incrassata

Anthemis arvensis var. incrassata, commonly known as field chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers and aromatic, feathery leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants.

The leaves and flowers are often used in herbal remedies due to their mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.
