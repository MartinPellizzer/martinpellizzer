Astragalus tekessicus

Astragalus tekessicus, commonly known as the Tien Shan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, enabling it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus tekessicus is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
