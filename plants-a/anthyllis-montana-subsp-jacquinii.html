Anthyllis montana subsp. jacquinii

Anthyllis montana subsp.

jacquinii, commonly known as the mountain anthyllis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators in its native range.
