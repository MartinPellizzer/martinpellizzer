Aristomenia

Aristomenia, commonly known as the rock aster, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Asteraceae family.

These plants are typically found in arid and rocky environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. They produce showy flowers that are often blue or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The leaves are usually narrow and succulent, helping to reduce water loss.

Aristomenia species are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological role in desert ecosystems.
