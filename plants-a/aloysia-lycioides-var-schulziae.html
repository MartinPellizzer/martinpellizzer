Aloysia lycioides var. schulziae

Aloysia lycioides var. schulziae, commonly known as the Schulz's verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub with slender, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its bloom period extends from late spring to early autumn, adding vibrant color to gardens during the warmer months.
