Achimenes ghiesbrechtiana

Achimenes ghiesbrechtiana, commonly known as the red-veined achimenes, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins and clusters of trumpet-shaped, tubular flowers that range from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 3 feet. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
