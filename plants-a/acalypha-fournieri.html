Acalypha fournieri

Acalypha fournieri, commonly known as the red-flowered false mallow, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that surround small, yellow flowers, making it a visually appealing plant. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
