Adiantum platyphyllum

Adiantum platyphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green and have a feathery appearance. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce roots that help anchor it to rocky or soil surfaces.

Adiantum platyphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
