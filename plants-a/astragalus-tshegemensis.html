Astragalus tshegemensis

Astragalus tshegemensis, commonly known as the Tshege Mountain milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
