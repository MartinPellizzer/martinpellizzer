Arundinella murayamae

Arundinella murayamae, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Murayama bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that resemble bamboo, though it belongs to the grass family (Poaceae). The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Arundinella murayamae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
