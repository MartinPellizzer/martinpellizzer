Astragalus orthanthoides

Astragalus orthanthoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus orthanthoides is often found in steppe and desert environments, contributing to the ecological diversity of these regions.
