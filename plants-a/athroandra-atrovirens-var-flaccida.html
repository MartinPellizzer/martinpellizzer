Athroandra atrovirens var. flaccida

Athroandra atrovirens var. flaccida, commonly known as the soft-leaved ironwood, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are often slightly curled and have a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged texture, while its branches are often drooping, giving it a distinctive, cascading form.

This species is well adapted to sandy soils and salt spray, making it a hardy plant in coastal environments.
