Acer × neglectum var. friderici

Acer × neglectum var. friderici, commonly known as the Frideric maple, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The tree typically grows to a height of 10–15 meters, with a rounded, symmetrical crown. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming more fissured with age.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and urban landscapes.
