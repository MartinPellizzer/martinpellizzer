Aster hispidus f. albiflorus

Aster hispidus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered hairy aster, is a variant of the hairy aster species native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the late summer to early fall. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, spherical heads, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
