Abolboda grandis var. minor

Abolboda grandis var. minor, commonly known as the lesser grand magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical with a smooth margin. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This variety is less common than its parent species and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
