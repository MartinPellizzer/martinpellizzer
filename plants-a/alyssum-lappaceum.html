Alyssum lappaceum

Alyssum lappaceum, commonly known as the narrowleaf alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum lappaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
