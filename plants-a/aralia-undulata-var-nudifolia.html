Aralia undulata var. nudifolia

Aralia undulata var. nudifolia, commonly known as the smooth aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves that are typically unlobed and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels during late spring to early summer. Its berries, which are black and slightly toxic, appear in the fall and are a food source for birds.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in moist, wooded areas.
