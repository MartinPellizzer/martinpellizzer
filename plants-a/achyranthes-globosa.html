Achyranthes globosa

Achyranthes globosa, commonly known as globe smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces distinctive, round, fleshy fruits that are borne in clusters, giving it its common name. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Achyranthes globosa thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp environments.
