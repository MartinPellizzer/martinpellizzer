Aspidistra erythrocephala

Aspidistra erythrocephala, commonly known as the red-headed aspidistra, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves with a distinctive red or purple midrib. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers enclosed within a spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in shady, humid conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Aspidistra erythrocephala is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
