Allium macrospermum

Allium macrospermum, commonly known as the giant bulb onion, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, bulbous stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with broad, strap-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer. Its bulb is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor, often used in cooking.

The plant is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, thriving in well-drained soils.
