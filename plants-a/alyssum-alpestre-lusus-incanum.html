Alyssum alpestre lusus incanum

Alyssum alpestre lusus incanum, commonly known as the alpine alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with compact foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine displays. Its flowers bloom in early summer, adding a delicate touch of color to its natural habitat.

The species is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold temperatures, which is a key feature of its alpine origin.
