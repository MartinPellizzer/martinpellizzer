Asteromoea pinnatifida

Asteromoea pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaf asteromoea, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy species in coastal and arid regions. Its leaves are pinnately divided, giving it a feathery appearance, and it produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow.

Asteromoea pinnatifida is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
