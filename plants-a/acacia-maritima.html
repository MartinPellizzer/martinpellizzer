Acacia maritima

Acacia maritima, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, bipinnate leaves that reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which attract a variety of pollinators. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong coastal winds.

Acacia maritima plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.
