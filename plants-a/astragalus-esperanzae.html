Astragalus esperanzae

Astragalus esperanzae, commonly known as Esperanza's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky soils and desert scrublands. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native insects and wildlife.

Its scientific name honors the Esperanza region where it is commonly found.
