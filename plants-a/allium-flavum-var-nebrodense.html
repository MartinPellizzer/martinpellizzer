Allium flavum var. nebrodense

Allium flavum var. nebrodense, commonly known as the Nebrodian leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is characterized by its mild, onion-like flavor, making it suitable for culinary use. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species such as garlic and chives.
