Adenophora denticulata var. linifolia

Adenophora denticulata var. linifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in temperate regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
