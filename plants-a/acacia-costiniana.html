Acacia costiniana

Acacia costiniana, commonly known as the African acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate and have a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by flat, bean-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and woodlands.

Acacia costiniana is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important component of nitrogen-cycling ecosystems.
