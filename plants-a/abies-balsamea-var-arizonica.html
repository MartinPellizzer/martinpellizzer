Abies balsamea var. arizonica

Abies balsamea var. arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona fir, is a variant of the balsam fir native to the southwestern United States.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant has dark green, flat needles that are arranged in spirals along the branches. It typically grows in high elevation forests and requires cool, moist conditions to thrive.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local ecosystem.
