Axonopus compressus var. itirapinensis

Axonopus compressus var. itirapinensis, commonly known as the Itirapina grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing foliage and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically forms compact mats and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in forest understories and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
