Aster pilosus subsp. parviceps

Aster pilosus subsp.

parviceps, commonly known as the short-headed blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its foliage is covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
