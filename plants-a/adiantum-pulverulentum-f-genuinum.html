Adiantum pulverulentum f. genuinum

Adiantum pulverulentum f. genuinum, commonly known as the powdered maidenhair fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to southern China.

It is characterized by its finely divided, dark green fronds that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it its common name. The plant prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters, typical of the Adiantum genus.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is a sought-after plant in horticulture.
