Adenostyles alpina subsp. macrocephala

Adenostyles alpina subsp.

macrocephala, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The subspecies macrocephala is distinguished by its larger, more prominent flower heads compared to other subspecies of Adenostyles.
