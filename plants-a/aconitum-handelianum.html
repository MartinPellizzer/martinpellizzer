Aconitum handelianum

Aconitum handelianum, commonly known as the Himalayan monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately compound leaves with a distinctive lobed structure, and its stems are typically erect and covered in fine hairs. Aconitum handelianum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows at high altitudes.
