Alchemilla monticola var. subpastoralis

Alchemilla monticola var. subpastoralis, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense, panicle-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
