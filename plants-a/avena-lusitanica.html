Avena lusitanica

Avena lusitanica, commonly known as Portuguese oat, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Avena lusitanica is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity and its potential use in sustainable agriculture.
