Anaphalis flavescens var. flavescens

Anaphalis flavescens var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow owl's clover, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its densely hairy, silver-green foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
