Aristolochia neinhuisii

Aristolochia neinhuisii, commonly known as the Neinhuis's Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, often twisted leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

The genus Aristolochia includes many species with similar morphological features and ecological roles.
