Anthyllis vulneraria proles intercedens

Anthyllis vulneraria proles intercedens, commonly known as the lesser burnet, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces dense clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves and its ability to thrive in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
