Argythamnia desertorum

Argythamnia desertorum, commonly known as the desert mallows, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss in harsh environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.

Argythamnia desertorum plays an important role in stabilizing desert soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
