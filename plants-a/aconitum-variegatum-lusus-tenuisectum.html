Aconitum variegatum lusus tenuisectum

Aconitum variegatum lusus tenuisectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved monkshood, is a rare and striking member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark blue to purple flowers, which are arranged in a helmet-like shape on tall, sparsely branched stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
