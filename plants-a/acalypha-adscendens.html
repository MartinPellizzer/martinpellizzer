Acalypha adscendens

Acalypha adscendens, commonly known as the red veined plant or velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins that contrast beautifully with its dark green background. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by clusters of orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant in many regions.
