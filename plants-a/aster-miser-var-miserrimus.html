Aster miser var. miserrimus

Aster miser var. miserrimus, commonly known as the dismal aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its small, purple to blue flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in disturbed or wetland habitats.
