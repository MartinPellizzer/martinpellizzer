Astragalus jaldiyanensis

Astragalus jaldiyanensis, commonly known as the Jaldiyan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. It has compound leaves and produces pods that contain small, round seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
