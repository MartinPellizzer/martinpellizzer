Achyranthes montana

Achyranthes montana, commonly known as mountain smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with erect, branched stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in pink to purplish hues, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a smooth or slightly hairy texture.

The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
