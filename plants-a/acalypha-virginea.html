Acalypha virginea

Acalypha virginea, commonly known as the red-hot catshrub, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble cat whiskers, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers clustered within the bracts. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Acalypha virginea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
