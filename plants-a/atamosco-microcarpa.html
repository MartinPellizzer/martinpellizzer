Atamosco microcarpa

Atamosco microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited atamosco, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves with three leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The fruit is a small legume pod containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
