Artemisia caerulescens var. latifolia

Artemisia caerulescens var. latifolia, commonly known as blue snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong, camphor-like scent. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and herbal medicine.
