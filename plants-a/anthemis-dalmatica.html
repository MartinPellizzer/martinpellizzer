Anthemis dalmatica

Anthemis dalmatica, commonly known as Dalmatian tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are aromatic and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
