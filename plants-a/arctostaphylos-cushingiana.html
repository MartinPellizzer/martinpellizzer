Arctostaphylos cushingiana

Arctostaphylos cushingiana, commonly known as Cushing's manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its reddish-barked stems and dense clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, coastal climates.

Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss.
