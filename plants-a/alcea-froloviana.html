Alcea froloviana

Alcea froloviana, commonly known as the Siberian hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Alcea froloviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
