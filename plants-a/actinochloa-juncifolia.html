Actinochloa juncifolia

Actinochloa juncifolia, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of spikelets. The plant typically grows in marshy or aquatic environments, often forming dense vegetation in shallow water. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Actinochloa juncifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
