Areca montana

Areca montana, commonly known as the mountain areca, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. Areca montana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
