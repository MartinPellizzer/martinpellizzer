Aloe ramosa

Aloe ramosa, commonly known as the tree aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern and can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of its stems. Aloe ramosa is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive drought conditions.

Its resilience and unique appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
