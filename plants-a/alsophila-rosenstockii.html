Alsophila rosenstockii

Alsophila rosenstockii, commonly known as the Rosentrock's tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its fronds, which are essential for its reproductive cycle. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in primary forests.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
