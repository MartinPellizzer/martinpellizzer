Aponogeton elongatus subsp. fluitans

Aponogeton elongatus subsp.

fluitans, commonly known as the floating arrowhead, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its elongated, arrow-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface. It originates from Southeast Asia and is adapted to slow-moving or still freshwater environments.

The plant's rhizomes are often submerged, anchoring it to the substrate while its leaves remain buoyant. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of aquatic conditions.

This species is popular in ponds and water gardens due to its ornamental appearance and ease of cultivation.
