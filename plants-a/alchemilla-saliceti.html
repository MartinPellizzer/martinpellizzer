Alchemilla saliceti

Alchemilla saliceti, commonly known as willow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, soft, and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically yellow or greenish-yellow, which are followed by distinctive cup-shaped seed heads. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Alchemilla saliceti is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
