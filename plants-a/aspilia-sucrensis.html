Aspilia sucrensis

Aspilia sucrensis, commonly known as the Sucrensis torchflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its striking, cone-like inflorescence. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
