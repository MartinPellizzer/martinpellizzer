Ardisia guttata

Ardisia guttata, commonly known as the spotted ardisia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive red or purple spots. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in umbels. Following flowering, it develops bright red berries that are attractive to birds.

Ardisia guttata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
