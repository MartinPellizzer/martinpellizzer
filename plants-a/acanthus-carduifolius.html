Acanthus carduifolius

Acanthus carduifolius, commonly known as the carduifolius acanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins and spikes of showy, tubular flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and floral display.
