Agapetes siangensis

Agapetes siangensis, commonly known as the Siang mountain heather, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly in Myanmar and China. This evergreen shrub typically grows in cool, humid climates at high altitudes. Its foliage consists of small, leathery leaves that are densely packed, giving it a compact appearance.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.
