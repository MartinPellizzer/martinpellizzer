Agrimonia striata subsp. pilosa

Agrimonia striata subsp.

pilosa, commonly known as the hairy agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and serrated leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by reddish-brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

The hairy texture of its stems and leaves gives it its subspecies name, "pilosa.".
