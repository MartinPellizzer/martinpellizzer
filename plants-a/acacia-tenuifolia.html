Acacia tenuifolia

Acacia tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, feathery leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or grey hairs. The plant produces yellow, globular flowerheads that appear in late winter or early spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Acacia tenuifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
