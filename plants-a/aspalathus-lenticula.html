Aspalathus lenticula

Aspalathus lenticula, commonly known as the rooibos plant, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, green, needle-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is well-known for its unique ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it highly adapted to its native environment. Its most notable feature is the production of a caffeine-free herbal tea, which is harvested from its young leaves and stems.

Aspalathus lenticula plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also valued for its economic and medicinal properties.
