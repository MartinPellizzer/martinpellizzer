Astragalus haematosemius

Astragalus haematosemius, commonly known as the blood-red milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep taproots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers. Its flowers typically range in color from pink to red, and it produces small, hairy pods that contain seeds.

Astragalus haematosemius is often found in steppe and desert scrub habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
