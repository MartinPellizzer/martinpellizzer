Abelia grandifolia

Abelia grandifolia, commonly known as the Chinese abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Abelia grandifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
