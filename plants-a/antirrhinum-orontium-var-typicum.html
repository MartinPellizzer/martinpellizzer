Antirrhinum orontium var. typicum

Antirrhinum orontium var. typicum, commonly known as the common snapdragon, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that resemble a dragon's snout. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, producing vibrant red, yellow, or orange flowers. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Antirrhinum orontium var. typicum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens due to its attractive appearance and ease of cultivation.
