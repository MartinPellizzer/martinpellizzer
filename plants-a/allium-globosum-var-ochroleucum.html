Allium globosum var. ochroleucum

Allium globosum var. ochroleucum, commonly known as the white globe onion, is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features a rounded, globe-shaped bulb with pale white skin and a mild, sweet aroma. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are broad, grass-like, and often have a slight bluish tint.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
