Albizia bequaertii

Albizia bequaertii, commonly known as the African siris, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feather-like segments, giving it a delicate appearance. It produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, adding ornamental value to its landscape.

Albizia bequaertii is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is adapted to well-drained soils in warm climates.
