Artemisia neomexicana

Artemisia neomexicana, commonly known as New Mexico wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its strong, camphor-like scent. Its leaves are finely divided and covered in a dense layer of tiny, hair-like structures.

Artemisia neomexicana is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its role in ecological systems.
