Amomum testaceum

Amomum testaceum, commonly known as the yellow cardamom, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, three-lobed fruits. The fruit contains aromatic seeds that are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its essential oils and volatile compounds contribute to its distinctive fragrance and therapeutic properties.
