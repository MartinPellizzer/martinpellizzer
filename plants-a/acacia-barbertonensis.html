Acacia barbertonensis

Acacia barbertonensis, commonly known as the Barberton acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its seed pods are elongated and woody, containing small, flat seeds.

Acacia barbertonensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
