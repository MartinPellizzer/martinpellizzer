Albertinia obtusata

Albertinia obtusata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed albertia, is a South African plant species belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils in regions with a Mediterranean climate. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a relatively rare and localized species.
