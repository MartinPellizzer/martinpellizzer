Agropyron repens var. atherotum

Agropyron repens var. atherotum, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and compact growth habit, which makes it suitable for ornamental and turfgrass applications. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. Its rhizomatous growth system allows it to form dense mats, enhancing its erosion control capabilities.

The leaves are generally narrow and have a distinct, silky texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in landscaped areas.
