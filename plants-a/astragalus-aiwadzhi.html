Astragalus aiwadzhi

Astragalus aiwadzhi, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Astragalus aiwadzhi is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.

Its root system helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.
