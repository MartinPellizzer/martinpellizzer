Achillea oravicensis

Achillea oravicensis, commonly known as the Oravice yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, silver-green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
