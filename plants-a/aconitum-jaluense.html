Aconitum jaluense

Aconitum jaluense, commonly known as the Jalu monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, helmet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, spicate inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
