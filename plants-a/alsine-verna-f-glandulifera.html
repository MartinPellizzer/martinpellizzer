Alsine verna f. glandulifera

Alsine verna f. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular alsike clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often covered with glandular hairs, which give it its specific epithet "glandulifera.".

This species is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.
