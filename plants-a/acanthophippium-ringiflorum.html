Acanthophippium ringiflorum

Acanthophippium ringiflorum, commonly known as the ringed flowering cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Bolivia. This plant is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant's name refers to the distinctive ring-like structures on its stems, which are a key identifying feature.

It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.
