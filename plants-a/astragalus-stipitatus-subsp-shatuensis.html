Astragalus stipitatus subsp. shatuensis

Astragalus stipitatus subsp.

shatuensis, commonly known as the Tibetan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Tibetan Plateau. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant produces pods that contain several hairy seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in high-altitude, arid environments and is adapted to poor, rocky soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and its potential medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
