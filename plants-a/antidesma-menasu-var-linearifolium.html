Antidesma menasu var. linearifolium

Antidesma menasu var. linearifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaved antidesma, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features simple, linear or lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with white or pale yellow petals.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe, which may be edible and attract various wildlife.
