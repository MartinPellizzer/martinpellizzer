Aspilia angusta

Aspilia angusta, commonly known as the narrowleaf torchflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and striking yellow-orange inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its flowers are composed of numerous small ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of disk florets.

Aspilia angusta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils.
