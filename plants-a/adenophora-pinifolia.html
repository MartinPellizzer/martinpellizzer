Adenophora pinifolia

Adenophora pinifolia, commonly known as the needle-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Adenophora pinifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its delicate appearance.
