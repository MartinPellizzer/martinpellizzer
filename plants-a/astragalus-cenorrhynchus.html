Astragalus cenorrhynchus

Astragalus cenorrhynchus, commonly known as the narrowpod milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and the fruit is a slender, curved pod that contains several seeds.

Astragalus cenorrhynchus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators in its native range.
