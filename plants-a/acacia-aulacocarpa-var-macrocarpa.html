Acacia aulacocarpa var. macrocarpa

Acacia aulacocarpa var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and compound leaves composed of numerous small, needle-like phyllodes. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in spring, which are followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
