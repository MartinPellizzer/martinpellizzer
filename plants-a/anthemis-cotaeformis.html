Anthemis cotaeformis

Anthemis cotaeformis, commonly known as the Italian tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small yellow flowers that are typically clustered in flat-topped inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers have been used historically in herbal medicine and as a source of essential oils.
