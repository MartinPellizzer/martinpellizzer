Anthericum vittatum

Anthericum vittatum, commonly known as the spotted lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often spotted or streaked with contrasting colors. The plant produces tall, erect stems that bear umbel-like clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous root system and fibrous roots, which help it thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Anthericum vittatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
