Acarna serratuloides

Acarna serratuloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf acarna, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces seeds in small, dry fruits.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.
