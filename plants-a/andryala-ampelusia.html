Andryala ampelusia

Andryala ampelusia, commonly known as the silver vine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or hanging baskets.

Andryala ampelusia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
