Alchemilla alpina var. vestita

Alchemilla alpina var. vestita, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense, umbrella-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its hirsute (hairy) leaves and frost-resistant nature make it a hardy and ornamental plant in gardens.
