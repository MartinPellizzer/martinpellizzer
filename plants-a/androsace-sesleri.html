Androsace sesleri

Androsace sesleri, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. Androsace sesleri is often used in rock gardens and alpine landscapes due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
