Acalypha amplexicaulis

Acalypha amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping-leaf spurges, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that often resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that often wrap around the stem, giving it a unique appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color.

Acalypha amplexicaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
