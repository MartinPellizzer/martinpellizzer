Agropyron amgunense

Agropyron amgunense, commonly known as the Afgan grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Agropyron amgunense is often used for soil stabilization and has potential for forage in certain ecological contexts.
