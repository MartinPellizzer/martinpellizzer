Alyssum alyssoides f. albineum

Alyssum alyssoides f. albineum, commonly known as the white-flowered alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in early spring. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in fine, silvery hairs, and its leaves are narrow and linear. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
