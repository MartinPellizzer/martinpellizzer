Aristida bromoides

Aristida bromoides, commonly known as slender dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.

Its fine texture and delicate appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
