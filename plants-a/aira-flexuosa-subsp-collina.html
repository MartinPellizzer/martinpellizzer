Aira flexuosa subsp. collina

Aira flexuosa subsp.

collina, commonly known as mountain airgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in cold, high-altitude environments.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight curl, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient component of mountain ecosystems.
