Amaranthus hybridus var. sanguineus

Amaranthus hybridus var. sanguineus, commonly known as red amaranth, is a flowering plant in the amaranth family (Amaranthaceae).

It is characterized by its vibrant red to purple foliage and erect, branching stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge, while the stems are sturdy and fibrous.

This species is native to the Americas but is now cultivated in many parts of the world for its ornamental value and nutritional benefits.
