Acacia oxycedrus var. densiflora

Acacia oxycedrus var. densiflora, commonly known as the Aleppo pine, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which are followed by woody, cone-like structures containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates.

The Aleppo pine plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
