Arundinaria fallax

Arundinaria fallax, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a striking golden-yellow color. This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps, making it a popular choice for ornamental purposes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Arundinaria fallax is also valued for its use in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of material for crafts.
