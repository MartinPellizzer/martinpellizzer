Amomum stenocarpum

Amomum stenocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-carpel cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant produces small, aromatic fruits that contain numerous black, peppery seeds. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a distinctive, fragrant aroma.

It is often cultivated for its aromatic seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
