Athyrium × mentiens

Athyrium × mentiens, commonly known as the Japanese painted fern, is a hybrid fern species resulting from the cross between Athyrium niponicum and Athyrium filix-femina.

It is characterized by its striking, bipinnate fronds that display a gradient of colors, typically green to silvery-white, creating a visually appealing appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. Its fertile fronds produce sporangia that release spores, facilitating reproduction through sexual means.

Athyrium × mentiens is a popular choice for woodland gardens due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
