Athyrium filix-femina var. dombeyi

Athyrium filix-femina var. dombeyi, commonly known as the Dombey's lady fern, is a varietal form of the common lady fern.

It is a deciduous fern characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that typically grow in a graceful, arching manner. The plant features a short, stout rhizome with hairy nodes, and its leaves are usually dark green with a slightly glossy appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
