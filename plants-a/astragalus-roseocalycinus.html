Astragalus roseocalycinus

Astragalus roseocalycinus, commonly known as the rose-colored milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive rose-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its roots are known to contain various bioactive compounds, contributing to its traditional medicinal uses.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its vibrant floral display.
