Alpinia paludosa

Alpinia paludosa, commonly known as the marsh ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in spikes, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in swampy areas with high humidity.

Alpinia paludosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
