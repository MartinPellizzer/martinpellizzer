Acer campestre f. acrocarpum

Acer campestre f. acrocarpum, commonly known as the dwarf maple, is a compact variant of the field maple.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves that are typically oval to heart-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a specimen plant.

Its slow growth rate and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
