Agrostis coromandeliana

Agrostis coromandeliana, commonly known as the Coromandel grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides, fields, and coastal regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and can tolerate a range of environmental conditions.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in certain regions.
