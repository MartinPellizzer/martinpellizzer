Adenophora divaricata f. manshurica

Adenophora divaricata f. manshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple, during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
