Astragalus dactylocarpus var. hystriops

Astragalus dactylocarpus var. hystriops, commonly known as the horned milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated pods and hairy, branched stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is adapted to thrive in poor, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.
