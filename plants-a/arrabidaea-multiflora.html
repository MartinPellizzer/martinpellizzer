Arrabidaea multiflora

Arrabidaea multiflora, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a woody shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
