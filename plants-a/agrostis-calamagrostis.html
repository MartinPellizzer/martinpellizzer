Agrostis calamagrostis

Agrostis calamagrostis, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along streambanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet or poorly drained soils.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family, which includes numerous economically important grasses.
