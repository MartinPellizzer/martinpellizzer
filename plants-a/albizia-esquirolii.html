Albizia esquirolii

Albizia esquirolii, commonly known as the African silk tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves, which are often trifoliate or with three leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellow or white flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its seed pods are long, slender, and contain several flat, bean-like seeds.

Albizia esquirolii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic foliage and flowering.
