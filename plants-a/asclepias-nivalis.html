Asclepias nivalis

Asclepias nivalis, commonly known as the snow flax, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and hairy stems, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Asclepias nivalis is also a vital host plant for the monarch butterfly, supporting the life cycle of this iconic species.
