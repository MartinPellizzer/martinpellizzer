Agave vera-crucis

Agave vera-crucis, commonly known as the Mexican hat plant, is a species of agave native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears showy, yellow-orange flowers resembling a Mexican hat. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in xeriscaping.
