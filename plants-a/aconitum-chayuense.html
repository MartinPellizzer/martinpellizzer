Aconitum chayuense

Aconitum chayuense, commonly known as the Chayu monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple hooded flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and its stems are often covered in a dense layer of hairs.

Aconitum chayuense contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
