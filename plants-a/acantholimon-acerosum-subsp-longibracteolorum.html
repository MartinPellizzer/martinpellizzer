Acantholimon acerosum subsp. longibracteolorum

Acantholimon acerosum subsp.

longibracteolorum, commonly known as the long-bract euphorbia, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is characterized by its robust, succulent stems and distinctive, elongated bracts that give it its common name.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
