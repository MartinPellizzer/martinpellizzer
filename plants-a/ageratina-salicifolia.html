Ageratina salicifolia

Ageratina salicifolia, commonly known as willowleaf ageratum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, willow-like leaves and produces small, fluffy white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.

Ageratina salicifolia prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden beds and containers.
