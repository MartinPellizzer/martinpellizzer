Apostasia stylidioides

Apostasia stylidioides, commonly known as the slender spathephylla, is a rare and enigmatic orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and possesses a single, narrow, leaf-like structure that resembles a stem. The plant produces a single, elongated, greenish-white inflorescence with small, intricate flowers that are typically less than 1 cm in size. Its floral structure is highly specialized, with modified leaves forming the spathe and a single, elongated sepal that serves as a protective sheath.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Apostasia stylidioides is considered a rare and ecologically significant species within its native range.
