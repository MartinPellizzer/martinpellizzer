Andropogon dealbatus

Andropogon dealbatus, commonly known as switchgrass, is a native North American grass species that thrives in a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. This perennial plant is well adapted to both wet and dry environments, making it a versatile component of native grasslands. Switchgrass is also noted for its ability to produce a large amount of biomass, which makes it a valuable resource for biofuel production.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
