Adenogyrus

Adenogyrus, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of velvety, dark green leaves and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in a variety of colors. Adenogyrus species are typically herbaceous, with some varieties growing as small shrubs. They thrive in moist, well-drained soil and prefer indirect sunlight, making them popular houseplants.

The genus includes around 50 species, many of which are widely cultivated for their ornamental value.
