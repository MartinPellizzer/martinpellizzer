Acacia brachystachya

Acacia brachystachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and yellow flower spikes that appear in spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Acacia brachystachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
