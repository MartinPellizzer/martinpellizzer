Astragalus hypsogeton

Astragalus hypsogeton, commonly known as the highland milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in harsh, arid conditions. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus hypsogeton is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited moisture.
