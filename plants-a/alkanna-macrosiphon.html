Alkanna macrosiphon

Alkanna macrosiphon, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, woody stems that exude a reddish resin when cut. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Alkanna macrosiphon is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in dyeing and medicinal applications.
