Althaea hirsuta var. grandiflora

Althaea hirsuta var. grandiflora, commonly known as the woollymallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy foliage and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with a rounded or spreading growth habit. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
