Ansonia elliptica

Ansonia elliptica, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with upright stems and produces clusters of brightly colored blooms in shades of purple, pink, and white. Its leaves are elliptical in shape, hence its scientific name, and are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Ansonia elliptica is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
