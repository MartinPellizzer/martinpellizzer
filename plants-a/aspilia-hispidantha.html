Aspilia hispidantha

Aspilia hispidantha, commonly known as the rough-haired devil's backbone, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, cylindrical flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats, often in well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while the stem is woody and branched.

Aspilia hispidantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
