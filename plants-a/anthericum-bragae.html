Anthericum bragae

Anthericum bragae, commonly known as the Baja California lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Baja California.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of multiple white or pale pink flowers clustered in a dense, umbel-like structure. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils. It blooms during the spring and early summer, adding a distinctive ornamental value to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
