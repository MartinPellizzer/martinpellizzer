Aneilema thomsonii

Aneilema thomsonii, commonly known as the Thomson's arum, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often dark green and glossy. Its flowers are small and clustered around the spadix, with the spathe serving as a colorful, showy structure to attract pollinators.

Aneilema thomsonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in moist, well-drained soils.
