Astragalus episcopus var. lancearius

Astragalus episcopus var. lancearius, commonly known as the lanceleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through its root nodules.

This variety is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.
