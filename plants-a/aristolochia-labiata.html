Aristolochia labiata

Aristolochia labiata, commonly known as the broadleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and unique, tubular flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, twisting flower structure that attracts specific pollinators. Its vines can grow up to 10 feet long and are often found in woodland habitats.

However, it contains toxic compounds and is considered invasive in some regions.
