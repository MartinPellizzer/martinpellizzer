Acacia enervia

Acacia enervia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing on rocky soils. Acacia enervia has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in harsh, drought-prone environments.

Its flowers are important for pollinators and contribute to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
