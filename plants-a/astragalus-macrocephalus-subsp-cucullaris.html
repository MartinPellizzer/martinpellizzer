Astragalus macrocephalus subsp. cucullaris

Astragalus macrocephalus subsp.

cucullaris, commonly known as the hooded milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems that often bear clusters of small, yellowish to brownish flowers.

The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ecological significance and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated for conservation or traditional uses.
