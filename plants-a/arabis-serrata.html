Arabis serrata

Arabis serrata, commonly known as the serrated rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or coastal areas. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have serrated edges, giving it its common name.

Arabis serrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
