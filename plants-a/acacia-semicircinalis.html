Acacia semicircinalis

Acacia semicircinalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and birds.

Its seeds are often used in traditional Aboriginal practices and for ornamental purposes.
