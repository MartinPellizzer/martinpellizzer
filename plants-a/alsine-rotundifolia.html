Alsine rotundifolia

Alsine rotundifolia, commonly known as roundleaf sandparsley, is a low-growing annual plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.

The species is also known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions.
