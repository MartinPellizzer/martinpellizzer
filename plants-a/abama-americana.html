Abama americana

Abama americana, commonly known as the American bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Abama americana is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
