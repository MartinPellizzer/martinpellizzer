Aster lautus var. prionoides

Aster lautus var. prionoides, commonly known as the Narrowleaf Blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as grasslands and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in a flat cluster.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
