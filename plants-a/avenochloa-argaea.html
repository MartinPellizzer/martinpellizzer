Avenochloa argaea

Avenochloa argaea, commonly known as the wild barleygrass, is a perennial grass species native to Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green hue.

Avenochloa argaea is valued for its potential in ecological restoration and as a forage plant for livestock.
