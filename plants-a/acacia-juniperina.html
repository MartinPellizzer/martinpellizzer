Acacia juniperina

Acacia juniperina, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding insects and birds. Acacia juniperina is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
