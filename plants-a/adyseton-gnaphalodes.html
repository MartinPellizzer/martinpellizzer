Adyseton gnaphalodes

Adyseton gnaphalodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, aromatic leaves and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
