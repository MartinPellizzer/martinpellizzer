Aurantium × bigarella

Aurantium × bigarella, commonly known as the citron orange, is a hybrid citrus plant resulting from the cross between the pomelo (Citrus maxima) and the mandarin orange (Citrus reticulata).

It is characterized by its large, thick-skinned fruit that is typically yellow to orange in color when mature. The plant is evergreen, with glossy, dark green leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

The fruit is often used for its high vitamin C content and is also valued in traditional medicine.
