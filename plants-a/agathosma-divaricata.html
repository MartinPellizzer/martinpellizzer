Agathosma divaricata

Agathosma divaricata, commonly known as Cape geranium, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 cm and has aromatic, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal gardens.

Its essential oils are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
