Alchemilla elisabethae

Alchemilla elisabethae, commonly known as the Elizabeth's Lady's Mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage and delicate, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, slightly acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and long-lasting flowers, which are often used in dried floral arrangements.

Alchemilla elisabethae is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine, though its primary appeal lies in its horticultural beauty.
