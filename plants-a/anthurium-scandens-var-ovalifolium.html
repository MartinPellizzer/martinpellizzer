Anthurium scandens var. ovalifolium

Anthurium scandens var. ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaf climbing anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that often have a waxy texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are modified leaves that enclose the true flowers, typically in shades of red or yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to climb or trail when grown in suitable conditions.
