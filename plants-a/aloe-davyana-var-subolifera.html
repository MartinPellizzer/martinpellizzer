Aloe davyana var. subolifera

Aloe davyana var. subolifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features rosettes of narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow-orange flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
