Androsace septentrionalis subsp. puberulenta

Androsace septentrionalis subsp.

puberulenta, commonly known as the northern sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and arctic regions of Europe and Asia. It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies of high-altitude ecosystems.
