Aerides multiflora var. veitchii

Aerides multiflora var. veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
