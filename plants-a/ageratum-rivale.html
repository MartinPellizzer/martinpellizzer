Ageratum rivale

Ageratum rivale, commonly known as the water ageratum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, purple, and pink. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is typically gray-green with a slightly fuzzy texture, adding visual interest to gardens.

Ageratum rivale is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
