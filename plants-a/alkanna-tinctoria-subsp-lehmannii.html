Alkanna tinctoria subsp. lehmannii

Alkanna tinctoria subsp.

lehmannii, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red dye plant, is a species native to arid regions of Central and South America. It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-orange roots, which contain a rich source of natural dye compounds.

The plant typically grows as a low shrub with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its roots are harvested for their vibrant pigments used in traditional dyes and medicinal applications.

The subspecies lehmannii is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and slightly different morphological features compared to the main species.
