Antirrhinum majus var. fallax

Antirrhinum majus var. fallax, commonly known as the fallax snapdragon, is a variant of the common snapdragon characterized by its unique floral morphology.

It typically features elongated, downward-facing flowers with a distinctive two-lipped structure, which is a hallmark of the genus Antirrhinum. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is often cultivated for its vibrant flower colors, which range from red to yellow and orange.
