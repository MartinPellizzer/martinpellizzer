Astragalus farakulumensis

Astragalus farakulumensis, commonly known as the Farakulum milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale purple. Native to specific regions in Central Asia, this species thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivores.
