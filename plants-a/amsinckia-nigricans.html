Amsinckia nigricans

Amsinckia nigricans, commonly known as the blackleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in chaparral and coastal sage scrub ecosystems.

Amsinckia nigricans is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and has some ornamental value due to its unique appearance.
