Asarum maximum

Asarum maximum, commonly known as giant wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a dense, woolly pubescence. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Asarum maximum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
