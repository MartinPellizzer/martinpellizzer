Anoplanthus luteus

Anoplanthus luteus, commonly known as the yellow panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets that are often tinged with yellow, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species.

Anoplanthus luteus is often used in ornamental gardening and as a forage plant for livestock.
