Aster henryi

Aster henryi, commonly known as Henry's aster, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster henryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
