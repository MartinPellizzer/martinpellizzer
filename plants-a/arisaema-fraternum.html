Arisaema fraternum

Arisaema fraternum, commonly known as the Chinese cobra lily, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy surface.

The spadix produces small, greenish flowers that are pollinated by insects, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
