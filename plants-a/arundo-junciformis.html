Arundo junciformis

Arundo junciformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense stands along water edges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like flower clusters. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist environments.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and useful in erosion control.
