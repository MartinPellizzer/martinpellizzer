Artemisia laciniata f. multifida

Artemisia laciniata f. multifida, commonly known as the finely divided artemisia, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features finely dissected, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are typically clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic foliage, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
