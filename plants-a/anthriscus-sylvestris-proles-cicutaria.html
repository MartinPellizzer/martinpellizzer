Anthriscus sylvestris proles cicutaria

Anthriscus sylvestris proles cicutaria, commonly known as hedge garlic, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, umbel-shaped clusters of white or greenish flowers. The plant produces small, round, black seeds that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

Its aromatic foliage and historical use in medicine make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
