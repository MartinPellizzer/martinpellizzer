Aspalathus cinerascens

Aspalathus cinerascens, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

Aspalathus cinerascens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
