Alternanthera lappulacea

Alternanthera lappulacea, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a silvery appearance, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and adaptation.
