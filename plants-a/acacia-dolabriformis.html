Acacia dolabriformis

Acacia dolabriformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and yellow, globular flower heads. It produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acacia dolabriformis is well adapted to drought conditions and plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils.
