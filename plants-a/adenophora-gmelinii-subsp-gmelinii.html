Adenophora gmelinii subsp. gmelinii

Adenophora gmelinii subsp.

gmelinii, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the colder regions of Asia. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.

The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often appearing in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
