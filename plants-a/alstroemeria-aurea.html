Alstroemeria aurea

Alstroemeria aurea, commonly known as the golden alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves and produces clusters of blooms along its stems. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

Alstroemeria aurea prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for various garden settings.
