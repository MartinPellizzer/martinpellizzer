Azalea lactea

Azalea lactea, commonly known as the white azalea, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its delicate blooms.

Azalea lactea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in temperate climates.
