Adoxa moschatellina subsp. cescae

Adoxa moschatellina subsp.

cescae, commonly known as the sweet flag or lesser sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant features slender, often reddish stems that are covered with small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in dense, greenish-white inflorescences, and it produces small, aromatic fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
