Angelica triquinata

Angelica triquinata, commonly known as the Chinese angelica or Dong quai, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic, often hollow stems and compound leaves. The plant is notable for its thick, fleshy roots, which are the primary part used in traditional medicine. Angelica triquinata typically grows in moist, temperate climates and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Its flowers, which appear in umbels, are usually greenish-yellow and attract various pollinators.
