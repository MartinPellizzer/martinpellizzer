Agoseris lanulosa

Agoseris lanulosa, commonly known as the woolly goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Agoseris lanulosa is noted for its soft, woolly texture, which gives it its common name.
