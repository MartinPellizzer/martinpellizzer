Arctostaphylos glandulosa var. zacaensis

Arctostaphylos glandulosa var. zacaensis, commonly known as the Zaca manzanita, is a shrub native to California's coastal regions.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and smooth, reddish bark. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically blooming in white or pink hues. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild temperatures and moderate rainfall.

Zaca manzanita plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
