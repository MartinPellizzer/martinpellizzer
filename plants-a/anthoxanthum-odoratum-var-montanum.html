Anthoxanthum odoratum var. montanum

Anthoxanthum odoratum var. montanum, commonly known as mountain sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale green to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, and it is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in floral arrangements.
