Artocarpus blancoi

Artocarpus blancoi, commonly known as the Philippine native breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fruit. The fruit, which is typically green when mature, turns yellow or orange when ripe and has a fibrous, starchy pulp. It is valued for its edible fruit, which is used in traditional dishes and for making bread.

The tree also plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
