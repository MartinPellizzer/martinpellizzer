Avenochloa aetolica

Avenochloa aetolica, commonly known as the Aetolian millet, is a small grain plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, oval seeds that are typically brown or gray in color. It is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient crop in regions with limited water availability.

Due to its historical significance and potential for sustainable agriculture, it has garnered interest among botanists and agricultural researchers.
