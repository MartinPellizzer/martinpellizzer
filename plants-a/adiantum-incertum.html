Adiantum incertum

Adiantum incertum, commonly known as the uncertain maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Adiantaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore cases called sorus, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. Adiantum incertum is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in greenhouses and as a houseplant.
