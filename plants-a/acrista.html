Acrista

Acrista, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to southern Africa. The plants are typically herbaceous, with opposite, linear leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. Acrista species often grow in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens.
