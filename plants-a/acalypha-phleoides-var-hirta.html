Acalypha phleoides var. hirta

Acalypha phleoides var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered inflorescences that range in color from yellow to red. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a hairy texture that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
