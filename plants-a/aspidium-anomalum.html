Aspidium anomalum

Aspidium anomalum, commonly known as the irregular water fern, is a fern species native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, irregularly shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its fronds are pinnately divided and often have a wavy or undulating margin.

Aspidium anomalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
