Aster venosus

Aster venosus, commonly known as the veined aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers with purple to pink petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, visible veins, giving it its common name.

Aster venosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
