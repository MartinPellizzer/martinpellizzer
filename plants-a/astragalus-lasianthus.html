Astragalus lasianthus

Astragalus lasianthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

Astragalus lasianthus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
