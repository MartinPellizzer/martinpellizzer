Atriplex buchananii

Atriplex buchananii, commonly known as Buchanan's saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it an important component of saltbush habitats.

Its ability to tolerate extreme drought conditions makes it a valuable plant for stabilizing soils in harsh environments.
