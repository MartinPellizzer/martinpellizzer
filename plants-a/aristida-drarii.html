Aristida drarii

Aristida drarii, commonly known as the desert dropseed, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor itself in loose, sandy soils and access water from deeper layers.

Aristida drarii plays a vital role in stabilizing desert ecosystems and providing forage for wildlife.
