Alchemilla natalensis f. bakeri

Alchemilla natalensis f. bakeri, commonly known as the Natal lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
