Alsine michauxii var. cymosa

Alsine michauxii var. cymosa, commonly known as the clustered alsike, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, which includes many flowering plants with showy blooms. The plant features small, white to pink flowers that are clustered in dense racemes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern at the base.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woods or along streambanks.
