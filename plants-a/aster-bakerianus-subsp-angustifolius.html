Aster bakerianus subsp. angustifolius

Aster bakerianus subsp.

angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aster, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowering heads that bloom in late summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family, with potential ornamental and ecological value.
