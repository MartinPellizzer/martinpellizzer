Abbotia filiformis

Abbotia filiformis, commonly known as the slender abbotia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. These flowers are borne on short pedicels and are often described as resembling tiny bells.

Abbotia filiformis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
