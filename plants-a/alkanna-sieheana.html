Alkanna sieheana

Alkanna sieheana, commonly known as the red sandalwood or alkanna, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Alkanna sieheana thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

Its unique coloration and drought tolerance make it a distinctive feature of its native desert ecosystems.
