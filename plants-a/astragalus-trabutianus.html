Astragalus trabutianus

Astragalus trabutianus, commonly known as the Trabut's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.

However, it is relatively rare and faces threats from habitat loss and over-collection.
