Acacia foliosa

Acacia foliosa, commonly known as the feathery wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Acacia foliosa is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots and a low water requirement.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
