Amorphophallus stuhlmannii

Amorphophallus stuhlmannii, commonly known as the giant waterleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant produces a striking, spadix-like inflorescence that can grow up to 3 meters tall, often with a strong odor resembling rotting meat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water sources.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated for its ornamental value.
