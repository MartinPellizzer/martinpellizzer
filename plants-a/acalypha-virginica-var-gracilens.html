Acalypha virginica var. gracilens

Acalypha virginica var. gracilens, commonly known as slender coralplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of greenish-white flowers, which are surrounded by bracts that give it a coral-like appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
