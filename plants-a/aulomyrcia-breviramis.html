Aulomyrcia breviramis

Aulomyrcia breviramis, commonly known as the short-armed bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to South America, particularly Brazil, it thrives in moist, tropical environments. The plant is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it forms dense stands.

Its rapid growth and ability to form clumps make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
