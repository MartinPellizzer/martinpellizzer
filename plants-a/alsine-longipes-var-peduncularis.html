Alsine longipes var. peduncularis

Alsine longipes var. peduncularis, commonly known as the slender alsine, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

This variant is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.
