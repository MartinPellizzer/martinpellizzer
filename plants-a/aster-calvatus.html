Aster calvatus

Aster calvatus, commonly known as the roughleaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with rough textures. The plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white to purple, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. Aster calvatus thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

It is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides.
