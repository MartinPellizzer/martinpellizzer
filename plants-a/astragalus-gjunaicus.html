Astragalus gjunaicus

Astragalus gjunaicus, commonly known as the Gjunaic milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, enabling it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus gjunaicus is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to fully understand its therapeutic uses.
