Agropyron turczaninowii var. tenuisetum

Agropyron turczaninowii var. tenuisetum, commonly known as the slender-stemmed wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the cold and arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous slender spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

This variety is often used for erosion control and as a forage plant in semi-arid regions.
