Acer craibianum

Acer craibianum, commonly known as the Craib's maple, is a rare and little-studied species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with shallow furrows that become more pronounced with age.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Acer craibianum is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
