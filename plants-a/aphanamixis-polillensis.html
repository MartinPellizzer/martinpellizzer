Aphanamixis polillensis

Aphanamixis polillensis, commonly known as the Philippine tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for its unique fruit, which is a woody pod containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
