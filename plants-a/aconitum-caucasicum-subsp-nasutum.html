Aconitum caucasicum subsp. nasutum

Aconitum caucasicum subsp.

nasutum, commonly known as the Caucasian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple hood-shaped flowers.

The plant has dark green, palmately lobed leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
