Alsine leucocephala

Alsine leucocephala, commonly known as white sand alsine, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It has a low-growing habit and forms dense mats, making it a common component of dryland vegetation.

Its ability to thrive in harsh environments makes it an important species in ecological studies of desert ecosystems.
