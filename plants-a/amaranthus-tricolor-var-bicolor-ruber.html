Amaranthus tricolor var. bicolor-ruber

Amaranthus tricolor var. bicolor-ruber, commonly known as red-leaf amaranth, is a vibrant annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored leaves that range from green to red and purple, often displaying a bicolor pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into fluffy, three-angled seed capsules. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to withstand drought conditions contribute to its adaptability in various climates.
