Alopecurus glaucus

Alopecurus glaucus, commonly known as bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and silver-blue hue, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single, dense spikelet that produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Alopecurus glaucus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in forage and erosion control.
