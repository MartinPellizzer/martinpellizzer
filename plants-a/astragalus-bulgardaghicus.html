Astragalus bulgardaghicus

Astragalus bulgardaghicus, commonly known as the Bulgardagh milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Astragalus bulgardaghicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native range.
