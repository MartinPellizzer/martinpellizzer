Arundinaria minomarsa

Arundinaria minomarsa, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Arundinaria minomarsa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional landscaping and paper-making.
