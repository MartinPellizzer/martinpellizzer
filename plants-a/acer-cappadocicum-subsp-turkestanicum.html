Acer cappadocicum subsp. turkestanicum

Acer cappadocicum subsp.

turkestanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan maple, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves, which are typically five-lobed and have a serrated margin.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
