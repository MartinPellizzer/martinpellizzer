Asphodeline tenuior

Asphodeline tenuior, commonly known as the narrowleaf asphodel, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Asphodeline tenuior is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
