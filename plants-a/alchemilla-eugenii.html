Alchemilla eugenii

Alchemilla eugenii, commonly known as the Hungarian lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Alchemilla eugenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
