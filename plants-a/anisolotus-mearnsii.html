Anisolotus mearnsii

Anisolotus mearnsii, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in horticultural settings.
