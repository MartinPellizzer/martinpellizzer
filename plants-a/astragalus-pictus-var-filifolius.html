Astragalus pictus var. filifolius

Astragalus pictus var. filifolius, commonly known as the filifolius milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry climates. It produces small, often yellow or greenish, pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and drought tolerance.
