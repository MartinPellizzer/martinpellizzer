Apargidium

Apargidium, commonly known as the false lily of the valley, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asparagaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, often branched stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette or along the stem. Apargidium species are often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their delicate floral display.
