Armeria mauritanica var. chamaeropicola

Armeria mauritanica var. chamaeropicola, commonly known as the dwarf sea thrift, is a low-growing, evergreen perennial native to the coastal regions of North Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring and early summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to withstand salty air and coastal conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking hardy, drought-tolerant plants.
