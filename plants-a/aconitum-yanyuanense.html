Aconitum yanyuanense

Aconitum yanyuanense, commonly known as the Yanyuan monkshood, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
