Aiouea tambillensis

Aiouea tambillensis, commonly known as the Madagascan ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood. The plant has simple, alternate, elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, dark purple fruits.

Aiouea tambillensis is valued for its hard, durable timber and is often found in dry, rocky habitats across the island.
