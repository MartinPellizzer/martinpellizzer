Azalea indica narcissiflora

Azalea indica narcissiflora, commonly known as the golden azalea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rhododendron genus.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soil. It produces small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
