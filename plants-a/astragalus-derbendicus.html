Astragalus derbendicus

Astragalus derbendicus, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. Astragalus derbendicus is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.
