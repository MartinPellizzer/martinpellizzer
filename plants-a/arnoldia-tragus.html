Arnoldia tragus

Arnoldia tragus, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers with prominent spurred petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Arnoldia tragus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
