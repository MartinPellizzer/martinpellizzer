Arenaria aucheriana

Arenaria aucheriana, commonly known as the sand pink, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soils in rocky or sandy landscapes.
