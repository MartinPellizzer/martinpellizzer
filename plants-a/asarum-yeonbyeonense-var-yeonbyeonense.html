Asarum yeonbyeonense var. yeonbyeonense

Asarum yeonbyeonense var. yeonbyeonense, commonly known as Korean wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Korea.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in a spathaceous sheath. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rhizomatous root system makes it a hardy and adaptable species.
