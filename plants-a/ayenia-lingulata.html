Ayenia lingulata

Ayenia lingulata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ayenia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in pine flatwoods and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Ayenia lingulata is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native gardens.
