Acaciella rekoi

Acaciella rekoi, commonly known as the Koa tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in coastal and lowland habitats, often growing in areas with well-drained soil.

Due to habitat loss and historical overharvesting, Acaciella rekoi is considered a vulnerable species.
