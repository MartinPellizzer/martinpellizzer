Astragalus djadjerudensis var. pilosus

Astragalus djadjerudensis var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has finely hairy foliage, which gives it its varietal name "pilosus.". It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
