Allium geyeri var. tenerum

Allium geyeri var. tenerum, commonly known as the Sierra onion or woolly garlic, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of gray-green, woolly leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice among gardeners interested in native plants.
