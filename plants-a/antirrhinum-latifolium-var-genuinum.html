Antirrhinum latifolium var. genuinum

Antirrhinum latifolium var. genuinum, commonly known as the broad-leaved snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and tall, branching stems that bear clusters of bright red or orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and long flowering period.

The species is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
