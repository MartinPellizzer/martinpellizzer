Apium dissectum var. typicum

Apium dissectum var. typicum, commonly known as the common celery.

It is a biennial or annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features a hollow, ridged stem and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

The plant is valued for its edible leaves and stalks, which are used in cooking and traditional medicine.
