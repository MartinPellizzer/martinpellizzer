Astragalus torreyi

Astragalus torreyi, commonly known as Torrey's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus torreyi plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
