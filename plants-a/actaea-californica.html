Actaea californica

Actaea californica, commonly known as California bugbane, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features tall, erect stems that bear clusters of white, lacecap flowers in late summer. The plant has large, palmately compound leaves that are deeply lobed and often hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a striking addition to native plant gardens.
