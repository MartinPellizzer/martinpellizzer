Arabidopsis petrogena

Arabidopsis petrogena, commonly known as the desert mustard, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and broccoli. This species is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and narrow, linear leaves adapted to conserve water. Arabidopsis petrogena is often found growing in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Its ability to thrive in harsh environments makes it an important species for studying plant adaptations to drought and extreme temperatures.
