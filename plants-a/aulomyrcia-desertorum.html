Aulomyrcia desertorum

Aulomyrcia desertorum, commonly known as the desert sandbush, is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Aulomyrcia desertorum plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
