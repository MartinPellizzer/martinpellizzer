Aeonium × tahodiense

Aeonium × tahodiense, commonly known as the "Tahodiense Aeonium," is a hybrid succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern with thick, fleshy leaves. The plant's leaves are typically blue-green in color, often with a powdery coating that helps reduce water loss. It produces vibrant yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in inflorescences at the center of the rosette.

Aeonium × tahodiense is well-suited for rock gardens and arid environments due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
