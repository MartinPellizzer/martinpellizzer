Arisaema multisectum

Arisaema multisectum, commonly known as the multilobe arisaema, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and possesses palmately divided, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it produces berries that contain toxic seeds.

Arisaema multisectum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
