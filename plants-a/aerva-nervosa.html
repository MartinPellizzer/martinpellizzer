Aerva nervosa

Aerva nervosa, commonly known as slender Indian plantain, is a flowering plant native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and alternate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically borne in the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Aerva nervosa is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
