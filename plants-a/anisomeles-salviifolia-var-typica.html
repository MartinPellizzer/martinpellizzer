Anisomeles salviifolia var. typica

Anisomeles salviifolia var. typica, commonly known as the silverleaf strobilanthes, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in the summer. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a velvety texture. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance once established.
