Appendicula rubens

Appendicula rubens, commonly known as the red appendicula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Appendicula rubens thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
