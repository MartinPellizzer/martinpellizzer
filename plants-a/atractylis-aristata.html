Atractylis aristata

Atractylis aristata, commonly known as the velvetleaf goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Atractylis aristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
