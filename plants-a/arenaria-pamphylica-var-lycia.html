Arenaria pamphylica var. lycia

Arenaria pamphylica var. lycia, commonly known as the Lycian sandpink, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in well-drained environments. The plant features small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapting to the arid conditions of its habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging landscapes.
