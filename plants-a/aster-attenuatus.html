Aster attenuatus

Aster attenuatus, commonly known as the slender aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

The slender aster is an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
