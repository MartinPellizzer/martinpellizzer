Astrocaryum gymnopus

Astrocaryum gymnopus, commonly known as the Brazilian wax palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and produces clusters of yellowish flowers followed by edible fruits. The plant has a solitary trunk and can grow up to 20 meters in height. Its fruit, which is roughly the size of a chicken egg, contains a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, yellow aril.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
