Andropogon retzii

Andropogon retzii, commonly known as switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in prairies and disturbed areas. Switchgrass is known for its resilience and is used in ecological restoration and as a bioenergy crop.

Its flowering spikes produce small, pale green to brown florets that are wind-pollinated.
