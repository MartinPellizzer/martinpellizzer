Alsine montana var. caucasica

Alsine montana var. caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian alsike, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It typically features small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
