Armeria ruscinonensis var. ciliata

Armeria ruscinonensis var. ciliata, commonly known as the Ciliate Sea Thrift, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing foliage and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has succulent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to salt spray and coastal conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and resilience in challenging environments.
