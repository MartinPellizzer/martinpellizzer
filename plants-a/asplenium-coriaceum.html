Asplenium coriaceum

Asplenium coriaceum, commonly known as the tough spleenwort, is a fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, evergreen epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in moist, shaded environments. The plant has lance-shaped, leathery leaves that are dark green and often have a rough texture. Its spore-producing structures, called soruses, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium coriaceum is well adapted to humid climates and is often found in temperate regions across the Northern Hemisphere.
