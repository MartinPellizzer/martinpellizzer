Amomum aculeatum var. gymnocarpum

Amomum aculeatum var. gymnocarpum, commonly known as the smooth-fruited cardamom, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, finger-like rhizomes. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elliptical, smooth-sepaled fruits. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a rough, serrated margin.

This variety is valued for its essential oils and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
