Aquilegia vulgaris var. ottonis

Aquilegia vulgaris var. ottonis, commonly known as the Otto's columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features striking, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple, with red and yellow markings. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are palmately arranged, and it thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. It is typically found in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky areas, preferring partial to full shade.

The botanical characteristics of this variety make it a popular choice for garden cultivation and ecological restoration projects.
