Albuca narcissifolia

Albuca narcissifolia, commonly known as the star of Bethlehem, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that resemble those of the narcissus. The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral display.

Albuca narcissifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
