Argemone munita var. robusta

Argemone munita var. robusta, commonly known as the Mexican prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers with prominent red stamens. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
