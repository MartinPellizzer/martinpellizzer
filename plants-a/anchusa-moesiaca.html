Anchusa moesiaca

Anchusa moesiaca, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Anchusa moesiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
