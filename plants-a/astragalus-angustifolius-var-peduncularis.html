Astragalus angustifolius var. peduncularis

Astragalus angustifolius var. peduncularis, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. The plant features slender, branching stems with alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and it produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
