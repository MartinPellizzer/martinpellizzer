Argyrolobium linnaeanum subsp. stipulaceum

Argyrolobium linnaeanum subsp.

stipulaceum, commonly known as the silverleaf flax or silverleaf bush, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

The plant has small, narrow leaves with a silvery or pale green underside, giving it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by elongated pods containing seeds.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
