Anthoxanthum odoratum var. pubiculmis

Anthoxanthum odoratum var. pubiculmis, commonly known as the pubescent sweetgrass, is a variant of the sweetgrass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and a distinctive, sweet, honey-like aroma when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to yellowish flowers.

The variety pubiculmis is distinguished by its pubescent (hairy) leaves, which give it a unique texture and appearance.
