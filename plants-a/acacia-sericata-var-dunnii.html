Acacia sericata var. dunnii

Acacia sericata var. dunnii, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, slender, and silky-haired leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The wood of Acacia sericata var. dunnii is hard and durable, historically used for construction and tool-making.
