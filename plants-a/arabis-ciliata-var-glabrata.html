Arabis ciliata var. glabrata

Arabis ciliata var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features basal leaves that are smooth and lance-shaped, with small, white, cruciform flowers that bloom in early spring. It is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

This variety is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a decorative plant in alpine settings.
