Arisaema acuminatum

Arisaema acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
