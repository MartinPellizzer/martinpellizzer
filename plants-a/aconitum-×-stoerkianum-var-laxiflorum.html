Aconitum × stoerkianum var. laxiflorum

Aconitum × stoerkianum var. laxiflorum, commonly known as the laxiflorous monkshood, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers, which are typical of the genus Aconitum. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and planted away from areas where children or pets may access it.
