Alsophila alata

Alsophila alata, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a silvery-blue hue on the underside, contributing to its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Alsophila alata plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
