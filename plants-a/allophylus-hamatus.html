Allophylus hamatus

Allophylus hamatus, commonly known as the knotted sycamore, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, knotted stem and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Allophylus hamatus is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appearance and traditional uses.
