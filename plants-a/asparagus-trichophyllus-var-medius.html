Asparagus trichophyllus var. medius

Asparagus trichophyllus var. medius, commonly known as the Middle Asparagus, is a variant of the asparagus plant characterized by its unique foliage and growth habits.

It features feathery, trifoliate leaves that are often more pronounced than those of the standard species. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically arranged in umbel-like clusters. Its stems are generally more robust and less spiny compared to other asparagus varieties.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic contribution.
