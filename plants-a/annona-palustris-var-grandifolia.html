Annona palustris var. grandifolia

Annona palustris var. grandifolia, commonly known as the swamp custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in wet, swampy areas. The plant produces large, showy white or pale yellow flowers with a strong fragrance. Its fruit is a fleshy, greenish-yellow berry that contains numerous seeds.

The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a slightly rough texture.
