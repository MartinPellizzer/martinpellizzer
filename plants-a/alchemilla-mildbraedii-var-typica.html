Alchemilla mildbraedii var. typica

Alchemilla mildbraedii var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in dense, shield-like clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage is commonly used in herbal remedies for its purported soothing properties.
