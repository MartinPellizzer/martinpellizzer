Artocarpus longifolius subsp. longifolius

Artocarpus longifolius subsp.

longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaf breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length.

The tree produces a large, spiky fruit that is typically harvested when ripe for its starchy, edible pulp. The fruit has a sweet, nutty flavor and is often used in traditional dishes.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
