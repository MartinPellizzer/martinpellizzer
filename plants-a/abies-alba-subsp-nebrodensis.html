Abies alba subsp. nebrodensis

Abies alba subsp.

nebrodensis, commonly known as the Nebroden fir, is a subspecies of the European silver fir. It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in the Alps and the Apennines.

This evergreen conifer has needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The cones are usually pendulous and release their seeds in the autumn.

It is adapted to grow in cooler, moist environments with well-drained soils.
