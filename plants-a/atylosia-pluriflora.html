Atylosia pluriflora

Atylosia pluriflora, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its flowering stems are upright and branched, bearing numerous small, composite flowers.

Atylosia pluriflora thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
