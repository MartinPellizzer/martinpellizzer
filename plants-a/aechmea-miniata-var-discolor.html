Aechmea miniata var. discolor

Aechmea miniata var. discolor, commonly known as the silver-tipped bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage that forms a central cup to collect rainwater. The leaves are typically narrow and dark green with a silvery or white midrib, giving it its distinctive appearance. It produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This plant is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
