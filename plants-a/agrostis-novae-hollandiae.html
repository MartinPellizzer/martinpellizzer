Agrostis novae-hollandiae

Agrostis novae-hollandiae, commonly known as the Australian bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and coastal regions. It has small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
