Aster molliusculus var. molliusculus

Aster molliusculus var. molliusculus, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its blue to violet flowers and hairy stems, which help it retain moisture in dry conditions. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
