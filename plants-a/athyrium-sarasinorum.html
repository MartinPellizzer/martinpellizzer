Athyrium sarasinorum

Athyrium sarasinorum, commonly known as the Sarasin's fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, compound fronds that are typically bipinnate. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions with high humidity. Its spores are released from specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds.

The botanical features of Athyrium sarasinorum make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
