Argentina victorialis

Argentina victorialis, commonly known as the Argentine grass, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered near the base.

Argentina victorialis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
