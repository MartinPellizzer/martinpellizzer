Amaranthus rigidus

Amaranthus rigidus, commonly known as rigid amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green to red in color. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Amaranthus rigidus is also valued for its potential use in erosion control and as a source of edible seeds.
