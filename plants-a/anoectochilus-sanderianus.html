Anoectochilus sanderianus

Anoectochilus sanderianus, commonly known as the Sander's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique slipper-shaped pouch, which is a distinctive feature of the Orchidaceae family. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires specific conditions to thrive. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

Anoectochilus sanderianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
