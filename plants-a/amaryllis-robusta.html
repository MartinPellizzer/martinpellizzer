Amaryllis robusta

Amaryllis robusta, commonly known as the robust amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped blooms, which are often adorned with contrasting white or pink markings. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its dramatic appearance.

Amaryllis robusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens or as a houseplant.
