Astragalus viscidus

Astragalus viscidus, commonly known as sticky milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to greenish flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky environments.

Astragalus viscidus is often found in sagebrush steppe and other dry, open habitats.
