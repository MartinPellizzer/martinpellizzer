Ardisia demissa var. bambusetorum

Ardisia demissa var. bambusetorum, commonly known as the bamboo-leaf ardisia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, resembling the appearance of bamboo leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its foliage and unique fruiting habit.
