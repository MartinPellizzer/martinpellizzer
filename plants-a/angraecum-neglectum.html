Angraecum neglectum

Angraecum neglectum, commonly known as the Christmas orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom during the holiday season. The plant produces pendulous, white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive, intricate structure. It is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Angraecum neglectum is prized for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators like moths and bats.
