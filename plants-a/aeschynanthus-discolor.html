Aeschynanthus discolor

Aeschynanthus discolor, commonly known as the violet flower vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters along its stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Aeschynanthus discolor is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
