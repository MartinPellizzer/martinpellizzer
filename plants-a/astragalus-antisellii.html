Astragalus antisellii

Astragalus antisellii, commonly known as the Anticline milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus antisellii is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
