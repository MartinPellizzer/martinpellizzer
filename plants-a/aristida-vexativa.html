Aristida vexativa

Aristida vexativa, commonly known as the burrgrass or rough bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often covered in bristly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
