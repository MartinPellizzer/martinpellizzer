Allium tulipifolium var. brachystemon

Allium tulipifolium var. brachystemon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features grass-like, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a loose cluster, resembling a cluster of tiny bulbs. It is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, thriving in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its culinary uses, particularly its mild garlic-like flavor, and is also appreciated in ornamental gardening.
