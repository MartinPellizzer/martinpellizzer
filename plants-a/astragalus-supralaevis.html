Astragalus supralaevis

Astragalus supralaevis, commonly known as the western prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus supralaevis is often found in mountainous regions and is considered a perennial species.
