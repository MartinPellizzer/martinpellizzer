Amphiglossa grisea

Amphiglossa grisea, commonly known as the gray-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated lip that is often grayish in color, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in the understory. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pale pink.

Amphiglossa grisea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
