Acer pseudoplatanus f. rubromaculatum

Acer pseudoplatanus f. rubromaculatum, commonly known as the red-spotted maple, is a unique foliage variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its striking leaves that display a red or orange mottling on the upper surface. This form is a natural mutation of the species, often found in specific geographic regions. The leaves are typically oval with a serrated margin and turn vibrant red in the fall.

It is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture and landscaping.
