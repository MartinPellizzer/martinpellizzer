Aegiphila insignis

Aegiphila insignis, commonly known as the Brazilian aster, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. It typically grows in dry, rocky areas and has hairy, aromatic leaves.

Aegiphila insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Morphology

Aegiphila insignis has leaves that are lanceolate in shape, arranged alternately along the stem, and typically range from 2 to 5 centimeters in length, with a dark green color and a slightly hairy surface.

The stem is erect, somewhat woody, with a rough, grayish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, with pale yellow petals and a tubular shape, blooming during the dry season and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.
