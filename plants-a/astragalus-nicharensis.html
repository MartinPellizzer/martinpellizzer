Astragalus nicharensis

Astragalus nicharensis, commonly known as the Nicaraugan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces pods that contain several small, bean-like seeds.

Astragalus nicharensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
