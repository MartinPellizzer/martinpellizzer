Astragalus exscapus var. puskarovii

Astragalus exscapus var. puskarovii, commonly known as the Puskarov's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
