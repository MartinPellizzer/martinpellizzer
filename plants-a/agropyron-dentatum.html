Agropyron dentatum

Agropyron dentatum, commonly known as the prairie grass or spike grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and stiff, leafy blades with a rough texture. The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered in dense inflorescences, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is commonly found in prairie and steppe ecosystems.

Agropyron dentatum is valued for its erosion control capabilities and is often used in restoration projects.
