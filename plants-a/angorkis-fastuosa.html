Angorkis fastuosa

Angorkis fastuosa, commonly known as the elegant orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often feature vibrant colors such as pink, purple, and white. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically broad and elliptical, with a smooth texture and a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Angorkis fastuosa is prized by horticulturists for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
