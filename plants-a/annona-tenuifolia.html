Annona tenuifolia

Annona tenuifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pawpaw, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small, shrub-like tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit, which resembles a small papaya, is edible and has a sweet, tropical flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
