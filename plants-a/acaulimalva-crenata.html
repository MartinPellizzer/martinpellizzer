Acaulimalva crenata

Acaulimalva crenata, commonly known as the hairy willow herb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have wavy edges, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Acaulimalva crenata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.
