Anthericum praemorsum

Anthericum praemorsum, commonly known as the death camas, is a toxic plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, white, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow berries that are highly poisonous to both humans and animals. Due to its toxicity, it is important to avoid ingestion or contact with this plant.
