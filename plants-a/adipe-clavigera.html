Adipe clavigera

Adipe clavigera, commonly known as the fat-tailed orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, fat-like appendages on its stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Its roots are thick and adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in humid, shaded environments.

Adipe clavigera is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
