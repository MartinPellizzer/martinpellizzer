Aster orarius

Aster orarius, commonly known as the southern aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grassy areas, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its showy purple to pink flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Aster orarius is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant fall color and ecological benefits.
