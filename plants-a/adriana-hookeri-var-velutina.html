Adriana hookeri var. velutina

Adriana hookeri var. velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf adriana, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and prefers well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a sought-after species among botanists and plant enthusiasts.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered endangered in its native range.
