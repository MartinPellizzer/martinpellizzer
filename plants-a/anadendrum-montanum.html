Anadendrum montanum

Anadendrum montanum, commonly known as mountain anadendron, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in woodlands and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, pink to red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Anadendrum montanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
