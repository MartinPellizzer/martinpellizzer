Acacia fulgens

Acacia fulgens, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in spherical heads and bloom in spring. The plant has slender, bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Acacia fulgens is an important species in Australian ecosystems, providing food and habitat for various native animals.
