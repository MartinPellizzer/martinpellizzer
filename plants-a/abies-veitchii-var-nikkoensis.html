Abies veitchii var. nikkoensis

Abies veitchii var. nikkoensis, commonly known as the Nikko fir, is a variant of the Veitch fir native to Japan.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The foliage is typically arranged in two ranks, giving the tree a distinctive appearance. It produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.
