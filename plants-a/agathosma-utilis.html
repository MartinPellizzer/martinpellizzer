Agathosma utilis

Agathosma utilis, commonly known as African geranium, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or sandy environments. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

Agathosma utilis is also used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
