Aloe leedalii

Aloe leedalii, commonly known as the Cape aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick, fleshy, and covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces striking yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on tall, branching stems. Aloe leedalii is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive drought conditions.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
