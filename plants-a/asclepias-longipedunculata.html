Asclepias longipedunculata

Asclepias longipedunculata, commonly known as the long peduncled milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pale greenish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that contain silky-haired seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. It is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its role in supporting monarch butterfly populations.

Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.
