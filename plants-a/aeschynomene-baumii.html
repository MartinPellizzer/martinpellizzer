Aeschynomene baumii

Aeschynomene baumii, commonly known as the African horsebean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
