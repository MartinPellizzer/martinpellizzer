Asperula beckiana

Asperula beckiana, commonly known as the Beck's woodruff, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its small, dark green leaves and fragrant, white to pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Asperula beckiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
