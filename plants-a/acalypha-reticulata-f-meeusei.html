Acalypha reticulata f. meeusei

Acalypha reticulata f. meeusei, commonly known as the Meeuse's nettleleaf, is a compact shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves with a distinctive reticulate pattern, giving it a finely veined appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
