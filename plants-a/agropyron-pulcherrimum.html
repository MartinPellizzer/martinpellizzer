Agropyron pulcherrimum

Agropyron pulcherrimum, commonly known as the beautiful wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces spikelets that are often adorned with long, soft hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Agropyron pulcherrimum is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and its use in landscaping and erosion control.
