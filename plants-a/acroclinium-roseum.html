Acroclinium roseum

Acroclinium roseum, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to lavender-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in sandy, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Acroclinium roseum is often found in coastal dunes and open woodlands, showcasing its adaptability to coastal environments.
