Adenocalymma hatschbachii

Adenocalymma hatschbachii, commonly known as the Cape York rock fig, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces edible, round fruits that are typically consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
