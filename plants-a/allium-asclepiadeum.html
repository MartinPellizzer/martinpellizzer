Allium asclepiadeum

Allium asclepiadeum, commonly known as the giant bulb onion, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, bulbous stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly hairy, forming a basal rosette.

Allium asclepiadeum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible bulbs.
