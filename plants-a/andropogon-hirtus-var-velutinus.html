Andropogon hirtus var. velutinus

Andropogon hirtus var. velutinus, commonly known as velvet bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that give it a velvety texture, especially on the leaves and stems. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often a deep green, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences that appear in late summer.

Velvet bluestem plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
