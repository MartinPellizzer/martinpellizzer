Arnicratea

Arnicratea, commonly known as the arnica plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its hairy, yellow-orange flowers and rough, hairy leaves. Native to Europe, Asia, and North America, it thrives in meadows and grasslands. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

It is often used in herbal remedies for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
