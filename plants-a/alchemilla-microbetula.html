Alchemilla microbetula

Alchemilla microbetula, commonly known as lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in upright clusters during late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their astringent properties.

Alchemilla microbetula thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
