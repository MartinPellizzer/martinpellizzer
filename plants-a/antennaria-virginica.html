Antennaria virginica

Antennaria virginica, commonly known as the Virginia spring beauty, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, showy flowers in the early spring. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens.

The plant thrives in moist, wooded areas and is an important early bloom for pollinators.
