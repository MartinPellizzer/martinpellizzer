Amomum spurium

Amomum spurium, commonly known as the fragrant ginger or wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and are typically greenish-white with a subtle fragrance.

The rhizomes of Amomum spurium are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications, similar to other members of its family.
