Amorphophallus reflexus

Amorphophallus reflexus, commonly known as the red ginger or red-veined arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins and its large, showy inflorescence that resembles a giant red flower. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes many ornamental and economically important species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value.

Amorphophallus reflexus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
