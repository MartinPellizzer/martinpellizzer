Acer pictum var. parviflorum

Acer pictum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the dwarf sweetgum, is a small deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of yellow and red in the fall. The plant produces small, spherical fruit clusters that contain spiny seeds, typical of the Acer genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for smaller gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for landscape use.
