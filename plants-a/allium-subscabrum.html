Allium subscabrum

Allium subscabrum, commonly known as the rough leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces upright, cylindrical flower stalks that bear small, white to pale pink flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor, making it a useful plant in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
