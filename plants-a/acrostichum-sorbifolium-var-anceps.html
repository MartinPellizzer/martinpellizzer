Acrostichum sorbifolium var. anceps

Acrostichum sorbifolium var. anceps, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fern, is a variant of the broad-leaf fern native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, upright clump. The plant produces small, clustered spore cases on the underside of its fronds, which are essential for its reproductive cycle. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its foliage provides both ornamental value and habitat for various small organisms.
