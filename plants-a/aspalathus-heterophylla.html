Aspalathus heterophylla

Aspalathus heterophylla, commonly known as the African red bush, is a shrub native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, reddish foliage and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers and wet winters. It is widely cultivated for its leaves, which are used in the production of rooibos tea.

Aspalathus heterophylla plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various insects and birds.
