Astragalus jagnobicus var. shtivensis

Astragalus jagnobicus var. shtivensis, commonly known as the Shtivenskaya milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep purple. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and its potential medicinal properties.
