Acacia lapidosa

Acacia lapidosa, commonly known as the rock acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Its wood is hard and durable, historically used by indigenous communities for various purposes.
