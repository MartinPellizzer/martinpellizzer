Alchemilla hoppeana subsp. anisiaca

Alchemilla hoppeana subsp.

anisiaca, commonly known as the velvetleaf lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It features basal rosettes of soft, velvety, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in summer, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
