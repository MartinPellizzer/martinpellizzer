Archidendron ellipticum subsp. subcordifoliolatus

Archidendron ellipticum subsp.

subcordifoliolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf legume, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its elliptic, subcordate leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
