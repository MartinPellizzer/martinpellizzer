Asplenium argutum

Asplenium argutum, commonly known as the serrated spleenwort, is a species of fern that belongs to the Aspleniaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often serrated along the edges. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rock crevices or on tree trunks. Its fronds are usually dark green and can vary in length, with the sterile fronds being shorter than the fertile ones.

Asplenium argutum is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various conditions.
