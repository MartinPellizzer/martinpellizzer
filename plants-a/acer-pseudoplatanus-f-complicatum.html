Acer pseudoplatanus f. complicatum

Acer pseudoplatanus f. complicatum, commonly known as the curly-leaf maple, is a unique cultivar of the common maple tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, twisted, and often curling leaves that give it a decorative appearance. The plant is native to the British Isles and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks. Its foliage changes color in the autumn, displaying shades of red and orange, adding to its visual appeal.

The tree typically grows to a moderate height and is well-suited for smaller landscapes due to its compact size.
