Acacia provincialis

Acacia provincialis, commonly known as the Cape ash, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in cylindrical inflorescences. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater. Its bark is smooth and greyish, often peeling in thin layers, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Acacia provincialis plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
