Actinostemon concolor var. platyphyllos

Actinostemon concolor var. platyphyllos, commonly known as the broad-leafed actinostemon, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spikelike inflorescences, typically in shades of green or red. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This variant is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to the typical form of Actinostemon concolor.
