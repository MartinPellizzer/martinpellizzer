Auricula glutinosa

Auricula glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky auricula, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family, characterized by its bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces showy, violet to blue flowers with a distinctive sticky secretion on the petals. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
