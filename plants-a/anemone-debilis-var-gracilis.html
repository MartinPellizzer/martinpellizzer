Anemone debilis var. gracilis

Anemone debilis var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender anemone, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, nodding flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are hairy and emit a mild, spicy scent. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for shade gardens and naturalized landscapes.
