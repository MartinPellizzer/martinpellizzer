Artemisia obtusiloba subsp. altaiensis

Artemisia obtusiloba subsp.

altaiensis, commonly known as Altai wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and small, yellowish-green flower heads that appear in the summer.

The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments. Its leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its strong, medicinal scent.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
