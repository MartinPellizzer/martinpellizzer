Anthericum nguluense

Anthericum nguluense, commonly known as the Nguluense arum, is a herbaceous plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking inflorescence. The plant produces a spathe that encloses a spadix, typical of arum species, which is often colorful and attracts pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties, though its botanical uses remain under investigation.
