Ageratum gaumeri f. fallax

Ageratum gaumeri f. fallax, commonly known as the fallax ageratum, is a compact, bushy annual plant native to Mexico.

It features showy, clustered flowers in shades of white, violet, and blue, which bloom from late summer to early fall. The foliage is typically gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
