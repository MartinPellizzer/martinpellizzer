Aquilegia formosa var. pauciflora

Aquilegia formosa var. pauciflora, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada columbine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features showy, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and forested areas.

This variety is noted for its relatively fewer flowers compared to other forms of Aquilegia formosa.
