Arum engleri

Arum engleri, commonly known as the Engler's arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces a starchy tuberous root that is used as a food source in some regions. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a glossy surface.

Arum engleri is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticultural settings.
