Argyreia capitiformis

Argyreia capitiformis, commonly known as the sacred lotus of India, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and traditional uses.
