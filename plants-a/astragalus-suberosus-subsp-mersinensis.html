Astragalus suberosus subsp. mersinensis

Astragalus suberosus subsp.

mersinensis, commonly known as the Mersin hairy milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Turkey. It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
