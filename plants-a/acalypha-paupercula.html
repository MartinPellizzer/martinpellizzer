Acalypha paupercula

Acalypha paupercula, commonly known as the poor man's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
