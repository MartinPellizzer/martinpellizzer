Amaryllis × herbertii

Amaryllis × herbertii, commonly known as the Herbert's amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis paradisicola.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with strap-like leaves that are broad and fleshy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and as a potted plant.

Its blooming period occurs in late summer to early autumn, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
