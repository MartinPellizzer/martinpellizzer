Adenophora morrisonensis subsp. morrisonensis

Adenophora morrisonensis subsp.

morrisonensis, commonly known as Morrison's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China. It typically grows in rocky or forested habitats at high elevations.

The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive yellow throat. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
