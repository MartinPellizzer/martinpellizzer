Alchemilla wischniewskii

Alchemilla wischniewskii, commonly known as the Wischniewsky's lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in upright clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
