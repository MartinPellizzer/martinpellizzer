Aconitum × clairvilleanum

Aconitum × clairvilleanum, commonly known as the hybrid aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Aconitum, which includes various species of monkshood and wolfsbane. The plant is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive palmate shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Aconitum × clairvilleanum is valued in horticulture for its striking appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
