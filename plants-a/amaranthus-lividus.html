Amaranthus lividus

Amaranthus lividus, commonly known as the purple amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple foliage and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in warm climates and well-drained soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional cuisines, while the seeds are a source of nutrition.

Amaranthus lividus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to drought conditions.
