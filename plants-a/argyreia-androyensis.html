Argyreia androyensis

Argyreia androyensis, commonly known as the Madagascar morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant produces violet to blue berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
