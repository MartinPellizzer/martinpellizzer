Armeniaca ansu

Armeniaca ansu, commonly known as the Chinese date or jujube, is a deciduous fruit-bearing shrub or small tree native to China.

It produces large, sweet, yellowish-red fruits that are rich in sugars, vitamins, and minerals. The plant thrives in warm climates and well-drained soils, often growing in temperate regions. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a rough texture.

The fruit is typically harvested in late summer to early autumn and is widely used in traditional Chinese medicine and culinary applications.
