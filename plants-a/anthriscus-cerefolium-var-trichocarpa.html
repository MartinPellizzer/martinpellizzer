Anthriscus cerefolium var. trichocarpa

Anthriscus cerefolium var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the hairy cow parsley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild aromatic and digestive properties.
