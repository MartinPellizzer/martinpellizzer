Aster alpinus var. brachyglossus

Aster alpinus var. brachyglossus, commonly known as the Alpine aster, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with blue to purple petals and a yellow center, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, hardy plants.
