Aralia ehrenbergii

Aralia ehrenbergii, commonly known as the white aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound, feather-like leaves that are often tinged with white or pale green, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, which are followed by bright red berries that attract birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araliaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
