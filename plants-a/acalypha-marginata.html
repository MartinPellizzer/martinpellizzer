Acalypha marginata

Acalypha marginata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-veined plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, red-veined leaves that are often variegated with green and white. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Acalypha marginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
