Aporum papilio

Aporum papilio, commonly known as the butterfly orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant features slender, erect stems and produces delicate, butterfly-shaped flowers in shades of white and purple. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Aporum papilio is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
