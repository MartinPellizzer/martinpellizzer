Acroptilon picris var. oxylepis

Acroptilon picris var. oxylepis, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its seeds are enclosed in pods, a typical feature of the Fabaceae family, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acroptilon picris var. oxylepis is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides, where it contributes to soil stabilization and biodiversity.
