Agave heteracantha var. vittata

Agave heteracantha var. vittata, commonly known as the striped agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are often lined with white or yellowish stripes. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk with small, greenish-white flowers, which appear after several years of growth. Its leaves are typically bluish-green with a waxy coating, and it is well adapted to arid environments.

This species is popular in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
