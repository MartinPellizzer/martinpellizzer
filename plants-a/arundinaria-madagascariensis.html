Arundinaria madagascariensis

Arundinaria madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically green and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional crafts in its native region.
