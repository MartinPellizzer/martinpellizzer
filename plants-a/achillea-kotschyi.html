Achillea kotschyi

Achillea kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine.

The showy white to pale yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
