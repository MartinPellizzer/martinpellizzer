Acacia howittii

Acacia howittii, commonly known as the Howitt's wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow, globular inflorescences that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is well adapted to drought and can thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Acacia howittii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat in its native environment.
