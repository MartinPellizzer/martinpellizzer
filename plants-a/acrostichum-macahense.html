Acrostichum macahense

Acrostichum macahense, commonly known as the giant reed or golden reed, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a tall, grass-like plant that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring long, narrow leaves with a distinctive golden hue on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it an important species in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
