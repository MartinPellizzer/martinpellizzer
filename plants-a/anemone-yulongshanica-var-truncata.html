Anemone yulongshanica var. truncata

Anemone yulongshanica var. truncata, commonly known as the Yulongshan anemone, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, palmate flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are deeply lobed, and the stems are often hairy.

The variety truncata is distinguished by its truncated or notched leaf margins, adding to its ornamental appeal.
