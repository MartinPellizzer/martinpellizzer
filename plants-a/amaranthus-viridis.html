Amaranthus viridis

Amaranthus viridis, commonly known as green amaranth, is an annual herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually light green to blue-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from green to reddish-brown, depending on the stage of development. It is often cultivated as a leaf vegetable in many parts of the world due to its nutritional value and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Amaranthus viridis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
