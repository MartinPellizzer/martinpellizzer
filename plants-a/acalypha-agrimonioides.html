Acalypha agrimonioides

Acalypha agrimonioides, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous true flowers surrounded by these showy bracts. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Acalypha agrimonioides is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
