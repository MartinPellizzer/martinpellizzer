Allium grandisceptrum

Allium grandisceptrum, commonly known as the giant bulb onion, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, bulbous stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces clusters of white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, often reaching several feet in length.

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is valued for its ornamental qualities and culinary uses.
