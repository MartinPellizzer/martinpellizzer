Adromischus pachylophus

Adromischus pachylophus, commonly known as the fat-tailed ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, white flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Adromischus pachylophus requires minimal water and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
