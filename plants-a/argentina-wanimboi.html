Argentina wanimboi

Argentina wanimboi, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, orange-red berries that mature in the winter. The plant has smooth, reddish bark and can grow up to 15 meters tall. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its invasive nature, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
