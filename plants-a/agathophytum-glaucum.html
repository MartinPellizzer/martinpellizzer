Agathophytum glaucum

Agathophytum glaucum, commonly known as the blue agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and a rosette of narrow, bluish-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears small, greenish-white flowers, which are followed by the development of its characteristic blue, fibrous leaves. Agathophytum glaucum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and striking appearance.

It plays a role in traditional uses, including the production of agave-based products.
