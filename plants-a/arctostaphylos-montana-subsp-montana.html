Arctostaphylos montana subsp. montana

Arctostaphylos montana subsp.

montana, commonly known as the Sierra foothill manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and reddish-brown bark that peels in thin layers.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is often found in chaparral and woodland ecosystems, showing resilience to drought and fire.
