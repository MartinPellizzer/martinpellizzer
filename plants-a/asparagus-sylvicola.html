Asparagus sylvicola

Asparagus sylvicola, commonly known as the wild asparagus or mountain asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, typically blooming in spring. Its berries are red or purple and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Asparagus sylvicola is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
