Aira littoralis

Aira littoralis, commonly known as saltgrass, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. This species is adapted to saline and windy environments, often found in dunes and tidal flats. Its root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Aira littoralis plays an important role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat and forage for various wildlife.
