Astragalus gomboeformis var. amblyocarpus

Astragalus gomboeformis var. amblyocarpus, commonly known as the narrow-carpel milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring. Its fruit is a narrow, elongated pod that contains several seeds.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological significance and potential medicinal properties.
