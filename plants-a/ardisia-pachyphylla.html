Ardisia pachyphylla

Ardisia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved beautyberry, is a member of the Myrsinaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, thick leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by clusters of bright purple berries, which are a notable feature. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its attractive foliage and berries.
