Anacamptis pyramidalis var. orientalis

Anacamptis pyramidalis var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental pyramidal orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of linear, grass-like leaves and produces tall, erect flowering stems that bear a dense cluster of violet or purple flowers. The inflorescence is characterized by its pyramidal shape, with flowers arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
