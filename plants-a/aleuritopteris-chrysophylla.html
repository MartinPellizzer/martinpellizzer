Aleuritopteris chrysophylla

Aleuritopteris chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, bright yellow to golden-green fronds that are often divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Aleuritopteris chrysophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
