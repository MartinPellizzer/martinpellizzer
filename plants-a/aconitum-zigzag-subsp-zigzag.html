Aconitum zigzag subsp. zigzag

Aconitum zigzag subsp.

zigzag, commonly known as the zigzag monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers arranged in tall, spire-like inflorescences.

The plant features palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive zigzag pattern along the leaf veins, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care and avoid ingestion.
