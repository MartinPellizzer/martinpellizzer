Arundinaria laotica

Arundinaria laotica, commonly known as the Laotian bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can reach heights of up to 5 meters. The plant forms dense clumps and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along riverbanks. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Arundinaria laotica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its ecological benefits in local ecosystems.
