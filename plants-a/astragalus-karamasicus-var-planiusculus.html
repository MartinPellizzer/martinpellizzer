Astragalus karamasicus var. planiusculus

Astragalus karamasicus var. planiusculus, commonly known as the flatleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
