Aster micromaackii

Aster micromaackii, commonly known as the smallflower aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, bogs, and stream banks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale purple. It blooms from late summer to early fall, and its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in wetland ecosystems.
