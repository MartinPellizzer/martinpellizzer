Antirrhinum coulterianum var. appendiculatum

Antirrhinum coulterianum var. appendiculatum, commonly known as the Coulter's snapdragon, is a rare and specialized plant native to specific regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand drought and poor soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

This variant is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique morphological features.
