Aster lanceolatus subsp. simplex

Aster lanceolatus subsp.

simplex, commonly known as the eastern blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
