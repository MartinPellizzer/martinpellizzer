Aesculus pavia var. flavescens

Aesculus pavia var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow horse chestnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, upright clusters of orange-red flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are compound, consisting of 5 to 9 elliptical leaflets with a pointed tip. The plant produces smooth, spiny fruits that contain one or two seeds each.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
