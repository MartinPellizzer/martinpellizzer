Aechmea fusca

Aechmea fusca, commonly known as the rusty-tipped air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The leaves are typically green with reddish or purplish tips, giving it its common name. It produces striking inflorescences with bright red or orange flowers that bloom in the spring.

This plant is well adapted to grow in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forests or on rocky outcrops.

Morphology

Aechmea fusca has leaves that are sword-shaped, arranged in a rosette, with a waxy, dark green surface and a sharp, pointed tip.

The plant has a short, thick stem covered in a dense, fibrous bark that is typically brown in color. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, with small, pale pink flowers that bloom during the summer months and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

The roots are fibrous and form a dense network near the soil surface, helping to anchor the plant and absorb nutrients.
