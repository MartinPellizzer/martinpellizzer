Aesculus × marylandica

Aesculus × marylandica, commonly known as the horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets. The tree produces showy, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during spring, followed by spiny, palmate fruits that contain bright red seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.
