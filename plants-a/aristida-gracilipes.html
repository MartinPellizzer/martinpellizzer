Aristida gracilipes

Aristida gracilipes, commonly known as slender beardgrass, is a species of grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender inflorescence that resembles a beard, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

Aristida gracilipes is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in challenging conditions.
