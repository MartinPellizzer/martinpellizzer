Asplenium dentatum var. jamaicense

Asplenium dentatum var. jamaicense, commonly known as the Jamaican spleenwort, is a fern native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica.

It is a small, evergreen fern with a rosette of narrow, dark green, toothed leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are leathery and have a distinctive serrated margin, which is a key identifying feature.

The species is part of the Aspleniaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
