Aspalathus versicolor

Aspalathus versicolor, commonly known as the variable pea bush, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Aspalathus versicolor is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
