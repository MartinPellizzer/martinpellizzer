Asperula flaccida

Asperula flaccida, commonly known as the creeping azalea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Asperula flaccida thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
