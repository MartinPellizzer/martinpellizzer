Acalypha discolor

Acalypha discolor, commonly known as the variegated iron plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring shades of green, yellow, and white on its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive leaves.

Acalypha discolor is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
