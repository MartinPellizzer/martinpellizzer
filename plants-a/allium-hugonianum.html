Allium hugonianum

Allium hugonianum, commonly known as the giant bulbous onion, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, bulbous underground stem and tall, narrow leaves. The plant produces showy, umbel-like clusters of white to pink flowers in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful for culinary purposes.
