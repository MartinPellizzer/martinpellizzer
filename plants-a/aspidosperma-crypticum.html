Aspidosperma crypticum

Aspidosperma crypticum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceriferia*, is a species of tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red fruits containing several seeds.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
