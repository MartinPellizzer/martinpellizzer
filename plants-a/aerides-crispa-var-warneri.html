Aerides crispa var. warneri

Aerides crispa var. warneri, commonly known as the Warner's crested vanda, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, long, wavy petals and a distinctive crest-like pattern on its sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, warm environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, with a faintly colored lip that adds to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
