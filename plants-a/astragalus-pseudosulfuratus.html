Astragalus pseudosulfuratus

Astragalus pseudosulfuratus, commonly known as the sulfur-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Astragalus pseudosulfuratus plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
