Ardisia xylosteoides

Ardisia xylosteoides, commonly known as the purple-leafed ardisia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn a striking purplish-red color during the winter months. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by bright red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a houseplant.
