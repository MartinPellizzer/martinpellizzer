Astragalus soyeri

Astragalus soyeri, commonly known as the Soyer's milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Astragalus soyeri is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various pollinators and small herbivores.
