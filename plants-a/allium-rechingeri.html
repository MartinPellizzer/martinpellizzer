Allium rechingeri

Allium rechingeri, commonly known as the Armenian garlic or Rechinger's onion, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant produces spherical bulbs covered in a papery, fibrous tunic, and its leaves are long, grass-like, and often bluish-green in color. It blooms with small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a dense, umbel-like cluster.

This species is valued for its culinary uses and ornamental appeal in gardens.
