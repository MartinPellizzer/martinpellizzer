Aframomum leonense

Aframomum leonense, commonly known as the African cardamom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval, dark blue or black berries. Its leaves are elongated, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive, fragrant scent.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine and as a spice.
