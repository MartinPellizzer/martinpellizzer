Aristida lignosa

Aristida lignosa, commonly known as rigid wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive drought conditions.

Aristida lignosa plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitats.
