Astragalus junceus

Astragalus junceus, commonly known as slender milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets that are often hairy, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus junceus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
