Acacia ovata

Acacia ovata, commonly known as the oval acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular inflorescences, which are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Acacia ovata is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry landscapes.

Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally for medicinal purposes by local communities.
