Afrohybanthus travancoricus

Afrohybanthus travancoricus, commonly known as the Travancore hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in dense forests. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, with five petals. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy surface.

Afrohybanthus travancoricus is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
