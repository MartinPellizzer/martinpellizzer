Achyropappus ambiguus

Achyropappus ambiguus, commonly known as the desert composite or sand mat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, mat-forming perennial with gray-green foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a compact growth habit, often growing close to the ground to conserve moisture. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

The species is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in areas with minimal rainfall.
