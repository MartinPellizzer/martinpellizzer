Amellus rosmarinifolius

Amellus rosmarinifolius, commonly known as rosemary, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, evergreen leaves that are needle-like and covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees. Rosemary is widely cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in cooking and traditional medicine.

Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are valued for their antimicrobial and antioxidant properties.
