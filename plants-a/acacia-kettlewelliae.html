Acacia kettlewelliae

Acacia kettlewelliae, commonly known as the Kettlewell's wattle, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, phyllodes that are typically linear and glabrous. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
