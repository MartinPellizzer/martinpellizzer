Acalypha grandis var. velutina

Acalypha grandis var. velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor plant collections.
