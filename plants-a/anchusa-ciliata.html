Anchusa ciliata

Anchusa ciliata, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Anchusa ciliata is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value.
