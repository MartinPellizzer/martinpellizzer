Artanthe pseudochurumayu

Artanthe pseudochurumayu, commonly known as the Andean artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
