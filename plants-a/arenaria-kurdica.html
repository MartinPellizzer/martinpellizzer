Arenaria kurdica

Arenaria kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Kurdistan.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in rocky and arid environments.

Arenaria kurdica is often found in alpine and subalpine zones, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
