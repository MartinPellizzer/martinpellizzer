Adiantum trifidum

Adiantum trifidum, commonly known as the ruffled maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, ruffled fronds that give it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a graceful, arching growth habit.

Adiantum trifidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
