Aralia octophylla var. cantonensis

Aralia octophylla var. cantonensis, commonly known as the Chinese angel's trumpets, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are palmately divided into eight to ten leaflets. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
