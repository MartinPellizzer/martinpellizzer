Amygdalus simonii

Amygdalus simonii, commonly known as the Chinese peach or Chinese almond, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy pink to white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to peaches but are typically smaller and have a more intense flavor. Amygdalus simonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its fruit, which can be used in traditional Chinese cuisine.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for various climates.
