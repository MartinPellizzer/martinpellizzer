Alpinia trichocalyx

Alpinia trichocalyx, commonly known as the Chinese ginger or wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other spices like ginger and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous growth and has aromatic, pungent leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and are clustered in inflorescences.

The rhizomes are valued for their medicinal properties and are used in both culinary and therapeutic applications.
