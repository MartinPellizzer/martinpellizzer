Astragalus pseudochorinensis

Astragalus pseudochorinensis, commonly known as the pseudochorinensis milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and may have potential medicinal uses, though further research is needed.
