Asystasia bojeriana

Asystasia bojeriana, commonly known as the silverleaf asystasia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, velvety silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Asystasia bojeriana is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a decorative and potentially invasive species in certain regions.
