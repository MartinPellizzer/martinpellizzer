Austrocactus pauxillus

Austrocactus pauxillus, commonly known as the Pauquill cactus, is a species native to the high Andes of southern South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its ribbed stems and spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes.

Austrocactus pauxillus is known for its resilience to cold and arid conditions, making it a hardy plant in suitable climates.
