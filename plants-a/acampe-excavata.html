Acampe excavata

Acampe excavata, commonly known as the African violets, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, often forming a compact rosette structure. It prefers well-drained soil and bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Acampe excavata is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
