Anthyllis montana subsp. atropurpurea

Anthyllis montana subsp.

atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple mountain bean-crop, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive purple flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and is adapted to harsh, alpine conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
