Aldama subdentata

Aldama subdentata, commonly known as the Mexican bristlegrass, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, typical of many grass species. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
