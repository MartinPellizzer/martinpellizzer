Asplenium paucivenosum f. minus

Asplenium paucivenosum f. minus, commonly known as the small-veined maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, finely veined fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The fronds are typically narrow and elongated, with a dark green, leathery texture and a distinctive, intricate venation pattern. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded gardens and indoor settings.

Its growth habit is clump-forming, with rhizomes that allow it to spread slowly over time.
