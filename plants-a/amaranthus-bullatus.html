Amaranthus bullatus

Amaranthus bullatus, commonly known as the purple amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that range in color from purple to red. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Amaranthus bullatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional culinary practices in some regions.
