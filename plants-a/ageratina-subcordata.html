Ageratina subcordata

Ageratina subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Ageratina subcordata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.
