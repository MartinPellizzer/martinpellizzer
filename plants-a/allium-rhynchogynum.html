Allium rhynchogynum

Allium rhynchogynum, commonly known as the narrowleaf garlic.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and the Tian Shan. The plant features a bulbous stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to purple flowers in umbels. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic bulbs used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.
