Althaea officinalis var. pseudoarmeniaca

Althaea officinalis var. pseudoarmeniaca, commonly known as Armenian marshmallow, is a variant of the marshmallow plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their mucilage content, which has soothing properties.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal applications.
