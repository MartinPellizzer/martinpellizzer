Amauropelta bonapartii

Amauropelta bonapartii, commonly known as the Bonaparte's shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures. The plant is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Amauropelta bonapartii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
