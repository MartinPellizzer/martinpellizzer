Acaena trifida var. argentella

Acaena trifida var. argentella, commonly known as silver bramble, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces spherical, woody fruits that contain seeds covered in hooked bristles. It thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
