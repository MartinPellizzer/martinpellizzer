Aeonium × laxiflorum

Aeonium × laxiflorum, commonly known as the "Laxiflorum Aeonium," is a hybrid succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are densely packed and often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring or early summer, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Aeonium × laxiflorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
