Alyssum micropetalum

Alyssum micropetalum, commonly known as the narrow-petaled alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features dense clusters of flowers atop slender stems, and its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy. Alyssum micropetalum thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant due to its ornamental value.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
