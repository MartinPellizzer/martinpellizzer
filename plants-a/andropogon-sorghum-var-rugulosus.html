Andropogon sorghum var. rugulosus

Andropogon sorghum var. rugulosus, commonly known as rough sorghum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its coarse, rough-textured leaves and tall, branched inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to drought conditions.

Its flowers are usually arranged in panicles, and it produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are wind-pollinated.
