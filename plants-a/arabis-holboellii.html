Arabis holboellii

Arabis holboellii, commonly known as the Holboell's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early spring.

The leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention in its arid habitat.
