Acalypha ciliata

Acalypha ciliata, commonly known as the silky mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety appearance, and it produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
