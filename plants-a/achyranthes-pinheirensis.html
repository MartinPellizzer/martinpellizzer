Achyranthes pinheirensis

Achyranthes pinheirensis, commonly known as Brazilian knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. Its roots are fibrous and can spread extensively, making it a potential invasive species in certain regions.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and riverbanks.
