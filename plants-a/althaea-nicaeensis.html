Althaea nicaeensis

Althaea nicaeensis, commonly known as the Cyprus marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pale pink to white flowers. The plant produces a mucilaginous sap, which is used in traditional medicine for its soothing properties. It thrives in wet, marshy habitats and is often found near freshwater sources.

Althaea nicaeensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native environment.
