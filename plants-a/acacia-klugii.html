Acacia klugii

Acacia klugii, commonly known as the Klug's acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its slender, branching structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in spherical heads, which are typical of the Acacia genus. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets.

Acacia klugii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
