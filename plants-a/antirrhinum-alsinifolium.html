Antirrhinum alsinifolium

Antirrhinum alsinifolium, commonly known as the slender toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
