Alnus × intermedia

Alnus × intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate alder, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between white alder (Alnus rhombifolia) and black alder (Alnus cordata).

It is a deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its compound leaves and catkin-like flowers. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in moist, temperate habitats. It plays a role in soil stabilization due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities through root nodules.

The wood of Alnus × intermedia is lightweight and has been historically used for various crafts and construction purposes.
