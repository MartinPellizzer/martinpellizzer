Agave evadens

Agave evadens, commonly known as the Mexican hat agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a thick, spiky rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears yellow flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardens and arid landscapes.
