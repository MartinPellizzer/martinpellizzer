Annona tomentosa

Annona tomentosa, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant has a rough, hairy texture on its leaves and stems, which gives it its scientific name "tomentosa," meaning "woolly.". Its fruit is characterized by a thick, sweet, creamy pulp with a distinct aroma.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
