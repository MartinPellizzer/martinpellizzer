Acacia adnata

Acacia adnata, commonly known as the ghost gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, pale grey bark that peels in thin strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, globular flowers that bloom in winter, followed by flat, ribbed pods. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feathery leaflets.

Acacia adnata is adapted to arid environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
