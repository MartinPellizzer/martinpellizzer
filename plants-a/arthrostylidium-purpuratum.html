Arthrostylidium purpuratum

Arthrostylidium purpuratum, commonly known as the purple-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to parts of Australia.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that allow it to attach to and draw nutrients from its host tree. The plant typically grows as a shrub with small, opposite leaves and produces small, clustered white or pink flowers. Its berries are usually red or purple and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant impacts on the health of its host plants.
