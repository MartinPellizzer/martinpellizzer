Acaena tenera subsp. pilosella

Acaena tenera subsp.

pilosella, commonly known as the silky burr, is a low-growing, perennial plant native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds.

The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is also known for its ability to form extensive colonies, which can be both ecologically significant and potentially invasive.
