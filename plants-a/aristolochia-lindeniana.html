Aristolochia lindeniana

Aristolochia lindeniana, commonly known as the Lindner's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and striking, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, winding growth habit. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive, wrinkled appearance.

The plant contains aristolochic acids, which are toxic and have historically been used in traditional medicine.
