Andropogon jwarancusa subsp. laniger

Andropogon jwarancusa subsp.

laniger, commonly known as purple bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its distinctive purple tinge on the stems and leaves, which becomes more pronounced as the plant matures.

The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small, spike-like inflorescences.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in ecological restoration projects.
