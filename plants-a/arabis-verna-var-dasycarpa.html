Arabis verna var. dasycarpa

Arabis verna var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or coastal regions. The plant features basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
