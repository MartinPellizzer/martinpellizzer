Alyssum baeticum

Alyssum baeticum, commonly known as the Iberian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Alyssum baeticum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.
