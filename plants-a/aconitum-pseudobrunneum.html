Aconitum pseudobrunneum

Aconitum pseudobrunneum, commonly known as the European monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves and can grow up to 1.5 meters tall. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
