Amaryllis viridiflora

Amaryllis viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered amaryllis, is a striking perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that bear large, showy flowers. The plant produces vibrant, often greenish-white blooms that are highly ornamental. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, emerging from a bulbous underground stem.

Amaryllis viridiflora is prized for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and its resilience to various growing conditions.
