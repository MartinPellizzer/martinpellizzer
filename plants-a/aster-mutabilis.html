Aster mutabilis

Aster mutabilis, commonly known as the New England aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, violet to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its scientific name, *Aster mutabilis*, reflects the changing nature of its flower color as it matures.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
