Acalypha insulana var. anisodonta

Acalypha insulana var. anisodonta, commonly known as the hairy sycamore or island glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy bracts that resemble petals, often appearing in clusters at the ends of branches. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a hairy texture, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
