Arenaria tenuior var. minutiflora

Arenaria tenuior var. minutiflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to arid and rocky habitats.

It features tiny, clustered white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May depending on the region. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value in rock gardens and xeriscapes due to its compact growth and delicate appearance.
