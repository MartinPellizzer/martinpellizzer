Alchemilla hoppeaniformis

Alchemilla hoppeaniformis, commonly known as Hoppe's lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Alchemilla hoppeaniformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
