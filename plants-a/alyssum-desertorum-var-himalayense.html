Alyssum desertorum var. himalayense

Alyssum desertorum var. himalayense, commonly known as Himalayan alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, linear, and covered in fine hairs, adapting to arid and high-altitude environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
