Arenaria gothica subsp. moehringioides

Arenaria gothica subsp.

moehringioides, commonly known as the Moehring's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and coastal environments in Europe. It typically features small, rounded leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant is adapted to harsh conditions, with a compact growth habit that helps it withstand wind and salt exposure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes or limestone areas.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscaping.
