Artemisia macrobotrys

Artemisia macrobotrys, commonly known as the golden thread, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray foliage and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late summer. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its aromatic leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its distinctive scent and have been used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
