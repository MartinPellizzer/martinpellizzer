Amaranthus spinosus f. inermis

Amaranthus spinosus f. inermis, commonly known as the smooth amaranth, is a variant of the spiny amaranth.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas, characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the stage of development. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible gardens.

The leaves and young shoots are edible and can be used in salads or cooked dishes.
