Augusta oblongifolia

Augusta oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy purple to pink flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Augusta oblongifolia is typically found in moist, woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade.
