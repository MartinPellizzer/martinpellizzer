Anisantha diandra var. rigida

Anisantha diandra var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid anisantha, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly rough texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and modest floral display.
