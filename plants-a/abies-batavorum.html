Abies batavorum

Abies batavorum, commonly known as the Batawa fir, is a species of fir native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its conical shape and needle-like leaves. The tree produces cones that mature in the second year, typically releasing seeds in the autumn. Its foliage is dark green and dense, providing habitat for various wildlife species.

The Batawa fir is valued for its timber and is often found in mixed forests at higher elevations.
