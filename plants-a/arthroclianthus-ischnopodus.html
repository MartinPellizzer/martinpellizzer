Arthroclianthus ischnopodus

Arthroclianthus ischnopodus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It has small, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and as a ground cover in dry landscapes.
