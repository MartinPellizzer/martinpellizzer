Aegopodium ternatum

Aegopodium ternatum, commonly known as cow parsley or bishop's weed, is a flowering plant in theApiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small white or greenish flowers that appear in umbels. The plant typically grows in moist, shady areas such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a long flowering period, often from late spring to early autumn.

Its aromatic foliage and flowers make it a notable presence in its native habitats across Europe and parts of Asia.
