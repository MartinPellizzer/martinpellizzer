Abies morinda

Abies morinda, commonly known as the Morinda fir, is a rare and lesser-known conifer native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown in color, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
