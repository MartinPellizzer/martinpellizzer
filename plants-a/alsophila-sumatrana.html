Alsophila sumatrana

Alsophila sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive crown of spirally arranged leaves, which contribute to its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane rainforests.

Alsophila sumatrana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various species.
