Armeriastrum bromifolium

Armeriastrum bromifolium, commonly known as coastal sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming from spring to early summer. It is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing dune habitats.
