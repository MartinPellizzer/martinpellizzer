Amaranthus venulosus

Amaranthus venulosus, commonly known as the veiny amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish or reddish flowers, which are typically borne in terminal spikes. Its seeds are small and often used as a grain in traditional cuisines.

Amaranthus venulosus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in various environmental conditions.
