Asclepias petherickiana

Asclepias petherickiana, commonly known as the Petherick's milkweed, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant features showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Asclepias petherickiana is an important host plant for monarch butterfly larvae and plays a vital role in local ecosystems.
