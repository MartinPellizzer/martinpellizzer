Astragalus rengifoi var. lejocarpa

Astragalus rengifoi var. lejocarpa, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is often found in open woodlands and grasslands at higher elevations.
