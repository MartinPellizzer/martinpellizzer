Anemone polypetala

Anemone polypetala, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, white to pink flowers with five to eight petals, arranged in a cup-like shape. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate along the stem. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

Anemone polypetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
