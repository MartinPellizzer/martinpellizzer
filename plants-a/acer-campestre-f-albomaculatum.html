Acer campestre f. albomaculatum

Acer campestre f. albomaculatum, commonly known as the white-spotted maple, is a cultivar of the common maple tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive white or pale yellow spots on the leaves, which contrast with its green or bronze foliage. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its unique leaf patterns.
