Amoora ferruginea

Amoora ferruginea, commonly known as the red-flowered hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers, which are typically five-petaled and have a showy appearance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Amoora ferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
