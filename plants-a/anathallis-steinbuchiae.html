Anathallis steinbuchiae

Anathallis steinbuchiae, commonly known as the Steinbuch's spider orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, thin inflorescences bearing small, white or pale green flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of dense forests. Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.

Its botanical significance lies in its specialized morphology and ecological niche within the rainforest ecosystem.
