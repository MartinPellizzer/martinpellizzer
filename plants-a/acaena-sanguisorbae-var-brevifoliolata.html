Acaena sanguisorbae var. brevifoliolata

Acaena sanguisorbae var. brevifoliolata, commonly known as the short-leaved bramble, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dense, thorny stems and small, lobed leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for native birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
