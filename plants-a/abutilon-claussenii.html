Abutilon claussenii

Abutilon claussenii, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains numerous seeds.

Abutilon claussenii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
