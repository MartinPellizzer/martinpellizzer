Amorpha emarginata

Amorpha emarginata, commonly known as the yellow indigo bush, is a shrub native to the central and southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, brown pods that contain several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Amorpha emarginata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

It is also a host plant for several species of butterflies and moths.
