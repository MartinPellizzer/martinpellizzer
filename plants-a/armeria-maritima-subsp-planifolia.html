Armeria maritima subsp. planifolia

Armeria maritima subsp.

planifolia, commonly known as the flat-leaved thrift, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features linear, fleshy leaves that are often blue-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its salt tolerance and ability to withstand harsh maritime conditions make it a valuable plant for coastal restoration projects.
