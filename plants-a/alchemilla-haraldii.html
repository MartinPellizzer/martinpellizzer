Alchemilla haraldii

Alchemilla haraldii, commonly known as the Harald's lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in loose clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Alchemilla haraldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
