Argyreia decemloba

Argyreia decemloba, commonly known as the velvet leaf or the sacred lotus of India, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a red or purple hue at the base. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also culturally significant in various traditional practices and rituals.
