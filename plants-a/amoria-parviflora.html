Amoria parviflora

Amoria parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered amoria, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Amoria parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in native plant gardens.
