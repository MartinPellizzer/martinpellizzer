Artemisia serreana

Artemisia serreana, commonly known as mountain wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in high altitudes and harsh climates. Its leaves are silvery-gray, covered in fine hairs, and emit a strong, bitter scent when crushed.

Artemisia serreana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and antiseptic properties.
