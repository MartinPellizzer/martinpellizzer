Artedia squamata

Artedia squamata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often accompanied by bracts that are reddish in color. Artedia squamata is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

It is considered an invasive species in some areas due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
