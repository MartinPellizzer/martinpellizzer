Ampelopsis micans var. cinerea

Ampelopsis micans var. cinerea, commonly known as the gray vine or silverleaf honeysuckle, is a woody vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with a silvery-gray underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It is often found climbing on trees or shrubs in its natural habitat.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in horticulture.
