Astragalus kukurttavicus

Astragalus kukurttavicus, commonly known as the Armenian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, it thrives in rocky and arid environments.
