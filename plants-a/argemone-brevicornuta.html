Argemone brevicornuta

Argemone brevicornuta, commonly known as the narrow-horned prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and spiny, lobed leaves. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain seeds with a high oil content. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
