Asarum japonicum

Asarum japonicum, commonly known as Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, aromatic underground tubers. The plant produces showy, hooded flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest floors or along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various ailments.
