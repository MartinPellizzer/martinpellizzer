Alnus × silesiaca

Alnus × silesiaca, commonly known as the Silesian alder, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Alnus glutinosa and Alnus incana.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is native to Central Europe and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

Alnus × silesiaca is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to grow in challenging environmental conditions.
