Asplenium cardiophyllum

Asplenium cardiophyllum, commonly known as the heart-leaf spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that often have a heart-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rock crevices. Its rhizomes are short and creeping, allowing it to form dense mats.

Asplenium cardiophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
