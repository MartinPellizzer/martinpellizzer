Arum orixense

Arum orixense, commonly known as the Orixan arum, is a tropical plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to humid, shaded environments.
