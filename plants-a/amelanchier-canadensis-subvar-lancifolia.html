Amelanchier canadensis subvar. lancifolia

Amelanchier canadensis subvar. lancifolia, commonly known as the Canadian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters, featuring a spreading, multi-branched structure. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its showy white flowers appear in early spring, followed by edible, dark blue berries in late summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
