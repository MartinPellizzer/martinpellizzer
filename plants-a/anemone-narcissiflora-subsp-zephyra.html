Anemone narcissiflora subsp. zephyra

Anemone narcissiflora subsp.

zephyra, commonly known as the Zephyr anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a yellow center.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its leaves are basal, palmately divided, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early spring blooms.
