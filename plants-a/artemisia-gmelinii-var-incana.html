Artemisia gmelinii var. incana

Artemisia gmelinii var. incana, commonly known as gray artemisia, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
