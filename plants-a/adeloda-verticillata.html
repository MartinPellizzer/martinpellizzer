Adeloda verticillata

Adeloda verticillata, commonly known as the mountain mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Adeloda verticillata is adapted to withstand drought and temperature extremes, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
