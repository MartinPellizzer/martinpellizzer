Anisopappus canescens

Anisopappus canescens, commonly known as desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky slopes. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fibrous stems and leaves are adapted to conserve water, making it well-suited for drought conditions.

The plant is also known for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in harsh landscapes.
