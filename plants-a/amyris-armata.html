Amyris armata

Amyris armata, commonly known as the red torch bamboo, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems. The plant produces small, fragrant, red flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a slight reddish tinge, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Amyris armata is valued for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various soil types, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
