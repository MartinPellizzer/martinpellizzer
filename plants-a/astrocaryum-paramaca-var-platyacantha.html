Astrocaryum paramaca var. platyacantha

Astrocaryum paramaca var. platyacantha, commonly known as the Brazilian wax palm, is a species of palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-pink flowers followed by clustered, woody fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in wetland and swampy areas, thriving in humid, tropical climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
