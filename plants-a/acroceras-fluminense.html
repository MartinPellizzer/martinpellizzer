Acroceras fluminense

Acroceras fluminense, commonly known as the Brazilian rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. Native to South America, particularly Brazil, it thrives in wetland environments such as marshes and riverbanks. The plant has fibrous roots that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in its natural habitat.

Its botanical structure supports its role in wetland ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and ecological balance.
