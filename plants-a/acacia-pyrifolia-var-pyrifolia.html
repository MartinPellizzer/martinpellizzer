Acacia pyrifolia var. pyrifolia

Acacia pyrifolia var. pyrifolia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bright orange to red flowers that appear in late winter to early spring, forming dense, fluffy clusters. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that are typically grey-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
