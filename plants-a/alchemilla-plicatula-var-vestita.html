Alchemilla plicatula var. vestita

Alchemilla plicatula var. vestita, commonly known as the velvet alkanet, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with dark green, velvety leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and alpine settings.
