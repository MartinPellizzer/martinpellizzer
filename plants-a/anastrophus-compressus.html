Anastrophus compressus

Anastrophus compressus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed aster, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are usually blue to purple in color.

Anastrophus compressus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
