Anacamptis × alata nothosubsp. sarcidani

Anacamptis × alata nothosubsp.

sarcidani, commonly known as the Sarcidani orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are often arranged in a raceme and are notable for their intricate structure and vibrant coloration.

This nothosubspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
