Andrographis hygrophiloides

Andrographis hygrophiloides, commonly known as water hyssop, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in moist environments, such as wetlands and stream banks, and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
