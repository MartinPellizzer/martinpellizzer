Abutilon cubanum

Abutilon cubanum, commonly known as the Cuban velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, brown seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its hairy stems and foliage give it a distinctive, velvety appearance.
