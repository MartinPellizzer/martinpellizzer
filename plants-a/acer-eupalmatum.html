Acer eupalmatum

Acer eupalmatum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 meters and thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
