Asperula cynanchica var. glauca

Asperula cynanchica var. glauca, commonly known as the blue honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers in early summer. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a bluish-gray waxy coating, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
