Aleuritopteris farinosa var. anceps

Aleuritopteris farinosa var. anceps, commonly known as the fern of the genus Aleuritopteris, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a fern characterized by its large, compound fronds that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce roots that help anchor it to the substrate.

The spores are released from the sporangia located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive cycle.
