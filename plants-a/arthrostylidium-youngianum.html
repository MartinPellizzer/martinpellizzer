Arthrostylidium youngianum

Arthrostylidium youngianum, commonly known as the young's arthrostylidium, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as sedges.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-poor soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of wetland ecosystems.
