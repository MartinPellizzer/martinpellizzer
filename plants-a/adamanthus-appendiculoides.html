Adamanthus appendiculoides

Adamanthus appendiculoides, commonly known as the Brazilian palm, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
