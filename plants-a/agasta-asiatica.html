Agasta asiatica

Agasta asiatica, commonly known as the Asian agastache, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Agasta asiatica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens in temperate climates.
