Ardisia miniscula

Ardisia miniscula, commonly known as the dwarf beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by bright red berries that are a striking feature. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ardisia miniscula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate partial shade.
