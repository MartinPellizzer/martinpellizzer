Austrocactus intertextus

Austrocactus intertextus, commonly known as the desert rose cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinct reddish-brown color and a spiny, segmented stem. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in inflorescences at the ends of the stems. It has a slow growth rate and is adapted to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.

Austrocactus intertextus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience in harsh environments.
