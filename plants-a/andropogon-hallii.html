Andropogon hallii

Andropogon hallii, commonly known as inland needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are often tinged with purple or red at the base. It is adapted to a wide range of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it a common component of native plant communities.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
