Amaryllis lucida

Amaryllis lucida, commonly known as the golden amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to deep yellow, often with a red or orange throat. The plant produces a tall, upright stem that bears multiple blooms during the summer months. Its foliage consists of strap-like, evergreen leaves that are broad and glossy.

Amaryllis lucida is well-suited for cultivation in USDA hardiness zones 8 through 10, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
