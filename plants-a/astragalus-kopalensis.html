Astragalus kopalensis

Astragalus kopalensis, commonly known as the Kopalensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Astragalus kopalensis is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing on rocky or sandy soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
