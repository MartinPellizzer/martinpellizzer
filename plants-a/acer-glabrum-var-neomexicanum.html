Acer glabrum var. neomexicanum

Acer glabrum var. neomexicanum, commonly known as the New Mexico maple, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental species.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
