Aesculus georgiana

Aesculus georgiana, commonly known as the horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets that are glossy and dark green. The tree produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers in upright clusters during late spring. Its fruit is a spiny, spherical capsule containing several glossy, chestnut-like seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
