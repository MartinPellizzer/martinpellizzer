Anulocaulis eriosolenus

Anulocaulis eriosolenus, commonly known as the woolly stemless gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually opposite and lance-shaped, while its flowers are tubular and bilaterally symmetrical.

Anulocaulis eriosolenus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
