Aphelandra euopla

Aphelandra euopla, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that are dark green with a glossy texture and prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, resembling flames. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Aphelandra euopla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
