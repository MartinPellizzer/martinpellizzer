Adina globiflora

Adina globiflora, commonly known as the globeflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spherical inflorescence that resembles a globe, composed of numerous small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Adina globiflora is valued not only for its ornamental appearance but also for its traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
