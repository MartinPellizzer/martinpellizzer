Antimima longipes

Antimima longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed antimima, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinct appearance.

Antimima longipes is of interest to botanists due to its unusual morphology and potential ecological significance.
