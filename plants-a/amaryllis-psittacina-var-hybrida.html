Amaryllis psittacina var. hybrida

Amaryllis psittacina var. hybrida, commonly known as the golden amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange, often with a red or purple throat. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are dark green and can grow up to 1 meter in length. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its showy blooms and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.
