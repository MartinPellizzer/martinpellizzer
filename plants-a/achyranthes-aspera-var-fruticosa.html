Achyranthes aspera var. fruticosa

Achyranthes aspera var. fruticosa, commonly known as the prickly chaff flower, is a shrub-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect or ascending stems, which are often covered in stiff, bristly hairs. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and prominent veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
