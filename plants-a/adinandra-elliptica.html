Adinandra elliptica

Adinandra elliptica, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its hard, dense wood is highly valued for its strength and durability.

Adinandra elliptica plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
