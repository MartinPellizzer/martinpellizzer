Ammannia pentandra

Ammannia pentandra, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces bright red or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Ammannia pentandra is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some regions.
