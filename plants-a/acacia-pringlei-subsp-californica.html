Acacia pringlei subsp. californica

Acacia pringlei subsp.

californica, commonly known as the California acacia, is a shrub native to coastal regions of California. It features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters.

The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and thrives in coastal climates. It is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and nectar for native pollinators.
