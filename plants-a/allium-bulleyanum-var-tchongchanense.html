Allium bulleyanum var. tchongchanense

Allium bulleyanum var. tchongchanense, commonly known as the Tchongchan onion, is a perennial herb native to China.

It features a bulbous stem with a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in a spherical inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its culinary use and ornamental appeal in gardens.
