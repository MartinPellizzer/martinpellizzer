Anacamptis × laniccae

Anacamptis × laniccae, commonly known as the woolly orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and tall, branched inflorescences that bear numerous showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it is often found in association with other orchid species in its native range.

The botanical features of Anacamptis × laniccae make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in both natural and cultivated settings.
