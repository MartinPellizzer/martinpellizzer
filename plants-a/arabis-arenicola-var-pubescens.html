Arabis arenicola var. pubescens

Arabis arenicola var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features basal leaves that are hairy or pubescent, which helps reduce water loss in dry climates. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and arranged in clusters, often blooming in spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience in xerophytic landscapes.
