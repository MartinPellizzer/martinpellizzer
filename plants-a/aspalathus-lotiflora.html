Aspalathus lotiflora

Aspalathus lotiflora, commonly known as the African red bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and bright red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Aspalathus lotiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscaping.
