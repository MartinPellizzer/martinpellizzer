Asplenium rebeccae

Asplenium rebeccae, commonly known as the Rebecca's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks in temperate regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the leaves.

Asplenium rebeccae is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
