Andropogon sorghum subvar. badius

Andropogon sorghum subvar. badius, commonly known as black sorghum, is a variant of sorghum native to Africa and cultivated for its hardy, drought-resistant characteristics.

It is a tall, robust grass with narrow, upright leaves and produces dense clusters of small, dark purple to black flowers. The plant is notable for its high biomass yield and is often used in forage and bioenergy applications. Its fibrous stalks are also valued for their strength and versatility in traditional crafts.

The subvariety is adapted to a wide range of soil types and is particularly suited to arid and semi-arid climates.
