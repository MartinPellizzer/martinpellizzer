Achnatherum calamagrostis var. colorata

Achnatherum calamagrostis var. colorata, commonly known as the colorful bluegrass, is a variant of the bluegrass genus characterized by its striking floral display.

This grass species typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in meadows and open woodlands. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters that are usually blue to purple in color, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
