Alsinastrum americanum

Alsinastrum americanum, commonly known as yellow starthistle, is an invasive annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, bract-like leaves and a yellow flower head that resembles a star. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and can quickly dominate rangelands, reducing forage quality for livestock. Its growth cycle is rapid, with seeds dispersed by wind, animals, and machinery.

Due to its aggressive nature, it is considered a noxious weed in many regions.
