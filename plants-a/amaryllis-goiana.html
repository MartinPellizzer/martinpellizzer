Amaryllis goiana

Amaryllis goiana, commonly known as the Brazilian belladonna lily, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Amaryllis goiana is also known for its ability to store water in its bulb, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.
