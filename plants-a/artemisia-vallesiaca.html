Artemisia vallesiaca

Artemisia vallesiaca, commonly known as Vallesia sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its essential oils contain compounds such as thujone and camphor, which contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

It is often used in traditional herbal remedies and as an ornamental plant in xeriscaped gardens.
