Anthericum lukiense var. intermedium

Anthericum lukiense var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate lukiense fern, is a variant of the lukiense fern, characterized by its intermediate morphological traits between the typical varietal forms.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as woodland floors or stream banks, where it can thrive in partial to full shade. Its spore-bearing structures, called sorus, are clustered on the underside of the leaflets, a feature typical of ferns.

The botanical interest of this variant lies in its unique morphology and its role in ecological studies of plant adaptation.
