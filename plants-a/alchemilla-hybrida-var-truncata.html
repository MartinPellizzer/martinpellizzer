Alchemilla hybrida var. truncata

Alchemilla hybrida var. truncata, commonly known as the cut-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed, and often cut foliage that gives it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
