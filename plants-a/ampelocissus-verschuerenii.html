Ampelocissus verschuerenii

Ampelocissus verschuerenii, commonly known as the climbing false ivy, is a member of the Vitaceae family.

It is a woody vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its ability to climb and spread makes it suitable for trellises or as ground cover in shaded areas.
