Abies procera f. glauca

Abies procera f. glauca, commonly known as the blue spruce, is a variant of the white spruce native to western North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage, which results from a dense layer of wax on the leaf surfaces. The plant has a conical shape with upward-curving branches, and its needles are typically four to five millimeters long. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens and landscapes.

The species is also valued for its durability and resistance to pests and diseases.
