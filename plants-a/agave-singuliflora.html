Agave singuliflora

Agave singuliflora, commonly known as the single-flower agave, is a striking succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape, often with a blue-gray hue and spiny margins. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears numerous white or pale yellow flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
