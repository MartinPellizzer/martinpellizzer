Astragalus onobrychis subsp. wagneri

Astragalus onobrychis subsp.

wagneri, commonly known as the Wagner's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
