Acoridium angustifolium

Acoridium angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh habitats.

It features slender, strap-like leaves that are typically narrow and elongated, often with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist or aquatic environments and is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds.

Acoridium angustifolium plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
