Acalypha parvula f. sericea

Acalypha parvula f. sericea, commonly known as silverleaf ironweed, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features small, ovate to elliptic leaves that are densely covered with silky, silver-white hairs, giving it a distinctive, silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
