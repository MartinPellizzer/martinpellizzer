Acacia sessiliceps

Acacia sessiliceps, commonly known as the sessile-headed acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and pairs of small, stipulate leaves. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that are typically clustered in dense, upright inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are dispersed by wind or animals.

Acacia sessiliceps is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry habitats.
