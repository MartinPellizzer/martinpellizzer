Asterocephalus suaveolens var. hirsutus

Asterocephalus suaveolens var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy asterocephalus, is a species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy foliage and distinctive floral structure, which includes small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

The flowers, which bloom in late spring, are usually white or pale yellow and attract various pollinators.
