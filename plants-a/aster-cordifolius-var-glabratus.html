Aster cordifolius var. glabratus

Aster cordifolius var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth-leaved aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the fall, typically from September to November. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are smooth and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
