Amaranthus anderssonii f. erectus

Amaranthus anderssonii f. erectus, commonly known as the upright amaranth, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its erect growth habit and distinctive foliage.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to regions of Central and South America. The plant features lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving them a silvery appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically displaying shades of red, orange, or yellow.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
