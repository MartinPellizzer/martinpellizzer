Adhatoda fasciata

Adhatoda fasciata, commonly known as the Indian Trumpet or Blue Trumpet, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark is rough and fissured, and it has a distinctive aromatic scent.

Adhatoda fasciata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
