Abies veitchii var. veitchii

Abies veitchii var. veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals around the stem. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, typically found at the ends of the branches. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and parks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
