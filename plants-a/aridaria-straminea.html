Aridaria straminea

Aridaria straminea, commonly known as the desert grass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh environments through deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area. It plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing minimal forage for desert wildlife.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in ecological restoration projects in dry landscapes.
