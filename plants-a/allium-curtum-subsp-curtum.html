Allium curtum subsp. curtum

Allium curtum subsp.

curtum, commonly known as the Armenian garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale purple flowers in umbels.

The plant is characterized by its strong garlic scent and is often grown for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

This subspecies is closely related to other Allium species and is valued for both its culinary and medicinal properties.
