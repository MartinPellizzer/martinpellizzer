Aster scapigerus

Aster scapigerus, commonly known as the slender aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, leafless stems that bear clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers bloom in the late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster scapigerus is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other familiar flowering plants.
