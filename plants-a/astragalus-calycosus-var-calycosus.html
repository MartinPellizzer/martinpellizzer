Astragalus calycosus var. calycosus

Astragalus calycosus var. calycosus, commonly known as the California milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinators.
