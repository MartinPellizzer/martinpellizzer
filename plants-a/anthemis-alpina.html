Anthemis alpina

Anthemis alpina, commonly known as alpine chamomile, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers with a central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand cold and dry conditions.

Anthemis alpina is often used in traditional medicine for its mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.
