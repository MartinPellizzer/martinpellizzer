Acinodendron integrifolium

Acinodendron integrifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leaf acinodendron, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, entire leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acinodendron integrifolium is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and riverine areas.
