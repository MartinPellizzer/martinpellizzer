Androsace maxima var. macrantha

Androsace maxima var. macrantha, commonly known as the giant rock jasmine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and hairy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
