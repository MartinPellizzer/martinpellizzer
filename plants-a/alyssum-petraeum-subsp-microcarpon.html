Alyssum petraeum subsp. microcarpon

Alyssum petraeum subsp.

microcarpon, commonly known as the rock alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and arid environments. It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to minimize water loss in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for coastal and Mediterranean climates.
