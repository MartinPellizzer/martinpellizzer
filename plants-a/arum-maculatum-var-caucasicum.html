Arum maculatum var. caucasicum

Arum maculatum var. caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Caucasus region.

It features arrow-shaped leaves that are often marked with distinctive white or yellow spots, giving it its common name. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant plants.
