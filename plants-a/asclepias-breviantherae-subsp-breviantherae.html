Asclepias breviantherae subsp. breviantherae

Asclepias breviantherae subsp.

breviantherae, commonly known as the narrow-lobed milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features showy, pink to reddish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a milky sap that contains toxic compounds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

This species is an important host plant for monarch butterfly larvae.
