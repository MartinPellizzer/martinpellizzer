Atriplex heterophylla

Atriplex heterophylla, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves, which are adapted to store water and reduce transpiration in harsh environments. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater, making it highly drought-tolerant. Its small, greenish flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.

Atriplex heterophylla plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
