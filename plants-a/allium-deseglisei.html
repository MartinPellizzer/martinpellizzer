Allium deseglisei

Allium deseglisei, commonly known as the Desert Garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered bulbs and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale to deep purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with a deep root system that helps it access water from deeper soil layers.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
