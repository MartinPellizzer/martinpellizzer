Aster yunnanensis var. labrangensis

Aster yunnanensis var. labrangensis, commonly known as the Labrang aster, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant is known for its showy, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
