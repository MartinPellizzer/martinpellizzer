Acianthera krahnii

Acianthera krahnii, commonly known as the Krahn's orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, often with a distinctive greenish hue on the dorsal sepal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Acianthera krahnii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
