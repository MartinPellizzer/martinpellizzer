Astragalus pomphocalyx

Astragalus pomphocalyx, commonly known as the broadleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in harsh conditions.

Astragalus pomphocalyx is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
