Acacia huegelii

Acacia huegelii, commonly known as the desert acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, bipinnate leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. Acacia huegelii is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or rocky soils, making it well-suited to harsh desert environments.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
