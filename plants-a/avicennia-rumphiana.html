Avicennia rumphiana

Avicennia rumphiana, commonly known as the mangrove apple or white mangrove, is a halophytic shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It thrives in brackish water environments, such as estuaries and coastal areas, and has specialized adaptations for salt tolerance. The plant possesses pneumatophores, which are root-like structures that facilitate gas exchange in waterlogged soils. Its small, white flowers are followed by edible berries that provide food for various wildlife.

Avicennia rumphiana plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and preventing erosion.
