Aiouea granatensis

Aiouea granatensis, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to the western Pacific, particularly found in parts of Indonesia and Papua New Guinea.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its durability and unique coloration. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, red berries.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its timber in traditional crafts.
