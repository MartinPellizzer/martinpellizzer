Attalea boehmii

Attalea boehmii, commonly known as the wax palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly found in Brazil, Bolivia, and Paraguay.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clusters of dark blue to black berries. Attalea boehmii is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used by indigenous communities for its fibers and as a source of food.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
