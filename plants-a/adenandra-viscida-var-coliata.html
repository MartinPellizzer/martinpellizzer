Adenandra viscida var. coliata

Adenandra viscida var. coliata, commonly known as the sticky adenandra, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its unique botanical features make it a distinctive addition to native plant collections.
