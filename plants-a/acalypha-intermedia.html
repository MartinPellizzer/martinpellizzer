Acalypha intermedia

Acalypha intermedia, commonly known as the red-rooted spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed capsules. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Acalypha intermedia is noted for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas, making it both a common weed and a plant of ecological interest.
