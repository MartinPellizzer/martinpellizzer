Astragalus acutus

Astragalus acutus, commonly known as sharpleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus acutus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local insect populations.
