Ampelopsis cantoniensis var. grossedentata

Ampelopsis cantoniensis var. grossedentata, commonly known as the broad-toothed honeysuckle, is a climbing vine native to southern China.

It features compound leaves with broad, serrated leaflets that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
