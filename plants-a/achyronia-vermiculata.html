Achyronia vermiculata

Achyronia vermiculata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in disturbed soils and agricultural areas. Its seeds are small and can remain viable in the soil for several years, contributing to its invasive potential in some regions.

The plant has been studied for its role in ecological systems and its impact on crop production.
