Achillea × confusa

Achillea × confusa, commonly known as the hybrid yarrow, is a perennial plant native to Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features finely divided, gray-green foliage. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Achillea × confusa is noted for its long blooming period, which extends from early summer to late autumn.
