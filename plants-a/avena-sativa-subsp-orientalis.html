Avena sativa subsp. orientalis

Avena sativa subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental oat, is a perennial grass species native to the Middle East and Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces spikelets that bear small, white to pale blue flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated for forage or ornamental purposes.
