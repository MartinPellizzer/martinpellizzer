Aspalathus shawii

Aspalathus shawii, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces oval, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.
