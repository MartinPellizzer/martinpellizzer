Aspidium tripinnatum

Aspidium tripinnatum, commonly known as the three-part maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are divided into three parts, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are clustered in sori.

Aspidium tripinnatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
