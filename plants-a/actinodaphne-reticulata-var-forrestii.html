Actinodaphne reticulata var. forrestii

Actinodaphne reticulata var. forrestii, commonly known as the Forest's actinodaphne, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the mountainous regions of Myanmar and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, smooth drupe that turns from green to yellow as it matures.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique foliage and fragrance.
