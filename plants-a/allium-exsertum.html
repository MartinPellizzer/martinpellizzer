Allium exsertum

Allium exsertum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes various species of onions and garlic. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine, similar to other Allium species.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it adaptable to rocky and arid environments.
