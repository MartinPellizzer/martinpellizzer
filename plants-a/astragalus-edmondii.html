Astragalus edmondii

Astragalus edmondii, commonly known as Edmond's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus edmondii is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub ecosystems.
