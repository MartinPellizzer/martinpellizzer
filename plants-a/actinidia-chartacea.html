Actinidia chartacea

Actinidia chartacea, commonly known as the creeping actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early summer, followed by edible, yellowish fruits that are slightly tart. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
