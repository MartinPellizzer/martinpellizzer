Antholyza revoluta

Antholyza revoluta, commonly known as the wavyleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, wavy-edged leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pale pink to lavender flowers that bloom in summer, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its botanical characteristics include a hairy stem and hairy calyces that enclose the flower buds.

Antholyza revoluta is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.
