Anthemis antilibanotica

Anthemis antilibanotica, commonly known as the Palestine chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are surrounded by white ray florets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
