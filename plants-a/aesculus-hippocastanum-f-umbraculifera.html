Aesculus hippocastanum f. umbraculifera

Aesculus hippocastanum f. umbraculifera, commonly known as the shade maple or umbraclifera horse chestnut, is a botanical variant of the horse chestnut tree.

It is characterized by its broad, dark green leaves that provide dense shade, making it suitable for ornamental and shade-providing purposes. The plant produces large, showy white flowers in spring, followed by spiny, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This form is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and ecological benefits in urban and garden settings.
