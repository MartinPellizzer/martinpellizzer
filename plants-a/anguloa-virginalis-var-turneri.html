Anguloa virginalis var. turneri

Anguloa virginalis var. turneri, commonly known as the Turnera's anguloa, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and floral structures. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its unique floral display and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
