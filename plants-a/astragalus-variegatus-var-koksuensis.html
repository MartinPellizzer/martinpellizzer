Astragalus variegatus var. koksuensis

Astragalus variegatus var. koksuensis, commonly known as the Koksu variant of the variegated milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and compound leaves with a distinctive variegation pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
