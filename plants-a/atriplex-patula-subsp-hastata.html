Atriplex patula subsp. hastata

Atriplex patula subsp.

hastata, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its succulent, gray-green leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often subtended by bracts. It is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert environments.

Its deep root system allows it to access water from deeper soil layers, enhancing its drought tolerance.
