Armeria plantaginea subsp. atlantica

Armeria plantaginea subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the coastal regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It features dense clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring and summer.

The plant has succulent, silver-gray foliage that is adapted to withstand salty sea air and strong coastal winds. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes and rocky coastal areas.

This hardy plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize coastal soils.
