Arachnanthe × maingayi

Arachnanthe × maingayi, commonly known as the hybrid hibiscus, is a flowering plant in the Malvaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Arachnanthe tetragona and Hibiscus tiliaceus, resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parent species. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. It thrives in coastal and brackish environments, often found in mangrove areas and estuaries.

Its adaptability to saline conditions makes it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
