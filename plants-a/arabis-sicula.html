Arabis sicula

Arabis sicula, commonly known as Sicilian rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and appear in clusters during the spring.

Arabis sicula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
