Abietia douglasii var. pendula

Abietia douglasii var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping white fir, is a unique variant of the white fir tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a distinctive, cascading appearance. The plant has needle-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath, providing a striking visual contrast. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
