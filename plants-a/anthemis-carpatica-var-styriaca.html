Anthemis carpatica var. styriaca

Anthemis carpatica var. styriaca, commonly known as the Styrian cudweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish-white composite flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and drought-tolerant addition to native plant gardens.
