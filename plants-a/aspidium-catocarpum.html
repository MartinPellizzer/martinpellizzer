Aspidium catocarpum

Aspidium catocarpum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bipinnate, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Aspidium catocarpum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
