Anoplophytum refulgens

Anoplophytum refulgens, commonly known as the golden flame cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are covered in bright yellow spines, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers during the warmer months, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
