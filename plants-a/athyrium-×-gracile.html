Athyrium × gracile

Athyrium × gracile, commonly known as the lace fern, is a hybrid fern species that combines traits from its parent species, Athyrium filix-femina and Athyrium capillus-veneris.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

The botanical features of Athyrium × gracile make it a visually appealing and ecologically valuable addition to diverse plant communities.
