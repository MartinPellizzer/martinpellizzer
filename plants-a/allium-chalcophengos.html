Allium chalcophengos

Allium chalcophengos, commonly known as the copperleaf garlic, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bulbous structures similar to garlic. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
