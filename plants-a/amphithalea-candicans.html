Amphithalea candicans

Amphithalea candicans, commonly known as the candleplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its unique appearance, featuring clusters of small, white, candle-like flowers that bloom along the stems. The plant has a succulent, fleshy stem that stores water, adapting it to arid environments. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a waxy surface that reduces water loss.

The candleplant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
