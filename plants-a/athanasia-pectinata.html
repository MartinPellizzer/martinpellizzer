Athanasia pectinata

Athanasia pectinata, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the East, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white, often with a prominent central stigma. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, flexible stems. Its root system helps stabilize the sediment in aquatic environments.

Athanasia pectinata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and holds cultural significance in various regions.
