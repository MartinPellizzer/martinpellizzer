Astragalus layneae

Astragalus layneae, commonly known as Layne's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils and rocky slopes. It is often found in areas with low precipitation and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Astragalus layneae plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native range.
