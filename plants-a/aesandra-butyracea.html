Aesandra butyracea

Aesandra butyracea, commonly known as the buttery tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in yellow or green hues. The plant produces a thick, buttery substance from its bark, which has been used traditionally for various purposes. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its unique botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and ethnobotanical study.
