Acacia decurrens var. mollissima

Acacia decurrens var. mollissima, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces yellow or pale yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it has a reputation for being drought-tolerant and adaptable to various soil types.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
