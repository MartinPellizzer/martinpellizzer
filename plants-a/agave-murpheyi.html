Agave murpheyi

Agave murpheyi, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, blue-gray leaves that are often lined with sharp spines, forming a rosette shape that helps it retain moisture. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear white to pale blue flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional practices.
