Amygdalus davidiana var. potaninii

Amygdalus davidiana var. potaninii, commonly known as the Chinese wild almond, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible nuts enclosed in a hard shell, similar to other members of the Prunus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique botanical features, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized horticultural settings.
