Asplenium × andringitraense

Asplenium × andringitraense, commonly known as the Andringitra spleenwort, is a hybrid fern species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lobed fronds that often exhibit a variegated appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
