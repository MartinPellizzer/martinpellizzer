Astragalus pseudocapito

Astragalus pseudocapito, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in racemes. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

Astragalus pseudocapito is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.
