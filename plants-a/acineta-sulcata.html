Acineta sulcata

Acineta sulcata, commonly known as the zigzag spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and distinctive zigzag pattern along its surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils or on slopes.

Acineta sulcata is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
