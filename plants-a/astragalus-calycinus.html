Astragalus calycinus

Astragalus calycinus, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to western North America.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and canyons. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces small, yellow to green flowers in clusters. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

Astragalus calycinus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
