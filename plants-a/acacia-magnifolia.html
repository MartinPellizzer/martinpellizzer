Acacia magnifolia

Acacia magnifolia, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria. Acacia magnifolia is often found in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to arid conditions.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
