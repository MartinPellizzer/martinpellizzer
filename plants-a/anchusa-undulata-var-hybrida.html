Anchusa undulata var. hybrida

Anchusa undulata var. hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple, and it grows in clusters along the stems. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
