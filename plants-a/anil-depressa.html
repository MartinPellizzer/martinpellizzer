Anil depressa

Anil depressa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed anil, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Anil depressa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
