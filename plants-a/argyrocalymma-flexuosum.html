Argyrocalymma flexuosum

Argyrocalymma flexuosum, commonly known as the silver-leaved daisy or the silverflower, is a small, mat-forming perennial plant native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It features narrow, silver-blue leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with a central yellow disc, blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
