Adesmia calopogon

Adesmia calopogon, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small lanterns. The plant is a climbing vine with opposite, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in local ecosystems.
