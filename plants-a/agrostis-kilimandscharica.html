Agrostis kilimandscharica

Agrostis kilimandscharica, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro grass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Agrostis kilimandscharica plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its ecosystem.
