Annona testudinea

Annona testudinea, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Americas.

It produces a large, spiky fruit with a thick, sweet, creamy pulp that is often used in culinary applications. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers are typically yellow with a red or purple center, and it relies on insects for pollination.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, making it a popular choice in subtropical regions.
