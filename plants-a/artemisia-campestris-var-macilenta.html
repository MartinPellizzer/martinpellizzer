Artemisia campestris var. macilenta

Artemisia campestris var. macilenta, commonly known as the slender wormwood, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean.

It features silver-gray foliage composed of finely divided, narrow leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads arranged in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for dry landscapes.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a companion plant in gardens.
