Androsace adfinis subsp. adfinis

Androsace adfinis subsp.

adfinis, commonly known as the alpine sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically forms dense cushions or mats, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats.

The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are usually succulent and densely packed, adapted to retain moisture in harsh environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing alpine soils.
