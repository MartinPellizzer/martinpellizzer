Asphodelus clavatus

Asphodelus clavatus, commonly known as the clustered asphodel, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It features upright, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow or orange flowers in early summer. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering stems can reach heights of up to 60 cm, and it is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Asphodelus clavatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscapes.
