Amaranthus lividus proles ascendens

Amaranthus lividus proles ascendens, commonly known as the ascending amaranth, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its upright growth habit and branching structure. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and clustered in panicles. It produces vibrant red or purple bracts that are often mistaken for flowers, adding visual interest to gardens.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
