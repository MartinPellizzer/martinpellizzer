Aretia hedraeantha

Aretia hedraeantha, commonly known as the rock wall vine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a ground cover.
