Arrudea bicolor

Arrudea bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue and white petals that form bell-shaped blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Arrudea bicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
