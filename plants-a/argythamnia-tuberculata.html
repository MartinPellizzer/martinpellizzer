Argythamnia tuberculata

Argythamnia tuberculata, commonly known as the crested sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has adapted to harsh desert conditions with deep roots and a compact growth form. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Argythamnia tuberculata is also known for its ability to thrive in sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
