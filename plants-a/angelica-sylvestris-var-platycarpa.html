Angelica sylvestris var. platycarpa

Angelica sylvestris var. platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited angelica, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The roots and leaves of this plant are valued for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.
