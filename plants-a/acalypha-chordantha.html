Acalypha chordantha

Acalypha chordantha, commonly known as the golden glory or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Acalypha chordantha is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
