Adhatoda anagalloides

Adhatoda anagalloides, commonly known as the Indian Lady's Finger or the Blue Trumpet, is a flowering plant native to southern and southeastern Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from April to September. Its stems are often woody and covered with a fine, powdery coating.

Adhatoda anagalloides is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic and Chinese medicine.
