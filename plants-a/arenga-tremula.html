Arenga tremula

Arenga tremula, commonly known as the bitter areca, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fibrous, inedible fruits. Its roots contain a starch-like substance used in traditional medicine.

Arenga tremula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in tropical regions.
