Amygdalus urumiensis

Amygdalus urumiensis, commonly known as the Indian almond or wild almond, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval leaves and white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain hard, edible seeds, which are similar to almonds in appearance and flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
