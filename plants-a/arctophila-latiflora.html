Arctophila latiflora

Arctophila latiflora, commonly known as the broadleaf arctophila, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a loose cluster, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal practices for their medicinal properties.
