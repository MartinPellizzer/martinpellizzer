Arenaria rossii var. daethiana

Arenaria rossii var. daethiana, commonly known as the Ross's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments. Its leaves are usually succulent and covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and limited geographic distribution.
