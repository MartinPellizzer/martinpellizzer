Andropogon cordatifolius

Andropogon cordatifolius, commonly known as broadleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive broad, heart-shaped base, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering spikes are usually pale blue to purple, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

It plays an important role in ecological systems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
