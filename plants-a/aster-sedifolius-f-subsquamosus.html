Aster sedifolius f. subsquamosus

Aster sedifolius f. subsquamosus, commonly known as the squamosous aster, is a variant of the sedifolius form of the aster species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, scale-like leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant is native to the western United States, particularly in regions with rocky or dry soils. It produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white to lavender, typically blooming in the summer months.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to arid environments.
