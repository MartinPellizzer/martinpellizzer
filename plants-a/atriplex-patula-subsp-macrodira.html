Atriplex patula subsp. macrodira

Atriplex patula subsp.

macrodira, commonly known as the largeleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments.

The plant typically grows in saline soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high salinity. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are borne in dense clusters, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
