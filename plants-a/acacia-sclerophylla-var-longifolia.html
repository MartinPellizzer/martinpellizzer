Acacia sclerophylla var. longifolia

Acacia sclerophylla var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It is adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate salt spray, making it well-suited for coastal environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
