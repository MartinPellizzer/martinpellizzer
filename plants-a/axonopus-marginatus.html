Axonopus marginatus

Axonopus marginatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf axonopus, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. Its inflorescence consists of compact spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Axonopus marginatus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
