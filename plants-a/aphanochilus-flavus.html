Aphanochilus flavus

Aphanochilus flavus, commonly known as the yellow moss orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant produces delicate, yellow flowers with a unique, intricate structure typical of orchids. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption from the soil.

Aphanochilus flavus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
