Arnica pulchella

Arnica pulchella, commonly known as the showy arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it grows in rocky or sandy soils. Arnica pulchella is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

Its flowers are often used in the production of topical ointments due to their anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
