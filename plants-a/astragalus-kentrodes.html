Astragalus kentrodes

Astragalus kentrodes, commonly known as the central spike vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish to brownish pea-like flowers. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Astragalus kentrodes is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in soil stabilization and as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
