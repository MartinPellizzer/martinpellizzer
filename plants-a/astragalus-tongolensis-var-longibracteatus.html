Astragalus tongolensis var. longibracteatus

Astragalus tongolensis var. longibracteatus, commonly known as the long-bract milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its root system is fibrous and contributes to soil stabilization, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.
