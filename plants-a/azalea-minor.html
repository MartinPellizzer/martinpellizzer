Azalea minor

Azalea minor, commonly known as the dwarf azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, making it suitable for rock gardens, borders, or as a foundation plant. Its flowers, which range in color from white to deep pink, bloom in early spring and are highly fragrant.

Azalea minor prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in humid climates.
