Ampelopsis quinquefolia var. spaethii

Ampelopsis quinquefolia var. spaethii, commonly known as the Spaeth's Virginia creeper, is a climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and is often found in woodland edges and moist soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and ecological benefits.
