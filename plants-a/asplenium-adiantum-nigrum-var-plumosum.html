Asplenium adiantum-nigrum var. plumosum

Asplenium adiantum-nigrum var. plumosum, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a soft, feathery appearance.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is native to various regions in Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or in shaded forest floors. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a velvety texture, which contributes to its common name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
