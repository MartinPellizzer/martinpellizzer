Arisaema simense

Arisaema simense, commonly known as the Chinese cobra lily, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are adapted for pollination by insects.

Arisaema simense is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its traditional uses in herbal medicine.
