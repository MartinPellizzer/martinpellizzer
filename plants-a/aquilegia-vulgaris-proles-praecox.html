Aquilegia vulgaris proles praecox

Aquilegia vulgaris proles praecox, commonly known as the early purple poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, with five petals that are often marked with darker veins. The plant has compound leaves composed of several lance-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both ornamental and ecological value.
