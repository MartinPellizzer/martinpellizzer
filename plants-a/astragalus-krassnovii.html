Astragalus krassnovii

Astragalus krassnovii, commonly known as the Krassnov's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the Astragalus genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
