Alnus incana var. hypochlora

Alnus incana var. hypochlora, commonly known as the white alder, is a variant of the alder tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves that are typically pale green to white in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found near streams or in wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody cones that release seeds.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
