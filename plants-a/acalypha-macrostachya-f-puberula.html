Acalypha macrostachya f. puberula

Acalypha macrostachya f. puberula, commonly known as velvetleaf or silky abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture, and its clusters of small, pale greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
