Artemisia ludoviciana var. integrifolia

Artemisia ludoviciana var. integrifolia, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It features aromatic, silvery-gray foliage with lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive, slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscaped gardens.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
