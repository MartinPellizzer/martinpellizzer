Angelica tschimganica

Angelica tschimganica, commonly known as the Tschimgan angelica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers, which are small and greenish-white, are arranged in umbels, a typical feature of the Apiaceae family.

Angelica tschimganica is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.
