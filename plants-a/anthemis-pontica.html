Anthemis pontica

Anthemis pontica, commonly known as the Crimean ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, and it produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
