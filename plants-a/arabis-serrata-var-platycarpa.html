Arabis serrata var. platycarpa

Arabis serrata var. platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many mustard plants. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and serrated, with a distinctive white or pale yellow flower cluster. Its fruits are capsule-like and broader than the typical variety, giving it its varietal name.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.
