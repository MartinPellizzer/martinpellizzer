Aster bodkinii

Aster bodkinii, commonly known as the Bodkin aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, daisy-like flowers bloom in shades of purple and white, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.

Aster bodkinii is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience in challenging landscapes.
