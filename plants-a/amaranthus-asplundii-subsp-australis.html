Amaranthus asplundii subsp. australis

Amaranthus asplundii subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian amaranth, is a species native to Australia. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its erect growth habit and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, red or purple flowers, which are typically borne on slender spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to dry conditions.
