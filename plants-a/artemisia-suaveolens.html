Artemisia suaveolens

Artemisia suaveolens, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and slender, branched stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves contain volatile oils, giving it a distinctive, camphor-like scent.

Artemisia suaveolens is often used in traditional medicine and is also a component in the production of certain herbal remedies.
