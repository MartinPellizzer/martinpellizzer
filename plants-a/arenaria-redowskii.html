Arenaria redowskii

Arenaria redowskii, commonly known as the Redowski's sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, fleshy leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. It thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
