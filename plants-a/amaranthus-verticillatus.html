Amaranthus verticillatus

Amaranthus verticillatus, commonly known as the whorled amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, whorled arrangement of leaves around the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the variety. Its leaves are often used in traditional cuisines, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal.

Amaranthus verticillatus thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for gardens and agricultural settings.
