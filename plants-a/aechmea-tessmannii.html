Aechmea tessmannii

Aechmea tessmannii, commonly known as the pink pineapple plant, is a bromeliad native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage that forms a central tank to collect rainwater. The leaves are stiff and covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces a tall inflorescence with vibrant red flowers that bloom in the summer.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ease of care.

Morphology

Aechmea tessmannii has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, strap-like, and often have a waxy coating, arranged in a compact circle around the plant's central axis.

The leaves are typically dark green with a bluish tint, and can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant has a short, thick stem that is covered in a fibrous, rough-textured bark. It produces striking violet and pink spathes that enclose clusters of small white flowers, blooming during the summer months and pollinated primarily by insects.

The fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by birds and water.
