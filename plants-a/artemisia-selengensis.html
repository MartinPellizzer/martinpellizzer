Artemisia selengensis

Artemisia selengensis, commonly known as Selengae wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in summer. The plant contains essential oils and is known for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and tundra environments.

Artemisia selengensis is also used in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential in modern pharmacology.
