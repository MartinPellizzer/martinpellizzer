Astragalus chaborasicus

Astragalus chaborasicus, commonly known as the Chaborasi milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters. Its seed pods are hairy and contain several small, lens-shaped seeds.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.
