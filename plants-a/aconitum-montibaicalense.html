Aconitum montibaicalense

Aconitum montibaicalense, commonly known as the Montibaical monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has palmately divided leaves and produces showy, spurs at the base of its petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas at elevations above 2,000 meters.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
