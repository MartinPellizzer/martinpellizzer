Amsinckia microcarpa

Amsinckia microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Amsinckia microcarpa is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
