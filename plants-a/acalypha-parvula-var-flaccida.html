Acalypha parvula var. flaccida

Acalypha parvula var. flaccida, commonly known as the hanging croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its pendulous, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant for its vibrant floral displays.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
