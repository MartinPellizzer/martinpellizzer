Adyseton montanum

Adyseton montanum, commonly known as the mountain adyseton, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white and green. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, which is well adapted to its alpine habitat.

Adyseton montanum is valued for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental and ecological significance.
