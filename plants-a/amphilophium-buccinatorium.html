Amphilophium buccinatorium

Amphilophium buccinatorium, commonly known as the scarlet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
