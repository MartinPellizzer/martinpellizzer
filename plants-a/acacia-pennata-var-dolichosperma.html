Acacia pennata var. dolichosperma

Acacia pennata var. dolichosperma, commonly known as the long-seeded wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, followed by elongated, flattened pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
