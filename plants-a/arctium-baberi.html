Arctium baberi

Arctium baberi, commonly known as the European burdock, is a biennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of purple to pink flowers that develop into spiny, bur-like fruits. Its root system is thick and fleshy, which is often used in traditional medicine.

Arctium baberi is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.
