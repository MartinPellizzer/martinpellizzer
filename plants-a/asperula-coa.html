Asperula coa

Asperula coa, commonly known as the Japanese evergreen azalea, is a flowering shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, providing an attractive backdrop to the vibrant blossoms.

Asperula coa is well-suited for shaded garden areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
