Aniba vaupesiana

Aniba vaupesiana, commonly known as the Amazonian myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red when mature, and they contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy pulp.

Aniba vaupesiana is valued for its aromatic properties and has traditional uses in local medicine.
