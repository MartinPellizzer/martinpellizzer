Allium drummondii

Allium drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's onion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Allium drummondii is also known for its mild, onion-like scent when crushed.
