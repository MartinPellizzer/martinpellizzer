Alopecurus mandshuricus var. glabratus

Alopecurus mandshuricus var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to greenish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture that distinguishes it from other varieties of Alopecurus.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
