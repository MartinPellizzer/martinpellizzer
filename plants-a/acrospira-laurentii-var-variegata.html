Acrospira laurentii var. variegata

Acrospira laurentii var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated laurentii spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that display a striking variegation of white and green patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red or black berries in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and erosion control.
