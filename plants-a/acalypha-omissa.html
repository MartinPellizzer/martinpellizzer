Acalypha omissa

Acalypha omissa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by three-winged fruits known as schizocarps. Acalypha omissa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and forest edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
