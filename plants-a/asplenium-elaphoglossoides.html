Asplenium elaphoglossoides

Asplenium elaphoglossoides, commonly known as the silver spleenwort, is a fern species belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic or lithophytic fern, typically found growing on rocks or trees in moist, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery or pale green coloration on the underside. Its fronds are usually short and compact, with a finely divided structure that gives it a lace-like appearance.

It is widely distributed in temperate regions and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
