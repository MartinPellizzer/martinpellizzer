Achillea pseudopectinata

Achillea pseudopectinata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
