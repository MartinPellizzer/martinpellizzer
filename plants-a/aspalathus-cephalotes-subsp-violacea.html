Aspalathus cephalotes subsp. violacea

Aspalathus cephalotes subsp.

violacea, commonly known as the violet-headed bushveld pea, is a shrub native to South Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, violet-colored flowers.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky outcrops, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
