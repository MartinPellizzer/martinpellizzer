Acmella intermedia

Acmella intermedia, commonly known as the "arrowhead plant," is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and ability to thrive in moist environments. It prefers partial to full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor gardens.

Acmella intermedia is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
