Aquilegia vulgaris var. genuina

Aquilegia vulgaris var. genuina, commonly known as the native columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features distinctive spurred petals that are typically blue or purple, with yellow anthers. The plant produces showy flowers in late spring to early summer, and its foliage consists of deeply lobed, palmate leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
