Acer platanoides f. bicolor

Acer platanoides f. bicolor, commonly known as the white maple, is a variant of the Norway maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive white to pale yellow leaves that contrast with the green veins. This ornamental tree is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth margin.

It thrives in temperate climates and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
