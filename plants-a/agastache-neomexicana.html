Agastache neomexicana

Agastache neomexicana, commonly known as New Mexico hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Agastache neomexicana is also valued for its aromatic foliage and its role in supporting local wildlife.
