Arthropogon lanceolatus

Arthropogon lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Arthropogon lanceolatus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
