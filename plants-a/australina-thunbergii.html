Australina thunbergii

Australina thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese mountain pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in spirals. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of conifers. It thrives in well-drained soils and cooler climates, making it suitable for temperate regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and horticultural use.
