Artanthe caracasana

Artanthe caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring, often with a white or pink center. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
