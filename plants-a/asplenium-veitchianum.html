Asplenium veitchianum

Asplenium veitchianum, commonly known as the Veitch's spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic or lithophytic fern, typically found growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has slender, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and its fronds are usually dark green with a velvety texture. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called sorus, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium veitchianum is noted for its adaptability to various humid environments and its role in ecological systems.
