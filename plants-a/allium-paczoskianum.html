Allium paczoskianum

Allium paczoskianum, commonly known as the Paczoskian garlic, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and is characterized by its bulbous stem and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces a strong garlic scent, similar to other allium species, and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its rarity, it is often found in specialized botanical gardens and conservation sites.
