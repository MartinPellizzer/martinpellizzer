Ammannia occidentalis

Ammannia occidentalis, commonly known as prairie ammannia, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in prairies, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive reddish or purple tinge when mature.

Ammannia occidentalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of wetland environments.
