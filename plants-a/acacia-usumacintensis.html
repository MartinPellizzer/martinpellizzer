Acacia usumacintensis

Acacia usumacintensis, commonly known as the Usumacinta acacia, is a flowering plant native to Central America, particularly found in regions of Mexico and Guatemala.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Acacia usumacintensis is often used in traditional medicine and for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.
