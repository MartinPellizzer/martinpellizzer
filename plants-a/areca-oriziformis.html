Areca oriziformis

Areca oriziformis, commonly known as the rice areca or the rice palm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish-white flowers, which eventually develop into edible fruits. The fruits are typically clustered in panicles and contain a single seed surrounded by a fibrous, starchy pulp.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various regions.
