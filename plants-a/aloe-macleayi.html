Aloe macleayi

Aloe macleayi, commonly known as the Macleay's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aloe family (Asphodelaceae) and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are fleshy and covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow flowers, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is sometimes used for its potential medicinal properties.

Aloe macleayi is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
