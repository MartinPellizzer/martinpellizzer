Acacia simulans

Acacia simulans, commonly known as the mimic acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in dense inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Acacia simulans is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
