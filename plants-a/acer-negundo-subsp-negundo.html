Acer negundo subsp. negundo

Acer negundo subsp.

negundo, commonly known as the box elder, is a deciduous tree native to North America. It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically with three to five leaflets, which are serrated and turn yellow in the fall.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, which appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age, and it often grows in moist, disturbed soils.

The species is adaptable and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in urban and rural landscapes.
