Arundinella hirta var. gracillima

Arundinella hirta var. gracillima, commonly known as slender hairy reed, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and hairy foliage, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, where it forms dense stands. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in panicles, typical of the grass family.

This variety is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
