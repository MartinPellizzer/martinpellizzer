Aphelandra cinnabarina

Aphelandra cinnabarina, commonly known as the red-veined stromanthe, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins against a dark green background. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity.

Aphelandra cinnabarina is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage.
