Alangium kingianum

Alangium kingianum, commonly known as the king's alangium, is a member of the Garcinieae tribe within the family Clusiaceae.

It is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, clustered yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
