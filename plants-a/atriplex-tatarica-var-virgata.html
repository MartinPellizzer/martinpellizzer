Atriplex tatarica var. virgata

Atriplex tatarica var. virgata, commonly known as silverleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, reflective hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its succulent leaves are adapted to store water, making it highly drought-tolerant.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
