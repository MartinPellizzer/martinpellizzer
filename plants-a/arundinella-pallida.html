Arundinella pallida

Arundinella pallida, commonly known as the pale bamboo or pale reed, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies, adapting well to moist environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often forming panicles, and it produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Arundinella pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
