Anthurium scolopendroides

Anthurium scolopendroides, commonly known as the snake plant or tiger's tail, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, waxy leaves that are often variegated with green and white patterns. The plant produces a spadix, a fleshy spike of tiny flowers, enclosed by a showy spathe, which is typically bright red or orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium scolopendroides is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
