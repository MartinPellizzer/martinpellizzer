Allium antiatlanticum

Allium antiatlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic garlic, is a perennial plant native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic Ocean.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous root system and grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy coastal habitats.
