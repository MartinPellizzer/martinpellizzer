Aechmea guaratubensis

Aechmea guaratubensis, commonly known as the Guaratubense Bromeliad, is a species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and protection from intense sunlight.

This bromeliad is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Morphology

Aechmea guaratubensis has rosette-shaped leaves that are narrow, pointed, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a dark green color and a waxy coating.

The plant lacks a prominent stem, with a short, fibrous base that resembles a pseudobark, and it grows upright from the base. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by bright red bracts, with small white to yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a fleshy capsule that disperses seeds through wind and animals.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in the soil, with some aerial roots that aid in water absorption.
