Acer pseudoplatanus f. worleei

Acer pseudoplatanus f. worleei, commonly known as the Worlee maple, is a rare and distinctive variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring leaves with a striking combination of green, red, and yellow hues. This form is typically found in specific microhabitats, often in shaded, moist environments. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Its botanical significance lies in its genetic uniqueness and potential for conservation efforts.
