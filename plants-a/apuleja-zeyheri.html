Apuleja zeyheri

Apuleja zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include long, strap-like leaves and a complex inflorescence that supports multiple blooms.

Apuleja zeyheri is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
