Astragalus aktauensis subsp. chodshamastonicus

Astragalus aktauensis subsp.

chodshamastonicus, commonly known as the Aktau milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers, which is crucial for survival in its native habitat.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
