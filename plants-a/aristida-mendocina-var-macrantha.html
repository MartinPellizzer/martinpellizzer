Aristida mendocina var. macrantha

Aristida mendocina var. macrantha, commonly known as the Mendocino false wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to California.

It typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
