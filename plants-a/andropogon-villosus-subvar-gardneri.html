Andropogon villosus subvar. gardneri

Andropogon villosus subvar. gardneri, commonly known as hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairies and open woodlands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
