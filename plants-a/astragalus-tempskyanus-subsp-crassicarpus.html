Astragalus tempskyanus subsp. crassicarpus

Astragalus tempskyanus subsp.

crassicarpus, commonly known as the thick-fruited milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its thick, fleshy fruits, which are adapted for seed dispersal in arid environments. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine or steppe habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
