Aconitum amoenum

Aconitum amoenum, commonly known as the Chinese aconite or the bellflower aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments, and its roots are tuberous, typical of many members of the genus Aconitum. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

However, it is highly toxic, containing potent alkaloids that can be dangerous to humans and animals.
