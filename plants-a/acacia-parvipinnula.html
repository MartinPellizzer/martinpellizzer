Acacia parvipinnula

Acacia parvipinnula, commonly known as the small-leaved acacia, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, narrow leaves arranged in clusters. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.

Acacia parvipinnula is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
