Aronicum

Aronicum, commonly known as sweet fern, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its fern-like, finely divided leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its aromatic foliage and unique appearance make it a notable species in woodland habitats.

Aronicum is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
