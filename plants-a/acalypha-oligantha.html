Acalypha oligantha

Acalypha oligantha, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges from silver to deep green, often with a metallic sheen. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed capsules. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Acalypha oligantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
