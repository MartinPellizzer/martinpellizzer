Abutilon cornutum

Abutilon cornutum, commonly known as the horned abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Abutilon cornutum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
