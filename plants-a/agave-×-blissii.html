Agave × blissii

Agave × blissii, commonly known as the hybrid agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern, with thick, fleshy leaves that are often blue-gray in color. The plant produces tall, flowering stalks that bear clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which are typical of the Agavaceae family. Its leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Agave × blissii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
