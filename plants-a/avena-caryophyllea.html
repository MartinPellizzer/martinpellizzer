Avena caryophyllea

Avena caryophyllea, commonly known as the purple oat or purple grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny purple or pink flowers. The plant produces oval, three-angled seeds that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate floral display and aromatic foliage.
