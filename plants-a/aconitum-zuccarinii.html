Aconitum zuccarinii

Aconitum zuccarinii, commonly known as the yellow aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces large, palmately divided leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. Its roots contain toxic alkaloids, making it a dangerous plant to handle without proper precautions.

Aconitum zuccarinii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
