Adenophora polymorpha var. rhombifolia

Adenophora polymorpha var. rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombifoliate bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, rhombic-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
