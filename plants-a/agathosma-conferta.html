Agathosma conferta

Agathosma conferta, commonly known as Cape garlic or wild garlic, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small white flowers. The plant is notable for its pungent, garlic-like scent, which is produced by compounds in its foliage. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Due to its unique fragrance and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated for use in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
