Angelica tomentosa var. hendersonii

Angelica tomentosa var. hendersonii, commonly known as the Henderson's angelica, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features tall, slender stems covered in a dense, white, woolly coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in compound umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged, aromatic foliage.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
