Asiasarum sieboldii f. chungbuensis

Asiasarum sieboldii f. chungbuensis, commonly known as the Chungbuensis variety of the Japanese arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features arrow-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in water gardens or as a ground cover.
