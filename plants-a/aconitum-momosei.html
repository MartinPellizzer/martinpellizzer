Aconitum momosei

Aconitum momosei, commonly known as the Momosei monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to blue flowers that resemble a hood, giving it its common name. The plant features large, palmately compound leaves with a coarse texture and a strong, toxic scent. Aconitum momosei grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care, as all parts contain potent alkaloids.
