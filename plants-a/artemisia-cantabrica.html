Artemisia cantabrica

Artemisia cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its strong, medicinal scent.

Artemisia cantabrica is valued for its historical use in herbal medicine and as a source of natural insect repellents.
