Armeria maritima var. ancarensis

Armeria maritima var. ancarensis, commonly known as the coastal thrift, is a perennial flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has succulent, gray-green foliage that is adapted to withstand salty air and coastal winds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for coastal gardens.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize sandy soils.
