Aconitum japonicum f. thunbergii

Aconitum japonicum f. thunbergii, commonly known as Japanese monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in tall, spires, which are typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has large, palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often grown in controlled environments.
