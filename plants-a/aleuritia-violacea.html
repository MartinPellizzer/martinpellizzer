Aleuritia violacea

Aleuritia violacea, commonly known as the purple-flowered elder, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has alternate, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, bright red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor.

Aleuritia violacea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
