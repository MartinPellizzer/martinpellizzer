Acer ornatum var. miyajimense

Acer ornatum var. miyajimense, commonly known as the Miyajima maple, is a variant of the Japanese maple characterized by its striking foliage and ornamental value.

It is native to Japan and is typically found in forested areas with moist, well-drained soils. The plant features palmate leaves that change color throughout the seasons, often displaying shades of red, orange, and yellow in autumn. It grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is widely cultivated in gardens and parks for its visual interest and adaptability to various climates.
