Acacia leptosperma

Acacia leptosperma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, needle-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by flat, pod-like seed pods. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Acacia leptosperma is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
