Allium makrianum

Allium makrianum, commonly known as the Armenian garlic or Persian leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its bulbs are often used in local cuisines for their mild garlic-like flavor.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in traditional dishes.
