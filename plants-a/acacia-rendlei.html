Acacia rendlei

Acacia rendlei, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and phyllodes that are typically yellow to golden in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Acacia rendlei is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various animals.
