Athyriopsis polyrhizon

Athyriopsis polyrhizon, commonly known as the broad-leaf holly fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that are divided into numerous small, pointed segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

The leaves are evergreen, providing a lush green appearance throughout the year.
