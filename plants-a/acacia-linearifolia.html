Acacia linearifolia

Acacia linearifolia, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, and often drooping leaves, which are typically green and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are borne on slender peduncles. Acacia linearifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
