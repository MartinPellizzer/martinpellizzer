Astragalus tesquorum

Astragalus tesquorum, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deeper underground.

Astragalus tesquorum plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
