Acacia horridula var. hastulatoides

Acacia horridula var. hastulatoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It features bipinnate leaves that are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils.
