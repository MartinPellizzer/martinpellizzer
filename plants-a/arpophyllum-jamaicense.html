Arpophyllum jamaicense

Arpophyllum jamaicense, commonly known as Jamaican arnica, is a flowering plant native to Jamaica.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Arpophyllum jamaicense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
