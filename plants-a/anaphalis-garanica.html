Anaphalis garanica

Anaphalis garanica, commonly known as the silverleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Anaphalis garanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
