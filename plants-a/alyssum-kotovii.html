Alyssum kotovii

Alyssum kotovii, commonly known as the Kotov's alyssum, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Alyssum kotovii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
