Ardisia grandis

Ardisia grandis, commonly known as the large arrowwood, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in whorls at the nodes. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by bright red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Ardisia grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
