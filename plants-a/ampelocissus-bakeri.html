Ampelocissus bakeri

Ampelocissus bakeri, commonly known as the African climbing fig, is a woody vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Ampelocissus bakeri thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
