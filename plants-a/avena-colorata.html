Avena colorata

Avena colorata, commonly known as the rainbow oat, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored bracts that range from red to orange, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Avena colorata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
