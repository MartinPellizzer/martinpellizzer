Astragalus eucosmus var. facinorum

Astragalus eucosmus var. facinorum, commonly known as the redstem milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is capable of fixing nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

The varietal name *facinorum* refers to its distinctive, often reddish stems and leaves.
