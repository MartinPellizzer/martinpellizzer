Anemone obtusiloba var. truncata

Anemone obtusiloba var. truncata, commonly known as the Pacific anemone or the western anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are showy, with pink to purple petals arranged in a cluster, and it blooms in early spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
