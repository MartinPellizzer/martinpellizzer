Acacia simplex

Acacia simplex, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Acacia simplex is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
