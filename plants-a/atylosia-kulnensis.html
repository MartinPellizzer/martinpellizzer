Atylosia kulnensis

Atylosia kulnensis, commonly known as the Kuln Desert Sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, succulent stems and large, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has adapted to harsh desert conditions with thick leaves that reduce water loss and deep roots for accessing groundwater. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

Atylosia kulnensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging environments.
