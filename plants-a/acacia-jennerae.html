Acacia jennerae

Acacia jennerae, commonly known as the Jenner's wattle, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia jennerae is often used in ecological restoration due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soils.
