Avena crassifolia

Avena crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaf oat, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with broad, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Avena crassifolia is well adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage structure.
