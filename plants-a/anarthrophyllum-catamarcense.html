Anarthrophyllum catamarcense

Anarthrophyllum catamarcense, commonly known as the Catamarca grass, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in dry and saline environments, making it a hardy species in harsh conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically arranged in spikelets, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Due to its ecological importance and unique adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
