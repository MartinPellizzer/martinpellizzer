Astragalus titovii

Astragalus titovii, commonly known as the Titov's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppes and semi-desert environments.

Astragalus titovii plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
