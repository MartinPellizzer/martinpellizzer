Aconitum degenii subsp. rhaeticum

Aconitum degenii subsp.

rhaeticum, commonly known as the Rhaetic aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps and adjacent mountain regions. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stem and striking blue to purple flowers.

The plant produces showy, helmet-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. Its leaves are palmately compound, with finely divided lobes, and the plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and alpine plant collections.
