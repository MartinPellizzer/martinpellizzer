Amaranthus mitchellii

Amaranthus mitchellii, commonly known as Mitchell's amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, lance-shaped leaves, and vibrant red or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Amaranthus mitchellii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and floral display.
