Alnus glutinosa subsp. glutinosa

Alnus glutinosa subsp.

glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky alder, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are often covered in a sticky resinous substance.

The tree produces small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers in catkins, followed by woody, cone-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with bacteria in its root nodules.
