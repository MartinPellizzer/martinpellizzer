Acinodendron octopetalum

Acinodendron octopetalum, commonly known as the eight-petaled acacia, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
