Amomum nutans

Amomum nutans, commonly known as the nodding amomum, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that droop downward, giving it its common name. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Amomum nutans thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
