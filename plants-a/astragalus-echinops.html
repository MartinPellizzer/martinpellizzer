Astragalus echinops

Astragalus echinops, commonly known as the spiny milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to harsh, semi-desert environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus echinops plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
