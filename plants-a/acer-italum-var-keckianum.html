Acer italum var. keckianum

Acer italum var. keckianum, commonly known as the Keck's maple, is a rare and less commonly cultivated variant of the Italian maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features palmate leaves with a serrated margin and a vibrant green coloration. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking autumn foliage, which can display shades of red, orange, and yellow.
