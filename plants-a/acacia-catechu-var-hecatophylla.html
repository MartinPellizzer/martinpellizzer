Acacia catechu var. hecatophylla

Acacia catechu var. hecatophylla, commonly known as the Indian gum acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are often feathery and composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow to golden-green flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Its bark and sap are rich in tannins, making it valuable for traditional medicine and industrial uses.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
