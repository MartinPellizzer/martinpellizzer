Athyrium acrostichoideum

Athyrium acrostichoideum, commonly known as the silver lace fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant has pinnate leaves with numerous small, pointed leaflets arranged in a regular pattern.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in indoor conditions.
