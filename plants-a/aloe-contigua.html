Aloe contigua

Aloe contigua, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear yellow flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Aloe contigua is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
