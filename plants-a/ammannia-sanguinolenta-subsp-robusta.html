Ammannia sanguinolenta subsp. robusta

Ammannia sanguinolenta subsp.

robusta, commonly known as the redroot ammannia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Americas. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to saturated soils.

The plant features erect stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a waxy coating. Small, clustered flowers appear in late summer, producing a pinkish to reddish hue, and the plant is known for its showy, bright red berries that persist into winter.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
