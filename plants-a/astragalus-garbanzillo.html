Astragalus garbanzillo

Astragalus garbanzillo, commonly known as the Mexican milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus garbanzillo is often found in scrublands and grasslands, contributing to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
