Allium thunbergii var. deltoides

Allium thunbergii var. deltoides, commonly known as the Japanese garlic chives or broadleaf garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to East Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often used in Asian cuisine for their mild garlic flavor. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
