Alcea ficifolia subsp. karsiana

Alcea ficifolia subsp.

karsiana, commonly known as the Karsian hollyhock, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Caucasus region. It features upright, branching stems covered in velvety, greenish-gray foliage.

The plant produces showy, rose-like flowers in shades of pink and red, typically blooming in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after addition to gardens and botanical collections.
