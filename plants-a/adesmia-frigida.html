Adesmia frigida

Adesmia frigida, commonly known as the cold-adapted legume, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and erect, sparsely branched stems. It produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color.

Adesmia frigida is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine or subarctic environments.
