Artocarpus rufescens

Artocarpus rufescens, commonly known as the red-fig or red-fruit breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spiky fruit clusters. The fruit, which matures to a reddish color, contains edible seeds encased in a fibrous pulp. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

It plays an important role in both ecological systems and local cuisines in its native regions.
