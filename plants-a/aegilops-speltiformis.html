Aegilops speltiformis

Aegilops speltiformis, commonly known as false spelt, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, two-lobed spikelets that are typically pale green to reddish in color. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Aegilops speltiformis has been studied for its potential in breeding programs due to its genetic contributions to wheat improvement.

Aegilops speltiformis has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically linear in shape, arranged in a basal rosette, and have a bluish-green color.

Its inflorescence consists of spikelets with small, two-lipped flowers that are pale green to brown in color, blooming in late spring to early summer, and are primarily wind-pollinated. The fruit is a dry, dehiscent spikelet that releases small, hard seeds through mechanical means, and the seeds are dispersed by wind.
