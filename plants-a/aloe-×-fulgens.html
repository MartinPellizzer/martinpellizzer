Aloe × fulgens

Aloe × fulgens, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Aloe vera and Aloe aristata.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that bloom in the winter months. The plant has thick, segmented stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, with a waxy coating that reduces water loss.

Aloe × fulgens is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
