Achillea fasciculata

Achillea fasciculata, commonly known as narrowleaf yarrow, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. It is often found in the western United States and parts of Canada.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
