Androcalva bivillosa

Androcalva bivillosa, commonly known as the two-horned orchid, is a unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, featuring two prominent, fleshy appendages that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate and often showy flowers. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
