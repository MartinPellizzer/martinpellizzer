Acalypha decumbens var. cordata

Acalypha decumbens var. cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf copperleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub with distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from red to yellow, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
