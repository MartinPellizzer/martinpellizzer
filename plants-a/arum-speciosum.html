Arum speciosum

Arum speciosum, commonly known as the showy arum, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive, spadix-bearing inflorescence. The plant produces a showy, often purple or greenish spathe that surrounds the central spadix, which contains the actual flowers. Arum speciosum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also noted for its ability to tolerate a range of soil types, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.
