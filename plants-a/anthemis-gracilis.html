Anthemis gracilis

Anthemis gracilis, commonly known as slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its slender stems and small, yellow flowers make it a distinctive yet often overlooked member of the flora.

It is also noted for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats, contributing to its widespread distribution.
