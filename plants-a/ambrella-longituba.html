Ambrella longituba

Ambrella longituba, commonly known as the long-stemmed ambrella, is a unique member of the Ambrellaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, tubular stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces oval, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It is native to specific regions in South America and thrives in moist, tropical environments.

Due to its rare occurrence, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
