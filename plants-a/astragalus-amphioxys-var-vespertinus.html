Astragalus amphioxys var. vespertinus

Astragalus amphioxys var. vespertinus, commonly known as the evening astragalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
