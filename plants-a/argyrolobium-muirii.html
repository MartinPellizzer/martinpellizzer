Argyrolobium muirii

Argyrolobium muirii, commonly known as the Muir's silver globular pea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive globular shape and silvery foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
