Abies forrestii

Abies forrestii, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central and western China.

It belongs to the genus Abies, which includes other firs such as the balsam fir and the grand fir. The tree is characterized by its conical shape, dark green needles, and distinctive cones that hang downward. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

The bark is typically reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age, adding to its ornamental value.
