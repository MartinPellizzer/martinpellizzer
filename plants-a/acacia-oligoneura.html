Acacia oligoneura

Acacia oligoneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, feathery leaves and yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.

The wood of Acacia oligoneura is hard and durable, making it useful for construction and fuel.
