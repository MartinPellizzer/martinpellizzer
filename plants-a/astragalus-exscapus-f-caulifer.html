Astragalus exscapus f. caulifer

Astragalus exscapus f. caulifer, commonly known as the cushion milkvetch, is a low-growing, perennial plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It features small, compound leaves with numerous leaflets, and its flowers are typically pale yellow to greenish in color. The plant produces small, legume-like pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh conditions.
