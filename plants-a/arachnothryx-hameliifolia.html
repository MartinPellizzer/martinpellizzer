Arachnothryx hameliifolia

Arachnothryx hameliifolia, commonly known as the Hameliifolia arachnothryx, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne on short peduncles. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
