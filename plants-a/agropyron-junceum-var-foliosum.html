Agropyron junceum var. foliosum

Agropyron junceum var. foliosum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in width. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects and as a forage plant for livestock.
