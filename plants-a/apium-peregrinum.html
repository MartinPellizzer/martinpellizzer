Apium peregrinum

Apium peregrinum, commonly known as wild celery, is a flowering plant in the family Apiaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in moist, calcareous soils. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellow flowers that appear in umbels. Its roots are often harvested for culinary or medicinal use.

Apium peregrinum is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
