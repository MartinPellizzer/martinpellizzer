Agrimonia eupatoria var. sepium

Agrimonia eupatoria var. sepium, commonly known as common agrimony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are pinnately compound with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in loose clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its fruits are dry, capsule-like structures containing numerous tiny seeds.

Agrimonia eupatoria var. sepium has been historically used in herbal medicine for its purported astringent and anti-inflammatory properties.
