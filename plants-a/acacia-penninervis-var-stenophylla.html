Acacia penninervis var. stenophylla

Acacia penninervis var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and narrow, phyllodes that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
