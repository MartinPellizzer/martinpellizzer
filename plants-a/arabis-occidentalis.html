Arabis occidentalis

Arabis occidentalis, commonly known as Western rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arid environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and the plant is generally low-growing with a compact habit.
