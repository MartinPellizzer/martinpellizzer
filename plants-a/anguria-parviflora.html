Anguria parviflora

Anguria parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered anguria, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features delicate, small white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated margin. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice among gardeners interested in native flora.
