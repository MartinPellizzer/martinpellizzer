Actinodaphne citrata

Actinodaphne citrata, commonly known as the citron laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its essential oils are also used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
