Acacia gemina

Acacia gemina, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. Acacia gemina is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Its wood is durable and has been used traditionally for various purposes, including construction and fuel.
