Artanthe rivinoides

Artanthe rivinoides, commonly known as the river artanthe, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Artanthe rivinoides is often found in moist, shaded environments such as riverbanks and forest understories.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for garden use.
