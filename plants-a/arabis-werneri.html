Arabis werneri

Arabis werneri, commonly known as Werneri's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many familiar garden plants such as cabbages and mustards. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and arranged in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its natural habitat.

Arabis werneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its native conditions.
