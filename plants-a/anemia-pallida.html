Anemia pallida

Anemia pallida, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a rare and delicate species characterized by its translucent, pale green leaves that resemble ghostly apparitions.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate rainforests or along stream banks. The plant's botanical features include thin, waxy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early spring. Its unique appearance is due to a lack of chlorophyll, which gives it a ghostly, almost translucent look.

Despite its fragile appearance, it plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for small invertebrates.
