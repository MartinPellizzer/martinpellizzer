Amomum dallachyi

Amomum dallachyi, commonly known as the Yunnan cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by aromatic, three-lobed fruits. Its rhizomes are valued for their medicinal properties, often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their warming and digestive effects.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in subtropical forests.
