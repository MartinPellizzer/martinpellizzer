Aspalathus biflora subsp. biflora

Aspalathus biflora subsp.

biflora, commonly known as the Cape tea or African red bush, is a shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa. It is characterized by its small, bright red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
