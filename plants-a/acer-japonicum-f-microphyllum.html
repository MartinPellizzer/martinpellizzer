Acer japonicum f. microphyllum

Acer japonicum f. microphyllum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with small leaves, is a compact cultivar of the Japanese maple.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, palmate leaves that are smaller than those of the typical species. The foliage displays a range of colors, from bright green in spring to fiery red or orange in autumn. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
