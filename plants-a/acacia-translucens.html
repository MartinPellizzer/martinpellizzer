Acacia translucens

Acacia translucens, commonly known as the transparent acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces flat, ribbed pods that contain seeds coated in a sticky, resinous substance. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval leaflets.

Acacia translucens is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.
