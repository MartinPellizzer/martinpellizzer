Astragalus oophorus var. oophorus

Astragalus oophorus var. oophorus, commonly known as the "eggplant milkvetch," is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across suitable habitats.
