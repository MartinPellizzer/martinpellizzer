Acanthocarpus parviflorus

Acanthocarpus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered burr grass, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, also known as the legume or bean family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive burr-like seed pods that are covered in hooked bracts, aiding in seed dispersal by attaching to animals. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland grassland habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects for soil stabilization.
