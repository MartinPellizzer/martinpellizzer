Astragalus distortus

Astragalus distortus, commonly known as the twisted milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, twisted pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments. It has small, compound leaves and produces yellow to pale purple flowers.

Astragalus distortus is a perennial species that plays a role in stabilizing soils in arid regions.
