Alchemilla roccatii

Alchemilla roccatii, commonly known as the Sicilian lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Alchemilla roccatii thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and ability to attract beneficial insects.
