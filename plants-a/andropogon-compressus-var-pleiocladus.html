Andropogon compressus var. pleiocladus

Andropogon compressus var. pleiocladus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, bluish-green leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
