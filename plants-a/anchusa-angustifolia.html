Anchusa angustifolia

Anchusa angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological value.

Anchusa angustifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
