Anthurium chorranum

Anthurium chorranum, commonly known as the Andean flame, is a tropical plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces vibrant red spadices, which are the inflorescences that house the small, white flowers. Anthurium chorranum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and greenhouse cultivation.
