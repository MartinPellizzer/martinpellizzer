Ampelopsis megalophylla var. puberula

Ampelopsis megalophylla var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy broadleaf grape, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture on both sides, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, sweet berries. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a striking, vine-like plant.
