Aconitum lobelianum f. brachytrichum

Aconitum lobelianum f. brachytrichum, commonly known as the short-haired monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, conical clusters. The plant features palmately divided, compound leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a fine, short trichome layer, giving it its specific epithet. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
