Amomum hirsutum

Amomum hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy cardamom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and spikes of fragrant, red or yellow flowers. The plant produces small, dark brown, three-lobed fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic properties.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
