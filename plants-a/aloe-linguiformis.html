Aloe linguiformis

Aloe linguiformis, commonly known as the tongue aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear fleshy, sword-shaped leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. The leaves are typically green with a waxy texture and may have a reddish or purple tinge at the base. It produces yellow flowers on tall, branched inflorescences, which bloom during the summer months.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
