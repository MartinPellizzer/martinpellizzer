Agrimonia eupatoria var. grandis

Agrimonia eupatoria var. grandis, commonly known as greater agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a slightly rough texture, while the plant is known for its medicinal properties.

Agrimonia eupatoria var. grandis is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
