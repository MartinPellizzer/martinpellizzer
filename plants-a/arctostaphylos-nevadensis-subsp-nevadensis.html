Arctostaphylos nevadensis subsp. nevadensis

Arctostaphylos nevadensis subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring.

The plant produces berries that are typically red or black, providing food for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky environments.

This subspecies is known for its resilience to drought and cold temperatures.
