Allium giraudiasii

Allium giraudiasii, commonly known as the Giraud's leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem bearing a single, umbel-like inflorescence composed of small, white to pale purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium giraudiasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
