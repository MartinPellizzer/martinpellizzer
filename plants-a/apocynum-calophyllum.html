Apocynum calophyllum

Apocynum calophyllum, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweed species known for their milky sap. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is often found in moist, open habitats like woodlands and along stream banks.
