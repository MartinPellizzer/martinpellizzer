Adenostylis gracilis

Adenostylis gracilis, commonly known as the slender leaching grass, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring.

Adenostylis gracilis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for invertebrates in its native ecosystem.
