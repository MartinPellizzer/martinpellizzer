Aristida densispica

Aristida densispica, commonly known as the dense spike grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets that are densely packed along the central axis, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and wiry, with a rough texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer.

Aristida densispica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
