Achillea oxyloba subsp. mucronulata

Achillea oxyloba subsp.

mucronulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branched stems that are covered with fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters called cymes. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a distinctive silvery-gray underside due to dense pubescence.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
