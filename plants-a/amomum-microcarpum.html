Amomum microcarpum

Amomum microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited cardamom, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces tiny, dark brown fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal properties.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves and fruits have been used for their antispasmodic and digestive benefits.
