Atalanta

Atalanta, commonly known as the "butterfly plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apocynaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The name "Atalanta" is derived from the Greek mythological figure, reflecting the plant's association with beauty and attraction.
