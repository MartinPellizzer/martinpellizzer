Arctagrostis arundinacea

Arctagrostis arundinacea, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Silky bluegrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
