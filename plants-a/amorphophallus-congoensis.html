Amorphophallus congoensis

Amorphophallus congoensis, commonly known as the Congo tiger's claw, is a tropical plant native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that resembles a flower spike. The plant produces a single, fleshy, underground tuber that serves as its storage organ. Its leaves are typically large and arrow-shaped, contributing to its striking appearance.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
