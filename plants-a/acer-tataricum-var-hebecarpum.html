Acer tataricum var. hebecarpum

Acer tataricum var. hebecarpum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by distinctive samaras with narrow, winged seeds. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
