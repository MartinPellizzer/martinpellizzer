Artocarpus erythrocarpus

Artocarpus erythrocarpus, commonly known as the red-fruit breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces large, round fruits that are typically reddish when mature, containing a starchy, edible pulp surrounded by a fibrous outer layer. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes. The fruit is often used in traditional cuisines and has cultural significance in various regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other economically important plants like the fig and mulberry.
