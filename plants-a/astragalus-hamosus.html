Astragalus hamosus

Astragalus hamosus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and steppe habitats.

Astragalus hamosus plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its ecosystem.
