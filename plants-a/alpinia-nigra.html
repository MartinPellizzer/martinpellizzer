Alpinia nigra

Alpinia nigra, commonly known as black ginger, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black rhizomes, which are rich in essential oils and have a strong, spicy aroma. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by red berries. Its botanical significance lies in its aromatic compounds and medicinal uses in traditional medicine.

Alpinia nigra thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its culinary and therapeutic properties.
