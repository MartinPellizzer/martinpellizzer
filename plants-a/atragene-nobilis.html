Atragene nobilis

Atragene nobilis, commonly known as the noble atragene, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant typically grows to a height of 30-60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in partial shade. Its blooming season occurs during the spring and early summer months.

Atragene nobilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
