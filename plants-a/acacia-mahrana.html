Acacia mahrana

Acacia mahrana, commonly known as the white acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, feather-like leaflets.

Acacia mahrana is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
