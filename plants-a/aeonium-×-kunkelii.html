Aeonium × kunkelii

Aeonium × kunkelii, commonly known as the "Coral Candelabra," is a hybrid succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features a compact, multi-stemmed rosette structure with thick, fleshy leaves that range from green to red or purple, depending on light exposure. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear yellow flowers, typically blooming in spring or early summer. Aeonium × kunkelii is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements contribute to its popularity among succulent enthusiasts.
