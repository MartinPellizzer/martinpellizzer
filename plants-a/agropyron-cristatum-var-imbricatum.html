Agropyron cristatum var. imbricatum

Agropyron cristatum var. imbricatum, commonly known as the crested wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the cold climates of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its distinctive crested inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant has a robust root system that makes it highly drought-resistant and suitable for erosion control. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often used in revegetation projects.

Its hardiness and adaptability make it a valuable species in arid and semi-arid regions.
