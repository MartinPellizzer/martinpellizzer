Aster moelleri

Aster moelleri, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and showy purple or pink flowers. Aster moelleri thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
