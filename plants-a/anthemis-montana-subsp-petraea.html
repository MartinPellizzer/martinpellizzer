Anthemis montana subsp. petraea

Anthemis montana subsp.

petraea, commonly known as the alpine everlasting, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers.

The plant produces narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and dry grasslands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh environmental conditions.
