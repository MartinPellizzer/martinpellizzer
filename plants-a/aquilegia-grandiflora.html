Aquilegia grandiflora

Aquilegia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered columbine, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features striking, spurred petals that come in shades of red, purple, and blue, arranged in a distinctive spurred shape. The plant produces showy, compound flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of palmately divided leaves, often with a bluish-green hue.

Aquilegia grandiflora thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
