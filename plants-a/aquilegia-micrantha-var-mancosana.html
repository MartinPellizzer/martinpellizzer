Aquilegia micrantha var. mancosana

Aquilegia micrantha var. mancosana, commonly known as the Manchosa columbine, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has palmate leaves with finely divided lobes, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
