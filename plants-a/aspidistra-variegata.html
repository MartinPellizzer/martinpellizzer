Aspidistra variegata

Aspidistra variegata, commonly known as the variegated aspidistra, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or yellow variegation. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring a short, stout rhizome and broad, lance-shaped foliage. It thrives in low-light conditions and prefers moist, well-draining soil.

Aspidistra variegata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive indoors with minimal care.
