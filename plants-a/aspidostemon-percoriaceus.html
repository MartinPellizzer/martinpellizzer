Aspidostemon percoriaceus

Aspidostemon percoriaceus, commonly known as the slender leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant is a climbing vine with a fibrous, hairy stem that often twines around supports. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Aspidostemon percoriaceus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
