Aeschynomene laxa

Aeschynomene laxa, commonly known as silverleaf desmodium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous annual that produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to violet. The plant has trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets and is often found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Aeschynomene laxa is also used in some regions for forage and as a cover crop due to its hardiness and adaptability.
