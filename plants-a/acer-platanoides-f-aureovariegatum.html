Acer platanoides f. aureovariegatum

Acer platanoides f. aureovariegatum, commonly known as the golden-leaved Norway maple, is a variegated form of the Norway maple.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which features a mix of green, white, and gold colors. The leaves are typically ovate to broadly ovate with a coarsely toothed margin. This plant is a deciduous tree that thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
