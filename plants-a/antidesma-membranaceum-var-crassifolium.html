Antidesma membranaceum var. crassifolium

Antidesma membranaceum var. crassifolium, commonly known as the thick-leaved antidesma, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, spherical berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
