Acer truncatum var. paxii

Acer truncatum var. paxii, commonly known as the Pacific maple, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellow-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that disperse its seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and ornamental contexts.
