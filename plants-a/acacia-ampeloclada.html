Acacia ampeloclada

Acacia ampeloclada, commonly known as the climbing acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twining branches and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky slopes or in open woodlands.

The species is adapted to drought conditions and can tolerate poor soil quality.
