Aristea racemosa

Aristea racemosa, commonly known as the clustered arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Aristea racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
