Acacia paucijuga

Acacia paucijuga, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spherical clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Acacia paucijuga is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its ornamental appeal.
