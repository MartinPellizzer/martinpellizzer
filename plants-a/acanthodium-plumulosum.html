Acanthodium plumulosum

Acanthodium plumulosum, commonly known as the plumose acanthodium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its showy, bract-like flowers that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences that resemble plumes.

This plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
