Aniseion

Aniseion, commonly known as wild anise, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and carrots. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in umbrella-shaped clusters. Its seeds are often used in culinary applications due to their licorice-like flavor.

Aniseion is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
