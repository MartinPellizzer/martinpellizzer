Andropogon esenbeckii

Andropogon esenbeckii, commonly known as bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green leaves that turn golden in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Andropogon esenbeckii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
