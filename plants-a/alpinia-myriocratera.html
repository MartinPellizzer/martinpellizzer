Alpinia myriocratera

Alpinia myriocratera, commonly known as the mountain ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect rhizomatous stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine. Alpinia myriocratera thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers shaded to semi-shaded environments.

It is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its medicinal properties.
