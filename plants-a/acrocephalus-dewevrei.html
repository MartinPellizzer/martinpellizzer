Acrocephalus dewevrei

Acrocephalus dewevrei, commonly known as the dwarf reed or small-headed reed, is a species of grass found primarily in wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshes or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grassy, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Acrocephalus dewevrei plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic and avian species.
