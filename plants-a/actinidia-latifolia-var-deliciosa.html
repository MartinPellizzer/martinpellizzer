Actinidia latifolia var. deliciosa

Actinidia latifolia var. deliciosa, commonly known as the fuzzy kiwi, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces edible berries that are covered in a fuzzy, hairy coating, which gives it its common name. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often consumed fresh or processed into juice.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires a trellis or support for its climbing growth habit.
