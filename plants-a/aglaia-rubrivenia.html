Aglaia rubrivenia

Aglaia rubrivenia, commonly known as the red-veined aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other valuable timber species. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves with prominent red veins, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red fruits that contain hard, angular seeds.

Due to its attractive appearance and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and for traditional uses.
