Allaeophania rugosa

Allaeophania rugosa, commonly known as the rough-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features distinctive, rough-textured stems and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Allaeophania rugosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
