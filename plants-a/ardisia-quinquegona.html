Ardisia quinquegona

Ardisia quinquegona, commonly known as the five-lobed beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that appear in the spring, followed by bright purple berries in the fall. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The berries are a food source for various birds and small mammals, contributing to its ecological importance.
