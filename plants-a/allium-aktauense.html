Allium aktauense

Allium aktauense, commonly known as the Aktau garlic, is a bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are often used in local cuisines for their pungent flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique characteristics, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
