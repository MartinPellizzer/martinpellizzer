Asarca venosa

Asarca venosa, commonly known as the veined asarum, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with prominent veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in a spike, typically blooming in early spring. Asarca venosa thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
