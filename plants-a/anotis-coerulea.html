Anotis coerulea

Anotis coerulea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Anotis coerulea thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.
