Abarema globosa

Abarema globosa, commonly known as the globular acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its distinctive globular inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, and the flowers are yellow, forming dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

Abarema globosa is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
