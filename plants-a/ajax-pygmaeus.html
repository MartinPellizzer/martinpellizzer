Ajax pygmaeus

Ajax pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf ajax, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
