Anthemis altissima

Anthemis altissima, commonly known as the tall tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
