Allium barsczewskii

Allium barsczewskii, commonly known as the Polish leek or mountain leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear umbels of white or pale pink flowers. Its bulbous roots are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor, making it a valuable culinary and medicinal plant.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
