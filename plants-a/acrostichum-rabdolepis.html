Acrostichum rabdolepis

Acrostichum rabdolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated fronds that are typically divided into small, lance-shaped pinnae. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures include spore-bearing soruses located on the underside of the fronds.

Acrostichum rabdolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
