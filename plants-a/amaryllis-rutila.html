Amaryllis rutila

Amaryllis rutila, commonly known as the red amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red and orange. The foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are dark green and often variegated. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, long-lasting blooms.

Its ability to produce multiple flower stalks from a single bulb adds to its ornamental appeal.
