Acinos acinos

Acinos acinos, commonly known as the alpine everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its compact, cushion-like form and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
