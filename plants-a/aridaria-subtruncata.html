Aridaria subtruncata

Aridaria subtruncata, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Aridaria subtruncata is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, where it thrives with minimal water input.
