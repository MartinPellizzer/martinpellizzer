Adiantopsis parvisegmenta

Adiantopsis parvisegmenta, commonly known as the small-segmented maidenhair fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as under forest canopies, where it thrives in high humidity and moderate temperatures. Its spores are produced on the underside of its fronds, and the plant reproduces through spore dispersal rather than seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Adiantopsis parvisegmenta is considered a rare and often endangered species.
