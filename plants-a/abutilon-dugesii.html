Abutilon dugesii

Abutilon dugesii, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its rough, woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom from summer to fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it well-suited for xeriscaping in dry climates.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and drought-tolerant addition to native plant gardens.
