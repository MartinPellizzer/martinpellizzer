Aconitum hemsleyanum var. chingtungense

Aconitum hemsleyanum var. chingtungense, commonly known as the Chingtung monkshood, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are compound, with deeply lobed foliage that resembles that of other Aconitum species.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
