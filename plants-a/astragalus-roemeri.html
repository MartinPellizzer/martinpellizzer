Astragalus roemeri

Astragalus roemeri, commonly known as the Roemer's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects.

Astragalus roemeri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
