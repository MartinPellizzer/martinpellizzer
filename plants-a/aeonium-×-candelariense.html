Aeonium × candelariense

Aeonium × candelariense, commonly known as the "Candelabra Aeonium," is a hybrid succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern, with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green or blue-gray in color. The plant produces tall, branching stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant among succulent enthusiasts.
