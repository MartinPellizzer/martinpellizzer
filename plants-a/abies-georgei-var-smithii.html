Abies georgei var. smithii

Abies georgei var. smithii, commonly known as the Smith's fir, is a variant of the George fir, native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern China.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The cones are typically small and reddish-brown, maturing to a darker hue. This species thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
