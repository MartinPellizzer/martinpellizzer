Andinia masdevalliopsis

Andinia masdevalliopsis, commonly known as the Masdevallia orchid, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features intricate, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, colorful flowers with a unique, spurred lip. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
