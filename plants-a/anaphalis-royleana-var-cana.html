Anaphalis royleana var. cana

Anaphalis royleana var. cana, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces fluffy, cloud-like inflorescences that are composed of numerous small, pale yellow flowers. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
