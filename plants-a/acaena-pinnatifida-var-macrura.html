Acaena pinnatifida var. macrura

Acaena pinnatifida var. macrura, commonly known as the giant New Zealand flax, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has fibrous stems that are used for making baskets and other woven items. It thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
