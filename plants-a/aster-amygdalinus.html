Aster amygdalinus

Aster amygdalinus, commonly known as the sweet William, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds enclosed in a capsule, which is a common feature among members of the daisy family. Aster amygdalinus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
