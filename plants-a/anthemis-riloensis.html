Anthemis riloensis

Anthemis riloensis, commonly known as the Armenian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by a ring of white to pale yellow disk flowers, typical of the genus Anthemis. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny, rocky habitats.

Its aromatic properties make it a subject of interest in traditional medicine and horticulture.
