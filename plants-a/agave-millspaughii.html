Agave millspaughii

Agave millspaughii, commonly known as the blue agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, which help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears small, white flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The plant's rosette structure and striking foliage make it a visually appealing addition to gardens and landscapes.
