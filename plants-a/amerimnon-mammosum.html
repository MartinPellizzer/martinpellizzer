Amerimnon mammosum

Amerimnon mammosum, commonly known as the mammillary cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. Its roots are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor, dry soils.

Overall, Amerimnon mammosum exemplifies the adaptive strategies of desert flora.
