Arenaria cossoniana

Arenaria cossoniana, commonly known as the Cosson's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually succulent and fleshy, helping it retain moisture in arid conditions.

Arenaria cossoniana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in difficult garden settings.
