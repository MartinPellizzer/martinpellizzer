Anthyllis macrocarpa

Anthyllis macrocarpa, commonly known as the broad-leaved broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of yellow flowers. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are often woody at the base. Anthyllis macrocarpa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
