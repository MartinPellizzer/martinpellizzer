Aster capillaris

Aster capillaris, commonly known as the threadleaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and features slender, hairy stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom from late summer into early fall. Its leaves are linear and finely toothed, contributing to its delicate appearance.

Aster capillaris is often found in moist soils along streambanks and in open woodlands.
