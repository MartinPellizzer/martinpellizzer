Afroaster zuluensis

Afroaster zuluensis, commonly known as the Zulu aster, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salty air. It features showy yellow flowers with a central disk, and its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like.

Afroaster zuluensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens.
