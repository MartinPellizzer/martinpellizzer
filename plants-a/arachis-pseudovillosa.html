Arachis pseudovillosa

Arachis pseudovillosa, commonly known as the hairy groundnut, is a species of legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods containing one to three kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used as a food source. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental or for its edible seeds.
