Amsinckia ammophila

Amsinckia ammophila, commonly known as the beach bur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of California.

The plant has a low, sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. Its flowers bloom in the spring, producing yellowish-green petals and producing small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Amsinckia ammophila is well adapted to sandy soils and coastal areas, making it a common sight along beaches and dunes.
