Artocarpus gomezianus subsp. zeylanicus

Artocarpus gomezianus subsp.

zeylanicus, commonly known as the Ceylon breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Sri Lanka. It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fruit with a fibrous outer layer.

The fruit, which is typically harvested when mature, contains a starchy, edible pulp and seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in traditional cuisines.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
