Andropogon sorghum var. wightii

Andropogon sorghum var. wightii, commonly known as Wight's sorghum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically pale green to purplish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Wight's sorghum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional ecological systems.
