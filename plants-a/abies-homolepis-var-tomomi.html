Abies homolepis var. tomomi

Abies homolepis var. tomomi, commonly known as the Tomomi fir, is a rare variant of the Japanese fir.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan, specifically found in the central and southern regions. The tree has slender, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged spirally around the branches. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family.

This variant is notable for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
