Acrostichum argyrophyllum

Acrostichum argyrophyllum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a shimmering appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and wetlands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The silver fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture.
