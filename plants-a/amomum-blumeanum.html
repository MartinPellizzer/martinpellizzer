Amomum blumeanum

Amomum blumeanum, commonly known as the Java cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by dark brown, three-lobed fruits. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is cultivated for both its medicinal value and culinary uses.
