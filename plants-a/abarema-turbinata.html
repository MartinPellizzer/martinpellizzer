Abarema turbinata

Abarema turbinata, commonly known as the Turbinated Abarema, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, turbinated (spiral-shaped) seed pods. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

Abarema turbinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
