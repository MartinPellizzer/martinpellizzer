Aloe uvaria

Aloe uvaria, commonly known as the tree aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, tree-like structure with a thick, fleshy trunk and a crown of fleshy, spiky leaves. The leaves are typically green with a waxy coating, which helps the plant retain moisture in arid environments. It produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Aloe uvaria is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
