Attalea bassleriana

Attalea bassleriana, commonly known as the Amazonian palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which develop into elongated, fibrous fruits. Its trunk is typically unbranched and covered with a thick layer of fibrous material.

This species is valued for its hardy nature and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
