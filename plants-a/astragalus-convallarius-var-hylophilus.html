Astragalus convallarius var. hylophilus

Astragalus convallarius var. hylophilus, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its botanical features include a taproot system and small, pod-like fruits that disperse seeds through the wind.
