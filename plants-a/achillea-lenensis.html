Achillea lenensis

Achillea lenensis, commonly known as the Lenensian yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust root system and upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are basal, pinnately divided, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

Achillea lenensis is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments.
