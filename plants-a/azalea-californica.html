Azalea californica

Azalea californica, commonly known as the California azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
