Acourtia arachnolepis

Acourtia arachnolepis, commonly known as the spider-hair acourtia, is a flowering plant native to California and Baja California.

It is a small annual herb with hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its unique name comes from the hairy, spider-like structures on its stems.

Acourtia arachnolepis is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and coastal areas.
