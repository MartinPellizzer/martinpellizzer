Arenaria tenuior var. balearica

Arenaria tenuior var. balearica, commonly known as the Balearic sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has slender, hairy stems and linear leaves that are adapted to arid conditions. It is often found in coastal areas and is well-suited to coastal gardens due to its drought tolerance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize sandy soils.
