Astragalus albispinus subsp. esfandiarii

Astragalus albispinus subsp.

esfandiarii, commonly known as the white-spined milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry environments, with deep root systems that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct morphological and ecological traits compared to other Astragalus species.
