Agave bourgaei

Agave bourgaei, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces an inflorescence of yellow flowers that bloom after several years. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.

Agave bourgaei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional crafts.
