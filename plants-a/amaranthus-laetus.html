Amaranthus laetus

Amaranthus laetus, commonly known as the purple amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy inflorescences that range from red to purple. The plant produces small, bead-like seeds that are often used as a grain or for decorative purposes. Its leaves are edible and can be consumed raw or cooked, making it a valuable crop in some regions.

Amaranthus laetus is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils.
