Anemone baicalensis var. titenkinii

Anemone baicalensis var. titenkinii, commonly known as the Baikal Anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It features showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate, with a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or wetland habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a species of interest in traditional medicine and its ornamental value in gardens.
