Acropera galeata

Acropera galeata, commonly known as the golden spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that resemble spiders. The plant typically grows in swampy or seasonally flooded areas, preferring rich, moist soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, delicate flowers during the spring and summer months.

Acropera galeata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate moisture conditions.
