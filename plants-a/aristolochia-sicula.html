Aristolochia sicula

Aristolochia sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian birthwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, winding, pipe-like flowers. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, musky odor that attracts specific pollinators.

The plant contains aristolochic acids, which have been studied for their potential pharmacological effects.
