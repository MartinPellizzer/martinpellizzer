Astragalus altus

Astragalus altus, commonly known as the tall milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Astragalus altus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
