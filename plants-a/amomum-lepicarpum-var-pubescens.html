Amomum lepicarpum var. pubescens

Amomum lepicarpum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces elongated, three-lobed fruits that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it its common name. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is cultivated for both its ornamental value and medicinal applications.
