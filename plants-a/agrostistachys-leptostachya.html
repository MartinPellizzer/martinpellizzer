Agrostistachys leptostachya

Agrostistachys leptostachya, commonly known as the slender-stalked bluemouth, is a perennial grass native to the grasslands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue-green leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces panicles of tiny, pale blue to white flowers, which bloom in late summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Agrostistachys leptostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
