Achyrophorus radicatus

Achyrophorus radicatus, commonly known as the redroot sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing, perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

The species is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh, drought-prone habitats.
