Andryala cheiranthifolia subvar. latifolia

Andryala cheiranthifolia subvar. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sandworts, is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant typically features small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
