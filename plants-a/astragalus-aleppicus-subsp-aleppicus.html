Astragalus aleppicus subsp. aleppicus

Astragalus aleppicus subsp.

aleppicus, commonly known as the Aleppo milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various health conditions.
