Alloplectus cucullatus var. substrigosus

Alloplectus cucullatus var. substrigosus, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodsy habitats and features basal rosettes of hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, often with a distinctive hooded appearance. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and foliage, which contribute to its common name.
