Apoleya

Apoleya, commonly known as the "silver leaf" plant, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage, which helps it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Apoleya thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal watering, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a desirable addition to drought-tolerant gardens.
