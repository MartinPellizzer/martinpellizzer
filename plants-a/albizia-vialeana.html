Albizia vialeana

Albizia vialeana, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a small to medium-sized leguminous tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate and have a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. Its pod fruits are slender and contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family.

Albizia vialeana is often found in dry forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
