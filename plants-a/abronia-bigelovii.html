Abronia bigelovii

Abronia bigelovii, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with narrow, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and providing nectar for pollinators.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptation to harsh, dry conditions.
