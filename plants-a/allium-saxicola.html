Allium saxicola

Allium saxicola, commonly known as the saxifrage garlic, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale purple flowers in late summer. The plant is adapted to grow in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arctic environments. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is known for its strong garlic-like odor.

Allium saxicola is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun and is often used in rock gardens due to its resilience and ornamental value.
