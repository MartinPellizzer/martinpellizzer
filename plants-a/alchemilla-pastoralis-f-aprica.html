Alchemilla pastoralis f. aprica

Alchemilla pastoralis f. aprica, commonly known as the spring alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are clustered in umbels.

Alchemilla pastoralis f. aprica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
