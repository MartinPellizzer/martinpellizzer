Artemisia lagopina

Artemisia lagopina, commonly known as wooly sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves and aromatic, pine-like scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Artemisia lagopina is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
