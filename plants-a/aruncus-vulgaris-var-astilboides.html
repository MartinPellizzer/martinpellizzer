Aruncus vulgaris var. astilboides

Aruncus vulgaris var. astilboides, commonly known as the white fern or Japanese fern, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features delicate, feathery foliage that turns a striking red or orange in the fall. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its elegant appearance.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for water gardens and woodland landscapes.
