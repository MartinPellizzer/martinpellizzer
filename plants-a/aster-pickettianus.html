Aster pickettianus

Aster pickettianus, commonly known as the Pickett's aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It has linear to lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a clustered inflorescence.

Aster pickettianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
