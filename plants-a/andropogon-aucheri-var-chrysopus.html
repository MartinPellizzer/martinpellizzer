Andropogon aucheri var. chrysopus

Andropogon aucheri var. chrysopus, commonly known as the golden beardgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves and produces dense clusters of golden-yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and a fibrous root system.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
