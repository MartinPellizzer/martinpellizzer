Anoplophytum setaceum

Anoplophytum setaceum, commonly known as the spiny cactus or desert cactus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the rainy season, adding a brief burst of color to the desert landscape. Its leaves are reduced to spines, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Anoplophytum setaceum is often used in traditional medicine and is popular as a decorative plant in dry climates.
