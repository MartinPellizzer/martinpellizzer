Acacia amblycarpa

Acacia amblycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

The bark and leaves of Acacia amblycarpa have been used by Indigenous Australians for various traditional purposes.
