Artemisia brittonii

Artemisia brittonii, commonly known as brittle sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its strong, camphor-like scent. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Artemisia brittonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
