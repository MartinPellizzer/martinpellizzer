Alternanthera villosa

Alternanthera villosa, commonly known as velvetleaf alternanthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
