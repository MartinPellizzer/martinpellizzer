Abutilon exonemum

Abutilon exonemum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its fibrous bark and hairy foliage contribute to its unique botanical appearance.
