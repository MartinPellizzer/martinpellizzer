Anil pedunculata

Anil pedunculata, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet petals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Anil pedunculata is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
