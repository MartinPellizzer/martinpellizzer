Artemisia lagopus subsp. abbreviata

Artemisia lagopus subsp.

abbreviata, commonly known as the shortleaf sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and gray-green foliage with a strong aromatic scent.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including drought and cold temperatures.
