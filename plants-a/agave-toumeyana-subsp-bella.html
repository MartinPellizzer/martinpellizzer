Agave toumeyana subsp. bella

Agave toumeyana subsp.

bella, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico. It is characterized by its rosette-forming structure with thick, bluish-gray leaves that have sharp, spiny edges.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, greenish-white flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and use in traditional practices, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
