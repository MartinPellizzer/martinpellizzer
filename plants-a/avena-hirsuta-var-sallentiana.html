Avena hirsuta var. sallentiana

Avena hirsuta var. sallentiana, commonly known as the Sallent hairy oat, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
