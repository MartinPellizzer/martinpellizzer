Astridia rubra var. alba

Astridia rubra var. alba, commonly known as the white starflower, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces oval, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its delicate appearance and unique flowering habit, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
