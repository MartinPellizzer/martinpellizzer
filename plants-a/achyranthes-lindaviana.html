Achyranthes lindaviana

Achyranthes lindaviana, commonly known as the Mexican knotweed, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in a distinctive reddish or purplish hue, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its roots are fibrous and can spread aggressively.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
