Astragalus durandianus

Astragalus durandianus, commonly known as Durand's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
