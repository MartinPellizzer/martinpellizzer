Abies religiosa subsp. glaucescens

Abies religiosa subsp.

glaucescens, commonly known as the blue fir, is a subspecies of the fir tree native to Mexico. It is characterized by its conical shape and dense, dark green foliage that often has a bluish cast.

The needles are short, stiff, and have a waxy coating, which contributes to its distinctive appearance. It produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of coniferous plants.

This subspecies thrives in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates.
