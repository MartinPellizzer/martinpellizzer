Asparagus acerosus

Asparagus acerosus, commonly known as the sharp asparagus or rock asparagus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are compound, with numerous small leaflets arranged along the stem.

Asparagus acerosus thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
