Allium dalmaticum var. laxiflorum

Allium dalmaticum var. laxiflorum, commonly known as the Dalmatian garlic.

This plant is a perennial bulbous species native to the Balkans, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It produces edible bulbs that are similar in taste to garlic but have a milder flavor. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
