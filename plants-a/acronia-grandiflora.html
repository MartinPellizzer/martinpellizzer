Acronia grandiflora

Acronia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered acronia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Acronia grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
