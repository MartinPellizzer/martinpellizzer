Albuca papyracea

Albuca papyracea, commonly known as the paperleaf albuca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy, papery sheath. The plant produces tall, branched stems with clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Albuca papyracea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
