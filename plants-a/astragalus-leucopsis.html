Astragalus leucopsis

Astragalus leucopsis, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, desert-like environments.

Astragalus leucopsis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
