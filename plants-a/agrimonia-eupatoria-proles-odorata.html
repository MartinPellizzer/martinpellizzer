Agrimonia eupatoria proles odorata

Agrimonia eupatoria proles odorata, commonly known as sweet agrimony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are pinnately compound with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves and flowers have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their astringent and antiseptic properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
