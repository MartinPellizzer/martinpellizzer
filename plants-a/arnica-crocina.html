Arnica crocina

Arnica crocina, commonly known as the Italian mountain arnica, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky and meadow habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Arnica crocina is valued for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds and should be used with caution.
