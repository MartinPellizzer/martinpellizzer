Acalypha filiformis subsp. rubra

Acalypha filiformis subsp.

rubra, commonly known as red threadplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are often reddish in color, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
