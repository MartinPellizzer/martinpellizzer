Astragalus batangensis

Astragalus batangensis, commonly known as the Batang milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus batangensis is often found in mountainous areas and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
