Acacia islana

Acacia islana, commonly known as the Socotra acacia, is a unique shrub or small tree native to the Socotra archipelago.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, thorn-like leaves and its ability to thrive in arid, rocky environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia islana is well adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Its ecological significance is notable in the region's biodiversity and its role in supporting local wildlife.
