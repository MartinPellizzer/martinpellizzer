Arthrostylidium auriculatum

Arthrostylidium auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate sand mat, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to Australia.

It features small, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and summer months. It is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or inland regions with low rainfall.

Its unique morphology and ability to thrive in harsh conditions make it an important species in its native ecosystem.
