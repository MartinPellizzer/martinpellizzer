Allocarya vestita

Allocarya vestita, commonly known as the velvet leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, velvety-textured leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
