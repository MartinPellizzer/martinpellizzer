Allium paniculatum var. intermedium

Allium paniculatum var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate grass leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features long, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Botanically, it is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and its ability to form dense colonies.
