Astragalus miser var. tenuifolius

Astragalus miser var. tenuifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its root system is fibrous and can form extensive colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and as a forage plant for wildlife.
