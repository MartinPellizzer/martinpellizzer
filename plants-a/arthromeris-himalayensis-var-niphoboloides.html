Arthromeris himalayensis var. niphoboloides

Arthromeris himalayensis var. niphoboloides, commonly known as the Himalayan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often appearing in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapting to the harsh mountain climate.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
