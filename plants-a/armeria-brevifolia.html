Armeria brevifolia

Armeria brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features dense clusters of bright white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hair. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Armeria brevifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
