Amaranthus macrocarpus var. macrocarpus

Amaranthus macrocarpus var. macrocarpus, commonly known as the Chinese amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces dense clusters of small, vibrant red or orange flowers. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental or for its edible leaves.

The seeds of this species are also used in some traditional cuisines.
