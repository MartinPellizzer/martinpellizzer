Acidosasa macula

Acidosasa macula, commonly known as the spotted acid bush, is a shrub native to the subtropical regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are often spotted with a distinctive red or purple coloration. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
