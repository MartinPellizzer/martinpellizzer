Artemisia campestris subsp. alpina

Artemisia campestris subsp.

alpina, commonly known as alpine wormwood, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small yellow flowers that appear in summer. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
