Ageratella microphylla var. microphylla

Ageratella microphylla var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features small, silver-gray leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
