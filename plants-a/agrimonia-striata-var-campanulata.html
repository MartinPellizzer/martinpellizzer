Agrimonia striata var. campanulata

Agrimonia striata var. campanulata, commonly known as the bell agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are often hairy, and the plant is known for its aromatic foliage.

Agrimonia striata var. campanulata is typically found in dry grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
