Anisantha flabellata

Anisantha flabellata, commonly known as the flabellate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

Anisantha flabellata is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
