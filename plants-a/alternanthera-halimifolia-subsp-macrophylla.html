Alternanthera halimifolia subsp. macrophylla

Alternanthera halimifolia subsp.

macrophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf sandparsley, is a herbaceous plant native to South America. It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge, and it produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in clusters.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy environments. It has a fibrous root system and is characterized by its ability to tolerate periodic flooding.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger, more robust leaves compared to other subspecies of Alternanthera halimifolia.
