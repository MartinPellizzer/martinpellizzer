Abies canadensis var. milfordensis

Abies canadensis var. milfordensis, commonly known as the Milford fir, is a rare variant of the balsam fir native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are shorter and more densely packed than those of the typical balsam fir. The cones are typically small, woody, and have a distinctive reddish-brown color when mature. This variety thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
