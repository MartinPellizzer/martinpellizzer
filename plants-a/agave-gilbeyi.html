Agave gilbeyi

Agave gilbeyi, commonly known as the Gilbeys agave, is a species native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, often with a bluish-green coloration. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears clusters of white flowers, which are typically followed by the development of a fruit. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes in suitable climates.
