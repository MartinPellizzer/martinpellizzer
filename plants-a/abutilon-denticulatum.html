Abutilon denticulatum

Abutilon denticulatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Abutilon denticulatum is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a common weed and a plant of ecological interest.
