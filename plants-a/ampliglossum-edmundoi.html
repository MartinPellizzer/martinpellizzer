Ampliglossum edmundoi

Ampliglossum edmundoi, commonly known as the Edmund's giant rosette, is a striking perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, rosette-forming leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter, adapted to store water in arid conditions. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its unique morphology and habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and conservation programs.
