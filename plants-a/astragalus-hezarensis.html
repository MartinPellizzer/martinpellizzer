Astragalus hezarensis

Astragalus hezarensis, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus hezarensis is of interest in botanical studies due to its potential medicinal properties and ecological significance.
