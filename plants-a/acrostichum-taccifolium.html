Acrostichum taccifolium

Acrostichum taccifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed fern, is a tropical plant native to the wetlands and marshes of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant thrives in moist, humid environments and is often found near water bodies. Its fronds are typically divided into numerous segments, giving it a feathery appearance.

Acrostichum taccifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
