Adenia subsessilifolia

Adenia subsessilifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved adenia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in dry or semi-arid environments. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive, fleshy corolla that attracts pollinators like bees and flies.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
