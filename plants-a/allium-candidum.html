Allium candidum

Allium candidum, commonly known as garlic chives, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow leaves and small white or purple flowers. The plant produces bulbs that are often used in culinary applications for their mild garlic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Allium candidum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
