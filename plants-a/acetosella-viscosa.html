Acetosella viscosa

Acetosella viscosa, commonly known as sticky sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, sticky hairs, which give the plant its common name. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow and appear in clusters during the spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its succulent stems and edible, sour-tasting leaves.
