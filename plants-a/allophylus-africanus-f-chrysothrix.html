Allophylus africanus f. chrysothrix

Allophylus africanus f. chrysothrix, commonly known as the golden-fruited alderleaf, is a variant of the African alderleaf plant.

It is native to parts of southern Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns bright orange or red when mature.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its vibrant seasonal color.
