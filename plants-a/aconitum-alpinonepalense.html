Aconitum alpinonepalense

Aconitum alpinonepalense, commonly known as the Himalayan aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves and produces follicular fruits containing seeds with silky appendages. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a dangerous plant and should be handled with care.
