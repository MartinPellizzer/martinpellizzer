Acalypha brasiliensis f. cordata

Acalypha brasiliensis f. cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf acalypha, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by three-lobed, red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This ornamental plant is valued for its attractive foliage and is often used in tropical gardens.
