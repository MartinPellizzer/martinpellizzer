Acacia tortilis var. raddiana

Acacia tortilis var. raddiana, commonly known as the umbrella thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the African savannas.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped crown formed by its thick, spreading branches. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that serve as the primary photosynthetic organs. It produces yellow flowers in winter, followed by pods that contain seeds.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and plays an important role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
