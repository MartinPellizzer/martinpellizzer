Acacia sericocephala

Acacia sericocephala, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The seeds are encased in hard pods and are dispersed by wind or animals.
