Adelia dodecandra

Adelia dodecandra, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in shrubland and grassland habitats, often in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branching.

Adelia dodecandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
