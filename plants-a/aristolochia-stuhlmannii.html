Aristolochia stuhlmannii

Aristolochia stuhlmannii, commonly known as the Stuhlmann's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is known for its strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is often studied in botanical research and conservation efforts.
