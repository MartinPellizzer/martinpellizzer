Astragalus lunatus

Astragalus lunatus, commonly known as the moonflower milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus lunatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
