Acalypha benguelensis var. trichogyne

Acalypha benguelensis var. trichogyne, commonly known as the velvetleaf or trichogyne acalypha, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its unique foliage.
