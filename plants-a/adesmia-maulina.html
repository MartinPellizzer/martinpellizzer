Adesmia maulina

Adesmia maulina, commonly known as the woolly burr.

It is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae, native to South America. The plant is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and it often grows in dry, rocky habitats.

Adesmia maulina is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in soil stabilization and as a food source for local wildlife.
