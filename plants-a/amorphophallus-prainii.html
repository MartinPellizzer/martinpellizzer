Amorphophallus prainii

Amorphophallus prainii, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces a unique, often foul-smelling flower that attracts pollinators such as flies and beetles. Its rhizome is tuberous and can store nutrients, allowing it to survive in seasonal climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
