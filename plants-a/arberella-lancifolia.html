Arberella lancifolia

Arberella lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arberella, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its botanical features include a woody stem and opposite leaf arrangement.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
