Aristida macrochaeta

Aristida macrochaeta, commonly known as the hairy dropseed, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed sites.

Aristida macrochaeta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
