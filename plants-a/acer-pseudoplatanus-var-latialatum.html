Acer pseudoplatanus var. latialatum

Acer pseudoplatanus var. latialatum, commonly known as the Latialatum maple, is a variant of the common maple, characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, where it thrives in temperate climates. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a serrated margin and a vibrant green color during the growing season. In autumn, the foliage turns shades of red, orange, and yellow, making it a striking ornamental plant.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various soil types.
