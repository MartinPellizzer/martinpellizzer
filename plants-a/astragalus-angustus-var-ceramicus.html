Astragalus angustus var. ceramicus

Astragalus angustus var. ceramicus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open grasslands.

Its leaves are narrow and trifoliate, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
