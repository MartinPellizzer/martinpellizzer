Abies spectabilis subsp. densa

Abies spectabilis subsp.

densa, commonly known as the dense fir, is a subspecies of the white fir native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly curved.

The cones of this plant are usually reddish-brown and maturing within 12 to 18 months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-elevation forests.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in mountain ecosystems.
