Acacia laricina var. laricina

Acacia laricina var. laricina, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flowerheads that appear in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

The acacia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
