Apicra cymbifolia

Apicra cymbifolia, commonly known as the flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers, which resemble flames, giving it its common name. The plant features elongated, strap-like leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. Apicra cymbifolia thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
