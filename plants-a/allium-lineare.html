Allium lineare

Allium lineare, commonly known as the linear leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color and can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in a loose umbel, which bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor similar to leeks or onions.

Allium lineare is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
