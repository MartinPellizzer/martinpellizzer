Aster patens f. roseus

Aster patens f. roseus, commonly known as the rose-flowered blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It blooms from late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The cultivar 'roseus' is distinguished by its more showy, rose-colored flowers compared to the typical blue or purple hues of the species.
