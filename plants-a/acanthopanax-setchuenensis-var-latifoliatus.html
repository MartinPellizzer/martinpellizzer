Acanthopanax setchuenensis var. latifoliatus

Acanthopanax setchuenensis var. latifoliatus, commonly known as the broadleaf Chinese angel's trumpet, is a climbing shrub native to China.

It features compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red berries that are edible for humans but toxic to livestock. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
