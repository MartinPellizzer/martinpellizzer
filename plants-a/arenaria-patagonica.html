Arenaria patagonica

Arenaria patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of Argentina and Chile.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in cold, arid environments, often found in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a delicate touch to its rocky habitat. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh conditions.

Arenaria patagonica plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
