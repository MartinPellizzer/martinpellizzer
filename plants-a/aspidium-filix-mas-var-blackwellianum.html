Aspidium filix-mas var. blackwellianum

Aspidium filix-mas var. blackwellianum, commonly known as the Blackwell's water fern, is a variant of the common water fern, characterized by its distinctive fronds and aquatic adaptations.

It is a perennial fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands and along stream edges. The plant's fronds are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance that enhances its ornamental value. It reproduces through spores, which are released from sporangia located on the underside of its leaves.

This variety is valued for its ability to purify water and its role in maintaining aquatic ecosystems.
