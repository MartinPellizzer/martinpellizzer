Argocoffeopsis rupestris subsp. rupestris

Argocoffeopsis rupestris subsp.

rupestris, commonly known as the rock coffee tree, is a species native to tropical regions of South America. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers.

The plant produces small, red berries that contain the seeds, which are used for propagation. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
