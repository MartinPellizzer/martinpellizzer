Aloe pluridens

Aloe pluridens, commonly known as the many-toothed aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water in arid environments. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are covered in a layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss through transpiration.

Aloe pluridens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
