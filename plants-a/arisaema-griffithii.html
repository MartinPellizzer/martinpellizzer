Arisaema griffithii

Arisaema griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's arisaema, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces a single, showy spathe that is typically green or yellowish in color, with a spadix that bears small, inconspicuous flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, shaded habitats.

Arisaema griffithii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions for medicinal purposes.
