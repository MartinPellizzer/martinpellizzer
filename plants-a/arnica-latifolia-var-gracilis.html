Arnica latifolia var. gracilis

Arnica latifolia var. gracilis, commonly known as narrow-leaved arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive central disc. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its flowers bloom from June to August, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
