Ajax pseudonarcissus var. ryticarpus

Ajax pseudonarcissus var. ryticarpus, commonly known as the Persian tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive red or purple blotch at the base. The plant produces strap-like, grassy leaves and has a short, underground stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early spring blooming habit.
