Acacia alemquerensis

Acacia alemquerensis, commonly known as the Alemquer acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, bipinnate leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in globular heads, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant's ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various desert animals.
