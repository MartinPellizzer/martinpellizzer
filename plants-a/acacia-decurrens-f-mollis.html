Acacia decurrens f. mollis

Acacia decurrens f. mollis, commonly known as the soft-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its soft, silky foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
