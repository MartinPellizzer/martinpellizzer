Axonopus amplifolius

Axonopus amplifolius, commonly known as broadleaf axonopus, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing foliage with broad, flat leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lawns, gardens, and disturbed areas.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is both a desirable ornamental grass and a potential weed in some environments.
