Azalea indica var. purpurea

Azalea indica var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple azalea, is a flowering shrub native to southern China and northern Vietnam.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of trumpet-shaped flowers that range from deep pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing fragrant blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
