Arctium horrescens

Arctium horrescens, commonly known as the lesser burdock, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple to pink flower heads that develop into spiny, bur-like fruits. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Arctium horrescens is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
