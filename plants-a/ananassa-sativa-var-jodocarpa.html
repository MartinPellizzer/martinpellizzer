Ananassa sativa var. jodocarpa

Ananassa sativa var. jodocarpa, commonly known as the Jodocarpa pineapple, is a cultivar of the pineapple plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny leaves and a compact, conical fruit with a distinctive reddish-brown coloration. The plant is adapted to warm, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It produces a sweet, aromatic fruit with a high water content and a unique flavor profile.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its edible fruit.
