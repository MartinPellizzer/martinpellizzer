Azukia riukiuensis

Azukia riukiuensis, commonly known as the Ryukyu azuki, is a flowering plant native to Japan's Ryukyu Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, round legumes that are typically reddish in color, which are used in traditional culinary practices. Azukia riukiuensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and cultural studies in the region.
