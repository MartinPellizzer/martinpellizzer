Amelanchier florida var. cusickii

Amelanchier florida var. cusickii, commonly known as the Cusick's serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, typically turning from red to dark purple. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
