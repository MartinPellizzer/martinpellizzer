Acacia flavipila var. ovalis

Acacia flavipila var. ovalis, commonly known as the oval-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features slender, often multi-branched stems and produces small, yellow, globular flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets that give the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide nectar for native pollinators.
