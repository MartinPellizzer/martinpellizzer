Aidia brisipensis

Aidia brisipensis, commonly known as the Cape York daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and has narrow, linear leaves. It produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in a radiate head, often surrounded by pale green ray florets.

Aidia brisipensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
