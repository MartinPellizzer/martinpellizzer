Arum italicum f. viridipetiolatum

Arum italicum f. viridipetiolatum, commonly known as the Green-veined Arum, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent green veins.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which includes many plants known for their unique inflorescences. The plant features a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the genus Arum, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is native to southern Europe and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and dramatic flowering display.

This variety is notable for its vigorous growth and ability to add a dramatic touch to shady garden spaces.
