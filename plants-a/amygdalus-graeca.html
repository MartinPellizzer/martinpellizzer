Amygdalus graeca

Amygdalus graeca, commonly known as the Greek peach, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by fuzzy, yellow fruits that ripen in late summer. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet, aromatic flavor, and it is valued for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Amygdalus graeca is also known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
