Arisaema serratum f. serrulatum

Arisaema serratum f. serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated arisaema, is a variant of the arisaema genus native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spathaceous inflorescence that resembles a leaf, with a spathe that is often green or yellowish in color. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers enclosed within the spathe, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The foliage consists of heart-shaped, serrated leaves that contribute to its ornamental value in gardens.
