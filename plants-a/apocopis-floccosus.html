Apocopis floccosus

Apocopis floccosus, commonly known as the fluffcap mushroom, is a species of fungus characterized by its distinctive, fluffy cap that resembles a cloud.

It belongs to the family Hygrocybaceae and is typically found in temperate forests, often growing in association with decaying wood. The cap is usually pale yellow to cream in color, with a soft, velvety texture that becomes more pronounced as the fungus matures. This species is notable for its unique appearance and is often sought after by mushroom enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal.
