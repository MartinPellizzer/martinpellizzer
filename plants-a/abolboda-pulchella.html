Abolboda pulchella

Abolboda pulchella, commonly known as the pretty bellflower, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Abolboda pulchella is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
