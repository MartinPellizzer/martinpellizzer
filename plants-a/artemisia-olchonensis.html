Artemisia olchonensis

Artemisia olchonensis, commonly known as the Mongolian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold climates. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other aromatic herbs and flowering plants.

Artemisia olchonensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
