Astragalus robbinsii subsp. harringtonii

Astragalus robbinsii subsp.

harringtonii, commonly known as Harrington's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral structure and habitat preferences, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native range.

This plant is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
