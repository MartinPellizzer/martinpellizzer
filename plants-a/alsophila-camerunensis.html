Alsophila camerunensis

Alsophila camerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical rainforests of Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Dicksoniaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, contributing to the dense canopy of the forest understory.

Alsophila camerunensis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
