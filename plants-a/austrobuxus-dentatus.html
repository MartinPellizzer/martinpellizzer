Austrobuxus dentatus

Austrobuxus dentatus, commonly known as the serrated boxwood, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated margins. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Austrobuxus dentatus is also valued for its ability to tolerate salt spray, making it suitable for coastal gardens.
