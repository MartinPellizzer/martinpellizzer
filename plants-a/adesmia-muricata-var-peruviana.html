Adesmia muricata var. peruviana

Adesmia muricata var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian thornapple, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume, containing several seeds, and it is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
