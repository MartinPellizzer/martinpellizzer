Amylocarpus inermis

Amylocarpus inermis, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Amylocarpus inermis is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its rhizomatous root system is notable for its ability to store starch, which is used in traditional culinary practices.
