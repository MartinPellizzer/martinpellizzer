Astragalus radkanensis

Astragalus radkanensis, commonly known as the Radkan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
