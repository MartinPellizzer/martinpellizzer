Adenophora nikoensis var. teramotoi

Adenophora nikoensis var. teramotoi, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
