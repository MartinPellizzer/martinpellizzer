Acacia subternata

Acacia subternata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, feathery leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Acacia subternata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in dry climates.

Its flowers are an important nectar source for various pollinators, including bees and birds.
