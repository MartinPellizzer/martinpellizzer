Aconitum koisikawense

Aconitum koisikawense, commonly known as the Koisikawa aconite, is a species of flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces follicles that contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to handle. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.
