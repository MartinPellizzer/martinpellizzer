Astragalus mikrophytoides

Astragalus mikrophytoides, commonly known as the dwarf milkvetch, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Astragalus mikrophytoides is also valued for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its habitat.
