Aquilegia pyrenaica f. thalictrifolia

Aquilegia pyrenaica f. thalictrifolia, commonly known as the Pyrenean columbine, is a rare and distinctive plant with unique botanical features.

It is a hybrid form between Aquilegia pyrenaica and Aquilegia thalictrifolia, exhibiting a blend of characteristics from both parents. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically arranged in a cluster. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive appearance that aids in identification.

It is native to the Pyrenees region and thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or alpine habitats.
