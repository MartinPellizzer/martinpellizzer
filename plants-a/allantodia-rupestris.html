Allantodia rupestris

Allantodia rupestris, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and is often found growing on rocky outcrops. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Allantodia rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
