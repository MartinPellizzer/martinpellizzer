Aegiphila subopposita

Aegiphila subopposita, commonly known as the silverleaf shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba and the Bahamas.

It is a small, shrub-like perennial that typically grows in coastal and limestone habitats. The plant features narrow, silvery-green leaves with a distinctive opposite arrangement, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are small, clustered, and often pale yellow or white, blooming in the summer months.

Aegiphila subopposita is adapted to salty air and well-drained soils, making it a resilient species in its native environment.
