Allium akirense

Allium akirense, commonly known as the Akiren onion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous structure and erect, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Allium akirense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
