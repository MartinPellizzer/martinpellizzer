Astragalus testiculatus

Astragalus testiculatus, commonly known as the testicle pea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
