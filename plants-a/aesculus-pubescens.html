Aesculus pubescens

Aesculus pubescens, commonly known as the downy buckeye, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The tree produces showy, yellowish-green flowers in upright clusters during late spring. Its fruit is a spiny, spherical capsule that contains one to three seeds.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
