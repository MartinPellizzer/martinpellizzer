Avena sativa var. tristis

Avena sativa var. tristis, commonly known as the slender oat, is a variety of the oat species characterized by its shorter stature and narrower leaves compared to typical oats.

It is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in cooler climates. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with florets that mature into awned grains. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a hollow, cylindrical stem.

This variety is often cultivated for forage or as a cover crop due to its hardiness and adaptability.
