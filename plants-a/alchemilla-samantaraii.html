Alchemilla samantaraii

Alchemilla samantaraii, commonly known as the Samantara lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value, particularly in shady or moist garden settings.

Alchemilla samantaraii is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient choice for landscaping.
