Amarenus

Amarenus, commonly known as the redroot pigweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive red stems, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a slightly serrated edge. Amarenus is native to North America and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

It is an annual plant that thrives in full sun and is known for its ability to spread rapidly.
