Anthyllis tejedensis subsp. mesatlantica

Anthyllis tejedensis subsp.

mesatlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to coastal and Mediterranean climates. It has compound leaves and produces pods that contain seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
