Ampelocalamus yongshanensis

Ampelocalamus yongshanensis, commonly known as the Yongshan bamboo, is a unique species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to China, specifically found in the Yunnan Province, where it thrives in mountainous regions with high altitudes and cool climates. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, grass-like leaves, which give it a distinct appearance. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for small fauna.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
