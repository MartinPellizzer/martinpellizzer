Atriplex lanfrancoi

Atriplex lanfrancoi, commonly known as the Lanfranco's saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, often hairy leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its salt-tolerant nature allows it to thrive in soils with high salinity, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.

Atriplex lanfrancoi plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
