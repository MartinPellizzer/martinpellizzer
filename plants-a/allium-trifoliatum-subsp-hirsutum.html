Allium trifoliatum subsp. hirsutum

Allium trifoliatum subsp.

hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are slightly hairy, giving it its subspecies name.

The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a useful plant in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and forest edges.
