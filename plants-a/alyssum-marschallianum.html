Alyssum marschallianum

Alyssum marschallianum, commonly known as marshmallow alyssum, is a low-growing flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a mat-forming habit, with hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum marschallianum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
