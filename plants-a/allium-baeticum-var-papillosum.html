Allium baeticum var. papillosum

Allium baeticum var. papillosum, commonly known as the globe flower or papillate garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features clustered, rounded bulbs with a white, fleshy interior and a strong garlic scent. The plant produces umbels of white or pale pink flowers, which are often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are broad, flat, and grass-like, growing from the base of the plant.

This variety is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its culinary uses in traditional Mediterranean cuisine.
