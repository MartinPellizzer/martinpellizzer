Astragalus sanctus var. macrocarpus

Astragalus sanctus var. macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-seeded milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a cylindrical pod containing several large, smooth seeds.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
