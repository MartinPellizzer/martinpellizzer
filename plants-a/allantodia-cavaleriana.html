Allantodia cavaleriana

Allantodia cavaleriana, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
