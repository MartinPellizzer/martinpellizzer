Asterochlaena sidifolia subsp. genuina

Asterochlaena sidifolia subsp.

genuina, commonly known as the slender leaf rockrose, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features a compact, shrub-like growth habit with small, fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, adding a delicate floral display. It is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, thriving in full sun and requiring minimal water.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it a desirable species for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
