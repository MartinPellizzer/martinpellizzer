Amomum lutescens

Amomum lutescens, commonly known as the yellow cardamom, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oval, yellowish fruits containing aromatic seeds. These seeds are widely used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.

Amomum lutescens thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its economic and therapeutic value.
