Avena rupestris

Avena rupestris, commonly known as cliff grass or rock grass, is a perennial grass species native to the rocky and arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Avena rupestris is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and can thrive in harsh, sun-exposed environments.

Its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion makes it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
