Abies sibirica f. nana

Abies sibirica f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf Siberian fir, is a compact, low-growing variant of the Siberian fir.

It features dense, conical foliage that is dark green on top and silvery white underneath. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It is well adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

This ornamental evergreen is often used in rock gardens and as a landscape accent due to its hardiness and attractive appearance.

Its small stature makes it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
