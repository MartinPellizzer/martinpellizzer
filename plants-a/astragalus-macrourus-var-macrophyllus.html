Astragalus macrourus var. macrophyllus

Astragalus macrourus var. macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, often hairy leaflets. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its fibrous root system makes it a valuable plant for soil stabilization and ecological restoration.
