Angraecum perhumile

Angraecum perhumile, commonly known as the slender star orchid, is a rare and delicate epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, pendant inflorescence bearing small, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive hooded lip.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
