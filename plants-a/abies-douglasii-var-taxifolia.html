Abies douglasii var. taxifolia

Abies douglasii var. taxifolia, commonly known as the Douglas fir var. taxifolia, is a variant of the Douglas fir native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its distinctive, flattened, needle-like leaves. The leaves are typically bluish-green and have a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique foliage and structural form.
