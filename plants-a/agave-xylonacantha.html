Agave xylonacantha

Agave xylonacantha, commonly known as the giant agave, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like growth habit, reaching heights of up to 10 feet or more. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with sharp, spiny margins. Its flowers, which appear at the top of the plant, are yellow and tubular, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Agave xylonacantha is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its succulent leaves and stems.
