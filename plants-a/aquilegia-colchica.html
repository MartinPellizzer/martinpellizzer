Aquilegia colchica

Aquilegia colchica, commonly known as the Caucasian lady's mantle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive palmate shape, and it typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
