Agropyron dolicholepis

Agropyron dolicholepis, commonly known as the long-leaved wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often blue-green colored blades. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Agropyron dolicholepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
