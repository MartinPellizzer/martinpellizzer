Arum elongatum f. virescens

Arum elongatum f. virescens, commonly known as the green-veined arum.

This plant is a variant of the elongated arum, characterized by its distinctive foliage with prominent green veins. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy texture.

It produces striking, spatulate-shaped inflorescences that are often green or reddish in color, making it a popular ornamental plant.
