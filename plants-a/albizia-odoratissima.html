Albizia odoratissima

Albizia odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of nectar for pollinators.

Albizia odoratissima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in tropical and subtropical regions.
