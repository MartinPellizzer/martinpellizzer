Alyssum scardicum

Alyssum scardicum, commonly known as sticky alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming from spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that produce a sticky secretion, which helps deter herbivores. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum scardicum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
