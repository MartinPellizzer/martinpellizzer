Alstroemeria ligtu f. flavens

Alstroemeria ligtu f. flavens, commonly known as the golden alstroemeria, is a hybrid cultivar prized for its vibrant, showy flowers.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is native to South America, specifically Chile and Argentina. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves and produces clusters of trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It is typically grown as a perennial in mild climates and is popular in gardens for its ornamental value.

Alstroemeria ligtu f. flavens requires well-drained soil and partial to full sun to thrive.
