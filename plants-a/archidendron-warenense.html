Archidendron warenense

Archidendron warenense, commonly known as the yellow tamarind tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The tree produces long, flat pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisine. Its bark and leaves are also valued for their medicinal properties.

Archidendron warenense thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and riverine areas.
