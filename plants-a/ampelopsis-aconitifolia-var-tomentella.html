Ampelopsis aconitifolia var. tomentella

Ampelopsis aconitifolia var. tomentella, commonly known as the woolly grape ivy, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, woolly pubescence, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into blue-black berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its unique foliage and ornamental berries make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
