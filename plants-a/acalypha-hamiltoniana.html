Acalypha hamiltoniana

Acalypha hamiltoniana, commonly known as the red veined nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves with prominent red veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or forest understory environments.

Acalypha hamiltoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
