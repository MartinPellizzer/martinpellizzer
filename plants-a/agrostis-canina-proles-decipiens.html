Agrostis canina proles decipiens

Agrostis canina proles decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive dog's grass, is a grass species characterized by its fine, tufted blades and dense growth habit.

It typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and colonize new areas effectively. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often mistaken for other similar grasses, contributing to its deceptive name.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability and ecological significance in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
