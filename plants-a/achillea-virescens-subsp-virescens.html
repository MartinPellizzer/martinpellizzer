Achillea virescens subsp. virescens

Achillea virescens subsp.

virescens, commonly known as green yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and basal rosettes of finely divided, dark green leaves.

The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and long flowering period make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
