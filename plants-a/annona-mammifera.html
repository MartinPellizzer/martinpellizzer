Annona mammifera

Annona mammifera, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a fragrant, spiny fruit with a thick, yellowish skin. The fruit contains a creamy, sweet pulp that is rich in nutrients and has a unique, tropical flavor. It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
