Ananas maxima

Ananas maxima, commonly known as the giant pineapple, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of sword-shaped leaves that form a central cup. The plant produces a large, spiky fruit that can weigh up to 30 kilograms. Its leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle that helps retain moisture in arid environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
