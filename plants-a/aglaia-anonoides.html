Aglaia anonoides

Aglaia anonoides, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by round, greenish fruits. The fruit's outer layer, or pericarp, is typically thick and leathery, while the inner part contains aromatic, edible arils.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of essential oils.
