Amomum subsericeum

Amomum subsericeum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, elongated leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its essential oils and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
