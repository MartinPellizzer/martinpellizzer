Aethionema gypsicola

Aethionema gypsicola, commonly known as the saltbush or gypsum plant, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its rocky habitat. Aethionema gypsicola is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.
