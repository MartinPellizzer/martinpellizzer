Alopecurus geniculatus var. bulbosus

Alopecurus geniculatus var. bulbosus, commonly known as the bulbous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, three-ranked leaves that are typically glabrous. The plant bears spikes of tiny, pale green to brown flowers, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in meadows, wetlands, and along streambanks.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
