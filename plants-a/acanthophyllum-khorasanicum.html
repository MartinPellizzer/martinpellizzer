Acanthophyllum khorasanicum

Acanthophyllum khorasanicum, commonly known as the Khorasan spiny shrub, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, branching structure and dense covering of spines. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and reduced leaf surfaces to minimize water loss.

Acanthophyllum khorasanicum plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
