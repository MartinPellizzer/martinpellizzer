Actinodaphne nitida

Actinodaphne nitida, commonly known as the slender laurel, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy on top and dull underneath. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary clusters, and it produces small, round berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.
