Alchemilla undecimloba

Alchemilla undecimloba, commonly known as the eleven-lobed lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, rounded leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in summer, arranged in loose panicles. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Alchemilla undecimloba is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and its ability to attract beneficial insects.
