Amaranthus rudis

Amaranthus rudis, commonly known as rough amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and branched stems that are often covered with fine hairs. The leaves are lanceolate, with a rough texture and a bluish-green hue, while the flowers are small, clustered in panicles. It produces numerous seeds that are small and typically red or brown in color.

This plant is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is also used in traditional medicine.
