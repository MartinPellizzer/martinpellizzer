Acroglochin multiflora

Acroglochin multiflora, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
