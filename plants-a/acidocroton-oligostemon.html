Acidocroton oligostemon

Acidocroton oligostemon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
