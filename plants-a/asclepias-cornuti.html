Asclepias cornuti

Asclepias cornuti, commonly known as the cornutus milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers with distinctive, horn-like structures at the base of each bloom. It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is known for its role as a host plant for the monarch butterfly.

Asclepias cornuti thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is typically found in open woods, fields, and along roadsides.
