Abarema curvicarpa var. rodriguesii

Abarema curvicarpa var. rodriguesii, commonly known as the Rodrigues's narrow-leafed acacia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to the island's unique climate.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing forage for herbivores and contributing to soil nitrogen through its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
