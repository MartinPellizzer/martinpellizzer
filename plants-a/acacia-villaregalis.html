Acacia villaregalis

Acacia villaregalis, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces long, flat pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland areas.

Acacia villaregalis is a symbol of Australia and plays an important role in local ecosystems.
