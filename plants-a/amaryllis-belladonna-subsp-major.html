Amaryllis belladonna subsp. major

Amaryllis belladonna subsp.

major, commonly known as the giant belladonna lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa. It produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink to red in color, with a strong, often unpleasant fragrance.

The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a bulbous base and can grow up to several feet in height. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

This subspecies is known for its dramatic blooms and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
