Abarema scutifera

Abarema scutifera, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble peacock feathers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural displays.
