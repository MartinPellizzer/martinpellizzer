Aleuritia firmipes

Aleuritia firmipes, commonly known as the red-flowered alder, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of bright red flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Aleuritia firmipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
