Anemone patens

Anemone patens, commonly known as the "buttercup" or "windflower," is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. The plant has a short, stout stem with palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its root system consists of a rhizome that helps it survive harsh winter conditions.

Anemone patens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
