Amygdalus furcata

Amygdalus furcata, commonly known as the forked almond.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its branched stems and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that contain bitter seeds, similar to other members of the Prunus genus. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a leathery texture.

Amygdalus furcata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive spring blooms and hardy nature.
