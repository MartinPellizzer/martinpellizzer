Acanthophyllum bracteatum var. gracile

Acanthophyllum bracteatum var. gracile, commonly known as the slender leafed acanthophyllum, is a succulent shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its root system is extensive and adapted to anchor the plant in loose, sandy soils.

This species is well-suited for xeriscaping and is often used in restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
