Aloe perfoliata var. natalensis

Aloe perfoliata var. natalensis, commonly known as Natal Aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a white powdery coating, known as farina. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.
