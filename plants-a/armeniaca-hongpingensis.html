Armeniaca hongpingensis

Armeniaca hongpingensis, commonly known as the Hongping peach, is a species native to China, specifically the Hubei province.

It is a deciduous tree belonging to the Prunus genus, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a fuzzy peach with a sweet, juicy flesh and a hard pit, similar to other peach varieties.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and culinary uses, though it is less commonly cultivated than other peach species.
