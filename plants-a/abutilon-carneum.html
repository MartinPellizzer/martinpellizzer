Abutilon carneum

Abutilon carneum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage.

Abutilon carneum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
