Abies balsamea var. balsamea

Abies balsamea var. balsamea, commonly known as the balsam fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green needles, and distinctive fluted bark. The tree produces small, spherical cones that release seeds in the autumn. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in boreal and mixed forests.

The balsam fir is also valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used historically for medicinal and aromatic purposes.
