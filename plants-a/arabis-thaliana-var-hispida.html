Arabis thaliana var. hispida

Arabis thaliana var. hispida, commonly known as the hispid rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, glandular hairs, giving it a rough texture.

The flowers, which bloom in early spring, are usually white or pale yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
