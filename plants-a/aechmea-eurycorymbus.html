Aechmea eurycorymbus

Aechmea eurycorymbus, commonly known as the broad-capped pineapple plant, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with showy, colorful flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Aechmea eurycorymbus is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and floral display.

Morphology

Aechmea eurycorymbus has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, sword-like, and covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, with a dark green to blue-gray coloration.

The leaves are arranged in a tight spiral at the base, forming a central rosette that holds water. The plant has a short, thick, and somewhat woody stem with a smooth, grayish bark that grows upward from the base. Its inflorescence is a compact, branched cluster of bright red flowers that bloom during the summer months, and it is pollinated by birds, particularly hummingbirds.
