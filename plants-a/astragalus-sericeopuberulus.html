Astragalus sericeopuberulus

Astragalus sericeopuberulus, commonly known as the silky-hairy milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has finely hairy leaves that are often covered in a soft, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Astragalus sericeopuberulus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
