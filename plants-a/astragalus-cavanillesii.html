Astragalus cavanillesii

Astragalus cavanillesii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. It has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Astragalus cavanillesii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
