Acalypha pilosa var. androgyna

Acalypha pilosa var. androgyna, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, three-lobed fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
