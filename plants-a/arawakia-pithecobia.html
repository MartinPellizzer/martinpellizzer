Arawakia pithecobia

Arawakia pithecobia, commonly known as the sacred forest vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its long, twisting stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white and yellow. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain seeds enclosed in a protective coating.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, Arawakia pithecobia is considered a valuable species in both traditional medicine and conservation efforts.
