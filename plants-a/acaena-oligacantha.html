Acaena oligacantha

Acaena oligacantha, commonly known as the New Zealand spinach or the bristly burr, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its prickly, woody stems and small, serrated leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by spiky, burr-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas and forest margins.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in regions outside its native habitat.
