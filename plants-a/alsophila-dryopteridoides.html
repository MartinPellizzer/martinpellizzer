Alsophila dryopteridoides

Alsophila dryopteridoides, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived species. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.
