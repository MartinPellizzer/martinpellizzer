Arachnorchis lindleyana

Arachnorchis lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley's spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance due to its elongated, thread-like petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in coastal or inland regions with moderate rainfall. Its flowering period occurs during the cooler months, producing small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green.

Arachnorchis lindleyana is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on specific insect species for reproduction.
