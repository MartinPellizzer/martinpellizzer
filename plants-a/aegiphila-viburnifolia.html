Aegiphila viburnifolia

Aegiphila viburnifolia, commonly known as the Florida beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces showy, dark purple berries in the fall, which are a key feature for identification. Aegiphila viburnifolia typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

It is often found in areas with partial shade and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
