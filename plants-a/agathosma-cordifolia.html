Agathosma cordifolia

Agathosma cordifolia, commonly known as Cape geranium, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, aromatic leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often found in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Agathosma cordifolia is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and attractive appearance.
