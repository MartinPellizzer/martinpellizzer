Andropogon hirtifolius

Andropogon hirtifolius, commonly known as hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with blue or purple.

Andropogon hirtifolius is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
