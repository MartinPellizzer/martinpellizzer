Adenia firingalavensis var. stylosa

Adenia firingalavensis var. stylosa, commonly known as the slender adenia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and open forests. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small and yellow-green, arranged in clusters, and it produces woody, elongated fruits.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique growth habit.
