Anthericum seineri

Anthericum seineri, commonly known as the meadow anthericum, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers surrounded by a spadix, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Anthericum seineri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
