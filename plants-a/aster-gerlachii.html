Aster gerlachii

Aster gerlachii, commonly known as the Gerlach's aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, grayish-green foliage. Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is a hardy species that thrives in full sun and well-drained soils.
