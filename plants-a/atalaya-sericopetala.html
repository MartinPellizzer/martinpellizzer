Atalaya sericopetala

Atalaya sericopetala, commonly known as the silverleaf atalaya, is a flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture.

Atalaya sericopetala is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden environments.
