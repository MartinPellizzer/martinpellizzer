Arundinaria victorialis

Arundinaria victorialis, commonly known as the river bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to 6 meters in height, forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread rapidly, making it an aggressive colonizer in wetland habitats.

Arundinaria victorialis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
