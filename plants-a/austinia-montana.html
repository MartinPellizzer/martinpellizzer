Austinia montana

Austinia montana, commonly known as the mountain Austinia, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in well-drained, rocky soils at higher elevations. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are usually narrow and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Austinia montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
