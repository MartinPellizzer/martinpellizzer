Acacia stenoptila

Acacia stenoptila, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and narrow, phyllodes that are typically a bright yellow-green color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Acacia stenoptila is also valued for its role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
