Alsophila × medinae

Alsophila × medinae, commonly known as the hybrid tree fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Alsophila costaricana and Alsophila decurrens.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bright green and can grow quite large, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

Alsophila × medinae is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for tropical plant enthusiasts.
