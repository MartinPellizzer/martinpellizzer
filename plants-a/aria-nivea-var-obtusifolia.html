Aria nivea var. obtusifolia

Aria nivea var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the white snowbell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a smooth, waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
