Abutilon mendocinum

Abutilon mendocinum, commonly known as the Mendo velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring hairy, broad leaves that are often lobed. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous seeds.

Abutilon mendocinum is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
