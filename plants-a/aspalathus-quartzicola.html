Aspalathus quartzicola

Aspalathus quartzicola, commonly known as the quartz rockbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils, often found in quartz-rich environments. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
