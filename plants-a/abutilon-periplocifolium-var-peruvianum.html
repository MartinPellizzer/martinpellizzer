Abutilon periplocifolium var. peruvianum

Abutilon periplocifolium var. peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian Abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and as a houseplant.
