Actinella carnosa

Actinella carnosa, commonly known as the woolly actinella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
