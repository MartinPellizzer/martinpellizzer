Astragalus urgutinus

Astragalus urgutinus, commonly known as the Urgut milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought conditions. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus urgutinus is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
