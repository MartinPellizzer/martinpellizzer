Acnida × tamariscina var. concatenata

Acnida × tamariscina var. concatenata, commonly known as the hybrid tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Tamaricaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor soil, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Its root system is extensive, allowing it to survive in areas with limited water availability.
