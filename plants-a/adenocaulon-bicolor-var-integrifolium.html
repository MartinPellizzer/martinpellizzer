Adenocaulon bicolor var. integrifolium

Adenocaulon bicolor var. integrifolium, commonly known as the white mountain bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant has simple, entire leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in high-altitude ecosystems.
