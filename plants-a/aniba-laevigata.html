Aniba laevigata

Aniba laevigata, commonly known as the Brazilian sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits.

The tree is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a natural varnish.
