Arthrostylidium amplissimum

Arthrostylidium amplissimum, commonly known as the giant spider orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that resembles a spider's web, composed of numerous pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant has a robust, erect stem with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforest or wetland habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
