Aconitum neomontanum subsp. bauhini

Aconitum neomontanum subsp.

bauhini, commonly known as the Alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or meadow habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and its roots contain toxic alkaloids.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
