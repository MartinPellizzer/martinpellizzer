Anthurium oblongispicum

Anthurium oblongispicum, commonly known as the broad-spined anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces striking, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface and prominent veins. Its flowers are typically small and clustered around the spadix, often in shades of white or pale yellow.

Anthurium oblongispicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
