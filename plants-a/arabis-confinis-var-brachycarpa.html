Arabis confinis var. brachycarpa

Arabis confinis var. brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-seeded rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are basal and spoon-shaped, while its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters.

The variety is distinguished by its shorter, more compact fruits compared to other varieties of Arabis confinis.
