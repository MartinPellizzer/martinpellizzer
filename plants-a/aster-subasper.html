Aster subasper

Aster subasper, commonly known as the rough aster, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and conical flower heads that typically bloom in shades of purple or blue. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

Its medicinal properties have been explored in traditional herbal practices for their potential anti-inflammatory effects.
