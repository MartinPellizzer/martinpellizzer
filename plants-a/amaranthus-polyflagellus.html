Amaranthus polyflagellus

Amaranthus polyflagellus, commonly known as the flagellate amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its branched, often reddish stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces tiny, green flowers that develop into three-angled, ribbed seeds. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a hardy species.

However, it is not widely cultivated for agricultural purposes due to its lack of economic value compared to other amaranth species.
