Asplundia albicarpa

Asplundia albicarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited asplundia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking white or pale yellow berries. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a spathe and spadix structure typical of the Araceae family.

The fruit is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor, making it a valuable resource in certain regions.
