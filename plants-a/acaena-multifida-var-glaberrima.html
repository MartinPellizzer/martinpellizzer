Acaena multifida var. glaberrima

Acaena multifida var. glaberrima, commonly known as the glabrous burr bush, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, spiny growth and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spherical, woody fruits that contain seeds surrounded by hooked bracts. It thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often forming dense thickets.

Due to its robust growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas, it plays a significant role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
