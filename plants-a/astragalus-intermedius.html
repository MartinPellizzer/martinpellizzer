Astragalus intermedius

Astragalus intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its legume-like pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, yellow to pale purple flowers that bloom in early summer.

Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of leaflets, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
