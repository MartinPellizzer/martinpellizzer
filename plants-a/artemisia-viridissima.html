Artemisia viridissima

Artemisia viridissima, commonly known as green wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and slightly fuzzy to the touch. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Artemisia viridissima is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
