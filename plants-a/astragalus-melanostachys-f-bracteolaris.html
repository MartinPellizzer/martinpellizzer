Astragalus melanostachys f. bracteolaris

Astragalus melanostachys f. bracteolaris, commonly known as the bracteolaris milkvetch, is a botanical variant of the species Astragalus melanostachys.

It is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of China and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, small yellow flowers, and distinctive bracteolaris structures that modify the inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This subspecies is often studied for its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
