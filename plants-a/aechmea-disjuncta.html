Aechmea disjuncta

Aechmea disjuncta, commonly known as the Disjunct Pineapple Bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant's leaves are typically dark green with a waxy texture and can grow up to several feet in length. It produces vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in the center of the rosette, often followed by the development of offsets.

Aechmea disjuncta is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its relatively easy care requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea disjuncta has rosette-shaped leaves that are sword-like, stiff, and covered with a dense layer of fine, white trichomes, giving them a silvery appearance.

The leaves are arranged in a tight spiral around the central rosette, with each leaf being relatively narrow and reaching up to 30 cm in length. The plant has a short, thick, and slightly woody stem with a smooth, light green bark that grows upright from the base of the rosette. It produces inflorescences with bright red bracts that enclose small, yellowish flowers, which bloom during the summer months and are pollinated primarily by insects.

The plant produces berries that are dispersed by birds, and its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in the soil.
