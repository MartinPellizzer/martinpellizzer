Azedarach elegans

Azedarach elegans, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lilac-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain three seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental tree in temperate climates.

The botanical features of Azedarach elegans make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable.
