Antirrhinum sempervirens var. gredense

Antirrhinum sempervirens var. gredense, commonly known as the Gredense Snapdragon, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
