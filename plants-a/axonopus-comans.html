Axonopus comans

Axonopus comans, commonly known as coastal setaria, is a grass species native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dune environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny seeds.

Axonopus comans is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate moderate drought conditions.
