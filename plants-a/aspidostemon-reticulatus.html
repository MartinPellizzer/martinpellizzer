Aspidostemon reticulatus

Aspidostemon reticulatus, commonly known as the net-veined mallow, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its hairy, oval-shaped leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in semi-arid regions.

Aspidostemon reticulatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
