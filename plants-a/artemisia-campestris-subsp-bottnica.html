Artemisia campestris subsp. bottnica

Artemisia campestris subsp.

bottnica, commonly known as the northern tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks.

The plant features silver-gray foliage with finely divided leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flower heads in the summer. It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to wetland environments and contribute to its ecological significance in these habitats.
