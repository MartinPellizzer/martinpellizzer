Acacia cuneata var. glabra

Acacia cuneata var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, pale green leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which attract a variety of pollinators. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods that split open to release the seeds.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
