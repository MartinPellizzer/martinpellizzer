Anthurium amnicola

Anthurium amnicola, commonly known as the river anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically deep green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, which can range from white to red. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium amnicola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its ability to add a lush, exotic look to indoor spaces.
