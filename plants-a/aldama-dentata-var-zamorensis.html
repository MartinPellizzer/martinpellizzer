Aldama dentata var. zamorensis

Aldama dentata var. zamorensis, commonly known as the Zamora's toothed aldama, is a shrub native to the highlands of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with moderate rainfall.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
