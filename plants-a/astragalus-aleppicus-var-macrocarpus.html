Astragalus aleppicus var. macrocarpus

Astragalus aleppicus var. macrocarpus, commonly known as the Aleppo milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow to green flowers in early summer. The plant is characterized by its robust root system and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing plants.

This variety is notable for its large, inflated pods that contain several seeds.
