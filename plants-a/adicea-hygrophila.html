Adicea hygrophila

Adicea hygrophila, commonly known as the water hibiscus, is a tropical perennial plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a prominent central boss. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found near water bodies such as ponds and streams. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a smooth, waxy surface.

Adicea hygrophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and water features.
