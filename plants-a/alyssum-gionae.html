Alyssum gionae

Alyssum gionae, commonly known as the Gionae candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many other flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Alyssum gionae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its delicate appearance.
