Adelmeria

Adelmeria, commonly known as the California rhododendron, is a genus of flowering plants native to the western United States.

These plants are evergreen shrubs characterized by their leathery, elliptic leaves and showy, tubular flowers. Adelmeria species typically grow in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. The flowers, which are usually pink or red, bloom in late spring to early summer.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
