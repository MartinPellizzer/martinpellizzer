Allophylus glabratus

Allophylus glabratus, commonly known as the smooth alder, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or oval in shape. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or yellowish and appear in clusters.

Allophylus glabratus produces small, dark blue berries that are often consumed by various bird species.
