Athanasia laevigata

Athanasia laevigata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Athanasia laevigata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its vibrant blooms.
