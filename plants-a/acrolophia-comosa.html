Acrolophia comosa

Acrolophia comosa, commonly known as the woolly thornveld grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, fibrous leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and savanna ecosystems. Its flowering stems produce small, clustered inflorescences, and it is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Acrolophia comosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small animals and insects.
