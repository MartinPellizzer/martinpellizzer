Aster albescens var. discolor

Aster albescens var. discolor, commonly known as the White Wood Aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a central disk that may change color, often appearing pink or red. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
