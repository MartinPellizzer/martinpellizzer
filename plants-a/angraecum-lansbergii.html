Angraecum lansbergii

Angraecum lansbergii, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night, attracting specific nocturnal pollinators. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique floral structure and pollination strategy make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.

Angraecum lansbergii is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts.
