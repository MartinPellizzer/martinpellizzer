Ardisia oldhamii

Ardisia oldhamii, commonly known as the Oldham's beautyberry, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and emit a pleasant fragrance when crushed. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. These berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Ardisia oldhamii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
