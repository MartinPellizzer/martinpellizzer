Aconitum karakolicum var. karakolicum

Aconitum karakolicum var. karakolicum, commonly known as Karakol aconite, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, terminal racemes. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and the plant has a strong, toxic scent.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows at high elevations.
