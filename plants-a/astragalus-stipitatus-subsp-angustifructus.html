Astragalus stipitatus subsp. angustifructus

Astragalus stipitatus subsp.

angustifructus, commonly known as the narrow-fruited milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of western North America. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring. Its fruit is a slender, curved pod that contains several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
