Aspidium irregulare

Aspidium irregulare, commonly known as the irregular water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often covered in a layer of sori, which are clusters of sporangia used for spore production.

Aspidium irregulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
