Aconitum ferox var. spicatum

Aconitum ferox var. spicatum, commonly known as the Spiculate Monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to blue flowers arranged in a spire-like cluster. The plant features palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its roots contain potent alkaloids, making it toxic. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its native habitat.

Due to its toxicity, it is often cultivated in gardens with caution.
