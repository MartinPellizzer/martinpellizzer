Adelia barbinervis

Adelia barbinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined adelia, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, narrow leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
