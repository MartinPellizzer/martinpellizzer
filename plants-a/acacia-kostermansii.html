Acacia kostermansii

Acacia kostermansii, commonly known as the Koster's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the savannas of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed structure and its distinctive, feathery foliage composed of small, opposite leaflets. The plant produces yellow, spherical flower heads that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it is often used in traditional medicine by local communities.

Acacia kostermansii is also valued for its role in preventing soil erosion and supporting local biodiversity.
