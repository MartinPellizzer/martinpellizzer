Arenaria serpyllifolia subsp. lloydii

Arenaria serpyllifolia subsp.

lloydii, commonly known as the coastal sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it has a mat-forming growth habit.

This subspecies is often found in dune ecosystems and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
