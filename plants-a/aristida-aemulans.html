Aristida aemulans

Aristida aemulans, commonly known as the mimosa grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types, making it a resilient choice for landscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate appearance and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
