Alyssum tortuosum f. rhomboideum

Alyssum tortuosum f. rhomboideum, commonly known as the rhomboid allyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial with dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are usually narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to ornamental horticulture.
