Aydendron obtusifolium

Aydendron obtusifolium, commonly known as the blunt-leaf aydendron, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features woody stems and oblong, thick, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers clustered in terminal inflorescences, which bloom during the cooler months. Its adaptability to dry conditions makes it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
