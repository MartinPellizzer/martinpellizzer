Angraecum longinode

Angraecum longinode, commonly known as the longinode orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendulous inflorescence and distinctive, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

Angraecum longinode is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
