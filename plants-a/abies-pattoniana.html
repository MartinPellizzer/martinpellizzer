Abies pattoniana

Abies pattoniana, commonly known as the Patton's fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Chile.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing winged seeds dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing some protection against environmental stress.

Abies pattoniana is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in cloud forests with high humidity and moderate temperatures.
