Andropogon villosus subvar. riedelianus

Andropogon villosus subvar. riedelianus, commonly known as the Riedel's hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy, narrow leaves that are often tinged with silver or gray. The plant produces inflorescences of spikelets that are typically arranged in a panicle, with the flowers being small and wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
