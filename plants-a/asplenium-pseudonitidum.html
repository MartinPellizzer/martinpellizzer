Asplenium pseudonitidum

Asplenium pseudonitidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive, narrow shape.

Asplenium pseudonitidum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and indoor spaces.
