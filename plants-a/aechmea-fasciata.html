Aechmea fasciata

Aechmea fasciata, commonly known as the pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater and nutrients. The plant produces a tall flower spike that can reach several feet in height, bearing vibrant orange or red flowers. Its foliage is often marked with distinctive silver or white stripes, giving it a striking appearance.

Aechmea fasciata is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea fasciata has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, strap-like, and arranged in a spiral around the central stem, with a striking silver-white and green coloration.

The stem is short and thick, often hidden beneath the leaves, with a smooth, dark green bark. Its flowers are arranged in a spike-like inflorescence, featuring vibrant red bracts that enclose small, white, tubular flowers, which bloom during the dry season and are pollinated by insects. The plant produces berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

The roots are fibrous and form a dense network around the base of the plant, aiding in water absorption and anchoring.
