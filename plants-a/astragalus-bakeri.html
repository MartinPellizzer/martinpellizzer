Astragalus bakeri

Astragalus bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus bakeri is known for its ability to thrive in arid environments and is often found in areas with well-drained soils.
