Aloe × hertrichii

Aloe × hertrichii, commonly known as the hybrid aloe or the hybrid aloe plant, is a succulent species resulting from the cross between Aloe arborescens and Aloe vera.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating called farina. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower clusters that bloom in the spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It is native to South Africa and thrives in well-drained soil with moderate sunlight.

Aloe × hertrichii is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
