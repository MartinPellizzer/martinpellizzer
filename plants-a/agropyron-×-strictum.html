Agropyron × strictum

Agropyron × strictum, commonly known as the wheatgrasses, is a hybrid species within the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant typically produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Agropyron × strictum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
