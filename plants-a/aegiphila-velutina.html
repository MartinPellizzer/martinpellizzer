Aegiphila velutina

Aegiphila velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf aegiphila, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round fruits.

Aegiphila velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
