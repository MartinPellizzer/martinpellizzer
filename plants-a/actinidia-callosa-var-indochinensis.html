Actinidia callosa var. indochinensis

Actinidia callosa var. indochinensis, commonly known as the Indochinese rough actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces aggregate berries that ripen to a dark purple color, typically in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
