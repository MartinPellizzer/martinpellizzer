Arisarum vulgare var. subexertum

Arisarum vulgare var. subexertum, commonly known as the lesser arum, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, spatulate flowers that are often surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
