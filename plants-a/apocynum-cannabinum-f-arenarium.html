Apocynum cannabinum f. arenarium

Apocynum cannabinum f. arenarium, commonly known as the sand hemp or sand milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and dunes.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use by Indigenous peoples.
