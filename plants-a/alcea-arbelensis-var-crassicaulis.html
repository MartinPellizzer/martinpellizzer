Alcea arbelensis var. crassicaulis

Alcea arbelensis var. crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed hollyhock, is a perennial plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, thick stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, providing a distinctive texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
