Asphodelus albus subsp. ramosus

Asphodelus albus subsp.

ramosus, commonly known as the white asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features strap-like, pale green to white leaves and produces tall, erect flowering stems with showy, white or pale yellow flowers. Its inflorescence is a raceme of small, three-lobed flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is noted for its drought tolerance and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
