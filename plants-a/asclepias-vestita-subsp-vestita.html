Asclepias vestita subsp. vestita

Asclepias vestita subsp.

vestita, commonly known as the woolly milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy stems and leaves covered in fine white hairs, which give it a distinctive, woolly appearance.

The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish flowers that attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees. Its milkweed sap contains cardenolides, which are toxic to many herbivores but serve as a defense mechanism.

This subspecies is particularly important as a host plant for the monarch butterfly, supporting the life cycle of this iconic species.
