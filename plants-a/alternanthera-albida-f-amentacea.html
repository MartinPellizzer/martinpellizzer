Alternanthera albida f. amentacea

Alternanthera albida f. amentacea, commonly known as the silver foliage alternanthera, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive white or pale silver variegated leaves that contrast beautifully with its green stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in loose panicles, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This cultivar is popular in aquatic and bog gardens due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to wet environments.
