Acacia nellyrenza

Acacia nellyrenza, commonly known as Nelly's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant can also serve as a source of fodder for livestock.

Acacia nellyrenza plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
