Andropogon rufispicus

Andropogon rufispicus, commonly known as the red-tipped grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often reddish in color. The plant is characterized by its robust growth habit and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
