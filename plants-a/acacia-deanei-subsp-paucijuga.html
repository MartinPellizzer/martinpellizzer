Acacia deanei subsp. paucijuga

Acacia deanei subsp.

paucijuga, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched structure and phyllodes that are linear to narrowly elliptic.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are reduced to phyllodes, a common adaptation in acacia species to reduce water loss.

The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
