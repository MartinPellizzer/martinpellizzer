Ampelopsis cardiospermoides

Ampelopsis cardiospermoides, commonly known as the heartseed grape, is a woody vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by distinctive, heart-shaped berries that turn red when mature. Its berries contain a single, large seed, which is a unique feature of the species.

Ampelopsis cardiospermoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
