Acanthocladium dockeri

Acanthocladium dockeri, commonly known as the dwarf rock orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with dark green, grass-like leaves and a single, slender flowering stem. The plant produces delicate, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and requires a cool, humid environment.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
