Artemisia japonica f. manshurica

Artemisia japonica f. manshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to northeastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features aromatic, silver-gray foliage with finely divided leaves that emit a strong, camphor-like scent. It produces small, yellowish flowers in panicles during the summer months.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant in gardens.
