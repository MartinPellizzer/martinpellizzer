Albuca rautanenii

Albuca rautanenii, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is known for its compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Albuca rautanenii is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
