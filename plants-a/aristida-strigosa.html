Aristida strigosa

Aristida strigosa, commonly known as the rough hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Aristida strigosa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
