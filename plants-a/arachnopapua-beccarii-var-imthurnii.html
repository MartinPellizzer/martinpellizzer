Arachnopapua beccarii var. imthurnii

Arachnopapua beccarii var. imthurnii, commonly known as the Imthurn's spider orchid, is a unique orchid species native to specific regions of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which includes specialized adaptations for pollination by specific insect species. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it relies on mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake. Its flowers display a range of colors, often with intricate patterns that aid in attracting pollinators.
