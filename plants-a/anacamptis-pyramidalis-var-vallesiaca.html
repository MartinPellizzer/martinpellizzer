Anacamptis pyramidalis var. vallesiaca

Anacamptis pyramidalis var. vallesiaca, commonly known as the Vallesi's orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
