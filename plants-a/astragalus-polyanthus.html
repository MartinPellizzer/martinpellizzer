Astragalus polyanthus

Astragalus polyanthus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus polyanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
