Adiantum sinicum

Adiantum sinicum, commonly known as the Chinese maidenhair fern, is a species native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, elegant appearance.

Adiantum sinicum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
