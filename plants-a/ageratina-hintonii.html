Ageratina hintonii

Ageratina hintonii, commonly known as Hinton's blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its fluffy, blue-gray inflorescences that resemble a misty cloud, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually opposite, narrow, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Ageratina hintonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
