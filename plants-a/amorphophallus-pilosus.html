Amorphophallus pilosus

Amorphophallus pilosus, commonly known as the naked lady or voodoo lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, striking purple spathe with a yellow spadix, which resembles a phallic shape, hence its scientific name. The plant produces a toxic, milky sap that can cause skin irritation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade.

Amorphophallus pilosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various regions.
