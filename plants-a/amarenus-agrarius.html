Amarenus agrarius

Amarenus agrarius, commonly known as the redroot pigweed, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant produces small, triangular seeds that are dispersed by wind, allowing it to spread rapidly in disturbed soils. It is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides, where it can become a weed.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
