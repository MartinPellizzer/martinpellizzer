Astragalus pseudomultijugus

Astragalus pseudomultijugus, commonly known as the false many-lobed milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant has a taproot system and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus pseudomultijugus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
