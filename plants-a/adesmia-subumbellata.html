Adesmia subumbellata

Adesmia subumbellata, commonly known as the pink burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in umbellate clusters. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its fruit is a legume that develops into a spiny, woody pod containing seeds.

Adesmia subumbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
