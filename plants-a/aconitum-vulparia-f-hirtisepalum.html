Aconitum vulparia f. hirtisepalum

Aconitum vulparia f. hirtisepalum, commonly known as the hairy-sepal monkshood, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers with deep purple petals and a distinctive, spurred lower lip. The plant features hairy sepals that distinguish it from other varieties of monkshood. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Its botanical features make it a notable ornamental plant in horticulture.
