Acacia argentea

Acacia argentea, commonly known as silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white bloom. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Acacia argentea is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
