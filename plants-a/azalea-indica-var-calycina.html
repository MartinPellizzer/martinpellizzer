Azalea indica var. calycina

Azalea indica var. calycina, commonly known as the Chinese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Korea.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and produces small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sun for optimal growth.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes.
