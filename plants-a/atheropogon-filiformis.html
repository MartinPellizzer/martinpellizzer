Atheropogon filiformis

Atheropogon filiformis, commonly known as the slender grass or filiform grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and is often found in dry or semi-arid environments.

Atheropogon filiformis is not widely cultivated but is of interest in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in local ecosystems.
