Arundinaria schmidiana

Arundinaria schmidiana, commonly known as the Schmid's bamboo, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Arundinaria schmidiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
