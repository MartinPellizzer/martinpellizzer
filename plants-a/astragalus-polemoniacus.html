Astragalus polemoniacus

Astragalus polemoniacus, commonly known as the Polemoniacus milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native habitat.
