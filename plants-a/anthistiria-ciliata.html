Anthistiria ciliata

Anthistiria ciliata, commonly known as the silky thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open grasslands and shrublands. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has a taproot system and is somewhat drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to arid environments.
