Aristolochia zonguldakensis

Aristolochia zonguldakensis, commonly known as Turkish Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its unique, hooded flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it should be handled with care as it contains toxic compounds.
