Astrocaryum echinatum

Astrocaryum echinatum, commonly known as the Brazilian buriti palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It features a smooth, gray trunk with a swollen base and produces large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. The fruit is an important food source for various wildlife, including birds and mammals.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in tropical ecosystems.
