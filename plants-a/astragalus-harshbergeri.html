Astragalus harshbergeri

Astragalus harshbergeri, commonly known as Harshberger's milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
