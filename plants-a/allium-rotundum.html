Allium rotundum

Allium rotundum, commonly known as round leek or Persian shallot, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, cylindrical bulbs with a mild, garlic-like flavor. The plant has grass-like, gray-green leaves that are often used in cooking. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and arranged in umbels.

Allium rotundum is valued for both its culinary and ornamental qualities in gardens.
