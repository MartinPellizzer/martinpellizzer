Artemisia pulchella

Artemisia pulchella, commonly known as the silky goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-gray leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Artemisia pulchella typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
