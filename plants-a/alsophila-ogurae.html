Alsophila ogurae

Alsophila ogurae, commonly known as the Ogunza tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds. The plant has a distinctive crown of pinnate leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The fronds are used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants in horticulture.
