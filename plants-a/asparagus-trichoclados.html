Asparagus trichoclados

Asparagus trichoclados, commonly known as the white asparagus or asparagus fern, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fern-like foliage and produces small, white, edible shoots that are harvested before they fully develop. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a vegetable in some regions.

Its botanical features include compound leaves and a fibrous root system that helps it adapt to arid environments.
