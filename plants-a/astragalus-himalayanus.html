Astragalus himalayanus

Astragalus himalayanus, commonly known as Himalayan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Astragalus himalayanus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
