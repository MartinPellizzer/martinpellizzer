Alyogyne hakeifolia

Alyogyne hakeifolia, commonly known as the hakea-flowered alyogyne, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense spikes. Alyogyne hakeifolia is often found in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
