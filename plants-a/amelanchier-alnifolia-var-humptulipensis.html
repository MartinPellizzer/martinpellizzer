Amelanchier alnifolia var. humptulipensis

Amelanchier alnifolia var. humptulipensis, commonly known as the Humptulip serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark purple berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological benefits, supporting local wildlife.
