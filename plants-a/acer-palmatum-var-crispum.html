Acer palmatum var. crispum

Acer palmatum var. crispum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with crinkled leaves, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, crinkled, and often lobed leaves that display a range of colors from light green to deep red, especially in autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and prefers well-drained, acidic soil in partial shade. It produces small, red flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red samaras.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
