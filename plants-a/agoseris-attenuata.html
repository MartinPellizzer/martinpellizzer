Agoseris attenuata

Agoseris attenuata, commonly known as the slender yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture.

Agoseris attenuata is part of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.
