Aspilia hassleriana var. scaberrima

Aspilia hassleriana var. scaberrima, commonly known as the rough aspilia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The leaves are typically rough and hairy, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
