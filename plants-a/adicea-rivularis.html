Adicea rivularis

Adicea rivularis, commonly known as the riverweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that often grow along the edges of streams and rivers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or pink in color. Adicea rivularis thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
