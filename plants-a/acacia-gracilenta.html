Acacia gracilenta

Acacia gracilenta, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, bipinnate leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals or through wind.

Acacia gracilenta is well-suited to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing soils in degraded areas.
