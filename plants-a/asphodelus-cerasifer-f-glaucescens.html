Asphodelus cerasifer f. glaucescens

Asphodelus cerasifer f. glaucescens, commonly known as the purple-flowered asphodel, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of showy, lavender to purple flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue, which is enhanced in this form, f. glaucescens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This ornamental plant is also valued for its resilience and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
