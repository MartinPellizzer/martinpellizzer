Adesmia confusa

Adesmia confusa, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pods. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, hairy pods that split open when mature to release the seeds.

Adesmia confusa is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
