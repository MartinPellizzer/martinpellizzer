Amaranthus tristis var. flexuosus

Amaranthus tristis var. flexuosus, commonly known as the creeping amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with branches that often trail along the ground. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

This species is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
