Acaena × ovina var. ambigua

Acaena × ovina var. ambigua, commonly known as the New Zealand bracken or bracken fern, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its fern-like fronds and spore-bearing structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along streambanks. This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
