Ammannia linearis

Ammannia linearis, commonly known as the slender ammannia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its linear, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its stems are often reddish in color and can spread along the ground or grow upright.

Ammannia linearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting aquatic and wetland ecosystems.
