Adenanthera falcata

Adenanthera falcata, commonly known as the sickle bean or African bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, curved pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown for its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Adenanthera falcata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural systems.
