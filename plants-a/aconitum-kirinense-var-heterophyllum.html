Aconitum kirinense var. heterophyllum

Aconitum kirinense var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the Japanese monkshood, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It features striking blue to purple spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
