Artemisia baimaensis

Artemisia baimaensis, commonly known as the Baima wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typical of the composite flower structure. It thrives in cold, dry environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
