Astragalus subhanensis

Astragalus subhanensis, commonly known as the Subhan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several narrow leaflets, and it produces pods that contain small, rounded seeds.

Astragalus subhanensis plays a role in local ecosystems by contributing to soil stabilization and providing nectar for pollinators.
