Acacia laevis

Acacia laevis, commonly known as the smooth wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Acacia laevis is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
