Anthericum tuberiferum

Anthericum tuberiferum, commonly known as the tuberous arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features broad, waxy leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, and it produces striking, umbrella-shaped inflorescences with white or pale pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its underground tubers, which serve as storage organs and are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
