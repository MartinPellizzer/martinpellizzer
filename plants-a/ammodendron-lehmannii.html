Ammodendron lehmannii

Ammodendron lehmannii, commonly known as Lehmann's date palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Palmae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. It produces small, clustered inflorescences and is adapted to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
