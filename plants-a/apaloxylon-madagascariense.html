Apaloxylon madagascariense

Apaloxylon madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar date palm, is a unique species native to Madagascar.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Arecaceae family, characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible fruits resembling dates. It thrives in tropical climates and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, conservation efforts are essential to protect this rare species.
