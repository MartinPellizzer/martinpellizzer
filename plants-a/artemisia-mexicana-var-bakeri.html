Artemisia mexicana var. bakeri

Artemisia mexicana var. bakeri, commonly known as Mexican tarragon, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves contain volatile oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
