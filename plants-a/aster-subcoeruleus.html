Aster subcoeruleus

Aster subcoeruleus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to blue, depending on the variety and environmental conditions. The plant has a compact, mounding growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Aster subcoeruleus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
