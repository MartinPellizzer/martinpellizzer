Argemone × hybrida

Argemone × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a cross between Argemone mexicana and Argemone oilcake, resulting in a plant with distinctive red or orange petals. The species is characterized by its erect, branching stem and capsule-like fruits that contain oily seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
