Anisostachya induta

Anisostachya induta, commonly known as the slender reedgrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and riverbanks.

Botanically, it is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
