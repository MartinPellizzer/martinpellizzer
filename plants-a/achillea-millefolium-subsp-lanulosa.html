Achillea millefolium subsp. lanulosa

Achillea millefolium subsp.

lanulosa, commonly known as the lanate yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that are covered in a soft, woolly layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces flat-topped clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and dry grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
