Acaciopsis californica

Acaciopsis californica, commonly known as the California acaciopsis, is a small, deciduous shrub native to coastal areas of California.

It has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets that are arranged in pairs along the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and dry summers.

Acaciopsis californica is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for native wildlife.
