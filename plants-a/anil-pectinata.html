Anil pectinata

Anil pectinata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces oblong, woody pods that contain several seeds. Anil pectinata is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to a variety of soil types and climates.
