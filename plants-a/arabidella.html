Arabidella

Arabidella, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Arabidella is also notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Its ecological importance makes it a valuable species in desert ecosystems.
