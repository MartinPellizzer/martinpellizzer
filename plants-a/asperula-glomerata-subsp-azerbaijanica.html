Asperula glomerata subsp. azerbaijanica

Asperula glomerata subsp.

azerbaijanica, commonly known as the Azerbaijani woodruff, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers.

The plant produces clusters of white or pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
