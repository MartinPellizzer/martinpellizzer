Agathosma parviflora

Agathosma parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered agathosma, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers and wet winters. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers during the winter months. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Agathosma parviflora is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
