Alsine aristata

Alsine aristata, commonly known as the arrowleaf sandmat, is a low-growing annual herb native to the western United States.

It has distinctive arrow-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and dunes.

Alsine aristata is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant in dry climates.
