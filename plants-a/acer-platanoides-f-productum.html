Acer platanoides f. productum

Acer platanoides f. productum, commonly known as the Norway maple, is a cultivar of the maple tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of yellow and orange in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It typically grows as a rounded, multi-trunked tree with smooth, light gray bark.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
