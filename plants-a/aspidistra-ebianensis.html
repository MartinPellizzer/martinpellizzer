Aspidistra ebianensis

Aspidistra ebianensis, commonly known as the Japanese evergreen, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to Japan.

It is a perennial evergreen plant characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that are borne on short pedicels. It thrives in low-light conditions and is known for its ability to tolerate shade, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Aspidistra ebianensis is also valued for its ornamental foliage and its resilience to poor soil conditions.
