Astridia latisepala

Astridia latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled aster, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, violet-blue to purple ray florets surrounding a central disk of yellow tubular flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Astridia latisepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
