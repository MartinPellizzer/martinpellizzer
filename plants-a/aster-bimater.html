Aster bimater

Aster bimater, commonly known as the two-petaled aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in the late summer and early fall.

Aster bimater is known for its unique floral structure, with two distinct petaled flowers per head, making it a distinctive and ornamental species.
