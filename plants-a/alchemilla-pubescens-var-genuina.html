Alchemilla pubescens var. genuina

Alchemilla pubescens var. genuina, commonly known as the downy lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features a rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in clusters atop leafy stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its common name reflects its historical use in herbal medicine and its ornamental value in gardens.
