Aquilegia bauhini subsp. thalictrifolia

Aquilegia bauhini subsp.

thalictrifolia, commonly known as the Alpine columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from May to June.

The plant has finely divided, pinnately arranged leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. It prefers well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in alpine gardens.
