Asplenium striatum var. lonchophyllum

Asplenium striatum var. lonchophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spleenwort, is a fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments.

It has slender, creeping rhizomes and produces small, lance-shaped leaves with a distinct midrib. The fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a finely serrated margin. It is native to parts of Asia and is often found on rock surfaces or in shaded forest floors.

This variety is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and is a hardy, low-maintenance plant suitable for woodland gardens.
