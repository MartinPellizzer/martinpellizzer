Aconitum sqarrosum

Aconitum sqarrosum, commonly known as the Scotch monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking purple to blue flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum sqarrosum is also known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
