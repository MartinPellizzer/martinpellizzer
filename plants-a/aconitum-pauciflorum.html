Aconitum pauciflorum

Aconitum pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has dark green, deeply lobed leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Aconitum pauciflorum is notable for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
