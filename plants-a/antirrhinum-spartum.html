Antirrhinum spartum

Antirrhinum spartum, commonly known as the desert snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Mediterranean.

It features upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive two-lipped structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in dryland environments.
