Ancylocladus rufescens

Ancylocladus rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the family Cycadaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk that often develops a reddish hue as it matures. The plant produces large, feather-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Its reproductive structures include male and female cones, typical of cycads.

Ancylocladus rufescens is native to Southeast Asia and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
