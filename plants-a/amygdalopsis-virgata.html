Amygdalopsis virgata

Amygdalopsis virgata, commonly known as the Virginia almond, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by small, almond-like fruits that are edible but have a bitter taste.

Amygdalopsis virgata is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
