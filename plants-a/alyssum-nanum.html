Alyssum nanum

Alyssum nanum, commonly known as dwarf alyssum, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tiny, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum nanum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs and hoverflies.
