Albizia tanganyicensis subsp. tanganyicensis

Albizia tanganyicensis subsp.

tanganyicensis, commonly known as the East African siris, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Tanzania and parts of eastern Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with small, ovate leaflets.

The tree produces yellow to orange pod fruits that contain lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats. Albizia tanganyicensis subsp.

tanganyicensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.
