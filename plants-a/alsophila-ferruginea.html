Alsophila ferruginea

Alsophila ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty chain fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The fronds are typically dark green with a metallic sheen, and the plant produces sporangia on the undersides of the leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The plant has a slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental appearance in horticulture.
