Acacia humifusa

Acacia humifusa, commonly known as the low acacia or coastal acacia, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems that often grow close to the ground. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with small, pointed leaflets.

Acacia humifusa is adapted to coastal environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
