Agave × cavanillesii

Agave × cavanillesii, commonly known as the Mexican stonecress, is a hybrid species within the Agavaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its rosette-forming structure with thick, fleshy leaves that are often blue-green in color. It produces a tall flowering stalk that bears clusters of small, white or pale green flowers.

Agave × cavanillesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens due to its drought tolerance.
