Aster consanguineus

Aster consanguineus, commonly known as the redstem aster, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands. Its showy purple to pink flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
