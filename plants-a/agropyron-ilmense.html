Agropyron ilmense

Agropyron ilmense, commonly known as the Ilmense wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to blue-gray florets. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Agropyron ilmense is valued for its adaptability and is used in ecological restoration projects.
