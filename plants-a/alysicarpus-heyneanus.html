Alysicarpus heyneanus

Alysicarpus heyneanus, commonly known as the African burr or wild cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine and agriculture. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
