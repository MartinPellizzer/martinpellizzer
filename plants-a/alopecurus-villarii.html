Alopecurus villarii

Alopecurus villarii, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils, often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant is typically low-growing, with a clump-forming habit, and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

Its botanical features make it an important component of diverse grassland ecosystems.
