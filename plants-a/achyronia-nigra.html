Achyronia nigra

Achyronia nigra, commonly known as black nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe, Asia, and Africa, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features small, dark purple to black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture, and it produces yellowish-white flowers.

The species is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
