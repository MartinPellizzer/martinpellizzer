Anaxeton virgatum

Anaxeton virgatum, commonly known as the slender sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy stems and small, pale green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes or inland deserts.

Due to its resilience and low maintenance, it is sometimes used in restoration projects to stabilize soil in degraded environments.
