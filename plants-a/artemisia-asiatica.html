Artemisia asiatica

Artemisia asiatica, commonly known as Chinese wormwood, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant contains essential oils and various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its hardy nature allows it to spread readily in suitable environments.
