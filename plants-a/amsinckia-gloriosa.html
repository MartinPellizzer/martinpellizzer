Amsinckia gloriosa

Amsinckia gloriosa, commonly known as the golden globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Amsinckia gloriosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
