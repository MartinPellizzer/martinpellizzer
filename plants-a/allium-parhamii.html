Allium parhamii

Allium parhamii, commonly known as the Parham's onion, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, spherical bulbs and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, arranged in a loose cluster at the top of the stem.

Allium parhamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and potentially medicinal properties.
