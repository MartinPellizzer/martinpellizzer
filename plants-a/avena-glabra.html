Avena glabra

Avena glabra, commonly known as slender oat, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in temperate climates.

Avena glabra is sometimes cultivated for forage or as a weed in agricultural settings.
