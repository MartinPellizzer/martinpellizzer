Ambrosia divaricata

Ambrosia divaricata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ambrosia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Ambrosia divaricata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
