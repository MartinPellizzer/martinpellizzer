Amaryllis umbella

Amaryllis umbella, commonly known as the clustered amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features showy, umbel-like clusters of bright red or pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has strap-shaped, evergreen leaves that are broad and glossy, often reaching several feet in length. Its bulbous stem stores nutrients, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

Amaryllis umbella is a popular ornamental plant appreciated for its vibrant blooms and elegant foliage.
