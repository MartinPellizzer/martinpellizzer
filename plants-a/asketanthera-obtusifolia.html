Asketanthera obtusifolia

Asketanthera obtusifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its roots are extensive and can help stabilize soil in arid environments.

It is often found in coastal and saline soils, showing some tolerance to drought and salt.
