Allium carneum

Allium carneum, commonly known as the purple onion, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a spherical bulb with a purple skin and white flesh, resembling a small onion. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Allium carneum is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
