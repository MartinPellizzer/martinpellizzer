Arthraxon hispidus f. quartinianus

Arthraxon hispidus f. quartinianus, commonly known as the Quartinian form of the hairy arthraxon, is a variant of the grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences.

This form is of interest in botanical studies due to its morphological distinctiveness and potential ecological significance.
