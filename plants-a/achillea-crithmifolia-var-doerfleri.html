Achillea crithmifolia var. doerfleri

Achillea crithmifolia var. doerfleri, commonly known as the rock daisy or Doerfler's yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal rosettes of silver-gray, spoon-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in summer. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found growing in rocky or gravelly soils. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscaping.
