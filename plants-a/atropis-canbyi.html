Atropis canbyi

Atropis canbyi, commonly known as the Canby's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the milkweed family, Apocynaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic to many animals, including mammals and insects. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a rough texture.

Atropis canbyi plays a vital role in supporting local pollinators, particularly butterflies and bees.
