Abutilon crassinervium

Abutilon crassinervium, commonly known as the broad-veined abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its rough, hairy stems and lobed leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

Abutilon crassinervium is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
