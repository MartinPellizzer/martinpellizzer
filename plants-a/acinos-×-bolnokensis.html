Acinos × bolnokensis

Acinos × bolnokensis, commonly known as the Bolnokensis acino, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Russia. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with hairy, gray-green leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
