Athamanta albanica

Athamanta albanica, commonly known as the Albanian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges. Its leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and branched.

Athamanta albanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
