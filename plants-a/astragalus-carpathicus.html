Astragalus carpathicus

Astragalus carpathicus, commonly known as Carpathian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus carpathicus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
