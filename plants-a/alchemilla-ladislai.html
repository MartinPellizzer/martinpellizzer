Alchemilla ladislai

Alchemilla ladislai, commonly known as Ladislav's lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of velvety, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Alchemilla ladislai is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in woodland settings.
