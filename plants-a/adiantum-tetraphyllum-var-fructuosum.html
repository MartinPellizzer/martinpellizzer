Adiantum tetraphyllum var. fructuosum

Adiantum tetraphyllum var. fructuosum, commonly known as the fruit-bearing maidenhair fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or wooded areas. Its unique feature is the presence of small, clustered fruiting structures, which are rare in ferns.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
