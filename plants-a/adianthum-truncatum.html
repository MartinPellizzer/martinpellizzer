Adianthum truncatum

Adianthum truncatum, commonly known as the cut-leaved maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Adiantaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically grayish-white in color due to the presence of trichomes. The plant is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with a distinctive truncate or cut-off shape at the tips.

Adianthum truncatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its graceful, airy appearance.
