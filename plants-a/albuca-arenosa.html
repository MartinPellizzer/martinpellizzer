Albuca arenosa

Albuca arenosa, commonly known as the sand albuca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative plant.

Albuca arenosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
