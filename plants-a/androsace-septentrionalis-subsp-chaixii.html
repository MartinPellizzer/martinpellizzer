Androsace septentrionalis subsp. chaixii

Androsace septentrionalis subsp.

chaixii, commonly known as the northern sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe. It typically thrives in open, rocky or sandy habitats, often found in alpine and subalpine zones.

The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in harsh environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid or rocky areas.
