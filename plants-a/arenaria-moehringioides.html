Arenaria moehringioides

Arenaria moehringioides, commonly known as the sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
