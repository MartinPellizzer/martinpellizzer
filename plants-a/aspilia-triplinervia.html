Aspilia triplinervia

Aspilia triplinervia, commonly known as the three-veined aster, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed with three main veins, giving it its scientific name.

Aspilia triplinervia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
