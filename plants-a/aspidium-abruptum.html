Aspidium abruptum

Aspidium abruptum, commonly known as the rough holly fern, is a species of fern native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often serrated fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Aspidium abruptum is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
