Apera spica-venti var. purpurascens

Apera spica-venti var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its distinctive purple-tinged inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and fields. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that becomes more purple in the inflorescence.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
