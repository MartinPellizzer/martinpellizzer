Andropogon breviaristatus

Andropogon breviaristatus, commonly known as the narrow-awn grass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a tufted growth pattern typical of many grasses.

It is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils.
