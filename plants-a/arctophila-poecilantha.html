Arctophila poecilantha

Arctophila poecilantha, commonly known as the mountain sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in rocky, alpine, or arid environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually small, fleshy, and adapted to conserve water, reflecting its drought-tolerant nature.

Arctophila poecilantha plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and provides nectar for pollinators.
