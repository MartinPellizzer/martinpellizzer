Astragalus purshii var. lectulus

Astragalus purshii var. lectulus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological restoration and as a forage plant for wildlife.
