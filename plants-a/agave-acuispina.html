Agave acuispina

Agave acuispina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The leaves are lined with sharp, spiny margins, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. It produces a tall flower stalk that bears yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in the spring.

This plant is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
