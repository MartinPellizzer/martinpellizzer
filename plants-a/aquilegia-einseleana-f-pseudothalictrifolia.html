Aquilegia einseleana f. pseudothalictrifolia

Aquilegia einseleana f. pseudothalictrifolia, commonly known as the False Meadow Rue, is a hybrid form of the genus Aquilegia, characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers.

It typically exhibits a compact growth habit with finely divided, fern-like leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in early to mid-summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This cultivar is valued for its unique foliage and floral display, making it a popular choice in garden landscapes.
