Aeginetia abbreviata

Aeginetia abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tiny, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Its leaves are simple and often reduced, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

Aeginetia abbreviata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
