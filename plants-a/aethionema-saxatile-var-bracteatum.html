Aethionema saxatile var. bracteatum

Aethionema saxatile var. bracteatum, commonly known as the Alpina Snowbell, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are pale yellow and resemble bells, giving it its common name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
