Alafia vallium

Alafia vallium, commonly known as the mountain alafia, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous forests and alpine meadows. Alafia vallium has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
