Asclepias arborea

Asclepias arborea, commonly known as the tree milkweed, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that can reach heights of up to 10 feet. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Asclepias arborea is an important host plant for monarch butterfly larvae and plays a vital role in supporting pollinators.
