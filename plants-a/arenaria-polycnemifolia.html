Arenaria polycnemifolia

Arenaria polycnemifolia, commonly known as the many-fruit sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are typically fleshy and succulent. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Arenaria polycnemifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is often found in tundra and mountainous areas.
