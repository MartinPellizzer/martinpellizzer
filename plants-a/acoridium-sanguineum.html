Acoridium sanguineum

Acoridium sanguineum, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-orange sap, which is responsible for its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments.

Acoridium sanguineum is often found in wetlands and along the edges of streams.
