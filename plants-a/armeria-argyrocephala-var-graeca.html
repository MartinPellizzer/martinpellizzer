Armeria argyrocephala var. graeca

Armeria argyrocephala var. graeca, commonly known as the Greek silver-leafed thrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically silver-gray in color, giving the plant its common name, and are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
