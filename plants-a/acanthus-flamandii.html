Acanthus flamandii

Acanthus flamandii, commonly known as the French acanthus, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy, spire-like inflorescences topped with small, clustered flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. Acanthus flamandii is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Its architectural form and ornate foliage make it a popular choice for garden borders and as a focal point in landscape design.
