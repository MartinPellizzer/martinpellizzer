Anadelphia chevalieri

Anadelphia chevalieri, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a unique plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its arid habitat.
