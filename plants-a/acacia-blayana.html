Acacia blayana

Acacia blayana, commonly known as the Blayana wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring a deep root system for water absorption. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for local wildlife.

Acacia blayana is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
