Alyxia schlechteri var. salicifolia

Alyxia schlechteri var. salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved alyxia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are typically alternate and lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
