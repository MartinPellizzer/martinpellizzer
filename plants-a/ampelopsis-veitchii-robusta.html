Ampelopsis veitchii robusta

Ampelopsis veitchii robusta, commonly known as the robust grape ivy, is a vigorous climbing vine native to China.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly pubescent underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by blue-black berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its rapid growth, it is often used for covering trellises or as a ground cover in gardens.
