Arachis pflugeae

Arachis pflugeae, commonly known as the Pfluge's groundnut, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential agricultural interest, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
