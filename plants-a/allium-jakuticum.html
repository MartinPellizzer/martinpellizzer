Allium jakuticum

Allium jakuticum, commonly known as the Siberian leek, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, grass-like leaves. The plant produces tall, slender flower stalks that bear small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor, often used in traditional cuisines.

Allium jakuticum is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cold temperatures.
