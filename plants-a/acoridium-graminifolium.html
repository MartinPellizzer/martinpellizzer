Acoridium graminifolium

Acoridium graminifolium, commonly known as the grass-leaf arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and inflorescence. The plant features a rhizomatous root system that stores starch, making it valuable for culinary use. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are small and clustered in a spadix.

Acoridium graminifolium is often found in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
