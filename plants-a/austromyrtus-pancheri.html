Austromyrtus pancheri

Austromyrtus pancheri, commonly known as the Tasmanian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Tasmania.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. It produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

Austromyrtus pancheri is adapted to moist, cool climates and is often found in forest understories.
