Acer palmatum f. atropurpureum

Acer palmatum f. atropurpureum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with purple leaves, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil. Its ornamental value comes primarily from its foliage, which displays a rich purple hue during the growing season.

It is a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various climates.
