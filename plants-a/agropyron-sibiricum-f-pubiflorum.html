Agropyron sibiricum f. pubiflorum

Agropyron sibiricum f. pubiflorum, commonly known as the Siberian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, floret-bearing branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
