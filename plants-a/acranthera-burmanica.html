Acranthera burmanica

Acranthera burmanica, commonly known as Burman's acranthera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy underside.

The flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are usually pink or purple and attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
