Anemone gredensis

Anemone gredensis, commonly known as the Gredensan anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple petals that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its leaves are basal, palmately lobed, and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Anemone gredensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
