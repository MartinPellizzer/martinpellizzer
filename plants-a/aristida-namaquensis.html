Aristida namaquensis

Aristida namaquensis, commonly known as theNamaqua needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its delicate appearance.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
