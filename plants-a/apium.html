Apium

Apium, commonly known as parsley, is a flowering plant in the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves and small, clustered white or yellow flowers. The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and north Africa, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its roots and seeds are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Apium is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often cultivated in gardens and farms.
