Asplenium adiantum-nigrum subsp. woronowii

Asplenium adiantum-nigrum subsp.

woronowii, commonly known as the black spleenwort, is a fern species belonging to the family Aspleniaceae. It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that are typically dark green with a glossy appearance.

The plant produces spores in clusters called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found growing on rocks or tree trunks in temperate regions.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
