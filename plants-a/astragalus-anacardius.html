Astragalus anacardius

Astragalus anacardius, commonly known as the heartleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Astragalus anacardius plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
