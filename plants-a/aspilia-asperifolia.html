Aspilia asperifolia

Aspilia asperifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its unique floral structures and milky sap. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in stiff, rough hairs, giving it its common name. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color.

Aspilia asperifolia is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
