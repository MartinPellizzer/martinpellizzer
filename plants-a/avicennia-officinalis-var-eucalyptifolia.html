Avicennia officinalis var. eucalyptifolia

Avicennia officinalis var. eucalyptifolia, commonly known as the mangrove apple or sea apple, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline environments.

It possesses fibrous roots that help in stabilizing coastal soils and filtering out salt. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive aromatic scent.

The fruit is a fleshy, red berry that provides food for various wildlife species.
