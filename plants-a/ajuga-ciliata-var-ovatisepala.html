Ajuga ciliata var. ovatisepala

Ajuga ciliata var. ovatisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled bugleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with ciliate margins. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive, glossy appearance.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments, making it suitable for woodland gardens.
