Amaranthus blitum subsp. blitum

Amaranthus blitum subsp.

blitum, commonly known as redroot amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive red stems, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into three-angled, ribbed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
