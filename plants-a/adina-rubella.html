Adina rubella

Adina rubella, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red adina, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or red in color, which bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

Adina rubella is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
