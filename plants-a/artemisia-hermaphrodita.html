Artemisia hermaphrodita

Artemisia hermaphrodita, commonly known as mugwort, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides. Mugwort has finely divided leaves and produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for centuries.
