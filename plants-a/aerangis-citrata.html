Aerangis citrata

Aerangis citrata, commonly known as the Citron Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that resemble citrus fruits, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often with a waxy texture.

Aerangis citrata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
