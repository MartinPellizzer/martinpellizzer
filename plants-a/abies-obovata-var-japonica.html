Abies obovata var. japonica

Abies obovata var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are slightly twisted. The tree produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. Its bark is smooth and grayish-white, becoming fissured with age.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant form and cold-hardy nature.
