Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. fennica

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

fennica, commonly known as the Finnish woundwort, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple.

The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as tundra, heathland, and mountain slopes.
