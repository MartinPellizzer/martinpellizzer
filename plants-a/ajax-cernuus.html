Ajax cernuus

Ajax cernuus, commonly known as the drooping ajax, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in shades of red and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

Ajax cernuus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as birds and bees.
