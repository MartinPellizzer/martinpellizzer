Astragalus monspessulanus subsp. monspessulanus

Astragalus monspessulanus subsp.

monspessulanus, commonly known as the Mediterranean milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, arid environments. It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

This subspecies is often found in Mediterranean climates and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
