Aralia racemosa var. occidentalis

Aralia racemosa var. occidentalis, commonly known as western aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with palmate lobes that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in upright clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and unique growth habit.
