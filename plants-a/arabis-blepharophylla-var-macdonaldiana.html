Arabis blepharophylla var. macdonaldiana

Arabis blepharophylla var. macdonaldiana, commonly known as the Macdonald's rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its rosette-forming structure. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, and its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in alpine gardens.
