Argentina stenophylla var. emergens

Argentina stenophylla var. emergens, commonly known as the Emerald Forest fern, is a striking member of the Blechnaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length. The plant features a robust, creeping rhizome that allows it to spread and form dense mats. Its fronds are covered with fine, silky hairs, giving them a soft, almost velvety texture.

This species is native to the subtropical regions of Argentina and thrives in moist, shaded environments.
