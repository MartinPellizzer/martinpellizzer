Agathaea scabrida

Agathaea scabrida, commonly known as the rough agathis, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its rough, bark-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by oval-shaped berries. It is valued for its aromatic wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
