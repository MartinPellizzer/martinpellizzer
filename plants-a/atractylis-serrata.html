Atractylis serrata

Atractylis serrata, commonly known as the serrated cowwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. The plant features narrow, serrated leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Atractylis serrata is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
