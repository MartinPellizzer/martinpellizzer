Astracantha ptilocephala

Astracantha ptilocephala, commonly known as the woolly-headed thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical stems and leaves covered in dense, woolly hairs. The plant produces purple to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
