Astragalus purshii var. incurvus

Astragalus purshii var. incurvus, commonly known as the bentleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

It is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
