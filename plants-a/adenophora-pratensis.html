Adenophora pratensis

Adenophora pratensis, commonly known as meadow bellflower, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped blue to purple flowers in late summer. Its flowers are clustered in umbels, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Adenophora pratensis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
