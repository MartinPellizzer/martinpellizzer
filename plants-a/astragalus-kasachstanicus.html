Astragalus kasachstanicus

Astragalus kasachstanicus, commonly known as the Kazakhstan milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showing adaptations to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or pale purple, and it produces small, lens-shaped seeds.

Astragalus kasachstanicus is often found in steppe and rocky habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native range.
