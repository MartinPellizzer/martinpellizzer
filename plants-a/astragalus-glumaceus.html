Astragalus glumaceus

Astragalus glumaceus, commonly known as the glume milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its seed pods are glabrous and contain several small, rounded seeds.

Astragalus glumaceus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local insect and wildlife populations.
