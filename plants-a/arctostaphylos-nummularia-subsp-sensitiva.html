Arctostaphylos nummularia subsp. sensitiva

Arctostaphylos nummularia subsp.

sensitiva, commonly known as the sensitive madrone, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States. It features broad, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a distinctive waxy coating.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, red-orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

Its unique leaf structure helps it retain moisture and resist drought, making it well-suited to its native habitat.
