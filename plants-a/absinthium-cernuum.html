Absinthium cernuum

Absinthium cernuum, commonly known as nodding wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that droop downward, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a strong, bitter aroma.

Absinthium cernuum is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade.
