Aconitum napellus var. boreale

Aconitum napellus var. boreale, commonly known as the alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound, pinnately arranged leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are typically erect and covered in coarse hairs. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
