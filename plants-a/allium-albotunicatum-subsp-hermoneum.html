Allium albotunicatum subsp. hermoneum

Allium albotunicatum subsp.

hermoneum, commonly known as the white tunic onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive white, papery sheath that encases the bulb, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
