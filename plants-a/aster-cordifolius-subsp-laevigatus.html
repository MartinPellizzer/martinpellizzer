Aster cordifolius subsp. laevigatus

Aster cordifolius subsp.

laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth-leaved aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are smooth and velvety in texture. Its showy blue to purple flower heads appear in the late summer and early fall.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinct leaf morphology and is often found in association with other native wildflowers.
