Acacia crenulata

Acacia crenulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy soils.

Acacia crenulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
