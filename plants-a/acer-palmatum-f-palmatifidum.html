Acer palmatum f. palmatifidum

Acer palmatum f. palmatifidum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with deeply cut leaves, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, lace-like foliage that turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its delicate leaves make it a popular choice for smaller spaces and container gardening.
