Anthericum capillatum

Anthericum capillatum, commonly known as the slender adder's tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale green to white spore-bearing structures called sporangia. The plant is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which resembles a tongue and is often hidden among the foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Anthericum capillatum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
