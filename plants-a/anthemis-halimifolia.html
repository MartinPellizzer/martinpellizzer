Anthemis halimifolia

Anthemis halimifolia, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant is notable for its aromatic, fragrant foliage, which emits a subtle, sweet scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its flowers bloom in shades of yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
