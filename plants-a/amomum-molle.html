Amomum molle

Amomum molle, commonly known as small cardamom or Chinese cardamom, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. The berries contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both culinary and medicinal applications.
