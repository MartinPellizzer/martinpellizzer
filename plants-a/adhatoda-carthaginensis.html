Adhatoda carthaginensis

Adhatoda carthaginensis, commonly known as the Indian Trumpet or Carthaginian's Trumpet, is a flowering plant native to India and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet, with five petals and a prominent spur. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
