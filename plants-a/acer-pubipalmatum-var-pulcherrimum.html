Acer pubipalmatum var. pulcherrimum

Acer pubipalmatum var. pulcherrimum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant thrives in partial to full shade and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its ornamental value lies in its striking foliage and elegant, branching structure.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal color changes.
