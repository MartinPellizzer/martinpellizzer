Astragalus amphioxys var. amphioxys

Astragalus amphioxys var. amphioxys, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, sagebrush steppe, and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from yellow to reddish-purple. Its compound leaves are pinnately arranged with small, oval leaflets.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
