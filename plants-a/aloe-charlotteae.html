Aloe charlotteae

Aloe charlotteae, commonly known as Charlotte's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, spiky leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically thick and have a serrated edge, with a pale green coloration.

Aloe charlotteae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
