Amorphophallus gracilis

Amorphophallus gracilis, commonly known as the slender giant arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that resembles a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces a tuberous root system that serves as a storage organ for nutrients. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible tubers, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
