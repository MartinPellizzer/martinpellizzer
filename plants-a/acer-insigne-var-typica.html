Acer insigne var. typica

Acer insigne var. typica, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-bark that peels in thin, papery layers, and its showy, white to pinkish flowers that appear in early spring. The tree produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and turn vibrant shades of orange and red in the fall.

It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
