Arabis covillei

Arabis covillei, commonly known as Coville's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops. This species typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.
