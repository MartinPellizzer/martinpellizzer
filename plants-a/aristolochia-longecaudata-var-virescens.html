Aristolochia longecaudata var. virescens

Aristolochia longecaudata var. virescens, commonly known as the green-veined Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that mature to a dark purple color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The species is notable for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
