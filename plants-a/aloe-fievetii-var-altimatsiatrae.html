Aloe fievetii var. altimatsiatrae

Aloe fievetii var. altimatsiatrae, commonly known as the highland aloe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to store water. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
