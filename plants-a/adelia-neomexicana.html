Adelia neomexicana

Adelia neomexicana, commonly known as New Mexico adelia, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Adelia neomexicana is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
