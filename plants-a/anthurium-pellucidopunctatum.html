Anthurium pellucidopunctatum

Anthurium pellucidopunctatum, commonly known as the translucent-spotted anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the humid forests of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with a unique pattern of translucent spots. The plant produces vibrant, spatulate-shaped flowers that are typically red or orange with a yellow spathe. It thrives in warm, moist environments with indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
