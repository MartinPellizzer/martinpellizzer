Acacia alata var. alata

Acacia alata var. alata, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its narrow, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, paired leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots and a slow growth rate.

Acacia alata var. alata is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and forage.
