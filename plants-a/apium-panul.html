Apium panul

Apium panul, commonly known as the clustered celery or wild celery, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like carrots and parsley. The plant features slender, hollow stems and small, clustered umbels of white or greenish flowers. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine.

Apium panul is typically found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
