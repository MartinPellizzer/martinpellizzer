Alsophila tenerifrons

Alsophila tenerifrons, commonly known as the Canary Islands tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a distinctive crown of pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas with nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
