Adiantum guilelmi

Adiantum guilelmi, commonly known as the royal fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are finely divided. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Adiantum guilelmi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
