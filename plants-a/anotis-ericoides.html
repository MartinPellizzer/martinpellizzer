Anotis ericoides

Anotis ericoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically green or reddish in color, and it produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Anotis ericoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense, hardy ground cover.
