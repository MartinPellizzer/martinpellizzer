Anisostichus crucigera

Anisostichus crucigera, commonly known as the golden aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and meadows, thriving in well-drained soils. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
