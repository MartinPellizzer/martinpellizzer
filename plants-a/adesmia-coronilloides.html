Adesmia coronilloides

Adesmia coronilloides, commonly known as the silverleaf burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The seeds are enclosed in hairy, woody pods that mature and disperse in dry conditions.
