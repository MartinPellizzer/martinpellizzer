Avenochloa albinervis subsp. occidentalis

Avenochloa albinervis subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western slender grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive white venation.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
