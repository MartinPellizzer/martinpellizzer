Avenella iberica

Avenella iberica, commonly known as the Iberian avenelle, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, often covered in fine hairs.

Avenella iberica produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
