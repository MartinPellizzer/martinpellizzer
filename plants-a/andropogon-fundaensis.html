Andropogon fundaensis

Andropogon fundaensis, commonly known as the Florida panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stalks that bear small, spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
