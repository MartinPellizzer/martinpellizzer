Aponogeton stachyosporus

Aponogeton stachyosporus, commonly known as the blackroot water clover, is a rare aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black rhizomes and its ability to produce small, delicate flowers. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. Its leaves are typically submerged, while its flowers emerge above the water surface.

Aponogeton stachyosporus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in aquatic ecosystems.
