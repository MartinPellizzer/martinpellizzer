Adelobotrys linearifolius

Adelobotrys linearifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved adelobotrys, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small shrub characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Its botanical features include woody stems and a compact growth habit, making it well adapted to its native habitat.

It is often found in moist, acidic soils in woodland or swampy areas.
