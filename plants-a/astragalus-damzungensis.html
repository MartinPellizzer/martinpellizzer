Astragalus damzungensis

Astragalus damzungensis, commonly known as the Damxung milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets that help reduce water loss, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus damzungensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
