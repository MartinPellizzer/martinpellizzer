Allium xanthicum var. chloranthum

Allium xanthicum var. chloranthum, commonly known as the yellow garlic or golden garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered bulbs underground. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique color, it is a sought-after plant among gardeners interested in cultivating rare and beautiful flora.
