Acer oblongum var. omeiense

Acer oblongum var. omeiense, commonly known as the Omei maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
