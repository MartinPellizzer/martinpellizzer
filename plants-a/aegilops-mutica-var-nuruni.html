Aegilops mutica var. nuruni

Aegilops mutica var. nuruni, commonly known as the naked wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has reduced glumes, giving it a "naked" appearance, which is a distinguishing feature of the species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is of interest in agricultural research due to its potential for forage and its genetic contributions to wheat improvement.
