Appunia brachycalyx

Appunia brachycalyx, commonly known as the short-calyx daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers bloom in spring, and the leaves are narrow and hairy.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
