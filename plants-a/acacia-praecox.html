Acacia praecox

Acacia praecox, commonly known as the early acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow, spherical flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that split open when mature.

Acacia praecox is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
