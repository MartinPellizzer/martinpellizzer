Astragalus candidissimus

Astragalus candidissimus, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Astragalus candidissimus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
