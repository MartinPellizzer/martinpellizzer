Asclepias incarnata var. longifolia

Asclepias incarnata var. longifolia, commonly known as swamp milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern and central United States.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting a variety of pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is particularly important as a host plant for monarch butterfly larvae.
