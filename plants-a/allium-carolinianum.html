Allium carolinianum

Allium carolinianum, commonly known as the Carolina garlic.

It is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States, characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a tall, leafy stem topped with a spherical cluster of small, white to pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are edible, with a mild garlic flavor, and are often used in culinary applications.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable habitats.
