Acacia perryi

Acacia perryi, commonly known as the Perry's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of leaves. Acacia perryi is well adapted to dry environments, with deep roots that help it access water from underground sources.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
