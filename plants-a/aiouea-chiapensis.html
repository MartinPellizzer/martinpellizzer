Aiouea chiapensis

Aiouea chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapan whitebeard tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America, particularly Mexico and Guatemala.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white flowers. The plant has a dense canopy and can grow up to 30 meters tall, making it a significant component of the rainforest ecosystem.

Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain seeds, and it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
