Aira flexuosa var. barstii

Aira flexuosa var. barstii, commonly known as the Barstow grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh conditions.
