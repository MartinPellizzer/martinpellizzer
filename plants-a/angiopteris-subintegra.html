Angiopteris subintegra

Angiopteris subintegra, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a complex life cycle involving both a sporophyte and a gametophyte stage. Its fronds are composed of tightly packed pinnae, which are arranged in a distinctive, symmetrical pattern.

Angiopteris subintegra thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
