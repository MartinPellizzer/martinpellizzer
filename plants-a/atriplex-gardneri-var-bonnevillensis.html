Atriplex gardneri var. bonnevillensis

Atriplex gardneri var. bonnevillensis, commonly known as the Bonneville saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, typical of the Amaranthaceae family. It thrives in saline soils and is often found in salt deserts and along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for wildlife in its native range.
