Amoria raddeana

Amoria raddeana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces clusters of tiny blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for wetland restoration projects.

Amoria raddeana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
