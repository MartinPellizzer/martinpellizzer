Abies magnifica

Abies magnifica, commonly known as the whitebark pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive bark, which is thick, scaly, and often white or gray in color. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain edible seeds, which are an important food source for wildlife. Its needles are dark green and grow in clusters of five, and it is well adapted to high-altitude environments.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in forest ecosystems and its ornamental appeal in landscaping.
