Albuca deaconii

Albuca deaconii, commonly known as the Deacon's albuca, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round bulbs that are used for propagation. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Albuca deaconii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
