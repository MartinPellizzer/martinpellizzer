Asyneuma linifolium subsp. podanthoides

Asyneuma linifolium subsp.

podanthoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, contributing to its role in local pollination networks. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
