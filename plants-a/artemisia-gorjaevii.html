Artemisia gorjaevii

Artemisia gorjaevii, commonly known as the Siberian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or steppe regions.

Its essential oils have been used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and digestive properties.
