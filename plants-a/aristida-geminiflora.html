Aristida geminiflora

Aristida geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered grass, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to brown, and it blooms during the summer months.

Aristida geminiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in desert landscapes.
