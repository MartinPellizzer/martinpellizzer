Aconitum japonicum var. iyariense

Aconitum japonicum var. iyariense, commonly known as the Japanese monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and heart-shaped, with a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
