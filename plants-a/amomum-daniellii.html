Amomum daniellii

Amomum daniellii, commonly known as the Chinese cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their warming and digestive properties.

Amomum daniellii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is cultivated for both its medicinal and culinary applications.
