Anemia lindsaeoides

Anemia lindsaeoides, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and endangered epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves, relying instead on its roots for photosynthesis. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to survive.

Due to habitat destruction and over-collection, it is critically endangered in the wild.
