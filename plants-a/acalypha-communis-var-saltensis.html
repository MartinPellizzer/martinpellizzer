Acalypha communis var. saltensis

Acalypha communis var. saltensis, commonly known as Saltgrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically appear in spikes. The plant is adapted to thrive in saline and drought-prone soils, making it a hardy species in challenging environments. Its growth habit is usually low and spreading, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small wildlife.
