Astragalus facetus

Astragalus facetus, commonly known as the smooth milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Astragalus facetus is known for its small, rounded seeds and its ability to thrive in arid environments.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
