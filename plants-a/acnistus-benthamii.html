Acnistus benthamii

Acnistus benthamii, commonly known as the bentham's acnistus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Acnistus benthamii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
