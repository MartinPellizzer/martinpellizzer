Aster laevis var. amplifolius

Aster laevis var. amplifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are typically broad and smooth, giving it its varietal name "amplifolius.". Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in the late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wet meadows.
