Apocynum pulchellum

Apocynum pulchellum, commonly known as showy milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds. It is part of the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweed species.

Apocynum pulchellum is often found in moist woodlands and is an important host plant for certain butterfly species.
