Amaryllis josephiniana

Amaryllis josephiniana, commonly known as the Josephine amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with contrasting green or yellow markings. The plant typically blooms in late spring or early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are broad and dark green, emerging from a bulbous base.

Amaryllis josephiniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively low maintenance requirements.
