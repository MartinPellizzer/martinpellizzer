Agapetes setigera var. verticillata

Agapetes setigera var. verticillata, commonly known as the clustered silverleaf, is a rare and delicate shrub native to the cloud forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are densely covered with silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, clustered in groups of three to five. It thrives in cool, moist environments with high humidity and partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
