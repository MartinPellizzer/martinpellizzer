Allophylus villosus f. aporeticus

Allophylus villosus f. aporeticus, commonly known as the hairy sycamore fig, is a botanical variant of the species Allophylus villosus.

It is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves and small, clustered fruits that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and ecological benefits.
