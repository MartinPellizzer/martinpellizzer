Apocynum orixense

Apocynum orixense, commonly known as the Mexican dogbane, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

Apocynum orixense contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
