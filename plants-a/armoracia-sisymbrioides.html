Armoracia sisymbrioides

Armoracia sisymbrioides, commonly known as the Siberian horseradish, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in racemes, and it is known for its strong, pungent odor.
