Astragalus lasiocalyx

Astragalus lasiocalyx, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces slender, leguminous pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Astragalus lasiocalyx plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
