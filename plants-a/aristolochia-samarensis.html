Aristolochia samarensis

Aristolochia samarensis, commonly known as the Samarensis Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pipe-like flowers that are often dark purple or brown in color. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a bright red, containing seeds enclosed in a fleshy aril. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
