Arnica amplexicaulis var. prima

Arnica amplexicaulis var. prima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers in the summer. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its medicinal properties.

Due to its habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
