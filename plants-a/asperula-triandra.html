Asperula triandra

Asperula triandra, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a flowering plant native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring with shades of pink, red, and white, making it a popular ornamental species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, often growing in woodland environments.

Asperula triandra is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
