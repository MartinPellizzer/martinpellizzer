Antennaria howellii subsp. petaloidea

Antennaria howellii subsp.

petaloidea, commonly known as the pink sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States. It features a mat-forming growth habit with hairy stems and small, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinctive petal-like bracts, which give it its common name. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
