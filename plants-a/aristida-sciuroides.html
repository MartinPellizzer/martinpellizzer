Aristida sciuroides

Aristida sciuroides, commonly known as silverleaf threeawn, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are covered in silky, silver hairs, giving it its common name. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray appearance due to the dense covering of fine hairs.

Silverleaf threeawn is adapted to arid environments and is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
