Achyranthes calceolata

Achyranthes calceolata, commonly known as the red balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or forest understory environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
