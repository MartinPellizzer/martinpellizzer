Artemisia lactiflora f. septemlobata

Artemisia lactiflora f. septemlobata, commonly known as the seven-lobed mugwort, is a variant of the common mugwort species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray foliage with a distinctive seven-lobed structure on its leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed areas.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
