Aconitum pyramidale

Aconitum pyramidale, commonly known as the Pyramidal Monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and prominent veins. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum pyramidale is part of the Ranunculaceae family and contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
