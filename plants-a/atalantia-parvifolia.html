Atalantia parvifolia

Atalantia parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf citrus or lemonade tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and emit a subtle citrus scent. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright orange, edible fruits resembling small lemons. Its fruit contains a high concentration of citric acid, making it a valued species in traditional medicine and culinary uses.

Due to its ornamental value and unique characteristics, it is often cultivated in warm climates as a decorative and functional plant.
