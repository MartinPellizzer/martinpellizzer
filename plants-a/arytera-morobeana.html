Arytera morobeana

Arytera morobeana, commonly known as the Morobe anemone, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warm season. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in various horticultural settings.
