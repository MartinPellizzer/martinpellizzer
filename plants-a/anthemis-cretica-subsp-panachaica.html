Anthemis cretica subsp. panachaica

Anthemis cretica subsp.

panachaica, commonly known as the Greek chamomile, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, branched stems and hairy, linear leaves that are often aromatic.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers with a central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both traditional medicine and horticultural use.
