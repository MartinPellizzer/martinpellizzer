Aechmea hamata

Aechmea hamata, commonly known as the pink pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater to support its growth. The leaves are typically broad, stiff, and covered in a dense layer of trichomes, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces showy inflorescences with colorful bracts that range from red to pink, often resembling a pineapple in appearance.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Morphology

Aechmea hamata has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, elliptical, and often have a waxy coating, arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem.

The leaves are typically dark green with a reddish or purplish hue along the margins, and they can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant has a short, stout stem that is often hidden beneath the leaves, with a smooth, light green bark. It produces striking inflorescences with bright red or orange flowers that bloom in the summer, relying on wind pollination.

The fruit is a capsule that disperses seeds through wind, and the plant has a fibrous root system that helps anchor it in its native habitat.
