Antennaria gormanii

Antennaria gormanii, commonly known as the Gorman's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features small, hairy leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with ray florets. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Antennaria gormanii is adapted to cold climates and is an important part of its native ecosystem.
