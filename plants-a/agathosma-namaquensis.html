Agathosma namaquensis

Agathosma namaquensis, commonly known as the Namaqua dagga, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

Due to its unique properties, it has been used traditionally for its medicinal and aromatic qualities.
