Anthericum liliago f. brachylepis

Anthericum liliago f. brachylepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lily of the valley, is a variant of the lily of the valley plant characterized by its narrower, more elongated leaves.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant features clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense ground cover.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrance, though it contains toxic compounds in its leaves and berries.
