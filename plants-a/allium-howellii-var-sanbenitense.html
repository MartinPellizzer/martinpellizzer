Allium howellii var. sanbenitense

Allium howellii var. sanbenitense, commonly known as the San Benito garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its aromatic bulbs, which are used in traditional culinary and medicinal practices. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
