Arabis permixta

Arabis permixta, commonly known as the mixed rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, showy white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers cool, moist environments. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Arabis permixta is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
