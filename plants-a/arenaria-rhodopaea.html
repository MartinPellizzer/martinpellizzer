Arenaria rhodopaea

Arenaria rhodopaea, commonly known as the pink sandplant, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It thrives in sandy and rocky coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, saline conditions. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Arenaria rhodopaea plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and preventing erosion.
