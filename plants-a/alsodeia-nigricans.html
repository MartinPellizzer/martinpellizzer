Alsodeia nigricans

Alsodeia nigricans, commonly known as blackstem alsike, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, dark brown pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Alsodeia nigricans is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
