Acacia argentina

Acacia argentina, commonly known as the Argentine acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system. Acacia argentina is also notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Its wood and foliage have been used traditionally for various purposes, including fuel and medicinal applications.
