Astragalus roborovskyi

Astragalus roborovskyi, commonly known as the Roborovsky milkvetch, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has small, spherical seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across its natural habitat.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
