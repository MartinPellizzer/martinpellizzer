Achillea albicaulis

Achillea albicaulis, commonly known as white yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It is perennial, with a compact growth habit and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
