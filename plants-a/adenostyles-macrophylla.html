Adenostyles macrophylla

Adenostyles macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf mountain-mahonia, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in terminal panicles. These flowers are followed by red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for wildlife.

Adenostyles macrophylla thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
