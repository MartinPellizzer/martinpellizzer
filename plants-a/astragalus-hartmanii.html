Astragalus hartmanii

Astragalus hartmanii, commonly known as Hartman's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, yellow to cream-colored blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Astragalus hartmanii is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
