Acacia lunata var. crassiuscula

Acacia lunata var. crassiuscula, commonly known as the broad-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces cylindrical pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

This variety is closely related to other acacia species but is distinguished by its broader leaves and compact growth habit.
