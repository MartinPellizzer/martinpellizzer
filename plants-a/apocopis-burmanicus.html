Apocopis burmanicus

Apocopis burmanicus, commonly known as the Burmese orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fragrant blooms that typically appear in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding environment.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Apocopis burmanicus is considered vulnerable and is often protected in conservation programs.
