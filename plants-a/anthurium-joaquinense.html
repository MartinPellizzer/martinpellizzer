Anthurium joaquinense

Anthurium joaquinense, commonly known as the Joaquin anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the undergrowth of tropical forests. Its foliage is dark green with a glossy texture, and the spathe can range from red to orange, depending on the variety.

Anthurium joaquinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
