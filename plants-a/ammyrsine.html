Ammyrsine

Ammyrsine, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, slender stalks rising from water. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, while its roots anchor it to the muddy bottom. Ammyrsine is known for its ability to purify water and provide habitat for various aquatic life.

Its cultural and spiritual significance is deeply rooted in many Asian traditions.
