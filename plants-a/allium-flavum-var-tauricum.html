Allium flavum var. tauricum

Allium flavum var. tauricum, commonly known as the Tauric leek, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features grass-like, hollow stems that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a loose umbel. Its leaves are narrow and slightly aromatic, often used in local cuisines.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and culinary potential.
