Atecosa

Atecosa, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the genus Tithonia in the family Asteraceae, characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flowers. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to 3 meters in height. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its flowers are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
