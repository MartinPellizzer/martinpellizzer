Anisantha sterilis var. stenantha

Anisantha sterilis var. stenantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curled at the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often overlooked due to their modest size. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This species plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
