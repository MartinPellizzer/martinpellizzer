Argythamnia bahiensis

Argythamnia bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia burrobrush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of northeastern Brazil.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in late summer. It is adapted to harsh, dry conditions and has a deep root system for water retention.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for local fauna in its native environment.
