Allium gracillimum

Allium gracillimum, commonly known as the slender leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, cylindrical inflorescences with small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which contains sulfur compounds responsible for its distinctive odor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary potential, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
