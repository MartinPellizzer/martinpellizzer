Armeria vulgaris var. elongata

Armeria vulgaris var. elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed thrift, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ability to attract pollinators make it a popular choice in horticulture.
