Aechmea linharesiorum

Aechmea linharesiorum, commonly known as the Linhares' pineapple plant, is a bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces vibrant orange-red flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and indirect sunlight.

Its unique structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both gardens and indoor cultivation.

Morphology

Aechmea linharesiorum has rosette-shaped leaves that are sword-like, arranged in a tight spiral, with a dark green color and a waxy texture.

The plant has a short, thick stem with a smooth, dark brown bark that grows from the base of the rosette. Its flowers are clustered in a terminal inflorescence, with pinkish-red bracts and white to yellowish flowers that bloom during the summer months, pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that disperses seeds through wind and animal consumption.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in its natural habitat.
