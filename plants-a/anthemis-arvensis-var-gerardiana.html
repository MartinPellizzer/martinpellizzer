Anthemis arvensis var. gerardiana

Anthemis arvensis var. gerardiana, commonly known as Gerard's tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has a strong, aromatic scent and is known for its medicinal properties.

Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies.
