Astragalus inopinatus subsp. oreogenus

Astragalus inopinatus subsp.

oreogenus, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant has small, pinnately compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
