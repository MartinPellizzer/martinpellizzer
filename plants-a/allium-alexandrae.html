Allium alexandrae

Allium alexandrae, commonly known as the Alexandra onion, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Allium alexandrae is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary potential in native plant gardens.
