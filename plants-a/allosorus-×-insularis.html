Allosorus × insularis

Allosorus × insularis, commonly known as the Canary Island orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, adapting to the island's specific climate and soil conditions. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
