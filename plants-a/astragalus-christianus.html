Astragalus christianus

Astragalus christianus, commonly known as the Christian's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus christianus is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
