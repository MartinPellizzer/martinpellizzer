Alternanthera muscoides

Alternanthera muscoides, commonly known as the moss-like alternanthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves that resemble moss, giving it its common name. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for aquatic and bog gardens.
