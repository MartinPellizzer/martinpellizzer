Alopecurus pedatus

Alopecurus pedatus, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and woodland edges. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

Alopecurus pedatus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
