Ananassa sagenaria

Ananassa sagenaria, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers and red, fleshy fruits. The plant can grow up to 15 meters tall and has glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Its rapid growth and invasive tendencies make it a significant ecological concern in some regions.
