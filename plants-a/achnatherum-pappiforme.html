Achnatherum pappiforme

Achnatherum pappiforme, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs on the lemma and palea, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Achnatherum pappiforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
