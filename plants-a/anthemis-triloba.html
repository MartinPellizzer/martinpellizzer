Anthemis triloba

Anthemis triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and thistles.

Anthemis triloba is often used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
