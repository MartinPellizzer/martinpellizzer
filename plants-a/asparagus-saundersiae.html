Asparagus saundersiae

Asparagus saundersiae, commonly known as the Cape asparagus, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces delicate white or greenish-white flowers in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
