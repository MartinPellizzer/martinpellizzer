Astragalus demirizii

Astragalus demirizii, commonly known as the Demirizii milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
