Aspalathus crassisepala

Aspalathus crassisepala, commonly known as the broad-stalked bushveld pea, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, rounded leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy areas.

Aspalathus crassisepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
