Astragalus zemuensis

Astragalus zemuensis, commonly known as the Zemu milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Astragalus zemuensis is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its ecosystem.

Its limited distribution makes it a subject of interest for botanical research and conservation efforts.
