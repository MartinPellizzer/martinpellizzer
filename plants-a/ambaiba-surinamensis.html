Ambaiba surinamensis

Ambaiba surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam ambarella, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its small, yellow, egg-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The fruit is edible when ripe and has a sweet, tangy flavor, making it a valuable crop in certain regions.
