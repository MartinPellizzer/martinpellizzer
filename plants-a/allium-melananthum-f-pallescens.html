Allium melananthum f. pallescens

Allium melananthum f. pallescens, commonly known as the pale-flowered garlic.

It is a variant of the common garlic plant, characterized by its pale to whitish flowers. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces small, bulbous structures that are edible and similar to regular garlic in flavor.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its culinary uses.
