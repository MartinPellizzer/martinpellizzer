Agave filifera var. schidigera

Agave filifera var. schidigera, commonly known as the Schidiger's threadleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, thread-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. This variety is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It produces a tall flower stalk with yellow flowers, though it rarely blooms in cultivation.
