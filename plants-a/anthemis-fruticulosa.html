Anthemis fruticulosa

Anthemis fruticulosa, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing, perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Anthemis fruticulosa is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
