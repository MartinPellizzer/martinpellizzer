Acaena platyacantha

Acaena platyacantha, commonly known as the broad-leaved spinifex, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, oval leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of yellow flowers in spring. Its fruit is a dry, hairy capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acaena platyacantha is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland dunes.
