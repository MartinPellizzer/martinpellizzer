Aster brickellioides var. glabratus

Aster brickellioides var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth aster, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers during the late summer and early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
