Aristida divaricata

Aristida divaricata, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, erect culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of numerous small, spikelet-like structures.

Aristida divaricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
