Anthericum setiferum

Anthericum setiferum, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features a rosette of broad, hairy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The woolly bellflower is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in woodland gardens.
