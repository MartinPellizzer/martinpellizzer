Asphodelus monticola

Asphodelus monticola, commonly known as the mountain asphodel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. The plant produces tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of yellow or orange flowers. It is characterized by its ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.

Asphodelus monticola is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
