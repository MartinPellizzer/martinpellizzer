Adenia lanceolata subsp. lanceolata

Adenia lanceolata subsp.

lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf adenia, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. These flowers are followed by small, round, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
