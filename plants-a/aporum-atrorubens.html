Aporum atrorubens

Aporum atrorubens, commonly known as the red-black sedge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Its distinctive reddish-brown coloration makes it visually striking in its natural habitat.
