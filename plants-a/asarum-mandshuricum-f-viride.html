Asarum mandshuricum f. viride

Asarum mandshuricum f. viride, commonly known as the green-flowered wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its aromatic rhizomes have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
