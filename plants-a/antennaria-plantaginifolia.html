Antennaria plantaginifolia

Antennaria plantaginifolia, commonly known as the mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, hairy leaves that resemble mouse ears, and it produces delicate white or pink flowers in clusters. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or rocky areas. Its flowering period occurs in early spring, and it is often found in disturbed soils.

Antennaria plantaginifolia is a member of the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and ability to form dense mats.
