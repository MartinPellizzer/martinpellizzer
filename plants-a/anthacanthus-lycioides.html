Anthacanthus lycioides

Anthacanthus lycioides, commonly known as the common anthacanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are succulent and can store water.

Anthacanthus lycioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
