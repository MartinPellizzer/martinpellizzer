Arabis glastifolia

Arabis glastifolia, commonly known as the white rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers are small, white, and arranged in a raceme, and it produces small, rounded capsules as fruits.

Arabis glastifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
