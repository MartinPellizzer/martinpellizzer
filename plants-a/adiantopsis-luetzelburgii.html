Adiantopsis luetzelburgii

Adiantopsis luetzelburgii, commonly known as the Luetzelburg's maidenhair fern, is a rare and delicate species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae, which includes various ferns characterized by their finely divided, often delicate fronds. The plant is distinguished by its narrow, arching fronds that are typically covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in shaded, moist forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
