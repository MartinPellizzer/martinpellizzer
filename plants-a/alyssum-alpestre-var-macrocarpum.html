Alyssum alpestre var. macrocarpum

Alyssum alpestre var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the alpine alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant features hairy, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh, cold climates.
