Ateleia multijuga

Ateleia multijuga, commonly known as the African butterbean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
