Asperula woronowii

Asperula woronowii, commonly known as the Korean sweet pepper or mountain azalea, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in forested areas and mountainous regions. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and it has a compact growth habit.

Asperula woronowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
