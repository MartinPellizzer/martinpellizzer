Alchemilla cornucopioides var. algeriensis

Alchemilla cornucopioides var. algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, silver-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are borne on slender, branched stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Algerian lady's mantle is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
