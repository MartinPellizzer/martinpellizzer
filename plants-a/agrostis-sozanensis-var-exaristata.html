Agrostis sozanensis var. exaristata

Agrostis sozanensis var. exaristata, commonly known as the exaristate bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The leaves are typically narrow and covered with short, appressed hairs, giving them a distinctive texture. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers.

This variety is often found in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.
