Arabis ramosa

Arabis ramosa, commonly known as the clustered rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly succulent texture.

Arabis ramosa thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
