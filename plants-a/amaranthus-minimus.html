Amaranthus minimus

Amaranthus minimus, commonly known as the dwarf amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, branching stems and vibrant, often red or purple, bracts that surround its small flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, making it a compact and ornamental species. Its leaves are lance-shaped and can vary in color from green to reddish hues.

Amaranthus minimus is often cultivated as a garden plant for its decorative appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
