Achimenes incisa

Achimenes incisa, commonly known as the cut-leaf achimenes, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors including red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Achimenes incisa is typically grown as an annual or in greenhouse conditions due to its sensitivity to cold temperatures.
