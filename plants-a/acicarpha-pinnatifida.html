Acicarpha pinnatifida

Acicarpha pinnatifida, commonly known as the slender panic grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, open habitats.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
