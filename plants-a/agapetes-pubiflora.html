Agapetes pubiflora

Agapetes pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy-flowered agapete, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in countries like Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is typically found in cloud forests.

Agapetes pubiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
