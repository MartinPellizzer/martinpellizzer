Aspalathus florifera

Aspalathus florifera, commonly known as the sweet cycad, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and large, pinnate leaves that give it a distinct appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, umbrella-like inflorescences. Aspalathus florifera is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its slow growth and long lifespan make it a notable species in its native habitat.
