Acantholimon quinquelobum var. quinquelobum

Acantholimon quinquelobum var. quinquelobum, commonly known as the five-lobed sandwort, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and gray-green foliage with a woolly texture. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, dry conditions typical of its natural habitat.
