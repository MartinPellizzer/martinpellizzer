Astragalus inopinatus subsp. fujisanensis

Astragalus inopinatus subsp.

fujisanensis, commonly known as the Fujisan Astragalus, is a perennial herb native to Japan, specifically found on the slopes of Mount Fuji. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its botanical features include a hairy stem, small pod-like fruits, and a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and limited distribution.
