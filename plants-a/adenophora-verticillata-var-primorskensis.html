Adenophora verticillata var. primorskensis

Adenophora verticillata var. primorskensis, commonly known as the Primorsky bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Russian Far East.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variant is closely related to other members of the Adenophora genus, which are known for their ornamental value and ecological importance in their native habitats.
