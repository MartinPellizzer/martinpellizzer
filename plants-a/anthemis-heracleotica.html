Anthemis heracleotica

Anthemis heracleotica, commonly known as the yellow buttonweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, surrounded by bracts that resemble a crown. It has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed or toothed.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some regions.
