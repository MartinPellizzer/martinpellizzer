Allium sikkimense

Allium sikkimense, commonly known as the Sikkim garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spherical bulbs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
