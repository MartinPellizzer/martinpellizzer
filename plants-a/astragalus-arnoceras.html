Astragalus arnoceras

Astragalus arnoceras, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Astragalus arnoceras is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
