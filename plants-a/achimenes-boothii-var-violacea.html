Achimenes boothii var. violacea

Achimenes boothii var. violacea, commonly known as the violet-flowered achimenes, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple tubular flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features upright, branching stems covered with soft, hairy foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display in gardens and as a potted plant.
