Aria griffithii

Aria griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped blooms in shades of white and pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool climate to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
