Angraecum physophorum

Angraecum physophorum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and large, white or pale pink flowers that resemble stars. The plant produces a unique structure called a "physophore," which is a specialized appendage at the base of the flower that aids in pollination. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to bloom.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
