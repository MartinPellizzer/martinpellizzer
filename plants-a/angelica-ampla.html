Angelica ampla

Angelica ampla, commonly known as the giant angelica, is a tall biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring large, umbrella-shaped compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in an umbel, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, containing compounds that have been historically used for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Angelica ampla thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.
