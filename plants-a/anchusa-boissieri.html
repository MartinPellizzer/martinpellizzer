Anchusa boissieri

Anchusa boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Anchusa boissieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
