Acosta coerulescens

Acosta coerulescens, commonly known as the blue acosta, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or gravelly soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to the harsh conditions of its habitat.

Acosta coerulescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
