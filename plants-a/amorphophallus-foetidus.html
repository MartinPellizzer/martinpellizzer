Amorphophallus foetidus

Amorphophallus foetidus, commonly known as the stinking corpse flower, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped leaves and a distinctive, foul-smelling inflorescence that resembles a decaying body. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and produces a single, large spathe and spadix. It is known for its unique reproductive strategy, where the spadix generates heat to attract pollinators.

The plant is often cultivated for its striking appearance and its role in botanical research and conservation efforts.
