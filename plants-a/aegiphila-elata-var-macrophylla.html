Aegiphila elata var. macrophylla

Aegiphila elata var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in whorls, with a distinctive purple or violet coloration.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
