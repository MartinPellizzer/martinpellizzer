Ayenia scabrida

Ayenia scabrida, commonly known as the rough ayenia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and features narrow, hairy leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Ayenia scabrida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
