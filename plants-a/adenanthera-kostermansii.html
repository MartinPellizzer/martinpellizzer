Adenanthera kostermansii

Adenanthera kostermansii, commonly known as the African burr bean, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive burr-like seed pods. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, spiny pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

The seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein in some regions.
