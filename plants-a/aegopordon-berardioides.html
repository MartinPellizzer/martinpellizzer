Aegopordon berardioides

Aegopordon berardioides, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant has narrow, silvery-gray leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Aegopordon berardioides plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
