Alpinia argentea

Alpinia argentea, commonly known as the silver bamboo or white ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery-white pubescence. Its inflorescence consists of small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a spike, which can be quite ornamental.

Alpinia argentea is valued both for its aesthetic appeal and for its use in traditional medicine.
