Alchemilla alexandri

Alchemilla alexandri, commonly known as Alexandrian lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers in upright clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Alchemilla alexandri is valued for its ornamental foliage and historical use in herbal medicine.
