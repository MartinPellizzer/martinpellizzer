Anticharis glandulosa var. intermedia

Anticharis glandulosa var. intermedia, commonly known as the glandular anticharis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular-dotted, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in wetland and riparian ecosystems.
