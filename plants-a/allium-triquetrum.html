Allium triquetrum

Allium triquetrum, commonly known as the three-angled garlic, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive triangular-shaped bulbs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are grass-like and form a dense rosette near the ground.

Allium triquetrum is often found in moist, calcareous soils and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
