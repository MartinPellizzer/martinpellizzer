Acer negundo f. aureolimbatum

Acer negundo f. aureolimbatum, commonly known as the golden-winged maple, is a variant of the box elder tree characterized by its distinctive foliage.

The leaves are palmately lobed and exhibit a striking yellow margin that contrasts with the green central portion. This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value, particularly in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The plant is relatively fast-growing and can reach heights of up to 20 feet, making it a popular choice for shade trees and ornamental planting.
