Acacia bavazzanoi

Acacia bavazzanoi, commonly known as the Socotra acacia, is a unique plant native to the island of Socotra in the Arabian Sea.

It is characterized by its distinctive bottle-shaped trunk and feathery, bipinnate leaves that are adapted to the arid environment. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is a member of the legume family and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Due to its ecological significance and unique morphology, it is considered a valuable species for conservation efforts.
