Aulomyrcia elaeodendra

Aulomyrcia elaeodendra, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Aulomyrcia elaeodendra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and humid environments.
