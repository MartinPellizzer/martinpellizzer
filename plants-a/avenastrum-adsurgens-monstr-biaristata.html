Avenastrum adsurgens monstr. biaristata

Avenastrum adsurgens monstr.

biaristata, commonly known as the creeping tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flower heads that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
