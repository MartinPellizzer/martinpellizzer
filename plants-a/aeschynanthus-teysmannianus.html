Aeschynanthus teysmannianus

Aeschynanthus teysmannianus, commonly known as the Christmas ivy, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, which bloom in clusters during the winter months. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Aeschynanthus teysmannianus is also valued for its ability to purify the air and enhance indoor aesthetics.
