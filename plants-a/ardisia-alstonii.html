Ardisia alstonii

Ardisia alstonii, commonly known as the Alston's beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, elliptic leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by bright purple berries that are highly ornamental. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

The berries are a food source for various birds and wildlife, adding ecological value to the landscape.
