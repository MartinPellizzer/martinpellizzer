Athyrium chingianum

Athyrium chingianum, commonly known as the Chinese maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Athyrium chingianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
