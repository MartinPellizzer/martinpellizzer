Avena sempervirens

Avena sempervirens, commonly known as everlasting grass or silver grass, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and tall, slender inflorescences that resemble plumes. The plant belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its fibrous roots make it a useful plant for erosion control and habitat creation.

Avena sempervirens is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
