Arnottia imbellis

Arnottia imbellis, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Arnottia imbellis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
