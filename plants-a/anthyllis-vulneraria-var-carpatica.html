Anthyllis vulneraria var. carpatica

Anthyllis vulneraria var. carpatica, commonly known as Carpathian woundwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of many legumes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
