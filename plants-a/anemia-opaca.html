Anemia opaca

Anemia opaca, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a species of fern characterized by its pale, almost translucent fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant's leaves are typically slender and have a delicate, lace-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity to flourish.
