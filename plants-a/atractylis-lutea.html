Atractylis lutea

Atractylis lutea, commonly known as yellow marsh grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or damp environments such as marshes and riverbanks. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small yellow flowers are arranged in a spike-like inflorescence and bloom in late summer.

Atractylis lutea is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist soils and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
