Acacia uncifera

Acacia uncifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and narrow, linear leaves that reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, spherical clusters, which attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Acacia uncifera is well-suited to dry climates and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
