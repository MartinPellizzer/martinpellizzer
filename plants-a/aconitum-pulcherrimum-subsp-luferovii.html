Aconitum pulcherrimum subsp. luferovii

Aconitum pulcherrimum subsp.

luferovii, commonly known as the Siberian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features striking blue-violet flowers arranged in a spurred raceme, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
