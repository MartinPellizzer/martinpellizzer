Aster sedifolius var. subsquamosus

Aster sedifolius var. subsquamosus, commonly known as the squamosous aster, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate with a rough, scaly texture, and stems that are hairy and often branched. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads with white to pale blue florets, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woods or along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
