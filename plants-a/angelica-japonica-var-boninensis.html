Angelica japonica var. boninensis

Angelica japonica var. boninensis, commonly known as the Bonin angelica, is a flowering plant native to the Bonin Islands.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compound leaves and umbellate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near the coast. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It is also valued as a ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and fragrance.
