Avena puberula

Avena puberula, commonly known as slender oat, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets, which are often wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Avena puberula is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
