Avena strigosa subsp. agraria

Avena strigosa subsp.

agraria, commonly known as the common oat, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides. Avena strigosa subsp.

agraria is cultivated for its grains, which are used in food and animal feed.
