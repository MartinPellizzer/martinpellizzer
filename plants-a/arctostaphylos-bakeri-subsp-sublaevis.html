Arctostaphylos bakeri subsp. sublaevis

Arctostaphylos bakeri subsp.

sublaevis, commonly known as the smooth manzanita, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and northern Baja California. It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and evergreen leaves that are elliptical in shape.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters during the spring, which are typically pink to red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild, wet winters and warm, dry summers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
