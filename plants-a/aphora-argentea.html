Aphora argentea

Aphora argentea, commonly known as silver aphora, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, Aphora argentea is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
