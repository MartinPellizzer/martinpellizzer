Astragalus guatemalensis var. oaxacanus

Astragalus guatemalensis var. oaxacanus, commonly known as the Oaxacan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its ecological role includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil in its natural habitat.
