Amelanchier ovalis subsp. integrifolia

Amelanchier ovalis subsp.

integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America. It features compound leaves that are alternate and typically have smooth margins, distinguishing it from other subspecies.

The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open forests.
