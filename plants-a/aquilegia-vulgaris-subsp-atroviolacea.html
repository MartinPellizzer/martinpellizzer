Aquilegia vulgaris subsp. atroviolacea

Aquilegia vulgaris subsp.

atroviolacea, commonly known as the dark violet cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred petals that range in color from deep purple to dark violet.

The plant typically grows in woodland areas and moist soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces clusters of bright red or purple flowers in spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker floral hues compared to the more common *A. vulgaris*.
