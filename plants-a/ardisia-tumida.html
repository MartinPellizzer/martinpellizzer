Ardisia tumida

Ardisia tumida, commonly known as the glossy leaf ardisia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant is typically evergreen and thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its berries, which are red when mature, are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

Ardisia tumida is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate partial shade.
