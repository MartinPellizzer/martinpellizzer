Artemisia × perinii

Artemisia × perinii, commonly known as wormwood, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its silvery-green, aromatic foliage and small yellow flowers that appear in autumn. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America. Artemisia × perinii is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought tolerance and distinctive appearance.

It also has historical uses in medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
