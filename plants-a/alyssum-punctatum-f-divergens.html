Alyssum punctatum f. divergens

Alyssum punctatum f. divergens, commonly known as the variegated alyssum, is a flowering plant characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, fragrant flowers.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops and ornamental plants. The foliage is typically covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. This form is distinguished by its variegated leaves, which exhibit a mix of green and white or cream-colored patches.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and landscapes for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
