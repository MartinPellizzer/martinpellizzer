Artemisia genipi proles eriantha

Artemisia genipi proles eriantha, commonly known as the silverleaf artemisia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.

Its aromatic properties make it a valuable plant in traditional medicine and as a natural pest deterrent.
