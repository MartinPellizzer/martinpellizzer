Aconitum napellus var. hayekianum

Aconitum napellus var. hayekianum, commonly known as the Hayek's monkshood, is a variant of the European monkshood plant.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Caucasus. The plant is characterized by its striking blue to purple hood-shaped flowers, which are typical of the genus Aconitum. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive appearance, with a rough texture and a dark green color.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
