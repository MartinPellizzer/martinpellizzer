Artanthe lacunosa

Artanthe lacunosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved artanthe, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy margin. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Amaranthaceae, which includes many species with similar ecological and structural traits.
