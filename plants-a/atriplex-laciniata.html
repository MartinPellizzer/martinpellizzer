Atriplex laciniata

Atriplex laciniata, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in saline soils and is adapted to withstand high salinity and drought conditions.

Atriplex laciniata plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
