Adenodolichos grandifoliolatus

Adenodolichos grandifoliolatus, commonly known as the large-leaved cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are large and often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its pods contain edible seeds that are used in various cuisines, and it is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability in agricultural settings.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to diverse soil conditions and its role in sustainable farming practices.
