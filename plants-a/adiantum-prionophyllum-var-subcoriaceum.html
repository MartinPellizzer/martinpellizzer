Adiantum prionophyllum var. subcoriaceum

Adiantum prionophyllum var. subcoriaceum, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a species of fern characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to tropical regions of South America. The fronds are typically dark green and have a slightly velvety texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
