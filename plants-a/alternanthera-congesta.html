Alternanthera congesta

Alternanthera congesta, commonly known as the red-veined dock, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, deeply lobed leaves that often display vibrant red veins against a green or purple background. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for gardeners seeking unique foliage in aquatic or bog gardens.
