Acacia browniana var. intermedia

Acacia browniana var. intermedia, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a soft, silky appearance. The plant produces yellow or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Its woody pods contain seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
