Acalypha karsteniana

Acalypha karsteniana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or karsten's spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that range from red to purple, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Acalypha karsteniana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and unique appearance.
