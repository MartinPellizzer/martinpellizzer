Asplenium adiantum-nigrum nothosubsp. demirizii

Asplenium adiantum-nigrum nothosubsp.

demirizii, commonly known as the Turkish maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern native to Turkey. It is a small, evergreen fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green with a glossy texture.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.
