Acacia squamata

Acacia squamata, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grayish stems covered in fine, scale-like structures, which give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, typically in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Acacia squamata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
