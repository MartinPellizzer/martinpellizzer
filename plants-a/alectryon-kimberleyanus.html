Alectryon kimberleyanus

Alectryon kimberleyanus, commonly known as the Kimberley waxwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of Western Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, providing resistance to arid conditions. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by woody capsules that contain numerous seeds.

The species is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland scrublands.
