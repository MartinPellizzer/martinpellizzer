Acacia subglauca

Acacia subglauca, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its bark is typically smooth and greyish, with a waxy coating that helps it retain moisture.

Acacia subglauca is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in native Australian landscapes.
