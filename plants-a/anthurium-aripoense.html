Anthurium aripoense

Anthurium aripoense, commonly known as the Aripo arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored. The plant produces an inflorescence known as a spadix, which is surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Anthurium aripoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
