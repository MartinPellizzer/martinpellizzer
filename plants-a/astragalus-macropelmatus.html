Astragalus macropelmatus

Astragalus macropelmatus, commonly known as the large-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a deep taproot system, which allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, and it plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.

Astragalus macropelmatus is also valued for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small animals.
