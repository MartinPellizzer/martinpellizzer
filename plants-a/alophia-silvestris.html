Alophia silvestris

Alophia silvestris, commonly known as the wild lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking white or pale pink flowers with a distinct, often fragrant scent. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a notable wildflower in its native habitat.

Alophia silvestris is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegance.
