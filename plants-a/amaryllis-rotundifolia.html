Amaryllis rotundifolia

Amaryllis rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically blooms in the spring and summer months, producing clusters of flowers on tall, sturdy stems. It is often cultivated as a ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and ease of care.

Amaryllis rotundifolia prefers well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various growing conditions.
