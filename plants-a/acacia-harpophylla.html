Acacia harpophylla

Acacia harpophylla, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its distinctive, fern-like phyllodes that are typically pale green to grey-green in color. The plant produces yellow globular flowerheads that appear in late winter to early spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. It is well adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

The acacia has small, hard, ribbed pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.
