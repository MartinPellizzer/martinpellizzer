Affonsea lucyi

Affonsea lucyi, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Affonsea lucyi is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
