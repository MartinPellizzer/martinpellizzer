Annesorhiza villosa

Annesorhiza villosa, commonly known as the hairy tansy, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Annesorhiza villosa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
