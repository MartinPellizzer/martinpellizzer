Anthocercis albicans var. tomentosa

Anthocercis albicans var. tomentosa, commonly known as the white-flowered anthocercis, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its white, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring, often in clusters. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy, silver-green foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.
