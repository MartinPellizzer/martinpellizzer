Alchornea glandulosa var. parvifolia

Alchornea glandulosa var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved marsh mallow, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its glandular leaves, which are often covered in small, sticky dots. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in humid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Alchornea glandulosa var. parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
