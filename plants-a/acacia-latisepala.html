Acacia latisepala

Acacia latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepal acacia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in savanna and woodland habitats. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish to pale green flowers that appear in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
