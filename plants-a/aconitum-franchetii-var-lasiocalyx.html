Aconitum franchetii var. lasiocalyx

Aconitum franchetii var. lasiocalyx, commonly known as the Franchet's Monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features striking blue to purple hood-shaped flowers that are characteristic of the Aconitum genus. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in controlled environments.
