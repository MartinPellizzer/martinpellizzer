Allardia incana

Allardia incana, commonly known as the hoary bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Allardia incana is a perennial herb that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
