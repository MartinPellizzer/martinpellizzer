Astragalus humistratus var. crispulus

Astragalus humistratus var. crispulus, commonly known as the curlypod milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive crinkled or curled appearance, which is a key identifying characteristic. Its flowers are small, yellowish to greenish, and clustered in dense, cylindrical racemes.

The species is valued for its role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
