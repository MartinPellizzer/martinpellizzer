Aspilia vulgaris

Aspilia vulgaris, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Aspilia vulgaris is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms.
