Amphicarpaea deltifolia

Amphicarpaea deltifolia, commonly known as the slender false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant has a unique reproductive strategy, producing two types of fruits: one above ground and one below ground, a trait known as amphicarpy. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
