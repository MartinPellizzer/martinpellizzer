Alsine biflora f. laxior

Alsine biflora f. laxior, commonly known as the lax flowered sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and the stems are often prostrate or ascending.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in stabilizing sandy soils.
