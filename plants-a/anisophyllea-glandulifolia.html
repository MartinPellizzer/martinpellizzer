Anisophyllea glandulifolia

Anisophyllea glandulifolia, commonly known as the glandular-leafed anisophyllea, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a distinctive glandular texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or yellowish in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Anisophyllea glandulifolia is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental foliage.
