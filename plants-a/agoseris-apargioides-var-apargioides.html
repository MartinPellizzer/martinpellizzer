Agoseris apargioides var. apargioides

Agoseris apargioides var. apargioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The species is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
