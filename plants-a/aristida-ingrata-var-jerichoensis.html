Aristida ingrata var. jerichoensis

Aristida ingrata var. jerichoensis, commonly known as Jericho grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

This grass is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological resilience and ability to stabilize soil in disturbed areas.
