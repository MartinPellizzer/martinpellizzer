Anthemis pedunculata var. faurei

Anthemis pedunculata var. faurei, commonly known as the Faure's tansy, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
