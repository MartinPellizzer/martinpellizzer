Acer platanoides var. aureovariegatum

Acer platanoides var. aureovariegatum, commonly known as the golden-leaved Norway maple, is a deciduous tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, palmate leaves that display a striking yellow-green variegation pattern. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often planted in gardens and urban landscapes.
