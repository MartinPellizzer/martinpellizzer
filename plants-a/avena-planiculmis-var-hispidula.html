Avena planiculmis var. hispidula

Avena planiculmis var. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy oat, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture on both surfaces. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along streambanks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small vertebrates.
