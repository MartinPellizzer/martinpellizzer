Anthemis philistea

Anthemis philistea, commonly known as the Palestine tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, with a strong, camphor-like scent.

Anthemis philistea is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
