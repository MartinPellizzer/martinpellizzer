Acaena sanguisorbae var. sericeinitens

Acaena sanguisorbae var. sericeinitens, commonly known as the silky burr, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, silky-hairy leaves and small, red or brown burrs that contain seeds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The silky texture of its leaves and the distinctive appearance of its seed-bearing structures make it a notable species in its native habitat.
