Anthemis tranzscheliana

Anthemis tranzscheliana, commonly known as the Alpine chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like yellow flowers and linear, aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The essential oils extracted from its flowers are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine for their calming and antiseptic properties.
