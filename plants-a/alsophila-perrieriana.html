Alsophila perrieriana

Alsophila perrieriana, commonly known as the Perrier's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, providing structural support and enabling it to thrive in shaded, humid environments.

The fronds are typically dark green and can be used in traditional medicine by local communities.
