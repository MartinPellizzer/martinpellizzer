Amygdalus nana subsp. campestris

Amygdalus nana subsp.

campestris, commonly known as the dwarf peach or wild peach, is a small shrub or tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, white or pink flowers.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that typically ripens in late summer. This subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.
