Aster depauperatus

Aster depauperatus, commonly known as the poor aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, narrow leaves. Its flowers are usually pale blue to white, and it produces small, cylindrical fruits called achenes.

Aster depauperatus is often found in open areas such as grasslands and slopes, where it thrives in full sun.
