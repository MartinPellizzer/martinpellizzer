Agropyron pseudoagropyrum

Agropyron pseudoagropyrum, commonly known as the prairie wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, compact clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and steppe habitats.

Agropyron pseudoagropyrum is valued for its erosion control capabilities and as a forage plant for livestock.
