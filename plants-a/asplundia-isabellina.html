Asplundia isabellina

Asplundia isabellina, commonly known as the Isabelline arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by edible, starchy tubers. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped, hence the common name, and are often used in traditional medicine.

Asplundia isabellina is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
