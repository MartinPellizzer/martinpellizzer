Aristolochia torta

Aristolochia torta, commonly known as the twisted Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, serpentine-shaped leaves and a distinctive, hooded flower that resembles a pipe. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a slender, curved capsule that contains numerous seeds.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
