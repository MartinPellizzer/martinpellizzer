Astragalus rubens

Astragalus rubens, commonly known as red milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

Astragalus rubens is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various pollinators.
