Ariocarpus prismaticus var. maior

Ariocarpus prismaticus var. maior, commonly known as the Mexican sun cactus, is a succulent plant native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Mexico.

It is characterized by its ribbed, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 30 cm in height. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and slow growth rate make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
