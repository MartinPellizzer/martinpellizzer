Aspidosperma cylindrocarpon

Aspidosperma cylindrocarpon, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Cerbera odollam*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, white to pink flowers. The tree produces small, red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a gelatinous pulp. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential pharmacological properties.

However, its seeds contain toxic alkaloids, making them dangerous if ingested.
