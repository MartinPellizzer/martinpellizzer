Aloe fievetii subsp. fievetii

Aloe fievetii subsp.

fievetii, commonly known as the Karoo aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a blue-gray tint.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Aloe fievetii subsp.

fievetii is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
