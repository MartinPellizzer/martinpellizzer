Arenaria polytrichoides var. densissima

Arenaria polytrichoides var. densissima, commonly known as the dense hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and sandy soils in arid regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including drought and poor soil, making it a hardy member of the Caryophyllaceae family.

Its compact form and resilience make it a valuable plant for stabilizing soil in rocky or coastal habitats.
