Albuca crudenii

Albuca crudenii, commonly known as the Cruden's albuca, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a bulbous perennial plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used in horticulture for propagation. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Albuca crudenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
