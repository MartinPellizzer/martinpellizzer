Adenochloa pole-evansii

Adenochloa pole-evansii, commonly known as the Evans' grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a hairy texture.

Adenochloa pole-evansii is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland grassland habitats.
