Acer mono subvar. pedatifolium

Acer mono subvar. pedatifolium, commonly known as the maple leaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves, which are typically five to seven lobes, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras that disperse its seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and parks.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming more rugged with age.
