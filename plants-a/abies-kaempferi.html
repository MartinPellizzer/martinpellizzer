Abies kaempferi

Abies kaempferi, commonly known as the Japanese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The tree has needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are typically cylindrical and hang downward, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Abies kaempferi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
