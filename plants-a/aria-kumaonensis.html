Aria kumaonensis

Aria kumaonensis, commonly known as the Kumaon arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and mountain streams. Its flowers are usually greenish-white with a central spadix, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

Aria kumaonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
