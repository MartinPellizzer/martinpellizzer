Acacia phacelia

Acacia phacelia, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive, delicate appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Acacia phacelia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
