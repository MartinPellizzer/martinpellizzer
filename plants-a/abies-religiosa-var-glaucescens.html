Abies religiosa var. glaucescens

Abies religiosa var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue spruce, is a variant of the Colorado spruce.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America. The plant features needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it its distinctive appearance. Its cones are small and typically hang downward, and it produces a fragrant resin.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
