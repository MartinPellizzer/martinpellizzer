Acinodendron buddlejoides

Acinodendron buddlejoides, commonly known as the African Buddleja, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale yellow, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It is adapted to dry conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in arid regions.
