Atriplex nudicaulis

Atriplex nudicaulis, commonly known as little saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in saline soils and is often found in desert scrub and coastal dunes.

Atriplex nudicaulis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
