Asyneuma virgatum subsp. mazanderanicum

Asyneuma virgatum subsp.

mazanderanicum, commonly known as the Mazanderan sunflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust stems.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

It is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
