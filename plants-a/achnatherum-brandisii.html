Achnatherum brandisii

Achnatherum brandisii, commonly known as Brandis' needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, spikelets.

Achnatherum brandisii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitats.
