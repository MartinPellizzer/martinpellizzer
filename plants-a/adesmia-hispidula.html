Adesmia hispidula

Adesmia hispidula, commonly known as the rough adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, compound leaves. Its flowers are usually pale or white, with a distinctive spurred petal.

Adesmia hispidula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
