Argyreia elongata

Argyreia elongata, commonly known as the red veined morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant produces small, round, orange berries that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
