Avena barbata subsp. lusitanica

Avena barbata subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and woodland edges.

The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed bracts.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in marginal environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
