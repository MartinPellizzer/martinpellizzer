Aspidosperma pyricollum

Aspidosperma pyricollum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Barringtonia asiatica*, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible and commonly consumed by both humans and animals. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.

Additionally, the plant contains various alkaloids, making it an important species in pharmacological research.
