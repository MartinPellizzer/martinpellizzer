Abies lanceolata

Abies lanceolata, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is typically smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Abies lanceolata thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.
