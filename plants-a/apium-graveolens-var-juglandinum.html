Apium graveolens var. juglandinum

Apium graveolens var. juglandinum, commonly known as the wild celery or giant parsley, is a biennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, feather-like leaves that are aromatic and slightly bitter. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along riverbanks.
