Alchemilla pentaphyllea var. sericans

Alchemilla pentaphyllea var. sericans, commonly known as the silverleaf alchemilla, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features silvery-white, woolly leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in early summer, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, shady conditions.
