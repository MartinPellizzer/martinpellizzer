Acaena pterocarpa

Acaena pterocarpa, commonly known as the mountain nettle, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, three-lobed, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have serrated edges.

Acaena pterocarpa is adapted to grow in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its resilience in harsh conditions.
