Aspidosperma compactinervium

Aspidosperma compactinervium, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceratonia siliqua* in some regions, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree's bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive, flaky texture. Its fruit is a small, hard, spiny capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
