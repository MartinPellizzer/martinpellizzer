Aegilops algeriensis

Aegilops algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian goatgrass, is a wild grass species native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Aegilops algeriensis is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential genetic contributions to wheat improvement.
