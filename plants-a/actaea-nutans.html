Actaea nutans

Actaea nutans, commonly known as the nodding herbaceous buttercup, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pale yellow flowers that droop downward, giving it its common name. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are palmately arranged, and its stems are typically hairy. Actaea nutans thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
