Adenosma capitata

Adenosma capitata, commonly known as the Cape jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring small, narrow leaves arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem and small, elliptical leaves with a smooth surface.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
