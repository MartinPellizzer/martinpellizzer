Aphelandra adscendens

Aphelandra adscendens, commonly known as the torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and vibrant, red-orange bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically hidden within the bracts. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Aphelandra adscendens is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
