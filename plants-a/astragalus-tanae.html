Astragalus tanae

Astragalus tanae, commonly known as Tana's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from pale yellow to pink. The plant has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus tanae plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
