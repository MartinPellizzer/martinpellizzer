Agave durangensis

Agave durangensis, commonly known as Durango agave, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its robust, spiky leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
