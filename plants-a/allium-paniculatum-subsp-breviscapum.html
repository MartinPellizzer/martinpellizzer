Allium paniculatum subsp. breviscapum

Allium paniculatum subsp.

breviscapum, commonly known as the short-capped onion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces upright, branched inflorescences adorned with small, white to pale purple flowers.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
