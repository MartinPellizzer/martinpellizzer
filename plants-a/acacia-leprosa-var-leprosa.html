Acacia leprosa var. leprosa

Acacia leprosa var. leprosa, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spindly growth habit and its distinctive white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are typically bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, thread-like leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
