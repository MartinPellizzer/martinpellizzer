Agathosma ventenatiana

Agathosma ventenatiana, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features delicate, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its succulent stems and leaves are adapted to survive in arid conditions, making it drought-tolerant.

The plant is also valued for its use in aromatherapy and as a source of essential oils.
