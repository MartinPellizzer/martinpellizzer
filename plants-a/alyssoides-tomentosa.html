Alyssoides tomentosa

Alyssoides tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly alyssum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Alyssoides tomentosa is often used in ornamental gardening for its ornate foliage and delicate blooms.
