Acinodendron elaeoides

Acinodendron elaeoides, commonly known as the African sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in rocky soils. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
