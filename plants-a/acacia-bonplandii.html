Acacia bonplandii

Acacia bonplandii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow globular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in dry conditions. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.

The golden wattle is also the national floral emblem of Australia, symbolizing the country's natural heritage.
