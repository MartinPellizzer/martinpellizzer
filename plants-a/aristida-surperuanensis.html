Aristida surperuanensis

Aristida surperuanensis, commonly known as the Peruvian threeawn, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, which helps it survive in arid conditions.

Aristida surperuanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
