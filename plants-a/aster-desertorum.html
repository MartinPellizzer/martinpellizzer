Aster desertorum

Aster desertorum, commonly known as the desert aster, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions through its deep root system and waxy leaf coating. Its small, showy flowers bloom in the spring and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster desertorum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
