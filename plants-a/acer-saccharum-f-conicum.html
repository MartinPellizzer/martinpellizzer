Acer saccharum f. conicum

Acer saccharum f. conicum, commonly known as the red maple, is a variant of the sugar maple characterized by its distinctive red foliage.

This form exhibits a more compact growth habit compared to the typical sugar maple, often forming a dense, rounded canopy. Its leaves are typically narrower and more elongated, with a vibrant red coloration during the autumn season. The plant is native to eastern North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and urban landscapes.
