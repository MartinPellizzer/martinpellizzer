Abies davidiana

Abies davidiana, commonly known as the David's fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain seeds surrounded by a wing for wind dispersal. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
