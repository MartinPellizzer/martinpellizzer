Aralia simplicifolia

Aralia simplicifolia, commonly known as American aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves that are typically divided into three to five lobes, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, which are clustered in the axils of the leaves. Its berries are red or black and serve as a food source for various birds and wildlife.

Aralia simplicifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
