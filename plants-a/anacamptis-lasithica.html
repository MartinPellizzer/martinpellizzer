Anacamptis lasithica

Anacamptis lasithica, commonly known as the Lasithi orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Anacamptis lasithica is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
