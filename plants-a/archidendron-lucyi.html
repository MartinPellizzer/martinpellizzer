Archidendron lucyi

Archidendron lucyi, commonly known as the silky senna, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnate leaves, which are often covered in soft, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release seeds, a common feature among plants in the legume family.

Archidendron lucyi is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
