Areca angulosa

Areca angulosa, commonly known as the angular areca, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval, red or orange fruits containing a single seed. Areca angulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
