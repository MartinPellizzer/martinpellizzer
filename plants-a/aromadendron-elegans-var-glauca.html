Aromadendron elegans var. glauca

Aromadendron elegans var. glauca, commonly known as the blue-leafed sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance in both summer and winter. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, with a waxy texture that contributes to its unique visual appeal.

Aromadendron elegans var. glauca is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
