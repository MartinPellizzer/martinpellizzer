Anathallis petropolitana

Anathallis petropolitana, commonly known as the Petropolitana spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, slender inflorescences and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are pollinated by specific species of flies, showcasing a specialized ecological relationship.

Anathallis petropolitana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts.
