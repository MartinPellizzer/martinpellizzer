Aegopogon geminiflorus var. muticus

Aegopogon geminiflorus var. muticus, commonly known as the silent twinflower, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the twinflower species.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, clustered white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in pairs along the stem, giving it the "geminiflorus" characteristic.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
