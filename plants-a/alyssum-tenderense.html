Alyssum tenderense

Alyssum tenderense, commonly known as the tender alyssum, is a low-growing annual plant native to South America.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has delicate, hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum tenderense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
