Actinodaphne elegans

Actinodaphne elegans, commonly known as the slender sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The species is often found in humid, lowland forests where it thrives in partial shade.
