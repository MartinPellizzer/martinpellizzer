Astragalus australis proles gerardi

Astragalus australis proles gerardi, commonly known as the Australian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize new areas efficiently.
