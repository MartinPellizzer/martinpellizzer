Astragalus tanguticus

Astragalus tanguticus, commonly known as the Tangut milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in dry, rocky soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its flowering period typically occurs in the summer months, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
