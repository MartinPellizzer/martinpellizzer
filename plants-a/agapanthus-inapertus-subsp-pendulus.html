Agapanthus inapertus subsp. pendulus

Agapanthus inapertus subsp.

pendulus, commonly known as the hanging agapanthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that hang in clusters from long, slender stems.

The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

The pendulous flowers bloom in summer, adding a dramatic visual impact to the landscape.
