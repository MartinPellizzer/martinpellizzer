Androsace occidentalis subsp. arizonica

Androsace occidentalis subsp.

arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States. It typically forms dense mats of hairy, succulent leaves that are adapted to arid environments.

The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its root system is fibrous and helps it retain moisture in sandy soils.

It is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
