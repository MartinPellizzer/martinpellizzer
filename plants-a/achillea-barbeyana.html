Achillea barbeyana

Achillea barbeyana, commonly known as the Barbey's yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its flowering period usually occurs in the summer months, adding a delicate touch to alpine landscapes.
