Anemone virginiana var. cylindroidea

Anemone virginiana var. cylindroidea, commonly known as the narrowleaf anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
