Abies × beissneriana

Abies × beissneriana, commonly known as the Beissner's fir, is a hybrid conifer resulting from the cross between Abies balsamifera and Abies lasiocarpa.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The plant produces cones that are typically cylindrical and contain edible seeds, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions across the northern hemisphere.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape tree.
