Allium fimbriatum var. aboriginum

Allium fimbriatum var. aboriginum, commonly known as the Aboriginal onion, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bulbous structures that are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This variety is valued for its culinary uses and is also an important plant in Indigenous Australian culture.
