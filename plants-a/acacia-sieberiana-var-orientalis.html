Acacia sieberiana var. orientalis

Acacia sieberiana var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark is typically smooth and grayish, with a distinctive flaky texture.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
