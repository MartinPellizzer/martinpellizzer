Aquilegia vulgaris var. sooi

Aquilegia vulgaris var. sooi, commonly known as the Chinese columbine, is a variant of the common columbine native to China.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which are typically spurred at the base and arranged in clusters. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often hairy. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in ornamental gardens and floral arrangements.
