Allium libani

Allium libani, commonly known as the Lebanon garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, round bulbs that are often used in traditional cooking, similar to other Allium species. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, Allium libani is cultivated in gardens and for local food traditions.
