Allium caucasicum

Allium caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant features long, slender leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor similar to garlic.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its culinary uses.
