Arundinaria andropogonoides

Arundinaria andropogonoides, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or giant bamboo, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, forming dense clumps. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in terminal clusters, which are typical of the grass family.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
