Acacia cumanensis

Acacia cumanensis, commonly known as the Koa tree, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense canopy of green foliage. Its flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in spherical inflorescences.

Acacia cumanensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various native species.
