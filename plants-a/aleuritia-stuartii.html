Aleuritia stuartii

Aleuritia stuartii, commonly known as Stuart's elder, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aleuritia stuartii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
