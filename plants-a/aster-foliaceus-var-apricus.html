Aster foliaceus var. apricus

Aster foliaceus var. apricus, commonly known as the Apricot Leaf Aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is distinctive for its soft, apricot-like coloration, which adds ornamental value to gardens.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to native plant landscapes.
