Aspalathus iniquua

Aspalathus iniquua, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils along riverbanks and in open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a resilient species with potential ecological and medicinal significance.
