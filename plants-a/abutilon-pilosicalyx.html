Abutilon pilosicalyx

Abutilon pilosicalyx, commonly known as the silky abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its soft, hairy foliage. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptic, with a velvety texture due to the dense pubescence. It produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, followed by small, woody capsules containing seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
