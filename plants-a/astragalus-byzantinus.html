Astragalus byzantinus

Astragalus byzantinus, commonly known as the Byzantine milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in racemes, and it is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Astragalus byzantinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in semi-arid regions.
