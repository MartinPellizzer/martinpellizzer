Allium strictum var. anodon

Allium strictum var. anodon, commonly known as the slender leek, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale purple flowers in umbels. Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild garlic-like flavor.

The plant is also known for its ability to naturalize and form dense clumps over time.
