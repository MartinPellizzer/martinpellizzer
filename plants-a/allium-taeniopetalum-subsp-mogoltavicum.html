Allium taeniopetalum subsp. mogoltavicum

Allium taeniopetalum subsp.

mogoltavicum, commonly known as the Mogoltav garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features bulbous stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive, strap-shaped petals that give it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential culinary uses.
