Arundinaria niitakayamensis var. microcarpa

Arundinaria niitakayamensis var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a unique variant of the bamboo genus characterized by its compact growth habit.

It features slender, culms that are typically shorter and more delicate compared to its parent species. The plant exhibits a clumping growth pattern, forming dense clusters that are well-suited for small garden spaces. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a subtle bluish-green hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in shady or partially shaded environments.
