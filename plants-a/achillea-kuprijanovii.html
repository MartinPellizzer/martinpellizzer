Achillea kuprijanovii

Achillea kuprijanovii, commonly known as Kuprianov's yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and flat-topped clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is often found in alpine meadows and steppe habitats.

The species is named in honor of the Russian botanist Vladimir Kuprianov.
