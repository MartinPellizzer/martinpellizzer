Amygdalus elaeagrifolia subsp.

leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-sepaled peach. It is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, white to pink flowers.

The fruit is a drupe with a smooth, thin skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other members of the Prunus genus.
