Alchemilla fokinii

Alchemilla fokinii, commonly known as the Fokin's lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Alchemilla fokinii thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
