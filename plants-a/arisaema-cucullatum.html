Arisaema cucullatum

Arisaema cucullatum, commonly known as the hooded arisaema, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and can reach several inches in size.

The plant produces striking white or greenish flowers, which are followed by berries that mature to a dark purple color.
