Astragalus steppicola

Astragalus steppicola, commonly known as the steppe milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish pea-like flowers that bloom in summer. It is adapted to dry, sunny environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Astragalus steppicola is often found in grasslands and is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
