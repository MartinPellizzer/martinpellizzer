Acacia hammondii

Acacia hammondii, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It produces long, slender pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acacia hammondii plays an important role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
