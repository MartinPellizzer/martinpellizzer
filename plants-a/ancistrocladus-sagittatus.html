Ancistrocladus sagittatus

Ancistrocladus sagittatus, commonly known as the arrowleaf ancistrocladus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Ancistrocladaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant is typically shrub-like, with simple, alternate leaves that are often leathery in texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish in color.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
