Arillaria robusta

Arillaria robusta, commonly known as the giant arrowroot or mountain arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, tuberous roots that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by small, red berries. Arillaria robusta thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions.

Its unique morphology and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical studies.
