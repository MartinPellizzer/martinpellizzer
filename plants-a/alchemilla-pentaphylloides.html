Alchemilla pentaphylloides

Alchemilla pentaphylloides, commonly known as the five-leaf sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
