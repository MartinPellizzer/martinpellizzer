Angiopteris evanidostriata

Angiopteris evanidostriata, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The fronds have a distinctive pale green color with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds.
