Artemisia tridentata subsp. bolanderi

Artemisia tridentata subsp.

bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander's sagebrush, is a subspecies native to the western United States. It is a shrub characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and aromatic, pungent scent.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, including sagebrush steppe and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive tridentate (three-toothed) margin.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
