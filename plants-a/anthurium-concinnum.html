Anthurium concinnum

Anthurium concinnum, commonly known as the red anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish or maroon tint. The plant produces spadices, which are dense clusters of tiny flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe. Anthurium concinnum thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
