Aspalathus mundiana

Aspalathus mundiana, commonly known as the small-leaved rooibos, is a flowering plant native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its small, serrated leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often grown for its leaves, which are used to produce a popular herbal tea. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a temperate climate with adequate sunlight.

Aspalathus mundiana is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
