Agrostophyllum denbergeri

Agrostophyllum denbergeri, commonly known as the Denberger's mistflower, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its flowers are tiny, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, adding to its ornamental value.

Agrostophyllum denbergeri is valued in horticulture for its unique foliage and ability to thrive in humid, tropical conditions.
