Alyssum caliacrae var. humerosum

Alyssum caliacrae var. humerosum, commonly known as the Armenian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum caliacrae var. humerosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
