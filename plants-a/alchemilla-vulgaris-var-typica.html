Alchemilla vulgaris var. typica

Alchemilla vulgaris var. typica, commonly known as lady's mantle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces upright, branched stems that bear small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Lady's mantle is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
