Argythamnia sponiella

Argythamnia sponiella, commonly known as the spiny eryngium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features spiny, bract-like leaves and produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
