Allium lacunosum var. micranthum

Allium lacunosum var. micranthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic.

It is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a valued crop in some culinary traditions.

The species is also appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its delicate appearance.
