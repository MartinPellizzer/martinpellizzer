Adesmia hispidula var. plumosa

Adesmia hispidula var. plumosa, commonly known as the hairy leaf adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
