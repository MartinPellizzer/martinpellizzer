Alstroemeria simsii

Alstroemeria simsii, commonly known as the Chilean lily, is a striking flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Alstroemeria simsii is also appreciated for its long-lasting blooms, making it a popular choice for floral arrangements.
