Acacia patula

Acacia patula, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spikes. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system. It produces pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acacia patula is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
