Astragalus atratus var. phyllophorus

Astragalus atratus var. phyllophorus, commonly known as the black milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender stems with small, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale purple to brown, arranged in clusters, and bloom in the summer months.

The plant is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.
