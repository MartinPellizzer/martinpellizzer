Astragalus ui-eilakensis

Astragalus ui-eilakensis, commonly known as the Eilaken Astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces small, yellow to green flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are usually compound with a pinnate structure. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
