Amaranthus asplundii

Amaranthus asplundii, commonly known as the asplundii amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, lance-shaped leaves, and vibrant red or orange bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.

Amaranthus asplundii is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in dry landscapes.
