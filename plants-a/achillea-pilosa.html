Achillea pilosa

Achillea pilosa, commonly known as the soft yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Achillea pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
