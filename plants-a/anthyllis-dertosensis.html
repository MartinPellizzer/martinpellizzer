Anthyllis dertosensis

Anthyllis dertosensis, commonly known as the Dertos thornvetch, is a flowering plant native to the island of Kefalonia in Greece.

It is a perennial herb that grows in rocky and coastal areas, adapted to the harsh maritime climate. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Anthyllis dertosensis is notable for its hardy nature and its role in stabilizing coastal soils.
