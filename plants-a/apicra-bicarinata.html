Apicra bicarinata

Apicra bicarinata, commonly known as the two-edged orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive two-lobed lip. Native to tropical regions of South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and showy, often colorful blooms that attract pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
