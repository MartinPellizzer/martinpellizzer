Acacia trachycarpa

Acacia trachycarpa, commonly known as the rough-seeded acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures in mature plants. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Acacia trachycarpa is adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems and a capacity for drought tolerance.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
