Aster hispidus var. tubulosus

Aster hispidus var. tubulosus, commonly known as the roughleaf aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features upright stems covered in stiff, hairy hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, bristly texture.

This species is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
