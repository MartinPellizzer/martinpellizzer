Anthemis auriculata

Anthemis auriculata, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its name derives from the auriculate (ear-shaped) leaves that are a notable botanical feature.
