Aerisilvaea

Aerisilvaea, commonly known as the skywood fern, is a rare and elusive plant found in high-altitude cloud forests.

It possesses delicate, feathery fronds that resemble the structure of traditional ferns, though its unique growth pattern sets it apart. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments where it can absorb moisture from the air. Its leaves are covered in a fine, silvery coating that aids in reflecting excess light and retaining water.

Due to its limited habitat and fragile nature, Aerisilvaea is considered a conservation concern.
