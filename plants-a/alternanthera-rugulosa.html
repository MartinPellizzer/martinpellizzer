Alternanthera rugulosa

Alternanthera rugulosa, commonly known as red-leaf alternate or red velvet plant, is a strikingly colorful aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, pink, or purple foliage, which intensifies under bright light and nutrient-rich conditions. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in freshwater environments and is popular in aquariums for its ornamental value.

Alternanthera rugulosa is a fast-growing plant that can be propagated through stem cuttings or by dividing the rhizomes.
