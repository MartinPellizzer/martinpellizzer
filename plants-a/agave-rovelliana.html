Agave rovelliana

Agave rovelliana, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves. The leaves are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. It produces an inflorescence of white to pale yellow flowers, which appear at the center of the plant.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional practices and as an ornamental plant.
