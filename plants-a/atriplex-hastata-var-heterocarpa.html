Atriplex hastata var. heterocarpa

Atriplex hastata var. heterocarpa, commonly known as desert saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, succulent leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its salt-tolerant nature allows it to thrive in soils with high salinity.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
