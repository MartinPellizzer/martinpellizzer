Apargia media

Apargia media, commonly known as the common tansy, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in moist, sunny to partially shaded areas, often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Apargia media is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
