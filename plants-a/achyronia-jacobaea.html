Achyronia jacobaea

Achyronia jacobaea, commonly known as the common ragwort, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of blue-green leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which are toxic to livestock and can cause liver damage. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.

Due to its toxicity, it is considered a weed in agricultural areas.
