Arabidopsis schimperi

Arabidopsis schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian mustard, is a small flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant has small, green, lobed leaves and produces small yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing tiny seeds. It is often used as a model organism in plant biology research due to its short life cycle and genetic simplicity.

The species is also valued for its potential in agricultural and ecological studies.
