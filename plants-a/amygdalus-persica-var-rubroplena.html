Amygdalus persica var. rubroplena

Amygdalus persica var. rubroplena, commonly known as the Persian almond with red plena, is a cultivar of the almond tree characterized by its distinctive floral features.

This variety is noted for its showy, double flowers that are predominantly pink to red in color, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of the Middle East and South Asia. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside.
