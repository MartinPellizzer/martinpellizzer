Alangium salviifolium subsp. hexapetalum

Alangium salviifolium subsp.

hexapetalum, commonly known as the six-petaled alangium, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth margin and a glossy dark green upper surface. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and are followed by small, fleshy berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
