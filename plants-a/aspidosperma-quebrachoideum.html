Aspidosperma quebrachoideum

Aspidosperma quebrachoideum, commonly known as yellow quebracho, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fissured bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, elongated pods containing seeds. It is valued for its hard, heavy wood used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant also contains alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
