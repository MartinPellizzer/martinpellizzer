Asphodelus albus subvar. arrondeaui

Asphodelus albus subvar. arrondeaui, commonly known as the white asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. It is often found in rocky habitats and has adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
