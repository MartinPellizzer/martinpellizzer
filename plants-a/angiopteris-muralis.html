Angiopteris muralis

Angiopteris muralis, commonly known as the wall fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Angiopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often lobed fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as rocky crevices or along walls. Its sporangia are clustered in sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Angiopteris muralis is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
