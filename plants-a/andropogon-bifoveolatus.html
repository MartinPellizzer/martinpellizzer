Andropogon bifoveolatus

Andropogon bifoveolatus, commonly known as the two-lobed bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and distinctive two-lobed seed heads. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelets.

Andropogon bifoveolatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
