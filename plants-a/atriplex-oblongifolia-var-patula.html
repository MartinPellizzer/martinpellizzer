Atriplex oblongifolia var. patula

Atriplex oblongifolia var. patula, commonly known as desert saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typical of the Amaranthaceae family. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it a valuable species for stabilizing soils in saline and disturbed areas.

The plant's extensive root system helps it survive in nutrient-poor and drought-prone habitats.
