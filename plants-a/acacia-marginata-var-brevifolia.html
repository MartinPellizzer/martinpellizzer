Acacia marginata var. brevifolia

Acacia marginata var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, feathery leaves and produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring. The plant is adapted to dry and arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its acacia pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, and the plant is often used in ecological restoration projects.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
