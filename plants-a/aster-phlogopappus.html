Aster phlogopappus

Aster phlogopappus, commonly known as the yellow aster, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
