Aechmanthera wallichii var. tomentosa

Aechmanthera wallichii var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly white jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly foliage and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a dense pubescence that gives it a fuzzy appearance.

The flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated primarily by insects.
