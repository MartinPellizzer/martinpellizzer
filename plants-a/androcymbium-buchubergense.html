Androcymbium buchubergense

Androcymbium buchubergense, commonly known as the Buchberg mountain orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes a strikingly colored lip and a specialized column for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowering period occurs during the late summer months, and it is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and low light conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
