Anthericum brehmeanum

Anthericum brehmeanum, commonly known as the Brehme's arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by various insects, including flies and beetles. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped and have a waxy texture, while its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine.

Anthericum brehmeanum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
