Astragalus campestris var. hylophilus

Astragalus campestris var. hylophilus, commonly known as the hillside milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
