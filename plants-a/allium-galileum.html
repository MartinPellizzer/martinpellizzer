Allium galileum

Allium galileum, commonly known as the Galilean leek, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces small, clustered white or pink flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor.

It is often cultivated for both culinary use and ornamental purposes in gardens.
