Arabis elata

Arabis elata, commonly known as the tall honesty, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its seed pods are distinctive for their papery, coin-like appearance.

Arabis elata is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
