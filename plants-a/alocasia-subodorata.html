Alocasia subodorata

Alocasia subodorata, commonly known as the fragrant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that emit a mild fragrance, especially in the evening. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine.

Alocasia subodorata thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
