Acisanthera lasiophylla

Acisanthera lasiophylla, commonly known as the silky-leaved acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are covered in a fine, silky hair that gives the plant its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Acisanthera lasiophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
