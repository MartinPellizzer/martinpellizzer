Acianthus exiguus

Acianthus exiguus, commonly known as the dwarf acianthus, is a small, flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, pale green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or greenish in color. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers a warm, dry climate.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
