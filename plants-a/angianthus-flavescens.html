Angianthus flavescens

Angianthus flavescens, commonly known as the yellow angianthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces bright yellow ray flowers surrounded by a ring of smaller, tubular florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a notable species in its native ecosystem.
