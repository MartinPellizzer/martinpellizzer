Azadirachta indica var. siamensis

Azadirachta indica var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam banyan, is a variant of the Indian banyan tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large evergreen tree characterized by its extensive aerial roots that form a dense network of support and contribute to its distinctive appearance. The leaves are large, heart-shaped, and glossy, providing dense shade beneath the tree. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by round, woody fruits containing seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in tropical environments.
