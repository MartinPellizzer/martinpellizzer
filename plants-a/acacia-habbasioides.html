Acacia habbasioides

Acacia habbasioides, commonly known as the Sudan acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It is characterized by its straight, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are densely clustered at the base of the branches. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important component of the local flora in these regions.

Acacia habbasioides is also valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a source of browse for wildlife.
