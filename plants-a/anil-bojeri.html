Anil bojeri

Anil bojeri, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are often borne in clusters and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.

Anil bojeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and fragrance.
