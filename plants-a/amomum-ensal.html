Amomum ensal

Amomum ensal, commonly known as the lesser cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that are followed by dark, aromatic berries. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The essential oils found in its rhizomes and fruits are valued for their spicy, sweet aroma and various medicinal properties.
