Astragalus amygdalinus

Astragalus amygdalinus, commonly known as the almond milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, hairy coating. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Astragalus amygdalinus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
