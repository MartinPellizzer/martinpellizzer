Acacia cardiophylla

Acacia cardiophylla, commonly known as the heartleaf acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its woody structure and drought tolerance make it well adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Acacia cardiophylla plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
