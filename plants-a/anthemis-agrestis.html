Anthemis agrestis

Anthemis agrestis, commonly known as the field chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with a central disk. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Anthemis agrestis is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
