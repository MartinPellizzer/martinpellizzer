Aconitum ciliare var. polytrichum

Aconitum ciliare var. polytrichum, commonly known as the fringed monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant features palmately divided leaves with finely toothed margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
