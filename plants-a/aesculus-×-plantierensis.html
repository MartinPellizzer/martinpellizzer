Aesculus × plantierensis

Aesculus × plantierensis, commonly known as the horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces spiny, capsule-like fruits containing shiny, chestnut-like seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also known for its role in traditional medicine.
