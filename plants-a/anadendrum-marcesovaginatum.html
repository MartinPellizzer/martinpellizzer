Anadendrum marcesovaginatum

Anadendrum marcesovaginatum, commonly known as the yellow mistflower, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
