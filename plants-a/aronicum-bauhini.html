Aronicum bauhini

Aronicum bauhini, commonly known as the white-tongued aronicum, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features upright, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Aronicum bauhini thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.
