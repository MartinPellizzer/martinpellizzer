Anthurium argyrostachyum var. glaucostachyum

Anthurium argyrostachyum var. glaucostachyum, commonly known as the silver-stem anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, dark green leaves that are often adorned with silvery or whitish veins. The plant produces vibrant red spadices, which are the spathes that enclose the true flowers. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
