Arundinaria mikurensis

Arundinaria mikurensis, commonly known as the Mikura bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Arundinaria mikurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
