Acanthus boissieri

Acanthus boissieri, commonly known as the rock acanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces spiky, spathaceous flower clusters in late summer, which are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments.

Acanthus boissieri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and architectural form.
