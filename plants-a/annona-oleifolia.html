Annona oleifolia

Annona oleifolia, commonly known as the oil tree or yellow annona, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with smooth, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by green, round fruits that turn yellow when mature. The fruit has a thick, leathery rind and contains a sweet, creamy pulp with numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some regions for its edible fruit and medicinal properties.
