Anguria diversifolia

Anguria diversifolia, commonly known as the variable-leaf cucumber, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that vary in size and color, often displaying a mix of green, white, and yellow hues. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, edible fruits resembling cucumbers. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
