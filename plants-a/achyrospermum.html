Achyrospermum

Achyrospermum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Achyrospermum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
