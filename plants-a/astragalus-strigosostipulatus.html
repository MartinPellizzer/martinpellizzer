Astragalus strigosostipulatus

Astragalus strigosostipulatus, commonly known as the hairy leaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with stipules that are often hairy, giving it its specific epithet. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Astragalus strigosostipulatus plays a role in soil stabilization and is part of the diverse flora of sagebrush ecosystems.
