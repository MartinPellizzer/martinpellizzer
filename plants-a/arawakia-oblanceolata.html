Arawakia oblanceolata

Arawakia oblanceolata, commonly known as the West Indian mahoe, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
