Argyrolobium marginatum

Argyrolobium marginatum, commonly known as the silverleaf burrobrush, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to harsh desert climates.
