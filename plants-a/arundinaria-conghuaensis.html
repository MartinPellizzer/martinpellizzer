Arundinaria conghuaensis

Arundinaria conghuaensis, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or Guangxi bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a pale green color.

Arundinaria conghuaensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
