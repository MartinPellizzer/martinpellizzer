Aneilema subovatum

Aneilema subovatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvet plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne on long, drooping stalks. Aneilema subovatum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
