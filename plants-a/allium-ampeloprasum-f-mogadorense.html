Allium ampeloprasum f. mogadorense

Allium ampeloprasum f. mogadorense, commonly known as the purple onion, is a cultivar of the wild leek.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, purple-pink flowers in a loose umbel, which bloom in the summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, often used in culinary applications.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value as well as its culinary versatility.
