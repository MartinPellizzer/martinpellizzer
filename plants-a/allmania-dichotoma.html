Allmania dichotoma

Allmania dichotoma, commonly known as the forked allmania, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters. Its inflorescence is distinctive, with branches that split into two or more forks, giving it its common name.

Allmania dichotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
