Acalypha heterodonta var. trichoclada

Acalypha heterodonta var. trichoclada, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in clusters. Its stems are woody and often have a reddish hue, adding to its ornamental value.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
