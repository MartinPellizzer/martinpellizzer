Arthrostemma caulialatum

Arthrostemma caulialatum, commonly known as the golden thread orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has limited distribution in the wild.
