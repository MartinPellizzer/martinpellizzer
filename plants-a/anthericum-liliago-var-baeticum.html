Anthericum liliago var. baeticum

Anthericum liliago var. baeticum, commonly known as the Baetican arum, is a rare and striking plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive spathe and spadix inflorescence. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions with rich, humus-filled soil. Its flowers are typically greenish-white, with a spadix that bears tiny, clustered female and male flowers.

This variant is highly valued in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
