Achyranthes glaucescens

Achyranthes glaucescens, commonly known as the bluish achyranthes, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish coating. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
