Acer campestre var. pubescens

Acer campestre var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent field maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its pubescent (hairy) young shoots and leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
