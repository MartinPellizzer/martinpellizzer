Alyssum kavadarcense

Alyssum kavadarcense, commonly known as the Kavadar alpine alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Alyssum kavadarcense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
