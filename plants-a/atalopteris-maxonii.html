Atalopteris maxonii

Atalopteris maxonii, commonly known as the Mexican water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically less than 1 meter in length. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with small, rounded lobes that help in photosynthesis and water retention.

Atalopteris maxonii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
