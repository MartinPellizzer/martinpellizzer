Anthurium polydactylum

Anthurium polydactylum, commonly known as the velvet leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch and come in shades of green, red, and purple. The plant produces small, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium polydactylum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
