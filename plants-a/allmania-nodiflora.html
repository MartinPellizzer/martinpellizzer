Allmania nodiflora

Allmania nodiflora, commonly known as the trailing allmania, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
