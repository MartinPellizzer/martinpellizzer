Aristida effusa

Aristida effusa, commonly known as the slender wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize various habitats.

Aristida effusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
