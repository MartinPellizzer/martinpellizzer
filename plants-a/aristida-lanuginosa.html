Aristida lanuginosa

Aristida lanuginosa, commonly known as silky grass or silky wild oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and panicle-like inflorescence composed of numerous small spikelets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

The botanical features of Aristida lanuginosa make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
