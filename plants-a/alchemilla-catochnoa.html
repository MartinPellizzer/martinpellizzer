Alchemilla catochnoa

Alchemilla catochnoa, commonly known as the Catochna lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Alchemilla catochnoa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
