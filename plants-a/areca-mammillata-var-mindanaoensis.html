Areca mammillata var. mindanaoensis

Areca mammillata var. mindanaoensis, commonly known as the Mindanao areca palm, is a tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible fruits that are similar to those of the betel nut. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are also used in cultural practices and as a source of fiber.
