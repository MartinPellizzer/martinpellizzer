Acanthus edulis

Acanthus edulis, commonly known as the edible acanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and showy, spiny flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its leaves are edible when young and tender, though they become more fibrous with age.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its distinctive appearance.
