Aristolochia labiosa

Aristolochia labiosa, commonly known as the pink pipevine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, pinkish-purple flowers that resemble a pipe, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic, sometimes spicy scent. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its vines can grow quite long.

The plant is also notable for containing aristolochic acids, which are toxic to humans and animals.
