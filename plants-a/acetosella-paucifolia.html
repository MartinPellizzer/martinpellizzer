Acetosella paucifolia

Acetosella paucifolia, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves that resemble mouse ears, typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces tiny white or pale purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky soils.

Acetosella paucifolia is often found in association with other woodland flora and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
