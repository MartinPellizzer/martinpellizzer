Ageratina liebmannii

Ageratina liebmannii, commonly known as the Liebmann's blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fluffy blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Ageratina liebmannii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
