Archidendron fagifolium

Archidendron fagifolium, commonly known as the chestnut-leaf legume, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a chestnut-like appearance, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in humid, lowland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Archidendron fagifolium is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
