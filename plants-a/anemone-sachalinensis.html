Anemone sachalinensis

Anemone sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Russian Far East and parts of northeastern China.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features a short, fibrous root system and produces showy, white or pinkish flowers with five to seven petals. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps retain moisture.

Anemone sachalinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
