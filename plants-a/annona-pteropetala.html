Annona pteropetala

Annona pteropetala, commonly known as the Brazilian custard apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a smooth, yellow or greenish-yellow berry that ripens to a yellowish-orange color, with a sweet, creamy pulp.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in its native habitat.
