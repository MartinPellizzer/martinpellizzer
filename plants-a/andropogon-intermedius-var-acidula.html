Andropogon intermedius var. acidula

Andropogon intermedius var. acidula, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, golden-yellow foliage that emits a pleasant scent when crushed. The plant typically grows in wet, sunny areas such as marshes and along stream banks. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are often used in traditional crafts and rituals.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
