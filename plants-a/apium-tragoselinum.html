Apium tragoselinum

Apium tragoselinum, commonly known as wild celery, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in moist, disturbed soils.

Wild celery is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and along roadsides.
