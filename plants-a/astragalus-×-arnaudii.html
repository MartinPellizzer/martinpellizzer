Astragalus × arnaudii

Astragalus × arnaudii, commonly known as the hybrid milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with stipules and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It is native to regions in Central Asia and is adapted to grow in dry, rocky, or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
