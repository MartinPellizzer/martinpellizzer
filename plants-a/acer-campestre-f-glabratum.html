Acer campestre f. glabratum

Acer campestre f. glabratum, commonly known as the smooth field maple, is a variant of the field maple tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds.

The smooth field maple is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
