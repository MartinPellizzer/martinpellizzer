Allium inconspicuum

Allium inconspicuum, commonly known as the common garlic chive, is a flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant produces bulbils along its stems, which can be used for propagation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium inconspicuum is often used in ornamental gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
