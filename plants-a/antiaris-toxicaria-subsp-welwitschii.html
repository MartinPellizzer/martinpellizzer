Antiaris toxicaria subsp. welwitschii

Antiaris toxicaria subsp.

welwitschii, commonly known as the Natal giant lily, is a large, evergreen shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and striking, large yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and resilience.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
