Anthurium jaimeanum

Anthurium jaimeanum, commonly known as the Jaime's arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to Colombia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces unique spadices, which are the modified leaves that house the tiny, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
