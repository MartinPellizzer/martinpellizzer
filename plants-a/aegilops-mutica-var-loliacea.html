Aegilops mutica var. loliacea

Aegilops mutica var. loliacea, commonly known as goatgrass, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species has historical significance in agriculture and is sometimes used as a forage plant.
