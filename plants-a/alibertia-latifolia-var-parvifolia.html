Alibertia latifolia var. parvifolia

Alibertia latifolia var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved senna, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it valuable for ecological studies and potential medicinal uses.
