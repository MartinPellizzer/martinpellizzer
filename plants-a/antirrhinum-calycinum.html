Antirrhinum calycinum

Antirrhinum calycinum, commonly known as the California snapdragon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking, two-lipped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming from spring to summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
