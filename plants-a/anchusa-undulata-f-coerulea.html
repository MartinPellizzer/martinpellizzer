Anchusa undulata f. coerulea

Anchusa undulata f. coerulea, commonly known as the blue flowering bellflower, is a horticultural form of the species Anchusa undulata.

It is characterized by its striking deep blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a perennial herb with a compact, bushy growth habit, featuring silver-gray foliage that adds ornamental value throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both cultivated gardens and naturalized landscapes.
