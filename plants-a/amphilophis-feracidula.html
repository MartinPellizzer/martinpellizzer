Amphilophis feracidula

Amphilophis feracidula, commonly known as the acid-loving fern, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soil and requires high humidity to flourish. It is often found in shaded, forested areas where it can receive indirect sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
