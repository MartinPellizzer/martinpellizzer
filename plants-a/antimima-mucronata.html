Antimima mucronata

Antimima mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-lobed antimony plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Apocynaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has elliptical leaves with a distinctive mucronate tip, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
