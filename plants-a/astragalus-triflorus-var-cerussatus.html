Astragalus triflorus var. cerussatus

Astragalus triflorus var. cerussatus, commonly known as the cerussate milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three leaflets and small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

It is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.
