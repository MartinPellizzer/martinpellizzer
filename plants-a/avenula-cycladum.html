Avenula cycladum

Avenula cycladum, commonly known as the Cycladic grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leaf-like blades. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bloom in early summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Avenula cycladum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in arid and semi-arid environments.
