Aucuba japonica var. marmorata

Aucuba japonica var. marmorata, commonly known as Japanese aucuba or variegated aucuba, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, mottled foliage with a combination of green, white, and yellow variegation. The leaves are thick, leathery, and oval in shape, providing a dense, bushy appearance. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used as a decorative plant in gardens or as a houseplant.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
