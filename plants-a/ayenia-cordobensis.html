Ayenia cordobensis

Ayenia cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoba ayenia, is a flowering plant native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Ayenia cordobensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
