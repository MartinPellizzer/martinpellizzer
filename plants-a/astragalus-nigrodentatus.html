Astragalus nigrodentatus

Astragalus nigrodentatus, commonly known as the black-toothed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus nigrodentatus is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
