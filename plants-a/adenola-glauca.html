Adenola glauca

Adenola glauca, commonly known as the blue adenola, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Adenola glauca is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
