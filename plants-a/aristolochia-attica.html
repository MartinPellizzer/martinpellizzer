Aristolochia attica

Aristolochia attica, commonly known as the Greek birthwort, is a climbing vine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique, intricate flowers that resemble a pipe or a horn. The plant produces a strong, aromatic odor that attracts specific pollinators, such as flies. Its berries are dark blue and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
