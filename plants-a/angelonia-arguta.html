Angelonia arguta

Angelonia arguta, commonly known as the winter angelonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of tubular, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is known for its ability to bloom continuously throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Angelonia arguta is also drought-tolerant and relatively low maintenance, contributing to its popularity among gardeners.
