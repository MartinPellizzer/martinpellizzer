Astragalus seidabadensis

Astragalus seidabadensis, commonly known as the Seidabad milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to specific regions in Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to survive in harsh conditions.

Its root system is known for its medicinal properties, making it valuable in traditional herbal medicine.
