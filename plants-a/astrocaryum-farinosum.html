Astrocaryum farinosum

Astrocaryum farinosum, commonly known as the farinha palm, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces edible fruits that are often used in traditional cuisine. Its leaves are also utilized for thatching and basket-making.

The farinha palm is valued for its versatility and plays an important role in both ecological and cultural contexts.
