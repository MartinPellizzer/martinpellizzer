Aspalathus biflora subsp. longicarpa

Aspalathus biflora subsp.

longicarpa, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is adapted to grow in sandy, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
