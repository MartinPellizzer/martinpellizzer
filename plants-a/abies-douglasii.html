Abies douglasii

Abies douglasii, commonly known as the Douglas fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The tree has needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are typically reddish-brown and hang downward, containing winged seeds.

Douglas fir is valued for its strong, durable wood and plays an important role in forest ecosystems.
