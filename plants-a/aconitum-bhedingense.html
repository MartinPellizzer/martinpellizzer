Aconitum bhedingense

Aconitum bhedingense, commonly known as the Bhutanese aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of Bhutan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant produces follicular fruits that contain several seeds, and its leaves are deeply lobed with a distinctive, wavy margin.

Aconitum bhedingense is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
