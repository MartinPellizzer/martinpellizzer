Andira lanei

Andira lanei, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring opposite, simple, and often glandular leaves. The plant produces small, clustered, white to pale pink flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. These berries are edible for some animals but are generally considered toxic to humans.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fruiting display.
