Arnica paucibracteata

Arnica paucibracteata, commonly known as the alpine tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool, dry climates. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers with prominent yellow centers. It is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
