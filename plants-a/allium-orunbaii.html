Allium orunbaii

Allium orunbaii, commonly known as the Mongolian leek, is a perennial plant native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes many edible and ornamental plants such as onions and garlic. The plant features long, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or purple flowers. Its bulbs are often used in traditional cuisine and have a mild, onion-like flavor.

Botanically, it is characterized by its adaptability to harsh environments and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
