Apium nodiflorum var. ocreatum

Apium nodiflorum var. ocreatum, commonly known as the clustered celery or ocreate celery, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in umbels. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its stems are often covered with a unique, leaf-like bracts that give it its common name.

This variety is valued for its culinary use in some regions, though it is less commonly cultivated than other celery species.
