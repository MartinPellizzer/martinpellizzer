Annona laurifolia

Annona laurifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pawpaw, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a yellowish, fuzzy berry that ripens in late summer and is edible when fully mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and fruit.
