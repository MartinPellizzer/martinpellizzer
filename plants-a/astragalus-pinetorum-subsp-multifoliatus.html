Astragalus pinetorum subsp. multifoliatus

Astragalus pinetorum subsp.

multifoliatus, commonly known as the pine mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and pinnately compound leaves with multiple leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellow to greenish-yellow flowers in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and pine forests.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
