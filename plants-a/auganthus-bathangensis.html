Auganthus bathangensis

Auganthus bathangensis, commonly known as the Bathang's auganthus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Auganthus bathangensis is a relatively rare species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
