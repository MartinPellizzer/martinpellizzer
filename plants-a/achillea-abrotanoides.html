Achillea abrotanoides

Achillea abrotanoides, commonly known as narrow-leaved yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
