Asperula lissocarpa

Asperula lissocarpa, commonly known as the silky woodbine, is a perennial climbing plant native to China.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Asperula lissocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
