Astragalus hispidulus

Astragalus hispidulus, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its compound leaves and pod-like fruits are typical of the genus Astragalus.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
