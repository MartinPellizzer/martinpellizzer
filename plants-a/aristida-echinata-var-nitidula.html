Aristida echinata var. nitidula

Aristida echinata var. nitidula, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with silky, awnless bristles. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its fine, grass-like leaves are typically covered with a layer of fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
