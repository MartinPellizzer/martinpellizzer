Aira litoralis

Aira litoralis, commonly known as saltgrass, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in tidal marshes and dunes. It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Aira litoralis is an important component of coastal ecosystems due to its adaptability and ecological significance.
