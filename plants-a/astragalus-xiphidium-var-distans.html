Astragalus xiphidium var. distans

Astragalus xiphidium var. distans, commonly known as the Desert Milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
