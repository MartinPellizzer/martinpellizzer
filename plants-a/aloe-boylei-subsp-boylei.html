Aloe boylei subsp. boylei

Aloe boylei subsp.

boylei, commonly known as Boyle's aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Namibia and Angola. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape, often with a bluish-green coloration.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in late summer. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, storing water in its leaves and having a shallow root system.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
