Acacia inaequiloba

Acacia inaequiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a species of acacia native to Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and yellow flowers that appear in clusters. Acacia inaequiloba is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes many leguminous plants.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
