Asplenium mattirolii

Asplenium mattirolii, commonly known as the Mattiroli's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the Aspleniaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks in temperate regions. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of its fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

Asplenium mattirolii is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
