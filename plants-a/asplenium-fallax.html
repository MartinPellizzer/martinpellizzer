Asplenium fallax

Asplenium fallax, commonly known as the false bird's nest fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like fronds that resemble a bird's nest, giving it its common name. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome with numerous stolons that produce new plantlets. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture.

Asplenium fallax thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its unique appearance.
