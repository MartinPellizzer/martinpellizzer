Acer × koenighoferae

Acer × koenighoferae, commonly known as the Koenighofer's maple, is a hybrid deciduous tree that exhibits a range of ornamental features.

It typically grows to a height of 10–15 meters and is characterized by its vibrant autumn foliage, which can display shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are palmately lobed, with a coarsely toothed margin, and the tree produces small, aggregate fruits known as samaras. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often planted in gardens and urban landscapes for its seasonal color.
