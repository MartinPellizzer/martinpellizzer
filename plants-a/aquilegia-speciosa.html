Aquilegia speciosa

Aquilegia speciosa, commonly known as the showy columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred petals that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring basal leaves that are palmately divided with lobes. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The showy columbine thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
