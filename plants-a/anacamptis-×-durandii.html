Anacamptis × durandii

Anacamptis × durandii, commonly known as the Durand's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces showy, spurred flowers that are attractive to pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Anacamptis × durandii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
