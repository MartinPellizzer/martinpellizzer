Acalypha commersoniana f. concolor

Acalypha commersoniana f. concolor, commonly known as the smooth-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
