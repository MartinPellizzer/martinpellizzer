Arundo phragmites var. isiaca

Arundo phragmites var. isiaca, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to wetland areas across the globe.

It is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in height. This plant thrives in moist or aquatic environments, often forming dense stands that can dominate wetland ecosystems. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are wind-pollinated and contribute to its reproductive success.
