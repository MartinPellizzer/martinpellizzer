Aster aethiopicus

Aster aethiopicus, commonly known as the Ethiopian aster, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Aster aethiopicus is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
