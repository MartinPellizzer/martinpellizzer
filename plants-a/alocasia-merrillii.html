Alocasia merrillii

Alocasia merrillii, commonly known as the Merril's elephant ear, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter, often with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of white, spadix-like flowers surrounded by a showy purple spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, rich soil.

Alocasia merrillii is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its dramatic foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
