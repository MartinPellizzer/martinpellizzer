Artocarpus vrieseanus var. vrieseanus

Artocarpus vrieseanus var. vrieseanus, commonly known as the "Breadfruit tree," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 25 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces a distinctive fruit with a thick, fibrous outer layer and a starchy, edible interior. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ornamental features.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its historical significance in traditional agriculture.
