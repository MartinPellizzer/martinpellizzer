Asarum yakusimense

Asarum yakusimense, commonly known as the Yakusim wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Korea.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, hooded flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
