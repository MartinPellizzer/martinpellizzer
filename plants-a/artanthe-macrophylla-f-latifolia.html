Artanthe macrophylla f. latifolia

Artanthe macrophylla f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf artanthe, is a cultivar of the artanthe plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 12 inches in length, providing a striking visual impact in gardens. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in the spring, adding a delicate floral display. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This ornamental plant is valued for its foliage and is often used in shaded garden settings for its aesthetic appeal.
