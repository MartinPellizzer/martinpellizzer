Asterocephalus pilosus

Asterocephalus pilosus, commonly known as the hairy-headed aster.

This plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf structures. It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are often white or pale blue in color.

Its hairy foliage helps reduce water loss and protect it from intense sunlight.
