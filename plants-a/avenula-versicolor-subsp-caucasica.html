Avenula versicolor subsp. caucasica

Avenula versicolor subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian variable sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers in spike-like clusters. It is characterized by its variability in leaf coloration, which can range from green to reddish or purplish hues.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
