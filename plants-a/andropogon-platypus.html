Andropogon platypus

Andropogon platypus, commonly known as the flat-leaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, flat leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Andropogon platypus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
