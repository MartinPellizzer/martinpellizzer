Attalea cohune

Attalea cohune, commonly known as the peach palm, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish flowers that develop into edible fruits resembling small peaches. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has been traditionally used for food and oil production.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
