Avena subvillosa

Avena subvillosa, commonly known as slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Avena subvillosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
