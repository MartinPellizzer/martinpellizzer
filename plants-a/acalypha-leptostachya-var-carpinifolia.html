Acalypha leptostachya var. carpinifolia

Acalypha leptostachya var. carpinifolia, commonly known as the flame acalypha, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that range from red to orange, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a popular choice in landscaping.
