Arundinaria ciliata

Arundinaria ciliata, commonly known as the silky bamboo or bamboo grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fine, silky hairs on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring.

Arundinaria ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its graceful, bamboo-like appearance.
