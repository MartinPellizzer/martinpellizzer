Actaea brachycarpa

Actaea brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-fruited actaea, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Actaea brachycarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
