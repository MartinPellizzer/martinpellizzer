Acalypha parvula var. procumbens

Acalypha parvula var. procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate croton, is a low-growing shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features small, ovate leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple underside, which adds visual interest to its foliage. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Due to its compact growth habit, it is suitable for ground cover or as a filler plant in gardens.
