Amblychloa dura

Amblychloa dura, commonly known as hardy grass or African grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky soils. Amblychloa dura is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

It is often used as a forage plant for livestock in arid regions.
