Achillea septentrionalis

Achillea septentrionalis, commonly known as northern yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.

Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
