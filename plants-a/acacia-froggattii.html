Acacia froggattii

Acacia froggattii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened, leaf-like structures that reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Acacia froggattii plays an important role in providing nectar for native pollinators and is a symbol of Australian national identity.
