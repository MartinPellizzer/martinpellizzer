Alchemilla jasiewiczii

Alchemilla jasiewiczii, commonly known as the Polish lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in branched inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Alchemilla jasiewiczii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
