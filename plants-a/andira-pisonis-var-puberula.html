Andira pisonis var. puberula

Andira pisonis var. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent andira, is a tropical plant characterized by its woody structure and climbing habit.

It features elliptical to ovate leaves that are often pubescent, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. The flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and typically have a greenish-white color with a faintly fragrant scent. It produces small, round berries that are initially green and turn red when mature.

This species is native to parts of South America and is often found in humid, shaded environments.
