Ancistrocactus uncinatus subsp. crassihamatus

Ancistrocactus uncinatus subsp.

crassihamatus, commonly known as the thick-hairied ancistrocactus, is a cacti species native to Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, pale green or grayish hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun with well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
