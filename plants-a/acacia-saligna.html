Acacia saligna

Acacia saligna, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its bark is smooth and greyish, often shedding in strips, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia saligna is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of timber and fodder.
