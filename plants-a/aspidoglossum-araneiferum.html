Aspidoglossum araneiferum

Aspidoglossum araneiferum, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spider-like inflorescence that resembles a web or spider's web. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often arranged in a spiral pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Aspidoglossum araneiferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
