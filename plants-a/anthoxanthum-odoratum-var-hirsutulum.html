Anthoxanthum odoratum var. hirsutulum

Anthoxanthum odoratum var. hirsutulum, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it its varietal name. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental value.

It plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
