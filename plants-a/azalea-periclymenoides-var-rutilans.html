Azalea periclymenoides var. rutilans

Azalea periclymenoides var. rutilans, commonly known as the golden azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a yellow or golden hue at the base. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its seasonal floral display.
