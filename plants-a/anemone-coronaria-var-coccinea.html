Anemone coronaria var. coccinea

Anemone coronaria var. coccinea, commonly known as the red anemone, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, typically blooming in spring. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is also known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
