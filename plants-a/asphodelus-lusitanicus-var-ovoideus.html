Asphodelus lusitanicus var. ovoideus

Asphodelus lusitanicus var. ovoideus, commonly known as the oval asphodel, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features grass-like leaves and produces erect, spike-like inflorescences with pale yellow to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is often found in Mediterranean climates.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
