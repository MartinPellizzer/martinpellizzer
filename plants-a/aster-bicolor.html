Aster bicolor

Aster bicolor, commonly known as the New England aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its scientific name reflects its two-colored appearance, with white rays surrounding a dark purple central disk.

Aster bicolor is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers.
