Acourtia oxylepis

Acourtia oxylepis, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acourtia oxylepis is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
