Astragalus surugensis

Astragalus surugensis, commonly known as the Japanese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
