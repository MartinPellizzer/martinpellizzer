Amethystanthus trichocarpus

Amethystanthus trichocarpus, commonly known as the purple-flowered rock orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of violet to purple flowers, which are highly fragrant and attract various pollinators. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular subject in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
