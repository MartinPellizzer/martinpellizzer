Aconitum callibotryon var. hians

Aconitum callibotryon var. hians, commonly known as the Hairy Aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant features large, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Aconitum callibotryon var. hians is known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
