Asplenium sarcodes

Asplenium sarcodes, commonly known as the black spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy fronds that are typically narrow and strap-like in appearance. The plant produces spores on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in structures called sori. Asplenium sarcodes is often found growing in shaded, moist environments such as rock crevices or on the trunks of trees.

It is a non-flowering plant and is widely distributed across temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
