Astragalus onobrychis proles linearifolius

Astragalus onobrychis proles linearifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with narrow, linear leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a forage plant for livestock.
