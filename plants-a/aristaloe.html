Aristaloe

Aristaloe, commonly known as the desert lily, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These succulent plants are characterized by their thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing them to thrive in arid environments. The flowers, which are typically yellow or orange, bloom in spring and are often tubular in shape. Aristaloe species are often used in xeriscaping due to their low water requirements and ornamental value.

They are also popular among succulent enthusiasts for their unique appearance and resilience.
