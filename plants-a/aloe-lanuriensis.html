Aloe lanuriensis

Aloe lanuriensis, commonly known as the Cape aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are covered in a white powdery substance called farina. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which bloom in late winter or early spring. Aloe lanuriensis is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and having a slow growth rate.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
