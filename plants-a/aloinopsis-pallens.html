Aloinopsis pallens

Aloinopsis pallens, commonly known as the White Stemmed Aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive rosette of fleshy, gray-green leaves that store water, typical of arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which bloom in spring. Its root system is adapted for efficient water absorption, allowing it to thrive in sandy soils.

Aloinopsis pallens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
