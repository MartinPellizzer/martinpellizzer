Arrhenatherum bulbosum var. variegatum

Arrhenatherum bulbosum var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated bulbous sweet grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that adds visual interest to gardens. Its variegated leaves, featuring white or yellow markings, make it a distinctive ornamental plant.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and wetlands.
