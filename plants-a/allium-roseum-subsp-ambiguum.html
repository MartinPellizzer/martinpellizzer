Allium roseum subsp. ambiguum

Allium roseum subsp.

ambiguum, commonly known as the purple onion, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features striking purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces small, spherical bulbs that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are grass-like and form a dense rosette near the ground.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
