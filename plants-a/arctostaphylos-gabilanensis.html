Arctostaphylos gabilanensis

Arctostaphylos gabilanensis, commonly known as the Gabilan manzanita, is a shrub native to California.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy red or pink flowers. The plant produces small, leathery berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its unique ecological role and limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
