Alysicarpus longifolius

Alysicarpus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved alysicarpus, is a tropical plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale yellow or white, followed by pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
