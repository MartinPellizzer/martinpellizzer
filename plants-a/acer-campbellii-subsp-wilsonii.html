Acer campbellii subsp. wilsonii

Acer campbellii subsp.

wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves, which are typically five-lobed and have a bright green upper surface.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras that disperse its seeds. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections around the world.
