Astragalus uliginosus

Astragalus uliginosus, commonly known as marsh milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with multiple pairs of leaflets, and its root system is fibrous and adapted to moist soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.

Astragalus uliginosus is also valued for its ecological significance in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its habitat.
