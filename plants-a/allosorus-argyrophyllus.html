Allosorus argyrophyllus

Allosorus argyrophyllus, commonly known as the silver-leafed fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, silver-tinged fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

The fronds are bipinnate, with small, lance-shaped pinnae that contribute to its ornamental value in gardens.
