Abies magnifica var. xanthocarpa

Abies magnifica var. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-cedar or golden-cedar, is a rare and distinctive variant of the white fir.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which contrasts with its dark green needles. Native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and cool, humid climates. Its cones are typically red or brown and contain small, winged seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
