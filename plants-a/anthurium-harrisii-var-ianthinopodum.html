Anthurium harrisii var. ianthinopodum

Anthurium harrisii var. ianthinopodum, commonly known as the red-veined anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins contrasting against its dark green foliage. The plant produces striking spadices, which are typically bright orange or red, surrounded by a showy, white to pink spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of care.
