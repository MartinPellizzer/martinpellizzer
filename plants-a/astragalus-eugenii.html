Astragalus eugenii

Astragalus eugenii, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

Astragalus eugenii plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its ecosystem.
