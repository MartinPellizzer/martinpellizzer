Adiantum glabrum

Adiantum glabrum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant plant belonging to the family Adiantaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that give it a light, airy appearance. The plant is native to various regions in the Northern Hemisphere, including parts of North America, Europe, and Asia. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a glossy sheen, with a unique ability to curl up when dry.

Adiantum glabrum thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found growing along stream banks or in forest undergrowth.
