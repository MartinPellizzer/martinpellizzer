Anemia × paraphyllitidis

Anemia × paraphyllitidis, commonly known as the hybrid hibiscus, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between *Hibiscus acetosella* and *Hibiscus parviflora*.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from deep red to pale pink. The plant is an herbaceous perennial with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid that showcases the genetic diversity within the Hibiscus genus.
