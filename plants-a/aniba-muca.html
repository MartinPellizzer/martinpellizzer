Aniba muca

Aniba muca, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, while the fruit is a small, fleshy drupe.

Aniba muca is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
