Agrimonia incisa

Agrimonia incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agrimony, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, often found in dry grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features upright stems with alternate, narrow, and serrated leaves. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in umbels, blooming from June to August.

Agrimonia incisa is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
