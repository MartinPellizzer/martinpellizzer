Agapetes odontocera var. mizoramensis

Agapetes odontocera var. mizoramensis, commonly known as the Mizoram silverleaf, is a shrub native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are densely covered with silvery-white hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with high humidity.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
