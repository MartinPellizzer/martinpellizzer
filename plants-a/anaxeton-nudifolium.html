Anaxeton nudifolium

Anaxeton nudifolium, commonly known as the smoothleaf thistle, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and lack of leaves during the growing season, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves, when present, are narrow and covered in fine hairs.

Anaxeton nudifolium is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, showcasing resilience through its deep root system and water-conserving features.
