Acalypha almadinensis

Acalypha almadinensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or redroot acalypha, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Acalypha almadinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unique floral display.
