Allium bekeczalicum

Allium bekeczalicum, commonly known as the Hungarian garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous structure, which is composed of layered, fleshy leaves. The plant produces upright, hollow stems that bear narrow, grass-like leaves and umbels of white or pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are used in traditional medicine and cuisine, valued for their pungent aroma and potential health benefits.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.
