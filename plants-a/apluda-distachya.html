Apluda distachya

Apluda distachya, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Apluda distachya is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
