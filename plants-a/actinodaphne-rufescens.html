Actinodaphne rufescens

Actinodaphne rufescens, commonly known as the red-leafed laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that turn reddish in color when mature. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
