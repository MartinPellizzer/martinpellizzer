Alsophila excelsa var. cooperi

Alsophila excelsa var. cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's tree fern, is a striking member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. This variant is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The fronds are typically a deep green, with a smooth, waxy surface that gives the plant a sleek appearance.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests or on mountain slopes.
