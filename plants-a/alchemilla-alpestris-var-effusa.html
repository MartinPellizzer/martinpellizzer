Alchemilla alpestris var. effusa

Alchemilla alpestris var. effusa, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features broad, velvety, shield-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish down. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and appear in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Alchemilla alpestris var. effusa thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.
