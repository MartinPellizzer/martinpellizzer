Alyxia rubricaulis subsp. poyaensis

Alyxia rubricaulis subsp.

poyaensis, commonly known as the Poya's coralwood, is a shrub native to the humid lowland forests of southeastern Queensland, Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive red-tinged stems and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape with a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a humid, tropical climate to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
