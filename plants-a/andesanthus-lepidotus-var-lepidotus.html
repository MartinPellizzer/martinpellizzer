Andesanthus lepidotus var. lepidotus

Andesanthus lepidotus var. lepidotus, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a striking plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from red to purple, often with contrasting patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at elevations between 3,000 and 5,000 meters. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand cold and strong winds.

The species is known for its resilience and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens with suitable conditions.
