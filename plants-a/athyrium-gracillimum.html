Athyrium gracillimum

Athyrium gracillimum, commonly known as the slender lady fern, is a delicate fern species native to eastern Asia.

It features finely divided, arching fronds that are typically 30 to 60 centimeters long. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Athyrium gracillimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
