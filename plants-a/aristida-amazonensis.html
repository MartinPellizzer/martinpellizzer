Aristida amazonensis

Aristida amazonensis, commonly known as the Amazon grass, is a perennial grass species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color. The plant thrives in wetland and swampy habitats, often forming dense mats that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture that contributes to its ecological resilience.

Aristida amazonensis plays a vital role in the biodiversity of its native ecosystems, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
