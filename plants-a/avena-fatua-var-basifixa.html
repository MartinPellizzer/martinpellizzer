Avena fatua var. basifixa

Avena fatua var. basifixa, commonly known as the basal-fixed wild oat, is a variant of the wild oat species characterized by its unique growth habit.

This plant is a perennial grass that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as fields and roadsides, and is often considered a weed. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces spikelets that bear small, wind-dispersed seeds. The basal fixation refers to the way its shoots emerge from the base of the plant, creating a dense, low-growing structure.

It is adapted to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and invasive species in many regions.
