Aulacospermum darwasicum

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, particularly Namibia and Angola. The plant is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves, which help it store water in its native habitat. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.
