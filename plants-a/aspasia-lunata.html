Aspasia lunata

Aspasia lunata, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is typically grown as a climbing vine or shrub. Its botanical name reflects its association with the moon due to its blooming habit.

Aspasia lunata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique nighttime display.
