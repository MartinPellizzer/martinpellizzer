Adenia reticulata var. cinerea

Adenia reticulata var. cinerea, commonly known as the pale adenia, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a pale green upper surface and a hairy underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, elongated fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
