Alchemilla alpina subsp. basaltica

Alchemilla alpina subsp.

basaltica, commonly known as the basalt alkanet, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found on slopes and meadows in mountainous areas. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in rock gardens and alpine plantings.
