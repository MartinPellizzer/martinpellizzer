Atriplex hispanica

Atriplex hispanica, commonly known as Spanish saltbush, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, green to grayish-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a hardy species in harsh conditions.

Atriplex hispanica is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and tolerate drought.
