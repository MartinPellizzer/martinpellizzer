Amaryllis carnosa

Amaryllis carnosa, commonly known as the pink amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces a single, upright stem that bears several flowers, each with six petals and a prominent central spadix. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and arranged in a rosette pattern around the base.

Amaryllis carnosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with adequate sunlight.
