Artemisia campestris var. scouleriana

Artemisia campestris var. scouleriana, commonly known as Scouler's wormwood, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage and slender, branched stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are typically clustered in dense, spherical clusters. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
