Allium chevsuricum

Allium chevsuricum, commonly known as the Afghan leek, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and clusters of pale blue to violet flowers. The plant produces small, bulbous structures that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium chevsuricum is valued for its aromatic leaves and its role in both ecological and cultural contexts.
