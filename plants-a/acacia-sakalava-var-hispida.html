Acacia sakalava var. hispida

Acacia sakalava var. hispida, commonly known as the Madagascar thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its spiny, compound leaves and yellow, globular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native environment.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its ecosystem.
