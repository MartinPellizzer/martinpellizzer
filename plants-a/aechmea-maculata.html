Aechmea maculata

Aechmea maculata, commonly known as the spotted bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant features vibrant, long-lasting bracts that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its foliage is often marked with distinctive spots or stripes, giving it its common name.

Aechmea maculata is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and striking appearance.

Morphology

Aechmea maculata has rosette-shaped leaves that are typically narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem.

The leaves are usually green with white or silver markings, and they can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant has a short, fibrous stem with a rough, bark-like texture, and it grows upright from the base. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by colorful bracts, often in shades of red or orange, and bloom during the summer months, relying on wind pollination.

The fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that disperses seeds through wind or by attaching to animals.
