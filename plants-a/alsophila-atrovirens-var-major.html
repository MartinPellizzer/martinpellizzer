Alsophila atrovirens var. major

Alsophila atrovirens var. major, commonly known as the black-veined tree fern, is a variant of the tree fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, frond-like leaves that are dark green with prominent, contrasting veins. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, moist forest areas. Its fronds are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the trunk, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
