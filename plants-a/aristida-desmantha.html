Aristida desmantha

Aristida desmantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf threeawn, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color. The plant thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, open woodlands, and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough, hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

Aristida desmantha plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
