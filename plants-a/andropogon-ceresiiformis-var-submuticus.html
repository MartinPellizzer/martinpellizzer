Andropogon ceresiiformis var. submuticus

Andropogon ceresiiformis var. submuticus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and compact growth habit, often forming dense stands in wetland habitats. The plant typically grows in marshes, swamps, and other moist environments, showing a preference for well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
