Agave haseloffii

Agave haseloffii, commonly known as the Desert Agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces a tall, branched flower stalk that bears yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in the spring. Agave haseloffii is adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its leaves and stems.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appearance.
