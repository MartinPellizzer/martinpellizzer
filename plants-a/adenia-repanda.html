Adenia repanda

Adenia repanda, commonly known as the smooth adenia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves. The leaves are usually ovate or elliptic in shape, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy appearance. It produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright orange berries.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas such as rainforests and understoreys.
