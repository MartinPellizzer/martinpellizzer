Aesculus × spectabilis

Aesculus × spectabilis, commonly known as the horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, white or pinkish bracts that resemble flowers. The tree produces spiny, capsule-like fruits containing bright red, shiny seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soils.

The horse chestnut is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
