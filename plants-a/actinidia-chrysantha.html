Actinidia chrysantha

Actinidia chrysantha, commonly known as the golden honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, green stems and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that are yellow-orange in color. These berries are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a food source for wildlife.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both beauty and ecological benefit.
