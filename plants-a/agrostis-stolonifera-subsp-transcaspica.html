Agrostis stolonifera subsp. transcaspica

Agrostis stolonifera subsp.

transcaspica, commonly known as the Transcaspian bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and fine, tufted leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous slender, two-rowed spikelets. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
