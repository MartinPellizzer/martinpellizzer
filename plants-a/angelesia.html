Angelesia

Angelesia, commonly known as the angel's trumpet, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apocynaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow, orange, or red. The plants are native to South America and are often cultivated for their ornamental value. They are evergreen shrubs or small trees with opposite, simple leaves.

Angelesia species are also noted for their toxic properties, containing cardiac glycosides that can be harmful to humans and animals.
