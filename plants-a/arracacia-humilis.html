Arracacia humilis

Arracacia humilis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Arracacia humilis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
