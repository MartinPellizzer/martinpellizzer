Achillea × reichardtiana

Achillea × reichardtiana, commonly known as the hybrid yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, flat-topped flower heads that range in color from white to pink. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate floral display to the landscape.
