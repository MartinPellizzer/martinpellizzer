Allium taschkenticum

Allium taschkenticum, commonly known as the Tashkent garlic, is a bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its cluster of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces edible bulbs that are similar in appearance to garlic but have a milder flavor. Its leaves are grass-like and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length.

Allium taschkenticum is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and culinary uses.
