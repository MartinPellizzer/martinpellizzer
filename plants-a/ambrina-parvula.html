Ambrina parvula

Ambrina parvula, commonly known as the dwarf ambrina, is a small flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Ambrina parvula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
