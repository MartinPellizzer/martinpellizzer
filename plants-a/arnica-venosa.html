Arnica venosa

Arnica venosa, commonly known as the veined arnica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features upright, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Arnica venosa is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
