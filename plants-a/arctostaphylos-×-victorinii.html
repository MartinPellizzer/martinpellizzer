Arctostaphylos × victorinii

Arctostaphylos × victorinii, commonly known as the Victorin's manzanita, is a hybrid shrub native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild, wet winters and warm, dry summers.

Its small, leathery leaves and persistent fruiting bodies make it a distinctive and resilient species in its native habitat.
