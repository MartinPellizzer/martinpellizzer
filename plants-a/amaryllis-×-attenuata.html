Amaryllis × attenuata

Amaryllis × attenuata, commonly known as the pink bell amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis bellona and Amaryllis paradisica.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to the landscape.
