Anacamptis × dafnii nothovar. solanoi

Anacamptis × dafnii nothovar. solanoi, commonly known as the Solano's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-blue floral spikes with prominent yellow markings. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its flowers are borne on tall, slender stems and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This nothovar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and showy blossoms.
