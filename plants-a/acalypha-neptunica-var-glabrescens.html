Acalypha neptunica var. glabrescens

Acalypha neptunica var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky copperleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The variety glabrescens is distinguished by its reduced pubescence compared to other forms of Acalypha neptunica.
