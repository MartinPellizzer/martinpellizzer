Acacia tenuifolia var. veraensis

Acacia tenuifolia var. veraensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, feathery foliage and yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping in dry climates.
