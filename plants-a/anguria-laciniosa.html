Anguria laciniosa

Anguria laciniosa, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, which are typically followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. Its leaves are modified into spines, providing protection from herbivores.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in various cultures.
