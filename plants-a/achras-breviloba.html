Achras breviloba

Achras breviloba, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical plant native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a dense, rounded canopy. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, star-shaped fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic to ovate in shape, with a rough texture.

The plant is well adapted to dry and semi-arid climates, making it a resilient species in tropical regions.
