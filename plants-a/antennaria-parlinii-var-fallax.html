Antennaria parlinii var. fallax

Antennaria parlinii var. fallax, commonly known as the fallax mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
