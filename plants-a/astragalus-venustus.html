Astragalus venustus

Astragalus venustus, commonly known as the beautiful milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, yellow or greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Astragalus venustus is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitats.
