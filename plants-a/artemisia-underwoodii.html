Artemisia underwoodii

Artemisia underwoodii, commonly known as the underwood sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and chaparral habitats. Its leaves contain volatile oils that contribute to its strong, minty fragrance.

Artemisia underwoodii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
