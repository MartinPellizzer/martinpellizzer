Aechmea corallina

Aechmea corallina, commonly known as the coral pine, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces vibrant red flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette, often resembling a coral-like structure. Its foliage is typically dark green with a waxy texture, and it thrives in humid, tropical environments.

Aechmea corallina is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea corallina has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, stiff, and leathery, arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem, with a bluish-green color and a waxy coating.

The stem is short and thick, with a smooth, dark green bark that grows upward from the base. The plant produces inflorescences with vibrant red or orange flowers, arranged in a spike, which bloom during the summer months and are pollinated by bees and other insects. The fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure containing small seeds, which are dispersed by birds and water.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the surrounding soil.
