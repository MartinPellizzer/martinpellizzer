Amelanchier ovalis var. genuina

Amelanchier ovalis var. genuina, commonly known as the Allegheny serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It features oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow or red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a dark red or purple color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for native landscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological and ornamental purposes.
