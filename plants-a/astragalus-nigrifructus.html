Astragalus nigrifructus

Astragalus nigrifructus, commonly known as black-fruited milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive black or dark purple pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Astragalus nigrifructus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and medicinal research.
