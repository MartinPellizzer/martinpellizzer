Astragalus uncialis

Astragalus uncialis, commonly known as the small milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native range.
