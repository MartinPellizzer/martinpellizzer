Arctostaphylos novoleontis

Arctostaphylos novoleontis, commonly known as the Novoleontis manzanita, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in white or pink hues. The plant produces small, leathery berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

Arctostaphylos novoleontis is often found in chaparral and coastal sage scrub habitats.
