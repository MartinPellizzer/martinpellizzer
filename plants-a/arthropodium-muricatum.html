Arthropodium muricatum

Arthropodium muricatum, commonly known as the mountain ginger or *Tuliparia*, is a perennial herb native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and striking, showy inflorescences that resemble large, fleshy tulips. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and shady borders.
