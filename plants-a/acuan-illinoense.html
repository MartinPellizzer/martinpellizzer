Acuan illinoense

Acuan illinoense, commonly known as the Illinois waterleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy margin and a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in clusters during the spring. Its roots are tuberous and often used in traditional medicine.

Acuan illinoense thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
