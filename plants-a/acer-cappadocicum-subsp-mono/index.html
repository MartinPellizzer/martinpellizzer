Acer Cappadocicum Subsp. Mono

Acer cappadocicum subsp. mono is a unique variant of the maple tree found primarily in the eastern Mediterranean region. It is distinguished by its distinctive single-lobed leaves that contrast with the typical multi-lobed structure of other maple species. This subspecies thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its presence in ancient landscapes highlights its historical significance in the region's flora. Despite its limited distribution, it remains an important subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
