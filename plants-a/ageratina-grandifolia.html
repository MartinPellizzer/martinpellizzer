Ageratina grandifolia

Ageratina grandifolia, commonly known as the whiteface ageratum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage.

Ageratina grandifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
