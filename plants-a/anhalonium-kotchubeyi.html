Anhalonium kotchubeyi

Anhalonium kotchubeyi, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating remarkable drought tolerance. Its leaves are often reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss.

Anhalonium kotchubeyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
