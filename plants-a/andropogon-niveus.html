Andropogon niveus

Andropogon niveus, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescence, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in the summer, producing small, pale blue to white flowers.

Andropogon niveus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in native plant landscaping.
