Acacia leptoneura var. eremophila

Acacia leptoneura var. eremophila, commonly known as the desert wattle, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant has small, leathery phyllodes that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry conditions. It is well-suited to harsh environments and is often found in sandy soils.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soils in degraded landscapes.
