Acanthus dusenii

Acanthus dusenii, commonly known as the Sierra acanthus, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and showy, spires of violet-blue flowers. This plant typically grows in rocky habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in California and parts of Nevada, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
