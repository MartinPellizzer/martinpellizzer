Athyrium ramosii

Athyrium ramosii, commonly known as the hairy maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce multiple upright shoots.

The leaves are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive appearance that makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
