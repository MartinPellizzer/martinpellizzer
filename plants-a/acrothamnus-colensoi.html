Acrothamnus colensoi

Acrothamnus colensoi, commonly known as Colenso's acrothamnus, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense heads, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. It is often found in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Acrothamnus colensoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
