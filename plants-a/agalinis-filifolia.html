Agalinis filifolia

Agalinis filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant has slender, hairless stems and small, opposite leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences and are usually pink to purple in color.

Agalinis filifolia is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in areas with high sunlight exposure.
