Agathophytum humifusum

Agathophytum humifusum, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its desert habitat. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to store water in a harsh environment.

Agathophytum humifusum is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its resilience in extreme conditions.
