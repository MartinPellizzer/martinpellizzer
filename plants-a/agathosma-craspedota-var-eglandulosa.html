Agathosma craspedota var. eglandulosa

Agathosma craspedota var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the Cape rose or sweet shrub, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a distinctive, pleasant fragrance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
