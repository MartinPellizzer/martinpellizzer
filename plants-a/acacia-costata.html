Acacia costata

Acacia costata, commonly known as the serrated wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Its bark is rough and fissured, and it often grows in coastal or sandy soils.

Acacia costata is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.
