Aster ageratoides var. ovatus

Aster ageratoides var. ovatus, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid regions.

The varietal form ovatus distinguishes it by its more rounded, ovate leaves compared to other varieties of Aster ageratoides.
