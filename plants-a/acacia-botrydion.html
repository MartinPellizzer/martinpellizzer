Acacia botrydion

Acacia botrydion, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its distinctive, flat, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing remarkable drought tolerance.

The bark and leaves have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.
