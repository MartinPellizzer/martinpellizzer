Avena hoppeana

Avena hoppeana, commonly known as Hoppe's oat, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, which are wind-pollinated. Avena hoppeana thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and on slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
