Acantholimon cataonicum

Acantholimon cataonicum, commonly known as the Cataonic thornweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features dense, spiny leaves and produces small, violet-colored flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its botanical structure reflects its evolutionary adaptation to withstand arid conditions and grazing pressure.
