Arachis paraguariensis subsp. paraguariensis

Arachis paraguariensis subsp.

paraguariensis, commonly known as the peanut plant, is a species of legume native to South America. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate and hairy.

The plant produces flowers that are typically yellow or pink, followed by the development of pods containing edible seeds. The seeds, known as peanuts, are rich in protein and oil, making the plant economically significant.

It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is cultivated for both its nutritional value and agricultural importance.
