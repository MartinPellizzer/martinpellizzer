Anemone wendelboi

Anemone wendelboi, commonly known as the Tibetan anemone, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and visually striking member of the Ranunculaceae family.
