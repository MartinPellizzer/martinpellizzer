Acalypha obtusifolia

Acalypha obtusifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian velvet, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and purplish or reddish on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Acalypha obtusifolia is also known for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage.
