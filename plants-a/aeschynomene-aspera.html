Aeschynomene aspera

Aeschynomene aspera, commonly known as rough sicklegrass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Aeschynomene aspera is also valued for its potential use in forage and erosion control.
