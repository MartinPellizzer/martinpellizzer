Acacia quadrilateralis

Acacia quadrilateralis, commonly known as the quadrilateral wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of many Acacia species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
