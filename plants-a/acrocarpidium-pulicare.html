Acrocarpidium pulicare

Acrocarpidium pulicare, commonly known as the slender moss, is a small, acrocarpous moss that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It has a low-growing, cushion-like appearance with dense, dark green shoots. The plant produces sporophytes that are typically short and stout, with a capsule that is often reddish-brown in color. Its leaves are narrowly lanceolate and gradually taper to a point, with a smooth, glossy surface.

Acrocarpidium pulicare is often found in forest floors and rocky crevices, contributing to soil stabilization and moisture retention.
