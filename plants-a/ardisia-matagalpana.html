Ardisia matagalpana

Ardisia matagalpana, commonly known as the Matagalpa ardisia, is a shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes many flowering plants adapted to acidic soils. The plant produces small, glossy green leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers followed by bright red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
