Apium ammi f. filamentosum

Apium ammi f. filamentosum, commonly known as the filamentoous cow parsley, is a botanical variant of the common cow parsley.

It is characterized by its slender, filamentous stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This form is notable for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
