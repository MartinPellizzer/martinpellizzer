Arundinaria inuii

Arundinaria inuii, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Arundinaria inuii is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
