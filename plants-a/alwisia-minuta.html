Alwisia minuta

Alwisia minuta, commonly known as the dwarf alwisia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with slender, branching stems and tiny, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. Alwisia minuta thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
