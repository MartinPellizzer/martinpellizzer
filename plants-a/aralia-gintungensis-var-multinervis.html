Aralia gintungensis var. multinervis

Aralia gintungensis var. multinervis, commonly known as the Philippine giant aralia, is a striking plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length, with a feathery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by spiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
