Arundinaria teijiroana

Arundinaria teijiroana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that form dense clumps, typical of many bamboo species. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated, and its leaves are long and narrow with a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
