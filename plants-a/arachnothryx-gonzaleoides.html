Arachnothryx gonzaleoides

Arachnothryx gonzaleoides, commonly known as the Brazilian star vine, is a climbing shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
