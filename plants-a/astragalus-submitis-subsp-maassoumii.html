Astragalus submitis subsp. maassoumii

Astragalus submitis subsp.

maassoumii, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
