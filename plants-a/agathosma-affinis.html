Agathosma affinis

Agathosma affinis, commonly known as the Cape aster, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are aromatic and have been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

Agathosma affinis is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
