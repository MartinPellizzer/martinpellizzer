Acianthera cristata

Acianthera cristata, commonly known as the cristate acianthera, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive crested or wavy pattern on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare orchids.
