Anthemis samia

Anthemis samia, commonly known as the Spanish needle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, thriving in dry, sunny environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are surrounded by bracts resembling tiny needles, giving it its common name. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The species is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
