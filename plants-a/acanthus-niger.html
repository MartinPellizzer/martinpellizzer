Acanthus niger

Acanthus niger, commonly known as black acanthus, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces tall spikes of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Acanthus niger is a perennial shrub that thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
