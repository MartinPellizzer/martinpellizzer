Aristida canariensis

Aristida canariensis, commonly known as the Canary grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Canary Islands.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous tiny florets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture.

Aristida canariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
