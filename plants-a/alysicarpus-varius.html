Alysicarpus varius

Alysicarpus varius, commonly known as the variable burr legume, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that enclose its seeds. It often grows in disturbed soils and can be found in agricultural areas or along roadsides.

Due to its ability to fix nitrogen, it is sometimes used in soil enrichment and as a forage plant.
