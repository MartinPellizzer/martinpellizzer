Acacia aneura var. stenocarpa

Acacia aneura var. stenocarpa, commonly known as the River Red Gum, is a hardy, drought-tolerant tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and small, yellow flowers that appear in summer. The tree produces flat, leathery pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is typically reddish-brown and peels in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various native animals.
