Artemisia granatensis

Artemisia granatensis, commonly known as the Granada wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring silver-gray, aromatic foliage with lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, contain compounds like thujone and camphor, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Artemisia granatensis is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, demonstrating its adaptability to harsh environments.
