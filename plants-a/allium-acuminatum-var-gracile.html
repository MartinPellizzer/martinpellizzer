Allium acuminatum var. gracile

Allium acuminatum var. gracile, commonly known as the slender leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, cylindrical leaves that are typically bluish-green in color and can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild garlic-like aroma.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and dry, well-drained soils.
