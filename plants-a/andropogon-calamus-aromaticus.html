Andropogon calamus-aromaticus

Andropogon calamus-aromaticus, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stalks that emit a pleasant, spicy scent when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft texture and a distinctive, sweet fragrance.

Sweetgrass is often used in traditional ceremonies and for crafting, due to its calming and symbolic properties.
