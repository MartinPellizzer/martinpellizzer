Ardisia crassiuscula

Ardisia crassiuscula, commonly known as the glossy privet, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters. The plant features dark green, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement and have a leathery texture. It produces small, white, five-petaled flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
