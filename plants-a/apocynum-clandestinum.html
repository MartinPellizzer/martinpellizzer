Apocynum clandestinum

Apocynum clandestinum, commonly known as the Indian tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its leaves are ovate and have a rough texture, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
