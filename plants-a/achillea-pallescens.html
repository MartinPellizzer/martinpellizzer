Achillea pallescens

Achillea pallescens, commonly known as the pale yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
