Ammi elatum

Ammi elatum, commonly known as the tall cowpea, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in loose spikes, which are followed by slender, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Ammi elatum is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
