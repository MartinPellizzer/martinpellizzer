Abies fansipanensis

Abies fansipanensis, commonly known as the Fan mountain fir, is a rare and endemic species native to the high altitudes of Vietnam.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil, typically found in mountainous regions. Its botanical features include a slender trunk and a conical shape, typical of many fir species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
