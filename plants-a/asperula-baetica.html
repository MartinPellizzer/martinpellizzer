Asperula baetica

Asperula baetica, commonly known as the Spanish heath, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are small, leathery, and arranged in a dense, mat-like structure.

Asperula baetica is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.
