Alyssum chrysanthum

Alyssum chrysanthum, commonly known as golden alyssum, is a low-growing annual plant native to southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has silvery-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, and its stems are often branched and upright. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum chrysanthum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs and hoverflies.
