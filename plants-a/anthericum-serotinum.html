Anthericum serotinum

Anthericum serotinum, commonly known as the late anthericum, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, umbrella-shaped inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are broad, arrowhead-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Anthericum serotinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
