Anagallis keniensis var. aberdarensis

Anagallis keniensis var. aberdarensis, commonly known as the Aberdare waterweed, is a flowering plant native to the Aberdare Range in Kenya.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist, rocky habitats. The plant produces delicate, spurred flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, often covered in fine hairs.

This variant is noted for its specific ecological preferences and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
