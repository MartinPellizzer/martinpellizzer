Ancylostemon flabellatus

Ancylostemon flabellatus, commonly known as the fan-leaf cycad, is a unique cycad species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fan-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant has a stout, unbranched trunk that is often covered in a thick, fibrous sheath. Its reproductive structures include a large, cone-like structure that produces both male and female spores.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered plant species.
