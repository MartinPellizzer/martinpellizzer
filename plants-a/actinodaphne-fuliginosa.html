Actinodaphne fuliginosa

Actinodaphne fuliginosa, commonly known as the black sand laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. It produces small, clustered white flowers followed by spherical, black berries.

Actinodaphne fuliginosa is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local cultures.
