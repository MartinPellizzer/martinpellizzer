Ananas comosus var. bracteatus

Ananas comosus var. bracteatus, commonly known as the pineapple guava, is a unique variant of the pineapple plant.

It is characterized by its dense, bract-like structures that enclose the fruit, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically less than an inch in diameter. These fruits have a sweet, aromatic flavor and are often used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and exhibits traits that make it distinct from the typical pineapple.
