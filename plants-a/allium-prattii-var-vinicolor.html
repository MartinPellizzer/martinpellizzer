Allium prattii var. vinicolor

Allium prattii var. vinicolor, commonly known as the purple garlic.

This perennial bulbous plant is native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, edible bulbs similar to garlic.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
