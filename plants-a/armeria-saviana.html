Armeria saviana

Armeria saviana, commonly known as the sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime climates.

Armeria saviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in coastal landscaping.
