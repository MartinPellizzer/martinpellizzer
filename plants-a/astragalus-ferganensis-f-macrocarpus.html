Astragalus ferganensis f. macrocarpus

Astragalus ferganensis f. macrocarpus, commonly known as the Fergana milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its subspecies, f. macrocarpus, is distinguished by its larger, more robust pods compared to the typical form.

This plant plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
