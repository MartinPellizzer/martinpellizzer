Arctostaphylos drupacea

Arctostaphylos drupacea, commonly known as the dwarf manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky habitats.

Arctostaphylos drupacea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
