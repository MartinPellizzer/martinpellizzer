Acalypha rhombifolia

Acalypha rhombifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped to rhombic leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by three-lobed, red or orange seed pods. Acalypha rhombifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.
