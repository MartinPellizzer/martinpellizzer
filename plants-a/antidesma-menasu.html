Antidesma menasu

Antidesma menasu, commonly known as the mountain antidesma, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
