Agauria salicifolia f. glandulifera

Agauria salicifolia f. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular willow-leaf agauria, is a botanical variant of the agauria species.

It is characterized by its willow-like leaves, which are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant features small, clustered inflorescences that produce tiny, greenish-yellow flowers. Its foliage often bears glandular dots, which give it its specific epithet "glandulifera.".

This variant is typically found in moist, temperate habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
