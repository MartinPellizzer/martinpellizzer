Adesmia cytisoides var. pugionata

Adesmia cytisoides var. pugionata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burro's tail, is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, narrow, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, bean-like seed pods that are often used in traditional medicine. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system and reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for xeriscaping and ecological restoration in dryland environments.
