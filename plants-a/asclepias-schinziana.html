Asclepias schinziana

Asclepias schinziana, commonly known as Schinz's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to reddish-orange flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is typical of the milkweed genus. Schinz's milkweed is a host plant for the monarch butterfly, providing essential nourishment for monarch caterpillars.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
