Atractylis lanata

Atractylis lanata, commonly known as the hairy cow-wheat, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.
