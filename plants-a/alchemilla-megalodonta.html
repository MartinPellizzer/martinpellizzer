Alchemilla megalodonta

Alchemilla megalodonta, commonly known as the giant lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters. Its roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional herbal medicine.
