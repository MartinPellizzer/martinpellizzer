Allium roseum var. confertum

Allium roseum var. confertum, commonly known as the clustered rose garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its ornamental bulbs, which are often used in gardens for their aesthetic value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to many landscapes.

Due to its hardiness and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking both beauty and resilience.
