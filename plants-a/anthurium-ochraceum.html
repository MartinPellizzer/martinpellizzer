Anthurium ochraceum

Anthurium ochraceum, commonly known as the yellow anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a yellowish tinge. The plant produces vibrant red spadices, which are the inflorescences that house the small, white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight to flourish.

Anthurium ochraceum is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and unique floral structure.
