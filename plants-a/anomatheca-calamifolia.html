Anomatheca calamifolia

Anomatheca calamifolia, commonly known as the sickleleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sickle-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable addition to native plant gardens.
