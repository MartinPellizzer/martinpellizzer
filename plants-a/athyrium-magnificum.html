Athyrium magnificum

Athyrium magnificum, commonly known as the magnificent male fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its large, elegant fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features a distinctive, short rhizome with a dense cluster of roots, and its leaves are typically divided into a series of pinnae with a serrated margin. Athyrium magnificum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories where it contributes to the biodiversity of the ecosystem.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for botanical gardens and private collections.
