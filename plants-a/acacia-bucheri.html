Acacia bucheri

Acacia bucheri, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched structure and its distinctive, feathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. Acacia bucheri is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suited to dry climates.

Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally in various cultural and medicinal practices.
