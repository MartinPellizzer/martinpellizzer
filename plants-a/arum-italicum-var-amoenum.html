Arum italicum var. amoenum

Arum italicum var. amoenum, commonly known as the Italian arum or the red dragon arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a reddish-purple underside. The plant produces striking, spatulate-shaped flowers that are usually a deep red or purple, surrounded by a showy, leaf-like spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also known for its ability to produce a toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
