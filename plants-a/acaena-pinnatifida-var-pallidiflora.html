Acaena pinnatifida var. pallidiflora

Acaena pinnatifida var. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered burr bush, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
