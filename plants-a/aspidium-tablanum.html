Aspidium tablanum

Aspidium tablanum, commonly known as the African shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically divided into segments with a shield-like appearance. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to rocky or soil substrates.

Aspidium tablanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
