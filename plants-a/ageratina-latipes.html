Ageratina latipes

Ageratina latipes, commonly known as the white crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, fluffy white or pale purple flower heads. The leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
