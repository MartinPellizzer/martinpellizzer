Alysicarpus glumaceus var. styracifolius

Alysicarpus glumaceus var. styracifolius, commonly known as the styracifolius variant of the genus Alysicarpus, is a tropical plant characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for leguminous plants that often form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish in color. Its fruit is a legume, containing several seeds, and it is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

This variant is notable for its potential use in forage and soil improvement due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
