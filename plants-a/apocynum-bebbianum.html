Apocynum bebbianum

Apocynum bebbianum, commonly known as Bebb's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a perennial herb with showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant contains toxic cardiac glycosides, which can be harmful to humans and livestock. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a hairy texture.

Bebb's milkweed is often found in moist soils along stream banks and in wet meadows.
