Allocasuarina thuyoides

Allocasuarina thuyoides, commonly known as the silver she-oak, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, cylindrical, scale-like leaves that are densely packed along the branches, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its seeds are enclosed in woody cones and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed soils.

Allocasuarina thuyoides is well adapted to coastal and sandy environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
