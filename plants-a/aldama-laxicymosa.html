Aldama laxicymosa

Aldama laxicymosa, commonly known as the Mexican broom, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves with a feathery appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Aldama laxicymosa is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in such habitats.
