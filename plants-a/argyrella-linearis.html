Argyrella linearis

Argyrella linearis, commonly known as the silverleaf lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value comes from its delicate appearance and long blooming period.

Argyrella linearis is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
