Aglaia zippelii

Aglaia zippelii, commonly known as the Zippel's aglaia, is a member of the Meliaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing several seeds.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
