Allium kasteki

Allium kasteki, commonly known as the Kastehi onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its robust, fleshy bulbs and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient plant suitable for cultivation in well-drained soils.

Allium kasteki is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its potential culinary and medicinal uses.
