Astragalus conjunctus var. rickardii

Astragalus conjunctus var. rickardii, commonly known as Rickard's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
