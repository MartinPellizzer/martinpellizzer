Allium porrum subsp. euampeloprasum

Allium porrum subsp.

euampeloprasum, commonly known as the giant leek, is a subspecies of the leek genus characterized by its large, elongated leaves and bulbous stem. It is native to the eastern Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with broad, sword-shaped foliage that forms a dense rosette. Its flowers are small, white or pale purple, and arranged in a loose umbel, typical of the Allium genus.

This subspecies is valued for its mild, onion-like flavor and is often used in culinary applications.
