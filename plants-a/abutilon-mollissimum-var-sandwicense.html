Abutilon mollissimum var. sandwicense

Abutilon mollissimum var. sandwicense, commonly known as the Kauaʻi hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Kauaʻi in Hawaii.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a prominent yellow or orange center. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

This species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
