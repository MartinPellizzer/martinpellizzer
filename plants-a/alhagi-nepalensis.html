Alhagi nepalensis

Alhagi nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal horsegram, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soils. It has small, trifoliate leaves and produces clusters of small white or pale pink flowers.

Alhagi nepalensis is valued for its nutritional content and is used as both a food source and for soil improvement.
