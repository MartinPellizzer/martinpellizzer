Archidendron fagifolium var. fagifolium

Archidendron fagifolium var. fagifolium, commonly known as the honey locust, is a deciduous legume native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves that are pinnately arranged, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, green, pod-like fruits that contain several flat, edible seeds. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Its flowers are typically yellow or white, adding ornamental value to the landscape.
