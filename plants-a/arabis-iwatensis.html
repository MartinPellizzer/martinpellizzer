Arabis iwatensis

Arabis iwatensis, commonly known as the Izu rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. This species is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
