Abelia integrifolia

Abelia integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are smooth and slightly glossy. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Abelia integrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
