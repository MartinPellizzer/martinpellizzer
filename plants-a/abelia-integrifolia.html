Abelia integrifolia

Abelia integrifolia, commonly known as smooth abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy, providing attractive foliage throughout the growing season. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Abelia integrifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and hummingbirds.
