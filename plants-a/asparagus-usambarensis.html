Asparagus usambarensis

Asparagus usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambarensis asparagus, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its stout, fibrous roots and sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
