Asarca sceptrum

Asarca sceptrum, commonly known as the sacred rod, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions. The plant features finely divided, feather-like leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal. Its small, white flowers are arranged in dense clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its medicinal and cultural significance, Asarca sceptrum has been historically valued in traditional herbal practices.
