Artanacetum fasciculatum

Artanacetum fasciculatum, commonly known as narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple or pinkish flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its flowers are composite, consisting of many small florets arranged in a spherical shape.

Artanacetum fasciculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
