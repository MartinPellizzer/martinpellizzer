Arctostaphylos mucronata

Arctostaphylos mucronata, commonly known as the smoothleaf manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of pink to red flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Arctostaphylos mucronata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
