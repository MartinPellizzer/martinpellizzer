Acer pseudoplatanus f. costorphinense

Acer pseudoplatanus f. costorphinense, commonly known as the Costorphine maple, is a rare horticultural form of the common maple.

It is characterized by its striking, lobed leaves that display a vibrant green on the upper surface and a contrasting white or pale underside. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its foliage turns a brilliant yellow in autumn, making it a desirable ornamental plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.
