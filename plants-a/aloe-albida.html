Aloe albida

Aloe albida, commonly known as the white aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick, fleshy, and covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value. Aloe albida is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and indoor gardening.
