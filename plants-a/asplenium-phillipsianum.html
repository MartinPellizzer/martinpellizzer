Asplenium phillipsianum

Asplenium phillipsianum, commonly known as the shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with spore-producing structures on the underside.

Asplenium phillipsianum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
