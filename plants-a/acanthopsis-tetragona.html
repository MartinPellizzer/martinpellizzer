Acanthopsis tetragona

Acanthopsis tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled acanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive four-angled stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
