Allium inderiense

Allium inderiense, commonly known as the Inder Island leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the island of Inder in the southern Pacific.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and umbels of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures similar to onions, which are used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.
