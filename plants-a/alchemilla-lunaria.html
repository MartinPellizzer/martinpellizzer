Alchemilla lunaria

Alchemilla lunaria, commonly known as mooncup, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, silver-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are surrounded by a cup-like structure, giving it the common name "mooncup.".

Alchemilla lunaria is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
