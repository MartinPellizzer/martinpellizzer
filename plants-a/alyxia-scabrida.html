Alyxia scabrida

Alyxia scabrida, commonly known as the rough alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it its common name. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically white or pale yellow and bloom in the summer months.

Alyxia scabrida is often found in wetland habitats and is considered an important plant for supporting local wildlife.
