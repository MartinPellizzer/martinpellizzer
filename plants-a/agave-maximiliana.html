Agave maximiliana

Agave maximiliana, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, blue-green leaves that are often lined with white teeth. The plant produces an inflorescence of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Agave maximiliana is drought-tolerant and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
