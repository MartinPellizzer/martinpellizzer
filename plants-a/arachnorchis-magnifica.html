Arachnorchis magnifica

Arachnorchis magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent spider orchid, is a striking terrestrial orchid native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble spiders, with long, slender petals and a distinctive, intricate labellum. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and under dense vegetation. Its flowering period occurs during the cooler months, usually between August and December.

Arachnorchis magnifica is a rare and protected species, highlighting its ecological significance and the need for conservation efforts.
