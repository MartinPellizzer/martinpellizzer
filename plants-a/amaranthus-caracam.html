Amaranthus caracam

Amaranthus caracam, commonly known as red amaranth, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to purple leaves and branched inflorescences that produce small, clustered flowers. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and thrives in warm, sunny climates. Its foliage is often used in culinary applications, while its seeds are valued for their nutritional content.

Amaranthus caracam is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.
