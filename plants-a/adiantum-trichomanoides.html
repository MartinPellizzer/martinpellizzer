Adiantum trichomanoides

Adiantum trichomanoides, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, dark green fronds that often have a silvery sheen. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Adiantum trichomanoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
