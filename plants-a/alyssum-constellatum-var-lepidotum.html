Alyssum constellatum var. lepidotum

Alyssum constellatum var. lepidotum, commonly known as starry alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, star-shaped white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
