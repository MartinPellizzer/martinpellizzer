Acer sieboldianum subvar. microphyllum

Acer sieboldianum subvar. microphyllum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with small leaves, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely toothed leaves that turn vibrant red and orange in the fall. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

The species is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.
