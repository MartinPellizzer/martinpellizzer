Anisotome patula

Anisotome patula, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along roadsides. Anisotome patula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Its growth habit and appearance make it a useful plant for ecological restoration projects.
