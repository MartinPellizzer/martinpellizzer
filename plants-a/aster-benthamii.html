Aster benthamii

Aster benthamii, commonly known as the Bentham's aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple to blue petals and yellow centers, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils in open, sunny habitats.

Aster benthamii is often found in desert scrub and chaparral environments, where it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
