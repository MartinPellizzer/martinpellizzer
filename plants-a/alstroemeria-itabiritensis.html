Alstroemeria itabiritensis

Alstroemeria itabiritensis, commonly known as the Itabira lily, is a striking South American plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for floral arrangements.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
