Apama brevipes

Apama brevipes, commonly known as the short-footed orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the wet tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and along stream banks. Its roots are often covered in a dense network of fine, white mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

Apama brevipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects.
