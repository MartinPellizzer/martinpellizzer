Ammannia sagittifolia f. swaziensis

Ammannia sagittifolia f. swaziensis, commonly known as the Swazi arrowleaf, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features lanceolate, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Ammanniaceae family.
