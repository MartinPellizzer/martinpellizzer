Ammannia sagittifolia f. ericiformis

Ammannia sagittifolia f. ericiformis, commonly known as the redroot sandbar willow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub-like perennial with bright red stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found near waterways and in swampy areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and ecological restoration projects.
