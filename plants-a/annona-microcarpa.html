Annona microcarpa

Annona microcarpa, commonly known as the little pawpaw, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small, evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a smooth, round, yellowish-green berry that ripens in late summer.

The leaves are thick, waxy, and have a distinctive heart-shaped appearance.
