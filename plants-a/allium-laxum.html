Allium laxum

Allium laxum, commonly known as the slender leek, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features grass-like basal leaves that are narrow and slightly curled, with a mild garlic scent. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers in a loose umbel, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a useful culinary plant.

Allium laxum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
