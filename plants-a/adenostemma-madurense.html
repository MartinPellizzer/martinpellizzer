Adenostemma madurense

Adenostemma madurense, commonly known as the Madura grass, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in loose panicles, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Adenostemma madurense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ability to thrive in humid conditions.
