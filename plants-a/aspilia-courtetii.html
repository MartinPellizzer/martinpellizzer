Aspilia courtetii

Aspilia courtetii, commonly known as the African crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and large, showy yellow flowers that resemble a crown. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Aspilia courtetii is typically found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
