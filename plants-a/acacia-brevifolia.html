Acacia brevifolia

Acacia brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, rounded phyllodes that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces yellow globular flowerheads that appear in late winter to early spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in coastal regions and heathlands.

Acacia brevifolia is well adapted to dry conditions and is drought-tolerant once established.
