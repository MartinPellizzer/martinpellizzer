Aristea cooperi

Aristea cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's arnoldia, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is widely cultivated in arid regions.
