Astracantha strictifolia

Astracantha strictifolia, commonly known as the rigidleaf star-thorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sparsely branched stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
