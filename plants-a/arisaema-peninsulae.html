Arisaema peninsulae

Arisaema peninsulae, commonly known as the Peninsular Arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces a single, showy spathe that is typically green with a purple or reddish tint. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Arisaema peninsulae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
