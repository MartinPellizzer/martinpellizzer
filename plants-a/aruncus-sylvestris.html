Aruncus sylvestris

Aruncus sylvestris, commonly known as mountain gothics, is a perennial plant native to the forests of eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces showy, feathery plumes that are particularly striking in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of large, compound leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a silvery underside.

Aruncus sylvestris thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
