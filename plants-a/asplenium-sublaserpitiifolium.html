Asplenium sublaserpitiifolium

Asplenium sublaserpitiifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant has a creeping rhizome and produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for shady, moist garden environments due to its ornamental value and adaptability.
