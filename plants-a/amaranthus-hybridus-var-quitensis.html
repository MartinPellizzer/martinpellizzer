Amaranthus hybridus var. quitensis

Amaranthus hybridus var. quitensis, commonly known as the Andean amaranth, is a tall, herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its branched stems, lanceolate leaves, and vibrant, showy flower heads that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into three-angled, ribbed seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nutritional seeds.

The leaves and seeds are also used in traditional cuisines and have been valued for their high protein and mineral content.
