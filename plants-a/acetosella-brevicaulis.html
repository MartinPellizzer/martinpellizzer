Acetosella brevicaulis

Acetosella brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-stemmed sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of oval, glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring season. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Acetosella brevicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in cool, humid climates.
