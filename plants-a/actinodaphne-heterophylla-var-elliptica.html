Actinodaphne heterophylla var. elliptica

Actinodaphne heterophylla var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic-leafed camphor tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, spherical berries. It is valued for its aromatic wood and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The leaves and bark contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
