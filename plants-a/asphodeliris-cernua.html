Asphodeliris cernua

Asphodeliris cernua, commonly known as the drooping asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.

The flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
