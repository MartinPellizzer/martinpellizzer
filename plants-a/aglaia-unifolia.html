Aglaia unifolia

Aglaia unifolia, commonly known as the white aglaia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other hardwood trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.
