Astragalus kentrophyta subsp. kentrophyta

Astragalus kentrophyta subsp.

kentrophyta, commonly known as the Kentrophytic milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
