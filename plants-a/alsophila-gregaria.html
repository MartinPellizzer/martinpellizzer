Alsophila gregaria

Alsophila gregaria, commonly known as the clustered tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to form dense clusters, often growing in shaded, moist environments. Its fronds are typically dark green and can reach lengths of up to 2 meters.

Alsophila gregaria is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
