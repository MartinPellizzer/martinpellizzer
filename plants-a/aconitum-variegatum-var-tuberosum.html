Aconitum variegatum var. tuberosum

Aconitum variegatum var. tuberosum, commonly known as the variegated aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a variegated pattern of green and white. It grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a poisonous plant and should be handled with care.
