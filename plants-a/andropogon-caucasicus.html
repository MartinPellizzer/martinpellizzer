Andropogon caucasicus

Andropogon caucasicus, commonly known as Caucasian bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, fluffy seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

However, it is not commonly cultivated in modern landscaping due to its preference for specific environmental conditions.
