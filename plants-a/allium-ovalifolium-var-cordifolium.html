Allium ovalifolium var. cordifolium

Allium ovalifolium var. cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved garlic.

This plant is a perennial member of the Amaryllidaceae family, characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
