Apium montanum

Apium montanum, commonly known as mountain parsley, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes familiar plants like carrots and parsley. The plant features slender, branching stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are finely divided, resembling those of other parsley species. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in umbels, a characteristic feature of the Apiaceae family.

The plant is often found in moist, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in cooler, mountainous climates.
