Andropogon hirtinoides

Andropogon hirtinoides, commonly known as hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture, and its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, blue-gray florets. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

Andropogon hirtinoides is also used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in disturbed environments.
