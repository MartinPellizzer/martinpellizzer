Amelanchier canadensis var. subintegra

Amelanchier canadensis var. subintegra, commonly known as the Canadian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin and a pointed tip. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that turn from yellow to red. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

It is often found in woodlands, stream valleys, and open areas, thriving in partial shade to full sun.
