Arctostaphylos glauca f. adenopuberula

Arctostaphylos glauca f. adenopuberula, commonly known as the Sierra buckthorn, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with leathery, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in late spring, followed by red or orange berries that are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and forested areas.

This subspecies is noted for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
