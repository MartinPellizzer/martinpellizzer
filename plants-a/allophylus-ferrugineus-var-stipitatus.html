Allophylus ferrugineus var. stipitatus

Allophylus ferrugineus var. stipitatus, commonly known as the rusty alder or red alder, is a small evergreen shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often reddish-brown in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Allophylus ferrugineus var. stipitatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
