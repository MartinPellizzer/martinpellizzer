Achnatherum occidentale

Achnatherum occidentale, commonly known as western needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Achnatherum occidentale plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
