Adiantum serratodentatum var. fuliginosum

Adiantum serratodentatum var. fuliginosum, commonly known as the sooty maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green with a sooty or grayish hue on the underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in humid forest understories. Its fronds are bioluminescent in the dark, a unique trait that aids in nighttime pollination by attracting specific insects.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant due to its aesthetic beauty and air-purifying qualities.
