Astragalus neochaldoranicus

Astragalus neochaldoranicus, commonly known as the Chaldaorian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense clusters, which attract various pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in pods that split open to release the seeds, a common reproductive strategy in the Fabaceae family.

Astragalus neochaldoranicus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
