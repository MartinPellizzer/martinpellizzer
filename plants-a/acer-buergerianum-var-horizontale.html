Acer buergerianum var. horizontale

Acer buergerianum var. horizontale, commonly known as the horizontal maple, is a striking ornamental tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its unique, horizontally oriented branches that create a distinctive, umbrella-like canopy. The tree produces small, bright red flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras in the fall. Its foliage is typically dark green with a slight bluish tinge, turning vibrant red in autumn.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscape design for its visual interest and adaptability to various soil conditions.
