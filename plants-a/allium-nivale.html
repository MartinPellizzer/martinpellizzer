Allium nivale

Allium nivale, commonly known as the snow onion, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a short, grass-like stem and is characterized by its clustered bulbs, which are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in cooler climates.

Allium nivale is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
