Asparagus sennii

Asparagus sennii, commonly known as the golden asparagus or the Mexican asparagus, is a species of flowering plant in the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to the southern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Asparagus sennii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and resilience to drought.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
