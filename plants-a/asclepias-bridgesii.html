Asclepias bridgesii

Asclepias bridgesii, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features upright stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, and it produces clusters of vibrant orange to yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its unique seed pods, which contain numerous small, hairy seeds attached to silky filaments, aiding in wind dispersal. Asclepias bridgesii is a host plant for the monarch butterfly, providing essential nutrients to the larvae.

Its foliage and flowers also attract a variety of pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in native habitats.
