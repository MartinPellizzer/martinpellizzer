Astelma milleflorum

Astelma milleflorum, commonly known as the starry bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its delicate appearance and long blooming period from late spring to early summer add ornamental value to gardens.
