Actinidia albicalyx

Actinidia albicalyx, commonly known as the white-flowered actinidia, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, fragrant berries. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Actinidia albicalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
