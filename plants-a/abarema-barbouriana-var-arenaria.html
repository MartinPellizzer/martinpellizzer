Abarema barbouriana var. arenaria

Abarema barbouriana var. arenaria, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
