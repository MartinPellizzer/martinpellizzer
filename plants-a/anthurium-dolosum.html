Anthurium dolosum

Anthurium dolosum, commonly known as the false calla, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive spathe and spadix inflorescence. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a colorful, petal-like structure called a spathe.

Anthurium dolosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in warm, humid environments.
