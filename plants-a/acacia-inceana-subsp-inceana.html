Acacia inceana subsp. inceana

Acacia inceana subsp.

inceana, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched form and phyllodes that are linear to narrowly elliptic.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical spikes, typically during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
