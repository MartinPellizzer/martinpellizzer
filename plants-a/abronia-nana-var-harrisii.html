Abronia nana var. harrisii

Abronia nana var. harrisii, commonly known as the dwarf sand verbena, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It typically reaches a height of only a few inches, with small, rounded leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize sandy soils, contributing to ecological restoration efforts in its native habitat.
