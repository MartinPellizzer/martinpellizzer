Angraecum vesiculiferum

Angraecum vesiculiferum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a unique epiphytic plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and striking, fragrant, white flowers with a distinctive central spur. The plant produces its flowers at night, attracting specific nocturnal pollinators like the hawkmoth. Its natural habitat includes humid, shaded environments in the rainforests of Madagascar.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
