Actinidia pentapetala

Actinidia pentapetala, commonly known as the Chinese mayapple, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces edible berries that ripen to a yellowish color in late summer. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
