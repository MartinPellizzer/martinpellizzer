Astragalus barnassari

Astragalus barnassari, commonly known as the Barnassan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Astragalus barnassari is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
