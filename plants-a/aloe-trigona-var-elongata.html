Aloe trigona var. elongata

Aloe trigona var. elongata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, spindly stems with narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its leaves are often used for their gel, which has various medicinal properties.
