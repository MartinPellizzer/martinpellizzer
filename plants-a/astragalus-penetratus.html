Astragalus penetratus

Astragalus penetratus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America, particularly in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Astragalus penetratus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
