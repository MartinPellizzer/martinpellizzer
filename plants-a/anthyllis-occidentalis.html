Anthyllis occidentalis

Anthyllis occidentalis, commonly known as the western anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and are often hairy.

Anthyllis occidentalis is known for its adaptability to poor soils and its role in supporting pollinators.
