Alyssum corymbulosum

Alyssum corymbulosum, commonly known as clustered alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum corymbulosum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
