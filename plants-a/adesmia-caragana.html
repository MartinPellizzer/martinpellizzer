Adesmia caragana

Adesmia caragana, commonly known as the white burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Adesmia caragana is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
