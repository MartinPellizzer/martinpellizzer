Aconitum japonicum subsp. subcuneatum

Aconitum japonicum subsp.

subcuneatum, commonly known as Japanese monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia. It features showy, blue to purple hooded flowers that are characteristic of the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant has large, deeply lobed leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
