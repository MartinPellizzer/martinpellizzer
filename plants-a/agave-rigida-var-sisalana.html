Agave rigida var. sisalana

Agave rigida var. sisalana, commonly known as sisal agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are fibrous and used in the production of sisal fiber. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces an erect flowering stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing yellowish-green flowers.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its architectural form.
