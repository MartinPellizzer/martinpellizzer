Acacia diallaga

Acacia diallaga, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments.

Acacia diallaga is adapted to arid environments and is often found in savannas and rocky areas.
