Azalea simsii var. vittata

Azalea simsii var. vittata, commonly known as the spotted azalea, is a compact evergreen shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy foliage and showy clusters of trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to pink and red. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers often have a distinctive spotted or mottled pattern. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
