Archiatriplex nanpinensis

Archiatriplex nanpinensis, commonly known as the Nanpin sandburs, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, spiny fruits. The plant produces clusters of tiny, wind-dispersed seeds that help it colonize sandy and rocky soils. Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is often studied for its potential in soil stabilization and ecological restoration.
