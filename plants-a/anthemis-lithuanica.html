Anthemis lithuanica

Anthemis lithuanica, commonly known as Lithuanian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often surrounded by white or pale blue ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in traditional herbal medicine and aromatherapy.
