Asystasia pinguifolia

Asystasia pinguifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf asystasia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often heart-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink, which bloom during the warm seasons. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy inflorescences.

Asystasia pinguifolia prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden areas.
