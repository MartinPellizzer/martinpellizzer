Athyrium filix-femina subsp. angustum

Athyrium filix-femina subsp.

angustum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
