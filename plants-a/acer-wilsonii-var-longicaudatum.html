Acer wilsonii var. longicaudatum

Acer wilsonii var. longicaudatum, commonly known as the long-tailed maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaf lobes that give it its common name. The leaves are typically bright green in summer and turn vibrant yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, red flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras for seed dispersal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
