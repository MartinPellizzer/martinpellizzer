Aglaia flavida

Aglaia flavida, commonly known as the golden aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow or orange as they mature. Aglaia flavida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of tropical flora.
