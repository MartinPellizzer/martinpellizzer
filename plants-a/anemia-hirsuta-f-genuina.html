Anemia hirsuta f. genuina

Anemia hirsuta f. genuina, commonly known as the velvetleaf.

It is a variant of the velvetleaf plant, characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and robust growth habit. This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in warm, humid environments. Its leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a distinctive velvety texture.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain ecosystems.
