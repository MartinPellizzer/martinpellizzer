Aconitum lycoctonum subsp. puberulum

Aconitum lycoctonum subsp.

puberulum, commonly known as the pubescent aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with deeply lobed foliage that is often covered in a fine pubescence.

This subspecies is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is known for its toxicity due to the presence of potent alkaloids.
