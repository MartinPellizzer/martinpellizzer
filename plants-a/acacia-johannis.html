Acacia johannis

Acacia johannis, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular heads, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets.

Acacia johannis is well adapted to dry conditions and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
