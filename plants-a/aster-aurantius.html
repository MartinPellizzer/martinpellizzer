Aster aurantius

Aster aurantius, commonly known as the golden aster, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant color and long-lasting appearance.
