Aesculus hippocastanum var. aureovariegata

Aesculus hippocastanum var. aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden variegated horse chestnut, is a cultivar of the horse chestnut tree.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which feature a mix of green, white, and gold patterns. The plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It produces large, showy white flowers in spring, followed by spiny, orange-red fruit capsules.

This variety is relatively slow-growing and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.
