Albizia perrieri var. monticola

Albizia perrieri var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain albizia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and grayish in color, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

Albizia perrieri var. monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
