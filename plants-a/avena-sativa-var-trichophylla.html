Avena sativa var. trichophylla

Avena sativa var. trichophylla, commonly known as the hairy oat, is a variant of the common oat characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and stems.

This plant belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, cylindrical spikes of florets. The leaves are narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it its common name.

It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and agricultural use.
