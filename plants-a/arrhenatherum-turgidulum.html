Arrhenatherum turgidulum

Arrhenatherum turgidulum, commonly known as the purple false oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are usually purple or pink in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Arrhenatherum turgidulum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
