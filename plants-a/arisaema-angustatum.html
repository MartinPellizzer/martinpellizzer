Arisaema angustatum

Arisaema angustatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence known as a spadix, enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a helical pattern along its stem. Its rhizomatous root system is used in traditional medicine and as a starch source.

Arisaema angustatum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
