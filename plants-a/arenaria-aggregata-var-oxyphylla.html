Arenaria aggregata var. oxyphylla

Arenaria aggregata var. oxyphylla, commonly known as the sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring. The plant has succulent leaves and stems, which help it retain moisture in dry environments. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its habitat.
