Abies nordmanniana var. tortifolia

Abies nordmanniana var. tortifolia, commonly known as the Caucasian fir, is a variant of the Nordmann fir native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The leaves are often twisted, giving the plant its specific epithet "tortifolia," which translates to "twisted leaf.". It produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and are an important food source for various wildlife.

This evergreen species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.

Native Distribution

Abies nordmanniana var. tortifolia is primarily native to the eastern Caucasus region, including parts of Georgia, Armenia, and Azerbaijan.

It is also found in the western parts of the Russian Federation, particularly in the North Caucasus. This variety grows in mountainous areas at elevations ranging from 1,200 to 2,500 meters. It is commonly found in mixed forests with other coniferous species.

The plant thrives in temperate climates with high humidity and well-drained soils.
