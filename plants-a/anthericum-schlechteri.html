Anthericum schlechteri

Anthericum schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family, with small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
