Asarum caudigerum var. triangulare

Asarum caudigerum var. triangulare, commonly known as the triangular wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy underside, and its flowers are small, greenish-brown spathes that give way to reddish berries. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family, known for its unique floral structures and medicinal properties.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
