Acrolophia lunata

Acrolophia lunata, commonly known as the moonflower, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant produces showy, star-shaped blooms with a strong, sweet scent that attracts nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

The botanical features of Acrolophia lunata make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
