Astragalus flexuosus var. fendleri

Astragalus flexuosus var. fendleri, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils and rocky plains. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
