Astragalus barclayanus

Astragalus barclayanus, commonly known as Barclay's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland ecosystems. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.

Astragalus barclayanus is also of interest in botanical research due to its adaptation to arid environments.
