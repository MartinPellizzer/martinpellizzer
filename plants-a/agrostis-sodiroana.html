Agrostis sodiroana

Agrostis sodiroana, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a grass species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 60 cm in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small, flowering structures typical of the grass family.

Agrostis sodiroana is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
