Asclepias reflectens

Asclepias reflectens, commonly known as the reflected milkweed, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Asclepias reflectens is known for its ecological importance as a host plant for monarch butterflies and other milkweed-dependent species.
