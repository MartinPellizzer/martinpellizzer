Acacia kulnurensis

Acacia kulnurensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and compound leaves composed of small, narrow phyllodes. The plant produces yellow globular flowerheads that appear in late winter to early spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. It is well adapted to drought and poor soils, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.

Acacia kulnurensis plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
