Agapetes macrantha var. grandiflora

Agapetes macrantha var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered silverleaf, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree with evergreen foliage and is characterized by its dense, silver-gray leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
