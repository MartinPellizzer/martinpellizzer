Aphelandra nephoica

Aphelandra nephoica, commonly known as the cloud forest angel's trumpet, is a tropical plant native to the humid forests of South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like flowers that hang from the plant, often in clusters. The leaves are typically large, ovate, and have a glossy surface. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires high humidity to flourish.

This plant is popular in horticulture for its striking appearance and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden areas.
