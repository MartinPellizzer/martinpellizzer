Acacia paniculata

Acacia paniculata, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, drooping branches and small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves, a common adaptation in acacia species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds, making it an important plant in local ecosystems.
