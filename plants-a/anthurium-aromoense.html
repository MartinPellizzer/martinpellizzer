Anthurium aromoense

Anthurium aromoense, commonly known as the fragrant anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spadix-like inflorescences. The plant is noted for its aromatic qualities, with a subtle, sweet scent that becomes more pronounced when the spadix is warmed. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium aromoense is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and unique fragrance.
