Aerides guttata

Aerides guttata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, stocky rhizome and a rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers with white petals and a distinctive yellow lip. Its flowers typically bloom in the summer months, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Aerides guttata is valued for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
