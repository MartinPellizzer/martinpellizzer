Anthonotha ferruginea

Anthonotha ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its name refers to the rust-colored hairs that cover its stems and leaves.

Anthonotha ferruginea is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in traditional medicine.
