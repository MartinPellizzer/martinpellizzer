Allium marathasicum

Allium marathasicum, commonly known as the Armenian chive, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks, and is characterized by its bulbous base and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves and bulbs are edible, often used in local cuisines for their mild, onion-like flavor.

Allium marathasicum is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
