Achillea paucidentata

Achillea paucidentata, commonly known as the slender yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a silvery underside, and it produces small white or pale yellow flowers in flat-topped clusters.

Achillea paucidentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
