Absolmsia

Absolmsia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent petals that resemble ghostly apparitions, a feature that has captivated botanists and enthusiasts alike. The plant thrives in extreme conditions, often growing in rocky soil with minimal water, showcasing its remarkable adaptability. Its blooming period is brief, typically occurring at night, which adds to its mysterious allure.

Due to its rarity and delicate nature, Absolmsia is highly sought after in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
