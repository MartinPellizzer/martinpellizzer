Alchornea triplinervia var. genuina

Alchornea triplinervia var. genuina, commonly known as the African coffee tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with three main veins, giving it the species name "triplinervia.". The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round, blackish fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
