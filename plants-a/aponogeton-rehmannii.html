Aponogeton rehmannii

Aponogeton rehmannii, commonly known as the water arrowhead, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically submerged or floating, with some leaves extending above the water surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive, boat-shaped fruits. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds and lakes.

Aponogeton rehmannii is valued in aquatic gardening for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
