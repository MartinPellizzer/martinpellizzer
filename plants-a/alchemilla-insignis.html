Alchemilla insignis

Alchemilla insignis, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a distinctive silvery appearance.

Alchemilla insignis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional herbal medicine.
