Allium lazikkiyense

Allium lazikkiyense, commonly known as the Lazikiyi onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in local cuisines for their pungent flavor. Its leaves are strap-shaped and can grow up to 30 cm in length, contributing to its ornamental value.

Allium lazikkiyense thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in mountainous regions with temperate climates.
