Acacia ericifolia var. tenuis

Acacia ericifolia var. tenuis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow globular flowers that appear in late winter or early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
