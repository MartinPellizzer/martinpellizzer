Anthopogon

Anthopogon, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, erect stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its most distinctive feature is the bright yellow flower head, which consists of many tiny florets arranged in a radiating pattern.

Anthopogon is often found in moist soils along stream banks and in wet meadows.
