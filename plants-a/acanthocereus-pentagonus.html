Acanthocereus pentagonus

Acanthocereus pentagonus, commonly known as the night blooming cactus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in sharp, spiny areoles. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert conditions.

The species is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and nighttime blooming habit.
