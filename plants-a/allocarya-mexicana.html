Allocarya mexicana

Allocarya mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, rough to the touch, and have a distinctive grayish-white underside.

The flowers are yellow and produce small, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its propagation across arid regions.
