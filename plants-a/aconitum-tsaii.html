Aconitum tsaii

Aconitum tsaii, commonly known as the Tsai's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and striking, hood-shaped flowers. The plant features palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Aconitum tsaii contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.
