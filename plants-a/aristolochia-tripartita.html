Aristolochia tripartita

Aristolochia tripartita, commonly known as Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is typically found in moist woodland habitats. It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its unique morphology and ecological role.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
