Alloberberis pinifolia

Alloberberis pinifolia, commonly known as the needleleaf berberis, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a fine layer of wax. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Alloberberis pinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh, arid conditions.
