Alsine juniperina var. eglandulosa

Alsine juniperina var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular alsine juniper, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically dark blue or black in color. It is well adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration in degraded landscapes.
