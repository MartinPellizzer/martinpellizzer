Acacia arborea

Acacia arborea, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a small to medium-sized tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and feathery leaves that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Acacia arborea is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for dry climates.

Its wood is lightweight and has been used traditionally for various purposes, including construction and fuel.
