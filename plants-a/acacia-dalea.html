Acacia dalea

Acacia dalea, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-green leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. Acacia dalea thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

It is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological importance as a source of food for wildlife.
