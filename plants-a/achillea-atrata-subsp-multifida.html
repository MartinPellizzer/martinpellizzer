Achillea atrata subsp. multifida

Achillea atrata subsp.

multifida, commonly known as the dark yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with a grayish-green hue.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, dark purple to black flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.
