Aubrieta × cultorum

Aubrieta × cultorum, commonly known as the common aubrieta, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to withstand dry conditions. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it ideal for rock gardens and borders.

Aubrieta × cultorum is known for its hardiness and ability to naturalize, often spreading to form a carpet of vibrant color.
