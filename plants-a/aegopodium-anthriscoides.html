Aegopodium anthriscoides

Aegopodium anthriscoides, commonly known as small cow parsley, is a flowering plant in the carrot family (Apiaceae).

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers that appear in umbels. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a hollow, aromatic stem and is often mistaken for other members of the Apiaceae family.

It is known for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
