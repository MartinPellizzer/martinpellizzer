Acer fulvescens

Acer fulvescens, commonly known as the red maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage in autumn, which ranges from bright orange to deep crimson. The tree has opposite, palmate leaves that are typically 5-9 cm in length, with coarsely toothed margins. Its flowers are small, yellow-green, and appear in clusters, while the fruit is a samara with two winged seeds.

Acer fulvescens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forests, wetlands, and along riverbanks.
