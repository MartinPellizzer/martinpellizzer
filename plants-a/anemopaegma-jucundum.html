Anemopaegma jucundum

Anemopaegma jucundum, commonly known as the joyful anemone, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions. Its botanical name reflects its cheerful appearance and the gentle breeze that often carries its scent.

Anemopaegma jucundum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators.
