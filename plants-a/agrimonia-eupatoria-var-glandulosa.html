Agrimonia eupatoria var. glandulosa

Agrimonia eupatoria var. glandulosa, commonly known as sticky agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in glandular hairs that give it a sticky texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges and are often covered in fine, sticky hairs.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
