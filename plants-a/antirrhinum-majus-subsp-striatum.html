Antirrhinum majus subsp. striatum

Antirrhinum majus subsp.

striatum, commonly known as the striped toadflax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange.

The flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems, and the plant produces capsules as its fruit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
