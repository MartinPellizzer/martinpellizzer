Abolboda ebracteata var. ebracteata

Abolboda ebracteata var. ebracteata, commonly known as the leafless mallow, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves that are often reduced or absent, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its stems are woody and often branched, with a rough, hairy texture.

This species is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland dunes.
