Aster tataricus var. minor

Aster tataricus var. minor, commonly known as the lesser tatarian aster, is a perennial herb native to eastern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This species is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.
