Aspidoglossum lanatum

Aspidoglossum lanatum, commonly known as the woolly aspidoglossum, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow throat. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Aspidoglossum lanatum is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
