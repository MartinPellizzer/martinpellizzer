Abies douglasii var. macrocarpa

Abies douglasii var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant Douglas fir, is a variant of the Douglas fir native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its tall, slender form and dense, dark green foliage. The leaves are needle-like, typically measuring 1 to 2 inches in length, and are arranged spirally along the branches. The cones are large and woody, with a distinctive shape and size, and they mature to release seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variant is notable for its ecological importance and is often found in mixed coniferous forests.
