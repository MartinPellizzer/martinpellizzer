Apium ammios

Apium ammios, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its narrow, bluish-gray leaves and tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats. Its structure is adapted to withstand coastal winds and salt spray, making it a hardy species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables.
