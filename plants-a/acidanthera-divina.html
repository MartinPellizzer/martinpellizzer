Acidanthera divina

Acidanthera divina, commonly known as the sacred lily, is a striking orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and a single, erect stem that supports its vibrant blooms.

Acidanthera divina is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural and spiritual significance in Andean traditions.
