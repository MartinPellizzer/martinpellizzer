Amomum calcaratum

Amomum calcaratum, commonly known as the rock amomum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, pale yellow flowers, which are followed by dark blue to black berries.

Amomum calcaratum is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
