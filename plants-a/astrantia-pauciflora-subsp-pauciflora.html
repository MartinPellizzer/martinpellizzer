Astrantia pauciflora subsp. pauciflora

Astrantia pauciflora subsp.

pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered aster, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has a rosette of basal leaves that are oval to lance-shaped.

The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers, blooming from late summer to early autumn. Its flowers are arranged in clusters, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in partial shade. Astrantia pauciflora subsp.

pauciflora is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
