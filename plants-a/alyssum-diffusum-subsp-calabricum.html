Alyssum diffusum subsp. calabricum

Alyssum diffusum subsp.

calabricum, commonly known as the California alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to California. It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in spring.

The plant has hairy, lobed leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and grassland habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
