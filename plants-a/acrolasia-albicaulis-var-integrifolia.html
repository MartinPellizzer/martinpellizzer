Acrolasia albicaulis var. integrifolia

Acrolasia albicaulis var. integrifolia, commonly known as the white-stemmed acrolasia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pale green stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are entire along their margins. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
