Amsinckia nana

Amsinckia nana, commonly known as dwarf ironweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features small, hairy leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow to brown flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact, mat-like growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for study in desert botany and ecological restoration.
