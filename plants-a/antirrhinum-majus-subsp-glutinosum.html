Antirrhinum majus subsp. glutinosum

Antirrhinum majus subsp.

glutinosum, commonly known as sticky snapdragon, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its glandular, sticky hairs that cover the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive texture.

The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming from summer to autumn. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
