Anemone narcissiflora subsp. nipponica

Anemone narcissiflora subsp.

nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan. It features showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late summer.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
