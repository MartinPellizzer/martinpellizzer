Alchemilla atrifolia

Alchemilla atrifolia, commonly known as black-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla atrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
