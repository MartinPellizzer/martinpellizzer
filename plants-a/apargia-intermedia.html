Apargia intermedia

Apargia intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has trifoliate leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its small, fragrant flowers bloom in late spring and are usually pale blue or purple in color.

Apargia intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators and wildlife.
