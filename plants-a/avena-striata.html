Avena striata

Avena striata, commonly known as the striped oat, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically greenish or pale yellow in color. Avena striata is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
