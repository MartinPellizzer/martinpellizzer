Anemone narcissiflora var. alaschanica

Anemone narcissiflora var. alaschanica, commonly known as the Alaskan anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Alaska and western Canada.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a distinctive central tuft of stamens. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its blooms. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This variant is notable for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
