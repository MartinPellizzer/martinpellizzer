Allium kungii

Allium kungii, commonly known as the Kungii garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, edible bulbs that are used in traditional cuisines. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. Its bulbs are rich in sulfur compounds, contributing to its pungent flavor and medicinal properties.

Allium kungii is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its potential health benefits.
