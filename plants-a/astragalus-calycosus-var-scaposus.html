Astragalus calycosus var. scaposus

Astragalus calycosus var. scaposus, commonly known as the scapose milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often branched and bear small, yellow flowers in late spring. Its leaves are pinnate, with numerous small leaflets, and it produces small, legume-like pods that contain seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
