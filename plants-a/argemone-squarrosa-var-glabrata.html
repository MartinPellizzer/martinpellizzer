Argemone squarrosa var. glabrata

Argemone squarrosa var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some areas.
