Astragalus jelenevskyi

Astragalus jelenevskyi, commonly known as the Jelenevsky's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its scientific study contributes to the understanding of plant adaptation strategies in extreme climates.
