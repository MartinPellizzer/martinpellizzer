Artanthe persicariifolia

Artanthe persicariifolia, commonly known as the pink or red artanthe, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Artanthe persicariifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
