Acer monspessulanum subf. cruciatum

Acer monspessulanum subf.

cruciatum, commonly known as the Spanish maple, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks.
