Astrophytum aureinitens

Astrophytum aureinitens, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species native to the deserts of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus with a distinctive star-shaped pattern on its stems, which are covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
