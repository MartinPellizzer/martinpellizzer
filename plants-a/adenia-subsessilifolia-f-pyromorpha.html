Adenia subsessilifolia f. pyromorpha

Adenia subsessilifolia f. pyromorpha, commonly known as the fire-flower or flame plant, is a unique variant of the Adenia genus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, red-orange flowers that resemble flames, hence its common name. The plant features a climbing habit with woody stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and are pollinated by birds and insects.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid conditions.
