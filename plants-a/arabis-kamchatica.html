Arabis kamchatica

Arabis kamchatica, commonly known as the Kamchatka rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Arabis kamchatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
