Acer campestre var. leiophyllum

Acer campestre var. leiophyllum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved field maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes.
