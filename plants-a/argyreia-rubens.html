Argyreia rubens

Argyreia rubens, commonly known as the holy leaf or sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a faintly scented aroma. The plant has heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Argyreia rubens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various traditional practices.
