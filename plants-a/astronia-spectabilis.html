Astronia spectabilis

Astronia spectabilis, commonly known as the showy astronia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Astronia spectabilis is often found in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions.
