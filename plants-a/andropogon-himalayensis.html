Andropogon himalayensis

Andropogon himalayensis, commonly known as Himalayan bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of the Indian subcontinent.

It typically grows in open woodlands, grasslands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flower heads. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Andropogon himalayensis is also known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
