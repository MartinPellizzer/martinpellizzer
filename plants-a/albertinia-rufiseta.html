Albertinia rufiseta

Albertinia rufiseta, commonly known as the redset sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often reddish in color. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.

Due to its habitat preferences, it is often found in areas with standing water or seasonal flooding.
