Alyxia racemosa

Alyxia racemosa, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in the early morning. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

Alyxia racemosa is adapted to salt spray and can thrive in harsh coastal environments.
