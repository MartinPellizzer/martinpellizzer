Axonopus ramosissimus

Axonopus ramosissimus, commonly known as the hairy axonopus, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often branch profusely. The leaves are narrow, slightly folded, and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lawns, gardens, and disturbed areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense mats.
