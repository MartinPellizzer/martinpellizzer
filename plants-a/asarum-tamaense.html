Asarum tamaense

Asarum tamaense, commonly known as Tama wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, hooded flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic root that is used in traditional medicine. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
