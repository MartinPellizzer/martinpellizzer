Asplenium subsessile

Asplenium subsessile, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to various regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often have a dark green color. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rock crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Asplenium subsessile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
