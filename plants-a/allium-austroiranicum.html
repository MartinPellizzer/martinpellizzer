Allium austroiranicum

Allium austroiranicum, commonly known as the southern Irano-Turanian garlic, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its robust, fibrous roots and basal leaves. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, cylindrical bulbs that are typically white to pale yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential culinary uses, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
