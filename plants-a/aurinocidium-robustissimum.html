Aurinocidium robustissimum

Aurinocidium robustissimum, commonly known as the golden torch cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are covered in dense, yellowish spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid to semi-arid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
