Astragalus cephalonicus

Astragalus cephalonicus, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Astragalus cephalonicus plays a role in soil stabilization and is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing plants.
