Amaryllis bagnoldii var. minor

Amaryllis bagnoldii var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf amaryllis, is a compact, bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation. It is prized for its ease of care and ability to bloom reliably when conditions are optimal.

This variety is particularly popular for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the year under proper care.
