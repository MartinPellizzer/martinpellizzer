Astilbe odontophylla var. bandaica

Astilbe odontophylla var. bandaica, commonly known as the Banda astilbe, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant thrives in partial to full shade and requires moist, well-drained soil rich in organic matter. Its foliage is typically dark green and remains attractive throughout the growing season.

Astilbe odontophylla var. bandaica is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to add color to shaded landscapes.
