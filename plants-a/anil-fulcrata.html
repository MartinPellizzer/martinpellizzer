Anil fulcrata

Anil fulcrata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is often smooth and greyish in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The tree is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in urban landscapes.
