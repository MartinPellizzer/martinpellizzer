Anaphalis margaritacea var. cinnamomea

Anaphalis margaritacea var. cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon-leafed mouse-eared gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive cinnamon-like scent when crushed. The plant produces pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
