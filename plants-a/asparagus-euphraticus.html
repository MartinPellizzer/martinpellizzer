Asparagus euphraticus

Asparagus euphraticus, commonly known as the Jordanian asparagus, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to store water efficiently. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Asparagus euphraticus is often found growing in rocky soils and is considered a resilient species in its native habitat.
