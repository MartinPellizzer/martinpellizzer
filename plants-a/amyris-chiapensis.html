Amyris chiapensis

Amyris chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapan amyris, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and woody stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its foliage is dense and glossy, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Amyris chiapensis is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
