Abarema cordifolia

Abarema cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf acacia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features simple, heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and it is often found in dry tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
