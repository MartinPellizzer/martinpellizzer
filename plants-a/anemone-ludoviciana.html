Anemone ludoviciana

Anemone ludoviciana, commonly known as the showy anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed and hairy, and its stems are usually unbranched. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland gardens and streambanks.

Anemone ludoviciana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
