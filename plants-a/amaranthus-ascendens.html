Amaranthus ascendens

Amaranthus ascendens, commonly known as the prostrate amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit, with branches that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red in color. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Amaranthus ascendens is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
