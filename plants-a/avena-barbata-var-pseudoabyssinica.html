Avena barbata var. pseudoabyssinica

Avena barbata var. pseudoabyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces spikelets that are often adorned with bristles. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it blooms with small, inconspicuous flowers.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
