Asparagopsis scoparia

Asparagopsis scoparia, commonly known as the rock asparagus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often found in coastal areas and dry habitats. The plant has a creeping growth habit with fleshy, succulent stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its small, white or greenish flowers are clustered in umbels, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

Asparagopsis scoparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
