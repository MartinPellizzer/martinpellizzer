Alyssum propinquum

Alyssum propinquum, commonly known as the common alyssum, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in clusters. The plant has slender stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum propinquum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
