Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. pseudovulneraria

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, which helps it thrive in nutrient-poor environments.

This subspecies is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
