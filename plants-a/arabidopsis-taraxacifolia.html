Arabidopsis taraxacifolia

Arabidopsis taraxacifolia, commonly known as the dandelion Arabidopsis, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a distinctive flower head composed of numerous small, yellow flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a short life cycle, completing its growth, flowering, and seed production within a single growing season.

The species is widely used in genetic research due to its small genome and rapid growth rate.
