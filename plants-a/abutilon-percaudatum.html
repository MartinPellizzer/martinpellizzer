Abutilon percaudatum

Abutilon percaudatum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance.

Abutilon percaudatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
