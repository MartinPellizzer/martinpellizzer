Asarum yeonbyeonense var. viridiluteolum

Asarum yeonbyeonense var. viridiluteolum, commonly known as the green-veined wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the Korean Peninsula.

It features heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves with prominent light green veins, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in umbels, typical of the Aristolochiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its aromatic leaves and unique floral structure make it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
