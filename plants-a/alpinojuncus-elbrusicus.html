Alpinojuncus elbrusicus

Alpinojuncus elbrusicus, commonly known as the Elbrus sedge, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Caucasus Mountains.

It is a member of the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically found at the tips of its stems. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, mountainous environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
