Astragalus aristatus var. australis

Astragalus aristatus var. australis, commonly known as the Southern Milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along creek banks.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for wildlife.
