Athanasia minuta subsp. inermis

Athanasia minuta subsp.

inermis, commonly known as the dwarf African daisy, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, attractive plants.
