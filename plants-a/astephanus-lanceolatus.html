Astephanus lanceolatus

Astephanus lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf stephanus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in spikes, and bloom in shades of pink to purple.

Astephanus lanceolatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
