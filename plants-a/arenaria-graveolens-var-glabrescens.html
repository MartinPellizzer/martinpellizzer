Arenaria graveolens var. glabrescens

Arenaria graveolens var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth sand-verbena, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters along the stems. The plant has slender, hairy stems that become smooth as they mature, and its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

This variety is distinguished by its reduced pubescence compared to other forms of Arenaria graveolens.
