Achyranthes helleri

Achyranthes helleri, commonly known as Heller's smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color, especially along the veins. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes and bloom in late summer.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
