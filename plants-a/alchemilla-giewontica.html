Alchemilla giewontica

Alchemilla giewontica, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Tatra Mountains in Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its silver-green, scalloped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Alchemilla giewontica is adapted to cold, rocky environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appearance and hardy nature.
