Ammannella

Ammannella, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Ammannella coccinea, which is native to the Mediterranean region. These plants typically have basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are yellow and arranged in composite heads, characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

Ammannella species are often found in dry, sunny habitats and are sometimes used in horticulture for their ornamental value.
