Anthericum liliago f. maius

Anthericum liliago f. maius, commonly known as the giant arum lily, is a striking plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is a hybrid form of Anthericum liliago, characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that resembles a lily. The plant features a tall, narrow leaf blade with a prominent midrib and a broad, waxy sheath at the base. Its flowers are typically green with a red or purple spathe, and it produces small, white berries that are dispersed by birds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in water gardens and bog habitats.
