Anthericum hispidum

Anthericum hispidum, commonly known as the hairy nut sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland areas in Europe and parts of Asia.

It features triangular, hairy stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are narrow with a rough texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in spikelets, which are typically found in clusters at the base of the stems. Anthericum hispidum thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies.

It plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and serving as a food source for some wildlife.
