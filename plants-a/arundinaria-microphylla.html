Arundinaria microphylla

Arundinaria microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length. The plant forms dense clumps and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its compact growth habit. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Arundinaria microphylla is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for small wildlife.
