Alsophila conferta

Alsophila conferta, commonly known as the clustered tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Alsophila conferta is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic value and ability to add a tropical feel to landscapes.
