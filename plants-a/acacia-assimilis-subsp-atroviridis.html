Acacia assimilis subsp. atroviridis

Acacia assimilis subsp.

atroviridis, commonly known as the black thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa. It is characterized by its dark, thorn-covered stems and compound leaves with small, serrated leaflets.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which are followed by elongated, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of wood.
