Ardisia speciosa

Ardisia speciosa, commonly known as the Christmas berry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy fruits, which are often used in floral arrangements.

Ardisia speciosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
