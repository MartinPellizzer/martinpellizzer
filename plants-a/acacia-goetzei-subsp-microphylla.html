Acacia goetzei subsp. microphylla

Acacia goetzei subsp.

microphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia. It features small, narrow, phyllodes that are typically yellow-green in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It is adapted to dry environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
