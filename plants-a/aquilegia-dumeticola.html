Aquilegia dumeticola

Aquilegia dumeticola, commonly known as the rock columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and vibrant flower colors, typically ranging from blue to purple. The plant produces showy flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately compound, with lobes that are often hairy and serrated.

Aquilegia dumeticola thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
