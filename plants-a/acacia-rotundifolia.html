Acacia rotundifolia

Acacia rotundifolia, commonly known as the globe acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its globular, rounded leaves that are clustered at the ends of its branches. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in spherical heads. Its small, pod-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Acacia rotundifolia is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-resistant species.
