Abies vejarii var. mexicana

Abies vejarii var. mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican fir, is a rare and endemic conifer native to the Sierra Madre Occidental in Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are blue-green in color. The tree produces cones that are typically pendulous and contain edible seeds. It thrives in cool, moist, high-altitude environments and is adapted to rocky soils.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species.
