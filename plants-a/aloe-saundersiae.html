Aloe saundersiae

Aloe saundersiae, commonly known as the Orange River aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright stems and fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow to orange flowers, which attract pollinators like birds and insects. Aloe saundersiae is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive dry conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in rocky or sandy soils.
