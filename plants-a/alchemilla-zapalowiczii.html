Alchemilla zapalowiczii

Alchemilla zapalowiczii, commonly known as the Zapalowicz's lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
