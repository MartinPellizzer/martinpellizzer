Aristida muhlenbergioides

Aristida muhlenbergioides, commonly known as Muhlenberg's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration, and it has a fibrous root system.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
