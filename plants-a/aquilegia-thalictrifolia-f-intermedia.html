Aquilegia thalictrifolia f. intermedia

Aquilegia thalictrifolia f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate thalictrifolia columbine, is a hybrid form of the thalictrifolia species.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in woodland or shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with finely divided leaflets, and it has a clump-forming growth habit.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability in garden settings.
