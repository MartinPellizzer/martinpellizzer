Afrorchis

Afrorchis, commonly known as the African orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to various regions in Africa, particularly in tropical and subtropical climates. They typically grow as terrestrial or epiphytic herbs, with their roots often forming dense mats. Afrorchis species are characterized by their showy, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple.

Due to their ornamental value, some species are cultivated for horticultural purposes.
