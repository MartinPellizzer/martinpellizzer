Alyssum kermanshahense

Alyssum kermanshahense, commonly known as Persian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is well adapted to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Alyssum kermanshahense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
