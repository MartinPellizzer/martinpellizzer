Asyneuma comosiforme

Asyneuma comosiforme, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features alternate, hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides.

The plant is notable for its ability to spread rapidly through its seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
