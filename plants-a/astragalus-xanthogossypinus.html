Astragalus xanthogossypinus

Astragalus xanthogossypinus, commonly known as the yellow cottonweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
