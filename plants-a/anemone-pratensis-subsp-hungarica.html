Anemone pratensis subsp. hungarica

Anemone pratensis subsp.

hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian meadow anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features showy, white to pale pink flowers with five to seven petals, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps retain moisture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
