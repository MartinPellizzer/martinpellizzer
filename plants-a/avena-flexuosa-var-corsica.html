Avena flexuosa var. corsica

Avena flexuosa var. corsica, commonly known as Corsican bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, arching stems that often form dense clumps. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and typically have a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
