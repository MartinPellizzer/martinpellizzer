Astragalus aktauensis subsp. aktauensis

Astragalus aktauensis subsp.

aktauensis, commonly known as the Aktau milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small, legume-like pods that contain seeds.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological significance and potential medicinal properties.
