Astragalus oleifolius subvar. alexandri

Astragalus oleifolius subvar. alexandri, commonly known as Alexandrian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
