Acantholimon lycaonicum var. cataonicum

Acantholimon lycaonicum var. cataonicum, commonly known as the Cataonic saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or pale yellow, blooming in the spring. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to coastal and desert areas.

This variety is valued for its resilience and is often used in ecological restoration projects in arid landscapes.
