Astragalus venturii

Astragalus venturii, commonly known as the Venturi milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern in some regions.
