Acacia nitidifolia

Acacia nitidifolia, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Acacia nitidifolia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
