Amelanchier canadensis subsp. spicata

Amelanchier canadensis subsp.

spicata, commonly known as the spicata serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America. It produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in the summer.

The plant is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves and a dense, rounded growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open fields.

The spicata subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance, providing food for wildlife.
