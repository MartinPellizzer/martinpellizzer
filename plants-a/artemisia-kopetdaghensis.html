Artemisia kopetdaghensis

Artemisia kopetdaghensis, commonly known as Kopetdag wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.

The plant's resilience and unique appearance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
