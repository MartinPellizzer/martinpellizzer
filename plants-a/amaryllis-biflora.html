Amaryllis biflora

Amaryllis biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting stripes. The plant produces two to three flowers per stem, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are dark green and grow from a basal rosette.

Amaryllis biflora is well-suited for container gardening and can thrive in well-drained soil with adequate sunlight.
