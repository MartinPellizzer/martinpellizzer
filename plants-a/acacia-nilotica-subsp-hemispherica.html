Acacia nilotica subsp. hemispherica

Acacia nilotica subsp.

hemispherica, commonly known as the umbrella acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped crown, formed by multiple branches radiating from a central trunk.

The plant produces small, yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval leaflets, and the bark is typically pale and smooth.

This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant plant.
