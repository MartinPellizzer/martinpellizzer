Amygdalus bucharica var. incana

Amygdalus bucharica var. incana, commonly known as the Karakum almond, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish, fuzzy leaves and produces small, pale pink flowers in the spring. The plant bears edible fruits that resemble almonds, though they are typically smaller and have a distinct flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in horticulture and local food systems.
