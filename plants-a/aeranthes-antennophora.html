Aeranthes antennophora

Aeranthes antennophora, commonly known as the spurred orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant with a short rhizome and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forests or rocky areas. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

The species is characterized by its unique floral structure and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
