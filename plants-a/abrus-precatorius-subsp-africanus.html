Abrus precatorius subsp. africanus

Abrus precatorius subsp.

africanus, commonly known as the African jequirity, is a climbing legume native to Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive red, bean-like pods that contain toxic seeds covered in a silky, white coating.

The plant produces small, white flowers and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its seeds contain abrin, a potent toxin that can be lethal if ingested.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
