Asperula pendula var. tubulifera

Asperula pendula var. tubulifera, commonly known as the tubular hanging azalea, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of small, tubular-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a prized addition to horticultural collections.
