Aphelandra ignea

Aphelandra ignea, commonly known as the flame orchid, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that are often a deep green with a reddish or purple hue. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red or orange throat. Aphelandra ignea thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
