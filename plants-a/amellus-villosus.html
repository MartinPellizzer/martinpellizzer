Amellus villosus

Amellus villosus, commonly known as the woolly amellus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, or villosus, stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Amellus villosus is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
