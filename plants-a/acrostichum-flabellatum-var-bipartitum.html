Acrostichum flabellatum var. bipartitum

Acrostichum flabellatum var. bipartitum, commonly known as the bipartite fern, is a variant of the flabellate fern, characterized by its distinctive bipartite fronds.

The plant is native to tropical regions of the Americas and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are divided into two distinct lobes, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The leaves are typically large, with a feathery structure that contributes to its lush, tropical aesthetic.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses due to its visual appeal and adaptability.
