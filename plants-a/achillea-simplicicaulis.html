Achillea simplicicaulis

Achillea simplicicaulis, commonly known as the simpleleaf yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems with finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in flat-topped clusters called corymbs.

Achillea simplicicaulis is often found in dry meadows and rocky soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
