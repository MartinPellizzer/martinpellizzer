Alchornea borneensis

Alchornea borneensis, commonly known as Bornean alchorneas, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ecologically significant members. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Alchornea borneensis is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its potential medicinal properties.
