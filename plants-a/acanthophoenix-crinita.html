Acanthophoenix crinita

Acanthophoenix crinita, commonly known as the hairy date palm, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible dates. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The hairy date palm is also valued for its resilience to drought and its ability to grow in poor soil conditions.
