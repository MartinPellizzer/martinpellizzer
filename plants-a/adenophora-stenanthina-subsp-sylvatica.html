Adenophora stenanthina subsp. sylvatica

Adenophora stenanthina subsp.

sylvatica, commonly known as the woodland bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soils.

The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often appearing in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly narrower leaves and more compact growth habit compared to other Adenophora species.
