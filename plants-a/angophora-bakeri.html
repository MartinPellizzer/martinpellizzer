Angophora bakeri

Angophora bakeri, commonly known as the narrow-leaf angophora, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. The tree is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. It produces small, pinkish-white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by woody, spherical capsules containing numerous seeds.

Angophora bakeri is typically found in coastal and near-coastal regions, often growing in sandy soils and open forests.
