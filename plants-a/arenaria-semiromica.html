Arenaria semiromica

Arenaria semiromica, commonly known as the Roman sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Arenaria semiromica is valued for its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
