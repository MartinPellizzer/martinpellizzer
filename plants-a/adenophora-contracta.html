Adenophora contracta

Adenophora contracta, commonly known as the narrowleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Adenophora contracta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
