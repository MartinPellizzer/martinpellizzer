Acetosella balsamifera

Acetosella balsamifera, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Violaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often prostrate or upright depending on the growing conditions. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating respiratory and skin conditions.
