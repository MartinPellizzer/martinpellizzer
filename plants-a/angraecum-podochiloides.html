Angraecum podochiloides

Angraecum podochiloides, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It features long, slender stems and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale blue flowers with prominent, spurred petals. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific nocturnal moths.

Angraecum podochiloides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its rarity and elegance.
