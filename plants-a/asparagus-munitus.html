Asparagus munitus

Asparagus munitus, commonly known as the mountain asparagus, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, fern-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are followed by small, edible berries. Asparagus munitus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
