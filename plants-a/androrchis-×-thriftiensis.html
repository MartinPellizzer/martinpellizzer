Androrchis × thriftiensis

Androrchis × thriftiensis, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a cross between Androrchis alpestris and Androrchis thyrsiflorus.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions of Europe. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
