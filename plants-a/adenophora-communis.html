Adenophora communis

Adenophora communis, commonly known as the common adenophora, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
