Amygdalus bucharica var. glabra

Amygdalus bucharica var. glabra, commonly known as the Central Asian peach or the Bukharan peach, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, often grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by oval, fuzzy fruits that ripen in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a sweet and aromatic flavor.

This variety is known for its hardiness and adaptability to arid and semi-arid climates.
