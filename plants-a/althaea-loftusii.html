Althaea loftusii

Althaea loftusii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to lavender flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and riparian areas. Its hairy stems and showy blooms make it a distinctive member of the mallow family.

Althaea loftusii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
