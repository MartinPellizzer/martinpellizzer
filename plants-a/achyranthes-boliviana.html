Achyranthes boliviana

Achyranthes boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian knotweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-yellow flowers that are typically inconspicuous. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along stream banks.

Achyranthes boliviana is valued for its ornamental potential and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
