Aquilegia recticornu

Aquilegia recticornu, commonly known as the straight-horned columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, purple, and blue. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are usually erect and hairy. Aquilegia recticornu thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
