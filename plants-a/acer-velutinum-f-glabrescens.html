Acer velutinum f. glabrescens

Acer velutinum f. glabrescens, commonly known as the velvet maple with a glabrous appearance, is a variant of the velvet maple.

It is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves that are less hairy compared to the typical variety. The plant is native to China and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, yellow-green flowers in spring, followed by winged seeds in the fall.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
