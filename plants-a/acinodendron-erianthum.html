Acinodendron erianthum

Acinodendron erianthum, commonly known as the woolly acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The woody stems are often used in traditional medicine and for making tools.
