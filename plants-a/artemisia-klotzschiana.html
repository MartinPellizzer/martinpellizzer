Artemisia klotzschiana

Artemisia klotzschiana, commonly known as Russian wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical clusters. Artemisia klotzschiana is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

It contains volatile oils that contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.
