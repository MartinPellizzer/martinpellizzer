Allium sipyleum

Allium sipyleum, commonly known as the Sipylean onion, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in the summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, similar to leeks.

Allium sipyleum is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
