Androcymbium melanthioides var. striatum

Androcymbium melanthioides var. striatum, commonly known as the striped sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant has narrow, ribbed leaves that are adapted to conserve water in dry environments. Its flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences at the base of the stems.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
