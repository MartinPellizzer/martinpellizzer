Ardisia violacea

Ardisia violacea, commonly known as the purple beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are visually striking and serve as a food source for various wildlife. Ardisia violacea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
