Aster × vimineus

Aster × vimineus, commonly known as the creeping aster, is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It blooms from late summer into early autumn, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Aster × vimineus is relatively drought-tolerant and prefers full sun to partial shade.
