Aellenia subaphylla subsp. turcomanica

Aellenia subaphylla subsp.

turcomanica, commonly known as the Turcoman sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low moisture. Its growth habit is prostrate, with stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground.

This subspecies is noted for its resilience in harsh environments, making it a hardy plant in xerophytic ecosystems.
