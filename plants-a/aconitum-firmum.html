Aconitum firmum

Aconitum firmum, commonly known as the alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its roots are toxic due to the presence of potent alkaloids. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Aconitum firmum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
