Acrostichum mascarenense

Acrostichum mascarenense, commonly known as the Mascarene fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal and forested areas. Its root system helps stabilize soil, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.

The fronds are used in traditional crafts and have cultural importance in the region.
