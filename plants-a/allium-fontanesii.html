Allium fontanesii

Allium fontanesii, commonly known as the Italian leek or mountain leek, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a useful plant in culinary applications.

Allium fontanesii thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
