Alternanthera paniculata

Alternanthera paniculata, commonly known as the red flame plant, is a striking aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to purple foliage, which becomes more intense under bright light conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in shallow water with moderate to high light exposure and is often used in aquariums and water gardens.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both decorative and ecological purposes.
