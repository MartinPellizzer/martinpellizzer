Asplenium lorentzii

Asplenium lorentzii, commonly known as the Lorentz's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the Aspleniaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically produces spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or forested areas.

Asplenium lorentzii is a relatively rare species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
