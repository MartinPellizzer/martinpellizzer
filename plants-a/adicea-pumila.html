Adicea pumila

Adicea pumila, commonly known as the smooth adicea, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling dandelion-like inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Adicea pumila thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
