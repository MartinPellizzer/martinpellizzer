Acacia caerulescens

Acacia caerulescens, commonly known as the blue acacia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Acacia caerulescens is adapted to arid conditions and is known for its drought tolerance.
