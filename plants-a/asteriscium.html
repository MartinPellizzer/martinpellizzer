Asteriscium

Asteriscium, commonly known as the star flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that resemble tiny stars, giving it its common name. The plants typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce showy inflorescences in shades of white, yellow, and pink. Asteriscium species are native to various regions in the Americas and are often found in open, sunny habitats.

They are popular in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
