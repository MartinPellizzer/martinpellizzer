Aspilia induta

Aspilia induta, commonly known as the white corymbia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hairs.

Aspilia induta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
