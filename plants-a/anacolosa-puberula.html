Anacolosa puberula

Anacolosa puberula, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom during the warm season.

The plant is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.
