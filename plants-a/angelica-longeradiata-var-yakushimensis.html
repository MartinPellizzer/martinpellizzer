Angelica longeradiata var. yakushimensis

Angelica longeradiata var. yakushimensis, commonly known as the Yakushiman Islands angelica, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is considered a valuable species in horticulture and traditional medicine.
