Arnicula

Arnicula, commonly known as mountain sorrel, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with reddish veins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in early spring. Arnicula is native to alpine and rocky regions in Europe and Asia, thriving in cool, moist environments.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties.
