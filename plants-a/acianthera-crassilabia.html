Acianthera crassilabia

Acianthera crassilabia, commonly known as the broad-lipped acianthera, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and possesses showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features a sympodial growth pattern, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette-like formation.

Acianthera crassilabia is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forest understories.
