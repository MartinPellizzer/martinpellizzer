Alnus crispa f. stragula

Alnus crispa f. stragula, commonly known as the hairy alder, is a variant of the alder tree characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage and bark.

This plant belongs to the Betulaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or along riverbanks. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

It produces small, catkin-like flowers and is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and habitat provision.
