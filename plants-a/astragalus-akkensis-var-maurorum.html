Astragalus akkensis var. maurorum

Astragalus akkensis var. maurorum, commonly known as the Turkish milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and thrives in semi-desert environments. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soils in arid landscapes.
