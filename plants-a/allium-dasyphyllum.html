Allium dasyphyllum

Allium dasyphyllum, commonly known as the giant garlic or mountain garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, strap-like leaves and produces large, spherical clusters of white or pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its strong garlic scent and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, preferring cooler climates.

Allium dasyphyllum is also valued for its edible bulbs, which are used in various culinary traditions.
