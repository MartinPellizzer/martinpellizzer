Astragalus pseudobagramiensis

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
