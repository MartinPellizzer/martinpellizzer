Astragalus georgicus

Astragalus georgicus, commonly known as the Georgian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, often growing at high altitudes. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Astragalus georgicus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
