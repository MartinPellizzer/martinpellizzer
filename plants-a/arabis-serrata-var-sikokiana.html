Arabis serrata var. sikokiana

Arabis serrata var. sikokiana, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptation to harsh environmental conditions. Its serrated leaves and compact growth habit make it a distinctive species within its genus.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.
