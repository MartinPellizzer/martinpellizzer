Antennaria aureola

Antennaria aureola, commonly known as the golden willow or yellow sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of North America and Eurasia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant features hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, yellowish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Antennaria aureola is adapted to harsh, cold environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
