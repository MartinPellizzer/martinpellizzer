Atriplex semibaccata f. tenuis

Atriplex semibaccata f. tenuis, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, typical of the Amaranthaceae family. It is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable plant for stabilizing degraded lands.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptation to harsh, dry environments.
