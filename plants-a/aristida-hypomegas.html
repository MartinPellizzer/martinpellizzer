Aristida hypomegas

Aristida hypomegas, commonly known as the narrow-leaf threeawn, is a grass species native to various regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-based flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Aristida hypomegas plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
