Anemone speciosa f. chrysantha

Anemone speciosa f. chrysantha, commonly known as the golden anemone, is a variant of the showy anemone characterized by its striking yellow flowers.

This plant is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States, particularly in woodland areas. Its flowers consist of numerous petal-like rays surrounding a central cluster of yellow stamens. The foliage is typically basal, with heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
