Aster pradensis

Aster pradensis, commonly known as the meadow aster, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. Aster pradensis belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other familiar flowering plants.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
