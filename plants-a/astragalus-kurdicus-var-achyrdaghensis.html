Astragalus kurdicus var. achyrdaghensis

Astragalus kurdicus var. achyrdaghensis, commonly known as the Achyrdagh milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its scientific study contributes to the understanding of plant diversity in the region.
