Acritopappus connatifolius

Acritopappus connatifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing a compact, shrub-like form with hairy, narrow leaves. Its flowers bloom in the spring, producing a showy display of yellow to orange hues, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant's adaptability to harsh conditions makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
