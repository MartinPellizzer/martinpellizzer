Arctium × scanicum

Arctium × scanicum, commonly known as the common burdock.

It is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of purple flowers that develop into spiky, burs-like fruits, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots are thick and fleshy, containing compounds that have been utilized for their potential health benefits.

Arctium × scanicum thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in waste areas.
