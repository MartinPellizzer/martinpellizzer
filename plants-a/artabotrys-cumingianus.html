Artabotrys cumingianus

Artabotrys cumingianus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow as they mature. Its leaves are typically ovate with a smooth texture, and the bark is rough and fissured.

Artabotrys cumingianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a shade tree.
