Acridocarpus alopecurus var. alopecurus

Acridocarpus alopecurus var. alopecurus, commonly known as the lesser bamboo grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight sheen, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
