Acalypha cordifolia var. polyadenia

Acalypha cordifolia var. polyadenia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or heartleaf acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
