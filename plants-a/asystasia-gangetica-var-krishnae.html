Asystasia gangetica var. krishnae

Asystasia gangetica var. krishnae, commonly known as Krishna's asystasia, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple to violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowers.
