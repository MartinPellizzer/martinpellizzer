Astrephia laciniata

Astrephia laciniata, commonly known as the lace-leaf astrephia, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage with finely cut leaves that give it a lacy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
