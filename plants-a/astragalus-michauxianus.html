Astragalus michauxianus

Astragalus michauxianus, commonly known as Michaux's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as prairies and open woodlands. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus michauxianus is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various pollinators.
