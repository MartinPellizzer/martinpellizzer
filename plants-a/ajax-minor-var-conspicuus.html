Ajax minor var. conspicuus

Ajax minor var. conspicuus, commonly known as the showy ajuga, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a distinctive blue-gray hue, and its flowers are small, tubular, and typically violet or blue in color. The plant thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil. It is often used in woodland gardens for its ornamental value and ability to suppress weeds.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to ecological landscapes.
