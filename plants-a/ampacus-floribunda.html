Ampacus floribunda

Ampacus floribunda, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Ampacus floribunda is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
