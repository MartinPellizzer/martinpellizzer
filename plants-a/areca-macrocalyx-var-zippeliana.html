Areca macrocalyx var. zippeliana

Areca macrocalyx var. zippeliana, commonly known as the Zippeliana areca, is a rare and ornamental palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that give it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by ornamental fruits that mature to a deep red color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
