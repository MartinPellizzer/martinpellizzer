Aporosa arborea

Aporosa arborea, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are often pink or white in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually have a serrated margin.

Aporosa arborea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
