Araeococcus lageniformis

Araeococcus lageniformis, commonly known as the bottlebrush plant, is a unique member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular flowers that resemble bottle brushes, typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant is native to tropical regions of South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually opposite and ovate, with a glossy surface that enhances its ornamental appeal.

Due to its striking floral display, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
