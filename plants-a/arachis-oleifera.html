Arachis oleifera

Arachis oleifera, commonly known as the wild peanut, is a species of legume native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is valued for its nutritional content, including proteins and oils, and is used in traditional medicine.

Due to its genetic diversity, it is also important in breeding programs for improving cultivated peanuts.
