Ampelopsis tricuspidata

Ampelopsis tricuspidata, commonly known as the three-toothed honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that transition into red, berry-like fruits. It is often found in moist woodland areas and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
