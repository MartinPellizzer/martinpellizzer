Adenocarpus boudyi var. pilosus

Adenocarpus boudyi var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the dandelion family. It thrives in grasslands and open habitats, often growing in dry conditions.
