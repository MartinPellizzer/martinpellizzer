Artemisia arbuscula subsp. longicaulis

Artemisia arbuscula subsp.

longicaulis, commonly known as the tall dwarf sage, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

This subspecies is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
