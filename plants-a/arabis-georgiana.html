Arabis georgiana

Arabis georgiana, commonly known as the Georgian rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Arabis georgiana thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments.
