Actaea taiwanensis

Actaea taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan bugbane, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that bear clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Actaea taiwanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
