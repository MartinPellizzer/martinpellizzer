Alstroemeria formosissima

Alstroemeria formosissima, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.

Alstroemeria formosissima is also valued for its long-lasting blooms and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
