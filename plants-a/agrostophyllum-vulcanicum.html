Agrostophyllum vulcanicum

Agrostophyllum vulcanicum, commonly known as the volcanic flax, is a rare and unique plant species native to the volcanic regions of New Zealand.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in nutrient-rich, volcanic soils and is adapted to the harsh environmental conditions of its habitat. Its growth is closely tied to the volcanic activity that shapes its environment.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
