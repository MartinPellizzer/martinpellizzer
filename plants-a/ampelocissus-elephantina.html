Ampelocissus elephantina

Ampelocissus elephantina, commonly known as the elephant's foot, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
