Aristolochia floribunda

Aristolochia floribunda, commonly known as the flowering Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, hooded flowers that are often maroon or purple in color. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and wasps. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, moist environments.
