Aspidium nigrescens

Aspidium nigrescens, commonly known as the black fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dark, leathery fronds that darken with age, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

Aspidium nigrescens is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
