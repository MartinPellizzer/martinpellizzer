Atraphaxis teretifolia

Atraphaxis teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf desertweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the rainy season. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater sources.
