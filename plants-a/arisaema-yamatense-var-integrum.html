Arisaema yamatense var. integrum

Arisaema yamatense var. integrum, commonly known as the Yamato arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are arrowhead-shaped, with a glossy green surface and prominent veins.

The spadix produces small, inconspicuous flowers, while the spathe serves as a protective structure.
