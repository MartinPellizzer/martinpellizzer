Antirrhinum glutinosum

Antirrhinum glutinosum, commonly known as sticky snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves and bright, showy flowers that range in color from red to yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds.

Antirrhinum glutinosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
