Avenula lodunensis subsp. albinervis

Avenula lodunensis subsp.

albinervis, commonly known as the white-veined sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of West Africa. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flower heads. Its leaves are typically pale green with distinct white veins, giving it its common name.
