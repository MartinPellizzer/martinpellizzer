Alopecurus fulvus var. natans

Alopecurus fulvus var. natans, commonly known as floating foxtail, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its floating growth habit, with leaves and inflorescences that remain on the water surface. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and ditches.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and serving as a food source for waterfowl.
