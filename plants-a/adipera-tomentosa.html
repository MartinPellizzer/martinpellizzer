Adipera tomentosa

Adipera tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly adipera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and small, clustered white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a soft, velvety texture.

Adipera tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
