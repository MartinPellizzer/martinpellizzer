Artemisia macrorhiza

Artemisia macrorhiza, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and distinctive, hollow stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Artemisia macrorhiza is known for its strong aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
