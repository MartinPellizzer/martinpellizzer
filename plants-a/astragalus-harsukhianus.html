Astragalus harsukhianus

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in racemes, and it produces pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Astragalus harsukhianus plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying plant resilience in extreme climates.
