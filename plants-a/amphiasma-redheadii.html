Amphiasma redheadii

Amphiasma redheadii, commonly known as the Redhead's amphiasma, is a rare and specialized plant species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and requires a humid, shaded environment to flourish. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in late spring or early summer.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Amphiasma redheadii is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists studying plant adaptation and conservation.
