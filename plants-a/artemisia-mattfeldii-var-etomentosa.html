Artemisia mattfeldii var. etomentosa

Artemisia mattfeldii var. etomentosa, commonly known as the wooly sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in dense, woolly hairs, providing a distinctive visual appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that appear in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, contributing to its strong, often pungent scent.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy component of sagebrush ecosystems.
