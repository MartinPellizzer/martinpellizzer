Aster puniceus var. perlongus

Aster puniceus var. perlongus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple petals and a prominent, spiky yellow center. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its bloom period typically occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
