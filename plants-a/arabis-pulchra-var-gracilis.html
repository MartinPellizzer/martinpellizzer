Arabis pulchra var. gracilis

Arabis pulchra var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, with small white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well-suited for alpine gardens. Its compact size and attractive foliage make it a desirable ornamental plant.

It is also valued for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
