Allium spathaceum

Allium spathaceum, commonly known as the white leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, pale green leaves. The plant produces upright flowering stems that bear spherical, pale purple to white flower clusters. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, often used in Mediterranean cuisine.

Allium spathaceum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental and culinary plant in temperate climates.
