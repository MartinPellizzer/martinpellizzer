Andropogon ivorensis

Andropogon ivorensis, commonly known as Ivorian bluestem, is a perennial grass native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Andropogon ivorensis is often used in traditional landscaping and for soil stabilization in its native habitat.
