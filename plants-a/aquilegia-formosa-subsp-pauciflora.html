Aquilegia formosa subsp. pauciflora

Aquilegia formosa subsp.

pauciflora, commonly known as the Sierra formosa columbine, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Nevada.

This subspecies is characterized by its slender stems and fewer flowers compared to other forms of Aquilegia formosa. Its flowers are typically blue to purple, with five sepals that are often not fully developed into petals.

The plant thrives in rocky soils and open habitats, often growing at higher elevations.
