Artemisia pengchuoensis

Artemisia pengchuoensis, commonly known as the Pengchuo wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cold, dry climates and well-drained soils. Its leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its strong medicinal properties.

Artemisia pengchuoensis is often used in traditional Tibetan medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic benefits.
