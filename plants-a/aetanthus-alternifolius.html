Aetanthus alternifolius

Aetanthus alternifolius, commonly known as the alternate-leaved aetanthus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
