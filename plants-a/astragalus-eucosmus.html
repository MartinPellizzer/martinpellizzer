Astragalus eucosmus

Astragalus eucosmus, commonly known as the western milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in late spring. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its seeds are often used in restoration projects due to their hardiness and adaptability.
