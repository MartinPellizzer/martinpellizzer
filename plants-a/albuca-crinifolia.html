Albuca crinifolia

Albuca crinifolia, commonly known as the featherleaf albuca, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, slender stems with clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant foliage and delicate floral display.
