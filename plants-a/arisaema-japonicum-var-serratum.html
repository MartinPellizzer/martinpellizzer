Arisaema japonicum var. serratum

Arisaema japonicum var. serratum, commonly known as the Japanese threadleaf arisaema, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its unique, umbrella-shaped inflorescence called a spadix, enclosed by a leaf-like bract. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, with a distinct, often unpleasant odor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
