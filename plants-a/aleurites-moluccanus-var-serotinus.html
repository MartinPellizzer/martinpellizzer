Aleurites moluccanus var. serotinus

Aleurites moluccanus var. serotinus, commonly known as the late-flowering nut tree, is a variant of the species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom later in the season compared to the standard variety. The tree produces small, spherical fruits that contain a single seed enclosed in a hard shell. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the oil extracted from its seeds.

This variant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils.
