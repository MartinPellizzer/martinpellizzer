Atriplex neurivalvis

Atriplex neurivalvis, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in desert or coastal environments. The plant has succulent, lobed leaves that help it conserve water in harsh conditions. Its small greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Atriplex neurivalvis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
