Astragalus podperae

Astragalus podperae, commonly known as the Podpera milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive pods that contain lens-shaped seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. Astragalus podperae is often found in rocky and arid habitats, showcasing its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of alpine ecosystems.
