Arctostaphylos mariposa

Arctostaphylos mariposa, commonly known as the Sierra sugar pine or the foothill manzanita, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, dense foliage, and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its adaptability to dry conditions makes it a resilient plant in Mediterranean climates.
