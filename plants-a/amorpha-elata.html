Amorpha elata

Amorpha elata, commonly known as yellow indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically light green in color. The plant produces showy, pea-like yellow flowers in early summer, which are followed by long, bean-like seed pods. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Amorpha elata is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
