Acacia saltiana

Acacia saltiana, commonly known as the saltbush acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, feathery leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is well adapted to saline and drought conditions, making it a hardy species for challenging environments. Its root system helps stabilize soil, and it is often used in rehabilitation of degraded lands.

Acacia saltiana is also valued for its potential in traditional medicine and as a source of animal forage.
