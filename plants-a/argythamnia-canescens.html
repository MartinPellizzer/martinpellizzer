Argythamnia canescens

Argythamnia canescens, commonly known as the desert sandthistle, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy, grayish foliage. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in spring, and the plant produces achenes that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in disturbed or unvegetated areas within its native range.
