Acacia coriacea subsp. coriacea

Acacia coriacea subsp.

coriacea, commonly known as the rough wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dark, ridged bark and compound leaves composed of multiple small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
