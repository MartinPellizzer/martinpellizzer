Anthericum curvifolium

Anthericum curvifolium, commonly known as the curly-leaved arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves that are often curled or twisted, giving it its common name. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a spathe, a typical feature of the Araceae family. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it relies on insects for pollination.

Anthericum curvifolium thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.
