Astragalus mesopotamicus var. olivieri

Astragalus mesopotamicus var. olivieri, commonly known as the oliveleaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

This species is valued for its potential in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
