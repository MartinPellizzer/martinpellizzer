Astragalus admirabilus

Astragalus admirabilus, commonly known as the noble milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. Astragalus admirabilus thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-elevation ecosystems.
