Acampe praemorsa

Acampe praemorsa, commonly known as the death's-head hawkmoth, is a striking plant characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, bioluminescent flowers.

Native to South Africa, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to conserve water. The plant's flowers emit a strong, sweet scent at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Acampe praemorsa is also notable for its ability to produce toxic compounds that deter herbivores.
