Andropogon sorghum var. irungiformis

Andropogon sorghum var. irungiformis, commonly known as the African millet, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence. The plant typically grows in dry, nutrient-poor soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Its small, spikelet-like flowers are wind-pollinated, and it produces small, hard grains that are used as a food source.

This species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
