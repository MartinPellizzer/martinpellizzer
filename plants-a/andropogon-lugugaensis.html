Andropogon lugugaensis

Andropogon lugugaensis, commonly known as the Luguga grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the highlands of eastern Africa, particularly in countries like Rwanda and Uganda. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Andropogon lugugaensis is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as montane forests and stream banks.
