Amaryllis × lateritia

Amaryllis × lateritia, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a popular ornamental plant native to South America.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically green with a slight waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Amaryllis × lateritia is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and ease of care, making it a favorite among gardeners.
