Aglaia urdanetensis

Aglaia urdanetensis, commonly known as the Urdanet angel's trumpet, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, orange or red aril. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its attractive appearance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest in both horticulture and pharmacological research.
