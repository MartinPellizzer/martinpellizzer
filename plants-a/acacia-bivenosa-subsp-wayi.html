Acacia bivenosa subsp. wayi

Acacia bivenosa subsp.

wayi, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures in adult plants.

The plant produces yellow to golden-yellow inflorescences, which are composed of numerous small, clustered flowers. Its seeds are encased in a woody pod, and the plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Acacia bivenosa subsp.

wayi is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fuelwood in its native habitat.
