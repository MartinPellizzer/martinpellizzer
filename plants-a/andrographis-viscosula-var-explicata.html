Andrographis viscosula var. explicata

Andrographis viscosula var. explicata, commonly known as the broad-leaved andrographis, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often broader than those of the typical species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes, and its stems are covered in a sticky resin. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The leaves and stems are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
