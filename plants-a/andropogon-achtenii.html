Andropogon achtenii

Andropogon achtenii, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves.

It is native to Africa and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its fibrous roots contribute to soil stabilization, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.

Andropogon achtenii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional practices.
