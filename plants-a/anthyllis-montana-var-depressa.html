Anthyllis montana var. depressa

Anthyllis montana var. depressa, commonly known as the mountain anthyllis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is adapted to rocky and arid environments, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important species in soil enrichment and ecological succession.
