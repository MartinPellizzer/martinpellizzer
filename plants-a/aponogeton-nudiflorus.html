Aponogeton nudiflorus

Aponogeton nudiflorus, commonly known as the naked-flower arrowhead, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, arrowhead-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, with the roots submerged in the substrate. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically hidden beneath the leaves, giving it its common name. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Aponogeton nudiflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in ponds and water gardens.
