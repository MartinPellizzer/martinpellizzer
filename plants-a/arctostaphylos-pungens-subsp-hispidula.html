Arctostaphylos pungens subsp. hispidula

Arctostaphylos pungens subsp.

hispidula, commonly known as the roughleaf manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with rough, hairy surfaces.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in white or pink hues, which bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a capsule that releases small, shiny seeds in the fall.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
