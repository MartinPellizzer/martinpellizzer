Acacia chinchillensis

Acacia chinchillensis, commonly known as the Chinchilla acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular inflorescences, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits.

Acacia chinchillensis is adapted to dry, arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
