Acer pictum f. ambiguum

Acer pictum f. ambiguum, commonly known as the variegated maple, is a unique variant of the striped maple.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and yellow patterns. This ornamental tree is native to the northeastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It typically grows to a height of 15 to 25 feet and features delicate, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental foliage.
