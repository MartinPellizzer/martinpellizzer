Abutilon pinkavae

Abutilon pinkavae, commonly known as the pink velvet abutilon, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Abutilon pinkavae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and attractive flowers.
