Aletris pauciflora var. pauciflora

Aletris pauciflora var. pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered aletris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in acidic soil conditions. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale green flowers in late spring. Its inflorescence is a loose cluster of flowers that are often subtended by bracts.

Aletris pauciflora var. pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
