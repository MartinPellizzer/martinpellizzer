Arundinella cochinchinensis

Arundinella cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or sweet bamboo, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically found at the top of the stems. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Arundinella cochinchinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
