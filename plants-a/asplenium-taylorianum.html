Asplenium taylorianum

Asplenium taylorianum, commonly known as the Taylor's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks.

Asplenium taylorianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
