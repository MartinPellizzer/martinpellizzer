Aechmea nudicaulis

Aechmea nudicaulis, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater and nutrients. The plant produces a tall flower spike with colorful bracts that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are typically broad, stiff, and covered with a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention.

Aechmea nudicaulis is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea nudicaulis has rosette-shaped leaves that are sword-like, arranged in a tight spiral, with a bluish-gray color and a waxy coating.

The plant has a short, thick stem that is often hidden beneath the leaves, and the bark is smooth with a grayish-green hue. Its flowers are clustered in a spike, with vibrant red bracts that enclose small white or pale pink flowers, blooming in the summer months and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and help anchor the plant in its epiphytic or terrestrial habitat.
