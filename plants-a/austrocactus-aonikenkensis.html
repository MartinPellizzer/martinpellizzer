Austrocactus aonikenkensis

Austrocactus aonikenkensis, commonly known as the Aonikenk cactus, is a rare and endangered species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height, covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
