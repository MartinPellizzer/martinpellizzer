Agoseris glauca var. parviflora

Agoseris glauca var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered blue gromwell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
