Aira ischoemoides

Aira ischoemoides, commonly known as the slender false oat, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its reduced leaf sheaths and the presence of glumes in its flowering structure.
