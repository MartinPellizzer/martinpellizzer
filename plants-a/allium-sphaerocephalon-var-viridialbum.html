Allium sphaerocephalon var. viridialbum

Allium sphaerocephalon var. viridialbum, commonly known as the green bulb onion, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features a bulbous stem that is typically green in color, with a mild, sweet flavor. The plant produces spherical, white or pale purple flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and culinary use in various cuisines.
