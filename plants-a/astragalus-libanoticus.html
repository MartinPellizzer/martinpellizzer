Astragalus libanoticus

Astragalus libanoticus, commonly known as the Lebanon milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Astragalus libanoticus plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local biodiversity in its native habitat.
