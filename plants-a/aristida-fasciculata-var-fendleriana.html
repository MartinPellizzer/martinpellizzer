Aristida fasciculata var. fendleriana

Aristida fasciculata var. fendleriana, commonly known as the Fendler's threeawn, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, three-awned spikelets, giving it its common name. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

This species is often found in disturbed areas and is used in some regions for erosion control due to its hardy nature.
