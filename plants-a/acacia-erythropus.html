Acacia erythropus

Acacia erythropus, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
