Aparisthmium cordatum

Aparisthmium cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts. It has hairy stems and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
