Astragalus leptus subsp. ghazniensis

Astragalus leptus subsp.

ghazniensis, commonly known as the Ghazni milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves.

This subspecies is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

The plant is also of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
