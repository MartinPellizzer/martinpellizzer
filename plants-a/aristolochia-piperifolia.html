Aristolochia piperifolia

Aristolochia piperifolia, commonly known as the Indian Birthwort, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy texture and a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor. The plant produces unique, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a prominent hood-like structure. It is often found in moist, shaded environments in its native habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is cultivated in some regions despite its toxic properties.
