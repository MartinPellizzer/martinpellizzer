Ardisia mexicana

Ardisia mexicana, commonly known as Mexican beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and rhododendrons. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. These berries are an important food source for various bird species.

Its evergreen foliage and ornamental berries make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
