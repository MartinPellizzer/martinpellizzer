Anoda urophylla

Anoda urophylla, commonly known as the hairy bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Anoda urophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
