Atriplex × hulmeana

Atriplex × hulmeana, commonly known as the hybrid saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a natural hybrid between Atriplex nummularia and Atriplex lentifolia, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable species for rehabilitation of degraded lands. Its leaves are small, succulent, and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Atriplex × hulmeana plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
