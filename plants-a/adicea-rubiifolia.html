Adicea rubiifolia

Adicea rubiifolia, commonly known as the red-leafed dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, velvety red foliage that turns vibrant orange in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries. Adicea rubiifolia thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for woodland gardens and naturalized landscapes.
