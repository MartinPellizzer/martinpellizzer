Achillea aucheri subsp. glabra

Achillea aucheri subsp.

glabra, commonly known as white yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features a compact, bushy growth habit with finely divided, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and long flowering period contribute to its ornamental value in horticulture.
