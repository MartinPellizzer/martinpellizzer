Apium luteum

Apium luteum, commonly known as yellow hogweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided leaves and umbel-shaped flowers. The plant can grow up to 2 meters tall, with a smooth, often reddish stem that becomes woody at the base. Its flowers are yellowish-green, and it produces small, oval berries that contain the seeds.

Apium luteum is often found in moist habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
