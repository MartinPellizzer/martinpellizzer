Amomum velutinum

Amomum velutinum, commonly known as the velvet ginger, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and erect, branched inflorescences that bear small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces dark brown, oval-shaped rhizomes that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
