Argophyllum

Argophyllum, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, white or pink flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. Argophyllum species are often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in horticulture for their unique appearance.
