Andromeda calyculata var. latifolia

Andromeda calyculata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf wintergreen, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically broad and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white, urn-shaped flowers in clusters during late winter to early spring. Its leaves and stems emit a strong, spicy fragrance when crushed, which is characteristic of the genus Andromeda.

The species is adapted to moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.
