Allium sintenisii

Allium sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenisi onion, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in Bolivia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, bulbous structures at the base, which are the primary storage organs for nutrients. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a slight bluish tinge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited habitat and fragile ecosystem, Allium sintenisii is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
