Artocarpus Altilis Var. Non-Seminifer

Artocarpus altilis var. non-seminifer is a lesser-known variant of the breadfruit tree, known for its unique botanical characteristics. This plant is native to tropical regions of the Pacific and Southeast Asia, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. Unlike its more common counterpart, this variant exhibits distinct features in its reproductive structures and growth patterns. Its fruit, though not typically used for human consumption, plays a role in local ecosystems and traditional practices. Studying this variant offers insights into genetic diversity and adaptation within the Artocarpus genus.
