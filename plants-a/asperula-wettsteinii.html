Asperula wettsteinii

Asperula wettsteinii, commonly known as the Wettstein's evergreen azalea, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and rhododendrons, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring season. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Asperula wettsteinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
