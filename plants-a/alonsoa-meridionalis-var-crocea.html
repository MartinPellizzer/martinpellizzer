Alonsoa meridionalis var. crocea

Alonsoa meridionalis var. crocea, commonly known as the yellow alonsoa, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. It is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely distributed beyond its native range.
