Acrocephalus hispidus

Acrocephalus hispidus, commonly known as the hairy marsh grass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. Its flowering stems bear spikelets that are often pale green to brown in color.

Acrocephalus hispidus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
