Allium flavum f. bulbilliferum

Allium flavum f. bulbilliferum, commonly known as the purple onion or bulbil-bearing leek, is a botanical variant of the common onion.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces bulbils along its stem, which are miniature bulbs used for propagation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in temperate regions.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential culinary use.
