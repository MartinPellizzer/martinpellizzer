Alstroemeria philippii subsp. adrianae

Alstroemeria philippii subsp.

adrianae, commonly known as the Adriana alstroemeria, is a South American flowering plant native to Chile. It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
