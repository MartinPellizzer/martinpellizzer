Acacia huegelii var. glauca

Acacia huegelii var. glauca, commonly known as the blue acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching form and small, glossy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It is adapted to hot, dry climates and has deep root systems to access water from underground sources.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine by indigenous peoples for their astringent and anti-inflammatory properties.
