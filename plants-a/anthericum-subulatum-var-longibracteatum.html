Anthericum subulatum var. longibracteatum

Anthericum subulatum var. longibracteatum, commonly known as the long-bract lily, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and striking, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces long, lance-shaped bracts that enclose the flower buds, giving it its distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
