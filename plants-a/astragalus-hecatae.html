Astragalus hecatae

Astragalus hecatae, commonly known as the sacred milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, featuring deep taproots and small, hairy leaves to reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to white, with a distinctive spurred structure at the base of the petals.

Astragalus hecatae is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
