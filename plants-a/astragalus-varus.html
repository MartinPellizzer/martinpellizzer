Astragalus varus

Astragalus varus, commonly known as the variable milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Astragalus varus has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh environments.

It is often found in prairies, deserts, and along dry streambeds.
