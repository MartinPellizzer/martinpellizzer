Acalypha adenophora

Acalypha adenophora, commonly known as the woolly hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, or woolly leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, often yellow or red, bract-based flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and may have a slight serration along the edges.

Acalypha adenophora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
