Aster multiflorus var. normalis

Aster multiflorus var. normalis, commonly known as the common aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of purple, blue, and white, which bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Aster multiflorus var. normalis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
