Atriplex gmelinii var. alaskensis

Atriplex gmelinii var. alaskensis, commonly known as Alaska saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Alaska and northern Canada.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to retain water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in compact clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in coastal and desert areas.

Its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil make it ecologically significant in its native habitat.
