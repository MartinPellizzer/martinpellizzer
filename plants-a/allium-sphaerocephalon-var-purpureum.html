Allium sphaerocephalon var. purpureum

Allium sphaerocephalon var. purpureum, commonly known as the purple globeflower, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, spherical purple or pink flower heads that bloom in late summer, making it a visually distinctive ornamental plant. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
