Astragalus drasianus

Astragalus drasianus, commonly known as the Drasian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Tajikistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of many Astragalus species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
