Asarum europaeum var. intermedium

Asarum europaeum var. intermedium, commonly known as European wild ginger, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive, spicy aroma. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by dark blue to black berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Aristolochiaceae family.
