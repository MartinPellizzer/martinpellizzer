Arthraxon langsdorffii var. submuticus

Arthraxon langsdorffii var. submuticus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arthraxon, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
