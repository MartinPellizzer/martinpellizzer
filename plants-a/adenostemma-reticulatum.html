Adenostemma reticulatum

Adenostemma reticulatum, commonly known as the net-veined adenostemma, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that typically grows to a height of 30 to 100 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with prominent net-like venation, which is a characteristic feature of the genus. Its flowers are usually small and clustered in loose inflorescences, with tubular corollas that are often pink or purple in color.

Adenostemma reticulatum is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.
