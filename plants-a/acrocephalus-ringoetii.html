Acrocephalus ringoetii

Acrocephalus ringoetii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water-dropwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, upright stems that bear small, white flowers arranged in dense, umbrella-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a distinct midrib.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and its role in supporting local aquatic ecosystems.
