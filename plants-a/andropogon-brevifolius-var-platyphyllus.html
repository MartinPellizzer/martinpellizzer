Andropogon brevifolius var. platyphyllus

Andropogon brevifolius var. platyphyllus, commonly known as the broadleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are broader than those of the typical variety, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, blue-green flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairie habitats.

Its growth habit makes it a valuable component of native plant communities and a potential candidate for restoration projects.
