Aeschynomene maculata

Aeschynomene maculata, commonly known as the spotted gothic, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous annual that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a distinctive purplish or reddish discoloration on the underside. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Aeschynomene maculata is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some regions for forage or ornamental purposes.
