Amygdalus communis var. pyramidalis

Amygdalus communis var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the Pyramidal almond, is a cultivar of the almond tree characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by the development of almond fruits. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive form and hardy nature.

The variety is also valued for its potential in nut production, although it may yield smaller nuts compared to other almond cultivars.
