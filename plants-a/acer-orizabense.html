Acer orizabense

Acer orizabense, commonly known as the Orizaba maple, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The Orizaba maple is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
