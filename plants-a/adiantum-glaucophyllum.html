Adiantum glaucophyllum

Adiantum glaucophyllum, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern native to the southeastern United States.

It features intricate, finely divided fronds that are typically silvery-white on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its fronds are biennial, with the fertile fronds appearing in the second year.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
