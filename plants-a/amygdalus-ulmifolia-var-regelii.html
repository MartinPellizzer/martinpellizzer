Amygdalus ulmifolia var. regelii

Amygdalus ulmifolia var. regelii, commonly known as the Regel's peachtree, is a variant of the peachtree species native to Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible fruits that are typically less sweet than those of the common peach.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates, making it a hardy ornamental plant in suitable environments.
