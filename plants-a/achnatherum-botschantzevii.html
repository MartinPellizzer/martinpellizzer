Achnatherum botschantzevii

Achnatherum botschantzevii, commonly known as the Botchanzev's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky soils. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a single, dense spikelet that is often surrounded by bracts.

Achnatherum botschantzevii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
