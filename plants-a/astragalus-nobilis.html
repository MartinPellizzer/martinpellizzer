Astragalus nobilis

Astragalus nobilis, commonly known as noble milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Astragalus nobilis is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
