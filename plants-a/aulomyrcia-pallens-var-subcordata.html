Aulomyrcia pallens var. subcordata

Aulomyrcia pallens var. subcordata, commonly known as the slender lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are clustered in terminal racemes, and the plant prefers moist, well-drained soils in partial to full shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in woodland gardens.
