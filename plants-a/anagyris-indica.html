Anagyris indica

Anagyris indica, commonly known as the Indian burr or Indian thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring woody stems and thorny branches. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Anagyris indica is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry climates.
