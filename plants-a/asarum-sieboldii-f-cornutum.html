Asarum sieboldii f. cornutum

Asarum sieboldii f. cornutum, commonly known as the horned wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often mottled with purple and green. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically hidden beneath its foliage. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its aromatic rhizomes.

The unique horn-like structures on its flowers give it its distinctive appearance and name.
