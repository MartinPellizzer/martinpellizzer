Atylosia marmorata

Atylosia marmorata, commonly known as the marble violet, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features a low-growing rosette of bluish-green, waxy leaves with a distinctive marbled pattern. The plant produces showy violet-blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Atylosia marmorata is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
