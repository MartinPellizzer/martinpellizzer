Acacia robusta

Acacia robusta, commonly known as the black wattle, is a tall, woody shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in globular clusters. Acacia robusta is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It plays an important ecological role, providing habitat and food for various native animals.
