Aconitum myoctonum

Aconitum myoctonum, commonly known as the purple aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a spire-like inflorescence, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, somewhat fern-like appearance.

Aconitum myoctonum is known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that make it dangerous to both humans and animals.
