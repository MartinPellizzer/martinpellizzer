Amaryllis fothergillii

Amaryllis fothergillii, commonly known as the Fothergill's amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that produce clusters of vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant blooms in late summer to early autumn, making it a notable addition to gardens during the transitional seasons. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are evergreen, providing visual interest throughout the year.

Amaryllis fothergillii is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
