Artemisia genipi var. accedens

Artemisia genipi var. accedens, commonly known as the golden thread, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of tiny hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid landscapes.

Due to its aromatic foliage, it is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
