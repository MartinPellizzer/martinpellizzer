Acacia gibsonii

Acacia gibsonii, commonly known as the Gibson's acacia, is a small, thorny shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves that are often reduced to spines, a common adaptation to minimize water loss in hot, dry climates. Its woody pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its survival in harsh environments.

Acacia gibsonii is valued for its hardiness and is often used in ornamental gardening in regions with suitable climates.
