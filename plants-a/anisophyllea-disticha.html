Anisophyllea disticha

Anisophyllea disticha, commonly known as the twining anisophyllea, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by oval, woody fruits. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a distinct venation pattern.

Anisophyllea disticha is often found growing in shaded, humid environments in the understory of forests.
