Acacia retinens

Acacia retinens, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular inflorescences. Acacia retinens is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

It is also valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in local communities.
