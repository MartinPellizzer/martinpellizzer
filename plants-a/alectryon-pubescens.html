Alectryon pubescens

Alectryon pubescens, commonly known as the silky acanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces showy, spire-like inflorescences with clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Alectryon pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
