Artabotrys longipetalus

Artabotrys longipetalus, commonly known as the white beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
