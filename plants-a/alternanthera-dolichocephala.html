Alternanthera dolichocephala

Alternanthera dolichocephala, commonly known as the long-headed Alternanthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescences that bear small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage is often bright red or purple, adding ornamental value to aquatic gardens.

It is a popular choice for water gardens due to its vibrant color and adaptability to aquatic conditions.
