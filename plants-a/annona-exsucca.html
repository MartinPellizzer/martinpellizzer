Annona exsucca

Annona exsucca, commonly known as the dry annona, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers with a strong, unpleasant odor, which are followed by round, green fruits that turn yellow when mature.

The fruit is typically eaten raw or used in local cuisines, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
