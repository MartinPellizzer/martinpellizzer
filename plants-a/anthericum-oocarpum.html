Anthericum oocarpum

Anthericum oocarpum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spiky bracts that surround the flowers. It thrives in saline and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species adapted to coastal environments.

Anthericum oocarpum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is considered a symbol of resilience in harsh climates.
