Angelica polymorpha f. lineariloba

Angelica polymorpha f. lineariloba, commonly known as the linear-leaved angelica, is a variant of the angelica plant characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and grows in alpine environments. The plant produces tall, branched stems with small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its root system is thick and aromatic, similar to other angelica species, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

This subspecies is valued for its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
