Acacia suaveolens var. platycarpa

Acacia suaveolens var. platycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruit acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
