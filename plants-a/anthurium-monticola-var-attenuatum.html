Anthurium monticola var. attenuatum

Anthurium monticola var. attenuatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved anthurium, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are usually dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are modified leaves that house the small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium monticola var. attenuatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
