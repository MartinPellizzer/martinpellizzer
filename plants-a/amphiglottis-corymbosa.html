Amphiglottis corymbosa

Amphiglottis corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. Amphiglottis corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
