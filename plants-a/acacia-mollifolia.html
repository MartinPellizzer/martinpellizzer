Acacia mollifolia

Acacia mollifolia, commonly known as the soft-leaved acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and woodland habitats.

Acacia mollifolia is also valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used in traditional crafts and construction.
