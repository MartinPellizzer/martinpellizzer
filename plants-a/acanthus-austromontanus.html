Acanthus austromontanus

Acanthus austromontanus, commonly known as the southern mountain acanthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its appeal in horticulture.
