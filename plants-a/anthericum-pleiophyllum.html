Anthericum pleiophyllum

Anthericum pleiophyllum, commonly known as the clustered fern, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae, which includes plants like philodendrons and peace lilies. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, heart-shaped leaves that form a rosette pattern. It produces spadices, which are the typical inflorescences of the Araceae family, containing both male and female flowers.

Anthericum pleiophyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
