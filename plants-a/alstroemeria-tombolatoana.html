Alstroemeria tombolatoana

Alstroemeria tombolatoana, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a striking South American plant native to the Andes region.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in rocky or mountainous habitats. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Alstroemeria tombolatoana is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for floral arrangements.
