Andropogon villosus f. apogynus

Andropogon villosus f. apogynus, commonly known as the silky grass or hairy bluestem, is a variant of the tallgrass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy stems that are often tinged with silver. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are often pale green to blue-gray in color.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
