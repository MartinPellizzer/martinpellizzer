Allium bajtulinii

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. This plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and exhibits a compact growth habit with small, narrow leaves. Its flowers are usually pale blue to purple, arranged in dense, spherical clusters.
