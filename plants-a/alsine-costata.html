Alsine costata

Alsine costata, commonly known as the serrated alsike, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and serrated, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer.
