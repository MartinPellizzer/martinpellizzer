Atractylodes japponica var. coriacea

Atractylodes japponica var. coriacea, commonly known as Japanese ginger or mountain ginger, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous root system and distinctive, rhomboidal leaves with a rough, coriaceous texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is valued in traditional herbal medicine for its digestive and anti-inflammatory benefits.
