Alternanthera pungens var. monocephala

Alternanthera pungens var. monocephala, commonly known as the red flame plant, is a strikingly ornamental species native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to purple foliage, which becomes more intense under full sun exposure. The plant typically grows as an upright herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge, adding to its visual appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is popular in water gardens and pond settings.
