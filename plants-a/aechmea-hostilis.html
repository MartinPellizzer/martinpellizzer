Aechmea hostilis

Aechmea hostilis, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround the flower, giving it its common name. The plant features a rosette of grass-like leaves that collect water, forming a central reservoir. Its flowering stems produce clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers.

Aechmea hostilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.

Morphology

Aechmea hostilis has rosette-shaped leaves that are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a dark green color and a waxy surface.

The plant has a short, thick stem that is often hidden beneath the leaves, with a smooth, greenish-brown bark. Its flowers are clustered in a spike-like inflorescence, with white petals and a yellow center, blooming during the summer months and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a bright red, berry-like structure that contains small seeds dispersed by animals.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the surrounding soil.
