Aptosimum

Aptosimum, commonly known as the tansy or goldenroot, is a perennial herb belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a persistent weed in some areas.

Aptosimum has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
