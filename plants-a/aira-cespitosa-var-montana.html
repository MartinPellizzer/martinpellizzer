Aira cespitosa var. montana

Aira cespitosa var. montana, commonly known as mountain air grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
