Agave parryi subsp. parryi

Agave parryi subsp.

parryi, commonly known as Parry's agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its rosette of thick, blue-gray leaves with white to pale green margins.

The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence bearing yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
