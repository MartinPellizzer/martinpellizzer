Argentina smithiana

Argentina smithiana, commonly known as the Smith's lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring, producing vibrant pink or red petals that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem, contributing to its elegant appearance.

Due to its ornamental value and unique botanical features, Argentina smithiana is a sought-after plant in horticultural collections.
