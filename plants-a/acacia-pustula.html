Acacia pustula

Acacia pustula, commonly known as the burr acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and spiny, papery pods that give it a unique appearance. The plant has small, leathery leaves and thrives in dry, sandy soils. It is often found in coastal and inland areas, adapting well to arid conditions.

Acacia pustula plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
