Arundo pallens

Arundo pallens, commonly known as the silver reed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and grayish-green foliage that gives it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or riparian habitats, often forming dense stands along riverbanks and marshy areas. Its flowering spikes are slender and produce small, white to pale green flowers.

Arundo pallens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
