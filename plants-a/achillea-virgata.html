Achillea virgata

Achillea virgata, commonly known as the slender yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.

Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
