Aquilegia nigricans

Aquilegia nigricans, commonly known as the black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features showy, pentagonal flowers that range in color from deep red to purple, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate divisions, and its stems are hairy and upright. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade.

The roots are often harvested for medicinal use, though its botanical characteristics are well-adapted to its natural environment.
