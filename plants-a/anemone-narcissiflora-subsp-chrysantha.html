Anemone narcissiflora subsp. chrysantha

Anemone narcissiflora subsp.

chrysantha, commonly known as the golden anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It features showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has palmate, deeply lobed leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and wildflower displays.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
