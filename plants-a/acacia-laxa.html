Acacia laxa

Acacia laxa, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves that are small and clustered. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular spikes, which appear in spring and attract various pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Acacia laxa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
