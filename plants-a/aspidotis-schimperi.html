Aspidotis schimperi

Aspidotis schimperi, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, attracting various pollinators. Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic, which serves as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Aspidotis schimperi is often found in disturbed habitats and is adapted to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
