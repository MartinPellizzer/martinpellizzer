Anemone oligotoma

Anemone oligotoma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has delicate, white to pale pink flowers with five petals. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its ability to thrive in woodland environments.

Anemone oligotoma is often used in native plant gardens due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological value.
