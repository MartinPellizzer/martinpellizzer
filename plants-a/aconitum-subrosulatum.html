Aconitum subrosulatum

Aconitum subrosulatum, commonly known as the alpine aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with deeply lobed margins, and its stems are often covered in a dense layer of silky hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
