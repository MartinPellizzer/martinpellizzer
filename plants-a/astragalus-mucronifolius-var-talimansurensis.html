Astragalus mucronifolius var. talimansurensis

Astragalus mucronifolius var. talimansurensis, commonly known as the Talimansurensis milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and trifoliate, with small, rounded leaflets, and it produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
