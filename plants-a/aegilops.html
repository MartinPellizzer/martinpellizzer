Aegilops

Aegilops, commonly known as goatgrass, is a genus of wild grasses belonging to the tribe Avenae.

These plants are native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Aegilops species are characterized by their slender, grass-like appearance and produce small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. They are often found growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Some species of Aegilops have been studied for their potential in breeding programs to improve wheat varieties.
