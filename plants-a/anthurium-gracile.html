Anthurium gracile

Anthurium gracile, commonly known as the slender anthurium, is a tropical flowering plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive spathe and spadix inflorescence. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. It produces striking, long-lasting flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which are followed by small, white berries.

Anthurium gracile thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
