Aruncus dioicus var. pubescens

Aruncus dioicus var. pubescens, commonly known as the fuzzy goat's beard, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence composed of numerous small, white to cream-colored bracts. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The varietal name "pubescens" refers to its pubescent or fuzzy texture, which is a notable botanical feature.
