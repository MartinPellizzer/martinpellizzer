Achillea bucharica

Achillea bucharica, commonly known as the Caucasian yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and flat, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Achillea bucharica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
