Acalypha karwinskii

Acalypha karwinskii, commonly known as the karwinskii spurflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble petals, often displaying vibrant colors such as red, orange, or yellow. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Acalypha karwinskii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
