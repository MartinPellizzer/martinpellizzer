Astragalus montanus var. coloradoensis

Astragalus montanus var. coloradoensis, commonly known as the Colorado milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces spiny, yellow to brown flowers in late spring. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
