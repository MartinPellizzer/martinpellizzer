Agoseris glauca var. monticola

Agoseris glauca var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats such as meadows and slopes. It has a taproot system and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Its small, daisy-like flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
