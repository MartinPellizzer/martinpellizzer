Alchemilla sintenisii

Alchemilla sintenisii, commonly known as the Swiss stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a powdery bloom. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
