Aloe trichosantha subsp. longiflora

Aloe trichosantha subsp.

longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features robust, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow to orange flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its growth habit is often shrub-like, with multiple stems emerging from a central base.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry climates.
