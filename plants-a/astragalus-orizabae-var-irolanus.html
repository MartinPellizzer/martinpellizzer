Astragalus orizabae var. irolanus

Astragalus orizabae var. irolanus, commonly known as the Mexican milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is known for its deep penetration, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

This species is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
