Arundarbor luconiae

Arundarbor luconiae, commonly known as the silver-veined sunleaf, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
