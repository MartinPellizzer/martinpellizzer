Amerimnon tabascanum

Amerimnon tabascanum, commonly known as the Mexican fireplant, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments and is known for its hardy nature. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
