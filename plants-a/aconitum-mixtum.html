Aconitum mixtum

Aconitum mixtum, commonly known as the mixed monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, hood-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed foliage that is often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Aconitum mixtum is known for its toxicity, containing potent alkaloids that make it dangerous to handle or ingest.
