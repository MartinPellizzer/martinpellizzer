Aconitum tauricum subsp. tauricum

Aconitum tauricum subsp.

tauricum, commonly known as the Caucasian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It features striking, hooded blue to purple flowers that are characteristic of the Aconitum genus.

The plant has dark green, doubly pinnate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic nature, it should be handled with care.
