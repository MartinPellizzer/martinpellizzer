Acer undulatum

Acer undulatum, commonly known as the wavycup maple, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy-edged leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by clusters of red, winged fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
