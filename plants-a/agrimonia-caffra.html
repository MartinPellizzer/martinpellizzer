Agrimonia caffra

Agrimonia caffra, commonly known as the African agrimony, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and branched stems. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically pinnately divided, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Agrimonia caffra is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
