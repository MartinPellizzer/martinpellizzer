Apium sium

Apium sium, commonly known as wild celery, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbels. Its roots and leaves are edible and have a mild, celery-like flavor.

It is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.
