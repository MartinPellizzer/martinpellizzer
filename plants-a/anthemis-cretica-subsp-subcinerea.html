Anthemis cretica subsp. subcinerea

Anthemis cretica subsp.

subcinerea, commonly known as the silver anthemion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves with a fuzzy texture, and its flowers are small, yellow, and often arranged in a flat-topped cluster. It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

The subspecies subcinerea is distinguished by its more silvery foliage compared to the typical form of Anthemis cretica.
