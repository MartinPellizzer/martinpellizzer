Asterias

Asterias, commonly known as the starfish, is a marine invertebrate belonging to the class Asteroidea.

It is characterized by its radial symmetry, typically featuring five or more arms radiating from a central disc. The body is covered in small, flexible tube feet used for movement and feeding. Asterias plays a vital role in marine ecosystems by controlling populations of bivalves and other prey.

Its unique anatomy and ecological significance make it an important subject in marine biology.
