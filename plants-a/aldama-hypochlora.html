Aldama hypochlora

Aldama hypochlora, commonly known as the white-flowered aldama, is a shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, oval fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its spread.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
