Acacia pulverulenta

Acacia pulverulenta, commonly known as the ghost gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, pale grey bark that peels away in thin layers, giving it a distinctive, almost ghostly appearance. The plant has compound leaves composed of several narrow, linear leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers in spherical clusters. Acacia pulverulenta is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for landscaping in dry regions.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
