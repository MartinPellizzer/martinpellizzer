Artemisia implicata

Artemisia implicata, commonly known as western goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its small, yellowish-green flower heads are clustered in dense, flat-topped clusters.

Artemisia implicata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
