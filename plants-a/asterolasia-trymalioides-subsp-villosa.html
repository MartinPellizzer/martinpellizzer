Asterolasia trymalioides subsp. villosa

Asterolasia trymalioides subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the hairy asterolasia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is a shrub characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its subspecies name "villosa.".

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
