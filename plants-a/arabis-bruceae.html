Arabis bruceae

Arabis bruceae, commonly known as Bruce's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a fine layer of hair, aiding in water retention.
