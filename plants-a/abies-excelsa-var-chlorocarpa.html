Abies excelsa var. chlorocarpa

Abies excelsa var. chlorocarpa, commonly known as the green-barked fir, is a rare and distinctive variant of the white fir.

It is characterized by its unique dark green bark, which contrasts with the typical silver-gray bark of the standard species. The tree has conical, dense foliage with needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions of the Andes.

This variant is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique morphological traits.
