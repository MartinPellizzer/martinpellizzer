Allium platyspathum var. falcatum

Allium platyspathum var. falcatum, commonly known as the flatleaf garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color and can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a popular choice in Asian cuisine.

The plant is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
