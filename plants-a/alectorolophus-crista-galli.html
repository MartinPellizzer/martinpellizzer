Alectorolophus crista-galli

Alectorolophus crista-galli, commonly known as the cock's-comb, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, feathery inflorescence that resembles a rooster's comb, composed of numerous bracts arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, and they bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

The species is well adapted to arid conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
