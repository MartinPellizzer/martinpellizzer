Acalypha carpinifolia var. genuina

Acalypha carpinifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the woolly knotweed, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, woolly, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
