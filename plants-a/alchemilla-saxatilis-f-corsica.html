Alchemilla saxatilis f. corsica

Alchemilla saxatilis f. corsica, commonly known as Corsican alpine lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Corsica.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features basal rosettes of heart-shaped, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and appear in clusters during the summer months.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
