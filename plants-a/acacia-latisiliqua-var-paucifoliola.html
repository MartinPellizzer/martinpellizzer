Acacia latisiliqua var. paucifoliola

Acacia latisiliqua var. paucifoliola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, narrow leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is notable for its unique foliage and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
