Androsace cylindrica

Androsace cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features small, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and its flowers are usually white or pink, clustered in dense inflorescences. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Androsace cylindrica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky or gravelly garden settings.
