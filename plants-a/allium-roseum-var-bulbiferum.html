Allium roseum var. bulbiferum

Allium roseum var. bulbiferum, commonly known as the bulbiferous rose garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are narrow and grass-like, and its bulbous stem produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters. It is closely related to other allium species and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens and borders.
