Avenula pratensis var. pilosa

Avenula pratensis var. pilosa, commonly known as hairy meadow grass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

It features slender, cylindrical stems covered in soft, hairy structures, which give it its varietal name "pilosa.". The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a hairy texture on both surfaces. It typically grows in moist meadows and wetlands, preferring nutrient-rich soils.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
