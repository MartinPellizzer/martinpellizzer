Angelica ubadakensis var. ubadakensis

Angelica ubadakensis var. ubadakensis, commonly known as the Ubadaken angelica, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, covered with soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces large, umbrella-shaped compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
