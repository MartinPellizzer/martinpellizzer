Acacia chrysobotrya

Acacia chrysobotrya, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a species native to Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in dry, open habitats. The plant is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring. Its flowers are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, and the plant produces small, woody pods that contain seeds.

Acacia chrysobotrya is also notable for its role in the Australian landscape and its use in horticulture.
