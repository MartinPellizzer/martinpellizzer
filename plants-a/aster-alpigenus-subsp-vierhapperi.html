Aster alpigenus subsp. vierhapperi

Aster alpigenus subsp.

vierhapperi, commonly known as the alpine aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white to pale blue.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
