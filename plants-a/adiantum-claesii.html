Adiantum claesii

Adiantum claesii, commonly known as the Chinese maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to China.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble a feather. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually bright green and have a distinctive, lacy appearance.

Adiantum claesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
