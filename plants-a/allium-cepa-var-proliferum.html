Allium cepa var. proliferum

Allium cepa var. proliferum, commonly known as the multi-headed leek, is a botanical variant of the common leek.

It is characterized by its multiple stalks emerging from a single base, creating a bushy appearance. The plant produces edible leaves and bulbous stems that are similar to those of the common leek but with a more robust growth habit. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its culinary use and ornamental appeal in gardens.
