Aglaia apiocarpa

Aglaia apiocarpa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland and hill forest regions.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
