Alaticaulia brachyantha

Alaticaulia brachyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alaticaulia, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the cooler months. It is adapted to survive in dry, nutrient-poor soils and can withstand periods of drought.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of desert flora and ecological resilience.
