Andropogon contortus subsp. allionii

Andropogon contortus subsp.

allionii, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States. It features slender, upright stems with narrow, blue-green leaves that are often rolled when young.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
