Arundinaria gracilis

Arundinaria gracilis, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria gracilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
