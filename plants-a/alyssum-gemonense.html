Alyssum gemonense

Alyssum gemonense, commonly known as the Italian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale purple, blooming in clusters. Alyssum gemonense is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense foliage, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
