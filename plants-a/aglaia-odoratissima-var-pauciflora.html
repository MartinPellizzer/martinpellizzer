Aglaia odoratissima var. pauciflora

Aglaia odoratissima var. pauciflora, commonly known as the fragrant aglaia, is a member of the Meliaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, clustered flowers that emit a sweet, aromatic fragrance. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The varietal name "pauciflora" refers to its relatively few flowers compared to other varieties of Aglaia.
