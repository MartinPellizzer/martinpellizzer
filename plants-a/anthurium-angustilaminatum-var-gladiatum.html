Anthurium angustilaminatum var. gladiatum

Anthurium angustilaminatum var. gladiatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, pointed leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are inflorescences that resemble a sword, giving it its common name. Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
