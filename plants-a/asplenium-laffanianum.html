Asplenium laffanianum

Asplenium laffanianum, commonly known as the Laffan's spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and features dark green, lanceolate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in groups called sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky crevices or on tree trunks.

Asplenium laffanianum is primarily distributed in Southeast Asia and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
