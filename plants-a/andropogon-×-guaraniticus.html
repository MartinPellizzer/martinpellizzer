Andropogon × guaraniticus

Andropogon × guaraniticus, commonly known as the Brazilian bluestem, is a hybrid grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically produces panicles of small, blue-gray flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Andropogon × guaraniticus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
