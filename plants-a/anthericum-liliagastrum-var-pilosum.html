Anthericum liliagastrum var. pilosum

Anthericum liliagastrum var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy tassel fern, is a variant of the tassel fern species native to the wet tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and elongated, tassel-like fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often near streams or in dense undergrowth. Its botanical features include compound leaves with a fibrous texture and a preference for well-drained, nutrient-rich soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
