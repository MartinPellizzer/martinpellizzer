Abies balsamea f. phanerolepis

Abies balsamea f. phanerolepis, commonly known as the balsam fir, is a variant of the balsam fir tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green needles, and distinctive aromatic resin. The foliage is typically dense and has a slightly rough texture, with the needles arranged in two rows along the branches. This form is often found in forested areas, growing in acidic, well-drained soils.

It plays an important ecological role, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
