Acacia portoricensis

Acacia portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican acacia, is a shrub native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow floral spikes. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in coastal areas and dry forests, often growing in sandy or limestone soils.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and is protected in some conservation programs.
