Aster harveyanus subsp. corymbosus

Aster harveyanus subsp.

corymbosus, commonly known as the clustered aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

It blooms in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
