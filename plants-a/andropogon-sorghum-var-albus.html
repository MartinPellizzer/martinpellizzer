Andropogon sorghum var. albus

Andropogon sorghum var. albus, commonly known as white sorghum, is a variant of the sorghum species native to Africa and widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a tall, grass-like plant with narrow, blade-like leaves and produces panicles of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its fibrous stalks and is valued for its drought tolerance and adaptability to poor soil conditions. Its seeds, which are typically white or pale in color, are used for food, animal feed, and industrial purposes.

The plant also has ornamental value due to its striking appearance and resilience.
