Arctostaphylos peninsularis subsp. keeleyi

Arctostaphylos peninsularis subsp.

keeleyi, commonly known as Keeley's manzanita, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in white or pink.

The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild, wet winters.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
