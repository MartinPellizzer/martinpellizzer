Aspilia angolensis

Aspilia angolensis, commonly known as the African torch or red torch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescence that resembles a torch. The plant produces small, tubular yellow flowers surrounded by red bracts, which give it its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Aspilia angolensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
