Ananas var. ovatus

Ananas var. ovatus, commonly known as the pineapple guava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that form a rosette around the stem. The plant produces a fragrant, edible fruit that is smaller and less acidic than the common pineapple. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value is also notable, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
