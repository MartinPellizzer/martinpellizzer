Astragalus remotiflorus

Astragalus remotiflorus, commonly known as the remote-flower milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in restoration projects.

Astragalus remotiflorus is also of interest to botanists due to its unique floral morphology and ecological significance.
