Alchemilla plicata f. autumnalis

Alchemilla plicata f. autumnalis, commonly known as autumnal lady's mantle, is a cultivar of the lady's mantle plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, overlapping, scalloped leaves that form a dense, low-growing mat. The plant produces small, yellow-green flowers in late summer to early autumn, which are clustered in panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This ornamental plant is valued for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
