Alstroemeria foliosa var. angustifolia

Alstroemeria foliosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf alstroemeria, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms and long-lasting cut flowers.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both landscape design and floral arrangements.
