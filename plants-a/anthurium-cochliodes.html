Anthurium cochliodes

Anthurium cochliodes, commonly known as the "Coral Anthurium," is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a colorful spathe. The plant produces striking, coral-colored bracts that are often mistaken for flowers, while the actual flowers are small and clustered at the base of the spadix. Anthurium cochliodes thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Its foliage is typically dark green with a waxy texture, adding to its ornamental value in indoor gardening.
