Aponogeton masoalaensis

Aponogeton masoalaensis, commonly known as the Masoala water clover, is a rare aquatic plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are divided into small, clover-like lobes. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with ample sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a critically endangered species.
