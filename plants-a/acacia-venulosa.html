Acacia venulosa

Acacia venulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, feathery leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system. Its flowers are typically clustered in cylindrical spikes, and it produces small, hard pods that contain seeds.

Acacia venulosa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
