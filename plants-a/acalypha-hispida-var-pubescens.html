Acalypha hispida var. pubescens

Acalypha hispida var. pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves, which give it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are often overlooked. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
