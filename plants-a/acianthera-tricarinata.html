Acianthera tricarinata

Acianthera tricarinata, commonly known as the three-ribbed orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant has three prominent ridges on its leaf sheaths, giving it its specific epithet "tricarinata.". It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Acianthera tricarinata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
