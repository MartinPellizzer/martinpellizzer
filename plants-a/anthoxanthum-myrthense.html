Anthoxanthum myrthense

Anthoxanthum myrthense, commonly known as the myrtle grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairless blades that often have a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale yellow to greenish flowers, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grassy areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
