Arctostaphylos columbiana subsp. nortensis

Arctostaphylos columbiana subsp.

nortensis, commonly known as the northern manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and reddish-brown bark that peels in thin layers.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in early spring, followed by edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to a variety of microhabitats, including rocky slopes and forest edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
