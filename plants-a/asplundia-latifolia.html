Asplundia latifolia

Asplundia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf asplundia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae, which includes many plants with showy inflorescences. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, a characteristic common in the Araceae family.

Asplundia latifolia is typically grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and unique floral structure.
