Aegilops turcomanica

Aegilops turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan goatgrass, is a wild grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces awned lemmas and is often found in arid and semi-arid environments. It has been studied for its potential role in wheat genetics due to its genetic relationship with cultivated wheat species.

Aegilops turcomanica is also valued for its use in forage and erosion control in certain regions.
