Astragalus kullmannii

Astragalus kullmannii, commonly known as Kullmann's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

Astragalus kullmannii is often found in alpine and steppe habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
