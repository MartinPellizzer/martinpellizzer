Acer negundo f. sanguineum

Acer negundo f. sanguineum, commonly known as the bloodtwig dogwood, is a decorative shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking red or pinkish twigs that persist through winter, adding visual interest to the landscape. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are typically oval with a pointed tip and turn yellow in the fall.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
