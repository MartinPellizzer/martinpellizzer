Astragalus austrotibetanus

Astragalus austrotibetanus, commonly known as the Tibetan milkvetch, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet and adjacent areas in China. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, compound leaves, and small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus austrotibetanus is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Tibetan medicine.
