Astragalus kopetdaghi var. orientikopetdaghensis

Astragalus kopetdaghi var. orientikopetdaghensis, commonly known as the Kopetdag milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
