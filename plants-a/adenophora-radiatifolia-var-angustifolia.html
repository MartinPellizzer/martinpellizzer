Adenophora radiatifolia var. angustifolia

Adenophora radiatifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its flowers are often adorned with prominent glandular hairs, which are characteristic of the genus Adenophora.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
