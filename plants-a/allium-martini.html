Allium martini

Allium martini, commonly known as the Martin's onion, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a compact growth habit. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, making it a valuable addition to culinary traditions in its native region.

Allium martini is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blossoms.
