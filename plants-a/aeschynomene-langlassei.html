Aeschynomene langlassei

Aeschynomene langlassei, commonly known as the African silver grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant has a deep root system that helps it thrive in poor soils and arid environments. It is often used for erosion control due to its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with bacteria.

Its seeds are also utilized in traditional practices and as a source of forage for livestock.
