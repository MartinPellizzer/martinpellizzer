Argyreia hylophila

Argyreia hylophila, commonly known as the red veined morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins and large, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant produces showy, spiral-shaped seed pods that contain seeds with a distinctive spiral pattern. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
