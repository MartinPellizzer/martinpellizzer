Aconitum kamtschaticum var. luxurians

Aconitum kamtschaticum var. luxurians, commonly known as the Siberian monkshood, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Russia and western Siberia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that bear large, hood-shaped purple-blue flowers, which are characteristic of the Aconitum genus. The plant has palmately divided, deeply cut leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its toxic nature, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
