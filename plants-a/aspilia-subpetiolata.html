Aspilia subpetiolata

Aspilia subpetiolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow to orange inflorescences that resemble flames. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
