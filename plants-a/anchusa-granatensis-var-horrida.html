Anchusa granatensis var. horrida

Anchusa granatensis var. horrida, commonly known as the Horrid Anchusa, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
