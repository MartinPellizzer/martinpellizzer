Aster daltonis

Aster daltonis, commonly known as Dalton's aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Dalton's aster is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
