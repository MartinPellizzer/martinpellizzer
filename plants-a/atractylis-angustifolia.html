Atractylis angustifolia

Atractylis angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Atractylis angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and contributing to local biodiversity.
