Amomum involucratum

Amomum involucratum, commonly known as the Chinese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale green flowers that develop into purple berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its medicinal properties and role in regional ecosystems.
