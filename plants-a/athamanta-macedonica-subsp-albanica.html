Athamanta macedonica subsp. albanica

Athamanta macedonica subsp.

albanica, commonly known as the Albanian Macedonian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
