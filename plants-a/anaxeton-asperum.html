Anaxeton asperum

Anaxeton asperum, commonly known as rough anaxeton, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features rough, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils.

Anaxeton asperum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
