Argyrolobium bottae

Argyrolobium bottae, commonly known as the white globular vetch, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical, white seed pods that resemble small globes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Argyrolobium bottae is a member of the legume family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
