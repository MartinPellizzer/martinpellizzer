Adenostyles alliariae subsp. pyrenaica

Adenostyles alliariae subsp.

pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean onion, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild onion-like flavor, making it a valued plant in traditional cuisine.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland and mountainous regions.
