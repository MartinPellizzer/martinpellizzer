Acer acutum var. quinquefidum

Acer acutum var. quinquefidum, commonly known as the five-lobed maple, is a variant of the Japanese maple characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features palmate leaves with five lobes, which are typically bright green in spring and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by samaroid fruits that disperse its seeds.

This ornamental tree is popular in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
