Adenophora omeiensis

Adenophora omeiensis, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

Adenophora omeiensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
