Abies concolor brevifolia

Abies concolor brevifolia, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its conical shape, silver-blue needle-like leaves, and distinctive cone structure. The tree typically grows in high-altitude regions, preferring cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its foliage is dense and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

The bark is thick and scaly, offering protection against environmental stressors.
