Aster occidentalis var. intermedius

Aster occidentalis var. intermedius, commonly known as the western aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, thriving in full sun. It produces showy purple to pink flowers in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
