Actaea erythrocarpa

Actaea erythrocarpa, commonly known as redfruit bugbane, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in late summer, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and showy, spiny seed pods that persist into winter.

It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
