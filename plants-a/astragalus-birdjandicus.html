Astragalus birdjandicus

Astragalus birdjandicus, commonly known as the Bird's Nest Astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny pods that resemble a bird's nest, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky environments.

Astragalus birdjandicus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
