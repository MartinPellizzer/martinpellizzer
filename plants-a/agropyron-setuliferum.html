Agropyron setuliferum

Agropyron setuliferum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Agropyron setuliferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
