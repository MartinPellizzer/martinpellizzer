Avena forskaolii

Avena forskaolii, commonly known as the Ethiopian oat, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit with dense clusters of spikelets. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into cylindrical spikelets containing numerous grains. Its grains are used in traditional foods and have been studied for their potential nutritional and agricultural value.
