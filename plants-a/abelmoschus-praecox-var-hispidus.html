Abelmoschus praecox var. hispidus

Abelmoschus praecox var. hispidus, commonly known as the Chinese okra, is a variant of the okra species native to Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain edible seeds, often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a versatile and economically important crop in various regions.
