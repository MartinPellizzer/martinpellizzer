Alpinia kusshakuensis

Alpinia kusshakuensis, commonly known as the Japanese ginger or Shikishima ginger, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features tall, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with three sepals and three petals, and it produces small, fragrant berries.

Alpinia kusshakuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
