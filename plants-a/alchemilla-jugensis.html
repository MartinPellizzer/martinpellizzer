Alchemilla jugensis

Alchemilla jugensis, commonly known as the Carpathian lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the Carpathian region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense, upright inflorescences. Alchemilla jugensis thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported healing properties.
