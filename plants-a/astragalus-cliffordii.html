Astragalus cliffordii

Astragalus cliffordii, commonly known as clifford's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. This perennial species typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open grasslands. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
