Allium bourgeaui subsp. cycladicum

Allium bourgeaui subsp.

cycladicum, commonly known as the Cycladic garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has strap-shaped, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild garlic-like scent.

This subspecies is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun.
