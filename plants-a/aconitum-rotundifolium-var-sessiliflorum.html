Aconitum rotundifolium var. sessiliflorum

Aconitum rotundifolium var. sessiliflorum, commonly known as the sessile-flowered monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, characteristic of the genus Aconitum. The plant has dark green, rounded leaves that are typically sessile, meaning they are attached directly to the stem without a petiole. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
