Acacia spilleriana

Acacia spilleriana, commonly known as the Spiller's wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, yellow-flowered spikes that bloom during the cooler months. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Acacia spilleriana is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in sandy soils.

Its ecological significance includes providing nectar for local pollinators and supporting various wildlife species.
