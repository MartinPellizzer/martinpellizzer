Acer caesium

Acer caesium, commonly known as the golden maple, is a species of maple native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed with five to seven segments, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
