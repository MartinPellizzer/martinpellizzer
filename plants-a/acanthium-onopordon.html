Acanthium onopordon

Acanthium onopordon, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in composite heads. It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.

Sandmat is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
