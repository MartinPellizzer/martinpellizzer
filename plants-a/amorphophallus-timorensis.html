Amorphophallus timorensis

Amorphophallus timorensis, commonly known as the Timor arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the island of Timor.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and a distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces a starchy tuberous root that is used as a food source in its native region. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its unique appearance and culinary value, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
