Adenia heterophylla subsp. australis

Adenia heterophylla subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Australia. It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive purple or red throat.

The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy, contributing to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its flowering period typically occurs in spring and early summer, making it a notable species in native gardens.
