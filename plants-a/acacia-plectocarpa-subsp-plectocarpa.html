Acacia plectocarpa subsp. plectocarpa

Acacia plectocarpa subsp.

plectocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and phyllodes that are narrow and linear.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the phyllodes. Its leaves are modified into phyllodes, which are flattened, leaf-like structures that perform photosynthesis. Acacia plectocarpa subsp.

plectocarpa is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
