Acer tataricum f. platypterum

Acer tataricum f. platypterum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maple, is a variant of the Tatarian maple.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically larger and more rounded than those of the typical species. The plant is native to eastern Asia, including regions of China and the Russian Far East. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
