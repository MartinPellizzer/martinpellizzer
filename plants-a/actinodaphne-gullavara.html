Actinodaphne gullavara

Actinodaphne gullavara, commonly known as the Keralan camphor tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically followed by elliptical, woody fruits. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in dense forests.
