Abbotia pumila

Abbotia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf abbotia, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Abbotia pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
