Astragalus leucargyreus

Astragalus leucargyreus, commonly known as white milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus leucargyreus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
