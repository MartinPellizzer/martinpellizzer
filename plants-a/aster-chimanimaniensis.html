Aster chimanimaniensis

Aster chimanimaniensis, commonly known as the Chimanimani aster, is a flowering plant native to the Chimanimani Mountains in eastern Zimbabwe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is known for its showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique habitat and limited distribution.
