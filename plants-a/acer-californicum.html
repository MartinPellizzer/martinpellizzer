Acer californicum

Acer californicum, commonly known as the California maple, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, red flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in areas with mild winters.

The California maple is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.

Native Distribution

Acer californicum is primarily native to the western United States, specifically California.

It is commonly found in coastal regions, particularly along the central and southern coastlines. This species thrives in sandy soils and is often associated with chaparral and coastal scrub habitats. It is also present in parts of Oregon and Baja California, Mexico.

The plant is well adapted to the mild, Mediterranean climate of its native range.
