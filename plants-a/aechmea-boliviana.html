Aechmea boliviana

Aechmea boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy coating, which helps it retain water in its native arid environment. The plant produces a vibrant flower spike that can reach several feet in height, often displaying red, orange, or yellow hues. Its unique structure allows it to collect and store rainwater in the central rosette, providing a microhabitat for small organisms.

Aechmea boliviana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Morphology

Aechmea boliviana has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, leathery, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a dark green color and a waxy surface.

The stem is short and thick, with a smooth, pale green bark that grows upward from the base of the rosette. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, purple flowers that bloom in the spring, and it is pollinated primarily by birds, especially hummingbirds. The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous black seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

The roots are fibrous and form a dense mat around the base of the plant, helping to anchor it and absorb moisture.
