Apocynum sibiricum

Apocynum sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian milkweed, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweed species. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its milky sap contains cardiac glycosides, which are toxic to mammals.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
