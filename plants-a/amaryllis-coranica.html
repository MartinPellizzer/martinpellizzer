Amaryllis coranica

Amaryllis coranica, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting stripes. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Amaryllis coranica is known for its long blooming period, typically from late winter to early spring.
