Acinodendron persicariifolium

Acinodendron persicariifolium, commonly known as the Persian shield, is a flowering plant native to the eastern regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its attractive, shield-shaped leaves that are often densely covered with small, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor plant collections.
