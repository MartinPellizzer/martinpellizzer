Acacia areolata

Acacia areolata, commonly known as the woolly acacia, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

Acacia areolata is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with low rainfall.
