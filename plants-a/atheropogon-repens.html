Atheropogon repens

Atheropogon repens, commonly known as the creeping grass or field grass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense mats. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of many ecological systems.
