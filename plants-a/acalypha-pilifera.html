Acalypha pilifera

Acalypha pilifera, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, silky hairs covering its stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, often red or yellow, bract-based inflorescences that resemble flowers. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptical with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Acalypha pilifera is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and vibrant floral displays.
