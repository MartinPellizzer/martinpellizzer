Agrostophyllum oliganthum

Agrostophyllum oliganthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandmat, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.

Agrostophyllum oliganthum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
