Atriplex volutans

Atriplex volutans, commonly known as the curled saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its salt-tolerant nature allows it to thrive in saline soils, making it an important species in coastal and desert ecosystems.

Atriplex volutans plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
