Abutilon macvaughii

Abutilon macvaughii, commonly known as the Desert Abutilon, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along canyons. Its fibrous roots help it survive in dry conditions, and it is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited water.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
