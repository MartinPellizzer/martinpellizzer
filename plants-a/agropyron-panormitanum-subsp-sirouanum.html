Agropyron panormitanum subsp. sirouanum

Agropyron panormitanum subsp.

sirouanum, commonly known as the Sirouan needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open, sunny areas.

This subspecies is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
