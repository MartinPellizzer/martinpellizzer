Apera spica-venti f. flavida

Apera spica-venti f. flavida, commonly known as the yellow cocklebur, is a variant of the common cocklebur.

It is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family, characterized by its yellowish flowers and spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides. It has a sprawling growth habit and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The foliage is usually rough and hairy, with narrow, serrated leaves.
