Asparagus touranensis

Asparagus touranensis, commonly known as the Touran asparagus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen, succulent leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptability to arid conditions makes it a resilient species suitable for dry climates.

Asparagus touranensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
