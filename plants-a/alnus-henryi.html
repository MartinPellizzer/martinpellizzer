Alnus henryi

Alnus henryi, commonly known as Henry's alder, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and catkin-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed areas.
