Alchemilla palmata subsp. acutangula

Alchemilla palmata subsp.

acutangula, commonly known as the acute-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features a rosette of basal, lanceolate leaves that are sharply pointed, giving it its specific epithet "acutangula.".

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in dense, spike-like inflorescences during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and drought tolerance.
