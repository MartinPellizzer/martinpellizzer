Acia

Acia, commonly known as wormwood, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often strongly scented foliage and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant is native to various regions across Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Many species of Acia are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

However, some species can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
