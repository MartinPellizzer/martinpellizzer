Acer pseudowilsonii

Acer pseudowilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's maple, is a small deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the genus Acer, which includes other maple species, and is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves with five lobes. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds known as samaras. It typically grows in mountainous regions and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
