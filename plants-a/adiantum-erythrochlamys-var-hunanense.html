Adiantum erythrochlamys var. hunanense

Adiantum erythrochlamys var. hunanense, commonly known as the Hunan maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, clustered sporangia on the undersides of its leaves, which are responsible for spore production. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to regions in China.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
