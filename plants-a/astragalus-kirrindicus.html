Astragalus kirrindicus

Astragalus kirrindicus, commonly known as Kirrin's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions.

Astragalus kirrindicus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
