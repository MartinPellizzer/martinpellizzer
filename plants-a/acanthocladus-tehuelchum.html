Acanthocladus tehuelchum

Acanthocladus tehuelchum, commonly known as the Tehuelchum thornplant, is a shrub native to the southern Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are often glandular and emit a mild aromatic scent.

Acanthocladus tehuelchum is adapted to grow in rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
