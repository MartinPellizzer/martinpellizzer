Acacia verniciflua var. brevifolia

Acacia verniciflua var. brevifolia, commonly known as the gum acacia or Australian wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in spherical heads. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and a deep root system.

The plant is also notable for its ability to produce a resinous gum, which has been historically used by Indigenous Australians for various purposes.
