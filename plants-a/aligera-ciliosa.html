Aligera ciliosa

Aligera ciliosa, commonly known as the hairy alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, blooming in late summer. Aligera ciliosa thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and hardy member of the Campanulaceae family.
