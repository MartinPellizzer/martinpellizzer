Agalma multiramosum

Agalma multiramosum, commonly known as the branched beauty, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, branched stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces oval-shaped, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Agalma multiramosum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its medicinal properties.
