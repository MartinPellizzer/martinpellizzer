Acianthera aurantiaca

Acianthera aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, aiding in water retention.

Acianthera aurantiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
