Allium involucratum

Allium involucratum, commonly known as the purple onion.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a tall flowering stem that bears clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor similar to garlic.

Allium involucratum is often found in dry meadows and rocky soils.
