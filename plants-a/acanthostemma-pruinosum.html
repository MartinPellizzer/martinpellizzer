Acanthostemma pruinosum

Acanthostemma pruinosum, commonly known as the sticky acanthostemma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and hairy, glandular leaves that give it a sticky texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
