Aristolochia triangularis f. minor

Aristolochia triangularis f. minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers.

The plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, wooded areas. Its leaves are heart-shaped and typically narrower than those of the typical species, giving it its varietal name. The flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive, intricate pattern that attracts specific pollinators.

It is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
