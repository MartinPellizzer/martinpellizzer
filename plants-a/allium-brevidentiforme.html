Allium brevidentiforme

Allium brevidentiforme, commonly known as the short-toothed garlic, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its bulbous structure and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild garlic-like scent.

Allium brevidentiforme is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil.
