Aster commutatus

Aster commutatus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Aster commutatus is an important plant for pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
