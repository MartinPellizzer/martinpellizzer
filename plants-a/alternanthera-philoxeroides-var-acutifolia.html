Alternanthera philoxeroides var. acutifolia

Alternanthera philoxeroides var. acutifolia, commonly known as alligator weed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are typically borne in clusters. It has a vigorous growth habit and can spread rapidly through both vegetative propagation and seed dispersal.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to colonize wetland and aquatic environments efficiently.
