Acer platanoides f. albescens

Acer platanoides f. albescens, commonly known as the white-leaf Norway maple, is a cultivar of the Norway maple characterized by its distinctive white variegation on the leaves.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10-15 meters, featuring a broad, rounded crown. The leaves are palmately lobed, with a smooth margin and a serrated edge, and they turn yellow in the fall. This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value, particularly in gardens and parks, where its unique foliage stands out.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.
