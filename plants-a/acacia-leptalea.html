Acacia leptalea

Acacia leptalea, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that appear in summer. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Acacia leptalea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in providing habitat for native wildlife.
