Amasonia campestris var. surinamensis

Amasonia campestris var. surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinamese goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Suriname and adjacent regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks. It features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
