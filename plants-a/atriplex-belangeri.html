Atriplex belangeri

Atriplex belangeri, commonly known as the Belanger's saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant typically grows in saline soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert habitats. Its flowers are small and greenish, clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Atriplex belangeri plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
