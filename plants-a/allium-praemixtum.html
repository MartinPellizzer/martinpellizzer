Allium praemixtum

Allium praemixtum, commonly known as the mixed garlic, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks, and is characterized by its bulbous structure and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the summer months. Allium praemixtum is valued for its aromatic bulbs, which are used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a hardy plant suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.
