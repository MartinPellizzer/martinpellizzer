Acacia auronitens var. mollis

Acacia auronitens var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-hairy wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and soft, hairy foliage, which gives it its varietal name. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
