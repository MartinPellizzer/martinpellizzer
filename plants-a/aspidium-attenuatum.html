Aspidium attenuatum

Aspidium attenuatum, commonly known as the slender wood fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically light green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori, a typical feature of ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Aspidium attenuatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
