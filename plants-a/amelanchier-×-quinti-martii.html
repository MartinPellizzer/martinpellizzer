Amelanchier × quinti-martii

Amelanchier × quinti-martii, commonly known as the western serviceberry, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a hardy and ornamental plant for gardens.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice for landscaping and wildlife habitat.
