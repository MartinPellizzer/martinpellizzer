Aster linariifolius f. leucactis

Aster linariifolius f. leucactis, commonly known as the white-flowered aster, is a rare botanical variant characterized by its pale to white flower heads.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to specific regions in Europe, particularly the Mediterranean. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its foliage is lance-shaped, with a silver or grayish underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and unique floral characteristics, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
