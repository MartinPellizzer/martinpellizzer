Armeniaca vulgaris var. amygdalifolia

Armeniaca vulgaris var. amygdalifolia, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It features fragrant, showy flowers that range from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, edible fruits known as apricots. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The species is closely related to the common apricot, sharing similar botanical characteristics.
