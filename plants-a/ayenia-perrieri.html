Ayenia perrieri

Ayenia perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's ayenia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous small, tubular flowers clustered together. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often woody and branched.

Ayenia perrieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
