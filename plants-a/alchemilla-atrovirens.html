Alchemilla atrovirens

Alchemilla atrovirens, commonly known as blackleaf lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla atrovirens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
