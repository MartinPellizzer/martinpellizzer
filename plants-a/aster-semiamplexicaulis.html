Aster semiamplexicaulis

Aster semiamplexicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and often clasping, giving it the specific epithet "semiamplexicaulis.".

It produces showy blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
