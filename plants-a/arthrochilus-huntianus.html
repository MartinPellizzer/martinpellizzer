Arthrochilus huntianus

Arthrochilus huntianus, commonly known as the Hunt's rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and features small, dark green leaves. The plant produces pale yellow to greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, narrow spur. It typically blooms during the winter months, adding a unique ornamental value to its habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
