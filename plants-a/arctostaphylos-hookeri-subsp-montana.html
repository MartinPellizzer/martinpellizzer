Arctostaphylos hookeri subsp. montana

Arctostaphylos hookeri subsp.

montana, commonly known as the Sierra manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring.

The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the dry, sunny conditions of its mountainous habitat.
