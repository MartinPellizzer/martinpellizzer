Aristida adscensionis subsp. bromoides

Aristida adscensionis subsp.

bromoides, commonly known as the bromoides false lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of southern Africa. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often clustered at the tips of the branches. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in dryland gardens due to its drought tolerance.
