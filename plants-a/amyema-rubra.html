Amyema rubra

Amyema rubra, commonly known as the red amyema, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its small, red flowers that bloom in winter, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Amyema rubra is often found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.
