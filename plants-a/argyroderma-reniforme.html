Argyroderma reniforme

Argyroderma reniforme, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in various regions for both landscaping and conservation purposes.
