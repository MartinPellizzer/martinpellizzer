Andrastis

Andrastis, commonly known as the "crown of thorns," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Anacardiaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Andrastis acanthifolia, which is native to tropical regions of South America. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed drupe that contains a single seed.

Andrastis is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental foliage and historical significance in traditional medicine.
