Achillea gigantea

Achillea gigantea, commonly known as giant yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a variety of medicinal compounds, including sesquiterpene lactones, which have been used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often used in herbal teas.

Achillea gigantea thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.
