Allium capillare

Allium capillare, commonly known as the slender leek, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives, and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and umbel-shaped flower clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and produces small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a useful plant for culinary and ornamental purposes.

Allium capillare thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in moist woodland areas.
