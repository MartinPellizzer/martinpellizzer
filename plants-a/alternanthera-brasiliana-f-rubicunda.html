Alternanthera brasiliana f. rubicunda

Alternanthera brasiliana f. rubicunda, commonly known as the red velvet plant, is a strikingly ornamental species native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety red foliage that contrasts beautifully with its green stems. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It prefers full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in water gardens and ponds.

The leaves are lance-shaped and densely packed, contributing to its lush, compact appearance.
