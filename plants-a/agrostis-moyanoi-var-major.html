Agrostis moyanoi var. major

Agrostis moyanoi var. major, commonly known as the giant meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

Botanically, it is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in ecological succession in grassland ecosystems.
