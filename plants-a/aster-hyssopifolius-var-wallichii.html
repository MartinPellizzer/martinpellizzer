Aster hyssopifolius var. wallichii

Aster hyssopifolius var. wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan aster, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
