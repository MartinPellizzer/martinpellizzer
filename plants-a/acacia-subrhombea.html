Acacia subrhombea

Acacia subrhombea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers arranged in compact, cylindrical spikes, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Acacia subrhombea is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.

It plays an important role in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
