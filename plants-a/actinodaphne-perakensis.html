Actinodaphne perakensis

Actinodaphne perakensis, commonly known as the Bornean camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. Its wood is valued for its fragrance and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
