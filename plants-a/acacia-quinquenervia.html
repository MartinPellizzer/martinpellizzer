Acacia quinquenervia

Acacia quinquenervia, commonly known as the five-veined acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves with five distinct veins on each leaflet. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Acacia quinquenervia is also known for its ecological importance, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
