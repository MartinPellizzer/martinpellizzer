Adiantum chilense var. glabrum

Adiantum chilense var. glabrum, commonly known as the Chilean maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically bright green and finely divided. The plant prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is noted for its smooth, glabrous leaf surfaces, distinguishing it from other varieties within the Adiantum genus.
