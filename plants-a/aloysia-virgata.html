Aloysia virgata

Aloysia virgata, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and often thrives in well-drained soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aloysia virgata is also noted for its drought tolerance and adaptability to coastal environments.
