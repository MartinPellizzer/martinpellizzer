Alphitonia

Alphitonia, commonly known as the native wattle, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are native to Australia and are characterized by their small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. Alphitonia species typically grow as small trees or shrubs, often found in coastal and dry forest regions. They have a shallow root system and are adapted to sandy soils.

The genus includes several species, such as Alphitonia excelsa, which is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
