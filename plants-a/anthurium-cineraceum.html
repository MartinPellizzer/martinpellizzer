Anthurium cineraceum

Anthurium cineraceum, commonly known as the silver anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, silver-green leaves that are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces vibrant red spadices, which are the spiky, clustered flowers that are actually modified leaves. Anthurium cineraceum thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
