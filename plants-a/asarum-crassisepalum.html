Asarum crassisepalum

Asarum crassisepalum, commonly known as broad-sepal asarum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a unique, tubular flower with a complex structure that resembles a hood, which is typical of the genus Asarum. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
