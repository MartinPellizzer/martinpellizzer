Acacia verek

Acacia verek, commonly known as the white acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and compound leaves that are typically bipinnate. The plant produces yellow to golden flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Acacia verek thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts and construction.
