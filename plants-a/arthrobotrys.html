Arthrobotrys

Arthrobotrys, commonly known as the root rot fungus, is a genus of fungi belonging to the family Aphanomycetaceae.

These fungi are primarily saprophytic, decomposing organic matter in soil and plant debris. They are known for their ability to produce zoospores, which are motile and aid in dispersal. Some species of Arthrobotrys can parasitize nematodes, making them important in biological control strategies.

Their life cycle involves both sexual and asexual reproduction, contributing to their ecological versatility.
