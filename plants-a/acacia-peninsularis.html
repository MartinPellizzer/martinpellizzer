Acacia peninsularis

Acacia peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Acacia peninsularis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various desert wildlife species.
