Acampe praemorsa var. praemorsa

Acampe praemorsa var. praemorsa, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, purple to pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces cone-shaped seed heads that persist into the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

This species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
