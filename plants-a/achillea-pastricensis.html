Achillea pastricensis

Achillea pastricensis, commonly known as Pastrina yarrow, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, soft, and fuzzy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flower heads that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
