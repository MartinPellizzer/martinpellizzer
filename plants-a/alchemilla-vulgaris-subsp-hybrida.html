Alchemilla vulgaris subsp. hybrida

Alchemilla vulgaris subsp.

hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and wetland restoration projects.
