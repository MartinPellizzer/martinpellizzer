Adenophora huangae

Adenophora huangae, commonly known as the Huang's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

Adenophora huangae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
