Abutilon subumbellatum

Abutilon subumbellatum, commonly known as the Mexican abutilon or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Abutilon subumbellatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
