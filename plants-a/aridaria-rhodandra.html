Aridaria rhodandra

Aridaria rhodandra, commonly known as the red-flowered desert rose, is a striking plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant has small, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its ability to thrive in low-water conditions makes it well-suited for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.

Aridaria rhodandra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
