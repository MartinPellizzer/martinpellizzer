Anthericum buchubergense

Anthericum buchubergense, commonly known as the Buchberg lily, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale green flowers in clusters. Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, it is critically important for conservation efforts.
