Amaryllis × princeps

Amaryllis × princeps, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, white, and pink. The plant produces a tall stem with strap-like leaves and blooms during the winter or early spring depending on the climate.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil with adequate sunlight.
