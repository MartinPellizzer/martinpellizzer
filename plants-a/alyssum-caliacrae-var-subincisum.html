Alyssum caliacrae var. subincisum

Alyssum caliacrae var. subincisum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
