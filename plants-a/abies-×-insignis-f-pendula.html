Abies × insignis f. pendula

Abies × insignis f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping fir, is a unique hybrid conifer that exhibits a distinctive cascading growth habit.

It is a naturally occurring form of the Abies × insignis, which itself is a cross between Abies koreana and Abies mariesii. The pendula form is characterized by its drooping branches that create a weeping appearance, making it visually striking in ornamental gardens. Botanically, it retains the typical coniferous features of firs, including needle-like leaves and cones.

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia and is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
