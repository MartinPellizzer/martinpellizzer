Alstroemeria acutifolia

Alstroemeria acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alstroemeria, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the family Alstroemeriaceae, characterized by its attractive, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and forest edges, and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces clusters of showy blooms that are often used in floral arrangements.

Alstroemeria acutifolia is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers and foliage.
