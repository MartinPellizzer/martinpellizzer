Aglycia distachya

Aglycia distachya, commonly known as the two-stemmed grass, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of two spikelets, giving it its common name.

Aglycia distachya is notable for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations and its use in traditional medicine.
