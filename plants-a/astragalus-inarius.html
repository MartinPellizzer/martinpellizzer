Astragalus inarius

Astragalus inarius, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Astragalus inarius is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
