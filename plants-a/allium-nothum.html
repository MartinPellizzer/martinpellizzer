Allium nothum

Allium nothum, commonly known as the alpine garlic, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives, and is characterized by its bulbous structure and grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. Allium nothum is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
