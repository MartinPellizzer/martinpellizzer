Alsophila albosetacea

Alsophila albosetacea, commonly known as the white-seta tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive white to light brown stipe, which is covered in fine, silky hairs. Its fronds are typically bright green and have a leathery texture.

Alsophila albosetacea is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
