Achyranthes amabilis

Achyranthes amabilis, commonly known as the Indian knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is an annual herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its roots are thick and fleshy, which help it store water and nutrients.

The plant is often found in moist soils along riverbanks and in cultivated fields.
