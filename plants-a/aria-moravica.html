Aria moravica

Aria moravica, commonly known as the Moravian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Aria moravica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
