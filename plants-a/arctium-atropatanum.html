Arctium atropatanum

Arctium atropatanum, commonly known as the purple burdock, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. The plant produces spiny, purplish-brown burrs that contain seeds, which are adapted for wind dispersal. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
