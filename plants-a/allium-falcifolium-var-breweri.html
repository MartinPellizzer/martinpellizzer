Allium falcifolium var. breweri

Allium falcifolium var. breweri, commonly known as the Breweri's garlic, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a short, stocky stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and flowering display.
