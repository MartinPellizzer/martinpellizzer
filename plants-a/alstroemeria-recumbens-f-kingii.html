Alstroemeria recumbens f. kingii

Alstroemeria recumbens f. kingii, commonly known as the King's Alstroemeria, is a rare and striking variant of the alstroemeria genus.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows in a compact, bushy form with narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to South America, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and botanical collections.
