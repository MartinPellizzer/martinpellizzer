Andropogon incurvatus

Andropogon incurvatus, commonly known as curly bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive curved inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and bluish-green, and it produces small, spike-like flowers in late summer.

Curly bluestem is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for wildlife.
