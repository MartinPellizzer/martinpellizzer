Alsine purpurea var. indurata

Alsine purpurea var. indurata, commonly known as purple sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant purple flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in dry conditions.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive, mat-like appearance that gives the plant its common name.
