Arrabidaea agnus-castus

Arrabidaea agnus-castus, commonly known as the velvet tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, giving the plant a velvety appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
