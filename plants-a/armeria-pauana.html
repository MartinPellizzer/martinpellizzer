Armeria pauana

Armeria pauana, commonly known as the Andean sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Armeria pauana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
