Ampelopsis radicantissima var. saint-paulii

Ampelopsis radicantissima var. saint-paulii, commonly known as the Saint-Paulii grape ivy, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, slightly lobed leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Its vigorous growth habit makes it suitable for trellises, walls, or as a ground cover in shaded garden areas.
