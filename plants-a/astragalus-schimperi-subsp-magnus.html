Astragalus schimperi subsp. magnus

Astragalus schimperi subsp.

magnus, commonly known as the Ethiopian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Ethiopia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional remedies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger size and more robust growth compared to other Astragalus species.
