Alibertia macrophylla

Alibertia macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved alibertia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Alibertia macrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
