Acacia pickardii

Acacia pickardii, commonly known as the Karoo acacia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, needle-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia pickardii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
