Adenocarpus boissieri

Adenocarpus boissieri, commonly known as the Syrian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky soils.

Adenocarpus boissieri is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
