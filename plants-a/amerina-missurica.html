Amerina missurica

Amerina missurica, commonly known as the Missouri evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats like prairies and disturbed soils. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Amerina missurica is a herbaceous perennial that plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
