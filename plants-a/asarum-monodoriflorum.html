Asarum monodoriflorum

Asarum monodoriflorum, commonly known as the single-flowered wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and a single, showy, hooded flower. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and has a rhizomatous root system. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive, intricate structure that attracts insects.

The plant is also noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
