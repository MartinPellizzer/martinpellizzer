Antidesma hirtellum

Antidesma hirtellum, commonly known as the rough antidesma, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and opposite, simple leaves. Its small yellow flowers are clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.
