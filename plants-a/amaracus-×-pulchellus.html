Amaracus × pulchellus

Amaracus × pulchellus, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between *Amaracus* and *Amaryllis* species.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, white, and pink. The plant typically grows as a bulbous perennial, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water and nutrients. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
