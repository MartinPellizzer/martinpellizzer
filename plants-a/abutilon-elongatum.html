Abutilon elongatum

Abutilon elongatum, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its hairy, elongated stems and palmate leaves that are often lobed. It produces vibrant orange to red bracts that resemble lanterns, which are not flowers but modified leaves.

Abutilon elongatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
