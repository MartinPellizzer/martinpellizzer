Acanthus dioscoridis subsp. brevicaulis

Acanthus dioscoridis subsp.

brevicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features distinctive, spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
