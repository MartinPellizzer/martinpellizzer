Acer campestre var. villosum

Acer campestre var. villosum, commonly known as the silky maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spreading habit and small, palmate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that disperse its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden use, though it is less commonly cultivated than other maple species.
