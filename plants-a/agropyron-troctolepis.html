Agropyron troctolepis

Agropyron troctolepis, commonly known as the tall wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its robust growth habit, with tall, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. Agropyron troctolepis is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

It is valued for its forage quality and ecological resilience.
