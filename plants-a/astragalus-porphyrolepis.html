Astragalus porphyrolepis

Astragalus porphyrolepis, commonly known as the purple-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive purple foliage and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus porphyrolepis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid landscapes.
