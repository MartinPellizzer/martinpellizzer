Asclepias incarnata var. glabra

Asclepias incarnata var. glabra, commonly known as swamp milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, pink to purple flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows up to 3 to 6 feet tall, with lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This species is an important host plant for monarch butterfly larvae and supports a variety of pollinators.
