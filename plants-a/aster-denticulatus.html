Aster denticulatus

Aster denticulatus, commonly known as the toothed aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with finely toothed margins, giving it its common name.

It produces showy purple to blue flowers in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
