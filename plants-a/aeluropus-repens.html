Aeluropus repens

Aeluropus repens, commonly known as sand couch grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to arid and coastal regions.

It thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dunes and salt marshes. The plant has slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are typically blue-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Aeluropus repens plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and preventing erosion.

Morphology

Aeluropus repens has leaves that are narrow, grass-like, and typically linear in shape, with a blue-green color and a flat, blade-like structure.

The leaves are arranged alternately along the stem, which is low-growing, prostrate, and often forms dense mats. The stem is smooth and cylindrical, with a light green color, and it spreads horizontally along the ground.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually pink or purple, blooming in the spring, and they are pollinated by wind. The fruit is a small, three-angled capsule that releases seeds through dryness, and the seeds are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, helping the plant thrive in sandy or saline soils.
