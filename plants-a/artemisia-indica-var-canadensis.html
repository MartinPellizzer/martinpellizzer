Artemisia indica var. canadensis

Artemisia indica var. canadensis, commonly known as Canadian tarragon, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in dry, rocky habitats. Its essential oils, particularly those containing thujone, contribute to its distinctive scent and historical use in herbal medicine.

It is also valued as a ornamental plant for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
