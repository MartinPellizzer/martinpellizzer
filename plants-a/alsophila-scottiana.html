Alsophila scottiana

Alsophila scottiana, commonly known as the Scott's tree fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a robust rhizome system that helps it anchor in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its striking appearance.

Alsophila scottiana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant and exotic foliage.
