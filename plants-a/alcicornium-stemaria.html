Alcicornium stemaria

Alcicornium stemaria, commonly known as the silverleaf spirea, is a flowering plant characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and early spring blooms.
