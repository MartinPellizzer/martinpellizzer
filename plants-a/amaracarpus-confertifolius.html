Amaracarpus confertifolius

Amaracarpus confertifolius, commonly known as the dense-leaved amaracarpus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, overlapping foliage and can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, lance-shaped leaflets. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to humid, tropical climates.
