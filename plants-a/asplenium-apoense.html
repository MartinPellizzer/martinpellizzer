Asplenium apoense

Asplenium apoense, commonly known as the Apo spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic or lithophytic fern that typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in humid environments. The plant has a compact rosette of lanceolate, serrated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It is native to the Philippines, specifically found in the mountainous regions of Mindanao.

Asplenium apoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also of interest in botanical studies due to its unique adaptations to its ecological niche.
