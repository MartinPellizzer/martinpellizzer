Aceras × duquesneyi

Aceras × duquesneyi, commonly known as the Duquesne bellflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in areas of the Appalachian region.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
