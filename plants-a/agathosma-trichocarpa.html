Agathosma trichocarpa

Agathosma trichocarpa, commonly known as Cape tea or Agathosma, is a succulent shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used to treat ailments such as colds and digestive issues.
