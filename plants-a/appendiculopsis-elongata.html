Appendiculopsis elongata

Appendiculopsis elongata, commonly known as the elongated appendiculate liverwort, is a small, thalloid liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, ribbon-like thallus that is often covered with small, leaf-like structures called appendages. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can retain adequate moisture. Its reproductive structures, known as gametangia, are prominently displayed on the thallus, facilitating sexual reproduction.

Appendiculopsis elongata is a valuable indicator species for assessing the health of humid, undisturbed ecosystems.
