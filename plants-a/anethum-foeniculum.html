Anethum foeniculum

Anethum foeniculum, commonly known as fennel, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its feathery leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in umbrella-like clusters. The plant is native to southern Europe and western Asia but is now cultivated worldwide. Fennel has aromatic seeds and is often used in cooking and traditional medicine.

Its essential oils contain compounds like anethol, which contribute to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.
