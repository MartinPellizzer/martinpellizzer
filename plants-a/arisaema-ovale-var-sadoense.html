Arisaema ovale var. sadoense

Arisaema ovale var. sadoense, commonly known as the Sado Island arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and spadix inflorescence, typical of the Araceae family. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing both male and female flowers on the spadix.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
