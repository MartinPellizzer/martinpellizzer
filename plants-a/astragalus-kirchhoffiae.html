Astragalus kirchhoffiae

Astragalus kirchhoffiae, commonly known as the Kirchhoff's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
