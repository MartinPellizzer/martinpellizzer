Acidanthera goetzei

Acidanthera goetzei, commonly known as the Goetze's orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically display a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant produces a single, erect stem that bears several blooms, each with a distinctive, spurred lip. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Acidanthera goetzei thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
