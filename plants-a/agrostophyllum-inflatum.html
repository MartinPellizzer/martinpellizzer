Agrostophyllum inflatum

Agrostophyllum inflatum, commonly known as the inflated grass tree, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated seed heads that resemble small, balloon-like structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has narrow, grass-like leaves.

Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, and the plant plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
