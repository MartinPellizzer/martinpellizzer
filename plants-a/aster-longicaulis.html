Aster longicaulis

Aster longicaulis, commonly known as the longstem aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
