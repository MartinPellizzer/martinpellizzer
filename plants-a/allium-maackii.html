Allium maackii

Allium maackii, commonly known as purple onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, rounded bulbs underground. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and flowers.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to garlic and onions.
