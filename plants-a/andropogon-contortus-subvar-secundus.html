Andropogon contortus subvar. secundus

Andropogon contortus subvar. secundus, commonly known as the curly bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
