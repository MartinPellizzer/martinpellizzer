Allium mauritanicum

Allium mauritanicum, commonly known as the Moroccan leek, is a hardy perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Allium mauritanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract beneficial insects.
