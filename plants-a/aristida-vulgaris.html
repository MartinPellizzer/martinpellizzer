Aristida vulgaris

Aristida vulgaris, commonly known as common dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and prairies. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it is drought-tolerant.

Aristida vulgaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
