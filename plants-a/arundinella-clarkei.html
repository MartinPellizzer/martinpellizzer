Arundinella clarkei

Arundinella clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's bamboo, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height, often forming dense clumps. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a tendency to droop under the weight of rain.

Arundinella clarkei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
