Athenaea cernua

Athenaea cernua, commonly known as the hanging ivy, is a climbing plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features dark green, heart-shaped leaves that hang in cascading clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its stems are flexible and can trail along surfaces or be trained to climb.

Athenaea cernua is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
