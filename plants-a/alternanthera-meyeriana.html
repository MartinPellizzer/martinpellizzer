Alternanthera meyeriana

Alternanthera meyeriana, commonly known as the red-leaf alternate or red velvet plant, is a striking aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or purple foliage, which becomes more intense under strong light conditions. The plant has slender, branching stems that can be rooted or floated in water, making it popular in aquariums and ponds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires moderate to high lighting for optimal coloration.
