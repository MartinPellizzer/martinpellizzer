Ardisia surinamensis

Ardisia surinamensis, commonly known as Surinam beautyberry, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Ardisia surinamensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid climates.
