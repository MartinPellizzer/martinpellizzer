Astragalus micrancistrus

Astragalus micrancistrus, commonly known as the small-celled milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus micrancistrus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
