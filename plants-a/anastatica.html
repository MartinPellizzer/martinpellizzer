Anastatica

Anastatica, commonly known as the resurrection plant, is a unique species of flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia. This plant has the remarkable ability to survive extreme desiccation, losing up to 95% of its water content and appearing dead, only to revive when exposed to moisture. Its small, white or yellow flowers bloom after rainfall, and it has small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss.

The plant's resilience makes it an interesting subject in botanical studies, particularly in understanding drought tolerance mechanisms.
