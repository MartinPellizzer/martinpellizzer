Allocasuarina mackliniana subsp. hirtilinea

Allocasuarina mackliniana subsp.

hirtilinea, commonly known as the Macklin's silky casuarina, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive needle-like leaves and cylindrical, woody cones.

The plant thrives in well-drained sandy or loamy soils and is often found in coastal or inland scrublands. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces woody, elongated fruits.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and salt spray.
