Asprella hystrix

Asprella hystrix, commonly known as the spiny hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant has distinctive spiny leaves and produces capsules containing small, hard seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed soils.

Asprella hystrix is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
