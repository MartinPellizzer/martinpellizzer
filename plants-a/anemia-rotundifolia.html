Anemia rotundifolia

Anemia rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf anemia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, succulent leaves and clusters of small, pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth. Its botanical features include thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, adapting it to its tropical habitat.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
