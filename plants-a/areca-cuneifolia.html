Areca cuneifolia

Areca cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf areca, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, feather-like leaves that grow in a fan shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into dark purple fruits. Areca cuneifolia thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
