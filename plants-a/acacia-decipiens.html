Acacia decipiens

Acacia decipiens, commonly known as the false acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
