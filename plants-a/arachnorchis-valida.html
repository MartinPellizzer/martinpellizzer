Arachnorchis valida

Arachnorchis valida, commonly known as the golden spider orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

Arachnorchis valida is a rare and protected species, reflecting its ecological significance and the need for conservation efforts.
