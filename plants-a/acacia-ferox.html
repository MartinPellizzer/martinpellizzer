Acacia ferox

Acacia ferox, commonly known as the black wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, bark and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. Acacia ferox is adapted to dry environments and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and has been used by Indigenous Australians for various purposes.
