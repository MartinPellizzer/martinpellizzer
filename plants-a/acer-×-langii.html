Acer × langii

Acer × langii, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Sapindaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its striking, often variegated foliage that displays vibrant colors throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows to a height of 6–15 meters and features a rounded, spreading crown. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a smooth margin, and turn brilliant shades of red, orange, and yellow in autumn.

Acer × langii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
