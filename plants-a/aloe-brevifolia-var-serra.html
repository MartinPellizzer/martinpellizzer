Aloe brevifolia var. serra

Aloe brevifolia var. serra, commonly known as the serrated aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped growth pattern and fleshy, bluish-green leaves with serrated edges. The leaves are thick and store water, enabling the plant to survive in arid conditions. It produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear yellow-orange flowers, which bloom in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
