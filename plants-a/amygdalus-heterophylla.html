Amygdalus heterophylla

Amygdalus heterophylla, commonly known as the variable almond.

It is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, characterized by its variable leaf morphology. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in spring, followed by edible, almond-like fruits. Its leaves can be either simple or compound, depending on the environmental conditions.

Amygdalus heterophylla is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a resilient species in such environments.
