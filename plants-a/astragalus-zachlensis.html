Astragalus zachlensis

Astragalus zachlensis, commonly known as the Zachlenskaya milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus zachlensis is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
