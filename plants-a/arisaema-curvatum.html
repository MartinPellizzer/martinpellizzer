Arisaema curvatum

Arisaema curvatum, commonly known as the curved arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are arrowhead-shaped, with a smooth texture and a deep green color.

The flowers are usually yellow or white, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
