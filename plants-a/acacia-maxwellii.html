Acacia maxwellii

Acacia maxwellii, commonly known as the Maxwell's wattle, is a shrub native to western Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Acacia maxwellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
