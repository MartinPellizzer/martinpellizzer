Angiopteris willinkii

Angiopteris willinkii, commonly known as the Willink's water fern, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, feathery appearance.

Angiopteris willinkii is often found in swampy areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
