Anagallis tenella var. anfrayi

Anagallis tenella var. anfrayi, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Pimpernel, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, erect stems that bear small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
