Aeschynomene aculeata

Aeschynomene aculeata, commonly known as goatweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by small, round pods containing seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Aeschynomene aculeata is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid growth and spread.
