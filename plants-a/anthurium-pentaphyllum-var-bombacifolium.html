Anthurium pentaphyllum var. bombacifolium

Anthurium pentaphyllum var. bombacifolium, commonly known as the giant anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, with a velvety texture and deep green coloration. The plant produces spadices, which are specialized inflorescences that consist of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture in its soil.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
