Aquilegia discolor subsp. reuteri

Aquilegia discolor subsp.

reuteri, commonly known as the alpine cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with finely divided lobes, and it produces a cluster of small, showy flowers in early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
