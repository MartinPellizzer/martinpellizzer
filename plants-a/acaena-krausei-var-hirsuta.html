Acaena krausei var. hirsuta

Acaena krausei var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy spinulose burr, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its hairy leaves and persistent fruits contribute to its distinctive appearance and ecological role.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
