Alnus × suginoi

Alnus × suginoi, commonly known as the Japanese alder, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Alnus hirta and Alnus japonica.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its small, serrated leaves and catkin-like flowers. The plant is native to Japan and is often found in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in forested areas. It produces small, winged fruits that aid in wind dispersal.

Alnus × suginoi is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.
