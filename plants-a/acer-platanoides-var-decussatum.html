Acer platanoides var. decussatum

Acer platanoides var. decussatum, commonly known as the Norway maple, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive five-lobed structure, which are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that disperse its seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is commonly planted in urban landscapes.
