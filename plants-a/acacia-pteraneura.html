Acacia pteraneura

Acacia pteraneura, commonly known as the ghost gum, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and its distinctive, feathery foliage composed of small, needle-like phyllodes. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, woody pods containing seeds. Acacia pteraneura is drought-tolerant and adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for landscaping in dry regions.

It plays an important role in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
