Allium rosenorum

Allium rosenorum, commonly known as the giant garlic or Roser's garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features large, fleshy bulbs with a strong garlic scent, and its leaves are broad, grass-like, and often covered in a fine, white powder. The plant produces tall, slender stems topped with clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its vigorous growth, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
