Aquilegia ochroleuca

Aquilegia ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow columbine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred petals that come in shades of yellow and white, forming a distinctive floral display. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are deeply lobed.

The yellow columbine is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
