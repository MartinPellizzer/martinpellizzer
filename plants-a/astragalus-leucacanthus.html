Astragalus leucacanthus

Astragalus leucacanthus, commonly known as the white thorned milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces small, hard pods that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots and a capacity for nitrogen fixation through symbiotic bacteria.

Astragalus leucacanthus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
