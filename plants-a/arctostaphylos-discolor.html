Arctostaphylos discolor

Arctostaphylos discolor, commonly known as the white mountain manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, leathery berries that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Arctostaphylos discolor is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
