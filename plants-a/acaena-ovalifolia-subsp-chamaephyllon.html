Acaena ovalifolia subsp. chamaephyllon

Acaena ovalifolia subsp.

chamaephyllon, commonly known as the dwarf nettle tree, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in regions of Chile and Argentina. It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in heads, followed by small, spherical fruits that are typically reddish or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to well-drained soils, often growing in coastal or mountainous areas.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
