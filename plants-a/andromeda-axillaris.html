Andromeda axillaris

Andromeda axillaris, commonly known as the coastal azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a leathery texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Andromeda axillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
