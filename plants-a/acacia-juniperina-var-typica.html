Acacia juniperina var. typica

Acacia juniperina var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched structure and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers arranged in compact inflorescences, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
