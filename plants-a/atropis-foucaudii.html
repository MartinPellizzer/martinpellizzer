Atropis foucaudii

Atropis foucaudii, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often with a distinctive orange throat. The plant has a short, fibrous root system and produces a single, erect stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Atropis foucaudii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and coastal landscapes.
