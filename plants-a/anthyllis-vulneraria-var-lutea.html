Anthyllis vulneraria var. lutea

Anthyllis vulneraria var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is typically found in dry, calcareous soils and can thrive in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility, and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

The species is also noted for its historical use in traditional medicine.
