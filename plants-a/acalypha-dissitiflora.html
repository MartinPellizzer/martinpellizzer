Acalypha dissitiflora

Acalypha dissitiflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

Acalypha dissitiflora is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
