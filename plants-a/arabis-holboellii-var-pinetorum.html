Arabis holboellii var. pinetorum

Arabis holboellii var. pinetorum, commonly known as the pine mountain rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, well-drained conditions.

Its foliage and flowers provide early-season nectar for pollinators in its native environment.
