Anthemis meinkeana

Anthemis meinkeana, commonly known as the Mexican tansy, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
