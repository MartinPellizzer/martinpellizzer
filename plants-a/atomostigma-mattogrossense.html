Atomostigma mattogrossense

Atomostigma mattogrossense, commonly known as the Amazonian orchid, is a striking plant native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a complex structure with a slender stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves that help it thrive in the humid, shaded understory of the forest. It relies on specific pollinators, such as bees and wasps, for successful reproduction.

Due to habitat loss, this species is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
