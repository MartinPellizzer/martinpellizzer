Aconitum napellus f. tauricum

Aconitum napellus f. tauricum, commonly known as the Tauric monkshood, is a hybrid form of the European monkshood.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe, particularly the Taurus Mountains. The plant features striking blue-violet flowers arranged in a raceme, with distinctive hooded petals. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deep lobes that give it a fern-like appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
