Armeria vulgaris pubescens

Armeria vulgaris pubescens, commonly known as the hairy thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are linear and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or coastal environments.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
