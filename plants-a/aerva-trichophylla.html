Aerva trichophylla

Aerva trichophylla, commonly known as the woolly aerva, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Aerva trichophylla is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
