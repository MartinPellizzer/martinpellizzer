Agrostis hyemalis var. elata

Agrostis hyemalis var. elata, commonly known as tall winter bentgrass, is a low-growing grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on moist, well-drained soils. This variety is particularly adapted to cooler climates and is often found in meadows, lawns, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth allows it to spread and establish quickly, making it a resilient plant in various habitats.

Agrostis hyemalis var. elata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
