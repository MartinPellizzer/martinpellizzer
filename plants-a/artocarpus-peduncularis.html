Artocarpus peduncularis

Artocarpus peduncularis, commonly known as the Indian native breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a fruit with a thick, spiky rind. The fruit contains a starchy, edible pulp that is often consumed cooked or raw. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This plant is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its traditional medicinal uses.
