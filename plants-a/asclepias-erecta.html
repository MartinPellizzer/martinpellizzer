Asclepias erecta

Asclepias erecta, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright orange to red flowers. The plant has a milky sap that contains cardiac glycosides, which are toxic to many animals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

Asclepias erecta serves as a vital host plant for monarch butterfly larvae and is an important component of native pollinator habitats.
