Arachniodes tripinnata

Arachniodes tripinnata, commonly known as the three-lobed fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are divided into three distinct lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients.

Arachniodes tripinnata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
