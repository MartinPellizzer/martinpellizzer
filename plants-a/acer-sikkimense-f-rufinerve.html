Acer sikkimense f. rufinerve

Acer sikkimense f. rufinerve, commonly known as the red-veined maple, is a distinctive variant of the Sikkim maple.

It is characterized by its striking red veins that run through its leaves, which are typically palmately lobed and have a vibrant green to yellowish-green color. Native to the Himalayas, this plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in spring followed by winged seeds in the autumn.
