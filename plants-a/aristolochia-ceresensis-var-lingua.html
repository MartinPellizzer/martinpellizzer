Aristolochia ceresensis var. lingua

Aristolochia ceresensis var. lingua, commonly known as the tongue-of-the-angel, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, intricate flowers that resemble a twisted tube. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking foliage.
