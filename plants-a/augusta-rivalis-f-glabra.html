Augusta rivalis f. glabra

Augusta rivalis f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved Augusta rivalis, is a variant of the Augusta rivalis species, characterized by its glabrous foliage.

This plant is native to specific regions in the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and exhibit a glossy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in terminal inflorescences. The plant is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
