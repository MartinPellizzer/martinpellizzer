Astragalus confertus f. albiflorus

Astragalus confertus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive white flowers, which set it apart from other Astragalus species.
