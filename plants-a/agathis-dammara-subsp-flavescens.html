Agathis dammara subsp. flavescens

Agathis dammara subsp.

flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-wood or yellow agathis, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Araucariaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers and spherical cones that mature into woody, elongated seed capsules.

It is valued for its durable timber and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.
