Astragalus belolipovii

Astragalus belolipovii, commonly known as the Belolipovii milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
