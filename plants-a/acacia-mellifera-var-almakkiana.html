Acacia mellifera var. almakkiana

Acacia mellifera var. almakkiana, commonly known as the Yemen acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless branches and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellowish-green to golden-yellow inflorescences, which are rich in nectar and attract various pollinators. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soils and high temperatures.

This variety is valued for its ecological role and traditional uses in the region.
