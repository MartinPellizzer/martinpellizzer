Acacia valida

Acacia valida, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and compound leaves that are typically feather-like in appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are used in traditional medicine.

Acacia valida is adapted to arid environments and is often found in savannas and rocky areas.
