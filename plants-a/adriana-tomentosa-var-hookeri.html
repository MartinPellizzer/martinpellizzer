Adriana tomentosa var. hookeri

Adriana tomentosa var. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's woolly adriana, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, which help protect it from intense sunlight and cold temperatures.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
