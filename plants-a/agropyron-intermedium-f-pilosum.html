Agropyron intermedium f. pilosum

Agropyron intermedium f. pilosum, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and meadows. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

This grass is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping and erosion control.
