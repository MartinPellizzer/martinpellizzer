Acaena fissistipula var. longibracteata

Acaena fissistipula var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bracted brackenweed, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spiny, berry-like fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a distinctive, serrated edge.

It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.
