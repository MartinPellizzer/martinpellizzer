Astrantia elatior

Astrantia elatior, commonly known as the greater sweet-cone, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flower heads that are composed of many small florets, creating a rounded, cone-like appearance. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Astrantia elatior is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
