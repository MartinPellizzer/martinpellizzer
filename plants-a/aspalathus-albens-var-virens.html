Aspalathus albens var. virens

Aspalathus albens var. virens, commonly known as the green aspalathus, is a variant of the aspalathus genus native to South Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate ornamental touch. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
