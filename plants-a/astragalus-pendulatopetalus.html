Astragalus pendulatopetalus

Astragalus pendulatopetalus, commonly known as the pendulous petal milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus pendulatopetalus is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
