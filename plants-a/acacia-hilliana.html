Acacia hilliana

Acacia hilliana, commonly known as the white acacia, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in globular clusters, which bloom during the warm season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Acacia hilliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
