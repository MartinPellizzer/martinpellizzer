Acacia aspera

Acacia aspera, commonly known as the rough acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia aspera thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soils and drought conditions.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fuelwood.
