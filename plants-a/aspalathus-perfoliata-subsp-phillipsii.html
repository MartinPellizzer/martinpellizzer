Aspalathus perfoliata subsp. phillipsii

Aspalathus perfoliata subsp.

phillipsii, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in southern regions with a Mediterranean climate.
