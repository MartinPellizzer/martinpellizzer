Artemisia campestris proles glutinosa

Artemisia campestris proles glutinosa, commonly known as sticky wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces a sticky resin, which gives it its common name and contributes to its distinctive scent. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
