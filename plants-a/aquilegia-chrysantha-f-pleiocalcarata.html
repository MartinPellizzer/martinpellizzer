Aquilegia chrysantha f. pleiocalcarata

Aquilegia chrysantha f. pleiocalcarata, commonly known as the golden monkeyflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features striking golden-yellow flowers with five petals, often adorned with red or orange markings, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that provide a delicate texture contrasting with its bold floral display. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique flower structure and vibrant color make it a standout addition to any botanical garden or native plant collection.
