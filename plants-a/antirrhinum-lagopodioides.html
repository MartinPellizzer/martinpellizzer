Antirrhinum lagopodioides

Antirrhinum lagopodioides, commonly known as the hare's foot toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and glandular leaves that resemble a hare's foot, hence its common name. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Antirrhinum lagopodioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and colorful blooms.
