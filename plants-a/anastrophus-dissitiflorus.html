Anastrophus dissitiflorus

Anastrophus dissitiflorus, commonly known as the scattered-flowered anastrophus, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
