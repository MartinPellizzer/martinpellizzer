Aegiphila macrantha

Aegiphila macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered aegiphila, is a perennial shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
