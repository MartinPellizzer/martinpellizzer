Aegiphila macrantha

Aegiphila macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered aegiphila, is a perennial shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.

Morphology

Aegiphila macrantha has leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, arranged alternately along the stem, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, with white petals and yellow stamens, blooming during the dry season and pollinated primarily by wind.
