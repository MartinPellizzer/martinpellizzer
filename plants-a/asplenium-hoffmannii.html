Asplenium hoffmannii

Asplenium hoffmannii, commonly known as the Hoffmann's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the Aspleniaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly curled. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rocky crevices or forest floors, where it can thrive in humid conditions. Its spores are produced on the undersides of its fronds, which are arranged in a dense, clustered formation.

Asplenium hoffmannii is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
