Acaena × vinosa

Acaena × vinosa, commonly known as the New Zealand spinach or silverweed, is a hybrid plant belonging to the genus Acaena in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is native to New Zealand, where it thrives in moist, temperate climates. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by persistent, spiny fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Acaena × vinosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
