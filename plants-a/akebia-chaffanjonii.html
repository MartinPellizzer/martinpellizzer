Akebia chaffanjonii

Akebia chaffanjonii, commonly known as the Japanese actinio, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by distinctive red berries that mature in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its foliage, flowers, and berries, which attract birds and other wildlife.
