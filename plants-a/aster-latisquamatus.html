Aster latisquamatus

Aster latisquamatus, commonly known as the broad-scale aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Aster latisquamatus is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role.
