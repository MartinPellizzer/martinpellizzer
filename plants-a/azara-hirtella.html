Azara hirtella

Azara hirtella, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish-orange berries containing several seeds. Azara hirtella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
