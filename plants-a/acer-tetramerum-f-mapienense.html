Acer tetramerum f. mapienense

Acer tetramerum f. mapienense, commonly known as the Mapien maple, is a rare and unique variant of the Pacific madrone.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring leaves with a striking combination of green and red hues. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with a smooth, reddish bark that becomes more pronounced with age. It thrives in well-drained soils and is native to specific regions in western North America.

Due to its limited distribution and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
