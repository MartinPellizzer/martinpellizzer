Alloteropsis distachya

Alloteropsis distachya, commonly known as the narrowleaf bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and distinctive spikelets that resemble bottlebrushes. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in dense clusters.

Alloteropsis distachya is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
