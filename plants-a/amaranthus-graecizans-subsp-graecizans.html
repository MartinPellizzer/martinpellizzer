Amaranthus graecizans subsp. graecizans

Amaranthus graecizans subsp.

graecizans, commonly known as Greek amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered with dense, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green to reddish in color.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from green to red, depending on the stage of development. Its seeds are small, oblong, and often have a distinctive reddish or blackish hue.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional agriculture and cuisine in its native habitat.
