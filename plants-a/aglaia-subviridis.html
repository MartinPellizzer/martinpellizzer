Aglaia subviridis

Aglaia subviridis, commonly known as the green aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other valuable hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, providing a striking visual contrast. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by ornamental fruits that turn red when mature.
