Aristolochia insolita

Aristolochia insolita, commonly known as the "insolite arrowhead vine," is a climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and complex, pipe-like flowers that are often mistaken for orchids. The plant produces a strong, aromatic odor that attracts specific pollinators, such as flies. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a darker throat, and it is known for its rapid growth habit.

However, it contains toxic compounds and is considered a potential invasive species in some regions.
