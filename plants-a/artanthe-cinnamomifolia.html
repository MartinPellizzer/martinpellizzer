Artanthe cinnamomifolia

Artanthe cinnamomifolia, commonly known as cinnamonleaf artanthe, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a cinnamon-like scent when crushed. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
