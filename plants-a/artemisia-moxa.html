Artemisia moxa

Artemisia moxa, commonly known as mugwort, is a perennial herb belonging to the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its gray-green, feathery foliage and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Artemisia moxa is widely used in traditional medicine and as a key ingredient in moxibustion, a practice in Chinese medicine.

Its aromatic compounds contribute to its medicinal properties and distinctive earthy scent.
