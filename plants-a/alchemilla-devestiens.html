Alchemilla devestiens

Alchemilla devestiens, commonly known as the naked alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, silky hairs, and the plant produces numerous stamens in its flowers.

Alchemilla devestiens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal remedies.
