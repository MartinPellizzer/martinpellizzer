Agrostis capillaris var. glaucina

Agrostis capillaris var. glaucina, commonly known as silver hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Eurasia.

It features fine, silver-blue blades that are often covered in a powdery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.

The inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish-brown flowers.
