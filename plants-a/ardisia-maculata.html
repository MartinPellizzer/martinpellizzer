Ardisia maculata

Ardisia maculata, commonly known as the spotted evergreen or red-berried ardisia, is a member of the Myrtle family (Myrtaceae).

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with reddish veins. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy berries, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
