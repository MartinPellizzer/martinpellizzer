Avena fatua var. intermedia

Avena fatua var. intermedia, commonly known as intermediate wild oat, is a variant of the wild oat species characterized by its intermediate features between the typical wild oat and other related grasses.

It is an annual grass that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as fields, roadsides, and waste areas. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces spikelets that are often loosely arranged along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

This variant is known for its adaptability and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
