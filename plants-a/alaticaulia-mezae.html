Alaticaulia mezae

Alaticaulia mezae, commonly known as the Meza's alate caudex plant, is a unique species native to specific arid regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive swollen, cylindrical stem that stores water, a feature typical of desert flora. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or pale in color. Its leaves are reduced to small scales, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Alaticaulia mezae is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and extreme temperature fluctuations.
