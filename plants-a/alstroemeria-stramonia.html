Alstroemeria stramonia

Alstroemeria stramonia, commonly known as the red alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant has strap-like leaves that are often a deep green, and it produces showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Alstroemeria stramonia is typically grown as an ornamental plant in gardens and is valued for its long-lasting blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
