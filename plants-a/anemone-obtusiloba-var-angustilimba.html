Anemone obtusiloba var. angustilimba

Anemone obtusiloba var. angustilimba, commonly known as the narrowleaf anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with a distinctive white to pale pink flower cluster at the top. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing showy, petal-like bracts that surround the true flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
