Antidesma pubescens var. menasu

Antidesma pubescens var. menasu, commonly known as the Menasu antidesma, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often pubescent leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
