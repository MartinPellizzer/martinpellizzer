Alyssum drabiforme

Alyssum drabiforme, commonly known as the dwarf alyssum, is a low-growing annual plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Alyssum drabiforme is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
