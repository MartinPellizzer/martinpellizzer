Artanthe berbicensis

Artanthe berbicensis, commonly known as the Berbice artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Guyana and Suriname.

It is a perennial herb with erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming during the wet season. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, humid areas near rivers or in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
