Alsophila camerooniana var. currorii

Alsophila camerooniana var. currorii, commonly known as the Cameroon tree fern, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Central and West Africa.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its rhizomes are fibrous and contribute to the structural integrity of the plant, while its fronds are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
