Archirhodomyrtus vieillardii

Archirhodomyrtus vieillardii, commonly known as the New Caledonian myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the island of New Caledonia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is typically found in moist, shaded forest environments. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine by local communities.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in its native range.
