Adhatoda betonica

Adhatoda betonica, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant contains various alkaloids, including vasicine and ajmaline, which contribute to its medicinal properties. It is often grown for its ornamental value and traditional uses in Ayurvedic medicine.

Its adaptability to a range of soil types makes it a versatile plant in both horticulture and pharmaceutical applications.
