Agave ellemeetiana var. subdentata

Agave ellemeetiana var. subdentata, commonly known as the Mexican toothed agave, is a succulent plant native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often adorned with small teeth or spines along the edges. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with showy, yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and collections.
