Arcytophyllum microphyllum

Arcytophyllum microphyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sagebrush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and aromatic, gray-green foliage that contains essential oils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and a low-growing habit to conserve moisture.

Arcytophyllum microphyllum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
