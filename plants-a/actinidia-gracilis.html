Actinidia gracilis

Actinidia gracilis, commonly known as the slender actinidia, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and trifoliate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by edible, fragrant berries that ripen in the fall. These berries are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and are valued for their nutritional content.

Actinidia gracilis thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and fruit.
