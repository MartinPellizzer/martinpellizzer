Aegiphila cuneata var. hirsutissima

Aegiphila cuneata var. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy cuneate aegiphila, is a shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid climates.
