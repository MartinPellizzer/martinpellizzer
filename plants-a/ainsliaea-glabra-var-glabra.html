Ainsliaea glabra var. glabra

Ainsliaea glabra var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth ainsliaea, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a branching stem that supports its delicate floral display.
