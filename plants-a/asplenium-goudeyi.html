Asplenium goudeyi

Asplenium goudeyi, commonly known as the Goude's spleenwort, is a rare and delicate fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its small, finely divided leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette formation. Native to tropical regions of Africa, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its fronds are usually dark green and possess a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture in its native habitat.

Asplenium goudeyi is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in crevices, demonstrating its adaptability to challenging botanical conditions.
