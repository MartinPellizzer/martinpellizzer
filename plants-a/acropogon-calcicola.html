Acropogon calcicola

Acropogon calcicola, commonly known as the calcicola acropogon, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves and compact growth habit, which help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or limestone areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
