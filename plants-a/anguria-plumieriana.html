Anguria plumieriana

Anguria plumieriana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a distinctive violet throat. Its rapid growth habit and ability to climb make it a common weed in agricultural settings.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil types and climates.
