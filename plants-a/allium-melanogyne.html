Allium melanogyne

Allium melanogyne, commonly known as the black garlic plant, is a member of the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East, often growing in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, round bulbs that are typically dark purple to black in color, giving it its common name. Its leaves are long, grass-like, and often have a strong, pungent odor similar to garlic.

The plant is valued for its potential culinary and medicinal uses, though it is less commonly cultivated than other Allium species.
