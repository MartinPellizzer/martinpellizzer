Argyreia roxburghii var. siamica

Argyreia roxburghii var. siamica, commonly known as the Siam morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The Siam morning glory is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
