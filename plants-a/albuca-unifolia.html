Albuca unifolia

Albuca unifolia, commonly known as the singleleaf albuca, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and emit a mild fragrance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Albuca unifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
