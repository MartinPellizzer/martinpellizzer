Arachnaria trilobulata

Arachnaria trilobulata, commonly known as the three-lobed fern, is a rare and delicate plant characterized by its finely divided, trifoliate leaves.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to the humid understories of tropical forests. The plant exhibits a unique reproductive strategy, producing spores in small, clustered structures called sori on the underside of its fronds. Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for undisturbed ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
