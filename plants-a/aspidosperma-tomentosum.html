Aspidosperma tomentosum

Aspidosperma tomentosum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pitomba*, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, elliptical leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers that give way to bright red, fleshy fruits. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.

The plant also contains alkaloids, making it of interest in pharmacological research.
