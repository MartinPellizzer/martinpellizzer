Allium isakulii subsp. nuratense

Allium isakulii subsp.

nuratense, commonly known as the Nuratense leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia. It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a potential culinary and medicinal plant.

It is often found in mountainous regions of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan.
