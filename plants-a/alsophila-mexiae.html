Alsophila mexiae

Alsophila mexiae, commonly known as the Mexican tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is a tall, evergreen tree fern characterized by its large, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a stout, unbranched trunk that is covered in remnants of old leaf bases. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Alsophila mexiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
