Andropogon sorghum var. perennis

Andropogon sorghum var. perennis, commonly known as perennial sorghum, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to drought conditions. It produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green or reddish in color.

Perennial sorghum is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
