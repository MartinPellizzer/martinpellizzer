Acacia calantha

Acacia calantha, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and yellow flowers that bloom in dense, globular clusters. The plant has phyllodes, which are modified leaf stalks that function as leaves, and these are typically narrow and slightly curved. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
