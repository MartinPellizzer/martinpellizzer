Albuca viscosa

Albuca viscosa, commonly known as the Natal lily or false onion, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in full sun.

Albuca viscosa is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
