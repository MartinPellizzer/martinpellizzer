Anchusa undulata var. barceloi

Anchusa undulata var. barceloi, commonly known as the Barceloi bluebell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine blue or grayish bloom.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
