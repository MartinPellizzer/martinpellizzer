Acer pseudoplatanus f. albovariegatum

Acer pseudoplatanus f. albovariegatum, commonly known as the white-variegated maple, is a striking ornamental variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive white and green variegated leaves, which add visual interest to gardens and landscapes. The plant is a compact shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soils.

This cultivar is prized for its unique foliage and is often used in horticultural displays for its aesthetic appeal.
