Adenacanthus acuminatus

Adenacanthus acuminatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved adenacanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Adenacanthus acuminatus is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
