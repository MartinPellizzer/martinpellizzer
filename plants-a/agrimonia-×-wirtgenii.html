Agrimonia × wirtgenii

Agrimonia × wirtgenii, commonly known as the hybrid agrimony, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Agrimonia eupatoria and Agrimonia pilosa, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with hairy stems and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Agrimonia × wirtgenii is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, and it has been used historically in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
