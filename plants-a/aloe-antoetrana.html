Aloe antoetrana

Aloe antoetrana, commonly known as the Madagascar aloe, is a succulent plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, typical of desert plants. The plant produces a gel-like substance in its leaves, which is often used for its medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Aloe antoetrana is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its striking foliage.
