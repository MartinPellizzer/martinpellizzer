Aralia inophylla

Aralia inophylla, commonly known as the Chinese angelica tree, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and showy white or yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Araliaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties. Its berries are small, black, and contain seeds that are dispersed by animals.

The botanical features of Aralia inophylla make it a valuable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
