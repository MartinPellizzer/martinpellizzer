Acacia pelloiae

Acacia pelloiae, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features slender, often spiny stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in racemes, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Acacia pelloiae is adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate salt spray, making it a hardy coastal species.
