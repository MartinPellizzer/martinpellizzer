Agave debaryana

Agave debaryana, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a species native to central Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves. The leaves are typically blue-green in color, with white to silver markings along their edges. It produces an inflorescence of yellow flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
