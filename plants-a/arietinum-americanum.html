Arietinum americanum

Arietinum americanum, commonly known as the American groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and peppers, and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are enclosed in a leafy calyx, resembling a cherry. These fruits are edible when fully ripe and are often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower landscapes.
