Aulacocalyx mapiana

Aulacocalyx mapiana, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 20 meters tall. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and greyish, while the wood is lightweight and used in traditional crafts.
