Astragalus orizabae

Astragalus orizabae, commonly known as the Orizaba milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus orizabae is often found in mountainous regions, including the Sierra Madre Occidental.

Its ecological significance lies in its role as a source of nectar for pollinators and its potential medicinal properties.
