Allium gypsaceum

Allium gypsaceum, commonly known as the gypsum onion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment.

Allium gypsaceum is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in low-nutrient conditions.
