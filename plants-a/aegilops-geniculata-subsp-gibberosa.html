Aegilops geniculata subsp. gibberosa

Aegilops geniculata subsp.

gibberosa, commonly known as the slender wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, often tufted stems and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically 3 to 5 mm long, with two to three florets per spikelet. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy surface.

It is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.
