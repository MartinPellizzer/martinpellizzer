Asparagus edulis

Asparagus edulis, commonly known as the edible asparagus, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its fibrous, edible stems that are typically harvested when young and tender. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that develop into bright red berries. Asparagus edulis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its cultivation dates back thousands of years, with evidence of its use in ancient Egyptian and Greek civilizations.
