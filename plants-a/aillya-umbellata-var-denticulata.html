Aillya umbellata var. denticulata

Aillya umbellata var. denticulata, commonly known as the toothed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly toothed edge, giving it its varietal name.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
