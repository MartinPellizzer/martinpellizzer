Acacia catenulata subsp. catenulata

Acacia catenulata subsp.

catenulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers that appear in globular heads.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in coastal areas and is well-suited to drought conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
