Astragalus pallidus

Astragalus pallidus, commonly known as the pale milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Astragalus pallidus is also sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
