Astragalus palmeri var. johnstonii

Astragalus palmeri var. johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as desert scrub and chaparral, where it has adapted to arid conditions. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

The species is also known for its role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
