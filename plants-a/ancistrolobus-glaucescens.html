Ancistrolobus glaucescens

Ancistrolobus glaucescens, commonly known as the blue glaucous bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a waxy, bluish coating. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its drought-resistant nature makes it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Ancistrolobus glaucescens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy growth habits.
