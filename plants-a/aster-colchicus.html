Aster colchicus

Aster colchicus, commonly known as the meadow aster, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Aster colchicus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
