Alstroemeria glaucandra

Alstroemeria glaucandra, commonly known as the silver alstroemeria, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy, silvery coating, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Alstroemeria glaucandra is also noted for its long-lasting blooms, which can persist for several weeks during the growing season.
