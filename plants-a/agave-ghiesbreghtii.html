Agave ghiesbreghtii

Agave ghiesbreghtii, commonly known as the Ghiesbreght's agave, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, succulent leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It is well adapted to dry climates and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a striking addition to desert gardens.
