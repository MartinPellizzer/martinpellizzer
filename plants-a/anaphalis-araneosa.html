Anaphalis araneosa

Anaphalis araneosa, commonly known as the spider plant or woolly cudweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features dense, woolly cobwebs of silky white hairs covering its stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Anaphalis araneosa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
