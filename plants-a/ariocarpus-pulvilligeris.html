Ariocarpus pulvilligeris

Ariocarpus pulvilligeris, commonly known as the "paddle cactus," is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, and its roots are adapted to absorb moisture efficiently in arid environments.

Ariocarpus pulvilligeris is known for its slow growth and ability to store water in its succulent stems.
