Amaranthus miamiensis

Amaranthus miamiensis, commonly known as Miami amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant red or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, three-angled seeds that are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in disturbed areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaranthaceae family, which includes many species with similar structural and ecological traits.
