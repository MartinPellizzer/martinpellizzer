Adiantum cunninghamii

Adiantum cunninghamii, commonly known as the mountain maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, bi-lobed fronds that give it a lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a distinctive rachis that is slightly hairy.

Adiantum cunninghamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
