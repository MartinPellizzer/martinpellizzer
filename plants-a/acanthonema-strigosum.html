Acanthonema strigosum

Acanthonema strigosum, commonly known as the hairy acanthonema, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
