Atherophora secunda

Atherophora secunda, commonly known as the lesser betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves, typical of the mint family. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, arranged in whorls along the stem.

It is often found in meadows and woodland edges, and it has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
