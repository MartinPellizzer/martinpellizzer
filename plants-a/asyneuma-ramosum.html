Asyneuma ramosum

Asyneuma ramosum, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and opposite leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, which bloom from spring to early summer. Asyneuma ramosum is typically found in moist, open habitats such as fields and woodland edges.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
