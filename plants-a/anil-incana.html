Anil incana

Anil incana, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and its small, star-shaped flowers. It produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not commonly cultivated for food or ornamental purposes.
