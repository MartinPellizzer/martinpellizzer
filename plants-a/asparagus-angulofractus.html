Asparagus angulofractus

Asparagus angulofractus, commonly known as the angular asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, succulent stems that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, often with a slightly zigzagged or angular appearance.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
