Anthericum omissum

Anthericum omissum, commonly known as the slender yellow flag, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and yellowish-green spadix surrounded by a white or green spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies.

Anthericum omissum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
