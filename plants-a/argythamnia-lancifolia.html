Argythamnia lancifolia

Argythamnia lancifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Argythamnia lancifolia plays an important role in stabilizing desert soils and providing food for wildlife.
