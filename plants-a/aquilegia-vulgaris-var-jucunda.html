Aquilegia vulgaris var. jucunda

Aquilegia vulgaris var. jucunda, commonly known as the garden columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features showy, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves with lobes that are often notched or toothed. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden borders and wildflower gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
