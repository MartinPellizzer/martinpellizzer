Astragalus mesopotamicus

Astragalus mesopotamicus, commonly known as Mesopotamian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are adapted for dispersal by wind, allowing it to thrive in harsh environments.
