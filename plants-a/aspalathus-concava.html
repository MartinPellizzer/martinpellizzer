Aspalathus concava

Aspalathus concava, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bushveld pea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, pale green to yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in grassland and rocky areas. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a shallow root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Aspalathus concava is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soils.
