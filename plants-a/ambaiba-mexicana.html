Ambaiba mexicana

Ambaiba mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive bluish-green color. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, with five petals and a prominent yellow stamen.

Ambaiba mexicana produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but contain toxic compounds, making them suitable only for use in traditional medicine under expert guidance.
