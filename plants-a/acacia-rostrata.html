Acacia rostrata

Acacia rostrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typically followed by long, slender pods. Acacia rostrata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought resistance and a capacity for resprouting after fire.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
