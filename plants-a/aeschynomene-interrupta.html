Aeschynomene interrupta

Aeschynomene interrupta, commonly known as partridge pea, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, which helps improve soil fertility.

Its adaptability makes it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and agricultural purposes.
