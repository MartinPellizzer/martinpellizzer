Antirrhinum chrysothales

Antirrhinum chrysothales, commonly known as the golden flame snapdragon, is a striking flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It produces tall, spires of vibrant orange to yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, preferring temperate climates.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
