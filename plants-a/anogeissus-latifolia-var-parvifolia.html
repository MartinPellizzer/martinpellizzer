Anogeissus latifolia var. parvifolia

Anogeissus latifolia var. parvifolia, commonly known as small-leaved Indian sandalwood, is a variant of the Indian sandalwood species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant wood. The plant is valued for its aromatic timber, which is used in traditional medicine and perfumery. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry deciduous forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
