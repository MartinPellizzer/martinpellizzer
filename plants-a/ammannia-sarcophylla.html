Ammannia sarcophylla

Ammannia sarcophylla, commonly known as the redroot ammannia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its reddish stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Ammannia sarcophylla thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland areas or along the edges of water bodies.

It is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
