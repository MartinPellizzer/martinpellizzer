Ajuga sikkimensis

Ajuga sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim bugleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its creeping rhizomes and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers bloom in early to mid-summer.

Ajuga sikkimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
