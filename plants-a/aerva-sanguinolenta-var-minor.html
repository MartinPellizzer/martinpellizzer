Aerva sanguinolenta var. minor

Aerva sanguinolenta var. minor, commonly known as the lesser bloodroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish hue, and it produces striking red or orange flowers during the spring. The plant is characterized by its milky sap, which is often reddish in color, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
