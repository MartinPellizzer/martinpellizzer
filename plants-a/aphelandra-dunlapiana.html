Aphelandra dunlapiana

Aphelandra dunlapiana, commonly known as the Dunlap's torch ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its vibrant, flame-like inflorescences that resemble torches, with bright orange or red bracts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically hidden beneath the showy bracts. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil and partial shade.

Aphelandra dunlapiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
