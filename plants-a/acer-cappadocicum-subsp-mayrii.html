Acer cappadocicum subsp. mayrii

Acer cappadocicum subsp.

mayrii, commonly known as the Mayr's maple, is a rare and distinctive species native to the mountainous regions of Turkey. It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring palmately lobed leaves with a striking combination of green and red hues.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable but endangered species in its native range.
