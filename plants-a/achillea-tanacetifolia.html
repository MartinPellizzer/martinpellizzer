Achillea tanacetifolia

Achillea tanacetifolia, commonly known as yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal medicine.

Its historical use dates back to ancient times for its medicinal properties.
