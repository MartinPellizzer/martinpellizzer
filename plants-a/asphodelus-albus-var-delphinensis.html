Asphodelus albus var. delphinensis

Asphodelus albus var. delphinensis, commonly known as the white asphodel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces tall, erect flowering stems that bear clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
