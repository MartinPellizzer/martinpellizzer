Anthopogon macounii

Anthopogon macounii, commonly known as the Macoun's aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges, and it produces showy purple to pinkish flowers in the late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, and the plant is often found in sandy or loamy soils.

Anthopogon macounii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
