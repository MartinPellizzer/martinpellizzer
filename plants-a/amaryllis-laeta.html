Amaryllis laeta

Amaryllis laeta, commonly known as the white amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant features strap-like leaves that are broad and grass-like, emerging from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Amaryllis laeta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
