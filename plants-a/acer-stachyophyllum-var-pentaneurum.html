Acer stachyophyllum var. pentaneurum

Acer stachyophyllum var. pentaneurum, commonly known as the five-veined maple, is a variant of the maple tree native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring leaves with five prominent veins that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
