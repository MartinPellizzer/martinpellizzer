Astragalus zerabulaki

Astragalus zerabulaki, commonly known as the Zerabulak milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its deep root system. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, which aids in seed dispersal.

Astragalus zerabulaki is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
