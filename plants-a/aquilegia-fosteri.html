Aquilegia fosteri

Aquilegia fosteri, commonly known as Foster's columbine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that come in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and reaches heights of 1 to 3 feet. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers bloom in early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
