Agrostis canina var. borneensis

Agrostis canina var. borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean mousegrass, is a grass species native to the tropical regions of Borneo.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, typical of the Poaceae family.

Agrostis canina var. borneensis plays a minor role in local ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
