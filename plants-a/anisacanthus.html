Anisacanthus

Anisacanthus, commonly known as the flame flower, is a genus of flowering plants native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often resembling flames. The plant is a shrub or small tree with woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Anisacanthus species are known for their drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
