Aspalathus sanguinea subsp. foliosa

Aspalathus sanguinea subsp.

foliosa, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
