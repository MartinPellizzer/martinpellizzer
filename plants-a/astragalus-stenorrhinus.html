Astragalus stenorrhinus

Astragalus stenorrhinus, commonly known as the narrow-nosed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus stenorrhinus is often found in steppe and desert environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
