Aspalathus erythrodes

Aspalathus erythrodes, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red flowers and woody stems, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a serrated margin, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Aspalathus erythrodes is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
