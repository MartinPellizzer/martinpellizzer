Amsinckia glauca

Amsinckia glauca, commonly known as the bluecurls, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often tinged with a bluish hue, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in arid and semi-arid regions.

Amsinckia glauca is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
