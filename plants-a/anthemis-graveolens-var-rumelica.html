Anthemis graveolens var. rumelica

Anthemis graveolens var. rumelica, commonly known as the Rumelian fennel, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, linear leaves and yellow ray florets that form composite flower heads. The plant produces a distinctive, sharp scent due to its essential oils, which are concentrated in the leaves and flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
