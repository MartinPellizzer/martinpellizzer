Acer pseudoplatanus f. flavovariegatum

Acer pseudoplatanus f. flavovariegatum, commonly known as the golden-leaved maple, is a variegated form of the common maple.

It features striking yellow and green foliage, with leaves that exhibit a distinctive variegation pattern. This plant is a cultivar of the European maple, known for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a smooth margin and a vibrant color palette.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often used for its aesthetic appeal rather than for fruit or timber production.
