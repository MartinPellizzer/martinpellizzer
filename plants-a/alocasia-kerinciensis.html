Alocasia kerinciensis

Alocasia kerinciensis, commonly known as the Kerinci Alocasia, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Indonesia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant is a member of the Araceae family, which includes many ornamental and edible plants. It thrives in high humidity and warm temperatures, typically requiring indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
