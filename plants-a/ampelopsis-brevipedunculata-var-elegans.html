Ampelopsis brevipedunculata var. elegans

Ampelopsis brevipedunculata var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, often with a reddish or bronze tint in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, grape-like berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in partial to full sun and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or walls.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both gardeners and botanists.
