Asparagus abyssinicus

Asparagus abyssinicus, commonly known as Abyssinian asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible, needle-like shoots that are harvested in the spring for culinary use. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and nutritional value, it is both ecologically and culturally significant in its native habitat.
