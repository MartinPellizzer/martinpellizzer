Aspidosperma nigricans

Aspidosperma nigricans, commonly known as black spurge, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It contains various alkaloids, some of which have medicinal properties.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in warm climates.
