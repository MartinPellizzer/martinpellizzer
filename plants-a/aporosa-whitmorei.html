Aporosa whitmorei

Aporosa whitmorei, commonly known as the Bornean ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Aporosa whitmorei plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
