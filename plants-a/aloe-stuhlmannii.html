Aloe stuhlmannii

Aloe stuhlmannii, commonly known as the Stuhlmann's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in a white powdery substance called farina. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow-orange flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Aloe stuhlmannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
