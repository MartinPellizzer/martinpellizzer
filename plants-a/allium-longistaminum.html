Allium longistaminum

Allium longistaminum, commonly known as leek, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to onions and garlic. The plant features a long, cylindrical stem that is white and bulbous at the base, with broad, sword-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically umbellate, with small, pale purple or white blossoms.

The edible part of the plant is the white, fleshy stalk, which is used in various cuisines around the world.
