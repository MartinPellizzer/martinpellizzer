Aloe pallidiflora

Aloe pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Aloe pallidiflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and resilience.
