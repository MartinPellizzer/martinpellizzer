Alchemilla longana

Alchemilla longana, commonly known as the longan alchemilla, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive, rounded, bead-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Alchemilla longana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
