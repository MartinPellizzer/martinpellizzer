Asperula conferta

Asperula conferta, commonly known as the creeping azalea, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It features dense, dark green foliage and produces clusters of small, fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for ground cover or rock gardens. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Asperula conferta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a variety of environmental conditions.
