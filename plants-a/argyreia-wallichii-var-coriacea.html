Argyreia wallichii var. coriacea

Argyreia wallichii var. coriacea, commonly known as the velvet bean or red veined morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and deep green color. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with prominent red veins. Its seeds are enclosed in a distinctive, thick, fleshy pod, and the plant is known for its rapid growth habit.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses, though it can be invasive in some regions.
