Allium acutangulum var. senescens

Allium acutangulum var. senescens, commonly known as the senescens garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features grass-like, slender leaves that are often used in culinary applications for their mild garlic flavor. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
