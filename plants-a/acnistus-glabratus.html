Acnistus glabratus

Acnistus glabratus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils. Acnistus glabratus is often considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spreading habit.

Its botanical features make it a notable plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
