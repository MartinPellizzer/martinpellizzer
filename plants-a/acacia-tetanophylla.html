Acacia tetanophylla

Acacia tetanophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia tetanophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soils in ecological restoration projects.
