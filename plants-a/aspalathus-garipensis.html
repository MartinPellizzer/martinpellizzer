Aspalathus garipensis

Aspalathus garipensis, commonly known as the Cape mountain tea, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant is known for its small, bright green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
