Azarolus crataegoides var. dulcis

Azarolus crataegoides var. dulcis, commonly known as the sweet azarolus, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The leaves are oval-shaped, with a glossy green surface and serrated edges. The plant produces small, red berries that ripen in late summer, which are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
