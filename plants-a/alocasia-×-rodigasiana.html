Alocasia × rodigasiana

Alocasia × rodigasiana, commonly known as the Black Velvet Plant, is a hybrid member of the Araceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety foliage with a striking contrast of deep green and purple hues. The plant produces clusters of small, white spadices surrounded by large, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant and in tropical gardens.
