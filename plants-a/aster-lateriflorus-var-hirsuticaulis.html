Aster lateriflorus var. hirsuticaulis

Aster lateriflorus var. hirsuticaulis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its botanical name. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the fall. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open meadows and grasslands.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native environment.
