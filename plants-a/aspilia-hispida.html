Aspilia hispida

Aspilia hispida, commonly known as the rough aspilia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a compact inflorescence. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and shrublands.

Aspilia hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
