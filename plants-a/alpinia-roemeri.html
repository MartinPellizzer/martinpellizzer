Alpinia roemeri

Alpinia roemeri, commonly known as the Java ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Alpinia roemeri is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
