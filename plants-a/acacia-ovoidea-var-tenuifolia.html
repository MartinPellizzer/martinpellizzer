Acacia ovoidea var. tenuifolia

Acacia ovoidea var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated phyllodes that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
