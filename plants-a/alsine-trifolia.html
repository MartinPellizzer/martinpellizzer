Alsine trifolia

Alsine trifolia, commonly known as three-leaved alsike, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It is typically found in moist, sandy soils and is often seen in open fields or along roadsides.

Alsine trifolia is an annual herb that plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
