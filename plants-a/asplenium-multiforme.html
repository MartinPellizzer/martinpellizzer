Asplenium multiforme

Asplenium multiforme, commonly known as the variable spleenwort, is a fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and small, lance-shaped leaves that often have a velvety texture. The plant typically grows on rocks, walls, or tree trunks, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Asplenium multiforme is a hardy species that is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability.
