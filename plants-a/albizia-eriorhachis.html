Albizia eriorhachis

Albizia eriorhachis, commonly known as the silky albizia, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in summer. Its pods are slender and hairy, containing flattened seeds.

Albizia eriorhachis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
