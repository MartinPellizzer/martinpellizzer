Acer campestre f. molle

Acer campestre f. molle, commonly known as the soft-leaved field maple, is a variant of the field maple tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its smooth, thin leaves that are typically lighter in color compared to the standard species. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
