Allium litvinovii

Allium litvinovii, commonly known as the Litvinov's onion, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild onion-like flavor, making it a potential candidate for culinary use.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
