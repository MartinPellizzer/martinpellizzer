Astragalus angarensis

Astragalus angarensis, commonly known as the Angarensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Astragalus angarensis is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
