Actinodaphne sessilifructa

Actinodaphne sessilifructa, commonly known as the sessile-fruited camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces oval, woody fruits that contain a single seed, and its bark exudes a fragrant, resinous substance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
