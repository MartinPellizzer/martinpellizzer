Arisaema meleagris

Arisaema meleagris, commonly known as the chicken wing plant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, leaf-like structures called bracts. The plant produces a spadix, which is surrounded by a modified leaf known as a spathe. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

Arisaema meleagris is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas.
