Alternanthera urbani

Alternanthera urbani, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage that ranges from red to purple, depending on light exposure and nutrient levels. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often used in ornamental ponds and aquariums. Its leaves are broad and succulent, with a waxy texture that gives it a distinctive appearance.

Alternanthera urbani is also notable for its ability to produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged.
