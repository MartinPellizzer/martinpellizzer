Acacia marginata

Acacia marginata, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that appear in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Acacia marginata thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in dune environments.

It is well adapted to salt spray and can tolerate drought conditions.
