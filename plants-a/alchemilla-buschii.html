Alchemilla buschii

Alchemilla buschii, commonly known as the Busch's lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of soft, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Alchemilla buschii is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ability to attract beneficial insects.
