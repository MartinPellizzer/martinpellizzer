Aristolochia weddellii subsp. duckeana

Aristolochia weddellii subsp.

duckeana, commonly known as the Brazilian birthwort, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America. It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers.

The plant produces a strong, aromatic odor that attracts pollinators such as flies and beetles. Its morphology includes tendrils for climbing and a complex floral structure with a hooded shape.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
