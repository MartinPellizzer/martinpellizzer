Amyris capensis

Amyris capensis, commonly known as the Cape star, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

Amyris capensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
