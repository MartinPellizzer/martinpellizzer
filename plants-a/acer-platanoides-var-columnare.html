Acer platanoides var. columnare

Acer platanoides var. columnare, commonly known as the columnar Norway maple, is a variant of the Norway maple characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit.

It features palmate leaves with five lobes, typically turning yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It is native to Europe and thrives in a variety of soil types, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact form, it is often used in urban landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability.
