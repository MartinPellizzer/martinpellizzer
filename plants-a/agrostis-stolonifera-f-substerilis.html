Agrostis stolonifera f. substerilis

Agrostis stolonifera f. substerilis, commonly known as the sterile form of bentgrass, is a variant of the common bentgrass species.

It is characterized by its reduced fertility, which results in the absence of viable seeds. This form typically exhibits a dense, low-growing turf that is often found in lawns and golf courses. Botanically, it has slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

Its sterile nature makes it less invasive compared to the fertile form, but it still contributes to the overall structure and appearance of the turf.
