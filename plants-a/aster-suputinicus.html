Aster suputinicus

Aster suputinicus, commonly known as the mountain aster, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, adapted to the harsh mountain environment.

Aster suputinicus is often found in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
