Adenostyles alpina subsp. pyrenaica

Adenostyles alpina subsp.

pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring rocky or gravelly soils.

The plant features erect, slender stems that bear showy blue to purple flowers clustered in terminal racemes. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often hairy, and adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in alpine ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
