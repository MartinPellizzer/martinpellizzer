Abutilon septemlobum

Abutilon septemlobum, commonly known as the seven-lobed abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with seven distinct lobes, giving it its common name. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, often blooming in the spring and summer.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.
