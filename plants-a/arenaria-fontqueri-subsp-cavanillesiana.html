Arenaria fontqueri subsp. cavanillesiana

Arenaria fontqueri subsp.

cavanillesiana, commonly known as the Canary Island sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Canary Islands. It typically features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has a compact, mat-forming habit with hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover in dry landscapes.
