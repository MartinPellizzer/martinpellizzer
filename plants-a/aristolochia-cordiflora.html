Aristolochia cordiflora

Aristolochia cordiflora, commonly known as the heartleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique, pipe-like flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant is a climbing vine that produces showy, aromatic blooms in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The genus Aristolochia contains many species with similar morphological features and ecological roles.
