Arundastrum canniforme

Arundastrum canniforme, commonly known as the candleplant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that resemble candles, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland areas with well-drained conditions.

Arundastrum canniforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
