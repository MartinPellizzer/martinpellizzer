Areca erythrocarpa

Areca erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited areca, is a tropical palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its fruits are edible and contain a single seed, making it valuable both for its ornamental qualities and its potential as a food source.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in tropical gardens.
