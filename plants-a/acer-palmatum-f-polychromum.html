Acer palmatum f. polychromum

Acer palmatum f. polychromum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with variegated leaves, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree known for its striking foliage.

It features leaves that display a mix of green, yellow, and white, creating a vibrant, colorful effect throughout the growing season. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil. It is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.

This cultivar is relatively low maintenance but requires protection from harsh winter conditions.
