Ailanthus imberbiflora var. macartneyi

Ailanthus imberbiflora var. macartneyi, commonly known as the Macartney's ailanthus, is a variant of the ailanthus tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with finely serrated margins and small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles. The plant produces woody, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Simaroubaceae family.
