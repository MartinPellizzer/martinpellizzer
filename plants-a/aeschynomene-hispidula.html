Aeschynomene hispidula

Aeschynomene hispidula, commonly known as hairy Aeschynomene, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical, hairy seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Aeschynomene hispidula is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
