Agave perringea

Agave perringea, commonly known as the Mexican hat agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears white or pale purple flowers. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Agave perringea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
