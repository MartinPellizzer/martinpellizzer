Aldama kunthiana

Aldama kunthiana, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters, characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Aldama kunthiana plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for desert wildlife.
