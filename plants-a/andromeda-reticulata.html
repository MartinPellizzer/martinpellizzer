Andromeda reticulata

Andromeda reticulata, commonly known as the sweet azalea, is a flowering shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known species like rhododendrons and heathers. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its fragrant blooms, which appear in early spring.
