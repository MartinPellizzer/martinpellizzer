Agropyron littorale var. acutiflorum

Agropyron littorale var. acutiflorum, commonly known as the coastal needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions of North America.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and spike-like, arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters.

This variety is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
