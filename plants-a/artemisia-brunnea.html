Artemisia brunnea

Artemisia brunnea, commonly known as black sage, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in chaparral and coastal sage scrub habitats. Its leaves contain volatile oils that give it a strong, pungent aroma.

Artemisia brunnea is also valued for its medicinal properties and historical use by indigenous peoples.
