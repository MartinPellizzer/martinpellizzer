Acacia pruinocarpa

Acacia pruinocarpa, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stem and bipinnate leaves that are covered in a fine, whitish bloom, giving it a silky appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
