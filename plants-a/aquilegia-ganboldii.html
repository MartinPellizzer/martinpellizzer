Aquilegia ganboldii

Aquilegia ganboldii, commonly known as the Mongolian columbine, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive central structure. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
