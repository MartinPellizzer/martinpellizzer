Astragalus reventus var. sheldonii

Astragalus reventus var. sheldonii, commonly known as Sheldon's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has adapted to arid conditions through its deep root system. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, and it produces small, rounded legumes as fruits.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators and soil stabilization in its native range.
