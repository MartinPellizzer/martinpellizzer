Abrus pulchellus var. tenuiflorus

Abrus pulchellus var. tenuiflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with small, elliptical leaflets and produces intricate, ornate flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant bears pods that contain flat, kidney-shaped seeds with a distinctive red aril. These seeds are toxic and should not be ingested, although the plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
