Alangium barbatum subsp. decipiens

Alangium barbatum subsp.

decipiens, commonly known as the false alangium, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The plant has a woody stem and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
