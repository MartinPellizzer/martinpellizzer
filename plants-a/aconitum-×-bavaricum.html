Aconitum × bavaricum

Aconitum × bavaricum, commonly known as the Bavarian monkshood, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features palmately compound leaves with deeply cut lobes, and it typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. Native to Central Europe, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

Its toxic properties, due to the presence of alkaloids, make it a plant that requires careful handling.
