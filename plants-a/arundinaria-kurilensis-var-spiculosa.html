Arundinaria kurilensis var. spiculosa

Arundinaria kurilensis var. spiculosa, commonly known as the Kuril bamboo, is a species native to the Russian Far East and the Korean Peninsula.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist, temperate climates. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
