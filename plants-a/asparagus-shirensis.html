Asparagus shirensis

Asparagus shirensis, commonly known as the Shirensis asparagus, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its arching stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible young shoots, which are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a tendency to form dense clumps over time.
