Adiantum venustum

Adiantum venustum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bright green fronds that resemble flowing hair, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its fronds are typically biennial, with the fertile fronds appearing in the second year.

Adiantum venustum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and indoor spaces.
