Ambassa

Ambassa, commonly known as the African starfruit or yellow starfruit, is a tropical plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange fruits with a star-shaped appearance. The plant produces small, green leaves and has a woody stem that can grow into a shrub or small tree. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it relies on insect pollination.

The fruit is typically harvested when it turns a bright yellow, indicating maturity and readiness for consumption.
