Artocarpus eberhardtii

Artocarpus eberhardtii, commonly known as the Eberhardt's breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and distinctive fruit. The fruit, which is typically round and covered in a rough, spiky husk, contains a sweet, starchy pulp surrounded by a fibrous layer. The tree is valued for its edible fruit and is often grown in home gardens and small-scale agriculture.

It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
