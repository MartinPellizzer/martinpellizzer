Artemisia magellanica

Artemisia magellanica, commonly known as Magellan's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and aromatic, finely divided leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical shapes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The essential oils found in its leaves contain compounds like thujone, which have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.
