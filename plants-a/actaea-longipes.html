Actaea longipes

Actaea longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed bugbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Actaea longipes thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and wetlands.
