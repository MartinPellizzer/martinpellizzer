Acacia curassavica

Acacia curassavica, commonly known as the flame acacia, is a fast-growing, deciduous tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often thriving in coastal areas. Its bark is smooth and light gray, while its wood is lightweight and durable.

Acacia curassavica is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
