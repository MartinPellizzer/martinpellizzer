Attalea humboldtiana

Attalea humboldtiana, commonly known as the Pataua palm, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It is a tall, slender palm that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, characterized by its pinnate leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible fruits, which are consumed by both wildlife and humans. Its fibrous trunk is valued for its strength and is used in traditional crafts and construction.

The Pataua palm plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various species.
