Albuca praecox

Albuca praecox, commonly known as the early albuca, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-shaped, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant is adapted to arid environments and prefers well-drained soil. It blooms in early spring, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
