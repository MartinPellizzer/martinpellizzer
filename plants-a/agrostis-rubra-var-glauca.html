Agrostis rubra var. glauca

Agrostis rubra var. glauca, commonly known as blue meadowgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which makes it visually distinct from other grasses. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. Its compact growth and dense turf-forming ability make it a valuable plant for erosion control and ornamental use.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
