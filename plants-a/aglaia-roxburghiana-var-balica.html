Aglaia roxburghiana var. balica

Aglaia roxburghiana var. balica, commonly known as the Balic silverwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, characterized by its aromatic wood and distinctive leaves. The plant features compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into woody capsules. Its bark is thick and often cracked, exuding a fragrant resin.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine and craftsmanship.
