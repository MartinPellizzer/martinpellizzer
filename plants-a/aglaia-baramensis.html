Aglaia baramensis

Aglaia baramensis, commonly known as the Baram balsam.

It is a member of the Meliaceae family, native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy surface.

The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds.
