Acianthera lepidota

Acianthera lepidota, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

The flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.
