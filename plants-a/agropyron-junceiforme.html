Agropyron junceiforme

Agropyron junceiforme, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, including high altitudes and arid conditions, due to its deep root system and drought tolerance. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, contributing to soil stabilization.

Agropyron junceiforme is valued for its ecological role in preventing erosion and supporting local wildlife.
