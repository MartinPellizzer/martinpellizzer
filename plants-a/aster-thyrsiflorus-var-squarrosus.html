Aster thyrsiflorus var. squarrosus

Aster thyrsiflorus var. squarrosus, commonly known as the squarrose aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of many small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are blue to purple in color.

The squarrose aster is known for its showy, clustered blooms that bloom in late summer to early fall.
