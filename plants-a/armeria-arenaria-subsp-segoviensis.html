Armeria arenaria subsp. segoviensis

Armeria arenaria subsp.

segoviensis, commonly known as the Segovia sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant features dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
