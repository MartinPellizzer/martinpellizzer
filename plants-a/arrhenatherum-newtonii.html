Arrhenatherum newtonii

Arrhenatherum newtonii, commonly known as Newton's feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems that bear whorled clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
