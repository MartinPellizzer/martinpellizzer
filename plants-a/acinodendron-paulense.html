Acinodendron paulense

Acinodendron paulense, commonly known as the Brazilian acacia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber and medicinal properties.
