Allium schergianum

Allium schergianum, commonly known as the Schergian garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are similar in appearance to garlic but have a milder flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
