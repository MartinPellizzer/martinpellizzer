Arctostaphylos acuta

Arctostaphylos acuta, commonly known as the Sierra prickly-pear, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, and it bears edible berries that are often consumed by wildlife.

Arctostaphylos acuta plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various animals.
