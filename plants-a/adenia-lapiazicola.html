Adenia lapiazicola

Adenia lapiazicola, commonly known as the mountain adenia, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of eastern Africa.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae, which includes passionflowers, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive crown of stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
