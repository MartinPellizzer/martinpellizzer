Amaranthus quitensis f. rufescens

Amaranthus quitensis f. rufescens, commonly known as the red amaranth, is a variant of the amaranth species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking reddish pigmentation in the leaves and bracts, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs, while its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both decorative and culinary purposes in certain regions.
