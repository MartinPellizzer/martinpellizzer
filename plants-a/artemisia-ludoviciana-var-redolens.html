Artemisia ludoviciana var. redolens

Artemisia ludoviciana var. redolens, commonly known as sweet fern, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage that emits a distinctive, fresh scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open woods and rocky areas.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for native plant gardens.
