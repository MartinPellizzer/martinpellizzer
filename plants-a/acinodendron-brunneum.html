Acinodendron brunneum

Acinodendron brunneum, commonly known as the brown acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its bark is rough and often fissured, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Acinodendron brunneum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand dry conditions.
