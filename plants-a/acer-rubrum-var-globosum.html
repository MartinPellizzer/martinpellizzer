Acer rubrum var. globosum

Acer rubrum var. globosum, commonly known as the red maple with globe-shaped fruits, is a variant of the red maple tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded samaras that form dense clusters, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks and in floodplain forests. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a vibrant red color in the fall, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits in urban and natural landscapes.
