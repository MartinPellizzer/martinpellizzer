Adesmia filipes var. obcordata

Adesmia filipes var. obcordata, commonly known as the heartleaf adesmia, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lanceolate leaves that are often heart-shaped at the base. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and extended blooming period.
