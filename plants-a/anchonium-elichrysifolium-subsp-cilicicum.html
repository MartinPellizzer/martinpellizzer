Anchonium elichrysifolium subsp. cilicicum

Anchonium elichrysifolium subsp.

cilicicum, commonly known as the Cilician goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its native habitat, making it a resilient species in Mediterranean climates.
