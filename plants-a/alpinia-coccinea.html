Alpinia coccinea

Alpinia coccinea, commonly known as the red ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround small, yellowish flowers, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained, fertile soil. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a glossy texture.

Alpinia coccinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
