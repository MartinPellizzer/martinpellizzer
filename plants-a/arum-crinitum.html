Arum crinitum

Arum crinitum, commonly known as the curly arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It features a large, heart-shaped leaf with a distinctive, curling edge that gives it its common name. The plant produces a spadix, a dense cluster of tiny flowers surrounded by a showy, petal-like spathe. Arum crinitum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its unique foliage and dramatic inflorescence make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor plant collections.
