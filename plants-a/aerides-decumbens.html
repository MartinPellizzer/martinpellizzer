Aerides decumbens

Aerides decumbens, commonly known as the creeping orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with creeping stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest floors or rocky areas.

Due to its unique growth pattern and ornamental flowers, it is occasionally cultivated by enthusiasts.
