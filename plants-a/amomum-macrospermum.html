Amomum macrospermum

Amomum macrospermum, commonly known as large-seeded cardamom, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green flowers that develop into dark brown, three-lobed fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, whitish bloom, which helps to reflect sunlight.

The plant is valued for its essential oils and is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
