Aesculus mexicana

Aesculus mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The tree produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in pendulous clusters, followed by spiny capsules containing shiny, chestnut-like seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry to moist woodland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
