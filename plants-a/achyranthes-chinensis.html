Achyranthes chinensis

Achyranthes chinensis, commonly known as Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its roots are used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.
