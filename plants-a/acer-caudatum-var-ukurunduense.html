Acer caudatum var. ukurunduense

Acer caudatum var. ukurunduense, commonly known as the Ukurundu maple, is a rare and ornamental tree native to the highlands of central Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
