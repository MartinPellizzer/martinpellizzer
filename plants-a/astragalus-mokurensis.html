Astragalus mokurensis

Astragalus mokurensis, commonly known as the Mokulungu milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological significance is notable in its native habitats due to its ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
