Acer ceriferum

Acer ceriferum, commonly known as the sugar maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. Its bark is smooth and gray when young, becoming more rugged with age.

Acer ceriferum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also important for timber and ecological habitats.
