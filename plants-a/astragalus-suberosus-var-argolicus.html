Astragalus suberosus var. argolicus

Astragalus suberosus var. argolicus, commonly known as the Argolic Astragalus, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is of interest in botany due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
