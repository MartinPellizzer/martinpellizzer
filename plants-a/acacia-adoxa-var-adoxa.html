Acacia adoxa var. adoxa

Acacia adoxa var. adoxa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
