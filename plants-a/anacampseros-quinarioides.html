Anacampseros quinarioides, commonly known as the quinarioides ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
