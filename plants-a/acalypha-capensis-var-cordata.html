Acalypha capensis var. cordata

Acalypha capensis var. cordata, commonly known as the African nettle or velvetleaf, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and riverine forests.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage.
