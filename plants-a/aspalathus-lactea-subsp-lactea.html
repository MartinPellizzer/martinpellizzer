Aspalathus lactea subsp. lactea

Aspalathus lactea subsp.

lactea, commonly known as the white milkbush, is a South African plant belonging to the Fabaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, spherical pods that contain several seeds. It is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils in the Western and Northern Cape provinces.

This subspecies is notable for its use in traditional medicine and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
