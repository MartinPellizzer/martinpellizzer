Arthrochilus laevicallus

Arthrochilus laevicallus, commonly known as the smooth-lobed rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are usually linear and arranged in a rosette, while its flowering stems are short and often bear a single flower.

Arthrochilus laevicallus is adapted to withstand drought and is often found in coastal or inland areas with limited water availability.
