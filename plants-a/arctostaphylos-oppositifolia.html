Arctostaphylos oppositifolia

Arctostaphylos oppositifolia, commonly known as the Sierra manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are opposite and leathery, and its clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
