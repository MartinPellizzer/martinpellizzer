Acmispon maritimus var. brevivexillus

Acmispon maritimus var. brevivexillus, commonly known as the sandbelly needlepod, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, and its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along the edges of salt marshes.
