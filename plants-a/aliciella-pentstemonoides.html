Aliciella pentstemonoides

Aliciella pentstemonoides, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Aliciella pentstemonoides is an important species for pollinators and is often found in rocky soils and desert scrub habitats.
