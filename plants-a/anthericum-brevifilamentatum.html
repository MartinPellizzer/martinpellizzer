Anthericum brevifilamentatum

Anthericum brevifilamentatum, commonly known as the short-filament arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a distinctive, leafless stem that bears a spatulate, greenish-white inflorescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly in moist, shady environments.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
