Atriplex recta

Atriplex recta, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in saline and alkaline soils. Its small, greenish flowers are typically clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

Atriplex recta plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
