Agave wildingii

Agave wildingii, commonly known as the Desert Agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces a tall, branched flower stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing small, yellowish-green flowers. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Agave wildingii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaping.
