Aristolochia schottii

Aristolochia schottii, commonly known as the Schott's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and possesses a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The genus Aristolochia is known for containing aristolochic acids, which are toxic to humans and animals.
