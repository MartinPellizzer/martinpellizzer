Apatesia helianthoides

Apatesia helianthoides, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Apatesia helianthoides plays a role in supporting local pollinators, particularly bees and butterflies.
