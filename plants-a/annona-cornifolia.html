Annona cornifolia

Annona cornifolia, commonly known as the horned paw or horned cucumber, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits with a green, smooth skin. The fruit contains a single large, inedible seed surrounded by a sweet, juicy pulp.

Annona cornifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its unique appearance and edible yield.
