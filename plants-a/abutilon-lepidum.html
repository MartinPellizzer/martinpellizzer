Abutilon lepidum

Abutilon lepidum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a velvety appearance. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in arid landscapes.
