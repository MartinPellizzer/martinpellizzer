Aechmolepis rosmarinifolia

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and vibrant floral displays.

Morphology

Aechmolepis rosmarinifolia has leaves that are narrow, lance-shaped, and arranged oppositely along the stem, with a dark green color and a slightly serrated edge.

The stem is slender, woody, and covered with a smooth, grayish bark that becomes rougher with age. Its small, white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, bloom during the summer months, and are pollinated by insects.
