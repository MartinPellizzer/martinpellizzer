Astragalus aureus var. chromolepis

Astragalus aureus var. chromolepis, commonly known as the golden milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its small, lens-shaped seeds are encased in a leathery pod.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
