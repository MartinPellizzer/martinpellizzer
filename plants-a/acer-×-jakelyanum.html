Acer × jakelyanum

Acer × jakelyanum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Sapindaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often variegated foliage that displays shades of red, orange, and yellow during the autumn season. The plant typically grows to a height of 5–15 meters, with a rounded crown and smooth, fissured bark. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and turn brilliant colors in the fall, making it a popular ornamental species.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is well-suited for cultivation in temperate climates.
