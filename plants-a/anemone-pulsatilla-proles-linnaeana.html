Anemone pulsatilla proles linnaeana

Anemone pulsatilla proles linnaeana, commonly known as the Pasque flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to white, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has palmately divided, compound leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in meadows and rocky habitats.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its purported healing benefits.
